The Rothschilds: ownership advantages in multinational banking<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

John P. McKay<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">约翰·P·麦凯</span></span></span>

Introduction<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">引言</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Exceptionally successful and influential, the House of Rothschild became truly multinational in strategy and in management at an early date. A study of this path-breaking family firm should therefore be of considerable interest for an understanding of the origins and the development of multinational banking.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">作为一家极为成功且影响深远的家族企业，罗斯柴尔德家族很早就实现了战略与管理的真正跨国化。因此，研究这家开创性的家族企业，对于理解跨国银行业的起源与发展，无疑具有相当重要的意义。</span></span></span>

Such a study may of course be criticized for dealing with a very atypical enterprise and having therefore limited relevance for an understanding of the representative multinational bank. Yet I would agree with the proposition that a tiny handful of dominant firms may sometimes be more representative in a very real sense than 'a random sample of the clustering of small and insignificant firms which were left with residual shares.'<sup>1</sup> Moreover, although considerable material on the early history of the Rothschilds does exist, there has been no attempt to examine this history in the light of theoretical and conceptual issues related to the development of banks as multinationals.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">当然，这类研究可能会被批评为针对一家非常典型的企业，因此对理解代表性跨国银行的借鉴意义有限。但我同意这样的观点：少数几家主导企业有时可能比"那些由残存份额构成的、无足轻重的小企业集群的随机样本"更具实际代表性。 <sup>1</sup> 此外，尽管关于罗斯柴尔德家族早期历史的资料确实不少，但尚未有人尝试从银行作为跨国企业发展的理论和概念视角来审视这段历史。</span></span></span>

This study attempts to address these issues. It must be admitted at the outset that I am not certain what theories should guide such an investigation. Much of my own research has been on industrial firms, specifically foreign entrepreneurs in Tsarist Russia and electric tramway companies in Europe before 1914.<sup>2</sup> I also began studying the Paris Rothschilds in order to evaluate their investment in the Russian petroleum industry.<sup>3</sup> In these studies, innovative production technology that conveyed a competitive advantage appeared to me as a critical factor in explaining why entrepreneurs created new foreign (and domestic) enterprises, as it had appeared in various versions to Marx, Schumpeter, and other investigators.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本研究试图解决这些问题。首先必须承认，我并不确定应该用何种理论来指导这样的调查。我自己的研究大多集中于工业企业，特别是沙俄时期的外国企业家和 1914 年之前的欧洲电车公司。 <sup>2</sup> 我还开始研究巴黎的罗斯柴尔德家族，以评估他们在俄罗斯石油产业的投资。 <sup>3</sup> 在这些研究中，带来竞争优势的创新生产技术在我看来是解释企业家为何创建新的外国（及国内）企业的关键因素，这一点在马克思、熊彼特及其他研究者的不同版本论述中也有所体现。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">A priori,</span> technological innovation and production advantages may seem less relevant in accounting for developments in the banking sector, and this suggests that the problem might best be <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_121"></span>approached in terms of financial considerations — availability of capital, interest-rate differentials, etc. However, there has been little conceptual work on the emergence of multinational banks in this vein. Conversely, the growth of industrial multinationals has stimulated extensive theoretical discussion and resulted in conceptual advances. Above all, the focus on technology as a key factor in explaining direct foreign investment has merged with a parallel stress on the internalization of transaction costs by administrative hierarchies in a more inclusive paradigm of ownership advantages. As successfully synthesized by Dunning, a combination of asset-ownership advantages and transaction-ownership advantages largely explains why some firms choose to engage in international production. Dunning distinguishes clearly between the general category of microeconomic ownership advantages and the category of 'location advantages', which includes macroeconomic theories of capital movements, trade, and location.<sup>4</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">理论上，技术创新和生产优势在解释银行业发展时可能看似不太相关，这意味着问题或许更适合从金融角度考量——如资本可得性、利率差异等。然而，关于跨国银行兴起的此类概念性研究却寥寥无几。相反，工业跨国公司的增长激发了广泛的理论探讨，并推动了概念上的进步。尤为重要的是，将技术视为对外直接投资关键因素的关注点，与强调行政层级对交易成本进行内部化的并行观点相融合，共同形成了一个更具包容性的所有权优势范式。正如 Dunning 所成功综合的那样，资产所有权优势与交易所有权优势的结合，在很大程度上解释了为何某些企业选择参与国际生产。 邓宁明确区分了微观层面的所有权优势这一大类与“区位优势”这一类别，后者涵盖了有关资本流动、贸易与区位的宏观经济理论。 <sup>4</sup> </span></span></span>

Upon reflection, it seems to me that the development of the Paris Rothschilds is better understood in terms of firm-specific ownership advantages than in terms of location advantages. Moreover, the paradigm crafted to explain the rise of industrial multinationals also serves to illuminate the emergence of an important financial multinational, a promising extension of the basic approach. In presenting this argument, I shall look at the development of the Rothschilds and its Paris branch in four distinct periods, examining at each point the firm's multinational activities in the light of its evolving pattern of ownership advantages.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">思量过后，我认为巴黎罗斯柴尔德家族的发展更宜从企业特有的所有权优势来理解，而非区位优势。此外，那个用以阐释工业跨国公司崛起的范式，同样有助于揭示一家重要金融跨国公司的兴起——这亦是基本思路一个颇具前景的拓展。在论证这一观点时，我将分四个不同时期考察罗斯柴尔德家族及其巴黎分行的发展，并依据其不断演变的所有权优势模式，逐一审视该企业的跨国经营活动。</span></span></span>

The wartime rise of the Rothschilds<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">战争期间罗斯柴尔德家族的崛起</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The founder of the Rothschild fortune was Meyer Amschel Rothschild (1744-1812), a Jewish merchant in Frankfurt.<sup>5</sup> With the help of two younger brothers, Rothschild gradually expanded his unspecialized trade in second-hand goods to include antiques and gold coins of numismatic value. His most important customer became the future William IX of Hesse, an avid coin collector and a particularly enterprising prince. Renting his soldiers as mercenaries, as to the British government during the American revolutionary war, the prince needed bankers to repatriate payments made in London and to invest his growing surplus in British bonds and in loans to less frugal German rulers. Competition for the prince's financial business was intense, and Rothschild attained the status of regular intermediary in some of William's larger transactions only in the late 1780s. By that time Rothschild had also emerged as a substantial general merchant, dealing in colonial <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_122"></span>products and English manufactured goods, especially textiles.<sup>6</sup> Thus the firm concentrated on international flows of money and goods by 1789, but it was still confined to Frankfurt's Jewish ghetto.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">罗斯柴尔德家族的财富奠基人是法兰克福的一位犹太商人梅耶·阿姆谢尔·罗斯柴尔德（1744-1812）。 <sup>5</sup> 在两个弟弟的帮衬下，罗斯柴尔德原本杂而不精的二手货生意逐步扩展，涉及古董和具有钱币学价值的金币。他最重要的客户后来成为黑森-卡塞尔的威廉九世——一位狂热的钱币收藏家，也是一位极具商业头脑的王子。这位王子曾将手下士兵租借给英国政府充当雇佣军（例如在美国独立战争期间），因此需要银行家帮他将伦敦的付款汇回国内，并将其不断增长的盈余投资于英国债券，以及借贷给那些不太节俭的德国统治者。争夺这位王子金融业务的竞争异常激烈，直到 18 世纪 80 年代末，罗斯柴尔德才在威廉的一些大宗交易中获得了常规中介的地位。 到那时，罗斯柴尔德已崛起为一位实力雄厚的一般商人，经营殖民地产品和英国制成品，尤其是纺织品。 <sup>6</sup> 因此，到 1789 年，该公司专注于国际资金和商品流动，但仍局限于法兰克福的犹太人聚居区。</span></span></span>

The period of almost-uninterrupted European warfare from 1792 to 1815 profoundly modified the whole pattern of international flows and posed enormous challenges to participants like Meyer Amschel Rothschild. In general terms, political actions —occupations, confiscations, embargoes, etc. — increased both the risk and the potential profit of many international financial transactions. This was especially true of financial relations between the warring titans - expansionist France and industrializing Britain —and their shifting constellations of opportunistic clients in central Europe. The essence of Rothschild action up to 1815, it seems to me, was to evolve into a specialized multinational firm that became exceptionally skilful in handling big difficult financial and commercial transfers between warring countries and across enemy lines. In doing so, the firm made organizational innovations which gave it ownership advantages in handling such transactions and which earned it the abnormal Schumpeterian profits of successful innovation. This interpretation is supported by a brief examination of key developments in the firm's history to 1815.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1792 年至 1815 年间几乎不间断的欧洲战争深刻改变了国际流动的整体格局，给梅耶·阿姆谢尔·罗斯柴尔德这样的参与者带来了巨大挑战。总体而言，占领、没收、禁运等政治行动既增加了许多国际金融交易的风险，也提高了潜在利润。这一点在交战巨头——扩张主义的法国与工业化的英国——及其在中欧摇摆不定的机会主义盟友之间的金融关系中尤为明显。在我看来，直到 1815 年，罗斯柴尔德行动的本质是逐步演变为一家专业化的跨国公司，在处理交战国家之间及跨越敌线的庞大而棘手的金融与商业转移方面变得异常娴熟。 该公司通过组织创新，在处理此类交易时获得了所有权优势，并因此赚取了成功创新所带来的超常熊彼特式利润。对该公司在 1815 年之前历史中关键发展的简要考察，印证了这一解释。</span></span></span>

An important step was taken in 1798, when the Frankfurt firm established a 'British branch' in the person of the 21-year-old Nathan Rothschild, the oldest of Meyer Amschel's five sons. Settling in Manchester as a merchant and drawing upon his experience as a travelling salesman for his father, Nathan Rothschild bypassed the English intermediaries which the firm had previously used and sold directly to the firm's clientele on the continent through a network of relatives and Jewish allies. Well-informed about the rapidly-changing opportunities for legal and illegal trade, Nathan Rothschild successfully exported textiles and other manufactured goods. 'I can get you any thing made in England and I assure you that I will give a price 10 per cent below that of any other Frankfurt firm', he boasted in one successful sales pitch to a merchant in Antwerp.<sup>7</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1798 年迈出了重要一步，当时法兰克福公司以 21 岁的内森·罗思柴尔德（迈耶·阿姆谢尔五个儿子中的长子）为代理人，设立了"英国分公司"。内森·罗思柴尔德在曼彻斯特以商人身份定居，凭借其为父亲担任旅行推销员的经验，绕过了公司此前使用的英国中间商，通过亲戚和犹太盟友网络直接向欧洲大陆的客户销售。他深谙合法与非法贸易中瞬息万变的机会，成功出口了纺织品和其他制成品。"我能为您弄到英国制造的任何商品，并且我保证价格比法兰克福任何其他公司都低 10%，"他在一次成功说服安特卫普商人的推销中夸口道。</span></span></span>

Yet competition was surely brisk. A substantial number of foreign mercantile houses were opening similar branches in Britain, especially after 1806, when Hamburg and Frankfurt came under French control and Napoleon sought to close the continent to British exports.<sup>8</sup> The risks of smuggling in British goods were also substantial on the receiving end. It appears therefore that the firm's success in international commerce was due in large part to its ability to combine trade and smuggling with its financial operations.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，竞争无疑十分激烈。大量外国商行在英国开设了类似的分支机构，尤其是在 1806 年之后，汉堡和法兰克福落入法国控制，拿破仑企图将大陆市场对英国出口关闭。 <sup>8</sup> 在接收端，走私英国商品的风险也相当高。因此，这家公司在国际贸易上的成功，很大程度上归功于其将贸易与走私结合到金融业务中的能力。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_123"></span>These financial operations continued to revolve around serving William of Hesse and winning, with the help of his secretary, a quasi-monopoly on the prince's banking business. The prince was wartime transfer problems personified. He received large payments for his mercenaries from the British government, and these funds had to be repatriated from London and loaned to continental rulers. These funds also had to be protected, along with the rest of the prince's large fortune, from repudiation or confiscation. Initially, for the repatriation of funds to Frankfurt the firm could use the prince's sterling credits to buy English goods, which were then sold in continental Europe to obtain local currency for the ruler's account. After 1806, when French troops entered Frankfurt and sent a deposed William fleeing into exile, Meyer Amschel and the prince's secretary convinced a suspicious William to reverse the flow of funds in order to protect his capital from French confiscation. Thus William purchased British bonds through Nathan, who was in London beyond the reach of Napoleon's forces. Between 1809 and 1810 the family partnership sent £550,000 of the prince's money to London for safekeeping.<sup>9</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些金融操作依旧围绕着为黑森的威廉服务展开，并借助其秘书之手，赢得了这位亲王银行业务的准垄断权。亲王本人简直就是战时资金转移问题的化身。他因出租雇佣兵而从英国政府获得巨额款项，这些资金必须从伦敦汇回国内，再贷给欧洲大陆的统治者们。与此同时，这部分资金以及亲王其余的大笔财富都需要妥善保护，以防被拒付或没收。起初，为了将资金汇回法兰克福，该公司可以利用亲王的英镑信用购买英国货物，然后在欧洲大陆出售，为亲王的账户换取当地货币。1806 年后，当法军进入法兰克福，被废黜的威廉被迫流亡时，梅耶·阿姆谢尔和亲王的秘书说服了多疑的威廉改变资金流向，以保护他的资本免遭法国没收。 于是，威廉通过身在伦敦、不受拿破仑军队威胁的内森购买了英国债券。1809 至 1810 年间，家族合伙将王子的 55 万英镑资金汇至伦敦妥善保管。 <sup>9</sup> </span></span></span>

Significantly, the firm's masterful exploitation of its intermediary role remained a function of international conflict and the resultant barriers to normal financial transactions. Because the British government had already sequestered some of William's funds on the grounds that he was aiding the enemy, the prince had to invest in secret behind a Rothschild facade. Thus he could do little but fume and fret when official certificates documenting his purchases were very slow to arrive, not reaching him in central Europe until 1813. The absence of normal market conditions for monitoring his investments enabled Nathan Rothschild to temporarily 'borrow' the large sums sent to Britain by Prince William by simply crediting the prince with the official rate of interest.<sup>10</sup> With these funds at his disposal, an audacious Nathan Rothschild could lead the firm to new heights in the specialized transfer business after 1811.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">值得注意的是，这家公司巧妙地利用其中介角色，这归根结底是国际冲突以及由此产生的正常金融交易障碍所致。由于英国政府以威廉王子援助敌方为由，扣押了他部分资金，这位王子不得不借罗斯柴尔德之名秘密投资。因此，当证明他购买行为的官方凭证迟迟未到，直至 1813 年才送达中欧的他手中时，他只能干着急却无能为力。由于缺乏正常的市场条件来监控他的投资，内森·罗斯柴尔德仅需按官方利率为王子记账，就能暂时“借用”汇往英国的大笔资金。 <sup>10</sup> 有了这些资金在手，胆大包天的内森·罗斯柴尔德得以在 1811 年后，带领公司在专业转账业务上攀登新的高峰。</span></span></span>

Opportunities for profit were great because war created enormous variations in exchange rates across different markets. In one famous operation in 1811, Nathan Rothschild took all of 'his' capital to buy an entire shipment of gold — £800,000 — from the East India Company. He shipped the gold coin to Spain to purchase bills on London, which had been issued by Wellington and which were selling in Spain at an enormous discount of perhaps 25 per cent. He then brought the bills to London for payment at par. In later years he described this coup as 'the best business I ever did'.<sup>11</sup> The market across the channel was also <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_124"></span>imperfect and arbitrage possibilities were also excellent. In 1811, they brought Jacob — later known as James — Rothschild, the youngest of the five brothers, from Frankfurt to France.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">战争导致不同市场的汇率剧烈波动，因此获利的机会巨大。1811 年有一桩著名的操作：内森·罗斯柴尔德将他所有的资本——80 万英镑——全部用于从东印度公司购买一整批黄金。他将这些金币运往西班牙，用来购买威灵顿发行的伦敦汇票，这些汇票在西班牙以大约 25%的巨大折扣出售。随后，他将这些汇票带到伦敦按面值兑现。多年后，他形容这次操作是"我做过最得意的一笔生意"。 <sup>11</sup> 海峡对岸的市场同样不完善，套利机会也非常可观。1811 年，他们将雅各布（后来被称为詹姆斯·罗斯柴尔德）从法兰克福派往法国——他是五兄弟中最年幼的一位。</span></span></span>

James Rothschild received large shipments of gold guineas, which his brother Nathan exported 'illegally' but certainly with tacit British approval and which were imported into France with Napoleon's mercantilistic approbation. The young Rothschild then sold the gold in Paris for francs and, in the words of Napoleon's treasury minister,<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

Other firms were active in this business, but the historian of the Rothschilds' Paris branch believes that by 1812 James Rothschild 'had more or less a monopoly on transactions on sterling in Paris'.<sup>13</sup> The firm had clearly developed a competitive advantage by internalizing sterling—franc transactions. Alternatively, in the famous account by Corti, James Rothschild sometimes bought bills of exchange in Paris on Spain, Portugal, Sicily and Malta with gold guineas, sending the proceeds through a network of largely Jewish bankers to Wellington's army in Spain and hence to London for payment. This path of transfer represented large savings as compared with the discounts on bills drawn on London in these small financial centres.<sup>14</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">其他公司也活跃于这一业务，但罗斯柴尔德家族巴黎分行的历史学家认为，到 1812 年，詹姆斯·罗斯柴尔德“几乎垄断了巴黎的英镑交易”。 <sup>13</sup> 该公司通过将英镑与法郎交易内部化，显然建立了竞争优势。或者，按照科尔蒂的著名描述，詹姆斯·罗斯柴尔德有时会用金几尼在巴黎购买西班牙、葡萄牙、西西里和马耳他的汇票，然后通过一个主要由犹太银行家组成的网络将收益送到西班牙的威灵顿军队手中，再转至伦敦进行支付。与在这些小型金融中心开具的伦敦汇票折扣相比，这种转移路径节省了大量成本。 <sup>14</sup> </span></span></span>

These accounts suggest how the Rothschild partnership, consisting of Nathan Rothschild and his four brothers after the death of their father in 1812, became by 1813 the preferred agent of the British government for payments to its armies and allies on the continent. The partners continued to make British payments in local currencies to British and allied armies fighting and occupying France after Napoleon's first and second defeats. This was very big business: the Paymaster General of the British government estimated in 1816 that one-half of the £42,500,000 paid on the continent between 1811 and 1815 had been handled by the Rothschilds.<sup>15</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些记录表明，在 1812 年父亲去世后，由内森·罗斯柴尔德及其四位兄弟组成的罗斯柴尔德合伙公司，到 1813 年已成为英国政府向其大陆军队和盟友付款的首选代理机构。在拿破仑第一次和第二次战败后，这家合伙公司继续以当地货币向在法国作战和占领法国的英军及盟军支付英方款项。这是一笔巨大的业务：英国政府的军需总长在 1816 年估算，1811 年至 1815 年间在欧洲大陆支付的 4250 万英镑中，有一半是由罗斯柴尔德家族经手的。 <sup>15</sup> </span></span></span>

In 1815, then, the firm was solidly established, though by no means the leader among European financial houses. The firm had evolved during the Napoleonic era from a local unspecialized general merchandizing and banking operation under Meyer Amschel into a highly-specialized multinational partnership of five brothers, a partnership concentrating on making difficult international transfers between branches on behalf of a few big clients, <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_125"></span>notably William of Hesse and the British government. The firm had three permanent branches in three leading financial centres —Frankfurt, London and Paris — and two additional partners were available for travel and ultra-secret operations. Information was already transmitted by the firm's own private courier service, often in an almost-impenetrable private language of coded Yiddish written in an abbreviated Hebrew script. The firm was tightly-structured financially, drawing a consolidated balance sheet of profits and losses for all operations each year at the home office in Frankfurt. In short, in its formative years the House of Rothschild created a multinational organization that successfully internalized certain large, risky, and potentially very profitable, international transfers within the firm. Thus the firm innovated successfully to achieve a valuable firm-specific transaction advantage in some imperfect exchange markets.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1815 年，这家公司已根基稳固，虽未在欧洲金融界独占鳌头，却也举足轻重。自拿破仑时代起，它从迈耶·阿姆谢尔旗下本地零散的综合商贸与银行业务，演变为由五兄弟组成的、高度专业化的跨国合伙制企业。其核心业务是代表少数大客户——尤其是黑森的威廉王子及英国政府——在各大分行间完成高难度的国际资金转移。公司于三大金融重镇——法兰克福、伦敦与巴黎——设立了常驻分行，另有两位合伙人专司出差及绝密行动。信息已通过公司自建的私人信使网络传递，常使用一套近乎无懈可击的密语：用希伯来字母缩写写就的意第绪语编码。 这家公司在财务上结构严密，每年在法兰克福总部为所有业务编制合并损益表。简言之，在其形成阶段，罗斯柴尔德家族创建了一家跨国组织，成功地将某些规模庞大、风险较高但潜在利润丰厚的国际转移业务内部化。因此，该公司在不完善的汇率市场中成功创新，获得了宝贵的公司特有交易优势。</span></span></span>

<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">持续创新与国家贷款，1815—35 年</span></span></span>

Emerging from the Napoleonic wars with exceptional expertise in transferring large sums between branches and across political barriers, the Rothschild enterprise could anticipate increased competition in their transfer business as peace brought more normal economic relations and more perfect markets. The firm met the challenges and the opportunities of peace with great success. Holding on to its initial advantage in big international transfers, the firm continued to innovate and expanded its domain into international underwriting. This section will analyse this second stage of the firm's development, which saw the individual branches still operating in closely co-ordinated fashion.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从拿破仑战争中崛起，罗斯柴尔德企业在各分支机构之间以及跨越政治障碍进行大额资金转移方面拥有卓越专长。随着和平带来更正常的经济关系和更完善的市场，其转账业务面临的竞争预计将加剧。该企业成功应对了和平带来的挑战与机遇。在保持大型国际转账领域初始优势的同时，企业持续创新，并将其业务领域拓展至国际承销。本节将分析企业发展的第二阶段，该阶段中各个分支机构仍以高度协调的方式运作。</span></span></span>

One may begin with the firm's postwar structure and its resources. The firm was an international partnership, in which each of the five Rothschild brothers had varying portions of the capital and the profits associated with each of the firm's branches. Gille's analysis of the small number of surviving balance sheets and partnership agreements illuminates early developments. The partnership's total capital, which stood at 800,000 florins (about 2,000,000 francs) in September 1810 (two years before the death of Meyer Amschel), had reached £136,000 (3,332,000 francs) on 21 March 1815, according to an agreement of that date. Total capital then leaped to £1,772,000 (42,528,000 francs) on 2 June 1818 and to £4,082,000 (102,050,000 francs) on 31 August 1825. As Gille and other scholars have noted, the spectacular increase from 1815 to 1818 is extremely difficult to explain, given the limits of surviving documentation.<sup>16</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">首先可以从公司战后的结构及其资源入手。该公司是一家国际合伙制企业，五位罗斯柴尔德兄弟各自持有不同比例的资本，并享有各分支机构的相应利润。吉尔对现存的少量资产负债表和合伙协议的分析揭示了早期的演变。根据 1810 年 9 月（迈耶·阿姆谢尔去世前两年）的数据，公司的总资本为 80 万弗罗林（约 200 万法郎）；而到 1815 年 3 月 21 日，根据当日签署的协议，资本已达到 13.6 万英镑（333.2 万法郎）。随后，总资本在 1818 年 6 月 2 日跃升至 177.2 万英镑（4252.8 万法郎），并在 1825 年 8 月 31 日达到 408.2 万英镑（1.0205 亿法郎）。正如吉尔及其他学者所指出的，鉴于现存文献资料有限，1815 年至 1818 年间这种惊人的资本增长极难解释。 <sup>16</sup> </span></span></span>

<span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_126"></span>It is possible that the 1815 act had secret clauses, which were designed to protect against 'event risk'. Certainly secret understandings had modified the text of the act drawn up in Frankfurt in 1810. That act did not include Nathan Rothschild, who was in London behind enemy lines, and for whom a large part was 'incontestably reserved'.<sup>17</sup> Thus the statement in the document drawn up in London on 21 March 1815 that Nathan Rothschild alone held £90,000 of the firm's total capital, while his four brothers together held only £46,000, may very well have reflected the most recent turn of political developments — Napoleon's escape from Elba on 1 March and his triumphal entry into Paris on 20 March. Prudence would dictate that the brothers should temporarily place their (understated?) assets in the hands of the partner most securely beyond Napoleon's reach. It would be another good example of the firm's cohesiveness and its skill in transferring funds between branches — in this case, its own — in the face of political uncertainty.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1815 年的法案可能包含秘密条款，旨在防范"事件风险"。的确，一些秘密谅解修改了 1810 年在法兰克福起草的法案文本。该法案并未将内森·罗斯柴尔德纳入其中——他当时身处伦敦敌后，而他的大部分份额"无可争辩地被预留了"。 <sup>17</sup> 因此，1815 年 3 月 21 日在伦敦起草的文件中称，内森·罗斯柴尔德一人持有公司总资本中的 9 万英镑，而他的四个兄弟加起来仅有 4.6 万英镑，这很可能反映了政治局势的最新转折——拿破仑于 3 月 1 日逃离厄尔巴岛，并于 3 月 20 日凯旋进入巴黎。出于谨慎起见，兄弟们应暂时将他们（可能被低估的？）资产交到那位最安全地处于拿破仑势力范围之外的合伙人手中。 这将是该公司凝聚力及其在政治不确定性下于各分支机构（此处指其旗下分公司）之间灵活调拨资金的又一明证。</span></span></span>

The distribution of earnings reflected the unity of the firm, while also providing incentive at the branch level. Each partner received a basic interest of 4 per cent on invested capital and only returns in excess of that 4 per cent were considered profits, an accounting technique that the firm would use throughout the nineteenth century. Each partner also received a variable share of the profits of each branch after the interest charge on capital and the overhead expenses were paid. In 1818, Nathan Rothschild received one-half of all profits generated by his London branch, while each of his four brothers received one-eighth. Nathan received 4/16 of the profits of Frankfurt and Paris, while each brother on the continent received 3/16 of the profits of those branches. The 1818 agreement stated that no profits could be withdrawn before the partnership expired in three years, reflecting the undeniable fact that the firm's rapidly-growing capital came almost exclusively from reinvested profits. The five associates also agreed to provide each other with detailed weekly information on all operations in progress, and to devote all their business efforts to the partnership. Thus the firm's multinational operations and its decision-making were closely linked, in an attempt no doubt to check the mutual recriminations that sometimes marked the war years.<sup>18</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">收益分配既体现了公司的整体性，又为各分行提供了激励机制。每位合伙人按其投入资本获得 4%的基本利息，只有超过 4%的部分才被视为利润——这一会计方法贯穿了公司整个 19 世纪的发展历程。在扣除资本利息和管理费用后，每位合伙人还能按比例分享其所在分行的利润。1818 年，内森·罗斯柴尔德获得了伦敦分行全部利润的一半，而其四位兄弟各分得八分之一。内森还从法兰克福和巴黎分行的利润中分得 4/16，而大陆的其他兄弟则各从这两个分行获得 3/16 的利润。 1818 年的协议明确规定，在为期三年的合伙关系到期前，任何利润均不得提取，这反映了一个不争的事实：公司迅速增长的资本几乎完全来自再投资的利润。五位合伙人还同意每周向彼此提供所有正在进行的业务的详细信息，并将全部商业精力投入合伙事业。因此，公司的跨国运营与决策制定紧密相连，无疑是为了遏制战争年代时有发生的相互指责。 <sup>18</sup> </span></span></span>

The detailed accounts oil the firm's capital in 1818 and 1825, which reappear only in the 1860s, suggest the rising importance of the Paris branch in the firm's activity and a corresponding diminution of London. (Frankfurt's role is overstated in 1825 because at that time it still included all operations in Vienna and Naples.) The accounts also highlight the firm's prodigious success and its leading position in European finance by 1825. For example, the firm's total capital of 100,000,000 francs in 1825 exceeded even that of the Bank of France (60,000,000 francs) and it dwarfed that of the Maison Laffitte, the second-largest commercial bank in Paris with a total capital of 7,000,000 francs. Nurtured in war, the Rothschilds adapted brilliantly to peace.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1818 年和 1825 年的公司资本明细账目（直到 1860 年代才再次出现）表明，巴黎分行在公司业务中的重要性日益上升，而伦敦分行的地位则相应下降。（1825 年法兰克福的作用被夸大了，因为当时它仍包含维也纳和那不勒斯的所有业务。）这些账目也凸显了公司惊人的成功，以及到 1825 年其在欧洲金融领域的领先地位。例如，1825 年公司总资本达 1 亿法郎，甚至超过了法兰西银行（6000 万法郎），更令巴黎第二大商业银行拉菲特商行（总资本 700 万法郎）相形见绌。在战争中成长起来的罗斯柴尔德家族，出色地适应了和平时代。</span></span></span>

In explaining the firm's on-going success, international operations continued to play the critical role. First of all, the firm still excelled in the business of international transfers, although global figures are lacking. Building upon its wartime system of private fast couriers, the House of Rothschild concentrated on bills of exchange traded in London, Paris, Frankfurt and Vienna, where the costs of transactions could be internalized by the firm. With superior information and a large network of correspondents in other centres, the firm handled a large flow of transactions, often <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_128"></span>anticipating the direction of currency fluctuations for extra profit. According to Gille, the Rothschilds in these years occupied on European exchange markets 'a considerable, even dominant position'.<sup>19</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在解释公司持续成功的原因时，国际业务继续扮演着关键角色。首先，尽管缺乏全球数据，该公司在国际汇款业务中依然表现出色。依托其战时建立的私人快递系统，罗斯柴尔德家族专注于伦敦、巴黎、法兰克福和维也纳的汇票交易，在这些地方，交易成本可由公司内部化。凭借优越的信息渠道和在其他金融中心庞大的代理网络，该公司处理了大量交易，并常常预判货币波动的方向以获取额外利润。根据 Gille 的说法，这些年间，罗斯柴尔德家族在欧洲外汇市场上占据了"相当大的、甚至主导性的地位"。</span></span></span>

Commissions on such transfers were high, with 1 to 1.5 per cent of the total sum customary. For example, in 1816, Rothschilds received a commission of 1.25 per cent to transfer by means of bills of exchange some indemnity money payable in francs from Paris to Austrian occupation forces in Colmar. In 1819, the firm received a 1 per cent commission on a transfer of 3,000,000 francs from Paris to Vienna.<sup>20</sup> Rates on international transfers fell subsequently, but remained significant. Until at least 1850 the Paris branch charged from 0.25 to 0.5 per cent for making payments in francs within France — a rather expensive check.<sup>21</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">此类转账的手续费很高，通常是转账总额的 1%到 1.5%。例如，1816 年，Rothschilds 通过汇票将一笔应付法郎的赔款从巴黎转到科尔马的奥地利占领军，收取了 1.25%的手续费。1819 年，该公司将 300 万法郎从巴黎转到维也纳，收取了 1%的手续费。 <sup>20</sup> 随后国际转账的费率有所下降，但仍然相当可观。至少到 1850 年，巴黎分行在法国境内以法郎付款收取 0.25%到 0.5%的手续费——这相当于相当昂贵的支票服务。 <sup>21</sup> </span></span></span>

A large if unknown portion of the international transfer business continued to be in the form of specie and precious metals, as during the war. The Paris and London branches were especially active in this movement, repeatedly using large shipments of hard money for arbitrage and for the balancing of accounts between branches.<sup>22</sup> The Paris branch (and probably the London branch as well) established at an early date a precious-metals refinery in the capital to complement its trade in bullion, and the French house also invested in private ateliers contracting to fabricate gold coin for the French and the Belgian states. All such contracts made Rothschilds the supplier of the necessary metal. Founded as a partnership with an important Belgian official in 1833, the private mint in Belgium divided capital and earnings on a 50-50 basis, although the Rothschild investment was in commandite and thus with limited liability.<sup>23</sup> It is indeed very probable that the House of Rothschild rapidly became the principal supplier of precious metal to the various states of Europe after 1815.<sup>24</sup> Trade in specie and precious metals complemented trade in bills of exchange, reinforcing the firm's strong position in international transfers.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">一战时期，大量国际汇款业务（具体规模未知）仍以硬币和贵金属形式进行。巴黎和伦敦分行在此类交易中尤为活跃，多次通过大规模硬通货运输进行套利及分行间账目结算。 <sup>22</sup> 巴黎分行（伦敦分行可能亦然）早在首都设立贵金属精炼厂以辅助金条交易，其法国办事处还投资私营工坊，承包为法国和比利时政府铸造金币的业务。所有此类合同均使罗斯柴尔德家族成为所需金属的供应商。1833 年，比利时某私人铸币厂与重要官员合作成立，双方平分股本与收益——尽管罗斯柴尔德家族以隐名合伙形式投资，仅承担有限责任。 <sup>23</sup> 1815 年后，罗斯柴尔德家族确实极有可能迅速成为欧洲各国贵金属的主要供应商。 <sup>24</sup> 铸币与贵金属贸易补充了汇票交易，进一步巩固了该家族在国际汇兑领域的强势地位。</span></span></span>

Continued success in international transfer operations was facilitated by an interrelated trade in the government obligations of European states. As the House of Rothschild rapidly came to dominate the state-loan business after 1815, it developed and traded a large portfolio of state bonds, which it constantly used for speculative purposes on the different national markets, as Gille has shown. This large-scale trading of existing debt issues complemented transfer operations and facilitated 'parallel action' in exchange markets — to use a favourite term for market manipulation of Jules Aron, a Rothschild manager of a later generation. This 'synergy' also makes the firm's outstanding success in state<span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_129"></span>loan underwriting all the more critical and intriguing.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">国家间政府债务的相互交易，进一步推动了国际转移业务的持续成功。自 1815 年后，罗斯柴尔德家族迅速主导了国家贷款业务，他们开发并持有一个庞大的国债组合，正如吉尔所揭示的那样，这些债券不断被用于在不同国家市场进行投机。这种大规模现有债务交易不仅补充了转移业务，还促进了外汇市场的"协同行动"——借用后世罗斯柴尔德经理人朱尔·阿隆偏好用来描述市场操纵的术语。这种"协同效应"也使得该公司在国债承销领域的卓越成就更加关键且耐人寻味。</span></span></span>

Much of the story is known. As the problems of William of Hesse showed, long-term funded public debt in the form of marketable securities was well-established in the eighteenth century. Amsterdam dominated international capital markets. Dutch bankers underwrote debt issues in florins for several states and they helped Dutch investors to purchase securities issued abroad and payable in foreign currencies.<sup>25</sup> The long period of warfare and financial dislocation to 1815 disrupted bond markets, but the Rothschilds had a model for underwriting public debt which they could use and improve.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">故事的大部分内容已广为人知。正如黑森-卡塞尔伯爵威廉所遇到的问题所示，以可流通证券形式存在的长期公共债务在 18 世纪已相当成熟。阿姆斯特丹主导着国际资本市场。荷兰银行家为多个国家承销以弗罗林计价的债券发行，并帮助荷兰投资者购买国外发行、以外币支付的证券。 <sup>25</sup> 持续到 1815 年的长期战争和金融动荡扰乱了债券市场，但罗斯柴尔德家族拥有一种承销公共债务的模式，他们可以利用并改进这一模式。</span></span></span>

The firm's ascension was rapid but not instantaneous. It played little or no role in the French indemnity loans of 1816-17, which totalled 700,000,000 francs. That loan was taken by the old leaders, Baring of London and Hope of Amsterdam, who worked in close partnership with French bankers to place most of these obligations in France.<sup>26</sup> Rothschilds' first major triumph came in 1818, when Nathan Rothschild reached an agreement to underwrite a Prussian loan of £5,000,000 after tough negotiations. This loan put the House in the ranks of the great financial powers and pointed the way to the attainment of a unique 'superpower' status by 1825.<sup>27</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">该公司的崛起速度虽快，却非一蹴而就。在 1816-1817 年总额达 7 亿法郎的法国赔偿贷款中，它几乎未扮演任何角色。这笔贷款由伦敦的巴林银行和阿姆斯特丹的霍普银行这两大老牌巨头主导，它们与法国银行家紧密合作，将这些债券中的大部分在法国本土消化。 <sup>26</sup> 罗斯柴尔德家族的首个重大突破出现在 1818 年，当时内森·罗斯柴尔德经过艰难谈判，成功承销了一笔 500 万英镑的普鲁士贷款。这笔交易使罗斯柴尔德家族跻身金融巨擘之列，并为其在 1825 年达到独一无二的"超级大国"地位铺平了道路。 <sup>27</sup> </span></span></span>

In 1820, the efforts of Solomon Rothschild to cultivate Metternich and the Austrians while attaining the rank of nobility for himself and his continental brothers resulted in a major breakthrough. Previously limited to transferring British subsidies and French indemnities to Austria, Rothschilds joined with their partner in Vienna, the Hamburg banker David Parish, to underwrite a loan of 20,000,000 florins paying interest at 6.5 per cent.<sup>28</sup> A second loan of 35,000,000 florins at 5 per cent, with a handsome underwriting commission of 4 per cent, was handled almost immediately thereafter. Taken at 66⅔ per cent of par, Rothschilds and Paris energetically placed the loan on different European markets with great success, encouraging speculation that drove the price of these 'Rothschild bonds' as high as 140 before retreating. With Solomon Rothschild now installed in Vienna and maintaining the very closest of relations with Metternich and his secretary, Friedrich von Gentz, the House had attained a dominant position in Austrian public finance.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1820 年，所罗门·罗斯柴尔德在争取贵族头衔、拉拢梅特涅和奥地利权贵方面取得了重大突破。此前他们只负责将英国补贴和法国赔款转移至奥地利，如今他们与维也纳的合作伙伴——汉堡银行家大卫·帕里什联手，操盘了一笔 2000 万弗罗林的贷款，年利率 6.5%。紧接着，他们又处理了第二笔 3500 万弗罗林的贷款，利率 5%，承销佣金高达 4%。罗斯柴尔德家族以面值 66.67%的价格接手后，迅速将这批债券成功推向欧洲各国市场，甚至炒得火热——这支"罗斯柴尔德债券"的价格一度飙升至 140，随后才回落。 随着所罗门·罗斯柴尔德在维也纳安顿下来，并与梅特涅及其秘书弗里德里希·冯·根茨保持着极为密切的关系，该家族在奥地利公共财政中占据了主导地位。</span></span></span>

This quickly led to involvement in the Kingdom of Naples and the establishment of the firm's fifth branch at Naples in conjunction with Austria's suppression of the Neapolitan revolt. The 'working capital' desperately needed by Austria's invading armies was furnished by Carl Rothschild, the youngest of the five <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_130"></span>partners. Then, backed by Austrian might, he negotiated a large 5 per cent loan with the nominal value of 6,000,000 ducats at 60 per cent of par, cutting in local Neapolitan bankers as co-underwriters to eliminate competition. This, and a subsequent Neapolitan loan payable in ducats, was distributed throughout Europe by the Rothschild network, with both the Paris and the London markets showing an excellent appetite. In 1824, the House of Rothschild successfully floated a 36-year, 5 per cent, sterling loan for £2,500,000 in London, which led to another Neapolitan sterling loan in January 1825.<sup>29</sup> Considering that the firm had already placed a Russian loan of £6,500,000 in London in 1822, it is not hard to see why Rothschilds appeared to liberals in the 1820s (and to some historians since) as closely tied to the Holy Alliance of Prussia, Austria, and Russia, and as bankers for the 'forces of Counter-Revolution'.<sup>30</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这很快就牵涉到那不勒斯王国的事务，并在奥地利镇压那不勒斯起义期间，于那不勒斯设立了公司的第五家分行。奥地利入侵军队急需的‘营运资金’由五位合伙人中最年轻的卡尔·罗斯柴尔德提供。随后，在奥地利势力的支持下，他谈判了一笔面值 600 万杜卡特、票面利率 5%的大额贷款，发行价仅为面值的 60%，并拉拢当地那不勒斯银行家作为联合承销商以消除竞争。这笔贷款以及后续另一笔以杜卡特偿还的那不勒斯贷款，通过罗斯柴尔德网络分销至欧洲各地，巴黎和伦敦市场均表现出极高的吸纳意愿。1824 年，罗斯柴尔德家族在伦敦成功发行了一笔为期 36 年、利率 5%、金额 250 万英镑的英镑贷款，次年 1 月又促成了另一笔那不勒斯的英镑贷款。 考虑到该公司已于 1822 年在伦敦发行了 650 万英镑的俄罗斯贷款，就不难理解为何在 19 世纪 20 年代的自由派人士（以及后来的某些历史学家）眼中，罗斯柴尔德家族与普鲁士、奥地利和俄罗斯的神圣同盟关系紧密，被视为“反革命势力”的银行家。 <sup>30</sup> </span></span></span>

James de Rothschild also gained a dominant position for the House in France, but only after a hard fight against well-entrenched groups of local private bankers. The turning point came in 1823, when Rothschild won his first large loan with a sealed bid of 89.55, as opposed to three identical (and collusive) bids of 87.55 submitted by three Parisian competitors.<sup>31</sup> The aggressive bidding of the Rothschilds almost certainly reflected a more general cost advantage in underwriting large state loans that undergirded the partnership's success. Playing the leading role in French state finance after 1823, often at the head of a monopolistic syndicate embracing all French bankers, the Rothschilds through the Parisian branch also financed the birth of the Belgian state in 1831 and guided Belgian finances for a considerable time thereafter.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德也为家族在法国赢得了主导地位，但这背后是与当地根深蒂固的私人银行家群体的激烈较量。转折点出现在 1823 年，当时罗斯柴尔德以 89.55 的密封报价赢得了第一笔大额贷款，而三位巴黎竞争对手提交了三份相同的（且串通的）87.55 报价。 <sup>31</sup> 罗斯柴尔德家族的激进报价几乎肯定反映了其在承销大型国家贷款方面的整体成本优势，这正是该家族合伙企业成功的基础。1823 年后，罗斯柴尔德家族在法国国家金融中扮演了主导角色，常常领导一个囊括所有法国银行家的垄断银团。通过巴黎分行，他们还在 1831 年为比利时国家的诞生提供了资金，并在相当长一段时间内指导了比利时的金融事务。</span></span></span>

The success of the House of Rothschild in underwriting state loans after 1815 may be explained in terms of an innovation which bestowed new ownership advantages. Previously, the market for state debt had been largely national (French loans through French bankers for French investors, for example) or bilateral (French loans through Dutch bankers for Dutch investors). Building on its multinational structure, the Rothschilds now innovated by treating the loans of the leading individual states as destined for, and forming part of, a single international market. This gave the firm a major advantage that allowed it to establish, for a time, a quasi-monopoly on underwriting and marketing state loans.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1815 年后，罗斯柴尔德家族在承销国家贷款方面的成功，可归因于一项赋予其全新所有权优势的创新。此前，国家债务市场基本局限于国内（例如法国贷款通过法国银行家面向法国投资者）或双边模式（例如法国贷款通过荷兰银行家面向荷兰投资者）。而罗斯柴尔德家族依托其跨国架构，开创性地将主要国家的贷款视为面向单一国际市场的组成部分，使这些贷款本身也成为该市场的一部分。这一策略为该公司带来了显著优势，使其一度在国家贷款承销与发行领域建立起近乎垄断的地位。</span></span></span>

The partnership's ability to treat previously-separate national loan markets as an integrated international whole within the firm had many aspects. First and foremost, it rested upon having a strong position in the leading financial centres through the <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_131"></span>branches in Paris and London, as well as in key secondary markets through the branches in Frankfurt, Vienna and Naples. No other banking house had such co-ordinated common ownership operating on several different markets. Such ownership enabled the partnership to replace market transactions with more efficient intrafirm transfers on many different financial axes and to reap corresponding economies of integration. Multinational integration, and superior intrafirm communications through its own fast courier service, also allowed Rothschilds to better exploit arbitrage possibilities, which were outstanding because large, rapidly-shifting gaps existed between different loan markets. As Gille puts it, 'Movements of capital were not yet substantial enough to bring about a certain equalization and financial liquidities varied greatly according to the market and the season'.<sup>32</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">该合伙企业将原先彼此分离的各国贷款市场视为公司内部统一国际整体，这一能力体现在多个方面。首先且最重要的是，它通过在巴黎和伦敦的分支机构在主要金融中心占据强势地位，并通过法兰克福、维也纳和那不勒斯的分支机构在关键次级市场也拥有优势。没有其他银行机构能像这样以协调一致的共同所有权在多个不同市场运营。这种所有权结构使合伙企业能够在多个金融轴线上以更高效的内部转移取代市场交易，并获取相应的一体化经济效应。跨国一体化以及通过自有快速信使服务实现的优越内部沟通，也使罗斯柴尔德家族能够更好地利用套利机会，而由于不同贷款市场之间存在巨大且快速变化的差距，这些套利机会尤为突出。 正如吉勒所言，“资本的流动尚未达到足以实现某种均衡的程度，而金融流动性则因市场和季节的不同而差异悬殊。” <sup>32</sup> </span></span></span>

In negotiating and winning a major loan contract, the multinational Rothschilds could choose the most suitable market and the most appropriate currency for the primary offering. They could simultaneously float the loan on several other markets and then profit from additional arbitrage possibilities in the after market. For example, the Neapolitan loan of 1822 was floated in Naples in ducats, then sold largely in Paris where market quotations were consistently higher. A second Neapolitan loan of 1824 was floated in sterling in London because liquid capital was particularly abundant there.<sup>33</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在谈判并赢得一份大型贷款合约的过程中，跨国巨头罗斯柴尔德家族能够为初次发行选择最合适的市场和最恰当的货币。他们可以同时在多个其他市场发行该贷款，然后通过上市后的额外套利机会获利。例如，1822 年的那不勒斯贷款以杜卡特货币在那不勒斯发行，随后主要在巴黎出售，而那里的市场报价持续偏高。1824 年的第二笔那不勒斯贷款则在伦敦以英镑发行，因为当时那里的流动资本特别充裕。 <sup>33</sup> </span></span></span>

The firm's strategy of being present on all the major markets, in order to take advantage of differences between them, may be clearly seen in a letter from Karl Rothschild to the Neopolitan government in 1824. With its loans quoted in different markets, Rothschild explained, the government of Naples would be able, through comparison, 'to see if new bonds should be French, English, or Neapolitan certificates and to place its loan on the market which appears most ready to receive this increase \[of debt\]'.<sup>34</sup> This statement also suggests how the Rothschilds served governments as skilled financial advisers and maintained favour by passing on some of the profits they reaped from their innovation.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">该公司在所有主要市场布局以利用市场间差异的策略，在 1824 年卡尔·罗斯柴尔德致那不勒斯政府的一封信中体现得淋漓尽致。罗斯柴尔德解释道，由于贷款在不同市场挂牌，那不勒斯政府可以通过比较"判断新债券应发行法国、英国还是那不勒斯凭证，并将贷款投向最可能接受这一债务增量的市场"。 <sup>34</sup> 这一表述也揭示了罗斯柴尔德家族如何以精明的财务顾问身份为各国政府服务，并通过让渡创新所得的部分利润来维持自身优势。</span></span></span>

Intrafirm integration of disparate loan markets shaped the technicalities of underwriting. The House charged itself with printing the loans it floated, which all shared a certain look and almost appeared for a time as a 'Rothschild bond'.<sup>35</sup> Thus the House championed and possibly pioneered in the diffusion of the anonymous bearer bond, which offered investors special protection in comparison with nominative registration. As Solomon Rothschild put it, in connection with a delicate loan involving the Duchy of Parma in 1827,<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">公司内部整合不同贷款市场，塑造了承销的技术细节。该家族银行自行印制它所发行的贷款，这些贷款都有统一的外观，一度几乎形成了“罗斯柴尔德债券”的风格。 <sup>35</sup> 因此，该家族银行倡导并可能率先推广了匿名无记名债券，与记名登记相比，这种债券为投资者提供了特殊保护。正如所罗门·罗斯柴尔德在 1827 年涉及帕尔马公国的一笔微妙贷款时所言：</span></span></span>

<div class="quotes">

<span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_132"></span>It ought to be important for the princess to realize the capital in such a way that no one can contest the inheritance of her legitimate heirs after her death. The creation of bearer bonds, issued by a famous banking house and constantly passing from hand to hand, takes care of all possible difficulties.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对这位公主而言，以这样一种方式变现资本至关重要，使得她去世后，她的合法继承人能够顺利继承，无人能提出异议。由一家著名银行发行、不断在手中流转的无记名债券，能够解决所有可能的麻烦。</span></span></span>

</div>

No state could risk the collapse of its credit by failing to honour all such anonymous obligations.<sup>36</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">任何国家都不敢冒着信用崩溃的风险，不履行所有这些匿名债务的兑付义务。 <sup>36</sup> </span></span></span>

The House even sought to develop a truly multinational bond, which would be payable in several markets in different currencies at a fixed rate of exchange. Thus James de Rothschild envisaged such a loan for Spain in the early 1820s, as a way of integrating a chronic debtor state with an unstable currency into the international capital market.<sup>37</sup> The project was not realized, but something similar was accomplished in the case of Belgian bonds, payable at a fixed rate of exchange in either sterling in London, or in francs in Paris or Brussels. Thus the firm reduced uncertainties and transaction costs for investors in different financial centres and expanded the market for Belgian securities.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">罗斯柴尔德家族甚至试图发行一种真正跨国界的债券，这种债券可在多个市场以固定汇率用不同货币支付。因此，19 世纪 20 年代初，詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德曾设想为西班牙提供这样一笔贷款，以此将这个货币不稳定的长期债务国融入国际资本市场。 <sup>37</sup> 该项目虽未实现，但在比利时债券上却取得了类似成果——该债券可在伦敦用英镑、或在巴黎或布鲁塞尔用法郎按固定汇率支付。这样一来，公司降低了不同金融中心投资者的不确定性和交易成本，并扩大了比利时证券的市场。</span></span></span>

The Rothschilds drew upon other advantages to dominate unquestionably the underwriting of loans by European states to about 1835. They successfully cultivated top officials and maintained direct access to the top political leadership of its clients, as Corti has shown in the case of Metternich, for example. Second, the firm's capital resources for underwriting were large and also very flexible. Keeping its assets liquid and accepting few deposits in order to avoid paying interest on them, the firm could move quickly and take sizeable risks as it negotiated aggressively to win a large loan at a fixed price. But, generally, it did so as the head of a syndicate which included many lesser banking houses. This allowed the firm to assemble quickly the sums needed, while cleverly sidestepping the dangers of rigid structures and long-term commitments. The syndicate system also spread risks, facilitated rapid placement of the loan, and forestalled competition, particularly from local bankers who could be enlisted in a united front in the negotiations with their governments. Third, the firm had a keen sense of timing, often succeeding with syndicate co-partners in 'bulling' up a new loan to a substantial premium and enhancing the already-hefty underwriting commission. Finally, the firm was adept at using its large resources to intimidate potential competitors and to keep them in line in the ad hoc syndicates. A powerful bear-market raid on a newly contracted loan could leave a rival underwriter with large losses, for example.<sup>38</sup> Thus did the House of Rothschild create and defend its 'franchise' in the multinational <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_133"></span>underwriting of large loans for the leading European states in the period from 1815 to about 1835. In doing so, it developed asset ownership advantages associated with size and monopoly power to complement its initial transactions advantages.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">罗斯柴尔德家族借助其他优势，在约 1835 年之前无可争议地主导了欧洲各国贷款的承销业务。他们成功拉拢高层官员，并与客户国的最高政治领导层保持直接联系——例如科蒂就曾以梅特涅为例证明这一点。其次，该家族用于承销的资本实力雄厚且极为灵活。通过保持资产流动性、几乎不接受存款以避免支付利息，该家族能够迅速行动并承担可观风险，以激进谈判策略争取以固定价格赢得大额贷款。但通常情况下，它是以银团牵头行的身份运作，该银团包含许多规模较小的银行。这样既能让家族快速筹集所需资金，又能巧妙规避僵化结构和长期承诺带来的风险。 辛迪加体系不仅分散了风险，促进了贷款的快速投放，还遏制了竞争，尤其是那些可能被拉拢进联合阵线的地方银行家，以便与各国政府谈判。第三，这家事务所对时机把握敏锐，常常与辛迪加合作伙伴成功地将新贷款“哄抬”至大幅溢价，从而提升本已丰厚的承销佣金。最后，该公司善于利用其雄厚实力威慑潜在竞争者，并在临时辛迪加中令其服从。例如，对新签贷款发起一场猛烈的空头袭击，可能使竞争对手的承销商蒙受巨额损失。 <sup>38</sup> 正是这样，罗斯柴尔德家族在 1815 年至 1835 年间，为欧洲主要国家的大型跨国贷款承销中创造并捍卫了自己的“特许经营权”。 通过这种方式，它积累了与规模和垄断力相关的资产所有权优势，从而补充了其最初的交易优势。</span></span></span>

Adaptation and innovation, 1835—75<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">适应与创新，1835—1875 年</span></span></span>
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The emergence of the House of Rothschild as a pioneering and pre-eminently important multinational suggests that theories of ownership advantages can be usefully extended to financial firms, at least in some cases. In order to test further this approach, I shall conclude with a brief two-part examination of the firm's activities until the end of the century.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">罗斯柴尔德家族作为一家开创性且极其重要的跨国企业的崛起表明，所有权优势理论至少在某些情况下可以有效地延伸至金融公司。为进一步验证这一思路，我将以对该公司在世纪末之前活动的简要两部分考察作为结语。</span></span></span>

The Rothschilds remained remarkably successful for at least fifty years after their rapid rise. The firm's total capital, which exceeded £4,000,000 (100,000,000 francs) in the late 1820s, reached £22,313,864 (557,821,600 francs) in 1863, a figure well above all other banks at that time. By 1874, the firm's total capital had increased substantially once again, to £35,520,496 (888,012,400 francs), and in 1899 it reached £41,500,000 (1,037,500,000 francs).<sup>39</sup> Moreover, the firm remained a complex international partnership, although the nature of relations between the branches is often obscure. Each branch had a share in the international partnership and a single unified balance sheet was struck throughout the century, often in conjunction with the death of a principal. Following the dissolution of the Naples branch in 1863, the ownership of the firm's capital was still distributed equally between the surviving branches — Paris, London, Frankfurt and Vienna.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">罗斯柴尔德家族在迅速崛起后至少五十年里依然保持着惊人的成功。19 世纪 20 年代末，该公司的总资本超过 400 万英镑（1 亿法郎），到 1863 年达到 22,313,864 英镑（557,821,600 法郎），这一数字远超当时所有其他银行。到 1874 年，公司总资本再次大幅增长，达到 35,520,496 英镑（888,012,400 法郎），而在 1899 年则达到 41,500,000 英镑（1,037,500,000 法郎）。 <sup>39</sup> 此外，该公司始终维持着一个复杂的国际合伙制，尽管各分支之间的关系性质往往难以捉摸。每个分支都持有国际合伙制的股份，并且在整个 19 世纪中，该公司都会编制一份统一的资产负债表，通常是在某位主要合伙人去世时进行。1863 年那不勒斯分支解散后，公司资本的所有权仍由剩余的巴黎、伦敦、法兰克福和维也纳分支平均分配。</span></span></span>

Nevertheless, the different branches increasingly took on a separate 'national' identity from the 1830s onward. After the death of Nathan Rothschild in 1836, the English house apparently operated with a large measure of independence. In continental Europe the Paris branch clearly became paramount, as James de Rothschild led the group until his death in 1868, when his son Alphonse, seconded by brothers Gustave and Edmond, assumed that leadership role. French leadership was reflected in a concentration of the firm's capital assets in Paris. The partnership agreements of both 1874 and 1899 showed roughly 60 per cent of total Rothschild capital at the disposition of the Paris branch, while only 40 per cent of assets was allotted for the business of the London, Frankfurt and Vienna branches. Such a concentration of assets (but not of ownership) probably occurred much earlier. The rest of this chapter will give special attention to the international activities <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_134"></span>of the Paris Rothschilds, the central figures in the firm's continental operations.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管如此，从 19 世纪 30 年代起，罗斯柴尔德家族的各个分支逐渐形成了独立的“民族”身份。1836 年内森·罗斯柴尔德去世后，英国分行显然开始以相当大的自主权运营。在欧洲大陆，巴黎分行无疑成为主导，因为詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德领导该集团直至 1868 年去世，之后其子阿方斯在弟弟古斯塔夫和埃德蒙的辅佐下接掌领导权。法国的主导地位还体现在公司资本资产集中于巴黎。1874 年和 1899 年的合伙协议均显示，罗斯柴尔德家族总资本的约 60%由巴黎分行支配，而只有 40%的资产分配给了伦敦、法兰克福和维也纳分行。这种资产（而非所有权）的集中很可能在更早时期就已出现。 本章其余部分将特别关注巴黎 Rothschild 家族的国际活动，他们是该公司在欧洲大陆业务的核心人物。</span></span></span>

In considering the entire partnership after about 1835, one may begin with the activities providing the initial ownership advantages in international transfers and in underwriting state loans. Unfortunately, little evidence has survived regarding the former. Nevertheless, one has a clear impression that international transfers must have provided a sharply-declining percentage of income after railroads and telegraphs progressively eliminated the bank's communication advantage and reduced its internalization advantage in exchange transactions. True, the Paris branch did use trusted employees to establish its own sub-branches — Brussels (1830), Madrid (1833-4), New York (1835-6), New Orleans (.1843), Havana (1843), Mexico (1843), Rome (1843), Turin (1850) and Trieste (around 1870) — and to supplement its circle of correspondents. Yet such branches facilitated primarily the firm's expanding role in the international trade of certain special or high-value commodities, such as American cotton or Mexican silver, and financial transfers were apparently rather incidental.<sup>40</sup> Within Europe, the voluminous published correspondence of the Brussels agent shows no interest in the transfer business, focusing almost exclusively on underwriting Belgian state loans and (occasionally) industrial enterprises.<sup>41</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">回顾 1835 年后的整个合作历程，不妨从那些最初为国际汇款和政府贷款承销提供所有权优势的活动说起。遗憾的是，关于前者的历史证据留存甚少。不过可以清晰感受到，在铁路与电报逐步消除银行的信息优势、削弱其在汇兑交易中的内部化优势后，国际汇款业务在收入中所占比重必然出现了断崖式下滑。诚然，巴黎分行确实派遣亲信员工建立了自己的子行——布鲁塞尔（1830 年）、马德里（1833-34 年）、纽约（1835-36 年）、新奥尔良（1843 年）、哈瓦那（1843 年）、墨西哥城（1843 年）、罗马（1843 年）、都灵（1850 年）和的里雅斯特（约 1870 年）——以此补充其代理行网络。 然而，这些分支机构主要服务于公司在某些特殊或高价值商品（如美国棉花和墨西哥白银）国际贸易中日益扩大的角色，而资金转移业务在当时显然只是附带功能。 <sup>40</sup> 在欧洲，布鲁塞尔代理人所公开的大量信函显示，他们对转移业务毫无兴趣，几乎全神贯注于承销比利时政府贷款，偶尔也会涉及工业企业。 <sup>41</sup> </span></span></span>

The bright spot in the declining transfer business was the trade in gold and precious metals, which may well have stimulated the firm's expansion into the trade in international commodities from the 1830s at the latest. The House as a whole long remained a major bullion dealer, and one finds large shipments of gold and silver between Paris and London throughout the 1870s, though much less thereafter.<sup>42</sup> Similarly, the Paris branch continued to oversee the minting of money from precious metals in Paris for several east European and Latin American states until the end of the century, long after such operations had been assumed by governments in western Europe.<sup>43</sup> Such coinage operations were, it seems, usually connected to some kind of state loan.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在日渐衰落的汇款业务中，黄金和贵金属交易成为一抹亮色，这很可能促使该公司最迟从 19 世纪 30 年代起就涉足国际大宗商品贸易。罗斯柴尔德家族整体上长期保持主要黄金交易商的地位，整个 19 世纪 70 年代，巴黎和伦敦之间仍有大量金银输送，尽管此后规模大幅缩减。 <sup>42</sup> 同样，巴黎分行继续负责为几个东欧和拉丁美洲国家在巴黎铸造贵金属货币，这一业务一直持续到世纪末，而西欧各国政府早已接手了此类操作。 <sup>43</sup> 这类铸币业务通常与某种国家贷款相关联。</span></span></span>

Certainly there can be no doubt that underwriting state loans remained the core business for the Parisian leadership in the period to 1875. This business remained international almost by definition, with the multinational partnership and its correspondents continuing to join together in ad hoc syndicates to underwrite and distribute simultaneously a given issue on different national markets. At the risk of oversimplification, one may say that the Rothschilds lost the quasi-monopoly they enjoyed in the 1830s, but that they nevertheless remained the leading group in <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_135"></span>international underwriting until at least the early 1870s, Among many aspects of this evolution, one notes that European states were generally in a better bargaining position after 1835 than after 1815. Improved financial conditions for these states reduced their need for new loans, facilitated direct public subscriptions, and encouraged serious bids from rival combinations. Between 1832 and 1839, France did not float a single state loan and Britain issued only one. The supply of loans coming from other European governments was also quite reduced from 1832 to 1847.<sup>44</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">毫无疑问，在 1875 年之前的这段时期，承销国家贷款仍然是巴黎金融领导层的核心业务。这项业务几乎从定义上就是国际性的，跨国合伙制企业及其联络人继续临时组成辛迪加，同时在不同的国家市场上承销和分销某期债券。虽然可能有些过于简化，但可以说罗斯柴尔德家族失去了他们在 19 世纪 30 年代享有的近乎垄断的地位，然而他们至少到 19 世纪 70 年代初仍是国际承销领域的领先集团。在这一演变的众多方面中，可以注意到 1835 年后的欧洲国家在谈判中的处境普遍比 1815 年后更有利。这些国家财政状况的改善减少了它们对新贷款的需求，促进了直接公开发售，并鼓励了来自竞争对手联合体的认真出价。 1832 年至 1839 年间，法国没有发行任何国家贷款，英国也只发行了一笔。从 1832 年到 1847 年，其他欧洲政府提供的贷款规模也大幅缩减。 <sup>44</sup> </span></span></span>

After 1850, joint-stock banks like the Credit Mobilier appeared to strengthen existing competition from private houses. The successes of such rivals, like the audacious coup of Hambros with Sardinia in 1851 (and again with Italy in 1881), despite energetic counterattacks from the Paris Rothschilds, chipped away at the House's pre-eminence.<sup>45</sup> Nevertheless, the firm remained at the head of the pack under the leadership of James de Rothschild. As a director of a rising joint-stock bank, the Credit Lyonnais, put it in 1867, whatever one might say, Rothschild was still 'the greatest and most important financial power'.<sup>46</sup> Similarly, Gille concludes that circa 1870 the international position of the 'Rothschild group' was 'incontestably superior to that of all other financial establishments, both old and new'.<sup>47</sup> In short, the ownership advantages of the 1830s — superior capital, multinational integration and organizational flexibility — remained intact.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1850 年后，像 Credit Mobilier 这样的股份制银行出现，加剧了与私人银行之间的竞争。尽管巴黎罗斯柴尔德家族奋力反击，但诸如汉布罗斯银行（Hambros）在 1851 年对撒丁岛的大胆突袭（以及 1881 年对意大利的再次行动）等对手的成功，逐步削弱了罗斯柴尔德家族的优势地位。 <sup>45</sup> 即便如此，在詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德（James de Rothschild）的领导下，该公司依然稳居行业前列。正如 1867 年一家新兴股份制银行——Credit Lyonnais 的一位董事所言：“无论人们怎么说，罗斯柴尔德仍然是最伟大、最重要的金融力量。” <sup>46</sup> 同样，Gille 总结道，到 1870 年左右，“罗斯柴尔德集团”的国际地位“毋庸置疑地超越了所有其他新旧金融机构”。 <sup>47</sup> 简而言之，19 世纪 30 年代的那些所有权优势——雄厚的资本、跨国整合以及组织灵活性——依然完好无损。</span></span></span>

Successfully defending its core business, the Pans branch also continued to innovate between 1835 and 1875. Above all, it cautiously embraced the adventure of nineteenth-century industrialization. In doing so, Paris diverged markedly from London, which avoided investment in railroads and industrial enterprise. The difference in strategy may help to explain the growing distance between the two branches.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">成功捍卫核心业务后，巴黎分行在 1835 年至 1875 年间持续创新。尤为重要的是，它审慎地拥抱了十九世纪工业化的冒险之旅。这一做法使巴黎与伦敦形成了鲜明对比——后者对铁路和工业企业的投资避而远之。两地战略上的差异，或许能解释为何两家分行之间的差距日益扩大。</span></span></span>

The meaning of Paris's expanding sphere of action from the viewpoint of multinational activities is somewhat ambiguous however. Certainly the declining supply of European state loans between 1832 and 1847 encouraged James de Rothschild to seek new sources of underwriting profits and to extend the techniques developed for states to private clients. The great difference was the temptation — or, more correctly, the necessity — to make quasi-permanent investments and loans to industrial firms, and thus become immobilized to at least some extent. The Paris branch of the House of Rothschild dealt with this problem in a two-step evolution.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，从跨国活动的角度来看，巴黎业务范围的扩张意义有些模糊不清。1832 年至 1847 年间欧洲国家贷款供应减少，这无疑促使詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德寻找新的承销利润来源，并将为国家开发的技术拓展至私人客户。最大的区别在于——更准确地说，是必要性——需要对工业企业进行准永久性投资和贷款，从而至少在某种程度上变得资金固化。罗斯柴尔德家族巴黎分行通过两步演变来处理这一问题。</span></span></span>

In the period from the late 1830s to 1848, James de Rothschild participated actively in railroad building and associated industrial <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_136"></span>enterprises, as underwriter, banker and large shareholder. This investment was grouped around two poles of attraction in southern and in northern France.<sup>48</sup> Subsequently, most of these railroad and related industrial participations were liquidated in the brutal crisis of 1848, so that only the important Northern Railroad Company was effectively developed under Rothschild control. In a sense, the turn towards railroads and industry before 1848 reflected the 'nationalization' of the Paris branch, for these investments were limited to France and neighbouring Belgium.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在 1830 年代末至 1848 年间，詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德作为承销商、银行家和大股东，积极参与铁路建设及相关的工业企业。这些投资主要集中在法国南部和北部两大吸引中心。 <sup>48</sup> 随后，在 1848 年的残酷危机中，这些铁路和相关的工业投资大多被清算，只有重要的北方铁路公司在罗斯柴尔德的控制下得到有效发展。从某种意义上说，1848 年前转向铁路和工业反映了巴黎分行“国有化”的趋势，因为这些投资仅限于法国及其邻国比利时。</span></span></span>

In the second period, in the 1850s and 1860s, James de Rothschild extended the scope of the firm's railroad interests to Austria (including northern Italy) and Spain, thereby giving it a very international dimension. However, this investment in state-supported, quasi-public railroads is probably best seen as part of the struggle with the Pereires and their Crédit Mobilier for primacy in public finance — the Rothschilds' core business.<sup>49</sup> This interpretation is also supported by the firm's unwillingness to underwrite or support iron, coal and railway equipment companies in this period, as it had done before 1848. In short, one may see the firm's multinational participation in railroads (and associated industrial firms) as a trial-and-error extension of its ownership advantages in negotiating and underwriting state loans to the closely-related business of raising capital for quasi-public monopolies.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在第二个阶段，即 19 世纪 50 至 60 年代，詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德将公司的铁路业务版图拓展至奥地利（包括意大利北部）和西班牙，从而使其呈现出高度的国际化特征。不过，这些投资于国家支持、半公共性质的铁路项目，很可能应被视作罗斯柴尔德家族与佩雷尔兄弟及其动产信贷银行争夺公共金融主导权（罗斯柴尔德的核心业务）的一部分。 <sup>49</sup> 这一解读也得到了佐证：该时期公司不再像 1848 年之前那样，为钢铁、煤炭及铁路设备企业提供承销或支持。简言之，我们可以将公司在铁路（及关联工业企业）上的跨国参与，视为其通过试错方式，将自身在政府贷款谈判与承销方面的所有权优势，延伸至为半公共垄断企业筹集资金这一密切相关的业务领域。</span></span></span>

Decline and reorientation, 1875—1900<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">衰落与转向，1875—1900</span></span></span>
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The evolution of the multinational activities of the Pans Rothschilds in the late nineteenth century may also be framed in terms of ownership advantages.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">十九世纪末期 Pans Rothschilds 的跨国活动演变也可以从所有权优势的角度来理解。</span></span></span>

On the one hand, the limited decline in government finance, which the firm had experienced before 1870, accelerated. The great joint-stock banks, with thick networks of branch outlets to drain savings and to sell securities, proved a much more serious threat than the Crédit Mobilier. Increasingly, as in the case of Russian loans and conversions at the end of the 1880s and in the 1890s,<sup>50</sup> Alphonse de Rothschild might still negotiate and lead a loan in the name of the multinational partnership for a large French (or international) banking syndicate. But his house could not do without the distribution networks of the large commercial banks in placing the paper, although those banks could increasing forgo Rothschild negotiating skills as they competed for loan contracts. Underwriting for state-affiliated railroads also declined, as this industry matured and Italian (but not Spanish) lines were <span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_137"></span>nationalized. Now playing a limited role in international transfers and refusing to modify or expand its organizational structure, the Paris branch faced a serious challenge.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">一方面，该公司在 1870 年前经历的那种政府金融业务有限下滑的趋势正在加速。那些拥有密集分支网络以吸收储蓄和销售证券的大型股份制银行，比 Crédit Mobilier 构成了更为严重的威胁。如同 19 世纪 80 年代末及 90 年代俄国贷款和债务转换的案例所示，阿尔方斯·德·罗斯柴尔德（Alphonse de Rothschild）或许仍能代表这个跨国合伙集团，以一家大型法国（或国际）银行辛迪加的名义谈判并牵头贷款。但他的家族公司在发行债券时已离不开大型商业银行的分销网络，尽管那些银行在竞逐贷款合同时，越来越可以抛开罗斯柴尔德的谈判技巧。随着该行业趋于成熟，以及意大利（而非西班牙）铁路线被国有化，为国家附属铁路承销的业务也在减少。 如今，巴黎支行在国际转账中仅扮演着有限的角色，且拒绝修改或扩大其组织结构，这使其面临严峻的挑战。</span></span></span>

The response in Paris, or at least the more creative part of that response, was to build upon another set of ownership advantages, advantages which it had developed in specialized international commerce and which were also in danger of wasting away. The House had been interested in trade in its early years, and by the late 1830s the Paris branch was active not only in precious metals but other items as well. According to Gille, the underlying strategy was to focus on commodities which had few buyers and sellers and which were believed to offer the best opportunities for monopolistic manipulation.<sup>51</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">巴黎方面的应对，至少其中较具创意的一部分，是建立在另一套所有权优势之上的——这些优势是它在专业化国际贸易领域发展起来的，而当时也正面临被荒废的风险。该商行早年便对贸易感兴趣，到了 19 世纪 30 年代末，巴黎支行的业务已不仅限于贵金属，还涉足其他商品。根据吉尔的说法，其基本策略是聚焦于买卖双方数量都较少的商品，据信这些商品能为垄断操纵提供最佳机会。 <sup>51</sup> </span></span></span>

Most notably, the Paris branch dominated the French market for copper as representative of the Demidovs of Russia until 1838, and the Rothschilds effectively monopolized the world market for mercury as the exclusive sales agent for the Spanish government's mines in Almadén. By the late 1860s, lead from Spain and petroleum from the United States were also particularly important items of commerce.<sup>52</sup> The Paris branch also owned and operated two refineries in France as part of its interest in metals.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">最为突出的是，巴黎分行作为俄罗斯杰米多夫家族的代表，在 1838 年之前一直主导着法国的铜市场；而罗斯柴尔德家族作为西班牙政府阿尔马登矿场的独家销售代理，实际上垄断了全球水银市场。到 19 世纪 60 年代末，来自西班牙的铅和美国的石油也成为特别重要的贸易商品。 <sup>52</sup> 此外，巴黎分行还在法国拥有并经营着两家炼油厂，作为其金属业务的一部分。</span></span></span>

As I have shown elsewhere, the Pans Rothschilds took a renewed interest in international commerce in the 1870s. Then in a burst of activity in the 1880s, the House participated in the formation of several large multinational firms which gained a strong position in the processing and marketing of lead, nickel, petroleum and copper. In doing so, it brought not only its capital resources as both long-term investor and banker, but also its expertise in the shipment and sale of commodities sold in oligopolistic markets.<sup>53</sup> These ventures were eventually quite successful. Less so were similar efforts to carve out both a French and multinational stake in the electrical industry. Nevertheless, on balance, the Paris Rothschilds caught and rode the restructuring wave in international commerce, which was associated with the rise of multinational oligopolistic firms in trade and industry.<sup>54</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正如我在别处所展示的，帕里-罗斯柴尔德家族在 19 世纪 70 年代重新燃起了对国际商业的兴趣。随后在 80 年代，该家族掀起一波活动热潮，参与组建了多家大型跨国公司，这些公司在铅、镍、石油和铜的加工与销售领域占据了强势地位。为此，它不仅投入了作为长期投资者和银行家的资本资源，还贡献了在寡头垄断市场中运输和销售大宗商品的专长。这些冒险最终相当成功。相比之下，在电气行业同时建立法国本土和跨国利益的类似努力则不那么尽如人意。不过总体而言，帕里-罗斯柴尔德家族抓住了国际贸易的重组浪潮并顺势而为，这一浪潮与贸易和工业领域寡头垄断跨国公司的崛起息息相关。</span></span></span>

In doing so, the firm remained resolutely multinational, but it gradually reorientated its focus from underwriting European state loans to overseeing interrelated industrial corporations. The bank became less and less of a bank.This evolution was summed up in 1899 by an ageing Alphonse de Rothschild. Pressed to participate in a variety of Russian ventures, the head of the firm replied, 'that so much as he was disposed to invest directly in industry, so much did he \[also\] wish to avoid everything that had any resemblance to the business of money and banking'.<sup>55</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如此一来，该公司依然坚定不移地走跨国路线，但逐渐将重心从承销欧洲国家贷款转向监管相互关联的工业企业。这家银行越来越不像银行了。1899 年，年迈的阿方斯·德·罗斯柴尔德概括了这一演变。当被催促参与各种俄罗斯项目时，这位公司负责人回应道：“尽管他倾向于直接投资工业，但同样也希望避免任何与货币和银行业务相似的事务。”</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch07.xhtml_page_138"></span>Conclusion<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">结论</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

To summarize, this chapter has argued that the Rothschilds of Frankfurt developed a transaction advantage over their competition in certain high-risk international transfers by establishing branches in London and then in Paris during the Napoleonic wars. Branches enabled the multinational family partnership to internalize the high transaction costs of very imperfect exchange (and then debt) markets. Thus the integrated multinational partnership operated more efficiently and built up a capital far in excess of competing firms in the 1820s. In doing so, the firm created another powerful set of ownership advantages, which were due largely to size, established position and monopoly power.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">总之，本章论证了法兰克福的罗斯柴尔德家族在拿破仑战争期间，通过在伦敦设立分行，随后又在巴黎设立分支，从而在某些高风险的国际转账业务中获得了相对于竞争对手的交易优势。这些分行使这个跨国家族合伙企业能够将高度不完善的交易市场（以及后来的债务市场）中高昂的交易成本内部化。因此，这个整合的跨国合伙企业运营效率更高，并在 19 世纪 20 年代积累了远超竞争对手的资本。在此过程中，该公司又创造了一套强大的所有权优势，这些优势主要源于其规模、既有的市场地位和垄断力量。</span></span></span>

Subsequently, the Paris branch — and really only the Paris branch — expanded into railroad (and some industrial) underwriting under James de Rothschild after about 1835. But under his guidance the firm used its size and position primarily to defend its role in international government finance. It did so with considerable success until the early 1870s, long after it had lost its internalization advantage in more perfect exchange markets. Finally, after about 1875, as joint-stock banking along nationalistic lines hastened the firm's decline in state-loan underwriting, the Paris branch innovated again. It revitalized and restructured longstanding ownership advantages in the international trade of metals and petroleum, successfully placing them within the rapidly-emerging framework of the oligopolistic multinational industrial corporation.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">随后，巴黎分行——实际上也只有巴黎分行——在 1835 年左右开始拓展铁路（及部分工业）承销业务，这是在詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德的主导下进行的。但在他的领导下，该公司主要利用其规模和地位来捍卫其在国际政府融资中的角色。这一策略相当成功，一直持续到 19 世纪 70 年代初，尽管早在更完善的汇率市场中，它已经丧失了内部化优势。最终，在 1875 年后，随着民族主义倾向的股份制银行加速了该公司在国家贷款承销业务中的衰退，巴黎分行再次创新。它重新激活并重组了在金属和石油国际贸易中长期积累的所有权优势，并成功地将这些优势融入到迅速崛起的寡头垄断跨国工业公司框架之中。</span></span></span>

The dynamic paradigm developed to analyse multinational industrial enterprise, which centres on the creation, preservation and attenuation of firm-specific ownership advantages, provides new insights into the history of modern Europe's first great multinational bank — the House of Rothschild.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">用于分析跨国工业企业、并聚焦于企业特定所有权优势的创造、维持与衰减的动态范式，为我们重新审视现代欧洲第一家伟大的跨国银行——罗斯柴尔德家族（House of Rothschild）的历史提供了全新视角。</span></span></span>
