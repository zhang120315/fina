<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">克里斯托弗·克莱</span></span></span>

The Imperial Ottoman Bank/Banque Itnp&riale Otto mane (BIO) opened for business in Constantinople in June 1863 as the state bank of the Ottoman Empire.<sup>1</sup> The idea of a state or 'national' bank had been in circulation on the shores of the Bosphorus for some time, but it was finally converted into reality by the great reforming statesman Fuad Pasha as one of a series of measures intended to try to rescue his country from the morass of government insolvency and monetary disorder into which it had fallen by the end of 1861. However, because he wanted to be able to draw upon the resources of both the London and the Paris money markets, and so as to avoid the political commitment that choosing between the rival groups of foreign financiers would involve, Fuad made it clear that the English and French bankers competing for the concession must combine forces.<sup>2</sup> The two principal contenders, the Ottoman Bank, a non-privileged English joint-stock bank which had been conducting ordinary commercial banking in Constantinople and a few other places since 1856, and a French group headed by the Credit Mobilier, thus had little choice but to enter into negotiations with each other. By mid-November 1862 they had reached agreement and by the end of January successfully struck a deal with the Porte.<sup>3</sup> But the joint Anglo-French concern which emerged was not what either the English or the French founders had wanted, and it had not been sought by the bankers involved for its own sake. From their point of view it was a second best (because it involved sharing spoils they had hoped to enjoy on their own), which had been forced on them by the Ottoman ministers.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">帝国奥斯曼银行（Imperial Ottoman Bank / Banque Impériale Ottomane，简称 BIO）于 1863 年 6 月在君士坦丁堡开业，作为奥斯曼帝国的国家银行。 <sup>1</sup> 关于设立一家国家或“国民”银行的想法，已在博斯普鲁斯海峡两岸流传了一段时间，但最终由伟大的改革家福阿德帕夏将其变为现实，这是他推出的一系列措施之一，旨在将国家从截至 1861 年底陷入的政府破产和货币混乱的泥潭中拯救出来。然而，由于他希望能够利用伦敦和巴黎两大金融市场的资源，同时避免在选择外国金融家竞争集团时涉及的政治承诺，福阿德明确表示，竞争特许经营权的英国和法国银行家必须联手合作。 <sup>2</sup> 两大主要竞争者——奥斯曼银行（一家非特许经营的英国合股银行，自 1856 年起在君士坦丁堡及少数其他地区开展常规商业银行业务）和以动产信贷公司为首的法国集团——几乎别无选择，只能相互展开谈判。到 1862 年 11 月中旬，它们达成了协议；至 1 月底，又成功与奥斯曼帝国政府谈成了交易。 <sup>3</sup> 然而，最终诞生的英法联合企业既非英国创始人也非法方创始人所愿，更不是参与其中的银行家们主动谋求的结果。在他们看来，这只能算是个次优选择（因为这意味着要瓜分原本希望独吞的果实），是奥斯曼大臣们强加给他们的。</span></span></span>

The BIO was financed by English and French capital and controlled by a committee, in effect a board of directors, half of whose members were English and half French. Nevertheless, for the first few years it was in many ways simply the old Ottoman Bank writ large, for it had taken over the premises, staff and <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_143"></span>business of the latter in Constantinople lock, stock and barrel, and simply grafted on to it the additional functions of acting as the government's banker. The Director General of the new concern inevitably had to be French so as to import at least some degree of French influence into the Bank's presence in the Levant, and the post had been given to a senior Finance Ministry official, the marquis de Ploeuc, who had been acting as an adviser to the Ottoman government since 1859. But of the fourteen senior members of the managerial staff in the summer of 1863 only one other was French, whilst ten were English and two Armenian. In the four provincial branches - Smyrna in Western Anatolia, Beirut on the Syrian coast, Galatz (Galati) and Bucharest in the Danubian Principalities — all the responsible staff were English.<sup>4</sup> And in western Europe, too, the English aspects of the Bank at first completely overshadowed the French. The London office carried on active business in its own right. As the then chairman of the English half of the committee, A.H. Layard, put it in 1867, 'l'agence de Londres a le caractére et le crédit d'un établissement indépendant disposant de grandes ressources ... (elle) a en outre des relations intimes avec des maisons et des institutions importantes á Londres', with the result that its affairs had assumed 'une grande importance dans le compte de profits et pertes de la Banque'. In 1867 itself, for instance, London had contributed LT 98,686 out of total profits of LT 281,846 (LT 1 = £0.909). Paris, by contrast, according to the accounts, had contributed nothing since the BIO did not yet have a Paris agency.<sup>5</sup> Although the Bank was organizationally quite independent of the Credit Mobilier, it operated from offices inside the headquarters of the latter at 15 place Vendome and used the Credit Mobilier as its financial agent for most, if not all, purposes.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">BIO 的资本来自英法两国，由委员会（实际为董事会）管理，成员半数英人半数法人。然而在最初几年，这家银行在许多方面不过是旧奥斯曼银行的翻版——它全盘接管了后者在君士坦丁堡的房产、员工及业务，并在此基础上新增了代理国库的职能。新机构的总干事一职自然须由法国人担任，以便为该行在黎凡特地区的运营注入至少些许法国影响力。此职最终授予了法国财政部高级官员、自 1859 年起担任奥斯曼政府顾问的普洛厄克侯爵。但在 1863 年夏季的十四名高级管理人员中，仅有一名法国人，其余十人为英国人、两人为亚美尼亚人。 在西方安纳托利亚的士麦那、叙利亚海岸的贝鲁特、多瑙河两公国的加拉茨和布加勒斯特这四个省级分行中，所有负责人都是英国人。 <sup>4</sup> 即便在西欧，该银行最初也以英国色彩为主，完全盖过了法国方面。伦敦办事处自主开展着活跃的业务。正如英国一方委员会时任主席 A.H.莱亚德在 1867 年所言：“伦敦代理处具有独立机构的性质与信誉，掌握着雄厚资源……此外还与伦敦各大商行及重要机构关系密切。”其结果就是，其业务“在该银行的损益账目中占据了极大比重”。以 1867 年为例，伦敦方面贡献了 98,686 里拉，而银行总利润为 281,846 里拉（1 里拉=0.909 英镑）。 与之相反，据记录显示，巴黎方面至今未有任何贡献，因为 BIO 当时尚未在巴黎设立机构。 <sup>5</sup> 尽管该银行在组织上相对独立于 Credit Mobilier，但其办公地点设在后者位于旺多姆广场 15 号的总部内，且绝大多数（即便不是全部）金融业务均通过 Credit Mobilier 作为其财务代理人进行操作。</span></span></span>

If operating branches in more than one country qualifies a bank to be regarded as a multinational, then clearly with branches in both London and the Ottoman Empire, the BIO was multinational from the first even without an independent Paris establishment. Yet it was some time before its credentials to be so regarded were more than minimal. Certainly all the new branches opened in the earliest years — fully-fledged agencies in the great port city of Salonica and at Larnaca in Cyprus (both 1864), and a number of sub-agencies at small towns in the cotton-growing districts inland from Smyrna — were firmly within Ottoman territory. On the other hand, the two branches in the Principalities, now united and only very nominally under the Sultan's suzerainty (although not fully independent until 1878), were sold in 1866 to the newly-founded Bank of Roumania. However, 1867 saw the opening of an agency <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_144"></span>in Egypt, at Alexandria. Egypt was also part of the Empire, but like Moldavia and Wallachia only very nominally. Certainly the basis upon which the BIO could expect to operate in Egypt was quite different from that on which it operated in the Ottoman lands proper. Its monopoly of note issue did not extend there, nor was it banker to the Egyptian government with all the advantages of being entrusted with the transmission of tax revenues and the rest of it. Rather the BIO opened at Alexandria as just one more foreign bank competing for ordinary banking business in what the rapid growth of Egypt's export economy since the 1820s, and especially the cotton boom of 1861-5, had made into the most dynamic commercial centre in the Levant.<sup>6</sup> It had a massive export-import trade with both France, especially Marseille, and England, larger than that of any Ottoman port. It also had extensive financial dealings with Constantinople because of the many social and family connections between the Egyptian and Ottoman elites. The opening of a branch at Alexandria was thus an obvious move for the BIO: good profits could be expected from strictly local operations, and it represented the final link in the chain of agencies which now stretched right round the eastern Mediterranean and gave the Bank a branch in all of its principal ports —Salonica, Constantinople, Smyrna, Beirut, Larnaca and Alex-andria.<sup>7</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如果一家银行在多个国家设有分行即可被视为跨国银行，那么帝国奥斯曼银行（BIO）即便最初没有独立的巴黎机构，但凭借伦敦和奥斯曼帝国的分行网络，从一开始就具备了跨国性质。不过，真正被视为合格的跨国银行还需时日。早期开设的分行——例如在重要港口城市萨洛尼卡和塞浦路斯拉纳卡设立的全功能代理处（均于 1864 年开业），以及士麦那内陆棉花产区小镇的多个附属代理处——都严格控制在奥斯曼帝国领土范围内。另一方面，当时已实现统一却仅名义上效忠苏丹（直至 1878 年才完全独立）的两个公国分行，于 1866 年被出售给新成立的罗马尼亚银行。然而到了 1867 年，帝国奥斯曼银行（BIO）在埃及亚历山大港设立了一处代理机构。 埃及也曾是帝国的一部分，但如同摩尔达维亚和瓦拉几亚一样，这只是名义上的。BIO 在埃及开展业务的基础，与其在奥斯曼本土的运作方式截然不同。它不享有在此发行纸币的垄断权，也未能成为埃及政府的银行，无法获得代理税收汇缴等业务带来的种种便利。相反，BIO 在亚历山大港开业时，不过是众多在黎凡特最活跃商业中心争夺普通银行业务的外资银行之一——自 19 世纪 20 年代以来，尤其是 1861-65 年棉花繁荣期，埃及出口经济的迅猛发展已使该地成为这一中心。 <sup>6</sup> 埃及与法国（尤其是马赛）及英国保持着庞大的进出口贸易，规模超过任何奥斯曼港口。同时，由于埃及与奥斯曼精英阶层之间广泛的社会和家族联系，它与君士坦丁堡之间也存在着大量的金融往来。 在亚历山大开设分行对 BIO 来说是一步显而易见的举措：纯本地业务即可带来可观的利润，同时它也是覆盖整个东地中海沿岸代理网络中的最后一环，使得该银行在其所有主要港口——萨洛尼卡、君士坦丁堡、士麦那、贝鲁特、拉纳卡和亚历山大——都拥有了分行。 <sup>7</sup> </span></span></span>

The next year (1868) saw another step in the evolution of the BIO as a multinational but, although it was one which would doubtless have come sooner or later, in the event it was forced on the Bank and so can hardly be regarded as part of a deliberate strategy. The step in question was the opening of a separate Paris agency. Initially the close involvement of the BIO with the Crédit Mobilier was a source of strength, for despite the controversial nature of this great joint-stock bank and its mode of operations, it had enjoyed a great measure of success. By 1867, however, the Crédit Mobilier was in extreme difficulties, and the connection was gradually transformed into an embarrassment, and indeed a serious liability. Exactly what arrangement was arrived at is obscure, but at some time in the early part of 1868 the link was broken. A full-scale Paris agency was then organized to carry on the business previously handled by the Credit Mobilier on the BIO's behalf, and with its newly-independent position the BIO progressively built up the same sort of role in France that it had come to play in England. That is to say that, in addition to handling various forms of business arising out of the financing of Franco-Ottoman trade and making payments on Ottoman securities held in France, it also participated in financial operations with a circle <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_145"></span>of money-market 'friends', which on occasion involved it in contributing to the capital needs of governments or transport undertakings in areas far away from the main focus of its operations in the Ottoman Empire and Egypt.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">次年（1868 年），BIO 作为跨国银行的演变又迈出了一步——尽管这一步迟早会到来，但实际却是被形势所迫，很难被视为刻意策略的一部分。这一步便是开设独立的巴黎代理处。最初，BIO 与 Crédit Mobilier 的紧密关联曾是其实力的来源，因为尽管这家大型股份制银行及其经营模式备受争议，但它确实取得了巨大成功。然而到了 1867 年，Crédit Mobilier 陷入极端困境，双方的关联逐渐变成累赘，甚至成为严重的负担。具体达成了何种协议已不可考，但在 1868 年初的某个时候，二者的联系被切断了。 于是，巴黎方面成立了一家规模齐全的机构，承接此前由动产信贷公司代表 BIO 经手的业务。凭借其新获得的独立地位，BIO 逐步在法国构建起与英国境内类似的角色——也就是说，除了处理法奥贸易融资中产生的各类业务、支付法国持有的奥斯曼证券之外，它还加入了一个由货币市场"伙伴"组成的圈子参与金融操作，有时甚至需要为远离其主要业务重心（奥斯曼帝国和埃及）地区的政府或交通企业提供资本支持。</span></span></span>

By the end of the 1860s, then, the BIO was firmly established in London, Paris, Constantinople and the main port cities of the Ottoman Empire and Egypt. But it made no move to extend its operations any further by opening branches in other parts of the Mediterranean. Indeed for the next twenty years, apart from strengthening its position in Egypt by opening at Port Said (1872) and Cairo (1881), the Bank followed a strategy that was essentially national rather than multi-national. It was, after all, the state bank of the Ottoman Empire, and it was this aspect of its business that absorbed most of its attention and resources. Even within the Empire new branches were opened only where the Bank was to assume responsibility for the handling of tax revenues, and the government was willing to pay a subsidy to cover the overheads. In 1874 it absorbed one of its European rivals in Constantinople, the Banque Austro-Ottomane, which had been founded in Vienna in January 1872 and had for a short time played an active role in the financing of the Ottoman government before being fatally weakened by the 1873 crash. The terms of the merger meant that the paid-up capital of the BIO, already increased from the £1,350,000 of 1863 to £2 million in 1866, was raised to £5 million. But although the Bank now had Austrian shareholders, the provision that a Vienna committee should be established alongside the London and Paris ones remained unimplemented and there was never any kind of agency in the Austrian capital.<sup>8</sup> A clear opportunity to develop a presence in another European financial centre, and one which had considerable commercial ties with the Turkish-ruled Balkans, especially Salonica, was thus allowed to slip. Rather the additional resources acquired were devoted to underwriting the considerable expansion of the BIO's role <span class="italic">vis-á-vis</span> the Ottoman government which resulted from the new convention with the Porte signed early the following year (1875).<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">到 1860 年代末，BIO 已在伦敦、巴黎、君士坦丁堡以及奥斯曼帝国和埃及的主要港口城市站稳脚跟。但它并未采取进一步行动，通过在地中海其他地区开设分行来扩展业务。事实上，在接下来的二十年里，除通过在波赛（1872 年）和开罗（1881 年）设立分行以加强在埃及的地位外，该银行奉行的策略本质上是国家层面而非跨国层面的。毕竟，它是奥斯曼帝国的国家银行，其业务中这一方面占据了大部分关注与资源。即使在帝国内部，新分行也仅开设在银行需负责处理税收收入，且政府愿意支付补贴以覆盖运营开支的地方。 1874 年，它在君士坦丁堡吸收了一家欧洲竞争对手——奥托曼-奥地利银行（Banque Austro-Ottomane）。该银行于 1872 年 1 月在维也纳成立，曾一度在奥斯曼政府的融资中扮演积极角色，但后来因 1873 年危机而元气大伤。合并条款意味着，BIO 的实收资本从 1863 年的 135 万英镑增至 1866 年的 200 万英镑，如今进一步提升至 500 万英镑。不过，尽管此时银行已拥有奥地利股东，但关于在伦敦和巴黎委员会之外另设维也纳委员会的条款始终未落实，且在奥地利首都也从未设立任何代理机构。一个本可在另一个欧洲金融中心——且该中心与土耳其统治下的巴尔干地区（尤其是萨洛尼卡）有着密切商业联系——建立据点的大好机会，就这样被错失了。 额外获得的资源反而被用于支持 BIO 相对于奥斯曼政府角色的显著扩张，这一变化源于次年（1875 年）初与高门签署的新公约。</span></span></span>

Under the new arrangements the Bank undertook greatly to increase the scale of its advances to the government and assumed the functions of <span class="italic">trésorier-payeur général</span> of the Empire. All revenues, of whatever sort, both in Constantinople and the provinces were to be paid in to the BIO 'a' l'exclusion de tout autre établissement', and all expenditures were to be made through it. And to facilitate the discharge of this new responsibility it was provided that, by arrangement with the government and in return <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_146"></span>for a subsidy of LT 5,000 per branch, the Bank would open an agency in the principal town of every <span class="italic">vilayet.</span><sup>9</sup> In the event the great crisis of the Ottoman state, which began with the revolts in Bosnia and Herzegovina in that very summer — and the effective declaration of bankruptcy in the autumn — and culminated in the Russian war of 1877-8, meant that the system envisaged by the new convention was never fully put into effect. But it was in part, and this — together with meeting the financial needs of the embattled Ottoman state at a time when its credit with almost all other lenders had been destroyed, and subsequently the problems of finding a basis upon which payments on the pre-1875 debt might be resumed — left the Constantinople <span class="italic">direction</span> with little opportunity or inclination to expand elsewhere.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">根据新协议，银行承诺大幅增加对政府的贷款额度，并承担起帝国总出纳官的职能。无论是在君士坦丁堡还是各省，所有各类收入均须存入 BIO，"排除其他任何机构"，所有支出也须通过该行办理。为便于履行这一新职责，协议规定，经与政府协商，并以每分行 5000 土耳其里拉的补贴作为回报，银行将在各省首府设立代理处。 <sup>9</sup> 然而，奥斯曼帝国的重大危机——从同年夏天波斯尼亚和黑塞哥维那的叛乱开始，到秋天实际宣告破产，并最终以 1877-78 年的俄土战争告终——意味着新公约所设想的体系从未完全付诸实施。 但这只是部分原因——加上在几乎所有其他贷款人信用尽失的情况下满足四面楚歌的奥斯曼帝国的财政需求，以及随后如何找到恢复 1875 年前债务支付基础的问题——使得君士坦丁堡方面几乎没有机会或意愿向其他地方扩张。</span></span></span>

The Ottoman government's Decree of Muharrem, which was issued in December 1881 and which established the famous Public Debt Administration, reflected the successful conclusion of a prolonged set of negotiations in which the Bank had been fully involved.<sup>10</sup> By finally dealing with the issues that had been left unresolved ever since the bankruptcy, it restored both the government's credit and stability to the world of Ottoman finance, and ushered in what was perhaps the classic period of the BIO as a state bank. Of the numerous other European banks which, in the later 1860s and early 1870s, had been competing to offer the government short-term advances and to secure the right to issue long-term loans on its behalf, most had either withdrawn from the fray or been wound up, and those that survived — like the Crédit Général Ottoman and the Banque de Constantinople — now maintained a much lower profile. The BIO was thus not just the state bank, but also what it had never previously been except during a brief period in 1863-5: almost the only significant European financial institution operating in the Ottoman capital. This provided it with role enough and during the 1880s it became almost an article of faith, held both by the <span class="italic">direction</span> and the committees, that, Egypt excepted, the Bank's proper role lay within the Empire, and that there it should as far as possible be restricted to providing financial services of various sorts for the government. It is true that the 1880s found the BIO operating in six politically distinct territories — England, France, the Ottoman Empire, Egypt, Cyprus and Eastern Rumelia — rather than the four of the early 1870s. However, this was only because the two last had been separated from the Empire during the course of the great crisis: Cyprus to fall under British occupation and Eastern Rumelia (which was incorporated into Bulgaria in 1885) to enjoy a quasi autonomy. Thus, except in so far as it did not withdraw from <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_147"></span>them on account of their separation, the presence of the BIO there cannot be said to be evidence of any kind of hankering after multinationalism.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1881 年 12 月，奥斯曼政府颁布《穆哈兰姆法令》，成立了著名的公共债务管理局，这标志着银行全程深入参与的长达数年的谈判圆满落幕。 <sup>10</sup> 这一举措最终解决了自破产以来悬而未决的问题，不仅恢复了政府的信誉，也稳定了奥斯曼金融体系，由此开启了 BIO 作为国家银行最为辉煌的时期。在 1860 年代末至 1870 年代初，曾有多家欧洲银行争相为政府提供短期贷款，并争取代表政府发行长期债券的权利，但其中大多数要么退出竞争，要么已经清算；而幸存下来的银行——如奥斯曼信贷银行和君士坦丁堡银行——如今已低调许多。 因此，BIO 不仅是国家银行，而且成为了奥斯曼帝国首都中唯一重要的欧洲金融机构——除了 1863 年至 1865 年那段短暂时期外，这种情况此前从未有过。这赋予了它足够重要的角色，到了 19 世纪 80 年代，无论是董事会还是各委员会都几乎深信不疑：除埃及外，该银行的正确定位应在帝国境内，并且应尽可能仅限于为政府提供各类金融服务。诚然，到了 19 世纪 80 年代，BIO 在六个政治独立的地区开展业务——英格兰、法国、奥斯曼帝国、埃及、塞浦路斯和东鲁米利亚——而不像 19 世纪 70 年代初那样仅有四个。 不过，这仅仅是因为在危机期间，这两个地区脱离了帝国：塞浦路斯落入英国占领，而东鲁米利亚（1885 年并入保加利亚）享有半自治地位。 因此，除了它并未因他们的分离而疏远他们之外，BIO 的存在并不能被视为其对多国主义有任何向往的证据。</span></span></span>

The extent to which the Bank was concentrating on its relations with the Ottoman state in these years is suggested by the fact that for most of the 1880s over half its entire <span class="italic">actif</span> was accounted for by holdings of various forms of Ottoman public debt, and by advances to the government. At the end of 1883 the proportion was 63.13 per cent, at the end of 1885 58.45 per cent, and only by the end of 1887 had the figure fallen decisively below one-half to 40.38 per cent. But it may be noted that the last figure was because the scale of the short-term advances had at last substantially diminished, rather than because there had been any marked increase in the other elements of the account, which included all other <span class="italic">valeurs</span> held by the bank, its entire bill portfolio, its lending to all other customers both in the Levant and Europe, and its holdings of cash. As for profits, which fluctuated enormously, being as much as LT 757,383 in 1885 and no more than LT 370,050 in 1887, they tell the same story. Not only was the proportion of the total deriving from the <span class="italic">siége central,</span> whose operations mainly concerned the government, extremely high —varying between 86.7 per cent in 1885 and 65.86 per cent in 1887 — but differences in the amount of total profits accruing each year were almost entirely a reflection of changes in the level of those made by the <span class="italic">siége central.</span><sup>11</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些年银行与奥斯曼帝国关系的紧密程度，从以下事实可见一斑：19 世纪 80 年代大部分时间里，其总资产中超过一半来自持有的各类奥斯曼公共债务以及对政府的贷款。1883 年底这一比例为 63.13%，1885 年底为 58.45%，直到 1887 年底才降至 40.38%，显著低于半数。但值得注意的是，最后这一数字主要源于短期贷款规模的大幅缩减，而非银行账户中其他项目的显著增长——这些项目包括银行持有的所有其他证券、全部票据组合、对黎凡特及欧洲其他客户的贷款，以及现金持有量。 至于利润，其波动幅度极大——1885 年高达 757,383 里拉，而 1887 年仅为 370,050 里拉——这也说明了同样的问题。不仅来自总行（其业务主要涉及政府）的利润占总利润的比例极高（1885 年为 86.7%，1887 年为 65.86%），而且每年总利润的差异几乎完全反映了总行利润水平的变化。 <sup>11</sup> </span></span></span>

Nevertheless, despite the fairly good financial results returned in the 1880s, there is no doubt that the Bank's management had become unadventurous and that opportunities were being missed. This fact was forcibly brought home to the committees in London and Paris by the sudden eruption of the Deutsche Bank on to the Constantinople scene at the end of the decade, securing the Fisheries Loan and the concession for the Anatolian Railway in 1889, and clearly bringing to an end the virtual monopoly of Ottoman finance which the BIO had possessed since the mid-18705.<sup>12</sup> Nor was it likely that the existing <span class="italic">haute direction</span> would be able to meet this challenge effectively, for all its members were elderly, or at least middle-aged, men who had given good service in their time but whose energy was now flagging.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管如此，尽管 19 世纪 80 年代的财务业绩相当不错，但无疑银行的管理层已变得过于保守，错失了不少机遇。这一事实在 80 年代末德意志银行突然介入君士坦丁堡事务时，给伦敦和巴黎的委员会敲响了警钟——该行于 1889 年拿下了渔业贷款和安纳托利亚铁路的特许权，明显终结了自 70 年代中期以来 BIO 对奥斯曼金融事实上的垄断。 <sup>12</sup> 而现有的高层领导恐怕也难以有效应对这一挑战，因为所有成员都已年迈，至少也是中年人，他们曾经尽职尽责，但如今精力已大不如前。</span></span></span>

The retirement of Morgan Foster as Director General in the autumn of 1889 after seventeen years' service, however, provided the committees with the opportunity to rejuvenate the Constantinople management. What was needed was someone willing and able to grasp the wider opportunities put within reach by the immensely strong position (in terms of personnel, resources, credit <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_148"></span>and contacts) which the BIO had built up in the quarter century of its existence. They therefore took advantage of Foster's departure to remodel the <span class="italic">haute direction</span> completely. One of the other members was persuaded to follow Foster into a rather premature retirement, another was shunted into a purely honorific role, and a third (Von Haas), who had generally and deservedly been seen as the heir apparent, was passed over. Considerately, and thereby saving everyone from embarrassment, he proceeded to throw himself from a high window in the delirium of dengue fever or (as some openly said) in a fit of depression induced by so cruel a disappointment. As the new Director-General the committees appointed the young, intelligent, unquestionably energetic Sir Edgar Vincent, who had been serving as Financial Adviser to the Khedive of Egypt under Baring, and they did so with the express brief of overhauling the Bank's entire organization and imparting a new impetus to its operations in the East.<sup>13</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1889 年秋，摩根·福斯特在服务十七年后卸任总干事一职，这为各委员会提供了重振君士坦丁堡管理层的契机。当时需要一位既有意愿也有能力的人，去把握 BIO 在其成立二十五年来所积累的雄厚实力（包括人员、资源、信用和人脉）所带来的更广阔机遇。因此，他们利用福斯特离职之机，对最高管理层进行了彻底改组。其中一位成员被说服跟随福斯特提前退休，另一位被调任至纯荣誉性职位，而第三位（冯·哈斯）——他一贯被视为当之无愧的法定继承人——则被擢升绕过。 他体贴地，并以此免去所有人的尴尬，在登革热引起的谵妄中（或如某些人公开所言，因如此残酷的失望而陷入抑郁）从高窗纵身跃下。委员会任命年轻、聪明、无疑精力充沛的埃德加·文森特爵士为新任总干事，他曾在巴林手下担任埃及赫迪夫的财务顾问，而委员会的明确使命便是彻底整顿银行整个组织，为其在东方地区的业务注入新的活力。 <sup>13</sup> </span></span></span>

Vincent was only 32, but he had already established a reputation as being extremely able, a knowledgeable financier, a capable administrator and as being good at dealing with 'Orientals'. On his record to date all this was not entirely unjustified, though it owed more than a little to his impressive good looks and charming manner, as well as to a formidable talent for self-advertisement. He was also arrogant, vain, intensely ambitious, over-keen to make money for himself, and without sufficient experience to be as good a judge of events as he fancied himself to be.<sup>14</sup> But he had a vision of future prospects for the BIO which appeared to coincide with the aspirations of the Paris committee for the concern under their charge, and he immediately threw himself into the task of creating a larger, more dynamic and more profitable bank.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">文森特虽然年仅 32 岁，却早已名声在外：人们说他能力超群，是位学识渊博的金融家、干练的管理者，还特别擅长与"东方人"打交道。从他迄今为止的履历来看，这些评价倒也不全是虚言——虽然这很大程度上得益于他出众的相貌、迷人的风度，以及那令人叹为观止的自我推销天赋。他同时也很傲慢自负、野心勃勃、对赚钱过于热衷，而且缺乏足够的经验，实际判断力远不如他自以为的那么高明。 <sup>14</sup> 但他对 BIO 未来前景的构想，恰好与巴黎委员会对这家机构的期许不谋而合，于是他立刻全身心投入，致力于打造一家规模更大、更具活力、也更赚钱的银行。</span></span></span>

Above all Vincent wanted to expand the Bank's operations beyond the frontiers of the Empire, and under his influence for the first — and, as it proved, the only time — the BIO began to pursue a clearly multinational strategy. Amongst his first thoughts on taking office were to push forward into the Balkans, and to open in some of Turkey's small neighbours whose recent economic performance had in some respects been more dynamic. Both Bulgaria, and to a somewhat lesser extent Greece, were countries whose exports, overwhelmingly cereals in the former case and currants in the latter, had been growing very fast in the 1880s, and which therefore appeared to offer a tempting field for banking operations.<sup>15</sup> The BIO already had a branch at Philippopoli (Plovdiv) dating from that time, and Sir Edgar was anxious that it should also open at Sofia, the capital. But the prospect of its state bank opening <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_149"></span>branches on the one hand there, and on the other in Athens, was one at which it was reasonable to assume the Ottoman government might well baulk. The project was agreed by the committees, but with the reservation that the Director-General should take care to 'procéder doucement pour ne pas créer chez les Turcs d'apprehensions politiques'. Correspondents only, therefore, were to be named to start with, although this was specifically intended to prepare the way for fully-fledged branches.<sup>16</sup> In the event the intended branch was duly opened at Sofia in 1890, and another at the flourishing grain port of Roustchouk (Ruse) on the Danube two years later, both of which performed creditably enough for a number of years.<sup>17</sup> At the same time, moreover, the BIO, in association with Austrian and German banks, began to contribute to the principality government's appetite for credit, agreeing in December 1890 to advance five million francs at 7 per cent to the National Bank, and in July 1891 to participate for this amount in the long-term loan for which negotiations were then beginning and which was eventually issued in 1892.<sup>18</sup> By contrast nothing came of the Athens idea, for reasons upon which the Bank's archives are silent, but which were presumably political. Bulgaria in the early 1890s was under the pro-Turkish Stambulov régime, both governments, albeit with very different long-term aims, being agreed on maintaining the <span class="italic">status quo</span> in Macedonia. Ottoman relations with Greece, however, were characterized by continuing tension over the question of Crete, so that for the BIO to have extended its operations there would inevitably have affronted both Palace and Porte in a way that doing so in Bulgaria did not.<sup>19</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">最重要的是，文森特希望将银行业务拓展到帝国疆域之外，在他的影响下，BIO 首次——事后证明也是唯一一次——开始推行明确的跨国战略。他上任后最先想到的计划之一，便是挺进巴尔干地区，并在土耳其的几个邻国开设分行，这些国家近年来的经济表现在某些方面更为活跃。无论是保加利亚，还是在较小程度上的希腊，这两个国家在 1880 年代出口增长迅猛——前者主要依赖谷物，后者则是葡萄干——因此似乎为银行业务提供了诱人的拓展空间。 <sup>15</sup> BIO 早在那个时期就在菲利波波利（普罗夫迪夫）设立了分行，埃德加爵士迫切希望它也能在首都索非亚开设据点。 但其国家银行一边打算在此开设分行，另一边又想在雅典设点，这一前景在当时看来，奥斯曼政府很可能会加以阻挠。该项目虽经委员会同意，但附带一项保留条件，即总干事应谨慎行事，"循序渐进，以免在土耳其人中引发政治担忧"。因此，初期仅任命了通讯员，尽管此举本意是为日后开设正式分行铺路。 <sup>16</sup> 最终，拟设的分行于 1890 年在索菲亚顺利开业，两年后又在多瑙河畔繁荣的粮食港口鲁斯丘克（鲁塞）开设了另一家分行，这两家分行在此后多年间均经营业绩不俗。 <sup>17</sup> 与此同时，BIO 还与奥地利和德国的银行联手，开始满足该国政府对信贷的渴求：1890 年 12 月，它同意以 7%的利率向国家银行垫付 500 万法郎；1891 年 7 月，又承诺以相同金额参与当时正在谈判、最终于 1892 年发行的长期贷款。 <sup>18</sup> 相比之下，雅典的构想则无疾而终，原因银行档案中并未提及，但很可能是出于政治考量。1890 年代初的保加利亚处于亲土耳其的 Stambulov 政权统治下，尽管两国长期目标迥异，但在维持马其顿现状上却达成了一致。 然而，奥斯曼帝国与希腊的关系因克里特岛问题而持续紧张，因此，若 BIO 将其活动扩展至该地区，势必会同时触怒宫廷与奥斯曼政府，而这种情形在保加利亚则不会发生。 <sup>19</sup> </span></span></span>

Another Balkan state which sought to maintain good relations with Turkey at this time, also largely in order to advance its long-term interests in Macedonia, was Serbia.<sup>20</sup> And, as in the case of Bulgaria, these circumstances made it feasible for the BIO to contemplate an extension of its operations thither. The initiative for this seems to have derived from the Paris committee rather than from Constantinople, but it clearly fitted in with the Vincent strategy. No branches were ever opened in the country, despite direct requests from the Serbian government in April 1893 and, following the visit of King Alexander to Constantinople, from the Porte on Serbia's behalf in August 1894. The reason why does not emerge, but it is likely to be connected with the fact that Serbian foreign trade was growing rather slowly at this time, so that it would be difficult for a new foreign bank to find sufficient local business to justify the expense of a full-scale establishment. However, the BIO had begun to show an interest in Serbian issues from the winter of 1890-91, and in December 1892 participated <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_150"></span>to the tune of Frs. 300,000 in a short-term advance of Frs. 700,000 to the Serbian government. From that time onwards the extent of its involvement in Serbia's finances grew rapidly, preceding via a minor share in the 1893 loan of 44 million francs nominal, through a full involvement in the negotiations which preceded the abortive 1894 conversion project (which was rejected by the <span class="italic">Skouptchina), </span>and in the successful conversion loan of 1895 for Frs. 335,392,000. The latter involved the establishment of a Monopoly Administration to manage the revenues pledged to service the country's foreign debt, and on its six-man council sat representatives of the BIO and the German bank (the Berliner HG) with which the former had come to co-operate in Serbian matters. From that time onwards the BIO was ensconced at the very heart of Serbian finance.<sup>21</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">此时另一个寻求与土耳其保持良好关系的巴尔干国家是塞尔维亚，其主要目的也是为了推进其在马其顿的长期利益。 <sup>20</sup> 与保加利亚的情况类似，这些因素使得 BIO 得以考虑将业务扩展至该国。这一倡议似乎源自巴黎委员会而非君士坦丁堡，但显然符合文森特战略。尽管塞尔维亚政府于 1893 年 4 月直接提出请求，且 1894 年 8 月亚历山大国王访问君士坦丁堡后，奥斯曼政府也代表塞尔维亚提出请求，但该国始终未设立任何分支机构。其原因不得而知，但很可能与当时塞尔维亚对外贸易增长缓慢有关——这使得新设外资银行难以在当地找到足够业务来支撑全面运营的成本。 然而，从 1890-91 年冬季起，BIO 开始对塞尔维亚问题表现出兴趣，并于 1892 年 12 月参与了向塞尔维亚政府提供的 70 万法郎短期贷款，出资额达 30 万法郎。此后，BIO 在塞尔维亚金融事务中的参与程度迅速扩大：先是小规模参与了 1893 年面值 4400 万法郎的贷款，随后全面介入 1894 年夭折的债务转换计划（该计划遭塞尔维亚议会否决）的谈判，最终成功参与了 1895 年总额 3.35392 亿法郎的债务转换贷款。后者促成了一个垄断管理机构的建立，用于管理用于偿还外债的质押收入，在其六人委员会中，BIO 与德国银行（柏林 HG）的代表占据席位，这两家机构自此在塞尔维亚事务中展开合作。从那时起，BIO 便稳稳扎根于塞尔维亚金融体系的核心。 <sup>21</sup> </span></span></span>

Any involvement in Roumania comparable to that undertaken in Bulgaria and Serbia was ruled out by the position held there by the Bank of Roumania, in which some of the bankers who controlled the BIO were also largely interested and of whose shares the BIO itself still held a large block. An extension of BIO operations to the Russian Black Sea ports of Odessa and Batum would probably also have been commercially interesting but had likewise to be ruled out: it was quite simply unthinkable for the Sultan's state bank to open in Russia. In another direction, 1893 saw some talk of possible branches in Persia, perhaps at Bushire on the Gulf, or more realistically (for they had a much more extensive trade with Ottoman Iraq) at Kermanshah and Hamadan. But in this case the <span class="italic">haute direction</span> seems to have had no real interest, and the suggestion that it might have had was part of a campaign to evict the Imperial Bank of Persia from its Ottoman bridgeheads at Baghdad and Basra by threatening in turn to invade IBP territory. In 1893, therefore, a formal agreement was reached with the IBP whereby the latter was to withdraw from Iraq and the BIO undertook not to open in Persia.<sup>22</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在罗马尼亚设立类似保加利亚和塞尔维亚的业务，因罗马尼亚银行的地位而无法实现——该银行由控制 BIO 的部分银行家持有大量股份，且 BIO 自身仍持有一大笔股份。将 BIO 业务扩展至俄国黑海港口敖德萨和巴统，在商业上或许有利可图，但同样难以实现：苏丹的国家银行在俄国开设分行简直是天方夜谭。而在另一方向，1893 年曾传可能于波斯设分行，或许选在波斯湾的布什尔，更实际些（因与奥斯曼伊拉克的贸易往来更广泛）则选在克尔曼沙阿和哈马丹。 但在此案中，高层似乎并未真正感兴趣，而所谓可能产生兴趣的说法，其实是一场旨在驱逐波斯帝国银行（IBP）退出其在巴格达和巴士拉的奥斯曼桥头堡的运动的一部分，手段则是威胁侵犯 IBP 的地盘。因此，1893 年，与 IBP 达成了一项正式协议，后者从伊拉克撤出，而 BIO 则承诺不在波斯开展业务。 <sup>22</sup> </span></span></span>

If the BIO was to find further new pastures, therefore, it was going to have to be in an area which could not be regarded in any way as an extension of its Ottoman home base. And where, to all appearances, were opportunities more promising as the last decade of the nineteenth century reached its middle years, than South Africa? Thanks in part to Vincent's energetic leadership and in part to improved economic conditions, the operations of the <span class="italic">siége central</span> and the branches in the east were generating very much larger profits than in the later years of the previous decade. Total profits, excluding the unrealized appreciation on investments, rose from LT 435,981 in 1890, to LT 546,500 in 1893 and <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_151"></span>LT 701,920 in 1894. And by no means all the increases were being distributed to the shareholders. Indeed in the years 1890-92 more was transferred to reserves than was paid out to the latter in dividends, and in his report on the year 1893 the Director-General was able to boast that in the preceding four years (that is 1890-93 inclusive) the reserves had been increased at the rate of nearly LT 1,000 <span class="italic">per day,</span> growing from some LT 1.4 million at the end of 1889 to LT 2.76 million at the end of 1893. The underlying strength of the Bank's position had, moreover, been further improved by a change in the structure of its investment portfolio whereby holdings of Ottoman government securities were reduced and those of British consols, and to a lesser extent French <span class="italic">rentes, </span>much increased. 'We have, by operating in this way', announced Vincent, 'diminished the amount of our assets depending for their value upon Turkish credit, and we are now in possession of a security upon which we can at any time raise money ...' Thus at the end of 1890, out of a portfolio valued in total at frs. 107.4 million, consols and <span class="italic">rentes</span> made up only frs. 3.7 million (3.4 per cent), whilst at the end of 1892 they accounted for frs. 26 million (20.3 per cent) and two years later frs. 37.75 million (30.2 per cent), by which time <span class="italic">fonds d'état Ottomans</span> were down to only 16.3 per cent.<sup>23</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">因此，如果 BIO 要进一步寻找新的牧场，就必须选择一个完全无法被视为其奥斯曼大本营延伸的地区。而在 19 世纪 90 年代中期，从各方面来看，哪个地方比南非更具潜力呢？部分归功于文森特富有活力的领导，部分归功于经济状况的改善，中央总部和东部各分支机构的运营利润远高于 80 年代后期。总利润（不包括未实现的投资增值）从 1890 年的 435,981 里拉增长到 1893 年的 546,500 里拉，再到 1894 年的 701,920 里拉。而这些增长中的相当一部分并未全部分配给股东。 确实，在 1890-92 年间，转入储备金的金额超过了以股息形式支付的金额。总干事在 1893 年的报告中自豪地指出，过去的四年（即 1890-1893 年）里，储备金以每天近 1000 土耳其里拉的速度增长，从 1889 年底的约 140 万里拉增至 1893 年底的 276 万里拉。此外，通过调整投资组合结构——减少奥斯曼政府证券的持有量，大幅增加英国统一公债（以及较小程度的法国公债）——该银行的潜在实力得到了进一步提升。文森特宣称：“通过这种操作，我们减少了依赖土耳其信用价值的资产数额，如今我们持有的证券随时可以变现……”因此，到 1890 年底，在一总额为 1.074 亿中，consols 和 rentes 仅占 370 万法郎（3.4%），而到 1892 年底，它们上升到 2600 万法郎（20.3%），两年后达到 3775 万法郎（30.2%），此时 fonds d'état Ottomans 仅占 16.3%。 <sup>23</sup> </span></span></span>

The BIO was well placed, therefore, generating plenty of cash and with a very sound reserve position, to participate in the boom in South African mining shares which was well under way by 1894. But its participation was more than mere dabbling. It was more than simply taking advantage of a few favourable opportunities that were pushed its way by other houses with which it customarily co-operated, as for instance when Baron Hottinguer, a member of the Paris committee, had offered frs. 3.25 million out of the frs. 30 million which his firm had been allocated in the Russian loan of 1889.<sup>24</sup> Both at this time and later such offers led the BIO into limited involvements in all kinds of far-flung operations, but these in no sense reflected any coherent regional strategy. The move into South Africa, by contrast, <span class="italic">was</span> a systematic attempt to develop an entirely new geographical focus of interest for the Bank, although exactly who, within the BIO organization, was responsible for the decision has not yet come to light. The impetus might have come just from Sir Edgar Vincent himself, for certainly he embraced the new policy with great enthusiasm and plunged deeply into South African finance on his personal account.<sup>25</sup> On the other hand it is unlikely that either Theodore Berger, the most active member of the Paris committee and the effective head of the Bank, or his other colleagues, would have needed prompting in this matter by <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_152"></span>their Director-General. For Paris banks and financiers in general were throwing themselves into gold mining issues in 1894-5 —indeed, according to Kubicek, Paris was largely responsible for the boom of those years. Some doubtful promoters were at work, but for a time even the unimpeachably respectable <span class="italic">Crédit Lyonnais </span>was putting capital into gold mining shares, as were at least two (and no doubt more) of the <span class="italic">haute banque</span> firms represented on the BIO's Committee, Pillet-Will and Heine.<sup>26</sup> The likelihood is that at no stage was any clear decision taken as to how deep the Bank's involvement ought to be, that it began in the early part of 1894 as all concerned took it for granted that the BIO should not miss out on the fashionable opportunities of the moment, and that it grew progressively greater in late 1894 and 1895 as Vincent took advantage of the freedom of action he had always enjoyed to indulge his desire to play the great financier.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

I consider this a most important arrangement and one that is likely to prove of immense value to the Ottoman Bank in the future ... No negotiation during the last five years has been of equal importance with this one, which opens out to us the means of employing a portion of our large available funds in countries of great future development and far-removed from the sphere of political trouble in Europe.<sup>32</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我认为这是一项极其重要的安排，未来很可能对 Ottoman Bank 产生巨大的价值……过去五年里，没有任何谈判能与这次相提并论——它为我们打开了将大量可用资金投入到那些远离欧洲政治动荡、具有巨大发展潜力的国家的大门。 <sup>32</sup> </span></span></span>

</div>

How far the BIO might have developed its interest in South Africa can never be known, for in the autumn of 1895 the new strategy received a set-back so damaging that it never recovered. Many others in Constantinople had been speculating in South African stocks, and doing so on borrowed money, at least some of which <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_154"></span>had seemingly been lent by the BIO itself.<sup>33</sup> The beginnings of a fall in kaffirs triggered a panic amongst the notoriously volatile financial community of Galata, which was exacerbated by rumours that the BIO had itself suffered serious losses. On 30 October Berger wrote to Vincent with a polite but firm request to be told 'bien exactement' the extent of the Bank's involvement in gold mining shares, continuing'... il sera prudent de faire voir au public que nos opérations étrangers á notre sphére d'action natureile, ne tiendrant jamais qu'un place secondaire dans notre activité et dans notre portefeuille',<sup>34</sup> But it was already too late. The panic precipitated a major 'run' on the Bank, encouraged and promoted by those who for business or political reasons wished to embarrass it or, through it, the Ottoman government. The latter had to declare a month's moratorium on cash payments and the French bankers who formed the Paris committee felt it necessary to establish a guarantee fund of frs. 22 million as a safety net. In fact there was never any real danger that the <span class="italic">siége central</span> would have to close its doors but everyone involved had had a nasty fright, and the price of the Bank's shares had dropped catastrophically. Vincent's prestige was badly dented, indeed to the point where he had lost the confidence of his employers, for there was no question that it had been the notoriety of his South African speculations that had largely weakened confidence in the Bank. And it had been his example which had encouraged — and the credit his managers had allowed which had to a large extent fuelled — the excesses of its customers.<sup>35</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">帝国银行对南非的兴趣究竟有多大，已无从知晓，因为 1895 年秋天，这一新策略遭受了毁灭性的打击，从此一蹶不振。君士坦丁堡的许多投机者都在用借来的钱炒作南非股票，而其中至少有一部分资金似乎是从帝国银行贷出的。 <sup>33</sup> 南非股票初现跌势，立即在加拉塔这个以动荡闻名的金融圈中引发了恐慌，而帝国银行自身也遭受重大损失的传闻更是雪上加霜。10 月 30 日，伯杰给文森特写了一封客气而坚决的信，要求他“准确无误”地告知该银行在黄金矿业股票上的涉入程度，并继续写道：“……明智的做法是向公众表明，我们在自然业务范围以外的操作，在我们的活动和投资组合中永远不会占据主要地位。” <sup>34</sup> 但为时已晚。 这场恐慌引发了大规模的银行挤兑潮，而那些出于商业或政治目的、想要给银行难堪，或是借机打击奥斯曼政府的人，更是推波助澜、极力煽动。奥斯曼政府不得不宣布暂停现金支付一个月，而由法国银行家组成的巴黎委员会则意识到必须设立一笔 2200 万法郎的担保基金作为安全网。事实上，总行从未面临真正关门歇业的危险，但所有相关方都吓得不轻，银行股价更是断崖式暴跌。文森的声望严重受损，甚至到了让雇主们对他失去信心的地步——毫无疑问，正是他在南非的投机活动声名狼藉，才在很大程度上削弱了外界对银行的信任。而他本人的所作所为更是起到了鼓励作用——加上他手下的经理们所提供的信贷在很大程度上推波助澜——最终导致了客户们的过度投机行为。 <sup>35</sup> </span></span></span>

The committee could scarcely get rid of Vincent immediately without compounding the damage, but his freedom of action was drastically curtailed by changes in the structure of the <span class="italic">haute direction,</span> and his departure sooner rather than later was a foregone conclusion. '<span class="italic">Delenda est Carthago'</span> ('Carthage must be destroyed') was how one member summed up his feelings on what another referred to as 'the Vincent question'!<sup>36</sup> The now discredited Director-General was allowed to stay until his contract expired in 1897, but a policy of extreme caution was imposed upon him in the meanwhile,<sup>37</sup> and even if 1896 had not seen a further heavy drop in the value of gold mining shares it is likely that the BIO would have pulled back in South Africa. As it was, the boom had well and truly broken, many of the shares the Bank had acquired were revealed to have been highly over-valued, and some (such as the notorious Buffelsdoorns, of which it had large numbers) proved incapable of ever paying a dividend.<sup>38</sup> No more, therefore, was heard of brave new worlds to conquer in the southern hemisphere. And when Vincent was finally replaced as <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_155"></span>Director-General in 1897 his successor was a different sort of man in all respects. Sir Hamilton Lang was elderly, he was dull, he had begun his career as a BIO branch manager in Cyprus in the 1860s, and had spent the rest of it in banking and financial administration in Turkey, the Balkan states and Egypt. Above all he was safe.<sup>39</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">委员会一时半会儿还无法直接把文森特扫地出门，否则只会让局面雪上加霜，但高层架构的调整已大大限制了他的行动自由，他早晚都得走人，这已是板上钉钉的事。有位委员在谈及所谓的“文森特问题”时，用了“Delenda est Carthago（迦太基必须被毁灭）”来总结自己的心情。 <sup>36</sup> 这位现已声名扫地的总干事被允许留任至 1897 年合同期满，但在此期间，他被强加了一条极度谨慎的行事方针， <sup>37</sup> 即便 1896 年黄金矿业股票价值没有进一步大跌，海外投资银行也很可能会撤出南非。而现实是，这场繁荣已然彻底破灭，银行购入的许多股票被证实严重高估，其中一些（比如银行持有大量股份、臭名昭著的 Buffelsdoorns 公司）更是永远无力支付股息。 自此，再无人提及征服南半球勇敢新世界之事。1897 年文森特终于卸任总干事一职时，继任者与他全然不同。汉密尔顿·朗爵士年事已高，沉闷无趣，他于 1860 年代在塞浦路斯从 BIO 分行经理做起，此后余生都在土耳其、巴尔干诸国和埃及从事银行与金融管理工作。最重要的是——他稳妥可靠。 <sup>39</sup> </span></span></span>

The possibility that the BIO might have become important in South Africa as well as in London, Paris, the Ottoman lands, Egypt, Cyprus and the Balkans, had been aborted by Vincent's over-confidence and inexperience. His other achievement in terms of geographical expansion, the new Bulgarian branches, also proved to be ephemeral, although this was not his fault. They had in fact performed well and when, in the aftermath of the 1895 crisis, branch closures had been considered as an economy measure they had not been regarded as candidates.<sup>40</sup> But continuing economic depression and political crisis within the Empire led to the question of closures being raised again in the latter part of 1897. Poor grain harvests in the later years of the decade led to a heavy drop in Bulgaria's main export and reduced the profits the BIO derived from their operations there, but it was above all a change in the political atmosphere that precipitated the decision to withdraw. Stambulov had been overthrown, and under his successors a latent antagonism against foreign capital came to the fore. 'L'hostilité systématique que l'élément Stranger recontre de la part des Autorités Princiéres . . . (et) la partialité des Tribunaux', explained the <span class="italic">direction générate</span> to the committees in 1899 'enlévement aux transactions une bonne parte de leur sécurité'. But it was the promulgation of a new Commercial Code by the Bulgarian government in 1898 which was the final straw. Reflecting the general xenophobia, the section of the Code dealing with foreign firms operating inside the country imposed a series of new, and in several respects, irksome restrictions on their freedom to act as they thought fit. Ultimately it was the requirement that the Bank establish a local <span class="italic">comité d'administration</span> composed of Bulgarian nationals, or at least of foreigners resident in Bulgaria, which was felt to be quite impracticable, that provoked the decision to close.<sup>41</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">BIO 在南非以及伦敦、巴黎、奥斯曼领地、埃及、塞浦路斯和巴尔干地区本有可能变得举足轻重，但这一可能性被文森特过于自信和缺乏经验所扼杀。他在地理扩张上的另一项成就——在保加利亚开设的新分支——也证明是昙花一现，尽管这并非他的过错。这些分支实际上表现不俗，在 1895 年危机过后，当关闭分支作为一项节约措施被考虑时，它们并未被列入候选名单。 <sup>40</sup> 然而，帝国境内持续的经济萧条和政治危机，使得关闭分支的问题在 1897 年下半年再次被提上议程。90 年代后期粮食收成不佳，导致保加利亚主要出口产品大幅下滑，减少了 BIO 在当地运营的利润，但最终促使撤退决定的，主要是政治氛围的变化。 斯塔姆博洛夫倒台后，继任者政府中潜藏的排外情绪逐渐浮出水面。1899 年，总管理层向各委员会解释时指出："外国资本在亲王政府当局所遭遇的系统性敌意……以及法庭的偏袒，使交易的保障大打折扣。"然而，压垮骆驼的最后一根稻草，是 1898 年保加利亚政府颁布的新《商业法典》。这部法典反映了当时普遍的排外心理，涉及外国企业在保运营的章节，对它们自主经营的自由施加了一系列新的、在某些方面颇为棘手的限制。最终，法典要求银行成立一个由保加利亚国民、或至少是定居保加利亚的外国人组成的本地管理委员会——这一规定被认为完全不可行——促使当局作出了关闭的决定。 <sup>41</sup> </span></span></span>

The Sir Edgar Vincent interlude was the one period in the history of the BlO before 1914 when the Bank can be said to have pursued an unequivocally multinational policy. At other times its multi nationalism was lukewarm, and usually forced upon it by outside events. In the beginning it was founded as an Anglo-French concern, with a footing both in London and in Paris, rather than an exclusively English or exclusively French one, only because this was insisted upon by the Ottoman ministers. Even so it was not until 1868 that the BIO had an independent presence in <span id="Ch08.xhtml_page_156"></span>Paris, and but for the difficulties which beset the <span class="italic">Crédit Mobilier</span> it would probably not have made the move as soon as it did. As far as the east was concerned, down to 1890 the extension of operations to Egypt was the only step that can be seen as a multi-nationalizing one. Otherwise the BIO found itself operating outside the frontiers of the Ottoman state only because certain territories where it had already become established were detached from the Empire in 1878.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">埃德加·文森特爵士主导的这一阶段，是 1914 年以前 BIO 历史上唯一可以被认为明确奉行跨国政策的时期。其他时候，其跨国主义都显得不温不火，且通常是被外部事件所迫。起初，它是以英法合资企业的形式成立的，在伦敦和巴黎都有立足之地，而非纯英国或纯法国企业，这仅仅是因为奥斯曼大臣们坚持如此。即便如此，直到 1868 年 BIO 才在巴黎有了独立的存在，而且若非动产信贷银行遭遇困境，它可能也不会那么快就迈出这一步。就东方而言，直到 1890 年，将业务扩展到埃及是唯一可以被视为跨国化的一步。 否则，BIO 发现自己是在奥斯曼帝国境外运作，这仅仅是因为某些它已立足的领土在 1878 年脱离了帝国。</span></span></span>

Certainly in the years after those discussed in the main part of this article, that is between the liquidation of the Bulgarian branches in 1899 and the First World War, the Bank remained content with its role as an essentially <span class="italic">Ottoman</span> bank, with subsidiary spheres of activity in Egypt, Cyprus and, until it was hived off to a separate <span class="italic">Banque Franco-Serbe</span> in 1910, Serbia. Within the Empire there was a change of emphasis from 1903 onwards as the <span class="italic">générate direction</span> began to expand the size of the branch network as a deliberate policy, and to look increasingly for opportunities other than those arising from their function as bankers to the government. The Revolution of 1908, and the advent of the Young Turks with a new set of economic policies aimed at accelerating the development of the country, and especially of Anatolia, tended to reinforce this development. These years, therefore, did see the Bank undertake a major programme of geographical expansion, with more than fifty new agencies and sub-agencies opened between 1904 and 1914.<sup>42</sup> But the expansion was <span class="italic">within</span> a single set of political frontiers, and proposals from various sources both inside and outside the Bank to go beyond them — into Persia (1901, 1907), Southern Russia (Batum, 1908; Tauris, 1909), Aden (1913) and New York (1913) — were resolutely resisted. The refusal to comply with the Ottoman Finance Ministry's suggestion that the BIO should open in Tauris was specifically justified on the grounds that the Bank did not want branches outside the Empire, and only in the case of the new branch at Manchester (1911) was this rule in fact breached.<sup>43</sup> There was in truth scope enough, and profit enough, for the BIO within the Ottoman world. It did not really make sense to look very far beyond it.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">当然，在本文主要部分讨论的那些年份之后——即 1899 年保加利亚分行清算完毕到第一次世界大战期间——该银行依然满足于其作为一家本质上属于奥斯曼帝国的银行，活动范围延伸至埃及、塞浦路斯，以及塞尔维亚（直到 1910 年该业务被剥离，划归新成立的独立的 Banque Franco-Serbe）。在帝国内部，从 1903 年起重点有所转移，总行管理层开始将扩大分行网络作为一项明确的政策，并且越来越多地寻求除担任政府银行家职能之外的其他机会。1908 年革命以及青年土耳其党人的上台，他们推行一系列旨在加速国家发展，特别是安纳托利亚发展经济政策，这一趋势因此得到进一步强化。 因此，这些年银行确实进行了一次重大的地域扩张，在 1904 至 1914 年间开设了五十多家新的代理处和分支机构。 <sup>42</sup> 然而，这次扩张始终局限在同一政治疆界之内，来自银行内外各方关于超越这一界限的提议——进入波斯（1901、1907）、南俄（巴统，1908；Tauris，1909）、亚丁（1913）和纽约（1913）——均遭到坚决抵制。奥斯曼财政部曾建议 BIO 在 Tauris 开设分行，银行之所以拒绝服从，具体理由是不想在帝国境外设立分支机构，而这一规定实际上只在与曼彻斯特开设新分行（1911）时被打破。 <sup>43</sup> 事实上，在奥斯曼世界内部，BIO 既有足够的发展空间，也有充足的利润。将目光投向远方确实没有太大意义。</span></span></span>
