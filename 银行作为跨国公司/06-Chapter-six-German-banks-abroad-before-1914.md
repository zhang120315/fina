Introduction<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">引言</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

World trade during the last three decades before the outbreak of the First World War was characterized, among other things, by a rapidly-growing share for Germany, and to a lesser extent, the United States, whereas Britain's part, so dominant around the middle of the nineteenth century, gradually shrank, remaining nevertheless ahead of all trading nations. If we refer only to the one-and-a-half decades before 1914 for which statistics are best, we can base ourselves on S.B. Saul's observation that:<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">第一次世界大战爆发前的最后三十年，世界贸易的一个显著特征是德国所占份额迅速增长，美国增长幅度较小，而英国——虽在 19 世纪中期占据绝对主导地位——其份额逐渐萎缩，但依然领先于所有贸易国。若仅参考统计数据最为完备的 1914 年前这十五年，我们可以引用 S.B.索尔的观点：</span></span></span>

<div class="quotes">

Between 1899 and 1913 Britain's share of world trade in manufactures fell from 34 per cent to 31 per cent, whilst that of Germany rose from 23 to 27.5 per cent and that of the United States from 11.5 per cent to 13 per cent.<sup>1</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1899 年至 1913 年间，英国在世界制成品贸易中的份额从 34%降至 31%，而德国从 23%升至 27.5%，美国则从 11.5%增至 13%。 <sup>1</sup> </span></span></span>

</div>

We can go further back and look at Germany's exports in the European total according to Paul Bairoch's estimates (keeping in mind, of course, that all kinds of goods are included here and that the development is therefore much less spectacular than if we had looked at manufactures only): in 1860 they amounted to 18.4 per cent, in 1890 they had even diminished to 17.4 per cent, but in 1910 they had finally risen to 20.4 per cent, whereas Britain's share had gone down continually during the entire period from 29.8 per cent in 1860 to 26.6 per cent in 1890, and to 23.7 per cent in 1910.<sup>2</sup>

One of the reasons for the rapid growth of German industry since the 1880s could have been the contribution of German banking in its 'mixed' form: one which combined short-term and long-term credit and established close ties between individual industrial firms and credit institutions. Many observers, for instance Rondo Cameron in 1961<sup>3</sup> and only recently Richard Tilly,<sup>4</sup> did not reach any clear-cut conclusions in this regard. They <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_100"></span>did not deny the prominent role German banks played in most sectors of industry but they did not find enough evidence for establishing an unequivocal causal relationship between German banking and the progress of German industrialization. On the other hand, many contemporary observers already agreed that the success of German exports in the world market, and especially of consumer goods which grew especially fast between 1872 and 1890,<sup>5</sup> was to a large extent due to the way these exports were credited. This aspect was discussed particularly during the First World War, when French and British observers tried to explain the reasons for German commercial supremacy and the means to fight it more successfully once the war was over.<sup>6</sup> The British ambassador to Italy, for instance, wrote in a report of 15 May 1916: 'The readiness of the German manufacturers to give three, six and even more months credit has been one of the most effectual means by which they have secured the Italian market'.<sup>7</sup> The Huth Jackson Committee, set up by the British Board of Trade in 1916, proposed the foundation of a British Trade Bank which should, among other things, grant six to twelve months' credit to British exporters, thus following the German model.<sup>8</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1880 年代以来德国工业迅速增长的原因之一，或许在于德国银行业的"混合"模式：这种模式将短期信贷与长期信贷相结合，并在各工业企业与信贷机构之间建立了紧密联系。许多观察者，例如 1961 年的龙多·卡梅伦 <sup>3</sup> 以及近期的理查德·蒂利 <sup>4</sup> ，对此并未得出明确结论。他们并不否认德国银行在多数工业领域所发挥的突出作用，但认为没有足够证据能够确凿证明德国银行业与德国工业化进程之间存在直接的因果关系。另一方面，当时已有不少观察者达成共识：德国商品在世界市场上的出口成功，尤其是 1872 年至 1890 年间增长尤为迅速的消费品出口 <sup>5</sup> ，在很大程度上得益于这些出口的信贷支持方式。 这个问题在第一次世界大战期间被特别讨论，当时英法两国的观察者试图解释德国商业优势的原因，以及战争结束后更成功地应对这种优势的方法。 <sup>6</sup> 例如，英国驻意大利大使在 1916 年 5 月 15 日的一份报告中写道："德国制造商愿意提供三个、六个甚至更长时间的信贷期，是他们占据意大利市场最有效的手段之一。" <sup>7</sup> 由英国贸易委员会于 1916 年成立的 Huth Jackson 委员会，提议设立一家英国贸易银行，该银行应仿效德国模式，向英国出口商提供六至十二个月的信贷。 <sup>8</sup> </span></span></span>

If we want to have a closer look at the success or failure of German banking abroad, it would certainly be advisable to make a distinction between three different types of foreign activities:<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">若要更深入地审视德国银行业在海外的成败，区分三种不同类型的海外活动无疑是明智之举：</span></span></span>

1.  <span class="italic">overseas banks,</span> normally founded by one of the great German banks or by a group of these credit institutions;<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">海外银行，通常由一家德国大型银行或若干此类信贷机构联合创办；</span></span></span>
2.  <span class="italic">foreign agencies,</span> almost all of them situated in London and again founded by one of the great German banks;<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">国外代理机构，几乎全部设在伦敦，同样由一家德国大型银行设立；</span></span></span>
3.  <span class="italic">shares held in foreign banks</span> by German banks which in some cases also participated in their foundation.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">德国银行持有的外国银行股份，这些银行有时也参与了其创办过程。</span></span></span>

The setting-up and development of overseas banks<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">海外银行的设立与发展</span></span></span>
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two principal motives for founding these banks can be alleged: on the one hand, as already mentioned, is the financing of German trade; on the other, the fostering of German portfolio and direct investment abroad.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">创办这些银行主要有两大动因：一方面，如前所述，是为德国贸易提供融资；另一方面，则是促进德国在国外的证券投资与直接投资。</span></span></span>

If we concentrate our attention first on trade finance, we will have to acknowledge that in the case of exports 'financial assistance was given mainly by way of advances to bridge over the interval between the shipment of goods and the arrival of the purchaser's remittance'<sup>9</sup>. In fact this could be done either by current account or acceptance credit 'on the strength of the exporter's general standing' or by a draft upon the importer, 'made <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_101"></span>out to order and accompanied by the "documents relating to the consignment' and handed over to the exporter's home bank or to an overseas bank; subsidiary to the home bank or linked otherwise to it. In some cases these drafts were sold outright or discounted by the exporter; in others 'advances were sought against goods not definitely sold but shipped to agents on consignment'. The same procedure, just reversed, took its course in the case of imports: the overseas banks made advances upon drafts done by the exporters.<sup>10</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">首先来看贸易融资这一块，我们必须承认，在出口方面，"资金援助主要以垫款形式提供，用于填补货物发运至买方汇款到账之间的时间差" <sup>9</sup> 。实际上，这可以通过两种方式实现：一种是凭借出口商的综合信誉，采用往来账户或承兑信贷；另一种是向进口商开立汇票，"该汇票为指定抬头，并附有'货物相关单证'，交予出口商的开户银行或海外银行（该银行可能是开户行的附属机构或有其他关联）"。在某些情况下，这些汇票由出口商直接出售或贴现；在另一些情况下，"针对尚未明确售出但已发运给代理商寄售的货物，出口商也会申请垫款"。进口方面则遵循完全相反的程序：海外银行根据出口商开具的汇票提供垫款。 <sup>10</sup> </span></span></span>

Looking at the way German foreign trade was financed, P. Barrett Whale concluded that:<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在审视德国对外贸易融资方式时，P. 巴雷特·怀尔得出结论：</span></span></span>

<div class="quotes">

there was little if anything that was new. The methods and forms adopted in assisting German trade were those of the English foreign trade banks and ... had been used by these, not in the service of British trade alone, but to a great extent also in the service of the commerce of other countries - including that of Germany.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">几乎没有什么新意可言。德国在支持贸易方面所采用的方法和形式，与英国的外贸银行如出一辙……这些方法和形式不仅服务于英国自身的贸易，在很大程度上也服务于其他国家的商业——包括德国在内。</span></span></span>

</div>

There was only one important difference and that is the already-mentioned length of credit terms where German traders and German credit institutions were far ahead of their British competitors.<sup>11</sup> One of the main aims meant to justify the establishment of German overseas banks was the gradual replacement of sterling bills by mark bills. It was only partially attained, since there were clear advantages in using sterling bills and the many services the London money market could offer.<sup>12</sup> London's role as an international clearing house, among others for short-term commercial credit, must certainly have been an important factor for the predominance of sterling bills.<sup>13</sup> But it was not only low transaction costs that made London so attractive. Another cause has to be sought in the fact that in the London money market sterling bills could be discounted at a comparatively low rate of interest, which was normally about 1 per cent below that of Berlin.<sup>14</sup> This was, of course, one of the reasons which induced some of the biggest German banks to establish agencies in London,<sup>15</sup> a phenomenon which will be discussed later in greater detail.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">唯一重要区别在于前文提及的信贷期限，德国商人和德国信贷机构在这方面远超其英国竞争对手。 <sup>11</sup> 建立德国海外银行的主要目标之一，便是逐步以马克汇票取代英镑汇票。这一目标仅部分实现，因为使用英镑汇票及伦敦货币市场提供的诸多服务具有明显优势。 <sup>12</sup> 伦敦作为包括短期商业信贷在内的国际清算中心，无疑是英镑汇票占据主导地位的重要因素。 <sup>13</sup> 但伦敦的吸引力不仅源于低廉的交易成本，另一原因在于伦敦货币市场上英镑汇票的贴现率通常比柏林低约 1 个百分点。 <sup>14</sup> 这当然是促使一些德国大型银行在伦敦设立分支机构的原因之一， <sup>15</sup> 这一现象将在后文详细讨论。</span></span></span>

Apart from London's important role, there can be no doubt that the importance of overseas banks was growing during the last decades before the outbreak of the First World War and that this development had by no means come to an end by the middle of the 1920s.<sup>16</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">除了伦敦的重要地位外，毫无疑问，在第一次世界大战爆发前的最后几十年间，海外银行的重要性与日俱增，而这一发展态势到 20 世纪 20 年代中期也远未终结。 <sup>16</sup> </span></span></span>

A considerable part of the overseas expansion of German banks can be understood simply by following the development of the Deutsche Bank, founded in January 1870 and licensed as a joint <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_102"></span>stock company two months later by the Prussian government.<sup>17</sup> The original idea, already discussed during the summer of 1869, had been to establish a 'German overseas bank' by uniting a group of Berlin and Hamburg private bankers.<sup>18</sup> in the end the Hamburg group did not participate and preferred to found a separate credit institution which never became successful. The statutes of the Deutsche Bank, however, clearly expressed in Article 2 that 'the aim of the company is the running of banking operations of all kinds, particularly the support and facilitation of commercial relations between Germany, the other European countries and overseas markets'.<sup>19</sup> Accordingly, one of the first steps of the Deutsche Bank was to participate in the foundation of the German Bank of London in March 1871 together with a group of other German and London banks. The Deutsche Bank subscribed to about a quarter of its capital of £600,000 but it never became a very successful enterprise and thus in 1879 these shares were sold.<sup>20</sup> The territorial expansion of the Deutsche Bank continued in the meantime by the establishment of agencies in Bremen and Hamburg in 1871-72 and the foundation of Far East agencies in Shanghai and Yokohama in May 1872. A proper agency of the Deutsche Bank in London was opened in March 1873, and its activities will be discussed further on in this chapter.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">德国银行海外扩张的很大一部分，只要追踪德意志银行的发展轨迹就能理解。这家银行成立于 1870 年 1 月，两个月后获得普鲁士政府批准，成为一家股份制公司。 <sup>17</sup> 最初的构想早在 1869 年夏天就已讨论过，计划联合柏林和汉堡的一批私人银行家，成立一家"德国海外银行"。 <sup>18</sup> 但最终，汉堡集团并未参与，而是选择另立门户，成立了一家信贷机构，可惜这家机构始终未能成功。不过，德意志银行的章程在第二条中明确写道："公司的宗旨是经营各类银行业务，特别是支持并促进德国与其他欧洲国家及海外市场之间的商业往来。" <sup>19</sup> 因此，德意志银行最初采取的措施之一，便是于 1871 年 3 月与其他德国及伦敦银行共同参与创立了伦敦德国银行。德意志银行认购了其 60 万英镑资本中约四分之一的股份，但这家企业始终未能取得显著成功，因而于 1879 年出售了这些股份。 <sup>20</sup> 在此期间，德意志银行通过于 1871-72 年在不来梅和汉堡设立代理处，以及 1872 年 5 月在远东上海和横滨设立代理处，持续进行地域扩张。德意志银行在伦敦的正式代理处于 1873 年 3 月开业，其业务活动将在本章后续内容中进一步讨论。</span></span></span>

The founding of the two agencies in China and Japan was certainly due to the initiative of Georg Siemens and Hermann Wallich, the two leading figures in the Deutsche Bank's directorate. Wallich had been active for the <span class="italic">Comptoir d'Escompte de Paris</span> in China and Japan between 1867 and 1870 and had become an expert in Far Eastern trade and monetary conditions.<sup>21</sup> The Shanghai and Yokohama agencies of the Deutsche Bank did not come up to initial expectations and had to be closed down two years later. They made a considerable profit by selling silver from German government stocks since the newly-founded Empire planned to adopt the gold standard - which it effectively did in 1875. On the other hand, heavy losses occurred due to the onset of the world-wide depression which also had its repercussions in Far Eastern trade; there were losses caused by the rapid decline of the price of silver in these markets too.<sup>22</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">德意志银行在中国和日本设立两家代理机构，无疑要归功于其董事会的两位核心人物——Georg Siemens 和 Hermann Wallich。Wallich 曾在 1867 至 1870 年间代表巴黎贴现银行（Comptoir d'Escompte de Paris）活跃于中日两地，并由此成为远东贸易与货币领域的专家。 <sup>21</sup> 德意志银行的上海和横滨代理机构并未达到最初的预期，两年后便被迫关闭。不过，它们通过出售德国政府储备的白银赚取了可观利润，因为新成立的德意志帝国计划改用金本位制（并于 1875 年正式实施）。另一方面，全球性经济危机的爆发给远东贸易带来了冲击，导致严重亏损；同时，白银价格在这些市场的急剧下跌也造成了损失。 <sup>22</sup> </span></span></span>

The Western hemisphere experienced a similar expansion, here appropriate overseas banks, not just agencies, were established. In 1872 the Disconto-Gesellschaft of Berlin, at that moment the most prestigious of the great German banks, founded almost two decades earlier than the newcomer Deutsche Bank, established the Deutsch-Belgische La Plata Bank together with the Cologne private banking firm of Sal. Oppenheim Jr. & Co., the Austrian <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_103"></span>Credit-Anstalt and several Belgian banks,<sup>23</sup> The La Plata Bank had branches in Buenos Aires and Montevideo. Two years later, in 1874, the founders sold one-third of its total shares to the Deutsche Bank. 'It was a bad bargain for the Deutsche Bank', Hermann Wallich wrote in his memoirs four decades later.<sup>24</sup> As a matter of fact, the La Plata Bank had invested a large part of its capital in Uruguayan state bonds and the new government which came to power in Montevideo at the end of 1874 refused to honour its debts. Interest payments were resumed a year afterwards but the debt itself was not redeemed and consequently the value of the La Plata Bank shares in the Deutsche Bank portfolio had to be written off to a third of their original value. It was only in 1884 that the Uruguayan government consented to a conversion of its old debt into a new series of bonds which the Deutsche Bank could then sell in the market at a reasonable price. The La Plata Bank was wound up during the following year, and it is not entirely clear whether the Deutsche Bank ended up without any losses.<sup>25</sup> This unlucky experience seemed to prove, however, that an overseas engagement of this kind had to be controlled right from the beginning and then entirely by the mother bank itself. Furthermore, in the case of the La Plata Bank, the capital input had been insufficient right from the start.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">西半球也经历了类似的扩张，在这里建立了合适的海外银行，而不仅仅是代理机构。1872 年，当时在德国各大银行中声望最为显赫的柏林贴现公司（Disconto-Gesellschaft）——其成立时间比新秀德意志银行早了近二十年——与科隆的私人银行 Sal. Oppenheim Jr. & Co.、奥地利信贷银行（Austrian Credit-Anstalt）以及几家比利时银行共同创立了德比拉普拉塔银行（Deutsch-Belgische La Plata Bank）。 <sup>23</sup> 拉普拉塔银行在布宜诺斯艾利斯和蒙得维的亚设有分行。两年后的 1874 年，创始人们将其总股本的三分之一出售给了德意志银行。四十年后，赫尔曼·瓦利希（Hermann Wallich）在其回忆录中写道："这对德意志银行来说是一笔糟糕的交易。" <sup>24</sup> 事实上，拉普拉塔银行将其大部分资本投资于乌拉圭国家债券，而 1874 年底在蒙得维的亚上台的新政府拒绝偿还这些债务。 一年后恢复了利息支付，但债务本身并未清偿，因此德意志银行资产负债表上的拉普拉塔银行股份价值不得不减记至原值的三分之一。直到 1884 年，乌拉圭政府才同意将旧债转换为一系列新债券，德意志银行随后得以在市场上以合理价格出售。拉普拉塔银行于次年清算，德意志银行最终是否完全避免损失并不明确。 <sup>25</sup> 然而，这次不幸的经历似乎证明，此类海外业务必须从一开始就由母行自身完全掌控。此外，就拉普拉塔银行而言，其资本投入从一开始就明显不足。</span></span></span>

There was another attempt in October 1886 when the Deutsche Uebersee Bank was founded in Berlin but this time it was to become a long-term success. The new institution was a 100 per cent daughter of the Deutsche Bank; three out of its five directors were also on the board of the mother firm. Only a quarter of its capital of ten million marks was paid in at the beginning; a year later this quota was raised to 35 per cent. "The support of commercial and monetary transactions and the bills of exchange business overseas' figured among its statutory objectives as well as the establishment of overseas agencies and participation in other overseas banks.<sup>26</sup> Consequently, a branch called Banco Alem&n Transattentico, Buenos Aires, was opened in the Argentinian capital in August 1887.<sup>27</sup> There is no space here to follow the development of this new credit institution in detail and, apart from that, unfortunately almost no archival sources have been preserved which could tell us more about internal decision-making and the special relationship between the Uebersee Bank and its mother firm, the Deutsche Bank. It should be said, however, that the first years of its existence were anything but easy, due to the particular problems of its primary host country, the Argentine Republic, to which its activities remained limited for more than a decade, despite its general designation as 'overseas bank'. The complicated <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_104"></span>vicissitudes of the Argentine currency, especially the rather uncontrolled issue of paper pesos during the 1880s, certainly did not facilitate the business of the Buenos Aires branch, which needed a minimum of monetary stability for its international transactions in support of trade.<sup>28</sup> In order to participate more fully in the circulation of <span class="italic">moneda legal</span> (i.e. paper money), the Buenos Aires branch took advantage of a new Argentinian law and became, within narrow limits, a bank of issue in 1887. When a new law was passed three years later which compelled the bank to hand its entire gold reserve over to the Argentinian government, the Banco Alem&n TransatMntico preferred to renounce this privilege.<sup>29</sup> The Argentinian crisis of 1890-1, with its serious repercussions on the European capital market, did not spare the new German branch bank; it survived but for two consecutive years (1891 and 1892) no dividends could be distributed. In 1893, when conditions had finally improved, the Deutsche Uebersee Bank, which then disposed of a paid-in capital of six million marks, was wound up and in June a new bank with a slightly different name, the Deutsche Uberseeische Bank, Berlin, was established. Its nominal capital amounted to twenty million marks of which 40 per cent (i.e. eight million marks), was paid. The entire operation was purely formal and at its origin there were only specific regulations of German commercial law.<sup>30</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1886 年 10 月，柏林又成立了一家德意志海外银行（Deutsche Uebersee Bank），但这一次取得了长期的成功。这家新机构是德意志银行（Deutsche Bank）的全资子公司；其五位董事中有三位同时也在母公司的董事会任职。其 1000 万马克资本中，最初只实缴了四分之一；一年后，这一比例提高到了 35%。"支持商业和货币交易以及海外汇票业务"被列为法定目标，此外还包括设立海外代理机构和参股其他海外银行。 <sup>26</sup> 因此，1887 年 8 月，在阿根廷首都布宜诺斯艾利斯开设了一家名为 Banco Alem&n Transattentico 的分行。 <sup>27</sup> 此处无法详述这家新信贷机构的发展历程，此外遗憾的是，几乎没有任何档案资料留存下来，能够告诉我们关于内部决策以及Übersee Bank 与其母公司 Deutsche Bank 之间特殊关系的更多信息。不过需要指出的是，其成立初期的日子绝非一帆风顺，这主要是由于其首要东道国阿根廷共和国的特殊问题所致——尽管它被称为"海外银行"，但其业务在十多年间一直局限于该国。阿根廷货币的复杂变迁，特别是 1880 年代纸币比索几乎不受控制的发行，无疑给布宜诺斯艾利斯分行的业务带来了困难，因为该行开展支持贸易的国际交易需要最低限度的货币稳定。 <sup>28</sup> 为了更全面地参与法定货币（即纸币）的流通，布宜诺斯艾利斯分行借助阿根廷新颁布的一项法律，于 1887 年在一定限度内成为了一家发行银行。三年后，新法通过，迫使该行将其全部黄金储备上交阿根廷政府，Banco Alem&n TransatMntico 宁愿放弃这一特权。 <sup>29</sup> 1890-91 年的阿根廷危机对欧洲资本市场造成了严重冲击，这家新成立的德国分行也未能幸免；虽然挺了过来，但连续两年（1891 年和 1892 年）无法派发股息。到了 1893 年，情况终于好转时，Deutsche Uebersee Bank（当时实收资本为 600 万马克）被清算，同年 6 月，一家名称略有不同的新银行——柏林的 Deutsche Uberseeische Bank——正式成立。 其名义资本为两千万马克，其中 40%（即八百万马克）已实缴。整个操作纯属形式，其根源仅在于德国商法的特定规定。 <sup>30</sup> </span></span></span>

From the mid-1890s Argentinian development went rather smoothly for several years, and this time was used by the bank for expansion in neighbouring Chile. There a first branch was established in February 1896 at Valparaiso. Nevertheless, here too the first years proved to be quite difficult since in 1898 Chile had to abandon the gold standard, only introduced in 1895. The following one and a half decades were, however, quite successful for the country and for the Banco Alemin TransatMntico (BAT) which profited from Chile's strong position on the world market as a supplier of nitrates and copper - for which prices were steadily rising - and from the presence of a strong group of German emigrants who generally preferred the services of a German branch bank to a local credit institution.<sup>31</sup> The BAT therefore took full advantage of this situation and founded, up until 1913, no less than twelve branch offices and local agencies in Chile. The solidity of its position can be proven by the fact that in 1905 the Chilean government decided to deposit a considerable part of its gold reserves, which were eventually to be used for the reintroduction of the gold standard, at the BAT and with its German competitor the Banco de Chile y Alemania.<sup>32</sup> In the meantime the BAT's territorial expansion did not omit Argentina: gradually new branch <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_105"></span>offices were distributed all over the country and in 1913 it was thus represented in Bahia Blanca, Cordoba, Mendoza, Rosario and Tucuman,<sup>33</sup> even if, according to Hermann Wallich's memoirs, these provincial offices became only marginally successful.<sup>34</sup> Argentina offered the lion's share of the BAT's business, even if we are not able to quantify it, but this does not mean that other countries were neglected. In 1905 an office was opened in Lima, and two others in Bolivia (La Paz and Oruro) in the course of the same year. Again in Peru two other agencies were inaugurated in 1906 (Callao and Arequipa). More important was the Montevideo branch, which was established in the same year, because it functioned as a complement to Argentinian business. Competition from other banks was quite stiff in the Uruguayan capital and the branch management did not act very successfully; consequently, at least until the First World War, the Montevideo office was considered to have been almost a failure. Expansion was also northbound: Brazil was too important a country to be left out, despite the presence of numerous competitors. In 1911 branches were opened in Rio de Janeiro and Sao Paulo, followed by one in Santos two years later.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

Two important moves were made outside of South America: in 1902 a branch was inaugurated in Mexico City, but the view was taken that US capital should participate. From an economic point of view the Spanish branches were much more important. Apart from the Berlin headquarters, these were the only European establishments: Barcelona opened in 1904, Madrid in 1907. In the latter case the private banking firm of Guillermo Vogel y Cia., in which the Deutsche Bank itself had a partnership, was taken over.<sup>35</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

Territorial expansion was, of course, only an expression of the total growth of the bank. In 1900 deposits and credits amounted to 69 million marks. In 1908 this sum had increased considerably to 186 million and in 1913 it was no less than 242 million marks. Capital had to increase accordingly: in 1908 the entire capital of 20 million marks was finally fully paid up; in 1909 capital was increased to 30 million marks and the remaining 10 million were gradually paid up until the beginning of 1913. From 1897 a yearly dividend of 8 per cent was distributed and from 1906 this was increased to 9 per cent. In 1914 in its twenty-seven branches and <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_106"></span>six agencies the bank employed 1,289 people plus another 166 in its Berlin headquarters.<sup>36</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

What interests us most here is the relationship between the Deutsche Bank and its daughter, the Deutsche Uberseeische. The overseas subsidiary functioned more or less as a traditional 'commercial' bank.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

The examples of other German overseas banks show quite clearly, however, that this interesting case cannot be generalized. There is on the one hand the Brasilianische Bank fur Deutschland, founded in 1887 by the Disconto-Gesellschaft and its close ally, the Norddeutsche Bank of Hamburg. Again, its establishment was justified by the need to finance German-Brazilian trade and this was certainly its main business. Until 1907 it mustered eight branches and agencies in Chile and by the same year it had expanded into Bolivia, with a branch at La Paz and another at Oruro. Its economic results were satisfactory, considering that in 1907 only a quarter of its nominal capital of ten million marks had been paid in and, above all, the fierce competition from the Deutsche Uberseeische Bank.<sup>42</sup> If we are to believe Paul Wallich, who visited Chile in 1909, the latter had then far outdistanced the Bank fur Chile und Deutschland and its volume of business in Chile was about twice that of its regional competitor (founded in the same year).<sup>43</sup> The Bank fiir Chile und Deutschland, in any case, limited its activities strictly to commercial banking in the most traditional sense.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，其他德国海外银行的案例清楚地表明，这一特殊案例并不具有普遍性。一方面，由贴现公司及其紧密盟友汉堡北德银行于 1887 年成立的巴西德国银行，其设立理由同样是资助德巴贸易，这确实也是其主要业务。不过，该行在 1889 年为西米纳斯铁路公司谈判了一笔 2200 万马克的贷款，但该贷款随后仍由其母公司贴现公司在德国市场发行。 <sup>39</sup> 除此之外，巴西德国银行的发展虽颇为成功，却远不及德意志海外银行的增长：其 1000 万马克的名义资本直到 1896 年才全部缴足，此后也从未增资。1909 年，该行在里约热内卢、圣保罗、桑托斯、阿雷格里港和巴伊亚设有分行。 <sup>40</sup> 根据其章程，它与两家德国母银行的关系必然极为密切，因为它在欧洲只能通过这两家银行开设正规账户。 <sup>41</sup> 1895 年，同样这两家德国银行（即贴现公司和北德银行）创立了智利与德国银行。与其巴西前身类似，其业务范围至少最初仅局限于一个国家，这与英国的南美银行颇为相似。到 1907 年，它在智利已设有八家分行和代理处，同年还扩展至玻利维亚，在拉巴斯和奥鲁罗各设一家分行。其经济成果令人满意——考虑到 1907 年其 1000 万马克的名义资本中仅实缴了四分之一，且还要面对德意志海外银行的激烈竞争。 <sup>42</sup> 倘若我们相信 1909 年造访智利的保罗·沃利奇所言，当时后者已远远甩开智利与德国银行（Bank fur Chile und Deutschland），其在智利的业务量约为这家同年成立的区域竞争对手的两倍。 <sup>43</sup> 而智利与德国银行（Bank fiir Chile und Deutschland）则始终将业务严格限定在最传统的商业银行范畴内。</span></span></span>

For the sake of completeness we should mention the establishment of the Deutsch-Sudamerikanische Bank in 1906. Its founders were a third group of great German banks: the Dresdner Bank, the Schaaffhausen'scher Bankverein, and the Nationalbank fur Deutschland. In 1907 half of its nominal capital of twenty million marks had already been paid in and branches in Hamburg, Mexico City, Rio de Janeiro and Buenos Aires had been opened. Other ones in Santiago and Valparaiso followed in 1909-10 and were considered there as serious competitors by the long established Deutsche Uberseeische.<sup>44</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">为求完整，还应提及 1906 年成立的德国南美银行（Deutsch-Sudamerikanische Bank）。其创始人是另一批德国大型银行：德累斯顿银行（Dresdner Bank）、沙夫豪森银行联合体（Schaaffhausen'scher Bankverein）以及德国国家银行（Nationalbank fur Deutschland）。到 1907 年，其两千万马克的名义资本中已有一半实缴到位，并在汉堡、墨西哥城、里约热内卢和布宜诺斯艾利斯开设了分行。1909 至 1910 年间，圣地亚哥和瓦尔帕莱索分行相继开业，在当地被历史悠久的德国海外银行（Deutsche Uberseeische）视为劲敌。 <sup>44</sup> </span></span></span>

If we now leave Latin America and move over to East Asia we meet the highly-interesting case of the Deutsch-Asiatische Bank, founded in 1889. This case is very interesting for three reasons:<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">现在我们离开拉丁美洲，转向东亚，会看到一个非常有趣的案例——成立于 1889 年的 Deutsch-Asiatische Bank。这个案例之所以令人关注，主要有三个原因：</span></span></span>

1.  it was founded by a group which consisted of most of the major German banks;<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">它由一群德国主要大银行组成的财团共同创立；</span></span></span>
2.  the German government intervened quite heavily before and during its establishment;<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在成立前后，德国政府都进行了相当深入的大力干预；</span></span></span>
3.  it was much more active in the field of loans and direct investment than the German banking subsidiaries in Latin America.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">相比于德国在拉丁美洲的银行子公司，这家银行在贷款和直接投资领域表现得更为活跃。</span></span></span>

Once the East Asian agencies of the Deutsche Bank had been closed down in 1875, German trade with China and Japan had to rely on its own resources or on foreign banks established in these countries. In 1885 Adolph von Hansemann of the Berlin Disconto-Gesellschaft tried to negotiate an understanding with the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation and with N.M. Rothschild & Sons by which future loans to China, particularly in the railway field, should be shared equally by the British and by a <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_108"></span>group of German banks. The British side refused to join such a syndicate, probably because it felt strong enough to do all the business with China alone.<sup>45</sup> The next initiative was then taken by the Prussian Minister of Finance and by the Auswartiges Amt, the Foreign Office of the Empire who, in November 1887, asked the President of the Seehandlung, a bank owned by the Prussian State, to get in contact with the major German banks in order to find out if and to what degree there was a willingness to concur in the foundation of a German bank in China.<sup>46</sup> The immediate reason indicated was the fact that Krupp had obtained a substantial contract from the Chinese authorities for the delivery of cannons but that the entire transaction had to be financed by the British firm of Jardine, Matheson & Co., which also represented Krupp's main competitors Armstrong and Schneider-Le Creusot. The German diplomats in China had pointed out that in this way foreign competitors could be preferred to German firms from one moment to the other and that, in any case, success in negotiations with the Chinese government always depended on the simultaneous provision of finance.<sup>47</sup> The President of the Prussian Seehandlung, who had been charged to look into the problem of the foundation of a German China Bank, soon found out that two banking groups opposed each other: The Disconto-Gesellschaft and the Deutsche Bank, who for once were united against the Berliner Handelsgesellschaft (the bank which possibly came closest to the British type of merchant bank) together with two private banking firms: Warschauer of Berlin and Jacob S.H. Stern of Frankfurt. Nevertheless, President Burchard managed to bring the two groups together after only a few weeks had elapsed, at the beginning of December 1887,<sup>48</sup> Again a few weeks later two other prestigious houses, the Bank fur Handel und Industrie (the so-called Darmstadter Bank) and the Berlin private banking firm Mendelssohn & Co. were admitted even though they were not members of one of the two groups of founders; a quota of 6 per cent each was assigned to them.<sup>49</sup> For reasons of internal policy of the Reich, a few months later even the Bavarian government was asked to nominate Bavarian banks able and willing to participate in the foundation of the future China Bank. When the foundation contract was finally signed at the beginning of 1889, one bank from Bavaria, the Bayerische Hypotheken- und Wechselbank joined with a share of 3.5 per cent.<sup>50</sup> In the end, two problems came together and blocked the establishment of the envisaged bank for an entire year:<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1875 年德意志银行的东亚分支机构关闭后，德国与中国、日本的贸易只能依靠自身资源，或是借助这两国境内设立的外国银行。1885 年，柏林贴现公司的阿道夫·冯·汉泽曼试图与汇丰银行及 N.M.罗斯柴尔德父子银行达成协议，拟将未来对华贷款（特别是铁路领域）由英方与德国银行集团均分。但英方拒绝了这一联合贷款团的提议，原因很可能是其自认实力足以独揽对华全部业务。 <sup>45</sup> 普鲁士财政部长与帝国外交部（Auswartiges Amt）随即采取了下一步行动。1887 年 11 月，他们要求普鲁士国家银行（Seehandlung）行长与德国各大银行接洽，以了解各方是否愿意以及愿意在多大程度上共同参与在中国建立一家德国银行。 <sup>46</sup> 此举的直接原因是，克虏伯（Krupp）公司从中国当局获得了一份大额火炮供应合同，但整笔交易却不得不由英国公司怡和洋行（Jardine, Matheson & Co.）提供融资，而这家公司同时也代理着克虏伯的主要竞争对手——阿姆斯特朗（Armstrong）和施耐德-勒克勒佐（Schneider-Le Creusot）的业务。 德国驻华外交官曾指出，外国竞争者随时可能比德国公司更受青睐，而且与中国政府谈判的成功始终有赖于同步提供融资。 <sup>47</sup> 受命研究成立一家德国中国银行的普鲁士海外贸易公司总裁很快发现，两个银行集团相互对立：Disconto-Gesellschaft 和德意志银行，它们难得地联合起来，反对柏林商业银行（这家银行或许最接近英国式的商业银行）以及两家私人银行：柏林的 Warschauer 和法兰克福的 Jacob S.H. Stern。 尽管如此，仅过了几周，到 1887 年 12 月初，布尔查德行长就成功将两派撮合到了一起。 <sup>48</sup> 又过了几周，另外两家显赫的金融机构——商业与工业银行（即所谓的达姆施塔特银行）和柏林私人银行门德尔松公司——也被接纳进来，尽管它们并不属于两个创始人集团中的任何一个，各获得了 6%的份额。 <sup>49</sup> 出于德意志帝国内部政策的考虑，几个月后，甚至巴伐利亚政府也被要求提名那些有能力且有意参与未来中国银行创立的巴伐利亚银行。1889 年初，当创立合同最终签署时，巴伐利亚的一家银行——巴伐利亚抵押与汇兑银行——加入进来，获得了 3.5%的股份。 <sup>50</sup> 然而最终，两个问题同时出现，使得这家拟议中的银行的成立被整整搁置了一年：</span></span></span>

1.  Despite the declared desire of all the other joint-stock and <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_109"></span>private banking houses that the Prussian Seehandlung should actively participate in the establishment of the new bank and subscribe a quota of its shares, the Prussian bank kept on refusing such an engagement on the grounds of its highly-conservative investment policy.<sup>51</sup> The other banks, of course, wanted its participation as an official acknowledgement and a pledge of the Reich's interest in the whole affair.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管其他所有股份制和私人银行都公开表示希望普鲁士海外贸易公司（Prussian Seehandlung）积极参与新银行的筹建并认购一定比例的股份，但普鲁士银行始终以自身极其保守的投资政策为由，拒绝承担这一义务。 <sup>51</sup> 其他银行当然希望它的参与能作为一种官方认可，并以此表明德意志帝国对整个事件抱有利益承诺。</span></span></span>
2.  Given the absolute predominance of silver in the Chinese currency system, the new bank was required to have its capital in silver. This was supposed to be supplied by the Reichsbank and would then eventually be paid back in gold, but only after a period of twenty years. The Reichsbank and the Prussian Minister of Finance were opposed to this project which had been approved by Bismarck himself; it was therefore abandoned by the founding banks themselves.<sup>52</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">鉴于白银在中国货币体系中占据绝对主导地位，新银行被要求以白银作为资本金。这笔白银本应由德国帝国银行（Reichsbank）提供，并计划在二十年后以黄金形式偿还。然而，尽管这一方案已得到俾斯麦本人的批准，德国帝国银行和普鲁士财政部长却均持反对意见；最终，发起成立该银行的各家银行自己放弃了这一计划。 <sup>52</sup> </span></span></span>

In January 1889 the Prussian Seehandlung finally decided to subscribe to the capital of the new China Bank, and this decision, which meant quite a change in its policy, was certainly influenced by the considerable pressure exercised by the Foreign Ministry and by the Chancellor himself.<sup>53</sup> The new bank, which was finally established on 12 February 1889, received the name Deutsch-Asiatische Bank; its seat was Shanghai. Its capital, of which one-quarter was paid in, consisted of five million Shanghai Taels, the Chinese silver currency's unit of calculation; at the time this sum corresponded roughly to twenty million goldmarks. The most important subscribers of the 5,000 shares of the Deutsch-Asiatische Bank were the Disconto-Gesellschaft, the Deutsche Bank and Bleichroder with 555 shares each. The Prussian Seehandlung, which had been so much courted, subscribed to 175 shares. The Reich's role in the entire affair was underlined by a statutory rule which required the President of the Supervisory Council of the Deutsch-Asiatische to be confirmed, after his election, by the Emperor himself.<sup>54</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1889 年 1 月，普鲁士海外贸易公司终于决定认购新成立的中华银行的股本，这一决定意味着其政策发生了重大转变，而外交部乃至宰相本人施加的巨大压力无疑对此产生了影响。 <sup>53</sup> 这家新银行最终于 1889 年 2 月 12 日成立，取名 Deutsch-Asiatische Bank，总部设在上海。其资本额为五百万上海银两——中国白银货币的计算单位，其中四分之一已实缴；当时这笔金额大约相当于两千万金马克。认购 Deutsch-Asiatische Bank 五千股股份的主要股东中，Disconto-Gesellschaft、Deutsche Bank 和 Bleichroder 各认购 555 股。此前备受追捧的普鲁士海外贸易公司则认购了 175 股。 帝国在整个事件中的作用通过一项法定规则得以凸显：该规则要求德亚银行监事会主席在当选后，必须由皇帝本人亲自确认。 <sup>54</sup> </span></span></span>

There is no time here to describe the activities and the development of this overseas bank in detail. Let us say, however, that its first years were rather difficult since the Far Eastern market was full of competitors from other European countries and since the German trading houses preferred to stay, at least during the first years of the bank's existence, with their British banking partners.<sup>55</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在此无法详述这家海外银行的活动与发展历程。但可以说，其最初几年颇为艰难——一方面，远东市场早已充斥来自其他欧洲国家的竞争者；另一方面，至少在银行成立的头几年，德国商行更倾向于继续与它们原有的英国银行合作伙伴保持业务往来。 <sup>55</sup> </span></span></span>

Its main competitor, the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation, always counted German Far East merchants among its directors: in 1914 there were no less than four of them on its board in Hong Kong.<sup>56</sup> The legal seat and the head office of the Deutsch-Asiatische were, as already mentioned, established in <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_110"></span>Shanghai; the board met in Berlin, and it was there that the strategic decisions were taken. This was exactly the same as with the Hongkong Bank which had its head office in Hong Kong and held board meetings in London.<sup>57</sup> It seems that the profit situation of the Deutsch-Asiatische only improved enduringly after it managed to participate in the Chinese State loan business. In this respect a decisive role must be attributed to the understanding which it reached in 1895 with the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation which also guaranteed parity between British and German banks in all future loan agreements with the Chinese government. Accordingly, the so-called second and third indemnity loans of 1896 and 1898, which served to pay reparations to Japan, were introduced by a syndicate consisting of these two banks as leaders, and other loans followed.<sup>58</sup> The collaboration of the two banks was undoubtedly quite successful and, until 1910, 'no private banking partnership shared top place in the business with the Hongkong and Shanghai and the Deutsch-Asiatische'.<sup>59</sup> From 1911, the Deutsch-Asiatische participated in loans which comprised more than two national banking groups, for instance the Hukuang 'Four Power' railway loan contracted in May 1911 by members of British, German, French and US syndicates as well as the so-called Reorganization Loan of 1913 granted by a consortium consisting of the Deutsch-Asiatische, the Hongkong Bank, the Banque de l'lndochine, the Russo-Asiatic Bank and the Yokohama Specie Bank while the US banking group had withdrawn because their government had asked them to do so.<sup>60</sup> From 1898, when Germany forced China to hand over the Bay of Tsingtao, with a 99-year lease, the Deutsch-Asiatische became increasingly involved in direct investment. In 1899 the Shantung Railway Company was founded and became fairly successful. A year later the Shantung Mining Company was started but this turned out to be quite a failure. Other railway investments were undertaken from 1908 onwards. If total German capital invested in China in 1914 amounted to about 1.1 million marks (equivalent to about 5 per cent of all German investments abroad and 16.7 per cent of all foreign investments in China), the role of the Deutsch-Asiatische Bank had certainly been an important one in the developments.<sup>61</sup> In 1913 it possessed, besides its head office in Shanghai and offices in Berlin and Hamburg, seven other branches and agencies in China, two in Japan (Kobe and Yokohama) and one each at Calcutta and Singapore; its capital amounted to 7.5 million Shanghai Taels.<sup>62</sup> The dividends had reached 10 per cent in 1903 and 1904 and even 11 in 1905 but then they continually went down and in 1911 and 1912 they amounted to only 5 per <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_111"></span>cent, increasing slightly to 7 per cent in 1913.<sup>63</sup> It is difficult to say whether this development was mainly a consequence of the politically-unstable situation in China itself during these years or if there were other important factors involved - like, for instance, an exaggerated dependency on the results of the loan business and of direct investment.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">其主要竞争对手汇丰银行，其董事会中向来不乏德国远东商人：1914 年时，该行香港董事会中竟有四位德国成员。 <sup>56</sup> 如前所述，德意志亚洲银行的法定注册地与总部均设在上海；但其董事会会议却在柏林召开，重大战略决策也在此做出。这与汇丰银行的情况如出一辙——该行总部设在香港，董事会会议却在伦敦举行。 <sup>57</sup> 德意志亚洲银行的盈利状况似乎只有在成功参与中国国家贷款业务后才得以持续改善。在这方面，1895 年它与汇丰银行达成的共识起到了决定性作用，该共识确保了英德两国银行在未来所有对华政府贷款协议中享有平等地位。 因此，1896 年和 1898 年所谓的第二、第三次赔偿贷款（用于支付对日赔款）是由以这两家银行为首的银团推出的，随后还有其他贷款。 <sup>58</sup> 这两家银行的合作无疑相当成功，直到 1910 年，“在银行业务中，没有哪家私营银行合伙能与汇丰银行和德华银行平起平坐”。 <sup>59</sup> 从 1911 年起，德华银行参与了由两个以上国家银行集团组成的贷款，例如 1911 年 5 月由英、德、法、美四国银团成员签订的湖广“四国”铁路贷款，以及 1913 年由德华银行、汇丰银行、东方汇理银行、俄亚银行和横滨正金银行组成的财团提供的所谓“善后大借款”，而美国银团因应政府要求已退出。 <sup>60</sup> 自 1898 年德国迫使中国以 99 年租期割让青岛湾起，德华银行在直接投资领域的参与日益加深。1899 年成立的山东铁路公司取得了相当的商业成功，而次年创立的山东矿务公司却以失败告终。自 1908 年起，该行还涉足了其他铁路投资项目。至 1914 年，德国在华投资总额约达 110 万马克（相当于德国对外投资总量的 5%，以及外国在华投资总额的 16.7%），在此发展进程中，德华银行无疑扮演了举足轻重的角色。 <sup>61</sup> 1913 年，除上海总行及柏林、汉堡分行外，该行在中国设有七家分支机构与代理处，在日本（神户与横滨）设有两处，并在加尔各答和新加坡各设一处；其资本总额达 750 万上海规银两。 <sup>62</sup> 股息在 1903 年和 1904 年达到了 10%，1905 年甚至达到 11%，但随后持续下降，1911 和 1912 年仅为 5%，1913 年小幅回升至 7%。 <sup>63</sup> 很难判断这一趋势主要是由于当时中国国内政治局势的不稳定，还是涉及其他重要因素——比如对贷款业务和直接投资结果的过度依赖。</span></span></span>

Just for the sake of completeness, two other overseas banks should be mentioned here. The Deutsche Palastina Bank, founded in 1899 by the Berlin private banking firm von der Heydt, never became very important, probably also because its founding bank was too small to give it much support, but nevertheless it did fairly well financially.<sup>64</sup> The other institute that should be mentioned is the Deutsche Orientbank, established in 1905 by the Dresdner Bank, the A. Schaaffhausen'sche Bankverein and the National-bank fur Deutschland. It grew rapidly and in 1911 disposed of a fully paid-in capital of thirty-two million marks. It had no fewer than seventeen branch offices and agencies, most of them in the Ottoman Empire, but there was also one in Casablanca and one in Tangiers.<sup>65</sup> It would be difficult to deny that it fitted nicely into the Middle East policy of the Wilhelmine Empire.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">为了确保完整性，这里还应提及另外两家海外银行。1899 年由柏林私营银行 von der Heydt 创办的 Deutsche Palastina Bank，始终未能占据重要地位，很可能是因为其创办银行规模太小而无法提供足够支持，不过其财务状况相当稳健。 <sup>64</sup> 另一家值得提及的机构是 Deutsche Orientbank，1905 年由 Dresdner Bank、A. Schaaffhausen'sche Bankverein 和 National-bank fur Deutschland 联合创立。该行发展迅速，到 1911 年已拥有 3200 万马克的实缴资本。其分支机构多达 17 处，大部分位于奥斯曼帝国境内，但也在卡萨布兰卡和丹吉尔设有办事处。 <sup>65</sup> 很难否认这家银行与威廉二世帝国在中东的政策配合得天衣无缝。</span></span></span>

There was also a group of colonial banks, founded purposely for the various German colonies in Africa by one or other of the great Berlin banks or, as in the case of the Handelsbank fur Ostafrika, established in 1911, by a group of these Institutes.<sup>66</sup> They never gained much importance since the German colonies remained almost insignificant for the economy of the Reich. In 1905 only 0.81 per cent of all German exports went to the colonies; in 1913 this percentage had diminished still more to a bare 0.57.<sup>67</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">当时还有一批殖民地银行，是由柏林的大银行或某些金融机构（如 1911 年成立的东非商业银行 Handelsbank fur Ostafrika）专门为德国在非洲的各殖民地建立的。 <sup>66</sup> 但这些银行始终未成气候，因为德国殖民地本身对帝国内经济而言几乎微不足道。1905 年，德国出口总额中只有 0.81%流向殖民地；到了 1913 年，这一比例更是跌至仅 0.57%。 <sup>67</sup> </span></span></span>

The role of agencies abroad<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">海外机构的作用</span></span></span>
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The fact that the Deutsche Bank had already set up its own agency in London in 1873 has already been mentioned. London's worldwide importance for the financing of international trade through acceptance credit cannot be overestimated, and the part of the Deutsche Bank London Agency in the entire volume of acceptance credits of the Deutsche Bank must always have been considerable, even If it cannot be exactly quantified from available sources.<sup>68</sup> Two other great German banks, the Dresdner Bank in 1895 and the Disconto-Gesellschaft in 1899, also opened agencies in the British capital, and this could serve as another indicator of the growing interdependence between the German and the British, as well as between the German and the world economy. At the beginning of 1910 the Deutsche Bank opened a <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_112"></span>branch office in Brussels.<sup>69</sup> There can be no doubt that this move originated from the growing importance of the Belgian capital market for the financing of German business and particularly of German business abroad. Even German government officials had to admit that 'capitalisation \[was\] nowhere as favourable as in Brussels', given the 'easy' terms of the Belgian law of 1873 on joint-stock companies, the comparatively low Belgian taxes and the fact that French capital was, precisely for these reasons, increasingly emigrating to the Belgian market.<sup>70</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">德意志银行早在 1873 年便在伦敦设立了代理处，这一点之前已经提及。伦敦凭借承兑信用证在国际贸易融资方面具有全球性的重要意义，其作用无论如何强调都不为过。而德意志银行伦敦代理处在其整个承兑信用证业务量中所占的份额必定相当可观，尽管现有资料无法精确量化。 <sup>68</sup> 另外两家德国大型银行——德累斯顿银行（Dresdner Bank）于 1895 年、贴现公司（Disconto-Gesellschaft）于 1899 年——也先后在伦敦开设了代理处，这进一步印证了德国与英国经济之间、以及德国与世界经济之间日益增强的相互依存关系。1910 年初，德意志银行又在布鲁塞尔设立了一家分行。 <sup>69</sup> 毫无疑问，这一举措源于比利时资本市场对德国企业（尤其是德国海外业务）融资的重要性与日俱增。就连德国政府官员也不得不承认，鉴于比利时 1873 年《股份公司法》的宽松条款、相对较低的税收，以及法国资本正是因此不断涌入比利时市场，“任何地方的资本化条件都不如布鲁塞尔优越”。 <sup>70</sup> </span></span></span>

A few months before, in the summer of 1909, a branch office of the Deutsche Bank had been established in Istanbul, the capital of the Ottoman Empire, and this was certainly just a logical consequence of its ever-growing engagement in the Turkish railway sector and in connected infrastructural investments.<sup>71</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">几个月前，即 1909 年夏天，德意志银行在奥斯曼帝国首都伊斯坦布尔设立了一家分行。这无疑是在其日益深入参与土耳其铁路行业及相关基础设施投资的背景下，水到渠成之举。 <sup>71</sup> </span></span></span>

The role of foreign partnerships<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">外国合伙企业的作用</span></span></span>
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Participation in already-existing foreign banks - by means of partnerships or, if the other bank is organized as a joint-stock company, share holding - is without any doubt (historically speaking) the oldest form of expansion abroad. All great German banks were engaged in such enterprises in one way or another: we shall just give some very few examples. The oldest cases are provided by the Bank fdr Handel und Industrie, the so-called Darmstadter Bank, founded in 1853. By 1854 it had already entered into a partnership with a New York bank and in 1857 with a banking house in Paris. In the 1860s a partnership was established in Vienna and during the next decade participation in Dutch, Italian and Hungarian banks followed. Of particular importance, because of its stake in the oil business, was the Bucharest bank of Marmorosch, Blank & Co. in which the Darmstadter Bank participated in 1890 and collaborated in 1905, together with the Berliner Handelsgesellschaft and, shortly afterwards, with the Banque de Paris et des Pays-Bas, in the transformation of the Bucharest house into a joint-stock company.<sup>72</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">以合伙形式参与现有外资银行，或当对方为股份公司时通过持股方式参与——从历史角度看，这无疑是银行拓展海外业务最古老的形式。所有德国大银行都曾以这样或那样的方式涉足此类事业，我们仅举几个例子。最早的案例来自 1853 年成立的工商银行（即所谓的达姆施塔特银行）。1854 年，它便与一家纽约银行建立了合伙关系，1857 年又与巴黎的一家银行行号合作。19 世纪 60 年代，它在维也纳建立了合伙关系，随后十年间又陆续参与了荷兰、意大利和匈牙利的多家银行。其中，布加勒斯特的 Marmorosch, Blank & Co.银行因涉足石油业务而尤为重要。 达姆施塔特银行于 1890 年参与其中，并于 1905 年与柏林贸易公司合作，不久后又与巴黎荷兰银行合作，将布加勒斯特的公司改组为一家股份公司。 <sup>72</sup> </span></span></span>

We should just mention here the cases of two other great Berlin banks: the Deutsche Bank had partnerships in private banking houses in New York, Paris and Vienna; these were all started in the 1870s. The last important partnership seems to have been the one started in 1895 in the firm of Guillermo Vogel, Madrid, which then, in 1905, merged with the Deutsch-Uberseeische.<sup>73</sup> In 1890 the Disconto-Gesellschaft took partnerships in the Buenos Aires firm of Ernesto Tornquist and in the Antwerp banking house of Albert de Bary. In 1897 another one, together with S. Bleichroder, <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_113"></span>followed in the Banca Generala Romana, Bucharest, and in 1898, together with other German and foreign banks, it participated in the foundation of the Banque Internationale de Bruxelles. Finally, in 1905 it collaborated with its long-time partners S. Bleichroder and the Norddeutsche Bank, in establishing the Banque de Credit at Sofia.<sup>74</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这里我们只需提及另外两家柏林大银行的情况：德意志银行在纽约、巴黎和维也纳的私人银行都建立了合作关系，这些合作均始于 19 世纪 70 年代。最后一项重要的合作似乎是 1895 年与马德里 Guillermo Vogel 公司达成的，后者于 1905 年与德意志海外银行合并。 <sup>73</sup> 1890 年，Disconto-Gesellschaft 与布宜诺斯艾利斯的 Ernesto Tornquist 公司以及安特卫普的 Albert de Bary 银行建立了合作关系。1897 年，它又联合 S. Bleichroder 在布加勒斯特的 Banca Generala Romana 进行合作；1898 年，它与德国及其他外国银行共同参与了布鲁塞尔国际银行的创立。最终，在 1905 年，它与长期合作伙伴 S. Bleichroder 及 Norddeutsche Bank 合作，在索菲亚成立了信贷银行。 <sup>74</sup> </span></span></span>

These various strategies of participation must have been aimed at gaining at least an outpost in a foreign country which could guarantee a steady stream of information on local monetary and investment conditions. Such a hold could furthermore serve as a link to the banking community and, if necessary, to the political authorities of that country. There was, of course, also the possibility to risk more considerable participation in an important foreign credit institution which already existed or which had still to be created. Such an investment carried quite some weight and could enable the investor to gain positions in national loan syndicates of that foreign country which he would otherwise never have been able to obtain.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些多样化的参与策略，其目的必然是在海外至少占据一个前哨据点，从而确保能持续获取当地货币与投资状况的信息。这样的立足点还能成为联系该国银行界的纽带，必要时甚至能对接其政治高层。当然，也存在另一种可能——对一家已存在或尚待筹建的重要外国信贷机构加大投资力度。此类投资分量十足，足以让投资者在该国的国家贷款银团中占据一席之地，而这是通过其他途径永远难以企及的。</span></span></span>

A good example in this respect is the participation by the Deutsche Bank in 1874 in the Wiener Bank-Verein. The Viennese bank, founded in 1869, had run into trouble during the months which followed the crisis of 1873 and had to be 'reorganized' by a group of German banks, among them the Deutsche Bank. Out of this a steady co-operation between the Wiener Bank-Verein and the Deutsche Bank developed during the following decades, the latter thus getting a more direct access to the Austrian capital market. The Vienna bank, on the other hand, became involved in numerous international syndicates, thanks to its connections with the powerful Berlin bank.<sup>75</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这方面的一个典型例子是，德意志银行于 1874 年入股维也纳银行联合会。这家成立于 1869 年的维也纳银行在 1873 年危机后的几个月里陷入困境，不得不由包括德意志银行在内的一组德国银行进行"重组"。此后几十年间，维也纳银行联合会与德意志银行逐渐发展出稳定的合作关系，后者借此更直接地进入了奥地利资本市场。而维也纳银行则凭借与这家强大的柏林银行的联系，参与了许多国际银团。 <sup>75</sup> </span></span></span>

Not all such links developed in the same way. In a number of cases an initial participation in the capital of a foreign bank was not followed up and lost, as time went on, much of its relative importance. Informal links, very often underlined by some representation on the board of such a foreign bank, persisted nevertheless. An excellent illustration is offered by the case of the Banca Commerciale Italiana, founded at Milan in 1894 by all of the major Berlin banks, the Austrian Credit-Anstalt, the Wiener Bank-Verein and the Credit Suisse. Half a decade later the German participation in the Milanese bank had already greatly diminished, whereas a certain amount of French capital via the Banque de Paris et des Pays-Bas had participated in the meantime. By 1914 the German quota had shrunk to not much more than 1 per cent of the share capital and the majority of the Banca Commerciale was by now safely in Italian hands. Nevertheless <span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_114"></span>even then almost half of the the seats on the <span class="italic">Commerciale's</span> board were held by German, Swiss, Austrian and French bankers who thus, and by their sheer presence, represented, so to speak, the guarantee of the international <span class="italic">haute banque</span> to this newcomer to the European banking community. Interestingly enough the Banca Commerciale's two top managers were of German origin and the bank itself was managed according to the principles and the practices of German 'mixed' banking.<sup>76</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">并非所有此类联系都以相同方式发展。有些案例中，最初对一家外国银行的资本参与并未持续跟进，随着时间推移，其相对重要性逐渐丧失。然而，非正式联系（往往通过在该外国银行董事会中的代表席位得以体现）依然长期存在。1894 年由柏林各大银行、奥地利 Credit-Anstalt、Wiener Bank-Verein 和 Credit Suisse 在米兰创立的 Banca Commerciale Italiana 便是一个绝佳例证。五年后，德国在该米兰银行的参与已大幅减少，而与此同时，通过 Banque de Paris et des Pays-Bas，一定数量的法国资本得以介入。到 1914 年，德国的持股比例已缩减至股本总额的仅 1%多一点，而 Banca Commerciale 的多数股权此时已牢牢掌握在意大利人手中。 即使在当时，意大利商业银行（Commerciale）董事会的近半数席位仍由德国、瑞士、奥地利及法国银行家掌控——可以说，他们仅凭其存在，便代表国际高级银行业向这位欧洲金融界的新成员提供了某种担保。有趣的是，该行两位最高管理者均为德裔，而银行本身也完全按照"德国式混业银行"的原则与实践进行运营。 <sup>76</sup> </span></span></span>

Up to a certain extent, a comparable situation could be found in Tsarist Russia. There a group of German and Austrian banks, and above all the Berlin Disconto-Gesellschaft, belonged in 1869 to the founders of the St Petersburg International Commerce Bank. When, two years later, another important credit institution, the Russian Foreign Trade Bank, was founded in St Petersburg, no German bank took part, but when it increased its capital in 1881 the Deutsche Bank and the Wiener Bank-Verein participated, and it is known that immediately afterwards they held about a quarter of its shares.<sup>77</sup> Contemporary diplomatic sources<sup>78</sup> as well as P.V. Ol' in his well-known study on 'Foreign Capital in Russia', published in 1922,<sup>79</sup> but more recently also Soviet historians<sup>80</sup> insisted on the fact of German control of these two and some other, albeit smaller, Russian banks until the outbreak of the First World War. Only recently the East German historian Heinz Lemke has, however, tried to show that there is really no proof of effective German control over each of these banks, even if one can find in most of these cases a minority of shares held directly by German owners or held by owners who were represented via German banks.<sup>81</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在一定程度上，沙皇俄国也存在类似情况。1869 年，一批德国和奥地利银行，尤其是柏林贴现公司，参与了圣彼得堡国际商业银行的创立。两年后，当另一家重要的信贷机构——俄罗斯外贸银行在圣彼得堡成立时，并无德国银行参与，但 1881 年该行增资时，德意志银行和维也纳银行联合会均参与其中，且据悉此后不久它们便持有该行约四分之一的股份。 <sup>77</sup> 当代外交史料 <sup>78</sup> 、P.V. 奥尔在其 1922 年出版的著名研究《俄国境内的外资》 <sup>79</sup> ，以及近年来的苏联史学家 <sup>80</sup> 均强调，直至第一次世界大战爆发前，德国实际上控制着这两家乃至其他一些规模较小的俄国银行。 然而，直到最近，东德历史学家海因茨·莱姆克才试图表明，即便在大多数情况下能发现少数股份由德国所有者直接持有，或由通过德国银行代表的所有者持有，实际上也并无确凿证据证明德国对这些银行实施了有效控制。 <sup>81</sup> </span></span></span>

Conclusion<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">结论</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If we try to transfer the concept of multinational business from manufacturing and a few service industries to a sector like banking, then we have to acknowledge that only some of the cases and developments mentioned above fit into such a framework. Of course, all of the agencies named in the second section of this chapter will qualify but not all of the overseas banks - for instance, not the Deutsch-Asiatische Bank - and probably very few of the partnerships, since effective control, exercised by a partner from abroad, must have been the exception, especially in the case of larger banks involved. Direct control was probably much more important in the case of commercial banking where continuous transactions were carried out. It was less important in all cases of merchant banking where a co-operative relationship could substi<span id="Ch06.xhtml_page_115"></span>tute for internalization. Only in those cases where the overseas bank depended clearly on a single domestic bank can a clear-cut precondition for multinational integration be demonstrated. The true degree of internalization within German international banking can only be guessed at, since internal sources for the development of German overseas banking are mostly non-existent. Nevertheless the illustrations given so far should provide us with at least a rough idea of the degree of world-wide economic interdependence already achieved well before the outbreak of the First World War,<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如果试图将跨国业务的概念从制造业和部分服务业转移到银行业这样的领域，那么我们必须承认，上述案例和发展中只有一部分符合这一框架。当然，本章第二节提到的所有机构都符合条件，但并非所有海外银行——例如，德亚银行就不算——而且很可能只有极少数合伙制银行符合要求，因为由海外合伙人行使有效控制必须是例外情况，尤其是在涉及较大银行的情况下。对于进行连续性交易的商业银行而言，直接控制可能更为重要。而在所有商人银行业务中，直接控制的重要性较低，因为合作关系可以替代内部化。 只有当海外银行明确依赖于某一家国内银行时，才能充分证明跨国整合的前提条件存在。德国国际银行业真正的内部化程度只能靠推测，因为关于德国海外银行业发展的内部资料几乎不存在。 然而，迄今为止提供的例证至少能让我们对第一次世界大战爆发前已经达到的全球经济相互依存程度有一个大致概念。</span></span></span>
