Paradise lost? British banks in Australia<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">第四章 失乐园？英国银行在澳大利亚</span></span></span>](#contents.xhtml_bck_ht0034)

D.T. Merrett

Many of the largest banks in the world today operate on a global basis. Foreign direct investment in financial services has increased markedly since the Second World War. This resurgence in multinational banking is attributed to many interconnected factors.<sup>1</sup> However, multinational banking has a long pedigree.<sup>2</sup> Many foreign banks, the majority of whom were domiciled in the United Kingdom, operated in the developing regions of the world from the 1830s onwards. The spread of British foreign banks has been well documented. However, for the most part, the process of outward expansion has been described rather than explained.<sup>3</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如今，全球许多大型银行都在开展国际业务。自二战以来，金融服务领域的外国直接投资显著增长。跨国银行业的复兴归因于许多相互关联的因素。 <sup>1</sup> 然而，跨国银行业务有着悠久的历史渊源。 <sup>2</sup> 自 19 世纪 30 年代起，许多外国银行（其中大多数总部设在英国）就开始在全球发展中地区运营。英国海外银行的扩张已有详细记载。但总体而言，这一对外扩张过程更多是被描述而非解释。 <sup>3</sup> </span></span></span>

This chapter focuses on the experiences of those British banks who entered Australia in the second and third quarters of the nineteenth century and whose sole survivor still plays a prominent part in its banking. These nine banks formed a significant part of the broader sweep of the imperial and foreign banks that connected many parts of the developing world with the United Kingdom. The coming of the Anglo-Australian banks, or Anglos as they were commonly known in the Antipodes, raises a number of interesting questions concerning their motives, and their performance relative to the locally-domiciled banks with whom they coḿpeted. What prompted them to come and to stay? Did the British banks have any particular competences that were superior to those of the local banks? Did the foreign and local banks compete in the same markets? Was the performance of British banks, whether measured in terms of market share or financial returns on their capital and assets, superior, worse or no better than the indigenous banks? Why was there a hiatus of over 100 years between the commencement of business by the Bank of Queensland in 1863 and the next influx by British financial institutions into Australia?<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本章聚焦于 19 世纪第二和第三季度进入澳大利亚的英国银行经历，其中唯一幸存至今的银行仍在当地银行业中扮演重要角色。这九家银行是连接发展中世界与英国的帝国及海外银行浪潮中的重要组成部分。随着盎格鲁-澳大利亚银行（即南半球常称的"盎格鲁银行"）的兴起，一系列有趣的问题随之而来：它们的动机何在？与当地本土银行相比，它们的业绩表现如何？是什么促使它们进入并留在了这里？英国银行是否拥有超越本土银行的特殊优势？海外银行与本土银行是否在相同的市场中竞争？ 从市场份额或资本与资产的财务回报来看，英国银行的表现是优于、逊于还是与本土银行不相上下？为什么自 1863 年昆士兰银行开业后，英国金融机构时隔逾百年才再次涌入澳大利亚？</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_63"></span>The early phase of Anglo-Australian banking 1835—1853<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英澳银行业的早期阶段（1835—1853）</span></span></span>

Early colonial Australia offered plentiful opportunities to entrepreneurs who could provide good-quality English or Scottish banking services. By 1835, when the newly-chartered Bank of Australasia led the invasion of the British banks formed specifically to serve this market, the demand for banking and financial services still outran the available supply.<sup>4</sup> The original penal settlement, dating from 1788, generated large financial transactions, principally through the purchases and disbursements of the Commissariat and the funding of the regiments stationed in local garrisons, that involved drawing on funds held to their credit in the United Kingdom. However, financial services within Australia remained rudimentary in the extreme. A dearth of coin had led to widespread barter. Paper of dubious quality circulated freely in the absence of bank notes or cheque facilities. Loan funds were scarce and interest rates high. All this began to change in the 1820s when a growing influx of free settlers, whose arrival was in response to the emergence of viable export staples, especially wool, transformed the size of the fledgling economy and its financial requirements. The chronic shortage of coin was eventually remedied by the actions of the British government culminating in the adoption of sterling. Despite these innovations much remained to be done. For example, other important financial services such as the issuance of bank notes, the provision of a domestic and an international payments system, and the establishment of a system of financial intermediation based on the collection of deposits from the public through a series of branches, all awaited the development of a banking system.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">早期的澳大利亚殖民地，为那些能提供优质英格兰或苏格兰式银行服务的企业家提供了大量机遇。到 1835 年，当新获得特许的澳大拉西亚银行（Bank of Australasia）率先闯入专为服务这一市场而成立的英国银行行列时，银行与金融服务的需求仍然远超现有供给。 <sup>4</sup> 最初自 1788 年建立的流放殖民地，通过军需部的采购与支出以及驻防当地驻军的资金筹措，产生了大规模的金融交易，这些交易涉及动用其在英国账户上的资金。然而，澳大利亚本地的金融服务仍然极度简陋。硬币的匮乏导致了广泛的以物易物。在缺乏纸币或支票服务的情况下，质量可疑的票据自由流通。贷款资金稀缺，利率居高不下。 这一切在 19 世纪 20 年代开始改变，当时自由移民的涌入日益增多，他们的到来是为了应对可行出口大宗商品（尤其是羊毛）的出现，从而改变了这个初生经济体的规模及其金融需求。长期的硬币短缺最终通过英国政府的行动得以解决，最终采用了英镑。尽管有了这些创新，但仍有许多工作要做。 例如，其他重要的金融服务，如发行银行券、提供国内和国际支付系统，以及建立基于通过一系列分支机构向公众吸收存款的金融中介体系，所有这些都有待银行体系的发展。</span></span></span>

The local banking system was still in a fairly embryonic state two decades after the creation of the Bank of New South Wales in 1817. A dozen small commercial banks, or trading banks in local parlance, and eight savings banks had begun before the arrival of the first of the British banks. Another ten local banks, including four more savings banks, opened before 1850. However, a depression in the 1840s claimed a number of victims including a recent British entrant, the Royal Bank. With this crisis behind them the survivors gradually spread their range of services. However, their operations were limited in several important respects. Shortages of capital resulting from partnership status restricted operations to a single branch and set a ceiling on the size of their discount and loan book. Banking skills were at a premium. Few experienced bankers had found their way to Australia as migrants by this time. The dominance of the state-run Commissariat in the foreign<span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_64"></span>exchange market limited the opportunities for local banks to gain experience in foreign-exchange dealings and international funds transfer.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在新南威尔士银行于 1817 年成立后的二十年里，当地银行体系仍处于相当初级的阶段。在英国银行首次进入之前，已有十几家小型商业银行（当地称为贸易银行）和八家储蓄银行开始营业。另有十家本地银行（包括四家储蓄银行）在 1850 年前开业。然而，19 世纪 40 年代的经济萧条导致多家银行倒闭，其中包括一家新进入的英国银行——皇家银行。度过这场危机后，幸存者逐步扩展了服务范围。但在几个重要方面，它们的业务仍受到限制。合伙制导致的资本短缺使业务局限于单一分行，并限制了贴现和贷款业务的规模。银行业技术人才十分稀缺。当时，很少有经验丰富的银行家作为移民来到澳大利亚。 国营粮食局在外汇市场的主导地位限制了当地银行获取外汇交易和国际资金转移经验的机会。</span></span></span>

The rapid expansion of the economy in the 1830s, together with the swelling volume of trade and capital imports, highlighted the deficiencies in the services offered by the domestic banks. British entrepreneurs saw the possibilities of high returns from the provision of banking services. Entry into Australia was relatively easy as the major obstacles were in the political arena rather than the market place. Banking in the colonies required the permission of the Treasury whose principles of banking were embodied in the charters it granted and the Colonial Bank Regulations. Complaints from local banks against the arrival of the newcomers, though vociferous, generally fell on deaf ears in London.<sup>5</sup> Once permission was forthcoming the English banks faced minimal barriers in operating in the Australian colonies. The countries shared a common language, culture, commercial custom and legal system. English sovereigns and lesser coins circulated as the colonies' currency. The trade and capital flows were almost exclusively bilateral.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪 30 年代经济的迅速扩张，加上贸易和资本进口的激增，凸显了国内银行服务存在的不足。英国企业家看到了提供银行服务可能带来的高回报。进入澳大利亚市场相对容易，因为主要障碍在于政治领域而非市场本身。在殖民地开设银行需要获得财政部的许可，而财政部的银行原则体现在其颁发的特许状和《殖民地银行条例》中。当地银行对新来者的抱怨虽然激烈，但在伦敦通常无人理会。 <sup>5</sup> 一旦获得许可，英国银行在澳大利亚殖民地运营几乎毫无障碍。两国共享相同的语言、文化、商业习惯和法律体系。英国的金币及小额硬币在殖民地作为货币流通。贸易和资本流动几乎完全是双向的。</span></span></span>

British banks entered the market in force in the 1830s quickly changing the practices and, most importantly, the structural characteristics of Australian banking.<sup>6</sup> The restrictive covenants embodied in the charters and Colonial Bank Regulations, particularly those prohibitions on accepting real property, livestock and wool on the sheep's back as collateral, were widely evaded. The changing practices of banking in an economy with pressing needs for long-term credit, and with little else that could be used to secure bank loans, was lagged by relaxation of Australian legislation following the achievement of self-government in the 1850s. British banks played their full part in bending to commercial necessity rather than obeying Treasury edicts. They transformed Australian banking within a relatively short space of time. It was they who introduced Scottish-style branch banking. The spread of<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪 30 年代，英国银行大举进入澳洲市场，迅速改变了当地的银行业务实践，更重要的是，也重塑了澳大利亚银行业的结构性特征。 <sup>6</sup> 原先章程和《殖民地银行条例》中那些限制性的约定，特别是禁止接受不动产、牲畜以及活羊身上的羊毛作为抵押品的条款，普遍被规避了。当时澳大利亚经济急需长期信贷，而可用于担保银行贷款的资产寥寥无几，银行业务因此不断调整，这种变化在 1850 年代自治政府成立后，随着澳大利亚立法的放松才得以滞后地跟上。英国银行在顺应商业现实而非一味听命于财政部指令方面，发挥了重要作用。它们在相对较短的时间内彻底改变了澳大利亚的银行体系，正是它们引入了苏格兰式的分行经营模式。</span></span></span>

Branch numbers grew faster than population, with the exception of the gold-rush influx of the early 1850s. The number of people per branch fell from around 9,000 in 1836 to less than 3,000 in the mid-1870s, and to 2,000 by 1890.<sup>7</sup> By this latter date, interest-bearing deposits comprised 68 per cent of trading bank liabilities to the public within Australia, towering above the 27 per cent of non-interest bearing deposits and 4 per cent of notes.<sup>8</sup> The acceptance of deposit liabilities offered by the banks underpinned the dramatic expansion of the financial structure with the proportion of bank assets within Australia to GDP rising from a quarter in 1836 to 43 per cent by 1876, and 70 per cent in 1890.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">随着牧区边疆的扩张，银行分支开始向内陆延伸，而利息的定期支付极大地推动了存款的吸收。除 1850 年代初的淘金热期间外，分支数量的增长速度超过了人口增长。每家分支服务的人数从 1836 年的约 9000 人下降到 1870 年代中期的不足 3000 人，到 1890 年进一步降至 2000 人。到这一时期，有息存款占澳大利亚境内交易银行对公众负债的 68%，远高于无息存款的 27%和钞票的 4%。银行吸收存款负债的能力支撑了金融结构的急剧扩张，澳大利亚境内银行资产占 GDP 的比例从 1836 年的四分之一上升到 1876 年的 43%，再到 1890 年的 70%。</span></span></span>

British banks quickly established an edge in domestic markets by virtue of their size and management skills.<sup>9</sup> The capital raised by the proprietors of the Bank of Australasia and the Union Bank of Australia dwarfed that of their Australian rivals for decades. Access to capital allowed them to expand their note issue and to fund a larger loan book than would have been possible from the existing deposit base. It also provided them with the resources to invest in the establishment of a widespread branch network. Senior staff in British banks undoubtedly possessed higher average levels of experience and skills than their local counterparts. Almost without exception they had been recruited from British or Eastern exchange banks before being sent to Australia. The great distance and slow communications between London and Sydney and Melbourne made the directors very careful in their selection of senior managers. London boards led those of the local banks in specifying responsibilities and designing administrative hierarchies for their managers. All in all, the pioneering British banks, particularly the Bank of Australasia and the Union Bank of Australia, led the way in enhancing banking skills and creating effective administrative structures.<sup>10</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国银行凭借其规模和经营管理上的优势，迅速在国内市场建立领先地位。 <sup>9</sup> 数十年来，澳大利亚银行与联合澳大利亚银行的创始人们所筹集的资本规模，远超其澳大利亚本土竞争对手。充裕的资金使他们得以扩大纸币发行量，并建立起比依赖现有存款基础更为庞大的贷款业务。同时，这些资金也为他们投资构建广泛的分行网络提供了资源。不可否认，英国银行高级职员的平均经验和技能水平普遍高于当地同行。他们几乎无一例外都是从英国或东方汇理银行招募而来，之后才被派往澳大利亚。伦敦与悉尼、墨尔本之间遥远的路程和迟缓的通讯，使得董事会成员在选拔高级管理人员时极为审慎。 伦敦的银行在为其管理者明确职责和设计行政层级方面，走在了地方银行的前列。总体而言，那些具有开创性的英国银行，尤其是澳大利亚银行（Bank of Australasia）和澳大利亚联合银行（Union Bank of Australia），在提升银行业务技能和构建高效行政管理架构方面，起到了引领作用。 <sup>10</sup> </span></span></span>

The most important distinction, however, between the British banks and their domestic competitors was the former's operational presence in London. It was the British banks that were the first to become involved in financing the growing trade between Australia and the rest of the world, and transfers of capital into Australia. Their London offices were actively engaged in bill acceptance, collection and discounting as well as foreign exchange business. In this latter area they completely routed the dominance of the Commissariat.<sup>11</sup> By 1851 the Australasia and Union were distinguished from the local banks as much by the composition of their <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_66"></span>balance sheets as by their size. The multinational nature of their business was revealed by the disproportionate share of bills in circulation relative to their note and deposit liabilities. Furthermore, the greater part of the bill business was written in the books of the London office rather than in the Australian branches. Staff in the London office built up skills that differed sharply from those employed in all but chief city branches in Australia. Their work was not confined to trade finance and exchange dealing. British banks used their London offices to run treasury-style functions that could not be performed in Australia. Surplus sterling credits accumulated during the export seasons and, after the 1850s, from the banks' dealings in gold, could be profitably employed in the discount market or placed in government securities to provide for a secondary reserve. Conversely, a presence in the City provided these banks with the ability to raise funds to cover seasonal shortfalls in their cash or foreign exchange positions. London office staff were also responsible for the creation and oversight of the extensive correspondent networks that grew up.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，英国银行与其本土竞争对手之间最根本的区别，在于前者在伦敦设有业务机构。正是这些英国银行率先涉足澳大利亚与世界各地日益增长的贸易融资，以及向澳大利亚的资本转移业务。其伦敦办事处积极参与票据承兑、托收、贴现以及外汇交易，并在外汇领域彻底击溃了 Commissariat 的主导地位。 <sup>11</sup> 到 1851 年，澳大拉西亚银行与联合银行不仅凭借规模，更通过其资产负债表的构成，与本地银行拉开了差距。其业务的跨国属性体现在：流通中的票据相对于纸币和存款负债的失衡比例，以及绝大多数票据业务记录在伦敦办事处账簿而非澳大利亚分行这一事实上。 伦敦办公室员工所掌握的技能，与除澳大利亚主要城市分行外的其他分支机构截然不同。他们的工作不仅限于贸易融资和外汇交易。英国银行利用伦敦办公室执行在澳大利亚无法开展的司库职能。出口季节积累的英镑盈余信贷，以及 19 世纪 50 年代后银行黄金交易产生的盈余，可投入贴现市场获利，或投资于政府证券作为二级储备。反之，在伦敦金融城设立分支机构使这些银行能够筹集资金，弥补现金或外汇头寸的季节性短缺。伦敦办公室员工还负责建立并监督日益庞大的代理行网络。</span></span></span>

The Anglo-Australian banks' standing in the City depended, in no small measure, on the names of their directors. For the most part the courts were dominated by men who were already heavily involved in the many facets of Anglo-Australian trade.<sup>12</sup> British-based shippers, commodity traders, indent merchants, pastoralists, miners, merchant bankers and joint-stock bankers were joined by a sprinkling of retired emigrant businessmen and vice-regal appointees. Cross-directorships between Anglo-colonial trading ventures and banks were commonplace, as were seemingly-permanent relationships between British banks and a small number of influential City dynasties. These well-connected directors not only used the services of the banks in their own business but introduced new clients from the United Kingdom end. Their commercial intelligence was invaluable to the London office in assessing the credit risks associated with most of its customers' bills. Not only were the British banks better placed initially to compete within Australia but they were the first to be able to offer comprehensive financial services to those engaged in commodity trade and funds transfer between the two countries.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英澳银行在伦敦金融城的地位，在很大程度上取决于其董事们的声望。占据这些董事会席位的，多半是那些早已深度参与英澳贸易诸多环节的人物。其中既有驻扎英国本土的船运商、大宗商品交易商、订货商、牧场主、矿主、商业银行家以及股份制银行家，也夹杂着少数退休移居商人及副王任命的人选。英澳殖民贸易企业与银行之间交叉任职司空见惯，英国银行与少数几家颇具影响力的伦敦金融城豪门之间也保持着看似恒久的关系。这些人脉广泛的董事不仅在自己的业务中借助银行的服务，还从英国本土引荐新客户。其商业情报对于伦敦办事处评估大多数客户票据的信贷风险而言，具有无可估量的价值。 英国银行不仅在澳大利亚境内一开始就占据了更有利的竞争地位，而且它们还是首批能够为从事两国间商品贸易和资金转移的客户提供全面金融服务的机构。</span></span></span>

The demand for banking services continued to rise strongly in the second half of the century. New entrants were attracted by the high profits enjoyed by the handful of banks operating in the early 1850s. The list of trading banks grew to twenty-eight by 1890 even after a number had failed during the preceding decades. Only five of the new banks were British. As shown in [Table 4.2](#Ch04.xhtml_fig0004) three of them opened their Australian businesses in 1853: the English, Scottish & Australian Chartered Bank, the London Chartered Bank of Australia and the Oriental Bank. This latter bank was similar to the Agra and Masterman that began in Australia in 1862, in that its Australian operations were peripheral to its main business. Neither of the last two British entrants, the Agra and Masterman nor the Bank of Queensland survived the reverberations of the Overend & Gurney crisis. Newly-formed British banks had headed the second wave of bank establishments but thereafter local entrepreneurs showed a greater willingness to enter the market. None of the new British banks had been able to outrun their colonial counterparts as the Asia, Union and the Bank of South Australia had done in the 1830s and 1840s. In 1851 these three banks held over two-thirds of the total commercial bank assets in Australia. The Asia and Union each possessed nearly 30 per cent. British banks lost market share steadily for the next forty years to surrender 60 per cent of private commercial bank assets to Australian banks by 1890.<sup>15</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

The British banks' edge in terms of the human capital of its staff was blunted over time. The constraints on the initial size of local establishments and on their balance-sheet growth were removed fairly quickly. Australian banks were better placed to raise capital following their new-found ability to register as companies and in the light of the development of colonial stock exchanges. Bank scrip was one of the leading blue chip investments on exchanges that were overwhelmingly concerned with speculative mining scrip.<sup>19</sup> Several of the larger domestic banks achieved listing on the London stock exchange.<sup>20</sup> On one hand, the secular increase in the banks' gearing ratios eased their capital requirements to support balance-sheet expansion.<sup>21</sup> Fresh deposits, representing additional liquid assets, increasingly displaced proprietors' funds as the source of working capital, and particularly as a source of funding for the ballooning loan books. Even so, the need for new capital to provide for investment in branches grew incessantly. Competition for deposits, both current and fixed-term interest-bearing, rested heavily on the number and location of a bank's branches. The number of branches rose from 25 in 1851 to a remarkable 1,553 by 1891.<sup>22</sup> Australian banks added more than three new branches for every one of the British banks over the interval.<sup>23</sup> The construction of branch networks by local banks altered the economics of their operations. Risks, particularly on the lending side, were lessened as a larger business was spread across industries and regions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

A few of the advantages held by British banks were not so easily matched. All of these related to financing international trade, foreign exchange dealing and treasury operations, and ultimately rested on having a London branch. As shown in [Table 4.3](#Ch04.xhtml_fig0005), Australian banks followed suit. Seventeen opened offices in the City before 1914. Not surprisingly, the lead was taken by the Bank of New South Wales who started banking in London in 1853. It is size that distinguishes the Australian banks that enter London from those who do not. Prior to their establishing a presence in London, Australian banks used the services of others as agents and correspondents. A considerable volume of trade-related business was needed to produce the revenues to justify the expense of an office in the City. The timing of office establishments appears to be related to the achievement of some threshold of a balance-sheet total of between one-and-a-half and three million pounds. The arrival of Australian banks in London is concentrated into two periods, the decade after 1859 and the 1880s. During the first episode the motivation was to offer the same range of services as could be provided by the British banks. London offices' activities revolved around accepting and receiving bills, dealing in foreign <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_70"></span>exchanges and bullion markets, arranging for the sale of goods taken on consignment and operating in the discount market. This strategy continued in the latter period. Australian banks slowly built up a powerful presence in the City that constituted a real threat to the Anglo-Australian banks. Eight of the indigenous banks had created local boards of advice before the end of the 1880s whose incumbents brought similar qualities to their courts, if less social prestige than their British counterparts.<sup>25</sup> The directors, usually retired vice-regal dignitaries and expatriated colonial businessmen, introduced business to the bank at the City end as well as providing a credit check on customers. Longstanding banking relationships were developed with a number of the big clearing banks such as the London Joint Stock Bank, the London and Westminster, Lloyds and the National Provincial, and the Bank of England, with whom they deposited surplus funds and drew lines of credit. The distinction between Anglo-Australian and the Australian banks in the City became increasingly blurred as they acted as Associated Banks to negotiate industry-wide rates on matters of exchange and charges, and to present a single voice in their discussions with English authorities.<sup>26</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">澳大利亚银行在伦敦金融城逐步建立起强大的影响力，对英澳银行构成了实质威胁。到 19 世纪 80 年代末，已有八家本土银行在当地设立了顾问委员会，其任职者虽不如英国同行那般享有显赫的社会声望，却为银行董事会带来了相似的特质。这些董事通常由退休的副王级显贵和移居海外的殖民地商人组成，他们既在伦敦金融城为银行招揽业务，也负责对客户进行信用审查。这些银行与多家大型清算银行建立了长期合作关系，包括伦敦合股银行、伦敦威斯敏斯特银行、劳埃德银行、国民地方银行以及英格兰银行，并将富余资金存放于这些银行，同时从中获取信贷额度。 在伦敦金融城（City），英澳银行（Anglo-Australian）与澳大利亚银行之间的界限日益模糊，它们作为联合银行（Associated Banks），就汇率和手续费等事项协商行业统一费率，并在与英国当局的谈判中发出单一声音。 <sup>26</sup> </span></span></span>

By late in the century nearly all of the larger Australian banks could match the range of services offered by the British banks. The first mover advantages of the British banks were not reinforced by further innovations in product range, process technology or administrative design. In the absence of insurmountable barriers to entry stemming from patents, prohibitions on entry by government agencies or exceptional capital requirements, Australian banks could compete successfully. However, emulating the British banks' international business was more difficult than the Australian retail side. Operating as multinational banks - some of the local banks extended their branches into New Zealand and the neighbouring Pacific Islands as well as to London - posed new challenges for management. Controlling the expanding retail network involved little more than an extension of existing reporting and control mechanisms. London was different in that the nature of the business being done required competences not easily learnt 12,000 miles away. Protected by a lengthy learning curve, British banks retained advantages in international business until well into the <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_71"></span>twentieth century that allowed them to take a disproportionate share of the market in a number of services. The available data does not permit more than an informed guess on this point. However, the list of correspondents used by some British banks, particularly the Australasia and Union, was far greater than those of any of the Australian banks up to the end of the First World War.<sup>27</sup> British banks had a greater share of their assets involved in their London office business and held a disproportionate share of bills in their balance sheets.<sup>28</sup> It was not until the interwar period that leading Australian banks, particularly the Bank of New South Wales,<sup>29</sup> made a really concerted effort to challenge the dominance of the British banks in the provision of correspondent banking facilities and foreign exchange.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">到本世纪末，几乎所有较大的澳大利亚银行都能提供与英国银行相同的服务范围。英国银行的先发优势并未通过产品范围、流程技术或行政设计方面的进一步创新得到加强。由于没有专利、政府机构的准入禁令或特殊资本要求等不可逾越的进入壁垒，澳大利亚银行能够成功竞争。然而，模仿英国银行的国际业务比模仿其澳大利亚零售业务更加困难。作为跨国银行运营——部分本地银行将分行扩展到新西兰、邻近的太平洋岛屿以及伦敦——给管理层带来了新的挑战。控制不断扩张的零售网络，无非是对现有报告和控制机制的延伸。 伦敦的不同之处在于，其业务性质所需的技能在 12000 英里之外难以轻易掌握。凭借漫长的学习曲线，英国银行在国际业务上一直保持优势，直至二十世纪仍能占据多项服务市场不成比例的份额。现有数据对此仅能提供基于专业判断的推测。然而，截至一战结束前，部分英国银行（尤其是澳大拉西亚银行和联合银行）使用的代理行数量远超任何一家澳大利亚银行。 <sup>27</sup> 英国银行将更大比例的资产投入伦敦办事处业务，其资产负债表中的票据占比也显著偏高。 直到两次世界大战之间的时期，澳大利亚的主要银行，特别是新南威尔士银行，才真正齐心协力，挑战英国银行在代理行服务和外汇业务中的主导地位。</span></span></span>

Despite losing market share to local banks up to the 1890s the surviving British banks had captured a significant share of the market and a permanent place in Australia. The experiences of individual banks differed markedly as the banking environment became increasingly competitive. The New Oriental Bank's demise was largely unrelated to its Australian business,<sup>30</sup> while the demise of the Bank of South Australia was a consequence of poor management rather than its multinational character.<sup>31</sup> Both the English, Scottish & Australian Chartered Bank<sup>32</sup> and the London Chartered Banks closed their doors during the infamous bank crashes of 1893. However, neither appears to have been insolvent, although both experienced serious losses through loan defaults, and soon resumed trading as reconstructed companies without their charters. The heavy cost of paying interest on the deferred liabilities to depositors in the old banks depressed their earnings for decades. In contrast, the Asia and Union emerged from the episode unscathed as a consequence of their more conservative lending policies and high liquidity ratios. None of the British banks had withdrawn from the market because of an unsatisfactory level of earnings. However, the profits being earned in the 1870s and 1880s were no longer sufficient to attract additional British banks. Not one of the next group of foreign banks that came to Australia, the Bank of New Zealand in 1872, Comptoir National D'Escompte De Paris in 1880, the Yokohama Specie Bank in 1915 and the Bank of China in 1942, sought a retail deposit base. They concentrated exclusively on trade-related business requirements to and from their own countries. Consequently, each of them was restricted to an extremely small Australian operation.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管直到 19 世纪 90 年代，英国银行在本土银行面前丢失了市场份额，但存活下来的英国银行仍在澳大利亚占据了可观的市场份额，并扎下了根。随着银行业竞争日益激烈，各家银行的经历也大相径庭。新东方银行的倒闭与其在澳大利亚的业务关系不大， <sup>30</sup> 而南澳大利亚银行的倒闭则更多是管理不善所致，与其跨国性质无关。 <sup>31</sup> 在 1893 年那场臭名昭著的银行倒闭潮中，英格兰、苏格兰及澳大利亚特许银行 <sup>32</sup> 和伦敦特许银行都关门歇业。不过，这两家银行似乎并未资不抵债，尽管因贷款违约遭受了重大损失，但它们很快便作为重建公司重新营业，只是不再持有银行执照。此后数十年，为旧银行存款人支付延期负债利息的沉重负担，一直压得它们的利润喘不过气来。 相比之下，亚洲与联合银行（Asia and Union）因其更为保守的贷款政策和高流动性比率，在此次风波中安然无恙。没有一家英国银行因收益水平不达标而退出市场。然而，1870 年代和 1880 年代所创造的利润已不足以吸引更多英国银行前来。随后进入澳大利亚的下一批外资银行——1872 年的新西兰银行、1880 年的法国商业银行、1915 年的横滨正金银行以及 1942 年的中国银行——无一寻求零售存款基础。它们完全专注于与本国之间的贸易相关业务需求。 因此，每家银行在澳大利亚的业务都局限于极小的规模。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_72"></span>Homogeneity 1893—1985<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">同质化时期 1893—1985</span></span></span>

Barriers to entry rose considerably from the late nineteenth century onwards. The increasing capital requirements of retail banking sharply reduced the emergence of new Australian banks. This development was largely a reaction to the debacle of the 1893 bank crashes. Depositors were more conscious of the prudential strengths and weakness of banks. Higher capital ratios not only provided an insurance function against losses but were also a prerequisite for a nationwide business which itself introduced an element of stability in both lending and deposit collection. The bank crashes delayed an amalgamation movement in Australian banking until after the First World War because many of the banks were still working off their obligations under their schemes of rearrangement until that time. Once those encumbrances were out of the way the number of private-sector trading banks operating in Australia fell from nineteen in 1917 to only nine by 1931.<sup>33</sup> The industry was becoming a tightly-knit oligopoly where a small number of banks adhered to industry agreements on rates and charges,<sup>34</sup> as well as controlling access to the clearing house for inter-bank chequing settlements. The obstacles facing any newcomers were formidable. The sole domestic entrant between 1888 and 1981, the Primary Producers' Bank of Australia, opened in 1923 and went into liquidation in 1931 despite having a paid-up capital of nearly half a million pounds. This bank did not have enough branches, and it could not offer a full range of international services. Its shareholders were never paid a dividend. The bank could not achieve even a half of one per cent of trading bank assets in Australia.<sup>35</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">自 19 世纪末起，银行业进入壁垒显著提高。零售银行业务对资本要求的不断攀升，导致澳大利亚新设银行数量锐减。这一变化很大程度上是对 1893 年银行危机溃败的回应。储户开始更关注银行的审慎稳健性。更高的资本充足率不仅发挥了抵御损失的保险功能，更是开展全国性业务的先决条件——这种全国性经营本身也为贷款发放和存款吸收带来了稳定性。银行危机拖慢了澳大利亚银行业的合并浪潮，直到第一次世界大战后才得以推进，因为此前许多银行仍在履行重整计划下的债务义务。一旦这些负担解除，澳大利亚私营贸易银行的数量便从 1917 年的 19 家骤降至 1931 年的仅剩 9 家。 <sup>33</sup> 这个行业正逐渐形成一个紧密的寡头垄断格局，少数几家银行不仅遵守行业关于利率和收费的协议， <sup>34</sup> 还控制着银行间支票结算的清算所准入权。任何新进入者面临的障碍都极为严峻。在 1888 年至 1981 年间，唯一一家进入该领域的国内银行是澳大利亚初级生产者银行，它于 1923 年开业，尽管实缴资本近 50 万英镑，却在 1931 年进入清算。这家银行分支机构不足，也无法提供全面的国际服务，其股东从未获得过股息。该银行在澳大利亚交易银行资产中所占份额甚至不到 0.5%。 <sup>35</sup> </span></span></span>

Domestic entrants were deterred by the combination of lowered earnings and higher start-up costs. However, British clearing banks were expanding their overseas operations in the early twentieth century. They competed in the City's foreign exchange markets and challenged the dominance of the merchant banks in the acceptance business.<sup>36</sup> A more dramatic development was the entry of colonial and foreign markets by a number of the clearers through the acquisition of English overseas banks.<sup>37</sup> At least one approach was made to the Bank of New South Wales that proved abortive.<sup>38</sup> However, the quality and costs of the banking service offered by Australian and Anglo-Australian banks was such that British clearing banks could not expect to earn better returns, particularly in the increasingly important retail markets.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">国内银行因收益下降和启动成本上升而望而却步。然而，20 世纪初，英国清算银行却在扩张海外业务。它们在伦敦金融城的外汇市场上展开竞争，并在承兑业务中挑战了商业银行的主导地位。 <sup>36</sup> 一个更引人注目的发展是，多家清算银行通过收购英国海外银行进入了殖民地及外国市场。 <sup>37</sup> 至少有一次试图收购新南威尔士银行，但最终失败了。 <sup>38</sup> 然而，澳大利亚及英澳银行提供的银行服务质量与成本使得英国清算银行难以获得更高回报，尤其是在日益重要的零售市场中。</span></span></span>

The banks' greatest monopoly was in the foreign exchange markets. British clearing banks left the bulk of Australian-Sterling <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_73"></span>market to these banks in London. Largely untroubled in the City, they reigned supreme domestically by shutting out the big export and import houses that might form a large outside market, and by denying banking facilities to foreign banks. Entry by foreign banks was effectively blocked from the early 1920s onwards. Refusal to grant current account facilities to foreign banks from 1909 until 1928, when the Commonwealth Bank agreed to open accounts but acquiesced to the onerous terms insisted upon by the Associated Banks, drove a number of American banks to abandon their Australian branches in the 1920s.<sup>39</sup> This predatory behaviour was reinforced by the threat of regulation. Any bank seeking to open a branch in Australia rather than use the locals as correspondents was required to seek registration from the Commonwealth Treasurer, who agreed to pass the matter to the Board of the Commonwealth Bank of Australia, the government-owned trading and savings bank-cum-nascent central bank, who in turn would consult the industry association, the Associated Banks of Victoria. Not surprisingly, the Associated Banks were fiercely opposed to entry and pressed the government to legislate against that eventuality. While no legislation found its way on to the statute books the government's hostile attitude must have been made known to prospective entrants.<sup>40</sup> The Australian authorities retained a general prohibition on foreign banks until February 1986 when sixteen new banking licences were issued. The unwillingness of the Australian authorities to countenance the entry of new foreign banks was reinforced by the restrictive attitudes of the Bank of England towards any further acquisitions of foreign banks by the Big Five clearing banks in the light of the recommendations of the Colwyn committee.<sup>41</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">银行在外汇市场中占据了最大的垄断地位。英国清算银行将大部分澳镑市场业务留给了伦敦的这些银行。在伦敦金融城，它们几乎未受干扰，通过排挤可能形成大型外部市场的大型进出口公司，并拒绝向外国银行提供银行服务，从而在国内占据了主导地位。自 20 世纪 20 年代初起，外国银行的进入被有效阻止。从 1909 年至 1928 年，外国银行被拒绝获得活期账户便利，直到联邦银行同意开户，但接受了联合银行坚持的苛刻条件，这导致多家美国银行在 20 世纪 20 年代放弃了它们在澳大利亚的分行。 <sup>39</sup> 这种掠夺性行为还因监管威胁而得到强化。 任何希望在澳大利亚设立分行而非仅使用当地银行作为代理的银行，都必须向联邦财政部长申请注册，而财政部长会同意将此事提交给联邦银行董事会——这家政府所有的贸易与储蓄银行同时也是新兴的中央银行——随后该董事会将咨询行业组织“维多利亚联合银行”。毫不意外的是，维多利亚联合银行强烈反对新银行进入，并施压政府立法阻止这种情况。虽然最终没有相关法律被正式写入法典，但政府敌视的态度想必早已为潜在进入者所知。 <sup>40</sup> 澳大利亚当局一直全面禁止外国银行，直到 1986 年 2 月才发放了 16 张新的银行牌照。 澳大利亚当局对外资银行进入持抵制态度，而英格兰银行基于科尔温委员会的建议，对五大清算银行进一步收购外资银行采取限制立场，这进一步强化了澳方的立场。 <sup>41</sup> </span></span></span>

These governmental restraints notwithstanding, it is unlikely that foreign banks would have found Australia an attractive market from the late 1920s until the 1960s.<sup>42</sup> Bank earnings were lowered by depression. Loans were written off as borrowers defaulted. The banks' foreign exchange earnings and commissions were hurt by the collapse of export incomes as commodity prices sagged and by the drying-up of the large scale capital inflows. The devaluation of the Australian currency in 1931 inflicted a loss on the British banks whose balance sheets were in sterling. These downward pressures on earnings were compounded by the increasingly difficult climate in which all Australian bankers found themselves. The Commonwealth Bank was having central banking functions thrust on it by force of circumstance as the government struggled to cope with the massive external forces driving the economy into depression. For the most part the private banks were <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_74"></span>unwilling to co-operate fully with an institution that competed with them as a commercial bank, or to surrender to its authority.<sup>43</sup> Relations between the private banks and the Australian Labor Party, many of whose members saw banks as blood-sucking usurers, were particularly strained during the depths of the depression, while that party was in power in the federal parliament. Political antagonism reached new heights in the 1930s as the Labor Party advocated nationalization of private banks. Even the succeeding conservative government felt compelled to assuage widespread hostility towards the banks by establishing a royal commission into Australia's monetary and banking systems. It recommended the continuance of the existing system of private banking but advocated greater formal powers for the central bank. No legislation giving effect to these recommendations was passed because of continued resistance by the private bankers. However, their opposition came to a sudden end. The Labor Party returning to government in 1941 almost immediately used its defence powers to impose comprehensive regulations on the trading banks and to give the Commonwealth Bank all the authority it needed to be an effective central bank.<sup>44</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管存在这些政府限制，但从 20 世纪 20 年代末到 60 年代，外资银行恐怕很难将澳大利亚视为一个有吸引力的市场。 <sup>42</sup> 大萧条导致银行收益下滑。随着借款人违约，贷款被核销。大宗商品价格疲软导致出口收入锐减，大规模资本流入枯竭，银行的汇兑收益和佣金收入也遭受重创。1931 年澳元贬值给那些以英镑结算资产负债表的英国银行造成了损失。雪上加霜的是，所有澳大利亚银行家都面临着日益艰难的经营环境。在政府努力应对将经济推入萧条的巨大外部力量时，澳大利亚联邦银行因形势所迫，被迫承担起中央银行的职能。 私营银行大多不愿与一家作为商业银行与其竞争的机构充分合作，也不愿服从其权威。 <sup>43</sup> 私营银行与澳大利亚工党之间的关系尤为紧张，尤其是在经济大萧条最严重的时期，当时工党在联邦议会执政，而工党许多成员将银行视为吸血的高利贷者。20 世纪 30 年代，随着工党主张将私营银行国有化，政治对抗达到了新的高度。即便是继任的保守派政府也感到有必要通过设立一个皇家委员会来调查澳大利亚的货币和银行体系，以缓解公众对银行的普遍敌意。该委员会建议维持现有的私营银行体系，但主张赋予中央银行更大的正式权力。然而，由于私营银行家的持续抵制，这些建议并未通过立法得以实施。 然而，他们的反对声戛然而止。工党于 1941 年重新执政后，几乎立即动用其国防权力，对商业银行实施了全面监管，并赋予联邦银行成为有效中央银行所需的一切权力。 <sup>44</sup> </span></span></span>

Regulation of trading banks in Australia restrained their profits and the growth of their balance sheets for decades.<sup>45</sup> Monetary policy played an active role in the postwar governments' armoury of stabilization policies.<sup>46</sup> The monetary authorities primarily relied on the blunt and partial instrument of varying the volume of bank lending. Bankers danced to the tune of the central bank as they adjusted their loan books in the light of variable frozen reserves and prescribed liquidity ratios. Bank interest rates were set by the authorities and they were adjusted infrequently. Furthermore, their real levels fell sharply in the inflationary postwar era. The limitations on balance-sheet growth, due to the asset ratios insisted upon by the authorities, and the setting of bank rates below market clearing levels inevitably led to a diminution in the importance of banks in the financial system. The trading banks' share of the assets of all financial institutions fell from roughly 37 per cent in 1936 to a trough of 20 per cent by 1972.<sup>47</sup> This contraction was part of the secular decline that had been evident since the 1890s.<sup>48</sup> However, the fastest-growing parts of the financial system before 1939 - thrift institutions such as savings banks, and life assurance companies - did not directly challenge the trading banks in terms of the sorts of loans being offered. However, non-bank financial intermediaries increasingly encroached into traditional banking areas in the 1950s and 1960s. The booming economy of postwar Australia generated great demands for financial services particu<span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_75"></span>larly for working and fixed capital, and personal finance. Regulation meant that the banks could not meet all the requirements of their customers because they could not compete for funds at market rates and as their ability to lend from any level of liquid resources was constrained.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">澳大利亚对贸易银行的监管数十年来制约了其利润和资产负债表规模的增长。货币政策在战后政府的稳定政策武器库中扮演了积极角色。货币当局主要依赖调整银行贷款规模这一粗放且片面的工具。银行家们根据可变的冻结准备金和规定的流动性比率调整贷款账簿，听从中央银行的指挥。银行利率由当局设定，且鲜有调整。此外，在战后通胀高企的时代，这些利率的实际水平大幅缩水。由于当局坚持的资产比率限制导致资产负债表规模增长受限，且银行利率被设定在市场出清水平之下，银行在金融体系中的重要性不可避免地下降。 商业银行在所有金融机构资产中的占比，从 1936 年的约 37%下降到 1972 年的 20%低谷。 <sup>47</sup> 这一收缩是自 19 世纪 90 年代以来就已显现的长期下滑趋势的一部分。 <sup>48</sup> 然而，1939 年前金融体系增长最快的部分——储蓄银行等储蓄机构和寿险公司——在贷款类型上并未直接挑战商业银行。但在 20 世纪 50 年代和 60 年代，非银行金融中介机构逐渐蚕食了传统银行业务领域。战后澳大利亚经济繁荣，对金融服务产生了巨大需求，尤其是营运资本、固定资本和个人融资。监管意味着银行无法满足客户的所有需求，因为它们无法按市场利率竞争资金，且从任何流动性水平放贷的能力都受到限制。</span></span></span>

Australia once again became a potentially attractive host to multinational banks. Local institutions were hamstrung by the nature of monetary policy that, although progressively relaxed from the 1960s, was not abandoned until the implementation of deregulation in the early 1980s.<sup>49</sup> A number of other factors need to be considered. Australia's high trade barriers and rich natural resources attracted foreign direct investment in manufacturing and in coal, oil and gas, and a range of minerals. Many of the new multinationals starting up in Australia were American, European and Japanese rather than predominantly British as had been the case previously. The trade flows were increasingly towards the United States and Japan. The pre-war hegemony of United Kingdom-Australian trade and capital flows was being broken.<sup>50</sup> Foreign banks were keen to retain their relationship with their clients. For their part the multinational, and domestic, companies looked to foreign banks because the local banks faced difficulty in providing the volume of funds necessary, especially in the resource area, and lacked skills in servicing corporate customers.<sup>51</sup> Australian banks used their London offices to trade in Eurocurrency markets and several joined consortiums with foreign banks.<sup>52</sup> These actions did not stop foreign banks from positioning themselves in a market which they were still forbidden to enter as banks. They opened representative offices and set up merchant banks. It was the foreign banks that acted as the main conduit between the increasingly sophisticated needs of local corporations and the world's banking centres.<sup>53</sup> Australia had become part of an integrated global capital market long before the removal of foreign exchange controls and the licensing of foreign banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">澳大利亚再次成为跨国银行眼中颇具吸引力的落脚地。本地的金融机构受制于货币政策的特点——尽管自 20 世纪 60 年代起逐步放宽，但直到 80 年代初放松管制才真正松绑。 <sup>49</sup> 还有若干因素需要考虑。澳大利亚的高贸易壁垒与丰富的自然资源，吸引了制造业以及煤炭、石油、天然气和一系列矿产领域的外国直接投资。当时在澳大利亚新设的跨国企业中，来自美国、欧洲和日本的居多，而非像过去那样以英国企业为主导。贸易流向日益转向美国和日本。战前英国与澳大利亚之间的贸易和资本流动霸权正在瓦解。 <sup>50</sup> 外资银行迫切希望维系与客户的关系。 跨国公司以及本土企业转而寻求外资银行，因为本地银行难以提供所需规模的资金——尤其是在资源领域——并且缺乏服务企业客户的专业能力。 <sup>51</sup> 澳大利亚银行利用其伦敦办事处参与欧洲货币市场交易，其中几家还与外资银行组成了银团。 <sup>52</sup> 这些举措并未阻止外资银行在一个它们仍被禁止以银行身份进入的市场中布局。它们开设了代表处，并设立了商人银行。正是外资银行扮演了主要渠道的角色，连接着本土企业日益复杂的需求与全球银行中心。 <sup>53</sup> 早在取消外汇管制和向外资银行发放牌照之前，澳大利亚就已经成为一体化全球资本市场的一部分。</span></span></span>

Performance of British banks in Australia: paradise lost?<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国银行在澳大利亚的表现：失乐园？</span></span></span>

British banks lost their prime position in the market before the end of the nineteenth century. Thereafter the dwindling number of British banks maintained around 30 per cent of the Australian-domiciled assets of private trading banks. This apparent constancy masks a continued loss of standing amongst their peers. The stability of the country averages owes much to the relative decline of some of the domestic banks offsetting the strong growth of their leaders. There were marked shifts within each group. For instance, <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_76"></span>in the interwar period the English, Scottish and Australian Bank's acquisition of two domestic banks helped to offset the slow decline in the market share of both the Asia and Union. By the late 1930s both these latter banks were drifting towards the bottom end of the listings.<sup>54</sup> Their global balance sheets, including their New Zealand, Pacific island and London businesses, pushed them ahead of some of the Australian banks who did not have a retail business overseas. However, they had forfeited any claims to being leaders in Australian banking. None of the British banks could keep pace with the dynamic Bank of New South Wales who built an unassailable base in the most populous state in the Commonwealth. The British banks were anxious to best it if they could. However, neither of the mergers of the Asia and Union in 1951 to form Australia and New Zealand Bank, nor its merger with the English, Scottish & Australian Bank in 1969 to create the Australia and New Zealand Banking Group allowed them to overtake the Wales. They, together with the Australian banks, struggled to hold their market share, particularly after 1945, in the face of strong competition from the government-owned Commonwealth Bank of Australia.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪末之前，英国银行便已失去了市场中的主导地位。此后，英国银行数量持续减少，在澳大利亚注册的私营贸易银行资产中，它们仍维持着约 30%的份额。这种表面上的稳定，实际上掩盖了其同行地位的持续下滑。全国平均数据的稳定，很大程度上得益于部分本土银行的相对衰落，恰好抵消了龙头银行的强劲增长。各银行集团内部也出现了显著分化。例如，在两次世界大战之间的时期，英澳苏格兰银行收购了两家本土银行，这在一定程度上抵消了亚洲银行和 Union 银行市场份额的缓慢下滑。到了 20 世纪 30 年代末，后两家银行已逐渐滑向榜单末尾。 <sup>54</sup> 它们的全球资产负债表——包括新西兰、太平洋岛屿和伦敦的业务——使其超越了那些未涉足海外零售业务的澳大利亚银行。 然而，他们已经丧失了在澳大利亚银行业中担任领军者的资格。没有一家英国银行能跟得上活力十足的新南威尔士银行，后者在英联邦人口最多的州建立了牢不可破的根基。英国银行都急欲击败它，但无论 1951 年亚洲银行与合众银行合并组成澳新银行，还是 1969 年澳新银行与英澳苏格兰银行合并成立澳新银行集团，都未能超越新南威尔士银行。 他们与澳大利亚的银行一道，艰难地维持着自己的市场份额，尤其是在 1945 年之后面对国有澳大利亚联邦银行的强劲竞争。</span></span></span>

The gloomy picture of the long decline of British banks is compounded by an examination of their earnings. Data from a sample of the Bank of Australasia and the Union Bank of Australia, and the Bank of New South Wales and the Commercial Banking Company of Sydney, shown in [Table 4.4](#Ch04.xhtml_fig0006) suggests that bank earnings were subject to strong cyclical patterns with a downward trend. Each of the four banks earned consistent profits through boom and depression. The two Australian banks earned an unweighted average rate of net profits on published shareholders funds of 8.45 per cent over the period from 1851 to 1950 compared to 7.54 per cent for the British banks. Does a difference of this magnitude support a hypothesis that the British banks did not perform as well as their domestic rivals? As they competed in similar markets - with the caveat that the British banks used a niche strategy of concentrating on international trade finance and exchange dealings - we might expect the rates of return to be equalized within a predictable range. A paired difference test was used to judge if the differences in average earning rates was statistically significant.<sup>55</sup> A comparison of the variance of the means at each quinquennial interval produced a very large standard deviation. Consequently, the null hypothesis that the two averages came from the same distribution of expected values is rejected at the 5 per cent confidence limit as the resulting t value of 1.96 exceeded the expected figure with 20 degrees of freedom.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对英国银行长期衰退的悲观图景，从其盈利状况的考察中进一步得到证实。表 4.4 中整理的澳大利亚银行、澳大利亚联合银行、新南威尔士银行及悉尼商业银行的样本数据显示，银行盈利受强烈的周期性波动影响且呈下行趋势。四家银行在经济繁荣与萧条时期均持续盈利。这两家澳大利亚银行在 1851 年至 1950 年间，基于已公布股东资金的净利率未加权平均值为 8.45%，而英国银行则为 7.54%。如此显著的差异，是否足以支撑英国银行业绩不及本土竞争对手的假设？ 虽然英国银行采取的是专注于国际贸易融资和外汇交易的利基策略，但考虑到它们在同一市场领域竞争，我们可以预期其收益率会在可预测的范围内趋同。我们采用配对差异检验来判定平均收益率差异是否具有统计显著性。 <sup>55</sup> 对每五年间隔期的均值方差进行比较后，发现标准差极大。 因此，在 5%置信水平下，两平均值源于同一期望值分布的原假设被拒绝，因为计算出的 t 值为 1.96，超过了自由度为 20 时的预期值。</span></span></span>

While this statistical test is not conclusive - for example, there are problems surrounding the sample choice, the time intervals, comparability of accounting conventions being used and so on - it is suggestive that British banks did not possess any advantages in terms of profitability over the local banks over the long haul. This conclusion is supported by another exercise of comparing the yields to shareholders in the Bank of New South Wales and the Bank of Australasia.<sup>56</sup> One hundred units of stock in both banks purchased in 1860 and held to 1880 would have returned the Wales' shareholder a compound rate of 6.86 per cent per annum compared to 5.23 per cent to the investor in the Asia. The investor <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_78"></span>in the Wales not only achieved a higher total return but also gained from receiving a more uniform stream of income in the form of dividends rather than realizing a capital gain from the liquidation of a stock holding that was enlarged by a number of rights issues. The results were similar when the exercise was repeated for the years 1916 to 1936. An investor in the Bank of New South Wales received a return of 5.41 per cent per annum, and a higher flow of cash payments rather than capital gain, compared to a rate of 5.07 per cent per annum in Asia stock.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管这一统计检验并非决定性——例如在样本选择、时间间隔、会计惯例的可比性等方面存在问题——但它表明，长期来看，英国银行在盈利能力上并不比本地银行拥有任何优势。这一结论得到了另一项比较新南威尔士银行和澳大利亚银行股东收益率的练习的支持。 <sup>56</sup> 1860 年购买并持有至 1880 年的两家银行各 100 单位股票，新南威尔士银行的股东年复合收益率为 6.86%，而澳大利亚银行的投资者为 5.23%。新南威尔士银行的投资者不仅获得了更高的总回报，还通过获得更稳定的股息收入流受益，而非通过清算因多次配股而扩大的股票持有量来实现资本收益。 当将分析时段重复至 1916 至 1936 年时，结果相似。 新南威尔士银行的投资者每年获得 5.41%的回报，且主要来自较高的现金支付，而非资本增值。相比之下，亚洲股票的回报率为每年 5.07%。</span></span></span>

The evidence of lower rates of returns on equity by the two British banks compared to the two Australian banks of similar ages is consistent with differences in their balance-sheet ratios. The British banks had higher ratios of shareholders' funds to total assets - 19.92 per cent compared to 14.67 per cent - over the period 1851 to 1950. This more conservative approach to balance-sheet management was reflected in a reduced yield on equity for every unit of earnings. Not only did the British banks have a higher capital base for their business but they increased their shareholders' funds significantly more slowly than the sample of Australian banks. All four banks paid out between 80 and 90 per cent of their net profits as dividends from their beginnings until 1910.<sup>57</sup> Payout ratios of this magnitude remained the industry norm up to the Second World War.<sup>58</sup> Consequently there was not a great scope for increasing reserves from profits. What distinguished the two British banks was the relative unwillingness of their directors to raise fresh funds from the shareholders. It is not unreasonable to suppose that this reluctance to approach the shareholders was due in some part to the relatively poor earnings performance.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">与成立时间相近的两家澳大利亚银行相比，这两家英国银行的股本回报率较低，这与两者资产负债表比率的差异相符。在 1851 年至 1950 年间，英国银行的股东资金与总资产比率更高，为 19.92%，而澳大利亚银行则为 14.67%。这种更为保守的资产负债表管理方式，体现在每一单位收益所对应的股本收益率降低上。英国银行不仅业务资本基础更为雄厚，其股东资金的增长速度也明显慢于所选取的澳大利亚银行样本。从成立之初到 1910 年，这四家银行均将 80%至 90%的净利润作为股息发放。 <sup>57</sup> 如此高的派息率直至二战前仍是行业常态。 <sup>58</sup> 因此，通过利润来增加储备的空间十分有限。 这两家英国银行的与众不同之处在于，它们的董事们相对不愿向股东筹集新资金。我们有理由认为，这种不愿接触股东的态度，部分是由于其相对较差的盈利表现。</span></span></span>

There is little ground for supposing that the British banks were less efficient than the two Australian banks with whom their earnings are compared. There is no significant difference in the average proportion of their assets held in zero or low-yielding areas such as cash, bullion, loans at call or government paper. The Australian banks held an average of 28.65 per cent while the British banks followed close behind with 27.5 per cent. The Bank of Australasia and Union Bank enjoyed lower-than-industry-average ratios of non-performing loans, at least in the 1880s when loan losses increased alarmingly.<sup>59</sup> The cost of funds and rates of interest earned on loans, and scales of fees and commissions, were the same for both sets of banks. It is possible that the non-interest operating costs were consistently higher in the British banks. Salary structures were far from uniform between banks before the 1920s, but the union-sponsored extension of industry awards <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_79"></span>thereafter meant that staff of similar age and those undertaking the same functions received the same remuneration.<sup>60</sup> In the light of these developments, it is highly unlikely that the British banks would have paid above-average rates over the long term. British banks may have been over-staffed but again this is unlikely to have been condoned by cost-conscious senior management and frugal boards of directors. It has been suggested recently that the cost structures of these two British banks were out of line with industry norms from the 1880s until 1914.<sup>61</sup> Unfortunately, that conclusion must be questioned as the output measurement data, defined as the value of deposits and advances, of nine of the other ten banks in the sample is grossly inflated by the inclusion of frozen deposit liabilities carried over from their reconstructions after the 1893 bank crashes.<sup>62</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">几乎没有理由认为英国银行比与之比较收益的两家澳大利亚银行效率更低。它们在现金、金条、短期贷款或政府票据等零收益或低收益资产上的平均持有比例并无显著差异。澳大利亚银行平均持有 28.65%，而英国银行紧随其后，为 27.5%。至少在上世纪 80 年代贷款损失惊人增加时，澳大利亚银行和联合银行的不良贷款率低于行业平均水平。 <sup>59</sup> 两家银行的资金成本、贷款利息收入以及费用和佣金标准都相同。有可能英国银行的非利息运营成本持续较高。 1920 年代以前，各家银行的薪资结构相差甚远，但此后由工会推动的行业奖励制度扩展，使得年龄相仿、职能相同的员工获得了相同的薪酬。 <sup>60</sup> 基于这些发展，英国银行长期支付高于平均水平的薪资是不太可能的。英国银行或许存在人员冗余，但这同样不太可能得到具有成本意识的资深管理层和节俭的董事会的认可。最近有观点指出，从 1880 年代到 1914 年，这两家英国银行的成本结构与行业标准存在偏差。 <sup>61</sup> 遗憾的是，这一结论必须受到质疑，因为样本中其他十家银行中的九家，其产出衡量数据（即存款和垫款的价值）被严重夸大了，原因是其中包含了 1893 年银行倒闭后重组时结转的冻结存款负债。 <sup>62</sup> </span></span></span>

Any explanation of the relatively poor financial performance of the British banks must rest on the quality of management decisions rather than on any inherent disability resulting from their Britishness. Compared to many - perhaps most - of the other British overseas banks they operated in a paradise. Their foreignness was not a serious problem in winning custom from the public or governments, although there were temporary expressions of antagonism in the 1890s and 1930s, particularly from the left of the political spectrum, towards British banks. They were not singled out for attack by state-sponsored banks, nor were their assets expropriated. Australian authorities did not block the remittance of their profits. The currency risks which faced those English banks trading in the East and Latin America with their depreciated silver or paper currencies were virtually eliminated by the stability of the sterling-Australian exchange rate. They were immune from the spreading competition from American and German banks that afflicted British overseas banks in central and Latin America.<sup>63</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对于英国银行相对较差的财务表现，任何解释都必须归结于管理决策的质量，而非其身为英国银行所固有的某种缺陷。与许多——甚至可能是大多数——其他英国海外银行相比，它们简直是在天堂里经营。尽管在 19 世纪 90 年代和 20 世纪 30 年代，尤其是政治光谱左翼对英国银行曾出现过短暂的敌意表达，但它们的“外国身份”在从公众或政府那里赢得客户方面并非严重问题。它们并未成为国有银行专门攻击的目标，资产也未被没收。澳大利亚当局并未阻止其利润汇回。而那些在东方和拉丁美洲以贬值的白银或纸币进行交易的英国银行所面临的货币风险，实际上因英镑与澳大利亚汇率的稳定而几乎被消除了。 它们免受美国和德国银行日益激烈的竞争影响，而这场竞争曾使英国在中美洲和拉丁美洲的海外银行深受其害。 <sup>63</sup> </span></span></span>

Ironically, the most serious problems facing British banks operating in Australia and New Zealand resulted from developments within the United Kingdom economy. The higher rates of the United Kingdom company tax placed the English banks at a disadvantage in matching their more lightly taxed competitors<sup>64</sup> and the complexity of obtaining tax relief for Australian resident shareholders contributed to a strong drift to the London register. A fragile balance of payments position in the United Kingdom weakened the value of sterling in the late 1960s and early 1970s. This posed serious difficulties for the Australia and New Zealand Banking Group as its shareholders' funds were falling in value relative to its liabilities in Australian and New Zealand dollars. <span id="Ch04.xhtml_page_80"></span>This realignment of currency values was exacerbated by the imposition of tighter controls on capital transfers out of the United Kingdom and widening restrictions on the inflow of capital into Australia. The transfer of domicile of the last British bank from London to Melbourne, Australia, in 1976 signalled that costs of being a foreign bank outweighed the benefits.<sup>65</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">讽刺的是，在澳大利亚和新西兰经营的英国银行所面临的最严重问题，竟源于英国国内的经济变化。英国较高的公司税率使这些银行在与其税率较低的竞争对手较量时处于劣势 <sup>64</sup> ，而澳大利亚居民股东获得税收减免的复杂程序，也导致大量业务向伦敦注册地转移。20 世纪 60 年代末至 70 年代初，英国国际收支状况脆弱，英镑价值随之走弱。这对澳新银行集团（Australia and New Zealand Banking Group）造成了严重困难，因为其股东资金相对于其在澳元和新西兰元计价的负债而言不断贬值。英国对资本外流实施更严格的控制，以及澳大利亚对资本流入的限制日益扩大，进一步加剧了这种货币价值的重新调整。 1976 年，最后一家英国银行将其注册地从伦敦迁至澳大利亚墨尔本，这标志着作为外资银行的成本已超过收益。 <sup>65</sup> </span></span></span>

Conclusion<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">结论</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

British banks have traded in the Australian market since the beginning of modern banking in that country. Their arrival was an entrepreneurial response to the opportunities presented by the inability of domestic institutions to meet the rapidly-expanding demands for a wider range of financial services. Their enormous initial success was gradually competed away as the source of the early advantages could not be protected by inalienable property rights. By the turn of the century there was little to distinguish British from Australian banks except in terms of financial performance. The very conservative attitudes of the British directors, particularly towards the capital base of their balance sheets, retarded their earnings on equity. Their financial performance was not only inferior to their Australian competitors but also to a number of other British overseas banks.<sup>66</sup> No fundamental changes in strategy, such as withdrawal, were ever contemplated. Entering other markets was not a realistic option as the Anglo-Australian banks did not possess the competences that would have been required to operate profitably in Africa, Latin America, the Middle East or the Far East. Their highly fragmented shareholdings, at least until the 1950s, enabled the directors and senior management to champion their own interests unopposed. Yet for all their sins, the British banks in Australia have outlived many of the other overseas banks that began life serving the financial consequences of Pax Britannica. The recent acquisition of Grindlays Bank, with a pedigree that extends back to the 1820s,<sup>67</sup> by the Australia and New Zealand Banking Group highlights the resilience of the first generation of multinational banks. It, together with the Standard Chartered, the Hongkong Bank, Lloyds and Barclays continue to build on the foundations laid so long ago.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">自现代银行业在澳大利亚诞生之初，英国银行便已涉足该国市场。它们的到来是一种企业家式的回应，抓住国内金融机构无法满足快速扩张的多元化金融服务需求所带来的机遇。然而，由于早期优势无法通过不可剥夺的产权来保护，它们最初取得的巨大成功逐渐被竞争所蚕食。到世纪之交，除了财务业绩之外，英国银行与澳大利亚银行已几无区别。英国董事们极为保守的态度——尤其是对其资产负债表资本基础的看法——拖累了它们的股权收益。它们的财务表现不仅逊于澳大利亚的竞争对手，也不及一些其他英国海外银行。 <sup>66</sup> 诸如撤资之类的根本性战略调整，从未被纳入考虑。 进入其他市场并非现实选择，因为英澳银行并不具备在非洲、拉美、中东或远东地区实现盈利运营所需的能力。它们高度分散的股权结构（至少直到 20 世纪 50 年代）使得董事和高管能够毫无阻碍地维护自身利益。尽管存在诸多弊端，这些在澳大利亚的英国银行却比许多其他因应"不列颠治世"金融后果而兴起的外国银行活得更久。近期，澳大利亚和新西兰银行集团收购了历史可追溯至 19 世纪 20 年代的 Grindlays Bank，这凸显了第一代跨国银行的韧性。该行与渣打银行、汇丰银行、劳埃德银行和巴克莱银行一道，继续在早已奠定的根基上发展壮大。</span></span></span>
