<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">序幕：“我们必须做点什么”</span></span></span>

The Yokohama Specie Bank — predecessor of the Bank of Tokyo —opened its doors in February 1880, a direct response of the Japanese government to western initiatives. The foreigners had, from their arrival in the treaty ports in 1859, drained Japan of gold and silver coins. This had a severe effect in Japan proper and weakened the Japanese economy and their ability to bargain. Several of the western banks had established offices in Yokohama; indeed the Oriental Bank acted for the Japanese government in its foreign dealings.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">横滨正金银行——东京银行的前身——于 1880 年 2 月正式开业，这是日本政府为应对西方势力主动出击而采取的直接举措。自 1859 年外国人进入通商口岸以来，他们便不断从日本攫取金银硬币。这给日本本土带来了严重影响，削弱了日本经济及其议价能力。当时已有数家西方银行在横滨设立办事处；事实上，东方银行曾代理日本政府处理其对外事务。</span></span></span>

As government finances, already precarious because of the changes going on internally, became more difficult, the Japanese government resolved to take control of its own external financing. To this end it set up the Yokohama Specie Bank. The office was in Yokohama, in the lion's den, where foreigners were most powerful and where, so close to Tokyo, they constituted the greatest affront to the Japanese. Its objective was to collect specie, in coins of whatever sort with a high gold and silver content, from anywhere in the world. Within months of the Bank's opening in Yokohama, offices were opened in London and New York. It was to work in close liaison with the Bank of Japan (f. 1882) to give Japan ultimately a position of strength in the world money markets which would eliminate the dangers of financial manipulation by outsiders. No other underdeveloped country had attempted such a programme. The outcome of this policy and the success and failure of the Yokohama Specie Bank are discussed in this chapter.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">由于国内形势变化已经岌岌可危的政府财政变得更加困难，日本政府决心掌控自身的对外融资渠道。为此，他们设立了横滨正金银行。总行设在横滨——这个"龙潭虎穴"之地，外国势力最为强大，且因紧邻东京，对日本人构成了最大的羞辱。该行的目标是搜罗来自世界各地的金币银币，只要金银含量高的硬币来者不拒。横滨分行开业后数月内，伦敦和纽约分行相继开业。它与日本银行（1882 年成立）紧密协作，最终使日本在全球货币市场占据强势地位，从而消除外部势力金融操纵的风险。没有任何其他欠发达国家曾尝试过这样的计划。 本章讨论了这一政策的结果以及 Yokohama Specie Bank 的成功与失败。</span></span></span>

The Meiji Restoration in 1868, the ensuing civil wars and rapid industrialization cost the Meiji government much. The huge expenditure was only met by the issue of masses of inconvertible government notes, which by the early 1870s had accelerated inflation and encouraged the disappearance of specie. The<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1868 年的明治维新以及随后的内战与快速工业化，让明治政府付出了高昂的代价。巨额的支出只能靠大量发行不可兑换的政府纸币来填补，到 19 世纪 70 年代初，这已加剧了通货膨胀，并导致硬通货逐渐消失。</span></span></span>

National Bank Decree of 1872 aimed to improve the situation, but only four banks were set up under the Decree. An amendment of 1876 did away with the requirement that the banknotes should be convertible into specie. This led to a wild bank mania.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1872 年的《国家银行法令》旨在改善这一局面，但该法令下仅成立了四家银行。1876 年的一项修正案取消了纸币需兑换为铸币的要求，这引发了狂热的银行投机潮。</span></span></span>

During the five years from 1876 to 1881, 603 banks joined the Japanese bank system bringing the total number to 607. These could be classified in three groups. The first and most important group, of 148 national banks, was based upon the Amended National Bank Decree of 1876. They were modelled on counterparts in America. The owners had limited liability and were authorized to issue banknotes. Their total capital stock amounted to ¥43.89 million, a considerable part of which came from the national bonds paid to <span class="italic">ex-samurais</span> as compensation for the loss of their former stipends. The second group consisted of 90 informal private banks, from which the <span class="italic">zaibatsu</span> banks grew later, with the total capital of ¥10.45 million. The last group was composed of 369 quasi-banking companies — in fact, these were small moneylenders with tiny deposits and a total capital of only ¥5.89 million.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从 1876 年到 1881 年的五年间，共有 603 家银行加入日本银行体系，使得银行总数增至 607 家。这些银行可分为三类。第一类也是最重要的一类，是依据 1876 年《修订国立银行法令》设立的 148 家国立银行。它们以美国同行模式为蓝本。所有者承担有限责任，并获准发行银行券。其总资本金达 4389 万日元，其中相当一部分来自支付给前武士作为其原俸禄补偿的国债。第二类包括 90 家非正式私人银行，后来的财阀银行即由此发展而来，总资本为 1045 万日元。最后一类由 369 家准银行公司组成——实际上，这些小规模放贷机构存款极少，总资本仅有 589 万日元。</span></span></span>

Such bank promotion was undoubtedly a response to strong demand from an active economy.<sup>2</sup> But the resultant accelerated inflation was dangerous and damaging. The Satsuma Rebellion of 1877, the last large-scale <span class="italic">ex-samurai</span> rising, sent government expenditure soaring. This caused note circulation to jump from ¥24 million in 1868 to ¥105 million in 1876, and further to ¥139 million in 1878. Inevitably, the specie reserve ratio decreased from 14 per cent in 1876 to 12 per cent in 1878. Specie shortage was seriously felt. It was against this disastrous background that the scheme for the Specie Bank was floated.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这种银行促销活动无疑是对活跃经济中强劲需求的回应。 <sup>2</sup> 但由此引发的通胀加速既危险又具有破坏性。1877 年的西南战争——最后一次大规模原武士起义——导致政府支出激增。这使得纸币发行量从 1868 年的 2400 万日元飙升至 1876 年的 1.05 亿日元，并在 1878 年进一步增至 1.39 亿日元。不可避免的是，硬通货储备率从 1876 年的 14%降至 1878 年的 12%。硬通货短缺问题变得极为严峻。正是在这一灾难性背景下，正金银行的计划应运而生。</span></span></span>

Specie was, thus, a crucial national issue from the outset of the <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_193"></span>Meiji government, and continued to be so right up to 1931 when Japan abandoned the gold standard. The projected Specie Bank was, therefore, destined to serve the nation by collecting specie worldwide. The progress of Japan and its economy was to be reflected by the success or failure of the Specie Bank.<sup>3</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">因此，硬通货从明治政府成立之初起就是一个关乎国运的关键问题，并且一直持续到 1931 年日本放弃金本位制。正金银行的规划也因此注定要肩负起在全球范围内收集硬通货、服务国家的使命。日本及其经济的发展进程，将通过正金银行的成败得以体现。 <sup>3</sup> </span></span></span>

At this point specie must be defined. Specie is said to be 'gold and silver coins and bullion, as distinguished from paper money'.<sup>4</sup> This definition was also applicable to the Japanese usage during the nineteenth century. The definition, however, changed over time certainly due to Japan's commitment, through the Specie Bank, with the London money market. From around the turn of the century, the term 'specie' began to include foreign currencies, particularly pounds sterling and dollars held in the account at the Specie Bank London office.<sup>5</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">此时需要对“specie”（硬币/正币）一词进行定义。所谓 specie，指的是“黄金和白银铸币及条块，以区别于纸币”。 <sup>4</sup> 这一定义同样适用于 19 世纪日本的用法。然而，随着时间推移，这一定义发生了变化——这无疑是由于日本通过 Specie Bank 与伦敦货币市场建立的紧密联系。大约从世纪之交开始，“specie”一词开始涵盖外币，特别是存储在 Specie Bank 伦敦办事处账户中的英镑和美元。 <sup>5</sup> </span></span></span>

A beginning is made, 1879—1889<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">一个开端：1879—1889 年</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

It was Shigenobu Okuma - vice-minister 1869-1871, and minister of finance from 1873 to 1880 - who was responsible for overall financial policies during the 1870s. In the wake of the assassination of Toshimichi Okubo the most distinguished Meiji leader in the government, in 1878, serious discord arose among the cabinet members and Okuma became a target of attack. In trying to outwit his opponents, Okuma put forward the proposal of setting up a Specie Bank. Japanese merchants, centred around Yokohama, the busiest treaty port, had also thought of a similar scheme. The two groups joined forces and the Yokohama Specie Bank was launched in November 1879. According to the prospectus, the Bank was expected 'to supply and circulate gold and silver coins'.<sup>6</sup> The nominal capital stock was ¥3 million, of which ¥1.4 million was to be paid in silver specie and ¥1.6 million in government notes. The government would contribute ¥1 million specie to the capital. The Bank was established according to the Amended National Bank Decree, though it had no right of issue. The Bank opened its doors in February 1880.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1870 年代，大隈重信（1869-1871 年任副大臣，1873-1880 年任大藏卿）负责总体财政政策。1878 年，明治政府中最杰出的领袖大久保利通遇刺后，内阁成员间出现严重分歧，大隈成为攻击目标。为挫败对手，大隈提出设立"正金银行"的方案。以最繁忙通商口岸横滨为中心的日本商人也曾构想类似计划。两方联手，横滨正金银行于 1879 年 11 月应运而生。据其章程，该行旨在"供应和流通金银铸币"。 <sup>6</sup> 名义资本为 300 万日元，其中 140 万日元以银币形式缴付，160 万日元以政府纸币形式缴付。政府将以 100 万日元银币注资。 该银行根据修订后的《国家银行法令》设立，但无发行权。银行于 1880 年 2 月开业。</span></span></span>

The regulations of the Bank, composed of 49 articles, stipulated precisely its organization, activities and procedures. According to Article 36, the Bank was allowed to conduct every sort of ordinary banking. Article 37 stipulated that, though the business was carried out chiefly by using Japanese ¥1 silver coins, the Bank was sanctioned to issue silver drafts if customers requested.<sup>7</sup> The silver drafts were expected to strengthen its lending capacity, supplement the amount of silver specie and encourage competitiveness with foreign, mainly British, banks at that time dominant in Yokohama, <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_194"></span>Kobe and Nagasaki. Article 49 of the Bank's regulations gave the minister of finance powers to interfere in the business conduct of the Bank and in the selection of the governor, directors and general manager.<sup>8</sup> The Bank was thus an institution tightly controlled by the government.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">该银行规章制度共 49 条，对其组织架构、业务活动及运作流程作出了详尽规定。第 36 条明确，银行可开展各类常规银行业务。第 37 条规定，尽管主要业务以日本 1 日元银币结算，但若客户提出要求，银行有权发行银票。 <sup>7</sup> 发行银票旨在增强贷款能力、补充白银储备，并提升与当时主导横滨、神户及长崎金融市场的外资银行（主要为英资银行）的竞争力。银行规章第 49 条赋予财政大臣干预银行经营决策、行长及董事总经理任免的权力。 <sup>8</sup> 由此可见，该银行实为政府严密掌控的金融机构。</span></span></span>

Before regular business could be undertaken, the Bank had to negotiate further with the government on a crucial matter. During the first half of the Meiji era, Japan's staple export goods were silk and tea,<sup>9</sup> both highly vulnerable to seasonal fluctuation. From time to time stocks of these goods accumulated in Japanese warehouses in Yokohama, Kobe and Nagasaki. A buyer's market sometimes forced the sellers to dump their goods at giveaway prices into the hands of foreign merchants. This resulted in heavy losses. Could the government not intervene and cushion the Japanese merchants from such daunting losses? It would be even better if government intervention could so operate as to encourage a net import of specie. This was the objective of both the government and the Bank.<sup>10</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在正式开展业务之前，银行必须先就一个关键问题与政府进一步协商。明治时代初期，日本的主要出口商品是生丝和茶叶， <sup>9</sup> 这两类商品都极易受季节波动影响。横滨、神户和长崎的日本仓库里时常积压着这些货物。买方市场有时迫使卖家以跳楼价将货物倾销给外国商人，导致惨重损失。政府能否出手干预，帮日本商人减轻这种可怕的损失？如果政府干预还能促使贵金属净流入，那就更理想了。这正是政府和银行共同的目标。 <sup>10</sup> </span></span></span>

In the first place the government had to loan out its notes to the Specie Bank. Then the Bank had to arrange with Japanese merchants, who used to sell directly to foreign merchants, to advance them government notes on the security of their exporting goods. Lastly the Bank's agencies had to be established in foreign financial centres, such as London and New York, the ultimate destination of the goods. If the plans worked specie would flow in.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">首先，政府不得不将纸币贷给正金银行。接着，银行需与那些过去直接向外国商人销售的日本商人协调，以他们的出口货物为抵押预支政府纸币。最后，银行必须在商品的最终目的地——如伦敦和纽约等国外金融中心设立代理机构。若此计划奏效，金银将随之流入。</span></span></span>

On the acceptance of securities for advances of government notes, the Bank drew bills of exchange on foreign importers on its customers' behalf. These were immediately forwarded to the agent. Upon receiving the bills, the agent quickly collected the payments, exchanging them into specie and remitting it by telegram to the head office in Yokohama. Arrivals of specie at Yokohama effectively meant the completion of repayments by Japanese merchants. Finally the working of the system required the transfer of specie from the Bank to the government. The mechanism, which was termed <span class="italic">goyogaikokunigawase,</span> authorized foreign exchange business for promoting direct exports — which was, indeed, the method of depriving the foreign banks of their customers.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在接受政府票据预付款的证券时，银行代表其客户向外国进口商开具汇票。这些汇票立即被寄送给代理行。代理行收到汇票后迅速收款，兑换成铸币并通过电报汇至横滨总行。铸币抵达横滨实际上意味着日本商人完成还款。最后，该机制的运行要求银行将铸币转移给政府。这一被称为“御用外国为替”的机制，授权了促进直接出口的外汇业务——这实际上是剥夺外国银行客户的方法。</span></span></span>

The agreement of the note loan was signed between the government and the Bank in May 1880 — the sum of ¥500,000 in notes was lent to the Bank. By the summer of 1880, the Bank's agents were set to work in London and New York. The business progressed moderately during the first year and a half. Demands for foreign exchange business were growing steadily. The amount of the government note loan was increased to ¥3.5 million in <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_195"></span>October 1880 and further to ¥4.5 million in July 1881. The busier the foreign exchange business became the more specie flew in. Indeed, by December 1880 the stock of specie in the government's safe amounted to ¥7.2 million, by December 1881 it was ¥12.7 million, and by December 1882 it was ¥16.7 million<sup>11</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">政府与银行于 1880 年 5 月签订了票据贷款协议——银行获得 50 万日元票据贷款。到 1880 年夏季，银行的代理机构在伦敦和纽约开始运营。业务在头一年半里进展平稳。外汇业务的需求稳步增长。政府票据贷款金额于 1880 年 10 月增至 350 万日元，1881 年 7 月进一步增至 450 万日元。外汇业务越繁忙，流入的黄金就越充裕。事实上，到 1880 年 12 月，政府金库中的黄金储备已达 720 万日元，1881 年 12 月为 1270 万日元，1882 年 12 月则为 1670 万日元 <sup>11</sup> 。</span></span></span>

The success of the Japanese plan for specie collection was marred by the constitutional crisis which culminated in a coup in October 1881, during which Okuma, the great supporter of the Specie Bank, was ousted. Immediately following the coup, Masay-oshi Matsukata came to power as minister of finance and at once put forward radical and deflationary policies. Their effects were soon felt. The consumer's price index fell sharply from 41.8 (1934/36=100) in 1881 to 32.3 in 1884. The business of the Specie Bank deteriorated seriously; the export trades, particularly those of silk, became very depressed. The Bank incurred a serious loss of ¥1.07 million. This led to a severe conflict between the Bank directors and the ministry of finance, which resulted in changes in the management of the Bank. The right of the government to intervene, according to Article 49 of the Bank's regulations, was invoked, and the governor of the Bank was dismissed. Rokuro Hara, president of the 100th National Bank and a close friend of Matsukata, was appointed to succeed as governor in March 1883.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">日本铸币收集计划的成功因宪政危机而受挫，这场危机在 1881 年 10 月的政变中达到顶峰，期间铸币银行的重要支持者大隈重信被赶下台。政变后不久，松方正義出任大藏大臣，随即推行激进的通货紧缩政策，其效果很快显现。消费者价格指数从 1881 年的 41.8（1934/36=100）急剧降至 1884 年的 32.3。铸币银行的业务严重恶化，出口贸易，尤其是丝绸出口，陷入极度萧条。该银行遭受了 107 万日元的巨额亏损，这导致银行董事与大藏省之间爆发严重冲突，最终引发银行管理层变动。政府依据银行章程第 49 条行使干预权，解除了银行总裁的职务。 1883 年 3 月，原六郎（第一百国民银行行长，亦是松方的密友）被任命接任总裁之职。</span></span></span>

Matsukata's tight control of the Specie Bank should be noted in relation to the establishment of a central bank, the Bank of Japan, in October 1882, After detailed examinations of European banking and at the suggestion of Leon Say, the French financial minister, Matsukata had decided to base the Bank of Japan on the model of the National Bank of Belgium.<sup>12</sup> However, the successful issue of Bank of Japan notes necessarily required an adequate supply of specie. Recognizing this, the new governor, Hara, thoroughly inspected the conduct of the Specie Bank's business and made important changes, especially in the methods of remittance and in the examination of exporting goods. Although there was a potential confrontation between the two Banks concerning supremacy in foreign exchange business, specie did increasingly accumulate in the government's safe. By the end of 1884, there was almost ¥34 million and the goal of parity between silver value and paper money was in sight. In May 1885, the Bank of Japan launched its issue of ¥5 million silver-convertible banknotes. The Specie Bank had indeed played an essential part in this achievement.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">松方对正金银行的严格控制，与 1882 年 10 月日本中央银行——日本银行的建立息息相关。在详细考察欧洲银行业并采纳法国财政部长莱昂·赛的建议后，松方决定以比利时国家银行为蓝本构建日本银行。 <sup>12</sup> 然而，日本银行纸币的成功发行必须要有充足的铸币储备作为支撑。新任总裁原氏深谙此道，对正金银行的业务运作进行了彻底审查，并实施了重大改革，尤其是在汇款流程和出口货物查验方面。尽管两家银行在外汇业务主导权上存在潜在对峙，但铸币储备确实在政府金库中日益积累。到 1884 年底，储备金已接近 3400 万日元，银价与纸币平价的目标指日可待。 1885 年 5 月，日本银行正式发行了 500 万日元的银本位兑换券。在这一成就中，横滨正金银行确实发挥了关键作用。</span></span></span>

In the meantime, the government strengthened the Specie Bank by The Yokohama Specie Bank Decree of 1887. This stipulated <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_196"></span>that the minister of finance could appoint the deputy governor of the Bank of Japan concurrently as governor of the Yokohama Specie Bank and also that the governor of the Yokohama Specie Bank could act concurrently as director of the Bank of Japan.<sup>13</sup> The government thus reinforced its grip over the two Banks, which were tightly interlinked. In this connection whenever the two Banks had to co-operate on national issues in later years they did it by exchanging senior personnel.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">与此同时，日本政府通过 1887 年颁布的《横滨正金银行条例》强化了该银行的地位。该条例规定，日本银行副总裁可由大藏大臣任命，同时兼任横滨正金银行总裁，而横滨正金银行总裁也可兼任日本银行理事。 <sup>13</sup> 如此一来，政府进一步巩固了对这两家紧密关联的银行的控制。正因如此，日后每当两行需要就国家事务展开合作时，都会通过互换高级管理人员来实现。</span></span></span>

Eventually in the autumn of 1889, when it became evident that the governor of the Bank of Japan was denying special facilities to the Specie Bank, he was dismissed. Under the new regime, the Bank of Japan arranged to give special discounting facilities up to ¥10 million at 2 per cent rate to the Specie Bank. In this way the Yokohama Specie Bank was firmly integrated into the banking system of Japan and its future was secured.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">终于在 1889 年秋，当日本银行行长明显拒绝为正金银行提供特殊便利时，他被解职了。新政权上台后，日本银行安排向正金银行提供最高 1000 万日元、利率 2% 的特别贴现便利。通过这种方式，横滨正金银行被牢固地纳入了日本的银行体系，其未来也有了保障。</span></span></span>

Struggle for survival, 1889—1898<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">求生之路，1889—1898</span></span></span>

The branch network of the Specie Bank during the 1880s was rather larger than those of the national and private banks, which usually did their business through one or, at most, two offices. The Specie Bank had, by 1889, five offices. These were branches in Kobe and London, and agent's offices in New York, San Francisco<sup>14</sup> and Lyons. The international connections which Japan needed at this stage of her economic development could only be achieved through an institution such as the Specie Bank with full government support.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪 80 年代，正金银行的分行网络远比通常只通过一个、最多两个营业点开展业务的国家银行和私立银行要庞大。到 1889 年，正金银行已拥有五个营业点，分别是神户分行、伦敦分行，以及设在纽约、旧金山和里昂的代理处。日本在这一经济发展阶段所需的国际联系，只有通过像正金银行这样得到政府全力支持的机构才能实现。</span></span></span>

Before 1889 the government itself carried the risks which arose from foreign exchange transactions. After being granted special discounting facilities at the Bank of Japan the Specie Bank had to consider ways of avoiding risks. This they did by hedging in foreign exchange markets. In November 1889, the Bank transferred ¥500,000 to the London branch to form an exchange fund there. This move coincided with a major economic crisis in Japan during the early months of 1890. Japan experienced unprecedented conditions of tight money and heavy falls in security prices. The Specie Bank strengthened its London position by adding £600,000 to the exchange fund. Though the crisis had passed by the middle of 1890, another disaster was looming that autumn when the United States moved on to a free coinage of silver. Silver prices fluctuated violently for a couple of months. The Bank incurred a heavy loss of ¥1.5 million mainly due to its large portfolio of bills payable in gold. The underlying weakness lay in the fact that the Japanese monetary system was still founded effectively on dual <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_197"></span>metals, silver and gold. Unfortunately for the Bank, another loss of ¥500,00, caused by a failure of one of its customers, fell upon it in May 1891. These misfortunes necessarily impelled the Bank to instigate a thorough review of its business conduct.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1889 年之前，日本政府自身承担着外汇交易带来的风险。在获得 Bank of Japan 的特别贴现便利后，Specie Bank 不得不考虑规避风险的方法。他们通过在远期外汇市场进行套期保值来实现这一目的。1889 年 11 月，该银行向伦敦分行转移了 50 万日元，在那里设立了一笔外汇基金。这一举措恰逢 1890 年初日本爆发重大经济危机。日本经历了前所未有的银根紧缩和证券价格大幅下跌。Specie Bank 又向外汇基金追加了 60 万英镑，以加强其在伦敦的头寸。尽管危机在 1890 年年中已过去，但当年秋天美国转向白银自由铸造时，另一场灾难又迫在眉睫。白银价格在几个月内剧烈波动。该银行因持有大量以黄金支付的票据而遭受了 150 万日元的重大损失。 根本问题在于，日本的货币体系实际上仍然以金银双本位为基础。不幸的是，1891 年 5 月，日本银行又因一家客户的倒闭而损失了 50 万日元。这些接连的打击，迫使日本银行不得不开始对其业务行为进行全面审查。</span></span></span>

In June 1891, the Bank re-organized its foreign exchange business, which had hitherto remained the same since the onset of <span class="italic">goyogaikokunigawase.</span> The Bank reported,<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1891 年 6 月，日本银行重组了其外汇业务，自 goyogaikokunigawase 启动以来，该业务一直未曾改变。日本银行报告称：</span></span></span>

<div class="quotes">

Reviews of foreign trades of the country reveal that Japan exports goods to the United States and France and imports products from Britain. The agent's offices in the United States and France are, therefore, requested to be engaged exclusively in collecting payments of export bills and forwarding them to London. Using them as resources, the London branch should draw import bills on Japan thus covering exchange risks.<sup>15</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对日本对外贸易的审查显示，日本向美国和法国出口商品，并从英国进口产品。因此，要求设在美国和法国的代理机构专门负责收取出口汇票的款项，并将其汇往伦敦。伦敦分行应利用这些资金，开具对日本的进口汇票，从而覆盖汇率风险。 <sup>15</sup> </span></span></span>

</div>

Following this observation the Bank divided the branch network into two groups. The first group, headed by the London branch, was composed of offices in gold-standard areas — agent's offices in New York, San Francisco and Lyons, and joined later, in 1892, by the Hawaii office. The second group, led by the head office, consisted of offices in silver-standard areas. In 1891 the Kobe branch was the only element of this group, but it was soon joined by three further offices — Shanghai (1893), Bombay (1894) and Hong Kong (1896). All were requested to direct their foreign exchange transactions in consultation with the London branch and Yokohama head office.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">根据这一观察，银行将分行网络划分为两组。第一组以伦敦分行为首，包括位于金本位地区的办事处——纽约、旧金山和里昂的代理处，随后在 1892 年又加入了夏威夷办事处。第二组以总行为首，由位于银本位地区的办事处组成。1891 年时，该组仅有神户分行一家，但很快又增加了三家办事处——上海（1893 年）、孟买（1894 年）和香港（1896 年）。所有办事处均被要求在与伦敦分行及横滨总行协商后开展外汇交易业务。</span></span></span>

The silver crisis reaffirmed the difficulties inherent in manipulating two metals. The classical issue of bimetallism thus again became a focus of political discussions, which culminated in the appointment of a monetary commission by the ministry of finance immediately following the departure of India from the silver standard in the autumn of 1893. A special committee, installed in the monetary commission, sat from October 1893 to March 1895, when by chance the Sino-Japanese War, which had started in July 1894, ended in Japan's victory. The proceedings of the commission were speeded up to consider the implications of large sums due as indemnity from China. Matsukata, who had resigned from the office of financial minister in August 1892, was again called in to keep a close watch on the decisions taken. In June 1895, the commission submitted its report recommending the adoption of the gold standard by Japan.<sup>16</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">白银危机再次证实了操控两种金属的固有困难。古典的复本位制问题因此再次成为政治讨论的焦点，最终在 1893 年秋印度脱离银本位制后，财政部立即任命了一个货币委员会。该委员会下设的一个特别小组从 1893 年 10 月持续工作到 1895 年 3 月，恰逢 1894 年 7 月爆发的甲午战争以日本胜利告终。委员会的议程因此加快，以审议中国应支付巨额赔款的影响。松方正义于 1892 年 8 月辞去财政大臣职务后，再次被召来密切监督各项决策。1895 年 6 月，委员会提交报告，建议日本采纳金本位制。 <sup>16</sup> </span></span></span>

The ministry of finance acted quickly in readiness to receive the indemnity money. The manager of the Bank of Japan was transferred to the board of directors of the Specie Bank, and one of the <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_198"></span>officials at the former became the head office manager of the latter. In October 1895 the first payment of the indemnity was effected in London. Before proceeding to the second receipt, the ministry instructed the Bank of Japan to give the Specie Bank London branch a mandatory command over the indemnity business. With power of attorney dated January 1896, the Specie Bank in London was entrusted with 'all the business thereof In any way'.<sup>17</sup> The Bank of England, which had stubbornly declined to open an account for the Specie Bank, finally agreed in September 1896. With the accolade of an account with the Bank of England in London, the Specie Bank was finally admitted to the international banking world. The London branch of the Specie Bank completed the collection and remittance of the indemnity money by May 1898. The sum received amounted to more than ¥360 million. The government passed ¥85 million to the Bank of Japan as specie reserve for conversion and of this at least ¥11 million was actually placed in the Specie Bank London branch. This was the origin of <span class="italic">zaigaiseika,</span> i.e., specie kept abroad, that which would constitute part of reserves for the Bank of Japan note issue and which was an essential portion of Japanese foreign exchange funds.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">日本财政部迅速行动，为接收赔款做好准备。日本银行总裁调任正金银行董事会，一名前官员则出任正金银行总行经理。1895 年 10 月，首批赔款在伦敦完成支付。在第二次收款前，财政部指示日本银行授予正金银行伦敦分行处理赔款业务的强制管辖权。凭借 1896 年 1 月出具的授权书，伦敦正金银行被委托"全权处理一切相关业务"。此前一直拒绝为正金银行开立账户的英格兰银行，最终于 1896 年 9 月同意。随着在伦敦英格兰银行开设账户这一殊荣的获得，正金银行终于得以跻身国际银行业界。 到 1898 年 5 月，正金银行伦敦分行完成了赔款的收付和汇兑。收到的赔款总额超过 3.6 亿日元。日本政府将其中 8500 万日元拨给日本银行作为兑换用的硬通货储备，其中至少 1100 万日元实际存放于正金银行伦敦分行。这便是"在外正货"（即存放于国外的硬通货）的起源，这些资金既构成日本银行纸币发行的部分准备金，也是日本外汇资金的重要组成部分。</span></span></span>

It was in this period that some curiously termed accounts began to make regular appearances in the Specie Bank balance sheets. The most noteworthy were 'selling exchange' on the liabilities side, and 'buying exchange' on the assets side. 'Selling exchange' was the sale of foreign bills of exchange, which meant that the Bank was supplying payment instruments to importers. Proceeds of 'selling exchange' joined deposits and other items on the liabilities side to enhance the Bank's lending abilities. 'Buying exchange' was the purchase of foreign bills of exchange, which meant that the Bank was supplying advances to exporters. These devices were a further development of <span class="italic">goyogaikokunigawase.</span> Interest rate, or discounting rate, charged on 'buying exchange' was higher than on inland bills, as the former involved foreign exchange risks. Another strangely termed account on the assets side was 'interest bills'. They were bills of exchange payable in yen mainly used in trades to China. The bill was so termed as the rate of interest charged on it was stamped on its face. They did not involve foreign exchange risks as they were payable in yen. Lastly, 'rediscounting', though not curious, should be mentioned. As arranged with the Bank of Japan in 1889, the Specie Bank was entitled to enjoy rediscounting of foreign bills of exchange up to ¥10 million at 2 per cent. From 1896, actual figures in this account exceeded the sum negotiated with the Bank of Japan. Indeed, the Specie Bank found another important source for bank money. In 1897, Parr's <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_199"></span>Bank, Union Bank of London and London Joint Stock Bank agreed to accept foreign bills of exchange on behalf of the Specie Bank, which were then rediscounted in the London money market. Limits of their acceptance were said to be between £100,000 and £200,000, though the exact sums are unknown.<sup>18</sup> This connection with the London money market would later become an indispensable source of money especially in a rapidly growing phase of the Specie Bank.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正是在这一时期，一些名称奇特的账户开始定期出现在正金银行的资产负债表中。最引人注目的是负债方的"卖出汇兑"和资产方的"买入汇兑"。"卖出汇兑"指出售外国汇票，意味着银行向进口商提供支付工具；其收入与存款及其他负债项目合并，增强了银行的放贷能力。"买入汇兑"指购买外国汇票，意味着银行向出口商提供垫款。这些操作是对"御用外国为替"的进一步拓展。"买入汇兑"收取的利率或贴现率高于国内汇票，因其涉及外汇风险。资产方另一个名称古怪的账户是"利息汇票"，这些是以日元计价的汇票，主要用于对华贸易。 该票据之所以得名，是因为其面额上印有利率。由于以日元结算，它们不涉及外汇风险。最后，虽非特别之处，但"再贴现"仍需一提。根据 1889 年与日本银行的协议，正金银行有权享受高达 1000 万日元、利率 2%的外汇票据再贴现。自 1896 年起，该账户的实际数字已超过与日本银行商定的额度。事实上，正金银行又找到了一项重要的银行资金来源。1897 年，帕尔银行、伦敦联合银行和伦敦合资银行同意为正金银行承兑外汇票据，随后这些票据在伦敦货币市场上进行再贴现。其承兑限额据说在 10 万至 20 万英镑之间，但具体金额不详。 <sup>18</sup> 与伦敦货币市场的这层联系，后来成为正金银行（Specie Bank）不可或缺的资金来源，尤其是在其高速发展阶段。</span></span></span>

The innovation of foreign trade financing technique certainly encouraged the Specie Bank to compete with foreign, chiefly British, overseas banks. At the beginning of 1896, the Specie Bank decided to offer advances at one sixteenth lower rate than those imposed by several British overseas banks such as the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation and the Mercantile Bank of India. The Specie Bank also promised to allow would-be customers to overdraw their accounts up to ¥200,000, which the British banks were doing too. In this way the Specie Bank deprived its British rivals of their important customers, such as Nippon Yusen, the biggest shipping company in Japan, Mitsubishi Corporation, one of the biggest trading companies, and even the Standard Oil Co.<sup>19</sup> However, it was also true that foreign, particularly British, overseas banks continued to be most formidable competitors<sup>20</sup> for the Specie Bank till 1916 when several big Japanese banks embarked upon foreign business.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">外贸融资技术的革新，无疑助长了正金银行与外国银行——尤其是英国海外银行的竞争。1896 年初，正金银行决定将放款利率较汇丰银行、印度有利银行等多家英国海外银行降低十六分之一，同时还承诺允许潜在客户透支账户高达 20 万日元，而英国银行此前也早已有此做法。凭借这一策略，正金银行成功从英国对手手中抢走了重要客户，例如日本最大的航运公司日本邮船、最大的贸易公司之一三菱商事，甚至连标准石油公司也未能幸免。 <sup>19</sup> 然而，直到 1916 年多家日本大型银行开始涉足海外业务之前，外国银行——特别是英国海外银行——始终是正金银行最强劲的竞争对手。 <sup>20</sup> </span></span></span>

Thanks to the half-yearly business reports between 1887 and 1897, we are provided with a rough picture of the Bank's customers. During this period, more than 40 per cent of the Bank's money was given at the head office and Kobe branch, both based upon the busiest ports of Japan. Although merchants were favoured in terms of the number of advances banks and foreigners were given much larger sums than the merchants. It is, therefore, not so dangerous to say that the Specie Bank was the bank of banks in the sphere of foreign trade financing and was doing business with first-rate foreign companies such as the Standard Oil Co.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">得益于 1887 年至 1897 年间每半年发布的业务报告，我们得以大致了解该银行的客户构成。在此期间，银行超过 40%的资金投放于总行和神户分行，这两处均位于日本最繁忙的港口。尽管从放贷笔数上看，商人们颇受青睐，但银行和外资机构获得的贷款总额远高于商人。因此，可以较为稳妥地说，这家正金银行在外贸融资领域堪称银行的银行，并与标准石油公司等一流外资企业保持着业务往来。</span></span></span>

Surviving the crises, conducting the specie transfer operations and innovating methods of financing foreign trade, the Specie Bank grew steadily between 1889 and 1898. Its total liabilities/assets increased from ¥32 million to ¥129 million. On the liabilities side, deposits undoubtedly led the jump in growth from ¥7.1 to ¥40.6 million. Selling exchange and rediscounting made substantial progress, growing from ¥3.9 and ¥7.8 million to ¥20.1 and ¥35 million respectively. This type of growth was an important factor in strengthening the lending resources of the Bank. On the assets side, the growth was stimulated by buying <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_200"></span>exchange. The sum increased from ¥14 million to ¥63 million reaching nearly half of the total assets, though advances and discounting also increased considerably from ¥5.3 million to ¥33.4 million. The main feature of the period was the operation to attract specie but the innovation of financial instruments to encourage imports and exports prepared the Specie Bank for an expansion in the new century.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在历经危机、开展贵金属转运业务并创新外贸融资方式的过程中，这家贵金属银行在 1889 年至 1898 年间稳步发展。其总负债/资产从 3200 万日元增至 1.29 亿日元。从负债端看，存款无疑是增长的主要驱动力，从 710 万日元跃升至 4060 万日元。外汇卖出及再贴现业务也取得显著进展，分别从 390 万日元和 780 万日元增至 2010 万日元和 3500 万日元。此类增长是增强银行贷款资金实力的重要因素。在资产端，增长主要受外汇买入业务推动，该项金额从 1400 万日元增至 6300 万日元，接近总资产的一半，尽管贷款及贴现也从 530 万日元大幅增至 3340 万日元。 这一时期的主要特征是开展吸引硬通货的操作，但为促进进出口而进行的金融工具创新，为正金银行在新世纪的扩张铺平了道路。</span></span></span>

Expansion, 1898—1907<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">扩张，1898—1907</span></span></span>
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The period from 1898 to 1908 was again highlighted by national engagements. In spite of the large amount of specie received from China, there were continuous deficits in the balances of current accounts and decreased specie reserves, which threatened the maintenance of the gold standard. Nor could the government contract its own money requirements, for Japan had itself embarked on an expansionist programme. Colonial commitments in Korea and Taiwan required further military expenditure as well as capital-intensive railway building. There was no Choice for the government but to have recourse to foreign loans, which Japan had not considered since 1873 but which now became a hot political issue.<sup>21</sup> The government, therefore, cautiously started to consider how to proceed to obtain Japanese funds from abroad.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1898 年至 1908 年间，国家大事再次成为焦点。尽管从中国获得了大量贵金属，但经常账户持续赤字，贵金属储备不断减少，金本位的维持岌岌可危。而政府也无法缩减自身的货币需求，因为日本自身也已踏上扩张主义之路——在朝鲜和台湾的殖民承诺，不仅需要更多的军事开支，还需投入资本密集型的铁路建设。政府别无选择，只能重拾外债，而自 1873 年以来，日本从未考虑过举借外债，如今却成了炙手可热的政治议题。 <sup>21</sup> 于是，政府开始小心翼翼地盘算，如何从海外筹措日本所需的资金。</span></span></span>

In February 1898, Korekiyo Takahashi, deputy governor of the Specie Bank, was commissioned by the minister of finance to investigate western money markets and enquire into the possibilities of raising funds there. Takahashi, who was later twice in office as prime minister and eight times as financial minister, had had a checkered career. At the age of 12 Takahashi had been employed as a messenger boy at the Yokohama branch of Chartered Mercantile Bank, where Alexander Allan Shand was the acting manager.<sup>22</sup> Then he worked for some years in America, returning to Japan in 1868. Thereafter he changed jobs every three or four years until 1892 when he entered a construction office affiliated to the Bank of Japan. He was quickly promoted. This was the man who was sent to the Specie Bank to be the head office manager in 1895.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1898 年 2 月，横滨正金银行副总裁高桥是清受大藏大臣委托，赴西方考察金融市场并探寻融资可能性。这位后来两度出任首相、八度执掌财政大权的政治家，早年经历颇为跌宕。12 岁时，他在横滨特许银行分行担任信差，当时该行代理经理为亚历山大·艾伦·尚德。 <sup>22</sup> 随后他赴美工作多年，于 1868 年返回日本。此后他每隔三四年便更换一次工作，直至 1892 年进入日本银行下属的建设局。他晋升迅速，1895 年被派往横滨正金银行担任总行经理。</span></span></span>

Takahashi travelled in Britain, Europe ana the United States between April and September 1898. He found his most useful contact in London in Alexander Shand, the acting manager of Parr's Bank London office who had in the 1870s had a decisive influence in Japan, in helping to train the first generation of young Japanese in western banking methods. He was extremely helpful to Takahashi, clarifying the problem of how to obtain a Japanese loan <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_201"></span>in London and of how to form a syndicate to do so. Perhaps Shand also suggested that the liquidity position of the Specie Bank in London should be made stronger in order to enhance its reputation and status. Immediately on returning to Japan Takahashi made his recommendation to strengthen the Specie Bank's position, and approximately £2 million was added to the London account. This money was supplied by the Bank of Japan.<sup>23</sup> In the meantime, there were important transfers of personnel from the Specie Bank to the Bank of Japan. Tatsuo Yamamoto, who had earlier been moved with Takahashi to the directorate of the Specie Bank, was in October 1898 appointed as governor of the Bank of Japan. Takahashi became the deputy governor. Was Takahashi himself the instrument by which the Bank of Japan kept a firm grip on the Specie Bank?<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">Takahashi 在 1898 年 4 月至 9 月间游历了 Britain、Europe 和 the United States。在 London，他发现最有用的联系人是 Parr's Bank London 办公室的代理经理 Alexander Shand，此人在 1870 年代对日本产生了决定性影响，帮助培训了第一代日本年轻人掌握西方银行方法。他对 Takahashi 极为 helpful，厘清了如何在 London 获得日本贷款以及如何组建辛迪加来达成此目的。或许 Shand 还建议加强 Specie Bank 在 London 的流动性状况，以提升其声誉和地位。一回到 Japan，Takahashi 立即提议增强 Specie Bank 的实力，London 账户中增加了约£2 million。这笔资金由 Bank of Japan 提供。 <sup>23</sup> 与此同时，Specie Bank 与 Bank of Japan 之间进行了重要的人员调动。 早先与高桥一起被调往正金银行（Specie Bank）董事会的山本达雄，于 1898 年 10 月被任命为日本银行（Bank of Japan）总裁。高桥则出任副总裁。高桥本人是否就是日本银行牢牢掌控正金银行的工具？</span></span></span>

The 'Prospectus of Imperial Japanese Government 4% Loan' for £10 million, secured by the customs' duties and redeemable in fifty-five years, was issued in June 1899. The syndicate included the Specie Bank, Parr's Bank, the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation and the Chartered Bank of India, Australia and China. Despite the first-rate titles of banks like Parr's Bank in London, Japan herself and the Yokohama Specie Bank were obviously of little consequence in the London money market. Indeed, the market price of the loan soon fell below the issuing price. The Japanese government feared the worst. Eventually the government itself bought through the Specie Bank the loan for £4.5 million, of which £2 million was the sum earlier transferred to London at the recommendation of Takahashi. Only 10 per cent of the total amount of the loan was subscribed in the open market. Of the rest, 45 per cent was placed privately by the syndicate. The 4 per cent loan was therefore not quite a success for the syndicate and Japanese government. Those involved, however, had gained experience, although at a high price, of what was, and was not, possible on the London money market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1899 年 6 月，日本政府发行了 1000 万英镑的"明治政府 4 厘外债债券"，以海关关税作为担保，分 55 年偿还。承销团包括横滨正金银行、帕尔银行、汇丰银行和印度澳中国 Chartered 银行。尽管有伦敦帕尔银行这样的顶级银行参与，但日本本身和横滨正金银行在伦敦金融市场显然无足轻重。果然，这批债券的市场价格很快跌破发行价。日本政府做了最坏的打算，最终通过正金银行自行购回 450 万英镑债券，其中 200 万英镑正是之前在高桥建议下提前转至伦敦的资金。这批债券在公开市场仅售出总额的 10%，其余 45%由承销团私下配售。 因此，对于银团和日本政府来说，4%的贷款并不算成功。不过，尽管代价高昂，参与方还是从中获得了经验，了解了在伦敦货币市场上什么可行、什么不可行。</span></span></span>

Tensions over the Korean peninsula between Russia and Japan grew towards the end of 1903, and the Russo-Japanese War broke out in February 1904. Takahashi was dispatched to London to negotiate a loan for £10 million. Again, Shand of Parr's Bank was the important Japanese contact. The Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation offered to join the syndicate, though they could only afford to find half the sum. Certainly Shand proved invaluable. He found useful names among Parr's Bank correspondents and discovered that Kuhn, Loeb & Co. in New York were interested. Kuhn, Loeb were soon joined by the National City Bank and the National Bank of Commerce also of New York. In <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_202"></span>May 1904, the 6 per cent loan for £10 million, secured by the customs' duties and redeemable in seven years, was successfully floated.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1903 年底，俄罗斯与日本围绕朝鲜半岛的紧张局势不断升级，1904 年 2 月日俄战争爆发。Takahashi 被派往伦敦谈判一笔 1000 万英镑的贷款。Parr's Bank 的 Shand 再次成为日本方面的重要联系人。香港上海汇丰银行提出加入银团，尽管他们只能承担一半的金额。Shand 无疑发挥了关键作用，他找到了 Parr's Bank 往来客户中的一些有用关系，并发现纽约的 Kuhn, Loeb & Co.对此感兴趣。很快，纽约国民城市银行和纽约国民商业银行也加入了 Kuhn, Loeb。1904 年 5 月，这笔利率 6%、以关税为担保、七年期可赎的 1000 万英镑贷款成功发行。</span></span></span>

Takahashi continued in London searching for further chances of raising larger sums. An opportunity arose in the autumn of 1904 when market conditions in London were easier. Another 6 per cent loan for £12 million was floated by the same syndicate based in London and New York. The Russian surrender in Lushun in January 1905 strengthened Japan's position and four more loans followed at lowered rates of interest. In the last two loans, the syndicate was joined by the Rothschilds, and the Americans were not involved in the last loan of 1907. All in all, between 1899 and 1907, Japanese loans totalled £140 million. The actual sum the Japanese government obtained was ¥1,211,549,929. This amount was more than 70 per cent of the 'Extraordinary War Expenditure Special Account' which the government had set up for the Russo-Japanese War. The Specie Bank London office had become therefore the indispensable agent through which foreign resources could be tapped.<sup>24</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">高桥在伦敦继续寻找筹集更大资金的机会。1904 年秋，伦敦市场环境趋于宽松，机会出现。同一财团（以伦敦和纽约为基地）再次发行了 1200 万英镑、利率 6%的贷款。1905 年 1 月俄军在旅顺投降，增强了日本的地位，随后又发行了四笔利率更低的贷款。最后两笔贷款中，罗斯柴尔德家族也加入了财团，而 1907 年的最后一笔贷款则没有美国参与。总计，1899 年至 1907 年间，日本贷款总额达到 1.4 亿英镑。日本政府实际获得金额为 12,1154,9929 日元，这一数额占政府为日俄战争设立的“临时军费特别账户”的 70%以上。因此，横滨正金银行伦敦办事处成为获取外国资金的不可或缺的代理机构。 <sup>24</sup> </span></span></span>

Back in Asia, the Specie Bank was expanding rapidly. From 1898 to 1907, the number of branches<sup>25</sup> doubled to twenty. The ten new branches were ail in eastern Asia. Inside Japan, they were in Tokyo (1899), Nagasaki (1899) and Osaka (1905). On the Chinese mainland, they were in Tianjin (1899), Niuzhuang (1900) and Beijin (1902). The location and timing of the setting up of these branches reflected the uprisings of Yihetuan, nationalistic reactions to the invasion of the foreign powers. The Manchurian bank branches in Dalian (1904), Fengtian (1905) and Changchun (1907) followed the Japanese army north as they chased the Russians. 'Following the army' was something very different from 'serving the nation'; such behaviour went far beyond the boundary of ordinary banking.<sup>26</sup> Indeed, this was an early example of what happened a quarter of a century later. The Manchurian branches became the agents of the Bank of Japan, accepting the war-notes issued by the Japanese army. The rest of the branches on the Chinese continent also issued yen-notes. After 1917 part of this business was handed over to the Bank of Korea, another Japanese creation on the Korean peninsula.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

The extraordinarily favourable situation resulted in a tremendous increase of specie reserve from 1915 on. This change of fortune temporarily liberated the Specie Bank for the first time in its history from the national specie problem. However big they were, deposits could not be elastic enough to respond instantly to rapidly expanding demands. Therefore the Specie Bank had to rely on other sources, i.e., interbank borrowing and rediscounting both inside and outside Japan. Inside Japan, the Specie Bank was provided with every sort of privilege by the Bank of Japan. Outside Japan, the most important market existed of course in London. Indeed, a report prepared by the Bank of Japan in 1914 reveals that the Specie Bank raised through rediscounting and interbank borrowing more than ¥80 million at one time, of which nearly ¥70 million was London money. The figures in the Bank balance sheets, as illustrated in [Table 11.2](#Ch011.xhtml_fig0018), were no more than those of the balances at the end of each year. The London money market, which was first exploited earlier in 1897, was now in full use by the Specie Bank. According to the report, there were three routes accessible to the London money market. First, the Specie Bank could rediscount at London banks foreign bills of exchange which it was holding mainly through buying exchange. The Bank could secure money by this method to the extent of ¥50 million. Second, the Bank could borrow in London on the security of national bonds and foreign bills of exchange, the latter of which were documents against payment and as such were not available for rediscounting. In this way the Bank could obtain the sum of up to ¥10 million. Third, the Bank could draw two types of finance bills on London. One type was the bills drawn on London banks. Usually the bills were £2,500 or £5,000 payable 30, 60 or 90 days after sight. The Bank pledged equities and debentures as fixed collateral securities with London banks, which were drawees of the bills. Another type was the bills drawn by the Specie Bank head office on its London branch. These were accepted by London banks and then were sold, or discounted. The bills were usually £5,000 or £10,000 payable 3 or 4 months after sight. In fact, all procedure for this last sort of finance bill was executed by the Specie Bank London branch. It prepared the printed forms of bills, which even had printed on their faces the sums of £5,000 or £10,000. Only date spaces were left blank to be filled up at any time. The London branch was thus able to draw the bills for any <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_210"></span>date to suit. This type of bill was termed inside the Specie Bank 'special bill'. Finance bills gave the Bank ¥10.5 million, of which more than 80 per cent was procured through this latter type.<sup>33</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">不论规模多大，存款都难以具备足够的弹性来即时满足迅速增长的需求。因此，正金银行不得不依赖其他资金来源，即日本内外的银行间拆借与再贴现。在日本国内，正金银行得到了日本银行赋予的种种特权。而在海外，最重要的市场自然在伦敦。事实上，日本银行在 1914 年编制的一份报告显示，正金银行曾一度通过再贴现和银行间拆借筹集了超过 8000 万日元，其中近 7000 万日元来自伦敦市场。如表 11.2 所示，银行资产负债表上的数字不过是一年年底的余额。早在 1897 年便已涉足的伦敦货币市场，如今已被正金银行充分利用。据该报告所述，进入伦敦货币市场有三条途径。 首先，正金银行可通过伦敦的银行对其持有的外汇汇票进行再贴现——这些汇票主要是通过购买外汇获得的。通过此方式，银行最多可筹措 5000 万日元。其次，银行可在伦敦以国债和外汇汇票为抵押借款，其中外汇汇票属于付款交单凭证，因此不能用于再贴现。通过这种方式，银行可获得高达 1000 万日元的资金。第三，银行可在伦敦开立两种金融汇票。第一种是针对伦敦银行开出的汇票，面额通常为 2500 或 5000 英镑，见票后 30、60 或 90 天付款。银行以股票和债券作为固定抵押品，交给作为汇票付款人的伦敦银行。第二种是由正金银行总行向其伦敦分行开出的汇票，这些汇票由伦敦银行承兑后，再行出售或贴现。 这些汇票的面额通常为 5000 或 10000 英镑，见票后三四个月付款。事实上，这类最后一种金融汇票的所有手续均由正金银行伦敦分行办理。该分行事先印制好汇票格式，甚至已将 5000 或 10000 英镑的金额印在票面上，仅留出日期栏空白，随时填写。伦敦分行因此能够根据需要签发任何日期的汇票。这类汇票在正金银行内部被称为"特别汇票"。金融汇票为该行带来了 1050 万日元的资金，其中超过 80%是通过后一种方式获得的。 <sup>33</sup> </span></span></span>

In conducting the London business, the Specie Bank negotiated with Parr's Bank, Lloyds' Bank, London Joint Stock Bank and Union of London & Smith Bank. Later the business was joined by Heine & Co. in Paris. The Bank of France, too, allowed the Lyons branch of the Specie Bank to overdraw its account at the former to the extent of fr. 10,000,000 (c. ¥4 million). New York also became available by this time, though unfortunately the names of the banks involved are not known. The Specie Bank could thus raise resources in Paris and New York up to ¥14 million.<sup>34</sup> Money from London, Paris and New York counted for nearly a quarter of the average sum of deposits during the Great War. It was an essential role for the Specie Bank to supply funds to the rapidly expanding economy and so strengthen itself. These years before 1920 were the 'Golden Years' not only for the Specie Bank, but also for the Japanese economy.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在伦敦业务中，Specie Bank 与 Parr's Bank、Lloyds' Bank、London Joint Stock Bank 以及 Union of London & Smith Bank 进行了接洽。后来，巴黎的 Heine & Co. 也加入了这项业务。法兰西银行还允许 Specie Bank 的里昂分行在其账户透支高达 1000 万法郎（约合 400 万日元）。此时纽约市场也已开通，可惜的是，涉及的相关银行名称不详。这样一来，Specie Bank 便能在巴黎和纽约筹集最多 1400 万日元的资金。 <sup>34</sup> 来自伦敦、巴黎和纽约的资金，几乎占到了一战期间平均存款总额的四分之一。对于 Specie Bank 而言，为快速扩张的经济提供资金、进而壮大自身，至关重要。1920 年之前的这些年，不仅是 Specie Bank 的“黄金岁月”，也是日本经济的黄金时代。</span></span></span>

It is almost impossible for the bank historians to know exactly to which sectors the bank was supplying money. Thanks again to surviving printed records of the Specie Bank,<sup>35</sup> we can rebuild its sectoral lending during this important period of rapid growth. The Specie Bank increased its share of export financing in the Japanese total from 34 per cent in 1910 to 44 per cent in 1919. Both before and after the war boom, Japan's most staple exports, silk and manufactured cotton, were generously financed by the Specie Bank. The Bank's share of their financing fell slightly from 1910 to 1919, but still in the latter year a tremendous sum was given by the Bank to finance the export of two goods. On the import side, metal, machineries and raw cotton were most essential, though in 1919 rice and soya beans emerged as other important factors. The total share of the Bank in import financing rose to more than 40 per cent in 1919. Although the share of the Bank in both export and import financing was very large, it was also true that more than half of resources for international trades came from other than the Bank. The rivals of the Bank were probably those in the list of correspondents we discussed above.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">银行历史学家几乎不可能确切知道银行资金流向了哪些行业。幸而留存下来的正金银行印刷记录，让我们得以重建其在快速增长的这段关键时期内的行业贷款情况。正金银行在日本出口融资中所占份额从 1910 年的 34%增至 1919 年的 44%。战前与战后经济繁荣期，日本最主要的出口商品——生丝和棉织品——都得到了正金银行的大力融资支持。该行对这两类商品的融资份额从 1910 年到 1919 年略有下降，但在 1919 年，银行仍为这两个商品的出口提供了巨额资金。在进口方面，金属、机械和原棉最为关键，不过到 1919 年，大米和大豆也成为重要的进口商品。正金银行在进口融资中的总份额在 1919 年攀升至 40%以上。 虽然该银行在出口和进口融资中所占的份额非常庞大，但事实上，国际贸易中超过一半的资金来源并非来自该行。该行的竞争对手很可能就是我们上文讨论的往来行名单中的那些机构。</span></span></span>

During this period of sustained growth, Junnosuke Inoue emerged as another Takahashi-type personality. As a Specie Bank man, he was trained at Parr's Bank in London. He became the governor of the Specie Bank in 1913 and led it in the remarkable development until 1919, when he moved over to become governor of the Bank of Japan. The interlinks between the Specie Bank and the Bank of Japan — not least the exchange of senior personnel —<span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_211"></span>had made Japanese banking progress possible, although the two men, Takahashi and Inoue, were very different in their policies from each other.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在这个持续增长的时期，井上准之助成为了另一个高桥式的人物。作为一位 Specie Bank 人，他曾在伦敦的 Parr's Bank 接受培训。他于 1913 年出任 Specie Bank 总裁，并领导该行取得了显著发展，直至 1919 年调任日本银行总裁。Specie Bank 与 Bank of Japan 之间的紧密联系——尤其是高层人员的互换——使日本银行业的发展成为可能，尽管高桥和井上这两个人在政策上截然不同。</span></span></span>

Stagnation, 1920—1931<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">停滞时期，1920—1931</span></span></span>
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The end of the War in 1918 marked the end of a period of progress and profit for the Japanese economy. For four years from 1919 imports exceeded exports. This resulted in heavy deficits in the balance of payment. In 1922, one year before the great earthquake of 1923, Japan experienced a further deficit of ¥237 million. The necessary reconstruction of Tokyo and its satellite cities required large amounts of building materials which naturally increased imports greatly. Nevertheless the coincidence of the post-war depression and the great earthquake was only an omen of the coming crisis. The accumulation of 'earthquake bills and drafts in moratorium' eventually, in March 1927, burst into a banking crisis of unprecedented scale, in which 44 banks with 10 per cent of total banking deposits were swept away. With Takahashi as minister of finance and Junnosuke Inoue as the Bank's governor, the Bank of Japan had to support the banking system by supplying it a relief money of ¥687 million, equivalent to 14 per cent of total banking deposits.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1918 年战争结束标志着日本经济繁荣与盈利时期的终结。自 1919 年起连续四年出现贸易逆差，导致国际收支严重失衡。1922 年（即 1923 年关东大地震前一年），日本财政赤字进一步扩大至 2.37 亿日元。东京及其卫星城的灾后重建需要大量建材，自然促使进口额急剧攀升。然而，战后经济萧条与大地震的叠加影响仅是危机来临的前兆。大量"延期支付的震灾票据"不断累积，最终在 1927 年 3 月引爆了一场史无前例的银行危机，占全国存款总额 10%的 44 家银行在这场危机中覆灭。 在高桥担任财政大臣、井上准之助担任日本银行总裁期间，日本银行不得不向银行体系提供 6.87 亿日元的纾困资金，相当于银行存款总额的 14%，以支持其运转。</span></span></span>

Meanwhile, there were heated arguments on the need, or not, to return to the gold standard, which Japan had left in 1917. Concurrently specie was slowly but inexorably draining away. Specie reserves fell from ¥2,080 million in 1921 to ¥1,199 million in 1928. Regardless of these warning signs, the government was intent on returning to the gold standard, following which it would try to promote the economy. Finance minister, Inoue, who had left the Bank of Japan in 1929, argued strongly for the return to gold. The Specie Bank was instructed by the government to borrow \$500 million and £1 million from American and British banks respectively. These sums were to be available to deal with anticipated increases of demand for dollar and pound sterling currencies. This was effectively the last business of substantial scale done by the Specie Bank on western money markets before the Pacific War.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">同时，关于是否要恢复金本位制（日本于 1917 年脱离该体系）的争论异常激烈。与此同时，硬通货正在缓慢但不可逆转地流失。硬通货储备从 1921 年的 20.8 亿日元下降至 1928 年的 11.99 亿日元。尽管有这些警示信号，政府仍执意要恢复金本位制，并试图在此之后推动经济发展。1929 年离开日本银行的财政大臣井上，强烈主张恢复金本位。政府指示 Specie Bank 分别从美国和英国的银行借款 5 亿美元和 100 万英镑。这些资金将用于应对预期中美元和英镑需求的增长。这实际上是太平洋战争前，Specie Bank 在西方货币市场上进行的最后一笔大规模业务。</span></span></span>

Japan returned to the gold standard in January 1930 against the background of 'Black Thursday' of 24 October 1929 when the Wall Street money market collapsed. This ushered in the worst period of financial disaster and trade depression. In Japan, prices of staple goods fell sharply. The government remained faithful to the gold standard for nearly two years until December 1931 when <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_212"></span>it was finally abandoned. Takahashi, succeeding Inoue as minister of finance, considered all options in his search for an escape from the quagmire. Between January 1930 and December 1931, Japan lost ¥785 million specie, which was more than one third of the sum held in 1920 at its highest. The Specie Bank was in the doldrums.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1929 年 10 月 24 日"黑色星期四"华尔街货币市场崩盘后，日本于 1930 年 1 月在一片萧条中重启金本位制。此举开启了金融灾难与贸易萧条最严重的时期。日本主要商品价格暴跌，政府坚守金本位制近两年，直至 1931 年 12 月最终放弃。接替井上出任大藏大臣的高桥，绞尽脑汁寻求摆脱困境的出路。1930 年 1 月至 1931 年 12 月间，日本损失了 7.85 亿日元的硬通货，超过 1920 年巅峰时期储备的三分之一。正金银行也陷入萎靡不振的境地。</span></span></span>

In this period, the Specie Bank advanced and set up branches in Guangdon (1924), Alexandria (1926), Paris (1931) and Berlin (1931), but retreated from Vladivostok (1924),<sup>36</sup> Changchun (1931), Jinan (1931) and Lyons (1931). This ambivalence about branch opening reflected both shrinkage of trade worldwide and political difficulties, both domestic and international. For the Specie Bank matters were very serious. Apart from securities, every item in [Table 11.2](#Ch011.xhtml_fig0018) performed very poorly. The indicator of multinationalization declined considerably compared with the previous periods, standing at 52 per cent on average. The decline was certainly due to difficulties of buying exchange for the two years of 1930 and 1931. It was even overtaken by securities. This critical situation was seen in the Bank money flow, too, set out in [Table 11.4](#Ch011.xhtml_fig0021). Europe, mostly London, did continue to provide the Bank with money, but this was moved mainly to Manchuria and the countries of South-East Asia where the Japanese interests were in direct confrontation with western interests. The Specie Bank stood exposed and shivering as the cold winds of financial crisis whirled around.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这一时期，Specie Bank 积极扩张，先后在广东（1924 年）、亚历山大（1926 年）、巴黎（1931 年）和柏林（1931 年）设立分行，但撤出了符拉迪沃斯托克（1924 年）、 <sup>36</sup> 长春（1931 年）、济南（1931 年）和里昂（1931 年）。这种在开设分行上的矛盾态度，既反映了全球贸易的萎缩，也体现了国内外政治困境。对 Specie Bank 而言，情况十分严峻。除证券外，表 11.2 中的每一项指标都表现极差。跨国化指数较前一时期大幅下滑，平均仅为 52%。这一下降无疑源于 1930 和 1931 两年购买外汇的困难。甚至证券指标也超越了它。这一危急局面同样体现在银行资金流动中，详见表 11.4。 欧洲（主要是伦敦）确实继续向银行提供资金，但这些资金主要流向了满洲和东南亚国家——这些地区日本利益与西方利益直接冲突。正金银行在金融危机寒风的肆虐中暴露无遗，瑟瑟发抖。</span></span></span>

Epilogue: In the Japanese interest<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尾声：日本利益之需</span></span></span>
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

From 1932 on, the half-yearly business reports of the Yokohama Specie Bank suddenly became utterly silent on the regional distributions of its resources from which we have been tracing its money flow. In spite of this concealment, we can easily surmise where the Specie Bank was pouring its money resources as from 1932 to 1945 it set up 32 offices on the Chinese mainland and 92 in the Straits countries. The Specie Bank was again closely following the Japanese army and navy towards south-eastern seas.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">自 1932 年起，横滨正金银行的半年度业务报告突然对其资金来源的地区分布绝口不提——而我们此前正是通过这一分布追踪其资金流向。尽管遭到隐瞒，我们仍能轻易推测该银行将资金倾注何处：1932 年至 1945 年间，它在中国大陆设立了 32 家分行，在东南亚海峡地区设立了 92 家。正金银行又一次紧跟日本陆军和海军，朝着东南海域扩张。</span></span></span>

In Japan, the Manchurian Affair produced a terrible militaristic atmosphere. Takahashi desperately tried to overcome the difficulties and float the Japanese economy. In February 1932, a few weeks before the declaration of founding the Empire of Manchuria, Junnosuke Inoue was assassinated by a right-wing activist. Exactly four years later, Korekiyo Takahashi became a target of the abortive coup and was assassinated. The elimination of the two men, who had led and encouraged the Specie Bank to <span id="Ch011.xhtml_page_213"></span>great success, marked the end of an era. Hereafter, the Yokohama Specie Bank would be required to serve not the nation but militarism.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在日本，满洲事变（九一八事变）催生了浓厚的军国主义氛围。高桥是清竭力克服重重困难，试图振兴日本经济。1932 年 2 月，即满洲国成立宣言发表前几周，井上准之助被一名右翼激进分子刺杀。整整四年后，高桥是清在一场未遂政变中成为目标，惨遭杀害。这两位曾引领并推动横滨正金银行走向辉煌的人物相继陨落，标志着一个时代的终结。此后，横滨正金银行不再为国家服务，而是为军国主义服务。</span></span></span>

The Japanese surrendér of August 1945 naturally 'disarmed' the Yokohama Specie Bank too. The overseas assets were either frozen or confiscated. The Bank was instructed by the General Headquarters of the Occupational Army to transform itself to an ordinary bank. It was reorganized as the Bank of Tokyo in December 1946.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1945 年 8 月日本投降，自然也让横滨正金银行“解除了武装”。其海外资产或被冻结，或被没收。盟军总指挥部指示该行改组为普通银行。1946 年 12 月，横滨正金银行重组为东京银行。</span></span></span>

The balance sheet of the Yokohama Specie Bank must now be concluded. Born in the middle of the specie problem and developing remarkably responding to the national requests, the Bank served the nation as well as commercial interests. The floating of Japanese loans on western money markets was enthusiastically done by the Bank. The rapid economic growth during the war boom owed much to the resources and know-how of the Bank. The situation eventually changed to become hostile to the Bank when Japan departed from the gold standard in December 1931. The Bank lost the leadership and the way it should orientate itself, and, thus, ceased to be multinational in its genuine terms. The Japanese interests, which the Yokohama Specie Bank had been serving, were something essentially different from those it was forced to serve in the aftermath of the Manchurian Affair.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">横滨正金银行的资产负债表如今必须做出总结。这家银行诞生于金银本位问题的背景之中，响应国家需求而迅速发展，既服务于国家利益，也兼顾商业利益。该银行曾积极在西方金融市场上推动日本债券的发行。战时经济繁荣期间的快速增长，很大程度上得益于该银行的资源与专业知识。然而，当日本于 1931 年 12 月放弃金本位制时，局势最终变得对这家银行不利。它失去了领导地位以及自身应遵循的发展方向，因而在其真正意义上不再是一家跨国银行。横滨正金银行此前所服务的日本利益，与它在满洲事变后被迫服务的那种利益，本质上截然不同。</span></span></span>
