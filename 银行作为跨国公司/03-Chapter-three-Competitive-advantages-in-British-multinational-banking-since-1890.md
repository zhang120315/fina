Multinational banking on a large scale originated in nineteenth-century Britain. 'Overseas banks' began to be formed in the 1830s. The first of these banks established branches in British colonies in Australia, the West Indies and North America; by the 1850s and 1860s, banks were also being formed to operate in India, the East, South Africa and Latin America. The banks were usually the product of initiatives from business groups or merchants in the intended area of operation allied with banking or other interests in the City of London. Their distinctiveness lay in the combination of banking products they offered. Like British merchant banks, they provided international banking services, especially trade finance. However, unlike the merchant banks which conducted their business through networks of interlocking partnerships and correspondent relationships, the British overseas banks also operated branches in foreign countries and British colonies through which they undertook domestic banking<sup>2</sup>.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">大规模跨国银行业起源于 19 世纪的英国。"海外银行"最早出现于 19 世纪 30 年代。首批此类银行在澳大利亚、西印度群岛和北美等英国殖民地设立分行；到了 19 世纪 50 至 60 年代，专营印度、东方、南非和拉丁美洲业务的银行也相继成立。这些银行通常由目标经营区域的商业集团或商人与伦敦金融城的银行界及其他利益集团联手创办。其独特之处在于所提供的银行产品组合。与英国商人银行一样，它们也提供国际银行服务，尤其是贸易融资。 然而，与商人银行通过连锁合伙和代理关系网络开展业务不同，英国海外银行还在外国和英国殖民地设有分行，通过这些分行经营国内银行业务 <sup>2</sup> 。</span></span></span>

By 1890 the thirty-two British-registered overseas banks had established 710 foreign branches and wholly-owned agencies. [Table 3.1](#Ch03.xhtml_fig0003) lists, by geographical area, the branches operated in certain benchmark years by banks registered in the United Kingdom and owned largely by British citizens. It does not include independent agencies and representative offices. There was a fast growth in the number of branches before the First World War (their number grew up by around 4 per cent per annum between 1890 and 1913) and a slightly slower growth rate between 1913 and 1928. There was a tiny expansion in the number of new branches in the following ten years, but then another period of fast growth up to 1955, most of the new branches appearing (for obvious reasons) in the years between 1946 and 1955. Subsequently the growth in the number of new branches was tiny until 1970. The following fifteen years<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

In 1890 branches of British banks were spread all over the world, but with an overwhelming number in Australia and New Zealand, followed by southern Africa; central and southern America (including the West Indies); and south Asia. Apart from Latin America, all these regions were areas of British political control. This geographical pattern remained in place in the first half of the twentieth century, although southern Africa steadily increased its relative importance compared to Australasia. By 1955 Australasia had 41 per cent of British bank branches, and southern Africa 35.9 per cent. East, west and north Africa had become the third most important location. Thirty years later the geographical distribution of British bank branches was very different. Australasia (effectively New Zealand only by then), Latin America and Africa except South Africa held the equal largest shares. Southern Africa had almost disappeared as a location for British bank branches.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1890 年，英国银行的分支机构遍布全球，但在澳大利亚和新西兰数量最多，其次是南部非洲、中美洲和南美洲（包括西印度群岛）以及南亚。除拉丁美洲外，这些地区均处于英国的政治控制之下。这一地域格局在 20 世纪上半叶基本保持不变，不过相比于澳大利亚与新西兰，南部非洲的重要性稳步提升。到 1955 年，澳大利亚与新西兰拥有 41%的英国银行分支机构，南部非洲占 35.9%。东非、西非和北非则成为第三重要的地区。30 年后，英国银行分支机构的全球分布发生了巨大变化。澳大利亚与新西兰（实际上到那时只剩新西兰）、拉丁美洲以及除南非以外的非洲地区，份额并列最高。而南部非洲作为英国银行分支机构的所在地，几乎已经消失不见。</span></span></span>

Unfortunately, the extent of British multinational banking is only partially captured by [Table 3.1](#Ch03.xhtml_fig0003). On the one hand, the use of branches as a measure of the geographical distribution of the British banking activity is not entirely satisfactory, and a more extended analysis would need to show the distribution of assets as well. On the other hand, there were a number of banks which were registered outside the United Kingdom but have to be regarded as 'British'. The Hong Kong-registered Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation (Hongkong Bank) was the most important case before the Second World War. Established by the international community of merchants in the colony, the Bank had five German directors as late as 1913. The Bank had share registers in London, Shanghai and Hong Kong, with over half the shares on the London register in the late nineteenth century. The senior management of the Bank was (and remained in 1990) almost entirely British.<sup>3</sup> After the mid-1950s the formation of many locally-registered (but wholly or partly-owned by British parents) banks led to a huge growth in foreign-registered, but British-owned, banks. In 1970 there were at least twenty-four such banks with some 1,718 branches, and in 1985 fifty banks with some 3,974 branches. The dramatic fall in the number of branches in southern Africa between 1955 and 1985 was caused by the decisions of the two British banks in the region, Standard Bank of South Africa and Barclays, to form locally-registered companies, in which they retained controlling interests. By 1985 banking groups such as Barclays and Standard Chartered effectively consisted of a large <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_33"></span>number of locally-registered subsidiaries, some wholly-owned, some majority-owned, some minority-owned. If banks of this nature are included, the total number of 'British' bank branches in foreign countries rose to 5,428 in 1970 and was 4,990 in 1985 only a moderate fall.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">遗憾的是，表 3.1 仅能部分反映英国跨国银行业的规模。一方面，用分行数量来衡量英国银行业的地理分布并不完全令人满意，更深入的分析还应展示资产分布情况。另一方面，有一些在英国境外注册的银行也必须被视为"英国银行"。二战前，在香港注册的香港上海汇丰银行（汇丰银行）就是一个最重要的例子。该行由殖民地商界国际人士创立，直至 1913 年仍有五位德国董事。其股东名册分别设在伦敦、上海和香港，19 世纪末伦敦名册上的股份占比超过一半。该行高管层几乎全部由英国人组成（直至 1990 年仍是如此）。 1950 年代中期以后，许多在当地注册（但由英国母公司全资或部分持股）的银行相继成立，这导致外国注册但由英国人拥有的银行数量大幅增长。1970 年，此类银行至少有 24 家，拥有约 1,718 家分行；到了 1985 年，增至 50 家银行，分行总数约 3,974 家。1955 至 1985 年间，南部非洲分行数量急剧下降，原因是该地区的两家英国银行——南非标准银行和巴克莱银行——决定组建在当地注册的公司，并保留控股权。到 1985 年，巴克莱和渣打银行等银行集团实际上由大量当地注册的子公司组成，其中部分为全资持有，部分为多数股权持有，还有部分为少数股权持有。 如果将这类银行算在内，1970 年“英国”银行在国外的分支机构总数增至 5,428 家，而 4,1985 年的 990，降幅适中。</span></span></span>

It is unlikely that any other country matched the United Kingdom in the number of foreign branches or affiliates operated by its banks. As other chapters in this book demonstrate, before 1914 there were only a few American banks with a handful of foreign branches. Germany (as well as France and some other European countries) had banks which operated branch networks in their colonial territories plus Latin America, but these had nothing like the vast branch networks of the British banks in Australasia and southern Africa.<sup>4</sup> Even after the 'second wave' of multinational banking began in the 1960s, the branch networks of British banks remained the most extensive. At least until the 1980s, the number of American overseas branches remained lower than those of Britain.<sup>5</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">任何其他国家在银行海外分行或附属机构的数量上，都难以与英国匹敌。正如本书其他章节所示，1914 年之前，仅有少数美国银行拥有寥寥几家海外分行。德国（以及法国和其他一些欧洲国家）的银行在殖民地和拉丁美洲设有分行网络，但这些远不及英国银行在澳大拉西亚和南部非洲的庞大分行体系。 <sup>4</sup> 即便在 20 世纪 60 年代跨国银行“第二波”浪潮兴起后，英国银行的分行网络依然最为广泛。至少到 20 世纪 80 年代，美国海外分行的数量仍低于英国。 <sup>5</sup> </span></span></span>

Britain was unusual not only in the number of its foreign bank branches but also in the peculiar institutional form that British multinational banking took. In 1890 all of the 710 foreign branches were owned by independent overseas banks. Each bank was a specialist in a particular region or even country. The two largest banks by asset size, the Bank of Australasia and Union Bank of Australia, operated substantial branch networks (149 and 90 branches respectively) in Australia and New Zealand. The smallest bank, Bank of Egypt, had a mere two branches in Egypt. The most geographically-diverse operation was the New Oriental Banking Corporation (ranked eighth in asset size in 1890) which had twenty-one branches in Aden, India, Ceylon, Hong Kong, Shanghai, Madagascar, Seychelles, Mauritius, Australia, Singapore and Japan. Typically, each bank had a London-based board of directors and at least one UK office, usually in London. However, New Oriental (for example) had two offices in London and two in Scotland.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国的不同寻常之处不仅在于其外国银行分支机构的数量，还在于英国跨国银行所采用的独特制度形式。1890 年，所有 710 家海外分支机构均由独立的海外银行所有。每家银行都专注于特定地区甚至某个国家。资产规模最大的两家银行——澳大利亚银行和澳大利亚联合银行——在澳大利亚和新西兰运营着庞大的分支网络（分别拥有 149 家和 90 家分支机构）。规模最小的埃及银行在埃及仅有 2 家分支机构。地理分布最广的是新东方银行（1890 年资产规模排名第八），在亚丁、印度、锡兰、香港、上海、马达加斯加、塞舌尔、毛里求斯、澳大利亚、新加坡和日本设有 21 家分支机构。通常情况下，每家银行都有一个设在伦敦的董事会，并在英国至少设有一家办事处（通常位于伦敦）。 然而，New Oriental 在伦敦有两个办公室，在苏格兰也有两个。</span></span></span>

These institutions were the banking cousins of Mira Wilkins's 'free-standing' companies.<sup>6</sup> However, unlike the classic 'free-standing' company in agriculture, manufacturing and most services, where the 'head office did not amount to very much'<sup>7</sup>, the London offices of the overseas banks performed important functions, even though their staff numbers were small. In the late nineteenth century the boards of these banks typically exercised a close monitoring role over banking policies, especially lending, often sanctioning larger loans themselves. Boards recruited the <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_34"></span>British staff who occupied all managerial posts in foreign branches (except in the settler colonies) and closely supervised personnel matters. The London offices of the overseas banks very rarely engaged in domestic lending, but they did at least until the 1900s receive domestic deposits. They arranged the 'London end' of foreign trade finance between the UK and 'their' respective regions. They also managed the investment portfolios of the banks, which were overwhelmingly kept in London.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些机构正是米拉·威尔金斯笔下“自由站立”公司的银行业表亲。 <sup>6</sup> 但与农业、制造业及多数服务业中那些经典“自由站立”公司不同——其“总部往往无足轻重” <sup>7</sup> ——海外银行的伦敦办事处尽管人员精简，却承担着重要职能。19 世纪末，这些银行的董事会通常对银行政策（尤其是贷款业务）实施严密监控，常常自行审批大额贷款。董事会负责招募英国职员，这些职员占据海外分行（移民殖民地除外）的所有管理岗位，并对其人事事务进行严格监督。海外银行的伦敦办事处极少参与国内贷款业务，但至少在 20 世纪之前，它们确实吸收了国内存款。同时，这些办事处还负责安排英国与各自相关地区之间对外贸易融资的“伦敦端”业务。 他们还负责管理银行的投资组合，而这些投资绝大多数都集中在伦敦。</span></span></span>

There was only a partial correlation between the asset size of a bank and the number of branches operated, an indication that the volume of business done by a bank was far from dependent on branch size. Branch size depended on the region of operation and the kind of business available and undertaken in that region. At the end of the nineteenth century, the banks operating in Australasia and southern Africa had large branch networks, reflecting their performance of retail banking functions in these settler economies. At the other end of the spectrum came the Eastern Exchange banks, such as the Chartered Bank of India, Australia and China (ranked third in asset size in 1890, but with only seventeen branches), which collected deposits and provided exchange and trade finance facilities at a few trading entrepôts in Asia. The Latin American banks came somewhere between these two extremes.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">银行的资产规模与分支机构的数量之间只存在部分相关性，这表明银行的业务量远非取决于分支机构的规模。分支机构的规模取决于其运营的地区以及该地区可开展和实际从事的业务种类。19 世纪末，在澳大拉西亚和南部非洲运营的银行拥有庞大的分支网络，这反映了它们在这些定居者经济体中履行零售银行职能的情况。而与之形成鲜明对比的是东方汇兑银行，例如印度、澳大利亚和中国渣打银行（1890 年资产规模排名第三，但仅有 17 家分支机构），它们主要在亚洲的几个转口贸易港吸收存款，并提供外汇和贸易融资服务。拉丁美洲的银行则介于这两个极端之间。</span></span></span>

The striking absentees from the list of British banks with overseas branches in 1890 were the domestic or 'clearing' banks. Unlike their German equivalents, they held no shares in the overseas banks. Before the merger movement in English domestic banking got underway in the late nineteenth century, the large numbers of domestic banks were quite small and ill-equipped - or at least unwilling - to venture abroad. In addition, a conservative view developed as to what constituted 'orthodox' banking, which was reinforced by the Bank of England which - in the interests of financial stability - discouraged product and geographical diversification by the clearing banks. In the late nineteenth century some domestic banks, such as City Bank (which merged with Midland Bank in 1898), developed a large network of foreign correspondent agencies (essentially acting as London banker for foreign banks) through which they met the foreign trade finance requirements of their corporate clients, and performed similar roles in Britain for foreign banks, but other forms of international business (such as dealing in foreign exchange until the 1900s) and direct investment abroad were left to others.<sup>8</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1890 年英国设有海外分行的银行名单中，一个显著的缺席者便是国内银行（或称清算银行）。与德国同行不同，它们并未持有海外银行的股份。在 19 世纪末英国国内银行业并购浪潮兴起之前，为数众多的国内银行规模尚小且准备不足——至少是不愿——涉足海外业务。此外，对于何为"正统"银行业务形成了保守观点，而英格兰银行出于金融稳定考虑，进一步强化了这一观点，限制清算银行进行产品和地域多元化。 19 世纪末，一些国内银行如花旗银行（1898 年与米兰银行合并）建立了庞大的国外代理行网络（本质上作为外国银行在伦敦的银行），通过这一网络满足企业客户的对外贸易融资需求，并为外国银行在英国履行类似职能。但其他形式的国际业务（如 20 世纪以前的外汇交易）以及海外直接投资，则留给了其他机构。 <sup>8</sup> </span></span></span>

The institutional framework of British multinational banking changed markedly over time. There were two main shifts. The first <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_35"></span>was, as [Table 3.1](#Ch03.xhtml_fig0003) indicates, growing concentration among the British-registered overseas banks. In 1890 there were thirty-two independent banks. By the mid-1980s British overseas banking was firmly in the hands of five banks, four of whom were the UK clearing banks. This concentration was largely achieved through mergers and acquisitions between the banks, though there were occasional bankruptcies (such as the failure of the New Oriental Banking Corporation in 1893) and disposals to local interests (the fate by 1918 of the five British banks operating in Canada and California). The seven British banks operating in Latin America in 1890 had, by 1936, become concentrated by merger into the Bank of London and South America (Bolsa). In 1951 two of the three British banks in Australia merged to form the Australia and New Zealand Bank, which merged in 1970 with the remaining British bank to form the ANZ Group. In 1976 ANZ shifted domicile to Australia, and thus ceased to be a British bank. Another group of banks were engaged in a series of amalgamations which finally led in 1970 to the merger of the Chartered Bank of India, Australia and China and Standard Bank of South Africa to form Standard Chartered. By 1985 Standard Chartered was the sole survivor of the thirty-two British-registered overseas banks active in 1890.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国跨国银行业的机构框架随着时间的推移发生了显著变化。主要有两大转变。首先，如表 3.1 所示，在英国注册的海外银行中集中度不断提高。1890 年，共有 32 家独立银行。到 20 世纪 80 年代中期，英国的海外银行业务牢牢掌握在五家银行手中，其中四家是英国清算银行。这种集中主要通过银行间的兼并与收购实现，尽管偶尔也有破产（如 1893 年 New Oriental Banking Corporation 的倒闭）以及将业务出售给当地利益集团（到 1918 年，在加拿大和加利福尼亚经营的五家英国银行便遭遇了这种命运）。1890 年在拉丁美洲经营的七家英国银行，到 1936 年通过合并集中为 Bank of London and South America (Bolsa)。 1951 年，澳大利亚的三家英国银行中有两家合并，成立了澳大利亚和新西兰银行；该行于 1970 年与剩下的那家英国银行合并，组成了澳新集团（ANZ Group）。1976 年，ANZ 将注册地迁至澳大利亚，从此不再是一家英国银行。另一批银行进行了一系列合并，最终于 1970 年促成印度、澳大利亚与中国渣打银行和南非标准银行合并，成立了渣打银行（Standard Chartered）。到 1985 年，渣打银行已成为 1890 年活跃的 32 家英国注册海外银行中唯一的幸存者。</span></span></span>

The second change in the institutional structure of British overseas banking was the advent of the British clearing banks in the sector and the corresponding decline over time of the independent overseas banks. After the First World War two of the 'Big Five' English clearers, Lloyds and Barclays, expanded abroad, mainly through acquisitions. In 1918 Lloyds acquired a majority stake in the London and River Plate Bank, the oldest British bank in Latin America. Through this shareholding, Lloyds became the major shareholder in Bolsa. In addition, Lloyds acquired a 6 per cent shareholding in National Bank of New Zealand in 1919 (and eventually full control in 1965), and in 1923 it acquired Cox & Co., with a branch network in India and Egypt, The Indian branches were finally sold in 1960 to another British overseas bank, National and Grindlays, in which Lloyds acquired first a 25 per cent and then a 40 per cent shareholding, held until 1984 when the bank was sold to ANZ. Meanwhile Barclays acquired two British overseas banks after the First World War, the Colonial Bank (which operated in the West Indies) and the Anglo-Egyptian Bank, merging them with the South African-registered National Bank of South Africa in 1925 to form Barclays Bank (Dominion, Colonial and Overseas), which soon became the largest British overseas bank. Barclays and Lloyds remained the main <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_36"></span>participants in foreign branch banking among the British clearers until the 1970s.<sup>9</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国海外银行业机构结构的第二个变化是，英国清算银行开始涉足该领域，而独立海外银行则随时间的推移相应衰落。第一次世界大战后，英国"五大"清算银行中的两家——Lloyds 和 Barclays——主要通过收购向海外扩张。1918 年，Lloyds 收购了 London and River Plate Bank 的多数股权，该行是英国在拉丁美洲历史最悠久的银行。通过这笔持股，Lloyds 成为了 Bolsa 的主要股东。此外，Lloyds 于 1919 年收购了 National Bank of New Zealand 6%的股份（并于 1965 年最终取得完全控制权），1923 年又收购了在印度和埃及拥有分支网络的 Cox & Co.。其印度分支机构最终于 1960 年出售给另一家英国海外银行 National and Grindlays，Lloyds 最初持有该行 25%的股份，随后增至 40%，一直持有至 1984 年该银行被出售给 ANZ。 与此同时，巴克莱银行在一战后收购了两家英国海外银行——殖民银行（在西印度群岛运营）和英埃银行，并于 1925 年将其与在南非注册的南非国民银行合并，成立了巴克莱银行（自治领、殖民地和海外银行），该行很快成为英国最大的海外银行。直到 20 世纪 70 年代，巴克莱和劳埃德一直是英国清算银行中参与海外分行业务的主要机构。 <sup>9</sup> </span></span></span>

The British overseas banks established and retained substantial market shares. In the nineteenth century (and later) they financed much of Britain's foreign trade with Australia, Africa and Asia, although trade and capital flows between Britain and continental Europe and the United States remained largely in the hands of merchant banks and private bankers such as Barings, Rothschilds and the Morgans. In foreign domestic markets, if we take percentage of commercial bank deposits as a proxy for market share, it can be said with confidence that British-owned institutions never made more than a marginal impact in North America, continental Europe or Japan. The Bank of British North America in Canada, which was British-owned until its acquisition by the Bank of Montreal in 1918, provides an exception to this generalization.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国海外银行建立并保持了相当大的市场份额。在 19 世纪（及以后），它们为英国与澳大利亚、非洲和亚洲之间的大量对外贸易提供了融资，尽管英国与欧洲大陆及美国之间的贸易和资本流动仍主要掌握在巴林银行、罗斯柴尔德银行和摩根银行等商人银行和私人银行手中。在国外本土市场，若以商业银行存款占比作为市场份额的代理指标，可以确信地说，英资机构在北美、欧洲大陆或日本从未产生过显著影响。不过，设在加拿大的不列颠北美银行（1918 年被蒙特利尔银行收购前为英资所有）是这一普遍规律的一个例外。</span></span></span>

By contrast, in the Australian, New Zealand and South African banking markets, British banks played prominent roles until recent years. In late-nineteenth-century Australia, British banks controlled 40 per cent of deposits. By 1939 the 'British' share had fallen, but the two largest British banks still controlled 20 per cent of deposits between them. When the ANZ Group was formed by the merger of the two remaining British banks in 1970, it held 22 per cent of total deposits, the second largest share. ANZ's move of domicile to Australia in 1976, however, eliminated the British presence from the market. In New Zealand, the British-owned National Bank of New Zealand has been one of the largest banks throughout this century - controlling around 20 per cent of deposits in 1966 when it became wholly-owned by Lloyds Bank and until 1976 the operations of ANZ further increased the 'British' Importance. In South Africa, Barclays DCO and the Standard Bank of South Africa were the dominant banks for at least thirty years after 1926, and even in 1970 they controlled 73 per cent of total commercial bank deposits. The British role in South Africa only ended in 1986-7 with the disinvestment from that country of Barclays and Standard Chartered.<sup>10</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">相比之下，在澳大利亚、新西兰和南非的银行市场中，英国银行直至近年仍占据重要地位。19 世纪末，英国银行控制了澳大利亚 40%的存款。到 1939 年，英国银行的市场份额虽有所下降，但最大的两家英国银行仍共同掌握着 20%的存款。1970 年，最后两家英国银行合并组建澳新银行集团时，其存款总额占比达 22%，位列第二。然而，1976 年澳新银行将注册地迁至澳大利亚，英国银行自此退出该市场。在新西兰，英资的新西兰国民银行在整个 20 世纪都是最大的银行之一——1966 年当劳埃德银行全资收购该行时，其控制约 20%的存款份额，直至 1976 年，澳新银行的业务进一步提升了英国银行在该市场的重要性。 在南非，巴克莱银行 DCO 和南非标准银行在 1926 年后的至少三十年间一直占据主导地位，甚至在 1970 年，它们控制了商业银行存款总额的 73%。英国在南非的角色直到 1986-1987 年才告终结，那时巴克莱银行和渣打银行从该国撤资。 <sup>10</sup> </span></span></span>

In the developing world, British banks held considerable market shares both in the Empire and outside it before 1914; indeed in many of these countries, British institutions had founded the modern banking sectors. In British Africa and the British West Indies, British banks were dominant. The British-owned Imperial Bank of Persia held virtually all commercial deposits in Iran until 1928. British banks controlled one-third of all Brazilian deposits and one-quarter of those in Argentina and Chile prior to 1914. Over time, and at varying paces, nationalism, regulations against <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_37"></span>foreign banks and the advent of indigenous modern banks took their toll of British market dominance. British banking in Iran was decimated by such forces from the 1930s, and exchange controls and restrictions greatly reduced its significance all over Latin America from the interwar years. However, where British banks had been permitted to operate they often continued to play important roles through the 1950s and 1960s, and beyond. In Africa, although some countries (like Tanzania in 1967) nationalized their banking systems, Barclays and Standard Chartered often retained substantial shares of deposits where they could do business. In 1980 Standard Chartered alone held 42 per cent of commercial bank deposits in Zimbabwe, 32 per cent in Zambia and 49 per cent in Gambia. In Asia in the 1980s, the Hongkong Bank and Standard Chartered retained large market shares in countries such as Hong Kong, Singapore and Malaysia. In those Gulf States which permit foreign banks to operate, such as Bahrain, Oman and Qatar, British institutions are still ranked near the top in their deposit shares.<sup>11</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在 1914 年之前，英国银行在发展中国家（无论是帝国内部还是外部）都拥有相当大的市场份额；事实上，在许多这样的国家，现代银行业正是由英国机构创立的。在英属非洲和英属西印度群岛，英国银行占据主导地位。英资的波斯帝国银行在 1928 年之前几乎持有伊朗所有的商业存款。1914 年之前，英国银行控制了巴西全部存款的三分之一，以及阿根廷和智利存款的四分之一。随着时间的推移，且速度各异，民族主义、针对外国银行的法规以及本土现代银行的崛起，逐渐侵蚀了英国的市场主导地位。自 20 世纪 30 年代起，这些因素重创了英国在伊朗的银行业务；而从两次世界大战之间的时期开始，外汇管制和限制措施显著降低了英国在整个拉丁美洲的影响力。 然而，在英国银行获准经营的地区，它们往往在 20 世纪 50、60 年代乃至更晚的时期继续扮演重要角色。在非洲，尽管一些国家（如 1967 年的坦桑尼亚）将银行体系国有化，但巴克莱银行和渣打银行在能开展业务的地区通常仍持有可观的存款份额。1980 年，仅渣打银行一家就占据了津巴布韦商业银行存款的 42%、赞比亚的 32%以及冈比亚的 49%。在 20 世纪 80 年代的亚洲，汇丰银行和渣打银行在香港、新加坡和马来西亚等国家仍占据着较大的市场份额。而在那些允许外资银行运营的海湾国家，如巴林、阿曼和卡塔尔，英国金融机构的存款份额排名依然位居前列。 <sup>11</sup> </span></span></span>

A number of conclusions emerge from this survey of the dimensions and institutional structure of British multinational banking. British banks were prolific in their multinational branching. This activity initially took a distinctive form (compared to other countries) with a large number of independent specialist overseas banks. Over time British domestic banks replaced many of these 'free-standing' type institutions. British banks captured, and retained over long periods, large market shares in a variety of countries, Empire and non-Empire, developing and settler. The following section offers an explanation of these historical trends.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从本次对英国跨国银行业的维度与制度结构的调查中，可以得出若干结论。英国银行在跨国分行布局方面非常活跃。与其他国家相比，这种活动最初呈现出独特的形式，即大量独立专业海外银行并存。随着时间的推移，英国本土银行取代了许多这类“独立型”机构。英国银行在多个国家——无论是帝国范围内还是帝国之外，无论是发展中国家还是移民定居国——都占据并长期保持了可观的市场份额。以下部分将对上述历史趋势进行解释。</span></span></span>

Competitive advantages and business strategy<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

As discussed elsewhere in this book, a variety of theoretical approaches can be taken to explain the emergence and functioning of multinational banks. The focus of this chapter is only tangentially why they developed. Instead, attention is concentrated on the related questions of how they were able to build such important market positions and why they sustained these positions for so long. The analysis here draws loosely on the concepts developed by Michael E. Porter.<sup>12</sup> The approach is chronological, and the period since 1890 will be divided into three.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正如本书其他部分所讨论的，有多种理论方法可以用来解释跨国银行的兴起与运作机制。本章的关注点并非它们为何发展，而是聚焦于一个相关的问题：它们如何能够建立起如此重要的市场地位，又为何能长期维持这些地位。这里的分析大致借鉴了迈克尔·E·波特提出的概念。 <sup>12</sup> 研究方法按时间顺序展开，并将 1890 年以来的时期分为三个阶段。</span></span></span>

### 1890—1914<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1890 年至 1914 年</span></span></span>

In Porter's terms, multinational banking in the nineteenth century <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_38"></span>was an 'emerging industry'. The world consisted of a large number of national markets, with very different characteristics, but linked to a greater or lesser extent by flows of trade, capital and migrants. The industry was characterized almost everywhere by high receptivity as there were, outside the United States, few government regulations on banking activity, and few central banks. There were few substitute products for modern banking services. Capital markets and non-banking modern financial intermediaries were rare. In Asia large indigenous credit networks existed, but these institutions typically avoided the kind of business sought by modern banks. International banking had obvious growth opportunities given rapid expansion of world trade, although the merchant banks demonstrated that the finance of this trade did not require multinational branch networks. Britain played a focal role in the world political economy of the period. Britain was the world's largest exporter (accounting for 14 per cent of world trade in 1914) and the world's leading capital exporter. The City of London was the centre of the world financial and international payments system. Sterling provided a common currency or standard for the British Empire, and was widely used to finance the trade of non-Empire countries. Britain had political control over large areas of Asia, Africa and Australasia, and 'informal' influence in Latin America, the Middle East and the Mediterranean. The pre-eminence of Britain meant that in many countries barriers to entry into international banking were low for British institutions, and higher for those of other nationalities. This clearly did not apply to a number of important countries. In particular, in the United States and continental Europe restrictions on foreign banks, strong indigenous banks, and the position in trade finance occupied by established merchant banks, continued to provide very few incentives or opportunities for British overseas banks, which for the most part avoided these regions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">用波特的术语来说，19 世纪的跨国银行业是一个“新兴产业”。世界由大量各具特色的国内市场组成，这些市场通过贸易、资本和移民的流动，或多或少地相互联系。几乎在所有地方，该行业都表现出高度的接纳性，因为除美国外，很少有政府对银行业活动进行监管，中央银行也寥寥无几。现代银行服务几乎没有替代品。资本市场和非银行的现代金融中介也很罕见。在亚洲，存在着庞大的本土信贷网络，但这些机构通常回避现代银行所追求的那种业务。随着世界贸易的快速扩张，国际银行业显然拥有增长机遇，尽管商业银行表明，为贸易融资并不需要跨国分行网络。 英国在当时的世界政治经济中扮演着核心角色。它是全球最大的出口国（1914 年占世界贸易的 14%），也是首屈一指的资本输出国。伦敦金融城是国际金融和支付体系的中心。英镑不仅是大英帝国的通用货币与标准，还广泛用于为非帝国国家的贸易提供融资。英国在亚洲、非洲和澳大拉西亚大片地区拥有政治控制权，并在拉丁美洲、中东和地中海地区拥有“无形”的影响力。英国的霸主地位意味着，在许多国家，英国金融机构进入国际银行业的门槛较低，而其他国家的机构则面临更高的门槛。但这显然不适用于一些重要国家。 特别是在美国和欧洲大陆，针对外国银行的限制措施、实力雄厚的本土银行，以及传统商人银行在贸易融资领域占据的主导地位，使得英国海外银行几乎没有什么动力或机会进入这些市场，因此它们大多避开了这些地区。</span></span></span>

Given this situation British multinational banks had obvious competitive advantages over those from other countries. Such was the importance of Britain, that British banks possessed advantages in all three areas identified by Porter - cost, differentiation and focus - which are more typically seen as mutually exclusive. However, the British banks were not entirely 'free riders' on British pre-eminence, but pursued generic strategies designed to exploit the potential competitive advantages that their British nationality gave them. The mere fact of British ownership was not a guarantee of success or survival in the nineteenth century, as banking failures in the 1860s, 1890s and other times demonstrated.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在这种情况下，英国的跨国银行相比其他国家的银行拥有明显的竞争优势。英国的重要性如此之大，以至于英国银行在波特提出的三个领域——成本、差异化和聚焦——中都占据了优势，而这通常被认为是相互排斥的。然而，英国银行并非完全是在英国卓越地位上的“搭便车者”，它们追求的是旨在利用其英国国籍所带来的潜在竞争优势的通用战略。在 19 世纪，仅仅拥有英国所有权并不能保证成功或生存，正如 1860 年代、1890 年代以及其他时期银行业倒闭事件所证明的那样。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_39"></span>British banks were, to some extent, able to raise funds and to conduct business at a lower cost than competitors. The alleged bias in British capital markets towards overseas investments may have hindered domestic industry but it probably facilitated raising equity in London for the establishment of an overseas bank. The founders of overseas banks, however, also frequently used colonial links to tap savings in the colonies. When the Colonial Bank was launched in 1837, £1.5 million of the capital was raised in Britain, but £0.5 million was allocated to subscribers in the West Indian colonies.<sup>13</sup> Overseas banks also raised deposits in Britain (especially London and Scotland) at lower rates of interest than they employed abroad. Yet the use of such funds required skill: the Australian banking collapse of 1893 was blamed in part on excessive reliance on British deposits which had added to the vulnerability of certain banks.<sup>14</sup> Overseas banks were also able with ease to obtain overdraft facilities from the well-developed British domestic banking system in order to meet temporary requirements.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国银行在某种程度上能够以低于竞争对手的成本筹集资金并开展业务。英国资本市场所谓偏向海外投资的倾向可能阻碍了国内工业，但或许也便利了在伦敦为设立海外银行筹集股本。然而，海外银行的创始人还经常利用殖民地的纽带关系来汲取殖民地储蓄。1837 年殖民地银行成立时，150 万英镑的资本在英国筹集，但 50 万英镑分配给了西印度群岛殖民地的认购者。 <sup>13</sup> 海外银行还以低于海外使用的利率在英国（尤其是伦敦和苏格兰）吸收存款。然而，使用这些资金需要技巧：1893 年澳大利亚银行业崩溃的部分原因被归咎于过度依赖英国存款，这加剧了某些银行的脆弱性。 <sup>14</sup> 海外银行也能轻松地从发达的英国国内银行体系获得透支便利，以满足临时资金需求。</span></span></span>

The working of the gold standard and the position of sterling gave British bankers enormous freedom for rapid switching of funds between countries. Surplus deposits collected abroad could be transferred and invested with ease in London. However, the successful use of the system again required continual judgemental decisions. Equity was not simply raised in Britain and transferred once-and-for-all abroad. There was a constant flow of funds within banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">金本位制度的运作以及英镑的地位，使英国银行家能够极为灵活地在各国之间迅速调配资金。在境外收取的过剩存款可以轻松转入伦敦并用于投资。然而，要成功运用这一体系，仍需持续做出审慎的判断决策。资金并非仅仅在英国筹集后一次性转移到海外，而是银行内部始终存在流动的资金循环。</span></span></span>

Especially if a country was not on the gold standard and there was an exchange risk, British banks preferred not to transfer funds from Britain but to operate from local deposits - and a significant proportion of these were often current accounts on which no interest would be paid. British banks were able to attract deposits because of their reputation for security, which far surpassed Asian indigenous credit institutions and, initially at least, modern local banks established in Latin America and the settler economies. Nineteenth-century banking markets, especially in developing countries, were characterized by asymmetric information, and the reputation of British banks was attractive to depositors who could reduce search and other costs by doing business with them. The mere fact of being British suggested stability, but the reputation was enhanced by the successful application of proprietary technology consisting of the know-how to run a financial institution honestly and efficiently. In the American case, such banking and other skills were developed inside an institution, and transferred abroad when that institution established foreign branches. In the <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_40"></span>British case, England and Scotland had well-developed domestic banking structures in the nineteenth century, and the 'free-standing' overseas banks successfully recruited bankers with a few years' training at their domestic counterparts by offering higher salaries.<sup>15</sup> The ability to acquire skills through the market was at least a permissive factor in allowing 'free-standing' banks to grow and flourish in the nineteenth century.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">特别是在那些未采用金本位制且存在汇率风险的国家，英国银行更倾向于不直接从本土调拨资金，而是利用当地存款进行运作——其中相当大一部分往往是无需支付利息的活期账户。凭借远超亚洲本土信贷机构、以及至少在初期阶段也优于拉美和移民经济体本土新兴银行的安全信誉，英国银行得以成功吸纳存款。19 世纪的银行业市场（尤其是发展中国家）充斥着信息不对称现象，英国银行的声誉对存款者极具吸引力——他们通过与其开展业务能降低搜寻成本及其他开支。 仅仅身为英国人这一点就暗示了稳定性，但这一声誉因成功应用专有技术——即诚实高效运营金融机构的诀窍——而得到加强。在美国，此类银行及其他技能是在机构内部发展起来的，并在该机构设立海外分行时转移到国外。而在英国，英格兰和苏格兰在 19 世纪已拥有完善的国内银行体系，那些“独立式”海外银行通过提供更高薪资，成功招募到在国内银行受过数年培训的银行家。通过市场获取技能的能力，至少是 19 世纪“独立式”银行得以成长壮大的一个促成因素。</span></span></span>

The cost advantages of British banks were not confined to sources of funds. Given the role of London as the premier centre of international finance, and the prominent role of British trade and shipping, British institutions had enormous cost advantages in financing trade. International payments were frequently made by sterling bills. London was the leading market for the flotation of government loan or railway stock. In the second half of the nineteenth century many British overseas banks participated in such business, though they had to fight for it with merchant banks and other City institutions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国银行的成本优势并不仅限于资金来源。由于伦敦作为国际金融首要中心的地位，以及英国贸易和航运的主导作用，英国机构在贸易融资方面拥有巨大的成本优势。国际支付常以英镑汇票进行。伦敦是政府贷款或铁路股票发行的领先市场。19 世纪下半叶，许多英国海外银行参与了此类业务，尽管它们不得不与商人银行及其他伦敦金融城机构竞争。</span></span></span>

The British multinational banks also had competitive advantages in differentiation which rested on the prestige and security associated with being British. In the non-settler parts of the Empire, colonial governments would only use British banks as bankers. This was not the case, say, in Australia even before the colonies were granted self-government in the 1850s. In non-Empire but diplomatically sensitive areas such as Iran and China, British banks had some support at least from the British Foreign Office, which sometimes included financial subsidies for branches.<sup>16</sup> Being British meant that banks were frequently more trusted for their financial probity than local competitors. The London and River Plate Bank, for example, acted as the clearing house in Buenos Aires until 1912. In the first half of the nineteenth century the British government assisted the stable image of British banks by the process of granting Royal Charters to overseas banks. A Royal Charter gave a bank the privilege of limited liability (not available in England until legislation in 1858 and 1862) but in return the Treasury exercised supervision over aspects of a bank's affairs. A Royal Charter thus gave a bank prestige, and its shareholders and customers confidence. The granting of Royal Charters, however, ceased in 1863, although it was revived in 1890 in the special circumstances of the Imperial Bank of Persia.<sup>17</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国跨国银行在差异化方面也拥有竞争优势，这得益于“英国”身份所附带的声望与安全感。在帝国的非殖民定居地区，殖民地政府只选用英国银行作为其金融机构。然而在澳大利亚，情况并非如此，甚至在 19 世纪 50 年代殖民地获得自治权之前就已经不同。在伊朗和中国等非帝国领土但外交敏感的区域内，英国银行至少获得了英国外交部的一些支持，有时还包括对分支机构的财政补贴。 <sup>16</sup> “英国”身份意味着这些银行在财务诚信方面往往比当地竞争对手更受信任。例如，伦敦与拉普拉塔银行（London and River Plate Bank）直到 1912 年一直担任布宜诺斯艾利斯的票据交换所。19 世纪上半叶，英国政府通过向海外银行颁发皇家特许状的方式，进一步强化了英国银行的稳定形象。 皇家特许状赋予银行有限责任的特权（这一权利直到 1858 年和 1862 年的立法才在英格兰普及），但作为回报，财政部对银行的某些业务环节进行监督。因此，皇家特许状为银行增添了威望，也让股东和客户更加放心。不过，皇家特许状的颁发在 1863 年中断，直到 1890 年才因波斯帝国银行的特殊背景而恢复。 <sup>17</sup> </span></span></span>

Being British, therefore, gave banks a competitive advantage, but they also developed strategies to exploit this advantage to the full. General policy was that all branch and executive management posts were held by United Kingdom subjects. The exception was in <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_41"></span>Australasia and South Africa, where local settlers and their descendants were counted as acceptably British. The British banks repeatedly maintained in their public pronouncements that they followed the strictest rules of British-style orthodox banking, even when they did not.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正因如此，英国背景本身就给了银行一种竞争优势，而它们也制定了各种策略，将这一优势发挥到极致。当时的总体政策是，所有分行及高管职位均由英国臣民担任。唯一的例外是澳大利亚和新西兰以及南非地区，那里的当地定居者及其后代被视为可接受的英国人。这些英国银行在公开声明中一再强调，它们恪守最严格的英式正统银行规则——即使事实并非如此。</span></span></span>

The British banks were stable institutions in practice as well as in image. They had often been established in countries at early stages of their modern economic growth. As the products of such entrepreneurial risk-taking, they frequently enjoyed powerful first mover advantages. These included longevity. Although the first banks in Australia, for example, were established in the 1810s, several of the leading British banks there had been established as early as the 1830s, while almost all of the British banks active in Latin America dated from the 1860s. Banks also established effective governance structures designed to control moral hazard problems in their multinational operations. Typically, London-based boards would closely monitor the operation of lending facilities, and by the end of the century most banks had Inspection (internal audit) departments well established. None of this prevented agent/principal problems - indeed tensions between London and the bank managers 'on the spot' are one of the persistent themes in British multinational banking.<sup>18</sup> There was a constant tension between prudence and entrepreneurial innovation.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国银行无论在实务上还是形象上都是稳定的机构。它们往往是在那些国家现代经济增长早期阶段就建立起来的。作为这种创业风险的产物，它们常常享有强大的先发优势，其中包括悠久的运营历史。例如，尽管澳大利亚的第一批银行成立于 1810 年代，但好几家主要的英国银行早在 1830 年代就已在那里扎根；而几乎所有在拉丁美洲活跃的英国银行都可追溯到 1860 年代。这些银行还建立了有效的治理结构，旨在控制跨国经营中的道德风险问题。通常情况下，设在伦敦的董事会会密切监控贷款业务的运作，到 19 世纪末，大多数银行都已设立了完善的 Inspection（内部审计）部门。 这一切都无法避免代理/委托人问题——事实上，伦敦与“现场”银行经理之间的紧张关系是英国跨国银行业中一个持续存在的主题。 <sup>18</sup> 谨慎与创业创新之间始终存在一种紧张关系。</span></span></span>

Perhaps surprisingly given their image, British multinational banks were also often successful before 1914 in achieving competitive advantages through pursuing strategies of focus. The British banks serviced the requirements of British business in each region. The banks, however, were less 'following the client' than the product of initiatives in the first place. London merchants and private banks with interests in the West Indies were behind the foundation of the Colonial Bank in 1836. British nitrate and other trading interests in Chile lay behind the origin of the Anglo South American Bank. The Standard Bank of South Africa originated as an initiative of Port Elizabeth merchants with close trading connections with Britain. Given the importance of British commerce in the world economy before 1914, a focus on servicing its requirements produced formidable competitive advantages. Links were particularly close because the boards of banks usually contained - and were constantly replenished by - representatives of mercantile or other British business interests in each region.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">也许令人意外的是，尽管形象如此，英国跨国银行在 1914 年之前也常常通过专注战略成功获得竞争优势。这些英国银行服务于各地英国企业的需求。然而，这些银行的创立更多是源于主动创新，而非简单地"跟随客户"。1836 年，伦敦商人和私人家族银行（因其在西印度群岛的利益）推动了 Colonial Bank 的成立。英智南美银行（Anglo South American Bank）则起源于英国在智利的硝石及其他贸易利益。南非标准银行（Standard Bank of South Africa）最初是由与英国有密切贸易往来的伊丽莎白港商人发起创立的。鉴于 1914 年前英国商业在全球经济中的重要地位，专注于服务其需求产生了巨大的竞争优势。银行董事会通常由各地商业或其他英国商业利益的代表组成，并且不断有新的代表加入，这使得它们之间的联系尤为紧密。</span></span></span>

Very few, if any, British multinational banks confined their business to the finance of trade and related exchange operations. Many proved skilled at developing niche markets elsewhere. Banks <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_42"></span>often financed the activities of the ethnic minorities who functioned as traders and intermediaries: the Indians and Chinese in Burma, for example. Intermediaries were employed. In late-nineteenth-century China, the <span class="italic">compradores</span> of the British banks lent to Chinese banks which on-lent to the indigenous commercial and agricultural sectors.<sup>19</sup> The British managers of British banks in Asia may have confined their lending to Europeans, but there was frequently a parallel stream of business activity in the same institution focused on parts of the indigenous economy.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">即便有，英国跨国银行中也极少有只专注于贸易融资及相关外汇业务的。许多银行在其他领域善于开拓利基市场。它们经常为充当商人和中介的少数民族提供融资，例如缅甸的印度人和华人。中介也被广泛使用。在 19 世纪末的中国，英国银行的买办向中国本土银行放贷，这些银行再转贷给本土商业和农业部门。 <sup>19</sup> 英国银行在亚洲的英籍经理们可能只向欧洲人放贷，但同一机构内部往往还存在一条并行的业务流，聚焦于本土经济的某些领域。</span></span></span>

In a number of regions the British banks developed flexible (if 'unorthodox') strategies to exploit particular niche markets. Sometimes the banks established separate companies to do 'unorthodox' things: the National Bank of New Zealand sponsored the formation of the National Mortgage and Agency Company of New Zealand in 1877. In Argentina from the 1890s, British banks responded to competitive pressures (and a growth in fixed deposits) to diversify into the provision of medium and long-term credit, mortgage lending and even the finance of occasional industrial ventures.<sup>20</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在一些地区，英国银行采取了灵活（即便有些“非正统”）的策略来开拓特定的利基市场。有时，银行会设立独立的公司来从事这些“非正统”业务：例如，1877 年，新西兰国民银行推动了新西兰国家抵押与代理公司的成立。自 19 世纪 90 年代起，在阿根廷，英国银行为了应对竞争压力（以及定期存款的增长），开始多元化发展，提供中长期信贷、抵押贷款，甚至为偶发的工业项目提供融资。 <sup>20</sup> </span></span></span>

Casualties among nineteenth-century banks frequently arose from a focusing on the wrong market at the wrong time. Banks which were highly exposed to the fortunes of a single commodity were vulnerable in this respect. Even the Oriental Bank Corporation, one of the most geographically diverse of the nineteenth-century British banks, was ultimately forced into liquidation in 1884 by a series of bad coffee crops in Ceylon.<sup>21</sup> In India, the Agra Bank was helped towards its liquidation in 1900 by over-exposure to the tea market. Other banks got into difficulty by excessive diversification into too many unrelated fields. One example was Cox and Co., Army Agents dating from the eighteenth century, who in the 1900s began opening branches (to undertake commercial business) in India, and later Egypt. By the early 1920s the firm had also acquired a shipping agency and owned cotton ginning factories in Egypt. Lloyds saved Cox from bankruptcy by acquiring them in 1923.<sup>22</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪银行的倒闭往往是由于在错误的时间专注于错误的市场所致。那些高度依赖单一商品命运的银行在这方面尤为脆弱。即便是地理分布最为广泛的 19 世纪英国银行之一——Oriental Bank Corporation，最终也在 1884 年因锡兰连续几季咖啡歉收而被迫清算。 <sup>21</sup> 在印度，Agra Bank 因过度暴露于茶叶市场，于 1900 年走向清算。其他银行则因过度多元化涉足过多不相关领域而陷入困境。一个例子是成立于 18 世纪的军需代理商 Cox and Co.，该公司在 1900 年代开始在印度开设分行（从事商业业务），随后又进入埃及。到 20 世纪 20 年代初，该公司还收购了一家航运公司，并在埃及拥有轧棉厂。Lloyds 于 1923 年收购了 Cox，使其免于破产。 <sup>22</sup> </span></span></span>

### 1914—1945

In the interwar years the structure of the world banking industry changed markedly. The decline in international trade and factor mobility had a fundamental influence. The problems of many primary producers and settler economies are familiar. The economic growth which did occur, in Brazil and India for example, stemmed more often from import substitution than foreign trade. <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_43"></span>British trade supremacy evaporated, while London's pre-eminence as a financial centre declined relative to New York and continental European centres. Receptivity began to decline. Central banks were established in most of Latin America in the interwar years. The South African Reserve Bank was formed in 1920, and by the 1930s the state-owned Commonwealth Bank was developing as an Australian central bank. Regulatory measures and exchange controls altered the parameters of banking as it had existed before 1914. Competitive pressures increased as locally-owned modern banks emerged in many places. In some cases, such as Iran and Australia, state-owned institutions combined central and commercial banking, to the chagrin and dismay of the British banks.<sup>23</sup> These changed conditions meant that barriers to entry began to rise for British institutions, but decline for those of other countries. Interwar banking was more difficult, more regulated and more competitive than before 1914. A symbol of the changed times was the collapse in 1931 of the largest (by asset size) of the British multinational banks, the Anglo-South American Bank, which was overcome by the collapse of the Chilean nitrate industry. The bank was saved from liquidation (and eventually merged into the Bank of London and South America in 1936), at an eventual cost of £4.5 million, provided by the Bank of England and a 'pool' of domestic banks.<sup>24</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">两次世界大战期间，全球银行业的格局发生了显著变化。国际贸易和要素流动的萎缩产生了根本性影响。许多初级产品生产国和移民经济体面临的困境已为人所熟知。此间实现的经济增长，例如在巴西和印度，更多源于进口替代而非对外贸易。英国的贸易霸主地位已然消逝，而伦敦作为金融中心的卓越地位也相对逊色于纽约及欧洲大陆的金融中心。对外资的接纳度开始下降。在两次世界大战期间，拉丁美洲大部分国家建立了中央银行。南非储备银行于 1920 年成立，到 20 世纪 30 年代，国有的联邦银行逐渐发展为澳大利亚的中央银行。监管措施和外汇管制改变了 1914 年之前银行业存在的格局。随着各地本土现代银行的涌现，竞争压力日益加剧。 在某些国家，比如伊朗和澳大利亚，国有机构将中央银行与商业银行职能合二为一，令英国银行懊恼不已。 <sup>23</sup> 这种变化意味着，英国金融机构的准入门槛开始提高，而其他国家机构的门槛却在降低。与 1914 年前相比，两次世界大战期间的银行业面临更多困难、更强的监管和更激烈的竞争。时代变迁的标志之一是，1931 年，按资产规模计算当时英国最大的跨国银行 Anglo-South American Bank 因智利硝石行业崩溃而倒闭。该银行最终避免了破产清算（并于 1936 年并入 Bank of London and South America），但代价是英格兰银行与国内多家银行组成的"资金池"提供了 450 万英镑的援助。 <sup>24</sup> </span></span></span>

The changes in industry structure worked almost uniformly in the direction of reducing the competitive advantages that British banks had enjoyed and developed prior to the First World War. In the area of cost, dollars began to rival sterling in the settlement of international transactions, especially in some areas of Latin America.<sup>25</sup> Exchange controls and other problems greatly curtailed the floating of foreign loans on the London market. New local institutions competed vigorously for deposits. Bank Misr in Egypt and Bank Melli in Iran were two examples in the Middle East.<sup>26</sup> The ability to move funds between countries was disrupted by exchange controls and exchange instability.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">产业结构的调整几乎一致地削弱了英国银行在第一次世界大战前所享有并积累的竞争优势。在成本方面，美元开始在国际交易结算中与英镑竞争，尤其在拉丁美洲部分地区。外汇管制及其他问题严重限制了外国债券在伦敦市场的发行。新兴本土机构积极争夺存款，中东地区的埃及 Misr 银行和伊朗 Melli 银行便是两个例子。资金跨境流动的能力因外汇管制和汇率波动而受阻。</span></span></span>

Competitive advantages in other areas also declined. As nationalist sentiments grew in developing economies, being British - indeed, being foreign at all - started to become a disadvantage.<sup>27</sup> While British trade and business interests provided a dwindling customer market in many areas, the ability of banks to select high-quality local business was challenged by competition. British banks which had developed to service trade finance and exchange business frequently had their branches in less than optimal places. In interwar Australia the British banks were disadvantaged in lending to the new manufacturing industries because their branch <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_44"></span>network had a rural/mercantile bias reflecting their once successful strategy of relying on agriculture (especially wool) and related exports.<sup>28</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">其他领域的竞争优势也在减弱。随着发展中经济体民族主义情绪高涨，成为英国企业——甚至仅仅是外国企业——开始变成一种劣势。 <sup>27</sup> 尽管英国贸易和商业利益在许多地区提供的客户市场日益萎缩，但银行筛选高质量本地业务的能力也因竞争而受到挑战。那些为服务贸易融资和外汇业务而发展起来的英国银行，其分支机构往往设在并非最优的地点。在两次世界大战期间的澳大利亚，英国银行在向新兴制造业放贷时处于不利地位，因为它们的分支网络偏向农村/商业领域，这反映了它们曾经依赖农业（尤其是羊毛）及相关出口的成功战略。 <sup>28</sup> </span></span></span>

Evidence of the changed industry structure and the decline in British competitive advantages came from the very sharp fall in new entrants into the industry. Virtually all the British overseas banks were founded before 1900. After the turn of the century the only new 'free-standing' banks were the Eastern Bank (1909), an offshoot of the trading house E.D. Sassoon, and the P & O Banking Corporation (1920), an offshoot of the Inchcape trading and shipping empire which only lasted eight years before coming under the control of Chartered Bank. The only other new entrants into British overseas banking were wholly-owned subsidiaries of the British clearers, such as a group of small banks established to operate in continental Europe around the First World War.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">行业结构的变化以及英国竞争优势的衰退，从新进入该行业企业的急剧减少中可见一斑。几乎所有的英国海外银行都成立于 1900 年之前。进入 20 世纪后，新成立的"独立"银行仅有东方银行（1909 年，贸易行 E.D. Sassoon 的附属机构）和 P&O 银行公司（1920 年，Inchcape 贸易与航运帝国的附属机构，仅运营八年便被渣打银行收购）。英国海外银行业仅有的其他新进入者，是英国清算银行的全资子公司，例如第一次世界大战前后为在欧洲大陆开展业务而设立的一批小型银行。</span></span></span>

Market signals, therefore, suggested that British multinational banks needed to renew and adapt their competitive strategies as the potential for 'free-riding' on British economic and political hegemony began to decline, but in many cases there was only a limited appreciation of the change in environmental factors. One possibility would have been to identify new market growth areas by shifting focus to new products. However, although the Imperial Bank of Persia's obstinate refusal to adapt to changed circumstances in Iran in the 1930s was an extreme case,<sup>29</sup> there was little evidence elsewhere of more than marginal cases of product innovation. The two leading British banks in Australia declined to become involved with the 'pernicious world-wide habit' of hire purchase.<sup>30</sup> Product innovation was often enforced rather than voluntary. By 1936 Eastern Bank's Calcutta branch was deriving most of its profits from interest on 'locked up' advances on property.<sup>31</sup> One of the more positive innovations was Barclays (DCO)'s lending to agricultural co-operatives in Cyprus at the end of the 1930s, which was a forerunner of larger schemes in post-war Africa.<sup>32</sup> The typical response to difficulty, however, was to seek to defend 'old' products by reaching collusive agreements with other banks (British or otherwise) in each market. These proliferated in the interwar years.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">市场信号表明，随着"搭便车"英国经济政治霸权的潜力开始减弱，英国跨国银行需要更新并调整其竞争策略，但在许多情况下，它们对环境因素变化的认知相当有限。一个可能的选择是将重心转向新产品，从而开拓新的市场增长领域。然而，尽管 1930 年代波斯帝国银行顽固拒绝适应伊朗环境变化属于极端案例， <sup>29</sup> 但在其他地区，产品创新的案例也仅停留在边际层面，几乎没有显著进展。在澳大利亚的两家主要英国银行拒绝涉足"有害全球的"分期付款业务。 <sup>30</sup> 产品创新往往是被迫而非自愿的。到 1936 年，东方银行加尔各答分行的大部分利润来自不动产贷款的"锁仓"利息。 <sup>31</sup> 20 世纪 30 年代末，巴克莱（DCO）向塞浦路斯农业合作社提供贷款，这算是较为积极的创新之一，也为战后非洲更大规模的项目开了先河。 <sup>32</sup> 然而，面对困难时的典型对策，却是在各个市场与其它银行（无论是英资还是其他银行）达成共谋协议，以捍卫“老旧”产品。这类做法在两次世界大战之间的年代里层出不穷。</span></span></span>

For many banks a constraint in shifting product-focus was potential conflict with the differentiation strategy - the emphasis on being stable institutions embodying British banking orthodoxy. It was on these grounds that the Chairman of Barclays (DCO) opposed suggestions that the bank should enter the mortgage market in South Africa in 1926:<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

Concern to maintain the standards of banking orthodoxy did not rule out product innovation, but it often constrained it.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对恪守正统银行准则的关切并未完全排除产品创新，但往往对其形成了制约。</span></span></span>

Given the declining value of being 'British' and the fall in foreign trade and factor mobility, a shift in strategy towards acquiring greater local identity in each market might have seemed an option. There were marginal movements in this direction. The Mercantile Bank of India (a small bank vulnerable in its South and Southeast Asian markets to competition from its bigger brethren, Hongkong Bank and Chartered Bank) became in 1936 the first of the Eastern Exchange Banks to begin training Asian nationals for executive positions. However, there were many doubts about how far the British image - and British standards of probity - could be maintained if Asians were appointed to, say, managerial positions.<sup>34</sup> The formation of local subsidiaries was another potential option, and was raised by the Bank of England in 1942 as a possible response to nationalism in Asia. The idea was coolly received by the banks.<sup>35</sup> The creation of local subsidiaries might have hindered the ability of the banks to switch funds between countries so as to borrow, lend and invest where the returns were highest, or the highest compatible with maintaining liquidity and banking probity.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">鉴于"英国"身份的价值日益下降，加上对外贸易和要素流动的减少，转向在每个市场获取更强的本地身份似乎是一种可选策略。这一方向上也曾有过一些微小的尝试。1936 年，Mercantile Bank of India（一家在其南亚和东南亚市场易受更大竞争对手 Hongkong Bank 和 Chartered Bank 冲击的小型银行）成为东方汇兑银行中首家开始培训亚洲本土人士担任高管职位的银行。然而，对于如果任命亚洲人担任比如管理职位，那么英国形象——以及英国的诚信标准——能在多大程度上得以维持，存在诸多疑虑。 <sup>34</sup> 成立本地子公司是另一个潜在选项，英格兰银行在 1942 年曾将其作为应对亚洲民族主义的一种可能方案提出。但各银行对此反应冷淡。 <sup>35</sup> 设立地方子公司可能会削弱银行在不同国家之间调配资金的能力，从而无法在最能获利、或是在维持流动性和银行诚信的前提下获利最高的地方进行借贷和投资。</span></span></span>

Partly because of tensions between differentiation and focus strategies, the main response of the British banks to the exogenously changing environment after 1914 was institutional. As explained earlier, the interwar years saw a growth of concentration in the British industry, or, more accurately, a complex pattern of cross holdings in which the clearing banks assumed a prominent position. By the late 1930s Lloyds Bank had a majority shareholding in the only British bank remaining in Latin America, had absorbed the former Cox branches in British India, and had a shareholding in the National Bank of New Zealand. In Europe, in 1917 National Provincial took a 50 per cent stake in Lloyd's prewar continental European subsidiary. Lloyds was also allied with National Provincial (and Westminster Bank and Standard Bank of South Africa) in ownership of the Bank of British West Africa (BBWA). National Provincial also acquired 100 per cent control of Grindlays Bank in 1924, while Westminster Bank had its own <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_46"></span>continental European venture. Barclays Bank formed the second large group of banks with its control of Barclays (DCO), 100 per cent control of continental European and Canadian subsidiaries and a shareholding in Eastern Bank acquired in the late 1930s. Most of the overseas banks which had remained independent of the clearers were involved in some form of merger or cross-holding. The Mercantile Bank of India purchased the Bank of Mauritius in 1916, Standard had its holding in BBWA, and Chartered Bank took over the P & O Banking Corporation and its Indian-registered subsidiary, Allahabad Bank. In Australia, the English, Scottish and Australian Bank acquired the London Bank of Australia in 1921, while more wide-ranging merger proposals began to be canvassed from 1929, although it was not until 1951 that the two largest banks eventually merged to form ANZ.<sup>36</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">部分原因是差异化战略与聚焦战略之间的张力，英国银行对 1914 年后外部环境变化的主要回应是制度性的。如前所述，两次世界大战期间，英国银行业集中度有所提高，更准确地说，形成了一种复杂的交叉持股模式，清算银行在其中占据了突出地位。到 20 世纪 30 年代末，劳埃德银行持有拉丁美洲仅存的一家英国银行的多数股权，并吸收了此前在英属印度的考克斯分行，同时持有新西兰国民银行的股份。在欧洲，1917 年国民地方银行收购了劳埃德战前欧洲大陆子公司 50%的股份。劳埃德还与国民地方银行（以及威斯敏斯特银行和南非标准银行）联合持有西非银行（BBWA）的所有权。 National Provincial 也在 1924 年全面收购了 Grindlays Bank，而 Westminster Bank 则在欧洲大陆有自己的业务。Barclays Bank 通过控制 Barclays (DCO)、全资控股欧洲大陆和加拿大的子公司，以及在 1930 年代末收购 Eastern Bank 的股权，组成了第二大银行集团。大部分独立于清算银行的海外银行都参与了某种形式的合并或交叉持股。Mercantile Bank of India 于 1916 年收购了 Bank of Mauritius，Standard 持有 BBWA 的股份，Chartered Bank 则接管了 P & O Banking Corporation 及其在印度注册的子公司 Allahabad Bank。在澳大利亚，English, Scottish and Australian Bank 于 1921 年收购了 London Bank of Australia，而从 1929 年开始，更大范围的合并提案开始酝酿，不过直到 1951 年，两家最大的银行才最终合并，成立了 ANZ。 <sup>36</sup> </span></span></span>

The enthusiasm of the independent British multinational banks for merger or acquiring a clearing bank as a shareholder can be readily understood in terms of corporate diversification in the face of changing and uncertain conditions. The banks were concentrated on a single region - or even a single country - and were thus highly exposed if nationalistic sentiments developed in their particular area or if the principal commodity of the country had a downturn. Geographical diversification reduced risk. A link with a clearing bank was a route to survival through the promise of secure access to funds, at a time when uncertain conditions may have made the capital markets less willing suppliers to the banks than before 1914.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">独立的英国跨国银行对并购清算银行作为股东的热情，可以从企业在面对变化和不确定环境时寻求多元化的角度来理解。这些银行集中于某一地区——甚至单一国家——因此，如果其所在地区出现民族主义情绪，或该国主要商品遭遇衰退，它们将面临巨大风险。地域多元化可以降低风险。在不确定条件下，资本市场可能不如 1914 年前那样愿意向银行提供资金，而与清算银行建立联系则是通过确保资金获取渠道实现生存的途径。</span></span></span>

As for the expansion of the clearers into multinational banking, it would be tempting to describe this in Chandlerian terms. From the late nineteenth century, domestic British banking had become highly concentrated through successive mergers, resulting in the 'Big Five' English clearing banks by 1920. These formidable banking groups could be seen as ready by the interwar years to employ their capital resources and experience abroad and thereby enhance the competitive advantages of British overseas banking.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">关于清算银行扩展为跨国银行业务，用钱德勒式的术语来描述这点颇具吸引力。自 19 世纪末起，英国国内银行业通过连续合并变得高度集中，到 1920 年形成了“五大”英格兰清算银行。到两次世界大战期间，这些庞大的银行集团已准备好运用其资本资源和海外经验，从而增强英国海外银行业的竞争优势。</span></span></span>

In practice, such a model only fits the British experience in the most general terms. Although the joint stock banks were leading examples of the separation of ownership from control in British business, the development of managerial hierarchies in the early twentieth century did not follow favoured Chandlerian lines. Barclays operated a highly decentralized system based on local boards of directors, who usually represented banks that had been absorbed into the Barclays empire. Lloyds had a more rudimentary system based on a main Board supported - in the early 1920s - by a local board and three committees of directors, 'all products of <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_47"></span>past amalgamations, set-up rather to smooth over periods of transition than with the intention of developing a comprehensive system of local centres of control'<sup>37</sup>.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">实际上，这种模式仅在最宽泛的层面上与英国的经验相吻合。尽管股份制银行是英国企业中所有权与控制权分离的典型案例，但 20 世纪初管理层的层级结构发展并未遵循钱德勒式路径。巴克莱银行（Barclays）依靠地方董事会实行高度分权制度，这些董事会成员通常代表已被巴克莱帝国吸纳的银行。劳埃德银行（Lloyds）则采用更基础的结构——以总行董事会为核心，在 20 世纪 20 年代初期辅以地方董事会及三个董事委员会，"这些均源于过往合并的产物，设立初衷更多是为了平稳过渡转型期，而非意图构建全面的地方控制中心体系" <sup>37</sup> 。</span></span></span>

Barclays came closest to having a strategic justification for its foreign expansion. F.C. Goodenough, the wartime chairman, agreed with a widespread contemporary view that British manufacturers would need the active assistance of their bankers in postwar world markets, and that in order to give this assistance Barclays needed foreign branches. However, the actual formation of DCO was opportunistic. Barclays appear to have targeted the Colonial Bank, whose board had a strategy to diversify out of the West Indies. The investment in the Anglo-Egyptian Bank, however, seems to have come more from chance than design, while Barclays' acquisition of the National Bank of South Africa came about because of the latter's near-collapse through injudicious expansion.<sup>38</sup> Lloyds justified its acquisition of the London and River Plate Bank in 1918 by the argument that it could thereby better serve its customers in the Latin American market.<sup>39</sup> However the small investments in the National Bank of New Zealand and the Bank of British West Africa seem to have been made with no clear aim in view. London and Brazilian Bank was acquired in 1923 apparently because the bank was faltering after the retirement of its long-serving ninety-year-old chairman. Oddly, the Bank of England was responsible for further expansions of Lloyds' interests, for although its Governor, Montagu Norman, vigorously opposed the overseas expansion of the clearing banks, he hated bank failures even more, and he 'encouraged' Lloyds to acquire Cox and King in 1923 and orchestrated the support given by the clearers to Anglo-South American after 1931.<sup>40</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">巴克莱银行的海外扩张最具有战略依据。战时董事长 F.C. Goodenough 认同当时普遍的观点：英国制造商在战后国际市场上需要银行的大力支持，而为了提供这种支持，巴克莱必须设立海外分行。然而，DCO 的实际成立却是机会主义的产物。巴克莱似乎瞄准了 Colonial Bank，该行董事会原本计划从西印度群岛业务中分散风险。但投资 Anglo-Egyptian Bank 更像是偶然之举而非精心规划，而收购 National Bank of South Africa 则是因为后者因盲目扩张几近崩溃。 <sup>38</sup> 劳埃德银行在 1918 年收购伦敦与拉普拉塔银行的理由是：这样能更好地为拉丁美洲市场的客户提供服务。 <sup>39</sup> 然而，对新西兰国民银行和英属西非银行的小额投资似乎并没有明确的目标。1923 年收购伦敦巴西银行，显然是因为该银行在任职多年的 90 岁董事长退休后运营不稳。奇怪的是，劳埃德银行权益的进一步扩张竟归因于英格兰银行——尽管其行长蒙塔古·诺曼强烈反对清算银行向海外扩张，但他更痛恨银行倒闭，于是 1923 年“鼓励”劳埃德收购科克斯金银行，并在 1931 年后精心策划了清算银行对英南美银行的救助支持。 <sup>40</sup> </span></span></span>

The wide geographical spread of Lloyd's interests created obvious potential for forging new forms of competitive advantage on the basis of a diversified, global banking group, In fact, there was no attempt to integrate the various operations, there was almost no sharing of business, and indeed Lloyds' refusal or inability to intervene in its foreign subsidiaries left it in the position of possessing ownership without control. 'Although we are carrying full responsibility for these Banks and must necessarily do so', a Lloyds' internal report noted in 1943, 'we have little knowledge of what is going on except when the Balance Sheets are produced and even then very scanty figures are given and little attention paid to them'<sup>41</sup>. Indeed, the main competitive advantage gained by the overseas banks from the association with Lloyds was that the clearing bank (or rather its shareholders) served as a barrier to exit. In 1943 Lloyds calculated it had invested £7.5 million in <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_48"></span>overseas banks, of which £4.8 million had been completely lost and a 'negligible income' secured. Lloyds and National Provincial Foreign Bank was insolvent by the 1930s and was saved by 'gifts' amounting to £1.5 million from its two English parents. Bolsa's 'real' profits declined steadily after 1924, culminating in 'real' losses amounting to £1.4 million in the three years 1931 to 1933.<sup>42</sup> It is plausible to suggest that without the backing of Lloyds for Bolsa which was paralleled by the £4.5 million provided by the Bank of England and the other clearers for Anglo-South American after 1931, British overseas banking would have been eliminated from Latin America.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">劳埃德银行的业务覆盖范围极广，这为其打造基于多元化全球银行集团的新型竞争优势创造了明显潜力。然而，实际上该行并未尝试整合各项业务，各分支之间几乎没有任何业务共享。事实上，劳埃德对海外子公司的拒绝或无力干预，使其陷入了拥有所有权却丧失控制权的境地。1943 年的一份内部报告指出：“尽管我们对这些银行负有全责，也必须如此，但除了看到资产负债表时，我们对它们的运营情况几乎一无所知——即便看到报表，所能获取的数据也极其有限，而且几乎未予重视” <sup>41</sup> 。事实上，这些海外银行从与劳埃德的关联中获得的主要竞争优势，在于这家清算银行（更确切地说是其股东）充当了它们退出的屏障。 1943 年，劳埃德银行（Lloyds）估算其在海外银行的投资总额为 750 万英镑，其中 480 万英镑已完全亏损，仅获得"微不足道的收入"。到了 20 世纪 30 年代，劳埃德银行与国民地方外国银行（National Provincial Foreign Bank）已资不抵债，靠其两家英国母行提供的 150 万英镑"赠款"才得以存活。博尔萨银行（Bolsa）的"实际"利润自 1924 年后持续下滑，最终在 1931 至 1933 这三年间累计出现高达 140 万英镑的"实际"亏损。 <sup>42</sup> 可以有理由认为，如果没有劳埃德对 Bolsa 的支持——这与 1931 年后英格兰银行及其他清算银行为 Anglo-South American 提供的 450 万英镑相辅相成——英国海外银行业务将从拉丁美洲消失。</span></span></span>

Barclays' merger of its overseas banking interests into DCO was a more effective solution than the Lloyds one. Barclays (DCO), like its parent, was run as a highly-decentralized organization with local boards of directors. There were closer links with Barclays, with the British bank providing training for new recruits to the overseas bank. Nevertheless, one of the main functions of the DCO organization in the interwar years was to use the rising profits from South Africa to subsidize the losses or poor returns achieved in the large branch network covering the West Indies and north, west and east Africa. The South African branches contributed 52 per cent of the net profits of all DCO's overseas branches between 1928 and 1933, and 60.5 per cent between 1934 and 1939.<sup>43</sup> Neither of the two wholly-owned subsidiaries in Canada and continental Europe seem to have been commercially viable in the interwar years. Barclays Bank (France) had to be 'given' £417,742 by its parent to keep it in existence in the 1930s.<sup>44</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">巴克莱将其海外银行业务并入 DCO 的做法，比劳埃德的方案更为有效。巴克莱（DCO）与其母公司一样，采用高度分权化的管理模式，设有地方董事会。它与巴克莱银行保持紧密联系，由这家英国银行为海外银行的新员工提供培训。尽管如此，在两次世界大战期间，DCO 组织的主要职能之一是利用南非日益增长的利润，来补贴覆盖西印度群岛以及北非、西非和东非的庞大分支网络所产生的亏损或低回报。1928 年至 1933 年间，南非分支机构贡献了 DCO 所有海外分支机构净利润的 52%，1934 年至 1939 年间则达到 60.5%。 <sup>43</sup> 在加拿大和欧洲大陆的两家全资子公司，在两次世界大战期间似乎都未实现商业上的可行性。 巴克莱银行（法国）在 20 世纪 30 年代不得不由其母公司“注资”41.7742 万英镑，才得以勉强维持生存。 <sup>44</sup> </span></span></span>

### 1945 to the 1980s<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1945 年至 1980 年代</span></span></span>

After 1945, most of the characteristics of the structure of the international banking industry which had favoured British institutions before the First World War disappeared. Receptivity of foreign institutions declined in many areas, a trend not reversed until the 1980s. Independence of former European colonies in Africa and Asia was universally followed by the creation of central banks, the development of regulatory controls, and often discrimination against foreign institutions. Between the 1950s and 1970s world trade and foreign direct investment expanded faster than ever before, but the British economy played a diminishing role in such movements. Britain's share of world exports declined continuously and, moreover, the focus of trading and investment interest shifted towards western Europe and North America, two regions where British banking interests were least represented.<sup>45</sup> Sterling's role as <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_49"></span>a reserve currency disintegrated. Competition increased sharply. American banks expanded their international branch networks from the 1960s. Pressure to operate internationally in dollar-denominated deals was substantial from the 1950s with the clear emergence of deficits in the American balance of payments. This resulted in a flow of dollars into the world economy which fostered the emergence of the Eurocurrency markets. Meanwhile locally-owned modern banks flourished all over the developing world.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1945 年后，国际银行业结构中那些在第一次世界大战前曾有利于英国机构的特点大多消失。许多地区对外国机构的接纳度下降，这一趋势直到 20 世纪 80 年代才得以扭转。非洲和亚洲的前欧洲殖民地独立后，普遍建立了中央银行，发展了监管控制，并常常对外国机构采取歧视政策。20 世纪 50 年代至 70 年代间，世界贸易和外国直接投资以前所未有的速度扩张，但英国经济在这些活动中的作用日益减弱。英国在世界出口中的份额持续下降，而且贸易和投资兴趣焦点转向西欧和北美——这两个地区恰恰是英国银行利益最薄弱的环节。 <sup>45</sup> 英镑作为储备货币的地位瓦解。竞争急剧加剧。 自 20 世纪 60 年代起，美国银行开始拓展其国际分行网络。自 50 年代以来，随着美国国际收支逆差的明显出现，以美元计价的交易国际化压力日益增大。这导致美元大量流入世界经济，推动了欧洲货币市场的兴起。与此同时，本土银行现代化浪潮也在发展中国家全面开花。</span></span></span>

These radical changes in industry structure necessitated new generic strategies if British multinational banks were to regain their competitive advantages. The declining importance of British business in many economies combined with the rivalry of indigenous institutions for local business, called for the banks to re-focus their strategies to new target segments of the industry. There was more movement in this direction than in the interwar years. The decade after the Second World War saw the formation by several banks of 'development corporations' designed to provide medium and long-term credit facilities in Africa and Asia's developing economies: Barclays Overseas Development Corporation, registered in 1946, led the field. In commercial banking proper, many banks expanded their local lending: the growth in branches shown in [Table 3.1](#Ch03.xhtml_fig0003) was one result, as banks sought to increase their local deposits and undertake new types of indigenous business.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">产业结构发生的这些根本性变革，迫使英国跨国银行必须制定新的通用战略，才能重拾竞争优势。英国企业在众多经济体中的地位逐渐式微，加之本土金融机构对当地业务的激烈竞争，促使银行将战略重心重新转向行业内新的目标细分市场。这一趋势的推进力度较两次世界大战期间更为显著。二战后十年间，多家银行相继成立"开发公司"，旨在为非洲及亚洲发展中经济体提供中长期信贷服务：巴克莱海外开发公司（Barclays Overseas Development Corporation）于 1946 年率先注册成立，成为该领域的先驱。在商业银行业务领域，诸多银行扩大了本地贷款规模：表 3.1 所示的分支机构增长便是成果之一，当时银行正致力于增加本地存款并拓展新型本土业务。</span></span></span>

From the 1960s (and earlier in some instances) more dramatic strategies were developed to re-focus in the market. Merchant banks were acquired. The newly-formed Standard Chartered engaged in widespread product diversification. In a single year (1973), the bank established a joint venture merchant banking operation, acquired a large British secondary banking and finance company (the Hodge Group) and purchased 55 per cent of one of the world's largest bullion dealers, Mocatta and Goldsmid.<sup>46</sup> The 1970s saw major innovations in lending techniques by British multinational banks, although these were usually copied from prior American practice. Growing size of investment projects led to the provision of syndicated loans by 'ad hoc' groups of banks, while term lending acquired increased importance at the expense of the traditional overdraft. The British multinational banks became involved in 'package' finance for large overseas projects, with loans and repayments phased so as to fit with timing of projects, and agreements frequently providing for multi-currency finance. Participation in 'consortium banks' was another development in this period.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">自 20 世纪 60 年代起（部分案例甚至更早），银行业开始采取更为激进的策略以重新聚焦市场。商业银行被收购，新成立的渣打银行（Standard Chartered）展开了广泛的产品多元化布局。仅在 1973 年这一年，该行就设立了合资商人银行业务，收购了一家英国大型二级银行及金融公司（霍奇集团），并购得全球最大黄金交易商之一莫卡塔与戈德史密斯（Mocatta and Goldsmid）55%的股权。 <sup>46</sup> 20 世纪 70 年代，英国跨国银行在贷款技术领域实现了重大创新——尽管这些创新大多借鉴了美国的先行实践。随着投资项目规模不断扩大，由特定银行团临时组建的银团贷款应运而生，而定期贷款的重要性也与日俱增，逐渐取代了传统的透支业务。 英国跨国银行开始涉足大型海外项目的“一揽子”融资，贷款与还款分期与项目时间节点相匹配，且协议通常提供多币种融资。参与“银团银行”也是这一时期的新动向。</span></span></span>

Effectively, after the Second World War the British banks abandoned their old focus strategy, and many attempted to enter a <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_50"></span>growing number of segments of their industry. The results were frequently not very satisfactory, with many banks exhibiting signs of Porter's 'stuck in the middle' syndrome. Part of the difficulty was that, after 1945, the strategies of banks were limited by the actions of regulatory central banks in Britain and elsewhere. In Britain the Bank of England resisted some product innovation. The Bank (for example) had been less than enthusiastic about Barclays' Development Corporation in the 1940s.<sup>47</sup> In the mid-1960s National and Grindlays was prevented from gaining full control of the merchant bank Brandts, while Standard Bank was discouraged from lending to hire purchase companies. By the 1960s many developing countries, even pro-western ones such as Malaysia, were restricting the number and location of foreign bank branches and ordering foreign banks to make specific types of flows to specific sectors of the community.<sup>48</sup> However, at least as great a constraint was that the managements of many banks appeared to possess only a limited range of skills, which meant that product diversification strategies were often retarded, or else plagued with problems. The banks did what they had been established to do well and they proved durable business institutions. However, perhaps because they have evolved as institutions capable of controlling risks over large distances, innovation was not encouraged and the overseas banks often floundered when they attempted to innovate. Their ability to learn new skills was limited.<sup>49</sup> The corporate cultures of the banks, and their recruitment policies, were both constraints in this respect.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">实际上，第二次世界大战后，英国银行放弃了原有的核心战略，许多银行试图进军行业内越来越多的细分领域。但结果往往不尽如人意，不少银行呈现出波特所谓的“卡在中间”综合征的迹象。部分困难在于，1945 年后，银行的战略受到英国及其他地区监管性中央银行的限制。在英国，英格兰银行抵制了一些产品创新。例如，在 20 世纪 40 年代，英格兰银行对巴克莱银行的发展公司并不热衷。 <sup>47</sup> 到 20 世纪 60 年代中期，国民与格林德莱银行被阻止完全控制商人银行布兰兹，而标准银行则被劝阻不要向租购公司提供贷款。 到 20 世纪 60 年代，许多发展中国家，包括像马来西亚这样亲西方的国家，都开始限制外资银行分行的数量和选址，并强制要求外资银行向特定社区领域提供特定类型的资金流动。 <sup>48</sup> 然而，至少同样重要的制约因素是，许多银行的管理层似乎技能有限，这导致产品多元化战略常常被拖延，或者问题丛生。这些银行出色地完成了它们设立之初的使命，并证明了自身是持久的商业机构。但也许是因为它们作为能够远距离控制风险的机构而演变，创新并未受到鼓励，海外银行在尝试创新时常常举步维艰。它们学习新技能的能力十分有限。 <sup>49</sup> 银行的企业文化及其招聘政策，在这方面都是制约因素。</span></span></span>

Postwar trends called, far more than in the interwar years, for a move from the emphasis on being 'British' banks towards assuming identities more in line with new business and political realities. The appointment of local staff to executive positions was at best only a palliative to nationalist pressures, and in many countries British banks found it hard to recruit staff given the often more lucrative employment opportunities in government service.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">战后趋势表明，英国银行远较两次世界大战期间更需将重心从强调自身"英国"身份，转向塑造更符合新商业与政治现实的形象。任命本地人员担任高管职位充其量只是缓解民族主义压力的权宜之计，而在许多国家，鉴于政府职位通常提供更为丰厚的就业机会，英国银行往往难以招募到员工。</span></span></span>

The creation of locally-registered subsidiaries began to be considered soon after the Second World War: the Chairman of Barclays (DCO) had reached the conclusion that the formation of 'a separate South African Bank' was the 'right move' as early as 1948.<sup>50</sup> In the event, this step was finally taken in 1971, and right through until the end of the 1960s the British multinational banks opposed localization, stressing the economies of scale and scope gained by their horizontally-integrated corporations.<sup>51</sup> By 1970 the banks began to bow to the inevitable. Grindlays decided on an East African strategy which continued establishing 'on a permanent basis an international and merchant banking business', while <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_51"></span>'in due course' giving up 'control of the local branch banking business'.<sup>52</sup> During the 1980s it became increasingly frequent for British banks to withdraw from retail banking altogether, thus abandoning any attempt to present themselves as local institutions in developing countries. They tended to focus entirely on the international trade and wholesale banking provision for the corporate sector.<sup>53</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">第二次世界大战结束后不久，建立本地注册子公司的计划便开始提上议程：早在 1948 年，巴克莱银行(DCO)董事长就已得出结论，成立“一家独立的南非银行”是“正确之举”。 <sup>50</sup> 然而，这一举措最终直到 1971 年才得以实施，而在整个 60 年代末期，英国跨国银行仍一直反对本地化，强调通过横向一体化公司获得的规模经济与范围经济优势。 <sup>51</sup> 到了 1970 年，这些银行开始向现实低头。格林德莱银行决定采取一项东非战略，即继续“永久性地建立国际及商业银行业务”，同时“在适当时候”放弃“对本地分行银行业务的控制权”。 <sup>52</sup> 进入 80 年代，英国银行愈发频繁地全面退出零售银行业务，从而彻底放弃在发展中国家树立本地机构形象的尝试。 它们往往只专注于为企业部门提供国际贸易和批发银行业务。 <sup>53</sup> </span></span></span>

In the two former bastions of British multinational banking, Australia and South Africa, the competitive advantages gained by being British became zero or even negative in the 1970s and 1980s. The logical conclusions were drawn, and the British banking interests there entirely severed their links with the United Kingdom. ANZ in the early 1970s was one of the few surviving 'free-standing' British-registered international banks. However, as David Merrett discusses in [Chapter four](#Ch04.xhtml_ht0034) the costs of being a British bank had come to outweigh the benefits, and the shift of domicile to Australia in 1976 was the obvious outcome. The disinvestment from South Africa by Barclays and Standard Chartered in 1986 and 1987 was due in part to the political problems caused for those British-based institutions by their business in that country; again, being British presented more problems than opportunities.<sup>54</sup> In some areas the Barclays South African subsidiary had lost competitive advantages in its local market through its ties with the British parent: it had invested, for example, in inadequate ICL computer technology rather than superior IBM systems generally in use in South Africa.<sup>55</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在英国跨国银行业的两大传统堡垒——澳大利亚和南非，英国身份所带来的竞争优势在 20 世纪 70 至 80 年代已荡然无存，甚至沦为负资产。顺势而为，这些英国银行在当地彻底切断了与母国的关联。70 年代初的 ANZ，是少数几家幸存、且仍在英国注册的“独立”国际银行之一。然而，正如 David Merrett 在第四章所述，作为一家英国银行的代价已远超其收益，1976 年将注册地迁至澳大利亚便成了顺理成章的选择。1986 至 1987 年间，Barclays 和 Standard Chartered 从南非撤资，部分原因正是它们在当地的业务给这些英国金融机构带来了政治困境——再次印证了“英国身份”带来的麻烦远多于机遇。 <sup>54</sup> 在某些领域，巴克莱南非子公司因与英国母公司的关联而在当地市场失去了竞争优势：例如，它投资了性能不足的 ICL 计算机技术，而非南非普遍使用的更先进的 IBM 系统。 <sup>55</sup> </span></span></span>

The competitive advantages in cost which the British multinational banks had once held declined very rapidly after the Second World War. Being British was no longer sufficient to attract plentiful deposits. Indeed through the 1950s and 1960s country after country introduced regulations to restrict deposit-raising by foreign banks. By 1949 Bolsa's ability to raise deposits in many Latin American countries was strictly limited by assorted capital or reserve to deposit ratios.<sup>56</sup> By the 1970s the British overseas banks often had to compete with other institutions for various natural deposit substitutes, such as swaps or margins on import letters of credit and guarantees, or else rely on money markets where these had developed.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">二战后，英国跨国银行曾一度拥有的成本竞争优势迅速减弱。仅凭"英国"这一身份已不足以吸引大量存款。事实上，在 20 世纪 50 年代至 60 年代期间，一个接一个的国家出台法规，限制外国银行吸收存款。到 1949 年，Bolsa 银行在多个拉美国家吸收存款的能力已受到各种资本或准备金与存款比例规定的严格限制。 <sup>56</sup> 到了 20 世纪 70 年代，英国海外银行往往不得不与其他机构竞争各种自然存款替代品，例如进口信用证和担保的互换或保证金，或者依赖已发展起来的货币市场。</span></span></span>

By the 1950s a sterling equity base was becoming a clear competitive disadvantage as the international use of sterling looked increasingly vulnerable. Bolsa felt the problem most acutely because nowhere was the decline of British economic influence so evident as in Latin America where most trade and investment was denominated in US dollars, and where many British investments in public <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_52"></span>utilities and railways, for example, were liquidated in the postwar period. In 1957 Bolsa's new chairman, George Bolton, became one of the first movers in the emerging Eurodollar market. The rise of this market, and its location in London, helped to reestablish London as the leading international financial centre of the world. Bolton immediately began a search for US deposits, and by the early 1960s his bank was in the top four in the London Eurodollar market. During September 1957 Bolsa's dollar deposits in London averaged US\$ 3.6 million. During September 1960 they averaged US\$ 247 million, and a year later US\$ 303 million.<sup>57</sup> After the 1967 devaluation made clear sterling's demise as an international trade currency, the other international banks followed the Bolsa route. 'Bolsa are perhaps four-fifths orientated towards London and the Continent and they make very large profits in the Eurodollar and Eurobond markets ...' the chief executive of Chartered Bank noted in 1968; 'it is impossible to escape the feeling that... we \[may\] be missing out in London'.<sup>58</sup> It should be added, however, that banks who followed Bolsa's route (such as Grindlays) found the Eurodollar market to be a very expensive source of funds.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">到了 20 世纪 50 年代，英镑的强势权益基础正逐渐成为明显的竞争劣势，因为英镑的国际使用越来越脆弱。Bolsa 银行对此感受最深，因为英国经济影响力的衰退在拉丁美洲表现得最为明显——该地区大部分贸易和投资都以美元计价，且战后许多英国在公共事业和铁路等领域的投资被清算。1957 年，Bolsa 银行新任董事长 George Bolton 成为新兴欧洲美元市场的先行者之一。该市场的崛起及其落户伦敦，帮助伦敦重新确立了世界领先国际金融中心的地位。Bolton 立即着手寻找美元存款，到 20 世纪 60 年代初，他的银行已跻身伦敦欧洲美元市场前四名。1957 年 9 月期间，Bolsa 银行在伦敦的美元存款平均为 360 万美元。 1960 年 9 月期间，他们的月均贸易额达到 2.47 亿美元，一年后增至 3.03 亿美元。 <sup>57</sup> 1967 年英镑贬值明确标志着其作为国际贸易货币的衰落，此后其他国际银行纷纷效仿 Bolsa 的做法。"Bolsa 大约有五分之四的业务以伦敦和欧洲大陆为主要方向，他们在欧洲美元和欧洲债券市场上赚取了巨额利润……"渣打银行首席执行官在 1968 年指出，"不免让人感到……我们（可能）在伦敦失去了机会。" <sup>58</sup> 但需要补充的是，那些效仿 Bolsa 做法（如 Grindlays）的银行发现，欧洲美元市场是一个成本极高的资金来源。</span></span></span>

Bolsa and Bolton also took the lead in attempting to overcome the comparative disadvantage of their sterling base by trying to raise dollar equity. In 1958 the Bank of Montreal was approached about forming a joint venture.<sup>59</sup> The upshot was the formation of a Bahamas-registered joint venture, the Bank of London and Montreal, to which Bolsa contributed its branch network in Central America and the Bank of Montreal provided Canadian dollars. By 1963 Bolton was trying to persuade American banks to invest in Bolsa itself.<sup>60</sup> The result of this strategy was the acquisition of a 15 per cent stake in Bolsa by Mellon Bank in 1965. Subsequently Chase Manhatten acquired 15 per cent of Standard Bank, and in 1968 Citibank acquired 40 per cent of National and Grindlays.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">博尔萨和博尔顿还率先尝试通过筹集美元股权来克服其英镑基础的比较劣势。1958 年，他们与蒙特利尔银行接洽，探讨组建合资企业的事宜。 <sup>59</sup> 最终，一家在巴哈马注册的合资企业——伦敦及蒙特利尔银行——成立，博尔萨将其在中美洲的分行网络注入其中，而蒙特利尔银行则提供了加元资金。到 1963 年，博尔顿开始尝试说服美国银行投资博尔萨本身。 <sup>60</sup> 这一战略的结果是，1965 年梅隆银行收购了博尔萨 15%的股份。随后，大通曼哈顿银行收购了标准银行 15%的股份，1968 年花旗银行则收购了国民与格林德莱银行 40%的股份。</span></span></span>

Participation in the Eurodollar market represented a successful attempt to overcome the disadvantage of being a sterling bank by taking advantage of London's pre-eminence in this new activity. In contrast, the strategy of acquiring a US\$ equity base was never very successful. There was a conflict between this aim and retaining a British identity. The Bank of England restricted Mellon's role in Bolsa and Citibank's investment in National and Grindlays to minority stakes so as to preserve them as British institutions. In addition, the new American shareholders were not comfortable with the British managements of the banks they had invested in. The Mellon shareholding in Bolsa and its successor disappeared in <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_53"></span>1973, and in the following year the bank was absorbed into Lloyds Bank International, wholly owned by Lloyds Bank. Chase sold its interest in Standard Chartered (as it had become) in 1975, by which time 'relations between managements had deteriorated to some degree'.<sup>61</sup> Citibank retained its shareholding in Grindlays, despite a poor financial performance and constant management problems, until 1984, when the whole bank was sold to ANZ.<sup>62</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">参与欧洲美元市场是一次成功的尝试，借助伦敦在这一新兴活动中的领先地位，克服了作为英镑银行的劣势。相比之下，获取美元股权基础的策略从未真正成功过。这一目标与保持英国身份之间存在冲突。英格兰银行限制梅隆在 Bolsa 的角色以及花旗银行在国民格里德利银行的投资为少数股权，以保全它们作为英国机构的身份。此外，新的美国股东对它们所投资银行的英国管理层感到不适。梅隆在 Bolsa 及其后继者中的持股于 1973 年消失，次年该银行被并入完全由劳埃德银行所有的劳埃德银行国际。大通银行于 1975 年出售了其在渣打银行（当时已更名）的权益，此时“管理层之间的关系已在一定程度上恶化”。 <sup>61</sup> 尽管 Grindlays 财务状况不佳，管理层问题频出，花旗银行仍一直保留着其股份，直到 1984 年将整个银行出售给澳新银行。 <sup>62</sup> </span></span></span>

The main competitive strategies pursued after 1945 focused on geographical diversification. Initially this took the form of founding new branches. The chief motives for geographical diversification were to spread political risks, and to move out of unprofitable regions into more profitable ones. Probably the most successful diversification in the 1940s was the transformation of the old Imperial Bank of Persia (which finally withdrew altogether from Iran in 1952) into the new British Bank of the Middle East (BBME), with a branch network in the oil-rich Gulf states and elsewhere in the Arab world.<sup>63</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1945 年后采取的主要竞争策略侧重于地域多元化。最初的形式是开设新的分行。地域多元化的主要动机是分散政治风险，并从亏损地区转向盈利更高的地区。20 世纪 40 年代最成功的多元化案例之一，或许是旧波斯帝国银行（最终于 1952 年彻底退出伊朗）转型为新的英国中东银行（BBME），并在石油资源丰富的海湾国家及阿拉伯世界其他地区建立了分行网络。 <sup>63</sup> </span></span></span>

Greenfield diversification strategies were, however, difficult for a number of reasons. The banking skills of the British multinational banks tended to be very region-specific, and disasters could occur in unfamiliar areas. Successful diversification required reserves of entrepreneurial talent which were not evident in many overseas banks. The cautious management of Mercantile Bank of India chose to focus their attention on India and rejected proposals to expand their branch network in Hong Kong.<sup>64</sup> Opportunities for successful greenfield expansion, at least in the developing world, progressively declined. In 1971 Grindlays isolated the 'Pacific Basin' as presenting 'the best opportunity for growth', but the bank had no representative there, and its attempts to penetrate the region (notably through the purchase of a Hong Kong bank) demonstrated the problems of such a strategy.<sup>65</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，由于多种原因，绿地多元化战略难以实施。英国跨国银行的银行业务往往具有很强的地域特定性，在陌生领域可能遭遇失败。成功的多元化需要具备创业人才储备，而许多海外银行显然缺乏这一点。印度商业银行谨慎的管理层选择专注于印度市场，拒绝了扩大香港分行网络的提议。 <sup>64</sup> 至少在发展中国家，成功的绿地扩张机会逐渐减少。1971 年，格林德莱银行认为“太平洋地区”提供了“最佳增长机遇”，但该银行在该地区并无代表机构，其打入该地区的尝试（尤其是通过收购一家香港银行）也暴露了此类战略的问题。 <sup>65</sup> </span></span></span>

In the late 1950s and the 1960s, attention switched to achieving geographical distribution through merger. The motives for the mergers were largely defensive. Standard Bank, its chairman told the Governor of the Bank of England, were 'under continued pressure to tie-up more of their resources in South Africa ... they cannot continue successfully without some tie-up or some "Big Brother" in London'<sup>66</sup>. Five years later George Bolton was emphasizing the need for mergers 'to economise on overheads and manpower' and to make the British banks more competitive against American banks: 'Their large resources and domestic earning power enable them successfully to break into established British preserves without worrying too much about profit and loss considerations'.<sup>67</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在 20 世纪 50 年代末至 60 年代，人们的关注点转向了通过合并实现地理分布。这些合并的动机在很大程度上是防御性的。标准银行（Standard Bank）的主席向英格兰银行（Bank of England）行长表示，他们“持续面临压力，需要将更多资源锁定在南非……如果没有某种联合，或者伦敦的‘老大哥’撑腰，他们就无法继续成功运营” <sup>66</sup> 。五年后，乔治·博尔顿（George Bolton）强调需要合并，“以节约间接费用和人力”，并让英国银行在面对美国银行时更具竞争力：“美国银行凭借其庞大的资源和国内盈利能力，能够成功打入英国的传统领地，而不必过分担心盈亏得失” <sup>67</sup> 。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_54"></span>The Bank of England, anxious that the small British overseas banks should not be bought up by American banks, provided a sympathetic audience for such views.<sup>68</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英格兰银行担心英国的小型海外银行会被美国银行收购，因此对这些观点持同情态度。 <sup>68</sup> </span></span></span>

It would be inappropriate to discuss here the various prolonged merger negotiations except to observe that they were very prolonged and less comprehensive than some had wished. In the mid-1960s the chairmen of both Standard Bank (Cyril Hawker) and Bolsa (Bolton) both argued the need for a comprehensive reorganization of British international banking. Hawker developed a 'Grand Design' which would bring together all the overseas banks into a 'British Overseas Bank', supported by the clearing banks and an American bank, while Bolton pursued schemes to unite Lloyds various interests and to combine them with those of Barclays.<sup>69</sup> Such wide-ranging recognition schemes were blocked by a number of factors. Banks regularly expressed suspicion of some of the geographical areas other banks operated in.<sup>70</sup> Lloyds and Barclays resisted schemes to merge their own overseas interests, while Lloyds all through the 1960s had no enthusiasm even for co-ordinating the diverse group of overseas banks in which it had large shareholdings. It is hard to resist the conclusion that a quicker and more effective reorganization of British overseas banking assets would have been desirable in the 1960s.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在此讨论各种漫长的合并谈判并不合适，只能说它们确实非常拖沓，且不如某些人所期望的那样全面。20 世纪 60 年代中期，Standard Bank（Cyril Hawker）和 Bolsa（Bolton）的董事长都认为英国国际银行业亟需全面重组。Hawker 提出了一项"宏伟计划"，旨在将所有海外银行整合为一家"英国海外银行"，由清算银行和一家美国银行提供支持；而 Bolton 则致力于将 Lloyds 的各类业务整合起来，并与 Barclays 的业务合并。 <sup>69</sup> 这些范围广泛的改革方案因多种因素而受阻。各银行通常对彼此业务运营的地理区域心存疑虑。 劳埃德银行和巴克莱银行均拒绝了合并各自海外业务的方案，而整个 20 世纪 60 年代，劳埃德银行甚至对其持有大量股份的多个海外银行集团协调合作也毫无兴趣。很难不得出这样的结论：在 20 世纪 60 年代，对英国海外银行资产进行更迅速、更有效的重组本应是可取的。</span></span></span>

It was only in the early and mid-1970s, after this prolonged struggle to spread risks in the Third World, that British multinational bankers began to recognize that over the last thirty years not only had three major economic power blocs emerged - the United States, continental Europe and Japan - but that they scarcely owned a single branch in any of them. Grindlays in 1971 recognised that it needed to become involved 'in European banking as it develops in the widening European Community'.<sup>71</sup> In 1975 Standard Chartered adopted a new plan 'to become big in the United States through the acquisition of a major banking unit, and ... to establish a strong domestic base in the UK'.<sup>72</sup> In the same period the British clearing banks entered a new era of foreign activity, as National Westminster and Midland Bank began establishing foreign branches and buying foreign banks. Lloyds and Barclays consolidated most of their foreign interests into Lloyds Bank International and Barclays Bank International respectively, although in the mid-1980s all these separate 'international' banks were completely merged into their parents, as the era of the 'global' bank dawned.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">直到 20 世纪 70 年代初中期，在第三世界经过漫长的风险分散博弈后，英国跨国银行家们才幡然醒悟：过去三十年里，不仅形成了美国、欧洲大陆和日本三大经济集团，而且他们在这些地区几乎连一家分行都没有。Grindlays 在 1971 年意识到，必须融入“随着欧洲共同体不断扩张而发展的欧洲银行业”。1975 年，Standard Chartered 制定了一项新战略，“通过收购一家大型银行机构在美国做大，并在英国建立起坚实的本土根基”。同一时期，英国清算银行也开启了海外扩张的新篇章，National Westminster 和 Midland Bank 开始设立海外分行并收购外国银行。 劳埃德银行和巴克莱银行此前分别将大部分海外业务整合至 Lloyds Bank International 和 Barclays Bank International，不过到了 80 年代中期，随着"全球银行"时代的到来，这些独立的"国际"银行均被完全并入其母公司。</span></span></span>

The efforts by British banks in the 1970s and 1980s to penetrate the United States and, in some cases, Europe represent a classic illustration of the problems likely to arise when entering <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_55"></span>markets without either a competitive advantage nor a strategy designed to achieve one. Although Standard Chartered's view that its dependence on the wholesale money markets for sterling deposits was a weakness was probably correct, its attempts to build a retail branch network in Britain in the 1970s was never likely to be a solution, while it was ill-equipped to deal with the management problems following from its acquisition of the Hodge finance company. The rejection of the bank's bid for the Royal Bank of Scotland by UK regulators in 1981 appeared to leave the bank with little European strategy at all. Standard Chartered's passionate resistance to a hostile Lloyds Bank takeover bid in 1986 ruled out an alternative solution to the bank's difficulties.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 70 至 80 年代，英国银行试图打入美国市场，部分银行还试图进军欧洲市场，这一努力经典地揭示了在既无竞争优势、又无制胜战略的情况下进入市场时可能出现的问题。尽管渣打银行认为其对英镑存款批发货币市场的依赖是一个弱点，这一看法很可能正确，但它在 70 年代试图在英国建立零售分行网络的做法，从来就不太可能成为解决方案，而且它也没有足够的能力应对收购霍奇金融公司后出现的各种管理问题。1981 年，英国监管机构否决了该银行收购苏格兰皇家银行的竞标，这似乎让渣打银行在欧洲几乎没有任何战略可言。1986 年，渣打银行对劳埃德银行敌意收购的强烈抵制，又排除了解决该行困境的另一条出路。</span></span></span>

The experiences of the British banks in the United States, especially in California, have been well-documented, Barclays and Lloyds purchased Californian banks in the early 1970s, Standard Chartered acquired Union Bank in 1979 and Midland Bank (which had begun to abandon its traditional reliance on correspondent banking in favour of direct representation abroad in the mid-1970s) purchased Crocker National in the followng year. There followed years of losses through heavy competition in retail banking, defaults on property loans, unpaid Latin American debts and poor management. By 1988 all British banking interests had been disposed to either the local bank, Wells Fargo, or to Japanese banks.<sup>73</sup> Foreign-owned institutions could prosper in California Japanese banks built up a 25 per cent share of outstanding assets in the second half of the 1980s - but only if they possessed rather clearer strategic aims and better management than that manifested by the British banks.<sup>74</sup> The East Coast presented a rather more optimistic scenario for British banking. During the 1980s NatWest USA developed as a modestly successful 'super-regional' bank in the north-east. However only Hongkong Bank, with its acquisition of control over Marine Midland Bank (of New York State) in 1980, had a real American success story to relate by 1990.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国银行在美国（尤其是加州）的遭遇早有记载：巴克莱银行和劳埃德银行在 20 世纪 70 年代初收购了加州银行，渣打银行于 1979 年收购了联合银行，而米德兰银行（这家银行在 70 年代中期已开始放弃传统的代理行模式，转而直接布局海外业务）则在次年收购了克罗克国民银行。随后几年，零售银行业务的激烈竞争、房地产贷款违约、拉丁美洲债务拖欠以及管理不善，导致连年亏损。到 1988 年，所有英国银行在加州的业务要么被卖给了当地富国银行，要么落入日本银行之手。外资机构在加州也能蓬勃发展——日本银行在 80 年代后半期便占据了未偿资产 25%的份额——但前提是它们必须拥有比英国银行更清晰的战略目标与更出色的管理水平。 <sup>74</sup> 东海岸为英国银行业展现了一幅更为乐观的景象。20 世纪 80 年代，NatWest USA 在美国东北部发展成为一家还算成功的"超级区域银行"。然而，到 1990 年为止，真正称得上拥有美国成功故事的，只有香港银行——它于 1980 年收购了 Marine Midland Bank（位于纽约州）的控制权。</span></span></span>

Conclusions<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">结论</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

During the nineteenth century the British overseas banks had taken advantage of British political and economic pre-eminence to build up a large and substantial business based on large branch networks covering Australasia, South Africa, Latin America and parts of Asia. They developed management and organizational skills which enhanced their competitive advantages.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在 19 世纪，英国海外银行借助英国政治和经济上的主导地位，以遍布澳大拉西亚、南非、拉丁美洲和亚洲部分地区的大型分行网络为基础，建立起庞大而坚实的业务。它们发展出了管理和组织技能，从而增强了自身的竞争优势。</span></span></span>

Over time many of the inherent competitive advantages gamed by being a British bank declined. The changing industry structure <span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_56"></span>called for new generic strategies to be pursued if competitive advantages were to be retained. In the interwar years, the main response to the new situation came through the forging of links with domestic banking groups and some merger activity. The links with clearing banks were successful in allowing the survival of British overseas banking interests in several areas, though opportunities for the attainment of new competitive advantages were passed. During the 1950s and 1960s the decline in the competitive advantages enjoyed by British multinational banks was more visible. There was a more flexible attitude towards, for example, product innovation, but the main response was to struggle to retain old competitive advantages, by spreading risks and erecting barriers to exit, rather than to develop new ones. In the 1960s there could have been a more rapid re-organization of British overseas banking interests, while Barclays and Lloyds (in particular) were slow to realize the potential of their extensive multinational assets. In the 1970s there was a somewhat belated, and troubled, scramble to develop new products and areas by the surviving overseas banks, notably Grindlays and Standard Chartered, while the British clearers led a disappointing foray into Californian banking. British multinational banking has been far from the unmitigated disaster of, say, the British-owned motor manufacturing Industry over the last thirty years, but the response to changing circumstances could have been more deft and vigorous. There were powerful exogeneous constraints on strategic change, but entrepreneurial factors within banks also restrained change.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">随着时间的推移，英国银行凭借其身份所获得的许多固有竞争优势逐渐减弱。若想保持竞争优势，不断变化的行业结构要求采取新的通用战略。在两次世界大战之间的时期，应对新形势的主要举措是与国内银行集团建立联系并进行了一些并购活动。尽管错失了获取新竞争优势的机遇，但与清算银行的联系成功使英国海外银行业务在多个地区得以存活。到了 20 世纪 50 年代和 60 年代，英国跨国银行所享有的竞争优势的衰退更为明显。例如，在产品创新方面的态度变得更加灵活，但主要应对措施仍然是努力通过分散风险和设置退出壁垒来维持旧的竞争优势，而非开发新的优势。 20 世纪 60 年代，英国海外银行业本可以更迅速地重组，但巴克莱银行和劳埃德银行（尤其是后者）却迟迟未能意识到其庞大的跨国资产潜力。70 年代，幸存下来的海外银行——特别是格林德利银行和渣打银行——在开发新产品和新领域时既迟缓又困难重重，而英国清算银行则在进军加州银行业的尝试中令人失望。过去三十年里，英国跨国银行业远未像英国本土汽车制造业那样一败涂地，但面对环境变化时本可以更灵活、更有力地应对。战略转型受到强大的外部因素制约，但银行内部的企业家精神因素同样阻碍了变革。</span></span></span>

An alternative approach to this question is to ask why, if the competitive advantages of British overseas banks began to decline after the First World War, did they last so long? As locally-owned banks developed these could provide all the domestic banking services offered by the British overseas banks, while also providing a basis through which trade could have been financed through correspondent relationships.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对于这个问题，另一种思考方式是：如果英国海外银行的竞争优势在第一次世界大战后就开始衰退，那它们为何还能持续那么久？随着本土银行的发展，它们能够提供英国海外银行所开展的所有国内银行服务，同时还能通过代理行关系为贸易融资提供基础。</span></span></span>

A number of factors worked to keep the overseas banks in existence. First, London never simply became the place where their boards were located and a majority of shareholders lived. The City never lost its position as a major financial centre, and the growth of the Euromarkets led to its re-birth as the world's premier financial centre. There continued to be real financial advantages to be gained from having a strong position in the City of London. Secondly, the British overseas banks were well-managed and efficient banking organizations. They also preferred survival through prudent policies to profit maximization by exces<span id="Ch03.xhtml_page_57"></span>sive risk-taking. Thirdly, by the interwar years formidable barriers to exit had been erected. During this period much of Britain's branch network in Latin America, the West Indies, and perhaps parts of Africa and the East, was unprofitable and might have closed down in a profit-maximizing world. But the Bank of England and the clearing banks were prepared to subsidize these losses, while within banking groups cross-subsidization was practised whereby the profits of some areas supported losses elsewhere.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">多种因素共同作用，使得这些海外银行得以存续。首先，伦敦从未仅仅沦为银行董事会所在地和多数股东居住的城市。金融城从未失去其作为主要金融中心的地位，而欧洲货币市场的兴起更是促使其重生为全球首屈一指的金融中心。在伦敦金融城占据强势地位，依然能够带来实实在在的金融优势。其次，英国海外银行自身管理有方、效率出众，它们更倾向于通过审慎经营来求生存，而非通过过度冒险去追求利润最大化。再者，到两次世界大战之间的年代，退出壁垒已显著形成。这一时期，英国在拉丁美洲、西印度群岛以及可能部分非洲和东方地区的分支网络大多无利可图，若在一个以利润最大化为目标的世界里，它们或许早已关闭。 但是，英格兰银行和清算银行准备补贴这些损失，而在银行集团内部则实行交叉补贴，即某些领域的利润用于弥补其他领域的亏损。</span></span></span>

The British banks were remarkable examples of multinational banking in the nineteenth century. In the twentieth century they proved durable, if not entirely flexible, banking organizations. In 1990 most of the old names have gone, as have the old bastions of Australia and South Africa. But the large number of branches and affiliates in foreign countries controlled by British banks remain a testament to the overseas banking legacy.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国银行是 19 世纪跨国银行的杰出典范。进入 20 世纪后，它们被证明是持久的银行组织，尽管并非完全灵活。到 1990 年，大多数老牌银行已不复存在，澳大利亚和南非的旧日堡垒也同样消失。但英国银行在海外控制的大量分行和附属机构，依然见证了其海外银行业务的传承。</span></span></span>
