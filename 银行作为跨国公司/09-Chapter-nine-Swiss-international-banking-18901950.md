In the course of the twentieth century, and particularly as a result of its neutrality during the two World Wars, Switzerland has emerged as one of the major financial centres of the world. Until the recent rise of Tokyo, it was usually considered to rank third after London and New York. Given the small size of the country, this position has been due to the very large extent of its international financial activities. From an early stage, Switzerland has been a major exporter of capital. According to Paul Bairoch, Switzerland had invested some 2.7 billion dollars abroad in 1913, corresponding to 700 dollars per head. Even allowing for a significant margin of error, due to the poor state of Swiss statistics in the nineteenth century, this would put Switzerland ahead of Great Britain in terms of capital exported per head at the eve of the First World War.<sup>1</sup> Both the volume of capital exports and the gap with other countries when calculated per head narrowed during the interwar period, with the total amount of foreign investments reaching 2.3 billion dollars in 1938. But after the Second World War, Switzerland was again far ahead in terms of capital exports per head which, in 1967, were 2.6 times higher than those of its immediate follower, namely Great Britain.<sup>2</sup> Although statistics are not available, it is also well known that Switzerland has been a major recipient of flows of foreign capital.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在二十世纪的发展进程中，尤其是得益于两次世界大战期间的中立地位，瑞士已崛起为全球主要金融中心之一。在东京最近崛起之前，瑞士通常被认为仅次于伦敦和纽约，位列第三。考虑到该国幅员狭小，这一地位在很大程度上归功于其极为广泛的国际金融活动。瑞士很早就成为重要的资本输出国。据保罗·拜罗克统计，1913 年瑞士海外投资总额约为 27 亿美元，相当于人均 700 美元。即便考虑到十九世纪瑞士统计数据不完善可能存在的较大误差，在第一次世界大战前夕，瑞士的人均资本输出量也已领先于英国。 <sup>1</sup> 在两次世界大战之间的时期，无论是资本出口总额还是人均计算与其他国家的差距，都出现了缩小，到 1938 年外国投资总额达到 23 亿美元。但第二次世界大战后，瑞士在人均资本出口方面再次遥遥领先，1967 年的人均资本出口量是紧随其后的英国的 2.6 倍。 <sup>2</sup> 尽管缺乏统计数据，但众所周知，瑞士一直是外国资本流入的主要接收国。</span></span></span>

To what extent did this international financial position lead to the development of Swiss banks as multinational enterprises? The answer is, perhaps surprisingly, only to a very limited extent until the 1950s. Although Switzerland has always had a great number of industrial multinational companies — again possibly the highest number per head before 1914, with some 13—18 per cent of all multinational industrial companies in the world being Swiss<sup>3</sup> — its banks did not expand very much abroad. This, combined with the scarcity of information available on Swiss banks due to bank secrecy, which remains a major constraint for Swiss historians, <span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_161"></span>could mean that the Swiss case does not present much interest in a debate about banks as multinationals. I would, however, take a different view and argue that one can learn as much from a negative as from a positive example, and Switzerland should be a particularly instructive case as a major international financial centre which does not seem to have produced many multinational banks during the period under review. In order to deal with this question, this chapter will be divided in three parts. The first part will consider some general trends in the development of international banking in Switzerland. The second part will analyse the most significant aspects of Swiss banks as multinational companies. Finally, in a third part, I shall try to draw some general conclusions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这一国际金融地位在多大程度上推动了瑞士银行发展成为跨国企业？答案或许令人意外：直到 20 世纪 50 年代，这种推动作用都非常有限。尽管瑞士一直拥有大量工业跨国公司——或许 1914 年前其人均数量仍居全球之首，全球约 13%至 18%的工业跨国公司是瑞士企业 <sup>3</sup> ——但瑞士银行的海外扩张却相当有限。加上银行保密制度导致瑞士银行相关信息匮乏（这至今仍是瑞士历史学家面临的主要限制），这意味着瑞士的案例在关于银行作为跨国企业的讨论中可能并不具有太大价值。 然而，本人持不同观点，认为从负面案例中汲取的教训与从正面案例中同样丰富。瑞士作为主要国际金融中心，在其研究期间却似乎未能催生出众多跨国银行——这一案例尤具启发意义。为深入探讨此问题，本章分为三部分：第一部分梳理瑞士国际银行业发展的总体趋势；第二部分解析瑞士银行作为跨国企业最显著的经营特征；第三部分将尝试归纳总结若干普适性结论。</span></span></span>

## I

The international activities of the Swiss banks are very difficult to evaluate in a systematic way before the 1960s.<sup>4</sup> Only since 1972 have all the banks and finance companies registered as banks given details to the Swiss National Bank about their foreign claims and liabilities.<sup>5</sup> During the interwar period, the Swiss Bankers' Association systematically refused to undertake any general inquiry among its members, whether about Swiss investments abroad or about foreign capital in Switzerland, for fear of a massive withdrawal of funds. However, the greater involvement of the Federal State in financial negotiations during the 1930s and the Second World War has resulted in the compilation of some data for this period. This material has recently been used in some studies of the economic background to Swiss diplomacy.<sup>6</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 60 年代以前，瑞士银行的国际活动很难进行系统评估。 <sup>4</sup> 直到 1972 年，所有注册为银行的金融机构才开始向瑞士国家银行详细报告其外国债权和债务。 <sup>5</sup> 两次世界大战之间，瑞士银行家协会出于对大规模资金撤离的担忧，系统性地拒绝在其成员中开展任何关于瑞士海外投资或外国在瑞士资本的普遍调查。然而，20 世纪 30 年代及二战期间，联邦政府更多地参与金融谈判，使得这一时期的部分数据得以汇编。这些资料近期已被用于一些研究瑞士外交经济背景的工作中。 <sup>6</sup> </span></span></span>

Only scattered data are available about the most famous aspect of Swiss international banking: foreign capital deposited in Switzerland. The amount must have varied considerably between the late nineteenth and the mid-twentieth century, depending on the economic and political conjuncture. At the end of 1929, G. Bachmann, head of the Swiss National Bank, estimated that foreign deposits in Switzerland amounted to some 1-1.3 billion francs,<sup>7</sup> which would represent between 5 and 7 per cent of the total deposits of the country for that year, but between 13 and 17 per cent of the deposits of the 'big banks' which were the main recipients of foreign capital.<sup>8</sup> This was a year of comparatively low foreign capital inflows, for in 1934 the Swiss National Bank estimated that German assets alone amounted to about 1 billion francs.<sup>9</sup> In 1942, some estimates put the Spanish assets in Switzerland as high as 1.5-2 billion francs.<sup>10</sup> In October 1945, French <span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_162"></span>frozen assets in Switzerland amounted to 822.5 million francs, whereas in 1947 undeclared French capital was estimated to be around 1 billion Swiss francs.<sup>11</sup> A precise estimate of the total amount of funds deposited in Switzerland during the Second World War remains, however, impossible. According to a recent study by Marc Perrenoud, they must certainly have exceeded 3 billion francs.<sup>12</sup> Some more systematic figures are available for the 1960s: in 1962, the total foreign liabilities of the Swiss banks amounted to 10.8 billion francs, which represented 17.2 per cent of their total liabilities; in 1972, they had jumped to 71.7 billion, or 32 per cent of the total liabilities of the Swiss banks.<sup>13</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">关于瑞士国际银行业最著名的一方面——存入瑞士的外国资本——仅有零散的数据可用。从 19 世纪末到 20 世纪中叶，这一数额随经济和政治形势起伏波动极大。1929 年底，瑞士国家银行行长 G. Bachmann 估计，瑞士的外国存款约为 10-13 亿法郎， <sup>7</sup> 这相当于当年瑞士全国存款总额的 5%至 7%，但占主要接收外国资本的"大银行"存款的 13%至 17%。 <sup>8</sup> 这是外国资本流入相对较低的一年，因为到 1934 年，瑞士国家银行估计仅德国资产就约达 10 亿法郎。 <sup>9</sup> 1942 年，一些估算认为西班牙在瑞士的资产高达 15-20 亿法郎。 <sup>10</sup> 1945 年 10 月，法国在瑞士的冻结资产为 8.225 亿法郎，而到 1947 年，未申报的法国资本估计约为 10 亿瑞士法郎。 <sup>11</sup> 然而，要精确估算二战期间存入瑞士的资金总额仍无从下手。根据 Marc Perrenoud 近期的一项研究，这一数字必定超过 30 亿法郎。 <sup>12</sup> 关于 20 世纪 60 年代，有更为系统的数据可供参考：1962 年，瑞士银行的外国负债总额为 108 亿法郎，占其总负债的 17.2%；到 1972 年，这一数字飙升至 717 亿法郎，占瑞士银行总负债的 32%。 <sup>13</sup> </span></span></span>

The figures related to Swiss capital exports are a first indication of the extent to which the Swiss banks used abroad the funds made available to them, even though these figures include direct foreign investments made by industrial companies. By the end of the Second World War, Swiss foreign investments had reached the considerable amount of 12.5-17.7 billion francs.<sup>14</sup> However, these figures include foreign capital attracted to Switzerland and reinvested abroad, which is in any case a significant aspect of Swiss international banking activities. In 1962, the foreign claims of the Swiss banks amounted to 9.4 billion francs, that is 15.8 per cent of their total assets; by 1972, these claims had reached 86.5 billion Swiss francs or 38.6 per cent of their total assets.<sup>15</sup> A distinction between the types of banking operations undertaken with foreign customers, such as advances, loans and overdrafts, bills of exchange, investments etc., is not possible before the 1960s. However, the statistics of the Swiss National Bank provide some details concerning the distribution between the Swiss banks' home and foreign investments.<sup>16</sup> These figures confirm the general trend of capital exports, which decreased during the interwar period before picking up again.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">瑞士资本输出的相关数据初步揭示了瑞士银行将获得的资金用于海外操作的规模，尽管这些数字包含了工业企业的直接对外投资。到第二次世界大战结束时，瑞士的海外投资已达到惊人的 125 至 177 亿法郎。 <sup>14</sup> 然而，这些数字也包括了吸引至瑞士并再投资于海外的外国资本，这无论如何都是瑞士国际银行业务的一个重要方面。1962 年，瑞士银行的外国债权为 94 亿法郎，占其总资产的 15.8%；到 1972 年，这些债权已达到 865 亿瑞士法郎，占总资产的 38.6%。 <sup>15</sup> 在 20 世纪 60 年代之前，还无法区分与外国客户进行的银行业务类型，如预付款、贷款和透支、汇票、投资等。 然而，瑞士国家银行的统计数据提供了有关瑞士银行国内与国外投资分布的一些细节。 <sup>16</sup> 这些数据证实了资本输出的总体趋势，在两次世界大战期间，这一趋势有所下降，之后又重新回升。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_163"></span>It is important to bear in mind that not all the Swiss banks were involved in the same degree in international banking. As is well known, the Swiss banking system is highly specialized and divided between the 'big banks', the cantonal banks, the local and medium-sized banks, the savings banks and finally the finance companies. Since 1945, a new category of 'other banks', which includes a high proportion of foreign-owned banks, has been introduced in the statistics of the Swiss National Bank. To be complete, one should add a further category, the private banks, which are not included in the National Bank's statistics because they do not publish their balance-sheet; some of them, however, have prospered to this day, particularly in Geneva.<sup>17</sup> Without entering here into the details of each type of bank's activities, it can be recalled that the cantonal banks were publicly owned by the various cantons. Their main customers were, in the urban areas, local businessmen, whereas in the rural areas, they were similar to mortgage banks. Although their combined total assets remained higher than those of the 'big banks' until after the Second World War, except during the 1920s, they were hardly involved in any kind of international banking activity. For their part, until the early 1960s and apart from a few wealthy customers, the 'big banks' left personal banking to the other banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">需要牢记的是，并非所有瑞士银行都同等程度地涉足国际银行业务。众所周知，瑞士银行体系高度专业化，分为"大银行"、州立银行、地方和中等规模银行、储蓄银行以及金融公司。自 1945 年以来，瑞士国家银行的统计数据中新增了一类"其他银行"，其中包含大量外资银行。若要完整列举，还应补充另一类——私人银行，它们未纳入国家银行的统计，因为这类银行不公布资产负债表；不过其中一些银行至今仍经营良好，特别是在日内瓦。 <sup>17</sup> 在此无需赘述各类银行的具体业务细节，只需指出州立银行是由各个州政府所有的。 他们的主要客户在城市地区是本地商人，而在农村地区，其业务性质类似于抵押银行。尽管除 1920 年代外，直至二战结束后，它们合计的总资产仍高于“大型银行”，但它们几乎不参与任何国际银行业务。而“大型银行”方面，直到 20 世纪 60 年代初，除了少数富裕客户外，它们将个人银行业务留给了其他银行。</span></span></span>

Three types of banks were therefore engaged in international banking: the 'big banks', the finance companies, and the private banks, for which, as has already been mentioned, there are no <span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_164"></span>available statistics. These three types of banks were also at the heart of another important international banking activity which should be mentioned here: the issues of foreign loans. Between 1924 and 1931, debentures to the value of 3.87 billion francs were issued on the Swiss financial markets, more than a quarter of which, that is 1.11 billion, being represented by foreign debentures.<sup>19</sup> This is of course quite apart from the participation of Swiss banks in international syndicates.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">因此，从事国际银行业务的银行有三类：大型银行、金融公司以及私人银行——关于后者，如前所述，并无现成统计数据可查。这三类银行还构成了另一项重要国际银行业务的核心——即外国贷款的发行业务。1924 年至 1931 年间，瑞士金融市场上发行的债券总价值达 38.7 亿法郎，其中超过四分之一（即 11.1 亿法郎）为外国债券。 <sup>19</sup> 这当然还不包括瑞士银行参与国际银团的部分。</span></span></span>

## II

Despite the extent of their international involvement, Swiss banks had very few foreign branches and subsidiary companies until the 1960s. Compared to the possible 2,000 foreign branches of British banks and the 500 of French and German banks in 1914,<sup>20</sup> Swiss banks did not have much more than three or four foreign branches in that year. Even allowing for the small size of the country, this is a very low figure. In 1943, for the first time, the Swiss National Bank introduced statistics about foreign branches of Swiss banks. There were only six: three in England, two in the United States of America — both opened in 1939 — and one in Argentina.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管瑞士银行的国际业务范围广泛，但直到 20 世纪 60 年代，它们在海外的分行和子公司仍然寥寥无几。相比之下，1914 年英国银行可能拥有 2000 家海外分行，法国和德国银行各有 500 家，而 <sup>20</sup> 瑞士银行当年仅有大约三四家海外分行。即便考虑到瑞士国土面积较小，这一数字也显得极低。1943 年，瑞士国家银行首次发布了关于瑞士银行海外分行的统计数据——当时只有六家：三家在英格兰，两家在美利坚合众国（均于 1939 年开业），以及一家在阿根廷。</span></span></span>

During the period under review, Switzerland had only one —and during five years two — overseas banks: The Schweizerisch-Argentinisch Hypothekenbank and its parent company the Schweizerisch-Südamerikanische Bank. Both banks were founded by the Crédit Suisse, of Zurich, at that time one of the two major Swiss commercial banks, the other one being the Swiss Bank Corporation. The Schweizerisch-Argentinische Hypothekenbank was founded in 1910 with a capital of 10 million francs, following a report presented to the Crédit Suisse about the promising opportunities offered by Argentina for developing this type of bank. The country needed foreign capital in order to exploit its natural resources and improve its agricultural production, and with a rate of interest exceeding 8 per cent, there was a considerable profit margin. In September 1913, the shares of the bank were introduced to the Zürich Stock Exchange at a 24 per cent premium and by the end of 1914 various loans totalling 35 million francs had been granted.<sup>21</sup> The success of the Hypothekenbank led the board of the Crédit Suisse to establish in 1912, as a parent company, a commercial bank, the Schweizerisch-Südamerikanische Bank (Banco Suizo-Sudamericano), with the aim to develop banking operations between Europe and South America and to encourage commercial relations between Switzerland and Argentina. Its nominal capital was 20 million francs, 10 million paid-up, 75 per <span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_165"></span>cent being owned by the Credit Suisse and 25 per cent by the Banque Fédérale (also of Zurich, and another of the Swiss 'big banks' although smaller than the Credit Suisse).<sup>22</sup> The Schweizerisch—Südamerikanische Bank had its headquarters in Zurich with branches in Buenos Aires and Lugano, in Switzerland. In Buenos Aires, it shared its management with the Hypothekenbank. The hopes of the Südamerikanische Bank, however, were dashed by the outbreak of the First World War and the commercial and financial crisis in Argentina, and in 1917 it was merged with the Banque Française et Italienne pour l'Amérique du Sud.<sup>23</sup> The Hypothekenbank fared better, although it had to undertake a reorganization during the depression, reducing its share capital from 10 million to 20,000 francs in 1934. After the Second World War, it gradually abandoned its mortgage business to become an investment and management company; in 1955, its name changed to Schweizerisch—Argentinische Anlagegesellschaft ADCA.<sup>24</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在审查期间，瑞士仅有一家——并在五年内增至两家——海外银行：瑞士-阿根廷抵押银行及其母公司瑞士-南美银行。这两家银行均由苏黎世的瑞士信贷创立，当时瑞士信贷是瑞士两大主要商业银行之一，另一家是瑞士银行公司。瑞士-阿根廷抵押银行成立于 1910 年，资本金为 1000 万瑞士法郎，此前向瑞士信贷提交了一份报告，称阿根廷在开发此类银行方面前景广阔。该国需要外国资本来开发自然资源、提高农业生产，且利率超过 8%，利润空间相当可观。 1913 年 9 月，该银行的股票以 24%的溢价在苏黎世证券交易所上市，截至 1914 年底，已累计发放各类贷款总额达 3500 万瑞士法郎。 <sup>21</sup> 抵押银行的成功促使瑞士信贷董事会于 1912 年成立了一家商业银行——瑞士-南美银行（Banco Suizo-Sudamericano）作为母公司，旨在拓展欧洲与南美洲之间的银行业务，并促进瑞士与阿根廷之间的商业关系。其名义资本为 2000 万瑞士法郎，实缴资本 1000 万瑞士法郎，其中瑞士信贷持股 75%，联邦银行（同样位于苏黎世，是瑞士另一家'大银行'，但规模小于瑞士信贷）持股 25%。 <sup>22</sup> 瑞士-南美银行总部设在苏黎世，在布宜诺斯艾利斯和瑞士卢加诺设有分行。在布宜诺斯艾利斯，该行与抵押银行共享管理团队。 然而，南美银行的期望因第一次世界大战的爆发以及阿根廷的商业与金融危机而化为泡影，并于 1917 年被并入法国意大利南美银行。 <sup>23</sup> 抵押银行则相对幸运，尽管在大萧条时期不得不进行重组，于 1934 年将股本从 1000 万法郎削减至 2 万法郎。第二次世界大战后，它逐步放弃了抵押贷款业务，转型为一家投资管理公司；1955 年，更名为瑞士阿根廷投资公司 ADCA。 <sup>24</sup> </span></span></span>

Before 1914, the Swiss Bank Corporation, of Basel, was the only big Swiss commercial bank with a branch in a foreign country. This was its London branch, opened in 1898. The move, originated with its chairman, JJ. Schuster-Burckhardt, intended to enable the bank to take advantage of the numerous business opportunities offered by the City of London.<sup>25</sup> It is interesting to note that by 1898, most European banks of some significance had already opened a branch in London, the first ones being the Crédit Lyonnais in 1870 and the Deutsche Bank in 1873,<sup>26</sup> within seven years and three years of their foundation respectively. The Swiss Bank Corporation, established in 1872, waited therefore 26 years before going to London. The London branch had a capital of 5 million francs — £200,000. At first associated with the London bank Blake, Boissevain & Co., it took over the latter's business in 1901. A.A.H. Boissevain became a director of the Swiss Bank Corporation in Basel while G.F. Blake became one of the managers of the London branch. The London branch was mainly active on current account operations, arbitrage in foreign currencies, Stock Exchange orders, and acceptance of bills of Exchange; it also joined several issuing and underwriting syndi-cates.<sup>27</sup> The Swiss Bankverein, as it was then known in London, soon became very active on the London capital market. It increased its stock dealings to a point that alarmed the head office in Basel, and in 1899 it contemplated the possibility of taking a £100,000 stake in the London arbitrage house S. Japhet & Co., with an option for a further equal amount, but the project was with-drawn.<sup>28</sup> Interestingly, in 1907 the financier Ernest Cassel became a sleeping partner in S. Japhet & Co. with a £200,000 interest.<sup>29</sup> In <span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_166"></span>1912, the Swiss Bank Corporation opened its West End branch in order to increase its share of the foreign exchanges business and other transactions emanating from tourism.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1914 年之前，总部位于巴塞尔的瑞士银行公司是瑞士唯一一家在海外设有分行的大型商业银行。该行的伦敦分行于 1898 年开业，这一举措由董事长 J.J. 舒斯特-布克哈特发起，旨在利用伦敦金融城提供的众多商业机会。值得一提的是，到 1898 年，大多数有一定规模的欧洲银行已在伦敦开设分行，最早的是 1870 年的里昂信贷银行和 1873 年的德意志银行，分别在其成立后的第七年和第三年便进军伦敦。而成立于 1872 年的瑞士银行公司，则等了 26 年才进入伦敦市场。该伦敦分行资本金为 500 万法郎（约合 20 万英镑）。起初，它与伦敦的布莱克、布瓦塞万公司合作，并于 1901 年接管了后者的业务。A.A.H. 布瓦塞万随后成为巴塞尔瑞士银行公司的董事，而 G.F. 布莱克成为伦敦分行的经理之一。伦敦分行主要从事活期账户业务、外汇套利、证券交易所订单和承兑汇票；它还参与了几次发行和承销银团。 <sup>27</sup> 当时在伦敦被称为“Swiss Bankverein”的这家银行，很快在伦敦资本市场上变得非常活跃。其股票交易量之大，甚至惊动了巴塞尔的总行，并于 1899 年考虑收购伦敦套利公司 S. Japhet & Co. 10 万英镑的股份，并享有再增持同等金额的选择权，但该项目后来被撤回。 <sup>28</sup> 有趣的是，1907 年，金融家 Ernest Cassel 以 20 万英镑的权益成为 S. Japhet & Co.的隐名合伙人。 <sup>29</sup> 1912 年，瑞士银行公司（Swiss Bank Corporation）开设了其西区（West End）分行，以增加在外汇业务和旅游业相关交易中的份额。</span></span></span>

In 1939, both the Credit Suisse and the Swiss Bank Corporation opened a branch in New York. The Crédit Suisse founded a subsidiary company, the Swiss American Corporation, with a capital of \$3 million, a former partner of the banking house Speyer & Co. as general manager, and the President of Nestlé America as chairman. The new agency was primarily to deal with the issuing and investment businesses; this was a natural result of the increasing financial relations between Switzerland and North America, in particular with respect to the export of Swiss capital.<sup>30</sup> The Credit Suisse had already established a subsidiary company in Amsterdam in 1922, the Effekten-Maatschappij 'Amsterdam', in order to strengthen the links between Zurich and Amsterdam, two growing international financial centres in the aftermath of the First World War. The links with the Netherlands were particularly useful to the Crédit Suisse in its issues of foreign loans, as its allied Dutch banks brought both support and new business initiatives. The Effekten-Maatschappij 'Amsterdam' was wound up in 1941.<sup>31</sup> The Swiss Bank Corporation opened its 'New York Agency' for much the same reasons as the Crédit Suisse. It banked on its London experience since 1898 to obtain similar results in New York.<sup>32</sup> Finally, in addition to these official foreign branches opened by the Swiss commercial banks, there were the 'unofficial' ones, for example the branch of the Basler Handelsbank in Paris, which opened in the mid-1920s, but without being registered with the Paris police. It was raided by the French police in October 1932 and became the centre of a scandal about tax-evasion: it was discovered that it allowed its customers to cash dividends on their coupons at the bank without paying the 20 per cent tax, and more generally helped them to avoid taxes on deposits and foreign investments.<sup>33</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1939 年，瑞士信贷银行和瑞士银行公司均在纽约开设了分行。瑞士信贷银行成立了一家子公司——Swiss American Corporation，注册资本 300 万美元，由银行机构 Speyer & Co.的前合伙人担任总经理，雀巢美国公司总裁担任董事长。这家新机构主要从事发行和投资业务，这是瑞士与北美之间金融关系日益紧密的自然结果，尤其是在瑞士资本输出方面。 <sup>30</sup> 早在 1922 年，瑞士信贷银行就在阿姆斯特丹成立了一家子公司 Effekten-Maatschappij 'Amsterdam'，旨在加强苏黎世与阿姆斯特丹这两个在第一次世界大战后日益崛起的国际金融中心之间的联系。 与荷兰的联系对瑞士信贷（Crédit Suisse）在发行外国贷款方面尤其有用，其联盟的荷兰银行既提供了支持，也带来了新的业务举措。阿姆斯特丹股票公司（Effekten-Maatschappij 'Amsterdam'）于 1941 年关闭。 <sup>31</sup> 瑞士银行公司（Swiss Bank Corporation）开设其“纽约代理处”的原因与瑞士信贷大致相同。它依托自 1898 年以来的伦敦经验，希望在纽约也能取得类似成果。 <sup>32</sup> 最后，除了瑞士商业银行开设的这些正式海外分支机构外，还存在一些“非官方”分支，例如巴塞尔商业银行（Basler Handelsbank）在巴黎的分支机构，该分支机构于 20 世纪 20 年代中期开业，但并未在巴黎警方登记。 1932 年 10 月，它遭到法国警方突击搜查，并成为一起逃税丑闻的核心：警方发现，该银行允许客户凭票据兑现股息，却未缴纳 20%的税款，而且更普遍地，它帮助客户规避存款和海外投资的税费。 <sup>33</sup> </span></span></span>

The two other categories of Swiss banks involved in international banking, the private banks and the finance companies, did not develop more into multinational companies than the commercial 'big banks'. The private banks, mostly based in Geneva —the most important of them being Pictet & Cie, Hentsch & Cie, Lombard, Odier & Cie — were part of the international network of the protestant <span class="italic">Haute banque.</span> Some of them had on occasions opened a branch abroad. For example in 1854 Henri Hentsch, the son of one of the partners in Geneva, went to California and established a banking house in San Francisco, Henri Hentsch & Cie.<sup>34</sup> Generally, however, the <span class="italic">Haute banque</span> could rely on its 'friends' in the various financial centres of the world.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">参与国际银行业务的其他两类瑞士银行——私人银行与金融公司——其跨国公司的发展程度并未超过商业银行中的"大型银行"。私人银行大多以日内瓦为基地，其中最重要的包括 Pictet & Cie、Hentsch & Cie、Lombard, Odier & Cie，这些银行均属于新教高级银行（Haute banque）的国际网络。它们中有些曾偶尔在海外开设分支机构。例如 1854 年，日内瓦某合伙人之子亨利·亨驰远赴加州，在旧金山创立了 Henri Hentsch & Cie 银行。 <sup>34</sup> 不过总体而言，高级银行更多依赖其遍布全球各金融中心的"盟友"网络。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_167"></span>Swiss finance companies have been closely involved in the financing of various industrial undertakings around the world, in particular in the electrical industry. The most important finance companies were to be found here: the Bank für elektrische Unternehmungen, known as Elektrobank, established in 1895 in Zurich; the Société franco-suisse pour l'industrie électrique, established in 1898 in Geneva; Motor, established in 1895 in Baden as a subsidiary of Brown, Boveri & Co.; or the Schweizerische Gesellschaft für elektrische Industrie, also known as Indelec, founded in Basel in 1896.<sup>36</sup> A few other major finance companies were interested in other industries, for example railways in the case of the Bank für orientalische Eisenbahnen, established in Zürich in 1890. Although most of these finance companies held a controlling stake in the industrial companies they were financing, some were organized along the principles of the investment trust, that is the spread of investments over a number of different stocks. This was true, for example, in the case of the Société financiére franco-suisse, established in Geneva in 1892, which specialized in the electrical industry but intended to spread its investments over a variety of securities; or the Société financiére suisse-américaine, established in Geneva in 1907 with the object of buying securities from all countries, but especially America.<sup>[36]</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">瑞士金融公司一直紧密参与全球各类工业项目的融资，尤其在电气行业。这里曾汇聚了最重要的金融公司：1895 年成立于苏黎世的 Bank für elektrische Unternehmungen，即 Elektrobank；1898 年成立于日内瓦的 Société franco-suisse pour l'industrie électrique；1895 年成立于巴登的 Motor，它是 Brown, Boveri & Co.的子公司；以及 1896 年成立于巴塞尔的 Schweizerische Gesellschaft für elektrische Industrie，又名 Indelec。 <sup>36</sup> 其他几家大型金融公司则专注于不同领域，例如 1890 年成立于苏黎世的 Bank für orientalische Eisenbahnen，就主攻铁路业务。 尽管这些金融公司大多持有其资助的工业公司的控股权，但也有一些是按照投资信托的原则来组织的，即把投资分散到多种不同的股票上。例如，1892 年在日内瓦成立的法瑞金融公司（Société financiére franco-suisse）就是如此，该公司专注于电气行业，但旨在将投资分散到多种证券中；再如 1907 年在日内瓦成立的瑞美金融公司（Société financiére suisse-américaine），其目标是从各国（尤其是美国）购买证券。 <sup>[36]</sup> </span></span></span>

The finance companies grew almost exclusively in the three main financial centres of the country — Zurich, Geneva and Basel —and were closely linked to the leading banks and bankers of these cities: the Crédit Suisse in Zürich, the <span class="italic">haute banque</span> in Geneva whose interests were gathered in the Union Financifére de Genéve; the Swiss Bank Corporation, and to a lesser extent the Easier Handlesbank in Basel. The most important finance companies were also closely associated with foreign banking and industrial groups. For example the Allgemeine Elektrizitäts-Gesellschaft (AEG) was the main shareholder of the Elektrobank, with the Deutsche Bank and the Crédit Suisse; Siemens had a minority stake in Indelec; while the Banque de Paris et des Pays-Bas and Schneider & Co. took part in the foundation of the Société franco-suisse pour l'industrie électrique, with the Union financiére de Genéve and the Union Bank of Switzerland.<sup>37</sup> Consequently there were several members of major foreign — in particular German —banks on the boards of these finance companies. To give but one example: Georg von Siemens, of the Deutsche Bank, was vice Chairman of the two biggest finance companies at the time of their foundation: the Bank für orientalische Eisenbahnen and the Elektrobank. After the First World War, however, Swiss capital regained a predominant position in these companies.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">金融公司几乎完全集中在瑞士三大金融中心——苏黎世、日内瓦和巴塞尔——发展，并与这些城市的顶级银行和银行家紧密关联：苏黎世的瑞士信贷银行、日内瓦的高端银行（其利益汇聚于日内瓦金融联盟）、瑞士银行公司，以及程度较低的巴塞尔瑞士东部商业银行。最重要的金融公司也与外国银行和工业集团关系密切。例如，通用电气公司（AEG）是 Elektrobank 的主要股东，德意志银行和瑞士信贷银行也参与其中；西门子在 Indelec 持有少数股权；而巴黎-荷兰银行和施耐德公司则与日内瓦金融联盟及瑞士联合银行共同参与了法瑞电力工业公司的创立。 因此，这些金融公司的董事会中有多名来自外国——尤其是德国——大型银行的成员。仅举一例：德意志银行的乔治·冯·西门子在两家最大的金融公司成立时担任副主席，即东方铁路银行和电气银行。然而，第一次世界大战后，瑞士资本重新在这些公司中占据了主导地位。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_168"></span>Although their statutes allowed them to do so, the finance companies do not appear to have opened branches or agencies abroad during this period. This was most probably due to the fact that their investment business could be done without a presence abroad and because they already had several connections through their links with major foreign banking and industrial groups. One company, however, the Société financiére pour entreprises électriques aux Etats-Unis, evolved in a different way. The company was founded in Geneva in 1910, with a capital of 7 million francs, under the chairmanship of Guillaume Pictet, one of the pioneers of Swiss investments in the American electrical industry; its vice chairman was W.P, Bon right, of the firm of the same name, in New York; C.A. Coffin, president of the General Electric Company, was also on the board, as well as a former senior engineer in that company who had settled in Geneva. There were therefore close links with the United States, and delegates from the board made regular visits, but apparently no branch or agency was opened there. In 1925, however, at an extraordinary annual general meeting, the board of directors proposed that, in order for the company to be able to pursue its activities in favourable conditions, its head office should be moved to New York. This was done through the creation of a new company, the American European Securities Corporation, which took over the assets of the existing company.<sup>38</sup> Out of a sample of 15 finance companies, this is the only example of such a development into a multinational enterprise.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管相关法规允许，但在此期间金融公司似乎并未在海外开设分支机构或代理处。这很可能是因为其投资业务无需海外机构即可开展，加之它们与大型外资银行及产业集团已有诸多联系。不过，有一家名为 Société financiére pour entreprises électriques aux Etats-Unis 的公司却另辟蹊径。该公司 1910 年成立于日内瓦，注册资本 700 万法郎，由瑞士投资美国电气工业的先驱之一纪尧姆·皮克泰担任董事长；副董事长是纽约同名公司的 W.P.邦莱特；通用电气公司总裁 C.A.科芬以及一位曾定居日内瓦的前高级工程师也位列董事会。 因此，该公司与美国有着密切的联系，董事会代表定期访问美国，但显然并未在那里设立任何分支机构或代理处。然而，在 1925 年的一次特别年度股东大会上，董事会提议，为了使公司能够在有利条件下开展业务，应将总部迁至纽约。这一举措是通过成立一家新公司——美国欧洲证券公司（American European Securities Corporation）来完成的，该公司接管了原公司的资产。 <sup>38</sup> 在 15 家金融公司的样本中，这是唯一一个发展成为跨国企业的案例。</span></span></span>

## III

The limited development of the Swiss banks as multinational companies despite their considerable foreign business is a good example of the extent to which, before the 1960s, international banking could be undertaken, even on a large scale, without having recourse to multinational banks. Despite the gaps in our knowledge of the Swiss banks' history, some general explanations can be attempted.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">瑞士银行尽管拥有大量海外业务，但其作为跨国企业的发展却相当有限，这很好地说明了在 20 世纪 60 年代之前，国际银行业务——即便是大规模开展——也未必需要依赖跨国银行。尽管我们对瑞士银行历史的了解尚存空白，但仍可尝试进行一些总体性的解释。</span></span></span>

In the first place, we must take into consideration the very nature of Switzerland as a financial centre. As a refuge for the flight of capital Swiss banks have been spared the effort of collecting foreign funds. Moreover, especially after the Second World War, foreign banks have come massively to Switzerland, their number increasing from 56 in 1947 to 167 in 1967.<sup>39</sup> In the same way, Swiss banks could use their funds abroad without the need of a presence there. Loans to foreign customers and foreign invest<span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_169"></span>ments could be done from the offices at home, the more so as these operations primarily concerned the neighbouring countries: Germany, Italy, France. When the United States became a major zone of Swiss financial interests, the two biggest banks opened, although somewhat belatedly, a branch in New York.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">首先，我们必须考虑到瑞士作为金融中心的本质。作为资本避风港，瑞士的银行无需费心吸收外国资金。此外，尤其在二战后，外国银行大批涌入瑞士，其数量从 1947 年的 56 家增至 1967 年的 167 家。 <sup>39</sup> 同样，瑞士银行无需在海外设立分支机构即可运用其资金。向外国客户发放贷款和进行海外投资都可以在瑞士国内办公室里完成，更何况这些业务主要涉及邻国：德国、意大利、法国。当美国成为瑞士金融利益的重要区域时，两家最大的银行虽然有些姗姗来迟，还是在纽约开设了分行。</span></span></span>

A foreign branch or agency was not the only way to ensure a presence in a foreign country. In many cases, a representative could be enough. Thus the Crédit Suisse had a representative in Paris from the 1890s, the Swiss Bank Corporation from the 1920s. An interest in a foreign bank could also prove useful in the absence of a formal presence through a branch. In 1890, for example, the Crédit Suisse and the Swiss Bank Corporation took together a 800,000 Mark — later to become 1,200,000 — interest in the private bank Dreyfus & Co. in Frankfurt. In 1898, the Swiss Bank Corporation took part in the foundation of the Banque Internationale de Bruxelles, in 1906 it took an important stake in the Banque Suisse et Française in Paris, and in 1907 it bought shares in the Labouchére & Oyens & Co.'s Bank in Amsterdam.<sup>40</sup> Similar moves were made in the 1920s. In 1924, for example, the Swiss Bank Corporation was among the founders of the Internationale Credit Companie in Amsterdam and in 1929 it took an interest in the Bank of Manhattan Co. to strengthen its relationships with the United States.<sup>41</sup> The only necessary presence abroad was in an international financial centre: London first, then New York, and this step was taken by the biggest Swiss banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在国外设立分支机构或代理处并非确保在当地存在的唯一方式。在许多情况下，派驻一名代表便已足够。因此，瑞士信贷银行自 19 世纪 90 年代起就在巴黎设有代表，而瑞士银行公司则从 20 世纪 20 年代开始这样做。若未通过分行正式设立机构，参股一家外国银行也不失为一种有效方式。例如，1890 年，瑞士信贷银行与瑞士银行公司共同参股了法兰克福的私人银行德雷福斯公司，持股金额为 80 万马克——后来增至 120 万马克。1898 年，瑞士银行公司参与了布鲁塞尔国际银行的创立；1906 年，它购入了巴黎的瑞士与法国银行的重要股份；1907 年，它又收购了阿姆斯特丹的拉布什尔与奥延斯银行公司的股份。 <sup>40</sup> 20 世纪 20 年代也采取了类似的举措。 例如，1924 年，瑞士银行公司（Swiss Bank Corporation）作为创始方之一，在阿姆斯特丹共同成立了国际信贷公司（Internationale Credit Companie）；1929 年，它又入股曼哈顿银行公司（Bank of Manhattan Co.），以加强与美国的业务联系。 <sup>41</sup> 当时，瑞士大型银行认为，只需在伦敦这一国际金融中心设立海外机构足矣，随后纽约也成为必要据点。</span></span></span>

Did, however, the behaviour of the Swiss commercial banks fundamentally differ from that of their European counterparts? More comparative analysis will be necessary in order to provide a complete answer to this question. But it appears that, contrary to what took place after the Second World War, the wave of formation of multinational banks in the late nineteenth and early twentieth century was accomplished through the establishment of special overseas banks rather than through the opening of branches in foreign countries by the commercial banks. This was certainly true of England, where the clearing banks had hardly opened any branch abroad before the First World War.<sup>42</sup> The 1,300 or so foreign branches of British banks in 1913<sup>43</sup> were almost exclusively branches of overseas banks. Although comparable data are not available for France and Germany, the situation was not very different, apart from the fact that French and German banks had to come to London whereas the British banks did not need to go to Paris or Berlin. Even though the Crédit Lyonnais became from a very early stage a multinational bank, with branches in London, Geneva, St Petersburg, Constantinople <span id="Ch09.xhtml_page_170"></span>and Alexandria before 1880.<sup>44</sup> French overseas banking was mostly undertaken by the <span class="italic">banques d'affaires</span> and their subsidiaries and by the overseas banks. German overseas banking was also undertaken by overseas banks. These were founded by the big banks,<sup>45</sup> but they were separate, specially established for that purpose, and should not be considered as a development of the German big banks into multinational companies. This is confirmed by the fact that the overseas banks were usually founded by a consortium of several German banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，瑞士商业银行的行为是否与欧洲同行存在根本差异？要完整回答这个问题，还需进行更多比较分析。但似乎与二战后情况不同，19 世纪末 20 世纪初的跨国银行浪潮是通过设立专门的海外银行来实现的，而非商业银行直接开设海外分行。英国的情况尤其如此——一战前，其清算银行几乎未在境外开设任何分行。 <sup>42</sup> 1913 年英国银行约 1300 家海外分行 <sup>43</sup> 几乎全部隶属于海外银行。 尽管法国和德国没有可比的详细数据，但情况大同小异，唯一不同在于，法国和德国的银行需要前往伦敦开展业务，而英国银行则无需奔赴巴黎或柏林。不过，里昂信贷银行（Crédit Lyonnais）很早就成为跨国银行，在 1880 年前就已将分支机构设于伦敦、日内瓦、圣彼得堡、君士坦丁堡和亚历山大港。 <sup>44</sup> 法国的海外银行业务主要由实业银行（banques d'affaires）及其子公司以及海外银行承担。德国的海外银行业务也由海外银行负责。这些海外银行由大型银行创立， <sup>45</sup> 但它们是独立的、专为此目的而设立，不应被视为德国大型银行向跨国公司发展的标志。这一点从海外银行通常由多家德国银行组成的财团共同创立这一事实中得到了印证。</span></span></span>

Switzerland therefore mainly differed from its bigger European neighbours — a comparison with countries of a similar size remains for the moment impossible<sup>46</sup> — in that it hardly possessed any overseas banks. The reason for this difference must be found in the general context of Switzerland's position in the world. Without entering here into a discussion of the problem, it can be stated that the emergence and development of the overseas banks coincided with the assertion of the imperialist policies of European powers, Switzerland's general policy in that respect, recently described by a Swiss historian as a 'colonialisme oblique',<sup>47</sup> favoured an indirect participation in world financial affairs rather than a direct presence.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">瑞士与欧洲较大的邻国的主要不同之处在于——目前尚无法与类似规模的国家进行比较 <sup>46</sup> ——它几乎没有任何海外银行。这种差异的原因必须从瑞士在世界上的总体地位来理解。在此不深入讨论该问题，但可以说，海外银行的兴起与发展与欧洲列强帝国主义政策的推行同步发生，而瑞士在那方面的总体政策——最近被一位瑞士历史学家描述为“间接殖民主义” <sup>47</sup> ——更倾向于间接参与世界金融事务，而非直接介入。</span></span></span>
