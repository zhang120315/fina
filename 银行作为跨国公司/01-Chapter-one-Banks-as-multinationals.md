## Multinational banking and international business

This book examines why banks of one country own banking facilities in other countries. Different writers have given this phenomenon different names, such as transnational banking, international banking and multinational banking. In this volume, the term multinational banking is used, and a sharp distinction drawn between this and international banking. <span class="italic">International banking</span> includes foreign trade finance and lending to corporations and governments resident in foreign countries. A bank may own a branch in the foreign country in question, but cross-border lending and trade finance can be - and often is - conducted without such facilities. Explanations of international banking need to draw on theories of international financial intermediation. <span class="italic">Multinational banks</span> own and control branches and affiliates in more than one country. They often perform international banking services, but the essential question is <span class="italic">why</span> such banks move across borders.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本书探讨了为何一国的银行会在其他国家拥有银行设施。不同学者对这一现象给出了不同的名称，例如跨国银行、国际银行和多国银行。在本卷中，采用“多国银行”这一术语，并严格区分其与国际银行的不同。国际银行包括对外贸易融资以及向外国企业和政府提供贷款。一家银行可能在相关外国设有分行，但跨境贷款和贸易融资通常也可以在没有此类设施的情况下进行。对国际银行的解释需要借助国际金融中介理论。多国银行在多个国家拥有并控制着分支机构和附属机构。它们通常提供国际银行服务，但关键问题是这些银行为何要跨越国界。</span></span></span>

The search for an explanation of multinational manufacturing has generated a large literature from the 1960s. In contrast, the subject of multinational banking languished until the publication in 1977 of Herbert G. Grubel's 'first attempt to develop a general theory of multinational banking'.<sup>2</sup> Subsequent literature has clarified many conceptual and empirical issues. John H. Dunning's 'eclectic model' of international production has been found useful as an explanatory tool by several writers, with particular attention being devoted to the sources of advantage of a foreign bank in a host economy.<sup>3</sup> Product differentiation, economies of scale, imperfections in markets for information and technology and government support have been discussed in this context, while Robert Z. Aliber has stressed the importance of differences between countries in the cost of capital.<sup>4</sup> Parallel with this work, the business strategy literature has argued that banks often become <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_2"></span>multinational by following their corporate clients over national borders.<sup>5</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">自 20 世纪 60 年代以来，关于跨国制造业成因的研究文献已大量涌现。相比之下，跨国银行这一课题却长期沉寂，直至 1977 年赫伯特·G·格鲁贝尔发表了"首次构建跨国银行一般理论的开创性尝试"。 <sup>2</sup> 此后涌现的文献厘清了许多理论和实证问题。多位学者发现约翰·H·邓宁的国际生产"折衷范式"颇具解释力，尤其聚焦于外资银行在东道国经济中的优势来源。 <sup>3</sup> 产品差异化、规模经济、信息与技术市场的不完全性以及政府支持成为此语境下的讨论热点，而罗伯特·Z·阿利伯则强调各国资本成本差异的关键作用。 <sup>4</sup> 与这项工作并行，商业战略文献指出，银行往往通过追随其企业客户跨越国界而成为跨国银行。 <sup>5</sup> </span></span></span>

Recent writers on international business have emphasized the need for interdisciplinary approaches to the subject, and there has been a renewed interest in the potential of business history in this respect.<sup>6</sup> This trend has coincided with a shift of opinion in some leading American business schools towards incorporating historical approaches in their teaching and research programmes.<sup>7</sup> This book, in line with this growing conviction about the value of historical analysis in understanding business behaviour, approaches the subject of multinational banking through the experience over time of countries and institutions. The empirical evidence presented here can be used to 'test' the explanatory models of economists and business strategists, but it also has an importance of its own. Institutional developments, such as the emergence of multinational banks, have to be set firmly in their contexts of time and space.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">近年来，国际商业领域的学者们纷纷强调，研究这一课题需要跨学科的方法，而商业史在此方面的潜力也重新引起了人们的关注。 <sup>6</sup> 这一趋势恰逢美国一些顶尖商学院观念转变，开始将历史视角融入其教学与研究项目之中。 <sup>7</sup> 本书正是顺应了这种日益形成的共识——历史分析对于理解商业行为具有重要价值，通过梳理不同国家和机构在跨国银行业务方面的长期经验来展开探讨。文中呈现的实证数据，既可用来“检验”经济学家和商业战略家的解释性模型，其本身也具有重要意义。诸如跨国银行兴起之类的制度发展，必须牢牢置于其特定的时间和空间背景中加以理解。</span></span></span>

The historical pattern<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">历史模式</span></span></span>

Multinational banking has a long pedigree. Italian bankers of the later Middle Ages sometimes established branches in foreign countries to assist their cross-border lending and trade finance activities. Bankers often sent members of their family or other staff to represent them abroad temporarily, or developed reciprocal agreements with their equivalents in foreign countries.<sup>8</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">跨国银行业历史悠久。中世纪后期的意大利银行家有时会在国外设立分行，以支持其跨境贷款和贸易融资活动。银行家们常常派遣家族成员或其他员工临时驻外代表，或与国外同行建立互惠协议。 <sup>8</sup> </span></span></span>

Modern corporate multinational banking, the primary focus of this book, has occurred in two waves. The first wave came in the nineteenth century. The earliest and most extensive examples were the British overseas banks, which began to appear in the 1830s and which are discussed in the chapters by Jones, King and Merrett in this volume. These institutions were headquartered in Britain but did not conduct domestic banking in that country, and initially had no shareholding links with domestic British banks. By 1914 they had large branch networks in parts of the British Empire, notably Australasia and southern Africa, and they were also influential elsewhere, especially Latin America.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">现代企业跨国银行，作为本书的核心主题，经历了两次发展浪潮。第一次浪潮出现在 19 世纪。最早且最具规模的例子是英国海外银行，它们自 19 世纪 30 年代开始涌现，本书中琼斯、金和梅雷特撰写的章节对此进行了论述。这些机构总部设在英国，但并未在该国开展国内银行业务，且最初与英国本土银行没有股权关联。到 1914 年，它们已在英帝国部分地区（尤其是澳大拉西亚和南部非洲）建立起庞大的分行网络，并在其他地区（特别是拉丁美洲）拥有重要影响力。</span></span></span>

Later in the nineteenth century the British overseas banks were joined by corporate banks from other countries. These usually had more limited branch networks and were generally linked to domestic banking institutions in their home economies. As Hertner observes, German multinational banking was essentially the creation of domestic German banks, which opened their own branches in London and established subsidiaries to operate in <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_3"></span>Latin America, Asia and elsewhere. Nineteenth-century French multinational banking followed a similar model. The Banque de l'lndochine, the most vigorous French overseas bank, was founded in 1875 by a domestic bank.<sup>9</sup> In the decade before the First World War, French domestic banks also took substantial equity stakes in leading Russian banks.<sup>10</sup> In Belgium, the largest domestic bank —Societe Generate - acquired a French bank in 1890, and in the 1900s founded a number of overseas banks, notably the Banque Sino-Belge in 1902 (to operate in China) and the Banque Italo-Belge in 1911 (to operate in South America).<sup>11</sup> The same pattern is discernible, as Cassis shows, in Swiss banking, although the Swiss showed little interest in multinational banking. The first Japanese multinational bank, the Yokohama Specie Bank, was a product not of domestic banks but of the Japanese government itself. One country which had few multinational banking interests in the nineteenth century was the United States, whose banks had, as Wilkins notes, only a tiny handful of foreign branches.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪后期，英国海外银行的行列中加入了来自其他国家的企业银行。这些银行的海外分支机构网络通常较为有限，且大多与母国经济体内的本土银行机构相关联。正如 Hertner 所指出的，德国跨国银行业务本质上是由德国本土银行创立的——这些银行在伦敦开设自己的分行，并在拉丁美洲、亚洲等地设立子公司开展业务。19 世纪的法国跨国银行业也遵循了类似模式。Banque de l'lndochine（东方汇理银行）作为当时最具活力的法国海外银行，于 1875 年由一家本土银行创立。 <sup>9</sup> 在第一次世界大战前的十年间，法国的本土银行还大量购入了俄罗斯主要银行的股权。 <sup>10</sup> 在比利时，国内最大的银行——Societe Generate——于 1890 年收购了一家法国银行，随后在 20 世纪初又设立了多家海外银行，其中最著名的当属 1902 年成立、专攻中国业务的华比银行（Banque Sino-Belge），以及 1911 年成立、布局南美的义比银行（Banque Italo-Belge）。 <sup>11</sup> 正如 Cassis 指出的那样，瑞士银行业也呈现出相似的发展模式，不过瑞士人对跨国银行业务兴趣寥寥。日本首家跨国银行——横滨正金银行（Yokohama Specie Bank）——并非由国内银行催生，而是日本政府一手推动的产物。19 世纪在全球跨国银行业务中涉足甚少的国家当属美国，据 Wilkins 观察，当时美国银行在海外设立的分支机构寥寥无几。</span></span></span>

A number of features of nineteenth-century multinational corporate banks deserve emphasis. Their branches were nverwhelmingly concentrated on developing economies. Brown's study illustrates, however, that developing economies were not simply virgin territories. In southeast Asia in the nineteenth century, powerful overseas Chinese business houses conducted international banking, sometimes through multinational branching, which co-existed alongside the Exchange Banks discussed by King. Banks also established branches in international financial centres, notably London, but foreign penetration of the domestic banking systems of Britain, continental Europe or (with the few exceptions noted by Wilkins) the United States was virtually non-existent. Secondly, the kind of banking services offered by the multinational banks abroad were diverse. Frequently domestic retail banking, trade finance, and investment banking were performed by the same institutions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪跨国企业银行的一些特点值得强调。其分支机构绝大多数集中于发展中经济体。然而，布朗的研究表明，发展中经济体并非简单的未开垦之地。在 19 世纪的东南亚，强大的海外华人商号通过跨国分支开展国际银行业务，与金所讨论的 Exchange Banks 并存。银行也在国际金融中心（尤其是伦敦）设立分支机构，但外资对英国、欧洲大陆或（除威尔金斯指出的少数例外情况外）美国国内银行体系的渗透几乎不存在。其次，跨国银行在国外提供的银行服务种类多样。通常，同一家机构同时从事国内零售银行业务、贸易融资和投资银行业务。</span></span></span>

Nineteenth-century multinational corporate banks operated in a world of diverse institutional and contractual arrangements. They existed alongside many private banks, whose international activities are less easily categorized as falling into distinct 'waves'. From the time of the medieval Italian banks, private bankers had undertaken international banking, sometimes establishing branches or other forms of representation in foreign countries. This practice continued in the nineteenth century. Merchant banks and private bankers handled much of the trade and capital movements between Europe and the United States largely through correspondent networks, partnerships and cross-holdings.<sup>12</sup> This book <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_4"></span>does not attempt to explore this complex world in detail, but McKay's chapter on the Rothschilds provides a valuable case study of the growth and business strategy of one major group, while Wilkins discusses the activities of British private banks such as Barings in the United States in the nineteenth century. Multinational corporate banking was one of a variety of ways to conduct banking over borders, and the challenge is to discover why and in which circumstances this mode was adopted in preference to others.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪的跨国企业银行运作于一个制度与合约安排多元化的世界中。它们与众多私人银行并存，而私人银行的国际活动则较难以清晰的“浪潮”来归类。自中世纪意大利银行时代起，私人银行家便已涉足国际银行业务，有时会在国外设立分支机构或其他形式的代表机构。这一做法在 19 世纪得以延续。商人银行和私人银行家主要通过代理行网络、合伙关系及交叉持股，处理了欧洲与美国之间大部分的贸易和资本流动。 <sup>12</sup> 这本书并未试图详尽剖析这个复杂的世界，但麦凯（McKay）关于罗斯柴尔德家族（Rothschilds）的章节，为一个大集团的成长与商业策略提供了宝贵的案例研究，而威尔金斯（Wilkins）则探讨了 19 世纪诸如巴林银行（Barings）等英国私人银行在美国的活动。跨国企业银行业务只是跨境银行多种方式中的一种，而挑战在于揭示为何以及在何种情境下，这种模式会被优先采用。</span></span></span>

Multinational banking in the nineteenth century also spilled over into multinational investment in other sectors. Again this important topic could not be discussed in great detail in this volume, but several chapters touch on it, if only tangentially. In the nineteenth-century Chinese business houses discussed by Brown, banking and other trading and commercial activities were closely integrated. Wilkins observes Barings' direct investment in land development in Maine in the 1790s. Gay describes how, one hundred years later, the Imperial Ottoman Bank was nearly ruined by acquiring a large portfolio of South African mining shares. The continental European practice of mixed banking often led to a combination of banking and non-banking multinational activities. Before 1914, for example, French banks became closely involved in Russian industry via their Russian banking affiliates. Deutsche Bank combined multinational banking with foreign direct investment in oil production and distribution. In the 1900s Banque d'Outremer of Belgium owned and managed considerable mining, natural resource and paper interests in Canada. This investment had been abandoned by 1923, but other continental European mixed banks continued to make diversified foreign direct investments in the interwar years. In the 1920s, for example, Banque de l'Union Parisienne invested in various banks in central Europe, but was also involved in direct investment in Czechoslovakian heavy industry in alliance with the French armaments company, Schneider.<sup>13</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪的跨国银行业务也扩展到了其他领域的跨国投资。尽管这一重要课题无法在本卷中详尽探讨，但有几个章节或多或少地涉及了它。在布朗所讨论的 19 世纪中国商行中，银行业与其他贸易及商业活动紧密交织。威尔金斯提到，巴林银行在 18 世纪 90 年代曾直接投资于缅因州的土地开发。盖伊则描述了大约一百年后，奥斯曼帝国银行因大量购入南非矿业股份而险些破产的情形。欧洲大陆常见的混业经营模式，往往导致银行业与非银行业的跨国活动相互结合。例如，1914 年之前，法国银行通过其在俄国的附属机构深度参与了俄罗斯工业。德意志银行则将跨国银行业务与石油生产和分销领域的对外直接投资融为一体。 20 世纪初，比利时海外银行（Banque d'Outremer）在加拿大拥有并管理着大量矿业、自然资源和造纸业权益。到 1923 年，这些投资已被放弃，但其他欧洲大陆混合银行在两次世界大战期间继续从事多元化的外国直接投资。例如，20 世纪 20 年代，巴黎联合银行（Banque de l'Union Parisienne）不仅投资了中欧多家银行，还与法国军火公司施耐德（Schneider）联手，直接参与捷克斯洛伐克重工业的投资。 <sup>13</sup> </span></span></span>

The multinational corporate banks established in the nineteenth century proved very durable institutions. Britain's continued role as the world's leading multinational banker (at least in terms of numbers of branches) owes much to this historical legacy. As [Chapter 3](#Ch03.xhtml_ht0026) shows, the British overseas banks engaged in extensive multinational branching after the Second World War, adding to their facilities in South Africa and Australasia, but also expanding rapidly in the developing economies of Black Africa and, later, the Middle East. Jones examines the shifting business strategies of the British overseas banks as environmental changes led to the decline <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_5"></span>of their original competitive advantages, while Merrett examines their continued role - and eventual demise in the 1970s - in Australia,<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪建立的跨国企业银行被证明是非常持久的机构。英国持续作为世界领先的跨国银行（至少就分行数量而言）的角色，在很大程度上归功于这一历史遗产。如第三章所示，二战后英国海外银行广泛开展跨国分行布局，在加强南非和澳大拉西亚业务的同时，也在黑非洲发展中国家迅速扩张，随后扩展至中东。琼斯考察了当环境变化导致其原有竞争优势衰退时，英国海外银行不断调整的业务策略；而梅雷特则研究了它们在澳大利亚的持续角色——以及 1970 年代的最终消亡。</span></span></span>

The 1960s produced a second wave of multinational corporate banking. As Huertas notes, there was a dramatic expansion in the overseas branches of American commercial banks. At the same time American investment banks abandoned their previous reliance on agency relationships and began making foreign direct investments, a process discussed in Quinn's chapter. American institutions led this second surge of multinational banking, but were not alone. By the 1970s, as Jones notes, large British domestic banks which had previously had none or very few overseas branches, such as Midland Bank and National Westminster, acquired banks in the United States and opened branches elsewhere. From a low base in the 1960s, Japanese banks also expanded overseas. By the mid-1980s Japanese institutions had over 200 branches abroad, were represented in all leading financial centres, and had built up through acquisition a significant share of the Californian banking market.<sup>14</sup> As part of this trend, ANZ was transformed in the 1970s from the last of the British overseas banks in that country into an Australian-owned and headquartered multinational bank.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1960 年代掀起了跨国银行企业的第二波浪潮。正如休尔塔斯所指出的，美国商业银行的海外分支机构出现了急剧扩张。与此同时，美国投资银行摒弃了以往依赖代理关系的模式，开始进行外国直接投资——这一过程在奎因撰写的章节中有所论述。美国机构引领了这第二波跨国银行扩张，但并非独领风骚。到 1970 年代，正如琼斯所言，此前几乎没有或仅有极少海外分支机构的大型英国本土银行，如米兰银行和国民威斯敏斯特银行，纷纷收购美国银行并在其他地区开设分行。日本银行也从 1960 年代的较低基础上开始了海外扩张。到 1980 年代中期，日本机构已在海外拥有超过 200 家分支机构，业务覆盖所有主要金融中心，并通过收购在加州银行市场占据了相当可观的份额。 作为这一趋势的一部分，ANZ 在 20 世纪 70 年代从该国最后一家英国海外银行转型为一家由澳大利亚拥有并总部设在澳大利亚的跨国银行。</span></span></span>

These 'second wave' multinational banks varied from their nineteenth-century predecessors in their geographical location and products. Like the first multinational banks, they established branches in international financial centres. The birth of the Eurodollar market in the late 1950s enhanced this trend and made London - its home - an even more attractive location for banks. New York had a key importance as a money centre - every bank with any pretension of being a multinational had some form of representation there. In the 1970s the rise of offshore centres and the growth of the Asian Dollar Market led to Bahrain, Singapore and Hong Kong attracting many branches. Beyond these financial centres, multinational banks were far more concerned with developed economies than their predecessors had been. While American banks were active in western Europe, European and Japanese banks invested in a direct and unprecedented manner in the United States. In the 1980s Canada and Australia also became attractive locations for branches when regulatory controls were liberalized to allow them. However, some American banks before the world debt crisis - did also build large branch networks in several Latin American countries, particularly Brazil, Mexico, Venezuela and Argentina, providing some continuity with the nineteenth-century branching pattern.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些“第二次浪潮”的跨国银行，无论是在地理位置还是业务产品上，都与 19 世纪的前辈们截然不同。和第一批跨国银行一样，它们也在国际金融中心设立了分行。20 世纪 50 年代末欧洲美元市场（Eurodollar market）的诞生进一步推动了这一趋势，使其所在地伦敦对银行而言更具吸引力。纽约作为货币中心具有举足轻重的地位——任何自诩为跨国银行的机构，都在那里设有某种形式的代表处。到了 20 世纪 70 年代，离岸中心的兴起以及亚洲美元市场（Asian Dollar Market）的发展，使得巴林、新加坡和香港吸引了大量分行。在这些金融中心之外，跨国银行对发达经济体的关注程度远超前辈。美国银行在西欧活跃布局，而欧洲和日本的银行则以前所未有的直接方式大举投资美国。 20 世纪 80 年代，随着监管管制的放宽,加拿大和澳大利亚也成为设立分支机构的理想地点。 然而,在全球债务危机之前,一些美国银行确实在多个拉丁美洲国家建立了大型分行网络,特别是在巴西、墨西哥、委内瑞拉和阿根廷。这与 19 世纪的分支模式形成了某种连续性。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_6"></span>Initially the products offered by the multinational banks of the 1960s were not too dissimilar from the nineteenth-century banks. Trade finance, servicing corporate customers from their home country, and retail banking were all found. The large domestic branch networks built up by American banks such as Chase and Citibank were similar to those in Australia and South Africa established by the nineteenth-century British overseas banks. Perhaps the use of the Eurodollar, and later Eurobond, markets was the main distinguishing feature of multinational banking from earlier periods. From the mid-1980s, however, as Huertas notes, American banks except Citicorp pulled out of foreign host country domestic retail banking. There were similar trends among the multinational banks of other countries. By 1990 there was a widespread conviction that a foreign bank was unwise to pursue a retail banking strategy except through the acquisition of a local bank, as with Hongkong's Bank's purchase of Marine Midland discussed in the chapter by King, although one favoured alternative strategy —participation in investment banking - proved a hazardous option for many commercial banks.<sup>15</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 60 年代，跨国银行最初提供的产品与 19 世纪的银行相比并无太大差异。贸易融资、为母国企业客户提供服务以及零售银行业务，这些都是当时常见的业务。美国大通银行和花旗银行等银行建立的大型国内分行网络，与 19 世纪英国海外银行在澳大利亚和南非建立的分行网络颇为相似。或许，利用欧洲美元市场（以及后来的欧洲债券市场）是跨国银行业务区别于早期时期的主要特征。然而，正如韦尔塔斯所指出的，自 20 世纪 80 年代中期起，除花旗集团外，美国银行纷纷撤出外国东道国的国内零售银行业务。其他国家的跨国银行也出现了类似趋势。 到 1990 年，人们普遍认为，外资银行除非通过收购一家本地银行来开展零售银行业务，否则是不明智的——正如金（King）在章节中讨论的汇丰银行收购 Marine Midland 一案所示。不过，另一种一度备受青睐的策略——参与投资银行业务——对许多商业银行而言最终被证明是风险极高的选择。 <sup>15</sup> </span></span></span>

Explaining multinational banking<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">跨国银行的解析</span></span></span>
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Explanations of multinational banking need to begin with the recognition that it has always been only one of a number of institutional and contractual arrangements that were possible. Correspondent or independent agent-type relationships provided profitable means of engaging in international banking without the risks often associated with owning branches abroad. This was usually the chosen strategy of nineteenth-century private banks, and in the main American commercial banks up to the 1960s.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">要解析跨国银行现象，首先需要认识到，它向来只是众多可能存在的制度与合约安排之一。代理行或独立代理人类关系，为开展国际银行业务提供了有利可图的途径，同时又避免了通常与拥有海外分行相伴的风险。这通常是 19 世纪私人银行所选择的策略，而且直到 20 世纪 60 年代，它也是美国商业银行的主要策略。</span></span></span>

Why, then, did banks branch over borders? As the essays in this volume demonstrate, nineteenth-century multinational banks were the product of a number of factors - entrepreneurial perceptions of profitable opportunities in conditions of expanding territories and imperial frontiers, a desire to apply domestic banking skills in foreign markets, and the wishes of politicians for banks, as in part in the cases of the Yokohama Specie Bank and the Imperial Ottoman Bank.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">那么，银行为何要跨境扩张？正如本文集中的文章所示，19 世纪的跨国银行是多种因素共同作用的产物——企业家在领土扩张和帝国边界拓展的背景下洞察到盈利机会，渴望将国内银行业技能应用于海外市场，以及政治人物对银行的需求，横滨正金银行和奥斯曼帝国银行便是部分例证。</span></span></span>

Banks established for such reasons located their branches where they perceived a sufficient 'advantage' over local (or other) institutions to enable a profitable business to exist. Government regulations and the strength of indigenous banks gave multinational banks no advantage - or even the possibility of extensive branching - in the United States, Japan or most of Europe in the <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_7"></span>nineteenth century. Trade and capital flows between the regions were, as a result, generally financed and arranged through looser arrangements. Conversely, high receptivity towards foreign institutions and unstable or weak indigenous banking created opportunities for multinational banking in Australasia, South Africa and Latin America. At the same time there were, especially when the first multinational banks were established, fewer opportunities to find trusted correspondents in these regions. Ties of language, culture and empire created further advantages - or not, as the case might be.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">出于这些原因而成立的银行，会将分支机构设在其认为比本地（或其他）机构更具“优势”从而能盈利的地方。19 世纪，在美国、日本和欧洲大部分地区，政府监管与本土银行的强势使得跨国银行毫无优势可言，甚至连大规模开设分支机构都几乎不可能。因此，区域间的贸易和资本流动通常只得通过较为松散的方式来进行融资和安排。相反，在澳大拉西亚、南非和拉丁美洲，对外国机构的高度接纳以及本土银行业的薄弱或不稳定，反倒为跨国银行创造了机遇。同时，尤其是在首批跨国银行成立时，在这些地区找到值得信赖的代理行的机会也相对较少。语言、文化和帝国纽带或能进一步带来优势——但情况也并非总是如此。</span></span></span>

As Casson argues in [Chapter two](#Ch02.xhtml_ht0014), internalization theory provides the most helpful tool for explaining, not the idiosyncratic factors which led individual institutions to be founded, but why in general these institutions survived and were preferred over time to alternative arrangements. Long-run survival was dictated by systematic factors - such as, whether it paid to internalize. However, because banks - like other businesses - rarely pursue pure profit-maximizing strategies in the 'real world', other factors worked to influence the choice of institutional and contractual arrangements. Governments were one important influence in the late nineteenth century. The Japanese government wanted a Japanese multinational bank and was prepared to subsidize its operation. Barriers to exit were erected by central banks concerned with overall financial stability. Once established, the behaviour of large banking organizations was influenced by the aims of managers and pressures within the institution. As a series of behavioural and managerial models of the firm have stressed, the result was a more complex mix of motives than maximizing returns to shareholders.<sup>16</sup> The managers of the British overseas banks had, perhaps as their first priority, the long-run survival of their corporations, a task they performed well.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正如卡森在第二章中所述，内部化理论为我们提供了最具启发性的分析工具，其价值不在于解释促使个别机构创立的特殊因素，而在于阐明为何这些机构总体上能够存续，并在长期发展中被视为优于其他替代安排。长期存续取决于系统性因素——例如，内部化是否能够带来收益。然而，由于银行与其他企业类似，在"现实世界"中很少完全遵循利润最大化的策略，其他因素也会影响制度安排和合约形式的选择。在 19 世纪末，政府就是重要的影响因素之一。日本政府希望拥有一家跨国银行，并愿意为其运营提供补贴。而中央银行出于维护整体金融稳定的考虑，设置了退出壁垒。 一旦确立，大型银行机构的行为便会受到管理者目标及机构内部压力的影响。正如一系列企业行为与管理模型所强调的那样，其结果便是形成了比最大化股东回报更为复杂的动机组合。 <sup>16</sup> 英国海外银行的管理者或许将企业的长期生存视为首要任务，而他们在这方面做得相当出色。</span></span></span>

Huertas and Quinn highlight the value of internalization theory in explaining the second wave of multinational banking from the 1960s. Changes in technology and a wave of financial innovation changed the transactions cost structure of the industry and provided incentives to internalize business rather than conduct it though market relationships. In investment banking, the advent of the brand name - and its associated guarantee of quality - as the most valuable asset of a firm led to sustained pressure for internalization. The old British overseas banks, the survivors of the first generation of multinational banks, made attempts to emulate these trends, by product and geographical diversification, but economic logic more often pushed them in the opposite direction, towards transfer of domicile or disinvestment (as in Australia and South <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_8"></span>Africa respectively in the 1970s and 1980s), or the creation of groups, consisting of large numbers of partly-owned or associated houses in developing countries.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">Huertas 和 Quinn 强调了内部化理论在解释 20 世纪 60 年代起第二波跨国银行浪潮中的价值。技术变革与一波金融创新改变了该行业的交易成本结构，并激励企业将业务内部化，而非通过市场关系进行。在投资银行业，品牌名称——及其所附带的品质保证——作为公司最有价值的资产出现，导致了持续的内部化压力。 老牌英国海外银行作为第一代跨国银行中的幸存者，曾试图通过产品多元化和地域多元化来效仿这些潮流，但经济逻辑往往将它们推向相反的方向——要么转移注册地，要么撤资（如 1970 年代在澳大利亚、1980 年代在南非），要么组建由发展中国家大量参股或关联企业构成的集团。</span></span></span>

Regulators and governments<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">监管机构和政府</span></span></span>
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Almost all writers on multinational banks have stressed the influence of government in explaining where they invest and what business they undertake.<sup>17</sup> Banking is by no means unique in this respect. Business historians have for long emphasized the role of tariffs and other government-imposed trade restrictions in prompting manufacturing companies to establish foreign factories, a consistent trend from the first manufacturing multinationals in the mid-nineteenth century to Japanese direct investments in the United States and the European Community in the 1980s.<sup>18</sup> Government intervention in banking, however, has been particularly extensive, and perhaps of a different dimension altogether. This is because banking is not merely one economic sector among many. Banks have an impact on all other sectors through their lending policies; on large numbers of individuals (or voters) through their deposit-taking function; and on the general financial and monetary condition of an economy. Problems of information asymmetry and moral hazard have led governments since the nineteenth century to establish non-commercial central banks. There has been a tradition of theoretical criticism of state intervention in banking (especially the role of central banks) from Bagehot to Hayek, but in practice regulation, restriction and control has been the fate of the banking industry in every developed economy.<sup>19</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">几乎所有研究跨国银行的学者都强调了政府在解释其投资地点和业务范围方面的影响。 <sup>17</sup> 银行业在这方面绝非特例。商业史学家长期以来一直强调关税及其他政府强加贸易限制措施在促使制造企业设立海外工厂中的作用——从 19 世纪中叶首批制造业跨国公司的出现，到 20 世纪 80 年代日本对美国及欧洲共同体进行直接投资，这一趋势始终如一。 <sup>18</sup> 然而，政府对银行业的干预尤为广泛，甚至可能具有全然不同的维度。这是因为银行业并非众多经济部门中普通的一员。银行的贷款政策影响着所有其他部门；其存款吸纳功能影响着大量个人（即选民）；同时还关乎整个经济体的金融与货币状况。 信息不对称和道德风险问题促使 19 世纪以来的政府建立非商业性中央银行。从 Bagehot 到 Hayek，在理论上一直存在着对国家干预银行业（尤其是中央银行角色）的传统批判，但在实践中，监管、限制和控制已成为每一个发达经济体银行业的宿命。 <sup>19</sup> </span></span></span>

The influence of government permeates practically every study in this volume. In the nineteenth century, governments regulated or promoted multinational banking activity by their nationals. Before the 1850s British overseas banks, as King notes in [Chapter 5](#Ch05.xhtml_ht0041), operated under Royal Charters issued by the British Treasury which obliged these banks to meet certain conditions. In the United States, Federal law forbade national banks to branch abroad until 1913. The Japanese government promoted the Yokohama Specie Bank in 1880, and sustained the bank by allowing it privileged rediscounting facilities and by not allowing domestic Japanese institutions to compete for its business until the First World War, by which time it was well-established. The Imperial Ottoman Bank, and the peculiar Anglo-French form which it took, stemmed from an initiative from the Ottoman government. Home governments have continued to influence their <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_9"></span>multinational banks in the twentieth century. The formidable surge in Japanese multinational banking in the 1980s was partly the consequence of the domestic regulatory environment and interest rate controls within Japan, which led to London branches being extensively used as a flexible funding source to support lending inside Japan, while American branches were used in part to extend loans to Japanese-based companies.<sup>20</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">政府的影响力几乎渗透到本书的每一篇研究中。19 世纪，各国政府对其国民的跨国银行业务进行监管或推动。正如金在第 5 章中指出的，1850 年代之前，英国海外银行依据英国财政部颁发的皇家特许状运营，这些特许状要求银行必须满足特定条件。在美国，联邦法律在 1913 年前禁止国民银行在海外设立分支机构。日本政府于 1880 年推动成立了横滨正金银行，并通过授予其特权再贴现便利、在一战前禁止国内机构与其竞争业务等方式支持该银行，使其得以稳固发展。奥斯曼帝国银行及其独特的英法联合模式，则源于奥斯曼政府的倡议。进入 20 世纪后，各国政府依然持续影响着本国的跨国银行。 20 世纪 80 年代日本跨国银行业的迅猛崛起，部分归因于日本国内的监管环境和利率管制，这使得伦敦分行被广泛用作灵活的融资来源，以支持日本国内的贷款业务，而美国分行则部分用于向日本本土企业发放贷款。 <sup>20</sup> </span></span></span>

Governments also exercised a strong influence on the direction of multinational banking investment through host-economy regulations. The most striking instances were in the United States, where regulations and restrictions from the early nineteenth century greatly curtailed the ability of foreign banks to make direct investments. In the major financial centre of New York foreign banks were only allowed to own and operate 'agencies', which —crucially - could not take local deposits. As Merrett shows, Australian governments played an important part in erecting barriers to entry to foreign banks in the Australian market down to 1986. In Africa, Latin America and Asia, the growth of host-government regulation on foreign banks was one element in the declining competitive advantages of the British overseas banks, which had grown up and flourished in conditions where funds could be shifted between countries with the minimum of regulation.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">各国政府也通过东道国法规对跨国银行投资方向施加了强大影响。最显著的例子发生在美国，从 19 世纪初开始，当地的法规与限制就极大地制约了外资银行进行直接投资的能力。在纽约这一主要金融中心，外国银行仅被允许拥有并运营“代理机构”——关键在于，这些机构不能吸收当地存款。正如 Merrett 所指出的，直至 1986 年，澳大利亚政府在设置外资银行进入澳大利亚市场的障碍方面一直扮演着重要角色。在非洲、拉丁美洲和亚洲，东道国政府对外资银行监管的加强，是导致英国海外银行竞争优势逐步削弱的一个因素——这些银行正是在资金可最低限度受监管地在各国间流动的条件下成长壮大的。</span></span></span>

In a wider sense, imperial connections were a factor in explaining the direction taken by multinational banking in the nineteenth century. British overseas banks did not confine themselves to countries under British control, but their geographical distribution was markedly skewed in that direction, as were the locations of the branches of banks from other European powers. Cassis suggests in his chapter that one reason for the relative absence of Swiss multinational banking was the absence of Swiss imperialism. More recently, the lead-up to the Single European Market in 1992 has encouraged cross-border acquisitions and alliances within the European Community as banks seek to take advantage of the new institutional context.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从更广泛的意义上看，帝国关系是解释 19 世纪跨国银行业发展方向的一个因素。英国海外银行并未局限于英国控制的地区，但其地理分布明显偏向于此，其他欧洲列强的银行分支机构的分布也是如此。Cassis 在其章节中指出，瑞士跨国银行相对较少的一个原因是瑞士缺乏帝国主义色彩。更近些时候，1992 年欧洲单一市场形成前的准备工作，促使欧共体内部出现跨境收购与联盟，因为各银行试图利用这一新的制度环境。</span></span></span>

Just as governments have sought to restrict and prohibit, banks have sought to evade or take advantage of such restrictions, and this too has stimulated multinational banking. The most striking instance was the growth of the Eurodollar market, which in effect owed its birth in the late 1950s to American restrictions on interest paid on deposits within the United States combined with British government restrictions on sterling lending by its banks. The Eurodollar market provided, as Huertas observes, a major incentive for American banks to establish a branch in London, and later in various offshore centres.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正如各国政府试图限制和禁止，银行则设法规避或利用这些限制，这同样刺激了跨国银行业的发展。最显著的例子是欧洲美元市场的兴起，该市场实际上起源于 20 世纪 50 年代末——美国对境内存款利率的限制，加上英国政府对银行英镑贷款的限制，共同催生了这一市场。正如 Huertas 所言，欧洲美元市场为美国银行提供了在伦敦设立分行的强大动力，后来更促使它们在多个离岸金融中心布局。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_10"></span>Although the process of multinationauzation is explicable and predictable within an internalization framework, it has to be recognized that governments have in the past - and are as likely to in the future - regulated and controlled banking. Often the perceived 'public' interest conflicted with that of the banks or their shareholders. Much of government intervention, too, stemmed from motives that were not economically 'rational', including ideologies based on past historical experience and concerns about national sovereignty. As a result, the process of selection of the most efficient organizational form for banking activities came less 'through competition and rational evaluation of alternatives' (to quote Mark Casson) than through the actions of politicians and regulators.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管在全球化框架下，跨国化进程是可解释且可预测的，但必须认识到，政府在过去——并且未来同样可能——一直对银行业进行监管与控制。通常，所谓的“公共”利益与银行或其股东的利益相冲突。此外，许多政府干预的动机并非出于经济上的“理性”，而是源于基于历史经验的意识形态以及对国家主权的担忧。因此，银行业最有效组织形式的选择过程，与其说是“通过竞争和对替代方案的理性评估”（引用马克·卡森的话），不如说是政客与监管者行为的结果。</span></span></span>

History and theory<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">历史与理论</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The growing trend towards interdisciplinary dialogue among students of international business has dented old-fashioned divisions between those who call themselves 'economists' and 'historians'. Nevertheless, as Casson notes, there continue to be historians who reject economic theory because of evidence from single institutions. Conversely, there are still economists who would dismiss the empirical studies in this book as of little value because they are mere case studies which have been carefully selected and are thus, in statistical terms, unrepresentative. It is hoped that this book will provide one illustration of how theory and history can be used hand-in-hand to approach the central task of obtaining valid generalizations about business behaviour, past and present.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">国际商务学生之间跨学科对话的趋势日益增强，这削弱了自称“经济学家”与“历史学家”之间的传统壁垒。然而，正如卡森所指出的，仍有一些历史学家因单一机构的证据而排斥经济理论。与此同时，也依然存在一些经济学家，认为本书中的实证研究价值甚微，因为它们不过是精心挑选的个案研究，从统计学角度看缺乏代表性。希望这本书能提供一个范例，展示如何将理论与历史相结合，共同致力于实现对商业行为（无论过去还是现在）进行有效概括这一核心任务。</span></span></span>

This introduction has sought, by drawing on the arguments and evidence of later chapters, to address briefly a number of the central issues about the development of multinational banks. When did they develop? What did they do? Where did they invest? Why did they emerge? It is worth recapitulating the main conclusions before turning to the substantive chapters.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本导论通过借鉴后续章节的论点与证据，简要探讨了跨国银行发展的几个核心问题：它们何时兴起？扮演了什么角色？投资于何处？为何会出现？在进入实质性章节之前，有必要先概括一下主要结论。</span></span></span>

Modern multinational corporate banks flourished in two historical waves, one in the nineteenth century from the 1830s and another from the 1960s. British institutions led the first wave, later joined by other European banks and a Japanese one. American banks led the second wave, though during the 1980s Japanese institutions became increasingly prominent. Alongside the corporate banks, private and merchant banks conducted international banking, sometimes using direct investment strategies. These banking 'waves' had parallels in the manufacturing sector, <span id="Ch01.xhtml_page_11"></span>but not exact ones. Multinational manufacturing started later, in the 1850s and 1860s. Manufacturing multinationals grew in the late nineteenth century, as did their banking equivalents, but there was also a considerable surge in the 1920s which had no clear banking parallel, while a further surge began in the mid-1950s, some years before the second wave of multinational banks. More strikingly, the vigorous growth of American multinational manufacturing in the second half of the nineteenth century and the interwar years contrasted with the limited American multinational presence until the 1960s.<sup>21</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">现代跨国企业银行经历了两次历史浪潮，第一次始于 19 世纪 30 年代，第二次则从 20 世纪 60 年代开启。英国银行机构引领了第一波浪潮，随后欧洲其他银行和一家日本银行也加入其中。美国银行主导了第二波浪潮，尽管在 20 世纪 80 年代，日本金融机构的影响力日益凸显。与企业银行并行发展的还有私人银行和商人银行，它们有时通过直接投资策略开展国际银行业务。这些银行业"浪潮"在制造业领域也有类似现象，但并非完全同步。跨国制造业起步较晚，始于 19 世纪 50 至 60 年代。19 世纪末，制造业跨国公司与其银行业同行同步增长，但 20 世纪 20 年代出现了一次显著激增，这在银行业中并无明确对应；而另一次激增则始于 20 世纪 50 年代中期，比第二次跨国银行浪潮早了数年。 更引人注目的是，19 世纪下半叶及两次世界大战期间，美国跨国制造业的蓬勃发展与直到 1960 年代前美国跨国公司寥寥无几的局面形成了鲜明对比。 <sup>21</sup> </span></span></span>

Multinational banks have engaged in a wider range of banking and other activities, seldom confining themselves to servicing the trade finance requirements of corporate clients from their home economies. They entered fields where they had an 'advantage'. This led many banks in the nineteenth century, for example, into retail banking in settler economies where indigenous institutions were not either completely dominant or protected by regulation. Many nineteenth-century multinational banks engaged in investment banking. A similar diversity of activity is noticeable among the second wave of multinational corporate banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">跨国银行涉足了更广泛的银行业务及其他活动，很少局限于为本国企业客户的贸易融资需求提供服务。它们进入了自身“具备优势”的领域。例如，在 19 世纪，许多银行便因此进入了定居型经济体的零售银行业务，而在这些地区，本土机构要么尚未完全占据主导地位，要么未受法规保护。同样，许多 19 世纪的跨国银行也从事投资银行业务。在第二波跨国企业银行浪潮中，同样可以看到类似的多元化业务活动。</span></span></span>

Banks established branches where they had an advantage over local banks and were permitted to operate. In the nineteenth century these considerations led multinational banks to build branch networks in colonial territories and regions of recent settlement, including Latin America and excluding the United States. In the 1960s and later there was more cross-investment in banking between developed economies. One consistent theme was the importance of representation in international financial centres. From the nineteenth century until the present day, multinational banks have sought a London branch, and from the 1960s (and indeed earlier), New York and various offshore centres have also been vital locations. Home and host economy regulatory activity has consistently played an important part in the location decisions of multinational banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">银行在那些相比本地银行具有优势且获准运营的地方设立分支机构。在 19 世纪，这些考量促使跨国银行在殖民地区和新定居地（包括拉丁美洲，但不包括美国）建立分支网络。20 世纪 60 年代及以后，发达经济体之间的银行业交叉投资有所增加。一个持续不变的主题是在国际金融中心设立代表处的重要性。从 19 世纪至今，跨国银行一直寻求在伦敦设立分行；而自 20 世纪 60 年代起（甚至更早），纽约及多个离岸中心也成为至关重要的地点。母国与东道国的监管活动始终在跨国银行选址决策中扮演着重要角色。</span></span></span>

Although individual banks became multinational for many idiosyncratic reasons, Casson suggests that the internalization model provides the most helpful insight into the systematic factors behind multinational banks. Banks established branches or affiliates abroad when this seemed a more profitable (and feasible) alternative to other contractual or institutional arrangements.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管个别银行成为跨国银行有着许多特殊原因，但 Casson 认为，internalization model 为理解跨国银行背后的系统性因素提供了最具洞察力的视角。当设立海外分行或附属机构比其他合约性或制度性安排看起来更具盈利性（且可行性）时，银行便会采取这一做法。</span></span></span>
