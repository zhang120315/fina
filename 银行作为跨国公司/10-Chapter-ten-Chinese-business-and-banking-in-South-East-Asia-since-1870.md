Introduction<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">引言</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Historically, multinational banking has not been confined to western or quasi-western institutions. This chapter examines the multinational banking activities of the overseas Chinese in South-East Asia since the late nineteenth century.<sup>2</sup> In the nineteenth century, Chinese business groups undertook banking across national borders through branch networks as well as interlocking partnerships and other modes. In the twentieth century, the overseas Chinese have promoted modem banks which again have been very active in multinational activity. A distinguishing feature of this Chinese enterprise has been that, throughout the period under discussion, multinational banking has rarely been isolated from other multinational activities. Like the Paris Rothschilds of the late nineteenth century, Chinese business groups have tended to combine financial, commercial and industrial activity in diversified groups.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从历史角度看，跨国银行业务并不仅限于西方或准西方机构。本章探讨自 19 世纪末以来东南亚海外华人的跨国银行业活动。 <sup>2</sup> 19 世纪，华人商业集团通过分行网络、连锁合伙关系及其他方式开展跨境银行业务。20 世纪，海外华人推动发展了现代银行，这些银行在跨国活动中同样极为活跃。这种华人企业的一个显著特征是，在所讨论的整个时期，跨国银行业很少与其他跨国活动相分离。如同 19 世纪末巴黎的罗斯柴尔德家族，华人商业集团倾向于在多元化集团中整合金融、商业与工业活动。</span></span></span>

Inevitably, the story of Chinese multinational banking forms part of the wider story of the overseas Chinese community. However, there were distinct factors which led some business groups more than others into direct investment across borders. The following analysis will proceed chronologically.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">华人跨国银行的故事，不可避免地成为海外华人社区宏大叙事的一部分。然而，某些商业集团之所以比另一些更倾向于进行跨境直接投资，背后有其独特的因素。以下将按时间顺序进行分析。</span></span></span>

Overseas Chinese business groups before 1914<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1914 年之前的海外华人商业集团</span></span></span>
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In the period from the 1870s until the First World War, Chinese economic power in South-East Asia grew rapidly in terms of both volume and diversity. Chinese business houses were, typically, based on family and dialect, and financial and other business activities were usually centralized within single houses. These business houses provided a range of international banking services. In particular, they handled remittances from the overseas Chinese <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_174"></span>communities in South-East Asia back to China and they also financed trade. These banking functions formed part of a wide variety of commercial activities ranging from revenue farming to tin-mining, shipping, insurance, domestic and external trade.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从 19 世纪 70 年代到第一次世界大战期间，华人在东南亚的经济实力无论在规模还是多样性上都迅速增长。典型的华商机构以家族和方言为基础，金融及其他商业活动通常集中于单一机构内部。这些商行提供一系列国际银行服务，特别是处理东南亚华侨社群向中国的汇款，并资助贸易活动。这些银行功能是其广泛商业活动的一部分，涵盖从包税制到锡矿开采、航运、保险以及内外贸易等多种领域。</span></span></span>

This international production, trade and shipping was financed through a variety of institutional arrangements. Business groups used family and dialect connections throughout South-East Asia and in China to transfer funds, in effect creating networks of correspondent relationships. However, some business groups had wholly-owned branches in a variety of countries and were thus fully multinational. Hokkien, Teochew and Hakka financial caucuses controlling the Siam-Saigon rice trade, Malayan-Siam tin fields, and Dutch East Indies sugar, formed syndicates to guarantee deals, raise credit, and conclude transactions merely by name (or seal, Chinese chop), thereby creating financial reputations, crucial for attracting deposits and customers to Chinese banks later.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这项国际性的生产、贸易和航运活动，通过多种制度安排来实现融资。商业集团利用遍布东南亚及中国的家族和方言纽带转移资金，实际上构建了一套往来关系网络。不过，也有一些商业集团在多个国家拥有全资分支机构，因此完全是跨国性质的。掌控暹罗-西贡大米贸易、马来亚-暹罗锡矿以及荷属东印度群岛糖业的福建、潮州和客家金融集团，组成联合体，仅凭名字（或印章，即中式图章）就能担保交易、筹措信贷、完成买卖，从而建立起金融声誉，这对后来吸引存款和客户到中国银行至关重要。</span></span></span>

Family and dialect had assured the cohesiveness of these groups. For example, the prominent Teochew families of Siam in the early twentieth century, Kim Seng Lee (KSL) and Lee Teck Oh, who dominated the rice and teak industries and were prominent revenue farmers, formed alliances with Teochew businessmen in Singapore, Saigon, Rangoon and Hong Kong. The KSL conglomerate, whose assets in rice totalled 2.5 million baht (£1=13 baht, 1908-31), floated companies in banking, transport and dockyards, valued in total at 13m baht in 1911. Through the family and dialect network, it ultimately controlled 40 per cent of the Thai rice trade with Singapore and Hong Kong. It formed the Siam Commercial Bank in 1906, with a registered capital of 3m baht, and in 1909 formed the Chino-Siam Bank with 2m baht, with partners from the Thai aristocracy and the Deutsch Asiatische Bank.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">家族与方言的纽带，让这些群体紧密凝聚。例如，20 世纪初暹罗的潮州望族——金成利（Kim Seng Lee, KSL）与李德奥（Lee Teck Oh），他们不仅掌控着稻米与柚木两大产业，更是赫赫有名的包税商。这些家族与新加坡、西贡、仰光及香港的潮商结盟，织起了一张庞大的商业网络。金成利集团在稻米领域的资产高达 250 万泰铢（1908-1931 年间，1 英镑约合 13 泰铢），其涉足银行、运输及船坞领域的公司，于 1911 年总估值已达 1300 万泰铢。凭借家族与方言网络，该集团最终掌控了泰国与新加坡、香港之间 40%的大米贸易。1906 年，他们出资 300 万泰铢注册成立了暹罗商业银行；1909 年，又联合泰国贵族及德亚银行（Deutsch Asiatische Bank）的合伙人，以 200 万泰铢资本创立了华暹银行。</span></span></span>

The Hokkien and Hakka groups displayed the same dialect cohesiveness and dispersal across borders. For instance Low Chon Min, a Hokkien tax farmer and rice and shipping magnate from Bangkok, set up the Hang Meng Seng rice exporting firm with overseas branches in Hong Kong (Hang Meng Joo Tye) and in Singapore (Hang Joo Seng) to handle the Bangkok-Hong Kong-Singapore trade. Equally prominent was Thio Thiau Siat, a shipping and trading magnate from Penang, who also operated in Sumatra, in revenue farming, commodity production and as a military contractor to the Dutch government. He established the Deli Bank of Medan and later the Batavian Bank, consolidating his financial empire through the family and dialect network.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">福建人和客家人群体展现出相同的方言凝聚力和跨境分布特点。例如，曼谷的福建包税商兼米业和航运大亨刘俊敏，创办了恒茂盛米业出口公司，并在香港（恒茂如泰）和新加坡（恒茂盛）设有海外分支机构，负责曼谷-香港-新加坡之间的贸易。同样显赫的是槟城的航运和贸易巨头张诏设，他在苏门答腊也经营包税、商品生产，并担任荷兰政府的军事承包商。他创办了棉兰德里银行，之后又成立了巴达维亚银行，通过家族和方言网络巩固了自己的金融帝国。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_175"></span>These Chinese business houses also recruited funds from Western banks and Indian capitalists. Western banks preferred to deal with intermediaries rather than with the community at large and here the Chinese presented themselves as useful agents. For example, the Hongkong Bank injected funds into domestic capital markets by providing loans to Chinese capitalists or bank compradores who then on-lent them to merchants, producers and specialized remittance houses. This limited risk in lending, and overcame the difficulties inherent in westerners' imperfect knowledge of the local population. The Chinese compradore—capitalist link hence channelled capital from Europe and elsewhere into the local market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些华商行号还从西方银行和印度资本家那里吸纳资金。西方银行更倾向于与中间人打交道，而非直接面向整个商界，而华人恰好在此充当了有用的中介角色。例如，汇丰银行通过向中国资本家或银行买办提供贷款，将资金注入国内资本市场，这些买办再转贷给商人、生产商和专业汇兑行号。这种做法既控制了贷款风险，又克服了西方人对当地民众了解不足的固有困难。由此，华商买办与资本家的纽带便将欧洲及其他地区的资金引入了本地市场。</span></span></span>

The ties between Chinese and Indian credit networks were as important as these Chinese—western relationships. Chinese dependence particularly on Chettyar moneylenders is clear from the experience of the Khaw business empire, discussed later in this chapter.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">华商与印度信贷网络之间的纽带，与上述华商-西方关系同等重要。从本章稍后讨论的许氏商业帝国的经历中可以看出，华人尤其依赖切蒂亚尔放债人。</span></span></span>

However, the major source of funds for the Chinese business houses in the period was revenue farming. The majority of the colonial states in South-East Asia, and even indigenous Thai administrations, granted the right to collect taxes or sell monopoly goods and services to private contractors, most of whom were Chinese. The revenue farmer then managed the entire operation, and in return paid the authorities a fixed rent while retaining the surplus. The items that were subject to farm administration varied from state to state, but were generally opium, alcohol, gambling, certain primary commodities and manufactured goods. The profits from these revenue farms are difficult to estimate. Evidence from Penang, however, indicates that profits for individual Chinese ranged from SS\$5000 (£581) to SS\$9000 (£1,046) per month in 1909.<sup>3</sup> The Khaw family's revenue from opium, spirits and gambling in Southern Siam for the years 1899-1900 was estimated at 15.7m baht (£1.5m) which was equivalent to half the total state revenue of Siam for that period.<sup>4</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，这一时期华商企业的主要资金来源是承包税收。东南亚大部分殖民国家，甚至泰国本土政府，都将征税权或专卖权授予私人承包商，其中多数为华人。承包商人全权管理运营，向当局缴纳固定租金，剩余利润则归己所有。实行承包管理的项目因国家而异，通常包括鸦片、酒类、赌博、某些初级产品和制成品。这些承包项目的利润难以估算，但槟城的证据显示，1909 年单个华商月利润从海峡币 5000 元（合 581 英镑）至 9000 元（合 1046 英镑）不等。 <sup>3</sup> 1899 至 1900 年间，Khaw 家族在暹罗南部从鸦片、烈酒和赌博中获得的收入估计高达 1570 万泰铢（约 150 万英镑），这一数额相当于同期暹罗国家总收入的一半。 <sup>4</sup> </span></span></span>

This era of family-controlled businesses and heavy reliance on revenue farming is dramatically epitomized by the history of the Khaw group. The Khaws were based in the British colony of Penang and had strong connections with the elite in neighbouring Siam. The family's fortunes from opium farming were invested in an insurance company and in the Koh Guan Trust which was estimated at SS\$320,000 (£37,209) in 1905.<sup>5</sup> These trustees later invested in Eastern Shipping (1907) and in Eastern Smelting (1907). The former had an initial capital of \$1,400,000 (£162,790) and 16 sailing vessels. By 1920 it had 40 sailing vessels <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_176"></span>(sold in 1923 to the Straits Steamship Company for \$2m).<sup>6</sup> Eastern Smelting, which smelted the ore from the Khaw tin mines of southern Siam, accounted for 18 per cent of all smelted ore shipped from Penang by 1908. Two years later this had increased to 29 per cent.<sup>7</sup> Obviously Khaw Soo Cheang's appointment as Governor of Southern Siam in the 1890s, and his close liaison with the British authorities in Penang, contributed to the influence of the firm in Siam, Malaya, Sumatra and even Burma.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这个家族控制、高度依赖税收承包的时代，在许氏集团的历史中得到了戏剧性的体现。许氏家族以英国殖民地槟城为基地，与邻国暹罗的精英阶层关系密切。家族从鸦片承包中积累的财富被投资于一家保险公司和许源泰信托，该信托在 1905 年估值约 32 万叻币（37,209 英镑）。 <sup>5</sup> 这些受托人后来投资了东方航运（1907 年）和东方熔炼公司（1907 年）。前者初始资本为 140 万叻币（162,790 英镑），拥有 16 艘帆船。到 1920 年，其帆船数量增至 40 艘（1923 年以 200 万叻币出售给海峡轮船公司）。 <sup>6</sup> 东方熔炼公司负责冶炼来自许氏在暹罗南部锡矿的矿石，到 1908 年，其冶炼的矿石已占从槟城运出全部冶炼矿石的 18%。两年后，这一比例升至 29%。 显然，19 世纪 90 年代，Khaw Soo Cheang 被任命为南暹罗总督，并且他与槟城英国当局保持密切联系，这些都增强了该公司在暹罗、马来亚的影响力苏门答腊，甚至缅甸。</span></span></span>

The Khaw multinational enterprise was also the focus of two important developments. First, it received significant amounts of Australian capital, and secondly it used Chettyar funds to extend credit throughout northern Malaya and southern Siam. Their multinational activities were sustained by borrowing from the Australian capital market, through joint ventures with Australians and through using capital derived from the Indian sub-continent. These sources of funds, in addition to what was available through the family and dialect network, enabled the firm to survive —though in an attenuated form — even after the abolition of revenue farming by 1910.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">考氏跨国企业也是两项重要发展的焦点。首先，它获得了大量澳大利亚资本；其次，它利用齐智人资金在整个北马来亚和南暹罗地区扩展信贷。这些跨国业务得以持续，是通过从澳大利亚资本市场借贷、与澳大利亚人合资经营，以及利用来自印度次大陆的资本。除了家族和方言网络可获得的资金外，这些资金来源使得该企业在 1910 年包税制废除后仍能存活——尽管规模已大为缩减。</span></span></span>

Access to significant capital also enabled the Khaws to obstruct the growth of British and European enterprise in tin mining in southern Siam, while introducing capital intensive technological innovations into the industry. The first Khaw—Australian joint venture started in 1906 with the Tongkah Harbour Tin Dredging Company, which had an issued capital of £250,000.<sup>8</sup> Investment funds poured in from Australia throughout the 1910s and 1920s and by 1926 three major groups of Australian—Chinese joint enterprises operated in southern Siam, Malaya and Lower Burma tin fields, with a nominal capital of £15m.<sup>9</sup> The Khaw influence over the management of these companies was considerable, even though the capital remained largely Australian.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">大量资本的获取也使许氏家族得以阻碍英国和欧洲企业在暹罗南部锡矿开采领域的发展，同时在该行业引入了资本密集型的技术创新。首个许氏与澳大利亚的合资企业始于 1906 年的通卡港锡矿疏浚公司，其发行股本为 25 万英镑。 <sup>8</sup> 1910 年代至 1920 年代，来自澳大利亚的投资资金源源不断涌入，到 1926 年，三大澳中合资企业集团在暹罗南部、马来亚和下缅甸的锡矿区运营，名义资本达 1500 万英镑。 <sup>9</sup> 尽管资本主要仍来自澳大利亚，但许氏对这些公司的管理有着相当大的影响力。</span></span></span>

The Khaw—Chettyar relationship remained principally in the areas of retail credit and in shipping. Khaw Sim Bee allowed Chettyar firms to distribute credit to small mines and Chinese traders throughout Phuket in southern Siam. The profits from these ventures were used to buy stocks on the Australian stock exchange under the title 'Eastern Shareholders'. Chettyar capital was also committed to the Eastern Shipping Company, the major figure here being A.M.K. Raman Chetty.<sup>10</sup> Hence while the Khaws used two Australian syndicates, Melbourne Hobart and Pratten King Munro, to block the Straits Trading Company's expansion into southern Siam, the use of Chettyar finances to thwart the expansion of the British India Steam Navigation Company was equally successful, at least until the 1920s, when the Chettyars <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_177"></span>chose to turn their attention to the lucrative rubber industry.<sup>11</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">许氏与切蒂亚尔家族的关系主要停留在零售信贷和航运领域。许心美允许切蒂亚尔公司在暹罗南部的普吉岛向小矿场及华商发放贷款，这些业务的利润被用于以"东方股东"名义在澳大利亚证券交易所购入股票。切蒂亚尔资本也注入了东方航运公司，其中关键人物是 A.M.K.拉曼·切蒂。 <sup>10</sup> 因此，尽管许氏家族借助墨尔本霍巴特与普拉滕·金·门罗这两家澳大利亚财团，成功阻止了海峡贸易公司向暹罗南部的扩张；但借助切蒂亚尔财资挫败英国印度蒸汽航运公司的扩张，也取得了同等成效——至少持续到 20 世纪 20 年代，彼时切蒂亚尔人选择将目光转向利润丰厚的橡胶产业。 <sup>11</sup> </span></span></span>

The Khaw group is a clear example of the way in which the extended family acted as the basic structure of a far-flung commercial and financial empire in the overseas Chinese business community in South-East Asia. The family, as a family, provided crucial links between the enterprise and local political authority, as for example, in the relationship of the Khaws with Thai royal and noble élites, and with foreign capital, both Australian and South Indian.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">Khaw 家族是一个鲜明的例子，展示了在东南亚华人商业圈中，大家庭如何成为庞大商业与金融帝国的基本结构。这一家族本身，为企业与当地政治权力——例如与泰国皇室及贵族精英的关系——以及外国资本（包括澳大利亚和南印度的资本）之间，提供了至关重要的纽带。</span></span></span>

Western challenge and Chinese response<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">西方的挑战与中国的回应</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

With the loss of opium farms by the end of the First World War, the Chinese had to turn to other sources of capital. The options open to them were: to arrange contracts with western or Japanese traders who could provide them with credit advances; to borrow from other wealthy Chinese, or from western banks, although these generally provided only short-term credit; or to borrow from the Indian moneylenders who were a reliable source of long-term loans. Another alternative was for them to establish their own banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">第一次世界大战结束时，鸦片种植园的丧失迫使中国人不得不另寻资本来源。他们面前的选择包括：与能够提供信贷预支的西方或日本商人签订合同；向其他富裕华人或西方银行借款（尽管后者通常只提供短期信贷）；或者向可靠的长期贷款来源——印度放债人借贷。另一种选择则是自行创办银行。</span></span></span>

This last alternative was chosen, and the emergence of banks was thus a major characteristic of the Chinese commercial community in the interwar decades. It is clear that funds from revenue farms did not always go directly into banking. In fact it is not always clear how the residual capital from revenue farming was dispersed. The banking elites of the interwar decades, Lim Peng Siang and Lee Kong Chian, prominent in banking in Malaya and Singapore, and Wang Lee and Lam Sam of Thailand, had not started out from tax farming. Their wealth was built on international trade — particularly in rice, rubber, shipping and import—export trade.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">最终选择了这一替代方案，因此银行的兴起成为两次世界大战之间中国商界的一个显著特征。显然，来自税收承包的资金并不总是直接流入银行业。事实上，税收承包的剩余资本如何分配往往并不明确。两次世界大战之间的银行业精英——马来亚和新加坡银行业中显赫的林秉祥与李光前，以及泰国的王李和林森——并非出身于税收承包业。他们的财富建立在国际贸易之上，尤其是大米、橡胶、航运和进出口贸易。</span></span></span>

In the period from 1914, five major Chinese banks were established in Malaya and the Straits Settlements, two in Thailand, two in the Dutch East Indies. All these banks had branches in Burma and often also in Hong Kong and China. In Malaya and the Straits Settlements, the major Chinese banks were the Kwong Yik (1912), the Ho Hong (1917), Chinese Commercial Bank (1912), Overseas Chinese Bank (1919), and the Lee Wah Bank (1920). The N.V. Bank Vereniging Oei Tiong Ham in Java, and Wang Lee Chan Bank and Thai Farmers Bank in Thailand were Chinese-owned and served a largely Chinese clientele.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从 1914 年起，马来亚和海峡殖民地先后成立了五家主要华资银行，暹罗（泰国）两家，荷属东印度群岛两家。这些银行均在缅甸设有分行，且大多在香港和中国内地也设点。在马来亚和海峡殖民地，主要华资银行包括：Kwong Yik（1912 年）、Ho Hong（1917 年）、Chinese Commercial Bank（1912 年）、Overseas Chinese Bank（1919 年）和 Lee Wah Bank（1920 年）。爪哇的 N.V. Bank Vereniging Oei Tiong Ham，以及暹罗的 Wang Lee Chan Bank 和 Thai Farmers Bank 均为华人所有，主要服务华人客户。</span></span></span>

Chinese banks were essentially an outgrowth of other Chinese activities such as tin mining, rubber, trading and shipping. There <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_178"></span>remained, therefore, no clear separation between these activities and banking. Chinese banks were not autonomous financial institutions; both their deposits and their lending profiles were dictated by these commercial priorities. The Ho Hong Bank, whose proprietors included Lim Peng Siang, Dr. Lim Boon Keng and Seow Poh Leng, had a wide range of commercial interests such as rice milling, coconut oil mills, soap factories, import-export trade and shipping. The role of the Ho Hong Bank in different countries in the region was to co-ordinate the financial transactions of the entire range of commercial activity. Its issued capital was \$3.5m (£406,976), (half of which was paid up) and by 1920, \$4m (£465,116) (all fully paid up).<sup>12</sup> Its main focus was on outward expansion in South-East Asia and China particularly in foreign exchange operations, which hitherto had been exploited by Chinese remittance houses and western banks. This explains the close connections that Chinese banks had initially with Chinese remittance houses which were already active before the First World War. Chinese banks were more keen to control this remittance business rather than provide long term commercial credit.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">中国的银行本质上是从其他华人商业活动中衍生出来的，例如锡矿开采、橡胶、贸易和航运。因此，这些活动与银行业务之间并没有明确的界限。当时的华人银行并非自主的金融机构；无论是存款还是贷款方向，都受制于这些商业活动的优先需求。和丰银行的股东包括林秉祥、林文庆医生和萧保龄，他们涉足米业、椰子油厂、肥皂厂、进出口贸易和航运等广泛的商业领域。和丰银行在东南亚各国的角色，就是统筹协调这些商业活动中所有的金融交易。其发行资本为 350 万叻币（约合 406,976 英镑），其中半数已实缴；到 1920 年，资本增至 400 万叻币（约合 465,116 英镑），且已全部实缴到位。 <sup>12</sup> 重点是向东南亚和中国大肆扩张，尤其是外汇业务——要知道，这块肥肉之前一直被中国侨汇庄和西方银行把持着。这就难怪中国银行最初会和那些早在第一次世界大战前就活跃异常的侨汇庄打得火热了。比起提供长期商业信贷，中国银行显然更想牢牢掌控汇款业务这块蛋糕。</span></span></span>

As the Chinese banks of this period were formed primarily to secure funds for their owners' commercial activities, they were vulnerable to serious overlending to their directors. Loans to directors' enterprises formed a substantial proportion of their overall loan portfolio. To aggravate this situation further, banks with strong dialect and kin sympathies were prone to injudicious lending. Thus while these banks were conservative in the management of reserves, they were not prudent in lending to directors' kin. The earlier difficulties experienced by the Chinese Commercial Bank, such as the run on the bank in 1913, was a result of such lending. It recovered during the war boom of 1914-18, and its aggregate deposits, loans and advances soared through to 1919. The recession of the early 1920s affected it but an upswing began again in 1925-29, assisted by a cautious lending policy. However, the 1931 crisis and the financial difficulties of its major shareholder, Tan Kah Kee, added to its problems, and amalgamation with both the Ho Hong Bank and Overseas Chinese Bank proved the only option.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这一时期中国的银行主要是为保障其所有者的商业活动而筹集资金，因此极易对其董事进行过度放贷。向董事所属企业的贷款在其整体贷款组合中占据了相当大的比例。更糟糕的是，具有强烈方言和宗族认同感的银行更倾向于进行不明智的放贷。因此，尽管这些银行在准备金管理上较为保守，但在向董事亲属放贷时却不够审慎。中华商业银行早期遇到的困难，如 1913 年的挤兑风波，便是此类放贷的结果。该行在 1914-18 年战争繁荣期得以恢复，其存款总额、贷款及垫款在 1919 年大幅攀升。20 年代初的经济衰退对其造成影响，但在 1925-29 年间，得益于审慎的放贷政策，该行再次迎来上升周期。 然而，1931 年的经济危机以及大股东陈嘉庚的财务困境，让情况雪上加霜，最终不得不与和丰银行（Ho Hong Bank）、华侨银行（Overseas Chinese Bank）合并，这一条路成了唯一选择。</span></span></span>

A major characteristic of Chinese banks was that their deposits and reserves varied with movements in international trade. There was a close correspondence between deposits and trade crises.<sup>13</sup> When rubber prices fell in 1920 because of over-production, total deposits in Chinese banks between June 1920 and June 1921 fell by a third, as depositors withdrew savings to tide them through the recession. In the boom years of 1925-29, aggregate profits were <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_179"></span>high, as displayed by the Ho Hong Bank, fluctuating between \$1,003,000 and \$572,000.<sup>14</sup> Capital and reserves were at \$6m in 1925-29, yet by June 1932 this had fallen to \$2.6m.<sup>15</sup> The large fall was a product of trading losses and losses in foreign exchange transactions. The banks' operations were affected dramatically by the 1930s depression.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">中国银行的一个主要特征是，其存款和储备随国际贸易变动而波动。存款与贸易危机之间存在密切关联。 <sup>13</sup> 1920 年因橡胶生产过剩导致价格下跌，1920 年 6 月至 1921 年 6 月间，中国银行总存款减少了三分之一，储户纷纷提取存款以渡过经济衰退。在 1925-29 年的繁荣时期，银行总利润颇高，以和丰银行为例，其利润在 1,003,000 美元至 572,000 美元之间浮动。 <sup>14</sup> 1925-29 年间，该行资本和储备为 600 万美元，但到 1932 年 6 月已降至 260 万美元。 <sup>15</sup> 这一大幅下降是交易损失和外汇交易损失共同导致的结果。1930 年代的大萧条对银行的运营造成了剧烈冲击。</span></span></span>

Local Chinese banks kept large liquid reserves in deposits with British overseas banks. This ratio of liquid assets to deposits varied from 20 per cent to 200 per cent at different times for different banks. The high liquid ratio severely constrained their lending activities. Arbitrage offered Chinese banks the most profitable and secure base in the 1920s. This was a result of the fact that the Hong Kong dollar remained on the silver standard while the Straits dollar was pegged to sterling and gold. The Chinese banks which conducted trade and remittance business with China took advantage of the fluctuation in the two dollar ratios. The remittance business afforded Chinese banks not only huge profits, but also an autonomy of immense advantage in their competition with western banks, their strength here being more cultural than economic.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本地华资银行在英国海外银行存有大量流动性储备。不同时期、不同银行的流动资产与存款比率从 20%到 200%不等。高流动性比率严重制约了它们的放贷活动。在 20 世纪 20 年代，套利为华资银行提供了最有利可图且最安全的基础。这是因为港币仍实行银本位制，而海峡元则与英镑和黄金挂钩。从事对华贸易和汇款业务的华资银行充分利用了这两种货币汇率的波动。汇款业务不仅为华资银行带来了巨额利润，还赋予了它们在与西方银行竞争时巨大的自主优势——其优势更多体现在文化层面而非经济层面。</span></span></span>

In essence, apart from this currency speculation, Chinese banks concentrated on loans to directors, and to those with letters of guarantee. Acceptance and the dealing in promissory notes for smaller Chinese businesses was largely left to the Chettyars. For example, the produce trade from Malayan smallholders, accounting for a third of Malayan rubber production by the 1920s, was undertaken by Chinese middlemen and Malay peasants, often with Chettyar credit. The smaller Chinese exporters purchased the produce from syndicates of Chinese producers, pedlars and shopkeepers, and Chettyars endorsed the promissory notes of such Chinese dealers. This acceptance business was essential for the small Chinese trader lacking access to the compradore of the western banks or to the Chinese commercial banks. Western banks were heavily involved in financing western agency houses' produce trade; Chinese banks were less involved in the business of bills of exchange and promissory notes, relying on Chettyars and western banks for these transactions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本质上，除了这种货币投机之外，中国的银行主要向董事以及持有保函的人提供贷款。而为规模较小的中国企业承兑和交易期票，则大多留给了切蒂亚尔。例如，到 20 世纪 20 年代，来自马来亚小农的农产品贸易——占马来亚橡胶产量的三分之一——由华裔中间商和马来农民进行，他们往往依靠切蒂亚尔的信贷。规模较小的中国出口商从中国生产商、小贩和店主组成的辛迪加手中购买农产品，而切蒂亚尔则为这些中国商人的期票背书。这种承兑业务对于缺乏西方银行买办或中国商业银行渠道的中国小商人来说至关重要。 西方银行深度参与了西方代理行的农产品贸易融资；而中国银行在汇票和本票业务上涉足较少，这些交易主要依赖切提亚尔人和西方银行进行。</span></span></span>

The Great Depression and its aftermath for Chinese banks<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">大萧条及其对中国银行的影响</span></span></span>

The Great Depression proved to be a crucial turning point in Chinese banking, when deposits plummeted and Chinese banks suffered a decline from \$44,171,000 to \$31,174,000.<sup>16</sup> Profits fell from \$8,620,000 in June 1931 to \$2,608,000 in December 1932.<sup>17</sup> This was accompanied by a fall in cash and deposits at other banks <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_180"></span>from \$25,529,000 to \$17,155,000 between December 1931 and June 1932.<sup>18</sup> Incorporation and partnership arrangements were used to rescue banks facing difficulties during the recession. The aftermath of the Great Depression therefore saw the emergence of a more autonomous development of Chinese financial entrepreneurship, in a position to exploit intra-regional links from Swatow to Hong Kong, Bangkok, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur and Jakarta. Banks were no longer dominated by single families but by partnerships forming large joint-stock banks assisting in the transmission of capital to and from South-East Asia, and also within it.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">大萧条被证明是中国银行业的一个关键转折点，期间存款急剧缩水，中国银行的存款从 44,171,000 美元暴跌至 31,174,000 美元。 <sup>16</sup> 利润从 1931 年 6 月的 8,620,000 美元骤降至 1932 年 12 月的 2,608,000 美元。 <sup>17</sup> 与此同时，其他银行的现金及存款在 1931 年 12 月至 1932 年 6 月间从 25,529,000 美元跌至 17,155,000 美元。 <sup>18</sup> 为救助衰退中陷入困境的银行，采用了公司化与合伙制安排。因此，大萧条之后，中国金融企业家精神走向更自主的发展道路，得以利用从汕头到香港、曼谷、新加坡、吉隆坡乃至雅加达的区域内部联系。银行不再由单一家族垄断，而是由合伙制组成的大型股份制银行占据主导，这些银行助力资本在东南亚内外流转。</span></span></span>

The new banking elite is best illustrated by the example of the Oversea Chinese Banking Corporation (OCBC) formed in 1932, absorbing three existing banks in the Malay peninsula. The bank (in contrast to western banks) continued the conservative tradition of Chinese banks of preserving a highly liquid position. The OCBS's liquidity ratio of 0.70 was high in comparison with, say, the Hongkong Bank's ratio of 0.33-0.25 between 1933-38.<sup>19</sup> It is important to recognize that Chinese banks like the OCBC acted as a principal; hence cash from trade, customers' deposits etc. were held by the bank in a liquid position. Further large amounts of liquid assets, in particular sterling balances, were held, and these yielded some interest and acted as a secondary reserve. The history of instability among the Chinese banks, particularly in the 1920-21 recession, had nurtured an over-cautious policy of cash reserves. The banks survived essentially by charging high interest rates on loans to offset this liquid position.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

The dominant Chinese financial groups in the post-1945 decades, particularly in Indonesia, were often new élites, quite distinct from the prewar interests. The new financial élite maintained its dominance, first in the import-substitution industrialization of the 1950s and 1960s and later in the export-led growth of the 1970s. Simultaneously, the Bank extended its domination of the corporate investment of the Thai economy through its <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_186"></span>affiliates Samrit Rice Company (in liaison with the Wong family of Singapore and the Tay family of Malaysia), Saha Union (a textile firm with Japanese links), Siam Motors, Thai Oil Refinery and United Flour Mill. Hence the Bangkok Bank, which by 1979 was the largest commercial bank in Thailand and the Asean countries, had its registered capital distributed as follows: 70 per cent in finance, 18 per cent in mining and manufacturing, 4 per cent in commerce and 8 per cent in real estate and services.<sup>26</sup> The OCBC and the Liem Banking group displayed a similar pattern of growth.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">以曼谷银行为例，该行成立于 1944 年，由陈弼臣家族持有多数股权。随后，该银行不仅牢牢掌控本地资本市场，还通过旗下多家金融公司（如 Bangkok Investment、Asia Credit、Commercial Trust、Sahakit Thai 和 Taksin Finance）将业务拓展至香港。与此同时，曼谷银行通过其关联企业——与新加坡王氏家族和马来西亚戴氏家族合作的 Samrit Rice Company、与日本有联系的纺织企业 Saha Union、Siam Motors、Thai Oil Refinery 和 United Flour Mill——进一步主导了泰国经济中的企业投资领域。到 1979 年，曼谷银行已成为泰国及东盟国家中最大的商业银行，其注册资本分布如下：70%投向金融业，18%投向矿业与制造业，4%投向商业，8%投向房地产与服务业。 <sup>26</sup> OCBC 与 Liem Banking 集团呈现出相似的增长模式。</span></span></span>

This financial growth was structured on a resource base both in South-East Asia and taking advantage of the influx of western and Japanese capital in the 1970s. For example, the growing importance of American capital led to the OCBC becoming a prominent intermediary. In 1970 an OCBC subsidiary, Finance Overseas Credit Ltd (FOCL) went into joint partnership with the Citibank to channel American capital into the area. This was followed by the formation of Singapore International Merchant Bankers Ltd (SIMBL) with American and British partners such as Continental Illinois, National Bank of Chicago, the Crown Agents and others. SIMBL was active in raising capital, underwriting loans, and providing financial advice on projects in South-East Asia and Hong Kong. It was also crucial for the growth of the Asian Dollar Market and the rise of Asian Currency Units. With these credentials, the OCBC in 1971 formed a financial consortium with European, American and Japanese banks to channel funds for private investment in Asia, already dominated by the Chinese network.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这种金融增长建立在东南亚的资源基础之上，并得益于 20 世纪 70 年代西方和日本资本的涌入。例如，美国资本日益重要，使得 OCBC 成为关键的中间机构。1970 年，OCBC 的子公司 Finance Overseas Credit Ltd (FOCL) 与花旗银行（Citibank）合资，将美国资本引入该地区。随后，新加坡国际商人银行有限公司（SIMBL）成立，合作伙伴包括 Continental Illinois、National Bank of Chicago、Crown Agents 等美英机构。SIMBL 积极筹集资金、承销贷款，并为东南亚及香港的项目提供财务咨询。它还对亚洲美元市场的扩张和亚洲货币单位的崛起起到了至关重要的作用。 凭借这些资历，华侨银行在 1971 年与欧美及日本银行共同组建了一个金融财团，旨在为亚洲的私人投资引入资金，而此时的亚洲早已由华人网络主导。</span></span></span>

The trend of Chinese multinational banks borrowing abroad is also demonstrated by Liem's offshore financial empire. In the 1970s and 1980s Liem was not only a major recipient of Chinese capital in South-East Asia, but also acted as a private merchant banker for international investment companies, receiving substantial offshore deposits from Korea, Japan, United States and Holland. His First Pacific Finance Company led him to be among the world's twelve richest bankers with assets exceeding US \$1 billion. Liem could buy into European and American capital markets and thus help redirect Chinese investment from South-East Asia to major western markets. This redirection of Chinese capital and investment from the late 1970s is an important development: their diversification away from South-East Asia meant they could not only draw funds into the region but also out of it.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">中国跨国银行海外借贷的趋势，也从林绍良的离岸金融帝国中可见一斑。上世纪七八十年代，林绍良不仅是东南亚地区中国资本的主要接收方，还充当着国际投资公司的私人商业银行家角色，吸收了来自韩国、日本、美国及荷兰的大量离岸存款。其旗下的第一太平金融公司（First Pacific Finance Company）使他跻身全球最富有的十二位银行家之列，资产超过 10 亿美元。林绍良得以进入欧美资本市场，从而帮助将中国投资从东南亚重新导向西方主要市场。自上世纪 70 年代末起，中国资本与投资的这种转向意义重大：它们的多元化布局不再局限于东南亚，意味着既能将资金引入该地区，也能从中抽离。</span></span></span>

how do we explain the extensive multinational banking <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_187"></span>activities of the overseas Chinese community in South-East Asia? First, Chinese banking activities service a community that is geographically dispersed and has considerable need for banking services because of its extensive business involvement. This supports a view of multinational banking as being related to emigration and flows of people. However, the Chinese tradition of the tightly-knit family firm operating through integrated credit and distribution networks within and throughout Asia cannot alone explain this dominance. Why indigenous groups failed to produce a banking élite is equally relevant to this question of Chinese financial pre-eminence in the region. There are therefore several dimensions to the problem of explaining this Chinese predominance in banking.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如何解释东南亚华人社群广泛的跨国银行业务活动？首先，华人银行业服务的是一个地理分布分散、因广泛商业参与而对银行服务有巨大需求的社群。这支持了跨国银行业与移民及人口流动相关的观点。然而，华人通过亚洲内部及跨区域整合的信贷和分销网络运作的紧密家族企业传统，并不能单独解释这种主导地位。本土群体为何未能产生银行精英阶层，同样与华人在该地区金融领域占据优势地位这一问题相关。因此，解释华人在银行业的主导地位涉及多个维度。</span></span></span>

The ties of culture, language and kinship working through integrated business groups meant banking activities were kept within the community. Second, Chinese business groups were flexible — they were able to service the needs of the colonial powers as the Khaws did with tax farming — without losing their independence. When the old style business was damaged by the abolition of tax farming, they were able to adjust and found modern banks. Third, western banks were keen to inject funds into the domestic economies and used Chinese as intermediaries channelling money through specialized alien minorities thereby limiting the risk in financing the large indigenous sector.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">通过整合性商业集团运作的文化、语言与亲缘纽带，使得银行业务得以在社群内部流转。其次，华人商业集团具有灵活性——它们既能像"卡奥"家族从事包税制那样为殖民政权服务，又未丧失自身独立性。当包税制废除重创传统商业形态时，这些集团能迅速调整并创立现代银行。第三，西方银行热衷于向本土经济注入资金，并通过华人中介将资本导向特定的少数族裔群体，从而降低对庞大本土部门融资的风险。</span></span></span>

However, all these advantages for the Chinese are accentuated by the lack of indigenous competition in the region. Indigenous groups faced tremendous obstacles in nurturing financial and commercial success. First, the apparent encouragement by the colonial authorities of alien minorities to undertake the financing of the produce trade while preserving the indigenous agrarian sector led to a clear demarcation between agrarian and commercial interests. While the indigenous peasantry was isolated from capital accumulation pursuits, their landed Elites were being transformed into politico-bureaucrats controlling alien capitalists through the control of resources not through the control of finance or through taking decisions or risks. Hence the Chinese capitalists of South-East Asia supported the bureaucratic polity and acted as intermediaries for the state in international financial activities such as floating loans etc. This relationship, though dependent and parasitic, is predictive, because of the changes in world trade, the oil boom, the rise of industrial Japan and Korea accompanied by economic recession in the West, and the impact of foreign aid, the involvement of the military in the economy and the emergence of Third World multinationals. All of this led to a vigorous form of <span id="Ch010.xhtml_page_188"></span>state trading in combination with sophisticated pnvate Chinese banking and Chinese commercial Elites.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，华人的所有这些优势在该地区本土竞争缺乏的背景下被进一步放大。本土群体在培育金融和商业成功方面面临巨大障碍。首先，殖民当局明显鼓励外来少数族群承担农产品贸易的融资，同时保留本土农业部门，这导致了农业与商业利益之间的明确界限。本土农民被隔绝于资本积累之外，而他们的地主精英则转变为政治官僚，通过控制资源而非掌控金融或承担决策与风险来制约外来资本家。 因此，东南亚的华裔资本家支持官僚政体，并在国家国际金融活动中充当中间人，例如发行债券等。这种关系尽管具有依附性和寄生性，却具有预测性，原因在于世界贸易的变化、石油繁荣、工业化日本与韩国的崛起伴随西方经济衰退、外援的影响、军方对经济的介入以及第三世界跨国公司的出现。所有这些因素共同催生了一种活跃的国家贸易形式，与精密的华人私营银行体系及华人商业精英相结合。</span></span></span>

Chinese enjoy oligopolistic status in selected industries and sectors. As local firms they benefit from investment incentives from the government. Hence their control of a large share of the domestic market, assisted by government protection and the influx of foreign capital, results in an entrenched position in the economies of South-East Asia.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">华人在某些行业和领域占据寡头地位。作为本地企业，他们享受来自政府的投资激励。因此，在政府保护和外资涌入的双重助力下，他们掌控了国内市场的很大份额，从而在东南亚经济体内部形成了根深蒂固的势力。</span></span></span>

The final question is why the Chinese should operate as multinationals, establishing branches in different political regions, rather than have arm's length agreement with the Chinese diaspora. What are the advantages of direct involvement throughout the region rather than as units within one country?<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">最后的问题是，为何华商要以跨国公司的形式运营，在不同政治区域设立分支机构，而不是与海外华人社群保持公平交易协议？在整个区域内直接参与经营，而非仅作为某个国家内的独立单元，这其中的优势何在？</span></span></span>

Here one has to calculate the political instability of South-East Asia, throughout the entire period though increasingly so in the postwar era, when the Chinese faced discrimination and political persecution. Hence this multinational growth and dispersal was fortuitous in reducing political risk. Again because of dialect and community links, this multinational operation reduced the costs of integration. Third, the increase in international capital investments made it imperative for the Chinese financial network to channel this circulating capital and act as multinational banker and investor.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在此，人们不得不评估东南亚的政治不稳定性——贯穿整个时期，尤其是战后时期，当时华人面临着歧视和政治迫害。因此，这种跨国增长与分散在降低政治风险方面起到了偶然的积极作用。同样，由于方言和社群联系，这种跨国运营也降低了整合成本。第三，国际资本投资的增加使得华人金融网络必须引导这些流动资本，并扮演跨国银行家和投资者的角色。</span></span></span>

The major historical question behind this chapter is whether the rise to prominence of Chinese financial networks in South-East Asia is a reflection of Chinese commercial strength or of indigenous weakness: this chapter has leant towards the former.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本章背后的核心历史问题是：东南亚华人金融网络的崛起，究竟是反映了华人的商业实力，还是本土势力的薄弱？本章倾向于前者。</span></span></span>
