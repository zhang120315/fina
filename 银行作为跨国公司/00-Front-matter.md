<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">前言</span></span></span>](#contents.xhtml_bck_ht0006)

This book originated at an international conference held at the University of Reading, Britain during 16-19 August 1989. Like an earlier conference on multinational manufacturing held at the European University Institute, Florence in 1983, which resulted in the volume <span class="italic">Multinationals: Theory and History</span> edited by Peter Hertner and myself, the Reading conference assembled economists and business historians to address a common problem. As at Florence, there were conflicting viewpoints and methodologies, but the discussions showed that they were by no means down straight 'economist' versus 'historian' lines. Academic disciplines are becoming more fluid, and with this comes the hope of greater advances in our understanding of business. The presence and contributions of one practising banker, Tom Huertas, and one former investment banker, Brian Scott-Quinn, helped the meeting to keep its feet on the ground. The papers published in this volume were extensively rewritten in the light of the conference exchanges.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本书源于 1989 年 8 月 16 日至 19 日在英国雷丁大学召开的一次国际会议。如同 1983 年在佛罗伦萨欧洲大学研究所举办的关于多国制造业的早期会议（该会议促成了由彼得·赫特纳和我共同编辑的《跨国公司：理论与历史》一书）一样，雷丁会议汇集了经济学家和商业史学家，共同探讨一个共通的问题。与佛罗伦萨会议类似，会上存在相互冲突的观点和方法论，但讨论表明，这些分歧绝非简单地沿着“经济学家”与“历史学家”的阵营划分。学术领域的界限正变得愈发模糊，这为我们在理解商业方面取得更大进展带来了希望。一位从业银行家汤姆·韦尔塔斯和一位前投资银行家布莱恩·斯科特-奎因的出席与贡献，帮助会议始终立足于现实。 本卷收录的论文根据会议交流内容进行了大幅改写。</span></span></span>

In addition to the essays published here, Professor Rondo Cameron (Emory) and Mr T. A. B. Corley (Reading) presented fine papers which are being published, in different formats, elsewhere. Professor Leslie Pressnell (Kent) chaired several sessions with consummate skill, while Paul Campayne, John Cantwell and Takeshi Yuzawa made valuable contributions to the discussions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">除本书所刊载的论文外，隆多·卡梅隆教授（埃默里大学）和 T. A. B. 科利先生（雷丁大学）也提交了出色的论文，这些论文将以不同形式在其他地方发表。莱斯利·普雷斯内尔教授（肯特大学）以精湛的技巧主持了数场会议，而保罗·坎佩恩、约翰·坎特韦尔和汤泽武则在讨论中做出了宝贵贡献。</span></span></span>

The Reading conference was made possible by grants of £1,000 each from the Nuffield Foundation and the Economic and Social Research Council, and over £3,000 from the Japan Foundation. Their willingness to help is enormously appreciated. The Economics Department at the University of Reading helped in several ways, including the provision of an unpaid taxi service. Numerous banks declined to sponsor such an 'academic' meeting. As a result many of the non-British participants had to appeal to their own institutions for funding for travel, or - in two cases - meet the costs <span id="Preface.xhtml_page_xii"></span>from their own pockets. The bizarre attack on the funding (and morale) of British universities in the 1980s, combined with the understandable unwillingness of private industry to meet resulting shortfalls, has reduced British academics to the status of paupers of the western world in terms of research funds, and made the holding of quality international conferences immensely difficult. This book is dedicated to the non-British colleagues who made sacrifices of time and money to attend the Reading conference, thereby bringing succour to their British colleagues under siege.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">雷丁会议得以举办，得益于纳菲尔德基金会和经济与社会研究理事会各捐赠 1000 英镑，以及日本基金会捐赠超过 3000 英镑。我们万分感激他们的慷慨支持。雷丁大学经济系提供了多种帮助，包括无偿提供出租车服务。许多银行拒绝赞助这种“学术性”会议。因此，许多非英国参会者不得不向自己所在的机构申请差旅费，或者——在两位参会者的情况下——自掏腰包。20 世纪 80 年代对英国大学经费（及士气）的离奇打击，加之私营企业不愿填补由此产生的资金缺口，使得英国学者在研究经费方面沦为西方世界的乞丐，举办高质量的国际会议也变得异常困难。 本书献给那些牺牲时间和金钱参加雷丁会议的非英国同事们，他们为陷入困境的英国同行提供了援助。</span></span></span>
