Introduction<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">引言</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Studies of financial services firms as multinationals<sup>2</sup> have traditionally concentrated on commercial banking with little attention being paid to investment banks. One possible reason for this relative lack of academic interest is that this industry sector has traditionally been organized through private partnerships, which are secretive about their operations compared with manufacturing companies or commercial banks. In contrast with commercial banks, which started to become joint stock companies around 1860, it was not until 1960 that investment banks started to become public companies. A second reason is that the size of the whole industry in the United States in terms of capital employed has until recently been less than that of any one of the largest US commercial banks. This is because commercial banks employ their own equity, debt and deposits to enable them to act as principals in their transactions with lenders and borrowers, while investment banks have traditionally acted as brokers. A third reason is that only in the 1980s has the securities industry come to the attention of the world outside Wall Street and the City of London, mainly as a result of the extent to which securities market transactions during this period have substituted for the deposit and loan transactions of commercial banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对金融服务公司作为跨国公司的研究 <sup>2</sup> 传统上聚焦于商业银行，而对投资银行关注甚少。学术界对此兴趣相对缺乏的一个可能原因是，该行业传统上以私人合伙制运作，相比制造企业或商业银行，其运营更为隐秘。与大约 1860 年开始转型为股份制公司的商业银行不同，投资银行直到 1960 年才陆续成为上市公司。另一个原因是，就所用资本规模而言，美国整个投资银行业直到最近仍不及任何一家最大型美国商业银行的体量。 这是因为商业银行利用自有资本、债务和存款，以主体身份与借贷双方进行交易，而投资银行传统上只扮演经纪人角色。第三个原因是，直至 20 世纪 80 年代，证券行业才引起华尔街和伦敦金融城以外世界的关注——这主要得益于该时期证券市场交易在很大程度上替代了商业银行的存贷款业务。</span></span></span>

The previous chapter in this book, by Huertas, while a study of one particular commercial bank, also explores the reasons for US commercial banks entering securities markets and for securities firms becoming multinational. While these chapters were prepared independently, there are many parallels in them. The framework of analysis — the internalization model — is the same. Citibank's move from correspondent relationships to its own overseas subsidiaries and branches parallels investment banks' move from commission-splitting arrangements with overseas brokers to own offices and <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_269"></span>branches overseas. Huertas notes that very few US banks (or overseas banks) were multinational until after 1960 and the reasons for the change he relates to macroeconomic, regulatory and microeconomic factors. This chapter also dates the start of the multinationalization of investment banks to 1960 and for similar reasons, but also sees 1980 as the start of a new period of 'globalization and integration' in investment banking, something not paralleled in the Huertas paper. Finally, the emphasis he places on the need for high throughput of product relative to inventory in a financial engineering operation is exactly parallel to the need for high throughput in a securities market making operation. In both cases this is an important factor generating the need to maximize customer volume by tapping business globally. When allied with the increasing demand for cross-border services in banking and securities, it becomes clear that there are many types of financial service that can only be provided economically by a multinational.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本书前一章由 Huertas 撰写，虽聚焦于一家特定商业银行，却也探讨了美国商业银行涉足证券市场以及证券公司走向跨国经营的原因。尽管各章独立成篇，但其中存在诸多相通之处。分析框架——内部化模型——是相同的。花旗银行从代理行关系转向设立自己的海外子公司和分行，这与投资银行从与海外经纪人分享佣金模式转向设立自己的海外办事处和分行的路径如出一辙。Huertas 指出，1960 年之前，极少有美国银行（或海外银行）实现跨国经营，而他将这一转变的原因归结为宏观经济、监管政策和微观经济因素。 本章同样将投资银行跨国化的起点定于 1960 年，基于类似原因，但也认为 1980 年是投资银行进入“全球化与整合”新阶段的开始，这一观点在 Huertas 的论文中并未出现。最后，他强调金融工程业务中对产品吞吐量相对于库存的高需求，与证券市场做市业务中对高吞吐量的需求如出一辙。在这两种情况下，这都是一个关键因素，促使企业必须通过全球业务来最大化客户规模。 当与银行业和证券业对跨境服务日益增长的需求相结合时，显而易见，有许多类型的金融服务只有跨国企业才能以经济的方式提供。</span></span></span>

The focus of this chapter is on firms that are classified by the SEC as investment banks.<sup>3</sup> In addition, however, we will be looking at the overseas investment banking subsidiaries of US commercial banks principally to see if their motivations for FDI differ from those of the investment banks. These subsidiaries are able to undertake overseas corporate securities market transactions that would be illegal for them in the United States under the Securities Acts (in particular the Glass Steagall Act). We will concentrate on activities in London, which was the overseas centre to which US investment banks were first attracted.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本章重点聚焦于被美国证券交易委员会（SEC）归类为投资银行的公司。 <sup>3</sup> 此外，我们还将考察美国商业银行的海外投资银行子公司，主要目的是探究它们的对外直接投资动机是否与投资银行有所差异。这些子公司有能力在海外从事企业证券市场交易，而根据美国证券法（特别是《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》），这类交易在美国境内是违法的。我们将重点关注伦敦的业务活动，因为这里是美国投资银行最初被吸引的海外中心。</span></span></span>

Concepts and definitions<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">概念与定义</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Investment banks are concerned principally with effecting securities transactions for their corporate and investor clients, thereby contributing towards the creation of public securities markets. In examining the structure of firms in the industry therefore, a principal focus of our attention will be on the nature of the costs involved in making markets and the means by which firms can minimize these in order to be more competitive.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">投资银行的核心业务是为企业和投资者客户执行证券交易，从而促进公开证券市场的形成。因此，在分析行业内公司结构时，我们的主要关注点将集中在做市过程中涉及的成本性质，以及企业为提升竞争力而最小化这些成本的方式。</span></span></span>

Internalization is one of the principal means by which firms in manufacturing industry minimize transactions costs.<sup>4</sup> Internalization means co-ordinating related activities within a single firm rather than through market transactions. In the case of international activities, internalization means co-ordinating activities in different countries within one firm through a management structure rather than through arm's-length transactions between <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_270"></span>independent firms.<sup>5</sup> Multinational enterprises are simply collections of firms in different countries under common ownership. Transactions between them thus become internal rather than market transactions. In the case of investment banks, our objective is to demonstrate that the extension of the scope and geographical spread of their activities has been a response to the need to compete in terms of price, speed and quality of service. Internalization has been the means by which they have improved their competitiveness in all three areas.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">内部化是制造业企业最小化交易成本的主要手段之一。 <sup>4</sup> 内部化意味着在单一企业内部协调相关活动，而非通过市场交易来完成。对于国际业务而言，内部化则是通过管理结构在同一企业内部协调不同国家的活动，而不是依赖独立企业之间的保持距离型交易。 <sup>5</sup> 跨国企业不过是不同国家企业的集合体，受共同所有权的控制。它们之间的交易因此成为内部交易，而非市场交易。就投资银行而言，我们的目标是证明，其业务范围与地理布局的扩展是对价格、速度及服务质量竞争需求的回应。而内部化正是它们在这三个领域提升竞争力的途径。</span></span></span>

### What investment banks do<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">投资银行做什么</span></span></span>

The range of activities of the twenty-one large investment banks in the SEC study differ widely. However, there are core activities that all are involved in and which clearly distinguish them from commercial banks. Investment banking involves assessing market risk, finding investors and minimizing the time for which assets are held on a balance sheet as distinct from traded. Deposit or commercial banking on the other hand involves seeking deposits, assessing credit risk and using deposits to make loans to build up a balance sheet. In investment banking, the objective is to maximize asset turnover and control <span class="italic">market</span> risk, while in commercial banking the objective is to maximize balance sheet size subject to controlling the level of <span class="italic">credit</span> risk. Given the difference in asset turnover rates, it is not surprising that commercial banks have for many decades been highly capitalized public companies while securities firms have historically been private partnerships with limited capital. One of the questions we shall examine is why, in the 1960s and 1970s, securities houses have experienced the need to raise additional capital either by dissolving their partnership status or by accepting an investment from an outside firm.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">SEC 研究中的 21 家大型投资银行活动范围差异很大。然而，所有投行都涉及一些核心活动，这些活动明显将它们与商业银行区分开来。投资银行业务涉及评估市场风险、寻找投资者以及尽可能缩短资产在资产负债表上的持有时间（而非交易时间）。而存款或商业银行业务则涉及吸收存款、评估信用风险，并利用存款发放贷款以扩大资产负债表。在投资银行中，目标是最大化资产周转率并控制市场风险；而在商业银行中，目标是在控制信用风险水平的前提下最大化资产负债表规模。鉴于资产周转率的差异，商业银行几十年来一直是高资本化的上市公司，而证券公司历史上则是资本有限的私人合伙制，这并不令人意外。 我们将探讨的一个问题是，为什么在 20 世纪 60 年代和 70 年代，证券公司纷纷感到有必要筹集额外资本，要么通过解散合伙制，要么通过接受外部公司的投资。</span></span></span>

The major activity of investment banks is their involvement in either primary or secondary securities markets or in both.<sup>6</sup> These remain roles distinct from those of commercial banks (<span class="italic">qua </span>commercial banks) and are the ones that we shall be examining in this paper. <span class="italic">Primary market</span> activity involves arranging the flotation of new securities issues in the primary market for corporate, sovereign and public sector customers (henceforth called simply corporates). Two types of new securities issue are arranged. The Initial Public Offering (IPO) and the secondary offering, i.e. one which is simply adding to the existing stock of securities. <span class="italic">Secondary market</span> activity involves the provision of liquidity to investors by participation in secondary markets either as broker (agent) or as dealer (principal) or both (broker/dealer).<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">投资银行的主要业务活动是参与一级证券市场、二级证券市场，或两者兼营。 <sup>6</sup> 这些角色与商业银行（作为商业银行）的职能截然不同，也是本文将重点探讨的领域。一级市场业务包括为企业、主权机构和公共部门客户（以下统称企业）安排新证券在一级市场发行上市。发行的新证券分为两类：首次公开募股（IPO）和增发，即仅增加现有证券存量。二级市场业务则通过以经纪商（代理人）、自营商（主事人）或两者兼营（经纪/自营商）的身份参与二级市场，为投资者提供流动性。</span></span></span>

### <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_271"></span>The processes involved<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">相关流程</span></span></span>

There are three principal processes involved in providing primary and secondary market services, as shown in [Figure 14.1](#Ch014.xhtml_cap_lbl0003).<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如图 14.1 所示，提供一级市场和二级市场服务主要涉及三个流程。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">Origination</span> involves developing contact with corporate clients, the arrangement of the terms of the contract between the corporate and the final investors, and the formation of a syndicate which will provide for the distribution of the issue. An origination house need only have one office in the major financial centre of a country.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">证券承销发起工作包括：与机构客户建立联系、安排机构与最终投资者之间的合同条款，以及组建负责证券分销的承销团。承销发起机构通常只需在国家的核心金融中心设立一个办事处即可。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">Distribution</span> involves continuous contact with potential investors in securities and the sale of securities to them when new issue merchandise is made available through a syndicate or, in the case of an integrated house, from the origination department. In order to maintain continuous contact and knowledge of investor requirements a distribution house also provides secondary market services to investors. Since investors are widely scattered, distribution houses have a chain of offices. The distribution side will normally also provide research, which is intended to indicate when the 'true' value of securities differs from their current market price.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">证券承销分销工作则涉及与潜在证券投资者的持续对接，并在承销团（若为综合型机构，则来自其发起部门）推出新发行证券时向投资者销售这些证券。为保持与投资者的持续联系并掌握其需求，分销机构还需为投资者提供二级市场服务。由于投资者分布广泛，分销机构通常设有连锁办事处网络。分销部门通常还提供研究服务，旨在揭示证券的"真实价值"与其当前市场价格之间的差异。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">Market making</span> involves providing the liquidity that is central to the operation of a secondary market. Given that there is frequently no double co-incidence of wants in time or deal size between buyers and sellers of securities, the trading house bridges the time and size gap between them by taking principal positions (long and<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

### Specialization or integration in the industry<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">行业专业化或整合</span></span></span>

Historically each of these activities was undertaken by a specialized intermediary. Origination was provided by what has always been called 'investment banks'. Distribution was handled by so called 'wire houses', i.e. nationwide offices linked together by telegraph. Professional trading was handled by trading houses in New York, whose skilf was in making markets, and by 'specialists' on the floor of the NYSE. Overseas distribution was undertaken by foreign houses with whom US firms had relationships similar to 'correspondent banking' relationships in the commercial bank environment.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">历史上，这些活动各自由专门的中介机构承担。项目发起由一直被称为“投资银行”的机构负责，分销则由所谓的“电报行”处理——即通过电报连接全国各地的办事处网络。专业交易由纽约的交易行负责，其专长在于做市，以及纽交所交易大厅的“专家”们。海外分销则由与美国公司存在类似商业银行环境中“代理行”关系的海外机构完成。</span></span></span>

Primary and secondary market activities are closely related, and they are thus frequently considered to be joint products. This arises from the fact that the client base for the distribution side of the primary market is likely to be the same client base as for the sales team on the secondary market side, since most investors participate in both primary and secondary markets. Also, as we have noted, prices for secondary offerings in the primary market are based on prices at which existing securities are trading in the secondary market. Thus there is a <span class="italic">prima facie</span> reason to believe that there may be benefits from integration of primary and secondary market activities.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">一级市场与二级市场活动密切相关，因此常被视为联合产品。这是因为一级市场分销端的客户群体与二级市场销售团队的客户群体很可能高度重合——大多数投资者同时参与一级和二级市场。此外，如前所述，一级市场中二次发行的价格是基于二级市场上现有证券的交易价格。因此，有初步理由相信，整合一级与二级市场活动可能带来协同效益。</span></span></span>

Closer examination suggests, however, that the links between the two activities are only at the distribution and sales level. Origination is not a joint product with a sales and trading operation except for the pricing input that trading can provide. In practice we find that since origination and distribution are two such different activities, with different personnel and a different client base, integrated investment banks are split into two sections — the buy side and the sell side. The buy (or origination) side transacts with corporates, while the sell (or distribution) side transacts with institutional investors.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，进一步观察表明，这两项活动之间的联系仅存在于分销和销售层面。除了交易部门能提供定价参考外，发起业务并非与销售及交易业务共同产生的产品。实践中我们发现，由于发起和分销是两种截然不同的活动，涉及不同的人员和客户群，综合性投资银行通常分为两个部门——买方和卖方。买方（或发起方）与企业客户进行交易，而卖方（或分销方）则与机构投资者打交道。</span></span></span>

The trading (market making) function differs from origination or distribution in that it is one which can be provided either within each separate firm, i.e. fragmented trading in an over-the-country (OTC) telephone market or consolidated in a central market place (floor trading). In the UK, the Stock Market prior to Big Bang was a central market place to which brokers brought their orders. Post <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_273"></span>Big Bang, the market has become fragmented, i.e. all but a few brokers now have in-house market making, though each house is linked to the others through screens and telephones.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">交易（做市）功能与发行或分销不同，它既可以在各公司内部独立进行（即场外电话市场的分散化交易），也可以整合至中央市场（场内交易）。在英国，"大爆炸"改革前的股票市场是经纪人提交指令的中央市场；而改革后市场走向分散化——除少数经纪人外，多数公司均内置了做市功能，尽管各公司通过屏幕和电话系统相互联接。</span></span></span>

### Breadth, depth and efficiency in market making<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">做市业务的广度、深度与效率</span></span></span>

Efficiency in a market is measured as total trading cost. Trading costs are classified either as explicit transaction costs or as implicit execution costs. Explicit transaction costs are visible and easily measured; they include commissions and taxes. Implicit execution costs are not easily measured; they exist because orders may, as a result of their size and/or sparsity of counterpart orders on the market, execute at relatively high prices (if they are buy orders) or at relatively low prices (if they are sell orders).<sup>7</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">市场效率的衡量标准是总交易成本。交易成本可分为显性交易成本和隐性执行成本。显性交易成本直观可见且易于衡量，包括佣金和税费。隐性执行成本难以测算，这类成本源于订单可能因其规模较大或市场上对手盘稀少，导致以相对较高的价格成交（若为买单）或以相对较低的价格成交（若为卖单）。 <sup>7</sup> </span></span></span>

The function of the market making department in an integrated firm is to balance risk and reward on the trading book in order to provide immediacy to clients with low implicit execution cost and minimum risk to the firm, Two factors that give rise to low transactions cost, immediacy and minimum risk to the market marker are the depth and breadth of the in-house market that the firm can create from its own client base. Depth is measured as the number of buy and sell orders that the firm has available to it at prices around the last transactions price, i.e. the supply and demand schedules of traders (investors) immediately available to the market maker. Market depth should lead to low execution costs. Breadth is the availability to a market maker of traders with different expectations and different motives for wanting to hold a particular security. Market breadth should further reduce execution costs and also reduce volatility.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">综合性公司中做市部门的核心职能，是在交易账簿上平衡风险与收益，从而为客户提供即时流动性，同时将隐性执行成本降至最低，并将公司面临的风险控制在最小范围内。降低交易成本、提供即时性并减轻做市商风险的两大关键因素，在于公司能够基于自身客户群构建的内部市场的深度与广度。深度，指的是公司在最近成交价附近可获取的买卖订单数量，即做市商可即时调用的交易者（投资者）供需盘口。市场深度充足有助于降低执行成本。广度，则是指做市商能否接触到持有不同预期、出于不同动机想要持有某特定证券的交易者。市场广度不仅能进一步降低执行成本，还能有效减少行情波动。</span></span></span>

Depth and breadth in a market making department mean that the firm will generally be able to match orders coming in on one side very quickly with clients who are willing to take the other side of the deal. Thus if the house (investment bank) sells merchandise to one client at the offer price, it can buy it in from another at the bid price or only slightly more within a very short time.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">做市部门的深度和广度意味着，这家公司通常能够迅速将一方的订单，与愿意承接另一方的客户进行匹配。因此，如果投行以卖出价向一位客户出售产品，它可以在极短时间内以买入价或略高一点的价格从另一位客户手中购入。</span></span></span>

The ability to do this is a function of the size of the sales team, the depth and breadth of their client base and their ability to know which clients might be interested in acquiring particular positions. This in turn is in part a function of having been involved in the primary distribution and hence knowing who currently owns the securities.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这种能力取决于销售团队的规模、客户基础的深度和广度，以及他们判断哪些客户可能对特定头寸感兴趣的能力。而这又在某种程度上取决于是否参与了首次发行，从而了解当前谁持有这些证券。</span></span></span>

If the firm cannot complete the deal quickly in-house it can either hold the position in inventory until another client comes along with the opposite side of the transaction, or it can close the <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_274"></span>position in the professional (inter-dealer) marketplace. If it does the latter, however, it is likely to have to pay the offer price to buy the security, and thus will not make any 'turn' on the transaction. Alternatively, if it waits until one of its own customers is able to unwind the deal, it has a carrying cost of holding inventory (the difference between the running yield and the cost of funds) and also a considerable price risk. Thus not only do the high fixed financial costs of a market making operation mean that a high throughput is necessary to keep down average running cost, but also the production process itself needs a very high throughput of transactions to minimize implicit trading costs and risk for the market maker.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">若该公司无法在内部迅速完成交易，则可将该头寸作为库存持有，直至遇到另一方向相反的客户，或在专业交易商间市场平仓。若选择后者，该公司很可能需按卖价买入证券，因而无法从该交易中获取任何价差收益。反之，若等待自有客户完成平仓，则需承担持有库存的成本（即当期收益率与资金成本之差），同时面临相当大的价格风险。因此，做市业务的高固定财务成本不仅要求高交易量以降低平均运营成本，其生产流程本身也需要极高的交易吞吐量，以最小化做市商的内含交易成本与风险。</span></span></span>

Investors' supply and demand schedules are thus an input to the immediacy creation and transactions cost minimizing process. The availability of potential client bids and offers to the dealing desk of an OTC market maker of itself makes the process more efficient because of better matching opportunities and hence risk reduction, quite apart from any consideration of fixed costs. Thus the availability of clients with heterogenous expectations with whom the market maker has strong relationships is likely to be critical to achieving competitiveness both in terms of minimum transactions cost and also immediacy. Thus to be a low cost provider of immediacy the private in-house market has to try to reproduce some of the features of the public market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">因此，投资者的供需计划是即时性创造和交易成本最小化过程的输入信息。场外交易做市商交易台获取潜在客户的买卖报价，本身就能通过更好的匹配机会和风险降低（撇开固定成本不谈）使这一过程更加高效。因此，拥有与做市商关系密切且预期各异的客户群体，对于在最低交易成本和即时性两方面实现竞争力至关重要。因此，要成为低成本即时性提供者，内部私人市场必须尝试复制公开市场的某些特征。</span></span></span>

The argument for centralized public floor markets (order consolidation) has always been that such centralization overcomes this problem of insufficient depth and breadth available to any particular private market making house. Centralizing turns marketability services into a public good since the benefits in terms of lowered transactions cost and immediacy that result from a consolidated order flow are available to all comers. Thus there is a private benefit to each participant from the existence of every other trader's transactions, i.e. from the broadest and deepest possible market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">支持集中式公开市场（订单聚合）的理由一直在于，这种集中化能够解决任何特定私人做市商所面临的市场深度和广度不足的问题。集中化将可售性服务转化为一种公共产品，因为订单流聚合所带来的交易成本降低和即时性提升，对所有市场参与者都是可及的。因此，每个参与者都能从其他交易者的存在——即从尽可能广泛和深厚的市场中——获得私人利益。</span></span></span>

Despite this clear benefit, markets have increasingly become fragmented since the 1960s, The eurobond market, since it began in 1963, has always operated as an OTC market since the firms involved in it are geographically distributed around the world. The NYSE is de-centralized for 'block' transactions which take place 'upstairs' in the trading houses away from the specialist. As institutional investors have come to dominate NYSE trading at the expense of individuals, block trades have become the major part of the market and thus the specialist's role has diminished. In London, what was once a centralized market became fragmented <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_275"></span>at the time of Big Bang. It was further fragmented in February 1989 with the introduction of a rule which eliminated the need to disclose large orders to the market on the day of the transaction.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管这一优势显而易见，但自 20 世纪 60 年代以来，市场已日益碎片化。欧元债券市场自 1963 年诞生以来，始终以场外交易市场形式运作，因为参与其中的公司分布在全球各地。NYSE 的大宗交易采用去中心化模式，这些交易在远离专家的交易大厅“楼上”进行。随着机构投资者取代个人主导 NYSE 交易，大宗交易成为市场主力，专家的角色也随之减弱。在伦敦，曾一度集中的市场在“大爆炸”改革时走向碎片化。1989 年 2 月，一项规则的实施进一步加剧了碎片化——该规则取消了交易当日向市场披露大额订单的要求。</span></span></span>

There has been a worldwide trend away from centralized public markets towards private in-house markets. There are two forces driving this trend. The first is the decline in profitability from offering immediacy in competitive central markets where price information is freely available to traders (investors) electronically. The second is the increasing proportion of 'block trades' in many markets which are difficult to transact on public exchanges with minimum impact on price. These factors have forced firms to take steps to expand the depth and breadth of their client base in whatever countries may offer them potential business, and to try to develop a private in-house market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">全球范围内正出现从集中式公开市场向私有内部市场的转变趋势。这一趋势由两大驱动力促成：首先，在交易者（投资者）可通过电子方式自由获取价格信息的竞争性中央市场中，提供即时交易服务的盈利能力正在下降；其次，许多市场中的"大宗交易"占比持续攀升，这类交易若在公开交易所进行，很难将价格影响降至最低。这些因素迫使企业采取措施，在可能带来业务机会的任何国家扩展客户基础的深度与广度，并尝试构建私有内部市场。</span></span></span>

The globalization of financial markets and intermediaries<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">金融市场与中介机构的全球化</span></span></span>
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Traditionally, financial markets in different countries have been segmented as a result of the high costs of international transactions, due to regulatory constraints and cartels and information assymetries between dealers in different countries which limited the extent of investor arbitrage. There were consequently considerable inefficiencies across markets. The 1960s and 1970s saw increasing cross-border financial activity as investors began to explore the possibilities of overseas investment. But it was not until the 1980s that markets became increasingly global and integrated as transactions costs fell and transactions volumes increased. Transactions cost has been falling sharply partly as a result of deregulation and partly from improving communications and computer technology which has facilitated international arbitrage.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">传统上，由于国际交易成本高昂，加上监管限制、卡特尔以及不同国家交易商之间的信息不对称，限制了投资者的套利空间，各国金融市场长期处于分割状态。因此，不同市场之间存在严重的低效现象。20 世纪 60 年代和 70 年代，随着投资者开始探索海外投资的可能性，跨境金融活动日益增多。但直到 80 年代，随着交易成本下降、交易量上升，市场才逐渐走向全球化和一体化。交易成本的急剧下降，一方面源于放松管制，另一方面得益于通信和计算机技术的进步，这两者共同促进了国际套利活动的发展。</span></span></span>

The fact that markets have become global and integrated does not of itself imply that the intermediaries which offer services to investors and borrowers in these markets need operate in more than one country. Every type of service which is available from these markets can be supplied by a financial firm in the same country as the investor or borrower and can be accessed through <span class="italic">trade</span> in financial services. Indeed, financial services are, <span class="italic">par excellence,</span> the type of value-adding activity that can, with modern technology, be transported at virtually no cost across national boundaries and would therefore seem to be highly suited in terms of cost minimization to be supplied by export rather than by local production. It is somewhat surprising therefore that from the 1960s to the present day, a period which saw the introduction of low cost communications technologies, US investment banks and <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_276"></span>the investment banking subsidiaries of US commercial banks also moved from being exporters to setting up or expanding their own facilities overseas.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">市场全球化与一体化本身并不意味着为这些市场中的投资者和借款人提供服务的中间机构必须在多个国家运营。这些市场提供的各类服务，均可由与投资者或借款人同国的金融企业提供，并通过金融服务贸易实现。事实上，金融服务作为一种增值活动，借助现代技术几乎可以零成本跨境传输，因此从成本最小化角度而言，通过出口而非本地生产来提供这类服务似乎极为合适。 因此，从 20 世纪 60 年代至今，这一时期见证了低成本通信技术的引入，美国投资银行及美国商业银行的投资银行子公司也从单纯的出口商转变为在海外设立或扩大自己的分支机构，这多少有些令人惊讶。</span></span></span>

We will look at this development in three historical stages:<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我们将分三个历史阶段来审视这一发展：</span></span></span>

1.  <span class="italic">Up to 1960,</span> investment banks relied principally on agency relationships similar in nature to correspondent banking relationships. As the period up to 1960 was prior to the development of the multinational investment bank, we only refer to it briefly.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">截至 1960 年，投资银行主要依赖代理关系，其性质类似于代理行关系。由于 1960 年之前的时期尚未出现跨国投资银行，我们仅简要提及。</span></span></span>
2.  <span class="italic">In the 1960s and 1970s</span> these firms set up sales branches or subsidiaries in London (or in a few instances, Paris or Geneva) to service existing foreign clients with US (domestic) investment products, i.e. US equities. They were thus simply replacing sales through overseas agents with sales through direct investment facilities. They then moved to producing low cost debt origination services for their existing US corporate clients when the eurobond market came into existence in London in the mid to late 1960s. At the same time, commercial banks which already had corporate banking activities in London set up eurobond origination subsidiaries to allow them to offer dollar bond origination services to their US corporate clients. While in the United States they could not offer bond origination, in London they could. They thus immediately came into competition with the investment banks for the first time.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在 20 世纪 60 至 70 年代，这些公司纷纷在伦敦（少数情况下为巴黎或日内瓦）设立销售分公司或子公司，以便向现有外国客户提供美国（本土）投资产品，即美股。此举实质上是用直接投资渠道的销售取代了海外代理销售。随后，当 60 年代中后期欧洲债券市场在伦敦兴起时，它们又开始为现有的美国企业客户提供低成本债券发行服务。与此同时，已在伦敦开展企业银行业务的商业银行也设立了欧洲债券发行子公司，从而能够向美国企业客户提供美元债券发行服务。虽然在美国本土它们无法从事债券发行，但在伦敦却可以。也正是从这时起，它们首次与投资银行展开了直接竞争。</span></span></span>
3.  <span class="italic">In the 1980s</span> the whole scale of foreign direct investment by investment banking institutions not only in London but also in Tokyo increased quite dramatically. In eurobonds, there was a large upstream investment in global distribution to replace the large syndicates that had been used before. In Treasury bonds, there was downstream investment in market making in London and Tokyo, which involved large oudays not only on people, equipment and software and buildings, but also on inventory. But most dramatic of all was the decision by many US investment and commercial banks to enter completely new territory by commencing market making and distribution in foreign domestic equity and bond markets. Finally, during this period, these various operations which had in many cases been relatively autonomous, were increasingly integrated with each other worldwide and with the parent company.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
    <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 80 年代，投资银行机构不仅在伦敦，在东京的外国直接投资规模也大幅增长。在欧洲债券领域，为了取代过去使用的大型银团，大量上游投资涌入全球分销网络。而在国债领域，伦敦和东京则出现了下游投资用于做市业务，这不仅涉及人员、设备、软件和楼宇的大额支出，还包括库存投入。但最引人注目的，当属许多美国投资银行和商业银行决定进军全新领域——开始在本土以外的外国股票和债券市场开展做市与分销业务。最后，在这一时期，许多原本相对独立的业务板块在全球范围内日益与母公司整合在一起。</span></span></span>

Given the central importance of transactions cost in determining the competitiveness of transactions-oriented investment banks, we <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_277"></span>will be looking at the phenomenon of overseas direct investment as the means by which these firms have been able to reduce transactions costs and increase immediacy for both domestic and overseas clients and thus increase their competitiveness.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">鉴于交易成本对于以交易为导向的投资银行竞争力具有核心意义，我们将把境外直接投资视为这些企业降低交易成本、提升对国内外客户的服务即时性，从而增强竞争力的关键手段。</span></span></span>

We will examine the progression of the representative firm in the industry from being single capacity (broker, market maker or originator), to being integrated across all three, then to being multinational and finally global. We will look also at the successive types of firm organization involved — the private partnership and the public company.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我们将考察行业中代表性企业的发展路径：从单一职能（经纪商、做市商或发起人），到整合全部三种职能，再到跨国经营，最终走向全球化。同时，我们还会审视所涉及的企业组织形式的演变——私人合伙制与公众公司。</span></span></span>

US securities houses up to 1960<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1960 年之前的美国证券商行</span></span></span>

Many US investment banks that are dominant today, can trace their origins back to the originating firms which dominated the great railroad financings in the nineteenth century. Some of these were partnerships of Jewish financiers who, unlike the Yankee houses, often enjoyed the business advantages of extensive family ties around the world.<sup>8</sup> There were also numerous joint ventures between European and US firms. In the early nineteenth century the need to fund public debt resulted in US bankers going to London, which proved a good source of capital for US needs.<sup>9</sup> However, only a few US houses had an office in London itself, the best known of which was J.S. Morgan.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">当今许多占主导地位的美国投资银行，其起源可追溯至 19 世纪主导铁路大融资的创始公司。其中一些由犹太金融家合伙经营，与洋基家族不同，他们往往因拥有遍布全球的庞大家族关系网而享有商业优势。 <sup>8</sup> 欧美公司之间也有诸多合资企业。19 世纪初，为筹措公共债务，美国银行家前往伦敦——事实证明，伦敦是满足美国资金需求的绝佳来源。 <sup>9</sup> 然而，当时仅有少数几家美国公司在伦敦设有办事处，其中最著名的是 J.S.摩根公司。</span></span></span>

Just as primary distributions were generally undertaken by cooperative arrangements between firms in different countries, until the beginning of the 1960s so also were secondary market transactions. The traditional way of doing this was through an arm's length relationship between a US investment bank (<span class="italic">qua</span> broker) and a broker in the country of the investor. Thus, for example, an investor in London would ask his own broker to obtain certain shares for him and his broker in turn would approach a US broker with whom he had a relationship and arrange to buy the securities. The brokers would normally have a commission-splitting arrangement. The London broker might also give his client access to the US broker's research.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正如在 20 世纪 60 年代之前，一级市场发行通常由不同国家企业之间的合作安排进行，二级市场交易同样如此。传统的做法是通过美国投资银行（作为经纪商）与投资者所在国的经纪商之间保持公平交易关系。例如，伦敦的投资者会请自己的经纪商代购某些股票，而该经纪商随后会联系与之有合作关系的美国经纪商，安排购买这些证券。双方经纪商通常会达成佣金分成协议。伦敦经纪商还可能让客户获取美国经纪商的研究报告。</span></span></span>

The process was quite effective but relatively slow in terms of information flow. There was also no certainty as to the price at which the securities would be acquired given the problems of communication until the 1960s. Thus in terms of transactions cost and immediacy, the procedure did not score highly.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">整个过程效率还算不错，但在信息流通方面却相对缓慢。此外，由于 20 世纪 60 年代之前通信条件受限，证券的最终收购价格也无法确定。因此，从交易成本和即时性角度来看，这一流程的表现并不理想。</span></span></span>

### The US domestic securities market<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">美国国内证券市场</span></span></span>

The 1960s and 1970s were an era when the structure of the investment banking industry in the United States changed dramatically. This change is well documented by Hayes<sup>10</sup> who notes that this period saw integration by brokers back to trading and origination, which coincided with those firms going public in order to replace 'nervous insider funds' with permanent public investors' money.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 60 至 70 年代是美国投资银行业结构发生巨变的时期。这一变化在 Hayes <sup>10</sup> 的著作中得到了充分记录，他指出这一时期，经纪商开始向后整合至交易和承销环节，同时这些公司纷纷上市，用永久性的公众投资者资金取代了"不安的内部人资金"。</span></span></span>

### Securities markets overseas<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">海外证券市场</span></span></span>

#### (a) Investor services<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">(a) 投资者服务</span></span></span>

The first question to be answered is why US investment banks did not continue to use agents overseas. The second question is why, when they did set up offices overseas, they did not undertake market making, which it might be thought would allow them to offer a much better service to their foreign clients in terms of immediacy.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">首先需要回答的问题是，为什么美国投资银行不再继续在海外使用代理。其次，当它们在海外设立办事处后，为何没有开展做市业务——按理说这能让它们为外国客户提供更具即时性的优质服务。</span></span></span>

In terms of explicit transactions cost (i.e. commissions and taxes), there is little reason to believe that any great reduction in cost would result simply from setting up an overseas office. The commission level would be the same and the taxes, etc. would be unchanged. In terms of implicit transactions cost, this would be unchanged too since the actual trading would still be carried on in New York. Thus we rule out a reduction in explicit or implicit transactions cost as being primary motivating factors.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">就显性交易成本（即佣金和税费）而言，几乎没有理由相信仅通过设立海外办事处就能大幅降低成本。佣金水平保持不变，税费等也不会有任何变化。至于隐性交易成本，由于实际交易仍将在纽约进行，因此同样不会发生改变。因此，我们排除了降低显性或隐性交易成本作为主要驱动因素的可能性。</span></span></span>

Why then did they set up overseas offices? Given that an investor has already made the decision to acquire foreign equities, the principal feature distinguishing the 'product' of different suppliers is the quality of service (principally advice) on which equities to buy and speed of information flow. It was never possible to achieve speedy information flow through agents. Furthermore, the quality of advice and general service is encapsulated in the purchaser's eyes in the brand name of the company which supplies the service to him.<sup>11</sup> If the agent provided a very high quality of service under his own name, the goodwill from providing the service would accrue to the agent. He would thus become the stronger partner in the arrangement with the US investment bank and could set the terms of trade between them in this intermediate product market. Conversely, if the US investment bank's trade name was used, he would be unlikely to want to push <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_279"></span>this name from a brand point of view since it would reduce his bilateral negotiating power with the US partner. The only solution to this bilateral negotiating problem is for the US partner to set up his own facility in order to capture all the economic rent that accrues from the creation of a brand identity. Thus by internalizing the international market link, he can achieve a higher rate of profitability on his overseas sales. While this problem existed well before the 1960s, the volume of such transactions before then was limited. In addition, the capital constraints that existed before the majority of investment banks became public companies in the 1960s and 1970s encouraged them to limit capital expenditure overseas.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">那么，他们为何还要设立海外办事处呢？既然投资者已经决定购买外国股票，不同供应商所提供"产品"的主要区别就在于服务质量（主要是建议质量）以及信息流动速度。通过代理人来实现快速的信息流动向来不可能。此外，在购买者眼中，建议质量和整体服务水平都凝聚在为其提供服务的公司品牌上。 <sup>11</sup> 如果代理人以自己的名义提供非常高质量的服务，那么提供服务所产生的商誉就会归属于代理人。这样，代理人就会在与美国投资银行的合作中成为更强势的一方，并能在中间产品市场上设定双方之间的贸易条件。 相反，如果美国投资银行的商号被使用，他从品牌角度可能不愿推广这一名称，因为这会削弱他与美国合作伙伴的双边谈判能力。解决这一双边谈判问题的唯一办法，就是让美国合作伙伴自行设立机构，以便捕获品牌身份创造过程中产生的所有经济租金。因此，通过内化国际市场链条，他可以在海外销售中实现更高的利润率。尽管这一问题早在 20 世纪 60 年代之前就已存在，但此前此类交易的规模有限。此外，在 20 世纪 60 年代和 70 年代大多数投资银行上市之前，资本约束促使它们限制海外资本支出。</span></span></span>

Why did US investment banks not set up overseas market making, which is the analogue to production in a manufacturing firm? It is useful at this point to compare manufacturing and securities markets products. The principal difference is that in the case of the latter, production and purchase need to be simultaneous while in the former case they need not be. The time zone problem makes the servicing of overseas clients with the same quality of service as domestic clients impossible unless a market making operation is set up. This is because immediacy can only be provided by having a market making operation in the time zone where the securities are being sold. Otherwise immediacy can only be offered when European (or Japanese) trading hours overlap with those in the US. However, a market making operation cannot function efficiently at providing immediacy at relatively low volumes of activity. In the case of US stocks, although there was considerable demand across all securities, for any individual stock there was insufficient demand to make market making economic. If all US investment banks had co-operated it is possible that a market could have been set up in a small number of securities such as GM, IBM and GE. But without such a central marketplace to aggregate orders as there is on the New York Stock Exchange no single equity could generate adequate volume. There was thus insufficient market depth in Europe or Japan outside New York opening hours in the absence of a centralized exchange to allow efficient market making. Until certain other changes took place in the 1980s, therefore, US equity operations outside the US remained as sales offices rather than also becoming market making centres.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">为什么美国的投资银行没有像制造业企业设立生产部门那样，在海外开展做市业务？此时不妨比较一下制造业与证券市场产品的区别。主要区别在于，证券市场产品的生产与购买必须同时进行，而制造业产品则无需如此。时区问题使得海外客户无法享受到与国内客户同等质量的服务，除非设立做市业务。这是因为，只有在证券销售所在的时区设立做市业务，才能提供即时性。否则，只有在欧洲（或日本）交易时间与美洲交易时间重叠时，才能提供即时性。然而，做市业务在交易量较低时，难以高效地提供即时性。 就美股而言，虽然各类证券整体需求旺盛，但任何单只股票的需求都不足以让做市具备经济可行性。如果所有美国投资银行能够通力合作，或许能在通用汽车(GM)、IBM 和通用电气(GE)等少数股票上建立起市场。但由于缺乏像纽约证券交易所那样汇集订单的中央市场，单只股票无法产生足够的交易量。因此，在纽约证交所休市时段，欧洲或日本由于没有中央交易所，市场深度不足以支撑高效的做市。直到 1980 年代发生某些其他变化之前，美国在海外的股票业务仍停留在销售办事处层面，并未同时成为做市中心。</span></span></span>

#### (b) Corporate issuer services — the eurodollar bond market<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">(b) 公司发行人服务——欧洲美元债券市场</span></span></span>

The commencement of business in the eurobond market by US investment banks and the investment banking subsidiaries of <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_280"></span>commercial banks was driven initially neither by the idea of servicing US corporate clients nor investors. The eurobond market came into existence in London in 1963 started by UK merchant banks, brokers and US investment banks in the wake of the closure of the foreign dollar bond market in New York. This happened when the US government imposed a punitive tax — interest equalization tax — designed to stop foreigners using the New York capital markets.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">美国投资银行及商业银行旗下的投行部门最初涉足欧洲债券市场，既非为了服务美国企业客户，也非为了迎合投资者。1963 年，在纽约外国美元债券市场关闭的背景下，英国商人银行、经纪商以及美国投行在伦敦共同创立了欧洲债券市场。当时，美国政府为阻止外国利用纽约资本市场，征收了一项惩罚性税收——利息平衡税。</span></span></span>

As a result, the market in dollar bond issues for non-US borrowers moved to London and it was logical for US investment banks which already had offices there (US equity sales) to start trading dollar bonds and also to originate bond issues for (non-US) borrowers who previously used their services to access the New York bond market. US investment banks had no obvious competitive advantage operating in London and at this time did not dominate the market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">结果，非美国借款人的美元债券发行市场转移到了伦敦，而已经在伦敦设有办事处（从事美国股票销售业务）的美国投资银行，自然开始交易美元债券，并为那些过去曾利用其服务进入纽约债券市场的非美国借款人发起债券发行。不过，美国投资银行在伦敦运营并无明显竞争优势，当时也并未主导这一市场。</span></span></span>

But in 1968 the US authorities issued new rules on fund raising by US multinationals wishing to make investments overseas. From then on such investment had to be financed by raising funds outside the US. These rules, the Foreign Direct Investment Rules (FDIR), resulted in an immediate and very sharp increase in demand for <span class="italic">origination</span> services by US corporations outside the United States. Note, however, that the reason for US investment banks setting up London eurobond subsidiaries post-FDIR was not to service the subsidiaries of multinationals operating overseas. It was to service the head offices of US multinationals in the United States. In this respect it was quite different from the branches and subsidiaries set up by commercial banks abroad to service the local subsidiaries of US multinationals. Initially it was simply that because of the Interest Equalization Tax (IET), intermediating offshore was cheaper than intermediating onshore. However, it was quickly apparent that there were other reasons why intermediating offshore was cheaper. The principal one was that while coupon income on US domestic bonds suffered withholding tax, coupon income on eurobonds was free of withholding tax. Thus even a lower before tax yield on a eurobond compared with a domestic bond could still give a higher after tax yield to an investor who, for whatever reason, did not pay tax on his coupon income. In addition the securities were bearer rather than registered which was attractive to certain types of investor who wished to avoid drawing official attention to their wealth.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，1968 年美国当局对有意进行海外投资的跨国公司出台了新的融资规定。自此以后，这类投资必须通过在美国境外筹集资金来完成。这项《外国直接投资规则》（FDIR）一经实施，便立即导致美国企业对美国境外融资服务的需求急剧飙升。不过需注意，美国投资银行在 FDIR 出台后设立伦敦欧洲债券子公司，其目的并非是为运营海外的跨国公司子公司提供服务，而是为了服务位于美国本土的跨国公司总部。这一点与商业银行在国外设立分行和子公司以服务美国跨国公司当地子公司的做法截然不同。最初的原因很简单：由于《利息平衡税》（IET）的存在，在离岸进行金融中介操作比在岸更为划算。 但人们很快发现，离岸中介之所以成本更低，还有别的原因。最主要的一点是，美国国内债券的利息收入需要缴纳预扣税，而欧洲债券的利息收入则免于预扣税。因此，对于那些因各种原因无需为其利息收入纳税的投资者来说，即使欧洲债券的税前收益率低于国内债券，其税后收益率也可能更高。此外，这些证券采用的是不记名形式而非记名形式，这对于希望避免财富引起官方注意的某些投资者来说颇具吸引力。</span></span></span>

The market was thus driven principally by the lower funding cost that could be offered to US corporate treasurers if they raised their funds offshore, a situation analogous to that in the eurodollar market where offshore intermediation could provide a lower cost <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_281"></span>of funds as an input to eurodollar corporate banking than could domestic intermediation. It was not possible to use agents in the United Kingdom to provide origination services on behalf of US investment banks. The transactions cost would have made such deals uneconomic. The clients were principally from the US and all the documentation and historical knowledge of the client was in files and working knowledge of personnel in New York. As a result, much of the work on origination continued to be conducted in the US with a team flying over to Europe if necessary at the time of the actual issue.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">因此，市场的主要驱动力在于，美国企业财务主管若在离岸市场融资，可以获得更低的资金成本——这类似于欧洲美元市场的情况：离岸中介服务能为企业银行业务提供比国内中介更低的资金成本。但英国境内无法代理美国投资银行的发行业务，交易成本会使此类交易失去经济性。客户主要来自美国，所有客户档案资料和历史交易记录均保存在纽约，相关业务知识也掌握在当地员工手中。最终，大部分发行筹备工作仍在美国进行，仅在正式发行时，如有必要，团队会飞往欧洲完成实际操作。</span></span></span>

Although something of the order of 50 per cent of US corporate fixed-rate funding since 1968 has been carried out in the eurobond market rather than the domestic market, the eurobond market did not replace the domestic market any more than the eurodollar market replaced the domestic deposit market. The reason is that while arbitrage between the two markets by both investors and issuers results in yields remaining relatively close, the yield differential moves continually over time, being sometimes positive and sometimes negative. It varies because of the tendency for interest rates and the exchange rate to be correlated, which means that foreign (but not domestic) investors believe that the currency gain from rising interest rates is likely to offset the fall in the bond price as a result of rising interest rates (and vice versa). Also, companies of different credit rating may have comparative advantage in either the domestic or the eurobond market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管自 1968 年以来，美国企业固定利率融资中约有 50%是在欧洲债券市场而非国内市场完成的，但欧洲债券市场并未取代国内市场，正如欧洲美元市场并未取代国内存款市场一样。原因在于，尽管投资者和发行人在两个市场之间的套利行为导致收益率保持相对接近，但收益率差会随时间持续变动，有时为正，有时为负。这种差异之所以存在，是因为利率与汇率之间存在相关趋势，这意味着外国（而非国内）投资者认为，利率上升带来的货币收益很可能抵消因利率上升导致的债券价格下跌（反之亦然）。此外，不同信用评级的公司可能在国内市场或欧洲债券市场中拥有比较优势。</span></span></span>

While US banks undertook origination in London and many also engaged in professional market making, they did not invest in the facilities necessary for international distribution of securities. This important element in the chain of activities in primary market issues was effected through syndicates which were dominated by the distribution capability (placing power) of the Swiss and German universal banks. In this era it was clear that it was not economic to internalize distribution within a single US investment bank or even a syndicate of them.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">虽然美国银行在伦敦开展证券发行（origination）业务，不少银行也从事专业做市（market making），但它们并未投资于国际证券分销所需的设施。一级市场发行链条中的这一关键环节，实则由瑞士和德国全能银行（universal banks）主导的承销团（syndicates）来完成，这些银行凭借强大的分销能力（placing power）占据主导地位。在那个时代，显而易见的是，将分销环节内化（internalize distribution）到某一家美国投行甚至其组成的承销团内部，在经济上并不划算。</span></span></span>

<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 80 年代投资银行的全球整合</span></span></span>

Up to 1979/80, the US multinational investment bank had little more than a large office in London and perhaps some much smaller ones in other European countries, perhaps an Arab country and possibly (though less likely) Japan.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">截至 1979/80 年，美国跨国投资银行（multinational investment bank）在海外无非是在伦敦设有一个大型办事处，或许在其他欧洲国家、某个阿拉伯国家，甚至可能（但可能性较小）在日本，设有规模小得多的办事机构。</span></span></span>

From 1980 onward, the development of the US investment bank as a multinational changed qualitatively. 1979/80 was a watershed time in the political world. Two political leaders, <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_282"></span>Margaret Thatcher and President Reagan, both committed to the doctrine of monetarism as the means to cure inflation, and to greater use of markets to achieve their political objectives, acceded to power. The Federal Reserve was also committed to an activist interest rate policy. The outcome was a number of macroeconomic and regulatory changes which have greatly impacted the financial sector.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">自 1980 年起，美国投资银行作为跨国机构的发展发生了质变。1979/80 年正值政坛的分水岭时期。两位政治领袖——玛格丽特·撒切尔和里根总统——相继上台执政，他们都信奉货币主义理论，视其为治愈通胀的良方，并主张更多地借助市场来实现政治目标。与此同时，美联储也采取了积极的利率政策。这些举措引发了一系列宏观经济和监管层面的变革，对金融行业产生了深远影响。</span></span></span>

### Macroeconomic changes<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">宏观经济变化</span></span></span>

Both leaders instituted monetarist policies. The outcome was much higher interest rates in both countries and an inversion of dollar and sterling yield curves. The interest rate rise caused sharp falls in stock markets. The new activist policy, using interest rates to attempt to control money supply combined with rapidly changing inflation expectations resulted in highly volatile financial asset prices — interest rates, exchange rates, bond prices and stock prices.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">两国领导人都推行了货币主义政策。结果导致两国利率大幅攀升，美元与英镑的收益率曲线出现倒挂。利率上升引发股市急剧下跌。这一新的激进政策，试图通过利率来控制货币供应，再加上通胀预期瞬息万变，最终使得各类金融资产价格——利率、汇率、债券价格和股票价格——剧烈波动。</span></span></span>

The so called 'supply side' economics which was being followed in the US held that cutting taxes would increase the growth rate of the economy. An unintended outcome was a large increase in the size of the external deficit. At this time also, the authorities in both the US and the UK foreswore the use of active intervention in the foreign exchange markets. Thus the financing of payments imbalances was left principally to the private sector.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">美国当时奉行的所谓“供给侧”经济学认为，减税会提高经济增长率。但一个意想不到的结果是，外部赤字大幅膨胀。与此同时，美英两国当局都承诺不再对外汇市场进行积极干预。因此，国际收支失衡的融资主要落到了私人部门肩上。</span></span></span>

### Deregulation<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">放松管制</span></span></span>

The generation of a current account surplus in the UK in 1979 led to the abolition of exchange controls. Switzerland also abolished its exchange controls in 1979/80. In Japan, the Foreign Exchange Control Act, designed to concentrate foreign exchange in official hands, was amended in 1980. In 1981, Germany abolished restrictions on non-resident purchases of domestic bonds. In 1984, the US abolished withholding tax on interest paid to non-resident investors in government and corporate bonds, making them much more attractive to foreign investors.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1979 年英国经常账户出现盈余，导致外汇管制被废除。瑞士也在 1979/80 年度取消了外汇管制。日本于 1980 年修订了旨在将外汇集中于官方手中的《外汇管理法》。1981 年，德国取消了对非居民购买国内债券的限制。1984 年，美国废除了对非居民投资者持有政府及公司债券所获利息征收的预扣税，这使得这些债券对外国投资者更具吸引力。</span></span></span>

In the financial markets the UK took the lead in 1982 by starting the process of allowing foreigners to buy into Stock Exchange firms and started a process of deregulation which culminated in Big Bang in 1986. The increased competitiveness of the UK financial markets led to competitive pressure on other financial markets to deregulate. In a number of countries, <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_283"></span>regulations restricting the proportion of assets held by pension funds that could be invested in foreign securities were also eased.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在金融市场上，英国于 1982 年率先允许外国人购买证券交易所公司的股份，并启动了放松管制的进程，这一进程最终在 1986 年的“大爆炸”中达到高潮。英国金融市场竞争力的提升，给其他金融市场带来了放松管制的竞争压力。许多国家也放宽了限制养老基金投资外国证券资产比例的规定。</span></span></span>

### Outcome of changes in the 1980s<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 80 年代改革的结果</span></span></span>

The result of these macroeconomic and regulatory changes was to alter the type of product that was required by investors. The first important change was that as a result of financial asset price volatility, immediacy became ever more important both for borrowers and for investors. The secular fall in profits, however, suggests that investors were unwilling to pay for the additional immediacy in an environment where the industry was already starting to suffer from excess capacity.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">宏观经济和监管变革的结果，改变了投资者所需的产品类型。第一个重要变化是，由于金融资产价格波动，即时性对借款人和投资者都变得愈发重要。然而，利润的长期下降表明，在行业已经开始面临产能过剩的环境下，投资者不愿为额外的即时性买单。</span></span></span>

The second change was that institutional investors worldwide started to diversify their portfolios more substantially than in the past through purchases of foreign securities. This resulted first from the increasing freedom to take investment funds out of a number of countries, the freedom to invest in a number of countries and a growing belief in the benefits of international rather than simply domestic diversification. An increasing porportion of bond funds became multicurrency, while equity portfolios began to be structured around 'international equities'. Quite apart from the increasing proportion of investment funds worldwide, which were diversifying internationally, there was also an increasing realization that foreign portfolio investment had become both easier as a result of telecommunications improvements and cheaper because of the deregulation of financial markets.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">第二个变化是，全球机构投资者开始比以往更大力度地通过购买外国证券来分散投资组合。这首先源于多个国家对资金外流的管制逐步放宽，投资者得以更自由地将资金投向海外市场，与此同时，人们越来越相信国际化分散投资比单纯局限于国内市场更有益处。债券基金中多币种配置的比例日益上升，股票投资组合也开始围绕“国际股票”进行构建。除了全球范围内进行国际化分散投资的资金比例不断增长之外，人们也越来越意识到，由于电信技术的进步，外国证券投资变得更为便捷；而金融市场的放松管制，则进一步降低了相关成本。</span></span></span>

### Response to changes in the economic and regulatory environment<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">对经济及监管环境变化的回应</span></span></span>

In <span class="italic">eurobonds,</span> there has been forward integration from origination into global distribution. In US <span class="italic">Treasury bonds,</span> there has been backward integration in London and Tokyo from sales into trading. At the same time, there has been global integration of the operations in New York, Tokyo and London. In US equities, there has, at least for a few actively traded 'international' US issues, been integration back to trading. A quite different response to the changing environment has been the move by some firms to enter the domestic securities markets of other countries and to try to build up a new client base.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在欧元债券领域，已经从最初发行环节向前延伸至全球分销。而在美国国债方面，伦敦和东京市场则出现了从销售环节向后整合至交易的趋势。与此同时，纽约、东京和伦敦的业务实现了全球一体化。至少对于部分交易活跃的"国际"美国股票而言，其流程已回归到交易层面的整合。面对环境变化，另一种截然不同的应对策略是：一些公司选择进入其他国家的本土证券市场，试图建立全新的客户基础。</span></span></span>

In our view, this increasing integration of US firms in the 1980s can been seen principally as a competitive response to the declining margins brought on by deregulation (the converse of lower <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_284"></span>transactions costs for investors) and increasing volatility in financial asset prices.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我们认为，20 世纪 80 年代美国企业这种日益增强的整合趋势，本质上是对监管放松（对投资者而言即交易成本降低的反面）导致利润空间收窄、以及金融资产价格波动加剧所做出的竞争性回应。</span></span></span>

Investment banks compete on price, immediacy and 'quality'. It was necessary therefore for them to take steps that would lead to improvements in these three areas. We will now look at each type of integration in detail.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">投资银行在价格、即时性和"质量"方面相互竞争。因此，它们必须采取措施来改进这三个方面。接下来，我们将详细探讨每种整合方式。</span></span></span>

#### Eurobonds<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">欧洲债券</span></span></span>

Traditionally in New York, the origination and distribution of bond and stock issues had been carried out by separate types of institution. The integration between the various functions in the vertical chain from origination, through distribution to wholesale market making was through syndicates and arm's-length relationships. The investment banks were the originators. They restricted their activity to relationships with corporate clients (buy side). They structured and priced deals and arranged the syndicates. The selling was undertaken by the wire houses, which were the brokers with a nationwide selling organization with hundreds of local offices. Finally there were the trading houses in New York who provided professional market making services to brokers across the nation and to institutional investors.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在纽约，传统的债券和股票发行与分销由不同类型的机构分别承担。从发行、分销到批发做市这一垂直链条中的各项职能，通过银团和保持距离的公平交易关系实现整合。投资银行作为发起人，其业务局限于与企业客户（买方）建立关系，负责交易结构设计、定价及银团组建。销售则由拥有全国性销售网络且设有数百个地方办事处的电信经纪公司（即券商）负责。最后，纽约的交易商为全国券商及机构投资者提供专业做市服务。</span></span></span>

The strength of the US investment banks in London was that they had a strong corporate client base in their US clients and, in addition, generally had bond trading skills. What none of them had was much distribution capability in bonds outside the United States. But prior to 1980, this was not a great disadvantage. Investment funds for eurobonds came principally from Europe and oil producing countries. Distribution in Europe was undertaken mainly by the European universal banks — German, and particularly Swiss. The Swiss banks had a captive non-resident client base whose money was mainly managed on a discretionary basis. This allowed the Swiss banks to place securities of their own choice at full issue price into client portfolios. US investment banks had reasonably good distribution in OPEC countries based on salesmen in London, and in this market were at no particular disadvantage <span class="italic">vis-á-vis</span> European banks. From 1982 onwards, however, OPEC surpluses were declining and in some OPEC countries, reserves started to fall. Thus this source of demand effectively dried up.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">美国投资银行在伦敦的优势在于，它们拥有以美国客户为核心的强大企业客户基础，此外通常还具备债券交易技能。但它们普遍缺乏的是在美国本土之外的债券分销能力。不过，在 1980 年之前，这并非什么致命短板。欧洲债券的投资资金主要来自欧洲和产油国。欧洲的分销业务主要由德国的全能银行，尤其是瑞士银行承担。瑞士银行拥有一个受其掌控的非居民客户群体，这些客户的资金大多以全权委托方式进行管理。这使得瑞士银行能够以发行价将自行选定的证券纳入客户投资组合。美国投资银行在欧佩克国家拥有相当不错的分销渠道，依靠的是伦敦的销售人员，在这个市场上，它们相比欧洲银行并无明显劣势。 然而，从 1982 年起，OPEC 的盈余开始下降，一些 OPEC 国家的储备也开始减少。于是，这一需求来源实际上已经枯竭。</span></span></span>

At the same time, Japan was becoming an ever-growing source of funds. This resulted in most major US investment banks believing that they would need to enter the Tokyo market in order to tap the funds available in this centre.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">与此同时，日本正成为一个日益增长的资金来源。这使得大多数美国大型投资银行认为，它们需要进入东京市场，以获取这个金融中心的资金。</span></span></span>

From 1980 onwards US investment banks were forced to increase greatly their investment in their London subsidiaries. <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_285"></span>Prior to this, the use of the large syndicate for distribution had allowed the level of investment in London to be minimized. But in the 1980s, it became clear that integrated houses were more competitive than non-integrated.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从 1980 年开始，美国投资银行被迫大幅增加对其伦敦子公司的投资。在此之前，利用大型银团进行分销使得在伦敦的投资水平得以最小化。但到了 1980 年代，人们清楚地认识到，一体化机构比非一体化机构更具竞争力。</span></span></span>

The reason for this lies in the increasing importance during this period of price in the awarding of mandates by treasurers. If we take a group of originators, all of whom have an equally good reputation and all of whom are tendering for a mandate, then the award of a mandate may become simply a question of price and immediacy. Deals were frequently being arranged by lead managers at a price such that the 2½ per cent spread (transactions cost) which is supposed to reward the lead manager, co-managers, underwriters and selling group had to be given away to price-sensitive investors (i.e. the issue sold at a price close to 97.5 per cent of par) in order to provide an attractive and competitive yield. For co-managers and underwriters participating in the deal, unless they had access to investors who were price insensitive, they would generate little revenue from the deal and indeed frequently suffered losses.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">其原因在于，这一时期司库在委托任务时越来越看重价格。如果考虑一组声誉同样良好的发起人，且他们都在竞标同一项委托，那么最终谁能中标可能仅仅取决于价格和时效性。频繁出现的情况是，主承销商以这样的价格安排交易：原本用于奖励主承销商、联合经理、承销商及销售团体的 2.5%价差（交易成本），不得不让利给对价格敏感的投资者（即发行价格接近面值的 97.5%），以便提供有吸引力且具竞争力的收益率。对于参与交易的联合经理和承销商而言，除非他们能接触到对价格不敏感的投资者，否则几乎无法从这笔交易中赚取收入，实际上还常常遭受亏损。</span></span></span>

The firms which were able to continue to generate revenue from such issues were of two types — those which lead managed the issue (originators) and therefore received the <span class="italic">precipium</span> on the issue (i.e. a payment out of the spread before any other member of the syndicate is paid) and those who had access to price-insensitive clients. These include the Swiss banks with non-resident discretionary clients and the Japanese securities houses with their domestic client base. These two groups are price insensitive because of an asymmetry of price information available to them relative to their securities house. The lack of information in the case of Swiss clients arises from the fact that they are often not institutional investors but rich private individuals often living far from Switzerland and unable to access (and frequently uninterested in accessing) price information services. In the case of Japanese clients, social relationships between investors and securities houses count for more than in the west and seem to allow a reward for service given rather than a pure free competitive market return. In addition, price information services are not yet widely available. US investment banks have attempted to build such a price-insensitive client base by integrating into distribution, but their relative lack of success can be judged by the decline in their position in the origination league tables. This lack of success is because the type of clients US investment banks generally have access to in turn have access to exactly the same information on 'true' price that they do.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">能够从这类发行中持续创收的公司主要有两类：一类是牵头管理发行（即发起人），因此能从发行中优先获得预扣费（即在辛迪加其他成员获得付款之前，先从价差中分得一笔费用）；另一类则是那些能够接触到对价格不敏感的客户的机构。后者包括拥有非居民全权委托客户的瑞士银行，以及拥有本土客户基础的日本证券公司。这两类客户之所以对价格不敏感，是因为他们与证券公司之间存在信息不对称。瑞士客户信息匮乏的原因在于，他们通常并非机构投资者，而是富裕的个人，往往居住在远离瑞士的地方，无法获取（且常常对获取）价格信息服务不感兴趣。 在日本客户中，投资者与证券公司之间的社会关系比西方更为重要，这种关系似乎更多是对过往服务的回报，而非纯粹自由竞争市场下的收益。此外，价格信息服务尚不普及。美国投资银行试图通过整合分销渠道来建立这种对价格不敏感的客户群体，但它们在承销排名表中的地位下滑，足以证明其相对缺乏成功。这种不成功的原因在于，美国投资银行通常能接触到的客户类型，恰恰也能获取关于"真实"价格的完全相同信息。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_286"></span>The eurobond new issue market is thus highly competitive at two interfaces — with the majority of corporates and with many professional institutional investors — but less competitive at certain other parts of the investor interface. It is competitive for issuers as a result of the high degree of sophistication that they have gained over the years and more importandy their access to 'up to second' prices from information providers such as Reuters on comparable secondary market trading prices. They are also able to force potential lead managers from all the major securities houses to bid competitively for a mandate to lead the issue (bought deal).<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">因此，欧元债券新发行市场在两个层面竞争激烈——面对多数企业客户和众多专业机构投资者时尤为如此，但在投资者关系的其他某些环节竞争程度相对较低。对发行人而言，这种竞争源于多年积累的高度专业性，更关键的是他们能通过路透等资讯服务商获取二级市场可比交易价格的最优报价。此外，发行人还能迫使各大证券机构中潜在的牵头经办人通过竞价方式争夺发行授权（即包销交易）。</span></span></span>

In order to fully distnbute an issue, US originators without inhouse distribution in Japan, Switzerland and Germany, must distribute through Japanese, Swiss and German investment banks who will not purchase an issue unless they can be assured a reasonable margin for themselves. The consequent low profitability has caused many US houses to cease offering origination services in this market.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">为了全面分销债券，那些在日本、瑞士和德国没有内部分销渠道的美国发行人，必须通过这些国家的投资银行进行分销，而后者只有在确保自身能获得合理利润时才会承销债券。由此导致的低利润率已使许多美国机构停止在该市场提供发行服务。</span></span></span>

Internalization theory predicts that as transactions costs in markets fall, market transactions between specialist firms become more attractive relative to internal transactions in integrated houses. It is surprising therefore that it was during a period when transactions costs <span class="italic">were</span> falling that US investment banks were integrating forward into distribution. The reason for this is explained by this interface being one where in certain countries, costs were not falling. If, however, the market interface between investment banks and investor clients in Switzerland and Japan becomes more competitive, we would expect to see, on the basis of this theory, that more firms would specialize once again and more originators would simply rent distribution when they had arranged an issue. This has already happened in the UK equity market as a result of the lowered transactions costs (and broker profits) resulting from Big Bang. One major originator that integrated into distribution in 1986 (Morgan Grenfell) closed its distribution arm in 1989 and now once again rents distribution capability through the market in the form of a syndicate.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">内部化理论预测，随着市场交易成本的下降，专业企业之间的市场交易相较于一体化机构内部的交易会更具吸引力。因此，令人惊讶的是，正是在交易成本下降的时期，美国投资银行却在向前整合进入分销领域。其原因在于，在某些国家，这一接口的成本并未下降。然而，如果瑞士和日本的投资银行与投资者客户之间的市场接口变得更具竞争力，根据这一理论，我们可以预期，更多企业将再次专业化，更多承销商在安排发行时只会简单租用分销渠道。这种情况在英国股票市场已经出现，这是由于"金融大爆炸"降低了交易成本（以及经纪商利润）的结果。 一家在 1986 年整合进入分销领域的主要发起人（Morgan Grenfell）于 1989 年关闭了其分销部门，如今再次以辛迪加形式通过市场租赁分销能力。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">Final Product Market Interfaces</span> Links between intermediaries and final clients can be of two clearly distinct types — transactional and relationship based. In a transactional link, the investor selects afresh which firm to conduct business with each time he wishes to transact, simply on the basis of cost and immediacy. Such sell side clients tend to be those who transact frequently in either the primary or secondary markets and for whom search costs are low. These tend to be the institutional fund managers who use the <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_287"></span>securities they buy and sell as inputs to their final retail financial product market. These final product markets are highly competitive in many countries as a result of the ease with which the performance of fund managers can be compared. On the buy side, the clients who are most price sensitive are those who access the bond markets perhaps a dozen times per year and have access to price information from screens.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">最终产品市场的接口中介与最终客户之间的关系可明确区分为两种类型——交易型与关系型。在交易型关系中，投资者每次进行交易时，仅依据成本和即时性重新选择合作机构。此类卖方客户通常是频繁参与一级或二级市场交易的群体，其搜寻成本较低。他们多为机构基金经理，将买卖的证券作为最终零售金融产品市场的投入要素。在许多国家，由于基金经理的业绩表现易于比较，这类最终产品市场的竞争异常激烈。在买方方面，对价格最为敏感的客户是那些每年约十几次进入债券市场、并能通过屏幕获取价格信息的群体。</span></span></span>

Those clients with whom investment banks have close relationships are those for whom it is likely that search costs are higher and where a relationship based on trust and knowledge by the investment bank of the client's business needs and objectives reduces the implicit transactions cost for the client. This would apply particularly to corporates and investors who have not transacted at all or only infrequently in a particular market. In seeking a firm with which to form a relationship (a partnership) they will choose to rely in large part on 'general reputation' of the firm.<sup>12</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">与投资银行关系密切的客户，往往是因为他们面临更高的搜寻成本，而投行基于对客户商业需求和目标的深入了解所建立的信任关系，能有效降低客户的隐性交易成本。这一情况尤其适用于那些在特定市场中从未或极少进行交易的机构投资者和企业。当这类客户寻求建立合作关系（即伙伴关系）时，他们很大程度上会依赖投行的"整体声誉"来做出选择。 <sup>12</sup> </span></span></span>

It is for this reason that we emphasize the importance of brand name identification and franchise. We view clients who have a relationship with an investment bank as being in a partnership with them in a not dissimilar way to partnerships between integrated parts of the same firm. This can thus be viewed as a type of 'internalization' of what would otherwise be a market (transactional) relationship. The outcome of the internalization of the client interface is that the client will accept a trade-off between the transactions costs that he incurs and the benefits he derives from the partnership that is more in favour of the firm. This seems to be the type of relationship which Japanese securities houses have with many of their clients. Thus a benefit that the multinational firm can develop is the value that is capitalized in its reputation and the ability this gives it to obtain a better price from clients who are 'quasi integrated' with them. Consideration of this type of internalization is critical in understanding vertical and horizontal (geographic) integration in the securities industry.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正是基于此，我们强调品牌识别与特许经营的重要性。我们认为，客户与投资银行之间的关系，本质上类似于公司内部各部门间的协作伙伴关系。因此，这种关系可被视为对原本纯粹市场（交易）关系的某种“内部化”处理。客户界面内部化的结果是：客户会接受自身交易成本与伙伴关系收益之间的权衡，而这种权衡更有利于企业。这似乎正是日本证券公司与其众多客户之间形成的关系模式。因此，跨国企业能够获得的一项收益在于：其声誉所凝结的价值，以及凭借这种价值从“准一体化”客户那里获取更优定价的能力。 对这类内部化因素的考量，对于理解证券行业中的纵向与横向（地理上）整合至关重要。</span></span></span>

Geographical dispersion of a client base (breadth) also makes it more likely that heterogeneity of expectations about the prospects for a particular security will allow a greater depth of internal (and hence more profitable) market for any investment bank. It will also allow more international arbitrage transactions to be set up by the investment bank on which the potential benefits for particular clients outweigh any higher trading cost, i.e. the client is willing to pay for the idea (information) and for the ability to execute the whole cross-border transaction with a high degree of immediacy and price certainty.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">客户基础在地理上的分散（广度）也增加了这样一种可能性：即对特定证券前景预期的异质性，使得任何投资银行都能形成更深的内部市场（从而获利更多）。同时，这也让投资银行得以开展更多国际套利交易——在这些交易中，特定客户获得的潜在收益超过了任何较高的交易成本，也就是说，客户愿意为想法（信息）以及为能够以高度即时性和价格确定性执行整笔跨境交易的能力买单。</span></span></span>

#### <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_288"></span>Treasury bonds

<span class="italic">Trading in London and Tokyo</span> In the 1980s, the ever-growing balance of payments deficit of the United States, in particular its bilateral deficits with Japan and Germany, have been financed in considerable part by the export of US Treasury bonds. In the case of London, which services European investors in Treasury bonds, secondary market transactions in the 1970s and early 1980s were batched during the London morning and then executed at Wall Street opening at 2 p.m. However, during the 1980s volume built up to such levels that it became economic to run a trading book in London. The same argument applies to Tokyo.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">20 世纪 80 年代，美国日益扩大的国际收支逆差，尤其是对日本和德国的双边贸易逆差，很大程度上是通过发行美国国债来弥补的。以伦敦为例，当地为欧洲投资者提供国债交易服务，在 70 年代至 80 年代初，二级市场交易通常在伦敦上午集中处理，待纽约市场下午两点开盘后执行。然而到了 80 年代，交易量激增到足以支撑伦敦独立开设交易账簿的水平。同样的逻辑也适用于东京。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">Global integration of trading in New York, London and Tokyo</span> Any US investment bank which did not trade US treasuries in other time zones would be at a competitive disadvantage to those who did. We have noted already that the best risk/return trade-off in running a market making book arises from maximizing the rate at which the positions on the book are turned over. By running a book which moves around the world from New York to Tokyo to London and back to New York, a trading house can maximize the revenue on the book while minimizing the risk. Thus running the 'immediacy creating plant' 24 hours a day almost as a continuous flow process has given rise to gains in efficiency and hence the ability to offer more competitive pricing. Given the large volume of activity in these securities undertaken by foreigners, any house which did not do this would generate less trading revenue relative to position losses arising between New York close and reopening the next morning than those houses which had 24 hour operations. They would thus be unlikely to be competitive in pricing. 'Passing the book' from country to country requires a high degree of trust between the individuals involved. It is also impossible to allocate profit generated on the book to different individuals in different time zones since there will be many deals in which the two sides of the transaction (the buy and the sell) will be generated in different countries. Integration of the various trading operations within a multinational is thus the only feasible way of operating.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">纽约、伦敦、东京三地交易实现了全球一体化。如果一家美国投行不参与其他时区的美国国债交易，就会在竞争中处于劣势。我们已指出，做市账簿的最佳风险收益权衡，源自最大化账簿头寸的周转率。通过让账簿从纽约到东京、到伦敦、再回到纽约的全球流转，交易机构可以最大化账簿收益，同时最小化风险。因此，将这种“即时性创造工厂”作为近乎连续的流程来全天候运营，提升了效率，进而能够提供更具竞争力的定价。 鉴于外国人在这些证券中进行大量交易，任何不提供 24 小时运营的交易所，其在纽约收盘至次日开盘期间产生的头寸损失，相对于交易收入而言，都会比那些提供全天候服务的交易所更少。因此，这类交易所将难以在定价上具备竞争力。在不同国家之间“传递账簿”需要相关个人之间高度的信任。此外，由于许多交易中买卖双方分别来自不同国家，因此无法将账簿产生的利润分配给不同时区的不同个人。因此，整合跨国公司内部的各项交易操作是唯一可行的运营方式。</span></span></span>

#### Foreign equity markets<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">外国股票市场</span></span></span>

The driving force in foreign equity markets appears to be different from that in the debt markets. It has become clear from market research undertaken by securities firms that the increasing degree of globalization of equity portfolios resulting from the economic <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_289"></span>and regulatory changes we have already noted will lead investors to seek the lowest 'search cost' solution to their global investment needs compatible with low transactions cost and immediacy. Potential investor clients, including the existing clients of US investment banks, will be seeking investment bank partners which are able to service them with global equities without the need to use a range of foreign brokers. Not only do clients want a global dealing capability, they also want research to be global, i.e. they want comparisons of stocks and sectors across countries.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">外国股票市场的驱动力似乎与债券市场有所不同。证券公司进行的市场调研明确显示，由于我们已经提到的经济和监管变化，股权组合的全球化程度不断提高，这将促使投资者在满足低交易成本和即时性要求的前提下，寻求全球投资需求中“搜寻成本”最低的解决方案。潜在投资者客户，包括美国投资银行的现有客户，将寻找能够提供全球股票服务、无需借助一系列外国经纪商的投资银行合作伙伴。客户不仅希望拥有全球交易能力，还希望研究也具有全球性，即他们希望跨国家和跨行业进行股票与板块的比较。</span></span></span>

Such a service can be provided in four ways — through arm's-length relationships with overseas stockbrokers, or strategic relationships with overseas firms, or by acquisition of existing overseas equity operations, or by setting up a greenfield operation from scratch.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">提供此类服务有四种方式：与海外股票经纪商建立公平交易关系，或与海外公司建立战略合作关系，或收购现有海外股票业务，或从零开始设立新建业务。</span></span></span>

Until the 1980s the first method was the only one that was used. The clear change in the 1980s has been to foreign direct investment in production, sales and research facilities initially in the UK and subsequently in Tokyo and in other major financial centres. In trying to explain this, we will look at the economics of the London market as this was the first market to deregulate and allow foreign firms to enter.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">直到 20 世纪 80 年代，第一种方法是唯一被使用的方式。80 年代的明显变化是转向外国直接投资，最初是在英国的生产、销售和研发设施，随后扩展到东京和其他主要金融中心。为了解释这一现象，我们将考察伦敦市场的经济学，因为它是第一个放松管制并允许外国公司进入的市场。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">Broker/dealer integration</span> Prior to Big Bang, when market making (jobber acting as principal) and stock broking (agent dealing with investors) were legally separate, brokers were remunerated by a fixed commission while market makers generated revenue from the bid/offer spread associated with the provision of Immediacy. Most US investment banks used a commission-splitting arrangement with a UK broker to service their own clients with UK equities. Since Big Bang, in the absence of a commission (i.e. net trading) on a high proportion of large deals, this arrangement was no longer possible.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">经纪商/交易商整合 在"大爆炸"改革之前，做市商（以自营身份运作的交易商）与股票经纪商（与投资者打交道的代理人）在法律上相互分离，经纪商通过固定佣金获取报酬，而做市商的收入则来源于提供即时流动性所产生的买卖价差。大多数美国投资银行与英国经纪商采用佣金分成安排，为其客户提供英国股票服务。自"大爆炸"改革后，由于大宗交易中高比例的交易不再收取佣金（即净价交易），这一安排已无法继续。</span></span></span>

<span class="italic">Global franchise integration</span> A further reason why US firms were forced to integrate into local production is that ultimately, as all major firms try to become global providers of financial services, the host country firms who would be able to offer them the most competitive service in international equities would be the very firms who would also be trying to build an international equity clientele themselves. Thus the problem described previously of building a global brand-name would arise.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">全球特许经营整合的另一个原因是，美国公司被迫融入当地生产——最终，当所有大型企业都试图成为全球金融服务提供商时，那些能够为它们提供最具竞争力国际股票服务的东道国公司，恰恰也是那些自己正在试图建立国际股票客户群的企业。因此，前面提到的建立全球品牌的问题就会出现。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_290"></span>Divergent market entry motives — investment banks v. commercial banks<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">市场进入动机的差异——投资银行 vs. 商业银行</span></span></span>

### Investment banks<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">投资银行</span></span></span>

US investment banks nearly all made a greenfield entry into UK securities markets while the commercial banks all made acquisitions. The strategy being followed by the investment banks has been different from that of the commercial banks and from the major UK firms which came into UK equities in 1986. Their entry strategy is to compete only in 'international equities', i.e. those high market capitalization stocks which interest international as well as domestic investors. It is clear that the investment banks which have eschewed acquisition have only a limited objective from their participation in foreign equity markets. It is to enable them to offer a service in 'international' equities to an international investment clientele in the US and abroad which they already service with US equities.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">美国投资银行几乎全部以全新方式进入英国证券市场，而商业银行则均采取收购策略。这些投资银行所遵循的战略与商业银行以及 1986 年进入英国股市的主要英国公司截然不同。它们的入市策略是只参与"国际股票"的竞争，即那些吸引国内外投资者的高市值股票。显然，这些避开了收购策略的投资银行，其参与外国股票市场的目标有限——仅为它们在美国及海外已提供美股服务的国际投资客户群，提供"国际"股票方面的服务。</span></span></span>

Such investors views on the desirability of holding or selling a financial asset may vary according to their location. For example, currency factors may be decisive for overseas investors whereas they may not be for domestic. The global firms can tap this nonhomogeneous investor base whereas a purely domestic firm cannot. The more stocks are held internationally, the more valuable it should be to have an international clientele. This clientele should give valuable information on changes in the supply/demand pattern between countries in particular securities, and hence on potential price movements. In addition, if investors view the provision of a service in global equities from all the major markets as a value-added service compared with a separate service from domestic firms for each market of the world, then there may be sufficient spread realized in the transaction for profitability, i.e. investors may be willing to pay more to reduce search and coordination costs. Thus this theory would predict that ultimately only international houses will be able to trade international equities profitably.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这类投资者对于持有或出售某一金融资产的意愿，可能因其所在地的不同而有所差异。例如，汇率因素对海外投资者可能起决定性作用，而对本土投资者则未必如此。全球性企业能够利用这种投资者基础的非同质性，而纯本土企业则无法做到。股票的国际持有比例越高，拥有国际客户群的价值就越大。这些客户群能提供有关特定证券在跨国之间供需格局变化的重要信息，进而反映出潜在的价格变动趋势。此外，如果投资者认为，能提供全球主要市场股票交易服务的企业，相较于仅能提供各市场本地服务的本土企业，具备更高的附加值，那么此类交易便可能产生足够的价差以实现盈利——换句话说，投资者或许愿意为降低搜寻与协调成本而支付更高的溢价。 因此，这一理论将预测，最终只有国际性机构才能在国际股票交易中获利。</span></span></span>

### Commercial banks<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">商业银行</span></span></span>

While most investment banks chose to set up greenfield operations, most investment banking subsidiaries of US commercial banks chose to enter the market through acquisition. Corporate acquisitions in general are made at a price such that the return on investment in the acquisition at the time of takeover is less than the <span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_291"></span>cost of capital of the acquirer.<sup>13</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管多数投资银行选择以新建方式开展业务，但大部分美国商业银行旗下的投资银行子公司却通过收购进入市场。企业收购的普遍定价原则是：收购时的投资回报率低于收购方的资本成本。 <sup>13</sup> </span></span></span>

The only logical reason for such an acquisition at a price above the 'true' market price is the belief by the acquirer that through the gains from integration into the new parent company, the return on assets and equity will be increased to a satisfactory level. This implies that the new parent will bring some benefit to the new subsidiary from internalizing it and integrating it with its existing operations. In the case of US commercial banks, however, given that they have no US or international equity client base and that they cannot deal in equities in the US, there are no obvious synergies to be achieved through the acquisition of a UK equity operation.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">以高于"真实"市场价格进行此类收购的唯一合理逻辑在于，收购方相信通过整合进入新母公司后，资产和股本回报率将提升至令人满意的水平。这意味着新母公司通过将收购对象内部化并与其现有业务整合，将为新子公司带来某些利益。然而，就美国商业银行而言，由于它们既没有美国或国际的股权客户基础，也不能在美国从事股票交易，因此收购一家英国股权业务显然无法实现明显的协同效应。</span></span></span>

If there were synergies to be achieved, now or in the future (for example, if the Glass Steagal Act were to be repealed), the one area in which they might be achieved would be in terms of a global franchise for the name of either the commercial bank or the equity market subsidiary. In practice, what has happened is that the UK stockbroking subsidiaries have in general resisted integration. Where this has been undertaken forcibly, the major revenue earners have left the firm and with them has gone much of the ability to generate rent from the brand name.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如果现在或将来存在协同效应（例如，若格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案被废除），那么可能实现协同效应的领域，便是商业银行或股票市场子公司的全球品牌特许经营权。而实际情况是，英国的股票经纪子公司普遍抗拒整合。一旦强行整合，主要收入来源便会流失，随之而去的是大部分通过品牌价值获取租金的能力。</span></span></span>

In contrast, for investment banks, setting up as domestic producers and distributors of a local equity market product has always been seen as part of a strategy for building a global franchise for the parent company's name based on their existing equity market expertise in the United States and their domestic and international equity client base. For them, therefore, it would have been illogical to have purchased the goodwill of a UK broking house which was principally in the acquired name, only to immediately change it to the parent company name.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">相比之下，对于投资银行而言，定位为本土股权市场产品的本土生产商和分销商，一直被视为其母公司基于美国现有股权市场专长及国内外股权客户基础，打造全球业务版图战略的一部分。因此，对它们来说，购入一家主要以被收购方名义运营的英国经纪公司的商誉，随即又立刻将其更名为母公司名称，这显然不合逻辑。</span></span></span>

We have not so far considered another one of the options, which is to form partnerships with firms overseas. Such relationships are unlikely to be successful for the same reason as we adduced for the failure of arm's-length relationships, i.e. the problem of the allocation of the rent that accrues from the global franchise inherent in the trading name of the provider of the service. Nonetheless, we would expect that as the problems of entry into foreign equity markets become more apparent, new types of relationship falling short of full ownership will come to be considered.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我们此前尚未考虑另一种选择，即与海外公司建立合作关系。此类合作很难成功，原因与我们之前论述的保持距离型关系失败的理由相同——即服务提供商的商号所固有的全球特许经营权产生的租金分配问题。尽管如此，我们预计，随着进入外国股权市场的问题日益凸显，那些尚未达到完全所有权的新型合作关系将开始被纳入考量。</span></span></span>

Summary and conclusions<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">总结与结论</span></span></span>
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Competition in investment banking takes place in three arenas —<span id="Ch014.xhtml_page_292"></span>the transactions price, the provision of immediacy and the 'guarantee of quality'. Most of the changes in the structure of the US investment banking industry since the Second World War, both domestic and overseas, can be explained by actions taken by individual firms to improve their service in these three areas.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">投资银行业的竞争体现在三个层面：交易价格、即时性服务以及"质量保证"。自二战以来，美国投资银行业在国内外结构上的大多数变化，都可以归结为各家公司为在这三个领域提升服务水平所采取的行动。</span></span></span>

Internalization of functions which were previously organized by separate companies has reduced the costs of providing final services. The change in corporate structure in the 1960s and 1970s from partnership to public company status has provided the capital that is necessary to increase the supply of immediacy. Effecting quasi-integration with certain non-price-sensitive clients overseas has allowed firms to extract the economic rent that results from clients having different trade-offs between price and immediacy of service on the one hand, and implicit quality guarantee provided by the brand name on the other. The capitalized value of this brand name and its implicit guarantee of quality has become the most valuable asset that a firm has from the point of view of attracting both clients and skilled staff. The reason for this is that there are no long-term unique or patentable technologies in the industry nor any firm specific advantages apart from the ability to internalize itself and the capitalized value of this which is inherent in the global use of the brand name.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">原本由不同公司分别组织的功能被内部化后，降低了最终服务的提供成本。20 世纪 60 至 70 年代，企业结构从合伙制向上市公司转变，为提升即时服务供应能力提供了必要资本。通过与海外某些对价格不敏感的客户实现准整合，企业得以获取经济租金——这种租金源于客户在价格与即时服务、以及品牌名称所隐含的隐性质量保证之间做出的不同权衡。从吸引客户和优秀人才的角度来看，这种品牌名称及其隐性质量保证的资本化价值已成为企业最宝贵的资产。 原因在于，行业中并无长期独有或可专利的技术，除了企业自身的内部化能力以及品牌全球使用所蕴含的资本化价值之外，也不存在任何企业特有的优势。</span></span></span>

Given that the profitability of market making is in large part a function of the volume of order flow and the non-homogeneity of customer expectations, capturing the potential overseas order flow has been necessary to improve the breadth and depth of internal market making in each investment bank. Extending on from this has been the need for each investment bank to be able to offer as full a geographical range of equity market products as is economic to provide in order to allow clients to arbitrage between geographical markets. Given the value of brand name in building a business and the problems and costs of sharing the use of it with partners overseas, it has been necessary to internalize its use and thus make direct investments overseas in both sales and production to service clients rather than continuing to use arm's-length relationships. This in turn has only become possible since the 1960s as a result of the move to incorporation and the additional capital this provided.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">由于做市业务的盈利能力在很大程度上取决于订单流量的规模以及客户预期的非均质性，因此，捕捉潜在的海外订单流对于提升各投资银行内部做市业务的广度和深度至关重要。在此基础上，每家投资银行还需要能够提供尽可能全面的、经济上可行的地域性股票市场产品，以便客户在不同地域市场之间进行套利。鉴于品牌在业务建设中的价值，以及与海外合作伙伴共享品牌所带来问题和成本，有必要将品牌使用内部化，并因此直接进行海外销售和生产投资以服务客户，而不是继续维持保持距离的合作关系。而这一转变自 20 世纪 60 年代起才成为可能，原因是公司化进程以及由此带来的额外资本。</span></span></span>
