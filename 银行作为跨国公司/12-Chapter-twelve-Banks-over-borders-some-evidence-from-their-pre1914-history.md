Much but not all of the early research on multinational enterprise (MNE) concentrated on industrial companies.<sup>2</sup> Now, this has changed and most authors accept that a MNE can be a producer of goods <span class="italic">or</span> services.<sup>3</sup> In the last thirty years, numerous attempts have been made to define, to analyse, and to clarify the history and nature of MNEs. There is now even a second generation literature, tracing the historiography of the theory of multinational enterprise.<sup>4</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">早期关于跨国企业（MNE）的研究，大部分（但并非全部）都集中在工业企业上。 <sup>2</sup> 如今，情况已经发生了变化，大多数研究者都认同跨国企业既可以生产商品，也可以提供服务。 <sup>3</sup> 过去三十年间，人们进行了大量尝试来定义、分析和阐明跨国企业的历史与本质。如今甚至出现了第二代文献，专门梳理跨国企业理论的历史编纂学。 <sup>4</sup> </span></span></span>

While there is by no means consensus on the theory of multinationals, I agree with Mark Casson that the modern theory of the MNE — a theory of a multiproduct, spatially extended firm — has the potential to become the general theory of the firm.<sup>5</sup> In this chapter, I am going to accept the view that a MNE is a firm and that the appropriate way to analyse the bank as a MNE is to concentrate on the firm, <span class="italic">per se.</span><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">尽管关于跨国公司的理论尚未达成共识，但我同意马克·卡森的观点：现代跨国企业理论——即关于多产品、空间延伸企业的理论——有潜力成为企业的通用理论。 <sup>5</sup> 在本章中，我将接受这样一种观点：跨国企业本身就是一家企业，而将银行作为跨国企业来分析，恰当的方式就是聚焦于企业本身。</span></span></span>

This chapter is in three parts. The first considers the bank as a firm, as a multinational enterprise. Part II looks at some evidence from the pre-1914 history of foreign banks in the United States and US banks abroad. The third part links the data from part II with the issues posed in the first part.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">本章分为三部分。第一部分将银行视为企业，视为跨国企业。第二部分考察了 1914 年前外国银行在美国以及美国银行在海外的一些历史证据。第三部分则将第二部分的数据与第一部分提出的问题联系起来。</span></span></span>

## I

In elementary economics courses, students learn of two private sector actors: the household and the firm. The firm is defined as the producing unit, the producer of goods and services. The modern theory of multinational enterprise does not, however, see the firm simply as a production function, but rather as a governance structure.<sup>6</sup> Transactions take place in the market (between households and firms and between independent firms); they also occur within the administered large multiproduct, spatially extended enterprise. The management of the firm cannot be <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_218"></span>ignored; the firm is not merely a profit-maxsmizing black box. The theory of multinational enterprise seeks to explain enterprise behaviour and to identify those aspects of the firm that will encourage or discourage its international endeavours.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN" style="">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在初级经济学课程中，学生们会了解到两类私营部门主体：家庭和企业。企业被定义为生产单位，即商品和服务的生产者。然而，现代跨国企业理论并不将企业简单地视为一种生产函数，而是看作一种治理结构。 <sup>6</sup> 交易既发生在市场上（家庭与企业之间，以及独立企业之间），也发生在受管理的、产品多样、空间广泛的大型企业内部。企业的管理不容忽视；它并非只是一个追求利润最大化的黑箱。跨国企业理论旨在解释企业行为，并识别那些会促进或阻碍其国际化经营的企业特征。</span></span></span>

No elementary economics course ignores the government as an actor in the economy. What differentiates the MNE from the purely domestic firm is its extension <span class="italic">over borders,</span> its functioning under more than one national sovereign.<sup>7</sup> Government actions at home <span class="italic">and abroad</span> affect the strategies of the MNE. The extent and nature of the impact are part of what we must study.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">任何基础经济学课程都不会忽略政府在经济中的角色。多国企业与纯国内企业的区别在于其跨国运营，受多个国家主权管辖。国内外政府的行为都会影响多国企业的战略。影响的程度和性质是我们必须研究的一部分。</span></span></span>

The economic historian looks at the firm through time, recognizing that resource allocation by a firm at any particular moment is not only influenced by existing costs and revenue potentials and appraisals of existing conditions, but also by what has transpired in earlier years; behaviour is biased (constrained) by limited knowledge; one of the major ways that information is incorporated in decision-making relates to a firm's prior encounters. Once most of us believed that multinational operations grew out of a firm's <span class="italic">domestic</span> activities. Recent work on so-called 'free-standing companies' has identified enterprises devoted to managing business abroad with a home-country headquarters but <span class="italic">no</span> initial home nation <span class="italic">operations.</span> Obviously, the earlier model proved inadequate in explaining the behaviour of all multinationals.<sup>8</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">经济史学家从时间维度审视企业，认识到企业在特定时刻的资源分配不仅受制于现有成本、收益潜力及对当前形势的评估，还深受早年历史的影响；行为因知识有限而存在偏差（受限）；信息融入决策的主要途径之一与企业过往经历息息相关。过去多数人认为跨国经营源于企业的国内活动。但近期关于所谓"独立公司"的研究发现，那些专攻海外业务的企业设有母国总部，却从未在国内开展过业务。显然，早期模型在解释所有跨国企业的行为时已显不足。 <sup>8</sup> </span></span></span>

MNEs make foreign direct investments (FDI), that is, they do more than just export or import; a firm lacking a presence abroad (i.e. a foreign direct investment) is not a MNE. The presence abroad in operations, however minor, is the foreign direct investment. Because the making of FDI defines the multinational enterprise, it has often been assumed that a theory of multinational enterprise and a theory of FDI are one and the same.<sup>9</sup> I do not assume this. FDI is <span class="italic">one</span> activity of the MNE. Others include the transfer of a wide variety of intangibles: management services, production methods, marketing know-how, product design, patents, trademarks, and the like.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">跨国企业进行外国直接投资（FDI），即它们不仅仅从事进出口业务；一家没有海外业务（即外国直接投资）的企业不能称之为跨国企业。海外业务运营，无论规模多小，都属于外国直接投资。由于进行外国直接投资是跨国企业的定义性特征，人们往往认为跨国企业理论与外国直接投资理论是等同的。 <sup>9</sup> 我并不同意这种观点。外国直接投资只是跨国企业的活动之一。其他活动还包括各类无形资产的转移：管理服务、生产方法、营销诀窍、产品设计、专利、商标等。</span></span></span>

Where specifically do banks fit into this overview? Conceptually, there should be no difficulty. A firm is a unit that produces goods and services; a bank produces banking services. A MNE makes foreign direct investments; to the extent that the bank does so it is a MNE. All this seems straightforward. Yet, for many years the US Department of Commerce, which assembled materials on MNEs, excluded banks from its discussions of multinational companies and from its tabulations of figures on foreign direct investments. Now, however, it includes banks in its statistical summaries, while still excluding them from its discussions on operations.<sup>10</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">银行在这个概述中具体处于什么位置？从概念上讲，这并不难理解。企业是生产商品和服务的单位，而银行提供的是银行服务。跨国公司从事对外直接投资，如果银行也这样做，那它本身就是一家跨国公司。这一切看起来都很直白。然而，多年来，负责收集跨国公司相关资料的美国商务部，在讨论跨国公司和编制对外直接投资数据时，一直将银行排除在外。不过，现在它虽然把银行纳入了统计摘要中，但在运营讨论中仍然将其排除在外。 <sup>10</sup> </span></span></span>

<span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_219"></span>What are the problems? Clearly, a bank is <span class="italic">not</span> an ordinary firm. The typical enterprise produces goods and services, and ultimately sells what it produces, receiving payments 'in money'. The bank —the financial intermediary — has as its service the taking in of money (deposits in the case of a commercial bank), which it pays for (interest on the deposits), and the lending of money (loans), for which it charges (an interest rate). The bank makes its profits on the difference between what it pays for money and what it charges (including fees) less added costs of providing the services.<sup>11</sup> Its <span class="italic">products</span> can create liabilities (when it takes deposits) <span class="italic">or</span> assets (when it makes loans). Its business is in money-related <span class="italic">products. </span>Thus, the transactions of an industrial firm or a retailer, wherein products (goods and non-banking services) are exchanged for payments (money), are not replicated. Likewise, that banks can 'create money' once more differentiates the bank from other firms. The nature of the products is different.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">问题何在？显然，银行并非普通企业。典型企业生产商品和服务，最终出售其产品，以“货币”形式收款。而银行作为金融中介，其业务是吸收资金（商业银行的存款），为此支付利息（存款利息），并发放贷款，为此收取利息（贷款利率）。银行的利润来源于资金成本与收费（含手续费）之间的差额，再减去提供服务的附加成本。 <sup>11</sup> 其产品可能产生负债（吸收存款时）或资产（发放贷款时）。银行的业务与货币相关产品密不可分。因此，工业企业或零售商的交易——产品（商品和非银行服务）与付款（货币）的交换——在银行身上并不复制。同样，银行能够“创造货币”这一点，也进一步将其与其他企业区分开来。 产品的性质不同。</span></span></span>

Also, the word <span class="italic">products</span> in bankers terminology applies to offers to savers (inputs) as well as to offers to borrowers (outputs). The terminologies are at variance with those used with regard to industrial firms. Perhaps we can, for purposes of uniformity, think of the marshalling of savings as equivalent to the purchasing functions of an industrial enterprise, i.e. input-related. Then, in our discussions of banks as multinational enterprise, we can view the process of financial intermediation as requiring inputs (savings) and outputs (loans). The administration of the process by the bank can involve a local activity (mobilization of savings nearby and lending in the same area) or more widely extended activity, including the crossing of international borders. If the obtaining of savings and making of loans over national boundaries can be done with no extension of the firm itself abroad, the effect seems analogous to goods <span class="italic">imports and exports.</span> Indeed, a bank can carry on business abroad with no extension abroad. The notion of 'correspondents' abroad, commonly used in the banking literature, seems to me equivalent to 'independent agents' in the terminology related to the industrial firm. Students of multinationals have long been aware that an alternative to the extension of the firm is to have an independent agent offering marketing services in foreign countries. The bank may use the 'correspondent' differently from the industrial firm's use of the independent agent, but the form provides no special conceptual difficulties.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">此外，银行用语中的“产品”一词既指向储蓄者（投入端）的提供物，也指向借款人（产出端）的提供物。这种术语与工业企业的用法存在差异。为了统一概念，或许我们可以将储蓄的汇集视为相当于工业企业的采购职能，即与投入相关。那么，在讨论银行作为跨国企业时，我们可以将金融中介的过程视为需要投入（储蓄）和产出（贷款）。银行对这一过程的管理可能涉及本地活动（在附近动员储蓄并在同一地区放贷），也可能涉及更广泛的活动，包括跨越国界。如果无需将企业自身扩展到海外就能实现跨国获取储蓄和发放贷款，其效果似乎类似于商品的进出口。 确实，银行无需在国外设立分支机构便可在海外开展业务。银行业文献中常用的"代理行"概念，在我看来相当于工业企业术语中的"独立代理"。跨国企业研究者早已意识到，企业扩张的替代方案之一，便是由独立代理在外国提供营销服务。银行使用"代理行"的方式或许与工业企业使用独立代理有所不同，但这种模式在概念上并不构成特殊难点。</span></span></span>

A bank's physical assets (buildings and office equipment) tend to be small compared with its financial assets, far smaller than in the case of industrial enterprises. Financial assets are, moreover, different from most other assets (tangible or intangible ones) for <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_220"></span>they are always a liability for some other individual or firm. This means that when a bank makes a loan by definition it does not complete a sale to a customer, but retains an on-going relationship, unlike that of most 'sales'.<sup>12</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">银行的实物资产（建筑物和办公设备）相比其金融资产通常较小，远小于工业企业的情况。此外，金融资产与大多数其他资产（有形或无形）不同，因为它们总是对其他个人或企业而言构成一项负债。这意味着，当银行发放贷款时，从定义上讲，它并非向客户完成一笔销售，而是保持一种持续的关系，这与大多数“销售”不同。 <sup>12</sup> </span></span></span>

A recent report described the financial intermediary as 'a specialist whose job is to eliminate, circumvent, or reduce the cost of ... financial market imperfections', which create 'blockages and distortions in the flow of funds from investors to borrowers'.<sup>13</sup> This gives the firm a special character, yet many other firms are specialists in eliminating non-financial market imperfections for third parties,<sup>14</sup> so these features do not seem unique to the banking 'industry'.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">最近一份报告将金融中介描述为“一种专家，其职责是消除、规避或降低……金融市场不完善所带来的成本”，这些不完善会造成“资金从投资者流向借款人的过程中的阻塞和扭曲”。 <sup>13</sup> 这赋予了企业一种特殊性质，然而许多其他企业也是为第三方消除非金融市场不完善的专家， <sup>14</sup> 因此这些特征似乎并非银行业所独有。</span></span></span>

Likewise, the extent and nature of government regulation in banking differ from that in other sectors, but the fact that home and host government actions often influence and have influenced in an important fashion the behaviour of banks in their extensions abroad is in no way peculiar to banking. So, too, the historical evolution of any particular bank — like any other firm — constrains it in its decision-making.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">同样地，政府监管在银行业的范围和性质与其他行业有所不同，但母国和东道国政府的行为经常并且曾经以重要方式影响银行在海外扩张的行为，这一点并非银行业所独有。同样，任何特定银行的历史演变——就像其他公司一样——也会制约其决策。</span></span></span>

Nonetheless, principally because of the characteristics of a bank's products (however defined) and the meaning and nature of the 'size of its assets', one rarely finds banks intermingled with industrials on lists of giant enterprises. The reason, of course, lies in the lack of coincidence in the statistical data; to rank such sums on the same rosters does not seem meaningful.<sup>15</sup> The difficulties notwithstanding, I will attempt — as others have before me — to look at the behaviour of banks as multinationals — as firms extending over borders, as producers of banking services. I will never deny that the bank is a very special type of firm. Yet, the institutional arrangements by which it carries forth its functions can in part be associated with a general understanding of the nature of the governance of the firm and, like every other firm, are in part 'product-specific', that is solely related to banking. The influence of governments can be stated and explained. So, too, historical constraints can be considered.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，主要由于银行产品（无论作何定义）的特性，以及"资产规模"这一概念的含义与本质，人们很少能在巨型企业榜单上看到银行与工业企业混杂排列。原因当然在于统计数据的不一致性——将如此不同性质的总额放在同一份排名中似乎并无意义。 <sup>15</sup> 尽管存在这些困难，我仍将效仿前人的做法，尝试将银行视为跨国企业来审视其行为——即将其视为跨越国界的经营实体、提供银行服务的生产者。我从不否认银行是一种极为特殊的企业类型。然而，银行履行职能所依赖的制度安排，部分可结合对企业治理本质的普遍理解来阐释；同时，与所有其他企业一样，这些安排部分也具有"产品特异性"，即仅仅与银行业务相关。政府的影响也可以被清晰地阐述和解释。 同样，历史限制因素也应纳入考量。</span></span></span>

In a recent, and valuable, article, Robert Aliber notes two separate subjects involved in the analysis of international banking, one being the industrial organization and the other, the international finance issues.<sup>16</sup> I would like to repeat that when we discuss banks as multinational enterprise, we view the behaviour of the bank as a firm, or as Aliber prefers to put it, the industrial organization issues. We try to explain patterns in the extension of banks abroad; the nature of the advantages that the bank has in operating abroad over host nation or third country competitors; <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_221"></span>the reasons behind its establishing itself abroad; how it uses its branches and affiliates overseas; and its success or alternatively lack of success in its business operations outside the headquarters country. The international finance questions seem to deal with the <span class="italic">products</span> of the bank as distinct from its <span class="italic">operations.</span><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在最近一篇颇具价值的文章中，罗伯特·阿利伯指出，分析国际银行业涉及两个不同的主题：一个是产业组织问题，另一个则是国际金融问题。 <sup>16</sup> 我想重申一点：当我们把银行视为跨国企业来讨论时，我们所关注的是银行作为企业的行为，或者用阿利伯更偏爱的说法，即产业组织层面的议题。我们试图解释银行向海外扩张的模式；银行相对于东道国或第三国竞争对手在海外经营中所拥有的优势本质；银行在海外设立分支机构的原因；它如何利用其海外分行和附属机构；以及它在母国以外业务经营中的成功或不足之处。而国际金融问题似乎更侧重于银行的产品本身，而非其运营过程。</span></span></span>

Just as industrial firms engage in exports and imports with no foreign offices, affiliates, or subsidiaries, banks can be, have been, and frequently are engaged in international banking, including the taking in of foreign deposits, making foreign loans, engaging in foreign exchange transactions, and so forth — without being multinational enterprises. The study of the bank as a multinational enterprise, as a firm that extends itself over borders, is separate from the study of international banking. At times, in fact often, the two topics coincide; they are, however, distinct. The student of the bank as a multinational asks questions about <span class="italic">the institution per se, </span>recognizing that many international transactions can be carried out without business operations over borders. Indeed, one challenging topic is to clarify the functions of and rationale for foreign bank branches, subsidiaries, and affiliates. In short, a survey of international banking or a theory of international banking is <span class="italic">not</span> the same as a discussion of the bank as a multinational enterprise.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正如工业企业无需设立海外办事处、附属机构或子公司即可进行进出口贸易，银行同样可以、也曾经并且经常从事国际银行业务——包括吸收外国存款、发放外国贷款、进行外汇交易等——而不必成为跨国企业。将银行视为跨国企业、视为跨越国界扩展自身的实体来研究，与国际银行业务的研究是分开的。有时，事实上常常是，这两个主题有所重合；然而，它们本质上是不同的。研究作为跨国企业的银行时，学者关注的是该机构本身，认识到许多国际交易可以在不进行跨境业务运营的情况下完成。实际上，一个具有挑战性的课题是阐明外国银行分行、子公司和附属机构的功能及其存在理由。 简而言之，对国际银行业务的考察或国际银行业务理论，与将银行视为跨国企业进行讨论并非同一回事。</span></span></span>

If we agree that 'the bank as a multinational' is <span class="italic">not</span> the same as 'the bank in international banking,' many of the problems of definition that Aliber identified evaporate.<sup>17</sup> Thus, 'the sale of sterling denominated deposits' in New York by a New York bank is a New York transaction; the currency of the deposit relates to international transactions, but does not define the bank <span class="italic">as an institution. </span> Likewise, the nationality of customers is not relevant to the definition of the bank as a multinational enterprise.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">如果我们认同“作为跨国企业的银行”与“国际银行业务中的银行”并非同一概念，那么阿里伯提出的许多定义问题就迎刃而解了。 <sup>17</sup> 因此，一家纽约银行在纽约“出售英镑计价存款”属于纽约本地交易；存款所涉货币与国际交易相关，但这并不能将银行定义为一种机构。同样，客户的国籍也与银行作为跨国企业的定义无关。</span></span></span>

The scrutiny of banks as multinationals must, however, look at the history of all the cross-border operating relationships, including international-interlocking partnerships and family houses. Likewise, when a bank makes a sizeable equity investment in a non-banking venture abroad (and holds it for more than a year), this probably should be seen as part of multinational business.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">然而，审视银行作为跨国公司时，必须全面考察其所有跨境经营关系的历史，包括国际连锁合伙以及家族企业。同样，当一家银行在国外非银行业的项目中进行了规模可观的股权投资（并持有一年以上），这也应被视为跨国业务的一部分。</span></span></span>

II<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">第二部分</span></span></span>

Before the First World War, a large number of banks had become multinational enterprises. Geoffrey Jones has established that twenty-eight UK-registered banks had 1,286 foreign branches and owned agencies in 1913.<sup>18</sup> In addition, certain British merchant banks had interlocking partnerships outside the United Kingdom. So, too, German, French, Belgian, Dutch, Swiss, and other European <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_222"></span>banks had salaried representatives, owned agencies, branches, subsidiaries, and interlocking partnerships abroad. Aliber writes that French and German banks in 1914 had 500 foreign branches.<sup>19</sup> Canadian banks had owned agencies, branches, and subsidiary banks in the United States in 1914.<sup>20</sup> By the early twentieth century, the Yokohama Specie Bank had overseas branches in Asia, the United States, and Europe.<sup>21</sup> As we will see, there were not many American banks with foreign <span class="italic">operations </span>before 1914.<sup>22</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">第一次世界大战前，大量银行已成为跨国企业。杰弗里·琼斯证实，1913 年，28 家英国注册银行拥有 1286 家海外分行及代理机构。 <sup>18</sup> 此外，部分英国商人银行在英国境外还设有连锁合伙关系。同样，德国、法国、比利时、荷兰、瑞士及其他欧洲银行的海外业务也通过领薪代表、自有代理机构、分行、子公司及连锁合伙关系展开。阿里伯指出，1914 年法国和德国银行共拥有 500 家海外分行。 <sup>19</sup> 1914 年加拿大银行在美国持有代理机构、分行及附属银行。 <sup>20</sup> 至 20 世纪初，横滨正金银行已在亚洲、美国和欧洲设立海外分行。 <sup>21</sup> 下文将说明，1914 年前开展海外业务的美国银行为数不多。 <sup>22</sup> </span></span></span>

In this chapter, I am going to look exclusively at foreign banks in the United States and US banks abroad in the period before 1914.<sup>23</sup> By way of introduction, the reader should note that as we study foreign banks in the United States before 1914, the patterns are not symmetrical with US banks abroad. As we consider foreign banks in the United States, the variety in terms of participants, form, and purpose is very striking. In America's early history, a number of the nation's key banks attracted foreign investment, some of which came from British and Dutch banking houses. This was the case with the country's-first real bank, The Bank of North America, founded in Philadelphia in 1781, with the Bank of the United States (1791-1811), and with the Second Bank of the United States (1816-36) and its successor (1836-41). In each of these instances, foreign banks (British merchant banks and Dutch private banks) were investors.<sup>24</sup> In the case of the first Bank of the United States (where foreign investment equalled 70 per cent of the stock in 1811) and the Second Bank of the United States (where foreign investment equalled 43 per cent of the stock in 1833), the charters prohibited any non-resident investors from voting their shares and thus the foreign investors could not control the running of the banks.<sup>25</sup> While many other American banks (and trust companies) obtained foreign investment in the first half of the nineteenth century, and again, while sometimes the investors included foreign merchant banks (principally, British and Dutch), I can find no evidence that these domestic banks were foreign-managed or foreign-run.<sup>26</sup> Sizeable investments by foreign banks and individuals in <span class="italic">American-established banks</span> began to disappear by the 1850s. A Hope & Co. investment in the Citizens' Bank, New Orleans, that lingered to the late nineteenth century was surely a rarity.<sup>27</sup> Do we call these investments in American banks when made by foreign banks foreign direct investments? They were often for business purposes. Most important, they provided essential financial resources that Americans lacked. They filled a gap.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在本章中，我将专门考察 1914 年之前在美国的外国银行以及美国在国外的银行。 <sup>23</sup> 作为引言，读者应注意，在研究 1914 年之前在美国的外国银行时，其模式与美国在国外的银行并不对称。当我们考虑在美国的外国银行时，参与方、形式及目的的多样性非常显著。在美国早期历史中，一些关键银行吸引了外国投资，其中部分来自英国和荷兰的银行机构。美国第一家真正的银行——1781 年在费城成立的北美银行、合众国银行（1791-1811 年）、第二合众国银行（1816-1836 年）及其后继者（1836-1841 年）皆是如此。在这些案例中，外国银行（英国商人银行和荷兰私人银行）都是投资者。 <sup>24</sup> 就美国第一银行（1811 年时外资持股比例达 70%）和第二银行（1833 年时外资持股比例达 43%）而言，其章程禁止任何非居民投资者行使投票权，因此外资无法控制这些银行的运营。 <sup>25</sup> 尽管 19 世纪上半叶许多其他美国银行（及信托公司）也获得了外国投资，且投资者中偶尔包括外国商业银行（主要是英国和荷兰的），但我找不到任何证据表明这些本土银行由外资管理或运营。 <sup>26</sup> 到 19 世纪 50 年代，外国银行及个人对美国本土银行的大规模投资开始消退。例如 Hope & Co.对新奥尔良公民银行的投资一直延续到 19 世纪末，但这无疑是个例。 <sup>27</sup> 外国银行对美国银行的投资是否被称为外国直接投资？这些投资通常出于商业目的。最重要的是，它们提供了美国人缺乏的关键金融资源，填补了空白。</span></span></span>

A notable item relating to foreign banks in the United States is <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_223"></span>the important role of Canadian banks, especially but not exclusively the Bank of Montreal. There was no question that the New York agency of the Bank of Montreal, opened in 1859, was a direct investment. This owned agency flourished during the Civil War, and in 1868 had assets of \$8.36 million. (To give the reader an idea of its size — and of the problems of comparing financial and industrial companies — note that John D. Rockefeller's Standard Oil Company of Ohio, America's first major industrial enterprise, formed in 1870, had a capital of \$1 million). National City Bank's total assets in 1868 were \$9.1 million — not much larger than those of the Bank of Montreal's New York agency.<sup>28</sup> The Bank of Montreal was for a number of years an important lender in New York. Its lending was based on its parent's resources;<sup>29</sup> borrowers' demands were greater in New York than in Montreal and other Canadian locations; and the new agency could meet the high demand.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在美国的外国银行中，有一个值得注意的现象：加拿大银行扮演了重要角色，其中尤以蒙特利尔银行为代表，但也不仅限于此。毫无疑问，蒙特利尔银行于 1859 年在纽约设立的代理处属于直接投资。这家自有代理处在美国内战期间蓬勃发展，到 1868 年其资产已达 836 万美元。（为让读者了解其规模——以及比较金融与工业公司时面临的问题——请注意，约翰·D·洛克菲勒的俄亥俄标准石油公司，美国第一家大型工业企业，成立于 1870 年，资本金仅为 100 万美元。）1868 年，国民城市银行的总资产为 910 万美元，比蒙特利尔银行纽约代理处的资产也大不了多少。 <sup>28</sup> 多年来，蒙特利尔银行一直是纽约重要的贷款机构。 其贷款业务依托母公司的资源； <sup>29</sup> 纽约的借款需求比蒙特利尔及其他加拿大地区更为旺盛；而这家新机构正好能够满足这种高需求。</span></span></span>

Unlike in US commercial banking (after the 1860s), Canadian banks had no statutory restrictions on branching.<sup>30</sup> The Bank of Montreal, behaving almost exactly like an industrial firm, had begun its extension into nearby America, with an independent agent in New York (in 1818).<sup>31</sup> Why? Part of the reason was information procurement; part was to gain contacts; part was to trade in specie; part was to make loans in an active port city.<sup>32</sup> In 1859, as noted, it had set up the owned New York 'agency', which flourished. The Bank of Montreal opened a full-fledged banking branch in Chicago in 1861, where unlike in New York, it took deposits as well as making loans. Here, the Bank was following its customers and providing services for them. It also offered retail banking services to Chicagoans. There were no restrictions in Illinois at that time to stop a foreign bank's entry. The Bank of Montreal followed its customers again when it opened a banking branch in Spokane, Washington, in 1903. It was a deposit-taking institution in Chicago and Spokane, but not in New York, because of New York state rules and regulations.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">与美国商业银行（1860 年代后）不同，加拿大银行在设立分行方面没有法定限制。 <sup>30</sup> 蒙特利尔银行的扩张行为几乎与工业企业如出一辙，它早在 1818 年就开始向邻近的美国拓展，在纽约设立了一个独立代理机构。 <sup>31</sup> 原因何在？部分是为了获取信息，部分是为了建立联系，部分是为了进行贵金属交易，还有部分是为了在这个活跃的港口城市发放贷款。 <sup>32</sup> 如前所述，1859 年，该行在纽约设立了自有“代理处”，业务蒸蒸日上。1861 年，蒙特利尔银行在芝加哥开设了一家功能齐全的银行分行，与纽约不同，这里既吸收存款也发放贷款。该行此举是在追随其客户，并为他们提供服务，同时也向芝加哥市民提供零售银行业务。当时伊利诺伊州并未设置任何障碍阻止外国银行进入。1903 年，蒙特利尔银行在华盛顿州斯波坎开设分行，再次追随其客户的步伐。 这是一家位于芝加哥和斯波坎的存款机构，但因纽约州的相关法规限制，未能在纽约开展业务。</span></span></span>

Other Canadian banks, the Bank of Nova Scotia and the Canadian Bank of Commerce, for example, extended into the United States. In 1899, the Merchants' Bank of Halifax (to be renamed in 1901 the Royal Bank of Canada), which had branches in British Columbia, purchased a small private bank in nearby Republic, Washington, and used it as the basis for its own branch operation 'to serve the needs of prominent Montreal customers who were interested in the considerable prospecting' taking place there.<sup>33</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">其他加拿大银行，比如加拿大丰业银行和加拿大商业银行，也拓展到了美国市场。1899 年，当时在不列颠哥伦比亚省设有分行的哈利法克斯商业银行（后于 1901 年更名为加拿大皇家银行），收购了附近华盛顿州共和市的一家小型私人银行，并以此为基础开设了自己的分行，旨在“满足那些对当地大规模探矿活动感兴趣的蒙特利尔重要客户的需求”。 <sup>33</sup> </span></span></span>

British overseas banks formed to do business <span class="italic">in Canada</span> took <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_224"></span>exactly the same approach as the strictly Canadian ones; they moved very quickly into the United States. The Bank of British North America, London, was in the lead, with an owned agency in New York in the mid-1850s and a branch in San Francisco in 1864. There was also the Bank of British Columbia, headquartered in London, which established a branch in San Francisco in 1864 and one in Portland, Oregon, in 1865; these branches were acquired by the Canadian Bank of Commerce in 1901. What seems evident in the case of the 'Canadian banks' (whether headquartered in Canada or in Great Britain) is that there was a recognition that opening outlets south of the border was a logical extension of domestic banking. There is nothing in this spread into the United States that is strange to the student of multinational enterprise. The latter would expect that banks with experience in branching would extend across the border, sometimes following commercial activities and connections; sometimes in anticipation of future business; and most often with both motives. In an earlier work, I discussed how American business before 1914 'spilled over' the frontier into Canada.<sup>34</sup> This is the identical pattern. Everything fits: the rationale for the direct investment (to meet opportunities), the locations where the banks went (nearby, accessible), and the activities they engaged in (those familiar at home in Canada). If they had an advantage or found an appropriate niche in the market, they proved highly successful. The Bank of Montreal, for example, was a major bank in Chicago.<sup>35</sup> On the other hand, the Merchants' Bank of Halifax, in Republic, Washington, 'made no headway' and closed after five years.<sup>36</sup> There was no 'international banking department' established by these Canadian banks for the American business. Their behaviour was a logical spread of the banks over borders.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在加拿大开展业务的英国海外银行，与纯正的加拿大银行采取了完全相同的策略：它们迅速挺进美国市场。伦敦的英属北美银行率先行动，19 世纪 50 年代中期在纽约设立了代理机构，1864 年在旧金山开设了分行。还有总部位于伦敦的不列颠哥伦比亚银行，也在 1864 年于旧金山设立分行，1865 年在俄勒冈州的波特兰增设分行；这些分行于 1901 年被加拿大商业银行收购。在"加拿大银行"（无论其总部设在加拿大还是英国）的案例中，一个明显的事实是：它们认识到在美国边境以南开设分支机构是国内银行业务的合理延伸。对于研究跨国企业的学者而言，这种向美国扩张的现象并不令人感到奇怪。 后者则认为，拥有分行经验的银行会跨越边境拓展业务，有时是追随已有的商业活动和联系，有时是为未来业务提前布局，更多时候则是两种动机兼而有之。在早期研究中，我曾探讨过 1914 年以前美国商业如何“溢出”边境进入加拿大。 <sup>34</sup> 这与当前模式如出一辙。一切都能印证：直接投资的逻辑（抓住机遇）、银行选址（就近、可及）以及业务类型（延用在加拿大本土熟悉的模式）。一旦它们具备优势或找到合适的市场切入点，便往往大获成功。例如，蒙特利尔银行曾是芝加哥的一家重要银行。 <sup>35</sup> 而位于华盛顿州共和镇的哈利法克斯商人银行则“毫无进展”，五年后便关门歇业。 <sup>36</sup> 这些加拿大银行并未为美国业务设立单独的“国际银行部门”。 他们的行为是银行跨境扩张的必然逻辑延伸。</span></span></span>

Before 1914, in addition to those banks that operated in Canada and 'spilled over' the Dominion's southern boundary, other British-headquartered banks took part in American business. Here, the form and purposes were different. Thus, the major domestic joint-stock banks (such as London City and Midland and Lloyds) had domestic branches in Britain, experience in branching, and if New York state law had not forbidden it, they probably would have established branches in New York City.<sup>37</sup> But New York state had prohibited deposit-taking branches of foreign banks. London City and Midland as well as Lloyds had a network of correspondent relationships in New York and other American cities. For these British banks to branch anywhere aside from New York City would have made no economic sense. The locations were relatively remote to Great Britain; the operations would have <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_225"></span>been unfamiliar; the knowledge of these British bank managers derived in the geographically small British domestic market would not have been particularly relevant to American cities. There were, moreover and perhaps most <span class="italic">important,</span> by the late nineteenth and early twentieth century, literally thousands of domestic banks in the United States that by being on the spot had the advantage of awareness of local conditions. There was nothing in this respect that British joint-stock banks could provide better than the American ones. The British banks, however, did have access to lower-cost savings in the United Kingdom and thus could conceivably have provided lower-cost consumer and commercial loans.<sup>38</sup> But the cost of administering from a distance retail (or wholesale) branches in the United States would in all probability have offset that theoretical advantage. It is little wonder that, even without regulations to exclude them, the British joint-stock banks confined themselves to correspondent relationships.<sup>39</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1914 年之前，除了那些在加拿大运营并"溢出"自治领南部边界（即美国边境）的银行之外，其他总部设于英国的银行也涉足了美国业务。不过，其形式和目的有所不同。因此，英国主要的国内股份制银行（如 London City and Midland 和 Lloyds）在英国本土设有国内分行，在分行运营方面经验丰富，若非纽约州法律禁止，它们很可能早已在纽约市开设分行。 <sup>37</sup> 但纽约州当时确实禁止外国银行设立吸收存款的分支机构。London City and Midland 以及 Lloyds 在纽约及其他美国城市建立了代理行关系网络。对于这些英国银行而言，在除纽约市以外的任何地方设立分行都不具经济合理性。 这些地点与英国相隔遥远；业务操作方式也迥然不同；这些英国银行经理们的经验主要源于地理范围狭小的英国本土市场，对于美国城市而言并不具备特别的相关性。此外，或许更为重要的是，到了 19 世纪末 20 世纪初，美国本土已有数千家银行，它们因身处当地而拥有了解本地情况的优势。在这方面，英国股份制银行根本无法比美国银行做得更好。不过，英国银行确实能够利用英国国内较低成本的储蓄资源，因此理论上可以提供成本更低的消费和商业贷款。 <sup>38</sup> 但远距离管理美国零售（或批发）分行的成本，十有八九会抵消这一理论上的优势。 难怪就算没有法规排除他们，英国的股份制银行也仅限于建立代理行关系。 <sup>39</sup> </span></span></span>

There were two other categories of British banking institutions that did have foreign direct investments in the United States. One category was the <span class="italic">overseas bank</span> (and here there were two subtypes: those established specifically to operate in the United States and those set up to operate in other markets but that had in addition a presence in America); the second category was the <span class="italic">merchant bank.</span> Let's start with the British overseas banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">另外两类英国银行机构确实在美国有外国直接投资。一类是海外银行（这里有两个子类型：专门为在美国经营而设立的，以及为在其他市场经营而设立但同时在美国有业务的）；第二类是商人银行。我们先从英国的海外银行说起。</span></span></span>

These were the banks with foreign (including imperial) operations that Geoffrey Jones and David Merrett have discussed elsewhere in this volume. They were <span class="italic">formed</span> to do business <span class="italic">outside</span> the United Kingdom, typically in a particular region. Few were set up with the declared object of doing business <span class="italic">in the United States</span> and those few had been organized mainly in the 1860s to do business in the frontier state of California.<sup>40</sup> One was the London and San Francisco Bank Ltd., started in London in 1865. It could not carry on its banking functions from a London headquarters, thus it opened in San Francisco. As a firm, it was operating over borders.<sup>41</sup> For a while, before California developed its own banking system, it proved very successful. It eventually lost out, because it could not compete from afar with domestic banks that were on the spot. Eventually (in 1905), it was sold to Americans.<sup>42</sup> It <span class="italic">never</span> went through the pattern of correspondent bank, international banking department, etc. It plunged — as did its counterparts — directly into banking abroad.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些银行经营着境外（包括帝国）业务，杰弗里·琼斯与戴维·梅雷特在本卷其他章节已有论述。它们成立的目的就是在英国本土以外开展业务，通常聚焦于特定区域。只有极少数银行公开宣称要进入美国市场，其中多数是在 19 世纪 60 年代为了在边疆州加利福尼亚开展业务而成立的。 <sup>40</sup> 伦敦旧金山银行有限公司便是其中之一，1865 年创立于伦敦。它无法从伦敦总部执行银行职能，于是便在旧金山设立了营业点。作为一家公司，它跨越国界开展业务。 <sup>41</sup> 在加利福尼亚尚未建立自己的银行体系之前，这家银行曾一度大获成功。但最终因为无法远程与立足当地的本土银行竞争而败下阵来。最终（1905 年），它被出售给了美国人。 <sup>42</sup> 它从未走过代理行、国际银行部等套路。和同行们一样，它直接一头扎进了海外银行业务。</span></span></span>

Interestingly, the largest number of British overseas banks with an American presence were of the second type, those <span class="italic">not</span> designed to do business specifically in the United States. Instead, they were the banks (like those already mentioned that did business in <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_226"></span>Canada) that were inaugurated to operate elsewhere overseas, in the Empire or outside. These banks felt a need to have a 'listening post' in the United States, yet most hoped to do more in America than merely obtain information. Thus, in December 1863, the Commercial Bank of India, Bombay, formed in England in 1845, opened in San Francisco; it expected to ship specie to the Orient. It did not survive, failing with the Overend, Gurney crisis of 1866.<sup>43</sup> Other British imperial banks set up branches in San Francisco, the most important one being the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation in 1875.<sup>44</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">有趣的是，数量最多的在美英国海外银行属于第二类，即那些并非专门为在美国开展业务而设立的银行。相反，它们是像前面提到的在加拿大开展业务的银行那样，最初旨在帝国境内或海外其他地区运营的银行。这些银行感到有必要在美国设立一个“监听哨”，但大多数银行希望在美国做的不仅仅是获取信息。因此，1863 年 12 月，1845 年成立于英格兰的孟买印度商业银行在旧金山开业，计划向东方运送金银铸币。这家银行未能存活，在 1866 年的奥弗伦德-格尼危机中倒闭。 <sup>43</sup> 其他英国帝国银行也在旧金山设立了分行，其中最重要的是 1875 年开设的香港上海汇丰银行。 <sup>44</sup> </span></span></span>

Much more pronounced was the entry of British overseas banks on the East Coast, specifically in New York City. By 1914, nine such banks with London headquarters had owned agencies in New York: the African Banking Corporation; Anglo South American Bank; Bank of British North America; Chartered Bank of India, Australia, and China; Colonial Bank; Commercial Bank of Spanish America; London and Brazilian Bank; London and River Plate Bank; and Standard Bank of South Africa.<sup>45</sup> So, too, the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation, Hong Kong, had an owned New York agency.<sup>46</sup> These banks ranked among the leading British overseas banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">更为显著的是英国海外银行在东海岸（尤其是纽约市）的入驻。到 1914 年，已有九家总部设在伦敦的此类银行在纽约设有代理机构：非洲银行公司、英南美洲银行、英属北美银行、印度、澳大利亚及中国特许银行、殖民地银行、西属美洲商业银行、伦敦与巴西银行、伦敦与拉普拉塔河银行、以及南非标准银行。 <sup>45</sup> 此外，总部位于香港的汇丰银行也在纽约设有代理机构。 <sup>46</sup> 这些银行均属于英国领先的海外银行之列。</span></span></span>

These British international and imperial banks were not in New York City to provide for the transfer of their home country's capital to America — although the United States was at this time a very large recipient of British capital. Their New York presence was, in part, to finance US and British international trade to, from, and in the regions where they had expertise. Most crucial, their New York agencies provided vital information. Only one of these agencies (that of the Bank of British North America) appears to have made purely domestic loans in New York. None took deposits in New York. It was these banks that had the formidable international banking networks that impress today's authors on international banking. In short, as of 1914, I can count eleven owned agencies of British overseas banks in New York, and two of the banks had 'branches' in San Francisco (the Bank of British North America and the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation); thus, as of 1914, eleven British overseas banks had thirteen outlets in the United States. By then none remained that was designed to do business exclusively in the United States.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这些英国的国际银行和帝国银行在纽约设立机构，目的并非是将本国资本转移至美国——尽管当时美国是英国资本的大额接收方。它们在纽约的存在，部分是为了资助英美两国与其专业领域所在地区之间的国际贸易。最关键的是，它们在纽约的分支机构提供了至关重要的信息。这些机构中，只有一家（Bank of British North America）似乎在纽约发放过纯国内贷款，且没有一家在纽约吸收存款。正是这些银行拥有强大的国际银行网络，令当今那些撰写国际银行著作的作者们印象深刻。 简而言之，截至 1914 年，我数出有 11 家英国海外银行在纽约设有自有代理机构，其中两家银行（Bank of British North America 和 Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation）在旧金山设有"分行"；因此，截至 1914 年，11 家英国海外银行在美国共拥有 13 个网点。到那时，已经没有哪家银行是专为在美国开展业务而设立的了。</span></span></span>

British <span class="italic">merchant banks</span> had entirely separate forms <span class="italic">and </span>rationales for their spread into America.<sup>47</sup> These firms, in contrast with the British joint-stock banks and the British overseas banks, date back earlier and over the years were highly significant in intermediating long-term British monies across the Atlantic. Many <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_227"></span>had sizeable business in America, from the handling of acceptances to the presentation of American issues in London.<sup>48</sup> One of the most, if not <span class="italic">the</span> most, important British merchant bank participating in American business was Baring Brothers & Co. Ltd. (and its predecessors); its relationship with America had its origins as far back as colonial times. The firm's first sizeable direct investment in the United States had been in the eighteenth century, a joint-venture, with the Dutch banking house, Hope & Co., in land development in Maine (in 1796). The story of the Barings in America mirrors US history. Yet its <span class="italic">direct investment presence </span>— its extension as a firm - was almost 'miscellaneous'.<sup>49</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">英国商人银行进入美国市场的方式和动因截然不同。 <sup>47</sup> 这些公司与英国的股份制银行和海外银行不同，其历史更为悠久，多年来在将英国长期资金跨大西洋中介过程中发挥了重要作用。许多公司在美国拥有可观的业务，从承兑汇票到在伦敦推介美国发行项目。 <sup>48</sup> 参与美国业务的最重要（若非最重要）的英国商人银行之一是巴林兄弟有限公司（及其前身）；其与美国的关系可追溯至殖民时期。该公司在美国的首笔大规模直接投资是在 18 世纪，与荷兰银行霍普公司合资开发缅因州土地（1796 年）。巴林家族在美的发展史堪称美国历史的缩影。 然而，它作为公司的直接投资业务——几乎是个“大杂烩”。 <sup>49</sup> </span></span></span>

The Barings were deeply involved in the Bank of the United States, the Second Bank of the United States, as well as its successor, the Bank of the United States (of Pennsylvania).<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">巴林银行深度参与了美国银行、第二美国银行及其后继者宾夕法尼亚美国银行的业务。</span></span></span>

From the early 1830s to the mid-1880s, Baring Brothers had salaried representatives in the United States. It also had independent sources of information and independent agents. In 1886, a Baring became a partner in Kidder, Peabody & Co., a Boston investment banking house, with a New York office. In 1891, the New York office became Baring, Magoun, & Co.; the Barings contributed \$2 million to the new firm's capital, a sum matched by Kidder, Peabody. For the first time in its over a century of American business, the Barings had a house in America that carried the family name. From 1906 to 1908, its New York firm was called Baring & Co. After 1908, there was no Baring family member stationed in the United States; the New York outlet once again became (as it had been from 1886 to 1890) a branch office of Kidder, Peabody, Boston, albeit unlike in that earlier four year period no Baring was a partner in the Boston firm. This sequence of Baring 'representation' in America — as is evident —involved a changing, but far from dramatic presence, despite the firm's substantial American business.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">从 19 世纪 30 年代初到 80 年代中期，巴林兄弟公司在美国设有带薪代表，同时拥有独立的信息来源和代理机构。1886 年，一位巴林家族成员成为波士顿投资银行基德尔-皮博迪公司（Kidder, Peabody & Co.）的合伙人，该公司在纽约设有办事处。1891 年，纽约办事处更名为巴林-马贡公司（Baring, Magoun, & Co.）；巴林家族向新公司注资 200 万美元，基德尔-皮博迪公司也出资了相同数额。这是巴林家族在美国开展业务一个多世纪以来，首次在美国设立以家族名字命名的商号。1906 年至 1908 年间，其纽约公司被称为巴林公司（Baring & Co.）。1908 年后，再无巴林家族成员常驻美国；纽约的机构再次成为波士顿基德尔-皮博迪公司的分支机构（如同 1886 年至 1890 年期间一样），但与之前那四年不同的是，此时波士顿公司中已没有巴林家族的合伙人。 尽管 Baring 在美国的业务规模庞大，但其在美洲的‘代表处’序列——显然——经历了变化，却远非显著的存在。</span></span></span>

Baring Brothers offered American securities to the British public; often in connection with its underwritings, the merchant bank acquired sizeable holdings in securities (equity in particular) and it held these securities for quite a long duration. We know, for example, that throughout the 1880s the Barings were the owners of a record of 22,300 shares in the Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe railway company.<sup>50</sup> Is this direct investment? It was obviously for business purposes, for information, and it did give the Barings influence on management when things went wrong. In another instance, in 1899, Baring Brothers took part in selling in Britain the securities of the New England Cotton Yarn Company. In the <span class="italic">1920s,</span> the Barings still held a large block of securities in the <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_228"></span>successor to this firm.<sup>51</sup> In short, the Barings — giant intermediators of capital to America — had direct investments in the United States in banking and in other activities as well.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">Baring Brothers 向英国公众推销美国证券；通常与其承销业务相关，这家商业银行会大量收购证券（尤其是股权），并且长期持有这些证券。例如，我们知道在整个 19 世纪 80 年代，Barings 曾持有 Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe 铁路公司创纪录的 22,300 股股份。 <sup>50</sup> 这算直接投资吗？显然是为了商业目的、获取信息，而且当问题出现时，的确让 Barings 对管理层有了影响力。另一个例子是，1899 年，Baring Brothers 参与了在英国出售 New England Cotton Yarn Company 证券的业务。到了 20 世纪 20 年代，Barings 仍持有这家公司继承者的大量证券。 <sup>51</sup> 简而言之，Barings——向美国输送资本的巨型中介——在美国银行业及其他领域都拥有直接投资。</span></span></span>

The Rothschilds paid the salary of August Belmont when he first went to the United States in 1837. (Before that the British Rothschilds had had independent agents in America). In time, Belmont developed his own private banking house; he became the 'independent' agent for the Rothschilds. A member of the Rothschild family (by marriage) had a banking house in California for a period of time in the mid-nineteenth century. On the whole, however, while the Rothschilds were active participants in American business, they, even less than the Barings, extended themselves, <span class="italic">as a banking firm</span> into the United States. Yet they too had equity interests in a number of American enterprises, interests directly related to business purposes (for example, assisting in their extensive mining business).<sup>52</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">罗斯柴尔德家族支付了奥古斯特·贝尔蒙特的薪水，当时他于 1837 年首次前往美国。（在此之前，英国的罗斯柴尔德家族在美国已有独立的代理人）。后来，贝尔蒙特发展了自己的私人银行，成为罗斯柴尔德家族的“独立”代理人。19 世纪中期，罗斯柴尔德家族的一位成员（通过联姻）在加州经营了一家银行一段时间。然而，总体而言，尽管罗斯柴尔德家族积极参与美国商业活动，但他们甚至比巴林家族更少以银行公司的形式扩展到美国。不过，他们也在许多美国企业中拥有股权，这些权益直接与商业目的相关（例如，协助其广泛的采矿业务）。 <sup>52</sup> </span></span></span>

All the literature that I have read on banks as multinationals assumes that when they extend <span class="italic">abroad</span> they invest in some kind of <span class="italic">banking</span> activity. This is obviously not always the case. If a bank makes, or obtains in exchange for services rendered, or holds a sizeable equity investment in a non-banking business abroad, should we call this foreign direct investment? Suppose such interests are large enough to exercise influence but not sufficient to exercise full control, how does a theory of multinational enterprise handle such stakes? I will return to this question.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我所读过的所有关于银行作为跨国企业的文献都假设，当银行向海外扩张时，它们投资的是某种银行业务。但事情显然并非总是如此。如果一家银行在海外非银行业务中进行了大额股权投资，或者通过提供服务换取、或持有了这样一笔投资，这算不算对外直接投资？假设这些投资足以施加影响，但还不足以实现完全控制，那么跨国企业理论该如何看待这种参股行为？我会在后续探讨这个问题。</span></span></span>

It is impossible to survey all the British merchant banks in America. While many had a large American banking business, only a few had a banking presence in America. It was <span class="italic">not</span> needed in order to conduct international banking.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">不可能对美国所有的英国商业银行进行逐一考察。尽管其中许多银行在美国有庞大的银行业务，但只有少数几家在美国设有实体银行机构。对于开展国际银行业务来说，这种实体机构并非必需。</span></span></span>

There were other British financial intermediaries that invested in the United States, mortgage companies, investment trusts, and insurance companies. While British building societies did not invest, specialized English and Scottish mortgage companies did provide loans to American borrowers and did develop a sizeable presence in the United States to do so. Numerous 'free-standing' British mortgage companies were formed to meet the needs of western farmers and, to a far lesser extent, urban requirements. American banking institutions did not have adequate capital. The British with available monies established themselves in the United States to participate in this very lucrative business.<sup>53</sup> It was impossible to do mortgage lending long distance. A US presence was required. Some of the Scottish investment trusts were at origin mortgage companies. Certain Scottish and English investment trusts had independent agencies or representatives in the United <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_229"></span>States to represent them in making purely portfolio American investments. The International Financial Society had numerous US involvements, but no direct representation in America.<sup>54</sup> British insurance companies were truly multinational enterprises. Many had branches and affiliates in the United States to sell insurance; if they sold insurance in America, they had to have (under state laws) American assets. Also, British insurance companies that did <span class="italic">not</span> sell insurance in America often had US investments, which were part of the firm's financial assets and did not require the extension of the firm abroad.<sup>55</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">还有其他英国金融中介机构在美国进行投资，比如抵押贷款公司、投资信托和保险公司。虽然英国建筑协会并未涉足这一领域，但专门从事抵押贷款业务的英格兰和苏格兰公司确实向美国借款人提供贷款，并为此在美国建立了相当规模的存在。众多"独立型"英国抵押贷款公司应运而生，主要满足西部农民的需求，相比之下，城市需求则少得多。当时美国银行机构的资本并不充足。拥有可支配资金的英国人在美国立足，参与这一利润丰厚的业务。 <sup>53</sup> 远程办理抵押贷款是不可能的，必须在当地有实体存在。一些苏格兰投资信托公司最初就是抵押贷款公司出身。 一些苏格兰和英格兰的投资信托在美国设有独立机构或代表，专门负责其在美纯投资组合的事务。国际金融协会虽在美国多有涉足，但并无直接代表处。 <sup>54</sup> 英国保险公司堪称真正的跨国企业，许多公司在美国设有分公司和附属机构销售保险；若要在美国开展保险业务，根据州法规定必须持有美国本土资产。此外，那些未在美国销售保险的英国保险公司也常持有美国投资，这些投资属于公司金融资产的一部分，无需将公司业务扩展至海外。 <sup>55</sup> </span></span></span>

Dutch bankers had significant interests in American lending from the 1780s onward. In 1789, four Dutch banking firms sent Theophile Cazenove to America to take charge of their investments in land, public debt, and to a lesser extent other securities.<sup>56</sup> Throughout the nineteenth and into the twentieth century, Dutch investments in the United States (with a few quiet periods) remained important. Dutch banking houses were intimately involved in facilitating the transfer of long-term capital to America,<sup>57</sup> yet, with the exception of some early interests of private banks and some later activities of the Dutch mortgage banks, the amount of Dutch <span class="italic">banking</span> presence in America was small, and intermittent. By contrast, in 1914 there were a dozen or so Dutch mortgage banks in the United States that took part in American lending. These firms did extend themselves into the United States.<sup>58</sup> At one time, a Dutch mortgage bank had foreclosed on the major real estate properties in downtown Spokane.<sup>59</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">自 18 世纪 80 年代起，荷兰银行家们便在美国贷款业务中占据重要份额。1789 年，四家荷兰银行公司派遣 Theophile Cazenove 前往美国，负责他们在土地、公共债务以及少量其他证券方面的投资。 <sup>56</sup> 整个 19 世纪直至 20 世纪，荷兰在美国的投资（虽有短暂沉寂期）始终举足轻重。荷兰银行机构深度参与了向美国输送长期资本的过程， <sup>57</sup> 然而，除少数私人银行的早期利益以及荷兰抵押银行后来的部分业务外，荷兰银行在美国的实际存在规模始终不大且断断续续。相比之下，1914 年约有十几家荷兰抵押银行在美国参与贷款业务。这些机构确实将业务拓展到了美国本土。 <sup>58</sup> 曾有一段时间，一家荷兰抵押银行甚至取消了斯波坎市中心主要房地产的赎回权。 <sup>59</sup> </span></span></span>

Dutch private bankers — like their British counterparts (the British merchant bankers) — and maybe even more so, had equity interests in a number of American companies whose securities they parlayed. I repeat my question: do we count such interests as direct investment? The motive was to keep in touch in case anything went wrong. The goal was clearly financial <span class="italic">not</span> operational. There was, moreover, another special feature of the Dutch bankers' activities in America. Very early the Dutch developed what they called 'administrative offices'. These offices held American securities and sold to Dutch individual investors a certificate —denominated in florins — that represented the securities. The Dutch banker then collected interest and dividends, handled the foreign exchange, and paid the ultimate investor in florins. When the securities were equities, this device served to concentrate voting power in the hands of the bankers.<sup>60</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">荷兰的私人银行家——与他们的英国同行（英国商人银行家）类似，甚至有过之而无不及——曾持有多家美国公司的股权，并对这些证券进行运作。我再重复一遍我的问题：这类权益是否应算作直接投资？其动机是为了保持联系，以防出现任何差错。目标显然是金融性的，而非运营性的。此外，荷兰银行家在美国的活动还有另一个特殊之处。他们很早就设立了所谓的“行政办事处”。这些办事处持有美国证券，并向荷兰的个人投资者出售一种以荷兰盾计价的凭证，代表这些证券。然后，荷兰银行家负责收取利息和股息，处理外汇兑换，并以荷兰盾向最终投资者支付。当这些证券为股票时，这种机制有助于将投票权集中在银行家手中。</span></span></span>

The Dutch associations in American business were often part and parcel of the cosmopolitan international banking structures that characterized nineteenth-century Europe. The literature on <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_230"></span>contemporary multinationals frequently deals with co-operative arrangements across borders between firms. There is an assumption that joint-ventures and co-operative enterprises are 'new forms'.<sup>61</sup> Yet, in nineteenth- and early twentieth-century banking, the permutations and combinations in the extension of firms abroad are extraordinary.<sup>62</sup> In American business, for example, the Dutch Hope & Co. and the Barings often worked in close association with one another. I have mentioned their joint-venture in land. According to Philip Ziegler, by 1813 'Hopes was an empty shell'. The Barings 'took it over'. It provided a 'berth' for family members. Hope & Co. revived and, integrated with the Barings, took part with them in numerous US financial undertakings.<sup>63</sup> In the 1890 crisis, Hope & Co. would assist the Barings, obtaining a small interest in Barings' capital at that time.<sup>64</sup> The Dutch Boissevain family had participated for generations in American business — sometimes through a co-operative venture (a partnership) in London, with interlocking American partners.<sup>65</sup> German and French banks, on occasion through Amsterdam branches, were frequent participants in US business — <span class="italic">without</span> US representation <span class="italic">per se.</span> The saga of the Bischoffsheims provides a case of international migration, international investments, and transatlantic connections.<sup>66</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">美国商业中的荷兰协会往往与十九世纪欧洲特有的国际银行体系密不可分。关于当代跨国企业的文献经常探讨企业间的跨境合作安排，有一种假设认为合资企业和合作企业是"新形态"。 <sup>61</sup> 然而，在十九世纪和二十世纪初的银行业中，企业在海外扩张的排列组合方式极为多样。 <sup>62</sup> 例如，在美国商业中，荷兰的 Hope & Co.与 Barings 经常紧密合作。我曾提到过他们在土地方面的合资项目。据 Philip Ziegler 记载，到 1813 年"Hope & Co.已是空壳"，Barings"接管了它"，并为其家族成员提供了"安身之处"。Hope & Co.得以复兴，并与 Barings 整合，共同参与了多项美国金融事业。 <sup>63</sup> 在 1890 年的危机中，Hope & Co. 将会协助巴林银行，在当时获取其资本的一小部分权益。 <sup>64</sup> 荷兰的 Boissevain 家族世代参与美国商业——有时通过伦敦的合作企业（合伙企业），并与美国合伙人形成连锁关系。 <sup>65</sup> 德国和法国的银行偶尔通过阿姆斯特丹分行，频繁参与美国商业活动——但自身并未在美国设立代表处。Bischoffsheims 家族的故事则提供了一个关于国际迁徙、国际投资与跨大西洋联系的实例。 <sup>66</sup> </span></span></span>

By 1914 Germans were second only to the British as foreign investors in America. German banks were intermediaries in the international investment process. Their American 'houses' were often private banks. The Deutsche Bank, for example, had a whole range of American interests. Its first US 'banking' connection was in 1872 when it obtained a 'silent partnership' in the private bank Knoblauch & Lichtenstein, New York, an interest it held until 1882 when the New York bank was liquidated.<sup>67</sup> From 1886 to 1890, Henry Villard served as the Deutsche Bank's representative. Villard was followed by Edward D. Adams.<sup>68</sup> The Deutsche Bank was active in selling American securities in Germany, and in no company was it more involved than in the Northern Pacific Railroad. It had equity interests in the Northern Pacific, and also in Edison General Electric Company. It brought the leading German electric firms, Siemens & Halske and AEG, into investments in Edison GE.<sup>69</sup> There is evidence that the Deutsche Bank aided its client by lending to US <span class="italic">customers</span> of Siemens & Halske.<sup>70</sup> The Deutsche Bank used its US representative to monitor these arrangements. Its men in America were crucial sources of information gathering.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">到 1914 年，德国人在美国的外国投资者中仅次于英国人。德国银行在国际投资过程中充当着中介角色。他们在美国的“分行”多为私人银行。例如，德意志银行在美国拥有广泛的利益。它首次涉足美国“银行业”是在 1872 年，当时与纽约的私人银行 Knoblauch & Lichtenstein 建立了“隐名合伙关系”，这一关系一直持续到 1882 年该纽约银行清算为止。 <sup>67</sup> 1886 年至 1890 年间，亨利·维拉德担任德意志银行的代表，此后由爱德华·D·亚当斯接任。 <sup>68</sup> 德意志银行积极在德国销售美国证券，其中参与最深的当属北太平洋铁路公司。它不仅持有北太平洋铁路的股权，还持有爱迪生通用电气公司的股份。德意志银行还引领德国顶级电气公司西门子与哈尔斯克以及 AEG 投资爱迪生通用电气。 <sup>69</sup> 有证据表明，德意志银行通过向西门子与哈尔斯克公司的美国客户放贷，为其客户提供了支持。 <sup>70</sup> 德意志银行还借助其在美的代理人来监督这些安排。它在美洲的常驻人员是情报收集的关键枢纽。</span></span></span>

The Deutsche Bank's extension as a banking firm in America was minor compared with its significant role in intermediating <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_231"></span>capital across the Atlantic. The Bank had all kinds of informal (along with its formal) relationships in America; some were through its London branch; and many were through German private banks. <span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">德意志银行作为一家银行机构在美国的扩张规模，与其在跨大西洋资本中介中发挥的重大作用相比，显得微不足道。该行在美国建立了各种正式与非正式的关系网络——有些是通过其伦敦分行，许多则是通过德国的私人银行。法兰克福、伦敦和纽约的私人银行家斯派尔家族，常与德意志银行联手参与美国的各类金融项目；同样，法兰克福的私人银行雅各布·斯特恩（其在伦敦设有办事处，但未涉足美国）也与德意志银行在美国的商业活动中关系密切。 <sup>71</sup> </span></span></span>

Space precludes a discussion of the numerous US outlets of German banks that engaged in American business, yet what seems evident is that German banks could take part in American issues and participate in the intermediation of capital to America without large stakes in extending as <span class="italic">firms</span> into banking operations in the United States.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">限于篇幅，我们无法详述德国银行在美国的众多分支机构及其参与美国业务的情况。但显而易见的是，德国银行能够参与美国的金融业务，并作为中介将资本引入美国，而无需在美国本土大规模拓展银行经营实体。</span></span></span>

French banks had many American alliances, more often informal than formal. Hottinguer & Co.'s US business came to be closely identified with Baring Brothers' American interests. Credit Foncier took part in the formation of the United States Mortgage Company (1871).<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">法国银行与美方建立了许多同盟关系，这些关系往往是非正式的。尽管这些银行在美国设有代理机构（有些独立经营，有些则不然），但其存在感不及英国、德国甚至荷兰的银行。然而，美国确实存在法国‘独立’银行机构。同样，法国罗斯柴尔德家族与英国罗斯柴尔德家族一样，在纽约都通过同一位私人银行家（奥古斯特·贝尔蒙公司）开展业务。霍廷格公司在美国的业务最终与巴林兄弟公司在美洲的利益紧密相连。法国土地信贷银行则参与了美国抵押贷款公司（1871 年）的创立。</span></span></span>

I could continue, discussing in great detail the Canadian, British, Dutch, German, and French banking associations and also those of Swiss, Swedish, Italian, Hungarian, Czech, Japanese, and other banks that had some sort of US presence before the First World War.<sup>72</sup> The point is that, Canadian banks excepted, of all the foreign banks' numerous US involvements, in 1914 none was important in purely <span class="italic">domestic</span> commercial banking. There were and had been over the years, however, many more foreign banks present in the United States in banking and non-banking activities than there were US banks with operations abroad.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我还可以继续详细讨论第一次世界大战前，加拿大、英国、荷兰、德国、法国的银行协会，以及瑞士、瑞典、意大利、匈牙利、捷克、日本等国在美国有一定业务的银行。 <sup>72</sup> 关键在于，除了加拿大银行外，在所有外国银行涉足美国的众多业务中，1914 年没有哪一家在纯粹的国内商业银行领域占据重要地位。然而，多年来，以银行或非银行业务形式出现在美国的外国银行，其数量远超在海外开展业务的美国银行。</span></span></span>

In 1910 there were in the United States 7,138 national banks and 17,376 state and private banks. No American bank ranked among the ten largest in the world. None participated in purely domestic banking abroad. Long before the First World War, National City Bank received foreign deposits and did foreign lending — the equivalent of goods imports and exports. It was thus involved in international banking. Yet, by law it could not branch abroad (until the passage of the Federal Reserve Act in December 1913, no national bank could branch abroad).<sup>73</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1910 年，美国共有 7138 家国民银行和 17376 家州立及私营银行。没有一家美国银行跻身全球前十。没有一家银行在海外从事纯粹的国内银行业务。早在第一次世界大战之前，国家城市银行（National City Bank）就已接收外国存款并发放外国贷款——这相当于商品进出口业务。因此它已涉足国际银行业。然而，根据法律，它不能在海外设立分行（直到 1913 年 12 月《联邦储备法案》通过之前，任何国民银行都不得在海外开设分行）。 <sup>73</sup> </span></span></span>

Kuhn, Loeb's associations with the Warburgs in Germany were family ones.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\

## III

How do we link this evidence from pre-First World War America into an analysis of banks as multinationals? How relevant is our application of what we know about non-bank multinationals to banks? A number of interesting items emerge.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">我们该如何将第一次世界大战前美国的这些证据，与银行作为跨国公司的分析联系起来？我们关于非银行跨国公司的知识，对银行的适用性有多强？其中浮现出了若干耐人寻味的问题。</span></span></span>

Obviously, a vast pot-pourri existed in the extent, the form, and the rationale behind the spread of foreign banks into the United States and of American banks abroad in the pre-1914 years. One <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_234"></span>is able, by considering these banks as multinationals, to explain how much, how, and why the banks moved or did not move over borders. Also clear is the variation over time. What seems evident in the American case is that foreign banks filled in where American financial institutions were inadequate. Likewise, when American banks went abroad, they did so to fill gaps.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">显然，在 1914 年前的岁月里，外国银行进入美国以及美国银行在海外扩张的规模、形式及其背后的原因，构成了一个庞杂的大杂烩。通过将这些银行视为跨国企业，我们能够解释银行跨境流动的规模、方式及动因——它们为何选择跨越国界，又为何止步不前。同时，这种流动也呈现出明显的时代差异性。在美国的案例中，显而易见的是，外国银行填补了美国金融机构力所不及的空白。同样地，当美国银行走向海外时，它们也是为了填补类似的空缺。</span></span></span>

The earliest banks with a presence in America were the British and Dutch merchant banks. From the mid-1850s onward, foreign banks in the United States developed an ever wider range of different types of banking presences: representative offices, owned agencies, branches, interlocking partnerships in private banks, and full-fledged banks. While some foreign banks entered the United States to serve their existing customers, most were in the country to take advantage of opportunities. It was useful for a bank to have a representative, an owned agency, a branch, or a subsidiary, or interlocking partnerships, to be near where 'the action was' — to be able to gain accurate information that could not be obtained from a distance. By the late nineteenth, early twentieth century, America was the great growth country of the world, <span class="italic">par excellence.</span><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">最早在美国开展业务的银行是英国和荷兰的商业银行。自 19 世纪 50 年代中期起，外国银行在美国不断拓展各类银行业务形式：代表处、自有代理机构、分行、私营银行的连锁合伙制以及全功能银行。虽然有些外国银行进入美国是为了服务现有客户，但大多数银行是来此抓住机遇的。银行设立代表处、自有代理机构、分行、子公司或连锁合伙制，便于贴近"业务前沿"——从而获取远距离无法获得的准确信息。到 19 世纪末 20 世纪初，美国已成为举世无双的超级增长大国。</span></span></span>

Sometimes, banks led their Industrial customers. Thus, as noted above, the Deutsche Bank's representative was responsible for Siemens & Halske's first US investment.<sup>85</sup> Sometimes they followed (this was the case with Merchants' Bank of Halifax in Republic, Washington, and some of the other Canadian banks in the United States).<sup>86</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">有时，银行会引领其工业客户。例如，如上所述，德意志银行代表负责了西门子与哈尔斯克在美国的首笔投资。 <sup>85</sup> 而有时，银行则会跟随（例如，华盛顿州共和市的哈利法克斯商业银行，以及在美国的其他一些加拿大银行就是如此）。 <sup>86</sup> </span></span></span>

Robert Aliber writes that 'casual observation' suggests that the expansion in the 1960s, 1970s, and early 1980s of banks abroad parallels that of industrial enterprises.<sup>87</sup> In very rough terms, this might well be said of foreign banks and foreign industrial enterprises in the United States in the post-1914 years, but for the pre First World War years the parallels are merely <span class="italic">very rough</span> and <span class="italic">very general.</span> When one concentrates on the specifics of industrial enterprises and foreign banks' establishments in the United States before 1914, the synchronization evaporates.<sup>88</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">罗伯特·艾利伯写道，“直观观察”表明，20 世纪 60 年代、70 年代及 80 年代初银行的海外扩张与工业企业的扩张是同步的。 <sup>87</sup> 粗略而言，这一说法或许适用于 1914 年后在美国的外国银行和外国工业企业，但就第一次世界大战前的时期而言，这种同步性仅仅是相当粗略且泛泛的。当我们聚焦于 1914 年前在美国的特定工业企业与外国银行机构时，这种同步性便不复存在了。 <sup>88</sup> </span></span></span>

Some foreign banks participated in domestic banking through their own banks, principally in San Francisco and Chicago. In San Francisco, the agency of the London and Paris Rothschilds (opened in 1849) was, by 1863, according to one bank historian, doing the 'largest banking business' in the city. Yet, in 1880, its successor shut down; and the Bank of California agreed to act as correspondent for the London Rothschilds.<sup>89</sup> Other foreign-owned banks opened in San Francisco and did a booming business. The Bank of British Columbia was one of the three leading banks in San Francisco in the immediate post-Civil War years. As noted <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_235"></span>earlier, the London and San Francisco Bank was also a significant institution.<sup>90</sup> Yet, in the late nineteenth century and certainly in the early twentieth century, the relative importance of this kind of direct participation by foreign banks in domestic banking diminished; American banks were in a far better position to meet domestic needs. Chicago was exceptional in 1914 in that one of the principal banks there was foreign-owned — the Bank of Montreal. The Bank of Montreal in Chicago in 1914 was the largest bank in that city (measured by capital and surplus), a commercial bank taking deposits and making loans.<sup>91</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">一些外国银行通过自身机构参与了美国国内银行业务，主要集中在旧金山和芝加哥。在旧金山，伦敦和巴黎罗斯柴尔德家族的代理处（于 1849 年开业）到 1863 年时，据一位银行史学家称，已在该城市开展"规模最大的银行业务"。然而，1880 年，其继任机构关闭；加利福尼亚银行同意担任伦敦罗斯柴尔德家族的代理行。 <sup>89</sup> 其他外资银行在旧金山开业，业务蓬勃发展。不列颠哥伦比亚银行是美国内战结束后最初几年旧金山三大领先银行之一。如前所述，伦敦与旧金山银行也是一家重要机构。 <sup>90</sup> 然而，在 19 世纪末，尤其是 20 世纪初，这类外国银行直接参与美国国内银行业的相对重要性有所减弱；美国银行在满足国内需求方面处于更有利的地位。 1914 年的芝加哥非同寻常，因为那里有一家主要银行是外资所有的——蒙特利尔银行。1914 年，位于芝加哥的蒙特利尔银行是该市最大的银行（按资本和盈余计算），是一家吸收存款、发放贷款的有商业银行。 <sup>91</sup> </span></span></span>

In California before the mid-1870s and in Illinois foreign banks had filled in where American ones were deficient. In California, over time, domestic institutions replaced the foreign ones. American banks could do everything that foreign ones could do in the domestic market — and do it better. In Chicago, which was linked to Canadian cities by railroads and trade connections, the extension of the Bank of Montreal continued — a logical and important association that filled Americans' domestic needs but also provided for international requirements. The presence of foreign banks (the Bank of Montreal and the Bank of Nova Scotia) in an important role in Chicago in 1914 was exceptional.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在 19 世纪 70 年代中期之前的加利福尼亚州和伊利诺伊州，外资银行填补了美国本土银行不足的空白。随着时间的推移，在加利福尼亚州，本土机构逐渐取代了外资银行。美国银行在国内市场可以做外资银行能做的一切——而且做得更好。在芝加哥，由于铁路和贸易往来与加拿大城市紧密相连，蒙特利尔银行的业务得以持续扩展——这是一种合乎逻辑且意义重大的关联，既满足了美国人的国内需求，也提供了国际服务。1914 年，外资银行（蒙特利尔银行和新斯科舍银行）在芝加哥扮演重要角色，这是非同寻常的。</span></span></span>

Foreign mortgage companies and mortgage banks with US representation in the late nineteenth and early twentieth century provided capital where American banking institutions lacked it. The mortgage companies were particularly in evidence in the 1880s, and, because mortgage lending was a very local activity, these firms required a US presence. While some remained in 1914, in terms of the overall mortgage lending in the country, their role was not substantial.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">19 世纪末 20 世纪初，在美国银行体系资金不足的领域，那些设有美国代表处的外国抵押贷款公司和抵押银行提供了资本。这些抵押贷款公司在 19 世纪 80 年代尤为活跃，由于抵押贷款业务具有极强的地域性，它们必须在美设立机构。尽管到 1914 年仍有部分公司留存，但从全美抵押贷款总额来看，其作用并不显著。</span></span></span>

In short, in the 'bread and butter' of banking, commercial banking, in most of the country by 1914, the numerous American commercial banks (both state and national ones) could handle routine American domestic banking; there was no place for foreign banks. American banks crowded out foreign ones. Foreign banks had no advantage.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">简而言之，到 1914 年，美国大部分地区的银行业核心业务——商业银行领域，众多美国商业银行（包括州立银行和全国性银行）已足以处理常规的国内银行业务，外国银行毫无立足之地。美国银行挤占了外国银行的生存空间，后者毫无竞争优势可言。</span></span></span>

Before 1914, however, banks in America developed little expertise with foreign trade financing, and foreign banks often were of substantial help in this regard. (The largest American banks, national banks, could not handle acceptances.)<sup>92</sup> But, it is interesting that, in the main, the foreign financial institutions that engaged in international trade financing could supervise most of the transactions from London. What they needed (and got) in New York was reliable information. To obtain information did not require a large US presence.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1914 年前，美国银行在外贸融资方面经验甚少，外国银行常在这方面提供实质性帮助。（美国最大的银行——国民银行——当时无法处理承兑业务。） <sup>92</sup> 然而有趣的是，从事国际贸易融资的外国金融机构大多能从伦敦监控大部分交易。它们在纽约所需（且已获得）的是可靠的信息。获取信息并不需要在美拥有庞大的业务布局。</span></span></span>

<span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_236"></span>So too, the foreign merchant banks that were important in financing trade <span class="italic">and</span> in the intermediation of long-term capital into the United States did not have to have a major banking presence in the capital-recipient country to accomplish their purposes. Yet, like the other foreign banks that financed American trade, they too filled needs not adequately provided for by American institutions.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">同样地，那些在贸易融资和将长期资本引入美国方面扮演重要角色的外国商人银行，并不需要在资本接收国拥有庞大的银行业务网点来实现其目标。然而，与其他为美国贸易提供融资的外国银行一样，它们也填补了美国金融机构未能充分满足的需求。</span></span></span>

American banks abroad were far fewer than foreign banks in the United States. The forms they took in their extension overseas were more limited (interlocking partnerships and offices overseas). The rationales were different. In the main, American banks had ample opportunity to employ funds at home. They looked outside the country for additional financial resources. US federal (and often state) laws blocked the moves of most commercial banking institutions abroad. If one studies the experiences of American banks and American manufacturers abroad, there is <span class="italic">no</span> synchronization whatsoever in the years before 1914.<sup>93</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">美国银行在海外的数量远少于外国银行在美国的数量。它们在海外扩张的形式较为有限（主要是联合伙伴关系和海外办事处），背后的动因也截然不同。总体而言，美国银行在国内有充足的资金运用机会，它们向海外寻求的是额外的资金来源。美国联邦（以及许多州的）法律阻碍了大多数商业银行机构向海外扩张。如果研究美国银行与美国制造商在海外的发展经历，会发现 1914 年之前，两者之间完全没有同步性。 <sup>93</sup> </span></span></span>

An inquiry into the history of banks as multinationals must, as I have shown, never assume that when a bank goes abroad it only participates in banking operations. We need to consider what other endeavours banks took part in and why. Sometimes foreign banks' US engagements had little to do with banking <span class="italic">per se.</span> For example, Baring's investment in Maine land (in 1796) was based on what the firm assumed would be a profitable investment. (The Barings would gain by developing the land and providing financing to settlers; thus, this was not entirely remote from 'banking'.) With its partner, Hope & Co., Barings was involved in this never-profitable direct investment for almost forty years (until 1835).<sup>94</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">正如我所展示的，在探究银行作为跨国企业的历史时，绝不能想当然地认为银行一旦踏出国门，就只参与银行业务。我们必须考虑银行还涉足了哪些其他领域，以及背后的原因。有时，外国银行在美国的业务与银行业本身几乎毫无关联。例如，巴林银行于 1796 年投资缅因州土地，其依据是该公司认为这将是一项有利可图的投资。（巴林银行通过开发土地并向定居者提供融资来获利，因此这并非完全与“银行业”无关。）巴林银行与其合作伙伴霍普公司共同参与了这项从未盈利的直接投资，持续了近四十年（直至 1835 年）。 <sup>94</sup> </span></span></span>

Foreign banks also made, obtained, or held on others behalf investments in American railroads (and industrials) that <span class="italic">might</span> be considered as 'direct investments'. Often, these were equity holdings where the shareholdings were substantial, but very rarely controlling. The interests were sufficient to exercise influence. Sometimes such stakes lasted many years.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">外国银行也曾以他人名义对美国的铁路（及工业企业）进行投资、收购或持有，这些行为或许可被视为"直接投资"。通常，这些投资属于股权持有，且持股比例相当可观，但极少达到控股程度。这些权益足以施加影响，有时这样的持股会持续多年。</span></span></span>

American banks before 1914 do not seem to have engaged in similar equity investments abroad. National banks were probably forbidden by law from doing so, although it is doubtful that they would have done so in any case. American private banks, such as J.P. Morgan & Co., were by 1914 deeply involved in foreign lending, but on reading Vincent Carosso's volume on the Morgans, I do not find evidence of such equity involvements abroad.<sup>95</sup> America was still a debtor nation in world accounts and this relates, in my view, to the absence of such interests abroad.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1914 年之前，美国银行似乎并未在海外进行类似的股权投资。国民银行（National banks）可能因法律限制而被禁止这样做，尽管即便没有法律禁令，它们也未必会参与此类投资。至于美国的私人银行，如 J.P. Morgan & Co.，到 1914 年时已深度参与对外贷款，但在阅读文森特·卡鲁索（Vincent Carosso）关于摩根家族的著作后，我并未找到其在海外进行股权投资的证据。 <sup>95</sup> 从全球账目来看，美国当时仍是一个债务国，在我看来，这与美国在海外缺乏此类权益投资的现象是相关联的。</span></span></span>

Here we come to two fundamental issues: (1) the firm's advantage as it goes abroad and (2) related investments. A bank's <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_237"></span>advantage in foreign lending would seem to lie in its access to relatively low cost capital and its ability to find appropriate locales to place that capital. Yet, how much did that require a foreign <span class="italic">direct investment</span> presence? Baring's investment in land in the United States seemed financially promising (that it was not is another matter). It could be called a related investment in two ways: Baring's goal in America was to gain a financial return, and in that its return on its land sales involved financing it was acting in its role as a financial intermediary.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这里便涉及两个根本性问题：（1）企业海外扩张中的优势所在；（2）相关投资行为。银行在海外贷款领域的优势，似乎在于其能获取较低成本的资金，并且具备将资本投向合适地点的能力。然而，这种优势在多大程度上需要依赖外商直接投资的存在？巴林银行在美国的土地投资，从财务角度看前景可观（至于最终是否如愿则是另一回事）。这可以从两方面被称为相关投资：一方面，巴林银行在美国的目标是获取财务回报；另一方面，其土地销售的收益涉及融资行为，本质上也是在履行金融中介的职能。</span></span></span>

More important, the investments in equity by foreign banks in the United States that were sufficient to exercise influence were typically associated with the banking business of the foreign bank. In that sense, we can call them business-related, or 'direct investments', investments by multinational enterprises. Yet, ordinarily the banks never intended to run (to manage) the business in which they held the equity stake, at least in the case of the pre-1914 US investments of foreign banks.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">更重要的是，外国银行在美国持有的足以施加影响的股权投资，通常与其银行业务密切相关。从这个意义上说，我们可以将其称为业务相关投资，或跨国企业的“直接投资”。然而，在 1914 年以前的外国银行对美投资中，这些银行通常并不打算经营或管理其持股的企业。</span></span></span>

Interests of this sort are not unfamiliar to the student of the history of multinational enterprise. Industrial companies often made (and make) 'related investments' abroad — investments in related activities. Other types of multinationals have made minority investments in related activities for information and other purposes. None of this is special to the banking sector.<sup>96</sup> Because foreign banks in the United States developed good information sources, capital rich foreign institutions could deploy their resources profitably; some equity investments were related solely to their financial expertise.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">这类利益对于研究跨国企业历史的学生而言并不陌生。工业企业过去（如今也）常常在海外进行"相关投资"——即对关联业务的投资。其他类型的跨国企业也会出于信息收集等目的，对关联业务进行少数股权投资。这些现象并非银行业独有。 <sup>96</sup> 由于外资银行在美国建立了优质的信息渠道，资金充裕的外国机构得以有效配置资源；部分股权投资仅与其金融专长相关。</span></span></span>

As I studied foreign banks' presence in the United States in the pre-1914 period, I was struck by the large number of banks with some 'presence', defined as a salaried representative, an owned agency, a branch, a partner in a private bank, a subsidiary or affiliate, or an equity interest in an American business that was large enough to exercise influence. At the same time, I was also impressed with how transient many such involvements were and if long-lived, how few individuals were typically involved.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在研究 1914 年前外国银行在美国的存在情况时，我惊讶于有大量银行以某种形式“存在”——无论是派驻领薪代表、设立自有代理机构、开设分行、成为私人银行合伙人、成立子公司或附属机构，还是持有足以施加影响的美国企业股权。同时，我也深刻感受到这类涉足往往转瞬即逝，即便长期存续，通常也只涉及极少数人。</span></span></span>

A number of the owned agencies persisted only a handful of years. Most of the twenty out-of-country banks with owned agencies in New York in 1914 probably had two or three men in a small office. Usually, banks extended into the United States to take advantage of opportunities and to gain information. They needed men on the spot. In the early twentieth century, New York City was an important financial centre. Many foreign banks wanted a presence there. While some equity investments did last decades, they seem to have been exceptional. Ordinarily, foreign banks did <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_238"></span>not wish to tie up their capital for long periods in equity investments, although at times they did so.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在这些自有代理机构中，不少只维持了短短几年。1914 年在纽约设有自有代理机构的 20 家境外银行，多数可能只是在小型办公室里安排了两三个人。通常而言，银行进驻美国是为了抓住机遇、获取信息，他们需要在当地有人手。20 世纪初，纽约市是个重要的金融中心，许多外国银行都希望在此立足。虽然有些股权投资确实持续了数十年，但这似乎只是特例。一般来说，外国银行不愿将资金长期锁定在股权投资上，尽管有时它们也会这么做。</span></span></span>

In San Francisco and Chicago and even more so, in New York (where branch banks were prohibited and agencies were forbidden to take deposits), by the early twentieth century foreign banks' main involvements in banking <span class="italic">per se</span> were in assisting foreign trade. British international banks, including the Hongkong and Shanghai Bank, the major Canadian banks, and the Japanese Yokohama Specie Bank were all involved in international transactions, using their New York base. All were experienced in handling foreign exchange. Likewise, some foreign banks catered to immigrant groups, arranging remittances home. In all these activities foreign banks had clear advantages. In international expertise, foreign banks were greatly superior to American banks and could serve where their American counterparts could not<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">在旧金山和芝加哥，尤其是在纽约（那里禁止设立分行，也不允许代办处吸收存款），到 20 世纪初，外国银行在银行业务方面的主要参与是协助对外贸易。英国的国际银行，包括汇丰银行、加拿大的主要银行以及日本的横滨正金银行，都利用其纽约基地从事国际交易。这些银行在处理外汇方面经验丰富。同样，一些外国银行服务于移民群体，安排汇款回家。在这些活动中，外国银行具有明显优势。在国际业务专长方面，外国银行远超美国银行，能够在美国银行无法涉足的领域提供服务。</span></span></span>

Recently, scholars have felt it appropriate to separate out the locations where banks expand into those in less developed, developed, and 'financial centre' countries.<sup>97</sup> When one views foreign banks in the United States, one finds all three host-country location rationales united in a single nation. Thus, Baring's participation in the Second Bank of the United States and the London and San Francisco Bank's role in California were activities of British banks in a less developed country; the Bank of Montreal in Chicago would seem to be in the same category. By contrast, the Deutsche Bank's representative (at least by the early twentieth century) was in a 'developed country'. And, surely the New York bank agencies and the interlocking partners in New York private banks were in a 'financial centre'.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">近年来，学者们认为有必要将银行扩张的地点区分为欠发达国家、发达国家和“金融中心”国家。 <sup>97</sup> 从外国银行在美国的布局来看，这三种东道国区位动机同时体现在同一个国家。例如，巴林银行参与美国第二银行（Second Bank of the United States）以及伦敦与旧金山银行（London and San Francisco Bank）在加利福尼亚的业务，都是英国银行在欠发达国家的活动；而蒙特利尔银行进入芝加哥，似乎也属于同一类型。相比之下，德意志银行的代表处（至少到 20 世纪初）则设在“发达国家”。而毫无疑问，纽约的银行代理机构以及纽约私人银行中的联锁伙伴关系，都处于“金融中心”之中。</span></span></span>

We all agree that the multinational enterprise is a firm that allocates international resources internally, that is, within the firm, and the multinational bank does the same thing with its financial resources. After this internal allocation, the output is 'sold' to the outsider, the customer. We usually take it for granted that the extension of the firm abroad facilitates its international business, and I see no difficulty in applying this to the banking firm. The structural difference <span class="italic">seems</span> to lie in how the business extension abroad carries forth that facilitation process. Here, there appears to be a major difference that lies in the characteristics of the bankers' products.

Let me explain by highlighting the point I wish to make with parallels with the familiar industrial enterprise, say a car company. Its international goal is to make profits by selling cars worldwide; it makes these cars where it must do so in order to meet this goal. Its foreign subsidiaries and affiliates are designed in an operating <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_239"></span>manner to make and to sell. Now, let s take the bank, which is seeking to provide banking services, international loans. What was true of most foreign banks in the United States in the pre-1914 period was that most typically the foreign outpost in the host country was not the <span class="italic">international</span> lender.<sup>98</sup> Usually, it was the parent that carried out the lending function, or arranged the financial intermediation. The foreign outpost (branch, owned agency, subsidiary, etc.) could have many purposes, but it seems that ordinarily, at least in the case of foreign banks in the United States and US banks abroad before 1914, the <span class="italic">fundamental</span> allocative role in relation to the key product was not undertaken <span class="italic">through</span> the outpost in the host country. The outpost provided information, offered an infrastructure of on-the-scene knowledge, might carry out negotiations, but was often by-passed in the essential transaction (the bond issue for the railroads, for example).<sup>99</sup><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">让我通过一个熟悉的工业企业——比如汽车公司——来类比说明我的观点。一家汽车公司的国际目标是向全球销售汽车以获取利润；为了实现这个目标，它会在必要的地方生产汽车。其海外子公司和附属机构的运营模式就是生产和销售。现在再看看银行，它寻求的是提供银行服务，即国际贷款。在 1914 年之前的美国，大多数外国银行的典型特征是，其在东道国的海外分支机构通常并不承担国际贷款职能。 <sup>98</sup> 通常，是母公司执行贷款功能，或安排金融中介业务。 海外分支机构（分行、自有代理机构、子公司等）可能承载多种职能，但通常——至少以 1914 年前美国境内的外资银行和美国银行的海外分支为例——其与核心产品相关的基本配置功能并非通过东道国的这一据点来实现。该据点负责提供信息、搭建基于现场知识的基础设施、开展谈判，但在关键交易（例如铁路债券发行）中往往被绕过。 <sup>99</sup> </span></span></span>

What the pre-1914 evidence suggests is that in America domestic banks came to be able to undertake domestic banking activities far more efficiently than international banks, and, accordingly, foreign banks took on a subordinate role in purely domestic banking. The key banks participating in the allocation of international financial resources <span class="italic">to</span> the United States could use their minimal representation to achieve their goals. The bulk of the substantial long-term financial flows that they directed into America in the late nineteenth, and early twentieth century did not go <span class="italic">through</span> the outposts of foreign banks in the United States.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">1914 年之前的证据表明，美国国内银行在开展本土银行业务方面远比国际银行高效，因此外国银行在纯粹的本土银行业中逐渐沦为次要角色。那些参与向美国配置国际金融资源的关键银行，仅需极少的驻点便能达成目标。19 世纪末至 20 世纪初，它们引导流入美国的大部分长期资本，并未经由外国银行在美国的分支机构实现。</span></span></span>

Often the owned agencies, branches, and subsidiaries of foreign banks in the United States were more important as support-systems for the financial flows (both the short-term trade financing as well as the long-term capital intermediation) than as operating units <span class="italic">per se.</span> The nature of the 'product' seems fundamental as a basis for explanation. As for the pre-1914 American banks abroad, the same point could be made.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">通常，外资银行在美国的所属代理机构、分行及子公司，其作为金融流动（包括短期贸易融资和长期资本中介）支持体系的重要性，往往远超其作为独立运营单元本身。而解释这一现象的根本，似乎在于“产品”的固有属性。同样，对于 1914 年前美国在海外的银行，这一点也适用。</span></span></span>

In short, banks are multinationals when they extend themselves internationally. The advantages of a bank are two-fold: access to lower cost capital than alternative sources of supply and ability to intermediate that capital (a skill gained from experience). Since before 1914 the lower cost capital was typically in the parent country of the bank, the extension to capital-recipient countries was only required for purposes of information (on where the capital should be placed) rather than for mobilization purposes. The banks that moved abroad (in a supply-oriented manner) to find capital and bring it to the home country were principally US banks (private ones and the trust companies) abroad. While there <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_240"></span>were some foreign banks in America that mobilized US capital and reallocated it domestically in America, these were the exceptions rather than the rule, because in the United States a viable domestic banking structure emerged that could serve this function far better than foreign banks. When a bank takes local deposits and makes local loans from them, it is acting as any industrial firm might (getting local inputs, supplies, deposits, and providing an output, the loan, the product — as indicated at the start of this chapter; its advantage lies in its skill in intermediation).<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">简而言之，银行在走向国际化时便成为了跨国公司。银行具有双重优势：一是能够以低于其他融资渠道的成本获取资本，二是具备中介这些资本的能力（这是从经验中获得的技能）。自 1914 年以前，低成本资本通常位于银行的母国，因此向资本接收国的拓展主要出于信息目的（了解资本应投向何处），而非资金筹集目的。那些以供给为导向、前往海外寻找资本并带回本国的银行，主要是美国的海外银行（包括私人银行和信托公司）。虽然也有一些外国银行在美国筹集美国资本并将其重新分配至美国国内，但这些属于例外情况而非普遍现象，因为美国本土已建立起远比外国银行更能胜任此功能的健全银行业结构。 当银行吸收当地存款并以此发放当地贷款时，其行为与任何工业企业无异（获取当地的投入、供给和存款，并产出贷款这一产品——正如本章开头所述；其优势在于其中介能力）。</span></span></span>

A theory of multinationals — which sees the bank as a firm, evolving through time — would predict that as time passes, banks abroad would either fail (and retreat or fall by the wayside), or alternatively become more adept at international activities. If and when the latter occurs, the earlier simplicity of the relationships would become more tangled, changing, and taking on new forms. We would expect multinational musterings of savings and international lending — i.e. the international spread of banking services. That is a function of <span class="italic">product.</span> We would also anticipate that a firm's foreign business <span class="italic">operations</span> would take on new dimensions and complexities in order to deal more effectively with its developing skills as a banking institution. We would expect this to be shaped by the rules and regulations of home and host governments. The pattern conforms to a broad, general theory of the growth of the firm — of the growth of multinational enterprise.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">一种关于跨国公司的理论——将银行视为一个随时间演进的企业——会预测，随着时间推移，海外银行要么失败（退缩或被淘汰），要么在国际业务上变得更加娴熟。若后者发生，早期简单的关系将变得更加错综复杂，不断变化，并呈现出新形态。我们可以期待跨国储蓄汇聚和国际贷款的出现——换言之，即银行服务的国际化扩展。这是产品功能的体现。同时，我们也有理由预期，一家企业的海外业务运营将呈现出新的维度和复杂性，以便更有效地应对其作为银行机构不断发展的技能。这一过程将受到母国与东道国规则和条例的塑造。这种模式符合企业成长——即跨国企业成长——的广义理论框架。</span></span></span>

In sum, foreign banks had a presence in the United States before 1914 in many roles. They had come to take advantage of opportunities and to gain information. Foreign banks made equity investments when there were profitable opportunities and when they saw this as leading to new prospects. Some US banks established themselves abroad to aid in bringing capital to America; a number of American private banks had interlocking partnerships with banks abroad — again, helpful principally in bringing capital to the United States. And, a very few American commercial banks were beginning to establish themselves in locales where Americans traded. Sometimes banks found informal associations over borders adequate. Sometimes, the route was correspondent banks. Banks as multinationals used 'hierarchy', the extension of the firm, when needed (and when permitted by law) to further their business endeavours. In operating in this manner, they were behaving exactly like industrial multinationals. The variations from the paradigms familiar to the student of industrial multinational enterprise rest almost entirely in the incongruities in measurement: the principal problems in discussing banks as multinationals lie in the characteristics of <span class="italic">financial</span> products and <span class="italic">financial</span> assets and <span id="Ch012.xhtml_page_241"></span>liabilities, which means that numerical representations of bank behaviour lack conformity with those of other multinationals. This difficulty notwithstanding, the exercise of applying the theory of multinationals as it is now taking shape to the institutional structure of banks provides substantial insights into which, how, and why banks establish operations outside their home country.<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-wrapper" lang="zh-CN">\
<span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper-theme-none immersive-translate-target-translation-block-wrapper" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1"><span class="notranslate immersive-translate-target-inner immersive-translate-target-translation-theme-none-inner" immersive-translate-translation-element-mark="1">总之，在 1914 年之前，外国银行在美国以多种角色存在。它们来到这里，是为了抓住机遇、获取信息。当有利可图且能开辟新前景时，外国银行就会进行股权投资。一些美国银行在海外设立机构，以帮助将资本引入美国；不少美国私人银行与海外银行建立了连锁合作关系——这同样主要有助于将资本引入美国。此外，极少数美国商业银行开始在美国商人进行贸易的地区设立据点。有时，银行认为通过非正式的跨境联盟就足够了；有时，则通过代理行这一途径。作为跨国企业的银行，在必要时（且法律允许的情况下）会采用“层级制”即企业扩张的方式，来推动其业务发展。在这样运作的过程中，它们的行为与工业跨国企业如出一辙。 与工业跨国企业研究范式不同的地方几乎全在于衡量标准上的不一致：将银行作为跨国企业进行讨论时，主要问题在于金融产品及金融资产负债的特性，这意味着银行行为的数字化表征与其他跨国企业缺乏一致性。尽管存在这一困难，将目前正在成型的跨国企业理论应用于银行制度结构的研究，仍能深入揭示银行在母国以外开展业务的方式、领域及动因。</span></span></span>
