# Chapter Twenty: Wizard

IT was now the twilight of the Diplomatic Age for the House of Morgan. Far from enjoying privileged access to the White House as it had in the twenties, it bore a special stigma. This new detachment from Washington was most apparent as the bank wrestled with the fate of the huge German loans from the 1920s—the celebrated 1924 Dawes loan and 1930 Young loan. Although these appeared under quasi-governmental auspices, Washington now dodged responsibility for their repayment and even showed a cavalier indifference. The New Dealers didn’t want to jeopardize trade and security interests to enforce debt repayment, and the Morgan partners felt cheated. After all, dating back to the first China consortium, they had cooperated with the government on the assumption that they would receive official support in negotiating with defaulting debtors. That was the *quid pro quo.* Now the House of Morgan, after carrying out the bidding of its political masters, felt abandoned as Germany threatened default with Hitler’s advent as chancellor in 1933.
如今已是摩根财团外交时代的黄昏。它不再像二十年代那样享有直通白宫的特权，反而背负着特殊的污名。这种与华盛顿的疏离，在银行处理 20 世纪 20 年代那批巨额德国贷款（即著名的 1924 年 Dawes 贷款和 1930 年 Young 贷款）的命运时表现得最为明显。尽管这些贷款打着准政府支持的旗号，华盛顿如今却对偿还责任避之不及，甚至摆出一副满不在乎的傲慢姿态。新政派不愿为了追讨债务而危及贸易和安全利益，摩根合伙人则有种被愚弄的感觉。毕竟，从第一个中国财团开始，他们一直与政府合作，前提是在与违约债务人谈判时能得到官方支持。这本是双方心照不宣的交换条件。 如今，摩根家族在执行了其政治主子的命令后，随着 1933 年希特勒上台出任总理、德国面临违约威胁，他们感到被彻底抛弃了。

To follow the saga of the Morgan involvement with the German reparations payments, it is helpful to retrace the odyssey of Dr. Hjalmar Schacht, who alternately posed as friend and foe of the House of Morgan. In 1930, he had resigned from the Reichsbank to protest the final terms of the Young Plan. After the Nazi election success in 1932, he sided with that party and prodded fellow bankers at the Deutsche and Dresdner banks to lend financial support. Among the German industrial class, Dr. Schacht conferred legitimacy on Hitler’s thugs. At Hermann Göring’s home in early 1933, he helped Hitler raise M 3 million from businessmen, a meeting climaxed by Gustav Krupp von Bohlen und Halbach’s pledge, on behalf of the rich guests, to give staunch support to the Nazis. Schacht even consented to a request from Hitler that he administer the new campaign fund.
欲了解摩根家族与德国赔款问题的纠葛，不妨追溯哈尔马·沙赫特博士的传奇经历——此人时而以摩根家族的盟友自居，时而摆出敌对姿态。1930 年，他因抗议杨格计划的最终条款而辞去德意志帝国银行行长职务。1932 年纳粹选举获胜后，他转向支持该政党，并鼓动德意志银行和德累斯顿银行的同行提供财政支援。在德国工业界眼中，沙赫特博士为希特勒的暴徒统治披上了合法外衣。1933 年初，他在赫尔曼·戈林的宅邸中协助希特勒从商界筹集了 300 万马克，这场聚会的高潮是古斯塔夫·克虏伯·冯·博伦-哈尔巴赫代表富商们承诺全力支持纳粹。沙赫特甚至同意了希特勒的请求，负责管理这笔新的竞选资金。

Hindenburg, yielding to Hitler’s wishes, restored Schacht to his post as Reichsbank president. After 1934, Schacht was also the minister of economics. As financial overlord of the Third Reich, Schacht would supervise public works, including construction of the Autobahn, his services winning him a reputation as the evil wizard of Nazi finance, the mountebank who could make financial magic for the Fuhrer. According to William Shirer, “No single person was as responsible as Schacht for Germany’s *economic* preparation for the war which Hitler provoked in 1939.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344317) In one panegyric, Hitler said that Schacht had accomplished more in a three-year period than had the entire Nazi party combined.
兴登堡顺从希特勒的意愿，重新任命沙赫特为帝国银行行长。1934 年后，沙赫特还兼任经济部长。作为第三帝国的金融霸主，沙赫特负责监督公共工程，包括高速公路的建设，他的“功绩”让他赢得了纳粹金融界邪恶巫师的名声——一个能为元首变出金融戏法的江湖术士。据威廉·夏伊勒所言：“对于希特勒 1939 年挑起的战争，德国在经济上的准备工作，没有人比沙赫特承担的责任更大。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344317) 在一篇颂词中，希特勒称赞沙赫特三年内取得的成就超过了整个纳粹党的总和。

As a Nuremberg war criminal, Schacht would portray himself as an early foe of Hitler’s, a beleaguered man trying to stop the mad progress of the war machine. He never joined the Nazi party and claimed that he had opposed the persecution of the Jews. But there was a great deal of humbug about Schacht, who liked to pretend his pure intentions were being subverted by unscrupulous German politicians. In his duplicitous style, he would tell Jewish bankers that Hitler was a temporary evil needed to restore order, and he would vocally oppose persecution of the Jews. (He feared that such persecution would tarnish Germany’s image in overseas banking circles.) Then he would boast privately to Hitler that he had blocked Jewish bank accounts and channeled the money into German rearmament. Because there was some truth to his self-defense, his story is more complex than that of his unreservedly diabolic associates. In the words of Nuremberg prosecutor Telford Taylor, “This self-righteous and stiff-necked individual was and remains the most enigmatic and controversial person of the pre-war years.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344495)
作为纽伦堡的战犯，沙赫特把自己塑造成希特勒的早期反对者，一个试图阻止战争机器疯狂推进的受困之人。他从未加入纳粹党，并声称自己曾反对迫害犹太人。但沙赫特的伪善成分极重——他喜欢假装自己纯洁的意图被无耻的德国政客们扭曲了。他两面三刀，一面告诉犹太银行家们希特勒只是恢复秩序所需的暂时性恶疾，并大声疾呼反对迫害犹太人（他担心这种迫害会损害德国在海外银行界的形象）；一面又私下向希特勒吹嘘，自己已冻结犹太人的银行账户，将资金转入德国重整军备的账户。由于他的自我辩护中存在几分真实，比起那些毫无保留的恶魔同伙，他的故事更为复杂。 用纽伦堡检察官特尔福德·泰勒的话来说，“这个自以为是、顽固不化的人，过去是，现在仍然是战前年代最神秘、最具争议的人物。”” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344495)

Dr. Schacht was an anomaly among high German officials. He remained a gentleman banker of the old school, giving Nazi finance a patina of dignity. Sporting rimless spectacles, parting his fine, white hair down the middle, smoking cigars, and wearing pinstripes and suspenders, he was indispensable to Hitler, not only for the ingenious way in which he harnessed German banking to a war economy, but for the respectability he won abroad. Having slain the 1923 hyperinflation, Schacht could fool international bankers into thinking they had a friend in Berlin who adhered to their own financial standards. And he had already won the lasting friendship of Montagu Norman. Where others saw a Nazi collaborator, Norman saw a courageous central banker fighting inflation and combating German rearmament for its incompatibility with sound finance. Schacht once told Hitler, “Only two things can bring about the downfall of the National-Socialist regime, war and inflation.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344659) This was the Hjalmar Schacht that Monty Norman preferred to see. The Morgan partners were more quickly disillusioned, believing that Schacht never wanted to pay reparations and had misled them into thinking he did.
沙赫特博士在德国高官中堪称异类。他是一位旧派绅士银行家，为纳粹财政披上了一层体面的外衣。戴着无框眼镜，将灰白秀发中分，叼着雪茄，身着细条纹西装和背带裤——他对希特勒而言不可或缺，不仅因为他以巧妙手段将德国银行业绑上战争经济，更因他在海外赢得的声望。由于曾平息 1923 年恶性通胀，沙赫特能让国际银行家误以为柏林有位恪守他们金融准则的朋友。他早已赢得了蒙塔古·诺曼的持久友谊。当旁人视其为纳粹同谋时，诺曼眼中却看到一位勇敢的央行行长：他抗击通胀，因军备扩张与稳健财政相悖而竭力抵制。沙赫特曾对希特勒直言：“能让国社党政权垮台的只有两件事——战争与通胀。”" [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344659) 这正是 Monty Norman 乐于见到的 Hjalmar Schacht。Morgan 的合伙人们更快地醒悟过来，认为 Schacht 从未打算支付赔款，并且误导了他们，让他们以为他有此意。

Unlike the many flunkies who surrounded Hitler, the arrogant Schacht exercised real power; finance was an area beyond the pale of the Fiihrer’s obsessions. At first, he gave Schacht *carte blanche* in running the Reichsbank. “He understood nothing whatever about economics,” Schacht later explained. “So long as I maintained the balance of trade and kept him supplied with foreign exchange he didn’t bother about how I managed it.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344818) Bullheaded and conceited, Schacht wouldn’t hesitate to yell at Hitler and took liberties that would have cost others their heads. Once the Fiihrer gave him a painting as a gift; Schacht returned it, saying it was a forgery. Nothing fazed him, and the cocksure banker had Hitler a bit bamboozled. Albert Speer noted of Hitler, “All his life he respected but distrusted professionals such as . . . Schacht.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345003)
与希特勒身边那些唯唯诺诺的走狗不同，傲慢的沙赫特手握实权；金融领域是元首无从染指的禁区。起初，希特勒让沙赫特全权掌管德意志帝国银行。“他对经济一窍不通，”沙赫特后来解释道，“只要我能维持贸易平衡，源源不断地为他提供外汇，他根本不会过问我是怎么做到的。”沙赫特刚愎自用，甚至敢对希特勒大吼大叫，做出那些换作别人早掉脑袋的越轨之举。有一次，元首送了他一幅画，沙赫特竟退回，说那是赝品。他无所畏惧，这个自命不凡的银行家把希特勒搞得有点晕头转向。阿尔伯特·施佩尔评价希特勒时说：“他一生都敬重但又提防诸如……沙赫特这样的专业人士。”

From a political standpoint, no instant alarm sounded at the House of Morgan when Hitler took office in 1933 and gained the power to rule by decree. Jack Morgan still nursed the old grudges against the Hun, but his reservations about Hitler were less moral than nationalistic. As he told his friend the Countess Buxton, “If I could feel more easy about your friends, the Boche, I should feel myself that we were all going to get along pretty well; but, except for his attitude toward the Jews, which I consider wholesome, the new Dictator of Germany seems to me very much like the old Kaiser.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345170)
从政治角度看，1933 年希特勒上台并获取独裁统治权时，摩根财团并未立即拉响警报。杰克·摩根仍对德国人怀有旧怨，但他对希特勒的保留态度更多是出于民族主义而非道德考量。正如他对朋友巴克斯顿伯爵夫人所说：“如果我对你们那些德国朋友能放心些，我会觉得我们大家都能相处得不错；但除了他对犹太人的态度——我认为那倒是有益的——这位德国的新独裁者在我看来与旧日的皇帝没什么两样。”

Nevertheless, a shift in Germany’s policy toward foreign debt appeared quickly. In May 1933, Hitler dispatched Schacht to Washington for eight days of talks. To divert him on his transatlantic crossing, Lamont sent biographies of Napoléon and Marie-Antoinette—volumes that perhaps contained their own tacit message about the corruption of absolute power. Meeting with Roosevelt and Secretary of State Cordell Hull, Schacht boisterously insisted that stories about the harassment of Jews were grossly exaggerated and said that foreign protest would only backfire. He also warned that Germany was running short of foreign exchange to service the \$2 billion in debt held by American investors. This White House meeting occurred during the Pecora hearings, and Schacht recorded this curious reaction from the president: “Roosevelt gave his thigh a resounding smack and exclaimed with a laugh: ’Serves the Wall Street bankers right!’ ”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345341) Afraid that Schacht might take this literally, Roosevelt’s advisers warned the president of the potential damage of his little jest. The next day, Hull rushed to tell Schacht that Roosevelt was actually shocked by the default threat. “It occurred to me that the President had not expressed any shock until twenty-four hours had elapsed,” observed Schacht.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345517) Roosevelt’s attitude may well have emboldened Schacht in his determination to repudiate German debt held in America.
尽管如此，德国对外债政策的转变还是很快出现了。1933 年 5 月，希特勒派遣沙赫特前往华盛顿进行为期八天的会谈。为了在他横渡大西洋途中分散其精力，拉蒙特送去了拿破仑和玛丽·安托瓦内特的传记——这些书卷或许本身也暗含着对绝对权力腐败的无声警示。沙赫特与罗斯福总统及国务卿科德尔·赫尔会面时，他高声坚称，关于犹太人遭受骚扰的报道被严重夸大，并指出外国的抗议只会适得其反。他还警告说，德国用于偿还美国投资者持有的 20 亿美元债务的外汇即将告罄。这次白宫会谈正值佩科拉听证会期间，沙赫特记录下了总统这一耐人寻味的反应：“罗斯福重重地拍了一下自己的大腿，大笑着说：‘华尔街的银行家们活该！’”' " [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345341) 担心沙赫特会当真，罗斯福的顾问们赶紧提醒总统，这个小小的玩笑可能引发不良后果。第二天，赫尔匆匆告诉沙赫特，罗斯福其实对违约威胁感到震惊。沙赫特评论道：“在我看来，总统过了整整二十四小时才表现出震惊，这有点奇怪。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345517) 罗斯福的态度很可能助长了沙赫特的决心，让他更加坚定要赖掉美国持有的德国债务。

That June, Schacht announced a moratorium on long-term overseas debt. The big German loans had been multinational—the Young loan, for instance, had appeared in nine markets and currencies—but the various creditor countries didn’t mount a united defense. Rather, they behaved like panicky creditors in a crowded bankruptcy court, each trying to get Germany to pay off his own bonds first. Articles appeared in the U.S. press reporting that European creditors wanted to strike separate deals with the Nazis. As a lever to pry open foreign markets to German goods, Schacht favored deals with countries running trade surpluses with Germany. The implicit message was buy more from us, and we might look more favorably upon your bonds. It was a policy of selective default, a clever divide-and-conquer strategy that broke down creditors’ unity and set them against each other. Schacht hoped that by stalling the creditors and driving down the price of the German bonds, he could buy them back at a price significantly lower than their face value—a tactic that apparently pleased Hitler.
那年六月，沙赫特宣布暂停偿还长期海外债务。德国的大规模贷款多为跨国性质——例如杨格贷款曾在九个市场和货币体系中发行——但各债权国并未采取一致行动。相反，它们表现得如同挤满破产法庭的恐慌债权人，争先恐后地想迫使德国优先偿还自己的债券。美国报章开始报道，欧洲债权国正试图与纳粹达成私下协议。为给德国商品开辟海外市场，沙赫特倾向于与那些对德保持贸易顺差的国家达成交易。其潜台词是：多买我们的产品，我们或许会对你们的债券更友好。这正是一种选择性违约策略，巧妙的分而治之手段瓦解了债权人的团结，使他们相互倾轧。 沙赫特希望通过对债权人拖延时间，压低德国债券价格，从而以远低于面值的价格回购这些债券——这一策略显然让希特勒颇为满意。

When Lamont learned that Schacht was contemplating selective repudiation in 1934, he reminded him that Morgans had supplied over half the Dawes funds and a third of the Young funds. With pardonable overstatement, he said the bank had always advocated moderation toward Germany. Most of all, Lamont appealed loftily to international law, promises made to investors that these loans superseded all others and enjoyed special political protection. Lamont was speaking reasonably to a man already hip-deep in diabolic machinations: “Of course, we expect to see the Reich obligations on \[the Young Loan\], as on the Dawes loan, carried out. Otherwise all international agreements might as well be torn up.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345642)
当拉蒙特得知沙赫特在 1934 年考虑选择性违约时，他提醒对方，摩根银行提供了道威斯计划超过一半的资金，以及杨格计划三分之一的资金。他略带夸张地表示，该银行一贯主张对德国采取温和态度。最关键的是，拉蒙特高高在上地援引国际法，声称向投资者作出的承诺使这些贷款凌驾于其他所有贷款之上，并享有特殊的政治保护。拉蒙特对早已深陷邪恶阴谋的沙赫特说得很在理："当然，我们期待德国在杨格贷款和道威斯贷款上的债务都能得到履行。否则，所有国际协议还不如统统撕毁。"

From Dr. Schacht’s reply, it was clear that the usual norms of business behavior no longer applied in Germany. Written in an extravagant, hysterical style, it was not the sort of letter people usually sent to the sedate precincts of 23 Wall. Schacht began by saying Germany’s problem was not default but a transfer difficulty resulting from a lack of foreign exchange. Then he veered off into bombast and mad whimsy:
从沙赫特博士的回信中可以看出，商业行为的常规准则在德国已不再适用。这封信写得夸张而歇斯底里，完全不是人们通常会寄往华尔街 23 号那种平静场所的信件风格。沙赫特在信的开头表示，德国的问题并非违约，而是因缺乏外汇造成的资金转移困难。随后，他话锋一转，开始大发雷霆，甚至带上疯狂的荒诞色彩：

> Whether you may threaten me with death or not will not alter the situation because here is the plain fact that I have no foreign valuta \[foreign exchange\], and whether you may call me immoral or stupid or whatever you like it is beyond my power to create dollars and pounds because you would not like falsified banknotes but good currency. . . .
> 无论你是否以死相威胁，都无法改变现状，因为事实明摆着——我手头根本没有外汇（外国货币）。不管你说我缺德也好，愚蠢也罢，随你怎么说，我都没本事凭空变出美元和英镑——毕竟你想要的可不是假钞，而是货真价实的硬通货……

> I would be willing to sell my brain and my body if any foreigner would pay for it and would place the proceeds into the hands of the Loan Trustees, but I am afraid that even the proceeds of such a sale would not be sufficient to cover the existing liabilities.[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345831)
> 我愿意把我的脑子和我这个人都卖了，只要外国人肯出钱，并且把钱交到贷款受托人手里。可我担心，就算这样卖光了，也不够还清现有的债。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2345831)

Schacht may have wanted to drive a wedge between England and America, perpetuating tensions over war debts and reparations. By threatening to strike a separate bargain by which British bondholders received some payments (albeit at lower interest rates) and Americans didn’t, he delivered a blow to Anglo-American amity. (Schacht argued that Germany’s trade surplus with England allowed it to make the interest payments. )The fight over unequal treatment, first with German, then with Austrian, debt, would prove the most divisive issue ever between J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Grenfell.
沙赫特或许是想在英美之间制造裂痕，延续两国在战争债务和赔款问题上的紧张关系。他威胁要单独与英国达成协议，让英国债券持有人拿到一些钱（尽管利率较低），而美国人却什么也得不到——这一招狠狠打击了英美之间的友好关系。（沙赫特辩称，德国对英国的贸易顺差足以支付这些利息。）围绕债务不平等待遇的争执，先是涉及德国，后又涉及奥地利，最终成了 J. P. 摩根公司和摩根建富之间最具分裂性的一道坎。

There had always been a latent contradiction at the heart of the Anglo-American Morgan empire. So long as U.S. and British interests coincided, it could be straddled. When those interests diverged, however, British and American partners were obliged to follow the wishes of their respective governments. They were too deep in politics to do otherwise. With J. P. Morgan and Company now a minority stockholder rather than a partner in Morgan Grenfell, there was also a new structural distance between the two firms.
英美摩根帝国的心脏地带始终潜藏着一股矛盾。只要美英利益一致，这种矛盾尚能调和。然而，当两国利益出现分歧时，英美合伙人便不得不各自遵从本国政府的意愿——他们涉足政治太深，别无选择。随着 J.P.摩根公司从摩根建富的合伙人降为少数股东，两家公司之间也悄然拉开了新的结构性距离。

For more than twenty years, Teddy Grenfell had been the Morgan ambassador to the British government. Now, somewhat unwillingly, he conveyed stiff protests from his New York partners to Whitehall. With rumours of a separate German deal with England swirling around Wall Street, Lamont drafted a letter to the British government, demanding that it take responsibility for American bondholders of German debt. Morgan Grenfell partners argued against its testy wording, but Lamont and Leffingwell refused to back down. Biting his tongue, Grenfell duly delivered the cable to Prime Minister Ramsay MacDonald. For a letter to a chief of state, it struck a faintly arrogant chord, a tone of mild menace:
二十多年来，泰迪·格伦费尔一直担任摩根大通派驻英国政府的代表。如今，他虽有些不情愿，却不得不将纽约合伙人的强硬抗议转达给白厅。当华尔街流传着德国可能与英国单独达成协议的传言时，拉蒙特起草了一封致英国政府的信函，要求其对德国债务的美国债券持有人负责。摩根建富的合伙人反对信中措辞尖锐，但拉蒙特和莱芬韦尔拒绝让步。格伦费尔忍气吞声，如期将电报呈交给首相拉姆齐·麦克唐纳。这封信函对一个国家元首而言，竟透出几分傲慢，带着一丝温和的威胁意味。

> Prior to the issue of the External Loan of 1924, we had not been associated with German finance, public or private, and we venture most respectfully to remind you that in your then capacity as Prime Minister you did us the honor of addressing our firm . . . by which you conveyed to us the requests of His Majesty’s Government that we should undertake the placing of the Dawes Loan in this country. . . . Meanwhile, however, for the reasons indicated above, we believe that His Majesty’s Government . . . will wish to use its good offices in every way possible for the protection of interests of all holders of these loans irrespective of nationality. . . .[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346026)
> 在 1924 年外债发行之前，我们与德国金融界（无论是官方还是民间）并无任何往来。我们谨此冒昧提醒您，当时您作为首相曾屈尊致函敝公司……向我们传达了陛下政府的请求，希望由我们在本国承销道威斯贷款。……然而，鉴于上述原因，我们相信陛下政府……将愿意尽一切可能运用其斡旋，以保护这些贷款所有持有人的利益，无论其国籍为何。…… [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346026)

Two weeks later, Lamont followed this up by meeting with Neville Chamberlain, then chancellor of the Exchequer. It was a vintage Lamont performance—tough resolve beneath suave civility. He said Morgans had become involved with Germany only because the Bank of England wanted to put Weimar Germany back on its feet and enable it to make reparations. Affable and noncommittal, Chamberlain asked what he would recommend. Lamont asked whether Chamberlain would scuttle a separate accord with Germany if no justice were done to American investors.
两周后，拉蒙特又与时任英国财政大臣的内维尔·张伯伦会面。这堪称拉蒙特的经典表现——温文尔雅的外表下隐藏着坚定的决心。他直言摩根公司之所以卷入德国事务，完全是应英格兰银行之请，旨在帮助魏玛德国恢复元气并履行赔款义务。张伯伦态度和蔼却不置可否，询问他有何建议。拉蒙特问道：倘若美国投资者的权益得不到公正对待，张伯伦是否愿意放弃与德国单独达成的协议？

> *Chamberlain:* I should not feel justified in going so far as to cancel my British arrangements if they fail to accede to my request re U.S.A.
> 张伯伦：如果德国方面拒绝我关于美国问题的请求，我还不至于因此取消与德国的协议安排。

> *Lamont:* No, I agree, nor should I expect you to do so. I believe that the representations you make will be so clear and strong as to go far toward getting similar treatment accorded to us.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346208)
> 拉蒙特：不，我同意，也不会指望您这样做。我相信您提出的交涉立场足够明确有力，足以促使他们给予我们类似的待遇。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346208)

The British never rode to the rescue. What made this so galling to Lamont was that he always believed Britain had initiated the side deal with Schacht. Morgan partners were thunderstruck by what they saw as British cynicism and the end of financial leadership they had always associated with the City. Schacht himself didn’t seem to dispute Lamont’s version of events. When George Harrison went to Germany with Roosevelt’s encouragement, Schacht professed dismay over discrimination against American bondholders. He said the British had blackmailed him into their deal and kept telling Harrison “God bless you!” for protesting to the foreign minister. Harrison came back to New York very disturbed. “He utterly disagrees with Monty about Hitler and Hitler-ism,” Leffingwell told Lamont about Harrison’s visit. “He didn’t see a smile on any face in Germany in two days.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346405)
英国人从未出手相救。让拉蒙特如此恼火的是，他一直认为英国率先与沙赫特达成了私下协议。摩根合伙人们被他们眼中的英国人的背信弃义震惊了，这标志着他们一直与伦敦金融城相关联的金融领导地位的终结。沙赫特本人似乎并未反驳拉蒙特的说法。当乔治·哈里森在罗斯福的鼓励下前往德国时，沙赫特对美国债券持有人受到歧视表示不满。他说英国人以要挟手段迫使他达成了协议，并不断对哈里森说“上帝保佑你！”，因为哈里森就此事向德国外交部长提出了抗议。哈里森十分不安地回到了纽约。莱芬韦尔在谈及哈里森的德国之行时告诉拉蒙特：“他完全不同意蒙蒂关于希特勒及其主义的看法。他在德国待了两天，没看到任何人脸上有一丝笑容。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346405)

To press his case, Lamont got Secretary of State Cordell Hull worked up about the discriminatory treatment of American bondholders. As he told Grenfell, “The American Government feels very strongly that the American investment community was had.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346606) Jack Morgan appealed to Monty Norman, whom he believed to be the one person outside Germany with any influence over Schacht.
为了推动他的主张，拉蒙特让国务卿科德尔·赫尔对美国债券持有人遭受的不公平对待感到愤慨。正如他对格伦费尔所说：“美国政府强烈认为，美国投资界被坑了。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346606) 杰克·摩根向蒙蒂·诺曼求助，他相信诺曼是唯一一个在德国之外能对沙赫特施加影响的人。

Norman wasn’t so upset by the German actions and was willing to make allowances for the Nazis. He continued to harbor more hostility toward France than toward Germany. In July 1934, he arrived in New York looking sickly and dispirited. He immediately telephoned Russell Leffingwell and took a cab down to 23 Wall. Leffingwell summarized their meeting for Lamont: “Monty says that Hitler and Schacht are the bulwarks of civilization in Germany and the only friends we have. They are fighting the war of our system of society against communism. If they fail, communism will follow in Germany, and anything may follow in Europe.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346794) This high regard for German culture had led Norman to back the 1924 Dawes loan in the first place. But the admiration now persisted under altered circumstances. As we shall see, most Morgan partners took a relatively benign view of German intentions, although there were skeptics from the start. The cynically acute Grenfell first penetrated Schacht’s disguises, already believing in 1934 that he was building up stocks of raw materials with which to prepare Germany for war.
诺曼对德国的行为并没有那么恼怒，甚至愿意为纳粹辩解。他对法国的敌意始终比对德国更深。1934 年 7 月，他抵达纽约时面色病态、精神萎靡。他立刻给拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔打了电话，然后乘出租车前往华尔街 23 号。莱芬韦尔向拉蒙特汇报了他们的会面情况：“蒙蒂说，希特勒和沙赫特是德国文明的支柱，也是我们仅有的朋友。他们正在为我们这种社会制度对抗共产主义而战。如果他们失败了，共产主义将席卷德国，欧洲也将随之陷入动荡。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2346794) 正是由于对德国文化的高度推崇，诺曼当初才支持了 1924 年的道威斯贷款。而在局势已然改变的情况下，这种钦佩之情依然延续。正如我们将看到的，尽管从一开始就有怀疑者，但摩根银行的大多数合伙人对德国的意图都持相对宽容的态度。 愤世嫉俗且眼光犀利的 Grenfell 最早看穿了 Schacht 的伪装，早在 1934 年就认定他是在囤积原材料，为德国备战做准备。

Meeting with Schacht at Baden-Baden in 1935, Lamont worked out a debt settlement that provided about 70 percent of the interest due on the two large German loans. After this meeting, Lamont and Schacht continued to perform a strange duet by mail. They pretended to be normal bankers in normal times, although Schacht’s behavior seemed increasingly erratic. In 1936, Morgan Grenfell partner Francis Rodd visited Schacht in Berlin and found him in a crazily jocular mood. He rather giddily instructed Rodd to “send his love” to Lamont and praised Morgans as the world’s premier bank. Schacht even invited Lamont to attend the Olympic Games, held that year in Berlin.
1935 年，Lamont 与 Schacht 在巴登巴登会面，敲定了一项债务解决方案，使得德国两大贷款的应付利息得以偿付约 70%。这次会面后，Lamont 和 Schacht 继续通过信件上演了一出奇特的二人转。他们假装自己还是寻常岁月里的普通银行家，尽管 Schacht 的行为愈发显得捉摸不定。1936 年，摩根建富合伙人 Francis Rodd 在柏林拜访 Schacht，发现他情绪亢奋，满口戏谑。他有些飘然地让 Rodd“代他向 Lamont 问好”，并盛赞摩根银行是全球首屈一指的金融机构。Schacht 甚至邀请 Lamont 前来观看当年在柏林举办的奥运会。

In a power struggle, Schacht finally lost out to his arch rival, Göring. His downfall began when he balked at buying foreign exchange for Nazi propaganda efforts abroad and tried to limit military imports of raw materials to what could be obtained by barter arrangements. In the last analysis, Schacht was too orthodox a banker, favoring slower growth and civilian production rather than a permanent war economy. In 1936, sitting on the terrace at Berchtesgaden, Albert Speer overheard Schacht arguing with Hitler in his office. As Speer recalled: “Some time around 1936 Schacht had come to the salon of the Berghof to report. . . . Hitler was shouting at his Finance Minister, evidently in extreme excitement. We heard Schacht replying firmly in a loud voice. The dialogue grew increasingly heated on both sides and then ceased abruptly. Furious, Hitler came out on the terrace and ranted on about this disobliging, limited minister who was holding up the rearmament program.[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347006)
在权力斗争中，沙赫特最终不敌宿敌戈林。他的失势始于拒绝为纳粹海外宣传购买外汇，并试图将军事原料进口限制在易货贸易所能获得的范围内。归根结底，沙赫特是一位过于正统的银行家，他倾向于放缓增长和民用生产，而非永久性的战争经济。1936 年，阿尔伯特·施佩尔坐在贝希特斯加登的露台上，无意中听到沙赫特在办公室里与希特勒争论。据施佩尔回忆：“大约在 1936 年，沙赫特来到贝格霍夫的客厅汇报……希特勒正冲着他的财政部长大喊大叫，显然极为激动。我们听到沙赫特用响亮的声音坚定回驳。双方的对话越来越激烈，然后戛然而止。希特勒怒气冲冲地走到露台上，开始痛骂这个碍事又狭隘的部长，说他拖累了重整军备计划。 [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347006) ”

Göring was put in charge of raw materials and foreign exchange. Although Schacht soon relinquished the economics ministry to Göring, he retained the Reichsbank presidency until January 1939.
戈林被委以掌管原材料和外汇的重任。尽管沙赫特很快将经济部移交给了戈林，但他仍保留帝国银行行长的职位，直至 1939 年 1 月。

Schacht will reappear in the Morgan saga at the time of the Austrian Anschluss. At this point, however, suffice it to say that the German debt quarrel left deep wounds on both sides of the Atlantic and dredged up the old issue of war debts. The British felt the United States should have canceled old war debts; Americans, even Morgan partners, believed Britain could have made a more determined effort to pay. Now that the Depression had finally retired lingering issues of debt and reparations, a new set of issues over the settlement of default debt would tear at Anglo-American financial harmony. The tension would last right up until the war.
在奥地利合并之际，沙赫特将再次出现在摩根的传奇故事中。不过，此刻只需指出，德国的债务纠纷在大西洋两岸都留下了深深的伤痕，并重新掀起了战争债务的老问题。英国人认为美国本应豁免旧的战争债务；而美国人——甚至包括摩根合伙人——则认为英国本可以拿出更坚定的决心来偿还。既然大萧条终于结束了挥之不去的债务与赔款问题，一系列关于违约债务清算的新争端又将撕裂英美之间的金融和谐。这种紧张局势将一直持续到战争爆发。

THE mid-1930s resounded with charges that to protect Allied loans the House of Morgan had led America into the First World War. Isolationists exploited this canard to try to ensure American neutrality in any future European war. They rallied the country against Wall Street, propounding a simplistic view of history that equated big business with bloodlust for war profits. A Wisconsin congressman, Thomas O’Malley, introduced a bill requiring the richest Americans to be drafted first—a foolproof way, he thought, to end wars. “It will be Privates Ford, Rockefeller, and Morgan in the next war,” he said.[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347173)
20 世纪 30 年代中期，充斥着摩根财团为保护协约国贷款而将美国拖入第一次世界大战的指控。孤立主义者利用这一谣言，试图确保美国在未来任何欧洲战争中保持中立。他们鼓动全国反对华尔街，提出一种简单的历史观，将大企业等同于对战争利润的嗜血渴望。威斯康星州议员托马斯·奥马利提出一项法案，要求最富有的美国人优先应征入伍——他认为这是结束战争的一个万无一失的办法。“下次战争里，上前线的将是福特、洛克菲勒和摩根这些大兵，”他说。 [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347173)

Portents of war were visible everywhere—for those who cared to see them. In March 1935, Hitler tore up the Versailles treaty and reintroduced obligatory military service. He boasted to Sir John Simon, the British foreign secretary, that the Luftwaffe had attained parity with the Royal Air Force. A year later, the Fiihrer occupied the Rhineland without any military rebuke from the Allies. Yet Sir Anthony Eden, secretary of state for foreign affairs, thought the best way to keep Germany from war was to strengthen Hitler’s economy. In 1936, Charles Lindbergh, at the invitation of Hermann Göring, toured Germany and marveled at its aircraft factories and technology, later urging Britain and France to retreat in self-defense behind a string of British dreadnoughts and the Maginot Line.
战争的征兆随处可见——但凡有人愿意去看。1935 年 3 月，希特勒撕毁《凡尔赛条约》，重新引入义务兵役制。他向英国外交大臣约翰·西蒙爵士夸耀，德国空军已能与英国皇家空军平起平坐。一年后，元首进占莱茵兰，而盟国未予任何军事惩戒。然而，外交大臣安东尼·艾登爵士却认为，阻止德国发动战争的最佳方式是强化希特勒的经济。1936 年，赫尔曼·戈林邀请查尔斯·林德伯格访德，林德伯格对其飞机制造厂和技术惊叹不已，随后力劝英法退守至英国无畏舰和马奇诺防线之后，以求自卫。

Isolationists might portray the Morgan partners as warmongers, but they weren’t alarmed by developments in Germany. In fact, they were strangely sanguine. After the Rhineland occupation, Lamont told Dr. Schacht, “The American public has to a considerable extent gotten the idea that Europe is about to plunge into the midst of another general war. . . . I may be too much of an optimist, but I do not share this view.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347323) Even while espousing cooperation with England, the bank steadfastly refused to interpret Axis rearmament as the prelude to a new European conflict. For all the rhetoric about mercenary bankers, the Morgan partners were more prone to appeasement than hawkishness.
孤立主义者或许会将摩根合伙人描绘成战争贩子，但他们并未对德国的事态发展感到警觉。事实上，他们出奇地乐观。莱茵兰被占领后，拉蒙特对沙赫特博士说：“美国公众在很大程度上已经认为欧洲即将再度陷入一场全面战争……我或许过于乐观了，但我并不认同这种看法。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347323) 即便在支持与英国合作的同时，这家银行也始终拒绝将轴心国的重新武装视为新一轮欧洲冲突的前奏。尽管满口都是关于唯利是图的银行家的说辞，但摩根合伙人更倾向于绥靖而非鹰派立场。

In early 1936, the ghost of World War I was revived by a Senate munitions investigation chaired by Senator Gerald P. Nye, a North Dakota Republican and adherent of Father Coughlin. With his pugnacious face and thrusting chin, Nye, like Pecora, formed a picturesque contrast to the stately Morgan partners he subpoenaed. He set out to prove that J. P. Morgan and other banks had dragooned America into war to safeguard loans and perpetuate a booming munitions business. Once again, the timid Jack Morgan was transmogrified into a venal, snarling monster. As *Time* magazine said, “Before the Committee for settlement was a scandalous question: should J. P. Morgan be hated as a war-monger second only to Kaiser Wilhelm?”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347526) For Jack, who so earnestly hated the Germans, it was a mortifying comparison.
1936 年初，第一次世界大战的幽灵被北达科他州共和党参议员、库格林神父的追随者杰拉尔德·P·奈主持的参议院军火调查重新唤醒。奈长着一张好斗的脸庞和咄咄逼人的下巴，与佩科拉一样，他与他传唤的庄重摩根合伙人形成了鲜明的对比。他试图证明 J·P·摩根及其他银行曾胁迫美国参战，以保护贷款并维持蓬勃发展的军火生意。于是，懦弱的杰克·摩根再次被扭曲成一个贪婪凶残的怪物。正如《时代》杂志所言：“摆在委员会面前的是一桩丑闻般的问题：J·P·摩根是否该被痛恨为仅次于德皇威廉的战争贩子？” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347526) 对于这位真心憎恨德国人的杰克来说，这种比较简直是一种羞辱。

Once again, a Morgan retinue departed for Washington, occupying an entire eighth-floor wing of the Shoreham Hotel and remaining barricaded behind a phalanx of plainclothes guards. (That same year, Polaroid founder Edwin H. Land visited Jack at 23 Wall and found him guarded by men with machine guns.) As if to show their sublime contempt for the hearings and disdain for the follies of petty men, the partners dressed in dinner jackets for their nightly meal. Newspapers showed George Whitney, legs folded, elegantly reading a newspaper in a smoking jacket, bedroom slippers, and bow tie before retiring for the night. Once again, Morgan staffers were sidetracked by a government inquiry. From a Brooklyn warehouse, they disinterred the bank’s wartime documents—twelve million of them, enough to fill up forty trucks.
又一次，摩根的随行队伍出发前往华盛顿，占据了肖勒姆酒店整个八楼的一翼，并在一队便衣警卫的护卫下固守其中。（同年，宝丽来创始人埃德温·H·兰德前往华尔街 23 号拜访杰克，发现他身边有持机枪的警卫保护。）仿佛是为了表达对听证会的极度蔑视和对小人物愚蠢行为的鄙夷，合伙人每晚都穿着晚宴夹克进餐。报纸上刊登了乔治·惠特尼的照片，他身着吸烟衫、卧室拖鞋、系着领结，优雅地翘着腿看报纸，然后才就寝。又一次，摩根员工因政府调查而分心。他们从布鲁克林的一个仓库里挖掘出了银行战时的文件——整整一千两百万份，足以装满四十辆卡车。

The Nye hearings were a flop. Unlike the Pecora hearings, where partners were defensive and stammered their way through sometimes incoherent answers, the Nye committee invited them to relive their proudest hour. “We were pro-ally by inheritance, by instinct, by opinion,” Lamont boasted, admitting that partners were glad to see America enter the war.[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347677) At that dawn of the Diplomatic Age, he contended, the bank had scrupulously heeded Washington’s wishes, waiting until Robert Lansing had replaced William Jennings Bryan and approved Allied credits.
奈伊听证会彻底失败了。与皮科拉听证会上合伙人支支吾吾、语无伦次的防御姿态不同，奈伊委员会邀请他们重温了最引以为傲的时刻。“我们天生、本能、观念上都是亲协约国的，”拉蒙特自豪地说道，并承认合伙人很高兴看到美国参战。 [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347677) 他辩称，在那个外交时代的黎明，银行严格遵守了华盛顿的意愿，一直等到罗伯特·兰辛接替威廉·詹宁斯·布赖恩并批准对协约国的贷款。

Far from seeming bellicose, Jack looked like a sleepy, avuncular old man. When Lamont said money was the root of all evil, Jack slyly interrupted: “The Bible doesn’t say ’money,’ ” he grinned. “It says, ’The *love* of money is the root of all evil.’ ”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347803) And while Lamont parried questions, Jack dozed or chatted with newsmen during breaks. Indeed, he had been appalled by the outbreak of war and had issued appeals to belligerents in 1914 to stop the combat. When it came to supporting the Allies, he was proudly secure in his position. “The fact that the Allies found us useful and valued our assistance in their task is the fact of which I am most proud in all my business life of more than 45 years.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347929) His personal defense was bluntly effective: “Do you suppose that because business was good I wanted my son to go to war? He did, though.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348166)
杰克看起来毫无好斗之意，反倒像个睡眼惺忪、慈祥和蔼的老人。当拉蒙特说金钱是万恶之源时，杰克狡黠地插了一句：“圣经里说的可不是‘金钱’，”他咧嘴笑道，“它说的是‘贪财是万恶之源’。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347803) 而在拉蒙特应付提问时，杰克要么打盹，要么在休息间隙和记者闲聊。事实上，他对战争的爆发感到震惊，早在 1914 年就曾呼吁交战各方停止战斗。至于支持协约国，他对自己立场充满骄傲与笃定。“协约国认为我们有价值，并在他们的使命中珍视我们的协助，这是我超过 45 年商业生涯中最引以为豪的事实。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2347929) 他的自我辩护直截了当且有力：“你以为因为生意好，我就愿意让我儿子去打仗吗？但他还是去了。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348166)

The hearings had as much to do with the Depression as with the war, and there was an uproar over Jack’s classic blooper: “If you destroy the leisure class, you destroy civilization.” Asked by reporters to define this class, Jack stumbled: “By the leisure class I mean the families who employ one servant, 25 million or 30 million families.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348308) Critics from the Housewives League of America gleefully pointed out to newspaper editors that there were fewer than thirty million families in the United States, and only two million of them had cooks or servants. As an amateur sociologist, Jack left something to be desired.
听证会既与大萧条有关，也与战争脱不开干系，而杰克那句经典口误更是引发了轩然大波：“如果摧毁了有闲阶级，就摧毁了文明。”记者追问他如何定义这个阶级时，杰克语无伦次地回答：“我所说的有闲阶级，是指雇佣一个仆人的家庭，大约有 2500 万到 3000 万个家庭。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348308) 美国家庭主妇联盟的批评者们幸灾乐祸地向报社编辑指出：全美国都不到 3000 万个家庭，而其中只有 200 万户拥有厨子或仆人。作为一名业余社会学家，杰克的表现实在差强人意。

Although Morgan partners considered the controversy a sideshow, it had an enduring influence, helping to muzzle their pro-Allied views and making them gun-shy of political controversy as World War II approached. In 1934, California senator Hiram W. Johnson, an isolationist, had sponsored the Johnson Act, which forbade loans to foreign governments that were in default on their dollar obligations. Neutrality Acts were also passed that blocked warring countries from purchasing arms or raising loans in the United States. This was part of an attempt to forestall any repetition of the Morgan Export Department or the Anglo-French loan in the event of war and to induce a steady American disengagement from Europe’s affairs.
尽管摩根合伙人们认为这场争议只是个小插曲，但它却产生了深远的影响——不仅压制了他们偏向协约国的观点，更使他们在二战临近时对政治争议变得畏首畏尾。1934 年，奉行孤立主义的加州参议员海勒姆·W·约翰逊发起了《约翰逊法案》，禁止向拖欠美元债务的外国政府提供贷款。随后又通过了《中立法案》，阻止交战国在美国购买军火或筹集贷款。这一切都是为了防患于未然，避免一战时摩根出口部或英法贷款的历史重演，同时促使美国逐步摆脱欧洲事务的纠缠。

Even as America debated its position in a hypothetical European war, Mussolini launched a full-scale invasion of Ethiopia in October 1935. II Duce had a megalomaniac vision of merging this territory with the colonies of Eritrea, Italian Somaliland, and Libya to forge an East African empire. Some five hundred thousand Ethiopians were sacrificed in a campaign infamous for its savage use of mustard gas. Like the Japanese in Manchuria, Mussolini’s army pretended to act in self-defense and had the effrontery to denounce Ethiopian aggression. Fifty League of Nations states condemned the violation of Ethiopian sovereignty and voted economic sanctions. Relying on voluntary compliance by American business, Secretary of State Cordell Hull asked for a “moral” embargo on sales of war materiel to Italy—shipments of oil, metal, and machinery. These exhortations were often ignored by American industry. Although Britain went along with the League’s economic sanctions, it stopped short of more extreme measures, such as cutting off all supplies of oil. Prime Minister Stanley Baldwin instructed his foreign secretary, Sir Samuel Hoare, “Keep us out of the war, Sam. We are not ready for it.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348479)
正当美国还在为某个假想的欧洲战争中的立场争论不休时，墨索里尼于 1935 年 10 月对埃塞俄比亚发动了全面入侵。这位领袖狂妄自大地幻想将这片领土与厄立特里亚、意属索马里兰和利比亚殖民地合并，打造一个东非帝国。在这场以残忍使用芥子气而臭名昭著的战役中，约五十万埃塞俄比亚人惨遭牺牲。如同日本人在满洲一样，墨索里尼的军队假借自卫之名，竟厚颜无耻地谴责埃塞俄比亚的侵略行为。国际联盟的五十个成员国谴责了这种侵犯埃塞俄比亚主权的行为，并投票通过经济制裁。美国国务卿科德尔·赫尔依靠美国企业的自愿配合，呼吁对意大利实施"道义"禁运，停止向其出售战争物资——包括石油、金属和机械。然而，这些呼吁常常被美国工业界置若罔闻。 尽管英国跟随国际联盟实施了经济制裁，但并未采取更极端的措施，例如切断所有石油供应。首相斯坦利·鲍德温曾指示外交大臣塞缪尔·霍尔爵士：“山姆，让我们置身战争之外。我们还没准备好。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348479)

By the mid-1980s, the House of Morgan’s enthusiasm for il Duce had cooled, as had Wall Street’s in general. One student of U.S. business support for Mussolini describes the post-1934 attitude as one of “a clamorous repudiation of the whole Fascist experiment.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348642) Not only did the Johnson Act block new Italian loans, but Mussolini’s behavior scared away American investors. There was apprehension about the dictator in high Anglo-American circles. Visiting Stanley Baldwin at 10 Downing Street in July 1935, Jack Morgan found him “terribly disturbed and apprehensive, as all are here, about Mussolini and Abyssinia.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348821) Lamont warned the bank’s Roman agent, Giovanni Fummi, that the rumored African campaign would jeopardize any renewal of credits to the Banca d’Italia.
到 20 世纪 80 年代中期，摩根大通对墨索里尼的热情已然消退，华尔街大体也是如此。一位研究美国商界对墨索里尼支持态度的学者，将 1934 年后的态度描述为“对法西斯实验的激烈否定”。 [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348642) 不仅《约翰逊法案》阻止了对意大利的新贷款，墨索里尼的行为也吓跑了美国投资者。英美高层圈内对这位独裁者普遍感到担忧。1935 年 7 月，杰克·摩根在唐宁街 10 号拜访斯坦利·鲍德温时，发现他“像这里所有人一样，对墨索里尼和阿比西尼亚感到极度不安和忧虑”。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2348821) 拉蒙特警告银行驻罗马代理人乔瓦尼·富米，传闻中的非洲战役将危及对意大利银行的任何信贷展期。

As before, the Morgan bank portrayed Giovanni Fummi as a non-Fascist who happened to have extraordinary access to Mussolini. They paid him handsomely for his services—some \$50,000 a year, or the same salary Parker Gilbert received as agent general for Germany. But Fummi wasn’t upset by the Ethiopian bloodshed and praised the country’s economic potential. He relayed a message to 23 Wall saying that Mussolini hoped U.S. capital could be funneled into the area. To dim such expectations, Lamont replied that Ethiopia would hurt Italy’s financial prospects abroad for a long time. In 1936, Mussolini sent a new Italian ambassador, Fulvio Suvich, to New York to drum up support for an Italian loan. When Italy sent troops to fight alongside Franco’s insurgents in the Spanish Civil War that summer, the effort was doomed (though Lamont supported Franco—and had heated quarrels with his son Corliss about the war). That fall, Hitler and Mussolini joined together in a Rome-Berlin Axis.
一如既往，摩根银行将乔瓦尼·富米描绘成一位非法西斯分子，只是碰巧与墨索里尼关系密切。他们为他的服务支付了丰厚的报酬——每年大约 5 万美元，与帕克·吉尔伯特担任德国代理总长时的薪水相当。但富米并未因埃塞俄比亚的血腥冲突感到不安，反而称赞该国的经济潜力。他向华尔街 23 号传递了一条消息，称墨索里尼希望美国资本能够注入该地区。为了打消这种期望，拉蒙特回应说，埃塞俄比亚事件将在很长一段时间内损害意大利在海外的金融前景。1936 年，墨索里尼派遣新任意大利大使富尔维奥·苏维奇前往纽约，为意大利贷款争取支持。同年夏天，当意大利派兵与西班牙内战中的佛朗哥叛军并肩作战时，这一努力注定失败（尽管拉蒙特支持佛朗哥——并因此与儿子科利斯就这场战争发生过激烈争吵）。那年秋天，希特勒与墨索里尼结成了罗马-柏林轴心。

After Ethiopia, relations between Mussolini and Lamont remained in abeyance for a time. In April 1937, Lamont visited Rome, ostensibly on a pleasure trip. There was a hidden agenda behind the holiday, however. Lamont had contacted British officials, who expressed hope that Mussolini could be weaned from Hitler. He also conferred with Cordell Hull about the latter’s program of lower global tariffs. In 1934, Congress had passed the Reciprocal Trade Agreements Act, an attempt to end the economic nationalism of the Depression by cutting tariffs to half their 1930 levels so long as other governments reciprocated with U.S. exports. Along with Germany, Italy was now on the road to autarky—that is, economic self-sufficiency—and Hull was alarmed by its retreat from the world economy. He thought that if the United States could conclude trade agreements with Axis powers, it might avert war. Lamont promised that in his talks with England, France, and Italy, he would promote Hull’s pet notion of lower tariffs. For Lamont, it was a momentary reversion to his heady Republican missions of the 1920s.
在埃塞俄比亚事件之后，墨索里尼与拉蒙特的关系一度陷入僵局。1937 年 4 月，拉蒙特访问罗马，表面上是去度假，但这趟行程背后另有玄机。拉蒙特此前已与英国官员接洽，对方表达了希望将墨索里尼从希特勒身边拉拢过来的意愿。他还与科德尔·赫尔就后者提出的全球降低关税计划进行了磋商。1934 年，美国国会通过了《互惠贸易协定法》，旨在将关税削减至 1930 年水平的一半，以此终结大萧条时期的经济民族主义，前提是其他国家也对美国出口商品给予对等优惠。如今，意大利与德国正走向自给自足——即经济上的闭关锁国——而赫尔对它们退出世界经济的趋势深感忧虑。他认为，倘若美国能与轴心国达成贸易协定，或许就能避免战争。 Lamont 承诺，在与英国、法国和意大利的会谈中，他会推动 Hull 钟爱的低关税主张。对他而言，这不过是短暂回归到 1920 年代那些令人振奋的共和党使命中。

Operating behind veils of mystery, Tom Lamont often had several reasons for his actions. He undoubtedly wanted to prevent a war and destroy the beggar-thy-neighbor spirit symbolized by the Hawley-Smoot Tariff Act of 1930. But he was also ready to forgive Mussolini his violent excesses and return to the *status quo ante.* Recently he had begun to meditate on ways of rehabilitating Mussolini in Anglo-Saxon eyes, telling a correspondent two weeks before his April trip to Italy, “I must say I prefer, of two foul evils, the fascists who make war, to the communists who seek to overthrow our governments. . . . The Duce should be presented to the public not as a warrior or in warlike attitudes, but in pastoral, agricultural, friendly, domestic and peaceful attitudes.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349011) This would have been news to the half a million Ethiopian dead.
汤姆·拉蒙特的行动常常笼罩在神秘面纱之下，且往往暗含多重动机。他无疑想阻止战争，摧毁 1930 年《霍利-斯穆特关税法》所代表的以邻为壑精神。但他也准备原谅墨索里尼的暴力过激行为，并恢复原状。最近他开始思考如何从盎格鲁-撒克逊人的视角重塑墨索里尼的形象——在四月意大利之行前两周，他对一名记者坦言："我不得不说，在两种邪恶中，我宁愿选择发动战争的法西斯，也不愿选择试图推翻我们政府的共产主义者……应当向公众展示的元首，不是战士或好战形象，而是田园牧歌、热爱农耕、友善亲切、居家平和的模样。" [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349011) 这番话对五十万埃塞俄比亚亡魂而言，简直是天方夜谭。

Soon after Lamont arrived in Rome, Vincenzo Azzolini, governor of the Banca d’Italia, learned of his visit. II Duce—ever eager to flaunt his popularity with world business leaders—then invited Lamont and Fummi in for a private audience. It occurred amid press reports that Mussolini would visit Hitler in late summer or early fall. It was Lamont’s first chat with the Italian leader since 1930. A transcript of the April 16, 1937, meeting has survived in Lamont’s papers. Mussolini began by spouting hysterical pleas for sympathy:
拉蒙特刚到罗马不久，意大利央行行长温琴佐·阿佐利尼便得知了他的来访。一向热衷于炫耀自己在全球商界领袖中人气的领袖墨索里尼，随即邀请拉蒙特和富米进行私人会面。此时正值媒体报道墨索里尼将在夏末或初秋访问希特勒。这是自 1930 年以来拉蒙特首次与这位意大利领导人交谈。拉蒙特的文件中保留了一份 1937 年 4 月 16 日会面的记录。墨索里尼一开口便歇斯底里地恳求同情：

> *Duce:* We have made a great conquest in Africa—that is finished now—I am for Peace, I am for World Peace—I am very strong for Peace. I need Peace—I need Peace—I am very strong for Peace. We are satisfied.
> 领袖：我们在非洲取得了伟大的征服——这件事已经结束了——我支持和平，我支持世界和平——我坚定地支持和平。我需要和平——我需要和平——我坚定地支持和平。我们很满足。

> *Lamont:* I believe you, Excellency, when you say that, I know it must be so, but the impression in America is very different. There you are pictured as a man who wants war rather than peace; that impression should be corrected. It is very important that in America your real attitude should be understood.[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349198)
> 拉蒙特：阁下，我相信您说的这些话，我知道事实一定如此，但在美国，人们的印象则大不相同。在那里，您被描绘成一个好战而非爱好和平的人；这种印象应当得到纠正。让美国人民了解您的真实态度非常重要。 [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349198)

As he had promised Hull, Lamont touted free-trade policies, and Mussolini hinted he would like a generous helping of American money in exchange: “America, Mr. Lamont, holds the key to economic cooperation. You see, Mr. Lamont, America has enormous quantities of gold, too much gold for the world’s good.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349418) Mussolini also expressed a wish for better relations with Britain, toward whom his policy had been wildly contradictory. He would talk about new agreements one day, then broadcast anti-British radio propaganda the next. In fact, the month before, he had secretly informed his army officers that he planned to destroy Britain. (Later it turned out he kept posted on British diplomacy by having his aids sift through the wastebaskets at the British embassy in Rome.) Mussolini’s plea for improved Anglo-Italian relations was marred by a comic slip:
正如他曾向 Hull 承诺的那样，Lamont 大力鼓吹自由贸易政策，而 Mussolini 则暗示他愿意用慷慨的美国资金来交换：“美国，Lamont 先生，掌握着经济合作的钥匙。你看，Lamont 先生，美国拥有巨量黄金，多到对世界都无益。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349418) Mussolini 还表达了改善与英国关系的愿望，尽管他对英政策此前一直自相矛盾。他今天还在谈论新的协议，第二天就通过广播进行反英宣传。事实上，就在一个月前，他还秘密告知手下军官，自己计划摧毁英国。（后来发现，他让助手翻查英国驻罗马大使馆的废纸篓来获取英国外交情报。）Mussolini 呼吁改善英意关系的提议，却被一个滑稽的口误所破坏：

(For a moment the Duce seemed to think that I alluded to the late Sir Austen Chamberlain whose friendship for Italy was well-known, but he remembered, and corrected himself with “Oh yes, I know that Mr. Neville Chamberlain is well disposed toward us.)”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349544)
> 拉蒙特：听您说正在尽一切努力增进与英格兰的友谊，我倍感欣慰。去年七月在伦敦，我听到了同样重要的表态。恰好那时我与当时的国王爱德华八世共进晚餐，他对我说：“既然制裁即将结束，我们必须回归与意大利的传统友谊基础。”即将接替斯坦利·鲍德温出任首相的财政大臣内维尔·张伯伦先生，也向我表达了同样的看法。（杜切似乎一时以为我指的是已故的奥斯汀·张伯伦爵士——他对意大利的友谊众所周知——但他随即记起，并纠正道：“哦，是的，我知道内维尔·张伯伦先生对我们很友好。”）

The interview had started uneasily, as if Lamont wished to register his moral disapproval for the record. Then he warmed appreciably and resorted to the old courtier’s mode, saying that Italians and Americans shared traits of industry, thrift, and imagination. He extolled Rome’s tuberculosis sanatorium and regretted that Americans missed such wonders. “We spend too much time gazing at what the Romans were doing in 100 A.D., and not enough time in looking at what the Romans are doing in 1937 A.D.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349677) In a vaguely surreal moment, he told Mussolini that tourism to post-Ethiopia Italy could be tremendously expanded. In a telling reminder of the old days, Lamont said he had jotted down fresh points on how Mussolini might handle public opinion. “Ah yes,” said Mussolini, “I am very grateful for your counsel . . . do not hesitate to advise me direct in regard to these matters. One of my mottoes is ’advice from everyone, collaboration by many, decision and responsibility by a few.’ ”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349810) Lamont and Fummi applauded this formulation.
访谈开始时气氛有些尴尬，仿佛拉蒙特想正式表明自己在道德上的不认同。随后他的态度明显缓和，转而采用一名老廷臣的口吻，说意大利人和美国人同样具备勤劳、节俭和想象力这些特质。他盛赞罗马的结核病疗养院，并为美国人无缘见识这样的奇迹感到遗憾。“我们花了太多时间盯着罗马人在公元 100 年做的那些事，却对罗马人在公元 1937 年正在做的事关注太少。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349677) 在一个略显超现实的时刻，他对墨索里尼说，埃塞俄比亚战争后，意大利的旅游业大有拓展空间。拉蒙特以一句意味深长的话提醒往日的交情：他又记下了一些关于墨索里尼应如何应对舆论的新要点。“啊，没错，”墨索里尼说，“我非常感激你的建议……在这些事情上，不妨直接给我意见。我的座右铭之一是‘人人献策，多方协作，少数人决策并担责’。”’ ” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349810) 拉蒙特和富米对这番表述表示赞同。

Toward the close, perhaps afraid that things had gotten too friendly, Lamont returned to American fears of Italian aggression. He said—with perhaps an icy smile—“The American people, Excellency, have unbounded admiration for the marvellous achievements you have accomplished for Italy since 1922, unbounded admiration for these great material developments, but as regards yourself, Excellency . . . they are really afraid of you.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349943) Smiling, Mussolini said the impression must be corrected. He urged Lamont to make a statement to the Italian press—which Lamont declined to do. Afterward, Lamont briefed William Phillips, the American ambassador in Rome, who seemed delighted by the talk.
接近尾声时，或许担心气氛过于友好，拉蒙特又转回到美国对意大利扩张的担忧。他说道——或许面带一丝冷笑——“阁下，美国人民对您自 1922 年以来为意大利创造的辉煌成就钦佩不已，对如此巨大的物质发展赞叹不已，但至于您本人，阁下……他们实在是对您感到畏惧。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2349943) 墨索里尼微笑着表示，这个印象必须纠正。他敦促拉蒙特向意大利媒体发表声明——但拉蒙特婉拒了。随后，拉蒙特向美国驻罗马大使威廉·菲利普斯通报了会谈情况，后者对这次谈话似乎颇为满意。

Clearly, part of Lamont’s Italian agenda was to curb Mussolini’s warlike tendencies and nudge him closer to the United States and Britain. His visit enjoyed official encouragement. Yet soon afterward, Lamont reverted to propaganda work that could hardly have been sanctioned by Washington and was reminiscent of his relationship with il Duce in the twenties. True to his pledge, Lamont forwarded a memo designed to help Mussolini “enlighten American and British opinion” about his peaceful intentions. It paralleled the memo drafted for funnosuke Inouye after Japan’s 1931 invasion of Manchuria, drawing specious analogies between Mussolini’s actions and American history, trying to convert the story of Ethiopian slaughter into a comforting tale of Italians conquering a wilderness. How should Mussolini quiet concern? By likening the Ethiopian campaign to America’s westward expansion: “In previous speeches in the last few months the Duce has spoken of the growth of new empire in Africa. His Government’s ends have been achieved. There now remains the task of agricultural and economic development in Ethiopia. There is a vast and fertile region as yet largely uninhabited and uncultivated. It would yield to the hard work and intelligent cultivation of Italian emigrants, just as a half century or more ago the vast resources of Western America were developed by American emigrants.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350076)
显然，拉蒙特的意大利之行部分目的在于遏制墨索里尼的好战倾向，并促使其向美英靠拢。他的访问得到了官方鼓励。然而不久后，拉蒙特又投入了宣传工作中，这些活动很难得到华盛顿的认可，反而让人想起他与墨索里尼在二十年代的关系。拉蒙特信守承诺，递交了一份备忘录，旨在帮助墨索里尼"让美英舆论了解"他的和平意图。这份备忘录与 1931 年日本入侵满洲后为井上准之助起草的那份如出一辙，将墨索里尼的行为与美国历史进行似是而非的类比，试图把埃塞俄比亚的屠杀故事包装成意大利人征服荒野的慰藉叙事。墨索里尼该如何平息外界担忧？只需将埃塞俄比亚战役比作美国的西进运动："在近几个月的演讲中，领袖曾多次谈及非洲新帝国的成长。" 他的政府目标已经实现。如今，埃塞俄比亚面临着农业和经济发展的重任。这里有一片广阔而肥沃的土地，至今大部分仍无人居住、未经开垦。只要经过意大利移民的辛勤耕耘和智慧开发，它就能焕发生机，正如半个多世纪前，美国移民开发了美国西部的丰富资源一样。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350076) ”

What exactly was Lamont’s purpose here? Was he trying to push Mussolini toward a new policy, or merely generating clever lines to hoodwink English and American opinion? Did he have any qualms about equating pioneers settling the American West with Italian troops hurling mustard gas? It is hard to imagine that the State Department or the British Foreign Office would have condoned this, notwithstanding *pro forma* lines about the need for world economic cooperation. After Libya, Corfu, Ethiopia, and Spain, these attempts at coaching Mussolini seem terribly misplaced. Lamont’s slick publicity lines were now as empty as the dictator’s own speeches:
拉蒙特此番究竟意欲何为？是要推动墨索里尼转向新政策，还是仅凭空编造些漂亮话以蒙蔽英美舆论？将西进拓荒的美国人等同于投放芥子气的意大利军队，他心里难道没有一丝不安？实在难以想象，国务院或英国外交部会纵容这种行为——即便表面上还要强调那套世界经济合作的老生常谈。经历利比亚、科孚岛、埃塞俄比亚与西班牙的种种行径后，这般试图对墨索里尼耳提面命的举动实在荒唐得令人错愕。拉蒙特那些油滑的宣传说辞，如今与这位独裁者自己的演讲一样空洞无物。

> It is true that each of the great nations of the world must have adequate defences. But preparation to that end is approaching completion on every side, including Italy’s own defences; so that now the primary end today and tomorrow must be the maintenance of world peace. . . . Italy was immeasurably the leader of the Renaissance, that great revival of the arts and learning that set the whole world upon a new path of enlightenment and progress. It is that same eager vitality that marks the Italian race today. . . . Italy welcomes the study of its past and is aware what attractions its galleries, its monuments, its cities, have for friends from abroad. They should study also the modern Italy, the material development of the past fifteen years, the public works, the reclamation projects, the industrial and agricultural policies, and perhaps above all the social and welfare system with its wonderful work as shown in hospitals, sanatoria, etc. Then indeed would Italy’s friends be impressed with what has been accomplished here.[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350245)
> 诚然，世界上的每一个伟大国家都必须拥有足够的防务。但为此所做的准备工作在各方都已接近完成，包括意大利自身的防务；因此，如今及未来的首要目标必须是维护世界和平……意大利曾是文艺复兴——这场伟大的艺术与学识复兴运动——无可比拟的引领者，它将整个世界引向了启蒙与进步的新道路。正是这种蓬勃的活力，至今仍标志着意大利民族……意大利欣然接受对其历史的研究，并深知它的画廊、古迹和城市对海外友人们有着何等魅力。 他们还应当研究现代意大利，了解过去十五年的物质发展、公共工程、土地改良项目、工农业政策，或许最重要的是，研究其社会与福利体系，以及医院、疗养院等机构中展现的出色成果。这样一来，意大利的朋友们必将对其所取得的成就印象深刻。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350245)

Lamont’s last fling with Mussolini again reveals his willingness to renounce principle for convenience. The most polished man on Wall Street, known for his thoughtful gifts and exquisite courtesies, was now a victim of his own disguises. Nothing mattered any longer but surfaces; his moral center had eroded and slipped away over the years. The bullying dictator and the eloquent banker no longer seemed as antithetical a pair as they had at the start of their friendship, when Lamont was fresh from his tutelage under Woodrow Wilson. The *New York Times* once said that Lamont “was a man who hated to see a friendship come to an end.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350418) His relationship with Mussolini perversely confirmed that insight.
拉蒙特与墨索里尼的最后一次风流韵事再次表明，他甘愿为便利而放弃原则。这位华尔街最温文尔雅的人，以贴心的礼物和精致的礼节著称，如今却成了自己伪装下的牺牲品。除了表面功夫，一切都已无关紧要；多年来的侵蚀，他的道德核心早已逐渐消逝。那个霸道的独裁者和那位雄辩的银行家，不再像他们友谊之初那样显得截然对立——那时拉蒙特刚走出伍德罗·威尔逊的教导。《纽约时报》曾评论说，拉蒙特“是一个不愿看到友谊结束的人”。 [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350418) 而他与墨索里尼的关系，却以反常的方式印证了这一洞察。

There was, however, yet another ongoing aspect of Morgan involvement in Italy—the Vatican account, which prospered even as government business stagnated. Lamont and other Morgan partners fed portfolio advice to Fummi, who in turn advised the Vatican on American securities holdings. The bank held papal securities in custody. (Fummi occasionally bungled the signals from Wall Street, advising the Vatican in 1938 to sell American stocks just as Lamont sent an updated report urging purchases. The Vatican then loaded up on American stocks, expecting that the Neutrality Act would be repealed, triggering a bull market on Wall Street.) Morgan judgments were always respectfully considered. As Fummi once told Lamont, “I hope you will approve of my above line of reasoning for there is no doubt that it has influenced the decision taken by the Amministrazione Speciale della Santa Sede a good deal.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350570)
然而，摩根在意大利的参与还有另一层面——梵蒂冈账户，即便政府业务日渐萧条，这个账户却蒸蒸日上。拉蒙特与其他摩根合伙人不断向富米提供投资组合建议，而富米则据此为梵蒂冈的美国证券持仓出谋划策。摩根银行还代为保管教皇的证券。（富米偶尔会误解华尔街的信号，例如 1938 年，就在拉蒙特发来最新报告力劝买入之际，他竟建议梵蒂冈抛售美国股票。梵蒂冈随后大举买入美国股票，预期《中立法案》将被废除，从而在华尔街掀起一波牛市。）摩根的观点向来备受尊重。正如富米曾对拉蒙特所言：“希望您能认同我上述的推理思路，因为它无疑在很大程度上影响了圣座特别管理处的决策。”

Lamont conducted his own personal diplomacy to restore traditional Anglo-Italian amity. Through his friend Lady Astor, he lobbied Lord Halifax, the foreign secretary, in April 1938 and argued the need to recognize the Ethiopian conquest as a *fait accompli.*[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350757) He apparently didn’t worry that tossing Ethiopia to Mussolini might embolden him. Meanwhile, Neville Chamberlain dispatched his sister-in-law Ivy—the widow of Sir Austen Chamberlain—to Rome to speak with her friend Mussolini in the hopes of drawing him away from Hitler. In early 1938, the British recognized Italy’s Ethiopian conquest in exchange for Italian troop withdrawals from the Spanish Civil War. Russell Leffingwell, who had denounced the Ethiopian invasion as a “predatory war,” told Lamont he thought Britain was “throwing Ethiopia to the wolves.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350958) The British diplomatic triumph was fleeting: in 1939, Mussolini would seize Albania and sign a “pact of steel” with the Nazis.
拉蒙特施展个人外交手腕，试图恢复英意之间的传统友好关系。1938 年 4 月，他通过好友阿斯特夫人向外交大臣哈利法克斯勋爵游说，主张将埃塞俄比亚被征服视为既成事实。 [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350757) 他似乎并不担心把埃塞俄比亚拱手让给墨索里尼会助长后者的气焰。与此同时，内维尔·张伯伦派他的弟媳——奥斯汀·张伯伦爵士的遗孀艾薇——前往罗马，与她的朋友墨索里尼会谈，试图将他从希特勒身边拉拢过来。1938 年初，英国承认意大利对埃塞俄比亚的征服，以此换取意大利从西班牙内战中撤军。曾谴责意大利入侵埃塞俄比亚是“掠夺性战争”的拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔对拉蒙特说，他认为英国是在“牺牲埃塞俄比亚”。 [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2350958) 英国的外交胜利昙花一现：1939 年，墨索里尼吞并阿尔巴尼亚，并与纳粹德国签订“钢铁条约”。
