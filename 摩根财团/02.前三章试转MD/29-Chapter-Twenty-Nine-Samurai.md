# Chapter Twenty-Nine: Samurai

LIKE Morgan Grenfell, Morgan Stanley entered the 1960s a model of civility, then turned itself inside out. In the early 1960s, it radiated a winner’s confidence. Nearly two dozen partners in Brooks Brothers suits and monogrammed shirts sat behind rolltop desks at 2 Wall Street. Decorated with English hunting prints, this platform area was a sanctum of mystical power. As one partner said, “It’s one of the few places where a single phone call can raise \$100 million.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412474) Morgan Stanley partners didn’t raid, compete, or crudely solicit business and had exclusive relations with their clients. If they hired somebody from another firm, they politely asked that firm’s permission.
如同摩根建富，摩根士丹利进入 1960 年代时也是一派温文尔雅，随后却彻底改头换面。1960 年代初，这家公司散发着胜利者的自信。在华尔街 2 号，近二十位合伙人身着 Brooks Brothers 西装、佩戴着字母组合衬衫，坐在卷盖式书桌前。这个办公区域装饰着英国狩猎版画，宛如一个充满神秘力量的圣地。正如一位合伙人所言：“这里是少数几个一通电话就能筹集一亿美元的地方之一。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412474) 摩根士丹利的合伙人们从不恶意收购、竞争或粗鲁地招揽业务，他们与客户保持着独家关系。若要从其他公司挖人，他们会礼貌地征得对方同意。

As befit a firm of illustrious heritage, tradition was venerated. The old House of Morgan had encouraged attendance at partners’ meetings by handing out gold coins. In a modern variant, Morgan Stanley gave out ten- or twenty-dollar bills to partners when they entered a meeting. They also got to divide the booty left by absentees. The only unanimous attendance occurred once, in a snowstorm, when everybody planned to make a killing.
作为一家历史悠久的公司，传统自然备受尊崇。老摩根家族曾通过发放金币来鼓励合伙人出席会议。到了现代，摩根士丹利则换了一种方式——合伙人开会时，每人能领到十或二十美元的钞票。此外，缺席者的那份也会被在场的人瓜分。唯一一次全员到齐是在一场暴风雪中，因为大家都打算趁机大赚一笔。

As students protested the Vietnam War in the 1960s, it was hard to lure graduates to Wall Street. When Frank A. Petito went to the Harvard Business School to try to recruit students, he ended up sitting alone in a classroom until a professor took pity on him and stopped by to chat. Although they had mostly gone to Princeton, Yale, or Harvard, Morgan Stanley partners came from diverse backgrounds. Like the old Morgan bank, Morgan Stanley was receptive to talented poor boys, even though it was unfairly stereotyped as a Social Register firm. Dick Fisher, a future president, was discouraged from applying for a job by a Harvard Business School professor who said Morgan Stanley required “blood, brains, and money” and that Fisher failed on two counts.
20 世纪 60 年代，当学生抗议越南战争时，很难吸引毕业生进入华尔街。弗兰克·A·佩蒂托前往哈佛商学院招募学生，结果独自坐在教室里，直到一位教授出于同情与他攀谈。尽管摩根士丹利的合伙人大多来自普林斯顿、耶鲁或哈佛，但他们的出身背景其实各不相同。和当年的老摩根银行一样，摩根士丹利对有才华的贫寒子弟持开放态度，尽管它被不公平地刻板描述为一家"社交名流公司"。未来的总裁迪克·费舍曾因哈佛商学院一位教授的话而打消求职念头——那位教授说摩根士丹利需要"血统、头脑和金钱"，而费舍在这两方面都不达标。

Nevertheless, the hauteur of the senior partners *could* be oppressive. Once at the firm, Fisher drove up to Canada with one of the partners to work on the Churchill Falls hydroelectric project. At the border, an immigration official, peering at Fisher in the backseat, asked the partner, “Who’s that in the back with you?” “I’m traveling by myself,” the partner answered. When the officer gestured toward the person sitting in the backseat, the partner said gruffly, “It’s no one. It’s a statistician.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412625)
尽管如此，高级合伙人的傲慢依然令人窒息。有一次在公司，费舍尔和一位合伙人开车去加拿大处理丘吉尔瀑布水电站项目。在边境，一名移民官打量着后座的费舍尔，问合伙人："你后面坐的是谁？""我是一个人来的。"合伙人答道。当官员示意后座还有人时，合伙人粗声粗气地说："没人，就是个统计员。" [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412625)

By the 1960s, Wall Street’s religious segregation was crumbling. Many Jewish firms had Protestant partners, especially in syndication, where they had to truckle to Morgan Stanley and First Boston. In 1963, Morgan Stanley hired its first Jew, Lewis W. Bernard, who had roomed at Princeton with Frank Petito’s son and frequently stayed at the Petito home. “When Bernard was interviewed, everybody was in favor of hiring him,” recalled a former partner. “But it was very hard for some older partners to overcome their ancient prejudice.” One Morgan Stanley partner even rushed over to Standard Oil of New Jersey to sound out an official: if Morgan Stanley ever sent over a Jewish employee, would the company be upset? “I think you ought to know, if you don’t,” the official bristled, “that our chief executive officer is Jewish.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412769) The partner slunk away. In 1973, at age thirty-one, Bernard became the youngest partner in Morgan Stanley history (except for the special case of Charlie Morgan), and he would develop into an important strategic thinker.
到了 20 世纪 60 年代，华尔街的宗教隔离逐渐瓦解。许多犹太裔公司开始接纳新教徒合伙人，尤其是在承销业务中，他们不得不对摩根士丹利和第一波士顿银行俯首帖耳。1963 年，摩根士丹利雇用了第一位犹太人刘易斯·W·伯纳德——他曾与弗兰克·佩蒂托的儿子在普林斯顿大学同住一室，还经常在佩蒂托家过夜。“面试伯纳德时，所有人都赞成录用他，”一位前合伙人回忆道，“但对一些年长的合伙人来说，克服根深蒂固的偏见实在艰难。”有位摩根士丹利的合伙人甚至匆忙赶到新泽西标准石油公司，试探一位高管：如果摩根士丹利派犹太员工前去接洽，公司是否会介意？“我想你应该知道，”那位高管不悦地回道，“我们的首席执行官就是犹太人。”那位合伙人只好灰溜溜地走了。 1973 年，三十一岁的伯纳德成为摩根士丹利史上最年轻的合伙人（除了查理·摩根这个特例），并逐渐成长为一位举足轻重的战略思想家。

Entering the 1960s, Morgan Stanley seemed secure, almost invulnerable, in its supremacy. The nonpareil of American investment banking, it counted as clients fifteen of the world’s twenty-five largest industrial companies, as well as Australia, Canada, Egypt, Venezuela, and Austria. These were comprehensive, exclusive relationships, a relic of the days when clients needed to wrap themselves in the aura of powerful banks. Morgan Stanley served its clients diligently and was always dreaming up new ways to finance AT&T or General Motors. As the Casino Age progressed, however, and capital was no longer so rare a resource, the traditional ties would erode.
进入 60 年代，摩根士丹利的霸主地位简直稳如泰山，几乎无人能撼动。作为美国投行界的翘楚，全球 25 家最大的工业公司中有 15 家是它的客户，连澳大利亚、加拿大、埃及、委内瑞拉和奥地利也都在其列。这些合作都是全面且排他的，算是旧时代客户需要傍着大银行光环的遗风。摩根士丹利对客户尽心尽力，整天琢磨着为 AT&T 或通用汽车搞出新的融资方案。然而，随着赌场时代步步逼近，资本不再是稀缺资源，这些传统纽带也开始松动瓦解。

Morgan Stanley would go to any length to serve a faithful client. During the 1950s, it managed securities issues for J. I. Case, a farm-equipment manufacturer. In 1961, when Case faced bankruptcy and bankers threatened to pull their loans, Samuel B. Payne of Morgan Stanley became temporary chairman of the company. For six months, Payne spent three or four days a week at Case headquarters in Racine, Wisconsin, turning the company around. Later, a rehabilitated Case was sold to Tenneco. Similarly, Morgan Stanley undertook a record financing for the billion-dollar Churchill Falls hydroelectric project in Labrador, Newfoundland, twice the size of Grand Coulee Dam. Some Morgan Stanley partners worked on it daily for eight straight years. In 1969, when the chairman of the Churchill Falls Corporation was killed in a plane crash, partner William D. Mulholland stepped in and ran the company.
摩根士丹利为了服务忠实客户，可谓不惜一切代价。20 世纪 50 年代，该公司为农用设备制造商 J. I. Case 管理证券发行。1961 年，Case 面临破产，银行家们纷纷威胁要撤贷，摩根士丹利的 Samuel B. Payne 临危受命担任公司临时董事长。连续六个月，Payne 每周有三到四天待在位于威斯康星州拉辛市的 Case 总部，最终让公司扭亏为盈。后来，重获新生的 Case 被出售给了 Tenneco。同样，摩根士丹利还为纽芬兰拉布拉多地区的丘吉尔瀑布水电项目（规模是大古力水坝的两倍，耗资十亿美元）完成了创纪录的融资。有些摩根士丹利的合伙人连续八年每天扑在这个项目上。1969 年，丘吉尔瀑布公司董事长因飞机失事遇难，合伙人 William D. Mulholland 挺身而出，接手了公司的运营。

The Morgan Stanley partner who first saw the cracks in this immaculate world of loyal bankers and loyal clients was Robert H. B. Baldwin, a protege of Perry Hall, who had retired in 1961. Baldwin was a man who sharply polarized opinion and was later seen as either the savior or the ruination of the firm. For better or worse, he would sweep away the cobwebs and drag Morgan Stanley into the modern era. At a place of proper gentlemen, Baldwin had a high energy level, fanatic drive, and a tremendous desire to manage people. Tall and athletic with cold piercing eyes and a brusque, humorless manner, he was the opposite of the archetypal Morgan man. His partners found him cold and awkward, a man who had trouble relaxing or making small talk, and he seemed misplaced in Wall Street’s most elegant firm. Yet that was perhaps an advantage, for he wasn’t shy about assuming power, as were the gentlemen.
率先察觉这个忠诚银行家与忠诚客户构成的完美世界出现裂缝的摩根士丹利合伙人，是佩里·霍尔的门徒罗伯特·H·B·鲍德温——霍尔本人已于 1961 年退休。鲍德温是个极易引发两极评价的人物，后来要么被视为公司的救星，要么被看作毁灭者。无论功过，他终将扫除陈腐积弊，把摩根士丹利拖进现代纪元。在这家绅士云集之地，鲍德温精力旺盛，干劲狂热，对掌控他人有着强烈渴望。他身材高大健硕，目光冷峻犀利，举止粗率寡言，缺乏幽默感，与典型的摩根人截然相反。合伙人们觉得他冷漠别扭，不擅放松或闲聊，仿佛错置在华尔街最优雅的投行里。但这或许正是他的优势——他不像那些绅士们那样怯于攫取权力。

Opinion divides on the question of Baldwin’s intelligence. He had an outstanding academic record: a triple threat in sports at Princeton—football, baseball, and basketball—he also received a *summa cum laude* degree in economics. Yet his intelligence wasn’t subtle or reflective but obsessive and suggestive of an implacable will. In his office, he had a needlepoint pillow on which was stitched, “The harder I work, the luckier I get.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412935) At a notably discreet firm, Baldwin would abruptly inform people that they were overweight or smoking too much. Entertaining clients, he would suddenly launch into extended monologues on his own achievements.
关于鲍德温的智力，意见不一。他学业成绩斐然：在普林斯顿大学是体育全能——足球、棒球和篮球样样精通，同时还以最优等成绩获得经济学学位。然而，他的才智并非细腻或内省，而是偏执且透露出不屈的意志。办公室里放着一个刺绣靠垫，上面绣着：“我越努力，就越幸运。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412935) 在一家以谨慎著称的公司里，鲍德温会直言不讳地告诉别人他们太胖了或烟抽太多。招待客户时，他会突然开始长篇大论地讲述自己的成就。

Bob Baldwin would develop into a classic hell-on-wheels boss who would dominate Morgan Stanley for years, making life memorably miserable for his subordinates. “He could be a real bastard in the supercilious way that he would exercise his power with subordinates,” said one ex-partner. “And he sometimes made a terrible fool of himself in the process of trying to be a big wheel.” Said another: “He lacks humility, he’s self-centered and insecure and quite humorless. You don’t want to have a drink with Bob Baldwin.” Yet he was also honest and forgiving. More to the point, he was extremely perceptive about the strategic direction of investment banking.
鲍勃·鲍德温后来成了摩根士丹利多年来的一个难缠的老板，让下属的日子苦不堪言。"他在下属面前行使权力时那种傲慢无礼的样子，简直是个混蛋，"一位前合伙人说，"他想装大人物的时候，经常闹得灰头土脸。"另一位则说："他缺乏谦逊，以自我为中心，内心缺乏安全感，又毫无幽默感。你绝对不想跟鲍勃·鲍德温一起喝酒。"但他也诚实宽容。更重要的是，他对投资银行的战略方向有着极其敏锐的洞察力。

Baldwin was relentless in pushing an idea. He once harangued legislators during testimony in Washington, then harangued his companion in a cab; when his companion got off, he harangued the driver. His hero was no dreamy poet or thinker, but Admiral Chester Nimitz. When his son was at Phillips Exeter Academy in the early 1970s, Baldwin, an unabashed defense hawk, addressed the student body on “the other side of the military-industrial society that was in such disrepute.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413086)
鲍德温不遗余力地推销他的观点。他曾在华盛顿作证时对立法者慷慨陈词，随后在出租车上又对同伴高谈阔论；同伴下车后，他转而对着司机滔滔不绝。他心目中的英雄不是那些天马行空的诗人或思想家，而是海军上将切斯特·尼米兹。20 世纪 70 年代初，当他儿子在菲利普斯埃克塞特学院就读时，这位毫不掩饰的国防鹰派人物，向全体学生发表了关于“备受诟病的军工复合体的另一面”的演讲。

Much like the Young Turks at Morgan Grenfell, Baldwin was maddened by what he called the white-shoe thing—the notion that Morgan Stanley partners were inept stuffed shirts who succeeded because of blood ties and social contacts. “My grandfather was a conductor on the Pennsylvania Railroad,” he pleaded. “My yacht is a 13-foot Sunfish.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413237) Or: “I get wild when they talk about that white-shoe thing. Why are we number one? Because we are nice people? Because we play golf? I stand on our record.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413387) As at Morgan Grenfell, this discomfort with a sedate past sparked revolt among younger partners and enabled Baldwin to push for sweeping changes in the firm’s *modus operandi.*
和摩根建富的少壮派一样，鲍德温被他所谓的“白鞋”做派激怒了——那种认为摩根士丹利的合伙人都是些无能僵化的老古板，全靠裙带关系和社会关系才能成功的说法。“我祖父是宾夕法尼亚铁路上的一名列车员，”他恳切地说，“我的游艇只是一艘 13 英尺的太阳鱼帆船。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413237) 或者：“他们一谈什么‘白鞋’做派我就上火。我们凭什么排名第一？就因为我们都是好人？就因为我们打高尔夫？我用业绩说话。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413387) 和摩根建富的情况一样，对这种沉闷传统的反感引发了年轻合伙人的反抗，也让鲍德温得以强力推动公司运营方式的全面变革。

Baldwin was also perceptive about Morgan Stanley’s defects in the mid-1960s. It was poorly managed and becoming too big for the old consensual style. There were no budgets, no planning, and no modern management—just endless collegial discussions. Bookkeeping was still done by clerks on high stools, who copied entries into leather-bound ledgers on tilt-top tables. All the while, the firm was growing and bursting in its small headquarters. In 1967, it vacated its cramped offices at 2 Wall Street. It was still unthinkable that Morgan Stanley would lack a Wall Street address. Harry Morgan was afraid that if the firm had a Broadway address, London friends might think him a theatrical producer. He was only reconciled to a new office building at 140 Broadway because it was the former Guaranty Trust address.
鲍德温对 1960 年代中期摩根士丹利的弊端也看得一清二楚。公司管理混乱，旧的共识决策模式已经跟不上日益膨胀的规模。没有预算，没有规划，更谈不上现代管理——有的只是无休止的同僚磋商。记账还靠坐在高脚凳上的文书，在斜桌上将账目誊抄到皮面分类账本里。与此同时，公司不断扩张，狭小的总部早已不堪重负。1967 年，它搬离了华尔街 2 号那间拥挤的办公室。当时仍难以想象摩根士丹利会没有华尔街的地址。哈里·摩根担心，如果公司地址设在百老汇，伦敦的朋友们会以为他改行做戏剧制作人了。直到新办公楼定在百老汇 140 号——原担保信托公司旧址——他才勉强接受。

During the 1960s, Baldwin made repeated efforts to run the firm but was rebuffed. Stymied by his slow advancement, he went down to Washington from 1965 to 1967 and served as under secretary of the navy. In these years, Baldwin was always promoting schemes to proselytize for the war on college campuses. Partners who found him pushy hoped he wouldn’t return. When he did, they again spurned his demand to take charge of daily operations, and he again decided to leave.
1960 年代，鲍德温多次试图掌管公司，却屡遭拒绝。因晋升缓慢而受挫，他于 1965 年至 1967 年间前往华盛顿，担任海军部副部长。那几年里，鲍德温总在推行各种计划，试图在大学校园里为战争摇旗呐喊。觉得他过于急切的合伙人们暗自希望他别再回来。当他真的回来后，他们再次驳回了他接管日常运营的要求，于是他再次决定离开。

He nearly escaped to the giant Hartford Insurance Company. Felix Rohatyn of Lazard Frères was playing matchmaker between ITT chairman Harold Geneen and the Hartford board. As Hartford’s investment banker, Baldwin frostily rejected an overture from Rohatyn. The Hartford board decided to bring in Baldwin as a one-man “white knight” to ward off ITT’s advances. In December 1968, Baldwin was set to become Hartford’s chief executive when Geneen, enraged by reports of his move, launched a hostile tender and forced Baldwin to retreat back to Morgan Stanley. Now it was a no-exit situation: Baldwin and Morgan Stanley had to come to terms. With his enormous frustration and bottled-up energy, Baldwin resumed his campaign to shake up the firm and in 1969 got it to call a rare planning session. Outvoted, he later conceded it was a “god-damned disaster.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413554) What saved him was partly a generational change. As older, Depression-era partners retired, they were slowly being replaced by a new group recruited in the early 1960s. In 1970, the firm’s twenty-eight partners admitted six young men, including Dick Fisher and Bob Greenhill. They were known as the “irreverent group of six,” and eventually they would tip the power balance toward Baldwin, giving him the votes to launch change. But at first, they wanted the nice, tight, rich Morgan Stanley of old.
他差点就成功跳槽到庞大的哈特福德保险公司。拉扎德公司的菲利克斯·罗哈廷正充当 ITT 董事长哈罗德·吉宁与哈特福德董事会之间的牵线人。作为哈特福德的投资银行家，鲍德温冷冰冰地拒绝了罗哈廷的示好。哈特福德董事会决定让鲍德温独自担任“白衣骑士”，以抵御 ITT 的进攻。1968 年 12 月，鲍德温即将出任哈特福德首席执行官，此时吉宁听闻他即将上任的消息后勃然大怒，发起敌意收购，迫使鲍德温撤回摩根士丹利。如今已是进退维谷：鲍德温和摩根士丹利必须达成和解。怀揣着巨大的挫败感和压抑已久的精力，鲍德温重新发起改革公司的运动，并于 1969 年促成了一轮罕见的规划会议。尽管投票失利，他后来承认那场会议简直是“一场该死的灾难”。 [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413554) 拯救他的，部分源于代际更替。 随着老一辈大萧条时期合伙人的退休，他们逐渐被 20 世纪 60 年代初招募的一批新人所取代。1970 年，公司的 28 位合伙人接纳了六位年轻人，包括迪克·费希尔和鲍勃·格林希尔。他们被称为"不羁六人组"，最终这些人将力量天平倾向了鲍德温，为他推动变革提供了所需的选票。但起初，他们渴望的是从前那个美好、紧密且富有的老摩根士丹利。

Contrary to the views of more myopic partners, Bob Baldwin saw Morgan Stanley as fighting for its life. He queasily noted the rise of Salomon Brothers and Goldman, Sachs, which were using their trading skills to chip away at the four dominant firms—Morgan Stanley, First Boston, Kuhn, Loeb, and Dillon, Read. At this point, Morgan Stanley still exhibited vintage snobbery about “traders” being socially inferior to “bankers”—a tradition dating back to Pierpont Morgan. This was also true at First Boston, which would call its underwriting wing the House of Lords and its trading room the House of Commons. Trading was still thought a coarse commodity business best left to Jewish firms such as Salomon and Goldman, Sachs. In the Salomon Brothers culture, in contrast, traders stigmatized corporate finance people as “light-bulb changers” or “order takers.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413705)
与那些目光短浅的合伙人不同，鲍勃·鲍德温认为摩根士丹利正在生死存亡之际挣扎。他忧心忡忡地注意到所罗门兄弟和高盛崛起，这些公司正利用其交易技巧蚕食四家主导公司——摩根士丹利、第一波士顿、库恩勒布和狄龙里德。此时，摩根士丹利仍保持着老派的势利态度，认为“交易员”社会地位低于“银行家”——这一传统可追溯至皮尔庞特·摩根。第一波士顿也是如此，他们称其承销部门为上议院，交易室为下议院。交易仍被视为粗鄙的商品生意，最好留给所罗门和高盛这样的犹太公司。相比之下，在所罗门兄弟的文化中，交易员却给企业融资人员贴上“换灯泡的”或“接单员”的标签。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413705)

John Gutfreund of Salomon Brothers was using the firm’s trading prowess to win new business and secure a better spot in syndicates. “Salomon and the rest were besieging chief financial officers with suggestions and ideas that we couldn’t match,” said Sheppard Poor, a former Morgan Stanley partner. “There was a proliferation of different financing vehicles.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413855) Morgan Stanley had always cultivated the big corporations, the users of capital. Salomon and Goldman, in contrast, had close relations with the suppliers of capital, the institutional investors who now accounted for three-quarters of the New York Stock Exchange trading. And power was now tilting toward these providers of capital.
所罗门兄弟公司的约翰·古特弗罗因德正利用该公司的交易专长来拓展新业务，并在承销团中争取更有利的位置。"所罗门和其他公司不断用我们无法企及的建议和想法围攻首席财务官，"摩根士丹利前合伙人谢泼德·普尔回忆道，"各种不同的融资工具层出不穷。" [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2413855) 摩根士丹利一直致力于培养大型企业——资本的使用者。相比之下，所罗门和高盛与资本提供者——即机构投资者——关系密切，这些投资者如今占据了纽约证券交易所交易量的四分之三。而权力天平正在向这些资本提供者倾斜。

In the volatile 1960s, with inflation induced by Vietnam spending, pension funds, insurance companies, and so on were managing their portfolios more actively. Instead of buying large blocks of bonds and holding them until maturity, they wanted to swap new blocks for old ones. This was impossible for an underwriter like Morgan Stanley, which had no trading operation. Large investors had other specialized needs. Trading big cumbersome blocks of stocks, they needed intermediaries to do “block positioning”—that is, temporarily taking the block off their hands and selling it whole or piecemeal. Salomon Brothers had the capital and trading strength to perform such intricate feats and used these services to expand its underwriting business. As an outsider, John Gutfreund had no scruples about raiding clients or doing other things anathema to the Wall Street club. He was the first to show that “the power to distribute securities would become the power to underwrite them.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414013)
在动荡的 1960 年代，受越南战争引发的通胀影响，养老基金、保险公司等机构开始更积极地管理投资组合。它们不再大举买入债券并持有至到期，而是希望用新债换旧债。这对于像摩根士丹利这样没有交易业务的承销商来说根本无法实现。大型投资者还有其他的特殊需求。在交易大宗笨重的股票时，他们需要中间商来“大宗股票持仓”——即暂时接手这些大宗股票，然后整体或分批卖出。所罗门兄弟公司拥有足够的资本和交易实力来执行这类复杂操作，并利用这些服务拓展其承销业务。作为局外人，约翰·古特弗罗因德对抢夺客户或做出其他华尔街俱乐部所不齿的行为毫无顾忌。他率先证明了“证券分销能力将成为承销能力”这一道理。

Gutfreund seemed to enjoy tweaking Morgan Stanley. When former defense secretary Robert McNamara became World Bank president in 1968—the bank’s first non-Wall Street president—he wanted to stimulate competition among underwriters and brought in Salomon Brothers along with Morgan Stanley and First Boston. In a tough bargaining session with the three firms, McNamara demanded a better price. Larry Parker of Morgan Stanley got up and said, “Well, I’ve got to go and consult my partner.” In a puckish mood—but also telegraphing that he would compete on price—Gutfreund rose to consult *his* partner. Then he suddenly sat back down. “Well,” he said slyly, “she’s always said yes to whatever I want to do.” This put pressure on Morgan Stanley and First Boston to follow his lead.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414164)
古特弗伦德似乎很享受捉弄摩根士丹利。当前国防部长罗伯特·麦克纳马拉于 1968 年出任世界银行行长——这是该行首位非华尔街出身的总裁——他希望刺激承销商之间的竞争，于是将所罗门兄弟与摩根士丹利、第一波士顿一同纳入麾下。在一场与三家公司的激烈谈判中，麦克纳马拉要求更优的价格。摩根士丹利的拉里·帕克起身说道：“嗯，我得去咨询一下我的合伙人。”古特弗伦德带着戏谑的心态——同时也传递出他愿意在价格上竞争的信号——起身去咨询他的合伙人。接着，他突然又坐了回去。“好吧，”他狡黠地说，“不管我想做什么，她总是会答应的。”这给摩根士丹利和第一波士顿施加了压力，迫使它们效仿他的做法。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414164)

To maintain syndicate leadership, Baldwin saw that Morgan Stanley would have to admit people long shunned as the rabble of the business—salesmen and traders. This move toward trading and distributing securities—and not simply allocating them to other firms to sell—would explode the small, posh Morgan Stanley, which then had about 250 people. The firm could no longer monitor securities markets from a lordly distance. At a 1971 planning session, Bob Baldwin finally got a decision to develop a sales and trading operation, and Morgan Stanley ceased to be the stately underwriting house created in 1935. It would develop relationships with institutional investors by trading and distributing stocks and bonds. “We made one decision,” said Dick Fisher later, “and that simple decision led to all the subsequent growth of our firm.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414337) The changes were implemented piecemeal, with Fisher put in charge of corporate bond trading. Later, Archie Cox, Jr., son of the Watergate special prosecutor, presided over equity trading.
为了维持投行圈的领导地位，鲍德温意识到，摩根士丹利必须接纳那些长期以来被视为行业“底层”——销售员和交易员的人。这一转向证券交易和分销业务（而不仅仅是分配给其他公司去卖）的决定，将让原本小而精的摩根士丹利（当时约 250 人）迅速膨胀。公司再也无法高高在上地旁观证券市场了。在 1971 年的一次战略规划会议上，鲍勃·鲍德温终于拍板决定发展销售和交易业务，摩根士丹利从此不再是 1935 年创立时那个高高在上的承销商。它开始通过股票和债券的交易与分销，与机构投资者建立关系。“我们只做了一个决定，”迪克·费舍尔后来说，“而这个简单的决定，催生了我们公司之后所有的增长。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414337) 变革是逐步推进的，费舍尔被任命负责公司债券交易，随后是小阿奇·考克斯。，Watergate 特别检察官的儿子，主持了股票交易。

Trading meant risk and required more than the \$7.5 million in capital that Morgan Stanley had in 1970. The younger partners had long feared that the firm’s precious capital might be depleted by the death of its aging partners. To preserve capital, Morgan Stanley switched in 1970 from a partnership to a partially incorporated firm. This also allowed dividends to flow in from Morgan et Compagnie International in Paris without punitive taxes.
交易意味着风险，所需资金远超 1970 年摩根士丹利所拥有的 750 万美元资本。年轻合伙人长久以来一直担心，公司宝贵的资本会因年长合伙人的离世而耗尽。为保全资本，1970 年摩根士丹利从合伙人制转变为部分公司化运营。这一转变也让来自巴黎摩根国际公司的分红得以顺利汇入，免遭高额税负。

As Morgan Stanley expanded into a full-service firm, the corporate culture changed. For almost forty years, Morgan Stanley men had traveled in a sedate, elite world, dealing only with chief executives. Traders inhabited a rougher world. “It was a different kind of culture,” said one trader. “Instead of the low-key, white-shoe style, this group was the raucous, tough-minded, four-letter-word kind of crowd that you get in a high-pressure situation.” Many older partners wrinkled their noses at the traders. “There were also young partners who looked down on us as if we had dirt under our fingernails and were an inferior breed,” recalled a former trader. Tastes changed: Morgan Stanley suddenly had a Sky Box at Madison Square Garden. A former partner noted that “Morgan Stanley partners didn’t go to basketball games up till then.”
随着摩根士丹利转型为全能型服务公司，其企业文化也随之改变。近四十年来，摩根士丹利的精英们一直穿梭在温文尔雅的上流圈层中，只与首席执行官们打交道。而交易员们则身处一个更为粗粝的世界。“这是种截然不同的文化，”一位交易员说道，“不再是低调含蓄的精英做派，这群人张扬暴躁、强硬固执，满嘴脏话，就是那种高压环境下催生出的类型。”许多资深合伙人对此嗤之以鼻。“还有些年轻合伙人瞧不起我们，仿佛我们指甲缝里藏着泥巴，是种低等生物，”一位前交易员回忆道。品味也变了：摩根士丹利突然在麦迪逊广场花园有了个空中包厢。一位前合伙人指出：“在此之前，摩根士丹利的合伙人是绝不会去看篮球比赛的。”

At first, it was difficult to recruit people: nobody believed the august Morgans was serious about trading. Traders lived in a world of split-second, high-pressure decisions. Where corporate finance people ambled in at 9:30 or 10:00, traders were at their desks by 8:00. When Fisher tried to ban employees from eating lunch at their desks, he couldn’t enforce the rule. In the superhuman effort to recreate the firm, some people worked all night. “I can remember someone asking me early one morning whether I was coming or going,” recalled Frederick H. Scholtz, who came in from General Foods to oversee planning.[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414518) His secretary would surreptitiously change her dress to hide that she had stayed all night.
起初很难招到人：没人相信高贵的摩根家族是认真要做交易的。交易员们活在分秒必争、压力重重的决策世界里。当公司金融部门的人慢悠悠地 9 点半或 10 点才到岗时，交易员们 8 点就已经坐在桌前了。当费希尔试图禁止员工在办公桌前吃午饭时，这项规定根本执行不下去。在重塑公司的超人般的努力中，有人整夜工作。“我记得有一天清晨，有人问我到底是刚来还是准备走，”从通用食品公司调来负责规划管理的弗雷德里克·H·肖尔茨回忆道。他的秘书会偷偷换掉裙子，以掩饰自己整夜未归的事实。

The trading operation was built from scratch. Morgan Stanley hadn’t had its own floor trader at the New York Stock Exchange. Partners had feared, rather vainly, that if the Morgan trader sold General Motors or AT&T, it would precipitate an avalanche of selling. Now traders were installed without setting off any market crashes.
交易业务是从零开始建立的。摩根士丹利此前在纽约证券交易所并没有自己的场内交易员。合伙人们曾颇为自负地担心，一旦摩根的交易员抛售通用汽车或 AT&T 的股票，就会引发抛售狂潮。如今，交易员已就位，但并未引发任何市场崩盘。

This helter-skelter expansion had healthy side effects—especially an end to the firm’s homogeneity. Before long, the Wasp citadel had “partners” with strange ethnic names. In 1975, Luis Mendez, a Cuban refugee with a distinctly Spanish accent, who had once wrapped packages in B. Altman’s basement, was made a partner from the bond-trading desk. His success reflected a new stress on performance. This trend was strikingly revealed when Robert McNamara visited the firm. At a luncheon with Morgan Stanley executives, McNamara ignored more senior figures to question Mendez, who sat in the rear and could clarify pricing mysteries about World Bank issues. The blunt, streetwise Mendez told McNamara that the World Bank was overpricing its issues and alienating customers. Afterward, McNamara said to his companion, Eugene Rotberg, “This firm isn’t as stuffy as I thought it was.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414683)
这种杂乱无章的扩张带来了积极的副作用——尤其是打破了公司内部的同质化局面。没过多久，这个 Wasp 堡垒里就出现了姓氏古怪的“合伙人”。1975 年，古巴难民路易斯·门德斯（Luis Mendez），带着浓重的西班牙口音，曾在 B. Altman 百货地下室打包包裹，如今却从债券交易部门晋升为合伙人。他的成功反映出公司对业绩的新重视。这一趋势在罗伯特·麦克纳马拉（Robert McNamara）访问公司时表现得尤为明显。在与摩根士丹利高管的午餐会上，麦克纳马拉没有理会那些更资深的人物，反而向坐在后排的门德斯提问，因为他能澄清世界银行债券定价中的一些谜团。直率而老练的门德斯告诉麦克纳马拉，世界银行的定价过高，正在疏远客户。事后，麦克纳马拉对同伴尤金·罗特伯格（Eugene Rotberg）说：“这家公司没我想象的那么古板。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414683)

Morgan Stanley tossed many traditions out the window. It no longer had the luxury of growing its own people and inculcating them with Morgan style. In recruiting traders, it had to favor those with youth, nerve, and stamina; almost half the managing directors enlisted after 1970 were under thirty-five. To attract traders, the firm introduced production-oriented compensation, which eroded collegiality and generated new rivalries and tensions. Baldwin gloried in this rough world of sharp elbows. Where Morgan men had disdained competition, he said approvingly, “The only way to make investment banking more competitive would be to gouge eyes out.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414842) As the firm increased tenfold in a decade, it suffered terrible growing pains and throbbed with new tensions.
摩根士丹利打破了许多传统惯例。它再也无法从容培养自家人才、向他们灌输摩根作风。在招聘交易员时，公司不得不青睐那些年轻、有胆识、精力充沛的人；1970 年后入职的董事总经理中，几乎有一半不到 35 岁。为了吸引交易员，公司引入了以业绩为导向的薪酬体系，这削弱了同僚情谊，催生了新的竞争和紧张关系。鲍德温在这个弱肉强食的残酷世界里如鱼得水。当摩根人对竞争嗤之以鼻时，他却赞许地说："要让投资银行业更具竞争性，唯一的办法就是把对方的眼珠子挖出来。"随着公司在十年间规模扩大十倍，它经历了痛苦的成长阵痛，新的紧张氛围弥漫其中。

To woo institutional investors, Morgan Stanley established a Stock Research Department. In April 1973, Frank Petito called in Barton Biggs, a Yale man and an ex-marine, who had managed a hedge fund in Greenwich, Connecticut. Biggs had been a go-go “gunslinger” portfolio manager of the late 1960s, but a respectable version of the breed. As *Institutional Investor* said, “Biggs was definitely the kind of gunslinger you could introduce to your daughter.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414993) Petito offered Biggs a partnership, which he accepted on the spot. It was one of the rare times in Morgan Stanley history that anybody was brought in as a partner.
为了吸引机构投资者，摩根士丹利成立了一个股票研究部。1973 年 4 月，弗兰克·佩蒂托邀请巴顿·比格斯加入，此人毕业于耶鲁大学，曾是海军陆战队员，在康涅狄格州格林威治管理过一只对冲基金。比格斯在 20 世纪 60 年代末是那种激进的“快枪手”式投资组合经理，不过算是这一流派中的体面人物。正如《机构投资者》杂志所说：“比格斯绝对是那种你可以放心介绍给女儿的‘快枪手’。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2414993) 佩蒂托给了比格斯一个合伙人席位，他当场接受了。这在摩根士丹利历史上实属罕见——直接吸纳外部人士成为合伙人。

The stock-research decision was controversial. Perry Hall and other senior people opposed it, claiming it might threaten their blue-chip franchise and open up conflicts of interest with clients. As partner Larry Parker said, when Morgan Stanley started equity research, “We took a very deep breath.” To establish his autonomy, Biggs fired salvos at IBM in a 1974 piece in *Barron ’s.* Scrapping an old Wall Street taboo, Morgan Stanley raided other firms, hiring analysts with such abandon that Baldwin was besieged with angry calls. “I’ve had a number of good friends call up and be damned mad at me,” he said. “I’ve promised we’d take no more.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415137)
涉足股票研究这一决策引发了争议。佩里·霍尔（Perry Hall）等资深人士表示反对，认为这可能损害公司蓝筹股业务的核心优势，并引发与客户之间的利益冲突。正如合伙人拉里·帕克（Larry Parker）所言，当摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）启动股票研究业务时，“我们着实深吸了一口气”。为确立自身独立性，比格斯（Biggs）在 1974 年《巴伦周刊》（Barron's）上发表文章，矛头直指 IBM。打破华尔街陈规，摩根士丹利开始从其他公司挖角，大肆招募分析师，以至于鲍德温（Baldwin）被愤怒的抗议电话团团包围。“好些老朋友打电话来，对我大发雷霆，”他说，“我已经保证不会再挖人了。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415137)

In rapid succession, major elements of the Gentleman Banker’s Code were breaking down. Like Morgan Grenfell, Morgan Stanley kept up its gentlemanly aura only so long as nobody poached on its territory; once threatened, it retaliated with a vengeance. Both on Wall Street and in the City, the graceful, leisurely world of securities syndicates was being replaced by the predatory world of mergers and the freewheeling, irreverent world of traders. Form was simply following function.
Gentleman Banker’s Code 的主要要素一个接一个地瓦解了。如同 Morgan Grenfell，Morgan Stanley 只有在无人侵犯其领地时才能保持绅士风度；一旦受到威胁，它就会猛烈报复。在华尔街和伦敦金融城，优雅闲适的证券承销团世界正被充满掠夺性的并购世界以及无拘无束、目空一切的交易员世界所取代。形式不过是追随功能而已。

During this transition, Harry Morgan continued to represent standards in a firm that was easily tempted to forget them. Although he became a limited partner in 1970 and technically lacked a vote, he still made his influence felt. Shortly after Morgan Stanley was incorporated, American Express tried to acquire it. Opinion was divided, some older partners favoring the acquisition, many younger ones dead set against it. Harry Morgan, in an emotional speech, said he wouldn’t sell his birthright for a mess of porridge. “You can do what you want, but the name Morgan is not for sale.” American Express was sent packing.
在这场转型中，哈里·摩根（Harry Morgan）依旧代表着一家容易忘记底线的公司坚守原则。尽管他在 1970 年成为有限合伙人，理论上不再拥有投票权，但他的影响力依然不减。摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）刚成立不久，美国运通（American Express）就试图收购它。公司内部意见分歧，一些年长的合伙人赞成收购，而许多年轻合伙人则坚决反对。哈里·摩根在一次慷慨激昂的演讲中表示，他绝不会为了一碗红豆汤而出卖自己的长子名分。“你们想怎么做都行，但‘摩根’这个名字不出售。”最终，美国运通被拒之门外。

Similarly in 1969, the firm entered into a new venture with Brooks, Harvey and Company to finance and invest in real estate. This real estate offshoot got off to a shaky start. At one point, the Teamsters came along with an irresistible proposal: they wanted Morgan Stanley to manage all their properties in the United States. Almost everyone favored the move except Harry Morgan, who sat silently through the discussion. “Young fellows,” he said at last, “as long as I’m alive, this firm is not going to do business with the Teamsters.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415263) The discussion ended.
同样在 1969 年，该公司与 Brooks, Harvey and Company 联手，拓展房地产投资与融资业务。这项房地产分支业务起步并不顺利。某次，卡车司机工会（Teamsters）抛出一个难以拒绝的提议：他们希望摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）管理其在美国的所有物业。几乎所有人都赞成这一举措，唯独哈里·摩根（Harry Morgan）在讨论中始终沉默。最后他开口道：“年轻人，只要我还在，这家公司就不会跟卡车司机工会做生意。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415263) 讨论就此结束。

By 1973, the burgeoning Morgan Stanley was surveying both uptown and downtown sites for new offices. Many partners refused to abandon Wall Street to follow corporate clients to midtown. Baldwin disagreed but couldn’t budge them. Then he lunched with Andre Meyer of Lazard Frères, which had moved up to Rockefeller Center. He told Meyer about the controversy. “Fine,” said Meyer, laughing, “I’ll be having lunch uptown with your clients while you’re having lunch downtown with your competitors.” Baldwin went back downtown all fired up. He prevailed upon his colleagues to look at three floors available in the Exxon Building on Sixth Avenue.
到 1973 年，蓬勃发展的摩根士丹利正在为上城区和下城区的选址考察新办公室。许多合伙人拒绝放弃华尔街，跟随公司客户迁往中城区。鲍德温并不认同，但无法说服他们。随后，他与已迁至洛克菲勒中心的拉扎德公司的安德烈·迈耶共进午餐。他向迈耶讲述了这场争议。“很好，”迈耶笑着说，“当你在下城区与你的竞争对手共进午餐时，我将在上城区与你的客户共进午餐。”鲍德温满腔热血地回到了下城区。他说服同事们去看了第六大道埃克森大厦的三个楼层。

Baldwin took them uptown via the Sixth Avenue subway, changing at West Fourth Street, rather than by way of the Seventh Avenue line, whose nearest stop, at Fiftieth Street and Seventh Avenue, was a popular spot for streetwalkers. The manipulation worked. In August 1973, Morgan Stanley, the very symbol of Wall Street, moved to the Exxon Building in midtown. The move underscored that paramount reality of the Casino Age—that once-proud and all but omnipotent bankers were now subservient to their corporate clients.
鲍德温带他们坐第六大道地铁前往上城区，在第四街换乘，而没有走第七大道线，因为那条线的最近一站（第五十街与第七大道交口）是街头妓女常出没的地方。这招果然奏效。1973 年 8 月，摩根士丹利——华尔街的象征——搬到了中城的埃克森大厦。这一举动凸显了赌场时代最残酷的现实：曾经趾高气扬、几乎无所不能的银行家们，如今只能唯企业客户马首是瞻。

BOB Baldwin’s palace coup at Morgan Stanley coincided with a top-secret attempt to recreate the old House of Morgan abroad. As finance became more global, the Morgan houses were colliding in obscure places and casting confusion. The problem was typified by fapan’s stubborn belief that Morgan Guaranty and Morgan Stanley—whatever their mischievous, self-serving denials—belonged to the same *zaibatsu.* The situation was endowed with comic-opera complexity on account of Morgan Grenfell’s global expansion in the late 1960s.
鲍勃·鲍德温在摩根士丹利发动的宫廷政变，恰逢海外秘密重建老摩根财团的绝密计划。随着金融全球化进程加速，摩根系机构在隐秘角落不断碰撞，引发市场困惑。问题症结在于日本方面固执地认为——无论摩根保证信托与摩根士丹利如何狡黠地矢口否认——它们根本同属一个财阀体系。而 20 世纪 60 年代末摩根建富的全球扩张，更让这出戏码平添了喜歌剧般的错综复杂。

In the dozy 1950s, Morgan Grenf ell was boxed into Britain by exchange controls and the fear of clashes abroad with Morgan Guaranty. In 1967, to jolt Morgan Grenfell from lethargy, Lord Harcourt had drafted his friend Sir John Stevens as the new chief executive. Like Harcourt, Stevens had been British economics minister in Washington and U.K. executive director of the IMF-World Bank. During his six-year tenure, Stevens would exert a pervasive influence on Morgan Grenfell. At a firm weak in broad strategic thinkers, his vision of a global future was exceptional. He also brought an air of adventure to the stodgy old bank. During World War II, he had parachuted into Italy as a member of the Special Operations Executive, Britain’s secret dirty-tricks intelligence unit, to finance the underground Piedmont Liberation Committee. He infiltrated occupied territory in Greece and France and in 1945 accepted the surrender of German divisions in northern Italy, receiving the freedom of the city of Turin for his work with Italian partisans. Fluent in six or seven languages, he became a roving emissary for the Bank of England and, in 1957, executive director. The next year, chattering in Russian and dazzling Muscovites, he made the initial contact between the Bank of England and the Soviet State Bank, later giving Morgan Grenfell a financial edge with the Soviets. By the early 1960s, Stevens was on the short list to be governor of the Bank of England. When he lost out, he accepted Harcourt’s offer to come to Morgan Grenfell.
在昏昏欲睡的 20 世纪 50 年代，摩根建富受制于外汇管制，又忌惮与摩根保证在海外的冲突，被困在英国境内动弹不得。1967 年，为了给摩根建富注入活力，哈考特勋爵将自己的朋友约翰·史蒂文斯爵士请来担任新任首席执行官。与哈考特一样，史蒂文斯曾任英国驻华盛顿经济部长，以及国际货币基金组织与世界银行的英国执行董事。在六年任期内，史蒂文斯对摩根建富产生了深远影响。这家公司历来缺乏宏观战略思维人才，而他放眼全球未来的远见卓识堪称独树一帜。他还给这家古板的老牌银行带来了一股冒险气息——二战期间，他曾作为英国秘密情报机构特别行动处的成员伞降意大利，为地下皮埃蒙特解放委员会提供资金支持。 他潜入了希腊和法国被占区，并于 1945 年接受了意大利北部几个德国师的投降，因与意大利游击队的合作而获得都灵荣誉市民称号。他精通六七种语言，后来成为英格兰银行的巡回特使，并于 1957 年出任执行董事。次年，他用俄语谈笑风生，令莫斯科人倾倒，促成了英格兰银行与苏联国家银行的首次接触，随后为摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）在与苏联的交易中赢得了金融先机。到 20 世纪 60 年代初，史蒂文斯已成为英格兰银行行长热门候选人之一。 当他落选后，他接受了哈考特的邀请，加入了摩根建富。

As Stevens toured the world opening up new Morgan Grenfell offices with Foreign Office recruits, the firm happily exploited 23 Wall’s fame. Morgan Grenfell’s overseas clients tended to be American companies who banked with Morgan Guaranty. As David Bendall, one of Stevens’s Foreign Office recruits, said, “As Morgan Grenfell went abroad, nobody knew the firm. But they did know J. P. Morgan and Company, and we used the name.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415422) “They were known worldwide as the number-one American bank,” conceded Stephen Catto, “and so the Morgan Guaranty stake was very helpful in our establishing business abroad.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415580) Of course, as Morgan Stanley and Morgan Grenfell traded on the name, Morgan Guaranty’s difficulties increased geometrically.
当史蒂文斯带着外交部的新人周游世界，为摩根建富开设新办事处时，该公司愉快地利用了华尔街 23 号的名气。摩根建富的海外客户往往是在摩根担保公司开户的美国公司。正如史蒂文斯招募的一位外交部新人戴维·本德尔所说：“摩根建富走出国门时，没人知道这家公司。但他们确实知道 J.P.摩根公司，于是我们就借用了这个名字。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415422) “他们在全球被视为头号美国银行，”斯蒂芬·卡托承认，“因此，摩根担保的持股对我们开拓海外业务帮助极大。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415580) 当然，随着摩根士丹利和摩根建富都借这个名字做文章，摩根担保的困境便呈几何级数增加。

Morgan Grenfell was growing restless with Morgan Guaranty, which prevented it from entering the vital, lucrative U.S. market. Under Glass-Steagall, Morgan Grenfell couldn’t function as an investment bank in New York. “And I’m sure that from the viewpoint of the older, more senior Morgan Grenfell people, we were American upstarts anyway—second-class citizens and clearing-bank types,” said Rod Lindsay.
摩根建富对摩根保证信托日益感到不满，因为后者阻止其进入重要且利润丰厚的美国市场。根据《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》，摩根建富无法在纽约作为投资银行运营。“而且我确信，从那些更年长、资历更深的摩根建富员工的角度来看，我们不过是一群美国暴发户——二等公民，顶多算清算银行那类角色，”罗德·林赛说道。

Morgan Guaranty, meanwhile, was going like gangbusters in the Euromarkets and by the early 1970s had nearly six hundred people based in London—nearly the size of the entire bank in the 1950s. Companies that used Morgan Guaranty in home markets, such as Michelin and Siemens, flocked to it in London. When Danny Davison became Morgan Guaranty’s London manager in the late 1960s, he found Morgan Grenfell such a dry, sleepy place that he preferred doing business with Lazards or even Warburgs—a sore point at Morgan Grenfell. As liaison with 23 Great Winchester Street, Davison imagined he was entitled to share trade secrets. Early in his tour, he attended a partners’ meeting but found that confidential sections were neatly snipped from his briefing book. Insulted, he swore never to return. It was the same old confusion bred by the bizarre situation in which Morgan Guaranty held a giant stake in Morgan Grenfell, but was supposed to remain passive. If this appeased the Bank of England and the Federal Reserve, it went counter to logic and human nature.
与此同时，摩根担保在欧洲市场势头迅猛，到 1970 年代初已在伦敦拥有近六百名员工——人数几乎相当于 1950 年代整个银行的规模。那些在国内市场与摩根担保合作的公司，如米其林和西门子，纷纷涌向其在伦敦的业务。1960 年代末，丹尼·戴维森出任摩根担保伦敦总经理时，发现摩根建富是个死气沉沉、昏昏欲睡的地方，他宁愿与拉扎德甚至华宝打交道——这让摩根建富颇为不快。作为与 23 Great Winchester Street 的联络人，戴维森自认有权分享商业秘密。上任初期，他出席了一次合伙人会议，却发现简报手册里涉及机密的部分被整齐地剪掉了。深感受辱，他发誓再也不会踏足。这一局面源自摩根担保持有摩根建富巨额股份却被迫保持被动的离奇安排，由此引发的困惑仍一如既往。 如果这能让英国央行和美联储满意，那它就有悖于逻辑和人性。

Around the same time, Lew Preston sent Sir John Stevens a letter protesting Morgan Grenfell’s mounting foreign-exchange dealings. As its New York banker, he was disturbed by excessively large open positions Morgan Guaranty had to cover. “From then on, there was a perceptible cooling off, although in the most friendly way,” recalled one ex-Morgan Grenfell executive. And as banker to Morgan Stanley’s new trading operation, Morgan Guaranty was also intermittently disturbed by *its* overdrafts.
大约在同一时间，卢·普雷斯顿致信约翰·史蒂文斯爵士，对摩根建富日益增加的外汇交易表示抗议。作为其在纽约的银行家，他对摩根保证信托不得不覆盖的过大未平仓头寸感到不安。“从那时起，双方关系明显降温，尽管是以最友好的方式，”一位前摩根建富高管回忆道。而作为摩根士丹利新交易业务的银行家，摩根保证信托也时常因其透支而受到困扰。

As shown by American Tobacco’s electrifying dash at Gallaher, Morgan Grenfell and Morgan Stanley had been drawn together by merger work. Sir John Stevens of Morgan Grenfell and Bill Sword of Morgan Stanley now contemplated a scheme for closer global cooperation. But they couldn’t proceed without Morgan Guaranty, which owned a third of Morgan Grenfell and shared in Morgan et Compagnie International, the Paris underwriting operation Morgan Stanley had inherited. (The latter now handled at least twice the Eurodollar financing of any other U.S. investment bank.) Morgan Guaranty was indeed intrigued by the notion of a foreign merger after a Morgan Grenfell delegation broached the idea at a confidential New York meeting in August, 1972. Disputes over the Morgan name, especially in Japan and the Middle East, had caused unending friction. Since some foreign customers would never fathom the whole Byzantine Morgan history, why not make an advantage of necessity? Paris had already shown the fantastic power latent in combined effort.
正如美国烟草公司对加拉赫的迅猛收购所显示的那样，摩根建富与摩根士丹利因并购业务而紧密相连。摩根建富的约翰·史蒂文斯爵士与摩根士丹利的比尔·斯沃德如今正谋划一个深化全球合作的方案。但若没有摩根保证信托的参与，这一计划便无法推进——摩根保证信托持有摩根建富三分之一股份，并共同持股摩根国际公司（这家巴黎承销机构原属摩根士丹利，如今其欧洲美元融资业务规模至少是其他美国投行的两倍）。1972 年 8 月，摩根建富代表团在纽约一场秘密会议上提出此构想后，摩根保证信托确实对海外合并的设想产生了兴趣。双方因“摩根”名称的争议（尤其在日本和中东地区）摩擦不断。既然部分海外客户永远理不清摩根家族这段错综复杂的历史，何不化劣势为优势？ 巴黎已经展示了联合努力中潜藏的惊人力量。

So on June 20, 1973, exactly forty years after Pecora’s thunderous denunciations, members of the three Morgan houses journeyed to the Grotto Bay Hotel in Bermuda, a honeymoon hideaway, for a secret meeting. Its purpose—to resurrect the House of Morgan beyond U.S. regulatory reach. The precautions for secrecy were extraordinary. The operation had taken the code name Triangle and was so hush-hush that only the most senior people knew of the meeting. (Over sixteen years later, when Ralph Leach, then chairman of Morgan Guaranty’s Executive Committee, was asked about the Bermuda meeting, he replied, “What Bermuda meeting?” When it was described to him, he said bitterly, “Gee, it must be nice to be a Morgan insider.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415746)) For older Morgan people, the proposed reunion seemed to be a chance to relive glory days. The plan called for the three houses to pool their overseas securities business in something called Morgan International. Morgan Guaranty and Morgan Stanley would each have a 45 percent stake and Morgan Grenfell the remaining 10 percent. The new entity, in turn, would own half of Morgan Grenfell. In foreign outposts where they had sparred, the three houses would cooperate instead. It would be an elegant solution to the chronic identity problem.
于是，1973 年 6 月 20 日，正值佩科拉雷霆万钧的谴责之后整整四十年，摩根三大家族的成员齐聚百慕大蜜月隐逸之地——石窟湾酒店，举行了一场秘密会议。其目的，是要在美国监管触角之外，重建摩根家族。保密措施异乎寻常。这次行动代号为“三角”，机密程度之高，只有最高层人士知晓会议内容。（十六年后，当摩根担保银行执行委员会主席拉尔夫·利奇被问及百慕大会议时，他反问道：“什么百慕大会议？”当有人向他描述会议情况时，他苦涩地说：“哎呀，做摩根内部人可真好啊。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415746) ）对于摩根老一辈而言，这场提议的重聚似乎是一次重温辉煌岁月的机会。计划要求三家家族将海外证券业务汇集到一个名为“摩根国际”的实体中。 摩根担保与摩根士丹利将各持股 45%，摩根建富则持有剩余 10%。而这家新实体反过来将拥有摩根建富一半的股权。此前曾在海外据点相互较量的这三家机构，今后将携手合作。这将成为解决长期身份困境的优雅方案。

Important to these discussions was a momentous development that had taken place at Morgan Guaranty. In 1969, the firm created a one-bank holding company called J. P. Morgan and Company, reviving a name dormant since the Guaranty merger in 1959. “There was controversy about dropping the Guaranty name for the holding company,” explained Guido Verbeck. “But we just wanted to keep pushing that Morgan name. It was magical.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415905) At first, Morgan Guaranty constituted virtually all of J. P. Morgan and Company, but it would gradually shrink as Morgans became a diversified, financial conglomerate. Such one-bank holding companies allowed banks to expand into leasing and other fields and issue commercial paper exempt from Federal Reserve interest-rate ceilings. Along with CDs, commercial paper, and Eurodollar deposits, they helped to liberate 23 Wall from the constraints of Glass-Steagall. This new freedom perhaps made the bank more skeptical of any alliance.
在这些讨论中，摩根保证信托公司（Morgan Guaranty）的一项重大进展至关重要。1969 年，该公司成立了一家单一银行控股公司，命名为 J. P. Morgan and Company，重新启用了自 1959 年与保证信托公司合并后便沉寂已久的名称。“放弃使用‘保证’作为控股公司名称曾引发争议，”吉多·弗贝克解释道，“但我们就是想继续强化‘摩根’这块招牌。它有种魔力。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2415905) 起初，摩根保证信托几乎构成了 J. P. Morgan and Company 的全部业务，但随着摩根逐渐发展为多元化的金融集团，其占比也逐步缩小。此类单一银行控股公司使银行得以拓展至租赁等领域，并发行不受美联储利率上限约束的商业票据。连同可转让定期存单、商业票据和欧洲美元存款，它们共同助力 23 Wall Street 摆脱了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的束缚。这种新获得的自由或许也让银行对任何联盟都更为审慎。

For all its imaginative appeal and historic resonance, the Bermuda meeting was a fiasco. A major obstacle was political: if banks no longer operated under direct political supervision, as they had with the 1920s foreign loans, they still behaved in a manner broadly consonant with national interests. They instinctively sought government protection for foreign loans and couldn’t casually defy the State Department or Foreign Office. As in the 1930s, geopolitical divergences in U.S. and British policy made cooperation difficult. “Morgan Grenfell was lending money to our friend Castro in Havana,” said Walter Page, then Morgan Guaranty president. “They were also lending money to North Korea. We couldn’t do that and Morgan Stanley couldn’t either. That kind of thing made it almost impossible.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416063) The Cuban and North Korean loans were, in fact, guaranteed by the British government. Thanks to Sir John Stevens, Morgan Grenfell was also strong in export credits to Iron Curtain countries.
尽管百慕大会晤充满了想象空间和历史回响，但最终却以惨淡收场。一个主要障碍在于政治层面：虽然银行不再像 20 世纪 20 年代对外贷款那样直接受政治监督，但它们的行为仍大体符合国家利益。银行本能地寻求政府对海外贷款的庇护，不会轻易违抗国务院或外交部的意愿。与 20 世纪 30 年代类似，美英两国在地缘政治上的分歧使得合作举步维艰。“摩根建富当时在贷款给我们在哈瓦那的朋友卡斯特罗，”时任摩根保证银行总裁的沃尔特·佩奇说道，“他们同时也在借钱给朝鲜。我们做不到，摩根士丹利也不行。这种局面让合作几乎无从谈起。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416063) 事实上，对古巴和朝鲜的贷款是由英国政府担保的。得益于约翰·史蒂文斯爵士，摩根建富在对铁幕国家的出口信贷领域也实力雄厚。

For Morgan Grenfell, the meeting brought out an old ambivalence toward “big brother” in the proudly sensitive junior partner. As the smallest of the three Morgan houses, the British firm feared that it would be overpowered by the bigger Americans. This sentiment was especially prevalent in the Corporate Finance Department, which threatened a revolt if the deal went through. Great Britain was about to enter the Common Market. On that basis, David Bendall of Morgan Grenfell pleaded for a special British role, arguing that his firm had the best access to Commonwealth countries and should “lead” the new joint venture into Europe. “I guess I was the one who put his foot in the plate,” said Bendall. “The Americans said it was the most chauvinistic thing they had ever heard. But we felt we were being fitted into somebody else’s structure.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416222) Lewis Preston and the Morgan Guaranty people felt they had been grossly misled as to the level of Morgan Grenfell enthusiasm. They had been prodded into the meeting in the belief of an imminent Morgan Grenfell-Morgan Stanley deal. The two American houses were also deeply offended by Bendall’s insinuation that Americans were disliked in Europe. Preston was so incensed that he threatened to sell Morgan Guaranty’s one-third stake, although he was soon calmed down by Morgan chairman Ellmore C. Patterson.
对摩根建富而言，这次会谈暴露了这家骄傲而敏感的小老弟对“老大哥”一贯的复杂心态。作为摩根三剑客中最小的一员，这家英国公司生怕被体量更大的美国同行压得喘不过气。这种情绪在企业融资部尤为强烈，该部门甚至放话：要是这笔交易成了，他们就要造反。彼时英国正筹备加入欧洲共同市场，摩根建富的大卫·本多尔便以此为由，力主英国应当扮演特殊角色——他坚称自家公司与英联邦国家往来最密切，理应由他们“领衔”将新合资企业带入欧洲。“大概是我第一个捅了马蜂窝，”本多尔坦言，“美国人说这是他们听过最沙文主义的论调。可我们真心觉得，自己正被硬塞进别人的框架里。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416222) 而刘易斯·普雷斯顿与摩根保证信托的人却认为，摩根建富此前表现出的热情程度，简直是在故意误导他们。 他们被召集到会议上，是因为相信摩根建富与摩根士丹利的交易即将达成。两家美国公司对本德尔暗示美国人在欧洲不受欢迎的言论深感不满。普雷斯顿怒不可遏，甚至威胁要出售摩根保证信托持有的三分之一股份，不过摩根董事长埃尔莫尔·C 很快安抚了他。Patterson

The Baldwin coup at Morgan Stanley added complications. It had installed a new generation whose brusque, thrusting energy and irreverent style offended Morgan Grenfell sensibilities. “The Morgan Stanley boys were the real go-ahead graspers,” recalled a Morgan Grenfell official. “You could see a mile off they were just there for the grab. They were assuming they would be entitled to boss the thing and lead it.” Many Morgan Stanley naysayers, in turn, thought Morgan Grenfell a morguelike merchant bank full of lazy, pompous dukes and earls employing anachronistic methods. They were not about to play second fiddle to the Brits.
摩根士丹利的鲍德温收购案让局面更加复杂。新上任的一代管理者作风粗鲁、锐气逼人且不拘礼节，这与摩根建富的传统风格格格不入。“摩根士丹利那帮人真是毫不掩饰的抢食者，”一位摩根建富高管回忆道，“隔着一英里都能看出来，他们就是来捞好处的，压根儿认为理所当然该由他们掌控全局、引领方向。”而摩根士丹利内部不少反对派也觉得，摩根建富活像个暮气沉沉的商人银行，净是些懒惰又傲慢的公爵伯爵，还在用老掉牙的手段。他们可不愿意给英国人当配角。

The Morgan Guaranty people had secret reservations about the new venture with Morgan Stanley. As a former official at 23 Wall noted, “There was always a feeling at the bank that Wall Street \[i.e., Morgan Stanley\] was full of get-rich people.” The salary differential had always been a sore point: a Morgan Stanley partner might earn \$150,000 in a bad year, \$500,000 in a good year, so that even a junior partner might earn more than Morgan Guaranty’s chairman. Anybody who stayed at Morgan Guaranty had to believe it represented something superior. Otherwise why not jump ship and go to the more lucrative Morgan Stanley?
摩根担保的人对与摩根士丹利的这项新合资企业心存芥蒂。正如 23 号华尔街的一位前高管所说：“银行内部始终有种看法，觉得华尔街（即摩根士丹利）那帮人全是想一夜暴富的主。”薪酬差距一直是个敏感话题：摩根士丹利的合伙人，即便在行情差的年份也能赚 15 万美元，好年景则高达 50 万美元，以至于一个初级合伙人的收入都可能超过摩根担保的董事长。但凡选择留在摩根担保的人，必须相信这里代表着某种更高层次的追求。否则，干嘛不跳槽去赚得更多的摩根士丹利呢？

Morgan Guaranty also jealously treasured the fruits of a spectacular decade of building up overseas branches and taking equity stakes in foreign banks. Of the three firms, it had experienced the most robust growth, traveling far beyond the little hothouse bank that stood in Morgan Stanley’s shadow in the 1950s. “By Bermuda, Morgan Guaranty was a real entity in this world and had more feet in a lot of places than any of the others,” Walter Page explained. “We would have been giving up a hell of a lot that we had accomplished already in Japan, Australia, Singapore, and Hong Kong.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416380) By 1972, a third of J. P. Morgan and Company’s profits were coming from abroad, a figure that would soar to over 50 percent within a few years. It had moved furthest and fastest toward globalization and didn’t want to share its booty with others.
摩根担保同样对过去十年间在海外设立分支机构和参股外国银行所取得的辉煌成果视若珍宝。在三家公司中，它经历了最为强劲的增长，早已不是 1950 年代那个活在摩根士丹利阴影下的温室小银行。“在百慕大，摩根担保是这个世界上的一个真实存在，而且在许多地方的触角都比其他任何一家都要多，”沃尔特·佩奇解释道。“我们本来就要放弃在日本、澳大利亚、新加坡和香港已经取得的巨大成就。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416380) 到了 1972 年，摩根公司三分之一的利润来自海外，这个数字在几年内飙升至超过 50%。它在全球化进程中走得最远、最快，不想与他人分享战利品。

The final set of reservations in this complicated game came from Morgan Stanley. Buoyed by its success in Paris, the firm felt understandably brash and confident and willing to go it alone abroad. “They thought they were big, independent, and successful and didn’t need nannies,” recalled an observer. Yet not everybody was opposed. Shep-pard Poor later said, “The internationalists thought it would be beneficial to expand our ties overseas. The domestic people thought we would be giving away more than we were getting.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416539) Worried about Morgan Stanley’s limited capital, Frank Petito felt strongly about his firm’s need for Morgan Guaranty’s deep pockets. (The visionaries at all three firms were always obsessed with capital.) But Baldwin, suspicious of foreign business, which he often saw as a waste of time and money, didn’t provide the necessary push, despite his sentimental attachment to the Morgan name and history.
这场复杂博弈中的最后一批保留意见来自摩根士丹利。由于在巴黎的成功，这家公司显得理所应当地趾高气扬、自信满满，甚至愿意单枪匹马闯海外。“他们觉得自己家大业大、独立自主、功成名就，根本不需要别人当保姆，”一位观察者回忆道。但并非所有人都持反对意见。谢泼德·普尔后来表示：“国际派认为拓展海外联系利大于弊。国内派则认为我们付出的远多于得到的。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2416539) 担心摩根士丹利资本有限，弗兰克·佩蒂托强烈感到公司需要摩根担保公司的雄厚财力。（这三家公司的远见者始终对资本念念不忘。）而鲍德温对海外业务心存疑虑，常视其为浪费时间和金钱的勾当，尽管他对摩根这个名字和历史怀有感情上的依恋，却未能提供必要的推动力。

At the Bermuda meeting, the House of Morgan—as a dream, a possibility, a will-o’-the-wisp—ceased to be. Afterward, the three firms largely went their own ways and evolved into separate and quite combative competitors. The age of interlocking partnerships, of spheres of interest and mysterious interplay among financial powers, was over. In 1974, Morgan Grenfell set up a New York representative office, the first beachhead of a counterinvasion. Far more than the two other firms, it would suffer from Bermuda’s failure, which might have given it a strong, early presence in global securities markets, albeit at the cost of its identity. A minority, led by Lord Catto, presciently believed that it was better to be a major player in global markets, even if in a junior role, than to be doomed to second-rate autonomy. In 1976, Morgan Stanley bought up the remaining one-third interest of the Paris operation, renamed it Morgan Stanley International, and packed it off to London under Archie Cox, Jr. In 1979, Morgan Guaranty, having at last recovered from the Schwab trauma, set up Morgan Guaranty Limited in London for Euromarket underwriting, facing off against the Cox venture. Both times Morgan Grenfell spurned invitations to participate, afraid of being swallowed up. Now the three Morgan houses would fight each other without quarter.
在百慕大会议上，摩根财团——作为一个梦想、一种可能、一场虚幻——就此终结。此后，三家事务所基本各走各路，演变为相互独立且竞争激烈的对手。那相互关联的合伙关系、势力范围以及金融巨头间神秘互动的时代，结束了。1974 年，摩根建富在纽约设立了代表处，这是反攻的第一个滩头阵地。相较于另外两家事务所，它受百慕大失败的打击更为沉重——那次本可让它以牺牲自身身份为代价，在全球证券市场早期就占据强势地位。以卡托勋爵为首的少数派有先见之明地认为，在全球市场成为主要参与者（哪怕处于次级角色）也好过注定沦为二流自主的境地。1976 年，摩根士丹利收购了巴黎业务剩余的三分之一权益，将其更名为摩根士丹利国际，并交由小阿奇·考克斯迁往伦敦。 1979 年，摩根担保（Morgan Guaranty）终于从施瓦布创伤（Schwab trauma）中恢复过来，在伦敦设立了摩根担保有限公司（Morgan Guaranty Limited），从事欧洲债券市场承销业务，与考克斯公司（Cox venture）正面交锋。摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）两次都拒绝了参与邀请，担心被吞并。如今，这三家摩根机构将展开殊死搏斗。

At Bermuda, the House of Morgan died a quiet death. In characteristic Morgan style, the funeral itself was unknown to the outside world. No obituaries appeared in the press; it died in secrecy.
在百慕大，摩根家族悄然消亡。一如摩根式的风格，葬礼本身不为外界所知。报上没有登载讣告；它在秘密中死去。

WHILE Morgan Guaranty retained a tweedy, well-bred style of banking in the mid-1970s, Morgan Stanley experimented with a more muscular approach to business. It faced competitive threats that didn’t permit the old mannerly Morgan style. For all its braggadocio and chest-thumping swagger, the firm was very vulnerable, as Bob Baldwin realized when he campaigned for a new trading operation in stocks and bonds. As his chief visual aid, Baldwin would hold up an old tombstone ad and point out the legions of competitors who had perished. Morgan Stanley now faced a host of brawny rivals, trading powers such as Salomon Brothers and Goldman, Sachs, and that retail behemoth, Merrill Lynch. The gentlemanly way of doing business was becoming a privilege the firm could no longer afford. Morgan Stanley betrayed no mood of crisis. With clients such as
尽管摩根担保在 20 世纪 70 年代中期仍保持着一种老派、优雅的银行风格，但摩根士丹利开始尝试更具冲击力的业务方式。它面临着竞争威胁，已不容许再延续过去那种彬彬有礼的摩根风格。尽管公司表面上吹嘘张扬、气势汹汹，但实际上非常脆弱——正如鲍勃·鲍德温在为推进新的股票债券交易业务而奔走时所意识到的那样。鲍德温时常拿出一份过时的墓碑广告作为视觉辅助，指出那些已消失的竞争对手。如今，摩根士丹利面对的是一群强悍的对手：所罗门兄弟、高盛这样的交易巨头，以及零售业巨擘美林证券。那种绅士般的行事方式，已成为公司再也负担不起的特权。不过，摩根士丹利并未显露出任何危机感。

通用汽车、埃克森、通用电气、AT&T 和德士古这些巨头，倒也没真慌了神。正如 1974 年《商业周刊》所言："它依然是投行圈最显赫的名号，这招牌无论走到哪儿都畅通无阻。"即便在穿着打扮上，摩根士丹利的合伙人们也仿佛与那个时代的随性风潮绝缘。当年那篇《商业周刊》文章配了一张二十多位面色凝重的合伙人的合影，有位作家调侃道："这照片摆的姿势，活像是在殡仪馆开行业交流会。"

Nevertheless, a crisis lurked below the surface. The investment banker’s historic role as middleman and gatekeeper of capital markets was being devalued. Mature companies could now sell commercial paper or place debt privately with institutions. Some companies had grown so rich—Ford; Sears, Roebuck; and General Electric—that they would serve as banks themselves. Lewis Bernard of Morgan Stanley accurately predicted: “Clients will try to do more for themselves. Our principal competition is our clients.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417004)
然而，危机潜伏在表面之下。投资银行作为资本市场中间人和守门人的历史角色正在贬值。成熟的公司现在可以直接发行商业票据或向机构私下配售债务。一些公司变得如此富有——比如福特、西尔斯·罗巴克和通用电气——以至于它们自己就能充当银行。摩根士丹利的刘易斯·伯纳德准确地预测道：“客户会试图自己多做一些事。我们最主要的竞争对手就是我们的客户。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417004)

The new trading and distribution wing shored up Morgan Stanley’s underwriting business. At the same time, it highlighted the fact that underwriting was becoming a humdrum commodity business. Morgan Stanley needed another main event, not just new sideshows. It found the answer in the predatory world of mergers and acquisitions, setting up Wall Street’s first M&A department in the early 1970s. As Morgan Grenfell had already discovered, it was an ideal business for posh but capital-poor firms.
新的交易与分销部门强化了摩根士丹利的承销业务，同时也突显出承销正沦为平淡无奇的商品化业务。摩根士丹利需要的不仅是锦上添花的副业，更需一项核心主业。它在充满掠夺性的并购世界中找到了答案——于 20 世纪 70 年代初设立了华尔街首个并购部门。正如摩根建富早已发现的，这对那些出身名门却资本拮据的投行而言，堪称理想之选。

Merger work was no novelty to the firm. In the 1950s, Northey Jones had consolidated several carpet companies into Mohasco Corporation. Alex Tomlinson had been a matchmaker for British Petroleum in its purchase of a large stake in Standard Oil of Ohio. (Morgan Stanley’s role was hidden to avoid angering its seven-sister clients, who might not warm to a new oil giant in the U.S. market.) And Bill Sword aided Morgan Grenfell in the American Tobacco takeover of Gallaher. In the past, Morgan Stanley had collected a modest fee for such work or used it as a free loss leader to generate underwriting business. Merger work formed part of a total advisory relationship with clients. Some Morgan Stanley partners now objected to marketing it as a discrete service. This segmentation of the business, known as transactional banking, would gradually supplant the earlier system of comprehensive dealings with clients, or relationship banking.
合并业务对这家公司来说并不新鲜。20 世纪 50 年代，Northey Jones 将几家地毯公司整合为 Mohasco Corporation。Alex Tomlinson 曾为 British Petroleum 牵线搭桥，助其收购 Standard Oil of Ohio 的大量股份。（Morgan Stanley 的角色被隐藏起来，以免激怒其“七姐妹”客户——它们可能不乐见美国市场上出现新的石油巨头。）而 Bill Sword 则协助 Morgan Grenfell 完成了 American Tobacco 对 Gallaher 的收购。过去，Morgan Stanley 对此类业务仅收取不高的费用，或将其作为免费引流手段以招揽承销业务。合并工作是公司与客户全面顾问关系的一部分。如今，Morgan Stanley 的一些合伙人反对将其作为独立服务进行推销。这种业务细分被称为交易型银行业务，它将逐渐取代早期与客户进行全面往来的关系型银行业务体系。

For the most part, Morgan Stanley had sat out the conglomerate wave of the 1960s. This movement attempted to reduce diverse companies to a common calculus of profit and loss. Conglomerates threw together scores of unrelated businesses to bypass antitrust restrictions that might block intra-industry mergers. The craze remade the corporate landscape, creating eighteen of America’s one hundred largest companies; twenty-five thousand businesses vanished in the 1960s. Many takeovers were financed by inflated share prices of the conglomerates themselves—financial hocus-pocus that made Morgan Stanley nervous. The firm wasn’t really pressed to participate in the craze. Most conglomerates were acquisitive upstarts and not the staid, blue-chip firms on the Morgan Stanley client roster.
在 20 世纪 60 年代的联合大企业浪潮中，摩根士丹利基本上置身事外。这股潮流试图将五花八门的公司简化成统一的盈亏计算方式。联合大企业把几十个毫不相干的业务拼凑在一起，以此规避可能阻碍行业内部并购的反垄断限制。这场热潮重塑了企业版图，美国最大的 100 家公司中有 18 家由此诞生；而 60 年代里，有 2.5 万家企业消失得无影无踪。许多收购案的资金来源是联合大企业自身虚高的股价——这种财务上的障眼法让摩根士丹利深感不安。这家公司并没有被这股热潮裹挟着非要参与其中。大多数联合大企业都是野心勃勃的暴发户，根本不在摩根士丹利那份沉稳、蓝筹的客户名单上。

Corporate restructuring was still curbed by Wall Street etiquette, which frowned on unsolicited takeovers. Afraid of conflicts with clients, Morgan Stanley had a rule against hostile takeovers. In 1970, it nearly engaged in its first hostile bid when Warner-Lambert decided to take over part of Eversharp’s shaving business; in that case, the mere threat of a hostile takeover made the target submit. So Morgan Stanley’s taboo-breaking hostile raid was postponed until 1974, when International Nickel (Inco) pursued the Philadelphia-based ESB, formerly called Electric Storage Battery.
企业重组仍受制于华尔街礼仪的约束，主动收购在当时被视为不合规矩。为避免与客户产生冲突，摩根士丹利曾明文禁止恶意收购。1970 年，华纳兰伯特决定收购永锋公司部分剃须业务时，摩根士丹利险些涉足其首次恶意竞购——仅凭恶意收购的威胁便迫使目标公司就范。然而，摩根士丹利真正打破禁忌的恶意收购行动，直到 1974 年才得以实施：当时国际镍业公司（Inco）盯上了总部位于费城的 ESB（原电储电池公司）。

By this point, an ambitious young partner named Bob Greenhill headed the four-man M&A Department. He had reluctantly entered the takeover area, regarding it as a slow track to the top. Then he saw there was money—lots of money—to be made. As Wall Street’s first takeover star, Greenhill would rewrite the rules of the game. He wanted the work to be tough, professional, and extremely disciplined. Most of all, he wanted it to be profitable and not a lagniappe thrown to a favored client. While some older partners still wanted to offer merger service free, Greenhill, Yerger Johnstone, and Bill Sword devised a fee schedule that took a percentage of the money involved in a takeover. From now on, the firm would ask for retainers just to scout mergers, a process in which employees sat around fantasizing matchups.
此时，一位雄心勃勃的年轻合伙人鲍勃·格林希尔（Bob Greenhill）领导着仅有四人的并购部门。他起初并不情愿涉足收购领域，认为这是一条晋升的慢车道。但很快，他意识到这里蕴含着巨大财富——是真正的金山。作为华尔街首位收购明星，格林希尔将彻底改写游戏规则。他要让并购工作变得硬核、专业，且纪律严明。最重要的是，他追求的是真金白银的利润，而非随手抛给老客户的赠品。当某些资深合伙人仍想免费提供并购服务时，格林希尔与耶格尔·约翰斯通（Yerger Johnstone）、比尔·斯沃德（Bill Sword）共同制定了一套收费方案：按收购交易金额的百分比收费。从此，仅仅是寻找并购标的——这个员工们终日围坐幻想配对的过程——公司就要收取预聘费。

When merger work was a free service designed to preserve an underwriting relationship with a client, the investment banker had no incentive to approve or reject a takeover, thus guaranteeing his objectivity. Now the incentive system was quite heavily loaded toward advocating takeovers. The bigger and more frequent the takeover, the more profit for Morgan Stanley. The new fee-for-service mentality directly related to the declining importance of underwriting. If blue-chip clients brought in less bond business, why pamper them with extras? “We charge for the services we provide,” Lewis Bernard explained. “When a client asks us to take on an assignment, we expect to get paid for it.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417156)
当并购业务还是一项为维护客户承销关系而提供的免费服务时，投资银行家缺乏批准或拒绝收购的动力，这保证了其客观性。如今，激励机制已严重偏向于推动收购。收购规模越大、频率越高，摩根士丹利的利润就越多。这种以服务收费为核心的新思维，与承销业务重要性下降直接相关。既然蓝筹客户带来的债券业务减少了，何必再额外优待他们？"我们对自己提供的服务收费，"刘易斯·伯纳德解释道，"当客户委托我们承担任务时，我们期望获得相应的报酬。"

Son of a Swedish immigrant and Baltimore clothing-company owner, Bob Greenhill came to Morgan Stanley via Yale, Harvard Business School, and the U.S. Navy. He was one of the “irreverent six” who participated in Bob Baldwin’s bloodless coup. In the navy, he was called Greenie, and he remained so called at Morgan Stanley, a firm with a preppy relish for nicknames. (Baldwin was Baldy.) Short and trim with curly hair, powerful shoulders, and a narrow waist, he had a boyish grin that masked an obsessive intensity.
瑞典移民之子、巴尔的摩服装公司主的后代，鲍勃·格林希尔（Bob Greenhill）历经耶鲁、哈佛商学院和美国海军，最终加入摩根士丹利。他是参与鲍勃·鲍德温（Bob Baldwin）不流血政变的“六位叛逆者”之一。在海军时，人们叫他"格林尼"（Greenie），到了摩根士丹利这个热衷给员工起绰号的精英机构，这个称呼也沿用下来（鲍德温被称为"鲍尔迪"Baldy）。他身材精干，卷发宽肩窄腰，带着少年般的笑容，实则暗藏执着到近乎偏执的劲头。

Greenhill personified the rock-’em, sock-’em style that would characterize Wall Street in the 1980s. He came alive in combat, and his stamina in all-night bargaining sessions was mythical. He was tailor-made for financial warfare. A former partner declared: “Bob is smart and completely insensitive. He couldn’t care less what you think and he has no need for peer acceptance or approval. He marches to his own drummer. He’s very rational, very focused. On the wall of his office, he has an Al Capp cartoon showing Fearless Fosdick riddled with bullets. The caption is MERE FLESH WOUNDS.’ That’s how Bob sees himself.” Once called the “ultimate samurai,” Greenhill had qualities that made him a formidable negotiator but a trying person. Another former partner remarked, “Bob knows he’s good and he talks down to clients. But CEOs can’t afford not to use the best. So they just decide to stomach him because he’s so good.”
格林希尔身上体现了那种硬碰硬、拳拳到肉的风格，这将成为 20 世纪 80 年代华尔街的特征。他在战斗中活力四射，通宵谈判的耐力近乎传奇。他简直是金融战的天生战士。一位前合伙人评价道："鲍勃聪明绝顶，但完全不通人情。他根本不在乎你怎么想，也不需要同僚的认可或赞同。他独行其是，极其理性，极其专注。他办公室墙上挂着一幅阿尔·卡普的漫画，画中的无畏福斯迪克浑身弹孔，标题写着'只是皮肉伤'。这就是鲍勃对自己的看法。"曾被称作"终极武士"的格林希尔既有令人敬畏的谈判才能，也有令人难以忍受的个性。另一位前合伙人说："鲍勃知道自己很厉害，所以对客户总是一副居高临下的口气。但 CEO 们舍不得不用最好的，所以只能忍着他，因为他实在太出色了。"

Greenhill was that Morgan rarity—a partner who emerges as a distinct personality in the public mind. Publicity accompanied transactional banking as naturally as secrecy did relationship banking. Where Morgan style of dress had been aloof and understated, Greenhill wore suspenders monogrammed with dollar bills. (Surely Harold Stanley would have cringed!) He resembled a commando more than the martini-drinking Morgan banker of yesteryear. He was a resourceful, derring-do character. Once in Saudi Arabia, after missing a scheduled commercial flight, he hired a private plane just so that he and two other partners could make a dinner appointment in another Saudi city.
格林希尔是摩根家族中罕见的人物——一位在公众心目中个性鲜明的合伙人。与交易银行业务相伴的自然是公开宣传，正如关系银行业务必然伴随保密一样。摩根风格的着装向来冷漠低调，而格林希尔却佩戴着绣有美元符号的背带。（想必哈罗德·斯坦利会不堪其扰！）他更像一名突击队员，而非昔日啜饮马提尼的摩根银行家。他足智多谋、胆识过人。某次在沙特阿拉伯，由于错过预定商业航班，他竟租用私人飞机，只为与另外两位合伙人赶往另一座沙特城市赴约晚餐。

Instead of tennis or golf, Greenhill liked solitary sports that tested endurance. Early each morning, he jogged near his Greenwich, Connecticut, home. He was also a motorcycle enthusiast. Once on a vacation, a bush pilot dropped him and several fellow canoeists into an icy river within the Arctic Circle. A month and five hundred miles later, the pilot rendezvoused with these wilderness explorers in the Atlantic. Green-hill’s takeovers resembled such holidays. As a friend told the writer David Halberstam, “Bob regards these battles as miniature Okinawas.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417299) Greenhill savored a new rhetoric of corporate battle: “It’s important to know about a chief executive, whether he has the stomach for a fight. You see people with the veneer stripped away, in their elemental form.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417469) The world of investment banking, once attractive for its leisurely elegance, was now a cockpit for pin-striped combatants.
格林希尔不喜欢网球或高尔夫，他偏爱那些考验耐力的独自运动。每天清晨，他都会在康涅狄格州格林威治的家附近慢跑。他还是一名摩托车爱好者。有一次度假时，一位丛林飞行员把他和几位划独木舟的同伴丢进了北极圈内一条冰冷的河流。一个月后，航程五百英里，这位飞行员在大西洋与这些荒野探险者会合。格林希尔的收购行为就像这样的假期。正如一位朋友告诉作家大卫·哈伯斯塔姆的那样：“鲍勃把这些战斗视为微型冲绳战役。”格林希尔乐于接受一种新的企业战斗说辞：“了解一位首席执行官很重要，看他是否有战斗的勇气。你会看到人们剥去外衣，露出本真的一面。”投资银行业曾经以其悠闲优雅而吸引人，如今却成了穿着细条纹西装的斗士们的战场。

In 1974, Greenhill was field marshal for Morgan Stanley’s first hostile takeover—International Nickel’s raid on ESB, the world’s largest maker of batteries. It wasn’t the first unsolicited takeover in Wall Street history: what was the 1901 Northern Pacific corner if not a raid by Edward H. Harriman? But the auspices shocked Wall Street, for Inco was a conservative, blue-chip firm and Morgan Stanley was the official custodian of the Gentleman Banker’s Code.
1974 年，格林希尔担任摩根士丹利首次敌意收购——国际镍业公司突袭全球最大电池制造商 ESB——的战场元帅。这并非华尔街历史上第一起非邀约收购：1901 年爱德华·H·哈里曼对北大平洋铁路的围猎，难道不算突袭吗？但这次行动的手腕令华尔街瞠目结舌，因为国际镍业是家保守的蓝筹公司，而摩根士丹利则是绅士银行家准则的官方守护者。

The old House of Morgan had long dominated mining, having financed Anglo-American; Kennecott; Anaconda; Newmont Mining; Phelps, Dodge; and Texas Gulf Sulphur. Inco’s plight typified that of Morgan Stanley’s mature mining clients. In the 1950s, the Canadian company had controlled an astonishing 85 percent of Western nickel output. By the 1970s, its monopoly was slipping. Buffeted by fluctuations in nickel and copper prices, management decided to diversify. After the 1973 Arab oil embargo, Inco was tantalized by Philadelphia-based ESB, and there was fanciful talk about electric cars powered by ESB car batteries, with Inco nickel powder in those batteries.
老摩根家族长期以来一直主导着矿业领域，曾为英美资源、肯尼科特、阿纳康达、纽蒙特矿业、菲尔普斯道奇以及德克萨斯湾硫磺等公司提供融资。加拿大国际镍公司（Inco）的困境，正是摩根士丹利那些成熟矿业客户的缩影。20 世纪 50 年代，这家加拿大企业一度控制了西方镍产量的 85%，令人咋舌。但到了 70 年代，其垄断地位开始松动。受镍、铜价格波动冲击，管理层决定走多元化路线。1973 年阿拉伯石油禁运后，Inco 对总部位于费城的 ESB 公司产生了浓厚兴趣，甚至异想天开地讨论起由 ESB 汽车电池驱动的电动车——而 Inco 的镍粉正是这些电池的关键原料。

It is important to note that the impetus for this landmark takeover originated not with Morgan Stanley but with Inco. Inco’s chief financial officer, Chuck Baird, had worked under Bob Baldwin as assistant navy secretary. It was Baird who convinced his superiors to undertake the ESB attack—with or without Morgan Stanley. By conducting such a raid, a new Inco management team wanted to shed the company’s stolid image. So the proposal came from a trusted client, putting Morgan Stanley on the spot.
需要强调的是，这场具有里程碑意义的收购案的推动力并非来自摩根士丹利，而是源自英可公司。英可的首席财务官查克·贝尔德曾在鲍勃·鲍德温手下担任海军助理部长。正是贝尔德说服公司高层发起对 ESB 的收购行动——无论有没有摩根士丹利参与。通过这次突袭式收购，英可的新管理团队希望摆脱公司一贯沉闷的形象。由于提案来自一位备受信任的客户，摩根士丹利顿时陷入了两难境地。

The merger business had boomed during the bear market of 1973-74. Hundreds of brokerage firms left the business, limousines disappeared from the canyons, and downtown rents plummeted. The Coachman Restaurant, a favorite luncheon spot, sported a sign that said ’ALL THE SALAD YOU CAN EAT” and “FREE FLOWING WINE.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417637) Old-timers said the Street hadn’t been so cheerless since the 1930s. Yet for the investment banks, there were hopeful signs in the new *stagflation*—the combination of inflation and economic stagnation. Stagflation suddenly made it cheaper to buy companies on Wall Street than to invest in bricks and mortar. The age of “paper entrepreneurialism,” to use Harvard economist Robert Reich’s term, had arrived.
在 1973-74 年的熊市期间，并购业务反而异常火爆。数百家经纪公司关门大吉，豪华轿车从街道上消失，市中心租金一落千丈。昔日热门午餐据点——马车夫餐厅（Coachman Restaurant）的招牌上赫然写着"沙拉畅吃"和"红酒无限续杯"。 [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417637) 老华尔街人感叹，自 1930 年代以来，这条街从未如此萧条。然而对投资银行而言，新的滞胀——通货膨胀与经济停滞的叠加——却暗藏希望。滞胀让华尔街收购公司的成本突然变得比投资实体资产更便宜。用哈佛经济学家罗伯特·赖克（Robert Reich）的话说，"纸面创业"时代悄然降临。

The nearly forty Morgan Stanley partners (technically directors after the 1970 partial incorporation) debated whether to spurn Inco or defy a code that had governed the world of high finance for almost 150 years. The firm still moved by consensus in major matters. Against more squeamish souls, Greenhill had lined up Baldwin and chairman Frank A. Petito. Petito’s role was curious. Although nominally the firm’s chairman, Petito, shy and introverted, shrank from administration, ceding the task to Baldwin. Yet on important policy matters, his vote carried tremendous weight. He was very much the statesman and the conscience of the firm, inheriting the Harry Morgan role. Petito recognized the need for a new pugnacity. A revealing term had slipped into the Morgan Stanley lexicon: when Greenhill’s squad needed to prod a reluctant senior man into aggressive action, they said he needed to be *japped*—a reference to frank *A.* Petito.
近四十位摩根士丹利的合伙人（严格来说，在 1970 年部分公司化后应为董事）就是否拒绝 Inco 的收购要约，还是打破那条主导了近 150 年高端金融世界的潜规则，展开了激烈辩论。这家公司仍遵循重大事项需达成共识的传统。格林希尔联合了鲍德温和董事长弗兰克·A·佩蒂托，以抗衡那些更为瞻前顾后的同仁。佩蒂托的角色颇为微妙。他虽然名义上是公司董事长，但为人腼腆内向，不喜行政事务，将管理权交给了鲍德温。然而，在重大政策问题上，他的投票分量极重。他更像是位政治家，堪称公司的道德标杆，继承了哈里·摩根的角色。佩蒂托意识到需要一种新的战斗精神。一个颇具深意的词汇悄然进入了摩根士丹利的词典：当格林希尔的团队需要敦促某位犹豫不决的高管采取激进行动时，他们会说这人需要被“佩蒂托一把”——这个说法正是源自弗兰克·A·佩蒂托。

Greenhill made a pitch for hostile takeovers as an irresistible trend that was fair to shareholders, if not always to management. The argument of inevitability was probably the decisive one. As one partner recalls, “The debate was, if we don’t do what our clients want, somebody else will.” The partners were more receptive to this argument after Morgan Stanley’s work with Morgan Grenfell, and Yerger (ohnstone cited unsolicited raids in London as a precedent for America. Bob Baldwin agreed: “We did a lot of mental gymnastics about it. . . . When the water rises in London, it will soon flood New York.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417788)
格林希尔将敌意收购视为不可抗拒的趋势，认为这对股东公平（即便对管理层未必如此）。这种"必然性"的观点很可能是决定性的。正如一位合伙人回忆的那样："争论焦点在于——如果我们不做客户想要的事，别人就会去做。"在摩根士丹利与摩根建富合作之后，合伙人们更容易接受这种论调，耶格尔（约翰斯顿）引用伦敦的主动邀约收购作为美国的先例。鲍勃·鲍德温表示赞同："我们在这件事上做了很多思想斗争……当伦敦的水位上涨时，很快就会淹没纽约。"

Frank Petito figured out how to twist the desecration of tradition into seeming veneration: in obliging Inco, the firm would simply be honoring an old Morgan tradition of serving faithful clients. But Petito had enough qualms about what they were doing to cast the upcoming Inco raid as an exception. A compromise was forged: the bank, in future, would engineer hostile raids only for existing clients and would fully warn them of unpleasant consequences. This, of course, didn’t rule out much business. Morgan Stanley’s large clients were just the sort that would now want to conduct raids, and they would know all about the unpleasant consequences. The compromise mostly reassured the firm’s clients that it wouldn’t be coming after *them.*
弗兰克·佩蒂托想出了一种办法，将亵渎传统的行为扭曲成看似敬重的举动：只要照顾到英可公司，这家企业就不过是在遵循摩根老字号服务忠实客户的悠久传统罢了。不过，佩蒂托对自己正在做的事终究心存疑虑，于是将即将展开的针对英可的突袭收购定性为例外。最终达成了一项折中方案：未来这家银行只对现有客户策划敌意收购，并且会充分警告他们可能带来的不良后果。当然，这并没有排除太多业务。摩根士丹利的大客户恰恰就是那种现在想要发动收购的企业，而且他们完全清楚不良后果是什么。这项折中方案主要让公司的客户们放心，银行不会对他们下手。

At this juncture, Morgan Stanley made another unorthodox decision. Like Morgan Guaranty, the firm had long relied on the Wasp white-glove law firm of Davis, Polk, and Wardwell, which had looked on takeover work as vulgar and had avoided it. With Morgan Stanley partners terrified of lawsuits ensuing from takeover work, they now wanted a tough, seasoned specialist. Greenhill insisted on hiring the experienced Joe Flom of Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher, and Flom, whom he had met through Bill Sword. Flom was a short, friendly man in glasses who had attended Manhattan’s tuition-free City College, then Harvard Law. He pioneered in hostile takeovers in the 1950s, when Skadden, Arps was still a humble, four-man operation. For twenty years, he thrived on the scraps from law firms that were too haughty or too dignified to conduct hostile raids.
此时，摩根士丹利做出了另一个非传统决定。与摩根担保信托一样，该公司长期依赖 Wasp 精英律师事务所戴维斯·波克·沃德韦尔，这家律所曾视并购业务为粗俗之举而避之不及。如今，摩根士丹利的合伙人们因担心并购工作引发诉讼，急需一位强硬且经验丰富的专家。格林希尔坚持聘请世达·阿普斯·斯莱特·米格·弗洛姆律师事务所中经验丰富的乔·弗洛姆——他通过比尔·斯沃德结识了此人。弗洛姆个子不高，戴着眼镜，待人友善，曾在免学费的曼哈顿城市学院就读，后进入哈佛法学院。他在 20 世纪 50 年代开创了敌意收购的先河，当时世达·阿普斯还是一家仅有四名律师的小型事务所。二十年来，他靠着那些高傲自持、不屑于从事敌意收购的律所放弃的业务，过得风生水起。

When Flom was made a special counsel to Morgan Stanley, there were stormy scenes with Davis, Polk partners, who were deeply offended by the decision. Whatever its other consequences, the trend in hostile takeovers democratized the New York legal world and provided an opening in Wall Street for Jewish lawyers. Both Joe Flom and Marty Lipton of Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen, and Katz profited from the early refusal of old-line Wasp firms to sully their hands with takeovers. In time, Flom would earn \$3 million to \$5 million a year, and his law firm would end up as New York’s largest, with nine hundred lawyers. Flom would be integral to Morgan Stanley’s takeover machine. Greenhill later said, “we know each other’s jobs so well that we’re almost interchangeable.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417946) In 1975, Morgan Stanley completed the incorporation process, not wanting to have unlimited liability with the risky Greenhill operation underway.
当 Flom 被任命为摩根士丹利的特别顾问时，与 Davis Polk 的合伙人发生了激烈冲突，他们对这一决定深感不满。无论其他后果如何，敌意收购的浪潮让纽约的法律界更加民主化，也为犹太律师在华尔街打开了大门。Joe Flom 和 Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen, and Katz 的 Marty Lipton 都从老牌 Wasp 律所最初不愿染指收购业务中获益。后来，Flom 的年收入达到了 300 万到 500 万美元，他的律师事务所最终成为纽约最大的律所，拥有 900 名律师。Flom 在摩根士丹利的收购机器中不可或缺。Greenhill 后来曾说：“我们对彼此的工作了如指掌，几乎可以互换角色。” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2417946) 1975 年，摩根士丹利完成了公司化进程，因为不愿在风险重重的 Greenhill 业务中承担无限责任。

As for the Inco raid—on July 17, 1974, Bob Greenhill called Fred Port of ESB to say that he and Inco representatives wanted to visit him in Philadelphia the next day. Port was about to depart for a Kenyan safari. Stunned, he privately dismissed Greenhill as a whippersnapper but canceled his plans. Inco’s outside directors didn’t learn about the imminent move until the next morning, when Chuck Baird and Greenhill briefed them. Approval was nearly unanimous; the sole holdout was Ellmore Patterson of Morgan Guaranty, who cited his board seat at Union Carbide, an ESB rival, as his reason for abstaining.
至于英可（Inco）的突袭收购——1974 年 7 月 17 日，鲍勃·格林希尔致电 ESB 公司的弗雷德·波特，称他和英可代表次日想前往费城拜访。波特正准备动身去肯尼亚狩猎，听闻此事大为震惊，私下里把格林希尔看作不知天高地厚的毛头小子，但还是取消了原计划。英可的外部董事直到翌日早上才得知这一即将采取的行动，当时查克·贝尔德和格林希尔向他们做了简要汇报。批准几乎全票通过；唯一弃权的是摩根保证信托的埃尔莫尔·帕特森，他给出的理由是自己在 ESB 的竞争对手联合碳化物公司董事会任职，因此选择不参与表决。

The raiders then flew off to Philadelphia by helicopter. Their attack would be vintage Flom-Greenhill—a *blitzkrieg* that gave them the advantage of surprise, a tactic that worked wonders in the early days when they faced inexperienced executives. ESB’s Fred Port was shocked when told that Inco wanted to buy his company for \$28 a share—a substantial premium over its \$19 market price. When Baird added that they would proceed whether ESB liked it or not, Port flushed deeply.
袭击者们随后乘坐直升机飞往费城。他们的行动堪称弗洛姆-格林希尔式的经典突袭——一场闪电战，出其不意地抢占先机，这种战术在早期面对缺乏经验的对手时屡试不爽。当 ESB 的弗雷德·波特得知 Inco 想以每股 28 美元收购他的公司时，他大为震惊——这比 19 美元的市场价溢价不少。而当贝尔德补充说无论 ESB 是否同意，他们都会推进收购时，波特的脸瞬间涨得通红。

Enlisting the aid of Steve Friedman of Goldman, Sachs, Port issued a letter denouncing such ungracious behavior. Friedman, suspecting shame was now obsolete on Wall Street, advised Port either to fight on antitrust grounds or find a “white knight.” All at once, Morgan Stanley and Goldman, Sachs had settled into their respective slots in the takeover world. Representing the restless, mature giants who wished to diversify into other fields, Morgan Stanley took the offense. Associated with the more medium-sized and retail firms likely to be prey, Goldman, Sachs found its *métier* in defense. Billing itself the Robin Hood of Wall Street, Goldman, Sachs would refuse to represent aggressors, although it would sometimes offer them advice. Gradually Wall Street divided into two camps—the offensive (Morgan Stanley, First Boston, Drexel Burnham, Merrill Lynch, and Lazard Frères) and the defensive (Goldman, Sachs; Kidder, Peabody; Salomon Brothers; Dillon, Read; and Smith, Barney). And Joe Flom would consistently square off against defense specialist Marty Lipton.
在高盛集团的史蒂夫·弗里德曼协助下，波特公司发布了一封公开信，对这种缺乏风度的行为予以谴责。弗里德曼认为华尔街早已不知羞耻为何物，便建议波特要么以反垄断为由展开反击，要么另寻"白衣骑士"。转眼间，摩根士丹利与高盛便在收购界各就各位——前者代表那些急于跨界扩张、躁动不安的行业巨头，主动发起攻势；后者则凭借与中型及零售企业（往往成为收购目标）的紧密联系，在防御战中找到了自己的定位。高盛自诩为华尔街的罗宾汉，虽偶尔提供咨询建议，但坚决拒绝代表侵略方。渐渐地，华尔街分裂为两大阵营：进攻方（摩根士丹利、第一波士顿、德崇证券、美林、拉扎德）与防守方（高盛、基德尔·皮博迪、所罗门兄弟、狄龙·里德、史密斯·巴尼）。乔·弗洛姆（Joe Flom）经常与防守专家马蒂·利普顿（Marty Lipton）正面交锋。

ESB did bring in a white knight—Harry Gray of United Aircraft (later United Technologies), who leapt into a bidding war that sent the battery maker’s price far above the initial bid. Spurred on by Greenhill, Inco delivered a knockout blow, boosting its final bid from \$38 to a victorious \$41 a share in a day. Inco was suddenly worth more than twice as much as before the frenetic bidding began.
ESB 确实找来了一位白衣骑士——联合飞机公司（后来的联合技术公司）的哈里·格雷（Harry Gray），他跳入了一场竞购战，使得这家电池制造商的股价远超最初的出价。在 Greenhill 的推动下，Inco 一记重拳终结了战斗，一天之内将最终报价从每股 38 美元提高到 41 美元，大获全胜。Inco 的市值瞬间暴涨，比竞购热潮开始前翻了一倍还多。

Greenhill would remember Inco nostalgically, whereas Frank Petito would prove more sober and ambivalent about the hostile raids legitimated by Morgan Stanley. “Many did not work out,” he later said. “But you’ve got to remember what the times were like. And it was always management that wanted to do them.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418094) This was Morgan Stanley gospel in the 1970s—that the firm was a passive instrument of its clients, even a somewhat unwilling party. Greenhill insisted, “Wall Street had not created the merger trend. . . . We get the transactions done.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418255) If this deterministic view spared the firm responsibility, it also betrayed some unspoken uneasiness. Greenhill could never quite mouth the words *hostile* or *unfriendly.* “We prefer not to call them unfriendly takeovers,” he told *Business Week* in 1974. “Just because the management doesn’t go along doesn’t mean that the deal isn’t in the best interest of the stockholders, and Morgan Stanley would never allow itself to get so involved in a deal that wasn’t.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418403)
格林希尔会怀念地记住英可，而弗兰克·佩蒂托对摩根士丹利认可的敌意收购则显得更为清醒和矛盾。“很多都没成功，”他后来说，“但你要记住当时的时代背景。而且，一直都是管理层想做这些事。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418094) 这是摩根士丹利在 1970 年代的信念——公司只是客户的被动工具，甚至有些身不由己。格林希尔坚持认为：“华尔街并没有创造并购趋势……我们只是促成交易。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418255) 这种决定论观点虽然让公司免于承担责任，却也流露出某种未言明的不安。格林希尔始终无法坦然说出“敌意”或“不友好”这样的字眼。“我们更倾向于不称它们为不友好收购，”他在 1974 年对《商业周刊》表示。 "管理层不配合，并不代表这笔交易不符合股东的最大利益。而且，摩根士丹利绝不会让自己深陷一笔不符合股东利益的交易之中。" [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418403)

Like Robert Young’s New York Central raid, Inco-ESB would show the shot-in-the-dark quality of hostile takeovers. When Greenhill swooped down on Fred Port, the company had just registered record earnings of over \$300 million for its fiscal year. For all its promises, Inco couldn’t improve on that. ESB slipped in its rivalry with Dura-cell batteries and the Delco-Sears maintenance-free car battery. By 1980, it was losing money, and its new chairman, Chuck Baird, put ESB up for sale, professing discomfort with its retail-oriented business. That Bob Greenhill was later wistful about Inco-ESB says much about the way in which investment banking was becoming disconnected from the economic realities of jobs, factories, and people. By what logic could the takeover be construed as a victory? Investment bankers were beginning to take a shorter-term view of their clients’ businesses.
如同罗伯特·杨突袭纽约中央铁路公司那般，Inco-ESB 收购案也彰显了恶意收购的赌运气成分。当格林希尔突袭弗雷德·波特时，该公司刚刚创下财年盈利超过 3 亿美元的纪录。但无论 Inco 如何承诺，都无法超越这一业绩。ESB 在与 Dura-cell 电池及 Delco-Sears 免维护汽车电池的竞争中逐渐落败。到 1980 年，公司开始亏损，新任董事长查克·贝尔德以不习惯零售导向型业务为由，将 ESB 挂牌出售。后来鲍勃·格林希尔对 Inco-ESB 收购案流露出惋惜之情，这充分说明投资银行业务与就业、工厂、百姓等经济现实之间日益脱节。按照何种逻辑，这次收购才能算作胜利？投资银行家们对客户业务的眼光正变得越来越短视。

Once Morgan Stanley sanctioned hostile takeovers, competitors jumped in. A year later, George Shinn of First Boston paired up Bruce Wasserstein and Joe Perella to launch a separate M&A operation. In 1974, \$100 million was still considered a big deal. By 1978, over eighty deals exceeded that amount, with a \$500- to \$600-million range already commonplace. Unlike the conglomerate takeovers of the 1960s, which were financed with the shares of the acquiring company, the new takeovers were largely effected with cash. Because Morgan Stanley booked fees as a percentage of the total, its profits soared.
一旦摩根士丹利批准了敌意收购，竞争对手便蜂拥而入。一年后，第一波士顿的乔治·欣恩联手布鲁斯·瓦瑟斯坦和乔·佩雷拉，单独成立了一家并购业务公司。1974 年，1 亿美元的交易仍被视为巨额。到了 1978 年，超过 80 笔交易突破了这一金额，5 亿到 6 亿美元的范围已司空见惯。与 20 世纪 60 年代用收购方股票融资的集团并购不同，新一轮收购大多以现金完成。由于摩根士丹利按交易总额比例收取费用，其利润迅猛增长。

Like the trading operation, takeover work helped to diversify the firm. Three female professionals were assigned to the M&A Department. Yet Greenhill and his male colleagues erected a wall around the women and segregated them as “statisticians.” Their wives also conspired to relegate the women to second-class status: when the unmarried Morgan Stanley women were about to travel to London and Cleveland on takeover business, two irate wives called, squawking about the arrangement. No Morgan Stanley woman would make managing director until the mid-1980s.
与交易业务类似，收购工作也帮助公司实现了多元化。三位女性专业人员被分配到了并购部门。然而，格林希尔和他男性同事们却对女性设置壁垒，将她们隔离起来，只当作“统计员”使用。他们的妻子也合谋将女性贬为二等地位：当未婚的摩根士丹利女性即将因收购工作前往伦敦和克利夫兰时，两位愤怒的妻子打来电话，对此安排大声抱怨。直到 20 世纪 80 年代中期，摩根士丹利才出现女性董事总经理。

The Wall Street move into merger work accelerated into a stampede after May 1, 1975, when the SEC abolished fixed commissions on stock trades. This stripped away an easy, dependable flow of revenue and forced securities houses to forage for new business. Morgan Stanley, which now executed block trades for institutions, lobbied hard against the measure. Borrowing a term from his navy days, Bob Baldwin warned that “Mayday” might prompt the failure of 150 to 200 regional firms—an alarming forecast that proved on the low side. Morgan Stanley regarded these regional firms as possible buffers against retail giants like Merrill Lynch. As head of a wholesale firm long reliant on regional brokers, Baldwin was reluctant to see the demise of his cherished world of syndicates.
1975 年 5 月 1 日，美国证券交易委员会（SEC）取消了股票交易的固定佣金制度，华尔街的并购业务由此迅速演变为一场混战。这一举措砍掉了券商一项稳定且可观的收入来源，迫使它们四处寻找新的业务机会。当时已为机构客户执行大宗交易的摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）极力游说反对这一改革。鲍勃·鲍德温（Bob Baldwin）借用海军服役时的术语警告说，"Mayday"（求救信号）可能导致 150 到 200 家地区性公司倒闭——这个令人震惊的预测后来证明还偏保守了。摩根士丹利将这些地区性公司视为抵御美林（Merrill Lynch）等零售巨头可能的缓冲。作为一家长期依赖地区券商的批发公司掌门人，鲍德温不愿看到自己珍视的承销团世界走向消亡。

When “Mayday” arrived, in 1975, Morgan Stanley tried to buck the tide and cling to old rates. Unsubstantiated rumors spread through Wall Street that the firm might blackball from its syndicates any firms that stooped to price cutting—a charge Baldwin labeled an “outrageous lie.” It was impossible, however, to hold back a sea of change, and commission rates plunged 40 percent for institutional investors. From these ashes arose the new, piratical Wall Street. Even conservative firms began adopting tactics once considered suitable only to disgruntled outsiders. In the Inco-ESB takeover, it was Morgan Stanley, the flagship of Wall Street, that first unfurled the Jolly Roger, and it would sail it through increasingly troubled seas.
1975 年"五一"佣金自由化来临之际，摩根士丹利试图逆流而上，固守旧有费率。华尔街流传着未经证实的传闻，称该公司可能将任何降价承销的机构逐出其辛迪加——鲍德温斥之为"无耻谎言"。然而，沧海桑田的巨变已无法阻挡，机构投资者的佣金率暴跌 40%。劫灰之中，新式海盗般的华尔街应运而生。就连保守的投行也开始采用曾被认为只适合心怀不满的局外人的策略。在美佳-ESB 收购案中，正是华尔街旗舰摩根士丹利率先升起骷髅旗，并将在日益汹涌的惊涛骇浪中继续航行。
