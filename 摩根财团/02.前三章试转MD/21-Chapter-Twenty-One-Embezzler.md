# Chapter Twenty-One: Embezzler

EMBITTERED by the New Deal, Jack Morgan didn’t age gracefully or happily and divided his time between apathy and rage. He was a lonely man who had never recuperated from his wife’s death. He didn’t remarry and continued to tend Jessie’s gardens. At his Gannochy Lodge shooting parties, he would invite the Queen Mother’s sister or some comparable dowager to serve as hostess. Whether attending Yale-Harvard regattas in his boater or browsing in the Morgan Library, he gave off a solitary air. This sense of loneliness was accentuated by the grandeur of his surroundings. At Matinicock Point, he lived alone in a forty-five room house. Although a widower for nearly ten years, he refused to shut his English or American estates or vary the annual ritual that called for Camp Uncas in the Adirondacks in the spring or Gannochy Lodge in August. At exorbitant expense, he maintained butlers, housekeepers, and gardeners, as well as the fifty-man crew of *Corsair IV.* This unchanging structure provided emotional solace and support but also frittered away much of the fortune that would have gone to his heirs.
被 New Deal 激怒的杰克·摩根，老得既不优雅也不快乐，他的时间在冷漠与愤怒之间摇摆不定。他是个孤独的人，从未从妻子的去世中恢复过来。他没有再婚，继续照料 Jessie 的花园。在他的 Gannochy Lodge 射击派对上，他会邀请 Queen Mother 的妹妹或某位类似的遗孀担任女主人。无论是戴着 boater 参加 Yale-Harvard 赛艇会，还是在 Morgan Library 浏览，他都散发着孤寂的气息。这种孤独感因周围宏伟的环境而愈发凸显。在 Matinicock Point，他独自住在一栋四十五个房间的房子里。尽管鳏居近十年，他拒绝关闭他在英国或美国的庄园，也不改变每年的惯例：春天去 Adirondacks 的 Camp Uncas，八月去 Gannochy Lodge。他以高昂的费用维持着男管家、管家、园丁以及 Corsair IV 号上五十名船员的生计。 这种一成不变的结构虽带来了情感上的慰藉与支持，却也挥霍掉了本应留给后代的大笔财富。

Jack took inordinate pride in his grandchildren, sixteen strong by 1935. When a four-year-old grandson asked why locomotive engineers blew whistles at grade crossings, Jack assigned high-priced Davis, Polk lawyers to find the answer. Yet he often seemed closed and aloof to his grandchildren. Once a week, he hosted a black-tie dinner for the entire family at Matinicock Point. Extremely punctual, he would stand at the door checking his watch and start exactly on time. Everybody lived in fear of being late. When he took five of his grandchildren across the Atlantic on the *Corsair*, he allowed them to read or play solitaire but not to enjoy deck games. If sensitive within, he seemed cold and distant without.
杰克对孙辈们引以为傲，到 1935 年，他已拥有 16 个孙子孙女。一个四岁的孙子问起火车司机为何要在平交道口鸣笛，杰克竟派了高薪聘请的 Davis, Polk 律所律师去寻找答案。然而，他面对孙辈时却常常显得疏离冷漠。每周一次，他会在马蒂尼科克角为全家举办一场正式晚宴，他极为守时，总是站在门口看表，一到时间便准时开席。每个人都生怕迟到。当他带着五个孙辈乘柯尔萨号横渡大西洋时，允许他们读书或玩纸牌，却不准他们在甲板上游戏玩乐。纵使内心敏感，外表看来却冷若冰霜。

Jack still reported regularly to the Corner, taking his spot at the far end of the double row of rolltop desks, beneath Pierpont’s portrait. He was an archaic figure in a world mad with reform. Change and experimentation were so alien to his nature that the crash and Depression produced no evolution in his philosophy. In 1936, he enunciated his business creed this way: “Do your work; be honest; keep your word; help when you can; be fair.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351237) Another favorite saying was “Keep your mouth shut and your eyes and ears open.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351400) His philosophy showed no scuff marks of the time, only a somber faith that with sufficient patience and fortitude traditional values would prevail.
杰克仍定期到“角落”报到，坐在那一排双层卷盖式书桌最远处、皮尔庞特画像的下方。在这个被改革狂潮席卷的世界里，他活像一个老古董。变革与尝试与他的本性格格不入，以至于经济大崩溃和大萧条都没能让他的理念产生丝毫演变。1936 年，他这样阐述自己的商业信条：“做好本分；诚实守信；言出必行；尽力助人；公平待人。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351237) 他还有一句口头禅是“少说话，多听多看。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351400) 他的理念里丝毫看不出时代的磨损痕迹，只有一种沉静的信念：只要拥有足够的耐心和坚毅，传统价值观终将胜出。

Jack didn’t travel in circles likely to challenge his views. He told U.S. Steel chairman Myron Taylor that he knew nobody in favor of the 1935 Wagner Act, which sanctioned collective bargaining; and he probably didn’t. Never attempting to broaden his outlook, he came to typify the New Deal stereotype of the “economic royalist.” In 1935, for the first time, he instituted personal economies. He trimmed living expenses to \$60,000 annually and halved his contributions to Saint John’s of Lattingtown, the millionaire’s church whose burial ground was so liberally graced with Morgan partners. Such economies, if arduous for Jack, still left him with a life style inconceivably majestic to ordinary citizens.
杰克从不涉足可能挑战他观点的圈子。他对美国钢铁公司董事长迈伦·泰勒说，他认识的人里没有一个支持 1935 年的《瓦格纳法案》——该法案承认了集体谈判权；而他大概也确实不认识。他从不试图拓宽自己的视野，最终成了新政时期典型的“经济保皇派”。1935 年，他头一回开始厉行节约：将年度生活开支压缩到 6 万美元，并把对拉廷敦圣约翰教堂的捐款减半——这座百万富翁教堂的墓地里，安息着众多摩根合伙人。这样的节俭，对杰克而言或许颇为艰难，但留给普通民众的，依然是不可思议的奢华生活方式。

The Nye “merchants of death” hearings in early 1936 confirmed Jack’s suspicion that he was the eternal target of demagogues and left him feeling depressed. During the hearings, his friend King George V died. He wrote a British friend: “the death of the King has caused a great feeling of sadness in this country as well as in yours.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351558) As if they were the unconquerable curses of his house, the combined strain and fatigue of Pecora and Nye had the same effect on Jack as the Pujo hearings had on Pierpont. In mid-June 1936, while visiting Jessie’s sister, Mrs. Stephen Crosby, in Massachusetts, he had his first heart attack, complicated by a severe neuritis attack, which made it difficult for him to walk.
1936 年初，奈伊主持的"死亡商人"听证会印证了杰克一直以来的猜测——自己永远是煽动家们瞄准的靶子，这让他郁郁寡欢。听证会期间，他的好友英王乔治五世逝世。他在给一位英国朋友的信中写道："国王的离世在贵我两国都引发了深切的哀悼。" [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351558) 仿佛是缠着这个家族无法摆脱的魔咒，佩科拉和奈伊听证会带来的双重压力与疲惫，对杰克的影响正如当年普约听证会对皮尔庞特的打击。1936 年 6 月中旬，他在马萨诸塞州拜访杰西的妹妹斯蒂芬·克罗斯比夫人时，首次心脏病发作，并伴有严重的神经炎，导致行走困难。

The Morgan family wanted to transport him back to Glen Cove with minimal publicity, and he was moved by stretcher to a private railroad car. His sons, Junius and Harry, waited for him at the Mill Neck Station on Long Island. They paced the platform anxiously, smoking pipes, their hats pulled low, trying to dissuade photographers from taking pictures. As the train pulled in, Jack, in blue silk robe and white scarf, saw the photographers and lowered his window shade, his old disgust for the press welling up. An ambulance hidden in the bushes moved toward the train, and four men lifted Jack in a chair to the ground. A photographer rushed to the ambulance window for a last shot of Jack inside and Harry went white with fury. A less inhibited Morgan guard smashed the photographer in the jaw.
摩根家族希望尽量低调地将他送回格伦科夫，于是用担架把他抬上一节私人车厢。他的儿子朱尼厄斯和哈里在长岛米尔尼克站等候。他们在月台上焦急地踱步，叼着烟斗，压低帽檐，试图阻止摄影师拍照。火车进站时，穿着蓝色丝绸睡袍、围着白色围巾的杰克看到摄影师，便拉下了车窗的遮阳帘，内心涌起对新闻界一贯的厌恶。一辆藏在灌木丛中的救护车驶向火车，四个人用椅子把杰克抬到地面。一名摄影师冲到救护车窗口，想拍下杰克在车内的最后一张照片，哈里气得脸色发白。一名不那么克制的摩根警卫一拳打在那名摄影师的下巴上。

That winter, Jack spent two weeks cruising the South Seas, convalescing with a heart specialist on board. By now, his views of the world were etched with a corrosive anger. In late 1936, King Edward VIII abdicated, and Jack saw nothing romantic or pitiable in his plight, merely a betrayal of trust. He told Lord Linlithgow: “What a pity that the little king had not the guts enough to do his job.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351721) The gutless action would prove highly advantageous for the House of Morgan. Only a year earlier, Jack had entertained the duke and duchess of York—now to be King George VI and Queen Elizabeth—at Gannochy for the Glorious Twelfth. They would continue to be guests at Gannochy and aboard the *Corsair.* In late April 1937, Jack sailed for Plymouth *en route* to the coronation, bearing a special invitation to sit in the royal family’s box. As the squire of Wall Hall, he invited two thousand guests, mostly local farmers, to celebrate the occasion at his estate. But he suffered a second heart attack and missed the Westminster Abbey coronation. He had to listen to the ceremonies over the radio.
那年冬天，杰克花了两个星期在南海巡游，船上还带着一位心脏病专家，边养身体边散心。此时，他对这个世界的看法早已被一种腐蚀性的愤怒所填满。1936 年底，爱德华八世退位，杰克在他的处境里看不到任何浪漫或可怜之处，只觉得这就是一种背叛信任。他对林利斯戈勋爵说：“真可惜，这小国王连干好自己份内事的胆量都没有。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351721) 这种懦弱之举，后来对摩根家族来说倒成了天大的好事。就在一年前，杰克还在甘诺奇庄园招待了约克公爵和公爵夫人——也就是后来的乔治六世国王和伊丽莎白王后——一起庆祝“光荣的第十二日”。此后，他们继续成为甘诺奇庄园和“海盗号”游艇上的座上宾。1937 年 4 月下旬，杰克启航前往普利茅斯，准备参加加冕典礼，他手持一封特殊邀请，得以坐在王室的专属包厢里。作为沃霍尔庄园的主人，他邀请了 2000 位宾客，大多是当地农民，到他的庄园共庆盛典。 但他遭受了第二次心脏病发作，错过了威斯敏斯特大教堂的加冕典礼。他只能通过收音机收听整个仪式。

When he returned to America aboard the *Queen Mary*, his physician advised him not to talk to reporters, lest his blood pressure rise again. (Trying to be more affable, Jack had taken to granting shipboard interviews.) As the ship docked in a thick Manhattan fog, reporters dashed all over the ship trying to find Jack. They finally tracked him down in a stuffy little room and got him talking on the subject that was his invariable downfall during the New Deal—taxes. He had already inflamed public opinion in 1935 by saying that “everybody who makes any money in the United States actually is working eight months of the year for the government.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351855) When he said this, a fifth of the work force was idle and many people relied on relief or public works programs for survival. Now Jack put his foot in his mouth once again. While he was in England, Roosevelt and Treasury Secretary Morgenthau had started a campaign against tax evasion by the rich to reverse declining federal revenues. Jack didn’t know how incendiary the topic had become. He told reporters: “Congress should know how to levy taxes, and if it doesn’t know how to collect them, then a man is a fool to pay the taxes. If stupid mistakes are made, it is up to Congress to rectify them and not for us taxpayers to do so.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352005)
当他乘坐玛丽皇后号返回美国时，医生建议他不要接受记者采访，以免血压再次升高。（为了显得更平易近人，杰克开始习惯在船上接受采访。）当船在浓密的曼哈顿大雾中靠岸时，记者们满船乱窜地寻找杰克的踪迹。他们最终在一间闷热的小房间里找到了他，并让他谈起了那个在新政时期屡屡让他栽跟头的话题——税收。早在 1935 年，他就因一句"在美国赚钱的人，实际上每年有八个月是在为政府打工"而激怒了公众舆论。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2351855) 他说这话时，全国五分之一的劳动力处于失业状态，许多人靠着救济或公共工程项目勉强度日。如今，杰克又一次祸从口出。他在英国期间，罗斯福和财政部长摩根索已经发起了一场针对富人逃税的运动，以扭转联邦财政收入下滑的局面。 杰克并不知道这个话题已经变得多么敏感。他对记者说：“国会应该知道如何征税，如果连税都收不好，那么一个人还乖乖交税就是傻瓜。如果出了愚蠢的错误，应该由国会去纠正，而不是我们这些纳税人来买单。”” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352005)

Once again, Jack was flabbergasted by the public outrage that ensued; he never ceased to be a political naif. Lamont had to explain to him patiently how inflammatory such remarks might sound in the current political atmosphere. Lamont said of Jack to Walter Lippmann’s wife, Faye, “You see, as a matter of fact, he is as simple as a child, and when he once gets started with newspaper men he talks with them just as carelessly as he would with his own partners.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352168). Even though Jack rushed to retract his statement, stressing that he had no sympathy for tax dodgers, the damage was already done. Two weeks later, the Treasury released the names of sixty-seven wealthy taxpayers who had used legal schemes to avoid taxes. Jack’s name didn’t appear on this list, but Lamont’s did.
杰克再次被随后的公众愤怒惊得目瞪口呆；他始终是个政治上的天真者。拉蒙特不得不耐心向他解释，在当前的政治氛围中，这种言论听上去多么具有煽动性。拉蒙特对沃尔特·李普曼的妻子费伊这样评价杰克：“你看，事实上，他单纯得像个孩子，一旦和记者聊起来，就会像对待自己的合伙人一样口无遮拦。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352168) 。尽管杰克匆忙撤回自己的声明，强调他绝不同情逃税者，但伤害已经造成。两周后，财政部公布了六十七位利用合法手段避税的富裕纳税人名单。杰克的名字并未出现在这份名单上，但拉蒙特的名字赫然在列。

For New Dealers, Jack Morgan symbolized the self-destructive complacency of America’s rich, those unable to adjust to changing times. Reading Jack’s shipboard comments, Felix Frankfurter seized on them as proof of the decadence of business leaders who couldn’t see that their real self-interest lay in New Deal reform. “What a temper of mind J.P. Morgan revealed in this morning’s press,” Frankfurter wrote to President Roosevelt. “I nearly exploded. . . . When the most esteemed of financiers discloses such a morally obtuse, anti-social attitude, one realizes anew that the real enemy of capital is not Communism but capitalists and their retinue of scribes and lawyers.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352351)
对当时的新政派来说，杰克·摩根象征着美国富人中那种自毁式的自满——那些无法适应时代变迁的人。读到杰克在船上的言论，费利克斯·法兰克福特立刻将其视为商业领袖腐朽堕落的证据：这些人根本看不清自己的真正利益其实在于新政改革。法兰克福特在给罗斯福总统的信中写道：“J.P.摩根今天早报上展现出的那种心态，简直让我气炸了……当最受敬重的金融家暴露出如此道德迟钝、反社会的态度时，人们才重新认识到：资本真正的敌人不是共产主义，而是资本家及其身边那帮文人和律师。”

Jack was far more affected by criticism than politicians realized. The public assumed that all tycoons were crusty, unemotional, and immune to public wrath. J. P. Morgan had become less a person than a political symbol for the rich and reactionary who opposed social justice. Yet Jack had been emotionally unhinged since Jessie’s death, and he remained terribly shy and unsure of himself. This tended to make him gruff, aloof, and elusive. Unsophisticated, he could be easily baited by clever reporters. A lonely widower in retirement, he poured out his grief to assorted duchesses, old college chums, and selected archbishops. He still found it hard to cope without Jessie’s emotional support.
杰克受批评影响的程度，远超政客们的想象。公众总以为所有大亨都冷漠无情、对舆论怒火无动于衷。J. P. 摩根已不仅仅是一个人，更成了反对社会正义的富人与反动派的政治象征。然而，自杰茜去世后，杰克始终情绪失衡，极度害羞且缺乏自信。这让他显得粗鲁、疏离、难以捉摸。他心思单纯，容易被狡猾的记者激怒。退休后，他成了孤独的鳏夫，向形形色色的贵妇、老同学及几位大主教倾诉哀伤。没有杰茜的情感支撑，他依然举步维艰。

Over time, Jack had come to see the Roosevelt administration as one giant conspiracy out to hound him. Gnashing his teeth, he told Monty Norman, “The state of affairs might be so satisfactory and helpful so easily if we did not have a crazy man in charge and my chief feeling is one of resentment at what he is putting us through.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352524) To Owen Young of General Electric, we owe a startling vignette that shows how dangerously frayed Jack’s nerves were in early 1938. The two men were chatting at 23 Wall when Jack erupted into a tirade. He lost all control of his emotions. Young was so thunderstruck that he recorded his impressions immediately afterward, together with strict instructions not to publish them until both were dead. Young recalled Jack saying:
随着时间推移，杰克逐渐将罗斯福政府视为一个步步紧逼的巨大阴谋。他咬牙切齿地对蒙蒂·诺曼说：“要是没有那个疯子在掌权，局面本可以如此令人满意且顺风顺水，而我最主要的感受就是对他让我们经历的这一切感到愤恨。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352524) 通用电气的欧文·杨为我们保留了一段令人震惊的片段，展现了 1938 年初杰克的神经紧张到了何等危险的地步。两人当时正在华尔街 23 号交谈，杰克突然爆发出一阵长篇怒斥，完全失去了情绪控制。杨震惊不已，随后立即记录下自己的感受，并严格指示要等两人都去世后才能公开这些内容。杨回忆道，杰克当时说：

> “I just want you to know, Owen Young, that I don’t care a damn what happens to you or anybody else. I don’t care what happens to the country. All I care about”—and he became vehement, almost passionate—“all I care about is this business! If I could help it by going out of this country and establishing myself somewhere else I’d do it—I’d do anything. In all honesty I want you to know exactly how I feel. And if things go on this way much longer I won’t put up with ’em. I’ll take the business and get out.” His hand trembling—under great emotional strain.
> “我只想让你知道，Owen Young，我压根儿不在乎你或任何其他人会怎样。我也不在乎这个国家会怎样。我在乎的”——他变得激动起来，几乎有些按捺不住——“我在乎的只有这桩生意！如果离开这个国家、到别处去能帮上忙，我早就走了——我什么都做得出来。跟你实话实说，我想让你清楚我的真实感受。再这么下去，我可忍不了。我会带着生意一走了之。”他情绪极度紧绷，手在发抖。

Attempting to calm him, Young put his arm around Jack and reminded him gently of Pierpont’s faith in America, the talents the Morgans had contributed to their bank. Then he tried to rouse his spirits: “You’ll stay right here and outface these passing discouragements, because if you ran away you wouldn’t be Jack Morgan. You owe it to the future and you owe it to yourself.’ When I was finished,” Young wrote, “he was silent, and I was startled to find that his eyes had filled. ’Well, Owen,’ he said, ’I guess I needed some one to talk to me like that. And I guess you’re the only one who could have done it.’ ”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352687)
杨试图安抚他，于是搂住杰克，温和地提醒他皮尔庞特对美国的信念，以及摩根家族为银行贡献的卓越才能。接着，他试图提振杰克的精神：“你要留在这里，直面这些暂时的失意，因为如果你临阵脱逃，那你就不是杰克·摩根了。这是你对未来的责任，也是你对自己的责任。” 杨写道：“我说完后，他沉默了片刻，我惊讶地发现他眼眶湿润了。然后他说：‘好吧，欧文，我想我需要有人这样对我说。而我想，你是唯一能这么做的人。’”

Jack never found peace under Franklin Roosevelt, at least not until the Second World War dissolved the feuds of the 1930s in a warm bath of patriotic fervor. Only when the focus of national attention switched from the Depression and domestic economic inequities to foreign menace did the Morgan bank and the New Deal again find any common ground.
杰克在富兰克林·罗斯福任内从未获得过安宁，至少直到第二次世界大战以爱国热情的暖流化解了上世纪 30 年代的种种恩怨，他才得以喘息。只有当国家关注的焦点从大萧条和国内经济不公转向外部威胁时，摩根银行与新政才再次找到了共同立场。

EVEN as the House of Morgan fended off assaults from Franklin Roosevelt, it experienced the ire of his successor, Senator Harry S. Truman of Missouri. In his first Senate term, Truman later said, he spent more time on railroad finance than any other single subject. This led to a collision with the House of Morgan, which, with Kuhn, Loeb, still dominated railroad issues in the 1930s. Struggling to compete with new truck and air traffic, the railroads were an intractable Depression problem, with bankers blamed for their mismanagement. In 1935, Truman joined a subcommittee chaired by Burton K. Wheeler, a progressive Montana Democrat, investigating banker influence over railroads. The Wheeler hearings studied the manacles that bound railroads to exclusive relations with traditional bankers. From the time of Louis Brandeis’s campaign against Morgan domination of the New Haven Railroad, reformers had urged an arm’s-length distance between bankers and clients. Now they again espoused competitive bidding to allow all bankers to compete for a given issue.
就在摩根财团奋力抵御富兰克林·罗斯福的猛烈攻势时，又迎来了来自密苏里州参议员哈里·S·杜鲁门的怒火。杜鲁门后来回忆说，在他的首个参议员任期内，花在铁路金融问题上的时间比任何其他议题都多。这导致他与摩根财团正面交锋——当时摩根财团与库恩-洛布公司仍主宰着 20 世纪 30 年代的铁路事务。在与新兴的卡车运输和航空业的竞争中举步维艰的铁路行业，成了大萧条时期一道棘手的难题，而银行家们则被指责为管理不善的罪魁祸首。1935 年，杜鲁门加入了一个由蒙大拿州进步派民主党人伯顿·K·惠勒主持的小组委员会，调查银行家对铁路行业的影响。惠勒听证会深入研究了束缚铁路公司与传统银行家建立独家关系的枷锁。自路易斯·布兰代斯发起反对摩根财团控制纽黑文铁路的运动以来，改革者们一直呼吁银行家与客户之间保持一定距离。 如今，他们再次主张采用竞争性招标，让所有银行家都能为某一特定发行项目展开竞争。

By a curious historical freak, Max Lowenthal, counsel to the Wheeler subcommittee, introduced Truman to that ubiquitous Morgan demon, Louis Brandeis, who was now a Supreme Court justice. In the late 1930s, justices still received visitors for tea one afternoon a week. At teas held at his California Street home, Brandeis would leave other visitors and buttonhole Truman for hours, quizzing him on the hearings and arguing for stricter regulation of railroads and a severing of their Wall Street links. Truman was converted to Brandeis’s gospel of a competitive economy based on small business and zealous antitrust regulation. This philosophy exerted a powerful hold during Roosevelt’s second term and naturally exacerbated the clash with that apostle of big-business planning and economic concentration, the House of Morgan.
在一种奇特的历史偶合中，惠勒小组委员会的法律顾问马克斯·洛温塔尔，将杜鲁门引荐给了那位无处不在的摩根恶魔——路易斯·布兰代斯，当时布兰代斯已是最高法院大法官。20 世纪 30 年代末，大法官们仍会在每周某个下午接待来访者喝茶。在布兰代斯位于加利福尼亚街的家中举办的茶会上，他会撇开其他访客，把杜鲁门拉去单独谈话，一谈就是几个小时，向他盘问听证会的细节，并主张对铁路实施更严格的监管，切断它们与华尔街的联系。杜鲁门最终皈依了布兰代斯的信条——建立以小企业和积极反垄断监管为基础的竞争性经济。这一理念在罗斯福第二任期内影响深远，自然也就加剧了与摩根财团——那个大企业规划与经济集权倡导者——之间的冲突。

Anticipating the onslaught of Senator Wheeler and his committee, the Morgan partners in 1935 acted to jettison that great embarrassment from the Jazz Age—the bankrupt Van Sweringen brothers. For five years, Morgans had secretly propped them up with a \$40-million “rescue” loan, even though they owed \$8 million in back interest charges. When the brothers again defaulted in May 1935, the bank decided it would be political suicide to take control of their collateral—the vast Alleghany railroad and their real estate empire. Political expediency demanded they cut their losses and sell off Alleghany stock. This need to propitiate Washington was a striking sign of diminished Morgan power and prestige. The bank placed a small newspaper ad announcing plans to sell off the collateral at an auction. It was a shabby anticlimax to the bank’s once-glamorous relationship with the Van Sweringens.
1935 年，摩根合伙人预见到惠勒参议员及其委员会即将发难，果断采取行动，甩掉了爵士时代那个令他们如鲠在喉的包袱——已然破产的范斯韦林根兄弟。五年来，摩根一直用一笔四千万美元的"救援"贷款暗中扶持他们，尽管这对兄弟还欠着八百万美元的逾期利息。当兄弟俩于 1935 年 5 月再次违约时，银行意识到，接管他们的抵押资产——庞大的阿勒格尼铁路及地产帝国——无异于政治自杀。为了政治上的权宜之计，他们只能及时止损，抛售阿勒格尼股票。这种向华盛顿俯首帖耳的姿态，昭示着摩根权势与声望的江河日下。银行在报纸上刊登了一则小广告，宣布计划拍卖这些抵押品。与昔日摩根同范斯韦林根兄弟风光无限的合作关系相比，这结局可谓寒酸落寞。

On September 30, 1935, the remnants of the Van Sweringen empire went under the gavel at the securities auction room of Adrian H. Muller and Sons. Mullers was known as the securities graveyard, and its offices had an appropriate view of the cemetery of Saint Paul’s churchyard. Beneath bare electric bulbs, in a drab room strewn with dusty paintings and worthless junk, George Whitney sat with legs crossed on a cheap folding chair. Smart and well tailored, he smiled blandly and tried to look blase at this moment of Morgan disgrace. The House of Morgan’s handsome, blond attorney, Frederick A.O. Schwarz of Davis, Polk, and Wardwell, brought along the Alleghany securities in two rich-looking leather portfolios. It was a packed house. In the rear, like a resurrected ghost of the 1929 crash, a tense, pale Oris Van Sweringen flitted about. With twenty-eight thousand miles of track, or a tenth of the entire American railroad system, Alleghany fetched only \$3 million, exposing a loss of \$9 million apiece for both Morgans and Guaranty Trust. And it turned out that the indestructible Van Sweringens had repurchased the railroads by creating one last holding company and getting two associates to advance the cash.
1935 年 9 月 30 日，范斯韦林根帝国的残余资产在 Adrian H. Muller and Sons 的证券拍卖厅被一锤定音。穆勒公司素有“证券墓地”之称，其办公室窗外恰好望见圣保罗教堂墓园。在昏暗的灯泡下，一间沉闷的房间里散落着积灰的画作与无用的杂物，George Whitney 翘着腿坐在一把廉价折叠椅上。他衣着考究、剪裁得体，脸上挂着平淡的微笑，努力在摩根家族蒙羞的时刻摆出一副漫不经心的姿态。摩根财团那位金发英俊的律师——来自 Davis, Polk, and Wardwell 律所的 Frederick A.O. Schwarz，随身带来了两只看起来颇为考究的皮革公文包，里面装着 Alleghany 证券。拍卖厅内座无虚席。后排，一个面色苍白、神情紧张的 Oris Van Sweringen 来回踱步，仿佛 1929 年崩盘后复活的幽灵。 两万八千英里的铁路，占美国整个铁路系统的十分之一，而 Alleghany 公司仅以 300 万美元售出，这意味着摩根财团和 Guaranty Trust 各自损失了 900 万美元。更令人意外的是，看似无法摧毁的 Van Sweringens 家族竟通过最后一层控股公司，并让两位合伙人垫付资金，重新购回了这些铁路。

Afterward, George Whitney—with a tight-lipped smile—shook hands with a happy, flushed Oris Van Sweringen. “I would rather have paid the bill,” Oris whispered to Whitney. The funereal auction room provided a fitting end to the fiasco. But following the strange copycat pattern of their lives, the Van Sweringens died in quick succession. Mantis died that December. Eleven months later, Oris arrived in Hoboken for a meeting at Morgans and died of a coronary thrombosis while still in his private sleeping car. He left an estate consisting of hardly much more than Mantis’s life insurance. The Van Sweringen railroads, meanwhile, remained heavily in hock to the banks.
随后，乔治·惠特尼——嘴角挂着一丝紧绷的微笑——与面色红润、神情激动的奥里斯·范斯韦林根握了手。“我宁愿是自己付了这笔账。”奥里斯低声对惠特尼说。这间葬礼般的拍卖室，恰如其分地为这场惨败画上了句号。但按照他们人生中那诡异的模仿模式，范斯韦林根兄弟相继离世。曼蒂斯在那年十二月去世。十一个月后，奥里斯前往霍博肯参加摩根的一次会议，在私人卧铺车厢内因冠状动脉血栓去世。他留下的遗产，几乎只有曼蒂斯的人寿保险金。而范斯韦林根的铁路帝国，依然深陷银行债务的泥潭。

The auction didn’t pacify the Wheeler investigators. Even Glass-Stea-gall and the creation of Morgan Stanley hadn’t modified Senator Wheeler’s belief that J. P. Morgan and Company controlled the railroad securities business. He asked one witness, “But generally in the Street is it not conceded that Morgan Stanley & Co. is the same thing, or is just as much dominated by Morgan, as before?”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352875)
这次拍卖并没有平息 Wheeler 调查员的质疑。即便有了 Glass-Stea-gall 法案，摩根士丹利也成立了，但 Wheeler 参议员依然坚信 J. P. Morgan and Company 掌控着铁路证券业务。他向一位证人问道："但华尔街普遍不都承认，摩根士丹利跟以前没什么两样，依然受摩根牢牢控制吗？" [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2352875)

For six months in 1936, Wheeler investigators pored over records at 23 Wall Street: once sacred, confidential documents were becoming increasingly smudged with the fingerprints of government investigators. Committee counsel Max Lowenthal became the new Morgan bogeyman, and George Whitney complained to Jack about the “Jewish lawyer element” behind the investigation.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353142) Whitney thought the Van Sweringens the real object of their investigation, with the bank serving as their proxy after they died. In 1937, Senator Wheeler, distracted by the battle over Supreme Court reform, appointed Truman as acting chairman of the railroad investigation. At this point, the committee turned to the Van Sweringen’s 1930 purchase of Missouri Pacific, bought with proceeds from the Alleghany underwriting of 1929, made notorious by the preferred list.
1936 年，惠勒调查员们在华尔街 23 号花了六个月时间仔细核查记录：那些曾经神圣不可侵犯的机密文件，如今却越来越多地沾上了政府调查人员的指纹。委员会法律顾问马克斯·洛温塔尔成了摩根财团的新噩梦，乔治·惠特尼向杰克抱怨调查背后有“犹太律师势力”在作祟。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353142) 惠特尼认为范斯韦林根兄弟才是他们真正的调查对象，而银行不过是他们死后被当作替罪羊罢了。1937 年，参议员惠勒因最高法院改革之争分心，任命杜鲁门为铁路调查的代理主席。此时，委员会转而调查范斯韦林根兄弟 1930 年收购密苏里太平洋铁路一案，这笔收购资金来自 1929 年阿勒格尼公司的承销收益，而那份优先股名单早已让此事臭名昭著。

A future president was now educated in the Wall Street plunder of the 1920s. As Margaret Truman recalled, “It was my father’s investigation of the Missouri Pacific that really enraged him and convinced him for all time that ’the wrecking crew,’ as he called Wall Street financiers, were a special interest group constantly ready to sacrifice the welfare of millions for the profits of a few.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353341) Under Alleghany—and ultimately Morgan control—the Missouri Pacific had become an open scandal. The railroad was milked for dividends while management fired thousands of workers, abandoned improvements, and made no provision for an emergency fund. There were also malodorous political dealings with Missouri legislators, one state senator having received \$1,000, which he itemized as “covering services in the Alleghany-Missouri Pacific matter.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353509)
一位未来的总统，如今在 1920 年代华尔街的掠夺行径中接受教育。正如玛格丽特·杜鲁门所回忆的那样：“正是我父亲对密苏里太平洋铁路公司的调查，真正激怒了他，并让他从此深信，‘破坏团伙’——他这样称呼华尔街的金融家们——是一个特殊利益集团，随时准备为了少数人的利益而牺牲数百万人的福祉。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353341) 在 Alleghany 公司——最终是摩根财团——的控制下，密苏里太平洋铁路公司已沦为公开的丑闻。铁路公司被榨取股息，管理层却解雇了数千名工人，放弃设备升级，也不设立应急基金。此外，还与密苏里州的议员们存在龌龊的政治交易，一名州参议员曾收受 1000 美元，并将其列为“涵盖在 Alleghany-密苏里太平洋事务中的服务费”。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353509)

The feisty Truman dug in his heels against tremendous Wall Street pressure to desist from the investigation. He blamed the House of Morgan for his troubles. As he wrote his wife, Bess, “It is a mess and has created a terrible furor in New York. Guaranty Trust and J. P. Morgan have used every means available to make me quit. I’m going to finish the job or die in the attempt.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353635) Truman saw himself as the upright country boy who wouldn’t be hoodwinked by smart-alecky New York types, and he had a cultural as well as a political aversion to the bank. Even as a young man, he had considered Pierpont a snob who consorted with decadent European royalty, and he was quick to pick up on George Whitney’s air of superiority, his disdain for little midwestern senators. “Mr. Whitney is very much inclined to feel his position,” he told Bess. “He came to my office at about a quarter to ten and told me what he was going to do. I simply asked him who the chairman of the committee happened to be and he immediately dismounted and went along like a gentleman.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353791) Truman’s experience left him with an enduring view of Wall Street bankers as smart, greedy, and oblivious to the hazards of concentrated wealth. The ordinary government bureaucrat, he declared, was no more a match for Wall Street lawyers than a lamb for a butcher.
性情刚烈的杜鲁门顶住了华尔街的巨大压力，坚决不放弃调查。他把自己的麻烦归咎于摩根财团。他在给妻子贝丝的信中写道：“这事一团糟，已在纽约掀起轩然大波。担保信托公司和 J.P.摩根用尽一切手段想让我退缩。可我非要把这件事干到底，哪怕搭上这条命也在所不惜。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353635) 杜鲁门自视为正直的乡下孩子，绝不会被纽约那帮自作聪明的家伙耍得团团转，他对这家银行既有文化上的反感，也有政治上的厌恶。早在年轻时，他就认为皮尔庞特是个趋炎附势的势利眼，整天跟堕落腐化的欧洲王室厮混；而乔治·惠特尼那副高高在上的姿态、对中西部小参议员们的鄙夷不屑，他更是看得一清二楚。“惠特尼先生显然对自己的地位非常得意，”他在信中告诉贝丝，“他大约九点三刻来到我办公室，然后就指手画脚地告诉我他要干什么。” 我只是随口问了他一句委员会主席是谁，他立刻翻身下马，像个绅士一样配合起来。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353791) 杜鲁门的这次经历让他从此对华尔街银行家们形成了根深蒂固的看法：聪明、贪婪，对财富集中带来的危险浑然不觉。他曾断言，普通的政府官僚根本不是华尔街律师的对手，就像羊羔斗不过屠夫一样。

The Wheeler hearings spawned a Morgan enemy who would plague both J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Stanley for twenty years. Robert Young was a self-styled Texas populist who had worked for General Motors in New York and made a fortune selling short in the 1929 crash. He left to form his own investment firm, buying for himself, GM president Alfred P. Sloan, Jr., and other auto company executives. After buying a major block of Alleghany stock in the early 1930s, he and his clients were rebuffed by Morgans and Guaranty Trust in securing a board seat. Young would never forget this insult.
惠勒听证会催生了一个摩根家族的敌人，此人此后二十年里一直给 J.P.摩根公司和摩根士丹利找麻烦。罗伯特·杨是个自封的得克萨斯民粹主义者，曾在纽约的通用汽车工作，并在 1929 年大崩盘中通过做空大发横财。随后他离职创办了自己的投资公司，为自己、通用汽车总裁小阿尔弗雷德·P·斯隆以及其他汽车公司高管打理投资。1930 年代初，他收购了阿勒格尼公司的一大笔股份，之后在与摩根家族和担保信托公司争夺董事会席位时遭到冷遇。杨永远忘不了这份羞辱。

After the Van Sweringens died, Young and his associate, Allen Kirby, an heir to the Woolworth fortune, bought control of the bankrupt Alleghany empire, still heavily mortgaged to J. P. Morgan and Company and Guaranty Trust. But rather than being a pliant client, Young decided to use Alleghany as a springboard for an assault on the House of Morgan itself. While other businessmen bucked the New Deal, Young cleverly mouthed its slogans and cast himself as a plucky outsider, proclaiming his mission as “saving capitalism from the capitalists.” He said he wanted to diffuse the power of Morgans and its associates. Lamont was outraged by Young’s testimony before the Truman subcommittee and called him on the carpet at 23 Wall. It was a dressing down that stung for the rest of Young’s life. When he told Lamont that he intended to keep him informed about his Alleghany rehabilitation plans, Lamont replied, “You don’t understand me. I want not only to be informed, but I want to help guide you in your policies.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353917)
范斯韦林根兄弟去世后，杨和他的合伙人、伍尔沃斯财富继承人艾伦·柯比收购了破产的阿尔勒格尼帝国，但该公司仍以高额抵押形式负债于摩根大通公司和担保信托公司。然而杨并未甘当顺从的客户，而是决定将阿尔勒格尼作为跳板，向摩根家族本身发起挑战。当其他商界人士抵制新政时，杨却精明地附和其口号，将自己塑造成一位大胆的局外人，宣称自己的使命是"从资本家手中拯救资本主义"。他表示要分散摩根及其合伙人的权力。杨在杜鲁门小组委员会前的证词令拉蒙特大为震怒，后者在华尔街 23 号对他进行了严厉训斥。这次斥责令杨终生铭记。当杨告诉拉蒙特，他打算随时向其通报阿尔勒格尼的重整计划时，拉蒙特回应道："你不了解我。" 我不仅想了解情况，更希望能为你们的政策制定提供指引。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2353917)

For Young, all was revealed in a flash of light. He often repeated the story, the way sinners retell their moments of conversion. Lamont had made him feel “just like a country boy” and had “literally put me on the carpet, spanked me and raked me over the coals for having the temerity to be developing a . . . plan without discussing it with Morgan’s.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354088)
对于 Young 来说，一切都在一道闪光中豁然开朗。他常常反复讲述这个故事，就像罪人回顾自己幡然悔悟的那一刻。Lamont 让他感觉自己“活像个乡下小子”，而且“毫不客气地把我叫去劈头盖脸训了一顿，狠狠数落了我一通，就因为我胆敢在没跟 Morgan's 商量之前就擅自制定出一个计划。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354088)

Inflamed by Lamont’s high-handed manner and emboldened by the Wheeler hearings, Young led a revolt against Morgan hegemony in railroad finance. His main target was the exclusive relations that gentleman bankers demanded from clients. The House of Morgan had managed issues for the C&.O railroad, which was part of the Alleghany empire. Young and his banking associates, Harold Stuart of Halsey, Stuart in Chicago and Cyrus Eaton of Otis and Company in Cleveland, laid a trap for the Morgan interests in November 1938. Young traveled out to Cleveland in a private railroad car with Harold Stanley of Morgan Stanley and Elisha Walker of Kuhn, Loeb for a meeting of the C&O finance committee. The New York bankers expected to negotiate a new \$30-million bond issue in private.
受到拉蒙特专横作风的激怒，并在惠勒听证会的鼓舞下，杨带头反抗摩根在铁路金融领域的霸权。他的主要目标是那些绅士银行家要求客户保持的独家关系。摩根财团曾为切萨皮克与俄亥俄铁路（C&O 铁路）处理过债券发行，这条铁路属于阿勒格尼帝国的一部分。1938 年 11 月，杨与他的银行合伙人——芝加哥哈尔西·斯图亚特公司的哈罗德·斯图亚特，以及克利夫兰奥蒂斯公司的赛勒斯·伊顿——为摩根利益集团设下了一个圈套。杨乘坐私人火车车厢前往克利夫兰，同行的有摩根士丹利的哈罗德·斯坦利和库恩·洛布公司的伊莱沙·沃克，参加 C&O 铁路财务委员会的一次会议。纽约的银行家们原本期望私下谈判一笔新的 3000 万美元债券发行。

Stanley and Walker must have known something was afoot, for they had been asked to submit sealed bids for the issue. It was unprecedented for a Morgan Stanley partner to travel to a board meeting in this way. In what he doubtless thought a great concession, Stanley told the meeting that he would allow Kuhn, Loeb’s name to appear alongside Morgan Stanley’s as co-manager. At this point, Young delivered his bombshell: “Mr. Stanley, we are not interested in the advertising, or whose name appears above whose. . . . What we are interested in is what C&O is to get for the bonds.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354222) Young suddenly disclosed that he had brought a competitive bid from Otis and Halsey, Stuart that would net the C&O \$3.5 million more than the terms proposed by Morgans and Kuhn, Loeb. Some old Van Sweringen loyalists on the board still wanted to accept the traditional Wall Street bankers. Young threw them into confusion by threatening to sue them if they rejected the lower bid. He pranced about the room singing, “Morgan will not get this business! Morgan will not get this business! ”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354370) The flustered directors recessed, conferred with lawyers, then came back and accepted the lower bid.
斯坦利和沃克一定隐约察觉到了什么，因为他们被要求就这次发行提交密封报价。摩根士丹利的合伙人以这种方式参加董事会会议，可谓史无前例。斯坦利大概自以为做出了极大让步，他在会上表示，允许库恩–洛布公司与摩根士丹利并列担任联席主承销商。就在这时，杨抛出了他的重磅炸弹：“斯坦利先生，我们对广告宣传，或者谁的名字排在谁上面不感兴趣……我们关心的是，切萨皮克与俄亥俄铁路公司能从这些债券中得到什么。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354222) 杨突然透露，他从奥蒂斯公司和哈西–斯图尔特公司带来了一份竞争性报价，按照这份报价，切萨皮克与俄亥俄铁路公司能比摩根和库恩–洛布提出的条件多获得 350 万美元。董事会中一些范斯韦林根家族的老忠诚派仍想接受华尔街传统银行家的方案。杨则威胁说，如果他们拒绝更低的报价，就将起诉他们，把他们搞得阵脚大乱。 他在房间里蹦蹦跳跳地唱着：“摩根别想拿到这笔生意！摩根别想拿到这笔生意！” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354370) 慌乱的董事们宣布休会，与律师们商议了一番，回来后接受了更低的报价。

Young’s palace coup inaugurated a brand-new era on Wall Street. Instead of having gentleman bankers privately negotiate issues with clients, more issues would be opened up to competitive bids. This typically meant smaller “spreads” between the price paid to the company and the price at which the issues were resold to the public. With smaller profit margins for the investment bankers, more money, in theory, would remain for the issuer.
杨格发动的宫廷政变，开启了华尔街的全新时代。原本由绅士银行家私下与客户协商发行的模式，被打破，更多的发行项目开始采用竞标方式。这通常意味着，公司实际收到的价格与向公众转售价格之间的“利差”变小了。投资银行的利润空间被压缩，理论上，发行方能留下的钱就更多了。

During the next two years, the *troika* of Young, Eaton, and Stuart got two other railroads to accept competitive bids. In 1941, the SEC promulgated Rule U-50, mandating competitive bidding for public utility holding company issues. In 1944, the Interstate Commerce Commission enacted a similar ruling for railroads. However notable these victories for anti-Wall Street forces, they didn’t touch the far more lucrative industrial issues outside of railroads and utilities. The major proponents of old-fashioned banking would be Harold Stanley and his firm. Stanley would argue against the “casual intermittent connections” between bankers and issuers produced by competitive bidding, warning that companies would receive poor advice and sell issues at improper prices. If the argument were transparently self-serving, industrial America would willingly submit to its logic. For another forty years, blue-chip America would agree to exclusive relations with Morgan Stanley, an alliance unbroken until IBM rebelled in 1979.
接下来的两年里，Young、Eaton 和 Stuart 这三驾马车成功让另外两家铁路公司接受了竞争性投标。1941 年，SEC 颁布了 U-50 号规则，强制要求公用事业控股公司发行证券时采用竞争性投标。1944 年，州际商务委员会也对铁路行业实施了类似规定。尽管这些反华尔街势力的胜利意义重大，但它们并未触及铁路和公用事业之外利润更为丰厚的工业发行领域。传统银行业的主要支持者是 Harold Stanley 及其公司。Stanley 反对竞争性投标导致的银行家与发行人之间“随意且断断续续的联系”，警告称企业将因此得到糟糕的建议，并以不当价格发行证券。如果说这种论点明显是出于自身利益，那么美国工业界却心甘情愿地接受了它的逻辑。 在此后的四十年里，美国蓝筹公司一直与摩根士丹利保持着独家合作关系，这一联盟直到 1979 年 IBM 打破局面才宣告终结。

CLEARLY, if there were going to be a rapprochement between the House of Morgan and the New Deal, it wouldn’t come from Jack Morgan, whose implacable bitterness made him politically valueless. It also wouldn’t come from George Whitney, the very model of the patrician banker that the reformers abhorred. Any new approach to the White House would have to involve Tom Lamont, who yearned to return to the political game and chafed under his Washington exile.
显然，如果摩根财团与新政之间要达成和解，这一角色不会落在杰克·摩根身上——他根深蒂固的怨恨使他在政治上毫无价值。也不会是乔治·惠特尼，他正是改革派深恶痛绝的贵族银行家的典型代表。任何与白宫重新接触的努力，都必须仰仗汤姆·拉蒙特——他渴望重返政治舞台，并为被逐出华盛顿而深感焦躁。

The turbulent year of 1937 presented a possible opening for the bank. After drifting from spring to late summer, the economy and the stock market nose-dived in September. So steep was the fall in stock and commodity markets that October 19 was dubbed Black Tuesday. Markets slumped almost halfway to their 1932 lows. Investment banks took such a severe beating on two issues—Bethlehem Steel bonds and Pure Oil preferred stock—that there was talk of closing the Stock Exchange. Assuming the Morgan role of Wall Street leadership, Harold Stanley called in the heads of several investment banks and took an informal survey of their condition. In return, he offered them a rare, confidential look at Morgan Stanley’s books. Glass-Steagall had left an investment banking field of small, poorly capitalized banks, and the inevitable shakeout now began. Suffering heavy underwriting losses, the firm of Edward B. Smith and Company—the successor to Guaranty Trust’s securities affiliate—merged with Charles D. Barney and Company to form Smith, Barney, a firm that fell into the Morgan group. The confidence of the New Deal was shaken by this sudden reversion to the unsettled financial markets of the early 1930s.
1937 年动荡不安，对银行来说似乎出现了一个可能的契机。从春末到夏末，经济与股市一路徘徊，到了九月骤然暴跌。股票和商品市场的跌势如此迅猛，以致 10 月 19 日被称为“黑色星期二”。市场几近腰斩，跌回了 1932 年低点的一半左右。两家投行——Bethlehem Steel 债券和 Pure Oil 优先股——遭受重创，甚至有人提议关闭交易所。Harold Stanley 扮演起摩根式华尔街领袖的角色，召集了数家投资银行的负责人，非正式地摸底他们的状况。作为回报，他让他们罕见地、机要地翻阅了摩根士丹利的账簿。格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案留下的投行领域资本薄弱、规模小，不可避免的洗牌由此开始。爱德华·B 的公司因承销损失惨重，陷入困境。 Smith and Company——Guaranty Trust 证券分支的继承者——与 Charles D. Barney and Company 合并，成立了 Smith, Barney，这家公司后来归入摩根集团。新政的信心因这次突然倒退至 20 世纪 30 年代初动荡的金融市场而动摇。

The industrial sector was also in turmoil. In January and February of 1937, the fledgling United Auto Workers paralyzed General Motors with sit-down strikes. In Flint, Michigan, police fired on strikers armed only with slingshots. From 14 percent in 1937, unemployment would zoom to 19 percent the following year. These events not only created a sense that the New Deal had stalled, but they intensified conflicts between the two chief administration factions. One group—inspired by Louis Brandeis and identified with Felix Frankfurter, Thomas G. Corcoran, and Benjamin V. Cohen—blamed big business for America’s failure to shake off the Depression and advocated more competitive markets. Their ally, Robert H. Jackson, chief of the Justice Department’s antitrust division, argued that monopolists had “priced themselves out of the market, and priced themselves into a slump.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354538) Echoing this theme, Interior Secretary Harold Ickes warned of the pernicious influence of America’s sixty ruling families. Roosevelt was fond of experimentation, and his political church had many pews. For the moment, he favored the antitrust faction and told brain truster Rexford G. Tugwell that it might “scare these people \[i.e., business\] into doing something.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354713)
工业领域同样动荡不安。1937 年 1 至 2 月间，羽翼未丰的联合汽车工人工会通过静坐罢工令通用汽车陷入瘫痪。在密歇根州弗林特市，警方对手持弹弓的罢工工人开火。失业率从 1937 年的 14%飙升至次年的 19%。这些事件不仅让人感觉新政步履维艰，更激化了政府内部两大派系间的矛盾。以路易斯·布兰代斯为精神领袖、费利克斯·弗兰克福特、托马斯·G·科科伦和本杰明·V·科恩为代表的一派，将美国未能摆脱大萧条归咎于大企业，主张建立更具竞争性的市场。他们的盟友、司法部反垄断部门负责人罗伯特·H·杰克逊指出，垄断者"定价过高自绝于市场，最终作茧自缚深陷萧条"。" [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354538) 秉承这一主题，内政部长哈罗德·伊克斯（Harold Ickes）警告称，美国六十个统治家族的影响有害无益。罗斯福热衷于实验，他的政治教堂里设有多样席位。彼时，他偏向反垄断派，并对智囊团成员雷克斯福德·G·特格韦尔（Rexford G. Tugwell）表示，此举或许能“吓唬那些（商界）人，让他们有所行动。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354713)

There was another wing of brain trusters who had been influential during the so-called First New Deal, from 1933 to 1935. They admired the technological efficiency of big business and regarded the Brandeis view of a small-scale, competitive economy as a fanciful wish for a bygone America. They accepted the inevitability of economic concentration and advocated public control of the large economic units rather than vainly trying to break them up. They denounced the Jackson-Ickes speeches as demagogic and counterproductive. By late 1937, they were emboldened to mount a counterattack when FDR told Tugwell that “perhaps a message addressed to him by a mixed group of labor and business leaders would be one way in which he could find means for retreat and a change of policy.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354839)
还有一批智囊团成员，在所谓的“第一次新政”（1933 年至 1935 年）期间颇具影响力。他们推崇大企业的技术效率，认为布兰代斯关于小规模竞争经济的观点不过是怀念逝去美国的空想。他们接受经济集中化的必然性，主张对大经济单位实施公共控制，而不是徒劳地试图将其拆散。他们斥责杰克逊-伊克斯的演讲是煽动民粹、适得其反。到 1937 年末，他们受到鼓舞，开始反击——当时 FDR 对特格韦尔表示，“也许由一群劳资领袖联合向他提交一份建议书，能为他提供一条退路和改变政策的契机。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354839)

In fashioning their group, these left-wing New Dealers found common cause with Morgans. This wasn’t as contradictory as it sounded. From Pierpont’s day, the House of Morgan had supported industrial planning, albeit under private control. What were the railway associations and U.S. Steel if not planned economic systems? (We recall the covert ideological link between the bank and the Progressives, epitomized by the friendship between Teddy Roosevelt and George Perkins.) At the same time, the partners were by no means hostile to all federal intervention to stop the Depression. If they hewed to the balanced-budget dogma and opposed higher taxes, Lamont, Leffingwell, and Parker Gilbert also advocated cheaper money to combat deflation. By contrast, the American Bankers Association attacked Roosevelt’s policy of low interest rates. The obscurantism of their fellow bankers sometimes bothered the Morgan men. “I sometimes wonder whether we ought to continue to give our silent sanction to the American Bankers Association by continuing our membership in it,” Leffingwell said, blaming tight Fed policy in 1936-37 for that year’s downturn.[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354994) In modern parlance, the Morgan partners were sympathetic to macroeconomic management of the overall economy, even if they deplored microeconomic regulation of specific industries.
在组建其团体时，这些左翼新政派人士与摩根家族找到了共同立场。这并不像听上去那样矛盾。自皮尔庞特时代起，摩根财团便支持工业规划，只不过是在私人控制之下。铁路协会和美国钢铁公司，若不算计划经济体系，又算什么呢？（我们还记得该银行与进步派之间隐秘的意识形态联系，以西奥多·罗斯福和乔治·珀金斯之间的友谊为代表。）与此同时，合伙人们对所有旨在遏止大萧条的联邦干预措施绝非一概敌视。尽管他们恪守平衡预算的信条并反对增税，但拉蒙特、莱芬韦尔和帕克·吉尔伯特也主张实行更廉价的货币以对抗通货紧缩。相比之下，美国银行家协会却攻击罗斯福的低利率政策。同行银行家们的蒙昧主义有时让摩根家族的人感到困扰。 “我有时会想，我们是否应该继续留在美国银行家协会，从而默许其做法，”莱芬韦尔说道，他将 1936-37 年的经济衰退归咎于当时美联储的紧缩政策。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2354994) 用现在的话说，摩根合伙人虽反对针对特定行业的微观调控，却对整体经济的宏观管理持同情态度。

Adolf A. Berle was an important theoretician of government planning, and in 1932, with economist Gardiner Means, he co-authored a classic text, *The Modern Corporation and Private Property.* Berle and Means insisted that the large corporation was an ineradicable fact of modern economic life and that government had to adjust to it. Disturbed by Robert Jackson’s speeches, Berle started to correspond with Lamont, who, of course, spoke kindly about big business, which he asserted had higher ethical standards than small business. He also stressed his allegiance to Roosevelt’s foreign policy and a good portion of his domestic policy as well. There was considerable poetic embellishment here. Not long before, Lamont had complained to his close friend Lady Astor about the “extravagance, waste, and loose administration” of Roosevelt’s White House.[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355227) But whatever license he took, Lamont was at least willing to talk and bargain with the New Dealers—a vast improvement over the fruitless rage of Jack Morgan and the rest of diehard Wall Street. Lamont struck a deal with Berle: he would support relief payments and deficit spending in exchange for a repeal of the surplus profits and capital gains taxes. At the same time, political attacks against business, especially utilities, had to end. This was the sort of political horse-trading so conspicuously absent from previous Morgan efforts to affect the New Deal.
阿道夫·A·伯利是政府规划领域的重要理论家，1932 年他与经济学家加德纳·米恩斯合著了经典著作《现代公司与私有财产》。伯利和米恩斯坚持认为，大型企业是现代经济生活中不可消除的现实，政府必须适应这一现实。罗伯特·杰克逊的演讲令伯利感到不安，于是他开始与拉蒙特通信——这位拉蒙特自然对大企业赞誉有加，宣称其道德标准比小企业更高。他还强调自己对罗斯福外交政策以及大部分国内政策的忠诚。这里面显然有不少粉饰之词。就在不久前，拉蒙特还向好友阿斯托夫人抱怨过罗斯福白宫的“奢侈、浪费和管理混乱”。 [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355227) 但无论他选择何种方式，拉蒙特至少愿意与新政派人士进行对话和谈判——这相比杰克·摩根及华尔街顽固派的徒劳愤怒来说，已是巨大的进步。拉蒙特与伯利达成了一项交易：他支持救济支出和赤字开支，以换取废除超额利润税和资本利得税。与此同时，针对商业尤其是公用事业的政治攻击也必须停止。这种政治上的讨价还价，在摩根家族此前试图影响新政的行动中明显缺失。

On the afternoon of December 22, 1937, eight members of a new Advisory Group met at New York’s Century Club, with Berle as chairman. Lamont and Owen Young of General Electric represented big business; Rexford Tugwell and Charles Taussig spoke for the New Deal; and Philip Murray, president of the steelworkers’ union, John L. Lewis of the Congress of Industrial Organizations, and CIO counsel Lee Pressman were there for the labor movement. In a decade badly polarized by class conflict, it was a unique moment. The eight men jointly opposed the antitrust prosecutions of Robert Jackson and endorsed the broad outlines of an agreement that had already been worked out by Berle and Lamont. At the end, Tugwell promised to set up a meeting with Roosevelt to discuss the pact.
1937 年 12 月 22 日下午，一个新成立的 Advisory Group 的八名成员在纽约世纪俱乐部召开会议，由 Berle 担任主席。Lamont 和通用电气的 Owen Young 代表大企业；Rexford Tugwell 与 Charles Taussig 代表新政派；钢铁工人工会主席 Philip Murray、产业工会联合会（CIO）的 John L. Lewis 以及 CIO 的法律顾问 Lee Pressman 则代表劳工运动。在那被阶级冲突严重两极分化的十年中，这一刻显得尤为独特。这八人共同反对 Robert Jackson 的反垄断诉讼，并认可了 Berle 与 Lamont 早已拟定好的协议大纲。会议结束时，Tugwell 承诺安排与 Roosevelt 会面，商讨该协议。

As a creature of the shadows, Lamont imagined the meeting with Roosevelt on January 14, 1938, would be a private, discreet affair. Instead, the participants had to run a gauntlet of photographers and reporters. There were press gibes about “Mr. Berle’s economic zoo” and front-page coverage supplied by unsympathetic White House leaks.[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355413) Nevertheless, it was a productive meeting, with the conferees approving expanded purchasing power through federal spending rather than the old deflationary shaving-wages approach to hard times. Despite Roosevelt’s desire for more meetings, the experiment was stillborn. Brandeis-influenced regulators in the administration—such as Thomas Corcoran and Ben Cohen, who drafted the securities law—opposed such overtures to business. And a far-left faction in the CIO was equally bent on spiking this nascent business-labor-government triumvirate.
作为暗影中的一员，拉蒙特原本以为 1938 年 1 月 14 日与罗斯福的会面会是一场私密、谨慎的会晤。结果，与会者却不得不穿过摄影师和记者组成的“长枪短炮”。媒体冷嘲热讽，称其为“伯尔尼先生的经济动物园”，而白宫方面不怀好意的泄密更让此事登上了头版头条。 [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355413) 不过，这次会议确实富有成效：与会者一致认为，应通过联邦政府支出扩大购买力，而非沿用经济困难时期那种过时的通缩式降薪手段。尽管罗斯福希望举办更多此类会议，但这一尝试最终胎死腹中。受布兰代斯影响的政府监管者——例如起草证券法的托马斯·科科伦和本·科恩——反对向企业界示好；而产联中的极左派也同样决心扼杀这个初具雏形的商业-劳工-政府三方联盟。

For his part, Lamont regretted that the White House meeting had degenerated into cheap political theater and that the cooperation offered by him and Owen Young “had been used to make third rate politics.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355588) At a time of political invective, it was a missed opportunity that demonstrated the potential benefit of practical discussions between business and labor. For the House of Morgan, it was an especially irretrievable chance, because the White House meeting occurred on the eve of a Morgan scandal that would turn the clock back to the dark days of 1933, calling into question the partners’ view of themselves as enlightened, public-spirited financiers.
在拉蒙特看来，白宫会议沦为廉价的政治作秀，他和欧文·杨提出的合作方案"被用来搞三流政治把戏"，这令他深感遗憾。在政治攻讦肆虐之时，这本是一次展现劳资双方务实对话潜在价值的契机，却最终错失。对摩根家族而言，这更是一次无可挽回的遗憾——因为白宫会议过后，一场摩根丑闻即将爆发，将局面拉回 1933 年的黑暗岁月，并让合伙人们自诩为开明、具有公益精神的金融家的形象备受质疑。

FOR the House of Morgan, the winter of 1937-38 turned into a time of debacle and mourning. In February 1938, worn by responsibility and the labors of a precocious early adulthood, forty-five-year-old S. Parker Gilbert died. The prodigy who ran the Mellon Treasury Department in his twenties had suffered from hypertension; his death was caused by heart and kidney problems, but many thought he had worked himself to death. The years of staying till two in the morning at the Treasury and the years in Weimar Berlin, where the Germans noted his unrelenting devotion to work, had taken their toll. Earlier, Gilbert and his bride, Louise, a Kentucky belle whose racy sayings were repeated around Wall Street, had postponed their honeymoon for five years. After joining the bank in 1931—Parker hadn’t asked for a set salary, waving it aside as a detail—the Morgan partners protected him, always urging him to vacation and conserve his strength. His prodigious work and dedication earned him decorations from France, Belgium, and Italy and honorary degrees from Harvard and Columbia. A year after he died, the pretty, round-faced Louise married Harold Stanley, whose first wife had died in 1934. This not only created a novel link between J. P. Morgan and Morgan Stanley but meant that Louise’s son, S. Parker Gilbert, Jr., Morgan Stanley chairman in the 1980s, would claim a unique Morgan lineage.
对于摩根家族而言，1937 年至 1938 年的冬天成了一段崩溃与哀悼的时光。1938 年 2 月，四十五岁的 S·帕克·吉尔伯特（S. Parker Gilbert）因不堪重负和过早承担成年重任的辛劳而离世。这位二十多岁就掌管梅隆财政部的神童长期受高血压困扰；虽然死因是心脏和肾脏问题，但很多人都认为他是积劳成疾。那些在财政部熬夜到凌晨两点的岁月，以及在魏玛柏林时——德国人曾注意到他不知疲倦地投入工作——都耗尽了他的心力。早年间，吉尔伯特和新婚妻子路易丝（一位来自肯塔基州的美人，她那些大胆泼辣的言论曾在华尔街流传）将蜜月推迟了五年。1931 年加入银行后——帕克没有要求固定薪水，只把它当作小事一桩——摩根合伙人一直保护着他，总劝他去度假、保存体力。 他卓著的工作与奉献精神为他赢得了法国、比利时和意大利的勋章，以及哈佛大学和哥伦比亚大学的荣誉学位。他去世一年后，面容姣好、圆脸的露易丝嫁给了哈罗德·斯坦利——他的第一任妻子已于 1934 年去世。这不仅在 J. P. 摩根和摩根士丹利之间建立了前所未有的联系，也意味着露易丝的儿子小 S. 帕克·吉尔伯特（20 世纪 80 年代的摩根士丹利董事长）将拥有独一无二的摩根血统。

Parker Gilbert’s death came two weeks before scandal broke. If the House of Morgan lost its investment banking business with Glass-Stea-gall, it perhaps lost its honor in the Richard Whitney case. Where Ferdinand Pecora had exposed questionable practices—things legitimate but of dubious wisdom—the Whitney scandal was for the House of Morgan a closer brush with the law. The case became a morality play of old versus new Wall Street, of private versus public trust. It would do more than just scotch Lamont’s attempt to ingratiate himself with the New Deal. It would also speed reforms of the New York Stock Exchange.
帕克·吉尔伯特去世两周后，丑闻爆发。如果说摩根银行因《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》失去了投行业务，那么它或许在理查德·惠特尼案中失去了声誉。费迪南德·佩科拉揭露的可疑操作——那些合法却欠妥的做法——相比于惠特尼丑闻，后者让摩根银行更切身地触碰到了法律红线。这起案件如同一场新旧华尔街的道德剧，上演着私人信托与公共信任的对决。它不仅粉碎了拉蒙特讨好新政的企图，更加速了纽约证券交易所的改革。

As president of the Exchange from 1930 to 1935, Richard Whitney had been the most arrogant Wall Street foe of federal securities regulation. For New Dealers, he personified the smug insolence of the *ancien régime* on Wall Street. When he testified about securities reform before the Senate Banking and Currency Committee in 1932, he lectured the senators on the need for a senatorial pay cut. Opposing creation of the SEC, he told Pecora’s investigators, “You gentlemen are making a great mistake. The Exchange is a perfect institution,” and he wouldn’t let brokers answer the Pecora questionnaires.[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355744) In 1937, he met his match in SEC chairman William O. Douglas, who succeeded Joe Kennedy that year. Douglas had engaged in talks with Stock Exchange president Charles R. Gay about Exchange reform, and Whitney led a board faction opposed to such efforts. In the autumn of 1937, Douglas gave the Stock Exchange leaders a stern tongue-lashing: “The job of regulation’s got to be done. It isn’t being done now, and, damn it, you’re going to do it or we are.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355921) Resigned to the need for change, Gay appointed a committee under Carle C. Conway of Continental Can to study reforms. In January 1938 it recommended a complete revamping of the Exchange, including a full-time paid president, a professional staff, and nonmember governors. It was amid such rancorous skirmishing that the Richard Whitney scandal would unfold.
在 1930 年至 1935 年间担任证券交易所总裁的理查德·惠特尼，是华尔街最傲慢的联邦证券监管反对者。对于新政派而言，他就是旧制度下华尔街狂妄自大的化身。1932 年，他在参议院银行与货币委员会就证券改革作证时，竟给参议员们上课，要求给他们减薪。他反对设立 SEC，对佩科拉的调查人员说：“诸位先生犯了个大错。证券交易所是个完美的机构，”还拒绝让经纪商回答佩科拉的问卷。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355744) 1937 年，他遇上了对手——接替乔·肯尼迪出任 SEC 主席的威廉·O·道格拉斯。道格拉斯此前与证券交易所总裁查尔斯·R·盖伊就交易所改革进行过会谈，而惠特尼领导董事会中的一个派系反对这些努力。1937 年秋，道格拉斯对证券交易所的领袖们严厉训斥道：“监管工作必须落实。” 现在还没有开始做，而且，该死的，你要么去做，要么我们来做。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2355921) 盖伊无奈地意识到必须变革，于是任命了一个由大陆罐头的卡尔·C·康威领导的委员会来研究改革方案。1938 年 1 月，该委员会建议对交易所进行全面改组，包括设立一位全职受薪主席、组建专业团队以及引入非会员理事。正是在这种充满敌意的冲突中，理查德·惠特尼丑闻即将爆发。

George and Richard Whitney were both tall, impressive, and patrician. Sons of a bank president, they had a Boston Brahmin upbringing and had attended Groton and Harvard. People would notice the gold watch chain with the Porcellian pig that Richard wore from his Harvard days. Morgan partner George had developed a dislike of his Groton classmate Franklin Roosevelt that he never shed. “My brother and I went to college, and we were always comfortable,” he said. “There was no poor-boy stuff about this.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356084) George came to Morgans via Kidder, Peabody, becoming a partner in 1919.
乔治和理查德·惠特尼身材高大、气质非凡，一派贵族风范。作为银行总裁之子，他们深受波士顿婆罗门式的精英教育熏陶，先后就读于格罗顿中学和哈佛大学。人们总能看到理查德佩戴着哈佛时期获得的波瑟里安猪会金表链。摩根合伙人乔治始终对自己的格罗顿同窗富兰克林·罗斯福心怀芥蒂，这份厌恶从未消散。“我和哥哥一路从大学走来，生活始终优渥，”他说道，“我们骨子里就没有寒酸气。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356084) 乔治经由基德尔-皮博迪公司进入摩根，于 1919 年成为合伙人。

With a ruggedly handsome face, solid jaw, and elegant hauteur, George was emblematic of the Morgan bank in those years. A British visitor later commented, “George Whitney—tall, slim, iron gray head, very goodlooking and altogether charming—Miss Macey regards him as dangerous both to men and women!”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356219) He perpetuated the Morgan tradition of fashion-plate partners. By a splendid coincidence, he had married Martha Bacon, daughter of Robert, the Greek God of Wall Street who had so entranced Pierpont.
乔治面容粗犷英俊，下巴线条坚实，气质高傲优雅，堪称当年摩根银行的典型代表人物。一位英国访客后来这样评价：“乔治·惠特尼——高挑清瘦，银灰色头发，相貌堂堂，魅力十足——梅西小姐认为他对男女而言都危险至极！” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356219) 他延续了摩根银行的传统，保持着时尚典范般的合伙人形象。巧合的是，他娶了一位风光无限的妻子——玛莎·培根，她是华尔街“希腊神祇”罗伯特·培根的女儿，这位先生当年曾令皮尔庞特神魂颠倒。

By the late 1930s, George Whitney ran the Morgan bank and was a director of Kennecott Copper, Texas Gulf Sulphur, Johns-Manville, and Guaranty Trust. As the head of domestic underwriting, he suffered more than other Morgan partners from Glass-Steagall and watched his business pass into Harold Stanley’s hands. He was greatly respected on Wall Street and, despite his reserve, very popular in the bank. Of the Morgan partners who trooped to Washington to answer questions, George Whitney often seemed the most snobbishly indignant, as if unwilling to concede the legitimacy of the proceedings. Just when it looked as if New Deal attacks might relent, the scandal that broke meant more government inquisitors trying to penetrate his polished defenses.
到 1930 年代末，乔治·惠特尼执掌摩根银行，同时担任 Kennecott Copper、Texas Gulf Sulphur、Johns-Manville 及 Guaranty Trust 等公司的董事。作为国内承销业务负责人，他比摩根其他合伙人更受 Glass-Steagall 法案的冲击，眼睁睁看着自己的业务落入哈罗德·斯坦利手中。他在华尔街备受敬重，尽管为人矜持，但在行内却极受欢迎。在成群结队前往华盛顿接受质询的摩根合伙人中，乔治·惠特尼往往显得最为傲慢愤慨，仿佛不愿承认这些调查的合法性。就在新政的攻势看似要缓和之际，爆出的丑闻却招来了更多政府调查人员，试图穿透他那层光鲜的防线。

George grew up in the shadow of his older brother, Richard, the star of the family. Richard’s early career on Wall Street seemed to live up to his family’s high expectations. On Black Thursday in 1929, as vice-president of the Stock Exchange, he had taken the fabled stroll, placing the bid for U.S. Steel and other stocks; the following spring he was elevated to president of the Exchange, the youngest person in history to hold the position. He became popularly known as the man who halted the panic of 1929 and emerged as something of a folk hero.[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356386) Cold and pompous, he was Mr. Wall Street, presiding in a black cutaway in his palatial suite on the Exchange’s top floor. In the private-club atmosphere, he represented the reactionary elements, the floor traders and specialists who resisted federal regulation, against the relatively more liberal retail brokers.
乔治是在哥哥理查德的阴影下长大的——理查德是家里的明星人物。理查德早年闯荡华尔街，似乎不负家人的厚望。1929 年那个黑色星期四，身为证券交易所副总裁的他，上演了那场传奇般的漫步，为美国钢铁公司及其他股票报出买价；次年春天，他升任交易所总裁，成为史上最年轻担任此职的人。他广为人知，被誉为阻止了 1929 年恐慌的人物，几乎成了民间英雄。 [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356386) 他冷漠而自大，是华尔街的化身，身着黑色燕尾服，在交易所顶层富丽堂皇的套房里发号施令。在这个私人俱乐部般的环境中，他代表的是保守势力——那些抵制联邦监管的场内交易员和专业经纪人，与相对开明的零售券商阵营形成了对立。

Richard’s association with J. P. Morgan went beyond his brother. His firm, Richard Whitney and Company, was the major broker handling gilt-edged bonds for the bank. Even if nobody at Morgans had been involved in the scandal, it would have reflected on the bank. As journalist John Brooks has said, “When the gods of 23 Wall materialized on the earthly market across the street, the bodily form they took was that of Dick Whitney.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356546) The bank generally stayed aloof, not involving itself in disputes at the Exchange, and was dismayed by the popular impression that Richard Whitney represented its views. By the time the scandal broke, it was too late to correct that impression.
理查德与摩根公司的渊源不止于他的兄弟。他旗下的理查德·惠特尼公司，正是为摩根银行处理优质金边债券的主要经纪商。就算摩根内部没人卷入这桩丑闻，它也会对银行声誉造成影响。正如记者约翰·布鲁克斯所言："当华尔街 23 号的神祇们降临到街对面的世俗市场时，他们化身的正是迪克·惠特尼的肉身。" [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356546) 银行向来持守超然姿态，从不参与交易所的争端，因此对公众误以为理查德·惠特尼代表其立场感到十分不满。当丑闻爆发时，想要纠正这一印象已然为时过晚。

Richard Whitney led a double life in the 1930s. As he defended pools, short sales, and other speculation from Washington attacks, he was struggling with an addiction to gambling. He was a sucker for fast-buck artists. He bought stock in a Florida fertilizer company right before that state’s economy collapsed and invested in a bootleg applejack called Jersey Lightning. All the while, he lived like a country squire. Married to the heiress daughter of a former president of the Union League Club, he bred thoroughbreds at a five-hundred-acre New Jersey estate, presided over the Essex Fox Hounds, owned a Fifth Avenue townhouse, and swaggered about like a tycoon.
20 世纪 30 年代，理查德·惠特尼过着双重生活。当他在华盛顿为联合坐庄、卖空及其他投机行为辩护时，他自己却深陷赌瘾无法自拔。他专吃投机分子的饵——在佛罗里达州经济崩溃前夕购入当地一家化肥公司的股票，又投资了一款名为"泽西闪电"的私酿苹果白兰地。与此同时，他仍保持着乡绅的派头：娶了联合联盟俱乐部前主席的富家女为妻，在新泽西州五百英亩的庄园里培育纯种马，担任埃塞克斯猎狐协会会长，在第五大道拥有联排别墅，举手投足间俨然一副大亨做派。

Chronically indebted, Richard was always borrowing and enlisting people in joint investment schemes. In 1929, he tried to lure his distant cousin, Jock Whitney, into an investment partnership. But by then Richard’s reputation was already sufficiently murky that lawyer Lewis Cass Ledyard talked Jock out of it. (Later, with his friend David O. Selznick, Jock would buy the movie rights to *The Story of Richard Whitney.*) The remarkably faithful George kept Richard solvent and indulged his fantasies of financial glory. Before the crash, George lent Richard \$500,000 to buy a Stock Exchange seat. After that, the loans grew more frequent, and Richard ran up a staggering \$3-million debt to his brother. These loans permitted others; as Richard panhandled on Wall Street, people assumed George stood behind him. The fear and respect accorded the House of Morgan was such that throughout Richard’s protracted financial crisis, nobody ever demanded repayment.
长期负债累累的理查德总是四处借钱，还拉人入伙搞投资。1929 年，他想忽悠远房表亲乔克·惠特尼一起搞投资合伙。但那时理查德的名声已经够臭了，律师刘易斯·卡斯·莱迪亚德劝住了乔克。（后来，乔克和朋友大卫·O·塞尔兹尼克还买下了《理查德·惠特尼的故事》的电影改编权。）忠心耿耿的乔治一直帮理查德撑着门面，还纵容他做金融大佬的白日梦。大崩盘前，乔治借给理查德 50 万美元买下证券交易所席位。之后借款越来越频繁，理查德欠哥哥的债居然滚到了 300 万美元。这些借款又让他能跟别人周转；当理查德在华尔街四处讨钱时，大家都觉得有乔治在背后撑腰。摩根财团让人又怕又敬，所以理查德财政危机拖了那么久，愣是没人来催他还钱。

In 1931, the Morgan bank made a \$500,000 loan to Richard that had to be continually renewed. The partners professed to like Richard’s roguish style, but with deep, unspoken reservations. At one point, they tried to get a veteran Stock Exchange governor to merge his firm with Richard’s in order to curb the latter’s excesses. Several times, Lamont warned George that Richard’s shrilly condescending attacks against securities reform were counterproductive. George himself knew Richard was being reckless. And when Morgans underwent its first inspection by state bank examiners in 1934, George had to supply his own securities as collateral for Richard’s loan.
1931 年，摩根银行向理查德提供了一笔 50 万美元的贷款，且不得不持续续期。合伙人们表面上欣赏理查德那副玩世不恭的做派，暗地里却深藏隐忧。某次，他们试图让一位资深的证券交易所总裁与理查德的公司合并，以制约其过激行为。拉蒙特多次警告乔治，理查德对证券改革尖刻傲慢的抨击适得其反。乔治自己也清楚理查德行事鲁莽。1934 年，当摩根银行首次接受州银行检查员审查时，乔治不得不拿出自己的证券作为理查德贷款的抵押品。

By the mid-1930s, in a sure sign of desperation, Richard was approaching Jewish Exchange members for loans, even though he had blackballed them from the Exchange’s upper echelons. In 1936, George asked partner Henry P. Davison, Jr., Harry’s son, to inspect Richard’s finances. While quizzing Richard in a polite, offhand manner, Davison noticed that his loans lacked sufficient collateral. Worse, Richard was using borrowed securities as collateral for more loans—the broad and open highway to financial ruin so memorably paved by William Crapo Durant a generation before.
到了 20 世纪 30 年代中期，理查德显然已走投无路，开始向犹太交易所成员借钱，尽管他此前曾将他们排斥在交易所高层之外。1936 年，乔治委托合伙人小亨利·P·戴维森（亨利之子）审查理查德的财务状况。戴维森以一种礼貌而随意的口吻旁敲侧击，发现他的贷款缺乏足够的抵押品。更糟的是，理查德还拿借来的证券作为抵押再去贷款——这正是上一代威廉·克莱普·杜兰特曾踏上的那条通往财务崩溃的宽阔大道。

At this point, Richard graduated from poor judgment to outright crime and began to loot two blue-blooded institutions. The Stock Exchange had a \$2.5-million Gratuity Fund, which provided death benefits to members’ families; Richard helped himself to \$1 million of its securities as collateral for loans to himself and his firm. As treasurer of the New York Yacht Club, he misappropriated \$150,000 in securities. The scandal was uncovered when Richard Whitney skipped a meeting of the Gratuity Fund trustees and a meek clerk divulged the missing securities. Suddenly Richard had to replace the “borrowed” shares. Among others, he tapped Averell Harriman for \$50,000 but needed bigger money. On November 23, 1937, he went to George for a \$ 1-million emergency loan. The bank’s formal culpability began here, because Richard admitted his criminal acts to his brother. It must have been a nightmare for George, who had spent years in Washington hotly defending the House of Morgan against insinuations of impropriety. As Richard said of George, “He was terribly disturbed and aghast that it could have been done and asked me many, many times why I had done it, and just couldn’t understand it—thunderstruck, as he had reason to be.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356708)
这时，理查德的所作所为已从糟糕的判断发展为赤裸裸的犯罪，开始掠夺两家贵族机构。证券交易所有一笔 250 万美元的抚恤基金，用于向会员家属提供死亡抚恤金；理查德从中挪用了 100 万美元的证券，作为自己和公司贷款的抵押品。作为纽约游艇俱乐部的财务主管，他挪用了 15 万美元的证券。丑闻败露是在理查德·惠特尼缺席抚恤基金受托人会议时，一名温顺的职员透露了证券短缺的情况。突然之间，理查德必须补回这些“借用”的股票。他先后向 Averell Harriman 等人筹措了 5 万美元，但仍需更大笔资金。1937 年 11 月 23 日，他去找乔治，申请 100 万美元的紧急贷款。银行的法律责任由此开始，因为理查德向自己的弟弟坦白了自己的犯罪行为。 这对乔治来说肯定是一场噩梦，他在华盛顿多年，一直激烈地为摩根财团辩护，驳斥那些关于不当行为的暗讽。正如理查德谈到乔治时所说：“他极为不安，震惊于这种事竟然会发生，一遍又一遍地问我为什么要这么做，完全无法理解——目瞪口呆，也难怪他会这样。”” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356708)

Lacking ready cash, George went to Lamont and told him Richard was in a “very serious jam.” (“Jam” would be the all-purpose euphemism of the scandal.) He admitted the misappropriation of Stock Exchange securities and said they had to be replaced the next day. Cool but sympathetic, Lamont said, “Well, that is a devil of a note, George. Why, Dick Whitney is all right; how could he mishandle securities even for a moment, no matter what the jam?”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2356963) The next day, in an extraordinary act of fear or friendship, Lamont sat down and wrote out a personal check for \$1 million; George then made it over to Richard. Two weeks later, after repaying Lamont, George asked Jack Morgan if he could withdraw money from his partnership capital, vaguely referring to Richard’s being in an “awful jam.” Jack didn’t inquire as to the reason. He later said he assumed the money was for a business matter.
手头缺现金，乔治去找拉蒙特，告诉他理查德“麻烦大了”。（“麻烦”在这里是丑闻的万能委婉说法。）他承认挪用了证券交易所的证券，并且第二天必须补上。拉蒙特冷静而同情地说：“哎呀，这可真是糟透了，乔治。喂，迪克·惠特尼不是那种人；他怎么会挪用证券呢，哪怕就一会儿，不管遇到什么麻烦？”第二天，出于恐惧或友情，拉蒙特做出一个非同寻常的举动——他坐下来，开了一张一百万美元的个人支票；乔治随后转给了理查德。两周后，乔治还清了拉蒙特，又去找杰克·摩根，说想从自己的合伙人资本中提些钱，含糊地提到理查德“遇到了大麻烦”。杰克没问原因。他后来说，他以为这笔钱是用在生意上的。

Because Lamont and George didn’t report Richard’s crime, they were guilty of misprision of a felony. For three months, they knew Richard was a crook but told nobody at the Stock Exchange and handled the embezzlement as a matter best settled privately among gentlemen. They faced an excruciating dilemma. The Morgan partners never paid bribes and prided themselves on their integrity, but now there were strong temptations to hush up the scandal. George was naturally reluctant to expose his brother’s crimes. And the bank knew the New Dealers would gladly exploit a scandal to impose further reforms on Wall Street. They didn’t want to throw Richard to the liberal Democratic wolves, especially to William O. Douglas, who was ready to pounce on the House of Morgan and the Stock Exchange.
由于拉蒙特和乔治没有举报理查德的罪行，他们犯下了隐瞒重罪。三个月来，他们明知理查德是个骗子，却未向证券交易所透露分毫，而是将这场挪用公款事件视为绅士间私下解决的私事。他们陷入了进退维谷的困境。摩根合伙人从不贿赂，素以诚信为傲，但如今却面临掩盖丑闻的巨大诱惑。乔治自然不愿揭发兄弟的罪行。而银行深知，新政派人士定会借机大做文章，对华尔街进一步推行改革。他们不愿将理查德抛给那些自由派民主党之狼，尤其是威廉·O·道格拉斯——他早已对摩根家族和证券交易所虎视眈眈。

A zealous regulator with a bottomless hatred of Wall Street, Douglas was a certified Morgan-hater. He had labeled the “Morgan influence . . . the most pernicious one in industry and finance today.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357089) He loathed the “goddamn bankers” and castigated the “financial termites” driven by a thirst for immediate profit. He continually plied Roosevelt with memos about the need for new regional industrial banks to “displace the Morgan influence in the various regions \[with\] a new and enlightened leadership in the business.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357277) Douglas was also conducting his crusade against the New York Stock Exchange, which he regarded as an archaic private club. In fact, he threatened to take over the Exchange the same month Richard went to George for his emergency loan.
道格拉斯是一个对华尔街恨之入骨的狂热监管者，堪称摩根的死对头。他曾将“摩根的影响力”定性为“当今工业和金融界最危险的力量”。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357089) 他痛恨那些“该死的银行家”，并严厉斥责那些急功近利的“金融白蚁”。他不断向罗斯福递交备忘录，强调有必要建立新的区域性工业银行，以“用全新的、开明的商业领导力取代摩根在各地区的影响力”。 [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357277) 道格拉斯还在对纽约证券交易所发起讨伐，他认为那不过是一个过时的私人俱乐部。事实上，就在理查德去找乔治申请紧急贷款的那个月，他扬言要接管该交易所。

It is apt at this point, before examining the final act of the Whitney scandal, to relate a small anecdote that deserves a place in the Morgan annals. In February 1938, Richard took a \$100,000 loan from a Walter T. Rosen. Evidently Rosen was well versed in Morgan lore, for in agreeing to the loan, he told Richard, “I have always been much impressed by the attitude of the elder Mr. Morgan who held the view that the personal integrity of the borrower was of far greater value than his collateral.” With a straight face, Richard replied, “Mr. Morgan was entirely right.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357421) By this point, Richard had racked up \$27 million in loans.
在深入探究惠特尼丑闻的终章之前，不妨先讲述一则值得载入摩根家族史册的小轶事。1938 年 2 月，理查德从沃尔特·T·罗森处借了十万美元。显然，罗森深谙摩根家族的掌故，因为他在同意贷款时对理查德说：“老摩根先生的态度一直令我印象深刻，他认为借款人的个人诚信远比抵押品更有价值。”理查德面不改色地答道：“摩根先生说得完全正确。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357421) 而到此时，理查德已累计负债两千七百万美元。

On March 5, 1938, while George was recuperating from an illness in Florida, Richard suddenly appeared at the Links Club. He interrupted Morgan partner Frank Bartow at a bridge game. “I am in a jam,” he blurted out and asked Bartow for a loan. He admitted that he had embezzled shares from the New York Yacht Club. Bartow said, “This is serious.” Richard replied, “This is criminal.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357584) Richard was about to appear before a Stock Exchange investigative committee and desperately needed money. Bartow refused to make a move before consulting a lawyer. The next day, he and Jack Morgan met with John Davis, who warned that any attempt to lend money to Richard could ruin the House of Morgan.[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357839) Their refusal to help sealed Richard’s fate. When they telephoned George in Florida and told him of his brother’s impending downfall, George simply gasped, “My God!”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358016)
1938 年 3 月 5 日，乔治在佛罗里达养病时，理查德突然出现在林克斯俱乐部。他打断了摩根合伙人弗兰克·巴托的桥牌局。“我遇到麻烦了，”他脱口而出，并向巴托借钱。他承认自己挪用了纽约游艇俱乐部的股票。巴托说：“这事很严重。”理查德回答：“这是犯罪。” [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357584) 理查德即将面对证券交易所调查委员会，急需资金。巴托在咨询律师之前拒绝采取行动。第二天，他和杰克·摩根会见了约翰·戴维斯，后者警告说，任何借钱给理查德的举动都可能毁掉摩根财团。 [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2357839) 他们拒绝帮忙，就此决定了理查德的命运。当他们打电话给佛罗里达的乔治，告知他弟弟即将垮台时，乔治只是倒吸一口气：“天哪！” [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358016)

On March 7, 1938, the board of governors of the Stock Exchange voted misconduct charges against Richard Whitney. The next morning, an Exchange representative sounded the gong on the trading floor and announced the suspension of Richard Whitney and Company for insolvency. Pandemonium followed, and share prices plunged. Soon afterward, New York County district attorney Thomas E. Dewey indicted Whitney for grand larceny and securities theft, including a \$100,000 theft from his wife. It came as a great shock to America’s aristocracy, including President Roosevelt. With old class loyalty surfacing, the president sat teary-eyed when William O. Douglas brought him the news as he breakfasted in bed. “Not Dick Whitney!” the president cried; “Dick Whitney—Dick Whitney. I can’t believe it!”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358271) For a moment, the economic royalists seemed as unconscionable as New Deal slogans claimed.
1938 年 3 月 7 日，证券交易所董事会投票指控理查德·惠特尼行为不当。次日清晨，一名交易所代表在交易大厅敲响铜锣，宣布因资不抵债暂停理查德·惠特尼公司的交易。随后场面一片混乱，股价暴跌。不久后，纽约县地方检察官托马斯·E·杜威以重大盗窃罪和证券盗窃罪起诉惠特尼，其中包括从他妻子那里窃取的 10 万美元。这一消息令包括罗斯福总统在内的美国上流社会大为震惊。当威廉·O·道格拉斯在总统卧床用早餐时带来这个消息时，旧日的阶级忠诚感涌上心头，总统泪眼婆娑。“不会是迪克·惠特尼吧！”总统喊道，“迪克·惠特尼——迪克·惠特尼。我简直不敢相信！” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358271) 那一刻，这些经济保皇派似乎正如新政口号所谴责的那般毫无道德底线。

The House of Morgan was outraged by the hastily arranged SEC investigation into the Whitney scandal. The crowded New York hearings took place at 120 Broadway, right near the Corner. Dean Acheson of Covington, Burling represented the Stock Exchange, while a young SEC lawyer named Gerhard A. Gesell led the questioning. When Ge-sell asked Jack Morgan whether he thought he had responsibilities to the Exchange in the matter, Jack replied, “No, none at all.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358453) When Gesell asked why Morgans had lent money to Richard, Jack replied that he had never inquired as to the reason. “Well, you didn’t think it was wine and women and horses, did you?” Gesell asked. When Jack said no, the sum was too large for that, everybody laughed.[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358617) Tired and defeated, Jack sat with eyes shut through much of the testimony, as if it were a bad dream from which he would soon thankfully awake. Gesell later praised him as a “perfectly delightful old gentleman . . . mellow and always truthful.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358804)
The House of Morgan 对仓促安排的 SEC 调查 Whitney 丑闻感到愤怒。拥挤的纽约听证会在百老汇 120 号举行，紧邻 the Corner。Covington, Burling 的 Dean Acheson 代表证券交易所，而一位名叫 Gerhard A. Gesell 的年轻 SEC 律师主导了质询。当 Ge-sell 问 Jack Morgan 是否认为自己在此事中对交易所有责任时，Jack 回答：“不，完全没有。” [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358453) 当 Gesell 问为什么 Morgans 借钱给 Richard 时，Jack 回答说他从没问过原因。“好吧，你不觉得那是酒、女人和马吧？” Gesell 问道。Jack 说不是，那笔钱太大了，所有人都笑了。 [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358617) 疲惫而挫败的 Jack 大部分作证时间里都闭着眼睛坐着，仿佛这是一场他很快就能如释重负醒来的噩梦。Gesell 后来称赞他为“一位非常令人愉快的老人……温和且总是诚实。” [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358804)

Lamont’s usual sangfroid deserted him. At the hearings, he admitted that it hadn’t occurred to him that Richard was a thief, that he lent the money to George, and that he assumed Stock Exchange officials knew of the share dealings. He indignantly asked, “Would you expect me, Mr. Gesell, to say to Mr. George Whitney, ’Yes, George, I will help you out to cure this default, which you believe is a perfectly isolated thing, but I must trot down to the district attorney’s office and denounce your brother forthwith?”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358930) Lamont said he had done what any friend would do. Similarly, George Whitney said he had done what any brother would do.
拉蒙特一反常态，失去了往日的镇定。在听证会上，他承认自己从未想过理查德是个贼，他借钱给乔治，并且以为证券交易所的官员知道这些股票交易。他愤愤不平地问道：“格塞尔先生，难道您指望我对乔治·惠特尼先生说‘好的，乔治，我会帮你解决这次违约，你相信这绝对是个孤立事件，但我必须立刻跑到地区检察官办公室去揭发你的兄弟’吗？” [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2358930) 拉蒙特说他做了任何一个朋友都会做的事。同样，乔治·惠特尼也说他做了任何一个兄弟都会做的事。

Lamont’s papers confirm his sense of bafflement. Even to his friend Lady Astor, he felt obliged to plead his innocence:
拉蒙特的文件证实了他的困惑。即便对他的朋友阿斯特夫人，他也觉得有必要为自己辩白：

> It is all a bit like Alice in Wonderland to me. Ought we all to forget the principles on which we were trained to help one another, to try to forgive and to try to give the fellow another chance? . . .
> 这一切对我来说有点像《爱丽丝梦游仙境》。难道我们都应该忘记那些我们从小被教导的原则——互相帮助、尝试原谅、给他人一次机会吗？……

> Of course, as the evidence proved, Dick was a thoroughgoing crook. He lied to George up to the last moment, he falsified his books, he deceived his wife and children, etc. etc. But all this was unknown to George last November at the time that he tried to help Dick undo the wrong that he had done.[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359103)
> 当然，正如证据所示，迪克是个彻头彻尾的骗子。他直到最后一刻都在对乔治撒谎，他篡改账目，欺骗妻儿，等等等等。然而，去年十一月乔治试图帮迪克弥补过错时，对这些还一无所知。 [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359103)

Although Richard Whitney pleaded guilty to grand larceny, George and Lamont escaped punishment. Prosecutor Dewey perhaps thought the rich had suffered enough. But the SEC report harshly criticized the pair and said they had known of Richard’s criminal conduct and financial difficulties. (Even before seeing the report, Jack told Lamont and George it would be another “poisonous” SEC document.[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359288)) Hard and relentless, William O. Douglas wanted Morgan blood. During the hearings, he summoned Gesell to his office and said, “The press tells me you’re being soft on George Whitney.” Gesell shot back, “Bill, that’s beneath you. I’ve been bringing out the facts, but I’m not going to rub George Whitney’s face in the dirt simply because he helped his brother. And I’m not being soft on him.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359508) Whitney respected Gesell and later encouraged Covington, Burling to hire him. “But you’d better get rid of this fellow Acheson,” he told Harry Covington. “He’s no good.”[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359685)
尽管理查德·惠特尼对重大盗窃罪供认不讳，但乔治和拉蒙特并未受到惩罚。检察官杜威或许认为这些富人已经吃够了苦头。然而，美国证券交易委员会（SEC）的报告严厉批评了这两人，并指出他们明知理查德的犯罪行为和财务困境。（甚至在看到这份报告之前，杰克就告诉拉蒙特和乔治，这又会是一份“毒害性”的 SEC 文件。 [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359288) ）冷酷无情的威廉·O·道格拉斯想要摩根家族付出代价。听证会期间，他把格塞尔叫到办公室说：“媒体告诉我，你对乔治·惠特尼手下留情了。”格塞尔反唇相讥：“比尔，这话可不像你说的。我一直在陈述事实，但我不会仅仅因为乔治帮了他弟弟，就把他往死里整。而且我对他并没有手软。” [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359508) 惠特尼很欣赏格塞尔，后来还鼓励科文顿·伯林律师事务所聘用他。“但你最好把这个叫艾奇逊的家伙赶走，”他对哈里·科文顿说。“他不是什么好鸟。” [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359685)

Douglas asked the Justice Department to review George Whitney’s and Lamont’s conduct for possible misprision of a felony. When Justice Department attorney Brien McMahon refused to prosecute them, Douglas saw a malign conspiracy at work. He later said McMahon would “cast our reports into the dustbin. . . . Somewhere in the background was a powerful figure with money and political connections.”[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359820) When he tried to get the Exchange to pursue the Morgan partners, only University of Chicago president Robert Hutchins voted for censure.
道格拉斯要求司法部审查乔治·惠特尼和拉蒙特的所作所为，看是否构成对重罪的知情不报。当司法部检察官布莱恩·麦克马洪拒绝起诉他们时，道格拉斯认定背后存在一股恶意的阴谋。他后来表示，麦克马洪“会把我们的报告丢进垃圾桶……背后肯定有个财大气粗、人脉广泛的大人物。” [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359820) 当他试图推动交易所追究摩根合伙人的责任时，只有芝加哥大学校长罗伯特·哈钦斯投票赞成惩戒。

Douglas capitalized on the scandal to push through a new constitution and reform slate at the Exchange. The embezzlement demonstrated the need for greater openness at the Stock Exchange. By mid-May, the reforms recommended by the Conway committee were enacted. The board of governors was broadened to include public members, and the thirty-four-year-old secretary of the Conway committee, William McChesney Martin of Saint Louis, was elected the first salaried president of the Exchange. Douglas thus converted the Exchange from a private club into a body responsive to SEC dictates. He also pushed another reform agenda—competitive bidding for securities issues. In December 1938, he won a partial victory when the SEC ruled that investment banks couldn’t collect underwriting fees from public utilities unless they engaged in arm’s-length bargaining. Other financial crusaders also took heart from Whitney’s disgrace. Railroadman Robert Young later said he had the courage to persist against Lamont’s opposition after reading about Whitney’s arrest, which he saw as proof of decaying Morgan power.
道格拉斯利用这桩丑闻，在证券交易所推行了新章程和改革方案。这起贪污案表明，证券交易所亟需更大的透明度。到五月中旬，康威委员会建议的改革措施得以落实。理事会扩员，引入了公众代表；康威委员会 34 岁的秘书、来自圣路易斯的威廉·麦克切斯尼·马丁当选为交易所首任领薪总裁。至此，道格拉斯成功将交易所从一家私人俱乐部转变为对 SEC 指令有所回应的机构。他还推动了另一项改革议程——证券发行的竞争性招标。1938 年 12 月，他取得部分胜利：SEC 裁定，投资银行若未进行公平议价，不得从公用事业公司收取承销费。其他金融改革者也因惠特尼的丑闻而备受鼓舞。 铁路大亨 Robert Young 后来表示，在读到 Whitney 被捕的消息后，他有了顶住 Lamont 反对继续下去的勇气，他认为这证明了 Morgan 势力的衰落。

And what happened to Richard Whitney? After his arrest he behaved like a French nobleman being dragged off to the guillotine. Determined to face down his executioners, he berated Gerhard Gesell for being five minutes late to one interrogation. He objected to being described as insolvent, saying in a huff, “I still can borrow money from my friends.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359998) Meanwhile wealthy sympathizers stacked up floral wreaths in front of his East Seventy-third Street townhouse. After he was convicted of grand larceny, a circus atmosphere attended his departure for a five- to ten-year prison term at Sing Sing. Five thousand spectators at Grand Central Terminal saw a tall bowler-hatted man being led to the train by police. He was shackled to two other prisoners—an extortionist and a man convicted of assault. Unlike these two criminals, the impassive Whitney made no attempt to hide his face from photographers. He became inmate number 94835 at Sing Sing, and the first Stock Exchange president ever to serve time there.
那么理查德·惠特尼后来怎样了？被捕后，他的做派活像个被拖去断头台的法国贵族。他决心要压倒那些处决者，还斥责格哈德·格塞尔在一次审讯中迟到了五分钟。他拒绝被描述为无力偿债，气冲冲地说：“我还能从朋友那里借到钱。” [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2359998) 与此同时，富有的同情者们在他东七十三街的联排别墅前堆满了花圈。被判重大盗窃罪后，他前往星星监狱服刑五到十年，整个过程被一种马戏团般的氛围所围绕。在纽约中央车站，五千名围观者看到一个戴着高顶礼帽的高个子男人被警察押上火车。他与另外两名囚犯铐在一起——一个敲诈勒索犯和一个被判攻击罪的犯人。与这两名罪犯不同，不动声色的惠特尼没有试图在摄影师面前遮挡自己的脸。 他成为新新监狱的第 94835 号囚犯，也是首位在那里服刑的证券交易所总裁。

In the long run, the scandal’s real beneficiary may have been George Whitney. For years, he profited from comparison with Richard and became the Nice Honest Whitney Brother, softening his image as a defender of privilege. His loyalty to Richard stirred even the New Dealers. Over the years, Gerhard Gesell would be touched by news photographs of George taking a glove or a bat to Richard so he could play on the prison baseball team. (Richard was also visited by his old Groton headmaster, the Reverend Endicott Peabody.) By August 1941, Richard was eligible for parole, and George drove up to meet him at the prison gate. Richard then served as superintendent of a dairy farm in Barnstable, Massachusetts. He never again entered the world of finance or public life.
从长远来看，这起丑闻的真正受益者或许是乔治·惠特尼。多年来，他因与理查德的对比而获益，成了“正派诚实的惠特尼兄弟”，软化了自己作为特权捍卫者的形象。他对理查德的忠诚甚至打动了新政派人士。多年来，格哈德·格塞尔被新闻照片所触动——照片中乔治为理查德递手套或球棒，好让他能参加监狱棒球队。（理查德的老校友、格罗顿中学校长恩迪科特·皮博迪牧师也曾来探望他。）到 1941 年 8 月，理查德符合假释条件，乔治开车到监狱门口接他。随后，理查德在马萨诸塞州巴恩斯特布尔的一家奶牛场担任主管。他再也没有涉足金融界或公共生活。
