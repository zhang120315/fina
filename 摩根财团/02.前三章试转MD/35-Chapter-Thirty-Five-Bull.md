# Chapter Thirty-Five: Bull

WALL Street in the Reagan era self-consciously retraced the experience of the 1920s. Commentators noted eerie parallels between the decades—a booming stock market, Republican tax cuts, a Latin American debt crisis, fluctuating currencies, a merger wave, trade wars, farm and energy slumps. Fed chairman Paul Volcker served up the disinflation tonic, much as Ben Strong had in the 1920s, and the world suddenly swam in cash. Newspapers superimposed stock market graphs from the Coolidge and Hoover days onto those of the Reagan years, comforting bulls and bears alike. As in the 1920s, sages said old value measures were outdated and again worried about a shortage of common stock. The speculative froth on Wall Street was again regarded as a sign of economic dynamism.
里根时代的华尔街刻意重蹈着 20 世纪 20 年代的覆辙。评论家们注意到这两个十年间存在着令人毛骨悚然的相似之处——股市飙升、共和党减税、拉丁美洲债务危机、汇率波动、并购浪潮、贸易战、农业和能源业衰退。美联储主席保罗·沃尔克效仿 20 年代的本·斯特朗，祭出了通货紧缩的强心针，全球瞬间被现金淹没。报纸将柯立芝和胡佛时代的股市走势图叠加在里根时代的图表上，让多头和空头都感到心安。正如 20 年代那样，智者们宣称旧有的价值衡量标准已然过时，同时又担忧普通股供应不足。华尔街的投机泡沫再次被视为经济活力的象征。

In the 1920s, the United States was the world’s chief creditor, a rising power with a bulging trade surplus. Armed with superior technology, American companies expanded around the globe. But the heady Wall Street of the 1980s masked America’s declining economic position relative to that of Japan and Europe. Thanks to Reagan’s tax cuts and budget deficits, the United States became a net borrower from the world. The stock market frenzy neither enhanced American competitiveness nor trimmed the trade deficits, which had been persistent since the early 1970s. Morning headlines screamed of billion-dollar deals that were supposed to improve the economy, but they never seemed to strengthen America’s position in world markets.
上世纪 20 年代，美国是全球头号债权国，凭借庞大的贸易顺差崛起为新兴强国。美国企业依托先进技术，在全球范围内开疆拓土。然而 80 年代华尔街的纸醉金迷，掩盖了美国经济地位相对日本和欧洲的式微。由于里根政府的减税政策和预算赤字，美国沦为全球净债务国。股市狂热既未提升美国竞争力，也未能扭转自 70 年代初便挥之不去的贸易逆差。每天早间头条都在鼓吹数十亿美元的交易能提振经济，可这些交易似乎从未真正强化美国在全球市场的地位。

As Wall Street returned to favor, America’s youth flocked to the casino. By 1986, one of every ten Yale College graduates applied for a job at First Boston and over 30 percent of Harvard Business School graduates ended up at Morgan Stanley; Goldman, Sachs; Merrill Lynch; or First Boston. They came to a world that had little in common with the sedate, gentlemanly Wall Street of the early postwar years. Even the name now seemed a misnomer. “Except for Brown Brothers Harriman,” said Martin Turchin of Edward S. Gordon Company, “I can’t think of a major investment banking firm that has its headquarters on Wall Street.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438657) They had all trailed their clients uptown.
随着华尔街重获青睐，美国年轻人纷纷涌向这座赌场。到 1986 年，耶鲁大学每十名毕业生中就有一人向第一波士顿银行求职，而超过 30%的哈佛商学院毕业生最终进入了摩根士丹利、高盛、美林或第一波士顿。他们踏入的世界，与战后初期那个温文尔雅的绅士华尔街截然不同。如今连“华尔街”这个称呼都显得名不副实。爱德华·S·戈登公司的马丁·图尔钦表示：“除了布朗兄弟哈里曼，我想不出还有哪家大型投行总部设在华尔街上。”这些投行早已追随客户迁往城北。

The Reagan years saw the demise of relationship banking and with it the end of grace and civility on Wall Street. Wall Street was tougher, meaner, smarter, and more macho than ever before. The leisurely syndicate world faded after Rule 415, and the Gentleman Banker’s Code was fully obsolete. As the taboo against raiding clients and making cold calls broke down, investment bankers clashed with one another. There was no longer any agreed-upon etiquette to temper the greedy impulses that always existed in finance. Wall Street was run by bright, young executives who seemed curiously devoid of larger political or social concerns in their narrow pursuit of profits.
里根时代终结了关系银行业务，华尔街的优雅与温情也随之消散。这里变得比以往任何时候都更强硬、更刻薄、更精明、更充满雄性气息。《415 规则》出台后，闲散的银团世界逐渐隐退，绅士银行家守则彻底作古。随着掠夺客户和打冷电话的禁忌被打破，投资银行家们开始相互倾轧。金融领域一贯存在的贪婪冲动，再没有约定俗成的礼仪来抑制。掌控华尔街的是那些年轻有为的高管，他们狭隘地追逐利润，却奇怪地对更广泛的政治或社会议题漠不关心。

In 1985, Morgan Stanley named Eric Gleacher to succeed Joe Fogg as head of Mergers and Acquisitions; at Lehman Brothers Kuhn Loeb, Gleacher had been Adrian Antoniu’s boss. A short, trim former marine commander of a rifle platoon, he had an MBA from the University of Chicago, believed in a gung-ho takeover style, and ran his department with martial discipline. Called a “steel wall” at the bargaining table, he had tremendous stamina and skied, golfed, and ran marathons in his spare time. Through Gleacher, Morgan Stanley would branch out from takeovers in behalf of blue-chip corporations to more direct involvement with corporate raiders. For example, Gleacher recruited as a Morgan Stanley client the cigar-smoking Ronald O. Perelman, who acquired Revlon Incorporated in a bitter 1985 fight.
1985 年，摩根士丹利任命埃里克·格利彻接替乔·福格出任并购业务主管；格利彻在雷曼兄弟库恩·洛布公司时曾是阿德里安·安东纽的上司。这位身材短小精悍的前海军步枪排指挥官，拥有芝加哥大学 MBA 学位，信奉激进进取的收购风格，并以军事化纪律管理其部门。在谈判桌上被称为"铁壁"，他精力极其充沛，业余时间滑雪、打高尔夫、跑马拉松。通过格利彻，摩根士丹利从代表蓝筹公司进行收购，扩展到更直接地与企业掠夺者合作。例如，格利彻将抽雪茄的罗纳德·O·佩雷尔曼招揽为摩根士丹利客户，后者在 1985 年一场激烈的争夺战中收购了 Revlon Incorporated。

In his merger factory, Gleacher had a team of ten bright young people who did nothing but cook up deals for the firm to sell to clients. In 1978, Bob Greenhill had said that Morgan Stanley was simply executing clients’ deals, not iniating them.[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438829) Now passivity was scrapped: every deal announced in the morning newspaper was studied for a profit angle. Each morning, the ten high-priced well-educated dreamers lined up outside Gleacher’s office to pitch their ideas in rapid-fire style. When a subordinate brought a three-inch-thick notebook full of figures for a possible deal, Gleacher dropped it in the wastebasket. “Come back when you know what you’re talking about,” he said.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438976)
在格莱彻的交易工厂里，他手下有十名聪慧的年轻人，他们的唯一工作就是构思交易方案，供公司出售给客户。1978 年，鲍勃·格林希尔曾表示，摩根士丹利只是执行客户的交易，而非主动发起。 [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438829) 如今，这种被动局面被打破：每天早上报纸上公布的每一笔交易，都会被研究以寻找盈利点。每天早晨，这十名高薪且受过良好教育的梦想家在格莱彻办公室外排队，以快节奏的方式推销他们的想法。当一名下属拿着一本三英寸厚、满载数据的笔记本汇报潜在交易时，格莱彻直接把它扔进废纸篓。“等你弄清楚自己在说什么再回来。”他说。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438976)

Wall Street no longer seemed subservient to corporate clients or simply the executor of their wishes; it had assumed a disturbing life of its own. Far from taking his cues from clients, Gleacher believed “deal makers should never take no for an answer,” asserted *Fortune.*[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439127) The magazine told how he had badgered Robert Cizik of Cooper Industries in Houston into buying McGraw Edison. “Let me fly down and talk to you,” Gleacher had said on a cold call. “It’s my nickel.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439252) His persuasive presentation the next day convinced Cooper Industries to make the \$1-billion acquisition, netting Morgan Stanley \$4 million. Gleacher also convinced Pantry Pride to take over Revlon, bringing in \$30 million in fees in a Ronald O. Perelman raid financed by a flood of junk bonds. A decade earlier, naive bankers had trembled to ask for a \$1-million fee.
华尔街不再对客户唯命是从，也不再仅仅是执行他们意愿的工具；它已呈现出一种扰人的生命力。《财富》杂志指出，格莱彻绝非被动听从客户指令，他坚信“交易撮合者绝不应接受否定答案”。该杂志讲述了他如何软磨硬泡，说服休斯顿库柏工业公司的罗伯特·西齐克收购麦格劳-爱迪生公司。“让我飞过去和你谈谈，”格莱彻在一次陌生拜访电话中说，“这钱我来出。”次日，他那极具说服力的陈述促使库柏工业做出了这笔价值 10 亿美元的收购，为摩根士丹利带来了 400 万美元的收益。格莱彻还说服潘特里·普莱德收购了雷夫隆公司，在罗纳德·O·佩雷尔曼通过大量垃圾债券融资发起的收购中，带来了 3000 万美元的费用。而在十年前，天真的银行家们连索要 100 万美元的费用都会战战兢兢。

The portion of corporate America now passing through the merger mills was staggering. Morgan Stanley handled \$8.5 billion in merger transactions in 1982. Within two years, that figure zoomed to a record \$52 billion. After some backsliding under Fogg, Morgan Stanley nosed out the First Boston team of Bruce Wasserstein and Joe Perella in 1985 to recover first place in merger work, booking an awesome \$82 million in fees and giving Morgan Stanley Wall Street’s highest return on equity. In the four years leading up to the 1987 crash, Morgan Stanley was involved in \$238 billion worth of mergers and acquisitions. As Joe Flom would say in 1989, “We’ve gone through the most massive corporate restructuring in history over a period of 15 years.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439377) The figures were now so fantastic that they seemed to have dazed and stupefied the public.
当时，美国企业界涌入并购潮流的规模令人震惊。1982 年，摩根士丹利经手的并购交易总额达 85 亿美元。短短两年后，这一数字飙升至创纪录的 520 亿美元。在 Fogg 领导期间经历一些波折后，摩根士丹利于 1985 年超越 Bruce Wasserstein 和 Joe Perella 率领的 First Boston 团队，重夺并购业务冠军宝座，收入高达 82 亿美元的手续费，并创下华尔街最高的股本回报率。在 1987 年股灾前的四年里，摩根士丹利参与了总值 2380 亿美元的并购交易。正如 Joe Flom 在 1989 年所说：“我们在 15 年间经历了历史上规模最大的企业重组。”这些数字如今已庞大到让公众看得目瞪口呆。

Many deals were spurred by needed change. At a time of technological flux, mature companies had to transfer money from dying industries to thriving ones. Foreign competition and deregulation were stimulating radical shifts in hitherto protected industries, including airlines, telecommunications, energy, media, and finance itself. Investment banks were the agents for the global integration of markets, much as they had welded together national markets in the era of Pierpont Morgan.
许多交易都是由变革需求推动的。在技术变革的浪潮中，成熟企业不得不将资金从夕阳产业转向朝阳产业。外国竞争和放松管制正在促使此前受保护的行业发生剧变，包括航空、电信、能源、媒体以及金融业本身。投资银行充当了全球市场一体化的推手，就像它们在皮尔庞特·摩根时代将各国市场紧密连接在一起那样。

But too many deals seemed to be hatched by investment banks and corporate raiders merely for self-enrichment. The prototypical 1980s raiders—Boone Pickens, Carl Icahn, and Sir James Goldsmith—talked self-righteously about “cleansing” or “liberating” companies from “entrenched management.” They claimed target companies were the victims of a harsh, Darwinian necessity, implying that they were invariably poorly run companies. Yet in 1978, before this became the party line, Bob Greenhill had said, “The acquiring companies are not simply looking for bargains, or corporations in trouble. Typically, they are interested in well-managed companies.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439524) This was often the case.
但太多交易似乎只是投资银行和公司掠夺者为了中饱私囊而策划的。20 世纪 80 年代的典型掠夺者——布恩·皮肯斯、卡尔·伊坎和詹姆斯·戈德史密斯爵士——总是自以为是地谈论着要"清洗"或"解放"那些被"固步自封的管理层"把持的公司。他们声称目标公司是残酷达尔文法则下的牺牲品，言下之意这些企业无一例外都经营不善。然而在 1978 年，当这套说辞还未成为主流论调时，鲍勃·格林希尔就曾说过："收购方并非单纯在寻找便宜货或陷入困境的企业。通常情况下，他们对管理良好的公司更感兴趣。"事实往往确实如此。

Aside from the painful dislocations suffered by towns and workers, as a result of mergers, the takeover flurry penalized companies with clean balance sheets, little debt, and lots of cash. To stay independent, they had to cripple themselves by loading up on debt. The old Morgan Stanley had favored sound, conservative finance and was protective of the credit ratings of clients. The new Morgan Stanley stampeded companies into taking on debt, whether to conduct raids or to ward them off.
除了并购给城镇和工人带来的阵痛之外，这场收购狂潮还惩罚了那些资产负债表干净、负债少、现金多的公司。为了保持独立，它们不得不背负大量债务，从而削弱自身实力。老摩根士丹利曾推崇稳健保守的财务策略，并注重保护客户的信用评级。而新摩根士丹利则驱赶着公司举债——无论是为了发动收购，还是为了抵御收购。

The firm’s M&A people dismissed the existence of any problem. In 1986, with nationwide takeovers running at a robust \$200 billion a year, Eric Gleacher said, “When you look at the debt of the world, the debt of the country, and the debt of the private sector, you can’t with a straight face tell me that a few speculative merger deals are going to tip the balance and create disaster?”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439671) Note that Gleacher doesn’t praise the debt: it’s more of a defensive everybody-is-doing-it kind of argument. There were also now more than “a few speculative merger deals.” In 1970, only 10 takeover deals passed the million-dollar mark; by 1986, the figure had surged to 346 in a wave of consolidations that engulfed the economy.
该公司负责并购的人士否认存在任何问题。1986 年，全国并购交易额高达 2000 亿美元，埃里克·格莱彻（Eric Gleacher）表示：“看看全世界的债务、国家的债务、私营部门的债务，你总不能厚着脸皮告诉我，几笔投机性的并购交易就能打破平衡、引发灾难吧？” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439671) 注意，格莱彻并非在赞美债务，这更像是一种“大家都在这么做”的防御性论调。况且，当时早已不止“几笔投机性的并购交易”。1970 年，仅有 10 笔并购交易突破百万美元大关；而到了 1986 年，在席卷经济的整合浪潮中，这一数字已飙升至 346 笔。

Joe Fogg similarly had dismissed any problem. When asked whether the debt binge wasn’t draining capital from productive uses, he called that notion “very silly and superficial. . . . The fact is, acquisition transactions merely reflect a change in ownership of capital assets. The money does not disappear. It is then used for other investments.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439822) This was surely true of money changing hands in a takeover. But how was that additional takeover money raised in the first place? Typically by bank loans or junk bonds whose interest payments then siphoned off money from productive investment. This bias in favor of debt was strikingly at odds with the old Morgan Stanley, whose culture Bob Baldwin had described as “risk-averse” as recently as 1980.
乔·福格同样对任何问题都不以为然。当被问及债务狂欢是否在从生产性用途中抽离资本时，他称这种想法“非常愚蠢且肤浅……事实上，收购交易仅仅反映了资本资产所有权的变更。钱并没有消失，它随后被用于其他投资。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439822) 这用在收购中的资金易手上确实不假。但那些额外的收购资金最初又是如何筹集的呢？通常是靠银行贷款或垃圾债券，而其利息支付则源源不断地从生产性投资中抽走资金。这种对债务的偏袒与老摩根士丹利形成了鲜明对比——直到 1980 年，鲍勃·鲍德温还将其文化描述为“厌恶风险”。

Merger mania on Wall Street produced a stunning paradox in the 1980s: corporations grew financially weaker during the sustained Reagan boom. By the 1987 crash, nonfinancial corporate debt stood at a record \$1.8 trillion, with companies diverting fifty cents of every dollar of earnings to pay off creditors—a far greater percentage than in earlier years. It was hard to see how corporate America could weather a severe recession without unspeakable destruction. As in the Jazz Age, much of the era’s financial prestidigitation seemed premised on an unspoken assumption of perpetual prosperity, an end to cyclical economic fluctuations, and a curious faith in the Federal Reserve Board’s ability to avert disaster.
80 年代华尔街的并购狂潮产生了一个惊人的悖论：在持续多年的里根繁荣期，企业财务状况反而越发脆弱。到 1987 年股市崩盘时，非金融企业债务已飙升至创纪录的 1.8 万亿美元，企业每赚 1 美元就要拿出 50 美分来偿还债权人——这一比例远超前些年。很难想象，美国企业若遭遇严重衰退，怎能不在毁灭性的打击中幸存。与爵士时代如出一辙，那个年代的大量金融戏法似乎都建立在一个不言自明的假设之上：繁荣永续、经济周期性波动已然终结，以及对美联储化解危机能力的某种奇异迷信。

Not surprisingly, Morgan Stanley was drawn to that most lucrative of 1980s fads—the leveraged buyout, or LBO. As a dangerous form of leverage, LBOs rivaled the pyramidal holding companies of the 1920s. In a basic LBO, a company’s managers and a group of outside investors borrow money to acquire a company and take it private; the company’s own assets are used as collateral for the loans, which are repaid from future earnings or asset sales. (Interest payments, significantly, are tax deductible.) In the 1970s, they were called “bootstrap financings” and seldom exceeded \$100,000. André Meyer of Lazard Frères had taken equity stakes for years, but Wall Street didn’t take notice until 1979, when First Boston did an LBO for a conglomerate called Congoleum. LBOs became popular as the conglomerates of the 1960s were dismantled and managers took over pieces of them. They became rampant as a byproduct of takeovers, with managers resorting to them to fend off raiders or even flush them out. The LBO was thus a natural sequel to the merger wave.
不出所料，摩根士丹利被上世纪 80 年代最有利可图的潮流所吸引——杠杆收购（LBO）。作为一种危险的杠杆形式，LBO 堪比 1920 年代的金字塔式控股公司。在基本的 LBO 中，公司管理层与一群外部投资者借钱收购一家公司并将其私有化；公司自身的资产被用作贷款抵押，贷款则通过未来收益或资产出售偿还。（值得注意的是，利息支付可以抵扣税款。）在 1970 年代，它们被称为"自筹资金"收购，金额很少超过 10 万美元。Lazard Frères 的 André Meyer 多年来一直持有股权，但华尔街直到 1979 年才注意到这一模式——当时 First Boston 为一家名为 Congoleum 的企业集团完成了 LBO。随着 1960 年代的企业集团被拆分、管理层接手其中部分业务，LBO 开始流行起来。作为收购的副产品，LBO 变得猖獗，管理层利用它们来抵御恶意收购者，甚至将其挤出局。 因此，杠杆收购是并购浪潮的自然延续。

With its unerring instinct for profitable activities, Morgan Stanley noted the LBO profits at First Boston and Merrill Lynch. In 1985, it joined with CIGNA Insurance to create a leveraged buyout fund. It struck spectacular deals that made merger profits look like small change in comparison. In 1986, it joined with an Irish paperboard concern, Jefferson Smurfit, to purchase Container Corporation of America from Mobil for \$1.2 billion. Morgan and Jefferson put up only \$10 million apiece, borrowing the rest. Morgan Stanley pocketed a fast \$32.4 million: \$11 million for arranging the buyout, \$20.4 million for underwriting nearly \$700 million in junk bonds to effect the purchase, and a \$1-million advisory fee. Such fees recalled investment banks feasting on new trust issues at the turn of the century. In LBOs, the target company suffered the pain and carried the risk, having to sell assets and cut costs to pay off the debt. Meanwhile, as a limited partnership, the Morgan Stanley buyout fund couldn’t lose more than its tiny, original \$10-million investment. The risks were remarkably limited in view of the enormous potential profits. And only three years later, Morgan’s \$10 million stake was worth \$140 million.
摩根士丹利凭借其对盈利活动精准的直觉，注意到第一波士顿和美林在杠杆收购中获利颇丰。1985 年，它与信诺保险联手成立了一只杠杆收购基金。其达成的几笔轰动性交易，让并购利润相比之下显得微不足道。1986 年，它与爱尔兰纸板企业杰斐逊·斯默菲特合作，以 12 亿美元从美孚手中收购了美国容器公司。摩根士丹利和杰斐逊各自仅出资 1000 万美元，其余资金均为借贷。摩根士丹利迅速入账 3240 万美元：其中 1100 万美元来自安排收购的费用，2040 万美元来自承销近 7 亿美元垃圾债券以完成收购的费用，还有 100 万美元咨询费。此类收费让人想起投资银行在上世纪初从新兴信托发行中大肆获利的景象。在杠杆收购中，目标公司承受痛苦并承担风险，不得不通过出售资产和削减成本来偿还债务。 与此同时，作为有限合伙制基金，摩根士丹利的收购基金最多不会损失超过其最初那笔区区 1000 万美元的投资。对比其巨大的潜在利润，风险极其有限。 而仅仅三年后，摩根士丹利那 1000 万美元的股份价值已高达 1.4 亿美元。

Soon afterward, Morgan Stanley helped Kohlberg Kravis Roberts, the leading LBO firm, take over Owens-Illinois for \$4.2 billion. Again it scored triple fees, this time of \$54 million. Even though it had only \$1.5 billion in capital, Morgan Stanley made a temporary \$600-million bridge loan to effect the deal. (What bank would have gotten away with *that?* Yet investment banks could bypass SEC safeguards by issuing bridge loans through their holding companies.) Where the old Morgan Stanley minimized its capital risk and remained a financial intermediary, the new Morgan Stanley was gambling more of its capital in a new push into “merchant banking.” Although Wall Street relished the historical associations of the term, it bore little resemblance to the mercantile financing engaged in by George Peabody and Junius Morgan. It simply referred to management buyouts, taking equity stakes in takeovers, or making temporary bridge loans to finance takeovers.
不久之后，摩根士丹利协助领先的杠杆收购公司科尔伯格-克拉维斯-罗伯茨以 42 亿美元收购了欧文斯-伊利诺伊公司。这一次，它再次收取了三重费用，金额高达 5400 万美元。尽管摩根士丹利只有 15 亿美元的资本，但它还是提供了 6 亿美元的临时过桥贷款来促成这笔交易。（哪家银行敢这么干？然而，投资银行可以通过其控股公司发行过桥贷款，绕过美国证券交易委员会的监管。）老摩根士丹利将资本风险降到最低，始终扮演金融中介的角色；而新摩根士丹利则在新兴的“商人银行”业务中投入更多资本进行豪赌。尽管华尔街对这个词的历史渊源津津乐道，但它与乔治·皮博迪和朱尼厄斯·摩根从事的商业融资几乎毫无相似之处。它不过是指管理层收购、在收购中参股，或为收购提供临时过桥贷款。

In 1986, to raise capital for these new activities and strengthen its position in world capital markets, Morgan Stanley sold 20 percent of its shares to the public. There was a wonderful irony here: it went public so it could take other companies private. To give these shares global distribution, a third of them were sold to buyers outside North America. The shares were offered in-house at \$15.33, with the allotted number per person determined by a complex formula based partly on the amount an employee already had invested in the firm. On its inaugural day, the Morgan Stanley stock jumped from \$56.50 a share to \$71.25. This netted over \$250 million for the firm and created instant fortunes for those irrevent young men who had transformed the firm in the early 1970s. Parker Gilbert had 772,133 shares worth \$57.3 million by day’s end (a rich reward for a compromise chairman); Dick Fisher had 729,574 shares (\$54.1 million); Bob Greenhill, 710,275 (\$52.7 million); and Lewis Bernard, 673,521 (\$50 million). With its public offering, Morgan Stanley lost one of its last links to the past. It was now a vast, publicly traded company, with 114 managing directors (including its first female), 148 limited partners, and 4,000 employees worldwide. The firm now employed an astonishing number of multimillionaires. Only Goldman, Sachs remained an old-fashioned Wall Street partnership, and even it sold a stake to Sumitomo of Japan.
1986 年，为筹措新业务所需资金并巩固其全球资本市场的地位，摩根士丹利将其 20%的股份公开发售。颇具讽刺意味的是：它通过上市募集资金，为的却是帮助其他公司私有化。为达成全球配售，其中三分之一的股份售予北美以外的买家。内部认购价为每股 15.33 美元，每人分得的配额由一个复杂的公式决定——员工既往对公司的投资额是重要参考之一。上市首日，摩根士丹利股价便从 56.50 美元飙升至 71.25 美元，为公司净赚超 2.5 亿美元，也让那些在 1970 年代初彻底扭转公司局面的离经叛道的年轻人一夜暴富。 Parker Gilbert 持有 772,133 股，当日收盘价值 5730 万美元（作为妥协的董事长，回报丰厚）；Dick Fisher 持有 729,574 股（5410 万美元）；Bob Greenhill 持有 710,275 股（5270 万美元）；Lewis Bernard 持有 673,521 股（5000 万美元）。上市后，摩根士丹利与过去的最后一丝联系也断裂了。如今它是一家庞大的上市公司，拥有 114 位董事总经理（包括首位女性）、148 位有限合伙人，以及全球 4000 名员工。这家公司现在雇用了数量惊人的百万富翁。 只有高盛仍然是老派的华尔街合伙制企业，而它甚至也向日本的住友银行出售了部分股权。

Bolstered by its public offering, Morgan Stanley pushed ahead with merchant banking. The next year, it launched the Morgan Stanley Leveraged Equity Fund II, with Tom Saunders eventually rounding up a \$2.2 billion war chest, the largest ever assembled by a major investment bank and second in the world after that of Kohlberg Kravis Roberts. In its new dual role, Morgan Stanley would both manage the fund and invest \$225 million of its own capital in it. As with hostile takeovers and junk bonds, Morgan Stanley took an activity of questionable benefit and made it acceptable in elite circles. It enlisted sixty institutions for its fund, including the General Motors and AT&T pension funds, Japanese trust companies, Middle East government agencies, Volvo, Barclays Bank, and several American commercial banks. Not only would Morgan Stanley receive about a third of the capital gains from the fund, but it would also skim off a 2-percent fee for managing the money. This was only the beginning, as the huge leveraged buyout of Burlington Industries would show.
得益于公开募股，摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）在商人银行业务上加速推进。次年，它推出了摩根士丹利杠杆股权基金二期（Morgan Stanley Leveraged Equity Fund II），汤姆·桑德斯（Tom Saunders）最终筹集了 22 亿美元的资金储备——这是大型投行有史以来规模最大的资金储备，仅次于科尔伯格-克拉维斯-罗伯茨（Kohlberg Kravis Roberts）位居全球第二。在这一双重角色下，摩根士丹利既负责管理该基金，又投入了 2.25 亿美元自有资金。正如其在敌意收购和垃圾债券领域的做法一样，摩根士丹利将一项备受争议的业务变得为精英阶层所接受。它为基金招募了 60 家机构投资者，包括通用汽车（General Motors）和 AT&T 的养老基金、日本信托公司、中东政府机构、沃尔沃（Volvo）、巴克莱银行（Barclays Bank）以及多家美国商业银行。摩根士丹利不仅将获得该基金约三分之一的资本收益，还会从中抽取 2%的管理费。 这仅仅是个开始。正如 Burlington Industries 那笔巨大的杠杆收购所表明的那样。

In early April 1987, reports surfaced that raider Asher B. Edelman was accumulating stock in Burlington, the largest textile company in America, based in Greensboro, North Carolina. Frank Greenberg, Burlington’s chief executive, cast about for a “white knight” to ward off Edelman and his partner, Dominion Textile of Canada. In a meticulous reconstruction of events published in an August 1987 issue of *Barron’s*, Benjamin J. Stein chronicled what happened next. On April 15, thirty-two-year-old Alan E. Goldberg of Morgan Stanley telephoned Greenberg and said Morgan would be interested in buying Burlington while retaining current management. In a follow-up letter of April 21, Bob Greenhill reinforced the naked appeal to Greenberg’s self-interest, saying, “We would have no interest in proceeding except upon a basis agreed to by your management.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2439969) At an April 29 meeting with Greenberg, Morgan Stanley laid out plans to give management a 10-percent stake in the buyout, plus another 10 percent if certain performance standards were met.
1987 年 4 月初，有消息称，激进投资者阿舍·B·埃德尔曼正在收购伯灵顿的股票。这家总部位于北卡罗来纳州格林斯博罗的公司，是美国最大的纺织企业。伯灵顿首席执行官弗兰克·格林伯格四处寻找“白衣骑士”，以抵御埃德尔曼及其合作伙伴加拿大 Dominion Textile 的攻势。在一篇发表于 1987 年 8 月《巴伦周刊》的详尽事件还原中，本杰明·J·斯坦记录了接下来发生的事。4 月 15 日，摩根士丹利 32 岁的艾伦·E·戈德堡致电格林伯格，表示摩根有意收购伯灵顿，同时保留现有管理层。在 4 月 21 日的后续信函中，鲍勃·格林希尔进一步直白地迎合格林伯格的私利，写道：“除非基于贵方管理层同意的条件，否则我们无意推进此事。”在 4 月 29 日与 Greenberg 的会议上，Morgan Stanley 提出了计划，给予管理层收购后 10%的股份，如果达到某些业绩标准，则再追加 10%。

Facing a clear-cut choice between a hostile raider, who threatened his livelihood, and the Morgan LBO fund, enticing him with lucrative incentives, could Frank Greenberg render a fair, impartial judgment for his shareholders? As Benjamin Stein noted, Morgan Stanley customarily dangled before management promises of salary increases ranging from 50 percent to 125 percent after a buyout. In this tempting situation, Greenberg granted some exceptional concessions to Morgan Stanley. He agreed to give Morgan a \$24-million “break-up” fee in the event it failed to acquire Burlington. Morgan Stanley justified this princely fee by citing interest it would allegedly forgo by locking up capital during the talks. Yet, as Stein noted, Morgan Stanley had no capital at risk until the takeover’s completion—and then only \$125 million of its own money. The break-up fee, however, worked out to interest that would have accrued on *\$7 billion* over a two-week period. As Stein concluded, “The ‘breakup’ fee could be understood only as a form of payoff to Morgan from its *partners* on the Burlington board for being included in the deal in the unlikely event that the deal cratered. It simply made no sense otherwise.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440118) Greenberg waited until mid-May before disclosing his secret talks with Morgan Stanley, which was privy to company secrets denied Asher Edelman and Dominion. It was hard to see how both bidder groups were being accorded equal treatment.
当弗兰克·格林伯格面临一个非此即彼的选择——一边是威胁他生计的恶意收购者，另一边是用丰厚激励诱惑他的摩根 LBO 基金——他能为股东做出公平公正的裁决吗？正如本杰明·斯坦所言，摩根士丹利通常会向管理层承诺，收购后薪酬将提升 50%至 125%。面对如此诱人的条件，格林伯格向摩根士丹利做出了若干特殊让步。他同意，若摩根未能成功收购伯灵顿，将支付其 2400 万美元的"分手费"。摩根士丹利声称，这笔巨额费用是为了补偿其在谈判期间锁定资金而放弃的利息。然而，正如斯坦所指出的，摩根士丹利在收购完成前并未承担任何资金风险——即便有，也仅是动用自有资金 1.25 亿美元。而分手费折算下来，相当于 70 亿美元资金在两周内产生的利息。 正如斯坦总结的那样：“‘分手费’只能被理解为伯灵顿董事会中的摩根合作伙伴为了让摩根加入交易，在交易万一失败时给予摩根的一种补偿。否则完全说不通。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440118) 格林伯格一直等到五月中旬才披露他与摩根士丹利的秘密会谈，而摩根士丹利掌握的公司机密是阿舍·埃德尔曼和多米尼恩无法得知的。很难看出两个竞标集团受到了平等对待。

In late June, the Morgan Stanley group made a bid of \$78 a share, or about \$2.4 billion, for Burlington, defeating the Edelman raid. It got about a third of America’s largest textile company for only \$125 million and even most of that came from Bankers Trust and Equitable Life Assurance. It also earned \$80 million in fees, including profits from underwriting almost \$2 billion in junk bonds to finance the deal. Did Burlington profit equally with Morgan Stanley from this financial alchemy? Before the buyout, the firm had a clean balance sheet, with debt less than half the value of common shareholders’ equity. When the LBO went through, the company suddenly struggled with over \$3 billion in debt, or *thirty times* as much debt as equity. It subsequently had to fire hundreds of middle-level managers, sell off the most advanced denim factory in the world (ironically, to Dominion Textile), close its research and development center, and starve its capital budget to \$50 million for a five-year period. All this not only upset the lives of Burlington employees, but drastically weakened the firm’s ability to compete in global markets.
6 月底，摩根士丹利集团以每股 78 美元、总价约 24 亿美元的价格收购伯灵顿，挫败了埃德尔曼的突袭。该集团仅用 1.25 亿美元就拿下了这家美国最大纺织品公司约三分之一的股份，而这笔钱大部分还来自银行家信托公司和公平人寿保险公司。此外，它还赚取了 8000 万美元的费用，其中包括承销近 20 亿美元垃圾债券为交易融资所获的利润。在这场金融魔法中，伯灵顿是否与摩根士丹利分得了同等的利益？在收购前，伯灵顿的资产负债表很健康，债务不到普通股股东权益的一半。而杠杆收购完成后，该公司突然背负了超过 30 亿美元的债务，债务是权益的 30 倍。 随后，该公司不得不裁减数百名中层管理人员，出售全球最先进的牛仔裤工厂（讽刺的是，买方是 Dominion Textile），关闭其研发中心，并将五年期间的资本预算压缩至 5000 万美元。这一切不仅打乱了伯灵顿员工的生活，更严重削弱了其在全球市场的竞争力。

By the last quarter of 1987, Burlington Industries was losing money despite higher earnings from operations. Why? It had to pay \$66 million in interest for the quarter. LBO defenders praised these high levels of debt as stimulating management to greater effort—an argument reminiscent of Dr. Johnson’s observation that hanging wonderfully concentrates the mind. Did companies have to stare at the gallows to perform better? Most cost cutting and asset sales that followed LBOs weren’t designed to improve company performance; they were just steps to pay for the LBOs and were often unnecessary without them. Many LBOs were quick bets on the bust-up values of firms rather than serious attempts to run a company for many years.
到 1987 年最后一个季度时，虽然伯灵顿工业的运营利润有所增长，但公司整体却在亏损。原因何在？该季度它必须支付 6600 万美元的利息。杠杆收购的拥趸们称赞这种高负债水平能激励管理层更加努力——这一论点让人联想到约翰逊博士的名言：绞刑能让人精神高度集中。难道公司非得盯着绞刑架才能表现更好吗？大多数杠杆收购后的削减成本和出售资产，并非旨在提升公司业绩；它们只是为了偿还杠杆收购的债务而采取的措施，如果没有杠杆收购，这些举措往往并无必要。许多杠杆收购不过是对公司拆分价值的短期投机，而非认真经营企业多年的长期尝试。

LBO supporters claimed that owners were more enterprising than managers and didn’t have to attend slavishly to their stock prices. The claim was parroted at every turn. “An enormous amount of management time in this country is devoted to managing the market price of the shares,” said Tom Saunders of Morgan Stanley. Yet for years, Bob Greenhill, Joe Fogg, and Eric Gleacher had warned companies to get up their share prices—or else. Who created that preoccupation with share prices and quarterly earnings from which companies were now being rescued? Ironically, many LBO specialists were being transferred over from merger work, where they suddenly had to adopt a long-term perspective. This defense also sounded odd coming from a firm that had managed the initial public offering of several companies in the 1980s. Finally, it ignored the simple fact that the objective of taking companies private was to take them public at a later date and reap a quick killing. Presumably, the firms would then tout the benefits of public ownership.
LBO 的支持者声称，所有者比管理者更具企业家精神，不必盲目关注公司股价。这种论调被到处重复。摩根士丹利的汤姆·桑德斯说："这个国家大量管理层的时间都花在管理股票的市场价格上。"然而多年来，鲍勃·格林希尔、乔·福格和埃里克·格莱彻一直警告公司要提升股价——否则后果自负。那么，是谁造成了这种对股价和季度业绩的过度关注——现在公司正从中被解救出来？讽刺的是，许多 LBO 专家正是从并购业务转岗而来，而并购领域突然需要他们采取长期视角。这种辩护听起来也很奇怪，因为这家公司本身在 20 世纪 80 年代曾为多家公司管理过首次公开募股。最后，它忽略了一个简单的事实：将公司私有化的目的，正是为了日后重新上市并快速大赚一笔。 可以想见，这些公司随后便会大肆宣扬公有制的种种好处。

There were profound political and social issues at stake in the LBO fad. Participants hailed the trend as a return to the “good old days” when bankers put their own capital at risk. They didn’t look closely at that history and the conflicts of interest that had resulted from excessive cosiness between bankers and the companies they financed. As we have seen, it took decades of agitation and reform to fulfill Louis Brandeis’s vision of investment bankers’ having an arm’s-length relationship with companies. This had been the purpose of endless investigations—Pujo, Pecora, and Wheeler—as well as the purpose of the Medina suit and various government measures to mandate competitive bidding in railroads and utilities. Just when the 1982 Rule 415 had seemed to end the problem of banker-company collusion, investment banks reinvented it with merchant banking. It was no coincidence that Morgan Stanley’s entry into merchant banking occurred after the advent of Rule 415, for through LBOs it could restore the exclusive relationships lost in the transactional age. What better hold to have on a company than to own a large piece of it? Three of the five Burlington board members were suddenly Morgan Stanley managing directors.
杠杆收购风潮背后，其实牵涉着深刻的政治与社会议题。参与者们欢呼这股风潮是重回“美好旧时光”——那时银行家们是拿自己的资本去冒险。但他们并未深究那段历史，也未看清银行家与所资助公司之间过度亲密所引发的利益冲突。正如我们所知，经过数十年的抗争与改革，才实现了路易斯·布兰代斯所倡导的——投资银行与公司之间保持“一臂之距”的关系。这正是无数次调查的目的所在——Pujo、Pecora 和 Wheeler 调查，也是 Medina 诉讼案以及政府强制铁路和公用事业采用竞价招标的各项措施的初衷。正当 1982 年 415 条款似乎终结了银行与公司勾结的问题时，投资银行却又通过商人银行业务让其死灰复燃。 摩根士丹利在 415 规则出台后进入商人银行业务并非巧合，因为通过杠杆收购，它可以重新建立交易时代所失去的排他性关系。还有什么能比持有公司大量股份更能掌控其命运呢？伯灵顿公司董事会的五名成员中，突然有三名是摩根士丹利的董事总经理。

The merger of industry and finance had made some sense in the Baronial Age and had given some stability to the American economy. Companies were then weak and had difficulty tapping capital markets, especially abroad. Only the banker’s reputation could reassure skittish creditors. That was no longer the case in the Casino Age, when companies were often better known than bankers. Burlington Industries needed no introduction to investors. The new merchant banking also differed from the old-fashioned relationship banking of the 1950s, when Wall Street firms had a purely advisory role and could provide objective advice. If the old Morgan Stanley had a firm grip on its clients, it also had no built-in temptation to mislead or abuse them.
产融结合在贵族时代曾有一定意义，为美国经济带来了一些稳定性。当时企业实力薄弱，难以进入资本市场，尤其是在海外。只有银行家的声誉才能安抚那些紧张的债权人。但在赌场时代，情况已截然不同，企业往往比银行家更知名。伯灵顿工业公司无需向投资者做过多介绍。新兴的商人银行业务也与 20 世纪 50 年代传统的关系型银行业务不同——那时华尔街公司仅扮演纯粹顾问角色，能提供客观建议。老摩根士丹利虽对客户握有牢固控制权，却也缺乏误导或滥用客户的内在诱因。

The LBO trend ensnared investment banks in another set of potential conflicts of interest. Were they now advisers or investors? Were the roles of principal and agent compatible? LBOs put investment banks into the position of competing against their underwriting clients. After Morgan Stanley added Fort Howard Paper to a portfolio that already included the Container Corporation of America, its paper-industry clients were aghast. David J. McKittrick, the chief financial officer of James River, had used Morgan Stanley to manage a number of stock offerings. “We have looked with concern at Morgan Stanley’s increasing equity position in the paper industry,” he said.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440244) There were similar problems with takeover clients. If Morgan Stanley’s merger people spied an undervalued company, should they take it to a client or keep it for the firm? And if they recommended an LBO to a company, could they claim objectivity when the firm stood to make windfall profits in advisory fees and junk bond underwritings? According to one report, CIGNA insurance stayed away from the second Morgan Stanley LBO fund from a belief that the firm was chasing “elephant” deals that would generate giant fees.
LBO（杠杆收购）趋势使投资银行陷入另一重潜在利益冲突：它们究竟是顾问还是投资者？委托人与代理人的角色能否兼容？LBO 让投资银行不得不与自己的承销客户同台竞争。当摩根士丹利将霍华德纸业纳入已包含美国容器公司的投资组合时，其纸业客户大为震惊。詹姆斯河公司的首席财务官戴维·J·麦基特里克曾委托摩根士丹利管理多起股票发行，他坦言：“我们对摩根士丹利在纸业领域不断增加的股权仓位深感忧虑。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440244) 收购业务同样问题频出——若摩根士丹利的并购团队发现某家被低估的公司，是该推荐给客户，还是留为己用？ 如果他们向一家公司推荐杠杆收购（LBO），那么当该公司在咨询费和垃圾债券承销中获取暴利时，他们还能声称自己保持客观吗？据一份报告显示，CIGNA 保险公司之所以未参与摩根士丹利的第二支 LBO 基金，是因为他们认为该机构正在追逐能产生巨额费用的“大象级”交易。

People at Morgan Stanley recognized the potential problem but too quickly waved it away. “Our business is full of conflicts,” said Dick Fisher. “In an industry as integrated as ours, I don’t see how they can be avoided.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440392) He noted that if Morgan Stanley scouted a company for a client, that client had first crack. But what if the firm spied an opportunity on its own initiative? Was its first loyalty to its clients? Or to its shareholders? Bob Greenhill replied, “The first party that steps forward is the client; we talked to several companies about Burlington, but none were interested. The point is, because of our tradition, we bend over backwards to accommodate our traditional clients.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440540) So Morgan Stanley would save the scraps for itself and only take deals rejected by everybody else? Wall Street was now tangled so deep in ethical thickets that it could no longer fight its way out.
摩根士丹利的人意识到了这个潜在问题，但很快就把它抛到脑后。“我们的业务充满冲突，”迪克·费舍尔说，“在我们这样一个高度整合的行业里，我看不出怎么避免这些冲突。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440392) 他指出，如果摩根士丹利替客户物色一家公司，客户有优先选择权。可如果公司主动发现了机会呢？它首先要忠诚于客户？还是忠诚于股东？鲍勃·格林希尔回应道：“第一个站出来的就是客户；我们跟好几家公司谈过伯灵顿的事，但没人感兴趣。关键在于，基于我们的传统，我们会竭尽全力照顾老客户。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440540) 这么说，摩根士丹利只能捡别人挑剩下的，只接手那些没人要的交易？华尔街如今深陷道德困境，难以自拔。

THE parallel with the 1920s had its inevitable sequel on October 19, 1987, when the Dow Jones industrial average fell 508 points. Some \$500 billion in paper value—equal to the gross national product of France—vanished, though there were no untoward scenes at the Corner or mobs of ruined investors. No stock brokers executed swan dives from high ledges. Seventy percent of stock trading was now done by institutions—mutual funds, pension funds, and the like—which were averse to theatrics and tracked the market on computer screens. Depression, migraine headaches, even sexual impotence were later reported among investors, but no aerial artistry as in 1929. Aside from a somewhat longer visitors’ queue at the New York Stock Exchange, the Corner betrayed little sense of calamity on this Black Monday.
与 20 世纪 20 年代的相似性在 1987 年 10 月 19 日不可避免地重现，当时 Dow Jones industrial average 暴跌 508 点。约 5000 亿美元的账面价值——相当于法国的国民生产总值——瞬间蒸发，尽管在华尔街街头并未出现混乱场面，也没有破产投资者的骚乱。没有股票经纪人从高楼跳下。当时 70%的股票交易由机构进行——共同基金、养老基金等——它们厌恶戏剧化，通过电脑屏幕追踪市场。后来有报道称投资者出现了抑郁、偏头痛甚至阳痿等症状，但并没有像 1929 年那样的空中表演。除了纽约证券交易所的参观队伍稍长一些外，在这个 Black Monday，华尔街几乎看不出灾难的迹象。

The Morgan houses were actually less remote from the 1987 crash than they’d been from the one in 1929. All the banks and brokerage houses were now trading operations. And Morgan Stanley was a major practitioner of stock-index arbitrage—computer-driven trades that exploited small price discrepancies between stocks in New York and stock-index futures in Chicago. Such transactions were blamed for wild market gyrations and even, unfairly, for the crash itself. In a secret, restricted computer room known as the Black Box for its sophisticated software—some programs forced traders to don 3-D glasses—fifty Morgan Stanley traders and analysts pored over information and scanned arbitrage opportunities. They took risks that would have harrowed Harold Stanley’s soul. On September 11, 1986, after the market suffered a steep drop, Morgan Stanley, gambling on an upturn, had bought *\$1 billion* in stock futures and suffered enormous losses. Such futures-related trading reintroduced leverage into the market that the government thought it had stamped out with stiffer margin requirements in the 1930s.
摩根家族与 1987 年股灾的距离，实际上比他们与 1929 年股灾的距离更近。当时所有银行和经纪公司都已涉足交易业务。摩根士丹利更是股票指数套利的主要实践者——这种由计算机驱动的交易，利用纽约股票与芝加哥股指期货之间的微小价差获利。这类交易被指是市场剧烈波动的元凶，甚至不公正地被认为是股灾的始作俑者。在一间被称为"黑匣子"的秘密限制级计算机房（因其复杂软件而得名，有些程序甚至要求交易员佩戴 3D 眼镜），五十名摩根士丹利的交易员和分析师埋头研究信息，搜寻套利机会。他们承担的风险足以让哈罗德·斯坦利灵魂颤抖。1986 年 9 月 11 日，市场大幅下跌后，摩根士丹利押注反弹，买入了 10 亿美元的股票期货，结果遭受了巨额亏损。 这种与期货相关的交易重新将杠杆引入市场，而政府原以为已在 20 世纪 30 年代通过更严格的保证金要求将其彻底消除。

From 1984 to 1987, stock prices had risen without a 10-percent correction. This cheered bulls, muzzled bears, and blinded people to warning signals. Following exactly the 1929 pattern, the bond market slumped in the spring of 1987, and the Federal Reserve raised the discount rate in September. In early October, Morgan Stanley, fearing clients would miss the next leg of the bull market, exhorted them to be *100-percent* invested in common stock. Later, when it sent financial commentator Adam Smith an exuberant buy recommendation, he mused, “You had me one hundred percent invested in October and I lost half my money. How am I supposed to buy something now?”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440666)
从 1984 年到 1987 年，股市持续上涨，从未出现过 10%的回调。这令多头欢欣鼓舞，空头哑口无言，也让人们对警示信号视而不见。完全遵循 1929 年的模式，债券市场在 1987 年春季暴跌，联邦储备委员会于 9 月上调了贴现率。10 月初，摩根士丹利担心客户会错过牛市的下一波行情，敦促他们百分百投资于普通股。随后，当它向财经评论员亚当·史密斯发出热烈的买入建议时，他沉思道：“你在 10 月让我百分百投入，结果我损失了一半的钱。现在我还怎么买？” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440666)

Where 1929 was a home-grown American crash, 1987 was a global panic. Around the world, stocks rose, crashed, then rebounded together. The same financial deregulation that had interlaced markets led to synchronized drops in Tokyo, Hong Kong, New York, London, Paris, and Zurich. “For days everyone just kept passing the bear market around the time clock,” said Barton Biggs of Morgan Stanley. New links among world stock markets seemed to exaggerate movements in both directions, accentuating the instability of the world financial system instead of ironing out fluctuations.
1929 年是美国本土爆发的危机，而 1987 年则是全球性的恐慌。全球股市同时上涨、崩盘、再反弹。同样的金融放松管制使得市场紧密相连，导致东京、香港、纽约、伦敦、巴黎和苏黎世同步暴跌。“有好几天，大家就像在时钟接力传递熊市一样，”摩根士丹利的巴顿·比格斯说道。全球股市之间的新联系似乎放大了双向的波动，非但没有熨平波动，反而加剧了全球金融体系的不稳定性。

From the standpoint of Morgan history, the significant aspect of Black Monday was the lack of any overt role in a rescue. *That* was the big script difference from 1929—the absence of a bankers’ rescue. President Reagan, eager to echo Hoover, said, “the underlying economy remains sound.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440818) John Phelan, the New York Stock Exchange chairman, played the Richard Whitney role, debating with advisers about whether to close the Exchange. There were again stock buybacks and early Exchange closings to deal with paperwork. But no bankers marched up the steps of 23 Wall. Phelan consulted mostly with William Schreyer of Merrill Lynch and John Gutfreund of Salomon Brothers—not with Morgan Stanley—reflecting the new importance of trading and retail houses, rank outsiders to the club in 1929.
从摩根的历史角度来看，黑色星期一的一个重要方面是摩根在救援中并未扮演明显的角色。这与 1929 年的剧本大相径庭——没有银行家出面救援。里根总统急于效仿胡佛，表示“基本经济面依然稳健”。纽约证券交易所主席约翰·费伦扮演了理查德·惠特尼的角色，与顾问们争论是否要关闭交易所。同样出现了股票回购和提前收盘以处理文书工作的情况。但没有银行家踏上华尔街 23 号的台阶。费伦主要咨询了美林证券的威廉·施雷尔和所罗门兄弟的约翰·古特弗罗因德，而不是摩根士丹利，这反映出交易和零售机构的新重要性，这些公司在 1929 年还是华尔街俱乐部的局外人。

The Federal Reserve moved with a dispatch that left no doubt as to its resolve. On October 20, Alan Greenspan issued a tersely effective statement affirming the Fed’s “readiness to serve as a source of liquidity to support the economic and financial system.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440969) The Fed bought dollars and engineered a sharp drop in interest rates. E. Gerald Corrigan, president of the New York Fed, personally pleaded with banks to continue lending to sound securities firms. Some New York Stock Exchange specialists ended Black Monday with bloated stock inventories as they tried to stem the decline, and they were floated by their chief lenders, including Morgan Guaranty, Manufacturers Hanover, and the Bank of New York. The Fed acted admirably; Wall Street no longer needed a House of Morgan, which couldn’t have coped with a crisis of such magnitude. As Continental Illinois had showed, private rescues were passe in huge, unfettered global markets. There would never be another financier who bulked as large as Pierpont Morgan or Tom Lamont.
美联储迅速采取行动，其决心不容置疑。10 月 20 日，艾伦·格林斯潘发布了一份简短而有力的声明，重申美联储“随时准备提供流动性，以支持经济和金融体系”。 [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2440969) 美联储买入美元，并促使利率大幅下降。纽约联邦储备银行行长 E·杰拉尔德·科里根亲自恳请银行继续向稳健的证券公司放贷。一些纽约证券交易所的专业交易员在黑色星期一收盘时因试图遏制跌势而库存大量股票，随后得到了其主要贷款机构——包括摩根担保、汉华实业和纽约银行——的纾困。美联储的表现令人敬佩；华尔街不再需要摩根银行，因为它根本无法应对如此大规模的危机。正如伊利诺伊大陆银行所显示的，在庞大且不受约束的全球市场中，私人救助早已过时。 再也不会出现像皮尔庞特·摩根或汤姆·拉蒙特那样举足轻重的金融家了。

The crash exposed underwriting hazards of a sort forgotten during the bull market. In November 1987, when the British government sold its 32-percent stake in British Petroleum, investors couldn’t absorb this mammoth \$13.2-billion issue so soon after Black Monday. Four U.S. underwriters—Morgan Stanley, Goldman, Sachs; Salomon Brothers; and Shearson Lehman—stared at \$350 million in paper losses. Disaster was averted when the Bank of England agreed to buy back shares at 70 pence per share. Higher oil prices and a Kuwaiti purchase of a 20-percent BP stake then rescued the Bank of England.
这场崩盘暴露了牛市期间已被人们遗忘的承销风险。1987 年 11 月，当英国政府出售其所持英国石油公司 32%的股份时，投资者在黑色星期一过后根本无法消化这笔高达 132 亿美元的巨额发行。四家美国承销商——Morgan Stanley、Goldman Sachs、Salomon Brothers 和 Shearson Lehman——面临 3.5 亿美元的账面亏损。最终，英格兰银行同意以每股 70 便士回购股票，才避免了这场灾难。随后，油价上涨以及科威特购入英国石油 20%的股份，又解救了英格兰银行。

After the crash, Main Street again prayed that Wall Street had learned its lesson and gloated over its misery. “It’s been a nice week for the have-nots of the world,” said a GM auto worker. Stock brokers retired the haloes they had worn for several years. “Before the Crash,” bemoaned one broker, “everyone wanted you to meet their daughter. Now we’re the scum of the earth.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441122) But the hobgoblins of sustained prosperity—consumerism, greed, and speculation—weren’t to be slain so quickly. When a *Newsweek* cover asked, “IS THE PARTY OVER?” the unrepentant young staffers at Morgan Stanley used a copy of the glossy page as an invitation to a big bash, with the letters *NFW*—no fucking way—scrawled defiantly across the top.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441266) Eric Gleacher, whose merger mill was grinding through two hundred deals at a time, asked, “Why shouldn’t it go on?”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441410) Morgan Stanley was proudly unscathed by the crash—it even offered to extend credit to one thunderstruck commercial bank—but the staff was shaken by a nearly 20-point drop in the firm’s own newly issued stock.
崩盘后，普通民众再次祈祷华尔街已吸取教训，对其惨状幸灾乐祸。“对全世界的穷人们来说，这周真不错，”一位通用汽车工人说道。股票经纪人们收起了他们佩戴多年的光环。“崩盘前，”一位经纪人哀叹道，“人人都想让你见见他们的女儿。现在我们成了人渣。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441122) 但持续繁荣的梦魇——消费主义、贪婪和投机——可不会这么快就被击退。当《新闻周刊》的封面发问“派对结束了吗？”时，摩根士丹利那些不知悔改的年轻员工竟用这本光鲜杂志的复制页当作大型派对的邀请函，并在顶部挑衅地潦草写上“NFW——没门儿”。 [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441266) 埃里克·格莱彻的并购机器当时正同时处理两百笔交易，他问道：“为什么不该继续呢？” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441410) 摩根士丹利在这次崩盘中自豪地毫发未损——它甚至主动向一家惊慌失措的商业银行提供信贷——然而，员工们却因公司自己新发行的股票暴跌近 20 个点而人心惶惶。

Because the crash didn’t usher in a recession, the public wasn’t immediately galvanized into reform. Yet financial reform had lagged behind the 1929 crash by three to four years, arising only after prolonged Depression exposed the full economic consequences of the 1920s speculation. The closest analogy to the post-1929 outcry over pools and short selling was the controversy over computerized program trading. Once again there was a tendency to trace the crash to the internal mechanics of the market itself. In January 1988, Merrill Lynch, Shearson Lehman Hutton, and Goldman, Sachs suspended index arbitrage trading for their own accounts. But Morgan Stanley didn’t need to worry about angry small investors and exhibited its new renegade stance, despite the fact that Parker Gilbert was a governor of the New York Stock Exchange. It suspended its own “proprietary” program trading only after pressure from Congressman Edward J. Markey’s Subcommittee on Telecommunications and Finance. It was also notified by Maurice R. Greenberg, chief executive of the American International Group, a New York insurer, that his firm would cease business with houses that persisted in stock-index arbitrage for their own accounts.
由于股市崩盘并未引发经济衰退，公众并未立即被推动进行改革。然而，金融改革在 1929 年崩盘后滞后了三到四年，直到长期的经济大萧条揭示了 20 世纪 20 年代投机活动的全部经济后果后才得以展开。与 1929 年后对坐庄和卖空行为的强烈抗议最相似的是后来对计算机程序化交易的争议。人们再次倾向于将崩盘归咎于市场自身的内部机制。1988 年 1 月，美林、希尔森·雷曼·赫顿和高盛暂停了自身账户的指数套利交易。但摩根士丹利无需担心愤怒的小投资者，展现出其新生的叛逆姿态，尽管帕克·吉尔伯特当时是纽约证券交易所的董事。直到国会议员爱德华·J·马基的电信与金融小组委员会施加压力后，摩根士丹利才暂停了自己的“自营”程序化交易。 莫里斯·R·格林伯格，这家纽约保险公司美国国际集团的首席执行官，也同时通知说，他的公司将停止与那些坚持为自己账户进行股票指数套利的公司开展业务。

On May 10, 1988, in a splashy coordinated effort, Morgan Stanley, Salomon Brothers, Bear Stearns, Paine Webber, and Kidder, Peabody announced they would stop the practice. Morgan Stanley had apparently orchestrated the move by alerting the others to its plans. Blocked from program trading in the United States, Morgan Stanley then went to Japan that December and caused a furor by introducing the practice there, spurring the Tokyo exchange to a record high. In 1989, it unabashedly led the resumption of program trading among the major firms after only a nine-month hiatus. The question of whether program trading increases volatility is complex and unresolved. What is worth noting is Morgan Stanley’s apparent disregard of public opinion and greater willingness to defy financial authorities—behavior conspicuously at odds with Morgan Guaranty’s strong sense of corporate citizenship.
1988 年 5 月 10 日，摩根士丹利、所罗门兄弟、贝尔斯登、佩恩韦伯、基德尔·皮博迪等公司高调联手，宣布将停止这一做法。摩根士丹利显然率先通知了其他公司，从而策划了这次行动。程序化交易在美国受阻后，摩根士丹利于同年 12 月转战日本，将这一做法引入当地，引发轩然大波，并推动东京交易所创下历史新高。1989 年，仅隔九个月，它又毫无愧色地牵头在各大公司中恢复了程序化交易。程序化交易是否加剧市场波动，这一问题复杂且尚无定论。值得关注的是，摩根士丹利明显漠视舆论，且更勇于挑战金融监管机构——这种作风与摩根保证所秉持的强烈企业公民意识形成了鲜明对比。

If Main Street hoped that Black Monday would throw a cautionary fear into Wall Street, it was dead wrong. It only prodded the Street into more assertive, reckless behavior. Securities firms had already lost many safe, reliable businesses. Brokerage commissions dropped after Mayday in 1975 and underwriting margins shrank after Rule 415 in 1982. Now trading profits plunged with the crash, creating a large contraction in Wall Street and City employment. Mergers already accounted for half of Wall Street’s profits, and with the decline of other opportunities, the attraction to such business became irresistible. To help things along, the crash and a weaker dollar created bargain prices for takeovers. And if raiders didn’t materialize, Wall Street was prepared to do the raids itself through its new merchant-banking departments and junk bond syndicates.
如果普通民众以为“黑色星期一”会让华尔街心生警惕，那就大错特错了。这场暴跌非但没让华尔街收敛，反而刺激它变得更加肆无忌惮、铤而走险。证券公司的传统安全业务早已流失殆尽：1975 年“五月节”后经纪佣金大幅缩水，1982 年“415 规则”又令承销利润急剧收窄。如今股灾导致交易利润暴跌，华尔街和伦敦金融城的就业岗位随之大幅萎缩。并购业务当时已占据华尔街利润的半壁江山，其他机会日渐凋零，这块业务的吸引力自然难以抗拒。更助推这一趋势的是，股灾和美元贬值让收购标的变得异常廉价。即便野蛮人没有现身，华尔街也早已备好后手——通过新设的商人银行部门和垃圾债券集团，亲自披挂上阵。

So many companies had already passed through the merger mill that it was becoming harder to find suitable candidates. Everybody was scrutinizing the same lists, often with the same techniques. One solution was to target industries that by law or custom had been immune to hostile takeovers. Eric Gleacher, and all of Morgan Stanley, now overturned a sacred financial taboo: thou shalt not mount a hostile raid against a bank. It was always thought such raids might shake depositor confidence or jeopardize the dividends of the proverbial widows and orphans who held bank stock. So in the past, bank mergers were either friendly or forced on troubled banks by regulatory authorities.
众多公司早已历经并购潮的洗礼，寻找合适的收购对象变得愈发困难。大家都在用相似的手法审视同一份候选清单。一个突破口是瞄准那些依法或依惯例免受恶意收购的行业。如今，埃里克·格里彻与整个摩根士丹利打破了一条神圣的金融禁忌：不得对银行发起恶意收购。过去人们普遍认为，这样的收购可能动摇储户信心，或危及那些持有银行股票的孤儿寡母们的股息收益。因此，以往的银行并购要么是友好协商，要么是由监管机构强制介入问题银行。

Shortly before the crash, this etiquette was shattered when the Bank of New York launched a \$1-billion raid against Irving Trust. It was a clash of two hoary institutions steeped in history. The Bank of New York was founded by Alexander Hamilton and his associates in 1784—no other bank had operated so long with the same name—while Irving was named after Washington Irving and dated from the mid-nineteenth century. The Bank of New York was advised by Eric Gleacher of Morgan Stanley and H. Rodgin Cohen of Sullivan and Cromwell and Irving Trust by Goldman, Sachs and Morgan Guaranty.
就在崩盘前不久，纽约银行对欧文信托发起了一场 10 亿美元的突袭收购，彻底打破了这场礼仪的宁静。这是两家历史悠久的古老机构之间的碰撞。纽约银行由亚历山大·汉密尔顿及其合伙人于 1784 年创立——没有哪家银行能以同样的名字运营如此之久——而欧文信托则以华盛顿·欧文命名，其历史可追溯至 19 世纪中期。纽约银行聘请了摩根士丹利的埃里克·格里彻和苏利文·克伦威尔的 H·罗金·科恩担任顾问，欧文信托则请来了高盛和摩根担保公司。

The year-long battle showed just how vicious and merciless Wall Street had become. These were genteel, old-line banks, the sort that pampered rich, elderly clients. Irving was so sleepy that it had sat out the trend for foreign-exchange trading that was a boon to other commercial banks. It seldom fired people and usually gave everybody a fat Christmas bonus equal to 15 percent of salary.
这场持续一年的鏖战，充分展现了华尔街已变得多么凶残无情。这两家银行都是温文尔雅的老牌机构，专为富有的老年客户提供贴心服务。欧文信托经营得如此懒散，以至于错过了外汇交易的热潮——而这一趋势本是其他商业银行的福音。它很少解雇员工，并且每年都会给所有人发放相当于年薪 15%的丰厚圣诞奖金。

Bank of New York chairman J. Carter Bacot initiated the Irving raid because he feared his own bank would be swallowed by another firm. In their gorgeous art deco headquarters at 1 Wall Street, Irving executives first scoffed at the bid, sure the Fed would never countenance a hostile bank takeover. Gleacher and Cohen correctly predicted that Fed chairman Alan Greenspan would break the precedent. The Bank of New York stalked Irving with lawsuits, proxy fights, and strident exchanges of press releases. After the Bank of New York won, Gleacher quickly threw fear across the economic landscape, telling the press the hostile fight was “a precursor of things to come.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441564) The Bank of New York had promised to pay for the takeover through normal attrition. By early 1989, it had begun to fire one hundred people from the foreign side. As an Irving executive said: “The bloodbath has begun.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441704) No form of financial marauding was any longer off-limits to Morgan Stanley.
纽约银行董事长 J. Carter Bacot 发起对欧文银行的收购战，是因为他担心自己的银行会被其他公司吞并。在华尔街 1 号那座装饰艺术风格的华丽总部里，欧文银行的高管们起初对这笔收购嗤之以鼻，坚信美联储绝不会容忍恶意收购银行。Gleacher 和 Cohen 精准预判，美联储主席 Alan Greenspan 会打破先例。纽约银行步步紧逼，官司、代理权之争、凌厉的新闻稿交锋轮番上阵。纽约银行胜出后，Gleacher 迅速在全经济领域投下恐慌，向媒体宣称这场恶意收购是“未来之事的先兆”。 [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441564) 纽约银行曾承诺通过自然减员来完成收购。然而到 1989 年初，他们已开始从海外部门裁掉一百人。正如一位欧文银行高管所言：“大清洗已经开始了。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441704) 从此，摩根士丹利在金融掠夺上再无任何禁忌。

IN the wake of Black Monday, a new spirit was also blowing through 23 Wall. As it evolved from a purely commercial bank into a novel hybrid with many investment banking activities, its character changed. At an opulent postcrash Christmas party, Lew Preston said he was pleased to see so many spouses present, because now they would know that their husbands and wives would be working late over the coming year. At a second reception the next evening, he said he wanted to make sure everyone could hear him because the night before “some people didn’t hear me and went home happy.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441856) As if to underscore new perils facing the bank, Moody’s Investors Service dropped its triple-A rating for J. P. Morgan and Company, the holding company of Morgan Guaranty. Standard and Poor’s kept its triple-A rating for the holding company, and both still honored Morgan Guaranty Trust with the only triple-A rating for a large American bank. Moody’s action was a small, but noticeable dent in Morgan’s shining armor.
黑色星期一过后，华尔街 23 号也迎来了一股新风。随着这家银行从纯粹的商业银行转型为融合诸多投行业务的新型机构，其性质也发生了变化。在灾后奢华的圣诞派对上，卢·普雷斯顿表示很高兴看到众多配偶出席，因为现在大家会明白，未来一年他们的丈夫或妻子都将频繁加班。次日晚间的第二场招待会上，他特意提高音量说道，要确保所有人都能听清，因为前一晚"有人没听清我说的话，高高兴兴地回家了"。 [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441856) 仿佛要凸显银行面临的新风险，穆迪投资者服务公司取消了摩根公司（摩根担保的控股公司）的 AAA 评级。标准普尔则维持了该控股公司的 AAA 评级，且两家机构均仍将摩根担保信托公司评为唯一一家获此最高评级的大型美国银行。 穆迪的举动，就像是在摩根那闪亮的盔甲上，留下了一道虽小却显眼的划痕。

As J. P. Morgan and Company grew into a fifteen-thousand-person conglomerate, it tried to preserve the old partnership flavor. New management trainees underwent a rigorous six-month training program meant to acculturate them to the bank. Rarely did 23 Wall recruit from outside, and it spent millions annually on free lunches to promote camaraderie. There remained an ethic of not stealing credit or upstaging colleagues. “The Morgan feeling of collegiality is the most important thing we’ve got,” said former Morgan president Rod Lindsay. Preston regularly sent memos admonishing employees that they worked for clients and the bank, not for themselves. In Lindsay’s words, “We don’t want people if they’re going to be by themselves at the top of the list all the time.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2441999)
随着 J. P. Morgan 公司发展成拥有 1.5 万名员工的大型企业，它依然努力维系着昔日合伙制的氛围。新入职的管理培训生要经历长达六个月的严格培训，以适应银行的文化。华尔街 23 号很少从外部招人，每年还要花上百万美元提供免费午餐，以促进同事间的友情。这里有一种不成文的规矩：不抢功，也别抢同事的风头。前摩根总裁罗德·林赛曾表示：“摩根那种同僚之间的情谊，是我们最宝贵的东西。”普雷斯顿时常发备忘录告诫员工，他们是为客户和银行工作，而不是为自己。用林赛的话说：“要是一心只想自己独占鳌头，我们这儿可不需要这样的人。”

Morgan Guaranty had been much more respectful of tradition than Morgan Stanley or Morgan Grenfell and was recognizably the heir of the old, aristocratic House of Morgan. Yet its unique civility was threatened. The bank was engaged in a full dress rehearsal for the day when it would again be a “universal” bank, like the old House of Morgan. At home, it fused commercial lending and capital market activities into a corporate finance group that was an embryonic investment bank. It specialized in “deals that are sufficiently complex that they don’t lend themselves to the commercial paper market,” according to Preston.[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442168) It milked every legal securities business. It dealt in Treasury bonds, underwrote municipal bonds, advised cities, provided stock research and brokerage, and traded in gold bullion, silver, and foreign exchange. It continued to have the fanciest private banking operation, wooing rich customers with ads that promised to relieve their anxiety about possessing \$50-million portfolios.
摩根担保公司比摩根士丹利或摩根建富更加尊重传统，显然是古老贵族摩根家族的继承人。然而，它独有的儒雅风范正面临威胁。这家银行正在进行一场全面的预演，为重返"全能银行"模式做准备——就像当年的摩根老店那样。在国内，它将商业贷款和资本市场业务融合成一个企业金融部门，这堪称投资银行的雏形。据普雷斯顿所言，它专攻那些"复杂到不适合商业票据市场的交易"。它充分利用每一块合法的证券业务：买卖国债、承销市政债券、为城市提供咨询、开展股票研究与经纪服务，同时还交易金条、白银和外汇。 它的私人银行业务依然最为高端，用承诺缓解客户对持有 5000 万美元投资组合焦虑的广告，吸引着富豪客户。

Abroad, Morgans stepped up capital market activity, specializing in interest-rate swaps, currency swaps, and other financial esoterica. It had merchant banks in Japan, Hong Kong, Belgium, and Germany. In London, the headquarters of its global capital markets operation, it spent \$500 million to outfit two historic buildings near the Thames—the vacant Guildhall School of Music and Drama and the City of London School for Girls—with vast trading floors. Eventually fifteen hundred employees would work beneath stained-glass windows and timbered ceilings, flanked by busts of Shakespeare and Milton. As Preston noted ruefully, “We may be in the ironic position of becoming a global securities firm without being able to underwrite in our own market.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442329)
在海外，摩根士丹利加大了资本市场活动力度，专注于利率互换、货币互换及其他金融衍生品。它在日本、香港、比利时和德国都设有商人银行。在伦敦——其全球资本市场业务的总部所在地——它斥资 5 亿美元，将泰晤士河畔两座历史建筑（闲置的市政厅音乐戏剧学院和伦敦金融城女子学校）改造成了宽敞的交易大厅。最终，1500 名员工将在彩色玻璃窗和木梁天花板下工作，两侧摆放着莎士比亚和弥尔顿的半身像。正如普雷斯顿不无感慨地指出：“我们可能身处一个颇具讽刺意味的境地：成为一家全球性证券公司，却无法在自己的市场上承销业务。”

To mark its emancipation from commercial banking, the House of Morgan increasingly substituted J. P. Morgan in its advertising for Morgan Guaranty, the old—and now shrinking—bank core. Morgan Guaranty Limited in London was rechristened J. P. Morgan Securities. Morgans had to reconstitute commercial bankers as deal makers and market men. It probably spent more on educating employees than any other bank. “We run almost a small university here,” said group executive Robert Engel.[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442478) The bank hoped to maintain its gentlemanly culture by being brainy and innovative. Yet it wasn’t clear that new products could be peddled with the old style. A lending officer could be a gentleman or a lady. But what about a currency trader? A takeover artist?
为宣告从商业银行体系中独立，摩根银行在广告中逐渐用"J. P. 摩根"替代了原先的核心品牌"摩根担保"——后者正日渐萎缩。其伦敦子公司"摩根担保有限公司"也更名为"J. P. 摩根证券"。摩根必须将商业银行家重新塑造成交易撮合者与市场操盘手。它在员工培训上的投入很可能超过任何一家银行。"我们这里简直像在办一所小型大学，"集团高管罗伯特·恩格尔表示。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442478) 该行希望凭借智慧与创新维系其绅士文化。然而，旧式作风能否兜售新产品尚未可知。贷款专员可以是绅士或淑女，但外汇交易员呢？并购专家呢？

Takeover work was the acid test of the new Morgans. Until the late 1960s, it had provided such advice *gratis* as part of a total package. This informal operation—just a file on buyers and sellers—was offered to clients in exchange for million-dollar deposits. As early as 1973, Morgans had tried to systematize this and even picked up a \$600,000 fee for arranging the takeover of Gimbel Brothers by British-American Tobacco. But the operation never got off the ground. As with Morgan Stanley, the bank was hampered by its long client list and found it hard to represent one client without injuring another. As a commercial bank, it still wanted to please everybody.
收购业务是对新摩根集团的严峻考验。直至 20 世纪 60 年代末，该行一直将此类咨询作为打包服务的一部分免费提供。这套非正式操作——仅凭一份买卖双方名录——便换取了客户数百万美元的保证金。早在 1973 年，摩根就曾尝试将此业务系统化，甚至因撮合英美烟草公司收购金贝尔兄弟公司而收取了 60 万美元费用。但这项业务始终未能真正开展起来。与摩根士丹利如出一辙，该行受制于庞大的客户名单，发现代表一位客户就难免得罪另一位。作为商业银行，它仍想取悦所有人。

The problem was glaringly exposed in 1979, when American Express attempted to take over the McGraw-Hill publishing empire. AmEx chairman James D. Robinson III—a Morgan alumnus who’d been an assistant to Tom Gates when he was chairman in the 1960s—turned to the bank to finance the takeover. At first, Robinson thought he could arrange a friendly merger. Instead, he touched off a furious reaction from Harold W. McGraw, Jr., who called in Yerger Johnstone of Morgan Stanley and defense lawyer Martin Lipton and unleashed a blistering counterattack.
1979 年，一个尖锐的问题暴露无遗：美国运通试图收购麦格劳-希尔出版帝国。运通董事长詹姆斯·D·罗宾逊三世——这位摩根出身、曾在 20 世纪 60 年代担任汤姆·盖茨董事长助理的高管——转向该银行寻求收购融资。起初，罗宾逊以为可以安排一场友好合并。然而，他反而激起了小哈罗德·W·麦格劳的强烈反应，后者召来了摩根士丹利的耶格·约翰斯通和辩护律师马丁·利普顿，发起了猛烈的反击。

McGraw picked up any brickbat at hand. He sued American Express for libel, said it cooperated with the Arab boycott of Israel, asked the Federal Communications Commission and the Federal Trade Commission to study antitrust problems, chastised American Express as a menace to the First Amendment, and raised a dozen other issues, bogus and legitimate. In the ultimate slap, McGraw chided American Express for not paying interest on the float from its traveler’s checks.
麦格劳随手捡起任何能用的攻击武器。他以诽谤罪起诉美国运通，指控其配合阿拉伯国家对以色列的抵制，要求联邦通信委员会和联邦贸易委员会调查反垄断问题，痛斥美国运通是对第一修正案的威胁，还提出了其他十几个真假难辨的问题。作为最后一记重击，麦格劳指责美国运通未对旅行支票的浮存金支付利息。

American Express had planned to borrow \$700 million for the merger, with Morgan Guaranty as lead banker. This outraged McGraw, who considered himself a faithful Morgan client. In 1977, Morgan chairman Pat Patterson had thrown a lunch for McGraw to commemorate the fiftieth anniversary of the publisher’s relationship with the bank. In his desk drawer, McGraw still kept a sterling silver cigar box he had received on that occasion.
美国运通原本计划为这次合并借款 7 亿美元，由摩根保证信托银行担任牵头行。这激怒了麦格劳，他自认为是摩根的忠实客户。1977 年，摩根董事长帕特·帕特森曾为麦格劳举办午餐会，纪念这家出版社与该银行合作五十周年。在麦格劳的办公桌抽屉里，至今还保留着当时获赠的一个纯银雪茄盒。

Now McGraw wondered aloud whether Robinson had deliberately chosen Morgans to rob him of his banker and whether Morgans had leaked confidential information to Robinson. He denounced AmEx for using “its financial power to cause a bank to violate its relationship with a client.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442638) It turned out the Morgan Trust Department held up to 1.8 million shares of McGraw-Hill stock in fiduciary accounts. To vote—or not to vote—the shares would hopelessly enmesh the bank in a conflict of interest. The proposed merger degenerated into such a noisy, unseemly squabble that American Express decided to quit, while Morgan Stanley collected a \$1.5-million fee. It was hard to see how the omnipresent Morgan Guaranty could perform hostile takeovers without constantly stirring up a hornet’s nest of lawsuits and client conflicts. At the same time, the bank’s close relations with corporate clients created the potential for a formidable merger department.
现在，麦格劳困惑地发问，罗宾逊是否故意选择摩根来夺走他的银行家，而摩根是否向罗宾逊泄露了机密信息。他谴责美国运通利用其金融力量，导致一家银行违反与客户的关系。结果发现，摩根信托部在信托账户中持有高达 180 万股麦格劳-希尔股票。投票或不投票这些股票，都会将银行无可救药地卷入利益冲突。拟议中的合并演变成了一场嘈杂、不体面的争吵，以至于美国运通决定退出，而摩根士丹利收取了 150 万美元的费用。很难想象，无处不在的摩根担保信托公司如何能在不频繁引发诉讼和客户冲突的麻烦的情况下进行敌意收购。与此同时，该银行与公司客户的紧密关系为其打造一个强大的并购部门创造了潜力。

In 1985, the Morgan bank formed a separate merger and acquisitions group, piloted by the Cuban-born, Yale-educated Roberto Mendoza. A big, brooding man with a slightly hooded gaze, he looked tougher and more determined than the average Morgan banker. He was more reminiscent of Morgan Stanley deal makers than of the sedate Morgan Guaranty bankers of old. A high-adrenaline type, he liked furious games of midnight tennis and drove his young charges to devise unorthodox deals. Mendoza thought investment banks were gouging clients and was willing to compete with them on price—perhaps the one taboo Wall Street still held sacred. He also thought high fees sometimes warped the judgment of investment bankers. Morgan Guaranty ran ads for its takeover group that needled the Street: “In M&A, clients who require totally objective advice, research free from conflict of interest . . . can rely on one firm. J. P. Morgan.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442790) One ad showed an empty tombstone and said pointedly: “We don’t promote M&A deals just to generate fees.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442939) It would soon pay a price for these holier-than-thou ads.
1985 年，摩根银行组建了一支独立的并购团队，由古巴出生、耶鲁毕业的罗伯托·门多萨领军。他身形魁梧、神情深沉，目光略带阴鸷，看上去比一般摩根银行家更强势果决。他更让人联想到摩根士丹利的交易高手，而非昔日沉稳老派的摩根保证银行家。他是那种肾上腺素旺盛的人，喜欢午夜打激烈网球，并驱使手下年轻员工设计非常规交易。门多萨认为投资银行在压榨客户，愿意在价格上与它们竞争——这或许是华尔街仍视为禁忌的底线。他还认为高昂的费用有时会扭曲投行家的判断。摩根保证银行为其收购团队打的广告直戳华尔街痛处：“在并购领域，需要完全客观建议、研究无利益冲突的客户……可以信赖一家公司。J.P.摩根。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442790) 一则广告展示了一块空白的墓碑，并一语中的地写道："我们宣传并购交易，并非只是为了收取费用。" [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2442939) 这家银行很快就会为这些自以为高人一等的广告付出代价。

The bank knew its genteel image hurt in the slash-and-burn takeover game. As Bob Engel said, “When a chairman wakes up in the middle of the night with a phone call from someone saying, ‘We want to buy your company,’ he thinks of Morgan Stanley instead of Morgan Guaranty. It’s going to be a selling job, no question.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443091) Besides the image problem, Morgans faced the reluctance of companies to share secrets with large lending institutions, which might have trouble preserving secrecy or might use information to deny future loans.
该银行深知，在残酷的收购游戏中，它那文雅的形象实在不占优势。正如鲍勃·恩格尔所言："当一位董事长半夜被电话吵醒，对方说'我们想收购你的公司'时，他想到的是摩根士丹利，而不是摩根保证银行。毫无疑问，这将是场推销战。" [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443091) 除了形象问题，摩根还面临着企业不愿与大型贷款机构分享秘密的困境——这些机构要么难以保守秘密，要么可能利用信息拒绝未来的贷款。

By the late 1980s, Preston and Mendoza stated publicly that Morgans would not only back unfriendly raids but might even finance both sides in a takeover, setting up opposing teams. Once J. P. Morgan and Company placed its authority behind hostile raids, Wall Street’s transformation seemed complete. The situation was not unlike 1929, when the bank submitted to the fad of forming holding companies with the Alleghany and United Corporation promotions.
到 20 世纪 80 年代末，普雷斯顿和门多萨公开宣称，摩根不仅会支持敌意收购，甚至可能为收购双方同时提供资金，组建相互对抗的团队。一旦 J.P.摩根公司将其权威置于敌意收购之后，华尔街的转型似乎就完成了。这一幕与 1929 年颇为相似，当时该银行也屈服于潮流，参与组建了联合公司和联合公司的控股公司结构。

Before the crash, Mendoza’s department scored small victories but no big, attention-getting deals. Then, in January 1988, Morgans became the first commercial bank to counsel a hostile raider in a billion-dollar deal, advising F. Hoffman-La Roche on its \$4.2-billion tender offer for Sterling Drug. The Swiss pharmaceutical company, F. Hoffman-La Roche, did a \$6-billion-a-year business. After its Valium patent expired in 1985, it needed new products in order to fill the sales pipeline. Sterling made Bayer aspirin, Phillips milk of magnesia, and many other products. With its cartellike cosiness, the pharmaceutical industry had been ruled by gentleman’s agreements and spared bloody takeover wars. This was the first time a large pharmaceutical firm had made a hostile assault against a healthy competitor. Adding to the shock was that J. P. Morgan and Company had been a banker to Sterling Drug for more than fifty years.
在危机爆发前，门多萨的部门虽然取得了一些小规模的成功，却未能斩获令人瞩目的重大交易。然而到了 1988 年 1 月，摩根银行成为首家为敌意收购者提供咨询服务的商业银行，协助瑞士制药巨头 F. Hoffman-La Roche 以 42 亿美元对 Sterling Drug 发起要约收购。这家年销售额达 60 亿美元的瑞士药企，在 1985 年旗下安定（Valium）专利到期后，急需新产品填补销售渠道。Sterling Drug 旗下拥有拜耳阿司匹林、菲利普斯镁乳等众多知名产品。制药行业长期以来依靠君子协定维持着类似卡特尔般默契的格局，从未发生过血腥的收购战。这是大型制药企业首次对经营健康的竞争对手发起恶意袭击，更令人震惊的是，摩根银行（J. P. Morgan and Company）作为 Sterling Drug 长达五十多年的合作银行，竟反戈相向。

Advised by Mendoza, Hoffman-La Roche followed a frenzied raiding strategy known as a bear hug. It made an opening bid of \$72 a share for Sterling, then twice stepped up the bid in rapid succession without any formal rejection of the earlier offers. Sterling chairman John M. Pietruski had jolted his firm from its former lethargy and lifted its earnings to healthy double-digit levels. He was consequently irate at Hoffman-La Roche’s overture and called in Joe Fogg of Morgan Stanley and Joe Flom of Skadden, Arps as advisers.
在门多萨的建议下，霍夫曼-拉罗氏采用了一种疯狂的收购策略，也就是所谓的“熊式拥抱”。他们对斯特林开出了每股 72 美元的初始报价，随后在没有收到正式拒绝的情况下，接连两次迅速加价。斯特林董事长约翰·M·皮特鲁斯基此前带领公司摆脱了昔日的低迷，实现了健康的两位数增长率。因此，他对霍夫曼-拉罗氏的主动示好感到愤怒，并聘请了摩根士丹利的乔·福格和斯卡登-阿普斯律所的乔·弗洛姆作为顾问。

Taking a leaf from Harold McGraw, Pietruski let loose a blast that Morgan Guaranty wouldn’t soon forget. In an open letter, he said he was “shocked and dismayed by what I consider to be Morgan bank’s unethical conduct in aiding and abetting a surprise raid on one of its longtime clients.” The bank, he said, was “privy to our most confidential financial information.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443251) He laid out their ties of recent years, including borrowing, Morgan work as Sterling’s registrar and transfer agent, and Morgan’s management of Euromarket issues for Sterling. It was a clever broadside, for Pietruski echoed the language of the old Wall Street, as if shaming the bank with recollection: “How many relationships of trust and confidence do you have to have with a client before you consider not embarking on a course of action that could be detrimental to \[his\] best interest?”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443402)
效仿哈罗德·麦格劳的做法，皮耶特鲁斯基猛烈抨击了摩根担保公司，这一击让后者久久难以忘怀。在一封公开信中，他表示自己“感到震惊和沮丧，因为摩根银行协助并教唆对其中一位长期客户发动突袭，我认为这是不道德的行为。”他还说，该银行“掌握着我们最机密的财务信息。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443251) 他列举了近年来双方的关系，包括借贷合作、摩根担任斯特林公司的登记和过户代理人，以及摩根为斯特林管理欧洲市场债券发行。这是一次巧妙的猛烈攻击，因为皮耶特鲁斯基沿用了老华尔街的措辞，仿佛通过回忆来让银行感到羞愧：“在你决定不采取可能损害客户最大利益的行动之前，你需要与客户建立多少信任与信赖关系？” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443402)

In an unusual public rejoinder, Lew Preston hinted that the letter was hypocritical bluster crafted by a publicist. “It’s kind of interesting that we’re still their transfer agent,” he said afterward. When the battle was over, Preston elaborated: “That wasn’t a letter from the chairman of Sterling. That was a letter written by the investment bankers to embarrass this firm.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443528) He disputed the notion that Morgan betrayed a deeply loyal client, noting that Sterling’s principal banker was Irving Trust and that 23 Wall had only performed “mechanical functions” for Sterling. For Preston, the surprise was less in the raising of the betrayal issue—obviously anticipated in planning for the takeover—than in the way that Wall Street ganged up to bad-mouth Morgan’s handling of the deal.
在一次罕见的公开回应中，卢·普雷斯顿暗示那封信不过是公关专家炮制的虚伪恫吓。他事后表示：“有趣的是，我们仍然是他们的过户代理。”战斗结束后，普雷斯顿进一步解释道：“那根本不是斯特林董事长写的信，而是投资银行家为了羞辱我们公司而炮制的。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443528) 他驳斥了摩根背叛忠诚客户的说法，指出斯特林的主要银行是欧文信托，而华尔街 23 号对斯特林仅履行了“机械性职能”。对普雷斯顿而言，最惊讶的倒不是背叛问题被提出——毕竟收购计划中早有预料——而是华尔街竟联手抨击摩根对这笔交易的处理方式。

Indeed, investment bankers believed that Hoffman had made too low an initial bid and only ended up drawing other sharks into the water. Morgan Stanley brought in Kodak as a friendly suitor, and it snatched Sterling away for \$89.50 a share. Although Wall Street crowed that Morgan Guaranty had bungled the deal—the glee was scarcely disguised—the bank hinted that Kodak had overpaid. The price was high—twenty-two times Sterling’s estimated 1988 earnings. The investment banks, of course, had a vested interest in denigrating the performance of Morgan Guaranty, which was poaching on their territory. They were especially incensed when Mendoza charged a paltry \$1 million for the losing effort, surely the most unforgivable sin of all.
确实，投资银行家们认为霍夫曼的初始出价过低，最终只是引来了其他鲨鱼。摩根士丹利将柯达作为友好收购方引入，最终以每股 89.50 美元的价格抢走了斯特林。尽管华尔街得意洋洋地宣称摩根担保搞砸了这笔交易——那幸灾乐祸几乎毫不掩饰——但该银行暗示柯达出价过高。这个价格确实高——是斯特林 1988 年预计盈利的 22 倍。投资银行们自然有既得利益去贬低摩根担保的表现，因为后者正在蚕食他们的领地。尤其令他们气愤的是，门多萨在这笔失败的交易中仅收取了区区 100 万美元的费用，这无疑是最不可饶恕的罪过。

Clearly, Morgans considered Hoffman-La Roche a much closer and more profitable client than Sterling Drug. But was this to be the new standard for choosing targets? Would the bank feed off stronger clients at the expense of weaker ones? Would it sacrifice those deemed less important? Then who would trust the bank in the future? The controversy also highlighted the multiple possibilities for conflicts of interest as big banks conducted raids. For instance, as transfer agent for Sterling Drug, Morgan Guaranty held its confidential shareholder list—information invaluable to any raider. (Later that year, the Morgan bank sold its shareholder service operation to First Chicago, terminating a corporate trust business over a century old. Preston denied a Sterling link, although such shareholder work was clearly incompatible with merger activity.) Even as the bank invoked its internal controls for protecting confidential information, it was encouraging loan officers to pass along ideas to the Mendoza team in its “one bank” concept of teamwork.
显然，摩根士丹利认为霍夫曼-拉罗什（Hoffman-La Roche）远比斯特林制药（Sterling Drug）更亲近、更赚钱。但这难道要成为选择目标的新标准吗？这家银行是否会以牺牲弱者的代价来滋养强者？是否会牺牲那些被认为不那么重要的客户？那未来还有谁敢信任这家银行？这场争议也凸显出，在大银行发起收购战之时，利益冲突的可能性层出不穷。例如，作为斯特林制药的过户代理商，摩根保证信托公司（Morgan Guaranty）持有其机密股东名单——这对任何收购方来说都是无价之宝。（同年晚些时候，摩根银行将其股东服务业务出售给第一芝加哥银行，终止了长达一个多世纪的企业信托业务。普雷斯顿否认这与斯特林制药有关，尽管此类股东服务工作显然与并购活动存在冲突。）即便银行声称其内部管控措施能保护机密信息，它鼓励信贷员在“一个银行”的团队合作理念下，将想法传递给 Mendoza 团队。

Lest McGraw-Hill or Sterling seem a freak, the bank again ran into trouble in April 1988. James R. Houghton, chairman of Corning Glass, announced an agreement to acquire International Clinical Labs, Incorporated, for \$26 a share. A stout opponent of hostile deals, Houghton thought this deal was done and used Morgan Guaranty as his depository and lender. Then Smithkline Beckman unveiled a surprise bid, quickly kicking up the price to a winning \$37 a share. The defeated Houghton was stunned: Smithkline’s adviser was his own stalwart banker, Morgan Guaranty. More shocking was that Houghton sat on the Morgan board! On the eve of Morgan’s annual meeting, Houghton threatened to resign and was pacified only after a talk with Lew Preston. Again, Morgan earned more by advising Smithkline than by being depository for Corning. But was this short-term calculus to be the new operating standard? Would the bank auction itself off to the highest bidder? It was moving toward a two-tier structure in which it coddled the larger clients and sacrificed the smaller. And in this, it was beginning—just beginning—to resemble the rest of Wall Street, which had now operated that way for many years.
尽管麦格劳-希尔或斯特林公司看起来像是特例，但这家银行在 1988 年 4 月再次陷入麻烦。康宁玻璃董事长詹姆斯·R·霍顿宣布达成协议，以每股 26 美元收购国际临床实验室公司。作为敌意收购的坚决反对者，霍顿本以为这笔交易已成定局，并指定摩根担保信托银行作为存托行和贷款行。然而，史密斯克莱恩·贝克曼突然杀出，迅速将报价提高至每股 37 美元并成功夺标。落败的霍顿震惊不已：史密斯克莱恩的顾问竟是他一直信赖的银行——摩根担保信托。更令人震惊的是，霍顿本人正是摩根董事会的成员！在摩根年会前夕，霍顿威胁要辞职，直到与卢·普雷斯顿谈话后才平息怒火。这次，摩根通过为史密斯克莱恩提供咨询服务赚取的收益，比担任康宁存托行还要高。但难道这种短期算计要成为新的经营准则吗？这家银行是要把自己拍卖给出价最高者吗？ 它正逐渐形成一种两层结构，偏袒大客户，牺牲小客户。在这一方面，它开始——只是刚刚开始——变得像华尔街的其他部分一样，而华尔街多年来正是如此运作的。
