# Chapter Eighteen: Midget

THE Wall Street of 1932 was a dismal ghost town. Securities firms declared “apple days”—unpaid vacation days each month that enabled destitute brokers to go out and supplement their income by selling apples on the sidewalk. Apple vendors appeared at the Corner. Downtown real estate was so depressed that building companies defaulted; astute investors who bought their bonds became the future owners of Wall Street. The misery extended everywhere. Riverside Park was lined with Hoovervilles, and sylvan retreats in Central Park looked like ragged hillbilly hollows. On Park Avenue, ten-room apartments that had been occupied by financiers of the twenties now lacked tenants. The new, half-occupied Empire State Building was mocked as the “Empty State Building.”
1932 年的华尔街宛如一座阴森的鬼城。证券公司宣布了“苹果日”——每月无薪休假的日子，让穷困潦倒的经纪人能去路边卖苹果贴补家用。苹果小贩出现在街角。市中心的地产惨淡至极，建筑公司纷纷违约；那些买下它们债券的精明投资者，反而成了华尔街未来的主人。苦难无处不在。河滨公园沿线布满了胡佛村，中央公园原本幽静的林间空地看起来就像破败的穷乡僻壤。公园大道上，那些二十年代曾被金融家占据的十居室公寓如今无人问津。新建的半空置的帝国大厦被讥讽为“空国大厦”。

For aristocrats in private clubs, it was a time of often macabre mirth. The Union League Club had a room wallpapered with stock certificates that were rendered worthless by the crash. (They were peeled off by itchy fingers when the market recovered.) After falling for over two straight years, the stock market hit bottom on July 8, 1932. By that point, two thousand investment houses had failed, and new underwritings stood at 10 percent of their 1929 peak volume. On the Stock Exchange floor, listless traders invented games to kill time. Big Board seats that fetched \$550,000 before the crash now sold for as little as \$68,000. The major financial work was refunding old bonds at lower interest rates.
对于私人俱乐部里的贵族们来说，那段日子里弥漫着一种常常是诡异又荒诞的喜乐氛围。联合联盟俱乐部里有一间房，墙上贴满了因股市崩盘而变得一文不值的股票凭证（市场回暖后，这些凭证又被手痒的人揭了下来）。在连续下跌两年多之后，股市于 1932 年 7 月 8 日触及最低点。到那时，已有两千家投资公司倒闭，新发行的证券规模仅为其 1929 年巅峰时期的 10%。在交易所大厅里，无精打采的交易员们发明各种游戏来消磨时光。崩盘前售价高达 55 万美元的纽交所席位，如今最低只卖 6.8 万美元。主要的金融工作就是以较低的利率发行新债券来偿还旧债券。

In 1932, almost thirteen million of America’s 125 million people were unemployed. Two million roamed America searching for work, boarding boxcars and sleeping in hobo camps. Hoover refused to renounce economic orthodoxy and mount a vigorous attack on the Depression. Sometimes he flirted with fanciful solutions to America’s despondency. At various times, he thought America needed a good laugh, a good poem, a good song. He even approached Will Rogers about writing a good joke to end panic hoarding. Hoover himself wore a funereal expression. Of a White House meeting with him, Secretary of State Henry Stimson said, “It was like sitting in a bath of ink.” And sculptor Gutzon Borglum remarked, “If you put a rose in Hoover’s hand it would wilt.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2321638) Hoover had a way of minimizing the nation’s suffering. In 1932, he insisted, “Nobody is actually starving. The hoboes, for example, are better fed than they have ever been. One hobo in New York got ten meals in one day.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2321827)
1932 年，美国 1.25 亿人口中近 1300 万人失业。200 万人四处流浪寻找工作，扒货运火车、住流浪汉营地。胡佛拒绝放弃正统经济理论，也未对经济大萧条发起有力反击。他有时会异想天开地寻找缓解美国困境的办法。不同时期，他曾认为美国需要开怀大笑、一首好诗、一首好歌。他甚至找威尔·罗杰斯编个笑话来终结恐慌性囤积。胡佛自己却一副哭丧脸。国务卿亨利·史汀生谈及在白宫与他见面时说：“就像坐在墨水池里。”雕塑家格曾·博格勒姆则评论道：“若把玫瑰放进胡佛手中，它也会枯萎。”胡佛总是轻描淡写国家的苦难。1932 年他坚称：“没有人真正挨饿。比如流浪汉，他们比以往吃得都好。纽约有个流浪汉一天就吃了十顿饭。”

That spring, Jack Morgan was briefly roused to a rare act of public activism. A believer in self-reliance, he cited as his favorite biblical text Ezekiel 2:1: “And he said unto me, Son of man, stand upon thy feet, and I will speak it unto thee.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322001) Jack construed this as God clucking his tongue at the welfare state. He preached the old-time religion, telling the marquess of Linlithgow that honesty, integrity, and economy were “the real solution of our troubles, most of which, in my opinion, come from greed. ”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322193) He rallied to Hoover’s plea for private benevolence instead of government intervention. In March 1932, he participated in a fundraiser for the Block Community Organization of New York. Dressed in dinner jacket at his Murray Hill mansion—with butler Henry Physick and other servants listening at a rear-hall receiver—he broadcast a radio appeal for help. “We must all do our bit,” he said, endorsing a plan by which workers contributed small weekly sums to a fund for the jobless. A shy man who dreaded public appearances, Jack’s cooperation reflected a fearful mood among the rich. Lamont, meanwhile, helped the Red Cross raise money for farmers victimized by the Midwest drought.
那年春天，杰克·摩根罕见地被激起了公众活动家的热忱。他笃信自立自强，最爱引用圣经《以西结书》2:1 中的话：“他对我说：‘人子啊，你站起来，我要和你说话。’” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322001) 杰克将此解读为上帝对福利国家的咂舌不满。他宣扬古老教义，对林利思戈侯爵坦言，诚实、正直和节俭才是“解决问题的真正出路，而在我看来，多数问题皆源于贪婪。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322193) 他响应胡佛的呼吁，主张靠私人慈善而非政府干预。1932 年 3 月，他参加了纽约街区社区组织的筹款活动。在默里山的宅邸里，他身着晚礼服——管家亨利·菲西克及其他仆人在后厅分机旁聆听——通过广播发出求助呼吁。“我们都得尽一份力，”他说道，并支持了一项由工人每周向失业基金小额捐款的计划。 杰克生性害羞，害怕公开露面，他的配合也反映了富人们普遍的惶恐心态。与此同时，拉蒙特则协助红十字会为受中西部干旱影响的农民筹集善款。

An outdated allegiance to classical economics tipped a postcrash recession into seemingly insoluble depression. In late 1931, Federal Reserve Banks hiked up their discount rate by 2 percentage points in two weeks. To balance the budget, the Federal Revenue Act of 1932 almost doubled tax rates—again, the perfect medicine to kill the patient. Not everyone at Morgans automatically resisted experimentation. Throughout 1932, Russell Leffingwell, the resident Democratic iconoclast and self-styled curmudgeon, laughed at those who feared inflationary spending as “people who in an Arctic winter worry about the heat of the tropics.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322417) Yet Leffingwell’s own views spun like a weathervane in a high gust, and at moments he reverted to budget-balancing orthodoxy. He told Walter Lippmann that a public works program would only prolong the Depression, and he doggedly maintained the need for the gold standard.
对古典经济学的过时忠诚，让一场危机后的衰退陷入了看似无法解决的萧条。1931 年底，美联储在两周内将贴现率提高了 2 个百分点。为了平衡预算，1932 年的《联邦税收法案》几乎将税率翻了一番——这又是给病人下猛药的完美方案。并非所有摩根的人都自动抵制实验。整个 1932 年，身为民主党异见者、自诩为老顽固的拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔（Russell Leffingwell）嘲笑那些担心通胀性支出的人，称他们为“在北极寒冬里担心热带酷暑的人”。然而，莱芬韦尔自己的观点却像在疾风中转动的风向标，有时他又回到平衡预算的正统观念。他告诉沃尔特·李普曼（Walter Lippmann），公共工程项目只会延长萧条期，并固执地坚持金本位制是必要的。

The major Hoover policy initiative of 1932, the Reconstruction Finance Corporation, was a major boon to Morgan interests. It was set up to make loans to banks, railroads, and other hard-pressed businesses. The previous year, Lamont had told Hoover that the plight of America’s railroads was “the principle impediment to domestic recovery.” The railroads were burdened with debt from the twenties and couldn’t service their bonds. When the Van Sweringens defaulted on their secret rescue loan in 1931, Morgans and Guaranty Trust invited the brothers in for a frank chat, telling them that “we are, in effect, the owners of all of their properties.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322635) So the bank dropped its usual objections to government bailouts when it came to railroads. Oris Van Sweringen said that he and Mantis were “on the doorstep waiting for them \[the RFC\] to open.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322795) The Van Sweringens borrowed \$75 million from the RFC, strengthening the case of those who saw it as a welfare agency for the rich.
1932 年胡佛政府推出的重大政策举措——复兴金融公司，对摩根财团而言是一大福音。该机构旨在向银行、铁路及其他陷入困境的企业发放贷款。此前一年，拉蒙特曾向胡佛坦言，美国铁路业的困境是"国内经济复苏的主要障碍"。这些铁路公司背负着二十年代的债务重担，无力偿还债券利息。当范斯韦林根兄弟在 1931 年拖欠秘密救援贷款时，摩根和保証信托公司邀请两位兄弟进行坦诚会谈，告知他们"实际上，我们才是你们所有资产的真正主人。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322635) 因此，当涉及铁路行业时，这家银行一反常态，不再反对政府救助。奥里斯·范斯韦林根表示，他和曼蒂斯"正站在门口，等待他们（复兴金融公司）开门。" [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322795) 范斯韦林根兄弟从复兴金融公司获得了 7500 万美元贷款，这进一步印证了那些视其为富人福利机构的人的观点。

Hard times didn’t touch the splendor of top-drawer Morgan partners. If their drawing rights from the bank—that is, their annual percentage take as partners—were halved, they still had wealth left from the twenties. The main problem was enjoying it free of guilt. What would Jack do with his new *Corsair*, big enough to house a small village of hoboes? He decided, for decency’s sake, to mothball it for a while, telling Cosmo Lang, the archbishop of Canterbury, “It seems very unwise to let the ’Corsair’ come out this summer. There are so many suffering from lack of work, and even from actual hunger, that it is both wiser and kinder not to flaunt such luxurious amusement in the face of the public.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2322928) He offered to charter the boat to John D. Rockefeller, Jr.
艰难时世并未撼动摩根顶级合伙人的奢华。即便他们在银行的提款权——即作为合伙人每年分红的比例——减半，二十年代积累的财富依然丰厚。真正的问题在于如何心安理得地享受这笔钱。杰克那艘能容纳一小群流浪汉的新 Corsair 游艇该如何处置？碍于体面，他决定暂时将其封存，对坎特伯雷大主教科斯莫·朗说道：“今年夏天让‘Corsair’出海似乎很不明智。如今失业者众多，甚至有人食不果腹，在公众面前炫耀这种奢靡的消遣，既不理智也不仁慈。”随后，他将这艘游艇租给了小约翰·D·洛克菲勒。

With over \$20 million in his partnership account, Tom Lamont had time to catch up on travel. Where Jack liked to sail with bishops and surgeons, Lamont preferred the company of writers, intellectuals, and socialites. In the spring of 1931, he and Florence went on a leisurely Aegean cruise with Walter Lippmann and his wife, and classics scholar Gilbert Murray. They were joined in Athens by the John Masefields. There are photos of this Depression party aboard the *Saturnia.* One shows Lamont in a double-breasted suit with pocket handkerchief and a jaunty striped vest. His shrewd eyes crease into crow’s-feet as he gazes into the camera. Small and balding, he has appraising eyes, sympathetic but vigilant, that seem to take in everything. In another photograph, taken at the captain’s table, the group is elegantly erect. Walter Lippmann looks dashing, while Lamont peers attentively down the table. Dining in this wood-paneled interior, with its fresh table linen, the group has a glitter that is remote from the American dreariness of the moment.
合伙账户里有两千多万美元，托马斯·拉蒙特便有了闲情逸致四处游历。杰克喜欢与主教和外科医生一同航行，而拉蒙特则更愿意与作家、知识分子和社交名流为伴。1931 年春天，他与弗洛伦丝、沃尔特·李普曼夫妇以及古典学者吉尔伯特·默里一道，悠闲地乘船畅游爱琴海。在雅典，约翰·梅斯菲尔德夫妇也加入了他们。这些大萧条时期的聚会照片留在了“萨图尼亚号”上。其中一张，拉蒙特身着双排扣西装，口袋里塞着手帕，俏皮地穿着条纹马甲。他精明的眼睛眯起，眼角泛起笑纹，凝视着镜头。他身材矮小，头顶微秃，目光中带着审视，既充满同情又保持警觉，仿佛一切尽收眼底。另一张在船长席拍的照片里，众人优雅地端坐。沃尔特·李普曼看起来风度翩翩，而拉蒙特则专注地顺着长桌望去。 在这家木饰装潢的餐厅用餐，桌布洁白如新，整个团队神采奕奕，与当下美国那种沉闷乏味的氛围截然不同。

The Lamonts arrived at Patras with forty-two pieces of luggage. The Greeks treated them as visiting foreign dignitaries and followed protocol carefully. The provincial governor carried Florence’s hatbox ashore while a Greek cabinet representative (probably wondering to what depths he had sunk) inspected every toilet on their hotel floor. Tom and Florence Lamont liked to affect a bohemian innocence. During this idyllic trip, Florence reported, “We almost always have a picnic luncheon because the hotels are most of them so bad. We sit in the sun and read poems about Greece after luncheon.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323161)
拉蒙特一家抵达帕特雷时，随身带了四十二件行李。希腊人将他们视为来访的外国政要，严守外交礼节。省长亲手提着弗洛伦斯的帽盒上岸，而一位希腊内阁代表（大概在心里嘀咕自己怎么混到了这步田地）逐一检查了他们酒店楼层的每间厕所。汤姆和弗洛伦斯·拉蒙特喜欢装出一副不拘小节的波西米亚作派。在这趟田园诗般的旅途中，弗洛伦斯写道：“我们几乎天天都吃野餐午餐，因为大多数酒店实在太差了。午餐后，我们就坐在太阳底下，读着关于希腊的诗篇。”

If the Morgan world seemed to survive the Depression intact, the surface was deceptive. Between 1929 and 1932, the bank saw its net worth—its basic capital cushion—drop with frightening speed, from \$118 million to half that before Hoover left office. Total assets plunged from \$704 million to \$425 million. Even for the House of Morgan, these were staggering blows. The real casualties were junior partners, who shared in the losses without having reaped the spectacular bull market profits. The bank still recruited based on talent. As an official Morgan history says, “The alternative of seeking additional capital by recruiting new partners with more money than talent, thereby diluting the quality of the firm, was deemed unacceptable.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323326)
如果说摩根家族看似毫发无损地挺过了大萧条，那只是表象而已。1929 年至 1932 年间，这家银行的净资产——即其核心资本缓冲——以骇人的速度缩水，从 1.18 亿美元骤降至胡佛离任时的一半。总资产也从 7.04 亿美元暴跌至 4.25 亿美元。即便对于摩根财团而言，这同样是沉重的打击。真正的输家是那些初级合伙人——他们分担了损失，却未能分享到牛市盛宴的巨大红利。银行依然坚持唯才是举的招聘原则。正如摩根官方史书所言：“通过吸纳资金雄厚但才能平庸的新合伙人来寻求额外资本，从而稀释公司品质，这条路被视为不可接受。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323326)

The House of Morgan retained Pierpont’s paternalism. When salaries were slashed by as much as 20 percent, the staff was told these cuts would be restored before partners resumed full drawing rights from their capital accounts. When the bank closed its employee dining room, it distributed cash allowances for lunch. Staff families also got two free weeks each year at a rustic Morgan camp in Maine. For the Morgan Grenfell staff, the Depression *ennui* was partly lifted by Jack’s gift of a sports ground in Beckenham, with a cricket pitch, hard tennis courts, trimmed lawns, and a tea pavilion. These touches inspired fierce loyalty and a cultlike intimacy among employees. Their Depression suffering, if real, was extremely modest compared with the unspeakable suffering beyond the marble walls.
摩根银行延续了皮尔庞特的家长式作风。当薪水削减高达 20%时，员工被告知这些削减将在合伙人恢复从资本账户全额提取权利之前恢复。银行关闭员工餐厅时，发放午餐现金津贴。员工家属每年还可免费在缅因州一处朴素的摩根营地度过两周。对摩根格伦费尔的员工来说，杰克赠送的贝肯汉姆运动场——配有板球场、硬地网球场、修剪整齐的草坪和茶亭——部分驱散了经济大萧条带来的沉闷。这些举措激发了员工极度忠诚和近乎崇拜的亲密感。若说他们在萧条中承受了痛苦，那与大理石墙外难以言表的苦难相比，也显得格外微不足道。

LET us consider 1932 politics, for the events that led to the Glass-Steagall Act—the Banking Act of 1933—and the division of the House of Morgan were rooted in that year. It was Herbert Hoover who first waged war on Wall Street and prompted hearings that led to new banking legislation. There had always been a slightly paranoid edge to Hoover’s dealings with his Morgan friends. After staying at the White House in the summer of 1931, Dwight Morrow told Lamont that Hoover was blue and felt “he had been trying to carry out the views of the banks here in New York and yet had got rather cold comfort from them.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323519) Lamont sent Hoover a note to cheer him up, yet there was an undercurrent of uneasiness in his relations with the president.
让我们审视 1932 年的政治，因为最终催生《Glass-Steagall Act》（1933 年银行法）以及摩根财团（House of Morgan）分裂的事件，其根源就在那一年。率先向华尔街宣战并推动听证会、促成新银行立法的，正是赫伯特·胡佛。胡佛在与摩根老友的交往中，始终透着一丝近乎偏执的戒备。1931 年夏天，德怀特·莫罗在白宫停留后告诉拉蒙特，胡佛情绪低落，觉得自己“一直在努力贯彻纽约这些银行的主张，可从它们那儿得到的却尽是冷遇。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323519) 拉蒙特给胡佛送去便条以表安慰，然而他与这位总统的关系之下，却暗流涌动，隐隐透着不安。

Hoover’s relationship with the House of Morgan dated back to his days as a mining engineer. In 1917, he acted as intermediary between Sir Ernest Oppenheimer, who wanted to take public his Johannesburg gold-mining group, and Morgans. To consolidate his new Wall Street tie, Oppenheimer insisted on the word *American* appearing in the new company’s name. Thus was born the Anglo-American Corporation, afterward Africa’s richest company. Evidently Lamont thought this would inaugurate a series of mining ventures tapping Hoover’s talents. As he told Morgan Grenfell, the Anglo-American deal was “part of a comprehensive plan involving association with Mr. Hoover in mining ventures generally.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323708) But Hoover reneged on the deal, and Lamont later applauded Oppenheimer for ousting Hoover and engineer William Honnold. “We never shall have any quarrel with \[Oppenheimer\] in regard to his feeling about Honnold and/or Hoover,” Lamont informed London.[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323909)
胡佛与摩根财团的关系可追溯到他担任矿业工程师的时期。1917 年，他充当了希望将其约翰内斯堡黄金矿业集团上市的欧内斯特·奥本海默爵士与摩根家族之间的中间人。为了巩固与华尔街的新联系，奥本海默坚持在新公司的名称中加入"美国"一词。由此诞生了英美资源公司，后来成为非洲最富有的企业。显然，拉蒙特认为这将开启一系列利用胡佛才能的矿业投资项目。正如他告诉摩根建富公司的那样，这笔英美资源交易是"一项涉及与胡佛先生广泛合作开发矿业项目的全面计划的一部分"。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323708) 但胡佛后来背弃了协议，拉蒙特后来称赞奥本海默赶走了胡佛和工程师威廉·霍诺德。拉蒙特在给伦敦的信中写道："关于他对霍诺德和/或胡佛的看法，我们永远不会与\[奥本海默\]产生任何争执。" [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2323909)

Beyond this history, Morgans and Hoover were doomed to have policy quarrels. Hoover felt straitjacketed by a Congress that cared little for Europe’s troubles, favored buy-America campaigns, and lacked interest in inheriting economic leadership from Britain. The House of Morgan, in turn, had European clients to protect, and its internationalism was no less problematic for Hoover than for his Republican predecessors. There were also clashing personal styles: Hoover was brusque and humorless, while Morgan partners were silky aristocrats.
除了这段历史渊源，摩根家族和胡佛注定会在政策上发生争执。胡佛感到被国会束缚手脚——国会既漠视欧洲的困境，又偏爱“购买美国货”运动，更无意从英国手中接过经济领导权的接力棒。而摩根财团则需要保护其欧洲客户，其国际主义倾向对胡佛而言，并不比对待他的共和党前任们更省心。两人的个人风格也格格不入：胡佛生硬刻板、毫无幽默感，而摩根家族的合伙人们则个个温文尔雅、一派贵族风范。

In July of 1932, it looked as if the world economy might finally cast off the twin burdens of German reparations and Allied war debt. At Lausanne, European leaders reached a gentleman’s agreement that effectively ended the debt charade; if they could stop paying off war debts, they would stop asking for reparations. Lamont was jubilant, seeing this as an end to the economic warfare that had been waged since Versailles. He dispatched Martin Egan to the White House, not to advise Hoover to cancel the war debts outright but simply to reexamine them.
1932 年 7 月，世界经济似乎终于要甩掉德国赔款和协约国战争债务这两大包袱。在洛桑，欧洲各国领导人达成了一项君子协定，实际上终结了这场债务闹剧：如果他们能停止偿付战争债务，就会停止索要赔款。拉蒙特欣喜若狂，认为这是自凡尔赛以来经济战的终结。他派马丁·伊根前往白宫，并非建议胡佛直接取消战争债务，而只是重新审视它们。

Returning from Washington, Egan said he had never seen the president so emotional about an issue. He had made a speech full of anger, self-pity, and impotent frustration. “Lamont has this matter all wrong,” Hoover had insisted, echoing widespread public sentiment. “If there is one thing the American people do not like and will not stand for it is a combination of this kind against them. . . . Lamont cannot appreciate the rising tide of resentment that is sweeping over the country. . . . They are trying to ’gang’ us. . . . Maybe they have settled German reparations but they did it the worst damned way they could.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324128) He wouldn’t extend his one-year debt moratorium and rejected French and British proposals for deferring upcoming payments; he forced France to default. So on the eve of Hitler’s advent, the Allies were squabbling over moldy financial issues that had bedeviled them for years.
从华盛顿回来后，伊根表示他从未见过总统对某个问题如此情绪激动。总统发表了一番充满愤怒、自怜和无可奈何的讲话。“拉蒙特完全搞错了这件事，”胡佛坚定地表示，这也反映了广泛的民意。“如果说有件事是美国人民不喜欢、也绝不会容忍的，那就是这种针对他们的联合……拉蒙特体会不到席卷全国的这股日益高涨的怨气……他们想联手整我们……也许他们解决了德国赔款问题，但他们用的是最糟糕、最缺德的方式。”他拒绝延长自己为期一年的债务延期偿付令，也驳回了法国和英国关于推迟即将到期付款的提议；最终他逼得法国违约。于是，在希特勒上台前夕，盟国仍在为那些纠缠多年的陈腐财政问题争吵不休。

The Morgan-Hoover feud over debt was mild compared with their debate over short selling on Wall Street. Moody and isolated, taciturn and stony-faced, Hoover now shared the average American’s view of Wall Street as a giant casino rigged by professionals. He saw the stock market as a report card on his performance, and it showed consistently failing grades. He came to believe in a Democratic conspiracy to drive down stocks by selling them short—that is, by selling borrowed shares in the hope of buying them back later at a cheaper price.
相比华尔街卖空问题上的激烈交锋，摩根与胡佛在债务上的争执反倒显得温和了。此时的胡佛郁郁寡欢、形单影只，沉默寡言、面色冷峻，他对华尔街的看法已与普通美国人无异——认为那不过是个被专业人士操纵的巨型赌场。他将股市视作自己执政成绩的晴雨表，而这张表上始终亮着不及格的红灯。他开始相信存在一个民主党人的阴谋：他们通过卖空股票来打压股价——即借入股票卖出，指望日后以更低价格买回。

The “bear raiders” first achieved notoriety in the 1930 suicide market. The master was Bernard E. “Sell-’Em Ben” Smith, a twenties pool speculator who was trounced by rising prices in 1929. That October, he suddenly came into his own and whooped it up on the day of the crash, shouting “Sell ’em all! They’re not worth anything.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324316) Such tales convinced Hoover of malevolent forces at work in the market. He began to compile lists of people in the bear cabal and even claimed to know they met every Sunday afternoon to plot the week’s destruction![16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324478) Hoover’s obsession was fed by confidants. Senator Frederick Walcott of Connecticut told Hoover that Bernard Baruch, John J. Raskob, and other Wall Street Democrats were planning bear raids to defeat his reelection.
“空头袭击者”最早在 1930 年的股市中声名狼藉。领军人物是伯纳德·E·“卖掉他们·本”·史密斯，一位 20 年代的投机商，曾在 1929 年因股价上涨而惨败。那年 10 月，他时来运转，在崩盘之日大肆狂欢，高喊：“全卖了！它们一文不值。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324316) 这类故事让胡佛确信市场中有恶意势力在作祟。他开始编纂空头集团的名单，甚至声称知道他们每周日下午集会，策划一周的破坏行动！ [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324478) 胡佛的执念被亲信们不断煽动。康涅狄格州参议员弗雷德里克·沃尔科特告诉胡佛，伯纳德·巴鲁克、约翰·J·拉斯科布等华尔街民主党人正策划空头袭击，以破坏他的连任。

Hoover thought Stock Exchange officials should openly denounce the culprits. In January 1932, he called Stock Exchange president Richard Whitney to the White House for a verbal drubbing. He said short sellers were preventing an economic rebound and warned that unless Whitney curbed them, he would ask Congress to investigate the Exchange and possibly impose Federal regulation. Whitney refused to admit any danger in short selling. Privately Morgan partners mocked Hoover’s obsession as absurd and fantastic, but they couldn’t dissuade him from his vendetta.
胡佛认为证券交易所的官员应当公开谴责那些罪魁祸首。1932 年 1 月，他将证券交易所总裁理查德·惠特尼召到白宫，当面狠狠训斥了一顿。胡佛指出，卖空行为正在阻碍经济复苏，并警告说，如果惠特尼不加以遏制，他将要求国会调查证券交易所，甚至可能实施联邦监管。惠特尼拒绝承认卖空有什么危险。私下里，摩根公司的合伙人嘲笑胡佛的执着简直是荒唐离谱，但他们也无法劝他放弃这场报复行动。

Although fearing that public hearings would dredge up “discouraging filth” and sabotage recovery efforts, in 1932 Hoover asked the Senate Banking and Currency Committee to start an inquiry into short selling. Wall Street bankers were so upset that Lamont lunched at the White House with Hoover and Secretary of State Stimson, trying to spike the inquiry. Hoover said destructive short sellers had offset his beneficial measures, a remark that led to a heated exchange about the hearings. “I tried to make clear to the President that if such an enquiry was encouraged to run riot it would create nothing but uneasiness throughout the country and would help to defeat the very constructive ends to which he was leading us,” Lamont said.[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324652)
尽管担心公开听证会会揭露“令人沮丧的丑闻”并破坏复苏努力，胡佛还是在 1932 年要求参议院银行与货币委员会开始对卖空行为进行调查。华尔街银行家们对此非常不满，以至于拉蒙特与胡佛和国务卿史汀生在白宫共进午餐，试图阻止这项调查。胡佛表示，破坏性的卖空者抵消了他推行的有利措施，这番话引发了一场关于听证会的激烈争论。“我试图让总统明白，如果这样的调查被放任自流，只会在全国范围内制造不安，反而会阻碍他引领我们走向的那些建设性目标，”拉蒙特说道。

In April, the first witness was Richard Whitney, who called Hoover’s charges “purely ridiculous.” Even as the hearings commenced, Hoover and Lamont were secretly trading barbed remarks about short selling. Hoover blamed the bears for everything—low public confidence, business stagnation, and falling prices. Lamont’s reply was candid to the point of comic cruelty. Responding to Hoover’s contention that “real values” were being destroyed by bear raids, he asked, “But what can be called ’real value’ if a security has no earnings and pays no dividends?”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324845) He blamed 99 percent of the market’s decline on poor business.
4 月，首位证人是 Richard Whitney，他称 Hoover 的指控“纯属荒谬”。听证会刚开始，Hoover 和 Lamont 就在私下就卖空问题互相讥讽。Hoover 将所有问题——公众信心低迷、商业停滞、物价下跌——都归咎于空头。Lamont 的回复直白得近乎残忍可笑。针对 Hoover 关于“真实价值”正被空头袭击摧毁的观点，他反问道：“但如果一只证券既无盈利又不分红，又怎能称之为‘真实价值’呢？” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2324845) 他将市场下跌的 99%归咎于商业状况不佳。

The press had a dandy time ridiculing the Senate bear hunt, which never unearthed a Democratic conspiracy. Nevertheless, at the end of April, a subcommittee broadened the hearings to include pools and market manipulations of the 1920s. The machinations of the RCA pool were unfolded before the public. Walter E. Sachs of Goldman, Sachs had to explain the losses of Eddie Cantor and forty thousand other investors in the Goldman Sachs Trading Corporation. Something curious now happened: as the hearings shifted from present to past, memories of the crash grew in the public mind. At first, Main Street smirked at the crash as a Calvinist thunderbolt hurled at big-city sinners. Only now, when the crash was seen as a forerunner of depression, did public rage against the bankers crystallize.
媒体对参议院调查股市操纵一事大肆嘲讽，这场调查始终未能挖出什么民主党阴谋。然而到了四月底，一个小组委员会将听证范围扩大，涉及 20 世纪 20 年代的联合操纵基金和股市操纵行为。美国无线电公司联合操纵基金的阴谋诡计被公之于众。高盛集团的沃尔特·E·萨克斯不得不解释埃迪·坎托及其他四万名投资者在高盛交易公司中的损失。此时一件耐人寻味的事情发生了：随着听证会焦点从当下转向过去，公众对股市崩盘的记忆愈发清晰。起初，普通民众还将崩盘视为加尔文教派劈向大城市罪人的雷霆。直到现在，当崩盘被视作大萧条的前兆时，公众对银行家的怒火才真正凝聚起来。

Amid the controversy, Hoover had to deal with a serious slump in the bond market—where short selling was prohibited. Corporate America couldn’t cope with the debt accumulated in the 1920s, much of it to finance takeovers. Many bonds defaulted and in extreme cases dropped 10, 20, or 30 points between sales, threatening the banking system. If savings banks couldn’t cash in bonds, they might have no money to pay off depositors, possibly causing runs and failures. The upshot was a Morgan-led operation to halt the bond market slide. Thirty-five banks pledged \$100 million to buy high-quality bonds in a pool nicknamed the Stars and Stripes Forever. It was chaired by Lamont, who sported more titles than the mikado during this period. The bank touted the patriotic nature of the venture, but it was again that Morgan specialty—public service for profit. The bank considered bonds seriously undervalued and had excess cash on hand during the Depression. “If the organization of the Corporation . . . should have any degree of reassuring effect upon the public so much the better,” J.P. Morgan and Company told the Paris partners.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325032)
在争议声中，胡佛不得不应对债券市场的严重低迷——当时卖空已被禁止。美国企业界无力偿还 20 年代积累的债务，其中大部分用于融资收购。许多债券出现违约，极端情况下在交易期间跌幅高达 10、20 甚至 30 个点，这威胁到了银行体系。如果储蓄银行无法兑现债券，可能就没有钱支付储户，进而引发挤兑和破产。最终结果是摩根牵头的一项行动，旨在阻止债券市场下滑。35 家银行承诺出资 1 亿美元，组建一个名为“星条旗永不落”的基金池来购买优质债券。该基金由拉蒙特主持，他在这一时期拥有的头衔比天皇还多。银行大力宣扬这项事业的爱国性质，但这又是摩根的拿手好戏——以公共服务之名行盈利之实。银行认为债券被严重低估，且在大萧条期间手头现金充裕。“如果这个公司组织起来……” “若能对公众起到任何程度的安抚作用，那就更好了，”J.P. Morgan and Company 对巴黎的合伙人表示。 [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325032)

Lamont kept Hoover posted on the pool. In the bond market operation, some cynics spied a move to improve Republican prospects in the fall election—as if Hoover would field his own hard-charging bulls against the bears. If so, the strategy almost backfired on Hoover. Lamont used the pool as a bargaining chip and threatened to disband it unless the short-selling hearings were canceled. In the end, the pool went ahead and made a tidy profit. The hearings would drag on and eventually assume dimensions unforeseen in early 1932. They would finally take their name from a new subcommittee counsel, Ferdinand Pecora, appointed in January 1933. The Pecora hearings would lead straight to Glass-Steagall and the dismemberment of the House of Morgan.
拉蒙特随时向胡佛汇报股市联营基金的情况。在债券市场操作中，一些冷眼旁观者认为这是为共和党在秋季选举中改善前景的举动——仿佛胡佛会派出自己猛冲猛打的多头军团来对抗空头。若真如此，这个策略几乎让胡佛自食其果。拉蒙特把联营基金当作谈判筹码，威胁说除非停止卖空调查听证会，否则就解散基金。最终，联营基金继续运作并赚取了可观的利润。而听证会则拖延下去，最终演变成 1932 年初难以预料的局面。它们最终以 1933 年 1 月任命的新小组委员会顾问费迪南德·佩科拉命名。佩科拉听证会直接导致了格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案的出台和摩根财团的瓦解。

IN the autumn of 1932, Hoover presided over one last humiliation—a nationwide banking crisis. Three years of deflation had eroded the collateral behind many loans. As banks called them in, the business slump worsened and produced more bank runs and failures. Before 1932, the thousands of bank closings were mostly confined to small rural banks. Then, that October, Nevada’s governor shut the state’s banks. There followed a frightening crescendo of state bank closings—euphemistically called holidays—climaxed by an eight-day closing of Michigan banks in February. The contagion spread so fast that thirty-eight states had shut their banks by Roosevelt’s inauguration.
1932 年秋，胡佛总统经历了最后一次屈辱——一场全国性的银行危机。三年的通货紧缩已侵蚀了许多贷款的抵押品价值。随着银行催收贷款，商业衰退进一步加剧，引发了更多的银行挤兑和倒闭。1932 年之前，数千家银行倒闭主要集中在农村小银行。然而那年十月，内华达州州长下令关闭了该州的所有银行。此后，各州银行倒闭（美其名曰“银行假期”）的势头愈发令人恐慌，并在二月份密歇根州银行停业八天时达到顶点。这场危机蔓延之快，以致罗斯福就职时已有三十八个州的银行关门停业。

Between the November election and the March 1933 inauguration was a time of paralysis and glowering hostility between Hoover and Roosevelt. Irritated, beleaguered, and resentful, the president refused to undertake new initiatives without Roosevelt’s cooperation; Roosevelt, on the other hand, wanted to wait until he assumed full power. For the House of Morgan, it was a season of peril. Through three consecutive Republican terms, it had probably enjoyed better access to Washington than any other bankers in American history. Under Hoover, the president was a telephone call away. Sometimes Morgan power had seemed as awesome as crude left-wing propaganda would have it. Now the bank combated threats to its survival as the political wheel came full circle.
从 11 月的大选到 1933 年 3 月的就职典礼，这期间是一段瘫痪期，胡佛与罗斯福之间剑拔弩张，充满敌意。胡佛总统既恼怒、四面楚歌又愤愤不平，在没有罗斯福配合的情况下拒绝采取任何新举措；而罗斯福则希望等到自己大权在握再行动。对摩根银行来说，这是一个危机四伏的季节。历经连续三届共和党执政，它可能比美国历史上任何银行家都更接近华盛顿的权力中心。在胡佛任期内，总统一个电话就能联系上。有时摩根银行的权力之大，简直就像粗俗的左翼宣传所描绘的那样令人敬畏。如今，随着政治轮盘转了一圈，这家银行不得不为自身的存亡而战。

As early as 1929, Hoover advanced the idea of separating commercial and investment banking, a notion that now took hold. It appeared in a banking bill introduced by Senator Carter Glass as early as 1930 and formed part of the Democratic party platform in 1932. During the campaign, Roosevelt blamed Hoover for the speculative binge of 1929 and the spate of foreign loans that left a bloody trail of defaults. After Bolivia became the first Latin American debtor to default in 1931, nearly every Latin American government followed suit.
早在 1929 年，胡佛就提出了分离商业银行与投资银行的构想，这一理念如今终于落地生根。1930 年参议员卡特·格拉斯提出的银行法案中已包含这一条款，1932 年更成为民主党竞选纲领的核心。竞选期间，罗斯福将 1929 年的投机狂潮和引发一连串违约的海外贷款乱象归咎于胡佛。自 1931 年玻利维亚成为首个违约的拉美债务国后，几乎所有拉美国家都纷纷效仿。

After Hoover’s “bear raid” crusade, the president’s departure wasn’t mourned at the Corner. Russell Leffingwell and Parker Gilbert formed a Morgan minority that voted for Roosevelt. “The truth is,” Leffingwell confessed to Walter Lippmann, “I can’t bring myself to vote for a desperate man who wishes to continue desperate remedies for a desperate situation.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325305) Nor was it self-evident that FDR would emerge as an enemy. Genial and aristocratic, he chastised Hoover as a big spender and advocated balanced budgets; he looked more bland than bold. Leffingwell almost patronized Roosevelt, calling him “a pleasant, kindly, well-meaning chap with a pleasing smile.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325516)
胡佛的“空头打压”运动结束后，华尔街对这位总统的离任并未感到惋惜。拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔和帕克·吉尔伯特这两位摩根家族的少数派，把票投给了罗斯福。“说实话，”莱芬韦尔向沃尔特·李普曼坦言，“我实在无法投票给一个绝望的人，他企图用孤注一掷的手段来挽救绝望的局势。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325305) 当时，富兰克林·D·罗斯福会站到对立面，也并非显而易见。这位温文尔雅的贵族斥责胡佛挥霍无度，主张预算平衡；他看上去平淡无奇，而非锋芒毕露。莱芬韦尔几乎是在用恩赐的态度看待罗斯福，称他为“一个和蔼可亲、心地善良、面带悦人笑容的老好人。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325516)

Socially, FDR fit the Morgan mold far more than Hoover. Leffingwell—who knew Roosevelt from his own Treasury days, when Roosevelt was in the Navy Department—excitedly set down his pedigree for Vivian Smith of Morgan Grenfell. He noted Roosevelt’s Groton and Harvard education, his Hudson River upbringing and old New York Dutch ancestry, and his employment at the Wall Street firm of Carter, Ledyard, and Milburn, which defended corporate clients against antitrust actions. Leffingwell ended sarcastically, “All that is the background of the man who is a peril to American institutions according to Hoover the foreign mining engineer.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325683) Lamont also knew Roosevelt, having rented his East Sixty-fifth Street house. Before the inauguration, he phoned him and busily dashed off “Dear Frank” letters.
在社会关系上，罗斯福比胡佛更符合摩根的圈层。曾在财政部任职时就认识罗斯福（当时罗斯福在海军部）的莱芬韦尔，兴奋地向摩根建富的维维安·史密斯介绍了罗斯福的出身背景：格罗顿和哈佛的教育经历，哈德逊河畔的成长环境，以及纽约荷兰裔的古老血统；还曾就职于华尔街的卡特、莱迪亚德和米尔本律师事务所，为面临反垄断诉讼的企业客户辩护。莱芬韦尔最后讽刺道：“这就是那个被外国矿业工程师胡佛视为美国制度威胁的人的全部背景。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325683) 拉蒙特也认识罗斯福，曾租住过他在东六十五街的房子。就职典礼前，拉蒙特还打电话给他，并匆忙写下“亲爱的弗兰克”的信函。

If the winter interregnum suggested possible good relations, there were also warning signs. Late in the summer of 1932, Leffingwell and Roosevelt had an exchange that previewed, in miniature, the titanic feud to come. In August, Leffingwell sent “Frank” a note deriding the banking reforms being advanced by Carter Glass; in it, he tried to strike a note of camaraderie and shared values: “You and I know that we cannot cure the present deflation and depression by punishing the villains, real or imaginary, of the first post-war decade, and that when it comes down to the day of reckoning nobody gets very far with all this prohibition and regulation stuff.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325897) Far from indulging Leffingwell, Roosevelt threw cold water in his face: “I wish we could get from the bankers themselves an admission that in the 1927 to 1929 period there were grave abuses and that the bankers themselves now support wholeheartedly methods to prevent recurrence thereof. Can’t bankers see their own advantage in such a course?”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326064) It would be the tragedy of the House of Morgan that it couldn’t see the advantage in such a course. The public demanded a *mea culpa* for 1929, which the bankers wouldn’t provide. As Leffingwell told Roosevelt, “The bankers were not in fact responsible for 1927-29 and the politicians were. Why then should the bankers make a false confession?”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326198) Yet such was Leffing-well’s disgust with Hoover’s tariffs, isolationism, and reparations policy that he gladly voted for FDR.
如果说冬季的过渡期预示着双方可能建立良好关系，那么也不乏预警信号。1932 年夏末，莱芬韦尔与罗斯福之间的一次交流，已预先勾勒出日后那场激烈冲突的雏形。八月，莱芬韦尔给"弗兰克"送去一纸便函，嘲讽卡特·格拉斯推行的银行业改革方案。信中，他试图展现一种同袍之情与价值观的共鸣："你我都清楚，靠惩罚战后第一个十年里那些真实或想象出的恶棍，根本治不了眼下的通缩与萧条；真要到了算总账那天，这套禁止与监管的把戏也走不了多远。" [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2325897) 罗斯福非但没有迎合莱芬韦尔，反而劈头浇了盆冷水："我倒希望银行家们能承认，1927 到 1929 年间确实存在严重弊端，并且现在能真心实意地支持采取办法防止重蹈覆辙。" 难道银行家们看不出这条路对他们自己也有利吗？” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326064) 摩根家族最终未能看清这条路的利益所在，这成了它的悲剧。公众要求银行家为 1929 年的危机认罪，但他们拒绝这样做。正如莱芬韦尔对罗斯福所说：“事实上，1927-29 年的责任不在银行家，而在政客。那银行家为什么要虚假忏悔呢？”” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326198) 然而，莱芬韦尔对胡佛的关税政策、孤立主义以及赔款政策深恶痛绝，以至于他欣然投票支持了富兰克林·德拉诺·罗斯福。

The bank campaigned to slip Leffingwell into a Treasury post, which became a litmus test of Roosevelt’s financial soundness. All aflutter, Monty Norman told Lamont, “I shall wait to hear that R.C.L. is established before I can rest happily.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326342) When Senator Carter Glass was approached about taking the Treasury secretary job again, he said he would want to hire two Morgan men and former deputies: Leffingwell and Parker Gilbert.[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326533) Walter Lippmann joined the bandwagon, but Roosevelt cringed: “We simply can’t tie up with 23.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326680) The shorthand betrayed a knowingness that would work to the bank’s disadvantage. Despite his failure to get a Treasury post, Leffingwell would remain a trusted friend and adviser of Roosevelt’s and something of a black sheep on Wall Street for his partial support of the administration.
该银行游说将莱芬韦尔安插进财政部，这成了检验罗斯福金融稳健性的试金石。蒙蒂·诺曼慌乱地对拉蒙特说：“我得等到听说 R.C.L.上任后才能安心。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326342) 当参议员卡特·格拉斯被问及是否愿意再次出任财政部长时，他表示想雇用两位摩根旧部兼前副手：莱芬韦尔和帕克·吉尔伯特。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326533) 沃尔特·李普曼也加入了这一阵营，但罗斯福却退缩了：“我们绝不能与 23 号捆绑在一起。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326680) 这个暗语暴露了内情，最终对银行不利。尽管未能获得财政部的职位，莱芬韦尔仍将是罗斯福信任的朋友和顾问，并且因部分支持政府而成为华尔街的异类。

The person who probably shot down Leffingwell’s trial balloon was Ferdinand Pecora, the fifty-three-year-old former assistant district attorney from New York, who took over the Senate’s Wall Street probe in January 1933. Smoking a blunt cigar, his shirtsleeves rolled up, the hard-bitten Pecora captured the public’s attention. For six months, the hearings had been stalled. Republicans and Democrats, with fine impartiality, had feared fat cats of both parties might be named and united in a conspiracy of silence. With Pecora as counsel, the hearings acquired a new, irresistible momentum. They would afford a secret history of the crash, a sobering postmortem of the twenties that would blacken the name of bankers for a generation. From now on, they would be called banksters.
捅破莱芬威尔试探气球的人，很可能是五十三岁的费迪南德·佩科拉——这位前纽约助理地区检察官，于 1933 年 1 月接手了参议院对华尔街的调查。他叼着粗雪茄，卷起衬衫袖子，一副硬汉做派的佩科拉牢牢抓住了公众的注意力。此前六个月，听证会一直停滞不前。共和党人和民主党人怀着"出色"的公正态度，生怕牵出两党的大金主，于是心照不宣地联手封口。而佩科拉担任顾问后，听证会势如破竹，不可阻挡。这场调查将揭开股市崩盘的秘史，对二十年代进行一场令人警醒的清算，让银行家的名声在整整一代人时间里蒙上污点。从此以后，他们将被称作"银行强盗"。

Even before Roosevelt’s inauguration, Pecora turned his investigative spotlight on the National City Bank, showing eminent bankers in sordid poses, particularly the bank’s head, Charles E. Mitchell, a member of the Black Thursday rescue squad. Through Pecora, the public got a view of bankers scheming while supposedly protecting the public. Pecora revealed that the \$12-million Morgan loan to preserve National City’s merger with the Corn Exchange Bank had represented more than 5 percent of Morgans’ net worth, sticking the bank with a substantial loss. It was also disclosed that to buffer crash losses at National City, one hundred top officers had borrowed \$2.4 million, interest free, from a special morale loan fund—loans never repaid.
甚至在罗斯福就职之前，佩科拉就将调查焦点对准了国民城市银行，揭露了那些显赫银行家的丑恶嘴脸，尤其是该行行长查尔斯·E·米切尔——这位“黑色星期四”救市小组成员。通过佩科拉，公众看到了银行家在声称保护公众利益的同时却在暗中谋划。佩科拉披露，摩根为保住国民城市银行与谷物交易银行合并而提供的 1200 万美元贷款，竟超过摩根净值的 5%，导致该行蒙受重大损失。此外还揭露，为缓冲国民城市银行在股灾中的损失，100 名高级管理人员从一笔特殊的“士气贷款基金”中借走了 240 万美元且免息——而这些贷款从未偿还。

Pecora also studied the operations of the National City Company, whose 1900 salesmen had unloaded risky Latin American bonds on the masses. It emerged that in touting bonds from Brazil, Peru, Chile, and Cuba to investors, the bank had hushed up internal reports on problems in these countries. After bank examiners criticized sugar loans made by the parent bank, the securities affiliate sold them as bonds to investors, an example of how commercial banks might palm off bad loans through securities affiliates. Pecora cited the case of an Edgar D. Brown of Pottsville, Pennsylvania, whose National City salesman had pushed him into “a bewildering array of Viennese, German, Peruvian, Chilean, Rhenish, Hungarian, and Irish government obligations.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2326843)
佩科拉还研究了国家城市公司的运作，其 1900 名销售员曾向大众兜售高风险拉丁美洲债券。调查发现，该银行在向投资者推销巴西、秘鲁、智利和古巴债券时，刻意隐瞒了关于这些国家问题的内部报告。当银行监管机构批评母行发放的蔗糖贷款后，其证券子公司便将这些贷款包装成债券出售给投资者，这凸显了商业银行如何通过证券子公司转嫁不良贷款。佩科拉援引了宾夕法尼亚州波茨维尔市埃德加·D·布朗的案例——国家城市公司的销售员硬塞给他"一堆令人眼花缭乱的维也纳、德国、秘鲁、智利、莱茵、匈牙利和爱尔兰政府债券"。

Another supposed hero of Black Thursday was Albert H. Wiggin of Chase, a poker-playing clergyman’s son who sat on fifty-nine corporate boards. He was also exposed as being up to his ears in mischief. For six weeks in 1929, he had shorted shares of Chase stock and earned several million dollars; the speculation was backed by an \$8-million loan from Chase itself. For good measure, Wiggin had set up a Canadian securities company to avoid federal taxes. The stories of Chase and National City showed the extent to which the traditional distinction between savings and speculation had disappeared in the 1920s—a distinction the Glass-Steagall Act would seek to restore.
黑色星期四的另一位"英雄"是大通银行的阿尔伯特·H·威金，他出身牧师家庭却酷爱打牌，身兼 59 家公司董事席位。他还被曝出深陷各种乱象之中。1929 年，他连续六周做空大通银行股票，赚取数百万美元，而这场投机居然靠着大通银行自身提供的 800 万美元贷款支撑。更过分的是，威金还设立了一家加拿大证券公司来逃避联邦税。大通银行和 National City 的案例表明，20 世纪 20 年代储蓄与投机之间的传统界限已消失殆尽——而 Glass-Steagall Act 正是试图恢复这一界限。

The Pecora findings created a tidal wave of anger against Wall Street, and against this backdrop Roosevelt vetoed the Leffingwell nomination. As people followed the hearings on their farms and in their offices, on soup lines and in Hoovervilles, they became convinced that they’d been conned in the 1920s. Yesterday’s gods were no more than greedy little devils. Even most of Wall Street was shocked by this phase of the hearings. Senator Burton Wheeler of Montana said, “The best way to restore confidence in the banks would be to take these crooked presidents out of the banks and treat them the same way we treated Al Capone when he failed to pay his income tax.” Even Carter Glass, a staunch Morgan friend, joked nastily, “One banker in my state attempted to marry a white woman and they lynched him.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327011)
佩科拉调查结果在华尔街激起了一片愤怒的浪潮，在此背景下，罗斯福否决了莱芬韦尔的提名。当人们在自己的农场、办公室、施粥队伍里以及胡佛村中关注着这些听证会时，他们确信自己在上世纪 20 年代被欺骗了。昨日的众神不过是贪婪的小恶魔。就连华尔街的大部分人也对听证会的这一阶段感到震惊。蒙大拿州参议员伯顿·惠勒说：“恢复银行信心的最佳方式，就是把那些不诚实的银行行长从银行里揪出来，像对待因未缴纳所得税的阿尔·卡彭那样对待他们。”就连摩根家族的坚定盟友卡特·格拉斯也恶狠狠地开玩笑说：“我所在州的一位银行家试图娶一位白人女性，结果他们把他私刑处死了。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327011)

When Roosevelt took office on March 4, 1933, he ran up a flag of independence from Wall Street. That morning, Governor Herbert Lehman shut New York’s banks, and Richard Whitney mounted the podium to close the Stock Exchange. The financial massacre was complete: of twenty-five thousand banks in 1929, some seven thousand had now failed. Amid this atmosphere of financial ruin, a grim Roosevelt delivered a stinging indictment of the bankers: “The money changers have fled from their high seats in the temple of our civilization. We may now restore that temple to the ancient truths.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327201)
当罗斯福于 1933 年 3 月 4 日宣誓就职时，他升起了一面脱离华尔街的独立旗帜。当天上午，州长赫伯特·莱曼关闭了纽约的银行，理查德·惠特尼登上讲台，宣布证券交易所休市。这场金融大屠杀尘埃落定：1929 年存在的两万五千家银行中，已有约七千家倒闭。在这金融废墟的氛围中，面色严峻的罗斯福对银行家们发出了尖刻的指控：“兑换银钱的人已从我们文明殿堂的高座上逃逸。如今，我们可让这座殿堂重归古老的真理。”

To offer advice on the banking crisis, Lamont had telephoned Roosevelt and urged him to avoid drastic measures. This counsel reflected a faith not only in market mechanisms but in political expediency. As J. P. Morgan and Company cabled London: “There is great reluctance to contemplate any form of federal action which it might be difficult later on to get rid of”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327335) Roosevelt brushed aside Lamont’s tepid remedies and announced a sweeping week-long bank holiday; over five hundred banks never reopened. Along with an emergency bank bill, this tough action restored public confidence and revealed a new public receptivity to emergency measures. Throughout the New Deal, the House of Morgan would repeat the same political error: it would advocate marginal reforms, which would be dismissed as self-serving. Instead of devising its own alternative reform package, it settled for scare tactics.
为了应对银行危机，拉蒙特曾致电罗斯福，劝他避免采取激进措施。这一建议不仅反映了对市场机制的信心，也体现了对政治权宜之计的依赖。摩根公司（J. P. Morgan and Company）在给伦敦的电报中写道：“对于任何日后可能难以摆脱的联邦政府行动，人们极不情愿去考虑。”罗斯福无视了拉蒙特的温和补救方案，宣布了一项为期一周的全面银行休假；超过五百家银行再未开业。这项强硬举措连同紧急银行法案，恢复了公众信心，并显示出公众对紧急措施的新接受度。在整个“新政”时期，摩根财团一再重复同样的政治错误：它主张边际改革，却被视为自私自利。它没有设计自己的替代改革方案，而是止步于恐吓策略。

Despite these early Roosevelt rebuffs, Hoover’s bleak record made even Morgan bankers ripe for experimentation. Jack Morgan was at first ecstatic about FDR. “Of course, it is quite possible that some of his cures may be wrong ones, but, on the whole, things were so bad that almost any cure may do some good. ”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327586) In correspondence from the Morgan files of March 1933, the partners sound remarkably like other frightened Americans: they, too, needed a savior. Hadn’t they seen their own prescriptions fail? After Roosevelt’s March 12 fireside chat and the reopening of the banks, 23 Wall reported with relief to Morgan Grenfell: “The whole country is filled with admiration for President Roosevelt’s actions. The record of his accomplishment in just one week seems incredible because we have never experienced anything like it before.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327750) The Stock Exchange soared and posted a 54-percent gain for 1933.
尽管早期受到了罗斯福的冷遇，但胡佛执政时期的惨淡景象，让即使是摩根银行家们也愿意放手一搏。杰克·摩根起初对罗斯福兴奋不已：“当然，他开出的药方可能有不对的地方，但总的来说，情况已经糟糕到几乎任何疗法都能带来些好处的地步。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327586) 从摩根档案中 1933 年 3 月的往来信函来看，这些合伙人听起来与其他惊恐的美国人如出一辙：他们也需要一位救世主。难道他们不也眼睁睁看着自己开的药方失效了吗？在罗斯福 3 月 12 日的炉边谈话以及银行重新开业后，华尔街 23 号如释重负地向摩根格伦费尔公司报告：“全国上下对罗斯福总统的举措充满钦佩。他在短短一周内取得的成就简直不可思议，因为我们从未经历过类似的事情。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2327750) 股市随之飙升，1933 年全年涨幅高达 54%。

The House of Morgan couldn’t see that, like a black speck on the horizon, the Pecora hearings were a hurricane heading in its direction. During this false honeymoon, the House of Morgan committed a famous act of apostasy: it applauded Roosevelt for taking America off the gold standard in April. It was hoped this would devalue the dollar, raise commodity prices, and reverse the lethal deflation. A radical measure in ordinary times, it was less controversial in 1933. Harking back to greenback currency (currency with no metal backing) and free-silver coinage, farmers and other debtors were reviving old inflationary nostrums from the days of William Jennings Bryan. Roosevelt was under pressure to choose *some* inflationary expedient. Gold was moving abroad in large quantities, and there was fear it would contract the monetary base, feeding deflation.
摩根家族没能看清，地平线上那个小黑点般的佩科拉听证会，其实是一股正朝他们袭来的飓风。在这个虚假的蜜月期里，摩根家族做出了一个著名的变节之举：他们为罗斯福在四月份让美国脱离金本位而鼓掌叫好。人们希望此举能压低美元、抬高商品价格，从而扭转致命的通货紧缩。这在平时算是激进举措，但在 1933 年却争议不大。回想到绿背纸币（无金属担保的货币）和白银自由铸造运动，农民和其他债务人正重拾威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩时代那些老掉牙的通胀偏方。罗斯福面临压力，必须选择某种通胀权宜之计。黄金正大量流向国外，人们担心这会收缩货币基础，加剧通货紧缩。

The House of Morgan provided intellectual support for leaving gold. Russell Leffingwell lunched with Walter Lippmann and advised him on a newspaper column favoring an end to a rigid gold standard. Leffingwell saw the need for higher commodity prices. He also felt the downward drift of European currencies had led to an overvalued dollar, hurting U.S. exports. After lunch, Leffingwell said, “Walter, you’ve got to explain to the people why we can no longer afford to chain ourselves to the gold standard. Then maybe Roosevelt, who I’m sure agrees, will be able to act.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328000) Lippmann let Leffingwell vet the article and sharpen its fine points.
摩根大通为放弃金本位制提供了智力支持。拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔与沃尔特·李普曼共进午餐时，建议他在报纸专栏中撰文支持终结僵化的金本位制。莱芬韦尔意识到需要提高大宗商品价格，同时也感到欧洲货币的持续贬值导致美元被高估，损害了美国出口。午饭后，莱芬韦尔说道：“沃尔特，你得向民众解释为什么我们不能再受制于金本位了。这样一来，我相信罗斯福也会同意，他就能采取行动了。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328000) 李普曼让莱芬韦尔审阅文章并打磨其论点。

Leffingwell had great intellectual stature among the New Dealers. When Roosevelt later accused Treasury Secretary Henry Morgenthau, Jr., of sounding like Leffingwell, Morgenthau retorted, “I wish I had half his brains.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328195) One of the more radical brain trusters, Columbia professor Rexford G. Tugwell, noted Leffingwell’s influence on Roosevelt in the gold decision. “Consulting widely among New York acquaintances he regarded as public-minded—Russell Leffingwell of the House of Morgan was perhaps the most trusted—he had concluded that gold must be sequestered entirely, hoarding forbidden, and shipment abroad prohibited.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328373) The day after Walter Lippmann’s column appeared, Roosevelt publicly advocated an end to gold. Through a series of executive orders, he prohibited gold exports and hoarding. Congress in June abrogated the clause in bond issues that mandated compulsory payment in gold coin. Even Jack Morgan smilingly applauded the move. For those who remembered Pierpont’s 1895 rescue of the gold standard and Morgan efforts to put countries back on gold throughout the twenties, such statements were wondrous to behold, proof that the safe nineteenth-century world of neoclassic economics had been turned topsy-turvy.
莱芬韦尔在新政人士中拥有极高的思想威望。当罗斯福后来指责财政部长小亨利·摩根索的言论听起来像莱芬韦尔时，摩根索反驳道：“我要是能有他一半的头脑就好了。” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328195) 更激进的智囊团成员之一、哥伦比亚大学教授雷克斯福德·G·特格韦尔指出莱芬韦尔在黄金决策上对罗斯福的影响。“他广泛咨询纽约那些他认为有公心的熟人——摩根集团的拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔或许是最受信任的一位——最终得出结论，必须完全封存黄金，禁止囤积，并禁止运往国外。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328373) 在沃尔特·李普曼专栏发表的第二天，罗斯福公开主张结束金本位。通过一系列行政命令，他禁止了黄金出口和囤积。国会于六月废除了债券发行中强制以金币支付的条款。就连杰克·摩根也微笑着对这一举措表示赞赏。 对于那些还记得皮尔庞特在 1895 年拯救金本位制、以及摩根在整个二十年代极力帮助各国恢复金本位制的人来说，这样的言论简直令人叹为观止，活生生证明了那个安稳的 19 世纪新古典经济学世界已经天翻地覆。

Many financial experts were in a state of shock, as if the ship of state’s rudder had been violently torn off. Budget Director Lewis Douglas intoned, “This is the end of Western civilization.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328548) Bernard Baruch felt a similar alarm at this sudden turn in financial policy: “It can’t be defended except as mob rule. Maybe the country doesn’t know it yet, but I think we may find that we’ve been in a revolution more drastic than the French Revolution.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328720) The perplexity was greater in Europe, where bankers wondered why the United States had cheapened its currency despite a trade surplus and an adequate gold store. When informed that Monty Norman thought the move would plunge the world into bankruptcy, Roosevelt—who called him Old Pink Whiskers—just laughed. The gold embargo showed that both the United States and England had renounced world leadership in favor of domestic ends. The world was adrift in a full-blown war of economic nationalism, fought with competitive currency devaluations.
许多金融专家震惊不已，仿佛国家这艘大船的舵被猛地扯掉了。预算主任刘易斯·道格拉斯哀叹道：“这是西方文明的终结。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328548) 伯纳德·巴鲁克对金融政策的这一突然转向同样感到恐慌：“除了暴民统治，这根本说不过去。也许这个国家还不知道，但我觉得我们恐怕正经历一场比法国大革命更剧烈的革命。” [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328720) 欧洲的困惑更甚，银行家们百思不解——美国明明贸易顺差且黄金储备充足，为何要贬值本币。当得知蒙蒂·诺曼认为此举将把世界拖入破产深渊时，罗斯福——那个被称作“老粉红胡子”的人——只是哈哈大笑。黄金禁运表明，美英两国都已放弃世界领袖责任，转而只顾国内利益。一场全面爆发的经济民族主义战争让世界随波逐流，各国以竞相货币贬值为武器互相厮杀。

For people schooled in the old economic verities, it was a disorienting new world. Bernard S. Carter, a Morgan et Compagnie partner in Paris, told J. P. Morgan partners how a Romanian banker walked into Morgans’ place Vendôme office and launched into the following diatribe:
对于受传统经济真理熏陶的人来说，这是一个令人困惑的新世界。巴黎摩根公司的合伙人伯纳德·S·卡特向 J·P·摩根的合伙人讲述，一位罗马尼亚银行家走进摩根公司所在的旺多姆广场办公室，发表了如下一番慷慨激昂的言论：

> Here are the 3 great financial countries of the world, who have been preaching the sanctity of contracts to us ever since the war, and who have now all resorted to repudiation of one kind or another in their turn. First England goes off the gold standard, then France refuses to pay her debts to America, and now America goes off the gold standard. I guess we Roumanians are not such crooks after all![40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328901)
> 当今世界有三大金融强国，自战后以来一直向我们宣扬契约的神圣性，如今却个个轮番搞起了某种形式的违约。先是英国放弃金本位，接着法国拒绝偿还对美国的债务，现在美国也放弃了金本位。我看我们罗马尼亚人终究还没那么不要脸嘛！ [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2328901)

By summer, Roosevelt was chiding the gold standard and other “old fetishes of so-called international bankers” and praising the brave new world of managed national currencies.[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329113) Although by background an internationalist and a strong supporter of the League of Nations, FDR pursued domestic recovery at the expense of global economic leadership. More cosmopolitan than Hoover, he had a vestigial fear of British finance. He ended British war-debt payments, as Leffingwell had advised, but couldn’t suppress a view of British bankers as a devious bunch out to trick the Yanks. “The trouble is that when you sit around the table with a Britisher he usually gets 80\$\$\$ of the deal and you get what’s left,” Roosevelt explained.[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329285)
到夏天时，罗斯福已经开始指责金本位制和其他所谓国际银行家的旧迷信，并赞美管理型国家货币这一勇敢的新世界。 [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329113) 尽管从背景上看，罗斯福是一个国际主义者，也是国际联盟的坚定支持者，但他以牺牲全球经济领导地位为代价推动国内复苏。他比胡佛更懂世界事务，却对英国金融抱有一种残存的戒心。他采纳了莱芬韦尔的建议，终止了英国战争债务的偿付，但仍难以摆脱对英国银行家的看法——认为他们是一群狡猾之徒，总想算计美国人。罗斯福解释说：“问题在于，当你和一个英国人坐在谈判桌前，他通常能拿到交易的八成，而你只能拿到剩下的。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329285)

So the early New Deal threatened the House of Morgan in two ways: the Pecora hearings were exposing practices that could bring fresh regulation to Wall Street. And the White House attitude toward European finance augured an end to the House of Morgan’s special diplomatic role of the 1920s. After an incestuous relationship with Washington in the twenties, the bank would suffer the curse of eternal banishment.
所以，早期的新政在两方面威胁到了摩根家族：佩科拉听证会曝光了可能给华尔街带来新监管的做法，而白宫对欧洲金融的态度则预示着摩根家族在 1920 年代的特殊外交角色将走向终结。在与华盛顿经历了二十年代的亲密关系后，这家银行将蒙受永久放逐的诅咒。

THAT spring, FDR urged the Senate Banking Committee to adopt a broader, more amorphous mandate to investigate “all the ramifications of bad banking.” It was nothing less than a license for a comprehensive inquest into Wall Street. The committee turned to private bankers—whom Pecora defined as men “who make their own rules and are not subject to examination”—with J. P. Morgan and Company topping the list. It was too much to expect America’s richest bankers to get off scot-free. What retrospective of the twenties would be complete without the bank that epitomized the decade’s power? As a former Republican party chairman said, “Never before in the history of the world has there been such a powerful centralized control over finance, industrial production, credit, and wages as is at this time vested in the Morgan group.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329420) It was time for Washington to storm the Bastille of Wall Street.
那年春天，罗斯福总统敦促参议院银行委员会采取一项更宽泛、更灵活的授权，调查“不良银行业的所有连锁反应”。这无异于为全面彻查华尔街颁发了一张通行证。委员会将目光投向了私人银行家——佩科拉将其定义为“自行制定规则且不受审查”的人——而 J.P.摩根公司首当其冲。想让美国最富有的银行家全身而退，未免太过天真。回顾二十年代，若不提及那家象征了十年权势的银行，又怎能算完整？正如一位前共和党主席所言：“世界历史上，从未有过像摩根集团这样，对金融、工业生产、信贷和工资拥有如此强大的集中控制权。” [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329420) 华盛顿是时候攻占华尔街这座巴士底狱了。

In Ferdinand Pecora, the committee’s \$255-a-month counsel, history provided a perfect foil for Morgan bankers. A Sicilian-born, anti-Tammany Democrat, he had thick, wavy black hair mixed with gray, a jaunty grin, and an assertive chin. A devoted Bull Mooser in 1912, he had switched to Wilson’s progressive Democrats in 1916. As assistant district attorney in New York, he took on tough assignments—from bucket shops to crooked banks, the Police Department to bail bondsmen—posting an 80-percent conviction rate. Even when his prosecutorial manner was mild, he had a talent for taunts and withering asides. He was also fearless and incorruptible and had rejected several offers from Wall Street law firms. When he took over the Senate probe, he thought he would be through before Roosevelt took office. Instead, the investigation went on until May 1934, producing ten thousand pages of testimony that filled up eight fat tomes.
在费迪南德·佩科拉这位月薪 255 美元的委员会法律顾问身上，历史为摩根银行家们找到了一个绝佳的对手。这位出生在西西里、反对坦慕尼协会的民主党人，有一头夹杂着灰白的浓密黑色卷发，常带着轻松的笑容，下巴透着果敢。1912 年，他曾是狂热的"公麋党"支持者，到 1916 年又转而投向了威尔逊的进步民主党。在担任纽约助理地方检察官期间，他接手过各种棘手案件——从非法投机商号到腐败银行，从警察局到保释担保人——保持着 80%的有罪判决率。即便他公诉时态度温和，也总能用犀利的嘲讽和冷言冷语直击要害。他无畏且廉洁，多次拒绝华尔街律师事务所的邀约。接手参议院调查时，他本以为在罗斯福就职前就能完成。不料，调查一直持续到 1934 年 5 月，最终形成了一万页证词，装订成厚厚的八卷。

At first, the House of Morgan snickered at the Pecora hearings, seeing them as a circus. Lamont thought they were a political ploy “designed to acquaint a curiosity-loving public with the nature and extent of our own banking institutions.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329567) With its fetish for secrecy, the bank tried to limit the inquiry’s scope. On March 22, 1933, Lamont and counsel John W. Davis—the 1924 Democratic presidential candidate, dubbed the Morgan prosecuting attorney—visited Pecora at his shabby, temporary offices at 285 Madison Avenue. Davis had assiduously protected the House of Morgan’s rights as a private bank and had written a New York State statute that exempted private banks from state inspection. Pecora was striking at an ancient privilege of gentleman bankers—keeping their capital position secret. On Davis’s advice, Lamont refused to give a statement of Morgan capital, opposed examination of the bank’s records, and insisted on the confidentiality of client accounts. As a close friend and near-neighbor of Jack Morgan’s and a fellow vestryman of Saint John’s of Lattingtown, Davis was in high dudgeon at any insinuation of Morgan dishonesty. He quickly elevated the affair into a matter of honor and constitutional rights. Two days later, he told Pecora he was “very chilly” to his request for five years of J. P. Morgan and Company balance sheets.
最初，摩根银行对 Pecora 听证会嗤之以鼻，视其为一场闹剧。Lamont 认为这是一个政治把戏，“旨在让好奇的公众了解我们自家银行机构的本质和规模。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329567) 这家银行对保密情有独钟，试图限制调查范围。1933 年 3 月 22 日，Lamont 和律师 John W. Davis——这位 1924 年民主党总统候选人，被称为摩根的控诉律师——来到 Pecora 位于麦迪逊大道 285 号的简陋临时办公室。Davis 一直竭力保护摩根银行作为私人银行的权利，并起草了一项纽约州法规，豁免私人银行接受州政府检查。Pecora 正在挑战绅士银行家们的一项古老特权——保密其资本状况。在 Davis 的建议下，Lamont 拒绝提供摩根银行的资本报表，反对检查银行记录，并坚持对客户账户保密。 作为杰克·摩根的好友兼近邻，以及拉廷顿圣约翰教堂的教区委员，戴维斯对任何暗示摩根不诚实的说法都极为恼火。他迅速将此事升级为关乎荣誉与宪法权利的问题。两天后，他告诉佩科拉，对于后者要求提供 J.P.摩根公司五年的资产负债表一事，他感到“非常冷淡”。

Along with Parker Gilbert, Lamont visited George Harrison of the New York Fed and tried to enlist his influence for withholding the annual statements. Not only did Harrison refuse, but in his diary he registered shock at the request. Pecora interpreted the Morgan refusal to answer his questions as barefaced defiance and waged war against the bank in the press and on Capitol Hill. He got the Senate to pass a resolution enabling the committee to investigate private banking—a timely reminder to Morgans that it remained unregulated only at government sufferance. Pecora had won. For over six weeks, his sleuths worked in a room at 23 Wall, sifting through records no outsider had ever before seen. In the sole concession to Morgan eminence, investigators stopped at six each evening, while their colleagues worked until midnight elsewhere on the Street.
与帕克·吉尔伯特一起，拉蒙特拜访了纽约联邦储备银行的乔治·哈里森，试图争取他的影响力来扣留年度报告。哈里森不仅拒绝，还在日记中记录了对这一请求的震惊。佩科拉将摩根拒绝回答问题的行为视为公然挑衅，并在媒体和国会山上向该银行宣战。他促使参议院通过决议，允许委员会调查私人银行业务——这及时提醒了摩根家族，其不受监管的地位仅凭政府默许。佩科拉赢得了胜利。在长达六周多的时间里，他的调查人员们在华尔街 23 号的一个房间里工作，筛选着外人从未见过的记录。唯一对摩根地位的让步是，调查人员每晚 6 点停止工作，而他们的同事在华尔街其他地方一直工作到午夜。

As bank image maker, Lamont tried to soften any impression that he was obstructing the investigation. On April 11, he wrote a clever letter to Roosevelt vowing cooperation; the bank would make political hay by yielding to the inevitable: “So far as this particular item is concerned, we haven’t the slightest hesitation at any time in showing our balance sheet to members of the Committee, and I may add that I think you would regard it as a highly satisfactory one.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329752) This last remark alluded to shared values, as if Lamont were reminding Roosevelt of his patrician background.
作为银行的形象设计师，拉蒙特竭力淡化任何可能让人觉得他在阻挠调查的印象。4 月 11 日，他给罗斯福写了一封巧妙的信，承诺配合调查；银行将通过顺应不可避免之事来获取政治资本：“就这一具体项目而言，我们随时都毫不犹豫地向委员会成员展示我们的资产负债表，我还可以补充一句，我认为您会认为这是一份非常令人满意的报表。” [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329752) 最后这句话暗示了共同的价值观，仿佛拉蒙特在提醒罗斯福他的贵族出身。

Jack Morgan was especially enraged by Pecora. He believed implicitly in Morgan integrity and interpreted any investigation, by definition, as a vendetta. He unpacked a colorful array of ethnic epithets; at age sixty-six, he wasn’t about to learn tolerance. Pecora was degraded to a “dirty little wop,” “a sharp little criminal lawyer,” and “a 2nd-rate criminal lawyer.”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329939) It never occurred to Jack that Pecora might uncover anything amiss; he, too, thought the hearings were cooked up to pander to public voyeurism. He told the marquess of Linlithgow: “The risk of finding anything crooked in our affairs, honestly looked at, is nil; but it is taking a large part of the time of all the partners, and one whole firm of lawyers, to go through all the bank history and get ready to answer \[the committee’s questions\].”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2330244) Lamont told his friend Lady Astor that he deplored the “Spanish Inquisition” in Washington and the conduct of the “young native Sicilian counsel, Ferdinand Pecora.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2330468) With such an inflated sense of virtue, the Morgan partners marched blindly into the hearings.
杰克·摩根对佩科拉尤为恼火。他深信摩根家族的清白，并将任何调查都视为别有用心的人身攻击。他嘴里蹦出一连串充满种族色彩的辱骂——毕竟六十六岁的他可没打算学会宽容。佩科拉被他贬损为“肮脏的小意大利佬”、“狡猾的底层刑法律师”以及“二流刑法律师”。 [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2329939) 杰克从未想过佩科拉能查出什么纰漏；他也认为这场听证会不过是用来迎合公众窥私欲的炒作。他对林利斯戈侯爵说：“说实话，想从我们的业务里找出什么不轨之事，可能性趋近于零。但为了梳理银行全部历史、准备回答（委员会的问题），我们已经耗尽了所有合伙人和一整家律所的大量时间。” [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2330244) 拉蒙特则向好友阿斯特夫人抱怨，他痛恨华盛顿上演的“宗教审判式闹剧”，更鄙夷“那位西西里裔的本土小律师费迪南德·佩科拉的行径”。”② 摩根合伙人带着如此膨胀的道德优越感，盲目地走进了听证会。

As the partners prepared for their May appearance, the hearings took on a new urgency. Sponsored by Senator Carter Glass of Virginia and Representative Henry Steagall of Alabama, a bill was working its way through Congress to separate commercial and investment banking. This would force large commercial banks to give up their securities affiliates; deposit-and-loan business would be severed from securities work. The political movement to punish Wall Street was becoming a juggernaut. Nobody had expected securities reform to dominate the early New Deal. But Pecora’s sensational findings pressured the Roosevelt administration to take action against Wall Street.
当合伙人准备 5 月的听证会时，这场听证会变得更加紧迫。由弗吉尼亚州参议员卡特·格拉斯和阿拉巴马州众议员亨利·斯蒂格尔提出的一项法案正在国会中推进，旨在将商业银行与投资银行业务分开。这将迫使大型商业银行放弃其证券附属机构；存贷款业务将与证券业务彻底分离。这场惩罚华尔街的政治运动已然势不可挡。没人料到证券改革会成为新政初期的重点。但佩科拉的惊人发现迫使罗斯福政府不得不对华尔街采取行动。

Amid an upsurge of populist feeling in 1933, demagogues of the left and right found the House of Morgan a convenient idol to smash. Responding to the Pecora inquiry, Louisiana’s Huey P. Long gave a speech entitled “Our Constant Rulers.” In it, he argued, against all evidence, that Roosevelt had stacked the Treasury Department with Morgan men. Roosevelt, claimed Long, was no less beholden to 23 Wall than Hoover: “Parker Gilbert from Morgan & Company, Leffingwell . . . what is the use of hemming and hawing? We know who is running the thing.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2330653)
1933 年，民粹主义情绪高涨，左右两派的煽动家都将 House of Morgan 视为一个方便砸碎的神像。作为对 Pecora 调查的回应，路易斯安那州的 Huey P. Long 发表了一篇题为《我们永恒的统治者》的演讲。在演讲中，他罔顾事实地声称，Roosevelt 在财政部安插的全是 Morgan 的人。Long 宣称，Roosevelt 与 Hoover 一样受制于 23 Wall：“Parker Gilbert 来自 Morgan & Company，Leffingwell……支支吾吾有什么用？我们都知道谁在掌权。” [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2330653)

Threats to the bank went far beyond redneck demagogues or professors in Roosevelt’s brain trust: they came from the banking community itself. In 1930, the Chase bank had merged with the Equitable Trust to form the world’s largest bank of its time. Winthrop W. Aldrich, a brother-in-law of John D. Rockefeller, Jr., had succeeded the disgraced Albert Wiggin as Chase president in early 1933 and wanted to refurbish the bank’s image. To this end, he got behind the push to divide commercial and investment banking. In March 1933, he took steps to spin off the Chase securities affiliate, Chase Harris Forbes. Similarly, James Perkins, who succeeded Charles Mitchell at National City, believed that its reckless stock affiliate had nearly ruined the bank, and he, too, favored a sequestration of financial functions. The bankers’ unity of the 1920s was breaking down into furious backbiting and jockeying for advantage. According to Arthur Schlesinger, Jr., “Aldrich’s action was interpreted as a Rockefeller assault on the House of Morgan; and for a time he achieved almost the dignity of a traitor to his class.” The counterattack came from William Potter of Guaranty Trust, who criticized Aldrich’s proposals as “quite the most disastrous . . . ever heard from a member of the financial community.”[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2330834) This division within the realm of banking sped the passage of the Glass-Steagall Act.
对银行的威胁远不止来自那些粗俗的煽动家或罗斯福智囊团里的教授们：威胁其实来自银行业内部。1930 年，大通银行与公平信托公司合并，成为当时全球最大的银行。小约翰·D·洛克菲勒的妹夫温思罗普·W·奥尔德里奇于 1933 年初接替声名狼藉的艾伯特·威金出任大通银行行长，并希望重塑银行形象。为此，他支持推动商业银行与投资银行的分业经营。1933 年 3 月，他着手剥离大通银行旗下的证券附属机构大通-哈里斯-福布斯公司。同样，在国民城市银行接替查尔斯·米切尔的詹姆斯·帕金斯也认为，该行冒进的股票附属机构几乎毁了银行，因此他也主张分离金融功能。20 世纪 20 年代银行家的团结已瓦解，取而代之的是激烈的相互攻讦和争权夺利。正如小阿瑟·施莱辛格所言。“奥尔德里奇的行为被视为洛克菲勒家族对摩根家族的袭击；有一阵子他几乎获得了本阶级叛徒的尊严。”反击来自保信银行的威廉·波特，他批评奥尔德里奇的提议是“有史以来从金融界人士口中听到过的最具灾难性的提议”。 [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2330834) 银行业内部的分裂加速了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的通过。

The House of Morgan was the first private bank investigated by Pecora. After three months of nonstop preparation, the Morgan entourage swept into a \$2,000-a-day suite of rooms at the Carlton Hotel attended by a small army of Davis, Polk lawyers. Jack was to be the first witness. The night before, John Davis rehearsed him with biting questions. Believing that Pierpont’s arrogance before the Pujo Committee had harmed the House of Morgan, Davis advised the men not to be coy, argumentative, or defensive. “I lined up the partners and held school every day,” he later recalled.[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331008) As star witness, Jack was awaited with feverish anticipation. That morning, crowds ringed Capitol Hill to get seats in a sweltering, overflowing Senate Caucus Room. On the way, Jack confided to his chauffeur that he was afraid he would lose his temper. Charles Robertson sniffed, “Oh, you would not lose your temper with the likes of them.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331178) Restored to his senses, Jack decided not to stoop to their level. No, he would conduct himself with honor. He entered the Capitol accompanied by several tough-looking bodyguards.
摩根财团是派科拉调查的首家私人银行。经过三个月昼夜不息的筹备，摩根团队浩浩荡荡入驻卡尔顿酒店日租两千美元的套间，戴维斯·波尔克律所的大批律师随行待命。杰克将作为首位证人出庭。前一晚，约翰·戴维斯用犀利刁钻的问题为他演练。鉴于皮尔庞特当年在普约委员会面前傲慢自负的做派已让摩根财团蒙受损失，戴维斯叮嘱众人切勿遮遮掩掩、争强好辩或一味防守。“我把合伙人们排好队，每天给他们开小灶，”他后来回忆道。 [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331008) 作为焦点证人，杰克的登场引发万众翘首。当天清晨，国会山被围得水泄不通，人们在闷热拥挤的参议院听证室里抢座。途中，杰克向司机吐露心声，担心自己会失控发火。查尔斯·罗伯逊嗤之以鼻：“哼，跟那帮人置气？您才不会呢。” [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331178) 杰克幡然醒悟，决意不与他们一般见识。 不，他会以尊严行事。他进入国会大厦时，身旁跟着几个面容凶悍的保镖。

Shortly before ten o’clock on Tuesday morning, May 23, guards cleared the way for Jack Morgan to enter the hearing room; he was flanked by Tom Lamont and John Davis. Flashbulbs exploded and spectators buzzed as the world’s most famous private banker stepped beneath the chandeliers and Corinthian pilasters. Despite his legendary name, Jack, age sixty-six, was a mystery man to most Americans, ghostly and insubstantial. He didn’t look fearsome. Over six feet two with broad shoulders and an egg-shaped head, he was a balding, white-haired old man with dark eyebrows. Within himself he might feel sheepish, but he had a kindly smile and radiated a well-tailored poise in his three-piece suit and gold watch chain. He and Pecora typified contrasting images—the imperturbable Bourbon and the assertive immigrant.
5 月 23 日星期二早上将近十点，警卫为杰克·摩根清出一条通往听证厅的路，他左右两侧是汤姆·拉蒙特和约翰·戴维斯。当这位全球最著名的私人银行家踏入水晶吊灯与科林斯式壁柱之间时，闪光灯此起彼伏，旁观者议论纷纷。尽管名震遐迩，六十六岁的杰克对大多数美国人而言仍是个神秘人物，若隐若现、难以捉摸。他看上去并不令人畏惧。身高六英尺二英寸有余，宽肩圆脸，头顶微秃，白发苍苍，长着浓黑的眉毛。内心深处或许有些腼腆，但他面带和蔼的微笑，身着剪裁得体的三件套西装，配着金表链，散发出从容不迫的气度。他与佩科拉形成了鲜明的对比——一个是镇定自若的贵族，一个是咄咄逼人的移民。

Nobody was less eager than Jack to be dragged from his semiretire-ment. At this moment of crisis, he reverted to the tradition upheld by three generations of Morgans, the Gentleman Banker’s Code, first pounded into Pierpont’s head by Junius sixty years before. Jack’s opening statement harked back to Pierpont’s statement at the Pujo hearings, that character was the basis of credit:
没人比杰克更不愿意从半退休状态中被拉出来了。在这危急关头，他回归了摩根家族三代人坚守的传统——绅士银行家准则，这是六十年前朱尼厄斯首先灌输给皮尔庞特的。杰克开场白中回溯了皮尔庞特在普约听证会上的发言：品德才是信用的基石——

> The private banker is a member of a profession which has been practiced since the middle ages. In the process of time there has grown up a code of professional ethics and customs, on the observance of which depend his reputation, his force and his usefulness to the community in which he works . . . if, in the exercise of his profession, the private banker disregards this code, which could never be expressed in any legislation, but has a force far greater than any law, he will sacrifice his credit. This credit is his most valuable possession; it is the result of years of faith and honorable dealing and, while it may be quickly lost, once lost cannot be restored for a long time, if ever.
> 私人银行家这一职业自中世纪起便已存在。随着时间的推移，逐渐形成了一套职业道德准则和行业惯例，他的声誉、影响力以及对他所服务的社会的价值，都取决于对这些准则的遵守……如果在执业过程中，私人银行家无视这套准则——它虽从未被任何法律条文所明确，却拥有远超任何法律的约束力——他将丧失自己的信用。信用是他最珍贵的财富，是多年诚信经营与公平交易的结晶；然而，它可能瞬间毁于一旦，一旦失去，即便能够挽回，也需要耗费极长的时间。

> If I may be permitted to speak of the firm, of which I have the honour to be the senior partner, I should state that at all times the idea of doing only first class business, and that in a first class way, has been before our minds.[53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331377)
> 容我提及本人有幸担任高级合伙人的敝公司，可以说，我们始终秉持"只做一流业务，并以一流方式经营"这一理念。 [53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331377)

This was as clear a statement of principles as Jack could muster: this was his birthright, what it meant to be a Morgan banker. Yet his attempt at candor sounded strangely anachronistic to American ears. Jack was an old-school banker, as out of place as an alchemist in the atomic age. Historian William E. Leuchtenburg has said, “On the witness stand, Morgan appeared to have been resurrected from some Dickensian countinghouse.”[54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331641) This was literally true, for Jack was trained in late Victorian London and never abandoned its banking folkways.
这是杰克所能表达的最明确的原则声明：这是他与生俱来的权利，也是身为摩根银行家的意义所在。然而，他试图坦诚的举动在美国人听来却显得不合时宜。杰克是个老派银行家，就像原子时代里的炼金术士一样格格不入。历史学家威廉·E·刘易斯滕伯格曾说：“在证人席上，摩根仿佛是从狄更斯笔下的账房里复活过来的人。” [54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331641) 这话毫不夸张，因为杰克是在维多利亚晚期的伦敦受训，从未摒弃过那里的银行业传统。

His black hair swept up in a pompadour, his chin jutting, Pecora jabbed the air and posed aggressive questions; sometimes he even pointed his cigar at Jack. Abiding by Davis’s advice, Jack didn’t joust with the attorney. He smiled nervously, called Pecora “Sir,” and hardly seemed a world-devouring tycoon. He breathed no fire, hurled no thunderbolts. The public saw the figure well-known to friends and associates but seldom, if ever, seen in public—the bluff, genial, but shy and vulnerable banker. “I should like it if the stuttering part were cut out of my answer to that question,” Jack asked at one point. “I am not used to this form of examination, Mr. Pecora, and I do not get my words quite straight always.”[55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2331831)
他的黑发向后梳成蓬巴杜发型，下巴微微扬起，佩科拉在空中指指点点，提出咄咄逼人的问题；有时甚至用雪茄指着杰克。杰克谨记戴维斯的建议，没有与这位律师正面交锋。他紧张地笑着，称佩科拉为“先生”，几乎看不出他是个吞并世界的大亨。他没有怒发冲冠，也没有雷霆震怒。公众看到的，是朋友和同事熟悉、但在公开场合几乎从未见过的形象——这位银行家豪爽、和蔼，却又羞涩、脆弱。“我希望那个问题的回答里，能把结巴的部分去掉，”杰克曾这样请求，“我不习惯这种质询方式，佩科拉先生，有时候我说话不太利索。”

Like Samuel Untermyer at the Pujo hearings, Ferdinand Pecora focused on the House of Morgan’s standing as the banker’s bank. Jack saw nothing wrong with Morgan partners sitting on the boards of Guaranty Trust and Bankers Trust. Nor was he ashamed of the Morgan bank making loans to sixty officers and directors of other banks, including Charles Mitchell of National City, Seward Prosser of Bankers Trust, and William Potter of Guaranty Trust. Denying that this afforded any special advantages, Jack said, “They are friends of ours, and we know that they are good, sound, straight fellows.”[56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2332001) Far from regretting the Morgan role as the Wall Street clubhouse, Jack boasted that a private bank offered neutral territory, where incorporated banks might “meet and discuss the general problems without rivalry or competition.”[57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2332220)
正如塞缪尔·昂特迈耶在普约听证会上的表现，费迪南德·佩科拉将矛头对准了摩根银行作为"银行的银行"的地位。杰克不认为摩根合伙人兼任担保信托银行和银行家信托公司的董事有任何不妥。他也毫不避讳摩根银行向六十位其他银行的高管及董事提供贷款，其中包括国民城市银行的查尔斯·米切尔、银行家信托公司的苏厄德·普罗瑟以及担保信托银行的威廉·波特。杰克否认这带来了任何特殊优势，说道："他们是我们的朋友，我们知道他们是靠谱、稳健、正直的人。" [56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2332001) 杰克非但对摩根银行充当华尔街俱乐部会所的角色毫无悔意，反而吹嘘这家私人银行提供了中立舞台，让商业银行能够"聚在一起讨论共同问题，而非彼此对抗或竞争。" [57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2332220)

Jack’s testimony exposed Depression America to a form of wholesale, private banking that it had never known existed. When Pecora asked for the firm’s partnership agreement, John Davis protested such public revelation. So in executive session, Pecora unrolled the agreement: a magnificently hand-lettered scroll that even some Morgan partners had never seen. It disclosed Jack’s absolute powers to arbitrate disputes, allocate undivided profits, and even dissolve the bank. Jack was proud of the bank’s secrecy. “Our relations with our clients are much more confidential, in my opinion, than the relations with an incorporated bank can be,” he said.[58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2332399)
杰克的证词揭露了大萧条时期的美国存在一种从未被知晓的批发性私人银行业务。当皮科拉要求查看该公司的合伙协议时，约翰·戴维斯对这种公开曝光表示反对。于是，在非公开会议上，皮科拉展开了这份协议：一份精美的手写卷轴，甚至连一些摩根合伙人也从未见过。其中揭示了杰克在仲裁纠纷、分配未分配利润乃至解散银行方面拥有绝对权力。杰克对银行的保密性引以为傲。“在我看来，我们与客户的关系比任何注册银行都更加私密，”他说道。

In a culture that worshiped the hard sell, the reticent J. P. Morgan and Company was a puzzling curiosity. As a private New York bank, it couldn’t advertise, solicit deposits from the general public, or pay interest on deposits of less than \$7,500. Apparently, getting a Morgan account was like being accepted at an exclusive country club. Even Senator Duncan U. Fletcher of Florida, the Chairman of the Senate Banking and Currency Committee, was perplexed by this:

Then who banked at this place? Pecora outlined a list of companies that kept million-dollar balances at Morgans—AT&T, Celanese, Du Pont, General Electric, General Mills, Ingersoll-Rand, ITT, Johns-Man-ville, Kennecott Copper, Montgomery Ward, New York Central, Northern Pacific, Standard Brands, Standard Oil of New Jersey, Texas Gulf Sulphur, and U.S. Steel. Their executives often chose J. P. Morgan for their personal bank accounts as well. Pecora had charts showing that Morgan partners held 126 directorships in 89 corporations with \$20 billion in assets. He later called this “incomparably the greatest reach of power in private hands in our entire history.”[60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2332745) He seemed incredulous when Jack said partners went on boards only at the “earnest request” of a company.
那么，到底哪些人在这个银行开户呢？佩科拉列举了一串公司，它们在摩根银行存有百万美元的余额——美国电话电报公司（AT&T）、塞拉尼斯、杜邦、通用电气、通用磨坊、英格索兰、国际电话电报公司（ITT）、约翰斯·曼维尔、肯尼科特铜业、蒙哥马利沃德、纽约中央铁路、北太平洋铁路、标准品牌、新泽西标准石油、德克萨斯海湾硫磺公司，还有美国钢铁公司。这些公司的高管往往也选择 J.P.摩根作为他们的个人开户银行。佩科拉用图表展示，摩根合伙人共在 89 家公司担任了 126 个董事职位，这些公司的资产总计高达 200 亿美元。他后来称这是“我国历史上私人手中掌握的最大的权力范围”。 [60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2332745) 当杰克说合伙人只是在公司的“恳切请求”下才进入董事会时，他似乎难以置信。

If Jack entered the hearings with serene confidence, he was soon engulfed in an issue that would shadow him throughout the New Deal—income taxes. Pecora revealed that Jack had paid no income tax for 1930, 1931, and 1932, and all twenty Morgan partners paid nothing for 1931 and 1932. (Jack had paid taxes in England for these years.) Pecora also showed that by making Parker Gilbert a partner on January 2, 1931—instead of December 31, 1930, as would have been customary—the firm claimed a \$31-million capital loss for 1931. Bumbling and flustered, Jack couldn’t recall the details of his tax picture; such vagueness was plausible to his associates, suspicious to the public. Although Jack and most of the partners hadn’t violated the law and had simply taken sizable write-offs from stock losses, their failure to pay taxes was politically explosive in the Depression. Tax shelters had not yet become a favorite American pastime, and the government desperately needed money. The next day, headlines trumpeted the Morgan partners’ “tax evasion.”

When his testimony was over, Jack dozed through the appearances of the other Morgan partners. At one point, he awoke abruptly to ask what year it was. Sometimes Jack could laugh about the experience. One day on the golf fairway, he was lining up a shot when his caddy, Frank Colby, said he should think of the ball as Pecora’s head. Afterward, he got a visit from William Jay Schieffelin, son-in-law of Dr. Markoe, who was trying to win support for a scheme enabling poor people to buy life insurance from savings banks. I’ve been so outraged that it leaves me cold when I hear of somebody else being outraged.”[71](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2334551) This sense of his own victimization would close Jack’s mind toward the Roosevelt programs. More and more, he would feel a revulsion from America, a sense of being abandoned by his own country, and profound anger at the tarnishing of his bank’s reputation.
有时候，杰克也能拿这段经历自嘲一番。一次在高尔夫球场上，他正准备击球，球童弗兰克·科尔比建议他把球想象成佩科拉的脑袋。杰克一杆挥出，球飞得漂亮极了，两人都心照不宣地笑了起来。 [70](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2334392) 但大多数时候，杰克对听证会耿耿于怀，这件事最终让他与新政渐行渐远。后来，马科博士的女婿威廉·杰伊·希弗林登门拜访，试图为一项让穷人能从储蓄银行购买人寿保险的计划争取支持。杰克不仅拒绝帮忙，还说了一句意味深长的话：“我真希望有你那种能力——对不公之事义愤填膺的能力。我自己受过的冤屈太多，以至于听到别人受欺负，心里反而冷冰冰的。” [71](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2334551) 这种自认受害的心态，让杰克对罗斯福的各项计划彻底关上了心门。 他越来越对美国感到厌恶，觉得自己被这个国家彻底抛弃了。对他银行声誉受损的深切愤怒。

The story of Lya Graf ends sadly. As sensitive as Jack, she was traumatized by the endless jokes about the episode—so much so that in 1935 she decided to return to her native Germany, even though she was half Jewish: her real name was Lia Schwarz. Two years later, she was arrested by the Nazis as a “useless person” and sent to Auschwitz, where she died in the gas chambers. All this was learned only after the war when Nate Eagle, the Ringling Brothers manager who cared for the midgets, traced her history. Jack Morgan never knew what became of her, nor that her extreme distress over their brief encounter set in motion events that led, ultimately, to her death.
莉亚·格拉夫的故事以悲剧告终。她和杰克一样敏感，那次事件带来的无尽嘲讽让她深受创伤——以至于 1935 年，即便她有一半犹太血统，她还是决定返回祖国德国（她的真名是莉亚·施瓦茨）。两年后，她被纳粹以“无用之人”的罪名逮捕，送往奥斯维辛集中营，最终死于毒气室。这一切直到战后才为人所知，当时那位照顾矮人的林林兄弟马戏团经理内特·伊格尔追溯了她的生平。杰克·摩根从未知道她后来的遭遇，也不知道那次短暂会面给她带来的极度痛苦，竟引发了一连串最终导致她死亡的事件。

AS Pecora adroitly exposed their subterfuges, the other partners fared no better than Jack. When George Whitney read a statement favoring disclosure of commissions in security offerings, Pecora sarcastically noted that the legislation had just been passed. Returning in his questioning to 1929, Pecora lobbed another grenade over to the Morgan side. That year, the bank had joined the craze for creating new holding companies and had sponsored Alleghany Corporation as a vehicle for the railroad and real estate interests of the Van Sweringen brothers; United Corporation, an electric-utility holding company; and Standard Brands, a merger of four food and consumer-products companies.
随着佩科拉巧妙地揭露他们的诡计，其他合伙人的处境并不比杰克好。当乔治·惠特尼宣读一份支持在证券发行中披露佣金的声明时，佩科拉讽刺地指出，这项法案刚刚通过。在追问中，佩科拉又将话题拉回 1929 年，向摩根阵营抛出了另一颗"手榴弹"。那一年，这家银行也加入了创建新控股公司的热潮，并赞助了阿勒格尼公司——作为范斯韦林根兄弟铁路和房地产利益的载体；联合公司——一家电力事业控股公司；以及标准品牌公司——由四家食品和消费品公司合并而成。

Instead of placing shares only with dealers, Morgans borrowed a British precedent and placed shares with scores of friendly individuals. These shares came from a block of stock the bank kept as its underwriting fee. On Wall Street in the 1920s, it wasn’t uncommon to have company officers or well-heeled individuals serve as underwriters. By allocating shares of the three holding companies to rich investors, Morgans claimed, it had tried to strike a compromise with its usual policy of not enticing individuals into risky stock transactions. As George Whitney said, they chose only those customers whom they knew were “competent financially and mentally to undertake the risks, whatever the risk may be.”[72](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2334688)
摩根没有只把股票分给承销商，而是借鉴英国先例，将股票分配给了数十位关系友好的个人。这些股票来自银行作为承销费用保留的一个股份池。在 20 世纪 20 年代的华尔街，由公司高管或富有人士担任承销商并不罕见。摩根声称，通过将三家控股公司的股票分配给富有的投资者，它试图在惯常的不诱导个人参与高风险股票交易的政策与这种做法之间达成妥协。正如乔治·惠特尼所言，他们只选择了那些他们知道“无论风险多大，都有能力和心理承受力去承担”的客户。

Such self-serving descriptions didn’t capture the reality of 1929. In the souped-up bull market, shares offered to Morgan intimates before the public issue were already selling on a when-issued basis at a steep premium. (When-issued sales occur before a public offering and anticipate the price that will prevail when trading begins.) Between the Morgan price for lucky friends and this provisional market price lay a wide gap, an instant windfall. For instance, the bank was giving Alleghany shares to friends at \$20 apiece; these could soon be cashed in for \$35; United shares at \$75 would soon go for \$99; and Standard Brands shares bought at \$32 could be redeemed just sixty days later at \$41. In the soaring 1929 market, there was no great risk in carrying these shares before public issue, and the potential rewards were colossal. The shares seemed almost to be outright gifts—the sort of royal bequest only the House of Morgan could bestow. In Alleghany stock alone, the bank had over \$8 million in profits to distribute. The setup was dubbed the gravy train.
这种自我标榜的描述并不符合 1929 年的现实。在那个狂热的牛市中，摩根内幕人士在公开发行前获得的股票，已经在“拟发行”交易中以高溢价出售。（“拟发行”交易发生在公开募股之前，是对交易开始后价格的预判。）摩根给幸运朋友的价格与这种临时市场价格之间存在巨大差距，瞬间就能带来暴利。例如，银行以每股 20 美元向朋友提供 Alleghany 股票，这些股票很快就能以 35 美元兑现；United 股票以 75 美元买入，不久后就能以 99 美元卖出；Standard Brands 股票以 32 美元购入，仅 60 天后就能以 41 美元赎回。在 1929 年飙升的市场中，持有这些公开发行前的股票风险极低，而潜在回报却极其丰厚。这些股票几乎像是白送的礼物——只有摩根家族才能赐予的这种皇室馈赠。 仅 Alleghany 这一支股票，该银行就获得了超过 800 万美元的利润可供分配。这一安排被称为“肥差大单”。

The revelation of the House of Morgan’s so-called preferred list of friends confirmed Main Street’s cynicism of Wall Street as a place of easy riches and loose morals. For Morgan critics, this was at last the smoking gun, the tangible proof of corruption. The stunning list of recipients encompassed the American business and political elite. It started at the very top. After leaving the White House, Calvin Coolidge had been advised on finances by Morgan partner Tom Cochran and received three thousand shares of Standard Brands; somewhat ashamed at this revelation, he told friends that it saddened him to appear on the preferred list while they were on the welfare list.[73](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2334865) Other Republican beneficiaries included Charles O. Hilles, chairman of the Republican National Committee, and Charles Francis Adams, Hoover’s secretary of the navy and the father-in-law of Jack’s younger son, Harry.
摩根银行所谓“优先朋友名单”的曝光，印证了普通民众对华尔街“轻松致富、道德沦丧”的讽刺看法。对摩根批评者而言，这无疑是铁证如山，腐败的实锤触手可及。这份令人瞠目的名单囊括了美国商界和政界精英，且从最高层开始。离任白宫后，卡尔文·柯立芝曾在摩根合伙人汤姆·科克伦的指导下理财，并获得三千股标准品牌股票；对此曝光他颇感羞愧，向友人坦言，自己出现在优先名单上而朋友们却在救济名单上，实在令人沮丧。其他共和党受益者还包括共和党全国委员会主席查尔斯·O·希尔斯，以及胡佛政府的海军部长、杰克幼子哈里之岳父查尔斯·弗朗西斯·亚当斯。

Hedging its bets, Morgans also cultivated Democrats. This side of the ledger was even more embarrassing to its recipients, among them William G. McAdoo, the former Treasury secretary, a mentor of Russell Leffingwell. What made McAdoo’s plight especially mortifying was that as a senator, he now sat on the Pecora committee. Also on the list was John J. Raskob, chairman of the Democratic National Committee. The list reached straight into the New Deal itself. When he was president of American Car and Foundry Company in 1929, William H. Woodin, now FDR’s Treasury secretary, had taken up Morgans on an offer.
为求稳妥，摩根家族也拉拢了民主党人。这份名单上的记录对收受者来说更为难堪，其中包括前财政部长、拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔的导师威廉·G·麦卡杜。麦卡杜的处境尤为尴尬，因为此时他已是参议员，正就职于皮科拉委员会。名单上还有民主党全国委员会主席约翰·J·拉斯科布。这条线索甚至直接延伸到了新政内部。1929 年时任美国汽车与铸造公司总裁的威廉·H·伍丁——也就是如今罗斯福政府的财政部长——也曾接受过摩根家族的提议。

Beyond politics, the preferred list exposed an astonishing range of Morgan corporate contacts. There were business chieftains—Owen Young of General Electric, Myron Taylor of U.S. Steel, Walter Teagle of Standard Oil of New Jersey, Walter Gifford of AT&T, and Sosthenes Behn of ITT; financiers—Albert Wiggin of Chase, George F. Baker of First National, Richard Whitney of the New York Stock Exchange, and Bernard Baruch; a war hero—General John Pershing; a national hero—Charles Lindbergh; distinguished lawyers—John W. Davis and Albert G. Milbank; and distinguished families—Guggenheims, Drexels, Biddies, and Berwinds.
超越政治层面，这份首选名单暴露了摩根公司惊人广泛的联系人网络。其中有商业巨头——通用电气的欧文·杨、美国钢铁的迈伦·泰勒、新泽西标准石油的沃尔特·蒂格尔、AT&T 的沃尔特·吉福德、ITT 的索斯滕斯·贝恩；金融家——大通银行的阿尔伯特·威金、第一国民银行的乔治·F·贝克、纽约证券交易所的理查德·惠特尼、伯纳德·巴鲁克；战争英雄——约翰·潘兴将军；民族英雄——查尔斯·林德伯格；杰出律师——约翰·W·戴维斯和阿尔伯特·G·米尔班克；以及显赫家族——古根海姆家族、德雷克塞尔家族、比德尔家族和伯温德家族。

The House of Morgan was shaken by the disclosures and the imputation of dishonesty. When hiring partners, both Pierpont and Jack had always made the same statement; they would say, “I want my business done up there”—holding their hands in the air—“and not down here”—pointing to the ground.[74](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335029) Jack would tell people that at the first sign of unethical conduct, they should come straight to him. Now the bank had to face charges that it had unscrupulously curried favor with a broad spectrum of business and political leaders. How to defend the indefensible?
摩根银行因这些揭露和背信弃义的指控而震动。皮尔庞特和杰克在招募合伙人时，总是不约而同地表明同一个立场：他们会高高举起手说，“我要我的生意做到这个高度”——然后指向地面——“而不是低到这种程度。”杰克常对人讲，一旦出现任何不道德行为的苗头，他们应该直接来找他。如今，这家银行却不得不面对这样的指控：它曾毫无原则地讨好商界和政界的各路领袖。要如何为这些无法辩解的行为辩护？

The task fell to George Whitney, whose Brahmin good looks and lacquered black hair made him the prototypically handsome Morgan partner of his generation. He had enjoyed Alleghany bounty himself, netting \$229,000 on the sale of eight thousand shares. A tough, unyielding witness, Whitney stuck to the line that the bank was shielding small investors from risk. “They took a risk of profit,” Whitney said of the preferred customers; “they took a risk of loss.” Pecora later rejoined: “Many there were who would gladly have helped them share that appalling peril!”[75](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335194) At moments, even the self-assured Whitney seemed confused, stammering at one point, “I don’t know, Senator Couzens. It is hard to say why we did things. It is even harder to say why we didn’t.”[76](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335362)
这项任务落在了乔治·惠特尼身上。他那一副贵族式的英俊相貌和油亮的黑发，使他成为同代人中最具典型摩根合伙人风范的银行家。他自己也从 Alleghany 的丰厚收益中分得一杯羹——出售八千股股票净赚了 22.9 万美元。作为一位强硬、不肯让步的证人，惠特尼始终坚持说，银行是在保护小投资者免受风险。“他们承担了盈利的风险，”惠特尼谈到那些优先客户时说，“也承担了亏损的风险。”佩科拉随即回击道：“当时有多少人愿意帮他们分担那令人胆寒的风险啊！” [75](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335194) 有时，连这位自信满满的惠特尼似乎也乱了阵脚，结结巴巴地说：“我不清楚，库曾斯参议员。很难说我们为什么要做那些事，更难说我们为什么没做。” [76](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335362)

Even as Morgan partners denied that shares were distributed to influence people, Pecora released subpoenaed bank records that confirmed a less than angelic intent. In 1929, Morgan partner William Ewing had written to William Woodin coyly acknowledging the bonanza being offered:
即便是摩根合伙人否认通过分发股票来影响他人，佩科拉仍公布了传唤获取的银行记录，证实其意图远非那么高尚。1929 年，摩根合伙人威廉·尤因致信威廉·伍丁，暧昧地承认了这份送上门的肥礼：

> I believe that the stock is selling in the market around \$35 to \$37 a share, which means very little, except that people wish to speculate. We are reserving for you 1,000 shares at \$20 a share, if you would like to have it. There are no strings tied to this stock, and you can sell it whenever you wish. . . . We just want you to know that we were thinking of you in this connection and thought you might like to have a little of the stock at the same price we are paying for it.[77](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335543)
> "我相信目前这只股票在市场上的售价约为每股 35 至 37 美元，这其实没什么意义，无非是人们想投机罢了。我们为您预留了 1000 股，每股 20 美元，如果您想要的话。这只股票没有任何附加条件，您随时可以出售……我们只是想告诉您，在这件事上我们惦记着您，想着您或许愿意以和我们相同的价格，买上一点这只股票。" [77](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335543)

Other documents suggested that the operation was conducted secretly. Partner Arthur Anderson told lawyer Albert Milbank, “It probably is unnecessary for me to add that I hope you will not make any mention of this operation.”[78](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335763) Some correspondence resorted to sly hints. Golfing in Palm Beach, John J. Raskob, a former Du Pont treasurer and General Motors director, thanked George Whitney for his shares with the sincere hope that “the future holds opportunities for me to reciprocate.”[79](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335898)
其他文件显示，这项行动是秘密进行的。合伙人阿瑟·安德森对律师阿尔伯特·米尔班克说：“我想不必多言，希望你不要对外提及此事。” [78](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335763) 部分信函中采用了隐晦的暗示。曾在杜邦公司担任财务主管、通用汽车公司董事的约翰·J·拉斯科布在棕榈滩打高尔夫时，向乔治·惠特尼表示感谢其赠送的股份，并真诚希望“未来能有机会回报”。 [79](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2335898)

Lamont was indignant at charges of influence peddling. Yet his own files contain a February 1929 memo that may be the most damaging of all. In a postscript written to Arthur Anderson, it shows how the distributed shares were discussed internally: “It occurred to me this morning to make inquiry from you whether in our distribution of Al-leghany common we had allotted anything to Frederick Strauss. He was so exceedingly helpful and at considerable sacrifice to himself in going over to Washington to testify in the matter of the stock issues, that I am not at all sure that we ought not to try to do something for him even at a date as late as this.”[80](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336033) Clearly, the preferred list had less to do with protecting small investors than with rewarding important friends.
拉蒙特对权钱交易的指控愤愤不平。然而，他本人的文件中却包含一份 1929 年 2 月的备忘录，这或许是最具杀伤力的证据。在写给亚瑟·安德森的附言中，它显示了内部是如何讨论股票分配的：“今早我突然想到问问你，在我们分配 Allegheny 普通股时，是否给弗雷德里克·施特劳斯留了一些。他在前往华盛顿就股票发行问题作证时，提供了极大的帮助，而且自身付出了相当多的牺牲。我完全不确定我们是否不应该试着为他做点什么，即使到了现在这么晚的时候。” [80](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336033) 显然，这份优先名单与其说是为了保护小投资者，不如说是为了回报重要的朋友。

The preferred list came at a particularly inopportune time both for the Roosevelt administration and the Morgan bank. High finance was on trial, and congressional hoppers bulged with securities-reform bills. The cabinet spent an hour deciding whether Woodin should remain in his post as Treasury secretary. Vice-President John Nance Garner favored his resignation to establish that the administration was free of Morgan influence, but Roosevelt feared abandoning a friend under fire. “The President took the position that many of us did things prior to 1929 that we wouldn’t think of doing now; that our code of ethics had radically changed,” Interior Secretary Harold Ickes wrote in his diary.[81](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336280) Woodin remained in the cabinet until November 1933, when, gravely ill, he was replaced by Morgenthau. The cabinet was also disturbed by the appearance of Norman Davis, its roving ambassador, on the preferred list. Some feared that if the Roosevelt administration moved closer to the League of Nations or the British government, the public would impute the action to Morgan influence over Davis. Despite this, Davis represented Washington at several high-level European conferences in the 1930s.
这份优先名单对罗斯福政府和摩根银行来说，出现得特别不合时宜。高层金融正备受质疑，国会里证券改革法案堆积如山。内阁花了一个小时讨论伍丁是否该继续担任财政部长。副总统约翰·南斯·加纳主张他辞职，以表明政府与摩根没有牵连，但罗斯福担心在朋友遭受攻击时弃他不顾。内政部长哈罗德·伊克斯在日记中写道："总统认为，我们许多人在 1929 年之前做过的事，现在想都不会去想；我们的道德准则已经发生了根本性的转变。" [81](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336280) 伍丁留在内阁直到 1933 年 11 月，当时他病重，由摩根索接替。诺曼·戴维斯这位巡回大使出现在优先名单上，也让内阁感到不安。 一些人担心，如果罗斯福政府靠拢国际联盟或英国政府，公众会认为这是摩根对戴维斯施加影响的结果。尽管如此，戴维斯在 20 世纪 30 年代仍代表华盛顿出席了多次欧洲高层会议。

The public reacted to the preferred list scandal with extreme disillusionment: the brightest angel on Wall Street had fallen. The bank had avoided the flagrant abuses of other banks—even Pecora called Morgans a “conservative” firm—but the preferred list cast it in the mud with other banks. A stunned Walter Lippmann told Morgan friends that no group of men should have such private power without public accountability. It was a bitter pill for Lippmann, who had often dined at Lamont’s table. His biographer, Ronald Steel, suggests that he and other journalists were lulled to sleep by Lamont, whose “charm and familiarity with the trade enabled him to persuade many journalists to look upon the activities of the Morgan firm no more critically than he did himself.”[82](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336458)
公众对优先股名单丑闻的反应是极度的幻灭：华尔街最耀眼的天使堕落了。这家银行避免了其他银行那种公然的滥用——就连佩科拉也称摩根公司是一家“保守”的企业——但优先股名单却让它与其他银行一同陷入泥潭。震惊的沃尔特·李普曼告诉摩根的朋友们，任何一群人都不应该拥有如此私人的权力而无需承担公共责任。这对李普曼来说是一剂苦药，他经常在拉蒙特的餐桌上用餐。他的传记作者罗纳德·斯蒂尔指出，李普曼和其他记者被拉蒙特哄得昏昏欲睡，拉蒙特“凭借其魅力和对行业的熟悉，成功说服了许多记者以不比他更批判的眼光来看待摩根公司的活动。” [82](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336458)

Lippmann wasn’t the only shocked journalist. As if some mighty public trust had been betrayed, the *New York Times* wrote an elegiac editorial: “Here was a firm of bankers, perhaps the most famous and powerful in the whole world, which was certainly under no necessity of practicing the small arts of petty traders. Yet it failed under a test of its pride and prestige. . . . They have given their warmest friends cause for feeling that somehow the whole community, along with numbers of men whom all had delighted to honor, has been involved in a sort of public misfortune.”[83](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336644)
李普曼并非唯一感到震惊的记者。《纽约时报》仿佛某种崇高的公众信托被背叛一般，发表了一篇哀婉的社论：“这家银行——或许是全世界最负盛名、最具权势的——本无需施展小商贩的伎俩。然而，在尊严与声望的考验面前，它却败下阵来……它让最亲密的朋友感到，整个社会，连同那些曾令万众敬仰的人物，都一同陷入了一场公共的不幸。” [83](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336644)

Reading this, Lamont became distraught. Of the Morgan partners, he had the most personal need for admiration. He wrote his friend Adolph Ochs, publisher of the *Times*, trying to extenuate the scandal. He said Morgans hadn’t expected people on the list to serve in public office again. He cited the risks of owning common stock and made it sound as if the list were made up only of family and friends. The explanations sounded strained: “We naturally turned in part to individuals who had ample means and who understand the nature of common stock—men who are prepared to take a chance with their money.”[84](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336792) All his arts couldn’t hide what had become a certifiable scandal, setting the stage for the bill that would dismember the House of Morgan.
读到此处，拉蒙特心烦意乱。在摩根合伙人中，他最渴望得到他人的敬重。他致信好友、时报出版商阿道夫·奥克斯，竭力为这桩丑闻开脱。他说摩根公司没料到名单上的人会再度担任公职。他列举了持有普通股的风险，并暗示这份名单只包含家人和朋友。这些解释听起来牵强附会：“我们自然会找那些财力雄厚、懂得普通股本质的人——那些愿意拿钱冒险的人。” [84](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336792) 他使尽浑身解数，也无法掩盖这桩已然坐实的丑闻，这为日后肢解摩根财团的那项法案埋下了伏笔。

THE Glass-Steagall Act was sponsored by a Virginia senator who felt more warmly toward 23 Wall than any of his Senate Banking Committee colleagues. Small and peppery, Carter Glass was a former Lynchburg newspaper editor with little formal education. As a congressman, he had helped to write the Federal Reserve Act and had espoused strong banker control. As Wilson’s Treasury secretary, he had been Russell Leffing-well’s boss. In early 1933, he was a mass of contradictions. After supporting Roosevelt’s election, he quickly emerged as an articulate critic of FDR. He rebuffed the president’s offer to become Treasury secretary and attacked New Deal activism from a Jeffersonian standpoint; he was the sole Democratic senator to oppose the gold devaluation. Glass sponsored his famous bill with no personal animus toward Wall Street. In fact, he and Leffingwell often exchanged nostalgic, syrupy notes about their years in the Treasury Department. Although Leffingwell described their friendship as one of his most cherished, he was frustrated in his efforts to capitalize on it that spring: when subcommittee members working on the bank-reform bill swore not to talk to outsiders about it, Glass had to abide by the decision.
《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》由一位弗吉尼亚州参议员发起，他对华尔街 23 号的热情远超参议院银行委员会的任何同僚。这位身材矮小、脾气火爆的卡特·格拉斯曾是一名林奇堡报纸编辑，几乎没有受过正规教育。担任众议员期间，他参与起草了《联邦储备法案》，并力主强化银行家控制权。作为威尔逊政府的财政部长，他曾是拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔的上级。1933 年初，他成了一个矛盾的集合体：在支持罗斯福当选后，迅速成为对 FDR 直言不讳的批评者；他拒绝了总统让他出任财政部长的提议，从杰斐逊主义立场抨击新政行动主义；他是唯一反对美元贬值的民主党参议员。格拉斯发起这项著名法案并非出于对华尔街的个人敌意——事实上，他与莱芬韦尔经常互寄充满怀旧与温情的手札，追忆他们在财政部共事的岁月。 尽管莱芬韦尔称他们之间的友谊是他最为珍视的情谊之一，但那年春天，他在试图借此谋利时却屡屡受挫——当时，负责银行改革法案的小组委员会成员宣誓不对外泄露任何信息，格拉斯不得不服从这一决定。

Glass-Steagall evolved in a way that owed much to fate. Huey Long and other congressional populists wanted federal deposit insurance in the bill, as well as restrictions on interstate branching. Both features were anathema to Roosevelt, who favored a national banking system that would put small-town Republican bankers out of business, not prop them up. Like Hoover, he feared that deposit insurance would pull strong banks down with the weak and thought it “puts a premium on sloppy banking and penalizes good banking.”[85](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336973)
《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的演变很大程度上得益于命运的安排。休伊·朗和其他国会中的平民主义者主张在法案中加入联邦存款保险制度，并限制银行的跨州经营。这两项条款都令罗斯福深恶痛绝，他更倾向建立一个全国性银行体系，借此淘汰小镇上的共和党银行家，而非扶持他们。和胡佛一样，他担心存款保险会让稳健的银行被不良银行拖累，认为这“是在奖励粗放经营，惩罚审慎管理”。 [85](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2336973)

Roosevelt kept the press guessing whether he would support Glass-Steagall. The Pecora hearings certainly contributed to public support of the bill. But what sealed its fate was the flood of mail to Congress favoring deposit insurance. The inclusion of deposit insurance was also important because nobody wanted to insure the securities affiliates of banks; if they had federal insurance, they would be obliged to stick to conservative loan-and-deposit banking. Finally, the bill set ceilings on savings interest rates. The Glass-Steagall Act was signed on June 16, 1933, by a president who didn’t even think the public was particularly eager for banking reform. From now on, banks would either take deposits and make loans or merchandise securities—but not both.
罗斯福一直让媒体猜测他是否会支持《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》。佩科拉听证会无疑推动了公众对该法案的支持，但真正决定其命运的，是涌入国会支持存款保险的雪片般信件。存款保险条款的纳入之所以至关重要，还因为没有人愿意为银行的证券附属机构提供担保——一旦这些机构拥有联邦保险，它们就不得不恪守保守的存贷业务。最终，该法案还对储蓄利率设置了上限。1933 年 6 月 16 日，一位甚至认为公众对银行改革并不热切的总统签署了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》。从此以后，银行要么吸收存款、发放贷款，要么承销证券——两者不可兼得。

A surprise last-minute insertion in the bill was a provision endorsed by Chase president Winthrop Aldrich that forced private banks to choose between deposit and securities businesses. This was the *coup de grâce* for the House of Morgan. Later Carter Glass told Leffingwell that Aldrich drafted this provision and that Roosevelt foisted it on him. Pecora’s disclosure about the Morgan partners’ avoidance of income taxes made it impossible to delete this provision, so strong was public wrath.[86](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337120) Adding to the pressure was Chase’s decision to disband its securities affiliate, whose refugees joined with renegades from the First National Bank of Boston to form First Boston, the first modern American investment bank.
法案在最后一刻意外加入了一项由大通银行总裁温斯罗普·奥尔德瑞奇支持的条款，迫使私人银行在存款业务与证券业务之间二选一。这给了摩根家族致命一击。后来卡特·格拉斯告诉莱芬韦尔，这一条款是奥尔德瑞奇草拟的，而罗斯福强加给了他。佩科拉披露摩根合伙人逃避所得税一事，使得该条款无法删除——公众的愤怒已势不可挡。 [86](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337120) 雪上加霜的是，大通银行决定解散其证券附属机构，其成员与波士顿第一国民银行的叛离者共同组建了第一家现代美国投资银行——第一波士顿。

The Glass-Steagall Act took dead aim at the House of Morgan. After all, it was the bank that had most spectacularly fused the two forms of banking. It had, ironically, proved that the two types of services could be successfully combined; Kuhn, Loeb and Lehman Brothers did less deposit business, while National City and Chase had scandal-ridden securities affiliates. The House of Morgan was the active double threat, with its million-dollar corporate balances and blue-ribbon underwriting business.
格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案的矛头直指摩根银行。毕竟，正是这家银行最惊人地融合了两种银行业务。讽刺的是，它恰恰证明了两种服务能够成功结合——库恩-洛布公司和雷曼兄弟吸收存款的业务较少，而国民城市银行和大通银行的证券附属公司却丑闻缠身。摩根银行凭借其数百万美元的企业存款和顶级承销业务，成为真正的双重威胁。

What was the theory behind the Glass-Steagall Act? Foremost, it was meant to restore a certain sobriety to American finance. In the 1920s, the banker had gone from a person of sober rectitude to a huckster who encouraged people to gamble on risky stocks and bonds. As Pecora noted, small investors identified commercial banks with security, so that National City stock salesmen “came to them clothed with all the authority and prestige of the magic name ’National City.’ ”[87](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337326) It was also argued that the union of deposit and securities banking created potential conflicts of interest. Banks could take bad loans, repackage them as bonds, and fob them off on investors, as National City had done with Latin American loans. They could even lend investors the money to buy the bonds. A final problem with the banks’ brokerage affiliates was that they forced the Federal Reserve System to stand behind both depositors and speculators. If a securities affiliate failed, the Fed might need to rescue it to protect the parent bank. In other words, the government might have to protect speculators to save depositors.
《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》背后的理论是什么？首先，它旨在让美国金融恢复某种清醒。在 20 世纪 20 年代，银行家从严谨正直的形象变成了怂恿人们投机高风险股票和债券的骗子。正如佩科拉指出的，小投资者将商业银行与安全性划等号，因此国民城市银行的股票推销员“来到他们面前时，身上带着‘国民城市’这个神奇名字所赋予的全部权威和声望。” [87](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337326) 还有一种观点认为，存款业务与证券业务的结合会引发潜在的利益冲突。银行可以把坏账打包成债券，再塞给投资者——就像国民城市银行对拉美贷款做的那样。它们甚至可以向投资者提供贷款，让他们购买这些债券。银行经纪附属机构的最后一个问题是，它们迫使美联储系统既要为储户兜底，也要为投机者兜底。 如果一家证券子公司出现问题，美联储可能需要出手救助以保护其母行。换句话说，政府或许不得不为了保护储户而保护投机者。

Ultimately, Glass-Steagall was as much an attempt to punish the banking industry as it was a measure to reform it. It was Main Street striking back at Wall Street, rounding out the 1929 disaster. The bill also had supporters among small investment banks eager to exclude large commercial banks from their domain. Many economic historians have pointed out the tenuous links between the crash and the subsequent bank failures. Bank failures were concentrated among thousands of country banks across America, while big Wall Street banks with securities affiliates withstood the Depression relatively well. Yet Glass-Steagall and other New Deal reform acts were directed at Wall Street and insulated little crossroads banks from big-city competition. This made political, but not economic, sense. The speculative fever of the 1920s had infected *all* securities houses, whether or not they were subsidiaries of deposit banks. The Jazz Age on Wall Street might have been no less effervescent if a Glass-Steagall arrangement had already been in place.
最终，格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案既是对银行业进行改革的手段，也是对其实施惩罚的措施。这是草根阶层对华尔街的反击，为 1929 年的那场灾难画上了句号。该法案还获得了一些小型投资银行的支持，它们迫切希望将大型商业银行挡在自家领地之外。许多经济史学家指出，1929 年的股市崩盘与随后的银行倒闭潮之间其实关联不大。当时倒闭的银行主要集中在全美各地的数千家乡镇银行，而那些设有证券附属部门的大型华尔街银行却相当好地挺过了大萧条。然而，格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案及其他新政改革法案却将矛头指向华尔街，反而让那些偏远的乡镇小银行免于大城市的竞争。这在政治上说得通，但在经济上却站不住脚。20 世纪 20 年代的投机狂潮早已席卷了所有证券机构，无论它们是否属于储蓄银行的附属机构。 即使当时已经有了 Glass-Steagall 安排，华尔街的爵士时代可能依然同样活力四射。

The House of Morgan had trenchant arguments to make against the bill, but nobody listened. After the Pecora hearings, even well-reasoned arguments by the financial elite resembled self-serving blather. Lamont pointed out that the flagrant scandals of the 1920s had involved retail-investment affiliates. Why, then, couldn’t wholesale banks, such as J. P. Morgan, distribute securities not to individuals but to dealers and large institutions? The Morgan partners also argued that disclosure requirements in the new Securities Act of 1933 would force banks to identify any loans outstanding to countries or companies whose bonds they issued—safeguards for bond investors lacking in the 1920s.
摩根家族曾对该法案提出尖锐反对意见，但无人理睬。经过佩科拉听证会后，金融精英们哪怕再合理的辩词也听起来像在胡搅蛮缠。拉蒙特指出，1920 年代那些臭名昭著的丑闻都涉及零售投资附属机构——既然如此，为什么像 J. P. Morgan 这样的批发银行不能向交易商和大型机构而非个人投资者分销证券？摩根合伙人还辩称，1933 年《证券法》新设的披露条款将迫使银行披露任何尚未结清的国家或企业贷款——这些债券投资者保护机制在 1920 年代根本不存在。

Lamont contended that size wasn’t to blame for America’s fragile banking system so much as fragmentation. The country had over twenty thousand banking institutions, resulting in a financial history peculiarly checkered with panics, failures, and runs. England, France, and Canada, by contrast, had a small number of large national banks, and these had passed through the Depression in far better shape. Then why not bigger, better-capitalized banks? To free banks from reliance on a single industry—whether Texas oil or Kansas farming—Lamont favored interstate banking. Russell Leffingwell contended as well that removing big commercial banks from underwriting work would produce capital-short investment banks—a prophecy not fully appreciated until decades later.
拉蒙特认为，美国银行体系脆弱的主要原因不在于规模，而在于碎片化。全国拥有超过两万家银行机构，导致金融史上充斥着恐慌、倒闭和挤兑事件。相比之下，英国、法国和加拿大只有少数几家大型全国性银行，这些银行在大萧条期间的表现要好得多。那么，为何不建立规模更大、资本更充足的银行呢？为了让银行摆脱对单一行业的依赖——无论是得克萨斯州的石油业还是堪萨斯州的农业——拉蒙特支持跨州银行业务。拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔也认为，让大型商业银行退出承销业务，会导致投资银行资本短缺——这一预言直到几十年后才被充分理解。

In 1933, however, such a perspective was bootless. The public wanted to see the giants slain and didn’t care about the dusty little banks that had faltered from bad luck or mismanagement. America’s atomized banking system might have contributed to its stormy financial history, but the political response was always to segment it further. With the Glass-Steagall Act, America experienced the catharsis awaited since Black Thursday. As Leffingwell said, “There is so much hunger and distress that it is only too natural for the people to blame the bankers and to visit their wrath on the greatest name in American banking.”[88](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337494) All the while, Morgan partners felt that they suffered for sins committed by others. George Whitney later remarked that “we never retailed while I was in our office, but that’s where the trouble started, and the New Deal was smart enough to realize that if they could cut the security business up in pieces, they would take this power away and they did.”[89](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337698)
然而在 1933 年，这种观点毫无意义。公众渴望看到巨头倒下，根本不在意那些因运气不佳或经营不善而摇摇欲坠的尘封小银行。美国碎片化的银行体系或许加剧了其动荡的金融历史，但政治回应总是将其进一步分割。随着《格拉斯-斯蒂高尔法案》的出台，美国终于迎来了自黑色星期四以来一直期盼的宣泄。正如莱芬韦尔所言：“饥饿与困苦如此普遍，人们将怒火倾泻到银行家身上，并迁怒于美国银行业最显赫的名字，这实在是再自然不过的事。” [88](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337494) 与此同时，摩根合伙人却自觉替他人背负了罪名。乔治·惠特尼后来评论道：“我在办公室任职期间，我们从未涉足零售业务，但问题恰恰始于零售领域。新政精于此道，他们明白，只要将证券业务拆解，就能夺走我们的权力——而他们确实做到了。” [89](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337698)
