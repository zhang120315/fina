# Chapter Three: Prince / 第三章 王子

AS Junius Morgan’s Wall Street agent for thirty years, Pierpont moved with the massed power of British capital behind him. A Wall Street jest said that his yacht, the *Corsair*, flew the Jolly Roger above the Stars and Stripes, and the Union lack above both. (Throughout his life, Pierpont would slyly hint at descent from the pirate Henry Morgan.) The young Morgan resembled a burly roughneck with a coat of British polish. Broad-shouldered and barrel-chested, he had dark hair and a pugilist’s hands. Over six feet tall, he was something of a dandy, now given to checkered vests. Where Junius had a hard and impenetrable stare, Pierpont’s hazel eyes were sad and cloudy. Where his father had unfailing composure, Pierpont was mercurial. In early pictures, he looks edgy, as if spoiling for a fight.
在长达三十年的时间里，皮尔庞特一直是朱尼厄斯·摩根在华尔街的代理人，他身后涌动着英国资本的磅礴力量。华尔街流传着一则笑话：他那艘名为“海盗号”的游艇，在星条旗上方飘扬着海盗旗，而在这两者之上，还飘着英国国旗。（皮尔庞特一生中，总爱狡猾地暗示自己是海盗亨利·摩根的后裔。）年轻的摩根活脱脱一个裹着英伦绅士外衣的粗犷莽汉——宽肩厚胸，黑发浓密，双手如拳击手般粗壮。他身高逾六英尺，却颇有几分花花公子的派头，尤爱穿格子马甲。朱尼厄斯目光如铁、高深莫测，而皮尔庞特那双淡褐色的眼睛却带着忧郁与迷惘；父亲永远沉着冷静，皮尔庞特却性情如风云变幻。在早期的照片里，他总显得锋芒毕露，仿佛随时准备与人干上一架。

There was plenty to fight about in the rough-and-tumble of the postwar railroad boom. Everybody had a sense of immense enterprise ahead. “We are going some day to show ourselves to be the richest country in the world in natural resources,” Pierpont predicted during the Civil War. The railroads would unlock the resources in the American wilderness. Perhaps no business has ever blossomed so spectacularly: within eight years of the war’s end, railroad trackage doubled to seventy thousand miles, a spree fed by tens of millions of acres in federal land grants. More than just isolated businesses, railroads were the scaffolding on which new worlds would be built. As Anthony Trollope noted during an American visit, railroads “were in fact companies combined for the purchase of land” whose value they hoped to increase by opening a road. Towns sprang up along the tracks, settled by European immigrants imported by the railroads.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194090)
战后铁路繁荣的乱局中，人们有太多可以争执的事情。每个人都预感到前方是巨大的机遇。“总有一天，我们会向世界证明自己是自然资源最丰富的国家，”皮尔庞特在内战期间如此预言。而铁路将解锁美国荒野中的这些资源。恐怕没有任何行业能像它这样蓬勃发展：战争结束后的八年内，铁路里程翻了一番，达到七万英里，这一狂欢背后是数千万英亩联邦土地赠予的滋养。铁路远非孤立的商业项目，而是构建新世界的骨架。正如安东尼·特罗洛普访美时所言，铁路“实际上是公司联合起来购买土地”，它们希望通过开通铁路来提升土地价值。沿着铁轨，欧洲移民被铁路公司运来，城镇如雨后春笋般涌现。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194090)

As speculation in rail shares grew frenzied, European investors were stumbling about in the dark. Between Kansas and the Rocky Mountains, schoolboy maps showed a blank space dubbed the great American desert.[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194251) Europeans relied on their American agents to guide them through this financial wilderness, and American bankers had to keep posted on developments. Soon after completion of the first transcontinental railroad, in May 1869, Pierpont and Fanny Morgan made an extended rail journey across the country, stopping to see Mormon leader Brigham Young in Utah. A competition was already underway on Wall Street between Jewish bankers, such as Joseph Seligman, who wooed German investors with railroad shares, and Yankee bankers, such as Pierpont Morgan, who drew on London money.
随着铁路股票投机愈演愈烈，欧洲投资者犹如在黑暗中摸索前行。从堪萨斯到落基山脉之间，学生地图上仍是一片空白，标注着"大美洲荒漠"的字样。欧洲人只能依赖在美的代理人指引他们穿越这片金融蛮荒之地，而美国银行家们则必须实时掌握最新动态。1869 年 5 月，首条横贯大陆的铁路竣工后不久，皮尔庞特与范妮·摩根便踏上横跨美国的漫长铁路之旅，途中在犹他州驻足拜访了摩门教领袖布里格姆·扬。彼时华尔街已燃起战火——一方是以约瑟夫·塞利格曼为代表的犹太银行家，用铁路股票招揽德国投资人；另一方则是以皮尔庞特·摩根为首的洋基银行家，依靠伦敦资本与之抗衡。

From the outset, railways were in a chaotic state as they covered the country in a crazy-quilt expansion that frequently produced more roads than traffic. Because of their exorbitant fixed costs, they should have been public utilities. But this was impossible in an age of free-booting individualism. As a result, assorted hucksters and rogues threw up twice the trackage actually needed. What appeared to be solid investment one moment was revealed as so much watered stock the next. In Henry Adams’s judgment, “The generation between 1865 and 1895 was already mortgaged to the railways and no one knew it better than the generation itself.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194405)
从一开始，铁路就以狂乱无序的扩张覆盖全国，往往铺设的路线比实际运输需求还多。由于固定成本高昂，它们本该是公共事业。但在那个崇尚野蛮个人主义的时代，这根本不可能实现。结果，各路奸商和骗子建起了实际所需两倍的轨道。前一秒看似稳健的投资，下一秒就被曝出是大量掺水股。用亨利·亚当斯的话来说，“1865 年至 1895 年间这代人已把未来抵押给了铁路，而再没有人比他们自己更清楚这一点了。”

Such anarchy could easily fire a moralistic young banker like Pierpont Morgan. In his early years, he was exposed to many incorrigible Wall Street rascals, including Daniel Drew, the rustic sharpster who sold Erie stock short while sitting on the railroad’s own board (he was called the speculative director), and Jay Gould, the small, swarthy, full-bearded financier who prodigally bribed legislators as he vied for control of the Erie and other railroads.[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194566) This was the infamous era of the Tweed Ring, Jay Gould’s 1869 attempt to corner the gold market, and other acts of larceny on a scale never before imagined. While Junius inhabited the white-glove world of the City, Pierpont had to deal with Wall Street squalor and found it alternately seductive and repellent. Confronted by corruption, he saw himself as a proxy for honorable European and American investors, a tool of transcendent purpose representing the sound men on Wall Street and in the City. But what he saw as a moral crusade others might regard simply as competing self-interest. In his early years, at least, he wasn’t always clearly distinguishable from the robber barons he was supposedly contesting.
这种无序状态很容易激怒像皮尔庞特·摩根这样道德感强烈的年轻银行家。他早年见识过许多无可救药的华尔街无赖，包括丹尼尔·德鲁——那个粗鄙的老滑头，一边坐在铁路公司董事席位上，一边做空伊利铁路股票（人称投机董事）；还有杰伊·古尔德——这个身材矮小、肤色黝黑、蓄着大胡子的金融家，在争夺伊利铁路及其他铁路控制权时，大肆贿赂议员。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194566) 这正是臭名昭著的特威德集团时期，1869 年杰伊·古尔德试图垄断黄金市场，以及各种规模空前的大规模盗窃案层出不穷。当朱尼厄斯置身于伦敦金融城上流社会时，皮尔庞特却不得不面对华尔街的污浊，时而觉得它诱人，时而感到厌恶。面对腐败，他自视为欧美正直投资者的代理人，是代表华尔街和伦敦金融城诚信人士的更高使命的工具。 但他眼中的道德征途，在别人看来可能不过是出于竞争的自利行为。 至少在他早年时期，他并不总能与那些他本应与之斗争的强盗大亨们清晰地划清界限。

In 1869, Pierpont, aged thirty-two, was enlisted in a dispute over a small upstate New York railroad that would establish his reputation as a self-assured young banker, unafraid to dirty his hands. This corporate fight would dramatize the transition of the American banker from a passive figure issuing shares for companies to a strong, active force in managing their affairs. The line in question, the 143-mile Albany and Susquehanna, was small and inconsequential. It had only 17 locomotives and 214 cars and ran through the sparsely populated Catskill Mountains between Albany and Binghamton, New York. Yet it became a battleground for competing powers when Jay Gould decided it could advance the fortunes of his Erie Railroad, the so-called Scarlet Woman of Wall Street. Through this road, Gould hoped to sell Pennsylvania coal to New England and also vie with the New York Central for freight from the Great Lakes.
1869 年，32 岁的 Pierpont 卷入了一场关于纽约州北部一条小铁路的争端，这让他确立了自己作为一名自信满满、不惧亲自动手的年轻银行家的声誉。这场公司之争戏剧化地展现了美国银行家从被动为公司发行股票的角色，转变为积极参与公司管理的强大力量。这条备受争议的铁路——全长 143 英里的 Albany and Susquehanna——规模小且无足轻重。它仅有 17 台机车和 214 节车厢，穿越纽约州 Albany 与 Binghamton 之间人口稀少的 Catskill Mountains。然而，当 Jay Gould 认定这条铁路能助推他的 Erie Railroad（所谓的“华尔街的红衣女人”）时，它便成为了各路势力争夺的战场。通过这条线路，Gould 希望将 Pennsylvania 的煤炭销往 New England，同时与 New York Central 争夺来自 Great Lakes 的货运业务。

To this end, Gould bought up a block of A&S stock, made an alliance with a dissident wing of directors, and had his pet judge, George C. Barnard, suspend the railroad’s founder, Joseph H. Ramsey, from the board. Ramsey countered by having several Gould partisans judicially suspended in turn. In these early days, corporate warfare was no mere euphemism, and the Ramsey and Gould forces sometimes slugged it out directly rather than filing suits and obtaining injunctions. In the Battle of the Susquehanna, Jim Fisk, a former circus roustabout and Gould’s chief lieutenant, and his Bowery boys—thugs scraped off New York’s streets and operating as Gould’s stooges—piled onto a train heading east from Binghamton, their army numbering about 800 men. The Ramsey forces loaded about 450 fighters onto a train heading west from Albany. In a cinematic finale, the two trains crashed head-on at the Long Tunnel near Binghamton. Their headlights were smashed, one locomotive was partly derailed, and eight or ten people were shot before the Gould forces fled. Governor Toots Hoffman summoned the state militia to stop the bloodshed.
为此，古尔德大量收购了 A&S 公司的股票，与董事会中持异议的派系结盟，并安排他宠信的法官乔治·C·巴纳德将铁路创始人约瑟夫·H·拉姆齐逐出董事会。拉姆齐则反过来借助司法手段，将古尔德的多名党羽逐出董事会。在那个年代，公司之间的战争绝非一句轻描淡写的比喻，拉姆齐和古尔德的势力有时干脆直接拳脚相向，而不是提起诉讼或申请禁令。在萨斯奎哈纳战役中，曾是马戏团勤杂工、现为古尔德左膀右臂的吉姆·菲斯克，带着他的“包厘街男孩”——一帮从纽约街头搜罗来的混混，充当古尔德的打手——挤上一列从宾厄姆顿向东行驶的火车，这支队伍大约有 800 人。拉姆齐的部队则装载约 450 名战士，登上一列从奥尔巴尼向西行驶的火车。在电影般的结局中，两列火车在宾厄姆顿附近的长隧道内迎头相撞。 他们的头灯被砸碎，一台机车部分脱轨，八到十人遭枪击后，Gould 势力才仓皇逃离。州长 Toots Hoffman 随即召集州国民警卫队，制止了这场流血冲突。

On September 7, 1869, momentarily putting down their weapons, the Gould and Ramsey forces converged on the annual board meeting of the A&S. Ramsey—“a little, grey-headed, sallow faced gentleman, weighing about 115 pounds, with a very bright eye”—had recruited the husky Pierpont, who had just returned from his western trip; Pierpont bought six hundred shares of stock in the road for Dabney, Morgan.[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194764) Pierpont’s son-in-law Herbert L. Satterlee later claimed that at the September 7 meeting, Pierpont hurled chubby Jim Fisk down a flight of stairs. The story may be apocryphal. But the meeting was so tense that Ramsey, who had hidden the subscription books in an Albany cemetery, had the documents lowered into the room from a back window to keep them from the hands of the Gould forces. In the end, the meeting was stalemated by competing injunctions, with each side again claiming control of the road based on two separate elections.
1869 年 9 月 7 日，古尔德与拉姆齐的部队暂时放下武器，齐聚 A&S 董事会年度会议。拉姆齐——一个“矮小、头发花白、面色蜡黄、体重约 115 磅、目光炯炯的绅士”——招募了刚从西部归来的魁梧壮汉皮尔庞特；皮尔庞特为达布尼·摩根公司买下了这条铁路的六百股股票。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194764) 皮尔庞特的女婿赫伯特·L·萨特利后来声称，在 9 月 7 日的会议上，皮尔庞特把胖墩吉姆·菲斯克直接扔下了楼梯。这故事或许只是传闻。但会议气氛紧张至极——拉姆齐把认股书藏在奥尔巴尼的一座墓地里，最后只能从后窗用绳子吊进房间，以防落入古尔德势力之手。最终，双方因相互发起禁令而陷入僵局，各自依据两场截然不同的选举结果，再次声称对铁路拥有控制权。

Under Pierpont’s tutelage, the Ramsey forces found a friendly judge in the upstate town of Delhi, New York, who obligingly ousted the Erie slate. Pierpont then advised the Ramsey forces, now back in control, to merge their railroad with the friendly Delaware and Hudson line, which they accomplished in February 1870. In settling the dispute, Pierpont made a move that marked his subsequent financial maneuvers: he took payment, not simply in money, but in power, becoming a director of the newly merged railroad. This first board seat was a sign of things to come, starting an era in which bankers sat on corporate boards and gradually came to rule them. Board membership would become a warning flag to other bankers to stay away from a captive company. During the 1870s, Pierpont began to style himself as far more than a mere provider of money to companies: he wanted to be their lawyer, high priest, and confidant. This wedding of certain companies to certain banks—“relationship banking”—would be a cardinal feature of private banking for the next century. It came about not because bankers were strong but because companies were still weak.
在皮尔庞特的指导下，拉姆齐阵营在纽约州北部德里镇找到了一位友好的法官，这位法官欣然将伊利铁路的董事会成员驱逐出局。随后，皮尔庞特建议重掌大权的拉姆齐阵营将他们的铁路与友好的特拉华-哈德逊铁路合并，这一合并于 1870 年 2 月完成。在平息这场争端的过程中，皮尔庞特采取了一项标志其后续金融操作风格的举措：他收取的报酬并非仅仅金钱，而是权力——他成为了新合并铁路公司的董事。这第一把董事会交椅预示了未来的趋势，开启了一个银行家入驻企业董事会并逐渐掌控董事会的时代。董事会席位后来成为警告其他银行家远离受控公司的警示信号。在 19 世纪 70 年代，皮尔庞特开始将自己定位为远不止是企业的资金提供者：他想要成为他们的律师、精神导师和心腹密友。 这些特定公司与特定银行之间的“联姻”——也就是所谓的“关系银行业务”——将成为接下来一个世纪里私人银行业务的一个核心特征。这种局面的形成，并非因为银行有多强势，而是因为当时的企业还太过弱小。

PIERPONT’S life was now prosperous and settled. He was making the gigantic salary of \$75,000 a year. He and Fanny lived in a brownstone at 6 East Fortieth Street, just across Fifth Avenue from the Croton Reservoir, which arose like a vast Egyptian tomb on the site of today’s New York Public Library. The Morgan home was comfortable and cluttered, furnished with rugs, heavy mahogany furniture, and gilt-framed pictures crowding one on top of the other. In 1872, Pierpont bought Cragston, a country retreat on the Hudson River near West Point. A three-story white Victorian house with rambling porches, its grounds comprised several hundred acres of spectacular river scenery and was Pierpont’s answer to Junius’s Dover House. There were horse stables, a dairy, tennis courts, and kennels for breeding collies. (When the collies got boisterous, he switched to breeding blooded cattle.) From April to October, Pierpont commuted to Wall Street, crossing the river on his steam launch, the *Louisa*, which seated about eight people. Then he took the train into Manhattan. The Morgans now had three children, Louisa, born in 1866, John Pierpont, Jr., or Jack, born in 1867, and Juliet, born in 1870. Before long, they would add another daughter, Anne.
皮尔庞特的生活如今蒸蒸日上，安稳富足。他拿着每年七万五千美元的巨额薪水，与范妮住在东四十街 6 号的一栋褐砂石房屋里，这栋房子就在第五大道对面，紧邻克罗顿水库——那座水库如同一座巨大的埃及陵墓，矗立在今天纽约公共图书馆的地基上。摩根的家舒适安逸，却也略显拥挤，铺着地毯，摆着厚重的桃花心木家具，镀金相框的画作层层叠叠地挂满墙壁。1872 年，皮尔庞特买下了克雷格斯顿，一个位于西点军校附近哈德逊河畔的乡间度假屋。这是一栋三层的白色维多利亚式住宅，拥有随性延伸的走廊，占地数百英亩，坐拥壮丽的河景，是皮尔庞特对朱尼厄斯的多佛庄园的回应。那里有马厩、奶牛场、网球场，还有专门用来繁育柯利牧羊犬的狗舍。（后来，当柯利犬变得过于吵闹时，他便转而繁育纯种肉牛了。）4 月到 10 月间，皮尔庞特乘着自己的蒸汽艇“路易莎”号——能坐大约八个人——渡河前往华尔街，然后再换乘火车进入曼哈顿。此时摩根家已有三个孩子：1866 年出生的路易莎、1867 年出生的小约翰·皮尔庞特（昵称杰克），以及 1870 年出生的朱丽叶。不久后，他们又添了一个女儿，安妮。

Behind the aura of comfort and precocity, Pierpont was a troubled young man. He continued to be bedeviled by headaches, fainting spells, and skin flare-ups. In 1871, his partner, Charles Dabney, retired and their partnership was dissolved. Not for the last time, Pierpont contemplated retirement. As if unable to stop his own ambition, he would assume tremendous responsibility, then feel oppressed. He never seemed to take great pleasure in his accomplishments, and for the rest of his life, he craved a restful but elusive peace.
在那层优渥与早熟的光环之下，皮尔庞特其实是个内心挣扎的年轻人。头痛、晕厥和皮肤出疹子这些毛病一直纠缠着他。1871 年，他的合伙人查尔斯·达布尼退休，两人的合伙关系随之解散。这已不是皮尔庞特第一次考虑退休了。仿佛无法抑制自己的野心，他总是不由自主地揽下巨大责任，随后又倍感压抑。他似乎从未真正为自己取得的成就感到多大快乐，而在余下的岁月里，他始终渴望一种安宁——那种安宁明明近在咫尺，却又遥不可及。

With Dabney retiring, Junius needed to find a partner for Pierpont. He also wanted to broaden the House of Morgan beyond its New York-London axis and strengthen its international securities business. Although we think of global finance as a modern invention, Victorian merchant banks were already multinational in structure and cosmopolitan in orientation. Instead of branch offices, they set up interlocking partnerships in foreign capitals—precisely what Junius now decided to do. In January 1871, he was approached in London by Anthony J. Drexel regarding an affiliation between his Philadelphia bank and the Morgans. Among the Philadelphia banks, Drexel’s was second only to Jay Cooke’s in government finance. Junius was already Drexel’s London correspondent. As when George Peabody approached him, a financial fortune was being laid at Junius’s feet. He was not only the ablest American banker of his day; he was also the luckiest.
随着达布尼退休，朱尼厄斯需要为皮尔庞特物色新的合伙人。他还希望将摩根银行的事业从纽约-伦敦双轴心进一步扩展，并加强其国际证券业务。尽管我们常将全球金融视为现代产物，但维多利亚时代的商人银行已然具备跨国架构和全球视野。它们不在海外设立分支机构，而是在外国首都建立连锁合伙关系——这正是朱尼厄斯此刻决意推行的策略。1871 年 1 月，他在伦敦收到了安东尼·J·德雷克塞尔关于其费城银行与摩根家族联姻的提议。在费城各家银行中，德雷克塞尔在政府融资领域的地位仅次于杰伊·库克。朱尼厄斯原本就是德雷克塞尔在伦敦的代理行。正如当年乔治·皮博迪主动找上门来一样，如今又一桩金融大运摆在了朱尼厄斯面前。他不仅是那个时代最杰出的美国银行家，更是最幸运的那一位。

Son of Francis M. Drexel, an itinerant Austrian portrait painter turned financier, Tony Drexel at forty-five was slim and refined with a smooth forehead, domed head, mild eyes, and handlebar mustache. At the time, Wall Street was shaping up as a provider as well as importer of capital as financial power gravitated from Philadelphia and Boston to New York. Sensing this seismic shift, the influential Drexel wished to fortify his New York operations. As before with Charles Dabney, Junius hoped to hedge the young Pierpont with safeguards and place him under the protective tutelage of an older man. So he suggested to Drexel that he take on Pierpont as his chief partner in New York.
弗朗西斯·M·德雷克塞尔之子托尼·德雷克塞尔，时年四十五岁，身材修长，温文尔雅，额头光滑，头顶圆润，目光柔和，留着两端上翘的八字胡。他的父亲曾是一位辗转各地的奥地利肖像画家，后转行成为金融家。彼时，随着金融势力从费城和波士顿向纽约转移，华尔街正逐步成为资本的提供者与输入者。敏锐察觉到这一巨大变革的德雷克塞尔希望加强其在纽约的业务。如同当年让查尔斯·达布尼为年轻的皮尔庞特保驾护航一样，朱尼厄斯也想为儿子设置多重保障，将他置于一位年长者的悉心指导下。于是，他向德雷克塞尔提议，让皮尔庞特担任他在纽约的首席合伙人。

However prodigious Pierpont’s gifts, he was still clay modeled by his father’s hands. Junius urged him to respond to any invitation from Drexel. Hence in May he dutifully traveled to Philadelphia, dined with Drexel, and chatted with him after dinner. He returned to New York with a partnership agreement scribbled on an envelope. According to the deal, Pierpont would become a partner of Drexel and Company in Philadelphia and Drexel, Harjes in Paris. He would also manage a New York partnership called Drexel, Morgan and Company. The order of the names reflected the importance of the partners. Tony Drexel and his two brothers, Francis and Joseph, were worth about \$7 million, while Pierpont had a puny \$350,000. To even the score, however, Junius pumped in \$5 million. Pierpont always acknowledged his debt to his father—he never pretended to be self-made—and later told New York governor Grover Cleveland, “If I have been able to succeed in the station of life in which I have been cast, I attribute it more than anything to the endorsement of my father’s friends.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194945) The new Drexel, Morgan was the forerunner of J. P. Morgan and Company.
然而，皮尔庞特的天资再卓绝，也不过是父亲手中精心雕琢的泥土。朱尼厄斯敦促他务必接受德雷克塞尔的一切邀请。于是，五月里他乖乖去了费城，与德雷克塞尔共进晚餐，饭后闲谈。回到纽约时，他带回一张写在信封上的合伙协议。根据协议，皮尔庞特将成为费城德雷克塞尔公司及巴黎德雷克塞尔-哈杰斯公司的合伙人，同时负责管理纽约的合伙公司——德雷克塞尔-摩根公司。名字的排序反映了合伙人的分量：托尼·德雷克塞尔和他的两位兄弟弗朗西斯与约瑟夫，身家约 700 万美元，而皮尔庞特仅有区区 35 万美元。不过，为了扳平局面，朱尼厄斯注入了 500 万美元。 皮尔庞特从不掩饰自己对父亲的感激——他从不假装是白手起家——后来他对纽约州长格罗弗·克利夫兰说："如果我在自己所处的社会地位上取得了成功，那首先要归功于我父亲朋友们的支持。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2194945) 新的德雷克塞尔-摩根公司正是 J.P.摩根公司的前身。

Before signing the deal, Pierpont laid down a curious condition—that he delay working on the new partnership. Far from itching to start, he felt a need to recuperate from emotional and physical travail. Apparently he was on the edge of a nervous breakdown. Under doctor’s orders, he took a fifteen-month vacation, traveling to Vienna and Rome and sailing up the Nile. At work, Pierpont could never relax and developed a powerful urge for escape. He would vacation three months each year and joked that he could perform twelve months of work in just nine months. His son-in-law Herbert Satterlee later wrote, “He seemed to feel better when he was actually travelling than when they settled down anywhere.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195099) In the late 1870s, when Pierpont tried to flee work by taking a vacation in Saratoga, New York, a blizzard of business letters and telegrams trailed after him. “There is only one way of getting real rest,” he told Junius, “and that is to get on board of a steamer.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195275)

Two years after its debut, in 1873, Drexel, Morgan moved to the corner of Wall and Broad streets. It would be the most celebrated address in banking, the financial crossroads of America. Tony Drexel had bought a parcel of land across the street from the New York Stock Exchange for \$349 a square foot, which stood as a record for the next thirty years. He built a heavily ribbed marble building with mansard roof, dormer windows, and ornate facade and allegorical figures above the doorway; the six-story building was one of the city’s first with an elevator. Splendidly symbolic, its unusual catercorner entrance simultaneously faced the Subtreasury Building on Nassau Street (the most important branch of the U.S. Treasury system) and the Stock Exchange on Wall Street. Appropriately, Drexel, Morgan would specialize in both railroad and government finance and occupy a pivotal place between Wall Street and Washington.
1873 年，也就是成立两年后，德雷克塞尔-摩根公司搬到了华尔街和布罗德街的拐角处。这将成为银行业最负盛名的地址，堪称美国金融的十字路口。托尼·德雷克塞尔以每平方英尺 349 美元的价格，在纽约证券交易所对面买下了一块地，这个价格在此后三十年里一直保持着纪录。他建造了一座厚重的肋纹大理石建筑，带有复折式屋顶、老虎窗、 ornate facade（ ornate facade 意为华丽的外立面），门口上方还有寓意人物雕像；这栋六层大楼是纽约首批安装电梯的建筑之一。寓意深远的是，它那不同寻常的斜角入口同时面向拿骚街的国库分库（美国财政系统最重要的分支机构）和华尔街的证券交易所。恰如其分地，德雷克塞尔-摩根公司将专注于铁路和政府金融业务，在华尔街与华盛顿之间占据枢纽地位。

From a personal standpoint, the Drexel-Morgan match wasn’t smooth. Pierpont was already gruff and difficult and insisted on having his own way. Joseph Seligman saw him as “a rough, uncouth fellow, continually quarreling with Drexel in the office.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195480) But the merger worked just as Junius had planned in terms of tempering Pierpont’s xcesses. An early Dun and Company report said, “This young man is smart and is perhaps the most venturesome member of the firm but he is kept in check by the Drexels.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195653)
从个人角度来看，德雷克塞尔与摩根的合并并不顺利。皮尔庞特本就粗暴难缠，还固执己见、一意孤行。约瑟夫·塞利格曼觉得他是“一个粗鲁无礼的家伙，整天在办公室里跟德雷克塞尔吵个没完。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195480) 不过，这次合并正如朱尼尔斯所料，成功收敛了皮尔庞特那股过分的锋芒。早期邓恩公司的报告指出：“这小子很精明，或许是公司里最大胆敢干的一个，但德雷克塞尔家的人总能把他按住。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195653)

The merger with the Drexels gave the Morgans new international breadth. In 1868, Drexel had sent John J. Harjes of Philadelphia to set up a Paris partnership, which performed with elan during the Paris Commune, switching operations to Switzerland to service American travelers and businessmen. (This wartime role would later be quintessentially Morgan’s.) As social butterflies who married into many prominent Philadelphia families, the Drexels also added a high-society image to the Morgan bank, and the Philadelphia house would always be a glamorous corner of the emerging empire. Through their interlocking partnerships, the Morgans now had footholds in New York, Philadelphia, London, and Paris. These would remain the brightest stars of the Morgan constellation for a century.
与德雷克塞尔家族的合并，为摩根家族带来了全新的国际视野。1868 年，德雷克塞尔派遣费城的约翰·J·哈杰斯前往巴黎设立合伙公司，这家公司在巴黎公社期间大显身手，将业务转移至瑞士，为美国游客和商人提供服务。（这种战时角色后来成了摩根的标志性特点。）德雷克塞尔家族如同社交蝴蝶，与费城众多显赫家族联姻，为摩根银行注入了上流社会的形象，费城公司也因此成为这个新兴帝国中光彩夺目的一隅。通过相互关联的合伙制，摩根家族如今在纽约、费城、伦敦和巴黎都站稳了脚跟。在接下来的一个世纪里，这些地方始终是摩根星座中最璀璨的星辰。

SOON after the Drexel-Morgan merger came an event that catapulted Pierpont Morgan, age thirty-six, into the empyrean of American finance. In 1873, Washington decided to refund, at lower interest rates, the \$300 million in bonded debt remaining from the Civil War. Until then, Jay Cooke—Tony Drexel’s main Philadelphia rival—reigned as the white-bearded emperor of federal finance. The self-made Cooke had started out as a bank clerk with a quick eye for counterfeit money. At a time when government bonds were the exclusive province of rich men and European banks, he marketed them to the masses. During the Civil War, he pioneered in retail distribution, sending twenty-five hundred “minute-man” agents to peddle Union bonds across America and winning Lincoln’s gratitude. With his riches, Cooke built a fifty-two-room castle outside Philadelphia. In the early 1870s, the phrase “rich as Jay Cooke” had the same magic resonance as “rich as Rockefeller” would have in a later day.
德雷克塞尔与摩根合并后不久，一场事件将 36 岁的皮尔庞特·摩根推向了美国金融界的巅峰。1873 年，华盛顿决定以较低的利率对内战遗留的 3 亿美元债券进行再融资。在此之前，托尼·德雷克塞尔在费城的主要竞争对手杰伊·库克，一直统治着联邦金融界，这位白胡子的金融皇帝。白手起家的库克最初是一名银行职员，对假钞有敏锐的识别力。当时政府债券是富人和欧洲银行的专属领地，他却将其推销给大众。内战期间，他开创了零售分销的先河，派遣 2500 名“一分钟人”代理人走遍美国兜售联邦债券，赢得了林肯的感激。凭借财富，库克在费城郊外建造了一座拥有 52 个房间的城堡。19 世纪 70 年代初，“富如杰伊·库克”这句话有着后来“富如洛克菲勒”般的魔力。

Cooke seemed invincible to competitors—at least until he financed the Northern Pacific Railroad in 1869. His promotion for \$100 million in Northern Pacific bonds was liberally spiced with invention, fraudulence, and political bribery. To lure European settlers to towns serviced by the railroad, he created a tissue of brazenly surreal lies. Colorful ads depicted fruit groves flourishing along its Great Plains tracks—fantastic claims that won the railroad the nickname of Jay Cooke’s Banana Republic. Cow towns were puffed up into vast metropolises, and Duluth, Minnesota, was trumpeted to European immigrants as the “Zenith City of the Unsalted Seas.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195808) When grain prices fell after the Franco-Prussian war, the fortunes of the Northern Pacific and other railroads fell along with them. Thus began Jay Cooke’s undoing. His vulnerability in relation to the Northern Pacific would provide an opening for Drexel, Morgan to usurp his exalted place in government finance.
库克在竞争对手面前似乎所向无敌——至少在他于 1869 年为 Northern Pacific Railroad 融资之前是这样。他为 Northern Pacific 推销一亿美元债券的手段充斥着捏造、欺诈和政治贿赂。为了吸引欧洲定居者前往铁路沿线的城镇，他编造了一套厚颜无耻的虚假谎言。色彩斑斓的广告描绘了大平原铁路沿线果园繁茂的景象——这些离谱的宣传让这条铁路获得了“Jay Cooke's Banana Republic”的绰号。牛镇被吹嘘成繁华大都市，明尼苏达州的 Duluth 则向欧洲移民鼓吹为“Zenith City of the Unsalted Seas”。 [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195808) 普法战争后粮食价格下跌，Northern Pacific 及其他铁路公司的财富也随之缩水。Jay Cooke 的败局由此开始。他在 Northern Pacific 上的脆弱之处，为 Drexel, Morgan 篡夺其在政府金融中的崇高地位提供了契机。

In 1873, Cooke teamed up with two Jewish houses—Seligman’s on Wall Street and the Rothschilds’ in Europe—to obtain the \$300 million refunding issue against a vigorous challenge from Drexel, Morgan; J. S. Morgan and Company; Morton, Bliss; and Baring Brothers. Large-scale finance was increasingly shaping up as a contest between powerful syndicates; the sums—and the risks—were now too large for single houses to shoulder alone. The Drexel, Morgan group contested the Cooke monopoly and also circulated insidious rumors that Cooke needed victory in the refunding issue to recoup his Northern Pacific losses. Tony Drexel, a close friend of President Grant, proselytized through his partial ownership of the Philadelphia *Public Ledger.* Bowing to intense pressure from the Drexel, Morgan group, the secretary of the treasury awarded half of the issue to each syndicate, although the status-conscious Junius was disturbed by Cooke’s name preceding theirs on the contract. The prominence of American banks in this display of federal financing reflected the new postwar power of Wall Street.
1873 年，库克与两家犹太财团——华尔街的塞利格曼家族和欧洲的罗斯柴尔德家族——联手，以 3 亿美元获得国债再融资发行权，此举遭到了德雷克塞尔-摩根、J.S.摩根公司、莫顿-布利斯以及巴林兄弟的强烈挑战。大规模金融运作日益演变为财团巨头间的角力；单打独斗已难以承担如此庞大的金额与风险。德雷克塞尔-摩根集团不仅挑战库克的垄断地位，还散布恶毒谣言，称库克必须赢得这笔国债再融资才能弥补北太平洋铁路的亏损。托尼·德雷克塞尔与格兰特总统私交甚密，他通过部分持股的《费城公共纪事报》游说。面对德雷克塞尔-摩根集团的巨大压力，财政部长最终将发行权平分给两大财团——尽管注重门第的朱尼厄斯对合同中库克的名字排在自己前面感到不快。 美国银行在此次联邦融资展示中的突出地位，反映了战后华尔街新崛起的力量。

The year 1873 was one of panicky markets that allowed the Morgans to leave behind their reputation as relative outsiders and achieve a commanding position in federal finance. Financial markets were at first unsettled by the scandal of the Credit Mobilier, builder of the Union Pacific Railroad, and exposed as a giant sinkhole of fraud and corruption. The scandal tarred the reputation of many congressmen holding the ephemeral company’s stock. By August 1873, London investors wouldn’t touch American bonds, one reporter said, “even if signed by an angel of Heaven.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195974) Then, debilitated by the Northern Pacific, the mighty house of Jay Cooke failed on Black Thursday, September 18, 1873.
1873 年是市场恐慌的一年，这让摩根家族得以摆脱相对局外人的名声，在联邦金融领域取得主导地位。金融市场最初因建造 Union Pacific Railroad 的 Credit Mobilier 丑闻而动荡不安，该公司被曝出是一个巨大的欺诈与腐败黑洞。这一丑闻玷污了许多持有这家短命公司股票的国会议员的声誉。到了 1873 年 8 月，伦敦的投资者对美国债券避之不及，一位记者甚至说，“就算债券是由天使签署的，他们也不会碰。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2195974) 随后，受 Northern Pacific 拖累，强大的 Jay Cooke 公司于 1873 年 9 月 18 日黑色星期四宣告破产。

The failure ignited a full-blown Wall Street panic. For the first time since its formation, the New York Stock Exchange shut its doors for ten days. The corner outside the exchange became a wailing wall of ruined men. Diarist George Templeton Strong noted that “the central focus of excitement was, of course, at the corner of Broad and Wall Streets. People \[were\] swarming on the Treasury steps looking down on the seething mob that filled Broad Street.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196109) Pierpont called in his loans and cabled Junius: “Affairs continue unprecedentedly bad.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196244) Five thousand commercial firms and fifty-seven Stock Exchange firms were dragged down in Cooke’s maelstrom, a cataclysmic experience for a generation of Americans. “To my parents and to the outside world,” financial journalist Alexander Dana Noyes would later recall, “the financial crash of September 1873 had been as memorable a landmark as, to the community of half a century later, was the panic of October 1929.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196399)
这场失败彻底点燃了华尔街的恐慌。自纽交所成立以来，首次闭市长达十天。交易所外的街角成了破产者们的哭墙。日记作者乔治·坦普尔顿·斯特朗记载道：“情绪的焦点当然集中在布罗德街与华尔街的交汇处。人们涌上财政部台阶，俯视着布罗德街上沸腾的人群。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196109) 皮尔庞特收回了他的贷款，并电告朱尼厄斯：“局势空前恶化。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196244) 库克的金融漩涡拖垮了五千家商业公司和五十七家交易所公司，给整整一代美国人带来了灾难性的打击。金融记者亚历山大·达纳·诺伊斯后来回忆道：“对于我的父母和外界而言，1873 年 9 月的金融崩盘如同半个世纪后 1929 年 10 月的大恐慌一样，是一个令人难忘的里程碑。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196399)

By today’s standards, Wall Street looked almost pastoral: Trinity Church was the tallest structure, and street lamps on the cobblestone streets stood higher than many buildings. The six-story Drexel Building soared above its neighbors. Yet after Jay Cooke’s failure, it was popularly seen as the street of sin, a place responsible for corrupting the manners and morals of a pristine frontier nation. Not for the last time, America turned against Wall Street with puritanical outrage and a sense of offended innocence. Thomas Nast’s cartoons in *Harper’s Weekly* showed heaps of slaughtered animals in front of Trinity Church, the church itself scowling, with the words MORAL, I TOLD YOU SO emblazoned on its steeple. Wall Street already had a way of being renounced once the party was over.
按今天的标准来看，华尔街几乎带有几分田园气息：三一教堂是最高的建筑，鹅卵石街道上的街灯比许多楼房还高。六层高的德雷克塞尔大厦在周围建筑中鹤立鸡群。然而在杰伊·库克破产后，华尔街普遍被视为罪恶之街，一个让纯真边疆国家的道德风尚走向腐化的地方。这绝非最后一次，美国以清教徒式的愤怒和被冒犯的纯真感，将矛头指向了华尔街。《哈珀周刊》上托马斯·纳斯特的漫画描绘了三一教堂前堆积如山的牲畜尸体，教堂本身面露愠色，尖塔上赫然印着"道德，我早告诉过你"的字样。华尔街早已有了狂欢散场即遭唾弃的宿命。

In much the same way as the Morgan bank would in 1929, Pierpont managed a handy profit in the panic year of 1873. He made over \$1 million, boasting to Junius: “I don’t believe there is another concern in the country \[that\] can begin to show such a result.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196556) With Jay Cooke conveniently wiped off the map, Drexel, Morgan stood, with miraculous suddenness, at the apex of American government finance. Never again would Pierpont Morgan be an outsider, and before long he would be the chief arbiter of the establishment. Drexel, Morgan couldn’t immediately capitalize on its fame, however, since the 1873 panic ushered in a period of extended deflation and depression, during which it became hard to credit Junius’s injunction to “remember one thing always. . . . Always be a ‘bull’ on America.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196711)
正如 1929 年的摩根银行一样，皮尔庞特在 1873 年的恐慌年份中轻松获利。他赚了超过 100 万美元，并向朱尼厄斯夸耀道：“我相信国内没有第二家公司能拿出这样的成绩。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196556) 随着杰伊·库克从地图上销声匿迹，德雷克塞尔-摩根公司奇迹般瞬间登顶美国政府金融的巅峰。皮尔庞特·摩根从此不再是局外人，不久后他便成为体制内的首席仲裁者。然而，德雷克塞尔-摩根公司无法立即将名声变现，因为 1873 年的恐慌引发了长期通缩与萧条，这使得朱尼厄斯的嘱托难以兑现——“永远记住一件事……永远做美国的‘多头’。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196711)

The House of Morgan’s future approach to business was shaped in the gloomy days of 1873. The panic was a disaster for European investors, who lost \$600 million in American railroad stocks. Stung by all the railroad bankruptcies, Pierpont decided to limit his future dealings to elite companies. He became the sort of tycoon who hated risk and wanted only sure things. “I have come to the conclusion that neither my firm nor myself will have anything to do, hereafter, directly or indirectly, with the negotiation of securities of any undertaking not entirely completed; and whose status, by experience, would not prove it entitled to a credit in every respect unassailable.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196901) Another time, he said, “The kind of Bonds which I want to be connected with are those which can be recommended without a shadow of doubt, and without the least subsequent anxiety, as to payment of interest, as it matures.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197107)This encapsulated future Morgan strategy—dealing only with the strongest companies and shying away from speculative ventures.
摩根家族未来的经营方针，是在 1873 年那段阴郁岁月中逐步成型的。这场恐慌对欧洲投资者而言堪称灾难——他们在美国铁路股票上损失了六亿美元。接连不断的铁路破产让皮尔庞特深受打击，他决定今后只与精英企业打交道。他成了那种厌恶风险、只求稳妥的金融巨擘。“我得出的结论是，无论我的公司还是我个人，今后都不得直接或间接参与任何尚未完全竣工项目的证券交易；按照经验，这些项目的情况无法证明其信用在任何方面都坚不可摧。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2196901) 另一次，他说：“我希望经手的债券，必须是在支付到期利息方面毫无疑义、且不会让人有丝毫后顾之忧的品种。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197107) 这一策略概括了摩根未来的方针——只与实力最强的公司打交道，规避投机性业务。

Under the Gentleman Banker’s Code, bankers held themselves responsible for bonds they sold and felt obligated to intervene when things went awry. And the railroads were going awry. Even before the 1873 panic, a new way of dealing with railroad rascality had appeared, devised, improbably, by Jay Gould. When investors boycotted an Erie bond issue in 1871, he proposed to bring in outside coal, railway, and banking interests to run the railroad as “voting trustees” who would control a majority of Erie stock. To placate the conservative side of Wall Street and the City, he proposed Junius Morgan as one trustee. The plan was stillborn but later was revived. By mid-decade, Junius was warning the president of the Baltimore and Ohio Railroad that rate wars among railroads were undermining investors’ confidence.[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197274) The following year, when the Erie went bankrupt, the irate bondholders shackled the road with a “voting trust” that would run the operation. It was a pivotal moment—the revenge of the creditors against the debtors, the bankers against the railwaymen. Later, in Pierpont’s hands, the simple device of the voting trust would convert Morgan into America’s most powerful man, placing much of the country’s railway system under his personal control. Through such trusts, he would convert financiers from servants to masters of their clients.
在“绅士银行家准则”下，银行家对自己售出的债券负有责任，当事情出现问题时，他们觉得自己有义务介入。而铁路行业当时确实在出问题。早在 1873 年恐慌之前，一种对付铁路乱象的新手段就已出现——令人意外的是，它出自杰伊·古尔德之手。1871 年，当投资者抵制伊利铁路公司的一批债券时，他提议引入外部煤炭、铁路和银行利益方，以“投票受托人”的身份运营这条铁路，并控制伊利铁路的大部分股份。为了安抚华尔街和伦敦金融城的保守派，他提议让朱尼厄斯·摩根担任其中一位受托人。这个计划虽然夭折了，但后来又被重新拾起。到 19 世纪 70 年代中期，朱尼厄斯向巴尔的摩和俄亥俄铁路公司总裁发出警告，称铁路之间的价格战正在削弱投资者的信心。 [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197274) 第二年，伊利铁路公司破产，愤怒的债券持有人用“投票信托”的方式束缚了这条铁路，由其接管运营。 那是一个决定性的时刻——债权人终于向债务人复仇，银行家向铁路工人反击。后来，在皮尔庞特手中，简单的表决信托工具将摩根变成了美国最有权势的人，全国大半铁路系统尽在其掌控之中。通过这种信托机制，他让金融家从客户的仆从摇身一变，成为他们的主宰者。

The story of Pierpont Morgan is that of a young moralist turned despot, one who believed implicitly in the correctness of his views. Strong-willed and opinionated, he had an unshakable faith in his own impulses—a quality that later made him appear as a force of nature, a child of the *Zeitgeist*, making snap decisions that were often eerily right. He differed from most of the Gilded Age robber barons in that their rapacity stemmed from pure greed or lust for power while his included some strange admixture of idealism. As he confronted an economy that offended his sense of business propriety, his very conservatism gave him a revolutionary zeal. He believed, quite arrogantly, that he knew how the economy should be ordered and how people should behave. By no coincidence, he was active in the Young Men’s Christian Association, which discouraged gambling among the working class. He also sponsored revival meetings at Madison Square Garden and backed the moral policeman Anthony Comstock, who favored the covering up of nude statues.
皮尔庞特·摩根的传说，讲述的是一个道德青年如何蜕变为独裁者的故事——他对自己观点的正确性深信不疑。他意志坚定、固执己见，对自己的直觉有着不可动摇的信念——这种特质后来让他看起来像是一股自然之力，一个时代的弄潮儿，总能做出近乎诡异的正确决断。他与镀金时代大多数强盗大亨的不同之处在于，那些人的贪婪源于纯粹的欲望或对权力的渴求，而他的贪婪中还夹杂着某种奇怪的理想主义成分。当他面对一个冒犯了他商业道德感的经济体系时，正是他骨子里的保守主义赋予了他一股革命般的狂热。他傲慢地坚信，他知道经济该如何运作，人们该如何行事。这绝非巧合，他活跃于基督教青年会，而该组织正是禁止工人阶级赌博的。 他还赞助了麦迪逊广场花园的复兴聚会，并支持道德警察安东尼·康斯托克，此人力主遮盖裸体雕像。

Pierpont developed a reputation for snappishness and barking at people, a propensity that grew with his fame. Even in letters to his father as early as the 1870s, he seemed committed to his own way of doing things and wrote less as a servile son than as a highly confident business partner. In 1881, a report by R. G. Dun and Company referred to Pierpont’s “peculiar brusqueness of manner” and said it had “made him and his house unpopular with many.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197429) He sat behind a glass partition in the mahogany partners’ room at 23 Wall Street, chewing on a big cigar and growling out “yes” or “no” when given offers on foreign exchange. He wouldn’t haggle and presented his bids for foreign exchange on a take-it-or-leave-it basis. He had a way of letting people cool their heels and knew all the silent tricks of authority. With his clear-cut sense of right and wrong, he quickly became accustomed to exercising leadership.
皮尔庞特以脾气暴躁、动辄训人而闻名，这种倾向随着他的名气与日俱增。早在 19 世纪 70 年代，即便在写给父亲的信中，他也显得固执己见，更像是一个充满自信的商业伙伴，而非恭顺的儿子。1881 年，邓白氏公司的一份报告提到皮尔庞特“态度异常生硬”，并称这“让他和他的公司在许多人中不受欢迎。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197429) 他坐在华尔街 23 号红木合伙人房间里的一扇玻璃隔断后，叼着一根大雪茄，对方报出外汇价格时，他只咆哮着说“行”或“不行”。他不讨价还价，提出的外汇报价都是最终价，爱买不买。他善于让人干等，深谙权威的各种无声手段。凭借其明确的是非观念，他很快就习惯了发号施令。

Not surprisingly, he had trouble delegating authority and low regard for the intelligence of other people. He agonized over finding new partners, and people never measured up to his inflated standards. To find suitable candidates in 1875, he pored over business directories from New York, Philadelphia, and Boston—in vain. “The longer I live the more apparent becomes the absence of brains—particularly soundly balanced brains,” he told Junius.[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197564) Once again, Pierpont flirted with the notion of quitting banking and casting off the oppressive weight of business. In 1876, when Joseph Drexel left the firm, Pierpont wanted to follow him, but he held back, awaiting word of Junius’s plans. He was chained to his bank by a sense of mission that never abandoned him. Perhaps never in financial history has anybody else amassed so much power so reluctantly. J. Pierpont Morgan was more exhausted than exhilarated by success. He didn’t enjoy responsibility and never learned to cope with it.
不足为奇的是，他难以授权，也看不上别人的智商。他苦于寻找新合伙人，而人们永远达不到他那些膨胀的标准。1875 年，为了找到合适人选，他翻遍了纽约、费城和波士顿的商业名录——结果徒劳无功。“我活得越久，就越发明显地看到缺乏头脑——特别是健全平衡的头脑，”他对朱尼厄斯说。 [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197564) 皮尔庞特又一次动了退出银行业、卸下沉重生意负担的念头。1876 年，约瑟夫·德雷克塞尔离开公司时，皮尔庞特本想跟他一起走，但他忍住了，等待朱尼厄斯的计划。他被一种从未离弃的使命感束缚在银行。也许金融史上，从未有人如此不情愿地积累起如此巨大的权力。J.皮尔庞特·摩根对成功感到的不是兴奋，而是疲惫。他并不享受责任，也从未学会如何应对。

Pierpont was a natural leader on Wall Street. Whatever the general public might think of the Morgans, businessmen respected them for their honest dealings. August Belmont, Sr., thought Pierpont “brusque but fair.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197699) Andrew Carnegie, who raised the money for his first rolling mill by brokering bonds to Junius, told the story of how during the 1873 panic the Morgans sold his interest in a railroad for \$10,000. He already had \$50,000 on deposit with Pierpont, and when he showed up to claim his \$60,000, Pierpont handed him \$70,000 instead. Pierpont said that they had underestimated his account and insisted he accept the additional \$10,000. Carnegie didn’t want to take the money. “Will you please accept these ten thousand with my best wishes?” Carnegie asked him. “No, thank you,” Pierpont replied. “I cannot do it.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197865) Carnegie decided that in future he would never harm the Morgans. Interestingly, Carnegie venerated Junius as the model of the sound, old-fashioned banker, but there was always friction between him and Pierpont. After one 1876 meeting with Carnegie, Pierpont bluntly chastised him—“You used language very offensive in its character”—and proceeded to rebut Carnegie’s statements about his firm’s role in a lawsuit.
皮尔庞特是华尔街天生的领袖。无论大众如何看待摩根家族，商界人士都因其诚信经营而敬重他们。老奥古斯特·贝尔蒙特认为皮尔庞特“虽显粗鲁，但处事公道”。 [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197699) 安德鲁·卡内基曾通过向朱尼厄斯推销债券筹钱建起第一座轧钢厂，他讲过这样一个故事：1873 年经济恐慌期间，摩根家族以 1 万美元的价格出售了他持有的一条铁路的权益。当时他在皮尔庞特那里已有 5 万美元存款，当卡内基去取那 6 万美元时，皮尔庞特反而给了他 7 万美元。皮尔庞特说他们之前算少了账户金额，坚持他必须收下多出的 1 万美元。卡内基本想推辞。“你愿不愿收下这一万美元，连同我最好的祝福？”卡内基问道。“不了，谢谢，”皮尔庞特回答，“我不能这么做。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2197865) 卡内基从此下定决心，日后绝不会与摩根家族为敌。 有趣的是，卡内基将朱尼厄斯视为稳健老派银行家的典范，但他与皮尔庞特之间始终存在摩擦。1876 年的一次会面后，皮尔庞特直截了当地斥责他——“你用的措辞极具冒犯性”——接着反驳了卡内基关于其公司在某起诉讼中角色的言论。

The standing of Drexel, Morgan rose steadily through the 1870s. In 1877, a congressional dispute held up payment due the army of General Miles, then fighting the Nez Perce Indians out West. In a flamboyant gesture, Drexel, Morgan volunteered to cash the army’s pay vouchers for a 1-percent commission—which made Pierpont very popular with the soldiers. By 1879, the ascendant Morgans were joining with August Belmont and the Rothschilds to market the last Civil War refunding loan. The United States resumed specie payment that year—that is, government notes were payable in silver or gold—and the issue was a great success.
德雷克塞尔-摩根公司的地位在 19 世纪 70 年代稳步上升。1877 年，国会的一场争论导致当时正在西部与内兹珀斯印第安人作战的迈尔斯将军部队的军饷被拖欠。德雷克塞尔-摩根公司高调亮相，主动提出以 1%的佣金兑付军队的工资凭证——这一举动让皮尔庞特在士兵中广受欢迎。到 1879 年，如日中天的摩根家族与奥古斯特·贝尔蒙特及罗斯柴尔德家族联手，推销最后一笔内战再融资债券。同年，美国恢复铸币支付——即政府票据可用白银或黄金兑付——此次债券发行大获成功。

Far from being thrilled by this new parity with the Rothschilds, Pierpont was offended by the supposed high-handedness of his partners. The more conciliatory Junius insisted that the Rothschilds share in any syndicate, but Pierpont’s enormous ego brooked no condescension. As he wrote his brother-in-law Walter Burns, now Junius’s partner in London: “I need scarcely tell you that having anything to do with Rothschilds & Belmont in this matter is extremely unpalatable to us and I would give almost anything if they were out. The whole treatment of Rothschild’s to all the party, from Father downwards is such, as to my mind, no one should stand.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198037) In fact, the Rothschilds had badly miscalculated America’s importance to the future of world finance, and it would prove an irremediable blunder. Their representative, August Belmont, bemoaned their “utter want of appreciation of the importance of American business.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198246) Now the Morgan star was on the rise, and within a generation it would outshine that of both the Rothschilds and the Barings.
皮尔庞特非但没为能与罗斯柴尔德家族平起平坐而兴奋，反而对合伙人那种自以为是的高傲态度深感不悦。向来圆融的朱尼厄斯坚持让罗斯柴尔德家族参与财团，但皮尔庞特那高傲的自尊心可容不得半点施舍。他在给妹夫沃尔特·伯恩斯（如今是朱尼厄斯在伦敦的合伙人）的信中写道：“无需多言，在这件事上与罗斯柴尔德和贝尔蒙打交道，简直让我们如鲠在喉；我宁愿倾尽所有，只求他们置身事外。罗斯柴尔德一家对待我们所有人——从父亲往下——的态度，在我看来，没人该忍气吞声。” 事实上，罗斯柴尔德家族严重低估了美国在全球金融未来的地位，这注定成为他们无法挽回的败笔。其代表奥古斯特·贝尔蒙哀叹道：“他们根本不懂美国生意有多重要。”” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198246) 如今，摩根家族如日中天，只需一代人的时间，其光芒便将盖过罗斯柴尔德家族和巴林家族。

THE financial writer John Moody said that until 1879 Pierpont Morgan was “merely the son of his grim-mouthed father.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198412) Junius, all business, found it hard to give up his all-consuming work. Now portly like “an East Indian merchant prince in an old English play,” he appears slightly bent in photographs, sedentary, heavy with care, gazing from beneath shaggy eyebrows.[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198568) The airy elegance of youth has settled into a craggy look of suspicion. In 1873, when he reached sixty, Pierpont was already urging him to cut back his schedule. He wrote, “It occurs to me to suggest that *you* need rest as much as I do, & I do not quite see why you cannot also take two days away from office per week.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198723) Junius wasn’t as rigidly attached to the office as Peabody, but he was domineering and at times had only one partner.
金融作家约翰·穆迪曾说过，直到 1879 年，皮尔庞特·摩根都“不过是他那位面色严峻的父亲之子”。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198412) 朱尼厄斯一心扑在事业上，很难放下自己全身心投入的工作。如今他身形臃肿，犹如“老式英国戏剧中的东印度商业巨贾”，照片中的他略显佝偻，久坐不动，忧心忡忡，从浓密的眉毛下凝视着远方。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198568) 年轻时那种飘逸优雅已化为一脸猜疑的粗犷神情。1873 年，他年满六十时，皮尔庞特已经开始催促他缩减日程。他写道：“我突然想到，你和我一样需要休息，我不太明白你为什么不能也每周抽出两天离开办公室。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198723) 朱尼厄斯不像皮博迪那样死守着办公室不放，但他专横跋扈，有时连合伙人也只有一位。

The elder Morgan now began to reap the honors of a semiretirement. On November 8, 1877, he enjoyed a last hurrah in his native country with a New York dinner at Delmonico’s in his honor, sponsored by the city’s business community. This impressive gathering of more than a hundred people numbered John Jacob Astor and the elder Theodore Ropsevelt among its dignitaries. Breaking a self-imposed ban on public appearances, Samuel J. Tilden, a former governor of New York and just-defeated presidential candidate, presided. Toasting Junius as America’s preeminent banker in London, Tilden lauded Junius for “upholding unsullied the honor of America in the tabernacle of the Old World.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198928) As in Peabody’s day, American businessmen believed they had to prove their worth in London. In reply, Junius said his lifelong crusade was that no evil should be spoken of America. Nobody in those days talked of British obligations or of nascent American power—only of how Americans should please British creditors. Under Pierpont, the financial position of the two countries would be strikingly reversed.
老摩根如今开始享受半退休带来的荣誉。1877 年 11 月 8 日，他在故乡迎来了最后的辉煌——纽约商界在德尔莫尼科餐厅为他举办了一场晚宴。这场盛大的聚会聚集了上百位宾客，其中包括约翰·雅各布·阿斯特和西奥多·罗斯福一世这样的显赫人物。前纽约州长、刚刚竞选总统失利的塞缪尔·J·蒂尔登打破了自己不公开露面的禁令，亲自担任主持。在为这位驻伦敦的美国杰出银行家祝酒时，蒂尔登盛赞朱尼厄斯“在旧世界的圣殿中，维护了美国荣誉的纯洁无瑕”。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2198928) 如同皮博迪时代一样，美国商界始终认为必须在伦敦证明自己的价值。朱尼厄斯在答辞中表示，他毕生奋斗的目标就是不让任何人说美国的坏话。 那时没有人谈论英国的责任或新兴的美国实力——只讨论美国人应如何取悦英国债权人。在皮尔庞特领导下，两国的财政地位将发生惊人逆转。

Pierpont’s relationship with his father was the most important in his life. Junius was the sort of punishing father who built character by stinting on praise and setting exacting standards, keeping up psychic pressure and always making Pierpont prove himself. Tough and demanding, he produced a son who lashed himself into ever greater exertion, only to lapse into sickness, fatigue, or depression. Junius strengthened those already relentless impulses in Pierpont’s nature—his overmastering need to achieve, his inordinate sense of responsibility, his hatred of disorder. Yet the patriarchal Morgan clan permitted no rebellion, only veneration of Father. Whatever fear and resentment Pierpont felt were transmuted into exaggerated love, and such filial worship would be equally apparent in Pierpont’s own children and grandchildren.
皮尔庞特与父亲的关系是他一生中最重要的纽带。朱尼乌斯是那种严苛的父亲，通过吝于表扬、设定高标准、施加精神压力来塑造儿子的品格，始终让皮尔庞特不断证明自己。这位强硬苛刻的父亲，培养出了一个把自己逼到精疲力竭，却只能陷入疾病、疲劳或抑郁的儿子。朱尼乌斯强化了皮尔庞特天性中那些本就难以遏制的冲动——他对成就的强烈渴望、过度的责任感、以及对混乱的憎恶。然而，这位父权至上的摩根家族不允许任何反抗，只允许对父亲的崇拜。皮尔庞特内心所有的恐惧与怨恨，都转化成了夸张的爱意，而这种对父亲的崇拜，同样会鲜明地体现在皮尔庞特自己的子女和孙辈身上。

Under his sometimes stern facade, Junius clearly adored Pierpont; the obsessive grooming was a tacit acknowledgment of his son’s gifts. In 1876, he decided to buy Pierpont a princely gift—Gainsborough’s portrait of the duchess of Devonshire, possibly the world’s most popular painting at the time. The Rothschilds had already bid for it, and Junius was prepared to top them by paying Agnew’s of Bond Street \$50,000. Before the sale was consummated, however, the painting was stolen from Agnew’s. Even a £1,000 reward couldn’t coax it back. Interestingly, when the painting resurfaced in 1901, Pierpont rushed to buy it for £30,000, or \$150,000. “If the truth came out,” he conceded regarding the staggering price, “I might be considered a candidate for the lunatic asylum.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199114) It was a deeply sentimental homage to his father. At 13 Princes Gate, the London townhouse he inherited from Junius, he hung the painting in the cherished spot over the mantelpiece.
在外表严厉的外表下，朱尼尔斯显然深爱着皮尔庞特——那份近乎苛刻的悉心栽培，其实是对儿子天赋的默许。1876 年，他决定送给皮尔庞特一份贵重的礼物——庚斯博罗所绘的德文郡公爵夫人肖像，这可能是当时全世界最受欢迎的画作。罗斯柴尔德家族已经出价竞购，朱尼尔斯准备加价，以五万美元从邦德街的阿格纽画廊买下。然而，交易尚未完成，画作便从阿格纽画廊被盗。即便悬赏一千英镑也无法将其追回。有趣的是，当这幅画在 1901 年重现时，皮尔庞特迫不及待地以三万英镑（合十五万美元）将其买下。"要是真相传出去"，他对这个令人咋舌的价格坦言，"恐怕大家会把我当成疯子。" [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199114) 这是对父亲充满深情的致敬。在朱尼尔斯留给他的伦敦宅邸——王子门 13 号，他把这幅画挂在壁炉上方最珍视的位置。

In 1879, Pierpont began to emerge from his father’s shadow and take charge of major deals. He was picked to market the largest block of stock ever publicly offered—250,000 shares of New York Central. It was a landmark event for the Vanderbilts, who owned the railroad.
1879 年，皮尔庞特开始走出父亲的阴影，主导大型交易。他被选中承销当时公开上市的最大规模股票——纽约中央铁路的 25 万股。这对拥有该铁路的范德比尔特家族而言，是一个里程碑式的事件。

Commodore Cornelius Vanderbilt had died two years before, at eighty-three, leaving a fortune of about \$100 million. Though he rejected champagne as too expensive in his last days, he probably ranked as America’s richest man. Crude and tobacco chewing, a white-haired, red-cheeked rogue, he chased pretty maids to the end. In his dotage, he fell under the influence of spiritualists and held business talks with the late Jim Fisk, the tough whom Pierpont bested over the Albany and Susquehanna, later killed by a rival suitor to his mistress.
“准将”科尼利厄斯·范德比尔特于两年前去世，享年 83 岁，留下了约 1 亿美元的遗产。尽管他在临终前因嫌香槟太贵而拒绝饮用，但他很可能仍是美国最富有的人。这个粗鲁无礼、嚼着烟草、白发红颊的老无赖，直到生命尽头还在追逐美貌的女仆。晚年时，他受到招魂术士的影响，甚至与已故的吉姆·菲斯克进行商业会谈——这个硬汉曾在奥尔巴尼与萨斯奎汉纳铁路争夺战中被皮尔庞特击败，后来被情妇的另一个追求者杀害。

Commodore Vanderbilt’s death was a pivotal moment in the shift of business from family to public ownership—a transition rich in possibilities for Pierpont Morgan. To keep his railroad empire intact, the Commodore bequeathed to his oldest son, William Henry, 87 percent of New York Central stock. William was a homely, torpid, thick-set man then in his late fifties whom the Commodore had thought a dunce, berated freely, and exiled to a rude farm on Staten Island. William certainly wasn’t groomed to manage the New York Central, which the rough-hewn Commodore ran from a cigar box full of records.
范德比尔特准将的去世，标志着商业从家族掌控转向公众所有制的一个关键转折点——这一转变为皮尔庞特·摩根带来了无限可能。为了保全自己的铁路帝国，准将把纽约中央铁路公司 87%的股票留给了长子威廉·亨利。威廉当时年近六旬，其貌不扬，反应迟钝，身材敦实，一直被准将视为蠢材，动辄厉声斥责，还被发配到斯塔滕岛一处粗陋的农场。威廉显然从未被培养成管理纽约中央铁路的料——这家公司一直被粗犷的准将用一个装满记录文件的雪茄盒来运营。

The Commodore had merged eleven small railroads to form the forty-five-hundred-mile New York Central. It branched north from New York City to Albany and then swept west to the Great Lakes, opening the interior to eastern ports. That such power would pass to William Vanderbilt appalled many people. As William Gladstone wrote the Vanderbilt’s lawyer, Chauncey M. Depew, “I understand you have a man in your country who is worth \$100,000,000, and it is all in property which he can convert at will into cash. The government ought to take it away from him, as it is too dangerous a power for any one man to have.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199270) William didn’t help to reassure the public, and talked his way into the history books with his retort: “The public be damned; I am working for my stockholders.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199433) The scope of Vanderbilt wealth spread fear and led to new calls for public accountability.
范德比尔特准将将十一条小铁路合并，建成了长达四千五百英里的纽约中央铁路。它从纽约市向北分支至奥尔巴尼，然后向西横扫至五大湖区，将内陆与东部港口连接起来。如此庞大的权力将移交给威廉·范德比尔特，令许多人感到震惊。正如威廉·格莱斯顿写给范德比尔特律师昌西·M·迪普的信中所言：“我了解到贵国有一个人拥有价值一亿美元的财富，且全部是能随时变现的资产。政府应该从他手中没收这些财产，因为任何一个人掌握如此庞大的权力都太过危险。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199270) 威廉的言行并未安抚公众，反而以其反驳之语载入史册：“公众该死，我是在为我的股东们工作。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199433) 范德比尔特家族的财富规模引发了恐惧，并催生了要求企业承担公共责任的新呼声。

What finally induced William Henry to reduce his New York Central stake was publicity generated by New York State Assembly hearings in 1879, chaired by A. Barton Hepburn. This investigating committee exposed secret deals made by the New York Central, which gave preferential rates to oil refiners. As the railroad’s chief executive and star witness, William Henry seemed ignorant or evasive about the clandestine maneuvering; to counter bad publicity, he approached Morgan, probably steered to him by Chauncey Depew. New York State was beginning to levy punitive taxes against the New York Central, and it was hoped that by having William Henry sell a huge chunk of stock, thus making him a minority shareowner, the state legislature might relent.
最终促使威廉·亨利减持纽约中央铁路股份的，是 1879 年由 A.巴顿·赫普本主持的纽约州议会听证会所引发的舆论。该调查委员会曝光了纽约中央铁路与炼油企业暗中交易、提供优惠费率的秘密协议。作为铁路公司的首席执行官和关键证人，威廉·亨利对这些暗箱操作要么佯装不知，要么闪烁其词。为扭转不利舆论，他找到了摩根——很可能是经昌西·迪皮尤引荐。当时纽约州已开始对纽约中央铁路征收惩罚性税款，而让威廉·亨利抛售大量股份、使其沦为小股东的举措，或许能让州议会手下留情。

That Vanderbilt chose the forty-two-year-old Pierpont to carry out this delicate operation probably stemmed from the House of Morgan’s Anglo-American structure. The principal concern was how to liquidate up to 250,000 shares without collapsing the stock’s price. The Morgan-led syndicate demanded that the Vanderbilts refrain from further sales for a year or until all syndicate shares were placed. Another technique to mask the high-volume sale was to sell shares abroad, and J. S. Morgan and Company took an initial 50,000-share block. Junius could act with a discretion impossible on Wall Street. But it was no easy sales job: British investors were still getting mauled by American railroads, and dozens more foundered that year. The world economy was still depressed, with a deep slump in foreign lending. And in the largely unregulated Baronial Age, stock prospectuses were comically skimpy. The New York Central prospectus, for instance, was grandly evasive: “The credit and status of the company are so well known, that it is scarcely necessary to make any public statement.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199567) With so little information about a company, the reputation of the sponsoring bank was critically important.
范德比尔特选择让四十二岁的皮尔庞特来执行这项敏感操作，很可能是因为摩根财团的英美双重架构。核心问题是如何在不引发股价暴跌的情况下清算多达 25 万股股票。摩根领导的辛迪加要求范德比尔特家族在一年内或直到所有辛迪加股票配售完毕前不得再进行抛售。另一种掩盖大规模抛售的手段是将股票销往海外，J.S.摩根公司率先接手了 5 万股。朱尼厄斯能施展华尔街所不具备的谨慎操作。但这并非易事：英国投资者当时仍被美国铁路股折腾得焦头烂额，当年又有十几家铁路公司倒闭。全球经济依然低迷，对外贷款更是深陷低谷。而在监管缺失的"强盗贵族时代"，股票招股说明书简略得令人发笑。 例如，纽约中央铁路公司的招股说明书就含糊其辞地写道：“公司的信誉和地位早已众所周知，实在无需多做公开声明。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199567) 在关于公司的信息如此匮乏的情况下，承销银行的声誉就成了决定性因素。

The New York Central deal had an unstated agenda. The syndicate allotted 20,000 shares to Jay Gould, 15,000 to Russell Sage, and 10,000 to Cyrus Field. The inclusion of the odious Gould was part of a truce between Vanderbilt’s New York Central and Gould’s Wabash, which had been feuding. At first, Vanderbilt wasn’t thrilled about this, but Gould effectively blackmailed his way into the syndicate by threatening to deprive the New York Central of Wabash traffic. Gould also felt this association with the Morgans might cloak him in a new respectability and perhaps entitle him to better credit in the future.
这笔纽约中央铁路的交易背后另有隐情。承销团分配给杰伊·古尔德两万股，拉塞尔·塞奇一万五千股，赛勒斯·菲尔德一万股。将声名狼藉的古尔德纳入其中，实则是范德比尔特旗下的纽约中央铁路与古尔德旗下的瓦巴什铁路之间长期交恶后达成休战协议的一部分。起初，范德比尔特对此并不情愿，但古尔德以切断瓦巴什铁路对纽约中央铁路的货运作为要挟，硬生生挤进了承销团。此外，古尔德也认为，与摩根家族扯上关系，或许能给他披上一层体面的外衣，甚至为他未来争取更优厚的信贷条件铺路。

When Pierpont announced that he had mysteriously sold the huge block of New York Central shares, much of it abroad, the financial world gaped with wonder. The commission was a colossal \$3 million. As he had during the feud over the Albany and Susquehanna, Pierpont demanded a seat on the railroad’s board of directors. As Junius told a partner, Pierpont was “to represent the London interest”—that is, he would vote their proxies.[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199757) Having long chafed at American railroad brigands—even organizing a \$300,000 defense committee to protect their stake in Gould’s Scarlet Woman—European investors now exacted their revenge. They were tired of railroad shenanigans—bankruptcy, skipped dividends, poor management. So Pierpont Morgan would be their blunt instrument with which to bludgeon American railroads into responsible behavior. He had just the right clubman’s pedigree to inspire their trust. Once he chastised a railroad president by exclaiming, “Your roads! Your roads belong to my clients!”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199912) Because railroads required constant capital and exhausted the resources of lone entrepreneurs, they were ripe for such banker domination.
当皮尔庞特宣布他已神秘地出售了那大笔纽约中央铁路的股票，其中大部分流向海外时，整个金融界都惊愕不已。这笔交易的佣金高达 300 万美元。正如他在奥尔巴尼-萨斯奎汉纳铁路之争中所做的那样，皮尔庞特要求在该铁路公司董事会中占有一席之地。正如朱尼厄斯对一位合伙人所说，皮尔庞特是“代表伦敦方面的利益”——也就是说，他将行使他们的代理投票权。欧洲投资者长期以来一直受够美国铁路的劫掠行径——甚至曾组织了一个 30 万美元的防御委员会来保护他们在古尔德“红颜祸水”铁路中的利益——如今他们终于要报复了。他们厌倦了铁路的把戏：破产、跳过股息、管理不善。因此，皮尔庞特·摩根将成为他们用来敲打美国铁路、使其行为负起责任的钝器。他拥有恰到好处的俱乐部会员背景，足以赢得他们的信任。他曾斥责一位铁路公司总裁，大声说道：“你的铁路！你的铁路属于我的客户！”” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2199912) 因为铁路需要持续的资金投入，且耗尽了个体企业家的资源，他们早就该被银行家牵着鼻子走了。

As intended, the sale of William Vanderbilt’s stock dispersed ownership and New York State slackened its assault against the road. But what the legislators didn’t reckon on was that Pierpont would take those scattered shares and effectively recreate their combined power in himself. He began placing his golden manacles on the road. Besides voting all the London proxies, he insisted that the New York Central maintain its \$8 dividend for five years, with the House of Morgan acting as fiscal agent to disburse those dividends in New York and London. Before long, the New York Central would be a Morgan road and the company whose shares were recommended most frequently by the Morgan family.
果然如所料，威廉·范德比尔特的股票出售分散了股权，纽约州也放缓了对这条铁路的攻击。但立法者们没料到的是，皮尔庞特会把这些散落的股份重新收拢，将其力量集中到自己手中。他开始给这条铁路套上金色的镣铐。除了代理所有伦敦股东的投票权，他还坚持要求纽约中央铁路在五年内维持每股 8 美元的分红，并由摩根银行担任财务代理，在纽约和伦敦支付这些股息。没过多久，纽约中央铁路便成了摩根家族的铁路，也成为摩根家族推荐频率最高的股票。

In standing up foursquare for British creditors, Pierpont took the risky step of identifying himself with a foreign power, creating confusion in the popular mind as to his political loyalties. From this time on, he would often be criticized as a mere appendage of London bankers, “a sort of colonial administrator; a representative in America of the financial might of Britain.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2200067) This ambiguity regarding the bank’s Anglo-American character would not only foster considerable paranoia in the American heartland but would also create an identity crisis within the Morgan empire itself.
皮尔庞特坚定地站在英国债权人一边，这一冒险举动使他与外国势力联系在一起，在公众心中对他的政治忠诚产生了困惑。从此以后，他常被批评为仅仅是伦敦银行家的附属品，“一种殖民管理者；英国金融力量在美国的代表。”这种关于银行英美双重身份的模糊性，不仅在美国腹地激起了相当大的偏执，也导致了摩根帝国自身的身份危机。

In the meantime, while Wall Street buzzed over the New York Central affair, Pierpont seemed to derive little joy from it. Far from puffing up with pride, he sounded frazzled and dispirited. Yet again he contemplated giving up business. An 1880 letter to his cousin Jim Goodwin shows how explicitly he began to view himself as an instrument of larger purpose, the representative of masses of investors. He wrote in part,
与此同时，当华尔街为纽约中央铁路的交易喧嚣不已时，皮尔庞特却似乎并未从中感到多少喜悦。他非但没有得意洋洋，反而显得疲惫不堪、心灰意冷。又一次，他开始考虑放弃生意。1880 年他写给表兄吉姆·古德温的信中，明确透露出他逐渐将自己视为更大目标的工具、广大投资者的代表。他写道：

> I am pressed beyond measure. I never have had such a winter—and although my health has been better than I have had for many winters, still, so far as time is concerned, I have had no leisure whatever. If it were simply my own affairs that were concerned, I would very soon settle the question, and give it up; but with the large interests of others on my shoulders, it cannot be done—and I do not suppose there is any reason why it should, except that I often think it would be very desirable if I could have more time for outside matters.[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2200233)
> 我已被压力逼得透不过气来。我从未经历过这样的冬天——尽管健康状况比以往许多个冬天都要好，但从时间的角度来看，我几乎没有片刻闲暇。如果只关乎我自己的事，我很快就能做出决定，然后放手不管；但肩上扛着别人的巨额利益，这事便做不成了——我想也没什么理由非得这么做，只是我常觉得，若能多些时间处理外部事务，那该多好。 [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2200233)

Several commentators have noted Pierpont’s “savior complex,” as seen in his private life by his marriage to the tubercular Mimi and in his business life by his crusades for the “London interests.” In his own mind, he often acted to benefit others, not simply for self-aggrandizement. This pronounced sense of martyrdom made him extremely sensitive to criticism and also shielded him from true self-knowledge. In more extreme moments, it could invite megalomania. It was too easy to camouflage selfish impulses by invoking a higher cause as the real cause. At the same time, he wasn’t motivated by purely selfish motives and had larger concerns than most bankers of his day. In future years, Morgan partisans would praise the bank’s high ethical standards and reputation for fairness, while critics would see the self-congratulatory rhetoric as sanctimonious and hypocritical.
