# Chapter Thirty: Sheiks

FOR Morgan Guaranty, too, the recession of 1973–74 disclosed a turbulent new world. The Arab oil embargo and consequent jump in world oil prices produced inflation and skidding financial markets. With an end to fixed exchange rates in the early 1970s, foreign-exchange trading became a wild poker game. In November 1973, Morgan president Walter Hines Page warned friends at Franklin National Bank against excessive foreign-exchange gambling and quietly alerted the New York Fed to the problem. In May 1974, Franklin’s foreign-exchange losses led to the first major bank run since the Depression and the biggest bank failure in U.S. history. When Bankhaus Herstatt, West Germany’s biggest private bank, mysteriously failed in June, it saddled Morgan Guaranty with a \$13-million loss. That fall, *Fortune* warned, “The nation’s financial system is facing its gravest crisis since the Bank Holiday of 1933. The crisis is one of confidence. The public has become increasingly worried about the solvency of even the most profitable banks.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418661)
对摩根保证信托银行而言，1973–74 年的衰退同样揭示了一个动荡的新世界。阿拉伯石油禁运以及随之而来的世界油价飙升，引发了通货膨胀，金融市场也随之下滑。随着 1970 年代初固定汇率的终结，外汇交易变成了一场疯狂的扑克游戏。1973 年 11 月，摩根总裁沃尔特·海因斯·佩奇警告富兰克林国民银行的朋友们不要过度进行外汇赌博，并悄悄向纽约联储通报了问题。1974 年 5 月，富兰克林银行的外汇亏损引发了自大萧条以来首次重大银行挤兑，也成为美国史上最大的银行倒闭案。同年 6 月，西德最大的私人银行赫斯塔特银行神秘倒闭，令摩根保证信托银行蒙受 1300 万美元损失。那年秋天，《财富》杂志警告说：“美国的金融体系正面临自 1933 年银行停业以来最严重的危机。这是一场信任危机。” 公众日益担忧，即便是利润最丰厚的银行，其偿付能力也岌岌可危。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418661)

With this thick pall hanging over the banking world, the banks were suddenly tempted by Arab petrodollars. If the Arabs caused the financial crisis, they also presented an apparent cure. For Morgan Guaranty, which had struggled to retain balances, the shower of petrodollars had a surreal beauty, like a rainbow in a storm. “We were so worried about dollars,” said Walter Page. “Then here came the Saudis with more dollars than we knew how to keep. You almost had to become Saudi Arabians—quickly.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418808) The petrodollars flowed mostly into four U.S. banks—Morgan Guaranty, Chase, Citibank, and the Bank of America. Thoroughgoing snobs, the Arabs preferred conservative blue-ribbon banks and prized Morgan’s old-money aura, its discreet style, and its resolutely Christian past (the bank had no high-ranking Jewish officer until the 1980s).
在这层厚重的阴云笼罩下，银行业突然被阿拉伯石油美元所诱惑。如果说阿拉伯人引发了这场金融危机，那他们也似乎提供了显而易见的解药。对于一度为留住存款而苦苦挣扎的摩根担保银行(Morgan Guaranty)而言，石油美元的倾泻而下呈现出一种超现实的美感，宛如风暴中的一道彩虹。“我们当时那么担心美元，”沃尔特·佩奇(Walter Page)说道，“结果沙特人带来的美元多得我们都不知道怎么存放。你几乎得立刻变成沙特阿拉伯人才行。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418808) 这些石油美元主要流入了四家美国银行——摩根担保银行(Morgan Guaranty)、大通银行(Chase)、花旗银行(Citibank)和美国银行(Bank of America)。阿拉伯人是一群彻头彻尾的势利眼，他们偏爱保守的蓝筹银行，尤其看重摩根银行的老钱光环、其低调的行事风格，以及其坚定不移的基督教传统——直到 20 世纪 80 年代，该银行都没有一位犹太裔高层管理人员。

As bankers swarmed across the Middle East groveling before Saudi sheiks, Morgan Guaranty enjoyed access no carpetbagger could duplicate. The secretive Morgan-Saudi relationship dated back to Ibn Saud’s forging of the Saudi kingdom in the early 1930s, when money-changing shops with mud floors served as makeshift banks. In April 1933, Standard Oil of California (Socal) negotiated the first oil concession with the Saudi finance minister, Abdullah Sulaiman. They agreed to an initial £30,000 gold loan, plus the first year’s rent of £5,000 in gold. Making payment posed a riddle, for the antediluvian Saudi monetary system employed only chunky metal coins; the kingdom wouldn’t adopt paper money for another twenty years. So gold—massive heaps of it—was shipped in to make the payments.
当银行家们蜂拥至中东，在沙特酋长面前卑躬屈膝时，摩根担保公司却享有任何外来者都无法企及的门路。神秘的摩根-沙特关系可追溯至 20 世纪 30 年代初伊本·沙特缔造沙特王国之时，彼时泥地钱庄还充当着临时银行。1933 年 4 月，加州标准石油公司（Socal）与沙特财政大臣阿卜杜拉·苏莱曼敲定了首份石油特许协议。双方商定先期提供 3 万英镑黄金贷款，外加首年 5000 英镑黄金租金。如何支付却成了难题——沙特陈旧的货币体系只认沉甸甸的金属硬币，这个王国要再过二十年才会使用纸币。于是成堆的黄金被船运至此完成交易。

The deal was nearly scuttled when FDR, heeding the advice of Walter Lippmann and Russell Leffingwell, embargoed U.S. gold exports. As an American company, Socal required U.S. Treasury permission to ship gold to Saudi Arabia. As it awaited the official go-ahead, its Saudi Arabian future seemed to ride on that one gold shipment. On July 26, 1933, Dean Acheson, then Treasury under secretary, turned down Socal’s request. So the panicky oil company bought thirty-five thousand gold sovereigns from a Guaranty Trust branch in London, flouting the new regulations.
这笔交易差点告吹，因为富兰克林·罗斯福听取了沃尔特·李普曼和拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔的建议，对美国的黄金出口实施了禁运。作为一家美国公司，加州标准石油公司（Socal）要将黄金运往沙特阿拉伯，必须获得美国财政部的许可。在等待官方批准的过程中，该公司在沙特阿拉伯的前途似乎就系于这一批黄金的运输。1933 年 7 月 26 日，时任财政部副部长的迪安·艾奇逊拒绝了 Socal 的请求。于是，这家惊慌失措的石油公司公然无视新规，从伦敦的一家担保信托银行分行购入了三万五千枚金币。

In early August 1933, this black-market gold sailed for the Persian Gulf aboard a P&O liner. When it arrived, a Socal representative counted out thirty-five thousand coins under Sulaiman’s vigilant gaze. When the Saudis asked what they should do with the money, Socal recommended Guaranty Trust. For years, American oilmen shipped millions of British gold sovereigns to the Saudis by boat or plane. By the 1940s, Socal had taken Texaco, Standard Oil of New Jersey, and Mobil into its desert oil kingdom in a new operation christened the Arabian-American Oil Company, or Aramco. Aramco erected structures ranging from hospitals to camel troughs, exerting an influence in the kingdom rivaling that of the Saudi royal family itself. By importing a great deal of material, Aramco had a constant need for letters of credit and other old-fashioned banking services. And its stalwart banker was Guaranty Trust.
1933 年 8 月初，这些黑市黄金被装上 P&O 公司的邮轮，驶向波斯湾。抵达后，加州标准石油公司的代表在苏莱曼的严密监视下清点了三万五千枚金币。当沙特人询问该如何处理这笔钱时，加州标准石油公司推荐了担保信托银行。多年间，美国石油商通过船运或空运，将数百万枚英国金镑源源不断地送到沙特人手中。到了 20 世纪 40 年代，加州标准石油公司将德士古、新泽西标准石油和美孚石油引入这片沙漠石油王国，组建了一家名为阿拉伯-美国石油公司（简称 Aramco）的新企业。Aramco 建造了从医院到骆驼饮水槽等各类设施，在沙特国内的影响力甚至可与沙特王室媲美。由于大量进口物资，Aramco 对信用证及其他传统银行业务有着持续的需求，而其坚定的合作伙伴正是担保信托银行。

Guaranty’s Harold Anderson was probably the only American banker who traveled regularly to Saudi Arabia after World War II, although the Dutch and French were well entrenched there. (The Netherlands Trading Society serviced Indonesian pilgrims to Mecca.) As long-time camel traders, the Arabs valued personal relationships, and the genial, easygoing Anderson brought them colorful gifts, like studded saddles, to win their friendship. Guaranty made loans to Saudi Arabia against oil revenues (possibly including small personal loans to King Saud) and also managed dollar accounts for leading Saudis. As Aramco’s banker, Guaranty also managed Saudi oil revenues in dollars.
担保银行的哈罗德·安德森大概是二战后唯一定期造访沙特阿拉伯的美国银行家，尽管荷兰和法国早已在那里站稳了脚跟。（荷兰贸易银行为前往麦加的印尼朝圣者提供服务。）作为世代经营骆驼贸易的商人，阿拉伯人十分看重人际关系，而性情温和、随遇而安的安德森则为他们带来色彩缤纷的礼物，比如镶满饰钉的马鞍，以此赢得他们的友谊。担保银行以石油收入为抵押向沙特提供贷款（可能还包括给沙特国王的小额个人贷款），同时还为沙特显要人物管理美元账户。作为阿美石油公司的合作银行，担保银行也负责管理沙特以美元结算的石油收入。

Although J. P. Morgan and Company had no dealings with Saudi Arabia in the 1950s, it was banker to that other ubiquitous giant in the Arabian Peninsula, Bechtel, the shadowy global construction giant that did the actual building for Aramco. Bechtel formed a close partnership with Saudi entrepreneur Suliman Olayan, a once penniless Aramco dispatcher who ended up with over \$1 billion and a 50-percent stake in Saudi Arabian Bechtel Company. As a member of Morgan Guaranty’s International Council, Olayan would form part of a dense, impenetrable web whose strands bound Morgan Guaranty, Bechtel, the Saudi royal family, and American oil companies.
尽管 J.P.摩根公司在 20 世纪 50 年代与沙特阿拉伯并无业务往来，但它却是阿拉伯半岛另一家无处不在的巨头——贝克特尔（Bechtel）的银行。这家神秘的全球建筑巨头实际上是阿美石油公司的实际建设者。贝克特尔与沙特企业家苏里曼·奥拉扬建立了紧密的合作伙伴关系。奥拉扬曾是一名身无分文的阿美公司调度员，最终积累了超过 10 亿美元财富，并持有沙特贝克特尔公司 50%的股份。作为摩根保证国际委员会的成员，奥拉扬将成为一个错综复杂、坚不可摧的关系网中的一环，这张网将摩根保证、贝克特尔、沙特王室和美国石油公司紧密联结在一起。

Like a storybook miser, Finance Minister Suliman was often said to hoard the nation’s wealth—silver riyals and gold sovereigns—in a chest tucked under his bed. It was one of the world’s few portable central banks. After 1950, as the Saudis split royalties with Aramco on the more equitable fifty-fifty basis, the coins came to fill a vault seventy feet long, seventy feet wide, and eight feet high. The old medieval finances would no longer suffice. Yet any attempt to modernize the monetary system ran up against the Islamic injunction against paying or receiving interest.
像童话里的守财奴一样，财政部长苏莱曼常常被说成是把国家财富——银里亚尔和金币——藏在自己床下的箱子里，堪称全球为数不多的“便携式央行”。1950 年后，随着沙特与阿美公司按更公平的五五开分享利润，这些硬币装满了一个长 70 英尺、宽 70 英尺、高 8 英尺的金库。古老的中世纪理财方式再也行不通了。然而，任何货币体系现代化的尝试，都会撞上伊斯兰教严禁收付利息的教规。

In 1952, when the Saudis created a central bank, they shrank from so labeling it, in order to avoid inflaming the faithful. Instead, they cunningly anointed it the Saudi Arabian Monetary Agency or SAMA, which started out with about \$15 million. It issued Saudi gold coins and the kingdom’s first paper money for use by pilgrims to Mecca. This gradually replaced the weighty coin of the realm. But many desert warriors and kingdom retainers preferred solid, precious metals, and King Faisal himself would keep bags of silver in the basement of the Netherlands Trading Society.
1952 年，沙特人建立中央银行时，因担心刺激虔诚的信众，不敢直呼其名。他们狡猾地将其命名为"沙特阿拉伯货币局"（SAMA），初始资本约 1500 万美元。该机构发行沙特金币以及王国首批供麦加朝圣者使用的纸币，逐步取代了王国沉重的金属货币。但许多沙漠勇士和王室旧臣更偏爱实在的贵金属，费萨尔国王本人就曾将成袋的银币藏在荷兰贸易会社的地下室里。

Morgan Guaranty helped reform Saudi finance through a remarkable man named Anwar Ali, a Pakistani who first went to Saudi Arabia on a two-week tour of duty as head of the IMF’s Middle East department. In 1958, the Saudis drafted him to be SAMA’s governor. His mission was to straighten out the kingdom’s finances, then in critical disarray as a result of corruption, inflation, and extravagant spending. (The Saudi royal palace had the world’s second largest air-conditioning systern after that of the Pentagon.) Ali was gentle and scholarly, a devout Muslim in silver-rimmed glasses and urbane Western suits. He became personal financial adviser to King Faisal. As SAMA governor, he commanded more petrodollars than anyone on earth, more gold than Midas. As journalist Tad Szulc wrote in 1974, “Not many kings and presidents held such personal power.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418966) With nice semantic juggling, he turned *interest* into *return on investment*, permitting SAMA to amass a modern securities portfolio without offending Allah. “One of the first things Anwar told me about the messy finances he faced was that he had discovered to his dismay that many Saudi accounts in New York were not drawing any interest,” recalled William D. Toomey, then with the U.S. embassy in Saudi Arabia. “He found it touching that the banks were so sensitive to the Saudis’ religious scruples against acceptance of interest.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419117)
摩根大通通过一位杰出人物——安瓦尔·阿里，帮助改革了沙特的金融体系。阿里是巴基斯坦人，最初作为国际货币基金组织中东部门负责人赴沙特，原本只打算工作两周。1958 年，沙特人把他请来担任沙特阿拉伯货币局（SAMA）行长。他的使命是整顿当时因腐败、通胀和挥霍无度而乱成一锅粥的王室财政——当时沙特王宫拥有仅次于五角大楼的全球第二大空调系统。阿里为人儒雅博学，戴着银丝眼镜，穿着西式套装，是一位虔诚的穆斯林。他后来成为费萨尔国王的私人财务顾问。作为 SAMA 行长，他掌握着比世界上任何人都多的石油美元，比迈达斯还多的黄金。正如记者塔德·舒尔茨在 1974 年所写：“没有多少国王或总统能拥有如此大的个人权力。”” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2418966) 他玩了一手漂亮的语义转换，把利息变成了投资回报，让 SAMA 在不冒犯安拉的前提下，积累起一套现代证券组合。“安瓦尔最早跟我谈起他面临的那堆混乱财务问题时，就提到他沮丧地发现，沙特在纽约的很多账户根本没产生任何利息，”当时在美国驻沙特大使馆工作的威廉·D·图米回忆道。“他觉得银行能如此顾及沙特人对收取利息的宗教禁忌，实在令人感动。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419117)

To map portfolio strategy, Ali recruited a tiny group of Western bankers, sometimes called the White Fathers or the Three Wise Men in this myth-shrouded operation. Among them was the tall, beetle-browed John M. Meyer, Jr., then head of Morgan’s International Division and later chairman. Ali favored such conservative investments as Treasury securities, and Meyer was just the old-school type to appeal to him. (Inside the bank, he was nicknamed Moody Meyer because he remembered in excruciating detail *Moody’s* entry on every security, down to the smallest indenture.) Meyer was also secretive, inscrutable, and trusted for his plain truthfulness. He, in turn, admired Ali’s incorruptibility in a land rife with corruption. (Harold Anderson’s assistant, John Bochow, would tell colleagues sarcastically, “Never do business in a country where they don’t wear overcoats part of the year.”) Diverting SAMA deposits to Morgan Guaranty, Ali made it the major Saudi depository. The bank, in turn, hired Ali’s son Pasha, a Yale graduate.
为规划投资组合策略，阿里招募了一小群西方银行家，在这项充满神秘色彩的行动中，他们有时被称为"白袍神父"或"三智者"。其中就有高大、浓眉的小约翰·M·迈耶，他时任摩根国际部主管，后来成为董事长。阿里偏爱国债这类保守投资，而迈耶正是那种能吸引他的老派作风。（在银行内部，他被称为"喜怒无常的迈耶"，因为他能精确记住穆迪对每只证券的详细评级，连最小的契约条款都不放过。）迈耶还行事隐秘、深不可测，因其坦诚直言而备受信赖。而他也钦佩阿里在腐败横行的国家中保持着廉洁本色。（哈罗德·安德森的助手约翰·博乔曾讽刺地对同事说："千万别在那些一年里有一半时间不穿大衣的国家做生意。"）阿里将沙特货币署的存款转入摩根担保信托，使其成为沙特的主要存管银行。 随后，该银行聘请了阿里的儿子帕夏——一位耶鲁大学的毕业生。

For several years, Morgan Guaranty provided SAMA with investment counseling. In the 1960s, however, the American bank became a casualty of its own success. As government adviser, it couldn’t solicit Saudi business without encountering a conflict of interest. Morgans needed to open some breathing space between itself and the Saudis. “You couldn’t have advisers in a government agency and deal with it at the same time,” explained an ex-Morgan executive. So Morgans bowed out and brought in White, Weld of New York and Baring Brothers and Richard Fleming of London.
在接下来的几年里，摩根保证银行为沙特阿拉伯货币局（SAMA）提供投资咨询。然而，到了 20 世纪 60 年代，这家美国银行却因其自身的成功而陷入困境。作为政府顾问，它无法在不产生利益冲突的情况下向沙特招揽业务。摩根需要在与沙特人之间拉开一些距离。“你不能同时担任政府机构的顾问，又与其进行业务往来，”一位前摩根高管解释道。于是，摩根选择退出，并引入了纽约的怀特韦尔德公司、伦敦的巴林兄弟银行以及理查德·弗莱明公司。

When the petrodollar gusher erupted, Morgan Guaranty was beautifully positioned. It could pose as protector of the defenseless Saudis against rapacious, self-serving bankers. Recognizing a need for new Saudi financial expertise, Ali toyed with the idea of an international merchant bank. In 1973, SAMA still operated out of a ramshackle building near the Riyadh airport and lacked telex machines. It was moving tens of billions of dollars in deposits around the world with a slim staff of only ten professionals.
当石油美元的洪流喷涌而出时，Morgan Guaranty 正好占据了绝佳的位置。它可以摆出姿态，扮演起无助的沙特人保护者角色，对抗那些贪婪又自私的银行家。察觉到沙特对新金融专业知识的需求，Ali 萌生了创办一家国际商人银行的念头。1973 年，SAMA 仍在利雅得机场附近一栋破旧的楼里办公，连电传机都没有。当时它靠着一支仅十名专业人士的精干团队，在全球范围内调动着数百亿美元的存款。

At a 1973 IMF meeting in Nairobi, Meyer, Walter Page, and Lew Preston cornered Ali with a plan for a London-based Saudi merchant bank to be the kingdom’s Euromarket outlet. Page recalled, “We told him, ‘You have to have a window on the world of finance. You have to invest in the right way and keep current with what’s going on in the world.’ ”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419289) The Eurodollar market was then moving into high gear in London, and the timing seemed auspicious.
在 1973 年于内罗毕举行的 IMF 会议上，Meyer、Walter Page 和 Lew Preston 围住 Ali，提出了一个在伦敦设立沙特商人银行的计划，作为沙特进入欧洲货币市场的窗口。Page 回忆道：“我们告诉他，‘你必须有一个面向金融世界的窗口。你得用正确的方式投资，并且紧跟全球动态。’” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419289) 那时欧洲美元市场在伦敦正高速运转，时机看起来恰到好处。

At first, the Saudis wanted to share this largesse in a consortium arrangement with their five principal bankers in Europe and Japan. Instead, Morgans deprecated the voguish consortium concept. “We told the Saudis that they had to tie someone with a bigger share to make it work,” said Page.[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419447) And who, pray, would that responsible party be? When the Saudi International Bank was announced in 1975, SAMA owned 50 percent and Morgan Guaranty 20 percent, with 5-percent shares distributed to other banks. Edgar Felton of Morgans was dispatched to London to manage the new bank. This seemed an inimitable coup for Morgan Guaranty—a partnership with the Saudi central bank.
起初，沙特人想与他们在欧洲和日本的五家主要银行组成财团来分享这笔慷慨的资金。但摩根却对这种时髦的财团概念不以为然。“我们告诉沙特人，他们必须找一个持有更大份额的伙伴才能成事，”佩奇说道。 [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419447) 那么，请问这个“负责任”的伙伴会是谁呢？1975 年沙特国际银行宣告成立时，沙特阿拉伯货币署持有 50%股份，摩根保证信托占 20%，其余 5%的股份分配给其他银行。摩根派的埃德加·费尔顿被派往伦敦管理这家新银行。这似乎是摩根保证信托的一次无与伦比的胜利——与沙特中央银行结成了合作伙伴关系。

News of the SIB deal, secretly crafted by Morgan Guaranty, dealt the *coup de grace* to the special relationship with Morgan Stanley. These were the waning days of the Paris partnership, and Morgan Stanley, learning the news only shortly before its public announcement, was stunned. A SAMA board member, Ali Alireza, told his nephew Hisham, then at Morgan Stanley, about the deal. The firm couldn’t believe its total ignorance of the negotiations. According to a former Morgan Stanley partner, “It was a big disappointment to Morgan Stanley and Morgan Grenfell. The lure of the vast Saudi Arabian fortune and all the money to be made was too much for Morgan Guaranty. There wasn’t an alliance any longer. Morgan Guaranty had clearly made its mind up to go its own way at the Bermuda meeting.” A former Morgan Guaranty official concurs: “When the petrodollars came along, we no longer needed Morgan Stanley.”
摩根担保秘密策划的 SIB 交易，给了与摩根士丹利的特殊关系致命一击。彼时巴黎合伙关系已日暮西山，摩根士丹利仅在公开宣布前片刻才得知消息，震惊不已。沙特货币局（SAMA）董事会成员阿里·阿利雷扎将此事告知了当时任职摩根士丹利的侄子希沙姆。公司难以置信自己对谈判竟毫不知情。据一位前摩根士丹利合伙人回忆：“这对摩根士丹利和摩根建富而言是重大打击。沙特巨额的财富和唾手可得的利益对摩根担保的诱惑实在太大。联盟已不复存在。在百慕大会议上，摩根担保显然已下定决心要单飞。”一位前摩根担保高管也认同：“石油美元出现后，我们就不再需要摩根士丹利了。”

The Saudi International Bank was a fertile source of fantasies at 23 Wall. Some thought the Saudis might funnel all their export-import financing through the bank; others thought the SIB might have a big account at 23 Wall. The most specific expectation was that the SIB would train Saudi Arabia’s future financial elite, allowing Morgans to seed loyal people throughout the Saudi power hierarchy. The country desperately needed a stratum of competent financiers, and the SIB promised to deliver them. The House of Morgan thus looked to be the one bank that would profit from a stress on Saudiizing Saudi finance.
在华尔街 23 号，沙特国际银行（SIB）曾是幻想的热土。有人猜测沙特人会把所有进出口融资业务都通过这家银行运作；也有人认为 SIB 会在华尔街 23 号开设一个巨额账户。最具体的期待是，SIB 将培养沙特未来的金融精英，让摩根银行能在沙特权力高层中安插忠诚的代理人。沙特亟需一批能干的金融人才，而 SIB 承诺能输送这些人才。如此一来，摩根银行似乎成了唯一能从"沙特化"沙特金融业中获利的银行。

In practice, the SIB never retained the rich young Saudis sent to train there. The explosion of oil prices in late 1973 and early 1974 put too much wealth at the disposal of young Saudis; business opportunities at home beckoned. These Bedouin Arabs were also too attached to culture and family to remain in London for an extended period. “There were never enough Saudis,” said one Morgan person. “They all wanted to make their name in Saudi Arabia or peddle influence. Banking was too boring. We ended up dealing with the technocrats, not the royal family.” Some Morgan people argue that the Saudi royal family never put its full weight and prestige behind the bank. It took small pieces of sovereign loans but never really blossomed. So its main utility to 23 Wall was simply in preserving the SAMA relationship.
在实践中，SIB 从未留住那些被派往伦敦培训的年轻沙特富家子弟。1973 年末至 1974 年初的油价暴涨，让这些年轻人手握巨额财富；国内商机也向他们频频招手。这些贝都因阿拉伯人又太过留恋本土文化和家庭，无法在伦敦久留。“沙特人总是不够用，”一位摩根人士表示，“他们都想回沙特打响名号或兜售影响力。银行业太无聊了。我们最终只能跟技术官僚打交道，而不是王室成员。”一些摩根人士认为，沙特王室从未真正倾注全力和声望来支持这家银行。它只参与了少量主权贷款，但从未真正繁荣。因此，它对华尔街 23 号的主要价值，仅仅在于维系与 SAMA 的关系。

Morgan Guaranty was protective of Saudi Arabia in U.S. politics. In early 1975, Senator Frank Church tried to extract figures on petrodollar deposits. He feared that by threatening to pull out their short-term deposits, the Arabs could blackmail the U.S. government. Morgans and other banks wouldn’t divulge the information. Morgan chairman Ellmore Patterson declared: “Much of the information you request would involve a breach of our obligation to keep confidential the affairs of particular clients.” Morgans was petrified that the Swiss banks would steal away the deposits. Fed chairman Arthur Burns brokered a deal with the banks, releasing aggregate deposit figures for Middle East states. Of \$14.5 billion in deposits by the OPEC states, 78 percent resided in six banks—Morgan Guaranty, Bank of America, Citibank, Chase, Manufacturers Hanover, and Chemical.
摩根担保银行在美国政治中一直力保沙特阿拉伯。1975 年初，参议员弗兰克·丘奇试图获取石油美元存款的具体数据，他担心阿拉伯国家可能通过撤出短期存款来要挟美国政府。摩根及其他银行拒绝透露这些信息。摩根董事长埃利莫尔·帕特森表示：“你要求的大部分信息涉及我们为客户保密义务的违反。”摩根担心瑞士银行会抢走这些存款。美联储主席阿瑟·伯恩斯与银行达成协议，公布了中东国家存款的总体数据。在欧佩克成员国 145 亿美元的存款中，78%存放在六家银行——摩根担保银行、美国银行、花旗银行、大通银行、汉华实业银行和化学银行。

Senator Church proved correct in worrying that petrodollars would enlist the political allegiance of bankers in disturbing ways. The sheiks wanted to use letters of credit as a way of enforcing compliance with the Arab boycott of Israel. Under this arrangement, banks had to certify that goods being exported to the Middle East didn’t originate in Israel or with blacklisted American companies, didn’t bear the Star of David, and wouldn’t travel aboard Israeli planes or ships. In 1976, the American Jewish Congress singled out Morgan Guaranty and Citibank for loyally executing this dirty work and cited their “pivotal role in the implementation of the Arab boycott.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419572) Morgan Guaranty executed 824 letters of credit including the language of the boycott, although they protested and successfully expunged the offensive language in two dozen cases. While some banks welcomed tough anti-boycott legislation, Chemical Bank and Morgans testified against it; it was finally enacted in 1977.
参议员丘奇的担忧被证实是正确的，石油美元以一种令人不安的方式收买了银行家的政治忠诚。阿拉伯酋长们试图利用信用证来强制执行对以色列的抵制。根据这一安排，银行必须证明出口到中东的商品并非源自以色列，也不是来自被列入黑名单的美国公司，不得带有大卫星标志，且不得通过以色列飞机或船只运输。1976 年，美国犹太人大会特别点名摩根担保银行和花旗银行，指责它们忠实地执行这一肮脏任务，并指出它们在“实施阿拉伯抵制中发挥了关键作用”。摩根担保银行处理了 824 份包含抵制条款的信用证，尽管他们提出抗议并成功删除了其中二十多份中的冒犯性措辞。虽然一些银行欢迎严厉的反抵制立法，但化学银行和摩根银行却对此表示反对；该法案最终于 1977 年通过。

As a general rule, the postwar Morgan bank had avoided the sort of political lobbying or proselytizing for foreign governments in which Tom Lamont had specialized. Yet in the case of the Saudis, the bank seemed to hark back to the old days. Along with Bechtel, GM, GE, Ford Motor, and the oil companies, Morgan Guaranty contributed to Georgetown University’s Center for Contemporary Arab Studies. “Violent criticism of Israel and American support for Israel are the single most dominant themes of the center’s extremely active program,” explained one observer.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419725) In 1980, the august Morgans also made an uncharacteristic foray into public television, after the broadcast of a British television movie, *Death of a Princess*. This controversial documentary told the story of a Saudi prince who ordered the execution of his own granddaughter after she balked at an arranged marriage. The woman was shot while the husband she had chosen instead watched; he was then beheaded. The Saudis were outraged, and the State Department tried to mollify them. So Morgans joined with Texas Instruments, the Harris Corporation, and Ford Motor to sponsor a glossy, benign three-part series on Saudi Arabia designed to counteract the movie.[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419885)
一般而言，战后的摩根银行一直避免汤姆·拉蒙特所擅长的政治游说或为外国政府进行宣传。然而，在沙特的问题上，该银行似乎又回到了老路上。摩根保证信托银行与柏克德、通用汽车、通用电气、福特汽车以及各大石油公司一道，为乔治城大学的当代阿拉伯研究中心提供了资助。一位观察者解释道：“对以色列以及对美国支持以色列的猛烈批评，是该中心极为活跃的项目中唯一最突出的主题。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419725) 1980 年，尊贵的摩根家族还一反常态地涉足了公共电视领域，起因是英国电视电影《公主之死》的播出。这部有争议的纪录片讲述了一位沙特王子因孙女拒绝包办婚姻而下令将其处决的故事。这名女子被枪杀，而她所选中的丈夫则在一旁目睹了这一切；随后他也被斩首。 沙特人怒不可遏，美国国务院试图安抚他们。于是，Morgans 与 Texas Instruments、Harris Corporation 和 Ford Motor 联手赞助了一个光鲜、温和的三集系列片，专门介绍沙特阿拉伯，旨在抵消那部电影的影响。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2419885)

UNLIKE Morgan Guaranty, Morgan Stanley had no experience in the Middle East. In its clumsy, often farcical rush to woo Arabs, it ended up in bed with the shady Adnan Khashoggi, then commonly billed as the world’s richest businessman. The son of a court physician to King Saud, Khashoggi brokered billions of dollars in arms deals with Saudi Arabia and skimmed off fees from over three-quarters of all defense contracts. He kept palatial homes in ten cities, had his own DC-8 and a yacht fitted with gold fixtures. In 1974, the Saudi ambassador to the U.N. steered Khashoggi to Morgan Stanley. Allegedly worried about a two-tier Arab world in which Saudi sheiks drove Cadillacs while the masses starved, Khashoggi told the Saudi royal family that they couldn’t live so luxuriously while the Sudan lay poverty stricken. The conservative oil states feared Sudan’s flirtation with socialism. To correct this, Khashoggi wanted to introduce agribusiness into the region. With the blessings of Egyptian president Anwar Sadat, he planned to create a seventeen-thousand-acre dairy farm near the Suez Canal and a millionacre cattle ranch near the Blue Nile in Sudan. Needing the appropriate technology, Khashoggi eyed an American company called Arizona-Colorado Land and Cattle, which had huge property tracts and cattle herds out West. But the company wouldn’t sell until Morgan Stanley came in and negotiated a \$9-million stake for him.
与摩根担保不同，摩根士丹利在中东毫无经验可言。在笨拙且往往荒唐可笑的讨好阿拉伯人的过程中，它最终与声名狼藉的阿德南·哈肖吉（Adnan Khashoggi）搅在了一起——此人当时常被标榜为世界首富。作为沙特国王御医之子，哈肖吉促成了与沙特价值数十亿美元的军火交易，并从超过四分之三的国防合同中抽取佣金。他在十座城市拥有豪宅，配有私人 DC-8 飞机和一艘镶金设备的游艇。1974 年，沙特驻联合国大使将哈肖吉引荐给了摩根士丹利。据称，哈肖吉对阿拉伯世界两极分化——沙特酋长开凯迪拉克而民众挨饿——深感忧虑，他告诉沙特王室，在苏丹深陷贫困之时，他们不能如此挥霍。保守的产油国担心苏丹与社会主义暧昧不清。为了扭转这一局面，哈肖吉打算在该地区引入农业综合企业。 在埃及总统安瓦尔·萨达特的支持下，他计划在苏伊士运河附近建造一座占地一万七千英亩的乳牛场，并在苏丹的青尼罗河畔打造一座百万英亩的肉牛牧场。为了获得合适的技术，卡舒吉盯上了一家名为亚利桑那-科罗拉多土地与畜牧的美国公司，这家公司在西部拥有庞大的土地和牛群。但公司原本不肯卖，直到摩根士丹利介入，为他谈下了 900 万美元的股份。

In pursuing his vision, Khashoggi was often accompanied by Jan Stenbeck, a handsome blond bachelor from one of Sweden’s richest families who was affiliated with Morgan Stanley. Stenbeck seemed to thrive on intrigue and entertained friends with stories about sitting on the tarmac at Khartoum with Sudan’s president while the sand swirled around them. Khashoggi would spout a thousand inventive ideas about irrigation and agricultural development but then quickly lose interest and turn to other matters. Mostly he delighted in playing pranks on Stenbeck. Once arriving late at a Cairo hotel for a rendezvous with the Arab, an exhausted Stenbeck told the desk clerk not to disturb him with phone calls. After midnight, when Khashoggi arrived, he was told of Stenbeck’s instructions, which suggested to him a practical joke. Mimicking an operator’s voice, he dialed the sleeping Stenbeck’s room and said his Morgan Stanley boss would soon come on the line from New York. As Stenbeck fought to stay awake, Khashoggi enjoyed his dinner. Periodically, he would pick up the phone to reiterate that Stenbeck’s boss would soon be on the line and he must hold on. Stenbeck held on in desperation until he finally succumbed to sleep.
在追逐梦想的路上，卡舒吉身边常伴扬·斯滕贝克——一位来自瑞典最富有家族之一、相貌英俊的金发单身汉，供职于摩根士丹利。斯滕贝克似乎天生喜欢冒险，常拿自己与苏丹总统坐等在喀土穆停机坪上、四周黄沙漫天的趣闻来逗乐朋友。卡舒吉总能滔滔不绝地抛出无数关于灌溉与农业开发的奇思妙想，可转眼又兴味索然，转而琢磨起别的事来。他最大的乐子就是捉弄斯滕贝克。有一次，疲惫不堪的斯滕贝克为赴约赶到开罗一家酒店，特意叮嘱前台别用电话吵他。午夜过后，卡舒吉抵达酒店，听闻斯滕贝克的嘱咐后，恶作剧的念头油然而生。他模仿接线员的嗓音，拨通了斯滕贝克的房间，告诉他摩根士丹利的大老板马上要从纽约来电。 斯滕贝克强撑着不让自己睡着，而卡舒吉则悠然自得地享用晚餐。他时不时拿起电话，反复强调斯滕贝克的上司马上就会打来，让他务必等着。斯滕贝克拼命坚持，最终还是抵挡不住睡意，昏昏沉沉睡了过去。

The clever, babbling Khashoggi tempted Morgan Stanley with another mesmerizing vision of riches. He said that his friend Crown Prince Fahd, having lost a reported \$6 million while gambling in Monaco, was in hot water with King Faisal; to improve his image, the prince planned to set up a \$1-billion foundation to perform good works, possibly with Morgan Stanley as its financial adviser. Would Morgan Stanley be interested in pursuing this with Prince Fahd at Deauville, in northwest France? Stenbeck, S. Parker Gilbert, and Bill Sword took a suite of rooms at a Deauville hotel. Khashoggi was a floor below, Fahd a floor above.
巧舌如簧的卡舒吉又抛出一个令人心动的财富愿景来诱惑摩根士丹利。他说，他的朋友法赫德王储在摩纳哥赌博输掉了大约 600 万美元，因此正被费萨尔国王问责；为了挽回形象，王储计划成立一个 10 亿美元的基金会来行善积德，并可能聘请摩根士丹利担任财务顾问。摩根士丹利是否有兴趣在法国西北部的多维尔与法赫德王储接洽此事？斯滕贝克、S.帕克·吉尔伯特和比尔·斯沃德在多维尔一家酒店订了一间套房。卡舒吉住在他们楼下，法赫德则住在楼上。

At 8:30 one evening, Khashoggi ushered the trio into his suite to meet the prince. There were over a dozen chairs set up in a circle, with a stool beside each. The prince entered ceremoniously, sat beside Sword, and expressed a noble wish to do great things for humanity. At one point, when a gorgeous woman in an evening dress walked in, Khashoggi came over and whispered to Sword, “Do you mind if my secretary sits next to Prince Fahd?” Sword, a short, church-going Presbyterian, said no and slid over a seat, wondering at this beautiful secretary. Soon after, a somewhat older, but no less attractive, lady in her forties came in and sat down on Fahd’s other side. “We have here the wife of the editor of *Paris Match*,” Khashoggi whispered to Sword. “She’s doing a feature story on the prince.” Now every few minutes another beautiful young woman entered and sat down on a stool until there was one beside each man. When the room was full, Fahd announced that he had booked a Trouville restaurant for the evening. As the meeting ended, Sword went over and chatted earnestly with the *Paris Match* woman about Henry Luce, Axel Springer, and other publishers; he was surprised by how little she knew about publishing.
晚上 8 点 30 分，卡舒吉领着三人走进套房觐见王子。厅内摆着十几把椅子围成一圈，每把椅子旁都放着一张矮凳。王子郑重其事地步入房间，在“剑”身旁落座，并表达了要为人类福祉建功立业的高尚志向。片刻间，一位身着晚礼服的绝色女子走了进来，卡舒吉凑到“剑”身边轻声问道：“让我的秘书坐在法赫德王子旁边，您不介意吧？”身材矮小、信奉长老会的“剑”应允了，挪开一个位子，心里却对这位美貌秘书暗自生疑。不一会儿，又一位年长些却风韵不减的四十来岁女士走进来，在法赫德另一侧坐下。“这位是《巴黎竞赛画报》主编的夫人，”卡舒吉低声对“剑”说，“她正在为王子撰写专题报道。”此后每隔几分钟，便有一位妙龄女郎款款而入，各自坐上一张矮凳，直到每位男士身旁都有了伴。 房间里挤满了人时，法赫德宣布他已在特鲁维尔订好了一家餐厅，准备晚上一起去。 会议结束后，斯沃德走过去，认真地和那位《巴黎竞赛》的女记者聊起了亨利·卢斯、阿克塞尔·施普林格以及其他出版人；他惊讶地发现，她对出版业的了解少得可怜。

Back at the hotel, with the door shut, Gilbert and Stenbeck burst out laughing. They had caught on sooner than Sword to Khashoggi’s game: he had flown in “models” from Paris for a party. Stenbeck kidded Sword: “The Prince and all the girls were very impressed that you had picked out the lead woman, the ‘editor from *Paris Match*.’ She was the biggest hooker of them all.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420019) What Khashoggi’s biographer concluded of Stenbeck might be an epitaph for Morgan Stanley’s early efforts to drum up Saudi business: “He traveled with \[Khashoggi\] to see heads of state about projects to turn deserts into Gardens of Eden. But when the trips were over and the glitter gone, there was little to show for it.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420180)
回到酒店，关上门后，吉尔伯特和斯滕贝克忍不住大笑起来。他们比斯沃德更早看穿了卡舒吉的把戏：他从巴黎空运了一群“模特”来参加派对。斯滕贝克打趣斯沃德说：“那位‘王子’和所有姑娘们都对你印象深刻，因为你一眼就挑中了那个领头的女人，那个所谓的‘《巴黎竞赛》编辑’。她其实是这群人里最厉害的高级妓女。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420019) 卡舒吉传记作者对斯滕伯克的评价，或许也能成为摩根士丹利早期在中东争取沙特业务的墓志铭：“他跟着\[卡舒吉\]周游列国，会见各国元首，大谈将沙漠改造成伊甸园的项目。可当旅途结束、繁华落尽，却几乎拿不出什么像样的成果。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420180)

FOR Morgan Grenfell, the petrodollar boom was providential, taking up slack as the late 1960s takeover boom wound down. Although it arranged the first Eurosterling issue, in 1972, it lacked the capital to be a top-flight Euromarket competitor and needed a new act in order to survive. It was a pretty dismal time. British exports flagged badly as a near-depression atmosphere overtook the nation’s industry. Amid rising interest rates, the City was rocked by a bust in the property market and a secondary banking crisis in 1973–74. When Lord Poole of Lazards was asked how he survived the debacle, he replied: “Quite simple: I only lent money to people who had been at Eton.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420351)
对摩根建富来说，石油美元的繁荣如同天赐良机，恰好填补了上世纪 60 年代末收购潮退去后的业务空缺。尽管它在 1972 年安排了首笔欧洲英镑发行，但资本实力不足以跻身顶级欧洲货币市场竞争对手之列，亟需另辟蹊径以求生存。那真是一段相当惨淡的时光。英国工业陷入近乎萧条的阴霾，出口形势急剧恶化。在利率不断攀升的背景下，伦敦金融城又遭房地产市场崩盘和 1973-74 年二级银行危机的接连重创。当有人问及拉扎德银行的普尔勋爵是如何挺过这场劫难时，他答道：“很简单：我只借钱给那些上过伊顿公学的人。”

At Morgan Grenfell, the Arabs would temporarily answer the firm’s problems. With their penchant for secrecy and their appreciation of the confidential style of British merchant banks, the Arabs were naturally drawn to the mysterious maze of the City. They loved the cachet of old stately houses. Also, sympathy for the Arab cause was far more prevalent in the Foreign Office than in the State Department. “In the Mideast, Morgan Grenfell could take advantage of the U.S. inability to act,” declared Christopher Whittington, the firm’s deputy chairman. “We could sell them Tornado fighter planes, while the U.S. couldn’t because of Congress.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420526) Morgan Grenfell had another edge: many London merchant banks were tainted by their Jewish ancestry. So 23 Great Winchester Street became the City firm most immersed in Middle East business. At its peak in the seventies, Arab business contributed up to 70 percent of the bank’s revenues.
在摩根建富，阿拉伯人暂时解决了公司的难题。他们生性偏好隐秘，又欣赏英国商业银行的保密风格，自然被伦敦金融城的神秘迷宫所吸引。他们钟爱那些庄严老宅的名望。此外，英国外交部对阿拉伯事业的同情远比美国国务院更为普遍。“在中东，摩根建富可以利用美国无力行动的弱点，”公司副董事长克里斯托弗·惠廷顿宣称，“我们可以向他们出售‘狂风’战斗机，而美国因为国会的原因做不到。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420526) 摩根建富还有另一项优势：许多伦敦商业银行因犹太血统而蒙上阴影。于是，大温彻斯特街 23 号成了金融城里最深入中东业务的机构。在 70 年代的巅峰时期，阿拉伯业务贡献了该行高达 70%的收入。

At first, the man leading the charge was Sir John Stevens, the inveterate traveler and former polyglot Bank of England executive who had advised Iran’s central bank. By then he had planted the Morgan flag in old imperial outposts of Hong Kong, Singapore, Australia, and New Zealand and got a Morgan office going in Moscow. David Bendall, brought in from the Foreign Office, did the same in Latin America.
起初，牵头的是约翰·史蒂文斯爵士——这位资深旅行家、前英格兰银行多语种高管，曾担任伊朗央行顾问。彼时，他已将摩根的旗帜插在香港、新加坡、澳大利亚和新西兰这些昔日帝国的前哨阵地，并在莫斯科设立了摩根办事处。从外交部挖来的大卫·本德尔，则在拉丁美洲如法炮制。

As at previous times in Morgan Grenfell history, stepped-up foreign business forged deeper links with Whitehall. Morgan Grenfell now specialized in arranging government-guaranteed export credits, which Britain was then using to win goodwill abroad. These credits led Morgan Grenfell to finance arms exports to Oman and Jordan as well as power plants, refineries, and other capital projects in the region. Through export credits, the bank also became more involved with Eastern Europe. In 1975, Morgan Grenfell became the first merchant bank to win the Queen’s Award for Export Achievement, for managing over a quarter of the government-guaranteed credits. Through all the vagaries of merger work, the firm’s rock-solid export credits and impressive portfolio management would lend strength to its balance sheet. In the last analysis, the dull, solid stuff would be its salvation.
一如摩根建富历来的传统，其海外业务的扩张加深了与英国政府的联系。摩根建富此时专门安排政府担保的出口信贷，英国借此在海外赢得良好声誉。这些信贷不仅让摩根建富为向阿曼和约旦的军火出口提供融资，还资助了该地区的电厂、炼油厂及其他大型资本项目。通过出口信贷，该银行与东欧的业务也日益紧密。1975 年，摩根建富成为首家荣获女王出口成就奖的商业银行，因其管理的政府担保信贷超过四分之一。尽管并购业务风云变幻，但该行稳固的出口信贷业务及卓越的投资组合管理为其资产负债表提供了坚实支撑。说到底，正是这些踏实稳健的业务，才是其立足之本。

Aside from Saudi Arabia, most Arab states before 1973 were too impoverished to be considered good credit risks. The sudden, almost overnight, revolution in their financial status was revealed in a controversial loan that Sir John Stevens secretly negotiated during the Yom Kippur War, in the fall of 1973. On October 6, Egypt, Syria, and Iraq attacked Israel. On October 20, during a fierce, bloody phase of fighting, news leaked out of a Morgan-led loan to Abu Dhabi. Israeli tanks had just advanced fifteen miles beyond the Suez Canal, knocking out Egypt’s surface-to-air missile batteries. Disclosure of the loan sent up an uproar, especially among Jewish firms in the City. Official British policy was neutral. Then as now, Morgan Grenfell insisted on the loan’s peaceful nature. According to Chris Whittington, “The Abu Dhabi loan was already in the works before the fighting began. We just didn’t cancel it.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420694)
除沙特阿拉伯外，1973 年前的大多数阿拉伯国家都过于贫困，难以被视为可靠的信贷对象。然而，在 1973 年秋赎罪日战争期间，约翰·史蒂文斯爵士秘密商谈的一笔有争议贷款，揭示了这些国家金融地位近乎一夜之间的剧变。10 月 6 日，埃及、叙利亚和伊拉克向以色列发起进攻。10 月 20 日，正值战事激烈、血流成河之际，摩根银行牵头向阿布扎比提供贷款的消息不胫而走。当时以色列坦克已越过苏伊士运河推进 15 英里，摧毁了埃及的地对空导弹阵地。这笔贷款的曝光引发了轩然大波，尤其在伦敦金融城的犹太企业中间。英国官方立场保持中立。而摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）当时和现在一样坚称，这笔贷款纯属和平性质。据克里斯·惠廷顿回忆：“阿布扎比贷款在战事爆发前就已着手推进。我们只是没有取消它。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420694)

It was the bank’s most controversial loan of the postwar era, sparking heated debate. With each new report, this mystery loan seemed to expand. First announced as a £40-million loan (\$100 million), it suspiciously mushroomed to \$200 million within three days. Even as Stevens talked of its use for hospitals or budgetary purposes, it sounded patently suspicious. Awash with oil money, the seventy thousand privileged residents of Abu Dhabi might have enjoyed the world’s highest percapita income. In those years, \$200 million was an enormous Eurodollar loan, representing nearly \$3,000 for each Abu Dhabi resident—absurdly wasteful and unnecessary borrowing for a tiny oil sheikdom under ordinary circumstances.
这是该银行战后最具争议的一笔贷款，引发了激烈争论。随着每一份新报告的出炉，这笔神秘贷款的规模似乎都在膨胀。最初公布为 4000 万英镑（合 1 亿美元），但可疑的是，短短三天内就飙升至 2 亿美元。即便史蒂文斯声称这笔钱将用于建设医院或填补预算，听起来也明显令人起疑。坐拥滚滚石油财富的七万阿布扎比特权居民，或许早已享受着全球最高的人均收入。在那些年份，2 亿美元是一笔数额巨大的欧洲美元贷款，相当于每个阿布扎比居民名下近 3000 美元——对于一个在正常情况下仅是个小小石油酋长国而言，这样借款简直荒谬至极、毫无必要。

Also heightening suspicion was the fact that on October 20, the London *Times* reported that the loan negotiations had just begun—suggesting that its origins hadn’t antedated the war after all. Even the loan’s dollar denomination raised eyebrows. All summer, the Soviets had sent weapons to Egypt and Syria and were now strapped for foreign exchange. It was known in diplomatic circles that in exchange for weaponry, they were demanding hard currency from the Arabs—in other words, dollars. Adding piquancy to the speculation was the fact that two of Morgan Grenfell’s Middle East specialists were the sons of cabinet ministers—David Douglas-Home, son of Foreign Secretary Alexander Douglas-Home, and Rupert F. J. Carrington, son of Defense Secretary Peter A. R. Carrington.
同样引人怀疑的是，10 月 20 日《伦敦时报》报道称贷款谈判才刚刚开始——这意味着这笔贷款的起源并非在战后。甚至贷款以美元计价也引发了质疑。整个夏天，苏联一直向埃及和叙利亚运送武器，如今自身外汇吃紧。外交圈内皆知，作为武器交换，他们要求阿拉伯国家支付硬通货——也就是美元。更让这些猜测耐人寻味的是，摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）的两名中东事务专家竟是内阁大臣之子——外交大臣亚历山大·道格拉斯-霍姆之子戴维·道格拉斯-霍姆，以及国防大臣彼得·A·R·卡林顿之子鲁珀特·F·J·卡林顿。

With Abu Dhabi having just slapped an oil embargo on the United States, American banks reacted skittishly to a loan that, if problematic in the City, was plain anathema on Wall Street. Morgan Guaranty and First National City quietly bowed out of the syndicate—the more striking in Morgan’s case in that it advised the small sheikdom on its \$2.5-billion portfolio. The Japanese had no qualms about joining, however. In fact, Japan’s government wanted to cultivate Abu Dhabi through direct oil purchases and thus bypass major oil companies. It saw a chance to buy friendship, and the Tokai Bank syndicated a \$30- to \$50-million piece of the loan among Japanese banks.
随着阿布扎比刚刚对美国实施石油禁运，美国银行对一笔贷款的反应显得格外紧张——这笔贷款若在伦敦金融城尚存争议，在华尔街则完全是禁忌。摩根担保银行和第一国民城市银行悄然退出了银团，而摩根担保的退出尤为引人注目，因为它曾为这个小小的酋长国管理着 25 亿美元的投资组合。然而，日本银行却毫无顾忌地加入了进来。事实上，日本政府希望通过直接购油来拉拢阿布扎比，从而绕过大型石油公司。它看到了一个用金钱换取友谊的机会，于是东海银行在日本银行间牵头组织了一笔 3000 万至 5000 万美元的贷款份额。

Admitting that money was fungible, even Sir John Stevens couldn’t vouch categorically for the ultimate destination of the jumbo loan. Others involved no longer pretend about it. One claimed:
就连约翰·史蒂文斯爵士也承认，资金是流动的，他无法绝对保证这笔巨额贷款的最终去向。其他相关方则不再掩饰这一点。有人声称：

> It was certainly a war loan. Eurodollar loans at that level were very few and far between. The loan was prepaid in a matter of weeks. In fact, it did Morgan Grenfell no good whatsoever. As soon as oil prices quadrupled, Abu Dhabi’s credit rating went from okay to extra special. So it was hard to explain to Abu Dhabi why the interest rate was so high. It seemed like pure usury to them. The reason the rates were high was because it was a war loan.
> 这显然是一笔战争贷款。当时这种级别的欧洲美元贷款少之又少，几周内就提前还清了。事实上，这笔贷款对摩根建富压根没好处。油价翻了两番后，阿布扎比的信用评级从还行一跃变成了特优级。所以很难向阿布扎比解释为什么利率这么高。在他们看来，这简直是高利贷。利率之所以高，就是因为这是战争贷款。

On October 27, 1973, after six years at Morgan Grenfell, Sir John Stevens died at the age of 59. He had already recruited his wife’s cousin Bill Mackworth-Young, from the corporate stock-brokerage firm of Rowe and Pitman. Mackworth-Young was the leading new issue broker in the City, a man of acute intellect who would succeed Stevens as chief executive and figure importantly in the firm’s future. In retrospect, Stevens’s death deprived the firm of a figure who might have propelled it into a global powerhouse, like the rival Warburgs. But he had already injected some dynamism into the firm and set it on an upward international course, restoring it to the front ranks of London merchant banking. Bolstered by lucrative Arab business, Morgan Grenfell would chalk up record profits in a recessionary environment.
1973 年 10 月 27 日，在摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）任职六年后，约翰·史蒂文斯爵士（Sir John Stevens）去世，享年 59 岁。此前，他已从企业股票经纪公司 Rowe and Pitman 招募了妻子的表弟比尔·麦克沃斯-杨（Bill Mackworth-Young）。麦克沃斯-杨是伦敦金融城顶尖的新股发行经纪人，才智过人，他将接替史蒂文斯出任首席执行官，并在公司未来的发展中扮演重要角色。回想起来，史蒂文斯的去世使公司失去了一位本可能推动其成为全球金融巨头的人物——就像竞争对手沃伯格家族（Warburgs）那样。但他已为公司注入了活力，并引领其走上国际化上升之路，使其重新跻身伦敦商业银行的前列。凭借利润丰厚的阿拉伯业务，摩根建富在经济衰退的环境下仍创下了创纪录的利润。

With the chameleonlike adaptability of a merchant bank, Morgan Grenfell took on Arab colors with remarkable speed. To please the Middle Easterners, it adopted a policy of not hiring Jews, at least not on the international side. The firm would resort to euphemisms to describe this rule—saying it was “Arab-oriented” or “non-Israel-oriented”—but it boiled down to blackballing Jewish employees and Israeli business. The newly Arabized Morgan Grenfell advised Qatar and Dubai on investment strategy, entered into a joint venture with Jordan’s Arab Bank, opened offices in Egypt and Iran, and formed a link with France’s Compagnie Financiere de Suez, whose subsidiary, Banque de l’lndochine, had branches throughout the Middle East.
像商业银行般善于变色，摩根格伦费尔以惊人的速度披上了阿拉伯色彩。为了讨好中东客户，它奉行不雇佣犹太人的政策——至少国际业务部是如此。这家公司会用委婉的说法来描述这条规矩，比如“以阿拉伯为导向”或“非以色列导向”，但归根结底就是排斥犹太员工和以色列业务。焕然一新的阿拉伯化摩根格伦费尔为卡塔尔和迪拜提供投资策略咨询，与约旦阿拉伯银行成立合资公司，在埃及和伊朗设立办事处，并与法国苏伊士金融公司建立联系——后者旗下的印度支那银行在中东各地都设有分行。

As its Middle East fame spread, Morgan Grenfell found at its doorstep people who required inside knowledge of Arab finance or introductions into Persian Gulf diplomatic circles. In 1975, it drew a suitor who demanded an ironclad guarantee of confidentiality—Henry Ford II. Emissaries from Ford Motor posed a maddening riddle: how could the company, blacklisted by the Arab boycott, operate in both Israel and Egypt? This seemed the political equivalent of squaring the circle.
随着在中东声名鹊起，摩根建富集团的门前开始出现一些需要了解阿拉伯金融内幕或希望打入波斯湾外交圈的人。1975 年，一位追求者找上门来，他要求得到铁一般的保密承诺——此人正是亨利·福特二世。福特汽车公司的特使们带来了一个令人费解的难题：这家被阿拉伯联合抵制运动列入黑名单的公司，怎样才能同时在以色列和埃及开展业务？这简直就像要在政治上化圆为方一样不可能。

Ford Motor was a pariah in the Middle East. From 1950 to 1966, it had operated an assembly plant in Alexandria, Egypt. Then an Israeli Ford dealer got permission to assemble Fords in Israel from imported parts. Despite the absence of direct Ford investment or personnel in Israel, the Arab League threatened a regional boycott of Ford cars if the Israeli deal weren’t scuttled. For Henry Ford, the decision was sensitive because of embarrassment about his grandfather’s anti-Semitism. So the grandson refused to renounce his principles or submit to Arab pressure, and the Israeli operation proceeded unhindered. “It was just a pragmatic business procedure,” he later said. “I don’t mind saying I was influenced by the fact that the company still suffers from a resentment against the anti-Semitism of the past. We want to overcome that. ”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420820) Some observers also credited Ford with a shrewd public relations maneuver.
福特汽车公司在中东曾一度被视为异类。1950 年至 1966 年间，该公司在埃及亚历山大港运营了一家组装厂。随后，一家以色列福特经销商获准从进口零部件在以色列组装福特汽车。尽管福特在以色列并无直接投资或派驻人员，但阿拉伯联盟仍威胁称，若不终止与以色列的交易，将在整个地区抵制福特汽车。对亨利·福特而言，这一决定颇为敏感，因为其祖父的反犹主义历史令他倍感尴尬。最终，这位孙子拒绝背弃原则或向阿拉伯压力屈服，以色列业务得以顺利推进。"这不过是务实的商业决策，"他后来表示，"我不讳言，公司至今仍因往昔反犹主义遭人记恨，这一点确实影响了我。我们想摆脱这种阴影。" [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420820) 也有观察者认为，福特此举不失为精明的公关手腕。

When Ford Motor appeared on the Arab blacklist in 1966, its Alexandrian operation was shut down, starting Ford’s exile from the Muslim world. Despite the loss of Arab business, Henry Ford never wavered in his decision. As he flatly told his close friend Max Fisher, a top American fund-raiser for Israel, “Nobody’s gonna tell me what to do.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420982) In 1972, Fisher accompanied Ford on a tour of Israel, where they were received by Prime Minister Golda Meir, Moshe Dayan, and Shimon Peres. Ford seemed quite comfortable with his decision.
当福特汽车在 1966 年被列入阿拉伯黑名单后，其在亚历山大的业务被迫关停，福特从此开始了在穆斯林世界的撤离之路。尽管失去了阿拉伯市场的生意，亨利·福特却从未动摇过自己的决定。他直截了当地告诉密友、以色列在美国的主要筹款人 Max Fisher：“没人能对我发号施令。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2420982) 1972 年，Fisher 陪同福特访问以色列，并受到了梅厄总理、摩西·达扬和西蒙·佩雷斯的接见。福特对自己的决定显得颇为满意。

What Henry Ford never told Max Fisher was that he undertook secret efforts through Morgan Grenfell to reintroduce his company into the Arab world. He wanted to reopen the Alexandria plant as a joint venture with Egypt to manufacture diesel engines, tractors, and trucks. There was a high-level political agenda. Ford thought his company’s presence in Egypt might erode Arab resistance, dissolving the sharp distinction between pro-Israeli and pro-Arab American companies. Egypt was more relaxed about the boycott than other Arab countries, although it still put up formidable obstacles. The Ford people had picked up encouraging hints in Washington and the Arab world that the company might soon come off the blacklist.
亨利·福特从未告诉麦克斯·费舍尔的是，他曾通过摩根建富秘密运作，试图让他的公司重返阿拉伯世界。他希望重启亚历山大港的工厂，与埃及合资生产柴油发动机、拖拉机及卡车。这背后有着高层政治考量——福特认为，公司在埃及的存在或将瓦解阿拉伯世界的抵抗，从而模糊亲以色列与亲阿拉伯的美国公司之间的尖锐界限。尽管埃及仍设置重重障碍，但相较于其他阿拉伯国家，它对抵制运动的态度已相对宽松。福特公司的人士已在华盛顿和阿拉伯世界捕捉到鼓舞人心的信号：公司或许很快就能从黑名单上除名。

Ford came to Morgan Grenfell circuitously, after sounding out Morgan Guaranty and other banks as to who had the best Middle East connections. In the early postwar years, Ford Motor had viewed the House of Morgan warily because of its historic association with General Motors. Over the years, however, Morgan Grenfell had handled a remarkable variety of Ford business. It supervised the final sale of Ford U.K. to the Detroit parent company (which then held only a partial interest), introduced Ford stock on the London exchange with Lazards, and managed Ford U.K.’s pension fund. As added incentives to Henry Ford II, Morgan Grenfell had been commissioned by Egypt’s central bank to study its nation’s foreign-investment law and had even entered into a joint venture with the speaker of Egypt’s Parliament.
福特进入摩根建富的过程颇为曲折，此前他曾向摩根保证及其他银行打听，谁在中东业务上人脉最广。战后初期，福特汽车对摩根财团一直心存戒备，因后者与通用汽车渊源颇深。然而多年来，摩根建富却经手了福特大量五花八门的业务：它监督了福特英国公司最终出售给底特律母公司（当时母公司仅持有部分股权），与拉扎德银行联手将福特股票引入伦敦交易所，并管理着福特英国公司的养老基金。更让亨利·福特二世心动的是，摩根建富还受埃及中央银行委托，研究该国外资法，甚至与埃及议会议长合资创办了企业。

In 1975, Morgan Grenfell outlined a precise sequence of steps to circumvent the Arab blacklist. The firm knew exactly which sheiks could fix things so that behind a facade of Arab militance, business could proceed unimpeded by religious or political zeal. Morgan Grenfell suggested selling equity stakes in the Alexandria operation to influential bankers, families, and institutions across the Arab world, not just in Egypt. This would build a powerful Arab constituency for getting Ford off the blacklist and would also help to line up cheap Middle East financing. This last was crucial, for Ford believed that only cheap financing could offset prohibitive operating costs in Egypt.
1975 年，摩根建富规划了一套精密步骤以规避阿拉伯黑名单。该公司清楚哪些酋长能够斡旋此事，使得在阿拉伯世界看似强硬的表象下，商业活动得以不受宗教或政治狂热的阻碍。摩根建富建议将亚历山大业务的股权出售给整个阿拉伯世界（不仅限于埃及）有影响力的银行家、家族及机构。此举既能构建强大的阿拉伯利益群体推动福特公司脱离黑名单，也有助于获取中东的低成本融资。最后这一点至关重要，因为福特公司坚信，唯有低成本融资才能抵消埃及高昂的运营成本。

President Anwar Sadat took a personal interest in furthering the project. He was sympathetic to removing American companies from the blacklist if they made investments in the Arab world comparable to those they had in Israel. He insisted that Ford be removed from the Arab blacklist as a precondition for operating in Alexandria but also intimated that he might just go ahead and unilaterally strike Ford from the Egyptian blacklist. It was never entirely clear whether in the end he would courageously defy his Arab brethren or back off.
安瓦尔·萨达特总统对推进该项目抱有个人兴趣。他倾向于支持，如果美国公司在阿拉伯世界的投资能与在以色列的投资相当，就将它们从黑名单中移除。他坚持要求将福特公司从阿拉伯黑名单中删除，作为其在亚历山大港运营的前提条件，但也暗示他可能会单方面将福特从埃及的黑名单中移除。最终他是否会勇敢地违抗阿拉伯兄弟国家的立场，还是选择退缩，始终没有完全明朗。

For two years, Morgan Grenfell and Ford Motor tried to seduce various sheiks. They played on the willingness of royal Arab families to exploit their positions for personal gain. The Morgan Grenfell strategy accurately gauged the depth of Arab cynicism. Some shieks wanted exclusive Ford dealerships before participating. Some bankers wanted a personal share of Ford’s Egyptian plant in exchange for loans. In Saudi Arabia, Morgan Grenfell had targeted Khalid Alireza, a shareholder in Morgan Grenfell and the Egyptian Finance Company. The Alirezas were a powerful, highly respected merchant family and importing agents for many American, British, and German companies. They had even held the Ford dealership before the Arab boycott. Nevertheless, as strict Muslims and uncompromising anti-Zionists, they finally refused to participate. In general, the Kuwaitis were more receptive than the more militant, hard-line Saudis.
两年来，摩根·格伦费尔与福特汽车公司不断试图拉拢各路酋长。他们利用了阿拉伯王室家族成员借职权谋私的意愿。摩根·格伦费尔的策略精准地把握了阿拉伯人对权钱交易的坦然接受程度。一些酋长要求先获得福特独家经销权才肯参与合作，一些银行家则要求以福特埃及工厂的股份作为贷款回报。在沙特阿拉伯，摩根·格伦费尔瞄准了哈立德·阿尔雷扎——他是摩根·格伦费尔与埃及金融公司的股东。阿尔雷扎家族是势力雄厚、备受敬重的商贾世家，代理着多家美英德企业的进口业务，甚至在阿拉伯抵制运动前曾持有福特经销权。然而，作为严守教义的穆斯林和坚定的反犹太复国主义者，他们最终拒绝了参与。总体而言，科威特人比立场更强硬激进的沙特人更容易接受合作。

This clandestine lobbying continued during the 1976 presidential campaign, when Henry Ford II was a leading business fund-raiser for Jimmy Carter. During the fall campaign, Morgan Grenfell shepherded Ford people around the Gulf states, a mission requiring airtight secrecy, given Ford’s link with Carter and the potential for political embarrassment among Jewish voters. In February 1977, promoting his proposed Egyptian operation, Ford met privately with President Sadat for several hours. Sadat saw the Ford plant as a magnet that might draw other companies into an Alexandrian industrial zone. To Ford Motor, Coca-Cola, Xerox, and other American companies barred from the Arab world, he wanted to offer a deal—invest in Egypt, and he would work to delete them from the blacklist.
1976 年总统竞选期间，这种秘密游说仍在继续，当时亨利·福特二世是吉米·卡特的主要商业筹款人。秋季竞选时，摩根建富带领福特的人在阿拉伯海湾国家活动，鉴于福特与卡特的关系以及可能对犹太选民造成的政治尴尬，这项任务必须严格保密。1977 年 2 月，为推进其拟议中的埃及业务，福特与萨达特总统私下会面数小时。萨达特将福特工厂视为吸引其他公司进入亚历山大工业区的磁石。对于被阿拉伯世界拒之门外的福特汽车、可口可乐、施乐及其他美国公司，他提出了一项交易——若在埃及投资，他将努力让这些公司从黑名单中除名。

In May 1977, Morgan Grenfell nearly pulled off the supreme trick of Middle East politics: Egypt announced a joint venture with Ford to assemble trucks and diesel engines; Egypt’s approval was contingent on Ford’s securing the removal of its name from the pan-Arab blacklist. Egypt was to contribute 40 percent of the capital, and Ford Motor 30 percent, with the remaining 30 percent parceled out to the Arab friends Morgan Grenfell had rounded up. In October 1977, after Egypt removed Ford from its own blacklist, the agreement was signed.
1977 年 5 月，摩根格兰费尔几乎完成了中东政治史上的一记妙招：埃及宣布与福特公司合资组装卡车和柴油发动机；埃及的批准条件是福特必须确保其名字从泛阿拉伯黑名单中移除。埃及出资 40%，福特汽车出资 30%，剩余 30%分给摩根格兰费尔召集的阿拉伯朋友。1977 年 10 月，在埃及将福特从本国黑名单中移除后，协议正式签署。

In the end, the project was stillborn, apparently for a variety of reasons. There was thunderous Arab opposition: the lobbying effort hadn’t silenced Arab militance or purchased the necessary high-level cooperation. Mohammed Mahgoub, Sudanese head of the Arab boycott, bitterly denounced Ford and threatened to boycott products made in the Alexandrian plant. And since the plant was to produce for export as well as domestic consumption, this would reduce its value to Ford. Perhaps the greatest sticking point was that the Egyptians, after endless haggling with Morgan Grenfell, refused to modify their Public Law 43, which set tough conditions on foreign investment. Without such changes, Ford felt that it couldn’t operate at a profit.
最终，这个项目还是夭折了，显然原因多种多样。阿拉伯方面强烈反对：游说努力既没能平息阿拉伯人的强硬态度，也没能买到必要的高层合作。苏丹阿拉伯抵制运动负责人穆罕默德·马赫朱布激烈谴责福特，并威胁要抵制亚历山大工厂生产的产品。而且，由于该工厂既面向出口也面向内销，这就会降低它对福特的价值。也许最大的症结在于，埃及人在与摩根·格伦费尔公司无休止的讨价还价后，拒绝修改其第 43 号公共法律，该法律对外国投资设置了苛刻的条件。没有这些修改，福特觉得自己根本无法盈利运营。

The Egyptian initiative would disappear, despite public announcement of the deal in 1977 and more meetings that year between Ford officials and Anwar Sadat. It was almost as if it had never happened, so completely was it buried and forgotten. When approached about it, Ford Motor wouldn’t comment, saying the information was “legally privileged.” And when Henry Ford’s close friend Max Fisher was asked, he said, “Frankly, I have never heard of any of this before.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421108) It was a Morgan operation in the classic style of Teddy Grenfell: it left no footprints behind.
埃及的这项计划最终不了了之，尽管 1977 年该协议曾公开宣布，当年福特公司高管与安瓦尔·萨达特也多次会面。它仿佛从未存在过一般，被彻底掩埋和遗忘。当被问及此事时，福特汽车拒绝置评，称相关信息"受法律特权保护"。而亨利·福特的挚友马克斯·费舍尔被问及时则说："说实话，我以前从没听说过这些事。" [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421108) 这是一次典型的泰迪·格伦费尔式摩根操作：没有留下任何蛛丝马迹。

THE exorbitant oil prices and interest rates that followed the Arab oil embargo produced many bankruptcies, and Morgan Guaranty spent much of 1975 desperately plugging fingers into dikes. It was lead banker to W. T. Grant, America’s third largest variety-store chain, which foundered that year in history’s largest retail failure. The House of Morgan took a \$50-million write-off. “We don’t make many mistakes,” said Morgan’s Rod Lindsay, “but when we do make one, it’s a beaut.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421280)
阿拉伯石油禁运后出现的畸高油价和利率导致了许多企业破产，1975 年大部分时间里，摩根担保银行都在拼命地堵漏洞。作为美国第三大连锁百货公司 W. T. Grant 的首席银行，该公司在那一年倒闭，成为史上最大的零售业破产案。摩根财团为此注销了 5000 万美元。“我们犯的错误不多，”摩根的罗德·林赛说，“但一旦犯错，那一定是个大错。”

However grand its global operations, the House of Morgan remained a New York City bank. It had always regarded the city’s credit as a proxy for America. To that end, it had saved New York in the 1907 panic, in August 1914, and in 1933. These earlier crises illustrated the strength of the Morgan bank. But by 1975, New York had a population the size of Sweden’s and a budget as big as India’s. During the city’s fiscal crisis that year, the Morgan role would seem marginal compared with that of the three earlier rescues.
尽管其全球业务规模宏大，摩根财团本质上仍是纽约市的一家银行。它始终将这座城市的信用视作美国的晴雨表。为此，它在 1907 年恐慌、1914 年 8 月及 1933 年三次挽救过纽约。此前的危机凸显了摩根银行的强大实力。但到了 1975 年，纽约的人口已与瑞典相当，预算规模也堪比印度。在当年那场城市财政危机中，摩根所扮演的角色与前三次救援相比，显得微不足道。

Starting with the administration of Mayor John V. Lindsay (brother of Morgan Guaranty’s Rod) in the 1960s, New York City had borrowed heavily for expanded social welfare programs. By late 1974, city paper saturated the markets, driving up interest rates and causing steep losses for underwriters, including Morgans. (Commercial banks could underwrite municipal issues backed by taxing power.) That December, Mayor Abraham Beame held an emergency breakfast with bankers at Gracie Mansion. There an advisory group of three influential bank chairmen was formed—David Rockefeller of Chase, Walter Wriston of First National City, and Ellmore C. Patterson of Morgans. Patterson became the leader, because Wriston was ideologically hostile to government and Rockefeller’s brother, Nelson, was then the nation’s vice-president.
自 20 世纪 60 年代市长约翰·V·林赛（摩根保证信托的罗德之兄）执政以来，纽约市便大举举债以扩张社会福利项目。到 1974 年底，市政债券充斥市场，推高了利率，导致包括摩根在内的承销商遭受惨重损失（商业银行可承销有征税权支持的市政债券）。那年 12 月，市长亚伯拉罕·比姆在格雷西公馆与银行家们举行紧急早餐会。会上成立了一个由三位颇具影响力的银行董事长组成的顾问小组——大通银行的大卫·洛克菲勒、第一国民城市银行的沃尔特·瑞斯顿，以及摩根银行的埃尔莫尔·C·帕特森。帕特森成为领头人，因为瑞斯顿在意识形态上对政府抱有敌意，而洛克菲勒的兄弟纳尔逊当时正担任美国副总统。

Ellmore (“Pat”) Patterson was very midwestern, with a relaxed manner and a slow drawl. His description of the Morgan staff might apply as well to himself: “We’re not known for geniuses charging around, but for good solid people with a strong feeling toward the bank.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421428) Tall and straight, with a friendly grin, he wasn’t a brainy executive, but he was popular and unpretentious. After Nixon devalued the dollar and imposed an import surcharge, Patterson lunched with the head of the Sumitomo bank, who wanted to know how Patterson could let Nixon take steps harming Japan. “I don’t know the president,” Patterson said breezily. “I never met him.” His luncheon guest was shocked. “You—the head of Morgan Guaranty—don’t know the president of the United States?” Patterson, smiling, said no. If the story says something about Japan, it also says something about Patterson’s candor. He would express no fake altruism about the New York City rescue: “I just didn’t want to have that much debt going bust—just protecting my own hide, so to speak. I sure didn’t want to write off all those investments we had.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421593) His Financial Community Liaison Group held its (mostly unpublicized) meetings at 23 Wall.
埃利摩尔（“帕特”）帕特森是个典型的中西部人，举止随和，说话慢吞吞的。他形容摩根员工的话，用在他自己身上也很贴切：“我们出名不是因为天才横冲直撞，而是因为一群踏实可靠、对银行有强烈归属感的人。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421428) 他身材高挑挺拔，带着友善的笑容，算不上那种头脑型高管，但人缘很好，毫无架子。尼克松让美元贬值并加征进口附加税后，帕特森与住友银行行长共进午餐，对方想知道帕特森怎么能容忍尼克松采取损害日本的措施。“我不认识总统，”帕特森轻松地说，“我从没见过他。”那位客人震惊了：“你——摩根担保银行的行长——不认识美国总统？”帕特森笑着说不认识。这个故事既说明了日本的情况，也体现了帕特森的坦诚。 他对纽约市救援一事毫不掩饰自己的私心：“我只是不想让那么多债务打水漂——说白了，就是自保。我当然不想把我们的所有投资都一笔勾销。”” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421593) 他的金融社区联络组在华尔街 23 号举行了（大多是私下进行的）会议。

In early 1975, when financial markets wouldn’t swallow more city paper, the Patterson group began to function as a *de facto* government. However Beame might bluster in public, he had to submit to the bankers’ coup. There was a transfer of power from the city’s highest elected official to a new, unelected mayor, Pat Patterson. The humiliated Beame would badger Patterson for news, sometimes telephoning him after midnight. When Patterson went golfing, he would see a golf cart speeding toward him and know it carried a message from the mayor. “He kept calling me and he’d say, ‘What’s going on?’ ” Patterson recalled. “As Beame lost more control, we gradually had to tell him what he could and couldn’t do.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421719)
1975 年初，金融市场已无力消化更多城市债券，帕特森集团开始实质上扮演起政府的角色。无论比姆在公开场合如何虚张声势，他最终还是屈服于银行家们发动的这场政变。权力从这座城市民选最高官员手中，转移到了新上任的、未经选举的市长帕特·帕特森手中。蒙受羞辱的比姆时常缠着帕特森打听消息，有时甚至半夜过后还打电话过去。帕特森打高尔夫时，总能看到一辆高尔夫球车疾驰而来——他知道那准是市长又捎来了口信。“他不停地给我打电话，问‘现在什么情况？’”帕特森回忆道，“随着比姆一点点失去控制权，我们渐渐不得不告诉他，哪些事他能做，哪些不能。”

Despite such seeming banker omnipotence, 1975 would actually demonstrate reduced banker influence. Unlike earlier Morgan-led rescues of the city, the bankers were as vulnerable as the city itself. They had granted multibillion-dollar credits to the city and held its paper; at one point, Morgan Guaranty alone had an estimated \$300 million of city notes and bonds in its portfolio. By May, trading in New York City debt wound to an eerie halt. Along with a balanced budget and a commission to review city finances, the Patterson group wanted federal guarantees to pry open the closing market. From now on, their “rescue” would involve lobbying Washington and Albany. They were appealing to the government to rescue *them* and not just the city.
尽管银行家们看似无所不能，但 1975 年却实际显示其影响力已大不如前。与早年摩根主导救市的情形不同，此时的银行家们与这座城市一样脆弱。他们向纽约市提供了数十亿美元的信贷，并持有其债券；仅摩根担保信托一家，其投资组合中估计就持有 3 亿美元的市政票据和债券。到了 5 月，纽约市债务交易竟诡异地陷入停滞。帕特森团队除了要求实现预算平衡和设立城市财政审查委员会外，还希望联邦政府提供担保，以打开这扇日益关闭的市场大门。从此以后，他们的“救市”行动转变为向华盛顿和奥尔巴尼进行游说。他们实际上是在恳求政府拯救自己，而不仅仅是拯救这座城市。

Patterson set up a White House appointment with President Ford, against whom he’d played college football. Accompanied by Rockefeller and Wriston, Patterson argued in the Oval Office that a New York City default would trigger general damage, depressing all municipal bonds. President Ford, thanking the group for coming, offered nothing in return. Long afterward, Beame would stand in Patterson’s office staring at a photograph taken during the Oval Office meeting. “If Ford had said yes that day,” sighed Beame, “he would have been president today.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421845)
帕特森安排了一次与福特总统的白宫会面，他曾在大学橄榄球赛中与福特交手。在洛克菲勒和瑞斯顿的陪同下，帕特森在椭圆形办公室争辩说，纽约市违约将引发全面损失，导致所有市政债券贬值。福特总统感谢了他们的来访，但没有做出任何承诺。很久以后，比姆站在帕特森的办公室里，凝视着椭圆形办公室会议期间拍摄的一张照片。“如果福特那天答应了，”比姆叹息道，“他今天就会是总统了。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421845)

The New York City crisis presented an ideological clash among conservative businessmen. Said Treasury Secretary William Simon: “It was one of the saddest days of my life when financial giants like Pat Patterson of Morgan Guaranty and Walter Wriston . . . caved in and finally joined the others in asking Washington for federal aid.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421971) Yet the House of Morgan had never adhered to extreme *laissez-faire* Republicanism. Much like Pierpont Morgan, it placed a premium on financial order. It was close to the Federal Reserve and favored government action to avoid financial disruption. It would never produce as ideological a hawk as Walter Wriston.
纽约市危机在保守派商人之间引发了一场意识形态冲突。财政部长威廉·西蒙曾表示："当像摩根担保公司的帕特·帕特森和沃尔特·瑞斯顿这样的金融巨头……屈服并最终加入其他人向华盛顿寻求联邦援助时，那是我人生中最悲哀的日子之一。" [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2421971) 然而，摩根家族从未遵循过极端的自由主义共和党路线。像皮尔庞特·摩根一样，它更看重金融秩序。它与美联储关系密切，支持政府采取行动避免金融动荡。它绝不会培养出像沃尔特·瑞斯顿那样意识形态强硬的鹰派人物。

On May 26, 1975, Dick Shinn, head of Metropolitan Life, hosted a meeting at his home with Felix Rohatyn of Lazard Freres and other representatives of New York governor Hugh Carey. They worked out a plan for a Municipal Assistance Corporation (“Big MAC”) to issue, under state auspices, bonds backed by city sales taxes. This permitted banks to exchange \$1 billion in shaky city paper for new debt with an A rating. It was Carey’s involvement, not the banks’, that was the critical turning point. With the city again facing default in September, Carey created an Emergency Financial Control Board to assume budgetary powers from the city.
1975 年 5 月 26 日，大都会人寿保险公司总裁迪克·辛恩在其家中主持了一场会议，与会者包括拉扎德投资银行的菲利克斯·罗哈廷以及纽约州长休·凯里的其他代表。他们制定了一项计划，由州政府牵头成立市政援助公司（即“大麦克”），发行以城市销售税为担保的债券。这使得银行能够将 10 亿美元摇摇欲坠的市政票据，兑换成评级为 A 级的新债券。关键在于，促成这一转折的并非银行，而是凯里的介入。同年 9 月，当纽约市再次面临违约时，凯里成立了紧急财政控制委员会，从市政府手中接管了预算权。

In mid-October 1975, world financial markets experienced one of those queer moments of falling pressure that sometimes presage storms. Amid fears of a New York City default, Patterson, Wriston, and Rockefeller pleaded for federal help before the Senate Banking Committee. Patterson warned that they were drifting into an unpredictable no-man’s-land that could create an “economic downpull of general economic activity.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422131) The three bankers asked for a direct federal loan or loan guarantee to prevent otherwise certain default.
1975 年 10 月中旬，全球金融市场经历了一次如暴风雨前气压骤降般的诡异时刻。在纽约市可能发生债务违约的恐慌情绪中，帕特森、瑞斯顿和洛克菲勒三位银行家共同向参议院银行委员会恳求联邦援助。帕特森警告称，他们正滑向一个不可预测的无人地带，这将引发"整体经济活动的下行拉力"。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422131) 三位银行家请求联邦政府提供直接贷款或贷款担保，以防本已注定的违约。

In November, New York State announced a moratorium on \$1.6 billion of short-term debt. Now fearing a generalized crisis, President Ford got spooked and had Congress approve a \$2.3-billion line of credit to the city. Much as the state had put the city in its power, so the federal government put New York State under its control. Patterson felt vindicated: “There were a lot of people who would just as soon have seen New York go bankrupt. They thought it was a good thing to clean it out and get rid of the labor contracts. But our committee, fortunately, stuck with it.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422369) Patterson was praised by labor leaders and government officials for his constructive, conciliatory approach.
11 月，纽约州宣布暂停 16 亿美元短期债务。由于担心危机全面爆发，福特总统感到不安，并促使国会批准向该市提供 23 亿美元信贷额度。正如州政府曾将纽约市置于其掌控之下，联邦政府如今也加强了对纽约州的控制。帕特森感到自己的判断被证实了：“有很多人巴不得看到纽约破产。他们认为清盘并解除劳动合同是件好事。但幸运的是，我们的委员会坚持了下来。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422369) 帕特森因其建设性的和解方式而受到劳工领袖和政府官员的赞扬。

In the end, the bankers exchanged their risky short-term paper for safe long-term MAC bonds. It had proven necessary to enlist state and federal help. The House of Morgan no longer presided over financial crises. As banks dwindled in power, they could cooperate with government-sponsored rescues instead of leading them. Even the largest could no more control the vast financial markets than they could bid the Red Sea part. The days when a Pierpont Morgan could sit down and extemporaneously write out a single sheet of paper to save the city were long gone.
最终，银行家们用他们风险较高的短期票据换取了安全的长期 MAC bonds。事实证明，争取州和联邦政府的援助是必要的。摩根财团不再主导金融危机。随着银行权力日渐削弱，它们能够配合政府支持的救援行动，而非充当主导者。即便是最大的银行也无法掌控庞大的金融市场，就如同它们无法令红海分开一样。皮尔庞特·摩根能坐下来即兴写下寥寥一页纸便拯救城市的时代，早已一去不复返了。
