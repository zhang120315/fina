# Chapter Seven: Panic

THE folk wisdom of Wall Street says that if a crash is widely expected, it won’t occur, for a saving fear will filter through the marketplace. This was refuted in 1907, when Wall Street spent a cliff-hanging year awaiting the crash that *came.* On March 25, panic selling roiled the Stock Exchange. The financial powers—Henry Clay Frick, Edward H. Harriman, William Rockefeller, and Jacob Schiff—assembled at the Corner for a secret meeting. They wanted a \$25-million pool to steady prices. Jack cabled Pierpont in London, saying Schiff “thought amount of money really needed would be very small, as moral effect of concerted action on part of large interests heretofore antagonistic would be sufficient without actual purchases.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235446) While Jack favored cooperation, Pierpont fired back a hostile cable, saying such an action “would be unwise, entirely at variance with all the policies we have ever adopted being at the head of a declared Stock Exchange manipulation.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235623) The next day, the market rallied—partly on the basis of incorrect reports that Pierpont had joined relief efforts—and the plan was scrapped. All spring, as Pierpont cruised around Europe, his partners wired him that a serious autumn drop appeared likely.
华尔街的智慧箴言说，如果一场崩盘被普遍预料到，它就不会发生，因为恐惧会像预警一样弥漫市场，从而挽救局面。然而，1907 年打破了这一说法——这一整年，华尔街都在提心吊胆地等待那场终究到来的崩盘。3 月 25 日，恐慌性抛售席卷了证券交易所。金融巨头们——亨利·克莱·弗里克、爱德华·H·哈里曼、威廉·洛克菲勒和雅各布·希夫——在“角落”秘密会晤。他们计划筹集 2500 万美元的资金池来稳定股价。杰克给在伦敦的皮尔庞特发了电报，转述希夫的话说：“实际所需的资金其实很少，因为此前相互对立的大财团若能采取一致行动，这种道德效应本身就足以稳定局面，甚至无需实际入市买入。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235446) 尽管杰克倾向于合作，皮尔庞特却回了一封措辞强硬、充满敌意的电报，声称这样的行动“实属不智，完全违背了我们一贯坚持的政策，无异于公开宣布进行股市操纵。”“第二天，市场反弹了——部分原因是基于错误报道，说皮尔庞特参与了救援行动——于是该计划被搁置。整个春天，皮尔庞特在欧洲巡游时，他的合伙人们不断电报告知他，秋季可能出现一场严重暴跌。

At age seventy, Pierpont was often in low spirits. In photographs, his eyes look slightly unfocused, as if telling of inner turmoil. The October 1907 panic found him at the Episcopal Convention in Richmond, Virginia. As a lay delegate from New York, he would attend these conventions in opulent style, bringing bishops down by private railroad car and throwing parties catered by Louis Sherry. Nothing pleased him more than recondite controversies over prayer-book revisions and other matters remote from the material world. At the same time, the contradictory Pierpont brought with him a lady friend, Mrs. John Markoe of Philadelphia, a relative of his personal physician, Dr. James Markoe, and often mentioned as a possible mistress.
皮尔庞特七十岁时常常情绪低落。照片中，他的眼神略显失焦，仿佛诉说着内心的动荡。1907 年 10 月的恐慌袭来时，他正在弗吉尼亚州里士满参加圣公会大会。作为纽约的平信徒代表，他参加这些大会总是排场奢华，用私人火车车厢载着主教们前来，并举办由路易斯·谢里负责宴会的派对。没有什么比关于祈祷书修订和其他远离物质世界的深奥争论更让他开心了。与此同时，矛盾的是，皮尔庞特还带来了一位女性友人——费城的约翰·马科夫人，她是他的私人医生詹姆斯·马科博士的亲戚，常被提及可能是他的情妇。

As the Richmond convention progressed, emergency telegrams came in thick and fast from 23 Wall Street. Morgan’s friend Bishop William Lawrence noted in his diary how Morgan would study the telegrams, place his palms on the table, then stare fixedly ahead. Though Pierpont was needed on Wall Street, his partners feared a premature return might itself touch off a panic. By Saturday, October 19, he decided to rush back by private railroad car to deal with a spreading bank crisis. “They are in trouble in New York,” he told Bishop Lawrence. “They do not know what to do, and I don’t know what to do, but I am going back.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235795)
随着里士满会议的进行，华尔街 23 号传来的紧急电报纷至沓来。摩根的朋友威廉·劳伦斯主教在日记中写道，摩根会仔细研读电报，双手按在桌上，然后凝视前方。尽管华尔街急需皮尔庞特坐镇，但他的合伙人担心过早返回本身就可能引发恐慌。到 10 月 19 日星期六，他决定乘坐私人火车赶回，应对正在蔓延的银行危机。“纽约那边乱了套，”他对劳伦斯主教说，“他们不知道该怎么办，我也不知道该怎么办，但我还是要回去。”

The 1907 panic was Pierpont’s last hurrah. Although semiretired, reporting to work periodically for only an hour or two, he suddenly functioned as America’s central bank. Within two week’s time, he saved several trust companies and a leading brokerage house, bailed out New York City, and rescued the Stock Exchange. His victory was Pyrrhic, however, as America decided that never again would one man wield such power. The 1907 panic would be the last time that bankers loomed so much larger than regulators in a crisis. Afterward, the pendulum would swing decidedly toward government financial management.
1907 年的金融恐慌是皮尔庞特的最后一次辉煌。尽管他已半退休，每天只去办公室一两个小时，但他突然扮演起美国央行的角色。短短两周内，他救下了多家信托公司和一家顶级经纪行，帮助纽约市渡过财政危机，并挽救了证券交易所。然而，他的胜利却是惨胜——美国决定绝不再让任何人独揽如此大权。1907 年的恐慌成为最后一次危机中银行家地位远高于监管者的时刻。此后，权力天平明显向政府金融管理倾斜。

The panic was blamed on many factors—tight money, Roosevelt’s Gridiron Club speech attacking the “malefactors of great wealth,” and excessive speculation in copper, mining, and railroad stocks. The immediate weakness arose from the recklessness of the trust companies. In the early 1900s, national and most state-chartered banks couldn’t take trust accounts (wills, estates, and so on) but directed customers to trusts. Traditionally, these had been synonymous with safe investment. By 1907, however, they had exploited enough legal loopholes to become highly speculative. To draw money for risky ventures, they paid exorbitant interest rates, and trust executives operated like stock market plungers. They loaned out so much against stocks and bonds that by October 1907 as much as half the bank loans in New York were backed by securities as collateral—an extremely shaky base for the system. The trusts also didn’t keep the high cash reserves of commercial banks and were vulnerable to sudden runs.
恐慌被归咎于多种因素——银根紧缩、罗斯福在 Gridiron Club 的演讲中抨击“巨富恶棍”，以及对铜业、矿业和铁路股票的过度投机。直接的诱因在于信托公司的鲁莽行事。20 世纪初，全国性银行及大多数州立银行无法承揽信托账户（遗嘱、遗产等），便将客户引向信托公司。传统上，这些信托公司一直被视为安全投资的代名词。然而到了 1907 年，它们已钻够法律空子，变得高度投机。为了为风险投资筹集资金，它们支付高得离谱的利率，信托公司的高管们则像股市投机客一样操作。它们大量以股票和债券作为抵押发放贷款，到 1907 年 10 月，纽约近一半银行贷款的抵押品都是证券——这对整个银行体系而言是极不稳固的基础。 信托公司也不像商业银行那样保持高额的现金储备，因此极易受到突然挤兑的冲击。

That Pierpont rescued the trusts was ironic, for they were anathema to the Wall Street establishment. As George Perkins said, “Indeed, we hadn’t any use for their management and knew that they ought to be closed, but we fought to keep them open in order not to have runs on other concerns.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235960) When J. P. Morgan and other prestigious houses referred clients to them for trust work, the unscrupulous trusts tried to steal the nontrust business of these clients. Two young bankers, Henry Pomeroy Davison of the First National Bank and Thomas W. Lamont of Liberty Bank, were among those who in 1903 set up a “captive” trust called Bankers Trust. Although commercial banks couldn’t do trust business, they could own trusts, and they pooled their money to set up the new bank. The idea was that the House of Morgan and its allies would refer trust business to Bankers Trust, which would politely return the customers once their trust business was complete. By no accident, the Morgan bank would stare vigilantly at Bankers Trust across the Corner of Broad and Wall.
皮尔庞特出手拯救信托公司，这本身就充满了讽刺意味——要知道，这些信托公司正是华尔街权贵们的眼中钉。正如乔治·珀金斯所言：“说实话，我们对他们的管理层毫无好感，也清楚这些机构理应关门大吉；但为了避免引发其他机构的挤兑潮，我们仍竭力维持它们的运营。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235960) 当 J. P. 摩根及其他声名显赫的财团将客户推荐给这些信托公司办理信托业务时，那些不择手段的信托公司竟试图趁机抢走客户的其他非信托业务。两位年轻的银行家——第一国民银行的亨利·波默罗伊·戴维森和自由银行的托马斯·W·拉蒙特——于 1903 年牵头成立了一家名为“银行家信托”（Bankers Trust）的“专属”信托公司。尽管商业银行不能直接经营信托业务，但它们可以拥有信托公司；于是它们集资创立了这家新银行。其设想是，摩根财团及其盟友将信托业务引荐给银行家信托，而银行家信托在完成客户的信托事务后，会礼貌地将客户送回原处。 绝非偶然，摩根银行会警惕地注视着位于布罗德街与华尔街街角对面的银行家信托公司。

On Monday, October 21, the day after Pierpont returned from Richmond, a collapse in copper shares undermined the trusts. There were fears of a copper glut, spurred partly by news that the Morgans would join the Guggenheims in developing new Alaskan copper mines. When an attempt to corner United Copper burst, its stock skidded 35 points in just two hours, spreading ruin and dragging stocks to levels unseen since the 1893 depression. Charles T. Barney, president of Knickerbocker Trust, was associated with F. Augustus Heinze and other speculators who had cornered United Copper. So the stock’s fall alarmed the Knickerbocker’s eighteen thousand depositors. At its new main office at Thirty-fourth Street and Fifth Avenue, customers lined up on Tuesday morning to empty their accounts.
10 月 21 日星期一，皮尔庞特从里士满返回的次日，铜矿股崩盘，拖垮了多家信托公司。市场担心铜供应过剩，部分原因是摩根家族将加入古根海姆家族，共同开发阿拉斯加的新铜矿。试图垄断联合铜业公司的行动失败后，其股价在两小时内暴跌 35 点，引发连锁崩盘，将股市拖至 1893 年大萧条以来的最低水平。尼克伯克信托公司总裁查尔斯·T·巴尼与曾试图垄断联合铜业的 F·奥古斯都·海因策等投机者有关联，因此股价暴跌令尼克伯克的一万八千名储户惊慌不已。周二早晨，在其位于第三十四街与第五大道交汇处的新总部，客户们排起长队，纷纷清空账户。

As panic spread to other trusts around town, Pierpont took charge of the rescue operation. Emergencies seemed to fortify his confidence even as they introduced doubt or terror in others. He formed a committee of young bankers, including Henry Pomeroy Davison of the First National Bank and Benjamin Strong of Bankers Trust. He sent them to audit the Knickerbocker’s books. Later, as all-powerful governor of the New York Federal Reserve Bank, Strong would recall peering out at grim depositors from the bank’s back room. “The consternation of the faces of the people in the line, many of them I knew, I shall never forget. I know that Harry left the building with a sense of dejection and defeat which it is quite impossible for me to describe.” Pierpont wrote off the Knickerbocker as hopeless and it failed on Tuesday afternoon, October 22.[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236121) “I can’t go on being everybody’s goat,” he said. “I’ve got to stop somewhere.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236290) A few weeks later, refused admission to see Pierpont, Charles Barney of the Knickerbocker shot himself, an act that produced a wave of suicides among the bank’s depositors.
随着恐慌蔓延到镇上的其他信托公司，皮尔庞特接管了救援行动。当别人陷入疑虑或恐惧时，危机反而更坚定他的自信。他组建了一个年轻银行家委员会，成员包括第一国民银行的亨利·波默罗伊·戴维森和银行家信托公司的本杰明·斯特朗。他派他们去审计尼克伯克信托的账目。后来，斯特朗成为纽约联邦储备银行大权在握的行长，回忆时提到自己曾从银行后窗望出去，看到那些面色阴沉的储户。"排着队的人们脸上那种惊恐的表情——其中许多人都与我相识——我永远无法忘怀。我知道哈里离开那栋楼时，内心充满了难以言表的沮丧与挫败感。"皮尔庞特断定尼克伯克信托已无药可救，于是在 10 月 22 日星期二下午，它宣告倒闭。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236121) "我不能一直当所有人的替罪羊，"他说，"总得有个止境。"” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236290) 几周后，尼克博克银行的查尔斯·巴尼因被拒见皮尔庞特而举枪自尽，这一举动在银行的储户中引发了一波自杀潮。

On Tuesday night, Pierpont and other bankers met at a Manhattan hotel with Treasury Secretary George B. Cortelyou, who pledged cooperation. The next day, Cortelyou put \$25 million in government funds at Pierpont’s disposal. It was an extraordinary transference of power to a private banker and further proof of Teddy Roosevelt’s high regard for Morgan.
周二晚上，皮尔庞特与其他银行家在曼哈顿的一家酒店与财政部长乔治·B·科特柳会面，科特柳承诺通力合作。次日，科特柳将 2500 万美元的政府资金交由皮尔庞特支配。这非同寻常地将权力移交给了这位私人银行家，也进一步证明了泰迪·罗斯福对摩根的器重。

The Knickerbocker’s failure triggered runs on other trusts, especially the Trust Company of America, which was just down Wall Street from the Morgan bank. On Wednesday, October 23, Pierpont summoned the trust presidents and tried to prod them into a rescue pool. It turned out they didn’t know one another, making it difficult for them to band together in a crisis. The situation illustrated why bankers believed implicitly in their old-boy networks. After Ben Strong delivered a favorable report on the Trust Company of America, Pierpont made his *ex cathedra* pronouncement: “This is the place to stop the trouble, then.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236444) Morgan, George F. Baker of First National Bank, and James Stillman of National City Bank provided \$3 million to save the Trust Company of America.
尼克博克信托公司的倒闭引发了其他信托公司的挤兑潮，尤其是位于华尔街摩根银行旁边的美国信托公司。10 月 23 日星期三，皮尔庞特召集各信托公司总裁，试图促成他们组建救援基金。结果发现这些人彼此并不相识，危机时刻难以联手协作。这一情况充分说明了银行家们为何深信其旧友圈子的重要性。当本·斯特朗提交了对美国信托公司有利的报告后，皮尔庞特以权威口吻宣布："那么，就该在这里阻止这场风波。"随后，摩根、第一国家银行的乔治·F·贝克以及国家城市银行的詹姆斯·斯蒂尔曼共同出资 300 万美元，拯救了美国信托公司。

For two weeks, Morgan and his associates stood fast against a spreading typhoon. As panic increased, depositors thronged banks across the city. People sat overnight in camp chairs, bringing food and waiting for the banks to open in the morning. New York police distributed numbers to people to save their places; in other cases, exhausted depositors paid enterprising standees to wait for them. (A later Wall Street eminence, Sidney Weinberg of Goldman, Sachs, earned \$10 a day holding down places in line.) To reduce withdrawals and avert the need for shutdowns, trust tellers counted out the money in slow motion, like people in a trance.
整整两周，摩根和他的伙伴们顶住了一场不断蔓延的风暴。随着恐慌加剧，全市各地的银行门前挤满了储户。人们自带折叠椅彻夜排队，备好食物等候银行清晨开门。纽约警察给人们分发号码牌以维持秩序；还有些疲惫不堪的储户花钱请能插队的闲人替自己排队。（后来成为华尔街大人物的高盛集团西德尼·温伯格，当时靠替人占位每天能赚 10 美元。）为了减少取款、避免银行被迫关门，信托出纳员像梦游一样慢吞吞地数着钞票。

Strapped for cash, the trusts called in margin loans from stock market speculators. The price of call money—that is, the interest rate on margin loans to buy stocks—zoomed to 150 percent. Nevertheless, there remained a shortage of ready funds. Perkins cabled Jack, who was in London: “At all times during the day there were frantic men and women in our offices, in every way giving evidence of the tremendous strain they were under.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236622) Pierpont was accosted by hundreds of distraught brokers who faced ruin and pleaded for help. Photographs of the Corner show dense throngs of men in derbies and dark coats, solidly massed along Wall Street in somber ranks. For these terrified men, Morgan emerged as the Redeemer, the one man who could save them. In a human wave, they surged right to the door of 23 Wall, where “the struggling mob fought their way on, all looking up at the windows of J. P. Morgan & Co.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236813)
资金紧张，信托公司向股市投机者追缴保证金贷款。通知放款利率——即用于购买股票的保证金贷款利率——飙升至 150%。尽管如此，现金仍然短缺。帕金斯给身在伦敦的杰克发电报说：“全天任何时候，我们办公室里都有狂躁的男男女女，他们的一举一动都显示出承受的巨大压力。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236622) 数百名面临破产的经纪商悲痛欲绝，拦住皮尔庞特恳求帮助。角落的照片显示，戴着圆顶礼帽、穿着深色大衣的人群密密麻麻，沿着华尔街阴沉地挤作一团。对这些惊恐万分的人来说，摩根宛如救世主，是唯一能拯救他们的人。人群如潮水般涌向华尔街 23 号的大门，“拥挤的暴民奋力前行，全都仰望着 J. P. Morgan & Co.的窗户。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236813)

On Thursday, October 24, with stock trading virtually halted, New York Stock Exchange president Ransom H. Thomas crossed Broad Street and told Morgan that unless \$25 million were raised immediately, at least fifty brokerage firms might fail. Thomas wanted to shut the Exchange. “At what time do you usually close it?” Morgan asked—though the Stock Exchange was twenty paces from his office, Pierpont didn’t know its hours: stock trading was vulgar. “Why, at three o’clock,” said Thomas. Pierpont wagged an admonitory finger. “It must not close one minute before that hour today.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2236979) At two o’clock, Morgan summoned the bank presidents and warned that dozens of brokerage houses might fail unless they mustered \$25 million within ten or twelve minutes. By 2:16, the money was pledged. Morgan then dispatched a team to the Stock Exchange floor to announce that call money would be available at as low as 10 percent. One team member, Amory Hodges, had his waistcoat torn off in the violent tumult. Then a blessed moment occurred in Morgan annals: as news of the rescue circulated through the Exchange, Pierpont heard a mighty roar across the street. Looking up, he asked the cause: he was being given an ovation by the jubilant floor traders.
10 月 24 日，周四，股市几乎陷入停滞，纽约证券交易所总裁兰塞姆·H·托马斯穿过百老汇大街，告诉摩根说，除非立即筹集 2500 万美元，否则至少五十家经纪公司可能倒闭。托马斯想关闭交易所。“你们通常几点关门？”摩根问道——尽管证券交易所离他的办公室只有二十步远，皮尔庞特却不知道它的营业时间：股票交易太粗俗。“呃，下午三点。”托马斯说。皮尔庞特摇了摇手指以示警告：“今天一分钟也不能提前关门。”两点钟，摩根召集银行行长们，警告说，除非他们在十到十二分钟内筹集到 2500 万美元，否则数十家经纪公司可能倒闭。到 2 点 16 分，款项已承诺到位。摩根随后派出一支团队前往证券交易所大厅，宣布通知贷款利率可低至 10%。团队成员之一阿莫里·霍奇斯在激烈的骚动中被扯掉了马甲。 随后，摩根家族编年史中迎来了一个幸运时刻：当救援消息在交易所传开时，皮尔庞特听到街对面传来震耳欲聋的欢呼声。他抬头询问缘由——原来，兴高采烈的场内交易员们正在为他起立鼓掌。

The next day, call money soared again to extortionate rates. Eight banks and trust companies had already failed during the week. Pierpont went to the New York Clearing House, the banker’s trade group for clearing checks, and got it to issue scrip as a temporary emergency currency to relieve the serious cash shortage. > Anyone who saw Mr. Morgan going from the Clearing House back to his office that day will never forget the picture. With his coat unbuttoned and flying open, a piece of white paper clutched tightly in his right hand, he walked fast down Nassau Street. That Friday night, Pierpont called in city religious leaders and asked them to preach calm in their Sunday sermons. Archbishop Farley held a special Sunday mass for businessmen. Grappling with a bad cold that had dogged him for days, Pierpont went up to Cragston for the weekend. On Monday, October 28, New York City mayor George B. McClellan came to the Morgan Library with another serious brush fire to extinguish. Alarmed by events on Wall Street, European investors were withdrawing money from America, and the city couldn’t place its warrants abroad. The city needed \$30 million to cover its obligations, McClellan said. Morgan, Baker, and Stillman agreed to provide the needed money—the first of four Morgan-led rescues of New York City in this century. In a bravura performance, the seventy-year-old Pierpont extemporaneously drafted a letter-perfect contract on Morgan Library stationery. He also demanded a bankers’ committee to monitor the city’s bookkeeping practices, a feature of later New York City crises as well.
那个周五的晚上，皮尔庞特召集了市里的宗教领袖，请他们在周日的布道中劝导人们保持冷静。法利大主教则为商界人士专门举行了一场周日弥撒。皮尔庞特被感冒缠身多日，周末回到克拉格斯顿休养。10 月 28 日，星期一，纽约市市长乔治·B·麦克莱伦又带着一个棘手的火情来到摩根图书馆——欧洲投资者被华尔街的动荡吓破了胆，纷纷从美国撤资，导致纽约市无法在国外发行债券。麦克莱伦表示，市里需要 3000 万美元来应付各项开支。摩根、贝克和斯蒂尔曼同意提供这笔资金——这是本世纪摩根主导的纽约市四次救助中的第一次。年届七旬的皮尔庞特即兴挥毫，在摩根图书馆的信笺上草拟了一份措辞无懈可击的合同，堪称精彩绝伦的表演。 他还要求成立一个银行家委员会来监督市政府的账目。这也成为了后来纽约市危机的一大特征。

For a seventy-year-old man with a bad cold, Pierpont handled the 1907 panic like a virtuoso. He sucked lozenges and worked nineteen-hour days. He said that he missed Jack. At moments, his physician, Dr. Markoe, plied his throat with sprays and gargles, as if the banker were an aging boxing champ being resuscitated between rounds. The doctor also extracted a pledge that Pierpont would cut down his cigar consumption to only twenty a day! When he dozed during an emergency meeting, nobody dared disturb the royal snooze. One banker “reached forward and lifted from the relaxed fingers, as one might take a rattle from a baby, the big cigar that was scorching the varnish on the table.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237282) For a half hour, he was fast asleep as bankers discussed a \$10-million loan.
对于一位年过七旬、身患重感冒的老人来说，皮尔庞特应对 1907 年恐慌的方式堪称大师级。他含着润喉糖，每天工作十九小时。他说他想念杰克。有时，他的私人医生马科博士会往他喉咙里喷药、让他漱口，仿佛这位银行家是位年迈的拳击冠军，在回合间隙接受复苏治疗。医生还让他承诺，每天抽雪茄不得超过二十支！当他在一次紧急会议中打起瞌睡时，没人敢打扰这位“皇家”小憩。一位银行家“伸手向前，从他松弛的手指间轻轻取下那支快把桌漆烧焦的大雪茄，动作就像从婴儿手中拿走拨浪鼓。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237282) 在他熟睡的半小时里，银行家们讨论着一笔一千万美元的贷款。

During the 1907 panic, Pierpont proved that American finance could aspire to high drama. In an elaborate finale on Saturday night, November 2, he devised a rescue for the still-shaky Trust Company of America, for Lincoln Trust, and for Moore and Schley, a speculative brokerage house that was \$25 million in debt. This last company held a gigantic majority stake in the Tennessee Coal and Iron Company as collateral against loans. If it had to liquidate that stake, it might collapse the stock market. If Moore and Schley, in turn, collapsed, it might topple other houses as well.
在 1907 年的恐慌中，皮尔庞特证明了美国金融也能上演一出大戏。11 月 2 日那个周六的夜晚，他精心策划了一场收官救援，对象包括仍摇摇欲坠的美国信托公司、林肯信托，以及负债 2500 万美元的投机经纪行摩尔与施莱。这家经纪行持有田纳西煤铁公司的巨额控股权作为贷款抵押——若被迫抛售这些股份，可能引发股市崩盘；而摩尔与施莱一旦倒下，其他机构也会跟着遭殃。

Like an impresario creating his theatrical masterpiece, Pierpont gathered the city’s bankers at his library. He settled commercial bankers in the East Room, beneath signs of the zodiac and a tapestry of the seven deadly sins, while in the West Room trust-company presidents sank into deep red couches and armchairs beneath the gaze of saints and Madonnas. In between, like lupiter above the fray, Pierpont played solitaire in Belle Greene’s office.
像一位戏剧大师在打造自己的舞台杰作，皮尔庞特将城里的银行家们召集到他的图书馆。他把商业银行家安排在东厅，头顶是黄道十二宫的标志和一幅描绘七宗罪的挂毯；而信托公司的总裁们则坐在西厅，深红色的沙发和扶手椅深陷其中，圣徒与圣母像的目光俯视着他们。而在两者之间，如同超然于纷争之上的朱庇特，皮尔庞特在贝儿·格林的办公室里玩着单人纸牌。

One spectator was Tom Lamont, now a vice-president of Bankers Trust. Then only an “experienced errand boy,” as he said, he was entranced by the pageantry. Of Pierpont’s successors, only Lamont would possess the flair to stage such events. He recalled: “A more incongruous meeting place for anxious bankers could hardly be imagined. In one room were lofty, magnificent tapestries hanging on the walls, rare Bibles and illuminated manuscripts of the Middle Ages filling the cases; in another, that collection of the Early Renaissance masters—Castagno, Ghirlandaio, Perugino, to mention only a few—the huge open fire, the door just ajar to the holy of holies where the original manuscripts were guarded.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237438)
一位旁观者是汤姆·拉蒙特，他后来成为银行家信托公司的副总裁。当时，正如他所说，他不过是个“经验丰富的跑腿小弟”，却被这场盛典深深吸引。在皮尔庞特的继任者中，唯有拉蒙特具备举办这类活动的天赋。他回忆道：“几乎找不到比这更不协调的场所，来让焦头烂额的银行家们会面了。一个房间里，墙上挂着高大华美的挂毯，书柜里摆满了珍稀的《圣经》和中世纪彩绘手稿；另一个房间则陈列着早期文艺复兴大师的作品——卡斯塔尼奥、吉兰达约、佩鲁吉诺，仅举几例——巨大的壁炉里火势正旺，通往那间保管原始手稿的至圣所的门半掩着。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237438)

To save Moore and Schley, Pierpont wanted some payoff for himself. With his usual sense of martyrdom, he felt it was his due. With his peculiar bifocal vision, he saw the panic as a time for both statesmanship and personal gain. At this point, he told friends that he had done enough and wanted some *quid pro quo.* He now took an appropriately big fee.
为了挽救摩尔和施莱公司，皮尔庞特希望自己也能从中捞些好处。带着他惯有的殉道者心态，他认为这是他应得的。凭借他那独特的双重眼光，他把这场恐慌看作既是展现政治家风范，又是谋求私利的好时机。此时，他告诉朋友们，他已经做得够多了，想要得到一些回报。于是，他收取了一笔相当可观的高额报酬。

Pierpont hatched a scheme that would save Moore & Schley, avert its need to sell the Tennessee Coal and Iron block in the open market, and benefit his favorite creation, U.S. Steel. He knew U.S. Steel could profit from Tennessee Coal’s huge iron ore and coal holdings in Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia. For antitrust reasons, it was a prize unattainable under ordinary circumstances. So he struck a deal: U.S. Steel would buy Tennessee Coal stock from Moore and Schley *if* the hesitant trust-company presidents assembled a \$25-million pool to protect the weaker trusts. What a characteristic mix of high and low motives\!
皮尔庞特策划了一个方案，既能挽救摩尔和施莱公司，避免其在公开市场上抛售田纳西煤铁公司的股票，又能让自己钟爱的美国钢铁公司受益。他知道，田纳西煤铁公司在田纳西、阿拉巴马和佐治亚拥有大量铁矿和煤矿，美国钢铁公司若能拿下这些资产，必将获利丰厚。但由于反垄断法的限制，在正常情况下，这是一块无法到手的肥肉。于是，他达成了这样一笔交易：如果那些犹豫不决的信托公司总裁们能凑集 2500 万美元的资金池来保护那些较脆弱的信托公司，那么美国钢铁公司就会从摩尔和施莱公司手中买下田纳西煤铁公司的股票。这真是高尚与卑劣动机交织的典型做派！

Ben Strong noticed that Pierpont had locked the enormous bronze doors and pocketed the key. He was up to his old tricks—confinement of adversaries, a deadline, the abrupt appearance of the menacing host after long hours of bargaining. At a quarter to five in the morning, Pierpont pushed a gold pen into the hand of Edward King, leader of the trust presidents. “Here’s the place, King. And here’s the pen.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237606) Beaten down by all-night bargaining, King and the other trust company presidents agreed to contribute to the \$25-million pool.
本·斯特朗注意到，皮尔庞特已经锁上了那扇巨大的青铜门，并把钥匙收进了口袋。他又在耍老把戏了——把对手关起来、设定最后期限、在漫长谈判后突然以威胁性的姿态现身。凌晨 4 点 45 分，皮尔庞特把一支金笔塞到信托公司总裁们的领袖爱德华·金手中，说道：“签这里，金，笔在这儿。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237606) 被整夜谈判折腾得精疲力竭的金和其他信托公司总裁只得同意出资，加入这 2.5 亿美元的联合基金。

On Sunday night, Henry Clay Frick and Judge Elbert Gary of U.S. Steel sped down to Washington on a midnight train. They traveled in a single Pullman car specially hitched up to a locomotive. They had to secure Roosevelt’s approval for U.S. Steel’s takeover of Tennessee Coal and Iron before the stock market opened on Monday morning. They ended up interrupting Roosevelt in the middle of his breakfast; mindful of the panic, TR said it was “no public duty of his to interpose any objections.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237773) In other words, the Sherman Antitrust Act wouldn’t be used against U.S. Steel. Five minutes before the stock market opened at 10:00 A.M., Gary called 23 Wall Street from the White House and told George Perkins that the president had agreed to the plan. The stock market rallied on the news.
周日晚，Henry Clay Frick 与 U.S. Steel 的 Judge Elbert Gary 搭乘午夜专列，飞驰南下华盛顿。他们乘坐的是一节专门加挂在一台机车后的 Pullman 车厢，必须在周一早上股市开盘前为 U.S. Steel 收购 Tennessee Coal and Iron 一事拿到罗斯福的首肯。最终他们打断了罗斯福的早餐；面对来势汹汹的恐慌，TR 表示，“他没有公职义务提出任何反对。” 换句话说，Sherman Antitrust Act 不会对 U.S. Steel 开刀。上午十点股市开盘前五分钟，Gary 从白宫致电华尔街 23 号，告诉 George Perkins 总统已同意该方案。消息传出，股市应声反弹。

Immediately, there were charges that Pierpont had duped Roosevelt into scuttling his antitrust policy and sanctioning, under duress, an anticompetitive steel merger. Wisconsin senator Robert La Follette even said the bankers had rigged up the panic for their own profit. Certainly the \$45-million distress sale price of Tennessee Coal and Iron was a steal. Financial analyst John Moody later said that the company’s property had a potential value of about \$1 billion. Grant B. Schley, head of Moore and Schley, also admitted later that his firm could have been rescued by an outright cash infusion rather than the purchase of the Tennessee Coal stock. So there was far more than altruism at work in the famous all-night rescue of the firm.
消息一出，立刻有人指控皮尔庞特诱骗罗斯福放弃反垄断政策，并在胁迫下批准了这场反竞争的钢铁合并案。威斯康星州参议员罗伯特·拉福莱特甚至直言，银行家们人为制造了这场恐慌，只为中饱私囊。田纳西煤铁公司以 4500 万美元的低价被迫出售，无疑是一笔白菜价交易。金融分析师约翰·穆迪后来表示，该公司的资产潜在价值约为 10 亿美元。摩尔-施利公司的负责人格兰特·B·施利后来也承认，他们公司本可以通过直接注入现金来脱困，而非必须收购田纳西煤铁的股票。可见，在那场著名的连夜拯救行动中，背后远不止是单纯的利他主义。

Despite this controversy, Pierpont reached the zenith of his influence with the 1907 panic. As his biographer Frederick Lewis Allen wrote, “Where there had been many principalities, there was now one kingdom, and it was Morgan’s.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237936) Pierpont was suddenly not a pirate but a sage. Woodrow Wilson, then president of Princeton University, said the nation should be advised on its future by a panel of intellectuals, and he recommended Pierpont Morgan as its chairman.[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238071) The tributes, nonetheless, coincided with new concern about America’s financial system. U.S. financial panics recurred with worrisome regularity, every ten years. The 1907 panic exposed many systemic defects. As people hoarded money and banks called in loans, there was no central bank to instill confidence or offset the sudden credit contraction. Sharp drops in the money supply then led to severe recessions. The country needed an elastic currency and a permanent lender of last resort.
尽管争议不断，皮尔庞特在 1907 年恐慌中达到了影响力的顶峰。正如他的传记作者弗雷德里克·刘易斯·艾伦所写：“从前诸侯割据，如今一统天下，而这一统者，便是摩根。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2237936) 皮尔庞特瞬间从海盗化身圣贤。时任普林斯顿大学校长的伍德罗·威尔逊表示，国家应由一群知识分子组成的小组为其未来出谋划策，并推荐皮尔庞特·摩根担任主席。 [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238071) 然而，这些赞誉也伴随着对美国金融体系的新忧虑。美国金融恐慌每隔十年便如期而至，其规律性令人不安。1907 年的恐慌暴露了许多系统性缺陷。当人们囤积货币、银行收回贷款时，没有一家中央银行来注入信心或抵消突如其来的信贷紧缩。货币供应量的骤降进而引发了严重的经济衰退。这个国家迫切需要一种弹性货币和一个永久性的最后贷款人。

From the ashes of 1907 arose the Federal Reserve System: everybody saw that thrilling rescues by corpulent old tycoons were a tenuous prop for the banking system. Senator Nelson W. Aldrich declared, “Something has got to be done. We may not always have Pierpont Morgan with us to meet a banking crisis.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238239) By confirming his storied powers, Pierpont also inadvertently fostered talk of an omnipotent Wall Street money trust. President Roosevelt now recommended federal regulation of the stock exchanges, while New York governor Charles Evans Hughes wanted margin requirements raised from 10 to 20 percent. If these suggestions had been enacted, the country might have been spared some of the lurid excesses of the 1929 crash.
1907 年的金融废墟中诞生了美联储：所有人都看到，那些大腹便便的老大亨们的惊天救援不过是银行体系的一根脆弱支柱。参议员尼尔森·W·奥尔德里奇宣称：“必须采取行动了。我们不可能总是指望皮尔庞特·摩根来应对银行危机。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238239) 皮尔庞特在证实自己传奇实力的同时，也意外地助长了关于华尔街万能金钱托拉斯的议论。罗斯福总统如今建议联邦政府监管证券交易所，而纽约州长查尔斯·埃文斯·休斯则希望将保证金要求从 10%提高到 20%。如果这些建议当时得以实施，美国或许能免于 1929 年股市崩盘中那些触目惊心的疯狂行径。

The one direct consequence of the 1907 panic was a universal clamor for banking reform. In 1908, Congress passed the Aldrich-Vreeland Currency Act, which created the National Monetary Commission to study changes in the banking system. The commission was chaired by Senator Aldrich of Rhode Island, and the House of Morgan quickly moved to exert influence on it. Perkins cabled Pierpont in London that he and George F. Baker, the walrus-mustached head of the First National Bank, had stayed away from Washington, lest the new legislation be seen as a Wall Street plot. At the same time, Perkins sent a coded cable saying that Harry Davison, Baker’s young protégé, would be Aldrich’s adviser: “It is understood that Davison is to represent our views and will be particularly close to Senator Aldrich.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238395) Davison had been Pierpont’s cool lieutenant during the 1907 panic and had greatly impressed him. When the Aldrich commission was about to depart for a tour of Europe’s central banks, Davison went ahead to confer with Pierpont, who wanted a private central bank on the Bank of England model. Davison would be the only banker to accompany the senators and congressmen on their mission.
1907 年恐慌的一个直接后果，就是全社会对银行改革的强烈呼吁。1908 年，国会通过了《奥尔德-弗里兰货币法案》，并成立了国家货币委员会来研究银行体系的变革。该委员会由罗德岛州的参议员奥尔德担任主席，而摩根财团迅速对其施加影响。珀金斯电告身在伦敦的皮尔庞特，他和长着海象胡子的第一国民银行总裁乔治·F·贝克都避开了华盛顿，以免新法案被视为华尔街的阴谋。与此同时，珀金斯还发了一封加密电报，称贝克年轻的门徒哈里·戴维森将担任奥尔德的顾问：“大家都明白，戴维森将代表我们的立场，并会与奥尔德参议员保持特别密切的关系。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238395) 戴维森在 1907 年恐慌期间曾是皮尔庞特冷静的得力助手，给他留下了极深的印象。 当奥尔德里奇委员会即将启程考察欧洲各国央行时，戴维森先行一步与皮尔庞特商讨。皮尔庞特希望建立一家类似英格兰银行的私人中央银行。戴维森将是此次任务中唯一陪同参众两院议员出行的银行家。

A central bank was by no means supported by all Democrats. William Jennings Bryan and the Populists feared that a central bank would be dominated by the same hard-money men who ran Wall Street. They saw it as an institution that would slay the silverites. In many ways, the concept was associated more with conservative, hard-money men. Pierpont was amenable to central banks so long as they were private and had boards composed of bankers. As Pierpont’s man on the commission, Davison reflected his mentor’s uncompromising preference for banker rather than politician control of a central bank. He also expected such a bank to introduce a “level playing field” and end the competitive advantage of the trusts.
建立中央银行的主张绝非得到所有民主党人的支持。威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩与民粹主义者担心，中央银行会落入掌控华尔街的同一批金本位强硬派之手。他们认为这家机构将扼杀白银派。从许多方面来看，这一构想更多地与保守的金本位强硬派相关联。皮尔庞特支持建立中央银行，前提是这些银行必须为私营性质，且董事会由银行家组成。作为皮尔庞特在委员会中的代言人，戴维森反映了其导师的坚定立场：宁愿由银行家而非政客掌控中央银行。他也期望这样的银行能引入"公平竞争环境"，终结托拉斯的竞争优势。

In November 1910, in what was billed to the press as a “duck-shooting holiday,” Davison (now a Morgan partner) and other Wall Street bankers met secretly at the Jekyll Island Club, a palm-shaded seaside compound of turreted buildings off the Georgia coast and a favorite Morgan hideaway. Known as the resort of the one hundred millionaires, Jekyll Island claimed among its organizers Pierpont’s chum George F. Baker. Pierpont kept an apartment in its San Souci building. The Jekyll Island meeting would be the fountain of a thousand conspiracy theories. Here Wall Street bankers worked out their plan for a central bank under private aegis, a system of regional reserve banks topped by a governing board of commercial bankers. Davison, an architect of the meeting, not only got a suspicious stationmaster in Brunswick, Georgia, to keep quiet about his suspicions, but often led the discussion. As Paul M. Warburg of Kuhn, Loeb, one of the key theoreticians at the meeting, later said, “Davison had an uncanny gift in sensing the proper moment for changing the topic, for giving the discussion a timely new turn, thus avoiding a clash or deadlock.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238570)
1910 年 11 月，一场被媒体粉饰为“猎鸭假期”的秘密会议在杰基尔岛俱乐部悄然举行。彼时已是摩根合伙人的戴维森，与其他华尔街银行家齐聚于这座棕榈树掩映、塔楼耸立的海滨庄园——它地处佐治亚州海岸外，是摩根钟爱的隐秘据点。杰基尔岛号称“亿万富翁度假胜地”，其组织者中便有皮尔庞特的密友乔治·F·贝克，而皮尔庞特本人则在俱乐部的“无忧宫”楼里保留着一套寓所。这场杰基尔岛会议，日后成了无数阴谋论的源头。正是在这里，华尔街银行家们密谋制定了一套由私人掌控的中央银行方案：以地区储备银行为底层架构，顶层由商业银行家组成的理事会进行监管。作为会议的设计者之一，戴维森不仅让佐治亚州布伦瑞克那位疑心重重的站长封口沉默，还时常主导讨论进程。正如保罗·M· 库恩-勒布公司的沃伯格是会议的重要理论家之一，他后来说：“戴维森有一种不可思议的天赋，能感知到改变话题的恰当时机，及时为讨论注入新方向，从而避免冲突或僵局。”” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238570)

When Senator Aldrich presented his bill for a central bank to Congress in 1910, the Democrats blocked it. In 1913, Congressman Carter Glass, a Virginia Democrat, used it as the basis for the Federal Reserve Act, although making extensive modifications. President Wilson successfully demanded that the system of twelve private regional reserve banks be placed under a central political authority, a Washington board that would include the Treasury secretary and presidential appointees. Progressives hoped the Federal Reserve would reduce the House of Morgan’s unique power. As we shall see, the truth was far more complex, for the bank would skillfully harness the Fed and use it to amplify its powers. In an ironic outcome unforeseen by reformers, it would become the private bank of choice for central banks throughout the world, giving it an incalculable new advantage.
1910 年，参议员奥尔德里奇向国会提交了建立中央银行的法案，但遭到民主党人否决。1913 年，弗吉尼亚州民主党众议员卡特·格拉斯以该法案为蓝本，在进行了大幅修改后，提出了《联邦储备法案》。威尔逊总统成功要求将十二家地区性私有储备银行置于一个中央政治权力机构之下——一个设在华盛顿的委员会，成员包括财政部长和总统任命的人选。进步派人士原本希望美联储能削弱摩根财团的独大势力。然而，正如我们将看到的，真相远比想象中复杂——摩根财团巧妙地驾驭了美联储，并借其放大了自身权力。改革者们始料未及的是，美联储竟阴差阳错地成了摩根财团服务全球央行的专属私人银行，为其带来了无可估量的全新优势。

WHEN the Republican president William Howard Taft took office in 1909, the wily George Perkins flattered himself, thinking that he had already wormed his way into its inner council. Taft sent him a confidential draft of his inaugural address, which was “in all respects conciliatory and harmonizing in tone,” Perkins reported to Pierpont.[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238721) He felt convinced Taft would water down the troublesome Sherman Antitrust Act. In coded cables to Morgan, who was vacationing in Egypt, Perkins made it sound as if he alone had picked the new cabinet. “Acting on suggestion made solely by me 2 weeks ago Franklin Mac-Veagh Chicago has been selected for Secretary of Treasury. Wicker-sham will be Attorney General and other places are filled to our entire satisfaction.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238892)
1909 年，共和党总统威廉·霍华德·塔夫脱上任时，狡猾的乔治·珀金斯自鸣得意，以为他已经成功混入了核心圈子。塔夫脱把就职演说的机密草稿发给他，珀金斯向皮尔庞特报告称，这份草稿“在各个方面都充满安抚与和谐的语气”。 [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238721) 他深信塔夫脱会淡化麻烦的《谢尔曼反托拉斯法》。在给正在埃及度假的摩根发的密电中，珀金斯暗示内阁人选全是他一人钦定。“完全按照我两周前独自提出的建议，芝加哥的富兰克林·麦克维被选为财政部长。威克沙姆将担任司法部长，其他职位的人选也让我们相当满意。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2238892)

Yet the one-term Taft administration would be deeply ambivalent toward the House of Morgan. On the surface, it would seem even more hostile than Roosevelt’s and surprisingly aggressive in battling the trusts. It filed antitrust suits against two cherished Morgan progeny—U.S. Steel and International Harvester. The Taft years also saw the dismemberment of John D. Rockefeller’s Standard Oil trust and James B. Duke’s American Tobacco trust. For all his windy attacks on the trusts, Teddy Roosevelt had been far more circumspect about translating his words into tough action.
然而，任期仅一届的塔夫脱政府对摩根家族的态度却相当矛盾。表面上，其敌意甚至超过罗斯福政府，在打击托拉斯方面也出人意料地强硬。它针对摩根家族引以为傲的两大产业——美国钢铁公司（U.S. Steel）和国际收割机公司（International Harvester）——提起了反垄断诉讼。在塔夫脱执政期间，约翰·D·洛克菲勒的标准石油信托（Standard Oil）和詹姆斯·B·杜克的美国烟草信托（American Tobacco）也被强制拆分。尽管西奥多·罗斯福曾对托拉斯大肆抨击，但他在将言论转化为强硬行动方面却远比塔夫脱谨慎。

Yet there was more to the Taft-Morgan relationship than a progressive crusade against a Wall Street cabal. If trust-busting made good political theater, the deeper story was one of foreign collaboration. Even as Washington chastised the banks at home, it was forging them into foreign-loan syndicates in a new age of dollar diplomacy. With the U.S. defeat of Spain and the colonization of the Philippines and Puerto Rico, the country had acquired a new taste for imperialist adventure, and the House of Morgan would be one of its main instruments.
然而，塔夫脱与摩根之间的关系远不止是一场针对华尔街金融集团的进步主义讨伐。如果说反托拉斯是一场精彩的政治戏剧，那么更深层的故事则是关于外国合作的。就在华盛顿谴责国内银行的同时，它却在美元外交的新时代里，将它们整合成对外贷款银团。随着美国战胜西班牙并殖民菲律宾和波多黎各，这个国家开始对帝国主义冒险产生新的兴趣，而摩根财团将成为其主要工具之一。

Henceforth, much of the Morgan saga revolves around incestuous dealings between the Morgan banks in New York and London and their respective governments, intrigue that would drape them in mysterious new raiment. The Baronial Age was one of unbridled *laissez-faire*, marked by often unqualified hostility on the part of bankers toward government. But in the dawning Diplomatic Age, there would be an explicit fusion of financial and government power. In time, it would become hard to disentangle the House of Morgan from various aspects of Anglo-American policy. Yet there would also be spectacular instances in which Morgan policy would take on a clandestine life of its own, diverging from official dictates.
此后，摩根传奇的许多篇章都围绕着纽约与伦敦的摩根银行及其各自政府之间错综复杂的权钱交易展开，这些阴谋为它们披上了神秘的新外衣。男爵时代是毫无节制的自由放任时期，银行家们对政府抱有普遍且毫不掩饰的敌意。然而，在初露曙光的外交时代，金融权力与政府权力开始明确融合。久而久之，摩根财团与英美政策的方方面面变得难以分割。但也不乏惊人案例，摩根的政策悄然发展出自身隐秘的路径，偏离了官方的指令。

The new alliance was mutually advantageous. Washington wanted to harness the new financial power to coerce foreign governments into opening their markets to American goods or adopting pro-American policies. The banks, in turn, needed levers to force debt repayment and welcomed the government’s police powers in distant places. The threat of military intervention was an excellent means by which to speed loan repayment. When Kuhn, Loeb considered a loan to the Dominican Republic, backed by customs receipts, Jacob Schiff inquired of his London associate Sir Ernest Cassel, “If they do not pay, who will collect these customs duties?” Cassel replied, “Your marines and ours.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239072)
新联盟对双方都有利。华盛顿希望借助这股新的金融力量，迫使外国政府向美国商品开放市场或采取亲美政策。而银行则需要施压手段以迫使债务国还款，并欢迎政府在这些遥远地区行使警察权。军事干预的威胁成为加速贷款偿还的绝佳手段。当库恩-洛布公司考虑向多米尼加共和国提供由关税收入担保的贷款时，雅各布·希夫询问他在伦敦的合伙人欧内斯特·卡塞尔爵士：“如果他们不还钱，谁来征收这些关税？”卡塞尔答道：“你们的海军陆战队，还有我们的。”

During its first year, the Taft administration recruited the House of Morgan in a scheme to create a financial protectorate over Honduras and bail out British bondholders at the same time. As part of a debt settlement, the bank would buy up old Honduran bonds, which were selling at a steep discount in London. Secretary of State Philander Knox would then impose an American lien on Honduran custom-house receipts and sell new Honduran bonds through a Morgan syndicate. The scheme would be backed up by American military might. Although Senator William Alden Smith, for one, was irate that the State Department supported the Morgan scheme, the bank had actually been dragooned by the government. Serving only prime government clients, the House of Morgan had a supercilious attitude toward small, backward countries. As Jack said in a cable to the London office, “Negotiations only undertaken because U.S. Government anxious get Honduras settled.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239207) He and Harry Davison refused to proceed without a treaty that provided ironclad guarantees for the bonds. After enraged mobs besieged the Honduran assembly, protesting threats to their sovereignty, the U.S. Senate vetoed the deal, and the operation was scrapped.
在执政的第一年，塔夫脱政府就拉拢摩根银行，策划在洪都拉斯建立一个金融保护国，同时解救英国债券持有人。作为债务解决方案的一部分，摩根银行将收购洪都拉斯旧债券——这些债券在伦敦市场上正以大幅折价交易。随后，国务卿菲兰德·诺克斯将对洪都拉斯海关收入施加美国留置权，并通过摩根银团发行新的洪都拉斯债券。这一计划将由美国的军事力量作为后盾。尽管参议员威廉·奥尔登·史密斯等人对国务院支持摩根方案感到愤怒，但实际上，这家银行是被政府硬拉上船的。摩根银行只服务于顶级政府客户，对弱小落后国家本就态度傲慢。正如杰克在发给伦敦办事处的电报中所说：“之所以参与谈判，完全是因为美国政府急于解决洪都拉斯的问题。”” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239207) 他和哈里·戴维森坚持，如果没有一份能为债券提供铁腕担保的条约，就绝不推进此事。愤怒的民众包围了洪都拉斯议会，抗议对其主权的威胁，而美国参议院否决了该协议，这项行动也就此告吹。

The new era was most vividly adumbrated in China. As with Honduras, the House of Morgan had no great relish for such a foreign operation. Backward and sprawling, lacking a central army and modern budgeting, *fin-de-siècle* China had proved exasperating for foreign bankers. Its officials excelled in playing off one group of foreign creditors against another. (The bankers were accused of exploiting the same strategy with Chinese officials.) This not only bred resentment among bankers but fostered a decided Wall Street prejudice in favor of China’s ancient enemy, Japan.
新时代的缩影在中国展现得最为鲜明。与洪都拉斯的情况类似，摩根财团对这类海外操作并无太大兴致。世纪之交的中国，落后而松散，缺乏统一的中央军队和现代化的预算制度，令外国银行家们头疼不已。其官员在挑拨不同外国债权人之间的关系上可谓炉火纯青。（银行家们也被指责对中国官员使用同样的策略。）这不仅引发了银行家们的不满，还让华尔街普遍偏向了中国的宿敌——日本。

The French, Germans, and British were already well entrenched in China, controlling their own spheres of influence. The European bankers had entered the picture in the late nineteenth century, when provincial Chinese merchants lacked the necessary capital to build railroads. In 1899, Secretary of State John Hay had declared an “open door” policy toward China that was supposed to guarantee unrestricted foreign access. Under Taft, however, the open door was converted into a blunt U.S. demand for inclusion in China on an equal basis with the European powers.
法国、德国和英国早已在中国站稳脚跟，掌控着各自的势力范围。19 世纪末，当中国地方商人缺乏修建铁路所需资金时，欧洲银行家便趁势介入。1899 年，美国国务卿约翰·海宣布对华"门户开放"政策，本意是确保外国资本能不受限制地进入中国。然而到了塔夫脱任内，这一开放政策却变成了美国直截了当的要求——必须与欧洲列强平起平坐地参与中国事务。

In 1909, the State Department prodded a reluctant Wall Street to undertake Chinese business. A consortium of British, French, and German banks had nearly completed negotiations for a \$25-million loan for the Hukuang Railway, which ran from Shanghai to Canton. Much to the European’s dismay, the State Department demanded an equal share for U.S. bankers. As Herbert Croly wrote, “The majority of these bankers had gone into the Group not because they were seeking Chinese investments but in order to oblige the administration.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239392)
1909 年，美国国务院敦促原本并不情愿的华尔街涉足中国事务。由英、法、德三国银行组成的财团已就一笔 2500 万美元的湖广铁路贷款谈判接近尾声——该铁路连接上海与广州。令欧洲人颇感意外的是，美国国务院要求为美国银行家争取同等份额。正如赫伯特·克罗利所写："这些银行家中的大多数人加入财团并非为了寻求在华投资，而是为了配合政府的安排。" [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239392)

The State Department placed the House of Morgan at the head of an American Bankers Group that included Kuhn, Loeb, the National City Bank, and the First National Bank. Only a few years before, these firms had viciously quarreled during the Northern Pacific corner. Now Washington was welding them into an instrument of national purpose, believing that banker unity would magnify American influence abroad. When Jack cabled his father in London about the arrangement, Pierpont couldn’t suppress his competitive instincts. “Strikes me favorably,” he responded, “but, strictly confidential and for your own use only, important J. P. M. & Co. take lead and name mentioned first. Suppose fact already recognized but must not be overlooked.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239554)
美国国务院将摩根财团置于一个美国银行家集团之首，该集团包括库恩-洛布公司、国家城市银行和第一国民银行。就在几年前，这些公司在北太平洋铁路争夺战中还激烈争吵。如今，华盛顿正将它们熔铸为国家目标的工具，相信银行家的团结能放大美国在海外的影响力。当杰克致电伦敦的父亲告知这一安排时，皮尔庞特抑制不住竞争本能。“我赞成，”他回复道，“但严格保密，仅供你参考，重要的是 J. P. M. & Co.要带头且名列首位。想必事实已得到认可，但不可忽视。”

The American Group met at 23 Wall Street, with Harry Davison in the chair but the State Department pulling the strings. Ordinarily commanding and good-humored, Davison chafed at the controls. He instructed Teddy Grenfell in London, “Think it would be very wise if you would casually but firmly point out to those with whom you come in contact that this is a proposition of the Government and not of the Bankers.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239732) The popular press applauded the latest salvo in the Morgan-White House wars and fancied that trustbusters now had bankers on the run. Meanwhile, Davison moaned: “Continue to be governed entirely by wishes of State Department.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239887) For bankers who had prided themselves in their fierce independence from government, this new strait-jacket was hard to tolerate.
美国集团在华尔街 23 号召开会议，由哈里·戴维森主持，但国务院才是幕后操盘手。一向强势且幽默的戴维森对受人掣肘感到恼火。他指示伦敦的特迪·格伦费尔：“你最好不动声色但坚定地向接触的人指出，这是政府的提案，而非银行家的。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239732) 大众媒体为摩根与白宫之间的最新交锋叫好，认为反垄断者如今已让银行家们节节败退。与此同时，戴维森哀叹道：“一切继续完全听从国务院的意愿。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2239887) 对于曾以独立于政府而自豪的银行家们来说，这种新的束缚实在难以忍受。

Teddy Grenfell, partner in J. S. Morgan and Company (soon to be Morgan Grenfell) represented the American group in its dealings with the British, French, and German banker groups of the China consortium. Now and in the future, he would be an important intermediary between 23 Wall Street and the British government. Bolted together internally, the Morgan banks acted autonomously in many matters. It was a tricky situation, fraught with conflicts, for the New York and London houses were always sensitive to requests from their respective governments. In 1908, for example, J. S. Morgan and Company withheld a Turkish loan at the Foreign Office’s behest, then extended it the following year when bureaucratic winds shifted. So long as British and American interests coincided, this situation posed no problem. But a conflict was buried here that would later tear apart the Anglo-American Morgan empire. However much it might camouflage it, the House of Morgan wasn’t a multinational bank but an American bank with partnerships abroad. Many times, it would be impossible to appease both the United States and Britain.
泰迪·格伦费尔，J.S.摩根公司（即将更名为摩根建富）的合伙人，代表美国集团与英、法、德三国银行团处理中国财团相关事务。无论现在还是将来，他都将成为华尔街 23 号与英国政府之间的重要中间人。摩根银行在内部紧密相连，但在许多事务上又各自为政。这种局面微妙而充满矛盾，因为纽约和伦敦的机构始终对各自政府的要求十分敏感。例如在 1908 年，J.S.摩根公司应英国外交部要求暂缓了一笔土耳其贷款，次年又因政策风向转变而重新发放。只要英美利益一致，这种状况便不成问题。但其中埋藏的冲突日后终将撕裂英美的摩根帝国。 无论怎样伪装，摩根财团本质上都不是一家跨国银行，而是一家与海外有合作关系的美国银行。 很多时候，想要同时讨好美国和英国是根本不可能的。

From 1909 to 1913, the American Group served as a conduit for all Morgan dealings with China. Its representative in China was the most dashing, adventurous agent in Morgan history—Willard Dickerman Straight. Straight’s life reads like a spy thriller. Fresh out of Cornell, he worked for the Imperial Maritime Customs Service in Peking and studied Mandarin. In 1904, he went to Japan to report on the Russo-Japanese War for Reuters and the Associated Press. A friend in those years described him as “tall, slim, with reddish-brown hair, of unusual frankness and charm of manner.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240013) While reporting from Seoul, Korea, he met Edward H. Harriman at a dinner, an experience that transformed his life. Harriman then controlled the Union Pacific Railroad and the Pacific Mail steamship line, which he saw as the first two legs of a round-the-world transportation system. He recruited the enthusiastic Straight to win the critical China rail link. Then, in 1906, Teddy Roosevelt invited Straight to the White House, saying he was signing up bright young Ivy Leaguers to join the Foreign Service and drum up business for American companies abroad. To assist Harriman’s venture, Roosevelt assigned Straight—then only in his twenties—to be the U.S. consul general in Mukden, a bustling rail center in Manchuria. He would be the sole State Department representative north of the Great Wall.
从 1909 年到 1913 年间，美国集团充当了摩根家族所有对华交易的媒介。其在华代表是摩根历史上最潇洒、最具冒险精神的代理人——威拉德·迪克曼·斯特雷特。斯特雷特的人生经历读起来就像一部谍战小说。刚从康奈尔大学毕业，他便入职北京的海关总税务司署，并学习汉语。1904 年，他前往日本为路透社和美联社报道日俄战争。一位当年的朋友形容他“身材高挑、瘦削，红棕色头发，举止格外坦诚而富有魅力。”在韩国首尔报道期间，他在一次晚宴上结识了爱德华·H·哈里曼，这次相遇改变了他的人生。当时哈里曼控制着联合太平洋铁路公司和太平洋邮船公司，他认为这两者是环球运输系统的前两段。他将充满热情的斯特雷特招至麾下，以争取至关重要的中国铁路连接权。 接着，在 1906 年，西奥多·罗斯福邀请斯特莱特到白宫，表示他正在招募常春藤盟校的年轻才俊加入外交部门，为美国企业在海外开拓业务。为了协助哈里曼的事业，罗斯福任命当时才二十多岁的斯特莱特担任美国驻奉天总领事——奉天是满洲一个繁忙的铁路枢纽。他将成为国务院在长城以北地区的唯一代表。

In those days, Manchuria was colorfully described as the cockpit of Asia, the place where Russian and Japanese imperial interests clashed and European powers vied for influence. Nobody could have savored this romantic crossroads more than Willard Straight. He was an improbable mix of frank imperialist and young idealist, viewing American bankers as a buffer against Japanese and Russian encroachment in Manchuria. Cloaking dollar diplomacy in a mantle of altruism, he thought unity among foreign bankers would prevent any single country from exploiting China. This argument would eventually be exposed as a self-serving American delusion. But Straight was young and ardent and easily convinced himself of his mission of salvation.
当年，满洲被生动地描绘成亚洲的决斗场——俄日帝国利益在此碰撞，欧洲列强竞相争夺影响力。没有人比威拉德·斯特雷特更能品味这片充满浪漫色彩的十字路口。他本身就是一个矛盾体：既是坦率的帝国主义者，又是年轻的理想主义者。在他眼中，美国银行家是抵御日俄染指满洲的屏障。他将金元外交披上利他主义的外衣，认为外国银行家的团结能防止任何单一国家掠夺中国。这一论调后来被证明不过是美国一厢情愿的错觉。但年轻的斯特雷特满腔热忱，轻易就说服自己肩负着拯救的使命。

An intimate of mandarins in the Manchu court, he had a poetic sensibility, sketching watercolors of queued street vendors and illustrating a book about China. He sang Kiplingesque lyrics as he strummed his guitar and loved the themes of imperial conquest. His letters were spiced with vivid, exotic imagery, describing China as “the storm center of world politics,” a place “where everyone more or less is spying on everyone else.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240193) In 1909, he met one of America’s richest heiresses, Dorothy Whitney, and they became engaged two years later. She was the orphaned daughter of William C. Whitney, a former navy secretary who had made a fortune in tobacco, traction, automobiles, and stock market speculation, and she had inherited \$7 million. Recently president of the Junior League in New York, she was touring China when she met Straight. She had a wild, romantic sensibility that matched his own. In Peking, she recalled, they “walked along the city wall at sunset time and watched the soft glow of the distant purple hills.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240355) Dorothy and Willard Straight would pass through the turbulence of revolutionary China with the cool insouciance of a couple in an elegant Hollywood farce.
他是满清朝廷官员的密友，富有诗意，曾用水彩画描绘街头小贩队列，并为一部关于中国的书籍绘制插图。他弹着吉他吟唱吉卜林风格的歌词，对帝国征服的主题情有独钟。他的信函中充满了生动奇异的异域意象，将中国描述为“世界政治的暴风眼”，一个“人人几乎都在互相窥探”的地方。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240193) 1909 年，他邂逅了美国最富有的女继承人之一多萝西·惠特尼，两年后两人订婚。她是前海军部长威廉·C·惠特尼的遗孤，其父在烟草、电车、汽车及股市投机中积累巨额财富，留给她 700 万美元遗产。她时任纽约青年联盟主席，在游历中国期间结识了斯特雷特。她身上奔放浪漫的气质，恰与他相契。 在北京，她回忆道，他们“在日落时分沿着城墙漫步，凝望远山泛起柔和的紫色光晕。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240355) 多萝西和威拉德·斯特雷特夫妇以一种好莱坞优雅闹剧中主角般的从容不迫，穿过了革命中国的动荡激流。

In 1909, Straight was appointed representative of the American Bankers Group. He had enough youthful idealism to be disturbed by much of what he saw within the group. During the summer of 1910, he worked at 23 Wall Street—he thought the address a good omen, because the street number was the same as Dorothy’s birthday—and was appalled at the way the House of Morgan bossed around the State Department. Davison might chafe at government control, but Straight saw things quite differently. When Pierpont instructed Davison, “You might as well make it clear that when we want to discuss things with the U.S. Government we want \[the secretary of state\] and not \[the assistant secretary\],”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240535) Straight commented sardonically, “It was not difficult to see where the real power lies in this country.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240670) Pierpont might have been so imperious because the secretary of state was Philander C. Knox, who, as attorney general under Roosevelt, had filed the suit against the Northern Securities Company. Knox dutifully came to 23 Wall whenever he wished to speak to the American Group.
1909 年，司戴德被任命为美国银行家集团代表。他当时尚带着青年理想主义，对集团内的诸多现象深感不安。1910 年夏天，他在华尔街 23 号工作——他视这个地址为吉兆，因为门牌号恰好与多萝西的生日相同——摩根财团颐指气使地操控国务院的做派令他震惊。戴维森或许对政府管制感到不满，但司戴德却有截然不同的看法。当皮尔庞特指示戴维森：“你最好把话说清楚：如果我们想与美国政府谈事情，我们要见的是（国务卿），而不是（助理国务卿）”时， [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240535) 司戴德讽刺地评论道：“不难看出这个国家的实权掌握在谁手里。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240670) 皮尔庞特之所以如此专横，或许是因为当时的国务卿是菲兰德·C·诺克斯——此人曾任罗斯福总统任内的司法部长，正是他提起了针对北方证券公司的诉讼。 诺克斯每当想与美国集团谈话时，都会恪尽职守地来到华尔街 23 号。

In 1910, the China enterprise expanded beyond the railway loan to include a massive \$50-million loan to China for currency reform. Willard rhapsodized about the new loan to Dorothy: “It’s history . . . and big history at that—the game for an empire.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240796) The Chinese objected to a provision that required a Western adviser as a new overseer of Chinese finances. As a compromise, a Dutchman was unobtrusively slipped into the post. In 1911, Straight and representatives from England, France, and Germany signed the loan with Chinese officials. Willard wrote excitedly to Dorothy, “We’ve arranged it so that we can practically dictate the terms of China’s currency reform. When you think of holding the whip hand in formulating the first real sound financial basis for a country of 400 million, it’s quite a proposition.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240931)
1910 年，对华业务从铁路贷款扩展到了高达 5000 万美元的货币改革贷款。威拉德热切地对多萝西描述这笔新贷款：“这是历史……而且是重大历史——一场争夺帝国的游戏。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240796) 中方反对其中要求西方顾问作为中国金融新监督者的条款。作为妥协，一位荷兰人被悄然安排到这个职位上。1911 年，司戴德与英、法、德代表共同签署了这笔贷款。威拉德兴奋地写信给多萝西：“我们已经安排妥当，几乎可以全权决定中国货币改革的条件。想想看，为一个四亿人口的国家制定第一个真正稳健的金融基础，而我们的主导权在握，这真是一件了不得的大事。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2240931)

The loan generated worldwide publicity and made Straight an instant hero. Along with his prestigious association with the House of Morgan, the China loan helped reconcile Dorothy’s family to her marrying beneath her social station. Teddy Roosevelt interceded to plead Willard’s cause. Dorothy belonged to the polo-playing set of Locust Valley and Westbury, two Long Island communities rich in Morgan partners. Robert Bacon and his wife had been almost substitute parents after her own parents died, and she knew Pierpont as well. “Dear Mr. J.P. he’s such a sweetie underneath the sternness,” she wrote to Willard.[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241099) In fact, Straight may have clung to the Morgan position longer than he wanted to because of its social utility.
这笔贷款引起了全球关注，使斯特雷特一举成名。凭借他与摩根银行声望卓著的合作关系，中国贷款一事也帮助多萝西的家人接受了她这场"下嫁"的婚姻。西奥多·罗斯福曾亲自出面为威拉德说情。多萝西属于洛斯特谷和韦斯特伯里的马球圈子——这两个长岛社区聚居着众多摩根合伙人。在亲生父母去世后，罗伯特·培根夫妇几乎充当了她代理父母的角色，她也与皮尔庞特熟识。"亲爱的 J.P.先生，他外表严厉，骨子里却是个温柔的人，"她在给威拉德的信中写道。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241099) 事实上，斯特雷特之所以在摩根的位置上坚持那么久，或许正是看中了这份关系带来的社会便利。

Straight’s naive hopes about the China loan were soon to be dashed by geopolitical realities. He and the bankers had cast their lot with the corrupt Manchu dynasty, which was oblivious to turmoil beyond the palace walls. Straight himself grew disillusioned with the “selfish, narrow-minded bigotry” of the Chinese officials. Yet he wanted to perpetuate the Manchu dynasty to save the loan. He was caught up with the wrong issues; he was worrying about the composition of banking syndicates and missed the popular revulsion from *all* foreign bankers. At a Paris conference on China’s finances in 1912, the Japanese and Russians demanded—and obtained—inclusion in the China consortium. This was Straight’s nightmare: the group now included China’s traditional enemies. Bankers, he saw, couldn’t operate in a void but were enmeshed in larger political forces. Gloomily he foresaw “the inevitable day when China’s finances will be administered like Egypt’s—by an international board. Another dream shattered!”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241279)
斯特莱特对中国贷款的幼稚幻想很快就被地缘政治的现实击得粉碎。他和银行家们押注于腐败的清王朝，而清廷对宫墙外的动荡浑然不觉。斯特莱特本人也逐渐对中国官员“自私、狭隘的偏见”感到幻灭。但他为了保住贷款，仍想延续清王朝的统治。他纠结于错误的问题：操心银行团的组成，却忽视了民众对所有外国银行家的反感。在 1912 年巴黎的一次中国财政会议上，日本和俄国要求加入中国贷款银团，并且得偿所愿。这正是斯特莱特的噩梦：银团如今囊括了中国世代的宿敌。他意识到，银行家无法在真空中行事，而是被裹挟在更宏大的政治力量之中。他悲观地预见到“那一天终将到来——中国的财政会像埃及那样，由一个国际委员会来管理”。 又一个梦想破灭了！ [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241279)

In 1911, a nationalist revolution in China, fueled partly by resentment of foreign bankers, ousted the Manchu dynasty and declared a republic. The liberal, activist Dorothy Straight was sympathetic to the revolutionaries. In January 1912, Sun Yat-sen became provisional president, heading a movement seeking to unify China and stop foreign meddling. Willard and Dorothy witnessed the panicky exodus of Manchu nobles from a Peking aflutter with radical banners. Willard slept with a loaded revolver by his side. The imaginative Dorothy thrived on the danger, writing, “It would be rather exciting to be attacked by a wild mob in the night.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241441)
1911 年，一场由对外国银行家的不满情绪所推动的民族主义革命在中国爆发，推翻了满清王朝，宣告共和国的成立。自由派活动家多萝西·斯特雷特对革命者抱有同情。1912 年 1 月，孙中山成为临时总统，领导着一场旨在统一中国、阻止外国干涉的运动。威拉德和多萝西亲眼目睹了满清贵族们惊慌失措地逃离北京，城中到处飘扬着激进的旗帜。威拉德睡觉时身边都放着一把上了膛的左轮手枪。富有想象力的多萝西却在危险中如鱼得水，她写道：“如果夜里被狂乱的暴民袭击，那倒挺刺激的。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241441)

One evening as the Straights were getting ready to dine with a British neighbor, shooting did erupt nearby. As Willard recalled, “The pop, pop, popping continued and our roof lines stood out sharply against the glow of the first fire. I told Dorothy that it looked like trouble. She didn’t mind a bit, but went on dressing for dinner, calm as you please, and objected strenuously when I advised her to get into street dress in order that, if necessary, we could clear out to the Legation.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241576) During a pause in the fighting, they made it over to the neighbor’s for dinner. But then soldiers began smashing and looting stores nearby. After gathering up their maid and proper clothing, they fled for the safety of the legation but were trapped by rioters on a dead-end street. Finally they were rescued by a contingent of American marines. Piling into a rickshaw, bags strapped to the back, Dorothy and Willard managed to thread their way through pillaging mobs to the legation.[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241721)
一天晚上，斯特雷特一家正准备与一位英国邻居共进晚餐时，附近突然响起了枪声。据威拉德回忆：“啪、啪、啪的枪声接连不断，屋顶的轮廓在最初的火光映照下格外清晰。我告诉多萝西情况不妙，她却毫不在意，继续从容地换着晚礼服，还坚决反对我建议她换上便装，以便必要时我们能逃到使馆区去。”在战斗间歇，他们设法去了邻居家吃饭。但随后士兵们开始砸抢附近的商店。他们带上女佣和体面的衣服，逃往使馆区寻求安全，却被暴徒困在了一条死胡同里。最终，一队美国海军陆战队员救了他们。多萝西和威拉德把行李绑在人力车后，在抢劫的人群中穿行，终于抵达了使馆区。

This Morgan foray into China ended with Woodrow Wilson’s election and the elevation of that Morgan *bete noire*, William Jennings Bryan, to secretary of state. On March 10, 1913, Harry Davison and Willard Straight visited the new secretary of state in Washington. (Unlike Knox, Bryan would never deign to travel to 23 Wall Street.) Bryan asked them flat out what the group expected from Washington if China defaulted. Davison didn’t mince words and said the government might “be called upon to utilize both its military and naval forces to protect the interests of the lenders.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241847) Neither Bryan nor Wilson sympathized with such foreign meddling. A week later, Wilson denounced the loan as “obnoxious to the principles upon which the government of our people rests.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242002) The government was obviously withdrawing its support.
摩根此番进军中国，最终因伍德罗·威尔逊当选总统而告终，威尔逊还将摩根的眼中钉威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩提拔为国务卿。1913 年 3 月 10 日，哈里·戴维森和威拉德·斯特雷特前往华盛顿拜访这位新任国务卿。（与诺克斯不同，布莱恩绝不屑于前往华尔街 23 号。）布莱恩直截了当地问他们，如果中国违约，这个集团期望华盛顿方面做些什么。戴维森毫不含糊地表示，政府可能“需要动用海陆军力量来保护贷款方的利益。” [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2241847) 无论是布莱恩还是威尔逊，都不赞同这种外国干涉。一周后，威尔逊谴责这笔贷款“违背了我国政府赖以立足的原则”。 [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242002) 显然，政府正在撤消对其的支持。

The next day, the American Bankers Group was effectively disbanded. As a creature of Washington, it couldn’t survive without its blessing. Most bankers were relieved, for they had come to doubt China’s willingness to repay the loan. The end of the China business wasn’t mourned within the House of Morgan, either. As Teddy Gren-fell, who had been consumed by it, wrote to Jack, “I think that all of us will have ’China’ written on our hearts when we die, with several uncomplimentary epithets after it.’ ”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242192) Yet the experience had bridged differences among big Wall Street banks and made them accustomed to working together abroad. Morgans, National City, and First National arrived at an understanding for participating together in all Latin lending. This Big Three agreement would vastly magnify Morgan power. (Kuhn, Loeb often formed a fourth member of their syndicates.) These same banks, ironically, would shortly be hauled before the Pujo Committee as the abominable Money Trust. What the public wouldn’t know was that the Money Trust had been forged, in part, by Washington itself in its quest for foreign influence.
次日，美国银行家集团正式解体。作为华盛顿一手扶持的产物，失去政府背书后便难以为继。多数银行家反倒松了口气——他们早已怀疑中国是否有意愿偿还贷款。摩根家族内部也无人为中国业务的终结扼腕叹息。深陷此事的泰迪·格伦菲尔致信杰克时写道：“我想，待我们离世之时，每个人心上都会刻着‘中国’二字，其后缀着几个难听的骂名。” [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242192) 不过，这段经历弥合了华尔街大银行间的分歧，让它们习惯了在海外并肩作战。摩根银行、花旗银行和第一国民银行达成共识，决定共同参与所有拉美贷款项目。这“三巨头”协约将极大扩展摩根的影响力。（库恩-勒布公司通常作为银团第四成员加入。）讽刺的是，正是这些银行，不久后便以“可憎的货币托拉斯”之姿被传唤至普约委员会面前。 公众不会知道的是，这个金钱信托在一定程度上是由华盛顿政府自身为了追求海外影响力而一手打造的。

The new age of banker-government collaboration mellowed even the vehemently antigovernment Jack Morgan. After wrangling with Washington over a Honduran loan in 1912, he cabled Grenfell, “You will understand we do not wish accuse our own Government too loudly in view of necessary relations with them other foreign matters.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242347) No less ideologically hostile to government than his father, Jack saw the need to mute his public anger. The days of brusque individualism were dead.
银行家与政府合作的新时代，甚至连一向强烈反政府的杰克·摩根都变得温和了。1912 年，他在与华盛顿就一笔洪都拉斯贷款争执不下之后，给格伦费尔发电报说：“你会明白，考虑到我们在其他外交事务上必须与本国政府保持的关系，我们不想过于激烈地指责我们自己的政府。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242347) 杰克在意识形态上对政府的敌意丝毫不亚于他的父亲，但他也意识到需要克制自己公开的愤怒。粗鲁的个人主义时代已经一去不复返了。

Willard Straight returned to work at 23 Wall, but never fit into a mundane office setting. In the 1912 election, he and Dorothy supported their friend from Oyster Bay, Teddy Roosevelt—an act that must have savored of subversive tendencies among the Morgan partners. They also secretly read Louis Brandeis’s attacks against Morgan’s handling of the New Haven Railroad. In 1914, they were the financial angels for a new political weekly, *The New Republic*, which initially had a strongly pro-Roosevelt slant. Harry Davison and other partners spurned the chance to participate, and only Thomas Lamont joined them. Restless and adventurous, Willard found it hard to submit to a banker’s discipline and chafed at not being made a Morgan partner. He was always concocting new schemes, such as the creation of India House on New York’s Hanover Square, a club dedicated to foreign trade, which he outfitted with model ships and antiques. In the end, even the spacious universe of J. P. Morgan and Company would be too confining for the large, venturesome spirit of Willard Straight. He would last only another two years at the bank.
威拉德·斯特雷特回到华尔街 23 号工作，却始终无法适应枯燥的办公室生活。在 1912 年的大选中，他和多萝西支持了他们在牡蛎湾的老友泰迪·罗斯福——在摩根合伙人眼中，这举动想必带了几分叛逆的意味。他们还私下阅读路易斯·布兰代斯抨击摩根处理纽黑文铁路的文章。1914 年，他们成为新发行的政治周刊《新共和》的财务天使，该刊最初带有强烈的亲罗斯福倾向。哈里·戴维森和其他合伙人拒绝参与，唯有托马斯·拉蒙特加入了他们。天性不安分、喜欢冒险的威拉德受不了银行家的条条框框，对没能成为摩根合伙人耿耿于怀。他总在捣鼓新点子，比如在纽约汉诺威广场创办了印度会所——一个专攻外贸的俱乐部，里面摆满了船模和古董。到头来，就连 J.P.摩根那广阔无垠的天地也容不下他了。 对于威拉德·斯特雷特这种胸怀大志、敢于冒险的人来说，摩根公司实在是太束手束脚了。他在那家银行也只多撑了两年。
