# Chapter Thirty-Two: Samba

BY the mid-1970s, J. P. Morgan and Company—the holding company of Morgan Guaranty—was drawing half its profits from more than twenty offices abroad. By a minor miracle, the bank’s pell-mell global expansion didn’t dilute staff cohesion. As Pat Patterson said, “Our operation is worldwide in a compact way.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426473) The bank used various devices—from providing free lunches at its dining rooms to rotating executives—to preserve an inbred feeling. The refusal to open branch networks in foreign countries concentrated personnel, furthering intimacy. “It would be a little like a fish out of water for us to run a system of branches in Germany or England when we don’t have it here,” said the avuncular, balding Walter Page, who succeeded Patterson as chairman in 1978.[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426623)
到 20 世纪 70 年代中期，摩根公司——摩根担保的控股公司——已有超过一半的利润来自海外二十多个办事处。说来也算个小奇迹，这家银行迅猛的全球扩张并未削弱员工的凝聚力。正如帕特·帕特森所说：“我们的业务虽遍布全球，但运作方式紧凑。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426473) 银行采用了多种手段——从餐厅提供免费午餐到高管轮岗制——来维系这种内部团结的氛围。由于拒绝在海外设立分支网络，人员得以集中，进一步增进了亲密感。“就像我们自己在国内没有分行体系，却跑到德国或英国去搞一套分行系统，那多少有点不合时宜。”头发稀疏、和蔼可亲的沃尔特·佩奇说道，他于 1978 年接替帕特森出任董事长。 [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426623)

When Morgans started underwriting in Paris in the early 1960s, it wasn’t clear where the Euromarkets would settle; even Geneva and Zurich were in the running. During the oil boom of the 1970s, however, London emerged as the clear winner, recycling OPEC surpluses at a furious rate to debtor countries. The City of London suddenly had more American banks than Wall Street! They leapt into syndicated Eurodollar loans, which formed the genesis of the Latin American debt crisis. Latin American governments paid much higher interest rates on loans than corporations back home. And in the Casino Age, those corporations were bypassing banks to borrow in securities markets. Thus, the lemming rush into Latin American lending was symptomatic of the deterioration of the banks’ commercial lending business. Foreign borrowing now expanded beyond the industrial countries that had received the bulk of cross-border lending in the 1950s and 1960s.
20 世纪 60 年代初，摩根银行在巴黎开始承销业务时，欧洲市场最终会落脚何处尚不明朗——甚至连日内瓦和苏黎世都在竞争之列。然而到了 70 年代石油繁荣时期，伦敦脱颖而出成为最大赢家，以前所未有的速度将欧佩克的盈余资金循环贷给债务国。伦敦金融城突然汇聚了比华尔街更多的美国银行！这些银行蜂拥涌入欧洲美元银团贷款市场，而这场狂欢恰恰埋下了拉美债务危机的种子。拉美各国政府承受的贷款利率远高于本土企业。在"赌场时代"里，这些企业纷纷绕过银行，直接通过证券市场融资。因此，向拉美放贷的"旅鼠式"狂潮，恰是银行商业贷款业务恶化的征兆。此时跨境借贷规模已突破 50-60 年代以工业国家为主的格局，开始向更广阔的范围蔓延。

Previous cycles of Latin American lending and default dated back at least to the 1820s. During the Great Depression, every Latin American country save Argentina had defaulted on its foreign debt. The nations had been sternly lectured by the bankers that they would be forever barred from future lending. Yet this history was conveniently forgotten by the young bankers on the swank London party circuit, who booked huge loans to those same countries. As members of a venerable old bank, Morgan people should have had a better memory, and to some extent they did. “Lew Preston and I spent a lot of time talking about the parallels,” recalled A. Bruce Brackenridge, the senior credit officer in the late 1970s. “We used to refer to the loans that the British made here to our railroads. The money that J. P. Morgan and Peabody raised to build America—that was the sort of loans we made to the Itaipu Dam in Brazil. There’s a very clear analogy there.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426748) It was, alas, the wrong analogy, skipping over all the disastrous Latin American precedents. It also overlooked the fact that many American state and railroad loans in the nineteenth century had defaulted—a history that haunted George Peabody and subsequently made the Morgan imprimatur so sacred to European creditors.
拉丁美洲的借贷与违约循环最早可追溯到 19 世纪 20 年代。大萧条期间，除阿根廷外的所有拉美国家都曾外债违约。银行家们曾严厉训诫这些国家，称它们将永远被排斥在未来的贷款市场之外。然而，在伦敦那些光鲜的派对圈里，年轻的银行家们早已将这段历史抛诸脑后，向这些国家大手笔发放贷款。作为一家历史悠久的银行，摩根的人本该记性更好——某种程度上确实如此。"卢·普雷斯顿和我花了很多时间讨论历史相似性，"70 年代末的资深信贷官 A·布鲁斯·布雷肯里奇回忆道，"我们常提及英国人当年给美国铁路放贷的例子。J·P·摩根和皮博迪为建设美国筹集的资金——那正是我们向巴西伊泰普水电站提供贷款的模式。这两者之间存在着非常清晰的类比关系。"“ [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426748) 可惜，这是一个错误的类比，完全忽略了拉丁美洲那些灾难性的先例。它也忽视了一个事实：19 世纪美国许多州政府和铁路债券曾发生过违约——这段历史曾困扰乔治·皮博迪，并随后让摩根家族的背书对欧洲债权人来说变得如此神圣。

In earlier generations, Rothschilds, Barings, and Morgans made Latin American loans through large bond issues that distributed risk among thousands of small investors. (An estimated half-million Americans were stuck with largely worthless foreign bonds during the 1930s.) Modern Latin American loans, in contrast, took the form of bank debt, concentrating the risk in the banking system. Large syndicate managers, such as Morgan Guaranty and Citibank, would unite up to two hundred banks for a loan. If this spread risk, it perhaps also created an illusory sense of safety in numbers.
在更早的几代人手中，罗斯柴尔德家族、巴林银行和摩根家族通过大规模债券发行来提供拉丁美洲贷款，将风险分散给成千上万的小投资者。（据估计，20 世纪 30 年代约有 50 万美国人被套牢在基本一文不值的外国债券上。）相比之下，现代拉丁美洲贷款则采取银行债务的形式，将风险集中在银行体系中。诸如摩根保证信托和花旗银行这样的大型银团牵头行，会联合多达两百家银行来发放一笔贷款。这种做法虽然分散了风险，但或许也制造了一种人多势众的虚假安全感。”

Why didn’t banks sell Latin American bonds? “Because you wouldn’t have been able to sell the bonds,” explained Brackenridge. This should have been a tip-off of high risk.[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426913) Since only a handful of developing countries were eligible to sell bonds, Morgan Stanley and other investment banks were mostly spared the Latin American debt crisis. (Both a commercial and an investment bank in American terms, Morgan Grenfell participated in some export credits and syndicated loans to Brazil and elsewhere.) So banks rushed in where investors feared to tread. This spared the “little people” the bloodshed of the earlier debt crisis but also introduced the potential for large disruptions in the global financial system.
为什么银行不销售拉丁美洲债券？“因为根本卖不出去，”布拉肯里奇解释道。这本身就透露出高风险信号。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426913) 由于只有少数发展中国家有资格发行债券，摩根士丹利和其他投资银行大多得以避开拉丁美洲债务危机。（摩根建富作为英美两地的商业银行兼投资银行，参与了部分对巴西等国的出口信贷和银团贷款。）于是，银行蜂拥而入投资者不敢涉足之地。这虽然让“小散户”免于上一轮债务危机的血洗，却也给全球金融体系埋下了巨大动荡的隐患。

Because the Latin American debt crisis originated with the recycling of Arab petrodollar deposits, the banks would later cite official approval of such lending. Indeed, Washington and the other Western governments cravenly ceded responsibility for the problem to the private banks. But as shown by the experience with German reparations and Allied war debt in the 1920s, even explicit official approval of loans didn’t guarantee government support in case of trouble. There would always be popular cynicism about spendthrift foreign debtors—not to mention an assumption of banker greed—that would arise to hobble governments in solving the problem. Ironically, the petrodollar blackmail so feared by Senator Church wasn’t the real problem. By *keeping* petrodollars and lending them out to Latin America, banks damaged themselves and the world economy.
由于拉美债务危机的根源在于阿拉伯石油美元存款的循环利用，银行业后来常以贷款获得官方批准为由为自己辩护。事实上，华盛顿及其他西方政府懦弱地将问题责任推给了私营银行。但正如 20 世纪 20 年代德国赔款与协约国战争债务的教训所表明的那样，即便贷款获得了明确的官方批准，一旦出了事，政府也未必会出手相助。民众始终对挥霍无度的外国债务人嗤之以鼻，更不用说对银行家贪婪成性的固有印象了——这些都让政府在解决问题时束手束脚。颇具讽刺意味的是，丘奇参议员当年忧心忡忡的石油美元讹诈并非真正的症结所在。银行一边囤积石油美元，一边将其贷给拉美，结果既害了自己，也拖累了全球经济。

Morgan Guaranty was a good bellwether of changing American attitudes toward Latin American lending. In the 1920s, the bank had proudly boasted of the number of South American governments it had turned down. In the 1940s, Tom Lamont was aghast when Franklin Roosevelt advocated postwar lending to Brazil, and Russell Leffingwell urged World Bank president John J. McCloy not to lend to the region. In the 1950s, the Eurocentric Morgans largely limited foreign lending to England and France. But with its core lending business eroded in the Casino Age, it suddenly emerged in the 1970s and 1980s as an “MBA bank”—so-called after the first initials of the three largest Latin American debtors: it made \$1.2 billion in loans to Mexico, \$1.8 billion to Brazil, and \$750 million to Argentina. For Wall Street’s most conservative bank to have its largest foreign stake in Brazil showed its reliance on progressively riskier loans for profitability.
摩根担保银行堪称美国对拉丁美洲贷款态度转变的风向标。20 世纪 20 年代，这家银行曾骄傲地吹嘘自己拒绝了多少南美政府的贷款请求；40 年代，当富兰克林·罗斯福主张向巴西提供战后贷款时，汤姆·拉蒙特惊愕不已，而拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔则敦促世界银行行长约翰·J·麦克洛伊不要向该地区放贷；50 年代，以欧洲为中心的摩根银行基本只向英法两国提供海外贷款。然而，当核心贷款业务在赌场时代被侵蚀后，它竟在 70 至 80 年代摇身一变成为"MBA 银行"——以拉美三大债务国英文首字母命名：向墨西哥提供 12 亿美元贷款，向巴西提供 18 亿美元，向阿根廷提供 7.5 亿美元。这家华尔街最保守的银行，将最大海外风险敞口压在巴西，足见其对日益高风险贷款的盈利依赖程度。

Several overriding illusions clouded judgment. One was that countries didn’t go bankrupt—a canard associated with Citicorp’s Walter Wriston. This almost inverted historic truth. Default on sovereign debt had been commonplace for 150 years. Even the discriminating old House of Morgan ended up with massive defaults on Austrian, German, and Japanese loans by World War II. There were more recent cases of debt repudiation as well, including China in 1949, Cuba in 1961, and North Korea in 1974. Banks could foreclose on companies but not on countries, making the latter more careless about repaying loans. And political risk was always piled atop economic risk.
几大根深蒂固的错觉扰乱了人们的判断。其中之一就是"国家不会破产"——这个谬论与花旗银行的沃尔特·瑞斯顿有关。这几乎与历史事实背道而驰。在过去 150 年里，主权债务违约屡见不鲜。即便是眼光挑剔的老牌摩根家族，到二战时也因奥地利、德国和日本的贷款而遭遇大规模违约。债务违约的案例更近的还有 1949 年的中国、1961 年的古巴和 1974 年的朝鲜。银行可以对公司进行破产清算，却无法对主权国家如此操作，这导致后者对偿债更加掉以轻心。而政治风险总是与经济风险叠加在一起。

Another factor of comfort to the bankers was the International Monetary Fund. By the 1970s, gunboat diplomacy was passe. For reasons of foreign policy, Washington was often more eager to appease Latin American governments than bully them about loans. Bankers didn’t like meddling in foreign countries, especially now that they had branches abroad. In 1976, when Peru was nearly bankrupt, Citibank, Morgans, and other banks imposed an austerity plan in exchange for a \$400-million loan. Requiring a steep rise in food and gas prices, it provoked riots in Lima and new charges of dollar diplomacy. The banks were appalled by the backlash. “It doesn’t take much to whip up the peasantry with stories about the House of Morgan and U.S. imperialism to explain why there’s no food,” said a congressional staffer.[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427038) Stung by bad publicity, the banks turned to the IMF as a surrogate that could withstand political criticism in debtor countries. It seemed a useful shield behind which to effect painful economic reforms.
另一个让银行家们感到安心的因素就是国际货币基金组织。到了 20 世纪 70 年代，炮舰外交已经过时。出于外交政策考虑，华盛顿方面往往更热衷于安抚拉美各国政府，而不是在贷款问题上对它们施压。银行家们不喜欢插手外国事务，尤其现在他们已经在国外设立了分支机构。1976 年，当秘鲁几近破产时，花旗银行、摩根银行等银行强制推行了一项紧缩计划，作为提供 4 亿美元贷款的条件。这项计划要求大幅提高食品和天然气价格，从而在利马引发了骚乱，也招来了新一轮关于“美元外交”的指责。银行方面对这样的强烈反弹感到震惊。“编一些关于摩根大通和美国帝国主义的故事，来解释为什么没有食物，这很容易煽动起农民的情绪，”一位国会工作人员说道。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427038) 遭受负面舆论打击后，银行转而求助于国际货币基金组织，将其作为能够承受债务国政治批评的替代机构。这似乎是一个有用的挡箭牌，可以在其掩护下实施痛苦的经济改革。

The IMF laid down strict conditions for loans. As banks made their loans contingent on agreement to the IMF austerity programs, the fund’s power soared. The problem was that the fund was set up to handle temporary payment imbalances, not protracted debt problems. Nobody knew whether its orthodox prescriptions—cutting spending, ending subsidies, and deflating economies—revived economies or simply squeezed them to pay off bankers. There was the further problem that strong Third World countries, such as Brazil, bypassed the fund altogether and borrowed only from commercial banks. Yet whatever the fund’s limitations, it encouraged bankers to believe that they had some control over errant debtors, forcing them to undertake sound policies. And during the Latin debt crisis, the fund would indeed provide forms of control over debtor countries unknown to earlier generations of bankers.
国际货币基金组织（IMF）为贷款设定了严格的条件。由于银行将贷款与 IMF 的紧缩计划挂钩，该基金的影响力急剧上升。问题在于，该基金当初是为应对临时性国际收支失衡而设立的，而非处理长期的债务问题。无人知晓其正统方案——削减开支、取消补贴、紧缩经济——究竟是让经济复苏，还是仅仅为了偿还银行债务而榨干经济。另一个问题是，像巴西这样的第三世界强国完全绕开了 IMF，只向商业银行借贷。然而，无论 IMF 存在何种局限，它都让银行相信，它们能够对不守规矩的债务国施加一定控制，迫使其实施稳健的政策。在拉丁美洲债务危机期间，该基金确实提供了某种形式的控制手段，这是前几代银行家从未见过的。

The structure of syndicated loans invited banks to abdicate responsibility and coast along with the others. Some fifteen hundred banks worldwide piggybacked onto the expertise of a Morgans or a Citibank, especially in Brazil. Often new to foreign lending, small banks left the scrutiny of loans to the larger banks. In a world of telex-driven anonymity, banks would receive cursory “offering memorandums” of mostly boilerplate language. Tens of billions of dollars in loans were assembled through \$10-million participations. By the late 1970s, a fierce price war cut profit margins on loans until they no longer reflected the gargantuan risks involved. Said one Morgan banker involved: “By the mid-1970s, it was very clear that things were getting out of control, with crazy lenders and crazy borrowers.” It was a giant mechanism gone mad.
银团贷款的结构让银行们纷纷推卸责任，随波逐流。全球约 1500 家银行，尤其是针对巴西的贷款，都搭上了摩根或花旗这类专业机构的便车。许多中小银行初涉海外贷款业务，便将贷款审查工作甩给了大银行。在电传驱动的匿名世界里，银行收到的“发行备忘录”内容粗疏，多为套话。数百亿美元的贷款通过 1000 万美元的参与份额拼凑而成。到 20 世纪 70 年代末，激烈的价格战将贷款利率压至极低，再也无法反映其中蕴含的巨大风险。一位参与的摩根银行家回忆道：“到 70 年代中期，情况已经非常明显——借贷双方都疯了，局面完全失控。”这简直是一台失控的疯狂机器。

Somewhat more than most, Morgans tried to resist the wild grab bag. In 1979, its London syndicate operation was run by a young Smith graduate, Mary Gibbons, known for her toughness. “At 31, wielding all the power that Morgan Guaranty’s position in the Eurocurrency market commands, Gibbons is unquestionably the most influential female decision maker in the City, if not in the entire world of international banking,” said *Institutional Investor*.[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427188) She balked at credits even for Britain, Sweden, and Canada, fearing watered-down standards. In general, however, Morgans was swept up in the bankers’ suicide dash. One ex-Morgan banker recalled, “There was a lot of unscrupulous lending and forcing loans down the throat of these countries. Anything to get a loan to a government.”
比起大多数银行，摩根更努力地抵制这场疯狂的争夺。1979 年，其伦敦银团业务由一位年轻的史密斯学院毕业生玛丽·吉本斯负责，她以强硬著称。《机构投资者》杂志称：“31 岁的吉本斯，凭借摩根担保在欧元市场的地位所赋予的权力，即使不是整个国际银行业，也无疑是伦敦金融城最具影响力的女性决策者。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427188) 她甚至对英国、瑞典和加拿大的信贷也犹豫不决，担心标准被降低。然而，总体而言，摩根还是被卷入了银行家的自杀式冲刺。一位前摩根银行家回忆道：“存在大量不择手段的放贷行为，强行向这些国家塞贷款。只要能向政府放贷，什么都干得出来。”

The most convoluted, baffling Morgan relationship was with Brazil, a newcomer among its clients. Even as the House of Morgan advised the country, Brazil balked at granting it a branch, which rankled at 23 Wall. “They said that if Morgan got a branch, they would be dominant and then the government would have to let in forty other banks,” said an ex-Morgan official. “It was a real sore point.” The Morgan people were proud of their Brazil loans, which went to seemingly well-managed mining and electric enterprises. Recipients included the vast Itaipu hydroelectric project, with its World Bank patronage. The bank also boasted that Brazil had a good credit profile—that is, its loans matured at nicely spaced intervals. Sometimes Morgan people sounded as if history had cheated them, making their splendid Brazilian portfolio look miserable.
摩根与巴西之间的关系堪称其客户中最复杂、最令人费解的一段。尽管摩根大通为巴西提供咨询服务，巴西却迟迟不愿批准其开设分行，这让华尔街 23 号深感不满。“他们说如果摩根开了分行，就会占据主导地位，届时政府将不得不让其他四十家银行也进来，”一位前摩根高管表示，“这真是个棘手的问题。”摩根人对他们在巴西的贷款引以为豪，这些资金投向了看似管理良好的采矿和电力企业，其中包括由世界银行支持的大型伊泰普水电站项目。该行还夸耀巴西信用状况良好——贷款到期时间间隔合理。有时摩根人听起来仿佛历史辜负了他们，让他们那批原本出色的巴西投资组合显得惨不忍睹。

As a latecomer to Latin America, Morgan’s position as chief adviser to Brazil was a startling achievement. It was accomplished through the virtuosity of an engaging young banker of mixed nationality named Antonio Gebauer. Born in Colombia to a wealthy Venezuelan brewer of German birth, Gebauer had been educated at Columbia University’s Graduate School of Business and was married to a Brazilian. He retained his Venezuelan citizenship while at Morgans. Short, with horn-rimmed glasses and sandy hair, he was fluent in Spanish, Portuguese, German, and other languages. He was both charming and impatient, bright but prone to a brusque arrogance. When he started at Morgans in the 1960s, domestic bankers were kings, and he seemed to have a slim chance for advancement. Then, as Latin American lending surged in the 1970s, the Anglophile Morgan bank, with its European bias, found Gebauer providential in catching up with Chase and Citibank in Latin America. His delighted bosses gave him a wide berth.
作为后来进入拉丁美洲的银行，摩根能成为巴西的首席顾问银行，是一项令人瞩目的成就。这一成就得益于一位魅力十足、多国血统的年轻银行家——安东尼奥·格鲍尔。他出生于哥伦比亚，父亲是一位拥有德国血统的委内瑞拉富有的啤酒酿造商。格鲍尔曾在哥伦比亚大学商学院接受教育，并娶了一位巴西妻子。他在摩根工作时仍保留着委内瑞拉国籍。他身材矮小，戴着角质架眼镜，头发沙黄色，能流利地说西班牙语、葡萄牙语、德语等多种语言。他既迷人又急躁，聪颖却常带着一种唐突的傲慢。20 世纪 60 年代他加入摩根时，国内银行家才是主角，他看似晋升机会渺茫。然而，随着 70 年代拉丁美洲贷款业务激增，这家亲英且偏重欧洲的摩根银行发现，格鲍尔在追赶大通银行和花旗银行在拉丁美洲的业务时，简直是天赐之选。 他那高兴的老板们都对他敬而远之。

Tony Gebauer spectacularly developed new business and was trusted by Brazilian officials. He socialized in elite circles and was probably on a first-name basis with every Latin finance minister and central banker. In the heady world of petrodollar recycling in the 1970s, Gebauer was a jet-setting star, a frequent guest at Brazilian coffee plantations, his doings covered by Rio de Janeiro gossip columnists. He appeared on Brazilian television, landed on the cover of the country’s top news magazine, *Veja*, and became president of the Brazilian-American Chamber of Commerce. It was highly unusual for the Morgan bank to tolerate such a high-profile approach to banking. Other bankers watched in wonder. At home, Gebauer threw flashy parties in his East Side apartment and at his East Hampton weekend home, which was called Samambaia, or “fern” in Portuguese. Carlos Langoni, Brazil’s young central bank president, spent weekends there. All the while, Gebauer was booking Brazilian loans 2 percentage points above Morgan’s own costs—spreads so profitable as to ease doubts about their soundness.
托尼·格鲍尔在巴西开拓新业务时表现极为出色，深得巴西官员信任。他活跃于精英社交圈，几乎与每位拉丁美洲的财政部长和央行行长都能直呼其名。在 20 世纪 70 年代石油美元循环利用的狂热浪潮中，格鲍尔如同乘喷气机环球穿梭的明星，是巴西咖啡庄园的常客，里约热内卢八卦专栏作家争相报道他的行程。他上过巴西电视节目，登上该国顶级新闻杂志《Veja》封面，还成为巴美商会主席。摩根银行能容忍如此高调的银行经营方式实属罕见，其他银行家无不瞠目结舌。在纽约，他时常在东区公寓和东汉普顿的周末别墅——取名 Samambaia（葡萄牙语意为"蕨类植物"）——举办奢华派对。年轻的巴西央行行长卡洛斯·兰戈尼也曾在那里共度周末。 与此同时，格鲍尔为巴西发放的贷款利率比摩根自身的融资成本高出整整两个百分点——如此丰厚的利差足以让人对其安全性不再存疑。

Occasionally there were fleeting concerns at high levels about this lending binge. At one point, Chairman Pat Patterson received an award from Brazil declaring him the country’s best banker. He was slightly jarred and confided to President Walter Page that it was perhaps a dubious achievement. “Maybe we better not get another award and be busted,” Patterson told Page.[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427332) But such doubts were momentary. By pushing back the exposure limits in each borrowing country by small increments, bankers averted their eyes from the developing danger. Brackenridge recalled, “We didn’t say, ‘How much of our capital should be in these loans?’ We played with it, but we really didn’t say, ‘Hey, we really shouldn’t have more than 50 percent of our capital in loans to Brazil just out of a spread of risk.’ ”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427490)
偶尔，高层会对这场贷款狂潮闪过一丝忧虑。有一次，董事长帕特·帕特森收到巴西政府颁发的一个奖项，表彰他是该国最出色的银行家。他心里略感不安，私下对总裁沃尔特·佩奇坦言，这成就或许有点蹊跷。“别回头再拿个奖，结果却破产了。”帕特森对佩奇说。 [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427332) 但这样的疑虑转瞬即逝。银行家们通过小步快跑的方式，逐步抬高每个借款国的贷款上限，从而对日益积聚的风险视而不见。布拉肯里奇回忆道：“我们压根没问过自己，‘这些贷款应该占我们资本的多少？’我们倒是琢磨过，但从未真正说过，‘嘿，为了分散风险，我们投给巴西的贷款绝不能超过资本的 50%。’” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427490)

Despite Gebauer’s virtuosity, the Morgan bank had limited power to force Brazil to curb its prodigal, inflationary spending. In 1980, it vainly badgered the country to go to the IMF. When the bank went to the IMF instead to get its perspective on Brazil—an exercise meant to instill market confidence—Delfim Netto, Brazil’s short, squat, bespectacled planning minister, got very angry. He thought Morgans was going behind the country’s back to check up on it. So the banks found it hard to police sovereign clients without antagonizing them. They began slipping into a situation in which they were hostages to their large debtors. The full extent of this bondage wouldn’t become apparent until the fall of 1982. Then everyone would rediscover the old adage that if a debtor is big enough, he controls the bank.
尽管格鲍尔技艺高超，摩根银行仍无力迫使巴西遏制其挥霍无度的通胀性支出。1980 年，它徒劳地催促巴西向国际货币基金组织求助。当银行转而向 IMF 寻求对巴西的看法——此举旨在灌输市场信心时——巴西那位矮胖、戴眼镜的计划部长德尔菲姆·内托大为光火。他认为摩根背着巴西在暗中查探。于是银行发现，要想监管这些主权客户而又不惹怒他们，实在难上加难。它们逐渐陷入一种被大债务人绑架的境地。这种受制于人的程度直到 1982 年秋天才彻底暴露。届时，所有人都会重新想起那句老话：债务人若足够庞大，便能反过来掌控银行。

THE April 1982 war over the Falkland Islands cast a black cloud over Latin American lending, projecting a view of the whole region as unstable. After Argentina invaded the islands, Britain retaliated by freezing its London-based assets. When hostilities ended, the House of Morgan undertook secret diplomacy to patch up relations between the two countries. The central banks of England and Argentina didn’t know how to resume relations without losing face. Who would initiate talks? Tony Gebauer, now the senior vice-president for Latin America, acted as matchmaker. Representatives from the two central banks flew to New York and were closeted in a conference room at 23 Wall—the ice-breaking contact between them.
1982 年 4 月福克兰群岛战争为拉丁美洲贷款蒙上阴影，整个地区被视为动荡不安。阿根廷入侵群岛后，英国冻结其伦敦资产作为报复。战事结束后，摩根银行展开秘密外交，试图修复两国关系。英格兰银行与阿根廷央行都不愿在维护颜面的前提下开启对话——谁先开口？时任拉丁美洲高级副总裁的托尼·格鲍尔充当起牵线人。两国央行代表飞抵纽约，在华尔街 23 号的会议室闭门会晤，这场会面打破了双方僵局。

After the war, it grew harder for bankers to make nice distinctions among Latin American debtors. Regional banks were less disposed to share Morgan’s view of Brazil as a textbook Third World country investing in sound infrastructure. Rather, they saw a nation grotesquely burdened with a \$90-billion debt—the world’s biggest—borrowing a stupendous \$1.5 billion monthly to stay afloat. Morgans urged Carlos Langoni to come to New York to make reassuring speeches. In a rare coup, it even got Secretary of State George Shultz—as Bechtel president, a Morgan director in the 1970s—to accept an award from the Brazilian-American Chamber of Commerce along with Ernane Galveas, Brazil’s finance minister; Shultz seldom consented to such mingling of private and public purpose.
战后，银行家们越来越难对拉丁美洲债务国做出细致区分。地区性银行不再像摩根那样，把巴西视为投资于健全基础设施的教科书式第三世界国家。相反，它们看到的这个国家背负着 900 亿美元——全球最高——的巨额债务，每月还需借入惊人的 15 亿美元才能勉强维持。摩根敦促卡洛斯·兰戈尼前往纽约发表安抚性演讲。在一次罕见的成功斡旋中，摩根甚至请动了国务卿乔治·舒尔茨——此人 70 年代曾任贝克特尔公司总裁兼摩根董事——与巴西财政部长埃纳内·加尔韦亚斯一同接受巴美商会的颁奖；舒尔茨极少同意这种公私事务混杂的安排。

When Mexico startled the world in August 1982 by announcing that it could no longer service its \$87-billion foreign debt, it blackened the image of all Latin American debtors. They were being drowned in a common economic deluge of rising interest rates, global recession, and steeply falling commodity prices. On September 21, 1982, Langhorne Motley, the U.S. ambassador to Brazil, told the State Department that Mexico’s troubles were sparking flight from Brazilian debt: “Japanese banks are out of the market, European banks are scared, regional U.S. banks don’t want to hear about Brazil, and major U.S. banks are proceeding with extreme caution.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427655)
1982 年 8 月，墨西哥宣布无力偿还其 870 亿美元外债，震惊世界，此举也让所有拉美债务国的形象蒙上阴影。利率攀升、全球衰退、大宗商品价格暴跌——它们被共同卷入一场经济洪流，几近窒息。1982 年 9 月 21 日，美国驻巴西大使朗霍恩·莫特利向国务院报告称，墨西哥的困境正引发对巴西债务的恐慌性逃离：“日本银行已退出市场，欧洲银行惶恐不安，美国地区性银行不愿再提及巴西，而美国大型银行也正以极度谨慎的态度行事。”

In October 1982, under cover of a UN speech made by Brazil’s president, Netto and Galveas visited 23 Wall for clandestine talks. Frightened by Mexico, banks had pulled up to \$3 billion in short-term Brazilian loans. Netto and Galveas didn’t see how Brazil could escape default without an emergency loan of \$2.5 to \$3 billion, plus a rescheduling to reduce interest and stretch out payments of principal. In the protocol of such crises, the bank with the largest debt exposure ordinarily managed the rescheduling. But the Brazilians’ faith in Tony Gebauer was such that they wanted Morgans to preside over this mammoth rescue, even though four other American banks had larger stakes. With \$4.6 billion in loans to Brazil, Citibank was the natural leader. To avoid bruised feelings, Gebauer suggested that Citi co-chair the committee. “You have to go and do proper protocol,” he told the Brazilians, to whom Citibank acquiesced. Gerard Finneran would be the Citibank representative.
1982 年 10 月，巴西总统借在联合国发表演讲之机，内托和加尔韦亚斯秘密前往华尔街 23 号进行谈判。受墨西哥危机惊吓，银行已从巴西抽走了高达 30 亿美元的短期贷款。内托和加尔韦亚斯意识到，若没有 25 亿至 30 亿美元的紧急贷款，再加上减息和延长本金偿还期的债务重组，巴西根本无法避免违约。按照此类危机的惯例，通常由风险敞口最大的银行主导债务重组。但巴西人对托尼·格鲍尔如此信任，以至于他们希望摩根银行来主持这场庞大的救援行动，尽管其他四家美国银行的贷款规模更大。花旗银行持有巴西 46 亿美元贷款，自然应是牵头行。为避免伤及情面，格鲍尔建议花旗银行担任委员会联合主席。“你们得按规矩办事，”他对巴西人说，而花旗也默许了这一点。杰拉德·芬纳兰将作为花旗银行代表。

The choice of Morgans and Citi had an intricate political backdrop. Some on Wall Street thought Morgans grabbed at the co-chairmanship in its frustrated quest for a Brazilian branch—a view that infuriated the bank. Perhaps more pertinent was the extremely intimate relationship between Fed chairman Paul Volcker and Lewis T. Preston, Walter Page’s successor as Morgan chairman in 1980. This hidden relationship never surfaced in the press. Yet behind Preston’s moves during financial crises, the cognoscenti sometimes discerned the fine hand of Paul Volcker. In 1980, Preston led a \$1-billion rescue for the Hunt brothers when their attempt to corner the silver market collapsed, nearly dragging down Bache and other brokerage houses. The Hunts were hardly typical Morgan clients, yet the bank performed the rescue at the behest of Volcker.
摩根银行和花旗银行的选择背后有着复杂的政治背景。华尔街一些人认为，摩根银行是因为在争取巴西分行方面受挫，才抢下了联席主席的职位——这种看法让该行大为光火。或许更关键的是，美联储主席保罗·沃尔克与刘易斯·T·普雷斯顿之间极为密切的关系。普雷斯顿于 1980 年接替沃尔特·佩奇出任摩根银行董事长。这段隐秘关系从未见诸报端。然而，在金融危机期间普雷斯顿的种种举动背后，知情人士有时能察觉到保罗·沃尔克的巧妙操盘。1980 年，亨特兄弟试图垄断白银市场的计划破产，险些拖垮巴奇公司及其他经纪行，普雷斯顿牵头提供了 10 亿美元救助。亨特兄弟绝非摩根银行的典型客户，但该行还是应沃尔克之请出手相救。

With Brazil, Volcker apparently again used Preston as his proxy. Just as the House of Morgan in the 1920s provided a convenient back channel for government action, so Volcker could direct bailouts through Preston without advertising his presence. Morgan’s smaller lending to Brazil was advantageous. A Preston confidant explained: “In the fall of 1982, Volcker told Lew that Morgan had to be in charge of the committee. He wanted Morgan to take on the Brazilian loan because we had far less exposure than other banks on Wall Street. We could, if necessary, take on more Brazilian debt without getting screwed up.” (Other bankers, it should be said, pooh-pooh this story, stressing the Gebauer link.) More than any chairman since Henry Alexander, Preston was imbued with a Morgan sense of *noblesse oblige* and Wall Street statesmanship. “Lew has been thinking more and more of the system, even to the detriment of the bank,” said the confidant. He tended to gripe at Citibank, which he often saw as acting selfishly and unilaterally without consulting the general good.
在巴西问题上，沃尔克显然再次将普雷斯顿作为自己的代理人。正如 20 世纪 20 年代的摩根大通为政府行动提供了便捷的秘密渠道一样，沃尔克也可以通过普雷斯顿直接指挥纾困行动，而不必暴露自己的存在。摩根对巴西的贷款规模较小，这反而成了优势。一位普雷斯顿的心腹解释道："1982 年秋天，沃尔克告诉刘易斯，摩根必须主导这个委员会。他希望摩根接手巴西贷款，因为我们在华尔街的敞口远小于其他银行。如果有必要，我们可以承担更多巴西债务，而不会把自己搞砸。"（不过据说其他银行家对此说法不以为然，强调格鲍尔那条线才是关键。）普雷斯顿比自亨利·亚历山大以来的任何一位董事长都更深谙摩根的贵族责任感和华尔街的政治智慧。那位心腹说道："刘易斯越来越把整个体系的利益放在心上，甚至不惜损害自家银行的利益。" 他时常抱怨花旗银行，认为它行事自私、一意孤行，不顾全大局。

As in interwar days, the debt crisis produced bad blood between American and European bankers. More than half of Brazil’s debt was held by non-American banks, yet Morgans and Citibank alone ran the show, as they had many of the earlier syndicated loans. Some in the City suspected that Brazil had groomed Morgans as its pet banker to secure lenient treatment. Guy Huntrods of Lloyds International Bank feared Brazil’s strategy was to cook up a sweetheart deal with New York bankers, then foist it on the Europeans. That October, he turned down Brazil’s request for an emergency loan unless accompanied by an IMF loan and stiff emergency measures. So the all-American team of Morgans and Citi led the first phase of Brazil’s rescue.
如同两次世界大战之间的那段日子，债务危机再次让美欧银行家之间的关系变得紧张。巴西一半以上的债务由非美国银行持有，然而摩根和大通银行却独自主导了局面，就像它们此前主导许多银团贷款一样。伦敦金融城的一些人怀疑，巴西有意将摩根培育为自己的关系银行，以争取宽松的待遇。劳埃德国际银行的盖伊·亨特罗兹担心，巴西的策略是先与纽约银行家达成一项优惠协议，然后强加给欧洲银行。那年 10 月，他拒绝了巴西的紧急贷款请求，除非附带国际货币基金组织的贷款和严厉的紧急措施。因此，摩根和大通这两家全美银行主导了巴西第一阶段的救助行动。

The debt rescues of the 1980s reflected global political realities as well as financial stakes. Again and again, steering-committee banks were predominantly American. Japan was second to the United States in Third World lending, yet in the early rescues it typically settled for a single, token representative from the Bank of Tokyo, which had the largest Latin exposure. Much as the rising financial power—the United States—had deferred to Monty Norman’s intellectual leadership in the 1920s, so the Japanese, even while starting to overtake Wall Street, bowed to Paul Volcker’s authority. Not until the late 1980s would Japan begin to demand a voice at the IMF and the World Bank fully commensurate with its new financial power.
20 世纪 80 年代的债务纾困既反映了全球政治现实，也体现了金融利害关系。主导委员会的银行屡屡以美国为主。日本在第三世界贷款方面仅次于美国，但在早期的纾困行动中，它通常只满足于让在拉丁美洲风险敞口最大的东京银行派出一名象征性的代表。正如崛起中的金融强国——美国——曾在 20 世纪 20 年代屈从于蒙蒂·诺曼的才智领导，日本即便开始超越华尔街，也依然对保罗·沃尔克的权威表示敬意。直到 80 年代末，日本才开始要求在 IMF 和世界银行获得与其新兴金融实力完全相称的话语权。

Back in the 1920s, Tom Lamont had represented two hundred thousand Mexican bondholders worldwide. In the unwieldy modern debt crisis, Morgans and Citibank had to deal with a bureaucratic monstrosity—some seven hundred banks with large and small loans to Brazil. After secretly hatching a rescue plan with Brazil and the IMF, the two banks summoned Brazil’s creditors to New York’s Plaza Hotel on December 20, 1982. Carlos Langoni shocked them by stating that Brazil couldn’t service debt coming due in 1983. Jacques de la Rosiere, the IMF’s managing director, unveiled a complex, four-part Morgan-Citi plan for saving Brazil. Citibank would reschedule \$4 billion in principal; Chase would maintain trade credits; and Bankers Trust would restore short-term “interbank” lines to Brazil. The linchpin was a Morgan-led effort to raise a new \$4.4-billion loan for Brazil, the biggest in Morgan history.
早在 1920 年代，汤姆·拉蒙特就曾代表全球二十万名墨西哥债券持有人。而在当下棘手的现代债务危机中，摩根和大通银行不得不应付一个官僚主义的庞然大物——约七百家向巴西提供大小贷款的银行。1982 年 12 月 20 日，两家银行在秘密与巴西和国际货币基金组织敲定救助计划后，将巴西的债权人召集到纽约广场酒店。卡洛斯·朗戈尼语出惊人，表示巴西无力偿还 1983 年到期的债务。国际货币基金组织总裁雅克·德拉罗西埃公布了摩根与花旗共同制定的四步走救助巴西方案：花旗银行将重新安排 40 亿美元本金；大通银行维持贸易信贷；银行家信托公司恢复对巴西的短期银行间拆借。而整项计划的关键，则是由摩根牵头为巴西筹集一笔 44 亿美元的新贷款——这不仅是摩根史无前例的大手笔，更是救巴西于水火的关键一环。

The plan set a fateful precedent of “curing” the debt crisis by heaping on more debt. In this charade, bankers would lend more to Brazil with one hand, then take it back with the other. This preserved the fictitious book value of loans on bank balance sheets. Approaching the rescue as a grand new syndication, the bankers piled on high interest rates and rescheduling fees. It was hard to stop the greed so prevalent for so many years. The Europeans watched sourly from the sidelines. “It was very much an American party,” said Guy Huntrods, a dogged, balding, talkative banker who became British point man on Latin American debt. “The Brazilians had taken no advice from anybody except Citi and Morgan Guaranty. We were told to go home and do what we were instructed. This created the most awful impression among us.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427805)
该计划开创了一个灾难性的先例：通过增加更多债务来“治愈”债务危机。在这场闹剧中，银行家们一只手向巴西提供更多贷款，另一只手又收了回来。这保住了银行资产负债表上贷款的虚假账面价值。银行家们将这次救助视为一场全新的大型联合贷款，堆高了利率和重新安排还款期限的费用。多年来如此盛行的贪婪难以遏制。欧洲人在一旁冷眼旁观。“这完全是美国人的派对，”盖伊·亨特罗德说道，他是一位执着、秃顶、健谈的银行家，后来成为英国在拉美债务问题上的关键人物。“巴西人除了花旗银行和摩根担保信托公司之外，没有听取任何人的建议。我们被告知该回家了，按指令行事。这让我们所有人留下了极其糟糕的印象。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427805)

Tensions rose between Wall Street banks, with their huge and irrevocable commitments to Brazil, and regional banks, which wanted to cut their smaller losses and run. One German banker observed, “I come into these sessions and I find all these hillbillies. The big American banks have made the loans and sold part of them to the little ones. And these fellows, who don’t know the Baltic from the Barents Sea, were all crying, ‘I want my money back.’ ”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427962) This split produced bitterness between the large and small banks and poisoned the atmosphere of the first rescue.
华尔街大行之间紧张局势升级，它们对巴西承担着巨额且不可撤销的债务，而地区性银行则想及时止损、抽身而退。一位德国银行家讥讽道：“我走进这些会议室，发现满屋子都是乡巴佬。大行们放出了贷款，又把一部分转售给小银行。这些连波罗的海和巴伦支海都分不清的家伙，一个个哭喊着‘还我钱来’。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2427962) 这一分歧导致大小银行之间心生嫌隙，也让首次救援行动的气氛剑拔弩张。

In early 1983, Morgan credit officers worked around the clock to raise the \$4.4 billion. Although the megaloan was assembled in a remarkable two months, it left a residue of ill will toward Tony Gebauer, who embodied the big-stick approach of the Wall Street banks. The smaller banks felt they had been browbeaten into participating, and some, piqued by Gebauer’s high-handed manner, balked at providing new money.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428113) But afraid of antagonizing the Fed and the Wall Street banks, they grudgingly abided by the plan.
1983 年初，摩根信贷官员们夜以继日地工作，筹集了 44 亿美元。尽管这笔巨额贷款在短短两个月内就组建完成，但却给托尼·格鲍尔留下了积怨，他代表着华尔街银行的强硬作风。小银行们觉得是被胁迫参与其中的，有些银行对格鲍尔的高压手段感到恼火，拒绝提供新的资金。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428113) 但由于担心得罪美联储和华尔街银行，它们还是勉强服从了这个计划。

On February, 24, 1983, Brazil hosted a dinner at the Waldorf-Astoria to thank their bankers for the rescue loans. Over dessert, the Brazilians let slip that they might not make timely payments on these new loans either. Nevertheless, the next day, several hundred bankers, bruised and battered, signed copies of the loan agreement that Morgans and Citi laid out for their signature at the Plaza Hotel. The IMF chipped in a \$5-billion loan for Brazil in what appeared a successful finale.
1983 年 2 月 24 日，巴西在华尔道夫酒店设宴，答谢银行家们提供的紧急贷款。席间吃甜点时，巴西人无意中透露，这些新贷款他们也可能无法按时偿还。然而第二天，尽管疲惫不堪、伤痕累累，数百位银行家还是在摩根和大通银行放在广场酒店供他们签署的贷款协议上签了字。国际货币基金组织也为巴西提供了 50 亿美元贷款，这场危机看似迎来了圆满收场。

This success was illusory. While the banks had committed \$4.4 billion for the Morgan-led loan, they had also drained off a corresponding amount in short-term credit lines to Brazil. Thus some banks got their secret revenge. This financial legerdemain neutralized the loan’s effect. Gebauer was furious as he saw banks sabotaging the agreement. The whole sham got him hopping mad because among the banks he suspected of bad faith was Citibank—his co-chair in the rescue operation. By the spring of 1983, Brazil was missing its IMF economic-reform targets, and the fund and the banks halted their emergency payments. The Fed was alarmed by Brazil’s eroding short-term credit lines. On May 31, Volcker called in Preston and other chairmen to discuss the rescue. The Fed was disturbed by reports of Gebauer’s treatment of the regional banks, and Preston feared that he was alienating the British bankers. Gebauer was squabbling openly at meetings with Citibank’s Finneran, demoralizing the bankers further. A decision was made to replace Gebauer with William Rhodes of Citibank, who had headed the effort to rescue Mexico.
这场胜利不过是镜花水月。虽然各银行承诺为摩根牵头的贷款提供 44 亿美元，但它们却同时削减了对巴西同等规模的短期信贷额度。于是，一些银行暗中实施了报复。这种金融戏法令贷款的实际效果化为乌有。看到银行们暗中破坏协议，格鲍尔怒不可遏。整场闹剧让他暴跳如雷，因为他怀疑失信银行中包括花旗银行——恰恰是他在救援行动中的联合主席。到了 1983 年春天，巴西未能实现国际货币基金组织的经济改革目标，基金组织和各银行随即暂停了紧急支付。巴西不断萎缩的短期信贷额度令美联储深感不安。5 月 31 日，沃尔克召集普雷斯顿及其他主席商讨救援方案。美联储对格鲍尔对待地区银行的方式感到不安，而普雷斯顿则担心他正在疏远英国银行家们。 Gebauer 在会上与花旗银行的 Finneran 公开争吵，这进一步打击了银行家们的士气。 决定将 Gebauer 替换为花旗银行的 William Rhodes他曾领导了拯救墨西哥的努力。

This came as a blow to Morgan pride, especially in view of the Morgan-Citi rivalry. “The Morgan bank was very high on Brazil and I think they were a little unhappy that the chairmanship had to be taken away,” remarked Anthony M. Solomon, then New York Fed chief.[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428260) Some Morgan people grumbled about a power-hungry Citibank bringing Brazil into its fold along with Mexico and Argentina. Yet it was Preston who urged Citibank chairman Walter Wriston to relieve Morgan of the leadership burden. And there was secret relief at 23 Wall, which was uncomfortable with its unaccustomed high-profile role in the debt talks. Said one former Morgan official, “People had never identified Morgan that much with Latin America, and it suddenly became a liability.” Gebauer’s role drew attention to the bank’s embarrassingly huge Latin American exposure.
这对摩根的骄傲是一记重击，尤其是考虑到摩根与花旗之间的竞争关系。时任纽约联储行长安东尼·M·所罗门评论道：“摩根银行当时非常看好巴西，我认为他们对不得不交出主席席位有些不满。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428260) 一些摩根内部人士抱怨说，野心勃勃的花旗银行企图将巴西、墨西哥和阿根廷一并纳入其势力范围。然而，正是普雷斯顿敦促花旗银行董事长沃尔特·瑞斯顿接过摩根的领导重任。实际上，华尔街 23 号内部对此暗自松了口气——他们原本就对在债务谈判中扮演这种不寻常的高调角色感到不适。一位前摩根高管表示：“人们向来不把摩根与拉丁美洲联系得太深，而这突然变成了一种负担。”格鲍尔的角色使人们注意到该银行在拉丁美洲的巨大风险敞口，这令人尴尬。

In a second Brazil rescue, Bill Rhodes didn’t want to work with Gebauer, whom he saw as tainted. To appease the bank, he brought in Leighton Coleman of Morgans as deputy chairman; to appease the British, he brought in Guy Huntrods of Lloyds as the other deputy chairman. The reschedulings became more global, creating tremendous creditor unity and averting the internecine feuds among nations that had so weakened the banks in the 1930s. Instead of the largely private bank solution of phase 1, Rhodes wanted to get creditor governments more involved and touched base with the IMF, the World Bank, the U.S. Treasury, the Fed, and the State Department. His actions confirmed the intrinsically political nature of sovereign lending—an old story.
在第二次巴西救援行动中，比尔·罗德斯不愿与他认为有污点的格鲍尔合作。为了安抚银行，他引入摩根斯的莱顿·科尔曼担任副董事长；为了安抚英国人，他又让劳埃德银行的盖伊·亨特罗兹出任另一位副董事长。债务重新安排变得更加全球化，形成了巨大的债权人团结，避免了国家间的内讧——正是这种内讧曾在 20 世纪 30 年代严重削弱了银行。与第一阶段主要依赖私人银行解决的方式不同，罗德斯希望让债权国政府更多参与，并与国际货币基金组织、世界银行、美国财政部、美联储及国务院进行了接触。他的行动印证了主权贷款本质上具有政治性——这早已不是什么新鲜事。

The specter of Tony Gebauer wasn’t yet banished. As Brazil’s economy deteriorated during the summer of 1983, Rhodes opted for secret talks, hoping that blunt language would shock the Brazilians into strong action. On August 16, 1983, Rhodes, Huntrods, and Coleman flew to Brazil in a private plane. Rhodes and Huntrods were faintly nervous about having Coleman along, not on a personal level, but because they feared he might have shared information with Gebauer. In Brasilia, they believed their worst fears had been confirmed. Meeting with Netto, Langoni, and other powers at the home of Finance Minister Galveas, they delivered a stern warning. Rhodes began: “We can’t keep the banks on board much longer.” Coleman chimed in: “You’ve got to speak with one voice.” Huntrods delivered a dramatic peroration: “There is a smell of defeat around the streets of Brasilia that reminds me of France before Dunkirk.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428423) Because Netto had never heard of Dunkirk, a short lesson in history ensued.
托尼·格鲍尔的阴魂尚未散去。随着 1983 年夏季巴西经济形势恶化，罗兹选择进行秘密谈判，希望用直白的话语震惊巴西人，促使他们采取强硬行动。1983 年 8 月 16 日，罗兹、亨特罗德和科尔曼乘坐私人飞机飞往巴西。罗兹和亨特罗德对科尔曼的同行隐约有些不安——倒不是针对他个人，而是担心他可能已向格鲍尔透露了信息。在巴西利亚，他们最坏的担忧似乎得到了印证。与内托、兰戈尼等人在财政部长加尔韦亚斯家中会面时，他们发出了严厉警告。罗兹开场道：“我们无法再让银行继续支持了。”科尔曼插话：“你们必须统一口径。”亨特罗德则慷慨激昂地总结道：“巴西利亚的街头弥漫着失败的气息，让我想起敦刻尔克前的法国。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428423) 因为内托从未听说过敦刻尔克，随后便上演了一堂简短的历史课。

Huntrods felt they had lost the critical element of surprise: he believed somebody had tipped off the Brazilians. “We had absolute certain proof that Gebauer, who was Coleman’s boss, had already telephoned the Brazilians what our game plan was,” said Huntrods adamantly. *“That* we knew beyond a shadow of a doubt.” He thought that Gebauer was either ingratiating himself with the Brazilians or, motivated by envy, was trying to sabotage phase 2. In the end, Gebauer never got back into the game and some say that his career at Morgans stalled afterward. Among bankers, the new Rhodes team would be credited with creating a more cooperative atmosphere and a spirit of shared sacrifice among the banks.
亨特罗兹认为他们失去了最重要的出其不意的要素：他相信有人向巴西人通风报信。“我们有确凿的证据表明，科尔曼的上司格鲍尔已经打电话把我们的计划告诉了巴西人，”亨特罗兹坚定地说，“这一点我们毫不怀疑。”他认为格鲍尔要么是在讨好巴西人，要么是出于嫉妒，试图破坏第二阶段。最终，格鲍尔再也没有回到这场游戏里，有人说他在摩根银行的职业生涯此后便停滞不前。在银行家们看来，新的罗德斯团队营造了更合作的氛围，并在各家银行之间树立了共担牺牲的精神。

In the last analysis, phase 2 was simply a more workable way of muddling through the debt crisis and postponing the inevitable. The collective power of the commercial banks kept the lid on the pressure cooker in a way impossible in earlier times. These giant global banks had many more levers than the investment banks of the 1920s with which to keep debtors from outright repudiation. Among other things, they could cancel the trade credits of defaulting countries or reduce their overnight “interbank” credit lines. In consequence, as the 1980s progressed the banks were able to boost their loan-loss reserves and weather the crisis, while living standards tumbled in indebted Latin American countries. For most of the 1980s, the Baker Plan—the principle of lending new debt in exchange for economic reforms—was enshrined as the solution to the crisis, and it enjoyed the support of Lew Preston. But the promised economic growth never appeared. Instead, oppressed by interest payments and despite a prolonged boom in the industrial countries, Latin America suffered through a severe depression. How Latin American debtors could withstand the next global recession without widespread default was unclear as the 1980s came to an end.
归根结底，第二阶段只是更务实地勉强应对债务危机、拖延不可避免结局的一种方式。商业银行的集体力量压制住了这口高压锅，这在过去是无法想象的。这些大型全球银行比 20 世纪 20 年代的投资银行拥有更多手段，能阻止债务人彻底赖账。例如，它们可以取消违约国家的贸易信贷，或削减其隔夜“银行间”信贷额度。因此，随着 80 年代推进，银行得以增加贷款损失准备金并度过危机，而负债累累的拉美国家的生活水平却一落千丈。在整个 80 年代的大部分时间里，贝克计划——即通过提供新贷款换取经济改革的原则——被奉为解决危机的方案，并得到了卢·普雷斯顿的支持。但承诺的经济增长从未出现。 相反，受利息支付的重压，尽管工业国家经历了长期繁荣，拉丁美洲却陷入了严重萧条。随着 1980 年代接近尾声，尚不清楚拉美债务国如何能在不出现大规模违约的情况下挺过下一场全球经济衰退。

In February 1987, Brazil, stifled by a \$121-billion debt, which had grown monstrously through the reschedulings, declared a moratorium on repayment that lasted for a year and a half. The country seen as the model debtor in the 1970s had rudely disappointed the House of Morgan. In early 1988, Argentina stopped payment on its debt and fell billions of dollars into arrears. For all the power and ingenuity of the banks in dealing with the debt crisis of the 1980s, the upshot appeared frustratingly similar to that of earlier waves of default. In 1989, the new administration of President George Bush conceded that the only real solution was debt forgiveness. By that point, mobs were ransacking supermarkets in Argentina, as they had earlier in Brazil. In September, 1989, the Morgan bank acknowledged that its Latin debt was a hopeless fiasco by adding \$2 billion to its loan loss reserves, fully covering its longer maturity loans. The Morgan fling with the Third World was temporarily over.
1987 年 2 月，巴西被高达 1210 亿美元的债务压得喘不过气——这些债务经过多次重新安排后已膨胀得面目全非——最终宣布暂停还款，这一停就是一年半。这个在 70 年代被视为模范债务国的国家，狠狠地打了摩根银行的脸。1988 年初，阿根廷也停止偿还债务，拖欠金额高达数十亿美元。尽管银行在处理 80 年代债务危机时展现了各种手段和 ingenuity，但最终结果却与早几轮的违约浪潮如出一辙，令人沮丧。1989 年，乔治·布什总统的新政府不得不承认，唯一的真正出路就是债务减免。而这时，阿根廷的暴民已经开始洗劫超市，就像巴西之前发生的那样。1989 年 9 月，摩根银行不得不为此前向拉丁美洲提供的长期贷款计提 20 亿美元的坏账准备金，全额覆盖了这些贷款——这无异于承认其在拉丁美洲的债务已经彻底血本无归。摩根银行与第三世界的这段风流韵事，暂时画上了句号。

THERE was another coda to the Brazilian debt crisis that tattered the image of Morgan invincibility and belied any notion that it alone was immune to the corruptions of the Casino Age. Even as he chaired Brazil’s rescue, Tony Gebauer was leading a secret, illicit life as an embezzler—a term everyone later danced around in embarrassment, for it savored of small-time crooks and greasy hands in the till, not of the world’s toniest bank. Embezzlement was rare in the world of high-finance for obvious reasons: people made stupendous amounts of money, and if they wanted more, there were legal ways to get it.
巴西债务危机还有一段尾声，这段往事让摩根不可战胜的形象蒙上了阴影，也打破了它独善其身、不受赌场时代腐败侵蚀的说法。就在托尼·格鲍尔主持巴西纾困方案之际，他却暗地里过着秘密且违法的生活——一个侵吞公款者。事后每个人提及这个词时都尴尬地闪烁其词，因为它让人想到的是小贼和偷摸公款的脏手，而非全球最奢华的银行。在高端金融领域，侵吞公款案极为罕见，原因显而易见：人们已经赚得盆满钵满，若还想要更多，完全有合法途径获取。

At 23 Wall, there had been a curious negligence about Gebauer, a tendency to look the other way. He enjoyed an entrepreneurial freedom that was rare at Morgans. Later people would recall the fruits of his suspiciously profligate spending—a \$5-million Manhattan duplex coop, two homes in East Hampton worth a combined \$2 million, an apartment in France, and a share in a Brazilian coffee farm. This didn’t square with a \$150,000-a-year salary. With mild shock, Walter Page learned of a yacht that Gebauer had bought from a wealthy friend on Long Island’s Shelter Island. Only later did such details cohere into a telltale picture.
在华尔街 23 号，人们曾对格鲍尔有一种奇怪的疏忽，总是不由自主地移开目光。他享受着摩根公司罕见的创业自由。后来，人们会想起他那可疑的挥霍无度的成果——一套价值 500 万美元的曼哈顿复式公寓，两栋总价值 200 万美元的东汉普顿住宅，一套法国公寓，以及一家巴西咖啡农场的股份。这与 15 万美元的年薪完全不符。沃尔特·佩奇震惊地得知，格鲍尔从长岛谢尔特岛的一位富朋友那里购买了一艘游艇。直到后来，这些细节才拼凑成一幅清晰的图景。

There were two reasons why no one examined Gebauer critically. Everybody had a vague, somewhat correct notion that he came from a wealthy Venezuelan family. More significantly, he had reaped tens of millions in profit for the bank, compensating for its late start and patrician discomfort in Latin America. From 1981 to 1984, as senior vice-president for Latin America, Gebauer controlled most of Morgan’s Western Hemisphere lending outside North America. He was one of the few irreplaceable stars at a bank with a chronic glut of talented young executives.
没人对格鲍尔进行严格审查，原因有二。所有人都隐约且大致正确地知道，他出身于委内瑞拉的一个富裕家族。更关键的是，他为银行带来了数千万美元的利润，弥补了该行在拉丁美洲起步较晚、贵族气派水土不服的劣势。从 1981 年到 1984 年，作为拉丁美洲业务的高级副总裁，格鲍尔掌控着摩根银行在北美以外西半球的大部分贷款业务。在一家长期人才济济、年轻高管过剩的银行中，他是少数几个不可替代的明星人物之一。

Along with the big loans, Gebauer supervised the accounts of several hundred Latin American businessmen. Technically, they weren’t personal accounts but belonged to executives with whom the bank had commercial dealings—an honored Morgan technique to please and befriend the influential. In 1976, Gebauer had started to divert money from some Brazilian accounts in order to furnish his duplex apartment. In the end, he would dip into four accounts, including those of a landowner and a construction mogul. The money mostly resided in six Panamanian holding companies from which he issued cashier’s checks to himself. These illegal diversions lasted over nine years and amounted to \$6 million—this at a bank that prided itself on tight internal controls. The thefts, remarkably, persisted right through the Brazilian debt rescue.
在经手大额贷款的同时，格鲍尔还管理着数百名拉美商人的账户。严格来说，这些并非私人账户，而是属于与银行有商业往来的高管——这是摩根银行惯用的高明手段，用以讨好并结交权贵。1976 年，格鲍尔开始从一些巴西账户中挪用资金，用于装修自己的复式公寓。最终，他染指了四个账户，其中包括一位地主和一位建筑大亨的账户。这些资金大多存放在六家巴拿马控股公司，他从中给自己开具本票。这种非法挪用持续了九年多，累计金额达 600 万美元——而这家银行曾以其严格的内部控制为傲。令人瞩目的是，这些盗窃行为甚至在巴西债务救援期间也未曾停歇。

This was more than a straight embezzlement case, for Gebauer apparently drew on some form of “flight capital”—money smuggled from Latin America to evade taxes or exchange controls. Even as he withdrew the money, there was discussion of how such capital was jeopardizing the debt-rescue effort headed by him and the Morgan bank. As Brazil, Mexico, and Argentina raised billions of dollars in new loans, their disloyal, unethical nationals were stuffing suitcases with bills and flying north to open bank accounts. The big Wall Street banks making the Latin American loans wooed the flight capital and ended up taking as deposits money they had recently lent out.
这不仅仅是一起简单的贪污案，因为格鲍尔显然动用了某种形式的“外逃资本”——为避税或逃避外汇管制而从拉丁美洲走私出境的资金。就在他提取这笔钱的同时，人们已在讨论此类资本如何危及由他和摩根银行主导的债务纾困行动。当巴西、墨西哥和阿根廷筹集数十亿美元新贷款时，那些不忠不义的本国国民却将钞票塞满行李箱，飞往北方开设银行账户。那些向拉丁美洲发放贷款的大型华尔街银行一面招揽这些外逃资本，一面又将刚刚贷出的资金作为存款吸纳回来。

Behind the title of international private banking, Morgans and other banks helped wealthy Latin Americans to invest in offshore trusts and investment companies. These devices could aid the unscrupulous in dodging taxes. In the 1970s, Morgan Guaranty and other banks also opened Miami subsidiaries to tap the personal wealth of visiting Latin Americans. In the wrong hands, confidential Morgan accounts could serve as excellent cover for illegal activity. All the Wall Street banks had mysterious Latin American depositors who seldom appeared in person. “They particularly don’t want monthly statements or any other mail sent to their home countries,” noted *Fortune* in 1982. “Their accounts at places like Morgan are labeled ‘hold mail.’ They drop by in person from time to time to look at the statements.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428590)
在国际私人银行业务的光环背后，摩根等银行曾帮助拉美富豪投资离岸信托和投资公司。这些工具可能被不择手段的人用来逃税。上世纪 70 年代，摩根担保等银行还在迈阿密开设子公司，以吸纳前来旅行的拉美富人的个人财富。若落入不当之手，摩根的保密账户可能成为非法活动的绝佳掩护。所有华尔街银行都拥有神秘的拉美存款人，这些人很少亲自现身。《财富》杂志在 1982 年指出：“他们尤其不希望月度账单或任何邮件寄回本国。他们在摩根等银行的账户都标注为‘保留邮件’。他们会时不时亲自来查看账单。”

By extreme estimates, commercial banks were booking more in deposits of flight capital than they extended in new Latin American loans, making them net borrowers from the region. Flight capital siphoned off an estimated one-half of Mexico’s borrowed money, one-third of Argentina’s. Among those bemoaning the problem was Morgan economist Rimmer de Vries. “Capital flight accelerates, enhances, and aggravates a problem that exists,” he stated.[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428741) Morgan chairman Lew Preston was no less disturbed, telling one annual meeting, “It’s a terrible problem for the banks. If the amount of Mexican investment abroad—if that interest—were brought back into Mexico, it would cover their debt service.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428891) Even though American banks could legally accept flight capital, Morgans had a stated policy of questioning depositors about the origin and purpose of any suspect accounts. Yet Gebauer was apparently plundering “hold mail” checking accounts. Otherwise, why did years elapse before depositors detected the theft? Why weren’t they monitoring their accounts more closely? One raided Brazilian depositor reportedly hadn’t shown up in five years.
根据最极端的估算，商业银行吸收的外逃资本存款规模已超过其对拉美地区的新增贷款，从而成为该地区的净借款方。外逃资本抽走了墨西哥约一半的借款，阿根廷三分之一的借款。摩根经济学家里默·德弗里斯对此问题痛心疾首，他宣称：“资本外逃会加速、放大并加剧现存的问题。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428741) 摩根董事长卢·普雷斯顿同样深感不安，在一次年会上表示：“这对银行来说是个可怕的问题。如果墨西哥在海外的投资——如果那笔利息——能回流到墨西哥，就足以覆盖其债务偿付。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2428891) 尽管美国银行在法律上可以接受外逃资本，摩根却有一项公开政策：对任何可疑账户的存款人追查资金来源和用途。然而，格鲍尔显然在盗用“邮件暂存”支票账户。 不然的话，为什么存户们过了好几年才发现存款被盗？为什么他们没有更密切地监控自己的账户？据报道，一位受害的巴西存户已经五年没有露面了。

Prohibited from maintaining dollar deposits in the United States, Brazilians customarily gave their Wall Street bankers wide latitude in managing their investments. It was later unclear whether Gebauer had permission to withdraw money from some clients’ accounts—something to which his lawyer would make cryptic allusion. Yet this couldn’t have been uniformly true, for Gebauer manufactured bogus statements on Morgan stationery, then mailed them to clients. To plug holes in the accounts, he secured Morgan loans of about \$2.9 million. Why would he have resorted to these extraordinary measures if he were acting with the consent of his depositors?
由于被禁止在美国持有美元存款，巴西人通常给予他们在华尔街的银行家们很大的自由度来管理他们的投资。事后尚不清楚格鲍尔是否有权从部分客户的账户中取款——他的律师对此只是含糊其辞地暗示过。但这显然不可能适用于所有情况，因为格鲍尔曾用摩根信纸伪造对账单，然后寄给客户。为了填补账户漏洞，他还从摩根获得了大约 290 万美元的贷款。如果他的行为得到了存户的同意，他又何必采取这些非常规手段呢？

In 1982, even as Brazil teetered on the edge, Gebauer took \$1.5 million from the account of a Brazilian named Francisco Catao. This money represented a “commission” Catao received from an arms dealer in exchange for the dealer’s being introduced to Gebauer. This, in turn, led to a \$35-million Morgan loan to the arms merchant. Might Gebauer have had a proprietary feeling toward that particular \$1.5 million? In an equally bizarre twist, he diverted embezzled money into his own Latin American business, using it to make loans at low interest rates—as if he were entering the banking field as a minicompetitor to the Morgan bank itself.
1982 年，就在巴西摇摇欲坠之际，格鲍尔从一个名叫弗朗西斯科·卡陶的巴西人账户中挪走了 150 万美元。这笔钱是卡陶从一名军火商那里获得的“佣金”，作为介绍格鲍尔给该商人的回报。而这次牵线最终促成了摩根银行向这位军火商提供 3500 万美元贷款。难道格鲍尔对那特定的 150 万美元产生了某种占有欲？同样离奇的是，他还将盗用的资金注入自己在拉丁美洲的业务中，以低利率放贷——仿佛他以迷你竞争对手的姿态，闯入了摩根银行自己的地盘。

Tony Gebauer lacked any of the standard motivations for committing a crime. Unlike the routine cases, his crime coincided not with failure but with stunning success in the sphere of international banking. He had no reason whatever to resent the bank or wish to embarrass it. In fact, he had a deep, abiding love for Morgan traditions, lining his bookshelf with Morgan history and taking great pride in his association with the bank. At tremendous sacrifice to his personal wealth, he remained there when he could have parlayed his connections into a \$1-million-a-year income at an investment bank. It’s even conceivable that he resorted to crime so he could remain at the bank yet live in a style befitting his fantasies. He apparently let months pass without touching the Brazilian accounts and wasn’t consumed by his crime. It was more tangential, gratifying some psychic need left unsatisfied even by his exceptional career.
托尼·格鲍尔没有任何常见的犯罪动机。与一般案件不同，他的犯罪行为并非发生在失败之际，而是在国际银行业取得惊人成功之时。他毫无理由怨恨银行或希望让其难堪。事实上，他对摩根传统怀有深厚而持久的感情，书架上摆满了摩根历史书籍，并为自己与这家银行的关联深感自豪。他个人财富付出了巨大代价，本可凭借人脉在投资银行获得百万美元年薪，却仍选择留在那里。甚至可以说，他之所以铤而走险，是为了既能留在银行，又能过上符合自己幻想的奢华生活。他显然数月未动巴西账户，也并未被罪行所吞噬。这更像是一种边缘行为，满足了某种即便他那非凡职业生涯也无法填补的心理需求。

Like many embezzlers, Gebauer planned to make restitution someday. Much like the Brazilians he rescued, he was defeated by the interest, not the principal, accumulating on his burdensome debt—\$2 million of it. Late in the summer of 1985, after a twenty-four-year Morgan career, he left for Drexel Burnham Lambert, to work with Michael Milken on a special project to repackage Third World debt into junk bonds (the 1920s solution). Some at Morgans thought his career had been derailed by the controversial Brazilian debt rescheduling. Shortly after he left, the bank was alerted to his crime by a puzzled Brazilian client whose money supposedly on deposit in New York was wired from Venezuela. The timing seemed coincidental: neither Brazil nor the bank needed Tony Gebauer any longer. He was found out when nobody but he would suffer from exposure of the crime. The House of Morgan sent Price Waterhouse auditors and trusty Davis, Polk lawyers to Brazil to investigate. They netted an accomplice to Gebauer—Keith McDermott, a vice-president who allegedly received \$200,000 in kickbacks for Morgan work on behalf of two clients. The bank’s investigators passed on their information to the Fed and the U.S. district attorney’s office. When confronted with the charges by Drexel Burnham officials, Gebauer resigned on the spot.
和许多贪污者一样，格鲍尔原本打算日后归还。和他所解救的那些巴西人一样，压垮他的不是本金，而是那笔沉重债务累积的利息——高达 200 万美元。1985 年夏末，在摩根工作了二十四年后，他跳槽到了德崇证券（Drexel Burnham Lambert），与迈克尔·米尔肯合作，负责一个将第三世界债务重新打包成垃圾债券的特殊项目（这正是 20 世纪 20 年代的解决方案）。摩根内部有人认为，他的职业生涯因巴西债务重组的争议而偏离了正轨。他离开后不久，一位困惑的巴西客户向银行举报了他的罪行——这位客户原本以为存放在纽约的存款，却从委内瑞拉被汇出。时机似乎纯属巧合：无论巴西还是摩根银行，都不再需要托尼·格鲍尔了。当除了他本人之外再无人会因罪行曝光而受害时，他的秘密才被揭穿。摩根大通派出了普华永道的审计师和值得信赖的戴维斯·波尔克律师事务所的律师前往巴西展开调查。 他们抓获了格鲍尔的一名同伙——副总裁基思·麦克德蒙特，据称他因代表两位客户为摩根士丹利处理业务而收取了 20 万美元的回扣。银行调查人员将信息转交给了美联储和联邦地区检察官办公室。当德崇证券的高管们当面提出指控时，格鲍尔当场辞职。

When the affair hit the news in 1986, it made headlines in Brazil as well as in New York. How had the world’s best-run bank missed the scandal for nine years? Gebauer reportedly believed the millions were too trivial to warrant the attention of a bank grappling with billion-dollar debts. After the scandal broke, the bank was in a sticky situation, guilty of either incompetence or complicity. It portrayed Gebauer as a lone culprit and swore that no customer lost a penny in the end. “Our investigation convinces us that the responsibility for wrongdoing lies with one person. . . . We think it’s unfair that other people be implicated,” declared a spokesman.[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429101) Gebauer quickly became a taboo subject at 23 Wall. Morgan officials still find it hard to utter his name and often refer to him as “that fellow,” as if they had never known him very well.
1986 年丑闻曝光后，巴西和纽约的头条新闻都被它霸占了。全球运营得最好的银行，怎么就能对这场风波视而不见整整九年？据报道，格鲍尔认为那区区几百万美元，在动辄背负数十亿债务的银行眼里根本不值一提。丑闻爆发后，该银行处境尴尬——要么是无能，要么是同谋。它把格鲍尔塑造成唯一的罪魁祸首，并信誓旦旦地表示最终没有一位客户损失一分钱。一位发言人宣称："我们的调查使我们确信，过错的责任在于一个人……我们认为牵连其他人是不公平的。" [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429101) 格鲍尔很快成了华尔街 23 号大楼里的禁忌话题。摩根高管至今仍难以启齿说出他的名字，常以"那家伙"代称，仿佛他们从未真正了解过他。

Gebauer didn’t contest the charges. To avoid the stigma of embezzlement, he pleaded guilty to bank fraud, tax evasion, and doctoring statements. Because he had submitted some surreal tax returns—one year he banked over \$1 million in taxable income but reported only \$21,000—he owed the Internal Revenue several million dollars in back taxes and penalties. He also paid back \$8 million in principal and interest to the bank. His clever lawyer, Stanley Arkin, referred obliquely to flight capital and hinted that Gebauer might have had authority to use some Brazilian money: “That authority was premised on the unusual and Byzantine relationships that often exist between bankers and flight capitalists.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429253) Such loose talk made Morgans jittery and eager to strike a deal.
格鲍尔没有对指控进行抗辩。为了避免背上挪用公款的恶名，他承认犯有银行欺诈、逃税和篡改报表等罪行。由于他提交了一些匪夷所思的纳税申报——有一年他的应纳税收入超过 100 万美元，却只申报了 2.1 万美元——因此欠美国国税局数百万美元的欠税和罚款。此外，他还向银行偿还了 800 万美元的本息。他的精明律师斯坦利·阿金巧妙地影射了外逃资本，并暗示格鲍尔可能有权动用部分巴西资金：“这种权力建立在银行家与外逃资本家之间常见的那种复杂而隐秘的关系之上。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429253) 这种不严谨的言论让摩根银行感到不安，急于达成和解。

In February 1987, a contrite Tony Gebauer stood in a blue pin-striped suit before Judge Robert W. Sweet for sentencing. The judge saw a large dimension of fantasy in Gebauer’s life, a venal excess characteristic of the age. “You are indeed a Lucifer, a fallen angel of the banking world,” he told him. “Although your employment at the top of your profession provided you with a princely income, you spent like an emperor.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429404) Gebauer received a three-and-a-half-year prison sentence but served only half that time.
1987 年 2 月，托尼·格鲍尔身穿蓝色细条纹西装，满脸悔意地站在法官罗伯特·W·斯威特面前等待宣判。法官认为格鲍尔的生活中存在严重的幻想成分，这正是那个时代贪婪泛滥的特征。“你确实是路西法，银行界的堕落天使，”法官对他说，“尽管你身处行业顶端，收入堪比王侯，但你的挥霍却如帝王一般。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429404) 格鲍尔被判三年半监禁，但实际只服了一半刑期。

The Gebauer affair left behind red faces and personal wreckage in the corporate suites at Morgans. Half a dozen executives were shifted about. In a sad conclusion, Tony Gebauer, so proud of his Morgan employment, ended up disgracing the bank.
格鲍尔事件让摩根公司的高管们面红耳赤，也留下了个人残局。六名高管被调职。结局令人唏嘘：曾以在摩根工作为傲的托尼·格鲍尔，最终却让银行蒙羞。
