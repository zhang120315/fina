# Chapter Six: Trust

ASSIGNED to J. S. Morgan and Company in London in 1898, Jack Morgan, now thirty-one, was a lonely prince in exile. Tall and broad-shouldered, he was a husky young man with a broad face, a direct gaze, a black mustache, and prominent nose that never assumed the gross proportions of his father’s. From afar, Jack watched the epochal events unfolding in New York—the formation of U.S. Steel and the cornering of Northern Pacific—with a vague yearning. He may have felt his date with destiny had been continually rescheduled. While conceding London’s pleasures, he complained to his mother, “when I think of home the time does seem a bit long.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223317) He grumbled how “profoundest peace” reigned at 22 Old Broad Street, while everything was “jumping about” at 23 Wall Street.[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223519) Worst of all, he had to watch Pierpont turn the spotlight of his favor on Robert Bacon.
1898 年，时年 31 岁的杰克·摩根被派往伦敦的 J. S. Morgan and Company 任职，像流落在外的孤独王子。他身材高大，肩膀宽阔，体格壮实，方脸盘，目光炯炯，留着黑色胡须，鼻子虽大却不似父亲那般夸张。他远远观望着纽约发生的划时代事件——美国钢铁公司的组建和对北太平洋铁路的控股——心中隐隐有些向往。或许他觉得自己与命运约定的时刻一再被推迟。尽管享受着伦敦的惬意，他还是向母亲抱怨道：“一想到家，就感觉日子特别漫长。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223317) 他吐槽老宽街 22 号笼罩在“最深沉的平静”中，而华尔街 23 号却处处“风生水起”。 [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223519) 最糟的是，他眼看着皮尔庞特把关注的聚光灯转向了罗伯特·培根。

At first, Jack’s stay in London was meant to be temporary, but it took a few years before tangled personnel problems at J. S. Morgan and Company were straightened out. In 1897, Pierpont’s brother-in-law Walter Hayes Burns died and was replaced by Jack’s cousin Walter Spencer Morgan Burns. The senior Burns’s death left the London bank short of experienced hands. Young Walter’s sister Mary married Lewis Harcourt, the first Viscount Harcourt, spawning a branch of “British Morgans” who were lineal descendants of Junius Morgan. From this blue-blooded lineage would spring Lord William Harcourt, a postwar Morgan Grenfell chairman. A photograph of Pierpont at a house party at the Harcourt estate, Nuneham Park, in 1902 shows Mary Harcourt seated next to King Edward VII.
起初，杰克在伦敦只是暂住，但 J.S.摩根公司内部错综复杂的人事问题花了几年时间才理顺。1897 年，皮尔庞特的姐夫沃尔特·海斯·伯恩斯去世，由杰克的表兄沃尔特·斯宾塞·摩根·伯恩斯接替其职位。老伯恩斯的离世使伦敦分行缺少经验丰富的干将。小沃尔特的姐姐玛丽嫁给了第一代哈考特子爵刘易斯·哈考特，由此衍生出朱尼厄斯·摩根直系后裔的"英国摩根"分支。这个蓝血世系后来孕育出威廉·哈考特勋爵，即战后摩根建富银行的董事长。一张 1902 年皮尔庞特在哈考特庄园纽纳姆公园参加家庭派对的照片中，玛丽·哈考特正坐在爱德华七世身旁。

During his London exile, which lasted until 1905, Jack often seemed embarrassed by his remoteness from Pierpont. To inquiries as to whether Pierpont would attend Edward VII’s coronation, he confessed sheepishly, “He is not easy to keep track of and I have almost given up.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223681) (In the end, Teddy Roosevelt made Jack a special attaché to the Westminster Abbey coronation.) Once when Jack wished to join his father for a naval pageant at Spithead, he lamented that Pierpont “will probably not think of asking us.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223814) He was often excluded from business deals and had to read about the U.S. Steel trust in the newspapers.
在伦敦流亡期间（直至 1905 年），杰克常因与皮尔庞特疏远而显得窘迫。当被问及皮尔庞特是否会出席爱德华七世的加冕礼时，他难为情地承认：“他行踪不定，我几乎都放弃了。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223681) （最终，西奥多·罗斯福任命杰克为威斯敏斯特教堂加冕礼的特派专员。）一次杰克想随父亲参加斯皮特黑德的海军阅兵式，他哀叹道，皮尔庞特“多半不会想起邀请我们”。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223814) 他常被排除在商业交易之外，只能从报纸上获悉美国钢铁托拉斯的消息。

Pierpont liked Jack but found him lacking in fire and grit, which only accentuated Jack’s insecurities. When Pierpont sailed from London in 1899, Jack wrote his mother how things couldn’t proceed in New York in Pierpont’s absence. He added, “I only hope it will never come to that with me. Probably it won’t owing to the fact that things always will move on without me.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223939) The scope of Pierpont’s business ventures was too vast to allow for a son’s self-doubt to be of concern, and the problem was exacerbated by Jack’s not being as bright or as forceful as his father.
皮尔庞特喜欢杰克，但觉得他缺乏冲劲和胆气，这反而加深了杰克的不安。1899 年皮尔庞特乘船离开伦敦时，杰克写信给母亲说，皮尔庞特不在，纽约那边的事根本推不动。他接着写道：“我只希望这种事永远不会发生在我身上。很可能不会，因为没了我，事情照样能转。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223939) 皮尔庞特的生意版图实在太大了，儿子那点自我怀疑根本不值得操心，更何况杰克远不如他父亲聪明、强势，这让问题更加棘手。

Another son might have rebelled. Jack sulked and pined, waiting for approval. Like Junius, he worried perpetually about Pierpont’s work binges and “imprudent” appetite and was steadily watchful of him. He described with whimsical humor the sight of his father playing dominoes with Mary Burns: “It is too funny to see Father and Aunt Mary gravely sitting down to play that imbecile game.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224128) He also saw his father’s vanity, noting how after one good deed he was “simply too pleased with himself.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224320) Jack also spied Pierpont’s inner pain, his secret well of loneliness: “He is very well and jolly by bits but sometimes I see he feels as lonely as I do and he looks as glum as if he hadn’t a friend in the world.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224510) Considering that Jack was also cheering up his mother—a partially deaf, sickly woman abandoned by Pierpont for months at a time—one finds admirable his capacity for evenhanded empathy and tender solicitude toward both parents.
换作别的儿子，或许早就反抗了。可杰克只是闷闷不乐、郁郁寡欢，默默等待着认可。和朱尼厄斯一样，他无时无刻不担心皮尔庞特工作过度，还有他那“欠妥”的胃口，并始终留心着他的举动。他用一种诙谐的幽默描绘父亲与玛丽·伯恩斯玩多米诺骨牌的情景：“看父亲和玛丽姑姑一本正经地坐下来玩那种蠢游戏，实在太好笑了。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224128) 他也看出了父亲的虚荣——某次做了一件好事后，他“简直对自己满意得不得了”。 [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224320) 杰克还窥见了皮尔庞特内心的痛苦，他那秘密的孤独之源：“他大部分时候心情不错，有说有笑，但有时我能看出他和我一样孤单，他垂头丧气的样子，仿佛全世界没有一个朋友。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224510) 考虑到杰克同时还在安慰母亲——一个耳朵半聋、体弱多病、常被皮尔庞特抛下数月之久的女人——他竟能对父母双方都保持如此公允的体恤与温柔的关怀，实在令人敬佩。

Jack’s fatalistic acceptance of the London years was eased somewhat by a show of generosity from Pierpont. When Jack arrived in 1898, his father gave him and his wife, Jessie, the use of 13 Princes Gate. Pierpont added 14 Princes Gate to the property and joined the two townhouses. The original house now had the magnificence of a great museum and was resplendent with oils by Velázquez, Rubens, Rembrandt, and Turner—export duties kept Pierpont from taking the collection to America. Jack also used Dover House, Junius’s country estate at Roe-hampton, with its jersey cattle and old-fashioned dairy. Ecstatic at this fatherly attention, Jack told his mother, “He has been dear to us ever since we landed, most thoughtful of everything and immensely interested in Jessie’s social *career!* I know he has much enjoyed our being in the house, for it must have been very lonely for him with no one there and we have not hampered him at all, or bothered him with responsibilities.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224672) In 1901, Pierpont gave Jack a Christmas gift—an amount of money so large that he bought a portrait by Sir Joshua Reynolds with just part of it.
皮尔庞特的一番慷慨之举，让杰克对伦敦岁月的宿命般顺受有所缓解。1898 年杰克抵达时，父亲将王子门 13 号交给杰克夫妇使用。皮尔庞特后来又买下王子门 14 号，将两幢联排别墅打通。原本的宅邸如今气派非凡，宛如一座宏伟博物馆，陈列着委拉斯开兹、鲁本斯、伦勃朗和透纳的油画——因出口关税高昂，皮尔庞特未能将这些收藏运往美国。杰克还使用了朱尼厄斯位于罗汉普顿的乡间庄园多佛府邸，那里有泽西奶牛和传统奶制品场。对父亲这番关爱，杰克欣喜若狂，他写信告诉母亲："自我们踏足此地，他对我们体贴备至，事事考虑周全，对杰西的社交生涯也极为上心！我知道他很享受我们住在宅子里，毕竟他独自一人时想必十分孤寂，而我们既未给他添任何麻烦，也未曾以责任相扰。"”1901 年，Pierpont 送给 Jack 一份圣诞礼物——一笔巨款，单用其中一部分就买下了 Sir Joshua Reynolds 的一幅肖像画。

Yet Jack and his family found life amid such splendor a shade overwhelming. Every evening—whether Pierpont was in Europe or not—the domestic staff would place periodicals and warm milk beside the master’s bed and adjust his reading lamp. And with the townhouse full of so many fragile masterpieces, the housekeeper just didn’t dust on days when she felt jittery. Jessie took pride that nothing was broken, but the Morgan children, who now numbered two boys and two girls, found the need for self-control in their play stifling. Later the children recalled family prayers, reading Thackeray and Trollope, strolling in Hyde Park—everything but fun at Princes Gate.
然而，Jack 和他的家人在如此奢华的生活中感到有些难以承受。每天晚上——无论 Pierpont 是否身在欧洲——佣人都会在主卧床头放上期刊和温牛奶，并调好他的阅读灯。由于联排别墅里摆满了脆弱的杰作，管家在自己感到紧张的日子干脆就不掸灰了。Jessie 为没有打碎任何东西而自豪，但 Morgan 家的孩子们——如今是两男两女——觉得玩耍时处处需要克制，令人窒息。后来孩子们回忆起家庭祈祷、读 Thackeray 和 Trollope、在海德公园散步——在 Princes Gate，什么都有，唯独没有乐趣。”

In 1901, Jack rented Aldenham Abbey, a three-hundred-acre country estate in Hertfordshire stocked with pheasant and Southdown sheep said to rival the king’s in quality. Jack had a British gentleman’s taste for solid country comforts. After buying the abbey in 1910, he restored its original name, Wall Hall. Landscaped by Humphrey Repton, the estate included a turreted house with fake ruins, a conservatory full of tropical plants, and a library that resembled a college chapel. In the Anglophile Morgan world, Pierpont’s Dover House staff would meet Jack’s Wall Hall crew for cricket matches. The Morgans counterbalanced this Britishness with American touches—for instance, by shipping New York State pippin apples to the London partners.
1901 年，杰克租下了赫特福德郡一座占地三百英亩的乡间庄园——奥登汉姆修道院，园内放养着野鸡和南唐羊，据说品质足以媲美国王的羊群。杰克有着英国绅士般的品味，钟情于踏实的乡村安逸感。1910 年买下这座修道院后，他恢复了其原名——沃尔庄园。这片由汉弗莱·雷普顿精心设计的庄园，包含一栋带有角楼的宅邸，配有人造假废墟、一间满是热带植物的温室，以及一座宛若学院礼拜堂的图书馆。在亲英的摩根家族圈子里，皮尔庞特的杜弗庄园员工常与杰克的沃尔庄园团队进行板球比赛。摩根家族用美国元素来平衡这种英式气息——例如，将纽约州的皮平苹果运往伦敦的合伙人处。

For Jack, the London years were passed in a gilded cage. He had many friends from merchant-banking families and worked out at Sandow’s gym with Eric Hambro. As neighbors there were Earl Grey and Florence Nightingale; for occasional dinner companions, Rudyard Kipling, Henry James, Sir James Barrie, and Mark Twain. Most of all, he had Jessie, a beautiful round-faced woman with pale golden hair, a fair complexion, and smoky blue eyes. Although she had gone to England grudgingly, its society soon reminded her of Boston’s, and she became a confirmed Anglophile. She hoped that one of her two sons—Junius Spencer, Jr., born in 1892, or Henry Sturgis, born in London in 1900—would marry an American and the other a British woman; they both ended up marrying Americans.
对杰克来说，在伦敦的岁月如同身处镀金的牢笼。他结交了许多出身商人银行家族的朋友，还和埃里克·汉布罗一起在桑多健身馆锻炼。邻居中有格雷伯爵和弗洛伦斯·南丁格尔；偶尔共进晚餐的则有鲁德亚德·吉卜林、亨利·詹姆斯、詹姆斯·巴里爵士和马克·吐温。而最重要的是，他有杰西相伴——这位美丽的圆脸女人，浅金色头发、白皙肌肤，还有一双烟灰色的蓝眼睛。尽管她起初是勉强来到英格兰，但当地的上流社会很快让她想起了波士顿的氛围，于是她成了一名坚定的亲英派。她希望自己的两个儿子——1892 年出生的小朱尼厄斯·斯宾塞和 1900 年生于伦敦的亨利·斯特吉斯——一个娶美国姑娘，另一个娶英国女子；结果兄弟俩最终都娶了美国人。

Jessie Morgan didn’t believe in an outside education for girls, and her daughters, Jane and Frances, were tutored at Wall Hall; they never set foot in a formal schoolroom. Jack held that a university education reduced a young woman’s femininity, so college was also out of the question. The girls weren’t allowed to talk to strangers on steamers or in public places and later saw their upbringing as a suffocating round of social duties.
杰西·摩根不相信女孩应该接受校外教育，她的女儿简和弗朗西斯都在沃尔庄园接受家教，从未踏进过正式的教室。杰克认为大学教育会削弱年轻女性的女性气质，所以上大学也是不可能的。女孩们不被允许在轮船或公共场所与陌生人交谈，后来她们把这段成长经历看作是被窒息般的社交责任所包围的岁月。

Jessie and Jack Morgan’s marriage was so all-encompassing and so absorbing as to exclude their own children at times. Jessie would not only rule Jack’s estates with crisp, managerial efficiency, but she would guide her husband, advise him, and support him emotionally. Having watched the chill descend upon his parents’ marriage and been conditioned by a confessional intimacy with his mother, Jack established a marriage that would be the exact opposite of his father’s; philandering, for instance, was one Morgan tradition he would not perpetuate.
杰西和杰克·摩根的婚姻如此包罗万象且令人沉醉，以至于有时将他们自己的孩子排除在外。杰西不仅以干练高效的管理方式掌管着杰克的庄园，还会引导丈夫、给他建议、并在情感上支持他。目睹了父母婚姻中的冷漠，加上与母亲那种忏悔式的亲密关系的熏陶，杰克建立了一段与他父亲截然相反的婚姻——例如，拈花惹草这种摩根家的传统，他绝不延续。

Jack’s London stay had immense advantages for the House of Morgan. England would be Jack’s second home, and he grew as tearfully patriotic as any British subject. In 1900, after watching Queen Victoria ride by, he said, “That wonderful little old woman in black and sables with the big spectacles means so much to so many—she represents in a current form so much of the past that it is very thrilling to see her driving through the crowd.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224865) During the Boer War, he stood in a cheering throng before the Mansion House after Ladysmith, under siege by the Boers for four months, had been relieved by British troops. Amid a fanfare of silver trumpets, he heard the new King Edward VII proclaimed at Saint James’s Palace. He always loved British pageantry.
杰克在伦敦的居留对摩根家族而言意义非凡。伦敦成了他的第二故乡，他的爱国热忱甚至不亚于任何英国臣民，常为此热泪盈眶。1900 年，目睹维多利亚女王乘车经过时，他感叹道：“那位身着黑貂皮、戴着大眼镜的矮小老妇人，对太多人而言意义非凡——她以鲜活的形式承载了太多过往，看到她驱车穿过人群，实在令人心潮澎湃。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2224865) 布尔战争期间，莱迪史密斯遭布尔人围攻四个月后终被英军解救，他站在市长官邸前欢呼的人群中。在银号角的嘹亮声中，他在圣詹姆斯宫听到了新国王爱德华七世的宣告。他始终钟爱英国的壮丽盛典。

Jack and Jessie were received into social circles that were closed to most American industrialists of the era. On February 21, 1898, Jack trooped along in sword and cocked hat as Jessie was presented in the throne room of Buckingham Palace. Bedecked in glittering jewels and black robes, Queen Victoria presided in solemn state while Jessie came forward in diamond tiara and obligatory ostrich feathers—the London *Daily Mail* later gushed in describing her beauty and her white satin train trimmed with blue velvet and pink roses. The Morgans also befriended the vivacious Lady Sybil Smith and her husband, Vivian Hugh Smith. Lady Sibyl took them to Windsor Castle to meet her mother, Lady Antrim, a lady-in-waiting, who gave them a private showing of the queen’s Holbein and Leonardo drawings. Almost without realizing it, Jack was forging connections that would provide the Morgans with a unique entrée into the society of British nobility and politicians.
杰克和杰西被接纳进入了当时大多数美国实业家无缘的社交圈。1898 年 2 月 21 日，杰克佩剑戴帽，与杰西一同进入白金汉宫的觐见厅。维多利亚女王身佩璀璨珠宝、身着黑色长袍，庄严地主持着仪式，杰西则戴着钻石冠冕和必备的鸵鸟羽毛走上前去——《伦敦每日邮报》后来在描述她的美貌以及她那镶有蓝丝绒和粉色玫瑰的白色缎面拖尾裙时，满篇溢美之词。摩根夫妇还与活泼的西比尔·史密斯夫人及其丈夫维维安·休·史密斯成了朋友。西比尔夫人带他们去温莎城堡拜见了她的母亲——担任女侍臣的安特里姆夫人，后者私下让他们欣赏了女王收藏的霍尔拜因和达·芬奇画作。几乎是在不经意间，杰克正在建立一种特殊的纽带，为摩根家族打开了进入英国贵族与政界圈子的独特通道。

As a microcosm of the Anglo-American alliance, the House of Morgan would faithfully reflect its internal power shifts. If the New York office basked in London’s glory after the Civil War, the situation was reversed in the new century, with J. S. Morgan and Company participating in creasingly in issues that originated in New York. Much of the London capital came from Pierpont, who by the early 1900s was pocketing anywhere from one half to three-quarters of the annual profits booked at 22 Old Broad Street. The London house reflected some of Pierpont’s rambunctious spirit. Pierpont’s first biographer, Carl Hovey, wrote, “Inside the office there is always a marked amount of bustle and confusion, contrasting with the sedate atmosphere of the typical London institutions surrounding it.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225055) Pierpont was just egalitarian enough to stop the practice of clerks bowing in his presence.
作为英美联盟的一个缩影，摩根财团忠实地反映了其内部权力格局的变迁。如果说内战后纽约分行还仰仗着伦敦的荣光，那么到了新世纪，局面已然反转——J.S.摩根公司越来越多地参与纽约发起的事务。伦敦的大部分资本来自皮尔庞特，到 20 世纪初，他每年从老布罗德街 22 号所获的利润分成，已占该行年度利润的一半到四分之三。伦敦分行也沾染了皮尔庞特那股不羁的作风。皮尔庞特的首位传记作者卡尔·霍维写道：“办公室里总是充满明显的忙乱与嘈杂，与周围典型伦敦金融机构的庄重氛围形成鲜明对比。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225055) 皮尔庞特颇有平等精神，他废除了职员们在他面前鞠躬的规矩。

Although the Morgans were the darlings of the British establishment, the relationship would always be fraught with tension—less a love affair than a tense jockeying for power. The British could never figure out whether Pierpont and Company were allies or the first wave of a barbarian horde. Wall Street was gaining on the City in the fight for financial supremacy, with the Morgans overtaking the Barings and Rothschilds. “In London, the resuscitated Barings are the only people nearly in the same rank with us,” said Sir Clinton Dawkins, a new partner of f. S. Morgan and Company, in 1901. “In the US they are nowhere now, a mere cipher, and the US is going to dominate in most ways.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225233) To combat the Yankee upstarts, Barings and Rothschilds, the great nineteenth-century rivals, became less antagonistic toward each other.
尽管摩根家族是英国当权者的宠儿，但双方的关系始终暗藏紧张——与其说是亲密合作，不如说是剑拔弩张的权力角逐。英国人始终拿不准，皮尔庞特公司究竟是盟友，还是蛮族入侵的先头部队。在争夺金融霸权的较量中，华尔街正逐渐超越伦敦金融城，而摩根家族也接连击败巴林家族和罗斯柴尔德家族。1901 年，F. S. Morgan & Company 的新合伙人克林顿·道金斯爵士表示："在伦敦，重整旗鼓的巴林银行几乎是唯一能与我们平起平坐的对手。而在美国，他们早已无足轻重，形同虚设，美国将在多数领域占据主导地位。" [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225233) 为了对抗这群来自美国的暴发户，19 世纪的两大宿敌——巴林银行和罗斯柴尔德家族——反倒放下了彼此间的敌意。

During the Boer War, the British government, its gold depleted, turned to Rothschilds in London and Morgans in New York to raise Exchequer bonds. When Pierpont initially balked, the British treasury brought in Barings as well, adding to his displeasure. Sir Clinton Dawkins called the chancellor, Hicks-Beach, “notoriously stupid and most unbusinesslike.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225398) The Boer War financing of 1900 had disquieting effects in the City. J. S. Morgan’s new office manager, Edward C. Grenfell, noted dismay in London when half of the issue was scheduled for New York. Where Junius had accommodated the Rothschilds, Pierpont defied them, secretly demanding a higher commission on the issue—blackmail to which Britain reluctantly acceded. On the 1902 issue, the Rothschilds unsuccessfully tried to freeze Morgan from the syndicate. From then on, Grenfell, with grim triumph, would note in his journal the mounting ascendancy of the House of Morgan over the House of Rothschild.
布尔战争期间，英国政府因黄金储备告急，转而向伦敦的罗斯柴尔德家族和纽约的摩根家族寻求发行国库债券。皮尔庞特最初有所犹豫，英国财政部便将巴林银行也拉入局中，这令他颇为不悦。克林顿·道金斯爵士称财政大臣希克斯-比奇“出了名的愚钝且最不懂生意经”。1900 年布尔战争的融资在伦敦金融城引起了不安。J. S. 摩根的新任办公室主任爱德华·C·格伦费尔注意到，当一半债券计划在纽约发行时，伦敦方面一片沮丧。当年朱尼厄斯曾与罗斯柴尔德家族合作，而皮尔庞特却公然对抗，秘密要求提高发行佣金——英国政府不情愿地屈服于这种勒索。到了 1902 年的债券发行，罗斯柴尔德家族试图将摩根排除在承销团之外，却未能成功。从此以后，格伦费尔带着冷峻的得意，在日记中记下了摩根家族相对于罗斯柴尔德家族日益上升的主导地位。

With the 1901 creation of U.S. Steel, British financiers were unnerved by Pierpont’s daring. The *New York Times* said they were “appalled by the magnitude of the American Steel combination,” and the London *Chronicle* termed the trust “little less than a menace to the commerce of the civilized world.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225533) Among other things, formation of the trust heralded an export boom of U.S. products to Europe, which would sharpen commercial rivalry between the two.
随着 1901 年美国钢铁公司（U.S. Steel）的成立，皮尔庞特（Pierpont）的大胆举动令英国金融家们感到不安。《纽约时报》称他们“对这家美国钢铁联合企业的规模感到震惊”，而《伦敦纪事报》则将这一托拉斯称为“对文明世界商业几乎无异于一种威胁”。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225533) 除此之外，这一托拉斯的成立预示着美国产品对欧洲的出口热潮，这将加剧双方之间的商业竞争。

Around this time, too, Pierpont took a controversial interest in proposals to electrify underground and surface rail lines in London. New tube lines were being built as inner-city congestion required new building in London’s outskirts. Pierpont competed to finance an underground line running from Hammersmith through Piccadilly, and into the City. By taking over tube financing, Pierpont also hoped to generate business for two companies in which he had a stake—British Thomson-Houston and Siemens Brothers. Eventually he lost the underground financing to a syndicate headed by Chicago tycoon Charles Tyson Yerkes, the Traction King, best known as the model for Theodore Dreiser’s ruthless Frank Cowperwood, protagonist of *The Financier, The Titan*, and *The Stoic.* Despite his rare loss, Pierpont’s involvement kindled fears that he would steamroller the English economy, and the London County Council warned that the metropolis was being handed over to the two Americans.
与此同时，皮尔庞特对伦敦地下及地面铁路电气化提案表现出了颇具争议的兴趣。随着市中心拥堵迫使伦敦郊区新建工程不断推进，新的地铁线路正在铺设。皮尔庞特竞相为一条从汉默史密斯经皮卡迪利延伸至金融城的地下线提供融资。通过掌控地铁融资，他还希望能为自己持股的两家公司——英国汤姆森-休斯顿公司和西门子兄弟公司——带来业务。然而最终，他在地铁融资案中败给了由芝加哥大亨查尔斯·泰森·耶基斯领导的财团。这位“电车大王”正是西奥多·德莱塞笔下冷酷无情的金融家弗兰克·卡帕伍德（《金融家》《巨人》《斯多葛》三部曲主角）的原型。尽管这次失利实属罕见，但皮尔庞特的介入仍引发了人们对他会碾压英国经济的担忧，伦敦郡议会警告称这座大都市正被拱手让给两个美国人。

There was now enormous British ambivalence toward Pierpont. On the streets of London, peddlers sold penny sheets entitled “License to Stay on the Earth” and signed “J. Pierpont Morgan.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225710) A 1901 cartoon in the New York *World* showed Pierpont asking John Bull, the personified Englishman, “What else have you for sale?”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225877) Yet however much the British were distressed by Pierpont’s bravado, they relied upon him in American financial matters. In 1901, to safeguard their American investments, London financiers insured his life at Lloyd’s for \$2 million, placing him, as Jack said, “in the same category with Queen Victoria and other rulers on this side of the Atlantic.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226028)
当时英国人对皮尔庞特的看法十分矛盾。伦敦街头小贩兜售着名为《地球居住许可证》的廉价传单，署名竟是“J.P.摩根”。 [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225710) 1901 年《纽约世界报》的一幅漫画中，皮尔庞特向代表英国人的约翰牛问道：“你还有什么要卖的？” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2225877) 然而，无论英国人多么反感皮尔庞特的虚张声势，他们在美国金融事务上仍需仰仗他。1901 年，为了保护在美投资，伦敦金融家们在劳合社为他购买了 200 万美元的人寿保险，正如杰克所说，这把他“与维多利亚女王以及大西洋彼岸的其他统治者归为一类。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226028)

No Morgan move could have aroused more primordial British fears than the one Pierpont made in 1902—the formation of a shipping trust to monopolize the North Atlantic. This was a natural extension of America’s new export orientation. Soon after he had formed U.S. Steel, Pierpont was asked by a shipping executive whether it was possible to put North Atlantic steamships under common ownership. “It ought to be,” he replied.[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226191) The shipping scene was then reminiscent of an earlier railroad era—too many ships and destructive rate wars. The Germans threatened British naval superiority, while Americans believed they should profit more from the immigrant traffic, as well as the new vogue among rich Americans for making luxurious transatlantic crossings.
没有哪一次摩根的举动比 1902 年皮尔庞特组建航运托拉斯垄断北大西洋更能激起英国人骨子里的恐惧了。这是美国转向出口导向的自然延伸。在组建美国钢铁公司后不久，一位航运高管问皮尔庞特，能否将北大西洋的轮船置于共同所有权之下。"理应如此，"他回答道。当时的航运景象让人回想起早期的铁路时代——船只过剩，运费战破坏性极强。德国人威胁着英国的海上霸权，而美国人则认为，他们应该从移民运输以及美国富人兴起的大西洋奢华航游新潮流中赚取更多利润。

Nakedly asserting American interests, Pierpont assembled a plan for an American-owned shipping trust that would transpose his “community of interest” principle—cooperation among competitors in a given industry—to a global plane. He created an Anglo-American fleet of over 120 steamships—the world’s largest under private ownership, dwarfing even the French merchant marine. From a political standpoint, his critical conquests were the Belfast shipyard of Harland and Wolff and the White Star line. In the new trust, Lord Pirrie of Harland and Wolff saw a captive market for his ships, but J. Bruce Ismay, whose father had co-founded White Star, balked at the deal. Pierpont offered White Star shareholders such a rich premium—ten times over the high 1900 earnings—that Ismay not only stayed on as White Star chairman but was coaxed by Pierpont into becoming president of the trust itself, to be called the International Mercantile Marine. Through the White Star purchase and his hiring of Ismay, Pierpont would become ensnared in the *Titanic* catastrophe ten years later.
皮尔庞特毫不掩饰地高举美国利益大旗，精心策划了一个由美国控股的航运托拉斯，意图将他那套"利益共同体"原则——即同行业竞争者之间的合作——推向全球舞台。他组建了一支由 120 多艘蒸汽轮船组成的英美联合船队，成为全球最大的私有船队，甚至让法国商船队相形见绌。从政治角度看，他的关键战果是拿下了哈兰德与沃尔夫造船厂（Harland and Wolff）位于贝尔法斯特的船坞，以及白星航运公司（White Star）。在新的托拉斯中，哈兰德与沃尔夫的皮里勋爵（Lord Pirrie）看到了自家船舶的稳定市场，但白星航运联合创始人之一的儿子 J·布鲁斯·伊斯梅（J. Bruce Ismay）却对此犹豫不决。皮尔庞特向白星股东开出了极为丰厚的溢价——高达 1900 年高额利润的十倍——这一手不仅让伊斯梅留任白星董事长，更被皮尔庞特说服，出任这个名为"国际商船公司"（International Mercantile Marine）的托拉斯总裁。 通过收购白星航运公司并雇佣伊斯梅，皮尔庞特在十年后深陷泰坦尼克号的灾难之中。

It was imperative that Pierpont bring the Germans, newly dominant in the North Atlantic, into his trust. Their jumbo transatlantic liners—multitiered wonders of wedding cake extravagance—were setting speed records for Atlantic crossings. An important architect of the shipping trust was Albert Ballin, whose Hamburg-Amerika Steamship Line, with hundreds of vessels, was the world’s largest shipping company. In a secret 1901 report, Ballin sketched out the scope of Morgan’s ambitions:
皮尔庞特必须将新近称霸北大西洋的德国人拉入他的托拉斯。那些德国巨型跨洋邮轮——如多层婚礼蛋糕般奢华的奇迹——正在不断刷新大西洋航行的速度纪录。航运托拉斯的重要构建者是阿尔伯特·巴林，他旗下的汉堡-美洲航运公司拥有数百艘船舶，是全球最大的航运企业。在 1901 年的一份秘密报告中，巴林勾勒出摩根野心的轮廓：

> It is no secret that Morgan is pursuing his far-reaching plans as the head of a syndicate which comprises a number of the most important and the most enterprising business men in the United States and that railway interests are particularly well represented in it. Morgan himself, during his stay in London a few months ago, stated to some British shipping men that, according to his estimates, nearly 70 percent of the goods which are shipped to Europe from the North Atlantic ports are carried to the latter by the railroads on Through Bills of Lading, and that their further transport is entrusted to foreign shipping companies. He and his friends, Morgan added, did not see any reason why the railroad companies should leave it to foreign-owned companies to carry those American goods across the Atlantic. It would be much more logical to bring about an amalgamation of the American railroad and shipping interests for the purpose of securing the whole profits for American capital.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226342)
> 众所周知，摩根正领导着一个由美国多位举足轻重且最具进取心的商界巨头组成的财团，铁路利益在其中尤为突出，这已不是什么秘密。几个月前在伦敦期间，摩根本人曾对英国航运界人士坦言，根据他的估算，从北大西洋港口运往欧洲的货物中，近 70%是通过铁路以联运提单运送至这些港口，而其后续运输则委托给了外国航运公司。他补充道，他和他的朋友们实在看不出有什么理由让美国铁路公司把这些跨大西洋运输美国货物的肥水留给外国企业。在他看来，更合乎逻辑的做法是将美国铁路与航运利益整合起来，确保全部利润都落入美国资本口袋。 [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226342)

In late 1901, Morgan struck a deal with Ballin for carving up the North Atlantic traffic: the Morgan syndicate wouldn’t inaugurate service to German harbors without express permission from the Germans, while they, in turn, vowed not to expand their service to Britain or Belgium. The partners in the shipping trust would also pool profits and jointly acquire the Holland-America Line.
1901 年末，摩根与巴林达成协议，瓜分北大西洋航运市场：摩根财团未经德国明确许可，不得开启通往德国港口的航线；而德方则承诺不将业务扩展至英国或比利时。这家航运托拉斯的合作伙伴还将共享利润，并共同收购荷兰美洲航运公司。

After meeting with Morgan in London, Ballin, the court Jew of his day, went to Kaiser Wilhelm’s Berlin hunting lodge and briefed him on the pact. At first, the kaiser feared American financial trickery. But Ballin pointed out that while the British companies were being swallowed whole, the Germans would remain independent partners. Impressed, the kaiser sat down on his bed and read the agreement, making changes and insisting on the inclusion of North German Lloyd in the cartel. Later, when the kaiser came aboard *Corsair III* at Kiel, Pierpont strolled the deck with him. But in inviting the kaiser to sit down, he committed a serious *faux pas;* Wilhelm, however, accepted the offer from the royal Morgan.
在伦敦与摩根会面后，当时的宫廷犹太金融家巴林前往柏林郊外德皇威廉的狩猎行宫，向他简要汇报了这项协议。起初，德皇担心美国人在金融上耍花招，但巴林指出，虽然英国公司被整个吞并，德国公司却仍能作为独立合作伙伴保留下来。德皇听后颇受触动，便坐在床上仔细阅读协议，并做了修改，坚持要将北德意志劳埃德公司纳入卡特尔。后来，德皇在基尔登上“海盗三号”游艇时，皮尔庞特与他一同在甲板上散步。然而，当皮尔庞特邀请德皇坐下时，却犯了一个严重的失礼之举；不过，威廉还是接受了这位“皇室摩根”的邀请。

As news of the German agreement leaked out, the public was shocked that consolidation had reared its head on a global scale. In an editorial entitled “Incredible,” the *New York Times* said, “If dispatches from Paris should tell us that Mr. Morgan had . . . cabled orders to his home office to take out all the telephones, discharging the stenographers and typewriters and smash the ticker, no man, woman, or child in New York would believe the yarn. Neither will intelligent persons accept as true the story about the terms of the agreement with the German lines.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226514) The *Times* saw this restraint of competition as outmoded and inefficient—a line of reasoning now gaining new adherents as revulsion from the trust kings increased.
随着德国协议的消息泄露，公众震惊地发现，全球范围内竟又出现了垄断的苗头。《纽约时报》在一篇题为《难以置信》的社论中写道：“如果巴黎传来的消息说，摩根先生……电令总部拆掉所有电话，解雇速记员和打字员，并砸碎股票行情机，那么纽约的男女老少绝不会相信这种鬼话。同样，明智之人也不会相信与德国线路达成的协议条款是真的。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226514) 《纽约时报》认为这种限制竞争的做法既过时又低效——随着对垄断巨头的不满日益加剧，这一观点如今正赢得更多支持者。

The British were especially edgy about Pierpont’s shipping cartel. They feared that International Mercantile Marine ships might exclusively transport to Europe those goods that originated in the American interior and traveled on Morgan railroads to East Coast ports. Morgan partner George Perkins confirmed this when he exulted that the shipping trust would “practically result in stretching our railroad terminals across the Atlantic.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226664) It seemed as if Pierpont Morgan were spinning a seamless web around the world.
英国人对皮尔庞特的航运垄断集团尤为警惕。他们担心国际商船公司的船只可能会独占那些从美国内陆经由摩根铁路运抵东海岸港口、再转运至欧洲的货物。摩根合伙人乔治·珀金斯得意洋洋地证实了这点，称航运托拉斯“实际上相当于将我们的铁路终点站延伸到了大西洋彼岸。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226664) 看来皮尔庞特·摩根仿佛正在编织一张笼罩全球的无缝之网。

Pierpont had to contend with a single holdout, Britain’s Cunard Line, whose exclusion Ballin thought might wreck the trust. (There may have been some personal pique here: once detained by a strike of Cunard workers at Liverpool, Pierpont had sworn, on the spot, never to use the line again.) Now, with near-panic in British shipping circles and a popular clamor for Parliament to “save” the seas for Britain, a cabinet committee pressed Cunard not to sell. The British admiralty wanted transatlantic liners available as warships in an emergency and feared having Cunard in foreign hands. To woo the line, the British government granted it lavish subsidies to build two new ships, the *Mauretania* and the *Lusitania*, which would be the world’s biggest steamships. In exchange, Cunard agreed to stay in British hands and keep its fleet at the government’s disposal.
皮尔庞特需要应对一个顽固的反对者——英国的丘纳德航运公司。巴林认为，若将这家公司排除在外，整个信托计划可能会功亏一篑。（这其中或许也夹杂着些许私人恩怨：当年皮尔庞特曾在利物浦被丘纳德工人的罢工滞留，当场发誓永不再乘坐这家公司的船。）如今，英国航运界人心惶惶，民众呼声四起，要求议会为英国“守住”海洋。为此，内阁委员会向丘纳德施压，阻止其出售。英国海军部希望跨大西洋邮轮能在紧急情况下充当军舰，并担心丘纳德落入外国之手。为拉拢这家公司，英国政府慷慨拨款补贴，资助其建造两艘新船——毛里塔尼亚号和卢西塔尼亚号，它们将成为世界上最大的蒸汽轮船。作为交换，丘纳德同意保持英国控股，并将其船队交由政府调度。

In crafting a trust, Pierpont had never before had to contend with foreign governments. But as finance became increasingly international and affected sovereign interests, it took on a more political coloring. To temper British fears, Pierpont lobbied Colonial Secretary Joseph Chamberlain, a vocal critic, and resorted to a ruse familiar to modern multinationals: he camouflaged American ownership, first with the trust’s very name, the International Mercantile Marine. Pierpont also agreed to man his British ships with British crews, fill their boards with British directors, and have them fly the Union Jack. Finally, his British ships would be in the reserves of the British navy and could be conscripted in case of war. Yet the IMM’s five-man voting trust would have an American majority, with Pierpont and his partner Charles Steele joined by P. A. B. Widener, along with Ismay and Lord Pirrie.
在构建信托时，皮尔庞特此前从未需要与外国政府周旋。但随着金融日益国际化且牵涉到主权利益，它便带上了更多政治色彩。为缓解英国的担忧，皮尔庞特游说了曾激烈抨击此事的殖民大臣约瑟夫·张伯伦，并采用了现代跨国公司熟悉的手法：他先是给信托冠以“国际商船队”之名，以此掩饰美国的所有权。同时，皮尔庞特同意让英国船员操作英国船只，董事会中安插英国董事，并让这些船只悬挂英国国旗。最后，他的英国船只将编入英国海军预备役，战时可供征用。然而，国际商船队的五人投票信托中，美国人将占据多数——皮尔庞特、其合伙人查尔斯·斯蒂尔以及 P.A.B.威德纳，加上伊斯梅和皮里勋爵。

The IMM would become a famous Pierpont Morgan flop. When shipping traffic slackened after the Boer War, the Morgan combine and Cunard exhausted each other in debilitating rate wars. From its inception in April 1902, the Morgan syndicate struggled to unload the IMM’s unwanted securities. The stock had so much water—that is, inflated value—that it couldn’t get a New York Stock Exchange listing. In 1906, the underwriters still held nearly 80 percent of the shares. As the *Wall Street Journal* concluded in a postmortem on Pierpont’s shipping trust, “The ocean was too big for the old man.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226814)
IMM 最终成了皮尔庞特·摩根一次声名狼藉的失败之作。布尔战争后，航运业逐渐萧条，摩根财团与冠达公司在恶性价格战中两败俱伤。自 1902 年 4 月成立之初，摩根联合体就一直在为 IMM 滞销的证券寻找买家。这支股票注水严重——即虚增价值——以至于无法在纽约证券交易所挂牌上市。到了 1906 年，承销商手中仍持有近 80%的股份。正如《华尔街日报》在皮尔庞特航运托拉斯的事后总结中所言：“这片海，老爷子终究是驾驭不了。”

The British revulsion toward Pierpont probably changed the complexion of his London partnership, J. S. Morgan and Company. Not only had the bulk of its capital been his, but its mostly American partners had largely been recruited from among family members. In the new century, more partners would be British, and the choices more political, as Pierpont spent lavishly to build up the London house. In 1900, he signed up as a partner Sir Clinton E. Dawkins, a distinguished civil servant who had just completed a tour of duty in Egypt and was about to become a finance minister in India. The press saw fresh plans to expand the Morgan domain into Asia.
英国人对皮尔庞特的反感，很可能改变了他伦敦合伙公司 J.S. Morgan and Company 的面貌。不仅大部分资本来自他本人，而且其多数美国合伙人主要从家族成员中招募。进入新世纪后，合伙人中英国人将增多，选择也更加政治化，因为皮尔庞特不惜重金打造伦敦公司。1900 年，他吸纳了杰出公务员克林顿·E·道金斯爵士作为合伙人——此人刚结束在埃及的任职，即将出任印度财政部长。媒体认为这是摩根势力向亚洲扩张的新计划。

It was dissatisfaction with Dawkins, apparently, that led Pierpont into merger talks with Barings in 1904. He also feared his new rivals on Wall Street. Lord Revelstoke of Barings, in recalling his meeting with Pierpont on the subject, wrote, “He inveighed bitterly against the growing power of the Jews and of the Rockefeller crowd, and said more than once that our firm and his were the only two composed of white men in New York.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226972) The two firms had long identified with each other as the leading Protestant houses in their respective cities.
显然，对道金斯的不满促使皮尔庞特在 1904 年与巴林银行展开合并谈判。他同时也忌惮华尔街的新对手。巴林银行的雷弗尔斯托克勋爵在回忆与皮尔庞特就此事的会面时写道："他激烈抨击犹太人和洛克菲勒集团日益增长的势力，不止一次地说，整个纽约只有我们两家公司是由白人组成的。" [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2226972) 这两家公司长期以来一直以来被视作各自城市中最具影响力的新教财团。

The proposed merger centered on a plan for the House of Baring to handle the London side, the House of Morgan the New York side; J. S. Morgan and Company would disappear. The talks foundered for two reasons, according to Lord Revelstoke: Pierpont was afraid of disappointing Dawkins by merging the London house; and with Jack Morgan spending so much time in London, his position in the merged firm would be a ticklish affair. “I expect there is little sympathy and less confidence between father and son,” said Revelstoke, who was also afraid of being smothered by Pierpont.[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227137) Soon after these talks collapsed in 1905, Dawkins had a heart attack and died. Jack was then entrusted with the sensitive assignment of recruiting well-connected British partners for the firm of J. S. Morgan and Company. Now the Morgans would buy some expensive British bloodlines.
拟议的合并计划核心内容是：巴林家族负责伦敦业务，摩根家族负责纽约业务，而 J. S. Morgan and Company 将被撤销。据雷弗尔斯托克勋爵所说，谈判搁浅有两个原因：皮尔庞特担心合并伦敦公司会让道金斯失望；此外，杰克·摩根在伦敦待的时间太长，他在合并后的公司里地位会很棘手。“我觉得父子之间没什么共鸣，信任就更别提了，”雷弗尔斯托克说，他也担心被皮尔庞特压得喘不过气。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227137) 1905 年，谈判破裂后不久，道金斯突发心脏病去世。随后，杰克接下一项敏感任务——为 J. S. Morgan and Company 招揽人脉广泛的英国合伙人。这一次，摩根家族要花重金买下英国贵族血统。

In 1904, Edward Grenfell was elevated to partner; he became a Bank of England director a year later. A cool, dapper young bachelor who wore smart clothes and had a sharp tongue, Grenfell was snobbish and conservative and possessed a penetrating intellect. He also had a taste for practical jokes. Educated at Harrow and Trinity College, Cambridge, he had eminent ancestors, both his father and grandfather having been directors of the Bank of England and members of Parliament. Even as a young man, he peered at the world unsentimentally and spied out the fraudulent and hypocritical in people. Grenfell would become the London firm’s political fixer and ace diplomat, its main contact with the British Treasury and the Bank of England.
1904 年，爱德华·格伦费尔晋升为合伙人；一年后，他成为英格兰银行董事。这位年轻单身汉冷静、衣冠楚楚、衣着考究、言辞犀利，既势利保守，又洞察力敏锐，还爱搞恶作剧。他曾在哈罗公学和剑桥大学三一学院接受教育，祖上显赫——父亲和祖父都曾任英格兰银行董事和议会议员。即便年轻之时，他已能以不动声色的目光审视世界，看穿人们的虚伪与欺诈。格伦费尔后来成为伦敦公司的政治操盘手和首席外交官，也是该公司与英国财政部及英格兰银行之间的主要联络人。

In 1905, Grenfell brought in his cousin and Jack Morgan’s friend, Vivian Hugh Smith, then working in a family business that managed wharves. A tall, handsome redhead and a charming raconteur, he had gone to Eton and Trinity Hall, Cambridge. He more than Grenfell was in Pierpont’s mold. He was a business go-getter, with his hand in many deals. He invested in Caucasian copper and African goldfields and in other Rhodesian enterprises. Smith’s father had been a Bank of England governor, and he was a member of the most prolific banking family England has produced, the so-called City Smiths, descended from a seventeenth-century Nottingham banker. (Grenfell wasn’t a Smith; he and Vivian were related through their mothers.) Charting the power of this prodigious brood in 1959, Anthony Sampson estimated that seventeen Smith descendants in the City controlled eighty-seven directorships in seventy-five companies and were chairmen of six companies. The Martin Smiths would intermarry with the Hambros, strengthening that banking alliance. Vivian Smith married the tall, slender, flaxen-haired Lady Sybil, the mischievous, high-spirited only daughter of the sixth earl of Antrim, who owned Glenarm Castle and several square miles of land in Ulster and whose mother had been a lady-in-waiting to Queen Victoria. Gradually, then, the London bank shed its character as an American colony in the City. When Jack returned to New York in 1905, Grenfell and Smith were in charge. When the firm was restyled Morgan, Grenfell in 1910, it was the first time it had ever carried a British name. The Morgans had built their Trojan horse well.
1905 年，格伦费尔引荐了他的表弟兼杰克·摩根的朋友维维安·休·史密斯加入，当时史密斯正在家族经营的码头管理企业中工作。这位身材高大、英俊的红发男子，是个魅力十足的讲故事高手，曾就读于伊顿公学和剑桥大学三一学院。他比格伦费尔更符合皮尔庞特的风格。他是个商业进取者，涉足众多交易。他投资了高加索地区的铜矿、非洲的金矿以及其他罗得西亚企业。史密斯的父亲曾担任英格兰银行行长，而他本人则出身于英国有史以来最庞大的银行家族——所谓的"伦敦金融城史密斯家族"，其祖上可追溯到 17 世纪诺丁汉的一位银行家。（格伦费尔并非史密斯家族成员；他与维维安是母系亲戚。）1959 年，安东尼·桑普森在描绘这个庞大家族的影响力时估算，金融城中 17 位史密斯后裔控制了 75 家公司的 87 个董事席位，并担任 6 家公司的董事长。 马丁·史密斯家族与汉布罗家族联姻，从而巩固了这家银行的联盟。薇薇安·史密斯娶了高挑纤细、一头淡金色秀发的西比尔女士——她是第六代安特里姆伯爵那调皮活泼的独生女，伯爵拥有格莱纳姆城堡和阿尔斯特省数平方英里的土地，其母亲曾是维多利亚女王的女侍官。渐渐地，这家伦敦银行褪去了它作为美国侨民区的色彩。1905 年杰克返回纽约后，格林费尔和史密斯接管了业务。1910 年，公司改名为摩根·格林费尔，这是它首次冠上英国姓氏。摩根家族精心打造的特洛伊木马，已然大功告成。

DURING Theodore Roosevelt’s presidency, Pierpont Morgan received his most pronounced comeuppance for his role in the American scene. He was now so grand and cloud-wreathed that only a president could chop him down to mortal scale. The public revulsion from him was easy to explain. Wall Street had flourished with the trusts: many were headquartered in New York and enjoyed closer relations with Wall Street bankers than with the companies from which they were compounded. Teddy Roosevelt wanted to correct the imbalance between government and corporate power, and in so doing he inevitably collided with Pierpont Morgan.
在西奥多·罗斯福总统任期内，皮尔庞特·摩根在美国舞台上受到了最显著的惩罚。彼时他位高权重、云遮雾绕，唯有总统才能将他拉回凡人尺度。公众对他的反感不难理解。华尔街随着托拉斯蓬勃发展：许多托拉斯总部设在纽约，与华尔街银行家的关系比与组成它们的公司还要密切。西奥多·罗斯福试图纠正政府与企业权力之间的失衡，在这一过程中，他不可避免地与皮尔庞特·摩根发生了碰撞。

Although he had created great industrial combines, Pierpont couldn’t allow commensurate power to accrue to labor and government. Despite his reverence for the past, patent in the religious and Renaissance art he collected, he was a radical force, unsettling to small-town America, with its agrarian traditions and faith in its own innocence. However much businessmen might respect him, he was now an ogre in the popular press. One Broadway hit show depicted devils blowing across a fiery seat as they sang in unison, “This seat’s reserved for Morgan, the great financial Gorgon.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227272)
尽管皮尔庞特缔造了庞大的工业联合体，却绝不容许劳工与政府获得与之匹配的权力。他痴迷于过往——从他收藏的宗教与文艺复兴艺术中可见一斑——但本质上却是激进的变革者，令恪守农耕传统、笃信淳朴本真的美国小镇感到不安。纵使商界对他满怀敬畏，在通俗报刊上他却成了狰狞的巨兽。百老汇一出热门戏剧中，魔鬼们齐声高唱，烈焰翻腾："这宝座留给摩根，金融界的蛇发魔怪。" [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227272)

Soon after President McKinley was shot, the House of Morgan tested his successor. Pierpont’s new lieutenant, the smooth, insinuating George W. Perkins, cabled the new president, “The country’s only consolation at this time is that it has an honest, fearless, loyal American to assume its world wide burdens.[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227443) A few weeks later, Perkins and Robert Bacon, a former classmate of TR’s at Harvard, visited the White House to urge caution and scout out Roosevelt’s intentions. The president said he wanted reform and afterward described Perkins and Bacon “arguing like attorneys for a bad case, and at the bottom of their hearts each would know this if . . . he were not the representative of so strong and dominant a character as Pierpont Morgan.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227624)
麦金利总统遇刺后不久，摩根财团便试探起他的继任者。皮尔庞特的新副手——圆滑谄媚的乔治·W·珀金斯——致电新总统：“国家此刻唯一的慰藉，便是有一位诚实、无畏、忠诚的美国人承担起全球重担。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227443) 几周后，珀金斯与罗伯特·培根——罗斯福在哈佛的同窗——一同造访白宫，劝其谨慎行事，并探察罗斯福的意图。总统表示希望推行改革，事后他将珀金斯和培根描述为“像为一桩糟糕案子辩护的律师那样争论，而他们内心深处各自都明白这一点，假如……他们不是代表皮尔庞特·摩根那样强势而主宰性的人物的代表的话。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227624)

As much a showman as Pierpont, TR would endlessly manipulate the Morgan symbolism. With the public appalled by the Northern Pacific corner, Roosevelt saw the political wisdom of filing an antitrust suit against the Northern Securities Company, whose formation had marked the Morgan-Harriman truce. Attorney General Philander C. Knox announced the suit after the stock market’s close on February 19, 1902. The news caught Morgan by surprise at a dinner. Clearly, *this* White House wouldn’t automatically succumb to Morgan pressure. The subsequent confrontations between TR and Morgan showed the tycoon in all his sublime arrogance. The two men shared membership in New York’s aristocracy; Pierpont and TR’s father were both founders of the American Museum of Natural History. This common background perhaps gave their feud a special rancor—a pattern that would repeat itself with Jack and another notable “class traitor,” Franklin Roosevelt.
和皮尔庞特一样善于表演，罗斯福不断玩弄着摩根这个象征符号。公众对北太平洋铁路囤积事件感到震惊，罗斯福意识到，对北方证券公司提起反垄断诉讼具有政治智慧——这家公司的成立标志着摩根与哈里曼的停战。1902 年 2 月 19 日股市收盘后，司法部长菲兰德·C·诺克斯宣布了这项诉讼。消息传来时，摩根正在晚宴上，不由得大吃一惊。显然，这届白宫不会轻易向摩根的压力低头。随后罗斯福与摩根的交锋，充分展现了这位大亨骨子里的傲慢。两人同属纽约上流社会；皮尔庞特与罗斯福的父亲都是美国自然历史博物馆的创始人。这一共同背景或许让他们的恩怨别具一番刻毒——这种模式后来在杰克与另一位著名的"阶级叛徒"富兰克林·罗斯福之间再次上演。

At a White House meeting that included Attorney General Knox, Morgan expressed indignation that he hadn’t received advance word of the Northern Securities suit. In what history has engraved as the ultimate hauteur, he suggested to Roosevelt that Knox and his lawyers meet privately. “If we have done anything wrong,” said Pierpont, “send your man to my man and they can fix it up.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227790) Knox said testily that they didn’t want to fix the merger, but stop it. Worried about U.S. Steel, his favorite stepchild, Morgan asked Roosevelt if he planned to “attack my other interests.” Not “unless we find out . . . they have done something we regard as wrong,” Roosevelt replied.[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227925)
在一次包括司法部长诺克斯出席的白宫会议上，摩根对自己未提前获知北方证券诉讼一事表示愤慨。他用历史铭记为极致傲慢的口吻，向罗斯福建议让诺克斯和他的律师私下会面。“如果我们有做错什么，”皮尔庞特说，“派你的人来找我的人，他们能解决。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227790) 诺克斯不耐烦地回应，他们不是要解决并购问题，而是要阻止它。摩根对自己偏爱的“宠儿”——美国钢铁公司忧心忡忡，问罗斯福是否打算“攻击我的其他利益”。“除非我们发现……他们做了我们认为不对的事，”罗斯福回答道。 [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2227925)

In Roosevelt’s reaction to the meeting, there was the keen relish and cynicism of the well-bred rebel. He told Knox how Morgan “could not help regarding me as a big rival operator, who either intended to ruin all his interests or else could be induced to come to an agreement to ruin none.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228051) Back at 23 Wall, Pierpont dashed off an angry letter to the president, but cooler associates dissuaded him from sending it. In 1903, a court in Saint Paul, Minnesota, backed the government in dissolving the Northern Securities Company and the Supreme Court narrowly upheld the decision a year later. The Sherman Antitrust Act, moribund under McKinley, suddenly took on new life with TR.
在罗斯福对这次会晤的反应中，流露出一个教养良好的叛逆者特有的敏锐玩味与冷嘲热讽。他告诉诺克斯，摩根“无法不把我视为一个强大的竞争对手，要么打算毁掉他所有利益，要么可以被拉拢过来达成协议，谁也不损害谁。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228051) 回到华尔街 23 号，皮尔庞特草草写了一封愤怒的信给总统，但更冷静的同事们劝阻了他，没让他寄出去。1903 年，明尼苏达州圣保罗的一家法院支持政府解散北方证券公司，一年后最高法院以微弱多数维持了原判。在麦金莱治下奄奄一息的《谢尔曼反托拉斯法》，在罗斯福手中突然焕发了新生。

Although the Roosevelt-Morgan relationship is sometimes caricatured as that of trust buster versus trust king, it was far more complex than that. The public wrangling obscured deeper ideological affinities, as first demonstrated in the anthracite miners strike of May 1902. The principal coal companies were owned by railroads, such as the Reading, Lehigh Valley, Erie, and others close to the House of Morgan. They wanted to avenge a 10-percent wage increase granted the miners in 1900—a deal that Pierpont had helped to broker—and reacted to the strikers with feudal ferocity. By the fall of 1902, schools were shut in New York for lack of coal, and the Republicans feared retribution in the elections. On October 11, 1902, Elihu Root, the secretary of war, met with Pierpont aboard *Corsair III* in the Hudson River. Roosevelt was ready to run the mines with soldiers and wanted Morgan’s support for an arbitration committee. TR was taking an enlightened stand for a president—strikebreaking had been the more typical presidential response.
尽管罗斯福与摩根之间的关系有时被简单描绘成反托拉斯斗士与托拉斯之王的对抗，但实际上远比这复杂。公开的争执掩盖了两人更深层的意识形态契合，这一点在 1902 年 5 月的无烟煤矿工罢工中首次得到印证。主要的煤炭公司由铁路公司所有，例如雷丁、利哈伊谷、伊利等与摩根财团关系密切的企业。它们想报复 1900 年煤矿工人获得的 10%加薪——那笔交易正是皮尔庞特促成的——因此对罢工者采取了封建领主般的凶残态度。到 1902 年秋，纽约因缺煤导致学校关闭，共和党人担心会在选举中受到惩罚。1902 年 10 月 11 日，战争部长伊莱休·鲁特在哈德逊河上的"科尔萨三世"号游艇上与皮尔庞特会面。罗斯福准备派军队接管煤矿，并希望摩根支持成立一个仲裁委员会。 TR（西奥多·罗斯福）身为总统，立场堪称开明——要知道，当时总统们更常见的应对方式是镇压罢工。

The approach appealed to Morgan, who liked order and negotiation. He and Root went straight to the Union Club to meet with some railroad presidents. Paternalistic in his own bank, he was more conciliatory toward the miners than the railroad presidents were. At a White House meeting on October 3, the railroad men angrily abused (ohn Mitchell, the young president of the United Mine Workers of America, who reacted with commendable dignity. Two days later, Roosevelt sent Robert Bacon a letter designed to enlist Pierpont’s further help. The president said of Mitchell, “He made no threats and resorted to no abuse. The proposition he made seemed to me eminently fair. The operators refused even to consider it; used insolent and abusive language about him, and in at least 2 cases assumed an attitude toward me which was one of insolence.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228177) While sympathetic to Roosevelt’s plea, Morgan lacked the total power over the railroad men popularly attributed to him, and Roosevelt complained to Henry Cabot Lodge that Morgan hadn’t been able to “do much with those wooden-headed gentry.”*[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228312)*
这种处理方式深得摩根之心，他一向喜欢秩序与谈判。他与鲁特径直前往联合俱乐部，与几位铁路总裁会面。摩根在自己的银行里虽然采取家长式作风，但对待矿工的态度却比铁路总裁们温和得多。10 月 3 日的白宫会议上，铁路大亨们怒气冲冲地攻击美国矿工联合会的年轻主席约翰·米切尔，而后者保持了令人敬佩的风度。两天后，罗斯福致信罗伯特·培根，希望争取皮尔庞特进一步的支持。总统评价米切尔说：“他没有发出任何威胁，也没有恶语相向。他提出的方案在我看来极为公平。可运营商们连考虑都不肯，还用傲慢无礼的言辞辱骂他，其中至少有两家对我本人也摆出了蛮横的态度。”” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228177) 尽管罗斯福的呼吁令人同情，但摩根对铁路大亨们的控制力远没有人们普遍认为的那么绝对。罗斯福曾向亨利·卡伯特·洛奇抱怨说，摩根对那些“榆木脑袋的绅士们”根本使不上什么力。”” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228312)

The crisis climaxed on October 15, 1902, when Perkins and Bacon visited the White House and stayed up close to midnight with Roosevelt, trying to find a way out of the impasse. Roosevelt again saw the two Morgan partners as melodramatic, even slightly ridiculous. As the night wore on, he said, they “grew more and more hysterical, and not merely admitted but insisted that failure to agree would result in violence and possible social war.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228447) Roosevelt finally hit upon a way that would allow the operators to save face: they would place the labor representative on the board in a seat reserved for an “eminent sociologist.” In the end, the arbitration board granted the miners a 10-percent wage increase but no union recognition. Roosevelt glowingly wrote Morgan, “If it had not been for your going in the matter, I do not see how the strike could have been settled at this time, and the consequences that might have followed . . . are . . . very dreadful to contemplate.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228573)
危机在 1902 年 10 月 15 日达到顶点，当天珀金斯和培根造访白宫，并与罗斯福一直待到接近午夜，试图为僵局寻找出路。罗斯福再次将这两位摩根合伙人视为过于戏剧化，甚至有些滑稽可笑。随着夜色渐深，他说，他们“变得越来越歇斯底里，不仅承认而且坚持认为，谈判破裂将导致暴力甚至可能的社会战争。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228447) 罗斯福最终想出了一个让经营者们能保住面子的办法：他们将劳方代表安排在董事会中一个为“杰出社会学家”预留的席位。最终，仲裁委员会为矿工争取到了 10%的加薪，但未承认工会。罗斯福热情洋溢地写信给摩根：“如果不是您介入此事，我真不知道这次罢工现在如何能解决，而可能随之而来的后果……想想都觉得非常可怕。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228573)

Even on the trust issue, Roosevelt and Morgan were far from antithetical. Roosevelt saw trusts as natural, organic outgrowths of economic development. Stopping them, he said, was like trying to dam the Mississippi River. Both TR and Morgan disliked the rugged, individualistic economy of the nineteenth century and favored big business; they wanted to promote U. S. entry into world markets. But whereas Roosevelt thought economic giantism warranted an equivalent growth in government regulation, Morgan saw no need for countervailing powers. A Victorian gentleman banker at bottom, Pierpont saw trust, honor, and self-regulation among businessmen as providing the needed checks and balances.
即使在对托拉斯问题的看法上，罗斯福和摩根也远非截然对立。罗斯福认为托拉斯是经济发展中自然而有机的产物。他形容说，阻止托拉斯就像试图拦截密西西比河一样。西奥多·罗斯福和摩根都不喜欢 19 世纪那种粗犷的个人主义经济，而是青睐大企业；他们都希望推动美国进入全球市场。不过，罗斯福认为经济巨型化需要政府监管的相应扩张，而摩根则认为没有必要建立制衡力量。作为维多利亚时代的绅士银行家，皮尔庞特深信，商界内部的信任、荣誉和自律已足以提供所需的制衡机制。

That Roosevelt and Morgan were secret blood brothers can be seen in the strange odyssey of Morgan partner George W. Perkins, who ended up a lieutenant to both. He was a handsome, highly imaginative man, with roguish, heavy-lidded gambler’s eyes and a sinister baby face behind a handlebar mustache. His father had founded a missionary slum school in Chicago, and George grew up on the grounds of a reform school that his father ran. Before he joined the bank in 1901, he was already an empire-building executive at New York Life Insurance. A voluble, glad-handing deal maker, he was an experiment on Pierpont’s part—more chief than Indian—and showed Morgan’s knack for picking bright people. He had come to the Corner to solicit a donation for preserving the Palisades, the high cliffs on the western bank of the Hudson. Pierpont gave \$25,000 of a requested \$125,000, then said to Perkins as he was leaving, “I will give you the whole \$125,000 if you will do something for me.” When Perkins asked what, Pierpont motioned toward the partners’ area. “Take that desk over there.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228708)
罗斯福与摩根是一对秘密的结拜兄弟，这一点从摩根合伙人乔治·W·珀金斯那段离奇的人生旅程中便可窥见——他最终成了两人的副手。珀金斯相貌英俊，想象力丰富，一双狡黠而眼皮沉重的赌徒眼，配上浓密的八字胡，衬着一张看似无害的娃娃脸，却透着几分邪气。他父亲曾在芝加哥创办了一所贫民窟传教学校，而乔治就是在父亲管理的感化院长大的。1901 年加入银行之前，他已是纽约人寿保险公司中一位开疆拓土的高管。这位能说会道、热络握手的交易撮合者，是皮尔庞特手下的一场实验——他更像是酋长，而非印第安人——也展现了摩根善于识人用人的天赋。他来到那家银行，是为了请求捐款保护哈德逊河西岸的帕利塞德峭壁。皮尔庞特在对方要求的 12.5 万美元中先给了 2.5 万，然后在珀金斯告辞时说道：“如果你肯替我做件事，我就把 12.5 万美元全给你。”当帕金斯询问是什么时，皮尔庞特朝合伙人区域示意了一下。“坐那边那张桌子。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228708)

Morgan gave Perkins a day to decide. President McKinley warned him against the killing regime of a Morgan partner, but the cocky Perkins accepted. Things were stormy from the start. J. P. Morgan and Company employed men for secretarial positions, and Perkins wanted to bring his female secretary from New York Life. “I will not have a damned woman in the place,” Pierpont roared, and poor Mary Kihm was stashed away in a bank building around the corner.[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228869) Later, Perkins moved her over to 23 Wall, but with the proviso that she remain upstairs and never appear on the banking floor.
摩根给了帕金斯一天时间考虑。麦金利总统警告他，成为摩根合伙人意味着要忍受残酷的压榨，但自负的帕金斯还是接受了。从一开始就风波不断。J.P.摩根公司雇佣男性担任秘书职位，而帕金斯想把他从纽约人寿带来的女秘书带过来。“我的地盘绝不允许有该死的女人，”皮尔庞特吼道，可怜的玛丽·基姆只好被安排到拐角处的一栋银行大楼里。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2228869) 后来，帕金斯把她调到了华尔街 23 号，但条件是她必须待在上层，绝不允许出现在银行大厅。

Flamboyant and outgoing, George Perkins stands out among early partners because he wrote about trusts even as he created them. He challenged the mores of tight-lipped bankers of the Baronial Age. In August 1902, he pulled off a deal that put him in Pierpont’s league. For a \$3-million fee, he merged the McCormick Harvesting Machine Company and the Deering Harvester Company plus three smaller companies into International Harvester. This new trust had an 85-percent share of the farm-equipment market. Perkins chose the name International Harvester because he foresaw the rise of global corporations and hoped the new trust would “comply with the laws of various countries and be at home everywhere.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229003) Because of the popularity of McCormick Harvesting among farmers, International Harvester was spared the trust-busting fervor that was directed against U.S. Steel.

As the Deering and McCormick families vied to control International Harvester, Perkins came up with an ingenious solution: the House of Morgan would control it. Perkins boasted to Pierpont, “The new company is to be organized by us; its name chosen by us; the state in which it shall be incorporated is left to us, the Board of Directors, the Officers, and the whole outfit left to us—nobody has any right to question in any way any choice we make.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229158) Cyrus Hall McCormick, Jr., later called Perkins the most brilliant negotiator he had ever known.[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229334) When International Harvester was listed on the Stock Exchange, Perkins proudly sent its first report to Roosevelt, writing that “so far as I know, this is the first instance on record that a corporation, on offering its securities to the public, has given to the public complete information as to its affairs.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229503)
在迪林家族和麦考密克家族争夺国际收割机公司控制权之际，珀金斯想出了一个巧妙的解决方案：由摩根家族掌控这家公司。珀金斯向皮尔庞特夸耀道：“新公司将由我们组建，名称由我们选定，注册地由我们决定，董事会、高管乃至整个架构全由我们说了算——任何人无权对我们的任何选择提出质疑。”小塞勒斯·霍尔·麦考密克后来称珀金斯是他所认识的最出色的谈判家。当国际收割机公司在证券交易所上市时，珀金斯自豪地将首份报告呈送给罗斯福，并写道：“据我所知，这是有史以来第一家公司在向公众发行证券时，完全公开其事务状况的案例。”

Perkins’s advent came at an auspicious time for Pierpont Morgan. The trusts had thrust Wall Street into the national spotlight and brought about growing federal scrutiny of high finance. Pierpont was still mired in a nineteenth-century businessman’s contempt for government—when a fellow vestryman at Saint George’s Church, William Jay Schieffelin, the son-in-law of Dr. Markoe, came one day to talk to him about a civil service reform movement, Pierpont thundered, “What do I care about civil service reform!”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229679) To worsen matters, Pierpont had a ferocious attitude toward the press, rarely granted interviews, violently refused to be photographed, and warned employees to withhold information from reporters.
珀金斯加入摩根大通时，正值皮尔庞特·摩根事业的黄金期。当时的托拉斯企业让华尔街成为全国焦点，联邦政府也开始加强对金融巨头的监管。皮尔庞特仍沉浸在 19 世纪商人对政府的那种不屑——当圣乔治教堂的一位教友、马科医生的女婿威廉·杰伊·希弗林来找他谈论公务员制度改革运动时，皮尔庞特吼道："我管什么公务员制度改革！"更糟的是，皮尔庞特对媒体态度强硬，极少接受采访，坚决拒绝拍照，还警告员工不得向记者透露任何信息。

The slick, cool George Perkins, with his natty gray alpaca suits and ingratiating manner, enjoyed the smoke-filled rooms. He was the House of Morgan’s first real power broker and high-level lobbyist. His later antagonist in the struggle for Theodore Roosevelt’s soul, the Kansas Progressive William Allen White, has left some marvelous impressions of Perkins as a silver-tongued devil. White became fascinated with Perkins after Senator Albert J. Beveridge urged White to go into the Senate and said that Perkins, who liked him, could arrange it. White observed that Perkins “made quick decisions, spoke in a soft voice, smiled ingratiatingly, easily.” He wrote, “I used to watch him fishing for men with a certain pride in his skill, which I greatly admired.” He also declared that “he exuded pleasantly the odor of great power that came from the Morgan connection.” At the Bull Moose National Convention in 1912, White saw a “smiling, simpering” Perkins, “spick-n-span, oiled and curled like an Assyrian bull, and a young one, trim and virile.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229826)
油头粉面的乔治·珀金斯，穿着整齐的灰色羊驼毛西装，举止谄媚，热衷于那些烟雾缭绕的密室交易。他是摩根大通旗下首位真正的权力掮客和顶级游说者。后来在争夺西奥多·罗斯福灵魂的较量中，他的对手——堪萨斯州进步党人威廉·艾伦·怀特——对珀金斯留下了绝妙印象，称其为巧舌如簧的魔鬼。怀特之所以对珀金斯着迷，始于参议员阿尔伯特·J·贝弗里奇劝他进入参议院，并说珀金斯欣赏他，可以帮忙安排。怀特观察到珀金斯“决策果断，说话轻声细语，笑容谄媚而自然”。他写道：“我常看他钓鱼般地网罗人才，欣赏他那种让我钦佩不已的技巧。”他还宣称：“他浑身上下散发着来自摩根关系网的庞大权力气息，令人愉悦。”在 1912 年的公牛驼鹿党全国大会上，怀特看到一个“笑眯眯、傻呵呵”的珀金斯，“干净利落，油光锃亮，卷曲着头发，活像一头亚述公牛，而且是一头年轻、精干有力的公牛。”” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229826)

From his days at New York Life, Perkins would always carry a faint spice of scandal and a reputation as a master manipulator. In 1905, the New York State legislature held sensational hearings regarding the life-insurance industry. They were named after Senator William Armstrong and they made the reputation of chief counsel Charles Evans Hughes, later secretary of state and chief justice of the Supreme Court. The committee showed how rapacious insurance executives poured money into trust companies in which they held stock and squandered policy-holders’ money on fancy balls. There were stories about a racy house of mirth in Albany and other devices used by New York Life and other insurance companies to sway legislators. Perkins had been in too high a position at New York Life to get off scot-free. Against Pierpont’s advice, he had retained his New York Life position and Hughes pummeled him with conflict-of-interest issues. Perkins was charged with illegal campaign contributions and falsifying company records related to the sale of railroad securities. Although the indictments were later thrown out, he had to resign from New York Life.
从他在纽约人寿的日子起，珀金斯身上就始终缠绕着一丝丑闻的余味，并享有操纵大师的名声。1905 年，纽约州立法机构就人寿保险行业举行了轰动一时的听证会。这场听证会以参议员威廉·阿姆斯特朗命名，并让首席法律顾问查尔斯·埃文斯·休斯声名鹊起——他后来成为美国国务卿和最高法院首席大法官。委员会揭露了贪婪的保险高管如何将资金注入他们持股的信托公司，并用投保人的钱挥霍于奢华的舞会。还流传着关于奥尔巴尼一处风流欢乐场的传闻，以及纽约人寿等保险公司用来左右立法者的其他手段。珀金斯在纽约人寿的职位太高，无法全身而退。他没有听从皮尔庞特的建议，保留了在纽约人寿的职务，结果遭到休斯就利益冲突问题穷追猛打。 珀金斯被指控在铁路证券销售中涉及非法竞选捐款和伪造公司记录。尽管这些指控后来被撤销，但他还是不得不从 New York Life 辞职。

Where Pierpont’s theorizing was largely nonexistent, Perkins’s was sophisticated. He gave speeches and published pamphlets on every conceivable subject. He was an oddity at the world’s most cryptic bank. He preached a gospel of industrial cooperation, contending that small-scale business depressed wages and retarded technological advance. Not Wall Street, he said, but steam engines and telephones produced trusts. “What is the difference,” he proclaimed, “between the U.S. Steel Corporation, as it was organized by Mr. Morgan, and a Department of Steel as it might be organized by the Government?”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2229996) He drew a parallel Pierpont wouldn’t admit to—that trusts, with their centralized production and distribution, were a form of private socialism. And unlike Pierpont, he saw that they had acquired a public character, and he favored government licensing of interstate companies and extended worker benefits, including profit sharing, social insurance, and old-age pensions. This, he boasted, would be “socialism of the highest, best, and most ideal sort.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230158) Although Teddy Roosevelt sometimes wondered whether Perkins simply rationalized a selfish Morgan agenda, there was a striking likeness between their views.
如果说皮尔庞特几乎没有理论可言，那么珀金斯则堪称深谋远虑。他事无巨细地发表演讲、出版小册子，简直就是全球最神秘银行里的异类。他鼓吹产业合作，认为小规模企业压低工资、阻碍技术进步。他宣称，托拉斯的缔造者不是华尔街，而是蒸汽机和电话。"摩根先生组建的美国钢铁公司，和政府可能组建的钢铁部，两者有什么区别？"他大声疾呼。他还提出了一个皮尔庞特断然不会承认的类比——那些实现集中生产和分配的托拉斯，本质上是一种私有社会主义。与皮尔庞特不同，珀金斯意识到托拉斯已带上公共性质，因此他支持对跨州企业实施政府许可，并扩大工人福利，包括利润分享、社会保险和养老金。 他夸口说，这将是"最高级、最美好、最理想的社会主义。" [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230158) 尽管泰迪·罗斯福有时怀疑珀金斯是否只是在为摩根的自私议程辩护，但他们的观点之间存在着惊人的相似之处。

That a Morgan partner should advocate socialism is not so startling. After all, Pierpont, starting with his railway associations of the late 1880s, espoused industrial cooperation instead of competition. He liked his capitalism neat, tidy, and under bankers’ control. The House of Morgan was banker to established enterprises—the great industrial planning systems that favored stability over innovation, predictability over experimentation, and were threatened by upstart companies; so the bank had a heavy stake in the status quo. Perkins wasn’t the only one in the Morgan camp to applaud moves toward a planned, integrated economy. Later on, Judge Elbert Gary of U.S. Steel, who held private dinners to fix prices in the steel industry, testified: “I would be very glad if we had some place where we could go, to a responsible governmental authority, and say to them, ’Here are our facts and figures, here is our property, here our cost of production; now you tell us what we have the right to do and what prices we have the right to charge.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230332)
摩根的一位合伙人倡导社会主义，这其实并不令人惊讶。毕竟，皮尔庞特从 19 世纪 80 年代末的铁路联合开始，就一直主张产业合作而非竞争。他喜欢资本主义井然有序、整洁利落，并且掌握在银行家手中。摩根财团是成熟企业的银行家——这些大型工业规划体系偏爱稳定而非创新，可预测性而非实验性，并且受到新兴公司的威胁；因此，该银行在维持现状上有着重大利益。珀金斯并非摩根阵营中唯一为迈向计划型一体化经济而喝彩的人。后来，美国钢铁公司的法官埃尔伯特·加里也加入了这一行列。 钢材行业的从业者 Steel 曾通过举行私人晚宴来操控定价，他在证词中说道：“如果能有这样一个地方，让我们可以去找一个负责任的政府机构，对他们说，‘这是我们的数据和报表，这是我们的资产，这是我们的生产成本；现在请你来告诉我们，我们有权做什么，以及有权收取什么价格。’那我会非常高兴。”” [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230332)

As we shall see, the mortal attacks on the House of Morgan came not from socialists but from such trustbusters as Louis D. Brandeis, Felix Frankfurter, and William O. Douglas, who favored small economic units and sharp competition. This tradition would lambaste the Morgan Money Trust as the biggest and most dangerous trust of all. Because the House of Morgan preached socialism for the rich, it always had a partial affinity for those who preached it for the poor.
正如我们将看到的，对摩根财团的致命攻击并非来自社会主义者，而是来自像路易斯·D·布兰代斯、费利克斯·弗兰克福特和威廉·O·道格拉斯这样的反垄断斗士，他们崇尚小型经济实体和激烈竞争。这一传统会将摩根金钱信托抨击为所有信托中最大、最危险的一个。由于摩根财团宣扬的是为富人的社会主义，它始终与那些为穷人宣扬此道者有着某种程度的亲和力。

Yet another dimension of the Pierpont Morgan-Teddy Roosevelt relationship may be seen in the Panama Canal affair. Even as TR fulminated against excessive financial power at home, he gratefully exploited it abroad. In 1902, Congress authorized Roosevelt to pay \$40 million to France to buy its uncompleted assets in the Isthmus of Panama for the construction of a canal. Two years later, Pierpont carried out the financing for this largest real estate transaction in history. He traveled to France to oversee the shipment of gold bullion and paid the rest in foreign exchange to the Banque de France. After receiving payment from the United States, the new state of Panama—which TR helped to pry loose from Colombia—named J. P. Morgan and Company its fiscal agent on Wall Street, with exclusive rights to receive its U.S. government payments. The House of Morgan also handled Panama’s single biggest investment: \$6 million of first mortgages on New York City real estate. So integral was Pierpont in the whole shady Panama Canal affair that one biographer has dubbed him “Roosevelt’s bagman in the taking of the Panama Canal.”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230502)
皮尔庞特·摩根与西奥多·罗斯福关系的另一面，或许可以从巴拿马运河事件中窥见。尽管罗斯福在国内痛斥金融资本过度膨胀，但在国外他却欣然加以利用。1902 年，国会授权罗斯福向法国支付 4000 万美元，购买其在巴拿马地峡尚未完工的运河工程资产。两年后，摩根为这笔史上最大规模的房地产交易完成了融资。他亲赴法国监督金条运输，其余款项则以外汇形式支付给法兰西银行。在美国支付款项后，新成立的巴拿马国——罗斯福助其从哥伦比亚脱离——任命 J.P. 摩根公司为其在华尔街的财务代理人，独家享有接收美国政府付款的权利。摩根家族还经办了巴拿马最大的一笔投资：600 万美元的纽约市房地产第一抵押贷款。 皮尔庞特在巴拿马运河这桩见不得光的交易中扮演了如此关键的角色，以至于一位传记作家将其称为“罗斯福夺取巴拿马运河时的收钱人”。” [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230502)

Thus, in the sparring between Roosevelt and Morgan there was always a certain amount of shadow play, a pretense of greater animosity than actually existed. In the 1904 campaign, the Morgan bank gave \$ 150,000 toward Roosevelt’s reelection. In return, Pierpont was sternly lectured by TR at a 1907 dinner of the Gridiron Club, the president wagging his finger at Morgan and Standard Oil’s Henry Rogers and thundering for business reform. “And if you don’t let us do this,” he insisted, “those who will come after us will rise and bring you to ruin.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230658) When TR enunciated the famous phrase about “malefactors of great wealth,” reporters thought he glanced in Morgan’s direction.[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230793)
因此，罗斯福与摩根之间的交锋总带着几分虚晃一枪的意味，他们刻意营造出比实际更深的敌意。在 1904 年的竞选中，摩根银行向罗斯福的连任捐出了 15 万美元。作为回报，皮尔庞特在 1907 年烤架俱乐部晚宴上被西奥多·罗斯福严厉训诫——总统对着摩根和标准石油的亨利·罗杰斯摇晃手指，厉声要求商业改革。“如果你们不让我们这么做，”他坚持道，“后来者必将揭竿而起，让你们彻底垮台。” [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230658) 当罗斯福说出那句关于“巨富的作恶者”的名言时，记者们注意到他的目光似乎扫向了摩根所在的方向。 [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230793)

Nevertheless, some of the most eloquent encomiums of Pierpont came from TR himself, who “was struck by his very great power and his truthfulness. Any kind of meanness and smallness were alike wholly alien to his nature.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230961) Morgan was less forgiving. When Roosevelt went on an African safari, Pierpont declared that he hoped the first lion he met would do its duty.
尽管如此，对皮尔庞特最真挚的赞美恰恰来自罗斯福本人，他"被其非凡的力量和真诚深深打动。任何卑鄙猥琐的品性都与他格格不入。" [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2230961) 摩根则没那么宽容。当罗斯福前往非洲狩猎时，皮尔庞特宣称，他希望罗斯福遇到的第一头狮子能尽职尽责。

BADGERED by trustbusters, Pierpont turned with relief to other matters in his later years. By the 1900s, in his early sixties, he was often an absentee boss. Cabling instructions to Wall Street two or three times daily from vacation haunts, he never loosened his grip. He was a restless, frustrated man. He didn’t gloat over the stupendous sums he earned, and one doesn’t picture him counting up his net worth in the dead of night. He never mistook business for the whole of life. His real passions and temptations were women, art, and religion.
饱受反垄断攻势困扰的皮尔庞特，晚年转而处理其他事务，倒也松了一口气。到了 20 世纪初，六十出头的他常常当起甩手掌柜。从度假胜地每天两三次电令华尔街，他从未放松掌控。他是个焦躁不安、郁郁寡欢的人。他不为赚取的巨额财富沾沾自喜，你也不会想象他在深夜里计算身家。他从未把生意当成生活的全部。他真正的激情与诱惑，是女人、艺术和宗教。

Pierpont tried to suppress press gossip about his escapades, but the Morgan estrangement was no secret. Husband and wife had little in common, and Fanny remained aloof from the social rigors required of a famous man’s wife. In a 1902 photograph, she still looks tall, refined, and handsome, with her wavy hair swept up. Yet she was frail and sickly and sometimes lacked the strength to travel. By the early 1900s, she had become rather deaf and used an enormous ear trumpet; she was a semi-invalid and ate alone upstairs when the family gathered for Sunday breakfast.
皮尔庞特试图压下媒体对他风流韵事的八卦报道，但摩根夫妇的疏远早已不是秘密。夫妻二人鲜有共同语言，而范妮始终对名门之妻所需履行的社交职责敬而远之。在 1902 年的一张照片里，她依然显得高挑优雅、容貌端庄，卷曲的秀发高高盘起。然而她体弱多病，有时连出趟远门都力不从心。到 20 世纪初，她的听力已严重衰退，不得不使用一只巨大的号角形助听器；她几乎成了半残废之身，每逢家人周日上午聚餐时，她总独自在楼上用餐。

Despite the tensions between Pierpont and Fanny, the Morgans were family-oriented. In 1904, Pierpont bought Jack a big Victorian brown-stone at the corner of Madison Avenue and Thirty-seventh Street, almost a twin of his own. Unexpectedly light and spacious inside, it had forty-five rooms, twenty-two fireplaces, and a dozen bathrooms. By tearing down an intervening house, Jack and his father lived as next-door neighbors, with a common garden in between, from 1905 until Pier-pont’s death, in 1913.
尽管皮尔庞特和范妮之间存在紧张关系，但摩根家族始终重视家庭。1904 年，皮尔庞特为杰克买下了一栋位于麦迪逊大道和三十七街拐角处的维多利亚式褐石大宅，几乎是他自己住所的翻版。出乎意料的是，这栋房子内部明亮宽敞，拥有四十五个房间、二十二个壁炉和十几间浴室。通过拆除中间的一栋房屋，杰克和他的父亲从 1905 年起成了邻居，之间共享一个花园，直到 1913 年皮尔庞特去世。

Jack continued to manage emotional acrobatics, propping up his mother’s failing spirits while retaining his father’s love. In later years, he functioned as a post office, informing his mother of Pierpont’s movements abroad and reporting to his father on his mother’s whereabouts. It was formal and awkward, yet Pierpont and Fanny never turned their children against one other. A thoroughgoing Victorian, Pierpont would inquire respectfully after Fanny and try to minimize Jack’s discomfort.
杰克一直在情感上扮演着平衡的角色，既提振母亲低落的情绪，又维系着父亲的关爱。晚年，他就像一座邮局，向母亲通报皮尔庞特在海外的动向，又向父亲汇报母亲的行程。这种沟通正式而尴尬，但皮尔庞特和范妮从未让孩子们彼此对立。作为地道的维多利亚时代人，皮尔庞特会恭敬地询问范妮的近况，并尽量减轻杰克的难堪。

In letters often heavy with piety, Jack preached resignation to Fanny. Life, he argued, was simply a matter of bowing to eternal verities. Hadn’t he dealt with his father by accepting the inevitable? In the stuffy, patriarchal Morgan world, Fanny’s options were terribly limited. In one 1900 letter, he congratulated her for her better health, then said, “Do keep hold of it now it’s come at last and don’t squander your health on things which seem a necessity to you because they would be a pleasure to others. Keep on letting people do things without you, you’ll be better able to do things for them later on. Here endeth the sermon—and there is no collection.”[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231116)
在那些常常充满虔诚的信件中，杰克劝诫芬妮要顺从。他认为，生活无非就是向永恒的真理低头。他自己不也正是通过接受无法改变的事实，才处理好与父亲的关系吗？在那个沉闷、家长制的摩根世界里，芬妮的选择极其有限。在 1900 年的一封信中，他先是为她身体好转表示祝贺，随后说道：“既然健康终于回来了，一定要好好把握，别把它浪费在那些你觉得必不可少、对别人却是享乐的事情上。继续让别人去做那些事，你不在场反而更好，这样以后你才能更好地为他们做事。训诫到此结束——不收奉献。” [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231116)

Fanny never achieved such holy resignation and suffered terrible anguish. In 1901, when she visited Rome, Jack wrote her a letter that poignantly stated his conviction that she had to submit to her fate. Although Pierpont isn’t mentioned, his ghost hovers in the air:
芬妮从未达到那种神圣的顺从，她承受着巨大的痛苦。1901 年，当她在罗马旅行时，杰克给她写了一封信，痛切地表达了他的信念：她必须向命运低头。虽然信中并未提及皮尔庞特，但他的阴影却始终萦绕在字里行间。

> Your letter from Rome struck me as distinctly blue. . . . I know there are lots of things in your circumstances which you and others would like to have differently but one must accept the inevitable as a thing which is not in one’s own hands, as one does a death or a great anxiety. Nothing one could ever have done and left undone would make two and two into five—if the four is unpleasant there is a moral and religious necessity for accepting the fact and believing in the eternal love which lies behind the troubles.[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231308)
> 你在罗马的来信让我感到明显的忧郁……我知道你的处境中有许多事情是你和其他人希望改变的，但我们必须接受那些无法掌控的必然，就像接受死亡或巨大的焦虑一样。无论我们做过或没做过什么，都无法让二加二等于五——如果这个四令人不快，那么从道德和宗教层面来说，我们有必要接受事实，并相信苦难背后那份永恒的爱。 [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231308)

It seems doubtful that any woman could have wholly gratified Pierpont’s appetites. There were two Pierponts—the proper banker and the sensualist—yoked together under extreme pressure; Pierpont could never integrate the two. His attitude toward women was characterized by the common double standard. At the bank, he was stoutly opposed to women employees, and he didn’t discuss business with women, whom he saw as inhabiting a separate realm. Once a year, on New Year’s Day, Fanny lunched at the Corner—the only time women were invited. At home, however, he was a different man. A female visitor to 219 Madison Avenue once teased Pierpont, saying that while he was charming at home, she heard of the fear he inspired at work. Pierpont blushed, began to protest, then said, “I’m afraid you are right.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231498)
似乎没有任何女性能够完全满足皮尔庞特的欲望。他体内住着两个截然不同的人——一个循规蹈矩的银行家，一个纵情声色的享乐主义者——两者在极度压力下强行捆绑在一起，却始终无法调和。他对女性的态度充斥着典型的双重标准。在银行里，他坚决反对雇佣女员工，也从不与女性谈论生意，认为她们属于另一个世界。每年元旦，范妮会在公司与他共进午餐——这是女性唯一受邀的时刻。然而在家里，他却判若两人。一位造访麦迪逊大道 219 号的女性曾打趣皮尔庞特，说他在家中魅力十足，却听说他在工作中令人畏惧。皮尔庞特脸一红，刚想反驳，随即说道：“恐怕你说得对。” [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231498)

For Pierpont, marriage required discretion, not fidelity. It was a matter of paying homage to convention. In January 1902, Charles Schwab, now president of U.S. Steel, motored to Monte Carlo with Baron Henri Rothschild; their scandalous escapades at roulette made the front pages of New York papers. Disgusted with the “wicked” Schwab, Andrew Carnegie wrote Pierpont, “Of course he never could have fallen so low with us. His resignation would have been called for instanter had he done so.”[53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231669) George Perkins cabled Schwab that the incident hadn’t scandalized Pierpont and that Schwab should go ahead “and have a bully good time.”[54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231837) When he returned to New York, Schwab defended himself, telling Morgan he hadn’t resorted to closed doors. “That’s what doors are for,” snapped Morgan.[55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231963) There’s no question he possessed a wide streak of cynicism. He once told an associate, “A man always has two reasons for the things he does—a good one and the real one.”[56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232132) A revealing comment from a man who styled himself Wall Street’s conscience.
对皮尔庞特而言，婚姻讲究的是体面，而非忠诚。这只是对世俗规范的致敬。1902 年 1 月，时任美国钢铁公司总裁的查尔斯·施瓦布与亨利·罗斯柴尔德男爵驱车前往蒙特卡洛；他们在轮盘赌上的丑闻逸事登上了纽约报纸的头版。安德鲁·卡内基对这位“放荡”的施瓦布深感厌恶，写信给皮尔庞特：“他当然不可能在我们这儿堕落到这种地步。要是他敢这么做，早就被要求立即辞职了。” [53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231669) 乔治·珀金斯发电报给施瓦布，说这件事并未让皮尔庞特感到震惊，并让施瓦布尽管“去痛快玩一场”。 [54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231837) 回到纽约后，施瓦布为自己辩护，告诉摩根自己并没有躲着别人。“门就是用来关的，”摩根冷冷说道。 [55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2231963) 毫无疑问，他骨子里有种玩世不恭的厚颜。他曾对一位同事说：“一个人做事总有两个理由——一个冠冕堂皇的，一个真正的。”” [56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232132) 一位自称华尔街良心的男人，说出了一句耐人寻味的话。

In matters of art, Pierpont’s standards were puritanical. As a member of the board of the Metropolitan Opera, he was instrumental in canceling production of Richard Strauss’s *Salome.* The first-night audience had found the story of the crazed princess who wanted John the Baptist’s head too daring for its tastes. Also, rehearsals had been held on Sunday mornings, which infuriated the local clergy. The production was spiked. In embarrassment, another board member, Otto Kahn, wrote to Strauss that “the responsibility for the *Salome* veto must be shared by the clumsiness and the honestly felt, but in this case, totally inappropriate religiosity of Morgan.”[57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232295)
在艺术问题上，皮尔庞特的标准清教徒式。作为大都会歌剧院的董事会成员，他在取消理查·施特劳斯的《莎乐美》制作中起了关键作用。首演观众觉得那个疯狂公主想要施洗约翰头颅的故事太过大胆，难以接受。此外，排练安排在周日早晨，激怒了当地神职人员。该剧最终被禁。另一位董事会成员奥托·卡恩感到尴尬，写信给施特劳斯说：“《莎乐美》被否决的责任，一半要归咎于摩根的笨拙，一半要归咎于他那虽然真诚但在这种情况下完全不恰当的宗教狂热。” [57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232295)

While protecting public morals, Pierpont conducted amorous escapades aboard his yachts, in private railroad cars, and at European spas. Wall Street wits said he collected old masters and old mistresses. “Few women could withstand his leonine love-making,” insisted an early Pierpont biographer.[58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232471) In his larks can be seen the familiar comedy of the older man suddenly unbuttoned—he could be a jovial Santa Claus. In Paris, he would squire mistresses to a jeweler on the rue de la Paix and invite them to indulge themselves. Once, in Cairo, he tossed a handful of gold jewelry on a hotel table and cried to the ladies, “Now, help yourselves!”[59](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232639) (The party included a bishop: did he join in the merriment?) During one Seattle outing, everyone was given a fur. A New York joke of the early 1900s apparently referred to Pierpont’s florid face and generosity. One chorus girl says to another, “I got a pearl out of a fresh oyster at Shankley’s.” “That’s nothing,” replies her friend. “I got a whole diamond necklace out of an old lobster.”[60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232804)
在维护公共道德的同时，皮尔庞特却在游艇、私人车厢和欧洲温泉胜地上演着风流韵事。华尔街的智者们说他既收藏大师画作，也收藏老情人。一位早期传记作者坚称：“很少有女人能抵挡他狮子般的求爱。”在他的玩笑中，可以看到熟悉的喜剧场面——年长男人突然松开了领口，他就像一个快乐的圣诞老人。在巴黎，他会带着情妇们去和平街的珠宝店，让她们尽情挑选。有一次在开罗，他把一把金首饰扔在酒店桌子上，对女士们喊道：“来吧，自己拿！”（同行的还有一位主教：他是否也加入了这场欢闹？）在西雅图的一次出游中，每个人都得到了一件皮草。20 世纪初纽约的一个笑话显然是指皮尔庞特红润的脸庞和慷慨。一个合唱团女孩对另一个说：“我在香克利的新鲜牡蛎里吃出了一颗珍珠。”她的朋友回答：“那算什么。” “我从一只老龙虾里弄出了一整条钻石项链。” [60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232804)

Given Pierpont’s theatrical approach to business, it is fitting that he preferred the company of actresses. He gravitated toward women who were free and independent, sassy and high-spirited. Rumors had him competing with Diamond Jim Brady for the affections of Lillian Russell. His most celebrated affair involved the tall, voluptuous Maxine Elliott. She was a stately woman with dark eyes, a long neck, and an imposing presence. She had a provocative tongue—something that always seemed to attract Morgan. “Why, you men in Wall Street are like a lot of cannibals,” she taunted him. “You devour anything that comes along—if it is edible.”[61](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232976) She made such withering comments about the design of *Corsair III*—especially Pierpont’s having placed the cabins below-decks—that he shifted the arrangements.
鉴于皮尔庞特那种戏剧化的经商方式，他偏爱与女演员为伍倒也合情合理。他总被那些自由独立、泼辣豪爽的女性所吸引。有传闻说，他曾与钻石吉姆·布雷迪争夺莉莲·拉塞尔的芳心。而他最轰动的一段情史，则是与高挑丰腴的玛克辛·艾略特。她是个端庄的女人，黑眼睛、长颈项，气场十足，言语间充满挑衅——这一点似乎总能吸引摩根。“哎呀，你们华尔街的男人简直就像一群食人族，”她揶揄道，“但凡能入口的东西，你们都照吞不误。” [61](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2232976) 她还对“海盗三号”的设计出言刻薄——尤其是皮尔庞特把船舱安排在甲板下——逼得他改了布局。

Maxine Elliott was the first woman to build a Broadway theater, purchasing the needed lot two months after the 1907 panic. Scandalmongers attributed the financing to Morgan. When he and Maxine returned from Europe aboard the same ship in 1908—a rare lapse in Morgan discretion—reporters asked him if he had a stake in the theater. “The only interest I have in Maxine Elliott’s Theatre is that I’d like to get a free ticket on opening night,” he said.[62](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233146) Legend claims he shared her favors with King Edward VII, whom she met at Marienbad in 1908.
Maxine Elliott 是首位建造百老汇剧院的女性，在 1907 年金融危机两个月后买下了所需地块。造谣者将这笔资金归因于摩根。1908 年，当他和 Maxine 乘坐同一艘船从欧洲返回时——摩根难得一次失态——记者问他是否在剧院有股份。他说：“我对 Maxine Elliott 剧院唯一的兴趣，就是想在开幕之夜拿到一张免费票。” [62](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233146) 传说他还与爱德华七世共享过她的青睐，这位国王是她在 1908 年于马里安巴结识的。

These larks, concentrated in Pierpont’s later life, were not without Falstaffian pathos. Yet Pierpont could also be a courtly, old-fashioned lover. His last mistress seems to have been Lady Victoria Sackville-West, the daughter of a former British ambassador to Washington. She recorded how the portly old banker, randy as a schoolboy, suddenly crushed her in his embrace. She wrote in her diary in 1912, “He holds my hand with much affection and says he would never care for me in any way I would not approve of, that he was sorry to be so old, but I was the one woman he loved and he would never change.”[63](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233321) For a financial god, how tenderly apologetic\!
这些风流韵事集中于皮尔庞特的晚年，不乏福斯塔夫式的悲凉。但他亦不失为一位温文尔雅、老派浪漫的情人。他最后的情人似乎是曾任英国驻华盛顿大使的女儿——维多利亚·萨克维尔-韦斯特夫人。她记录下这位体态臃肿的老银行家，竟像少年般情欲勃发，突然将她紧紧拥入怀中。1912 年她在日记中写道：“他深情地握着我的手，说绝不会做任何令我不悦的事，遗憾自己如此年迈，但我是他一生挚爱，此情永不渝。” [63](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233321) 身为金融之神，这番歉意何其温柔！

Even at the end of his life, Pierpont had a craving for romance that had probably not been satisfied since his brief marriage to Mimi Sturges fifty years before. Some spot inside him was left untouched by the storied maneuvers on Wall Street, some emptiness that his giant exploits couldn’t fill. Even after Pierpont’s death, his family would track his liaisons as *objets d’art* he had owned mysteriously surfaced in the collections of other families. In 1936, a German wrote to Jack claiming to be a bastard from Pierpont’s student days at Gottingen. Jack wasn’t sure the whole thing was a hoax until he established that the man hadn’t been born until after his father had left the university. Yet years after his father’s death, Jack didn’t dismiss the notion out of hand.
即便到了生命尽头，皮尔庞特对浪漫的渴望仍未消减——这份渴求自五十年前与咪咪·斯图奇斯那段短暂婚姻后便再未满足过。华尔街那些传奇般的博弈不曾触及他心底某个角落，那些惊天动地的成就也填不满某种空虚。甚至在皮尔庞特去世后，他的家人仍能通过他生前收藏的艺术品神秘地出现在其他家族藏品中，追查出他的风流韵事。1936 年，有个德国人写信给杰克，自称是皮尔庞特在哥廷根大学求学时的私生子。杰克起初怀疑这是个骗局，直到确认此人在父亲离开大学后才出生。然而即便父亲去世多年，杰克也并未完全否定这种可能。

In spite of their number, these affairs consumed less of Pierpont’s time and interest than his true aphrodisiac—art collecting. When Junius died, Pierpont had a Thackeray manuscript and a few Egyptian antiquities. Then his collecting blossomed along with his banks’ profits. At first, he concentrated on books and manuscripts and letters of British royalty, storing them in his Madison Avenue basement. Soon they were heaped upon chairs, and he couldn’t keep track of them. Other works gathered dust in 23 Wall’s vaults and in a warehouse on East Forty-second Street.
尽管这些事务数量众多，但它们占用的皮尔庞特的时间和精力，远不及他真正的兴奋剂——艺术品收藏。朱尼厄斯去世时，皮尔庞特仅有一部萨克雷的手稿和几件埃及古董。随后，随着银行利润的增长，他的收藏热情也蓬勃绽放。起初，他专注于收集英国王室的书信、手稿和书籍，将它们存放在麦迪逊大道的地下室里。很快，这些藏品堆满了椅子，连他自己也记不清都有哪些了。其他作品则尘封在华尔街 23 号的金库和东 42 街的一间仓库里。

In 1900, he bought property adjoining his house, on East Thirty-sixth Street and drafted architect Charles F. McKim to design a library for his collection. McKim created an Italian Renaissance palace of a coldly remote and balanced beauty. Its marble blocks were so perfectly fitted they required no binding material—a method McKim copied, at considerable expense, from the Greeks. When he settled into the library in 1906, Pierpont took for his office the magnificent West Room, with its walls of crimson damask from the Chigi palace in Rome. A door in the corner opened into the vault. Junius’s portrait hung above the mantel. The library was nicknamed the Uptown Branch of J. P. Morgan and Company.
1900 年，他在东三十六街购得毗邻宅邸的地产，并委托建筑师查尔斯·F·麦基姆为其藏书设计一座图书馆。麦基姆打造了一座意大利文艺复兴风格的宫殿，呈现出冷峻疏离而均衡的美感。大理石砌块严丝合缝，无需任何粘合材料——这是麦基姆不惜重金从希腊人那里学来的工艺。1906 年皮尔庞特迁入图书馆后，将恢弘的西厅辟为自己的办公室，墙面覆以来自罗马基吉宫的深红色锦缎。转角处的一扇门通向保险库，壁炉上方悬挂着朱尼厄斯的肖像。这座图书馆被戏称为"J.P.摩根公司上城分馆"。

To catalogue the collection, Pierpont in 1905 hired a pretty young woman named Belle da Costa Greene. Only twenty-two, she had impressed Pierpont’s nephew with her knowledge of rare books at Princeton’s library. She was the product of a broken marriage—she grew up in New Jersey with her mother, who was a music teacher—and had no college education. Dark and enchanting, with green eyes, she had a complexion so dusky that she referred fancifully to her “Portuguese origins,” and she was probably part black. Belle Greene had a ferocious wit and remarkable self-confidence. She became more than Pierpont’s librarian: she was his confidante, soul mate, and possibly mistress. She read Dickens and the Bible to him and would even attend him at the all-night library session during the 1907 panic.
为整理这批藏品，皮尔庞特于 1905 年雇佣了一位名叫贝儿·达·科斯塔·格林的年轻女子。她年仅二十二岁，便以对普林斯顿图书馆善本的渊博学识打动了皮尔庞特的侄子。她出身于一个破碎的婚姻家庭——在新泽西州随母亲（一位音乐教师）长大，且未受过大学教育。她肤黑眸绿、魅力非凡，因肤色较深而戏称自己有"葡萄牙血统"，实际上可能带有黑人血统。贝儿·格林才思敏捷，自信非凡。她不仅是皮尔庞特的图书管理员，更成了他的红颜知己、灵魂伴侣，甚至可能是情人。她为他朗读狄更斯和《圣经》，在 1907 年金融危机期间，甚至在通宵图书馆里陪伴他。

If the financier liked saucy women, Belle Greene surpassed all rivals. When a lumber magnate proposed to her, she cabled back “All proposals will be considered alphabetically after my fiftieth birthday.”[64](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233491) She daringly posed nude for drawings and enjoyed a Bohemian freedom. Also the toast of the Harrimans and the Rockefellers, she stayed at Claridge’s in London and the Ritz in Paris when on Morgan missions. She could be a buccaneer as well; she once told an assistant, “If a person is a worm, you step on him.”[65](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233651) Even when she became famous as the director of the Pierpont Morgan Library, she was as mysterious as her mentor and never lectured in public or accepted any honorary awards. Like Pierpont, she burned her letters and diaries before she died in 1950.
如果这位金融家喜欢风情万种的女子，那贝儿·格林绝对让所有对手望尘莫及。有位木材大亨向她求婚时，她回电报说：“所有求婚将在五十岁生日后按字母顺序考虑。” [64](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233491) 她大胆地裸体摆造型作画，享受着波西米亚式的自由。作为哈里曼家族和洛克菲勒家族的座上宾，她在执行摩根的任务时，住伦敦的 Claridge's 酒店和巴黎的 Ritz 酒店。她也有海盗般的手段；曾对助理说：“如果一个人是条虫，你就踩他。” [65](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233651) 即使后来成为皮尔庞特·摩根图书馆馆长而声名显赫，她仍像导师一样神秘，从不公开演讲或接受任何荣誉奖项。和皮尔庞特一样，她在 1950 年去世前烧掉了所有信件和日记。

In Belle Greene, Pierpont’s infatuation with women and art converged. There was some sexual element to the relationship. When she had a four-year affair with connoisseur Bernard Berenson, she insisted that he keep it secret, so as not to awaken Pierpont’s jealousy. She flowered in her role as doyenne of the library, presiding in Renaissance gowns, gesturing with a green silk handkerchief, and personally representing Pierpont at art auctions. The forty-six-year age difference between tycoon and librarian didn’t seem to matter. “He was almost a father to me,” she said after Pierpont died. “His never-failing sympathy, his understanding, and his great confidence and trust in me bridged all the difference in age, wealth, and position.”[66](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233777) She would be an important figure for many members of the Morgan family and would later appeal to Jack no less than to his father.
在 Belle Greene 身上，Pierpont 对女性和艺术的迷恋合二为一。两人关系中存在某种性吸引的成分。当她与鉴赏家 Bernard Berenson 保持了一段长达四年的私情时，她坚持要求对方保密，以免激起 Pierpont 的嫉妒。她以图书馆女主人之姿绽放光彩，身着文艺复兴风格的礼服，挥动着绿色丝绸手帕，并亲自代表 Pierpont 出席艺术品拍卖会。这位大亨与图书管理员之间四十六岁的年龄差似乎无关紧要。“他几乎就像我的父亲，”Pierpont 去世后她这样说道，“他永不枯竭的关怀、理解，以及对我极大的信任，弥合了我们之间在年龄、财富和地位上的所有差距。” [66](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233777) 她日后成了 Morgan 家族许多成员的重要人物，对 Jack 的吸引力丝毫不亚于对他父亲。

Eventually Pierpont put together the largest art collection of any private individual of his day, perhaps of any day. It had Napoleon’s watch, Leonardo da Vinci’s notebooks, Catherine the Great’s snuff box, jewelry of the Medici family, Shakespeare first folios, a five-page letter of George Washington’s, Roman coins showing the heads of all twelve Caesars save one. Oblivious to Impressionists and modern American artists, he favored objects with long, romantic histories, European art sanctified by age. The banker of old money did prefer old masters, and valued exquisite craftsmanship and costly materials. Yet paintings accounted for a scant 5 percent of his collection. He preferred tapestries, jewel-encrusted books, gilded altarpieces, illuminated manuscripts, gold and silver cups, porcelains, and ivory. In stressing decorative arts, he followed in the footsteps of the Rothschilds, the Medicis, and other merchant princes. He was proud of his holdings and printed up private catalogues of his collection, which he distributed to the royal households of Europe.
最终，皮尔庞特汇聚了当时——或许也是史上——最庞大的私人艺术收藏。其中包括拿破仑的怀表、达芬奇的手稿、叶卡捷琳娜大帝的鼻烟盒、美第奇家族的首饰、莎士比亚第一对开本、一封长达五页的乔治·华盛顿亲笔信，以及刻有十二位凯撒头像（缺一位）的古罗马钱币。他对印象派和现代美国艺术家视而不见，偏爱那些拥有漫长浪漫历史、被岁月封圣的欧洲艺术品。这位老钱家族的银行家确实钟情于古典大师之作，珍视精妙工艺与昂贵材质。然而，绘画仅占其收藏的区区 5%。他更青睐挂毯、镶珠宝的书籍、镀金祭坛画、泥金手抄本、金银杯盏、瓷器与象牙制品。在强调装饰艺术方面，他追随了罗斯柴尔德家族、美第奇家族及其他商业王侯的脚步。 他对自己收藏的藏品颇为自豪，专门印制了私人藏品目录，分发给欧洲各王室。

Morgan the collector was recognizably the same man as Morgan the banker. He hated to haggle. He would come to terms by asking a dealer what he had paid and then tacking on 10 or 15 percent; one recalls Pierpont barking bids for foreign exchange on a take-it-or-leave-it basis. In art and finance, he relied on the deal maker as much as the deal. Francis Henry Taylor, who studied Morgan’s habits as a collector, wrote, “He was accused of not looking at the objects when in reality he was looking into the eye of the man who was trying to sell it to him. It was, after all, how he had reached the summit in finance and it had paid off well.”[67](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233912) To protect himself, he would buy a picture conditionally and leave it on a chair, gathering the free comments of other dealers before completing the purchase. Once, to test art dealer Joseph Duveen’s knowledge of Chinese ceramics, he set out five on display. “Only three of them are genuine,” he said. “Now tell me which they are.” Duveen smashed the two fakes with his cane.[68](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234072)
收藏家摩根和银行家摩根其实是同一个人。他讨厌讨价还价。谈交易时，他通常直接问卖家当初花了多少钱，然后在此基础上加价 10%或 15%——还记得皮尔庞特在外汇市场上那种爱买不买的喊价方式吗？无论在艺术还是金融领域，他同等地看重交易本身和撮合交易的人。研究过摩根收藏习惯的弗朗西斯·亨利·泰勒写道：“人们指责他买东西时不看货，实际上他盯着的是卖家的眼睛。毕竟，他就是靠这招登上金融巅峰的，而且屡试不爽。” [67](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2233912) 为了保险起见，他买画时会先附条件购买，把画放在椅子上，听听其他 dealers 的免费意见，再决定是否完成交易。有一次，为了测试 art dealer 约瑟夫·杜维恩对中国陶瓷的了解，他摆了五件瓷器。“其中只有三件是真品，”他说，“现在告诉我哪三件是真的。”杜维恩用手杖砸碎了那两件赝品。 [68](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234072)

The godfather of U.S. Steel knew that to create a *big* collection he had to buy art in huge batches and purchase entire collections. He roared tenaciously through art history like a freight train shunting from one track to the next. “I have done with the Greek antiquities,” he wrote his sister Mary Burns. “I am at the Egyptian.”[69](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234232) His determination was awesome. Wanting manuscripts owned by one of Lord Byron’s relatives in Greece, he stationed an agent there, armed with a letter of credit. For several years, this lonely sentinel bought Byron manuscripts as they came on the market until the collection was complete.
美国钢铁公司（U.S. Steel）的教父明白，要打造一个庞大的收藏，就必须批量购入艺术品，并整批收购别人的全部藏品。他像一辆在轨道间切换的货运列车，顽强地咆哮着穿越艺术史。“希腊古物我已经搞定了，”他在给妹妹玛丽·伯恩斯的信中写道，“现在轮到埃及文物了。” [69](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234232) 他的决心令人敬畏。为了得到拜伦勋爵的一位希腊亲戚所持有的手稿，他派了一名代理人驻扎当地，并备好了一封信用证。在那之后的几年里，这位孤独的哨兵只要市场上出现拜伦的手稿就出手买下，直到凑齐整套收藏。

Pierpont could also be childishly impulsive. He loved to hear the stories behind works of art, which he would commit to memory. This genuine interest served him better than the feigned sophistication of insecure millionaires who bought “fine art” and ended up with high-priced junk. When one art dealer appeared with a Vermeer, Pierpont asked, “Who is Vermeer?” After being told, he peered at the \$100,000 painting again. “I’ll take it,” he said. The story may be apocryphal—Morgan had visited European museums for decades and would have seen Vermeers—yet it captures his enthusiasm. In the last analysis, Pierpont relied on his own fallible judgment. In 1911, Jack excitedly reported that a dealer had offered \$176,000 for an original 1530 Copernicus manuscript, the basis of modern astronomy. In a huff, Pierpont cabled back: “Do not care for Copernicus, certainly not at such absurd price.”[70](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234388)
皮尔庞特有时也会表现出孩子气的冲动。他喜欢了解艺术品背后的故事，并牢记于心。这种真诚的兴趣，远比那些缺乏安全感的百万富翁们装模作样地附庸风雅要高明得多——那些富翁花钱买“高雅艺术”，结果却弄回来一堆高价垃圾。有一次，一位艺术品商人带来一幅维米尔的作品，皮尔庞特问道：“维米尔是谁？”听完介绍后，他又仔细打量了一下那幅标价十万美元的画作。“我要了，”他说。这个故事可能不足为信——摩根几十年来走访过欧洲各大博物馆，理应见过维米尔的画作——但它确实展现了他的热情。归根结底，皮尔庞特更依赖自己并不完美的判断力。1911 年，杰克兴奋地报告说，有位商人开价十七万六千美元出售一部 1530 年的哥白尼手稿——这是现代天文学的基础。皮尔庞特气冲冲地回电：“对哥白尼没兴趣，更不可能接受如此荒谬的价格。”

And Pierpont could be disarmed by sentiment. One dealer tried to sell him a manuscript collection that included Poe’s *Tamerlane* and Hawthorne’s *Blithedale Romance.* When Morgan wouldn’t budge, the dealer played his trump card. He noted a Longfellow poem about his grandchildren that, the dealer said, reminded him of Pierpont and *his* grandchildren. “Let me see it,” replied Morgan. He put on his spectacles, read the poem, then pounded the table. “I’ll take the collection.”[71](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234565)
皮尔庞特有时会被情感打动。一位经销商试图向他出售一批手稿，其中包括爱伦·坡的《帖木儿》和霍桑的《福谷传奇》。摩根不为所动，经销商便使出了杀手锏。他注意到一首朗费罗写给他孙辈的诗，经销商说这首诗让他想起了皮尔庞特和他的孙辈。“让我看看，”摩根回答。他戴上眼镜，读了那首诗，然后敲了敲桌子。“这套手稿我要了。” [71](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234565)

The scale of Pierpont’s collection was so outsize—it included 225 works of ivory, 140 pieces of majolica, 150 works of Continental silver, and so on—that vanity alone cannot explain it. Rather, it was founded in an impulse that paralleled his banking ambition—to put America on a par with the European civilization he so admired. As in banking, he honored Old World traditions even as he ransacked them. It was said he wished to acquire a collection so huge that Americans wouldn’t have to travel to Europe for culture. After 1897, he gave steadily to the Metropolitan Museum of Art and became its board president in 1904. The board of trustees often met in his house. To mount a patriotic assault on European masterpieces, he packed the board with millionaire friends—Frick, Harkness, Rogers, and other industrial captains. In 1905, he brought Sir Purdon Clarke from the South Kensington Museum to direct the museum and then Bloomsbury art critic, Roger Fry, as its curator of paintings. Fry would later taunt Pierpont for his “perfect insensibility” and “crude historical imagination.”[72](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234716) But the high quality of the Morgan collection would be proof against Fry’s petty gibes.
皮尔庞特收藏的规模如此庞大——包括 225 件象牙制品、140 件马约利卡陶器、150 件欧洲大陆银器等——绝不仅仅是虚荣心可以解释的。相反，这种收藏冲动与他在银行业中的野心如出一辙：让美国与他所仰慕的欧洲文明并驾齐驱。就像在银行业一样，他在掠夺旧世界传统的同时，也对其表示敬意。据说，他希望获得如此庞大的收藏，以至于美国人不必为了寻求文化熏陶而远赴欧洲。1897 年后，他持续向大都会艺术博物馆捐赠，并于 1904 年成为该馆董事会主席。董事会经常在他的家中开会。为了对欧洲杰作发起一场爱国式的“进攻”，他将董事会塞满了百万富翁朋友——弗里克、哈克尼斯、罗杰斯等工业巨头。1905 年，他从南肯辛顿博物馆请来珀登·克拉克爵士担任馆长，随后又聘请布卢姆斯伯里的艺术评论家罗杰·弗莱担任绘画策展人。 弗莱后来嘲笑皮尔庞特“完全麻木不仁”和“粗鄙的历史想象力”。 [72](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234716) 然而，摩根收藏的高品质足以让弗莱那些琐碎的嘲讽不攻自破。

In 1904, after acquiring the townhouse next-door to 13 Princes Gate, he considered converting the two buildings into a museum as a memorial to his father. He also hoped to create memorials to Junius in the four cities in which he had lived—Holyoke, Massachusetts, Hartford, Boston, and London. After deciding that the enlarged London house still couldn’t encompass his collection, he commemorated Junius by building the \$1.4-million Morgan Memorial in Hartford, doubling the size of that city’s art museum, the Wadsworth Atheneum. This single bequest, Pierpont’s largest, surpassed the \$1 million he had given to the Harvard Medical School in 1901 to honor his father.
1904 年，在买下王子门 13 号隔壁的联排别墅后，他考虑将两栋建筑改建成一座博物馆，作为对父亲的纪念。他还希望在他父亲朱尼厄斯曾居住过的四座城市——马萨诸塞州霍利奥克、哈特福德、波士顿和伦敦——为他建立纪念碑。在意识到扩建后的伦敦住宅仍然无法容纳他的全部收藏后，他决定在哈特福德建造耗资 140 万美元的摩根纪念博物馆，使该市的沃兹沃思学会艺术博物馆规模扩大了一倍。这笔皮尔庞特最大的一笔单项捐赠，超过了 1901 年他为纪念父亲而向哈佛医学院捐赠的 100 万美元。

A final note on Pierpont’s collection concerns the rashness with which he financed it. Usually buying art during the summer, he would postpone payment until early the next year—extraordinary to think of the world’s foremost banker buying art on credit! As early as 1902, Teddy Grenfell noted in his journal “vague and disquieting rumors” in the City about the Morgan banks’ financial soundness as a result of the whirlwind art collecting.[73](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234905) He also noted the tension when the time came to settle these purchases at the London or Paris offices. The sums weren’t trivial. At Pierpont’s death, the collection was valued at an estimated \$50 million, or nearly half his entire fortune.
关于皮尔庞特那批藏品，最后要提的是他融资时的鲁莽。他通常在夏天购买艺术品，却要等到次年年初才付款——很难想象，这位全球首屈一指的银行家竟是靠赊账买画的！早在 1902 年，泰迪·格伦费尔就在日记中写道，伦敦金融城里流传着关于摩根银行财务状况的“模糊而令人不安的传言”，起因正是那场旋风式的艺术品收购。 [73](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2234905) 他还提到，每逢要在伦敦或巴黎的办事处结清这些交易时，气氛总是格外紧张。这些款项可不是小数目。皮尔庞特去世时，这些藏品估值约为 5000 万美元，几乎占他全部财产的一半。

This nonstop buying posed a potential threat to Pierpont’s banking capital. This was especially serious because he chose partners for their talent, not to inject fresh capital into the business. It was one of the House of Morgan’s glories that poor boys could join its exclusive club. Yet Pierpont didn’t always husband his capital. Years later, Morgan partner Russell C. Leffingwell passed along the insider stories about the problems created by the art sprees. “The notion that the elder Morgan bought pictures and tapestries partly to make money is certainly contrary to the fact,” he told a colleague. “It was a self-indulgence on a magnificent scale, and a source of great anxiety and at times weakness to his firm, which could well have used the money as capital in the business if he had not spent it so lavishly.”[74](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235121) In the last analysis, the collector’s impulse to spend won out over the banker’s impulse to save.
这种无休止的购买行为对皮尔庞特的银行资本构成了潜在威胁。尤其严重的是，他选择合伙人看重的是才能，而非注入新鲜资本。穷小子也能加入摩根财团这个精英俱乐部，这曾是摩根大通引以为傲的传统。然而皮尔庞特并不总是善于守护自己的资本。多年后，摩根合伙人拉塞尔·C·莱芬韦尔向同事透露了因挥霍无度的艺术品收藏而引发的内幕故事："认为老摩根购买画作和挂毯部分是为了赚钱的想法，显然与事实相悖。这是一种极致奢靡的自我放纵，给他的公司带来了巨大的焦虑，有时甚至削弱了公司实力——若非他如此挥霍，这些资金本可充作商业资本。" [74](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2235121) 归根结底，收藏家挥金如土的冲动压倒了银行家储蓄的习惯。
