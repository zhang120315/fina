# Chapter Nine: Metamorphosis

IN early 1912, the House of Morgan bought 23 Wall Street and its property from Elizabeth Drexel. The hallowed soil fetched the highest price per square foot ever recorded for a real estate deal. A month after Pierpont’s death, wrecking crews demolished the old brownish-gray Drexel Building to clear the way for a new marble palace. Never ones to stint, the Morgan partners bought a quarry of Tennessee marble to guarantee a supply of high-quality construction material.
1912 年初，摩根财团从伊丽莎白·德雷克塞尔手中购得华尔街 23 号及其地产。这块风水宝地创下了房地产交易史上每平方英尺的最高成交价。皮尔庞特去世一个月后，拆迁队便拆除了那座灰褐色的德雷克塞尔旧楼，为一座崭新的大理石宫殿腾出空间。摩根合伙人向来不惜血本，他们买下了一个田纳西大理石采石场，以确保优质建筑材料的供应。

Pierpont had insisted the new building retain a catercorner entrance, facing both Broad and Wall streets. On his last trip to Rome, he had planned to bring home triumphal columns to frame the entrance. Although he never set eyes on the Italian Renaissance building, designed by Trowbridge and Livingston, it preserved his spirit. On December 30, 1913, Jack set the cornerstone, which contained a special copper box. Sealed inside, like saintly relics, were Pierpont’s will, a copy of his Pujo testimony, the articles of partnership, and an appropriate merchant-banking touch—the form used for letters of credit. It was a homage to the past even as the firm moved ahead.
皮尔庞特坚持新建筑要保留一个斜角入口，同时面向百老汇街和华尔街。在他最后一次罗马之行中，他曾计划带回来一些凯旋柱来装饰入口。尽管他从未亲眼见到由特罗布里奇与利文斯顿设计的意大利文艺复兴式建筑，但这栋建筑保留了他的精神。1913 年 12 月 30 日，杰克安放了一块基石，里面有一个特制的铜盒。盒中密封着皮尔庞特的遗嘱、一份他在普乔听证会上的证词副本、合伙契约，以及一项颇具商人银行特色的物品——信用证的格式文件，这些都如同圣物一般。这是对过去的致敬，即便公司正在向前迈进。

Oddly, the angular building, completed in 1914, was smaller than its predecessor. “I wonder what people will think in 300 years or less as to the progress made by Morgans in 35 years,” Teddy Grenfell said slyly to Lamont. In shrinking the building’s size, the firm expelled other tenants, keeping the Corner to itself. Dwarfed by skyscrapers, the short building made extravagant use of such precious land, as if the bank wished to flaunt its immunity to everyday concerns of cost.
奇怪的是，1914 年竣工的这栋棱角分明的建筑，反而比它之前的楼还要小。"我不禁好奇，三五百年后的人们会如何看待摩根家族在三十五年间取得的进步，"泰迪·格伦费尔狡黠地对拉蒙特说道。在缩建大楼后，公司把其他租户都赶走了，让"街角"完全归自家独享。这栋矮楼在高楼大厦的映衬下显得矮小，却如此铺张地占用着寸土寸金的土地，仿佛银行存心要炫示自己不受日常成本困扰。

The new building was compact and mysterious, reflecting the bank’s penchant for privacy. Curtains always shrouded its deeply inset windows. As the *Times* said, “The men of the House of Morgan keep in the background as far as possible. They shun the limelight as they would a plague.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253593) Whereas the old Drexel building had the firm’s name over the doorway, the bank now reverted to London tradition and posted no name.
新大楼紧凑而神秘，彰显了银行对私密性的偏好。它深陷的窗户上始终拉着窗帘。《泰晤士报》曾这样评价："摩根财团的人尽可能退居幕后，躲开聚光灯如同躲瘟疫一般。" [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253593) 旧德雷克塞尔大楼的门楣上还挂着公司名号，如今这家银行却回归伦敦传统，连招牌都不挂了。

The interior reflected the layout of London merchant banks, with an open banking floor on the street level. Set off by a marble-and-glass partition, a double row of partners’ rolltop desks and brass spitoons stood along the Broad Street side. There were dark wood walls and mosaic panels. Fires blazed at the back of the partners’ room, beneath a portrait of Pierpont. Upstairs, each partner had a private office, lined in English oak, and a fireplace. The upper floors housed a private dining room and Jack Morgan’s barber shop.
办公室内部格局仿照伦敦商业银行的设计，街面层是开放式银行大厅。大理石与玻璃隔断的映衬下，沿布罗德街一侧整齐排列着两排合伙人用的卷盖式办公桌和黄铜痰盂。深色木墙与马赛克镶板相得益彰。合伙人房间后方，壁炉里炉火熊熊，上方悬挂着皮尔庞特的肖像。楼上每位合伙人都有独立办公室，内衬英式橡木，并配有壁炉。再往上还有私人餐厅和杰克·摩根的理发室。

When Jack arrived for his inaugural day as the new Senior, his office was heaped with roses. Now forty-six, he must have taken charge with some trepidation. He was milder, less truculent than his father—he griped and grumbled where his father barked. One journalist wrote that there was in Jack “a suavity . . . that was missing in his father,” and Wall Street scuttlebutt compared him with Pierpont unfavorably.[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253735) As we have seen, his confidence hadn’t been bolstered by his father. And for a Morgan partner, he had been mixed up in a surprising number of fiascoes, including the shipping trust. When he solicited a gold loan in Paris during the 1907 panic, the Banque de France rebuffed him—a hard knock for Junius Morgan’s grandson. Wall Street wits said that after returning to New York in 1905, Jack’s chief innovation at the Corner was introducing English afternoon tea. He was seen as pleasant, friendly, but second-rate.
当杰克以新任高级合伙人的身份上班第一天，他的办公室里堆满了玫瑰。时年四十六岁的他，接手这份重任时想必难免忐忑。他比父亲温和得多，少了几分咄咄逼人——他父亲动辄咆哮，而他只是嘀嘀咕咕地发牢骚。一位记者写道，杰克身上有种"他父亲所欠缺的温文尔雅"，而华尔街的流言飞语则将他与皮尔庞特相提并论，且评价颇为不利。 [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253735) 正如我们所见，他父亲并未给予他多少信心。而且，作为一名摩根合伙人，他竟与数量惊人的失败项目纠缠不清，其中包括航运信托。1907 年恐慌期间，他在巴黎寻求黄金贷款时，被法兰西银行断然拒绝——这对朱尼厄斯·摩根的孙子来说，无疑是一记重击。华尔街的揶揄者说，1905 年回到纽约后，杰克在"街角"的最大创新就是引进了英式下午茶。人们觉得他这人讨喜、友善，但终究是二流角色。

Jack handled the succession in an intelligent, self-protective way. He did what Pierpont could never do—presided in a relaxed manner, delegating authority to Davison, Lamont, and others. Not hampered by his father’s flaming temper or ego, he didn’t feel threatened by talented men of his own age and prided himself on his stable of *prima donnas.* The way he restructured the bank suited the needs of the Diplomatic Age, which required a team of strong, independent partners to undertake government missions. The general caliber of the partners would improve measurably under Jack’s tutelage.
杰克以明智且自保的方式完成了接班。他做到了皮尔庞特永远做不到的事——以轻松的姿态主持大局，将权力下放给戴维森、拉蒙特等人。他不像父亲那样性情火爆、刚愎自用，也不忌惮身边有才华的同龄人，反而以自己麾下拥有一群"天之骄子"而自豪。他对银行的重组方式恰好契合了"外交时代"的需求——那个时代需要一支由强大而独立的合伙人组成的团队来承担政府使命。在杰克的调教下，合伙人的整体素质将显著提升。

Decisions were reached by consensus. Where Pierpont held no regular meetings until the 1907 panic, Jack scheduled daily partners’ meetings in the informal style of a British merchant bank. No stenographer was present, and no minutes were kept, only lists of attending partners. Where Pierpont preferred subservient partners, Jack would create a bank almost top-heavy with executive talent. Whether from insecurity, shrewdness, tact, or sheer laziness, he put together a symphony orchestra that could, if necessary, play without a conductor.
决策经协商一致达成。皮尔庞特在 1907 年恐慌前从不召开定期会议，而杰克则仿效英国商人银行的非正式风格，每日安排合伙人会议。无速记员在场，也不保留会议记录，仅列出出席合伙人名单。皮尔庞特偏爱顺从的合伙人，杰克却打造了一家几乎因高管人才过多而头重脚轻的银行。无论是出于不安全感、精明、得体，还是纯粹的懒惰，他组建了一支交响乐团，必要时甚至可以在没有指挥的情况下演奏。

Even with this looser grip on the business, Jack could still yank the leash and take control. He held \$32.3 million in Morgan capital, which was the bank’s major cushion. He also reserved his father’s extraordinary powers, which included the right to allocate profits among partners, arbitrate disputes, fire partners, and determine a fired partner’s departing share of capital. These were the trump cards in a private partnership. So long as he was alive, Jack insisted upon certain central Morgan values—such as conservative management, avoidance of speculation, and loyalty to Britain—that set invisible but real fences around his lieutenants.
即使对业务掌控有所放松，杰克依然能随时收紧缰绳，重掌大权。他手握 3230 万美元的摩根资本，这是银行最主要的缓冲垫。他还保留了父亲授予的特别权力，包括在合伙人之间分配利润、仲裁纠纷、解雇合伙人，以及决定被解雇合伙人的退出资本份额。这些是私人合伙制中的王牌。只要他活着，杰克就坚持某些核心的摩根价值观——比如保守经营、避免投机、忠于英国——这些为他的副手们设下了无形但真实的围栏。

Financial partnerships are combustible affairs that frequently blow up as a result of personality clashes and disputes over money. Yet the House of Morgan was always marked by harmony among the partners. If Jack Morgan was devoid of unhealthy egotism and bashful to a fault, his lieutenants, Harry Davison and Tom Lamont, were genial and deferential toward him. A tacit bargain was struck: they would treat Jack with impeccable courtesy, bow to his wishes on important matters, and venerate the Morgan name. In return, they would enjoy day-to-day executive control. Had there been management consultants in those days, they couldn’t have devised a better or wiser compromise.
金融合作往往是易燃易爆的，常因性格冲突和金钱纠纷而破裂。然而，摩根家族内部却始终保持着合伙人的和谐。如果说杰克·摩根缺乏有害的自负且谦逊得过头，那么他的副手哈里·戴维森和汤姆·拉蒙特则对他和蔼可亲且恭敬顺从。他们达成了一种默契：对杰克礼数周全，在重大事务上顺从于他的意愿，并尊崇摩根之名。作为回报，他们则享有日常的经营控制权。即便当时有管理顾问，恐怕也想不出比这更好或更明智的折中方案。

This wasn’t a polite charade in which the partners smirked behind the boss’s back; they had genuine affection for Jack. Years later, Morgan partner and then chairman George Whitney would say:
这并非一场合伙人在老板背后窃笑的礼貌假象；他们真心喜爱杰克。多年后，摩根合伙人兼董事长乔治·惠特尼曾这样说道：

> I always find that I have to guard myself because of a fear that I will sound soft and foolish, but he was a great gentleman, a cultured gentleman, if you know what I mean . . . and he’d deny it like hell if he ever heard me say it to anyone. He was a simple and just as sweet a man as you ever saw. . . . As I say, he was never given credit, because he was shy, but he kept that bunch of primadonnas working, the partners, and he was the unquestioned boss and there was never any argument about it. . . . He wasn’t a buccaneer like his father, but he was a hell of a guy.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253860)
> 我总觉得自己得设防，生怕显得软弱愚蠢，但他真是个顶好的绅士，有教养的绅士，你懂我意思吧……要是让他听见我跟谁这么说，他准得拼命否认。他这人单纯质朴，又温柔体贴，你再也见不到比他更和善的人了……照我说，他从来得不到应有的赞誉，因为他太腼腆，可就是他让那帮难伺候的大牌合伙人乖乖干活，他是毫无争议的老板，从没人质疑过……他不像他父亲那样是个海盗式的人物，但他真是个了不起的家伙。

Had there been rebellion in Jack’s nature, it would have surfaced after Pierpont’s death. Instead, he plunged into a Morgan specialty—father worship. Even after having nursed his mother through her dreadful marriage, he cared for the Hartford grave site of Mimi Sturges Morgan, Pierpont’s first wife. With his New England sense of self-reliance, he didn’t think it sporting or fair to blame one’s parents for one’s troubles; he was no more prone to introversion than Pierpont had been. In 1916, he said of Charles Francis Adams’s autobiographical work, “The depressed and gloomy point of view, and the anger at everyone who had anything to do with his bringing up, because he feels himself not a complete success, are rather distressing.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253995) And he docilely accepted the dynastic nature of merchant banking, nudging his eldest son, Junius, into the bank just as he was pushed by Pierpont. “Junius is not going into the firm,” he told a friend, “but he is coming into the office to see if he is fit to go into the firm later on, which I hope and trust he will be.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254195)
如果杰克本性中有反抗的种子，那么在皮尔庞特去世后早就该发芽了。然而，他却一头扎进了摩根家族的专属领域——对父亲的崇拜。即便他曾在母亲那段可怕的婚姻中悉心照料过她，后来仍不忘为皮尔庞特的第一任妻子米米·斯特奇斯·摩根打理哈特福德的墓地。他骨子里带着新英格兰式的自强精神，认为把自己遭遇的麻烦归咎于父母既不公平也不厚道；他像皮尔庞特一样，从不轻易陷入内省。1916 年，他评价查尔斯·弗朗西斯·亚当斯的自传时说道：“那种抑郁阴郁的视角，以及因为觉得自己不够成功而迁怒于所有参与他成长过程的人，实在令人沮丧。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253995) 他温顺地接受了商业银行的世袭性质，就像当年皮尔庞特推他一样，他也把自己的长子朱尼厄斯推入了银行。 "朱尼厄斯不会直接进入公司，"他告诉一位朋友，"但他会先来办公室历练，看看自己是否适合日后加入公司——我希望也相信他能胜任。" [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254195)

In many respects, Jack’s life evolved into an eerie act of homage as he tried to metamorphose into his father. If children identify with parents to relieve their fear of them, as some psychologists suggest, then Jack must have had a great deal of fear, for he tried very hard to resemble his father. As a *New Yorker* columnist said, “His similarity to his father in thought and outlook is almost weird.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254371) To encourage the confusion, Jack dropped the *Jr.* from his name after Pierpont’s death—a common practice—and took to being called Senior—the name that had been Pierpont’s. Only Tom Lamont and, later, Russell Leffingwell, called him Jack.
从许多方面来看，杰克的人生演变成了一场诡异的致敬仪式——他试图将自己蜕变成父亲的翻版。若如心理学家所言，子女通过认同父母来缓解对父母的恐惧，那么杰克内心必定积压着巨大的恐惧，因为他如此竭尽全力地模仿父亲。正如《纽约客》专栏作家所说："他在思想与见解上与父亲的相似程度近乎诡异。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254371) 雪上加霜的是，皮尔庞特去世后，杰克便不再使用名字中的"小"字（这是常见做法），转而以"老"自称——而这原本是皮尔庞特的称呼。只有汤姆·拉蒙特，以及后来的拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔，仍叫他"杰克"。

That Jack successfully mimicked Pierpont had much to do with their sheer resemblance. There were differences: Jack’s mustache was smaller and trimmer than Pierpont’s walrus affair, and his eyes were gentler and less forbidding than the Senior’s. Jack also had a peculiar stoop, his shoulders hunching forward as if he were muscle-bound or ducking to pass through a low doorway. But the similarities were more striking. Both were six foot two, broad shouldered, and burly—cartoonists scarcely had to alter their sketches of the pear-shaped, top-hatted tycoon. Jack even wore Pierpont’s bloodstone on his watch chain—a favorite touch of the radical caricaturists, who had added it to the iconography of paunchy plutocrats. The strong Morgan nose remained, though without Pierpont’s skin disease.
杰克之所以能成功模仿皮尔庞特，很大程度上得益于两人外貌的惊人相似。当然，差异也是存在的：杰克的胡子比皮尔庞特那副海象般的胡须更小巧整洁，眼神也比前辈更温和、少了几分威严。杰克还有种古怪的驼背，双肩前倾，像是肌肉僵硬，又像要低头穿过矮门。但相似之处更为显著。两人都身高六英尺二，宽肩粗壮——漫画家几乎不需要修改他们笔下那梨形身材、戴着高帽的大亨形象。杰克甚至把皮尔庞特的血石挂在表链上——这是激进漫画家最爱添加的细节，他们将其融入了对脑满肠肥的富豪的描绘中。摩根式的大鼻子保留了下来，只是没有皮尔庞特那种皮肤病。

Contemporaries said the two J. P. Morgans even walked and talked alike. Occasionally, one sees a snapshot of “J. P. Morgan” threatening a reporter with his stick and momentarily cannot tell which Morgan it is. Both were high-strung, thin-skinned, moody, and prone to melancholic self-pity. Deeply emotional, they feared their ungovernable passions. A gruff, snappish way of relieving tension and dealing with disappointment was also conspicuous in both.
同时代的人说，两位 J. P.摩根甚至连走路和说话的方式都如出一辙。偶尔看到一张"J. P.摩根"用手杖威胁记者的照片，会让人一时分不清究竟是哪一位摩根。两人都高度紧张、敏感易怒、情绪多变，且容易陷入忧郁的自怜中。他们情感深沉，却害怕自己难以控制的激情。此外，两人都明显带着一种粗声粗气、急躁易怒的作风，用来缓解压力和应对失望。

It is fascinating to follow Jack as he assumed his father’s trappings. A sampler: In 1915, he wrote a Piccadilly hat shop for “another hat (felt) of the same shape as those you used to make for the late Mr. Pierpont Morgan.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254522) Like his father, he went for his London tailoring to Henry Poole and Company of Savile Row and to Brooks Brothers in New York. He adopted his father’s yen for gigantic cigars, ordering five thousand at a time. As his caterer, he retained Louis Sherry, who distributed to favored partners fifty bottles of brandy, one hundred of Musigny, and one hundred of Madeira at a clip. He maintained Pierpont’s tradition of sending chests of Chinese tea to friends at Christmastime, wrapped in pretty paper covers. This special Morgan blend, Mandarin Mixture, came from a tiny garden on an inland Chinese plantation. On Christmas Eve, Jack perpetuated the ritual of reading to Morgan children from Dickens’s *Christmas Carol*—using the author’s own manuscript.
跟随杰克沿袭他父亲的行事风格，着实令人着迷。举个例子：1915 年，他给皮卡迪利大街的一家帽子店写信，要一顶“（毛毡）帽子，款式和你们以前为已故的皮尔庞特·摩根先生做的那种一样”。 [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254522) 和他父亲一样，他的伦敦西装找萨维尔街的亨利·普尔公司定制，纽约的西装则交给布克兄弟。他继承了父亲对大雪茄的嗜好，一次就订五千支。他保留了路易斯·谢里作为自己的餐饮供应商，此人每隔一阵子就会向那些受青睐的合作伙伴赠送五十瓶白兰地、一百瓶慕西尼红葡萄酒和一百瓶马德拉酒。他延续了皮尔庞特在圣诞节给朋友送中国茶箱的传统，用漂亮的包装纸裹好。这种特制的摩根混合茶，名为“官茶混合”，来自中国内陆一家茶园的极小一块产地。圣诞前夜，杰克延续了为摩根家的孩子们朗读狄更斯《圣诞颂歌》的惯例——而且用的是作者本人的手稿。

In religion, Jack was pious but less mystical than Pierpont. He, too, became a vestryman of Saint George’s Church, sailed with bishops aboard the *Corsair III*, and resumed Morgan patronage of the Episcopal church, financing a revision of the *American Book of Common Prayer.* The New York Yacht Club got a new Commodore J. P. Morgan, while the Harvard Board of Overseers and the Metropolitan Museum of Art also got a new J. P. Morgan. New York City’s orphans lost nothing from generational change, Jack made up the annual \$100,000 deficit at the New York Lying-in Hospital. (In view of his happy marriage, he was spared the cruel barbs that greeted Pierpont’s generosity.) As a philanthropist, Jack permitted small variations, so long as Morgan themes were preserved. Where Pierpont underwrote Egyptian excavations, Jack specialized in Aztec digs for the American Museum of Natural History. More an Anglophile than his father, Jack joined Lamont in an anonymous donation to Britain’s National Trust to buy the land surrounding Stonehenge, saving the area from development.
在宗教信仰方面，杰克虔诚但不及皮尔庞特那般神秘。他也成为了圣乔治教堂的教区委员，与主教们一同乘坐“海盗三号”出海，并延续了摩根家族对圣公会的资助，出资修订了《美国公祷书》。纽约游艇俱乐部迎来了新任会长 J·P·摩根，而哈佛大学监事会和大都会艺术博物馆也迎来了新的 J·P·摩根。纽约市的孤儿们并未因世代更替而失去什么，杰克填补了纽约产科医院每年 10 万美元的赤字。（得益于他幸福的婚姻，他避免了皮尔庞特慷慨时遭遇的尖刻讽刺。）作为慈善家，杰克允许小幅调整，只要保留摩根家族的主题。皮尔庞特资助埃及考古发掘，而杰克则专注于为美国自然历史博物馆开展阿兹特克遗址的挖掘。 比父亲更热爱英国文化，杰克与拉蒙特匿名向英国国民信托捐款，买下了巨石阵周围的土地，使该地区免于开发。

Before Pierpont’s death, Jack hadn’t shown a particular interest in the library. But soon he developed his father’s habit of leafing through its treasures each morning. Jack lacked the capital to mimic Pierpont’s sweeping romps through European culture—Pierpont’s own collecting had precluded that—so he concentrated instead on books and manuscripts, his specialty being incunabula, books printed before 1500.
在皮尔庞特去世前，杰克对图书馆并未表现出特别的兴趣。但很快，他养成了父亲的习惯——每天早上翻看其中的珍藏。杰克没有足够的资本像皮尔庞特那样在欧洲文化中尽情驰骋——皮尔庞特自己的收藏已经让他望尘莫及——于是他转而专注于书籍和手稿，尤其是古版书，即 1500 年以前印刷的书籍。

Under Pierpont’s strict instructions, Jack retained librarian Belle da Costa Greene, who never fully recovered from Pierpont’s death; over time, Greene’s bright banter would enchant the son as much as it had the father. And over time another amusing generational resemblance between the two became evident—the bullheaded way in which the Morgan men cornered the market in one artist after another. In 1905, Jack had given his father a manuscript version of Thackeray’s *Vanity Fair* and later rounded up remaining Thackerays on the market. Then he marched on Tennyson, eliciting a memorable remark from Greene: “In regard to the Tennyson items which, personally, I loathe, it is a question of perfecting your already very large and fine collection of imbecilities.” No less than Pierpont, Jack found the librarian’s fresh mouth piquant. He replied, “I reluctantly confirm that we ought to have the Tennyson idiocies.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254740)
在皮尔庞特的严格指示下，杰克留用了图书馆员贝尔·达·科斯塔·格林——她从未真正从皮尔庞特的离世中走出来；久而久之，格林那轻快风趣的谈吐同样让儿子着迷，正如当年迷住父亲一样。而随着时间推移，两代人之间另一桩有趣的相似之处也愈发明显——摩根家的男人们那固执地垄断一位又一位艺术家市场的作风。1905 年，杰克曾送给父亲一份萨克雷《名利场》的手稿，随后又将市场上剩余的萨克雷作品悉数搜罗。接着他又对丁尼生出手，引得格林留下一句令人难忘的评语：“至于那些我个人深恶痛绝的丁尼生藏品，这无非是让你那本就庞大而精美的蠢物收藏锦上添花罢了。”和皮尔庞特一样，杰克觉得这位图书馆员口无遮拦的俏皮话颇为有趣。他回复道：“我勉强同意，我们确实该收下那些丁尼生的愚蠢玩意儿。”

With less of a gypsy nature than Pierpont, Jack concentrated on creating stately residences. In 1909, he paid \$10,000 for barren East Island off the North Shore of Long Island, near Glen Cove. To make the grounds fertile, he had manure shipped in by the bargeload. And after constructing a stone bridge to the mainland, Jack built a \$2.5-million red-brick chateau, modeled after Denham Place, a Buckinghamshire mansion, and called Matinicock Point (sometimes spelled Matinecock). Set on an estate of 250 acres, the mansion was graced with a columned entrance, dormer windows, and high chimneys. It had forty-five rooms in all, including twelve bedrooms, thirteen bathrooms, eighteen marble fireplaces, a sixteen-car garage, and even a small gymnasium.[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254903) After Jack and Jessie moved there in 1911 (while still retaining their Madison Avenue brownstone), Pierpont had twitted his son about his proximity to Teddy Roosevelt’s estate. “I too regret my nearness to Oyster Bay,” Jack cabled back, “but expect outlive the troublesome neighbor.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255053) Jack commuted to Wall Street by water each morning, pulling up at the New York Yacht Club’s pier at East Twentieth Street.
比起皮尔庞特那种吉普赛式的随性，杰克更专注于打造气派的宅邸。1909 年，他斥资一万美元买下了长岛北岸格伦科夫附近荒芜的东岛。为了让土地肥沃，他成船地运来肥料。在修建了一座石桥连接大陆后，杰克花费 250 万美元建造了一座红砖城堡，仿照白金汉郡的德纳姆庄园，命名为马蒂尼科克角（有时拼作马蒂内科克）。这座占地 250 英亩的庄园，入口处有廊柱装饰，屋顶有老虎窗，烟囱高耸。总计四十五个房间，包括十二间卧室、十三间浴室、十八座大理石壁炉、一个可停十六辆车的车库，甚至还有一个小型健身房。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2254903) 1911 年杰克和杰西搬去那里后（同时保留了麦迪逊大道的褐石屋），皮尔庞特还打趣儿子，说他住得离西奥多·罗斯福的庄园太近了。 “我也后悔住得离牡蛎湾太近，”杰克回电道，“但估计能熬过这个讨厌的邻居。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255053) 杰克每天早晨从水路前往华尔街，在纽约游艇俱乐部位于东二十街的码头靠岸。

Jack was an inveterate hunter and loved the world of English country houses. With his friend Eric Hambro, Jack bought Gannochy, a shooting lodge with seventeen thousand acres of highland moors in east central Scotland. It was a romantic spot, covered with heather and crossed by deep gorges and salmon-filled streams. Each August, Jack joined the merchant bankers and aristocrats who headed north to Scotland for grouse shooting. His guests sometimes bagged up to a thousand birds a day, while Jack’s daughters, watching from an upstairs lodge window, cheered every missed shot. The Gannochy shoots, which later would include King George VI, helped to seal a new intimacy between England and the House of Morgan.
杰克是个痴迷的狩猎爱好者，热爱英国乡间庄园的世界。他和朋友埃里克·汉布罗一起买下了加诺奇——一座狩猎小屋，附带苏格兰中东部一万七千英亩的高原荒野。那里浪漫如画，遍地石楠，深谷纵横，溪流中游弋着鲑鱼。每年八月，杰克都会加入北上苏格兰猎松鸡的商业银行家和贵族行列。他的客人有时一天能猎获上千只鸟，而杰克的女儿们则从楼上小屋的窗户望出去，为每一次脱靶欢呼喝彩。加诺奇的狩猎活动，后来甚至包括英王乔治六世，为英格兰与摩根家族之间缔结了一段崭新的亲密关系。

Jack and Jessie Morgan spent up to six months of each year in England. *Fortune* magazine left a portrait of their assimilation into British life, starting with their first stay, from 1898 to 1905: “They lived for eight years in England not as exiled Americans but as all but naturalized Englishmen. Mrs. Morgan by background and training took easily to English country ways, English houses, English gardens—the whole domestic economy of a life of which the life in Boston was merely a more meager copy. And her husband found . . . that the life of a gentleman and an Episcopalian could be more gracefully and naturally led in London than on Wall Street in New York.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255230)
杰克和杰西·摩根每年在英国度过长达六个月的时间。《财富》杂志描绘了他们融入英国生活的过程，从 1898 年至 1905 年的首次居留开始：“他们在英国生活了八年，与其说是流亡的美国人，不如说是几乎已归化的英国人。摩根太太凭借自身的背景和教养，轻松地融入了英国的乡村生活方式、英式房屋、英式花园——整个家庭生活模式，而波士顿的生活不过是其较为简陋的翻版。她的丈夫则发现……身为绅士和圣公会教徒，在伦敦比在纽约华尔街更能优雅而自然地生活。”

Socially, Jack shared his father’s snobbery and disdained the hurly-burly of American life. He never tried to broaden his social sphere or enlarge his sympathies. He might switch from the Union Club to the Union League Club, but that was the extent of his social experimentation. He had a special horror of arrivistes. Summering in Newport might be fine for others, but for Jack the place was “swamped by the horrid vulgar lot who make or rather ruin the reputation of it.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255376)
社交方面，杰克继承了他父亲的势利，对美国生活中喧嚣纷扰的种种不屑一顾。他从未试图拓展自己的社交圈或增进对他人理解。他最多不过是从联合俱乐部换到联合联盟俱乐部，这就是他社交尝试的全部范围了。他对暴发户尤为厌恶。在纽波特度夏对别人来说或许不错，但杰克觉得那里“被那帮庸俗讨厌的家伙淹没了，他们造就了——准确说是毁掉了——这个地方的名声。”

The most conspicuous difference between Jack and his father was in their attitude toward the sexes. Both frowned on divorce among partners or employees and preferred male secretaries in the bank. (Until about the 1940s, women who married had to leave the bank, a regulation that led to several secret marriages.) But Jack was also puritanical in private—it is hard to imagine him swearing or telling off-color stories—and he once blushed to tell his children the facts of life. Perhaps reacting to his father’s lechery, he was courtly with women, and he remained absolutely faithful to Jessie, a pretty, somewhat matronly woman.
杰克和他父亲最明显的差异在于对待两性的态度。两人都反对合伙人或员工离婚，并偏好银行聘用男秘书。（直到 20 世纪 40 年代左右，已婚女性必须离职，这条规定导致过好几桩秘密婚姻。）但杰克在私生活上也很清教徒——很难想象他会骂脏话或讲低俗笑话——他甚至在给孩子讲述性知识时都会脸红。或许是对父亲好色之风的反弹，他对女性总是彬彬有礼，对妻子杰西——一位端庄娴雅的漂亮女性——保持着绝对的忠诚。

Jack and Jessie’s marriage was almost suffocatingly close. Jessie filled that little spot of doubt inside her husband. Confident and decisive, she propped up his ego, and he relied implicitly on her judgment in many matters. Jessie was strict with the four children and ran the estates with a firm, expert hand. She was cool and businesslike, and her daughters found it easier to take their problems to their father. But to Jack, Jessie was the supportive presence who compensated for his lifelong insecurity and guaranteed he would be spared his father’s terribly loveless fate.
杰克和杰西的婚姻几乎亲密得令人窒息。杰西填补了丈夫心中那一丝不安的疑窦。她自信果断，支撑着他的自尊，而他在许多事情上也毫无保留地信任她的判断。杰西对四个孩子要求严格，并以果敢老练的手腕打理庄园。她冷静干练，女儿们觉得找父亲倾诉问题更容易。但对杰克而言，杰西始终是那个给予他支持的人，弥补了他一生的不安全感，确保他不会重蹈父亲那极度缺乏爱的命运。

AS the new lord of the House of Morgan, Jack instantly faced two crises inherited from Pierpont. Coming on the heels of the Pujo hearings, they would further embitter him toward the public and confirm his sense of national ingratitude toward Morgan bounty. The first crisis involved his father’s art collection, whose disposition Pierpont had left to him in his will.
作为摩根家族的新掌门人，杰克一上任就面临承袭自皮尔庞特的两大危机。紧随普约听证会之后，这些危机进一步加深了他对公众的怨恨，也让他确信国民对摩根家族的恩惠忘恩负义。第一场危机涉及他父亲的艺术收藏——皮尔庞特在遗嘱中将这批藏品的处置权留给了他。

Originally, most of the paintings and decorative objects were housed at Princes Gate, which, for lack of sufficient space, Pierpont had despaired of turning into a museum. (The books and manuscripts had always been under Belle Greene’s care in New York.) And until 1909, American import duties made it prohibitively expensive to bring home this “foreign” wing of the Morgan collection; then Pierpont, who was big enough to move congressional mountains, spurred the enactment of a duty-free exemption for works of art more than one hundred years old. The decision to transport the collection was hastened by another consideration: if it were in London when Pierpont died, his heirs would have to pay heavy death duties. So in 1912, thousands of pieces of art were packed in giant crates and shipped to New York. To please Morgan, U.S. customs inspectors were sent to London to speed the process.
原本，大部分绘画和装饰品都存放在普林斯盖特，但由于空间不足，皮尔庞特已对将其改造成博物馆不抱希望。（图书和手稿一直由贝尔·格林在纽约保管。）直到 1909 年，美国高昂的进口关税使得将摩根收藏的这个“海外”分支运回国内变得代价高昂；随后，皮尔庞特凭借足以撼动国会山的影响力，推动了一项百年以上艺术品免征关税的法案。加速运输决定的还有另一个考量：如果皮尔庞特去世时这些藏品仍在伦敦，其继承人将不得不支付高额的遗产税。于是 1912 年，数千件艺术品被装入巨型板条箱，运往纽约。为取悦摩根，美国海关检查员被派往伦敦以加快流程。

Since Pierpont had expressed a desire to keep his collection together, its eventual destination was a matter of great speculation. At first, he had bequeathed it to the Metropolitan Museum, of which he was president. As a precondition, however, he asked New York City to appropriate money for a special Morgan wing. This was a rich man’s way of asking for a token of respect and gratitude. Instead, it provoked a vituperative campaign, spearheaded by the Hearst newspapers and some city officials, who excoriated Pierpont for not providing the funds himself.
由于皮尔庞特曾表示希望自己的收藏能完整保存，这批藏品的最终归宿便引发了诸多猜测。起初，他将其遗赠给了自己担任会长的大都会博物馆，但前提条件是要求纽约市拨款建造一座专门的摩根侧厅。这是富人以自己的方式索取一份尊重与感激的表示。然而，此举却引发了一场口诛笔伐，由赫斯特报系和部分市政官员带头，他们痛斥皮尔庞特没有自掏腰包。

In this year of the Money Trust campaign, taxpayers were ripe for Morgan-baiting and prepared to believe that his bank account was bottomless. Stung by the campaign, Pierpont told shocked Metropolitan officials in late 1912 that they might not receive the collection after all. Easily injured, he could be sulky and childish when his pride was hurt. So he left the final decision to Jack. It would be his son’s first large posthumous decision. Under a new state law, Jack had two years from the time of Pierpont’s death to donate the art if he wished to receive an exemption from the inheritance tax.
在这一年的“货币信托”运动中，纳税人早已对摩根充满敌意，并倾向于相信他的银行账户深不见底。被这场运动刺痛后，皮尔庞特在 1912 年底震惊地告诉大都会博物馆的官员，他们可能最终无法得到这批藏品。他容易受伤，当自尊心受挫时，会变得闷闷不乐甚至孩子气。于是，他将最终决定权交给了杰克。这将是儿子在他身后做出的第一个重大决定。根据纽约州的新法律，杰克在皮尔庞特去世后有两年时间决定是否捐赠这些艺术品，以便获得遗产税豁免。

While pondering his decision, Jack temporarily permitted the collection to be exhibited at the Metropolitan Museum. It was a breathtaking event that brought together 4,100 works from London and New York—the one time the complete Morgan collection could be viewed in its entirety. America had never seen artistic riches in such profusion. The word *exhibition* didn’t capture its scope: it was like the unveiling of a major museum, revealing the fruits of the most frenzied buying spree in art history. There were 550 enamels, 260 Renaissance bronzes, nearly 700 pieces of porcelain from the eighteenth century, 39 tapestries, 900 miniatures, more than 50 European paintings. By glimpsing these treasures, the public developed not only a fuller sense of their worth but a possessive feeling toward them as well.
杰克在权衡之际，暂时允许这批藏品在大都会博物馆展出。那是一场令人屏息的盛会，来自伦敦与纽约的 4100 件作品汇聚一堂——这是摩根收藏唯一一次完整亮相。美国从未见过如此丰沛的艺术财富。"展览"一词根本不足以形容其规模：这简直像一座大型博物馆的揭幕，展示了艺术史上最疯狂抢购潮的累累硕果。550 件珐琅器、260 件文艺复兴青铜雕塑、近 700 件 18 世纪瓷器、39 幅挂毯、900 件细密画、50 多幅欧洲绘画。公众在惊鸿一瞥间，不仅更深刻地认识到它们的价值，更油然而生一种占有的渴望。

Now Jack had to weigh the competing claims of his bank and American culture. He and other Morgan partners recalled the unpleasant suspense each year as they wondered whether the Senior’s balance would cover the bills pouring in from London and Paris. And now Jack wondered whether he could cover the \$3 million in inheritance taxes and the \$20 million in individual bequests mandated by Pierpont’s will. The approximately \$20 million in liquid assets in the estate simply did not match the scale of Pierpont’s generosity. While he required liquid capital for bequests, estate taxes, and his business, Jack held, instead, mostly illiquid art masterpieces. What to do?
现在，杰克必须在银行的诉求与美国文化之间做出权衡。他和摩根公司的其他合伙人每年都提心吊胆地揣测，老摩根先生的账户余额能否覆盖从伦敦和巴黎涌来的账单。而此刻，杰克思忖着自己能否凑足皮尔庞特遗嘱中规定的 300 万美元遗产税和 2000 万美元个人遗赠。遗产中约 2000 万美元的流动资产，根本配不上皮尔庞特慷慨大方的气度。杰克虽然需要流动资金来支付遗赠、遗产税并维持生意，可他手中握有的却大多是难以变现的艺术珍品。该如何是好？

The answer came in February 1915 and scandalized the art world: Jack decided to dismantle the collection. First he sold the Chinese porcelains for \$3 million to Duveen Brothers, who resold them to Henry Clay Frick. Then Fragonard’s magnificent *Progress of Love*, four panels executed for Mme du Barry, went for \$1.25 million, also to Frick, who adorned a room of his Fifth Avenue mansion with them. Frick’s new ascendancy as foremost American collector, heir to Pierpont, evidently pleased Jack, who said he had been kinder to him than any of Pierpont’s other business associates. Sugar baron H. O. Havemeyer bought the Vermeer that had captivated Pierpont. “It seems we need the money,” Belle Greene sighed.[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255539)
答案在 1915 年 2 月揭晓，令艺术界一片哗然：杰克决定拆散这批藏品。他先将中国瓷器以 300 万美元卖给了 Duveen Brothers，后者又转手卖给了 Henry Clay Frick。随后，弗拉戈纳尔为杜巴里夫人绘制的四幅精美画作《爱的进程》，以 125 万美元的价格也售予 Frick，Frick 用它们装饰了自己第五大道豪宅的一个房间。Frick 作为皮尔庞特继承人的新身份——美国顶级收藏家——显然令杰克感到满意，他说 Frick 比皮尔庞特的其他商业伙伴对他都要友善。糖业大亨 H. O. Havemeyer 买下了曾令皮尔庞特着迷的维米尔画作。“看来我们确实需要这笔钱，”Belle Greene 叹息道。

By the end of this avalanche of sales—during which Greene battled tenaciously for higher prices—\$8 million worth of art had changed hands at handsome prices. Pierpont’s death hadn’t devastated the art market—the new fortunes being amassed by munitions makers in the World War, fortunes often awarded by the Morgan bank itself, picked up the slack. Greene’s friend Bernard Berenson commented that Pierpont might be dead, “but his soul goes marching on.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255692)
在这场拍卖的狂潮结束时——期间格林为争取更高价格而顽强抗争——价值 800 万美元的艺术品以可观的价格易手。皮尔庞特的去世并未摧毁艺术品市场——第一次世界大战中军火制造商积累的新财富，这些财富往往由摩根银行本身授予，填补了空缺。格林的朋友伯纳德·贝伦森评论说，皮尔庞特或许已经去世，“但他的精神仍在前进。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255692)

The cognoscenti were horrified by the sale, which they portrayed as a brutal, unfeeling massacre of the world’s premiere art collection. Profiting from it, Joseph Duveen nonetheless classified the breakup “with that other great artistic tragedy, the dispersal by the Commonwealth of the carefully chosen treasures of King Charles the First.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2255856) As a salve for bruised feelings, the Metropolitan was given 40 percent of the collection, a monumental bequest of about seven thousand objects, including Raphael’s *Colonna Madonna*, which was the world’s most expensive painting when purchased by Pierpont for £100,000. For all the disappointment, this was the biggest windfall in the museum’s history, forming the heart of its medieval collection.
鉴赏家们对这桩交易感到震惊，称之为一场残酷无情的屠戮——世界顶级艺术收藏就此分崩离析。约瑟夫·杜维恩虽从中获利，但仍将这次拆解比作"另一场伟大的艺术悲剧：共和国政府将查理一世精心挑选的珍宝四散处置"。作为对受伤情感的抚慰，大都会博物馆分得了这批收藏的 40%，约 7000 件不朽遗赠，其中包括拉斐尔的《科隆纳圣母》——当年皮尔庞特以 10 万英镑购得时，它是世上最昂贵的画作。尽管失望在所难免，这仍是该馆史上最大的一笔意外之财，奠定了其中世纪收藏的核心。

Pierpont’s literary collection—about twenty thousand items, including Gutenberg Bibles, papyruses, and manuscripts by Keats, Shelley, Swift, and Dr. Johnson—stayed intact at the library, as did many splendid oddities, such as Marie-Antoinette’s fan, which Jack would give to the French government in 1925. The other major beneficiary was the Morgan Memorial at the Wadsworth Atheneum in Hartford, which Pierpont had built in tribute to Junius. (As Pierpont had insisted, portraits of him and Junius hung side by side at the head of the museum’s grand staircase.) In 1917, Jack gave the museum such a massive bequest of ancient bronzes and European decorative arts—more than thirteen hundred items—that the Wadsworth at once leapt into fifth place among American museums.
皮尔庞特的文学收藏——约两万件，包括古腾堡圣经、纸莎草文献，以及济慈、雪莱、斯威夫特和约翰逊博士的手稿——完好无损地留在图书馆里，同样留下的还有许多奇珍异宝，比如玛丽·安托瓦内特的扇子，1925 年杰克将其赠予了法国政府。另一位主要受益者是哈特福德沃兹沃思艺术馆内的摩根纪念堂，这是皮尔庞特为纪念朱尼厄斯而建造的。（正如皮尔庞特所坚持的那样，他和朱尼厄斯的肖像并肩挂在博物馆主楼梯的顶端。）1917 年，杰克向该博物馆捐赠了大量古代青铜器和欧洲装饰艺术品——超过一千三百件——使沃兹沃思顿时跃居美国博物馆第五位。

Instead of explaining his decision, Jack sprang it unexpectedly on the public. Then he retreated into a touchy silence, heeding Pierpont’s dictum of never answering press attacks. This made him seem guilty and defensive. One can only speculate as to the reasons behind his self-defeating silence. As a private banker, he would have refrained from any statement suggesting a need to shore up the bank’s capital—no secret was more closely guarded by merchant bankers than their capital position. At this point, the House of Morgan had never been examined by regulators or revealed a balance sheet; Jack wasn’t about to discuss Morgan capital in public. It might have also been hard to explain the urgent need for money without indirectly criticizing his father’s prodigality. If blame was to be meted out, it probably should have been directed toward Pierpont, whose collection had outpaced any provision for its storage and display. It was Pierpont, not Jack, who failed to provide for both bank and art collection. Although he did it in boorish, public-be-damned style, Jack may only have been setting things’ aright.
杰克没有解释自己的决定，而是突然向公众抛出这个重磅消息。随后他陷入一种敏感而沉默的状态，谨记皮尔庞特的箴言——绝不对媒体攻击作出回应。这让他显得既心虚又防卫过当。人们只能猜测他这种自毁式的沉默背后有何缘由。作为私人银行家，他绝不会发表任何暗示需要补充银行资本的声明——对于商业银行家而言，资本状况是比任何秘密都更讳莫如深的事。此时，摩根财团从未接受过监管机构审查，也从未公布过资产负债表；杰克绝不会在公开场合讨论摩根资本。此外，若想解释急需资金的缘由，又难免间接批评父亲挥霍无度。若真要追究责任，或许该指向皮尔庞特——他的收藏品规模早已远超为其配备的存储与展示条件。 未能为银行和艺术收藏提供保障的是皮尔庞特，而非杰克。尽管杰克以粗鄙的、一副“公众去死”的做派行事，但他或许只是在拨乱反正而已。

THE second crisis shadowing Jack’s first days at the helm involved the New York, New Haven and Hartford Railroad. Joseph Morgan—Pierpont’s grandfather—had sponsored one of its predecessors, giving it a special place in the family. Going on the road’s board after 1892, Pierpont came to rule it with a mixture of sentimentality, explosive rage, and willful blindness almost without equal in Morgan annals. In 1903, he had brought in Charles S. Mellen—called “the last of the railway czars”—to run the New Haven. Mellen had a smooth, domed head, white mustache, and a cold, sarcastic manner that made him the most hated man in Boston. The New Haven would be a *folie a deux* for Morgan and Mellen, bringing out the worst in both in their contempt for the public.
笼罩在杰克掌舵初期头上的第二场危机，涉及纽约、纽黑文和哈特福德铁路。约瑟夫·摩根——皮尔庞特的祖父——曾资助过这条铁路的前身之一，使它在家族中拥有特殊地位。1892 年后，皮尔庞特进入该铁路董事会，并以一种在摩根家族史上几乎绝无仅有的方式统治着它：多愁善感、雷霆暴怒、刻意装瞎，三者交织。1903 年，他请来了查尔斯·S·梅伦——人称“最后一位铁路沙皇”——来运营纽黑文铁路。梅伦有着圆润的秃头、白色的胡子，以及一副冷嘲热讽的做派，使他成为波士顿最遭人恨的人。纽黑文铁路成了摩根和梅伦之间的“双向疯狂”，两人对公众的蔑视，把他们最糟糕的一面都激发了出来。

The two planned to take over every form of transportation in New England and wantonly usurped steamship lines, interurban electric trolleys, rapid transit systems—anything that threatened their monopoly. The New Haven gobbled up every railroad in Rhode Island, Connecticut, and southern Massachusetts. The centerpiece of their plan was the purchase of the Boston and Maine Railroad in 1907. This was so controversial that Pierpont and Mellen met with President Roosevelt in order to forestall antitrust problems. Though the president offered his tacit consent, he later confessed that he had gone “beyond the verge of propriety in condoning offenses” committed by the New Haven.[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256012)
两人计划接管新英格兰地区所有的交通方式，肆无忌惮地吞并了轮船航线、城际电车、快速交通系统——任何可能威胁到他们垄断地位的运输工具。纽黑文铁路公司吞并了罗德岛、康涅狄格和马萨诸塞州南部的每一家铁路公司。他们计划的核心是 1907 年收购波士顿和缅因铁路。此举引发了巨大争议，以至于皮尔庞特和梅伦不得不与罗斯福总统会面，以防出现反垄断问题。尽管总统当时默许了此事，但他后来承认，自己“在纵容纽黑文铁路公司的违法行为时，已经越过了正当性的边界”。

The New Haven’s expansion was both unwise and unscrupulous. As it paid exorbitant prices to swallow up competitors, its debt load grew crushing. The railroad became a bloated monster of a holding company, with 125,000 employees in 336 subsidiaries. To hide its financial chicanery, it set up hundreds of dummy corporations, some headed by mystified clerks who were periodically called in and told to sign contracts. The House of Morgan made enormous profits from this corporate maze, booking nearly a million dollars in commissions from an incessant flow of stocks and bonds. Meanwhile, the New Haven’s real future competitor—the automobile—escaped the wide net that Pierpont had flung over New England transportation.
纽黑文铁路的扩张既不明智，也不道德。它不惜重金吞并对手，导致负债累累。这条铁路变成了一家臃肿不堪的控股公司，旗下 336 家子公司共有 12.5 万名员工。为了掩盖财务上的花招，它设立了数百家空壳公司，有些甚至由一脸茫然的职员挂名负责，这些人定期被叫去签合同。摩根家族从这个错综复杂的公司迷宫中赚得盆满钵满，仅从源源不断的股票和债券中就拿走了近百万美元的佣金。而与此同时，纽黑文铁路真正的未来竞争者——汽车——却悄然挣脱了皮尔庞特撒向新英格兰交通的巨网。

Unbeknownst to the public, the House of Morgan itself was queasy about Mellen’s stewardship. In May 1908, George Perkins wrote to Pierpont, “I still feel, as I have for a couple of years, that Mr. Mellen is getting the New Haven road into considerable of a muddle by his financial methods, and this, I think, is becoming more or less the general opinion.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256189) The bank began quietly to sell off its securities in the road.
公众并不知晓，摩根家族内部对梅伦的管理也深感不安。1908 年 5 月，乔治·珀金斯写信给皮尔庞特：“这两年来我一直觉得，梅伦先生用他的财务手段把纽黑文铁路搞得一团糟，而且我认为，这已经逐渐成了大家的共识。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256189) 银行开始悄然抛售所持的该铁路公司证券。

Unfortunately for Pierpont’s image, Mellen was a vocal admirer and later said he never undertook any initiative without first consulting Pierpont. “I wear the Morgan collar,” he boasted to reporters, “but I am proud of it. If Mr. Morgan were to order me tomorrow to China or Siberia in his interests, I would pack up and go.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256364) He would leave an indelible portrait of Pierpont as an autocratic board member. “It was Mr. Morgan’s way, when he wished to cut opposition and discussion short, to fling his box of matches from him, bring his fist down, and say, ’Call a vote. Let’s see where these gentlemen stand.’ ”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256531) Other board members, Mellen said, cowered and submitted to him.
不幸的是，对于皮尔庞特的形象而言，梅伦是一位直言不讳的崇拜者，后来他坦言，若不先请教皮尔庞特，他绝不敢轻举妄动。“我戴着摩根牌项圈，”他向记者夸耀道，“但我以此为荣。如果摩根先生为了他的利益明天就命令我去中国或西伯利亚，我会立刻打包出发。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256364) 他笔下皮尔庞特那种独裁董事的形象令人难以忘怀。“摩根先生的做法是，每当他想打断反对意见和争论时，就会把火柴盒扔到一边，一拳砸在桌子上喊道：‘投票表决。看看这些先生们站在哪边。’” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256531) 梅伦说，其他董事会成员都畏缩不前，对他唯命是从。

The Morgan patronage had definite advantages for the railroad. The New Haven’s stock was considered the safest of blue-chip investments and sported a high dividend. And Charles Mellen had redeeming features as a railroad man. For the first time, he enabled passengers to travel from New York to Boston without switching lines. The problem was that Mellen was a thorough rascal. Here was William Allen White’s verdict: “Mellen, in the eyes of economic liberals, was the head devil of the plutocracy in Massachusetts and New England. . . . In politics, Mellen walked to his ends directly, justified by the conscience of a plutocrat, which held in contempt the scruples of democracy.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256702)
摩根的资助对这条铁路来说确实大有裨益。新 haven 铁路公司的股票被视为最稳妥的蓝筹股，分红也很丰厚。而查尔斯·梅伦作为铁路经营者也有其可取之处——他首次让乘客无需换线就能从纽约直达波士顿。问题在于，梅伦是个彻头彻尾的恶棍。威廉·艾伦·怀特对他有这样的评价：“在经济自由派眼中，梅伦是马萨诸塞州乃至新英格兰地区财阀集团的魔鬼头子……在政治上，梅伦行事直截了当，其财阀的良心让他对民主的顾虑嗤之以鼻。”

Congressional investigators later revealed that Mellen handed out about a million dollars in bribes on one suburban line alone. Beyond shame, he even suborned a Harvard professor to deliver lectures favoring lenient regulatory treatment for trains and trolleys. So pervasive was New Haven power in New England that it was termed the “invisible government.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256865) Mellen’s largesse extended right up to the Republican National Committee. When later granted immunity from prosecution, Mellen almost gloried in the vicious squalor, the total absence of business scruples. Testifying about the competition between the New Haven and a rival, he was asked what form it took. “Any form you can imagine—one man cutting the heart out of another, except they were two railroads.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257028)
国会调查人员后来揭露，仅在一段郊区线路上，梅伦就分发了约一百万美元的贿赂。他不仅寡廉鲜耻，还收买了一位哈佛教授，让其发表演讲鼓吹对火车和有轨电车放松监管。纽黑文铁路在新英格兰的势力无孔不入，以至于被称为“隐形政府”。 [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2256865) 梅伦的慷慨甚至延伸到了共和党全国委员会。后来获得豁免权后，他几乎对那些卑鄙龌龊、毫无商业道德的行径引以为豪。在就纽黑文铁路与一家竞争对手的竞争作证时，他被问及竞争的形式。他回答：“你能想象到的任何形式——就像一个人把另一个人的心挖出来，只不过这次是两家铁路公司。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257028)

An open scandal, the New Haven attracted the attention of the most cunning and resourceful foe the House of Morgan would ever face—Louis D. Brandeis, now a “people’s lawyer” but later a Supreme Court justice. The son of eastern European immigrants, a Harvard Law graduate, Brandeis was already a millionaire lawyer in 1907 when he took on the New Haven as a public-interest cause. That year, he spearheaded the fight against the purchase of the Boston & Maine.
一桩公开的丑闻，让纽黑文铁路吸引了摩根财团有史以来最狡猾、最足智多谋的对手——路易斯·D·布兰代斯。他当时是“人民的律师”，后来成为最高法院大法官。作为东欧移民之子、哈佛法学院毕业生，布兰代斯在 1907 年已是百万富翁律师，却把纽黑文案当作公益事业接手。同年，他带头反对收购波士顿缅因铁路。

Brandeis conducted a searching critique of the Gentleman Banker’s Code—those rituals that governed competition among elite banking houses. He sounded themes of excessive banker influence that would be amplified by the Pujo hearings and echoed in the New Deal, later shaping Securities and Exchange Commission policy. He argued for an arm’s-length distance between bankers and companies. For Brandeis, bankers who sat on corporate boards were in a conflict-of-interest situation. Far from being neutral confidants of companies, they were tempted to load up clients with unneeded bonds or charge them inflated commissions. The House of Morgan was his major object lesson; he said it symbolized “a monopolistic and predatory control over the financial and industrial resources of the country.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257162) The Brandeis critique was predicated not on government regulation of monopolies but on breaking them up and reverting to a small-scale competitive economy. Over time, this view would prove far more threatening to the House of Morgan than the trust-busting of Teddy Roosevelt and other supporters of large-scale industry.
布兰代斯对“绅士银行家守则”——那些精英银行家族之间竞争规则的潜规则——进行了犀利的剖析。他反复强调银行家影响力过大的问题，这一主题在普约听证会上被放大，在新政时期得到呼应，并最终影响了美国证券交易委员会的政策制定。他主张银行与企业之间应保持“一臂之距”。在布兰代斯看来，银行家兼任公司董事必然陷入利益冲突。他们绝非企业中立的顾问，反而会诱使客户购买不必要的债券，或收取过高的佣金。摩根财团成了他最主要的反面教材；他说，这家财团象征了“对该国金融与工业资源的垄断性掠夺控制。”布兰代斯的批判并非主张政府监管垄断，而是将其拆分，回归到小型化的竞争性经济模式。 随着时间的推移，这种观点对摩根家族的威胁，将远远超过西奥多·罗斯福和其他大规模工业支持者的反托拉斯行动。

The New Haven’s day of reckoning came in 1911, when its debt burden forced layoffs, pay cuts, and deferral of critical track maintenance. The road piled up a grisly record of train wrecks—four that year, seven the next—that caused dozens of deaths. As the train wrecks mounted in 1912, Brandeis found an ever-wider audience for his attacks on the New Haven, and the Interstate Commerce Commission began to hold hearings on the matter. That summer, Brandeis went to Sea Girt, New Jersey, to consult with Woodrow Wilson, the Democratic presidential nominee. Brandeis advised Wilson on economic matters, wrote speeches, and slipped the Money Trust into his rhetoric, getting Wilson to espouse an end to interlocking directorates between bankers and industrial companies. For Brandeis, the New Haven was an archetypal battle in the eternal war between “the people” and “the interests.”
新黑文铁路的清算日降临于 1911 年，沉重的债务负担迫使公司裁员减薪，就连关键的轨道养护也被搁置。这条铁路线留下了一连串触目惊心的列车事故记录——当年发生四起，次年七起——造成数十人死亡。随着 1912 年事故频发，布兰代斯对黑文铁路的抨击赢得了越来越多的听众，州际商业委员会也开始就此举行听证会。那年夏天，布兰代斯前往新泽西州锡格特，与民主党总统候选人伍德罗·威尔逊会晤。布兰代斯为威尔逊提供经济事务方面的建议，代笔撰写演讲稿，并将"金钱信托"一词植入其演说中，促使威尔逊公开主张终结银行家与工业企业之间的连锁董事制度。在布兰代斯看来，黑文铁路是"人民"与"利益集团"之间永恒战争中的一场典型战役。

Threatened by Brandeis, Mellen fought back in inimitably dirty style. A Boston publication called *Truth*, subsidized by the New Haven, portrayed Brandeis as an agent of Jacob Schiff and described his campaign as part of the “age-long struggle between Jew and Gentile.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257295) In December 1912, Mellen and Morgan issued a stinging press release, accusing Brandeis of trying to destroy confidence in the New Haven. But Brandeis was winning converts, and Mellen was indicted by a federal grand jury on antitrust charges. He waived immunity, apparently hoping to spare Pierpont the strain of a subpoena during the Pujo investigation. The Pujo report further bolstered Brandeis’s case against Morgan and the New Haven. And that was where matters stood at Pierpont’s death.
受布兰代斯威胁，梅伦以极其卑劣的手段反击。由纽黑文铁路公司资助的波士顿刊物《真相》将布兰代斯描绘成雅各布·希夫的代理人，称其行动是"犹太人与非犹太人之间旷日持久的斗争"的一部分。1912 年 12 月，梅伦与摩根发表了一份措辞尖锐的新闻稿，指责布兰代斯试图破坏人们对纽黑文铁路公司的信任。但布兰代斯逐渐赢得支持，而梅伦则因反垄断指控被联邦大陪审团起诉。他放弃豁免权，显然是希望避免在普乔调查期间传唤皮尔庞特带来的压力。普乔调查报告进一步强化了布兰代斯对摩根和纽黑文铁路公司的指控。这便是皮尔庞特去世时的局势。

That Pierpont’s sins would be visited on Jack became evident on June 12, 1913, when a New Haven collision at the Stamford station killed seven passengers. Wilson’s new attorney general, James C. McReynolds, already had civil and criminal suits against the New Haven in the works, and the climate was ripe for trustbusters to intensify their campaign. On July 9, the Interstate Commerce Commission published a report criticizing the New Haven’s financial management and recommending that the New Haven be stripped of its trolley and steamship holdings. Here came a critical watershed in Morgan history. As a banker of the Baronial Age, Pierpont would have stood obstinately by Mellen, spewing rage. But Jack had replaced his father on the railroad board. Heeding the ICC warning, he ousted Mellen and overrode the rest of the board to do so. It wasn’t that Jack had any ideological sympathy with government regulation; he was as rabid on the subject as his father. But as a tactical matter, he was more conciliatory—more a banker of the Diplomatic Age. The New Haven board brought in Howard Elliott of the Northern Pacific to replace Mellen.
1913 年 6 月 12 日，纽黑文铁路在斯坦福德车站发生撞车事故，造成七名乘客死亡。皮尔庞特的罪孽降临到杰克身上，这一点变得显而易见。威尔逊新任命的司法部长詹姆斯·C·麦克雷诺兹已经着手对纽黑文铁路提起民事和刑事诉讼，反垄断势力加强行动的时机已经成熟。7 月 9 日，州际商务委员会发布报告，批评纽黑文铁路的财务管理，并建议剥离其有轨电车和轮船资产。这成为摩根家族历史上的一个关键分水岭。作为男爵时代的银行家，皮尔庞特本会固执地支持梅伦，怒不可遏。但杰克已经接替父亲在铁路董事会中的职位。他听从了州际商务委员会的警告，罢免了梅伦，并强行压制了董事会的反对意见。这并不是说杰克对政府监管有什么意识形态上的认同；他和父亲一样对此深恶痛绝。 但从策略上看，他更倾向于和解——更像是一位外交时代的银行家。纽黑文铁路的董事会请来了北太平洋铁路公司的霍华德·埃利奥特，接替梅伦的职位。

The New Haven would always be a touchy subject with the Morgans, who considered themselves benefactors of New England. Pierpont had been the proud president of the New England Society. His grandson Harry Morgan later said that Pierpont “was so loyal to the region” that he had “a blind spot when it came to New England and the New Haven’s place in it.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257430) Facing a chorus of criticism, Jack tried to defend his deceased father, claiming that in his last years he had spent half his time abroad and couldn’t possibly be held responsible for the railroad’s excesses. Yet Jack’s cables reveal that Pierpont stayed in touch on New Haven matters. He might have been gallivanting on the Riviera or cruising up the Nile, but he followed the railroad’s affairs. In 1910, Mellen had wanted to extend the New Haven’s territory to the newly completed Pennsylvania Station in Manhattan. Sensing a competitive threat to his other ward, the New York Central, Pierpont threatened to resign if Mellen persisted. All the way from Rome, he bellowed, “You can tell C. S. Mellen with my compliments that if he persists in proposed policy he will, in my opinion, make mistake of his life.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257585) Pierpont was remote in body but not in spirit.
纽黑文铁路对摩根家族来说始终是个敏感话题，他们自视为新英格兰地区的恩人。皮尔庞特曾是新英格兰协会引以为傲的主席。他的孙子哈里·摩根后来提到，皮尔庞特“对这片区域忠诚至极”，以至于“一提到新英格兰和纽黑文铁路的地位，他就完全失去了判断力。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257430) 面对四面八方的批评声，杰克试图为亡父辩护，声称父亲晚年半数时间都在海外度过，不可能为这条铁路的过度扩张负责。然而，杰克的电报记录显示，皮尔庞特始终在关注纽黑文铁路的事务。他或许正在里维埃拉逍遥快活，或泛舟尼罗河之上，但从未放下过这条铁路的运营动态。1910 年，梅伦曾计划将纽黑文铁路的版图延伸至刚落成的曼哈顿宾夕法尼亚车站。皮尔庞特察觉这可能威胁到他另一家产业——纽约中央铁路的竞争地位，便威胁说如果梅伦执意如此，他就辞职。 他从罗马一路大吼道：“你替我转告 C. S. Mellen，如果他坚持他提议的政策，在我看来，他将犯下他一生中最大的错误。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257585) 皮尔庞特虽身在远方，心却在近处。

Even after Howard Elliott’s appointment, horror stories still abounded at the New Haven. In September 1913, another wreck outside New Haven killed twenty-one passengers and trapped forty boys returning from summer camp. An ICC report blamed Morgan and Mellen. Then, in a final humiliation for the bank, the debt-riddled New Haven skipped its dividend in December for the first time in forty years. It was a classic widows-and-orphans stock, and thousands of small investors lost their income before Christmas. Whether from shame, anger, or a desire to avoid blame, both Jack Morgan and George F. Baker missed the meeting at which the historic vote was taken. Attorney General McReynolds still breathed down the necks of the New Haven board, which he thought was dominated by bankers. The Morgan men knew they were outflanked. “Whole situation disgusting,” Harry Davison cabled to Jack, “but must recognize that Brandeis et al have ear of President and Attorney General just now.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257765) Jack told Davison that he would resign from the New Haven board, except that it might be seen as confirming Brandeis’s attacks on him and his father.
即便在霍华德·埃利奥特上任之后，纽黑文铁路公司依然噩耗不断。1913 年 9 月，纽黑文郊外又发生一起撞车事故，导致 21 名乘客丧生，40 名从夏令营归来的男孩被困。州际商务委员会的报告将矛头直指摩根和梅伦。随后，对于这家银行而言，羞辱接踵而至：负债累累的纽黑文铁路公司于 12 月宣布暂停派发股息，这是四十年来头一遭。这只股票本是经典的“孤儿寡妇股”，成千上万的小投资者在圣诞节前失去了收入来源。或许是出于羞愧、愤怒，或是想要避责，杰克·摩根和乔治·F·贝克双双缺席了那次历史性投票的会议。司法部长麦克雷诺兹仍对纽黑文董事会步步紧逼，他认为这个董事会已被银行家们所掌控。摩根的人明白自己已处于劣势。“整个局面令人作呕，”哈里·戴维森电告杰克，“但必须承认，眼下布兰代斯等人深得总统和司法部长的信任。”” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257765) 杰克告诉戴维森，他本想从纽黑文董事会辞职，只是担心这会被解读为认同布兰代斯对他和他父亲的抨击。

During the New Haven controversy, there was an important sideshow that never came to light. During the fall of 1913, Brandeis published his influential series *Other People’s Money*—*and How the Bankers Use It*, in *Harper’s Weekly.* His critique of the Gentleman Banker’s Code argued that bankers on corporate boards introduced nepotism and double-dealing. As a result of these articles, Tom Lamont decided to put into effect his new public relations policy of meeting privately with bank critics. Through Norman Hapgood, editor of *Harper’s Weekly*, he arranged for a private chat with Brandeis in December 1913 at the University Club on Fifth Avenue. A verbatim transcript of the meeting survives.
在纽黑文争议期间，还有一个始终未曝光的插曲。1913 年秋天，布兰代斯在《哈珀周刊》上发表了影响深远的系列文章《别人的钱——银行家如何利用它》。他对“绅士银行家守则”的批评指出，银行家们安插在企业董事会中，助长了任人唯亲和两面交易。受这些文章影响，汤姆·拉蒙特决定实施新的公关策略——私下约见银行的批评者。他通过《哈珀周刊》主编诺曼·哈普古德，于 1913 年 12 月在第五大道的大学俱乐部安排与布兰代斯私下会面。这次谈话的逐字记录至今仍保存完好。

Let us picture the antagonists as they settled into their armchairs. Speaking with a Kentucky drawl, the young Brandeis had a wide face, large jug ears, powerful shoulders, and flaring eyes. Lamont was short and elegant, had a look of keen, watchful amusement, and was very tough beneath the charm. Confident of his persuasive powers, Lamont was as refined with strangers as Jack was awkward. In his meeting with Brandeis, we see him emerging as the principal image maker and ideologist of the House of Morgan.
让我们想象一下两位对手坐在扶手椅上的情景。年轻的布兰代斯说话带着肯塔基口音，宽脸庞、大扇风耳、肩膀健硕，眼神锐利。拉蒙特身材矮小却举止优雅，神情机敏而带着玩味的笑意，魅力之下隐藏着强硬。拉蒙特对自己的说服力十分自信，对待陌生人时彬彬有礼，与杰克·摩根的生硬笨拙形成鲜明对比。在会见布兰代斯时，我们看到他已俨然成为摩根家族的首席形象设计师与思想代言人。

Lamont cast Pierpont’s faith in Charles Mellen as a virtue: “Mr. Morgan had that large nature which led him almost blindly to have faith in a man when once it was established.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257965) He reiterated Morgan dogma that bankers were responsible to investors and had to be on boards to safeguard their interests. Brandeis retorted, “You could be kept precisely as fully in touch and informed off the board as you are on.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258169) Lamont seemed caught off guard. Rather than having bankers negotiate private deals with clients, Brandeis espoused open, competitive bidding for securities offerings. Lamont said this worked fine in good times, when investors readily took new issues, but left companies adrift in bad times, when investors became apprehensive. These arguments would reverberate for forty years.
拉蒙特将皮尔庞特对查尔斯·梅伦的信任描绘成一种美德：“摩根先生生性宽厚，一旦对某人建立起信任，几乎会毫无保留地信赖他。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2257965) 他重申摩根的信条——银行家要对投资者负责，必须进入董事会以维护投资者利益。布兰代斯反驳道：“即便不在董事会，你也能完全掌握信息，与在董事会时别无二致。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258169) 拉蒙特似乎措手不及。布兰代斯主张证券发行应采取公开竞标，而非银行家与客户私下协商交易。拉蒙特称这在市场景气时固然可行——投资者踊跃认购新股——但若市场低迷、投资者心生疑虑，企业便会陷入困境。这些论战在此后四十年间持续回响。

Both Lamont and Brandeis tried to sound friendly, although Brandeis was more dogged, relishing a chance to confront his adversary face-to-face. After a time, it grew clear that both men circled around something unspoken—namely, mythical Morgan power, the belief on Wall Street that if the bank had a single director on a board, he would dictate to all the others. Lamont was exasperated by glancing references to this power and finally confronted it directly:
拉蒙特和布兰代斯一开始都试图表现得友好，不过布兰代斯更加固执，他乐于抓住机会与对手正面交锋。过了一阵，两人明显都在回避某个心照不宣的话题——那就是摩根神话般的权力，华尔街普遍相信，只要这家银行在某家董事会里安插一位董事，他就能左右其他所有人。拉蒙特被这些旁敲侧击的暗示激怒了，终于直接挑明：

> *Lamont:* You are picturing our firm . . . as having this gigantic power over men and matters.
> 拉蒙特：你心目中把我们公司……描绘成对人和事拥有巨大权力的形象。

> *Brandeis:* But it has that power, Mr. Lamont. You may not realize it, but you are feared, and I believe the effect of your position is toward paralysis rather than expansion.
> 布兰代斯：但它确实拥有那种权力，拉蒙特先生。你可能没意识到，但你们让人畏惧，而且我相信，你们的地位带来的效果是抑制而非扩张。

> *Lamont:* You astonish me beyond measure. How in the world did you arrive at the belief that people are afraid of us, or that we have this terrific power?
> 拉蒙特：你让我无比震惊。你到底是怎么得出人们害怕我们，或者我们拥有这种可怕权力的结论的？

> *Brandeis:* From my own experience.[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258295)
> 布兰代斯：从我自己的经验中得出的。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258295)

Brandeis told how he had foreseen the New Haven debacle, had gone to Boston bankers to complain about the railroad’s management, and was told the road was “Mr. Morgan’s particular pet” and that they feared being excluded from future Morgan bond syndicates if they offered any protest. This was probably true: any firm that refused to participate on one Morgan issue might be penalized on others.
布兰代斯讲述了他如何预见纽黑文铁路的崩溃，他曾前往波士顿与银行家们抱怨该铁路公司的管理问题，却被告知这条铁路是“摩根先生的特别宠儿”，而且他们担心一旦提出任何异议，就会被排除在未来的摩根债券承销团之外。这很可能是真的：任何拒绝参与摩根某一期债券发行的公司，都可能在其他项目上受到惩罚。

In the end, Brandeis scored more points in the debate—one senses Lamont was unprepared for the attorney’s fierce intelligence—but neither side budged in his position. Yet the conversation resonated in Lamont’s mind, particularly Brandeis’s charge that Wall Street lacked interest in small businesses. Years later, when advising Woodrow Wilson at Versailles, Lamont asked the president if he could cite a single instance of a deserving company being denied credit on Wall Street; according to Lamont, Wilson could not. The Brandeis encounter started a lifelong effort by Lamont to present a coherent case for Morgan power. He needed to make others believe in the bank’s virtue. Through him, Wall Street’s most reticent bank would acquire a refined voice and an explicit ideology.
最终，布兰代斯在辩论中占了上风——让人感觉拉蒙特对这位律师敏锐的才智毫无准备——但双方都未改变立场。然而，这场对话在拉蒙特心中挥之不去，尤其是布兰代斯指责华尔街对小企业缺乏兴趣的那番话。多年后，在凡尔赛为伍德罗·威尔逊提供建议时，拉蒙特问总统能否举出一个华尔街拒绝为有价值公司提供信贷的例子；据拉蒙特说，威尔逊答不上来。与布兰代斯的这次交锋，开启了拉蒙特终其一生的努力，他要为摩根财团的权力提供一个自圆其说的理由。他需要让别人相信这家银行的德行。通过他，华尔街最沉默寡言的银行将拥有优雅的发言权和明确的意识形态。

In the Diplomatic Age, companies remained tied to their Wall Street bankers, but the strings were already loosening. The Baronial Age was based on the immaturity of industry. Now large companies were accumulating cash reserves and financing expansion from retained earnings. When private bankers were better known than the companies they sponsored, exclusive relations with clients guaranteed their access to scarce capital. But such Morgan offspring as AT&T, U.S. Steel, and International Harvester were now becoming established companies on a national and even a global scale, outgrowing the need for banker protection.
在外交时代，企业仍然依赖着华尔街的银行家们，但束缚已在悄然松动。巨头时代建立在工业尚未成熟的根基之上。如今，大型公司开始积累现金储备，并依靠留存收益来为扩张提供资金。当私人银行家的名气甚至超过他们扶持的企业时，与客户的独家关系确保了他们对稀缺资本的掌控。然而，那些摩根家族扶持的产物——如 AT&T、美国钢铁公司和国际收割机公司——如今已成长为全国乃至全球性的成熟企业，不再需要银行家的庇护。

For Pierpont’s generation of bankers, membership on the boards of client companies was an article of faith. But in January 1914, hoping to placate the Wilson administration, Morgan partners startled Wall Street by resigning as directors from thirty companies, including banks, railroads, and industrial firms. Jack resigned not only from the New Haven, but from the New York Central, the National City Bank, the First National Bank, and the National Bank of Commerce. (By lumping the New Haven with the others, he didn’t give Brandeis the satisfaction of a lone resignation.) He hoped this would stop legislation, supported by Wilson, outlawing bank-company interlocks. The Clayton Antitrust Act of 1914 forbade interlocking boards of competing companies but didn’t stop bankers from sitting on the boards of client companies.
对皮尔庞特那一代银行家来说，在客户公司的董事会任职是信条。但 1914 年 1 月，为安抚威尔逊政府，摩根合伙人突然从包括银行、铁路和工业企业在内的 30 家公司辞职，震惊了华尔街。杰克不仅辞去了纽黑文铁路的董事职务，还退出了纽约中央铁路、国家城市银行、第一国民银行和国民商业银行。（将纽黑文与其他公司并列辞职，他没有给布兰代斯留下单独辞职的满足感。）他希望此举能阻止威尔逊支持的、将银行与企业互兼董事定为非法的立法。1914 年的《克莱顿反托拉斯法》禁止竞争公司间的连锁董事，但并未阻止银行家担任客户公司的董事。

Changes in the government-business balance were now occurring with amazing speed. In 1913, the Sixteenth Amendment was ratified; the following year, income taxes soared, and the Federal Trade Commission was created. Jack accepted the changes bitterly. Like Pierpont, he would store up anger silently until it overwhelmed him. Now he stewed inwardly, indulging in jeremiads that prefigure his remorseless hostility toward the New Deal. He inveighed against “destructive elements” that had supposedly controlled the country since Teddy Roosevelt. He wrote a friend in June 1914: “A greater lot of perfectly incompetent and apparently thoroughly crooked people has never, as far as I know, run, or attempted to run, any first-class country. The Mexicans are far better off, because their various bosses only murder and rape, and our bosses run the country and make life intolerable for a much larger number of people.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258421)
政府与企业之间的平衡正以惊人的速度发生着变化。1913 年，第十六修正案获得批准；次年，所得税率飙升，联邦贸易委员会也应运而生。杰克痛苦地接受了这些变化。和皮尔庞特一样，他把愤怒默默积压在心底，直到最终不堪重负。此刻他内心翻涌，不断发出阵阵哀叹，这种情绪预示了他日后对“新政”毫不留情的敌意。他痛斥那些自西奥多·罗斯福时代以来据称掌控国家的“破坏性分子”。1914 年 6 月，他在给朋友的信中写道：“据我所知，从没有一帮如此无能、显然又如此彻底腐败的家伙管理或试图管理过任何一个一流国家。墨西哥人都比我们强，因为他们的各个头目顶多杀人强奸，而我们的头目管理国家，却让更多人的生活变得无法忍受。”

One final episode in this last flowering of Progressive reform should be noted. On December 23, 1913, President Wilson signed the Federal Reserve Act. Wilson, of course, had insisted on a Federal Reserve Board in Washington under political, not banker, control. “There are only two choices,” he said. “Either to give the central control to the bankers or to give it to the government.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258619) Earlier in the year, Jack had gone down to Washington with the Morgan plan for a central bank under private control. J. P. Morgan and Company had not only formulated a scheme but had had it beautifully printed up. When Wilson’s close adviser, Colonel House, saw what Jack had brought, he hastily told him to present it to Wilson typed on ordinary paper, lest Bryan and the Progressives think the House of Morgan was dropping off a prearranged plan.
在进步主义改革的最后一次浪潮中，有一件值得注意的收尾之事。1913 年 12 月 23 日，威尔逊总统签署了《联邦储备法案》。威尔逊自然坚持联邦储备委员会设在华盛顿，归属政治而非银行家控制。“只有两个选择，”他说，“要么把中央控制权交给银行家，要么交给政府。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258619) 同年早些时候，杰克带着由私人控制的中央银行摩根计划前往华盛顿。J.P.摩根公司不仅草拟了方案，还精美地印制了它。当威尔逊的亲密顾问豪斯上校看到杰克带来的东西时，他赶紧让他用普通纸张打印后呈给威尔逊，以免布赖恩和进步派人士认为摩根家族抛出了一份预先安排好的计划。

The Federal Reserve System that went into operation in November 1914, was, in many ways, a Morgan godsend. It took some political heat off the bank. As Fed historian William Greider has written, “As an economic institution, the Fed inherited the noblesse-oblige role that the House of Morgan could no longer perform—and also some of the resentment.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258795) The diminution of Morgan power was less than met the eye. In many ways, the Washington board, which oversaw the twelve regional banks, was toothless. The New York Fed, in contrast, emerged as the focal point for dealing with European central banks and the foreign exchange markets. So, real financial power remained where it had always resided—on Wall Street.
1914 年 11 月开始运作的美联储，从许多方面来看，简直是摩根银行的及时雨。它为这家银行分担了不少政治压力。正如美联储历史学家威廉·格雷德所写：“作为经济机构，美联储继承了摩根家族再也无力承担的高贵角色——同时也承接了某些怨恨。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2258795) 摩根权力的削弱并没有表面上看起来那么明显。在许多方面，监管着十二家地区银行的华盛顿董事会其实形同虚设。相反，纽约联储成为与欧洲央行及外汇市场打交道的核心枢纽。因此，真正的金融权力始终留在它一贯所在的地方——华尔街。

The critical position in the new system was the governor of the New York Federal Reserve Bank. Its first occupant, Benjamin Strong, had Morgan written all over his resume. He was a protege of Harry Davison, who had made him a secretary of Bankers Trust and brought him in as Pierpont’s personal auditor during the 1907 panic. There was an emotional bond between the two men. When Strong’s wife committed suicide after childbirth and a daughter died a year later, the Davisons took the three surviving Strong children into their home. Strong then married Katherine Converse, daughter of Bankers Trust’s president, and had become president himself by 1914.
新体系中的关键职位是纽约联邦储备银行行长。首任行长本杰明·斯特朗的履历上处处可见摩根的影子。他是哈里·戴维森的门徒——戴维森曾任命他为银行家信托公司的秘书，并在 1907 年恐慌期间将他引入，担任皮尔庞特的私人审计师。两人之间有着深厚的情感纽带。斯特朗的妻子因产后自杀身亡，一年后一个女儿也夭折了，戴维森一家便将斯特朗幸存的三名子女接到家中抚养。此后，斯特朗与银行家信托公司总裁的女儿凯瑟琳·康弗斯结婚，到 1914 年时，他自己也成为了该公司的总裁。

That year, when the New York Fed job became available, Strong balked at taking it. Not only had he supported the bankers’ Aldrich plan, but he had even campaigned against the Federal Reserve Act. Only after spending a long country weekend with Harry Davison and Paul Warburg did he take the job. Strong wanted to endow the New York Fed with the dignity and prestige of the Bank of England. The House of Morgan directed him to Teddy Grenfell for tutorials on how that bank operated. Through Strong’s influence, the Federal Reserve System would prove far more of a boon than a threat to Morgans. The New York Fed and the bank would share a sense of purpose such that the House of Morgan would be known on Wall Street as the Fed bank. So, contrary to expectations, frustrated reformers only watched Morgan power grow after 1913.
那年，当纽约联储的职位空缺时，斯特朗却犹豫不决。他不仅支持银行家的奥尔德里奇计划，甚至还曾公开反对《联邦储备法案》。直到与哈里·戴维森和保罗·沃伯格共度了一个漫长的乡村周末后，他才接受了这一职务。斯特朗希望赋予纽约联储与英格兰银行同等的尊严与威望。摩根大通指示他向泰迪·格伦菲尔请教，了解那家银行的运作方式。通过斯特朗的影响力，联邦储备体系对摩根而言，与其说是威胁，不如说是福音。纽约联储与摩根大通目标一致，以至于华尔街干脆将摩根大通称为“联储银行”。因此，出乎众人意料的是，1913 年之后，失望的改革者们眼睁睁看着摩根的权势反而与日俱增。
