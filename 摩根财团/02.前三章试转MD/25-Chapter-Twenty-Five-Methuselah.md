# Chapter Twenty-Five: Methuselah

AFTER Tom Lamont’s death, Russell Leffingwell served as chairman of J. P. Morgan from 1948 to 1950. Sucking on a long, straight pipe, he had an air of Methuselah-like wisdom, with his large pointed nose and white hair. As chairman of the Council on Foreign Relations from 1946 to 1953, he would stop by its offices on his way home to East Sixty-Ninth Street. Bookish and witty, a masterly rhetorician, he could write a trenchant essay or speak extemporaneously on any subject. His mind was promiscuously rich. Once, after delivering a fiery opinion at a board meeting, he asked, “Does anyone disagree?” Tom Lamont replied softly, “Would anyone dare, Russell?” He had a gift for comebacks. When his daughter went on her first cruise, she asked how many people she could tip. “Well,” he said dryly, “if you have enough money, you can go right up to the captain.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382529) The writer Edna Ferber left this impression of Leffingwell at a dinner party: “He seemed to me to be wise, tolerant, sound, liberal; and, combined with these qualities, he has, astoundingly enough, humor.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382687) She couldn’t imagine a Scrooge behind his “florid rather Puckish face.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382853)
汤姆·拉蒙特去世后，拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔于 1948 年至 1950 年间担任 J. P. 摩根董事长。他总叼着一根笔直的长烟斗，尖尖的大鼻子配上一头白发，浑身透着一股玛土撒拉般的智慧。1946 年至 1953 年，他还兼任外交关系委员会主席，每天回家路过东六十九街时，总会顺道去委员会办公室坐坐。他书卷气十足，谈吐风趣，修辞造诣极高，无论写犀利的文章还是即兴演讲任何话题，都得心应手。他的头脑博杂而丰饶。有一次，他在董事会上慷慨激昂地发表完意见后，问道：“有人不同意吗？”汤姆·拉蒙特轻声回应：“莱芬韦尔，谁敢呢？”他还有着过人的回嘴天赋。女儿第一次出海巡游时，问他能打赏多少人。他干巴巴地说：“嗯，只要钱够多，你连船长都能打赏。”” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382529) 作家埃德娜·费伯在一次晚宴上对莱芬韦尔留下了这样的印象：“在我看来，他睿智、宽容、稳健、开明；而且，令人惊讶的是，在这些品质之外，他还富有幽默感。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382687) 她无法想象，在他那张“红润而略带调皮的面孔”背后，竟会藏着一个吝啬鬼。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382853)

Leffingwell was the last of the handpicked thinkers that Morgans bred prolifically between the wars, when Wall Street still produced Renaissance men. As members of small partnerships, the elite financiers straddled all aspects of business. They had time to read, to ponder, to enter politics: the gray era of specialization hadn’t dawned. Leffingwell thought that Glass-Steagall, by segmenting banking, had destroyed the most interesting jobs on the Street.
莱芬韦尔是摩根家族在两次世界大战期间大量培养的精选思想家中最后一位，那时华尔街仍能孕育出文艺复兴式的人物。作为小型合伙企业的成员，这些精英金融家涉足商业的方方面面。他们有时间阅读、思考、从政：专业化的灰色时代尚未到来。莱芬韦尔认为，《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》通过分割银行业，摧毁了华尔街最有趣的工作。

After World War II, the Morgan bank was upstaged by a new set of multilateral institutions. Between the wars, the mysterious *troika* of the Bank of England, the New York Fed, and Morgans had largely governed the international monetary order. At Bretton Woods, New Hampshire, in 1944, they were superseded by a proposed World Bank and International Monetary Fund. These twin bodies would try to lift currency stabilization and European reconstruction to a supranational plane. In the postwar era, there would also be greater collaboration among central banks and finance ministries of the major industrial countries. The upshot was that financial tasks entrusted to private bankers in the 1920s were placed irretrievably in public hands. Bankers were distanced from politicians by a new sense of public propriety, with secret collaboration regarded as corrupting by government. The Diplomatic Age was dead.
二战之后，摩根银行被一套全新的多边机构抢了风头。战间期，英格兰银行、纽约联储和摩根组成的“神秘三驾马车”基本掌控着国际货币秩序。1944 年，在新罕布什尔州的布雷顿森林，它们被拟议成立的世界银行和国际货币基金组织所取代。这两个孪生机构试图将货币稳定与欧洲重建推升至超国家层面。战后时代，主要工业国家的央行与财政部之间也展开了更紧密的合作。其结果是，1920 年代托付给私人银行家的金融职责，不可逆转地转移到了公共部门手中。新的公共道德观念让银行家与政客之间拉开了距离，私下合作被视为腐败行为，为政府所不容。外交时代就此终结。

In the new Casino Age, as we shall call it, banks would operate in a broader competitive sphere. The banker had grown powerful when capital markets were limited, with few financial intermediaries to tap them. In the post-World War II period, however, capital markets would burgeon and become globally integrated. At the same time, the financial field would grow crowded with commercial banks, investment banks, insurance companies, brokerage houses, foreign banks, government lending programs, multilateral organizations, and myriad other lenders. Gradually Wall Street bankers would lose their unique place in world finance. Never again would a private bank such as J. P. Morgan be the most powerful financial agency on earth. Far from standing guard over scarce resources, bankers would evolve into glad-handing salesmen, almost pushing the bountiful stuff on customers.
在这个我们称之为“赌场时代”的新时期，银行将在更广阔的竞争舞台上运作。当资本市场有限、可供利用的金融中介寥寥无几时，银行家曾权势熏天。然而，二战后资本市场蓬勃发展并走向全球一体化。与此同时，金融领域变得拥挤不堪：商业银行、投资银行、保险公司、经纪公司、外资银行、政府贷款项目、多边组织以及形形色色的放贷机构纷纷涌入。华尔街银行家们逐渐丧失了在世界金融中的独特地位。像 J. P. Morgan 这样的私人银行，再也不可能成为地球上最有权势的金融机构。银行家们不再守着稀缺资源，反而蜕变成笑脸迎人的推销员，几乎是把丰沛的资金硬塞给客户。

The new Bretton Woods bodies were shaped by the interwar lending disaster. The memories of the 1920s were fresh, with over a third of foreign government securities still in default. The World Bank’s decision to finance only meticulously conceived projects was a reaction to this loose sovereign lending. Even so scrupulous a lender as Morgans smarted from a flood of outstanding defaulted bonds—\$197 million in Japanese debt, \$20 million from Austria, \$151 million from Germany. No banker was foolish enough to assert that countries never went bust or that government loans were less risky than commerical loans. Since the World Bank had to tap U.S. capital markets—only the United States had spare cash—it needed to please Wall Street and erase the stigma of foreign lending.
新的布雷顿森林体系机构是在两次世界大战期间贷款灾难的阴影下形成的。20 世纪 20 年代的记忆仍历历在目，当时超过三分之一的外国政府证券仍处于违约状态。世界银行决定只为精心策划的项目提供融资，正是对这种宽松主权放贷的回应。即便是像 Morgans 这样审慎的贷款机构，也因大量未偿违约债券而焦头烂额——日本债务 1.97 亿美元，奥地利 2000 万美元，德国 1.51 亿美元。没有哪个银行家会愚蠢到断言国家永远不会破产，或者政府贷款的风险低于商业贷款。由于世界银行必须依赖美国资本市场——当时只有美国有闲余资金——它需要取悦华尔街，并洗刷外国放贷的污名。

The World Bank’s second president, John J. McCloy, had to safeguard the new institution’s credit and consulted Leffingwell about Morgan’s interwar experience. With his usual style of passionate urgency, Leffingwell told McCloy about the bank’s sense of betrayal over foreign loans that had enjoyed phantom government guarantees—most notably the German loans. McCloy concurred in Leffingwell’s critique of 1920s lending—that politics had gotten confused with finance, encouraging debtors to regard loans as disguised foreign aid. This destroyed discipline and invited excessive borrowing, followed by default.
世界银行第二任行长约翰·J·麦克洛伊（John J. McCloy）不得不维护这家新机构的信誉，于是向莱芬韦尔（Leffingwell）咨询摩根银行在两次世界大战间的经验。莱芬韦尔以他一贯的急迫热情，向麦克洛伊讲述了银行对那些享有虚幻政府担保的外国贷款——尤其是德国贷款——所感到的背叛。麦克洛伊赞同莱芬韦尔对 1920 年代贷款的批评：政治与金融纠缠不清，导致债务人将贷款视为变相的外国援助。这破坏了纪律，诱发了过度借贷，随后便是违约。

Considering the defaults on Latin American loans, McCloy asked whether the bank should lend to the region. Leffingwell shot back, “Except for the Argentine, I do not think of any Central or South American country that hasn’t got a contemptible, discreditable record of default to American investors.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382978) (Argentina was always a special case: when Juan Perón came to power in 1946, the country boasted a large supply of gold amassed from food exports to wartime Europe; Peron even favored paying off foreign debt to avert bondage to foreign bankers.) If the World Bank made Latin American loans, Leffingwell warned McCloy, it might tarnish the World Bank’s own bonds with American investors. McCloy had greater sympathy for the Latin Americans than Leffingwell did, arguing that bankers had tempted the region into over-borrowing. “The competition that went on in Europe and in Latin America for loans was something to see,” he told Leffingwell. “I know because I was a part of it.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383183) Although the World Bank made Latin American loans, it insisted that Peru and other nations first settle outstanding debt with private bondholders. This shored up creditors and prevented Latin American debt from contaminating the bank’s own credit.
考虑到拉丁美洲贷款违约的情况，麦克洛伊询问银行是否还应向该地区放贷。莱芬韦尔反驳道：“除了阿根廷，我想不出任何中美洲或南美洲国家没有对美国投资者留下可鄙、可耻的违约记录。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2382978) （阿根廷始终是个特例：1946 年胡安·庇隆上台时，该国因向战时欧洲出口粮食而积累了巨额黄金储备；庇隆甚至主张偿还外债，以免受外国银行家的束缚。）莱芬韦尔警告麦克洛伊，如果世界银行向拉丁美洲放贷，可能会损害其在美国投资者心中的债券信誉。麦克洛伊比莱芬韦尔更同情拉美国家，他认为银行家们引诱该地区过度借贷。“欧洲和拉丁美洲争夺贷款的那番盛况真是难得一见，”他对莱芬韦尔说，“我知道，因为我就是其中一员。”“尽管世界银行向拉丁美洲提供了贷款，但它坚持要求秘鲁等国首先清偿与私人债券持有人的未偿债务。这巩固了债权人的地位，并防止拉丁美洲的债务影响世界银行自身的信用。”

Leffingwell thought private lending to Europe couldn’t resume until political turmoil in the area ended. In 1946, Churchill sounded the alarm with his “Iron Curtain” speech in Fulton, Missouri. His fears of European disintegration were spookily analogous to those following World War I, especially with food shortages and poor crops in early 1947. As Under Secretary of State Robert Lovett had warned Lamont, “At no time in my recollection have I seen a world situation which was so rapidly moving toward real trouble.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383364) Leffingwell feared a stingy, punitive approach to rebuilding Europe, reminiscent of Versailles. He, in turn, warned Lovett, his friend and Locust Valley neighbor: “Western Europe is drifting toward catastrophe. Penny-wise and pound-foolish, we dribble out little loans and grants, too little and too late, meeting a crisis here or there . . . while we neglect to deal constructively on a great scale with the problem of the reconstruction of western Europe”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383531) He stressed aid to Britain and France, without strings: “The British and French are not infants nor aborigines to be dictated to by the nouveaux riches Americans.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383738)
莱芬韦尔认为，在欧洲的政治动荡结束之前，无法恢复对欧洲的私人贷款。1946 年，丘吉尔在密苏里州富尔顿发表"铁幕"演说，敲响了警钟。他对欧洲解体的担忧与第一次世界大战后的情形惊人地相似，尤其是 1947 年初的粮食短缺和作物歉收。正如副国务卿罗伯特·洛维特曾警告拉蒙特的那样："在我记忆中，从未见过世界局势如此迅速地滑向真正的麻烦。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383364) 莱芬韦尔担心，重建欧洲时采取吝啬惩罚性的做法，会重蹈凡尔赛的覆辙。他反过来警告他的朋友、洛斯特谷的邻居洛维特："西欧正走向灾难。我们因小失大，零敲碎打地发放小额贷款和赠款，太少太迟，只应付了一处又一处的危机……" 当我们未能以建设性的方式大规模处理西欧重建问题时，” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383531) 他着重强调了对英国和法国的援助，且不附带任何条件：“英国人和法国人既不是婴儿，也不是土著，无需让这些暴发户的美国佬对他们发号施令。”” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383738)

With U.S. investors still skittish about foreign bonds, the World Bank couldn’t cope with the Western European crisis. In December 1947, Truman presented Congress with plans for the multibillion-dollar Marshall Plan to raise Europe from wartime rubble behind a NATO defense shield. “What took place after World War I was the forerunner of the Marshall Plan,” noted McCloy, who had worked on the Dawes loan in the 1920s. “But back then the rehabilitation of Europe was done in a private capacity.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383956) The scope of the Marshall Plan—\$5 billion for the first year alone—far exceeded Wall Street’s meager resources, still depleted by the Depression, war, and Glass-Steagall.
由于美国投资者对外国债券仍心存芥蒂，世界银行无力应对西欧危机。1947 年 12 月，杜鲁门向国会提交了耗资数十亿美元的马歇尔计划方案，旨在借助北约防御盾牌，将欧洲从战后废墟中重新崛起。“一战后发生的事情便是马歇尔计划的先兆，”曾在 20 世纪 20 年代参与道斯贷款的麦克洛伊指出，“但那时欧洲的重建是以私人身份进行的。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2383956) 马歇尔计划的规模——仅第一年就高达 50 亿美元——远远超出了华尔街本已捉襟见肘的资源，这些资源仍因大萧条、战争以及《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》而元气大伤。

The internationalism that had always ostracized the House of Morgan in the hinterlands was now irrevocably established in Washington. The war, television, and foreign travel all acted to reduce American parochialism. As the Republicans shed their traditional isolationism, the bank had a party in which it could place implicit faith. No longer would Morgans rise as an alien institution, conspiratorially aligned with foreign powers. If this increased the bank’s political comfort, it also reduced its influence. Foreign governments with better entree in Washington had less need of a Wall Street agent to conduct their diplomacy.
那个长期以来在偏远地区排斥摩根财团的国际主义思潮，如今已不可逆转地扎根于华盛顿。战争、电视和出国旅行都削弱了美国的地方狭隘性。随着共和党人抛弃传统的孤立主义，这家银行终于迎来了一个它足以托付信任的政党。摩根财团不再被视为与外国势力勾结的异类机构。如果说这增加了该银行在政治上的舒适度，那么也同时削弱了其影响力。那些在华盛顿拥有更好门路的外国政府，不再需要华尔街代理人为它们处理外交事务。

During the early summer of 1947, the Truman administration was in a quandary over whether to include the Soviets in the Marshall Plan. George F. Kennan wanted to invite the Soviets to participate because he assumed they would spurn the offer and get blamed for partitioning Europe. Lovett wasn’t convinced and received permission from Truman to sound out Leffingwell at 23 Wall. According to his son-in-law, after pondering whether to invite the Soviets, Leffingwell told Lovett, “Bob, the answer is very simple. If you don’t ask Soviet Russia, there will be hell to pay. If you do ask them, they’ll tell *you* to go to hell.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384123) Leffingwell managed to convince Lovett where Kennan had failed. As Kennan and Leffingwell predicted, the Soviets later rebuffed the overture.
1947 年初夏，杜鲁门政府是否应邀请苏联加入马歇尔计划一事陷入两难。乔治·F·凯南主张邀请苏联参与，因为他推测对方会拒绝提议，从而让苏联背负分裂欧洲的骂名。洛维特对此并不认同，他获准去华尔街 23 号试探莱芬韦尔的态度。据其女婿回忆，莱芬韦尔在斟酌是否邀请苏联后对洛维特说：“鲍勃，答案很简单。如果不邀请苏联，你们准没好果子吃；如果邀请，他们只会叫你们滚蛋。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384123) 莱芬韦尔成功说服了洛维特，而凯南却未能做到。正如凯南和莱芬韦尔所料，苏联后来果真拒绝了这一橄榄枝。

In the late 1940s, it looked as if Morgan political influence would be limited to such sophisticated advisory roles. As an investment bank before Glass-Steagall, it had floated many government bond issues. As a commercial bank lending its own money, it strained just to eke out postwar loans to England and France. When J. P. Morgan and Company and Chase co-managed two French loans totalling \$225 million in 1950, they nearly exhausted Morgan resources. Leffingwell wanted to aid France despite his rather harsh view of de Gaulle: “There is no place in modern France for the general on horseback. De Gaulle can be and is I think a disturbing influence. . . . He has never shown statesmanship, judgment or common sense. In a way it was the very lack of these qualities which made him a great war leader of the resistance.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384282)
在 20 世纪 40 年代末，摩根的政治影响力似乎仅限于提供这类高水准的顾问服务。在《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》出台前，作为一家投资银行，它曾承销过多批政府债券；而转型为用自有资金放贷的商业银行后，它勉力向英国和法国发放战后贷款已属不易。1950 年，当 J.P.摩根公司与大通银行联合管理两笔总额达 2.25 亿美元的对法贷款时，几乎耗尽了摩根的财力。莱芬韦尔尽管对戴高乐持相当严苛的看法，却仍希望援助法国：“现代法国没有这位骑马将军的位置。戴高乐可能——而且我认为确实——是一种令人不安的影响力……他从未展现过治国才能、判断力或常识。从某种意义上说，正是缺乏这些品质，才使他成为一位伟大的抵抗运动战时领袖。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384282)

The capital-short House of Morgan had to neglect many former foreign clients and was powerless to help devastated Japan. Clinging to a dated view of England and America as coequal partners, Leffingwell couldn’t fathom Britain’s demotion to a second-class power. In 1947, he wrote his friend T. J. Carlyle Gifford of the British Treasury, “However bungling we may think the governments of the West, it is plain that there can be no hope for democracy and a world of free men except that England be restored and aided to take her place again in the world.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384450) To his friend Lady Layton he said, “Nothing matters as much as the British empire and the United States of America and their collaboration.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384585) Britain’s diminished place in world affairs would lessen the value of Morgan ties to the British Treasury and the Bank of England. Unlike the 1920s, after World War II the United States no longer deferred to Britain’s financial leadership. When John Maynard Keynes proposed that the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund be based in London or New York, the United States, in a symbolic act, placed its Bretton Woods wards a short walk from the White House.
资金短缺的摩根银行不得不放弃许多昔日的外国客户，也无力援助满目疮痍的日本。莱芬韦尔仍抱着英美是平等伙伴的过时看法，无法理解英国已沦为二流强国。1947 年，他在给英国财政部友人 T·J·卡莱尔·吉福德写信时写道："无论我们觉得西方各国政府多么无能，但显而易见，除非英国得以复兴并重新立足于世界，否则民主与自由世界绝无希望。"他对友人莱顿夫人则说："没有什么比大英帝国和美利坚合众国的协作更重要。"英国在国际事务中的地位下降，将使摩根与英国财政部及英格兰银行的纽带价值大打折扣。与 20 年代不同，二战后美国不再屈从于英国的金融主导地位。 当约翰·梅纳德·凯恩斯提议将世界银行和国际货币基金组织设在伦敦或纽约时，美国以一种象征性的姿态，将其布雷顿森林体系的附属机构安置在了离白宫仅几步之遥的地方。

For Leffingwell, the touchstone of any policy was how it would affect both America and Britain. Like others at Morgans, he was violently anti-Zionist, imagining that agitation for a Jewish homeland would stir up the Moslem world against the British Empire. J. P. Morgan and Company was still a Wasp, homogeneous bank, drawing a large fraction of its people from Ivy League schools and prominent families. Leffingwell championed minority rights but was impatient with minorities who asserted those rights too aggressively. In 1946, his close friend Morris Ernst, a Jewish lawyer active in civil liberties causes, chided Morgans for having no Jewish directors. Leffingwell fairly breathed fire in defense: “Why not be just citizens and Americans and drop all this talk about the rights of Jews. . . . So long as some Jews regard themselves as a racial and religious minority in other people’s countries, and agitate for their rights, I fear they will be disliked.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384727) After delivering this churlish judgment, Leffingwell ended with a tribute to Ernst’s own brilliance. Ernst, in turn, urged Truman to consult Leffingwell as an adviser, assuring the president he wasn’t a publicity monger like Tom Lamont.[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384862)
对于莱芬韦尔而言，衡量任何政策好坏的试金石，就是看它如何影响美英两国。和摩根公司的其他人一样，他极端反对犹太复国主义，认为鼓吹建立犹太家园会煽动穆斯林世界对抗大英帝国。J.P. 摩根公司当时依然是一家纯白人新教徒的银行，员工大多来自常春藤名校和显赫家族。莱芬韦尔虽然支持少数族裔的权利，却对那些过于激进地争取自己权利的少数族裔感到不耐烦。1946 年，他的密友、积极投身公民自由事业的犹太律师莫里斯·恩斯特批评摩根公司没有犹太裔董事。莱芬韦尔当即怒不可遏地反驳道：“为什么不能只是做普通公民和美国人，别再谈什么犹太人的权利了……只要有些犹太人把自己当成其他国家的种族和宗教少数群体，并不断为自己的权利鼓噪，恐怕就难免会招人反感。”” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384727) 在发表这番粗鲁的评判后，莱芬韦尔以对恩斯特自身才华的赞誉作为结尾。恩斯特随即敦促杜鲁门聘请莱芬韦尔担任顾问，并向总统保证，他并非像汤姆·拉蒙特那样爱出风头的人。 [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2384862)

If there was a bilious quality to Leffingwell’s thoughts in later years, it probably came from too many political disappointments. Known around Wall Street as the resident Morgan liberal, he was less of a dreamer than a hard, practical man. And he loved the cut and thrust of debate. He thought the League of Nations was a sad mistake, a cover for taking territory from Germany and Austria. He once told Lamont, “The truth of the matter is that this is a predatory world in which some if not all nations go out to take what they want sooner or later by force.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385034) In the early 1950s, he believed the Soviets were hellbent on world domination and cited Berlin, the Balkans, Iran, Yugoslavia, and Korea as examples.[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385202) He had little use for disarmament and talked unapologetically about the United States serving as the world’s policeman. He had seen too many dictators.
如果说莱芬韦尔晚年思想中带有尖酸刻薄的气质，那很可能是因为经历了太多政治失意。他在华尔街以摩根派自由主义者著称，与其说是个梦想家，不如说是个冷酷务实的人。他热衷于辩论中的唇枪舌剑。他认为国际联盟是个可悲的错误，不过是掩盖从德国和奥地利夺取领土的遮羞布。他曾对拉蒙特说：“事实是，这是一个弱肉强食的世界，就算不是所有国家，至少有一部分迟早会用武力夺取想要的东西。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385034) 到了 20 世纪 50 年代初，他坚信苏联一心想要统治世界，并列举柏林、巴尔干地区、伊朗、南斯拉夫和朝鲜作为例证。 [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385202) 他对裁军几乎不抱希望，并毫不讳言地谈论美国应当充当世界警察的角色。他见过的独裁者实在太多了。

While deploring McCarthyism, Leffingwell wanted to root out subversives and argued that schools and governments should have a free hand to fire such persons. Later appointed by Truman to an internal security commission headed by Admiral Chester W. Nimitz, he thought civil liberties should yield priority to national security: “I think employee tenure and civil rights generally have to be subordinated to the rights of the nation to defend itself against Russia, which is the enemy of all civil rights and all the freedoms.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385407)
尽管 Leffingwell 对 McCarthyism 持谴责态度，但他仍想清除颠覆分子，并主张学校和政府应有自由处置权解雇这类人。后来，他被 Truman 任命为由 Admiral Chester W. Nimitz 领导的内部安全委员会成员，他认为公民自由应优先服从国家安全：“我认为员工任期和公民权利总体而言，必须让位于国家自卫的权利，以对抗那个敌视一切公民权利和自由的俄国。”

At the start of the Korean War, during the summer of 1950, George Whitney wrote to Truman pledging the bank’s support. While the two had had a testy exchange during the Wheeler railroad hearings years before, Truman now recognized the need for national harmony. The president told Whitney rather shamelessly that his letter had “brought back pleasant memories of our meeting so many years ago.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385575)
1950 年夏，朝鲜战争爆发之初，George Whitney 致信 Truman，承诺银行将给予支持。尽管多年前在 Wheeler 铁路听证会上两人曾有过激烈交锋，但 Truman 此时意识到国家团结的必要性。总统颇有些厚着脸皮地对 Whitney 表示，他的来信“勾起了多年前我们那次会面的美好回忆”。

Despite their support for the Korean War, the Morgan officials grew alarmed in the fall of 1950 when South Korean troops reached the Chinese border and General Douglas MacArthur seemed to yearn for a showdown with the Chinese Communists. This brought out an old Morgan bias against China as well as a fear that the United States would save Asia at the expense of Western Europe. Leffingwell warned Truman that the country shouldn’t go to war “with those miserable 400 million Chinamen. They have been the victims of their own war lords, and of their own misgovernment, and of their Japanese conquerors, and now of their Communist conquerors. We have no mission to kill Chinamen; and to get involved with them will leave us defenseless at home and in Europe.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385743) Truman agreed. In April 1951, he relieved MacArthur of his duties after the latter urged the United States to emphasize Asia instead of Europe and take the war to the Chinese mainland.
尽管摩根高层支持朝鲜战争，但 1950 年秋，当韩国军队逼近中国边境，而道格拉斯·麦克阿瑟将军似乎渴望与中国共产党一决高下时，他们开始感到警觉。这暴露了摩根家族长期以来对中国的偏见，也反映出他们担心美国会以牺牲西欧为代价来拯救亚洲。莱芬韦尔警告杜鲁门，美国不应与“那四亿可怜的中国佬开战。他们长期以来一直是本国军阀、腐败政府、日本征服者以及如今共产党征服者的受害者。我们没有使命去杀戮中国人；卷入其中只会让我们在本土和欧洲毫无防备。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385743) 杜鲁门表示同意。1951 年 4 月，在麦克阿瑟敦促美国将战略重心转向亚洲而非欧洲、并将战火引至中国大陆后，杜鲁门解除了他的职务。

The House of Morgan shared Truman’s cold war liberalism yet differed with him on economics, where the president reverted to his earlier cynicism about Wall Street. This became clear when Leffingwell met with Truman at the White House in early 1951 to make a plea for market-based interest rates. Since early in the Second World War, the Federal Reserve had pegged long-term rates at 2.5 percent, a policy prolonged after the war with Truman’s blessing. In the early 1920s, Truman had been dumbfounded when his government bond dropped in price after Ben Strong raised interest rates. He didn’t see this as bad luck but as a sinister betrayal of the bondholder, and it made him disposed to fix interest rates. The Fed was now spending billions of dollars to keep prices high and yields low on Treasury bonds. Along with Allen Sproul of the New York Fed, Leffingwell thought this a waste of money and wanted to return to free market interest rates.
摩根家族与杜鲁门在冷战自由主义上立场一致，但在经济问题上却存在分歧，总统在此议题上重拾早年对华尔街的冷嘲热讽。1951 年初，莱芬韦尔前往白宫与杜鲁门会面，恳求推行市场化利率，这一分歧由此昭然若揭。自二战初期起，美联储便将长期利率锁定在 2.5%，战后在杜鲁门的支持下这一政策得以延续。20 世纪 20 年代初，当本·斯特朗加息导致自己持有的政府债券价格下跌时，杜鲁门曾惊愕不已。他并未将此视为运气不佳，而是看作对债券持有人的恶意背叛，这使他倾向于固定利率。当时美联储正投入数十亿美元，以维持国债价格高企、收益率低下的局面。莱芬韦尔与纽约联储的艾伦·斯普劳尔均认为此举纯属浪费钱财，主张回归自由市场利率。

Treasury Secretary John Snyder spied in Sproul and Wall Street a cabal intent on returning to the good old days, when the New York Fed and Morgans dictated monetary policy. Truman was eager to stifle inflation jitters during the Korean War and was irritated by what he saw as the bankers’ patent selfishness. He gave Leffingwell a tongue-lashing reminiscent of the earlier New Deal diatribes:
财政部长约翰·斯奈德（John Snyder）在斯普劳尔（Sproul）和华尔街（Wall Street）发现了一个阴谋集团，意图重回往日的好时光——那时由纽约联储（New York Fed）和摩根家族（Morgans）主导货币政策。杜鲁门急于在朝鲜战争期间抑制通胀恐慌，他反感银行家们赤裸裸的自私自利，对其大加斥责，措辞令人想起当年新政时期的激烈抨击：

> I appreciate your interest in this matter but it seems to me that an emergency is a very poor time for bankers to try to upset the financial apple cart of the nation. The stability and confidence of the nation are entirely wrapped up in the two hundred and fifty-seven billion dollar debt that is now outstanding. . . . For my [part I](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_007.html_filepos40688) can’t understand why the bankers would want to upset the credit of the nation in the midst of a terrible emergency. It seems to be what they want to do and if I can prevent it they are not going to do it.[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385955)
> 我感谢你对此事的关注，但在我看来，紧急时期绝不是银行家们试图搅乱国家金融秩序的好时机。国家的稳定与信心，完全系于那笔高达两千五百七十亿美元的未偿债务之上……就我而言，我实在无法理解，为什么银行家们要在如此危急关头，去破坏国家的信用。这似乎正是他们想做的——而只要我能阻止，他们就休想得逞。 [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2385955)

There was something strained in Truman’s cordiality toward the House of Morgan, and at moments his real but usually carefully guarded feelings would flare up again.
杜鲁门对摩根财团的热情友好中带着一丝勉强，他那些真实但通常被小心隐藏的情感，有时又会突然爆发出来。

WHEN George Whitney became Morgan chairman in 1950—leaving Russell Leffingwell to hover as a wise man during the decade, firing off position papers—J. P. Morgan and Company was a hothouse bank surpassed in size by ten other New York banks alone. It was compactly squeezed into 23 Wall, with Whitney seated at a rolltop desk at the end of the glass-enclosed room along Broad Street, his white hair well-brushed, his elegance unbending, and his tailoring faultless. As publicity man fames Brugger recalled, he was “patrician, reserved, terse in speech and blunt in comment, \[his\] countenance cool but capable of crinkling with a mischievous grin.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386130) The elegance was sometimes belied by a booming voice and a gruff manner.
1950 年，乔治·惠特尼接任摩根公司董事长——让拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔在此后十年间以智者身份垂帘听政，不断抛出政策备忘录——此时的 J.P.摩根公司已是一家温室般的银行，仅纽约一城就有十家银行规模超过它。公司局促地挤在华尔街 23 号，惠特尼坐在布罗德街一侧玻璃隔间尽头的卷盖式办公桌前，白发梳得一丝不苟，举止优雅而不失强硬，衣着无可挑剔。正如公关人詹姆斯·布鲁格所说，他“贵族气十足，矜持寡言，措辞简洁，评论直率，面容冷峻，却也能漾起促狭的笑容。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386130) 那优雅有时会被洪亮的嗓音和粗粝的做派所打破。

Whitney was always haunted by his brother’s embezzlement scandal and vowed to pay back every penny, even though doing so markedly thinned his own fortune and that of his heirs. “It was emotionally debilitating to him,” said his grandson George Whitney Rowe. “The reputational disaster was even harder than the money. It cost him a tremendous amount of money near the end of his earning power, but he made every penny good.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386305) He was forced to sign away easy, pretax money of the 1920s. Worried about his grandchildren, he asked John M. Meyer, Jr., a later chairman, to watch out for their interests. In the Morgan tradition of nepotism, several Whitney heirs ended up working at the bank. The Whitneys tried not to treat Richard like a pariah, but the subject was so touchy and explosive that family members came to blows over it. Barred from finance, Richard performed odd jobs—he imported Florida oranges at one point—but was mostly supported by his heiress wife, Gertrude.
惠特尼一直因他哥哥的贪污丑闻而耿耿于怀，并发誓要还清每一分钱，尽管这样做明显耗损了他自己和继承人的财富。他的孙子乔治·惠特尼·罗说：“这让他心力交瘁。名誉上的打击比金钱上的损失更难承受。在他赚钱能力的末期，这让他付出了巨额代价，但他还是分文不差地还清了。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386305) 他被迫放弃了 20 世纪 20 年代那些轻松且免税的收入。出于对孙辈的担忧，他请后来的董事长小约翰·M·迈耶多关照他们的利益。遵循摩根家族的裙带关系传统，几位惠特尼的继承人最终都在这家银行工作。惠特尼家族尽量不把理查德当作异类对待，但这个话题过于敏感和火爆，以至于家庭成员为此大打出手。被金融业拒之门外的理查德只好打些零工——他曾一度进口佛罗里达的橙子——但主要还是靠他那位女继承人妻子格特鲁德养活。

Perhaps as a result of his brother’s crimes, George Whitney made a fetish of honesty. In 1955, J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Stanley teamed up on a General Motors “rights issue”—new shares offered at a discount to existing shareholders. The company wanted to raise \$325 million for retooling in order to produce cars with power steering, power brakes, and V-8 engines. Morgan Stanley handled the financial end and J. P. Morgan the clerical end—the typical arrangement of the day. In a massive team effort, Whitney pitched in to deal with crowds. A *New Yorker* reporter recorded a telling vignette of J. P. Morgan’s Boston Brahmin as recounted by a broker:
或许是因为他兄弟的罪行，乔治·惠特尼对诚实格外执着。1955 年，J.P.摩根公司与摩根士丹利联手为通用汽车做了一次“配股”——即以折扣价向现有股东增发新股。通用汽车当时希望筹集 3.25 亿美元用于设备改造，以生产配备动力转向、动力刹车和 V-8 发动机的汽车。摩根士丹利负责财务端，J.P.摩根负责文书端——这是当时典型的合作模式。在一场大规模团队协作中，惠特尼也投身于处理人群事务。一位《纽约客》记者记录了一段由一名经纪人讲述的、关于 J.P.摩根那位波士顿贵族的生动轶事：

> While \[Whitney\] was on duty, a lady stockholder came in to exercise her rights for two shares and handed him a stack of bills that supposedly totalled a hundred and fifty dollars. Whitney, it seems, was too polite to count them in her presence, so he just took them, smiled, shook her hand, and gave her a receipt. Well, after she had left, he counted the money and was flabbergasted to find that it came to a hundred and *seventy* dollars. Everybody was in a terrible tizzy until it was discovered that the papers hadn’t yet disappeared into the files, so they knew who the stockholder was and could send the overpayment back to her, along with her stock.[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386464)
> \[惠特尼\]值班时，一位女股东前来行使她持有两股股票的权利，并递给他一叠钞票，据称总额为一百五十美元。惠特尼似乎太客气了，不好意思当着她的面数钱，于是只是接过钱，微笑着与她握手，并给了她一张收据。等她离开后，他一数钱，惊愕地发现竟然有一百七十美元。大家顿时慌乱成一团，直到发现那些文件还没被归档，这才知道了那位股东的身份，于是把多付的钱连同股票一起退还给了她。

The 1950s would expose the extreme damage done to the House of Morgan by Glass-Steagall in a way not clear during the Depression, when nobody needed loans anyway. As an investment bank, J. P. Morgan and Company had towered over its rivals, while as a commercial bank it couldn’t match the more plebeian banks that courted retail deposits. Nationally it fluctuated in size somewhere between twentieth and thirtieth. It was hard to believe that this small, genteel, somewhat stuffy bank had been the glowering red-eyed dragon of American finance.
20 世纪 50 年代，格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案对摩根家族的损害才彻底显现——大萧条时期无人需要贷款，所以问题并不明显。作为投资银行，J.P. 摩根公司曾傲视群雄；但作为商业银行，它却难以匹敌那些争抢零售存款的平民化银行。在全国范围内，其规模排名在二十至三十之间摇摆。很难相信，这家规模不大、温文尔雅、略显古板的银行，曾经是美国金融界那只怒目圆睁、血盆大口的红龙。

Even in its reduced state, the House of Morgan still fancied itself Wall Street nobility. Employing seven hundred people, it retained the gentlemanly mood of an old Wall Street partnership. It was so tiny that to celebrate staff members who were returning from the service in 1947, the entire staff was able to fit into a dinner dance at the Waldorf-Astoria. George Whitney did the hiring himself, mostly bringing in men with prep-school and Ivy League backgrounds; everybody started in the mail room and rotated upward. Once a year, Whitney would stroll over to Davis, Polk, ask for the year’s legal bill, go back to his desk, and write out a check. Morgan style was simple, British and informal. The bank of the fifties would have still been recognizable to partners of the twenties. At 10:30 A.M., the top twenty officers met around a large table to comment on world affairs and swap news, an exchange that continued over free lunches.
即便是规模缩小后的摩根银行，依然自视为华尔街的贵族。这家拥有七百名员工的机构，保留着老派华尔街合伙企业的绅士风度。它规模之小，以至于 1947 年欢迎归国员工的庆祝晚宴，全体员工都能挤在华尔道夫酒店的一场舞会上。乔治·惠特尼亲自负责招聘，主要招募预科学校和常春藤联盟背景的人；所有人从收发室干起，随后逐级晋升。每年，惠特尼会踱步到 Davis Polk 律师事务所，索要当年的法律账单，然后回到办公桌旁，直接签一张支票。摩根的风格简单、英式且不拘小节。即便到了五十年代，二十年代的合伙人依然能辨认出这家银行的风貌。上午十点半，二十位高管围坐在一张大桌前，评论天下大事，交换小道消息，这种交流一直持续到免费午餐结束。

The paternalistic Morgans pampered its people. Employees lived in a warm cocoon and received better pay and longer vacations than anyone else on Wall Street. The bank had a plantation atmosphere. Its dining room was staffed by white-gloved black waiters who ladled out soup from beautiful metal tureens. One newcomer nearly protested when a waiter seemed to drop dirty ice cubes into his iced tea. Then he realized that the cubes were *made* from tea, so that they wouldn’t dilute the drink. It was that kind of place.
家长式的摩根银行对员工关怀备至。员工们生活在温暖的茧房里，享受着比华尔街任何机构都优厚的薪酬和更长的假期。这家银行弥漫着庄园般的气息。餐厅里服务的都是戴着白手套的黑人侍者，他们从精美的金属汤瓮中为顾客盛汤。有位新员工差点因为侍者往冰茶里放入看似脏兮兮的冰块而抗议——直到他意识到这些冰块是用茶冻成的，为的就是不会稀释饮料的浓度。这就是摩根银行的做派。

The bank lovingly tended its image, the glamorous *23* on its door. Its phone number was Hanover 5-2323, the license plate on its black company Cadillac G-2323. As banker to old money and high society, it obeyed strict etiquette. When calling, young bankers wore hats, and in the office risked irreparable career damage if they removed their jackets *en route* to the men’s room. In this prudish place, the ladies’ room of the Trust Department was left unmarked because red-faced bankers couldn’t agree on the sign’s proper wording. The preferred style was low profile. Clients were never mentioned to outsiders, annual reports contained no pictures, and advertising was strictly forbidden. When one new arrival asked the publicist his job, he was told, “I’m the guy paid to keep the bank out of the press.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386614) With client relations still close and raiding the business of competitors taboo, there was no particular need for self-promotion.
这家银行小心翼翼地呵护着自己的形象，门上那颇具魅力的“23”号便是明证。它的电话号码是 Hanover 5-2323，那辆黑色凯迪拉克公司车的牌照则是 G-2323。作为老钱和上流社会的专属银行，它恪守着严格的礼仪。年轻银行家打电话时必须戴着帽子，在办公室里，若是在去洗手间的路上脱下外套，都可能招致无法挽回的职业灾难。在这个古板的地方，信托部的女洗手间连标识都没有，因为面红耳赤的银行家们始终无法就标识的措辞达成一致。他们崇尚低调。客户的任何信息都不得对外透露，年度报告中不放任何图片，广告更是绝对禁止。当一位新员工问公关人员他的职责是什么时，对方答道：“我就是那个拿钱让银行上不了新闻的人。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386614) 由于与客户的关系依然密切，且挖竞争对手的业务被视为大忌，因此并没有刻意自我推销的必要。

Even as the Morgan bank exploited its mystique, a lot of bluffing was going on. “The reputation of doing business only with the biggest of the big, the image of aloofness, could be off-putting to a new generation of entrepreneurs and corporate executives,” remarked Jim Brugger, the bank’s publicist. “Without enunciating it in so many words, the bank in this period worked to shed some elements of the mythology that clung to it.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386774) The Gentleman Banker’s Code dictated that clients come to bankers. Yet Morgans could no longer afford such imperial passivity, and Whitney dispatched young “bird dogs” across the country to scout up business. He wanted greater geographic breadth in the client base. Without putting too fine a point on it, the mighty Morgans was begging for new clients, which offended some old-timers. As Longstreet Hinton of the Trust Department later wrote, “A few people within the organization believed that potential customers should take the initiative to do business with the bank and some even had the strange notion that no existing clients would ever even dream of taking their business elsewhere.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386949)
即便摩根银行极力渲染其神秘色彩，虚张声势的成分也着实不少。“只与顶尖中的顶尖客户打交道、摆出一副高高在上的姿态——这种名声可能会让新一代企业家和公司高管望而却步，”该银行公关人士吉姆·布拉格（Jim Brugger）评论道。“虽然没有明说，但这家银行在那一时期确实在努力摆脱某些附着于身的传奇光环。”《绅士银行家守则》规定客户应当主动登门拜访银行家，但摩根已再难承受这般傲慢的被动。惠特尼（Whitney）派出了年轻的“猎犬”奔赴全国各地招揽业务，希望扩大客户群的地域覆盖范围。说白了，昔日的摩根巨擘已经开始四处乞求新客户，这令一些老派人物颇为不悦。 信托部的朗斯特里特·辛顿后来写道：“组织内部有些人认为，潜在客户应该主动来找银行做生意，甚至还有人抱有奇怪的想法，觉得现有客户绝不会考虑把业务转到别处去。”"Immersive Translate" [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2386949)

An enduring Morgan myth was that the bank required a \$1-million balance for personal checking accounts. These rare Morgan checks were cashed on sight anywhere in the world and were good for cultivating executives. At a Bond Club spoof in the 1950s, a vaudeville team satirized the Morgan approach, singing “Our tellers have a million-dollar smile. They only smile at people with a million dollars.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387142) This exclusivity could be self-defeating. At one annual meeting, George Whitney created a sensation by denying the \$l-million minimum. “WHITNEY EXPLODES ‘MORGAN MYTH’” ran the the incredulous *New York Times* headline; “LESS THAN MILLION DEPOSIT TAKEN.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387294) But further down in the article, Whitney seemed to waver, saying the bank wasn’t “geared physically” to small accounts. He finally left the impression that perhaps there was a \$l-million minimum for personal deposits after all.
一个流传已久的摩根神话是，该银行要求个人支票账户的最低余额为 100 万美元。这些稀有的摩根支票在全球任何地方都能见票即兑，是培养高管的绝佳工具。20 世纪 50 年代，在 Bond Club 的一场滑稽表演中，一个杂耍班子讽刺了摩根的做法，唱道：“我们的出纳员有百万美元的笑容。他们只对拥有百万美元的人微笑。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387142) 这种排他性可能适得其反。在一次年度会议上，George Whitney 否认了 100 万美元最低存款的说法，引起轰动。《纽约时报》以难以置信的标题报道：“WHITNEY EXPLODES 'MORGAN MYTH'”；“LESS THAN MILLION DEPOSIT TAKEN。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387294) 但在文章后面，Whitney 似乎有所动摇，称银行在“物理上”没有为小额账户做好准备。最终他给人留下的印象是，个人存款可能确实有 100 万美元的最低限额。

THIS posturing hid problems at the House of Morgan that would intensify through the decade. They stemmed from the way Wall Street banks financed their operations—especially a practice called compensating balances. In exchange for a loan, companies would leave up to 20 percent of the money behind in interest-free deposits. By paying such tribute, the borrowers preserved the banking relationship and received free services, such as the right to consult the bank economist or have a merger arranged. Compensating balances also guaranteed credit during times of scarcity, an assurance that reflected historic corporate anxiety about maintaining a constant flow of capital. This setup bound Wall Street banks to their customers in an intimate relationship and gave banks free cash to lend at high spreads. It was a wonderful racket. In these fading days of relationship banking, profits seemed almost guaranteed, producing a pleasant but stolid generation of bankers.
这种姿态掩盖了摩根银行内部的问题，而这些问题会在接下来的十年里愈演愈烈。其根源在于华尔街银行融资的方式——尤其是一种名为“补偿性余额”的做法。作为贷款的条件，企业会将其中的高达 20%的资金以无息存款的形式留在银行。通过支付这样的“贡金”，借款人维系了与银行的合作关系，并获得了免费服务，比如有权咨询银行经济学家，或由银行安排并购事宜。补偿性余额还确保了在资金紧张时期也能获得信贷，这种保障反映了企业对维持资本持续流动的历史性焦虑。这一安排让华尔街银行与客户之间建立了紧密的关系，并为银行提供了可用于高利差放贷的免费资金。这简直是一桩绝妙的勾当。在关系型银行业务逐渐式微的日子里，利润几乎是有保障的，从而催生了一代舒适但沉闷的银行家。

In retrospect, it may seem peculiar that companies should have left so much idle money with their banks. But while inflation and interest rates were low, they really sacrificed little. Leffingwell was in the forefront of those arguing for free-market interest rates. The bank knew that the easy days of compensating balances were numbered. Nevertheless, it got a jolt in September 1949, when it found itself the unexpected victim of a sensational crime—a tabloid story that didn’t make the financial pages but had a profound impact at the bank.
回想起来，企业将大量闲置资金留在银行的做法或许显得有些奇怪。但在通胀和利率都处于低位的时期，它们实际上并未付出多少代价。莱芬韦尔是主张利率市场化的先驱之一。银行深知，靠补偿性余额轻松赚钱的日子已经屈指可数。然而，1949 年 9 月，银行还是遭遇了一次震惊——它意外成为一桩耸人听闻的罪案的受害者。这则小报新闻虽未登上财经版面，却对银行产生了深远影响。

A French-Canadian jeweler known as J. Albert Guay had an illicit passion for a nineteen-year-old waitress named Marie-Ange. Determined to get rid of his interfering wife, Guay tucked a bomb into her suitcase just before she boarded a Quebec Airways flight. He wanted not only to indulge his illicit ardor but to collect his wife’s \$10,000 in life insurance as well. Fifty miles northeast of Quebec, the plane exploded, incinerating Quay’s wife and twenty-two other passengers. The scheming jeweler collected neither cash nor mistress, but ended up condemned to death by hanging.
一位名叫 J. Albert Guay 的法裔加拿大珠宝商，对一名 19 岁的女服务员玛丽-安吉心生不伦之恋。为了甩掉碍事的妻子，Guay 在她登上魁北克航空航班前，偷偷将一枚炸弹塞进她的手提箱。他不仅想沉溺于这段禁忌激情，还打算骗取妻子一万美元的人寿保险。飞机在魁北克东北方向五十英里处爆炸，Guay 的妻子和其他二十二名乘客瞬间葬身火海。这个机关算尽的珠宝商既没拿到钱，也没得到情人，最终被判处绞刑。

Such melodrama seemed worlds away from the sedate J. P. Morgan and Company. Yet the plane victims included E. Tappan Stannard, head of Kennecott Copper. Stannard belonged to Kennecott back when Dwight Morrow helped the Guggenheims to organize it during World War I. In 1942, soon after Morgan’s incorporation, he became the first “outside” director on the bank’s board. Now Stannard’s mystified successor asked his chief financial officer about a \$60-million deposit the copper company kept at Morgans. The flustered CFO said the company always kept big balances there. Not schooled in such absurdities, the new president asked, why not leave \$10 million and invest the other \$50 million? This bright idea shocked 23 Wall: Kennecott was withdrawing 10 percent of the bank, despite the fact that George Whitney was a Kennecott director. (According to other versions of the story, Morgans actually encouraged Kennecott to spread its deposits among several banks for reasons of safety.) The move foreshadowed a central feature of the Casino Age—the death of relationship banking, which had been characterized by exclusive ties that bound major companies to the House of Morgan and other Wall Street banks.
这种闹剧般的场景，与沉稳持重的 J. P. Morgan and Company 简直像是两个世界。然而，那架飞机上的遇难者中，却包括了 Kennecott Copper 的掌门人 E. Tappan Stannard。早在一战期间，Dwight Morrow 协助 Guggenheim 家族组建 Kennecott 时，Stannard 就已加入公司。1942 年，就在 Morgan 公司改制后不久，他成为了该银行董事会中的第一位“外部”董事。如今，Stannard 的继任者满腹狐疑，问其首席财务官：铜业公司在 Morgans 存放的 6000 万美元存款是怎么一回事？慌了神的 CFO 说，公司历来在那里保留大笔余额。这位新总裁从未领教过如此荒唐之事，于是问道：何不留 1000 万，把剩下的 5000 万拿去投资？这个“妙计”震惊了 23 Wall：Kennecott 要从银行抽走 10% 的资金，尽管 George Whitney 还是 Kennecott 的董事。（另有版本称，其实是 Morgans 出于安全考虑，建议 Kennecott 将存款分散到多家银行。）) 这一举措预示了赌场时代的一个核心特征——关系型银行的消亡，这种银行业模式曾以大型企业与摩根大通及其他华尔街银行之间排他性的绑定关系为标志。

Morgans needed these big cash balances to survive. Under legal lending limits, it couldn’t commit more than 10 percent of its working capital to a single customer. (Bank capital was actually smaller than deposits—essentially, what would remain after the bank had paid off all its debts.) This meant it could provide only a piddling \$5 million or \$6 million to a General Motors, a U.S. Steel, or a General Electric. With directors on these companies’ boards, Morgan still had an inside edge, but its shortage of capital threatened to rob it of major business. As Leonard McCollom of Continental Oil (afterward Conoco) told George S. Moore of National City, “J.P. Morgan’s not big enough to be an oil bank, but you are, and you should gear up for it.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387446) Continental, it may be noted, was formed by a Morgan-arranged merger back in the 1920s, and McCollom was even a J. P. Morgan director. If they had to, companies would bolt traditional bankers and no longer feared antagonizing Wall Street. Their options in the Casino Age were far more diversified than they had been in the old days of captivity.
摩根需要这些巨额现金余额才能生存。根据法定贷款限额，它不能将超过 10%的营运资金借给单一客户。（银行资本实际上比存款少——本质上就是银行清偿所有债务后剩余的部分。）这意味着，它最多只能向通用汽车、美国钢铁或通用电气提供区区 500 万或 600 万美元的贷款。尽管摩根在这些公司的董事会中派驻了董事，拥有内部优势，但资本短缺可能使其失去大笔业务。正如大陆石油（后更名为康菲）的伦纳德·麦科洛姆对国民城市银行的乔治·S·摩尔所说：“J.P.摩根还不够大，成不了石油银行，但你们够大，应该为此做好准备。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387446) 值得一提的是，大陆石油是 1920 年代由摩根安排合并成立的，而麦科洛姆甚至曾是 J.P.摩根的董事。如果有必要，企业会抛弃长期合作的银行，不再担心得罪华尔街。 在赌场时代，他们的选择远比过去被囚禁时更加多样化。

The House of Morgan wrestled with the unpleasant fact that it was too small to survive as a major financial institution and that only a merger could restore its former power. In 1953, John J. McCloy, the former World Bank president who was now chairman of the Chase Bank, made a merger overture to Whitney. Chase was now a colossus beside Morgans; its vast assets put it third in size nationwide. Yet the House of Morgan believed unquestioningly in its special destiny. When Whitney explored the possible merger with McCloy, he bargained as if J. P. Morgan were the bigger bank. Whitney inquired who would control the merged bank and extracted a surprise concession from McCloy: “I am quite prepared to step aside if, as a result of. . . analysis, it would seem that others should conduct the affairs of this bank.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387611) When Whitney pursued this extraordinarily generous offer with his colleagues, he found no jubilation. Rather, he faced intransigent opposition from two sons of famous partners—Henry P. Davison and Tommy S. Lamont—who refused to merge with anyone, let alone Chase. They didn’t want to adulterate the purebred Morgan culture. By decade’s end, this clan-nishness would belatedly force the Morgan bank into a lifesaving merger. McCloy, meanwhile, resumed talks with the Bank of the Manhattan and consummated a merger that turned Chase from a wholesale bank into the leading retail bank, Chase Manhattan.
摩根银行不得不面对一个令人不快的现实：它规模太小，难以作为一家大型金融机构生存下去，唯有合并才能重振昔日的雄风。1953 年，曾任世界银行行长、当时担任大通银行董事长的约翰·J·麦克洛伊向惠特尼提出了合并的意向。此时的大通银行与摩根相比已如巨人般庞大，其巨额资产使其规模位居全国第三。然而，摩根银行依然坚信自己肩负着特殊使命。当惠特尼与麦克洛伊探讨合并可能性时，他讨价还价的姿态仿佛 J.P.摩根才是规模更大的那家银行。惠特尼询问合并后银行的控制权归属，并从麦克洛伊那里获得了一个出人意料的让步："如果经过分析表明应由他人来掌管这家银行的事务，我完全愿意退居二线。" [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387611) 然而，当惠特尼与同事们商讨这一异常慷慨的提议时，却并未感受到丝毫欣喜。 实际上，他遭遇了两位知名合伙人子嗣——亨利·P·戴维森与汤米·S·拉蒙——的强硬抵制。这两人拒绝与任何银行合并，更别提大通银行了。他们不愿让纯正的摩根文化受到玷污。到了十年末，这种家族式的排外心态才迫使摩根银行匆忙寻求合并以求自保。 与此同时，麦克洛伊重启了与曼哈顿银行的谈判，并成功促成了一项合并，使大通银行从一家批发银行转型为领先的零售银行。大通曼哈顿银行。

DURING the Truman years, the Morgan bank was still subject to political attacks that echoed the New Deal. It was now accused of the old political crimes without having actually enjoyed committing them. Yet reformers couldn’t believe the House of Morgan had been emasculated. In 1950, Representative Emmanuel Celler of New York showed that J. P. Morgan directors sat on the boards of companies whose assets totaled over \$25 billion, which he called a “breath-taking figure.” Similarly, during a brief brouhaha about Morgan power, U.S. Steel chairman Irving S. Olds reassured an annual meeting with these words: “It happens that a member of J.P. Morgan &. Co. is on this board. I say that J. P. Morgan & Co. or no other financial interest or group controls U.S. Steel.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387762) The imagery suggested here, borrowed from Money Trust days, now seemed anachronistic. The giant American corporations, multinational in scope, were no longer beholden to a single Wall Street bank.
在杜鲁门时代，摩根银行仍承受着带有新政色彩的政治抨击。如今它被指控犯有昔日政治罪行，却从未真正享受过实施这些罪行的欢愉。然而改革者们并不相信摩根财团已被削弱。1950 年，纽约州众议员伊曼纽尔·塞勒指出，J.P.摩根董事们同时在总资产逾 250 亿美元的公司董事会任职，他称之为"令人瞠目的数字"。无独有偶，在一场关于摩根权势的短暂争议中，美国钢铁公司主席欧文·S·奥尔兹在年会上这样安抚道："J.P.摩根公司恰好有成员在本董事会任职。但我要说，无论是 J.P.摩根公司还是其他任何金融势力或集团，都无法掌控美国钢铁公司。" [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387762) 这种沿袭自货币信托时代的论调，如今看来早已不合时宜。那些业务遍及全球的美国巨型企业，已不再受制于某一家华尔街银行。

By the early 1950s, the anti-Wall Street vendetta that had raged for twenty years was petering out, and Morgan executives could again function as political allies. Yet the political involvement was of a different nature. George Whitney and others felt the bank had gotten burned by fooling around in politics. Gun-shy, they shrank from the power-broker role that Tom Lamont had played in the Republican party. Although a lifelong Republican, Whitney had no stomach for public fights and associated politics with public exposure, scandal, and demeaning interrogation. His influence would be more personal than institutional in nature and so discreet as to be invisible to the general public.
到 1950 年代初，持续了二十年的反华尔街仇杀逐渐偃旗息鼓，摩根的高管们重新得以扮演政治盟友的角色。然而，此次政治参与的性质已然不同。乔治·惠特尼等人觉得，银行之前涉足政治是搬起石头砸了自己的脚。一朝被蛇咬，十年怕井绳，他们避开了汤姆·拉蒙特曾在共和党内充当的权力掮客角色。尽管惠特尼终身都是共和党人，但他无意参与公开斗争，并将政治与公开曝光、丑闻和侮辱性审讯联系在一起。他的影响力更多是个人性质的，而非机构层面的，且低调隐秘到普通公众无从察觉。

Whitney had a close relationship with Dwight D. Eisenhower, which came about almost by chance. Whitney’s son Robert had served on Eisenhower’s staff during the war and worked in his presidential campaign; he introduced his father to Ike, who lunched with the Whitneys in Old Westbury when he was president of Columbia. In 1951, George Whitney helped bankroll the volunteer group Citizens for Eisenhower, which encouraged Ike Clubs to sprout across America.
惠特尼与德怀特·D·艾森豪威尔关系密切，而这几乎纯属机缘巧合。惠特尼的儿子罗伯特战时曾在艾森豪威尔麾下任职，后来又参与了他的总统竞选活动；他将父亲引荐给艾克，艾克在担任哥伦比亚大学校长期间，曾与惠特尼一家在旧韦斯特伯里共进午餐。1951 年，乔治·惠特尼出资赞助了志愿组织“艾森豪威尔公民团”，该组织促使全美各地如雨后春笋般涌现出众多“艾克俱乐部”。

When Eisenhower went to Paris in 1951 as the military commander of SHAPE (Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe), he invited Whitney to draft weekly or monthly letters outlining his views on topical issues at home. Whitney obliged with long, opinionated letters that were acerbic in their judgments of most politicians, labor leaders, and businessmen but deferential and affectionate toward Eisenhower. Ike felt at sea on economic and financial issues and welcomed these lectures. “Your letters are one of the brightest things in my office life,” he told Whitney.[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2387909)
1951 年，艾森豪威尔以欧洲盟军最高司令部（SHAPE）军事指挥官身份前往巴黎时，他邀请惠特尼每周或每月撰写信件，阐述他对国内热点问题的看法。惠特尼欣然应允，写下了长篇大论、观点鲜明的信件——对大多数政客、劳工领袖和商界人士的评判尖刻犀利，唯独对艾森豪威尔充满敬重与温情。艾克在经济金融问题上常感力不从心，对这番点拨求之不得。“您的来信是我办公室生活中最亮眼的事之一，”他对惠特尼说道。

Whitney’s letters reflect a frustration with the contemporary economy that says much about the fallen state of bankers in the new age. By his own admission, his favorite bugbear was organized labor, yet he was no less reluctant to chastise management for caving in to labor’s demands. Although he had sat on the General Motors board for twenty-seven years, he took more pot shots at GM president Charles E. Wilson than anybody else. He was especially irate that Wilson had negotiated a cost-of-living allowance with the United Auto Workers, which he thought would foster inflation, even if it might benefit the company. At one point, Whitney mockingly sent Ike a Wilson speech about stopping inflation, pointing out the incongruity between author and subject. The days when the House of Morgan dictated to its industrial clients were over.
惠特尼的信件反映出他对当代经济的不满，也道出了新时代银行家地位一落千丈的窘境。他坦言自己最痛恨的是工会组织，但同时也毫不留情地抨击管理层对工会要求的妥协。尽管他在通用汽车董事会任职二十七年，但他对通用汽车总裁查尔斯·E·威尔逊的批评最为犀利。尤其让他恼火的是，威尔逊竟与美国汽车工人联合会协商了生活成本津贴调整方案——即便这对公司可能有利，在他眼里这无疑会助长通胀。惠特尼甚至曾讽刺地寄给艾克一份威尔逊关于遏制通胀的演讲稿，直指作者与主题之间的荒谬反差。摩根财团对工业客户颐指气使的时代，已经一去不复返了。

Whitney loathed the Truman administration, which he saw as perpetuating the worst New Deal tendencies—a welfare-state mentality that encouraged people to expect support from government, imposition of federal controls over business, and a bias toward fighting unemployment rather than inflation. He thought Truman scapegoated the rich and exploited class divisions. Yet he was no less fearful of the Republican candidacy of Senator Robert Taft of Ohio, whom Lamont had rejected in favor of Wendell Willkie a decade earlier. In late 1951, Ike was still evading any commitment to run for president, citing the nonpartisan requirements of his position at SHAPE. But when Whitney heard that Taft had announced his candidacy in October 1951, he went beyond gentle prodding and made a strong plea for Eisenhower to get into the race: “It is quite clear that the work you are now carrying on would be put in jeopardy if Taft’s candidacy succeeded because his strongest backers represent the strongest isolationist movement in this country. . . . I see little comfort in a Republican Administration headed by Taft.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388075) Ike’s election confirmed the ascendancy of the internationalists in the postwar Republican party.
惠特尼厌恶杜鲁门政府，认为它延续了新政最糟糕的倾向——一种鼓励人们依赖政府支持的福利国家心态、联邦政府对商业的过度控制，以及重就业轻通胀的偏颇。他指责杜鲁门将富人当作替罪羊，刻意激化阶级矛盾。然而，他对俄亥俄州参议员罗伯特·塔夫脱的共和党候选人身份同样深感不安——十年前拉蒙特正是弃塔夫脱而选择了温德尔·威尔基。1951 年底，艾森豪威尔仍以北大西洋公约组织欧洲盟军最高司令一职需保持无党派立场为由，回避任何参选总统的承诺。 然而，当惠特尼得知塔夫脱于 1951 年 10 月宣布参选后，他不再满足于温和的催促，而是强烈恳请艾森豪威尔加入角逐：“显而易见，如果塔夫脱竞选成功，你目前从事的工作将面临风险，因为他最坚定的支持者代表着美国最强大的孤立主义势力……我看不出一个由塔夫脱领导的共和党政府能带来什么希望。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388075) 艾克的当选证实了战后共和党内国际主义者的崛起。

Only a month after Eisenhower’s election, Whitney’s pleasure in the victory was cut short. His thirty-six-year-old son, Robert, an assistant VP at the bank in charge of Southwest business and a ruggedly handsome, athletic man, was hit by a car one evening in late December 1952 and was killed instantly. Robert Whitney left behind a wife and four children.
艾森豪威尔当选刚满一个月，惠特尼胜利的喜悦便戛然而止。1952 年 12 月下旬的一个夜晚，他那 36 岁的儿子罗伯特——一位身材健硕、相貌英俊的运动型男子，时任银行负责西南业务的助理副总裁——被一辆汽车撞倒，当场身亡。罗伯特·惠特尼留下了妻子和四个孩子。

For a man whose early life had suggested easy prosperity, George had led a life full of trouble. Dwight and Mamie Eisenhower sent a handwritten note of condolence: “We can find no words to express the shock and grief we suffer from the news we have just received of Bobby’s tragic accident.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388246)
对于早年生活看似一帆风顺的乔治来说，他的一生却充满了坎坷。德怀特·艾森豪威尔和玛米·艾森豪威尔夫妇亲手写了一封慰问信：“我们无法用言语表达刚刚得知博比不幸遇难的消息时所承受的震惊与悲痛。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388246)

In Eisenhower, the Morgan bank had a nearly perfect ally—conservative on economic issues yet opposed to economic nationalism and political isolationism. Not since Hoover had the bank enjoyed such a neat fit. Calling Whitney his Wall Street “listening post,” Eisenhower invited him to his White House “stag dinners” for business friends—occasions that produced charges of Ike’s being corrupted by rich friends. The president clearly heeded Whitney’s advice. In the early 1950s, there was a movement to unfix the price of gold. Some wanted higher, other lower, gold prices. Whitney and Leffingwell convinced Eisenhower to keep the gold price at \$35 an ounce, where it had stood since 1934. Ike thought Leffingwell’s memo on the gold question the best he had read.
在艾森豪威尔时代，摩根银行拥有一个近乎完美的盟友——他在经济问题上持保守态度，但却反对经济民族主义和政治孤立主义。自胡佛以来，银行从未如此称心如意。艾森豪威尔称惠特尼为他在华尔街的“耳线”，并邀请他参加白宫为商界朋友举办的“男士晚宴”——这些场合曾引发批评，称艾克被富有的朋友们带坏了。总统显然对惠特尼的建议言听计从。20 世纪 50 年代初，曾有一股推动黄金价格浮动的运动。有人希望金价上涨，有人则希望下跌。惠特尼和莱芬韦尔说服艾森豪威尔将金价维持在每盎司 35 美元，这个价格自 1934 年以来一直未变。艾克认为莱芬韦尔关于黄金问题的备忘录是他读过的最好的。

The early Eisenhower years certified that the long-standing Morgan preference for international economic cooperation was firmly entrenched in Washington. The historic split that had so bedeviled Morgans—between rural isolationists, who favored inflation, and eastern-seaboard bankers, who favored hard money and had financial ties to Europe—had become a thing of the past, a topic for history students. American companies were going abroad, farmers were cultivating export markets, and Washington was operating military bases around the world. America no longer seemed so distant from the rest of the world and was explicitly tied to Europe through the Atlantic alliance. The House of Morgan had ceased to be an alien presence in America’s political culture.
早期艾森豪威尔任期内确认了摩根家族长期推崇的国际经济合作理念已在华盛顿牢牢扎根。曾长期困扰摩根家族的历史性分裂——一边是支持通胀的农村孤立主义者，另一边是奉行硬通货且与欧洲有金融纽带的东海岸银行家——已成为过去，仅供历史学者钻研。美国企业纷纷走向海外，农民开拓出口市场，华盛顿在全球运营军事基地。美国与世界其他地区的距离不再遥远，并通过大西洋联盟与欧洲紧密相连。摩根财团在美国政治文化中已不再是异类。
