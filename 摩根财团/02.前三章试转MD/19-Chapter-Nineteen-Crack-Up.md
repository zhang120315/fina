# Chapter Nineteen: Crack-Up

AFTER passage of the Glass-Steagall Act, there was a grace period, during which the House of Morgan had to choose between deposit and investment banking. The partners still hoped the measure would be repealed. But after its unrivaled political influence in the 1920s, the bank seemed paralyzed, unable to exercise influence. As Arthur Schlesinger, Jr., has noted, no group lost more in public esteem, or more keenly lamented its exclusion from Washington, than the bankers. They became a caste of untouchables right at the start of the New Deal. For the House of Morgan, there were moments when the rout by enemy troops seemed terrifyingly complete. Its old foes were entrenched in Washington. For the new securities-disclosure law, the White House had asked Samuel Untermyer, of Pujo fame, to prepare a draft. Untermyer lost standing with Roosevelt, however, when he bragged too much about his supposed intimacy with the president.
《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》通过后，有一段宽限期，在此期间摩根银行必须在存款银行与投资银行之间做出选择。合伙人仍寄希望于该法案会被废除。但这家在 20 世纪 20 年代拥有无与伦比政治影响力的银行，此刻似乎已陷入瘫痪，无力施加影响。正如小阿瑟·施莱辛格所指出的那样，没有哪个群体像银行家那样失去公众的敬重，也没有哪个群体更为自己被排除在华盛顿权力圈之外而痛心疾首。新政伊始，他们便沦为了不可接触的贱民阶层。对摩根银行而言，某些时刻，敌军溃败的景象似乎已令人恐惧地彻底呈现。其旧敌已在华盛顿站稳脚跟。针对新的证券披露法，白宫曾请来因普约听证会而闻名的塞缪尔·昂特迈耶起草草案。然而，昂特迈耶因过分吹嘘自己与总统的亲密关系，在罗斯福面前失宠了。

The intellectual mentor of much legislation was that scourge of the New Haven Railroad, Louis Brandeis, now a Supreme Court justice. In May 1933, the precepts he had expounded to Lamont at the University Club twenty years before became law in the Securities Act. This truth-in-securities law required the registration of new securities and full disclosure of information about companies and underwriters. *Caveat vendor* replaced *caveat emptor* as the regulatory philosophy. When FDR spoke in favor of the bill, he alluded to Brandeis’s book about the New Haven railroad, *Other People’s Money;* the law, said Roosevelt, would embody “the ancient truth that those who manage banks, corporations, and other agencies handling or using other people’s money are trustees acting for others.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337944)
众多立法背后的思想导师，正是那个曾让纽黑文铁路头疼不已的人物——如今已是最高法院大法官的路易斯·布兰代斯。1933 年 5 月，他在二十年前于大学俱乐部向拉蒙特阐述的那些原则，终于写进了《证券法》。这部"证券真相法"要求新证券必须登记注册，并且发行公司与承销商的信息必须完全披露。监管哲学从此从"买家自负"转变为"卖家负责"。当罗斯福为这项法案发声时，他特别提到了布兰代斯那本关于纽黑文铁路的著作《他人的钱》；罗斯福说，这部法律将体现"一个古老的真理：那些管理银行、公司以及其他处理或使用他人资金的机构的人，是代表他人行事的受托人。" [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2337944)

For the House of Morgan, Louis Brandeis was more than just a critic; he was an adversary of almost mythical proportions. In early 1934, Leffingwell told Lamont he should read a new edition of *Other People’s Money* and blamed Brandeis for the Glass-Steagall provision pertaining to private banks: “I have little doubt that he inspired it, or even drafted it. The Jews do not forget. They are relentless. . . . The reason why I make so much of this is that I think you underestimate the forces we are antagonizing. . . . I believe that we are confronted with the profound politico-economic philosophy, matured in the wood for twenty years, of the finest brain and the most powerful personality in the Democratic party, who happens to be a Justice of the Supreme Court.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338120) Despite the separation of powers, Brandeis advised Roosevelt through an emissary—his daughter, Elizabeth Raushenbush. Roosevelt referred to Brandeis by the code name Isaiah.
对摩根家族而言，路易斯·布兰代斯不仅是一位批评者，更是近乎神话般强大的对手。1934 年初，莱芬韦尔告诉拉蒙特，他应该读一读《他人的钱》的新版本，并将格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案中涉及私营银行的部分归咎于布兰代斯：“我几乎毫不怀疑，是他促成了这项条款，甚至亲手起草了它。犹太人不会忘记，他们毫不留情……我之所以如此强调这一点，是因为我认为你低估了我们所对抗的力量……我相信，我们正面对民主党中最出色的头脑和最强大的人格所酝酿了二十年的深厚政治经济哲学，而这个人恰好是一位最高法院大法官。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338120) 尽管存在三权分立，布兰代斯仍通过他的女儿伊丽莎白·劳申布什作为中间人向罗斯福提供建议。罗斯福则用代号“以赛亚”来称呼布兰代斯。

In 1934, the House of Morgan joined with New York Stock Exchange president Richard Whitney in a zealous lobbying effort to defeat the Securities Exchange Act. Operating from a Georgetown townhouse nicknamed the Wall Street Embassy, they warned that federal regulation would convert the Street into “a deserted village.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338322) It was a campaign of such harrowing intensity that despite the anti-Wall Street mood, the bill’s authors were surprised by their victory. One of them, Thomas G. Corcoran, exulted, “Rayburn and I stood alone against all the batteries of lawyers sent by Morgan’s and the Stock Exchange—and we won out!”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338493) Another Morgan hobgoblin, Joseph Kennedy, snubbed by Jack before the crash, became the Securities and Exchange Commission’s first chairman. Ferdinand Pecora, who worked on the bill, was named a commissioner. The money changers had indeed been chased from the Temple, by the Irish, the Italians, and the Jews—the groups excluded from Wasp Wall Street in the 1920s.
1934 年，摩根财团联手纽约证券交易所主席理查德·惠特尼，发起了一场声势浩大的游说活动，企图扼杀《证券交易法》。他们以一座名为“华尔街大使馆”的乔治敦连排别墅为大本营，警告说联邦监管会让华尔街沦为“一座鬼城”。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338322) 这场攻势异常猛烈，以至于尽管当时反华尔街情绪高涨，法案的起草者们对自己的胜利也感到意外。其中一位起草者托马斯·G·科克伦得意洋洋地说：“雷伯恩和我孤军奋战，顶住了摩根和证交所派来的所有律师大军——但我们赢了！” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338493) 另一位摩根的心头大患——约瑟夫·肯尼迪，在大崩盘前曾被杰克冷落，却成了证券交易委员会的首任主席。参与起草法案的费迪南德·佩科拉被任命为委员。那些货币兑换商确实被赶出了神殿——赶走他们的，是爱尔兰人、意大利人和犹太人，这些群体在 1920 年代被排斥在 WASP 主导的华尔街之外。

The Morgan partners resorted to hyperbolic criticism when they should have been conciliatory. Jack Morgan inveighed against “absurd” federal deposit insurance and warned of dying capital markets if securities laws were enacted. Faced with the decline of the bank’s power, he emitted an air of subdued defeat. He complained to friends that he was a punching bag for every political propagandist. Like other partners, he felt muzzled in contesting the New Deal—perhaps the reason why he didn’t join his friend and lawyer John Davis in forming the anti-New Deal Liberty League in 1934. “If anybody lifts his voice in protest . . . he is at once held up to public scorn as a totally selfish, grasping individual, wholly unresponsive to the new thought,”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338653) he declared. He was an easy butt for critics. He often antagonized reporters by curtly refusing interviews: “I do not think my opinion is worth a damn.” Other times, he would talk and denounce progressive income taxes or take other inflammatory stands. Either way, his popularity declined.
摩根公司的合伙人本应采取安抚态度，却选择了夸张的批评。杰克·摩根抨击联邦存款保险制度“荒谬”，并警告说如果通过证券法，资本市场将会消亡。面对银行影响力的衰退，他流露出一种隐忍的挫败感。他向朋友抱怨，自己成了每一个政治宣传家的出气筒。与其他合伙人一样，他在反对新政时感到被钳制——这或许是他 1934 年没有加入朋友兼律师约翰·戴维斯组建的反新政自由联盟的原因。“如果有人提高声音抗议……他立刻就会被公众视为完全自私、贪婪、对新思想毫无反应的人，” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338653) 他宣称道。他成了批评者轻易攻击的靶子。他常常因粗暴拒绝采访而激怒记者：“我觉得我的意见一文不值。”而另一些时候，他会大肆抨击累进所得税，或发表其他煽动性言论。无论哪种情况，他的声望都在下降。

Teddy Roosevelt had been Pierpont’s tormenter, and now another Roosevelt served the same role for Jack. At moments, the Roosevelt family seemed one big throng of Morgan-hating harpies. When somebody mentioned TR, Jack spluttered: “God damn all Roosevelts!”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2338845) He was fond of quoting Richard Hooker, the English Renaissance divine, that to live by one man’s will was every man’s misery. For Jack, that man was FDR, whom he saw as a frightening left-wing charlatan out to destroy his own class. In 1934, he said, “I am gradually coming to the opinion, which I did not have at first, that the United States will probably outlive even the attacks upon it by Franklin Roosevelt and I am particularly satisfied to see the rising tide of opposition to his fierce methods and his wholesale slaughter of reputations.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339018) The Roosevelt hatred became obsessive. When Jack developed a heart condition, his grandchildren were instructed not to mention the president’s name in his presence. Other accounts tell of retainers snipping Roosevelt photos from Jack’s morning paper, in deference to the master’s high blood pressure.
泰迪·罗斯福曾是皮尔庞特的折磨者，如今另一位罗斯福又对杰克扮演了同样的角色。在某些时刻，罗斯福家族简直就像一群仇视摩根的恶妇。每当有人提起西奥多·罗斯福，杰克就气急败坏地骂道："所有罗斯福都该死！"他喜欢引用英国文艺复兴时期神学家理查德·胡克的话：屈从于一个人的意志，是每个人的不幸。对杰克而言，那个人就是富兰克林·罗斯福——他眼中的危险左翼骗子，一心要摧毁自己的阶级。1934 年，他说："我逐渐得出一个最初没有的结论：美国或许能挺过富兰克林·罗斯福的攻击，看到反对他激进手段和全面毁人名节的浪潮日益高涨，我尤其感到满意。"这份对罗斯福的仇恨变得偏执。当杰克患上心脏病后，他的孙辈们被叮嘱不要在他面前提总统的名字。 其他故事则提到，仆人会从杰克早晨的报纸上剪掉罗斯福的照片，以免刺激他易怒的血压。

Rather than bending with the time, Jack’s conservatism grew crustily defensive. The old swipes at Congress lapsed into ugly diatribes against democracy and universal suffrage. Congressmen were “wild men” who controlled his destiny, while the intelligent, propertied class were subjected to the whims of a fickle, emotional majority. He regarded the New Deal less as a set of economic reforms than as a direct, malicious assault on the social order, aimed at the “extinction of all wealth and earning power.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339181) Notwithstanding a 25-percent jobless rate, he wanted balanced budgets and low taxes. “The more I see of the New Deal,” he said, “the more I realize that there is nothing new about it except its name.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339388)
杰克的保守主义非但没有与时俱进，反而变得愈发顽固刻薄。他过去对国会的抨击，现在演变成了对民主和普选制度的恶毒谩骂。国会成员成了掌控他命运的“野蛮人”，而聪明有钱有势的阶层，却要受制于反复无常、情绪化的多数人的一时兴起。在他看来，新政与其说是一系列经济改革，不如说是对社会秩序的直接恶意攻击，其目的是“消灭所有财富和赚钱能力”。 [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339181) 尽管当时失业率高达 25%，他依然坚持平衡预算、压低税收。“我对新政看得越多，就越觉得它除了名字新，其他一点新意都没有。”他说。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339388)

As chief bank lobbyist, Lamont wasn’t reflexively antagonistic to the New Deal and applauded measures to combat deflation, such as open-market operations (the purchase and sale of government securities) by the Fed. At moments in the 1930s, Morgans supported easy-money policies while hidebound Wall Street fretted about inflation. But even Lamont never presented a reform program that would steal the bank critics’ thunder; Wall Street let its enemies write the new laws.
作为首席银行说客，拉蒙特对新政并非条件反射般地持反对态度，他对美联储通过公开市场操作（买卖政府证券）等措施抗击通货紧缩表示赞赏。1930 年代，摩根财团一度支持宽松货币政策，而保守的华尔街则对通胀忧心忡忡。但即便是拉蒙特，也从未提出过足以抢走银行批评者风头的改革方案；最终让华尔街的对手们主导了新法律的起草。

As was his wont, Lamont used different voices as he spoke to different people. At a private dinner in 1934, he told relief administrator Harry Hopkins, “Well, if the country was willing to spend thirty billion dollars in a year’s time to try to lick the Germans, I don’t see why people should complain about its spending five or six billion dollars to keep people from starving.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339558) Here he sounded like a free-spending liberal. Yet in chatting with Chancellor of the Exchequer Neville Chamberlain that year, he praised Britain for overcoming the Depression through sound, old-fashioned policies, not deficit spending. He joked, “I suppose I mustn’t hold you personally responsible for having sent Keynes over and to have made our President spend another *Vi* billion dollars in public works.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339732)
拉蒙特向来对不同的人用不同的口吻说话。1934 年的一次私人晚宴上，他对救济署长哈里·霍普金斯说：“好吧，如果国家愿意一年花三百亿美元去打败德国人，那花五六十亿美元不让人民挨饿，我看不出有什么好抱怨的。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339558) 这番话听起来像个大手大脚的自由派。可同年与英国财政大臣内维尔·张伯伦闲聊时，他又称赞英国靠稳健老派的政策而非赤字开支走出了大萧条，并打趣道：“我想我不能怪你个人把凯恩斯送过来，害得我们总统又砸了十亿美元搞公共工程。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2339732)

The best weapon the Morgan bank had for changing New Deal policy was Russell Leffingwell. With his pure white hair and Pinocchio nose, he looked like a sage or an elder statesman. He was an omnivorous reader, a man of wide vision who could offer cogent opinions on any subject. Leffingwell had the most balanced view of the New Deal and often told friends Roosevelt had saved America from revolution in 1933. He wasn’t afraid to scandalize Wall Street by consorting with the president. Sometimes he used his friend Morris Ernst, a liberal lawyer, as an intermediary to the White House, so that Walter Winchell and other columnists wouldn’t get wind of his influence. Yet even Leffingwell couldn’t make the intellectual adjustment required by the economic emergency. When Roosevelt brought him to the White House in October 1934 to discuss a new public works program, Leffingwell rejected the plan with ritual assertions that it would cause inflation and crowd out private capital from financial markets. Yet deflation was the major problem, and far from being overcrowded, capital markets were empty. Leffingwell was a nineteenth-century liberal and found it hard to approve of many forms of government intervention in the economy.
摩根银行用来改变新政政策的最佳武器，就是拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔。他满头银发，长着匹诺曹式的鼻子，看起来像一位圣人或政界元老。他博览群书，视野开阔，对任何话题都能提出鞭辟入里的见解。莱芬韦尔对新政的看法最为平衡，常对朋友说，罗斯福在 1933 年将美国从革命中拯救了出来。他毫不避讳与总统往来，即便这会让华尔街震惊。有时，他会借由自己的朋友——自由派律师莫里斯·恩斯特——作为通往白宫的中介，以免沃尔特·温切尔等专栏作家闻风而动，察觉他的影响力。然而，即便是莱芬韦尔，也无法在经济危机所需的智力调整上迈出那一步。 1934 年 10 月，罗斯福将莱芬韦尔请到白宫，商讨一项新的公共工程计划，莱芬韦尔却照例以老调重弹的理由拒绝——声称该计划将引发通胀，并挤出金融市场的私人资本。然而，当时的主要问题是通缩，资本市场远非拥挤不堪，而是空空如也。莱芬韦尔是位 19 世纪的自由派，对政府干预经济的诸多形式都难以认同。

In many ways, the House of Morgan was a muscle-bound giant, afraid its lobbying efforts would be twisted by opponents into proof of insidious power. By late 1933, the airwaves were filled with demagogic voices that ascribed the Depression to Wall Street-inspired monetary policy. Father Charles E. Coughlin, the radio priest, stoked the old prairie fires once lighted by William Jennings Bryan. From his Shrine of the Little Flower near Detroit, he incited his nationwide audience with tales of a bank that had enslaved America to the gold standard, had long colluded with the British crown, and had forced debt and deflation on farmers. That the same bank had hailed Britain and America’s departure from gold mattered not a whit. In a November 1933 broadcast entitled “Thus Goeth the Battle!” Coughlin dredged up old myths about the House of Morgan: “Who in God’s name ever accused the Morgans of having patriotism to this country? Who doesn’t know that they have been playing the British game for years; that they pay taxes to England and none to America?”
在很多方面，摩根财团都是一个肌肉发达却行动笨拙的巨人，生怕自己的游说努力会被对手扭曲成暗藏权谋的证据。到了 1933 年末，电波中充斥着煽动性的言论，将大萧条归咎于华尔街主导的货币政策。广播神父查尔斯·E·库格林，点燃了当年威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩曾燃起的草原之火。在底特律附近的小花神殿，他用一家银行的故事煽动全国听众——这家银行让美国沦为金本位的奴隶，长期与英国王室勾结，并将债务与通缩强加给农民。至于正是这家银行曾欢呼英美脱离金本位，这一点根本不重要。在 1933 年 11 月题为《战事如此进行！》的广播中，库格林翻出了关于摩根财团的旧日迷思："看在上帝份上，有谁曾指责摩根家族对这个国家怀有爱国之心？" 谁不知道他们多年来一直在玩英国人的把戏？他们向英国交税，却从不给美国交一分钱？"

He then evoked a football team of politicians—Morgan stooges all—who had pushed America into the Depression:
接着，他搬出了一支由政客组成的足球队——全是摩根的傀儡——正是他们把美国推入了大萧条：

> And on the sidelines there sits J. Pierpont Morgan—the Knute Rockne of the grand old guard—the scout in the pay of England, the master mind of tax-dodging, the strategist of the financial huddle. . .
> 场边坐着的是 J. Pierpont Morgan——老派卫队中的 Knute Rockne——一个受雇于英格兰的探子，偷税漏税的幕后黑手，金融混战中的战略家……

> There are two powerful generations of Morgans—the elder who sold guns to the Civil War soldiers—guns that couldn’t shoot—and the younger who arranged money for more guns that shot to no avail in the last war. . . . Father Coughlin would ask his listeners to mail in dollar bills. It later was revealed that he used some of the money to speculate in silver futures through a personal account at Paine Webber.
库格林神父曾让听众邮寄美元钞票。后来有爆料称，他用其中一部分钱通过自己在 Paine Webber 的个人账户投机白银期货。

In these scurrilous attacks by Coughlin, the Hearst press, and other isolationist organs, a powerful theme emerged—that World War I and the Depression had been instigated by the same Wall Street bankers. The argument was that the bankers drew America into the war to safeguard their Allied loans and that the debts and reparations produced by the war led to the Depression, *ergo*, Morgans and other international bankers were to blame for American participation in the war and for the Depression. For Anglophobic populists, this was a convenient equation. They could exploit discontent with Wall Street to argue against closer ties with Britain, and they could tap isolationist sentiment to press for tougher bank controls. The House of Morgan was the natural target for this attack.
在库格林、赫斯特报业以及其他孤立主义媒体的这些恶意攻击中，浮现出一个强有力的论调——即第一次世界大战和大萧条都是同一批华尔街银行家煽动起来的。该论据声称，银行家们为了保障他们对协约国的贷款而将美国拖入战争，而战争产生的债务和赔款又导致了经济大萧条，因此，摩根家族及其他国际银行家应对美国参战和大萧条负责。对于仇英的民粹主义者而言，这是个顺理成章的等式。他们可以利用民众对华尔街的不满来反对与美国加强联系，同时也能借助孤立主义情绪来推动对银行更严格的管控。摩根财团自然成了这场攻击的靶心。

ROOSEVELT was as perplexed and annoyed with the Wall Street bankers as they were with him. He saw himself as saving the patient with radical surgery, not killing him. His talent for experimentation, for latching onto new ideas, was profoundly disturbing to bankers who had lived by sacred, immutable laws. To try to patch up relations, FDR invited Morgan loyalist George Harrison, Ben Strong’s successor at the New York Fed, for a weekend cruise aboard his yacht, the *Sequoia.* Commenting on the bankers’ mistrust, Roosevelt said ruefully, “They oppose everything I do, even though it is with the intention of helping them.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340148)
罗斯福对华尔街银行家们的困惑与恼怒，丝毫不亚于他们对他的态度。他自认为是在用激进的手术拯救病人，而非将其置于死地。他那种大胆尝试、随时接纳新想法的天赋，让那些一直恪守神圣不变法则的银行家们深感不安。为了缓和关系，富兰克林·罗斯福邀请摩根的忠实拥护者、本·斯特朗在纽约联储的继任者乔治·哈里森，乘坐自己的游艇“红杉号”进行周末巡游。谈及银行家们的不信任，罗斯福不无遗憾地说道：“他们反对我做的每一件事，哪怕我本意是要帮他们。”

Eager to mediate, Harrison arranged for FDR to address a meeting of the American Bankers Association in Washington. Lamont and Parker Gilbert attended, the first time Morgan partners ever graced an ABA meeting. The effort at mediation only worsened matters. Jackson Reynolds of the First National Bank of New York delivered a keynote speech lauding FDR. But when it was discovered that Roosevelt himself had vetted the speech, the bankers felt cheated, and the New Deal-bankers truce was ended. Both sides retreated into a bitter standoff.
急于调停的哈里森安排了罗斯福在华盛顿的美国银行家协会会议上发表讲话。拉蒙特和帕克·吉尔伯特出席了会议，这是摩根合伙人首次出席 ABA 会议。但调解努力反而让事态恶化。纽约第一国民银行的杰克逊·雷诺兹发表了赞扬罗斯福的主旨演讲。然而，当发现罗斯福本人审阅过演讲稿后，银行家们深感被欺骗，新政与银行家之间的休战宣告终结。双方陷入僵持对峙的局面。

Perhaps the most sophisticated foray against the House of Morgan came from those who sought changes in the Federal Reserve System. A little-noticed provision of the Glass-Steagall Act forbade the New York Fed to conduct negotiations with foreign banks. This was Washington’s response to the elaborate connivance between Ben Strong and Monty Norman—a relationship so important for the House of Morgan. The seemingly innocuous measure was one of Washington’s canniest moves against the bank.
或许对摩根财团最老谋深算的打击，来自那些试图改革美联储体系的人。格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案中有一条鲜为人知的条款，禁止纽约联储与外国银行进行交涉。这是华盛顿对本·斯特朗与蒙蒂·诺曼之间精密勾结的回应——这段关系对摩根财团至关重要。这条看似无害的措施，实则是华盛顿对付该银行最精明的手段之一。

Then in 1934, a young Utah banker, Marriner Stoddard Eccles, advised the Roosevelt administration on revisions to the Federal Reserve Act. Eccles wanted to emasculate the New York Fed and shift power to the Federal Reserve Board in Washington so as to purge the influence of Wall Street bankers from the system. Leffingwell was especially incensed at this move because he blamed the 1929 crash on the Washington board’s interference with the New York Fed, which had wanted to raise interest rates and arrest speculation. George Harrison tried to marshal enough conservative senators to defeat the Banking Act of 1935, but his efforts were in vain. Under the Eccles legislation, the district banks lost much of their autonomy; power was now lodged in the seven-member Washington board. In two symbolic steps underscoring the Fed’s new independence, the Treasury secretary was removed from the board, and the Fed, which had operated from Treasury premises, got its own building.
然后在 1934 年，一位来自犹他州的年轻银行家马里纳·斯托达德·埃克尔斯向罗斯福政府建言，要求修改《联邦储备法》。埃克尔斯意图削弱纽约联储的势力，将权力转移到华盛顿的联邦储备委员会，从而清除华尔街银行家对金融体系的影响。勒芬韦尔对此尤为震怒，因为他将 1929 年股市崩盘归咎于华盛顿委员会对纽约联储的干预——当时纽约联储本打算加息以遏制投机。乔治·哈里森试图集结足够多的保守派参议员来否决 1935 年《银行法案》，但最终徒劳无功。根据埃克尔斯主导的立法，各地区储备银行丧失了大部分自主权；权力从此集中到华盛顿七人委员会手中。有两个象征性举措凸显了美联储全新的独立性：财政部长被逐出委员会，而此前一直借用财政部办公场所的美联储，也终于拥有了自己的大楼。

Later Eccles tried to put Parker Gilbert on the reorganized Fed board, but Morgan partners dismissed the move as a sop, knowing that the Fed now responded to new political masters. In many ways, the Eccles reforms belatedly accomplished the aims of the Fed’s progressive supporters, who had wanted an American central bank to curb Wall Street power. The 1920s Republican abdication of an international role had permitted Ben Strong and the House of Morgan to subvert that intention. Now, over twenty years later, the ghost of the Money Trust was finally exorcised.
后来，埃克尔斯试图让帕克·吉尔伯特进入重组后的美联储董事会，但摩根合伙人认为此举不过是一种安抚手段——他们心知肚明，美联储如今已听命于新的政治主子。从许多方面来看，埃克尔斯的改革迟来地实现了美联储进步派支持者的初衷，这些人曾希望美国的中央银行能遏制华尔街的势力。20 世纪 20 年代共和党放弃国际角色的做法，让本·斯特朗和摩根财团得以扭曲这一意图。如今，二十多年过后，金钱信托的幽灵终于被彻底驱散了。

AMONG Wall Street banks, none agonized more than the House of Morgan over whether to choose deposit or investment banking. It postponed a final decision until the summer of 1935. By that point, it had been legally barred from securities work for a year. Disturbed by the paucity of industrial issues on Wall Street, Carter Glass inserted an amendment into the proposed Banking Act of 1935 that restored limited securities powers to deposit banks. The partners rested their final hopes on this peg.
在华尔街各大银行中，没有哪家比摩根财团更纠结于选择存款银行还是投资银行了。它一直拖延到 1935 年夏天才做出最终决定。而那时，它已被法律禁止从事证券业务整整一年。由于对华尔街工业证券发行匮乏深感忧虑，卡特·格拉斯在《1935 年银行法案》草案中加入了一项修正案，恢复存款银行有限的证券业务权限。摩根家族的合伙人们便将最后的希望寄托在了这一条款上。

George Whitney, as head of corporate underwriting, was under mounting pressure to inform Morgan clients of the bank’s decision. With interest rates down, many companies wanted to refund maturing bonds at lower rates. They kept asking Whitney what to do. In late July, Charles Mitchell, former chairman of the National City Bank and now a partner in Blyth and Company, found Whitney still hoping for a last-minute reversal of Glass-Steagall. “I think they are waiting . . . to see if the underwriting amendment in the banking bill will pass,” Mitchell told a partner, “and regarding this they are more optimistic than they have been.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340309) In late August, the banking amendment reached a House-Senate conference committee. But President Roosevelt—bringing down his fist in one last blow to the House of Morgan—interceded to kill the amendment. He refused to consider any modification of Glass-Steagall.
作为企业承销业务主管的乔治·惠特尼承受着越来越大的压力，因为他必须将银行的决定告知摩根公司的客户。由于利率下降，许多公司希望以更低的利率对到期债券进行再融资。他们不断询问惠特尼该如何行动。七月底，前国家城市银行董事长、现任布莱斯公司合伙人的查尔斯·米切尔发现惠特尼仍对格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案能在最后一刻出现转机抱有希望。"我认为他们正在等待……看银行法案中的承销修正案能否通过，"米切尔对一位合伙人说道，"而且在这方面，他们比之前更加乐观。" [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340309) 八月底，银行修正案提交至众议院与参议院联席会议审议。但罗斯福总统——以雷霆之势给摩根家族最后一击——出面否决了这项修正案。他拒绝考虑对格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案做任何修改。

As if too depressed to face the truth, Jack had kept assuring Teddy Grenfell that the amendment would pass. Yet mysterious doings in the art market of early 1935 betrayed his underlying pessimism. Citing inheritance taxes and a desire to put his estate in order, Jack sold six magnificent paintings for \$1.5 million. To Fritz Thyssen, the German steel magnate, went Domenico Ghirlandajo’s portrait of Giovanna Tor-nabuoni while the Metropolitan Museum got a Fra Filippo Lippi triptych and Rubens’s Anne of Austria. Through Christie’s in London, Jack auctioned seven cases of coveted miniatures amassed over thirty years. In oppressive July heat, with a nurse in attendance in case anyone fainted, Christie’s sold a portrait by Hans Holbein the Younger, a gold pendant with Queen Elizabeth’s profile, and other rarities. Only astute press commentators connected this sudden need for cash with a pending decision about the House of Morgan. As he had demonstrated after his father’s death, Jack was ready to pare his art collection ruthlessly if he needed to conserve the bank’s capital.
仿佛被绝望压得抬不起头来面对现实，Jack 一直向 Teddy Grenfell 保证修正案会通过。然而，1935 年初艺术品市场上那些神秘的操作，暴露了他内心深处的悲观。他以遗产税和整理财产为由，卖掉了六幅精美画作，售价 150 万美元。德国钢铁巨头 Fritz Thyssen 买走了 Domenico Ghirlandajo 为 Giovanna Tor-nabuoni 绘制的肖像，而大都会艺术博物馆则得到了 Fra Filippo Lippi 的一幅三联画和 Rubens 的《Anne of Austria》。通过伦敦的 Christie’s，Jack 拍卖了七箱他三十年来收藏的珍稀袖珍画。在七月闷热的天气里，还有护士在场以防有人晕倒，Christie’s 售出了一幅 Hans Holbein the Younger 的肖像、一枚带有伊丽莎白女王侧面像的金吊坠，以及其他珍品。只有敏锐的媒体评论员将这个突然的资金需求，与摩根家族悬而未决的命运联系了起来。 正如他在父亲去世后所展现的那样，如果为了保全银行资本，杰克会毫不留情地削减自己的艺术收藏。

Before J. P. Morgan and Company made its decision, a new arrangement was devised with Morgan Grenfell in June 1934 to comply with Glass-Steagall. The British house became a limited company in which the New York firm held a one-third stake. This was designed to buffer the new commercial bank, J. P. Morgan and Company, from British securities work, a structure preferred by the Bank of England as well. New York would now be a passive investor. “It was definitely a hands-off situation,” noted Tim Collins, a later Morgan Grenfell chairman.[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340549) There remained a close, familial feeling between 23 Wall and 23 Great Winchester, and the London *Times* called it only a “slight technical alteration.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340705) Yet for a firm that had always been subordinate to New York, the change represented a new level of British autonomy. It also occurred at a time when the City could no longer float huge foreign loans, as it had before the war, except for the British Empire. Morgan Grenfell and London’s other merchant banks concentrated instead upon securities and merger work for domestic companies.
在 J.P.摩根公司做出决定之前，1934 年 6 月与摩根格伦费尔达成了一项新安排，以符合格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案的要求。这家英国机构改组成一家有限公司，纽约公司持有其三分之一股份。此举旨在让这家新成立的商业银行——J.P.摩根公司——与英国的证券业务保持距离，这一结构也受到英格兰银行的青睐。纽约方面将转变为被动投资者。“这完全是一种放手不管的局面，”后来担任摩根格伦费尔董事长的蒂姆·柯林斯指出。 [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340549) 华尔街 23 号与温彻斯特街 23 号之间仍保持着亲密的家族式情感，《伦敦泰晤士报》称这仅仅是“技术上的细微调整”。 [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340705) 然而，对于一家向来从属于纽约的公司而言，这一变化意味着英国自主权提升到了新高度。此时正值伦敦金融城不再能像战前那样大规模发行巨额外国贷款（英帝国除外）的时期。 Morgan Grenfell 和伦敦的其他商人银行转而专注于为国内企业提供证券和并购业务。

In August 1935, Tom Lamont gathered the J. P. Morgan chieftains at his island farm off the Maine coast. The group included Morgan partners Leffingwell, Whitney, S. Parker Gilbert, and Harold Stanley, and Lansing Reed of Davis, Polk, and Wardwell. At this secret meeting, the House of Morgan decided irrevocably to remain a deposit bank and spin off an investment bank called Morgan Stanley. No minutes survive from this summit, thus leaving unanswered several essential questions. Why did Morgans—nonpareil of underwriters—opt for “commercial” rather than “investment” banking? Why the preference for deposits and loans rather than securities and brokering? Why an action that, in retrospect, seems a failure of vision?
1935 年 8 月，汤姆·拉蒙特在缅因州海岸外的私人岛屿农场上召集了摩根财团的核心人物。与会者包括摩根合伙人莱芬韦尔、惠特尼、S·帕克·吉尔伯特、哈罗德·斯坦利，以及戴维斯-波尔克-沃德韦尔律师事务所的兰辛·里德。在这次秘密会议上，摩根家族做出了不可逆转的决定：保留存款银行身份，同时剥离出一家名为摩根士丹利的投资银行。这次峰会的会议记录未能留存，因此几个关键问题至今悬而未决。为何作为承销领域翘楚的摩根选择"商业银行"而非"投资银行"？为何偏好存贷款业务而非证券与经纪业务？为何采取了这一事后看来缺乏远见的举措？

Fifty years later, the choice seems strange. Between 1919 and the Pecora hearings, Morgans had sponsored \$6 billion in securities for blue-chip companies and foreign governments. The Morgan cachet on bond issues brought in collateral banking business, such as the payment of dividends on bonds. As Russell Leffingwell had told Lamont, “I believe further that our securities business is a necessary feeder to our banking business, and that without it the banking business would in time dry up.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340852) Except for Brown Brothers Harriman, most distinguished partnerships—Kuhn, Loeb; Goldman, Sachs; and Lehman Brothers—opted for “investment banking” (a misnomer for the securities business the term denotes). The world of commercial banking—with its letters of credit, loans, foreign exchange, and stock-transfer work—seemed prosaic for a bank of such rarefied tastes and as active in secret diplomacy as J. P. Morgan and Company.
五十年后回顾，当初的选择似乎令人费解。从 1919 年到 Pecora 听证会期间，Morgans 为蓝筹公司和外国政府承销了 60 亿美元的证券。Morgans 在债券发行上的金字招牌，还能带来 collateral banking business（附属银行业务），例如债券股息支付。正如 Russell Leffingwell 对 Lamont 所言：“我深信，我们的证券业务是银行业务不可或缺的养分来源，一旦失去它，银行业务终将枯竭。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2340852) 除 Brown Brothers Harriman 外，大多数声名显赫的合伙制公司——Kuhn, Loeb、Goldman, Sachs 以及 Lehman Brothers——都选择了“investment banking”（这个用来指代证券业务的术语其实并不准确）。至于商业银行的世界，那些信用证、贷款、外汇和股票过户业务，对于 J. P. Morgan and Company 这样品味挑剔、暗地里又深涉外交斡旋的银行来说，未免显得过于平淡乏味了。

The choice was heavily influenced by the moribund state of the securities markets. Securities underwriting had become the firm’s least profitable activity, and new securities laws strapped underwriters with large potential liabilities. Stung by the preferred-list scandal, perhaps Jack Morgan favored commercial banking as more stable and consistently profitable than investment banking. In urging repeal of Glass-Steagall, Leffingwell wrote a letter to Roosevelt that shows the way in which securities work was regarded during the Depression:
这一选择深受证券市场奄奄一息状态的影响。证券承销已成为公司最不赚钱的业务，而新证券法又让承销商背负了巨大的潜在责任。或许是被优先股名单丑闻刺痛，J.P. 摩根更倾向于商业银行，认为它比投资银行更稳定、利润更持续。在敦促废除《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》时，莱芬韦尔给罗斯福写了一封信，信中揭示了在大萧条时期证券业务是如何被看待的：

> The business of underwriting capital issues is and should be a byproduct business. It is occasional and sporadic. Nobody can afford to be in the business unless he has a good bread and butter business to live on. A house exclusively in the underwriting business is under too much pressure to pay overhead and living expenses to pick and choose the issues it will underwrite.
> 资本发行承销业务是，也应该是，一种副产品业务。它时断时续，毫无规律。没有人能仅靠这项业务为生，除非他有稳定的饭碗业务作为支撑。一个纯粹从事承销业务的公司，面临支付日常开支和生活费用的巨大压力，根本难以精挑细选它愿意承销的项目。

> One reason why our record is so good . . . may be . . . that we have no salesmen, very little overhead attributable to the underwriting business and that we have a good bread and butter banking business. So we could and did say no to half of Europe and of South America.[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341055)
> 我们业绩如此出色的原因之一……可能在于……我们没有销售人员，承销业务相关的管理费用极低，并且我们拥有一项稳健的主营银行业务。因此，我们能够——也确实做到了——对半个欧洲和半个南美洲的业务说“不”。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341055)

This approach of keeping overhead low, not having salesmen, and selecting only prime clients would shape Morgan Stanley’s philosophy for the next forty-five years.
这种保持低成本、不设销售人员、只筛选优质客户的策略，塑造了摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）接下来四十五年的经营理念。

The human factor was undoubtedly also significant in choosing commercial banking. In 1935, about 20 percent of American workers were unemployed. It would have been hard to renounce the labor-intensive activities on the commercial-banking side. To have entered investment banking would have meant wholesale firings—an egregious betrayal for a paternalistic firm. Says an official Morgan history: “At the time of that decision the partnership, J.P. Morgan &. Co., had a staff of about 425 people. If the firm had chosen to remain solely in the securities business, a large part of this staff probably would have become surplus. . . . Approximately 400 people were busy in commercial banking and other activities and remained with the firm; about twenty left to form Morgan Stanley & Co.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341280)
人的因素无疑也很重要。1935 年，大约 20%的美国工人处于失业状态。要放弃商业银行方面劳动密集型的业务是很难的。如果选择进入投资银行业，那就意味着大规模裁员——对一家家长式管理的公司来说，这是极为严重的背弃行为。摩根官方历史记载：“做出那个决定时，合伙制企业 J.P. Morgan & Co.约有 425 名员工。如果公司选择只留在证券业务领域，那么其中大部分员工很可能会成为冗余人员……大约 400 人忙于商业银行业务及其他活动，并留在了公司；约有 20 人离开，成立了 Morgan Stanley & Co.”

There were also less benign motives. Morgan partners wanted to retain the option of someday recreating the House of Morgan without losing its clients. In late 1934, Lamont wrote to his partner Charles Steele: “We all feel, I think, that ways and means will be found to get us back into the securities business, either through the amendment of the existing laws or through *some separate corporate plan* or otherwise. We are considering all these matters now, but have by no means accepted the idea that . . . we are to be eliminated from the security business”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341473) (italics added). The reference to a separate corporate plan hints at Morgan Stanley’s genesis. Lamont, it seems, believed they could stay in securities without changes in Glass-Steagall, suggesting he had a trick or two up his sleeve.
此外，还有一些不那么良善的动机。摩根合伙人希望保留有朝一日重组摩根财团而不失客户的可能。1934 年末，拉蒙特在给合伙人查尔斯·斯蒂尔的信中写道：“我想我们都觉得，总能找到办法让我们重返证券业务，要么通过修订现有法律，要么通过某种独立公司计划，或者另辟蹊径。我们现在正考虑所有这些方案，但绝不接受……我们将被逐出证券业务的想法” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341473) （原文斜体）。所谓独立公司计划，暗示了摩根士丹利的诞生。看来拉蒙特相信，即便格拉斯-斯蒂高尔法案不做修改，他们也能留在证券行业，暗示他还有几招后手。

Both at 23 Wall and elsewhere, Morgan Stanley was regarded as a branch of the main trunk, a successor firm. As a cable to Morgan Gren-fell explained, “The fact it is a segregation is apparently well understood. At the same time, everyone looks to the new company to carry on the traditions of the firm.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341663) The House of Morgan probably wanted to create a firm that later, under a friendly Republican administration, could be cleanly folded back into J. P. Morgan and Company. Lamont may have recalled the birth of Bankers Trust as a “captive” bank, one that would politely return customers referred to them for trust business. If Morgans had chosen investment banking and released 90 percent of its staff, it would have been impossible to rebuild the House of Morgan if Glass-Steagall were rescinded.
无论是在华尔街 23 号还是在其他地方，摩根士丹利都被视为主干的一个分支，一家继承公司。正如一封发给摩根建富的电报所解释的那样："分立的事实显然已被广泛理解。与此同时，所有人都期待这家新公司能延续老公司的传统。" [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341663) 摩根财团可能希望创建一个后来在友好的共和党政府领导下能够干净地重新合并回 J.P.摩根公司的实体。拉蒙特或许还记得银行家信托作为一家"附属"银行的诞生——它会礼貌地将因信托业务而推荐来的客户归还。如果摩根选择了投资银行业务并释放了 90%的员工，那么一旦《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》被废除，重建摩根财团将变得不可能。

At four o’clock on the afternoon of September 5, 1935, the eve of Jack Morgan’s sixty-eighth birthday, the House of Morgan was officially divided. Lamont, Whitney, and Stanley stood before the fireplace at the end of the long narrow partners’ room, below the oil portrait of Pierpont. They announced to two dozen newsmen that several people from the Morgan Bond Department would leave to form Morgan Stanley. The new firm would have three J. P. Morgan partners—Harold Stanley, who had joined the firm in late 1927, after Dwight Morrow was appointed ambassador to Mexico; Jack’s younger son, Harry; and William Ewing—and two Drexel partners, Perry Hall and Edward H. York. Lamont said the new firm would conduct a securities business “of the character formerly handled by our firm.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341897)
1935 年 9 月 5 日下午四时，在杰克·摩根六十八岁生日的前夕，摩根财团正式分拆。拉蒙特、惠特尼和斯坦利站在狭长合伙人房间尽头的壁炉前，壁炉上方挂着皮尔庞特的油画肖像。他们向二十多名记者宣布，摩根债券部门的部分成员将离职成立摩根士丹利。新公司将包含三位 J. P. 摩根合伙人——哈罗德·斯坦利（于 1927 年末德怀特·莫罗被任命为驻墨西哥大使后加入公司）、杰克的小儿子哈里、威廉·尤因——以及两位德雷克塞尔合伙人佩里·霍尔和爱德华·H. 约克。拉蒙特表示，新公司将开展“此前由我们公司经手的那类”证券业务。 [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2341897)

Jack and Harry Morgan were absent from the conference, refusing to forgo the pleasures of grouse shooting, even on this historic occasion. It was a mournful moment. One reporter, noting the solemn faces, observed, “The segregation of the old firm was taken as seriously as a separation of any private family.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342041) Yet however lugubrious for Morgans, the new Morgan Stanley was cheered as a sign of returning prosperity, a tonic to Wall Street’s mood.
杰克和哈里·摩根缺席了会议，即便是在这个历史性时刻，他们也不愿放弃猎松鸡的乐趣。那是个令人悲叹的时刻。一名记者注意到众人神色凝重，评论道：“这家老字号公司的分离，就像任何一个私人家庭的分裂一样被严肃对待。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342041) 然而，尽管这对摩根家族而言令人黯然神伤，但新的摩根士丹利却被视为繁荣回归的征兆，成为华尔街情绪的强心剂。

Morgan Stanley looked less a distant cousin than a richly endowed stepson of J. P. Morgan and Company, which financed it almost fully. Morgan Stanley officers owned virtually all the \$500,000 of common stock, and they retained voting control. But the real start-up capital was \$7 million in nonvoting preferred stock, \$6.6 million of it held by J. P. Morgan partners. Jack and his family held about 50 percent of the preferred shares; Tom Lamont and his family, over 40 percent. Small wonder the new offshoot caused grumbling from some competitors, a feeling that J. P. Morgan and Company had honored the letter, but violated the spirit, of Glass-Steagall.
摩根士丹利与其说像是 J.P.摩根公司的远房表亲，不如说更像是它财大气粗的干儿子——几乎完全由后者出资成立。摩根士丹利高管几乎持有了全部 50 万美元的普通股，并保留了投票控制权。但真正的启动资金是 700 万美元的无投票权优先股，其中 660 万美元由 J.P.摩根的合伙人持有。杰克及其家族持有约 50%的优先股；汤姆·拉蒙特及其家族持有超过 40%。难怪这家新分支引来部分竞争对手的怨言，他们认为 J.P.摩根公司虽遵守了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的字面要求，却违背了其精神实质。

On September 16, 1935, Morgan Stanley opened for business at 2 Wall Street, about a hundred yards from 23 Wall. The office of this splendid bastard had a view of the Trinity Church steeple and its decor evoked its blue-blooded parentage. Prints of old New York adorned the walls, and signature rolltop desks were lined up in the manner of 23 Wall. It had something of the mood of a J. P. Morgan branch office. “My first job in the bank was going to Morgan Stanley,” recalled Ellmore C. Patterson, later chairman of Morgan Guaranty. “They were short-handed. It got very busy and they borrowed two of us for about a year.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342193)
1935 年 9 月 16 日，摩根士丹利在华尔街 2 号正式开业，距离华尔街 23 号约一百码之遥。这家卓越的“私生子”公司的办公室可以看到三一教堂的尖顶，其装饰风格透露出蓝血血统的传承。墙上挂着老纽约的版画，标志性的卷盖式办公桌也按照华尔街 23 号的样式排列。整个氛围颇有几分 J.P.摩根分支机构的味道。后来担任摩根保证信托公司董事长的埃尔莫尔·C·帕特森回忆道：“我在银行的第一份工作就是去摩根士丹利帮忙。他们当时人手不足，业务非常繁忙，就把我们两个人借调了大约一年。”

September 16 was one of the most bizarre opening days in American business history: it didn’t resemble that of a new business. The night before, Perry Hall asked the janitor to set out a table in case anybody sent flowers. When he reported to work, he found two hundred floral displays. “In fact, the entire length of our office was just one row after another of these magnificent flowers in vases. . . . Nearly every one of them was from our competitors and associates on the Street.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342358) One reporter said it resembled a flower show. The *New York Times* noted the eerie sense of continuity: “The inauguration of business proceeded as if it were just the beginning of another week in any old-established firm.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342539) One Morgan Stanley legend, perhaps apocryphal, claims that so many companies came for business the first week that when one utility chairman arrived to discuss financing, Stanley said, “Tell him to come back next week.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342693)
9 月 16 日是美国商业史上最离奇的开业日之一：它一点也不像新公司的开业。前一天晚上，Perry Hall 让门卫摆一张桌子，万一有人送花。当他来上班时，发现了两百个花篮。“实际上，我们办公室整个长度就是一排接一排这些精美花瓶里的花……几乎每一束都来自我们在华尔街的竞争对手和同行。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342358) 一位记者说这简直像个花展。《纽约时报》注意到了那种奇特的连续性：“业务的开张就像任何老牌公司又一周的开始。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342539) 一个关于 Morgan Stanley 的传说（也许是杜撰的）声称，第一周前来洽谈业务的公司太多了，以至于当一家公用事业公司董事长来讨论融资时，Stanley 说：“让他下周再来。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342693)

At the new firm, the leading personalities were recognizable Morgan types. The press, as if covering the debut of a new country club, showed them golfing or emerging from the surf. Harold Stanley, a utility-bond expert, was handsome and distinguished looking with thick, prematurely gray hair, a long face, and steady eyes. Now nearly fifty, he was president and senior statesman of the firm and a figure of enormous stature on Wall Street.
在新公司里，核心人物都是典型的摩根式人物。媒体仿佛在报道新成立的乡村俱乐部的首秀，展示着他们打高尔夫或从海浪中走出的照片。哈罗德·斯坦利，一位公用事业债券专家，相貌英俊、气质出众，有着浓密而早白的头发、长脸和沉稳的眼神。如今他年近五十，是公司的总裁兼资深元老，在华尔街享有极高的声望。

Son of a General Electric engineer—the inventor of the Thermos bottle—Stanley had a very Morgan pedigree: he was a Massachusetts Episcopalian, a star hockey and baseball player at Yale, and a Skull and Bones member. He owned a home in Greenwich, Connecticut, and an apartment on Sutton Place. As a negotiator, he was tough and stubborn but honest. “While others banged conference tables he sat shyly by; but he seldom budged in an argument,” reported *Newsweek.*[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342860) Coolly diplomatic, he was well suited for the political attacks that would shadow Morgan Stanley for twenty years.
通用电气工程师之子——热水瓶的发明者——斯坦利的摩根血统纯正：他是马萨诸塞州圣公会教徒，在耶鲁大学是曲棍球和棒球明星，也是骷髅会成员。他在康涅狄格州格林尼治有栋房子，在萨顿坊有套公寓。作为谈判者，他强硬固执但诚实正直。“别人拍桌子争论时，他羞怯地坐在一旁；但辩论中他几乎从不让步。”《新闻周刊》报道说。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2342860) 他沉着冷静、长于外交，这让他足以应对未来二十年笼罩摩根士丹利的政治攻击。

Absent from the opening-day festivities was the firm’s new treasurer, Harry Morgan, thirty-five, then returning from England aboard a cruise ship. Harry’s aloofness from the affairs of the firm foreshadowed later developments. Yet it would be important for the new firm to have not only the Morgan name and money, but a real, breathing Morgan on the premises.
开业庆典当天，公司新财务主管哈里·摩根却缺席——这位三十五岁的年轻人当时正乘邮轮从英国返航。哈里对公司事务的疏离态度，预示了后来的发展。不过，对于这家新公司而言，拥有的不只是摩根的名号和资金，更要有一位活生生的摩根坐镇现场，这至关重要。

Harry Morgan had shiny, pomaded hair, a sharp chin, and electrically intense eyes. He was brusque and aggressive like his grandfather and with some of Pierpont’s flamboyance. He bought up the Eaton’s Neck peninsula on the North Shore and commuted by seaplane to Wall Street. As had happened with Jack in relation to Pierpont during the Pujo hearings, Harry became deeply embittered by the Pecora hearings’ treatment of his father. It would be the formative event of his life, making him fanatically private and aloof from any public role other than in the world of yachting. It would likewise make Morgan Stanley far less prone than the old House of Morgan to dabble in politics or seek publicity. In 1935, the press portrayed Harry as the real heir to the Morgan business talents, and he shone in comparison with his more languid, gentle older brother, Junius, who remained with J. P. Morgan and Company. Harry would function more as the conscience of Morgan Stanley, the custodian of its traditions, than as a day-to-day executive. He also had important business contacts through his friendship with European banking families, including the Wallenbergs and the Ham-bros. He explained his role thus: “My father, as my grandfather got older, brought into his firm some very brilliant and desirable new partners. He built a team, and he was immensely successful in doing that and in acting as a moderator and team captain. In many ways when this firm was started, I thought that there was a place for me to act in such a capacity.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343012)
哈里·摩根有一头油光发亮的头发，下巴尖削，目光如电。他像祖父一样粗鲁好斗，还带着几分皮尔庞特式的浮夸。他买下了北岸的伊顿颈半岛，每天乘坐水上飞机往返华尔街。如同杰克在普约听证会上对皮尔庞特的态度一样，哈里因佩科拉听证会对其父亲的对待而深感愤懑。这成为他生命中的决定性事件，使他变得极度注重隐私，除了在游艇界，他疏离于任何公共角色。这也让摩根士丹利远不如老摩根银行那样愿意涉足政治或追求曝光。1935 年，媒体将哈里描绘成摩根商业才华的真正继承者，与留在 J.P.摩根公司的更为懒散温和的兄长朱尼厄斯相比，他光彩照人。 Harry 更像是 Morgan Stanley 的良心和传统守护者，而非日常执行者。他还通过与欧洲银行家族的友谊，包括 Wallenbergs 和 Ham-bros 家族，拥有了重要的业务联系。他这样解释自己的角色：“随着我祖父年事渐高，我父亲引入了一些极其出色且令人向往的新合伙人。他组建了一个团队，并在充当调解者和队长的角色上取得了巨大成功。在很多方面，当这家公司创立时，我认为也有一个位置让我扮演类似的角色。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343012)

The founders of Morgan Stanley romanticized their early days, stressing the perils they braved. “We were going out into a rough sea in a little tiny rowboat,” said Perry Hall. “We didn’t know how we would be received.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343194) They were, in fact, received like members of a Renaissance court in exile. There were many links between J. P. Morgan and the new firm. Morgan Stanley closings—that is, payments for and deliveries of securities—took place at 23 Wall. And George Whitney, acting as “family physician” to clients, steered them to Morgan Stanley. Morgan Stanley started out with only a few spillover deals from the Corner—but what deals! George Whitney steered such clients as Wendell Willkie, chairman of Consumers Power, to Harold Stanley; before September 1935 was over, Morgan Stanley had its first big electric utility issue. Early in the summer, Walter Gifford of AT&T had asked Stanley about rumors that Morgan partners would form a securities firm. When Stanley confirmed this, Gifford said, “That solves my problem.” He then put on his hat and left.[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343352) AT&T needed to do some new financing, and the Securities and Exchange Commission was eager for AT&T to return to capital markets to prove their health under the new regulations. In a historic issue for Illinois Bell Telephone, Morgan Stanley published the first newspaper prospectus conforming to New Deal securities laws, following consultations in Washington between Stanley and SEC chairman Joseph P. Kennedy.
摩根士丹利的创始人常将创业初期浪漫化，强调他们曾冒的风险。佩里·霍尔说：“我们像坐着一叶小舟驶向惊涛骇浪，完全不知前路如何。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343194) 事实上，他们受到的待遇如同流亡的文艺复兴宫廷贵族。J.P.摩根公司与新公司之间存在着千丝万缕的联系。摩根士丹利的交割业务——即证券的付款与交付——最初都在华尔街 23 号进行。而乔治·惠特尼作为客户的“家庭医生”，将他们引荐至摩根士丹利。起初，摩根士丹利仅从“街角”承接了一些零散业务——但都是何等重量级的交易！乔治·惠特尼将消费者电力公司董事长温德尔·威尔基这样的客户引荐给了哈罗德·斯坦利；1935 年 9 月尚未结束，摩根士丹利便完成了首笔大型电力公用事业发行。初夏时节，AT&T 的沃尔特·吉福德曾向斯坦利打听摩根合伙人是否真要组建证券公司的传闻。 当斯坦利确认后，吉福德说：“那我的问题就解决了。”他随即戴上帽子离开。 [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343352) AT&T 需要进行新一轮融资，而证券交易委员会迫切希望 AT&T 重返资本市场，以证明它们在新规下的健康运营。在摩根士丹利为伊利诺伊贝尔电话公司发行的历史性债券中，斯坦利与 SEC 主席约瑟夫·P·肯尼迪在华盛顿协商后，发布了首份符合新政证券法的报纸招股说明书。

Despite predictions from the House of Morgan that the New Deal would kill capital markets, they boomed in 1935, and underwritings jumped fourfold. In its first year of operation, Morgan Stanley handled an astounding \$1 billion in issues, sweeping a quarter of the market. *Forbes* hailed a wonder: “Most firms, institutions and companies start off modestly. Unique is the record of Morgan Stanley & Co. . . . never remotely approached by any other newly created organization.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343516) The firm originated issues but generally wouldn’t participate in others’ issues. SEC rules limited the size of underwriting stakes relative to a firm’s capital, and so syndicates grew huge. On telephone issues, Morgan Stanley might marshal up to one hundred underwriters and five hundred or six hundred distributors. Its power to exclude firms from issues made it feared. Gradually much of J. P. Morgan’s clientele—lovingly referred to as the Franchise—shifted to the new firm. By the late 1930s, New York Central, AT&T, General Motors, Johns-Manville, Du Pont, U.S. Steel, and Standard Oil of New Jersey, as well as the governments of Argentina and Canada, had come for securities work. Morgan Stanley was strong in the same areas as the pre-Glass-Steagall unified bank—utilities, telephone companies, railroads, heavy industry, mining, and foreign governments.
尽管摩根家族预测新政会扼杀资本市场，但 1935 年市场却一片繁荣，承销业务量激增四倍。摩根士丹利成立首年便惊人地处理了价值 10 亿美元的发行项目，一举拿下四分之一的市场份额。《福布斯》盛赞这一奇迹："大多数公司、机构和企业起步时都谨小慎微。摩根士丹利公司的记录独一无二……任何新创组织都望尘莫及。" [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343516) 这家公司自己主导发行项目，但通常不参与其他公司的发行。美国证券交易委员会的规定限制了承销份额相对于公司资本的比例，因此银团规模变得十分庞大。在电话业务发行中，摩根士丹利可能召集多达一百家承销商和五六百家分销商。它有权将某些公司排除在发行项目之外，这使其令人畏惧。渐渐地，J.P.摩根的大量客户——被亲切地称为"特许经营权"——转移到了这家新公司。 到 20 世纪 30 年代末，纽约中央铁路、AT&T、通用汽车、约翰斯-曼维尔、杜邦、美国钢铁、新泽西标准石油，以及阿根廷和加拿大政府，都来找摩根士丹利做证券业务。摩根士丹利在《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》出台前摩根联合银行擅长的领域同样强势——公用事业、电话公司、铁路、重工业、矿业和外国政府。

The rest of Wall Street assumed that Morgan Stanley had inherited its parent’s mantle of authority. Charles Blyth and his partner, Charles Mitchell, sought to ingratiate themselves with the new leaders. “Our main job is to get under the covers and as close to them as possible,” Blyth told Mitchell.[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343662) To cultivate Morgan Stanley, he suggested opening an account at J. P. Morgan and Company. “It is true our account won’t be very important,” he told Blyth, ”. . . but it would show that our hearts are in the right place.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343902) Such was the persisting faith in any firm bearing the Morgan name.
华尔街其余人认为摩根士丹利继承了其母公司的权威衣钵。查尔斯·布莱斯和他的合伙人查尔斯·米切尔试图讨好新领导人。"我们的主要任务就是钻进被窝，尽可能贴近他们，"布莱斯对米切尔说。 [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343662) 为了拉拢摩根士丹利，他建议在 J.P.摩根公司开设账户。"确实，我们的账户不会很重要，"他告诉布莱斯，"……但这表明我们的心是向着他们的。" [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2343902) 这就是人们对任何冠以摩根之名的公司始终抱有的信念。

For New Deal reformers, it was also hard to believe that J. P. Morgan and Company didn’t lurk somewhere in the shadows. That Morgan Stanley assumed so many former J. P. Morgan clients bred suspicion. One powerful enemy who resolutely tracked Morgan Stanley conquests was Interior Secretary Harold L. Ickes. After the firm’s formation, he wrote in his diary, “Meanwhile, taking advantage of the depression, the Morgan people have extended their financial domination. Ordered to put a stop to the underwriting business of their bank, they have organized a separate company which is doing even more business than was done by the bank itself along this line.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344036) Ickes and other enemies bided their time. But they would soon strike back in a sustained attack, through Congress and the courts.
对于新政改革者来说，J·P·摩根公司没有在幕后操纵实在是难以置信。摩根士丹利接手了那么多 J·P·摩根的客户，这自然引发了猜疑。内政部长哈罗德·L·伊克斯是坚决追踪摩根士丹利扩张脚步的劲敌之一。该公司成立后，他在日记中写道：“与此同时，摩根家族趁经济萧条之机，进一步扩大了他们的金融掌控。他们被要求停止自己银行的承销业务，便另组了一家公司，而这家公司在这方面的业务量甚至超过了银行原本的规模。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2344036) 伊克斯和其他对手们暂且按兵不动，但他们很快就会通过国会和法院发起持续的反击。

For J. P. Morgan partners down the street, the sudden boom in the securities market was bitterly ironic, for the parent firm was sleepy in the late 1930s. Almost the entire bank was squeezed into 23 Wall, with some scattered offices in 15 Broad Street, next door. With \$430 million in total resources, J. P. Morgan and Company still ranked as the biggest private bank in the world. But Glass-Steagall had meant more than a loss of business, money, and power. It robbed the bank of some ineffable mystery that had surrounded it. With the Pecora hearings, the bank had published a balance sheet for the first time. Now the firm had to publish statements and submit to government examination. Likewise in London, Monty Norman asked Teddy Grenfell for the firm’s balance sheet for the first time in 1936. Slowly, gradually, the gentleman bankers’ world was being bureaucratized, and the financiers were emerging, dazed and blinking, into unaccustomed sunlight.
对于街角的 J. P. 摩根合伙人来说，证券市场的突然繁荣充满了苦涩的讽刺，因为这家母公司在 20 世纪 30 年代末期显得昏昏欲睡。几乎整个银行都挤在华尔街 23 号，只有少数几个办公室分散在隔壁的宽街 15 号。凭借 4.3 亿美元的总资产，J. P. 摩根公司依然是全球最大的私人银行。但《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》带来的不仅仅是业务、金钱和权力的损失，它还夺走了银行周围那种难以言喻的神秘感。随着佩科拉听证会的举行，银行首次公布了资产负债表。如今，公司必须发布财务报表并接受政府审查。同样在伦敦，蒙蒂·诺曼在 1936 年首次向泰迪·格伦费尔索要公司的资产负债表。慢慢地，逐渐地，绅士银行家的世界正在被官僚化，金融家们昏头转向地、眨着眼睛，走进了不习惯的阳光之中。
