# Chapter Twenty-Three: Hostages

ON June 22, 1940, the new French Premier, Marshal Henri Philippe Petain, submitted to the Nazi *blitzkrieg* and signed an armistice with Hitler, leaving Britain to fight alone against the Axis powers. This left Morgan et Compagnie in a vulnerable situation. The stately *Banque Morgan*, as the French termed it, occupied an imposing mansion at 14 place Vendôme, its marble banking floor illuminated by a huge skylight. Established by the Drexels in 1868, the firm had an illustrious heritage, having stayed open during the Franco-Prussian War and World War I. It was known as Morgan, Harjes until 1926, when partner Herman Harjes died in a Deauville polo accident and the name was changed to Morgan et Compagnie. Under the interlocking partnership structure of the pre-Glass-Steagall Morgan empire, Jack Morgan was senior partner, and New York had provided most of the bank’s capital.
1940 年 6 月 22 日，新任法国总理，亨利·菲利普·贝当元帅，向纳粹的闪电战屈服，与希特勒签署了停战协议，留下英国独自对抗轴心国。这一局势让摩根公司（Morgan et Compagnie）陷入了岌岌可危的境地。被法国人称为“庄严的摩根银行”坐落于旺多姆广场 14 号一栋气势恢宏的公馆内，其大理石营业厅上方是一个巨大的天窗。这家公司由德雷克塞尔家族于 1868 年创立，拥有辉煌的历史，曾在普法战争和第一次世界大战期间坚持营业。1926 年之前，它名为“摩根·哈杰斯公司”，直到合伙人赫尔曼·哈杰斯在多维尔的一场马球事故中去世，才更名为“摩根公司”。在《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》之前，摩根帝国采用连锁合伙结构，杰克·摩根是资深合伙人，而纽约方面提供了该银行的大部分资本。

If Morgan et Compagnie never achieved quite the renown of the New York and London banks, it still ranked as one of the largest foreign financial institutions in Paris even in the 1980s. It was a conduit for dealings between the French government and J. P. Morgan and Company and was very close to the Banque de France. Its French officers had often held high government posts. Morgan et Compagnie served the subsidiaries of most American companies in France, provided traveler’s checks and letters of credit for rich American tourists, handled currency transactions for Americans in France, and had vaults brimming with securities owned by Americans and Frenchmen. It exchanged young apprentices with Morgan Grenfell and J. P. Morgan. Yet in the last analysis, the French house was always stymied by Morgan intimacy with Great Britain and France’s nationalistic resistance to American business penetration.
如果摩根公司从未像纽约和伦敦的银行那样声名显赫，但即使在 20 世纪 80 年代，它仍堪称巴黎最大的外国金融机构之一。它是法国政府与 J. P. Morgan and Company 之间的交易渠道，并与法兰西银行关系密切。其法国高管常出身政府要职。摩根公司为大多数美国公司在法国的子公司提供服务，为富裕的美国游客提供旅行支票和信用证，处理在法美国人的货币兑换业务，其金库中存放着美法两国客户的大量证券。它还定期与 Morgan Grenfell 和 J. P. Morgan 交流年轻学徒。然而归根结底，这家法国事务所始终受制于摩根与英国的亲密关系，以及法国对美国商业扩张的民族主义抵抗。

So total was the wartime news blackout that an account of what happened to Morgan et Compagnie during the Nazi occupation wasn’t known until September 1944; it can now be reconstructed from unpublished memoirs at Morgan Guaranty. The story begins with the Banque de France, which didn’t trust the phony peace promulgated by the Munich Pact and in 1938 began making plans to protect its gold. It shipped gold to New York as a fund for future war purchases and took gold bullion stored at its vaults on the rue de la Vrilliere and distributed it to fifty-one strategic sites around the country.
战时新闻封锁如此彻底，以至于摩根公司在纳粹占领期间的遭遇直到 1944 年 9 月才为人所知；如今，根据摩根担保公司未出版的回忆录，这段历史得以还原。故事始于法兰西银行，它并不相信慕尼黑协定所粉饰的虚假和平，早在 1938 年就开始制定保护黄金的计划。该行将黄金运往纽约，作为未来战争采购的储备资金，同时把存放在弗里利埃街金库中的金条取出，分发到全国 51 个战略地点。

Many Parisian banks made similar contingency plans. Morgan et Compagnie bought a run-down hotel in Niort, a town southeast of Nantes. It was redesigned as a self-sufficient unit for protecting securities, with safes in the basement and sleeping quarters upstairs for staff. After war was declared, the French government advised Morgans to set up an office in Chatel-Guyon, in unoccupied France, to protect its exchange dealings with the Banque de France. Several weeks before the Nazis stormed Paris, Morgans and other banks shipped out securities to these safe houses in south-central France and left behind skeleton staffs in Paris. Then, five days before France fell, two American partners of Morgan et Compagnie, Bernard S. Carter and Julian Allen, fled Paris along roads swollen with refugees and clogged with horse-drawn carts and bicycles. All of these acts proved wise precautions.
许多巴黎银行都制定了类似的应急计划。摩根公司买下了南特东南部小镇尼奥尔的一家破旧旅馆，将其改造为自给自足的证券保管中心——地下室设有保险库，楼上则配备员工宿舍。战争爆发后，法国政府建议摩根公司在未被占领的沙泰尔吉永设立办事处，以保障其与法兰西银行的汇兑业务。纳粹攻陷巴黎的数周前，摩根及其他银行已将证券转移至法国中南部的这些安全据点，仅留下留守人员在巴黎。随后，在法国沦陷前五天，摩根公司的两位美国合伙人伯纳德·S·卡特与朱利安·艾伦，沿着挤满难民、堵塞着马车与自行车的大道逃离了巴黎。这些举措事后都被证明是明智的预防之策。

During the German occupation, the Nazi flag flew over the Justice Ministry and the Ritz Hôtel, Morgan et Compagnie’s neighbors on the place Vendôme. Three of the American banks with Paris branches—J. P. Morgan, Guaranty Trust, and Chase National—stayed open, while the fourth—National City—shut down. In late June 1940, Leonard Rist of Morgan et Compagnie was arrested and dispatched to a German prison camp in the Sudetenland. Rist was the son of an eminent French economist, Charles Rist, and had been personally recruited by Jack Morgan. When Leonard was in New York in 1928, he recalled, Jack “asked me what the hell I was doing in any other place than Morgan’s in exactly those words; whereupon I decided to apply for a job at Morgan’s in Paris.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370536)
德国占领期间，纳粹旗帜悬挂在司法部和丽兹酒店上空，而摩根公司（Morgan et Compagnie）在旺多姆广场的邻居们则目睹了这一切。四家在巴黎设有分行的美国银行中，J. P. Morgan、Guaranty Trust 和 Chase National 继续营业，而第四家 National City 则关闭了。1940 年 6 月下旬，摩根公司的伦纳德·里斯特（Leonard Rist）被捕，并被送往苏台德地区的一座德国战俘营。里斯特是著名法国经济学家查尔斯·里斯特（Charles Rist）的儿子，由杰克·摩根（Jack Morgan）亲自招募。伦纳德回忆说，1928 年他在纽约时，杰克“用原话问我，除了摩根公司，我到底在别的地方干什么；于是，我决定申请巴黎摩根公司的工作。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370536)

Rist ended up spending eighteen months behind barbed wire while his parents worked out emergency plans to secure a Wall Street job for their younger son through Russell Leffingwell. The House of Morgan finally sprang Leonard through their old Vatican friend, Bernardino Nogara, the treasurer of the Special Administration of the Holy See. Nogara somehow convinced the Germans that Rist’s release was needed to maintain French financial health. The combined force of the Morgan mystique and Rist’s reputation was such that the argument worked. After the war, the French Treasury assigned Leonard Rist to the World Bank, and he ended up as head of its Economic Department.
里斯特最终在铁丝网后度过了十八个月，期间他的父母通过拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔紧急制定了让次子进入华尔街工作的计划。摩根财团则借助他们在梵蒂冈的老朋友、圣座特别管理署财务主管贝尔纳迪诺·诺加拉的关系，最终让伦纳德获得释放。诺加拉设法让德国人相信，释放里斯特对维持法国金融健康至关重要。摩根财团的神秘光环与里斯特个人声望的双重作用，竟使这一说辞奏效了。战后，法国财政部将伦纳德·里斯特分配至世界银行工作，他最终担任了该机构经济部门负责人。

For the rest of the war, executive control of Morgan et Compagnie fell to two stubborn, courageous Frenchmen—the elegant Maurice Pesson-Didion, a veteran wounded in the Battle of the Marne, and Louis Tute-leers, chief of the Credit Department, who limped from a wartime injury suffered while serving in the Belgian army. The two bankers had to contend with constant, hovering Nazi interference and menacing surveillance of their activities. To finance his conquests, Hitler set a policy of plundering gold and foreign currency from banks in occupied territories. As part of his revenge for Versailles, he chose to extort money from France. Like other banks, Morgan et Compagnie could conduct business in franc accounts, but foreign-exchange transactions were outlawed. The bank had to apprise the Germans of any foreign currency it held as well as any property in safe deposit boxes.
战争余下的岁月里，摩根公司的管理权落到了两位顽强勇敢的法国人手中——优雅的莫里斯·佩松-迪迪翁，一位在马恩河战役中负伤的老兵，以及信贷部主管路易·蒂特莱尔，他在比利时军队服役时因战争受伤而跛行。这两位银行家不得不应对纳粹持续不断的干涉和对其活动的威胁性监视。为了资助他的征服行动，希特勒制定了从占领区银行掠夺黄金和外币的政策。作为对《凡尔赛条约》报复的一部分，他选择向法国勒索钱财。与其他银行一样，摩根公司可以用法郎账户开展业务，但外汇交易被禁止。银行必须向德国人通报其持有的任何外币以及保险箱中的任何财产。

The House of Morgan has always been proud that it operated in Nazi-occupied Paris without compromising its principles. Yet the bank may have had a secret patron: Marshal Petain, head of the collaborationist Vichy government. As a celebrated war hero in 1917, Petain had associated with many fund-raising society ladies, including Herman Harjes’s wife and Anne Morgan. It was perhaps through such meetings that he came to have an account at Morgan et Compagnie. This embarrassing account was disclosed in November 1941, during a boisterous debate in the British House of Commons. It came out that Petain had signed an annuity plan with a Canadian company in 1937; even after the fall of France and the British blockade, the Canadian company duly paid £600 annually to Morgan Grenfell, which then credited Petain’s account at Morgan et Compagnie. The transfers were sanctioned by a British Treasury license.
摩根家族一向引以为傲的是，他们在纳粹占领的巴黎经营业务时，始终未曾背弃自己的原则。然而，这家银行或许暗藏着一位秘密客户：维希傀儡政府的首脑贝当元帅。1917 年身为赫赫战功的英雄时，贝当曾与众多筹款社交名媛往来，其中就包括赫尔曼·哈耶斯的夫人和安妮·摩根。或许正是通过这些会面，他最终在摩根公司开设了账户。这个令人难堪的账户于 1941 年 11 月在英国下议院一场激烈的辩论中被曝光。原来贝当早在 1937 年便与一家加拿大公司签署了年金计划；即便在法国沦陷和英国封锁之后，这家加拿大公司仍每年按时向摩根建富银行支付 600 英镑，再由该银行记入贝当在摩根公司的账户。而这一切转账，竟得到了英国财政部的许可。

In the House of Commons, Dr. Russell Thomas protested to Chancellor of the Exchequer Kingsley Wood, “Will the right honorable gentleman consider that the payment tends to irritate the public temper, lowers the prestige of the Government, and opens up avenues of suspicion at a time when national unity is essential?”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370670) In defending the transfers, Kingsley Wood noted that Canada had maintained diplomatic relations with Vichy France and that Pétain was a head of state. Nevertheless, at some point, the transfers were stopped.
在下议院，Dr. Russell Thomas 向 Chancellor of the Exchequer Kingsley Wood 抗议：“尊敬的先生是否考虑过，这种支付倾向于激怒公众情绪、降低政府威望，并在民族团结至关重要的时刻打开猜疑的通道？” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370670) 在为其转移辩护时，Kingsley Wood 指出加拿大与 Vichy France 保持着外交关系，且 Pétain 是国家元首。尽管如此，这些转移在某个时刻被叫停了。

After Pearl Harbor, Morgan et Compagnie was branded an enemy bank and assigned a special German overseer, Herr Caesar, who operated out of 18 place Vendôme. He insisted that the firm accept accounts from Nazi banks and businesses. To avoid this indignity, Pesson-Didion informed the Nazis that J. P. Morgan and Company had instructed him not to accept new accounts or expand old ones; if forced to break this rule, he said bluntly, he would have to liquidate the bank. This prearranged strategy worked, and the bank took no Nazi deposits.
珍珠港事件后，Morgan et Compagnie 被定性为敌国银行，并被指派了一位特殊的德国监督人 Herr Caesar，他的办公地点在旺多姆广场 18 号。他坚持要求该行接收纳粹银行和企业的账户。为了免于这种屈辱，Pesson-Didion 告知纳粹，J. P. Morgan and Company 已指示他不得接受新账户或扩展现有账户；如果被迫违反这一规定，他直言不讳地说，他将不得不清算这家银行。这一事先商定的策略奏效了，该银行最终没有接收任何纳粹存款。

With Jewish accounts, Morgan et Compagnie had less success. The Nazis had assembled a special administrative team to ransack Jewish securities and accounts. At Morgans and other Paris banks, they emptied the accounts and safety deposit boxes of Jewish clients and looted Fr 11.5 million in all. Morgans lodged protests to no avail. It seems doubtful whether any bank could have operated during the occupation if it had resisted these efforts too strenuously.
而在犹太账户方面，Morgan et Compagnie 就没那么走运了。纳粹组建了一个专门的行政团队，旨在洗劫犹太人的证券和账户。在摩根以及其他巴黎银行，他们清空了犹太客户的账户和保险箱，总共掠夺了 1150 万法郎。摩根方面提出了抗议，但无济于事。在占领期间，如果任何银行过于激烈地抵制这些行动，它是否还能继续运营，这似乎很值得怀疑。

The most dramatic encounter with the Nazis occurred in 1944, when a Defense Corps—SS—officer marched into the bank and demanded money kept by a certain depositor. When the redoubtable Tuteleers resisted, the officer drew a gun and shoved it in his back, forcing him to limp out into the street. Tuteleers and Leonard Rist were taken to SS headquarters on the rue des Saussaies and informed that unless the depositor’s money were promptly handed over, they would be sent to a German prison camp. Prodded with gun butts and sequestered for an hour or two in a dark broom closet, the two were released when the \$8,000 ransom was paid.
与纳粹最戏剧性的一次交锋发生在 1944 年，当时一名党卫军军官闯入银行，要求取出某位储户存放的钱款。当勇猛的图特勒尔拒绝时，军官拔出手枪抵住他的后背，迫使他一瘸一拐地走到街上。图特勒尔和莱昂纳德·里斯特被带到索赛街的党卫军总部，并被告知若不立即交出储户的钱款，他们将被送往德国集中营。在枪托的推搡下，两人被关进一个黑暗的扫帚间长达一两个小时，直到支付了 8000 美元赎金后才获释。

On another occasion, defying threats of prison or deportation, Maurice Pesson-Didion refused to hand over some French Treasury bills. A Gestapo officer then demanded to see a list of securities owned by Morgan et Compagnie and was incredulous that aside from government securities, the bank owned so little. Evidently imbued with a sense of mythical Morgan power, he swore that Pesson-Didion must be mocking him and the Reich. Citing supposed Morgan influence over other French firms, the officer expected to find lists of huge holdings of Credit Lyonnais and other bank stocks. He wouldn’t abandon the belief that the House of Morgan held reams of French bank shares. Lamont later retold the story of what the German officer had said: ” ’If they did not, how in the world were they able to control all the banks?’ Pesson-Didion replied they held none. Then the German official asked him to explain ’the immense influence which the Morgan firms seemed to have all over the Continent and everywhere.’ Pesson-Didion replied quietly that he could think of no explanation unless it lay in the character of the men who made up the Morgan institutions.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370820) Lamont may have embellished the tale, but Morgan et Compagnie doubtless exercised less influence in reality than in the overheated minds of Nazi officialdom. There was always a mistaken sense that the House of Morgan principally exerted power through direct-share ownership in companies rather than through exclusive banking and advisory relations. With the full panoply of J. P. Morgan and Company power behind it, Morgan et Compagnie didn’t need vast capital resources.
另一次，莫里斯·佩松-迪迪翁不顾坐牢或被驱逐出境的威胁，拒绝交出一些法国国库券。一名盖世太保军官随后要求查看摩根公司持有的证券清单，当发现除了政府证券外，这家银行拥有的资产少得可怜时，他简直不敢相信。显然，这位军官深信摩根财团的神话般影响力，他断言佩松-迪迪翁一定是在愚弄他和德意志帝国。他援引摩根对其他法国公司所谓的控制力，以为能在清单上找到大量里昂信贷银行和其他银行股票。他固执地认为摩根家族持有海量法国银行股份。后来，拉蒙特复述了那名德国军官的话："'如果他们没持有，那怎么能控制所有银行？'佩松-迪迪翁回答说他们一家都没持有。然后那名德国官员让他解释'摩根公司在整个欧洲大陆乃至世界各地拥有的巨大影响力'。"'佩松-迪迪翁平静地回答，他无法解释这一现象，除非原因在于构成摩根机构那些人的品性。" [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370820) 拉蒙或许对这个故事添油加醋，但摩根公司实际上的影响力，比起纳粹官场过度狂热头脑中的臆想，无疑要小得多。人们总是错误地认为，摩根家族主要通过直接持股公司来施展权力，而非借助独家的银行与咨询关系。有了 J. P. 摩根公司全部力量的支撑，摩根公司根本不需要巨额资本。

Morgan et Compagnie was the sole American bank in Paris to stay open throughout the war. It even turned a small profit. Leonard Rist was smart enough to see that such success might smack of collaboration, or at least of moral corner cutting. Perhaps as a result, he frequently cited his decoration from General Eisenhower for “gallant service in assisting the escape of Allied soldiers from the enemy.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371022) That the U.S. government approved Morgan et Compagnie’s wartime conduct was confirmed in late 1944 when the Treasury and War departments asked J. P. Morgan and Company to send senior Paris partner Dean Jay and other Americans back to the place Vendôme to restore a semblance of normality. Of the small, white-haired Dean Jay, it was said that American businessmen in France seldom made a major move without consulting him, and so his return carried symbolic weight. In the highest tribute of all, Morgan et Compagnie was assigned to handle deposits for American troops in liberated France.
摩根公司是巴黎唯一一家在整个战争期间始终营业的美国银行，甚至还略有盈利。伦纳德·里斯特头脑清醒，明白这种成功可能带有通敌之嫌，或至少是在道德上走了捷径。也许正因如此，他时常提及艾森豪威尔将军授予他的勋章，表彰他“在协助盟军士兵逃离敌境时表现出的英勇服务”。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371022) 美国政府认可摩根公司在战时的行为，这在 1944 年末得到证实——当时财政部和陆军部要求 J.P.摩根公司将巴黎高级合伙人迪安·杰伊及其他美国人送回旺多姆广场，以恢复某种正常秩序。据说，在法国，美国商人鲜有不先咨询那位矮小、白发苍苍的迪安·杰伊便采取重大行动的，因此他的回归具有象征意义。作为最高的赞誉，摩根公司被指定负责处理美军在解放后的法国的存款业务。

AS the lights went out across Europe in 1940, Tom Lamont made a last-ditch effort to steer Benito Mussolini away from Adolf Hitler. His faith in Mussolini had survived many atrocities. In January 1939, after Mussolini gassed villages in Libya and Ethiopia, Lamont was still reassuring the Morgan agent in Rome, Giovanni Fummi, of his “genuine admiration for the Duce’s extraordinary domestic achievements in behalf of his people.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371182) He clung to the fiction prevalent on Wall Street in the 1920s that there were two Mussolinis—the good domestic manager and the bad foreign adventurer—who somehow coinhabited the same stocky body.
1940 年欧洲陷入黑暗之际，汤姆·拉蒙特仍作最后努力，试图将贝尼托·墨索里尼从阿道夫·希特勒身边拉开。他对墨索里尼的信念经历了无数暴行却始终未灭。1939 年 1 月，墨索里尼在利比亚和埃塞俄比亚对村庄施放毒气后，拉蒙特仍向驻罗马的摩根代理人乔瓦尼·弗米保证，自己“由衷钦佩领袖为人民所取得的非凡国内成就”。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371182) 他固守着 1920 年代华尔街盛行的虚构——存在两个墨索里尼：一个善于国内治理，一个热衷海外冒险——而这两个人竟奇迹般共存于同一具矮胖身躯之中。

By the spring of 1939, Lamont’s overtures to Mussolini were inextricably intertwined with U.S. government policy. In his last mission of the Diplomatic Age, he operated as a private diplomat for Roosevelt as he tried to pull Italy back from war. In serving the White House, Lamont had to overcome a hurdle—how to explain to FDR Fummi’s matchless access to Mussolini? However much the bank might cast Fummi as a neutral agent, for twenty years he had fulsomely praised il Duce. Fummi had predicted that Mussolini would make Italy a great Mediterranean power. Now Lamont trotted out the standard formula regarding Fummi: “While he is loyal to his Government, he is not a fascist.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371369) Whether Roosevelt believed this or not, Lamont was an uncommonly handy intermediary between the United States and Italy.
到 1939 年春天，拉蒙对墨索里尼的示好已与美国政府政策紧密交织。在"外交时代"的最后一次任务中，他作为罗斯福的私人外交代表，试图将意大利从战争边缘拉回。服务于白宫时，拉蒙必须克服一个难题——如何向罗斯福解释福米与墨索里尼的无与伦比的关系？无论银行如何将福米包装成中立代理人，二十年来他一直对这位领袖极尽赞颂。福米曾预言墨索里尼将使意大利成为地中海强国。此刻拉蒙搬出了关于福米的标准说辞："他虽然效忠本国政府，但并非法西斯分子。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371369) 无论罗斯福是否相信，拉蒙都不失为美意之间一个极其便捷的中间人。

That spring, Lamont toyed with the idea of traveling to Rome and picknicking *alfresco* in the countryside. “I have every now and then a sort of longing or a nostalgia for the sunshine and brightness of Italy,” he told Fummi.[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371562) But he canceled a proposed trip, fearing a reporter might spot his name on a steamship list. He jovially told Joe Kennedy that “the Duce’s antics in Albania”—Italy’s April 1939 conquest of Albania—lay behind his cancellation of a scheduled stay at the American Academy in Rome, an institution subsidized by Morgan partners since Pierpont’s days.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371748)
那年春天，拉蒙特动了去罗马旅行、在乡间露天野餐的念头。“我时不时会有一种对意大利阳光明媚的向往或眷恋，”他对富米说。 [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371562) 但他最终取消了这个计划，担心有记者会在轮船乘客名单上看到他的名字。他兴致勃勃地告诉乔·肯尼迪，之所以取消原定在罗马美国学院的行程，是因为“领袖在阿尔巴尼亚搞的那些把戏”——指 1939 年 4 月意大利吞并阿尔巴尼亚一事。这个学院自皮尔庞特时代起就一直由摩根合伙人资助。 [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371748)

Instead of visiting, Lamont addressed a letter to the Italian government, warning that the United States would staunchly resist German—and, by implication, Italian—aggression. In the intricate ways of Morgan secret diplomacy, Fummi passed the letter to Bernardino Nogara, the Vatican’s financial secretary, who passed it to Azzolini at the Banca d’Italia. It thus arrived on Mussolini’s desk with the incontestable authority of God and money behind it.
拉蒙特没有亲自出访，而是致函意大利政府，警告美国将坚决抵制德国——言下之意也包括意大利——的侵略行为。通过摩根秘密外交那套错综复杂的手段，富米将这封信转交给了梵蒂冈财政秘书贝尔纳迪诺·诺加拉，诺加拉又将其转给意大利银行的阿佐利尼。最终，这封信带着上帝与金钱无可辩驳的权威，摆在了墨索里尼的办公桌上。

The Vatican figured importantly in both Lamont’s and Roosevelt’s efforts to sway Mussolini. In February 1939, Roosevelt had sent Joe Kennedy to the funeral of Pope Pius XI as a way of currying favor with the Vatican. A year later, he became the first president to assign a personal representative to the Vatican—Myron Taylor, former head of U.S. Steel and, in earlier years, an admirer of Mussolini. The Vatican feared its own political isolation if Hitler and Mussolini made an alliance, so it welcomed the Roosevelt opening, which aroused intense opposition from American Protestants.
梵蒂冈在拉蒙特和罗斯福试图影响墨索里尼的过程中扮演了重要角色。1939 年 2 月，罗斯福派乔·肯尼迪出席教皇庇护十一世的葬礼，以此向梵蒂冈示好。一年后，他成为首位向梵蒂冈派遣私人代表的总统——此人便是美国钢铁公司前总裁、早年曾仰慕墨索里尼的迈伦·泰勒。梵蒂冈担心希特勒与墨索里尼结盟后自身陷入政治孤立，因此对罗斯福的示好表示欢迎，此举却引发了美国新教徒的强烈反对。

In the spring of 1940, Lamont made a final approach to Mussolini. He sent a letter that he cleared first over the telephone with Roosevelt and that Tom Catto also showed to the British foreign secretary, Lord Halifax. Lamont tried to puncture Mussolini’s delusion that in the event of war, he could count on faithful support from Italian-Americans. Lamont said that Italian-Americans were rabidly anti-Hitler and that Italy shouldn’t be fooled by American isolationists. He warned against a Nazi *blitzkrieg.* Once again, Fummi handed the message to Nogara at the Vatican, who promised to transmit its contents to Mussolini. Not only did the mission fail, but it perhaps backfired, planting the notion in Mussolini’s mind that Fummi, as a Morgan courier, could function as an Anglo-American spy. Lamont’s maneuvers coincided with a mission to Mussolini undertaken by Undersecretary of State Sumner Welles for FDR. After a rather frigid interview with Welles, Mussolini told his son-in-law Galeazzo Ciano, “Between us and the Americans any kind of understanding is impossible because they judge problems on the surface while we go deeply into them.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371937) Mussolini also rebuffed a mission undertaken by Francis Rodd of Morgan Grenfell, who believed that the British War Office was bungling the chance to co-opt il Duce. Shortly after the June 1940 evacuation of the British Expeditionary Force at Dunkirk, Mussolini permanently locked arms with Hitler.
1940 年春天，拉蒙特向墨索里尼发出了最后的试探。他先通过电话征得罗斯福同意，随后寄出一封信件，汤姆·卡托也将此信转呈给英国外交大臣哈利法克斯勋爵。拉蒙特试图戳破墨索里尼的幻想——他以为一旦开战，意大利裔美国人会忠诚地支持他。拉蒙特指出，意大利裔美国人其实极度憎恨希特勒，意大利不应被美国孤立主义者蒙蔽。他警告纳粹可能发动闪电战。富米再次将消息转交给梵蒂冈的诺加拉，后者承诺将内容传达给墨索里尼。这次行动不仅未能奏效，反而适得其反——让墨索里尼怀疑富米作为摩根的联络员，可能充当英美间谍。拉蒙特的行动与副国务卿萨姆纳·威尔斯为罗斯福总统出使墨索里尼的任务恰好同时进行。 在经历了一次相当冷淡的与威尔斯的会面后，墨索里尼对自己的女婿加莱亚佐·齐亚诺说：“我们与美国之间任何形式的理解都是不可能的，因为他们只看表面问题，而我们则深入探究。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2371937) 墨索里尼还拒绝了摩根建富银行的弗朗西斯·罗德所进行的一项使命，罗德认为英国陆军部正在错失拉拢这位领袖的机会。1940 年 6 月英国远征军从敦刻尔克撤离后不久，墨索里尼便与希特勒永远地绑在了一起。

In September 1940, Mussolini ordered the arrest of Giovanni Fummi—his way of rewarding the House of Morgan for its years of thankless loyalty. According to Morgan records, Fummi was abducted from his Roman hotel and held incommunicado at the Regina Coeli prison. Mussolini was now a financial as well as a political renegade and no longer had to flatter Lamont. Two months before, Italy had defaulted on its municipal and government loans. Officially, Fummi was charged with expressing pro-British sentiments through the mail. This was a specious, legalistic indictment that thinly veiled a political vendetta. For Fummi, it was a crushing end to twenty years of selling Mussolini to Wall Street’s most powerful bankers. It was also an unmerited slap, for even after Mussolini embraced Hitler, Fummi still rationalized it as the only course left. Up until the war, both Lamont and Fummi had contended that Mussolini was driven into Hitler’s arms not by madness or megalomania, but by Western diplomatic ineptitude.
1940 年 9 月，墨索里尼下令逮捕乔瓦尼·富米——这是他用以回报摩根大通多年来毫无感恩的忠诚的方式。据摩根档案记载，富米在罗马的酒店被绑架，随后被关押在雷吉娜·科埃利监狱，与外界隔绝联系。此时的墨索里尼在金融和政治上均已背信弃义，不再需要讨好拉蒙特。两个月前，意大利已对其市政和政府贷款违约。官方指控富米通过邮件表达亲英情绪。这不过是一份虚伪的法律程序，赤裸裸地掩盖了政治报复。对富米而言，这标志着二十年来向华尔街最有权势的银行家兜售墨索里尼的生涯以毁灭告终。这更是一记莫名的耳光，因为即便在墨索里尼投靠希特勒之后，富米仍将其辩护为唯一可行的出路。 战争爆发前，拉蒙特和富米都认为，墨索里尼并非出于疯狂或自大症才投入希特勒的怀抱，而是西方外交的无能所致。

Lamont, stunned and blindsided, felt personally responsible for securing Fummi’s release. The two men had had a close, if curiously unequal, relationship. Fummi would address him as Mr. Lamont, while Lamont would reply using the diminutive Nino. A professional hand-wringer, the sentimental, hypochondriacal Fummi had shared many trials with Lamont—his first wife’s death from cancer in 1930, several breakdowns from overwork, and arthritis. In Morgan annals of crisp business letters, Fummi’s notes stand apart as the musings of a tender, melancholic man who bared his grief in a most un-Morgan-like manner.
拉蒙特震惊之余，深感自己肩负着营救富米脱险的责任。两人关系密切，但奇妙地不平等：富米尊称他为“拉蒙特先生”，而拉蒙特却以昵称“尼诺”回称。富米多愁善感，疑心病重，总爱自寻烦恼，曾与拉蒙特共同经历诸多磨难——1930 年第一任妻子因癌症去世，多次因过度劳累而精神崩溃，以及关节炎之苦。在摩根银行那些干练冰冷的商业信函中，富米的信件独树一帜——那是一个温柔忧郁的男子在倾诉衷肠，以最不符合摩根风格的方式袒露自己的悲伤。

Whether operating from the Via Veneto in Rome or a Saint-Tropez villa—made possible by his ample Morgan retainer—Fummi was always vulnerable to charges of his being a foreign agent. For twenty years, he had performed a tightrope act, balancing patriotism with professional necessity. Most of the time, he could serve both his Wall Street masters and Mussolini. But what if their interests clashed? Fummi often told Lamont that if a conflict ever arose, the bank would take precedence over Italy. Then, in 1939, he conceded that if war came, he would serve in the Italian army. He never resolved the confusion over his national identity.
无论是在罗马的威尼托大街，还是在圣特罗佩的别墅——靠着摩根公司丰厚的聘用金，他得以出入这些场所——富米始终难逃被指责为外国间谍的嫌疑。二十年来，他如同走钢丝般在爱国情怀与职业需求之间周旋。大多数时候，他既能服务于华尔街的老板们，也能为墨索里尼效劳。可万一双方利益冲突呢？富米常对拉蒙特说，若真有矛盾，银行会优先于意大利。然而到了 1939 年，他又承认战争一旦爆发，自己将加入意大利军队。他始终未能理清自己国家认同的迷局。

Compounding Fummi’s trouble was that in 1934 he had married Lady Anne Crawford, daughter of the Earl of Crawford and Balcarres and niece of Sir Ronald Lindsay, the British ambassador in Washington. This English veneer must have excited Mussolini’s suspicions. For Fummi’s wedding, the House of Morgan sent the couple a pair of George II silver mugs. In 1938, Fummi chattered happily about how Anglicized he had become, with “an English wife, an English secretary and an English nannie!”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2372110) When war came, however, he knew he was in a ticklish situation and packed off his English wife, children, and nannie to Scotland while he stayed behind in Rome. Perhaps the arrest came as less than a total surprise.
让福米更加头疼的是，1934 年他娶了安妮·克劳福德小姐——她是克劳福德和巴尔卡雷斯伯爵之女、英国驻华盛顿大使罗纳德·林赛爵士的侄女。这种亲英色彩想必让墨索里尼心生猜忌。福米大婚时，摩根财团送了他一对乔治二世时期的银质酒杯。1938 年，他乐滋滋地闲聊自己变得多么英国化，拥有"一位英国妻子、一位英国秘书和一位英国保姆！"然而战争爆发后，他意识到处境微妙，便把英国妻子、孩子和保姆都打发到苏格兰，自己则留在罗马。或许被捕对他来说并非完全出乎意料。

Lamont orchestrated Fummi’s release in a masterful way. First he got the State Department involved. Then, through Myron Taylor, he sent confidential messages to the papal secretary. There was good reason to expect Vatican sympathy: Fummi was a close confidant of Nogara, who headed the Vatican’s investment arm, the Special Administration of the Holy See. While Pope Piux XI was alive, it had even been assumed that Fummi would someday replace Nogara as the Vatican’s chief portfolio manager. It is also likely that Nogara was secretly hostile to the Germans, for neither before nor after the war would he invest Vatican funds in German securities. In addition, Lamont had once lunched with the new pope, Pius XII, in New York. Responding to Lamont’s pleas, the Vatican cabled back that they were doing “utmost privately and officially in order to obtain release friend.” The papacy underscored Mussolini’s personal involvement in the affair: “We understand ultimate decision has to be taken by Government chief.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2372300)
拉蒙特以巧妙的手腕策划了富米的获释。他先让美国国务院介入，随后又通过迈伦·泰勒向教皇秘书发送密函。梵蒂冈方面有充分理由表示同情：富米是诺加拉的密友，而诺加拉掌管着梵蒂冈的投资机构——圣座特别行政署。在教皇庇护十一世在世时，人们甚至曾以为富米有朝一日会接替诺加拉，成为梵蒂冈的首席投资管理人。此外，诺加拉私下很可能对德国人怀有敌意，因为战前和战后他都从未将梵蒂冈资金投入德国证券。还有一件事：拉蒙特曾在纽约与新任教皇庇护十二世共进午餐。应拉蒙特的恳求，梵蒂冈回电称，他们正“尽最大努力，从私下和官方双渠道，以争取朋友获释”。教皇强调墨索里尼亲自插手此事：“我们明白最终决定得由政府首脑拍板。”“ [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2372300) ”

In the end, only a personal appeal to Mussolini by Lamont would work. It was as if the sadistic Duce wanted to extract one last tribute, one last insufferable humiliation, from his banker. Lamont had to check his anger and argue that Fummi had represented Italy to the House of Morgan and not the reverse. If there were more truth to this than Lamont would ever care to admit, it now had to be grossly overstated. He wrote:
到头来，只有拉蒙特亲自向墨索里尼恳求才能奏效。这位残忍的领袖似乎非要从他这位银行家身上榨取最后的敬意、再让他忍受最后一次难以启齿的羞辱。拉蒙特不得不压下怒火，强调富米是代表意大利与摩根商行打交道，而非相反。即便事实比拉蒙特愿意承认的更加接近真相，此刻也必须被极度夸大。他写道：

> It was Fummi, and Fummi alone, who urged my original visit to Rome in 1923 and the subsequent visits which resulted in these favorable loan operations for your Government. On every occasion he was active in the negotiation and zealous to advance the good name of his Government and to protect it at every point. While it is true that Fummi was our own representative in Italy, yet it is even truer, so to speak, that he acted broadly and wisely as a financial ambassador for your Government.
> 是 Fummi，也唯有 Fummi，促使我于 1923 年首次访问罗马，并促成后续多次访问，最终为贵国政府争取到了这些有利的贷款业务。每一次谈判中，他都积极奔走，满腔热忱地提升其政府的声誉，并在方方面面竭力维护。诚然，Fummi 是我们驻意大利的代表，但更可以说，他实际上是贵国政府一位视野开阔、行事睿智的金融大使。

Far from evading the subject of Fummi’s marriage to an Englishwoman, Lamont rather brazenly advanced it as an extra guarantee of Fummi’s patriotism: “As time went on, we became more and more impressed with the fidelity which he did show and was bound to show towards his own Government and people. The fact of his having married Lady Anne Crawford only served to make him more meticulous in the manner in which he handled himself and in the correctness of his attitude toward his own Government.”
拉蒙特非但没有回避 Fummi 与一位英国女子结婚这件事，反而毫不避讳地将其作为 Fummi 爱国心的一份额外担保：“随着时间的推移，我们越发对他所展现、也注定会对其本国政府和人民保持的忠诚印象深刻。他娶了安妮·克劳福德夫人这件事，反而让他更加谨言慎行，对本国政府的态度也更加端正。”

It is noticeable that Lamont never directly accused Mussolini of arresting Fummi or of having prior knowledge. He wrote as if he were entreating a wise, neutral, and all-powerful arbiter. In the end, Lamont groveled one last time: “Finally, it is because of your kindly and generous attitude towards me personally in all our interviews, and perhaps especially because of the charming sense of proportion that you have always shown in such interviews, that I have ventured to address you this urgent personal appeal in Fummi’s behalf.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2372517)
显而易见，Lamont 从未直接指控 Mussolini 逮捕了 Fummi，也未曾暗示他事先知情。他的措辞仿佛是在恳求一位睿智、中立且全能的仲裁者。最后，Lamont 又一次卑躬屈膝地写道：“归根结底，正是因为在我们的所有会面中，您对我个人始终保持着和善与慷慨的态度，尤其是您在那些会面中展现出的那种迷人的分寸感，才让我斗胆就 Fummi 之事向您发出这封紧急的个人恳求。”

About ten days after Fummi’s arrest, a cable arrived at Morgans from Vatican City. It reported that Fummi had been safely released and would be exiled to Switzerland. For Lamont, it was an ironic end to seventeen years of having scraped and bowed and hoped that Mussolini could be reformed. He wasn’t left with the dignity of any comforting illusions. As he wrote in a somber letter to Fummi in Saint-Moritz, Switzerland, “Some time or other, dear Nino, a new day will dawn and America and Italy will once more be friends. But before that day comes there will be fire and flame and sword, grief for us all.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2372746)
富米被捕大约十天后，摩根银行收到一封来自梵蒂冈城的电报。电报称富米已安全获释，将被流放至瑞士。对拉蒙特而言，这讽刺地终结了他十七年来低声下气、卑躬屈膝，指望能改造墨索里尼的岁月。他连最后一丝聊以自慰的幻想都未能保留。他在一封写给瑞士圣莫里茨的富米的阴郁信件中写道：“亲爱的尼诺，总有一天，黎明会再次降临，美国和意大利必将重归于好。但在那一天到来之前，将会有烈火、硝烟与刀剑，带给我们所有人悲伤。”

In February 1941, the Morgan office in Rome was closed. Two weeks later, the irrepressible Fummi popped up in London to supervise a secret transfer of Vatican gold bullion stored in a basement room at Morgan Grenfell. Throughout the 1930s, the Vatican had bought the gold at the fixed price of \$35 an ounce, never selling any. Fummi would discreetly refer to it as the “special commodity.” For security reasons, the Vatican now decided to ship the gold to New York. The wartime transfer was carried out under the official aegis of Lord Halifax, until recently Britain’s foreign secretary. The gold ended up at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York. There it would dizzily appreciate in the postwar years.
1941 年 2 月，摩根在罗马的办事处关闭了。两周后，那位精力充沛的富米现身伦敦，监督一项秘密转移行动——将存放在摩根建富银行地下室里的梵蒂冈金条运走。整个 1930 年代，梵蒂冈一直以每盎司 35 美元的固定价格购入黄金，从未出售过任何一盎司。富米谨慎地将其称为“特殊商品”。出于安全考虑，梵蒂冈当时决定将这批黄金运往纽约。这项战时转移行动，在前英国外交大臣哈利法克斯勋爵的官方庇护下进行。最终，这些金条被存入了纽约联邦储备银行，并在战后迎来了惊人的增值。

In 1942, Bernardino Nogara tried to call in his IOU for his help in the release of Leonard Rist and Giovanni Fummi. The Vatican held a large stake in a South American banking group, Sudameris, which was headquartered in Paris but had branches in Argentina, Brazil, and other Latin American countries. America’s wartime blacklist had produced heavy losses for the Brazilian bank, and it faced possible liquidation; Nogara wanted to get Sudameris off the list. To this end, he proposed that Morgans buy half of the company. In exchange, he said the House of Morgan would have final approval over its actions. Although Fummi was ready to go to New York to negotiate and Nogara promised to “guarantee the fullest respect of the Allied interests in the management of the South American branches of Sudameris,” Lamont explained the political and legal impossibility of buying shares in foreign banks that had French and Italian backing.[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2372937) Vatican appeals to the State Department bore no fruit, either. But the discussion reveals an interesting example of the Vatican’s diplomatic independence in Axis Italy.
1942 年，贝尔纳迪诺·诺加拉试图收回当年协助释放伦纳德·里斯特和乔瓦尼·富米时对方欠下的人情债。梵蒂冈在南美银行集团 Sudameris 持有大量股份，该集团总部虽设在巴黎，但在阿根廷、巴西等拉美国家设有分行。美国战时黑名单导致这家巴西银行损失惨重，甚至面临清盘风险；诺加拉希望将 Sudameris 从黑名单中剔除。为此，他提议摩根买下该银行一半的股份。作为交换条件，摩根家族将拥有对该行事务的最终审批权。尽管富米已准备赴纽约谈判，诺加拉也承诺"将全力确保 Sudameris 在南美各分行在运营中充分尊重盟国利益"，但拉蒙特解释说，从政治和法律角度来看，购买这家由法国和意大利资本支持的海外银行股份根本行不通。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2372937) 梵蒂冈向美国国务院提出的申诉同样毫无结果。 但这场讨论揭示了一个有趣的例证，即梵蒂冈在轴心国意大利统治下仍保持外交独立。

IN May of 1940, Neville Chamberlain resigned in favor of Winston Churchill, a man with whom the House of Morgan had always had a peevish, family quarrel. Teddy Grenfell had been blind to Churchill’s merits, saying of him after the crash, “His record for thirty years has shown him to be the most unreliable of statesmen as well as the most unstable of friends . . . I wish he would change his party for the third time and go over to Ramsay MacDonald, or even further to the left.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373142) During the summer of 1940, Nancy Astor grudgingly conceded to Tom Lamont that Churchill was doing a good job but regretted Lloyd George’s absence from the cabinet.
1940 年 5 月，内维尔·张伯伦辞职，由温斯顿·丘吉尔接替——摩根家族与丘吉尔一直有着一种别扭的、家族式的争执。泰迪·格伦费尔一直无视丘吉尔的优点，在大萧条后这样评价他：“他过去三十年的记录表明，他既是最不可靠的政治家，也是最不稳定的朋友……我希望他能第三次改变党派，投奔拉姆齐·麦克唐纳，甚至更左一些。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373142) 1940 年夏天，南希·阿斯特勉强向汤姆·拉蒙特承认丘吉尔干得不错，但对劳合·乔治未能入阁感到遗憾。

In August 1940, the Battle of Britain began. Reported vividly to America by Edward R. Murrow’s broadcasts, the nightly blitz drove Londoners into the Underground. Morgan Grenfell had girded for war, adding air-raid shelters and gas-proof access to street and stairways. Although 23 Great Winchester escaped a direct hit, the square mile of the City was badly bombed, and the Dutch Church across the narrow lane from Morgan Grenfell was destroyed. When a parachute mine in its rubble exploded, the blast stripped wood panels from the Morgan partners’ room and blew out several doors. A nearby conflagration at Carpenters’ Hall was extinguished in time to save 23 Great Winchester. Later on, a V-1 missile fell in Old Broad Street, where George Peabody and Junius Morgan had once worked. After each such pounding of London, Harold Nicolson would send Charles Lindbergh a needling postcard, saying, “Do you still think we are soft?”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373340)
1940 年 8 月，不列颠之战拉开序幕。爱德华·R·默罗的广播向美国生动报道了战况，夜间的空袭将伦敦市民逼入地铁站避难。摩根格林菲尔公司早已做好战争准备，增设了防空洞，并在街道和楼梯处设置了防毒通道。尽管大温彻斯特街 23 号幸免于直接轰炸，但伦敦金融城一平方英里的区域遭受重创，与摩根格林菲尔一巷之隔的荷兰教堂被摧毁。当废墟中的一颗伞降地雷爆炸时，冲击波剥落了摩根合伙人房间的木镶板，并炸飞了数扇门。邻近木匠厅的一场大火被及时扑灭，保住了大温彻斯特街 23 号。后来，一枚 V-1 导弹落在老宽街——当年乔治·皮博迪和朱尼厄斯·摩根曾在此工作过的地方。每次伦敦遭受这样的重创后，哈罗德·尼科尔森都会给查尔斯·林德伯格寄去一张带有讽刺意味的明信片，上面写道：“你还觉得我们软弱吗？” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373340)

As British children were evacuated from London, the House of Morgan proudly did its duty. No cause warmed Jack Morgan’s blood more than England at war. Gray and tired, he went to a West Fourteenth Street pier for the arrival of almost four hundred British children aboard two ocean liners. There he met the eleven-year-old Lord Primrose and two of Lord Bicester’s grandchildren, all with governesses and nurses in tow. They would be his wartime guests at Matinicock Point, along with three other City Smith offspring. “Jack Morgan lived in considerable Victorian splendor, with armed guards all over the place,” recalled Charles E. A. Hambro, who spent part of the war with the Harry Morgans in New York before rushing back to play on Eton’s cricket team in 1943, “and there was Lord Primrose in isolation with the old boy.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373490) In a further caretaker service for Britain, the vaults of J. P. Morgan and Company received British government rowing trophies, along with two Gutenberg Bibles.
随着英国儿童撤离伦敦，摩根家族自豪地履行了他们的职责。没有哪个事业比英国参战更能让杰克·摩根热血沸腾。疲惫而苍老的他在西十四街码头等候，两艘远洋客轮载着近四百名英国儿童抵达。他在那里见到了十一岁的普里姆罗斯勋爵和比斯特勋爵的两位孙辈，随行还有家庭教师和保姆。他们将与另外三位伦敦金融城的史密斯家孩子一起，成为他在马蒂尼科克角战时的座上客。“杰克·摩根依然过着维多利亚时代的奢华生活，到处都是武装警卫，”查尔斯·E·A·汉布罗回忆道，他曾在战时间断地住在纽约的哈里·摩根家，随后于 1943 年匆匆赶回英国，参加伊顿公学的板球队，“而普里姆罗斯勋爵则与那位老绅士一同隐居。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373490) 作为对英国的进一步照管，J.P.摩根公司的金库还接收了英国政府的赛艇奖杯，以及两本古登堡圣经。

Chartering George Whitney’s old boat, the *Wanderer*, Jack transferred *Corsair IV* to Britain for war service. He donated many of its interior decorations, from a blue rug to wicker deck chairs, to a “Bundles for Britain” benefit at Gimbel’s, and Harry Morgan sold his Grumman amphibian plane to the Canadian government for use in coastal patrols. After France fell, a British visitor at 23 Wall expressed fear for the future. “Our turn is next,” he told Jack. “The Huns will let loose on us a blitz that will be hard to withstand.” Jack brimmed with emotion. “My good friend,” he said, “you need not be downcast for a single moment. I tell you, Britain will never give in, never, never, never!”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373655) His fecund imagination now had fresh cause to picture pursuing Germans. In escaping from London raids, young Luftwaffe pilots would empty surplus bombs over Wall Hall, and in October a load blew out the mansion’s windows. For safekeeping, his silver collection was brought into the 23 Great Winchester vaults that housed the Vatican gold.
租赁乔治·惠特尼的老船“漫游者”后，杰克将“海盗四号”移交给英国用于战时服役。他将船上的诸多内饰——从蓝色地毯到柳条甲板椅——捐赠给金贝尔百货举办的“为英国送温暖”慈善义卖，而哈里·摩根则将自己的格鲁曼水陆两栖飞机出售给加拿大政府，用于海岸巡逻。法国沦陷后，一位英国访客在华尔街 23 号表达了对未来的担忧。“下一个就轮到我们了，”他对杰克说，“那群野蛮人会对我们发动一场难以抵御的闪电战。”杰克激动不已，说道：“我的朋友，你大可不必有片刻消沉。我告诉你，英国永远不会屈服，永远不会，永远不会！” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373655) 他那丰沛的想象力此刻又有了新的素材——想象着追击的德军。在逃离伦敦空袭时，年轻的德国飞行员会将剩余的炸弹倾泻在沃尔庄园上空，而十月份的一轮轰炸更是炸毁了庄园的窗户。 为了妥善保管，他的白银藏品被存入了存放梵蒂冈黄金的 23 号大温彻斯特金库。

Morgan Grenfell was depopulated as partners entered government service, a logical culmination of the firm’s activities during the Diplomatic Age. It was already something of a branch office for the Bank of England, the Treasury, and the Foreign Office. Francis Rodd, who had explored the southern Sahara in the 1920s and won a medal from the Royal Geographic Society, was posted to Africa, while Willy Hill-Wood spent much of the war in Washington as a U.K. censor. Lord Bicester and Lady Sybil converted their Oxfordshire estate, Tusmore, into a fifty-bed convalescent home, as other British country houses were turned into barracks or troop hospitals.
摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）因合伙人纷纷进入政府任职而人员锐减，这可谓是该银行在外交时代各项活动的合理终局。它早已在一定程度上成为英格兰银行、财政部和外交部的分支机构。曾于 20 世纪 20 年代勘探南撒哈拉并荣获皇家地理学会奖章的弗朗西斯·罗德里（Francis Rodd）被派往非洲，而威利·希尔-伍德（Willy Hill-Wood）则以英国审查官的身份在华盛顿度过了大部分战时岁月。比斯特勋爵（Lord Bicester）和西比尔夫人（Lady Sybil）将他们在牛津郡的庄园塔斯莫尔（Tusmore）改建成了一座拥有五十张床位的疗养院，与此同时，其他许多英国乡间别墅也被改造成了兵营或部队医院。

Monty Norman recommended Tom Catto for a new, unpaid position as financial adviser to the chancellor of the Exchequer, Kingsley Wood. A short, shrewd, dignified Scot of humble background, Catto had been a Morgan Grenfell partner since 1928. Before that, he had managed the large Indian merchant house of Andrew Yule and Company, owned by Morgan partners in London and New York. He had the exotic, global connections of an empire-building entrepreneur, having done deals with Vivian Smith in the Middle East and Russia. He and John Maynard Keynes were assigned rooms on opposite sides of the chancellor’s office; Keynes to represent the independent, theoretical view; Catto, the practical, banking side. They were soon dubbed Catto and Doggo, and Lord Bicester, with muted glee, reported to 23 Wall that Catto was liberally throwing out many of Doggo’s impractical ideas. Monty Norman preferred dealing with Catto, who would succeed him as governor, perpetuating Morgan Grenfell’s charmed access to the Bank of England.
蒙蒂·诺曼推荐汤姆·卡托担任财政大臣金斯利·伍德的新职位——无薪财务顾问。卡托身材矮小，精明干练，气质庄重，出身寒微，自 1928 年起便是摩根建富的合伙人。在此之前，他管理着大型印度商行安德鲁·尤尔公司，这家公司由伦敦和纽约的摩根合伙人共同拥有。他拥有帝国建设企业家特有的异域全球人脉，曾与薇薇安·史密斯在中东和俄罗斯做过交易。他与约翰·梅纳德·凯恩斯被安排在财政大臣办公室对面的房间，凯恩斯代表独立的理论观点，卡托则代表务实的银行实务。两人很快被戏称为“卡托与狗托”，比斯特勋爵暗自欣喜地向华尔街 23 号报告说，卡托慷慨地抛掉了狗托的许多不切实际的想法。 蒙蒂·诺曼更倾向于与接替他出任行长的卡托打交道，这一安排延续了摩根建富与英格兰银行之间那份得天独厚的密切关系。

With much of Europe under Nazi control, Churchill knew he had to woo America with all the wit, charm, and energy at his disposal. He faced a new opponent, an organization equally determined to keep America out of the war—America First. Formed by two Yale graduate students, R. Douglas Stuart, Jr., and Kingman Brewster, it was a response to the William Allen White committee and promptly recruited Charles Lindbergh. Through his America First speeches, Lindbergh destroyed the last remnants of the hero worship he once aroused. Stumping the country, he would claim that “the three most important groups which have been pressing this country toward war are the British, the Jewish and the Roosevelt administration.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373844) He talked of insidious Jewish influence in the American government and media.
在欧洲大部分地区沦入纳粹控制之际，丘吉尔深知自己必须使出浑身解数——凭借智慧、魅力和精力——来争取美国。他面对着一个新的对手，一个同样决心让美国置身战争之外的团体——"美国第一"委员会。这个组织由耶鲁大学的两名研究生小 R·道格拉斯·斯图尔特和金曼·布鲁斯特发起，作为对威廉·艾伦·怀特委员会的回应，并迅速将查尔斯·林德伯格招致麾下。通过"美国第一"的演讲，林德伯格亲手摧毁了曾经在自己身上激起的最后一丝英雄崇拜。他在全国巡回演讲时声称："一直把美国推向战争的三个最重要群体是英国人、犹太人和罗斯福政府。" [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373844) 他还大肆谈论犹太人对美国政府与媒体的隐形渗透。

Despite the impact of Lindbergh’s speeches, the nightly terror in London engendered a wave of sympathy for Britain. Strengthened by his November 1940 reelection, Roosevelt stepped up efforts to aid England. He and Churchill negotiated an exchange of fifty old U.S. destroyers for eight British air bases in the West Indies. By late November 1940, Lord Lothian sounded the alert regarding a looming British cash crisis, and in early December, Churchill told Roosevelt the time was coming when England would “no longer be able to pay cash.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2373994)
尽管林德伯格的演讲产生了一定影响，但伦敦夜间的恐怖袭击激起了一波对英国的同情。罗斯福凭借 1940 年 11 月的连任而实力增强，他加紧了对英国的援助。他与丘吉尔达成协议，用美国五十艘旧驱逐舰换取英国在西印度群岛的八个空军基地。到 1940 年 11 月底，洛西恩勋爵就英国即将面临的现金危机发出警告；12 月初，丘吉尔告诉罗斯福，英国很快将“无力再支付现金”了。

During this desperate autumn in Britain, the House of Morgan and the Roosevelt administration were reunited in a campaign to provide all aid short of war. This rapprochement produced a sense of mutual relief. After chatting with Roosevelt at the White House, Leffingwell told him on December 24, 1940, that “whatever differences there may have been about domestic affairs, I and my colleagues are heart and soul with you for unlimited material aid to Britain and for national defense.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374169) That weekend, Roosevelt was broadcasting a fireside chat in support of Britain, and Leffingwell offered some pointers. “When you say ’give,’ you mean give or lend goods, guns, ships, planes, munitions and whatnot . . . you are not interested in giving England a bank account, but in giving her the things she needs.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374336) In his radio address, Roosevelt exhorted Americans to make the United States “the great arsenal of democracy,” and a week later he asked Congress to enact a lend-lease program that would let Washington guarantee payment for British war orders in the United States and lease supplies indefinitely. There would be no immediate cost to the Allies. Roosevelt hoped that the Lend-Lease Act would avert another war debts/reparations mess after the war. Churchill called it “the most unsordid act in the history of any nation.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374470) Morgan support of the plan is notable in that it precluded any repetition of the bank’s financing role in World War I.
在这个令英国绝望的秋天，摩根财团与罗斯福政府重归于好，共同发起了一场“除参战外全力援助”的运动。这次和解让双方如释重负。1940 年 12 月 24 日，莱芬韦尔在白宫与罗斯福交谈后告诉他：“无论我们在内政问题上曾有何分歧，我和我的同事们真心实意地支持您向英国提供无限的物质援助，并加强国防。”那个周末，罗斯福正在广播炉边谈话支持英国，莱芬韦尔还提供了一些建议。“当您说‘给予’时，您指的是提供或借出物资、枪支、舰船、飞机、弹药等等……您对给英国开银行账户不感兴趣，而是给予她所需要的一切。”” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374336) 罗斯福在广播讲话中呼吁美国人民将美国打造成“民主国家的伟大兵工厂”，一周后他要求国会通过租借法案，允许美国政府为英国在美国的战争订单提供付款担保并无限制租赁物资。盟国无需立即承担费用。罗斯福希望《租借法案》能够避免战后再次出现战争债务与赔款的混乱局面。丘吉尔称其为“任何国家历史上最纯洁的行为”。 [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374470) 摩根对该计划的支持意义重大，因为这避免了银行在第一次世界大战中的融资角色重演。

As Lindbergh and other isolationists testified against the Lend-Lease Act, Roosevelt and Treasury Secretary Morgenthau sought a dramatic way to rebut charges that Britain had billions of dollars in idle assets salted away around the world. They decided to ask for an act of bloody public self-sacrifice—nothing less than the sale of a major British industrial holding in America to show that Britain had exhausted all options before pleading for aid. In March 1941, on the eve of congressional passage of lend-lease, Roosevelt and Morgenthau notified Whitehall that it would have to consummate an important sale at once. The White House itself selected Britain’s single most valuable industrial possession in America—the American Viscose Company, a subsidiary of the Cour-taulds’ textile empire. With seven plants and eighteen thousand employees, it was probably the world’s largest rayon producer. Washington urged extreme haste and imposed a seventy-two-hour deadline for announcing the sale.
当林德伯格及其他孤立主义者作证反对《租借法案》时，罗斯福与财政部长摩根索试图用一种戏剧性的方式，反驳"英国在海外藏匿了数十亿美元闲置资产"的指控。他们决定要求英国做一个血淋淋的公开自我牺牲——那就是出售英国在美国的一项重大工业资产，以证明英国是在穷尽一切办法后才请求援助的。1941 年 3 月，在国会通过《租借法案》前夕，罗斯福和摩根索通知英国政府，必须立即完成一项重要资产出售。白宫亲自选定了英国在美国最有价值的工业资产——美国粘胶公司（American Viscose Company），它是考陶尔兹纺织帝国旗下的子公司。这家公司拥有七家工厂和一万八千名员工，很可能是当时全球最大的人造丝生产商。华盛顿方面敦促迅速行动，并限令在七十二小时内宣布出售消息。

The British found this need to demonstrate faith to an old friend degrading. A somber delegation, including Tom Catto, broke the news to chairman Samuel Courtauld, who reacted in exemplary fashion. He asked only one question: “Was the sale essential in the national interest, whatever the hardship on him and his company?”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374630) When Catto replied that it was required by the essential interests of wartime finance, the patriotic Courtauld fell on his sword. The Courtaulds’ board was given thirty-six hours to make arrangements—surely the fastest such major divestment in history.
英国人认为，向老友证明忠诚的要求有失体面。一个包括汤姆·卡托在内的沉重代表团，将这个噩耗告知了董事长塞缪尔·库尔托。库尔托的反应堪称表率——他只问了一个问题："无论对他本人和公司造成多大困难，这笔出售是否对国家利益至关重要？" [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374630) 当卡托回答这是战时财政的根本利益所必需时，这位爱国企业家毅然牺牲了自己。库尔托公司董事会仅获 36 小时来安排相关事宜——这无疑是史上最快的重大资产剥离。

To sell American Viscose to American investors, J. P. Morgan and Company recommended to the British Treasury that Morgan Stanley and Dillon, Read manage the sale, with 23 Wall providing the necessary bank loans. The handling of the sale rankled the British for years. In unsettled, wartime conditions, it was hard to know what price might attract American investors. Textile shares had been fluctuating wildly, and underwriting tasks that normally took weeks were compressed into days. While Britain received \$54 million, the seventeen-firm syndicate headed by Morgan Stanley and Dillon, Read resold the shares publicly for \$62 million, pocketing the difference. Some Britons—most notably Winston Churchill—thought they had been fleeced by the bankers. At the time, the Courtaulds’ directors claimed the company’s tangible assets alone were worth \$128 million. Obviously, there was a fantastic discrepancy.
为了将美国粘胶纤维公司出售给美国投资者，J. P. 摩根公司向英国财政部建议，由摩根士丹利和狄龙-里德管理这笔交易，华尔街 23 号提供所需的银行贷款。这笔交易的运作方式让英国人耿耿于怀多年。在战乱不安的时期，很难判断什么价格能吸引美国投资者。纺织类股票剧烈波动，通常需要数周的承销工作被压缩到几天内完成。虽然英国获得了 5400 万美元，但由摩根士丹利和狄龙-里德牵头的 17 家承销商联合以 6200 万美元公开转售了这些股票，将差价收入囊中。一些英国人——尤其是温斯顿·丘吉尔——认为他们被银行家们欺骗了。当时，考陶尔德公司的董事们声称，仅该公司的有形资产就价值 1.28 亿美元。显然，两者之间存在巨大差异。

After the war, Churchill described the sale in dryly cynical terms: “The great British business of Courtaulds in America was sold by us at the request of the United States Government at a comparatively low figure, and then resold through the markets at a much higher price from which we did not benefit.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374790) When Harold Stanley read this description in a 1949 newspaper excerpt of Churchill’s memoirs, he was shocked. Through Lord Harcourt of Morgan Grenfell, he made extensive efforts to get Churchill to modify it. He even tried to draw on Churchill’s old friendship with his wife, Louise (formerly Mrs. Parker Gilbert), who had aided Churchill when he had an accident in New York years before. In revising his book, Churchill agreed to delete the impression that the bankers were too richly rewarded for their services. But he wouldn’t budge in his opinion that American Viscose had fetched far less than its intrinsic worth. At the time of the sale, it was agreed that the matter would be referred to a three-man arbitration tribunal. In bitter postwar litigation, Courtaulds received additional compensation from the British government.
战后，丘吉尔以冷嘲热讽的口吻描述这笔交易：“我们应美国政府要求，以相对较低的价格出售了英国最大企业 Courtaulds 在美国的资产，随后这些资产在市场上被高价转售，而我们分文未得。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2374790) 1949 年，当哈罗德·斯坦利从报纸上读到丘吉尔回忆录的这段摘录时，他大为震惊。他通过摩根建富银行的哈考特勋爵，竭力游说丘吉尔修改这段文字。他甚至试图搬出丘吉尔与妻子路易丝（前帕克·吉尔伯特夫人）的旧谊——多年前丘吉尔在纽约遭遇意外时，路易丝曾施以援手。在修订著作时，丘吉尔同意删除那些暗示银行家酬劳过高的措辞。但他坚持认为美国人造丝公司的售价远低于其实际价值，这一点寸步不让。 在出售时，双方约定将该事宜提交由三人组成的仲裁庭裁决。在战后激烈的诉讼中，Courtaulds 从英国政府那里获得了额外赔偿。

After congressional passage of Lend-Lease on March 11, 1941, Roosevelt approved a long list of supplies to be shipped to England. The progressive wing of the isolationist movement resented not only its defeat on Lend-Lease but also Roosevelt’s about-face in his attitude toward Wall Street and the House of Morgan. That April, Senator Burton Wheeler of Montana, who had pursued Morgans in the railroad hearings, castigated Roosevelt for inviting the “money changers” and “Wall Street lawyers” into his camp. He angrily noted that people such as Willkie and Lamont were suddenly portrayed as “liberals,” while progressives were being styled as “Tories, Nazi sympathizers, or anti-Semites” because of their opposition to U.S. participation in the war.[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375021)
1941 年 3 月 11 日国会通过《租借法案》后，罗斯福批准了一长串运往英国的物资清单。孤立主义阵营中的进步派不仅因《租借法案》的失利而愤愤不平，更对罗斯福在对待华尔街和摩根财团态度上的 180 度大转弯深感不满。同年 4 月，蒙大拿州参议员 Burton Wheeler——此人曾在铁路听证会上对摩根家族穷追猛打——痛斥罗斯福将“金钱兑换商”和“华尔街律师”招入麾下。他愤怒地指出，像 Willkie 和 Lamont 这样的人突然被描绘成“自由派”，而进步派却因反对美国参战而被扣上“托利党人、纳粹同情者或反犹分子”的帽子。 [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375021)

While attacked by progressives as warmongers, the House of Morgan was actually engaged in a hidden feud with its British friends for taking the opposite position. Tom Lamont had helped Roosevelt lobby for Lend-Lease yet insisted that the United States not enter the war. Ostensibly, this was so America could remain an arsenal for England, but there were also festering sores from the 1930s feuds. Lamont and Catto at the British Treasury shared their own diplomatic back channel, and Lamont’s letters became increasingly petulant. In May 1941, he wrote a remarkable letter to Catto, full of bile and defending the U.S. failure to go to war:
虽然进步派指责摩根财团是战争贩子，但摩根家族实际上正因持有对立立场，与其英国朋友们暗地里较着劲。汤姆·拉蒙特曾帮助罗斯福游说《租借法案》，却又坚持美国不应参战。表面上看，这是为了让美国能继续充当英国的兵工厂，但背后也藏着 1930 年代那些恩怨留下的旧伤疤。拉蒙特与英国财政部的卡托之间有一条隐秘的外交联络通道，而拉蒙特的信件也变得越来越急躁。1941 年 5 月，他给卡托写了一封措辞刻薄的信，信中充满怨气，竭力为美国迟迟不参战辩护：

> If the American people have seemed sluggish in coming to Britain’s aid, nevertheless, it must be acknowledged that the U.S.A. is the first nation to go all-out in their opposition to Hitler without having faced an immediate, desperate threat to its existence. On that point does not America deserve praise for such progress rather than implied criticism for its slowness? Every country in Europe, including Britain, waited almost until Hitler had his thumb on its windpipe before it waked up and got started.
> 如果美国人民在援助英国时显得迟缓，但必须承认，美国是第一个在自身尚未面临迫在眉睫的生死威胁时，就全力反对希特勒的国家。就这一点而言，难道美国不更应因其行动而受到赞扬，而非因其迟缓而遭到隐晦的批评吗？欧洲的每个国家，包括英国在内，几乎都是等到希特勒的手已掐住自己的喉咙时，才幡然醒悟、开始行动。

> Most English people look upon America, because it was (150 years ago) a British colony, as simply a younger, perhaps more vigorous, less polished England. That picture is emphasized by our former habit of calling England ’the Mother Country.’
> 大多数英国人看待美国，仅仅因为它（150 年前）曾是英国殖民地，就视其为更年轻、或许更有活力但不够精致的英国。我们过去习惯称英国为"母国"，这种印象也因此加深。

At this point in the letter, Lamont dredged up the quarrels of the 1930s. He recalled Britain’s double cross on German debt and its unwillingness to make payments on the World War I debt, which might have won American sympathy. He recalled how in 1935 he had begged Neville Chamberlain to consider a commercial treaty with the United States to create goodwill for England in America, saying it might be needed in some future crisis. “Mr. Chamberlain smiled an icy smile and was not interested in American good will.”
写到信的这个当口，拉蒙特又翻起了 20 世纪 30 年代的旧账。他回忆起英国在德国债务问题上的背信弃义，以及一战债务上迟迟不肯履约的态度——这些本可以赢得美国的同情。他还提到 1935 年自己曾恳求内维尔·张伯伦考虑与美国签订一项商业条约，为英国在美国争取好感，并称这份好感或许会在未来的某场危机中派上用场。“张伯伦先生冷冷一笑，对美国的善意毫无兴趣。”

The letter ended by implying that British snobbery toward America was no less galling to Lamont than were the financial betrayals of the 1930s. He referred to an inequality behind the fraternal Anglo-American facade: “Meanwhile, Britain, with the exception of a few of us, has, as I have intimated, never shown any great interest in America unless or until she needed America’s help desperately. Tens of thousands of Americans would journey annually to the other side. But I can number on the fingers of less than my two hands the number of eminent British persons who have ever been interested to visit America.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375197)
这封信最后暗示，英国人对美国的傲慢态度，对蒙特而言，丝毫不亚于 1930 年代那些背信弃义的金融行为带给他的刺痛。他在信中提到了英美两国表面兄弟情谊背后隐藏的不平等：“与此同时，除我们少数人外，英国——正如我此前所指出的——从未对美国表现出多大兴趣，除非或直到它急需美国的帮助。每年成千上万的美国人会前往英国，但曾有兴趣访问美国的英国名流，我用十个手指头都数得过来。”

It seemed a strange time to kick the British, who had suffered the winter bombings in London, Coventry, and Plymouth. Those radical American pamphleteers who had portrayed the House of Morgan as fawning, uncritical Anglophiles—how startled they would have been by this letter from Lamont. He showed it to Leffingwell, who actually thought it sounded too apologetic. “If I were talking to Americans, I might be saying the same things,” Leffingwell confessed, “but talking to Britishers I think it unduly encourages their sense of superiority to colonials and Americans.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375396)
在伦敦、考文垂和普利茅斯饱受冬季轰炸之苦的英国人面前，落井下石似乎有些不合时宜。那些曾将摩根财团描绘成阿谀奉承、毫无批判精神的亲英派的激进美国小册子作者们——若是看到拉蒙特的这封信，该会多么震惊啊。他把信拿给莱芬韦尔看，莱芬韦尔却觉得这封信听起来过于道歉了。“如果我是跟美国人谈话，我可能也会说同样的话，”莱芬韦尔坦言，“但跟英国人谈话时，我认为这会过分助长他们对殖民者和美国人的优越感。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375396)

Tom Catto replied with gallantry. To be sure, he was a high Treasury adviser and afraid to alienate an influential American. But Catto also had considerable personal skill in handling delicate matters. He wrote a letter of such dignity that it perhaps reminded the J. P. Morgan partners of why Britain’s restoration as a world financial center had for decades been such an emotional issue with them:
汤姆·卡托风度翩翩地回了信。诚然，他作为财政部高级顾问，不愿得罪一位有影响力的美国人。但卡托在处理棘手事务上也颇具个人技巧。他写了一封如此庄重的信，或许让 J.P.摩根的合伙人想起了为何几十年来，英国作为世界金融中心的复兴对他们而言一直是个如此情感化的问题：

> I was much interested in your letter and you must never think that hitting straight from the shoulder on such matters upsets me in any way. We have known each other too many years for that. . . . Whatever our shortcomings and however short our memory may be, we are cheered and encouraged by the knowledge that your great country is with us in our struggle. We have entire confidence that in the end that will mean victory! . . . It is a long road that has no turning. When we reach that turning, I believe Hitler and his gangsters will get a surprise. . . . Do not worry about us. We are all cheerful. We have had a few knocks but we can take them, indeed one hears less of the proverbial British grousing in these times than in days of peace.[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375577)
> 你的来信让我非常感兴趣，千万别以为这样直截了当地谈论这些事情会让我感到任何不适。我们相识多年，早已无需顾虑这些……无论我们自身有多少缺点，无论记忆多么短暂，得知伟大的贵国与我们并肩作战，这都令我们备受鼓舞。我们坚信，最终这将意味着胜利！……路虽漫长，必有转弯之时。等到了那个转机，我相信希特勒和他的暴徒们定会大吃一惊。……不必为我们担忧。我们所有人都情绪高昂。虽然遭受了一些打击，但我们扛得住——说实话，比起和平时期，现在反倒少了英国人那种出了名的抱怨声。

LATER Lamont would tell of the time when a “heavy fire curtain” fell between J. P. Morgan and Morgan Grenfell, and the House of Morgan was internally divided.[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375780) One partner didn’t live to see the curtain rise. Teddy Grenfell—Lord Saint Just—died ten days before Pearl Harbor. In the late 1930s, he’d had heart and lung problems and was frail and bedridden for months at a time. Doctors recommended golf at Sandwich or West Indian cruises with his wife to restore his health.
后来拉蒙特会讲述那段时期，一道“密集的火幕”横亘在 J. P. 摩根与摩根建富之间，摩根家族内部因此分裂。有位合伙人没能活着看到这道火幕拉开——泰迪·格伦费尔，即圣贾斯特勋爵，在珍珠港事件前十日去世。三十年代末，他患有心肺疾病，身体孱弱，每次都要卧床数月。医生建议他去桑威奇打高尔夫，或与妻子乘船游览西印度群岛，以恢复健康。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375780)

Grenfell belonged to a vanishing species—the diplomatic banker. His work was often an inseparable blend of private and public purpose. Cool and dapper, he had been a Morgan sphinx, cloaked in mystery, working unseen in the top echelons of government and finance. “English Bankers and Houses are very much more secretive than those in New York,” he told Lamont, and secrecy was his unchanging creed.[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375972) He believed implicitly in the wisdom of his class, country, and profession and had no patience with reformers. His mind was acute, his predictions unerring, his tailoring immaculate, and his pose debonair. But his sympathies were limited and his tolerance dim. He saw bankers defending immutable truths against political folly and public ignorance. He would have been misplaced in the coming Casino Age, when governments, not private bankers, would assume financial leadership. So strong was Gren-fell’s friendship with Jack Morgan that his death would weaken the tie between the New York and London houses.
格伦费尔属于一个即将消失的物种——外交官式的银行家。他的工作常常是私利与公益难分彼此的混合体。冷静而整洁，他是摩根银行的一位斯芬克斯，裹在神秘之中，在政府和金融界的最高层暗中运作。"英国的银行家和机构比纽约的要神秘得多，"他告诉拉蒙特，而保密是他始终不变的信念。 [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2375972) 他深信自己阶层、国家和职业的智慧，对改革者毫无耐心。他头脑敏锐，预言准确，衣着考究，姿态潇洒。但他的同情心有限，容忍度也黯淡。他认为银行家们是在捍卫永恒不变的真理，对抗政治愚蠢和公众无知。他注定无法适应即将到来的赌场时代——那时，金融领导权将由政府而非私人银行家掌握。 Gren-fell 与 Jack Morgan 的友谊如此深厚，以致他的去世削弱了纽约分行与伦敦分行之间的联系。

EVEN with Europe at war, Tom Lamont didn’t shed his Panglossian tendency to predict favorable outcomes in world affairs. He expected Japan to refrain from war against the Allies, not from any scruples, but because self-interest dictated that it be on the winning side. Three weeks before Pearl Harbor, he told Walter Lippmann that if Japan “were on the losing side she would lose complete influence in the whole Pacific region and would sink there to the status of a second or third rate power. . . . I may be 100% mistaken, but I am really not worried at all about the Far East for the moment.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376166) On December 7, 1941, Japan attacked Pearl Harbor, and yet another Lamont illusion was shattered. In the most eloquent expression of disgust with Japan, Lamont joined that year with Henry Luce in merging eight China aid groups into United China Relief. The Japanese presence on Wall Street was abolished as the New York State superintendent of banks seized the assets of the Yokohama Specie Bank, Japan’s fiscal agent before the war.
即使欧洲战火纷飞，Tom Lamont 依然没有改掉他那盲目乐观地预测世界事务的倾向。他预计日本不会对同盟国开战，并非出于任何顾忌，而是因为自身利益决定了它应该站在胜利者一方。珍珠港事件前三周，他对 Walter Lippmann 说，如果日本“战败，它将丧失在整个太平洋地区的全部影响力，沦为二流甚至三流国家……我可能百分之百错了，但我目前确实一点也不担心远东。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376166) 1941 年 12 月 7 日，日本袭击珍珠港，Lamont 的又一个幻想破灭了。在那一年，他以最强烈的姿态表达对日本的厌恶，与 Henry Luce 一同将八个援华团体合并为 United China Relief。 日本在华尔街的势力被彻底清除——纽约州银行监管局没收了横滨正金银行的资产，这家银行战前曾是日本的财务代理机构。

U.S. entry into the war in 1941 repaired the breach within the House of Morgan. With the United States and Britain fighting side by side, Morgan partners revived the belief that their countries were destined to rule the world jointly. In a new spirit of forgiveness, Lamont took to citing English, Scottish, and Irish blood in American veins as the country’s real source of strength. Vindictive toward Britain two years before, Russell Leffingwell said warmly, “To my mind the only thing worth fighting for is to save England and the British Empire. For that I would shed every drop of blood in my own veins, and let many millions of Americans shed theirs too.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376362)
1941 年美国加入二战，弥合了摩根财团内部的裂痕。随着美英并肩作战，摩根合伙人重新燃起信念：他们的国家注定要共同主宰世界。怀着宽恕的新心态，拉蒙特开始引用美国血液中流淌的英格兰、苏格兰和爱尔兰血统，称其为美国真正的力量源泉。两年前还对英国怀有敌意的拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔，此时热情洋溢地说道：“依我看，唯一值得为之战斗的就是拯救英国和大英帝国。为此，我愿意流尽自己最后一滴血，也愿意让数百万美国人同样献出生命。”

J. P. Morgan and Company resumed its customary role of defending the mother country. When *Life* magazine published an open letter saying the war shouldn’t be fought so Britain could keep her empire, Lamont sparred with Henry Luce. The bank had known Luce well ever since his Yale classmate Henry P. Davison, Jr., became the first investor in *Time* magazine and a company director. Lamont now told him that America had its own imperialism and backed its own Latin American dictators: “Why do we yell about ’imperialism’ when we are busy day and night scheming to get the whole Caribbean under our control and sweeping all of Latin America into our orbit by lavish loans and diplomatic manoeuvres?”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376554)
J. P. 摩根公司重新拾起保卫祖国的老本行。当《生活》杂志发表公开信称不应为了英国保住其帝国而打这场战争时，拉蒙特与亨利·卢斯展开了辩论。自打亨利·P·戴维森二世（卢斯的耶鲁同学）成为《时代》杂志首位投资者并担任公司董事以来，摩根银行与卢斯一直交情匪浅。拉蒙特此时告诉他，美国也有自己的帝国主义行径，并且扶持着拉丁美洲的独裁者们：“当我们日日夜夜忙着谋划把整个加勒比海置于控制之下，又通过慷慨贷款和外交手段把整个拉丁美洲拉入自己的轨道时，我们凭什么对‘帝国主义’大喊大叫？” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376554)

A new rapport between Roosevelt and Jack Morgan was evident in November 1941, when labor leader John L. Lewis ordered a strike against captive coal mines owned by the steel companies. When Roosevelt appealed for patriotic restraint, Lewis said his adversary should also be restrained. “My adversary is a rich man named Morgan, who lives in New York,” he declared.[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376750) To Roosevelt, Lamont protested this insinuation that U.S. Steel was just a tool of Jack’s. Not only did Roosevelt side with Jack—a novelty in itself—but he did so with a new geniality. A class traitor no more, he told Lamont: “I was really angry at Lewis’ unwarranted, untrue, and demagogic statement about Jack. . . . When you see Jack, tell him for me not to concern himself any more about Lewis’ attack, for after many years of observation, I have come reluctantly to the conclusion that Lewis’ is a psychopathic condition.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376921)
1941 年 11 月，罗斯福与杰克·摩根之间建立了一种新的默契，当时劳工领袖约翰·L·刘易斯下令对钢铁公司拥有的自营煤矿举行罢工。当罗斯福呼吁以爱国精神克制时，刘易斯表示他的对手也应该克制。"我的对手是个叫摩根的富翁，他住在纽约，"他宣称。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376750) 对此，拉蒙特向罗斯福抗议这种暗示——即美国钢铁公司只是杰克的工具。罗斯福不仅站在杰克一边——这本身就很新奇——而且以一种前所未有的和蔼态度这样做。他不再是阶级叛徒，他对拉蒙特说："刘易斯关于杰克的那番毫无根据、不真实且煽动性的言论让我非常生气……你见到杰克时，替我告诉他，别再为刘易斯的攻击烦恼了，因为经过多年观察，我不得不遗憾地得出结论：刘易斯有心理问题。" [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2376921)

Able to slough off divisive, prewar domestic issues, FDR and the Morgan partners became fast friends. After Lamont congratulated him for declaring war, Roosevelt wired back, “Generous words of approval from an old friend like you are heartening.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2377120) They swapped jokes, anecdotes, and amusing press clips, including one citing Communist leader Earl Browder’s accusation that Roosevelt had prevailed upon Lamont and Walter Lippmann to engineer Willkie’s nomination. In early 1942, Lamont spent almost an hour at the White House speculating as to how the U.S. might use Fort Knox gold in the postwar world to stabilize currencies. Roosevelt said America was trusted more in continental Europe than was Britain. This was the relationship Lamont had craved—full of secrets, confidences, and back scratching. Turning to the subject of Churchill, FDR confided to Lamont that Winston didn’t have the economic mind they had.[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2377301) (Yet in 1939, the British embassy in Washington filed this tart appraisal of Roosevelt: “His knowledge of certain subjects, particularly finance and economics, is superficial.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2377517)) At Roosevelt’s request, Lamont appeared at a Madison Square Garden rally for Soviet-American friendship—the one time Tom appeared politically with his left-wing son, Corliss.
能够摆脱战前那些分裂国内局势的琐事，罗斯福与摩根合伙人们很快就成了密友。拉蒙特祝贺他宣战后，罗斯福回电说：“能得到你这样的老朋友的慷慨赞许，实在令人振奋。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2377120) 他们互相分享笑话、趣闻和有趣的新闻剪报——其中一则引用共产党领袖厄尔·白劳德的指控，称罗斯福曾说服拉蒙特和沃尔特·李普曼策划了威尔基的提名。1942 年初，拉蒙特在白宫待了将近一个小时，探讨美国在战后如何利用诺克斯堡的黄金来稳定货币。罗斯福表示，在欧洲大陆，美国比英国更受信任。这正是拉蒙特梦寐以求的关系——充满秘密、信任与相互扶持。谈到丘吉尔时，罗斯福向拉蒙特透露，温斯顿并不具备他们那样的经济头脑。 然而在 1939 年，英国驻华盛顿大使馆对罗斯福做出了这样尖刻的评价：“他在某些领域，尤其是金融和经济方面的知识相当肤浅。”应罗斯福的请求，拉蒙特出现在麦迪逊广场花园的一场美苏友好集会上——这是汤姆唯一一次在政治上与他的左翼儿子科利斯同台亮相。

What strengthened ties between Roosevelt and the House of Morgan was that both felt beleaguered by the same isolationist forces. In the spring of 1942, Leffingwell told the president that the war effort required more parades, brass bands, and flag-waving. Agreeing, Roosevelt added that “the real trouble is not in the people or the leaders, but in a gang which unfortunately survives—made up mostly of those who were isolationists before December seventh and who are actuated today by various motives in their effort to instill disunity in the country.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2377668) So the new concord between Roosevelt and Morgans corresponded to the old political axiom that the enemy of my enemy is my friend. War had finally made peace between the White House and the House of Morgan.
让罗斯福与摩根家族关系更加紧密的原因是，他们都感受到了同一股孤立主义势力的围攻。1942 年春，莱芬韦尔向总统表示，战争需要更多游行、军乐队和挥舞旗帜的场景。罗斯福对此表示赞同，并补充道：“真正的麻烦不在于民众或领袖，而在于一个不幸残存下来的团伙——他们主要由 12 月 7 日之前的孤立主义者组成，如今出于各种动机，竭力在国内制造分裂。” [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2377668) 因此，罗斯福与摩根家族之间的新和解，印证了那句古老的政治格言：敌人的敌人就是朋友。战争最终让白宫与摩根家族握手言和。
