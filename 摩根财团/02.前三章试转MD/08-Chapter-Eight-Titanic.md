# Chapter Eight: Titanic

MOROSE and fatalistic in his last years, Pierpont felt misunderstood by the public and angered by the uproar over his trusts. He shook his cane menacingly at reporters, a murderous gleam in his eyes. He wouldn’t admit to legitimate public curiosity about his affairs. At Dover House in 1911, he burned the bound letters he had sent to Junius for thirty-three years, destroying perhaps the most important chronicle of Anglo-American finance in the late nineteenth century. He craved a privacy impossible for the world’s most famous banker. Like a ghost, he brooded in the West Room of his library, beneath stained-glass windows and thick draperies that muffled the sounds of a changing world.
暮年之际，皮尔庞特郁郁寡欢，宿命论笼罩心头。他深感公众误解自己，对其托拉斯帝国的口诛笔伐更令他怒不可遏。面对记者，他威胁般挥舞手杖，眼中闪过凶光。至于公众对他事务的合理好奇，他绝不肯承认。1911 年在多佛庄园，他亲手焚毁了三十三年来写给朱尼厄斯的全部信件——这些本可能是记录十九世纪末英美金融史最珍贵的编年史料。作为举世闻名的银行家，他渴望的私密空间却永远无法企及。他像幽灵般逡巡于图书馆西厅，彩绘玻璃窗和厚重帷幔隔绝了变革时代的喧嚣，他就在那里沉思默想。

He spent much of his time in Europe, escaping the din of Progressive politics. His *wanderlust* never deserted him. From European spas, he would notify Jack of the next stop on his itinerary, adding those ever awkward words, “advise mother.” He felt at home in many places. Once asked to name his favorite spots, he replied, “New York, because it is my home; London, because it is my second home; Rome and Khargeh.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242635)
他大部分时间都待在欧洲，躲避进步政治的喧嚣。他对旅行的热爱从未消退。从欧洲的各个温泉胜地，他会通知杰克自己行程的下一站，并附上那句总是令人尴尬的话：“告知母亲。”他在许多地方都感到自在。有一次被问及最喜欢的地方，他回答道：“纽约，因为那是我的家；伦敦，因为那是我的第二故乡；还有罗马和哈尔加。”

Egypt, in particular, held a mystical charm for him, and he visited it three times in his last three years and helped to bankroll the Metropolitan Museum’s Egyptian excavations. (One 1909 photograph shows an oversize Pierpont on a small donkey galloping into the desert ahead of his flabbergasted guides.) The excavations at Khargeh, four hundred miles southwest of Cairo, so intrigued him that he asked Thomas Cook and Sons to construct a steel Nile steamer named the *Khargeh.* From this paddle-wheel boat, he would pitch coins into the water, which were fished up by boys diving from the Nile’s bank.
他对埃及尤其着迷，在生命的最后三年里三度造访，并为大都会艺术博物馆在埃及的考古发掘提供资助。（一张 1909 年的照片中，身材魁梧的皮尔庞特骑着一头小毛驴飞奔入沙漠，把向导们惊得目瞪口呆。）开罗西南四百英里处的哈尔加遗址让他深感兴趣，于是他委托托马斯·库克父子公司建造一艘名为“哈尔加号”的尼罗河钢制蒸汽船。他常常从这艘明轮船向水中抛掷钱币，引得尼罗河岸边的男孩们纷纷跃入水中捞取。

Pierpont was a lonely man, and fame probably only deepened his isolation. His first biographer, Carl Hovey, wrote, “It is said there are scarcely fifty men in the financial district who have a speaking acquaintance with Morgan.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242811) Pierpont had a wide business acquaintance, but few associates knew him well. Hence, he relied on his family for emotional sustenance. This made especially bruising a feud with his youngest child, Anne Tracy, who was six years Jack’s junior. Pierpont Morgan could conquer the world but not his daughter Anne. She was an athletic, spirited girl who liked golf and tennis and rebelled against her formal upbringing. Of all Pierpont’s children, Anne most resembled him temperamentally: she was bright, stubborn, imperious, and highly opinionated. Elizabeth Drexel, later the wife of socialite Harry Lehr, recalled her as a “thin lanky child with an elfin face and penetrating eyes” but with “a personality and a will as strong as \[Pierpont’s\] own and a disconcerting habit of putting her elders in the wrong.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242995) Once, at a dinner party with Pierpont’s cronies, her father peered down the table and asked her what she planned to be when she grew up. “Something better than a rich fool, anyway,” she snapped.[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243171) Despite these gibes, she was close to her father and often accompanied him to Europe aboard *Corsair III.* Once, she served as host to the kaiser aboard the yacht.
皮尔庞特是个孤独的人，名声或许只会让他的孤寂更深。他的首位传记作者卡尔·霍维写道：“据说金融区里能与摩根说得上话的人，不超过五十个。”皮尔庞特在商场上交际甚广，但真正了解他的人寥寥无几。因此，他在情感上极度依赖家人。这也使得他与最小的孩子安妮·特雷西——比杰克小六岁的女儿——之间的冲突格外伤人。皮尔庞特·摩根能征服世界，却拿自己的女儿安妮毫无办法。她是个活泼好动的姑娘，喜欢打高尔夫和网球，对刻板的教养方式颇为叛逆。在所有孩子中，安妮在性情上与皮尔庞特最为相像：聪明、固执、专横，且极有主见。 伊丽莎白·德雷克塞尔，后来嫁给了社交名流哈里·莱尔，回忆起她时形容为“一个瘦高个儿的孩子，精灵般的面孔，目光锐利”，但“拥有和皮尔庞特一样坚强的个性和意志，而且有个令人不安的习惯，总能让她长辈们理亏。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2242995) 有一次，在皮尔庞特那些老友的晚宴上，她父亲从餐桌那头盯着她，问她长大后想做什么。“反正比一个有钱的傻瓜强，”她厉声说道。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243171) 尽管有这些冷嘲热讽，她与父亲关系却很亲近，经常陪他乘坐“Corsair III”号去欧洲。有一次，她还在游艇上担任了德皇的东道主。

By the early 1900s, Anne, now in her early thirties, had grown into a tall young woman with short hair swept back on the sides, a strong nose, dark eyebrows, and her father’s intense gaze. She had his executive talents and childlike simplicity and hated cartoonists who mocked her father’s nose. She was big and somewhat matronly but also stylish in dress. In 1903, Daisy Harriman, a famous Washington hostess, brought her in as a founder of the Colony Club, the first American ladies’ club, patterned after a British gentlemen’s club. At Thirtieth Street and Madison Avenue, it was designed by Stanford White and had a marble swimming pool and Turkish baths. Rules forbade men above the first floor. Pierpont had no sympathy for the project and lectured the ladies that “a woman’s best and safest club is her own home.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243296) Predictably, Dorothy Whitney was an early member.
到 20 世纪初，三十出头的安妮已长成高挑的年轻女子，短发向后梳拢，鼻梁挺拔，浓眉下透着父亲那般锐利的目光。她继承了父亲的干练与赤子之心，痛恨那些嘲讽父亲鼻子的漫画家。她身材高大略显富态，却衣着时髦。1903 年，华盛顿名媛黛西·哈里曼邀请她共同创办殖民地俱乐部——美国首家女性俱乐部，仿照英国绅士俱乐部的模式，选址在第三十街与麦迪逊大道交汇处，由斯坦福·怀特设计，内设大理石泳池和土耳其浴室。规定禁止男士进入二楼以上。皮尔庞特对此毫无兴趣，还训诫女士们说：“女人最好最安全的俱乐部就是自己的家。”不出所料，多萝西·惠特尼成了早期会员。

During the founding of this project, Anne met two older women who would change her life. One was the stoutly mannish Bessie Marbury, the American theatrical agent for George Bernard Shaw and Oscar Wilde; the other was Elsie de Wolfe, the voguish former society girl and actress, now a famed interior designer for her work on the Colony Club. In 1908, Anne, thirty-five, entered into a *menage a trois* with these two women at their Villa Trianon in Versailles. With its formal gardens, topiary, and trimmed lawns, the Villa Trianon was an incongruously aristocratic setting for such a daring arrangement. De Wolfe designed a dressing room that fit Anne’s contradictory nature; on its formal mantelpiece were both a French bust and a leopard-skin velvet rug.
在这个项目创立之初，安妮遇到了两位改变她一生的年长女性。一位是身材壮硕、气质偏男性的贝西·马伯里，她是萧伯纳和王尔德在美国的戏剧经纪人；另一位是埃尔西·德·沃尔夫，这位昔日时髦的上流社会名媛兼演员，如今已是因科洛尼俱乐部室内设计而声名鹊起的知名设计师。1908 年，35 岁的安妮与这两位女性在凡尔赛的特里亚农别墅开始了三人共居的生活。这座别墅有着规整的花园、修剪整齐的树篱与草坪，如此前卫的居住安排，却偏偏发生在这样一座极具贵族气质的宅邸里，显得格外另类。德·沃尔夫为安妮设计了一间贴合她矛盾天性的更衣室——正式壁炉台上，既摆放着一尊法国半身像，又铺着一张豹皮绒地毯。

Over the years, these three patrician ladies pioneered in many cultural areas. They opened a Broadway dance hall and sponsored Cole Porter’s first musical. They also took up many liberal and feminist causes. Anne supported the strike by women shirtwaist-workers, a largely Jewish group, inspected the sanitary conditions in factories, opened a temperance restaurant in Brooklyn, started a thrift association and vacation fund for young working women, and championed women’s suffrage. On December 31, 1908, she lunched at the White House to discuss social welfare with Teddy Roosevelt, who may well have savored the idea of Pierpont’s extreme discomfiture. Anne’s exposure to her father’s business friends bred considerable cynicism in her. When Lincoln Steffens once told her he liked Judge Gary of U.S. Steel, she said impatiently, “Oh, he’s too plausible. He has taken you in as he does others.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243455)
多年来，这三位贵族女性在诸多文化领域开风气之先。她们开办了一家百老汇舞厅，并为 Cole Porter 的第一部音乐剧提供赞助。同时，她们还投身于许多自由主义与女权主义事业。安妮支持衬衫女工的罢工——这群女工多为犹太裔，她检查工厂的卫生条件，在布鲁克林开设了一家禁酒餐厅，为年轻女工创办储蓄协会和度假基金，并力挺妇女选举权。1908 年 12 月 31 日，她在白宫与西奥多·罗斯福共进午餐，讨论社会福利问题，罗斯福总统很可能颇为乐见皮尔庞特为此感到极度难堪。安妮因接触父亲生意圈的朋友而对这类人深感不屑。当林肯·斯蒂芬斯有一次告诉她，他很欣赏美国钢铁公司的加里法官时，她不耐烦地说：“哦，他太会糊弄人了。他把你骗了，就像他骗别人一样。”

Pierpont was outraged by Anne’s liberal, unconventional behavior. If the three women were discreet about their private affairs—even de Wolfe’s biographer shrinks from using the word *lesbian*—they threw gala parties that attracted attention. Bernard Berenson attended their gatherings, as did Pierpont’s mistress, Maxine Elliott, who had acted with de Wolfe. The chain-smoking Anne was in an agonizing situation. As one of the world’s richest young women, she was relentlessly courted by titled Europeans. Scandal sheets frequently reported her upcoming engagement to the French count Boni de Castellane, which never came about. All the while, she dove deeper into causes and took stands that aligned her with her father’s critics.
皮尔庞特对安妮那种自由不羁、离经叛道的行为大为光火。虽说这三个女人对自己的私生活守口如瓶——连德沃尔夫的传记作者都避讳使用"女同性恋"这个词——但她们举办的那些盛大派对，却总逃不过众人的目光。伯纳德·贝伦森常来参加她们的聚会，皮尔庞特的情妇玛克辛·埃利奥特也常来——她曾与德沃尔夫同台演出。烟不离手的安妮处境极为煎熬。作为全球最富有的年轻女性之一，欧洲贵族们对她穷追不舍。花边小报隔三差五就报道她即将与法国伯爵博尼·德·卡斯特拉纳订婚的消息，然而这桩婚事始终未曾成真。与此同时，她却愈发深入地投身于各种事业，并且屡屡站在与她父亲的批评者们一致的立场上。

The facts of the rift between Pierpont and Anne are fragmentary. De Wolfe’s biographer Jane S. Smith says Pierpont thought that Bessie Marbury had poisoned Anne’s mind against him. She apparently told Anne that Pierpont used her to cover up his trysts with mistresses when Anne accompanied him to Europe on *Corsair III.* Pierpont’s other children violently disagreed with this interpretation. Pierpont’s middle daughter, Juliet, bristled at references to de Wolfe, while Jack was deeply upset by Anne’s behavior. In her memoirs, Marbury handled the controversy tactfully: “Mr. Morgan was patriarchal in his views. The emancipated woman enjoyed no favor in his eyes, therefore as his daughter, she grew up determined that she must think for herself. ”*[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243616)* She also said of him, “To acknowledge defeat was foreign to his temperament. He was always loyal to his mistakes.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243778)
皮尔庞特与安妮之间裂痕的真相已支离破碎。德沃尔夫的传记作者简·S·史密斯称，皮尔庞特认为是贝西·马伯里在安妮心中埋下了对他的敌意。据称，马伯里曾告诉安妮，当安妮陪同皮尔庞特乘坐"科西嘉三世号"游轮前往欧洲时，皮尔庞特利用她来掩饰自己与其他情妇的幽会。皮尔庞特的其他子女激烈反对这种说法。二女儿朱丽叶听到有人提及德沃尔夫时怒不可遏，而儿子杰克则对安妮的行为深感痛心。马伯里在她的回忆录中巧妙处理了这一争议："摩根先生秉持家长式观念。在他眼中，解放女性从不讨喜，因此作为他的女儿，安妮自幼便决心要独立思考。" [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243616) 她亦如此评价他："承认失败与他的天性相悖。他始终执迷于自己的错误。" [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243778)

Pierpont was wounded by the estrangement. “It broke her father’s heart when she elected to part from him,” one of Anne’s friends told Clarence Barron.[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243912) As we have seen, Pierpont could be grimly implacable when crossed, and he blamed Bessie Marbury for stealing away his daughter. Hence, he found an ingenious way to torture her. Marbury coveted the French Legion of Honor and believed she deserved it for her work in officially representing French dramatists in the English-speaking world. By chance, in 1909, Robert Bacon, the ex-Greek God of Wall Street, was named ambassador to France. Bowing to Pierpont’s wishes, he made sure she was denied the honor. Knowing that the House of Morgan objected prevented Bessie Marbury from ever receiving the government award—even after she spent years raising money for France and donated her Versailles home as a hospital during World War I. De Wolfe won the Croix de Guerre, and Anne was decorated as a commander of the Legion of Honor for running an ambulance corps and performing relief work. But Marbury—notwithstanding letters of praise from former presidents Roosevelt and Taft—couldn’t overcome the French fears of offending Morgan interests. Even beyond his grave, Pierpont Morgan would not be thwarted.
皮尔庞特因这场疏远而深受伤害。安妮的一位朋友告诉克拉伦斯·巴伦：“她决定离开父亲时，简直伤透了他的心。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2243912) 正如我们所见，皮尔庞特在被违逆时会变得冷酷无情、毫不妥协，他将女儿被夺走的账算在了贝西·马伯里头上。于是，他想出一个巧妙的方式来折磨她。马伯里觊觎法国荣誉军团勋章，她自认为凭借在英语世界正式代理法国剧作家作品的工作，理当获此殊荣。巧合的是，1909 年，曾被誉为华尔街希腊男神的罗伯特·培根出任驻法大使。他屈从于皮尔庞特的意愿，确保马伯里无缘此荣誉。由于深知摩根家族抵制，贝西·马伯里始终未能获得这一政府奖项——哪怕她在第一次世界大战期间为法国筹款多年，并将自己凡尔赛的宅邸捐作医院，也未能如愿。 德沃尔夫荣获了法国十字勋章，安妮则因领导救护车队和从事救济工作而被授予荣誉军团指挥官勋章。但马伯里——即便有前总统罗斯福和塔夫脱的亲笔表扬信——也无法消除法国人担心冒犯摩根利益的顾虑。皮尔庞特·摩根即便长眠地下，也无人能阻其锋芒。

PIERPONT’S relationship with Jack improved in his last years, perhaps in reaction to his troubles with Anne and Fanny. Nobody doubted that Jack would take over at the Corner, if only because the bank needed the Morgan name and money. Jack was no slouch and ably handled affairs in his father’s absence. Yet he didn’t have Pierpont’s gargantuan ego. Since boyhood, he had been plagued by secret doubts about himself—it wasn’t clear to him whether he had the intestinal fortitude to head a banking empire. In 1910, he had a collapse that was diagnosed as strain and fatigue. So for a number of reasons, he wanted a strong lieutenant, a powerful regent to take charge of the bank on a day-to-day basis. He preferred the role of constitutional monarch, shaping policy and delegating authority.
皮尔庞特晚年与杰克的关系有所改善，或许是因为他与安妮和范妮之间的矛盾促成了这种转变。没有人怀疑杰克最终会接管“角落”（指摩根银行），光是因为银行需要摩根这个姓氏和资金就足以证明这一点。杰克并非等闲之辈，在父亲不在时总能妥善处理事务。但他没有皮尔庞特那种膨胀的自我。从少年时代起，他就一直被内心的自我怀疑所困扰——他不确定自己是否有足够的胆魄去掌控一个银行帝国。1910 年，他因压力与疲劳而精神崩溃。因此，出于多种原因，他需要一位得力的副手，一位强有力的摄政者来负责银行的日常运营。他更愿意扮演立宪君主式的角色，制定政策并下放权力。

Two people competed for the position—Harry Davison and George Perkins. Perkins carried several liabilities. He was always shadowed by the insurance scandal from his years at New York Life. But the cause of Perkins’s downfall would be that he saw himself as a king in his own right, not simply a Morgan vassal. At his Riverdale estate, he had nine servants, a swimming pool, a ballroom, and a bowling alley. In 1906, he bought the world’s largest custom-made car—an eleven-foot French monstrosity with ebony woodwork, a writing desk, and a washstand-table. His worst sin may have been not showing due deference to the Morgans. He sneered at Jack and thought he was more highly qualified to run the bank. He sometimes made decisions without consulting the Morgans. In 1910, Pierpont told Harry Davison in London that Perkins had defied his wishes on a financing arrangement for the Studebaker Company, news that Davison passed along to Perkins. Perkins then wrote to Pierpont saying, “I am very deeply disturbed by one remark that Davison made, viz., that you felt I had gone ahead and deliberately disregarded an understanding with you and concluded the business to suit myself.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244073) Six months later, Perkins left the bank. He was apparently forced out. Tom Lamont later said that Perkins “didn’t leave of his own accord. Morgan thought he had been a little second-rate on some deals.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244249) When he resigned, Perkins took \$5.5 million of his own securities out of the bank—one of many fortunes harvested at the House of Morgan.
两人争夺同一个职位——哈里·戴维森和乔治·珀金斯。珀金斯身上背着几个包袱：他在纽约人寿任职期间的保险丑闻始终如影随形。但他栽跟头的真正原因，是他把自己当成了一方诸侯，而非摩根家族的附庸。在里弗代尔的庄园里，他雇了九名仆人，拥有游泳池、舞厅和保龄球馆。1906 年，他买下了全球最大的定制汽车——一辆 11 英尺长的法国怪物车，配有乌木内饰、写字台和洗手台。他最大的罪过或许是在摩根家族面前缺乏应有的敬意。他对杰克嗤之以鼻，自认更有资格执掌银行。有时他不经摩根家族同意就擅自决策。1910 年，皮尔庞特在伦敦告诉哈里·戴维森，珀金斯在斯图德贝克公司的融资安排上违抗了他的意愿——戴维森随后把这话传给了珀金斯。 珀金斯随后给皮尔庞特写信说：“戴维森有句话让我非常不安，他说你感觉我无视了与你之间的默契，擅自把事情定下来，只图自己方便。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244073) 六个月后，珀金斯离开了银行。他显然是被逼走的。汤姆·拉蒙特后来评价道，珀金斯“并非自愿离开。摩根觉得他在某些交易上有点二流水平。”” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244249) 辞职时，珀金斯从银行拿出了自己名下的 550 万美元证券——这只是摩根财团里挖出的众多财富之一。

For those skilled at reading the tea leaves, it grew clear that Henry Pomeroy Davison would become chief operating executive. After he became a partner in January 1909, he seemed to have almost exclusive access to Pierpont in his library. As was clear in the 1907 panic, the handsome Davison had star quality, a square-jawed toughness noticed by everyone on Wall Street. He had grown up in a small Pennsylvania town, the son of a farm-tools dealer and poor relation in a family of bankers. He skipped college when Harvard denied his scholarship application. He had a steely, distinguished look—long eyebrows, hair parted down the middle, and a wide, firm mouth.
对于善于察言观色的人来说，局势已日渐明朗：亨利·波默罗伊·戴维森将成为首席运营执行官。1909 年 1 月他成为合伙人后，似乎几乎能独享与皮尔庞特在其图书室会面的机会。1907 年金融危机时已可见端倪，这位英俊的戴维森具备明星般的气质——方下巴的坚毅模样令华尔街所有人为之侧目。他成长于宾夕法尼亚州的一个小镇，父亲是农具经销商，而他在银行家家族中属于穷亲戚。因哈佛拒绝了他的奖学金申请，他便放弃了大学。他拥有一副钢铁般冷峻出众的相貌——长眉、中分发型，以及宽厚而坚定的嘴唇。

Davison started out working for a bank in Bridgeport, Connecticut. One bank director was P. T. Barnum, who liked him and invited him to join a weekly whist game. In 1893, Davison married Kate Trubee, and they moved to New York so Harry could start work at the Astor Trust Company. One day, a crank appeared at his teller’s window, pointed a gun at Davison, and passed him a \$1-million check he wanted to cash, payable to “The Almighty.” The cool, quick-witted Davison figured out a way to foil the holdup. He doled out the money in small bills and kept saying in a loud, reverential voice, “A million dollars for the Almighty.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244411) This gave a bank guard time to notify the police, who arrested the man.
戴维森最初在康涅狄格州布里奇波特的一家银行工作。银行的一位董事是 P·T·巴纳姆，他很欣赏戴维森，便邀请他参加每周的惠斯特牌局。1893 年，戴维森与凯特·特鲁比结婚，随后夫妇俩搬到纽约，好让哈里进入阿斯托信托公司工作。有一天，一个怪人出现在他的出纳窗口前，拿枪指着戴维森，递过一张百万美元支票要兑现，收款人写的是“全能之神”。冷静机智的戴维森想出了办法智取这场劫案。他故意用小额钞票慢慢数钱，同时用庄重洪亮的声音不断念叨：“一百万美金献给全能之神。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244411) 这为银行保安争取到时间报警，警方随即逮捕了那名劫匪。

Davison rose quickly as a protege of George F. Baker, Pierpont’s jowly, side-whiskered chum and head of the First National Bank. He moved from the Astor Trust to another Baker bank, the Liberty. Then Baker said, “Davison, I think you’d better move your desk up here with us,” and he became a First National vice-president. While there, he organized Bankers Trust in 1903, assisted in the 1907 panic negotiations, and represented Wall Street on Senator Aldrich’s National Monetary Commission. These exploits won the attention of Pierpont, who later said, “I always believe everything Mr. Davison tells me.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244562)
戴维森作为皮尔庞特那位下巴松弛、蓄着鬓须的老友兼第一国民银行总裁乔治·F·贝克的得意门生，迅速崛起。他从阿斯特信托转投到贝克旗下的另一家银行——自由银行。随后贝克说道：“戴维森，我觉得你最好把办公桌搬到我这儿来。”于是他便成了第一国民银行的副总裁。在此期间，他于 1903 年创办了银行家信托公司，在 1907 年金融危机中协助谈判，并代表华尔街参加参议员奥尔德里奇领导的国家货币委员会。这些成就引起了皮尔庞特的注意，后来皮尔庞特曾说：“戴维森先生说的话，我句句信以为真。”

Anecdotes about Davison convey vigor, geniality, and self-confidence. Manly and decisive, he shot moose in Maine and elephant, buffalo, rhino, hippos, and antelope during a shooting trip up the White Nile. Once he dreamed he was a small-town Pennsylvania bank clerk. In a sweat, he couldn’t balance the books. When he awoke, his wife asked what had happened. “I finally solved the problem; I bought the bank,” he replied.[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244688) Immensely sociable, he seldom sat down to dine at his North Shore estate, Peacock Point, with fewer than twenty guests. Taking people under his wing, he had a way of guiding them, sometimes brusquely and a bit intrusively. He was the great talent scout in Morgan history and brought Tom Lamont, Dwight Morrow, Ben Strong, and John Davis into the bank’s orbit.
关于戴维森的轶事展现了他的活力、亲和力与自信。他刚毅果决，曾在缅因州射杀驼鹿，在沿白尼罗河狩猎途中猎杀大象、水牛、犀牛、河马和羚羊。有次他梦见自己是宾夕法尼亚小镇银行的职员，急得满头大汗，账目怎么都对不平。醒来后妻子问发生了什么，他答道："我总算解决了问题——我把那家银行买下来了。" [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244688) 他极爱社交，在自己位于北岸的"孔雀角"庄园用餐时，餐桌上鲜少少于二十位宾客。他乐于提携后辈，指导方式有时直率到近乎冒昧。作为摩根史上最伟大的伯乐，他将托马斯·拉蒙特、德怀特·莫罗、本·斯特朗和约翰·戴维斯等人招入银行麾下。

Tom Lamont said that to young bankers on Wall Street, Davison “was not simply a leader. He was a king, an idol, if you please.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244851) Lamont was Davison’s most important find. After college, he had worked for two years as a reporter on the *New York Tribune.* (Later he would brilliantly parlay this fleeting experience into an image of himself as an old newspaperman.) After salvaging a failing import-export house through clever newspaper advertisements, he renamed it Lamont, Corliss and Company. On Wall Street, he acquired a reputation for straightening out troubled companies. This caught the attention of Harry Davison, his neighbor in Englewood, New Jersey.
汤姆·拉蒙特曾评价，在华尔街年轻银行家眼中，戴维森"远不止是一位领导者。他简直是国王、是偶像。"拉蒙特是戴维森最重要的伯乐。大学毕业后，他曾在《纽约论坛报》当了两年记者。（后来，他巧妙地将这段短暂经历包装成老报人形象。）通过巧妙的报纸广告挽救了一家濒临破产的进出口公司后，他将公司更名为"拉蒙特-科利斯公司"。在华尔街，他以擅长整顿陷入困境的企业而闻名。这引起了新泽西州恩格尔伍德邻居哈里·戴维森的注意。

Tom Lamont never pushed or clawed his way to the top. He did everything easily, jauntily, effortlessly. In 1903, at the age of thirty-three, he was returning home on the commuter train to Englewood when Harry Davison took his life in hand. As he entered the car, Davison was, musing about choices for a secretary-treasurer post at the new Bankers Trust. When Lamont appeared, Davison saw his man. Lamont laughed at the offer. “But I don’t know the first thing about banking. All my brief business life I have been borrowing money—not lending it.” “Fine,” said Davison, “that’s just why we want you. A fearless borrower like you ought to make a prudent lender.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244986) It was a momentous intuition.
汤姆·拉蒙特从未费尽心机或奋力攀爬至顶峰。他做任何事都轻松自如、潇洒从容、毫不费力。1903 年，他三十三岁，正乘坐通勤火车返回恩格尔伍德的家时，哈里·戴维森掌控了他的人生走向。戴维森走进车厢时，正思索着为新组建的银行家信托公司物色一位秘书兼财务主管的人选。拉蒙特一出现，戴维森便认定了他。拉蒙特对这个邀请付之一笑：“可我对银行业一窍不通。我短暂的商界生涯里一直在借钱——从未贷过款。”“很好，”戴维森说，“这正是我们想要你的原因。像你这样无所畏惧的借款人，理应成为谨慎的贷款人。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2244986) 这是一次意义深远的直觉判断。

Lamont followed in Davison’s footsteps, taking his spot as vice-president at First National Bank in 1909. In late 1910, Pierpont summoned him. “You see that room over there? It’s vacant,” he said. “Beginning next Monday, I want you to occupy it.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245154) Lamont professed bewilderment. “But what can I do for you that is worth while?” he asked. “Oh, you’ll find plenty to keep you busy, just do whatever you see before you that needs to be done.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245306) Was Lamont’s reluctance simple candor—or splendid calculation?
拉蒙特步戴维森后尘，于 1909 年接替其职位，成为第一国民银行的副总裁。1910 年末，皮尔庞特把他叫来。“你看见那边那个房间了吗？空着呢，”他说，“从下周一算起，我要你坐进去。”拉蒙特一脸茫然。“可是，我能为您做什么有价值的事呢？”他问道。“哦，有的是活让你忙，眼前有什么需要做的，你就做什么。”拉蒙特的推辞究竟是出于单纯的坦诚——还是精明的算计？

Interestingly, with both Davison and Pierpont, Lamont refused the crown being proffered. He told Pierpont he had a dream of traveling three months each year. Far from being put off, Pierpont said, “Why, of course, take off as much time as you like. That is entirely in your hands.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245474) He advised Lamont to take a cruise down the Nile, bringing along a couple of nurses for his children. There was again a certain guile in Lamont’s handling of the offer. He must have known that Pierpont spent months abroad each year. Was he holding up a mirror to the old tycoon, saying tacitly, “Look here, don’t I remind you of yourself in younger days?” Behind Lamont’s urbane charm stood a man of exceptional talent, the more winning for its being presented with such apparent modesty.
有趣的是，面对戴维森和皮尔庞特这两位，拉蒙特都婉拒了递到面前的王冠。他对皮尔庞特说自己梦想着每年旅行三个月。皮尔庞特非但没有退缩，反而说道：“这有什么，当然可以，你想休息多久就休息多久，完全由你决定。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245474) 他还建议拉蒙特带着孩子的保姆去尼罗河上游览一番。拉蒙特在处理这份邀约时，同样使了些巧思。他应该知道皮尔庞特每年都要在国外待上好几个月。他是否在向这位老巨头照镜子，无声地说：“瞧，我是不是让你想起了年轻时的自己？”在拉蒙特温文尔雅的魅力背后，藏着一位才华横溢的人，而这份才能以其谦逊的姿态展现出来，更显动人。

To complete preparations for the succession, Pierpont made his final disposition of J. S. Morgan and Company in London. Stipulating that it survive for only a generation, or as long as Pierpont lived, Junius had permitted his name to be used posthumously. Now the twenty years was about to elapse. Jack explained that “as we approached 1910, Father said, ’You will have trouble enough when I die without having to think of a new name for this firm, and I suggest that we should now change it to Morgan Grenfell & Co., and make J. P. Morgan & Co. partners in it, they to keep one million pounds in capital.’ ”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245608)
为顺利完成继承事宜，皮尔庞特对伦敦的 J. S. Morgan and Company 做了最终安排。朱尼厄斯曾允许身后仍使用其名，但规定该公司仅存续一代，或至皮尔庞特有生之年。如今二十年期限将至。杰克解释道：“临近 1910 年时，父亲说：‘我去世后你面临的麻烦已足够多，不必再为这家公司想新名称。我建议现在就把公司改为 Morgan Grenfell & Co.，让 J. P. Morgan & Co.成为合伙人，他们需保留一百万英镑资本。’” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245608)

On January 1, 1910, Morgan Grenfell was born. If it bore, for the first time, a British name, its prestige was guaranteed by its New York money and connections. While Teddy Grenfell’s name lent a protective British coloring in the City, the capital remained largely American. Before 1910, Pierpont and Jack had been partners of J. S. Morgan and Company. Under the new dispensation, J. P. Morgan and Company itself would be a partner in London and draw half its profits along with Drexel and Company in Philadelphia. Significantly, this arrangement never worked in reverse. Partners at Morgan Grenfell in London or Morgan, Harjes in Paris would thus hold second-class citizenship within the Morgan universe. The Morgan dynasty was always carefully arranged so that 23 Wall Street remained *primum inter pares.*
1910 年 1 月 1 日，摩根·格伦费尔（Morgan Grenfell）诞生了。虽然它首次冠以英国之名，但真正为其声望背书的，仍是来自纽约的资金与人脉。特迪·格伦费尔（Teddy Grenfell）的名字在伦敦金融城为其披上了一层保护性的英式外衣，然而其资本主体依旧来自美国。1910 年之前，皮尔庞特（Pierpont）和杰克（Jack）一直是 J. S. Morgan and Company 的合伙人。在新的格局下，J. P. Morgan and Company 本身将成为伦敦的合伙人，并与费城的 Drexel and Company 平分一半利润。值得注意的是，这种安排从未反向运作过。因此，伦敦 Morgan Grenfell 或巴黎 Morgan, Harjes 的合伙人在摩根体系中只能算是“二等公民”。摩根家族始终精心布局，确保华尔街 23 号（23 Wall Street）稳居“同侪之首”的地位。

DURING Pierpont’s last year, he was beset by calamities, as if the gods were punishing him on a scale worthy of his grandeur. His shipping trust, the International Mercantile Marine, faced stiff competition from the Cunard Line, which had built the swift and luxurious *Mauretania* and *Lusitania* with British government subsidies. To counter Cunard, J. Bruce Ismay, president of the IMM, and Lord Pirrie, the shipbuilder, decided to build a pair of mammoth ships. Pierpont, always partial to grandiose ventures, approved the plan. The ships were White Star’s *Titanic* and *Olympic.* The House of Morgan even lobbied the New York Harbor Board for a hundred-foot extension of a Hudson River pier so it could receive the twin ships.
在皮尔庞特的最后一年，他接连遭遇灾祸，仿佛众神在以与他显赫地位相称的规模惩罚他。他旗下的国际商船航运信托公司（International Mercantile Marine）面临着来自丘纳德航运公司（Cunard Line）的激烈竞争——后者凭借英国政府补贴，建造了迅捷而豪华的毛里塔尼亚号（Mauretania）和卢西塔尼亚号（Lusitania）。为对抗丘纳德，国际商船航运公司总裁 J. 布鲁斯·伊斯梅（J. Bruce Ismay）与造船商皮里勋爵（Lord Pirrie）决定建造两艘巨型轮船。向来偏爱宏伟事业的皮尔庞特批准了这一计划。这两艘船便是白星航运公司（White Star）的泰坦尼克号（Titanic）和奥林匹克号（Olympic）。摩根财团甚至游说纽约港务局，将哈德逊河的一个码头延长一百英尺，以便容纳这对姊妹船。

In May 1911, Pierpont attended the Belfast christening of the *Titanic* and studied the spot on B deck where his personal suite would be. It would contain a parlor and promenade deck, with timbered walls in Tudor style, and there would be special cigar holders in the bathroom. Though Pierpont and Vivian Smith of Morgan Grenfell both booked spots for the April 1912 maiden voyage, both had to cancel.
1911 年 5 月，皮尔庞特出席了泰坦尼克号在贝尔法斯特的下水仪式，并实地查看了 B 甲板上属于他的私人套房位置。套房内将设一间会客厅和一条散步甲板，墙壁采用都铎风格木饰，浴室里还专门配有雪茄架。尽管皮尔庞特和摩根建富银行的薇薇安·史密斯都预订了 1912 年 4 月首航的船票，但最终两人都不得不取消行程。

Reports of a North Atlantic disaster reached Pierpont in France on the eve of his seventy-fifth birthday. “Have just heard fearful rumor about Titanic with iceberg,” he wired New York. “Without any particulars. Hope for God sake not true.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245777) As the news spread, European reporters tried to track Pierpont down. When he was finally located in a French chateau, he seemed devastated. “Think of the lives that have been mowed down and of the terrible deaths,” he said.[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245957)
皮尔庞特在法国获悉北大西洋海难的消息时，正值他七十五岁生日前夕。“刚刚听到关于泰坦尼克号撞上冰山的可怕传闻，”他发电报给纽约，“尚无任何细节。看在上帝的份上，但愿不是真的。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245777) 随着消息传开，欧洲记者试图找到皮尔庞特。当人们最终在法国一座城堡里找到他时，他显得万分悲痛。“想想那些逝去的生命，想想那惨烈的死亡，”他说道。 [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2245957)

Over fifteen hundred people perished, including John Jacob Astor IV, George Widener, the son of P. A. B. Widener, and Benjamin Guggenheim. Survivors were picked up by the Cunard Line’s *Carpathia.* It was a crowning disaster for the shipping trust, unleashing denunciations against both White Star and Morgan himself. The British-run but American-owned ship was charged with many deficiencies—an insufficient number of lifeboats, a crew who ignored warnings of icebergs, a poorly organized rescue, even failure to put binoculars in the crow’s nest. Newspapers depicted luxurious staterooms laid out for Pierpont and others as proof of a misplaced emphasis on winning the carriage trade from Cunard rather than on safety.
超过一千五百人遇难，其中包括约翰·雅各布·阿斯特四世、乔治·怀德纳（P.A.B.怀德纳之子）以及本杰明·古根海姆。幸存者由库纳德航运公司的卡帕西亚号救起。这场灾难成为航运托拉斯的致命打击，引发了对白星航运公司和摩根本人的猛烈抨击。这艘由英国人运营、美国人拥有的船只被指存在诸多缺陷——救生艇数量不足、船员忽视冰山预警、救援组织混乱，甚至瞭望台连望远镜都没配备。报纸揭露了为皮尔庞特等人准备的豪华舱房，以此证明公司将重心放在与库纳德争夺高端客源而非保障安全上。

Though the Morgan partners had long regarded White Star chairman Bruce Ismay as abrupt and ill-mannered—he had often threatened to quit—they stuck by him at first. Jack deplored the public drubbing that Ismay took, cabling the message that “from telegraphic accounts his treatment New York infernally brutal.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246109) Later, Jack and Pierpont insisted he resign his post. The *Titanic* was the last nail in the coffin of the shipping trust. Although the cartel enjoyed a brief revival as Morgan’s Export Department sent war supplies to the Allies during World War I, that wasn’t enough to keep it afloat. In October 1914, Jack Morgan decided it had to default on its bonds. Almost four years after the *Titanic* went down, White Star conceded responsibility in court, paying out \$2.5 million in damages.
尽管摩根合伙人早就认为白星航运董事长伊斯梅粗鲁无礼——他常以辞职相威胁——但他们起初仍力挺他。杰克对伊斯梅遭受的公开抨击深感痛惜，致电称“据电报报道，他在纽约受到的待遇简直残暴至极。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246109) 后来，杰克和皮尔庞特坚持让他辞职。泰坦尼克号成了压垮这家航运托拉斯的最后一根稻草。尽管该垄断集团曾短暂复苏——一战期间摩根出口部向协约国运送战争物资——但这并不足以让它维持运转。1914 年 10 月，杰克·摩根决定必须让旗下债券违约。在泰坦尼克号沉没近四年后，白星航运在法庭上承认责任，支付了 250 万美元的赔偿金。

IN 1912, the crusade against the trusts had already reached a thunderous crescendo as much of the presidential campaign revolved around Pierpont and his enterprises. Morgan represented everything that had bothered Americans for a generation—factories thrown up helter-skelter across the landscape, brutal mergers, a carnival atmosphere on Wall Street that produced boomlets and busts in crazy, unending succession. A newspaper cartoon from 1912 shows Pierpont jovially sitting atop a heap of gold coins and dollar bills, clutching industrial plants and office buildings in his fist; the legend reads: “I have not the slightest power.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246287) Indeed, the Morgans saw themselves not as financial pirates but as public benefactors. When Harry Morgan was born in 1900, Jack noted a resemblance to Pierpont and said he only hoped his son would help as many people in his lifetime as Pierpont had in his. This sense of virtue contrasted with the reality of their being the target of public calumny, leaving the Morgan family angry and bewildered.
1912 年，一场针对托拉斯的讨伐已如雷霆般席卷全美，总统大选的大部分议题都围绕着皮尔庞特和他的企业展开。摩根几乎代表了让美国人困扰了一代人的一切——杂乱无章地拔地而起的工厂、冷酷无情的兼并、华尔街如嘉年华般疯狂推出一轮又一轮短暂繁荣与崩溃的狂欢氛围。当年的一幅报纸漫画中，皮尔庞特乐呵呵地坐在一堆金币和美元钞票上，手里攥着工厂和办公楼，标题写着：“我一点权力都没有。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246287) 事实上，摩根家族从不视自己为金融海盗，而是公众的恩人。1900 年哈里·摩根出生时，杰克觉得他长得像皮尔庞特，并表示只希望儿子有生之年能像皮尔庞特一样帮助那么多人。 这种道德优越感与现实中的公众诽谤形成鲜明对比，令摩根家族既愤慨又困惑。

Progressive Democrats criticized the trusts as cruel and inefficient and destructive of the entrepreneurial spirit. Bellwether of the new mood was Woodrow Wilson, then governor of New Jersey. He accused Republican-supported tariffs of shielding the trusts from foreign competition. In January 1910, while still president of Princeton, he had lectured an audience of New York bankers, including Pierpont and George F. Baker, on their duties, saying banking was “founded on a moral basis and not on a financial basis” and chiding them for penalizing small businesses.[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246501) As Wilson spoke, Pierpont gloomily puffed on his cigar; afterward, injured, he told Wilson the remarks seemed directed at him. Wilson, saying he meant no offense, contended that he spoke merely of principles.
进步民主党人抨击托拉斯冷酷、低效，且扼杀了企业家精神。这种新情绪的代表人物是 Woodrow Wilson，时任新泽西州州长。他指责共和党支持的关税政策让托拉斯免受外国竞争。1910 年 1 月，尚在担任普林斯顿大学校长期间，他就曾对包括 Pierpont 和 George F. Baker 在内的纽约银行家们训话，称银行业“建立在道德基础之上，而非金融基础之上”，并批评他们打压小企业。 [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246501) Wilson 发言时，Pierpont 闷闷不乐地抽着雪茄；事后，他感到委屈，对 Wilson 说那些话似乎是在针对他。Wilson 表示无意冒犯，并声称自己只是就事论事。

That the Democrats attacked Morgan wasn’t surprising. Far more telling was how he became a divisive issue among Republicans and helped to split the party in 1912 over several issues. One involved a Morgan syndicate formed with the Guggenheims in 1906 to exploit the copper of the Kennecott Glacier in Alaska. This “Morganheim” group, as it was dubbed, had launched a veritable financial invasion of the state, buying up steamship lines, coal fields, and canneries and investing \$20 million in a railroad to carry copper ore to Prince William Sound on the coast. The press lampooned this “second purchase of Alaska,” and one cartoonist introduced a composite monster called Guggenmorgan.
民主党攻击摩根并不令人意外。但更具深意的是，他如何成为共和党内部的争议焦点，并在 1912 年因若干议题导致该党分裂。其中一项争议涉及 1906 年摩根与古根海姆家族组建的财团，旨在开发阿拉斯加肯尼科特冰川的铜矿。这个被称为“摩根海姆”的集团，对阿拉斯加发动了一场真正的金融入侵——收购轮船公司、煤矿和罐头厂，并斥资 2000 万美元修建铁路，将铜矿石运往沿海的威廉王子湾。媒体嘲讽这是“第二次购买阿拉斯加”，有位漫画家甚至创造了一个名为“古根摩根”的复合怪物形象。

Such wholesale development of Alaska became a test case of the government’s attitude toward wilderness areas. It pitted Gifford Pin-chot, director of the U.S. Forest Service and a Teddy Roosevelt holdover, against Secretary of the Interior Richard Ballinger, a Taft appointee. Pinchot wanted to preserve the Alaskan wilderness for posterity, while Ballinger thought only the Guggenheim-Morgan combination could finance development in such a remote, costly spot. After public feuding between Pinchot and Ballinger, Taft dismissed Pinchot. When Teddy Roosevelt, on an African safari, heard about this, it fed his sense of having been betrayed by Taft.
这种对阿拉斯加的大规模开发，成了检验政府对荒野地带态度的试金石。这场对峙发生在吉福德·平肖与内政部长理查德·巴林杰之间——前者是美国林业局局长、西奥多·罗斯福的旧部，后者则是塔夫脱任命的官员。平肖主张将阿拉斯加荒野留给子孙后代，而巴林杰认为只有古根海姆-摩根联合财团才能承担得起这片偏远、开发成本高昂之地的投资。平肖与巴林杰公开交恶后，塔夫脱将平肖解职。正在非洲狩猎的西奥多·罗斯福闻讯后，愈发觉得自己遭到了塔夫脱的背叛。

Toward the end of his second term, Roosevelt had decided not to file an antitrust suit against the Morgan farm-equipment trust, International Harvester. In 1911, Taft not only filed such a suit, but later released papers purportedly showing that George W. Perkins had blocked an antitrust suit against Harvester back in 1907 by lobbying the head of the U.S. Bureau of Corporations, who warned Roosevelt not to antagonize the Morgan interests without any proof of major wrongdoing.
在他的第二个任期即将结束时，罗斯福决定不对摩根旗下的农机托拉斯——国际收割机公司提起反垄断诉讼。而到了 1911 年，塔夫脱不仅提起了这样的诉讼，随后还公布了一些文件，声称乔治·W·珀金斯早在 1907 年就曾通过游说美国公司局局长，阻挠对国际收割机公司的反垄断诉讼。该局长当时告诫罗斯福，在没有重大不当行为的证据之前，不要与摩根利益集团为敌。

In October 1911, the Taft administration lodged a suit against U.S. Steel in a further rebuff to the Morgans. “Am horrified at character of bill which beyond everything I thought possible,” Harry Davison wrote to the London partners.[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246679) To the Paris partners, he denounced the “cheap political methods of Taft and his associates.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246859) What made this especially galling to both Morgan and Roosevelt was the stress on U.S. Steel’s acquisition of Tennessee Coal and Iron during the 1907 panic. This was the deal that Judge Gary and Henry Frick had gotten TR to approve during his breakfast. The former president was hypersensitive to allegations of having been hoodwinked. Defending his actions, Roosevelt said that the suit against U.S. Steel “has brought vividly before our people the need for reducing to order our chaotic Government policy as regards business.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247033) The combination of the Pinchot firing and the U.S. Steel and International Harvester suit helped convince Roosevelt to bolt from the Republicans in 1912 and run as presidential candidate of the Progressive, or Bull Moose, party.
1911 年 10 月，塔夫脱政府对美国钢铁公司提起诉讼，这进一步打了摩根家族的脸。哈里·戴维森在给伦敦合伙人的信中写道：“我对这份法案的恶劣程度感到震惊，这远远超出了我的想象。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246679) 他在给巴黎合伙人的信中则痛斥“塔夫脱及其同僚的卑劣政治手腕”。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2246859) 让摩根和罗斯福尤其恼火的是，诉讼矛头直指美国钢铁公司在 1907 年恐慌期间收购田纳西煤铁公司一事。正是这笔交易，当年加里法官和亨利·弗里克在早餐会上说服了罗斯福予以批准。这位前总统对被指责受蒙骗一事极为敏感。罗斯福为自己的行为辩护称，对美国钢铁公司的诉讼“让美国民众清楚地看到，我们亟需整顿混乱的政府商业政策”。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247033) 平肖被解雇一事，加上美国钢铁公司的案子…… 钢铁与国际收割机公司（International Harvester）的反垄断诉讼案帮助说服罗斯福在 1912 年脱离共和党，作为进步党（又称公麋党）的总统候选人参选。

The issue of Morgan influence still dogged Roosevelt because of the prominence in his campaign of ex-Morgan partner George W. Perkins. Perkins was furious about Taft’s trust-busting. He urged Roosevelt to run, covered many of his preconvention expenses, stage-managed the convention, and chaired the new party’s executive committee. It was said he traveled so often to Oyster Bay to see Roosevelt that his chauffeur “knew every pebble in the road, even in the dark.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247199) Among Roosevelt’s Progressive followers, there lurked residual fear that Pierpont had planted Perkins in the campaign. But Perkins had left the bank on bad terms, and this seems unlikely. The 1912 split between Taft and Roosevelt brought to power the man who had lectured Pierpont on his moral duty: Woodrow Wilson. Meanwhile, the U.S. Steel suit miscarried, and International Harvester had to divest only three small subsidiaries.
摩根的影响力问题仍然困扰着罗斯福，因为他的竞选活动中有一位显赫的人物——前摩根合伙人乔治·W·珀金斯。珀金斯对塔夫脱的反托拉斯行动怒火中烧。他力劝罗斯福参选，承担了他会前的大部分开支，一手操办了大会，并主持了新党的执行委员会。据说他频繁前往牡蛎湾与罗斯福会面，以至于他的司机“连路上的每一颗石子都了如指掌，即使在黑暗中也不例外。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247199) 在罗斯福的进步派追随者中，仍有人暗自担心皮尔庞特把珀金斯安插进了竞选团队。但珀金斯离开银行时关系并不融洽，这种猜测似乎不太可能。1912 年塔夫脱与罗斯福的分裂，让那个曾训诫皮尔庞特履行道德义务的人——伍德罗·威尔逊——登上了权力宝座。与此同时，美国钢铁公司的诉讼半途而废，国际收割机公司也仅需剥离三家小型子公司。

The intellectual and political leap most damaging to the House of Morgan was a spreading notion that a Wall Street trust had created the industrial trusts and governed their subsequent destiny. Minnesota congressman Charles A. Lindbergh, Sr., father of the future aviator, coined the title Money Trust, describing it as the most sinister trust of all. Senator George Norris later said of Lindbergh’s attack on the Money Trust that “the gentleman from Minnesota is entitled to more credit than any other member.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247361) The *Wall Street Journal* correctly noted that the Money Trust was just a code name for Morgan. Legions of young muckraking reporters fanned out across Wall Street and rooted out insidious banking connections. Aided by his young assistant, Walter Lippmann, Lincoln Steffens exposed a web of links among ostensibly competitive New York banks. His exposes in *Everybody’s* magazine termed Pierpont “the boss of the United States.”
对摩根家族最具破坏性的思想与政治冲击，是一种日益蔓延的观念——华尔街的信托公司缔造了工业托拉斯，并主宰了它们日后的命运。明尼苏达州众议员老查尔斯·A·林德伯格（即未来飞行家之父）创造了“金钱托拉斯”一词，称其为最险恶的托拉斯。参议员乔治·诺里斯后来评价林德伯格对金钱托拉斯的抨击时说：“这位来自明尼苏达的先生比任何其他议员都更值得称赞。”《华尔街日报》一针见血地指出，所谓金钱托拉斯不过是摩根家族的代名词。大批年轻的扒粪记者涌向华尔街，深挖暗藏的银行关系网。林肯·斯蒂芬斯在其年轻助手沃尔特·李普曼的协助下，揭露了那些表面相互竞争的纽约银行之间千丝万缕的联系。他在《大众杂志》上发表的揭黑报道直指皮尔庞特为“美国的老大”。

During the summer of 1912, swollen Wall Street power was a hot issue at the Democratic National Convention. In a hell-raising speech, William Jennings Bryan introduced a resolution stating opposition “to the nomination of any candidate for president who is the representative or under obligation to J. Pierpont Morgan, Thomas F. Ryan, August Belmont, or any other member of the privilege-hunting and favor-seeking class.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247510) Wilson was more circumspect. While refusing contributions from Morgan, Belmont, and Ryan, he made exceptions for such financial notables as Jacob Schiff and Bernard Baruch. In accepting the nomination, Wilson said, “A concentration of the control of credit . . . may at any time become infinitely dangerous to free enterprise.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247678) That summer, he was tutored in economics by lawyer Louis Brandeis, who had combated Morgan control of the New Haven Railroad for several years. Financial reform would form a major part of Wilson’s campaign.
1912 年盛夏，华尔街的膨胀权力成为民主党全国代表大会上的热议焦点。威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩在激烈的演说中提出一项决议，声明反对“提名任何与 J·P·摩根、托马斯·F·瑞安、奥古斯特·贝尔蒙特或其他钻营特权、追逐利益的阶层有牵连或受其约束的总统候选人”。 [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247510) 威尔逊则更为谨慎。他拒绝摩根、贝尔蒙特和瑞安的捐款，却对雅各布·希夫、伯纳德·巴鲁克等金融界名流网开一面。在接受提名时，威尔逊表示：“信贷控制权的集中……随时可能对自由企业造成无限威胁。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247678) 那年夏天，律师路易斯·布兰代斯为他讲授经济学——此人曾与摩根对新英格兰铁路的控制展开数年斗争。金融改革将成为威尔逊竞选纲领的重要部分。

Congressman Lindbergh introduced a resolution in the House calling for a congressional probe into the concentration of power on Wall Street. The resulting 1912 hearings of the House Banking and Currency Committee were commonly known by the name of subcommittee chairman Arsene Pujo, a Louisiana Democrat, and they got into high gear after Wilson’s victory in November 1912. Pierpont Morgan and his friends, colleagues, and partners were to be the star witnesses.
国会议员林德伯格在众议院提出一项决议，要求国会调查华尔街的权力集中问题。由此引发的 1912 年众议院银行与货币委员会听证会，因其小组委员会主席、路易斯安那州民主党人阿尔塞纳·普若之名而广为人知。在 1912 年 11 月威尔逊获胜后，该听证会全面提速。皮尔庞特·摩根及其朋友、同事和合伙人将成为重要证人。

The Pujo hearings are always portrayed as Pierpont’s martyrdom, the public confrontation that led to his death. Of equal relevance to our story is their haunting effect on Jack Morgan. He had coped with the fear of his overpowering father by resorting to awe-struck worship. As Pierpont returned the affection in later years, Jack’s gratitude contained an extra element of relief, and he deeply resented the blistering political attacks against his father. A new bitterness, a darker shading, crept into his letters: “As to attacks on the Senior,” he wrote Vivian Smith, “. . . owing to a laborious and prolonged press attack . . . in the public mind J.P.M. is no longer a benefactor, or a citizen who would be a credit to any country, but is an ogre lying in the background, and always ready to devour.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247847) “The politicians that run our two countries appear to have been seized with a madness,” he told Grenfell. “Our country is full of hatred and bitterness and talk.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248034)
普乔听证会总被描绘成皮尔庞特的殉难之旅，那场公开对峙最终导致了他的离世。而对我们这个故事同样重要的是，它对杰克·摩根造成的挥之不去的阴影。他通过将父亲视为令人敬畏的偶像来应对对其强大父亲的恐惧。随着皮尔庞特晚年回应这份感情，杰克的感激中还夹杂着如释重负，因此他对父亲遭受的猛烈政治攻击深感愤恨。一种新的苦涩、更阴郁的色调渗入了他的信件：“至于对老先生的攻击，”他写信给薇薇安·史密斯，“……由于一场苦心经营且旷日持久的新闻攻击……在公众心目中，J.P.M.已不再是一位恩人，或能为任何国家增光的公民，而是一个潜伏在暗处、随时准备吞噬一切的恶魔。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2247847) “管理我们两国的政客们似乎都疯了，”他告诉格伦费尔。“我们的国家充满了仇恨、苦涩和空谈。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248034)

At first, Jack regarded the Pujo investigation as a “nuisance.” He took heart from the opinion of Morgan lawyer, Francis Stetson, that as a private bank they could withhold their books and refuse testimony. Jack even fancied Pierpont might lay out some constructive measures for Pujo’s consideration. But in late April 1912, the committee chose as its counsel Samuel Untermyer, a rich, shrewd New York trial lawyer whose pedigree collies had once beaten Pierpont’s in competition. Untermyer had already railed against the Money Trust, and Jack was aghast: “Investigation will probably proceed now on as unpleasant lines as can be arranged,” he cabled his father.[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248207) The hearings would sharpen Jack’s hostility toward Jews, reporters, Democrats, reformers—all those troublemakers who stirred up the populace. Scarred by the experience, he would grow disenchanted with democracy and what he referred to as America’s “amateur Government.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248386)
起初，杰克把普乔调查视为一场“麻烦”。摩根律师弗朗西斯·斯特森的意见让他稍感安心——作为私营银行，他们可以拒绝交出账簿并拒绝作证。杰克甚至幻想皮尔庞特能拿出些建设性举措供普乔考量。但 1912 年 4 月下旬，委员会选定了塞缪尔·昂特迈耶为顾问律师——这位精明富有的纽约庭审律师，其参赛的纯种柯利犬曾在比赛中赢过皮尔庞特的。昂特迈耶此前已猛烈抨击过“货币托拉斯”，杰克惊愕不已，电告父亲：“调查现在很可能会朝着最不愉快的方向推进。”这场听证会加剧了杰克对犹太人、记者、民主党人和改革派——所有那些煽动民众的捣乱分子——的敌意。饱受创伤的他，对民主以及他所谓美国“业余政府”日益幻灭。

The hearings occurred in December 1912, just as Pierpont hoped to wash his hands of worldly cares. The money kept rolling in—he was making about \$5 million a year—and the bank under Jack and Davison almost ran itself. Pierpont was probably more *au courant* on Egyptian excavations than on Wall Street underwritings. At first, he brusquely said he would testify alone in Washington. But on this cusp of the Diplomatic Age, a new accountability was expected, and bankers had to tend their images more prudently. The new team at 23 Wall adopted an aggressive attitude toward public relations dramatically at odds with historical reticence.
听证会于 1912 年 12 月举行，正值皮尔庞特试图摆脱世俗烦扰之时。财富依然滚滚而来——他每年进账约五百万美元——而杰克和戴维森掌管的银行几乎能自行运转。彼时的皮尔庞特，恐怕对埃及考古发掘的热忱远胜于华尔街的承销业务。起初，他生硬地宣称要独自前往华盛顿作证。然而在外交时代的这个转折点上，公众开始期待新的问责机制，银行家们也不得不更审慎地经营自身形象。华尔街 23 号的新团队一改往日的沉默作风，对公共关系展现出前所未有的积极姿态。

Silence was Wall Street’s golden rule of conduct. Its leading exemplar was Pierpont’s pal George F. Baker of the First National Bank, whose mutton-chop whiskers and gold watch chain across his paunch made him a prototypical Victorian banker. His bank was as mysterious as 23 Wall itself. Known as the Sphinx of Wall Street, Baker was director of more than forty companies. He gave his first newspaper interview in 1863 and not another until 1923, when a young woman said she was promised a job if she gained access to the reclusive Baker. Breaking his silence, he said, “Businessmen of America should reduce their talk two-thirds. Everyone should reduce his talk. There is rarely ever a reason enough for anybody to talk.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248559) By then, Baker’s fortune was estimated at between \$100 and \$300 million. He would richly endow the Harvard Business School, in part through the intercession of Tom Lamont.
沉默是华尔街的金科玉律。其最佳典范便是皮尔庞特的密友、第一国民银行的乔治·F·贝克——他那络腮胡须和横跨肚腩的金表链，让其成为典型的维多利亚时代银行家。他的银行与华尔街 23 号一样神秘莫测。被称为"华尔街斯芬克斯"的贝克，曾在四十多家公司担任董事。他 1863 年接受了首次报纸采访，此后直到 1923 年才再次开口——当时一位年轻女子声称，若能见到这位深居简出的贝克，她就能获得一份工作。打破沉默的贝克说道："美国的商人们应该把话减少三分之二。每个人都该少说话。几乎没有什么值得任何人开口的理由。" [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248559) 到那时，贝克的财富估值已在一亿至三亿美元之间。他通过汤姆·拉蒙特的斡旋，向哈佛商学院慷慨捐资。

As a private merchant, Pierpont felt no obligation to inform the public and never hired a publicist. Now a new generation of Morgan partners took charge of a public relations offensive. Not only was Pierpont coached for the hearings by Davison and Lamont, but the bank hired its first publicist. It was the ideal moment for that quintessential banker of the new age, round-faced smiling Tom Lamont. He laid out a secret plan, approved by Pierpont, that would govern Morgan public relations for a generation. To improve the bank’s image, Morgan partners would meet with selected reporters, stay in touch with publishers, monitor newspapers, contribute articles, and privately protest critical articles to editors.
作为私人银行家，皮尔庞特并不觉得有必要向公众透露信息，也从未聘用过公关人员。如今，新一代摩根合伙人接手了公关攻势。不仅皮尔庞特在听证会前接受了戴维森和拉蒙特的指导，银行还首次聘请了公关人员。这正是那位新时代的典范银行家——圆脸微笑的汤姆·拉蒙特登场的绝佳时机。他制定了一项秘密计划，并得到了皮尔庞特的批准，该计划将主导摩根银行未来一代人的公关策略。为了改善银行形象，摩根合伙人将与选定的记者会面，与出版商保持联系，监控报纸动态，撰写文章，并向编辑私下抗议批评性报道。

Lamont’s publicity operation for the Pujo hearings went beyond the lone publicist usually mentioned. An associate of his named Brainerd bought the big Maclures Newspaper Syndicate, which sold material to newspapers across America; this would be their vehicle for countering Pujo. “Our idea is for Brainerd to continue this strictly sub rosa,” Lamont cabled Davison, who replied, “Much pleased learn of Brainerd’s purchase. Find Senior and others here much impressed with the importance of doing something promptly. We all agreed it is most important have publicity man put to work sub rosa at once on money trust investigation.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248731) This flowered into a full-blown scheme for entering publishing. Along with Wall Street friends, the Morgan partners planned to buy papers in major cities—Washington, Chicago, and New York—and purchase two newspaper groups that sold inserts to papers around the country. This part of the campaign apparently lapsed, as did negotiations to buy the *Washington Post.* But the moves reflected a new wish to shape opinion and emerge from the old Morgan cocoon of secrecy.
拉蒙特为普约听证会策划的公关行动，远不止通常提到的那位公关人员。他的一位名叫布雷纳德的同事收购了规模庞大的麦克卢尔报业集团——该集团向全美报纸供稿；这将成为他们反击普约的工具。“我们的想法是让布雷纳德继续严格秘密地操作，”拉蒙特在电文中告知戴维森，戴维森回复道：“得知布雷纳德收购成功，甚感欣慰。请转告 Senior 及诸位同仁，他们对此事必须立即采取行动的重要性印象深刻。我们一致认为，让公关人员立即秘密介入金钱信托调查，至关重要。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2248731) 此事逐渐演变为一个涉足出版业的完整计划。摩根合伙人联手华尔街友人，计划收购华盛顿、芝加哥、纽约等主要城市的报纸，并买下两家向全国报纸提供增刊的报业集团。不过，这部分计划似乎不了了之，收购《华盛顿邮报》的谈判也告吹了。 但这些举动反映出一种新的愿望，即塑造舆论并摆脱摩根家族那种旧有的保密茧壳。

Instead of going alone to Washington, as he first hoped, Pierpont headed a sixteen-person entourage. The morning of the hearings, he emerged from a big, high-topped limousine and marched up the steps of the Capitol in striped pants, a velvet-collared coat, and silk top hat, grasping a cane. An immense crowd ringed the block: Pierpont was the most famous banker on earth. He was flanked by his daughter Louisa, her hands stuffed deep in a fur muff and her mouth tight with prim disapproval, and Jack, who wore a derby hat, his black mustache flecked with gray. As Pierpont sat in the hearing room, he wore the tragic mask of an old clown, his head mostly hairless, his nose bulbous and grotesquely gnarled, his posture erect and stubbornly proud.
皮尔庞特并未像最初希望的那样独自前往华盛顿，而是率领了一支 16 人的随行队伍。听证会那天早上，他从一辆高大的豪华轿车中出来，穿着条纹裤、天鹅绒领外套，头戴丝绸高顶礼帽，手握拐杖，大步走上国会大厦的台阶。街区周围挤满了人群：皮尔庞特是世界上最著名的银行家。他两侧分别是他的女儿路易莎和杰克。路易莎双手深深插在毛皮手筒里，嘴巴紧闭，带着刻板的不满；杰克戴着圆顶礼帽，黑色胡须中夹杂着灰色。当皮尔庞特坐在听证室时，他脸上带着老小丑般的悲剧面具，头上几乎秃顶，鼻子球根状且怪异地扭曲，姿势挺直而倔强地骄傲着。

The Pujo hearings are celebrated for Pierpont’s triumphant retorts and spirited defense of his business honor; in a moment, we shall hear the well-worn phrases. But let us first note the awesome Morgan power that was revealed, lest the Money Trust theorists seem malcontents. Some 78 major corporations, including many of the country’s most powerful holding companies, banked at Morgans. Pierpont and his partners, in turn, held 72 directorships in 112 corporations, spanning the worlds of finance, railroads, transportation, and public utilities. In this era of relationship banking, board seats often meant a monopoly on a company’s business. During the previous decade, the House of Morgan had floated almost \$2 billion in securities—an astronomical figure for the time.
普约听证会因皮尔庞特犀利的反驳和对其商业声誉的激昂辩护而闻名；稍后我们将听到那些老生常谈的片段。但首先，让我们留意一下那次被揭露出的令人敬畏的摩根势力，以免“货币托拉斯”理论家显得像是牢骚满腹之辈。大约 78 家主要企业——包括许多美国最强大的控股公司——都在摩根银行开户。而皮尔庞特及其合伙人则在 112 家公司中担任 72 个董事席位，业务横跨金融、铁路、运输和公用事业领域。在那个关系型银行业务盛行的年代，董事席位往往意味着对某家公司业务的垄断。在过去十年间，摩根家族发行了近 20 亿美元的证券——这在当时堪称天文数字。

The Money Trust hysteria stemmed from a wave of bank mergers; Wall Street was snowballing into one big, Morgan-dominated institution. In December 1909, Pierpont had bought a majority stake in the Equitable Life Assurance Society from Thomas Fortune Ryan. This gave him strong influence over America’s three biggest insurance companies—Mutual Life, Equitable, and New York Life. Although he subsequently “mutualized” the Equitable and sold it to policyholders, the potential for abuse seemed terrifying.
"货币信托"恐慌源于一波银行合并潮，华尔街正逐渐聚合为一个由摩根主导的庞大机构。1909 年 12 月，皮尔庞特从托马斯·福琼·瑞安手中购入了 Equitable Life Assurance Society 的多数股权，从而对美国三家最大的保险公司——Mutual Life、Equitable 和 New York Life——拥有了强大影响力。尽管他随后将 Equitable"互助化"并出售给保单持有人，但其中潜在的滥用风险仍令人不寒而栗。

Pierpont also controlled several New York City trusts through that old trick from railroad days, the voting trust. His Bankers Trust had taken over three other banks. In 1909, he had gained control of Guaranty Trust, which through a series of mergers he converted into America’s largest trust; it had two Morgan partners on its voting trust. As a director of both Bankers Trust and Guaranty Trust, Harry Davison blithely claimed that Morgans had no more control over the two banks than over the Pujo Committee itself. But Morgan records reveal a distinctly proprietary tone toward the banks. When Davison vacationed, for instance, Lamont dashed off such memos as “Banking matters—everything running along smoothly and successfully at the Bankers. . . . At the Guaranty Trust things are in good shape.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249011) Besides these Morgan-controlled trust companies, the core Money Trust group included J. P. Morgan and Company, First National Bank, and National City Bank. Over the National Bank of Commerce, America’s second biggest, Pierpont had such influence that it was styled “J. Pierpont Morgan’s bank.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249196)
皮尔庞特还通过铁路时代的老把戏——投票信托，控制着纽约市的几家信托公司。他的银行家信托公司已经吞并了另外三家银行。1909 年，他掌控了担保信托公司，随后通过一系列并购将其打造成美国最大的信托公司；该公司的投票信托中设有两名摩根合伙人。作为银行家信托公司和担保信托公司的董事，哈里·戴维森轻描淡写地声称，摩根对这些银行的控制程度，甚至还不如对普乔委员会的控制。然而，摩根的记录显示，他们对这些银行带有明显的所有权语气。例如，在戴维森度假期间，拉蒙特匆匆写下了这样的备忘录：“银行业务——银行家信托那边一切顺利……担保信托公司的情况也井井有条。” [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249011) 除了这些摩根控制的信托公司，核心货币信托集团还包括 J.P.摩根公司、第一国家银行和国民城市银行。 在美国第二大银行——国民商业银行（National Bank of Commerce）之上，皮尔庞特的影响力之大，以至于该行被称为“J. 皮尔庞特·摩根的银行”。 [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249196)

Wall Street bankers incestuously swapped seats on each other’s boards. Some banks had so many overlapping directors it was hard to separate them. Five of nine Chase directors were also First National directors, giving George F. Baker control over Chase. The banks also shared large equity stakes in each other. Pierpont was the biggest outside shareholder in Baker’s First National Bank. After the 1907 panic, Pierpont also took a large block of National City stock and put Jack on its board. The public could be forgiven for suspecting that these “Morgan banks” avoided competition and exercised veto power over new entrants to the capital markets.
华尔街的银行家们互相在对方的董事会里兼任董事，如同近亲繁殖一般。有些银行的董事高度重叠，几乎难以分清彼此。大通银行的九位董事中有五位同时也是第一国民银行的董事，这使得乔治·F·贝克得以控制大通。银行之间还相互持有大量股权。皮尔庞特是贝克的第一国民银行最大的外部股东。1907 年恐慌之后，皮尔庞特还大量吃进国民城市银行的股票，并让杰克进入其董事会。公众完全有理由怀疑，这些“摩根银行”在刻意避免竞争，并对资本市场的新进入者行使否决权。

In part, the new financial giants resulted from the stupendous scale of industrial financing. Business gravitated to New York as companies became national in scope. For instance, in 1906 J. P. Morgan and Company captured American Telephone and Telegraph’s business from Boston’s Kidder, Peabody, which had marketed AT&T bonds in New England but couldn’t handle its new need for national financing. Banks had to grow with their customers, and the industrial trusts created a Money Trust as much as the other way around. Similarly, with large-scale foreign financing in China, Latin America, and elsewhere, Washington had forged Wall Street banks into an instrument of statecraft but was then dismayed when they cooperated at home.
新的金融巨头在一定程度上是工业融资规模庞大的产物。随着公司业务扩展至全国，商业活动也向纽约集中。例如，1906 年，J. P. Morgan 公司从波士顿的 Kidder, Peabody 手中夺走了 American Telephone and Telegraph 的业务——后者曾在新英格兰地区销售 AT&T 债券，却无法满足其全国性融资的新需求。银行必须跟随客户一同成长，工业托拉斯催生了金钱信托，而非反之。同样，在中国、拉丁美洲及其他地区的大规模海外融资中，华盛顿曾将华尔街银行打造成外交工具，但随后却对它们在国内的联手合作感到不安。

Why didn’t banks just merge instead of carrying out the charade of swapping shares and board members? Most were private partnerships or closely held banks and could have done so. The answer harked back to traditional American antipathy against concentrated financial power. The Morgan-First National-National City trio feared public retribution if it openly declared its allegiance. In 1911, the group thought of merging the Bank of Commerce and Chase National Bank, but the move was vetoed by National City president James Stillman. As Jack had cabled Pierpont, “His objection arises from his feeling that it is better at present not to call attention to the great power of trio, which might increase public sentiment against that power throughout United States. . .. None of the trio wishes further large investment in bank stocks for long period.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249351)
为什么银行不直接合并，反而要上演互换股份和董事会成员的把戏？大多数银行都是私人合伙或股权集中的机构，本可以这么做。答案源于美国传统上对金融权力集中的反感。摩根、第一国民银行和国民城市银行这"三巨头"担心，如果公开宣布结盟，会招致公众的报复。1911 年，这个集团曾考虑合并商业银行和蔡斯国民银行，但国民城市银行总裁詹姆斯·斯蒂尔曼投了反对票。正如杰克电告皮尔庞特的那样："他反对的原因在于，他认为目前最好别让外界注意到三巨头的强大势力，否则可能会加剧全美公众对这种权力的反感……三巨头都不希望长期继续大举增持银行股。" [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249351)

At the Pujo hearings, Pierpont faced a crafty adversary. Short, sharp-nosed, and mustachioed, Samuel Untermyer was no scruffy radical but an affluent lawyer who sported fresh orchids in his lapel. A close student of trusts—he had investigated Equitable Life Assurance and Standard Oil—he had a suave, insinuating style. Pierpont, by contrast, was rough and uncouth in public. At this moment of supreme crisis, he reverted to those precepts that Junius had pounded into his head—the Gentlemen Banker’s Code of the City. The famous exchange went as follows:
在普约听证会上，皮尔庞特遇到了一位狡猾的对手。塞缪尔·昂特迈耶身材矮小、鼻子尖尖、留着八字胡，他并非邋遢的激进分子，而是一位富有的律师，翻领上还别着新鲜的兰花。作为研究托拉斯问题的行家——他曾调查过美国公平人寿保险和标准石油——他说话风格圆滑又暗藏机锋。反观皮尔庞特，他在公开场合显得粗鲁无礼。在这一危急关头，他又回到了朱尼厄斯灌输给他的那些信条——即所谓的“绅士银行家城市守则”。那段著名的交锋如下：

> *Untermyer:* Is not commercial credit based primarily upon money or property?
> 昂特迈耶：商业信用难道不是主要基于金钱或财产吗？

> *Morgan:* No, sir, the first thing is character.
> 摩根：不，先生，首要的是人品。

> *Untermyer:* Before money or property?
> 昂特迈耶：在金钱或财产之前？

> *Morgan:* Before money or anything else. Money cannot buy it. . . . Because a man I do not trust could not get money from me on all the bonds in Christendom.[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249531)
> 摩根：在金钱和任何东西之前。金钱买不到它……因为一个我不信任的人，就算拿基督教世界所有的债券来，也休想从我这里拿到一分钱。 [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249531)

Spectators applauded, and businessmen across America stood rapt by this eloquence. The usually taciturn Pierpont had ennobled banking in an unexpected way. On Wall Street, banker Henry Seligman said, stock prices leapt 5 to 10 points on the strength of this testimony.[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249696) Pierpont phrased the point more colorfully: “I have known a man to come into my office, and I have given him a check for a million dollars when I knew that they had not a cent in the world.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249851)
观众们鼓掌喝彩，全美国的商人们都沉浸在这篇雄辩之词中。平日里寡言少语的 Pierpont 以一种出人意料的方式为银行业赋予了崇高感。在华尔街，银行家 Henry Seligman 表示，这份证词令股票价格飙升了 5 到 10 个点。 [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249696) Pierpont 用更生动的语言点明了这一点：“我见过有人走进我的办公室，我给他开了一张一百万美元的支票，而我知道他当时身无分文。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2249851)

However much financiers might cheer such sentiments, to outsiders the statements sounded like cant preached to dupes. Yet, as we have seen, early merchant bankers used character and class as crude forms of credit screening; ever since the Medicis and Fuggers, it was a practical way for private bankers to protect their precious capital base. Pierpont’s statement was neither as cynical as critics thought nor as noble as friends imagined. It was a workable business strategy.
无论金融家们如何为这些言论欢呼，在外人听来，这些话就像是向傻瓜布道的伪善之词。然而，正如我们所见，早期的商人银行家们将人品和阶层当作粗放的信用筛选手段；自 Medicis 和 Fuggers 以来，这一直是私人银行家保护其宝贵资本基础的实用做法。Pierpont 的言论既不像批评者认为的那样愤世嫉俗，也不像朋友们想象的那样高尚。这是一种可行的商业策略。

In the history books, Pierpont’s epigrammatic sayings stand out. In the transcript of the Pujo hearings, however, they appear against an arid backdrop of denials and monosyllabic grunts, as if he wouldn’t concede the hearing’s legitimacy. Stamping his cane, Pierpont grew bullheaded and snorted like some angry god held hostage by heathens. Grudging in his explanations, he was led by Untermyer into some absurd statements. For instance, Untermyer got Pierpont to state his rationale for the one-man control of the railroads he sponsored:
在史册中，皮尔庞特那些警句式的妙语格外醒目。然而，在普约听证会的记录里，这些话却是在一连串否认和含糊其辞的哼声衬托下说出的，仿佛他根本不承认这场听证会的合法性。他用手杖戳着地面，变得像头犟牛，气喘吁吁，活像一位被异教徒挟持的愤怒神祇。他极不情愿地解释，被昂特迈耶牵着鼻子走，说出了一些荒谬的话。例如，昂特迈耶让皮尔庞特道出他倡导铁路由一人控制的理由：

This was Pierpont in a nutshell: he represented the bondholders and expressed their wrath against irresponsible management. But Untermyer saw more than passive surveillance at stake in the directorships and voting trusts. Besides representing bondholders, the House of Morgan represented itself to ensure a steady flow of business. It could intervene to protect its own interests. 这恰恰就是皮尔庞特的写照：他代表债券持有人，对不负责任的管理层表达愤怒。但在昂特迈耶看来，董事席位和投票信托所牵涉的远不止是被动监控。摩根财团除了代表债券持有人，也代表自身以确保业务的稳定流转。它随时可以出手维护自身利益。正因为皮尔庞特不肯承认这一点，他才满嘴胡话：

One senses that Untermyer, far from being displeased, gladly used such intransigence to showcase Pierpont’s arrogance.
可以感觉到，昂特迈耶非但没有不悦，反而乐于用这种毫不让步的语气来凸显皮尔庞特的傲慢。

> *Untermyer:* Your firm is run by you, is it not?

Despite a mass of circumstantial evidence, the Pujo committee never proved a Money Trust in a strict conspiratorial sense. Rather, it found a “community of interest” that concentrated “the control of credit and money in the hands of a few men, of which J.P. Morgan &. Co. are the recognized leaders.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250458) It said that six houses—J.P. Morgan and Company, First National, National City, and Kuhn, Loeb along with Boston’s Lee, Higginson and Kidder, Peabody—acted in concert in sponsoring securities of prime corporations and governments. It was hard for large companies to market bonds without this group or for rivals to take business away from them.
尽管有大量间接证据，普约委员会从未严格从共谋角度证明存在一个"货币信托"。相反，它发现了一个"利益共同体"，将"信贷和货币的控制权集中在少数人手中，而 J.P. Morgan & Co. 被公认为其中的领袖"。 [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250458) 该委员会指出，六家机构——J.P. Morgan and Company、First National、National City、Kuhn, Loeb，以及波士顿的 Lee, Higginson 和 Kidder, Peabody——协同行动，共同承销优质企业及政府证券。大型企业若想发行债券，几乎绕不开这个集团，竞争对手也很难从他们手中抢走业务。

The Pujo Committee documented the gentlemanly rules of conduct among old-line Wall Street banks. They competed, but in a manner as formal and ritualized as a minuet. They wouldn’t bid against each other for bond issues. Rather, a single house would privately negotiate a deal and then assign syndicate allotments to other firms. Over time, these allotments tended to be unvarying for a particular company. As Jacob Schiff told Pujo, “It was not good form to create unreasonable interference of competition. Good practices did not justify competition for security issues.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250628) Whether this was a barefaced plot to bar outsiders or just a natural response to market conditions would be debated for the next forty years. The issue would not be settled until the Medina trial of the early 1950s, when the House of Morgan would again be branded the kingpin of the conspiracy.
普约委员会记录了华尔街老牌银行之间绅士般的竞争准则。它们彼此竞争，但方式却如小步舞曲般正式而充满仪式感。它们不会在债券发行上互相竞价。相反，由一家银行私下谈判达成交易，再将银团份额分配给其他公司。久而久之，特定公司的份额分配往往一成不变。正如雅各布·希夫对普约所言：“制造不合理的竞争干扰并非得体之举。良好的做法并不赞成对证券发行进行竞争。” [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250628) 这究竟是赤裸裸地排挤外来者的阴谋，还是对市场环境的自然反应，此后四十年间一直争论不休。直到 20 世纪 50 年代初的梅迪纳审判中，摩根家族再次被定性为密谋的核心，这个问题才终于尘埃落定。

The Pujo hearings had one immediate consequence that seemed to threaten Morgan power. In December 1913, President Wilson signed the Federal Reserve Act, providing the government with a central bank and freeing it of reliance on the House of Morgan in emergencies; the new Federal Reserve System was a hybrid institution, with private regional reserve banks and a public Federal Reserve Board in Washington. Yet the House of Morgan moved so artfully to form an alliance with the Federal Reserve Bank of New York that for the next twenty years it would actually gain power from the new financial system. The bankers had not yet been tamed.
普乔听证会直接引发了一个看似威胁摩根财团权力的后果。1913 年 12 月，威尔逊总统签署《联邦储备法》，为政府建立了中央银行，使其在紧急情况下不再依赖摩根财团；新成立的联邦储备系统是一个混合机构，包含私有的地区储备银行和位于华盛顿的公共联邦储备委员会。然而，摩根财团巧妙运作，与纽约联邦储备银行结成了联盟，以至于在接下来的二十年里，它反而从这一新金融体系中获得了更大的权力。银行家们尚未被驯服。

AFTER the Pujo hearings, Jack and his sister Louisa sat with their father in a private railroad car as he recovered from the strain of his testimony. As soon as servants had brought their luggage from the hotel, they all returned to New York. Jack lauded his father’s testimony—thought him “perfectly frank, very helpful to situation”—but developed a visceral loathing for Untermyer, whom he tagged the Beast.[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250802) He thought the Pujo hearings a blatant assault on the Morgan bank, with other bankers only drawn in as a smokescreen. From Yankee pride, both father and son professed to be immune to the whin-ings of such little men. Striking a brave tone, Jack said, “We have all here maintained the note which \[Pierpont\] struck so well in Washington that he was much too big to be annoyed by miserable little things like that.”[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250983) The reality, however, was that Senior never recuperated from the ordeal of this public inquisition.
普约听证会结束后，杰克和妹妹路易莎陪着父亲坐在私人车厢里，让他从作证的压力中缓过劲来。仆人们刚把行李从酒店搬来，他们便启程返回纽约。杰克对父亲的证词赞不绝口，认为他“极为坦诚，对局势大有裨益”，但对昂特迈耶却生出由衷的厌恶，私下称其为“野兽”。 [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250802) 在他看来，普约听证会是对摩根银行的公然攻击，其他银行家不过是被拉来当烟幕弹。出于扬基人的骄傲，父子俩都声称对这类小角色的聒噪无动于衷。杰克强作镇定地说：“我们在场的所有人都保持了皮尔庞特在华盛顿定下的调子——他境界太高，根本不屑为这种鸡毛蒜皮的破事烦心。” [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2250983) 然而事实是，老摩根从未从这场公开审讯的煎熬中真正恢复过来。

Pierpont was too thin-skinned to be philosophical about political attacks and didn’t recognize himself as the ogre of the newspaper cartoons. He thought himself a generous, paternalistic boss and an avuncular grandfather, not a bloodthirsty monster. He was baffled by the new public scrutiny of businessmen and predicted that “the time is coming when all business will have to be done with glass pockets.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251146) He thought Jack might fare better in the new environment. In his last months, Pierpont possessed a melancholy sense of history as having passed him by. He told a visitor in 1913, “When you see Mr. Wilson, tell him for me that if there should ever come a time when he thinks any influence or resources that I have can be used for the country, they are wholly at his disposal.”[53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251339) Such a time never came.
皮尔庞特脸皮太薄，无法坦然面对政治攻击，也不认为自己就是报纸漫画里那个吃人的恶魔。他觉得自己是个慷慨仁厚的老板，像个慈祥的祖父，而不是嗜血的怪物。他对公众开始用放大镜审视商人感到困惑，并预言“总有一天，所有生意都不得不在透明口袋里进行。” [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251146) 他认为杰克或许能更好地适应这个新环境。在生命的最后几个月里，皮尔庞特带着一种被时代抛弃的悲凉感。1913 年，他对一位访客说：“你见到威尔逊先生时，请替我转告他，如果有一天他觉得我的影响力或资源能为国家所用，我随时听候调遣。” [53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251339) 但这一天从未到来。

Fleeing up the Nile with Louisa, Pierpont could find no respite from his troubles. As always, his ailments were a mass of amorphous symptoms rather than a definable illness. Louisa privately reported to Jack on his digestive upsets, depression, insomnia, and nervous attacks. “Bilious attack practically overcome but result months of strain very apparent now,” she cabled as they sailed to Luxor.[54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251507) Jack—always in the wrong place, always full of yearning—now wished to join Pierpont. But theirs was no ordinary father-son relationship. A political succession—no less momentous than a presidential transition—was underway, and Louisa reported that executive power was being placed in his hands. “Your suggestion coming yourself has touched and pleased him, but he is anxious you should remember how much depends upon your being on the spot in New York—how many interests are in your hands. He is too weak make decision; he wishes leave it you.”[55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251689) It was the first time Pierpont had ever explicitly delegated top authority to his son.
和路易莎一起沿尼罗河逃亡时，皮尔庞特始终无法摆脱烦恼。他的老毛病依旧是一堆模糊的症状，而非明确的疾病。路易莎私下向杰克汇报他的消化不良、抑郁、失眠和神经紧张。当他们乘船前往卢克索时，她发电报说：“胆病发作基本控制，但数月紧张的后遗症如今非常明显。” [54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251507) 杰克——总不在对的地方，总满怀渴望——此刻想要加入皮尔庞特。但他们之间并非寻常父子关系。一场不亚于总统交接的政治传承正在进行，路易莎报告说执行权力正在交到他手中。“你提出亲自前来让他既感动又欣慰，但他担心你该记得纽约有多少事仰仗你坐镇——多少利益系于你手。他太虚弱无法做决定，想由你来定夺。”” [55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251689) 这是 Pierpont 第一次明确地将最高权力委托给儿子。

As Pierpont weakened, fresh doctors were shipped out from New York. The corpulent banker fancied that fresh butter and cream from Cragston might restore him and asked Jack to send some. The final siege came in a \$500-a-day suite of Rome’s Grand Hotel. News of Pier-pont’s terminal illness rattled the art world, which braced for a general collapse of prices. The ground floor of the Grand Hotel teemed with art dealers, antiquarians, foppish noblemen, shabby peddlers—all trying to unload a last painting or statue on the dying financier. So zealous were their assaults that the *New York Times* described them as being “repulsed with the regularity of surf on the beach.”[56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2251864) Meanwhile, Pierpont’s condition required that politics and business not be mentioned. He was groggy but sleepless. Even grains of morphine couldn’t soothe his tormented mind or slow his racing pulse. On the night of March 31, he grew delirious and mumbled about his boyhood. Imagining himself back at school in Hartford or Switzerland, he praised “a fine lot of boys” in his class. Before he died, he said, “I’ve got to go up the hill.”[57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252017) He died shortly after midnight. Within twelve hours, the pope and 3,697 other people had telegraphed their regrets to the Grand Hotel.
随着皮尔庞特日渐衰弱，一批又一批的新医生从纽约派来。这位肥胖的银行家幻想着克雷格斯顿的新鲜黄油和奶油能让他恢复元气，便让杰克送些过来。最后的围攻发生在罗马大酒店那间每天五百美元的套房里。皮尔庞特病危的消息震动了艺术界，大家纷纷预料价格将全面崩盘。大酒店一楼挤满了艺术品经销商、古董商、浮夸的贵族、寒酸的小贩——所有人都想在这位奄奄一息的金融家身上再捞一笔，兜售最后一幅画或一尊雕像。他们的攻势之猛，以至于《纽约时报》形容他们是“像海浪拍岸一样规律地被击退”。与此同时，皮尔庞特的病情要求不得提及政事和商业。他昏昏沉沉却无法入睡，连吗啡都安抚不了他备受折磨的心灵，也压不住他狂跳的脉搏。3 月 31 日夜里，他开始胡言乱语，喃喃念叨着自己的童年时光。 他想象自己回到哈特福德或瑞士的学校，称赞班上的“一群好孩子”。临终前，他说：“我得翻过那座山。” [57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252017) 他在午夜后不久去世。 十二小时内，教皇和 3,697 人向 Grand Hotel 发去唁电。

The Morgan partners attributed the death to Pujo. The charge may be overstated. Pierpont was seventy-five when he died. Almost twenty years before, worried doctors wouldn’t approve a life insurance policy in his name. He smoked dozens of cigars daily, stowed away huge breakfasts, drank heavily, and refused to exercise. If Jack lost weight, Pierpont would grow alarmed. When Jack began playing squash regularly, Pierpont said, “Rather he than I.”[58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252185) From boyhood, he had been chronically sick, often spending several days in bed each month. Hardly a period of his life was free of illness and depression. That he lasted until seventy-five, with his myriad ailments and resolutely bad habits, is close to miraculous, testimony to a powerful constitution. Then, in his last years, there were numerous disappointments—the *Titanic*, the U.S. Steel and International Harvester suits, Woodrow Wilson s attacks on the Money Trust, and so on—that may have created unbearable stress.
摩根合伙人将他的死归咎于普约。这种说法可能有些夸张。皮尔庞特去世时七十五岁。大约二十年前，忧心忡忡的医生都不愿意为他名下的寿险保单签字。他每天抽几十支雪茄，早餐吃得很多，酗酒，而且从不锻炼。如果杰克瘦了，皮尔庞特就会很担心。当杰克开始定期打壁球时，皮尔庞特说："他愿意打就让他打吧，反正我是不打。" [58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252185) 从童年起，他就长期患病，每个月常常有几天卧病在床。他一生中几乎没有哪段时间不受疾病和抑郁的困扰。以他众多的疾病和顽固的坏习惯，能活到七十五岁，近乎奇迹，这证明了他身体底子很好。然而，在晚年，他遭遇了许多打击——泰坦尼克号沉没、美国钢铁公司和国际收割机公司的诉讼、伍德罗·威尔逊对货币托拉斯的攻击等等——这些可能给他带来了难以承受的压力。

But at Morgans, everybody *knew* Untermyer was the murderous scoundrel. As Lamont told historian Henry Steele Commager, “Within three or four months, out of a seemingly clear sky, his health failed and after a two weeks’ illness, from no particular malady, he died.”[59](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252320) Certainly, the hearings hastened Pierpont’s death, but who can say they caused it? Nevertheless, the belief was widespread at the bank and only hardened partners’ feelings toward politicians and reformers. Jack began to follow Untermyer’s affairs with a morbid curiosity. When a senator attacked the lawyer in 1914, he fairly gloated: “I enjoyed reading every account of it . . . and the more I see him caught in the machinery of his evil deeds, the better pleased I am.”[60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252475)
但在摩根公司，人人都知道昂特迈耶是个心狠手辣的无赖。正如拉蒙特对历史学家亨利·斯蒂尔·康马杰所言：“三四个月后，仿佛从万里晴空中，他的身体突然垮了，病了两周，却没什么特别的病症，人就没了。” [59](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252320) 听证会无疑加速了皮尔庞特的死亡，但谁能断言这就是直接原因呢？尽管如此，银行上下普遍相信这一点，而这只会让合伙人对政客和改革派的敌意更加根深蒂固。杰克开始带着一种病态的好奇心关注昂特迈耶的一举一动。1914 年，当一位参议员抨击这位律师时，他简直幸灾乐祸：“关于此事的每一篇报道我都读得津津有味……越是看到他自食恶果，我越是心满意足。” [60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252475)

How much had Pierpont amassed? Apart from his art collection, his estate came to \$68.3 million, of which about \$30 million represented his share in the New York and Philadelphia banks. (Pierpont’s \$68.3 million estate would be equivalent to \$802 million in 1989 dollars.) The value of his art collection was estimated by the Duveens at \$50 million. It was testimony to Pierpont’s Olympian standing that the release of the figures occasioned some disbelief, even some pity. Andrew Carnegie was truly saddened by the revelation of poor Pierpont’s poverty. “And to think he was not a rich man,” he sighed.[61](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252660) Pierpont’s fortune didn’t approach those of the great industrialists—Carnegie, Rockefeller, Ford, or Harriman—and he didn’t quite edge out Jay Gould. One magazine writer even saw the paltry estate as proof that Pierpont hadn’t profited from inside information at his disposal.
皮尔庞特究竟积累了多少财富？除去他的艺术藏品，他的遗产总额达 6830 万美元，其中约 3000 万美元是他持有的纽约和费城银行的股份。（皮尔庞特的 6830 万美元遗产，按 1989 年币值计算，相当于 8.02 亿美元。）杜维恩兄弟对其艺术藏品的估价为 5000 万美元。这些数字公布后，引发了一些质疑甚至同情，足见皮尔庞特在当时超凡脱俗的地位。安德鲁·卡内基看到可怜的皮尔庞特竟如此贫穷，真心为之难过。“想想看，他居然不算富人，”他叹息道。 [61](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252660) 皮尔庞特的财富远不及那些工业巨头——卡内基、洛克菲勒、福特或哈里曼——也未能略胜杰伊·古尔德一筹。一位杂志撰稿人甚至认为，这份寒酸的遗产恰恰证明皮尔庞特并未利用手中的内幕信息谋取私利。

When Pierpont’s will was disclosed, it contained many surprises. Overflowing with religious fervor, it had a florid opening, in which he committed his soul into the hands of Jesus Christ. He distributed money with great liberality. Besides the Morgan bank capital, lack was bequeathed S3 million outright, the *Corsair*, the property at Princes Gate and Dover House, and that inestimable jewel, the Morgan collection. Daughters Louisa Satterlee and Juliet Hamilton received \$1 million apiece, with an extra million thrown in for their husbands. The long-suffering Fanny received Cragston, the Madison Avenue house, a \$100,-000 guaranteed annuity, and a \$l-million trust fund. She survived until 1924, faithfully attended by Jack. There was friction in the family regarding Anne Morgan’s award of \$3 million. Since she would have no children and planned to donate the money to philanthropic activities, some thought she should have received much less.
皮尔庞特的遗嘱一经公布，内含诸多出人意料之处。开篇充满宗教热忱，辞藻华丽，他将自己的灵魂交托于耶稣基督手中。他慷慨分配财产。除了摩根银行资本外，遗赠给杰克 300 万美元现金，还有“海盗号”游艇、王子门和多佛豪斯的房产，以及无价之宝——摩根收藏。女儿路易莎·萨特利和朱丽叶·汉密尔顿各得 100 万美元，她们的丈夫额外获得 100 万美元。长期受苦的范妮得到了克拉格斯顿庄园、麦迪逊大道的房子、10 万美元的终身年金，以及 100 万美元的信托基金。她由杰克忠心陪伴，一直活到 1924 年。家族中对于安妮·摩根获得 300 万美元一事存在分歧。由于她没有子女，且计划将钱捐给慈善事业，有人认为她本应获得少得多的份额。

For Morgan retainers, it was a red-letter day, fulfilling their most delirious dreams. Librarian Belle da Costa Greene got her first Morgan bequest of \$50,000—Jack would later match it—plus a guarantee of continued employment at the library. Dr. James Markoe, who pumped Pierpont with medication during the 1907 panic, received a \$25,000 annuity, which was to revert to his pretty wife, Annette, should she outlive him. (This bequest, along with legends claiming that doctors at the Lying-in Hospital married Pierpont’s former mistresses, kept alive rumors that Annette Markoe had been a mistress of Pierpont’s.) Even Pierpont’s sailing master, Captain W. B. Porter, received \$15,000. In the most astounding act of paternalism, every J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Grenfell employee received a free year’s salary. (When the bill came due, Jack paid out \$373,000.) There was close to \$10 million in charitable bequests, including \$1.35 million to Dr. Markoe’s New York Lying-in Hospital, \$1 million to Harvard, \$560,000 to Saint George’s Church, and \$500,000 for the Cathedral of Saint John the Divine in New York City.
对摩根的部下们来说，这是一个大喜日子，实现了他们最疯狂的梦想。图书管理员贝儿·达·科斯塔·格林获得了摩根的第一笔遗赠 5 万美元——杰克后来也给了同样数额——此外还保证她能在图书馆继续工作。1907 年恐慌期间为皮尔庞特注射药物的詹姆斯·马科医生获得了一笔 2.5 万美元的年金，如果他的漂亮妻子安妮特比他长寿，这笔年金将转给她。（这笔遗赠，加上一些传说称产科医院的医生娶了皮尔庞特的前情妇，使得安妮特·马科曾是皮尔庞特情妇的流言一直流传。）甚至连皮尔庞特的航海船长 W.B.波特船长也获得了 1.5 万美元。最令人震惊的家长式做法是，J.P.摩根公司及摩根建富的每位员工都获得了一年的免费薪水。（账单到期时，杰克支付了 37.3 万美元。）慈善遗赠接近 1000 万美元，其中 135 万美元给了博士。 Markoe 向纽约产科医院捐赠 100 万美元，向哈佛大学捐赠 56 万美元，向圣乔治教堂捐赠 56 万美元，并向纽约圣约翰座堂捐赠 50 万美元。

By no coincidence, Pierpont’s last rites resembled the Anglo-American tribute he had arranged for Junius. He turned his own funeral into a last act of father worship. As Jack said, Pierpont had “left full instructions in regard to funeral which is to be as like his Father’s as possible.”[62](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252815) Again, the mourning was transatlantic, with Pierpont honored by both a memorial service at Westminster Abbey and the closing of the New York Stock Exchange. At sea, flags of the shipping trust flew at half-mast. Back in New York, his body lay in state at the Morgan Library. For the funeral service at Saint George’s, a full complement of Episcopal bishops—one each from New York, Connecticut, and Massachusetts—came in response to a summons in Pierpont’s will. Harry T. Burleigh, a black baritone, the grandson of an escaped slave, and a favorite of Pierpont’s, sang the hymns. Pierpont was buried in the family mausoleum at Hartford’s Cedar Hill Cemetery, according to his wishes: “opposite the place where my father’s remains are interred.”[63](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252991)
皮尔庞特的葬礼与当年他为朱尼厄斯操办的英美联合悼念仪式如出一辙，这绝非巧合。他将自己的葬礼化作了一场最后的“父亲崇拜”仪式。正如杰克所言，皮尔庞特“留下了详尽的葬礼安排指示，要求尽可能与其父亲的葬礼保持一致。” [62](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2252815) 这场跨大西洋的哀悼再次上演——威斯敏斯特教堂为他举行追思会，纽约证券交易所则暂停交易。航运托拉斯的船只在大洋上降半旗致哀。在纽约，他的遗体安放在摩根图书馆供人瞻仰。圣乔治教堂的葬礼上，纽约、康涅狄格和马萨诸塞三州的主教应皮尔庞特遗嘱要求悉数到场。皮尔庞特生前最喜爱的黑人男中音歌唱家哈里·T·伯利——一位逃亡奴隶的孙子——为他唱响了赞美诗。 皮尔庞特按照遗愿安葬在哈特福德雪松山公墓的家族陵墓中，位置“正对着我父亲长眠之地”。《Immersive Translate》

Perhaps no other event of the year 1913 received as many lines of newspaper copy as Pierpont Morgan’s death. Momentarily the critical drumbeat—which had grown so loud and insistent with the Pujo hearings—was silenced. In lengthy obituaries, no analogy was too large to encompass the personage who had just died. The *Economist* called Pierpont “the Napoleon of Wall Street.”[64](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253147) The *Wall Street Journal* said, “Such men have no successors. . . . There were no successors to Napoleon, Bismarck, Cecil Rhodes or E. H. Harriman, and their authority was not perpetuated.”[65](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253303) These articles suggested that the last titan had died, and the world of banking would never again see a figure of such scope.
也许没有哪件 1913 年的事件，能像皮尔庞特·摩根的去世那样占据如此之多的报纸版面。顷刻间，那些因普若听证会而变得异常响亮而紧迫的批判声浪，都沉寂了下来。在连篇累牍的讣告中，无论多么宏大的比喻，都用来描述这位刚刚离世的显赫人物。《经济学人》称皮尔庞特是“华尔街的拿破仑”。 [64](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253147) 《华尔街日报》写道：“这样的人没有后继者……拿破仑、俾斯麦、塞西尔·罗兹或 E·H·哈里曼都没有后继者，他们的权威也未能延续。” [65](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2253303) 这些文章暗示着最后一位巨擘已经陨落，银行界再也看不到如此格局的人物了。

From our later perspective, Pierpont Morgan seems large because of certain characteristics of the Baronial Age. The companies Pierpont Morgan controlled were weak and primitive by today’s standards, without a vast, highly trained managerial corps. Many firms had just graduated from the regional to the national level and needed Wall Street bankers in order to obtain broader financing. Even the governments Pierpont lent money to were relatively unsophisticated and lacked the central banks, systems of taxation, and large treasuries of today. Despite the multinational reach of Pierpont’s empire, his great exploits—the 1895 rescue of the gold standard, the creation of U.S. Steel, the cornering of Northern Pacific, the negotiations in the 1907 panic—were exclusively American in character.
从后世的角度看，皮尔庞特·摩根的强大，很大程度上源于那个“大亨时代”的特定背景。他控制的企业，以今天的标准衡量，显得原始而脆弱，缺乏庞大且训练有素的管理梯队。许多公司刚刚从地方性企业跃升为全国性企业，需要华尔街银行家来获取更广泛的融资。就连皮尔庞特提供贷款的那些政府，也相对缺乏经验，没有如今的中央银行、税收体系和大规模国库。尽管皮尔庞特的帝国触角遍及全球，但他那些赫赫有名的壮举——1895 年拯救金本位制、创建美国钢铁公司、垄断北太平洋铁路、1907 年恐慌中的斡旋——本质上都带有浓厚的美国色彩。

After Pierpont Morgan’s death, the House of Morgan would become less autocratic, less identified with a single individual. Power would be diffused among several partners, although Jack Morgan would remain as figurehead. In the new Diplomatic Age, the bank’s influence would not diminish. Rather, it would break from its domestic shackles and become a global power, sharing financial leadership with central banks and governments and profiting in unexpected ways from the partnership. What nobody could have foreseen in 1913 was that Jack Morgan—shy, awkward, shambling Jack who had cowered in the corners of Pierpont’s life—would preside over an institution of perhaps even larger power than the one ruled by his willful, rambunctious father.
皮尔庞特·摩根去世后，摩根家族不再独断专行，也不再与某个个人紧密捆绑。权力分散到几位合伙人手中，尽管杰克·摩根仍作为名义领袖存在。在新的外交时代，这家银行的影响力非但没有减弱，反而挣脱了国内束缚，成为全球性力量，与各央行及政府共享金融领导权，并借此合作以意想不到的方式牟利。1913 年无人能预见的是，那个在皮尔庞特阴影下畏缩胆怯、笨拙邋遢的杰克·摩根，竟会执掌一个权势可能远超其任性躁动父亲所统治的机构。
