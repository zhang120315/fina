# Chapter Fourteen: Golden

BY the mid-1920s, the Morgan story had come full circle. Where George Peabody and Pierpont and Junius Morgan had attained power by tapping the flow of British capital to America, the relationship was now fully reversed. London’s merchant banks, hobbled by the postwar embargo on foreign loans, had to act on a smaller stage, their overseas lending largely limited to British dominions or colonies and reconstruction loans. Meanwhile, Wall Street thrived, and J. P. Morgan and Company far surpassed Morgan Grenfell in power. Managing the British portion of the international loans sponsored by 23 Wall, Morgan Grenfell was somewhat buffered from the general London decline.
到 20 世纪 20 年代中期，摩根的故事已轮回一周。当年乔治·皮博迪与皮尔庞特·摩根、朱尼尔斯·摩根借助英国资本流入美国而发迹，如今这一关系已彻底逆转。伦敦的商业银行受战后对外贷款禁令掣肘，只能在较小的舞台上施展拳脚，其海外放贷大多局限于英联邦领地或殖民地及重建贷款。与此同时，华尔街蓬勃崛起，J. P. Morgan & Company 在权势上已远超 Morgan Grenfell。由于负责管理由华尔街 23 号发起的国际贷款中的英国部分，Morgan Grenfell 得以在伦敦整体衰落的格局中稍获缓冲。

In early 1927, Morgan Grenfell left 22 Old Broad Street and took up residence at 23 Great Winchester Street. The new headquarters stood at the angle of a small, L-shaped street around the corner from Liverpool Street Station. As former home to the British India Steam Navigation Company, its exterior was adorned with exotic tropical motifs—cornucopias and vines. The firm had these stripped away, to create an unmarked townhouse with a tall City doorway that dwarfed visitors. It was a posh, leisurely place with butlers. A 1926 photograph of Morgan Grenfell’s cricket team shows what looks like a set of pipe-smoking aristocrats—yet some of these young men were clerks or messengers.
1927 年初，摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）搬离了老宽街 22 号，迁至大温彻斯特街 23 号。这栋新总部坐落在利物浦街车站附近一条 L 形小街的拐角处。大楼原为英国印度蒸汽航运公司所在地，外墙上装饰着充满异域风情的热带图案——丰饶角与藤蔓。摩根建富将这些装饰悉数清除，打造出一栋没有任何标识的联排别墅，只留下一扇高大的城市风格大门，让来访者显得格外渺小。这是个奢华闲适的地方，配有管家。一张 1926 年摩根建富板球队的照片中，一群看起来像是叼着烟斗的贵族——然而其中一些年轻人其实是职员或信差。

The resident partners at Morgan Grenfell were handpicked power brokers. Though their names aren’t conspicuous in history books, they mediated dealings between the British and American financial establishments. J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Grenfell always enjoyed intimate relations. They swapped young apprentices, visited regularly, and kept up a vast correspondence that provides a comprehensive portrait of Anglo-American finance between the wars. Within the Morgan empire, however, the British house was subservient. And though the two houses collaborated on many deals, they also did much separate business.
摩根建富的常驻合伙人都是精挑细选的权势掮客。尽管他们的名字在史书中并不显眼，但他们却在英美金融界之间牵线搭桥。J. P. Morgan and Company 与摩根建富始终保持着亲密关系。双方交换年轻学徒、定期互访，并维持着大量书信往来，这些信件生动描绘了两次世界大战期间英美金融的完整图景。然而，在摩根帝国中，这家英国机构始终处于从属地位。尽管两家机构合作完成了许多交易，但各自独立开展的业务也不在少数。

If Lamont set the tone in New York, the London grandee was Teddy Grenfell, later Lord Saint Just. Fastidious and dashing, with pocket handkerchief, trim mustache, and smooth, glossy hair, he had a smart air and a brittle wit. His keen eyes sloped down in a penetrating stare that spied out people’s thoughts; Grenfell had the clear vision of the coldly unsentimental. Outwardly, he was neat and formal and correct in his behavior. But his judgments, expressed in a torrent of letters to 23 Wall and to his close friend, Jack Morgan, were funny, unsparing, and fiercely opinionated. Nobody in the House of Morgan was more prescient about people and affairs than Teddy Grenfell. He especially delighted in exposing the folly of social reformers. In his sneering but incisive way, he exhibited much racial and religious bigotry and poured buckets of scorn on his targets. This probably appealed to Jack, who shared Grenfell’s prejudices but was more reticent about expressing them. By 1922, Grenfell was not only a Bank of England director, but a conservative member of Parliament for the City.
如果说拉蒙特为纽约定下了基调，那么伦敦的显贵便是泰迪·格伦费尔——后来的圣贾斯特勋爵。他讲究衣着、风度翩翩，口袋巾、修剪整齐的胡须、光滑油亮的头发，气度不凡，言辞犀利。那双敏锐的眼睛微微下斜，目光如炬，能看透人心；格伦费尔有着冷静而无情的清晰洞察力。外表上，他一丝不苟、举止端庄得体。然而，他在写给华尔街 23 号和挚友杰克·摩根的大量信件中所表达的看法，却风趣幽默、毫不留情，且观点极其鲜明。在摩根家族中，无人能像泰迪·格伦费尔那样对人事具有如此先见之明。他尤其热衷于揭露社会改革者的愚蠢。他以嘲讽却一针见血的方式，展现出大量的种族和宗教偏见，对其抨击对象泼尽冷水。这一点或许很合杰克的口味——摩根与格伦费尔抱有同样的偏见，但表达起来更加含蓄。 到 1922 年，格伦费尔不仅是英格兰银行的董事，还是代表伦敦金融城的保守党议员。

The slim, handsome Grenfell remained an eligible bachelor until the age of forty-three, when he married twenty-three-year-old Florence Henderson. Florence’s father was a director of the Bank of England and chairman of the Borneo Company, a Far East trading concern. At the wedding, legend has it, the church was crammed with women weeping at their loss. With the exception of Virginia Woolf, who found Florrie “dulled” and “coarsened” by wealth, everybody seemed enchanted by Grenfell’s bride.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290952) She was tall, beautiful, and distinguished-looking, with a deep, fascinating voice. Anne Morrow Lindbergh found her “fawnlike and fragile” and picked up a certain gamine quality about her, a feeling she was “incorrigibly young . . . and likes to be teased.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291102) She likened Florrie to Virginia Woolf’s Mrs. Dalloway—a sociable woman who knew everybody and loved to organize events.
这位清瘦英俊的格伦费尔一直单身到四十三岁才结婚，娶了二十三岁的弗洛伦斯·亨德森。弗洛伦斯的父亲是英格兰银行董事，也是远东贸易公司婆罗洲公司的主席。据说在婚礼上，教堂里挤满了为他"痛失良婿"而落泪的女人。除了弗吉尼亚·伍尔夫认为弗洛丽被财富"磨钝"了"粗俗了"，所有人都对格伦费尔的新娘着迷不已。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290952) 她身材高挑，容貌出众，气质不凡，声音低沉而迷人。安妮·莫罗·林德伯格觉得她"像小鹿一般脆弱"，还捕捉到她身上那种顽皮少女的气质——"无可救药的年轻……喜欢被人逗着玩"。 [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291102) 琳德伯格把弗洛丽比作弗吉尼亚·伍尔夫笔下的达洛维夫人——一个交际甚广、认识所有人、且热衷于组织聚会的社交能手。

It proved a difficult marriage. Florrie had an unconventional streak. She danced, took voice lessons, and became an early patron of Diaghilev’s Ballets Russes. The impresario referred to her as his *fidèle amie* and invited her to rehearsals. But she was more than just a society patroness plowing Morgan Grenfell money into dance. She was a woman of sophistication and flair who went to the theater every night and wrote penetrating blurbs on what she saw. All this was lost on the brilliant but limited Grenfell, who golfed and sailed and had no time for the artistic modernism that entranced his young wife. He would come home to their townhouse at 4 Cavendish Square and find partly clad Russian dancers and musicians strewn about the place. The famous ballerina Markova attended parties there, and Florrie also befriended Lydia Lopokova, later the wife of John Maynard Keynes. Snobbish and aloof, settled in his ways, Grenfell was jarred by the bohemian activity, despite his affection for Florrie. His wife, in turn, wasn’t ready to submit to the stuffy pleasures of the countinghouse crowd. The marriage lasted, but more through friendship than love.
这段婚姻终究难以维系。弗洛丽生来就带着几分不羁——她跳舞、学声乐，还成了佳吉列夫俄罗斯芭蕾舞团的早期赞助人。那位剧团经理称她为"挚友"（fidèle amie），常邀她观览排练。但她绝非只是把摩根建富的钱砸进舞蹈的社交名媛。她品位出众、见识不凡，每夜流连剧场，观剧后总能写下鞭辟入里的短评。可惜这一切，都被才华横溢却视野狭隘的格伦费尔辜负了。他只热衷打高尔夫和出海航行，对令年轻妻子着迷的艺术现代主义毫无兴致。每当他回到卡文迪什广场 4 号的联排别墅，总能看到半裸的俄罗斯舞者与乐师散落满屋。著名芭蕾女伶玛科娃也曾在此赴宴，弗洛丽还结识了后来成为约翰·梅纳德·凯恩斯之妻的莉迪娅·洛普科娃。 格伦费尔生性势利、孤傲，又固执己见，尽管他对弗洛里怀有感情，却还是被那种波西米亚式的生活方式搅得心烦意乱。而他的妻子也并不打算屈就于账房圈子里那种沉闷乏味的乐趣。这段婚姻维系了下来，但更多是靠友情，而非爱情。

The other reigning partner at Morgan Grenfell was the tall, redheaded Vivian Hugh Smith, later the first Lord Bicester. He had a broad, open face and handlebar mustache and a nature more equable than Grenfell’s. In early pictures, he sucks on a pipe and smiles like the cat who swallowed the canary, as if he knew many City secrets but wouldn’t tell. He seldom got depressed, loved to tell shocking stories in deadpan style, and had unshakable composure. He was a country squire, with a mad passion for horses and steeplechase contests—while still at Eton, he was disciplined for sneaking off to Ascot. He occupied strategic posts—governor of the Royal Exchange Assurance Company, chairman of the City’s Conservative party, and a director of Associated Electrical Industries. His family connections ramified everywhere, and among his five brothers were bankers, admirals, and businessmen. (His brother Lancelot, senior partner with stock brokers Rowe and Pitman, executed many Morgan Grenfell deals.) Family scuttlebutt claimed Smith was envious of his cousin Ted Grenfell, whom, he felt, stole too much of the glory.
摩根格伦费尔的另一位合伙人是高个子、红发的维维安·休·史密斯，后来成为第一代比斯特勋爵。他面容宽阔开朗，留着八字胡，性情比格伦费尔更加平和。在早期的照片中，他叼着烟斗，笑得像偷吃了金丝雀的猫，仿佛知晓城里无数秘密，却守口如瓶。他很少情绪低落，喜欢面无表情地讲些耸人听闻的故事，沉着镇定得令人叹服。他是个乡绅，对马匹和越野障碍赛马抱有狂热——还在伊顿公学时，就因偷偷溜去阿斯科特赛马会而受过处分。他身居要职——皇家交易保险公司总裁、伦敦金融城保守党主席，以及联合电器工业公司董事。他的家族关系网四通八达，五个兄弟中不乏银行家、海军上将和商人。（他的兄弟兰斯洛特是股票经纪商 Rowe & Pitman 的高级合伙人，经手了许多摩根格伦费尔的交易。））家族里的小道消息称，史密斯其实嫉妒他的表兄泰德·格伦费尔，觉得他抢了太多风头。

In aristocratic circles, Smith was known as the establishmentarian with the outrageous wife. Slim and very Irish, with pale gold hair, Lady Sybil Smith brought seven more City Smiths into the world. She combined great charm and conviviality with deep political commitment. Beyond her interest in theosophy, which she shared with Monty Norman, she was a dedicated suffragette. As treasurer of the Women’s Social and Political Union in 1913, she was its chief fund-raiser in Mayfair drawing rooms; with her delightful voice, she interspersed her pitch with musical interludes. The daughter of her great friend, Emmeline Pankhurst, remembered Lady Sybil singing “in her long straight gown, like a nymph from a Greek vase.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291281)
在上流社会圈子里，史密斯是出了名的“老派人物”——因为他那位惊世骇俗的妻子。西比尔·史密斯夫人身形纤瘦，带着浓厚的爱尔兰气质，一头淡金色秀发，为城里的史密斯家族又添了七个孩子。她将迷人的魅力与欢快的性情，同深厚的政治热忱融为一体。除了和蒙蒂·诺曼共同热衷的神智学，她还是一位坚定的妇女参政论者。1913 年，她担任妇女社会政治联盟的财务主管，在梅菲尔的客厅里负责主要筹款活动；凭借悦耳的嗓音，她会在募捐演说中穿插音乐片段。她的挚友埃米琳·潘克赫斯特的女儿回忆道，西比尔夫人“身穿长直筒礼服，宛如希腊花瓶上的仙女”。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291281)

Vivian Smith was very amused by Lady Sybil, and they teased each other mercilessly. Yet tolerance had its limits. One day, Vivian picked up the newspaper and read the headline “LADY SYBIL SMITH ADOPTS CHILD.” Vivian quickly unadopted the child, a waif Sybil had picked up. In July 1913, came the great test of his composure. Sybil joined a delegation of militant suffragettes who invaded the House of Commons to oppose the Male Suffrage Bill. After an unsatisfactory interview with Reginald McKenna, then the home secretary, some of the women began making speeches; scuffles and arrests ensued. Lady Sybil pushed a constable who had taken a suffragette into custody and was sentenced to fourteen days. She insisted upon going to prison in solidarity with her sisters. Once in prison she gaily launched a hunger strike, as if it were a lark. Cool as a cocktail-party hostess, she wore a “tea-gown and golden slippers,” as another suffragette recalled.[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291450) But the government didn’t like having an earl’s daughter in prison, and Lady Sybil’s sentence was commuted to only four days. Still, McKenna swore no favoritism was shown. During her prison stint, Grenfell cabled Jack that Vivian was trying to be a good sport, apparently with difficulty: “V.H.S. distressed, but as usual considerate and dignified.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291584)
Vivian Smith 觉得 Sybil 夫人特别逗趣，两人互相打趣起来毫不留情。不过，忍耐终归有限度。有一天，Vivian 拿起报纸，看到头版赫然写着“SYBIL SMITH 夫人收养孩子”。Vivian 立马把那个 Sybil 捡来的流浪儿给退了回去。到了 1913 年 7 月，考验他定力的大戏上演了。Sybil 加入了一支激进的女权运动代表团，冲进下议院抗议《男性选举权法案》。在与当时的内政大臣 Reginald McKenna 进行了一次不愉快的会面后，一些女人开始发表演讲，接着就是扭打和逮捕。Sybil 夫人推搡了一名正在拘留女权运动者的警察，被判 14 天监禁。她坚持要入狱，与姐妹们同甘共苦。一进监狱，她兴高采烈地发起绝食抗议，仿佛是在闹着玩似的。另一位女权运动者回忆说，她像鸡尾酒会的女主人一样从容，穿着“茶会礼服和金色拖鞋”。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291450) 但政府并不乐见一位伯爵的女儿入狱，因此西比尔夫人的刑期被减至仅为四天。尽管如此，麦肯纳坚称绝无偏袒。在她服刑期间，格伦费尔发电报给杰克，说薇薇安正努力表现得若无其事，显然这很艰难：“V.H.S. 感到痛苦，但一如既往地体贴且端庄。”” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291584)

The Smiths were as odd a couple as the Grenfells. Sylvia Pankhurst, Emmeline’s daughter, saw Vivian as a “very dull foil” to Sybil, who managed a day nursery for destitute mothers and their babies in London’s East End. Sybil exhibited an idealism never apparent in her husband. As Pankhurst wrote of her, “She possessed a rare absence of class consciousness, a sensitive perception of the good qualities in other people and the precious ability to perceive that, at bottom, we most of us have the same needs.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291763) Lady Sybil naturally became friends with her New York counterpart in the Morgan universe, the activist Dorothy Whitney Straight.
史密斯夫妇和格伦费尔夫妇一样奇特。埃米琳的女儿西尔维娅·潘克赫斯特认为，与西比尔相比，薇薇安是个“极其乏味的陪衬”。西比尔在伦敦东区经营一家日间托儿所，为贫困母亲和她们的婴儿提供服务。她身上展现出一种在她丈夫身上从未显现过的理想主义。正如潘克赫斯特在描述她时所说：“她拥有一种罕见的无阶级意识，对他人的优点有着敏锐的感知，并且具备一种宝贵的能力，能体会到从根本上说，我们大多数人都有着相同的需求。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291763) 西比尔夫人自然与她在摩根家族中的纽约同行——活动家多萝西·惠特尼·斯特雷特——成为了朋友。

MORGAN Grenfell emerged as an important conduit between the City and Wall Street at a time when U.S.-British rivalry threatened to overwhelm friendship. As American exports boomed under the tutelage of Herbert Hoover’s aggressive Commerce Department, British heavy industry languished. Britain felt threatened by America’s new industrial strength and marketing talent. There was a fad for American movies and cosmetics in Britain, an Anglo-American battle for raw materials, and a first wave of investment in Britain by American industry, symbolized by Ford’s new factory at Dagenham on the Thames and General Electric’s assault on the British electric industry.
在美英之间的竞争一度几近盖过友谊的背景下，摩根建富（MORGAN Grenfell）成为连接伦敦金融城与华尔街的重要桥梁。当美国出口在赫伯特·胡佛（Herbert Hoover）激进的商务部主导下蓬勃发展时，英国的重工业却陷入低迷。美国的新兴工业实力和营销才能令英国感到威胁。美国电影和化妆品在英国风靡一时，英美之间展开了一场原材料争夺战，而美国工业对英国的第一轮投资浪潮也随之而来——福特（Ford）在泰晤士河畔达格纳姆新建的工厂，以及通用电气（General Electric）对英国电力行业的猛攻，便是其中的标志。

Montagu Norman, who would enjoy an extraordinary reign as governor of the Bank of England from 1920 to 1944, wished to reestablish London financial preeminence in the 1920s and reverse the decline in British industry. To do this, he needed Wall Street money and connections. He found both in the House of Morgan, giving him power fantastically disproportionate to Britain’s postwar wealth. The Morgan partners in New York shared his vision of transatlantic cooperation and Anglo-American partnership, resisting the insular American mood of the 1920s.
蒙塔古·诺曼在 1920 年至 1944 年间以英格兰银行行长的身份执掌大权，他希望在 20 世纪 20 年代重振伦敦的金融霸主地位，扭转英国工业的颓势。为此，他需要华尔街的资金和关系网，而这两样都在摩根财团找到了。凭借摩根的支持，他拥有了与英国战后经济实力极不相称的巨大影响力。纽约的摩根合伙人认同他关于跨大西洋合作与英美伙伴关系的愿景，抵制了 20 年代美国孤立主义的氛围。

In 1919, Britain was forced off the gold standard. Restoring the link between sterling and gold became an indispensable first step in restoring the City’s status as a world financial center. London’s strength in foreign lending had always been premised on a stable pound. Like king and country, the gold standard was an abstraction that made British bankers feel snug and cosy. Norman thought it the best means by which to stop fluctuating exchange rates, and he wanted England to lead in restoring this monetary discipline.
1919 年，英国被迫脱离金本位制。恢复英镑与黄金的挂钩，成为重振伦敦金融城世界金融中心地位不可或缺的第一步。伦敦在外贷领域的实力，始终建立在英镑稳定的基础之上。如同国王与国家一样，金本位制是一种抽象概念，让英国银行家们感到安心自在。诺曼认为这是遏制汇率波动的最佳手段，他希望英国能够率先恢复这种货币纪律。

The American Morgan partners were instrumental in putting England back on gold. It was their holy cause. As early as September 1923, Russell Leffingwell told Jack in Scotland that after the grouse season he wanted to speak to him about “this dream of mine”—that England go back on gold. New to the firm, Leffingwell said he would “sell his shirt to help England out of this mess” and asked, “Could anything be more heartening than for England and America to lock arms for honest money?”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291921)
美国的摩根合伙人在推动英国回归金本位制上起到了关键作用，这是他们引以为傲的使命。早在 1923 年 9 月，Russell Leffingwell 就在苏格兰对 Jack 说，等猎鸟季节过后，他想和他谈谈"我的这个梦想"——让英国回归金本位。作为公司新人，Leffingwell 表示他愿意"倾其所有帮英国摆脱困境"，并问道："还有什么比英美联手推行稳健货币更令人振奋的呢？" [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2291921)

Like Monty Norman, the Morgan partners feared that if exchange rates weren’t tied to gold, they would be managed by politicians. Sober finance might then be held hostage to political expediency, giving a bias toward inflation and paper money. Keynes was already advancing such heretical ideas. Leffingwell warned Morgan: “Keynes . . . is flirting with strange gods and proposing to abandon the gold standard forever and to substitute a ’managed’ currency . . . it is better to have some standard than to turn our affairs over to the wisdom of the publicist-economists for management.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292107)
像蒙蒂·诺曼一样，摩根合伙人也担心，如果汇率不与黄金挂钩，就会被政客们操控。这样一来，稳健的财政可能沦为政治权宜之计的牺牲品，导致通胀和纸币泛滥的倾向。凯恩斯已经在鼓吹这种异端思想。莱芬韦尔警告摩根说：“凯恩斯……正在与邪神调情，提议永久放弃金本位，代之以'管理型'货币……与其把我们的命运交给那些公共经济学家去管理，不如坚持某种标准。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292107)

Teddy Grenfell was Monty Norman’s intermediary between the Bank of England and Wall Street. He briefed the New York partners about Norman’s strange temperament and fragile nerves: “Norman elaborates his own schemes by himself and does not take anyone into his counsel unless he is obliged to do so in order to combat opposition. . . . As I have explained to you before, our dear friend Monty works in his own peculiar way. He is masterful and very secretive.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292309) As a Bank of England director, Grenfell also alerted New York partners to impending changes in Bank of England interest rates—priceless information—just as Herman Harjes reported on upcoming gold movements by the Banque de France.
泰迪·格伦费尔是蒙蒂·诺曼在英国央行与华尔街之间的中间人。他向纽约的合伙人透露了诺曼古怪的脾气和脆弱的神经：“诺曼独自谋划自己的方案，除非迫不得已要对抗反对意见，否则他绝不会向任何人透露自己的想法……正如我之前跟你们解释过的，我们亲爱的朋友蒙蒂行事风格独特。他专横跋扈，又极度神秘。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292309) 作为英国央行的董事，格伦费尔还向纽约合伙人透露了英国央行即将调整利率的消息——这无疑是价值连城的情报——就像赫尔曼·哈杰斯汇报法国央行即将进行的黄金动向一样。

In late 1924, Norman got cold feet about gold. To bolster his confidence, he went to New York to see Jack and Ben Strong. For Jack, the return to gold was gospel. Hadn’t his father saved America’s gold standard in 1895? He fervently told Norman that if Britain failed to return to gold, centuries of goodwill and moral authority would have been squandered. And Treasury Secretary Andrew Mellon told Norman that Washington approved of J. P. Morgan and the New York Fed helping Britain to go back on gold.
1924 年底，诺曼对金本位打起了退堂鼓。为了重拾信心，他专程前往纽约拜访杰克和本·斯特朗。对杰克而言，回归金本位犹如铁律——他父亲不正是 1895 年拯救了美国金本位制度吗？他热切地告诉诺曼，如果英国未能重返金本位，数百年积累的善意与道德权威将付诸东流。而财政部长安德鲁·梅隆则向诺曼表示，华盛顿支持摩根大通和纽约联邦储备银行协助英国恢复金本位。

Morgan cooperation was vital. For the pound to maintain its new, higher value in currency markets, the dollar couldn’t pose too strong a competitive threat. Otherwise, speculators would sell pounds and buy dollars, driving the pound back down. Either Norman had to maintain high interest rates in London—drawing money into the pound—or Strong had to keep rates low in New York—making dollar investments less attractive. The House of Morgan insisted on higher British interest rates as the answer. Instead, Monty’s loyal friend, Ben Strong, depressed American interest rates. This was no small technical matter: it would be blamed by some for causing the 1929 crash on Wall Street.
摩根的合作至关重要。要让英镑在货币市场上维持新的更高价值，美元就不能构成过于强大的竞争威胁。否则，投机者会抛售英镑、买入美元，导致英镑汇率再度下跌。诺曼要么必须在伦敦维持高利率——吸引资金流入英镑，要么斯特朗必须在纽约维持低利率——降低美元投资的吸引力。摩根财团坚持认为提高英国利率才是解决之道。然而，蒙蒂的忠实朋友本·斯特朗却压低了美国利率。这绝非微不足道的技术性问题：一些人会将 1929 年华尔街崩盘归咎于此。

Strong was always sensitive to insinuations of his being in league with Norman and wanted to involve the House of Morgan in the gold operation as political cover. J. P. Morgan and Company could be useful in another way as well. The British needed a large credit to defend the pound against any speculative attacks that might occur. Under the law, Strong could make loans to other central banks, such as the Bank of England, but not to other governments—for instance, the British Treasury. So it would take Strong and J. P. Morgan together to provide money to the bank and the Treasury.
斯特朗对有人暗示他与诺曼勾结一直很敏感，他希望通过将摩根财团卷入黄金操作来获得政治掩护。J. P. 摩根公司还能在另一个方面发挥作用。英国需要一笔巨额信贷来抵御可能出现的投机攻击，以捍卫英镑。根据法律，斯特朗可以向其他央行（如英格兰银行）提供贷款，但不能向其他政府（例如英国财政部）提供贷款。因此，需要斯特朗和 J. P. 摩根联手，才能同时向银行和财政部提供资金。

Norman had already manipulated a series of short-lived chancellors of the Exchequer. In 1925, the chancellor was Winston Churchill, who would later regard the gold decision as possibly his worst political mistake. At sea in finance, he privately confessed to feeling inadequate in that area and was easy prey for the devious Norman. Churchill’s son recalled that Norman would appear at Chartwell and mesmerize Winston by intoning, “I will make you the golden Chancellor.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292509)
诺曼已经操纵了一系列任期短暂的英国财政大臣。1925 年，财政大臣是温斯顿·丘吉尔，他后来认为黄金决策可能是自己最大的政治失误。在金融领域如坠云雾的他私下承认对此力不从心，因而轻易成了狡黠的诺曼的猎物。丘吉尔的儿子回忆道，诺曼会造访查特韦尔庄园，用“我会让你成为黄金财政大臣”的吟诵令温斯顿如痴如醉。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292509)

Grenfell disliked Churchill and privately criticized him as “still at heart a cheeky, over confident boy.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292676) Both Norman and Grenfell wanted pliant politicians who would relegate financial decisions to the merchant bankers, for in the twenties the merchant bankers still dominated the Court of the Bank of England. (The five large commercial, or “clearing,” banks still didn’t have power commensurate with their resources, which grew fantastically through mergers in the 1920s.) Before announcing the gold decision in April 1925, Grenfell watched Churchill as if he were an unpredictable truant who might do something stupidly independent: “We, and especially Norman, feel that the new Chancellor’s cleverness, his almost uncanny brilliance, is a danger. At present he is a willing pupil but the moment he thinks he can stand on his own legs and believes that he understands economic questions he may, by some indiscretion, land us in trouble.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292876)
格伦费尔不喜欢丘吉尔，私下批评他“骨子里仍是个厚脸皮、过于自信的小子”。 [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292676) 诺曼和格伦费尔都希望有顺从的政客，把金融决策权交给商业银行家——因为在 20 年代，商业银行家仍掌控着英格兰银行董事会。（五家大型商业银行，即“清算”银行，尽管通过 20 年代的兼并使其资源急剧膨胀，但影响力仍未与其财力相称。）1925 年 4 月宣布金本位决策前，格伦费尔像盯着可能做出愚蠢独立举动的不可预测逃学生一样注视着丘吉尔：“我们，尤其是诺曼，觉得这位新任财政大臣的聪明才智——近乎诡异的锋芒——是个危险。目前他是个顺从的学生，但一旦他自以为能独立站稳脚跟、相信他懂经济问题，就可能因某些轻率行为让我们陷入麻烦。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2292876)

As an attempt to restore the old imperial pound, the 1925 gold-standard decision was a colossal miscalculation, a nostalgic attempt to restore Britain’s former power. It was so lethal because Norman wished to return to gold at the lofty prewar rate of £1 to \$4.86. At that rate, British industry couldn’t compete with world exports; even Russell Leffingwell thought Norman too heedless of the British employment situation.
作为试图恢复旧日帝国英镑地位的尝试，1925 年恢复金本位的决策堪称一次重大的误判，是一场试图重振英国昔日荣光的怀旧之举。其危害之深，根源在于诺曼执意要以战前 1 英镑兑 4.86 美元的高昂汇率回归金本位。在这种汇率下，英国工业根本无法在全球出口中竞争；就连拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔都认为诺曼对英国的就业形势太过掉以轻心。

Not everybody cheered the plan. Keynes thought it would weaken British industry and force a sharp decline in wages to offset the stronger currency. (Perhaps in retaliation, the merciless Grenfell took to describing Keynes’s new bride, Lydia Lopokova, as the “little ballet girl.”) Many British industrialists echoed this alarm, and the jittery Norman almost backed down. One last boost was needed. Grenfell cabled Jack, “I think the Governor would like me to give him assurance that you personally still approve of his course of action in this whole matter.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293051) Jack did.
并非所有人都为这个计划叫好。凯恩斯认为，这会削弱英国工业，并迫使工资大幅下跌，以抵消货币升值的冲击。（或许是为了报复，冷酷的格伦费尔竟将凯恩斯的新婚妻子莉迪亚·洛波科娃描述为“跳芭蕾的小妞”。）许多英国实业家也纷纷发出同样的警报，而本就忐忑不安的诺曼几乎打起了退堂鼓。此时只差最后一把推力。格伦费尔致电杰克：“我想行长希望我向他保证，你本人仍然认可他在整件事上的行动方针。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293051) 杰克确实给了他这份保证。

On April 28, 1925, in the House of Commons, with Norman in the distinguished-visitors’ gallery, Churchill announced Britain’s return to gold. Fearing a runaway chancellor, Grenfell was relieved that the chancellor didn’t depart from his prepared text. The New York Fed provided a \$200-million credit to the Bank of England, and J. P. Morgan and Company a \$100-million credit to the British Treasury. Because the pound soared and speculative attacks didn’t materialize, the credits weren’t needed. By November, Churchill announced that the embargo on foreign loans was lifted.
1925 年 4 月 28 日，在下议院，诺曼坐在贵宾席上，丘吉尔宣布英国回归金本位制。格伦费尔担心财政大臣会失控，所幸他并未偏离事先准备好的讲稿。纽约联邦储备银行向英格兰银行提供了 2 亿美元信贷，J. P. 摩根公司则向英国财政部提供了 1 亿美元信贷。由于英镑飙升，投机性攻击并未出现，这些信贷最终未被使用。到 11 月，丘吉尔宣布解除对外贷款的禁令。

The architects were in a self-congratulatory mood. Jack’s friend Prime Minister Stanley Baldwin praised Strong and the House of Morgan as “men than whom there are none in the world who stand higher for financial ability and moral rectitude.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293270) The left wing, however, was up in arms about the threat to British industry and the more than 1-percent commission J. P. Morgan charged for keeping the unused credit ready. Grenfell itched to counterattack but was dissuaded by Churchill: “As we are paying a commission to Messrs Morgan, it would obviously be unsuitable for you to speak in the debate and it would I’m sure expose you to annoyance at the hand of the Socialists.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293416) Grenfell withdrew to that natural habitat of Morgan men—the shadows.
策划者们沉浸在自我庆祝的氛围中。杰克的朋友、首相斯坦利·鲍德温赞扬斯特朗和摩根财团，称他们是“世界上在金融能力和道德正直方面无人能及的人。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293270) 然而，左翼人士对英国工业受到的威胁以及摩根大通为保留未使用的信贷而收取的超过 1%的佣金表示强烈不满。格伦费尔渴望反击，但被丘吉尔劝阻：“既然我们正在向摩根先生们支付佣金，你在辩论中发言显然不合适，而且我肯定这会使你遭到社会党人的刁难。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293416) 格伦费尔退回到了摩根家族成员的自然栖息地——阴影之中。

Before long, Keynes’s worst fears were vindicated, and British coal, textiles, and steel lost their competitiveness in world markets. Far from revivifying Britain, the gold standard seemed to accelerate its decline. There arose the predicted pressures for lower wages to counterbalance a higher pound. But it was impossible for wages and retail prices to adjust to the world price level. By late spring 1926, England had suffered a coal strike and a general strike with venomous overtones of class warfare. (During the strikes, Grenfell joked that he was glad to be relieved of the roar of motor buses and found the office pleasantly devoid of business.) When Ben Strong visited London during the strike, he met with Churchill and Norman. They managed to avoid talk of the gold standard altogether. Stanley Baldwin and Monty Norman didn’t mention the big blunder either. They forgot their troubles by playing duets together—Baldwin at the piano, Norman singing. It was a curiously civilized form of escape as strikers and policemen clashed in the streets.
不久之后，凯恩斯最担心的局面便一一应验：英国的煤炭、纺织和钢铁业在全球市场中彻底丧失了竞争力。金本位制非但没有提振英国经济，反而似乎加速了其衰退。正如所料，为了抵消英镑高企的压力，降低工资的呼声随之而来。然而，工资和零售价格根本无法适应世界价格水平。到 1926 年春末，英国爆发了煤矿罢工和总罢工，其中弥漫着浓厚的阶级斗争火药味。（罢工期间，格伦费尔开玩笑说，他很高兴听不到公共汽车的轰鸣声，办公室里没生意可做反而很惬意。）罢工期间，本·斯特朗访问伦敦，会见了丘吉尔和诺曼。他们设法完全不谈金本位制。斯坦利·鲍德温和蒙蒂·诺曼也闭口不提那个大错误。他们通过合奏来忘掉烦恼——鲍德温弹钢琴，诺曼演唱。 这是一种奇特的、文明化的逃避方式——彼时罢工者与警察正在街头激烈冲突。

AS the House of Morgan moved from sterling to stabilize other currencies, it drew closer to the Italian government, which was alarmed by a sudden fall in the lira in 1925. The fascist government of Benito Mussolini had been in power for three years, and Wall Street bankers were comforted by the Italian’s macho pride in the lira’s strength. Ben Strong and Monty Norman favored a loan to stabilize Italy’s currency but had qualms about il Duce himself. Shocked by a 1926 visit to Italy, Strong said of the dictator: “I should imagine that he would not hesitate to cut off a man’s head instantly if he failed to deliver what was expected of him.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293634) And Norman was dismayed by political meddling at the Banca d’Italia—an affront to the chastity of a central bank.
随着摩根银行从帮助英镑转向稳定其他货币，它也与意大利政府走得越来越近。1925 年，里拉突然暴跌，让意大利当局大为恐慌。贝尼托·墨索里尼的法西斯政府已执政三年，而这位意大利人对里拉坚挺所展现出的硬汉式自豪，让华尔街的银行家们感到安心。本·斯特朗和蒙塔古·诺曼倾向于提供贷款以稳定意大利货币，但对这位领袖本人心存顾虑。1926 年访问意大利后，斯特朗震惊之余评价这位独裁者：“我猜，如果有人没达到他的期望，他会毫不犹豫地立刻砍下那人的脑袋。”而诺曼则对意大利银行受到的政治干预感到沮丧——这简直是对央行纯洁性的一种侮辱。

Tom Lamont, however, viewed Mussolini in a rosier light. In New York political circles, Lamont enjoyed the reputation of a liberal. His son Corliss, a socialist and later a professor of philosophy at Columbia, saw his father’s foreign-policy views as spotless: “Although my father was a successful banker, and a Republican in politics, he was in essence a liberal, particularly in international affairs.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293798) Corliss praised the tolerant atmosphere of the Lamont household, dubbed the International Inn for its many visiting celebrities and intellectuals. One visitor, H. G. Wells, interested Corliss in socialism, and the two would team up in arguments against the *paterfamilias.* To his credit, Lamont handled Corliss’s radical politics with admirable tact. Corliss would view his own politics not as a negation of his parents’ views but as an extension of their liberalism.
然而，汤姆·拉蒙特对墨索里尼却抱有更为乐观的看法。在纽约政界，拉蒙特享有自由派的美誉。他的儿子科利斯是一名社会主义者，后来成为哥伦比亚大学哲学教授，他认为父亲的外交政策观点无可挑剔：“尽管我父亲是一位成功的银行家，政治上也是共和党人，但本质上他是一位自由派，尤其是在国际事务中。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293798) 科利斯赞扬了拉蒙特家宽容的氛围，这里因常有众多名流和知识分子来访而被戏称为“国际客栈”。来访者之一 H.G.威尔斯使科利斯对社会主义产生了兴趣，两人时常联手与这位家长辩论。值得称赞的是，拉蒙特以令人钦佩的巧妙方式处理了科利斯的激进政治观点。科利斯认为自己的政治立场并非对父母观点的否定，而是他们自由主义思想的延伸。

Always proud of his work for Woodrow Wilson, Lamont seemed to stand out as the great exception, refuting, in Corliss’s words, the “stereotype of rich people and Republicans as conservative or reactionary plutocrats opposed to all forms of progress and liberalism.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293955) Nor was this just a loving son’s bias; such accolades tumbled in upon Lamont. To poet John Masefield, the Lamonts were an exemplary couple, representing everything civilized: “Their political views, national and international, were ever generous and liberal. They always seemed to be in touch with the generous and the liberal of every country.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294081) Even General Smuts of South Africa told Lamont, “There is no doubt that your house is an international meeting place, and an influence for good . . . second to none in the world.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294247)
拉蒙特始终以自己为伍德罗·威尔逊所做的工作为荣，他似乎是个特例，用科利斯的话来说，他打破了“富人和共和党人都是反对一切进步与自由的保守或反动的财阀”这种刻板印象。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2293955) 这并非出于一个孝顺儿子的偏爱——这样的赞誉纷纷涌向拉蒙特。诗人约翰·梅斯菲尔德称赞拉蒙特夫妇是模范夫妻，代表了文明的一切：“他们在国内和国际上的政治观点始终慷慨而开明，似乎总能与每个国家的慷慨开明之士保持联系。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294081) 就连南非的史末资将军也对拉蒙特说：“毫无疑问，你的家是一个国际交流中心，也是一股向善的力量……在世界上首屈一指。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294247)

Why should they have thought otherwise? Reasonable and fond of discourse, an engaging letter writer, Lamont lacked the smug conservatism of many on Wall Street. He was one of the major contributors to the League of Nations Association and the Foreign Policy Association. For many years, he was financial angel of the *Saturday Review of Literature* and knew poets ranging from Robert Frost to Stephen Vincent Benét. He was that rare banker with an appreciation for words, a zest for ideas. Because Lamont was a partner in a mysterious, private bank, his admirers couldn’t compare his stated beliefs with his business behavior. That he served as banker to Italy during its Fascist period apparently didn’t faze them. They doubtless imagined that he kept a businesslike distance from Mussolini and served his client with thinly disguised distaste.
他们何以有别的想法？拉蒙特理性而健谈，写起信来引人入胜，没有华尔街许多人那种自鸣得意的保守。他是国际联盟协会和外交政策协会的主要捐助者之一。多年来，他一直是《星期六文学评论》的财务天使，结识了从罗伯特·弗罗斯特到斯蒂芬·文森特·贝内等诗人。他是那种罕见的、懂得欣赏文字、对思想充满热情的银行家。由于拉蒙特是一家神秘私人银行的合伙人，他的崇拜者无法将他公开的信念与他的商业行为相比较。他在意大利法西斯时期担任其银行家的角色，显然并未让他们感到不安。他们无疑想象他对墨索里尼保持着一种公事公办的距离，并以毫不掩饰的厌恶来服务客户。

But Lamont could do nothing halfway. As a Morgan man, he had to render those thousand and one special touches that made a client feel pampered. As with Pierpont, unvarnished banking lacked some final satisfaction for Lamont. His elaborate letters and memorandums seem almost a substitute for the writing career he never had. He always wanted to go beyond mere dollars and invest his deals with some larger meaning. He tried to make loans a total experience, by immersing himself in the politics and culture of client countries. In Italy, he would meet with Mussolini one day, then picnic in the Roman *campagna* the next. Despite the Fascist regime, he saw an Italy touched with poetry and romance. As president of the Italy-America Society, he hosted meetings of its Dante Committee at his East Seventieth Street townhouse and once screened a Florentine film on Dante and Beatrice. At the office, he worked at a beautiful Italian refectory table. Yes, his life would be all of a piece, a fusion of business and pleasure.
但拉蒙特做事从不半途而废。作为摩根家族的一员，他必须提供那些无微不至的贴心服务，让客户感到备受呵护。和皮尔庞特一样，仅仅是银行业务本身，并不能给拉蒙特带来最终的满足感。他那些精心雕琢的信件和备忘录，几乎成了他从未真正从事的写作生涯的替代品。他总想超越单纯的金钱交易，为自己的生意赋予某种更宏大的意义。他试图将贷款打造成一种全方位的体验，为此他深入沉浸于客户所在国的政治与文化之中。在意大利，他可能今天与墨索里尼会面，明天又去罗马郊野野餐。尽管身处法西斯政权之下，他眼中看到的却是一个充满诗意与浪漫的意大利。作为美意协会的主席，他曾在东七十街的联排别墅里主持但丁委员会的会议，还曾放映过一部关于但丁与贝雅特丽齐的佛罗伦萨电影。而在办公室里，他用的是一张精美的意大利修道院式长桌。 是的，他的生活将会浑然一体，事业与享乐交融在一起。

The Morgan agent in Rome was Giovanni Fummi, whom Lamont had met at the Paris Peace Conference. Fummi was a former stockbroker with an American wife, a charming, extroverted man with a trim mustache and laughing eyes. He lived well at the Hotel Excelsior and was tanned both summer and winter. He was typical of the powerful but discreet lobbyists the bank employed in foreign capitals. He was rich in contacts, both in the government and in the Vatican. Lamont would boast of Fummi’s high standing with Mussolini yet insist he was free of Fascist taint. Fummi was perhaps less a Fascist than a conformist willing to sacrifice his principles for *la dolce vita.* He was an expert rationalizer and even when faced with Italian atrocities would contend that criticism might only polarize the Fascist party and bring more extreme elements to the fore. Funny, charming, and sentimental, Fummi made a curious match with the cool, patrician House of Morgan.
摩根银行在罗马的代表是乔瓦尼·富米，拉蒙特在巴黎和会上与他相识。富米曾是一名股票经纪人，娶了位美国妻子，留着修剪整齐的小胡子，双眼含笑，是个魅力四射、性格外向的人。他在埃克塞尔西奥酒店过着优渥生活，无论冬夏都晒得一身古铜色。他是该银行派驻外国首都的那些有权有势却行事低调的说客中的典型代表。他在政府和梵蒂冈都人脉亨通。拉蒙特常夸耀富米在墨索里尼面前很吃得开，却又坚称他未受法西斯玷污。与其说富米是个法西斯分子，不如说他是个随波逐流者，为了享受"甜蜜生活"甘愿牺牲原则。他精于自圆其说，即便面对意大利的暴行，也会争辩说批评只会让法西斯党两极分化，反而让更极端的势力得以上台。富米风趣、迷人又多愁善感，与冷静、贵族气派的摩根家族形成了奇妙的搭配。

After the war, J. P. Morgan and Company sparred with Dillon, Read for business with the Italian government. Lamont wanted an exclusive relationship, as understood by the Gentleman Banker’s Code. In 1923, six months after taking power, Mussolini first met with Lamont to discuss how to restore Italian credit. Initially Wall Street viewed il Duce benignly, as the man who had saved strike-torn Italy from Bolshevik hands. The Blackshirt terror that killed a hundred people in the 1921 elections was conveniently overlooked. Traveling through Italy, Jack Morgan reported to a friend, “We had the great satisfaction of seeing Mr. Mussolini’s Revolution.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294381) In the early days, Mussolini stuck to conservative financial policies and kept flunkies from the key financial posts. Italian financial policy was something of a showcase for the outer world.
战后，J. P. Morgan and Company 与 Dillon, Read 为争取意大利政府的业务而展开竞争。拉蒙特希望建立独家合作关系，这是“绅士银行家守则”所默认的。1923 年，墨索里尼上台六个月后，首次与拉蒙特会面，商讨如何恢复意大利的信贷。起初，华尔街对这位“领袖”颇有好感，视他为将饱受罢工动荡的意大利从布尔什维克手中拯救出来的人物。至于 1921 年选举中黑衫党暴行导致百人丧生的事实，则被刻意忽略。杰克·摩根在游历意大利时向友人报告：“我们非常满意地见证了墨索里尼先生的革命。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294381) 在早期，墨索里尼坚持保守的财政政策，并将亲信排除在关键财政职位之外。意大利的财政政策在某种程度上成了对外展示的样板。

During their fifteen-year relationship, Lamont and Mussolini would form an implausible pair. Lamont was stylish and natty with wonderful manners, scores of friends, and a refined sense of beauty. Mussolini was sloppy and unshaven, an insecure, misanthropic loner with a mega-phonic voice and a black view of human affairs. Their relationship had a Beauty and the Beast quality that hid one likeness: both men were former journalists and newspaper owners and were fascinated by the art of public relations. Both had the knack of giving a pretty verbal gloss to ugly things, and much of their relationship revolved around the manipulation of words.
在长达十五年的交往中，拉蒙特与墨索里尼成了一对看似极不搭调的组合。拉蒙特仪表堂堂、讲究穿着，举止优雅，朋友众多，对美有着独到的鉴赏力。而墨索里尼则邋遢不修边幅，缺乏安全感，愤世嫉俗、孤僻离群，嗓门大如喇叭，对世事抱着阴暗的看法。他们的关系颇有几分“美女与野兽”的味道，却隐藏着一个共同点：两人都曾是记者和报社老板，对公关艺术着迷不已。他们都擅长用漂亮的言辞粉饰丑陋的事物，而他们的关系在很大程度上正是围绕如何操纵语言展开的。

Lamont didn’t start out an apologist for Mussolini. As usual, the path to perdition comprised a series of small steps. In the summer of 1923, Italian troops occupied the Greek island of Corfu, and their bombing of civilians outraged world opinion. If the League of Nations thwarted him, Mussolini promised to destroy it. Appalled, Lamont told Fummi, “I think you ought to know direct from me that the action of Mr. Mussolini in the Grecian affair has given us all a tremendous jolt here.” The fact of the occupation bothered Lamont less than the manner: “There was no reason in the world why he shouldn’t have been able to occupy it peacefully instead of shooting up and killing several innocent civilians, including children.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294545) The indignation wasn’t simply humanitarian, for Lamont realized that Corfu would make impossible the financing he had discussed with Mussolini the previous May.
拉蒙特一开始并非墨索里尼的辩护者。和往常一样，通往堕落之路是由一连串小步组成的。1923 年夏天，意大利军队占领了希腊的科孚岛，他们对平民的轰炸激起了世界舆论的愤怒。如果国际联盟阻挠他，墨索里尼便扬言要摧毁它。拉蒙特大惊失色，对富米说：“我认为你应该直接从我这里知道，墨索里尼先生在希腊事件中的行动让我们所有人都大为震惊。”让拉蒙特不安的与其说是占领的事实，不如说是其方式：“他完全有理由和平占领，而不是开枪射杀并杀害几名无辜平民，其中包括儿童。”这种愤怒不仅仅是出于人道主义，因为拉蒙特意识到，科孚事件会使他去年五月与墨索里尼商讨的融资计划无法实现。

The following year, Blackshirt violence intensified. Hundreds were murdered or wounded during the rigged 1924 elections, scores of judges were later dismissed, and Italian democracy was dismantled. Mussolini now controlled six of the thirteen cabinet departments and the three armed services. For the first time, a conflict arose between Lamont’s business commitment to Italy and the humane indignation of some important friends, most notably Walter Lippmann, who attacked the Corfu invasion in the *New York World.* When Lippmann returned from Rome in 1924, he had dinner with Lamont and told him il Duce needed these violent antics to stay in power. Lamont didn’t disagree.
次年，Blackshirt 暴力活动愈演愈烈。在舞弊丛生的 1924 年选举中，数百人惨遭杀害或受伤，随后数十名法官被解职，意大利民主体制就此瓦解。此时 Mussolini 已掌控十三个内阁部门中的六个，以及三军兵权。Lamont 对意大利的商业投入，与他几位重要朋友（尤其是 Walter Lippmann）的人道义愤首次发生冲突——后者在 New York World 上撰文抨击了对 Corfu 的入侵。1924 年 Lippmann 从罗马归来后，与 Lamont 共进晚餐时告诉他，il Duce 需要靠这些暴力手段才能保住权力。Lamont 未置可否。

How would Lamont deal with the growing strain between his liberalism and his desire to expand Morgan business in Italy? He would paper it over with words. He had a politician’s talent for speaking in different voices to different people. He never exactly lied but shaded the truth and pretended to side with everyone. Only Lamont was bright enough to keep all his stories straight and wrap them up in outward consistency. After the dinner with Lippmann, he wrote to Prince Gelasio Caetani, the Italian ambassador in Washington, about the chat: “This all sounds to me like silly gossip; nevertheless I had to keep within bounds because I was the host.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294733) This was a more cynical voice than the one Lippmann heard. Through nods, winks, and pats on the back, Lamont would keep everyone happy.
拉蒙特该如何应对自己自由主义倾向与拓展摩根在意大利业务之间的日益紧张关系？他会用言辞来掩饰。他拥有政客般的能力，对不同的人说不同的话。他从未真正撒谎，但总会模糊真相，假装与每个人都站在一边。只有拉蒙特足够聪明，能把所有说辞都圆得滴水不漏，并在表面上保持一致。与李普曼共进晚餐后，他写信给意大利驻华盛顿大使杰拉西奥·卡埃塔尼王子，提及这次谈话：“在我看来，这全是无聊的闲话；但身为东道主，我不得不有所克制。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294733) 这番语气比李普曼所听到的要世故得多。拉蒙特通过点头、眨眼和拍拍后背，让每个人都感到满意。

A certain convenience of vision, a selective filtering out of details, began to accompany the verbal touch-ups. Mussolini’s henchmen had now killed Giacomo Matteotti, the regime’s eminent foe, causing Socialist deputies to boycott Parliament. Yet when Lamont visited Italy in April 1925 to meet with Mussolini, he seemed oblivious to these grisly developments. Bonaldo Stringher, the governor of the Banca d’Italia, assured Lamont that il Duce resorted to strong-arm tactics only when absolutely necessary. With Corliss, the Lamonts motored through Italian hill towns and stopped by Bernard Berenson’s villa, I Tatti, for tea and a chat about Italian Renaissance art. Afterward, Lamont wrote this paean: “The Italy through which I traveled seemed to be industrious and prosperous. The newspaper headlines in the New York and even London papers seemed to me exaggerated. Everybody, both in and out of the Government, laughed at these stories of street fights, unrest upsetting the Government etc.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294930) Back at 23 Wall, Lamont received an autographed photograph from Mussolini, which was now featured as prominently on his wall as the earlier picture of Woodrow Wilson had been.
随着言辞的修饰，一种视觉上的便利——对细节的选择性过滤——也开始悄然发生。墨索里尼的党羽此时已杀害了政权的重要对手贾科莫·马泰奥蒂，导致社会党议员抵制议会。然而，当拉蒙特于 1925 年 4 月访问意大利会见墨索里尼时，他似乎对这些血腥事件浑然不觉。意大利银行行长博纳尔多·斯特林赫向拉蒙特保证，领袖只是在绝对必要时才动用强硬手段。拉蒙特夫妇与科利斯驾车穿越意大利的山城，顺道拜访了伯纳德·贝伦森的别墅“伊塔蒂”，一边品茶一边畅谈意大利文艺复兴艺术。事后，拉蒙特写下这样的赞歌：“我所游历的意大利似乎勤劳而繁荣。纽约甚至伦敦报纸上的头条在我看来都言过其实了。” 政府内外的人都在嘲笑这些关于街头斗殴、动荡不安、政府混乱的传闻。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2294930) 回到华尔街 23 号，拉蒙特收到了墨索里尼亲笔签名的照片。如今，这张照片被挂在他办公室的墙上，与之前伍德罗·威尔逊的照片一样显眼。

In reviewing Lamont’s files, one gets the impression that in 1925 he made a moral leap and cast his lot with Mussolini. The year was rife with rumors, encouraged by Lamont’s trip, of a \$100-million Morgan emergency loan. In part, Mussolini wanted Morgan money to rebuild Rome as a monument to his own maniacal splendor. The new secretary of state, Frank Kellogg, made it clear that no loan would be forthcoming unless Rome settled more than \$2 billion in war debts with Washington. In October 1925, Mussolini sent a mission to Washington, headed by his finance minister, Count Giuseppe Volpi, to negotiate the debt.
翻阅拉蒙特的档案，会让人产生这样一种印象：1925 年，他在道德上做出了一个巨大的转变，选择与墨索里尼同舟共济。那一年，拉蒙特的意大利之行助长了各种传言，称摩根将提供 1 亿美元的紧急贷款。墨索里尼之所以想要摩根的这笔钱，部分原因是为了重建罗马，将其打造成自己疯狂辉煌的纪念碑。新任国务卿弗兰克·凯洛格明确表态：除非意大利向华盛顿清偿超过 20 亿美元的战争债务，否则贷款免谈。1925 年 10 月，墨索里尼派遣以财政部长朱塞佩·沃尔皮伯爵为首的代表团前往华盛顿，就债务问题进行谈判。

As the big \$100-million loan hung in the balance, Lamont made his most startling shift with Mussolini, one that went far beyond basic banking requirements. This former champion of the League of Nations began to coach the Italian dictator on how to appeal to Anglo-American opinion. He fed him sugared phrases, language that would make reprehensible policies palatable abroad. A modern man, Lamont knew that any product, if attractively packaged, could be marketed to the public. The Italian problem was redefined as one of public relations. In public appearances, Lamont tried to deflect attention from Mussolini’s politics to his economic record. Wall Street enjoyed pretending that there were two Mussolinis—the sound economic leader and the tough politician—who could be treated separately. Mussolini spouted the predictable litany of promises—balanced budgets, low inflation, and sound money—that bankers adored. Resorting to sophistry, Lamont said he was only praising the Italian economy, not Mussolini or fascism. In a January 1926 speech before the Foreign Policy Association, Lamont extolled Italy’s record in lowering inflation, stopping strikes, and reducing unemployment. He even endorsed Mussolini’s highway and public works projects—measures that would be roundly denounced by Morgan partners during the Roosevelt administration. Lamont’s trump card was his claim that the Italians supported Mussolini: “At this gathering to-day, we all count ourselves liberals, I suppose. Are we sure that we are liberal enough to be willing for the Italian people to have the sort of government which they apparently want?”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2295319)

Lamont’s efforts were crowned with success: in early 1926, Washington reached a lenient settlement of Italy’s war debt, paving the way for a Morgan operation. Treasury Secretary Andrew Mellon had already advised President Coolidge that without a conciliatory debt settlement, Wall Street might lose the Italian loan to Britain. So Coolidge was pleased when, a week later, Lamont announced the \$100-million Morgan loan. It triggered a vituperative debate in Congress, with critics such as Representative Henry Rainey, Democrat of Illinois, calling Mussolini a murderous dictator and protesting the favoritism shown toward the Fascist regime. As with the Dawes Loan to Germany, the Morgan loan to Italy proved a catalyst for further American investment. The bank itself went on to provide loans to Rome and to two industrial clients, Fiat and Pirelli. In December 1927, J. P. Morgan again joined with Ben Strong and established a credit for the Banca d’Italia that permitted a return to the gold standard.
拉蒙的努力终于开花结果：1926 年初，华盛顿方面同意对意大利的战争债务给予宽大处理，为摩根财团的运作铺平了道路。财政部长安德鲁·梅隆早已告知柯立芝总统，若不达成和解性的债务协议，华尔街可能会把这笔意大利贷款拱手让给英国。因此，一周后当拉蒙宣布摩根提供 1 亿美元贷款时，柯立芝相当满意。此举在国会引发了一场激烈的争论，伊利诺伊州民主党众议员亨利·雷尼等批评者将墨索里尼称为残忍的独裁者，并抗议政府对法西斯政权给予的偏袒。与对德国的道斯贷款一样，摩根对意大利的贷款也成了进一步吸引美国投资的催化剂。该银行随后又向罗马政府以及菲亚特和倍耐力两家工业企业提供了贷款。1927 年 12 月，J. P. 摩根再次与本·斯特朗合作，为意大利银行建立了一笔信贷，使其得以回归金本位制。

On a Wall Street disturbed by European radicalism and worshipful of economic progress, Lamont wasn’t the only Mussolini supporter. Jack Morgan and George Whitney both hailed him as a great patriot. Otto Kahn of Kuhn, Loeb likened his iron rule to that of a tough receiver straightening out a bankrupt company. With a poetic flourish, Willis Booth of Guaranty Trust said Mussolini lifted Italy “out of the slough of despair into the bright realm of promise.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2295545) Judge Elbert Gary of U.S. Steel and publicist Ivy Lee joined the fan club. As a self-professed “missionary” for Mussolini, Lamont’s contribution was singular. One scholar has noted, “Of all the American business leaders, the one who most vigorously patronized the cause of Fascism was Thomas W. Lamont. . . . Though not the most voluble business spokesman for the Italian government, Lamont was clearly the most valuable. For it was he who translated verbal apologetics into hard cash, securing for Mussolini a \$100 million loan.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2295714)
在华尔街因欧洲激进主义而躁动不安、并对经济进步顶礼膜拜的年代，拉蒙特并非唯一支持墨索里尼的人。杰克·摩根和乔治·惠特尼都称赞他是伟大的爱国者。库恩-洛布公司的奥托·卡恩将他铁腕统治比作一位强硬的接管人整顿破产企业。担保信托银行的威利斯·布斯则以诗意的笔触宣称，墨索里尼将意大利“从绝望的泥潭中拖出，带入了充满希望的璀璨疆域。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2295545) 美国钢铁公司的法官埃尔伯特·加里和公关专家艾维·李也加入了拥趸行列。作为一名自诩为墨索里尼“传教士”的人，拉蒙特的贡献堪称独一无二。一位学者指出：“在所有美国商业领袖中，最热忱地赞助法西斯事业的人是托马斯·W·拉蒙特……尽管他并非意大利政府最健谈的商业代言人，但显然是最有价值的那一个。因为他将口头辩护转化为真金白银，为墨索里尼争取到了一亿美元贷款。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2295714)

Was Lamont ignorant of events inside Italy? Not likely. As lender to sovereign states, the bank maintained thick clipping files and received abundant intelligence from around the world. (It was partly the excellence of Lamont’s files that enabled him to keep abreast of an extraordinary range of clients.) In January 1926, publicity man Martin Egan passed on to Lamont an anguished letter from a friend in Anticoli, Italy:
拉蒙特对意大利境内发生的事情一无所知？不太可能。作为向主权国家放贷的银行，它保存着厚厚一叠剪报档案，并从世界各地获取大量情报。（拉蒙特档案的卓越性，部分也让他能够跟上异常广泛的客户动态。）1926 年 1 月，公关人马丁·伊根转交给拉蒙特一封来自意大利安蒂科利朋友的痛苦来信：

> I wonder if you all in New York know just what you are doing in backing Fascistism in Italy. We had a taste of it last night here. A party of Fascists motored up from Rome armed with revolvers, rapiers and loaded whips, arrived at nine and proceeded to beat up with fierce brutality the peasants who could not produce a Fascisti card. . . . If any peasant objects he is shot. This is happening all over the place. It seems funny for American money to be perpetuating it.
> 我不知道你们在纽约的人是否清楚，你们支持意大利法西斯主义到底在做什么。昨晚我们在这里就尝到了滋味。一伙法西斯分子从罗马驾车而来，带着左轮手枪、长剑和装满铅弹的鞭子，九点钟到达，然后开始凶狠残暴地殴打那些拿不出法西斯党证的农民……如果有农民反抗，就会被枪毙。这种事到处都在发生。用美国人的钱来延续这种暴行，真是滑稽。

On top of this, Lamont scribbled, “Pretty terrible, I must say.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2295859) In addition, an Italian opposition leader reported to him how his house in Rome was plundered by Blackshirts; he passed along a sheaf of bellicose speeches in which Mussolini boasted of his readiness for war. These speeches occasionally upset Lamont, but he always ended up recasting the problem as public relations.
除此之外，拉蒙特潦草地写道：“不得不说，实在太糟了。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2295859) 此外，一位意大利反对派领袖向他报告说，自己在罗马的住所遭到黑衫党洗劫；他还转交了一叠好战言论，墨索里尼在其中吹嘘自己已做好战争准备。这些言论偶尔会让拉蒙特感到不安，但他最终总是将问题归结为公关事务。

At the same time, Lamont was plied with constant requests from a new Italian ambassador, Giacomo de Martino, whom he used to put up at the University Club in New York. Most of de Martino’s requests were for more sympathetic press treatment of Mussolini. To this end, Lamont lined up favorable editorials in the New York *Sun*, protested dispatches of an “anti-Fascist” correspondent in the *World*, and arranged for de Martino to plead with Walter Lippmann at his home. Mussolini took a personal interest in winning over Lippmann and even sent him a personal photograph with a tribute to Lippmann’s “wisdom” scribbled across it.[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296057) (Lippmann worked in a penthouse under signed photos of the British ambassador and Morgan partner Thomas Cochran.) Lippmann’s antagonism toward Mussolini only deepened, however, confirmed by Italian press censorship, which he saw as symptomatic of weakness. “As long as the censorship lasts, I shall remain persuaded that the Mussolini Government is not certain of its hold on the Italian people,” he told Lamont. “If the opposition to it inside of Italy were as negligible as Fascists like the Ambassador make out, there would be no occasion for a censorship of this character.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296225)
与此同时，Lamont 不断收到新任意大利大使 Giacomo de Martino 的请求——他过去常安排这位大使住在纽约的 University Club。de Martino 的多数请求是希望媒体对 Mussolini 给予更友善的报道。为此，Lamont 在《纽约太阳报》安排了有利的社论，抗议《世界报》一位“反法西斯”记者的报道，并安排 de Martino 前往 Walter Lippmann 家中游说。Mussolini 本人对争取 Lippmann 很上心，甚至送了他一张亲笔签名照，照片上题词赞颂 Lippmann 的“睿智”。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296057) （Lippmann 在一间顶楼套房办公，墙上挂着英国大使和 Morgan 合伙人 Thomas Cochran 的签名照。）然而，Lippmann 对 Mussolini 的反感反而加深了——意大利的新闻审查制度让他确信这种高压手段恰恰是软弱的征兆。 “只要审查制度持续下去，我就始终相信墨索里尼政府并没有真正掌控意大利人民，”他对拉蒙特说道。“如果意大利国内对政府的反对力量真像法西斯分子大使所声称的那样微不足道，那就根本没有必要实施这种审查制度。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296225)

Mussolini stamped out all press freedoms in Italy. He was so preoccupied by his image that he would examine in advance front-page layouts of national newspapers. By 1930, half of his ministers were recruited from the press corps, and he decreed that all journalists be Fascists. Dissenting editors were jailed, and many foreign journalists were mugged by street toughs. Hence, Mussolini’s only fear of press exposure was from abroad.
墨索里尼扼杀了意大利所有的新闻自由。他极度沉迷于自身形象，甚至会提前审查全国各大报纸的头版版面。到 1930 年，他的内阁部长中有一半来自新闻界，他还下令所有记者都必须是法西斯分子。持不同政见的编辑被投入监狱，许多外国记者遭到街头暴徒的殴打。因此，墨索里尼唯一担心的媒体曝光，就是来自国外的报道。

At the time of the \$100-million loan, Lamont and Martin Egan convinced Ambassador de Martino to suggest an American press service to Mussolini. Its purpose, Lamont said, would be to “acquaint our financial community more faithfully with the proper situation in Italy.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296392) Mussolini was excited by the idea, and the secret operation went into effect in 1927. Paid for by Italy, it would write up favorable press releases and bring over speakers. There was some difficulty in finding an appropriate American journalist to head the operation. The first choice was Associated Press correspondent Percy Winner, who once wrote of Mussolini, “An artist in the use of millions of human beings as tools and a nation as a canvas, the Duce rises so far above the typical politician or even dictator as to defy definition.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296582) Even de Martino was relieved when Winner’s name was dropped for a less slavish admirer. Early preparations for the press bureau were cleared with Lamont, and it eventually operated under the aegis of his Italy-America Society.
在提供 1 亿美元贷款时，拉蒙特和马丁·伊根说服德马蒂诺大使向墨索里尼建议设立一家美国新闻机构。拉蒙特表示，其目的是“让我们的金融界更准确地了解意大利的真实情况。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296392) 墨索里尼对这个想法感到兴奋，于是这项秘密行动于 1927 年启动。资金由意大利提供，该机构会撰写有利的新闻稿并邀请演说家。寻找一位合适的美国记者来主持此项工作遇到了一些困难。首人选是美联社记者珀西·温纳，他曾这样描述墨索里尼：“这位元首将百万民众当作工具、将整个国家视为画布，其艺术境界远超凡俗政客甚至独裁者，令人无法定义。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296582) 当温纳的名字被替换为一位不那么奴颜婢膝的崇拜者时，连德马蒂诺也松了一口气。 新闻局的早期筹备工作已经征得了拉蒙特的同意，并最终在他的意大利-美国协会（Italy-America Society）的支持下运作。

How did Lamont, a Woodrow Wilson protege, end up an accomplice of Benito Mussolini? The answer is partly personal. He had a romantic attachment to Italy and a proprietary feeling toward the Italian account, which he had won. His training as a Morgan banker taught him to transcend the mundane and to move mountains for important clients. This personal approach to business suited him, for he had varied and contradictory ambitions. He yearned to be a sleuth, a statesman, a political fixer, and a *bon vivant.* He loved politics, not so much as an ideological contest, but for the intrigue and the high-stakes gambling with fate. As a result, he could cooperate with politicians of many stripes. Washington’s tacit support for Italian loans perhaps also removed any inhibitions that might otherwise have existed.
身为伍德罗·威尔逊（Woodrow Wilson）门徒的拉蒙特，怎么会沦为贝尼托·墨索里尼（Benito Mussolini）的帮凶？答案部分源于个人原因。他对意大利怀有浪漫情结，对自己赢得的意大利业务也带有一种占有欲。他作为摩根银行家的训练，让他学会了超越凡俗，为重要客户移山开路。这种个性化的商业作风很适合他，因为他怀有各种矛盾且多样的抱负。他渴望成为一名侦探、政治家、政治掮客，以及享乐主义者。他热爱政治——并非将其视为意识形态的较量，而是醉心于其中的权谋以及与命运豪赌的刺激。正因如此，他能与各式各样的政客合作。华盛顿方面对意大利贷款项目的默许，或许也消除了本可能存在的任何顾虑。

Lamont’s Italian adventure exposed other problems. The highly intimate style of “relationship banking” meant that bankers came to share their clients’ interests and identify with them. They felt almost too responsible for the success of their issues. As Lamont once said, when the House of Morgan undertook to market a block of common shares, it assumed responsibility, not simply for the solvency of the corporation involved but for its brilliant, successful management. This was the old London tradition upheld by Pierpont in dealing with bankrupt, profligate railroads. Now that tradition was being transposed into a policy of backing dictators whose bonds were floated by the House of Morgan. Although lending to sovereign states had political overtones and moral imperatives quite absent from conventional business banking, the style of “relationship banking” was transferred intact.
Lamont 的意大利之行暴露了其他问题。"关系银行"这种高度亲密的风格意味着银行家们会与客户共担利益，甚至产生身份认同。他们对所经办业务成功的责任感几乎到了过度的地步。正如 Lamont 所言，当摩根银行承销一批普通股时，它承担的责任不仅限于相关公司的偿付能力，更要确保其经营管理的卓越与成功。这正是当年皮尔庞特在处理那些破产、挥霍无度的铁路公司时所秉持的伦敦传统。如今，这一传统已转化为支持那些由摩根银行发行债券的独裁者的政策。尽管主权国家贷款带有传统商业银行业务所不具备的政治色彩和道德约束，但"关系银行"的风格却被原封不动地移植了过来。

There was another factor of paramount importance in Morgan’s growing involvement in Italy—the Vatican. Earlier, Pope Pius X had grown wistful and regretted not having requested investment tips from Pierpont. This papal prayer was belatedly answered in the late 1920s, during the reign of Pius XI. The new relationship owed something to the friendship between Jack Morgan and the pope. In his earlier incarnation as Monsignor Ratti, prefect of the Vatican Library, the pope had restored the Morgan collection of sixty Coptic texts dug up from an old stone well in an Egyptian monastery. As an expert on early Christian documents, he had hardened the parchments until they became legible. The task consumed twelve years before the texts were finally returned to the Pierpont Morgan Library.
在摩根逐步深入意大利事务的过程中，还有一个至关重要的因素——梵蒂冈。早在之前，教皇庇护十世曾不无遗憾地感慨，后悔当初没有向皮尔庞特请教投资之道。这份迟来的愿望终于在 20 世纪 20 年代末，在庇护十一世在位期间得以实现。这段新关系的建立，多少要归功于杰克·摩根与教皇之间的情谊。庇护十一世早年在担任梵蒂冈图书馆馆长、身份还是拉蒂蒙席时，曾修复过摩根收藏的一批科普特文献——这些文献是从埃及一座修道院的古石井中发掘出来的，共六十份。作为早期基督教文献专家，他通过特殊处理使这些羊皮纸变得清晰可读。这项工作耗时十二年，最终这些文献才得以归还皮尔庞特·摩根图书馆。

Even more important to the future Morgan relationship with the Vatican was the 1929 Lateran Treaty, which resolved a fifty-eight-year dispute between Italy and the papacy. Back in 1871, Italy had taken control of the Papal States, which included much of southern Italy and had provided the Vatican with enormous revenues. In 1929, Mussolini not only recognized the Vatican’s sovereignty but paid almost \$90 million in compensation for the seized lands. This vast sum was paid in the form of Italian treasury bonds worth 1.5 billion lira.
对摩根家族与梵蒂冈未来关系而言，更关键的当属 1929 年《拉特兰条约》，这项条约终结了意大利与教廷之间长达 58 年的争端。早在 1871 年，意大利就已将教皇国收入囊中——这片涵盖意大利南部大部分地区的领土，曾为梵蒂冈带来巨额财政收入。1929 年，墨索里尼不仅承认了梵蒂冈的主权，还就当年被没收的土地偿付了近 9000 万美元的补偿金。这笔巨款以 15 亿里拉的意大利国债形式支付。

Before this, the Vatican had managed money in a conservative and rather primitive way. At the turn of the century, Pope Leo XIII had simply filled a trunk with gold coins and stored it under his bed. But with his good Milanese business head, Pius XI wanted to manage the Vatican’s assets in a modern, secular manner. On June 2, 1929, he met with Bernardino Nogara of the Banca Commerciale Italiana, one of the rare meetings in papal history not recorded on the Vatican calendar. Nogara was not only a highly experienced banker but had so many priests and nuns among his siblings as to make him an acceptable layman for confidential Vatican work.
在此之前，梵蒂冈的财务管理方式保守而原始。世纪之交时，教皇利奥十三世只是将一箱金币塞在床底下。但拥有米兰人精明的商业头脑的庇护十一世，希望以现代世俗的方式管理梵蒂冈的资产。1929 年 6 月 2 日，他会见了意大利商业银行的贝尔纳迪诺·诺加拉——这是教皇史上罕见的、未记录在梵蒂冈日历上的会面之一。诺加拉不仅是经验丰富的银行家，他的兄弟姐妹中还有众多神父和修女，这使他成为梵蒂冈机密工作中可被接受的世俗人士。

The Pope asked Nogara to create the Special Administration of the Holy See and convert the Italian treasury bonds into a diversified stock portfolio. The operation was so secret that only one report was produced each year. Nogara would hand-deliver it to the pope, who would inspect it and then place it in his personal safe. Pius XI put no restrictions on Nogara’s investments, and the banker was given full power to invest in stocks, gold, and real estate and even take equity stakes in different companies. Nogara decided to select the best investment advisers in a number of foreign financial centers and was perhaps swayed by his friendship with Giovanni Fummi. In New York, he chose J. P. Morgan and Company; in London, Morgan Grenfell; in Paris, Morgan et Compagnie (the new name given to the Paris house in the late 1920s); in Holland, Mees and Hope; in Sweden, the Wallenbergs’ Enskilda Bank of Stockholm; and the Union Bank of Switzerland.
教皇委托诺加拉创立圣座特别行政署，并将意大利国债转换为多元化的股票投资组合。这项操作高度机密，每年仅出具一份报告。诺加拉亲手将报告呈递教皇，教皇审阅后便锁入私人保险柜。庇护十一世对诺加拉的投资未设任何限制，这位银行家被授予全权，可投资股票、黄金、房地产，甚至收购不同公司的股权。诺加拉决定在多个海外金融中心遴选顶级投资顾问，或许也受其与乔瓦尼·富米的私交影响。他在纽约选择了摩根大通公司，伦敦的摩根建富，巴黎的摩根公司（1920 年代末巴黎分行更名后的称谓），荷兰的米斯霍普银行，瑞典的瓦伦堡家族旗下斯德哥尔摩安斯基尔达银行，以及瑞士联合银行。

The Vatican would be very grateful for the investment advice provided by the House of Morgan. Jack Morgan, who had once lobbied to keep a Catholic off the Harvard board, would become a favorite in Vatican City. For investment advice, Pope Pius XI would confer upon both Jack and Tom Lamont the Grand Cross of Saint Gregory the Great. As an account of both Morgan Grenfell’s and J. P. Morgan’s, the Vatican is an important explanation of some of the alacrity with which Lamont performed services for Mussolini. After all, the devil’s work was now sprinkled with holy water.
梵蒂冈对摩根家族提供的投资建议感激不尽。杰克·摩根曾游说反对天主教徒进入哈佛董事会，却成了梵蒂冈的座上宾。为表谢意，教皇庇护十一世授予杰克和汤姆·拉蒙特圣格里高利大十字勋章。梵蒂冈作为摩根建富银行和 J.P.摩根银行的客户，很好地解释了为何拉蒙特如此积极地替墨索里尼效力——毕竟，魔鬼的勾当如今已洒上了圣水。
