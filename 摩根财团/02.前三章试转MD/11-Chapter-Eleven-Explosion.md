# Chapter Eleven: Explosion

THE United States emerged from the First World War with thriving industries and a record trade surplus, while much of Europe lay in ruins, urgently in need of reconstruction loans. Sovereign states, city governments, and corporations flocked to Wall Street, as they had once courted London’s merchant princes. Because of sterling’s postwar weakness, the British Treasury had to impose an informal embargo on all foreign loans in the City, leaving wide open the door to traditional British clients. London had surrendered its historic role of financing world trade.
美国在第一次世界大战后工业蓬勃发展，贸易顺差创下纪录，而欧洲大部分地区却沦为废墟，急需重建贷款。主权国家、市政府和公司纷纷涌向华尔街，就像它们曾讨好伦敦的商业巨头一样。由于战后英镑疲软，英国财政部不得不在伦敦金融城对所有外国贷款实施非正式禁令，从而为传统的英国客户敞开了大门。伦敦已放弃了其在全球贸易融资中的历史角色。

Sunning in postwar glory, the House of Morgan was the world’s most influential private bank, able to select the most creditworthy customers and alone capable of handling many huge state loans. Its seal of approval guaranteed a warm reception for bond issues at a time when foreign issues were still new and unfamiliar to American investors. The House of Morgan spoke to foreign governments as the official voice of the American capital markets. Its influence didn’t simply stem from money but from intangibles—cachet, political connections, and banking alliances.
沐浴在战后的荣光中，摩根财团是当时全球最具影响力的私人银行，既能筛选最优质的客户，也能独力承揽多笔巨额国家贷款。在美国投资者对海外证券尚感陌生之际，摩根财团的认证书便为债券发行铺平了道路。它代表美国资本市场，充当与外国政府对话的官方喉舌。其影响力不仅源于雄厚资金，更来自无形的资产——声望、政界人脉与银行同盟。

With the Jewish banks weakened, the Yankee axis of J. P. Morgan-National City Bank-First National Bank held the keys to the kingdom. For any credit-hungry finance minister, it was a formidable machinery to defy. In October 1919, Baron Emile du Marais, a member of a French financial mission, reported on Morgan power to French president Raymond Poincare: “I have the impression that Morgan’s has put together here a group which includes all the necessary elements for the placement of securities, and that one can in no way manage without their support. It is a fact about which we can do absolutely nothing. In these conditions, wisdom seems to dictate that we accept the fait accompli; and try to give Morgan’s the impression that we have full confidence in them.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268313) This analysis is reminiscent of Asquith’s fatalistic, wartime lament that Britain, willy-nilly, had to reckon with the bank.
犹太银行家势力削弱后，J. P. 摩根-国民城市银行-第一国民银行组成的扬基轴心掌握了关键命脉。对于任何渴望信贷的财政部长而言，这都是一台难以违抗的庞大机器。1919 年 10 月，法国金融代表团成员埃米尔·杜·马雷男爵向法国总统雷蒙·普恩加莱报告摩根的影响力时写道：“我的印象是，摩根公司在这里组建了一个囊括证券发行所有必要环节的集团，没有他们的支持我们根本无法运作。面对这一事实我们完全无能为力。在这种形势下，明智之举似乎是接受既成事实，并让摩根公司相信我们对他们充分信任。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268313) 这番分析令人联想到阿斯奎斯在战时那句宿命论的哀叹——英国无论情愿与否，都不得不重视这家银行。

Nobody was more emboldened by the new financial power than President Wilson, who was eager to underwrite liberal dreams with Wall Street money. This was the same Woodrow Wilson who had made caustic comments about the Money Trust and snubbed Jack’s offer of the Export Department. In December 1918, he set sail for Europe and received a euphoric welcome. He was the man of the hour, and it was thought he could mediate among European powers while rehabilitating Belgium and northern France. At this critical juncture, a metamorphosis took place in the role of the banker. In Pierpont’s day, captains of finance had indulged an honest hatred of government. But after World War I, financial diplomacy shifted into a gray area between business and politics, with bankers often functioning as their governments’ ambassadors. The advent of the Diplomatic Age was most striking at the House of Morgan, which would evolve into a shadow government and move in tandem with official policy. There would be moments when it acted as a rogue ministry, pursuing its own secret agenda, but for the most part it faithfully followed Washington. As Jack later said, “We were always most scrupulous in our relations with our Government.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268498)
威尔逊总统比任何人更因这股新的金融力量而底气十足，他迫切渴望用华尔街的资金来支撑自由主义梦想。正是这位伍德罗·威尔逊，曾对金钱信托发表尖刻评论，并拒绝了杰克关于出口部门的提议。1918 年 12 月，他启航前往欧洲，受到了狂热的欢迎。他是当时的风云人物，人们认为他能在重建比利时和法国北部的同时，调解欧洲列强间的矛盾。在此关键时刻，银行家的角色发生了蜕变。在皮尔庞特时代，金融巨头们对政府抱有一种纯粹的反感。但第一次世界大战后，金融外交滑入了商业与政治之间的灰色地带，银行家们常常充当各自政府的大使。外交时代的到来在摩根大通最为显著，这家机构逐渐演变为一个影子政府，与官方政策步调一致。 它有时会像一个自行其是的部门，暗中追求自己的秘密议程，但大多数时候还是忠实地追随华盛顿。正如 Jack 后来说的，“我们与美国政府的关系总是极其审慎。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268498)

During this period, Tom Lamont acquired his keen interest in foreign affairs. In 1917, he was already traveling with Colonel House to Europe to study the European situation. Then Treasury Secretary Carter Glass appointed him as a financial adviser to the U.S. delegation at the Paris Peace Conference. Lamont was horrified by a wartime visit to Flanders and remembered the battlefield as a “Dantaesque inferno,” with fires erupting from smoking artillery.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268681) The experience made him a convinced advocate of world peace organizations. He developed an ardent faith in Wilson’s vision of a League of Nations and funneled large amounts of money into organizations supporting American entry into the League.
在此期间，Tom Lamont 对外交事务产生了浓厚兴趣。1917 年，他就已随 Colonel House 前往欧洲研究欧洲局势。随后，财政部长 Carter Glass 任命他为美国代表团在 Paris Peace Conference 的财务顾问。Lamont 对战时访问 Flanders 的经历深感震惊，将战场铭记为“Dantaesque inferno”，烟火从炮火中喷涌而出。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268681) 这段经历使他成为世界和平组织的坚定倡导者。他对 Wilson 的国际联盟愿景怀有热忱信仰，并投入大量资金支持推动美国加入国联的组织。

Lamont’s political beliefs dovetailed with the Morgan bank’s financial requirements, for as it expanded its foreign lending, it looked for stable governments, global security, and free trade. The late 1910s would be the heyday of Morgan idealism. In those years, Dwight Morrow penned a brief study, entitled *The Society of Free States*, that examined how nations had negotiated their conflicts in the past. His daughter Anne later recalled, “The talk I heard around the family table in my school years was full of enthusiasm for Woodrow Wilson’s Fourteen Points: ’The right of self-determination’ for nations and ’a new order of world peace.’ ”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268848)
拉蒙特的政见与摩根银行的金融需求不谋而合——随着该行扩大海外贷款，它需要稳定的政府、全球安全与自由贸易。1910 年代末期正是摩根理想主义的全盛时期。那些年，德怀特·莫罗撰写了一篇题为《自由国家联盟》的短文，探讨各国历史上如何化解冲突。他的女儿安妮后来回忆道："我学生时代在餐桌上听到的谈话，充满了对伍德罗·威尔逊'十四点原则'的热忱：民族'自决权'与'世界和平新秩序'。" [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268848)

Against all expectations, the insouciant Lamont dazzled Wilson in Paris. Wilson told him, “I have more and more admired the liberal and public-spirited stand you have taken in all our counsels.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269018) A new Morgan partner, George Whitney, said that Wilson seemed to place more trust in Lamont’s financial judgment than in that of anybody else.[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269186) Indeed, Morgan men were so ubiquitous at the Paris Peace Conference in 1919 that Bernard Baruch grumbled that J. P. Morgan and Company was running the show.[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269375) It is worth stressing that it was a progressive Democratic president who first mobilized the new Wall Street power for political ends (although the exploitation would become more blatant under Wilson’s Republican successors). A decade of attacks on the Money Trust seemed to melt into a rapturous embrace.
出乎所有人意料，漫不经心的拉蒙特在巴黎让威尔逊刮目相看。威尔逊对他说：“你在我们的每一次商议中都展现出自由和公益精神，我对此越来越钦佩。”摩根的新合伙人乔治·惠特尼表示，威尔逊对拉蒙特财务判断的信任似乎超过任何人。确实，1919 年巴黎和会上摩根的人无处不在，以至于伯纳德·巴鲁克抱怨说，实际上是 J.P.摩根公司在操纵一切。值得强调的是，首先将华尔街的新权力用于政治目的的正是一位进步的民主党总统（尽管在威尔逊的共和党继任者手下，这种利用变得更加明目张胆）。对“金钱信托”长达十年的攻击，似乎融化在了热烈的拥抱之中。

Tom Lamont found his *métier* in Paris and helped to write the financial clauses of the peace treaty. He developed a vast circle of new friends, including Philip Kerr, later Lord Lothian and then secretary to Lloyd George and a close friend of Nancy Astor, and Jan Smuts of South Africa. Lamont would be the ace financial diplomat of the era. Where Jack Morgan was incapable of guile, Lamont was fast on his feet and ideologically flexible, able to hint to politicians of both parties that he sided with them. He was a man of many masks who played his parts so masterfully that he sometimes fooled himself. He had a gift for straddling political fences. To Wilson, in a typically artful formulation, he labeled himself “a poor Republican . . . who has faith in our present Democratic administration.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269574) His tolerance was sometimes indistinguishable from a lack of conviction and his open-mindedness sometimes had a dash of opportunism. On domestic economic issues, he was a conventional Republican. But he espoused enough liberal views on international organizations and civil liberties to make himself uniquely palatable to the Democratic intelligentsia, who marveled at this *rara avis* of Wall Street. By the end of his career, Lamont would count Herbert Hoover *and* Franklin Roosevelt among his close friends.
汤姆·拉蒙在巴黎找到了自己的事业方向，并协助起草了和平条约的金融条款。他结交了大批新朋友，包括菲利普·克尔（后来成为洛锡安勋爵，担任劳合·乔治的秘书，也是南希·阿斯特的密友），以及南非的扬·斯穆茨。拉蒙堪称那个时代的顶级金融外交家。杰克·摩根不会耍心眼，而拉蒙却反应敏捷、意识形态上灵活变通，能让两党政客都觉得他站在自己这边。他是个多面人，角色扮演得如此炉火纯青，有时连自己都骗过了。他天生擅长左右逢源。对威尔逊，他用了一招典型的巧妙措辞，称自己是“一个可怜的共和党人……却对现任民主党政府充满信心。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269574) 他的宽容有时与缺乏信念无异，而他的开放心态也时常带点机会主义色彩。在国内经济问题上，他则是一个传统的共和党人。 然而，他在国际组织和公民自由问题上秉持的足够自由的观点，使他成为民主党知识分子眼中独一无二的人物——华尔街里的稀有珍禽（rara avis），令他们惊叹不已。到职业生涯末期，拉蒙特已将赫伯特·胡佛和富兰克林·罗斯福视为自己的密友。

For a generation, Lamont and the House of Morgan were entangled by the Treaty of Versailles and the problem of German reparations. It was a quagmire from which they could never escape. At the peace conference, Lamont joined a subcommittee studying Germany’s capacity to pay war reparations to the Allies. Since much of the war was fought on French soil—northern France was left a lunar landscape of bomb craters—the French were implacable about receiving massive compensation. They had paid reparations to Germany in 1819 and 1871 and wanted their pound of flesh. Compared with the vengeful Allies, Lamont was less hawkish and recommended that Germany pay \$40 billion—only one-fifth the French request and one-third the British, but still quite substantial and the highest among the American advisers.
整整一代人的时间里，拉蒙特与摩根家族都被《凡尔赛条约》和德国赔款问题牢牢捆住，陷入一个永远无法脱身的泥潭。在和平会议上，拉蒙特加入了一个专门研究德国向协约国支付战争赔款能力的小组委员会。由于战争大部分在法国领土上进行——法国北部被炸得满目疮痍，遍地弹坑——法国人铁了心要拿到巨额赔偿。他们在 1819 年和 1871 年曾向德国支付过赔款，这次非得连本带利讨回来不可。与满腔复仇情绪的协约国相比，拉蒙特的态度没那么强硬，他建议德国支付 400 亿美元——这仅是法国要求的五分之一、英国要求的三分之一，但数额依然相当可观，也是美国顾问中开出的最高价。

When the Reparations Commission set the burden at S32 billion, its magnitude shocked Ben Strong, who foresaw—prophetically—a weaker German mark and subsequent inflation. Yet Lamont would never renounce his belief that the reparations burden was quite tolerable and that John Maynard Keynes, in his famous polemic *The Economic Consequences of the Peace*, gave the Germans the impression that they had been penalized and thus only fostered their resentment and weakened their resolve to pay. This, he thought, paved the way for Hitler’s rise. Lamont belonged to the school of thought that saw the Germans manipulating world opinion into a better postwar financial deal than they deserved. Right through the Second World War, he clung to the belief that the Treaty of Versailles “was more than just to Germany and less than just to the allies.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269759)
当赔款委员会将数额定为 320 亿马克时，其规模之大震惊了本·斯特朗，他预言性地预见到德国马克将贬值并引发后续通胀。然而，拉蒙特始终坚信赔款负担完全可以承受，并认为约翰·梅纳德·凯恩斯在其著名论战著作《和平的经济后果》中，给德国人留下了他们受到惩罚的印象，只会助长其怨恨情绪，削弱其支付赔款的决心。他认为，这为希特勒的崛起铺平了道路。拉蒙特属于那种认为德国人在操纵世界舆论，以获取战后不合理的金融优惠的学派。直到二战期间，他仍坚持认为《凡尔赛条约》“对德国过于公平，而对盟国则有失公允”。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269759)

Whatever the truth of this complex historic debate, Lamont proved prescient in his forecast of lukewarm American support for the League. Sensing mounting isolationism at home, he asked Dwight Morrow to report to him from New York on sentiment toward the League. When he relayed Morrow’s pessimistic appraisals, Wilson either pooh-poohed them or seemed puzzled by American doubts. Lamont plied Wilson with memos advocating tactical alterations in the treaty, more consultation with Republican opponents, and even a Washington lobbying effort to pinpoint the position of the dissenting senators and build bipartisan support. Always sensitive to style, Lamont suggested more humor in Wilson’s speeches and recommended an “almost childlike language” in explaining the League covenant.[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269928) Wilson reacted to Morrow’s reports in a high-minded but myopic way. “The key to the whole matter is the truth,” he told Lamont, “and if we can only get the people at home to see the picture as we see it, I think the difficulties will melt away.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270144) By nature a creature of compromise, Lamont watched in horror as Wilson stuck rigidly to his beliefs. They had a last wistful trip across the Atlantic together. By November 1919, the Treaty of Versailles was dead in the Senate, and Wilson was a shattered man. The United States never joined the League of Nations.
无论这场复杂的历史辩论真相如何，拉蒙特对美国对国际联盟支持度偏冷的预测都堪称有先见之明。察觉到国内孤立主义情绪日益高涨，他请德怀特· Morrow 从纽约向他报告民众对国联的态度。当他把 Morrow 的悲观评估转达给威尔逊时，威尔逊要么不以为然，要么对美国人的疑虑感到困惑。拉蒙特不断向威尔逊提交备忘录，建议在条约上采取策略性调整、更多与共和党反对派协商，甚至提议在华盛顿进行游说，以摸清持异议参议员的立场并争取两党支持。拉蒙特一向注重风格，建议威尔逊在演讲中增加幽默感，并用“近乎童稚的语言”来解释国联盟约。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2269928) 威尔逊对 Morrow 报告的反应虽高尚却目光短浅。 “整件事的关键在于真相，”他对拉蒙特说，“只要我们能说服国内的人像我们一样看待局势，困难就会迎刃而解。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270144) 拉蒙特天生崇尚妥协，当他看到威尔逊固执己见时，不禁感到震惊。他们一起进行了最后一次满怀惆怅的横渡大西洋之旅。到 1919 年 11 月，《凡尔赛和约》在参议院宣告失败，威尔逊身心俱疲。美国始终未能加入国际联盟。

Versailles was a formative event for Tom Lamont, his debut on the world stage, from which he took away contradictory lessons. On the one hand, the peace conference left a residual strain of idealism, and Wilson would remain a sacred figure in his memory. He lauded Wilson’s “delightful personality” and “ready wit” and his “Scotch mixture of wonderful idealism and stubbornness.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270313) Yet he saw that politics was the art of the possible, that Wilson had suffered from excessive purity, and that the world wasn’t prepared for Utopia. Of Wilson, he said, “He was a curious character—a great man in so many ways, yet so apt to stand firm at the wrong moments and give in at the right ones. ”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270482) In time, Lamont’s own talent for compromise would become pronounced, so that his own political tragedy would be the reverse of Wilson’s.
凡尔赛和约对汤姆·拉蒙特而言是一次成长性的经历，是他登上世界舞台的首次亮相，他从中汲取了相互矛盾的教训。一方面，和会残留着理想主义的余晖，威尔逊在他记忆中始终是一位神圣人物。他称赞威尔逊“令人愉悦的个性”、“机智敏捷”，以及“苏格兰人特有的理想主义与固执的奇妙混合”。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270313) 然而他也看到政治是可能性的艺术，威尔逊因过于纯粹而受挫，世界尚未为乌托邦做好准备。谈及威尔逊时他说：“他是个古怪的人物——在许多方面堪称伟人，却又如此善于在错误时刻固执己见，在正确时刻妥协退让。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270482) 后来，拉蒙特自己的妥协天赋变得愈发突出，以至他个人的政治悲剧与威尔逊正好相反。

After returning to America, Lamont, imbued with the Wilsonian spirit, proudly hung pictures of the president and Colonel House over his desk at 23 Wall. He had recently become publisher of the *New York Evening Post*, and he departed from a policy of noninterference to insist that the paper adopt a pro-League of Nations stand. As America’s premier foreign lender, the House of Morgan also had a certain natural affinity with Wilson’s brand of liberal Democratic internationalism. While American industrialists often remained protectionist and provincial in their views, bankers became more cosmopolitan in the 1920s. Only through free trade could countries export and earn foreign exchange to retire their debts. As had happened in the City in the nineteenth century, Wall Street became far more outward-looking than the commercial deposit banks. As exponents of global cooperation, the House of Morgan would often feel uncomfortable with the isolationist Republicans.
回到美国后，深受威尔逊思想影响的拉蒙特在华尔街 23 号办公室的办公桌上方自豪地挂上了总统和豪斯上校的画像。他最近成为了《纽约晚邮报》的发行人，并一改不干预政策，坚持要求该报采取支持国际联盟的立场。作为美国首屈一指的外贷机构，摩根财团对威尔逊那种自由主义的民主国际主义自然也有几分亲近感。当美国实业家们往往仍持保护主义和地方观念时，银行家们在 20 世纪 20 年代却变得更加国际化了。只有通过自由贸易，各国才能出口商品、赚取外汇来偿还债务。正如 19 世纪发生在伦敦金融城那样，华尔街变得远比商业储蓄银行更加外向。作为全球合作的倡导者，摩根财团常常对孤立主义的共和党人感到不自在。

Returning home for the Republican National Convention of 1920, Lamont was shocked by its smoke-filled rooms, its arrogant isolationism, and its mean-spirited xenophobia. He saw America suddenly retreating from the world and refusing to take responsibility for postwar European reconstruction. In the election that year, Lamont cast his lone Democratic presidential vote, favoring Governor James M. Cox of Ohio over Warren G. Harding because Cox endorsed the League. Even Jack Morgan supported the League, although with even-handed disgust he boycotted America on Election Day, reviling both the “jellyfish Republican” and the “pro-German Ohio editor.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270674) While the bank would have intimate relations with the three Republican administrations of the 1920s, there would always be a tension between its sense of global responsibility and the blinkered vision of the provincial Republicans. Increasingly multinational in scope, the House of Morgan would fit uneasily into an America that was tired of European entanglements.
1920 年，拉蒙特返乡参加共和党全国代表大会时，烟雾弥漫的密室里充斥的傲慢孤立主义与狭隘排外情绪令他震惊。他目睹美国突然从世界舞台上抽身，拒绝为战后欧洲重建承担责任。当年大选中，拉蒙特将唯一一张民主党总统选票投给了支持国际联盟的俄亥俄州长詹姆斯·M·考克斯，而非沃伦·G·哈丁。就连杰克·摩根也支持国联，但他带着一碗水端平的厌恶在选举日抵制了美国投票，既唾骂"软体共和党人"，又痛斥"亲德俄亥俄编辑"。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270674) 尽管摩根银行与 1920 年代三届共和党政府关系密切，但其秉持的全球责任感与地方共和党人的短视始终存在张力。 越来越具有跨国性质的 House of Morgan，将难以融入一个厌倦了欧洲纠葛的美国。

WHILE Lamont was negotiating peace at Versailles, Jack was wrestling with his own private demons. He didn’t want to negotiate with the Germans but only to see them punished for their “barbarous” misdeeds. In 1917, he wrote a friend that “after the conduct of the Germans during this war, it would be impossible for any civilized nation . . . to have anything to do commercially or financially with people who have shown themselves of such evil character.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2270875) He said he would rather have General Pershing march on Berlin with half a million men than a merciful peace treaty.[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271079) Pierpont Morgan could have acted on such spite, but postwar Morgan lending would increasingly reflect U.S. interests rather than partners’ whims. Despite Jack’s bluster, his bank would sponsor the vast loans that made reparations possible, linking his bank more closely to Germany than he would have ever dreamed possible.
当拉蒙特在凡尔赛谈判和平时，杰克却在与自己内心的恶魔搏斗。他不想与德国人谈判，只想让他们为自己的“野蛮”行径受到惩罚。1917 年，他在给朋友的信中写道：“经过德国人在战争中的所作所为，任何文明国家……都不可能再与这些表现出如此邪恶品性的人有任何商业或金融往来。”他表示宁愿让潘兴将军率领五十万大军进军柏林，也不愿签署一份仁慈的和平条约。皮尔庞特·摩根本可以出于这种怨恨行事，但战后的摩根贷款越来越反映美国的利益，而非合作伙伴的一时之念。尽管杰克言辞激烈，他的银行却主导了那些使赔款成为可能的大规模贷款，这使得他的银行与德国的联系之紧密远超他曾经的想象。

Outwardly, Jack remained the sedate banker, but inside he was fearful and haunted. His insecurity didn’t end with the armistice. Even in the postwar atmosphere, it was easy for a prominent banker to feel as if he were a sitting target for terrorists. The rich grew alarmed by events in Russia—the seizure of power by Trotsky and Lenin, the assassination of Czar Nicholas II, and the Bolshevik repudiation of foreign debt. (Barings froze its large Russian deposits after the Bolsheviks tried to transfer them to Guaranty Trust in New York.) During the Mexican Revolution, the Mexican government also defaulted on its foreign debt, and in 1917 it passed a radical constitution that threatened to nationalize American oil interests.
表面上，杰克依旧是那个沉稳的银行家，但内心却充满恐惧与不安。这种不安全感并未随着战争结束而消散。即便在战后的氛围中，一位声名显赫的银行家也很容易感觉自己成了恐怖分子的活靶子。俄国发生的一系列事件让富人们惶恐不安——托洛茨基和列宁夺取政权、沙皇尼古拉二世遇刺、布尔什维克宣布废除外债。（布尔什维克试图将巴林银行在俄国的巨额存款转移到纽约的 Guaranty Trust，但未能成功。）墨西哥革命期间，墨西哥政府也对其外债违约，并于 1917 年通过了一部激进宪法，威胁要将美国石油利益国有化。

There were predictions that revolution would spread to the shores of North America, and the political air grew thick with talk of class warfare and strikes. During 1919, four million Americans went on strike, with the city of Seattle the scene of a major general strike. Attorney General A. Mitchell Palmer rooted out “Reds” and other foreign agitators in the raids that came to bear his name. The unrest strengthened Jack’s suspicion that “destructive elements” wanted to smash the industrial machinery. He applauded Massachusetts governor Calvin Coolidge for suppressing the Boston police strike and Judge Gary for supporting an open shop during a strike at U.S. Steel.
曾有预测认为革命将蔓延至北美大陆，政治空气中弥漫着阶级斗争与罢工的论调。1919 年期间，四百万美国人走上街头罢工，西雅图更爆发了一场大规模总罢工。司法部长 A·米切尔·帕尔默在其主导的多次突袭行动中铲除了“赤色分子”及其他外国煽动者，这些行动后来以他的名字命名。社会动荡加剧了杰克对“破坏分子”意欲摧毁工业机器的怀疑。他赞扬马萨诸塞州州长卡尔文·柯立芝镇压波士顿警察罢工，并为加里法官在美国钢铁公司罢工期间支持自由雇佣制而喝彩。

On May Day of 1919, Jack was one of twenty eminent Americans who received identical letter bombs. The intended victims were spared when the packages were intercepted at a New York City post office on account of insufficient postage. Jack and his daughter Jane were also blackmailed by a Michigan janitor named Thorn, who claimed he had poisoned them with slow-acting, secret microbes; he would hand over the antidote for \$22,000. Ordinarily, Jack might have shrugged this off, but in the tense atmosphere he thought it advisable to make an example of the blackmailer. Thorn was eventually arrested, convicted, and spent fifteen months in Leavenworth. By 1921, the bank felt so menaced by saboteurs that publicity chief Martin Egan suggested that the bank’s private railway car, the Peacock Point, be given a nondescript name, lest the association with Morgan partner Harry Davison’s North Shore estate invite trouble.
1919 年五一劳动节，杰克是收到相同邮件炸弹的二十位美国知名人士之一。这些包裹在纽约市邮局因邮资不足被截获，目标人物得以幸免。杰克和他的女儿简还遭到密歇根州一名叫索恩的门卫勒索，对方声称已用缓慢发作的隐秘微生物给他们下毒，并索要 22,000 美元以换取解毒剂。平时杰克或许会对此不予理会，但在紧张氛围下，他认为有必要杀一儆百。索恩最终被捕、定罪，在利文沃斯监狱服刑十五个月。到 1921 年，银行深感破坏分子威胁，公关主管马丁·伊根建议将银行私人列车"孔雀角"改为不起眼的名字，以免与摩根合伙人哈里·戴维森在北岸的庄园产生关联而招致麻烦。

The incidents that did occur help to explain the factual basis of Jack Morgan’s fears, which now drove him to obsessive lengths. Crazy things *were* happening around him. In addition, there was the 1920-21 recession, which was perhaps closer to a depression in its severity. To curb the inflation that followed the war, Ben Strong of the New York Fed raised interest rates sharply. It was the first recession deliberately engineered by the Fed to moderate a boom. As unemployment quintupled to 12 percent, four million people were thrown out of work, and over five hundred banks failed in 1921 alone.
所发生的事件有助于理解杰克·摩根恐惧心理的根源——如今这种恐惧已驱使他走向偏执。周遭荒诞之事层出不穷。此外，1920 至 1921 年的经济衰退，其严重程度或许更接近于大萧条。为遏制战后通胀，纽约联储的本·斯特朗大幅提高利率。这是美联储首次刻意制造经济衰退以抑制过热。随着失业率飙升五倍至 12%，四百万人失去工作，仅 1921 年一年就有超过五百家银行倒闭。

By early 1920, Jack Morgan had an almost inverted worldview: the rich struck him as impotent, the masses as all-powerful in the hands of demagogues. In this frightened state of mind, he hired a private detective, William Donovan, a lawyer and a highly decorated officer in World War I. (Later known as Wild Bill Donovan, he would head the Office of Strategic Services, the forerunner of the CIA.) Intelligence work burgeoned with the spread of radicalism around the world. Jack asked Donovan to investigate the Communist International (Comintern), formed in 1919, which had singled out bankers as archenemies of the working class. As a former banker to Czar Nicholas II, Jack was watching the Bolsheviks with extra apprehension. He also asked Donovan to dig up information about the new nations emerging from the old Austro-Hungarian Empire, for it was thought that political chaos in Central Europe could be a breeding ground for communism. Donovan’s investigation was fairly prosaic—he uncovered only dusty papers and speeches—but the job launched him in high finance and accustomed Jack to a new manner of dealing with his enemies.
到 1920 年初，杰克·摩根的世界观几乎完全颠倒：富人在他看来软弱无力，而民众在煽动家手中却变得无所不能。在这种惶恐不安的状态下，他雇佣了一名私家侦探——威廉·多诺万，此人既是律师，又是在一战中获得殊荣的军官。（后来被称为"疯狂的比尔·多诺万"，他将执掌战略情报局，即 CIA 的前身。）随着激进主义在全球蔓延，情报工作应运而生。杰克委托多诺万调查 1919 年成立的共产国际（Comintern），该组织已将银行家列为工人阶级的头号敌人。作为前沙皇尼古拉二世的银行家，杰克对布尔什维克格外警惕。他还要求多诺万搜集关于从奥匈帝国旧土中崛起的新兴国家的情报，因为人们认为中欧的政治混乱可能成为共产主义的温床。 唐诺文的调查相当平淡无奇——他翻出的不过是些积灰的文件和演讲稿——但这差事让他踏入了金融界的顶层，也让杰克逐渐习惯了对付敌人的新方式。

Two other events in 1920 contributed to Jack’s sense of omnipresent danger. On Sunday morning, April 18, an anarchist and escaped mental patient named Thomas W. Simpkin wandered into Saint George’s Church on Stuyvesant Square. The London-born Simpkin had been obsessed with death since the sinking of the *Titanic.* He later said he had come to America to kill Pierpont Morgan, only to discover he was already dead. On this Sunday morning, he was drawn to Saint George’s by its beautiful chimes. “The chimes were playing and I was soothed,” he said. “Then I went into the church.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271283) He knew that it was the church of the Morgan family.
1920 年还发生了另外两件事，让杰克感到危机四伏。4 月 18 日星期天清晨，一个名叫托马斯·W·辛普金的无政府主义者和精神病人逃出了医院，溜进了斯泰弗森特广场的圣乔治教堂。这位出生于伦敦的辛普金自从"泰坦尼克号"沉没后就一直对死亡着迷。他后来说，自己来美国本是为了刺杀皮尔庞特·摩根，结果发现摩根已经死了。这个星期天早上，他被圣乔治教堂优美的钟声吸引而来。"钟声响起，我感到很平静，"他说，"然后我走进了教堂。" [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271283) 他知道这是摩根家族的教堂。

Jack’s brother-in-law Herbert Satterlee was there, as was Dr. James Markoe, friend and physician to the Morgans. As Markoe was passing the collection plate, the dingy little Simpkin pulled out a gun and shot him point-blank in the forehead. The collection plate fell to the floor with a noise “like crashing glass.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271433) The rector, Karl Reiland, flung his Bible to the pulpit and leapt over the chancel rail. Although the organist stopped playing, the church choir continued to sing angelically as vestrymen in cutaways pursued Simpkin; they caught him in Stuyvesant Square. By coincidence, Dr. Markoe was rushed to the Lying-in Hospital, the hospital he had persuaded Pierpont to endow, and died there a few minutes later. As it turned out, Simpkin had mistaken Dr. Markoe for Jack Morgan. When interrogators asked him why he thought of killing J. P. Morgan, Jr., Simpkin replied that he had heard that Morgan and a Congressman Miller had said that the International Workers of the World ought to be killed.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271559)
杰克的内兄赫伯特·萨特利当时也在场，同来的还有摩根家的朋友兼家庭医生詹姆斯·马科。马科正在传递募捐盘时，邋遢的小个子辛普金突然掏出手枪，近距离朝他的额头开了一枪。募捐盘哐当一声掉在地上，发出"如同玻璃碎裂"的巨响。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271433) 教区长卡尔·赖兰猛地将《圣经》摔在讲坛上，翻过圣坛栏杆一跃而下。尽管管风琴师停止了演奏，教堂唱诗班依然天使般继续吟唱，穿着燕尾服的教区委员们则追捕着辛普金——最终在斯图文森特广场将他擒获。巧合的是，马科医生被紧急送往他当年说服皮尔庞特捐资建立的产科医院，几分钟后便在那里去世。事后查明，辛普金误将马科医生当成了杰克·摩根。当审讯人员问他为何想要杀害小 J.P.摩根时...辛普金回应说，他曾听闻摩根和一位名叫米勒的国会议员扬言，世界产业工人组织（International Workers of the World）就该被赶尽杀绝。 [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271559)

Then came the blast of September 16, 1920. Shortly after noon, a horse-drawn wagon carrying five hundred pounds of iron sash weights pulled up on Wall Street between Morgans and the U.S. Assay Office across the street. Suddenly it exploded, blowing holes in the pavement, bursting like shrapnel through a terrified lunchtime crowd, killing thirty-eight people and injuring three hundred. Walking by 23 Wall, the young Joseph P. Kennedy was hurled to the ground. Throughout a half-mile radius, the blast punched out windows, including those on the Wall Street side of Morgans. Fire and a weird greenish smoke belched upward, igniting awnings as high as twelve stories above the street. Inside the New York Stock Exchange, panicked traders fled the imploding windows as shattered glass burst through the heavy silk curtains.
紧接着，1920 年 9 月 16 日的爆炸声轰然响起。正午刚过，一辆载着五百磅铁制窗框配重的马车，停在华尔街摩根大厦对面、美国化验局门口。霎时间，马车爆炸，路面被炸出一个个窟窿，弹片如榴霰弹般射向惊恐的午餐人群，三十八人丧生，三百人受伤。年轻的约瑟夫·P·肯尼迪正路过 23 号华尔街，被气浪掀翻在地。半英里范围内，爆炸震碎了无数玻璃窗，连摩根大厦临华尔街一侧的窗户也未能幸免。火焰与诡异的绿色烟雾冲天而起，连街头十二层楼高的遮阳篷都被点燃。纽约证券交易所内，交易员们惊慌失措地逃离震碎的窗户，碎裂的玻璃冲破厚重的丝绸窗帘，四散飞溅。

In *Once in Golconda*, John Brooks describes the chaos inside the House of Morgan:
在《Once in Golconda》一书中，约翰·布鲁克斯描述了摩根大通内部的混乱场景：

> The cavernous interior of J.P. Morgan & Company, the office most seriously affected, was a shambles of broken glass, knocked-over desks, scattered papers, and the twisted remains of some steel-wire screens that the firm had providentially installed over its windows not long before, and that undoubtedly prevented far worse carnage than actually took place. One Morgan employee was dead, another would die of his wounds the next day, and dozens more were seriously injured. Junius Morgan \[Jack’s older son\], sitting at his desk near the north windows on the ground floor, had been pitched forward by the blast and then nicked by falling glass. . . . Another young Morgan man, William Ewing, was knocked unconscious, and awoke a few minutes later to find his head wedged in a wastebasket.[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271685)
> J.P. Morgan & Company 那洞穴般的大厅——受影响最严重的办公室——一片狼藉：碎玻璃、掀翻的桌子、散落的文件，以及几扇钢纱窗扭曲的残骸——这些纱窗是公司不久前有先见之明地安装在窗户上的，无疑避免了比实际发生的更惨重的伤亡。一名摩根员工当场死亡，另一人次日因伤重不治，还有数十人受重伤。Junius Morgan \[杰克的长子\] 坐在一楼北窗附近的办公桌前，被爆炸的气浪猛地向前推去，随后被掉落的玻璃划伤……另一名年轻的摩根员工 William Ewing 被震昏，几分钟后醒来时，发现自己的头卡在了废纸篓里。 [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271685)

The blast left glass strewn thick as sugar across the main banking floor. Bill Joyce, seated on a high stool, was killed by an iron sash lodged in his body; John Donahue died of burn wounds. A row of pockmarks was engraved deep in the Tennessee marble on the Wall Street side of the building. Whether as a badge of pride or a memorial to its two dead employees, the Morgan bank has never repaired the marble blocks, and they are still clearly visible to pedestrians on Wall Street. One partner later cited the inordinate expense of repairing them but then conceded, “It is right and proper that they should stay there.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271846) For a generation, bankers asked, Where were *you* when the blast occurred?
爆炸过后，主银行大厅的地面上铺满了厚厚一层碎玻璃，像糖粒一样密布。坐在高脚凳上的比尔·乔伊斯被飞来的铁窗框击穿身体而身亡，约翰·多纳休则因烧伤致死。建筑靠华尔街一侧的田纳西大理石墙面上，深深嵌着一排弹坑。不知是作为自豪的徽章，还是对两名遇难员工的悼念，摩根银行从未修复过那些大理石砌块，至今华尔街上的行人仍能清晰看到。一位合伙人曾以修复费用过高为由，但随后又坦言："它们留在那里，理所应当。"整整一代银行从业者都会问：爆炸发生时你在哪里？

Because the blast occurred in September, Jack was at his Scottish shooting lodge. But other partners were gathered in his office at the time, which, luckily, faced Broad Street. A visiting Frenchman, laughing nervously, said he felt as if he were back in the war. To inspect the carnage, George Whitney went into the street. On the bank’s scarred north wall, he saw a macabre sight: “One of those scars had a woman’s head and hat plastered up against it. I’ll always remember that. It hit her so hard that it just took her head off and it stuck right on the wall.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2271980)
由于爆炸发生在九月，杰克当时正在他位于苏格兰的狩猎小屋。但其他合伙人当时聚集在他的办公室里——幸运的是，这间办公室正对着宽街。一位来访的法国人紧张地笑着，说他感觉自己仿佛又回到了战场上。为了查看伤亡情况，乔治·惠特尼走到街上。在银行那面伤痕累累的北墙上，他看到了一幅令人毛骨悚然的景象：“其中一处裂痕上粘着一个女人和她的帽子。我永远忘不了那一幕。爆炸的冲击太猛烈了，直接把她的头削掉，然后紧紧贴在了墙上。”

In another memory from that dreamlike day with its montage of slow-motion horrors, Whitney recalled that Dwight Morrow, a man of legendary absentmindedness, had a noon luncheon appointment with a government official. As the smoke cleared, Whitney saw Morrow trotting punctually downstairs and greeting the official as if it were an ordinary business day. The two strolled off for lunch at the Bankers Club, threading their way among dead bodies, firemen, overturned cars, and craters gouged in the street. “They didn’t pay any attention to it, not knowing what they were doing 1 suppose,” said Whitney.[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272116)
在那个如梦似幻的日子，伴随着一系列慢动作般的恐怖片段，惠特尼回忆起另一件事：以心不在焉著称的德怀特·莫罗，中午约好与一位政府官员共进午餐。烟雾散去时，惠特尼看到莫罗准时小跑下楼，像平常工作日一样招呼那位官员。两人一起走向银行家俱乐部吃午餐，穿梭在尸体、消防员、翻倒的汽车和街面的弹坑之间。“他们根本没在意，大概是不知道自己在干什么吧。”惠特尼说。 [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272116)

In the weeks ahead, J. P. Morgan and Company muddled through, with canvas sheets draped over its windows and a shaky dome, propped up by scaffolding, above the central banking floor. For this most foppish bank, it was a strange interlude, with many employees wearing slings and bandages to work. Whether Morgans or the Assay Office was the real target of the blast was never known; it went down as a great unsolved crime. It may have been a spontaneous chemical accident, although it coincided with a rash of anarchist acts and has always been attributed to anarchists. The new Stock Exchange building at 11 Wall Street was under construction at the time, which may have accounted for there being explosives in the area. The bank hired the Burns International Detective Agency, which offered a \$50,000 reward for information about the incident; nobody ever collected the money.
在接下来的几周里，J. P. Morgan and Company 勉强维持着运营，窗户上挂着帆布帘，银行大厅上方的穹顶摇摇欲坠，靠脚手架支撑着。对于这家最讲究的银行来说，这是一段奇怪的插曲——许多员工带着吊带和绷带上班。爆炸的真正目标究竟是摩根公司还是 Assay Office，始终无人知晓；这成了一桩悬而未决的大案。尽管当时恰逢无政府主义行动频发，且一直被认为与无政府主义者有关，但也可能只是一场自发的化学事故。当时华尔街 11 号的新证券交易所大楼正在施工，这可能解释了该地区为何会有爆炸物。银行聘请了 Burns International Detective Agency，并悬赏 5 万美元征集线索，但始终无人领取这笔赏金。

As soon as the explosion occurred, thirty private detectives took up positions around Jack’s brownstone on Madison Avenue. Jack construed the explosion as an attack against Wall Street rather than an attack against the bank. Yet along with the 1915 shooting, the Thorn case, the Markoe shooting, and the million and one crank letters, it must have fed his sense of vulnerability and his growing apprehension of conspiracies.
爆炸一发生，三十名私家侦探便迅速在麦迪逊大道上杰克那栋褐石住宅周围就位。杰克认为这次爆炸是针对华尔街的袭击，而非针对银行。然而，结合 1915 年的枪击事件、索恩案、马科枪击案以及数不清的匿名恐吓信，这件事无疑加剧了他的不安全感，也加深了他对阴谋的日益恐惧。

This period of turbulence provides the backdrop for Jack’s deepening anti-Semitism, which played an important part in his outlook and became his shorthand explanation of many incidents, particularly attacks against his family and firm. His anti-Semitism was of a familiar variety. He saw Jews as a global fifth column feigning loyalty to host governments while furtively advancing foreign plots. He generalized the presence of the German-Jewish banks on Wall Street into a broader phenomenon. Like his father, Jack was extremely warm and affectionate toward those within his own circle of intimates, but, again like Pierpont, he often showed coldness and suspicion toward outsiders. In his anti-Semitism, Jack never saw himself as lashing out at the weak; instead, his enemies were more powerful than he, a mere Morgan, and deserved what they got.
这段动荡时期加深了杰克的反犹太主义倾向，这一观念在他的世界观中占据重要地位，并成为他解释许多事件的惯用理由，尤其是针对其家族和公司的攻击。他的反犹太主义属于常见的那一类。他将犹太人视为全球性的第五纵队，假装效忠所在国政府，暗中却推进外国阴谋。他将华尔街德裔犹太银行的存在泛化为一种普遍现象。与父亲一样，杰克对亲密圈内的人极为热情友善，但正如皮尔庞特，他对外人往往表现出冷淡和怀疑。在他的反犹太主义中，杰克从未觉得自己是在攻击弱者；相反，他认为他的敌人比他这个小小的摩根更强大，他们遭受的一切都是咎由自取。

In May 1920, serving as an overseer of Harvard University, he rushed to alert President A. Lawrence Lowell of the grave danger posed by a board vacancy:
1920 年 5 月，时任哈佛大学督学委员会成员的他，急忙向校长 A. Lawrence Lowell 发出警告，指出董事会席位空缺可能带来的严重隐患：

> I think I ought to say that I believe there is a strong feeling among the Overseers that the nominee should by no means be a Jew or a Roman Catholic, although, naturally, the feeling in regard to the latter is less than in regard to the former. I am afraid you will think we are a narrow-minded lot, but I would base my personal objection to each of these two for that position on the fact that in both cases there is acknowledgement of interests or political control beyond and, in the minds of these people, superior to the Government of this country—the Jew is always a Jew first and an American second, and the Roman Catholic, 1 fear, too often a Papist first and an American second.[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272250)
> 我觉得有必要说明，据我所知，督学委员会内部普遍认为，候选人绝不能是犹太人或者罗马天主教徒——当然，对后者的抵触情绪比前者稍弱一些。恐怕您会觉得我们狭隘，但我个人反对这两个群体担任此职的理由在于：他们都承认存在某种超越美国政府的利益或政治控制——在犹太人心中，自己首先是犹太人，其次才是美国人；而罗马天主教徒，我担心，他们在很多时候首先是教皇的追随者，其次才是美国人。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272250)

From this letter, one may discern that Jack had in mind his wartime feud with Kuhn, Loeb, now blown up into a universal theory. It’s ironic that he would soon float the biggest German loan in American history and later be decorated by the Vatican for his investment advice.
从这封信中可以看出，杰克对当年与 Kuhn, Loeb 之间的战时恩怨始终耿耿于怀，如今竟被放大成一套普世理论。讽刺的是，他很快就将发行美国历史上最大一笔德国债券，后来还因投资建议获得了梵蒂冈的嘉奖。

In 1920, convinced of an anti-Morgan cabal among the German-Jewish bankers, Jack recruited a man named Charles Blumenthal to infiltrate their activities. For two years, Blumenthal reported to Jack periodically. His methods have not been documented, but one target was clearly Samuel Untermyer, whom Jack still planned to punish for his role in the Pujo hearings. Another was the German-born Otto Kahn, the Kuhn, Loeb partner and financial angel behind the Metropolitan Opera. Far more than Jacob Schiff, the ostentatious Kahn mingled with tony society, earning the nickname of the Flyleaf between the Old and the New Testaments.[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272451) Kahn had subscribed generously to the 1915 Anglo-French loan, and Jack had even praised his patriotic wartime speeches, which were widely circulated by the Allies. Kahn had even been reviled by the kaiser as a traitor to his native country. Then, in 1919, Jack learned about a small loan to several German cities made by Kahn and Kuhn, Loeb early in the war. Kahn was still a naturalized British citizen, and Jack thought the loan *prima facie* evidence of treason. Hopping mad, he wrote Grenfell, “Great Britain cannot shut him in gaol, he now being an American citizen, but it does not strike me as being high-class conduct, and I think it should be known.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272622) Kahn’s wartime patriotism was forgotten.
1920 年，杰克深信德裔犹太银行家之间存在一个反摩根阴谋，便招募了一个名叫查尔斯·布卢门撒尔的人潜入他们的活动。两年间，布卢门撒尔定期向杰克汇报。他的具体手法未见记载，但其中一个目标显然是塞缪尔·昂特迈耶——杰克仍想为他在普约听证会上的角色惩罚他。另一个目标是德裔的奥托·卡恩，库恩-勒布公司的合伙人，大都会歌剧院的幕后金主。卡恩远比起雅各布·希夫更为张扬，跻身上流社会，赢得了“新旧约之间的扉页”的绰号。卡恩慷慨认购了 1915 年的英法贷款，杰克甚至曾赞赏他那被协约国广泛传播的爱国战时演讲。卡恩甚至被德皇斥为祖国的叛徒。到了 1919 年，杰克得知卡恩和库恩-勒布公司在战争初期向几个德国城市提供了一笔小额贷款。 卡恩当时仍是归化英国公民，杰克认为这笔贷款是叛国的初步证据。他气急败坏地写信给格伦费尔：“大不列颠无法把他关进监狱，因为他现在是美国公民，但在我看来这绝非高尚行为，我认为此事应当公之于众。”” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272622) 卡恩的战时爱国之举就此被遗忘。

Pursuing his quarry, Jack sought proof linking Kahn with the German loan. He apparently got it from Blumenthal in 1920. He wrote Grenfell, “Enclosed is a photographic copy of a letter from Lindheim, who is a Jewish lawyer here in New York with 50 connections with the Untermyer tribe, to Dr. Albert, which, I think, quite sufficiently identifies Mr. Otto Kahn with the proposed German cities loan.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272823) It seems both Jack and Teddy Grenfell were swapping intelligence with British authorities, because Grenfell already knew Dr. Albert had spent a lot of German money during the early stages of the war.[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273025) A couple of years later, the bank got a London source to consult Admiralty records on Samuel Untermyer.
杰克追寻着猎物，试图找到卡恩与德国贷款有关的证据。显然，他在 1920 年从布卢门撒尔那里得到了线索。他写信给格伦费尔说：“随信附上一封林德海姆写给阿尔伯特博士的信件照片。林德海姆是纽约的一名犹太律师，与昂特迈耶家族有 50 条联系渠道。我认为这封信足以证明奥托·卡恩先生与拟议的德国城市贷款有关。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2272823) 看来杰克和泰迪·格伦费尔都在与英国当局交换情报，因为格伦费尔早已得知阿尔伯特博士在战争初期大量动用了德国资金。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273025) 几年后，这家银行通过伦敦的消息源查阅了海军部关于塞缪尔·昂特迈耶的记录。

Another possible source of Jack’s information was Henry Ford’s *Dearborn Independent*, which served as mouthpiece for Ford’s bizarre anti-Semitic views and was distributed through Ford dealerships around the country. In 1921, the paper conducted a campaign against “hyphenated Americans”—immigrants of allegedly dubious loyalty to the country. In a warmly fraternal note to the editor, Jack endorsed the campaign: “Owing to the war, I became fully aware of the danger, to the community, of hyphenated Americans; and it seemed to me that the Jews were the only lot of that class of people who had been able to do their work quietly and were steadily working to maintain their hyphenated attitude of mind without calling public attention to it.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273225) Jack said he would make information available to the *Independent.* When Charles Blumenthal traveled to Detroit to consult with Henry Ford on the Jewish menace, Jack followed up with a note inviting Ford to visit him in New York.
杰克信息的另一个可能来源是亨利·福特的《迪尔伯恩独立报》，该报是福特荒谬反犹观点的喉舌，并通过全国各地的福特经销商发行。1921 年，该报发起了一场反对“带连字符的美国人”的运动——这些移民据称对国家的忠诚度存疑。杰克在给编辑的一封充满兄弟情谊的信中支持了这场运动：“由于战争，我充分意识到带连字符的美国人对社会的危害；在我看来，犹太人是这类人中唯一能默默行事、并持续保持那种带连字符的心态而不引起公众注意的群体。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273225) 杰克表示他愿意向《独立报》提供信息。当查尔斯·布卢门撒尔前往底特律与亨利·福特商讨犹太人的威胁时，杰克随后写信邀请福特到纽约拜访他。

Jack’s confused anti-Semitism was intermingled with business rivalries. The Yankee and Jewish banks still formed warring groups on Wall Street. In 1921, a former Justice Department agent tipped off the bank to a plan by Jewish bankers and German industrialists to restore German fortunes. He told how a Mr. Lehman and a Mr. Rothschild met with Kuhn, Loeb partners in New York to perfect this plot and how they hoped the new combine would drive J. P. Morgan and Company out of business. This may well have happened and been dressed up in alarming, conspiratorial language. Jack had a way of looking at the Yankee-Jewish rivalry on Wall Street and seeing it in conspiratorial and religious terms rather than in the more mundane terms of business.
杰克那混乱的反犹主义与商业竞争交织在一起。当时，扬基银行和犹太银行在华尔街依然各自为营，形成敌对阵营。1921 年，一名前司法部特工向银行透露了一个由犹太银行家和德国实业家策划的恢复德国财力的计划。他描述了某位雷曼先生和某位罗斯柴尔德先生如何在纽约与库恩-洛布公司的合伙人会面，以完善这一阴谋，并称他们希望这个新联合体能够击垮 J.P.摩根公司。这很可能确有其事，只是被渲染成了令人不安的阴谋论调。杰克看待华尔街扬基与犹太之争的方式，总是带着阴谋论和宗教色彩，而非更平常的商业视角。

Relations between Jack and Blumenthal soon deteriorated. Jack advanced him money for a home mortgage, and he failed to make a timely payment. For a banker such as Jack, deadbeats occupied a lower rung in hell than Jews. Relations grew frosty. In 1922, Blumenthal’s payments were phased out. Later, when Blumenthal tried to use Morgan’s name to raise cash, Jack denied he had ever employed the man. Was this pique—or Jack covering his tracks?
杰克和布卢门撒尔的关系很快就恶化了。杰克为他垫付了购房抵押贷款，但他未能按时还款。像杰克这样的银行家看来，赖账的人比犹太人在地狱里的地位还要低。两人的关系变得冷淡。1922 年，布卢门撒尔的款项被逐步停发。后来，当布卢门撒尔试图借用摩根的名义筹集资金时，杰克否认曾雇佣过此人。这究竟是出于恼怒，还是杰克在掩盖自己的行迹？

In any event, the hocus-pocus of German plots and Jewish bankers would soon seem silly and irrelevant. No Jew on Wall Street ever did as much for Germany as Jack Morgan would. Even as he terminated relations with Blumenthal in 1922, the State Department was urging him to sit on a committee of bankers who would outline the conditions needed for a massive German loan. After years of doggedly hunting German partisans, Jack Morgan would find himself Germany’s master banker. The phantom he had been pursuing all those years turned out to be himself.
无论如何，那些关于德国阴谋和犹太银行家的神秘把戏很快就会显得荒唐且无关紧要。华尔街没有哪个犹太人为德国做过杰克·摩根那样多的事。就在 1922 年他与布卢门撒尔断绝关系的同时，美国国务院正敦促他加入一个银行家委员会，该委员会将制定一笔巨额德国贷款所需的条件。经过多年锲而不舍地追查德国支持者后，杰克·摩根最终成了德国的首席银行家。他多年来一直追逐的那个幻影，原来就是他自己。

DURING the war, Jack had confounded critics who mocked him as a figurehead, a pale, plodding imitation of Pierpont Morgan. His British connections strengthened relations with the Allies, as did the partners he recruited for Morgan Grenfell. Having worked steadily in wartime, he continued to work eight or nine hours a day into the early 1920s. Yet he was a banker *malgré lui*, lacking the giant, locomotive energies that had propelled his father. As he readily acknowledged, he was a loafer, a studious amateur in the style of a British country squire. He loved gardening, yachting, reading detective fiction—activities of a mildly sedative nature. Once, in an indolent mood, he likened his brain to a soft, overboiled cauliflower. Also, he was haunted by his father’s breakdowns, illnesses, and death, which he associated with politics and overwork. So he was ready to rely on a strong lieutenant.
战争期间，杰克让那些嘲讽他是傀儡、不过是皮尔庞特·摩根苍白而呆板的仿冒品的人大跌眼镜。他在英国的人脉增强了他与协约国的关系，他为摩根建富招募的合伙人同样起到了作用。战争期间他工作勤勉，进入 20 世纪 20 年代早期后仍每天工作八九个小时。然而，他并非自愿成为银行家，缺少那种驱动他父亲的巨大而如同火车头般的精力。他坦然承认，自己是个闲散之人，不过是像英国乡绅那样用功的业余爱好者。他热爱园艺、游艇、阅读侦探小说——这些活动都有些许镇静功效。一次，在慵懒的情绪中，他将自己的大脑比作一团柔软、煮得过烂的花椰菜。此外，父亲的崩溃、疾病和死亡一直困扰着他，他认为这些都和政治与过度劳累有关。因此，他准备依赖一位得力的副手。

Jack was a great fan of Harry Davison, who seemed the clear favorite as Morgan overlord in the postwar period. Davison had natural authority; Paul Warburg of Kuhn, Loeb once said that “men enjoyed following him.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273404) His dedication to the bank was exemplary, as is attested by a cable that he sent to Nelson Aldrich after Pierpont died. Davison’s house, Peacock Point, had just burned to the ground and he would spend the summer on a houseboat while it was being rebuilt; he cabled Aldrich, “Loss of house mere incident in view of other crushing loss.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273555)
杰克是哈里·戴维森的超级粉丝，后者显然是战后摩根帝国领袖的热门人选。戴维森天生具有领导权威；库恩-洛布公司的保罗·沃伯格曾说，“人们乐于追随他。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273404) 他对银行的奉献堪称典范，皮尔庞特去世后他发给纳尔逊·奥尔德里奇的一封电报便是明证。戴维森的豪宅“孔雀角”刚刚被烧成平地，重建期间他打算住在船屋里消夏；他电告奥尔德里奇：“与那场毁灭性打击相比，失去房屋不过是小事一桩。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273555)

Davison’s standing was greatly enhanced by the war. As head of the Red Cross War Council, he was elevated in 1919 to the presidency of a global league of Red Cross societies; during his Red Cross tenure, eight million volunteers were signed up. Many stories testify to Davison’s massive self-confidence. At one Red Cross rally, he heard former president Taft say, “It gives me great pleasure to introduce to you one of our most distinguished citizens, a man who would rather face a German battery than an audience.” Davison was halfway out of his seat when Taft thundered, “General Pershing!”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273723)
战争极大地提升了戴维森的声望。作为红十字会战争委员会主席，他于 1919 年升任全球红十字会联盟主席；在红十字会任职期间，他招募了八百万志愿者。许多故事都证明了戴维森无比的自信。在一次红十字会集会上，他听到前总统塔夫脱说："我很荣幸向各位介绍我国最杰出的公民之一，一位宁愿面对德国炮兵也不愿面对听众的人。"戴维森正要从座位上站起来，塔夫脱却大声宣布："潘兴将军！" [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273723)

Another Davison story concerns a trip he made to London in 1918. Upon arriving, he was informed that King George V wished to see him. En route to Buckingham Palace, he was briefed on protocol by the king’s equerry and given a short list of royal taboos. He wasn’t to cross his legs, offer his hand first, or depart until dismissed by the king. Davison spent a pleasant hour with His Majesty, then, suddenly remembering another appointment, sprang to his feet—a breach of protocol. Who but a Morgan partner would be so blase about the king or not wish to prolong the experience? Buckingham Palace was just another stop on a busy itinerary; the House of Morgan had become aristocracy in its own right.
另一个关于戴维森的故事，说的是他在 1918 年去伦敦的事。一到那里，他就被告知国王乔治五世想见他。前往白金汉宫的路上，国王的侍从向他简要说明了礼仪规矩，并交代了几项王室禁忌：不能翘二郎腿，不能主动伸手，国王没示意离开就不能走。戴维森与陛下愉快地交谈了一个小时，然后突然想起还有个约会，猛地站起身来——这违反了礼仪。除了摩根合伙人，谁会对国王如此漫不经心，或者不希望能多待一会儿呢？白金汉宫不过是繁忙行程中的一站；摩根家族本身已经成了贵族。

After the war, Davison’s prestige was such that friends mentioned him as a possible presidential candidate. Davison himself demurred, saying it “could never be,” apparently because he had been involved in an extramarital affair that ended tragically in the spring of 1915.[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273886) He was afraid the story would be dredged up. It turns out to be a grisly one. Davison and his wife, Kate, were close friends of a couple named Boocock, who had been neighbors in Englewood, New Jersey. Howard Boocock was treasurer of the Astor Trust Company. Davison was having an affair with Adele Boocock, a close friend of his wife’s, and Howard Boocock was at first unaware of the liaison.
战后，戴维森的声望之高，以至于有朋友提及他可能成为总统候选人。戴维森本人却婉拒了，表示"绝无可能"，这显然是因为他曾卷入一场婚外情，而这段感情在 1915 年春天以悲剧告终。 [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2273886) 他担心这段往事会被重新翻出。事实证明，这确实是一桩令人毛骨悚然的事件。戴维森与妻子凯特同一位名叫布科克的夫妇关系密切，后者曾是他们在新泽西州恩格尔伍德的邻居。霍华德·布科克担任阿斯托信托公司的财务主管。戴维森与妻子的密友阿黛尔·布科克有染，而霍华德·布科克起初对此毫不知情。

When Howard Boocock did learn of the affair, he became deranged, although in a style appropriate to his position. On March 22, 1915, he came home from the bank early and rather agitated. Yet he and his wife dressed for dinner as usual at their East Seventy-fourth Street town-house, and the servants noticed nothing untoward during the meal. Afterward, Howard retired briefly to the upstairs library to read his newspaper, while Adele played the piano in the drawing room below. Then Howard joined her. The servants heard the piano music stop abruptly, followed by two pistol reports. When the terrified maids rushed into the room, they discovered that Howard had shot Adele behind the right ear with an old army revolver; then he had shot himself above the left eye. The first person the distraught servants thought to telephone was Adele Boocock’s best friend, Kate Davison. It fell to Kate to notify the Boococks’ relatives. Kate agreed to take in the two Boocock children—who had slept through the shooting—and the Davisons paid for their education as well. This action was reminiscent of the Davisons’ earlier generosity in taking in Ben Strong’s children. The double Boocock shooting was one of the sensational “unsolved” crimes of 1915. A coroner’s jury concluded that Howard Boocock went haywire from worry over the possibility that he was suffering from intestinal cancer. The truth would remain buried until the present.[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274037)
当霍华德·布库克最终得知这段婚外情时，他彻底崩溃了——尽管方式完全符合他的身份。1915 年 3 月 22 日，他提前从银行回家，情绪异常激动。然而，他和妻子还是照常在他们东七十四街的联排别墅里换好晚礼服，用餐时仆人们也没发现任何异样。饭后，霍华德上楼到书房看了会儿报纸，而阿黛尔则在楼下的客厅弹钢琴。随后，霍华德下楼来到她身边。仆人们突然听到琴声戛然而止，紧接着传来两声枪响。惊恐万分的女佣冲进房间，发现霍华德用一把旧式军用左轮手枪射中了阿黛尔右耳后方，然后朝自己左眼上方开了一枪。吓疯了的仆人们第一个想到打电话给的人是阿黛尔·布库克最好的朋友——凯特·戴维森。通知布库克家亲属的苦差事，就这样落在了凯特肩上。 凯特同意收养布库克家的两个孩子——他们在枪击发生时一直在睡觉——戴维森夫妇也为他们支付了教育费用。这一举动让人想起戴维森夫妇早前慷慨收养本·斯特朗孩子的往事。布库克枪击案是 1915 年轰动一时的"未解"犯罪案件之一。验尸陪审团最终认定，霍华德·布库克因担心自己患上肠癌而精神崩溃。真相直到今天才得以重见天日。 [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274037)

In 1920, when Harry Davison returned to the Corner from the Red Cross, he had lost his magnetic, buoyant charm. He complained of queer headaches and sleeplessness and took a year off with his family, which they spent at Magnolia Plantation, his estate in Thomasville, Georgia. A photograph of Davison on a picnic there shows him smoking a blunt cigar and wearing a white shirt and a dark three-piece suit; even in poor health and on a rustic outing, a Morgan man couldn’t let his image languish. But the stay didn’t put an end to his headaches and dizziness. In August 1921, Davison was diagnosed as having a brain tumor.
1920 年，当哈里·戴维森从红十字协会回到那个角落时，他已失去了往日那种迷人的活力与神采。他抱怨自己莫名头痛、失眠，于是带着家人休假一年，在佐治亚州托马斯维尔的木兰庄园度过。一张戴维森在那里野餐的照片里，他叼着一支粗雪茄，身着白衬衫和深色三件套西装——即便身体抱恙、置身乡野，摩根银行的人也不能让形象有丝毫懈怠。然而，这段休养并未终结他的头痛与眩晕。1921 年 8 月，戴维森被确诊患有脑瘤。

He was a manly type who refused to become an invalid. One day at Peacock Point—his sixty-acre Greek-columned estate on Long Island’s North Shore that nearly formed a continuous line of property with Jack Morgan’s and George Baker’s estates—he and Dr. Frederick Tilney were watching a school of porpoises in Oyster Bay. Tilney remarked that he had always wanted a porpoise brain for his research. “Bring me the elephant rifle and tell them to have the motor boat ready for us at once,” Davison ordered a servant.[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274211) Davison went out and shot his porpoise.
他是个硬汉，拒绝让自己变成一个病秧子。有一天，在 Peacock Point——他在长岛北岸那座占地六十英亩、矗立着希腊式廊柱的庄园，几乎与 Jack Morgan 和 George Baker 的产业连成一片——他和 Frederick Tilney 医生正在牡蛎湾观看一群海豚。Tilney 随口说自己一直想弄个海豚大脑做研究。“把大象步枪给我拿来，让他们立刻准备好摩托艇等我们，”Davison 对仆从吩咐道。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274211) Davison 出发射杀了那只海豚。

Harry Davison died in May 1922, at the age of fifty-four, during an operation to remove the tumor. He left an estate estimated at \$10 million, including \$4.5 million destined for his son, Frederick Trubee, who had been confined to a wheelchair since his college days. During a summer off from Yale during the war, Trubee and several classmates had formed the first naval reserve air unit, and Davison had bought his son a plane. As Trubee participated in a demonstration show at Peacock Point, the plane’s rear engine came loose and hit him in the head, leaving him a paraplegic. His father’s special bequest was meant to allow him to pursue a political career without any material distractions. Trubee became an assistant secretary of war for aviation in the Coolidge and Hoover administrations and served as president of the American Museum of Natural History. As indomitable as his father, in spite of his handicap he played tennis and shot big game for display in the museum.
哈里·戴维森于 1922 年 5 月去世，享年 54 岁，死因是切除肿瘤的手术。他留下的遗产估值约 1000 万美元，其中 450 万美元指定留给儿子弗雷德里克·特鲁比——这位年轻人自大学时代起便因意外而终身困于轮椅。一战期间耶鲁大学放暑假时，特鲁比与几位同学组建了第一支海军预备航空队，戴维森曾为儿子购置一架飞机。当特鲁比在孔雀角参加飞行表演时，飞机后引擎松脱击中他的头部，导致他下半身瘫痪。父亲这份特殊的遗产安排，意在让他免受物质困扰，全心投入政治生涯。特鲁比后来在柯立芝和胡佛政府中担任航空助理部长，并出任美国自然历史博物馆馆长。他继承了父亲不屈不挠的品格，尽管身有残疾，依然坚持打网球，还曾狩猎大型动物用以陈列于博物馆中。

At 23 Wall Street, Davison’s death left the path to power unobstructed, and Tom Lamont strolled into a leadership position. Deeply indebted to his mentor, Davison, Lamont perpetuated a Morgan tradition of building monuments to dead kings by writing a hagiographic biography of Davison. Of his other role model, Pierpont, Lamont wrote, “He was not interested in little matters, conducted or proposed by little men.” He viewed Pierpont’s reign as one of a vanishing gentility—“a kind of golden age of chivalry in affairs.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274374) This early exposure to Pierpont and Davison gave Lamont a vision of the banker as statesman and empire builder rather than as bureaucrat or paper pusher.
在华尔街 23 号，戴维森的离世扫清了通向权力之路的障碍，汤姆·拉蒙特轻松步入领导层。拉蒙特深感其恩师戴维森的栽培之恩，并延续了摩根家族为逝去君王树碑立传的传统，为戴维森撰写了一部充满溢美之词的传记。谈及他另一位榜样——皮尔庞特时，拉蒙特写道：“他对小人物操办或提议的琐事毫无兴趣。”他将皮尔庞特的执政时代视为一种已逝的高雅风范——“堪称商业领域中骑士精神的黄金时代”。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274374) 早期与皮尔庞特和戴维森的接触，让拉蒙特将银行家视为政治家与帝国缔造者，而非官僚或繁琐的文书员。

During the 1920s, Tom Lamont would be the brains of the Morgan bank and the most powerful man on Wall Street. When journalists talked of “prominent banking opinion,” they had usually been speaking with Lamont. A Wall Street saying held that “Mr. Morgan speaks to Lamont and Mr. Lamont speaks to the people.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274552) In his early banking days, Lamont had been deferential, even obsequious toward his elders, content to play the serviceable courtier. He always knew how to handle the Morgans. Both Pierpont and Jack were brooding loners who liked charming extroverts of an equable disposition. Pierpont had the sociable Bacon and Perkins; Jack, Davison and Lamont. Where the Morgan family was intensely private and domestic, these regents lent the bank a high-society gloss. And Lamont was perceptive enough to give Jack the confidence-bolstering praise that had been withheld by Pierpont.
在 20 世纪 20 年代，汤姆·拉蒙特是摩根银行的智囊，也是华尔街最具权势的人物。每当记者们提到“银行界权威意见”时，他们通常指的就是拉蒙特。华尔街流传着一句话：“摩根先生对拉蒙特说话，拉蒙特先生对大众说话。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274552) 在银行生涯早期，拉蒙特对待前辈总是谦卑有加，甚至近乎逢迎，甘愿扮演一位得力的朝臣角色。他始终懂得如何应对摩根家族的人。无论是皮尔庞特还是杰克，都是内向孤僻、喜欢性情温和且富有魅力的外向者。皮尔庞特身边有善于交际的培根和珀金斯；杰克则倚重戴维森和拉蒙特。摩根家族极度注重隐私、以家庭为中心，而这些代理人则给银行镀上了一层上流社会的光彩。拉蒙特敏锐地察觉到，若能给予杰克那些皮尔庞特从未给予的、能增强自信的赞美，便能赢得他的信任。

It is a mystery how Tom Lamont, a poor parson’s son, became everybody’s image of Wall Street elegance. The first Lamont came to America from Scotland in 1750. Lamont’s father was a former Greek professor and a Methodist parson (Tom would later become a Presbyterian). The senior Lamont had an Old Testament face—a broad, square forehead, full beard, and eyes that burned with severity. He banned dancing, cards, and even neighborhood Sabbath strolls for the family; Lamont’s mother, luckily, was gentler. Tom spent a thrifty boyhood in upstate Claverack, New York, plotting his escape and devouring novels. He attended both Phillips Exeter Academy and Harvard on scholarship. He admired, but wasn’t awed by, the wealthy boys he met. He was a completely self-invented figure and as such would be emblematic of an age based on wild speculation and a frothy optimism. Like Jay Gatsby, he lived in the manner of a poor boy acting out his most lavish fantasies. He was so successful at playing the aristocrat that he passed for the genuine article.
汤姆·拉蒙特，一位穷牧师的儿子，如何成为华尔街优雅风范的化身，这至今是个谜。第一位拉蒙特于 1750 年从苏格兰来到美洲。拉蒙特的父亲曾是希腊语教授，也是一位卫理公会牧师（汤姆后来改宗长老会）。老拉蒙特长着一张《旧约》式的面孔——宽阔方正的额头、浓密的胡须，以及严厉燃烧的双眼。他禁止家人跳舞、打牌，甚至禁止在安息日去邻里散步；幸运的是，拉蒙特的母亲要温和得多。汤姆在纽约州北部克拉弗拉克度过节俭的童年，谋划着逃离，并如饥似渴地阅读小说。他靠奖学金先后就读于菲利普斯埃克塞特学院和哈佛大学。他欣赏那些富家子弟，却不为他们所震慑。他是一个完全自我塑造的人物，也因此成为那个以疯狂投机和泡沫般乐观为特征的时代象征。如同杰伊·盖茨比，他活像一个穷小子在尽情演绎自己最奢华的幻想。 他扮演贵族如此成功，以至于被当成了货真价实的人物。

Short and slim with rounded shoulders, smiling eyes, and thinning hair, Lamont was often photographed before his office fireplace, hands in pockets, relaxed, and debonair. Usually he wore an amused, searching expression, as if inviting intimacy yet skeptically probing his guest. He looked at the world closely, as if sizing it up, taking the measure of someone in a glance. He seemed immune to depression, congenitally cheerful, and unflappably poised. His favorite expression was “easy does it” and his son, Corliss, said he never saw his father angry. He had a staggering capacity for work, and his voluminous papers at the Harvard Business School resemble the work of ten busy men. Tom Lamont was a prodigy—in business, finance, and diplomacy—and his career, dazzling in scope, would rival that of Pierpont Morgan himself.
拉蒙特身材矮小精瘦，圆肩，笑眼，发际线稀疏。他经常被拍成站在办公室壁炉前，双手插兜，神态轻松、温文尔雅。他通常带着一种饶有兴味、审视探究的表情，仿佛在邀人亲近，却又带着怀疑地打量着来客。他仔细观察世界，仿佛在估量一切，一眼就能看透一个人。他似乎从不会沮丧，天生乐观，镇定自若。他最爱说的口头禅是“慢慢来”，他的儿子科利斯说他从未见父亲发过火。他工作能力惊人，在哈佛商学院留下的卷帙浩繁的文件，简直像十个忙碌的人的作品。汤姆·拉蒙特是个天才——在商业、金融和外交领域——他的职业生涯光彩夺目，其广度足以与皮尔庞特·摩根本人媲美。

Lamont had a genius for friendship and was irresistible to the literary world. He was a newspaper publisher and a large shareholder in Crowell Publishing, the only Morgan partner drawn to that industry. When British poet John Masefield toured the United States during the war to generate sympathy for England, he became so attached to Lamont that he dedicated his *War and the Future* to him. Lamont also befriended Walter Lippmann, John Galsworthy, and H. G. Wells. He had a writer’s itch to record his thoughts and preserve them for posterity, dashing off hundreds of personal letters monthly.
拉蒙特天生具备交友的天赋，这让他在文学界备受青睐。他既是报社出版商，也是 Crowell Publishing 的大股东，是摩根合伙人中唯一涉足这一行业的人。英国诗人 John Masefield 在战争期间赴美巡游，为英国争取同情时，与拉蒙特结下了深厚情谊，甚至将他的《战争与未来》一书题献给了拉蒙特。拉蒙特还与 Walter Lippmann、John Galsworthy、H. G. Wells 等人成为好友。他怀揣着作家般的冲动，渴望记录下自己的思绪并留给后世，每月都会挥笔写下数百封私人信件。

His conviviality wasn’t restricted to celebrities. Each spring, he holed up with three old college chums in Atlantic City, where they fished, played bridge, and talked. He maintained hundreds of relationships—like juggler’s balls, he kept them magically aloft—and each acquaintance felt especially singled out by thoughtful gifts, cards, and invitations from 23 Wall.
他的热情好客并不局限于名人。每年春天，他都会与三位大学老友聚在大西洋城，一起钓鱼、打桥牌、畅谈人生。他维系着数百段人际关系——就像杂技演员抛接着球，他总能神奇地让这些关系保持活跃——而每一位相识之人都觉得自己受到了来自 23 Wall 的特别关照，无论是精心挑选的礼物、温馨的贺卡，还是诚挚的邀请。

If Tom Lamont assumed Morgan royalty with such ease, it had much to do with Wall Street’s extraordinary self-confidence in the twenties and the banker’s new diplomatic role. Lamont was a born politician and meshed exquisitely with his historical moment. In 1928, the Egyptian king said to him, “Mr. Lamont, I will wager I am the only head of a foreign state who has ever received you without asking for a loan for his government.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274714) He was probably correct. Later Lamont appeared on a list of sixty-three citizens who ruled America and would certainly have made far shorter lists. In 1937, Ferdinand Lundberg, the radical journalist, would say that Lamont “has exercised more power for 20 years in the western hemisphere, has put into effect more final decisions from which there has been no appeal, than any other person. Lamont, in short, has been the First Consul de facto in the invisible Directory of postwar high finance and politics, a man consulted by presidents, prime ministers, governors of central banks.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274880) Overheated prose aside, Lundberg erred in the right direction.
如果汤姆·拉蒙特能如此轻松地坐上摩根家族的“王座”，很大程度上要归功于二十年代华尔街非同寻常的自信心，以及银行家所扮演的新外交角色。拉蒙特天生就是位政治家，与他的时代完美契合。1928 年，埃及国王对他说：“拉蒙特先生，我敢打赌，我是唯一一位接见你却没有为自己的政府开口要贷款的外国元首。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274714) 他说的很可能没错。后来，拉蒙特出现在一份“统治美国的六十三位公民”名单上，而若是有更精炼的榜单，他必定名列前茅。1937 年，激进记者费迪南德·伦德伯格曾评价道：“二十年来，拉蒙特在西半球行使的权力、拍板生效的最终决定——那些不容上诉的决定——比任何其他人都要多。” 简而言之，拉蒙特（Lamont）一直是战后高层金融与政治无形“督政府”（Directory）中事实上的“第一执政”（First Consul），各国总统、总理、央行行长都要向他请教。” [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2274880) 撇开那些过热的辞藻不谈，朗德伯格（Lundberg）的错误方向倒也算歪打正着。

THAT Lamont had no ordinary dreams can be seen from a 1916 effort to induce Henry Ford to take his car company public. The move was not accomplished until 1956, after Ford’s death. The House of Morgan, with its large stake in railroads, had been myopic in recognizing the importance of the automobile industry, and Pierpont had rebuffed an early Ford request for financing. Then George Perkins lost a chance to finance General Motors in 1907, when he sneered at William Crapo Durant’s forecast that sales would soar to half a million automobiles per year and cars would someday outnumber horses on America’s streets. For turn-of-the-century Wall Street, cars were rich men’s toys, plagued by unreliability and poor roads. This attitude rankled Henry Ford and reinforced his contempt for Wall Street bankers.
拉蒙特绝非凡夫俗子，从 1916 年他试图推动亨利·福特将汽车公司上市便可见一斑。然而这一举动直到 1956 年福特去世后才得以实现。摩根大通因在铁路领域持有大量股份，对汽车工业的重要性缺乏远见，皮尔庞特还拒绝了福特早期的融资请求。随后在 1907 年，乔治·珀金斯因嘲笑威廉·克拉波·杜兰特关于汽车年销量将飙升至五十万辆、有朝一日美国街头汽车数量将超过马匹的预测，错失了资助通用汽车的机会。对于世纪之交的华尔街而言，汽车不过是富人的玩具，既不可靠又受困于糟糕的道路条件。这种态度激怒了亨利·福特，也加深了他对华尔街银行家的蔑视。

By 1916, the car companies had acquired new respectability on Wall Street. General Motors declared its first stock dividend—the largest in New York Stock Exchange history—and early skepticism turned to voguish enthusiasm. Henry Ford had introduced the assembly line in his Highland Park plant and in 1914 declared a \$5, eight-hour day for his workers—terms generous enough to draw twelve thousand job seekers. Ford now cranked out over half a million Model Ts annually, and Lamont saw the chance for a splashy deal in the Pierpont tradition. That the Senior’s ghost hovered in Lamont’s mind was clear from a letter he wrote to a Ford associate in which he stated that if Ford took his company public, there would be “nothing just like it since the Steel flotation 15 years ago.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275055) As a rule, Ford opposed public ownership and thought shareholders should work for the company. Nevertheless, he invited Lamont to unite the “best ideas” of J. P. Morgan and Ford. What blend of guile and geniality could tame Henry Ford?
到 1916 年，汽车公司在华尔街赢得了新的声望。通用汽车宣布了其首次股票分红——这是纽约证券交易所历史上最大的一笔——早先的怀疑态度转变为时髦的热情。亨利·福特在海兰帕克工厂引入了装配线，并在 1914 年宣布为工人实行 8 小时工作日、日薪 5 美元——如此慷慨的条件吸引了一万二千名求职者。福特如今每年生产超过五十万辆 T 型车，拉蒙特看到了像皮尔庞特传统那样做一桩轰动交易的机会。从拉蒙特写给福特合伙人的一封信中可以明显看出，老摩根的灵魂依然萦绕在他心头。他在信中写道，如果福特将其公司上市，“自 15 年前的钢铁股票发行以来，再无如此盛事。” [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275055) 通常来说，福特反对公有制，认为股东应当为公司工作。尽管如此，他仍邀请拉蒙特将 J. P. 摩根和福特的“最佳理念”结合起来。 要怎样兼具狡诈与和蔼，才能驯服亨利·福特？

In a memo, Lamont flattered but provoked Ford. He began: “You have the premier motor car industry of the country and of the world. . .. From nothing you and your associates have built it up to its present splendid proportions.” With Ford softened up, Lamont became shockingly blunt: “The present make-up of your company is your only weakness. So long as the control of the company rests absolutely in your hands, just so long is the future of the business dependent upon the life of one man. . . . There must . . . come to you moments of almost deep oppression for the responsibility that you have to carry day by day.” Having expressed sympathy, he stirred up anxiety, pointing to potentially troublesome minority shareholders. Then came the proposal itself, wrapped in a delicate tissue of jargon. Lamont suggested a “large financial operation” that might relieve Ford of burdensome responsibilities—in short, the first public offering of Ford stock.[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275243)
在一份备忘录中，拉蒙特既恭维了福特，又激将了他。他写道：“你拥有全国乃至全球最顶尖的汽车产业……你和你的团队从零开始，把它打造成了如今这般辉煌的规模。”待福特态度缓和后，拉蒙特的话锋突然变得尖锐起来：“你公司目前的架构是你唯一的弱点。只要公司的控制权完全掌握在你一人手中，企业的未来就始终系于你的一己之命……日复一日承担如此重任，想必你心中也常感压抑难当。”在表达同情之余，他又刻意制造焦虑，指出那些可能制造麻烦的少数股东。随后，拉蒙特用一套精心包装的行话提出了建议——他建议进行一次“大规模财务运作”，以减轻福特肩上的沉重负担：简而言之，就是首次公开发行福特股票。 [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275243)

In a second letter, Lamont drew a parallel between Ford’s sale of his company and Carnegie’s sale of his steelworks to U.S. Steel. Since Ford was an individualist of the Carnegie type, it was a smart analogy. Lamont proposed that Ford, like Carnegie, retain a substantial interest in the company, holding senior debt “of the highest character, insuring handsome and stable returns to you and your heirs, or nominees, for years to come”—Lamont liked this tony style with fancy clients. But once he had advanced his idea, he backed off and pretended to offer his idea impartially for Ford’s consideration. A few weeks later, Ford cordially acknowledged the letters, expressed interest, but let the matter lapse. It was a noble failure, in the end showing only Lamont’s fearless ambition and his rare power to manipulate words.
在第二封信中，拉蒙特将福特出售公司之举与卡内基将钢铁厂卖给美国钢铁公司相提并论。鉴于福特是卡内基那类个人主义者，这个类比十分巧妙。拉蒙特提议，福特可以像卡内基一样，在公司中保留大量权益，持有“最高级别的优先债务，确保您及您的继承人或指定受益人在未来多年都能获得丰厚且稳定的回报”——拉蒙特喜欢用这种浮华风格来讨好高端客户。但一旦抛出自己的构想，他又立即退后一步，假装不偏不倚地将其呈现给福特考虑。几周后，福特客气地回复了信件，表示感兴趣，但随后便搁置了此事。这最终是一次虽败犹荣的尝试，只彰显了拉蒙特无畏的野心以及他驾驭文字的罕见能力。

After the Ford proposal was rejected, the House of Morgan stayed on the alert for opportunities in the automobile field. One finally came, through the Morgan link with the du Ponts, whose explosives and chemical business had profited from the Morgan Export Department. The war left the du Ponts awash with cash and with large paint, varnish, and artificial-leather manufacturing plants. They saw a potential market for these products in cars and so accumulated General Motors stock until in 1919 they held a 23-percent stake. They had every spot on the GM finance committee save one, that of founder William Crapo Durant.
在福特提案被否决后，摩根家族并未放弃对汽车领域的关注，一直在伺机而动。终于，通过摩根与杜邦家族的纽带，机会降临了——杜邦家族的炸药和化工生意曾从摩根出口部门获益，战后他们手握大把现金，还拥有规模庞大的涂料、清漆和人造革制造厂。他们敏锐地察觉到汽车领域对这些产品的潜在需求，于是不断吸纳通用汽车的股票，到 1919 年已持有 23%的股份。在通用汽车的财务委员会中，除了创始人 William Crapo Durant，其他席位尽数落入他们囊中。

A handsome, sporty man with a winsome grin and a flair for invention, Durant had started out as a rich buggy manufacturer. In September 1908, after being turned down by George Perkins, he financed the new General Motors Company himself, merging the car operations of Ransom E. Olds and David Buick and subsequently acquiring Cadillac. Unlike Henry Ford, who stamped out endless Model Ts, Durant favored a diversified product line. He was a persuasive, charming character—he “could coax a bird right out of a tree,” Walter Chrysler once said—but a disastrous manager, impetuous and erratic.[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275423) This son of a failed bank clerk was also an inveterate stock market gambler whose specialty was GM stock itself. Lamont said he tossed around millions as if they were billiard balls.
杜兰特是个英俊、热爱运动的男人，笑容迷人，富有发明才能，最初以制造豪华马车起家。1908 年 9 月，在遭到乔治·珀金斯拒绝后，他自掏腰包创办了通用汽车公司，将兰塞姆·E·奥兹和大卫·别克旗下的汽车业务合并，随后又收购了凯迪拉克。与亨利·福特那种只生产 T 型车的方式不同，杜兰特偏爱多元化的产品线。他极具说服力，魅力十足——沃尔特·克莱斯勒曾说他“能把鸟从树上哄下来”——但作为管理者却一塌糊涂，冲动且反复无常。 [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275423) 这个失败的银行职员的儿子还是个沉迷股市的赌徒，尤其擅长炒作通用汽车自己的股票。拉蒙特说他挥霍数百万美元就像扔台球一样随意。

In 1920, J. P. Morgan and Company sponsored a \$64-million stock offering to finance a General Motors expansion. To please the du Ponts, the bank retained a sizable block and privately placed the remaining shares in safe hands. Then Ben Strong at the New York Fed engineered the 1920 recession. Henry Ford slashed car prices, and unsold GM cars piled up at dealerships. As GM stock plummeted, the underwriters—including the House of Morgan, the du Ponts, and Durant himself—struggled with huge losses in unsold shares. Durant had also formed a pool to prop up GM—a stock syndicate kept secret from the du Ponts and J. P. Morgan.
1920 年，J. P. 摩根公司（J. P. Morgan and Company）牵头进行了一笔 6400 万美元的股票发行，为通用汽车（General Motors）的扩张提供资金。为了取悦杜邦家族（du Ponts），该银行保留了一大块股票，并将剩余股份私下配售给可靠的人。随后，纽约联邦储备银行（New York Fed）的本·斯特朗（Ben Strong）策划了 1920 年的经济衰退。亨利·福特（Henry Ford）大幅下调汽车价格，导致通用汽车滞销车型在经销商处堆积如山。随着通用汽车股价暴跌，承销商——包括摩根家族（House of Morgan）、杜邦家族以及杜兰特（Durant）本人——因未售出的股票而蒙受巨额亏损。杜兰特还成立了一个基金来支撑通用汽车，这是一个对杜邦家族和 J. P. 摩根都保密的股票辛迪加。

Cool as a flimflam artist, Durant pretended to take the disaster in stride. He didn’t slacken his opera attendance, and he affected a cavalier air. Meanwhile, he faced ruin, for he had used his huge holding of GM stock as collateral for loans. If he had to sell stock to pay creditors, he would not only collapse the stock price but panic the Exchange and ruin GM’s credit. To make matters worse, he had freely lent GM shares as collateral for other people’s borrowings. If he were ruined, he would ruin many others at the same time.
杜兰特装得像个江湖骗子似的，一副对灾难满不在乎的样子。他照常去听歌剧，摆出一副洒脱的姿态。而与此同时，他正面临破产——他用自己持有的巨额通用汽车股票作为贷款抵押。如果他不得不抛售股票来偿还债权人，不仅会压垮股价，还会引发股市恐慌，毁掉通用汽车的信用。更糟的是，他还随意将通用汽车股票借给别人作为贷款抵押。如果他垮了，同时也会拖垮许多其他人。

Where the du Ponts trusted Durant, Dwight Morrow and other Morgan partners were suspicious. As GM shares broke below 20, Durant kept trying to hold back the tide by buying up more shares on margin. He continued to deny that there might be a problem. As the stock dropped as low as 12, his losses steadily mounted. By the night of November 18, 1920, Durant needed close to \$1 million to meet margin calls before the market opened the next morning. Like Henry Ford, Durant despised bankers, viewing them as complacent men with tunnel vision who plundered the inventions of more original minds. Now he had to phone the House of Morgan and ask whether they would buy his GM stock at the closing price of \$12 a share. Pierre du Pont and the Morgan partners, who thought Durant an incompetent, feared a market crash unless he were rescued.
杜邦家族信任杜兰特，而德怀特·莫罗等其他摩根合伙人则心存疑虑。通用汽车股价跌破 20 美元时，杜兰特仍试图通过融资买入更多股票来力挽狂澜。他始终否认可能存在危机。当股价一路跌至 12 美元时，他的亏损不断累积。到了 1920 年 11 月 18 日晚，杜兰特需要近 100 万美元才能在次日开盘前满足追加保证金的要求。和亨利·福特一样，杜兰特鄙视银行家，认为他们是一群自满且目光狭隘的人，只会掠夺那些更具独创性头脑的发明。如今，他不得不致电摩根银行，询问他们是否愿意以每股 12 美元的收盘价收购他持有的通用汽车股票。皮埃尔·杜邦和摩根合伙人们都认为杜兰特无能，但担心若不施以援手，市场将崩盘。

When Dwight Morrow, George Whitney, and Tom Cochran went to Durant’s Fifty-seventh Street offices, they found a scene out of a melodrama. His debts had bulged to an extraordinary \$38 million, and his anteroom was crowded with creditors demanding repayment. The Morgan partners saw a possible repeat of the 1907 panic, with Durant defaults shutting down a string of brokers. In a frenetic, all-night rescue session, the Morgan men bought up Durant’s shares at \$9.50 per share—a steep discount from the closing price. The du Ponts put up \$7 million, and the House of Morgan raised another \$20 million to save Durant from margin calls. By dawn, a new company had been formed to buy Durant’s stock. Durant’s share of the new company was only 40 percent, while the du Ponts held 40 percent, and the Morgan-led bankers took 20 percent as their commission. Pierre du Pont was ready to deal leniently with Durant, but the pitiless Morgan partners insisted that he resign from GM. Overnight, the du Ponts and J. P. Morgan and Company had kidnapped an industrial empire. Two weeks later, Pierre du Pont emerged from retirement to become president of General Motors, a position he held until Alfred P. Sloan, Jr., replaced him three years later.
当德怀特·莫罗、乔治·惠特尼和汤姆·科克伦走进杜兰特位于五十七街的办公室时，映入眼帘的是一幕戏剧化的场景。他的债务已膨胀至惊人的 3800 万美元，候客室里挤满了讨债的债权人。摩根合伙人担心 1907 年大恐慌会重演——杜兰特一旦违约，会导致一连串经纪商倒闭。在一场彻夜不休的紧急救援中，摩根方面以每股 9.50 美元的价格买下杜兰特的股票——这比收盘价打了很大折扣。杜邦家族出资 700 万美元，摩根银行又筹措了 2000 万美元，才帮杜兰特躲过追加保证金的要求。到天亮时，一家新公司已经成立，用以收购杜兰特的股票。杜兰特在新公司中的持股比例仅为 40%，杜邦家族也持有 40%，而由摩根牵头的银行家们则以 20%的股份作为佣金。皮埃尔·杜邦本打算对杜兰特宽容处理，但冷酷无情的摩根合伙人坚持要他辞去通用汽车的职务。 一夜之间，du Ponts 和 J. P. Morgan and Company 绑架了一个工业帝国。两周后，Pierre du Pont 从退休状态重返战场，出任 General Motors 总裁，直到三年后 Alfred P. Sloan, Jr. 接替他的位置。

It was a double coup in Morgan history, for it confirmed the bank’s relationship with General Motors and won the loyalty of the du Ponts. As Pierre du Pont wrote to his brother, Irenee, “Throughout the whole transaction the Morgan partners have appeared to the greatest advantage. They threw themselves into the situation wholeheartedly, stating at the start that they asked no compensation. They have acted with remarkable speed and success, the whole deal involving \$60 million or more, having planned and practically completed it in less than 4 days.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275575)
这在摩根历史上堪称双料政变，既巩固了银行与 General Motors 的关系，又赢得了 du Ponts 的忠诚。正如 Pierre du Pont 在给兄弟 Irenee 的信中所写："整个交易过程中，摩根合伙人表现得极为亮眼。他们全身心投入，从一开始就声明不求任何回报。行动之迅速、成效之显著，令人叹服——涉及金额高达 6000 万美元甚至更多的交易，他们在不到 4 天的时间里就完成了规划并基本落地。" [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275575)

What about William Crapo Durant? An unreconstructed plunger, he lost half his net worth in the 1929 crash. In later years, he ran a bowling alley in Flint, Michigan. Poor and almost forgotten, he died in New York in 1947.
威廉·克拉波·杜兰特呢？一个死不悔改的投机者，他在 1929 年崩盘中损失了一半身家。晚年在密歇根州弗林特经营一家保龄球馆。穷困潦倒，几乎被人遗忘，1947 年在纽约去世。

DURING the 1920s, a cash-rich America embarked on a binge of buying foreign bonds, a new experience for a country that had long relied on European capital markets to finance its own development. The investing fad had begun when the Treasury sold Liberty and Victory bonds in denominations as small as \$50, enticing a public new to buying bonds. After the war, the habit of investing persisted. If Americans traditionally put their money into savings banks, insurance policies, and old mattresses, now they bought bonds *en masse.* Brokerage houses encouraged Americans to think of themselves as potential tycoons, global benefactors, embryonic J. P. Morgans.
20 世纪 20 年代，手头阔绰的美国开启了一场购买外国债券的热潮——对于这个长期依赖欧洲资本市场为本国发展融资的国家来说，这还是一种全新体验。这场投资风潮始于财政部发行面额低至 50 美元的“自由债券”和“胜利债券”，吸引了初涉债券市场的公众。战后，这种投资习惯延续了下来。如果说美国人传统上把钱存进储蓄银行、买保险或塞进旧床垫里，那现在他们则开始大量购买债券。券商机构鼓励美国人把自己想象成潜在的大亨、全球的施惠者、初出茅庐的 J.P.摩根。

The big New York City banks scrambled for the new business. National banks were barred from underwriting and distributing securities, but they could bypass such restrictions by creating separate securities subsidiaries. Chase, National City, and Guaranty Trust opened such affiliates. They sent out thousands of agents across the country, plying investors with a dizzying array of foreign bonds from Brazil and Peru, Cuba and Chile. At the same time, many American banks invaded overseas markets. Before the 1913 Federal Reserve Act, only state-chartered banks could have overseas branches—one reason why J. P. Morgan and Company had an enormous head start with foreign clients. Now nationally chartered banks could do the same. The glad-handing, fast-talking American banker became a figure of folklore around the world.
纽约各大银行竞相争夺这些新业务。国民银行被禁止承销和分销证券，但它们可以通过设立独立的证券子公司来绕过这些限制。大通银行、国民城市银行和担保信托公司都开设了这样的附属机构。它们派出数千名代理人前往全国各地，向投资者兜售来自巴西、秘鲁、古巴和智利等国的各种外国债券，令人眼花缭乱。与此同时，许多美国银行也大举进军海外市场。在 1913 年《联邦储备法》颁布之前，只有州立银行才能在海外设立分行——这也是 J.P.摩根公司在外国客户中占据巨大先发优势的原因之一。如今，国民银行也获得了同样的权利。那些善于握手寒暄、口若悬河的美国银行家，成了世界各地广为人知的传奇形象。

In a burst of activity, National City went into Russia (where its branches were confiscated by the Bolsheviks), set up a thriving business in China, and established branches in Buenos Aires and Rio de Janeiro. Where Barings had long been dominant in Argentinean business, it was overtaken in the postwar years by National City, J. P. Morgan and Company, and Kuhn, Loeb. At the same time, the City was paralyzed by the Treasury embargo on foreign loans and lost many long-standing sovereign clients. When Argentina invited Barings to share management of a \$40-million loan with J. P. Morgan in 1925, the Treasury embargo forced Barings to pass on the large financing.
在一阵迅猛的扩张中，国民城市银行进入了俄罗斯（其分行被布尔什维克没收），在中国业务蒸蒸日上，并在布宜诺斯艾利斯和里约热内卢设立了分行。巴林银行长期以来在阿根廷业务中占据主导地位，但战后被国民城市银行、J.P.摩根公司和库恩-洛布公司超越。与此同时，美国财政部对外国贷款的禁运令导致花旗银行（此处 The City 指花旗银行前身？注意上下文：前文提到 National City 是国民城市银行，而"The City"可能指代花旗银行或伦敦金融城？但根据语境，前面说"National City"进入俄罗斯等，后面说"the City was paralyzed"，应指花旗银行（City Bank）或国民城市银行本身？原文"The City"首字母大写，通常指伦敦金融城，但结合"Treasury embargo on foreign loans"是美国财政部的禁运，所以"the City"可能指代纽约金融界或国民城市银行。更为合理的解释是：此处"The City"指代花旗银行（即 National City Bank 的简称），因为常被称为"City Bank"。故译为"花旗银行"或保留"国民城市银行"。但更常见的是：Citibank 即花旗银行，其前身就是 National City Bank。为保持一致，将"The City"译为"花旗银行"。）陷入瘫痪，失去了许多长期的主权客户。当 1925 年阿根廷邀请巴林银行与 J.P.摩根共同管理一笔 4000 万美元的贷款时，美国财政部的禁运迫使巴林银行放弃了这笔巨额融资。

Washington watched the investing craze with growing fascination and wondered how to exploit it politically. Even after a Republican president, Ohio newspaper publisher Warren Harding, captured the White House in 1920, his *laissez-faire* ideology didn’t stop his administration from trying to mobilize the new Wall Street power. The paradox of the Roaring Twenties was that three free-market Republican administrations would confer new, semiofficial status on foreign lending, assuming the right to veto loans—something no Democratic administration would have dared to do, lest it be accused of socialist tendencies.
华盛顿带着越来越浓厚的兴趣注视着这股投资热潮，思忖着如何在政治上加以利用。即便 1920 年共和党人、俄亥俄州报纸出版商沃伦·哈丁入主白宫，其自由放任的理念也未能阻止政府试图调动华尔街这股新兴力量。咆哮的二十年代的悖论在于，三届信奉自由市场的共和党政府竟然赋予了对外贷款某种半官方地位，并拥有了否决贷款的权力——这是任何民主党政府都不敢做的，生怕被扣上社会主义倾向的帽子。

The driving force behind the new loan policy was Secretary of Commerce Herbert Hoover. Hoover saw a precedent in Wilson administration policy toward Russian and Chinese lending, where the government had maintained a close eye on the bankers. At a White House conference on May 25, 1921, President Harding told Tom Lamont and other Wall Street bankers that henceforth *all* foreign loans had to be certified by the State, Treasury, and Commerce departments as being in the national interest. The secretaries in question—Charles Evans Hughes, Andrew Mellon, and Hoover—were there to back him up. Morgans had to notify other banks about the arrangement. Afterward, as spokesman for the influential private banks and trust companies, Jack Morgan pledged to Harding that the bankers would “keep the State Department fully informed of any and all negotiations for loans to foreign governments which may be undertaken by them.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275748) For a pro-business administration, it was an astounding extension of governmental power. Carter Glass, now a Virginia senator, denounced the violation of bankers’ rights.
新贷款政策背后的推动力是商务部长赫伯特·胡佛。胡佛从威尔逊政府时期对俄国和中国贷款的政策中看到了先例——当时政府一直密切监督着银行家们的动向。在 1921 年 5 月 25 日白宫的一次会议上，哈丁总统告知汤姆·拉蒙特和其他华尔街银行家，今后所有对外贷款必须经国务院、财政部和商务部认证，确认符合国家利益。上述三位部长——查尔斯·埃文斯·休斯、安德鲁·梅隆和胡佛——当场表示支持。摩根公司需要将这一安排通知其他银行。随后，作为有影响力的私营银行和信托公司的代言人，杰克·摩根向哈丁承诺，银行家们将“随时向国务院充分汇报他们可能进行的任何对外政府贷款谈判”。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275748) 对于一届亲商政府来说，这简直是政府权力的惊人扩张。如今已是弗吉尼亚州参议员的卡特·格拉斯，谴责此举侵犯了银行家的权利。

During the Republican-dominated 1920s, bankers probably attained their peak of influence in American history. It would be the heyday of Morgan power. Yet the bank’s relations with the White House were never smooth, however much collusion and back scratching the radical pamphleteers might have discerned. From the outset, Morgan partners thought Harding a simpleton, inadequate to the challenge of postwar reconstruction. Upon Harding, Tom Lamont would later deliver a scathing judgment, seeing him as a “pathetic figure . . . the last man in the world to lead 120 million people from the darkness and confusion of World War I out into the light.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275947) Even Jack, who was relieved by the Democratic rout and rushed to offer his services to the president, sniped at Harding as a “wishy-washy” chauvinist who lacked vision.
在共和党主导的 1920 年代，银行家的影响力可能达到了美国历史的巅峰。这将是摩根大亨们的全盛时期。然而，无论激进的小册子作者们能看出多少暗中勾结和利益交换，摩根银行与白宫的关系始终谈不上顺畅。从一开始，摩根的合伙人就认为哈丁是个头脑简单的家伙，根本无力应对战后重建的挑战。后来，汤姆·拉蒙特对哈丁做出了尖刻的评价，称他是“一个可悲的人物……世界上最不适合带领 1.2 亿人走出第一次世界大战的黑暗与混乱、迈向光明的人。” [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2275947) 就连因民主党溃败而松了口气、急忙向总统献殷勤的杰克，也讥讽哈丁是个“优柔寡断”、缺乏远见的沙文主义者。

The disdain for Harding was more than personal, for the White House and the House of Morgan represented quite different factions of the Republican party. By instinct and self-interest, the Morgan bank was liberal and internationalist on global financial issues. It advocated U.S. leadership, close consultation with the Allies, and vigorous lending abroad. On foreign policy issues it felt some kinship with Wilsonian Democrats. With England handicapped in its resumption of foreign lending, J. P. Morgan and Company wanted the United States to inherit British leadership and initiate the rebuilding of Europe. The Harding brand of Republicanism, by contrast, was provincial, protectionist, and wearily contemptuous of European conflicts. These Republicans regarded foreign loans as ways to manipulate foreigners or as wasted welfare payments better spent inside America. Throughout Morgan history, the bank would be strongly drawn to internationalist leaders, not necessarily Republican.
对哈定的蔑视不仅仅是针对他个人，因为白宫与摩根财团在当时代表着共和党内部截然不同的派系。无论出于本能还是自身利益，摩根银行在全球金融问题上都持自由派和国际主义立场。它主张美国发挥领导作用，与协约国密切磋商，并向海外积极提供贷款。在对外政策议题上，摩根与威尔逊派的民主党人颇有共鸣。由于英国在恢复对外贷款方面处境艰难，J.P.摩根公司希望美国能够接过英国的领导权，启动欧洲的重建。相比之下，哈定式的共和主义则显得狭隘、保护主义，并对欧洲的纷争抱着厌倦的轻蔑态度。这些共和党人认为对外贷款要么是操纵外国人的手段，要么是浪费了的福利支出，还不如用在美国国内。纵观摩根银行的历史，它始终强烈倾向于国际主义领袖——并不一定是共和党人。

Early in the new administration, the House of Morgan feuded with Harding over some \$10 billion that the Allies owed to Washington from wartime loans. (These were the loans extended after the United States entered the war, not those sponsored by J. P. Morgan on Wall Street.) The pro-English House of Morgan argued strenuously to cancel this debt. Jack Morgan said the Allies had sent soldiers against Germany while America was still sending only dollars; decency demanded that the war debt be regarded as a subsidy and not as a loan. For the Harding administration, it was a question of whether Yankees would again be snookered by corrupt, wily Europeans. Collecting war debts was also a way to keep U.S. taxes low. When Lamont went to talk about getting the debt canceled, he found Harding floating in a sea of papers. “Lamont, this job is too much for me,” the president said. “Whatever shall I do with all that pile? Well, I suppose I might as well try to learn something about these debts.”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276116)
新政府上任之初，摩根家族与哈丁政府就协约国拖欠华盛顿的约 100 亿美元战争贷款争执不下（这些是美国参战后发放的贷款，并非 J. P. 摩根在华尔街经手的那批）。亲英的摩根家族极力主张免除这笔债务。杰克·摩根辩称，协约国已派兵对抗德国，而美国当时只出钱不出力；从道义上讲，战争债务应视为补贴而非贷款。对哈丁政府而言，问题在于美国佬是否又要被狡猾腐败的欧洲人戏耍。追讨战争债务也是维持美国低税率的手段。当拉蒙特去找哈丁商议取消债务时，发现总统正淹没在文件堆里。“拉蒙特，这差事我实在扛不住，”总统说道，“这么多文件可叫我如何是好？唉，我看还是先试着了解了解这些债务吧。” [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276116)

Lamont’s subsequent meetings weren’t any more encouraging. Charles Evans Hughes, the secretary of state, had unsuccessfuly campaigned for U.S. membership in the League of Nations and felt uncomfortable with the insular debt policy. But he cited the lack of a popular mandate to cancel the debt—the same refrain the House of Morgan would hear for a dozen years to come. Lamont loftily suggested to Hughes that the United States take British Honduras in a swap for a piece of the debt—this was thrown out casually! Lamont found the other cabinet members overjoyed at the prospect of squeezing the debtor nations.
拉蒙特随后的几场会面也没带来什么转机。国务卿查尔斯·埃文斯·休斯曾为美国加入国际联盟奔走游说却未能成功，他对这种孤立的债务政策也感到不安。但他援引“缺乏公众授权”为由拒绝取消债务——这句话摩根财团在接下来十几年里会反复听到。拉蒙特傲慢地向休斯建议，美国可以用英属洪都拉斯来抵偿部分债务——这提议简直像是随口抛出的！拉蒙特发现其他内阁成员对压榨债务国的前景欣喜若狂。

The administration adopted a policy of barring Wall Street loans to any foreign government that had not settled its war debts with the United States. After a sobering encounter with Treasury Secretary Andrew Mellon, Lamont reported in horror to Jack: “He is the watchdog of the Treasury and naturally considers it his duty to see that the Treasury gets every penny out of its debtors . . . he seems to think, too, that if we keep alive all these notes owing to us from dinky little countries all over Europe the fact that we are holding the notes will give us a sort of strangle hold politically.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276251)
政府出台了一项政策，禁止华尔街向任何未与美国结清战争债务的外国政府提供贷款。在与财政部长安德鲁·梅隆进行了一次令人警醒的会面后，拉蒙特惊恐地向杰克报告说：“他是财政部的看门狗，自然认为自己的职责就是确保财政部能从债务人那里榨出每一分钱……他似乎还觉得，如果我们把那些欧洲小国欠我们的所有票据都攥在手里，持有这些票据的事实反而会让我们在政治上占据某种扼喉般的优势。”

This was an extraordinarily shortsighted attitude that would weigh on world finance for a generation. The mountain of debt would retard world trade, undermine political leadership, and poison relations among the Western nations. Faced with Washington’s obstinacy, the House of Morgan and Ben Strong reluctantly advised their British friends to settle the debt with Washington. After meetings between Mellon and Stanley Baldwin, the chancellor of the Exchequer, the British agreed to make payments stretching over sixty-two years. But they didn’t accept the bullying cheerfully. When Prime Minister Bonar Law heard the terms from Stanley Baldwin, he fairly howled with rage. The issue would fester throughout the interwar period, placing Morgans in the cross fire between Washington and Whitehall. At the same time, the failure to cancel Allied debt meant that the House of Morgan had to take a tough line toward German reparations. For if the Germans didn’t pay reparations to the Allies, how would the Allies pay Washington? This created a destructive merry-go-round of debt that would spin ever faster until the whole system would break down in the 1930s.
这是一种极其短视的态度，它将在整整一代人的时间内给世界金融带来沉重负担。堆积如山的债务不仅阻碍了世界贸易，削弱了政治领导力，还毒化了西方各国间的关系。面对华盛顿的固执，摩根财团和本·斯特朗不情愿地建议他们的英国朋友与华盛顿达成债务协议。在梅隆与英国财政大臣斯坦利·鲍德温会晤后，英方同意分期偿还债务，还款期长达六十二年。但他们并非心甘情愿地接受这种欺凌。当首相博纳·劳从斯坦利·鲍德温口中得知协议条款时，他愤怒地咆哮起来。在整个两次世界大战之间的时期，这一问题不断恶化，使摩根财团陷入华盛顿与白厅之间的交叉火力之中。同时，未能取消协约国债务意味着摩根财团不得不在德国赔款问题上采取强硬立场。 如果德国不向协约国支付战争赔款，协约国又拿什么来偿还欠华盛顿的债务？这便形成了一台破坏性的债务旋转木马——它越转越快，最终在 20 世纪 30 年代拖垮了整个体系。

If Washington at first demanded control over foreign lending out of concern for the Allied war debt, it soon grew accustomed to exercising its new power. The arrangement took on an unexpected longevity; the procedure became so entrenched that J. P. Morgan and Company would brief the incoming Coolidge and Hoover administrations on how it worked. Later, in remarkable testimony to government-banker ties in the Diplomatic Age, Tom Lamont would state categorically that no sizable loan of the 1920s was made without Washington’s tacit approval. The line between politics and finance blurred, then disappeared. The cognoscenti who interpreted Morgan actions as a mirror of official policy were seldom far off the mark.
华盛顿起初因担心协约国战争债务而要求掌控对外贷款，但很快便习惯于行使这一新权力。这一安排竟出乎意料地延续了许久；相关程序变得根深蒂固，以至于 J.P.摩根公司要向即将上任的柯立芝和胡佛政府汇报其运作机制。后来，在外交时代那场关于政府与银行家关系的著名证词中，汤姆·拉蒙特斩钉截铁地表示，1920 年代任何一笔数额可观的贷款，若未得到华盛顿的默许，都不可能放出。政治与金融的界限日渐模糊，最终消失殆尽。那些将摩根行为解读为官方政策风向标的圈内人，几乎从未失手。

If this arrangement later collapsed in recriminations, it started in a spirit of mutual convenience. Hiding behind Wall Street banks, the government could disclaim responsibility when countries were approved or rejected for loans. The banks, in turn, saw it as a security treaty, committing the government to protecting loans made under its aegis. It also provided the banks with government intelligence about debtor states. As the United States became a creditor nation, Wall Street confronted that ageless problem of how to enforce payment from sovereign states. Washington seemed to be the answer.
虽然这种安排在后来因互相指责而瓦解，但起初却是在互惠互利的精神下展开的。政府躲在华尔街银行身后，当各国贷款申请获批或遭拒时，便可推卸责任。而银行则将其视为一份安保条约，承诺政府会保护在其庇护下发放的贷款。同时，这也让银行得以获取政府关于债务国的情报。随着美国成为债权国，华尔街面临了那个永恒的难题——如何迫使主权国家履行还款义务。华盛顿似乎就是答案。

With the Harding review process came a notion—never explicitly stated, but always there—that a government safety net was in place, which would catch investors who fell off the high wire. As Lamont said, the government’s stamp of approval “led many American investors into big foreign issues under the impression, whether so stated or not, that the Government had approved the issue or it could not have been made.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276442) The arrangement encouraged a lot of wishful thinking and spared bankers unpleasant thoughts about what might happen in the case of default. There was an unspoken invitation to dispense with close examination of debtor nations. In the 1920s, Wall Street operated under an assumption of government protection, a notion that would prove illusory. But while it lasted, it created a mood of intoxication such as the Street had never known before and helped to trigger a decade of dreams that ended in the 1929 crash.
在哈丁审核流程实施的过程中，一种从未明说却始终存在的观念悄然形成——政府设置了一道安全网，能接住从高空钢丝上坠落的投资者。正如拉蒙特所言，政府的批准印章"让许多美国投资者大举投资外国债券，他们无论是否被告知，都认为政府已经批准了这些债券，否则它们不可能发行。" [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276442) 这种安排催生了许多一厢情愿的想法，也让银行家们不必去思考违约可能带来的不愉快后果。这等于默许人们不再仔细审视债务国的状况。20 世纪 20 年代，华尔街在政府保护的假设下运作，而这一假设后来被证明是虚幻的。但在其存续期间，它营造出一种华尔街前所未有的陶醉感，并助长了持续十年的美梦，最终在 1929 年的崩盘中化为泡影。
