# Chapter Two: Polonius / 第二章 波洛涅斯


IF Emerson was correct that “an institution is the lengthened shadow of a man,” then the shadow-caster of the House of Morgan was Junius Spencer Morgan. Pounded into his son, Pierpont, his precepts codified Morgan philosophy for a century. He was a fussbudget father, fretting over son and bank, a figure so massive and willful that only his son, retrospectively, could reduce him to merely the “father of J. Pierpont Morgan.” As one journalist said, “The Morgans always believed in absolute monarchy. While Junius Morgan lived, he ruled the family and the business—his son and his partners.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190887) Until Junius died, in 1890, his massive shadow dominated his son’s life.
如果爱默生说得对，“一个机构就是一个人的拉长了的影子”，那么摩根银行的影子制造者就是 Junius Spencer Morgan。他的信条被灌输给儿子 Pierpont，并在一百年间成为摩根哲学的准则。他是一位过分操心的父亲，为儿子和银行焦虑不安；他的人物如此庞大而固执，以至于只有他的儿子，在事后回想中，才能将他缩小为仅仅是“J. Pierpont Morgan 的父亲”。正如一位记者所言：“摩根家族始终信奉绝对君主制。只要 Junius Morgan 在世，他就统治着家族和生意——包括他的儿子和合伙人。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190887) 直到 1890 年 Junius 去世，他那巨大的阴影一直笼罩着儿子的生活。

Junius was cool and steady and seldom showed his hand. He had a dry wit and a genial manner and employed iron discipline. His friend George Smalley praised his “grave, strong beauty” and his “eyes full of light” but noticed the face ended “in an immovable jaw, all will.” Sometimes the stone facade broke down, but imperceptibly. “Once or twice I have seen him angry, and he showed his anger by a sudden restraint of speech and of manner.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191075) That was as far as Junius betrayed emotion.
朱尼厄斯冷静沉稳，从不轻易表露内心。他说话带着冷幽默，态度亲切，却严守铁一般的自律。他的朋友乔治·斯莫利曾称赞他“庄重而英挺的俊美”和“目光炯炯的双眸”，但也注意到他的脸最终归结为“一副纹丝不动的下颌，全是意志”。有时这张石雕般的面孔会崩裂，但几乎难以察觉。“有一两次我见他动怒，而他表现愤怒的方式，是突然收敛了言辞和举止。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191075) 朱尼厄斯流露情绪的程度，仅止于此。

Where George Peabody bore the scars of early poverty, Junius Morgan had the smooth manners and poise of inherited wealth. Among the possessors of great American fortunes, the Morgans boasted a uniquely pampered lineage. They didn’t claw their way up from poverty or legitimize a bloody frontier fortune with later respectability. By the early nineteenth century, they were well-to-do, enjoying a cushion of security generations thick. Affluent and well-bred, they weren’t rejected by European aristocracy, as were the Vanderbilts. One finds it hard to track down those poor, benighted Morgans whose early suffering made later wealth glorious. By no accident, the family produced defenders of the social order whose vices sprang from too much comfort and too little exposure to ordinary human misery.
乔治·皮博迪身上镌刻着早年贫困的伤痕，而朱尼厄斯·摩根则流淌着继承财富带来的优雅与从容。在美国巨富家族中，摩根家族以备受娇宠的血统自傲——他们并非从底层挣扎而出，也无需用后来的体面为血腥的边疆发家史洗白。早在 19 世纪初，这个家族就已殷实富足，享受着数代人积淀的安稳屏障。他们既有教养又有底蕴，不像范德比尔特家族那样被欧洲贵族排挤。你很难找到那些早年受尽苦难、后来才靠困苦发迹的摩根先辈。这绝非偶然——这个家族孕育出的社会秩序守护者，其劣根性恰恰源于过度的安逸与对凡人疾苦的疏离。

The first Morgan in America was Miles, who emigrated from Wales to Springfield, Massachusetts, sixteen years after the *Mayflower* landed at Plymouth. He prospered as a farmer and fighter of Indians, spawning generations of land-owning Morgans. His descendant Joseph Morgan fought with Washington’s army during the American Revolution. In 1817, Joseph sold his farm in West Springfield, Massachusetts, and moved to Hartford, Connecticut, which would become the Morgans’ ancestral home. Joseph had a refined air, a straight, delicate nose, and coolly discerning eyes. Like later Morgans, he was a hymn-singer and Bible-thumper and subscribed to the Wadsworth Atheneum, the city’s new art museum. As a businessman, he strikingly resembled his progeny: he bought a stagecoach line and the Exchange Coffee House, on whose premises he helped to organize the Aetna Fire Insurance Company. In irrepressible Morgan style, he added the City Hotel, invested in canal and steamboat companies, directed a bank, and helped finance the Hartford and New Haven Railroad, whose grisly train wrecks would haunt his descendants. Joseph made his great windfall in December 1835, when a fire in the Wall Street area destroyed over six hundred buildings. As an Aetna founder, he insisted that the firm pay customers promptly and even bought up Aetna stakes from investors who hesitated to pay. Joseph Morgan’s quick action made the firm’s reputation on Wall Street and later enabled it to triple its premiums.
美国第一位摩根是迈尔斯，他在“五月花号”登陆普利茅斯十六年后，从威尔士移民到马萨诸塞州的斯普林菲尔德。他靠务农和与印第安人作战发家，繁衍出数代拥有土地的摩根家族。他的后代约瑟夫·摩根在美国独立战争期间曾随华盛顿的军队作战。1817 年，约瑟夫卖掉了他在马萨诸塞州西斯普林菲尔德的农场，搬到康涅狄格州的哈特福德，这里后来成为摩根家族的祖籍地。约瑟夫气质文雅，鼻梁挺直纤细，眼神冷静而锐利。和后来的摩根家族成员一样，他爱唱赞美诗、笃信圣经，还订阅了市里新开的沃兹沃思艺术博物馆的会员。作为商人，他与后代惊人地相似：他买下了一条驿马车线路和交易所咖啡馆，并协助在这家咖啡馆的场地上成立了怡安消防保险公司。 以摩根那种无法抑制的做派，他买下了城市酒店，投资运河和汽船公司，掌管一家银行，并出资帮助修建哈特福德与纽黑文铁路——这条铁路日后频繁发生的惨烈事故，将成为他子孙后代的梦魇。约瑟夫在 1835 年 12 月迎来了他的巨额横财，当时华尔街地区的一场大火烧毁了六百多栋建筑。作为怡安保险的创始人之一，他坚持公司迅速赔付客户，甚至从那些犹豫不决的投资者手中买下了怡安的股份。约瑟夫·摩根的果断行动让公司在华尔街声名鹊起，随后更是让保费翻了三倍。

To Joseph’s wife, Sarah, the Morgans owe those strange eyes—fearful, querulous, and burning—that shone with such famous intensity in the face of young Pierpont. Sarah had a fleshy chin and bulbous nose, adding a peasant roundness to the patrician Morgan face.
摩根家族从约瑟夫的妻子莎拉那里继承了那奇怪的眼神——恐惧、多疑、炽烈——在小皮尔庞特脸上以如此著名的强度闪耀。莎拉长着肉嘟嘟的下巴和蒜头鼻，为摩根家族那种贵族式的面容平添了几分乡土圆润。

In 1836, Joseph bought his son, Junius, a partnership in the Hartford dry-goods house of Howe and Mather. That same year, Junius married Juliet Pierpont, daughter of the Reverend John Pierpont of Boston, pastor of the Old Hollis Street Church. This union of Morgan and Pierpont joined together in their infant son, John Pierpont, born in 1837, a wildly improbable set of genes. A poet and preacher, the Reverend John Pierpont was a fiery abolitionist and friend of William Lloyd Garrison and Henry Ward Beecher. With craggy face and tousled hair, he spurned the Morgans’ Yankee trader values. He was a failed merchant from an old New England family and had a romantic temperament and a crusading spirit. He engaged in a bitter public row with his Boston parishioners and was charged with “moral impurity” for speaking the word “whore.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191245)With the church cellar rented to a local rum merchant, the congregation found his views on temperance subversive. It was said that in the heat of argument, the Reverend Pierpont’s prominent nose became inflamed—as would his grandson’s. To Rev. Pierpont, the Morgans probably owe the streak of repressed romanticism and moralism in their later history. Not by chance would the House of Morgan fancy itself Wall Street’s conscience and attract many sons of preachers and teachers.
1836 年，约瑟夫为儿子朱尼厄斯买下了哈特福德豪斯与马瑟干货公司的合伙人资格。同年，朱尼厄斯娶了波士顿老霍利斯街教堂牧师约翰·皮尔庞特牧师之女朱丽叶·皮尔庞特为妻。摩根与皮尔庞特两家的联姻，在他们 1837 年出生的幼子约翰·皮尔庞特身上，凝聚了一组极其不可思议的基因。身为诗人与传道者的约翰·皮尔庞特牧师，是位激烈的废奴主义者，也是威廉·劳埃德·加里森和亨利·沃德·比彻的挚友。他面容嶙峋，头发蓬乱，对摩根家族那种扬基商人的价值观不屑一顾。他出身新英格兰古老世家，却是个失败的商人，生性浪漫，又怀揣着十字军般的理想主义激情。他曾与波士顿的教区教众公开激烈争吵，因说出了“妓女”一词而遭“道德不洁”的指控。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191245) 教会的酒窖又租给了当地的朗姆酒商人，于是教徒们认为他关于戒酒的观点极具颠覆性。 据说，皮尔庞特牧师在激烈争辩时，他那高耸的鼻子会变得通红——日后他的外孙也是如此。摩根家族后来的历史中那股压抑的浪漫主义与道德主义气质，大概要归功于皮尔庞特牧师。并非偶然的是，摩根财团自诩为华尔街的良心，并吸引了许多牧师和教师的后代加入。

When Joseph died, in 1847, he left an estate of more than \$1 million. Four years later, Junius cashed in his stake in Howe and Mather for an estimated \$600,000 and moved to Boston to hunt bigger game. As partner in the restyled J. M. Beebe, Morgan and Company—the city’s largest mercantile house—he operated on a global scale, exporting and financing cotton and other goods carried by clipper ships from Boston harbor. It was here that he came to George Peabody’s attention.
1847 年约瑟夫去世时，留下了超过 100 万美元的遗产。四年后，朱尼厄斯将其在豪-马瑟公司中的股份套现，估计获得 60 万美元，随后移居波士顿，寻求更大的机遇。作为重组后的 J. M. 比伯-摩根公司——波士顿最大的商行——的合伙人，他经营着全球业务，为从波士顿港驶出的飞剪船所载的棉花及其他货物提供出口和融资。正是在这里，他引起了乔治·皮博迪的注意。

By this point, Junius’s son Pierpont already seemed quite contradictory. One side of him was pure *homo economicus.* As a small boy, he was restricted to a twenty-five-cent weekly allowance and minutely noted candy and orange purchases in a ledger. At twelve, he charged admission to a viewing of his diorama of Columbus’s landing. As an adolescent, he was ardent and high-spirited but also petulant and prone to sudden mood swings. He was afflicted with facial rashes, which made him morbidly self-conscious, and his childhood was marred by constant headaches, scarlet fever, and ailments of mysterious provenance. Perhaps the contrast between his own steady nature and Pierpont’s unruly temper made Junius fret unduly about his boy. With granite will, he began to mold Pierpont, instructing him to associate with those of his grammar-school classmates “as are of the right stamp & whose influence over you will be good.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191430) This Polonius-like voice would drone on for decades.
此时，朱尼厄斯的儿子皮尔庞特已经显得相当矛盾。他的一面是纯粹的理性经济人。还是个小男孩时，他每周只有二十五美分的零花钱，并会在账本上详细记录买糖果和橙子的开支。十二岁时，他为自己制作的哥伦布登陆西洋景展览收取门票。少年时期的他热情洋溢、精神饱满，但也任性急躁，情绪波动剧烈。他因面部皮疹而饱受折磨，变得病态地自我敏感；童年时期还不断被头痛、猩红热和来源不明的疾病所困扰。或许正是因为自己沉稳的性格与皮尔庞特难以驾驭的脾气形成鲜明对比，朱尼厄斯对儿子过分担忧。他用花岗岩般的意志开始塑造皮尔庞特，教导他要与文法学校里“那些品格端正、能对你产生良好影响”的同学交往。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191430) 这种波洛涅斯式的说教将持续数十年。

When his father moved the family to Boston, Pierpont enrolled in the English High School there, from which he graduated in 1854. While there, he suffered a severe bout of inflammatory rheumatism and in 1852 spent several months recuperating in the Azores, the illness left one leg shorter than the other. For the rest of his life, assorted ailments would confine Pierpont to bed several days each month. He was a curious study in contrasts, sometimes sickly, sometimes capable of great bursts of energy that would exhaust him and send him back to bed.
当他的父亲举家迁至波士顿后，皮尔庞特进入当地的英文高中就读，并于 1854 年毕业。在校期间，他患上了严重的炎性风湿病，1852 年曾在亚速尔群岛休养数月，这场病导致他一条腿比另一条腿短。此后余生，各种疾病缠身，皮尔庞特每个月都要卧床数日。他是一个充满矛盾的研究对象：时而病弱，时而爆发出惊人的精力，但往往又因过度劳累而不得不再次卧床。

Early on, Pierpont figured in his father’s business plans. Junius knew that the houses of Baring and Rothschild operated largely as family enterprises, grooming sons to inherit their respective businesses. In fact, the Rothschild insignia of five arrows commemorated five sons dispatched to five European capitals. The British economist and journalist Walter Bagehot noted, “The banker’s calling is hereditary; the credit of the bank descends from father to son; this inherited wealth brings inherited refinement.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191583) Since merchant bankers financed foreign trade, their bills had to be honored on sight in distant places, so their names had to inspire instant trust. As a twentieth-century Hambros Bank chairman would put it, “Our job is to breed wisely.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191747) The family structure also guaranteed the preservation of the bank’s capital.
早年，皮尔庞特便在父亲的商业计划中扮演了角色。朱尼厄斯深知，巴林银行和罗斯柴尔德家族基本上都是以家族企业的模式运作，悉心培养儿子们继承各自的事业。事实上，罗斯柴尔德家族那五支箭的标志，正是为了纪念被派往欧洲五国首都的五位儿子。英国经济学家兼记者沃尔特·白芝浩曾指出："银行家的职业是世袭的；银行的信誉代代相传；这种继承而来的财富，也带来了与生俱来的教养。" [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191583) 由于商人银行家为对外贸易提供融资，他们的票据必须在遥远的地方见票即付，因此他们的名字必须能让人瞬间产生信任。正如 20 世纪汉布罗斯银行的一位董事长所言："我们的工作就是明智地繁衍。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191747) 这种家族结构也确保了银行资本的保全。

Besides his three sisters—Sarah, Mary, and Juliet—Pierpont had a younger brother, Junius, Jr., fondly nicknamed “the Doctor,” who died in 1858 at age twelve. So it was onto Pierpont, the lone surviving male heir, that Junius Morgan projected his imperial ambitions, in preparation for which he provided him with a gentlemanly education. To allow him to attain fluency in foreign languages and to season him for global business, Junius in 1854 sent Pierpont to the Institut Sillig, a boarding school on Lake Geneva. This was followed by a stint at the German university in Gottingen in 1856, where Pierpont enjoyed the bluff camaraderie of student clubs. He was a dashing, foppish boy, partial to polka-dot vests, bright cravats, and checkered pants. Already self-conscious about his skin eruptions, he shied away from the popular student duels that might disfigure his face.
除了他的三个姐妹——莎拉、玛丽和朱丽叶——皮尔庞特还有一个弟弟小朱尼厄斯，昵称"博士"，于 1858 年夭折，年仅十二岁。因此，作为唯一幸存的男性继承人，朱尼厄斯·摩根将他的帝国野心寄托在皮尔庞特身上，为了培养他，为他提供了绅士般的教育。为了让皮尔庞特掌握流利的外语并适应全球商业环境，朱尼厄斯于 1854 年将他送到日内瓦湖畔的西利格寄宿学校。随后，1856 年他又在哥廷根的德国大学学习了一段时间，皮尔庞特在那里享受了学生俱乐部里豪爽的友谊。他是一个风度翩翩、爱打扮的少年，偏爱波点马甲、鲜艳的领带和格子裤。由于对自己的皮肤问题已经相当敏感，他回避了可能毁容的流行学生决斗。

Throughout his life, Pierpont had little intellectual curiosity or aptitude for theorizing, and at Gottingen he excelled most at math. Beneath a rough boyish swagger, he was sensitive to art. He also collected autographs of presidents and famous figures and broken shards of stained glass found in cathedral closes. In later years, these fragments would be embedded in the windows of the West Room of his famous library.
皮尔庞特一生中很少对理论探索产生过学术好奇心或表现出天赋，但在哥廷根，他的数学最为出色。在粗犷顽皮的外表下，他对艺术颇为敏感。他还收集总统和名人的签名，以及在大教堂庭院里捡到的彩色玻璃碎片。后来，这些碎片被镶嵌在他那座著名图书馆西屋的窗户上。

Junius Morgan feared his son’s hot temper and moaned to friends, “I don’t know what in the world I’m going to do with Pierpont.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191890) He said the boy needed “restraining” and tried to inculcate a strong sense of responsibility.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192045) When Pierpont was twenty-one, Junius told him he was “the only one \[the family\] could look to for counsel and direction should I be taken from them . . . I wish to impress upon you the necessity of preparation for such responsibilities—have them ever in view, be ready to assume & fulfill them whenever they shall be laid upon you.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192212) Weighty injunctions for a young man.
朱尼厄斯·摩根担心儿子脾气火爆，向朋友哀叹道：“我真不知道拿皮尔庞特怎么办才好。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2191890) 他说这孩子需要“约束”，并试图向他灌输强烈的责任感。 [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192045) 皮尔庞特二十一岁时，朱尼厄斯告诉他，若自己不幸离世，他将是“家族唯一可以指望寻求建议和指引的人……我希望让你明白，为承担这些责任做好准备是必要的——要时刻牢记于心，随时准备承担并履行它们。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192212) 对于一个年轻人来说，这是何其沉重的嘱托。

After Pierpont started work at Duncan, Sherman during the panic year of 1857, he displayed awesome but unsettling precocity. While visiting New Orleans in 1859, he entered into a rash, unauthorized speculation. He gambled the firm’s capital on a boatload of Brazilian coffee that had arrived in port without a buyer. He bought the entire shipment and resold it at a quick profit. This first proof of his supreme confidence petrified the gray men of Duncan, Sherman. It was probably on the basis of this incident that the firm refused to make Pierpont a partner. In 1861, he struck off on his own, forming J. P. Morgan and Company at 54 Exchange Place with his cousin James J. Goodwin. At age twenty-four, he was now New York agent for George Peabody and Company. (This J. P. Morgan and Company would be short-lived. The name would be revived in 1895.) A photo of Pierpont from this period shows he had lost his look of teenage frivolity. He was now burly and handsome, with handlebar mustache, full lips, and an intense gaze. Unlike his father’s composed look, his already seemed restless.
皮尔庞特在 1857 年恐慌年进入邓肯-谢尔曼公司工作后，便展现出令人惊叹却又不安的早熟。1859 年他前往新奥尔良时，进行了一次轻率且未经授权的投机——他擅自将公司资金押注在一批抵港后无人问津的巴西咖啡上。他买下整批货物并迅速转手获利。这份首次昭示的极度自信令邓肯-谢尔曼公司的老成们目瞪口呆。很可能正是基于此事，公司拒绝让皮尔庞特成为合伙人。1861 年，他自立门户，在交易广场 54 号与表兄詹姆斯·J·古德温共同创办了 J.P.摩根公司。二十四岁的他此时已成为乔治·皮博迪公司在纽约的代理人（这个 J.P.摩根公司存在时间不长，该名号于 1895 年重新启用）。这一时期皮尔庞特的照片显示，他脸上已褪去少年的轻浮神色。 他现在身材魁梧，相貌英俊，留着八字胡，嘴唇饱满，目光犀利。与他父亲沉稳的神情不同，他的目光已经显得躁动不安。

An important part of Pierpont’s duties in New York was supplying his father with political and financial intelligence. Merchant banks required news about government financings or the credit of client companies and placed a premium on such information. The Rothschilds had a celebrated covey of carrier pigeons and courier boats at Folkestone. In a famous lament, Talleyrand sighed, “The English ministry is always informed of everything by Rothschilds ten to twelve hours before Lord Stuart’s dispatches arrive.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192365)
皮尔庞特在纽约的一项重要职责，就是为父亲提供政治和金融情报。商业银行需要关于政府融资或客户公司信用状况的消息，并对这类信息格外看重。罗斯柴尔德家族在福克斯通拥有一支著名的信鸽队和快艇船队。塔列朗曾发出一声著名的叹息：“斯图亚特勋爵的公文到达之前十到十二个小时，英国政府总是已经被罗斯柴尔德家族告知了一切。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192365)

Pierpont began drafting lengthy letters to his father, outlining political and economic conditions in America and posting them on Nassau Street. He reserved Tuesday and Friday evenings for these reports. For thirty-three years, Junius not only digested them but bound them, like sacred relics, and set them on his shelf. Whether less sentimental than Junius—or else aghast at their contents—Pierpont burned the collection in 1911, twenty-one years after his father’s death.
皮尔庞特开始给父亲写长信，概述美国的政治和经济状况，并张贴在拿骚街。他专挑周二和周五的晚上撰写这些报告。三十三年来，朱尼厄斯不仅仔细阅读这些信件，还像对待圣物一样将它们装订成册，放在书架上。或许是不及朱尼厄斯那般多愁善感，又或许是被其中的内容吓到——皮尔庞特在 1911 年，即父亲去世二十一年后，烧毁了这批收藏。

For these thirty-three years, Junius and Pierpont had an intense relationship, despite the geographical distance. They managed to spend an enormous amount of time together: in the fall of each year, Junius made an annual trip to the United States of up to three months, and in the spring Pierpont made his ritual London pilgrimage. But their separation at other times of the year only heightened Junius’s anxiety that he couldn’t tame his son’s wayward nature. He pumped the poor boy full of endless advice and was full of maxims. No aspect of Pierpont’s life was too trivial to be overlooked. “You are altogether too rapid in disposing of your meals,” he told him. “You can have no health if you go on in this way.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192526)
这三十三年里，尽管相隔万里，朱尼厄斯和皮尔庞特的关系却异常紧密。他们设法共度了大量时光：每年秋天，朱尼厄斯都会前往美国，一住就是三个月；春天，皮尔庞特则雷打不动地前往伦敦朝圣。然而，一年中其他时间的分离只会加剧朱尼厄斯的焦虑——他担心自己无法驯服儿子那任性的天性。于是，他拼命给这个可怜的孩子灌输无尽的建议，满口都是人生箴言。皮尔庞特生活的方方面面，事无巨细，他都要过问。“你吃饭太快了，”他告诉儿子，“再这样下去，身体会出问题的。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192526)

During the Civil War, Pierpont confirmed his father’s fears concerning his rashness. Amid a mad rush of Wall Street profiteering, Pierpont financed a deal in 1861 that, if not unscrupulous, showed a decided lack of judgment. One Arthur M. Eastman purchased five thousand obsolete Hall carbines, then stored at a government armory in New York, for \$3.50 apiece. Pierpont loaned \$20,000 to a Simon Stevens, who bought them for \$11.50 each. By “rifling” these smooth-bore weapons, Stevens increased their range and accuracy. He resold them to Major General John C. Fremont, then commander of the Union forces in Missouri, for \$22 each. Within a three-month period, the government had bought back its own, now altered, rifles at six times their original price. And it was all financed by). Pierpont Morgan.
美国内战期间，皮尔庞特果然印证了父亲对他冲动鲁莽的担忧。在华尔街投机热潮的疯狂裹挟下，皮尔庞特于 1861 年为一笔交易提供了资金——这笔交易即使算不上不择手段，也明显缺乏判断力。当时，一位名叫亚瑟·M·伊斯特曼的人以每支 3.5 美元的价格买下了五千支存放在纽约政府军械库中的过时霍尔卡宾枪。皮尔庞特借给西蒙·史蒂文斯两万美元，后者又以每支 11.5 美元的价格将这些枪买下。史蒂文斯给这些滑膛枪加装了来复线，提升了射程和精度，然后以每支 22 美元的价格转卖给了时任密苏里州联邦军指挥官的约翰·C·弗里蒙特少将。短短三个月内，政府又以原价六倍的价格买回了这批已被改装的步枪——而这一切的背后资金，正是由皮尔庞特·摩根提供的。

The extent of Pierpont’s culpability in the Hall carbine affair has been endlessly debated. The unarguable point is that he saw the Civil War as an occasion for profit, not service—though he had an alternative role model in his grandfather, the Reverend Pierpont, who served as a chaplain for the Union army when it was camped on the Potomac. Like other well-to-do young men, Pierpont paid a stand-in \$300 to take his place when he was drafted after Gettysburg—a common, if inequitable, practice that contributed to draft riots in July 1863. (A future president, Grover Cleveland, also hired a stand-in, although he had a widowed mother to support.) In later years, Pierpont would humorously refer to his proxy as “the other Pierpont Morgan,” and he subsidized the man. During the war, he also leapt into wild speculation in the infamous “gold room” at the corner of William Street and Exchange Place. Prices would gyrate with each new victory or defeat for the Union army. Pierpont and an associate tried to rig the market by shipping out a large amount of gold on a steamer and earned \$160,000 in the process.
关于皮尔庞特在霍尔卡宾枪事件中应承担多少责任，人们一直争论不休。但无可争议的是，他把内战视为牟利的机会，而非效力的时机——尽管他的祖父、牧师皮尔庞特才是他该效仿的榜样，这位老人在联邦军驻扎波托马克河时曾担任随军牧师。像其他富裕的年轻人一样，葛底斯堡战役后皮尔庞特被征召入伍时，他花了 300 美元雇人顶替——这种虽不公平却普遍的做法，成为了 1863 年 7 月征兵暴动的导火索之一。（后来的总统格罗弗·克利夫兰也曾雇人顶替，尽管他还要赡养寡母。）晚年时，皮尔庞特常幽默地称自己的替身为“另一个皮尔庞特·摩根”，并一直资助此人。战争期间，他还一头扎进了威廉街与交易广场拐角处臭名昭著的“黄金交易所”，进行疯狂投机。联邦军的每一次胜败都让金价随之剧烈波动。 皮尔庞特和一个同伙试图通过用轮船运出大量黄金来操纵市场，并从中赚取了 16 万美元。

If Pierpont seemed corrupted by rowdy wartime Wall Street, he could also be unexpectedly tenderhearted. In 1861, the year of the Hall Carbine Affair, Pierpont, then twenty-four years old, had a quixotic love affair with Amelia Sturges, a frail girl with oval face and hair parted down the middle whom Pierpont had known for two years. Her father was a patron of the Hudson River school of artists, and her mother was an excellent pianist. When Pierpont wed Mimi in the parlor of her family’s East Fourteenth Street townhouse, she already had a terminal case of tuberculosis. Pierpont had to carry Mimi downstairs and prop her up during the ceremony. Guests watched this vignette from a distance, through an open door. After the ceremony, Pierpont carried his bride to a waiting carriage.
如果说皮尔庞特被战时华尔街的粗野风气所腐蚀，那他有时也会出人意料地心软。1861 年，也就是霍尔卡宾枪事件发生的那一年，24 岁的皮尔庞特与阿米莉亚·斯特奇斯展开了一段近乎痴情的恋情。这位面容娇弱、鹅蛋脸、中分头发的姑娘，皮尔庞特已认识两年。她的父亲是哈德逊河画派艺术家的赞助人，母亲则是一位出色的钢琴家。当皮尔庞特在米米家东十四街联排别墅的客厅里与她成婚时，她已身患晚期肺结核。婚礼上，皮尔庞特不得不抱着米米下楼，并在仪式中扶着她站立。宾客们隔着敞开的门，远远地目睹了这一幕。婚礼结束后，皮尔庞特将新娘抱上一辆等候的马车。

They had a touching if bizarre honeymoon, Pierpont toting Mimi around the warm Mediterranean ports and hoping to restore her health. When she died in Nice four months later, Pierpont was inconsolable, and his pious adoration for her never ceased. When he afterwards bought his first painting, it was of a young fey woman, and he hung it in an honored place over his mantle. The experience with Mimi may have taught Pierpont the wrong lessons—a fear of his best impulses, a need to stifle his deep-seated romanticism. Beneath their straitlaced exteriors, the Morgans would always be a sentimental clan, their public reserve often warring with powerful private emotions. Over fifty years later, Pierpont in his will bequeathed \$100,000 to endow a rest home for consumptives, called the Amelia Sturges Morgan Memorial. Even his son, Jack, would regard the memory of Mimi as sacred and to be discussed only in hushed tones.
他们度过了一段感人至深却又略显奇特的蜜月——皮尔庞特带着咪咪在地中海温暖的海港间游历，希望帮她恢复健康。四个月后，她在尼斯离世，皮尔庞特悲痛欲绝，此后对她始终怀着一份虔诚的爱慕。日后他买下第一幅画时，画的是一位纤弱的年轻女子，他将画悬于壁炉上方最尊贵的位置。与咪咪的这段经历或许让皮尔庞特学会了错误的教训——对内心最美好的冲动产生畏惧，不得不压抑自己根深蒂固的浪漫情怀。摩根家族的人表面上一本正经，骨子里却始终是多情之人，他们公开场合的克制往往与内心的强烈情感交战。五十多年后，皮尔庞特在遗嘱中留下十万美元，为肺病患者设立了一家疗养院，命名为"阿米莉亚·斯特奇斯·摩根纪念疗养院"。就连他的儿子杰克，也视咪咪的记忆为神圣之物，只宜低声谈起。

Observing his son’s reckless dealings and startling choice of a wife, Junius decided to take Pierpont’s life in hand. Between Pierpont and Junius Morgan, there would be total loyalty but also a fierce contest of wills. In 1864, Junius orchestrated an alliance between Pierpont, then twenty-seven, and Charles H. Dabney, thirty years his elder, to form the new firm of Dabney, Morgan and Company. Bolstered by capital from Junius, it would serve as his New York agent. He would retain final control over the credits it issued and the clients it selected. Dabney was expected to exert a steadying influence on Pierpont, and for the next twenty-six years Junius kept a moderating father figure near his son.
看到儿子皮尔庞特在生意场上鲁莽行事，又惊世骇俗地选了那样一位妻子，朱尼厄斯决定亲自掌控儿子的人生。父子之间，既要有绝对的忠诚，也免不了一场意志的激烈交锋。1864 年，朱尼厄斯撮合当时 27 岁的皮尔庞特与年长他 30 岁的查尔斯·H·达布尼联手，成立了新公司“达布尼-摩根公司”。有了朱尼厄斯的资金撑腰，这家公司将成为他在纽约的代理机构。而他本人则保留对公司所发信贷和所择客户的最终决定权。达布尼被指望能对皮尔庞特起到稳重的引导作用——此后二十六年里，朱尼厄斯始终让一位能起安抚作用的父辈人物守在儿子身边。

In his private life, too, Pierpont fell into line. In May 1865, he married Frances Louisa Tracy—Fanny, as she was known—daughter of a successful lawyer, Charles Tracy, who later performed legal work for Pierpont. She was tall and pretty, with a rosebud mouth. She had a taste for elegant gloves and earrings and seemed thoroughly safe and respectable. If Mimi was a temporary madness, Frances was a return to sanity. Yet it was Mimi whose memory Pierpont would cherish, while the “practical” marriage to Fanny would prove the fiasco, causing terrible pain to them both. Pierpont’s unrequited romantic longings would only grow over the years until they later found other—and notoriously varied—outlets.
皮尔庞特的私生活也同样循规蹈矩。1865 年 5 月，他娶了弗朗西斯·路易莎·特雷西——人们都叫她范妮——一位成功律师查尔斯·特雷西的女儿，后者后来为皮尔庞特处理法律事务。她高挑漂亮，嘴唇像玫瑰花蕾，偏爱精致的手套和耳环，看起来完全稳妥体面。如果说米米是一时的疯狂，那么弗朗西斯就是回归理智。然而，皮尔庞特珍藏的记忆却是关于米米的，而与范妮那场“务实”的婚姻最终成了彻底的失败，给两人都带来了巨大的痛苦。皮尔庞特那无法回应的浪漫渴望随着岁月流逝只增不减，直到后来找到了其他——而且出了名地多样化——的宣泄途径。

THE father-son team of Junius and Pierpont Morgan came on the world banking scene at a time of phenomenal expansion of banking power. We shall call it the Baronial Age. It coincided with the rise of railroads and heavy industry, new businesses requiring capital far beyond the resources of even the wealthiest individuals or families. Yet, despite these tremendous needs for capital, financial markets were provincial and limited in scope. The banker allocated the economy’s scarce credit. His imprimatur alone reassured investors that unknown companies were sound—there were no government agencies to regulate securities issues or prospectuses—and he became integral to their operation. Companies would come to be associated with their bankers. The New York Central Railroad, for instance, would later be called a Morgan road.
朱尼厄斯·摩根和皮尔庞特·摩根这对父子在银行业权力空前扩张的时代登上了世界舞台。我们不妨称这个时代为“豪绅时代”。彼时，铁路与重工业蓬勃兴起，这些新型企业所需的资本之巨，即便最富有的个人或家族也难以承担。然而，尽管资本需求如此巨大，金融市场却仍局限于地方，规模有限。银行家掌控着经济体中稀缺的信贷资源。仅凭他们的认可，就能让投资者相信那些陌生的公司是可靠的——当时并无政府机构监管证券发行或招股说明书——银行家因此成为企业运营中不可或缺的一环。公司往往与其银行家紧密相连。例如，纽约中央铁路后来就被称为“摩根铁路”。

In this phase of the Industrial Revolution, companies were dynamic but extremely unstable. In an atmosphere of feverish growth, many businesses fell into the hands of unscrupulous promoters, charlatans, and stock manipulators. Even visionary entrepreneurs often lacked the managerial skills necessary to convert their inspirations into national industries, and no cadre of professional managers yet existed. Bankers had to vouch for securities and often ended up running companies if they defaulted. As the Baronial Age progressed, the line between finance and commerce would blur until much of industry passed under the control of the bankers.
在这一阶段的工业革命中，企业充满活力却也极不稳定。在狂热的增长氛围下，许多公司落入了不择手段的发起人、江湖骗子和股票操纵者之手。即便是有远见的企业家，往往也缺乏将灵感转化为全国性产业所需的管理技能，而专业经理人阶层当时尚未形成。银行家不得不为证券背书，一旦企业违约，他们往往亲自接手运营。随着贵族时代不断演进，金融与商业之间的界限逐渐模糊，直到大部分产业落入银行家的掌控之中。

With such leverage over companies, the leading bankers developed a superior style, behaving like barons to whom clients paid tribute. They operated according to a set of customs that we will call the Gentleman Banker’s Code. The House of Morgan would not only transplant this code from London to New York but would honor it until well into the twentieth century. Under this code, banks did not try to scout out business or seek new clients but waited for clients to arrive with proper introductions. They didn’t open branch offices and refused to take on new companies unless the move was first cleared with their former banker. The idea was not to compete, at least not too openly. This meant no advertising, no price competition, and no raiding of other firms’ clients. Such an arrangement worked to the advantage of established banks and kept clients in an abject, dependent position. But it was a stylized competition—a world of sheathed rapiers—not a cartel, as it often seemed. The elegance of the surface often blinded critics to the vicious underlying relations among the banks.
凭借对企业的这种掌控力，顶级银行家们形成了高人一等的姿态，如同贵族般接受客户的朝贡。他们遵循一套可称为"绅士银行家准则"的行规行事——摩根财团不仅将这套准则从伦敦移植到纽约，更是恪守至二十世纪中叶。根据这套准则，银行不会主动招揽业务或寻找新客户，而是静候持推荐函的客户登门；不开设分行；若未事先征得原合作银行的同意，也绝不接手新公司。核心理念就是避免竞争，至少不能过于明目张胆。这意味着不登广告、不搞价格战、不挖其他银行的客户。这种安排既有利于老牌银行，也使得客户始终处于卑微依附的地位。但这实则是一种优雅的竞争——如同藏锋的剑术较量，而非表面看去那般像个垄断联盟。 表面的优雅往往让批评者忽视了银行之间暗藏的恶性关系。

No less than to industry, bankers dictated terms to sovereign states, and countries, like companies, had their “traditional bankers.” Benjamin Disraeli wrote of “the mighty loan-mongers on whose fiat the fate of kings and empires sometimes depended.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192681) Byron’s witty couplet claimed their “every loan . . . seats a Nation or upsets a Throne.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192856) The bankers acquired such power because many governments in wartime lacked the sophisticated tax machinery to sustain the fighting. Merchant banks functioned as their ersatz treasury departments or central banks before economic management was established as a government responsibility. The London banks didn’t lend their own funds but would organize large-scale bond issues. Through conspiring closely with governments, they acquired a quasi-official aura. Joseph Wechsberg has referred to merchant banks operating “in the twilight zone between politics and economics.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193020) This was turf the Morgans would later claim as their own. It was also very lucrative turf, for bankers to sovereign states might also handle their foreign-exchange transactions and pay out dividends on their bonds.
银行业不仅控制着企业，甚至还向主权国家发号施令，各国也如同公司一样，拥有自己的"专属银行家"。本杰明·迪斯雷利曾写道："那些强大的放贷者，他们的意志有时决定着国王和帝国的命运。" [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192681) 拜伦的诙谐对句则称，他们"每放一笔贷款……就能扶植一个国家或颠覆一个王位"。 [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2192856) 银行家之所以能获得如此权力，是因为战时许多政府缺乏完善的税收机制来支撑战争开支。在经济管理尚未成为政府职责之前，商业银行充当了替代性的财政部或中央银行。伦敦的银行并非动用自有资金放贷，而是组织大规模债券发行。通过与政府密切合作，它们获得了一种半官方的光环。约瑟夫·韦克斯伯格称商业银行"在政治与经济的灰色地带"运作。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193020) 而这片领地，日后将成为摩根家族的专属舞台。 这也是非常有利可图的地盘，因为向主权国家提供服务的银行家还可能处理他们的外汇交易并支付他们的债券股息。

Every London house could unfurl a scroll of illustrious state loans. From their Saint Swithin’s Lane townhouse, the Rothschilds financed Wellington’s peninsular campaign and the Crimean War. A familiar adage said that the wealth of the Rothschilds consisted of the bankruptcy of nations. In 1875, Lionel Rothschild would arrange the £4-million financing that permitted Britain to wrest control of the Suez Canal from France. Disraeli laughingly confided to Queen Victoria, “I am of the opinion, Madame, that there never can be too many Rothschilds.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193155)
每家伦敦的商行都能展出一份辉煌的国家贷款清单。Rothschilds 家族从他们位于 Saint Swithin’s Lane 的宅邸，资助了 Wellington 的半岛战役和 Crimean War。有句老话说，Rothschilds 的财富来自各国的破产。1875 年，Lionel Rothschild 安排了 400 万英镑的融资，让英国得以从法国手中夺走 Suez Canal 的控制权。Disraeli 笑着对 Queen Victoria 吐露心声：“陛下，我认为 Rothschilds 再多也不嫌多。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193155)

Besides bankrolling the Louisiana Purchase, Barings financed the French indemnity payment after Waterloo, prompting a lapidary tribute from the due de Richelieu: “There are six great powers in Europe: England, France, Prussia, Austria, Russia, and Baring Brothers.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193303) After the failure of Ireland’s potato crop in 1845, the Peel government used Barings to buy American corn and Indian meal to relieve the famine—so-called Peel’s brimstone. By the time of the Civil War, Barings was the agent bank for Russia, Norway, Austria, Chile, Argentina, Canada, Australia, and the United States. For their trouble, the grandees at 8 Bishops-gate were awarded with four peerages by the close of the nineteenth century—Ashburton, Northbrook, Revelstoke, and Cromer.
除了为路易斯安那购地案提供资金外，巴林银行还在滑铁卢战役后为法国的赔款支付提供融资，这引来了黎塞留公爵的一句精辟评价：“欧洲有六大强权：英国、法国、普鲁士、奥地利、俄罗斯，以及巴林兄弟公司。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193303) 1845 年爱尔兰马铃薯歉收后，皮尔政府利用巴林银行购买美国玉米和印度粗粮来缓解饥荒——这也就是所谓的“皮尔硫磺”。到了美国内战时期，巴林银行已成为俄罗斯、挪威、奥地利、智利、阿根廷、加拿大、澳大利亚和美国的代理银行。因其卓越贡献，比晓普斯盖特 8 号的这些权贵在 19 世纪末共获得了四个贵族封号——阿什伯顿、诺斯布鲁克、雷韦尔斯托克和克罗默。

Why this perfect mesh between merchant banks and statecraft? As private partnerships, these small banks were free of prying depositors or shareholders and could indulge their political biases. They didn’t have to submit to outside examination, and their naturally discreet style made them ideal channels for diplomacy. Because they financed overseas trade, they were far more internationalist in outlook than the High Street bankers who financed British industry and dealt largely with shopkeepers.
为什么商业银行与治国之道能如此完美契合？作为私人合伙企业，这些小银行无需面对多管闲事的储户或股东，可以尽情施展其政治倾向。它们不必接受外部审查，天生谨慎的风格使其成为外交渠道的理想选择。由于为海外贸易提供融资，它们的视野远比那些主要为英国工业融资、整天与店主打交道的街边银行家更具国际主义色彩。

The rarefied world of the Rothschilds and the Barings was the one Junius Morgan aspired to—a world hitherto barred to Americans. After Peabody’s death, he needed some dazzling derring-do with which to leap into the top ranks of Victorian finance. Only so much glory could be gained from trading Chinese tea or Peruvian guano or selling iron rails to Commodore Vanderbilt. Now in his late fifties, Junius had grown stout with wealth. He was an imposing six-foot figure, with high forehead, beetling brow, and watchful eyes. As an early American patron of Savile Row’s “bespoke” tailors, he dressed in suits conservatively tailored by Poole’s.
罗斯柴尔德家族和巴林家族那种精英阶层，正是朱尼厄斯·摩根心向往之的世界——一个此前对美国关闭大门的世界。皮博迪去世后，他需要一些令人惊叹的大胆举动，才能跃入维多利亚时代金融界的顶尖行列。单靠贩卖中国茶叶、秘鲁鸟粪，或者向范德比尔特准将出售铁轨，所能赢得的荣耀终究有限。年近六旬的朱尼厄斯已因财富而身形发福。他身高六英尺，气宇轩昂，天庭饱满，眉骨高耸，目光如炬。作为萨维尔街定制西装的早期美国主顾，他身着普尔缝制的保守剪裁西装，尽显考究。

With Peabody gone, he urgently needed to replenish his capital base, which was still meager compared to the Rothschilds and the Barings. Yet he was extremely selective about the business he did and had learned the need for caution. As he lectured Pierpont, “Never under any circumstances do an action which could be called in question if known to the world.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193467)
皮博迪离开后，他急需补充自己的资本基础——尽管与罗斯柴尔德家族和巴林家族相比，这点资本仍然微不足道。然而，他对经手的生意极其挑剔，并且早已学会谨慎行事。他曾这样教导皮尔庞特：“无论在任何情况下，都不要去做一件一旦被世人知晓就会招致质疑的事情。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193467)

Junius’s big chance for a state financing came in 1870, when the Prussians crushed French troops at Sedan in September, seized the emperor, Napoleon III, and laid siege to Paris. After a republic was proclaimed, French officials retreated to Tours and set up a provisional government. Otto von Bismarck, the Prussian chancellor, tried to isolate the French diplomatically. When they approached London for financing, he conducted a propaganda campaign, blustering that a victorious Germany would make France repudiate its debt.
朱尼厄斯在国家融资方面的重大机遇出现在 1870 年。当年 9 月，普鲁士军队在色当击溃法国军队，俘虏了皇帝拿破仑三世，并包围了巴黎。法国宣布成立共和国后，政府官员撤退到图尔，建立了临时政府。普鲁士首相奥托·冯·俾斯麦试图在外交上孤立法国。当法国向伦敦寻求融资时，俾斯麦发起了一场宣传攻势，扬言胜利的德国将迫使法国拒绝偿还债务。

A rare opportunity opened up for an enterprising banker. This was one of the few times in the century that financially self-sufficient France needed to raise money abroad. Barings had floated Prussian loans and didn’t wish to upset delicate relations by dealing with France; the Rothschilds dismissed the French cause as hopeless. The City had lately been rocked by defaults in Mexico and Venezuela, and nobody was in a particularly venturesome mood for foreign loans. Enter Junius, who decided to float a syndicated issue for France of £10 million, or \$50 million. The French hoped that by using an American banker, they might also be better positioned to purchase American arms.
一位富有进取心的银行家迎来了一个难得的机会。这是百年间为数不多的几次，财政自给自足的法国需要向海外筹集资金。巴林银行此前已经承销了普鲁士的贷款，不愿因与法国打交道而破坏微妙的关系；罗斯柴尔德家族则认为法国的事业毫无希望，直接将其拒之门外。那时的伦敦金融城刚被墨西哥和委内瑞拉的违约事件搅得天翻地覆，没人对海外贷款有多大冒险的兴致。这时，朱尼厄斯出手了，他决定为法国发行一笔 1000 万英镑（合 5000 万美元）的银团贷款。法国人也希望，通过与一位美国银行家合作，他们或许能更顺利地购买美国军火。

The French loan showed that he hid a riverboat gambler’s flair behind the steely air. This would be Junius’s signature deal, complete with that obligatory Rothschild touch—carrier pigeons. In backing France, he had to contend with Bismarck, who was privy to his moves. It later turned out that the private secretary of the French finance minister was a German spy and was feeding Bismarck daily reports on their dealings. Because Junius couldn’t speak French and wouldn’t take anything on faith, he brought over from France his son-in-law and later partner Walter Hayes Burns to act as translator. Junius insisted that every French document be accompanied by a certified translation.
这笔法国贷款显示，在他钢铁般的外表下，隐藏着一种密西西比河赌徒般的气质。这将成为朱尼厄斯的标志性交易，并附带罗斯柴尔德家族标志性的手笔——信鸽。在支持法国时，他不得不与俾斯麦周旋，而俾斯麦对他的动向心知肚明。后来发现，法国财政大臣的私人大秘竟是德国间谍，每天都在向俾斯麦汇报他们的交易细节。由于朱尼厄斯不会说法语，也不愿轻信任何未经核实的信息，他便从法国带来了女婿——后来也成为他的合伙人——沃尔特·海耶斯·伯恩斯担任翻译。朱尼厄斯坚持每份法语文件都必须附有经认证的翻译件。

An innovation in European finance was then enhancing the bankers’ power—the syndicate, elite groups of banks that practiced what the French called *haute banque.* Instead of floating bond issues alone, the banks pooled their capital to share the risk of underwriting. Reflecting the extraordinary risks of the French loan, a Morgan-led syndicate offered the bonds at 85. This was 15 points below par—the value at which the bonds could later be redeemed. This sharp discount was designed to coax a skittish public into buying. The French felt blackmailed by these degrading terms, which they thought suitable for a Peru or Turkey. Yet Junius hadn’t exaggerated the risks. After Paris fell in January 1871, followed by the Paris Commune, the bonds dropped from 80 to 55, and Junius desperately bought them to prop up the price, nearly wiping himself out. This was all very strange for a man who had urged caution on Pierpont: he was betting the future of his firm on one roll of the dice.
此时，欧洲金融界的一项创新正在增强银行家的权力——那就是由精英银行组成的辛迪加，即法国人所谓的“高级银行业”。银行不再单独发行债券，而是汇集资本，共同承担承销风险。为了应对法国贷款的巨大风险，摩根领导的辛迪加以 85 折的价格发行这批债券，这比面值低了 15 个百分点——即债券日后可以赎回的价值。如此大幅度的折价，意在打动惶恐不安的公众，让他们愿意购买。法国人觉得这种屈辱的条款无异于勒索，认为这种条件只配给秘鲁或土耳其。然而，朱尼厄斯并未夸大风险。1871 年 1 月巴黎沦陷后，紧接着巴黎公社爆发，债券价格从 80 跌至 55，朱尼厄斯拼命买入以托住价格，几乎将自己拖垮。对于一个劝诫皮尔庞特要谨慎的人来说，这着实奇怪——他竟把自己的整个公司的未来押在了一次孤注一掷上。

Whatever the risks, it must have been a heady experience for an American to be swaggering like a Rothschild and playing with gigantic sums. The loan had its full complement of theatrics. A brief Morgan Guaranty history still pulsates with the excitement of the episode: “Some communications between Paris and London were implemented by the use of a fleet of carrier pigeons. Several of them, bearing capsules filled with text on tissue paper, actually completed their journeys. One particularly bulky package of documents was sent from Paris to London by balloon! ”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193621) Some pigeons were apparently shot down and gobbled up by starving Parisians. This left French politicians in the dark during critical moments in the bargaining.
无论风险多大，对一个美国人来说，像罗斯柴尔德家族那样趾高气扬地挥霍巨额资金，必定是一次令人陶醉的体验。这笔贷款本身也充满了戏剧性。摩根担保信托公司的一段简短历史至今仍能让人感受到当时的激动氛围：“巴黎和伦敦之间的一些通信是通过一群信鸽实现的。其中几只鸽子，携带着装有薄纸文件的胶囊，竟然成功完成了旅程。还有一包特别厚重的文件是通过气球从巴黎送到伦敦的！” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193621) 显然，有些鸽子被击落并落入了饥饿的巴黎市民之口。这使得法国谈判代表在关键时刻对情况一无所知。

When the war ended, the defeated French didn’t renege on the loan, as Bismarck predicted. Instead, they prepaid the bonds in 1873, bringing them up to par, or 100. As with Peabody and his Maryland bonds, Junius pocketed a fortune from this sudden windfall. The loan netted him a whopping £1.5 million. This vastly augmented his firm’s capital and propelled him into the upper ranks of government financing. Now the name J. S. Morgan and Company would appear frequently in “tombstone ads” (apparently so called because of their rectangular shape and placement on newspaper obituary pages) announcing underwriting syndicates.
战争结束后，战败的法国并没有像俾斯麦所预料的那样赖账，反而在 1873 年提前赎回了债券，使其恢复到面值，即 100。正如皮博迪与他的马里兰债券一样，朱尼厄斯从这笔意外之财中赚取了巨额利润。这笔贷款让他净赚了惊人的 150 万英镑，极大地扩充了他的公司资本，并将其推入政府融资的高层行列。从此，J. S. 摩根公司的名字将频繁出现在宣布承销银团的“墓碑广告”（据说得名于其矩形形状和刊登在报纸讣告页上的位置）中。

George Smalley said that with the 1870 French loan, his friend Junius went from being a successful man to a power in the City. His impressions of Junius at this moment are telling. On the one hand, he was modest and breezily dismissive about his triumph. He said he had researched the history of twelve French governments since 1789, and “not one of these governments had ever repudiated or questioned the validity of any financial obligation contracted by any other. The continuing financial solidarity of France was unbroken.” But Smalley wasn’t fooled by such nonchalance. He noted “a fire in his eyes as he spoke which showed he was not insensible to the triumph he had won. Why should he be? It was considered, and has ever since been considered, an event in the history of English finance.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2193814)
George Smalley 表示，凭借 1870 年的法国贷款，他的朋友 Junius 从一个成功人士一跃成为伦敦金融城的权势人物。他对 Junius 此时状态的印象颇为深刻。一方面，Junius 对自己的成就表现得谦虚且轻描淡写。他说自己研究过 1789 年以来十二届法国政府的历史，“这些政府中，没有一届曾拒绝承认或质疑前任政府所承担的任何金融义务的有效性。法国金融的持续团结从未中断。”但 Smalley 并未被这种淡然所迷惑。他注意到“他说话时眼中闪烁的光芒，表明他对自己取得的胜利并非无动于衷。他又何须如此？这件事在当时——而且自那以后——一直被视作英国金融史上的一桩大事。”

As Junius developed into the wealthiest American banker in London, he acquired the trappings of magnificence. He lived in a Knightsbridge mansion, 13 Princes Gate, a five-story building of neoclassic design facing the south side of Hyde Park. The Morgan household was very dignified. Attended by butlers, the family dressed formally for dinner, which concluded with claret and Havana cigars. It was also a pious place, with Junius lining up the servants each morning for prayers. Following merchant-banking tradition, Junius dabbled in art collecting and often visited galleries with Pierpont when his son was in town. Junius’s friends said his home resembled a museum, with sixteenth-century Spanish embroidery on the walls, silver-filled vaults, and an excellent collection of paintings by Reynolds, Romney, and Gainsborough.
当朱尼乌斯成为伦敦最富有的美国银行家后，他的生活也尽显奢华。他住在骑士桥的王子门 13 号——一栋五层新古典主义风格的豪宅，正对海德公园南侧。摩根府邸气派非凡，家中仆从成群，家人们晚宴时身着正装，佐以红葡萄酒和哈瓦那雪茄。这里也是个虔敬之地，朱尼乌斯每天早晨会召集仆人们一同祈祷。遵循商业银行家的传统，朱尼乌斯也涉足艺术收藏，每当儿子皮尔庞特来伦敦时，父子俩便常去画廊赏画。朱尼乌斯的朋友们说，他的宅邸宛如一座博物馆：墙上挂着 16 世纪的西班牙刺绣，地窖里堆满银器，还珍藏了雷诺兹、罗姆尼和庚斯博罗的精美画作。

Seven miles away, in the London suburb of Roehampton, Junius purchased Dover House, a ninety-two-acre estate with rolling lawns that swept down to the Thames. It was a miniature kingdom. Its dairy flowed with fresh milk and cream, its hothouses yielded blooms, gardeners tended strawberry beds, and children played on playground swings. Dover House was rustic in a formal way, with well-spaced trees and trimmed lawns. In a photograph from 1876, Junius is playing tennis dressed in bowler hat and a three-piece suit and is clutching his racket like a club; he looks incongruous in a recreational setting. Periodically he performed his patrician duty and shot pheasants on a moor.
七英里外，在伦敦郊区的罗厄汉普顿，朱尼厄斯买下了多佛庄园——一片占地九十二英亩的产业，起伏的草坪一直延伸到泰晤士河畔。那是一个微缩的王国。奶牛场里流淌着新鲜的牛奶和奶油，温室里鲜花盛放，园丁照料着草莓苗床，孩子们在秋千上玩耍。多佛庄园带着一种规整的田园风情，树木间距匀称，草坪修剪齐整。在一张 1876 年的照片中，朱尼厄斯穿着圆顶礼帽和三件套西装打网球，把球拍像棍棒一样攥在手里；置身休闲场景中，他显得格格不入。他偶尔也会履行贵族的职责，在荒野上猎射雉鸡。

Junius—tall, sociable, self-confident—and his wife, Juliet Pierpont Morgan, made an odd pair. She was a short, plain, buxom woman who grew increasingly sickly and hypochondriacal. Often homesick, she frequently sailed to New York to stay with Pierpont. While her husband blossomed into one of London’s magnificoes and was blessed with robust health, Juliet became more feeble and withdrawn. In her later years, she was an invalid, often closeted in an upstairs bedroom. She seems to have suffered some form of premature senility. This pattern of the sickly wife and the autocratic, headstrong husband would be repeated in the life of their son Pierpont. It also set a pattern of private grief and loneliness that would come to haunt the spectacularly successful Morgan family.
朱尼厄斯——身材高大、善于交际、充满自信——和他的妻子朱丽叶·皮尔庞特·摩根形成了奇怪的一对。她是个矮小、相貌平平、丰满的女人，身体越来越虚弱，还总是疑神疑鬼。她经常想家，时常乘船回纽约与皮尔庞特同住。当丈夫在伦敦成长为显赫人物、身体健壮如牛时，朱丽叶却愈发虚弱孤僻。晚年她已是个病秧子，常把自己关在楼上的卧室里。她似乎患上了某种早衰症。这种病妻与专横倔强丈夫的模式，将在他们的儿子皮尔庞特身上重演。这也为日后辉煌成功的摩根家族埋下了隐秘的伤痛与孤独的阴影。
