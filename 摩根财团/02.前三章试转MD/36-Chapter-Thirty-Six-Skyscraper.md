# Chapter Thirty-Six: Skyscraper

IN 1989, Morgan Stanley, with sixty-four hundred employees, occupied seventeen floors of the Exxon Building on Sixth Avenue—more than Exxon itself. The building was now owned by a Mitsui unit. Stepping off the elevator into the thirtieth-floor reception area, one was greeted by a portrait of Jack Morgan and glimpses of old rolltop desks salvaged by now-retired partners. In the posh dining room, with its well-spaced tables and leather armchairs, uniformed waiters served Madeira or dry sherry, but (by Morgan tradition) no hard liquor. Such touches aside, the new Morgan Stanley—bold, rich, swaggering—had little in common with the mandarin firm that started life in September 1935 in a flower-banked room at 2 Wall Street.
1989 年，摩根士丹利拥有六千四百名员工，占据了第六大道埃克森大厦十七个楼层——比埃克森公司本身还多。这栋楼当时已归三井旗下某子公司所有。踏出电梯步入三十层接待区，映入眼帘的是杰克·摩根的肖像，以及由现已退休的合伙人抢救下来的几件老式卷盖办公桌。在布置雅致的餐厅里，餐桌间距宽敞，配着皮面扶手椅，身着制服的侍者奉上马德拉酒或干雪利酒，但（按摩根传统）不供应烈酒。除却这些细节，这家大胆、富裕、趾高气扬的新摩根士丹利，与 1935 年 9 月在华尔街 2 号一间花团锦簇的房间里起家的那家儒雅商行，已相去甚远。

Instead of underwriting, Morgan Stanley now stressed takeovers and merchant banking. After the crash, it de-emphasized securities distribution and edged out dozens of managing directors, largely from the sales and trading side. In 1988, Merrill Lynch, once scorned as plebeian, led domestic underwriters for the first time as Morgan Stanley slumped to sixth place. Morgan mostly pursued junk bonds, now the most profitable form of underwriting and an indispensable adjunct to takeover work. When Drexel Burnham lost ground with the investigation into junk bond king Michael Milken, Morgan Stanley briefly emerged as the top junk bond firm in America! Did the ghosts of Pierpont, Jack, and Harry Morgan shudder?
不再侧重承销业务，摩根士丹利转而专注于并购和商人银行业务。股灾之后，它弱化了证券分销，并挤走了几十位董事总经理，其中大部分来自销售和交易部门。1988 年，曾被讥讽为平民券商的美林证券首次成为国内承销业务的领头羊，而摩根士丹利则跌至第六位。摩根士丹利此时主要追逐垃圾债券——这是当时最具盈利能力的承销业务，也是并购业务不可或缺的辅助工具。当德崇证券因对垃圾债券之王迈克尔·米尔肯的调查而元气大伤时，摩根士丹利一度成为美国垃圾债券市场的头号玩家！不知皮尔庞特、杰克和哈里的幽灵们是否会为此战栗？

Morgan Stanley was an undoubted success story, an awesome performer. For over fifty years, it had stood at or near the peak of investment banking—a claim no other firm except First Boston could make. It had survived every competitive threat. Smart, with an uncanny surefire strategic sense, it alone seemed immune to the postcrash blues on Wall Street. In 1987, it was the one publicly traded securities house to boost earnings. As if invincible, it lifted the pay of its five top executives to about the \$3-million mark, so that each made more than the chairmen at rival firms; Parker Gilbert was paid \$4.4 million in salary and bonus. It registered \$395 million in profits for 1988—an extraordinary 71-percent rise in a sluggish trading environment. At the same time, it had avoided the chronic dissension that debilitated many rivals.
摩根士丹利无疑是一个成功典范，表现卓越。五十多年来，它一直稳居投资银行业巅峰或接近巅峰的位置——除了第一波士顿，没有哪家公司能与之比肩。它经受住了每一次竞争威胁。凭借敏锐且直觉精准的战略眼光，它似乎是华尔街唯一不受股灾后低迷情绪影响的机构。1987 年，它是唯一一家盈利增长的上市证券公司。仿佛不可战胜一般，它将五位高管的薪酬提升至约 300 万美元，使得每位高管的收入都超过了竞争对手的董事长；帕克·吉尔伯特的薪资和奖金合计高达 440 万美元。1988 年，公司录得 3.95 亿美元利润——在交易环境疲软的情况下，实现了惊人的 71%增长率。与此同时，它也避免了长期内耗，而这种内耗削弱了许多竞争对手。

Yet for all its astounding success, the Morgan Stanley story was profoundly troubling. It had followed a flawless instinct for profit into ever-riskier activities with greater potential hazard for the nation’s economy. As the 1980s ended, it resembled an industrial holding company more than a financial services firm. It had stakes in forty companies with over \$7 billion in assets and seventy-two thousand employees. Morgan Stanley was suddenly a part owner of food chains and paper mills, textile plants and airplane-engine makers. These investments were reaping 40-percent returns and foreshadowed an even stronger tilt toward merchant banking and away from the trading and distribution of securities in New York, London, and Tokyo that had been the firm’s salvation in the 1980s.
尽管摩根士丹利取得了令人瞩目的成功，但其发展历程却让人深感不安。它凭借对利润近乎完美的直觉，一步步涉足风险更高的业务，给美国经济带来了更大的潜在隐患。到 20 世纪 80 年代末，它更像是一家工业控股公司，而非金融服务企业。它在 40 家公司持有股份，资产超过 70 亿美元，员工总数达 7.2 万人。摩根士丹利摇身一变，成了连锁餐饮、造纸厂、纺织厂乃至飞机发动机制造商的股东。这些投资带来了 40%的回报，也预示着它将更大幅度地向商人银行业务倾斜，逐渐远离其在纽约、伦敦和东京的证券交易与分销业务——而这正是该公司在 20 世纪 80 年代得以重生的关键所在。

In the 1970s, LBOs had been small, largely friendly deals involving stable, recession-proof companies. Now the sheer scale of institutional money mustered by LBO funds—\$25 billion in 1988, or enough to acquire \$250 billion worth of companies—created irresistible pressure to take over all kinds of companies. In striking testimony to the speculative bent of American finance, 40 percent of the loan portfolios at large Wall Street banks were going to LBOs. Through pension fund stakes in such activity, corporate America was cannibalizing itself. With so much easy money at their disposal, LBO funds turned to hostile raids and now operated by the same ruthless logic as merger work.
20 世纪 70 年代，杠杆收购规模尚小，多为涉及稳定、抗衰退企业的友好交易。而如今，LBO 基金汇聚的机构资金规模惊人——1988 年达 250 亿美元，足以收购价值 2500 亿美元的公司——这形成了接管各类企业的不可抗拒压力。华尔街大型银行 40%的贷款组合流向 LBO 项目，有力地证明了美国金融业投机成性。通过养老基金参与此类活动，美国企业界正在自我吞噬。手握如此轻易得来的资金，LBO 基金转向敌意收购，如今其运作逻辑与并购交易同样冷酷无情。

The *reductio ad absurdum* was the \$25 billion RJR Nabisco deal, the largest LBO ever. In 1985, Morgan Stanley had represented Nabisco Brands when it was being acquired by R. J. Reynolds; everybody lauded the diversification. Now, three years later, the same people spotted hidden values in busting it up. Along with Drexel Burnham, Merrill Lynch, and Wasserstein, Perella, Eric Gleacher of Morgan Stanley advised Henry Kravis, who defeated an investor group led by RJR Nabisco chief executive F. Ross Johnson and his investment banker, Shearson Lehman.
荒谬至极的是 250 亿美元的 RJR 纳贝斯克交易，这是有史以来最大的杠杆收购。1985 年，摩根士丹利曾代表纳贝斯克品牌公司被雷诺兹收购时的交易，当时所有人都称赞这种多元化。三年后，同样是这些人，却发现了拆分公司所隐藏的价值。摩根士丹利的埃里克·格莱彻与德崇证券、美林、瓦瑟斯坦及佩雷拉一起，为亨利·克拉维斯提供咨询，他击败了由 RJR 纳贝斯克首席执行官 F·罗斯·约翰逊及其投资银行希尔森·雷曼领导的投资者集团。

For a takeover that promised no economic benefit for the company, the RJR Nabisco deal showered bankers with huge rewards—almost \$1 billion in fees and expenses. Morgan Stanley came away with a cool \$25 million. Like most LBOs, it was executed almost entirely with borrowed money. In return, RJR Nabisco was burdened with over \$20 billion in debt. Before it sold a cigarette or a biscuit each year, it was already in the hole for \$3 billion in interest payments. It was forced to shoulder debt equal to the combined national debt of Bolivia, Jamaica, Uruguay, Costa Rica, and Honduras. Only ten countries in the world were more indebted. In more innocent days, investment bankers had put companies on a sound footing and jealously guarded their credit rating. Now, even as the Kravis forces toasted their victory, RJR Nabisco bondholders saw their A-rated bonds deteriorate into junk bonds, with \$1 billion in value wiped out overnight. And by the summer of 1989, the company had announced plans to fire 1,640 workers as a way to save money to service the oppressive debt burden. That fall, a collapse in the junk bond market suggested that RJR Nabisco had indeed been the era’s crowning folly.
一场对 RJR 纳贝斯克公司毫无经济利益的收购，却让银行家们赚得盆满钵满——近 10 亿美元的费用和支出落入他们囊中。摩根士丹利轻松卷走 2500 万美元。与大多数杠杆收购一样，这笔交易几乎全靠借来的钱完成。作为代价，RJR 纳贝斯克背上了超过 200 亿美元的债务。在它卖出哪怕一根香烟或一块饼干之前，每年就得先填补 30 亿美元的利息窟窿。它被迫承担的债务，相当于玻利维亚、牙买加、乌拉圭、哥斯达黎加和洪都拉斯五国的国债总和。全球只有十个国家的负债比它更高。在更为天真的年代，投资银行家们会让公司财务稳健，小心翼翼地守护它们的信用评级。如今，就在克拉维斯团队庆祝胜利之际，RJR 纳贝斯克的债券持有人眼睁睁看着 A 级债券沦为垃圾债，一夜之间蒸发掉 10 亿美元市值。 到了 1989 年夏天，公司宣布计划裁员 1640 人，以节省资金来偿还沉重的债务负担。那年秋天，垃圾债券市场的崩盘表明，RJR Nabisco 确实是那个时代最荒唐的闹剧。

In Morgan Stanley’s high-risk, high-reward LBO strategy, some observers saw the prelude to a final deal—the firm would sell out to the highest bidder. Its executives had already profited royally from the 1986 public offering, earning tens of millions of dollars apiece; now they would gain a second bonanza. According to one theory, this ultimate deal would await S. Parker Gilbert’s stepping down as chairman. As son of a J. P. Morgan partner and stepson of Harold Stanley, the prediction went, he didn’t want to be Morgan Stanley’s last chairman. The firm was also being rent by turf battles, especially between Dick Fisher and Bob Greenhill, and top people wearied of the infighting.
在摩根士丹利高风险、高回报的杠杆收购策略中，一些观察者看到了最终交易的序幕——该公司将出售给出价最高者。其高管们已从 1986 年的公开上市中获利丰厚，每人赚得数千万美元；如今他们将迎来第二次财富盛宴。根据一种说法，这项终极交易将等待 S. Parker Gilbert 卸任董事长。作为 J. P. Morgan 合伙人之子、Harold Stanley 的继子，按此预测，他并不想成为摩根士丹利的最后一位董事长。公司内部也因地盘争夺而四分五裂，尤其在 Dick Fisher 和 Bob Greenhill 之间，高层人士已厌倦了内斗。

In another sign of the times, Morgan Stanley ended up in an insider trading scandal second in size only to Ivan Boesky’s. In June 1986, Morgan Stanley hired Stephen Sui-Kuan Wang, Jr., who had recently left the University of Illinois, evidently without graduating. Wang, twenty-four, was first assigned to the LBO division, then in March 1987 was moved over to mergers.
另一个时代标志是，摩根士丹利卷入了一起内幕交易丑闻，其规模仅次于伊凡·博斯基案。1986 年 6 月，摩根士丹利雇用了小斯蒂芬·隋宽·王（Stephen Sui-Kuan Wang, Jr.），他不久前刚从伊利诺伊大学辍学，显然并未毕业。时年 24 岁的王最初被分配至杠杆收购部门，随后于 1987 年 3 月调至并购部。

In mid-1987, Wang was coaxed into an insider trading scheme by a Taiwanese investor named Fred Lee. From July 1987 to April 1988—undeterred by the crash—Wang fed Lee tips on twenty-five pending deals in exchange for a modest \$250,000. Within a year, Wang—young and green—had obtained information on twenty-five proposed takeovers despite his personal lack of involvement in many of them. He started his criminal career right after the extensive publicity generated by the Dennis Levine and Ivan Boesky insider trading scandals. Armed with Wang’s tips, Lee earned \$16.5 million in ten months—while Boesky had taken five years to earn \$50 million and Levine had taken five years to make a scant \$12.6 million.
1987 年中，一位名叫 Fred Lee 的台湾投资者哄骗王参与了一起内幕交易计划。从 1987 年 7 月到 1988 年 4 月——即便经历了股市崩盘，王仍不为所动——他向 Lee 提供了 25 笔待定交易的线索，仅收取了 25 万美元的微薄报酬。在一年之内，年轻且缺乏经验的王便获取了 25 起拟议收购案的信息，尽管其中许多交易他本人并未直接参与。他的犯罪生涯始于 Dennis Levine 和 Ivan Boesky 内幕交易丑闻被广泛曝光之后不久。凭借王提供的线索，Lee 在十个月内赚取了 1650 万美元——而 Boesky 用了五年才赚到 5000 万美元，Levine 更是花了五年时间才勉强赚到 1260 万美元。

Though Morgan Stanley had no criminal involvement, it didn’t escape criticism. U.S. District Attorney Rudolph Giuliani said, “You would think there would have been better controls, better procedures.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443803) According to affidavits, junior analysts sat in a big space called the bull pen, where they openly discussed their deals. More embarrassing, the brazen Fred Lee had five trading accounts at Morgan Stanley and routed many trades through the firm itself. His account there showed over \$2 million in profit. He frequently visited Morgan Stanley and had even developed an in-house reputation for pestering LBO fund analysts with calls. Morgan Stanley’s computers had flagged Lee’s trades, but when investigators challenged him on nine of them, he attributed his extraordinary luck to rumors and newspaper stories. The Morgan Stanley investigators swallowed this story, even though the trades occurred right before public announcements and tallied with Morgan Stanley’s own deals. Among others, SEC commissioner David Ruder was puzzled by the firm’s failure to detect such shameless operators.
尽管摩根士丹利并未卷入刑事案件，但依然未能逃脱批评。美国地区检察官鲁道夫·朱利安尼表示：“你可能会认为他们应该有更好的管控和流程。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443803) 根据宣誓证词，初级分析师坐在一个被称为“牛棚”的大空间里，公开讨论他们的交易。更令人尴尬的是，厚颜无耻的弗雷德·李在摩根士丹利拥有五个交易账户，并通过该公司进行了大量交易。他的账户显示盈利超过 200 万美元。他频繁拜访摩根士丹利，甚至因其不断打电话纠缠杠杆收购（LBO）基金分析师而在公司内部小有名气。摩根士丹利的电脑曾标记过李的交易，但当调查人员就其中九笔交易向他提出质疑时，他将自己的非凡运气归因于谣言和新闻报道。摩根士丹利的调查人员竟相信了这套说辞，尽管这些交易都发生在公告发布前夕，且与摩根士丹利自身的交易高度吻合。 其中，美国证券交易委员会委员大卫·鲁德对该公司未能识破如此无耻的经营者感到困惑。

In October 1988, Stephen Wang was sentenced to three years in federal prison, Federal District Judge Kevin T. Duffy telling him, “You had a brilliant future and you blew it on greed. . . . The first time you could have been a crook, you were.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443956) Lee remained a fugitive from justice. Public reaction to the Wang case differed markedly from that to the Adrian Antoniu case early in the decade. Once again, the press noted the prestige of Morgan Stanley, but without the same sense of disbelief, the mournful sense of a smashed idol, that accompanied the earlier news. The firm had squandered its moral franchise. Morgan Stanley was now another big, wealthy Wall Street house out to make a buck and was distinguished only by the fact that it did so better than anybody else.
1988 年 10 月，斯蒂芬·王被联邦地区法官凯文·T·达菲判处三年联邦监禁，达菲对他说："你本有大好前程，却因贪婪毁于一旦……你在第一次有机会当骗子的时候，就选择了这条路。" [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2443956) 李仍然在逃，逍遥法外。公众对王案的看法，与八十年代初阿德里安·安东纽案的反应截然不同。新闻界再次提及摩根士丹利的声望，但已没有当年那种难以置信的感觉，也没有那种偶像破碎的哀悼之情。这家公司已经挥霍掉了自己的道德资本。摩根士丹利如今不过是华尔街另一家财大气粗、一心逐利的大机构，唯一特别之处在于它比任何同行都更擅长赚钱罢了。

INSIDE 23 Great Winchester Street, Morgan Grenfell hardly seemed in ferment. With its green carpets and ornately framed portraits on pale yellow walls, it maintained its aristocratic dignity. Yet Morgan Grenfell was fighting for its future. After Guinness, it hired John Craven as chief executive, buying his boutique firm, Phoenix Securities, for \$25 million and giving him a 5-percent stake in Morgan Grenfell. He already had several ports of call on his crowded résumé. A protégé of Sir Siegmund Warburg, he had been chairman of Crédit Suisse White Weld and Merrill Lynch International. He had quit Merrill after confronting then-chairman Don Regan over what he saw as meddling from New York. Creating and operating Phoenix Securities from his Chelsea home, he engineered two dozen Big Bang mergers and emerged as the deal maker’s deal maker. Handsome, demanding, and restless, he didn’t pass a full week in England in 1986 and crisscrossed the Atlantic forty times.
位于温彻斯特大街 23 号的摩根建富内部，似乎并未掀起波澜。绿色地毯、淡黄色墙壁上挂着精致镶框的肖像画，依然保持着贵族般的高傲姿态。然而，摩根建富正在为生存而战。继吉尼斯事件后，它聘请约翰·克雷文担任首席执行官，以 2500 万美元收购了他的精品公司凤凰证券，并给了他摩根建富 5%的股份。在他丰富的履历中，已经有过多次任职。作为西格蒙德·沃伯格爵士的门徒，他曾担任瑞士信贷白威尔德和美林国际的董事长。因不满时任董事长唐·里根从纽约的干预，他辞去了美林的职务。他在切尔西的家中创建并运营凤凰证券，策划了二十多起金融大爆炸合并案，成为交易撮合者中的佼佼者。他英俊、要求苛刻、永不停歇，1986 年他在英国待的时间没有一整周，而是横跨大西洋往返了四十次。

Although Craven envisioned Morgan Grenfell as a global investment bank on the American model, the firm wouldn’t fulfill his goals. Unlike Morgan Stanley and Morgan Guaranty, 23 Great Winchester had gone too long without a carefully conceived strategy. The world of securities trading had been too alien to its former leadership. It hadn’t anticipated change or focused the scattered energies of the firm. In retrospect, it had erred fatally by spurning its Morgan brethren at Bermuda in the early 1970s. It had sacrificed that one inestimable advantage—its association with the American House of Morgan.
尽管克雷文曾设想将摩根建富打造成美国模式下的全球投资银行，但公司并未能实现他的目标。与摩根士丹利和摩根担保不同，温彻斯特大楼 23 号早已缺乏精心构思的战略。证券交易的世界对前任领导层来说太过陌生。它既没有预见到变革，也未能凝聚公司分散的精力。事后看来，它在 1970 年代初拒绝百慕大的摩根兄弟公司是致命的失误。它牺牲了那个无可估量的优势——与美国摩根财团的联系。

Worst of all, distracted by the casual riches of takeovers, it had moved gingerly in preparing for Big Bang and gotten stuck with bit players. It never established a major presence in trading gilts (British government bonds) or equities. The firm’s takeover clients never broadened their business to the firm’s weak securities side. Craven himself had sounded warnings of disaster in the overcrowded London markets after the drop in share volume that followed Black Monday. Morgan Grenfell’s trading rooms were bleeding the firm.
最糟糕的是，由于沉迷于并购带来的轻松暴利，它对金融大爆炸（Big Bang）的准备进展缓慢，最终只能搭上些小角色。它从未在国债或股票交易领域建立起重要地位。公司的收购客户也从未将业务拓展到其薄弱的证券业务领域。在黑色星期一之后成交量暴跌、伦敦市场过度拥挤的情况下，克拉文本人也曾发出灾难预警。而摩根格林菲尔（Morgan Grenfell）的交易部门正在不断亏损，拖垮整个公司。

On December 6, 1988, Morgan Grenfell abruptly shut its securities operation, ending its chances of becoming an integrated global investment bank. There was a wholesale firing of 450 people, or a quarter of its entire payroll—one of the biggest sackings in City history. Some traders had earned £200,000 a year (about \$370,000), and their fate underscored the City’s transient wealth. Even the method of their dismissal was emblematic: by an accidental leak, the news first appeared on their trading screens. To offset the leak, the firm rushed out an announcement as people arrived for work. In this confused situation, some gilt traders continued dealing for an hour, unaware that they were jobless. Though Craven handled the massacre with commendable tact, arranging generous severance packages, it was a terrible blow to Morgan Grenfell. For the City, this biggest firing since Big Bang was a thunderclap that symbolically closed the frantic 1980s. In March 1989, John Craven announced a loss for 1988—perhaps the first in Morgan Grenfell’s 151-year history.
1988 年 12 月 6 日，摩根格林菲尔（Morgan Grenfell）突然关闭其证券业务，彻底断送了成为全球综合投资银行的机会。一次性解雇 450 人，占全体员工四分之一，堪称伦敦金融城史上最大规模裁员之一。部分交易员年薪曾达 20 万英镑（约 37 万美元），他们的遭遇凸显了金融城财富的转瞬即逝。就连解雇方式也颇具象征意味：消息通过交易屏幕意外泄露，率先出现在这些交易员眼前。为应对泄密，公司在员工上班时匆忙发布公告。混乱中，一些金边债券交易员毫不知情自己已被解雇，还继续交易了一小时。尽管克莱文（Craven）以令人赞赏的得体方式处理了这场"大清洗"，安排了丰厚的遣散费，但对摩根格林菲尔而言仍是沉重打击。对金融城来说，这是自"大爆炸"（Big Bang）以来最大规模的裁员，如同一记惊雷，象征性地宣告了狂热的 1980 年代落下帷幕。 1989 年 3 月，John Craven 宣布 1988 年出现亏损——这可能是 Morgan Grenfell 成立 151 年来的首次。

The modern world wasn’t charitable to capital-short banks, and Morgan Grenfell was stuck in that perilous middle rut. It still had a collection of choice businesses, especially the booming merger and global-asset-management departments. It had bought C. J. Lawrence in New York, an excellent institutional research and brokerage firm, which it merged with its American operation. Thanks to strength in export and project finance, it had over \$600 million in government-guaranteed loans to strengthen its balance sheet. Finally, as a specialist in financing trade with the Soviet Union, it was the merchant bank best poised to capitalize on *perestroika.* And Craven moved aggressively to capitalize on such assets. Yet without a securities operation, these strengths didn’t coalesce into a modern global bank.
现代世界对资本不足的银行并不宽容，而摩根·格伦费尔正深陷这一危险的中等困境。它仍保留着一些优质业务，尤其是蓬勃发展的并购部门和全球资产管理部。它在纽约收购了 C. J. Lawrence——一家优秀的机构研究与经纪公司，并将其与美国业务合并。得益于出口和项目融资领域的实力，它拥有超过 6 亿美元的政府担保贷款来强化资产负债表。最后，作为对苏贸易融资的专家，它是最能抓住苏联改革机遇的商业银行。而克雷文果断行动，积极利用这些资产。然而，缺乏证券业务使这些优势未能凝聚成一家现代化的全球银行。

Sheared of its money-losing securities business, Morgan Grenfell suddenly looked like a takeover target. Many City cynics suspected that Craven, an inveterate job hopper and shrewd deal maker, had a mandate to make the investment bank’s strategy work or else auction off the firm. “I think John’s goal is to turn Morgan Grenfell around, get it in shape, and then sell it, probably to Deutsche Bank,” a friend remarked. (Deutsche Bank had bought a 4.9-percent stake in 1984.) “He’s a bulldog—he won’t let anything slip out of his bite.” Morgan Grenfell had long been protected by loyal institutional shareholders. Yet the day after the crash, insurance broker Willis Faber said its one-fifth stake was up for sale. Morgan Grenfell was joining the faithless, rootless world of modern finance.
剥离了亏损的证券业务后，摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）突然成了收购目标。伦敦金融城的许多圈内人怀疑，这位惯于跳槽、精于交易的克莱文（Craven）实际上接到的指令是——要么让这家投资银行的战略奏效，要么就把它拍卖掉。“我认为约翰的目标是先让摩根建富扭亏为盈，把它整顿好，然后再卖掉，很可能卖给德意志银行（Deutsche Bank）。”一位朋友评论道（德意志银行在 1984 年已购入其 4.9%的股份）。“他就像一头斗牛犬——咬住了就不会松口。”长期以来，摩根建富一直受到忠诚的机构股东庇护。然而，股市崩盘后的第二天，保险经纪公司 Willis Faber 就宣布要出售其持有的五分之一股份。摩根建富就此踏入了这个缺乏忠诚、根基尽失的现代金融世界。

By 1989, Morgan Grenfell seemed ripe for the taking by bigger rivals. In shedding the bank’s securities business and swiftly restoring profitability, Craven only added to its allure as a takeover target. And so the bank that had specialized in hostile takeovers found itself in November the object of an unwelcome embrace from Banque Indosuez of France. Craven brought in Deutsche Bank as a white knight and extracted a rich price for the firm: over \$1.4 billion, or more than twice its book value. This breathtaking bid settled the contest. Craven, the consumate negotiator, became the first foreigner invited onto the Deutsche Bank board. The hoopla was spiked by the grisly slaying of Deutsche Bank head Alfred Herrhausen by terrorists. The generous settlement also obscured the fact that 151 years of noble independence had been suddenly swept away.
到 1989 年，摩根·格伦菲尔似乎已成强敌环伺的囊中之物。克雷文剥离其证券业务并迅速恢复盈利后，反而让这家银行作为收购目标的魅力更添几分。于是，这家专攻敌意收购的银行，在同年 11 月发现自己竟成了法国东方汇理银行不情愿拥抱的对象。克雷文请来德意志银行作为白衣骑士，并为公司争取到丰厚价码：超过 14 亿美元，是账面价值的两倍有余。这份令人瞠目的报价终结了争夺战。作为炉火纯青的谈判高手，克雷文成为首位受邀加入德意志银行董事会的外国人。然而这场喧闹，却因德意志银行掌门人阿尔弗雷德·赫尔豪森惨遭恐怖分子杀害而戛然而止。丰厚的和解协议也掩盖了一个事实：这家银行长达 151 年高贵的独立，就这样骤然烟消云散。

OVER fifty years later, J. P. Morgan and Company appeared to have erred in its choice of commercial banking in 1935. If it saved Depression-era jobs, it also burdened the House of Morgan with what proved to be a dying business—wholesale lending. Large companies no longer turned to banks for short-term credit or seasonal lending—activities now relegated to the commercial paper market. So Morgans had gradually undone its history and grown into a hybrid investment bank, much like its rival down the block, Bankers Trust.
五十多年后，摩根大通银行在 1935 年选择专注商业银行业务的决策似乎并不明智。尽管这一选择在大萧条时期保住了就业岗位，却也让摩根家族背负了一个逐渐式微的行业——批发贷款。如今大型企业不再向银行寻求短期信贷或季节性贷款，这些业务早已转向商业票据市场。因此，摩根大通逐渐摆脱历史包袱，转型为一家混合型投资银行，与街对面的竞争对手 Bankers Trust 颇为相似。

The House of Morgan led the fight to repeal Glass-Steagall. Like other banks, it tried to scramble into so many investment bank activities that Congress would have to rubber-stamp the marketplace reality. Lew Preston also believed in making an intellectual case for change, and in 1984 the bank produced a treatise called *Rethinking Glass-Steagall.* One patron was Alan Greenspan, then a Morgan director, who followed Paul Volcker as Fed chairman. “As a director, Greenspan was very instrumental in getting that document out,” said a Morgan insider.
摩根银行带头推动废除《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》。与其他银行一样，它试图争先恐后地涉足大量投行业务，迫使国会不得不对市场现实予以形式上的认可。刘·普雷斯顿也认为应该从理论层面论证变革的必要性，于是 1984 年该行推出了一部名为《反思格拉斯-斯蒂格尔》的专著。时任摩根董事的艾伦·格林斯潘是背后的支持者之一，他后来接替保罗·沃尔克出任美联储主席。一位摩根内部人士回忆道：“作为董事，格林斯潘在推动这份文件出炉的过程中发挥了关键作用。”

Lew Preston knew that after Rule 415, straight blue-chip underwriting was a less lucrative auction business. The Morgan bank wanted to underwrite corporate bonds mainly to offer customers a full line of financial services. It was also necessary to finance takeovers. Although Morgans was dubbed the Fed’s bank, Preston could never budge Paul Volcker on Glass-Steagall. Still worried about banks performing “risky” securities work, Volcker would reply that he didn’t worry about Morgans, but about three or four other banks. Morgans was also reaping the highest return on equity and assets of any American bank during the Volcker years. “Unfortunately, we were having some pretty good earnings,” Preston conceded, “and so there was skepticism on the part of the Fed chairman.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444107)
卢·普雷斯顿知道，在 415 条款出台后，纯粹的蓝筹股承销业务就变成了一桩利润微薄的拍卖生意。摩根银行承销公司债，主要是为了向客户提供一条龙金融服务，同时也为收购融资所必需。尽管摩根被称为美联储的银行，但普雷斯顿始终无法让保罗·沃尔克在《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》上松口。沃尔克仍然担心银行从事"高风险"的证券业务，他会说，他担心的不是摩根，而是其他三四家银行。在沃尔克执掌美联储期间，摩根银行也是所有美国银行中股本回报率和资产回报率最高的。"不幸的是，我们的盈利相当不错，"普雷斯顿承认，"所以美联储主席对我们心存疑虑。" [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444107)

Having helped Volcker at least three times—with the Hunt brothers, Continental Illinois, and the Brazil debt rescheduling—Preston fretted about the lack of a *quid pro quo.* A friend of Preston’s noted that “Lew was very close to Volcker in pulling acorns out of the firm. He had broken his back on those three cases. I remember Lew saying, ‘I had a lot of chits on Paul, and yet Paul is still against banks going into the debt markets.’ I’m absolutely positive that Preston felt somewhat betrayed by Volcker.” Other Morgan officials felt they had gained little for being such a model of obedience. As one Morgan insider said bitterly, “I’m tired of being the Fed’s pet.”
至少帮了沃尔克三次——亨特兄弟案、伊利诺伊大陆银行案和巴西债务重组案——普雷斯顿却为没有获得对等回报而烦恼。普雷斯顿的一位朋友指出：“卢在帮公司摆平这些麻烦事时跟沃尔克走得很近。那三桩案子他真是拼了老命。我记得卢说过：‘我给了保罗那么多情分，可他还是反对银行进入债务市场。’我敢肯定，普雷斯顿觉得沃尔克多少有点忘恩负义。”其他摩根高管也认为，如此唯命是从却没捞到什么好处。一位摩根内部人士酸溜溜地说：“我受够了当美联储的乖宝宝。”

As recently as 1984, the *New York Times* had said it would be “a matter of seconds” before Morgan Guaranty and Morgan Stanley got back together if Glass-Steagall fell.[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444267) Twenty years before, this was indisputable. But as the 1980s progressed, the warmth between the Morgan brethren waned. Young hotshots at Morgan Stanley felt more kinship with the flamboyant Bankers Trust, which specialized in risky trading and merchant banking, than with the austere, circumspect Morgan Guaranty. One could more readily imagine Morgan Guaranty teamed up with Goldman, Sachs than with Morgan Stanley. The two Morgan houses no longer made a natural match and were especially keen adversaries in Tokyo and London.
就在 1984 年，《纽约时报》还曾断言，一旦格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案被废除，摩根担保与摩根士丹利"用不了几秒"就会重新合并。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444267) 二十年前，这说法确实无可争议。但随着 80 年代推进，摩根两兄弟之间的温情逐渐冷却。摩根士丹利的年轻精英们与以高风险交易和商人银行业务闻名的浮夸银行家信托更为亲近，反而对严肃谨慎的摩根担保敬而远之。人们更容易想象摩根担保与高盛联手，而非与摩根士丹利合作。这两家摩根系机构已不再天然匹配，尤其在东京和伦敦，它们更是针锋相对的劲敌。

Investment bankers were fatalistic about Glass-Steagall’s fall. As Fred Whittemore said of Morgan Stanley’s decision to go public in 1986, “We are taking advantage of a 3-to-5 year window, before the banks become full-fledged competitors, to become as large and powerful as quickly as we can.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444419) Morgan Stanley’s LBO war chest and large capital freed it from reliance on commercial banks in financing takeovers. With its new merchant-banking orientation, Morgan Stanley didn’t much care about Glass-Steagall’s demise, which would affect only a small, declining part of its operations. Morgan Stanley managing director Robert A. Gerard warned Congress, in strangely populist tones, that if Glass-Steagall fell, “there will be a vast increase in the concentration of economic power in large banking organizations.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444609) But his firm made only perfunctory moves against what it saw as a largely irrelevant law.
投行对《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的废止持听天由命的态度。正如弗雷德·惠特莫尔谈及摩根士丹利 1986 年上市的决定时所说："我们正利用银行成为羽翼丰满的竞争对手之前这 3 到 5 年的窗口期，尽可能快地壮大自己。" [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444419) 摩根的杠杆收购资金储备和雄厚资本使其在收购融资上摆脱了对商业银行的依赖。凭借新确立的商人银行定位，摩根士丹利对《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的消亡并不太在意——该法案只会影响其一小部分且日益萎缩的业务。摩根士丹利董事总经理罗伯特·A·杰拉德以奇怪的民粹主义口吻警告国会，如果《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》被废除，"大型银行机构的经济权力将急剧集中。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444609) 但他的公司对这项被视为无关紧要的法律仅做了些敷衍了事的应对。

For commercial banks, exasperation over Glass-Steagall mounted as everything from car loans to mortgages was packaged as securities and placed beyond their reach. Preston quietly chafed at Volcker’s obstinacy. In a *Fortune* article in April 1986, he made a shocking admission: the Morgan bank had considered surrendering its commercial bank charter and simply becoming an investment bank. This would have sacrificed perhaps 20 percent of its business, forcing it to forgo checking accounts and deposit insurance. Although the statement apparently wasn’t a case of clever premeditation, Preston didn’t mind the uproar. Bob Engel reiterated the point: “If we became convinced that we would never get full expanded securities power, we would owe it to our shareholders to reconsider whether we still wanted to be a bank. We could still become a private bank—drop out of the Fed and the payments system.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444762) Some of this was tactical bluster, but it revealed the impatience at 23 Wall.
对于商业银行而言，随着从车贷到房贷的各种贷款都被打包成证券并脱离其业务范围，它们对《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的不满日益加剧。普雷斯顿私下对沃尔克固执己见感到恼火。在 1986 年 4 月《财富》杂志的一篇文章中，他惊人地承认：摩根银行曾考虑放弃商业银行牌照，干脆转型为投资银行。这将牺牲其约 20%的业务，迫使其放弃支票账户和存款保险。尽管这番言论显然并非精心策划的计谋，但普雷斯顿并不介意引发的轩然大波。鲍勃·恩格尔进一步强调：“如果我们确信永远无法获得全面的证券业务权限，那我们就有责任重新考虑是否还要继续做一家银行。我们可以转型为私人银行——退出美联储和支付系统。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444762) 其中不乏战术性虚张声势，但这揭示了华尔街 23 号日益焦躁的情绪。

The 1929 crash had led straight to Glass-Steagall. Ironically, the 1987 crash would prove its undoing, as Black Monday deepened national discontent with Wall Street. Morgan Stanley and the other big securities houses looked increasingly like a cosy cartel protected by Glass-Steagall —an outcome quite different from that expected by the New Deal reformers, who wanted to bust up concentrated Wall Street power. Meanwhile, commercial banks were the clear casualties of the 1980s. The Latin American debt crisis showed that lending was now far riskier than trading. The crisis mocked the spirit of Glass-Steagall, which had tried to guarantee the stability of deposit banks. With foreign banks able to underwrite securities in the United States, Glass-Steagall seemed only to penalize American banks and invite them to make rash decisions. With Japan now claiming seven of the world’s ten largest banks, this competitive disadvantage was no small matter.
1929 年的崩盘直接催生了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》。讽刺的是，1987 年的崩盘却成了它的终结——黑色星期一加深了国民对华尔街的不满。摩根士丹利和其他大型证券公司愈发像是一个受《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》庇护的舒适卡特尔，这与新政改革者们的初衷大相径庭——他们本想打破华尔街的集中权力。与此同时，商业银行在 80 年代明显沦为输家。拉丁美洲债务危机表明，贷款的风险如今远高于交易。这场危机嘲讽了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的精神——该法案曾试图保障存款银行的稳定。而外国银行能在美国承销证券，使《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》似乎只惩罚了美国银行，并诱使它们做出草率决策。当时日本已占据全球十大银行中的七席，这种竞争劣势不容小觑。

In Senator William Proxmire, chairman of the Senate Banking Committee, the banks found an unexpected ally. He was willing to grant them power to underwrite stocks and bonds provided big commercial and investment banks didn’t merge. “Washington does not like the thought of Morgan Guaranty and Morgan Stanley making up after all those years,” said the *Economist*.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2444921) As a J. P. Morgan director for ten years, Alan Greenspan had promised to excuse himself as Fed chairman from decisions affecting the bank. Yet Greenspan was the tutelary spirit behind a partial Glass-Steagall repeal. The banks had already secured permission to underwrite commercial paper and municipal revenue bonds. In January 1989, the Fed added limited powers to float corporate bonds. Among the first five banks streaming through the open gates was J. P. Morgan Securities—easily the largest such operation, with \$400 million in capital and seven hundred employees. And in October 1989, it became the first American commercial bank since the Depression to float a corporate debt issue, managing a \$30 million bond issue for the Savannah Electric and Power Company.
在参议院银行委员会主席威廉·普罗克斯迈尔参议员身上，银行家们发现了一个意想不到的盟友。他愿意授予他们承销股票和债券的权力，前提是大型商业银行和投资银行不能合并。《经济学人》评论道：“华盛顿不喜欢摩根担保银行与摩根士丹利在多年后重修旧好的想法。”艾伦·格林斯潘曾在摩根大通担任董事十年之久，他承诺在担任美联储主席期间，会避开影响该银行的决策。然而，正是格林斯潘在幕后推动部分废除了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》。银行早已获准承销商业票据和市政收益债券。1989 年 1 月，美联储又增加了有限度的企业债券发行权。首批涌过敞开大门的五家银行中，摩根大通证券赫然在列——它无疑是规模最大的此类业务，拥有 4 亿美元资本和 700 名员工。 1989 年 10 月，它成为自大萧条以来首家发行公司债券的美国商业银行，为萨凡纳电力和照明公司（Savannah Electric and Power Company）成功筹办了一笔 3000 万美元的债券发行。

There was a disquieting side to all this. Would banks soon underwrite corporate raids with junk bonds? Would they palm off Latin American debt on bondholders, as they had in the 1920s? And how would banks insulate depositors from any future risks in securities work? The concerns were genuine. But such problems would have to be dealt with by Congress and the wisdom of bank regulators, for the status quo had become more dangerous to the commercial banks than any risks introduced by expanded securities powers.
这一切背后也潜藏着令人不安的一面。银行是否会很快用垃圾债券为企业收购提供承销？它们是否会像 20 世纪 20 年代那样，将拉丁美洲的债务转手甩给债券持有人？银行又该如何保护储户免受未来证券业务可能带来的风险？这些担忧确实真实存在。但这些问题需要由国会和明智的银行监管机构来处理，因为对商业银行而言，维持现状比扩大证券业务所引入的任何风险都要危险得多。

The years ahead promise to witness the rise of vast universal banks at home and abroad, the Morgan bank certainly among them. In the view of *Institutional Investor*, commercial banks had “grandiose plans” to become “shimmering financial institutions as omnipotent as the old House of Morgan was prior to Glass-Steagall.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2445070) J. P. Morgan and Company was now a global entity, not just an American bank operating abroad. Three of its six top executives were non-American, as were half the people in its management-training program in New York. Every high official had served a tour of duty abroad.
未来数年间，国内外必将涌现出众多大型全能银行，摩根银行自然位列其中。在《机构投资者》看来，商业银行曾怀有“宏伟蓝图”，立志成为“如格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案颁布前的摩根财团般无所不能、光彩夺目的金融机构”。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2445070) 此时的摩根大通已是一家全球性实体，而不仅仅是在海外经营的美国银行。其六位最高层管理者中有三位非美籍人士，纽约管理培训项目的成员也有一半来自海外。每一位高级官员都曾在海外任职历练。

By 1989, the bank had outgrown its shrine at 23 Wall. Lew Preston wanted a personal computer on every banker’s desk, and trading desks required an exotic jungle of electric wiring. To accommodate a high-tech bank, Morgans bought a new forty-seven-story glass-and-stone tower at 60 Wall Street that was designed by Kevin Roche. It wasn’t custom-made for the House of Morgan, as 23 Wall had been. To save time and money, the bank bought a real estate package assembled by developer George Klein. First budgeted at \$530 million, the cost overruns at 60 Wall pushed the price up to \$830 million. In 1988, the Morgan bank borrowed \$400 million from the Dai-Ichi Mutual Life Insurance Company to help finance the new tower. Preston said that he hadn’t picked the furnishings for his new office because Dennis Weatherstone might not like them. He thus mischievously telegraphed the message that Weatherstone, self-made son of a London transport worker, would succeed him as the first foreign-born bank chairman on Wall Street. On the eve of the bank’s departure for 60 Wall in the summer of 1989, reports circulated that the bank would have to cut ten percent of its work force, or about fifteen hundred people. It was another reminder that the days of paternalism and coddled, lifelong employment were long gone.
到 1989 年，这家银行已不再满足于华尔街 23 号那座如神殿般的老楼。卢·普雷斯顿希望每位银行家的办公桌上都能摆上一台个人电脑，而交易柜台则需要铺满各种复杂的电线。为了适应这家高科技银行，摩根公司买下了华尔街 60 号由凯文·罗奇设计的四十七层玻璃与石材新塔楼。这座大楼并非像华尔街 23 号那样为摩根家族量身打造。为了节省时间和成本，银行直接收购了开发商乔治·克莱恩打包的地产项目。最初预算为 5.3 亿美元，但华尔街 60 号的成本超支将价格推高至 8.3 亿美元。1988 年，摩根银行从第一相互生命保险公司借款 4 亿美元，用于资助这座新塔楼的建设。普雷斯顿说，他没有为自己挑选新办公室的家具，因为丹尼斯·韦瑟斯通可能不喜欢。 他调皮地发电报宣布，伦敦运输工人出身的白手起家者 Weatherstone 将接替他，成为华尔街首位外国出生的银行董事长。1989 年夏，银行即将迁入华尔街 60 号前夕，有传闻称银行将裁员 10%，约 1500 人。这再次提醒人们，家长式作风和娇惯的终身雇佣时代早已一去不返。

TWENTY-THREE Wall had always reflected the House of Morgan. From the moment you stepped through the doors and stood beneath the radiant Louis XV chandelier, with its nineteen hundred crystal pieces, you could feel the self-confidence of the place, the massive weight of tradition. It had a splendid touch of theater about it. As the bank transferred to 60 Wall Street, there was talk of selling 23 Wall, which Lew Preston denied. “It’s a monument,” he said wistfully. “It’s really got no value to anybody except us.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2445217) The little temple of finance, which had witnessed more history than any American banking house, was now a costly relic from a vanished world of civility.
华尔街 23 号一直是摩根银行的缩影。当你跨过大门，站在闪耀的路易十五水晶吊灯下——那盏吊灯缀着一千九百片水晶——便能感受到这里的自信，以及那份沉甸甸的传统分量。它带着一丝华丽的剧场感。当银行迁往华尔街 60 号时，曾有人提议出售华尔街 23 号，但刘·普雷斯顿否决了。“这是一座纪念碑，”他略带感伤地说，“除了我们，它对任何人都毫无价值。”这座小小的金融圣殿，见证的历史比任何美国银行大厦都要多，如今不过是文明世界已逝去后，一座昂贵的遗迹。

Would any banking house ever again have the mystery of the old House of Morgan? Probably not. The Morgan partners were adornments of a world too closed and too collusive by today’s more egalitarian standards. The spacious vision and cultivation of Tom Lamont, Dwight Morrow, and Russell Leffingwell sprang from a world of small partnerships and few competing sources of financial power. They worked on a quieter, slower Wall Street and could afford to be gentlemen and scholars.
还会不会有哪家银行能重现昔日摩根财团的神秘色彩？恐怕不会了。以今日更平等的标准来看，摩根合伙人不过是一个过于封闭、沆瀣一气的世界里的点缀。汤姆·拉蒙特、德怀特·莫罗、拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔这些人的开阔视野与深厚修养，源自一个由小型合伙制公司和寥寥几个金融力量竞争者构成的世界。他们在更宁静、更缓慢的华尔街工作，因而得以成为绅士和学者。

Much of the bank’s special flavor derived from its global outlook. As a conduit for capital transfers between America and Europe, the old House of Morgan had naturally looked abroad and was uniquely cosmopolitan at a time when America was still provincial and isolationist. Now the rest of the country had caught up. The Morgan bank’s foreign connections, once incomparable, might today be matched by those of many foreign ministries, central banks, or even multinational corporations. Financial power has become widely dispersed among American, European, and Japanese firms. No single firm will ever again be as lordly or preeminent as the House of Pierpont and Jack Morgan.
摩根银行的独特魅力很大程度上源于其全球视野。作为美欧资本流动的通道，老摩根财团自然将目光投向海外，在美国仍处于地方主义和孤立主义时期，它已是独一无二的世界性机构。如今，美国其他地区已迎头赶上。摩根银行昔日无与伦比的海外联系，今天或许已与许多外交部、央行甚至跨国公司的全球网络不相上下。金融实力已广泛分散至美、欧、日各企业。再也没有哪家机构能像皮尔庞特和杰克·摩根的财团那样称雄一世、卓尔不群了。

The old House of Morgan’s power stemmed from the immature state of government treasuries, companies, and capital markets. It stood sentinel over capital markets that were relatively small and primitive. Today, money has become a commonplace commodity. A company in need of capital can turn to investment banks, commercial banks, or insurance companies; it can raise it through bank loans, bond issues, private placements, or commercial paper; it can draw upon many currencies, many countries, many markets. Money has lost its mystique, and banking, therefore, has lost a bit of its magic.
旧的摩根财团的权力源于政府财政、企业和资本市场的不成熟。它守护着相对弱小且原始的市场。如今，金钱已成为一种普通的商品。需要资金的企业可以找投资银行、商业银行或保险公司；它可以通过银行贷款、债券发行、私募或商业票据来筹集资金；它可以利用多种货币、多个国家、多个市场。金钱已失去了神秘感，因此银行业也褪去了些许魔力。

The Morgan story is the story of modern finance itself. A Pierpont Morgan exercised powers that today are dispersed among vast global banking conglomerates. The activities once performed by a knot of side-whiskered men in mahogany parlors are now spread across trading rooms around the world. We live in a larger, faster, more anonymous age. There will be more deals done and more fortunes made, but there will never be another barony like the House of Morgan.
摩根的故事就是现代金融本身的故事。皮尔庞特·摩根曾执掌的权力，如今分散在庞大的全球银行集团手中。那些曾经由一群留着连鬓胡的男人在桃花心木客厅里完成的活动，如今遍布世界各地的交易大厅。我们生活在一个更大、更快、更匿名化的时代。会有更多的交易达成，更多的财富创造，但再也不会有像摩根财团这样的金融王朝了。
