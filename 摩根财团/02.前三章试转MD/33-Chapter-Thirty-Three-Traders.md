# Chapter Thirty-Three: Traders

IN the early 1980s, as the final vestiges of fraternity among the Morgan houses disappeared and Morgan Guaranty abandoned wholesale lending to enter global investment banking, it ran into Morgan Stanley. It was also on a convergence path with Morgan Grenfell. When Morgan Guaranty occupied a sleek building of brown granite and smoked glass near the Bank of England—snobbishly named the Morgan Bank, disregarding poor Morgan Grenfell some blocks away—the ancient Anglo-American link, too, was threatened. Starting in 1979, the London-based Morgan Guaranty Ltd. became a major underwriter in the Euromarkets. How could Morgan Guaranty retain a one-third stake in Morgan Grenfell as they clashed in foreign outposts and invaded each other’s home turf? As Bill Mackworth-Young of Morgan Grenfell said, “It doesn’t make sense to be 33\$\$\$ owned by one of your competitors.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429676)
20 世纪 80 年代初，随着摩根家族各成员间仅存的兄弟情谊烟消云散，摩根担保公司放弃批发贷款业务，转而进军全球投资银行业，与摩根士丹利狭路相逢。同时，它也与摩根建富走上了趋同之路。当摩根担保公司在英格兰银行附近建起一栋棕色花岗岩配烟色玻璃的 sleek 大楼——并势利地将其命名为“摩根银行”，全然不顾几个街区外可怜的摩根建富时，这古老的英美纽带也受到了威胁。自 1979 年起，总部位于伦敦的摩根担保有限公司成为欧洲债券市场的主要承销商。当它们在海外据点发生冲突，并入侵彼此的本土市场时，摩根担保公司又怎能继续持有摩根建富三分之一的股份呢？正如摩根建富的比尔·麦克斯沃斯-扬所说：“由你的竞争对手之一持有 33%的股份，这不合情理。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429676)

Morgan Grenfell needed expansion capital but couldn’t pry it loose from 23 Wall. The London bank’s home success had bred hopes for bigger things abroad, especially in New York, where it had had a small office since 1974. To transcend that token presence was impossible so long as Morgan Guaranty owned a one-third stake. So in 1981, the Morgan chairman, Lew Preston, and president, Robert V. “Rod” Lindsay, flew to London to inform Lord Stephen Catto, over dinner, that 23 Wall had decided to sell its stake. The House of Morgan petered out, mourned by few. “It was a bit of a twinge for me and a few seniors at Morgan Grenfell and a few others around here,” recalled Lindsay. “But it became clear to everybody that they needed more freedom to go their own way.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429829) Lew Preston had grown uncomfortable with Morgan Grenfell as the old, aristocratic families faded from the scene, people who had all trained at 23 Wall. He explained, “The Bank of England expected us to share one-third of every loss, but there was a management evolution where we didn’t know the people who were running the firm.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429990) The new breed was typified by chief executive Christopher Reeves, a former assistant personnel manager at Hill Samuel, who had never passed through the Morgan Guaranty training program.
摩根建富需要扩张资金，却难以从华尔街 23 号手中撬开。这家伦敦银行在英国本土的成功，催生了其向海外拓展的雄心，尤其渴望进军纽约——自 1974 年起，它已在当地设立了一个小型办事处。然而，只要摩根担保信托持有三分之一股份，这一象征性的存在就永远无法突破。于是 1981 年，摩根董事长卢·普雷斯顿与总裁罗伯特·V·“罗德”·林赛飞赴伦敦，在晚宴上告知斯蒂芬·卡托勋爵：华尔街 23 号决定出售所持股份。摩根财团就此黯然退场，鲜有人为之哀悼。“对我、摩根建富的几位资深同事以及周围一些人来说，确实感到一丝刺痛，”林赛回忆道，“但大家都清楚，他们需要更多自由去走自己的路。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429829) 随着那些曾在华尔街 23 号受训的古老贵族家族逐渐淡出舞台，卢·普雷斯顿对摩根建富的处境也愈发感到不安。 他解释道：“英格兰银行原本希望我们承担每笔损失的三分之一，但管理层后来发生了变化，而我们根本不认识经营这家公司的人。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2429990) 这种新生力量的代表便是首席执行官克里斯托弗·里夫斯，他曾是希尔·塞缪尔银行的人事助理经理，从未接受过摩根担保银行的培训计划。

Thus ended a transatlantic axis more than a century old, the armature on which the House of Morgan had been built. Catto said, “I had seen it coming with regret. We had one request: that they not sell it all at once, which would look like a loss of confidence in us. They agreed to sell it piecemeal.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430150) Within a year, the bank took its stake below 4 percent, pocketing \$40 million and leaving the Lloyd’s insurance broker Willis Faber as chief shareholder, with 24 percent. In a declaration of freedom in 1981, Morgan Grenfell set up an investment banking subsidiary in New York, expanding its money management and international M&A businesses. By 1985, it belonged to the New York Stock Exchange. The pretense of brotherhood had given way to raw competition.
一个延续了超过一个世纪的跨大西洋轴心就此终结，而摩根家族正是建立在这一核心架构之上的。卡托说：“我遗憾地看着这一切发生。我们只有一个请求：要求他们不要一次性抛售所有股份，那会显得对我们缺乏信心。他们同意了分批出售。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430150) 一年之内，该银行将持股比例降至 4%以下，获利 4000 万美元，劳合社的保险经纪人威利斯·费伯成为第一大股东，持股 24%。1981 年，摩根建富宣布自由，在纽约设立了投资银行子公司，拓展其资产管理和国际并购业务。到 1985 年，它已成为纽约证券交易所的成员。兄弟情谊的伪装让位于赤裸裸的竞争。

A creature of markets, J. P. Morgan and Company—the parent company of Morgan Guaranty—now operated by new principles. It raised billions of dollars daily in the money markets and was emancipated from dependence on loan spreads and deposits. Though the bank still had no retail branches, Morgan people joked that they *had* a retail bank—Merrill Lynch, whose money market fund bought Morgan CDs. The House of Morgan had all but given up on wholesale lending as an anachronistic business for a bank whose blue-chip clients could raise money more cheaply in the marketplace, as they increasingly did in the early 1980s. In 1983, international bond offerings, for the first time, passed global bank lending in scope. Lew Preston didn’t want to join an extinct breed. “Basic lending is never going to return to the profitability that existed in the Fifties and Sixties,” he predicted.[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430315) Foreign bank competition also thinned loan spreads.
作为市场中的一员，摩根担保公司的母公司摩根大通如今以全新的原则运作。它每天在货币市场筹集数十亿美元，摆脱了对贷款利差和存款的依赖。尽管该银行仍没有零售分行，摩根员工却打趣说他们有一家零售银行——美林证券，其货币市场基金购买摩根的存单。摩根银行几乎放弃了批发贷款业务，认为对其蓝筹客户而言这一业务已不合时宜——这些客户在市场上能以更低成本融资，且这一趋势在 20 世纪 80 年代初愈发明显。1983 年，国际债券发行规模首次超过全球银行贷款。卢·普雷斯顿不想成为濒危物种的一员。“基础贷款业务永远不会恢复五六十年代的那种盈利能力，”他预测道。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430315) 外国银行竞争也进一步压缩了贷款利差。

The upshot was that the Morgan bank began making more money from investment banking fees and trading income. The future bank took shape in London, where Morgan Guaranty had become the top Eurobond underwriter among American commercial banks, with clients including Exxon, IBM, Du Pont, and even Citicorp. From number forty-six in 1980, it zoomed to second place in Eurobonds four years later. It also accelerated trading in gold bullion, foreign exchange, and financial futures.
结果是，摩根银行开始从投资银行业务手续费和交易收入中赚取更多利润。这家未来银行的雏形在伦敦显现——摩根保证信托已成为美国商业银行中最大的欧洲债券承销商，客户包括埃克森、IBM、杜邦，甚至还有花旗集团。从 1980 年的第 46 位，它在短短四年后跃升至欧洲债券市场的第二位。同时，它还加速了金条、外汇和金融期货的交易。

The locomotive behind these changes was Lew Preston, who embodied the bank’s old silken charm but imbued it with a new, sometimes fierce energy. A Harvard graduate from a rich Westchester family, he had started in the Morgan mailroom (as everybody did) in the early 1950s. He was first viewed by elders as a playboy, socialite, and jock. Tall and broad-shouldered, he played semipro ice hockey with the Long Island Ducks until he came home one night with six stitches in his head. “You damned fool,” his wife said, “why don’t you grow up.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430463) His second wife, Patsy, was a granddaughter of newspaper publisher Joseph Pulitzer and mixed with Brooke Astor, Jane Engelhardt, and other socialites.
这些变革背后的驱动力是卢·普雷斯顿，他继承了摩根银行往日的优雅风范，却又注入了一股崭新乃至有时咄咄逼人的活力。他出身于威斯特彻斯特的富裕家庭，毕业于哈佛大学，20 世纪 50 年代初从摩根银行的收发室起步（这是每个新人的传统）。起初，长辈们视他为花花公子、社交名流和运动健将。他身材高大、肩膀宽阔，曾效力于长岛鸭队打半职业冰球，直到有天晚上顶着头上缝了六针的伤口回家。妻子说：“你这该死的傻瓜，能不能成熟点。”他的第二任妻子帕齐是报业大亨约瑟夫·普利策的孙女，与布鲁克·阿斯特、简·恩格尔哈特等社交名流交往甚密。

This Lew Preston seemed all tradition. Among the antique furnishings in his office were an oil portrait of Jack Morgan, a rolltop desk, and a photograph of Pierpont and Jack striding manfully into the Pujo hearings. Wearing half-moon glasses and red suspenders and smoking Don Diego cigars, he could effect an extremely dignified presence. Once, after making a presentation to Noboru Takeshita, then Japan’s finance minister and later prime minister, the dignitary breathed with admiration. “You were prime ministerial in your presentation,” he said. “I am stunned.”
这位卢·普雷斯顿看起来满身都是传统气息。他办公室的古董家具中，有一幅杰克·摩根的油画肖像、一张卷盖式书桌，还有一张皮尔庞特和杰克昂首阔步走进普约听证会的照片。他戴着半月形眼镜，穿着红色吊带裤，抽着唐·迪亚哥雪茄，能营造出一种极其威严的气场。有一次，他在向时任日本财务大臣（后来成为首相）的竹下登做演示后，这位政要钦佩地感叹道：“你的演示很有首相风范，让我叹为观止。”

The elegant manner and dryly mischievous wit covered early scars. When Lew was a boy, his father died of tuberculosis. He also struggled with dyslexia. (“It’s very fashionable now,” he remarked. “Everybody seems to have it.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430609)) At seventeen, he enlisted in the Marine Corps and was sent to China. He ended up as a bodyguard to James Forrestal, later Truman’s secretary of defense and a close family friend. Demobilized, Preston attended Harvard, from which he graduated in 1951. He would always be a cross between a Harvard socialite and a tough marine. Curt with fools, sometimes abrupt at meetings, he would show exemplary kindness to someone who was hospitalized, bereaved, or recently divorced. Some at 23 Wall revered Lew Preston, some were slightly afraid of him, and some both revered and feared him.
优雅的举止和冷幽默掩盖了早年的创伤。卢小时候，父亲死于肺结核，他自己也饱受阅读障碍之苦。（“现在这病可流行了，”他调侃道，“好像人人都有似的。”）十七岁时，他加入海军陆战队，被派往中国，后来成为詹姆斯·福雷斯特尔的保镖——此人后来出任杜鲁门的国防部长，也是卢家的至交。退役后，普雷斯顿进入哈佛大学，1951 年毕业。他始终是哈佛社交名流与剽悍陆战队员的混合体：对蠢人毫不客气，开会时有时很生硬，但对住院、丧亲或刚离婚的人却会表现出典范般的善意。在华尔街 23 号，有人敬重卢·普雷斯顿，有人对他略感畏惧，也有人既敬重又畏惧。

This dual personality mirrored the Morgan transition. Preston tried to perpetuate the old Morgan culture of teamwork and subordination of the individual to the group: “I want people who want to do something rather than be someone.” With department heads, he held the traditional weekly meetings and encouraged senior people to lunch together in the executive dining rooms. This Preston conceded that “a little bit of conservatism in a bank is not a bad thing” and said rather loftily of Citicorp’s Walter Wriston, “He’s running a financial conglomerate and we’re running a bank.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430769) He tended the bank’s image as if it were a stage set. “We spend an extraordinary amount of time just worrying about the environment,” he said.[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430918)
这种双重性格反映了摩根银行的转型。普雷斯顿试图延续老摩根那种强调团队合作、个人服从集体的文化：“我要的是想做些事情的人，而不是想成为某种人物的人。”他与部门主管保持传统的每周例会，并鼓励高级职员在行政餐厅共进午餐。普雷斯顿承认“银行里带点保守味儿不是坏事”，并略带高傲地评价花旗银行的沃尔特·里斯顿：“他经营的是金融集团，我们经营的是银行。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430769) 他像打理舞台布景一样维护着银行的形象。“我们花了大量时间，仅仅是为了营造环境。”他说道。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2430918)

At the same time, an avuncular style no longer worked completely in a bank with over fifteen thousand employees. Morgan elders had taken a fatherly interest in their staff, with talk of one’s being “brought up” at 23 Wall. Now in a vastly speeded up bank, there wasn’t time for prep-school camaraderie. Preston had to retrain masses of old-time commercial bankers and credit analysts, making them into risk-taking market whizzes. This meant encouraging aggressiveness and imagination, not just politeness and caution. Competing with investment banks, Preston had to pay huge bonuses and use other compensation methods that fostered divisiveness. By the 1987 crash, some Morgan traders earned more than Preston’s own \$1.3-million salary. As the eighties progressed, many people left the bank or were gently nudged out. Even among those who stayed, there was a bittersweet sense that the bank was less fun and caring than in the old days. It was also a far more diversified firm. In 1984, for instance, Boris S. Berkovitch became vice-chairman of the bank—the first Jew ever to rise to the top of Morgan officialdom.
与此同时，在拥有超过一万五千名员工的银行里，那种慈父式的管理风格已不再完全奏效。摩根的老一辈高管曾对员工怀有父亲般的关怀，常谈起某人是如何“在华尔街 23 号被培养起来的”。而在如今节奏急剧加快的银行中，已无暇顾及那种预科学校式的同事情谊。普雷斯顿不得不对大批传统商业银行家和信贷分析师进行再培训，将他们打造成敢于冒险的市场奇才。这意味着要鼓励进取心和想象力，而不仅仅是彬彬有礼和谨小慎微。为了与投资银行竞争，普雷斯顿必须支付巨额奖金，并采用其他会导致内部隔阂的薪酬方式。到 1987 年股市崩盘时，一些摩根交易员的收入已超过普雷斯顿 130 万美元的年薪。随着八十年代的推进，许多人离开了银行，或者被温和地劝退。即便是那些留下来的人，也带着一种苦乐参半的感觉：这家银行已不像过去那样有趣、那样充满关怀。同时，它也变得前所未有的多元化。 例如，1984 年，Boris S. Berkovitch成为该银行副董事长——这是首位登上摩根高层管理层的犹太人。

A major protagonist in this shifting drama was Preston’s protégé, Dennis Weatherstone, the foreign-exchange wizard from London. A short, trim Englishman with crinkly hair and a quick smile, Weather-stone never lost his working-class accent. He had a natural grace and friendliness, not the cultivated polish of his Morgan colleagues. He joked about his early bookkeeping days as the time he had “no shoes.” During a brief Royal Air Force stint, he had scanned radar screens in simulated air flights, computing fuel usage for planes—an experience, he said, that sharpened his mind for foreign-exchange trading. Weather-stone was the quintessential Casino Age banker—a man versed in new financial instruments, interest swaps, and currency swaps. Early on, he saw the impact of “securitization”—the packaging of loans as tradable securities—on the traditional lending business. In 1980, he became chairman of the bank’s executive committee, right under the blue-blooded president, Rod Lindsay, and then succeeded Lindsay in 1987.
这场戏剧性转变的主要主角之一是普雷斯顿的门徒丹尼斯·韦瑟斯通，那位来自伦敦的外汇奇才。他身材矮小精悍，一头卷发，笑容爽朗，从未改掉他那工人阶层的口音。他天生优雅友善，不像摩根那些同事那样矫揉造作。他常拿自己早年的记账生涯开玩笑，说那是他"没鞋穿"的日子。在短暂服役英国皇家空军期间，他曾在模拟飞行中扫描雷达屏幕，计算飞机的燃油消耗——据他说，这段经历磨练了他做外汇交易时的敏锐思维。韦瑟斯通是典型的赌场时代银行家——精通新型金融工具、利率互换和货币互换。他很早就看到了"证券化"——将贷款打包成可交易证券——对传统借贷业务的影响。 1980 年，他成为该银行执行委员会主席，直接受命于出身高贵的总裁罗德·林赛，随后于 1987 年接替了林赛的职位。

Preston and Weatherstone were complementary and inseparable. “They spoke in a patois,” recalled a colleague. “They were like Siamese twins. One would start a sentence and the other would finish. They were very unlike, but they thought the same.” Since much Morgan influence with central banks derived from its Treasury operation, Weatherstone fit handily into the special relationship with the Fed. “Both he and Preston probably have more credibility with Washington policy makers and regulators than any other bankers I can think of,” said Anthony Solomon, former president of the New York Fed.[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431043) The Preston-Weatherstone team was therefore, predictably, at the center of the 1984 rescue of Continental Illinois Bank and Trust Company.
普雷斯顿与韦瑟斯通相辅相成，形影不离。“他们说话时带着一种只有彼此才懂的行话，”一位同事回忆道，“他们就像连体双胞胎。一个人开了个头，另一个人就能接上。他们性格迥异，但想法却如出一辙。”摩根大通与各国央行的深厚关系很大程度上源于其国库业务，这使得韦瑟斯通与美联储之间那种特殊关系相得益彰。“在我印象中，他和普雷斯顿在华盛顿政策制定者与监管机构面前的可信度，比其他任何银行家都要高，”纽约联邦储备银行前行长安东尼·所罗门如此评价。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431043) 因此，不出所料，普雷斯顿-韦瑟斯通组合在 1984 年大陆伊利诺伊银行与信托公司的救援行动中扮演了核心角色。

The Morgan role had some irony to it. The Chicago bank was a stiff competitor of Morgans and so similar in style and structure that it was called the Morgan of the Midwest. A prestigious, old-line wholesale bank, it had courted rich families and financed much American auto and steel business from its stately, pillared building on South LaSalle Street. In the early 1980s, it vied with Morgans for the title of premier corporate lender. Like Morgans, it had plunged into the roulette world of “liability management”—that is, it financed its operation from the money markets rather than by deposits. Rounding up \$8 billion daily, it borrowed overnight Fed funds, sold CDs, or issued commercial paper. The House of Morgan had played this game with such panache since the days of Ralph Leach that its risks were often obscured. Continental’s collapse would show the extraordinary perils inherent in the new banking.
摩根银行这一角色带有几分讽刺意味。这家芝加哥银行曾是摩根银行的强劲对手，其风格与架构如此相似，以至于被称为"中西部摩根"。作为一家声望卓著的旧式批发银行，它坐落于南拉塞尔街，那座立柱林立、气派不凡的建筑中，专门服务富裕家族，并为美国大量汽车和钢铁业务提供融资。20 世纪 80 年代初，它与摩根银行争夺着顶级企业贷款机构的桂冠。与摩根银行一样，它一头扎进了"负债管理"——这场如轮盘赌般的游戏之中，即通过货币市场而非存款来为其运营融资。每天需要筹集 80 亿美元，它通过借入隔夜联邦基金、出售存单或发行商业票据来实现。自拉尔夫·利奇时代以来，摩根银行一直以如此潇洒的姿态玩着这场游戏，以至于其风险常常被掩盖。而大陆银行的崩溃，将揭示新银行业务中潜藏的巨大危机。

Morgans had long suspected that Continental’s success was a mirage. It undercut competitors too vigorously on real estate, agriculture, and energy loans and rather cavalierly made loans to Chrysler, International Harvester, and other troubled firms. One Morgan official recalled, “All our younger bankers were saying, ‘How do these guys do it? They must be doing it with mirrors.’ They were making loans that any number of banks had shied away from.” Continental was also paying exorbitant interest rates for its \$8 billion. It relied mostly on “hot money”—large, volatile deposits from foreign and domestic institutions. Such jumbo deposits ran anywhere from \$5 million to \$200 million and far exceeded the \$100,000 lid covered by deposit insurance. Managers of such deposits were skittish and apt to pull funds at the first hint of trouble. Yet even so conservative a bank as Morgan Guaranty drew 75 percent of its deposits from “hot money.”
摩根银行一直怀疑大陆银行的成功只是海市蜃楼。后者在房地产、农业和能源贷款上激进打压竞争对手，对克莱斯勒、国际农机等陷入困境的公司更是轻率放贷。一位摩根高管回忆道："我们年轻银行家都在问，'这帮人怎么做到的？肯定是在玩障眼法。'他们做的贷款，不少银行都避之不及。"大陆银行还为吸纳 80 亿美元存款支付了过高的利息，主要依赖"热钱"——来自国内外机构的大额、不稳定的存款。这些巨额存款单笔从 500 万美元到 2 亿美元不等，远远超过存款保险 10 万美元的上限。管理这些存款的机构神经敏感，一有风吹草动就会抽走资金。然而，就连摩根保证银行这样保守的机构，也有 75%的存款来自"热钱"。

Continental began to unravel during the Fourth of July weekend of 1982 with the failure of the Penn Square Bank. This was the notorious Oklahoma shopping-center bank that had booked and resold to Continental \$1 billion in bum energy loans. (One picturesquely modern aspect of Penn Square’s downfall was a run at its drive-in window.) To reassure institutions holding its paper, Continental began to pay higher rates on its CDs. When domestic money managers balked, the bank relied more on Japanese and European funds and sent its financial evangelists abroad to preach calm. “We had the Continental Illinois Reassurance Brigade and we fanned out all around the world,” said David Taylor, Continental’s chairman in 1984.[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431194)
1982 年独立日周末，随着宾州广场银行的倒闭，大陆银行开始分崩离析。这家臭名昭著的俄克拉荷马购物中心银行，曾向大陆银行打包并转售了 10 亿美元的不良能源贷款。（宾州广场银行倒闭时一个颇具现代感的情景，是它的免下车窗口出现了挤兑。）为了稳住持有其票据的机构，大陆银行开始为其存单支付更高的利率。当国内资金管理人犹豫不决时，该银行更多地依赖日本和欧洲的资金，并派遣其金融布道者到海外游说稳定局势。“我们成立了‘伊利诺伊大陆银行安抚团’，向全球各地散布出去，”1984 年时任大陆银行董事长的戴维·泰勒说道。

The bank never fully recuperated from Penn Square, which led to the first global electronic bank run in May 1984. It began with a fugitive rumor floating around Tokyo that an American investment bank was shopping Continental to possible buyers. This triggered the sale of up to \$1 billion in Continental CDs in the Far East, spilling over into panicky European selling the next day. The Continental run was like some modernistic fantasy: there were no throngs of hysterical depositors, just cool nightmare flashes on computer screens.
这家银行从未从 Penn Square 事件中完全恢复过来，这导致了 1984 年 5 月的第一次全球电子银行挤兑。事件始于东京流传的一则捕风捉影的谣言，称一家美国投资银行正在为 Continental 寻找买家。这引发了远东地区高达 10 亿美元的 Continental 存单抛售，次日蔓延至欧洲恐慌性抛售。Continental 挤兑就像某种现代主义幻想：没有歇斯底里的储户人群，只有电脑屏幕上冰冷的噩梦般闪烁。

The bank’s new chairman, David Taylor, pencil-slim, aristocratic, and with a grave voice, struggled to contain the damage. To spike rumors, he sent what he thought was a reassuring telex to two hundred banks around the world. By spotlighting Continental’s troubles, however, it only intensified fears. The next day, Paul Volcker was on the phone with Lew Preston, who expressed skepticism about a private safety net. But in Washington, there was hope that a private credit raised by big banks could restore confidence in Continental. It was a political preference: Reagan administration ideologues were tantalized by a “market solution.” Bankers also thought they could more legitimately claim expanded securities powers if they didn’t always beg for federal protection. Even as the private credit was being organized that Friday, Continental borrowed \$4 billion from the Chicago Fed. During the next week, the “private rescue” would have a slightly fictitious flavor, masking the federal government’s far deeper and more critical involvement.
大陆银行新任董事长戴维·泰勒身材清瘦，气质高贵，嗓音低沉，竭力试图控制事态损失。为平息谣言，他向全球两百多家银行发送了一封自认为能安抚人心的电传。然而，这反而将大陆银行的困境暴露在聚光灯下，加剧了恐慌。次日，保罗·沃尔克致电卢·普雷斯顿，后者对私人救助计划表示怀疑。但华盛顿方面仍寄望于大型银行联合提供的私人信贷能重振市场对大陆银行的信心。这背后是政治考量：里根政府的意识形态信徒对“市场解决方案”跃跃欲试。银行家们也认为，若不再动辄乞求联邦保护，他们要求扩大证券业务权限的诉求将更具正当性。就在那个周五私人信贷方案紧锣密鼓筹备之际，大陆银行已从芝加哥联邦储备银行借入 40 亿美元。 在接下来的一周里，“私人救助”会带上一丝虚构色彩，掩盖联邦政府更为深远和关键的参与。

Why did Continental choose Morgan to lead the rescue—a choice so reminiscent of 1907 and 1929? “Morgan Guaranty was the obvious choice,” explained a former high Continental official. “It had the strongest financial situation and an unquestioned reputation.” Morgans was also Continental’s twin. “We felt Morgan was a similar institution that didn’t have the problems we had, but was similarly funded,” Taylor recalled.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431351) Morgans also got the job by default. Citibank had earlier tried to invade Continental’s Illinois turf, leaving behind acrimonious feelings.
为什么大陆银行会选择摩根来主导这场救助——这个选择让人不禁想起 1907 年和 1929 年的情形？“摩根担保是显而易见的选择，”大陆银行一位前高管解释道。“它财务状况最强，声誉无可挑剔。”摩根同时也是大陆银行的孪生兄弟。“我们觉得摩根是一家与我们相似的机构，没有我们所面临的问题，但资金来源类似，”泰勒回忆道。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431351) 摩根接手这笔业务也几乎是顺理成章。花旗银行此前曾试图入侵大陆银行的伊利诺伊州地盘，留下了不愉快的情绪。

Through a Mother’s Day weekend, with telephone circuits jammed, Preston and Taylor assembled a \$4.5-billion credit line from sixteen banks. These sophisticated bankers relied on primitive methods. Often, they simply called banks, got the security guards on duty, and had them track down their chairmen. Amazingly, the Federal Reserve Board didn’t possess emergency home phone numbers of America’s most powerful bankers. Security Pacific’s chief credit officer was found windsurfing. While bankers haggled over their credit shares, they all knew the gravity of the crisis. As a Continental official said, “They knew that Continental’s problems could spill over into a couple of other banks.” There was a fear that Continental would focus unwelcome attention on Manufacturers Hanover’s Third World debt or the Bank of America’s bad real estate loans. “There were also fifty-odd Midwestern banks that had more than their entire bank capital on deposit at Continental,” said Preston. *“That’s* why it was worth saving.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431477) By Sunday night, the \$4.5-billion credit line was ready.
母亲节周末期间，电话线路被挤得水泄不通，普雷斯顿和泰勒从十六家银行拼凑出了一笔 45 亿美元的信贷额度。这些精明的银行家们却采用了最原始的手段——他们常常直接打电话到银行，找到值班的保安，让他们帮忙追踪行长的下落。令人震惊的是，美联储竟然没有美国最有影响力的银行家们的紧急家庭电话号码。太平洋安全银行的首席信贷官被发现正在玩帆板冲浪。就在银行家们为各自承担的信贷份额讨价还价时，所有人心里都清楚这场危机的严重性。正如大陆银行的一位高管所言："他们都知道大陆银行的问题可能会波及到其他几家银行。"人们担心大陆银行的麻烦会让人们把不友好的目光投向汉华银行（Manufacturers Hanover）的第三世界债务，或者美国银行（Bank of America）的不良房地产贷款。"还有五十多家中西部银行，它们存放在大陆银行的存款超过了自身的全部银行资本金，"普雷斯顿说道。 “所以它才值得挽救。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431477) 到周日晚上，45 亿美元的信贷额度已准备就绪。

On Monday morning, global markets yawned at this show of strength by America’s richest banks. A Pierpont Morgan might have commanded the gold market, but private resources now paled in global markets. The runs continued amid telephone calls between Volcker and Preston. “That Monday, Volcker didn’t call anybody else but Preston, not even the administration,” recalled Irvine Sprague of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation. “It became clear the bankers’ rescue plan was not going to work. Obviously, the government would have to step in the next day.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431638)
周一早上，全球市场对美国最富有的银行展示出的实力无动于衷。或许皮尔庞特·摩根能主宰黄金市场，但私人资源在全球市场面前已显得微不足道。在沃尔克和普雷斯顿之间的电话往来中，挤兑仍在继续。“那个周一，沃尔克除了普雷斯顿谁都没打，连政府也没通知，”联邦存款保险公司的欧文·斯普拉格回忆道。“很明显，银行家的救援计划行不通。显然，政府必须在第二天介入。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431638)

The stakes were tremendous: Continental was larger than all the banks that failed during the Depression *combined*. As a “hot money” bank, it was insured for only about 10 percent of its \$40 billion in “deposits.” Could the world really cope with \$36 billion in losses? Nobody wanted to find out. At a meeting on Tuesday morning, May 15, Volcker, Comptroller of the Currency Todd Conover, and William Isaac and Irving Sprague of the FDIC agreed that a Continental failure would be cataclysmal and decided on an FDIC capital infusion.
局势异常严峻：大陆伊利诺伊银行的规模，比大萧条时期所有倒闭银行的总和还要庞大。作为一家“热钱”银行，其 400 亿美元“存款”中仅有约 10%受保险保障。世界真能承受 360 亿美元的损失吗？没人愿意去验证。在 5 月 15 日周二早上的会议上，沃尔克、货币监理官托德·康诺弗，以及 FDIC 的威廉·艾萨克和欧文·斯普拉格一致认为，大陆银行倒闭将带来灾难性后果，并决定由 FDIC 注入资金。

They sold this idea to Treasury Secretary Donald T. Regan, who wanted to keep alive the private rescue. The banks were to put up a portion of the money. After lunch, Volcker phoned Preston and asked him to set up a summit of seven bank chairmen in New York the next morning. They met in secrecy at Morgan Guaranty’s offices at Fifth Avenue and Forty-fourth Street, an assemblage including the chairmen of Morgans, Chase, Citibank, Bank of America, Chemical, Bankers Trust, and Manufacturers Hanover and the top bank regulators, including Volcker. Chaired by Lew Preston, the meeting had both a sentimental and a combative mood. Some bankers made resounding speeches about past days of Wall Street glory, when the House of Morgan managed private rescues. John McGillicuddy of Manufacturers Hanover argued that the bankers should go it alone. Preston, low-key and conciliatory, let the more vehement bankers talk themselves out. “His style was quite cool,” recalled Irvine Sprague. “He lay back and sort of nudged people. I thought he was very skillful.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431797)
他们向财政部长唐纳德·T·里根兜售了这个想法，而里根本人则希望继续推进私人援救计划。银行需承担部分资金。午餐过后，沃尔克致电普雷斯顿，提议次日上午在纽约召开一场由七家银行董事长参与的峰会。会议秘密举行，地点设在摩根担保信托公司位于第五大道与四十四街交汇处的办公室。与会者包括摩根、大通、花旗、美国银行、化学银行、银行家信托、汉华实业银行的董事长，以及沃尔克等银行监管高层。会议由卢·普雷斯顿主持，气氛既怀旧又充满火药味。几位银行家慷慨陈词，追忆华尔街过往的荣光岁月——那时摩根财团尚能主导私人援救。汉华实业银行的约翰·麦吉利卡迪主张银行家们应独立行动。而普雷斯顿则以低调温和的姿态，任由那些情绪激昂的银行家们畅所欲言。欧文·斯普拉格后来回忆道：“他的风格相当冷静。” 他往后一靠，轻轻推了推身边的人。我觉得他手法相当老练。 [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431797)

There was an element of make-believe in this “bankers’ rescue,” for they pretended to mount a rescue without the necessary resources. Some bank regulators saw the bankers trying to grab credit but pushing the real risk and responsibility onto the government. Citi vice-chair Thomas C. Theobald (later Continental’s chairman) laid down especially stringent conditions for his bank’s participation, asking for absolute government guarantees against risk. As Sprague later wrote, “They wanted it to look as if they were putting money in but, at the same time, wanted to be absolutely sure they were not risking anything. I said I would not vote for such a sham.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431923)
这场“银行家救援行动”带有几分做戏的色彩——他们装作要施以援手，却拿不出足够的资金。一些银行监管者看穿了他们的心思：银行家们既想揽下救援的名声，又把真正的风险和责任甩给了政府。花旗集团副董事长托马斯·C·西奥博尔德（后来担任大陆银行董事长）为本行的参与设下了极其苛刻的条件，要求政府提供绝对的风险担保。正如斯普拉格后来所写：“他们想摆出一副掏钱救援的姿态，同时又确保自己一丁点风险都不沾。我说我绝不会为这种虚伪的把戏投票。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2431923)

That day and the next, restless regulators invited the bankers to provide \$500 million of a \$2 billion capital injection. At the last minute, Citi tried to insert language protecting the bankers from losses. Only a call from Volcker to Citi chairman Walter Wriston in California ended the impasse. It was largely sham heroics by the bankers: after agreeing to their \$500 million, they sat around arguing about how to “lay off” the risk on other banks. William Isaac of the FDIC has said flatly: “The bankers lost no money, and in hindsight their participation was unnecessary.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432078)
当天及次日，不安的监管机构要求银行家们为一项 20 亿美元的注资提供 5 亿美元。最后一刻，花旗试图加入保护银行家免遭损失的条款。直到沃尔克致电远在加州的花旗董事长沃尔特·瑞斯顿，僵局才得以打破。银行家们的表现不过是虚张声势：同意出资 5 亿美元后，他们便围坐在一起争论如何将风险"转嫁"给其他银行。FDIC 的威廉·艾萨克直言不讳："银行家们分文未损，事后看来，他们的参与根本多此一举。" [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432078)

In the end, the FDIC effectively nationalized Continental, taking an 80-percent ownership stake. Setting a breathtaking precedent, it decreed that *all* depositors were insured; it had never before given such a blanket insurance for small bank failures. Washington was now saying that some banks were too big to fail. Yet even the full faith and credit of the U.S. government couldn’t immediately stem the bank run. “Bankers around the world said, ‘So what?’ “ recalled Preston. “They weren’t impressed that the deposits were guaranteed by the U.S. government. *That* surprised me.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432239) Continental Illinois’s aftermath was ironic: although the affair exposed the unacceptable peril of large bank failures in modern financial markets, the government had created new incentives to bypass small banks and keep deposits at large ones.
最终，FDIC 实际上将 Continental 银行国有化，持有了 80%的股份。它开创了一个令人震惊的先例，宣布所有储户都受到保险保护——此前在对小银行倒闭的处理中，从未有过如此全面的一揽子保险措施。华盛顿现在表态：有些银行“大到不能倒”。然而，即便有了美国政府十足的信誉担保，也无法立即止住银行挤兑。“世界各地的银行家都说，‘那又怎样？’”Preston 回忆道，“他们对存款由美国政府担保这一点并不买账。这让我很惊讶。”Continental Illinois 事件的后遗症颇具讽刺意味：尽管该事件暴露了现代金融市场中大型银行倒闭所引发的不可接受的风险，但政府却创造了新的激励，让人们绕过小银行，将存款留存在大型银行中。

Continental Illinois served as a warning about the state of commercial banking in the 1980s. As banks lost their core lending business and tried to maintain profits, they ran into a lengthening list of disasters—in shipping, real estate investment trusts, energy loans, farm loans, and Latin American lending. The Glass-Steagall Act had attempted to insulate commercial banks from risk by separating them from securities work. Instead, it had confined them to a dying business and starved them of profits that might have kept them sane and healthy. By 1984, bank failures were running at a post-Depression high. During the 1980s, the commercial banks faced chronic instability while securities houses raked in record profits. This reversed Glass-Steagall’s intentions and confirmed J. P. Morgan and Company in its decision to move further in the direction of becoming a global investment bank. By the 1987 crash, it would be earning more money from such fee business than from standard loan spreads.
大陆伊利诺伊银行给 1980 年代的商业银行业务敲响了警钟。随着银行失去核心贷款业务，又试图维持盈利，结果遭遇了一连串灾难——航运、房地产投资信托、能源贷款、农业贷款，以及拉丁美洲贷款，问题清单越拉越长。《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》原本试图通过将商业银行与证券业务分离来隔离风险，结果却把银行困在了一个日渐式微的行业里，让它们失去了本可以保持清醒和健康的利润来源。到 1984 年，银行倒闭数量创下了大萧条以来的新高。整个 1980 年代，商业银行长期动荡不安，而证券公司却赚得盆满钵满。这完全违背了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的初衷，也印证了摩根大通公司向全球投资银行转型的决定是正确的。到 1987 年股灾时，摩根大通从这类收费业务中赚到的钱已经超过了传统的贷款利差收入。

FUNERAL rites for the old Wall Street were held in March of 1982, when the SEC enacted Rule 415, which provided for “shelf registration.” This bland technical name masked a bold revolution. Instead of registering each new security issue separately, blue-chip companies could register a large block of stock and sell it off piecemeal on short notice over two years. Thus corporate treasurers could capitalize on sudden dips in interest rates. Rule 415 converted underwriting into a world of fast trades and split-second decisions rather than the old Morgan Stanley world of elegant syndicates formed over several weeks. Companies could even dispense with investment banks and sell straight to institutions. As one Dillon, Read executive gloated, “A lot of Morgan’s biggest clients are the most sophisticated ones. They’re more likely to say, ‘Take a walk, pal.’ ”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432400)
1982 年 3 月，华尔街旧时代的葬礼正式举行，当时 SEC（美国证券交易委员会）通过了 Rule 415（第 415 条规定），即所谓的“暂搁注册”。这个平淡无奇的技术术语背后，隐藏着一场大胆的革命。蓝筹公司无需再逐一注册每一支新证券，而是可以一次性注册一大笔股票，然后在两年内随时分批出售。这样一来，公司财务主管就能抓住利率突然下降的机会套利。Rule 415 将承销业务变成了一场快速交易和瞬间决策的战场，彻底颠覆了昔日摩根士丹利那种花上几周精心组建银团的优雅模式。公司甚至可以不通过投资银行，直接向机构出售证券。正如 Dillon, Read 公司一位高管得意洋洋地说：“摩根许多最大的客户都是最精明的。他们更可能会说：‘一边凉快去，哥们儿。’” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432400)

For Morgan Stanley syndicate chief Thomas A. Saunders III, a sinewy Virginian with thin lips and a wide mouth, the potential of Rule 415 hit him while out jogging one day. Staggered by the implications, he came into work the next morning and spluttered, “Hold it, fellers, this thing is *unbelievable*.” Before long, he was phoning around the Street, telling people, “Holy God, this is insane.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432549) As with the Mayday end to fixed commissions in 1975, Bob Baldwin led the effort to quash the ruling, again dressing up the effort as a crusade to save the regional firms. He hand-delivered a protest letter to the SEC: “The rule may produce fundamental changes in the capital-raising process . . . with undesirable consequences that have not been explored.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432710) His warnings that Rule 415 would damage smaller firms and lead to a Wall Street monopolized by a few large, well-capitalized firms duly materialized.
对于摩根士丹利银团主管托马斯·A·桑德斯三世（Thomas A. Saunders III）——这位嘴唇薄、嘴巴宽、身材精瘦的弗吉尼亚人而言，415 条款的潜在影响是在某天慢跑时猛然击中他的。被其深意震撼后，第二天早上他冲进办公室，结结巴巴地说：“等等，伙计们，这事儿简直难以置信。”不久，他便开始满华尔街打电话，告诉人们：“天哪，这太疯狂了。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432549) 正如 1975 年“五月天”终结固定佣金时那样，鲍勃·鲍德温再次带头试图推翻这项规定，并将此举粉饰成一场拯救地区性公司的圣战。他亲自向美国证券交易委员会递交抗议信：“该规则可能引发资本筹集过程的根本性变革……并带来尚未被充分探究的不良后果。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432710) 他关于 415 条款会损害小公司、导致华尔街被少数资金雄厚的大公司垄断的警告，果然应验了。

Though Morgan Stanley claimed twenty-eight of America’s one hundred largest corporations as clients, many of them favored Rule 415. Exxon, U.S. Steel, and Du Pont even plied the SEC with supporting letters. These rich captives were finally shrugging off their chains. Some critics feared 415 would sweep away fifty years of “due diligence,” with investment banks vouching for the soundness of issues. A Morgan Stanley stamp of approval had always reassured investors. In the Casino Age, however, blue-chip firms no longer needed bankers to certify their health. They often had better credit ratings than their bankers.
尽管摩根士丹利声称美国 100 家最大企业中有 28 家是其客户，但其中许多企业都支持 415 规则。埃克森、美国钢铁和杜邦甚至向美国证券交易委员会提交了支持信。这些富有的"俘虏"终于挣脱了枷锁。一些批评者担心 415 规则会摧毁五十年来投资银行对证券发行稳健性进行担保的"尽职调查"传统。摩根士丹利的批准印章曾始终让投资者放心。然而，在"赌场时代"，蓝筹公司不再需要银行家来证明其健康状况——它们的信用评级往往比其银行家还要高。

The force of 415 was swiftly revealed in its trial usage by AT&T in 1982. A year earlier, Morgan Stanley had mustered a traditional AT&T syndicate of 255 houses. Now, for a \$100-million shelf issue, AT&T invited bids from twenty-one underwriters. Instead of syndicates, Rule 415 operated through “bought deals” in which a firm or group of firms bought the whole issue and quickly resold it. Salomon Brothers and First Boston had been doing such deals for years. Morgan Stanley felt itself under excruciating pressure to win the open contest. “We had been AT&T’s banker previously for all of their equity, as well as much of their debt, and we needed to show that we still were,” said Saunders.[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432854) An internal memo warned of “reputation risk” on the AT&T deal: “We don’t *need* to be first, but it will help establish our role.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433014) This set the stage for a rash attempt by Morgan Stanley to show it could measure up in the savagely competitive new environment. That the crazy stunt succeeded didn’t detract from its folly.
415 条款的威力在 1982 年 AT&T 的试用中迅速显现。一年前，摩根士丹利曾集结了 255 家机构组成的传统 AT&T 承销团。如今，对于一笔 1 亿美元的暂搁注册发行，AT&T 向 21 家承销商公开招标。415 条款不再采用承销团模式，而是通过"买断交易"运作——由一家或多家机构一次性买断全部发行份额，再快速转售。所罗门兄弟和第一波士顿多年来一直在做这种交易。摩根士丹利感到自己在公开竞争中承受着巨大的压力。"我们此前一直是 AT&T 所有股权以及大部分债券的承销商，我们必须证明自己依然是，"桑德斯说道。 [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2432854) 一份内部备忘录就 AT&T 交易发出"声誉风险"警告："我们不必非做第一，但这将有助于确立我们的地位。" [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433014) 这为摩根士丹利急于证明自己能在残酷竞争的新环境中站稳脚跟埋下了伏笔。 那个疯狂的特技虽然成功了，但依然掩盖不了它的愚蠢。

On May 6, 1982, Morgan Stanley agreed to buy two million shares of AT&T stock at \$55.40 a share, \$.15 above the current market price. It hoped to resell the entire parcel the next day. This “bought deal” was really a block trade from AT&T to Morgan Stanley with no syndicate to cushion the risk. Morgan’s chief equity trader, Anson Beard, passed a miserable, sleepless night and later admitted that it was a reckless deal done “for the bragging rights.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433140) Luckily, he disposed of the two million shares the next day, mostly at \$55.65 or a \$.25 profit over cost. If clever advertising, the maneuver exposed the risks associated with 415. For carrying a \$100-million exposure overnight, Morgan Stanley netted a paltry \$400,000. Later in the year, when AT&T raised \$1 billion through a traditional syndicate, Morgan Stanley suddenly had to tolerate four co-managers. Corporate treasurers were the new potentates, and soon even General Motors relied on four investment banks, including Morgan Stanley.
1982 年 5 月 6 日，摩根士丹利同意以每股 55.40 美元的价格买入 200 万股 AT&T 股票，比当时市价高出 0.15 美元。它打算次日将整批股票转手。这笔“包销交易”实际上是 AT&T 与摩根士丹利之间的大宗交易，没有承销团来分担风险。摩根的首席股票交易员安森·比尔德度过了一个痛苦难眠的夜晚，后来承认这是一笔“为了吹嘘资本”而做的鲁莽交易。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433140) 幸运的是，他次日成功脱手了那 200 万股，大部分以 55.65 美元成交，每股净赚 0.25 美元。这场操作如同一次巧妙的广告宣传，暴露了规则 415 所伴随的风险。在持有 1 亿美元风险过夜之后，摩根士丹利仅净赚 40 万美元的微薄利润。同年晚些时候，AT&T 通过传统承销团筹集了 10 亿美元，摩根士丹利不得不突然容忍四个联席主承销商。企业财务官成了新权贵，很快连通用汽车也依赖包括摩根士丹利在内的四家投资银行。

In 1981, Morgan Stanley reigned as number one in underwriting, as it mostly had since 1935. By 1983—after the 415 shake-up—it plummeted to sixth place, with the trading-oriented Salomon Brothers arising as the new leader. Underwriting was now a banal commodity business in which capital and trading prowess counted for far more than did contacts with companies. In a stunning rout, trading firms that were once the outcasts of Wall Street high society toppled the patricians. Morgan Stanley was hardly impoverished: it remained first in stock underwriting and second in the Euromarkets, but it had slipped in relative position and lost its special halo of success. It sometimes seemed to resent this new world. As syndicate chief Saunders griped: “But corporate treasurers are the same as you and me. They want to be innovative, they want to tell the board, ‘Look what I did! I created this competitive situation, I got those five banks beating a path to our door, and wasn’t it wonderful? I’ve taken the shackles off. The issuer is now in control of the world, and here I come.’ ”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433289)
1981 年，摩根士丹利稳居承销业务榜首，自 1935 年以来基本如此。然而到了 1983 年——经历了 415 条款带来的剧变后——它骤然跌至第六位，以交易见长的所罗门兄弟异军突起，成为新的领头羊。承销业务已沦为平庸的商品化生意，资本实力和交易能力远比企业人脉重要。在这场令人瞠目的溃败中，那些曾经被华尔街上流社会排斥的交易型公司，一举击垮了贵族精英。摩根士丹利当然谈不上穷困潦倒：它仍是股票承销的头把交椅，在欧洲市场位居第二，但相对地位已然下滑，昔日成功的光环也黯淡了几分。有时它似乎对这个新世界心怀怨念。正如银团业务负责人桑德斯抱怨的那样："企业财务主管和我们没什么两样。他们想创新，想对董事会说，‘看看我做了什么！我营造了这种竞争局面，让那五家银行争相找上门来，难道不精彩吗？'" 我已挣脱枷锁。发行人如今掌控世界，我来了。’ ” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433289)

The arm’s-length relationship between companies and bankers advocated by Louis Brandeis now evolved in response to market forces and at the behest of corporate America. Far from democratizing Wall Street, this market-based reform merely led to reshuffling among the top firms. Only the Wall Street powerhouses—Morgan Stanley; Goldman, Sachs; First Boston; Merrill Lynch; Salomon Brothers; and Shearson Lehman—had the capital to take big blocks of shares and unload them quickly. Where the six major firms handled a quarter of new debt before 415, they underwrote almost half of it afterward. So the demise of relationship banking didn’t open the doors to scrappy newcomers, as reformers had hoped, but only strengthened the position of those firms that were already dominant.
路易斯·布兰迪斯所倡导的公司与银行之间保持距离的关系，如今在市场力量和美国企业的推动下发生了演变。这种市场化的改革非但没有让华尔街变得民主化，反而只是导致了顶级公司之间的重新洗牌。只有华尔街的巨头——摩根士丹利、高盛、第一波士顿、美林、所罗门兄弟和希尔森·莱曼——才有足够的资本承接大宗股票并迅速抛售。在 415 规则出台前，这六大公司承销了四分之一的新债券；之后，它们几乎承销了一半。因此，关系银行业务的消亡并没有像改革者希望的那样为充满拼劲的新来者打开大门，反而只巩固了那些本就占据主导地位的公司的地位。

Rule 415 came near the end of Bob Baldwin’s tenure at Morgan Stanley, bringing down the curtain on his world of syndication. He once said that if Glass-Steagall were repealed, he would be first on line at Morgan Guaranty’s doorstep the next morning. Yet as chairman of the Securities Industry Association in 1977-78, he had vigorously contested expanded underwriting powers for the commercial banks. “He dealt us a couple of mortal blows,” said Jack Loughran, then Morgan Guaranty’s lobbyist.[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433450) After his SIA stint, Baldwin returned to a Morgan Stanley he understood less and less well. Colleagues thought him lost in a new world of trading and risk that he himself had created.
第 415 号法则的出台，标志着鲍勃·鲍德温在摩根士丹利任期的尾声，也为他所主导的承销世界拉下了帷幕。他曾说过，如果《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》被废除，他第二天一早就去摩根担保公司门口排队。然而在 1977-78 年担任证券业协会主席期间，他却激烈反对扩大商业银行的承销权限。"他给了我们几记致命打击，"时任摩根担保公司说客的杰克·劳伦回忆道。 [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433450) 在证券业协会任职结束后，鲍德温回到一个他越来越难以理解的摩根士丹利。同事们觉得，在他亲手开创的交易与风险新世界里，他显得无所适从。

One ex-partner observed, “When Baldwin came back from the SIA, he became an obstacle. He had lost control of the firm and only made more enemies. He treated everybody like a kid. He would start making a speech and nobody could talk. He was always trying to relive his days of glory as Under Secretary of the Navy. He wouldn’t listen. At the end, nobody wanted him around.” Said another: “He talked at people, he postured, he always liked to be the lordly guy from Morgan Stanley. He was always talking about himself, telling stories where he was the hero.”
一位前合伙人评价道：“鲍德温从 SIA 回来后，就成了绊脚石。他失去了对公司的掌控，反而树敌更多。他把所有人都当小孩看。他一开口演讲，别人就别想插话。他总想重温自己担任海军部副部长时的辉煌岁月。他根本听不进别人的话。到最后，没人愿意他待在身边。”另一位则说：“他只会对人说教，喜欢摆架子，总是一副摩根士丹利大佬的派头。他张口闭口都是自己，讲的故事里，他自己永远是主角。”

To the last, Baldwin remained hypercompetitive and bent on having his way. Yet for all his flaws, the tough, tactless Baldwin had emerged as the most important person in modern Morgan Stanley history, giving the firm the market skills to compete in a world no longer based on old-school ties. He had saved it from genteel obscurity, a languid demise. As the Baldwin era ended in late 1983 without an obvious successor, there was nervous jockeying among three prime contenders—Bob Greenhill, Dick Fisher, and Lewis Bernard. Much was at stake. In a decade, Morgan Stanley had grown tenfold. It now employed about three thousand people and had about \$300 million in capital. Although there was no black or female managing director, the ethnic mix among its eighty managing directors was otherwise surprisingly diverse. But it was more tense and confrontational than in the old days, full of ambitious overachievers.
到最后，鲍德温依然好胜心极强，一意孤行。然而，尽管有种种缺点，这位强硬、直率的鲍德温却成为了现代摩根士丹利历史上最重要的人物，为这家公司注入了在不再依赖老派关系生存的世界中竞争所需的市场技能。他拯救了公司，使其免于沦为默默无闻的优雅衰落。1983 年底，鲍德温时代在没有明确继任者的情况下落幕，三位主要竞争者——鲍勃·格林希尔、迪克·费舍尔和刘易斯·伯纳德——之间展开了紧张的角逐。赌注巨大。十年间，摩根士丹利规模扩大了十倍，员工约三千人，资本约三亿美元。尽管没有黑人或女性董事总经理，但其八十位董事总经理的种族构成却出人意料地多元化。但公司内部的氛围比过去更加紧张和对立，充满了雄心勃勃的过度成就者。

To prevent squabbling, Baldwin was replaced by a surprise choice: forty-nine-year-old Yale-educated S. Parker Gilbert, who claimed a unique Morgan lineage. His *wunderkind* father was the agent general of Germany in the 1920s and a J. P. Morgan partner in the 1930s. Tall and polished, with a wide face, aquiline nose, and urbane manner, Gilbert had his father’s boyish smile but the reserve of Harold Stanley, his stepfather. “Parker was a compromise who wouldn’t piss off Greenhill, Fisher, or Bernard,” explained one former partner. Among the hard-charging deal makers, he had a light touch in arbitrating disputes. According to Robert A. Gerard, a former managing director, “Parker has two things going for him. He has a tremendous instinct for minimizing awkward situations and getting people to work together. Everybody respects his integrity. He really is the glue that holds the firm together.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433608)
为了避免争执，鲍德温被一个出人意料的人选取代：四十九岁、耶鲁毕业的 S.帕克·吉尔伯特，他声称拥有独特的摩根血统。他的天才父亲在 20 世纪 20 年代担任德国总代理，并在 30 年代成为 J.P.摩根的合伙人。吉尔伯特身材高大、举止优雅，宽脸、鹰钩鼻、彬彬有礼，有着父亲孩子气的笑容，却兼具继父哈罗德·斯坦利般的内敛克制。“帕克是个折中人选，不会惹恼格林希尔、费舍尔或伯纳德，”一位前合伙人解释道。在那些雷厉风行的交易撮合者中，他调解争端时手法轻巧。据前董事总经理罗伯特·A·杰拉德所言：“帕克有两样优势：他极善于化解尴尬、促成合作。每个人都敬重他的正直。他真是维系公司团结的粘合剂。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433608)

Gilbert was the most international of the top executives, having done tours in Paris and handled Middle East business. As one would expect, he was close to Morgan Guaranty and specialized in preserving old clients. One Morgan Guaranty vice-president called Parker “the old breed. It was the barracudas down below who had no sense of the relationship.” He was meant to connote tradition at a time of rapid change. Said a former colleague: “Parker is where he is because his father and stepfather were where they were. He’s a symbolic figure, like Harry Morgan.” According to another ex-partner: “The general fiction that people lived by at Morgan Stanley was that he wanted to prove that he was getting ahead for reasons other than being Harold Stanley’s stepson. Then he would go off and play golf.” In fact, whatever the original plans, Gilbert turned into more than a figurehead and became an unexpectedly strong-willed chairman. His caution would be credited with sparing Morgan Stanley some of the ravages of the 1987 crash.
吉尔伯特是最高管理层中最具国际视野的一位，他曾在巴黎任职并负责过中东业务。不出所料，他与摩根保证信托银行关系密切，并擅长维系老客户。一位摩根保证信托的副总裁称帕克为“老派人物”。而“下面那些毫无关系观念的梭子鱼们”则指代另一类人。在快速变革的时代，他象征着传统。一位前同事评价道：“帕克能有今天的位置，是因为他父亲和继父曾身居高位。他是个象征性人物，就像哈里·摩根。”另一位前合伙人则说：“摩根士丹利的人普遍相信一个虚构故事，即他想证明自己出人头地并非因为他是哈罗德·斯坦利的继子。然后他就会去打高尔夫球。”事实上，无论最初的计划如何，吉尔伯特最终不仅是个象征性人物，还成为了一个出人意料地意志坚定的董事长。 他的谨慎被认为帮助摩根士丹利减轻了 1987 年股灾的重创。

The real heir to Bob Baldwin was probably Dick Fisher. A polio victim who was paralyzed from the hip down and walked with a cane, Fisher applied for a job at Morgan Stanley upon graduating from Harvard Business School, but first he needed to conquer his own doubts about his ability to perform. Partners also had wondered how a handicapped person could take business trips and move around freely. Bright, sociable, and popular, he would prove a master politician and an adept handler of people. “Dick is tough underneath but doesn’t appear so,” remarked a former colleague. “He can be a ruthless man with velvet gloves.”
鲍勃·鲍德温真正的继承人恐怕是迪克·费希尔。费希尔患有小儿麻痹症，腰部以下瘫痪，走路需要拄拐。从哈佛商学院毕业后，他申请了摩根士丹利的工作，但首先要克服对自己能力的疑虑。合伙人曾担心残疾人如何出差、如何自由活动。但他聪明、善于交际、人缘好，最终证明自己是一位杰出的政治家和善于用人的高手。一位前同事评价道：“迪克看似温和，实则强硬。他是一位戴着天鹅绒手套的狠角色。”

Fisher found the answer to his handicap in the sedentary world of trading. Working out of a soundproof glass box, the so-called lean-to on the trading floor, he organized the new bond trading operation in the 1970s. (With nice symmetry, his brother David would head J. P. Morgan Securities.) It was a lucky choice. Like Dennis Weatherstone at Morgan Guaranty, Fisher’s stock rose at Morgan Stanley in direct proportion to the growing vogue for trading on Wall Street.
费希尔在交易这个久坐不动的行业里找到了应对自身缺陷的答案。他当时在一个隔音玻璃箱里——也就是交易大厅所谓的“披屋”里工作，在 1970 年代组建了新的债券交易业务。（颇有对称意味的是，他的兄弟戴维后来执掌了 J.P.摩根证券。）这是个幸运的选择。正如摩根担保信托的丹尼斯·韦瑟斯通一样，费希尔在摩根士丹利的地位，直接随着华尔街对交易的日益追捧而水涨船高。

Another critical figure was Lewis Bernard, nicknamed Brainy Bernard, the first Jewish partner and probably the best strategic thinker in the firm. He chaired a 1979 task force to devise a ten-year plan for Morgan Stanley. It was Bernard who introduced the computerized, multicurrency system for global trading that would make Morgan Stanley a trendsetter. In 1983, he headed a new fixed-income division that belatedly led the firm into the trading of gold, precious metals, and foreign exchange and the issuing and trading of commercial paper, municipal bonds, and mortgage-backed securities—all tools to please increasingly demanding corporate customers. These activities also required more traders and salespeople, further transforming Morgan Stanley into an anonymous global financial conglomerate.
另一个关键人物是刘易斯·伯纳德，绰号“智多星伯纳德”，他是公司第一位犹太合伙人，也可能是最出色的战略思想家。1979 年，他主持了一个特别工作组，为摩根士丹利制定十年规划。正是伯纳德引入了全球交易的电脑化多币种系统，使摩根士丹利成为行业风向标。1983 年，他主管新成立的固定收益部门，这使公司终于涉足黄金、贵金属及外汇交易，以及商业票据、市政债券和抵押贷款支持证券的发行与交易——所有这些都是为了满足日益挑剔的企业客户需求。这些业务也要求更多的交易员和销售人员，进一步将摩根士丹利转变为一家匿名的全球金融集团。

In a critical strategic step, Morgan Stanley, so averse to foreign markets in the early post-war era, made a major commitment to trade and distribute securities overseas. It wisely abolished all managerial distinctions between its domestic and international operations. As trading and mergers superseded underwriting at Morgan Stanley, elegance was out and brashness was in—a jarring experience for its once sedate culture. As the *New York Times* said in 1984, “Morgan executives, who for generations have seemingly enjoyed their reputation for being the aristocrats of investment banking, seem confused these days about their image.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433769) As its exclusive ties with clients lapsed, it had to hustle for business and encourage aggression. As at Morgan Grenfell, a firm once mocked for stuffiness proved it could be ruthless if necessary in preserving its privileges.
在战后初期极度排斥海外市场的摩根士丹利，做出了一项关键的战略性举措——大力投身海外证券交易与分销业务。它明智地废除了国内与国际业务之间的所有管理界限。随着交易与并购逐渐取代承销成为摩根士丹利的主业，优雅让位于张扬——这对曾以沉稳著称的企业文化而言，是一次颠覆性的冲击。正如《纽约时报》1984 年所言：“世代以来似乎都乐于享有‘投行贵族’美誉的摩根高管们，如今却在自身形象问题上显得困惑不已。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433769) 当独家客户关系逐渐瓦解，它不得不主动争抢业务，并鼓励员工更具进攻性。就像摩根建富一样，这家曾因保守古板而被嘲笑的机构，在必要时为保住自身特权展现出了毫不留情的魄力。

In the takeover area, Morgan Stanley shed all pretense of passivity. In 1978, merger specialist R. Bradford Evans had said, “We don’t pester our clients and say, ‘Why don’t you accept this company, or that company, anything to get a deal.’ ”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433918) In 1981, Morgan Stanley still led Wall Street with \$40 million in merger fees, doing a third of all deals. Then Goldman, Sachs, First Boston, and Lehman Brothers leapt ahead. By 1983, under intense competitive pressure, Morgan’s takeover department of seventy-five professionals was scouring the landscape in search of target companies. They started banging on doors, using the hard sell. “In the past there was a reluctance to call people,” said Bob Greenhill. “It was the culture to let the client call you.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434066) Now Morgan Stanley would increasingly serve as an engine of the takeover boom.
在收购领域，摩根士丹利彻底撕下了被动面具。1978 年，并购专家布拉德福德·埃文斯曾表示："我们不会纠缠客户，不会说'为什么你不接受这家公司，或者那家公司，只要能成交什么都行'。" [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2433918) 1981 年，摩根士丹利仍以 4000 万美元的并购费用领跑华尔街，占据了所有交易的三分之一。随后，高盛、第一波士顿和雷曼兄弟迎头赶上。到了 1983 年，在激烈的竞争压力下，摩根士丹利 75 位专业人士组成的收购部门开始四处搜寻目标公司。他们开始主动敲门，采取硬性推销策略。"过去我们不愿意主动打电话给别人，"鲍勃·格林希尔说，"等待客户来电是公司的文化。" [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434066) 如今，摩根士丹利正日益成为并购热潮的引擎。

As its underwriting business declined, Morgan Stanley turned to businesses it once would have rejected haughtily, entering the netherworld of junk bonds. These high-risk high-yield bonds were often issued to support takeovers by companies of questionable solidity. The new junk bond department coincided with Morgan Stanley’s sudden interest in small start-up companies. As Bob Greenhill explained, “Morgan was building a high-technology effort at that time, and I said, ‘How can we not be in a business that is so necessary for so many of our growing clients?’ ”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434215)
随着承销业务的下滑，摩根士丹利转而涉足那些它曾一度傲慢拒绝的业务，进入了垃圾债券的领域。这些高风险高收益的债券通常是为支持那些根基不稳的公司的收购而发行的。新的垃圾债券部门恰逢摩根士丹利突然对小型初创公司产生兴趣。正如鲍勃·格林希尔所说：“摩根当时正在打造高科技业务，我说，‘我们怎么能不参与一项对我们众多成长型客户如此重要的业务呢？’”

Junk bonds revolutionized Wall Street by magnifying the money available to corporate raiders. Where conglomerate takeovers in the 1960s used share exchanges, and cash was the method of choice in the 1970s, the junk bond market let corporate raiders flout the Wall Street establishment and finance their incursions by selling bonds to investors. The merger frenzy was also fueled by abundant money from commercial banks, whose dwindling prospects in wholesale lending attracted them to the financing of takeovers. Thus both sides of Wall Street—commercial and investment banking—found takeover work a salvation from the fundamental crises in their core lending and underwriting business. The razzle-dazzle of Wall Street in the Reagan years would obscure this underlying fragility, the irreversible decline of traditional businesses, the obsolescence of the traditional banker.
垃圾债券通过放大企业掠夺者可调用的资金，彻底改变了华尔街的格局。20 世纪 60 年代的集团收购依赖股票交换，70 年代则以现金为主，而垃圾债券市场让企业掠夺者得以无视华尔街的既有秩序，通过向投资者出售债券来为其入侵融资。并购狂潮还得到了商业银行大量资金的助推——这些银行在批发贷款领域前景黯淡，因此转向为并购提供融资。于是，华尔街的两大阵营——商业银行和投资银行——都将并购业务视为摆脱其核心放贷与承销业务根本危机的救赎。里根时代华尔街的浮华喧嚣掩盖了这种潜在的脆弱性：传统业务的不可逆转衰退，以及传统银行家的过时落伍。

With considerable hoopla, Morgan Stanley talked about gentrifying junk bonds, but self-congratulation proved premature. In mid-1982, Morgan Stanley joined with Hambrecht and Quist to sponsor the first public offerings of People Express, the pioneering no-frills discount airline. Buying up used Lufthansa planes and ripping out their first-class sections, People founder Donald C. Burr wanted to create cheap air travel for the masses; his feisty, hustling airline was the anti-thesis of a classic Morgan client. Between 1983 and 1986, Morgan Stanley underwrote more than \$500 million in junk bonds for People. As if still leary of its junk bond departure, the firm overruled custom and let Charles G. Phillips, head of its junk bond department, take a seat on People’s five-member board.
摩根士丹利大张旗鼓地谈论着将垃圾债券推向高端市场，但事实证明，他们的沾沾自喜为时过早。1982 年年中，摩根士丹利与 Hambrecht and Quist 联手，为"人民快线"（People Express）——这家开创性的低价无附加服务航空公司——进行了首次公开募股。人民快线的创始人 Donald C. Burr 收购了汉莎航空的二手飞机，拆掉了头等舱，一心要为大众打造廉价航空。这家充满干劲、活力四射的航空公司，与摩根士丹利传统的客户形象截然相反。从 1983 年到 1986 年，摩根士丹利为人民快线承销了超过 5 亿美元的垃圾债券。或许是对自己涉足垃圾债券这一步仍心存疑虑，该公司打破常规，允许其垃圾债券部门主管 Charles G. Phillips 进入人民快线的五人董事会。

From modest beginnings at Newark International Airport, People rocketed to the top rank of American airlines. In the end, Burr was victimized by his own ambition, making an ill-fated purchase of Frontier Airlines and trying to beat the major carriers on their own turf. He financed his frantic expansion by a staggering accumulation of debt. Later it was alleged in lawsuits that Phillips egged on Burr to borrow and expand too quickly. Whatever the truth, when crisis struck People Express, some bondholders felt betrayed by Morgan Stanley. They not only suffered serious losses, with some paper trading as low as 35 percent of the original issue price, but accused Morgan Stanley of failing to maintain a market during the turbulence. By one account, Morgan Stanley bought People bonds until it had \$40 million worth in its inventory.
从纽瓦克国际机场的简陋起点起步，People Express 火箭般蹿升至美国航空公司的顶尖行列。最终，Burr 被自己的野心反噬：他鲁莽收购了 Frontier Airlines，并试图在巨头们的领地上与其正面交锋。为了支撑这场疯狂的扩张，他背负了令人咋舌的巨额债务。后来，诉讼中指控 Phillips 怂恿 Burr 过度借贷、急速扩张。无论真相如何，当 People Express 陷入危机时，部分债券持有人感到被 Morgan Stanley 背叛了。他们不仅蒙受了惨重损失——部分债券的交易价格一度跌至发行价的 35%——还指责 Morgan Stanley 未能在动荡期维持市场秩序。据一种说法称，Morgan Stanley 曾持续购入 People 债券，直至其库存累积到 4000 万美元。

There was a second dimension to the controversy. For two and a half months, as bondholders suffered, Morgan’s M&A Department shopped People to potential customers, exposing the firm to a possible conflict of interest. Honoring the Chinese wall, Charles Phillips kept the merger talks secret and didn’t notify the bond traders of these efforts. Yet bondholders felt deprived of information that should have been provided by Morgan Stanley as the bond underwriter. The problem here was not that Morgan Stanley neglected its duties. The problem was that the conglomerate structure of modern Wall Street presented Morgan Stanley with incompatible duties. For People investors, the end was short, nasty, and brutish. When Frank Lorenzo of Texas Air bought People Express, he paid 75 percent on the face value of unsecured bonds and 95 percent on secured bonds.
这场争议还有第二个层面。在两个半月的时间里，当债券持有人蒙受损失时，摩根士丹利的并购部却在向潜在客户兜售人民捷运公司，这使该公司面临潜在的利益冲突。为了维护"中国墙"，查尔斯·菲利普斯对并购谈判秘而不宣，没有将相关努力告知债券交易员。然而，债券持有人认为摩根士丹利作为债券承销商，本应向他们提供这些信息。问题不在于摩根士丹利玩忽职守，而在于现代华尔街的联合企业结构赋予了摩根士丹利相互冲突的职责。对人民捷运的投资者而言，结局来得短暂、糟糕而残忍。当德州航空的弗兰克·洛伦佐收购人民捷运时，他对无担保债券支付了面值的 75%，对有担保债券支付了面值的 95%。

In the early takeover days, there had existed a clear-cut distinction for Morgan Stanley between its stalwart clients and their takeover targets. It would never represent a raider against a client because it then might sacrifice that client’s lucrative underwriting fees. But as underwriting ties with blue-chip companies decayed, there was no reason why the firm shouldn’t represent raiders as well. The very notion of a client list—a sacred group of companies—broke down. In the new transactional environment, Morgan Stanley might represent a raider one year, then defend another client against that same raider the next. So many deals were germinating in the big merger departments that shifting loyalties and conflicts of interest were inevitable.
在摩根士丹利早期接管业务的时代，其稳健的老客户与收购目标之间界限分明。它绝不会代表掠夺者与客户对抗，因为那样可能会牺牲客户带来的丰厚承销费。但随着与蓝筹公司的承销关系逐渐弱化，公司没有理由不也为掠夺者服务。"客户名单"这一神圣的公司群体概念就此瓦解。在新的交易环境中，摩根士丹利今年可能代表某个掠夺者，明年又可能为另一位客户抵御同一掠夺者的进攻。大型并购部门中酝酿着如此多的交易，忠诚的转变和利益冲突在所难免。

The inescapable danger was illustrated by Morgan Stanley’s relationship with T. Boone Pickens of Mesa Petroleum. Back in the 1970s, Morgan Stanley couldn’t have dealt with a corporate predator like Pickens, because he menaced its seven-sister clients. But as the big oil companies grew less loyal to Morgan Stanley, so the firm gladly dealt with other energy firms. In 1982, T. Boone Pickens enlisted Morgan Stanley to raid the Dallas-based General American Oil. “They were delighted to get the business,” recalled Pickens, “for GAO was not part of the establishment that Morgan caters to.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434363) On January 6, 1983, Pickens signed a standstill agreement with GAO stipulating that he wouldn’t buy stock or try to gain control of the firm; it was bought the next day by Phillips Petroleum. Morgan brokered a deal by which Phillips Pete bought 38 percent of Mesa’s shares and even picked up its \$15 million in fees to the bankers and lawyers. During the summer of 1983, Morgan Stanley and Pickens briefly thought of teaming up to bust up a major oil company. The deal fell through, Pickens thought, when Morgan decided not to “alienate some of their clients—the Good 01’ Boys in spades.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434516)
摩根士丹利与梅萨石油公司 T·布恩·皮肯斯的关系，正揭示了这种无法避免的危险。早在 20 世纪 70 年代，摩根士丹利不可能与像皮肯斯这样的企业掠夺者打交道，因为他威胁到了其“七姐妹”客户。但随着大型石油公司对摩根士丹利的忠诚度下降，该行也乐于与其他能源公司合作。1982 年，T·布恩·皮肯斯聘请摩根士丹利对达拉斯的通用美国石油公司发起突袭。皮肯斯回忆道：“他们很高兴拿到这笔生意，因为通用美国石油并非摩根士丹利所迎合的业界巨头。”1983 年 1 月 6 日，皮肯斯与通用美国石油签署了一份暂停协议，规定他不得购买该公司股票或试图获得控制权；次日，该公司被菲利普斯石油收购。摩根士丹利促成了一项交易，菲利普斯石油购买了梅萨公司 38%的股份，甚至为其支付了 1500 万美元的银行家和律师费用。 1983 年夏天，摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）和皮肯斯（Pickens）曾短暂考虑联手拆解一家大型石油公司。但皮肯斯认为，这笔交易最终告吹，因为摩根士丹利决定不去“疏远某些客户——那些根深蒂固的老派势力”。 [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434516)

In December 1984, Pickens tried to swallow Phillips Pete, now represented by Morgan Stanley’s Joseph G. Fogg III, a young, prematurely balding man who wore clear-framed glasses and had a cool, precise, intellectual look. As with Greenhill, clients found him abrasive but tolerated him for his brilliance. He was a whirling dervish in the nonstop oil takeovers. In 1984, he advised Standard Oil of California in its \$13.4-billion takeover of Gulf Oil, the largest on record. Morgan Stanley banked a royal fee of \$16.5 million for its work, even though it had committed no capital and used perhaps a dozen people.
1984 年 12 月，皮肯斯试图吞并菲利普斯石油（Phillips Pete），而这家公司当时由摩根士丹利的约瑟夫·G·福格三世（Joseph G. Fogg III）代理。这位年轻人过早谢顶，戴着透明框架眼镜，外表冷静、精准，一副学者模样。和格林希尔（Greenhill）一样，客户们觉得他说话刻薄，但因其才华横溢而忍让三分。在无休止的石油收购战中，他就像个不知疲倦的旋风斗士。1984 年，他协助加州标准石油（Standard Oil of California）以 134 亿美元收购海湾石油（Gulf Oil），创下当时最大收购纪录。摩根士丹利为此赚取了 1650 万美元的丰厚佣金，尽管它既没有投入任何资本，也只用了大概十几个人。

The 1984 battle for Phillips Petroleum ended up pivoting on the standstill agreement of early 1983. It was a ticklish situation. Fogg and Pickens, who were then partners, were now opponents, and their recollections of the agreement were different. Fogg said he and Joe Flom had told Pickens the agreement would apply to Phillips as well as GAO. Pickens had “recognized the fact, expressed lack of concern and proceeded to execute the agreement.” contended Fogg.[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434651) “Other people involved in the GAO deal remembered it differently,” replied Pickens, who claimed that the deal applied only to GAO. And it was Pickens who won a favorable court ruling. He observed, “Since Fogg was working for us at the time, it cost him and Morgan credibility on Wall Street.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434803)
1984 年争夺菲利普斯石油公司的战役，最终的关键落在了 1983 年初的那份暂停协议上。当时情况相当棘手。曾是合作伙伴的福格和皮肯斯此时成了对手，两人对那份协议的解读也出现了分歧。福格声称，他和乔·弗洛姆曾明确告知皮肯斯，协议同样适用于菲利普斯公司，而不仅仅是 GAO。福格坚持道：“皮肯斯当时认可了这一事实，表示并不在意，并随后签署了协议。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434651) 皮肯斯则反驳：“参与 GAO 交易的其他人的记忆可不一样。”他坚称协议仅针对 GAO。最终，法院作出了有利于皮肯斯的裁决。他评论道：“既然福格当时是替我们办事的，这件事让他在摩根士丹利以及华尔街的信用大打折扣。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434803)

Just as it seemed that Rule 415 had liberated companies from their Wall Street bankers and opened up a permanent distance between them, a new trend, called merchant banking, arose to obliterate the trend. Morgan Stanley had always been a service outfit, never compromising its objectivity by acting as an investor as well. Bob Baldwin had said in 1980, “We’re so client-oriented and so busy that most of us have done very poor jobs of investing our money. That would not be the case with some firms, which have had all kinds of entrepreneurial investments.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434956) That year, the firm conquered its prudery. It took a share in an Exxon oil-shale project in Australia, scouted timber investments, expanded its real-estate portfolio, and started to invest in high-tech start-ups. For the first time, Morgan Stanley was acting as a principal (investing its own money) and not just as an agent (advising clients on investing their money.) These investments foreshadowed a Wall Street fad that would entail a retrogression to some of the worst excesses of the robber baron era.
就在规则 415 看似让公司摆脱华尔街银行家、拉开两者永久距离之时，一种名为"商人银行"的新趋势兴起，逆转了这一潮流。摩根士丹利向来是一家服务机构，从不以投资者身份行事，以免损害其客观性。鲍勃·鲍德温在 1980 年曾说："我们太以客户为导向，业务太过繁忙，以至于大多数人都不擅长投资自己的钱。而有些公司则不然，它们进行了各种创业投资。" [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2434956) 那一年，摩根士丹利摒弃了这种矜持。它入股了埃克森美孚在澳大利亚的一个油页岩项目，考察了木材投资，扩充了房地产投资组合，并开始投资高科技初创企业。摩根士丹利首次以委托人身份（用自己的钱投资）行事，而不仅仅是作为代理人（为客户投资提供建议）。) 这些投资预示着一股华尔街风潮，它将导致倒退至强盗贵族时代最糟糕的过度行为。

In 1982, to inaugurate the new merchant-banking operation, Morgan Stanley hired Donald P. Brennan, a former vice-chairman and fifteen-year veteran of International Paper. It was rare for an industrialist to work on Wall Street. The tough, fierce-willed Brennan would bring unique management skills, for he thought like a corporate executive. The firm set up a short-lived leveraged buyout fund with CIGNA Insurance, which reaped twenty-five times its original investment and whetted its appetite for more. Nobody envisioned how central the small merchant-banking group would eventually become to Morgan Stanley.
1982 年，为启动新的商业银行业务，摩根士丹利聘请了唐纳德·P·布伦南——他曾任国际纸业副董事长，并在该公司任职十五年。实业家投身华尔街实属罕见。这位强硬果敢的布伦南将带来独特的管理技能，因为他以企业高管的思维模式行事。公司与信诺保险共同设立了一个短暂的杠杆收购基金，该基金获得了原始投资 25 倍的回报，并助长了其更大胃口。当时无人预见，这个小型商业银行部门最终会成为摩根士丹利的核心支柱。

Breaking with another tradition, Morgan Stanley in 1980 began managing money for individuals and institutions. Another Chinese wall went up around another department. Some Wall Street firms balked at entering the business, fearing possible conflicts as they invested money in companies that were also takeover or underwriting clients. Once again, Morgan Stanley’s decision conferred legitimacy on a dubious practice. As money manager Sanford Bernstein said, “If Morgan Stanley is in it, that means we’re kosher.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435102) In 1980, the firm had less than \$1 billion under management, an amount that swelled to \$10 billion by 1985. After details appeared in the press about Citibank’s management of Kuwait’s money, the sheikdom switched over about \$4 billion in securities to Morgan Stanley’s money managers. In the days of Harry Morgan, the firm had refused to do business with the Teamsters. Now it beat out twelve other firms for a contract to manage America’s largest and most controversial pension fund, the \$4.7-billion Teamsters Central States fund.
打破另一项传统，摩根士丹利于 1980 年开始为个人和机构管理资金。又一道防火墙在另一个部门竖起。一些华尔街公司不愿涉足这一业务，担心在投资那些同时也是并购或承销客户的企业时可能产生利益冲突。摩根士丹利的决定再次为一项有争议的做法赋予了合法性。正如资金管理公司桑福德·伯恩斯坦所言：“如果摩根士丹利参与其中，那就意味着我们没问题。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435102) 1980 年，该公司管理的资产不足 10 亿美元，到 1985 年已膨胀至 100 亿美元。当花旗银行管理科威特资金的细节见诸报端后，这个酋长国将约 40 亿美元的证券转交给摩根士丹利的资金管理人管理。在哈里·摩根时代，该公司曾拒绝与卡车司机工会合作。 如今，它击败了其他 12 家公司，赢得了一份管理美国规模最大、也最具争议的养老基金的合同，即价值 47 亿美元的 Teamsters Central States fund。

Morgan Stanley’s new wing caused a small revival of the relationship with Morgan Guaranty. The Morgan bank was still legally barred from distributing mutual funds but could offer investment advice. So it agreed with Morgan Stanley in 1982 to create the first Pierpont Fund, with Morgan Stanley managing and Morgan Guaranty advising it. This family of funds would attract \$2 billion through a time-honored Morgan formula of scorning the vulgar herd. They had a \$25,000 minimum, versus the standard \$1,000, and unlike other mutual funds, they didn’t report their daily results in the newspapers. In the words of Morgan Stanley’s Matthew Healey, “We didn’t want to take the Pierpont funds into the competitive arena.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435245) Everywhere else, however, Morgan Stanley was right smack in the middle of competition for the first time and was playing as rough as anybody.
摩根士丹利的新部门让该公司与摩根担保的关系略有回暖。尽管法律上仍禁止摩根银行分销共同基金，但它可以提供投资建议。因此在 1982 年，摩根银行与摩根士丹利达成协议，共同创立了首只皮尔庞特基金，由摩根士丹利负责管理，摩根担保提供投资建议。这一系列基金沿袭了摩根家族一贯蔑视平庸大众的传统，最低投资额设定为 2.5 万美元，而行业标准仅为 1000 美元，并且不像其他共同基金那样在报纸上公布每日业绩。用摩根士丹利马修·希利的话来说：“我们不想让皮尔庞特基金卷入竞争漩涡。”然而，在其他领域，摩根士丹利却破天荒地首次正面投身竞争，手段之强硬丝毫不逊于任何对手。
