# Chapter Seventeen: Depression

AFTER the crash, Herbert Hoover wasn’t quite as passive or impotent as legend suggests. He announced tax cuts and public works programs and asked utilities to embark on new construction. Bringing business leaders to the White House, he extracted pledges to maintain wages and thus avert an erosion of purchasing power. Henry Ford cut car prices and boosted workers’ wages to \$7 a day. Meanwhile, the New York Fed orchestrated a swift series of interest-rate cuts that more than halved its discount rate, to 2½ percent, by June of 1930. Clearly, the principle of government action to ease economic misfortune was enshrined before the New Deal.
大萧条爆发后，赫伯特·胡佛并不像传说中的那般被动或无能。他宣布减税和公共工程计划，并要求公用事业公司启动新工程。他把商界领袖请到白宫，让他们承诺维持工资水平，从而避免购买力下滑。亨利·福特降低了汽车价格，并将工人日薪提高到 7 美元。与此同时，纽约联邦储备银行迅速策划了一系列降息行动，到 1930 年 6 月，贴现率已降至 2.5%，比之前降低了一半有余。显而易见，政府采取行动缓解经济困境的原则，早在“新政”之前就已确立。

Wall Street tried to face the crash with stoic fortitude and treat it as a stern but salutary lesson. Everybody sounded philosophical. In late 1929, Lamont described the crash as an unpleasant warning of no lasting harm: “I cannot but feel that it may after all be a valuable lesson and the experience gained may be turned to our future advantage. . . . There has never been a time when business as a whole was on a sounder basis.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312051) This reasonable approach reflected a belief that the financial trouble had ended; in fact, it had just begun.
华尔街试图以坚忍的态度面对崩盘，将其视为一场严厉但有益的教训。每个人都显得淡定从容。1929 年末，拉蒙特将崩盘形容为一次令人不快的警告，不会造成持久伤害：“我不得不认为，这或许终究是一堂宝贵的课，汲取的经验未来可能对我们有利……从未有过哪个时期，商业整体处于比现在更坚实的基础之上。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312051) 这种理性的态度反映了一种信念：金融困境已经结束；而事实上，它才刚刚开始。

Never entirely comfortable with the radical, tax-cutting Republicanism of the twenties, Morgan partners hoped the crash presaged a return to more conservative economics. They had been uneasy with the speculative debauch of the twenties and welcomed a return to thrift and hard work. Dwight Morrow, then a New Jersey senator, agreed that “there is something about too much prosperity that ruins the fiber of the people.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312235) Russell Leffingwell viewed the slowdown as a “healthy purge” after a seven-year bacchanalia: “The remedy is for people to stop watching the ticker, listening to the radio, drinking bootleg gin, and dancing to jazz . . . and return to the old economics and prosperity based upon saving and working.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312388) Such comments savored of puritans punishing the wicked. Treasury Secretary Andrew Mellon, who had ducked a leadership role after the crash, now said of the downturn, “It will purge the rottenness out of the system. People will work harder, live a more moral life.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312585) Keynes, however warned that such austerity would only deepen the Depression.
摩根合伙人对二十年代激进的减税共和主义始终心存芥蒂，他们期盼这次崩盘能预示经济回归保守路线。二十年代的投机狂欢让他们忧心忡忡，如今总算盼到了勤俭节约的回归。时任新泽西参议员的德怀特·莫罗也认为："过度繁荣总有某种东西会腐蚀人民的筋骨。" [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312235) 拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔则将这场经济放缓视为七年纵欲后的"健康排毒"："良方在于人们别再盯着股票行情机、别再听广播、别再喝私酿杜松子酒、别再跳爵士舞……回归基于储蓄和劳动的旧式经济学与繁荣。" [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312388) 这番言论充满了清教徒惩戒罪人的意味。财政部长安德鲁·梅隆在崩盘后一直逃避领导角色，如今却对经济萧条评论道："这将涤荡体制中的腐朽。人们会更努力劳动，过更有道德的生活。" [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312585) Keynes,然而有人警告说，这种紧缩政策只会加深大萧条。

Many of the people who voiced these soothing statements were living off the riches of the 1920s. Although Morgan partners suffered huge losses, they still boasted wealth of embarrassing proportions. At Christmas 1928, each partner had received a bonus of \$1 million. In 1929, Jack’s son Junius moved into Salutation, a forty-room stone mansion on an island beside his father’s island estate. Even as stock brokers jumped from building ledges in October, workmen in Bath, Maine, rushed to complete *Corsair IV*, a six-thousand-horsepower yacht, 343 feet long, with gross tonnage of 2,181. Reputed to be the biggest private yacht of all time, a floating palace with elevators, beamed ceilings, India teak paneling, mahogany armchairs, and fireplaces, it required a crew of over fifty and cost Jack an estimated \$2.5 million. If the price tag was stupendous, it amounted to only about half the annual income that Jack took from the bank in the late 1920s.
许多说出这些安抚之词的人，实则依仗着 20 世纪 20 年代的财富过活。尽管摩根公司的合伙人蒙受了巨额亏损，他们拥有的财富仍多到令人咋舌。1928 年圣诞节，每位合伙人都分到了 100 万美元的奖金。1929 年，杰克的儿子朱尼厄斯搬进了"问候庄园"，这是一栋拥有四十个房间的石砌宅邸，坐落在父亲庄园旁的一座小岛上。就在同年 10 月股票经纪人纷纷跳楼之际，缅因州巴斯市的工人们正赶工建造"海盗四号"——一艘 6000 马力、长 343 英尺、总吨位 2181 吨的游艇。这被誉为有史以来最大的私人游艇，堪称水上宫殿，配有电梯、雕花天花板、印度柚木镶板、红木扶手椅和壁炉，需要 50 多名船员操作，据估算花费了杰克约 250 万美元。虽说这个价签令人瞠目，但这也不过是杰克 20 年代末从银行获得的年收入的一半左右。

Jack Morgan spent the Christmas of 1929 with his fifteen grandchildren at Matinicock Point, and it was a warm, happy time. “It really resembled nothing so much as some of the families of pigs I have seen on the farm,” he said.[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312744) In the new year, he looked forward to a cruise to Palestine with his friend Dr. Cosmo Lang, the archbishop of Canterbury.
杰克·摩根（Jack Morgan）在马蒂尼科克角（Matinicock Point）与他的十五个孙辈一起度过了 1929 年的圣诞节，那是一段温馨快乐的时光。“这真是跟我在农场上看到的那些猪的一家子没什么两样。”他打趣道。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312744) 新年伊始，他盼着与朋友坎特伯雷大主教科斯莫·朗博士（Dr. Cosmo Lang）一起乘船去巴勒斯坦旅行。

What made the postcrash lull tolerable on Wall Street was that a political backlash hadn’t yet gathered force. Nobody yet demanded radical overhauls of the system. That December, learning of proposed staff cuts at the American Museum of Natural History, Jack covered the budget shortfall; the munificence of rich men still meant something. Soon, though, the Depression would unleash a popular fury against bankers that would rage for years.
华尔街之所以能忍受股灾后的短暂平静，是因为政治反击尚未蓄势待发。那时，还没人要求对体系进行大刀阔斧的改革。那年 12 月，得知美国自然历史博物馆（American Museum of Natural History）计划裁员，杰克便填补了预算缺口——富人的慷慨在当时依然举足轻重。然而很快，大萧条就会引爆一场针对银行家的全民怒火，这股愤怒将持续数年之久。

Wall Street perhaps had better excuses for postcrash complacency in 1929 than in 1987. America boasted a trade and budget surplus and was finishing the most triumphant economic decade in its history. In the world economy, it was the ascendant power and the leading creditor nation. J. P. Morgan and Company was so flush with cash that it was giving large gifts to its less fortunate London and Paris partners in the late 1920s. The age can be forgiven some of its hubris.
华尔街或许在 1929 年股市崩盘后的自满比 1987 年更有道理。美国当时坐拥贸易和预算盈余，正迎来其历史上最辉煌的经济十年。在世界经济中，它是崛起的霸主和主要债权国。摩根大通公司现金充裕，以至于在 1920 年代末向伦敦和巴黎那些境况不佳的合作伙伴慷慨赠送大笔礼物。那个时代的些许傲慢，倒也情有可原。

The speculative mood didn’t immediately disappear. Those with money who rushed in to buy stocks were at first vindicated: by early 1930, the market had regained much lost ground. People chattered about a little bull market. Business investment rose, accompanied by an upturn in car and home sales. On March 7, 1930, President Hoover proclaimed: “All the evidence indicates that the worst effects of the Crash upon unemployment will have passed during the next sixty days.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312948)
投机情绪并未立即消散。那些有钱冲进去抄底的人起初似乎是对的：到 1930 年初，市场已收复大部分失地。人们热议着这轮小牛市。商业投资回升，汽车和房屋销量也随之上涨。1930 年 3 月 7 日，胡佛总统宣称：“所有证据表明，股灾对失业的最严重影响将在未来六十天内过去。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2312948)

In April, however, the stock market began to slide, then dropped in May and June with each new Hoover expression of hope. Unlike the spectacular downward swoops of the previous October, the deterioration in prices was small and steady but unrelenting. In mid-1932, the market would bottom out at one-tenth of its September 1929 peak. So the yokels who sold in terror after the Crash fared better, in the long run, than the canny traders who scouted for bargains.
然而，股市在四月份开始下滑，随后随着胡佛每次表达乐观预期，五、六月份继续下跌。与去年十月那场惊心动魄的暴跌不同，这次股价的下跌幅度虽小却持续不断，且毫不留情。到了 1932 年中期，市场将触底，市值仅为 1929 年 9 月峰值的十分之一。因此，从长远来看，那些在股灾后因恐慌而抛售的"乡巴佬"，反而比那些四处搜寻廉价股的"精明"交易者过得更好。

We shall never know whether wise economic management might have averted the Great Depression. But two events led to a frightful, downward momentum. On June 17, 1930, ignoring the pleas of over a thousand American economists, President Hoover took up six gold pens and signed the Hawley-Smoot Tariff Act. Its heavy tariffs would account for more than half the price of some imports. The day before Hoover signed the bill, the stock market, in nervous anticipation, suffered its worst day since Tragic Tuesday.
我们永远无法知道，如果当时有明智的经济管理，是否能避免这场大萧条。但有两件事导致了可怕的下行势头。1930 年 6 月 17 日，总统胡佛无视了上千名美国经济学家的恳求，拿起六支金笔，签署了《霍利-斯穆特关税法》。该法案规定的重关税，占某些进口商品价格的一半以上。在胡佛签署该法案的前一天，股市因紧张预期，遭遇了自"悲惨星期二"以来最糟糕的单日表现。

As a major sponsor of foreign loans, the House of Morgan was naturally dismayed. If debtors couldn’t export goods to the United States, how would they ever earn foreign exchange and pay off loans? “I almost went down on my knees to beg Herbert Hoover to veto the asinine Hawley-Smoot tariff,” Lamont declared.[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313095) He soon referred to the world trading system as an insane asylum.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313255) Apprehensively, America’s most international bank watched the rise of a new economic nationalism. It would dismantle the structure of free trade and free flow of capital that the House of Morgan, along with Montagu Norman and Ben Strong, had struggled to create in the twenties. Within two years, two dozen countries would retaliate against the Hawley-Smoot tariff by raising their own tariffs and slashing U.S. imports. The age of “beggar thy neighbor” economics had begun.
作为对外贷款的主要赞助方，摩根财团自然感到沮丧。如果债务国无法向美国出口商品，他们又怎能赚取外汇来偿还贷款呢？拉蒙特宣称：“我几乎要跪下恳求赫伯特·胡佛否决那愚蠢至极的霍利-斯穆特关税法。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313095) 很快，他将世界贸易体系形容为一座疯人院。 [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313255) 这家美国最具国际性的银行忧心忡忡地注视着新经济民族主义的崛起。这股浪潮将摧毁摩根财团与蒙塔古·诺曼、本·斯特朗在二十年代苦心构建的自由贸易和资本自由流动的体系。两年之内，二十多个国家纷纷提高本国关税、削减美国进口，以此报复霍利-斯穆特关税法。“以邻为壑”的经济时代就此拉开序幕。

The second great mid-1930 blunder was made by the Federal Reserve Board in Washington: it ended the liberal provision of credit and shrank the money supply. This was part of an attempt to rein in the New York Fed and end its backdoor diplomacy with European ministries. Treasury Secretary Andrew Mellon wanted higher interest rates to stop the flow of gold to Europe. Many at the Fed saw austerity as a bitter but necessary medicine. “The consequences of such an economic debauch are inevitable,” said the Philadelphia Fed governor. “Can they be corrected and removed by cheap money? We do not believe that they can.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313447) By the second half of 1930, the postcrash calm was gone. That fall, Hoover complained to Lamont about bear raids, short selling, and other unpatriotic assaults against national pride. The following year would be the worst in stock market history.
第二次大萧条中的重大失误来自华盛顿的美联储理事会：它终止了宽松的信贷政策，并缩减了货币供应量。此举旨在约束纽约联储，切断其与欧洲各国财政部之间的秘密外交。财政部长安德鲁·梅隆希望提高利率，以阻止黄金流向欧洲。美联储内部许多人将紧缩政策视为良药苦口。费城联储行长表示：“这种经济放纵的后果在所难免。难道靠廉价货币就能纠正和消除吗？我们不相信。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313447) 到了 1930 年下半年，崩盘后的平静已不复存在。那年秋天，胡佛向拉蒙特抱怨熊市袭击、卖空等损害国家尊严的不爱国行径。而接下来的那一年，将成为股市历史上最黑暗的一年。

While the Fed had assumed responsibility for the health of the entire financial system after the 1929 crash, the House of Morgan still played a part in specific, smaller crises. The Fed had no obligation to rescue individuals, banks, or companies; its concerns were more general. The crash’s aftermath revealed much about Morgan priorities. While claiming to represent the public interest, the firm actually represented its clients, cronies, and fellow bankers. Part of its power had always stemmed from its fidelity to Wall Street friends, its generosity in making loans to bankers and other financial houses. This was strikingly demonstrated after the crash.
尽管 1929 年股市崩盘后，美联储承担起维护整个金融体系健康的重任，但在某些具体的小规模危机中，摩根家族依然扮演着重要角色。美联储没有义务拯救任何个人、银行或公司——它的关注点更为宏观。这场大崩盘的余波充分揭示了摩根的优先考量：尽管它声称代表公众利益，实际上却始终为自己的客户、密友和银行家同行服务。其部分权力始终源于对华尔街友人的忠诚，以及向银行家和金融机构慷慨放贷的作风。这一点在崩盘后的表现中尤为突出。

Take, for instance, the case of Charles E. Mitchell, chairman of the National City Bank. Right before the crash, Mitchell had put together a deal to merge with the Corn Exchange; if he had succeeded, he would have produced the world’s largest bank, surpassing even the Midland Bank in London. Since the deal was to be effected with National City shares, Mitchell needed to maintain their price at \$450. During the crash, the price plunged through this floor, and even furious buying by the National City Company, the bank’s securities affiliate, couldn’t sustain it. On his way to work, Mitchell stopped by 23 Wall and walked out with a \$ 12-million personal loan, secured by his own National City stock. When he later failed to meet payments, the House of Morgan temporarily became the second largest stockholder in National City. Later Mitchell said of Morgans, “That firm stood at the very peak as to ethics, understanding, and leadership.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313612) Yet however laudable in terms of loyalty, it was a reckless loan from a financial standpoint.
以国家城市银行董事长查尔斯·E·米切尔为例。就在崩盘前夕，米切尔刚敲定了一桩与谷物交易所银行的合并案；若成功，他将缔造全球最大的银行，甚至超越伦敦的米德兰银行。由于交易将以国家城市银行股票进行，米切尔需要将股价维持在 450 美元。崩盘期间，股价暴跌突破这一底线，即便国家城市公司（该银行的证券关联机构）疯狂买入也无法支撑。上班途中，米切尔顺道去了华尔街 23 号，出来时已用自己的国家城市银行股票作抵押，获得了一笔 1200 万美元的个人贷款。后来他未能如期还款，摩根财团暂时成了国家城市银行的第二大股东。米切尔后来谈及摩根家族时说道：“那家公司在道德、远见和领导力上都处于巅峰。””然而，尽管从忠诚角度看值得称赞，但从财务角度看，这却是一笔鲁莽的贷款。

Loyalty to clients was always the vice of the House of Morgan. Sometimes it got in so deep it couldn’t get out. After the crash, the Van Sweringen brothers, those financial acrobats of the 1920s, suddenly lost their balance. Their overly indebted railroads were among the worst crash performers. Much like William C. Durant with General Motors in 1920, the Van Sweringens kept buying Alleghany stock on its way down. Using borrowed money, they only augmented their losses. They ignored polite warnings from Morgans to stop their rash purchase of more railroads, including the huge Missouri Pacific. Buying on credit had become a Van Sweringen habit.
对客户的忠诚一直是摩根银行的软肋。有时深陷其中，难以自拔。市场崩盘后，1920 年代那些金融杂技演员——范斯韦林根兄弟突然失去了平衡。他们负债累累的铁路公司是崩盘中表现最差的股票之一。就像 1920 年威廉·C·杜兰特面对通用汽车时那样，范斯韦林根兄弟在阿勒格尼股票持续下跌时仍不断买入。他们靠借来的钱操作，反而放大了损失。摩根银行曾委婉警告他们停止鲁莽收购更多铁路公司（包括庞大的密苏里太平洋铁路公司），但他们置若罔闻。透支消费已成了范斯韦林根兄弟的惯常做法。

The Alleghany shares feverishly bid up in the frothy days of early 1929 now led the market down in the autumn of 1930. They fell from 56 to 10 in just two months. On the evening of October 23, 1930, Oris and Mantis Van Sweringen met at Tom Lamont’s East Seventieth Street townhouse along with representatives of Guaranty Trust. The short, moon-faced brothers were \$40 million in hock to their broker. Having sponsored \$200 million in securities in their behalf, Morgans and Guaranty felt bound to prop them up. Lamont was pessimistic about the prospects of railroads. He was already telling Hoover how two hundred affluent passengers were arriving daily by air in New York City. Yet he also feared a domino effect among Wall Street brokers who dealt with the brothers.
1929 年初春，那些在狂热氛围中被竞相抬高的阿勒格尼股票，到 1930 年秋已领跌市场。短短两个月内，它们就从 56 美元暴跌至 10 美元。1930 年 10 月 23 日晚，奥里斯与曼蒂斯·范·斯沃林根兄弟在汤姆·拉蒙特位于东七十街的联排别墅，与担保信托公司的代表会面。这对身材矮小、圆脸盘的兄弟欠经纪人 4000 万美元。由于曾为他们承销 2 亿美元的证券，摩根与担保信托公司深感有责任出手扶持。拉蒙特对铁路行业的前景并不乐观。他早已向胡佛提及，每天有两百位富有的乘客乘飞机抵达纽约。但他也担心，与这两兄弟打交道的华尔街经纪商会引发连锁反应。

The two banks led a syndicate that furnished a \$40-million rescue loan for the Van Sweringens. The rescue was handled with a delicacy and secrecy that seldom accompanies personal bankruptcy. The Van Sweringens would remain as figureheads so that nobody would suspect their true plight. They were rewarded for their profligacy with a personal allowance of \$ 100,000 a year. In the words of Matthew Josephson, “The Van Sweringens’ personal insolvency during 5 years was one of the best kept secrets in Wall Street.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313855) The following year, when the brothers missed payments on their loans, Morgans and Guaranty Trust foreclosed on their Alleghany Railroad empire. Ultimately Alleghany stock would fall to 37½ cents per share.
那两家银行牵头组成银团，为范斯韦林根兄弟提供了 4000 万美元的救助贷款。这笔救援行动处理得极为谨慎和隐秘，个人破产案中极少能做到如此。范斯韦林根兄弟继续担任名义上的负责人，以免外界察觉他们的真实困境。作为对其挥霍无度的“奖励”，他们每年获得 10 万美元的个人津贴。用马修·约瑟夫森的话来说：“范斯韦林根兄弟五年来的个人资不抵债状态，是华尔街保守得最好的秘密之一。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2313855) 次年，当兄弟俩无力偿还贷款时，摩根银行与担保信托公司没收了他们旗下联合铁路帝国的抵押品。最终，联合铁路公司的股价跌至每股 37.5 美分。

As a lender of last resort, the House of Morgan favored like-minded institutions of similar character and background. Kidder, Peabody was just such a firm. It didn’t hustle business or steal clients and always played by Morgan rules. In 1930, it was hit by multiple blows. The Italian government removed \$8 million in deposits, and the new Bank for International Settlements instructed Kidder to switch big sums to a Swiss bank. This led to another rescue at Jack Morgan’s home, chaired by George Whitney, who had started his career as a Kidder clerk. The House of Morgan arranged a \$10-million line of credit. Under Whitney’s tutelage, the old Kidder, Peabody was folded. Whitney brought in his friends Edwin Webster, Chandler Hovey, and Albert H. Gordon to take over the company’s name and goodwill. “Incidentally, we are slowly making the grade socially,” Gordon reported to the elder Webster. “Yesterday for the first time Morgan invited us to tea on our way out from the almost daily conference.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314025)
作为最后贷款人，摩根财团青睐那些秉性相似、背景相仿的机构。Kidder, Peabody 正是这样一家公司——它不抢生意、不挖客户，始终遵循摩根的游戏规则。然而 1930 年，这家公司连遭重创：意大利政府撤走了 800 万美元存款，新成立的国际清算银行又要求它将大笔资金转入一家瑞士银行。这迫使杰克·摩根在家中主持了又一次救援会议，由乔治·惠特尼牵头——此人职业生涯正是从 Kidder 的职员起步。摩根财团安排了 1000 万美元的信贷额度。在惠特尼的操盘下，老牌 Kidder, Peabody 被重组，惠特尼拉来朋友埃德温·韦伯斯特、钱德勒·霍维和阿尔伯特·H·戈登，接过了公司的名号与商誉。“顺带说一句，我们在社交圈里总算慢慢站稳脚跟了，”戈登向老韦伯斯特汇报，“昨天从几乎每天一次的会议出来时，摩根第一次邀请我们喝了下午茶。”“ [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314025)

Unstinting in serving its friends, the House of Morgan could be heart-less to those whose image didn’t fit the preferred profile. This became apparent with the failure of the Bank of United States on December 11, 1930. With 450,000 depositors, it was New York’s fourth largest deposit bank. In general, the crash and subsequent deflation had damaged the collateral behind bank loans. From a rate of 60 bank failures a month in early 1930, the figure snowballed to 254 in November and 344 in December of 1930. There were over a thousand bank failures for the year. The failure of the Bank of United States was the largest thus far and threatened more general ruin.
摩根银行对朋友不遗余力，却对那些形象不合其喜好的人冷酷无情。这一点在 1930 年 12 月 11 日美国银行倒闭事件中暴露无遗。这家银行拥有 45 万储户，是纽约第四大储蓄银行。总体而言，股市崩盘及随后的通缩严重损害了银行贷款的抵押品价值。1930 年初，每月倒闭的银行数量为 60 家，到 11 月已滚雪球般增至 254 家，12 月更是达到 344 家。全年共有超过一千家银行倒闭。而美国银行的倒闭是当时规模最大的一次，并引发了更广泛的崩溃危机。

But this bank wasn’t a high-class operation. Its Jewish owners had chosen its grand name in an effort to fool its Jewish immigrant customers into thinking it had government support. In the lobby hung a large oil portrait of the U.S. Capitol, reinforcing the misleading message. A proposed rescue plan for the Bank of United States got a cool reception on Wall Street, even after Lieutenant Governor Herbert H. Lehman, the state banking authorities, and the New York Fed all pleaded for it. The regulators wanted to merge the Bank of United States with three other banks, backed by a \$30-million loan from the Wall Street banks.
但这可不是什么高档银行。它的犹太老板起了个气派的名字，就是想糊弄那些犹太移民储户，让他们以为这银行有政府撑腰。大厅里挂着一幅美国国会大厦的大幅油画，更是加深了这种误导。就连副州长赫伯特·H·莱曼、州银行监管机构和纽约联储都出面求情，华尔街对这家美国银行的救助方案还是冷眼相待。监管机构想把美国银行和其他三家银行合并，再向华尔街银行借 3000 万美元来撑腰。

At an emotional meeting, Joseph A. Broderick, the state banking chief, warned that if the bankers rejected the rescue plan, it might drag down ten other banks. One in ten New York families using bank accounts would be stranded. As the Wall Street bankers sat stony-faced, Broderick reminded them how they had just rescued Kidder, Peabody and how they had banded together years before to save Guaranty Trust. But they refused to save the Jewish bank, pulling out of their \$30-million commitment at the last minute. “I asked them if their decision to drop the plan was still final,” Broderick recalled. “They told me it was. Then I warned them they were making the most colossal mistake in the banking history of New York.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314202) The biggest bank failure in American history, the Bank of United States bankruptcy fed a psychology of fear that already gripped depositors across the country.
在一次情绪激动的会议上，州银行业主管约瑟夫·A·布罗德里克警告说，如果银行家们拒绝接受救援计划，可能会拖垮另外十家银行。纽约每十个拥有银行账户的家庭中，就有一个会陷入困境。当华尔街的银行家们面无表情地坐着时，布罗德里克提醒他们，不久前他们刚刚救过 Kidder, Peabody，几年前他们还联手拯救了 Guaranty Trust。但他们拒绝拯救这家犹太银行，在最后一刻撤回了 3000 万美元的承诺。“我问他们放弃这个计划是否仍是最终决定，”布罗德里克回忆道，“他们告诉我是的。然后我警告他们，他们正在犯下纽约银行业历史上最大的错误。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314202) 美国历史上最大的一次银行倒闭——美国银行破产事件，进一步加剧了全国范围内储户们已经弥漫的恐惧心理。

The failure of the Bank of United States has been attributed to anti-Semitism among Wall Street bankers. At the time, there were few commercial banks owned by Jews, Manufacturers Trust being the only other important one in New York. It is impossible to verify whether anti-Semitism stopped the bankers from rescuing the Bank of United States. But Morgan records show that its clientele’s Judaism was very much in the partners’ minds. When informing Morgan Grenfell of events in New York, Lamont’s son Tommy noted that it was patronized largely by foreigners and Jews.[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314387) Russell Leffingwell described it as “an uptown bank with many branches and a large clientele among our Jewish population of small merchants, and persons of small means and small education, from whom all its management was drawn.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314668) Their attitude was shortsighted, for the bank’s failure shook confidence across America. It was a failure that could have been easily avoided by the proposed merger.
美国银行的倒闭被归咎于华尔街银行家的反犹太主义。当时犹太人拥有的商业银行寥寥无几，制造商信托银行是纽约另一家重要的此类银行。无法证实反犹太主义是否阻止了银行家们救助美国银行。但摩根的记录显示，其客户的犹太身份在合伙人心中举足轻重。在向摩根建富通报纽约情况时，拉蒙特的儿子汤米指出，该银行的顾客主要是外国人和犹太人。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314387) 拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔将其描述为“一家位于上城区的银行，拥有多家分行，客户群主要是我们犹太人口中的小商人、收入微薄且受教育程度不高的人，其管理层也全部来自这一群体。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314668) 他们的目光短浅，因为该银行的倒闭动摇了全美的信心。而这场倒闭原本可以通过拟议的合并轻易避免。

Had it not been for the large number of depositors, the Bank of United States would not have deserved to survive. Its securities affiliate had sponsored shoddy stocks and issued misleading prospectuses, and had been manipulated by the bank’s own offices. Two of its owners were jailed for loose banking practices. One, bank president Bernard K. Marcus, was the uncle of Roy Cohn, who always blamed the bank’s failure on an anti-Semitic plot. Even banking superintendent Broderick was indicted for not having shut the bank sooner. (He was acquitted, after two trials.) To have to bail out such a bank undoubtedly grated on the patrician bankers. But with so many Morgan rescues occurring in those years, all backed up with high-flown rhetoric about saving the banking system, it’s hard to believe religion wasn’t a major factor behind Wall Street’s refusal to act. Hundreds of thousands of Jewish depositors were not worth one Charles Mitchell. Jews were always a blind spot in the Morgan vision, no less than in the days when Pierpont Morgan had vied with Jacob Schiff.
若不是有大量储户，美国银行根本不值得被挽救。其证券附属机构曾兜售劣质股票、发布误导性招股说明书，并被银行内部高管操纵。两位所有者因银行管理混乱而入狱，其中一位是银行总裁伯纳德·K·马库斯，他正是罗伊·科恩的叔叔——科恩始终将银行倒闭归咎于反犹太阴谋。就连银行监管长布罗德里克也因未及早关闭银行而遭到起诉（经两次审判后无罪释放）。救助这样一家银行无疑让那些贵族银行家们感到厌恶。但在那些年，摩根系救助行动频频上演，每次都冠以拯救银行体系的高调说辞，实在让人难以相信华尔街拒绝出手的背后没有宗教因素在作祟。数十万犹太储户的分量，竟抵不过一个查尔斯·米切尔。 在摩根的视野中，犹太人始终是一个盲点，与当年皮尔庞特·摩根和雅各布·希夫较劲的年代毫无二致。

THE City of London had reacted to the New York crash with alarm, but also with some quiet satisfaction and *schadenfreude*, After Black Thursday, the *New York Times* reported that the “selling left London’s ’City’ in a comfortable position saying ’I told you so.’ It had been expected for a long time. ”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314874) In many ways, London profited from the crash as investors switched funds from New York, easing the strain on British gold reserves. In 1930, there was even a brief spurt in foreign lending as London became a safe haven for investors. At the same time, the deeper prognosis for Britain remained grim. Its industry languished, its unemployment rose, and London’s port was vulnerable to spreading protectionism. Several Commonwealth countries dependent on agricultural exports—Australia, Canada, and India—were hit early by the Depression, and this hurt the City.
伦敦金融城对纽约股市崩盘的反应是警惕，但也夹杂着几分暗自得意与幸灾乐祸。黑色星期四之后，《纽约时报》报道称，"抛售潮让伦敦金融城处境从容，一副'我早就告诉过你们'的姿态。人们对此已期待已久。" [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2314874) 从许多方面来看，伦敦从这场崩盘中获益，因为投资者将资金从纽约转移过来，缓解了英国黄金储备的压力。1930 年，伦敦甚至出现了一波短暂的外贷热潮，成为投资者的避风港。与此同时，英国更深层的预后依然严峻。工业萎靡不振，失业率攀升，伦敦港易受日益蔓延的保护主义影响。几个依赖农产品出口的英联邦国家——澳大利亚、加拿大和印度——早早受到大萧条的冲击，这也让金融城雪上加霜。

England’s real crisis, however, originated in Central Europe, just as Montagu Norman had always suspected it would. Reparations continued to burden Germany’s economy and polarize its politics. In March 1930, Dr. Schacht submitted his resignation as Reichsbank president to protest additional German debt mandated by the Young Plan. Germany’s day of reckoning—so feared and so long predicted—was at hand. In the elections of September 1930, the National Socialists and the Communists scored sizable gains, and Chancellor Heinrich Brüning adopted an antireparations policy. The right wing capitalized on the reparations issue. On January 5, 1931, Dr. Schacht attended a dinner party thrown by Hermann Göring. For his tough stand on reparations, Schacht had become a great favorite of the National Socialists. At the dinner, he met Hitler and Joseph Goebbels and became a critical link between the Nazis and German big business. That spring, as political street fights broke out in Germany, pressure mounted to cast off the Versailles burden.
然而，英格兰真正的危机源于中欧，正如蒙塔古·诺曼一直所预料的那样。赔款问题持续拖累德国经济，并使其政治走向两极分化。1930 年 3 月，沙赫特博士辞去德意志帝国银行行长一职，以抗议扬格计划强加给德国的额外债务。德国清算的日子——如此令人恐惧且长期被预言——终于来临了。在 1930 年 9 月的选举中，国家社会党（纳粹）和共产党取得了大幅进展，总理海因里希·布吕宁转而采取反对赔款的政策。右翼势力借赔款问题大做文章。1931 年 1 月 5 日，沙赫特博士出席了赫尔曼·戈林举办的一场晚宴。由于在赔款问题上的强硬立场，沙赫特深得国家社会党的青睐。晚宴上，他会见了希特勒和约瑟夫·戈培尔，由此成为纳粹与德国大企业之间的关键纽带。 那年春天，当德国的政治街头斗殴愈演愈烈时，摆脱凡尔赛和约枷锁的压力与日俱增。

Into this already volatile situation came the powerful jolt of a major bank failure. On May 11, 1931, the Credit Anstalt failed. It was not only Austria’s largest bank but probably the most important bank in Central Europe. A rescue plan announced by the Austrian National Bank and Rothschilds only served to alert the world to trouble and brought on a run. The disaster spread through Central Europe, collapsing Austrian and German banks. In June, Norman gave emergency credit to Austria’s central bank to prop up the schilling—his swan song as a global lender of last resort. Along with an emergency loan to Germany, it marked the end of British financial leadership in the 1930s.
就在这风声鹤唳之际，一场大型银行倒闭事件犹如当头一棒。1931 年 5 月 11 日，奥地利信贷银行宣告破产。这家银行不仅是奥地利最大的银行，恐怕也是中欧最重要的金融机构。奥地利国家银行和罗斯柴尔德家族随后公布的纾困计划，反而向全世界敲响了警钟，引发大规模挤兑。危机席卷中欧，奥地利和德国的银行接连崩塌。6 月，诺曼向奥地利中央银行提供紧急贷款，以支撑先令——这竟是他作为全球最后贷款人的绝唱。加上对德国的一笔紧急贷款，此举标志着 20 世纪 30 年代英国金融霸权的终结。

It was against this backdrop that Lamont telephoned Hoover on June 5, 1931, to propose a holiday on payment of war debts and reparations. Without it, he warned, there might be a European crash that could prolong America’s Depression. As Lamont’s files show, Hoover reacted in a grumpily defensive manner: “I will think about the matter, but politically it is quite impossible. Sitting in New York, as you do, you have no idea what the sentiment of the country at large is on these inter-governmental debts.” A banker of the Diplomatic Age, Lamont didn’t merely couch his argument in economic terms: he made an unashamedly political appeal. “These days you hear a lot of people whispering about sidetracking the Administration in the 1932 Convention,” Lamont told Hoover. “If you were to come out with such a plan as this, these whisperings would be silenced overnight.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315026) In closing, Lamont said that if the plan ever reached fruition, the bank would hide its role and let Hoover take the credit: “This is your plan and nobody else’s.” What a cunning fellow Lamont was when he whispered in Hoover’s ear\!
正是在这样的背景下，拉蒙特于 1931 年 6 月 5 日致电胡佛，提议暂缓战争债务和赔款的偿付。他警告说，若不如此，欧洲可能崩溃，进而拖长美国的萧条期。从拉蒙特的文件来看，胡佛的反应颇为抵触，带着防御姿态："这事我会考虑，但从政治角度看，根本行不通。你坐在纽约，根本不明白整个国家对这些政府间债务的真实情绪。"身为外交时代的银行家，拉蒙特并非仅从经济层面阐述理由——他毫不掩饰地打起了政治牌。"这些天来，常有人私下嘀咕，要在 1932 年的党代会上把你挤下去，"拉蒙特对胡佛说，"如果你能拿出这样一个方案，那些流言蜚语一夜之间就会烟消云散。"” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315026) 最后，拉蒙特说，如果这个计划最终得以实现，银行将隐藏自己的角色，让胡佛独揽功劳：“这是你的计划，不是别人的。”拉蒙特在胡佛耳边低语时，真是个狡猾的家伙！

Treasury Secretary Mellon tried to spike the idea and dismiss the debt as Europe’s mess, but Hoover had now had enough with myopic isolationism. On the evening of June 20, 1931, he telephoned Lamont at Torrey Cliff, his home on the Palisades, to say that he had just announced a one-year moratorium on war debt and reparations payments. He knew France would be indignant at the mercy shown toward Germany and asked whether Lamont could sell the plan to the French. Lamont expressed sympathy for the French position but also reminded Hoover that they were the world’s most difficult people to deal with—a recurring theme in his letters. Finally, though, he agreed to lobby the French government through the Banque de France. True to Hoover’s predictions, the French thought the moratorium an Anglo-American plot to let Germany escape reparations.
财政部长梅隆试图压制这一想法，将债务问题轻描淡写地归咎于欧洲自找的麻烦，但胡佛已经受够了这种短视的孤立主义。1931 年 6 月 20 日晚，他给正在帕利塞兹家中托里悬崖的拉蒙特打电话，告知他自己刚刚宣布暂停战争债务和赔款一年。他深知法国会对德国受到的宽待感到愤慨，于是问拉蒙特能否向法国推销这一计划。拉蒙特对法国的立场表示同情，但也提醒胡佛，法国人是世界上最难打交道的人——这是他信中的老生常谈。不过，他最终还是同意通过法兰西银行向法国政府进行游说。正如胡佛所料，法国人认为这次暂停是英美合谋让德国逃脱赔款。

The Hoover moratorium was a belated response to a crumbling world financial system. The Danat Bank, one of Germany’s largest, failed on July 13, 1931. A teary-eyed Chancellor Briining rejected a New York rescue out of fear that a bad loan to President Hindenburg’s son Oskar might surface in such an operation. After the Danat failure, Germany had to shut the Berlin bourse and the city’s banks. Around the world, creditors were calling in German loans. The Morgan-led bond issues for Germany and Austria, heralded with trumpet peals in the 1920s, plummeted with frightening speed. All the laborious work of that decade was coming apart.
胡佛缓债令是对摇摇欲坠的世界金融体系的迟来之举。1931 年 7 月 13 日，德国最大银行之一的达纳特银行宣告倒闭。含泪的总理布吕宁拒绝了纽约方面的救助，担心在此过程中会暴露兴登堡总统之子奥斯卡的一笔不良贷款。达纳特银行倒闭后，德国不得不关闭柏林交易所及市内所有银行。全球各地的债权人纷纷向德国追讨贷款。摩根牵头的德国与奥地利债券发行——曾在 20 年代吹响号角般辉煌——此刻以骇人的速度暴跌。那十年间所有艰苦努力，皆在分崩离析。

Now the crisis shifted to London, as investors traced financial ties between Germany and England. During the summer of 1931, investors dumped sterling in massive amounts. Even without Germany, the pound was already in a parlous state. In late July 1931, a committee of bankers and economists, the May Committee, had predicted that Britain’s budget deficit would widen to £120 million, with no end of red ink in sight. The committee recommended higher taxes and a 10-percent cut in the dole. A few days later, sterling cracked on world markets. The Bank of England told Philip Snowden, the chancellor of the Exchequer, that Britain had almost exhausted its foreign exchange. Despite the need for stringency, Ramsay MacDonald’s Labour government was stymied in coping with the problem. With 2.5 million unemployed, the unions wouldn’t surrender unemployment benefits.
现在，危机转移到了伦敦，因为投资者发现了德国与英国之间的金融联系。1931 年夏天，投资者大量抛售英镑。即便没有德国的问题，英镑本身也岌岌可危。1931 年 7 月下旬，由银行家和经济学家组成的梅委员会（May Committee）预测，英国的预算赤字将扩大到 1.2 亿英镑，而且赤字似乎看不到尽头。委员会建议增税并削减 10%的失业救济金。几天后，英镑在世界市场上崩溃。英格兰银行告知财政大臣菲利普·斯诺登（Philip Snowden），英国的外汇储备几乎耗尽。尽管需要紧缩政策，拉姆齐·麦克唐纳（Ramsay MacDonald）的工党政府在应对这一问题时却陷入僵局。由于有 250 万人失业，工会拒绝放弃失业救济金。

A few days before the May report was published, Monty Norman left the bank “feeling queer.” A year before, tired and wrung out, he had taken a two-month vacation in South America. Now haggard from overwork, the high-strung Norman was ordered to bed by doctors. When he was again ambulatory, it was recommended that he travel abroad to recover from his nervous collapse. Norman was temporarily replaced by his deputy governor, Sir Ernest Harvey. As a sterling crisis loomed, Jack Morgan and Teddy Grenfell decided to smuggle Norman out of England. Fearing he might break, the House of Morgan plotted with British authorities to place him in temporary exile. After clearing Norman’s removal with Edward Peacock, a Bank of England director, Grenfell reported to New York, “M.N. has not made any progress and it has been intimated to him that he should keep away and leave No. 2 to run the show.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315246) It is unclear whether the doctors were part of this scheme or whether they were invoked as a cover for the operation.
在五月报告发布前的几天，蒙蒂·诺曼带着“不对劲”的感觉离开了银行。一年前，他因疲惫不堪，曾去南美休养了两个月。如今，这位神经紧张的诺曼因过度劳累而形容憔悴，被医生强制要求卧床休息。待他能下床走动后，医生建议他出国旅行以从精神崩溃中恢复。诺曼的职位由副行长欧内斯特·哈维爵士暂代。随着英镑危机逼近，杰克·摩根和特迪·格伦费尔决定偷偷将诺曼送出英国。担心他会彻底垮掉，摩根财团与英国当局密谋，让他暂时流亡海外。在征得英格兰银行董事爱德华·皮科克的同意后，格伦费尔向纽约报告说：“M.N.毫无起色，已有人暗示他应避避风头，让二号人物来主持大局。”” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315246) 尚不清楚这些医生是否参与了这个计划，还是仅仅被用作行动的掩护。

One marvels at both Morgan’s imperial hauteur and the tender solicitude for Norman. The bank wished to banish him with dignity. Jack telegrammed with a gesture of royal magnanimity: if he wished, Norman could take the *Corsair IV* anywhere in Europe, North Africa, or the Far East, attended by a doctor of his own choosing. “There are rooms for six beside himself,” Jack told Grenfell, “and for all the servants he could want.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315462) Norman was steaming to Quebec when Jack’s message came by radio, and he declined the “glorious offer.” To scotch rumors of a bankers’ cabal, he wanted to avoid the United States altogether. He recuperated at the Chateau Frontenac, where he also conferred with George Harrison. In exile, Monty was spared the need to take the ax to his beloved gold standard, and Grenfell said afterward he wouldn’t have been able to withstand the strain.
人们既惊叹于摩根那种帝王般的傲慢，又感慨他对诺曼那般的温柔体贴。银行方面希望他能体面地离开。杰克以皇室般宽宏大量的姿态发来电报：只要诺曼愿意，他可以带着自己选定的医生，乘坐“海盗四号”游艇前往欧洲、北非或远东的任何地方。“除了他自己，船上还有六个房间，”杰克告诉格伦费尔，“以及他能想到的所有仆人的空间。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315462) 当杰克的消息通过无线电传来时，诺曼正乘船驶向魁北克，他婉拒了这份“慷慨的提议”。为了平息关于银行家阴谋的谣言，他干脆想避开美国。他在芳堤娜城堡酒店休养，同时也在那里与乔治·哈里森进行了磋商。流放中的蒙蒂不必再亲手对他心爱的金本位制下刀，格伦费尔后来说，他原本也承受不住那样的压力。

The House of Morgan had helped Britain back onto the gold standard in 1925 and now underwrote a last-ditch effort to save it. Prime Minister Ramsay MacDonald and Philip Snowden knew the pound couldn’t be defended without a foreign loan. New York and Paris owned most of the world’s gold, and George Harrison suggested a joint U.S.-French loan. It fell to 23 Wall to keep MacDonald informed of Wall Street opinion *vis-à-vis* British chances for a credit. The messenger was Teddy Grenfell, who had triple authority—as a Bank of England director, a Conservative member of Parliament from the City, and the senior Morgan Grenfell partner. Pitiless toward Labour politicians and staunchly opposed to their program of nationalizing industry, he had a scathing opinion of MacDonald, whom he found coarse and gutless. “The only white thing about him is his liver, and the only portion of him that is not red is his blood.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315729) In early August, Grenfell warned MacDonald that halfway measures wouldn’t do and that a British loan from Wall Street would be dimly received unless he took courageous action and cut the budget deficit. Sensing a crisis in the offing, Grenfell tracked down the Conservative leader, Stanley Baldwin, then in France, and suggested he return to England at once.
1925 年，摩根财团曾帮助英国重返金本位制，如今又为挽救这一制度担保了最后一笔贷款。首相拉姆齐·麦克唐纳和菲利普·斯诺登明白，没有外国贷款，英镑根本无法守住。当时全球黄金大多掌握在纽约和巴黎手中，乔治·哈里森提议由美法联合提供贷款。华尔街 23 号负责向麦克唐纳传递华尔街对于英国获得信贷可能性的看法。传递消息的是特迪·格伦费尔，他身兼三职——英格兰银行董事、伦敦金融城选出的保守党议员，以及摩根建富的高级合伙人。他对工党政客毫不留情，强烈反对他们的工业国有化计划，对麦克唐纳更是尖刻评价，认为他粗鄙且软弱。他说：“他身上唯一白的地方就是他的肝脏，唯一不红的地方就是他的血液。”” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315729) 八月初，Grenfell 警告 MacDonald，折中方案行不通——除非他采取果断行动削减预算赤字，否则华尔街对英国贷款的接收意愿会相当冷淡。Grenfell 预感危机将至，便追查到了当时身在法国的保守党领袖 Stanley Baldwin，建议他立即返回英国。

Depending on one’s viewpoint, Ramsay MacDonald’s story in 1931 is either that of a farsighted prime minister who nobly sacrificed ideology to the national good or that of a blackguard who betrayed his party and platform to satisfy the foreign bankers. (There are striking parallels between MacDonald’s actions and Grover Cleveland’s alienation from his Democratic followers during the 1895 gold crisis.) A fiery, opinionated socialist, MacDonald had taken office in 1929 promising to fight unemployment, and he enacted unemployment benefits that became sacred to the trade unions. For all his rabble-rousing, however, he had a true-blue Englishman’s faith in sterling as the medium of world finance. So his dilemma was stark in August 1931. Foreign bankers insisted that he close the budget gap as the precondition for a loan. But any such austerity talk brought outcries from Labour ministers, who saw it as a betrayal of their followers to appease rich bankers.
从不同的角度看，1931 年拉姆齐·麦克唐纳的故事要么是一位远见卓识的首相，为民族大义而高尚地牺牲了意识形态，要么是一个为了讨好外国银行家而背叛自己政党和纲领的无耻之徒。（麦克唐纳的行为与 1895 年黄金危机期间格罗弗·克利夫兰疏远其民主党追随者的情况惊人相似。）作为一名性格火爆、固执己见的社会主义者，麦克唐纳在 1929 年上任时承诺要解决失业问题，并推行了工会视为神圣的失业救济金。然而，尽管他善于鼓动群众，他却有着一位地地道道英国人对英镑作为世界金融媒介的坚定信念。因此，在 1931 年 8 月，他陷入了两难境地。外国银行家坚持要他填补预算缺口，以此作为贷款的前提条件。但任何此类紧缩言论都会引起工党部长们的强烈抗议，他们认为这是为了安抚富有的银行家而背叛自己的支持者。

Speaking for Wall Street, Grenfell talked bluntly to MacDonald. “We are all getting tired of promises,” he warned in mid-August.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315944) Grenfell watched MacDonald warily, suspecting he would opt for expediency. As with Churchill in 1925, the Morgan partner took a rather mordant view of his target: “The P.M. is at last alarmed but he is so conceited and fluffy headed that it will be difficult to keep him up to the scratch.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316160) He greatly underestimated MacDonald. When trade unions proved intransigent about cuts in unemployment benefits, MacDonald, angered by their obstinacy, was converted to Grenfell’s cause. Many of his own ministers dug in their heels and resisted cuts in the dole.
代表华尔街的格伦费尔直言不讳地对麦克唐纳说。"我们都厌倦了承诺，"他在八月中旬警告道。 [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2315944) 格伦费尔警惕地观察着麦克唐纳，怀疑他会选择权宜之计。如同 1925 年看待丘吉尔那样，这位摩根合伙人对他盯防的目标颇带挖苦："首相终于警觉了，但他如此自负且头脑简单，很难让他坚持到底。" [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316160) 他大大低估了麦克唐纳。当工会对削减失业救济金表现出强硬态度时，麦克唐纳被他们的顽固激怒，转而支持格伦费尔的主张。而他自己的许多内阁大臣却固执己见，坚决反对削减救济金。

The next steps in the crisis were intricate. The Bank of England sounded out the New York Fed on whether Chancellor Snowden’s compromise plan for budget cuts would guarantee a Wall Street loan. MacDonald was afraid his cabinet would be offended by the idea of consulting New York bankers and wanted to test opinion covertly. George Harrison of the New York Fed referred the Bank of England to Morgans.
危机的后续发展错综复杂。英格兰银行试探性地询问纽约联储，财政大臣斯诺登提出的削减预算折中方案能否确保获得华尔街的贷款。麦克唐纳担心与纽约银行家商议的做法会触怒他的内阁，因此希望私下试探各方意见。纽约联储的乔治·哈里森让英格兰银行直接联系摩根财团。

Throughout the crisis, J. P. Morgan and Morgan Grenfell had a secret back channel with the Bank of England. As Grenfell explained, “If the Prime Minister were to tell his Cabinet that he had already exposed his plan to foreign bankers and asked for a loan his Cabinet would be much incensed. . . . You must understand that neither Prime Minister nor his Cabinet have ever seen any of the cables between J.P. Morgan & Co. and Morgan Grenfell though many of them have been shown to Governor Norman and Deputy Governor.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316357) On August 22, 1931, Harrison received a cable from the Bank of England outlining the new compromise budget that MacDonald would discuss with his cabinet on Sunday, August 23. The prime minister wished to know whether they could be sure of a New York loan if the cabinet adopted it. Harrison showed the cable to George Whitney and other Morgan partners, who had gathered at the Glen Cove home of partner Frank D. Bartow.
在整个危机期间，J.P. 摩根与摩根格林菲尔公司一直与英格兰银行保持着秘密沟通渠道。正如格林菲尔所解释的那样：“如果首相向内阁透露他已向外国银行家透露计划并寻求贷款，他的内阁将会大为震怒……你必须明白，首相及其内阁从未见过 J.P.摩根公司与摩根格林菲尔公司之间的任何电报，尽管其中许多已呈送诺曼行长和副行长过目。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316357) 1931 年 8 月 22 日，哈里森收到英格兰银行发来的电报，概述了麦克唐纳将于 8 月 23 日（星期日）与内阁讨论的新妥协预算方案。首相想知道，如果内阁通过该方案，他们是否能确保获得纽约的贷款。哈里森将电报展示给乔治·惠特尼以及其他聚集在合伙人弗兰克·D·巴托位于格伦科夫住所的摩根合伙人。

This backdoor intrigue set the stage for a showdown between Mac-Donald and his cabinet. That Sunday evening, the cabinet ministers paced through a warm twilight in the garden of 10 Downing Street. They had awaited New York’s verdict since noon. Morgan partners pored over figures calling for £70 million in budget cuts—including a 10-percent dole cut—and an extra £60 million in taxes. Finally, at 8:45 P.M., Sir Ernest Harvey at the Bank of England called Downing Street to announce a telephone message from New York. He offered to carry it right over.
这场幕后阴谋为麦克唐纳与其内阁的对决埋下了伏笔。那个周日傍晚，内阁大臣们在唐宁街 10 号的花园里，于温暖的暮色中踱步。他们从中午起就一直等待纽约方面的裁决。摩根公司的合伙人正在仔细研究数字，要求削减 7000 万英镑的预算——其中包括将失业救济金削减 10%——并额外增加 6000 万英镑的税收。终于，在晚上 8 点 45 分，英格兰银行的厄内斯特·哈维爵士致电唐宁街，宣布收到来自纽约的电话信息。他主动提出立刻转达。

For MacDonald, the suspense must have been excruciating: his political career hinged on the message. When Harvey arrived, MacDonald tore the cable from his hand and rushed back to the cabinet. This split-second action was fraught with historic consequence, for MacDonald didn’t stop to screen the contents of the message or even ascertain the sender’s identity. He introduced it as being from nameless New York bankers. The cabinet ministers assumed, wrongly, that the message came from the New York Fed. It actually came from George Whitney and was addressed to the Bank of England, not to the cabinet.
对麦克唐纳而言，这份悬念必然令人备受煎熬：他的政治生涯就押在这条消息上。哈维抵达后，麦克唐纳一把从他手中扯过电报，疾步返回内阁。这电光石火间的举动承载着历史性的后果——麦克唐纳甚至没有停下来审视电文内容，更未核实发件人身份。他声称这是来自匿名的纽约银行家。内阁大臣们误以为消息出自纽约联邦储备银行。实际上，它来自乔治·惠特尼，且收件方是英格兰银行，而非英国内阁。

Coming upon the cable in Morgan Grenfell’s files, it is disappointing to see the blandness of this government-toppling document. It briefly expresses sympathy for a British credit but stipulates no specific budget cuts. It’s a sterile document, as if the bankers who penned it were being extremely cautious. But the cabinet ministers were hot and tired, and their nerves were frayed from debate. They spied sinister meaning behind its final lines: “In the foregoing we have as always given you the precise trend of our thought. Let us know promptly as indicated above what the Government’s desires are and within 24 hours we shall be able to give you our final judgment. Are we right in assuming that the programme under consideration will have the sincere approval and support of the Bank of England and the City generally and thus go a long way toward restoring international confidence in Great Britain?”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316564)
翻到摩根建富档案里的那封电报，这封企图倒阁的文件平淡无奇，着实令人失望。它仅仅对一笔英国信贷略表同情，却未规定任何具体的预算削减。这是一份干巴巴的文件，仿佛起草的银行家们谨小慎微到了极点。但内阁大臣们又热又累，辩论已让他们神经紧绷。他们从文件末尾几行嗅到了险恶意味：“如上所述，我们一如既往向您坦陈我们的确切思路。请按上述方式尽快告知政府意向，24 小时内我们将给出最终判断。我们可否认为，审议中的计划将获得英格兰银行及伦敦金融城整体的真诚认可与支持，从而大大有助于恢复国际对英国的信心？” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316564)

When MacDonald read these words aloud, there was such a commotion in the cabinet room that Sir Ernest Harvey heard it outside, and he later recalled that “pandemonium had broken loose.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316768) This last paragraph, clearly, was intended for the Bank of England alone. To those present, it awakened old fears of dark dealings between private banks in London and New York. The other stumbling block was MacDonald’s apparent mention of a 10-percent dole cut. This wasn’t mentioned by Morgans. Later, in reconstructing events, Grenfell told Lamont that “the Cabinet have gone on repeating . . . that the American Bankers insisted on a 10\$\$\$ cut. . .. If he made mention of a 10\$\$\$ cut as a condition MacDonald must have invented it as it does not appear in your cable.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316935) The cable supports Grenfell.
当麦克唐纳大声念出这些话时，内阁会议室里一片哗然，连门外的欧内斯特·哈维爵士都听到了，他后来回忆说“简直乱了套”。 [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316768) 显然，这最后一段话完全是说给英格兰银行听的。对在场的人而言，这唤起了他们对伦敦和纽约私人银行之间暗箱操作的那些旧日恐惧。另一个绊脚石是麦克唐纳似乎提到了削减 10%的救济金。这一点摩根方面从未提及。后来在重构事件经过时，格伦费尔告诉拉蒙特：“内阁一直在翻来覆去地说……美国银行家坚持要削减 10\$\$\$……如果他提到削减 10\$\$\$作为条件，那一定是麦克唐纳自己编的，因为你的电报里根本没有这个说法。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2316935) 电报内容证实了格伦费尔的说法。

MacDonald felt that he lacked the cabinet mandate to proceed with the emergency budget cuts required to restore foreign confidence in sterling. The bickering grew so fierce that at 10:20 P.M. he arrived at Buckingham Palace and laid his resignation before King George V. Looking wild and distracted, he told the king that “all was up.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317138) In insisting upon the budget cuts, MacDonald set himself against powerful segments of his own party, and he now knew he had crossed some personal Rubicon. The king asked him to return to Buckingham Palace the next morning along with the opposition leaders—Stanley Baldwin of the Conservatives and Sir Herbert Samuel of the Liberals. To spread political risks and ensure passage of the cut in unemployment benefits, the king invited the three to form a coalition government. MacDonald would remain prime minister of a government that was Tory at heart.
麦克唐纳认为自己缺乏内阁授权，无法推进恢复外界对英镑信心所需的紧急预算削减。争吵愈发激烈，以至于晚上 10 点 20 分，他抵达白金汉宫，向国王乔治五世递交了辞呈。他神情狂乱而恍惚，告诉国王“一切都完了”。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317138) 在坚持削减预算的问题上，麦克唐纳与党内强大势力针锋相对，此刻他明白自己已踏过了一条个人意义上的卢比孔河。国王要求他第二天早晨与反对党领袖——保守党的斯坦利·鲍德温和自由党的赫伯特·塞缪尔爵士——一同返回白金汉宫。为了分散政治风险并确保削减失业救济金的方案通过，国王邀请三人组建联合政府。麦克唐纳将继续担任首相，但这个政府实质上由保守党主导。

The new government cut the budget deficit with higher taxes on gasoline, beer, tobacco, and income and lower salaries for civil servants. J. P. Morgan and Company provided a \$200-million revolving line of credit, and another \$200 million came from France. Unfortunately, it proved impossible to restore confidence in the pound. Many Labourites now regarded MacDonald as a traitor and were vitriolic in their criticism of him. In September, the Communists marched on Parliament and insisted that a cold-blooded bankers’ “ramp,” or conspiracy, had imposed unfair hardship on British workers. Unemployed workers rioted in Battersea and mounted police charged demonstrators on Oxford Street. It was widely believed that the New York Fed had brought down the government. London’s *Daily Herald* featured a photograph of George Harrison on the front page, charging that a New York-led conspiracy had plotted against the British welfare state. “The *Daily Herald* to-day discloses a startling and apparently successful attempt by US bankers to dictate the internal policy of Great Britain,” the headline read.[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317298)
新政府通过提高汽油、啤酒、烟草和所得税，并削减公务员薪资来减少预算赤字。摩根大通提供了 2 亿美元的循环信贷额度，另有 2 亿美元来自法国。遗憾的是，恢复对英镑信心的努力最终化为泡影。许多工党人士如今视麦克唐纳为叛徒，对他极尽尖刻批评。九月份，共产党人向议会游行，坚称冷酷无情的银行家们搞了一场“阴谋”，给英国工人带来了沉重打击。失业工人在巴特西爆发骚乱，骑警则冲击牛津街上的示威者。普遍认为纽约联邦储备银行导致了政府的垮台。伦敦《每日先驱报》在头版刊登了乔治·哈里森的照片，指责一场由纽约策划的阴谋针对英国福利国家。 "《每日先驱报》今日披露，美国银行家企图支配英国内政，此举令人震惊且显然已得逞。" [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317298)

One can picture Grenfell’s sardonic smile as he followed this misunderstanding. To operate in the political shadows, to pass wraithlike through a crisis and exert an unseen influence on large events—for Grenfell, these were the perfections of his art. When he was pumped for information in Parliament, he played the “village idiot,” he said. He confided to Lamont, “I believe it is the opinion of the late Cabinet that George Whitney’s long telephone message. . . . was a message from the Federal Reserve Bank. For the present, therefore, the fall of Ramsay MacDonald will be put down to the domineering action of poor George Harrison, who will not I imagine lose his sleep in consequence.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317448)
可以想象，当格伦费尔看到这场误解时，他脸上露出了讥讽的微笑。在政治阴影中运作，像幽灵般穿越危机，对重大事件施加无形的影响——对格伦费尔而言，这正是他这门艺术的极致。当他在议会中被追问信息时，他自称扮演了“乡巴佬”的角色。他向拉蒙特吐露：“我相信，已故内阁的意见是，乔治·惠特尼的长篇电话留言……来自联邦储备银行。因此，目前来看，拉姆齐·麦克唐纳的倒台将被归咎于可怜的乔治·哈里森的专横举动——我想他并不会因此失眠。”

Did the House of Morgan bring down the Labour government? MacDonald himself exonerated the bankers and stressed the need to maintain the place of sterling in world finance. Morgan records confirm that the bank refrained from recommending specific budget cuts. Yet it was no secret that Wall Street wanted the dole cuts. And the broad body of American bankers had a veto over any big British loan on Wall Street. There was no hidden Morgan agenda, only the usual bankers’ mentality of favoring austerity and cuts in spending. It was Britain’s choice to defend the gold standard, which placed it in the thrall of foreign investors. Morgans merely expressed the consensus among bankers.
摩根财团真的搞垮了工党政府吗？麦克唐纳本人为银行家们开脱，强调必须维持英镑在国际金融中的地位。摩根财团的记录证实，该行并未建议具体的削减预算方案。不过，华尔街希望削减失业救济金早已是公开的秘密。而且，美国银行家群体对英国在华尔街的任何大额贷款都拥有否决权。摩根财团并没有什么隐藏议程，只是秉持银行家一贯偏爱紧缩和削减开支的心态。英国选择捍卫金本位制，这使它受制于外国投资者。摩根不过是表达了银行家的共识。

A few days after the Sunday cabinet meeting, Lamont spoke by phone with Hoover, who gave qualified approval to the British credit. Since the large credit would enlist 110 American banks, Hoover warned that Wall Street would be accused of funneling money to Britain at a time of American distress.[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317663) Not for the first time, small-town America looked askance at Morgan assistance to Britain, even as Britain’s left accused American bankers of treacherous interference.
周日内阁会议后几天，拉蒙特与胡佛通了电话，胡佛对英国的信贷方案表示了有条件的支持。由于这笔巨额信贷将涉及 110 家美国银行，胡佛警告说，在美国自身陷入困境之际，华尔街会被指责向英国输送资金。这并非首次——美国小镇的民众对摩根援助英国的做法投以怀疑的目光，而英国左翼则指责美国银行家进行险恶的干预。

The *coup de grâce* to Britain’s gold standard came in September 1931, when naval units at Invergordon, Scotland, struck against proposed pay cuts. This little mutiny terrified foreign investors, who saw it as proof that the British public would never accept an austerity budget. Sterling crashed again. Monty Norman was sailing home from Canada on September 21, 1931, when England went off gold. Hence, it would no longer redeem sterling for gold at a fixed rate: the old imperial fantasy was dead, and the pound fell a shocking 30 percent. Keynes exulted over gold’s demise: “There are few Englishmen who do not rejoice at the breaking of our gold fetters.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317878) But Monty Norman, arriving at Liverpool, was stunned that the edifice he built was shattered. He took the train down to Euston Station and had a temper tantrum when he arrived at the bank. Yet his associates, Harvey and Peacock, believed that he would have done the same thing had he been in charge. Twenty-five countries followed Britain off gold in a rush of competitive devaluations.
给英国金本位制致命一击的是 1931 年 9 月，当时苏格兰因弗戈登的海军部队因反对拟议的减薪而发动罢工。这场小小的兵变吓坏了外国投资者，他们视其为英国民众绝不会接受紧缩预算的铁证。英镑再度暴跌。1931 年 9 月 21 日，当英国脱离金本位时，蒙蒂·诺曼正从加拿大乘船回国。从此，英国不再以固定汇率用黄金兑换英镑：昔日的帝国幻想破灭了，英镑暴跌了惊人的 30%。凯恩斯对金本位的终结欣喜若狂：“没有几个英国人不为我们挣脱黄金枷锁而欢欣鼓舞。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2317878) 但抵达利物浦的蒙蒂·诺曼却惊愕地发现自己一手构建的大厦已然崩塌。他乘火车抵达尤斯顿站，一到银行就大发雷霆。然而，他的同僚哈维和皮科克认为，如果他当时仍在掌权，也会做出同样的决定。 二十五个国家紧随英国之后，纷纷竞相贬值本币，脱离了金本位制度。

In an Associated Press interview from London, Jack Morgan applauded Britain’s departure from gold. When Lamont read this in New York, he was thunderstruck. Hadn’t they just enlisted over one hundred banks to save that gold standard? And wouldn’t those banks now feel betrayed? Lamont, who almost never got angry, was beside himself.
在美联社一次从伦敦发来的采访中，杰克·摩根对英国放弃金本位大加赞赏。拉蒙特在纽约读到这篇报道时，简直目瞪口呆。他们不是刚刚动员了一百多家银行来捍卫金本位吗？那些银行如今岂不会感到被背叛？拉蒙特几乎从不发火，这次却气得不轻。

Now came the moment that was destined to occur—when the power relationship at the bank was revealed and even Jack Morgan would feel the sting of Lamont’s pen. They had for some time a tacit deal: Jack would be semiretired figurehead, and Lamont would retain executive control. Now in his early sixties, Jack was an absentee boss who preferred golfing and yachting; he was aging and no longer traveled on the *Corsair* without a surgeon aboard. In most banking matters, control had slipped from his grasp.
如今，那个注定要来的时刻终于到了——银行内部的权力格局暴露无遗，就连杰克·摩根也要为拉蒙特的笔锋感到刺痛。他们之间早已心照不宣：杰克是半退休的挂名领袖，拉蒙特则掌握实际执行权。年过六十的杰克成了甩手掌柜，更喜欢打高尔夫和开游艇；他日渐老迈，如今再坐“海盗号”游艇出行，船上都得备着外科医生。在大多数银行事务上，控制权早已从他手中滑落。

Lamont had never challenged Jack openly. Now, in his fury, he confronted him directly. So unprecedented was the accusatory letter he sent that he cosigned it with Charles Steele, the other major partner in terms of capital share and an old-timer from Pierpont’s days. Steele was a friend of Jack’s and was regarded around the bank as a pleasant, wise old man.
拉蒙特从未公开挑战过杰克。此刻，他怒火中烧，直接与他对峙。他寄出的那封指责信前所未有，竟与查尔斯·斯蒂尔联名签署——斯蒂尔是另一位资本份额相当的大股东，也是皮尔庞特时代的老将。斯蒂尔本是杰克的朋友，在整个银行界都被视为一个和蔼可亲、睿智的老者。

It may be said that this letter of September 25, 1931, marks the moment when the House of Morgan ceased operating as a family bank. Lamont wrote: “The point that we must make to you—a point that we fear you little realize—is to have you know fully the uncomfortable position in which the New York firm has been placed before the whole American world and the public generally. What the banks here, without exception, fail to understand, is why this enormous credit operation should have been permitted to blow up in our faces over night so to speak.”
可以说，1931 年 9 月 25 日的这封信标志着摩根银行不再作为家族银行运营的时刻。拉蒙特写道：“我们必须向您强调的一点——我们担心您几乎没有意识到——是让您充分了解纽约公司在整个美国公众面前所处的尴尬境地。这里的各家银行无一例外都无法理解的是，为什么这项巨额信贷业务会被允许在我们眼前几乎一夜之间化为泡影。”

Lamont reminded Jack of the solemn pledges to preserve the gold standard that were made to participating banks:
拉蒙特提醒杰克，当初向参与银行作出的维护金本位的郑重承诺：

> It was upon that prophecy, made wreckage in just three weeks to the day, that we were able to complete the group for the credit. There was, as we told you at the time, great reluctance upon the part of many banks to join in the credit. . . . Now the outcome has manifestly and inevitably diminished our prestige, not only publicly but with the American banking community which has for years so largely supported us in our efforts for the preservation here of British credit. And this is a fact that every partner of the firm, which (under you and your Father before you) has built up its American reputation upon careful judgment and prudent dealing, must keep in the forefront of his mind for a long time to come. . . .
> 正是基于那个预言——然而在短短三周内便化为泡影——我们才得以凑齐信贷集团。正如当时所言，许多银行当初极不情愿加入这项信贷安排……如今结果显而易见，不可避免地损害了我们的声誉，不仅是在公众面前，更是与多年来鼎力支持我们维护英国信用的美国银行业之间。而这一点，这家公司在您（以及您先父）的领导下，凭借审慎判断和稳健交易在美积累声誉，每位合伙人都必须在未来很长一段时间里铭记于心……

> Now, we have said our say about the situation over here, and will try not to allude to the subject again. But with you so far away, it has been we have thought, quite important to acquaint you with the unpleasant facts which have come to us all.[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318058)
> 现在，关于这边的情况我们已经说完了，并将尽量不再提及此事。但鉴于你远在别处，我们认为，让你了解我们全体所遇到的那些不愉快的事实，还是相当重要的。 [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318058)

Ten years before, Lamont would never have dared this. He had always dealt with Jack gingerly so that he would never lose face. Now, however, Lamont’s money and position gave him incontestable power. Still, nobody confronted a Morgan without grave anxiety. At one point in the letter, Lamont gave Jack an out: he hinted that the AP quote must have come at the end of a long interview and he closed the letter “with much love from us all,” signing it “Faithfully.” Lamont knew the letter was uniquely candid and bare-knuckled. After posting it, he telephoned Jack to say no blame was intended and that he, Lamont, would have acted the same under the circumstances. Yet the letter showed that a palace revolution had taken place at the House of Morgan and that the Morgan family had surrendered its absolute power. From that time onward, the influence of the Morgan family would steadily diminish within the House of Morgan and then all but disappear.
十年前，拉蒙特绝不敢如此行事。他过去总是小心翼翼地对待杰克，生怕让他失了颜面。然而如今，拉蒙特的财富与地位赋予了他无可争议的权力。即便如此，面对摩根家族的人时，谁都无法不心怀忐忑。在信中的某处，拉蒙特给了杰克一个台阶下：他暗示美联社的那段引文可能是在一次长采访接近尾声时说的，并在信末附上"我们全体致以深切的爱"，署名"您忠实的"。拉蒙特深知这封信坦率至极、锋芒毕露。寄出信后，他致电杰克，称绝无指责之意，并说自己若身处其境也会同样行事。然而这封信表明，摩根大厦内部已发生了一场宫廷政变，摩根家族交出了其绝对权力。从那时起，摩根家族在摩根大厦内部的影响力逐渐式微，最终几乎消失殆尽。

AS the political skies darkened in 1931, Tom Lamont seemed blind to the spread of political extremism and militarism around the world. This was partly a reflection of his innate optimism, his almost instinctive faith in the future. He kept thinking the Depression couldn’t get any worse, that the world would suddenly return to its senses, that the dictators would be held in check. The gregarious Lamont often found it hard to credit people’s malevolence and was reluctant to probe beneath their reassuring smiles.
随着 1931 年政治天空日渐阴沉，汤姆·拉蒙特似乎对全球蔓延的政治极端主义与军国主义视若无睹。这在一定程度上反映了他与生俱来的乐观——那种近乎本能的未来信念。他始终相信经济大萧条不会更糟了，相信世界会突然恢复理智，相信独裁者们终将被遏制。这位生性爱交际的拉蒙特往往难以相信他人的恶意，也不愿去探究那些令人安心的微笑背后隐藏着什么。

This blind spot was especially apparent with sovereign clients, where a banker’s self-interest bolstered his preference for looking on the bright side of things. Partisan in behalf of clients, he tried to keep their reputations as unblemished as that of the House of Morgan itself. Their good name was especially vital in the Depression’s volatile market for foreign bonds. Unfortunately, a concern with the financial standing of foreign states could slip over into questionable dealings with them. In extreme cases in the 1930s, the House of Morgan would function as an unfettered government in its own right, conducting a secret foreign policy at odds with that of Washington.
这种盲点在对主权客户时尤为明显，银行家的自身利益促使他更倾向于看好的一面。他偏袒客户，竭力保全他们的声誉，使其与摩根银行本身一样洁白无瑕。在大萧条时期动荡的外国债券市场上，客户的良好声誉尤为关键。不幸的是，对外国政府财务状况的关注，有时会滑向与这些国家的可疑交易。在 20 世纪 30 年代的极端案例中，摩根银行甚至自行扮演起不受约束的政府角色，奉行与华盛顿相左的秘密外交政策。

As fiscal agent for the imperial Japanese government in the late 1920s, Lamont had devotedly served his client. For a Western banker, he had achieved remarkable, unheard-of triumphs. After the mammoth earthquake loan, he had floated loans for Tokyo, Yokohama, and Osaka, advised on a merger between Tokyo Electric Power and Tokyo Electric Light, mediated between the Bank of Japan and the New York Fed, and extended a \$25-million credit that restored Japan to the gold standard in January 1930. On the eve of the crash, the House of Morgan was exploring a possible working link with the House of Mitsui, talks that enjoyed official Japanese patronage. When it came to Japanese business, Lamont took great pride in his accomplishments.
作为 20 世纪 20 年代末日本帝国政府的财务代理人，拉蒙特始终兢兢业业地服务着他的客户。他以一个西方银行家的身份，取得了前所未有、令人惊叹的辉煌成就。继那笔巨额地震贷款之后，他又先后为东京、横滨和大阪发行了债券，为东京电力与东京电灯的合并提供咨询，在日本央行与纽约联储之间进行斡旋调解，并提供了 2500 万美元的信贷，使日本在 1930 年 1 月恢复了金本位制。就在股市崩盘前夕，摩根财团正在探索与三井财阀建立可能的合作关系，而这些谈判也得到了日本官方的支持。在涉足日本业务方面，拉蒙特对自己的成就深感自豪。

His early faith in Japan was understandable. When he first visited in 1920, Japan stood on the brink of more than ten years of liberal, pro-Western party rule. He developed distinguished and cultured friends, especially Junnosuke Inouye, the commanding figure of Japanese finance, with whom he corresponded frequently. Inouye was finance minister for a third time after 1929. Humane and courageous, he was known for his conciliatory views on foreign affairs and was often at loggerheads with the army. He represented the enlightened antimilitarist forces in Japan. At Inouye’s request, Lamont would lobby the New York press and argue Japan’s case. In 1928, after meeting with editors of the *New York Times*, he told his friend, “I also told them of the patient and tolerant attitude of your people toward China and the Chinese. . . . It therefore is a matter of satisfaction to me to see how fair and sound the *Times* has been.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318293)
他早期对日本的信任是可以理解的。1920 年首次造访时，日本正站在长达十余年的自由、亲西方政党统治的门槛上。他结交了卓越而有教养的朋友，尤其是日本金融界的领军人物井上准之助，两人通信频繁。井上在 1929 年后第三次出任大藏大臣。他为人仁慈且果敢，以在外交事务上持和解立场而闻名，时常与军方意见相左。他代表了日本开明的反军国主义力量。应井上的请求，拉蒙特会游说纽约媒体，为日本辩护。1928 年，在会见了《纽约时报》的编辑后，他告诉朋友：“我也向他们讲述了贵国人民对中国人民所展现的耐心与宽容态度……因此，看到《纽约时报》如此公正客观，我感到十分欣慰。”

The House of Morgan attained peak involvement in Japan just as that nation’s experiment with liberal rule began to crumble. Following a wave of bank failures and the stock market closing in 1927, it slipped into a depression before most Western countries. That year, Japan was enraged by China’s boycott of its goods in protest of foreign encroachment—a slap at national pride that Japan would invoke during the Manchurian invasion. In 1930, the Morgan-assisted restoration of the gold standard, under Inouye’s aegis, proved a masterpiece of bad timing. It made exports expensive just as world trade contracted. When Depression-plagued America economized on luxury clothes, Japanese silk exports plunged. Silk was still a staple of Japan’s economy, with two of every five families drawing income from it. Poverty spread throughout the countryside, breeding a vicious new strain of rural nationalism. Rice prices also tumbled. The budding Japanese export boom was blighted by Western protectionism, feeding xenophobia. These economic setbacks enhanced the power of the militarists, who blamed foreign powers for Japan’s troubles. Militarism would be bloodily manifested in Manchuria.
正当日本尝试自由统治之际，摩根财团在该国的影响力达到顶峰。1927 年银行倒闭潮和股市崩盘后，日本比大多数西方国家更早陷入萧条。同年，中国抵制日货以抗议外侵，令日本震怒——这一民族自尊心的挫伤，日后成为日本入侵满洲的借口。1930 年，在井上准之助主导下，摩根协助日本恢复金本位制，结果证明是糟糕透顶的时机选择。正值全球贸易萎缩之际，此举导致日本出口商品价格高企。当深陷大萧条的美国削减奢侈品服装开支时，日本生丝出口骤降。生丝仍是日本经济支柱，每五户家庭中就有两户依赖此收入。贫困在乡村蔓延，滋生出一种危险的农村民族主义新形态。米价同样暴跌。 初现端倪的日本出口热潮因西方保护主义而受挫，这进一步助长了仇外情绪。经济上的接连受挫增强了军国主义者的话语权，他们将日本的困境归咎于列强。军国主义随后在满洲以血腥的方式展现了出来。

The Japanese had long coveted Manchuria, that resource-rich north-eastern corner of China. Whenever problems beset Japanese society—whether overpopulation, too great a reliance on foreign raw materials, or the need for new export markets—militarists saw China as the solution. They claimed Manchuria almost as a matter of divine right. China was still fragmented and chaotic, with warlords ruling parts of the country, and it appeared to be easy prey for aggressors. It was weakened by a civil war that had culminated in 1927 with Chiang Kai-shek’s defeat of the Communist rebels under Mao Tse-tung. By treaty with China, Japan controlled the South Manchuria Railway and even stationed a garrison in the region. This treaty gave the Japanese militarists a legitimate cover behind which to carry out their plunder. Japan’s Kwantung army plotted to use Manchuria as the base for military expansion in China.
日本长期觊觎中国东北这片资源富饶的土地。每当日本社会遭遇困境——无论是人口过剩、过度依赖外国原材料，还是需要开拓新的出口市场——军国主义者总把中国视为解决之道。他们几乎将满洲视为天赐之物。彼时的中国仍处于分裂动荡之中，各地军阀割据，在侵略者眼中不过是唾手可得的猎物。1927 年，蒋介石击败毛泽东领导的共产党武装，结束了内战，但中国国力已因此严重削弱。根据中日条约，日本不仅掌控南满铁路，还在该地区驻扎守备队。这份条约成了日本军国主义者劫掠的合法掩护。日本关东军密谋以满洲为基地，进一步扩大对华军事侵略。

In many ways, the House of Morgan shared Japan’s jaundiced view of the Chinese, a common one in Western financial circles. China was unpopular on Wall Street and in the City. It was prone to default and adept at playing foreign bankers off against each other. Ever since the abortive China consortium under Woodrow Wilson, Lamont had looked upon the Chinese as wily and duplicitous. He perceived them less as victims of foreign intruders than as two-faced opportunists.
在许多方面，摩根大通与美国金融界普遍看法一致，对中国持有偏见。中国在华尔街和伦敦金融城都不受欢迎。它常常违约，又精于让外国银行家相互制衡。自伍德罗·威尔逊时期对华银团计划失败后，拉蒙特便视中国为狡诈多端、口是心非之辈。在他看来，中国与其说是外国入侵者的受害者，不如说是两面三刀的投机者。

It was an easy attitude to assume. Japan was a major Morgan client, and no business came from China, which was still in default on a substantial portion of its foreign debt. (National City Bank, on the other hand, did a thriving business in China, which generated almost one-third of the bank’s profits in 1930.) So Lamont was quick to find merit in Japanese claims that Manchuria was economically indispensable, lay well within her sphere of influence, provided a buffer against bolshevism, and had been won with Japanese blood and treasure in the Russo-Japanese War of 1905. With billions of yen invested in Manchuria and millions of Japanese living there, some nationalists saw the region as a simple extension of Japan.
这种态度很容易形成。日本是摩根大客户，而中国没有带来任何业务，当时中国大部分外债仍处于违约状态（不过，花旗银行在中国生意兴隆，1930 年该行近三分之一利润来自中国）。因此，拉蒙特很快就认同了日本的主张：满洲在经济上不可或缺，完全处于其势力范围之内，是抵御布尔什维主义的缓冲地带，并且是日本在 1905 年日俄战争中用鲜血和财富赢得的。由于日本在满洲投入了数十亿日元，并有数百万日本人居住在那里，一些民族主义者将这一地区视为日本的自然延伸。

In mid-1931, while the West was distracted by the Credit Anstalt failure and the sterling crisis, the Kwantung army set in motion a plot to seize Mukden and other Manchurian towns. On September 18, it launched a surprise raid against Chinese barracks in Mukden; by the next day, the city had fallen to the Japanese. As a pretext for this aggression, the Japanese military manufactured stories of Chinese assaults against the Japanese-controlled South Manchuria Railway—stories that were later exposed as fraudulent or exaggerated. Emboldened by popular support in Japan, the military flouted civilian officials, such as Inouye and Foreign Minister Kijuro Shidehara, who opposed the use of force. Japan’s Foreign Office was afraid that if it tried to rein in the Kwantung Army, it might face an armed revolt in the ranks. As fifteen thousand Japanese troops swarmed across Manchuria, diplomats lamely said that the moves were temporary and that the troops would be evacuated shortly. As historian Richard Storry said, these were “weeks of public embarrassment and secret humiliation for the Wakatsuki government.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318481)
1931 年中期，当西方世界正为奥地利信贷银行倒闭和英镑危机而焦头烂额时，关东军策划了夺取奉天及其他满洲城镇的阴谋。9 月 18 日，日军突袭奉天中国军营，次日该城即告陷落。为给这次侵略制造借口，日军编造了中方袭击日本控制的南满铁路的故事——这些说法后来被揭露为伪造或夸大。凭借国内民众支持而壮胆的军方，公然无视像井上和外相币原喜重郎这样反对武力侵华的文官。日本外务省担心若试图约束关东军，可能会引发部队内部的武装叛乱。当一万五千名日军横扫满洲时，外交官们软弱地辩解称这些行动只是暂时的，部队很快就会撤离。 正如历史学家理查德·斯托里所言，若槻内阁经历了“数周的公开难堪与私下屈辱”。 [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318481)

Stunned by the Mukden raid, Secretary of State Henry L. Stimson swiftly protested to Japan, and Hoover later called it “an act of rank aggression.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318651) Financial markets clamored for an explanation. As finance minister, the proud, dignified Inouye had to issue a statement. He was in a precarious spot, for he had spearheaded cabinet opposition to reinforcing troops in Manchuria. He was also identified with demands for cuts in the defense budget, which earned him the lasting enmity of the military (much as Dr. Hjalmar Schacht’s faith in old-fashioned balanced budgets would finally doom him with the Nazis).
突如其来的沈阳事件让国务卿亨利·L·史汀生大为震惊，他迅速向日本提出抗议，后来胡佛更是将其称为“赤裸裸的侵略行径”。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318651) 金融市场哗然，急切要求给出解释。作为财政大臣，那位骄傲且威严的井上不得不发表声明。他处境岌岌可危，因为他曾在内阁中带头反对向满洲增兵。他还主张削减国防预算，这让他与军方结下了不解之仇（正如亚尔马·沙赫特博士对传统预算平衡的执念，最终也让他栽在了纳粹手中）。

Inouye consoled financial markets with an amazingly artful statement about Mukden. The *New York Times* printed it verbatim on October 22 in a dispatch with a Tokyo dateline. Entitled “INOUYE SAYS JAPAN IS EAGER TO RETIRE,” it became the statement that defined Japan’s position for Western financial markets. Observant readers must have been struck by its clever analogies to the Panama Canal, its quoting of Daniel Webster, and its sure feel for American sensibilities:
井上用一席极为巧妙的关于奉天的说辞安抚了金融市场。《纽约时报》于 10 月 22 日将其全文刊登于一篇东京电讯中，标题为“井上称日本急于撤出”，这篇声明就此成为西方金融市场界定日本立场的基准。细心的读者定会为其与巴拿马运河的精妙类比、对丹尼尔·韦伯斯特的引述，以及对美国情绪的了然把握所震撼。

> A clear understanding of the present state of affairs in Manchuria shows that the question is simply one of self defense. A long, narrow strip of territory, along which runs the vital nerve called the South Manchuria Railway, is and has been by treaty arrangements since the Russo-Japanese War of 1904-5 under the complete administration of Japan. By treaty with Russia, duly recognized and adopted by China, Japan administers this “South Manchuria Railway Zone”—polices and protects it much as the United States Government polices and protects the Panama Canal Zone.
> 对满洲现状的清晰认识表明，这纯粹是一个自卫问题。自 1904-5 年日俄战争以来，根据条约安排，南满铁路这条生命线所贯穿的狭长地带一直由日本全权管辖。根据中国正式承认并采纳的与俄国签订的条约，日本管理着这个"南满铁路附属地"——其管辖与保护方式，正如美国政府管辖和保护巴拿马运河区一样。

> On the 18th of September last, a night attack was made on this zone by regular Chinese troops, and the railway line was destroyed. It was evidently necessary for Japan to take strong and immediate steps. When points under the protection of one’s army are invaded by regular troops, and the extent of the threatened invasion is utterly unknown, the obvious means of self defense is to proceed at once to the headquarters of the offending troops. The emergency was one which, in Mr. Webster’s classic words, was ’instant, overwhelming, leaving no choice of means, and no moment for deliberation.’
> 去年 9 月 18 日夜间，中国正规军对本区域发动了一次夜袭，并破坏了铁路线。日本显然有必要立即采取强硬措施。当己方军队所护卫的据点遭到正规军入侵，且威胁的规模完全无法预判时，最合理的自卫方式就是直接奔赴肇事部队的指挥部。这场危机用韦伯斯特先生那句经典的话来说，就是"突如其来、势不可挡，令人别无选择，也无暇思索。"

The middle section of the statement portrays Japan as saving Manchuria from anarchy. It brushes aside as “minor military measures” the actions taken at Mukden. The closing is forceful:
声明中间部分将日本描绘成将满洲从无政府状态中拯救出来的角色，并将奉天采取的行动轻描淡写为"小规模军事措施"。结尾则语气强硬：

> In the final analysis, there is nothing in the situation that should create a war and the whole affair has been magnified beyond reason in being deemed an actual danger to the peace of the world. The Japanese, as repeatedly stated, have no intention whatsoever of making war on China. On the contrary, the Japanese Government and people entertain the friendliest feelings towards the Chinese. They are probably more anxious than any other nation of the earth could possibly be, to maintain friendly relations with the Chinese.[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318813)
> 归根结底，当前局势没有任何理由引发战争，整件事被过度夸大，被视作对世界和平的实际威胁，实属毫无道理。正如一再重申的那样，日本绝无对中国开战之意。相反，日本政府和人民对中国怀有最友好的感情。他们可能比世界上任何其他国家都更渴望与中国保持友好关系。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2318813)

The press release was actually drafted by Tom Lamont. It was issued, with only cosmetic changes, by Japan’s Ministry of Finance. (The preceding is quoted from the original in Lamont’s files.) The Japanese wanted Lamont to release the statement himself, but he replied that Morgans would be thought biased and might offend the Chinese—an understatement. Perhaps he also feared his reputation among American liberals would be blackened by any revelation of his authorship; as a former champion of the League of Nations, he probably didn’t want to side publicly with an aggressor. To assuage the Japanese, he explained that if Inouye “will let me know what day he plans to issue the statement, I will arrange to have it gain extra publicity here.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319022)
这份新闻稿实际上是由汤姆·拉蒙特起草的。日本财务省仅做了些表面修改就发布了。（上文引自拉蒙特档案中的原稿。）日本方面希望拉蒙特亲自发表声明，但他回应称，若由摩根方面发布，会被认为有失公允，还可能触怒中国——这已是轻描淡写的说法。或许他也担心，一旦透露自己是作者，在美国自由派中的声誉会受损；作为前国联支持者，他大概不愿公开站在侵略者一边。为安抚日本，他解释说，如果井上“能告知我计划发表声明的日期，我会安排让声明在此地获得更多曝光。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319022)

Lamont now found himself in stark opposition to Washington’s policy and faced the dilemma always latent in his role as a banker *cum* diplomat. Why did he conspire with a foreign power in a military action condemned by the U.S. government and the League of Nations? Could he have accepted, at face value, Japan’s story about Manchuria? Reporters in China pointed out that versions of the Mukden incident originated with Japan’s military and were suspect. There were also widespread suspicions of a staged incident, a premeditated invasion. As the London *Times* said on September 21, the Japanese army, three days before taking Mukden, had conducted “something like a dress rehearsal” for the invasion, and “though it is reported that the incident of the South Manchuria Railway was the cause of the developments, the truth is that the whole movement was on foot before the alleged incident occurred.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319212) There was, in short, plenty of evidence to give a reasonable man pause. Add to this the clear public impression that the cabinet was being duped by the army, and Lamont’s alacrity is puzzling.
如今，拉蒙特发现自己与华盛顿的政策截然对立，并陷入了其银行家兼外交官角色中始终潜藏的困境：他为何要与一个外国势力合谋，参与一项遭到美国政府及国际联盟谴责的军事行动？他能否照单全收日本关于满洲的故事？在华记者指出，关于“九一八事变”的说法源自日本军方，其可信度存疑。外界也普遍怀疑这是一场策划好的事变，一场蓄谋已久的入侵。正如《伦敦时报》9 月 21 日所言，日军在占领沈阳的三天前，曾进行了一场“近乎入侵的彩排”；并且“尽管有报道称，南满铁路事件是事态发展的起因，但事实是，整场行动在所谓的‘事件’发生前就已经全面展开。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319212) 简而言之，有大量证据足以让一个理性的人三思而行。 再加上，公众普遍认为内阁被军方蒙骗了，这让拉蒙特的急切举动显得颇为费解。

Cynicism toward China certainly explains much of the Morgan sympathy for the Mukden attack. Russell Leffingwell, in a hot-blooded letter to Walter Lippmann, said the indignation over Mukden was entirely misplaced. “It is grotesque for the League or for America to interfere on the side of Chinese raiders and revolutionaries, who have kept their people in war and fear and misery all these long years; and on the side of red Russia; and against the side of Japan, who in pursuance of her treaty rights has been keeping order in Manchuria and maintaining the only safe-asylum open to the fear-ridden Chinese.” He hoped the Japanese would “thumb their noses” at any League of Nations or U.S. protest against its action.[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319364)
对中国的敌意确实在很大程度上解释了摩根对沈阳事变持同情态度的原因。罗素·莱芬韦尔在写给沃尔特·李普曼的一封激烈信件中表示，对沈阳事件的义愤完全用错了地方。“国际联盟或美国介入，去支持那些长年让人民处于战争、恐惧和苦难中的中国抢劫犯和革命者，支持赤色俄国，而去反对日本——日本根据条约权利在满洲维持秩序，为那些饱受恐惧的中国人提供了唯一安全的庇护所——这简直是荒谬至极。”他希望日本能对国际联盟或美国对其行动的抗议“嗤之以鼻”。 [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319364)

Along with his secret work for Mussolini, the Mukden incident is probably the most disturbing episode in Lamont’s career (although nobody knew about it then). Was he trying to impress the Japanese with elite Morgan services? Or was he simply trying to maintain the value of Japanese securities? He undoubtedly wanted to shore up Inouye’s tenuous position in the government. The finance minister had to demonstrate to the military that he wouldn’t betray or work against it. In fact, in November, Lamont warned the Japanese that if Inouye were expelled from the cabinet—as the military favored—there would be a “distinct chill” on Wall Street and in the City.[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319575) But if Inouye felt a need to appease the military, why did Lamont join him?
除了为墨索里尼秘密工作外，九一八事变可能是拉蒙特职业生涯中最令人不安的一段插曲（尽管当时无人知晓）。他是在用摩根精英的服务来取悦日本人吗？还是仅仅为了维持日本证券的价值？他无疑是想巩固井上在政府中摇摇欲坠的地位。这位财政大臣必须向军方证明，他不会背叛或与之作对。事实上，拉蒙特在 11 月警告日本人，如果井上被逐出内阁——正如军方所希望的那样——那么华尔街和伦敦金融城将迎来“明显的寒意”。但如果说井上需要安抚军方，拉蒙特为何要与他同流合污呢？

As with Mussolini, Lamont was going beyond public relations to something approaching propaganda for a foreign power. It was a strange new application of the Gentleman Banker’s Code of absolute loyalty to one’s clients. Any banker could underwrite securities, but only Lamont could lobby politicians, shape newspaper editorials, and sway public opinion. The Mukden press release exposed the dangers in having bankers act like politicians and adopt the same proprietary feeling toward foreign governments as toward industrial concerns. It pointed up the perils of blurring politics and finance in the Diplomatic Age.
和墨索里尼的情况类似，拉蒙特已超越公关范畴，近乎为外国势力进行宣传。这是绅士银行家准则——对客户绝对忠诚——的一种怪异新应用。任何银行家都能承销证券，但唯有拉蒙特能游说政客、左右报纸社论、操纵公众舆论。奉天事件新闻稿暴露出银行家像政客般行事、对外国政府生出如同对工业企业般占有欲的危险性。它揭示了在"外交时代"模糊政治与金融界限的风险。

If Lamont were truly taken in by Mukden, then he was soon rudely stripped of his illusions. In December 1931, a less liberal Japanese cabinet took power, and Inouye was replaced by Korekiyo Takahashi, who promptly took Japan off the gold standard. In late January 1932, the world was horrified by Japanese bombing of Chinese civilians in thickly populated suburbs of Shanghai. Once again, the Japanese blamed Chinese provocation. The terrorist tactics were far more naked than those used in Mukden, and the evidence of brutality more graphic and abundant. Newsreels brought shocking pictures of the carnage into American movie theaters. Lamont was so dismayed he told his friend Saburo Sonoda of the Yokohama Specie Bank that Japan could no longer raise money in American markets—so ghastly was the impression left by Shanghai.[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319764) For the House of Morgan, Shanghai initiated a slow process of disenchantment. A chastened Leffingwell wrote to Teddy Grenfell, “I confess to having had a good deal of sympathy with the Japanese in Manchuria, though none at all with the Japanese at Shanghai.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319952)
如果拉蒙特真被奉天所迷惑，那么他很快就残酷地摆脱了幻想。1931 年 12 月，一个不那么开明的日本内阁上台，井上被高桥是清取代，后者立即让日本脱离金本位。1932 年 1 月下旬，日本轰炸上海人口稠密郊区的中国平民，令世界震惊。日本再次将责任归咎于中国的挑衅。这次使用的恐怖手段比奉天时更加赤裸裸，野蛮行径的证据也更加直观和充足。新闻短片将大屠杀的可怕画面带入美国电影院。拉蒙特深感震惊，他对横滨正金银行的朋友园田三郎说，上海留下的恐怖印象让日本再也无法在美国市场筹集资金。 [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319764) 对摩根家族而言，上海开启了一段缓慢的幻灭过程。 一个受挫的 Leffingwell 写信给 Teddy Grenfell 说：“我承认对满洲的日本人相当同情，但对上海的日本人则毫无同情。”” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2319952)

Now Lamont was to absorb one stunning blow after another. Right-wing terrorism—which had already claimed the life of Prime Minister Hamaguchi in a 1930 shooting—turned on the world of finance. One by one, Lamont’s Japanese friends were killed. During the Shanghai fighting in February, he received a telegram from Sonoda that said: “WITH SORROWING HEART INFORM YOU OF ASSASSINATION AND DEATH OF MR. I. INOUYE IT SEEMS \[AS\] IF A GREAT LIGHT HAS BEEN EXTINGUISHED AND MY DEAR COUNTRY IS FALLING INTO DARK DAYS.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320155)
如今 Lamont 接连遭受沉重打击。右翼恐怖主义——此前已在 1930 年的枪击中夺走了首相 Hamaguchi 的生命——如今将矛头指向金融界。Lamont 的日本朋友们一个接一个地被杀害。二月的上海战事期间，他收到 Sonoda 发来的电报：“沉痛告知您，I. Inouye 先生遇刺身亡。仿佛一盏明灯熄灭，我深爱的国家正坠入黑暗岁月。” [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320155)

Inouye, sixty-three, was in the midst of a general-election campaign. As leader of the Minseito, he was expected to become the next prime minister. As he stepped from his car at a suburban Tokyo school, a twenty-two-year-old rural youth stepped from the shadows in a tattered kimono and black felt hat. He shot Inouye in the chest. The assassin was a member of the secretive, superpatriotic Blood Brotherhood, a group of fanatic young nationalists. At the police station, he boasted of his deed and blamed rural poverty on Inouye’s deflationary policies. Speaking to reporters at the Imperial University Hospital, Inouye’s somber, dry-eyed widow explained that she had readied herself for this moment while her husband was in the cabinet.
六十三岁的井上当时正处于大选竞选活动中。作为民政党的领袖，他有望成为下一任首相。当他在东京郊区的一所学校下车时，一名二十二岁的农村青年从阴影中走出，身穿破旧的和服，头戴黑色毡帽，朝井上的胸口开了一枪。刺客是秘密且极端爱国的血盟团成员，这是一个由狂热的年轻民族主义者组成的组织。在警察局，他夸耀自己的行为，并将农村的贫困归咎于井上的通货紧缩政策。在帝国大学医院面对记者时，井上面色阴沉、眼不干涸的遗孀解释说，她在丈夫担任内阁成员时就已经为这一刻做好了准备。

Lamont was profoundly upset; after all, it was Inouye who gave him hope that the old illustrious families and their liberal allies could keep militarism at bay. He wrote a touching letter of condolence to his friend Sonoda: “Such a gentle soul he was—it seems the more inexplicable that his end should be like this.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320347)
拉蒙特感到非常难过；毕竟，正是井上让他看到了希望，那些古老的显赫家族及其自由派盟友能够遏制军国主义。他给朋友园田写了一封感人的慰问信：“他是如此温和善良的灵魂——他的结局竟会如此，这更让人难以理解。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320347)

The more Lamont resisted the truth about Japan, the more forcibly it intruded. A few weeks after Inouye’s assassination came the murder of Lamont’s other major Japanese friend, Baron Takuma Dan, the MIT-trained mining engineer and chief executive of Mitsui, who had hosted him at his villa in 1920. Baron Dan was shot as he emerged from his car at the white marble Mitsui Bank. Again the assassin was a rural youth and was apparently also a member of the Blood Brotherhood. Lamont wrote to Baron Dan’s family, recalling the 1920 trip: “I had thought at times of him as a poet in business and this impression came to me as he showed me his house and garden and we stood together looking at Fujiyama, a majestic picture towering above a superb landscape.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320518)
拉蒙特越是抗拒有关日本的真相，真相就越是强行闯入他的生活。井上被刺几周后，拉蒙特另一位重要的日本朋友——团琢磨男爵也遭杀害。这位麻省理工毕业的矿业工程师、三井公司的首席执行官，曾在 1920 年于自家别墅招待过拉蒙特。团琢磨男爵在东京白色大理石的三井银行前下车时，遭到枪击。凶手同样是一名农村青年，据信也是"血盟团"成员。拉蒙特致信团琢磨家人，追忆 1920 年的那次旅行："我有时觉得他是商界的诗人——当他带我参观他的宅邸和庭院，我们并肩远眺富士山，那巍峨壮丽的景象高悬于绝美风景之上时，这种印象便油然而生。" [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320518)

Baron Dan’s killing was an act of revenge against the House of Mitsui, which rightists had accused of treacherous profiteering in the so-called dollar-buying scandal. After England left the gold standard in September 1931, Mitsui and other *zaibatsu* banks expected the yen to be forced off gold, too, an effective devaluation. So they furiously sold yen and bought dollars. These foreign-exchange transactions netted Mitsui an estimated \$50 million. But they also triggered a patriotic uproar about banks speculating against their country’s currency. The issue proved an emotional one during the 1932 election. In the growing atmosphere of political extremism, many Japanese sympathized with Inouye’s and Baron Dan’s assassins, who received lenient sentences. Both were released from prison within a few years.
团琢磨男爵被杀，是三井家族遭遇的复仇——右翼分子指控三井在所谓"美元购买丑闻"中牟取暴利。1931 年 9 月英国脱离金本位后，三井及其他财阀银行预判日元也将被迫脱离金本位，这等同于有效贬值。于是他们疯狂抛售日元、抢购美元。这些外汇交易让三井净赚约 5000 万美元，却也引发了一场爱国怒火：民众痛斥银行投机做空本国货币。在 1932 年大选中，这一问题成为引爆情绪的焦点。随着政治极端主义氛围日益浓厚，许多日本人对刺杀井上准之助与团琢磨的刺客抱有同情，二人最终获从轻判决，数年内便相继出狱。

Lamont didn’t readily admit error and didn’t know how to abandon clients. By now, the strong rightward shift of Japanese politics was evident. The Kwantung army had overrun Manchuria, creating the puppet state of Manchukuo in March and installing Pu Yi, the last Manchu emperor, as its pliant figurehead. The incident at Mukden, the bombing of Shanghai, the murders of Inouye and Baron Dan—these events should have opened Lamont’s eyes. He could no longer plead ignorance. His files from early 1932 do reveal a deep displeasure with the Japanese as he warned them not to repeat the Shanghai error, which had destroyed any sympathy they still had on Wall Street.
拉蒙特并不轻易认错，也不知道如何放弃客户。到这时，日本政治的强烈右倾已显而易见。关东军占领了满洲，并于 3 月建立了伪满洲国，将末代清帝溥仪扶植为温顺的傀儡。奉天事变、轰炸上海、刺杀井上准之助和团琢磨男爵——这些事件本该让拉蒙特看清现实。他不能再推说不知情。他 1932 年初的档案确实显示出对日本的强烈不满，他曾警告他们不要重蹈上海的覆辙，那次事件已经摧毁了他们在华尔街仅存的一点同情。

Nevertheless, that spring, in a bizarre turn, Lamont and Martin Egan drifted back to a pro-Japanese stance. The two had become close friends with Count Aisuke Kabayama, who had been educated at Princeton, was married on Long Island, and was close to Emperor Hirohito. Kabayama’s grandfather had been an admiral and a governor of Taiwan. Lamont and Egan encouraged him to set up a Japanese information bureau in America on the Mussolini model and proudly briefed him on their Italian work. In the late spring, Egan went to Japan for talks about Manchuria. When he returned talking about “banditry and disorder in Manchuria” and blaming China for hostilities, he sounded like a Japanese militarist.[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320704)
然而，那年春天，局势却怪异地一转——Lamont 和 Martin Egan 又倒向了亲日立场。两人与樺山愛輔伯爵成了密友。这位伯爵曾在普林斯顿求学，在长岛成婚，与裕仁天皇关系密切。樺山的祖父曾任海军大将及台湾总督。Lamont 与 Egan 鼓励他仿效墨索里尼模式，在美国设立日本情报局，并得意洋洋地向他介绍自己在意大利的“成果”。春末，Egan 赴日就满洲问题展开谈判。待他归来后，满口都是“满洲的匪患与混乱”，将冲突归咎于中国，活脱脱一副日本军国主义者的腔调。

The House of Morgan no longer knew which master it served—America or Japan. A few days later, on May 15, 1932, another political murder blackened Japan’s image: the aging prime minister, Tsuyoshi Inukai, was gunned down in his official residence by nine young army officers, probably because he wanted to curb the military. He was replaced by Admiral Makoto Saito. It would be the end of party government in Japan until after World War II.
摩根财团已不知自己究竟是在为美国效力，还是在为日本卖命。几天后，1932 年 5 月 15 日，又一场政治暗杀令日本形象蒙上阴影：年迈的首相犬养毅在官邸被九名年轻军官枪杀，原因很可能是他试图约束军方。接替他的是海军大将斋藤实。自此，日本的政党政治宣告终结，直到二战结束后才得以恢复。

In the fall of 1932, Lamont had to confront the unpleasant truth about Mukden, the knowledge that his press release for Inouye had been a hollow piece of propaganda. The League of Nations had dispatched an investigative commission to the Far East under Lord Lytton. Even before the Lytton report was endorsed by the League, Lamont’s assistant, Vernon Munroe, dined one evening with General Frank McCoy, the commission’s American member. The next morning, Munroe told Lamont, “The General said there was a grave question as to whether there was any explosion, that the Japanese had never been able to explain how the regular trains continued to run immediately after the explosion was supposed to have taken place and the more they had explained the more of a contradiction they had gotten into.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320888) A month later, the Lytton report condemned Japanese aggression as violating the League’s Covenant and branded Manchukuo a puppet state. Although the report was critical of Chinese provocations, Japan walked out of the Assembly of the League and brazenly tightened its grip on Manchuria.
1932 年秋，拉蒙特不得不直面关于奉天（沈阳）的残酷真相——他替井上代笔的那篇新闻稿，不过是空洞的宣传套路。国际联盟已委派李顿勋爵率领调查团远赴远东。就在李顿报告获得国联正式认可之前，拉蒙特的助手弗农·门罗曾与调查团美方成员弗兰克·麦考伊将军共进晚餐。次日清晨，门罗向拉蒙特透露："将军说，当时究竟有没有发生爆炸都是个重大疑点——日本人始终无法解释，为什么在所谓爆炸发生后，正常列车还能继续运行；而且他们越是解释，就越是漏洞百出。" [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2320888) 一个月后，李顿报告裁定日本侵略行为违反了国联盟约，并将"满洲国"定性为傀儡政权。 尽管报告也批评了中方的挑衅行为，日本退出国联大会，厚颜无耻地加紧了对满洲的控制。

By this point, Lamont was in a quandary. He wanted to maintain a belief in Japan’s good intentions amid overwhelming, contradictory evidence. To sort out his feelings, he sat down and wrote a memo marked “Secret and Strictly Confidential.” Whether he ever circulated it is uncertain, but it shows a man fleeing reality. “These are entirely my private thoughts,” it starts out, then continues, “American suspicions as to Japan’s motives are essentially these: that Japan has aggressive designs on the Asiatic Continent and that Japan may even be courting war with the United States—which are not true.” To correct such misconceptions, he recommends a joint U.S.-Japanese declaration on trade and peaceful relations. The conclusion is a desperate pipe dream: “If such a joint declaration can be made, all war talk will immediately be silenced, the psychology of men will undergo a change and whatever question may arise between our two countries will become capable of an easy solution.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2321082)
至此，拉蒙特陷入进退两难的困境。面对铺天盖地、自相矛盾的事实，他仍想相信日本的善意。为了理清思绪，他坐下来写了一份标有“绝密”字样的备忘录。这份备忘录是否曾传阅已无从考证，但它却暴露了一个逃避现实之人的内心。备忘录开篇写道：“这纯属个人私见，”接着写道，“美国对日本动机的怀疑，本质上不外乎两点：其一，日本对亚洲大陆怀有侵略野心；其二，日本甚至可能在蓄意挑起与美国之间的战争——但这些都不是事实。”为了纠正这些误解，他建议美日两国发表一份关于贸易与和平关系的联合声明。其结论简直是一个绝望的镜花水月：“倘若能发表这样一份联合声明，所有关于战争的言论都将即刻平息，人们的心理也将发生转变，我们两国之间无论出现任何问题，都将迎刃而解。”

It became progressively more difficult for Lamont to sustain any belief in Japan’s imminent return to civil government. As lords of Manchukuo, the army built huge dams and industries to strengthen the nation’s preparedness for war. The new finance minister, Takahashi, known as the Japanese Keynes, boosted military spending to almost half the Japanese budget. The liberalism of the twenties, along with its foremost exponents, was dead.
拉蒙特越来越难以相信日本会很快恢复文官政府。作为满洲的主宰者，军方修建了巨大的水坝和工业设施，以加强国家的战备。新任财政大臣高桥，被称为日本的凯恩斯，将军事开支提高到几乎占日本预算的一半。二十年代的自由主义，连同其主要代表人物，都已消亡。

In 1934, Lamont underwent a sudden change of heart. Once his eyes were open, he felt fooled, and his trust turned to bitterness. He cut off subscriptions to Japanese cultural groups, snubbed visiting Japanese dignitaries, and warned Japan’s consul general that the Japanese should not mistake America’s peaceful spirit for cowardice. When he heard rumors that the British cabinet might renew an alliance with Japan, he lobbied against the move. He sent an impassioned letter to Grenfell, which he expected to be passed around Whitehall: “In place of the fair liberal government that existed in the first twenty years of this century there has arisen a military clique which . . . if accounts from the liberal elements in Japan are true, have been conducting itself a good deal as a lot of the young German Nazis have been conducting themselves.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2321336)
1934 年，拉蒙特突然改变了心意。一旦看清真相，他感到自己被愚弄，信任化为了怨恨。他中止了对日本文化团体的资助，冷落来访的日本要人，并警告日本总领事，日本人不应将美国的和平精神误认为怯懦。听闻英国内阁可能恢复与日本结盟的传言，他极力游说反对此举。他给格伦费尔写了一封充满激情的信，期望能在白厅传阅：“本世纪最初二十年存在的自由开明政府已被一个军国主义集团取代，如果来自日本自由派人士的报道属实，这个集团的行径与那群年轻的德国纳粹分子如出一辙。” [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2321336)

The Japanese army would continue to annex parts of northern China, a campaign that in 1937 would culminate in the Sino-Japanese War and the butchering of tens of thousands of Chinese civilians in the rape of Nanking. It was a dismal, ironic denouement to Morgan involvement in China, which began with Willard Straight’s dream of America acting as a buffer against Japanese encroachment in Manchuria and ended with a senior Morgan partner serving as apologist for that very action.
日本军队继续吞并华北地区，这一行动在 1937 年演变为中日战争，并在南京大屠杀中屠杀了数万中国平民。这是摩根公司在中国参与的一个凄凉而具有讽刺意味的结局——始于威拉德·斯特雷特梦想美国能成为抵御日本侵占满洲的缓冲，却以一位摩根高级合伙人为此暴行辩护而告终。
