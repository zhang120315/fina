# Chapter Thirty-One: Tombstones

TO the outside world, Morgan Stanley still presented a debonair facade in the late 1970s. An *Atlantic Monthly* reporter, visiting its six floors atop the Exxon Building, marveled at its aplomb, its artfully modulated decor in brown and ocher. “To stroll through the hallways of Morgan Stanley is to move through a landscape of rolltop desks and Brooks Brothers suits,” the reporter declared.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422648) If it stumbled in the Middle East, it profited handily from the oil boom, arranging an astounding 40 percent of the money raised by the big oil companies. As investment banker to Standard Oil of Ohio, it did a record \$1.75-billion private placement for the Trans Alaska Pipeline. In 1977, it supervised Wall Street’s end of a \$1-billion offering of British Petroleum shares owned by the British government, the largest stock offering in history. Right through the mid-1970s, it ranked first in the stock and bond offerings it managed.
在 20 世纪 70 年代末，摩根士丹利对外仍保持着风度翩翩的形象。一位《大西洋月刊》记者走访其位于埃克森大厦顶部的六层办公室时，对其镇定自若的气度以及精心搭配的棕色与赭石色装饰啧啧称奇。“漫步在摩根士丹利的走廊，就像穿行于翻盖书桌与布克兄弟西装构成的风景中，”记者写道。尽管在中东偶有失足，但它凭借石油热潮大赚特赚，经手了大型石油公司募资额中高达 40%的业务，令人咋舌。作为俄亥俄标准石油的投资银行，它为阿拉斯加输油管道完成了一笔 17.5 亿美元的私募，创下纪录。1977 年，它主导了华尔街端一笔 10 亿美元的英国石油公司股票发售——这些股票由英国政府持有，是史上最大规模的股票发行。直至 70 年代中期，它在所承销的股票和债券发行中始终排名第一。

It didn’t seem a place in ferment, yet it was. Each year, it sprouted a new wing: portfolio management (1975), government bond trading and automated brokerage for institutions (1976), and retail brokerage for rich investors through its purchase of Shuman Agnew and Company in San Francisco (1977). The pride, even smugness, of the old Morgan Stanley stemmed from its extreme selectivity in hiring. Now, in a decade, the firm grew from about two hundred to seventeen hundred employees, with capital soaring from \$7.5 million to \$118 million. It was growing too fast to preserve a homogeneous culture.
看似平静无澜，实则暗流涌动。每年，它都会长出新的枝干：1975 年推出投资组合管理业务，1976 年涉足国债交易与机构自动化经纪业务，1977 年通过收购旧金山的 Shuman Agnew and Company，为富裕投资者提供零售经纪服务。老摩根士丹利的骄傲——甚至可以说是自满——源于其对招聘的极端挑剔。而如今，仅仅十年间，公司员工从大约两百人增长至一千七百人，资本从 750 万美元飙升至 1.18 亿美元。它发展得太快，以至于无法维持一种同质化的文化。

As architect of this brave new world, Bob Baldwin often seemed disoriented by the range of new businesses. He had instinctively understood the need to trade and distribute securities yet never quite mastered these alien operations. He found it hard to adjust to a bizarre new world of fluctuating market signals and elevated risk. Risk, after all, had been foreign to the old Morgan Stanley, which only wanted sure things. When a \$20-million bet on long Treasury bonds went the wrong way, Baldwin, in a sweat, summoned a meeting of all the senior partners. Another time, when bad news from Washington sent the market tumbling, Baldwin appeared on the floor insisting, “The market should go up. The market’s wrong!” This world couldn’t be controlled, even by somebody as strong and willful as Bob Baldwin.
作为这个勇敢新世界的缔造者，鲍勃·鲍德温面对层出不穷的新业务常常显得手足无措。他本能地明白交易和证券分销的必要性，却始终未能真正驾驭这些陌生的业务。市场信号变幻莫测、风险高企的诡异新世界让他难以适应。毕竟，风险对昔日的摩根士丹利而言是个陌生概念——这家公司只追求稳操胜券的买卖。当一笔 2000 万美元的长期国债押注失误时，鲍德温急得满头大汗，紧急召集全体资深合伙人开会。另一次，华盛顿传来的坏消息导致股市暴跌，鲍德温冲到交易大厅坚持宣称：“市场本该上涨！是市场错了！”这个世界，即便强势固执如鲍勃·鲍德温，也根本无法掌控。

Bob Baldwin probably saved the firm and destroyed its soul. This new Morgan Stanley was a monument to his force and clear vision, a brilliant adaptation to altered circumstances. Yet he badly politicized a firm long unified by a special *esprit de corps*. His management philosophy played people off against each other. If meant to improve performance, it produced a tense, unpleasant atmosphere. For the first time in the firm’s history, senior partners defected for other firms. To some extent, turf fights were inevitable in a larger, richer firm. Baldwin, however, exacerbated the tensions. One example involved extremely close friends, Luis Mendez and Damon Mezzacappa, the two stars of the new trading operation. Yet Baldwin gave Mendez a \$25,000 bonus, then went out of his way to tell Mezzacappa about it, saying Mendez was doing a better job. This was either obtuse or insensitive. As Baldwin became more abrasive and difficult, Bill Black, son of the former World Bank president, functioned as the great mediator, pleading for those who found it hard to deal directly with the difficult Baldwin. By softening Baldwin’s rough edges, Black held the firm together and prevented an outright split between bankers and traders, such as would later shatter Lehman Brothers.
Bob Baldwin 或许拯救了公司，却也摧毁了它的灵魂。这家崭新的摩根士丹利，是他雷厉风行与远见卓识的丰碑，也是顺应时势的精妙转型之作。然而，他将长期以来靠团队精神凝聚一心的公司严重政治化。他的管理哲学就是挑拨员工互相争斗。就算初衷是为了提升业绩，结果却营造出紧张、压抑的氛围。公司历史上头一回，资深合伙人跳槽去了别家。在某种程度上，随着公司规模扩大、财力增强，地盘之争在所难免。但 Baldwin 却加剧了这种紧张关系。有个例子涉及两位极其亲密的朋友——Luis Mendez 和 Damon Mezzacappa，他们是新交易业务的两颗明星。然而 Baldwin 给了 Mendez 25,000 美元奖金，还特意跑去告诉 Mezzacappa 这件事，说 Mendez 干得更好。这要么是迟钝，要么就是麻木不仁。 随着鲍德温变得越来越刻薄难缠，前世界银行行长的儿子比尔·布莱克扮演了重要的调解者角色，为那些难以直接与这位难缠的鲍德温打交道的人说情。布莱克磨平了鲍德温的棱角，从而维系了公司的团结，避免了银行家和交易员之间彻底分裂——这种分裂后来曾导致雷曼兄弟轰然倒塌。

The major threat to Morgan Stanley’s preeminence was its celebrated but increasingly tenuous policy of appearing as sole manager atop tombstone ads, those black-bordered boxes of underwriters’ names that appear in newspapers. Tombstone positions were a life-and-death matter for Wall Street firms. Those in higher layers, or brackets, received larger share allotments, while the smaller firms tried to struggle their way upward. Within brackets, firms were listed alphabetically. During the Great Alphabet War of 1976, Halsey, Stuart adopted its parent’s name, Bache, just to bootstrap up a few lines in tombstones. This was no joking matter. On May 13, 1964, Walston and Company had been demoted from a top bracket in a Comsat offering; the next day its managing director, Vernon Walston, shot himself, giving a macabre new aptness to the term describing the ad.
摩根士丹利的主导地位面临的主要威胁，是其曾引以为傲但日渐脆弱的策略——在墓碑广告（报纸上那些刊登承销商姓名的黑框广告）中作为唯一主承销商亮相。墓碑广告的排位对华尔街公司而言是生死攸关的大事。排位越高（即层级越高），获得的股票分配额度就越大，而小公司则拼命向上攀爬。在同一层级内，公司按字母顺序排列。在 1976 年的大字母战争中，Halsey, Stuart 公司直接改用了其母公司名称 Bache，只为在墓碑广告中上移几行。这绝非儿戏。1964 年 5 月 13 日，沃尔斯顿公司在一次通信卫星公司（Comsat）的发行中被从顶级承销商行列降级；次日，其董事总经理弗农·沃尔斯顿饮弹自尽，为描述这种广告的术语赋予了令人毛骨悚然的新意。

In the late 1960s and early 1970s, the top tier—called the bulge bracket—consisted of Morgan Stanley; First Boston; Kuhn, Loeb; and Dillon, Read. The first two originated most business, and Morgan Stanley was reluctant to relinquish the undivided profits of sole managership. A former managing director explained, “When I first went to Morgan Stanley, a senior person laughed and said to me, We only have to scare people into using us as sole manager 50 percent of the time and we’re still better off.” There was a touch of narcissism in wanting to appear alone in the top left corner of tombstones. There was also an unstated agenda: before the 1970s, Morgan Stanley lacked selling power and disguised this weakness by leading syndicates and having other firms do the selling. As Lewis Bernard later said, the firm “had to keep the Street from realizing the emperor had no clothes.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422791) While other firms tried to ape the sole-manager strategy, none succeeded nearly as often as Morgan Stanley.
在 20 世纪 60 年代末到 70 年代初，顶级投行圈（被称为“承销圈顶级梯队”）由摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）、第一波士顿（First Boston）、库恩·洛布（Kuhn, Loeb）和狄龙·里德（Dillon, Read）组成。其中前两家公司承揽了大部分业务，而摩根士丹利更是舍不得放弃独家主承销带来的全部利润。一位前董事总经理解释道：“我刚进摩根士丹利时，一位资深人士笑着对我说，我们只需要吓唬住客户，让他们一半的时间都选我们做独家主承销，我们就已经赚翻了。”在墓碑广告的左上角独占鳌头，多少带着点自恋的味道。此外，还有一层未明说的意图：在 70 年代之前，摩根士丹利缺乏销售能力，于是通过牵头承销团、让其他公司来负责销售的方式掩盖了这一短板。正如刘易斯·伯纳德（Lewis Bernard）后来所说，该公司“必须防止业界看穿皇帝的新衣。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422791) 其他公司也曾试图效仿这种独家主承销策略，但没有一家能像摩根士丹利那样屡屡得手。

In order to make the policy stick, Morgan Stanley had to sacrifice even powerful clients who demanded co-managers on issues. (The Japanese rebuff was an early and notorious example of this.) It skipped one underwriting after Houston Industries insisted on rotating lead managers. It skipped another when Singer wanted to reward Goldman, Sachs for some merger work by appointing it co-manager. But such was Morgan Stanley’s evergreen mystique that many firms, from Du Pont to J. P. Morgan and Company itself, still submitted to its golden chains on all their underwritings.
为了让这项政策得以贯彻，摩根士丹利甚至不惜牺牲那些要求在发行中任命联席管理人的大客户（早期日本客户遭拒就是一个广为人知的例子）。当休斯顿工业公司坚持要轮换主承销商时，它放弃了一次承销业务；当胜家公司为了回报高盛在并购中的贡献而指定其为联席管理人时，它又放弃了一次。但摩根士丹利始终保持着经久不衰的魅力，从杜邦到摩根公司本身，许多企业仍心甘情愿地将其所有承销业务都交由它掌控。

Because up to two hundred firms participated in Morgan Stanley syndicates, they feared its displeasure. Before 1975, Morgan’s syndicate manager was Fred Whittemore. Bright, sardonic, and voluble, an avid collector of Pierpont Morgan memorabilia, he was called the Godfather or Father Fred. He had a pervasive power on Wall Street. When William Simon wished to return to Salomon Brothers after serving as treasury secretary, it was Father Fred who interceded with John Gutfreund. In the early 1970s, many attributed E. F. Hutton’s stunning rise to Father Fred’s patronage, and he didn’t hesitate to thwart competitors, such as Lehman Brothers. After each issue, Father Fred filled out large yellow cards listing each firm’s performance. Sometimes participants lied or took losses just to look good.
由于多达两百家公司参与了摩根士丹利的承销团，它们都唯恐惹其不快。1975 年之前，摩根士丹利的承销负责人是弗雷德·惠特莫尔。他聪明、刻薄、健谈，热衷于收藏 J.P.摩根的纪念品，被称为“教父”或“弗雷德神父”。他在华尔街拥有广泛的影响力。当威廉·西蒙在担任财政部长后想重返所罗门兄弟公司时，正是弗雷德神父出面与约翰·古特弗罗因德交涉。20 世纪 70 年代初，许多人将 E.F.赫顿公司的惊人崛起归功于弗雷德神父的扶持，而他也会毫不犹豫地打压竞争对手，比如雷曼兄弟。每次发行结束后，弗雷德神父都会在一张大黄卡上填写各家公司的表现。有时，参与者会撒谎或承担亏损，只是为了让自己看起来表现良好。

There was always suspicion that Morgan Stanley exploited its sole-manager power to fend off competitive threats. “We could be talking to their clients about an investment banking relationship, and if Morgan saw this, instead of giving us half-a-million shares, they might hold back on us,” one rival told the *New York Times* in 1975.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2422939) Morgan Stanley bristled at these anonymous snipes in the press, which appeared periodically. Father Fred created the modern Wall Street lineup. He kicked out the fading Kuhn, Loeb and Dillon, Read from the bulge bracket and brought in Merrill Lynch, Salomon Brothers, and Goldman, Sachs. After Kuhn, Loeb—historically the most redoubtable Morgan adversary—was absorbed by Lehman Brothers in 1977, senior partner John Schiff met Harry Morgan at a board meeting of the Metropolitan Museum of Art. When Morgan asked how this had happened, Schiff replied, “Henry, you chose your partners better than I did.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423086) Schiff’s remark pointed to a continuing strength of the Morgan houses—the sheer excellence of their people.
一直有人怀疑摩根士丹利利用其独家承销商的权力来抵御竞争威胁。1975 年，一位竞争对手对《纽约时报》表示：“我们可能正与他们的客户洽谈投资银行业务，如果摩根士丹利发现了，他们非但不会给我们 50 万股，反而可能扣着不给。”对于这些不时出现在媒体上的匿名抨击，摩根士丹利颇为恼火。老弗雷德打造了现代华尔街的顶级投行阵容。他将日渐衰落的 Kuhn, Loeb 和 Dillon, Read 踢出了第一梯队，并引入了 Merrill Lynch、Salomon Brothers 和 Goldman, Sachs。1977 年，历史上曾是最令人生畏的摩根对手 Kuhn, Loeb 被 Lehman Brothers 收购后，其高级合伙人 John Schiff 与大都会艺术博物馆董事会上遇到了 Harry Morgan。当摩根问起此事如何发生时，Schiff 回答说：“亨利，你选合伙人的眼光比我强。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423086) 希夫的话指出了摩根家族历久弥新的优势——其成员卓越非凡的才华。

But by the late 1970s, Morgan Stanley’s sole-manager policy was a gilded anachronism. How could you handcuff clients in global financial markets when corporate treasurers enjoyed so many options, so much room in which to maneuver? The firm, significantly, had never made the sole-manager policy stick at its Paris joint venture with Morgan Guaranty. A loyal home client like General Motors Acceptance Corporation openly used other bankers abroad. In April 1977, in a final break with 23 Wall, Morgan Stanley closed up shop in Paris and set up Morgan Stanley International in London, linchpin of its Euromarket operations. The new operation had a rude shock when Australia, a faithful client since 1946, jumped to that old Morgan nemesis, Deutsche Bank. The event underscored not only the new power of global distribution, but the far more anonymous world of interlinked financial markets.
但到了 20 世纪 70 年代末，摩根士丹利的独家经理政策已沦为镀金的时代错误。当企业财务主管手握如此之多的选择、拥有如此广阔的操作空间时，你又如何在全球金融市场中束缚客户？值得注意的是，摩根士丹利与摩根保证信托在巴黎的合资企业里，从未真正贯彻过这项独家经理政策。像通用汽车承兑公司这样的忠实本土客户，也公然在海外雇佣其他银行。1977 年 4 月，摩根士丹利最终与华尔街 23 号分道扬镳，关闭了巴黎办事处，在伦敦成立了摩根士丹利国际公司——这是其欧洲债券市场业务的关键支柱。然而，这家新机构刚起步就遭遇了当头一棒：自 1946 年便忠实合作的澳大利亚客户，竟转投摩根士丹利的老对手德意志银行。这一事件不仅凸显了全球分销的新力量，更揭示了金融市场紧密互联后更为匿名的世界。

Even at home, there were new forces corroding the chains that bound companies to bankers. Since the days of Louis Brandeis, political reformers had advocated an arm’s-length relationship between companies and investment bankers. It was the theme broadcast by Robert Young during his testimony before Judge Medina and in his fight for the New York Central. The system had survived, however, because companies craved the association with the august House of Morgan, a vestige of the days when capital was scarce. But how could bankers still lord it over companies when capital was no longer rationed—when it was available in many markets in many forms? What leverage did they have as new financial intermediaries sprang up? From the clients’ standpoint, was there any longer a rationale for having an exclusive banker relationship? The answer was no.
即使在家庭内部，也有新的力量在腐蚀着束缚企业与银行家的锁链。自路易斯·布兰代斯时代以来，政治改革者就一直倡导企业与投资银行之间保持距离关系。这正是罗伯特·杨在梅迪纳法官面前作证以及争夺纽约中央铁路时所宣扬的主题。然而，这一体系之所以得以延续，是因为企业渴望与尊贵的摩根财团建立联系——这是资本稀缺时代的遗风。但当资本不再稀缺，当资本以多种形式出现在多个市场中时，银行家们又如何能继续凌驾于企业之上？随着新的金融中介机构不断涌现，他们还有什么筹码？从客户的角度来看，维持独家银行关系还有任何理由吗？答案是否定的。

So corporate America now did the work that was once solely the cause of reformers. One by one, corporate treasurers broke the links in the bankers’ chains. In the 1970s, Texaco, Mobil, International Harvester, and other clients circumvented Morgan Stanley and placed their debt directly with institutional investors. Other companies used dividend-reinvestment plans or employee stock purchase plans to raise capital. Having to cope with inflation and unstable exchange rates, corporate treasurers were receptive to bright ideas thought up by competing banks to deal with the new volatility. Jack Bennett of Exxon delighted in making Morgan Stanley spar with other firms. “We decided that any time a banker came up with a good idea, we’d talk to him,” said Bennett. When he set up “Dutch auctions” for issues, encouraging several competing syndicates, Morgan Stanley began to sense that its sole manager policy faced a mortal threat.
如今，美国企业界承担了曾经只属于改革者的事业。一家接一家，企业财务主管们斩断了银行家锁链上的环节。20 世纪 70 年代，德士古（Texaco）、美孚（Mobil）、国际收割机公司（International Harvester）等客户绕开摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley），直接将债务发行给机构投资者。其他公司则通过股息再投资计划或员工股票购买计划来筹集资本。面对通胀和汇率不稳定的局面，企业财务主管们乐于接受竞争银行提出的应对新波动的好点子。埃克森（Exxon）的杰克·贝内特（Jack Bennett）很喜欢让摩根士丹利与其他公司一较高下。贝内特说：“我们决定，只要银行家想出好主意，我们就跟他谈。”当他为发行设立“荷兰式拍卖”，鼓励多个承销团相互竞争时，摩根士丹利开始意识到，其独家管理政策正面临致命威胁。

For Morgan Stanley, the doomsday trumpet sounded in 1979. That year, IBM asked the firm to accept Salomon Brothers as co-manager on a \$1-billion debt issue needed for a new generation of computers. It was a telling sign of corporate autonomy in the Casino Age that IBM had a \$6-billion pile of cash on hand. It had never needed a public debt offering. (Some Morgan people say the IBM relationship—nominally Bob Greenhill’s account—was mishandled because nobody ever expected the company to require money.) In applying its sole-manager policy, Morgan Stanley had never before been obliged to turn down a client of such stature. Now here was one of the world’s largest corporations, a twenty-year client with a triple-A rating, undertaking the largest industrial borrowing in history.
对于摩根士丹利来说，末日号角在 1979 年吹响。那一年，IBM 要求该公司接受所罗门兄弟作为联合承销商，共同处理一笔为新一代计算机融资的 10 亿美元债务发行。这标志着赌场时代企业自主权的一个鲜明信号——IBM 当时手头拥有 60 亿美元的现金储备，它从未需要过公开发行债券。（一些摩根士丹利的人认为，与 IBM 的关系——名义上是 Bob Greenhill 的客户——被处理得一团糟，因为没人预料到这家公司会需要资金。）在施行独家承销政策时，摩根士丹利从未被迫拒绝过如此重量级的客户。而现在，这家全球最大的企业之一，一家评级为 AAA 级、合作长达二十年的客户，正在开展史上最大规模的工业借贷。

Morgan Stanley directors had an emotional, protracted debate about whether to reject the IBM offer and miss a fee of approximately \$1 million. The meeting was filled with high-flown rhetoric about upholding tradition. Bob Baldwin and Fred Whittemore were among the hawks who feared an IBM exception would embolden the other slaves to cast off their chains. After much resounding talk, nearly everybody voted to defy IBM and demand sole management. Morgan Stanley was shocked when word came back that IBM hadn’t budged in its demand: Salomon Brothers would head the issue, as planned. It was a landmark in Wall Street history: the golden chains were smashed.
摩根士丹利的董事们就是否拒绝 IBM 的邀约、放弃约 100 万美元的费用，展开了一场情绪激烈且旷日持久的辩论。会上满是对捍卫传统的慷慨陈词。鲍勃·鲍德温和弗雷德·惠特莫尔等鹰派人物担心，一旦为 IBM 破例，其他受制于人的投行也会纷纷挣脱枷锁。在一番高谈阔论之后，几乎所有人都投票决定拒绝 IBM，坚持要求独家承销。然而，当 IBM 态度强硬、坚持按原计划由所罗门兄弟主导发行时，摩根士丹利震惊不已。这成为华尔街历史上的一个里程碑：黄金枷锁就此被砸碎。

Before long, investment banks were raiding other Morgan Stanley clients with abandon, destroying the Gentleman Banker’s Code. A competitor observed cheerily, “Once the client list starts unwinding, it’s going to unwind all the way. It’s just a matter of time.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423230) Afterward, most of IBM’s business went to Salomon. Swallowing its pride, Morgan Stanley agreed to share issues for General Electric Credit, Du Pont, and Tenneco. It even began to participate in syndicates below the level of manager—a sight as shocking to old-timers as that of a master suddenly donning the livery of his footman. The age of relationship banking was dead.
不久，投资银行开始肆意抢夺摩根士丹利的其他客户，彻底打破了绅士银行家的守则。一位竞争对手兴高采烈地说：“客户名单一旦开始松动，就会一路崩盘，这只是时间问题。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423230) 之后，IBM 的大部分业务都流向了所罗门兄弟。摩根士丹利忍辱负重，同意与通用电气信贷、杜邦和天纳克共同发行股票。它甚至开始参与低于主承销商级别的银团——这一幕在资深人士看来，无异于一位老爷突然穿上了仆人的制服。关系型银行的时代就此终结。

Snubbed by its blue-chip clients, Morgan Stanley displayed a new receptivity to emerging-growth companies. It had long been chary of lending its imprimatur to untested companies—the name Morgan was synonymous with *established*—and had refrained from initial public offerings of stock. This squeamishness dated back at least to the preferred-list disaster of 1929. In 1980, perhaps taking a swipe at IBM, Morgan Stanley introduced rival Apple Computer to the stock market. (It also bought Hitachi computers for the office, something it wouldn’t have done before 1979.) For a long time, the firm had resisted high-tech start-ups. Now Morgan Stanley would lend its name to new ventures. Much like the indigent aristocrat who rents his castle to tourists, the firm would shamelessly trade on its class.
被蓝筹客户冷落后，摩根士丹利对新兴成长型公司展现出前所未有的开放态度。长久以来，它一直不愿为未经市场检验的企业背书——"摩根"这个名字本身就是成熟稳健的代名词——也因此始终未涉足首次公开募股业务。这种谨慎态度至少可追溯至 1929 年的优先股名单灾难。1980 年，或许是为了给 IBM 一记重击，摩根士丹利将竞争对手苹果公司推上了股市。（它还开始为办公室采购日立电脑，这在 1979 年前是不可想象的。）长期以来，这家公司一直对高科技初创企业持抗拒态度。如今，摩根士丹利却愿意将自己的名号借给新兴企业。就像落魄的贵族把自己的城堡租给游客一样，这家公司恬不知耻地利用自己的身份地位来赚钱了。

AS underwriting became a more mundane, impersonal business, Morgan Stanley relied more on its takeover department, which boomed under Bob Greenhill’s tutelage. Already in the late 1970s, merger work was being hailed as the last gold mine by investment bankers who assumed that Glass-Steagall would someday collapse and lead to a securities business overrun by commercial banks.
随着承销业务逐渐变成平淡无奇的流水线生意，摩根士丹利愈发依赖其并购部门，该部门在鲍勃·格林希尔的带领下蒸蒸日上。早在 20 世纪 70 年代末，投资银行家们就将并购业务誉为最后一座金矿——他们笃信《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》终将崩塌，届时证券行业将被商业银行淹没。

Takeovers transformed Morgan Stanley’s ethos. As a sponsor of securities, the old Morgan Stanley had fashioned a stately, incorruptible image. Emerging from the fury of Ferdinand Pecora, early partners took fright at the first breath of scandal. This culture was now tested by the more lucrative takeover work. By the late 1970s, the four-man M&A Department had expanded to a crack squad of fifty. Just five years after the watershed Inco-ESB raid, the firm was handling deals worth \$10 billion yearly, with a hundred potential deals in the hopper at any time. M&A was now the firm’s major source of profits. At the same time, takeover work had become divorced from the old seamless relationship with faithful clients. It was a giant, disciplined machine separated from the rest of the firm.
收购业务改变了摩根士丹利的企业文化。作为证券承销商，老摩根士丹利曾塑造出庄重清廉的形象。自费迪南德·佩科拉的猛烈抨击后，早期合伙人一遇丑闻风声便如惊弓之鸟。如今，这种文化正遭受着利润更丰厚的收购业务的考验。到 70 年代末，仅四人的并购部门已扩充为五十人的精锐团队。自英可收购 ESB 这一标志性事件仅过去五年，该公司每年处理的交易额就高达百亿美元，任何时候都有上百笔潜在交易在酝酿中。并购业务已成为公司的主要利润来源。与此同时，收购工作已与过去同忠实客户间无缝衔接的亲密关系脱节——它成了一台与公司其他部门割裂的、纪律严明的庞大机器。

Greenhill’s brawny raids didn’t fit easily into the old collegial firm, especially with his department contributing so disproportionately to profits. As one former partner recalls, “Greenhill was making a hell of a lot of money, and he was lording it over everybody.” Opposition, predictably, emerged from the syndicate side. Thomas A. Saunders III, who replaced Father Fred as syndicate chief, issued truculent warnings: “Greenhill should remember that whatever success he has comes from the franchise.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423380)
格林希尔的强硬突袭难以融入旧的合伙制公司，尤其是他所在的部门利润贡献如此不成比例地高。正如一位前合伙人回忆的那样：“格林希尔赚了非常多的钱，而且他还在大家面前趾高气扬。”不出所料，反对声音来自辛迪加那边。接替弗雷德神父成为辛迪加主管的托马斯·A·桑德斯三世发出了咄咄逼人的警告：“格林希尔应该记住，他无论取得什么成功，都源于这个特许经营权。”

By now, Morgan Stanley had left the white-shoe stereotype far behind, as the brash style of corporate marauders replaced the sedate style of underwriters. The old leisurely syndicate pace gave way to the fast, staccato beat of takeovers, with their weeks of frenetic activity. People now wore beepers, worked ninety-hour weeks, and remained on call over weekends, restricting their outside cultural and political activities—hallmarks of partners in the old House of Morgan. As the corps of managing directors ballooned in size, decisions were no longer made by milling around, and the firm was run in a more autocratic, from-the-top-down style.
如今，摩根士丹利早已将昔日“白鞋”精英的刻板印象远远甩在身后，企业掠夺者的张扬作风取代了承销商的稳重做派。过去银团那种悠闲的慢节奏，已被收购浪潮中急促断奏般的节拍所取代——接连数周的狂热活动成为常态。人们腰间别着寻呼机，每周工作 90 小时，周末随时待命，无暇参与外部文化与政治活动——而这些曾是老摩根合伙人身上的标志。随着董事总经理的队伍迅速膨胀，决策不再靠众人围聚商议，公司转向更为独裁的自上而下管理模式。

Expanding swiftly, Morgan Stanley found it harder to screen people or instill the old culture. As happened in the 1920s, a burgeoning financial industry rapidly attracted a new generation of young people. Untested college graduates were slipped into positions of great responsibility, with almost instant access to information worth millions. The demographic accent tilted to youth.
摩根士丹利快速扩张，筛选人才或灌输原有文化变得愈发困难。正如 20 世纪 20 年代那样，蓬勃发展的金融业迅速吸引了新一代年轻人。未经考验的大学毕业生被安排到责任重大的岗位上，几乎立刻就能接触到价值数百万的信息。员工结构也呈现出年轻化的趋势。

As questions of possible conflicts of interest in merger work surfaced, Bob Baldwin would quote Jack Morgan’s dictum of doing first-class business in a first-class way: “Nobody’s perfect, but we think we have the highest ethical standards in the industry.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423535) In 1973, the *New York Times* ran an article on insider trading with this caption below Baldwin’s photo: “ROBERT H. B. BALDWIN OF MORGAN STANLEY THINKS THE PRACTICE IS PASSÉ.” “Maybe I’m naive,” he said, “but I think the day of partners swapping that kind of information is long gone.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423660) Baldwin wasn’t cavalier about ethics, but he placed extraordinary faith in the power of so-called Chinese walls to insulate Greenhill’s operation from the rest of the firm.
随着合并工作中潜在利益冲突问题的浮现，鲍勃·鲍德温常引用杰克·摩根的名言——以一流的方式做一流的生意：“人无完人，但我们认为自己在业内拥有最高的道德标准。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423535) 1973 年，《纽约时报》刊登了一篇关于内幕交易的文章，鲍德温照片下的图注写道：“摩根士丹利的罗伯特·H·B·鲍德温认为这种做法已经过时。”“也许是我太天真，”他说，“但我认为合伙人之间交换那种信息的时代早已一去不返。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423660) 鲍德温并非对道德问题掉以轻心，但他极度信任所谓的“中国墙”能够将格林希尔的业务与公司其他部分隔离开来。

Morgan Stanley tried to throw the fear of God into merger specialists and monitored their activities closely. Briefed on legal and ethical issues, young professionals had to sign statements that they understood house rules. To foster a healthy paranoia about using inside information for personal gain, scare memos listing grounds for dismissal were circulated periodically. Oil analyst Barry Good remarked, “I have visions of someone stalking into my office to rip the epaulettes off my shoulders, break my calculator over his knee and drum me right out of the corps.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423807) Every fortnight, security officers conducted electronic sweeps, and projects were camouflaged with the names of English kings or Greek philosophers. Staff members weren’t permitted to discuss them in halls or elevators and weren’t supposed to know each other’s deals. Stock-research people couldn’t even browse in the library’s corporate-finance section.
摩根士丹利试图让并购专家们胆战心惊，并对他们的活动实施严密监控。在接受了法律与道德问题的培训后，年轻的专业人士必须签署声明，表明自己已理解公司规定。为了培养一种对利用内幕信息牟取私利的健康警觉，公司定期发布列举解雇理由的恐吓备忘录。石油分析师巴里·古德评论道：“我仿佛看到有人冲进我的办公室，撕掉我肩上的肩章，把我的计算器往膝盖上一磕，然后当场把我赶出公司。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423807) 每两周，安全人员会进行电子扫描，项目则以英国国王或希腊哲学家的名字作为代号。员工不得在走廊或电梯里讨论这些项目，也不应知晓彼此的交易内容。股票研究人员甚至不得在公司图书馆的企业融资区浏览资料。

These safeguards grew more important as more major deals churned through the Greenhill mill. The deals—and the fees—were growing astronomically. In a 1977 milestone, Morgan Stanley got a \$2.7-million fee for representing Babcock and Wilcox against a takeover by McDermott, advised by John A. Morgan (Harry Morgan’s bulbous-nosed, ruddy son, rejected by Morgan Stanley after the Charlie Morgan controversy) of Smith, Barney. Babcock demolished the myth that billion-dollar companies were immune to takeovers. Because its stock doubled during the bid—way above the usual 40-percent premium—it attracted a new breed of professional arbitrageurs. These speculators swept up the outstanding stock of takeover candidates, concentrating it in fewer hands, thus setting the stage for merger mania.
随着越来越多的大型交易在 Greenhill 的交易流水线上推进，这些防范措施也愈显重要。交易规模及其手续费都在急剧膨胀。1977 年是一个里程碑式的年份——摩根士丹利为代理巴布科克与威尔科克斯公司抵御麦克德莫特公司的收购，获得了 270 万美元的佣金。当时麦克德莫特公司的顾问是史密斯·巴尼公司的约翰·A·摩根（哈里·摩根那个酒糟鼻、面色红润的儿子，在查理·摩根风波后遭摩根士丹利拒之门外）。巴布科克公司打破了百亿美元级企业不会被收购的神话。由于在收购期间其股价翻了一倍——远高于通常 40%的溢价——吸引了一批新型专业套利者。这些投机者纷纷抢购潜在收购标的的流通股，将股权集中到少数人手中，从而为并购狂潮奠定了基础。

In the fall of 1977, Morgan Stanley became involved in an ethical tangle from which it never fully extricated itself. Like other big, Morgan-financed mining firms, Kennecott Copper wanted to diversify, and it turned to Greenhill as adviser. Among the prospects he scouted was a Louisiana forest-products concern, Olinkraft. While a friendly bid still seemed possible, Olinkraft provided Kennecott with confidential earnings estimates. Then Kennecott’s attention was distracted by a company named Carborundum, which it finally bought. Losing interest in Olinkraft, it returned the confidential data. Morgan Stanley apparently did not.
1977 年秋天，摩根士丹利卷入了一场让其日后一直难以彻底脱身的道德纠纷。就像其他由摩根提供融资的大型矿业公司一样，肯尼科特铜业也想搞多元化，于是找来了格林希尔当顾问。在他物色的目标中，有一家路易斯安那州的林产品公司——奥林克拉夫特。就在友好收购似乎仍有可能的时候，奥林克拉夫特向肯尼科特提供了机密的盈利预测。随后，肯尼科特的注意力被一家名为卡博伦丹的公司吸引，并最终将其收购。对奥林克拉夫特失去兴趣后，肯尼科特退还了那些机密数据。但摩根士丹利显然没有。

In early 1978, another Morgan-organized mining conglomerate, Johns-Manville, showed up for diversification advice and was assigned to Greenhill’s sidekick, Yerger Johnstone. When talk turned to Olinkraft, Morgan Stanley mentioned earlier talks with the company but didn’t divulge the valuable data. By late June, Johns-Manville had decided not to pursue Olinkraft. Two weeks later, Texas Eastern made a \$51-a-share offer for Olinkraft, which the latter’s board approved. Now, having seen confidential projections that Olinkraft would earn over \$8 a share by 1981, Morgan Stanley knew the company was selling out very cheap. So it shared the data with Johns-Manville, which reversed its decision, stepped straight into a bidding war with Texas Eastern, and won with a top bid of \$65 a share. As the dust settled, the question arose: had Morgan Stanley betrayed Olinkraft?
1978 年初，另一家由摩根组织的矿业集团 Johns-Manville 前来寻求多元化建议，公司被指派给了 Greenhill 的副手 Yerger Johnstone。当话题转向 Olinkraft 时，摩根士丹利提及了早前与这家公司的会谈，但并未透露那些有价值的数据。到了 6 月下旬，Johns-Manville 决定放弃收购 Olinkraft。两周后，Texas Eastern 向 Olinkraft 提出每股 51 美元的收购要约，Olinkraft 董事会批准了该方案。此时，摩根士丹利已经看到了内部预测数据——Olinkraft 到 1981 年每股收益将超过 8 美元，明知这家公司卖得太便宜。于是它把数据分享给了 Johns-Manville，后者推翻原有决定，直接与 Texas Eastern 展开了竞购战，最终以每股 65 美元的最高出价胜出。尘埃落定后，问题浮出水面：摩根士丹利是否背叛了 Olinkraft？

According to its later defense, Morgan Stanley consulted Davis, Polk, and Wardwell and Joe Flom’s law firm of Skadden, Arps before making a move. Both approved disclosing data to Johns-Manville provided the confidential estimates appeared in an SEC filing connected with the bid. This was duly done. Yet when published in September 1978, the filing caused shock, since Morgan Stanley hadn’t received Olinkraft’s permission to share such internal information. It seemed that client-banker trust—the bedrock of merchant banking for a century—was being violated in an opportunistic way. When the *Wall Street Journal* broke the story on October 26, it saw the flap as betokening larger problems: “No one is accusing Morgan Stanley of any wrongdoing, but some close observers of the firm, including some clients, lately have grown uneasy about what they see as mounting aggressiveness at Morgan Stanley as it scrambles for sizable takeover-bid advisory fees.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2423950)
根据其后来的辩护，摩根士丹利在行动前曾咨询了 Davis, Polk & Wardwell 以及 Joe Flom 所在的 Skadden, Arps 律所。两家律所都同意，只要这些保密估算出现在与收购相关的 SEC 文件中，就可以向 Johns-Manville 披露数据。这一步骤确实照做了。然而，1978 年 9 月该文件公布时，却引发了轩然大波，因为摩根士丹利并未获得 Olinkraft 的许可就分享了这些内部信息。客户与银行家之间的信任——百年来商人银行的基石——似乎正在被机会主义地践踏。当《华尔街日报》在 10 月 26 日率先报道此事时，认为这场风波预示着更严重的问题：“没有人指责摩根士丹利有任何不当行为，但一些密切关注该公司的观察人士，包括部分客户，最近对其在争夺巨额收购咨询费时愈发咄咄逼人的做法感到不安。”

At first, Morgan Stanley couldn’t produce a coherent defense. After its managing directors met for several hours, a spokesman said lamely, “I’m afraid we’ve decided we can’t comment.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424102) While some Morgan people reacted angrily toward the press, others, troubled by Greenhill’s bravado, welcomed what they saw as a salutary rebuke. Petito and Baldwin published a nine-paragraph defense in the *Wall Street Journal*, which asserted that the firm had “acted with the highest standard of professional responsibility” in showing the Olinkraft data to Johns-Manville.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424228) They pointed out that Morgan Stanley’s action had benefited Olinkraft shareholders, who reaped a 25-percent premium over the Texas Eastern bid. True enough. But was such bidding fair to Texas Eastern? Greenhill argued that the withholding of vital information from Johns-Manville might have posed questions, too. “If someone tried to stir up trouble, he might come in and say, ‘Hey, these guys are trying to buy a company with undisclosed, secret information.’ ”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424380) This was a valid point—and a perfectly good argument for bowing out of the deal altogether.
起初，摩根士丹利无法给出连贯的辩护。几位董事总经理开会数小时后，一位发言人有气无力地说道：“恐怕我们决定不予置评。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424102) 部分摩根士丹利员工对媒体反应激烈，另一些则被格林希尔的豪言壮语所困扰，反而对这种他们认为有益的批评表示欢迎。皮蒂托和鲍德温在《华尔街日报》上发表了九段辩护文章，声称公司在向约翰斯-曼维尔披露奥林克拉夫特的数据时，“秉持了最高标准的职业责任感”。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424228) 他们指出，摩根士丹利的做法让奥林克拉夫特的股东受益——他们最终获得的价格比德克萨斯东部的出价高出 25%。这倒是不假。但这样的竞标对德克萨斯东部公平吗？格林希尔认为，向约翰斯-曼维尔隐瞒关键信息同样可能引发问题。 "如果有人想找麻烦，他可能会跑进来嚷嚷：‘嘿，这些人正试图用未披露的秘密信息收购一家公司。’” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424380) 这个观点站得住脚——也是彻底退出这笔交易的绝佳理由。

Morgan Stanley’s attempts at explanation only worsened matters. Speaking to *Institutional Investor*, Greenhill and Dick Fisher said the firm had neither a verbal nor a written agreement with Olinkraft that enforced confidentiality. For the House of Morgan—the historic custodian of the “my word is my bond” approach to business—this defense seemed a betrayal of the Morgan tradition. As *Institutional Investor* said, “Morgan Stanley appeared to be enunciating a new investment banking doctrine: that any information a corporation provides to an investment banker will not necessarily be kept in complete and lasting confidence unless that corporation obtains either a written or oral promise from the investment banker to keep the information confidential.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424528)
摩根士丹利的解释反而让情况变得更糟。在接受《机构投资者》采访时，格林希尔和迪克·费舍尔表示，该公司与奥林克拉夫特之间既没有口头也没有书面协议来强制保密。对于历来秉持“言出必行”商业理念的摩根家族而言，这样的辩解似乎是对摩根传统的背叛。正如《机构投资者》所言：“摩根士丹利似乎在宣告一种新的投资银行准则：企业向投资银行家提供的任何信息，除非企业获得该银行家书面或口头的保密承诺，否则不一定会被完全、永久地保密。”

There was more bad news. About two years before, Morgan Stanley had set up a “risk arbitrage” department to speculate in takeover targets. As would become clear during the insider trading scandals of the 1980s, such operations were incompatible with M&A work. How could one side of a firm execute takeovers while another side was betting on them? Again Morgan Stanley extolled its Chinese wall, insisting its arbitrageurs existed in a sealed universe apart from Greenhill’s group. Then, a second *Wall Street Journal* story disclosed that the Arbitrage Department had taken a 150,000-share position in Olinkraft in mid-July, soon after the original Olinkraft-Texas Eastern discussions were revealed. This \$7-million stake was unusually large. Only two months later did Johns-Manville learn that one wing of Morgan Stanley had a huge vested interest in seeing it pay top dollar for Olinkraft.
又有坏消息传来。大约两年前，摩根士丹利成立了一个“风险套利”部门，专门对并购目标进行投机。到了 20 世纪 80 年代的内幕交易丑闻中，这种操作与并购业务水火不容的本质便暴露无遗——一家公司一边操盘收购，另一边却押注收购，这怎么说得通？摩根士丹利再次大谈特谈其“防火墙”，坚称套利部门与格林希尔团队完全隔绝。然而，第二篇《华尔街日报》报道揭露：早在 7 月中旬，即奥林克拉夫特与得克萨斯东方初次洽谈的消息传出后不久，套利部门就已购入该股 15 万股。这笔价值 700 万美元的仓位异常庞大。而直到两个月后，约翰斯-曼维尔公司才得知，摩根士丹利的另一只臂膀早已在奥林克拉夫特交易中手握巨额既得利益，巴望着自己为收购付出天价。

Bob Baldwin refused to concede any lapse in the firm’s vaunted integrity: “If you ask any 50 investment bankers on Wall Street which firm has the highest standards of ethics, I can assure you that Morgan Stanley will be the firm that is most often mentioned.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424654) Elsewhere in Wall Street, the Olinkraft episode produced deep uneasiness. Morgan Stanley was the flagship of Wall Street and its troubles tarred everyone. “The Morgan Stanley situation is going to hurt all of us,” said a rival. “For years we have all been cloaked in the integrity Morgan Stanley has shown in the corporate world.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424780)
鲍勃·鲍德温拒绝承认公司引以为傲的诚信有任何闪失：“如果你在华尔街随便问 50 位投资银行家，哪家公司的道德标准最高，我可以向你保证，摩根士丹利一定是被提及最多的那家。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424654) 而在华尔街的其他地方，Olinkraft 事件引发了深深的不安。摩根士丹利是华尔街的旗舰，它的困境让所有人都蒙上了阴影。“摩根士丹利的情况会伤害我们所有人，”一位竞争对手表示，“多年来，我们一直沐浴在摩根士丹利在企业界展现的诚信光辉中。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424780)

Olinkraft showed that as Wall Street firms grew and diversified, there were myriad opportunities for cheating and cutting corners. For some ex-partners who had grimly watched the firm evolve over the previous ten years, Olinkraft confirmed their fears. Some had thought it a matter of time before “accidents” occurred. One former partner said:
Olinkraft 事件表明，随着华尔街投行的扩张与多元化，作弊和偷工减料的机会层出不穷。对于过去十年间忧心忡忡地目睹公司演变的几位前合伙人来说，Olinkraft 事件印证了他们的担忧。一些人曾认为，发生“意外”只是时间问题。一位前合伙人说：

> Morgan Stanley took on jobs that visibly represented conflicts of interest and sooner or later they got into trouble. Before, the attitude was that if you saw a conflict of interest, you said “no” right away. There was no idea that you had to go for the last nickel. And you never looked at an individual buck outside of its effect on that basic business of preserving client relationships. That’s what Morgan Stanley slipped away from for quite awhile. I always felt they lost their soul.
> 摩根士丹利承接了一些明显存在利益冲突的业务，最终惹上了麻烦。过去，一旦发现利益冲突，大家会立刻说“不”。那时根本不会想着去赚最后那一分钱，也不会只看眼下的那点蝇头小利而忽略维护客户关系这个基本业务。这正是摩根士丹利偏离正轨已久的地方。我一直觉得他们丢失了自己的灵魂。

By now, the merger business had acquired an irresistible momentum. In 1979, Morgan Stanley earned a stratospheric \$14.3-million fee for advising Belridge Oil on its sale to Shell Oil—then history’s largest takeover. Among the losing auction bidders were two furious Morgan Stanley clients—Mobil and Texaco. The irate Mobil gradually shifted business to Merrill Lynch, while Greenhill pretended to be blase: “We’ll always do our best for a client, and Belridge was the client.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424906) Unlike syndicate work, takeover business required antagonizing some clients to please others. It therefore eroded historic ties on Wall Street.
此时，并购业务已势不可挡。1979 年，摩根士丹利因建议贝尔里奇石油公司将其出售给壳牌石油公司，赚取了高达 1430 万美元的佣金——这也是当时史上最大的一笔收购案。而落败的竞拍者中，包括两位愤怒的摩根士丹利客户——美孚石油和德士古石油。恼羞成怒的美孚逐渐将业务转向美林，格林希尔则故作淡定：“我们永远会为客户全力以赴，而贝尔里奇就是那个客户。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2424906) 与承销业务不同，并购业务往往需要得罪一些客户来取悦另一些客户，因此也悄然瓦解了华尔街的传统纽带。

This was again revealed in August 1981, when Du Pont bought Conoco for \$7.8 billion. Advised by Morgan Stanley, Conoco turned to Du Pont as a white knight to ward off Seagram’s advances. Because Greenhill and Flom were already teamed up with Conoco, Du Pont—a House of Morgan mainstay from the World War I Export Department and the 1920 General Motors takeover—had to drop Morgan Stanley and turn to the surging First Boston team of Joe Perella and Bruce Wasserstein. The three-month battle netted Morgan Stanley \$15 million. Afterward, Morgan Stanley found itself sharing Du Pont under-writings with First Boston. The new banker ties developed through takeovers translated into less loyalty in underwriting as well.
这一情况在 1981 年 8 月再次得到印证：当时杜邦以 78 亿美元收购康菲石油。在摩根士丹利的建议下，康菲石油选择杜邦作为白衣骑士，以抵御西格拉姆的收购攻势。由于格林希尔和弗洛姆已与康菲石油结盟，杜邦——这个自一战出口部及 1920 年收购通用汽车以来便是摩根大通核心客户的企业——不得不放弃摩根士丹利，转而投向乔·佩雷拉和布鲁斯·瓦瑟斯坦领导的、势头正盛的第一波士顿团队。这场持续三个月的争夺战为摩根士丹利带来了 1500 万美元的收入。此后，摩根士丹利发现自己在杜邦的承销业务上不得不与第一波士顿分一杯羹。通过收购建立的新型银行关系，也意味着承销业务中的忠诚度随之降低。

In 1981, Morgan Stanley was destined to suffer an embarrassment greater than that precipitated by the Olinkraft takeover. The case would darkly foreshadow later Wall Street scandals. It started with the hiring of Adrian Antoniu, a Romanian refugee whose family settled in New York in the 1960s. The Antonius had no money and spoke no English; Adrian’s would be a classic success story: after his father died, he supported his mother, worked his way through NYU, and in 1972 graduated from the Harvard Business School. Hired as a Morgan Stanley associate that year, he worried about money. He fretted about his mother’s failing fabric business in Queens and was concerned about making payments on his student loan.
1981 年，摩根士丹利注定要遭遇比 Olinkraft 收购案更令人难堪的尴尬。这件事后来也成为华尔街丑闻的黑暗预兆。一切始于雇佣阿德里安·安东纽（Adrian Antoniu），他是一名罗马尼亚难民，其家人于 1960 年代定居纽约。安东纽一家身无分文，也不会说英语；阿德里安的故事堪称经典的成功范例：父亲去世后，他一边供养母亲，一边半工半读完成纽约大学的学业，并于 1972 年从哈佛商学院毕业。同年，他被摩根士丹利录用为助理，但他一直为钱发愁。他担心母亲在皇后区的布料生意每况愈下，也为学生贷款的还款问题而焦虑。

Bright and sociable, Antoniu was mesmerized by the new wealth around him and took up a trendy lifestyle, complete with BMW and Park Avenue apartment. He belonged to a tony club called Doubles, frequented smart restaurants, and hung out at the Hamptons. The more perceptive wondered what lay below the aura of sophistication. “He just looked too good, too well-pressed and too well-groomed,” said an acquaintance.[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425055) Starting in corporate finance, Antoniu was soon drawn into Greenhill’s growing merger operation, where a newcomer could quickly lay his hands on valuable information.
安东纽阳光开朗、善于交际，被周遭的新贵生活所迷惑，开始追求时髦的生活方式，开宝马、住派克大道的公寓。他加入了一家名为“双星”的高级俱乐部，常去高档餐厅，在汉普顿社交圈出入。那些目光更敏锐的人不禁怀疑他光鲜外表下藏着什么。“他看起来简直太完美了，西装笔挺，打扮得一丝不苟，”一位熟人说。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425055) 安东纽最初从事企业融资工作，很快就被格林希尔日益壮大的并购业务吸引，在那里，新人能迅速接触到敏感信息。

In 1973, Antoniu hatched a deal with a former N.Y.U. classmate, James Newman, who worked in a brokerage house. Antoniu would feed names of takeover candidates to Newman, who put up the money to buy the stocks; profits were to be shared equally. He cut similar deals with two other graduates from his business-school class. At first, the bets were touchingly modest. In the first of eighteen deals, Antoniu told Newman that Morgan Stanley was defending CertainTeed in a tender offer by Compagnie de Saint-Gobain-Pont-a-Mousson. Their CertainTeed purchases netted \$1,375. In a second deal—Newman had now moved to Miami and taken another brokerage job—Antoniu revealed that Ciba-Geigy, advised by Morgan Stanley, would soon launch a bid for Funk Seeds. Soon they were placing bigger bets. For instance, when Morgan Stanley helped North American Philips in its bid for Magnavox, Antoniu and Newman bought 17,600 shares of Magnavox. Starting to show real flair, the young men took to using offshore Bahama bank accounts.
1973 年，安东纽与前纽约大学同学詹姆斯·纽曼达成了一笔交易，纽曼当时在一家经纪公司工作。安东纽将收购目标公司的名单提供给纽曼，由纽曼出资购买股票，利润五五分成。他还与商学院同班的另外两名毕业生达成了类似的协议。起初，这些赌注小得令人动容。在 18 笔交易的第一笔中，安东纽告诉纽曼，摩根士丹利正代表 CertainTeed 公司，抵御圣戈班-穆松桥公司的收购要约。他们购买 CertainTeed 股票净赚了 1375 美元。第二笔交易时——此时纽曼已搬到迈阿密，在另一家经纪公司任职——安东纽透露，由摩根士丹利提供咨询的汽巴-嘉基公司很快将向 Funk Seeds 发起收购。很快，他们开始下更大的注。例如，当摩根士丹利协助北美飞利浦竞购 Magnavox 时，安东纽和纽曼买入了 17600 股 Magnavox 股票。 年轻人开始崭露头角，纷纷用起了巴哈马的离岸银行账户。

They grew strangely heedless of danger. Later on, they read a newspaper account of an insider trading case against three people at Sorg Printing who used inside information from tender-offer documents they were printing. “Look what happened to these people at Sorg,” said Antoniu, briefly dismayed. “Well, you see the worst that could happen in a case like this,” Newman replied. “They ask for your money back, and they give you a slap on the hand. People have to steal or kill to get this kind of money, but you don’t have to go to jail for it.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425208)
他们渐渐变得对危险毫不在意。后来，他们在报纸上读到一则内幕交易案，索格印刷公司（Sorg Printing）的三个人利用正在印刷的收购要约文件中的内幕信息牟利。“看看这些在索格的人下场如何，”安东尼乌说道，一时有些沮丧。“嗯，你看到了这类案件最坏的结果，”纽曼回应道，“他们不过是让你把钱吐出来，再轻轻拍一下你的手。别人要偷要抢才能弄到这些钱，而你根本不用为此坐牢。”

In early 1975, the conspiracy nearly ended when Antoniu was edged out of Morgan Stanley and hired for M&A work by Kuhn, Loeb, soon to merge with Lehman Brothers. Luckily, he found a new Morgan Stanley confederate in yet a fifth member of the Harvard Business School class of 1972. Unlike the free-and-easy Antoniu, the French-Canadian E. Jacques Courtois had an intense, tight-lipped expression. His father, a rich Montreal lawyer who headed a group that owned the Montreal Canadiens, sat on a bank board. Over chess at the Harvard Club, Antoniu drew Courtois into his scheme. Courtois promptly repaid his confidence with a tip—that Pan Ocean Oil, a Morgan Stanley client, was involved in merger talks with Marathon Oil. They made a quick killing of \$119,000. Between 1973 and 1978, they would earn \$800,000.
1975 年初，这场阴谋险些告终——安东纽被挤出摩根士丹利，转而受雇于库恩勒布公司从事并购业务，这家公司不久后便与雷曼兄弟合并。幸运的是，他在哈佛商学院 1972 届毕业生中找到了第五位同谋：法裔加拿大人雅克·库尔图瓦。与随性洒脱的安东纽不同，库尔图瓦表情严肃、寡言少语。他的父亲是蒙特利尔一位富有的律师，掌管着拥有蒙特利尔加拿大人队的集团，还在银行董事会任职。在哈佛俱乐部的棋局中，安东纽将库尔图瓦拉入自己的计划。库尔图瓦很快用一条内幕消息回报了他的信任——摩根士丹利的客户泛洋石油正与马拉松石油洽谈合并。他们迅速赚得 11.9 万美元。从 1973 年到 1978 年，两人共获利 80 万美元。

It took time before the authorities zeroed in on Antoniu. Meanwhile, he had fallen in love with Francesca Stanfill, daughter of Dennis Stanfill, the powerful chairman of Twentieth Century Fox. By the spring of 1978, when the government targeted him as a prime suspect, Antoniu was engaged to Francesca, who wrote about fashion for the Sunday magazine of the *New York Times*. He somehow neglected to tell Eric Gleacher, his boss at the M&A Department of Lehman Brothers Kuhn Loeb, that he was being investigated. Learning of this fact on the eve of Antoniu’s wedding, Gleacher saw double disaster: not only was Antoniu his employee but Twentieth Century Fox was a major Lehman client. He insisted to Antoniu, “If there is nothing to the charges and you want to have the Stanfills stand by you in defending against them, you really ought to tell them.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425367)
当局花了一段时间才将目标锁定在安东尼乌身上。而此时，他已与弗朗西斯卡·斯坦菲尔坠入爱河——她的父亲丹尼斯·斯坦菲尔正是权势显赫的二十世纪福克斯公司董事长。到 1978 年春天，政府将安东尼乌列为头号嫌疑人时，他已与弗朗西斯卡订婚，后者则在《纽约时报》周日杂志撰写时尚专栏。不知为何，他竟没有告诉自己在雷曼兄弟库恩洛布并购部的主管埃里克·格里彻自己正在被调查。格里彻在安东尼乌婚礼前夕得知此事，意识到这是双重灾难：安东尼乌不仅是他的下属，而二十世纪福克斯更是雷曼的重要客户。他坚持对安东尼乌说：“如果那些指控子虚乌有，而你又希望斯坦菲尔家族站在你这边为你辩护，你真的应该告诉他们。”

On June 28, 1978, Antoniu married Stanfill at a civil ceremony in Venice, neglecting to tell her family about the federal probe. Discovering this, Gleacher roared into the telephone from New York: “Unless you tell Mr. Stanfill before the church wedding, I will!”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425502) On July 1, the church wedding took place at the Basilica di San Pietro di Castello in Venice, with Albino Cardinal Luciani, about to become Pope John Paul I, bestowing his blessing on the couple in a written message. Adrian delivered a poetic toast: “Here’s to the longest run Twentieth Century Fox will ever have.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425628) As guests waved good-bye, the newlyweds drifted off in a white gondola. Back in New York, Gleacher cleared out Antoniu’s desk. Within a month, the wedding was annulled, presumably because the Stanfills learned of the investigation.
1978 年 6 月 28 日，安东尼乌与斯坦菲尔在威尼斯举行了一场民事婚礼，但她没有告知家人联邦调查一事。格利彻发现后，从纽约打来电话咆哮道：“除非你在教堂婚礼前告诉斯坦菲尔先生，否则我来说！” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425502) 7 月 1 日，教堂婚礼在威尼斯的圣彼得堡大教堂举行，即将成为教皇约翰·保罗一世的阿尔比诺·卢恰尼枢机主教以书面形式为这对新人赐福。阿德里安献上了一段诗意的祝酒词：“敬 20 世纪福克斯有史以来最长的合作。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425628) 宾客挥手告别时，这对新人乘坐白色贡多拉缓缓漂离。回到纽约后，格利彻清理了安东尼乌的办公桌。不到一个月，这段婚姻就被宣告无效，想必是因为斯坦菲尔一家得知了调查内情。

E. Jacques Courtois’s voluntary departure from Morgan Stanley in 1979 caused great anguish. “Morgan Stanley was rocked at the time,” said a colleague. “They had lost 3 people, including Jacques, in something like 3 weeks. They had a series of meetings to make sure they were hanging on to the rest of us.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425754) Courtois said he might go into computer software or manage his investments. Marrying the niece of Colombia’s president, he moved to Bogota. Courtois was fingered by government investigators because he alone in the M&LA Department *hadn’t* worked on the takeovers in question. This raised questions about Morgan Stanley’s claim that their people never discussed takeovers with others.

Jacques Courtois 在 1979 年从摩根士丹利自愿离职，引发了极大的痛苦。“当时摩根士丹利受到了震动，”一位同事说。“他们在大约三周内失去了三个人，包括 Jacques。他们召开了一系列会议，以确保能留住我们其他人。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425754) Courtois 表示他可能会进入计算机软件行业或管理自己的投资。他娶了哥伦比亚总统的侄女，随后搬到了波哥大。政府调查人员之所以盯上 Courtois，是因为在并购部门中，只有他没有参与被调查的收购案。这引发了人们对摩根士丹利声称其员工从未与他人讨论收购案的质疑。

The criminal indictments handed down in February 1981 were the first such ever brought against investment bankers. Newman got a one-year prison sentence, while Antoniu’s plea bargaining got him a suspended sentence. Antoniu said, “Anyone familiar with the securities markets knows these circumstances are not uncommon.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425907) Courtois spent a year in prison and paid \$150,000 in fines.
1981 年 2 月下达的刑事起诉书是首次针对投资银行家提起的此类指控。纽曼被判处一年监禁，而安东尼乌通过认罪协商获得了缓刑。安东尼乌表示：“任何熟悉证券市场的人都知道，这种情况并不罕见。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2425907) 库尔图瓦在监狱中服刑一年，并支付了 15 万美元罚款。

Morgan Stanley cooperated with the government and contacted clients to reaffirm its integrity. Lewis Bernard was chosen to inform the firm’s managing directors. He recalled, “People in that room cried. They cried out of anger. We have the feeling of being violated.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426066) Although the overwhelming majority of inside tips came from Morgan Stanley, Bob Baldwin complained that Lehman Brothers received less publicity: “What do the headlines say? Morgan. We make the headlines in these darned situations . . . we had people practically crying around here, they work so hard to do a first-class job in a first-class way.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426219)
摩根士丹利与政府合作，并联系客户以重申其诚信。刘易斯·伯纳德被选去通知公司董事总经理们。他回忆道：“房间里的人都哭了。他们是愤怒而哭。我们有种被侵犯的感觉。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426066) 尽管绝大多数内幕消息都来自摩根士丹利，但鲍勃·鲍德温抱怨雷曼兄弟受到的关注较少：“头条新闻写了什么？摩根。我们在这些该死的局面中上了头条……这里的人几乎都在哭，他们那么努力地想以一流的方式做出一流的工作。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2426219)

Public reaction to this insider trading ring distinctly echoed that to the 1933 preferred-list scandal and the Richard Whitney affair. People unconnected to Morgan Stanley felt as if a public trust had been violated. “I’ve always thought of Morgan Stanley as the *creme de la creme*,” said Benedict T. Haber, dean of Fordham’s Graduate School of Business. “It’s like an icon has been knocked down.”
公众对这起内幕交易圈的反应，与对 1933 年优先名单丑闻和 Richard Whitney 事件的反应如出一辙。那些与摩根士丹利毫无关联的人，感觉仿佛有一项公共信托遭到了践踏。“我一直以为摩根士丹利是精英中的精英，”福德汉姆大学商学院院长 Benedict T. Haber 说道，“就像一座偶像被推倒了。”
