# Chapter Ten: War

EVEN as domestic troubles crowded in upon the House of Morgan, the bank was on the eve of its most spectacular foreign triumph, one that would make Pierpont Morgan look provincial in comparison. During the early summer of 1914, an industrial recession was accompanied by a bear market on Wall Street. Businessmen grumbled that Woodrow Wilson’s crusade against the “interests” had chilled the entrepreneurial spirit. In this gloomy frame of mind, American investors panicked when they learned of Austria-Hungary’s declaration of war against Serbia on July 28, 1914. Wall Street, which prided itself on its prescience, was once again caught napping by a historic event.
就在国内问题纷至沓来、困扰摩根银行之际，这家银行却即将迎来其最辉煌的海外胜利——相较之下，皮尔庞特·摩根的格局反倒显得有些局限了。1914 年初夏，工业衰退与华尔街的熊市同步到来。商人们抱怨伍德罗·威尔逊对“利益集团”的讨伐浇灭了创业精神。在这种悲观情绪中，当美国投资者于 1914 年 7 月 28 日得知奥匈帝国向塞尔维亚宣战的消息时，顿时陷入恐慌。一向以先见之明自居的华尔街，再次被历史事件打了个措手不及。

The House of Morgan had closely followed European events. Although later accused of World War I profiteering, it nearly engaged in clandestine diplomacy to stop fighting between the Balkan states and Turkey in 1912. The plan was to have the House of Morgan provide loans to both sides on condition that they submit to American mediation, and President Taft was to have acted as mediator. The scheme was apparently hatched by Herman Harjes, senior partner of Morgan, Harjes in Paris, and U.S. ambassador to France Myron Herrick. Jack Morgan finally vetoed the idea, fearing that the loan money would be used to further the war effort, which the House of Morgan wished to stop.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259055) He also refused to proceed without the full cooperation of the European powers.
摩根财团密切追踪着欧洲局势。尽管后来被指责在一战中大发战争财，但在 1912 年，它几乎暗中斡旋，试图阻止巴尔干国家与土耳其之间的战事。当时的方案是：摩根财团向交战双方提供贷款，条件是双方必须接受美国的调停，而时任总统塔夫脱将出任调停人。这项计划显然由摩根巴黎分行的高级合伙人赫尔曼·哈杰斯和美国驻法大使迈伦·赫里克共同策划。最终，杰克·摩根否决了这一提议，他担心贷款资金反而会被用于推动战事——而这正是摩根财团希望遏制的。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259055) 此外，在没有欧洲列强全面配合的情况下，他也拒绝推进此事。

The hysteria that seized Wall Street in late July 1914 stemmed from a misguided fear that transatlantic trade would collapse and worsen the recession. Americans thought they couldn’t survive without European capital and feared that gold would be withdrawn from New York and hoarded in London. After the czar mobilized over a million Russian troops on July 29, all the European markets shut down. As overseas investors rushed to liquidate securities through New York, the Stock Exchange took its steepest one-day dive since the 1907 panic.
1914 年 7 月末席卷华尔街的恐慌源于一种误解，人们担心跨大西洋贸易会崩溃，加剧经济衰退。美国人认为没有欧洲资本就无法生存，还担心黄金会从纽约流出并被囤积在伦敦。7 月 29 日，沙皇调动了超过一百万俄罗斯军队后，欧洲所有市场都关闭了。随着海外投资者争相通过纽约清算证券，证券交易所遭遇了自 1907 年恐慌以来最严重的单日暴跌。

By the morning of July 31, 1914, a staggering accumulation of overnight sell orders threatened a thunderous crash. Even though Pierpont Morgan was now dead, his star pupil, Harry Davison, had been well tutored in the 1907 panic. Bankers still instinctively resorted to 23 Wall Street in an emergency. The House of Morgan was more than a man; it had acquired an institutional continuity. Davison summoned Wall Street’s bankers to the old Mills Building at 15 Broad Street, the provisional Morgan home while the new headquarters was being readied. Before the start of trading, the Stock Exchange president rushed over for consultation.
1914 年 7 月 31 日清晨，一夜之间堆积如山的卖单预示着股市即将迎来一场惊天崩盘。尽管皮尔庞特·摩根（Pierpont Morgan）已然离世，但他的得意门生哈里·戴维森（Harry Davison）在 1907 年那场恐慌中早已学到了精髓。银行家们遇到紧急情况时，仍会本能地涌向华尔街 23 号。摩根银行早已超越个人的存在，拥有了机构延续性。戴维森召集华尔街的银行家们来到布罗德街 15 号的老米尔斯大楼——这是新总部还在筹备期间，摩根银行的临时办公地。开盘前，证券交易所主席匆忙赶来商议。

Even though Jack was there, Davison presided. Also present was a new Morgan banker, Dwight W. Morrow, a distinguished tax and utility lawyer. Morrow recalled the frantic discussion: “The Stock Exchange authorities wanted to know whether to open or not, and nobody knew what to tell them. It got down to about five minutes of ten, and the President . . . called up the Exchange and told them to announce that the Exchange would be closed.” It was a hairbreadth reprieve: the man who rang the opening gong had already assumed his post, and traders shrugged with relief. “It was in my very early days in a banking firm,” Morrow added, “and I can remember that I was impressed with how little anybody knew what he was doing.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259245) Curiously, Morgan accounts of this meeting omit a 9:30 A.M. phone call that Jack made to Treasury Secretary William G. McAdoo, who advised him, “If you really want my judgment, it is to close the Exchange.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259406)
尽管杰克在场，但主持大局的是戴维森。另一位在场的是摩根银行的新任合伙人德怀特·W·莫罗，他是一位出色的税务和公用事业律师。莫罗回忆起当时慌乱的讨论：“证券交易所当局想知道到底该不该开盘，可没人知道该怎么答复他们。一直拖到差五分钟十点，总统……给交易所打电话，让他们宣布休市。”这简直是千钧一发的喘息之机——负责敲开盘钟的人已经就位，交易员们如释重负地耸耸肩。莫罗补充道：“那时我刚进银行不久，至今还记得，当时大家对自己的所作所为都心里没底，这给我留下了深刻印象。”奇怪的是，摩根的会议记录中却遗漏了杰克在上午九点半打给财政部长威廉·G·麦卡杜的那通电话，麦卡杜在电话里建议他：“如果你真要问我的意见，那就是关闭交易所。”

The New York Stock Exchange didn’t resume restricted trading until December, and normal trading didn’t return until the following spring. A curious fugitive institution sprang up—the so-called gutter market of outlaw brokers, who loitered on the curbs trading stocks. According to Wall Street lore, it started out with “four boys and a dog,” but soon a hundred brokerage firms jumped into sidewalk trading on New Street—to the point where the Stock Exchange clamped down. As Alexander Dana Noyes noted, this ragtag band was probably “at the time the only actual stock market in the world.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259565)
纽约证券交易所直到 12 月才恢复限制性交易，而正常交易则要等到次年春天。一个奇特的"地下市场"应运而生——那些被吊销执照的经纪人聚集在路边进行股票交易，被称为"路边市场"。据华尔街传说，这个市场最初只有"四个小伙子加一条狗"，但很快就有上百家经纪公司涌入新街的人行道进行交易，以至于证券交易所不得不采取打压措施。正如亚历山大·达纳·诺伊斯所言，这群乌合之众在当时很可能是"世界上唯一真正活跃的股票市场"。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259565)

The war was initially a bleak time for the House of Morgan. Like other banks, it made a great deal of money from broker call loans—loans made to buy stock on margin—and so started the war in low spirits. This despondent mood obscured a momentous shift in world finance: the United States was about to capture financial supremacy from England and emerge as the leading creditor nation. Although nobody quite realized it at first, the English era was over. After the war, world currency markets would shift from a sterling to a dollar standard.
战争初期对摩根家族而言是一段黯淡时光。与其他银行一样，他们通过经纪人通知贷款（用于保证金交易股票的贷款）赚取了大量资金，因此开战时情绪低落。这种沮丧情绪掩盖了世界金融格局的一次重大转变：美国即将从英国手中夺取金融霸权，成为领先的债权国。尽管起初几乎无人察觉，但英国的时代已经终结。战后，世界货币市场将从英镑本位转向美元本位。

The news of war was greeted with melodramatic foreboding by Jack Morgan, who foresaw “the most appalling destruction of values in securities which has ever been seen in this country.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259722) Later reviled as a “merchant of death” by isolationists, his first reaction, in fact, was spotlessly humane. On July 31, he even issued a rare public appeal for peace: “If the delicate situation can be held in abeyance for a few weeks, I should expect a rising tide of protest from the people who are to pay for war with their blood and their property.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259910) Far from rubbing his hands at the prospect of war profits, he scoffed at the notion that New York might supplant London as the world’s financial center.
战争消息传来时，杰克·摩根（Jack Morgan）带着戏剧性的预感说道，他预见到“这个国家将出现有史以来最骇人的证券价值崩盘。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259722) 后来他被孤立主义者唾骂为“死亡商人”，但事实上他的第一反应完全是出于人道。7 月 31 日，他甚至罕见地公开发出和平呼吁：“如果这一微妙局势能暂缓几周，我预计，那些将用鲜血和财产为战争买单的人民，会掀起一波日益高涨的抗议浪潮。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2259910) 他非但没有对战争利润摩拳擦掌，反而对纽约可能取代伦敦成为世界金融中心的说法嗤之以鼻。

The partner with the best antennae for the seismic shift was Harry Davison. The war would be his glory time. Almost at once, he sensed a Morgan bonanza and immediately dispatched telegrams to Lamont, then trout fishing and horseback riding on a Montana ranch. These telegrams throb with excitement:
对这场剧变嗅觉最敏锐的伙伴是哈里·戴维森。这场战争将成为他的辉煌时代。他几乎立刻嗅到了摩根公司大发横财的机会，随即向正在蒙大拿牧场钓鳟鱼、骑马的拉蒙特发去了电报。这些电报中透着一股兴奋劲儿：

> THE CREDIT OF ALL EUROPE HAS BROKEN DOWN ABSOLUTELY SPECIE PAYMENTS SUSPENDED AND MORATORIUM IN FORCE IN FRANCE AND PRACTICALLY IN ALL COUNTRIES THOUGH NOT OFFICIALLY IN ENGLAND. . . .
> 全欧信用已然崩溃，法国及几乎所有国家（虽未正式波及英国）均暂停了硬币支付并实施延期偿付……

> PROBABLY COULD DO LITTLE IF YOU WERE HERE THE ONLY POINT BEING THAT IS FILLED WITH EXTRA ORDINARY INTEREST AND OF COURSE GREAT POSSIBILITYS. . . . PERHAPS I MIGHT EXPRESS THE SITUATION BY STATING THAT IT IS AS IF WE HAD HAD AN EARTHQUAKE ARE AS YET SOMEWHAT STUNNED BUT WILL SOON GET TO RIGHTING THINGS.[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260125)
> 若你在此，恐怕也无能为力，唯一可说的是，这情况充满了异乎寻常的趣味，当然也蕴含着巨大的可能性……或许我可以这样描述现状：仿佛刚经历过一场地震，我们还在恍惚中，但很快就会着手把一切扶正。 [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260125)

An immediate war casualty was that chronic Morgan stepchild, the city of New York, which had about \$80 million in European obligations coming due. As the dollar plunged—making repayment more expensive—and the United States faced a possible standstill in transatlantic trade, sentiment was strong for suspending payment on the debt. Why not exploit the European chaos to save some money? Forming a syndicate to pay off the bonds, the House of Morgan and Kuhn, Loeb organized an impromptu rescue. Gold was shipped to the Bank of England and then credited to Morgan Grenfell, which paid off New York City notes as they matured. The operation was a mark of financial maturity, a signal to the world that New York as a financial center could offer safety comparable to that of London.
战争首当其冲的受害者是摩根长期以来的“累赘”——纽约市，其当时约有 8000 万美元的欧洲债务即将到期。随着美元暴跌（使得偿债成本更高）以及美国面临跨大西洋贸易可能停滞的局面，暂停偿还债务的呼声高涨。何不利用欧洲的混乱省下一笔钱呢？摩根银行与库恩-洛布公司组成财团，临时发起救援行动，买下了这些债券。黄金被运往英格兰银行，随后记入摩根建富的账户，用于在纽约市票据到期时偿付。这一操作标志着金融的成熟，向世界表明：纽约作为金融中心，能够提供与伦敦相媲美的安全性。

For many Americans, the war was at first a distant irrelevance; for isolationists, it provided yet another example of why America should steer clear of foreign imbroglios. Despite his sympathy for the Allies, President Wilson issued a proclamation of neutrality, entreating Americans to be “impartial in thought as well as action.” For Morgan partners, this was impossible. As Tom Lamont said, “we wanted the Allies to win, from the outset of the war. We were pro-Ally by inheritance, by instinct, by opinion.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260319) As cosmopolitan bankers with London and Paris affiliates, the Morgan partners were deeply enmeshed in European life and had too abiding a faith in Anglo-Saxon civilization to stand on the sidelines. Yet it was also a cardinal rule of the Diplomatic Age not to defy government edicts, and the bank abided by Washington’s policy.
对许多美国人来说，这场战争起初不过是遥远的局外事；而在孤立主义者看来，它恰恰印证了美国为何要远离国际纷争。尽管威尔逊总统同情协约国，仍发表了中立宣言，恳请美国人"在思想和行动上保持公正"。但对摩根合伙人而言，这绝无可能。正如汤姆·拉蒙特所言："从战争一开始，我们就希望协约国获胜。我们生来、本能、骨子里都支持协约国。"作为与伦敦、巴黎分支机构紧密相连的跨国银行家，摩根合伙人深深融入欧洲生活，对盎格鲁-撒克逊文明抱有坚定信念，绝不可能袖手旁观。然而，外交时代的一条铁律便是不得违抗政府法令，这家银行始终恪守华盛顿的政策。

In early August, the French, who had appointed J. P. Morgan and Company as their financial agent, sounded out the bank on a possible \$100-million loan. The Wilson administration did more than deny this request. The secretary of state, William Jennings Bryan—the toad in the garden of Morgan history—denounced loans to belligerents as “the worst of contrabands.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260470) A few days later, he told the press that loans by American bankers to warring nations were “inconsistent with the true spirit of neutrality.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260630)
8 月初，指定 J.P.摩根公司为财务代理人的法国人，试探性地向该银行提出一笔 1 亿美元的贷款意向。威尔逊政府不仅拒绝了这一请求，还采取了更强硬的姿态——国务卿威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩，这位摩根家族发家史中如鲠在喉的人物，公开谴责向交战国提供贷款是“最恶劣的违禁品”。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260470) 数日后，他又向媒体宣告，美国银行家向交战国放贷的行为“与真正的中立精神背道而驰”。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260630)

Within six weeks, Bryan’s policy on contraband financing was reversed as Wilson tilted—subtly but unmistakably—toward the Allies. Robert Lansing, the State Department counselor and acting secretary of state that fall, figured out a way to sidestep U.S. neutrality through legal legerdemain. He persuaded Wilson to adopt a serviceable distinction between forbidden “loans” made through foreign war bonds and permissible “credits” for Allied purchases of materiel. Why the sudden shift after only two months of war? American exports to Europe had lifted the United States from recession, and even parochial farmers worried that Allied purchases of grain, meat, and cotton might be curtailed for lack of credit. As Davison told Treasury Secretary McAdoo, “to maintain our prosperity we must finance it.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260780) The House of Morgan offered a convenient cover for preserving the appearance, while denying the spirit, of neutrality.
六周之内，布赖恩的违禁品融资政策便被推翻，因为威尔逊——微妙却明确地——开始偏向协约国。时任国务院顾问、当年秋季代理国务卿的罗伯特·兰辛想出一招，通过法律上的巧妙手段绕开美国的中立立场。他说服威尔逊接受一种看似合理的区分：禁止通过外国战争债券提供"贷款"，但允许协约国以"信贷"方式购买物资。为何开战仅两个月就出现如此急转？美国对欧出口已将美国从衰退中拉出，就连目光短浅的农民也担心，协约国因信贷不足而削减谷物、肉类和棉花的采购。正如戴维森对财政部长麦卡杜所言："要维持我们的繁荣，就必须为此提供资金。" [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260780) 摩根财团提供了便利的掩护，既能保留中立的表象，却又违背了中立的实质。

With much industrial slack, the United States was an ideal arsenal for the war. But as the Allies bid against each other for American supplies, they drove prices sky-high; even separate departments of the British government ended up in bitter competition. To relieve such price pressure, Lloyd George, then the chancellor of the Exchequer, asked Teddy Grenfell if Morgans in New York could do anything about expanding American rifle production, and Jack Morgan made inquiries at the Remington and Winchester arms companies. But more than expanded production was needed to stop war profiteering. In October 1914, the British Treasury sent over Sir George Paish and Basil Blackett to look into the problem. The most mandarin of Whitehall bureaucracies, the British Treasury needed a Wall Street outpost and found it in their New York agent, the House of Morgan. When the Treasury men returned to London in late November, they had another passenger stowed away on board, Harry Davison. Because Willard Straight was restless, Davison took him and Dorothy along. The Straights’ new magazine, *The New Republic*, was already running a letter from Ray Stannard Baker warning American business not to exploit the war “to promote its own business and trade.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260940)
由于国内工业产能充足，美国成为了这场战争的理想兵工厂。然而，协约国之间为争夺美国物资相互抬价，导致价格飙升；甚至连英国政府内部的不同部门也陷入了激烈竞争。为缓解这种价格压力，时任英国财政大臣的劳合·乔治询问特迪·格伦费尔，纽约的摩根公司能否扩大美国步枪产量，随后杰克·摩根便向雷明顿和温彻斯特两家武器公司进行了咨询。但要遏制战争牟利行为，光靠扩大生产远远不够。1914 年 10 月，英国财政部派遣乔治·佩什爵士和巴兹尔·布莱克特前往美国调查此事。作为白厅中最为官僚的机构，英国财政部需要在华尔街设立一个前哨站，最终选定了其在纽约的代理人——摩根财团。当这两位财政部官员于 11 月底返回伦敦时，船上还藏着另一位乘客——哈里·戴维森。 由于威拉德·斯特雷特坐不住，戴维森便带上了他和多萝西。斯特雷特夫妇的新杂志《新共和》已经刊登了雷·斯坦纳德·贝克的一封信，信中警告美国企业不要利用战争“来促进自己的业务和贸易。”” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2260940)

Davison had come up with an inspired idea, which Straight claimed was stolen from him. Davison wondered whether the House of Morgan could get rid of plundering middlemen by concentrating Allied purchases in a single agency that would negotiate from a position of strength. He knew the preferred Morgan style was never to grandstand and suggested that Jack Morgan take the boat with the Treasury men. Never one to steal glory, Jack replied, “You jump on the steamer yourself, this is your idea.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261120) Jack’s friend Sir Cecil Arthur Spring-Rice, the British ambassador in Washington, had lobbied for a similar idea, telling the Foreign Office that it would require a firm of stature in both London and New York. The Anglo-American House of Morgan was the logical choice.
戴维森想到了一个绝妙的主意，斯特雷特声称这主意是从他那里偷来的。戴维森在考虑，摩根财团能否通过将协约国的采购集中到一个能凭借强势地位谈判的单一机构，从而摆脱掠夺性的中间商。他知道摩根财团偏爱的方式是绝不出风头，于是建议杰克·摩根与财政部官员一起乘船。杰克从不贪功，他回答说：“你自己跳上船吧，这是你的主意。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261120) 杰克的朋友塞西尔·亚瑟·斯普林-赖斯爵士，即英国驻华盛顿大使，也曾游说推行类似的想法，他向英国外交部表示，这需要一家在伦敦和纽约都颇具实力的大公司。英美两地的摩根财团自然是合乎逻辑的选择。

Once Davison was installed at Claridge’s, Teddy Grenfell led him on a tour of Bank of England and Whitehall officials. British officials liked the Davison plan, and not only because it would lower prices. Politically, it would convert the House of Morgan into a lightning rod for the inevitable charges of favoritism that go with wartime contracts. The firm’s liabilities were also apparent. Some officials feared that British radicals would have a field day with this Wall Street link, and others worried about the bank’s unpopularity among certain sectors of American society. The House of Morgan knew its own unpopularity west of the Mississippi. In April 1914, it had considered setting up a rare branch in Chicago to soften midwestern sentiment against it.
戴维森入住克拉里奇酒店后，泰迪·格伦菲尔带着他拜访了英格兰银行和英国政府的高层官员。英国官员们对戴维森的计划颇为青睐，这不仅是因为它能压低价格。在政治层面，这个计划能让摩根财团成为战时合同不可避免的偏袒指控的"避雷针"。当然，摩根的短板同样显而易见。部分官员担心，英国激进派会将这个华尔街关系网大肆利用；另一些人则忧虑，这家银行在美国社会某些群体中并不受欢迎。摩根财团自己也清楚，它在密西西比河以西地区口碑欠佳。早在 1914 年 4 月，摩根就曾考虑在芝加哥设立一个罕见的分支机构，以缓和美国中西部民众对其的抵触情绪。

On December 16, 1914, Davison lunched with the prime minister, Herbert H. Asquith, and the chancellor, David Lloyd George. He brought along a contract for a proposed Morgan purchasing agency for the Allies. The prime minister reviewed it paragraph by paragraph and said he “approved every word.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261281) On January 15, 1915, the House of Morgan signed the Commercial Agreement with the Army Council and the Admiralty. The first purchase was \$12 million for horses—then an urgently needed item. In the spring, a similar arrangement was concluded with the French through the senior Morgan partner in Paris, Herman Harjes.
1914 年 12 月 16 日，戴维森与首相赫伯特·H·阿斯奎斯及财政大臣大卫·劳合·乔治共进午餐。他随身携带了一份拟议中的摩根公司为协约国担任采购代理的合同。首相逐段审阅后，表示“每个字都批准”。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261281) 1915 年 1 月 15 日，摩根公司与陆军委员会及海军部正式签署了商业协议。首笔采购额为 1200 万美元，用于购买当时急需的马匹。同年春天，通过摩根在巴黎的高级合伙人赫尔曼·哈耶斯，与法国方面也达成了类似安排。

Nobody foresaw the magnitude of the proposed operation. Lord Kitchener, secretary of war, told Davison the purchases might amount to £10 million—and he stressed that he was guessing on the high side. In fact, the purchases came to an astronomical \$3 billion—almost half of all American supplies sold to the Allies during the war. Skimming off a 1-percent commission, the House of Morgan booked an astounding \$30 million in fees. It was probably the most important deal in its history, not only for the money but for the political and corporate contacts it produced. Jack Morgan had qualms about the bank going into such alien business but feared a political backlash against the United States in Britain if war profiteering continued. At the White House in late January 1915, Jack got Woodrow Wilson’s blessing, who said he wouldn’t interfere with any action in “furtherance of trade.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261432)
无人预见到拟议行动的庞大规模。陆军大臣基奇纳勋爵告诉戴维森，采购金额可能达到 1000 万英镑——他还强调自己已经往高了猜。实际上，采购总额高达惊人的 30 亿美元——几乎占战争期间美国向协约国出售物资的一半。摩根财团抽取 1%的佣金，入账了令人咋舌的 3000 万美元手续费。这很可能是其史上最重要的一笔交易，不仅因为金钱，更因为它所催生的政治与企业人脉。杰克·摩根对银行涉足如此陌生的业务心存疑虑，但担心如果战争暴利持续下去，英国会对美国产生政治反弹。1915 年 1 月下旬，杰克在白宫得到了伍德罗·威尔逊的认可，威尔逊表示不会干涉任何"促进贸易"的行动。

The old private banks of Wall Street and the City had a chameleon quality and could quickly adapt to opportunities. To head what became the Export Department, Tom Lamont recruited Edward R. Stettinius, Sr., president of the Diamond Match Company. A former speculator in the Chicago wheat pits, Stettinius had well-brushed silver hair, a mustache, and rimless spectacles. His neat exterior reflected a meticulous, almost obsessive, attention to detail. Later, Secretary of War Newton Baker would refer to his “almost terrifying sense of responsibility.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261566) From 9:00 A.M. until midnight daily, he lashed a Morgan staff of 175 known as SOS—Slaves of Stettinius. He didn’t simply hire people: he conscripted them, squeezed them, drove them to exhaustion. One drone later said, “If any fellow quit at 9 o’clock at night he was usually congratulated by the others on being about to take a half-holiday.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261727)
华尔街和伦敦城的那些老牌私营银行有着变色龙般的特性，能迅速适应各种机遇。为了领导后来成立的出口部，汤姆·拉蒙特招募了钻石火柴公司总裁老爱德华·R·斯特蒂纽斯。这位曾在芝加哥小麦期货市场投机的人物，梳着整齐的银发，留着胡须，戴着无边眼镜。他整洁的外表反映了他对细节近乎偏执的苛求。后来，战争部长牛顿·贝克曾提及他那“近乎可怕的的责任感”。从早上 9 点到午夜，他鞭策着摩根公司 175 名被称为“SOS”（斯特蒂纽斯的奴隶）的员工。他不只是雇佣人：他征召他们、压榨他们、驱使他们直到精疲力竭。一位下属后来说：“如果有人晚上 9 点下班，其他人通常会恭喜他，说他算是提前半天放假了。”

The purchasing operation reflected the size and the complexity of modern warfare. World War I seemed both primitive and modern, an incongruous mixture of cavalry charges and zeppelin raids, cannon fire and mustard gas. There were endless salvos of deadly projectiles: at the Battle of the Marne alone, two hundred thousand shells were exploded in a day. So the logistical needs were immensely varied and of decisive importance in the war effort.
采购行动反映了现代战争的规模和复杂性。第一次世界大战既原始又现代，骑兵冲锋与齐柏林飞艇袭击、炮火与芥子气交织在一起，显得格格不入。致命的投射物不断齐射：仅在马恩河战役中，一天之内就发射了二十万发炮弹。因此，后勤需求极为多样，对战争进程具有决定性意义。

Stettinius became the single most important consumer on earth, rounding up \$10 million in goods per day. He bought, shipped, and insured supplies on an unprecedented scale and stimulated methods of mass production. As word of his operation spread, 23 Wall Street was mobbed by brokers and manufacturers of every description; the bank had to post guards at every door and assign them to partners’ homes. Each month, Stettinius presided over purchases equivalent to the world’s gross national product a generation before. He bargained hard for corned beef and barbed wire, locomotives and artificial limbs.
斯泰丁纽斯成了地球上最重要的单一消费者，每天采购价值 1000 万美元的物资。他以前所未有的规模购买、运输和保险供货，并推动了批量生产方式。随着他的运作消息传开，华尔街 23 号被各种经纪人和制造商挤得水泄不通；银行不得不在每扇门安排警卫，并派人守护合伙人的住所。每个月，斯泰丁纽斯主持的采购额相当于上一代人的全球国民生产总值。他为咸牛肉、铁丝网、火车头和假肢都讨价还价，毫不含糊。

The German general staff had never imagined that the United States could switch so quickly to war production. As the capacity of plants became strained, Stettinius promoted the building of new factories. The House of Morgan and Great Britain made loans to Winchester Repeating Arms for new gun capacity and advanced money to many other firms to fulfill their contracts. By war’s end, the United States had an arms-making capacity that eclipsed that of England and France combined. For his efforts, Stettinius would bear the unlovely tag of father of the military industrial complex. Even General Erich von Ludendorff was heard to say that Stettinius was worth an army corps to the Allies.[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261861) He became a czar of American industry. Boris Bakhmeteff, head of a Russian Industrial Mission to the United States, recalled a meeting at which Stettinius assembled the heads of some of America’s largest companies and “gave them hell in words that I was ashamed of.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262040)
德国总参谋部从未料到美国能如此迅速地转入战时生产。当现有工厂产能吃紧时，Stettinius 推动新建厂房。摩根财团和英国向 Winchester Repeating Arms 提供贷款以扩大枪支产能，并向其他多家公司预付资金以助其履行合同。到战争结束时，美国的军工产能已超过英法两国之和。正因如此，Stettinius 背负了"军工复合体之父"这个不讨喜的称号。据说连德国将军 Erich von Ludendorff 都曾评价，Stettinius 对协约国的价值胜过一支集团军。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2261861) 他成了美国工业界的铁腕人物。俄国驻美工业使团团长 Boris Bakhmeteff 回忆，在一次会议上，Stettinius 召集了美国多家巨头公司的负责人，"用连我都脸红不堪的粗话狠狠训斥了他们。" [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262040)

Because Stettinius was the linchpin of the Allied supply operation, his safety became a high priority, especially after Erich von Falkenhayn, chief of Germany’s general staff, decided to achieve victory by cutting off the Allied supply line. British intelligence agents informed Stettinius of threats against his life. They told of a “certain lovely lady” in New York who had seen a German agent carrying letters addressed to him. As a security precaution, Stettinius’s family was uprooted without warning from their thirteen-acre mansion on Staten Island and relocated on Long Island. Stettinius himself spent the war aboard the cruiser *Margaret*, anchored in New York harbor. His room was tastefully appointed with vases, linen, china, and plated silver, all picked by that well-known decorator Harry Davison.
由于斯特蒂纽斯是盟军补给行动的关键人物，他的安全成了头等大事，尤其是当德国总参谋长埃里希·冯·法尔肯海因决定通过切断盟军补给线来赢得胜利之后。英国情报人员告知斯特蒂纽斯有人要谋害他。他们提到纽约有位"美丽的女士"曾看到一个德国特工携带写给他的信件。作为安全防范措施，斯特蒂纽斯的家人被毫无预警地从斯塔滕岛那座占地十三英亩的豪宅中迁出，搬到了长岛。而斯特蒂纽斯本人在战争期间则一直待在停泊于纽约港的"玛格丽特号"巡洋舰上。他的房间布置得雅致考究，花瓶、亚麻布、瓷器和镀银餐具一应俱全，全部由著名室内设计师哈里·戴维森精心挑选。

The Morgan bank also performed intelligence work for the British. When the Morgan partners learned of a plan by German investors to buy up Bethlehem Steel, they met with company officials and had them put their shares in a voting trust, making the defense contractor impregnable to an unwanted takeover. In an extraordinary act of faith, the British exempted the House of Morgan from mail censorship in and out of Britain, allowing it to retain an in-house code developed by Stettinius and his British contact, Charles F. Whigham of Morgan Grenfell. Hence, in wartime cables, Jack retained his code name Chargeless and Lamont, Chalado. Sticking to tradition, the bank wouldn’t let any outsider have access to its code book.
摩根银行还为英国从事情报工作。当摩根合伙人得知德国投资者计划收购伯利恒钢铁公司时，他们立即与公司管理层会面，安排其将股份交由投票信托管理，从而让这家国防承包商免于被恶意收购。出于非同寻常的信任，英国政府豁免了摩根家族进出英国的邮件审查，允许其保留由斯泰蒂纽斯与英国联络人——摩根建富银行的查尔斯·F·惠格姆共同开发的内部密码。因此，在战时电报中，杰克仍使用代号"Chargeless"，拉蒙特则用"Chalado"。遵循传统，该银行绝不允许任何外人接触其密码本。

Nonetheless, the Export Department wasn’t an unqualified success. The French never used it as much as the British did, and the British Admiralty remained cool compared with the War Office—a tension unrelieved by a meeting between Jack and First Lord of the Admiralty Sir Winston Churchill. There were also persistent suspicions that the bank favored friends. Though contracts were distributed to almost one thousand companies, many big winners—General Electric, Bethlehem Steel, Du Pont, and U.S. Steel—were firmly in the Morgan camp.
尽管如此，出口部并非尽善尽美。法国人对其的使用远不及英国人频繁，而英国海军部与陆军部相比始终态度冷淡——杰克与海军大臣温斯顿·丘吉尔爵士的会晤也未能缓解这一紧张局面。此外，始终存在银行偏袒友商的嫌疑。尽管合同分配给了近千家公司，但许多大赢家——通用电气、伯利恒钢铁、杜邦和美国钢铁——都坚定地站在摩根阵营一边。

The war was especially profitable for the Guggenheims. In 1914, the House of Morgan helped them to organize Kennecott Copper, America’s biggest copper producer, as a public company; Daniel Guggenheim was a frequent wartime visitor to Morgan partner Thomas Cochran, who sat on Kennecott’s board. The Export Department bought up three-quarters of all the electrolytic copper mined in the United States for the British, and the Guggenheims and many others made fortunes from it. Another Guggenheim company, American Smelting and Refining, enjoyed a boom as the Allies bought lead for rifles and bullets. The distribution of billions of dollars in contracts enabled the House of Morgan to win the loyalty of dozens of powerful companies.
这场战争对古根海姆家族来说尤其有利可图。1914 年，摩根财团帮助他们将美国最大的铜生产商肯尼科特铜业公司改组为上市公司；丹尼尔·古根海姆在战时频繁拜访摩根合伙人托马斯·科克伦，后者正是肯尼科特公司的董事会成员。出口部为英国买下了美国开采的全部电解铜的四分之三，古根海姆家族及其他许多人因此大发横财。古根海姆旗下的另一家公司——美国冶炼与精炼公司，也因协约国大量采购步枪和子弹所需的铅而迎来繁荣。数十亿美元合同的分配，使得摩根财团赢得了数十家实力雄厚公司的忠诚。

Within bounds, the British tried to prevent the bank from abusing its extraordinary powers. To investigate charges of favoritism, Great Britain sent a mission to New York under the Welsh coal magnate David Alfred Thomas, later Lord Rhondda. Staying at the Plaza Hotel for three weeks during the summer of 1915, Thomas hovered around the bank and found Stettinius’s work faultless. He did report to England that the bank was buying excessively from Republicans, and Lloyd George advised Davison to spread the wealth around. Davison replied that they would try to distribute contracts geographically.
在可控范围内，英国试图阻止银行滥用其非凡权力。为调查偏袒指控，英国派遣威尔士煤炭大亨戴维·阿尔弗雷德·托马斯（后成为朗达勋爵）率团前往纽约。1915 年夏季，托马斯在广场酒店住了三周，围绕银行展开调查，最终认定斯特蒂纽斯的工作无可挑剔。他确实向国内报告称银行从共和党人那里购买过多，劳合·乔治建议戴维森将订单分散。戴维森回应称，他们会尽量按地域分配合同。

Thomas’s stay in New York had one uneasy moment. One day, he got a call from his secretary at the Plaza, saying a sudden gust of wind had blown some confidential memos out the window; three sheets of top-secret onionskin had fluttered down onto Fifth Avenue. This breach of security was so grave that Lloyd George was notified in London. Through late-afternoon drizzle, Morgan employees scoured the avenue, ducking under parked cars and staring down drains. The sheets were lost. To console Thomas, his staff took three identical sheets, dragged them through bathwater, and showed him how they decomposed.
托马斯在纽约的逗留中有一段不愉快的插曲。一天，他接到广场酒店秘书的电话，说一阵狂风把几份机密备忘录吹出了窗外；三张薄如洋葱皮的绝密文件飘落到了第五大道上。这次泄密事件严重到伦敦的劳合·乔治都收到了通知。在傍晚的细雨里，摩根公司的工作人员仔细搜寻整条大道，弯腰查看停放的汽车底下，还盯着排水沟检查。那些文件最终还是找不到了。为了安慰托马斯，他的员工拿来三张一模一样的纸张，在水里浸湿后，让他亲眼看着它们如何分解。

Notwithstanding Thomas’s report, the British remained wary of Morgans and believed it rewarded friendly steel, chemical, and shipping concerns. Asquith consoled himself with the thought that the bank kept its back scratching within tolerable limits. He wrote to Reginald McKenna, who had succeeded Lloyd George as chancellor of the Exchequer: “In regard to Morgan’s, while I do not doubt that they have made and will continue to make all that they can out of us, I see no reason to think that they have been acting unfairly, still less treacherously. The original contract with them may or may not have been wise, but it would be bad policy to swop horses now, or to make them suspect that we distrust them.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262177)
尽管托马斯提交了报告，但英国人对摩根银行仍心存戒备，认为该行偏袒与其关系友好的钢铁、化工及航运企业。阿斯奎斯聊以自慰地认为，这家银行将利益交换控制在了可容忍的范围内。他在致信接替劳合·乔治出任财政大臣的雷金纳德·麦肯纳时写道：“关于摩根银行，我毫不怀疑他们过去和将来都会从我们身上榨取一切，但我认为没有理由认定他们行事不公，更谈不上背信弃义。当初与他们签订合同或许明智，或许失策，但如今中途换马，或让他们察觉我们心存芥蒂，绝非上策。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262177)

In fact, the British were never foolishly or blindly in love with the House of Morgan. They welcomed having an Anglo-American listening post on Wall Street, especially as financial power shifted across the Atlantic. But the government’s deliberations during the war were veined by a certain cynicism, a belief that Morgan partners drove a hard bargain and needlessly offended people with their arrogance. Relations between the Morgans and the British would always be close but seldom harmonious, a fraternal tension lurking beneath protestations of mutual devotion.
事实上，英国人从未对摩根家族有过愚蠢或盲目的爱慕。他们欢迎在华尔街设立英美两国的情报站，尤其是在金融力量跨越大西洋转移之际。但战争期间，英国政府的决策中透露出某种犬儒主义——认为摩根合伙人谈判苛刻，且因傲慢无端得罪人。摩根家族与英国人的关系始终亲密却鲜少融洽，在彼此忠诚的表象下潜藏着兄弟般的紧张。

WHERE other partners at 23 Wall Street harbored some secret envy or suspicion of their British brethren, Jack Morgan had no such reservations. He regularly spent up to six months a year in England and was fully bicultural. For him, the war was a holy cause as well as a business opportunity. Even more than Pierpont, Jack was simple and guileless. He inhabited a black-and-white world in which loyalty to England found its equal and opposite emotion in hatred of the Germans. Unstinting in serving England, he donated Dover House, Junius’s old country house at Roehampton, as a convalescent home for wounded officers. He instructed his steward at Wall Hall to plough up parkland and plant wheat for the war effort. Once Jack’s passions were engaged, his commitment was total. J. P. Morgan and Company even took a stake in Montana wheat fields to supply more war provisions.
当华尔街 23 号的其他合伙人对他们的英国同行暗藏几分嫉妒或猜疑时，杰克·摩根却没有丝毫保留。他每年通常要在英国待上六个月，完全融入了两种文化。对他而言，这场战争既是神圣的事业，也是商业良机。与皮尔庞特相比，杰克更加单纯和率真。他的世界非黑即白，对英国的忠诚恰恰对应着对德国的仇恨。他毫不吝啬地为英国服务，将朱尼乌斯在罗汉普顿的旧乡间别墅——多佛庄园——捐出，作为伤兵疗养院。他指示沃尔庄园的管家犁开苑园草地，种植小麦以支援战争。一旦杰克的热情被点燃，他的投入便是全力以赴。摩根大通公司甚至入股蒙大拿州的麦田，以提供更多战争补给。

With America officially neutral, Stettinius’s Export Department exposed the bank to inflammatory criticism. It fanned anti-Morgan sentiment that had existed in the hinterlands ever since William Jennings Bryan’s Cross of Gold speech. During rallies at the Corner, agitators would point to 23 Wall and blame Morgan partners for killing thousands of innocents. Senator Robert La Follette echoed small-town sneers when he asked, “What do Morgan and Schwab \[head of Bethlehem Steel\] care for world peace, when there are big profits in world war?”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262333)Minnesota congressman Charles Lindbergh, who had prompted the Pujo hearings, now condemned the “money interests” for trying to lure the country into war on the side of the Allies. A dual myth was being born—that the Morgans were stooges of the British crown and that their money was drenched in blood. The bank received a flood of hate mail. Lamont received one note that said, “My dear Mr. Lamont—Your death doom is marked by your activity for the British war loan, which will deal death to my brothers on the battlefield in Germany. It shall be a distinct pleasure for me to puncture your black heart with lead some time in the distant future.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262493)
随着美国正式中立，斯退丁纽斯的出口部门使银行招致了激烈的批评。这更煽起了内陆地区自威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩发表“金十字架”演说以来就一直存在的反摩根情绪。在华尔街拐角处的集会上，鼓动者会指着华尔街 23 号，指责摩根合伙人杀害了数千无辜者。参议员罗伯特·拉福莱特附和小镇的讥讽，问道：“当世界大战能带来巨额利润时，摩根和施瓦布（伯利恒钢铁公司负责人）又怎会在意世界和平？”明尼苏达州众议员查尔斯·林德伯格曾推动普乔听证会，如今他谴责“金钱利益集团”试图将国家拉入战争，站到协约国一边。一个双重神话正在诞生——摩根家族是英国王室的傀儡，他们的金钱沾满了鲜血。银行收到了大量仇恨信件。拉蒙特收到了一张便条，上面写着：“亲爱的先生，” 拉蒙特——你为英国战争贷款所进行的活动，已为你敲响了死亡的丧钟，这会让我的兄弟们死在德国的战场上。有朝一日，用子弹打穿你那颗黑心，对我来说将是莫大的享受。“ [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262493)

Jack tried to avoid publicity that might incite Congress. When Harry Davison and lawyer Paul Cravath wanted to form a political committee to proselytize for the Allies, Jack refused. He also shied away from public appearances with his close friend Sir Cecil Arthur Spring-Rice, the British ambassador. In January 1915, writing about an upcoming trip, Jack told Springy that it might be “wiser for me not to be actually living at your house when I am in Washington. We are endeavouring to conduct this transaction with the British Government as inconspicuously as possible . . . but I must say that I do not see why, when you get away, you should not come and stay with us, which would be more quiet than staying in a hotel.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262679)
杰克竭力避免引起可能刺激国会的公众关注。当哈里·戴维森和律师保罗·克拉瓦特想要组建一个政治委员会来为协约国游说时，杰克拒绝了。他也不愿与密友、英国大使塞西尔·阿瑟·斯普林-赖斯爵士一同公开露面。1915 年 1 月，在谈及一次即将到来的旅行时，杰克告诉斯普林：“我在华盛顿期间，最好还是不要真的住在你家里。我们正尽力让与英国政府进行的这笔交易尽可能不引人注目……但我必须说，我不明白为什么你离开时不能来和我们住在一起，这比住酒店要安静得多。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262679)

Jack had always lived with a heightened awareness of danger. While at Harvard, a detective had tagged along behind him. After Jack’s younger son, Harry, returned to New York with his British tutor, the boy had become obsessed with fears of kidnapping. While Pierpont was still alive, Jack had experienced a burglary at Madison Avenue that smelled bizarrely of class revenge: the burglar had casually sat around the house, smoking his cigars. Another time, a blackmailer threatened to blow up Jack’s house unless money was deposited beneath a bush in Central Park; no money was paid, and no bomb went off.
杰克一直对危险有着高度警觉。在哈佛时，曾有个侦探跟在他身后。杰克的次子哈里跟着英国家教回到纽约后，便对绑架事件产生了深深的恐惧。皮尔庞特还在世时，杰克的麦迪逊大道的住所曾遭入室盗窃，那起案件隐约透着阶级复仇的气息：窃贼不慌不忙地在屋里闲坐，还抽起了他的雪茄。还有一次，一个敲诈者威胁要炸毁杰克的房子，除非他把钱藏在中央公园的某处灌木丛下；结果杰克没付钱，炸弹也没爆炸。

The House of Morgan was also an irresistible magnet for crackpots, who were attracted by its aura of mystery. Early in the war, a stream of abusive letters arrived from a madman named Schindler, who believed the bank had stolen his interest in an Alaska mine but refused to admit it. Such constant threats stoked Jack’s already fertile imagination, and he was wont to see conspirators everywhere.
摩根家族那种神秘的光环，也总像磁铁一样吸引着各路怪人。战争初期，一个叫 Schindler 的疯子寄来了一连串辱骂信，坚称银行偷走了他阿拉斯加金矿的股份却死不认账。这种没完没了的威胁，更是点燃了 Jack 本就过于发达的想像力，让他看谁都觉得像是阴谋家。

As it turned out, however, Jack’s fears weren’t entirely groundless. On the balmy Sunday morning of July 3, 1915, Jack and Jessie were having breakfast at their North Shore estate with Spring-Rice and his wife. They were just finishing the meal when the Morgan butler, Henry Physick, went to answer the door. There wasn’t yet a guardhouse at the causeway that connected the island to the Long Island shore, and interlopers could walk straight up to the door. A slight, gray-suited stranger greeted Physick and handed him a card saying “SUMMER SOCIETY DIRECTORY, REPRESENTED BY THOMAS c. LESTER.” He asked to see Mr. Morgan.
然而，事实证明，杰克的担忧并非毫无根据。1915 年 7 月 3 日，一个温暖宜人的周日早晨，杰克和杰西正在他们位于北岸的庄园里，与斯普林-赖斯夫妇共进早餐。就在他们快要吃完时，摩根家的管家亨利·菲西克前去开门。当时连接岛屿与长岛岸边的堤道上还没有警卫室，不速之客可以径直走到门前。一个身材瘦削、身着灰色西装的陌生人与菲西克打了招呼，递上一张名片，上面写着“夏季社交名册，由托马斯·C·莱斯特代理”。他说要见摩根先生。

Physick was a British butler of the old school. He usually wore a dark coat and gray striped trousers and was precise in his manners. Tactful but scenting danger, Physick refused to let the insistent stranger pass. He quickly raced to the library, found Jack and Jessie, and shouted “Upstairs!” Following these cryptic instructions, the Morgans went upstairs and searched the bedrooms, trying to figure out the problem. Then, at the top of the staircase, they saw the gunman, brandishing two pistols and leading the two Morgan daughters up the steps. (Later the gunman confessed that his major mistake was walking in front of the Morgan children, not behind them, thus reducing their value as hostages.) Trying to remain calm, the gunman told the Morgans not to be frightened, that he wanted to talk with them.
菲斯克是一位老派英式管家。他通常穿着深色外套和灰色条纹长裤，举止一丝不苟。尽管为人圆滑，但嗅到危险气息时，菲斯克坚决不让那位执意闯入的陌生人通过。他迅速跑进书房，找到杰克和杰西，大喊一声"上楼！"听到这没头没脑的指令，摩根夫妇走上楼去，把卧室挨个搜查了一遍，想弄清究竟出了什么事。接着，在楼梯顶端，他们看到了那个持枪歹徒——他挥舞着两把手枪，正把摩根家的两个女儿往楼上赶。（后来歹徒供认，他最大的失误是走在了摩根家孩子的前面，而不是后面，这大大降低了他们的人质价值。）歹徒试图保持镇定，告诉摩根夫妇别害怕，他只是想和他们谈谈。

If the later police depositions are accurate, everyone showed phenomenal courage. A woman of steely self-control, Jessie Morgan threw herself at the gunman. Her courage gave the big, burly Jack time enough to wade in and tackle the man; he took two bullets in the groin as he subdued him. While servants pinioned his arms, Jessie and Jack pried loose his two pistols. Then, with timing so exact it resembles Hollywood stagecraft, Physick rushed in and smashed a chunk of coal over the man’s head, rendering him unfit for further mischief. (This splendid touch, alas, isn’t mentioned in the police depositions.) Only after subduing the man did the Morgans see a large stick of dynamite protruding from his pocket. The assassination attempt ended with the Morgan servants submerging the dynamite in water and tightly binding the man in ropes. Dr. James Markoe, the Morgan family physician, was rushed out to Glen Cove to treat Jack’s bullet wounds.
如果后来的警方证词准确无误，那么每个人都表现出了惊人的勇气。一位自制力如钢铁般的女性，杰西·摩根，挺身扑向持枪歹徒。她的勇敢给了身材魁梧的杰克足够的时间冲上前去制服歹徒；他在制伏对方时腹股沟中了两枪。仆人们摁住歹徒的手臂时，杰西和杰克撬下了他手中的两把手枪。接着，在时机精准得如同好莱坞舞台剧般的瞬间，菲西克冲进来，用一大块煤砸向歹徒的脑袋，让他再无作恶之力。（可惜，这精彩的一幕并未被警方证词提及。）直到制伏歹徒后，摩根一家才发现他口袋里露出一大根炸药棒。这次刺杀行动最终以摩根家的仆人们将炸药浸入水中、并用绳索将歹徒紧紧捆绑而告终。摩根家的家庭医生詹姆斯·马科博士被紧急召到格伦科夫为杰克处理枪伤。

At the Nassau County jail, the gunman gave his name as Frank Holt, which turned out to be an alias for Erich Muenter. A man with a shadowy past and a former German instructor at Harvard, Muenter had vanished in 1906 after having been indicted for poisoning his wife with arsenic. Under questioning, he confessed to being a pacifist opposed to American arms exports to Europe. He hadn’t planned to kill Jack, he said, only to hold him hostage until munitions shipments were stopped. He possessed a delirious, dreamlike sense of Morgan power. The interrogator asked, “Do you think that you singlehandedly could arrest the whole trend of the age?” “No, but Mr. Morgan could.” “Do you think he could control those countries?” “With his money, if his money didn’t flow into their cash drawers, and stop the flow of ammunition.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262892) To supplement his attack against Morgan, Muenter had secreted a bomb the day before in the U.S. Senate chambers. Whether Muenter had confederates will never be known. Two weeks later, he committed suicide in the Nassau County jail.
在纳苏县监狱，枪手报上姓名弗兰克·霍尔特，但此人实为埃里希·明特的化名。这位哈佛前德语讲师背景神秘，1906 年因被控用砒霜毒杀妻子而销声匿迹。审讯中他承认自己是反对美国向欧洲出口武器的和平主义者，并称本无杀杰克之意，只想挟持人质直至军火运输停止。他对摩根财团的力量怀有一种痴心妄想、梦幻般的认知。审问官问他：“你以为单凭一己之力就能阻挡整个时代的洪流吗？”“不能，但摩根先生可以。”“你认为他能掌控那些国家吗？”“用他的钱——只要他的钱不流入他们的钱柜，就能切断弹药输送的命脉。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2262892) 为配合对摩根的袭击，明特前一天还在美国参议院议事厅暗藏了炸弹。至于明特是否有同党，恐怕永远无人知晓。 两周后，他在 Nassau County 监狱自杀身亡。

Outwardly, Jack seemed phlegmatic and even clinical about the shooting, as if he had gone through a mildly unpleasant experiment and were jotting down the results. Miraculously, the bullets missed all vital organs, and his wounds healed quickly as he recuperated aboard the *Corsair III.* “It was a most disagreeable experience, though it is not as painful as I imagined it would be to be shot as I was,” he said.[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263142) He credited Jessie’s coolness for foiling the plot and said he had done only what any parent would have with an intruder pointing a gun at his family. Dismissive of his own bravery, he was taken aback by the congratulatory messages that swamped the local telegraph office. On August 16, when he emerged from 23 Wall after his first day back at the bank, he was cheered by waiting crowds as he slipped into his limousine. Boyishly surprised, he touched his hat brim and gave a little wave. Unaccustomed to public adulation, he attained a fleeting status as a national hero.
外表上看，杰克对枪击事件表现得冷漠甚至近乎麻木，仿佛只是经历了一场略感不适的实验，正在平静地记录结果。奇迹般的是，子弹避开了所有要害部位，他在"海盗 III 号"上休养时伤口迅速愈合。他说："虽然被枪击的滋味并不像我想象中那么痛苦，但绝对称不上愉快的体验。" [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263142) 他将挫败阴谋的功劳归于杰西的冷静沉着，并说自己只是做了任何父亲在面对持枪闯入者威胁家人时都会做的事。他对自己的英勇行为轻描淡写，却被当地电报局收到的如潮贺电惊得手足无措。8 月 16 日，当他重返银行后第一次走出华尔街 23 号大楼时，等候的人群爆发出欢呼声，他钻进豪华轿车时还略显羞涩地碰了碰帽檐，轻轻挥了挥手。这位不习惯公众赞美的银行家，就这样短暂地成为了国民英雄。

Jack’s calm was deceptive, for the shooting had deep effects that he hid beneath an offhanded manner. While a plot was never proved, Jack insisted Muenter was no isolated lunatic but part of a terrorist scheme. At his Adirondack retreat, Camp Uncas, he had the steward “get rid” of Germans and Austrians on the payroll.[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263340) The shadows suddenly teemed with enemies. From the *Corsair*, Jack wrote Teddy Grenfell that Jessie had “an impression that people are trying to get another shot at me, and I have to look out for this even more than I otherwise would do, in order to satisfy her.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263508) There were many reminders that Muenter wasn’t a lone Morgan-hater. When news of the 1915 shooting reached Vienna, it was celebrated by fireworks, speeches, and jubilant crowds.
杰克的冷静只是假象，枪击事件对他产生了深远的影响，只是他用一副漫不经心的态度掩盖了这些。虽然从未证实有任何阴谋，但杰克坚持认为穆恩特并非孤立的疯子，而是一个恐怖计划的一部分。他在阿迪朗达克的隐居地安卡斯营地，让管家“处理掉”了雇用的德国人和奥地利人。 [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263340) 阴影中突然充满了敌人。杰克在《海盗船》号上写信给泰迪·格伦费尔，说杰西“总觉得有人还想再朝我开枪，为了让她放心，我不得不比平时更加警惕。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263508) 有很多迹象表明，穆恩特并非唯一憎恨摩根的人。1915 年枪击事件的消息传到维也纳时，那里燃放了烟花，举行了演讲，欢腾的人群对此表示庆祝。

The shooting reinforced Jack’s reclusiveness, his penchant for privacy at the retreats of the rich. As a result, he probably spent more time at English country estates or cruising aboard his yacht; it was no coincidence that he recuperated aboard the *Corsair.* The shooting also filled him with a sense of omnipresent danger, playing to a cloak-and-dagger side of his personality. He frequently moved about by stealth. Visiting his older son, Junius, in Baltimore during the war, he wrote a friend about hotel arrangements: “I should greatly prefer that the hotel would not force me to register or to say that I am coming, owing to the fact that, apparently, the Germans are still after me, and I am requested by my family not to state where and when I am going in other towns.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263689) After the shooting, Jack would be accompanied by bodyguards, a team of former marines. Such heavy security had the unfortunate effect of further distancing him from ordinary people and making the everyday brands of human misery more remote to him.
枪击事件加剧了杰克的隐居倾向，也加深了他对隐私的执着，这种执着在富人们的避世寓所中尤为明显。因此，他更多时间都待在英国的乡间庄园，或乘游艇出海——他在“海盗号”上养伤绝非偶然。这次袭击也让他时刻感到危机四伏，助长了他性格中神秘莫测的一面。他常常悄然行动。战争期间去巴尔的摩探望长子朱尼厄斯时，他在给朋友的信中写道，关于酒店安排：“我极不愿酒店强迫我登记入住或透露行程，因为显然德国人仍在追杀我，家人也嘱咐我不得向他人透露在别处的行踪。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263689) 枪击之后，杰克身边总有保镖跟随——一支由前海军陆战队员组成的护卫队。如此严密的安保措施，不幸地让他与普通人更加疏远，也使他对人间苦难的日常形态更加陌生。

Jack’s security was also a constant preoccupation of his highly protective partners. He was often unaware of security guards in a crowd. In Paris, senior partner Herman Harjes would notify the *surete general* whenever Jack visited. The detectives would stay close but not reveal their presence. Jack moved behind that invisible shield accorded heads of state.
杰克的安全问题，也是他那群高度警惕的合作伙伴们一直操心的事。他常常意识不到人群中的安保人员。在巴黎，每当杰克到访，高级合伙人赫尔曼·哈杰斯都会通知公安总局。侦探们会与他保持距离，但不会暴露自己的存在。杰克就在那道专为国家元首设下的无形屏障后移动。

The shooting would be but one in a series of episodes that darkened Jack’s view of the world and produced a settled malice toward his enemies. These episodes made him feel frightened and beleaguered and quickened his tendency to lash out against his enemies. For all his wealth and power, Jack felt vulnerable to forces outside his control.
枪击事件只是诸多经历之一，这些经历让杰克对世界的看法变得阴暗，并对他的敌人产生了深深的敌意。这些经历让他感到恐惧和四面楚歌，也加剧了他向敌人猛烈反击的倾向。尽管拥有巨大的财富和权力，杰克仍然感到自己无力掌控那些超出他控制范围的力量。

JACK told friends that the shooting had made him more fervently anti-German and more eager to see the United States enter the war on the Allied side. He reviled the Germans as “Huns” and “Teuton savages”—he relished colorful epithets—and exhibited a latent bias against Germany that he had inherited from his father. As partner George Whitney later explained, Pierpont “always accused the Germans of doublecrossing him. . . . So there was an edict put down that we would never do business with the Germans.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263891)
JACK 告诉朋友们，这次枪击让他更加坚定地反对德国，也更渴望看到美国加入协约国一方参战。他痛斥德国人为“野蛮人”和“条顿蛮族”——他特别喜欢用这些生动的贬称——并表现出从父亲那里继承来的对德国的潜在偏见。正如合伙人 George Whitney 后来解释的那样，Pierpont “一直指责德国人背信弃义……所以我们立下规矩，绝不与德国人做生意。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2263891)

World War I was perhaps the last war in which bankers behaved as if they were sovereign states, indulging their biases and waging their own foreign policy. On Wall Street, spoils of war were divided strictly according to political and religious differences among the bankers. The House of Morgan was superbly positioned. Through its London and Paris houses, it had helped France finance the Franco-Prussian War and England the Boer War. Jack even had a soft spot for the czar, to whom he had extended credit.
第一次世界大战或许是人类历史上最后一场银行家们仍像主权国家般行事、任由偏见作祟并推行各自外交政策的战争。在华尔街，战争红利严格依照银行家们政治与宗教立场的差异来划分。摩根财团当时占据极其有利的地位：通过其在伦敦和巴黎的分支机构，它曾为法国筹措普法战争经费，也为英国提供布尔战争贷款。杰克·摩根甚至对沙皇抱有好感，并曾向其提供信贷。

If a bonanza for Yankee Wall Street, the war was a catastrophe for Jewish firms, which were encumbered by anti-Russian and pro-German sympathies. Jacob Schiff, the autocratic head of Kuhn, Loeb, had been aghast at Russian pogroms, branding the czarist government the “enemy of mankind”; in revenge, he financed Japan in the 1904-5 Russo-Japanese War. Nonetheless, he moderated his German sympathies after 1914, endorsed a negotiated peace, and “dutifully stopped speaking German to his family in public.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264027) The less circumspect Henry Goldman of Goldman, Sachs espoused pro-German views, spouting Nietzsche and glorifying Prussian culture—much to the dismay of his partners. The Guggenheims, of German-speaking Swiss ancestry, suppressed any sympathy they might have had for Germany as munitions contracts rolled in.
对华尔街的美国人来说是一场横财，但对犹太公司而言却是一场灾难，它们因反俄亲德的倾向而处处受制。库恩-勒布公司的独裁掌门人雅各布·希夫对俄罗斯的集体迫害深感震惊，将沙皇政府斥为"人类公敌"；作为报复，他在 1904-1905 年的日俄战争中资助了日本。尽管如此，他在 1914 年后克制了自己的亲德情绪，支持通过谈判实现和平，并"尽职地停止在公开场合对家人说德语。" [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264027) 高盛公司的亨利·戈德曼则没那么谨慎，他宣扬亲德观点，大谈尼采、美化普鲁士文化——令他的合伙人们大为不满。而古根海姆家族虽有德语瑞士血统，但随着军火合同源源不断，他们把对德国的任何同情都压在了心底。

During the war, Wall Street and the City were full of scurrilous attacks on supposedly disloyal Jews. In 1915, Edward Kraftmeier of the British Nobel Company came to New York to warn the du Ponts that their company, a major Allied manufacturer of smokeless powder, could fall under the share control of “pro-German” Kuhn, Loeb. There were fears that Coleman du Pont might sell his large stake to them. To counter this threat, the du Ponts obtained an \$8.5-million loan from Morgans, tightly locking up their shares in a holding company called Du Pont Securities. (When Sir William Wiseman, head of British intelligence in the United States, investigated the warning about Kuhn, Loeb, he found it baseless.) German financial penetration was a concern in the City as well, and the Bank of England “Anglicized” foreign-owned banks; for instance, it brought in the Pearson group to take over Lazard Brothers, fearing the London house might fall into German hands if its Paris affiliate were taken over.
战争期间，华尔街与伦敦金融城里充斥着对所谓不忠犹太人的恶意攻击。1915 年，英国诺贝尔公司的爱德华·克拉夫特迈尔来到纽约，警告杜邦公司——这家协约国无烟火药的主要制造商——其股份可能落入“亲德”的库恩–勒布公司之手。人们担心科尔曼·杜邦会将手中大量股权卖给他们。为应对这一威胁，杜邦家族从摩根公司获得 850 万美元贷款，将其股份牢牢锁定在名为杜邦证券的控股公司中。（英国驻美国情报头目威廉·怀斯曼爵士调查了关于库恩–勒布的警告后，发现纯属无稽之谈。）德国资本的渗透同样令金融城忧心忡忡，英格兰银行于是将外资银行“英国化”——例如，它引入皮尔逊集团接管拉扎德兄弟银行，原因是担心若该行巴黎分行被吞并，其伦敦总部可能落入德国之手。

In this highly charged atmosphere, Jack Morgan’s pro-British passions and his anti-Semitism began to feed on one another. In September 1914, he complained to Teddy Grenfell that the ” ’peace’ talk has been fomented and worked up in a large measure by the German Jew element, which is very close to the German Ambassador.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264201) Antagonism toward German-Jewish banks sharpened in December, when the House of Morgan extended a \$12-million credit to Russia; the next month, Great Britain initiated war purchases for the czar through the House of Morgan. Noting Russia’s treatment of Jews, Schiff stiffly protested to Jack, who had to tread gingerly, since the two co-managed big bond issues. The syndicate structure of investment banking made it a world of sharp but sheathed rapiers. Exercising self-control, Jack wrote to Schiff: “I do not think it is for us to endeavor to change the attitude of Russia by applying financial pressure. It seems to me that the question of whether or not Russia is a good and solvent debtor can hardly be mixed up with questions of internal social or policing regulations.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264408) Of course, Jack himself didn’t regard foreign loans so dispassionately and often mingled his political and financial beliefs.
在这般紧张的氛围下，杰克·摩根亲英的热情与他的反犹情绪开始相互助长。1914 年 9 月，他向泰迪·格伦费尔抱怨说，“和平”言论很大程度上是由与德国大使关系密切的德裔犹太势力煽动并炒作的。 [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264201) 12 月，摩根财团向俄国提供了 1200 万美元的信贷，这进一步激化了对德裔犹太人银行的敌意；次月，英国开始通过摩根财团为沙皇进行战争采购。注意到俄国对犹太人的待遇后，希夫向杰克提出了严正抗议，而杰克不得不小心应对，因为两人共同管理着大规模债券发行。投资银行的银团结构使得这个世界充满了锋利却藏锋的利剑。杰克克制住自己，在给希夫的信中写道：“我认为我们不该试图通过施加财政压力来改变俄国的态度。” 在我看来，俄罗斯是否为可靠且有偿付能力的债务国，这一问题恐怕很难与其内部社会或治安法规等议题混为一谈。”① 当然，杰克本人对待外债的态度并非如此冷静，他时常将政治信念与金融观点交织在一起。

The friction between Jack and Schiff led to a tense exchange in May 1915, when a German submarine off the Irish coast sank the Cunard’s *Lusitania*, one of two lavish ships built in response to Pierpont’s shipping trust. Over a thousand people died, including 63 children. Alfred Gwynne Vanderbilt was among the 128 American casualties. There was grief across America. That morning, amid heavy gloom, Schiff suppressed his pride and offered his regrets at the Corner. Haughty and formal, he never made this sort of call. When he entered, he found Jack in the partners’ room. Instead of receiving Schiff with courtesy, Jack muttered some angry words and stormed off, leaving Schiff in mystified silence. He shuffled out alone.
杰克与希夫之间的摩擦在 1915 年 5 月达到顶峰，当时一艘德国潜艇在爱尔兰海岸附近击沉了丘纳德旗下的卢西塔尼亚号——这艘船是皮尔庞特航运托拉斯催生的两艘豪华邮轮之一。事故造成超过一千人遇难，其中包括 63 名儿童。128 名美国伤亡者中就有阿尔弗雷德·格温·范德比尔特。举国陷入悲痛。那个阴沉的早晨，希夫压抑着自尊，前往“角落”表达哀悼。他一向高傲刻板，从不会主动登门。进门后，他在合伙人的房间里见到了杰克。然而杰克非但没有以礼相待，反而气冲冲地咒骂了几句，摔门而去，留下希夫困惑地站在原地。他只好独自悻悻离去。

The other partners gasped. It was a flagrant breach of the Gentleman Banker’s Code, the need to maintain civility on the surface. Jack remarked rather sheepishly, “I suppose I went a little far? I suppose I ought to apologize?” Nobody dared to speak. Then the quick-witted Dwight Morrow scratched a biblical quote on a piece of paper and handed it to Jack. It said, “Not for thy sake, but for thy name’s sake, O House of Israel!”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264599) Taking the hint, Jack took his hat and went off to Kuhn, Loeb to apologize. The tale vividly captures Jack’s contradictory nature—the polite and courtly surface, the mass of churning emotions within—as well as the strains of a highly artificial world that demanded constant civility. With the big syndicated loans, one couldn’t antagonize a powerful bank that might be an ally on the next issue.
其他合伙人都倒吸一口凉气。这公然违背了绅士银行家准则——表面上的体面必须维持。杰克有些讪讪地说："我是不是有点过头了？是不是该去道个歉？"没人敢接话。这时，机敏的德怀特·莫罗在一张纸上抄了句圣经经文递给杰克，上面写着："以色列家啊，我行这事不是为你们，乃是为我的圣名。" [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264599) 杰克心领神会，拿起帽子就去库恩-洛布公司登门道歉了。这个故事生动地捕捉了杰克矛盾的本性——彬彬有礼的外表下翻涌着澎湃的情绪，也折射出那个要求处处维持体面的高度造作世界带来的压力。面对那些巨额银团贷款，你绝不能得罪一家强大的银行，因为下一笔生意你们也许还要联手合作。

This muted warfare flared into the open in September 1915, during the creation of the biggest foreign loan in Wall Street history—the \$500-million Anglo-French loan. It was five times as big as the previous record holder, the \$100-million loan to Great Britain for the Boer War. The Stettinius mill was chewing up £2 million a day and threatened to exhaust British financial resources. As early as April 1, 1915, Jack lunched with Lloyd George and discussed a loan of at least \$ 100 million to prop up the pound. Teddy Grenfell and other directors of the Bank of England were worried about the makeshift war financing.
这场暗流涌动的角力在 1915 年 9 月猛然浮上台面——当时华尔街历史上最大的一笔对外贷款，即 5 亿美元的英法贷款，正在酝酿之中。其规模是此前最高纪录（为布尔战争向英国提供的 1 亿美元贷款）的五倍。斯泰丁纽斯的军火工厂每天消耗 200 万英镑，几乎要耗尽英国的财力。早在 1915 年 4 月 1 日，杰克就曾与劳合·乔治共进午餐，商议至少 1 亿美元的贷款以支撑英镑。泰迪·格伦费尔和英格兰银行的其他董事们对临时拼凑的战争融资忧心忡忡。

The financing problem grew acute that July. The British canceled one contract for the Russians in New York for lack of foreign exchange. To meet a dollar deadline at Morgans, Reginald McKenna had to commandeer American securities owned by Prudential Assurance—a piece of *ad hoc* midnight desperation that deeply disturbed Prime Minister Asquith. It was a rickety way to run a war. For the House of Morgan, stymied by the State Department ban on loans, it was an excruciating time. The one riddle Morgans could never resolve was what to do when U.S. and British policy diverged.
那年七月，融资问题变得格外棘手。英国因外汇短缺，取消了俄国在纽约的一份合同。为了凑齐摩根银行的美元欠款，雷金纳德·麦克纳不得不强行征用保诚保险公司名下的美国证券——这手孤注一掷的午夜操作令首相阿斯奎斯深感不安。靠这种摇摇欲坠的方式打仗，实在荒诞。而对于被国务院贷款禁令掣肘的摩根财团而言，这更是一段煎熬的岁月。摩根们始终解不开的谜题是：当美英政策分歧时，究竟该如何自处。

Wilson opposed a jumbo Allied loan but was finally persuaded by his cabinet that, without it, U.S. exports would suffer. Treasury Secretary McAdoo argued in late August that U.S. prosperity depended on trade with the Allies. Robert Lansing, who had replaced Bryan as secretary of state, starkly warned that without a loan, “the result would be restriction of outputs, industrial depression, idle capital and idle labor, numerous failures, financial demoralization, and general unrest and suffering among the laboring classes.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264771) Wilson was convinced.
威尔逊起初反对向同盟国提供大规模联合贷款，但最终在内阁的劝说下意识到，若无此贷款，美国的出口将遭受重创。财政部长麦卡杜在 8 月下旬指出，美国的繁荣依赖于与同盟国的贸易。接替布莱恩出任国务卿的罗伯特·兰辛更是直言不讳地警告：若无贷款，“后果将是产出受限、工业萧条、资本与劳动力闲置、企业大量倒闭、金融秩序崩溃，以及劳工阶级普遍的动荡与苦难。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2264771) 威尔逊最终被说服。

In September, the British cabinet dispatched an Anglo-French mission to New York to arrange a huge private loan. The North Atlantic swarmed with submarines, and Grenfell was told not to inform Morgans of the group’s membership. The commission was headed by Lord Reading, the lord chief justice, and included Sir Edward Holden, chairman of the Midland Bank, Basil Blackett of the British Treasury, and M. Octave Hombert, the French representative. Harry Davison and Jack went down to the pier to greet the *Lapland* and saw the group settled in at the Biltmore Hotel.
九月，英国内阁派遣了一个英法联合使团前往纽约，筹划一笔巨额私人贷款。当时北大西洋潜艇密布，格伦费尔接到指示，不得向摩根集团透露使团成员的名单。使团由首席大法官雷丁勋爵带队，成员包括米德兰银行董事长爱德华·霍尔登爵士、英国财政部的巴兹尔·布莱克特，以及法国代表奥克塔夫·翁贝尔先生。哈里·戴维森和杰克前往码头迎接“拉普兰号”客轮，并安排使团入住比尔莫尔酒店。

Once again, the Anglo-American Morgan love affair was full of spats and recriminations. Unwavering in support of Britain, Morgan partners felt bruised and demeaned by having to compete for the loan. Nevertheless, they gave the visiting group a red-carpet reception. Lord Reading, *ne* Rufus Isaacs, presented a formidable challenge to Jack Morgan’s prejudices. Son of a London fruit merchant, he was British, brilliant, titled—and Jewish. He had risen to the position of attorney general, cross-examining witnesses during the British *Titanic* inquiry. Jack and Davison visited Reading at the Biltmore, feted him at the Morgan Library, and entertained him aboard the *Corsair.* Against all odds, personal chemistry between Jack and Reading would help to seal the deal.
英美摩根家族的这场恋情再次充满了争吵与指责。尽管摩根合伙人们坚定不移地支持英国，但被迫为这笔贷款竞标仍让他们感到自尊受挫。不过，他们还是以红毯规格接待了来访的代表团。雷丁勋爵——原名鲁弗斯·艾萨克斯——对杰克·摩根根深蒂固的偏见构成了巨大挑战。他出身伦敦水果商家庭，血统纯正、才华横溢、受封贵族——还是个犹太人。他一路升至总检察长，曾在英国泰坦尼克号调查中盘问证人。杰克和戴维森在比尔特莫尔酒店拜访了雷丁，在摩根图书馆设宴款待，还邀请他登上"海盗号"游艇。出人意料的是，杰克与雷丁之间产生的化学反应，最终促成了这笔交易的达成。

The Anglo-French loan tested New York’s capacity as a financial market. The victorious Morgans had to contend with broad hostility toward Britain. One in ten Americans was of German ancestry, and many first-generation Irish immigrants opposed the loan. Fantastic numbers were bandied about—up to \$1 billion—and skeptics doubted it could be done. Such sums staggered and frightened Americans, much as had the huge trusts a few years before. In retrospect, the Anglo-French loan would mark the rise of America as the world’s chief creditor nation. Yet, even as the House of Morgan superintended this transfer of financial power, Jack was dubious that it would last, assuring Grenfell that “when the war is over, you will find the United States settling down again into using the European money markets as a clearing house, very much as before.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265054) Jack didn’t exult over Britain’s decline and found it hard to foresee the demotion of his beloved London.
英法贷款考验了纽约作为金融市场的实力。大获全胜的摩根家族不得不应对美国社会对英国的普遍敌意。当时每十个美国人中就有一个是德裔，而许多第一代爱尔兰移民也反对这笔贷款。外界传闻的贷款金额大得惊人——最高达到 10 亿美元——怀疑论者认为这根本不可能实现。如此巨款让美国人震惊又惶恐，正如几年前那些庞大的信托基金带来的冲击一样。如今回顾，英法贷款标志着美国崛起为世界主要债权国。然而，即便摩根家族正主导着这场金融权力的交接，杰克对此能否持久仍心存疑虑，他向格伦费尔保证说：“战争结束后，你会发现美国会重新把欧洲货币市场当作清算中心，跟从前没什么两样。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265054) 杰克并未因英国的衰落而沾沾自喜，也难以预见他所挚爱的伦敦会地位下降。

After an honorary dinner at the Morgan Library, Jack invited Reading up to his second-floor study for cigars. He and his partners had to dampen inflated British expectations. Through the haze of cigar smoke, Jack casually knocked several hundred million dollars off the loan. “Reading,” Jack said, “I wouldn’t ask a billion if I were you. I think you’d be wiser to limit your first large bond issue to half a billion.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265259) To Jack’s surprise, Reading consented to a \$500-million (£100-million) issue. After syndicate charges were factored in, the interest rate was a steep 6 percent. Jack said the House of Morgan would waive any extra compensation as syndicate manager.
在摩根图书馆的荣誉晚宴结束后，杰克邀请雷丁上到二楼的雪茄室。他和他的合伙人需要给英国人过高的期望降降温。在缭绕的雪茄烟雾中，杰克轻描淡写地砍掉了数亿美元的贷款。“雷丁，”杰克说道，“如果我是你，就不会开口要十亿。在我看来，明智之举是把首批大规模债券发行控制在五亿以内。” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265259) 令杰克意外的是，雷丁竟同意了发行五亿美元（合 1 亿英镑）的债券。将银团费用计入后，利率高达 6%。杰克表示，作为银团牵头方，摩根家族将放弃任何额外报酬。

Fascinated by Lord Reading, Jack was preoccupied with his religion:
杰克对雷丁勋爵着迷不已，对他所信奉的宗教也充满好奇：

> Lord Reading impressed me enormously. His mind is so clear, and he sees the bearing of each point so quickly that it was a great pleasure to discuss things with him. His only drawback was that he was, and of necessity must be, so much in with the Jews that he takes their point of view to a certain extent. This of course is natural, but seeing that most of the Jews in this country are thoroughly pro-German, and a very large number of them are anti-J. P. Morgan & Co., it would have been desirable if he had not had quite so close affiliations with them.[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265411)
> 瑞丁勋爵给我留下了极其深刻的印象。他思路清晰，对每个要点的意义都能迅速把握，与他交谈真是一大乐事。他唯一的不足是，他与犹太人交往过密——而且出于必要也必然如此——以至于在一定程度上会采纳他们的观点。这当然可以理解，但鉴于这个国家的大多数犹太人都彻底亲德，而且其中很大一部分人反对 J. P. 摩根公司，如果他没与他们有如此密切的关联就好了。 [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265411)

It was a curious letter. Lord Reading’s position as head of the loan mission should have dispelled any doubts as to his loyalty and dashed notions of a monolithic Jewish viewpoint; instead, Jack implausibly perceived some common denominator between Reading and German Jews. In fact, when Reading met Jacob Schiff, the latter laid down a suicidal precondition for Kuhn, Loeb participation in the loan—that not one penny could go to England’s ally, Russia. Reading bluntly replied that “no government could accept conditions which discriminated against one of its allies in war.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265616) In one stroke, Kuhn, Loeb became *persona non grata* in London finance, further clearing the way for the Morgan triumphal march.
这封信颇为蹊跷。按理说，雷丁勋爵身为贷款使团团长，本应足以打消任何对他忠诚度的质疑，并粉碎所谓犹太群体立场一致的臆断；然而杰克却不可思议地认为，雷丁与德国犹太人之间存在某种共同点。事实上，当雷丁会见雅各布·希夫时，后者为库恩勒布公司参与贷款设置了一个自杀式的前提条件——不能有一分钱流向英国的盟友俄国。雷丁直截了当地回复道，"没有哪个政府能接受在战争中歧视其盟友的条件。" 自此以后，库恩勒布公司在伦敦金融界便成了不受欢迎的人，从而为摩根公司的凯旋进一步扫清了道路。 [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265616)

Still more damaging was the controversy at Goldman, Sachs, where partners exercised vetoes in important matters. Loyal to Germany, Henry Goldman refused to share in the Morgan-sponsored issue, provoking a crisis at the firm and causing its voluntary exile from wartime finance on Wall Street. According to Stephen Birmingham, when the “Kleinwort bank in London cabled to New York to say that Goldman, Sachs was in danger of being blacklisted in England,” Henry Goldman was forced to resign from the family firm.[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265790) Feelings ran so high that Goldman and Philip Lehman, dubbed Wall Street’s “hottest underwriting team,” stopped speaking to each other. For a generation, Jewish banks on Wall Street were handicapped by their affiliation with Germany.
更具破坏性的是高盛内部的争议，合伙人会在重大事项上行使否决权。出于对德国的忠诚，亨利·高德曼拒绝参与摩根主导的债券发行，这在高盛内部引发危机，并导致该公司主动退出华尔街的战时金融业务。据斯蒂芬·伯明翰所述，当“伦敦的克莱恩沃特银行发电报到纽约，警告高盛面临被英国列入黑名单的风险”时，亨利·高德曼被迫从家族公司辞职。 [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265790) 双方情绪高涨，高德曼与被称为华尔街“最火爆承销团队”的菲利普·莱曼从此互不理睬。此后整整一代人的时间里，华尔街的犹太银行都因与德国的关联而举步维艰。

The \$500-million Anglo-French loan was far larger than any bond issue orchestrated by Pierpont. Sixty-one underwriters and 1,570 financial institutions marketed the bonds. (The House of Morgan resented not being appointed sole agent responsible for paying the bond’s interest.) It was an extremely tough selling job, especially in isolationist sections of the Midwest. To sweeten the deal, participating banks were allowed to keep some of the money that they raised on deposit for a while. It was also widely advertised that the money would be spent only in America. Despite these inducements, only one major bank in Chicago—where pro-German depositors threatened a boycott—joined the syndicate, and there were none from Milwaukee. The Morgan partners signed up many famous individuals, including Andrew Carnegie and even Samuel Untermyer of Pujo fame, as well as suppliers of war materiel such as the Guggenheim brothers and Charles Schwab of Bethlehem Steel, who felt obliged to safeguard their thriving war business. But they couldn’t offset the poor midwestern performance, and the syndicate was stuck with \$187 million in unsold bonds by year’s end.
这笔 5 亿美元的英法贷款规模远超皮尔庞特经手的任何债券发行。共有 61 家承销商和 1570 家金融机构参与债券销售。（摩根家族对未能被指定为唯一负责支付债券利息的代理机构心存不满。）销售工作异常艰难，尤其是在中西部孤立主义情绪浓厚的地区。为了促成这笔交易，参与银行被允许暂时保留部分筹集的存款。同时，广泛宣传这笔资金将只用于美国国内。尽管有这些诱人条件，芝加哥只有一家主要银行加入了银团——当地亲德储户曾威胁抵制——而密尔沃基则无一家银行参与。 摩根合伙人签下了许多知名人物，包括安德鲁·卡内基，甚至还有以普约调查闻名的塞缪尔·昂特迈耶，以及战争物资供应商，如古根海姆兄弟和伯利恒钢铁公司的查尔斯·施瓦布，他们觉得有必要保护自己蒸蒸日上的战争生意。但这些都无法弥补中西部地区的惨淡表现，到年底，这个银团手里积压了价值 1.87 亿美元无人认购的债券。

To raise additional dollars, the British levied a tax on any dividends received from American shares, and British citizens rushed to give their shares to the government. So many securities were tendered that the Bank of England Court Room was heaped high with certificates. Morgans liquidated \$3 billion of these securities, delicately feeding them into the New York market so as to prevent a collapse in share prices.
为了筹集更多资金，英国政府对从美国股票中获得的任何股息征收税款，英国公民纷纷争相将股票交给政府。涌入的证券数量之多，以至于英格兰银行法庭里堆满了证书。摩根公司将这些证券变现了 30 亿美元，小心翼翼地逐步投放纽约市场，以防股价崩盘。

The Anglo-French loan was soon exhausted. Before the war ended, the House of Morgan had arranged over \$1.5 billion in Allied credits. The British would lavish many encomia on the Morgan role before U.S. entry into the war. In Morgan Grenfell’s Tea Room hangs a Lloyd George letter of 1917 that says, in part, “We were fortunate enough to secure the assistance of a firm which have throughout done everything in its power to protect the interests of the British Government.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265925) Visiting the Corner years later, Lord Northcliffe, the British press baron, exclaimed, “The war was won within these walls.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266101) Lord Moulton, head of the British Munitions Board, said that Du Pont, Bethlehem Steel, and J. P. Morgan and Company had rescued the French and British armies in 1915.
英法贷款很快便告罄。战争结束前，摩根财团已为协约国安排超过 15 亿美元的信贷。在美国参战之前，英国人对摩根的作用不吝溢美之词。摩根建富银行的茶室里悬挂着 1917 年劳合·乔治的信函，其中写道：“我们有幸获得一家公司的协助，这家公司始终竭尽全力维护英国政府的利益。” [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2265925) 多年后，英国报业大亨诺斯克利夫勋爵造访“角楼”时感叹道：“这场战争就是在这四壁之内赢得的。” [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266101) 英国军需委员会主席莫尔顿勋爵表示，杜邦、伯利恒钢铁公司以及 J.P.摩根公司在 1915 年拯救了法英两国军队。

Yet, as was always true of Morgan relations with Britain, the public embrace concealed a fair degree of tension. The British often felt the bank bungled its political role, however well it handled the financial side. Arthur WilLer, the London *Times* correspondent in Washington, described the House of Morgan in 1916: “The most unpopular house in the country, the personification for the radical West of the malign money power of Wall Street, it has done nothing to propitiate either the people or the politicians.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266284) That year, Jack campaigned for the Republican presidential candidate, Charles Evans Hughes—which the British thought unwise. Jack and Harry Davison also treated the new Federal Reserve Board in a somewhat high-handed manner. Davison, in particular, seemed to offend the British. He had a brash, decisive manner that inspired subordinates but could be clumsy and arrogant. The Foreign Office called him “injudicious,” while Ambassador Spring-Rice said Davison had “all the aggressiveness of the older Morgan without his genius.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266494)
然而，摩根与英国的关系向来如此——公开的拥抱掩盖了相当程度的紧张。英国人常觉得，摩根大通即便在金融事务上处理得再妥当，也总把政治角色搞砸。1916 年，伦敦《泰晤士报》驻华盛顿记者阿瑟·威勒这样描述摩根财团：“这是全美最不得人心的商行，对激进的西部而言，它是华尔街邪恶金钱势力的化身，却从未做过任何事来安抚民众或政客。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266284) 那年，杰克为共和党总统候选人查尔斯·埃文斯·休斯助选——英国人认为此举实属不智。杰克与哈里·戴维森对待新成立的美联储，态度也有些专横。尤其是戴维森，他似乎特别得罪了英国人。他行事果决甚至鲁莽，虽能激励下属，却也难免显得笨拙傲慢。 外交部称他“缺乏判断力”，而大使斯普林-赖斯则评价戴维森“有着老摩根的全部侵略性，却没有他的天赋。” [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266494)

Davison either mishandled his relationship with Willard Straight or decided that the romantic, impetuous Straight would just never fit in at Morgans. Straight expected to help negotiate with the Anglo-French loan. “I thought I might have been of service in connection with these negotiations, but I was asked to perform no work, and this rankled,” he said.[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266628) He was given little responsibility, and the august House of Morgan didn’t share his interest in poor countries. That September, at age thirty-four, he resigned from the bank. He had never translated his precocious China success into a mundane Wall Street setting and was offended that he hadn’t become a Morgan partner. He preferred polo, golf, and his outside literary interests to the consuming dedication that was *de rigueur* at 23 Wall. Shortly after wartime service, in 1918, he died of influenza and pneumonia. His widow, Dorothy, would help to found the New School for Social Research in New York and Dartington Hall, an experimental school in South Devon, England.
戴维森要么在处理与威拉德·斯特雷特的关系时出了差错，要么就是认定这位浪漫冲动、性情急躁的斯特雷特根本不适合摩根银行。斯特雷特原本期待能参与英法贷款的谈判工作。“我原以为自己在这些谈判中能派上用场，但他们却吩咐我不用做什么事，这让我耿耿于怀。”他说道。 [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266628) 他几乎没什么实权，而声望显赫的摩根银行对他那些关于贫困国家的兴趣也毫无共鸣。那年九月，年仅三十四岁的他毅然从银行辞职。他未能在平凡的华尔街环境中延续早年在中国的成功辉煌，而未能成为摩根合伙人更是让他深感屈辱。比起华尔街 23 号所必需的那种全身心投入的敬业精神，他更偏爱马球、高尔夫以及圈子之外的文学爱好。战后不久，1918 年，他因流感和肺炎去世。 他的遗孀多萝西（Dorothy）后来协助创办了纽约社会研究新学院（New School for Social Research），以及位于英格兰南德文郡的实验学校达廷顿学堂（Dartington Hall）。

By 1917, British credit was practically exhausted. Their salvation was German resumption of unrestricted submarine warfare against American shipping. When the United States entered the war, on April 6, 1917, Washington immediately granted the Allies \$1 billion in credit, lifting the onus from J. P. Morgan and Company. After the United States entered the war, the House of Morgan expected to be repaid a \$400-million loan to Britain from the proceeds of the first Liberty Loan drive. But Treasury Secretary McAdoo feared Congress would be upset if government money went to that old Democratic bogeyman—the Money Trust. To the amazement of Morgan partners, the British government didn’t seem bothered by the double cross. In his journal, Teddy Grenfell noted wounded feelings among the Morgan partners: “Although JPM &. Co. had placed all their resources monetary and otherwise at the disposal of the British Government, the ministers especially finance showed little appreciation. . . . The Morgan houses felt very bitterly not only that no appreciation was shown of their services but also that as soon as the Government had got all their monies which Morgan & Co. could lend or borrow from friends for England, that the British Treasury intentionally kept all information from them.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266791)
到 1917 年，英国的信贷几乎耗尽。唯一的救命稻草，是德国恢复对美国船只的无限制潜艇战。当美国于 1917 年 4 月 6 日参战后，华盛顿立即向协约国提供了 10 亿美元的信贷，解除了 J. P. 摩根公司的重负。美国参战后，摩根家族原本指望英国能用第一次自由公债的收益偿还其 4 亿美元的贷款。但财政部长麦卡杜担心，如果政府资金流向那个让民主党深恶痛绝的宿敌——"货币信托"，国会可能会感到不安。令摩根合伙人惊讶的是，英国政府似乎对这次"背信弃义"并不在意。泰迪·格伦费尔在日记中记录下摩根合伙人的受伤情绪："尽管 JPM 公司已将其所有货币及其他资源交由英国政府支配，但大臣们尤其是财政大臣却并未表现出多少感激之情……" 摩根家族感到非常不满，不仅是因为他们的贡献未得到认可，更因为当英国政府从摩根公司及其从朋友处借来的资金中获取全部款项后，英国财政部却故意对他们隐瞒所有信息。“ [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2266791) ”

During the summer of 1917, Lord Cunliffe, the abrasive, despotic governor of the Bank of England, argued the Morgan case against the less sympathetic chancellor of the Exchequer, Bonar Law. It formed part of a larger struggle between the bank and the Treasury for control of British financial policy. The row became so vitriolic that Prime Minister Lloyd George threatened to nationalize the Bank of England. On July 4, Grenfell was summoned to a Cabinet meeting at 10 Downing Street, and Lloyd George angrily asked him why the House of Morgan was making such a fuss. (Grenfell referred to Lloyd George as “our little Welsh goat.”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267007)) In the end, the British Treasury, incensed at Cunliffe’s behavior, spiked his reelection as governor in 1918. This paved the way not for a Morgan foe but for Montagu Norman, who took over the bank in 1920 and proved the most influential British ally in Morgan history.
1917 年夏天，英格兰银行那位专横跋扈的行长坎利夫勋爵，与态度同样强硬的财政大臣博纳·劳就摩根案展开了激烈交锋。这场争执其实是央行与财政部争夺英国金融政策主导权这场大博弈的一部分。双方针锋相对，甚至气得首相劳合·乔治扬言要将英格兰银行国有化。7 月 4 日，格伦费尔被召至唐宁街 10 号参加内阁会议，劳合·乔治怒气冲冲地质问他摩根财团为何如此小题大做。（格伦费尔私下称劳合·乔治为“我们那位威尔士小山羊”。）最终，英国财政部对坎利夫的行径忍无可忍，在 1918 年打破了他的连任计划。这为摩根财团的死对头——蒙塔古·诺曼——铺平了道路。他于 1920 年执掌英格兰银行，并成为摩根历史上最具影响力的英国盟友。

When the United States declared war, Jack was jubilant. With naive, patriotic generosity, he told President Wilson he could transfer the Export Department intact to Washington. He was ready to give Stet-tinius a leave of absence, cover staff salaries for a time, and forgo commissions. It didn’t dawn on him that this was politically impossible. Isolationists continued to accuse the House of Morgan of whipping up pro-war sentiment. And traveling across America, Treasury Secretary McAdoo noted intense ill will toward the house for having profited from the munitions purchases.
美国宣战时，杰克欣喜若狂。怀着天真的爱国热忱，他告诉威尔逊总统，自己可以将出口部门完整移交华盛顿。他愿意让斯泰蒂纽斯休假，暂时承担员工薪资，并放弃佣金。他完全没意识到这在政治上行不通。孤立主义者继续指责摩根集团煽动主战情绪。而财政部长麦卡杜在周游全美时注意到，公众对该公司从军火采购中获利怀有强烈的敌意。

To head the powerful new War Industries Board, Wilson chose Daniel Willard of the Baltimore and Ohio Railroad and then Democratic party stalwart, Bernard Baruch; to appease Morgans, he made Stettinius surveyor general of supplies for the U.S. Army. Only half-comically, Baruch confessed relief that Pierpont had spurned his offer of help during the 1907 panic, for had it been accepted, it might have jeopardized his political prospects under Wilson. There was now a political stigma attached to Morgan partners. White House aides noted that President Wilson frowned when he saw Dwight Morrow’s name on a list of prospective appointees. Although he did appoint him, to the Allied Maritime Council, he said firmly, “We mustn’t have any more of those men.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267206) Morrow would, in fact, become an important civilian adviser to General Pershing at Chaumont. Harry Davison, upon being named head of the Red Cross War Council, expected to assume full powers. When he then clashed with Red Cross organizer Mabel Boardman, former president William Howard Taft went to the White House to mediate. Though siding with Davison, Wilson told Taft that “New York bankers liked unrestricted powers, that they had been used to it in their business . . . but that in such a matter it was not wise.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267353)
为领导这个权力强大的新战时工业委员会，威尔逊选择了巴尔的摩与俄亥俄铁路公司的丹尼尔·威拉德以及民主党坚定分子伯纳德·巴鲁克；为了安抚摩根家族，他任命斯泰丁纽斯为美军军需物资总监。巴鲁克半开玩笑地坦言，当初皮尔庞特在 1907 年恐慌期间拒绝了他的援助提议，他反倒松了口气——因为一旦接受，可能会危及他在威尔逊政府下的政治前途。如今，摩根合伙人已成为政治上的污点。白宫助手注意到，当威尔逊总统看到德怀特·莫罗出现在拟任命名单上时，不禁皱起了眉头。尽管他最终还是任命了莫罗为盟国海事委员会成员，但态度坚决地说：“我们不能再要那些人了。” [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267206) 事实上，莫罗后来成了潘兴将军在肖蒙的重要文职顾问。而哈里·戴维森在担任红十字会战争委员会主席时，原本期望能获得全权。 当他与红十字会组织者梅布尔·博德曼发生冲突时，前总统威廉·霍华德·塔夫脱前往白宫进行调解。尽管威尔逊站在戴维森一边，但他告诉塔夫脱，“纽约银行家喜欢不受限制的权力，他们在自己的业务中已经习惯了这一点……但在这种事情上，这样做并不明智。”” [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267353)

From the standpoint of later Wall Street history, the government’s wartime Liberty Loan drives have an important place. The United States sold nearly \$17 billion in Liberty Bonds. The spirited promotional campaign brought Charlie Chaplin and Douglas Fairbanks, Sr., to rallies at the Corner. Treasury Secretary McAdoo wanted to reach small farmers, businessmen, and workers and thus created a new generation of American investors. One bureaucratic genius of the campaign was a Wall Street lawyer, Russell C. Leffingwell, who had been a neighbor of McAdoo in Yonkers, New York. McAdoo made him counsel and then assistant treasury secretary in charge of the Liberty Bond drives. He would later be a famous Morgan partner and a critical link with the Democratic party.
从后来华尔街历史的角度看，政府战时的自由贷款运动占据重要地位。美国售出了近 170 亿美元的自由债券。这场声势浩大的促销活动吸引了查理·卓别林和道格拉斯·范朋克（老）来到华尔街转角处参加集会。财政部长麦卡杜希望触及小农场主、商人和工人，从而造就了一批新一代的美国投资者。这场运动中的一位行政天才就是华尔街律师拉塞尔·C·莱芬韦尔，他曾是麦卡杜在纽约扬克斯的邻居。麦卡杜任命他为顾问，随后又让他担任助理财政部长，负责自由债券的推销工作。后来他成为摩根公司的著名合伙人，也是与民主党的重要纽带。

The House of Morgan emerged from the war with greatly enlarged power. For Jack Morgan, so widely discounted when he took over in 1913, there was a sense of psychic relief, a knowledge that he had measured up to his father. He told Paris partner Herman Harjes, “I am glad to say that our firm stands, as it always has stood, in the middle of things. . . . I feel that I am able in a measure to take Father’s place in the community and help out in many ways.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267504) As a young man in London, it had amused him when Lloyd’s took out a \$2-million insurance policy on Pierpont’s life. Now he shattered all records by taking out a \$2.5-million policy on his own.
摩根家族在战后权势大增。对 1913 年接手时备受质疑的杰克·摩根而言，这让他如释重负——他终于证明了自己不逊于父亲。他告诉巴黎合伙人赫尔曼·哈耶斯：“我很欣慰，我们的公司一如既往地立于潮头……我感觉自己某种程度上接过了父亲在社会上的位置，能在多个领域发挥作用。” [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267504) 年轻时在伦敦，劳合社为皮尔庞特投保 200 万美元寿险曾让他觉得有趣。如今他为自己投保 250 万美元，一举打破所有纪录。

But Jack’s hypersensitive nature was such that he seemed more disturbed by criticism than gratified by success. After Wilson rejected this offer of the Export Department, he sulked and licked his wounds. He was a man of fundamentally incompatible desires, who wished to be fabulously rich *and* loved; useful *and* appreciated; not only famous but understood fairly by the masses. He had a way of magnifying enemies. Even as he emerged as the world’s best-known banker, he still felt embattled. As he wrote in 1917:
但杰克生性过于敏感，似乎批评比成功更能让他心烦意乱。在威尔逊拒绝了他提出的出口部职位后，他闷闷不乐，独自舔舐伤口。他内心深处充满了相互矛盾的渴望：既想富甲天下，又想被人爱戴；既想有所作为，又想获得赏识；不仅要名扬四海，还要被大众真正理解。他总爱把敌人想象得无比强大。即使他已成为全球最知名的银行家，依然觉得自己四面楚歌。正如他在 1917 年所写：

> I have come to the conclusion that the chief reason of the dislike that exists in Washington for J.P. Morgan & Co. . . . originates in the fact that we ask for no favors, that the Democratic party has tried its best to cripple us in every way it could, that they had Steel investigations, Pujo investigations and Clayton bills and all that sort of thing, devised and directed with the intention of making life impossible for us—and still we have gone ahead and got along pretty well . . . the whole feeling against us is really a political grudge, and they cannot change our feeling, nor can we change theirs.[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267705)
> 我得出结论，华盛顿对 J.P. Morgan & Co.反感的主要原因……其实是因为我们从不乞求恩惠，民主党想尽一切办法打击我们，他们搞了钢铁调查、普乔调查、克莱顿法案等等，这些手段都是针对我们而来，想让我们的日子过不下去——可我们依然挺过来了，而且过得相当不错……他们对我们的敌意，说到底就是政治上的怨恨，他们改变不了对我们的看法，我们也改变不了对他们的看法。 [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267705)

Another perspective on Morgan power came later from Sir Harold Nicolson in his biography of Dwight Morrow. Nicolson had written that at the outbreak of the war, the House of Morgan “ceased to be a private firm and became almost a Department of government”—which he meant as a mighty compliment.[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267902) Yet Jack thought it insulting to liken his bank to the government. “I have no right to ask you to alter this,” Jack wrote to Nicolson upon reading it in draft form, “but it would be interpreted as if we were reduced to the status of a department subordinate to the Government.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268071) The House of Morgan no longer thought itself subordinate to anyone, not even Washington.
对摩根权力的另一种解读，后来出自哈罗德·尼科尔森爵士为德怀特·莫罗所写的传记。尼科尔森曾写道，战争爆发时，摩根银行“不再是一家私人公司，几乎成了政府的一个部门”——他本意是将其视为莫大的赞誉。 [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2267902) 然而杰克却认为，把自己的银行比作政府是一种侮辱。“我无权要求你修改这句话，”杰克在读到底稿后写信给尼科尔森，“但人们会解读成我们沦为了政府的一个下属部门。” [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2268071) 摩根银行已不再认为自己是任何人的附庸，连华盛顿也不例外。
