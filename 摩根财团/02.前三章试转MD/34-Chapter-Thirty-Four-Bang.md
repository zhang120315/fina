# Chapter Thirty-Four: Bang

IN late 1986, Morgan Grenfell presented a curious study in contrasts. Outwardly it maintained a sedate air. Upholding a tradition of nearly 150 years, the bank posted no nameplate outside—the old brass plaques hung in the reception area—and the interior paid homage to the past. In the thickly carpeted, arched hallways, there were prints of Saint Paul’s and the Bank of England, of hansom cabs rolling through gaslit streets in a *fin de siècle* City. Morgan Grenfell was said to have the last man in the City to wear a bowler hat—one Julian Stamford. It still seemed a civilized place.
1986 年末，摩根格兰弗呈现出一种奇妙的对比。表面上，它保持着沉稳矜持的氛围。秉承近 150 年的传统，银行门外并未悬挂招牌——旧铜牌挂在接待区内——而内部装潢则向历史致敬。铺着厚地毯的拱形走廊里，挂着圣保罗大教堂和英格兰银行的版画，描绘着汉瑟姆马车在煤气灯照耀的街道上穿行的世纪末伦敦金融城景象。据说摩根格兰弗是金融城里最后一个还有人戴圆顶礼帽的地方——那人叫朱利安·斯坦福德。它仍仿佛是一个文明优雅的所在。

But the calm was deceptive. Morgan Grenfell had charted a course roughly parallel to Morgan Stanley’s, dispensing with politeness and becoming a tough, aggressive firm. For twenty years, it had revolted against its sleepy past. Starting with the American Tobacco fight for Gallaher in the late 1960s, it had enjoyed testing the rules. “They liked the image of being the most buccaneering firm in the City, of pushing out the boundaries,” observed an ex-merger specialist with the firm. “Every deal was a little more provocative and cheeky and less British than the one before.”
但这种平静只是表象。摩根格林菲尔（Morgan Grenfell）选择了与摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）几乎平行的发展轨迹，摒弃了温文尔雅，摇身一变成为一家强硬、进取的公司。二十年来，它一直反抗着自己曾经沉闷的过去。从 20 世纪 60 年代末美国烟草公司（American Tobacco）对加拉赫（Gallaher）的收购战开始，它就热衷于挑战规则。“他们喜欢将自己塑造成伦敦金融城里最具海盗气质的公司，一个不断突破界限的形象，”一位曾在该公司任职的并购专家评论道。“每一笔交易都比前一笔更大胆、更厚脸皮、更不英国。”

Morgan Grenfell’s two leaders in the early 1980s reflected both the old and the new City. Chairman Bill Mackworth-Young had spent twenty-one years with the aristocratic brokerage firm of Rowe and Pitman, second in prestige only to Cazenove. If Morgan Grenfell wasn’t particularly well known for brilliant executives, the bookish Mackworth-Young gave the firm intellectual cachet. He had been a star Eton pupil, along with his classmate Robin Leigh-Pemberton, later governor of the Bank of England. The son of an English civil servant and archaeologist in India and married to an earl’s daughter, Mackworth-Young was a good salesman and a charming after-dinner speaker. He was especially adroit with Americans, having gone yearly to Bohemian Grove in California, the rustic stag camp for American power brokers. A warm, stocky man with a ready, avuncular smile, he lacked malevolence and was universally popular.
20 世纪 80 年代初，摩根建富的两位掌舵人恰好映射出伦敦金融城新旧交融的面貌。董事长比尔·麦克斯沃斯-杨曾在贵族经纪商罗·皮特曼公司效力二十一年，这家公司的声望仅次于卡泽诺夫。如果说摩根建富并非以明星高管团队著称，那么书卷气十足的麦克斯沃斯-杨则为公司注入了智识底蕴。他曾是伊顿公学的优等生，与后来担任英格兰银行行长的同窗罗宾·利-彭伯顿齐名。作为英国驻印度公务员兼考古学家的儿子，又娶了伯爵之女为妻，麦克斯沃斯-杨既是出色的推销员，也是餐后演讲的魅力担当。尤其擅长与美国人周旋——他每年都前往加州波西米亚丛林，那个专为美国权势人物打造的乡间男性营地。他身材敦实、待人热忱，脸上总挂着慈父般的笑容，毫无恶意，人缘极佳。

A heavy smoker, Mackworth-Young died abruptly of lung cancer in 1984. Later, the City would ritually repeat that had he lived, Morgan Grenfell would have been spared the Guinness scandal. According to a rival executive, “Mackworth-Young would have given free rein to the takeover *prima donnas*, but he would have been quite aware of what was happening. He would have taken a longer term view of how the business should be conducted.” When things later went awry at Morgan Grenfell, Mackworth-Young only grew more saintly in memory.
麦夸特-杨是个重度烟民，1984 年因肺癌猝然离世。后来伦敦金融城总爱念叨：要是他还活着，摩根·格林菲尔也许就能躲过吉尼斯丑闻了。一位竞争对手的高管曾评价说："他会放手让那些收购界的明星尽情发挥，但也完全清楚局势的走向。他对业务运作方式会有更长远的考量。"后来摩根·格林菲尔出了岔子，麦夸特-杨在人们记忆中的形象反而愈发圣洁了。

Morgan Grenfell’s chief executive and deputy chairman was Christopher R. Reeves, who epitomized the new, self-made City executive. A graduate of Malvern College, he worked at the Bank of England and the merchant bank of Hill Samuel before Sir John Stevens recruited him to Morgan Grenfell in 1968. He belonged to the first generation of Morgan Grenfell executives who didn’t come from the old families and hadn’t apprenticed at the Morgan bank in New York. Slim and blond with a chiseled, angular face, he had a photogenic grin and a look of gritty determination. In a firm once faintly embarrassed about seeking new business, Reeves didn’t mind the hard sell. Tough and adept, he was enigmatic to his subordinates. “He was a superb presence but a bit of a sphinx,” a former corporate finance director recalled. “Silence was his partner.” He was drawn to the tough tactics of “corporate finance”—a term that in England signifies takeover work.
摩根建富的首席执行官兼副董事长克里斯托弗·R·里夫斯，堪称新一代白手起家的伦敦金融城高管典范。他毕业于马尔文学院，曾在英格兰银行和商业银行 Hill Samuel 任职，后于 1968 年被约翰·史蒂文斯爵士招募至摩根建富。他属于摩根建富第一代非世家出身、也未曾赴纽约摩根银行学徒的高管。他身材清瘦、金发碧眼，脸庞棱角分明，笑容极具镜头感，眼神中透着一股坚韧。在一家曾对主动拓展业务略感难为情的公司里，里夫斯却毫不介意强势推销。他强硬而老练，在下属眼中颇具神秘感。“他气场强大，但有点像个斯芬克斯，”前企业融资总监回忆道，“沉默是他的伙伴。”他被“企业融资”——在英国指代收购业务——的强硬策略所吸引。

Neither Reeves nor Mackworth-Young was a master strategist. Unlike Morgan Guaranty or Morgan Stanley, Morgan Grenfell never operated by a blueprint or comprehensive vision of the financial future. Its history was devoid of planning conferences or retreats during which the firm could strategically reorient. Its postwar history had no Bob Baldwins or Lewis Bernards, no Henry Alexanders or Lew Prestons, and certainly no Siegmund Warburgs. Its moves seemed improvised, a snatching at sudden opportunities. To paraphrase Winston Churchill, it was a pudding without a theme, and this absence of a clear design would be its downfall. Both Reeves and Mackworth-Young ran a series of successful, often unrelated businesses without any single thread binding them together. By comparison, Morgan Guaranty and Morgan Stanley had a seamless quality, a smoothly coordinated approach to business that seemed to anticipate changes in financial markets.
里夫斯和麦克斯沃斯-扬都不是什么运筹帷幄的战略家。与摩根担保或摩根士丹利不同，摩根格伦菲尔从未按照某种蓝图或对金融未来的全面愿景来运作。其历史中缺少战略转型的规划会议或务虚会。二战后的发展历程里，没有鲍勃·鲍德温、刘易斯·伯纳德，也没有亨利·亚历山大、卢·普雷斯顿，当然更没有什么西格蒙德·沃伯格。它的每一步都像是即兴而为，逮着偶然的机会就扑上去。套用温斯顿·丘吉尔的话说，它就是一盘没有主题的布丁——而这种清晰设计的缺失，最终成了它的致命伤。里夫斯和麦克斯沃斯-扬先后经营了一系列成功却往往互不关联的业务，没有任何一条主线把它们串联起来。反观摩根担保和摩根士丹利，则呈现出浑然一体的气质，对业务有着协调顺畅的应对之道，仿佛总能预判金融市场的变化。

Morgan Grenfell had enjoyed some remarkable successes, which masked long-term problems. It managed money for the world’s two richest people—the sultan of Brunei and Queen Elizabeth II—and surpassed all other British banks in handling American pension funds. It specialized in international portfolios when many short-sighted asset managers were still mired in local markets. After years of torrid growth, by the 1987 crash it managed \$25 billion. This dwarfed America’s top asset manager among wholesale firms, Morgan Stanley, with its meager \$11 billion. At 23 Great Winchester, pension money for San Francisco, the state of California, Fort Worth, and the Rockefeller Foundation was handled.
摩根格伦菲尔曾取得过一些令人瞩目的成功，但这些成功掩盖了长期存在的问题。它不仅为全球最富有的两个人——文莱苏丹和伊丽莎白二世女王——管理资产，还在处理美国养老金基金方面超越了所有其他英国银行。当许多目光短浅的资产管理公司仍深陷本地市场时，它早已专注于国际投资组合。经历了多年的迅猛增长后，到 1987 年股市崩盘时，它管理的资产已达 250 亿美元，远超美国顶级批发机构摩根士丹利那区区 110 亿美元。在大温彻斯特街 23 号，来自旧金山、加州、沃斯堡以及洛克菲勒基金会的养老金资金都在此得到管理。

It was also distinguished in trade and project financing. Morgan Grenfell had led in financing North Sea oil and chalked up several energy triumphs, including the record \$1.6-billion financing for Woodside Petroleum’s natural gas project in Australia, the biggest such loan ever to hit the Euromarkets. It was also active in financing projects in the Soviet Union. And when other banks wrote off Africa in the 1970s as poor and hopelessly indebted, Morgan Grenfell established a business advising black African states. To please black Africa, it even ended most of its dealings with South Africa. Among the forty countries it advised outside of Europe were Sudan, Uganda, Tanzania, and Zambia.
摩根格林菲尔在贸易和项目融资领域同样表现卓越。它曾主导北海石油项目的融资，并在能源领域屡创佳绩，其中包括为伍德赛德石油公司在澳大利亚的天然气项目提供创纪录的 16 亿美元融资——这是欧洲货币市场上最大的一笔此类贷款。它还在苏联的项目融资中表现活跃。当其他银行在 20 世纪 70 年代将非洲视为贫困且债务深重的弃地时，摩根格林菲尔却建立起为非洲黑人国家提供咨询的业务。为了取悦黑非洲，它甚至终止了与南非的大部分交易。在其服务的欧洲以外的四十个国家中，包括苏丹、乌干达、坦桑尼亚和赞比亚。

Yet despite these accomplishments, Morgan Grenfell was vulnerable. Like other capital-short merchant banks, it was somewhat anachronistic in modern, global markets. Unlike Warburgs, it never graduated into the front ranks of the City’s Eurobond and foreign-exchange markets. In that larger City, capital was decisive, with cosy ties counting for little—the reason takeover work had been such a godsend to Morgan Grenfell. The firm had thrived only in the insular City of British work, which would be a dangerous shortcoming as the decade progressed.
然而，尽管成就斐然，摩根格伦费尔依然脆弱。与其他资本短缺的商业银行一样，它在现代全球化市场中显得有些不合时宜。与沃伯格家族不同，它从未跻身伦敦金融城欧洲债券和外汇市场的一线行列。在那个更广阔的金融城中，资本才是决定因素，亲密关系无足轻重——这也是为何收购业务对摩根格伦费尔而言如同天赐良机。这家公司只在不列颠本土业务的封闭金融城中繁荣过，而随着这十年逐渐推进，这一短板将变得危险。

The so-called Big Bang deregulation of October 1986 tore down the walls that had divided the two Cities since the Euromarkets emerged in the early 1960s. To guarantee London’s survival as a financial center, the Thatcher government decided to stop cosseting London banks and expose them to more domestic and foreign competition. Despite their evocative names, British merchant banks were tiny beside the new global conglomerates. Japan’s Nomura Securities, with a \$20-billion capitalization, *was forty times* the size of Morgan Grenfell. It could swallow all the merchant banks for breakfast. By opening the City’s gates to foreign firms, the British government ensured London’s survival as a financial center but not the survival of individual London houses. They would have to compete against American commercial banks, which wanted to build investment banking operations in London that they could then repatriate to the United States after Glass-Steagall tumbled. At the same time, the big British clearing banks—National Westminster, Midland, Barclays, and Lloyds—had begun to encroach on the traditional turf of the merchant banks.
所谓的 1986 年 10 月“大爆炸式放松管制”，拆除了自 20 世纪 60 年代初欧洲货币市场兴起以来将两个城市分隔的围墙。为了确保伦敦作为金融中心的生存，撒切尔政府决定不再娇惯伦敦的银行，而是让它们直面更多的国内和国外竞争。尽管名字听起来很有历史感，但英国的商业银行与新兴的全球巨头相比简直微不足道。日本野村证券（Nomura Securities）市值 200 亿美元，是摩根格伦费尔（Morgan Grenfell）的 40 倍，它可以轻松吞掉所有商业银行当早餐。通过向外国公司敞开伦敦城的大门，英国政府确保了伦敦作为金融中心的生存，但并未保证伦敦本土机构的存活。它们将不得不与美国商业银行竞争，这些银行希望在伦敦建立投行业务，以便在 Glass-Steagall 法案倒台后将业务搬回美国。 与此同时，英国的大型清算银行——国民西敏寺银行、米兰银行、巴克莱银行和劳埃德银行——开始蚕食传统上属于商人银行的业务领域。

In its specifics, Big Bang sounded innocuous. It ended the City’s antiquated fragmentation into bankers, brokers, and market makers, let foreign firms enter these areas, and scuttled fixed brokerage commissions. These measures collectively threw the formerly closed City wide open to competition. Christopher Reeves was aware of the carnage on Wall Street after fixed commissions ended in May 1975. He warned, “Greater risks will be run by those firms which do not adjust their business to exploiting the new opportunities.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435502) Oddly, Morgan Grenfell would be just such a laggard and would figure as a major casualty of Big Bang. In a failure of vision, it would move too gingerly, too indecisively, and would squander its chance to translate its eminence to an international sphere.
从具体内容来看，“大爆炸”改革听起来无伤大雅。它终结了伦敦金融城将银行家、经纪商和做市商割裂开来的陈旧格局，允许外国公司进入这些领域，并取消了固定的经纪佣金。这些举措共同使昔日封闭的金融城彻底向竞争敞开了大门。克里斯托弗·里夫斯深知，1975 年 5 月美国取消固定佣金后，华尔街曾一片血雨腥风。他警告说：“那些不调整业务以抓住新机遇的公司，将面临更大的风险。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435502) 奇怪的是，摩根建富恰恰就是这样一个落后者，并将成为“大爆炸”的主要牺牲品。由于缺乏远见，它行事过于谨慎、优柔寡断，最终浪费了将其显赫地位拓展至国际舞台的机会。

The little cottage industries of the City were swept away by Big Bang. Scores of small private partnerships, which had given the City its pleasantly Dickensian flavor, were taken over by world-devouring giants. At the same time, banker-company relations grew looser, more impersonal. As British corporate treasurers were besieged by foreign bankers, they got a new sense of what could be accomplished in global markets and were less content to rely on a single banker. Consolidations also meant sudden wealth for young deal makers and traders, whose salaries jumped as much as tenfold in a few years. Young bond traders were suddenly driving Ferraris and making six-figure salaries.
伦敦城里那些小作坊式的产业，在金融大爆炸的浪潮中被一扫而空。曾经让这座城市充满狄更斯笔下那种怡人风情的众多小型私营合伙企业，纷纷被那些吞并世界的巨头们收入囊中。与此同时，银行与企业之间的关系也变得越来越松散，越来越缺乏人情味。当英国企业的财务主管们被外国银行家们团团围住时，他们对于全球市场能达成怎样的成就有了全新的认知，也不再满足于只依赖一家银行。合并浪潮也让年轻的交易员和做市商一夜暴富，他们的薪酬在短短几年内飙升了十倍之多。年轻的债券交易员们突然开上了法拉利，拿着六位数的薪水。

The elite world of merchant banking faded as the rhythms of the trader speeded up City life. Long lunches at Boodle’s or White’s gave way to twelve-hour days. It was impossible to equip all the trading desks with old Etonians, and so the City became a more egalitarian place. Some people, of course, resisted the new ways. When the *Economist* tried to track down City executives, it discovered several absentees: “Many were sighted at the Wimbledon tennis tournament, the Henley regatta and the Ascot horse races.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435652) For the most part, however, the City was now a more hectic, grueling place, with people grabbing lunches at the fast-food restaurants and crowded sandwich shops scattered among the Wren churches and new office blocks. It grew in such helter-skelter style that it almost made Wall Street seem gracious by comparison.
精英银行家们的世界逐渐褪色，交易员们快节奏的生活重塑了伦敦金融城的生态。在布多尔或怀特餐厅享用悠长午餐的日子，被每天十二小时的工作所取代。曾经伊顿公学毕业生遍布交易大厅的景象已不复存在，金融城因此变得更加平等开放。当然，也有人抗拒这种新变化。《经济学人》试图寻找金融城高管们的身影时，发现不少人缺席：“许多人在温布尔登网球锦标赛、亨利皇家赛舟会和阿斯科特赛马会上被目击。”不过总体而言，如今的金融城已变成一个更忙碌、更疲惫的地方——人们在雷恩教堂与新建写字楼间穿梭，匆匆在快餐店或拥挤的三明治店里解决午餐。这里以如此杂乱无序的方式扩张，甚至让华尔街相比之下都显得优雅从容了。

Along with Warburgs and Kleinwort Benson, Morgan Grenfell had a chance to parlay past glory into modern power. Christopher Reeves welcomed Big Bang as an opportunity to form an integrated securities firm that could compete in world markets. This was a radical shift for Morgan Grenfell which had always shied away from the risks and capital investment associated with securities trading. Like Morgan Stanley in the 1950s, it had maintained an aristocratic distance from markets, drawing on brokers to price the securities it issued. Under the British underwriting system, the august “houses of issue,” such as Morgan Grenfell, didn’t put their capital directly at risk. They placed the issues with institutional underwriters, who provided a contingency backing for issues. Big Bang elicited predictions that “bought deals” would revolutionize London as they had New York after Rule 415. This meant that underwriting would suddenly require enormous piles of capital. Of the merchant banks, Warburgs responded with great clarity to Big Bang, while Morgan Grenfell vacillated and lagged fatally behind.
与沃伯格家族、克兰沃特·本森银行并肩的摩根格林菲尔，曾有机会将昔日荣光转化为现代影响力。克里斯托弗·里夫斯将"大爆炸"改革视为组建全能证券公司的良机，以参与全球市场竞争。这对向来规避证券交易风险与资本投入的摩根格林菲尔而言，堪称颠覆性转变。如同 20 世纪 50 年代的摩根士丹利，它始终与市场保持着贵族般的距离，依靠经纪商为其发行的证券定价。在英国承销体系下，摩根格林菲尔这类尊贵的"证券发行行"并不直接动用自有资本承担风险，而是将发行份额分配给机构承销商，由后者提供应急担保。这场金融改革催生了预测："买断交易"将像 415 条款重塑纽约那样彻底改变伦敦。这意味着承销业务将骤然需要海量资本支撑。 在投资银行中，Warburgs 对 Big Bang 反应明确，而 Morgan Grenfell 则摇摆不定，最终严重落后。

Morgan Grenfell’s bungling of Big Bang would do more lasting damage than the Guinness scandal. Early on, a split developed, mostly between the younger directors, who favored audacious, far-reaching action in response to the challenge, and the traditional directors, who were fearful of venturing into risky new businesses. In 1984, Morgan Grenfell was approached by the brokerage house of Rowe and Pitman, Mackworth-Young’s old firm, about a possible alliance. Afraid it would cost too much, 23 Great Winchester dithered and lost its chance; later Warburgs grabbed the firm, helping to make it preeminent among merchant banks in international markets. The most insistent foe of the Rowe and Pitman deal was evidently Graham Walsh, the takeover chief. “Walsh was *very* adamant against it,” a former colleague recalled angrily. “He said, ‘We’re doing beautifully as we are, we’re on the top of the pile, and it’s risky to do this.’ “ So the easy, seductive profits of takeovers clouded the firm’s strategic vision at a critical moment.
相比健力士丑闻，摩根建富在金融大爆炸中的失误将造成更持久的伤害。矛盾很早就已显现，主要是年轻董事与保守派之间的分歧：前者主张以大胆激进的举措应对变革，后者则对涉足高风险新业务心存畏惧。1984 年，麦金沃思曾任职的罗·皮特曼经纪公司向摩根建富抛出橄榄枝，提议结盟。但总部设在 23 大温彻斯特街的摩根建富因担心代价过高而犹豫不决，错失良机；后来沃伯格集团趁机吞并了这家经纪公司，由此奠定了其在跨国银行中全球市场的领先地位。据知情者回忆，对罗·皮特曼交易反对最坚决的显然是并购主管格雷厄姆·沃尔什。“他当时态度极其强硬，”一位前同事愤然说道，“他宣称‘我们现在风光无限，已是行业翘楚，冒这个险毫无必要。’””因此，在关键时刻，收购带来的轻松诱人的利润蒙蔽了公司的战略眼光。”

Morgan Grenfell frittered away other chances: it passed up the opportunity to buy Phillips and Drew and Wood Mackenzie; fumbled a chance to merge with Hoare, Govett by demanding majority control; and was blocked from taking over Exco, a financial services group, first by a Bank of England veto, then by its own indecision. It ended up doing Big Bang on the cheap, buying two firms that many thought booby prizes—the antiquated Pember and Boyle (a broker) and Pinchin Denny (a jobber, or market maker). Expressing a common judgment, an indignant former director said, “They hemmed and hawed and finally bought a cheap jobber and a cheap broker. They got the worst of all worlds.” As time has confirmed, the new financial order would tolerate global firms offering a comprehensive package of services or domestic boutiques specializing in a few functions. It would be pitiless toward those, such as Morgan Grenfell, stuck in the middle bracket.
摩根·格伦费尔还挥霍了其他机会：它放弃了收购菲利普斯与德鲁以及伍德·麦肯齐的机会；因要求控股而错失了与霍尔·戈维特合并的良机；收购金融服务集团 Exco 的计划先是遭到英格兰银行否决，后又因自身犹豫不决而受阻。最终，它以低成本参与了"大爆炸"改革，收购了两家被许多人视为"安慰奖"的公司——老旧的彭伯与博伊尔（一家经纪商）和平钦·丹尼（一家做市商）。一位愤愤不平的前董事表达了普遍看法："他们支支吾吾，最后只买了个廉价的做市商和廉价的经纪商，落得个最糟糕的下场。"正如时间所证明的，新的金融秩序要么容忍提供全方位服务的全球性公司，要么容忍专注于少数领域的本土精品店。对于那些像摩根·格伦费尔这样不上不下的公司，市场毫不留情。

Right before Big Bang, in June 1986, Morgans overcame historic reservations and sold shares to the public to amass capital for trading. It raised £154 million (\$229 million) and borrowed another £140 million (\$200 million). Although some jowly die-hards were petrified that shareholders might demand shorter lunches or even interfere with weekend shooting (God forbid), most accepted the grim necessity. The Bank of England had just chastised the firm for having inadequate capital to back its share purchases during the Guinness takeover of Distillers, and raiding was becoming a more capital-intensive art. Like its Wall Street counterparts, Morgan Grenfell might someday have to provide temporary “mezzanine” financing or even issue junk bonds in takeovers.
就在大爆炸前夕的 1986 年 6 月，摩根公司终于克服了历史性的顾虑，向公众出售股票以筹集交易资金。它募得 1.54 亿英镑（约合 2.29 亿美元），又借入 1.4 亿英镑（约合 2 亿美元）。尽管一些顽固的老派人物担心股东们会要求缩短午餐时间，甚至干预周末的狩猎活动（天理难容），但多数人还是无奈地接受了这一残酷现实。英格兰银行刚刚批评该公司在吉尼斯收购蒸馏酒商过程中资本金不足，而企业并购正日益成为资本密集型艺术。像华尔街同行一样，摩根建富有朝一日或许不得不在并购中提供临时性的“夹层”融资，甚至发行垃圾债券。

There were also unspoken reasons for going public. A former corporate finance director explained: “A principal reason was to have publicly quoted shares as acquisition currency. It didn’t work out that way.” Another speculated that it was a way to appease the firm’s takeover stars, Roger Seelig and George Magan: “I suspect they weren’t making enough money and needed an equity hit. They both had standing offers from American investment banks to pay them \$1 million to go to them—much more than they were getting from Morgan Grenfell.”
除此之外，上市背后还有一些未说出口的考量。一位前企业财务总监解释道：“主要目的之一是希望拥有公开交易的股票作为并购筹码，但结果并未如预期。”另一位人士则推测，这是为了安抚公司内部的收购明星 Roger Seelig 和 George Magan：“我猜他们当时赚得不够多，需要靠股权来提振。两家美国投行曾分别向他们开出 100 万美元的 offer 挖角——这远比他们在 Morgan Grenfell 挣得多。”

The confused and halfhearted way that Morgan Grenfell reacted to Big Bang reflected an unhealthy dependence on takeover work. The firm might boast that it offered thirty-two services, and it could point to twenty-two overseas offices, yet the heart of the operation was mergers. By Big Bang, the 120-person takeover unit was reportedly chipping in almost half the pretax profits in a firm of 2,000 people. As London’s premier takeover house, it was handling an awesome volume of business—some fifty-one deals in 1986 valued at almost £14 billion.
摩根建富对金融大爆炸那种迷茫而摇摆不定的反应，暴露出它对并购业务的畸形依赖。这家公司或许可以吹嘘自己提供三十二种服务，也能炫耀拥有二十二家海外办事处，但其业务核心始终是并购。据报道，到金融大爆炸时期，这家拥有两千名员工的公司里，仅有一百二十人的并购部门就贡献了将近一半的税前利润。作为伦敦首屈一指的并购机构，它处理的业务量相当惊人——1986 年完成了约五十一笔交易，总价值接近一百四十亿英镑。

Like Morgan Stanley, Morgan Grenfell usually took the offensive, earning a reputation as “the most aggressive agent in the City for the hostile bidder,” in *Euromoney’s* words.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435804) As a blue-blooded firm, it legitimated a new, swashbuckling style of finance. On Wall Street and in the City, traditionalists were appeased when a Morgan firm validated controversial practices. Aware of the mounting takeover frenzy in the United States, Morgan Grenfell was determined to show it could slug it out with any Yanks that came to London.
和摩根士丹利一样，摩根建富通常也采取攻势，被《欧洲货币》称为"伦敦金融城里最激进的敌意收购代理人"。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435804) 作为一家蓝筹公司，它为一种全新的、横冲直撞的金融风格正了名。在华尔街和伦敦金融城，当摩根系的公司认可了有争议的做法时，传统主义者们也就安心了。意识到美国并购狂潮愈演愈烈，摩根建富决心证明，它敢和任何来伦敦的美国人硬碰硬。

Mackworth-Young and Reeves presided with a light hand over their circus of takeover *prima donnas*. The Seeligs and the Magans had great power in the firm, for they captured new clients; the old taboo about poaching clients was fading. Morgan Grenfell had an individualistic culture very unlike the team spirit drilled into recruits at Morgan Guaranty, S. G. Warburg, and Goldman, Sachs. Not surprisingly, it encouraged a flamboyant, free-wheeling star system among its young professionals, who became recognizable figures in London, like pop stars. But such freedom, if conducive to inventive takeover work, could also induce a perilous euphoria, a giddy sense of invulnerability.
麦华扬和里夫斯以轻松的姿态驾驭着这场收购大腕们的狂欢。西利格家族和马根家族在公司内权势很大，因为他们能带来新客户；过去关于挖客户的老禁忌正在淡化。摩根建富的文化强调个人主义，与摩根担保、S.G.华宝、高盛等公司给新员工灌输的团队精神截然不同。毫不奇怪，这种文化在其年轻专业人士中鼓励一种浮夸、随性的明星制，他们像流行歌星一样成为伦敦的知名人物。但这种自由，虽然有利于创意的收购工作，也可能引发危险的亢奋——一种飘飘然的、自以为无懈可击的感觉。

The group’s superstar was Roger Seelig. In a more innocent age, his background might have fitted him for tamer pursuits. In 1971, after taking a degree from the London School of Economics and working at Esso, Seelig joined Morgan Grenfell. (That he was Jewish apparently mattered less in those days before the petrodollar boom.) He and his mother shared a three-story Gloucestershire mansion—a very grand, formal place topped by a row of balusters. It wasn’t far from the Prince and Princess of Wales’s Highgrove House. The bachelor Seelig’s girlfriend lived with him at his Marble Arch apartment, but he professed to be “too busy” to marry, as if matrimony might cost a valuable deal or two. He rode with the Beaufort hunt, belonged to the Royal Society of Arts, and was partial to official balls—not your usual corporate stalker.
这群人中的明星人物是罗杰·西利格。在更为纯真的年代，他的背景或许更适合他从事些温和的追求。1971 年，西利格从伦敦政治经济学院毕业后，先供职于埃索，随后加入了摩根建富。（在石油美元热潮兴起之前，他的犹太身份显然没那么重要。）他和母亲共同拥有一座位于格洛斯特郡的三层豪宅——那是一栋极其气派、正式的宅邸，顶端排列着一排栏杆。它离威尔士亲王与王妃的海格罗夫庄园不远。这位单身汉西利格的女友与他同住在他在大理石拱门的公寓里，但他自称“太忙”而无法结婚，仿佛婚姻可能会让他损失一笔宝贵的交易。他随博福特猎狐队骑马，是皇家艺术学会的成员，还偏爱官方舞会——这可不像你印象中那种典型的公司猎头。

At Morgan Grenfell, Seelig ran his own show and Reeves proudly touted him to clients as the most “entrepreneurial” of the takeover stars. Seelig made his own hours and served as his own boss. Carrying a mobile telephone, he would pop up at theaters or expensive nightclubs, Annabel’s in Berkeley Square being a favorite spot. He was a high-tech dandy, financial impresario, and gentleman masher. Speaking in a clipped, affected manner, he had a smugly theatrical smile, with lips pursed and corners drawn down, like a rogue in a Restoration comedy. His debonair ascot-and-handkerchief style transported a country-house ethos into the cutthroat takeover world.
在摩根建富，西利格独揽大权，里夫斯自豪地向客户吹嘘他是收购界最具“创业精神”的明星。西利格自己安排工作时间，自己做主。他随身携带手机，会突然出现在剧院或昂贵的夜总会，伯克利广场的安娜贝尔酒吧是他钟爱的去处。他是个高科技派的花花公子、金融界大亨，同时也是位优雅的浪荡子。他说话时带着一种利落而做作的腔调，脸上露出自鸣得意的戏剧化微笑，嘴唇紧抿，嘴角下拉，活像是王政复辟喜剧中的恶棍。他那温文尔雅的阿斯科特领巾与手帕风格，将乡间庄园的气息带入了残酷的收购世界。

One former corporate finance director remembers him thus: “Roger loved to fly his spinnaker. He wore suits nipped heavily at the waist and had a Beau Brummel stride, with his chest thrown out. It was a metaphor for his personality. He liked to ‘mingle’ with grand people, like Jacob Rothschild or Henry Kravis. He was a talented banker, but his manner masked an inferiority complex.” Earning £250,000 annually for snuffing out targets, Seelig was London’s top deal maker. He was especially good at latching onto acquisition-hungry firms, such as the giant retailer Storehouse PLC, which owned the Habitat home-furnishing stores in England and Conran’s in the United States; Storehouse was chaired by Seelig’s friend Sir Terence Conran. With sixteen professionals in Morgan Grenfell’s takeover department, Seelig reportedly hauled in a quarter of the profits, encouraging management to view his tactics benignly and allow him considerable leeway. He developed an attitude that takeover rules were silly hindrances to be tested by clever financiers. In 1985, he boasted that rivals “may just be reading the rules. We *changed* most of the rules.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435952)
一位前企业财务总监对他记忆犹新：“罗杰喜欢扬帆疾行。他穿的西装腰部收得很紧，走起路来像花花公子博·布鲁梅尔那样挺胸阔步。这正是他性格的写照。他喜欢和雅各布·罗斯柴尔德或亨利·克拉维斯这样的显赫人物‘交往’。他是个才华横溢的银行家，但这种姿态掩盖了他内心的自卑感。”西利格靠协助客户消灭目标企业，每年赚取 25 万英镑，是伦敦顶级交易撮合者。他尤其擅长锁定那些并购欲望强烈的公司，比如大型零售商 Storehouse PLC——这家公司旗下拥有英国的 Habitat 家居用品店和美国的 Conran's；Storehouse 的董事长正是西利格的朋友特伦斯·康兰爵士。据报道，摩根格伦费尔收购部门的 16 名专业人员中，西利格一人就贡献了四分之一的利润，这让管理层对他的策略持宽容态度，并赋予他相当大的自主权。 他开始认为，收购规则不过是愚蠢的障碍，只会被精明的金融家用来挑战。1985 年，他夸口道，竞争对手“可能只是在照本宣科。而我们改写了大部分规则。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2435952)

Mirroring Morgan Grenfell’s split personality, the other takeover star was George Magan, a short, sharp-faced man with glasses. His manner was entirely different from Seelig’s. The offspring of an old Irish family, an accomplished pianist with a dry, delightful wit, Magan was popular with his colleagues. He was nicknamed Teddy, short for teddy boy, because of his slicked-back hair and slick suits. Though unlike Seelig in other ways, Magan also gloried in aggressive tactics and talked of “using every inch of the playing surface.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436118) Ubiquitous on London’s takeover scene, he was involved in six of Britain’s top ten deals in 1985.
与摩根建富的双重性格相呼应，另一位收购明星是乔治·马根（George Magan），他个子矮小，脸庞棱角分明，戴着眼镜。他的作风与西利格截然不同。出身于古老的爱尔兰家族，是一位造诣颇深的钢琴家，谈吐风趣而犀利，马根深受同事欢迎。因为喜欢梳着油头、穿着考究西装，他得了个外号“泰迪”（泰迪男孩的简称）。尽管在其他方面与西利格不同，马根同样热衷于激进的策略，并常说“要利用好场地的每一寸面积。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436118) 在伦敦的收购领域无处不在，1985 年英国十大交易中，有六起都与他有关。

There was a lot of jockeying and rivalry among the *prima donnas*, and Morgan Grenfell created an unstructured environment that allowed them to wheel and deal without bumping into each other. This required a conciliatory figurehead to oversee the Corporate Finance Department. His name was Graham Walsh. An accountant and former director general of the Takeover Panel, Walsh was a shy, neat, introverted man who never quite made eye contact with other people. Known as a hypochondriac, he would shuffle about shutting windows in winter. Walsh feuded with Seelig, and they ended up scarcely on speaking terms. As he kept his department humming tidily along, Graham Walsh little dreamed that Margaret Thatcher would someday take a personal interest in his job.
首席女高音们之间明争暗斗不断，而摩根建富营造出的非结构化环境，既让她们能自由运作，又不至于相互碰撞。这需要一位善于调和的象征性人物来掌管企业金融部。此人名叫格雷厄姆·沃尔什，是一名会计师，曾担任收购与兼并委员会主任。他生性腼腆、衣着整洁、性格内向，几乎从不与人眼神接触。众所周知他是个疑病症患者，冬天里会拖着步子四处关窗。沃尔什与西利格不合，两人最终几乎到了互不理睬的地步。就在他把部门打理得井井有条时，他万万没想到，有朝一日玛格丽特·撒切尔会亲自过问他的工作。

Such an operation required a strong chief executive to curb the animal spirits of Walsh’s takeover professionals. Yet Christopher Reeves accentuated the impetuosity of the takeover team. He sometimes made rules sound optional: “Merchant banking is all about innovation. . . . We must not believe that rules are written in tablets of stone.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436243) As one former Morgan Grenfell official said bitterly, “He was imbued with this win-at-any-cost or by-any-means attitude. And it communicated itself down to the operatives in the Corporate Finance Department.” From 1980 to 1984, a healthy equilibrium existed between Reeves’s red-blooded dynamism and Mackworth-Young’s wise discretion. An outside adviser recalled, “Christopher Reeves had tremendous thrusting ability, but he needed the balancing factor of Mackworth-Young’s slightly wider and more public-spirited view. Christopher Reeves was all accelerator and no brake.” In 1984, when Mackworth-Young suddenly died, it robbed Morgan Grenfell of this temperate influence.
如此运作需要一位强有力的首席执行官来遏制沃尔什收购团队那股狂热的"动物精神"。然而克里斯托弗·里夫斯反而助长了收购团队的冲动。他有时让规则听上去可有可无："商人银行的核心就是创新……我们绝不能以为规则是刻在石板上的。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436243) 正如一位前摩根建富官员愤愤不平地说："他骨子里就是那种'不惜一切代价、不择手段要赢'的态度，这种态度自上而下传染给了企业金融部门的每个操作人员。"1980 年至 1984 年间，里夫斯那股热血澎湃的冲劲与麦夸特-杨老成持重的谨慎形成了健康的平衡。一位外部顾问回忆道："克里斯托弗·里夫斯有着惊人的推动力，但他需要麦夸特-杨那种更开阔、更具公共精神的视野来制衡。克里斯托弗·里夫斯是只有油门，没有刹车。"1984 年，麦克斯沃斯-杨猝然离世，摩根建富自此失去了这股温和的制衡力量。

The dazzling team of Seelig and Magan made Morgan Grenfell untouchable in takeover work, with only second-place Warburgs remotely approaching it. Between 1982 and 1987, Morgan Grenfell ranked number one in mergers and acquisitions year after year. It was on such an intoxicating ride—in 1985, directors received twice the pay of the year before—that the gray old men of Morgan Grenfell tolerated the antics of the young hotshots. As the scale of the takeover wars escalated crazily, the temptation to break the rules heightened. Before 1985, London had never seen a £1-billion takeover bid; there were four on the table by year’s end. Much to the British Treasury’s annoyance, 23 Great Winchester blithely refused to work on Margaret Thatcher’s privatization boom. Why? Because corporate finance people thought the fees trivial and didn’t want to pull stars off the glitzy merger carousel.
西利与马根这对耀眼的组合，让摩根建富在收购业务中无人能敌，唯有第二名的华宝银行勉强能望其项背。1982 年至 1987 年间，摩根建富在并购领域年年独占鳌头。正因处在这般令人陶醉的上升期——1985 年董事们的薪酬是前一年的两倍——摩根建富的老派元老们才容忍了那些年轻气盛者们的荒诞行径。随着收购战的规模疯狂升级，违规的诱惑也与日俱增。1985 年之前，伦敦从未出现过 10 亿英镑的收购要约；而到那年年底，这样的要约已出现了四起。令英国财政部大为恼火的是，温彻斯特街 23 号（摩根建富）竟轻率地拒绝参与玛格丽特·撒切尔的私有化热潮。原因何在？因为企业金融部门的人认为那点费用微不足道，不愿将明星员工从光鲜的并购旋木上拉下来。

The momentum was pushing Morgan Grenfell toward ever more hazardous enterprise. “Merchant banks tend to attract business by their reputation,” explained a former corporate finance director. “And Morgan Grenfell was being approached by more racy people because they were doing racier things.” The press noted this vicious circle. As London’s *Business* magazine said in 1986, “The mention of Morgan Grenfell in some banking circles gets the sort of reaction the sight of Attila the Hun must have provoked in Rome.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436417) One observer remarked, “They are trotting into action with all the arrogance of a crack Polish cavalry regiment in 1939.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436559) These would be prophetic judgments.
这股势头正把摩根建富推向风险越来越高的生意。“商业银行往往靠声誉吸引业务，”一位前公司金融总监解释道，“而摩根建富之所以吸引更多大胆的人物找上门来，是因为他们自己也在做更大胆的事情。”媒体注意到了这个恶性循环。正如伦敦《商业》杂志在 1986 年所言：“在某些银行圈子里，提起摩根建富，反应就像罗马人看到匈奴王阿提拉一样。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436417) 一位观察者评论道：“他们正以 1939 年波兰精锐骑兵团般的傲慢，趾高气扬地冲入战场。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436559) 这些判断后来都被证明是预言性的。

Morgan Grenfell’s balloon was finally punctured by the Guinness scandal, which convulsed Britain and focused more anger against the City than had any scandal since the “rascally stock jobbers” of the eighteenth century. It started with an exceptionally aggressive client—Guinness chairman Ernest Saunders. As his most famous product, black Irish stout, lost favor with wine-tippling yuppies, Saunders wanted to diversify his huge conglomerate. Like Robert Maxwell, Saunders was an immigrant who craved acceptance by the British establishment. His Jewish parents had fled Nazi-controlled Austria in 1938 and settled in London. The Jewish-born Ernst Schleyer was re-invented as the Anglican Ernest Saunders. He would acquire all the trappings of upper class success, including a Buckinghamshire mansion.
摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）的泡沫最终被吉尼斯（Guinness）丑闻戳破。这场丑闻震动了整个英国，激起的对金融城的愤怒，自 18 世纪那些“无赖股市投机者”以来，从未有过。事件的起因是一位极具攻击性的客户——吉尼斯董事长欧内斯特·桑德斯（Ernest Saunders）。随着他旗下最著名的产品——黑爱尔兰烈性黑啤，逐渐失去那些嗜饮葡萄酒的雅皮士们的青睐，桑德斯决心让这个庞大的企业集团走向多元化。和罗伯特·马克斯韦尔（Robert Maxwell）一样，桑德斯也是一位渴望得到英国体制接纳的移民。他的犹太裔父母于 1938 年逃离纳粹控制的奥地利，定居伦敦。这个原名恩斯特·施莱尔（Ernst Schleyer）的犹太裔男孩，后来化身为英国圣公会信徒欧内斯特·桑德斯。他获得了上层阶级成功的一切象征，包括一栋位于白金汉郡的豪宅。

At the start of his buying spree, Saunders booted out Guinness’s old adviser, N. M. Rothschild, and brought in Morgan Grenfell. At a dinner at the Connaught hotel in early 1985, Christopher Reeves advised Saunders to expand aggressively, lest Guinness itself be a takeover target. In June 1985, advised by Tony Richmond-Watson of Morgan Grenfell, Saunders launched a £330-million hostile bid for the Scotch whisky and hotel company, Arthur Bell and Sons. Bell’s chairman, Raymond Miquel, was flabbergasted, because Morgan Grenfell had represented his firm for twenty years, assisting in its purchase of the Gleneagles Hotel Group and even handling its public offering in 1971. When Miquel complained to the Takeover Panel, Morgan Grenfell countered with evidence that Bells had terminated its services in November 1984; Miquel cited more recent links.
桑德斯在疯狂收购之初，就炒掉了吉尼斯的老牌顾问 N.M.罗斯柴尔德银行，转而与摩根·格伦费尔合作。1985 年初，在康诺特酒店的一次晚宴上，克里斯托弗·里夫斯建议桑德斯大举扩张，否则吉尼斯本身就可能成为收购目标。1985 年 6 月，在摩根·格伦费尔的托尼·里士满-沃森建议下，桑德斯以 3.3 亿英镑对苏格兰威士忌及酒店公司亚瑟·贝尔父子发起恶意收购。贝尔公司董事长雷蒙德·米克尔震惊不已——摩根·格伦费尔二十年来一直是其公司的代表，不仅协助收购了格伦伊格尔斯酒店集团，甚至在 1971 年还经办了公司的上市事宜。当米克尔向收购与合并委员会投诉时，摩根·格伦费尔拿出证据反击，称贝尔公司已于 1984 年 11 月终止了服务合同；而米克尔则列举了双方更近期的往来关系。

Whatever the exact truth, Morgan Grenfell had twenty years’ worth of intimate knowledge about Bells and thus subverted a tradition of merchant banker confidentiality. (This was the rationale for having traditional bankers: a company’s innermost secrets were safe from competitors.) At first, the panel mildly rebuked Morgan Grenfell, then retracted even that. Some attributed its leniency to Christopher Reeves’s informing the panel that Morgan Grenfell had booked only £20,000 in fees from Bells versus £6 million from Guinness in the previous two years. Such an argument, it was thought, swayed the banker-oriented panel.
无论真相如何，摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）二十年来一直对贝尔斯（Bells）公司知根知底，从而打破了商业银行家严守机密的老传统。（这正是传统银行家存在的理由：公司最深层的秘密不会泄露给竞争对手。）起初，调查小组只是对摩根建富进行了轻微训诫，后来连这点训诫也撤销了。有人认为，小组之所以如此宽容，是因为克里斯托弗·里夫斯（Christopher Reeves）向他们说明，在过去两年里，摩根建富从贝尔斯公司收取的费用仅为 2 万英镑，而从吉尼斯（Guinness）收取的却高达 600 万英镑。据说，这样的说法打动了这个由银行家主导的调查小组。

There was no outright illegality in the Bell’s takeover, but things were skirting the edge. Apparently a Guinness employee, pretending to be a Scottish reporter, questioned Raymond Miquel.[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436684) Also, Saunders had assured Bell’s shareholders that he might sell the Piccadilly Hotel in London but keep the rest. Then through Morgan Grenfell’s property arm, he set up a Dutch auction for the Caledonian and North British hotels in Edinburgh. The winning bidder, Norfolk Capital, came as a shock, because its chairman was none other than Tony Richmond-Watson of Morgan Grenfell. There were, predictably, accusations of favoritism and double-dealing. By this point, both Guinness and Morgan Grenfell seemed ripe for trouble. Christopher Reeves, meanwhile, kept telling Saunders that Guinness would have to digest another company if it wished to stay independent.
在收购 Bell's 的过程中，虽然并未出现明目张胆的违法行为，但事情已经游走在灰色地带。显然，一名 Guinness 员工冒充苏格兰记者，对 Raymond Miquel 进行了盘问。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436684) 此外，Saunders 曾向 Bell's 股东保证，他可能会出售伦敦的 Piccadilly Hotel，但保留其余资产。随后，通过 Morgan Grenfell 的房地产部门，他为爱丁堡的 Caledonian 和 North British 酒店安排了一次荷兰式拍卖。中标方 Norfolk Capital 令人震惊，因为其董事长正是 Morgan Grenfell 的 Tony Richmond-Watson。不出所料，外界指责存在偏袒和两面三刀的行为。到这时，Guinness 和 Morgan Grenfell 似乎都已麻烦缠身。与此同时，Christopher Reeves 不断告诉 Saunders，如果 Guinness 想保持独立，就必须再吞并另一家公司。

In January 1986, Saunders entered into a more massive takeover whose record size would make the city tremble. A supermarket chain, Argyll, had made a bid for the far larger Scottish brewer, Distillers. This David assault on Goliath—a new City phenomenon—had clear class overtones. Argyll’s James Gulliver was a husky, rough-hewn grocer’s son, while Distillers was an aristocratic Scottish firm. From plush headquarters on London’s St. James’s Square, Distillers marketed drinks with a classy ring—Johnnie Walker, Haig and Haig, and White Horse scotch, Booth’s and Gordon’s gin. Behind the tony facade, however, the poorly run company was losing ground in the scotch market. And it had never completely overcome its infamy as the manufacturer of thalidomide, a sedative that when taken by pregnant women apparently caused cruel deformations in the developing fetus.
1986 年 1 月，桑德斯发起了一场规模更为庞大的收购，其创纪录的金额令整个金融城为之震动。连锁超市企业阿盖尔向规模远大于己的苏格兰酿酒商迪斯提勒斯提出收购要约。这场以弱胜强的突袭——金融城的新现象——带有鲜明的阶级色彩。阿盖尔的詹姆斯·格列佛是个体格魁梧、粗犷豪爽的杂货商之子，而迪斯提勒斯则是苏格兰的贵族企业。这家公司在伦敦圣詹姆斯广场的豪华总部里，经营着尊尼获加、海格与海格、白马威士忌、布斯及戈登金酒等高档酒类。然而在这光鲜外表之下，这家管理不善的企业在威士忌市场节节败退，且始终未能摆脱因生产沙利度胺而背负的恶名——这种镇静剂曾导致孕妇服用后，胎儿出现严重畸形。

Distillers scoffed at the Scottish commoner Gulliver. “Gulliver deals in potatoes and cans of beans,” said a deputy chairman of Distillers. “We are not selling brown water in cheap bottles. We are selling Scotch.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436833) The Gulliver people exhibited their own class bias. Hoping to swoop down on Distillers by surprise, they favored an August raid, saying, “Let’s shoot them up the backside while they’re out on the grouse moor.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437012)
蒸馏酒业的高管们对苏格兰平民出身的小人物格列佛嗤之以鼻。“格列佛不过是倒腾土豆和罐头豆子的，”蒸馏酒业的一位副董事长嘲讽道，“我们卖的可不是装在廉价瓶子里的浑水，我们卖的是苏格兰威士忌。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2436833) 格列佛那帮人也毫不掩饰他们的阶级偏见。为了打蒸馏酒业一个措手不及，他们主张在八月下手，理由是：“趁他们都在猎松鸡的时候，给他们屁股上来一枪。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437012)

So along came Ernest Saunders in January 1986, volunteering to be the “white knight” who would save Distillers from that coarse ruffian, Jimmy Gulliver. There was more than altruism here: Saunders was already considering pouncing on Distillers, anyway. If he could make a ceremonial entrance through the front door, well, so much the better. He even secured an agreement by which Distillers would defray Guinness’s fees for taking it over—a highly unusual measure.
于是，1986 年 1 月，欧内斯特·桑德斯及时出现，自告奋勇要当那个“白衣骑士”，把蒸馏酒业从粗暴的流氓吉米·格列佛手里解救出来。这背后可不只是利他主义：桑德斯自己本来就在盘算着如何拿下蒸馏酒业。要是能名正言顺地从正门风光入场，那更是求之不得。他甚至跟蒸馏酒业谈妥了一笔交易，让对方来支付健力士收购它所产生的费用——这可不是一般的操作。

Saunders liberally distributed promises to win Distillers’ support. Chairman John Connell was apparently led to believe he would chair the merged entity. Ditto for Sir Thomas Risk, governor of the Bank of Scotland. The duping of Risk would be one of the most disgraceful parts of the Guinness affair. The Guinness forces needed to placate Scottish nationalists, who would protest the loss of Distillers’ autonomy. Charles Fraser, an Edinburgh lawyer and chairman of Morgan Grenfell, Scotland, was among those pressing for a Scottish non-executive chairman. To this end, Saunders and Seelig cajoled the respected Risk into agreeing to take such a post in the new firm, which would have its headquarters in Scotland. This promise was incorporated into takeover documents, and the Distillers board made it a precondition of the “friendly” bid; many institutional investors backed Guinness on its strength. Nevertheless, Guinness later reneged on its pledge, claiming that the two-tier structure was unworkable from a commercial standpoint. It even circulated rumors to blacken Risk’s reputation, claiming that he was unduly concerned with retaining Guinness business for his bank. Morgan Grenfell protested the treatment of Risk but didn’t resign. After an extraordinary general meeting, Risk was dropped by Guinness without legal repercussions. But the lingering resentment over the dispute would add extraordinary venom to the Guinness affair.
桑德斯慷慨地做出各种承诺以赢得帝亚吉欧的支持。董事长约翰·康奈尔显然被引导相信他将领导合并后的实体。苏格兰银行行长托马斯·里斯克爵士也同样被误导。对里斯克的欺骗是吉尼斯事件中最可耻的部分之一。吉尼斯方面需要安抚苏格兰民族主义者，他们会抗议帝亚吉欧丧失自主权。爱丁堡律师、摩根建富苏格兰分行董事长查尔斯·弗雷泽等人主张任命一位苏格兰籍的非执行董事长。为此，桑德斯和西利说服备受尊敬的里斯克同意在新公司担任这一职位，该公司总部将设在苏格兰。这一承诺被写入收购文件，帝亚吉欧董事会也将其作为“友好”收购的前提条件；许多机构投资者因此支持吉尼斯。 然而，健力士后来背弃了承诺，声称从商业角度看，这种双层结构根本行不通。它甚至散布谣言，恶意诋毁瑞斯克的名声，说他过分操心，只为让自己的银行保住健力士的业务。摩根·格伦费尔虽为瑞斯克打抱不平，但并未因此辞职。在一次特别股东大会后，健力士解除了瑞斯克的职务，且未追究其法律责任。但这场争端积下的怨恨，日后竟成了健力士丑闻中一颗剧毒的种子。

Tony Richmond-Watson should have been Morgan Grenfell’s point man in the Guinness takeover. But he was busy helping United Biscuit complete a £1-billion merger with Imperial Group, so he bowed out. Roger Seelig took his place. (Some accounts say Saunders demanded the more aggressive Seelig.) According to Saunders, Reeves warned him that Seelig was a “very powerful personality and will want to do things his way.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437179) As a member of the so-called Guinness war cabinet, Seelig sat by Saunders at meetings and was integral to everything that happened. Even before the later disclosure of share manipulation, it seemed a nasty campaign. Both sides stooped to such scurrilous ads that the Takeover Panel had to curb their use.
托尼·里士满-沃森本应是摩根格伦菲尔在吉尼斯收购案中的关键人物。但他正忙于协助联合饼干公司完成与帝国集团价值 10 亿英镑的合并，因此退出了。罗杰·西利格接替了他的位置。（有说法称桑德斯点名要更具进攻性的西利格。）据桑德斯说，里夫斯曾警告他，西利格“个性极强，凡事都要按他的方式来。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437179) 作为所谓的吉尼斯战时内阁成员，西利格在会议上紧挨桑德斯而坐，对发生的每一件事都至关重要。甚至在后来曝出股票操纵之前，这看上去就已经是一场肮脏的战役。双方都沦落到使用如此下作的广告，以至于收购委员会不得不加以限制。

The essence of the Guinness scandal involved manipulation of share prices. The Lilliputian grocer, Jimmy Gulliver, was trying to take over a liquor company three times his company’s size. Where a comparable bid in the United States would have been financed with cash and junk bonds, Gulliver hoped to pay primarily with shares of his own company, much as American conglomerates did in the 1960s. As it started bidding against Gulliver, Guinness also relied on a swap of its own shares and cash. So the decisive factor would be the price of Argyll and Guinness stock; the more they rose, the more their individual bids would be worth.
健力士丑闻的核心在于股价操纵。小杂货商出身的吉米·格列佛，当时正试图吞并一家规模三倍于己的酿酒公司。若是在美国，类似的收购要约往往会通过现金加垃圾债券的方式融资，而格列佛则希望主要用自家公司的股票来支付，这就像上世纪 60 年代美国企业集团的做法一样。当健力士开始与格列佛竞价时，也同样依赖自家股票与现金的交换。因此，决定性因素就成了阿盖尔与健力士两家公司的股价——股价涨得越高，各自的收购报价就越值钱。

Guinness embarked on a campaign to pump up its own share price and thus enhance its bid. It wasn’t illegal for people to buy Guinness stock, nor for Morgan Grenfell to tout it. Roger Seelig got his friend, the beefy, bespectacled Lord Spens—formerly of Morgan Grenfell but now a corporate finance director with Henry Ansbacher—to coax his clients into buying two million shares. Jacob Rothschild bought. Robert Maxwell reportedly picked up two million shares. L. F. Rothschild Unterberg Towbin bought a six-million-share Guinness block, which it sold back to Morgan Grenfell when the battle was over. The illegality would arise only if Guinness underwrote share purchases or guaranteed buyers against loss by doing so. That would violate the Companies Act of 1985, which prohibited companies from buying their own shares or assisting others in doing so. Whether or not criminal acts took place in connection with the Guinness “affair” will, of course, be resolved in criminal proceedings to be commenced in 1991, in which the defendents denied all charges.
健力士发起了一场拉升自身股价的行动，以此增强其收购竞标的实力。人们购买健力士股票并不违法，摩根建富为其宣传推广同样无可厚非。罗杰·西利格找到了他的朋友——曾任职于摩根建富、如今在亨利·安斯巴赫公司担任企业融资总监、身材魁梧且戴眼镜的斯彭斯勋爵——请他劝说自己的客户买入两百万股。雅各布·罗斯柴尔德也加入了购买行列。据报道，罗伯特·麦克斯韦购入了两百万股。L. F. 罗斯柴尔德·温伯格·托宾公司则买下了六百万股健力士股票，并在收购战结束后将这批股票卖回给了摩根建富。只有当健力士为股票购买行为提供担保，或保证买家不会因此亏损时，才会构成违法。这将违反 1985 年《公司法》，该法禁止公司购买自身股份或协助他人进行此类操作。 关于健力士“事件”是否涉及犯罪行为，当然会在 1991 年启动的刑事诉讼中得到裁定，被告人对所有指控均予以否认。

Despite the law, Seelig, Saunders, and Guinness finance director Olivier Roux allegedly orchestrated what is called a concert party, or a fan club, to inflate the Guinness share price while deflating Argyll stock. They are said to have conducted their secret work on such a large and brazen scale that one wonders how, in the long run, they hoped to escape detection. The list of deals cut by the Guinness war cabinet makes for sobering reading. British businessman Gerald Ronson, chairman of Heron Corporation, had introduced Seelig to the notorious American arbitrageur, Ivan Boesky. At Seelig’s urging, Boesky poured £100 million into Guinness and, for good measure, “shorted” Argyll stock to depress it. The Boesky link would later clarify a mystery of the Guinness takeover: why Argyll shares dipped each afternoon in London, just about the time New York trading opened. The American company, Schenley Industries, bought £60 million of Guinness stock. By coincidence, it received an extended contract afterward to market Dewar’s whiskey in the United States. The biggest stake was taken by the venerable Bank Leu, Switzerland’s oldest bank, which bought tens of millions of shares and was allegedly indemnified against any losses.
尽管有法律明文禁止，但据称西利格、桑德斯以及健力士财务总监奥利维尔·鲁尔策划了一场所谓的“联手行动”或“粉丝俱乐部”，人为抬高健力士股价，同时打压阿盖尔股票。他们以如此大规模且明目张胆的方式开展秘密操作，令人不禁质疑：他们究竟是如何指望长期逃避追查的？健力士“作战内阁”达成的一连串交易，读来令人触目惊心。英国商人、苍鹭集团董事长杰拉尔德·罗森将西利格引荐给了臭名昭著的美国套利者伊万·博斯基。在西利格的怂恿下，博斯基向健力士注资 1 亿英镑，不仅如此，他还“做空”阿盖尔股票以打压其股价。与博斯基的这层关系，后来解开了健力士收购案中的一个谜团：为何阿盖尔股价每天下午在伦敦都会下跌，而这恰好是纽约开盘的时间。美国顺雷工业公司则买入了价值 6000 万英镑的健力士股票。 巧合的是，该公司随后获得了一份延长合同，负责在美国市场推广 Dewar's 威士忌。最大股东是瑞士最古老的银行——尊贵的 Bank Leu，该行购入了数千万股，据称其任何损失都得到了赔偿。

As Guinness’s share prices spiraled wildly skyward, buoyed by the coordinated buying, Gulliver complained to the Takeover Panel about the share levitation. As it had since 1968, Britain still relied on the private panel to enforce the Takeover Code. This self-regulatory body seemed too genteel to deal with the harsh tactics and mercenary culture of the modern City. Much to its later embarrassment, the Takeover Panel failed to act on Argyll’s complaint. Morgan Grenfell also accused Argyll of inflating *its* share price. A 25-percent jump in Guinness’s share price finally tipped the scales in the takeover. On April 18, 1986, Ernest Saunders declared victory over Argyll, claiming to have more than 50 percent of Distillers’ shares in a bid valued at £2.53 billion. Morgan Grenfell had won the biggest, ugliest, most searing takeover of the decade and basked in dubious glory.
随着联合买入推动健力士股价疯狂飙升，格列佛向收购委员会投诉了股价操纵行为。自 1968 年以来，英国一直依赖这个私人委员会来执行《收购守则》。这一自律机构似乎过于温文尔雅，难以应对现代金融城冷酷的手段与雇佣军文化。事后令收购委员会颇为尴尬的是，它未能对阿盖尔的投诉采取行动。摩根建富也指控阿盖尔哄抬股价。健力士股价 25%的飙升最终扭转了收购战的局面。1986 年 4 月 18 日，欧内斯特·桑德斯宣布战胜阿盖尔，声称在价值 25.3 亿英镑的竞购中已获得蒸馏器公司超过 50%的股份。摩根建富赢得了十年来规模最大、最丑陋、最激烈的收购案，并沐浴在令人质疑的荣耀中。

Ethics aside, Guinness involved some risky financial footwork for Morgan Grenfell, providing further proof of its win-at-any-cost mentality. It had bought £180 million of Distillers shares, straining its capital resources of only £170 million—to say the least. This behavior was deemed so irresponsible by the Bank of England that it promptly issued new rules, limiting such share buying by a bank to 25 percent of its capital—an implicit rebuke of Morgan Grenfell. Once again, the firm that was the Bank of England’s staunchest ally in Teddy Grenfell’s day now seemed its chief nemesis.
撇开道德不谈，健力士公司为摩根建富带来了风险极高的财务操作，进一步证明了其不惜一切代价取胜的心态。摩根建富购买了 1.8 亿英镑的 Distillers 公司股票，而其资本金仅有 1.7 亿英镑——至少可以说是捉襟见肘。这种行径被英格兰银行视为极其不负责任，以至于它迅速颁布新规，将银行购买此类股票的比例限制在资本的 25%以内——这无疑是对摩根建富的含蓄谴责。曾经在泰迪·格伦费尔时代，这家公司是英格兰银行最坚定的盟友，如今却成了其头号对手。

As in any share-rigging scheme, there was a critical weakness: what would happen to artificially elevated share prices when the secret props were removed? Apparently Roger Seelig feared that friendly parties might suddenly dump up to 20 percent of Guinness’s shares on the market. And the price indeed began to slide sharply from the 355-pence takeover price. If, as alleged, Guinness had promised to insure the concert party against losses, its liability could be tremendous if the share price plummeted. Seelig appealed to institutions to hold their shares until autumn. Mayhew of Cazenove, Guinness’s broker, also worked out a plan for Guinness to buy back Morgan Grenfell’s stake in Distillers, which would be converted into Guinness shares after the takeover.
与任何操纵股价的骗局一样，其中存在一个致命弱点：当暗中支撑被撤走时，被人为抬高的股价将何去何从？显然，罗杰·西利格担心，友善的关联方可能突然在市场上抛售高达 20%的吉尼斯股票。而股价确实从 355 便士的收购价开始急剧下滑。如果如指控所说，吉尼斯曾承诺为"合作方"兜底亏损，那么一旦股价暴跌，其潜在赔偿责任将极为巨大。西利格呼吁各机构持股至秋季。吉尼斯经纪商 Cazenove 的梅休也制定了一项计划，让吉尼斯回购摩根建富持有的 Distillers 股份，这些股份将在收购完成后转换为吉尼斯股票。

More direct measures were taken to stop selling. Lord Spens had a two-million-share block for which Guinness allegedly paid him £7.6 million. Seelig and Oliver Roux, Saunders’s financial adviser, are said to have characterized the £7.6 million as an interest-free deposit in order to dissuade Spens from selling. It was hard to see what the difference would be between such a *de facto* guarantee and an outright purchase. It is also alleged that Bank Leu in Switzerland also got a £50-million “deposit” from Guinness to ensure that it wouldn’t liquidate its shareholding. During the summer of 1986, Guinness transferred £69 million to a venture fund managed by Ivan Boesky, who had invested so heavily in Guinness shares. It was this aspect that later outraged the Guinness board and precipitated the dismissal of Ernest Saunders. When all the deals finally surfaced in the press, share manipulation apparently added up to a staggering £200 million.
为阻止抛售，采取了更直接的手段。斯彭斯勋爵（Lord Spens）持有两百万股的大宗股份，据称吉尼斯（Guinness）为此向他支付了 760 万英镑。据说，桑德斯（Saunders）的财务顾问西利格（Seelig）和奥利弗·鲁（Oliver Roux）将这 760 万英镑描述为一笔无息存款，以此劝阻斯彭斯抛售。很难看出这种事实上的担保与直接收购之间有何区别。此外，据称瑞士的洛伊银行（Bank Leu）也从吉尼斯获得了一笔 5000 万英镑的“存款”，以确保其不会清算所持股份。1986 年夏季，吉尼斯向伊万·博斯基（Ivan Boesky）管理的一只风险基金转移了 6900 万英镑，而博斯基此前已重仓吉尼斯股票。正是这一环节后来激怒了吉尼斯董事会，并最终导致欧内斯特·桑德斯（Ernest Saunders）被免职。当所有这些交易最终被媒体曝光时，股票操纵涉及的总金额竟高达惊人的 2 亿英镑。

To understand the fierce public outcry that greeted the exposure of the Guinness scandal, one must note several factors. Billion-pound hostile deals were becoming common for the first time. And during the Thatcher years, the number of shareholders in Britain had more than tripled, to nine million. Never before had so many people been riveted on the doings of the City. Big Bang was accompanied by a numbing barrage of publicity. And at least part of the populace revered the new money makers. At the time of Guinness, the white-hot Morgan Grenfell was receiving about fifteen hundred job applications yearly from university graduates, half from Oxford and Cambridge, for thirty places. So some of the public disenchantment reflected the earlier worship of new idols.
要理解吉尼斯丑闻曝光后引发的强烈公众愤慨，必须注意到几个因素。十亿英镑级别的恶意收购案首次变得司空见惯。而在撒切尔执政时期，英国股东数量翻了三倍有余，达到九百万。此前从未有如此多的人密切关注金融城的动向。金融大爆炸伴随着铺天盖地的宣传攻势。至少部分民众对新兴的财富创造者怀有敬意。吉尼斯事件发生时，如日中天的摩根建富每年从大学毕业生中收到约一千五百份求职申请——其中半数来自牛津剑桥——而录取名额仅三十个。因此，公众的幻灭感中，多少折射出先前对新时代偶像的盲目崇拜。

Among other factors was a growing sense that the City was corrupting the general culture and harming the economy. The *New Statesman* called the City “an unpatriotic casino which pays itself obscenely high salaries for dancing on the grave of British industry.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437337) Sir Claus Moser of N. M. Rothschild warned, “the City is absorbing too many of our ablest people. If I were a dictator over Britain, I’d move 9/10 of them into manufacturing, industry and teaching.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437489) As on Wall Street, high finance was no longer principally involved in financing the operations of industry. Instead, it was financing changes in the ownership of industry, to the stunning benefit of bankers and raiders. Add to this the sheer envy of ordinary Britons at the stratospheric salaries in the City, and one can understand the incendiary public reaction to Guinness.
除此之外，越来越多的人开始意识到，伦敦金融城正在腐蚀整个社会的文化，并损害经济。《新政治家》周刊称金融城为“一个不爱国的大赌场，在英工业的坟墓上起舞，却给自己支付高得离谱的薪酬。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437337) N.M.罗斯柴尔德公司的克劳斯·莫泽爵士警告道：“金融城吸纳了我们太多最优秀的人才。如果我是英国独裁者，我会把其中十分之九的人调去制造业、工业和教育领域。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437489) 就像华尔街一样，高端金融不再主要负责为工业运营提供融资，而是为工业所有权的变更提供融资，这让银行家和强盗大亨赚得盆满钵满。再加上普通英国人对金融城天文数字般薪水的纯粹嫉妒，就不难理解公众对健力士事件的激烈反应了。

The year 1986 was one of double scandal for Morgan Grenfell. Right after Big Bang and before the Guinness revelations surfaced, the firm was tarred by an insider trading scandal. Its new chief securities trader, thirty-five-year-old Geoffrey Collier, had been hired for Big Bang at a salary of \$284,000. He had joined the firm the year before after setting up a New York office for Vickers da Costa, a broker later taken over by Citicorp. In the past, British authorities had taken a relaxed attitude toward insider trading, which wasn’t even a criminal offense until 1980. This *laissez-faire* attitude was incompatible with the new conglomerates encouraged by Big Bang. There were now fears that traders would abuse information from their mergers departments. In global financial markets, there was also a need for higher universal standards—a particular concern of the Thatcher government in its quest to insure the City’s worldwide standing. Right before Big Bang, Morgan Grenfell distributed a little in-house manual in which it stated that any staff purchases of shares had to be channeled through Morgan Grenfell’s own brokers. The penalty for violating the rule was summary dismissal.
1986 年对摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）来说是双重丑闻的一年。金融大爆炸刚刚结束，吉尼斯（Guinness）内幕尚未曝光之时，这家公司就因一起内幕交易丑闻而蒙上污名。新任首席证券交易员、35 岁的杰弗里·科利尔（Geoffrey Collier）以 28.4 万美元的年薪被招来应对金融大爆炸。他前一年才加入公司，此前曾在维克斯达科斯塔（Vickers da Costa）——这家券商后来被花旗银行（Citicorp）收购——设立纽约办事处。过去，英国当局对内幕交易持宽松态度，直到 1980 年这甚至不构成刑事犯罪。这种放任自流的作风与金融大爆炸催生的新型金融集团格格不入。如今人们担心交易员会滥用并购部门获取的信息。在全球金融市场中，也需要更高的统一标准——这是撒切尔政府为确保伦敦金融城全球地位而特别关注的问题。 就在 Big Bang 之前，Morgan Grenfell 发布了一本小小的内部手册，其中明确规定任何员工购买股票都必须通过 Morgan Grenfell 自己的经纪商进行。违反该规定的处罚是立即解雇。

Collier made illegal purchases through his old firm, now Scrimgeour Vickers. He made small profits on a sixty-thousand-share purchase of Associated Engineering after he learned that Hollis, a Morgan client, was about to acquire it. He almost made a killing on his purchase of call options on Cadbury Schweppes, then being acquired by General Cinema, another Morgan Grenfell client. But his trade in Associated Engineering led to suspicions and forced him to sell the Cadbury options prematurely, at a loss. After Scrimgeour Vickers tipped off Morgan Grenfell about Collier’s trading, the firm forced his resignation, on November 10, 1986, shocking the City. Collier got a one-year suspended sentence and £25,000 (\$44,250) fine. The case fortified the resolve of those who wanted stricter regulation as firms turned into conglomerates fraught with potential conflicts of interest.
科利尔利用他曾经的供职公司斯克林杰-维克斯公司进行了非法交易。在得知摩根大通的客户霍利斯即将收购联合工程公司后，他买入了 6 万股该公司的股票，从中赚取了微薄利润。随后，他又买入吉百利史威士的看涨期权——当时这家公司正被摩根格伦费尔的另一客户通用影院公司收购——差点大赚一笔。但他在联合工程公司的交易引发了怀疑，迫使他提前抛售吉百利的期权，最终亏损离场。斯克林杰-维克斯公司向摩根格伦费尔举报了科利尔的交易行为后，该公司于 1986 年 11 月 10 日迫使科利尔辞职，此事震惊了整个伦敦金融城。科利尔被判处一年有期徒刑缓期执行，并处罚金 2.5 万英镑（约合 4.425 万美元）。此案加剧了那些主张加强监管者的决心——当时企业正演变成潜藏利益冲突的庞然大物。

The Collier scandal was a mere curtain raiser for the next act in the drama. In late 1986, Morgan Grenfell was winding up a superlative year, bolstered by \$833 million in capital, double the year before. Then Ivan Boesky pleaded guilty in November to one felony charge and agreed to pay a \$100-million penalty for insider trading. Nobody yet dreamed of any Morgan Grenfell connection. But in the Collier investigation, the SEC had already shared information with Britain’s Department of Trade and Industry under a new, bilateral pact. It was information from Boesky, relayed by American authorities to the British, that provoked the Guinness investigation. The suspicious stock gyrations during the takeover had not in themselves produced a full-blown probe.
科利尔丑闻不过是接下来这场大戏的序幕。1986 年底，Morgan Grenfell 正以 8.33 亿美元的资本（是前一年的两倍）为支撑，圆满收官。随后，Ivan Boesky 在 11 月对一项重罪指控认罪，并同意因内幕交易支付 1 亿美元罚金。当时还没人想到这与 Morgan Grenfell 有任何关联。但在科利尔调查中，美国 SEC 已根据一项新的双边协议，与英国贸易工业部共享了信息。正是来自 Boesky 的信息，经美国当局转交英国方面，引发了 Guinness 调查。收购期间可疑的股价波动本身并未促成全面调查。

Equipped with new Big Bang powers, investigators from the Department of Trade and Industry moved into action. In early December, three weeks after the Collier affair, they staged simultaneous morning raids on Guinness headquarters in Portman Square and on Morgan Grenfell to round up records. Then a month of revelations flooded London’s press, drawing to the surface the latent public resentment of the City. Nobody at 23 Great Winchester quite saw the gathering storm. Seelig himself thought the firm would stand behind him. When a journalist said, “Mr. Seelig, your colleagues are going to throw you to the wolves,” he was incredulous. “Now you’re making me very angry,” he replied. “We’re very solid here.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437640) But under intense pressure, Morgan Grenfell told Seelig he was drawing too much publicity and had to be sacrificed. In a tumultuous series of late-December decisions, Morgan Grenfell resigned as Guinness’s merchant banker and fired its brightest star, Roger Seelig. Since the Guinness board had already brought in Lazard Brothers as their new adviser, some observers saw an empty public relations gesture in Morgan Grenfell’s resignation.
配备了大爆炸新权力的贸工部调查人员立即行动起来。12 月初，在 Collier 事件三周后，他们于一个早上同时对波特曼广场的 Guinness 总部和 Morgan Grenfell 实施了突击搜查，以收集相关记录。随后一个月里，伦敦媒体被接连曝光的消息淹没，将公众对伦敦金融城的积怨推向了表面。而位于大温彻斯特街 23 号的 Morgan Grenfell 却对这场即将来临的风暴浑然不觉。Seelig 本人以为公司会站在他这一边。当一名记者说：“Seelig 先生，您的同事们要把您扔出去喂狼了。”他难以置信地回应：“您这么说让我很生气，我们这里非常团结。”然而，在巨大压力下，Morgan Grenfell 告诉 Seelig，他引起了过多公众关注，必须被牺牲。在一系列动荡的 12 月底决策中，Morgan Grenfell 辞去了 Guinness 商业银行的职务，并解雇了其最耀眼的明星——Roger Seelig。 由于健力士董事会已经聘请了 Lazard Brothers 作为其新顾问，一些观察人士认为，摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）的辞职不过是一种空洞的公关姿态。

Morgan Grenfell hoped this would cleanse the firm and close the scandal. It circulated a reassuring letter to shareholders to that effect. But the public pressure to go further was unrelenting. Even within the City, there was thinly veiled satisfaction at seeing Morgan Grenfell punished. “When a firm has been so outstandingly successful on the back of very aggressive people and go no distance out of their way to be popular or help other firms—then the absence of friends shows up at once when they trip up,” said a rival executive. “It was very noticeable that people didn’t jump to their rescue.”
摩根·格林菲尔（Morgan Grenfell）原本以为这样做能清洗公司、平息丑闻，还为此给股东们发了一封安抚信。但公众要求采取更严厉措施的压力却丝毫不减。即便在伦敦金融城（City）内部，看到摩根·格林菲尔受罚，人们也毫不掩饰地流露出几分快意。“一家公司要是靠着一帮咄咄逼人的家伙取得了空前成功，却从不费心去让自己受欢迎或帮助同行——那么一旦他们栽跟头，没人会立刻伸出援手。”一位竞争对手的高管如是说，“很明显，大家并没有急着去救他们。”

Inside the firm, the shifting ground suddenly turned to quicksand. A former staffer recalled, “The first reaction was absolute shock and horror. It didn’t happen as one big announcement. It was like a faucet dripping lousy water. It kept on coming. It was a real horror that tore the guts out of the firm.” On January 9, 1987, Ernest Saunders stepped down as Guinness’s chairman. A week later, Price Waterhouse outlined the £200-million share-support operation, mapping out links across several countries. They reported to the Guinness board the existence of £25 million in mysterious invoices. The operation seemed almost inconceivably vast.
公司内部，脚下的坚实土地骤然化为流沙。一名前员工回忆道：“第一反应是彻底的震惊和恐惧。这一切并非以一则重磅公告的形式发生，而是像水龙头不断滴着脏水，一波未平一波又起。这是真正的恐惧，把公司的魂都抽干了。”1987 年 1 月 9 日，欧内斯特·桑德斯辞去 Guinness 董事长一职。一周后，Price Waterhouse 概述了那项耗资 2 亿英镑的股价支撑行动，梳理了横跨多个国家的关联脉络。他们向 Guinness 董事会报告存在 2500 万英镑的来路不明发票。整项行动规模之大，几乎令人难以置信。

Once Morgan Grenfell could have counted on the Bank of England’s goodwill, but it had let that historic relationship lapse. Since the death of Sir John Stevens, in 1973, Morgan Grenfell hadn’t had a director on the Court of the Bank of England. Some saw this as reflecting official displeasure; for others, it simply betokened an absence of executives of real stature at Morgan Grenfell. The bank’s deputy governor, George Blunden, wasn’t appeased by Seelig’s removal and thought Reeves and Walsh were either knaves or fools. As late as January 18, 1987, Reeves, earning £300,000 a year, and Walsh, earning £200,000 a year, felt secure and insisted they would stay. They saw no systematic corruption that merited further action and so informed the staff. “They called together everyone in corporate finance,” said an ex-director. “They said management had done an exhaustive, internal look at the big deals and not to worry. They said, ‘We’ll be sticking here; there’ll be no resignations.’ ”
曾几何时，摩根·格伦费尔本可倚仗英格兰银行的善意，但它却让这段历史关系渐渐淡去。自 1973 年约翰·史蒂文斯爵士去世后，摩根·格伦费尔在英格兰银行董事会中便再无董事席位。有人视此为官方不满的体现；也有人认为，这不过意味着摩根·格伦费尔缺乏真正有分量高管。解雇西利格并未平息该行副行长乔治·布伦登的怒火，在他看来，里夫斯和沃尔什不是无赖就是蠢货。直至 1987 年 1 月 18 日，年薪 30 万英镑的里夫斯和年薪 20 万英镑的沃尔什仍觉地位稳固，坚称自己不会离开。他们不认为存在需要进一步处理的系统性腐败，并以此告知员工。“他们召集了企业金融部的所有人，”一位前董事说道，“他们表示管理层已对那些重大交易进行了彻底内部审查，让大家不必担心。他们还说：‘我们会留在这里，绝不会有人辞职。’”

This wishful thinking was exploded within forty-eight hours. Expecting a general election in the near future, the Tories feared retribution if they seemed to coddle the City. Presumably, Margaret Thatcher saw a chance to steal the thunder of the Labour Party by seeming tough in disciplining her own kind. Many in the City also feared that the spreading scandal would bring calls for statutory regulation of a sort that was always abhorrent to the City. Robin Leigh-Pemberton, Governor of the Bank of England, told a dinner audience that Guinness was “a threat to the entire basis of trust which still predominates in our business life and in the City of London in particular.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437809) So great was the fear of seeming soft on the financiers that John Wakeham, chief Tory whip and close Margaret Thatcher adviser, was heard to say, “We’ve got to get the handcuffs on quick.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437960)*Handcuffs* became a metaphor that thrilled the public, with its suggestion of rough justice and a leveling of the mighty. It worked its way into several anonymous newspaper threats leaked by government officials.
这种一厢情愿的想法在四十八小时内就被彻底击碎了。由于预计近期将举行大选，保守党担心如果他们对金融城过于纵容，会遭到选民的报复。玛格丽特·撒切尔想必看到了一个机会——通过严厉惩戒自己人，来抢走工党的风头。金融城内许多人还担心，不断发酵的丑闻会引发对法定监管的呼声，而那种监管向来为金融城所深恶痛绝。英格兰银行行长罗宾· Leigh-Pemberton 在一次晚宴讲话中指出，吉尼斯事件“对我们商业生活、尤其是伦敦金融城赖以维系的信任根基构成了威胁。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437809) 由于极度害怕显得对金融家们手软，保守党首席党鞭、撒切尔的亲密顾问约翰·韦克姆据说曾放话：“我们得赶紧给他们戴上铐子。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2437960) “手铐”一词成了让公众津津乐道的隐喻，因为它暗示着粗暴的正义以及对强权者的拉平。 它悄然出现在政府官员泄露的几份匿名报纸威胁中。

Blunden called in Sir Leslie O’Brien, the former Bank of England governor who had set up the Takeover Panel and, ironically, chastised Morgan Grenfell a generation earlier for its behavior in Gallaher-American Tobacco and *News of the World* battles. In an odd turnaround, O’Brien was now on Morgan Grenfell’s International Advisory Council. Blunden laid down the law, citing Section 17 of the Banking Act, which gave the Bank of England authority to oust officials for imprudently managing a bank. This apparently didn’t have the desired effect on Reeves and Walsh. The firm issued a statement denying any further dismissals.
布伦登请来了前英格兰银行行长莱斯利·奥布莱恩爵士——他一手建立了收购与合并委员会，讽刺的是，早在一代人之前，他就曾因摩根建富在盖拉赫美国烟草和《世界新闻报》收购战中的行为而对其加以训斥。如今风水轮流转，奥布莱恩居然成了摩根建富国际顾问委员会的成员。布伦登搬出了《银行业法》第 17 条——该条款赋予英格兰银行以管理不善为由撤换银行高管的权力。但这番警告显然没对里夫斯和沃尔什产生预期效果。该公司发表声明，否认会进一步解雇员工。

Now Thatcher began to pressure Nigel Lawson, chancellor of the Exchequer, to draw more blood from the Guinness affair. In an extraordinary intervention, she reportedly told Lawson, “I want Reeves and Walsh out today, not next week or next month, but by lunchtime today.” This was duly communicated to the Bank of England and transmitted, in turn, to a Morgan Grenfell delegation led by Lord Catto. In the office of Robin Leigh-Pemberton, governer of the Bank of England, the Morgan men were bluntly warned that if certain actions weren’t taken, Lawson would appear before the House of Commons to announce that the government was selecting a new Morgan Grenfell management. Afterwards, Catto and Sir Peter Carey, a Morgan director, met with Reeves and Walsh who were, as a Morgan Grenfell official phrased it, “politely forced out.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438103) As Thatcher had demanded, the heads rolled by lunchtime. It was a breathtaking reversal: the free-market prime minister striking down the City’s most entrepreneurial firm in the worst disaster to hit a City firm in many years. A columnist for London’s *Financial Times*, reflecting on the double disaster of the Collier and Guinness scandals, said, “It seems that there can be no end to the disasters that the market can dream up (or wish upon) Morgan.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438255)
现在，撒切尔开始向财政大臣奈杰尔·劳森施压，要求他在吉尼斯丑闻中再下狠手。据报道，她以一种非同寻常的干预方式对劳森说：“我要里夫斯和沃尔什今天就走人，不是下周或下个月，而是今天午饭前。”这番话随即传到了英格兰银行，又转达给了由卡托勋爵率领的摩根建富代表团。在英格兰银行行长罗宾·利-彭伯顿的办公室里，摩根的人被直白地警告：如果不采取某些行动，劳森就会在下议院宣布，政府正在为摩根建富物色新的管理层。事后，卡托和摩根董事彼得·凯里爵士与里夫斯和沃尔什见了面——用摩根建富一位官员的话说，他们被“客气地请出了局”。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438103) 正如撒切尔所要求的那样，午饭前，人头已然落地。 这是一场令人瞠目结舌的逆转：信奉自由市场的首相，在一场多年来重创伦敦金融城的灾难中，击倒了金融城内最具创业精神的公司。《伦敦金融时报》的一位专栏作家在反思科利尔和吉尼斯双重丑闻的灾难时感慨道：“市场能构想（或强加于）摩根的灾难，似乎永无止境。”” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438255) ”

The Guinness scandal had broad repercussions for the City. There were parliamentary calls to crack down on the bankers. As part of Big Bang, the government had set up a new Securities and Investment Board to oversee a cluster of self-regulatory groups. After Guinness, reformers wanted to toughen up the SIB, making it more like the SEC and less of a City-dominated body. There were proposals to outlaw guarantees for third parties who bought shares during a takeover (the “Seelig clause”) and to avert share buying by companies with a commercial stake in the outcome (the “Riklis clause,” after Meshulam Riklis of Schenley Industries). The City shifted further from the old-boy network to a strictly policed financial center.
吉尼斯丑闻对伦敦金融城产生了广泛影响。议会呼吁严惩银行家。作为金融大变革的一部分，政府设立了新的证券与投资委员会（SIB），以监管一系列自律组织。吉尼斯事件后，改革者希望强化 SIB，使其更像美国证券交易委员会（SEC），而不再是一个由金融城主导的机构。有人提议取缔为收购期间购买股票的第三方提供担保的做法（即“西利格条款”），并禁止与结果存在商业利益的公司购买股票（即“里克利斯条款”，以申利工业公司的梅舒拉姆·里克利斯命名）。金融城进一步从旧友网络转向一个严格监管的金融中心。

Awaiting the outcome of the interminable government investigations, Morgan Grenfell found itself in a terrible limbo. In May 1987, Ernest Saunders was arrested for allegedly destroying and fabricating Guinness documents. Later that year, Roger Seelig was arrested and charged with faking £2.5 million in invoices that were used, at least in part, as covers to indemnify members of the “fan club.” Seelig was further charged with conspiring to create a false market and stealing £1 million from Guinness two weeks after the Department of Trade and Industry’s first raid on Morgan Grenfell in early December, 1986. Five other prominent City figures were arrested.
在无休止的政府调查中苦苦等待结果，摩根格兰费尔（Morgan Grenfell）陷入了令人窒息的悬而未决状态。1987 年 5 月，欧内斯特·桑德斯（Ernest Saunders）因涉嫌销毁和伪造健力士（Guinness）文件被捕。同年晚些时候，罗杰·西利格（Roger Seelig）被捕，他被指控伪造了价值 250 万英镑的发票——这些发票至少部分用于向“粉丝俱乐部”成员提供赔偿。此外，西利格还被指控合谋制造虚假市场，并在 1986 年 12 月初英国贸易工业部（Department of Trade and Industry）首次突击搜查摩根格兰费尔的两周后，从健力士窃取了 100 万英镑。另有五位伦敦金融城的知名人物也相继被捕。

That year, the Takeover Panel ruled that Guinness had violated the code in the Distillers battle. This later made Guinness liable for compensatory payments of £85 million to former Distillers shareholders. Guinness could survive such a blow. But if the company turned around and filed a counterclaim against Morgan Grenfell, the smaller bank would clearly stagger. Throughout the DTI investigations, Morgan Grenfell officials lived with this constant nightmare; it was feasible that Guinness could deliver a mortal blow.
那一年，收购与合并委员会裁定，健力士在帝亚吉欧争夺战中违反了相关守则。这一裁决后来迫使健力士向帝亚吉欧的前股东支付 8500 万英镑赔偿。健力士尚能承受这一重创，但如果它反过来向摩根建富提起反诉，这家规模较小的银行显然会摇摇欲坠。在贸易工业部的调查期间，摩根建富的高管们始终活在挥之不去的噩梦中——健力士随时可能给予致命一击。

Short of such an outcome, however, it didn’t seem that Guinness would do lethal damage. The firm’s long-time rival, Warburgs, stepped briskly over its bleeding body to gain the number-one takeover spot, but Morgan Grenfell clung to second place in 1987. Looking only at publicly quoted companies, it actually remained in first place that year. Clients generally stuck by the firm, and there was no mass defection of staff. Perhaps most upset were the Third World countries that dealt with Morgan Grenfell for its classy aura and now had a tainted banker.
然而，若非如此，健力士似乎也不会造成致命伤害。其长期竞争对手沃伯格公司迅速踏过其流血的身躯，夺下并购榜首之位，而摩根建富则在 1987 年屈居第二。若仅看上市公司，它当年实际上仍位居首位。客户普遍仍忠于该行，员工也未出现大规模流失。或许最感不安的是那些曾因摩根建富的高贵光环而与之合作的第三世界国家，如今却面对一个信誉受损的银行家。

For several months in early 1987, Morgan Grenfell remained a ward of the Bank of England, which installed Sir Peter Carey—a short, mustachioed, highly respected former civil servant—as chairman. The firm could no longer indulge its takeover stars and give them free rein. Committees, bureaucracy, regimentation—these were the necessary correctives. Guinness exposed an absence of strong leadership and the managerial defects of an “oligarchy” that ran the firm like a private partnership. These older executives seemed a universe apart from the young takeover artists. As merger specialists were now subordinated to more organizational discipline, some people quit, notably George (“Teddy”) Magan and much of the takeover team in New York.
1987 年初的几个月里，摩根格林菲尔公司（Morgan Grenfell）仍处于英格兰银行的监护之下，后者任命了矮个子、留着小胡子、备受尊敬的前公务员彼得·凯里爵士（Sir Peter Carey）担任董事长。这家公司再也无法纵容其收购明星们自行其是了。委员会、官僚制度、严格管控——这些都是必要的纠偏措施。吉尼斯事件暴露了公司缺乏强有力的领导，以及以私人合伙制方式运营的“寡头”管理层存在的管理缺陷。这些年长的高管与年轻的收购高手们相比，简直是来自两个不同的世界。随着并购专家被要求服从更严格的组织纪律，一些人选择了离职，其中最引人注目的是乔治（“泰迪”）马根（George "Teddy" Magan）以及纽约收购团队的大多数成员。

“They were putting a saddle on a horse that never had one,” said one departing star. “It had been an organic, free-wheeling organization of entrepreneurial spirit. People had booked into that. The most buccaneering of merchant banks started forming committees. But it was a franchise that hadn’t been built on caution and procedures and checks and balances. Reeves and Walsh had created a magic bubble, a hothouse, and there wasn’t the opportunity to do that anymore.” Many people in Britain would have said “Amen.”
“他们正在给一匹从未上过鞍的马套上鞍子，”一位即将离职的明星级人物说道。“这本是一个充满创业精神的、有机而自由奔放的组织。大家当初就是冲着这一点才加入的。那些最具冒险精神的商业银行开始组建各种委员会。但这个品牌的根基从来不是谨慎行事、规章制度和制衡机制。里夫斯和沃尔什创造了一个神奇的泡沫、一个温室，而那样的机会再也不复存在了。”英国许多人大概都会应一声“阿门”。

Shortly after the takeover, a Morgan Grenfell official had told the press, “No man of the calibre of Ernest Saunders is going to employ Morgan Grenfell if we just tell him that Takeover Panel rules suggest that something can’t be done.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438406) This habit of bending or rewriting the rules had been growing remorselessly for twenty years. Now it had led Morgan Grenfell straight over the precipice. The Morgan name had always been synonymous with integrity and trust. Now, with Guinness, it became a byword for scandal in the modern City.
收购发生后不久，一位摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）的高管向媒体放话："如果我们只是告诉欧内斯特·桑德斯（Ernest Saunders）这样的人物，并购委员会（Takeover Panel）的规则说这事儿不能干，那他怎么可能还愿意跟我们合作？" [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2438406) 这种钻空子或改写规则的风气，在过去二十年里愈演愈烈，毫无收敛。如今，这终于把摩根建富直接推下了悬崖。"摩根"这块招牌向来是诚信与信赖的代名词。但经此一役，它却成了现代伦敦金融城里丑闻的同义词。
