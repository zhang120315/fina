# Chapter Twenty-Two: Appeasement

FROM its inception, the House of Morgan had been Anglo-American in spirit and character. The Great War, in particular, fused the London and New York banks in a belief in Anglo-American responsibility for world peace and prosperity. Morgan partners subscribed to an idea expressed by Walter Lippmann in 1915 that U.S. foreign policy would experience a “crowning disaster” if uninformed by “a vision of the Anglo-American future.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2360296) That vision was Morgan dogma, the bedrock of partners’ political beliefs. Yet the Second World War—both its prelude and the early stages, before Pearl Harbor—would prove a divisive experience, exposing tensions between New York and London that had been unacknowledged or long suppressed.
摩根银行自创立之初，便带有英美的精神与特质。尤其是一战，将伦敦与纽约的银行紧密联结，共同信奉英美对世界和平与繁荣负有责任。摩根合伙人赞同沃尔特·李普曼 1915 年提出的观点：美国外交政策若缺乏“对英美共同未来的远见”，必将遭遇“灭顶之灾”。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2360296) 这一远见正是摩根的信条，也是合伙人政治信仰的基石。然而，第二次世界大战——无论是其前奏还是珍珠港事件前的初期阶段——却成为一场引发分裂的经历，暴露出纽约与伦敦之间那些久未承认或长期被压制的紧张关系。

The Anglo-American comradeship had always been a bit one-sided. The Wall Street partners were ardent Anglophiles who celebrated British culture and made annual trips to London. Whether renting a Scottish castle or buying Sir Joshua Reynolds paintings, they identified with the British and affected their manners. This pro-British sentiment owed much to the fact that during most partners’ early adulthood, London stood supreme in world banking. The partners at 23 Wall belonged to a generation that had eagerly boarded transatlantic luxury liners in the early 1900s to partake of British sophistication. Of his first visit to London, Lamont recalled, “For me London was the most thrilling spot that I had ever known or could imagine existed.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2360480) The test of a true J. P. Morgan partner was whether he saw the City as his ancestral home.
英美之间的伙伴关系向来有些失衡。华尔街的合伙人们是狂热的亲英派，他们推崇英国文化，每年都要前往伦敦朝圣。无论是租下苏格兰城堡，还是收藏约书亚·雷诺兹爵士的画作，他们事事以英国人为榜样，连举止做派都刻意模仿。这种亲英情结很大程度上源于：在他们大多初入职场之际，伦敦在全球银行业中占据着至高无上的地位。华尔街 23 号的合伙人们属于这样一代人——20 世纪初，他们迫不及待地登上跨大西洋豪华邮轮，只为感受英伦的精致雅趣。拉蒙特回忆首次伦敦之行时写道："对我而言，伦敦是我所到过或能想象到的最令人心潮澎湃的地方。" [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2360480) 检验一位真正的 J. P. Morgan 合伙人的标准，就是看他是否将伦敦金融城视为精神故乡。

Jack Morgan preferred to be in England, where he wasn’t caricatured as an uncaring plutocrat. He enjoyed the secluded privacy of Wall Hall outside London and had a wood-paneled office at 23 Great Winchester Street. England respected his privacy and was an ideal sanctuary from the strident New Deal denunciations. While Franklin Roosevelt hounded him, British royalty lionized him. George V said he felt comfortable with only two Americans—Jack Morgan and Ambassador Walter Hines Page. (Jack’s granddaughter Jane married Walter Page’s grandson, who bore the ambassador’s name and became a postwar Morgan Guaranty chairman.) After shooting at Gannochy as Jack’s guest, George VI told Sir Gerald Campbell, “I consider Mr. Morgan the world’s greatest gentleman. Whenever he comes into the room, I instinctively feel that I must arise.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2360647) When Lamont reported this, Jack blushingly said it made him feel “a little shy; but it is naturally very pleasant to me to hear of such nice things being said by a man whom I have known for a considerable number of years.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2360810) Jack bounced the king’s daughter, the future Queen Elizabeth, on his knee, and his friendship with the royal family was a factor in Morgan Grenfell’s later handling of a significant share of Elizabeth II’s personal wealth.
杰克·摩根更喜欢待在英国，在那里他不会被人漫画成冷酷的富豪。他享受伦敦城外沃尔庄园的隐秘宁静，并在大温彻斯特街 23 号拥有一间木质镶板的办公室。英国尊重他的隐私，是躲避新政尖刻抨击的理想避风港。当富兰克林·罗斯福穷追不舍时，英国王室却对他推崇备至。乔治五世曾说他只对两个美国人感到自在——杰克·摩根和沃尔特·海因斯·佩奇大使。（杰克的孙女简嫁给了沃尔特·佩奇的孙子，后者继承了大使的名字，后来成为战后摩根担保公司的董事长。）在加诺奇随杰克狩猎后，乔治六世对杰拉德·坎贝尔爵士说：“我认为摩根先生是世界上最伟大的绅士。每当他走进房间，我都会本能地觉得必须起身。””当拉蒙特报告此事时，杰克红着脸说，这让他感到‘有点害羞；不过，听到一位相识多年的老友说出这样暖心的话，我自然非常高兴。’杰克曾把国王的女儿、未来的伊丽莎白女王抱在膝上逗弄，他与王室的交情后来也成为摩根建富打理伊丽莎白二世大量个人财富的重要因素之一。”

Morgan Grenfell partners never fully reciprocated this admiration. Despite their real affection for the New York partners, they weren’t enthralled by American history and probably found the country charming but provincial. By the late 1930s, several of the London partners were exalted personages, peers of the realm—Grenfell (Lord Saint Just), Smith (Lord Bicester), and Tom Catto (Lord Catto). Their institutional ties bound them as strongly to the British power structure as to their New York brethren. Smith was governor of the Royal Exchange Assurance Company and chaired the City of London Conservative and Unionist Association. Grenfell—now suffering from heart and lung problems and laid up with a patch on his lung—was a member of Parliament and a Bank of England director and had worked a Bank of England symbol into his coat of arms.
摩根格伦费尔公司的合伙人从未完全回报这种钦佩。尽管他们对纽约的合伙人怀有真挚感情，但他们并未对美国历史着迷，或许只觉得这个国家迷人却略显土气。到 20 世纪 30 年代末，伦敦的几位合伙人已成为显赫人物，跻身贵族之列——格伦费尔（圣贾斯特勋爵）、史密斯（比斯特勋爵）和汤姆·卡托（卡托勋爵）。他们与英国权力结构的机构纽带，如同与纽约同僚的联系一样牢固。史密斯担任皇家交易保险公司总裁，并主持伦敦金融城保守党与统一党协会。格伦费尔——此时正受心肺疾病困扰，肺部贴着膏药卧床休养——同时是议会议员和英格兰银行董事，还将英格兰银行的标志融入了自己的盾形纹章中。

J. P. Morgan and Company had always hired gifted outsiders—Perkins, Davison, Morrow, Lamont, and Leffingwell—who rose on the strength of their intelligence. Morgan Grenfell recruited from a smaller circle of family members and friends. This would give the firm an inbred feeling, a genteel hothouse atmosphere, and a stuffy complacency that would make it dangerously ossified by the 1950s. Lord Bicester’s son Rufus became a partner, and Francis Rodd, the son of a former British ambassador to Rome, was married to Rufus’s sister. Morgan Grenfell partners displayed an upper-crust insularity. The first Lord Bicester, Vivian Hugh Smith, is the best example. As squire of Tusmore Park in Oxfordshire, he indulged a mad passion for steeplechase horses. Every year he went to Ireland to buy them and was frustrated in his great ambition of winning the Grand National. In a remark that some might have deemed insulting—but Bicester doubtless treasured—Lamont told him, “It is a great life you lead. You are my ideal of the English gentleman of the Victorian Age.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361003) These weren’t the sort of people to be enamored of American culture.
J.P.摩根公司向来青睐外部英才——珀金斯、戴维森、莫罗、拉蒙特和莱芬韦尔，无不是凭才干崭露头角。而摩根格伦费尔却局限于家族与友人的小圈子招贤纳士。这给公司注入了一种近亲繁殖的气息，一派温文尔雅的温室氛围，以及令人自满的迂腐，到 20 世纪 50 年代时，这种僵化已危如累卵。比斯特勋爵的儿子鲁弗斯成了合伙人，曾任英国驻罗马大使的儿子弗朗西斯·罗德则娶了鲁弗斯的妹妹。摩根格伦费尔的合伙人尽显上流社会的孤傲之气。第一代比斯特勋爵维维安·休·史密斯便是绝佳写照。作为牛津郡塔斯莫尔庄园的主人，他对越野障碍赛马痴迷成狂。每年他都会赴爱尔兰购马，却始终未能实现在英国国家障碍赛马大赛中夺冠的夙愿。拉蒙特对他说过一句或许会被视为冒犯——但比斯特无疑珍视的话：“你过得可真是好日子。” “你是维多利亚时代英国绅士的完美典范。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361003) 这种人可不会对美国文化着迷。

After Glass-Steagall, J. P. Morgan and Company not only became a minority shareholder in Morgan Grenfell but became more distanced from its affairs. As Lamont explained, “Morgan Grenfell & Co. considers that business done through them is their business.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361197) After J. P. Morgan chose commercial banking, New York and London couldn’t issue securities together, as they had in the 1920s. And foreign lending was down throughout the Depression. Hobbled by a weak pound and government restrictions on overseas lending, the City’s merchant banks, tired and unimaginative, entered a deep sleep from which they wouldn’t awaken until the aluminium war of the late 1950s.
格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案之后，J. P. 摩根公司不仅成为摩根建富的少数股东，还进一步与其事务疏远。正如拉蒙特解释的那样：“摩根建富公司认为，通过他们进行的业务就是他们自己的业务。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361197) 在 J. P. 摩根选择商业银行之后，纽约和伦敦不能再像 20 世纪 20 年代那样共同发行证券。整个大萧条时期，海外贷款都在下降。受英镑疲软和政府对外贷款限制的拖累，伦敦城的商业银行疲惫不堪、缺乏创意，陷入沉睡，直到 20 世纪 50 年代末的铝业战争才苏醒过来。

The most serious threat to J. P. Morgan-Morgan Grenfell unity was over foreign debt, which, like a bad hangover, remained from the 1920s lending binge. The first split had occurred with German debt. The Nazi policy of selective defaults generated ill will between the Morgan houses in London and New York. Then in March 1938, it looked as if history would repeat itself. Hitler ordered his troops into Austria and made a triumphant entry into Vienna, cheered by ecstatic crowds. Fulfilling his *Mein Kampf* prophecy, he reduced Austria to a German province while the Gestapo unleashed a wave of violence against Jews and other undesirables.
对摩根-摩根格伦费尔联合体统一性最严重的威胁来自外债问题，这笔债务如同宿醉般挥之不去，源自 20 年代疯狂放贷的遗留。第一次分裂源于德国债务。纳粹选择性违约的政策在伦敦和纽约的摩根公司之间制造了敌意。随后在 1938 年 3 月，历史似乎要重演。希特勒命令军队进入奥地利，并在狂喜人群的欢呼声中凯旋进入维也纳。他实现了《我的奋斗》中的预言，将奥地利降格为德国的一个省份，而盖世太保则对犹太人及其他不受欢迎群体掀起了一波暴力浪潮。

The J. P. Morgan and Company partners immediately feared default on a huge 1930 Austrian reconstruction loan. No less than in Pierpont’s day, the bank had a fanatic sense of responsibility toward bonds it had issued. The British portion of the loan had been managed by several London banks, including Morgan Grenfell. Would the Nazis honor Austrian debt? Or would they classify it with German reparations loans and claim it was foisted upon Austria by the Allies? Most important, would Germany again cut a separate deal with England?
J. P. 摩根公司的合伙人立刻担心起 1930 年一笔巨额奥地利重建贷款可能违约。与皮尔庞特时代如出一辙，摩根对自身发行的债券怀有一种近乎狂热的责任感。这笔贷款的英国部分由包括摩根建富在内的几家伦敦银行负责管理。纳粹会承认奥地利债务吗？还是将其与德国战争赔款混为一谈，声称这是协约国强加给奥地利的？最关键的是，德国会不会再次绕过其他国家，单独与英国达成协议？

Hjalmar Schacht’s power had continued to wane. Increasingly disgruntled with the Nazis, he feared the inflationary consequences of Germany’s military buildup: he had defiantly told his arch rival, Göring, “Your foreign-exchange policy, your policy regarding production, and your financial policy \[are\] unsound.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361389)
亚尔马·沙赫特的权势持续衰落。他对纳粹政权愈发不满，忧心德国扩军备战可能引发通货膨胀，甚至公然向死对头戈林放话：“你的外汇政策、生产政策以及财政政策，全都站不住脚。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361389)

After the Austrian Anschluss, Schacht said, he secretly lost all sympathy with Hitler and began to contemplate his overthrow. But his apostasy was carefully disguised. Schacht was charged with running Austria’s National Bank and subordinating its financial system to German monetary policy. Two weeks after the bloodless invasion, he assembled the staff of the central bank and delivered a terrifying speech: “Not a single person will find a future with us who is not wholeheartedly for Adolph Hitler. . . . The Reichsbank will always be nothing but National Socialist or I shall cease to be its manager.” After administering a loyalty oath to the Fiihrer, he led the bank staff in a brisk chanting of *“Sieg Heil!”*[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361553) Schacht fired Dr. Kienbock, the Austrian banker who had offered Gobelin tapestries to Morgans as loan collateral in the early 1920s. With his usual self-congratulatory bent, Schacht later explained, “I saw to it that he was able to retire on a full pension and with flying colors though he was known to be of partly Jewish extraction.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361731) The old Jewish Viennese banks were torn asunder. Baron Louis von Rothschild was arrested, jailed, and released only after signing over all Rothschild assets in Austria to the state.
沙赫特说，在奥地利被吞并之后，他暗自对希特勒彻底失去了好感，并开始谋划推翻他。但他的背叛行为被精心掩饰了起来。沙赫特受命管理奥地利国家银行，并将其金融体系纳入德国货币政策之下。在兵不血刃的入侵两周后，他召集中央银行全体员工，发表了一番令人胆寒的讲话：“任何不是全心全意拥护阿道夫·希特勒的人，在我们这里都不会有未来……帝国银行永远只能是国家社会主义的银行，否则我就不再担任其行长。”在对元首宣誓效忠后，他带领银行员工高喊“胜利万岁！” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361553) 沙赫特解雇了基恩博克博士——这位奥地利银行家曾在 20 世纪 20 年代初将戈布兰挂毯作为贷款抵押品提供给摩根家族。 沙赫特以他惯常的自鸣得意口吻后来说道：“尽管此人被公认为有部分犹太血统，我还是设法让他带着全额退休金风风光光地退了休。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361731) 那些古老的维也纳犹太银行纷纷解体。路易·冯·罗斯柴尔德男爵被捕入狱，直到将罗斯柴尔德家族在奥地利的所有资产移交给国家后才获释。

The House of Morgan closely monitored German speeches about Austrian debt. Before long, Walther Funk, who had replaced Göring as economics minister just before the Anschluss, was making statements that equated Austrian loans with German loans and claiming that they, too, were made by the Allies merely to ensure reparations. He ranted about scheming bankers and craven politicians who had conspired to draw Germany into “debt and interest slavery.” In New York, Lamont watched nervously for signs of a deal between England and the Nazis. On April 25, 1938, his son, Tommy, spotted an item in the London financial press that alerted them to an impending settlement. “In other words,” Tommy said, “. . . our good friends in the Bank of England and the City are contemplating pulling a fast one to the disadvantage of the American holders of Austrian bonds.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361907)
摩根财团密切关注着德国关于奥地利债务的言论。没过多久，就在德奥合并前接替戈林出任经济部长的瓦尔特·丰克，开始将奥地利贷款等同于德国贷款，并声称这些贷款同样是协约国为收取赔款而设。他大肆抨击那些阴谋将德国拖入"债务和利息奴役"的诡计多端的银行家和懦弱政客。在纽约，拉蒙特紧张地观察着英国与纳粹之间达成协议的迹象。1938 年 4 月 25 日，他的儿子汤米在伦敦金融报刊上发现了一条消息，暗示即将达成和解。"换句话说，"汤米说，"……我们在英格兰银行和伦敦金融城的好朋友们，正在盘算着耍花招，损害美国奥地利债券持有者的利益。" [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2361907)

Lamont was furious: the man who never got angry flew into a rage. To Sir Frederick Leith-Ross, the reparations expert at the British Treasury, he wrote a letter in stiletto-sharp prose. Recalling the 1934 British deal with Germany, he said:
拉蒙特怒不可遏——这个从不发火的人竟勃然大怒。他给英国财政部赔款专家弗雷德里克·莱斯-罗斯爵士写了一封信，措辞如匕首般锋利。信中他忆及 1934 年英国与德国的交易时写道：

> I am recalling all this not in a spirit, my dear Leith, of anything except good will in pointing out to you the advantage of considering American interests in connection with the 1930 Austrian loan. The new fashion in the world is that every country should develop its own nationalism to the nth degree. But over here when our people listen to polite inquiries from our British friends as to what America’s attitude might be in the case of Britain’s becoming involved in a general war, the inclination is to wonder a little why the British sometimes overlook these matters (like the Dawes and Young loan matter) which are small in themselves, but which constitute an unceasing cause of irritation.
> 我亲爱的莱斯，我重提这些往事并非心怀怨气，而是出于善意，想向你指出在 1930 年奥地利贷款问题上兼顾美国利益的好处。当今世界的新风尚是每个国家都把自己的民族主义推向极致。但在我们这里，当国人听到英国朋友彬彬有礼地探询——若英国卷入一场全面战争，美国会作何态度时，他们不免有些纳闷：为何英国人有时会忽略那些本身虽小、却不断构成刺激源的事（比如道威斯和杨格贷款事宜）？

Lamont ended by alluding to the State Department’s “deep interest” in the Austrian loan.[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362102)
拉蒙特最后提到国务院对奥地利贷款“深感兴趣”。 [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362102)

While Lamont’s exquisite courtesy toward the British now turned into elegant taunts and insults, his warnings proved fruitless. Schacht and Monty Norman kept up their mysterious dialogue, meeting monthly at the Bank for International Settlements in Basel. In June, an Anglo-German debt settlement was announced in Parliament, and professions of British-American financial solidarity yielded to brazen opportunism. It is interesting to note that Neville Chamberlain, in his desire to appease Germany, was indifferent to reports of Schacht’s secret defection from Hitler. That summer in Basel, Schacht told Norman of his decision to abandon Hitler and work for his overthrow. When Norman repeated this to Chamberlain, the prime minister retorted, “Who is Schacht? I have to deal with Hitler.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362296)
尽管拉蒙特对英国人的那份无微不至的礼貌已化作优雅的嘲讽与羞辱，但他的警告终究是徒劳无功。沙赫特和蒙蒂·诺曼继续着他们那场神秘的对话，每月在巴塞尔的国际清算银行碰头。六月，议会宣布了英德债务解决方案，而英美金融团结的宣言，也让位给了厚颜无耻的投机主义。值得玩味的是，内维尔·张伯伦一心想要安抚德国，对沙赫特暗中背离希特勒的传闻根本无动于衷。那年夏天在巴塞尔，沙赫特告诉诺曼，他决定抛弃希特勒并致力于将其推翻。当诺曼将此事转告张伯伦时，这位首相反驳道：“沙赫特是谁？我打交道的是希特勒。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362296)

How did Britain justify its deal? Norman told Lamont that Britain tried to settle Austrian debt on a nonpartisan international basis but that the Nazis insisted on discriminatory treatment. At the same time, the British—echoing Schacht’s viewpoint—said they were running a trade deficit with Germany and that Austrian debt repayments would recycle to Britain some of the money they were paying for German goods. It was a depressing reversal from the Diplomatic Age of the 1920s. Monty Norman—the man who wanted to lift finance above the muddy realm of politics and into the clear air—now submitted to nationalistic pressures. With his usual theatrics, he wrote Lamont a lachrymose explanation: “For few debtor countries nowadays are willing to treat debts from the standpoint of ethics and equity and not from the standpoint of politics and convenience. . . . You cannot answer this because I am going away for a long time to heal my wound and I only write to clear your views and my conscience!”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362473)
英国是如何为其交易辩护的？诺曼告诉拉蒙特，英国试图在非党派的国际基础上解决奥地利债务问题，但纳粹坚持歧视性待遇。与此同时，英国附和沙赫特的观点，称他们对德国存在贸易逆差，而奥地利偿还的债务将使英国从支付德国商品的款项中收回部分资金。这是对 1920 年代外交时代的令人沮丧的逆转。蒙蒂·诺曼——那个曾想将金融提升到政治的泥潭之上、带入清澈空气的人——如今屈服于民族主义压力。他带着惯有的戏剧性，给拉蒙特写了一封悲切的解释：“因为如今很少有债务国愿意从伦理和公平的角度，而非政治和便利的角度来看待债务……你无法回复这封信，因为我将长期离开以疗愈创伤，我写这封信只是为了阐明你的观点、抚平我的良心！”

The feud between J. P. Morgan and Morgan Grenfell lingered; that the latter had placed British interests ahead of joint Morgan interests could not be lightly dismissed. In a tone he usually reserved for the browbeating of debtors, Lamont warned the London partners not to take Anglo-American cooperation for granted in the event of war—a shockingly grave threat. He wrote, “Must we accept that the high sanction of Great Britain is to be given to the growing habit of ignoring international connections and the rights of property?”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362629) This sort of reprimand would have surprised isolationists, who saw only collusion between the House of Morgan and England.
J. P. Morgan 与 Morgan Grenfell 之间的宿怨挥之不去；Morgan Grenfell 将英国利益置于摩根共同利益之上，这一事实不容轻描淡写。Lamont 用他通常用来恐吓债务人的语气，警告伦敦的合伙人不要想当然地认为一旦开战英美合作会顺理成章——这是一个极其严重的威胁。他写道：“难道我们非得接受英国的高层认可，去姑息那种日益盛行的、无视国际联系与财产权的行为吗？” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362629) 这种斥责会让孤立主义者大吃一惊，因为他们只看到了摩根家族与英国之间的暗中勾结。

Apparently fearing the Austrian feud would imperil Anglo-American financial relations, Francis Rodd circulated Lamont’s letter at the British Treasury—without consulting New York. When Lamont learned of this, he exploded, believing his letter had been sent in strict confidence and could damage Morgan relations with the Treasury and the Bank of England. He sent a stinging rebuke to 23 Great Winchester Street:
显然担心奥地利冲突会危及英美金融关系，弗朗西斯·罗德在没有征询纽约意见的情况下，将拉蒙特的信函传阅给了英国财政部。拉蒙特得知后勃然大怒，认为自己的信是严格保密的，此举可能损害摩根与财政部及英格兰银行的关系。他向大温彻斯特街 23 号发出了一封严厉的斥责信。

> You are aware that for generations past the partners of our house have always felt it to the great advantage of both our countries that the friendliest possible relations should exist between them. . . . As you know now we never meant our letter to be filed with the British Treasury. . . . There are lots of things one can say to a man that he cannot write to him, and that is a thousand times more true with regard to governments. The priceless value of Morgan Grenfell & Co. to us . . . has been precisely in the ability of the partners of Morgan Grenfell & Co. to interpret us to the British Treasury and the British Treasury to us. We have never thought of Morgan Grenfell & Co. as a post office for the transmission of our letters to the British Government.[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362816)
> 您应该知道，我们公司的前辈们始终认为，我们两国之间保持最友好的关系对双方都极为有利……如您所知，我们从未打算将那封信提交给英国财政部……有些话可以当面告诉一个人，却无法写在信里，而这一点在政府之间更是千真万确。Morgan Grenfell & Co. 对我们的无价之处……恰恰在于其合伙人能够向我们解读英国财政部，同时向英国财政部解读我们。我们从未将 Morgan Grenfell & Co. 视为向英国政府转交信件的邮局。 [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2362816)

The pitfalls of the Anglo-American Morgan relationship were here apparent: did Morgan Grenfell represent the British government to J. P. Morgan or J. P. Morgan to the British government? How could New York partners expect Morgan Grenfell to be so intimate with Whitehall yet detached at the same time? These question had never been adequately posed, much less answered, because no serious conflict had arisen during the 1920s, the heyday of financial internationalism. Now the nationalistic squabbling of the 1930s destroyed many illusions about the supposed allegiance of the London partners to J. P. Morgan and Company. The “Trojan horse” strategy followed since the early 1900s—of giving the London house a British complexion and character—had, in the last analysis, backfired on the New York house.
英美摩根关系中的陷阱在此显而易见：Morgan Grenfell 究竟是代表英国政府与 J. P. Morgan 打交道，还是代表 J. P. Morgan 与英国政府沟通？纽约的合伙人如何能期望 Morgan Grenfell 既与 Whitehall 保持密切关系，同时又与之保持距离？这些问题在 20 世纪 20 年代金融国际主义的鼎盛时期从未被充分提出，更谈不上解答了——因为当时并未爆发严重的利益冲突。如今，20 世纪 30 年代民族主义的争吵，摧毁了人们对伦敦合伙人理应效忠 J. P. Morgan and Company 的诸多幻想。自 20 世纪初以来一直奉行的“特洛伊木马”策略——即赋予伦敦分行一副英国面孔与性格——最终反而对纽约本部构成了反噬。

THE New York partners traveled in aristocratic British circles and were frequent visitors at the Astor estate at Cliveden. No less than the House of Morgan itself, Nancy Astor represented a marriage of American capital and British aristocracy. Born Nancy Langhorne in Virginia, she ended up as the first woman to hold a seat in the House of Commons (having campaigned for office in pearls and accompanied by a liveried coachman). A stylish, pretty woman with a sharp tongue and a zest for political rows, she liked to heckle, tease, and argue. Once, while visiting her adversary Winston Churchill at Blenheim, Astor said, “If I were married to you, I would put poison in your coffee.” Churchill replied, “And if I were married to you, I would drink it.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363007)
纽约的合作伙伴们穿梭于英国贵族圈，常出入阿斯特家族在克利夫登的庄园。南希·阿斯特与摩根财团一样，都是美国资本与英国贵族联姻的产物。她出生于弗吉尼亚，原名南希·兰霍恩，最终成为英国下议院首位女议员（竞选时她佩戴珍珠首饰，由身着制服的马车夫陪同）。这位时尚貌美的女性言辞犀利，热衷政治论战，喜欢诘问、戏谑和辩论。一次在布伦海姆宫拜访对手温斯顿·丘吉尔时，阿斯特说：“如果我嫁给你，我会往你的咖啡里下毒。”丘吉尔答道：“如果我娶了你，我会喝下那杯咖啡。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363007)

Nancy was married to the rich but feckless Waldorf Astor, second viscount and the grandson of John Jacob Astor III. Waldorf drew the bulk of his income from rentals of his Manhattan real estate holdings, so the transatlantic structure of the House of Morgan perfectly suited his business needs. Waldorf also consulted Tom Lamont about his personal finances, and Lamont had switched him out of American securities and into Canadian municipal bonds after the 1929 crash. The Lamonts and the Astors socialized and even vacationed together.
南希嫁给了富有但无能的沃尔多夫·阿斯特，他是第二子爵，也是约翰·雅各布·阿斯特三世的孙子。沃尔多夫的主要收入来自他在曼哈顿持有的房地产租金，因此摩根银行的跨洋结构完美契合了他的商业需求。沃尔多夫还就个人财务问题咨询汤姆·拉蒙特，而拉蒙特在 1929 年股市崩盘后，已将他名下的美国证券换成加拿大市政债券。拉蒙特一家和阿斯特一家经常社交往来，甚至一起度假。

Lady Astor bewitched Tom Lamont, and for twenty years they kept up an abundant correspondence. There was a likeness between them. Both were romantics with a taste for *noblesse oblige*, self-invented aristocrats who had acted out extravagant dreams and confidently inhabited their stations. From government offices, Cunard staterooms, hotel rooms—even once while Astor set her hair—the two exchanged long, often effusive letters. They traded gossip, personal confessions, and political intelligence. After the Richard Whitney scandal, Lamont sent her clippings to establish his innocence and Astor replied, “Dearest Tom, I don’t have to read your cuttings, or anything else for that matter, to know that you would never do wrong. Such is my affection for you!”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363160)
阿斯特夫人令汤姆·拉蒙特神魂颠倒，二十年间两人书信往来频繁。他们骨子里有些相似：都是崇尚贵族责任感的浪漫主义者，都是自我塑造的贵族，把天马行空的梦想活成了现实，心安理得地居于各自的位置。从政府办公室到丘纳德邮轮的客舱，从酒店房间——甚至有一次阿斯特还在卷头发——两人交换着一封封长篇累牍、往往热情洋溢的信件。他们互通八卦、倾诉私密、交流政治情报。理查德·惠特尼丑闻爆发后，拉蒙特把剪报寄给她以证清白，阿斯特回信说：“最亲爱的汤姆，我根本不用看你的剪报，也不需要别的什么证明，就知道你绝不会做错事。这就是我对你的感情！”

Their correspondence had a vaguely romantic cast. Lamont termed Lady Astor “the kindest-hearted and best friend in the world” and called her “the girl I love most.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363347) In his inimitable fashion, he showered her with gifts and favors. He could break down anybody’s resistance, conquer anyone with charm, such was his genius for cultivating friends. Golfing with him at Cliveden in 1930, she had admired a set of clubs owned by another guest, Frank Kellogg, until recently the U.S. secretary of state. Back on Wall Street, Lamont tracked down the original manufacturer and had identical clubs made for her. “I am really ridiculously excited and grateful,” she wrote back.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363575) Another time, Lamont slipped away from 23 Wall, went uptown, and bought her two frocks at Saks Fifth Avenue. It was a warm friendship, indeed.
他们的来往信件带有几分浪漫色彩。拉蒙特称阿斯特夫人是“世上最善良、最好的朋友”，还叫她“我最爱的女孩”。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363347) 他以自己独特的方式，送她礼物、施以恩惠。他能瓦解任何人的抵抗，用魅力征服所有人，他结交朋友的天赋就是如此了得。1930 年，阿斯特夫人在克利夫登与他打高尔夫时，看中了另一位客人弗兰克·凯洛格（不久前还是美国国务卿）的一套球杆。回到华尔街后，拉蒙特找到了原生产商，为她订做了一套一模一样的球杆。“我真的又兴奋又感激，简直有点荒唐了，”她回信道。 [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363575) 另一次，拉蒙特悄悄溜出华尔街 23 号，去了上城区，在萨克斯第五大道给她买了两条裙子。这确实是一段温暖的友情。

On the eve of World War II, Lamont’s friendship with the Astors took on important political dimensions. Cliveden, the Astor estate on the Thames, had become a gathering place for politicians and intellectuals who favored appeasement of the Nazis. They thought England could coexist with Hitler, feared a war would shatter the British Empire, and supported the appeasement policies of Stanley Baldwin and Neville Chamberlain. In time, the name Cliveden became synonymous with a phobic hatred of Russia, a benign or even admiring view of Fascist intentions, and a rejection of Churchill’s warnings about German rearmament.
二战前夕，拉蒙特与阿斯特家族的友谊承载了重要的政治维度。泰晤士河畔的阿斯特庄园克利夫登，成为了主张对纳粹采取绥靖政策的政客与知识分子聚集之地。他们认为英国能与希特勒共存，担心战争会摧毁大英帝国，因而支持斯坦利·鲍德温和内维尔·张伯伦的绥靖政策。久而久之，“克利夫登”一词便成了对俄罗斯的病态仇视、对法西斯意图的宽容甚至崇拜，以及对丘吉尔关于德国重整军备警告的排斥之代名词。

Like his Cliveden friends, Lamont believed that Europe’s dictators could be held at bay through diplomacy and that war could be avoided. He also thought Britain and France were woefully unprepared for war. To some extent, Lamont and his partners were still cowed by the Nye committee charges that they had been “merchants of death” in World War I. They weren’t eager to stick their necks out in support of another war. “As for our dictators, Hitler and Mussolini,” Lamont wrote Lady Astor in 1937, “they don’t seem to have changed their spots very much, but I seem to think that raging at them will do no good, and if there is a possibility of methods of appeasement, these are our only chance.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363765) Earlier, Lamont had asked Lady Astor to lobby the Foreign Office in support of its recognizing Mussolini’s conquest in Ethiopia. When Hitler took over Austria, Lamont assured her that his Italian friends were “aghast” at the coup and said their view must surely reflect il Duce’s own horror. Right up to the war, he believed that Italy had sided with the Germans only under extreme duress.
与他的 Cliveden 朋友一样，拉蒙特相信通过外交手段可以遏制欧洲的独裁者，战争是可以避免的。他还认为英法两国对战争毫无准备，情况令人担忧。在某种程度上，拉蒙特和他的合伙人们仍被奈委员会指控他们在第一次世界大战中充当"死亡商人"的阴影所笼罩。他们并不急于为支持另一场战争而冒险。"至于我们面对的独裁者希特勒和墨索里尼，"拉蒙特在 1937 年写信给阿斯特夫人说，"他们似乎本性难移，但我认为对他们发怒无济于事，如果存在绥靖政策的可能，那便是我们唯一的机会。" [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363765) 早些时候，拉蒙特曾请阿斯特夫人游说外交部，支持其承认墨索里尼对埃塞俄比亚的征服。当希特勒占领奥地利时，拉蒙特向她保证，他的意大利朋友对这场政变感到"惊骇"，并表示他们的看法必然反映了领袖本人的恐惧。 直至战争爆发前，他一直认为意大利是在极度胁迫下才与德国结盟的。

Lamont took a more alarmist view of events in the Pacific. He had never fully recovered from his sense of betrayal by the Japanese militarists, and this only deepened his sense of their malevolence. During Japan’s fierce aggression against China in July 1937 and the slaughter of thousands of Chinese civilians in the rape of Nanking, he spied a design to subdue all of East Asia. He didn’t mince words with Japanese businessmen who made overtures to the bank. In September 1937, he assured the Japanese consul general that he would not “find one American in one hundred thousand who is not shocked and distressed beyond measure” by Japanese military operations in and around Shanghai.[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363952) (In fact, a few weeks later Russell Leffingwell told Lamont that China would fare better under Japanese domination.[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364141)) In contrast with his gullible acceptance of the Mukden incident of 1931, an irate Lamont now protested to the Bank of Japan that “faked stories” about China were being circulated by Japan all over the world.[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364350)
拉蒙特对太平洋地区的局势持更为危言耸听的看法。他从未完全从日本军国主义者带给他的背叛感中恢复过来，这反而加深了他对日本人的恶意认知。1937 年 7 月日本悍然侵略中国，并在南京大屠杀中残害成千上万中国平民时，他察觉到了日本企图征服整个东亚的阴谋。当有日本商人向银行示好时，他直言不讳。1937 年 9 月，他向日本总领事保证，对于日军在上海及周边地区的军事行动，“十万个美国人里找不出一个不感到极度震惊和悲痛的”。（事实上，几周后拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔告诉拉蒙特，中国若受日本统治反而会过得更好。） [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2363952) [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364141) ) 与他在 1931 年轻信并接受九一八事变的态度形成鲜明对比的是，如今愤怒的拉蒙特向日本银行抗议，指出日本正在全世界散布关于中国的“假新闻”。 [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364350)

In September 1938, Neville Chamberlain flew to Munich and capitulated to Hitler’s demand for the Sudetenland. Hitler forswore further territorial ambitions, and Chamberlain hoped that the partition of Czechoslovakia would sate the dictator’s appetite for conquest. In accepting the Munich Pact, the British cabinet wasn’t completely naive about Hitler’s intentions: many thought England needed time to mount an expensive rearmament program and that war with Germany would be suicidal. Returning to Downing Street talking of “peace with honour,” Chamberlain received a tumultuous welcome. The London *Times* said, “No conqueror returning from a victory on the battlefield has come adorned with nobler laurels.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364534) Amid a rapturous greeting in the House of Commons, Churchill was the sour, lonely voice of dissent, branding Munich a “total and unmitigated defeat.” He was predictably heckled by Nancy Astor.[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364707)
1938 年 9 月，内维尔·张伯伦飞往慕尼黑，向希特勒对苏台德地区的要求低头。希特勒发誓不再觊觎更多领土，张伯伦则指望瓜分捷克斯洛伐克能满足这位独裁者的征服欲。接受《慕尼黑协定》时，英国内阁并非全然天真地看待希特勒的意图——许多人认为英国需要时间推行代价高昂的军备重整计划，而与德国开战无异于自取灭亡。回到唐宁街，张伯伦口口声声谈论“光荣的和平”，他受到了狂热的欢迎。《伦敦泰晤士报》写道：“没有哪位从战场凯旋的征服者，能比此刻的他佩戴更崇高的桂冠。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364534) 在下议院一片欢呼致意中，丘吉尔是那个尖刻而孤独的反对声音，他将慕尼黑斥为“一场彻头彻尾、无可挽回的失败”。不出所料，南希·阿斯特当即对他进行了一番奚落。 [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364707)

The House of Morgan stoutly supported Munich. In a flight of fancy, Lamont predicted a new German regime within two years. Jack Morgan was sure that in the end Hitler would have to be stopped forcibly. In the meantime, he thought his friend Chamberlain had bought valuable time. “What an achievement!” he wrote to the prime minister in breathless tones. “I little thought when you were at Gannochy at tea and I said I had a hunch that there would be no war and you said hunches were the only thing to go on, and that you had the same hunch as I did, that you were going to be the one to have the imagination and courage to make that hunch come true! It never occurred to me that a single man could do so much by sheer force of courage, fairness and reasonableness.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2364877) With rather heavy snickering, Jack said that if Churchill or Lloyd George were in charge, the world would have been at war long ago.[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365051) Vivian Smith, now Lord Bicester, was more muted in his support of Munich, warning that Hitler was a “fanatic” and that Göring and Goebbels were “gangsters” using the Nazis as a cloak for their evil-doing.[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365186) While congratulating the Morgan Grenfell partners that Chamberlain had averted war, Russell Leffingwell privately lamented to Lamont that Britain had submitted to blackmail.[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365400)
摩根财团坚定地支持了慕尼黑协定。拉蒙特一时异想天开，预言德国两年内会建立新政权。杰克·摩根则认为，希特勒最终必须用武力阻止。与此同时，他觉得自己的朋友张伯伦买到了宝贵的时间。“多么了不起的成就！”他以激动不已的语气写信给首相，“当你在 Gannochy 喝茶时，我说我预感不会爆发战争，你说预感是唯一可凭借的东西，而且你和我有同样的预感——我万万没想到，你竟会是那个凭借想象力与勇气让这预感成真的人！我从未想过，单凭一个人的勇气、公正和理性，竟能成就如此之多。”杰克带着几分轻蔑的窃笑说，如果当时是丘吉尔或劳合·乔治掌权，世界早就陷入战火了。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365051) 如今已是比斯特勋爵的维维安·史密斯，对慕尼黑协定的支持语气更为克制，他警告说希特勒是个“狂热分子”，而戈林和戈培尔则是“流氓恶棍”，借着纳粹的外衣为非作歹。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365186) 拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔在祝贺摩根建富的合伙人张伯伦避免了战争的同时，私下向拉蒙特哀叹，英国竟然屈服于勒索。 [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365400)

Hitler was puffed up with the success of his blackmail. In March 1939, he devoured the rest of Czechoslovakia, and the German army marched into Prague. Czechoslovakia’s extinction shattered the appeasement movement. Nancy Astor’s intimate friend Lord Lothian sent a despondent note to Lamont saying he had abandoned hope of decent behavior from that gangster, Hitler. Two days later, Lady Astor herself urged Chamberlain to condemn Germany. By the end of the month, Chamberlain reversed his course and guaranteed Poland’s independence.[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365568)
希特勒因勒索得手而得意忘形。1939 年 3 月，他吞并了捷克斯洛伐克的其余部分，德军开进布拉格。捷克斯洛伐克的覆灭粉碎了绥靖运动。南希·阿斯特的密友洛锡安勋爵给拉蒙特发去一封沮丧的信，表示他对那个恶棍希特勒体面行事的希望已经破灭。两天后，阿斯特夫人本人敦促张伯伦谴责德国。到 3 月底，张伯伦改弦更张，保证波兰的独立。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365568)

The British public dealt harshly with the complacency Baldwin and Chamberlain had shown in the face of the German threat. Political adulation turned into vitriolic abuse as the British solidly closed ranks behind a determined response to Hitler. In America, however, the division of public opinion toward the European turmoil only grew more contentious. For Morgan partners in New York, it was an especially problematic dispute. As Lamont had warned Morgan Grenfell and the British Treasury, there was a residue of pent-up American hostility left over from the financial disputes of the 1930s. And the power of American isolationists was such that the bank couldn’t immediately proclaim the proud, unalloyed support for Britain that it had in 1914. J. P. Morgan and Company would find itself in the uncomfortable position of antagonizing the isolationists for doing too much for Britain while disappointing the British for not doing enough.
英国公众对鲍德温和张伯伦在面对德国威胁时所表现出的自满态度给予了严厉的批评。当英国上下团结一致，决心对希特勒作出强硬回应时，昔日的政治吹捧瞬间变成了尖刻的谩骂。然而在美国，公众舆论对欧洲动荡的分歧却愈发激烈。对于纽约的摩根合伙人来说，这场争论尤其棘手。正如拉蒙特曾警告摩根建富和英国财政部的那样，1930 年代的金融争端在美国人心中留下了未消的敌意。而美国孤立主义者的势力之强，使得摩根银行无法像 1914 年那样立即高调宣布对英国毫无保留的支持。J. P. Morgan and Company 陷入了两难境地：为英国做得太多会激怒孤立主义者，做得太少又会让英国人失望。

An indirect casualty of Munich was Hjalmar Schacht, who had joined in the secret generals’ plot of September 1938 to overthrow Hitler. He later claimed the conspirators were disheartened by the Allies’ cowardice at Munich. Schacht’s standing in Nazi Germany had grown precarious in late 1938. At the Reichsbank Christmas party, held several weeks after the burning of Jewish shops and synagogues on Kristallnacht, he deplored such actions. In early 1939, the deluded Schacht still churned out Reichsbank memos on the need to cut inflationary arms expenditures, as if Hitler cared about neoclassical economics. In London that December, he presented a plan for the emigration of fifty thousand Jews from Germany—to be paid for with all their belongings and ransom payments from the world Jewish community. In the first week of January, Monty Norman made a last trip to Germany, to attend the christening of his godson—Schacht’s grandson Norman Hjalmar—named in tribute to him. When Hitler fired Schacht from the Reichsbank on January 20, Norman belatedly awoke to the full horror of the Nazi menace.
慕尼黑协定的一个间接牺牲品是亚尔马·沙赫特，他曾参与 1938 年 9 月军方高层推翻希特勒的秘密计划。他后来声称，同盟国在慕尼黑表现出的懦弱令密谋者们心灰意冷。1938 年底，沙赫特在纳粹德国的地位已岌岌可危。在“水晶之夜”犹太店铺和会堂被焚毁数周后举行的帝国银行圣诞派对上，他公开谴责了这类暴行。1939 年初，执迷不悟的沙赫特仍在不断炮制德意志帝国银行的备忘录，强调必须削减引发通胀的军备开支，仿佛希特勒会在乎新古典经济学那套理论。同年 12 月，他在伦敦提出了一项从德国迁出五万名犹太人的计划——费用由这些犹太人的全部财产以及世界犹太社区支付的赎金承担。1 月的第一周，蒙蒂·诺曼最后一次前往德国，参加其教子——沙赫特的孙子诺曼·亚尔马——的洗礼仪式，孩子取名正是为了向他致敬。 当希特勒在 1 月 20 日将沙赫特从帝国银行解职时，诺曼才后知后觉地意识到纳粹威胁的全面恐怖。

RIGHT before the outbreak of World War II came the first state visit by a British monarch to the United States—a piece of pageantry and propaganda in which the House of Morgan participated. The trip was inspired by Joseph Kennedy, who became ambassador to the Court of Saint James’s in 1938. Like many Roosevelt appointments, this one infuriated 23 Wall. Several of his biases simultaneously aroused, Jack Morgan told Monty Norman, “I share your wonder that an Irish Papist and a Wall Street punter should have been selected for the London Embassy. Of course you must expect him to have to be a New Dealer, because Franklin would not appoint anyone else.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365741) Although Norman patronized Kennedy as a social climber of inferior Irish stock, they met weekly, and Norman shared his pessimistic views about England’s prospects in a war against Germany.
就在第二次世界大战爆发前不久，英国君主首次对美国进行了国事访问——这是一场融盛典与宣传于一体的活动，摩根财团也参与其中。这次访问的促成者是约瑟夫·肯尼迪，他于 1938 年出任美国驻圣詹姆斯宫廷大使。和罗斯福的许多任命一样，这一决定激怒了华尔街 23 号。杰克·摩根同时被触动了多重偏见，他对蒙蒂·诺曼说：“我也纳闷，怎么会选一个爱尔兰天主教徒兼华尔街投机者去当驻伦敦大使。当然，你得料到他会是个新政派，因为富兰克林不会任命其他人。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365741) 尽管诺曼把肯尼迪视为出身低微的爱尔兰裔攀附权贵者，但他们每周都会面，诺曼也向肯尼迪分享了自己对英国与德国开战前景的悲观看法。

What made Kennedy’s appointment doubly galling for Jack was that as ambassador the Irishman was living at Princes Gate, which Jack had given to the State Department as an ambassador’s residence in the 1920s. (Joe Kennedy had his revenge on Morgan snubs: today the blue marker outside the house commemorates John F. Kennedy’s brief residence there and says nothing of the Morgans’ original ownership of the property.) Princes Gate would enjoy only a fleeting existence as an official residence, however. After the war, Barbara Hutton, the Wool-worth heiress, donated her Winfield House in Regent’s Park, and that became the new residence of American ambassadors.
肯尼迪的任命让杰克格外难堪的一点是，这位爱尔兰大使住在 Princes Gate——那可是杰克在 1920 年代捐给国务院作为大使官邸的房产。（乔·肯尼迪算是报了摩根冷落之仇：如今房子外那块蓝色纪念牌只写着约翰·F·肯尼迪曾短居于此，对摩根家族曾拥有的产权只字不提。）不过 Princes Gate 作为官方官邸的时光很短暂。战后，伍尔沃斯连锁店继承人芭芭拉·赫顿捐出了她在 Regent's Park 的 Winfield House，这里便成了美国大使的新官邸。

The 1939 visit came about when Queen Elizabeth one day said to Kennedy, “I only know 3 Americans—you, Fred Astaire, and J. P. Morgan—and I would like to know more.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365905) To remedy this, Kennedy suggested a goodwill trip to the United States. Through their private secretary, the royal couple sounded out Jack Morgan and John Davis, who agreed that a visit would indeed be timely. When the king and queen came to the United States in June 1939, Joe Kennedy was pointedly snubbed and excluded from attending their party.
1939 年的那次访问源于伊丽莎白王后有一天对肯尼迪说：“我只认识三个美国人——你、弗雷德·阿斯泰尔和 J.P.摩根——我想多了解一些。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2365905) 为了弥补这一点，肯尼迪建议他们去美国进行一次友好访问。通过他们的私人秘书，王室夫妇试探了杰克·摩根和约翰·戴维斯的意见，两人都认为此时访问确实恰逢其时。1939 年 6 月，当国王和王后抵达美国时，乔·肯尼迪明显受到了冷落，甚至被排除在欢迎他们的派对之外。

As planned, the American trip elicited a tremendous outpouring of pro-British sympathy. The royal couple enjoyed hot dogs at Hyde Park, and Roosevelt outlined limited naval steps he could take to support Britain in case of war. But it didn’t help the House of Morgan, for it reinforced the old stereotype of the firm’s being in league with the British crown. At a garden party at the British embassy in Washington, the king and queen sat up on the porch in remote splendor with several private citizens—Jack Morgan, John D. Rockefeller, Jr., and Mrs. Cornelius Vanderbilt. Only two New Dealers, James Farley and Cordell Hull, were allowed to join them. Stranded down on the lawn with other commoners, the saturnine Harold Ickes enviously watched Morgan and the other economic royalists up on the porch and felt demeaned. He wasn’t mollified when the king and queen descended to mingle with the “common herd.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366078)
按照计划，美国之行引发了大量亲英情绪的宣泄。国王夫妇在海德公园享用了热狗，罗斯福也概述了他在战争情况下可以采取的有限海军措施以支持英国。但这并未帮到摩根公司，反而强化了人们对其与英国王室勾结的固有印象。在英国驻华盛顿大使馆的一次花园派对上，国王和王后高高在上地坐在门廊上，与几位私人宾客——杰克·摩根、小约翰·D·洛克菲勒以及科尼利厄斯·范德比尔特夫人——一起，显得遥不可及。只有两位新政派人士——詹姆斯·法利和科德尔·赫尔——被允许加入他们。性情阴郁的哈罗德·伊克斯与其他人一起被留在草坪上，他嫉妒地望着门廊上的摩根和其他经济保皇派，感到受了侮辱。即使后来国王和王后走下门廊与“平民百姓”打成一片，他的不满也并未因此平息。

In late August 1939, Jack Morgan and King George VI were shooting together at Balmoral in Scotland, complaining about the bird shortage, when Europe suddenly mobilized for war. Like sovereigns retreating to their respective capitals, the king returned to London and Jack to Wall Street. On September 1, Germany invaded Poland. Soon Neville Chamberlain, his voice shaking, announced that Britain was at war with Germany. The New York Stock Exchange reacted with its best session in two years, and the bond market leaped upward with the heaviest one-day volume in history. Unlike the outbreak of World War I, American investors weren’t fooled as to who would profit from the conflict and foresaw an economic boom. It was the Second World War—not the New Deal—that would wipe away the vestiges of the Depression.
1939 年 8 月下旬，杰克·摩根与英王乔治六世正在苏格兰的巴尔莫勒尔一同狩猎，抱怨着飞禽稀少。就在这时，欧洲突然动员了起来，战争一触即发。如同君主们各自返回首都一般，国王回到了伦敦，杰克也回到了华尔街。9 月 1 日，德国入侵波兰。不久，内维尔·张伯伦用颤抖的声音宣布，英国与德国进入战争状态。纽约证券交易所迎来了两年来表现最佳的一个交易日，债券市场也以史上单日最大成交量飙升。与第一次世界大战爆发时不同，美国投资者这次没有被迷惑，他们看清了谁能从冲突中获利，并预见了经济繁荣的到来。将大萧条痕迹一扫而空的，并非“新政”，而是第二次世界大战。

It dawned on the House of Morgan that the firm might dust off the World War I purchasing-agency concept. Might not the bank again aid the Allies behind a shield of neutrality? After pondering such a move, the bank informed the British, French, and U.S. governments that it wouldn’t try to repeat the experience. After so many years of hearings, the bank felt politically vulnerable and feared a revival of war-profiteering charges.
摩根财团意识到，或许可以重新拾起第一次世界大战时期的采购代理模式。银行能否再次以中立为掩护，向同盟国提供援助？在权衡了这一举动后，摩根财团告知英国、法国和美国政府，不打算重蹈覆辙。经过多年的听证会，摩根财团感到自身在政治上易受攻击，担心被重新扣上发战争财的帽子。

The bank also contended with an anti-Wall Street faction in Washington, which was determined to block any Morgan role. This opposition was apparent when Roosevelt created a short-lived War Resources Board. In an amazing coincidence, he chose as chairman Edward R. Stettinius, Jr., son of the Morgan genius of the Export Department in World War I. A handsome man with prematurely silver hair, Stettinius had risen through the ranks of two Morgan clients, General Motors and U.S. Steel, ending up chairman of the latter. The war board included another Morgan favorite, Walter Gifford of AT&T. Roosevelt wanted to counteract charges of being an enemy of business, but his liberal subordinates smelled extreme danger in this tactical retreat. Hugh Johnson, former head of the National Recovery Administration, told Assistant Secretary of War Louis Johnson that the government did not “intend to let Morgan and DuPont men run the war.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366253) Henry Wallace, then FDR’s secretary of agriculture, also warned against bringing Wall Street bankers to Washington.
银行还要应付华盛顿的反华尔街派系，这些人铁了心要阻止摩根掺和进来。罗斯福成立那个短命的战时资源委员会时，这种抵制就暴露无遗了。巧得离谱的是，他居然选了小爱德华·R·斯特蒂纽斯当主席——他爹正是摩根在一战时期出口部门的那位天才人物。斯特蒂纽斯一表人才，头发早早就白了，从摩根的两个客户通用汽车和美国钢铁一路升上来，最后当上了后者的董事长。这个委员会里还有另一位摩根的红人，AT&T 的沃尔特·吉福德。罗斯福本来想借此反击“他是商业公敌”的指控，可他手下那帮自由派下属却觉得这次战术退让意味着极大的危险。前国家复兴管理局局长休·约翰逊对助理陆军部长路易斯·约翰逊直言，政府“绝不能让摩根和杜邦那帮人来操控战争。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366253) 亨利·华莱士，当时是罗斯福的农业部长。他还警告说，不要让华尔街的银行家们来到华盛顿。

The assiduous Harold Ickes quickly gathered his cabal of Brandeis men, Tom Corcoran and Bob Jackson. He noted, “We wondered how far the President would go or would permit others to go in abdicating in favor of big business, as Wilson did at the time of the First World War.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366438) Ickes thought Wilson’s liberal credentials were tarnished by his wartime closeness to Wall Street and he hoped Roosevelt could avoid such a fate. His efforts to keep the Morgan bank out of war work dovetailed with his friend Cyrus Eaton’s efforts to break up Morgan power in the financial world. In late 1939 and early 1940, the Temporary National Economic Committee investigated an alleged monopoly in the investment banking field, with Morgan Stanley its prime suspect.
勤勉的哈罗德·伊克斯迅速召集了他的布兰代斯派系人马——汤姆·科克伦和鲍勃·杰克逊。他指出：“我们想知道，总统会在多大程度上效仿威尔逊在第一次世界大战时的做法，或者说允许别人效仿，向大企业让权。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366438) 伊克斯认为，威尔逊因战时与华尔街走得太近而玷污了自己的自由主义声誉，他希望罗斯福能避免重蹈覆辙。他努力阻止摩根银行参与战时工作，这与他的朋友赛勒斯·伊顿试图在金融界打破摩根势力的努力不谋而合。1939 年底至 1940 年初，临时国家经济委员会对投资银行业涉嫌垄断的行为展开了调查，摩根士丹利是首要怀疑对象。

These anti-Morgan maneuvers, coming from several directions, prevented the bank from resuming its World War I role, as did earlier U.S. entry into the war. In the Second World War, Washington would take charge of industrial mobilization through the War Production Board and other agencies. The federal government was vastly more powerful now than it had been in Woodrow Wilson’s day, and it didn’t hesitate to intervene in the economy for political ends. In fact, government resources now eclipsed those of private banking houses. By World War II, banks were no longer large enough to bankroll wars, as Barings, Rothschilds, and Morgans had done in their heydays. With their large budgets, central banks, and taxing powers, the modern nation-states no longer needed to rely on the good offices of private bankers.
这些针对摩根家族的多方围攻，使得该银行无法像一战时期那样扮演关键角色，而美国较晚参战也产生了类似影响。到了二战期间，华盛顿通过战时生产委员会等机构直接主导工业动员。联邦政府如今的权力远超伍德罗·威尔逊时代，为达成政治目的干预经济已毫无顾忌。事实上，政府资源已全面超越私人银行业的实力。到二战时期，银行已无力像巴林、罗斯柴尔德和摩根家族鼎盛时期那样为战争融资。凭借庞大的财政预算、央行体系和征税权，现代民族国家已不再需要依赖私人银行家的斡旋。

The House of Morgan championed economic support for Great Britain. As a belligerent, Britain was covered by the arms embargo of the Neutrality Act (passed, *inter alia*, to prevent a recurrence of the House of Morgan’s role in World War I). Lamont lobbied Roosevelt to repeal it, contending that it not only favored but emboldened Germany. In November 1939, Congress did repeal the embargo, permitting arms exports to countries at war on a “cash and carry basis”: that is, they could purchase U.S. supplies so long as they paid for and transported them. Under this arrangement, American planes would fly to the U.S.-Canadian border, and Canadian pilots would then fly them to Britain.
摩根家族主张对英国提供经济支援。作为交战国，英国受到《中立法案》武器禁运的约束（该法案的通过，部分原因正是为了防止摩根家族在第一次世界大战中的角色重演）。拉蒙特游说罗斯福总统废除该法案，认为它不仅偏向德国，更助长了其气焰。1939 年 11 月，国会最终废除了禁运令，允许以“现购自运”原则向交战国出口武器：即只要这些国家支付货款并自行运输，就可以购买美国的物资。根据这一安排，美国飞机飞至美加边境，由加拿大飞行员接手再飞往英国。

The cash-and-carry decision created an urgent need for gold or dollars for the massive purchases. As in World War I, Britain raised money by commandeering American securities held by its nationals. The House of Morgan was charged with selling these securities in New York without triggering sharp price declines. It handled the British operation alone but shared a comparable French operation with Lazard Frères. Only a few people in each brokerage house knew the seller’s identity, and Morgans warned brokers that if any information leaked out, their services would be terminated within twenty-four hours. To oversee the operation, the British Treasury sent T. J. Carlyle Gifford to 23 Wall. He was already known to Morgans as chairman of the Scottish Investment Trust of Edinburgh, which used J. P. Morgan as custodian for U.S. securities. Impressed by the House of Morgan’s performance, Gifford nonetheless agreed with Roosevelt’s assessment that the bank’s participation was a severe political liability: “It seems the President and \[Treasury Secretary\] Morgenthau would much have preferred us not to go to J.P. Morgan & Co. and so have probably resented that and are now afraid it may cause difficulties when they appear \[before\] Congress,” he reported back to London.[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366613)
"现购自运"的决定使得大规模采购急需黄金或美元。如同第一次世界大战时一样，英国通过征用本国国民持有的美国证券来筹集资金。摩根财团受命在纽约出售这些证券，同时避免引发价格剧烈下跌。它独自处理了英国的这笔交易，但与拉扎德·弗雷尔公司共同承担了法国类似的操作。每家经纪行中只有少数人知道卖家的身份，摩根公司警告经纪人，若有任何信息泄露，他们的服务将在二十四小时内被终止。为了监督这一操作，英国财政部派遣 T·J·卡莱尔·吉福德前往华尔街 23 号。摩根公司此前已认识他，因为他担任爱丁堡苏格兰投资信托公司的主席，而该公司曾委托 J·P·摩根保管美国证券。 尽管吉福德对摩根财团的表现印象深刻，但他仍认同罗斯福的判断，认为该银行参与其中会带来严重的政治包袱：“总统和\[财政部长\]摩根索似乎更希望我们不要去找 J.P. Morgan & Co.，因而可能对此心怀芥蒂，现在又担心他们出席国会时会因此惹来麻烦。”他向伦敦如此汇报。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366613)

Both for Nancy Astor’s and Britain’s sakes, Lamont assisted Lady Astor’s great spiritual companion, Lord Lothian, who was sent to Washington as British ambassador in April 1939. A former secretary of the Rhodes Trust and a founder of the Royal Institute of International Affairs, Lothian was shy, professorial, and, like Lady Astor, a devout Christian Scientist. Immediately after his appointment, he cabled Lamont, “Shall want all your advice and help.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366780) In Washington, Lord Lothian found the mood very much opposed to Hitler but, at the same time, resolutely opposed to war. Drawing on his Wall Street ally, he would fly up to New York on a late-afternoon flight, speak to a dinner assembled by Lamont, then take a night train back to Washington. Lothian, repenting of his Cliveden appeasement period, would prove a superbly eloquent spokesman in enlisting support for Britain.
为了南希·阿斯特和英国双方的利益，拉蒙特协助了阿斯特夫人的精神挚友——洛西恩勋爵，他于 1939 年 4 月被派往华盛顿出任英国大使。洛西恩勋爵曾是罗德信托基金的秘书，也是皇家国际事务研究所的创始人之一。他性格腼腆、学究气十足，并且像阿斯特夫人一样，是一名虔诚的基督教科学派信徒。上任后不久，他便致电拉蒙特：“我亟需你的所有建议与帮助。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366780) 在华盛顿，洛西恩勋爵发现民众情绪虽然强烈反对希特勒，但同时也坚决反对战争。他借助华尔街盟友的关系，常常乘坐傍晚的航班飞往纽约，在拉蒙特筹办的晚宴上发表演讲，之后再搭乘夜班火车返回华盛顿。洛西恩对自己在克莱夫登庄园时期的绥靖立场深感懊悔，后来证明他在争取对英国的支持方面成为了一位极具说服力的发言人。

In 1939, the most vociferous opposition to U.S. entry into the war came from the German and Italian immigrants, midwestern farmers, and labor unions. The isolationist agenda didn’t change from World War I: there was the same disgust with European broils and the same suspicion that Britain would dupe the United States into saving its own empire. Complicating matters was the still fresh memory of the Great War.
1939 年，最强烈反对美国参战的声音来自德国和意大利移民、中西部农民以及工会。当时的孤立主义议程与一战时期如出一辙：对欧洲纷争同样深恶痛绝，同样怀疑英国会哄骗美国去拯救它自己的帝国。而第一次世界大战的惨痛记忆犹新，更让事态变得复杂。

The Morgan partners flatly opposed U.S. entry into the war and were dubious about the Allies’ chances of beating Germany. As Russell Leffingwell said right before the war’s outbreak, the British and French “cannot subjugate the Germans. There are too many of the devils, and they are too competent.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366977) In May 1940, Lamont joined William Allen White in forming the Committee to Defend America by Aiding the Allies, whose outlook perfectly reflected the Morgan position. The group assailed the same nemeses—the Hearst newspapers and Senators Wheeler and Nye—that had hounded the House of Morgan for years. While fervently attacking the isolationists, it parted company from a kindred group, Fight for Freedom, which endorsed U.S. entry into the war; instead, it heeded FDR’s call for all aid short of war. (At this time, Jack’s sister Anne formed the American Friends of France and by June 1940 was sailing to that country to evacuate refugees and head an ambulance unit. She would have a special plaque dedicated to her memory in Les Invalides, the Paris war memorial, after she died in 1952.)
摩根合伙人坚决反对美国参战，并对盟军击败德国的可能性持怀疑态度。正如拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔在战争爆发前夕所言，英国和法国“无法征服德国人。那些家伙太多了，而且太厉害了。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2366977) 1940 年 5 月，拉蒙特与威廉·艾伦·怀特共同成立了“援助盟国保卫美国委员会”，该委员会的立场完美反映了摩根财团的立场。这个团体攻击了同样的对手——赫斯特报业集团以及参议员惠勒和奈伊——这些对手多年来一直困扰着摩根财团。在激烈抨击孤立主义者的同时，该团体与另一个志同道合的“为自由而战”组织分道扬镳，后者支持美国参战；相反，它听从了富兰克林·罗斯福的号召，提供除战争外的一切援助。（此时，杰克的妹妹安妮成立了“美国法国之友”组织，到 1940 年 6 月，她已乘船前往该国疏散难民并领导一支救护车队。） 她于 1952 年去世后，将在巴黎荣军院（法国战争纪念馆）获得一块纪念她的专属牌匾。)

One of Lamont’s special committee assignments was to neutralize Herbert Hoover’s Anglophobia. Still bruised from his presidency and wishing to repeat his wartime triumph with European food relief, Hoover favored a scheme for feeding Nazi-occupied countries; Lamont supported Britain’s blockade against such activity. When Lamont and White visited Hoover, they couldn’t change his mind and vowed to fight him. Afterward, a press account popped up describing Hoover truculently pacing the room and swearing that he would tour the country and fight Britain on the issue. Lamont assured Hoover that *he* hadn’t talked to a newsman and said the account must have been a misrepresentation. Even at the end, the Lamont-Hoover relationship—once seen as a Faustian pact between Wall Street and Washington—was tense and querulous.
拉蒙特在一个特别委员会中的任务之一，就是化解赫伯特·胡佛的反英情绪。胡佛因总统任期的挫败而心有余悸，又想重现一战时期通过欧洲粮食援助取得的胜利，因此他主张一项向纳粹占领区提供食物的计划；而拉蒙特则支持英国对此类活动的封锁。当拉蒙特和怀特拜访胡佛时，未能改变他的想法，于是他们发誓要与他斗争到底。随后，一篇新闻报道突然出现，描述胡佛暴躁地在房间里踱步，扬言要巡游全国，在这个问题上与英国对抗。拉蒙特向胡佛保证自己并未与记者交谈过，并称那篇报道一定是失实之词。即便到了最后，拉蒙特与胡佛之间的关系——曾被视为华尔街与华盛顿之间的浮士德式交易——依然紧张且充满争执。

The House of Morgan’s pro-British views brought it into conflict with the nation’s most visible isolationist, Charles Lindbergh. In late 1935, the Lindberghs had moved to England, hoping to find a tranquillity denied them in America after their son’s kidnapping. At the prompting of the U.S. military, Lindbergh visited Nazi Germany in 1936 to tour German aircraft factories. He made subsequent trips in 1937 and 1938 and developed a growing admiration for German air power—admiration that he expressed to Stanley Baldwin at Downing Street and in the drawing rooms of Cliveden. Lindbergh insisted that a war against Germany was unwinnable, would destroy American democracy, and would open the way for Communist infiltration. When he accepted a decoration from Hermann Göring at a reception, it added to suspicion in some quarters that Lindbergh was not only awed by the Nazis but sympathetic to them.
摩根财团亲英的立场使其与全国最知名的孤立主义者查尔斯·林德伯格产生了冲突。1935 年底，林德伯格一家移居英国，希望能在儿子被绑架后，找到在美国无法拥有的安宁。在美国军方的推动下，林德伯格于 1936 年访问纳粹德国，参观了多家德国飞机制造厂。随后，他在 1937 年和 1938 年再次访德，并对德国的空军力量日益钦佩——他在唐宁街向斯坦利·鲍德温表达过这种钦佩，也在克利夫登的客厅里提及过。林德伯格坚称，与德国开战不仅不可能获胜，还会摧毁美国的民主，并为共产主义渗透打开大门。当他在一次招待会上接受了赫尔曼·戈林颁发的勋章时，这更让一些人心生疑虑：林德伯格不仅对纳粹充满敬畏，甚至对他们抱有同情。

By the time the Lindberghs returned to America in April 1939, Charles had a settled belief in German invincibility and French and British decadence. That fall, he began making radio speeches urging U.S. neutrality and arguing against repeal of the arms embargo. His remarks were sometimes laced with racist innuendos. On October 13, 1939, he said, “If the white race is ever seriously threatened, it may then be time for us to take our part for its protection, to fight side by side with the English, French, and Germans. But not with one against the other for our mutual destruction.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367189) Whether consciously or not, Lindbergh had absorbed many Nazi doctrines. In the *Atlantic Monthly* of March 1940, he cynically saw England and France as fighting for their possessions and ethics, whereas the Germans claimed “the right of an able and virile nation to expand—to conquer territory and influence by force of arms as other nations have done at one time or another throughout history.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367339)
1939 年 4 月林德伯格夫妇返回美国时，查尔斯已深信德国不可战胜，而法国和英国则腐朽没落。那年秋天，他开始在广播演讲中呼吁美国保持中立，反对废除武器禁运。他的言论时常掺杂着种族主义暗示。1939 年 10 月 13 日，他说道：“如果白种人真的面临严重威胁，那时我们才该挺身而出保卫它，与英国人、法国人和德国人并肩作战。而不是让他们互相残杀，最终两败俱伤。” [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367189) 无论有意无意，林德伯格已吸纳了许多纳粹信条。在 1940 年 3 月的《大西洋月刊》上，他冷嘲热讽地认为英法是在为自身利益和道义而战，而德国则主张“一个强大而有活力的民族有权扩张——像历史上其他民族在不同时期所做的那样，用武力征服领土、扩大影响。” [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367339)

During the debate over the war, Lindbergh increasingly reverted to his father’s midwestern populism, with its reflexive hatred of the Money Trust and its dark vision of Anglo-American finance. While the younger Lindbergh might have taken comfort from following in his father’s footsteps, the situation was more complicated for his wife. Anne Morrow Lindbergh was torn between her isolationist husband and the memory of her late internationalist father. She had always admired the Morgan partners, a type she saw as “keen man-of-the-world, discreet, kindly and cultured.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367474) She once talked of the “whole warm rich world of my father and mother” and remembered idealistic talk over breakfast between her father and Jean Monnet, a French economist and diplomat. She cherished memories of her father, and after reading Harold Nicol-son’s biography of him, she wrote, “I suddenly feel my heritage, feel him in me. It is mine.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367649)
在关于战争的辩论中，林德伯格愈发回归其父的中西部民粹主义立场，那种对金钱信托本能的憎恨，以及对英美金融资本的阴暗看法。虽然小林德伯格或许因追随父亲的足迹而感到安慰，但对他妻子来说，情况要复杂得多。安妮·莫罗·林德伯格在坚持孤立主义的丈夫与已故国际主义父亲的记忆之间挣扎。她一直钦佩摩根合伙人这类人，认为他们是“敏锐的世事通、谨慎、友善且有教养”。 [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367474) 她曾谈起“我父母那个温暖丰富的完整世界”，并回忆起父亲与法国经济学家、外交家让·莫内之间在早餐桌上充满理想主义色彩的谈话。她珍藏着对父亲的记忆，读完哈罗德·尼科尔森为父亲写的传记后，她写道：“我突然感受到我的传承，感觉到他在我体内。这就是我的。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367649)

Now Anne was in an excruciating situation. Her mother felt strongly that Dwight would have favored aid for the Allies. Most of the Lindberghs’ friends on Long Island held similar views. The Lindberghs also had many French and British friends, and as soon as Germany began mobilizing for war in 1938, Anne imagined Florrie Grenfell, Lady Astor, and the rest wiped out by air raids.[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367826) Yet Anne shared Charles’s simplistic views of European politics, albeit without the nasty overlay of racism. In 1940, she published a book called *The Wave of the Future* in which she saw the war not as a contest between good and evil but as one between the “Forces of the Past” (the Allies) and the “Forces of the Future” (Germany).
如今安妮陷入了极其痛苦的境地。她母亲坚信德怀特会支持援助盟国。林德伯格家在长岛的大多数朋友也持同样观点。林德伯格夫妇还有许多法英友人，1938 年德国开始备战之际，安妮就曾想象弗洛丽·格伦费尔、阿斯特夫人等人会在空袭中丧生。 [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367826) 然而安妮对欧洲政治的看法与查尔斯一样简单化，只是少了那些令人作呕的种族主义色彩。1940 年她出版了《未来浪潮》一书，书中认为这场战争并非善恶之争，而是"旧势力"（盟国）与"未来力量"（德国）之间的较量。

If Charles felt buoyed by his identification with his father, Anne was tormented by the ghost of hers. She told herself that Charles was as idealistic as her father but that it was the idealism of a later age. After William Allen White formed the Committee for Defending America by Aiding the Allies, Anne asked herself, “I wonder where Daddy would stand? Probably behind the committee *et al.* And yet he was among those idealists, very practical, intensely practical—that was his great gift.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367997) Nevertheless, as the Lindberghs were socially ostracized by old friends—including Harry Guggenheim, who had sponsored Charles’s three-month tour following the solo flight—Anne was haunted by her father’s specter. She lamented that “Charles . . . has the memory of his father with him,” but “I’m entirely alone.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368178)
如果说查尔斯因与父亲的认同感而感到振奋，安妮则被父亲的幽灵所折磨。她告诉自己，查尔斯和她父亲一样理想主义，但那是一种属于后世的理想主义。当威廉·艾伦·怀特成立“援助盟国保卫美国委员会”后，安妮自问：“我不知道爸爸会站在哪一边？大概会支持委员会及其同僚吧。可他也是那些理想主义者中的一员，非常务实，极其务实——那是他的天赋。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2367997) 然而，当林德伯格夫妇被包括哈里·古根海姆在内的老友们社交排斥时——哈里曾资助查尔斯在单飞后的三个月巡回之旅——安妮始终被父亲的阴影所困扰。她哀叹道：“查尔斯……有他父亲的回忆相伴，”而“我完全孤身一人。” [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368178)

Anne’s dilemma was sharpened on May 19, 1940, when Charles made a radio speech entitled “The Air Defense of America.” By then, the Nazis had conquered Denmark and were overrunning Holland and Belgium. Lindbergh’s speech made menacing reference to “powerful elements in America” who controlled the “machinery of influence and propaganda.” These elements, he said, wanted to push America into war for profit and to serve their foreign allies.[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368328) The Morgan bank wasn’t named, but Lindbergh’s language echoed that used in attacks on the bank ever since the Nye hearings. President Roosevelt told Treasury Secretary Henry Morgenthau the next day, “I am absolutely convinced that Lindbergh is a Nazi.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368550)
1940 年 5 月 19 日，查尔斯发表了一篇题为《美国的空中防御》的广播讲话，安妮的困境由此愈发尖锐。彼时，纳粹已征服丹麦，并席卷荷兰与比利时。林德伯格在讲话中语带威胁地提及“美国国内某些强势势力”掌控着“影响和宣传的机器”。他说，这些人为了牟利和效忠外国盟友，想将美国拖入战争。 [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368328) 虽然未指名道姓摩根银行，但林德伯格的措辞与自奈伊听证会以来对该银行的攻击如出一辙。次日，罗斯福总统对财政部长亨利·摩根索表示：“我完全确信林德伯格就是个纳粹分子。” [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368550)

Betty Morrow, now acting president of Smith College (having attained the academic distinction denied Dwight), was disturbed by Charles’s insinuations. Five days after the radio speech, she had an emotional lunch with Anne at the Cosmopolitan Club. Betty felt ashamed that America hadn’t rushed to join England and said in anguish to Anne, “How they will *hate* us—oh, how they will *hate* us.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368726) Yet despite her candor with her daughter, Morrow felt constrained in challenging her son-in-law. The day after the lunch, she secretly wrote to Lamont asking him to reason with Lindbergh: “I am in a difficult position just now . . . but my chief worry is over Anne. She is torn in spirit and it is telling on her health.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368907)
贝蒂·莫罗如今已是史密斯学院的代理校长（她获得了德怀特未能企及的学术殊荣），查尔斯的含沙射影让她忧心忡忡。广播演讲五天后，她在 cosmopolitan 俱乐部与安妮共进午餐，情绪激动。贝蒂为美国没有立即与英国并肩作战感到羞愧，痛苦地对安妮说："他们会多么恨我们——哦，他们会多么恨我们。" [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368726) 尽管对女儿坦诚相待，莫罗却觉得在挑战女婿时有所顾虑。午餐后的第二天，她秘密写信给拉蒙特，请他规劝林德伯格："我现在处境艰难……但我最担心的是安妮。她精神备受折磨，这已经影响了她的健康。" [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2368907)

Taking the tone of a concerned uncle, Lamont wrote to the rather aloof Charles Lindbergh. He said he had hesitated to contact him and cited his affection for the Morrow family. Then he asked point-blank who those nameless conspirators in his speech were. He added that he didn’t know of any such elements. Trying to recapture the personal warmth of earlier years, Lamont admonished him tactfully: “Dear Charles—It is so important that we shall have unity in our country that we must not broadcast suspicions and accusations unless we have complete basis for the charges.”[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369092)
拉蒙特以一位关心晚辈的叔叔的口吻，写信给那位相当冷淡的查尔斯·林德伯格。他说自己曾犹豫是否要联系他，并提到自己对莫罗家族的深厚感情。接着，他直截了当地询问，林德伯格在演讲中提到的那些无名阴谋家究竟是谁。他补充说，自己并未听说过任何这类人。为了重拾早年间的私人温情，拉蒙特委婉地劝诫道：“亲爱的查尔斯——我们国家的团结至关重要，因此除非有确凿证据，否则我们绝不能随意散布猜疑和指控。”

The return letter must have chilled Lamont. It wasn’t hostile so much as cool and correct, as if Lindbergh had turned into a stranger. “I intentionally did not specify individuals, groups, or organizations in my address because I still hope that it will not be necessary to do this,” Lindbergh said, claiming that to do so would only stir up dangerous class antagonisms. He warned that U.S. entry into the war would cause “chaotic conditions” and destroy American moderation. He concluded, “I have great respect for your judgment, but I am afraid that our viewpoints differ in regard to the attitude this country should take toward the war in Europe.”[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369265) Later, when a reporter asked Lamont why he didn’t visit the Lindberghs, he snapped, “I have nothing to do with them.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369440)
回信肯定让拉蒙特心里凉了半截。那封信谈不上敌意，却冷淡而拘谨，仿佛林德伯格一下子变成了陌生人。“我在演讲中有意没有指明具体的个人、团体或组织，因为我仍希望不必走到那一步，”林德伯格写道，声称点名只会激起危险阶级对立。他警告说，美国参战将导致“混乱局面”，破坏美国人的温和理性。他最后写道：“我十分尊重你的判断，但恐怕我们对于美国在这场欧洲战争中应持何种立场，观点大相径庭。” [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369265) 后来，当有记者问拉蒙特为何不去拜访林德伯格夫妇时，他厉声答道：“我跟他们毫无瓜葛。” [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369440)

Her secret overture through Lamont having failed, Betty Morrow decided to make public her opposition to her son-in-law. During the Dunkirk evacuation in June, she made a speech for the William Allen White committee rebutting Charles’s views. She telephoned Anne first to soften the blow. “Your father would have wanted me to do it,” she told Anne, who thought her mother was being exploited for the purposes of publicity.[53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369599) Betty came to agree with this appraisal after White made a speech boasting of his “smart trick” in setting her against Charles. After that, Betty Morrow was “very humanly, unwilling to appear again in a public clash with Lindbergh,” as Russell Leffingwell informed Roosevelt.[54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369770)
贝蒂·莫罗通过拉蒙特进行的秘密试探失败后，决定公开表达对女婿的反对。在六月的敦刻尔克大撤退期间，她为威廉·艾伦·怀特委员会发表演讲，驳斥查尔斯的观点。她先打电话给安妮缓和打击。"你父亲会希望我这么做，"她对安妮说，而安妮认为母亲被利用来博取公众关注。 [53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369599) 在怀特发表演讲吹嘘自己"耍了个聪明把戏"让她与查尔斯对立后，贝蒂·莫罗也认同了这一判断。此后，正如拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔向罗斯福汇报的那样，贝蒂·莫罗"非常人性化地，不愿再公开与林德伯格发生冲突"。 [54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369770)

THERE was a moment in the spring of 1940 when it seemed the House of Morgan might simultaneously defeat the New Deal and advance the British cause. The long-awaited middle path opened up in the Republican party. Along with the British ambassador, Lord Lothian, Lamont attended a dinner at the home of publisher Ogden M. Reid, who presented two would-be Republican presidential candidates. Robert A. Taft, son of the former president and a Republican senator from Ohio, was predictably anti-internationalist. But the other aspirant, Wendell Willkie, was a revelation. As president of the giant utility holding company Commonwealth and Southern Corporation, he had clashed with FDR over the Tennessee Valley Authority’s takeover of his generating plants. At the dinner, Willkie came out in favor of unqualified support for Britain, including the provisioning of planes and naval equipment. On the spot, Lamont and Reid championed his candidacy, and Lamont was instrumental in getting him to run. Willkie then repeated his pro-British pep talk before a dinner at Lamont’s house, a gathering credited with enlisting Wall Street support for his run for the presidency.
1940 年春，曾有那么一刻，摩根家族（House of Morgan）似乎既有可能挫败新政（New Deal），也能推进英国的战事。共和党内终于出现了一条期待已久的中庸之道。在英国大使洛西恩勋爵（Lord Lothian）陪同下，拉蒙特（Lamont）出席了出版人奥格登·M·里德（Ogden M. Reid）家中的晚宴。席间，里德引见了两位有意竞选共和党总统的候选人。前总统之子、俄亥俄州共和党参议员罗伯特·A·塔夫脱（Robert A. Taft）毫不意外地持反国际主义立场。但另一位角逐者温德尔·威尔基（Wendell Willkie）却令人眼前一亮。作为大型公用事业控股公司联邦南方公司（Commonwealth and Southern Corporation）的总裁，他曾因田纳西河流域管理局（Tennessee Valley Authority）接管其发电厂而与罗斯福（FDR）发生冲突。晚宴上，威尔基公开支持无条件援助英国，包括提供飞机和海军装备。拉蒙特和里德当场力挺他参选，而拉蒙特在推动他投身竞选一事上起到了关键作用。 Willkie 接着在 Lamont 家的一次晚宴前重复了他那番亲英的激励演说，这场聚会被认为成功争取到了华尔街对他竞选总统的支持。

For Morgan partners, Willkie was tailor-made. Ever since McKinley, the bank had been baffled by a Hobson’s choice in American politics. It could side either with Democrats, who were too interventionist at home, or with Republicans, who were too isolationist abroad. As a major foreign lender, it favored free trade and free flow of capital at a time when big business was still mostly protectionist. On balance, the bank had opted for Republicans, but not without considerable discomfort on foreign issues.
对于摩根合伙人来说，Willkie 简直是量身定做。自 McKinley 时代以来，该银行在美国政治中一直面临 Hobson's choice。它要么支持在国内过于干预主义的 Democrats，要么支持在国外过于孤立主义的 Republicans。作为一家大型外国贷款机构，在大企业仍然大多保护主义的时候，它青睐自由贸易和资本自由流动。总的来说，该银行选择了 Republicans，但在外交问题上并非没有相当大的不安。

Willkie was decidedly in the Morgan grain. A former Democrat, he was an outward-looking Anglophile, a supporter of reciprocal trade agreements, and generally attuned to FDR’s foreign policy. At the same time, he was a supporter of domestic free markets and wished for a midcourse correction in New Deal policies and a more favorable environment for investment. He had plenty of Wall Street friends, including Perry Hall of Morgan Stanley (where Willkie was one of Harold Stanley’s first clients in 1935), and provided a version of Republicanism they could unreservedly support.
威尔基无疑是摩根范儿。他曾是民主党人，却是一位放眼世界的亲英派，支持互惠贸易协定，大体上认同罗斯福的外交政策。与此同时，他推崇国内自由市场，希望对新政政策进行中期调整，并为投资创造更有利的环境。他在华尔街朋友众多，包括摩根士丹利的佩里·霍尔（1935 年威尔基便是哈罗德·斯坦利最早的一批客户之一），他所展现的共和党理念，让这些人毫无保留地鼎力支持。

With his broad, open face, big grin, and Indiana twang, Willkie was folksy and sophisticated and uniquely able to advance Wall Street’s cause without seeming like an ambassador for the rich. *Fortune* called him a “clever bumpkin,” and Harold Ickes memorably mocked him as “a simple barefoot Wall Street lawyer.”[55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369986) The verdict was too harsh, for Willkie wanted to retain many New Deal innovations—collective bargaining, minimum wages, and maximum hours—that were anathema to Wall Street bankers. Though Willkie declared his candidacy only seven weeks before the Republican National Convention in June 1940, his dark-horse candidacy galloped fast. He had to soft-pedal his Morgan support so as not to alarm the small-town, anti-Wall Street wing of the party. To foster a down-home image, he took a modest two-room suite at the Benjamin Franklin Hotel in Philadelphia, site of the convention, and Tom Lamont was expressly instructed to avoid his headquarters.
威尔基面相宽厚，笑容爽朗，带着印第安纳州的口音，既接地气又老练圆滑，能够在不显得像富人代言人的情况下推进华尔街的事业。《财富》杂志称他为"聪明的土老帽"，哈罗德·伊克斯则刻薄地嘲笑他是"一个光着脚的华尔街乡巴佬律师"。 [55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2369986) 这个评价过于苛刻了，因为威尔基希望保留许多罗斯福新政的创新成果——集体谈判、最低工资和最高工时——而这些正是华尔街银行家们深恶痛绝的。尽管威尔基在 1940 年 6 月共和党全国大会前仅七周才宣布参选，但他的黑马竞选势头却异常迅猛。他不得不淡化与摩根集团的关系，以免惊扰党内那些反华尔街的小城镇派系。为了塑造亲民形象，他在大会举办地费城的本杰明·富兰克林酒店订了一套简朴的双室套房，并明确指示汤姆·拉蒙特不要出现在他的竞选总部附近。

Despite this sanitary distance, the anti-Willkie troops wasted no time in fastening onto his Wall Street links in order to discredit him. Representative Usher L. Burdick of North Dakota circulated an alarmist tract to delegates that said, “I believe I am serving the best interests of the Republican Party by protesting in advance and exposing the machinations and attempts of J.P. Morgan and the other New York City bankers in forcing Wendell Willkie on the Republican Party. Money, I know, talks.”[56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370125)
尽管保持着这种社交距离，反威尔基势力仍迅速抓住他与华尔街的关系不放，试图诋毁他。北达科他州众议员厄舍·L·伯迪克向代表们散发了一份危言耸听的传单，其中写道：“我相信，我提前抗议并揭露 J.P.摩根及纽约其他银行家试图将温德尔·威尔基强加给共和党的阴谋和企图，是在为共和党的最大利益服务。我知道，金钱说了算。” [56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370125)

The Republicans were famished for new leaders and finally chose Willkie on the sixth ballot over prosecutor Thomas Dewey (who had indicted Richard Whitney) and Senator Taft. The following month, FDR was nominated for a third term in Chicago, with Henry A. Wallace of Iowa as his running mate. Willkie tried gamely to forge a compromise between support for FDR’s foreign policy during the war emergency and moderate reform of New Deal policy. He even sounded out Roosevelt about a deal in which the president would consult him on foreign policy in exchange for a pledge to keep the war out of the campaign. Roosevelt didn’t trust the Republicans enough to accede to this and was loath to confer the prestige of such a deal upon Willkie.
共和党渴求新领袖，最终在第六轮投票中选择了 Willkie，击败了检察官 Thomas Dewey（他曾起诉过 Richard Whitney）和参议员 Taft。次月，FDR 在芝加哥被提名为第三任期候选人，来自艾奥瓦州的 Henry A. Wallace 作为他的竞选搭档。Willkie 勇敢地试图在战争紧急状态下支持 FDR 外交政策与适度改革新政政策之间达成妥协。他甚至试探 Roosevelt，希望总统在外交政策上征求他的意见，以换取将战争议题排除在竞选之外的承诺。Roosevelt 对共和党信任不足，不愿答应此事，也不愿意让 Willkie 通过这样的交易获得声望。

In November, Roosevelt won by over five million votes. Willkie’s defeat didn’t end charges of Wall Street machinations but only strengthened the conviction of those who saw wily bankers as having foisted him upon the party. As historian Harry Elmer Barnes afterward charged: “It is doubtful if any man was ever nominated for the Presidency on the basis of less popular knowledge and approval. There were at least a dozen or more persons in the famous ’smoke-filled room’ in the Chicago hotel where Warren G. Harding was chosen for the nomination in 1920. Two men decided that Mr. Willkie should be the Republican nominee. . . . These men were Ogden Mills Reid . . . and Thomas W. Lamont.”[57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2370287)
十一月，罗斯福以超五百万票的优势胜出。威尔基的落败并未终结外界对华尔街操纵选举的指控，反而加深了那些认为狡猾的银行家们不过是将其强加于党内的人士的信念。正如历史学家哈里·埃尔默·巴恩斯事后所言：“很难说还有哪位总统候选人是像他这样，在如此缺乏公众了解和认可的情况下获得提名的。1920 年沃伦·G·哈定在芝加哥酒店那间著名的‘烟雾弥漫的房间’里获得提名时，房间里至少有十多名决策者。而决定让威尔基先生成为共和党提名人的，只有两个人……这两位便是奥格登·米尔斯·里德……和托马斯·W·拉蒙特。”

As it turned out, the House of Morgan didn’t suffer as much from Willkie’s defeat as might have been expected. Bolstered by his election victory, Roosevelt moved more vigorously to support Britain, and in this effort he needed the Morgan bank. With marvelous suddenness, the chill in Morgan-Roosevelt relations thawed and was replaced by cordiality from the White House of a sort that 23 Wall hadn’t known since the twenties. As America’s attention shifted from blistering debates over domestic policy to ways in which to deal with Europe’s dictators, the power of the House of Morgan surged accordingly.
结果证明，摩根家族并没有因为威尔基的失利而遭受预期中的重创。罗斯福凭借竞选胜利的势头，更加积极地支持英国，而在这项行动中他需要摩根银行的协助。令人惊奇的是，摩根与罗斯福之间冰冷的紧张关系迅速解冻，白宫方面甚至展现出自 20 年代以来华尔街 23 号从未感受过的热忱。随着美国社会的关注焦点从激烈的内政辩论转向如何应对欧洲独裁者，摩根家族的势力也随之重新崛起。
