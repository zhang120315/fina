# Chapter One: Scrooge / 第一章 斯克鲁奇

WHEN Baltimore merchant George Peabody sailed for London in 1835, the world was in the throes of a debt crisis. The defaulting governments weren’t obscure Balkan nations or South American republics but American states. The United States had succumbed to a craze for building railroads, canals, and turnpikes, all backed by state credit. Now Maryland legislators, with the bravado of the ruined, threatened to join other states in skipping interest payments on their bonds, which were largely marketed in London. As one of three state commissioners assigned to renegotiate the debt, Peabody urged officials to tone down their rhetoric and placate British bankers. But American legislators found it easier to pander to the hatred of foreign bankers rather than to raise new taxes to service debt.
1835 年，当巴尔的摩商人乔治·皮博迪乘船前往伦敦时，世界正深陷一场债务危机。当时违约的政府并非什么名不见经传的巴尔干国家或南美共和国，而是美国的各个州。美国曾陷入狂热的铁路、运河和收费公路建设浪潮，这一切都由各州的信用作担保。如今，马里兰州的立法者们带着破产者的虚张声势，扬言要加入其他州的行列，拒绝支付其债券的利息——而这些债券大多在伦敦市场发售。作为负责重新谈判债务的三位州专员之一，皮博迪敦促官员们收敛言辞，安抚英国银行家。然而，美国的立法者们发现，迎合民众对外国银行家的仇恨，远比征收新税来偿还债务要容易得多。

London was the sun in the financial solar system. Only Britain had a huge surplus of funds in a capital-short world, and sterling was the currency of world trade; its official use dated back to William the Conqueror. In the afterglow of the Napoleonic Wars, bankers of the City—London’s financial district—were self-styled potentates, often with access to more money than the governments and companies they financed. Firms such as Barings and Rothschilds maintained an imperial reserve, omitting their names from doorways and letterheads, refusing to solicit business or open branches, and demanding exclusive client relations. Statesmen from Europe and Latin America trooped humbly to their doorsteps. One observer remarked, “to be asked for lunch was like being received in audience by a king.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2184461)
伦敦是金融太阳系的中心。在那个资本匮乏的世界里，只有英国拥有巨额资金盈余，而英镑是世界贸易的货币，其官方使用可追溯至征服者威廉时期。拿破仑战争的余晖中，伦敦金融城的银行家们自封为权贵，往往比他们资助的政府和企业掌握更多资金。像巴林银行和罗斯柴尔德家族这样的机构保持着帝国般的矜持，门面和信纸上都不署公司名，从不主动招揽业务或开设分行，并要求客户保持独家关系。来自欧洲和拉丁美洲的政治家们谦卑地排队登门求见。有观察者评论道："能被邀请共进午餐，简直就像被国王召见一样。"

Though intensely patriotic, the forty-year-old Peabody identified with the British creditors. When the other Maryland commissioners returned home in despair, Peabody threw a glittering dinner for a dozen bankers to persuade them that Americans weren’t all rustic swindlers. He argued that only new loans could guarantee repayment of the old—a convenient line to be echoed by many future debtor states. Far from cutting off Maryland’s credit, the bankers advanced another \$8 million. As his friend the English political leader George Owen said of Peabody, “He borrowed the money on his face.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2184625) To mitigate British prejudice against “venal” Americans, he boldly waived his \$60,000 commission from Maryland.
尽管四十岁的皮博迪有着强烈的爱国情怀，但他内心更倾向于站在英国债权人一边。当其他马里兰专员绝望地打道回府时，皮博迪却为十多位银行家举办了一场奢华晚宴，试图说服他们——美国人并非都是土里土气的骗子。他辩称，只有新的贷款才能确保旧债的偿还——这套说辞后来被许多负债国反复引用，可谓方便至极。银行家们非但没有断绝马里兰的信贷，反而又提供了 800 万美元。正如他的朋友、英国政治领袖乔治·欧文评价皮博迪时所说：“他是靠刷脸借到这笔钱的。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2184625) 为了消除英国人对所谓“贪婪成性”的美国人的偏见，他毅然放弃了马里兰付给他的 6 万美元佣金。

Peabody, a good talker, was not prepossessing. Over six feet tall with light blue eyes and dark brown hair, he had a rumpled face, with knobby chin, bulbous nose, side whiskers, and heavy-lidded eyes. That this homely man would found the House of Morgan—later a white-glove affair with high-society partners famous for good looks and stylish dress—is ironic. He carried the scars of early poverty and was quick to feel slights and perceive enemies. Like many who have overcome early hardship by brute force, he was proud but insecure, always at war with the world and counting his injuries.
皮博迪虽然能说会道，但长相并不讨喜。他身高六英尺有余，浅蓝眼睛和深棕色头发，脸上布满皱纹，下巴突出，鼻子圆鼓鼓的，两鬓蓄着胡须，眼睑厚重。这个相貌平平的男人竟创立了摩根财团——后来这家机构以俊朗时髦的高端社交圈搭档而闻名——实在颇具讽刺意味。他带着早年贫困留下的伤痕，很容易感到被轻视，也善于察觉敌人。像许多靠蛮力克服早期困境的人一样，他既骄傲又缺乏安全感，始终与世界为敌，不断细数着自己的创伤。

Born in Danvers, Massachusetts, he had only a few years of schooling. When he was a teenager, his father died, and Peabody worked in his brother’s shop to support his widowed mother and six siblings. When he later prospered in a Baltimore dry-goods business with a rich older partner, Elisha Riggs, he remained haunted by his past. “I have never forgotten and never can forget the great privations of my early years,” he later said.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2184786) He hoarded his money, worked incessantly, and retained a lonely air.
他出生于马萨诸塞州丹弗斯，只念了几年书。十几岁时父亲去世，皮博迪便在哥哥的店里干活，赚钱养活守寡的母亲和六个兄弟姐妹。后来，他与年长富有的合伙人埃利沙·里格斯在巴尔的摩合伙经营干货生意，生意兴隆，但他始终无法摆脱过往的阴影。“我从未忘记，也永远不会忘记早年的极度贫困，”他后来这样说。他节衣缩食，拼命工作，始终带着孤独的神情。

In 1837, Peabody moved to London. A year later he opened a merchant house at 31 Moorgate in London, furnishing it with a mahogany counter, a safe, and some desks. He joined a select group of merchant bankers who traded in dry goods and also financed such trade; hence, their businesses became known as merchant banks. They developed a form of wholesale banking remote from the prosaic world of bank books, teller windows, and checking accounts. Their specialty was “high finance”—serving only governments, large companies, and rich individuals. They financed overseas trade, issued stocks and bonds, and dealt in commodities. Ordinary people could no more do business with George Peabody than they can today place a deposit with Morgan Guaranty, Morgan Grenfell, or Morgan Stanley.
1837 年，皮博迪移居伦敦。一年后，他在伦敦穆尔盖特街 31 号开设了一家商行，配备了红木柜台、保险柜和几张办公桌。他加入了一个精英商人银行家群体，这些银行家既经营干货贸易，也为这类贸易提供融资，因此他们的业务逐渐被称为商人银行。他们发展出一种批发银行业务，远离了寻常的银行账簿、出纳窗口和支票账户的世界。他们的专长是“高端金融”——只服务政府、大企业和富豪。他们为海外贸易提供融资，发行股票和债券，并从事大宗商品交易。普通人无法与乔治·皮博迪做生意，就像今天人们无法在摩根担保、摩根建富或摩根士丹利存款一样。

In setting up in London, Peabody planted the American flag in alien territory. The United States relied on British capital to finance development and often resented that its economic fate was decided abroad. As one congressman said in 1833, “the barometer of the American money market hangs up at the stock exchange in London.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2184946) Peabody, hoping to tap this transatlantic money flow, became a leading dealer of American state bonds in London, reversing a contemporary trend in which London banks sent representatives to America. The House of Baring—which bankrolled the Louisiana Purchase and always had an American on its board—employed Thomas Ward as its American agent, while the Rothschilds, who were ambivalent about America, posted August Belmont, Sr., to New York.
在伦敦立足时，皮博迪将美国国旗插在了异国他乡的土地上。美国曾依赖英国资本发展经济，并常因自身经济命脉受制于海外而愤懑不平。正如 1833 年一位国会议员所言："美国货币市场的晴雨表，就挂在伦敦证券交易所。"皮博迪试图抓住这股跨大西洋资本流动的机遇，成为伦敦市场上美国州债券的主要交易商——这逆转了当时伦敦银行向美国派驻代表的潮流。巴林银行（曾为路易斯安那购地案提供资金，且董事会始终保留一名美国席位）指派托马斯·沃德担任其美国代理人，而对美国态度矛盾的罗斯柴尔德家族则将老奥古斯特·贝尔蒙派遣至纽约。

Instead of blending into his British milieu, Peabody shrewdly flaunted his Americanism, wrapping himself in the flag and boosting American products. He declared that George Peabody and Company would be “an American house,” and that he wanted to give it “an American atmosphere—to furnish it with American journals—to make it a centre for American news, and an agreeable place for my American friends visiting London.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185110) Yet amid the patriotic pride lurked a colonial mentality, possibly a sense of his own inferiority, a constant need to impress the British. He hoped to refute what had “almost become a byword among the English. hat no American House in London could long sustain their credit.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185268)
皮博迪没有融入他的英国环境，反而精明地彰显自己的美国身份，高举美国旗帜，推广美国产品。他宣称乔治·皮博迪公司将是"一家美国商行"，并希望赋予它"美国氛围——用美国期刊来装点——让它成为美国新闻的中心，也成为我造访伦敦的美国朋友们惬意的去处。" [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185110) 然而，在这份爱国自豪背后，潜藏着一种殖民地心态，或许还有自身的自卑感，一种不断想要给英国人留下深刻印象的需要。他希望驳斥那种"几乎已成为英国人中的口头禅——没有一家在伦敦的美国商行能长期维持信誉。" [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185268)

Beneath a genial air, Peabody was a solitary miser. He lived in furnished rooms in a Regent Street hotel and aside from taking occasional fishing trips, worked nonstop. During one twelve-year period, he never took off two consecutive days and spent an average of ten hours per day at work. Notwithstanding his stirring speeches about America’s destiny, he didn’t return home for twenty years, and during that time his personality darkened along with the dismal performance of American state bonds. During the severe depression of the early 1840s—a decade dubbed the Hungry Forties—state debt plunged to fifty cents on the dollar. The worst came when five American states—Pennsylvania, Mississippi, Indiana, Arkansas, and Michigan—and the Florida territory defaulted on their interest payments. In an early debtors’ cartel, some American governors banded together to favor debt repudiation. To this day, the reprobate Mississippi remains in unashamed default.
在平易近人的外表下，Peabody 其实是个孤僻的守财奴。他住在 Regent Street 一家旅馆的带家具出租房里，除了偶尔外出钓鱼，几乎一刻不停地工作。曾有整整十二年，他从未连续休息过两天，平均每天工作十小时。尽管他关于美国命运的演讲激动人心，但自己却二十年没有回国，而在此期间，随着美国各州债券表现日益惨淡，他的性格也愈发阴郁。1840 年代初的严重萧条——那个被称为 "Hungry Forties" 的十年——各州债券价格跌到面值的 50 美分。最糟的是，有五个美国州——Pennsylvania、Mississippi、Indiana、Arkansas、Michigan 以及 Florida 领地——相继违约，停止支付利息。一些州长早早联手组成债务国联盟，倾向于赖账。直到今天，臭名昭著的 Mississippi 依然恬不知耻地处于违约状态。

British investors cursed America as a land of cheats, rascals, and ingrates. State defaults also tainted federal credit, and when Washington sent Treasury agents to Europe in 1842, James de Rothschild thundered, “Tell them you have seen the man who is at the head of the finances of Europe, and that he has told you that they cannot borrow a dollar. Not a dollar.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185401) Clergyman Sydney Smith sneered at the American “mob” and said that whenever he met a Pennsylvanian at a London dinner, he felt “a disposition to seize and divide him. . . . How such a man can set himself down at an English table without feeling that he owes two or three pounds to every man in the company, I am at a loss to conceive; he has no more right to eat with honest men than a leper has to eat with clean men.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185582) Even Charles Dickens couldn’t resist a jab, portraying a nightmare in which Scrooge’s solid British assets are transformed into “a mere United States’ security.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185741)
英国投资者咒骂美国是一片骗子、无赖和忘恩负义之徒的土地。各州的违约行为也玷污了联邦的信用。1842 年，华盛顿派财政部特使前往欧洲时，詹姆斯·德·罗斯柴尔德厉声道：“告诉他们，你见到了欧洲金融的掌控者，而他亲口对你说，他们一个子儿也借不到。一个子儿也没有。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185401) 牧师悉尼·史密斯嘲讽美国的“暴民”，称每当他在伦敦的宴会上遇到一个宾夕法尼亚人，就“想把他抓住分了……这样的人怎么能心安理得地坐在英国人的餐桌旁，而不觉得自己欠在座每人两三英镑？我实在想不通；他有权与诚实的人同桌吃饭吗？不，这就像麻风病人有权与健康的人同席一样。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185582) 就连查尔斯·狄更斯也忍不住揶揄，描绘了一个噩梦：斯克鲁奇那坚实的英国资产变成了“一张美国证券”。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185741)

When his beloved Maryland defaulted, Peabody’s own nightmare was complete. Whenever he met a British investor, he said, he felt shame. The British were especially incensed over Maryland and Pennsylvania because those states were settled by Anglo-Saxon stock and therefore should have known better. Having marketed about half of Maryland’s securities to individual investors in Europe, Peabody was victimized by his own success. The brouhaha had direct repercussions, and he became *persona non grata* around London. The London *Times* noted that while Peabody was an “American gentleman of the most unblemished character,” the Reform Club had blackballed him for being a citizen of a country that reneged on its debts.[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185903) Gloomily he wrote a friend, “You and I will, I trust, see that happy day, when as formerly, we can own ourselves Americans in Europe, without a blush for the character of our Country.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186063)
当心爱的马里兰州违约时，皮博迪的个人噩梦也随之彻底降临。他坦言，每次遇到英国投资者，内心都充满羞愧。英国人对马里兰州和宾夕法尼亚州尤为愤怒，因为这两个州由盎格鲁-撒克逊移民建立，本应更懂规矩。皮博迪将马里兰州近一半的证券卖给了欧洲散户投资者，结果反被自己的成功所累。这场轩然大波带来直接后果——他在伦敦成了不受欢迎的人。《伦敦泰晤士报》指出，尽管皮博迪是“品格无瑕的美国绅士”，改革俱乐部却因他来自一个赖账的国家而投票将他拒之门外。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2185903) 他郁郁寡欢地写信给朋友：“我相信，你我终将看到那一天——我们又能像从前一样，在欧洲坦然承认自己是美国人，而不再为国家的信誉感到脸红。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186063)

A hallmark of merchant bankers was that they vouched for the securities they sponsored. At first, Peabody merely sent letters to Baltimore friends, scolding them about the need for Maryland to resume interest payments. Then he tired of persuasion and rewarded reporters with small gratuities for favorable articles about the state. At last, in 1845 he conspired with Barings to push Maryland into resuming payment. They set up a political slush fund to spread propaganda for debt resumption and to elect sympathetic legislators; they even drafted the clergy into giving sermons on the sanctity of contracts. By means of a secret account, the two firms transferred £1,000 to Baltimore, 90 percent from Barings and 10 percent from Peabody—a strategy Barings duplicated in Pennsylvania. Most shocking of all, Barings bribed Daniel Webster, the orator and statesman, to make speeches for debt repayment. The bankers conducted this shabby campaign with a skulking sense of guilt; it wasn’t their preferred style. “Your payment to Mr. Webster would not appear very well if it should get out,” Joshua Bates, the senior Baring partner, warned Thomas Ward, American bagman for the operation.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186222) Bates, a sober, diligent Bostonian, cringed at what they were doing: “I have a sort of instinctive horror of doing one thing to effect another, or using any sort of subterfuge or reserve,” he confessed to Ward.[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186387)
商人银行家的一大标志性特征，就是他们会为自己承销的证券做担保。起初，皮博迪只是写信给巴尔的摩的朋友们，训斥他们马里兰有必要恢复支付利息。后来他厌倦了劝说，便用小额酬金奖励那些撰写对该州有利文章的记者。最后在 1845 年，他与巴林银行合谋，推动马里兰州恢复支付。他们设立了一笔政治黑金，用于宣传债务恢复并选举同情他们的立法者；甚至动员神职人员布道，宣讲契约的神圣性。通过一个秘密账户，两家公司向巴尔的摩转移了 1000 英镑，其中巴林银行承担 90%，皮博迪承担 10%——巴林银行在宾夕法尼亚州也复制了这一策略。最令人震惊的是，巴林银行贿赂了演说家兼政治家丹尼尔·韦伯斯特，让他发表支持偿还债务的演讲。 银行家们偷偷摸摸地进行着这场卑劣的勾当，内心愧疚不安——这绝非他们惯常的行事风格。巴林银行的高级合伙人约书亚·贝茨警告美国方面的经办人托马斯·沃德："如果这件事传出去，您给韦伯斯特先生的这笔钱恐怕不太好看。" [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186222) 贝茨是个严谨勤勉的波士顿人，对自己的所作所为感到局促不安，他向沃德坦言："我本能地对这种声东击西的把戏感到恐惧，也厌恶任何形式的欺瞒或保留。" [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186387)

Whatever their scruples, the conspiracy thrived: pro-resumption Whigs were elected in both Maryland and Pennsylvania, and London bankers again received payments from both states.[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186532) Peabody, never one to forget an injury, excluded the most persistent debtors, Florida and Mississippi, from his later philanthropies. Even altruism had its limits.
尽管他们心怀芥蒂，这场阴谋还是得逞了：支持恢复偿付的辉格党人在马里兰州和宾夕法尼亚州双双当选，伦敦的银行家们再次收到了这两州的还款。 [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186532) 一向记仇的皮博迪，后来在慈善事业中，把欠债最久的佛罗里达州和密西西比州排除在外。毕竟，就算博爱也是有底线的。

When the depreciated state bonds Peabody had bought up in the early 1840s paid interest again, he reaped a fortune. Then, as revolution swept across the Continent in 1848, American securities seemed a safe haven in comparison with Europe. And as the California gold rush and Mexican War wiped away the last vestiges of depression by the late 1840s, Peabody took new pride in his native roots. Now he fancied himself the ambassador of American culture in London and dispensed barrels full of American apples, Boston crackers, and hominy grits.
当皮博迪在 19 世纪 40 年代初大量购入的贬值州债券重新支付利息时，他赚得盆满钵满。随后，1848 年革命浪潮席卷欧洲大陆，相比之下，美国证券成了安全的避风港。随着 40 年代末加州淘金热和美墨战争彻底抹去萧条的最后痕迹，皮博迪对自己的故土愈发引以为傲。此时，他自视为美国文化在伦敦的代言人，慷慨地分送着一桶桶美国苹果、波士顿饼干和玉米碴。

On July 4, 1851, he hosted the first of his Independence Day dinners, featuring the elderly duke of Wellington as guest of honor. Beneath a portrait of Queen Victoria and a Gilbert Stuart of George Washington, the British minister in Washington and the American minister in London drained an oak loving cup and toasted the start of the Great Exhibition in London’s new Crystal Palace. Because Congress wouldn’t finance American exhibitors, Peabody played the impresario, paying to display Cyrus McCormick’s reaper and Samuel Colt’s revolvers. But not all of Peabody’s July Fourth pageants of Anglo-American friendship followed the desired script. In 1854, when Peabody toasted Queen Victoria before President Pierce—an act Washington thought arch heresy—James Buchanan, the U.S. ambassador in London and later President, indignantly stormed from the room.
1851 年 7 月 4 日，他举办了首届独立日宴会，特邀年迈的威灵顿公爵作为主宾。在维多利亚女王画像和吉尔伯特·斯图亚特绘制的乔治·华盛顿肖像下，英国驻华盛顿公使与美国驻伦敦公使共饮橡木爱杯，为伦敦新水晶宫举办的大博览会祝酒。由于国会不愿资助美国参展商，皮博迪亲自扮演起赞助人的角色，自掏腰包展示了赛勒斯·麦考密克的收割机和塞缪尔·柯尔特的左轮手枪。然而，并非所有皮博迪策划的 7 月 4 日英美友谊盛会都按预期剧本上演。1854 年，当皮博迪在皮尔斯总统之前为维多利亚女王祝酒时——此举被华盛顿视为大逆不道——美国驻伦敦大使（后成为总统）詹姆斯·布坎南愤然离席。

As banker and cicerone for Americans in London—once, in a single week, he dined eighty visiting Americans and took thirty-five to the opera—Peabody was constantly exposed to the fierce snobbery of British aristocrats toward the American commercial class. This condescension was particularly flagrant during Commodore Vanderbilt’s trip to London in 1853. The Commodore—vulgar, profane, and lecherous—wanted to show London society the full splendor of America’s richest man. With his wife and twelve children, he had sailed to England aboard his ornate, two-thousand-ton *North Star*, equipped with caterer, doctor, and chaplain. Peabody squired the Vanderbilts about Hyde Park and installed them in his box at Covent Garden; meanwhile, the court ostracized the ostentatious Commodore.
作为在伦敦的美国人银行家和导游——曾经一周内，他宴请了八十位来访的美国人，并带其中三十五人去听歌剧——皮博迪时常领教英国贵族对美国商业阶级那种赤裸裸的势利眼。这种轻蔑在 1853 年范德比尔特船长访问伦敦时表现得尤为露骨。那位船长——粗俗、满口脏话、好色成性——想向伦敦上流社会展示美国首富的排场。他带着妻子和十二个孩子，乘坐自己那艘装饰华丽的 2000 吨级"北极星"号蒸汽船抵达英国，船上配备了餐饮服务员、医生和牧师。皮博迪陪着范德比尔特一家在海德公园游览，又安排他们坐进自己在科文特花园歌剧院的包厢；然而，宫廷上下却冷落了这位爱炫耀的船长。

Peabody amassed a \$20-million fortune in the 1850s as he financed everything from the silk trade with China to iron rail exports to America. Although he built a lyceum and library for his native Danvers in the early 1850s, he mostly hoarded his money in preparation for the next panic. His insecurities only worsened as he had more to lose. He told a friend in 1852, “My capital is . . . ample (certainly nearer 400,000 pounds than 300,000) . . . but I have passed too many money panics, unscathed, not to have seen how often large Capitals are swept away, and that even with my own I must use caution.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186741)
皮博迪在 19 世纪 50 年代积累了 2000 万美元的财富，他资助了从对华丝绸贸易到向美国出口铁轨等各类业务。尽管他在 19 世纪 50 年代初为家乡丹弗斯建造了一所讲堂和图书馆，但他大部分时间都在囤积金钱，以应对下一场恐慌。随着他拥有的财富越来越多，他的不安全感只增不减。1852 年，他对一位朋友说：“我的资本……很充足（肯定更接近 40 万英镑而非 30 万英镑）……但我经历过太多次金融危机而毫发无损，因此深知大资本常常被一扫而空，甚至对自己的资本也必须谨慎行事。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186741)

Junius Morgan, who became Peabody’s partner in 1854, later told how he found him one morning at the countinghouse looking sickly and rheumatic. The miserly Peabody didn’t own a carriage but came to work by public horsecar. “Mr. Peabody, with that cold you ought not to stick here,” Morgan said. Taking hat and umbrella, Peabody agreed to go home. Twenty minutes later, on his way to the Royal Exchange, Morgan found Peabody standing in the rain. “Mr. Peabody, I thought you were going home,” the younger man said. “Well, I am, Morgan,” Peabody replied, “but there’s only been a twopenny bus come along as yet and I am waiting for a penny one.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186900) By this time, Peabody’s bank account bulged with over £1 million.
1854 年成为 Peabody 合伙人的 Junius Morgan 后来讲述了他一天早上在账房发现 Peabody 面色苍白、风湿发作的情景。吝啬的 Peabody 没有私人马车，而是乘坐公共马车上班。"Peabody 先生，您感冒了，不该还在这儿熬着。"Morgan 说道。Peabody 拿起帽子和雨伞，同意回家。二十分钟后，Morgan 在前往 Royal Exchange 的路上，发现 Peabody 站在雨中。"Peabody 先生，我以为您回家了呢。"年轻些的 Morgan 说道。"我确实是要回去的，Morgan，"Peabody 回答，"但刚才只有两便士的巴士来了，我在等一便士的。" [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2186900) 此时，Peabody 的银行账户里已经存有超过一百万英镑。

Enjoying the clerk’s revenge, Thomas Perman, Peabody’s assistant, handed down a trove of nasty stories that tarnish the halo Peabody acquired as a result of his benevolence. He told how his boss, who ate lunch at his desk each day from a small leather lunch box, would dispatch an office boy to buy him an apple. These apples cost one pence halfpenny, and Peabody would give the boy twopence; although the boy dreamed of keeping the halfpenny change as a tip, Peabody always demanded it back.
皮博迪的助手托马斯·珀曼享受着这位老板的"现世报"，抖落出一堆黑料，给皮博迪因慈善事业而戴上的光环蒙上了阴影。他讲述道，老板每天都会拎着一个小皮午餐盒在办公桌前吃饭，还差遣办公室小工去给他买个苹果。这些苹果一个要一又二分之一便士，皮博迪会给小工两便士；尽管小工满心想着把找零的半便士当小费留下，皮博迪却总是要他还回来。

By the early 1850s, Peabody was approaching sixty and plagued by gout and rheumatism. His annual savings were staggering: he spent only about \$3,000 of a total annual income of \$300,000.[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187068) With such wealth and such stinjiness, he was ripe for spiritual conversion. As he later said, “When aches and pains came upon me, I realized I was not immortal . . . I found that there were men in life just as anxious to help the poor and destitute as I was to make money.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187239)
到了 19 世纪 50 年代初，皮博迪年近六十，饱受痛风和风湿病折磨。他每年的储蓄惊人：年收入总计 30 万美元，自己却只花掉大约 3000 美元。 [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187068) 腰缠万贯却又如此吝啬，他正处于精神救赎的绝佳时机。正如他后来所说："当病痛袭来，我意识到自己并非永生……我发现，生活中有人和我急于赚钱一样，渴望帮助穷苦之人。" [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187239)

Wanting to dedicate himself to philanthropy, Peabody had only one problem. As an autocratic banker, he had never shared authority and only reluctantly made his office manager, Charles C. Gooch, a junior partner in 1851, so that someone could act in his absence. Gooch was a sad-faced Bob Cratchit who addressed Peabody like a trembling clerk; in fact, he had started as head clerk. He started one letter to his boss by writing, “Dear Sir, I do not often trouble you with letters, for I know you do not like the trouble of reading them, & mine are on subjects not over agreeable.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187399) Gooch was being groomed for a career of permanent subordination and forelock tugging.
皮博迪一心投身慈善，却面临一个难题。作为独断专行的银行家，他从不分享权力，直到 1851 年才勉强任命办公室经理查尔斯·C·古奇为初级合伙人，以便自己不在时有人打理事务。古奇就像个愁眉苦脸的鲍勃·克拉奇特，跟皮博迪说话时活像个战战兢兢的职员——事实上，他最初就是首席文员出身。他在给老板的一封信中写道：“亲爱的先生，我很少写信打扰您，因为我知道您讨厌读信，而我的信又总是不太讨喜。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187399) 古奇注定要永远卑躬屈膝、唯命是从。

Ordinarily, Peabody would have chosen a son or nephew to take over the business. Most merchant banks were family partnerships with a few talented outsiders. But as a bachelor, Peabody was in the unusual position of having to shop for an heir and bequeath his empire to a stranger. He was, however, no stranger to the company of women. While he didn’t smoke or drink, he resorted to the shadowy world of illicit pleasures. The tale-bearing Perman regaled the Morgans with the story of Peabody’s mistress in Brighton, whom he liberally favored with advances of £2,000. He excluded this woman and her illegitimate daughter from his will, and for years after his death, Peabody’s daughter Mrs. Thomas would materialize and badger the Morgans for money. In the late 1890s, the Morgans received an appeal from her two sons—one training to be a barrister, the other at Oxford or Cambridge. The aging Perman was dispatched to verify their Peabody genes. When he returned, he breathed with amazement, “Both of them have the old man’s nose to a dot.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187591)
通常，皮博迪会选儿子或侄子来接班。多数商业银行是家族合伙制，仅吸纳少数外部人才。但作为单身汉，皮博迪处境特殊，不得不物色继承人，把帝国传给一个外人。不过，他对女伴并不陌生。虽然他不烟不酒，却沉湎于隐秘的欢场世界。爱传八卦的珀曼向摩根家族津津乐道地讲述皮博迪在布莱顿的情妇——他慷慨地给她预支了两千英镑。他在遗嘱中排除了这个女人和她的私生女；他去世后多年，皮博迪的女儿托马斯夫人总会现身，纠缠摩根家要钱。1890 年代末，摩根家收到她两个儿子的求助——一个正在实习当律师，另一个在牛津或剑桥读书。年迈的珀曼被派去验证他们身上的皮博迪血统。 他回来后，惊讶地倒吸一口气：“这两个孩子的鼻子简直和那老头一模一样，分毫不差。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187591)

We don’t know why Peabody relegated love to the dim corners of his life. In general, he specialized in what Dickens called telescopic philanthropy—bountiful love for abstract humanity combined with extreme stinginess toward the individuals he knew personally. He would enjoy a reputation for generosity throughout the Victorian world—everywhere, in fact, but among his unacknowledged family and employees.
我们无从得知，Peabody 为什么要把爱恋丢进生命最阴暗的角落。大体说来，他专攻狄更斯所说的“望远镜式博爱”——对抽象的人类慷慨施爱，却对自己认识的活人极度吝啬。在整个维多利亚时代的世界里，他都享有慷慨大方的美誉——事实上，无论到哪里都是如此，唯独在他不愿承认的家人和雇员中间，却没有这样的名声。

Peabody had definite requirements for his successor: he wanted a sociable American with a family and experience in foreign trade. His Boston associate, James Beebe, recommended his junior partner, funius Spencer Morgan. Junius had been with J. M. Beebe, Morgan for three years. In May 1853, he visited London with his family, bringing along his high-spirited but sickly son, John Pierpont, then recovering from rheumatic fever. Pierpont was boyishly thrilled with his first exposure to British culture. He visited Buckingham Palace and Westminster Abbey, excitedly handled a million pounds of bullion at the Bank of England, and listened to a Sunday sermon at Saint Paul’s. Meanwhile, his father talked business with Peabody, whom Pierpont found “pleasant but smoky.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187779) In general, Pierpont found Peabody a queer, likable old buzzard.
皮博迪对自己的继任者有明确的要求：他想要一位善于交际、有家室且具备外贸经验的美国人。他在波士顿的合伙人詹姆斯·毕比推荐了自己的初级合伙人——朱尼厄斯·斯宾塞·摩根。朱尼厄斯在 J. M. 毕比与摩根公司已工作三年。1853 年 5 月，他携家带口前往伦敦，随行的还有他活泼好动却体弱多病的儿子约翰·皮尔庞特——当时他正从风湿热中康复。首次接触英国文化的皮尔庞特带着少年般的兴奋。他参观了白金汉宫和威斯敏斯特教堂，在英格兰银行兴奋地触摸了价值百万英镑的金条，还在圣保罗大教堂听了一场周日布道。与此同时，他的父亲正在与皮尔庞特眼中“风趣但烟不离手”的皮博迪谈生意。 [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187779) 总的来说，皮尔庞特觉得皮博迪是个古怪又讨喜的老家伙。

Junius Spencer Morgan was tall with sloping shoulders and the thickening midriff of a strong but sedentary man. He had a wide face, light blue eyes, a prominent nose, and a firm mouth. He was witty and genial, but a deep reserve and watchfulness lay behind the charm. Junius Morgan always had a gravely mature air. His skeptical eyes gave him a hooded gaze, a banker’s air of vigilance. Big and brooding, he was the sort of prematurely middle-aged young man old financiers found consoling. A contemporary writer called him grim-mouthed; indeed, it is hard to imagine him young or carefree. He was solemn and businesslike and always master of his emotions.
朱尼厄斯·斯宾塞·摩根身材高挑，溜肩膀，腰身已有些发福——那是久坐办公却依然健硕之人的特征。他脸庞宽阔，浅蓝色的眼睛、高挺的鼻梁和紧抿的嘴唇格外醒目。他风趣随和，但这份魅力之下掩藏着深沉的矜持与警觉。朱尼厄斯·摩根总带着一种与年龄不符的成熟老练。那双怀疑的眼睛使他目光如罩，透着银行家特有的警惕。他身形魁梧、神情阴郁，是那种让老金融家们感到安心的早熟青年。当时的作家形容他“神色严峻”，的确，很难想象他有过年少轻狂的模样。他总是一本正经、公事公办，情绪从不外露。

Peabody asked Morgan to be his partner and receive his empire on a silver platter. Junius’s grandson, J. P. Morgan, Jr., later recounted their exchange:
皮博迪邀请摩根成为他的合伙人，并将他的帝国拱手相让。后来，朱尼厄斯的孙子小 J. P. 摩根回忆了他们的对话：

“You know,” said Peabody, “I shall not want to go on much longer but, if you will come as a partner for ten years, I shall retire at the end of them, and at that time shall be willing to leave my name, and, if you have not accumulated a reasonable amount of capital in the concern, some of my money also, and you can go ahead as the head of it.”
“你知道吗，”皮博迪说，“我不打算再做太久了，但如果你愿意作为合伙人跟我干十年，十年后我就退休。届时我不仅愿意把名号留给你，如果你在这期间没能积累起足够的资本，我还可以再留些钱给你，让你全权接手。”

“Well, Mr. Peabody,” replied Morgan, “that sounds like a very good offer, but there are many things to be considered, and I could not think of giving an answer until I have looked over the books of the firm and have some idea of the business and of the methods by which it is done.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187929)
“嗯，皮博迪先生，”摩根回答道，“听起来这确实是个不错的提议，但有很多事情需要斟酌。在我查阅公司的账目、对业务和经营方式有了大致了解之前，我没办法给出答复。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2187929)

It is revealing that Morgan didn’t leap at the fortune but responded with cool self-control. Evidently he was mightily pleased by the books—capital of £450,000, a caliber of business only one rung below the houses of Baring and Rothschild. So in October 1854, he was admitted into partnership, and he settled into new walnut-paneled headquarters at 22 Old Broad Street. The partnership document stipulated that the firm would buy and sell stocks, engage in foreign exchange, extend banking credits, and broker railroad iron and other commodities. To entertain American visitors, Peabody gave Morgan an expense account of £2,500 per year. A fortune had been deeded over—or so it seemed at the time. A decade later, as Peabody was being canonized for his philanthropy, Junius Morgan would bitterly recall the promises Peabody had made to him. And he would join the ranks of those spurned during George Peabody’s ascent to sainthood.
耐人寻味的是，摩根并没有对这笔财富趋之若鹜，而是以冷静的自制力回应。显然，他对这些账目极为满意——45 万英镑的资本，在商业层级上仅比巴林和罗斯柴尔德家族低一个台阶。于是，1854 年 10 月，他被接纳为合伙人，并入驻老宽街 22 号那间崭新的胡桃木镶板总部。合伙协议规定，公司从事股票买卖、外汇交易、银行信贷，以及铁路铁轨与其他商品的经纪业务。为款待美国访客，皮博迪给了摩根每年 2500 英镑的招待费账户。一笔财富似乎已经转交给他——至少当时看来如此。十年后，当皮博迪因其慈善事业被封圣时，朱尼厄斯·摩根痛苦地回忆起皮博迪对他许下的承诺，并成为乔治·皮博迪封圣路上被抛弃者中的一员。

WHEN Morgan moved to London in 1854, it was a more auspicious time for an American banker than it had been when Peabody was flogging the hated Maryland bonds in the 1830s. American grain prices soared during the Crimean War, and western railroads that transported grain boomed as well, creating a mania for their shares. Railroads devoured vast amounts of capital, and in the decade before the Civil War, investors poured \$1 billion into their development, triple any former commitment. As a leading London dealer of American railroad securities, George Peabody and Company was well placed to exploit this latest craze.
1854 年摩根迁居伦敦时，对美国银行家而言，时机已远胜于 1830 年代皮博迪兜售遭人唾弃的马里兰州债券之时。克里米亚战争期间，美国粮价飞涨，运输粮食的西部铁路亦随之繁荣，引发了对铁路股票的狂热追捧。铁路业吞噬着巨额资本，内战前十年间，投资者为铁路建设投入了十亿美元，是此前任何行业投资的三倍。作为伦敦美国铁路证券的顶级交易商，乔治·皮博迪公司得天独厚，尽享这轮投资狂潮的红利。

Yet, as the decade passed, Junius Morgan must have doubted the wisdom of transplanting his family to England. Peabody was a trying partner, and no real warmth existed between the two, as shown by their correspondence when the junior partner visited America each year. Their letters are formal and correct but notably lacking even in pleasantries. Morgan would make obligatory inquiries about Peabody’s health—always apt to please his hypochondriacal partner—but addressed him as “Dear Sir” and signed each letter with frosty respect—“J. S. Morgan.” Morgan found Peabody petty and vindictive and told how his partner once spent half the afternoon hauling some poor cab driver down to the police station for overcharging him.
然而，随着十年光阴流逝，朱尼厄斯·摩根想必曾怀疑过将全家迁至英国是否明智。皮博迪是个令人头疼的合伙人，两人之间并无真正的情谊，从这位初级合伙人每年回国时二人的通信中便可见一斑。他们的信件措辞正式、中规中矩，甚至连客套话都少得可怜。摩根总会例行公事般地询问皮博迪的健康状况——这招对那位总疑心自己有病的合伙人一向很管用——但称呼时只写“亲爱的先生”，每封信末尾签上冷冰冰的敬称“J. S. 摩根”。摩根觉得皮博迪心胸狭隘、睚眦必报，还曾讲过一件事：有回皮博迪因为某个可怜的马车夫多收了几文钱，硬是花了大半个下午把那家伙拖进了警察局。

Then, in 1857, it looked as if Morgan would be denied his promised fortune. Wheat prices tumbled with the end of the Crimean War, causing hardship for American banks and railroads. By October, New York banks stopped gold payments, preventing American correspondents from transferring funds to Peabody in London. He was suddenly overextended on his American bills. At the same time, London investors sold American securities, siphoning more funds from Peabody and provoking a serious cash squeeze. Rumors raced through London that George Peabody and Company was about to fail, a prospect heartily relished by rivals, who disliked the old American. Morgan had also earned the displeasure of Barings by aggressively cutting prices on American securities and trying to steal their accounts.
随后，在 1857 年，摩根眼看就要与承诺的财富失之交臂。随着克里米亚战争的结束，小麦价格暴跌，给美国银行和铁路行业带来了困境。到了 10 月，纽约的银行停止支付黄金，致使美国的代理行无法向伦敦的皮博迪转移资金。他手头的美国汇票突然变得捉襟见肘。与此同时，伦敦的投资者纷纷抛售美国证券，进一步抽走了皮博迪的资金，引发了严重的现金短缺。伦敦街头流言四起，称乔治·皮博迪公司即将倒闭，这让那些素来不喜欢这位老美国人的竞争对手们暗自窃喜。而摩根此前因激进压低美国证券价格、企图抢走账户，也招致了巴林银行的不满。

Now the major London houses told Morgan they would bail out the firm—but only if Peabody shut down the bank within a year. When Morgan relayed this patent blackmail to Peabody, the older man reacted “like a wounded lion.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188106) Defiant, he dared them to bring down his firm. George Peabody and Company was saved by an emergency credit line of £800,000 from the Bank of England, with Barings a guarantor of the loan. The vengeful Peabody, who felt Barings had mercilessly pressed him to pay outstanding bills, asked that the name of the firm be stricken from a published list of banks rescuing his firm. For Peabody, who had just made a resplendent return to America after a twenty-year absence, the incident confirmed his innate pessimism. “It is not yet three months since I parted from you, and left the country prosperous and the people happy,” he wrote his niece. “Now all is gloom and affliction.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188260)
如今，伦敦各大银行向摩根表示，他们愿意出手救助他的公司——但前提是皮博迪必须在一年内关闭银行。当摩根将这个赤裸裸的讹诈条件转告给皮博迪时，这位长者反应激烈，如同“一头受伤的雄狮”。 [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188106) 他毫不示弱，公然挑衅他们，看谁敢真的搞垮他的公司。最终，乔治·皮博迪公司凭借英格兰银行提供的 80 万英镑紧急信贷额度得以保全，巴林银行则充当了这笔贷款的担保人。心怀怨愤的皮博迪认为巴林银行此前冷酷无情地逼他偿还到期账单，于是要求将巴林银行的名字从公开刊登的救助公司名单中剔除。对皮博迪而言——他刚刚在阔别二十年后风光重返美国——这件事更印证了他骨子里的悲观。“我与你分别还不到三个月，当时国家繁荣，人民幸福，”他在给侄女的信中写道，“如今却只剩阴霾与苦难。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188260)

The 1857 panic made a deep impression on Morgan’s twenty-year-old son, Pierpont, who had just started on Wall Street as an unsalaried apprentice at Duncan, Sherman and Company, New York agent for Peabody. Tutored by partner Charles Dabney, an excellent accountant, Pierpont learned to evaluate ledgers and fathom the mysteries of the chaotic American banking system. Ever since Andrew Jackson killed the second Bank of the United States in 1832, the United States lacked a uniform currency. Each state had a separate banking system, and in many places debts could be settled in foreign currency. Pierpont, new to Wall Street, was vexed by rumors of his father’s pending default and heard about the Bank of England rescue while visiting Cyrus Field’s office. His later tolerance for the proposed Federal Reserve System has often been traced to this early Bank of England bailout of his father’s firm.
1857 年的恐慌给摩根二十岁的儿子皮尔庞特留下了深刻印象，当时他刚踏入华尔街，在邓肯-谢尔曼公司——皮博迪在纽约的代理机构——做无薪学徒。在合伙人查尔斯·达布尼（一位出色的会计师）的指导下，皮尔庞特学会了评估账目，并摸清了混乱的美国银行体系的奥秘。自 1832 年安德鲁·杰克逊废黜第二合众国银行以来，美国一直缺乏统一的货币。各州各自为政，许多地方甚至可以用外币清偿债务。初来乍到的皮尔庞特，因父亲可能违约的传闻而烦恼，在拜访赛勒斯·菲尔德办公室时听到了英格兰银行出手相救的消息。他后来对拟议中的联邦储备体系所持的宽容态度，通常可以追溯到这次英格兰银行对他父亲公司的早期救助。

It was a baptism by fire for the Morgan family. Shaken, the elder Morgan became a more cautious and skeptical banker. He now demanded to see statements from correspondent banks in America, even if it meant offending them. And he began to lecture his son, often at wearisome length, on the need for conservative business practice; the 1857 panic would be the text of many sermons. “You are commencing upon your business career at an eventful time,” he wrote. “Let what you now witness make an impression not to be eradicated . . . *slow* &, *sure* should be the motto of every young man.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188421) Junius Morgan developed a lofty disdain for price competition and adopted the royal passivity of the Rothschilds and the Barings, who refused to offer cut-rate terms: “If we cannot keep the account on such a basis we must be content to let others outbid us.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188575)
对摩根家族来说，这是一场烈火洗礼。饱受惊吓的老摩根变得愈发谨慎多疑，如今他坚持要查看美国往来银行的报表，哪怕得罪对方也在所不惜。他还开始不厌其烦地训诫儿子，长篇大论地强调保守经营之道——1857 年的恐慌成了他反复布道的经文。“你正逢多事之秋开启职业生涯，”他写道，“愿你眼下所见之事，刻骨铭心，永不磨灭……‘稳健求成’，当是每位年轻人的座右铭。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188421) 朱尼厄斯·摩根对价格竞争产生了居高临下的鄙夷，并效仿罗斯柴尔德家族和巴林家族那套皇室般的超然姿态——他们拒绝提供低价条款：“若无法在此基础上留住客户，我们便甘愿接受他人以更高出价取而代之。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188575)

Another disaster soon followed. Like the French *banques d’affaires* or the universal German banks, London merchant banks took equity stakes in ventures. For instance, George Peabody and Company had helped to bankroll Sir John Franklin’s expedition in search of the Northwest Passage. But its most farsighted bet was a £100,000 investment in Cyrus Field’s transatlantic cable, which would unite Wall Street and the City. The scheme looked inspired on August 16, 1858, when Queen Victoria made the first cable call, to President James Buchanan. In a burst of national pride, New York City engaged in two weeks of fireworks and euphoric celebration. Peabody dizzily wrote to Field, “Your reflections must be like those of Columbus after the discovery of America.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188710) He spoke too soon, however: in September, the cable snapped, the venture’s share prices plummeted, and Peabody and Junius Morgan absorbed steep losses. Eight years would pass before full service was restored.
另一场灾难接踵而至。伦敦的商人银行与法国的实业银行或德国的全能银行相似，也直接投资企业股权。例如，乔治·皮博迪公司曾资助约翰·富兰克林爵士寻找西北航道的探险，但其最具远见的一笔投资，是向赛勒斯·菲尔德的大西洋电缆投入了 10 万英镑——这条电缆将把华尔街与伦敦金融城连接起来。1858 年 8 月 16 日，维多利亚女王通过这条电缆首次致电詹姆斯·布坎南总统，这一计划看似前景光明。纽约市在民族自豪感中爆发，连续两周燃放烟花、欢欣庆祝。皮博迪兴奋地写信给菲尔德：“你的感受一定如同哥伦布发现美洲大陆一般。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188710) 然而他高兴得太早了：9 月份，电缆断裂，该项目的股价暴跌，皮博迪和朱尼厄斯·摩根承受了巨额损失。直到八年后，电缆才完全恢复运行。

Although Peabody was nominal head until 1864, Junius Morgan assumed control of George Peabody and Company in 1859. In increasingly poor health, Peabody took his first European vacation in twenty-one years. After the outbreak of the American Civil War, Morgan traded Union bonds, which seesawed with the outcome of each battle. After the Union army was routed at Bull Run, bonds plunged, then rebounded sharply when Union troops stopped the Confederate advance at Antietam Creek. Sending a telegram via Nova Scotia, Pierpont alerted his father to Vicksburg’s fall in July 1863—in time for the elder Morgan to profit from a sudden rise in American securities. Such calamity trading wasn’t thought bloodthirsty or reprehensible among merchant bankers but had an honored place in their mythology. As one Rothschild boasted, “When the streets of Paris are running with blood, I buy.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2188875)
尽管皮博迪名义上是负责人直到 1864 年，但朱尼厄斯·摩根在 1859 年就控制了 George Peabody and Company。由于健康日益恶化，Peabody 在 21 年后首次前往欧洲度假。美国内战爆发后，Morgan 交易联邦债券，这些债券随着每场战役的结果而起伏。联邦军在 Bull Run 溃败后，债券暴跌，随后在联邦军在 Antietam Creek 阻止了南方邦联的进攻后大幅反弹。Pierpont 通过新斯科舍省发送电报，在 1863 年 7 月向父亲通报了 Vicksburg 的陷落——正好让老 Morgan 从美国证券的突然上涨中获利。这种灾难交易在商人银行家中并不被认为是残忍或应受谴责的，反而在他们的神话中占有一席之地。正如一位 Rothschild 所吹嘘的那样：“当巴黎的街道流淌着鲜血时，我买入。”

Despite his Yankee sympathies, Morgan was stymied in undertaking Union financing. After southern banks drained their deposits from the North, Lincoln cast about for new sources of funds. With Lancashire textile mills closely allied with southern cotton plantations, the City was cool to any large-scale operation for the North. To finance the war debt, the president turned to Philadelphia banker Jay Cooke—later dubbed a financial P. T. Barnum—whose agents fanned out across America to sell war bonds in the first mass-market securities operation in the country’s history. Among the buyers in London were George Peabody and Junius Morgan. Yet the Civil War was the one major military conflict in which the Morgans were handicapped by political circumstances: it was a bonanza for German-Jewish bankers on Wall Street, who raised loans from the numerous Union sympathizers in Germany. In future, the Morgans’ political impulses would mesh perfectly with profitable opportunities.
尽管摩根同情北方联邦，但在为联邦政府筹集资金时却处处受阻。南方银行从北方抽走存款后，林肯四处寻找新的资金来源。由于兰开夏郡的纺织厂与南方棉花种植园关系密切，伦敦金融城对任何支持北方的重大金融操作都态度冷淡。为了筹措战争债务，总统转而求助于费城银行家杰伊·库克——后来被誉为金融界的 P. T. 巴纳姆——他的代理人遍布全美，在美国历史上首场大众市场证券操作中推销战争债券。伦敦的买家包括乔治·皮博迪和朱尼厄斯·摩根。然而，南北战争是摩根家族因政治环境而受限的唯一重大军事冲突：这对华尔街的德裔犹太银行家来说是笔意外之财，他们从德国众多支持联邦的人士那里筹集了大量贷款。此后，摩根家族的政治冲动将完美契合利润丰厚的机会。

THE Civil War years saw the metamorphosis of George Peabody from Scrooge to Santa Claus. He had been a prototypical heartless banker, a one-dimensional hoarder. As a contemporary said, “Uncle George, as Americans . . . call him—was one of the dullest men in the world: he had positively no gift, except that of making money.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189027) Yet this dour man suddenly became prodigal in his gifts; his philanthropy was as immoderate as his earlier greed. He found it hard to break his miserly habits. “It is not easy to part with the wealth we have accumulated after years of hard work and difficulty,” he confessed.[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189187) Now a lifetime of hoarding was disgorged in one compensatory binge, cleansing his Yankee conscience. Perhaps as a young man Peabody had worked too much for others and as an adult too much for himself. In any event, he could do nothing by halves and again went to extremes.
南北战争期间，乔治·皮博迪完成了从吝啬鬼到圣诞老人的蜕变。他曾是典型的冷酷银行家，一个只会敛财的单调角色。正如当时的人所说：“美国人所称的‘乔治叔叔’——实则是世上最无趣之人：他除了赚钱，毫无天赋可言。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189027) 然而这位阴郁之人突然变得挥金如土；他的慈善事业与其早年的贪婪同样毫无节制。他也深知难以摆脱守财奴的习性。“要把我们历经艰辛积累的财富拱手让人，谈何容易。”他坦言道。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189187) 如今，他用一场补偿性的挥霍倾泻出毕生积蓄，以此涤荡他那清教徒式的良心。或许青年时代的皮博迪为他人付出太多，而成年后又为自己索取过多。无论如何，他做事从不半途而废，再次走向了极端。

By 1857, he had begun to endow a Peabody Institute in Baltimore. (Unlike later Morgan benefactions, often anonymous and discreet, Pea-body wanted his name plastered on every library, fund, or museum he endowed.) In 1862, he began to transfer £150,000 to a trust fund to build housing projects for London’s poor. These Peabody Estates, with gas lamps and running water, would be a vast improvement over the medieval poorhouses of Victorian London, and they still dot the city. He deeded a five-thousand-share block of the Hudson’s Bay Company to finance the operation. For this revolutionary act of generosity, he became the first American to receive the Freedom of the City of London. “From a full and grateful heart,” he declared at a Mansion House dinner, “I say that this day has repaid me for the care and anxiety of fifty years of commercial life.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189348) Peabody’s openhandedness became so proverbial that he was soon besieged with a thousand begging letters a month.
到 1857 年，他已开始为巴尔的摩的皮博迪学院捐资建校。（与后来摩根家族那些往往匿名且低调的捐赠不同，皮博迪要求自己的名字刻在他捐建的每一座图书馆、基金或博物馆上。）1862 年，他开始将 15 万英镑转入一支信托基金，用于为伦敦穷人建造住房项目。这些配备煤气灯和自来水的皮博迪住宅区，将大大改善维多利亚时代伦敦那些中世纪济贫院的状况，如今这些住宅区仍遍布伦敦各处。他捐赠了哈德逊湾公司的五千股股票来资助这一项目。凭借这一革命性的慷慨之举，他成为首位获得伦敦荣誉市民称号的美国人。在市长官邸的一次晚宴上，他说道：“满怀感激之情，我要说，今天这一刻回报了我五十年商海生涯的操劳与忧虑。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189348) 皮博迪的慷慨大方变得如此家喻户晓，以至于他很快每月收到上千封求助信。

During Peabody’s last years, the scope of his charity grew dazzling. He endowed a natural history museum at Yale University, an archaeology and ethnology museum at Harvard, and an educational fund for emancipated southern blacks. For this last, he handed over a \$l-million batch of defaulted Mississippi and Florida bonds, hoping these states would someday resume payment and enrich the fund. There were further bequests for the housing projects, finally amounting to £500,000. As Peabody turned into a one-man welfare state, admirers saw celestial virtues in this former skinflint. Victor Hugo remarked, “On this earth there are men of hate and men of love. Peabody was one of the latter. It is on the face of these men that we see the smile of God.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189576) Gladstone said that he “taught men how to use money and how not to be its slave.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189735) Queen Victoria tried to honor him with a baronetcy or a knighthood, but Peabody—as if a stranger to worldly pleasures—declined this one. Instead, the queen dashed off a fulsome personal note from Windsor Castle, praising Peabody’s “princely munificence” to London’s poor and enclosing a miniature portrait of herself, wearing the Koh-i-noor diamond and the decoration of the Order of the Garter.[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189896)
在皮博迪生命的最后几年，他的慈善事业规模令人叹为观止。他为耶鲁大学捐资建立了一座自然历史博物馆，为哈佛大学捐建了一座考古学与民族学博物馆，还为南方解放的黑人设立了一项教育基金。为了这最后一项，他交出了一批价值 100 万美元的密西西比州和佛罗里达州违约债券，希望这些州有朝一日能恢复还款，使基金更加充裕。此外，他还为住房项目留下了更多遗赠，最终总额达到 50 万英镑。当皮博迪变成一个人的福利国家时，崇拜者们在这位曾经的守财奴身上看到了神圣的美德。维克多·雨果评论道：“在这个世界上，有充满仇恨的人，也有充满爱的人。皮博迪属于后者。正是在这些人的脸上，我们看到了上帝的微笑。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189576) 格莱斯顿说他“教导人们如何使用金钱，以及如何不做金钱的奴隶”。 [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189735) 维多利亚女王试图授予他从男爵或骑士爵位，但皮博迪——仿佛对世俗享乐漠不关心——拒绝了这一荣誉。 相反，女王从温莎城堡发出了一封热情洋溢的亲笔信，赞扬皮博迪对伦敦穷人的“王侯般慷慨”，并随信附上一幅她本人的微型肖像，画中的她佩戴着科伊诺尔钻石和嘉德勋章。 [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2189896)

Throughout this apotheosis, Peabody never extended his charity to Junius Morgan. In 1864, their ten-year agreement expired, and Peabody retired. At this point, according to the promise Peabody had made to lure Morgan to London, the junior partner was to receive the use of his name and possibly his capital. Instead, Peabody decided to pull both his name and his capital from the concern. Perhaps in his new sanctity he wanted to erase his name from the financial map and enshrine it in the world of good works. But to Morgan, as later recorded by his grandson, “it was, at that time, the bitterest disappointment of \[his\] life that Peabody refused to allow the old firm name to be continued.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190123) Junius reluctantly renamed the firm J. S. Morgan and Company (its name until Morgan Grenfell was formed in 1910). Peabody also forced Morgan to buy the office lease at 22 Old Broad Street on onerous terms. J. P. Morgan, Jr., wrote, “My Grandfather always used to say that Mr. Peabody had been very hard on him as to the price of the lease.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190291) Of course, Junius Morgan’s anger toward Peabody was tempered by the extraordinary profits they had divided—over £444,000 earned in a ten-year period. And he *had* inherited the chief American bank in London.
在这场封神大戏中，皮博迪从未对朱尼厄斯·摩根施舍过半点仁慈。1864 年，他们为期十年的协议到期，皮博迪退休了。此时，按照皮博迪当初引诱摩根前往伦敦时许下的承诺，这位年轻的合伙人本应有权继续使用他的名字，或许还能动用他的资本。然而，皮博迪却决定将自己的名字和资金一并撤出公司。也许在他新晋的圣洁光环下，他想把自己的名字从金融版图上抹去，将其供奉在善行义举的世界里。但对摩根而言，正如后来其孙子所记载的那样，“皮博迪拒绝让老公司的名字延续下去，在当时，这成了（他）一生中最苦涩的失望。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190123) 朱尼厄斯不情愿地将公司更名为 J. S. 摩根公司（这个名字一直沿用至 1910 年摩根建富成立）。皮博迪还强迫摩根以苛刻的条件买下老宽街 22 号的办公室租约。小 J. P. 摩根写道：“我祖父过去常说，皮博迪先生……” 皮博迪在租约价格上对他非常苛刻。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190291) 当然，朱尼厄斯·摩根对皮博迪的怒气，因他们分得的巨额利润而缓和——十年间获利超过 44.4 万英镑。而且，他还继承了伦敦最大的美国银行。

When Peabody died, in 1869 at age seventy-four, the British government dug a grave for him in Westminster Abbey, but his deathbed words, “Danvers—Danvers, don’t forget” deprived London of his remains. The Prince of Wales, later Edward VII, unveiled a statue of Peabody behind the Royal Exchange—a rare honor, considering the scarce space in the City. Even in death, Peabody managed to foster Anglo-American harmony. The British had just built a forbidding warship, the *Monarch*, whose sheer size caused consternation in America and scare talk of the vessel’s being used to demand tribute from American cities. The young Andrew Carnegie sent an anonymous cable to the British cabinet: “First and best service possible *tot Monarch*, bringing home body Peabody.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190425) Whether this was the genesis of the idea or not, Queen Victoria shipped Peabody’s corpse to America aboard the ironclad. The ship rigged up a maudlin funeral chapel, with tall candles burning above a black-draped coffin. In America, the ship was met by Admiral Farragut’s squadron. Pierpont Morgan, in charge of funeral arrangements, devised a tribute of martial splendor, with British and American soldiers marching together behind the financier’s coffin.
1869 年，皮博迪去世，享年七十四岁。英国政府本打算将他安葬在威斯敏斯特大教堂，但他临终遗言“丹弗斯——丹弗斯，别忘了”让伦敦失去了他的遗体。后来的爱德华七世，当时还是威尔士亲王，在皇家交易所后为皮博迪的雕像揭幕——考虑到伦敦金融城空间稀缺，这堪称罕见殊荣。即便离世，皮博迪依然促进了英美和谐。英国当时刚造出一艘令人生畏的战舰“君主号”，其庞大体积在美国引发恐慌，甚至有传闻称该舰可能被用于向美国城市勒索贡金。年轻的安德鲁·卡内基匿名致电英国内阁：“为‘君主号’效劳的首要之举，莫过于运送皮博迪遗体归国。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2190425) 不论这个想法是否由此而生，维多利亚女王最终让这艘铁甲舰载着皮博迪的遗体驶向美国。舰上临时搭建了一间哀戚的礼拜堂，黑色帷幔覆盖的棺木旁，高烛长燃。 在美国，这艘船受到了法拉格特上将舰队的迎接。 皮尔庞特·摩根负责葬礼安排，策划了一场军容盛大的悼念仪式，英美士兵一同行进在这位金融家的灵柩之后。
