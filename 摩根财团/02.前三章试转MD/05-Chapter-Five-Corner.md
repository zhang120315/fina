# Chapter Five: Corner

IN 1895, Pierpont Morgan engineered his most dazzling feat: he saved the gold standard and briefly managed to control the flow of gold into and out of the United States. The concept behind the gold standard was simple. Ever since January 1879, the government had pledged to redeem dollars for gold, thus insuring the value of the currency. To make this more than an empty boast and reassure worried investors, Washington had a policy of keeping on hand at least \$100 million in gold coin and bullion.
1895 年，皮尔庞特·摩根完成了他最耀眼的一笔操作：他拯救了金本位制，并短暂地掌控了黄金进出美国的流向。金本位制背后的逻辑很简单。自 1879 年 1 月起，美国政府就承诺用黄金兑换美元，以此确保货币的价值。为了让这个承诺不只是空喊口号，也为了安抚忧心忡忡的投资者，华盛顿方面规定，手头必须始终保有至少 1 亿美元的黄金硬币和金条。

In the early 1890s, huge amounts of gold began to flow from New York to Europe. In the circuitous way of world finance, the trouble started in Argentina. In the 1880s, the City of London was swept by a craze for Argentinean securities, which attracted almost half of British money invested abroad. The principal conduit was Baring Brothers, which shared a good deal of Argentinean business with Junius Morgan. Then the Argentinean wheat crop failed and was followed by a coup in Buenos Aires. The prospect of default hurt the Morgan bank in London but nearly collapsed the august Barings, which lost heavily on its Argentinean bonds.
19 世纪 90 年代初，大量黄金开始从纽约流向欧洲。在全球金融的迂回链条中，问题始于阿根廷。19 世纪 80 年代，伦敦金融城掀起一股阿根廷证券热潮，几乎吸引了英国海外投资的一半。主要渠道是 Baring Brothers，这家银行与 Junius Morgan 共同承揽了大量阿根廷业务。随后阿根廷小麦歉收，布宜诺斯艾利斯又发生政变。违约风险重创了摩根银行在伦敦的业务，几乎让赫赫有名的 Barings 银行垮台——该行因阿根廷债券损失惨重。

To save Barings from bankruptcy in 1890, the Bank of England organized a rescue fund, to which J. S. Morgan and Company and other rivals contributed. The old Baring partnership was liquidated; the reorganized firm would never regain its former power, and a major Morgan rival was weakened. Before long, Barings shared supremacy in Argentina with the Morgans. Meanwhile, with a stigma attached to foreign holdings, British investors retrenched and drained gold from America. This exodus of metal was greatly accelerated by the 1893 panic, with its bank failures and railroad bankruptcies.
1890 年，为拯救濒临破产的巴林银行，英格兰银行组织了一笔救助基金，J.S.摩根公司及其他竞争对手纷纷出资。老巴林合伙制企业随之清算，重组后的公司再也无法恢复往日的权势，摩根的一大劲敌就此削弱。不久后，巴林与摩根在阿根廷平分秋色。与此同时，由于国外资产饱受非议，英国投资者紧缩开支，从美国抽离黄金。1893 年的恐慌——伴随着银行倒闭与铁路破产——更是极大地加速了这场黄金外流。

Adding to European jitters were American attempts to tamper with the U.S. currency. Under the Sherman Silver Purchase Act of 1890, the U.S. Treasury had to buy 4.5 million ounces of silver monthly and issue certificates redeemable in gold or silver. This effectively put America on a bimetal basis—that is, money was backed by both gold and silver—expanding the money supply. For the hard-money men of Europe, this looked as if it were an effort by American debtors to debauch the currency and repay loans in cheaper dollars. These creditors venerated the gold standard as their safeguard against such backdoor default. So European bankers redeemed their dollars for gold and shipped the gold back to Europe. For Pierpont Morgan, this was an alarming throwback to the days when George Peabody had to prove that Americans honored their debt. The Silver Act was repealed in 1893 under pressure from Morgan and other bankers. But wary Europeans feared that Populist forces might yet wreck the gold standard and force them to accept unwanted silver for dollars.
令欧洲更加不安的是美国试图操纵本国货币。根据 1890 年的《谢尔曼白银购买法案》，美国财政部每月须购入 450 万盎司白银，并发行可用黄金或白银兑换的凭证。这实际上使美国进入了金银复本位制——即货币同时以黄金和白银为支撑——从而扩大了货币供应量。在欧洲的硬通货派眼中，这无异于美国债务人企图贬损货币，用贬值后的美元偿还贷款。这些债权人将金本位奉为抵御此类隐性违约的护身符。于是欧洲银行家纷纷将持有的美元兑换成黄金运回欧洲。对皮尔庞特·摩根而言，这令人不安地重演了当年乔治·皮博迪不得不证明美国人会偿还债务的日子。在摩根与其他银行家的压力下，《白银购买法案》于 1893 年被废除。 但谨慎的欧洲人担心，民粹势力仍可能破坏金本位制，并迫使他们接受不受欢迎的银币兑换美元。

Among the indebted farmers of the South and West, the gold standard generated fanatic hatred. The United States was still an agrarian debtor nation, and poor, rural debtors far outnumbered big city bondholders. These farmers had many legitimate grievances, for they contended with the curse of steadily falling prices in the late nineteenth century. Deflation meant they had to repay debt in dearer money—a recipe for ruin. There was no central bank to expand credit during hard times. At the same time, because of tariffs and industrial trusts, the prices of finished goods didn’t fall as fast as the price of food. (Thanks to Pierpont and the railway barons, freight rates actually rose.) So farmers welcomed inflation—specifically, higher prices for their own produce—as the only way to remain equal in the contest against bankers and industrialists.
在南方和西部负债累累的农民中，金本位制激起了狂热的仇恨。当时的美国仍是一个农业债务国，贫穷的农村债务人数量远超大城市的债券持有者。这些农民有诸多合理的不满，因为他们饱受 19 世纪末物价持续下跌的诅咒。通货紧缩意味着他们必须用更值钱的货币偿还债务——这简直是破产的处方。困难时期没有央行来扩大信贷。同时，由于关税和工业托拉斯的存在，成品价格的下跌速度远不及食品价格。（多亏了皮尔庞特和铁路大亨，运费实际上还上涨了。）因此，农民们欢迎通货膨胀——具体来说，就是自己农产品价格上涨——认为这是在与银行家和实业家的对抗中保持平等的唯一途径。

This discontent made bankers the favorite bogeymen in rural political demonology. So venomous was the mood that several western states outlawed bankers, and Texas banned them altogether until 1904.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2211688) This pervasive anger in the hinterlands crystallized around the House of Morgan, which was seen as a mouthpiece for European finance. A popular, grass-roots mythology claimed that the Bank of England and New York bankers had suborned Congress into enacting the gold standard. For decades, William Jennings Bryan rallied the Populist faithful by inveighing against America’s “financial servitude” to British capital.[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2211847) From this period dates the folklore of the House of Morgan as heartless moneymen, traitors in the pay of British gold, glorying in the ruin of American farmers.
这种不满情绪使得银行家成为乡村政治妖魔化中最受欢迎的靶子。民怨之深，以至于美国西部几个州立法禁止银行家从业，德克萨斯州更是直到 1904 年才解除全面禁令。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2211688) 这股在腹地弥漫的怒火集中烧向了被视为欧洲金融喉舌的摩根家族。当时流行的一种草根神话宣称，英格兰银行与纽约银行家们早已买通国会，使其强行推行金本位制。数十年来，威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩一直通过抨击美国对英国资本的"金融奴役"来凝聚民粹主义信徒的忠诚。 [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2211847) 正是从这一时期开始，民间传说将摩根家族描绘成冷酷无情的金融寡头——他们是英国黄金收买的叛徒，以摧毁美国农民为乐。

The nineteenth-century inflationary nostrums that make for tedious study today—greenbacks, free silver coinage, bimetallism, and so on—were attempts by indebted farmers to lighten their debt load. As the 1893 panic worsened, agrarian populists asked the government to mint silver coins and create cheap money, a move supported by the new silver-producing states. Farming districts scoffed at the notion that any damage might be done by going off gold. The *Atlanta Constitution* remarked that “the people of this country, outside the hotbeds of gold-buggery and Shylockism, don’t care how soon gold payments are suspended.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2211996) For Pierpont, however, destruction of the gold standard would subvert European faith in American securities and destroy his life’s work. As he later said, his aim in 1895 was “to build up such relations of confidence between the United States and the money markets of Europe, that capital from there could be secured in large sums for our needs.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2212158)
19 世纪的通货膨胀旧方子——绿背纸币、自由银币铸造、双本位制等等——如今读来令人乏味，但当时却是负债农民试图减轻债务负担的手段。随着 1893 年恐慌加剧，农业民粹主义者要求政府铸造银币、创造廉价货币，这一主张得到了新产银州的支持。农业地区对脱离金本位可能带来损害的说法嗤之以鼻。《亚特兰大宪章报》评论道：“这个国家的人民，除了那些金本位狂热分子和放高利贷者的温床外，根本不在乎何时停止黄金支付。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2211996) 然而对皮尔庞特而言，破坏金本位会动摇欧洲对美国证券的信任，毁掉他毕生的事业。他后来回忆道，1895 年的目标就是“在美国与欧洲货币市场之间建立起信任关系，以便能从那里大量获取我们所需的资本。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2212158)

During 1894, the U.S. gold reserve dipped below the \$100-million floor. Bad money (silver) was driving good money (gold) out of circulation. By January 1895, gold was fleeing New York at a frightening pace. One could watch this “flight capital” in action as gold bullion was loaded onto ships in New York harbor, bound for Europe. At fashionable Manhattan restaurants, sporting men placed wagers as to when America would go bust and declare its inability to redeem dollars for gold.
1894 年间，美国黄金储备跌破了 1 亿美元底线。劣币（白银）正在驱逐良币（黄金）退出流通。到 1895 年 1 月，黄金正以惊人的速度逃离纽约。人们能看到这种“资本外逃”的真实上演——金条被装上纽约港的船只，驶向欧洲。在曼哈顿的高档餐厅里，赌徒们纷纷下注，赌美国何时会破产、宣布无力兑现美元与黄金的兑换。

The beleaguered president, Grover Cleveland, was a friend of the House of Morgan and a staunch advocate of the gold standard. During the four years he spent on Wall Street between his two presidential terms, Cleveland worked in the law offices of Bangs, Stetson, Tracy, and MacVeagh. This was the law firm of Pierpont’s father-in-law, Charles Tracy, located next door to the Morgan bank, at 15 Broad Street. Cleveland had been good friends with the shrewd Francis Lynde Stetson, Pierpont’s lawyer for the railroad reorganizations and known on Wall Street as Morgan’s attorney general. He also befriended many Wall Street people and was one of the twelve pallbearers at the funeral of August Belmont, Sr., in 1890. Although Pierpont was a Republican, he wasn’t antagonistic toward the Democratic Cleveland. In 1884, he cast his lone Democratic vote for Cleveland precisely because the candidate endorsed sound money.
这位身陷困境的总统格罗弗·克利夫兰，是摩根家族的挚友，也是金本位的坚定拥护者。在两次总统任期之间的四年里，他曾在华尔街的邦斯、斯特森、特雷西和麦克维律师事务所工作。这家律所正是皮尔庞特岳父查尔斯·特雷西创办的，位于宽街 15 号，与摩根银行比邻而居。克利夫兰与精明干练的弗朗西斯·林德·斯特森交情甚笃——后者是皮尔庞特在铁路重组案中的御用律师，在华尔街素有"摩根的首席法律顾问"之称。他还结交了许多华尔街人士，并在 1890 年担任老奥古斯特·贝尔蒙特的十二位护柩者之一。尽管皮尔庞特是共和党人，但他对民主党籍的克利夫兰并无敌意。1884 年大选时，正是因为这位候选人支持稳健的货币政策，他才破天荒地把唯一一张民主党选票投给了克利夫兰。

As the gold reserve dipped, Cleveland faced a hostile Republican Congress, which favored free coinage instead of gold; many prairie Democrats concurred. Amid this gloomy deathwatch, Congress refused to grant President Cleveland the authority to replenish the gold reserve through a public bond offering. At the same time, Populist fury made resorting to private bankers like Morgan unthinkable. Cleveland sat paralyzed. By January 24, 1895, gold reserves had declined to \$68 million, and gold coin was especially scarce at the nine Subtreasuries around the country, including that in New York, across Wall Street from the Morgan bank. As a crisis approached, Cleveland turned to the Rothschilds in London, perhaps to deflect charges of being in Wall Street’s pocket. When approached by Rothschilds about a bond issue, J. S. Morgan and Company agreed to participate only if Pierpont handled the American end with the Rothschild representative, August Belmont, Jr. On January 31, Pierpont and Belmont met at the New York Subtreas-ury with William E. Curtis, the assistant secretary of the Treasury. Although no action was taken, the report of the meeting relieved skittish investors, and \$9 million in gold on ships in the harbor was returned to land overnight. For Populists, news of the Morgan-Belmont-Curtis meeting confirmed suspicions of a Wall Street-Washington conspiracy.
随着黄金储备不断减少，克利夫兰面对的是一个充满敌意的共和党国会，他们更倾向于自由铸币而非金本位；许多草原州的民主党人也持相同立场。在这片阴郁的临终观望中，国会拒绝授予克利夫兰总统通过公开发行债券来补充黄金储备的权力。与此同时，民粹主义者的怒火使得向摩根这样的私人银行家求助也变得不可想象。克利夫兰陷入了束手无策的境地。到 1895 年 1 月 24 日，黄金储备已降至 6800 万美元，而全国九个国库分库——包括纽约那个与摩根银行隔街相望的分库——尤其缺乏金币。随着危机临近，克利夫兰转而向伦敦的罗斯柴尔德家族求助，或许是为了摆脱自己受华尔街操控的指责。当罗斯柴尔德家族就债券发行一事接触 J.S.摩根公司时，后者同意参与，但前提是皮尔庞特与罗斯柴尔德家族的代表小奥古斯特·贝尔蒙特共同处理美国一端的业务。 1 月 31 日，皮尔庞特和贝尔蒙特在纽约国库分库与财政部助理部长威廉·E·柯蒂斯会面。虽然当时未采取任何行动，但这次会面的消息安抚了紧张不安的投资者，港口船只上的 900 万美元黄金一夜之间被重新运回岸上。对于平民党人来说，摩根-贝尔蒙特-柯蒂斯会面的消息坐实了他们对华尔街与华盛顿勾结的猜疑。

In the cables he sent to the London partners during this period, Pierpont affords a glimpse into his deepest ideological impulses—his contempt for politics, his regard for European opinion, his allegiance to neoclassic economics, and his disdain for certain Jewish firms. Referring to one leading Jewish house, he said, “we should dislike see business largely in the hands of Speyer & Co. & similar houses.” His identification with the London creditors was patent: “We all have large interests dependent upon maintenance sound currency U.S. Important use every exertion . . . success negotiations . . . greater factor is European absorption even temporarily of bonds.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2212312) His dispatches were often fervent and even melodramatic in tone.
在此期间，皮尔庞特发给伦敦合伙人的电报中，可窥见他内心最深处的意识形态冲动——对政治的蔑视、对欧洲意见的重视、对新古典经济学的忠诚，以及对某些犹太公司的鄙夷。谈及一家领先的犹太银行时，他说：“我们不愿看到业务大量落入施派尔公司及其类似公司之手。”他与伦敦债权人的认同感显而易见：“我们都持有大量利益，依赖于维持美国货币稳健。务必竭尽全力……谈判成功……更重要的因素是欧洲即便暂时吸收债券。”他的电文往往语气激烈，甚至带有戏剧性。

By early February, the New York Subtreasury was losing gold rapidly. Default seemed imminent. Yet the Treasury Secretary John G. Carlisle informed Morgan and Belmont that the Cabinet had flatly rejected their proposed private bond issue. So on Monday, February 4, Belmont set off for Washington, followed by Morgan. Aware of Francis Stetson’s friendship with Cleveland, Morgan told him, “There may be papers to be drawn and I want you,” and brought him along with a new Morgan partner, the handsome young Robert Bacon.[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2212524) Pierpont told his London partners that the United States was on “the brink of the abyss of financial chaos” and that he wanted to help the U.S. government avert calamity.[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2212696)
到二月初，纽约国库分库的黄金正迅速流失，违约似乎迫在眉睫。然而，财政部长约翰·G·卡莱尔告知摩根和贝尔蒙特，内阁已断然否决了他们提出的私募债券发行方案。于是，2 月 4 日周一，贝尔蒙特先行前往华盛顿，摩根紧随其后。摩根知道弗朗西斯·斯泰森与克利夫兰交好，便对他说：“可能会有文件需要起草，我需要你同行。”并带上他与一位英俊年轻的摩根新合伙人罗伯特·培根一同前往。皮尔庞特致信伦敦的合伙人，称美国正处于“金融混乱深渊的边缘”，他希望帮助美国政府避免这场灾难。

Morgan, Bacon, and Stetson took a private railroad car down to Washington, hitched up to the Congressional Limited. When they arrived, they were greeted by Secretary of War Daniel Lamont, who said that the president had decided against a private syndicate and refused to see the party. Pierpont said magisterially, “I have come down to see the president, and I am going to stay here until I see him.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2212871) While Stetson tried to lobby Cleveland, Bacon applied his charms to Attorney General Richard Olney. That night, in a technique he used to steady his nerves, Pierpont played solitaire—a game called Miss Milliken—until the early hours. After breakfast at the Arlington Hotel, he crossed a snowy Lafayette Square to the White House. One pictures the famous stride, described by a biographer as “elemental, jungle-like.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213036)
摩根、培根和斯泰森乘坐一节私人火车车厢南下华盛顿，与国会特快列车接轨。抵达时，战争部长丹尼尔·拉蒙特迎接他们，并告知总统已决定不与私人财团会面，拒绝接见这一行人。皮尔庞特以威严的口吻说道：“我是来见总统的，不见到他，我就不走。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2212871) 斯泰森试图游说克利夫兰，而培根则施展魅力，争取司法部长理查德·奥尔尼的支持。当晚，皮尔庞特用他惯用的镇定神经的方法——玩一种叫“米利肯小姐”的纸牌游戏——一直玩到凌晨。在阿灵顿酒店用过早餐后，他穿过雪花纷飞的拉斐特广场，走向白宫。人们可以想象他那著名的步伐——传记作者形容为“原始而野性，如丛林般。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213036)

Pierpont was often taciturn in meetings. At the White House, obedient as a schoolboy, he sat wordless while Cleveland, Attorney General Olney, and Treasury Secretary Carlisle debated the issue. Edgy, he crushed an unlighted cigar, leaving a pile of tobacco on his pants. Cleveland still clung to the hope of a public bond issue, which would spare him congressional obloquy. Not until a clerk informed Carlisle that only \$9 million in gold coin remained in government vaults on Wall Street did Pierpont pipe up, saying he knew of a \$ 10-million draft about to be presented. “If that \$ 10-million draft is presented, you can’t meet it,” Pierpont said. “It will be all over before 3 o’clock.” “What suggestions have you to make, Mr. Morgan?” replied the president.[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213204)
皮尔庞特在会议上常常沉默寡言。在白宫，他像个听话的小学生一样一言不发，任凭克利夫兰、司法部长奥尔尼和财政部长卡莱尔争论不休。他焦躁不安，捏碎了一支未点燃的雪茄，烟叶碎屑掉落在裤子上。克利夫兰仍抱有希望发行公债，以此避免国会的责难。直到一名职员告知卡莱尔，政府在华尔街的金库里只剩下 900 万美元金币时，皮尔庞特才开口说话，称他知道有一张 1000 万美元的汇票即将提交。“如果那张 1000 万美元的汇票递上来，你们根本应付不了，”皮尔庞特说，“三点之前一切就完了。”“你有什么建议吗，摩根先生？”总统答道。

Pierpont laid out an audacious scheme. The Morgan and Rothschild houses in New York and London would gather 3.5 million ounces of gold, at least half from Europe, in exchange for about \$65 million worth of thirty-year gold bonds. He also promised that gold obtained by the government wouldn’t flow out again. This was the showstopper that mystified the financial world—a promise to rig, temporarily, the gold market. There was some question as to the legality of the proposed issue, and either Morgan or Carlisle dusted off an 1862 statute that granted the Lincoln administration emergency powers to buy gold during the Civil War. When the deal was concluded, Cleveland gave Pierpont a fresh cigar to replace the one he had nervously ground up. Pierpont’s blood was now at full boil. He wired London, “We consider situation critical, politicians appear to have absolute control. If fail & European negotiations abandoned, it is impossible overestimate what will be result U.S.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213372)
皮尔庞特提出了一项大胆的计划。纽约和伦敦的摩根与罗斯柴尔德家族将筹集 350 万盎司黄金，其中至少一半来自欧洲，用以换取价值约 6500 万美元的 30 年期黄金债券。他还承诺，政府获得的黄金不会再流出。这一承诺令金融界大惑不解——他竟要临时操纵黄金市场。拟议的债券发行是否合法存在争议，摩根或卡莱尔中有一人翻出了一部 1862 年的法令，该法令授予林肯政府在内战期间紧急购买黄金的权力。交易敲定后，克利夫兰递给皮尔庞特一支新雪茄，换掉了他之前紧张嚼碎的那支。皮尔庞特的情绪已完全高涨。他给伦敦发电报称：“我们认为形势危急，政客们似乎已完全掌控局面。若失败且欧洲谈判放弃，对美国造成的后果将难以估量。”

Populist pressure still demanded a public bond issue. As a practical matter, Cleveland awaited congressional action on the Springer Bill, which would have allowed the Treasury to sell long-term bonds; if Congress defeated it, Cleveland thought, he could then resort to Wall Street bankers with far less popular abuse. At the Tuesday-morning meeting, it was agreed that Morgan and Belmont should return when the Springer Bill was killed. By the time it was defeated on Thursday evening, Pierpont was already *en route* to Washington, arriving in a blizzard.
民粹压力依然要求公开发行债券。实际上，克利夫兰在等待国会就《斯普林格法案》采取行动——该法案允许财政部出售长期债券；克利夫兰认为，如果国会否决该法案，他就可以转而向华尔街银行家求助，且能避免太多民众指责。在周二早上的会议上，大家同意等《斯普林格法案》被否决后，摩根和贝尔蒙特再返回。到周四晚间该法案被否决时，皮尔庞特已在暴风雪中动身前往华盛顿。

News of the Morgan-Rothschild operation was a sedative for the financial markets. When the syndicate bonds were offered, on February 20, 1895, they sold out in two hours in London, in only twenty-two minutes in New York. Pierpont was jubilant and exhausted: “You cannot appreciate the relief to everybody’s mind for the dangers were so great scarcely anyone dared whisper them.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213590) Yet the syndicate was a victim of its success. It took up the bonds at 104½, then sold them at an opening price of 112¼; they quickly soared to 119. For the cynical, this sudden appreciation proved the syndicate had cheated the government and underpriced the issue. The interest rate of 3¾ was thought extremely harsh. In just twenty-two minutes, the bankers had booked \$6 or \$7 million in profits. Morgan would later claim these figures were vastly exaggerated and that the syndicate had earned less than a 5 percent return. Even commentators such as Allan Nevins and Alexander Dana Noyes, otherwise sympathetic to the operation, condemned the stiff terms. Nonetheless, the bankers believed that they themselves had induced the confidence that had led to the higher prices.
摩根-罗斯柴尔德联合操作的消息对金融市场而言是一剂镇定剂。1895 年 2 月 20 日，这批银团债券一经发行，在伦敦两小时内售罄，在纽约仅用了二十二分钟。皮尔庞特既欣喜又疲惫：“你无法体会大家如释重负的感觉，因为危险如此巨大，几乎没人敢私下议论。”然而，银团却成了自身成功的牺牲品。他们以 104.5 的价格承购债券，随后以 112.25 的开盘价卖出；价格迅速飙升至 119。对愤世嫉俗者而言，这种突发增值证明银团欺骗了政府，定价过低。3.75%的利率被认为极其苛刻。仅在二十二分钟内，银行家们就录得了 600 万至 700 万美元的利润。摩根后来声称这些数字被严重夸大，银团实际收益率不到 5%。 连艾伦·内文斯和亚历山大·达纳·诺耶斯这样的评论员，尽管对此次操作持同情态度，也谴责了其苛刻的条款。然而，银行家们却认为，正是他们自己唤起了信心，从而推高了价格。

The Populist uproar was furious and laced with anti-Semitism because of the Rothschild participation. Populist rabble-rouser Mary Lease called President Cleveland a tool “of Jewish bankers and British gold.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213745) The New York *World* described the syndicate as a pack of “bloodsucking Jews and aliens.” In his vehement denunciation in Congress, William Jennings Bryan asked the clerk to read Shylock’s bond from *The Merchant of Venice.* Bryan always denied that his attacks pandered to anti-Semitism. Campaigning in 1896, he told Jewish Democrats in Chicago, “Our opponents have sometimes tried to make it appear that we are attacking a race when we denounced the financial policy of the Rothschilds. But we are not, we are as much opposed to the financial policy of J. Pierpont Morgan as we are to the financial policy of the Rothschilds.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213933)
民粹主义者的愤怒异常激烈，且因罗思柴尔德家族的介入而带上了反犹色彩。民粹煽动者玛丽·利斯称克利夫兰总统是“犹太银行家和英国黄金”的工具。 [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213745) 《纽约世界报》将那个财团描述为一群“嗜血的犹太人和外国佬”。威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩在国会激烈抨击时，让书记员朗读《威尼斯商人》中夏洛克的借据。布莱恩始终否认他的攻击是在迎合反犹主义。1896 年竞选期间，他对芝加哥的犹太民主党人说：“我们的对手有时试图让我们看起来是在攻击一个种族，当我们谴责罗思柴尔德家族的金融政策时。但我们并非如此，我们反对 J·皮尔庞特·摩根金融政策的程度，丝毫不亚于反对罗思柴尔德家族的金融政策。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2213933)

The gold syndicate, alas, was just a temporary victory: even Pierpont could dam up the gold supply for only so long. By the summer, gold again left the Treasury in large amounts. When a new loan was raised in early 1896, Pierpont had a fresh scheme for a global syndicate which would include the National City Bank of New York, Deutsche Bank of Berlin, and Morgan, Harjes of Paris. (Perhaps to appease the anti-Semites, it was a syndicate of Christian bankers.) But Cleveland didn’t want to incite Populist wrath a second time and decided on a public loan, with Morgan taking only about half of a \$67-million bond issue.
黄金财团，唉，不过是暂时的胜利：就连 Pierpont 也无法无限期地堵住黄金外流。到了夏天，黄金再次大量流出国库。1896 年初发行新国债时，Pierpont 又策划了一个全球财团的新方案，成员包括纽约 National City Bank、柏林 Deutsche Bank 和巴黎 Morgan, Harjes。（或许是为了安抚反犹主义者，这个财团由基督教银行家组成。）但 Cleveland 不想再次激起人民党人的怒火，决定公开发行国债，而 Morgan 只认购了 6700 万美元债券中约一半的份额。

Despite his venality, the gold operation had been a *tour de force* for Pierpont. He had functioned as America’s central bank, stepping into the historic breach between Andrew Jackson’s 1832 veto of the second Bank of the United States and passage of the Federal Reserve Act in 1913. So long as governments were financially weak, with primitive monetary methods and small budgets, they had to rely on private bankers. For his part, Grover Cleveland never regretted his decision, praising the “lightning-like rapidity” with which Pierpont Morgan reached his decision and extolling him as a man “of clear-sighted, far-seeing patriotism.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214109) By stubbornly adhering to principle, Cleveland alienated smalltown farm elements in his own party. In 1896, the Democrats rejected him in favor of William Jennings Bryan. For Bryan, Morgan was a Pontius Pilate who nailed starving farmers to a cross of gold. The sheer savagery of these attacks contributed to the secretive, cautious style of the Morgan bank, which, in turn, further fed popular fantasy about its power.
尽管他贪得无厌，但金本位操作对皮尔庞特而言可谓一记绝妙手笔。他充当了美国的中央银行，填补了从 1832 年安德鲁·杰克逊否决第二合众国银行到 1913 年《联邦储备法》通过之间的历史空白。只要政府财力薄弱，货币政策原始、预算规模有限，就不得不依赖私人银行家。而格罗弗·克利夫兰从未后悔自己的决定，盛赞皮尔庞特·摩根以“闪电般的速度”做出决断，称其为“目光敏锐、有远见卓识的爱国者”。但克利夫兰因固执己见疏远了党内代表小镇农业利益的派系。1896 年，民主党抛弃了他，转而支持威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩。在布莱恩眼中，摩根就是那个将饥饿的农民钉死在黄金十字架上的本丢·彼拉多。 这些攻击的野蛮行径促使摩根银行形成了隐秘而谨慎的风格，而这反过来又进一步助长了公众对其权力的幻想。

During the 1896 presidential campaign, Pierpont lobbied for a gold-standard plank in the Republican party platform. He entertained Mark Hanna, Ohio banker and chairman of the Republican National Committee, aboard the *Corsair II.* Generous contributions by Morgan and other bankers to the campaign of William McKinley—23 Wall Street was hung with banners in his support—were thought instrumental in persuading him to champion the gold standard, and in 1900 he signed a law bestowing upon it new legal status. The farmer-banker conflict subsided somewhat when a European wheat famine pushed up farm prices. Also, the Yukon gold rush and gold strikes in South Africa and Australia helped expand the U.S. money supply and led to higher prices. The bitter deflationary politics of the late nineteenth century subsided.
1896 年总统竞选期间，皮尔庞特积极游说共和党党纲中加入金本位条款。他在“科西嘉二世号”游艇上款待了俄亥俄州银行家、共和党全国委员会主席马克·汉纳。摩根及其他银行家对威廉·麦金莱竞选的慷慨捐赠——华尔街 23 号挂满了支持他的横幅——被认为在说服他支持金本位方面起到了关键作用，1900 年麦金莱签署法律，赋予金本位新的法律地位。欧洲小麦歉收推高了农产品价格，农民与银行家之间的矛盾有所缓和。此外，育空地区的淘金热以及南非和澳大利亚的金矿发现，扩大了美国的货币供应，推动了物价上涨。19 世纪末激烈的通货紧缩政治因此消退。

In the 1890s, Pierpont Morgan represented a fact unpalatable to Americans—that America was still financially dependent on Europe. As a debtor nation, the United States had to placate its creditors abroad. England exerted much the same influence over American economic policy as Japan would nearly a century later, when it financed much of the U.S. budget deficit in the 1980s. Like Japan, England was criticized for curbing homegrown American excesses. As Keynes noted, “A debtor nation does not love its creditor, and it is fruitless to expect feelings of goodwill.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214301) The ill will descended upon the House of Morgan.
19 世纪 90 年代，皮尔庞特·摩根代表了一个令美国人难以接受的事实——美国在金融上仍然依赖欧洲。作为债务国，美国必须安抚海外债权人。英国对美国经济政策的影响，与近一个世纪后日本在 20 世纪 80 年代为美国大量赤字融资时的影响如出一辙。和日本一样，英国因遏制美国本土的过度行为而遭到批评。正如凯恩斯所言：“债务国不会爱它的债主，指望它心怀善意是徒劳的。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214301) 这股敌意最终落到了摩根财团头上。

Tutored in London finance, Pierpont knew that British bankers considered the pound’s stability the basis of British wealth. In the nineteenth century, it was the currency every investor wanted to hold. Pierpont adopted the same attitude toward the dollar. Sound monetary policy in the United States would be a precondition of America’s rise as the chief creditor nation. In the 1920s, by one of those ironies so abundant in Morgan annals, the bank would put England itself back on the gold standard, forcing a later British prime minister to suffer the same repudiation by his own party as Grover Cleveland experienced in 1895.
皮尔庞特在伦敦金融界受过熏陶，深知英国银行家视英镑稳定为英国财富的根基。19 世纪，英镑是每个投资者都想持有的货币。皮尔庞特对美元也持同样态度。稳健的货币政策将成为美国崛起为主要债权国的先决条件。到了 20 世纪 20 年代，历史戏剧性的一幕降临——摩根银行竟亲手将英国重新推上金本位制，迫使后来的一位英国首相重蹈 1895 年格罗弗·克利夫兰的覆辙，遭受自己党派的背弃。

IN Pierpont Morgan’s career, success often bred more controversy than acclaim, so the twentieth century was his time of bittersweet triumph. Sleek and portly in top hat and black overcoat, gray slacks reaching the tops of shiny shoes, and a watch chain stretched across his paunch, he personified the new tycoon and the industrial gigantism threatening pastoral America. His exploits were rendered in mythic terminology. *Life* magazine produced a lasting catechism: “Q. Who made the world, Charles? A. God made the world in 4004 B.C., but it was reorganized in 1901 by James J. Hill, J. Pierpont Morgan and John D. Rockefeller.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214492) Finley Peter Dunne’s character Mr. Dooley pictured Morgan this way: “Pierpont Morgan calls in wan iv his office boys, th’ prisidint iv a national bank, an’ says he, ’James,’ he says, ’take some change out iv th’ damper an’ r-run out an’ buy Europe f’r me,’ he says. ’I intind to reorganize it an’ put it on a paying basis.’ ”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214659) When Pierpont was quoted as saying “America is good enough for me,” William Jennings Bryan’s *Commoner* snapped back, “Whenever he doesn’t like it, he can give it back.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214785) Editorial writers competed to mint Morgan titles—king of trusts, morganizer of the world, financial titan, Napoleon of finance, or, more simply, Zeus or Jupiter.
在皮尔庞特·摩根的职业生涯中，成功带来的往往不是赞誉，而是争议。因此，二十世纪对他而言是一个苦乐参半的胜利时代。他身材圆润，头戴礼帽，身着黑色外套，灰色长裤垂至锃亮的皮鞋，怀表链横跨凸起的腹部——他正是新兴大亨和工业化巨人症的化身，这种巨兽正威胁着田园牧歌般的美国。他的事迹被赋予神话般的描述。《生活》杂志留下了一段经久不衰的问答录：“问：查尔斯，世界是谁创造的？答：上帝在公元前 4004 年创造了世界，但 1901 年，詹姆斯·J·希尔、J·皮尔庞特·摩根和约翰·D·洛克菲勒重新改组了它。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214492) 芬利·彼得·邓恩笔下的人物杜利先生这样描绘摩根：“皮尔庞特·摩根叫来他办公室的一个小弟——那人是某国家银行的行长——说道：‘詹姆斯，’他说，‘从保险柜里拿点零钱，跑出去给我把欧洲买下来。’他说，‘我打算把它重组一下，让它能盈利。’”’ ” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214659) 当皮尔庞特被引用说“美国对我来说已经足够好了”时，威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩的《平民》报立刻回怼道：“要是他不喜欢，大可以把它还回去。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214785) 社论写手们争相为摩根创造头衔——托拉斯之王、世界的摩根化者、金融巨擘、金融界的拿破仑，或者更简单点，宙斯或朱庇特。

For a republican country lacking a feudal tradition, Morgan and other robber barons were ersatz aristocrats, their feats avidly chronicled by the press. The public reacted with fear and resentment but also with some vicarious pleasure. When Pierpont brusquely ordered his chauffeur to bypass traffic and drive up on a curb, the public was shocked by his arrogance but admiring of his implacable will. When Wall Street broker Henry Clews said of Morgan, “He has the driving power of a locomotive,” he suggested something brutish and uncontrollable, but also something of superhuman strength.[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214960)
对于一个缺乏封建传统的共和制国家而言，摩根和其他强盗大亨成了假贵族，媒体狂热地记载着他们的丰功伟绩。公众对此既怕又恨，但同时也带着些许代入式的快感。当皮尔庞特粗暴地命令司机绕过车流、直接开上人行道时，公众虽震惊于他的傲慢，却也敬佩他那不可动摇的意志。当华尔街经纪人亨利·克卢斯评价摩根“他有火车头般的驱动力”时，这话既暗示了某种蛮横且难以控制的力量，也透露出一种超人的气魄。 [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2214960)

Now the world’s most powerful private banker, Pierpont regarded himself as a peer of royalty. With regal munificence, he dispensed benefactions to the masses. Regretting the dark interior of Saint Paul’s Cathedral in London, he underwrote the expense of electric lighting. He visited the kaiser aboard his yacht and advised King Leopold of Belgium on his finances. In 1901, Jack reported to his mother how his father and London partner Sir Clinton Dawkins went down to Gravesend “and dined with the King of the Belgians who wanted to see them about some business and brought his yacht over because Father would not go to Brussels.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215128) Pierpont did business on his own territory, even if it sometimes meant treating a king as a commoner.
如今，世界上最强大的私人银行家皮尔庞特自视为与皇室平起平坐的人物。他以帝王般的慷慨向大众施以恩惠。因惋惜伦敦圣保罗大教堂内部的昏暗，他出资承担了电力照明的费用。他登上自己的游艇接见德皇，并为比利时国王利奥波德提供财务建议。1901 年，杰克在给母亲的信中提到，父亲与伦敦合伙人克林顿·道金斯爵士一同前往格雷夫森德，“与比利时国王共进晚餐，国王因有要事相商，便将游艇驶来，因为父亲不愿去布鲁塞尔。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215128) 皮尔庞特只在自己的地盘上谈生意，即便这意味着有时要让国王屈尊就驾。

In 1906, Pierpont vouchsafed a private tour of his art collection at 13 Princes Gate, the townhouse he inherited from his father, to King Edward VII. He had given the king financial advice, and the two often met at European watering holes. Gazing at Sir Thomas Lawrence’s famous portrait of the countess of Derby, the king said the ceiling was too low for the picture. “Why do you hang it there?” he asked. “Because I like it there, sir,” said Pierpont tersely, feeling no need to elaborate. His son-in-law Herbert Satterlee noted a perfect equality between king and banker: “They were just two friends together and seemed quite content to sit in silence sometimes and not try to entertain each other.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215318) As a coronation gift, Pierpont had given the king a \$500,000 tapestry, which set off a long-lasting relationship between the House of Morgan and British royalty.
1906 年，皮尔庞特在继承自父亲的王子门 13 号豪宅，为爱德华七世安排了一场私人艺术收藏导览。他常为国王提供财政建议，二人也常在欧洲社交胜地会面。当国王驻足凝视托马斯·劳伦斯爵士为德比伯爵夫人绘制的著名肖像时，直言天花板太低，配不上这幅画。"为何要挂在此处？"他问道。"因为我喜欢，陛下。"皮尔庞特简短作答，无意赘述。其女婿赫伯特·萨特利观察到，银行家与国王之间完全平等："他们就像两位老友相伴，有时静默无言也怡然自得，无需刻意取悦对方。" [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215318) 作为加冕贺礼，皮尔庞特向国王赠送了一幅价值五十万美元的挂毯，由此开启了摩根家族与英国王室的世纪渊源。

Pierpont also pleased Italian royalty. In 1904, he was honored by Italy for returning a treasured cope that turned out to have been stolen from the Cathedral of Ascoli. King Victor Emmanuel conferred upon him the Great Cordon of Saints Mauritius and Lazarus, making him a cousin of His Majesty whenever he set foot on Italian soil.
皮尔庞特也曾让意大利皇室欣喜不已。1904 年，他因归还一件珍贵的祭披（后被发现是从阿斯科利大教堂盗走的）而受到意大利的表彰。维托里奥·埃马努埃莱国王授予他圣莫里斯和圣拉撒路大十字勋章，从此他只要踏上意大利的土地，便享有与国王表亲同等的礼遇。

Even as Pierpont aspired to heaven, he made religious men think in earthly terms. After a 1905 audience, Pope Pius X breathed with regret: “What a pity I did not think of asking Mr. Morgan to give us some advice about our finances!”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215486) The House of Morgan would later advise the papacy on its purchases of American stocks.
即便皮尔庞特心怀天堂，他也让宗教人士从世俗的角度思考问题。1905 年的一次会面后，教皇庇护十世遗憾地叹息道：“真可惜，我怎么就没想到请摩根先生给我们指点一下财务呢！” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215486) 后来，摩根银行还真为教皇国购买美国股票出谋划策。

As a rule, Pierpont didn’t assemble palatial homes. In business as well, he showed surprisingly scant interest in real estate, which produced so many fortunes among his contemporaries. He would say laughingly that he only needed “a place to live in and a lot in the cemetery,” and his son, Jack, proudly confessed himself an ignoramus about land.[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215646) Instead of grand estates, Pierpont had his solid but unpretentious Madison Avenue townhouse and his Hudson River retreat, Cragston, with its kennels, dairies, and gardens.
通常来说，皮尔庞特并不热衷于建造豪宅。在商场上，他对房地产也表现出令人意外的淡漠，而当时他的许多同时代人正是靠房地产发家致富的。他常笑着说自己只需要“一个住的地方和一块墓地”，他的儿子杰克也坦然承认自己对地产一窍不通。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215646) 皮尔庞特没有宏伟的庄园，只有他坐落于麦迪逊大道上那座朴实却不失坚固的联排别墅，以及位于哈德逊河畔的乡间居所克莱格斯顿——那里有犬舍、奶牛场和花园。

The splendid exception was Camp Uncas, in the Adirondack Mountains of upstate New York, and that came to him only by accident. In 1898, a friend, architect William West Durant, defaulted on a loan and signed over the rustic camp as payment. Deep in the woods, Camp Uncas crouched beneath wooded cliffs that were thick with evergreens. It covered more than a thousand acres and required a year-round staff of thirty to care for the main lodge and dozens of outlying buildings. Durant had popularized such millionaire retreats in wilderness areas, producing the most lavish log cabins ever made. They had thick wooden posts, walk-in fireplaces, and heavy exposed beams. To lend a rustic, woodland atmosphere, the furniture was nicked with ax scars, and bark was left on the pine logs. Wool Indian blankets, moose heads, and prize fish decorated the walls. When Pierpont threw parties there, he would bring up a private railroad car full of friends, and a baggage car loaded with racks of vintage champagne would rattle along behind them.
一处绝妙的例外是位于纽约州北部阿迪朗达克山脉的“昂卡斯营地”，而这块宝地也是他机缘巧合下才得到的。1898 年，他的朋友、建筑师威廉·韦斯特·杜兰特无力偿还贷款，便将这座质朴的营地签下作为抵债。昂卡斯营地深藏于密林之中，蜷伏在覆盖着茂密常青树的林木悬崖之下。它占地超过一千英亩，需要一支三十人的全年团队来维护主楼和数十座外围建筑。杜兰特正是在荒野中推广了这类百万富翁的度假胜地，打造出了有史以来最为奢华的圆木小屋。这些小屋拥有粗大的木柱、可步入的壁炉和沉重的裸露横梁。为了营造质朴的林间氛围，家具上故意留有斧痕，松木上保留着树皮。墙上装饰着印第安羊毛毯、麋鹿头和获奖的鱼类标本。 每当皮尔庞特在那里举办派对时，他会带来一整车私人车厢的满座朋友。一辆载满陈年香槟酒架的行李车会跟在后面，哐当哐当地行进。

With his vagabond nature, Pierpont was too restless to be a member of the landed gentry. His splendor shone most fully at sea. As commodore of the New York Yacht Club, he offered Morgan Cups for races and helped finance the *Columbia*, which defended the America’s Cup. He even provided land for the yacht club’s new headquarters, on West Forty-fourth Street.
皮尔庞特生性漂泊，根本坐不住，做不了地主乡绅。他在海上才真正大放异彩。作为纽约游艇俱乐部的准将，他出资设立了 Morgan Cups 帆船赛奖杯，还资助了捍卫美国杯的 Columbia 号帆船。他甚至为俱乐部位于西 44 街的新总部提供了土地。

Pierpont’s boats, more impressive than his homes, were the real monuments to his wealth. In 1898, over his heated protest, the navy conscripted *Corsair II* for use in the Spanish-American War. The Morgans had opposed the war, and Jack (later labeled a warmonger for his role in World War I) lamented the “needless waste of life & property.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215822) The navy paid Pierpont \$225,000 for the ship and transformed it into the gunboat *Gloucester.* It saw action in the Battle of Santiago and was damaged by a Spanish shell. Pierpont kept a piece of the ship’s splintered mast as a memento.
皮尔庞特的游艇比他的豪宅更令人印象深刻，这些游艇才是他财富的真正纪念碑。1898 年，尽管他强烈抗议，海军还是征用了“海盗二号”，用于美西战争。摩根家族曾反对这场战争，杰克（后来因在第一次世界大战中的角色被称为战争贩子）哀叹“对生命与财产的无谓浪费”。 [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2215822) 海军向皮尔庞特支付了 22.5 万美元买下这艘船，并将其改装成炮舰“格洛斯特号”。它参与了圣地亚哥战役，并被一枚西班牙炮弹击中受损。皮尔庞特保留了一片破碎的桅杆作为纪念。

*Corsair III* was an even more megalomaniacal affair, a modern phar-oah’s tomb. Like a lover mourning his dead mistress, Pierpont had reproduced, at fantastic expense, the carpeting and other details of *Corsair II.* Measuring over three hundred feet at the waterline and requiring a crew of seventy, this black-hulled oceangoing ship was built on an altogether new and more garish scale. Among its many details was a special humidor to freshen Pierpont’s black eight-inch Meridiana Kohinoor cigars. He reveled in nautical spectacle. When he returned by liner from Europe, the *Corsair* would steam out to greet him as he waved his handkerchief from the larger ship’s deck. By transferring to the *Corsair*, he could slip through quarantine without having to mingle with the liner’s steerage passengers.
“科尔萨三世”号堪称一场更为狂妄的宏大工程，宛如一座现代法老陵墓。皮尔庞特像哀悼亡妻的情人，不惜重金复刻了“科尔萨二世”号的地毯及其他细节。这艘黑壳远洋游艇水线长度超过三百英尺，需配备七十名船员，整体建造规模全然一新且更加浮夸。诸多细节中包括一个专用保湿箱，用于养护皮尔庞特那八英寸长的黑色 Meridiana Kohinoor 雪茄。他沉迷于海上排场：当乘远洋班轮从欧洲归来时，“科尔萨”号会破浪迎候，而他则站在大船甲板上挥舞手帕。转乘“科尔萨”号后，他便可避开检疫，无需与统舱旅客混杂。

Pierpont often slept aboard his yacht and took clients for sunset cruises. Sometimes, after entertaining friends at Cragston for the weekend, they would all steam back to Manhattan on a Sunday evening, sleep on board, and then awake to a plentiful breakfast before disembarking. The *Corsair* was a therapeutic, if expensive, toy for Pierpont. He continued to slip into depressions that he couldn’t shake, and his triumphs seemed only to deepen his gloom. The sea alone would lighten his mood. As Jack told his mother of one 1898 ocean voyage, “JPM has been so worried and bothered by the number of things on his mind and this annoyance of war rumor that it will be a great thing for him to have this voyage. Then if things calm down . . . he will come back for his Aix cure and get 2 more voyages. Those are the only things which really seem to do him any good.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216013) Though this may have been partly a cover story—Jack’s way of shielding his mother from his father’s growing number of affairs—it was also true that for Pierpont Morgan the sea was always his sovereign remedy.
皮尔庞特经常在游艇上过夜，并带着客户欣赏日落巡航。有时，周末在克雷格斯顿招待完朋友后，大家会在周日晚上一起乘船返回曼哈顿，在船上过夜，醒来享用丰盛的早餐后再下船。对皮尔庞特来说，这艘“海盗号”游艇虽然昂贵，却是一种疗愈的玩具。他时不时陷入无法摆脱的抑郁情绪，而他的成功似乎只会加深他的阴郁。只有大海才能让他心情好转。正如杰克在 1898 年一次远航中对他母亲所说的那样：“JPM 因为太多事情烦恼，加上战争谣言的困扰，这次航行对他来说真是件好事。等事情平息下来……他会回来进行他的艾克斯治疗，再安排两次航行。这些才是真正对他有益的事情。”" [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216013) 虽然这或许在某种程度上只是托辞——杰克想借此保护母亲，不让她知晓父亲日益增多的风流韵事——但对皮尔庞特·摩根而言，大海确实始终是一剂灵丹妙药。

THE dawning of the new century was accompanied by the first great wave of mergers in American history. Spurred by the telephone and telegraph and better transportation, local markets were newly interlaced in regional and national markets. And with American victory in the Spanish-American War, the attention of business also shifted from internal expansion to a global quest for markets. Driven by such changes in the economy, the number of mergers jumped from a modest sixty-nine in 1897 to over twelve hundred by 1899.
新世纪的曙光，伴随着美国历史上第一次并购浪潮而来。在电话、电报以及更便捷的交通推动下，地方市场前所未有地交织成区域性和全国性市场。而随着美国在美西战争中获胜，商业的目光也从内部扩张转向全球市场的争夺。受经济领域这些变革的驱动，企业并购数量从 1897 年区区 69 起，跃升至 1899 年的 1200 多起。"

So long as markets were local, industry seldom required large-scale financing, and there was a Wall Street and City bias against manufacturers as small-time businessmen. The Morgans had been mostly associated with railroad securities. (As late as 1911, the second Baron Revelstoke of Barings could snobbishly protest, “I confess that personally I have a horror of all industrial companies.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216188)) Now, as the great merger wave gathered pace, the focus of elite Wall Street banks shifted from railroads to industrial trusts. In a trust, stockholders would trade their shares in constituent companies for the “trust certificates” of a super holding company. After enacting a law that permitted one company to own another, New Jersey became the preferred state for trust incorporation. By 1901, these new corporate leviathans dominated a long list of industries—sugar, lead, whiskey, plate glass, wire nails, smelting, and coal.
只要市场局限于本地，工业通常不需要大规模融资，而华尔街和伦敦金融城则带有偏见，将制造商视为小生意人。摩根家族主要与铁路证券打交道。（直到 1911 年，巴林银行的第二代雷弗尔斯托克男爵仍能带着优越感抗议道：“我承认，我个人对所有的工业公司都感到恐惧。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216188) ）如今，随着并购大潮加速推进，华尔街精英银行的目光从铁路转向了工业信托。在信托结构中，股东将所持各成员公司的股票，换取一家超级控股公司的“信托凭证”。新泽西州通过允许一家公司持有另一家公司股份的法律后，成为信托公司注册的首选之地。到 1901 年，这些新兴的企业巨兽主宰了从蔗糖、铅、威士忌、平板玻璃、钢丝钉、冶炼到煤炭的一长串行业。

Wall Street bankers effected many of these industrial transformations, and their power swelled in tandem with their creations. Often, trusts were cobbled together from family-owned or closely held firms that had a visceral contempt for competitors’ joining the same trust; the bankers were the honest brokers who arbitrated the disputes among them. Since the bankers appraised the value of participating companies, they had to be fair; since this appraisal was seldom accepted by everyone, they had to be stern. Most of all, they had to be trusted. The populace might dread the power of Pierpont Morgan, but he paid his bills promptly, always stuck by his word, and was almost universally respected among businessmen. He also saw competition as a destructive, inefficient force and instinctively favored large-scale combination as the cure. Once, when the manager of the Moet and Chandon wine company complained about industry problems, Pierpont blithely suggested he buy up the entire champagne country.[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216353)
华尔街银行家们主导了诸多产业变革，其权势也随着缔造的产业版图同步膨胀。这些托拉斯常由家族企业或私营公司拼凑而成，而加入同一托拉斯的竞争者之间往往充满敌意；银行家们便充当诚实的中介，调解各方纷争。由于银行家要对参与企业进行估值，他们必须秉持公正；而估值结果罕有让各方全盘接受，所以他们又必须铁面无私。最为关键的是，他们必须值得信赖。民众或许畏惧皮尔庞特·摩根的权势，但他向来准时结账、信守承诺，在商界几乎有口皆碑。他视竞争为破坏性的低效力量，本能地推崇大规模整合作为解决之道。 有一次，当酩悦香槟公司的经理抱怨行业问题时，皮尔庞特轻描淡写地建议他把整个香槟产区都买下来。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216353)

In William McKinley, the business community had a Republican president who approved of consolidation and didn’t interpose any bothersome antitrust obstacles. The genesis of United States Steel in 1901 was inseparable from this permissive regulatory mood, which followed the 1900 GOP landslide. With the defeat of William Jennings Bryan and his anti-imperialist, trust-busting supporters, the business community felt emboldened to try bigger things. A few weeks after the GOP’s massive victory, Vice-President Theodore Roosevelt invited Elihu Root, the secretary of war, to attend a dinner in honor of Pierpont Morgan. “I hope you can come to my dinner to J. Pierpont Morgan,” he wrote. “You see, it represents an effort on my part to become a conservative man in touch with the influential classes and I think I deserve encouragement.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216517)
在威廉·麦金莱执政时期，商界拥有一位共和党总统，他认可企业兼并，且不设置任何反垄断障碍。1901 年美国钢铁公司的诞生与这种宽松的监管氛围密不可分，而这种氛围正源于 1900 年共和党压倒性的选举胜利。随着威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩及其反帝国主义、反垄断支持者的落败，商界倍感振奋，开始尝试更大规模的整合。共和党大获全胜几周后，副总统西奥多·罗斯福致函战争部长伊莱休·鲁特，邀请他出席为皮尔庞特·摩根举办的宴会。“希望你能来参加我为 J·皮尔庞特·摩根设的晚宴，”他写道，“你看，这代表我努力向保守派靠拢，与有影响力的阶层保持联系——我想我值得鼓励。”

This dinner preceded by a week the first discussions about U.S. Steel and must have reassured Pierpont that the McKinley administration would be supine in its attitude toward trusts. The inception of the steel trust is still debated. The more colorful versions attribute the idea to steelman John W. “Bet-a-Million” Gates, who allegedly came up with it while shooting pool at the Waldorf-Astoria Hotel, then on Fifth Avenue at Thirty-fourth Street. A former barbed-wire salesman and stock market plunger, Gates was a stout, raffish-looking character, with a derby always tipped back on his head and a big cigar stuck in the corner of his mouth. He used to bet on the speed of raindrops running down a train window and won his nickname from an enormous wager he once made on an English thoroughbred. Not content with an American steel trust, Gates wanted to include German manufacturers and attempt a global cartel.
这场晚宴发生在美国钢铁公司首次讨论的一周前，无疑让皮尔庞特确信，麦金莱政府将对托拉斯持放任态度。钢铁托拉斯的起源至今仍有争议。更具戏剧性的版本将创意归功于被称为"赌百万"的钢铁大亨约翰·W·盖茨，据说他是在华尔道夫-阿斯托里亚酒店（当时位于第五大道与三十四街交汇处）打台球时灵光一现。这位曾贩卖铁丝网、在股市豪赌的人物，身材魁梧，气质不羁，总是歪戴圆顶礼帽，嘴角叼着粗雪茄。他连雨滴在火车玻璃窗上滑落的速度都要赌，而他的绰号则源于一次对英国纯血马的天价赌局。盖茨并不满足于建立美国钢铁托拉斯，他还想将德国制造商纳入麾下，试图打造一个全球卡特尔。

The more sober versions of U.S. Steel trace the trust to a looming collision between Andrew Carnegie’s steel company and two of Pier-pont’s steel creations, Federal Steel and National Tube. As the top manufacturer of crude steel, Carnegie decided in July 1900 to branch out into finished products, such as pipe and wire. As head of the second largest steel group, Pierpont feared a replication of the railroad chaos, with overbuilding and price wars. He growled that Carnegie would “demoralize” the entire industry through competition. Bracing for a grim battle, he had his makers of finished products prepare to meet Carnegie head-on in crude steel.
更冷静的美国钢铁公司版本将信任追溯到安德鲁·卡内基的钢铁公司与皮尔庞特两家钢铁企业——联邦钢铁公司和国家钢管公司——之间即将爆发的冲突。作为粗钢的龙头制造商，卡内基于 1900 年 7 月决定进军管道和线材等成品领域。身为第二大钢铁集团的掌舵人，皮尔庞特担心重蹈铁路行业的覆辙，出现过度建设和价格战。他抱怨道，卡内基的竞争会“搞垮”整个行业。为了一场恶战做准备，他让旗下的成品制造商做好准备，在粗钢领域与卡内基正面交锋。

On December 12, 1900, a week after he was feted by Teddy Roosevelt, Pierpont attended a famous dinner held for Charles M. Schwab at the University Club in Manhattan. A handsome young man with a long, smooth face, dark hair, and clear brow, Schwab was a faithful lieutenant of Andrew Carnegie’s. Morgan sat at Schwab’s right and stared at his plate as the young man delivered his after-dinner address. A mellifluous orator and self-dramatizing individual, he evoked for Morgan and the eighty other financiers present a vision of a steel trust which would handle all phases of the business, from mining ore to marketing steel products; the Carnegie and Morgan steel enterprises would be the trust’s obvious nucleus. The steel trust was to be a superior sort of conspiracy. Through economies of scale, it would attempt to lower prices and compete in burgeoning world markets. It was a form of national industrial policy, albeit conducted by businessmen for private gain.
1900 年 12 月 12 日，在受到西奥多·罗斯福款待一周后，皮尔庞特出席了曼哈顿大学俱乐部为查尔斯·M·施瓦布举办的著名晚宴。施瓦布面容清俊、五官修长、黑发浓密、额角分明，是安德鲁·卡内基的忠实副手。摩根坐在施瓦布右侧，当这位年轻人发表餐后演说时，他只是低头盯着自己的餐盘。施瓦布口若悬河、善于自我表现，他向摩根和在座的八十多位金融家描绘了一个钢铁托拉斯的愿景——它将掌控从矿石开采到钢材销售的全产业链。卡内基与摩根的钢铁企业自然构成这个托拉斯的绝对核心。这个钢铁托拉斯将是一种更高明的垄断形式：通过规模经济压低价格，在蓬勃发展的全球市场展开竞争。这实质上是国家产业政策的民间实践，只不过由商人主导，以谋取私利为目的。

After the dinner, Morgan, intrigued, conferred with Schwab for half an hour. As Morgan partner Robert Bacon later said, “It was apparent that \[Morgan\] had seen a new light.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216683) It has never been clear whether Schwab acted at Carnegie’s behest or whether he planned to recruit Pierpont first, then take the proposal to Carnegie. In any event, within three weeks, Morgan, Bacon, Gates, and Schwab worked out a proposal in an all-night session at Morgan’s “black library.” The proposed trust would control more than half the steel business. Besides Carnegie Steel and Morgan’s Federal Steel, it would include American Tin Plate, American Steel Hoop, American Sheet Steel, American Bridge, American Steel and Wire, National Tube, National Steel, Shelby Steel Tube, and Lake Superior Consolidated Mines.
晚餐过后，摩根兴致浓厚，与施瓦布商议了半小时。正如摩根合伙人罗伯特·培根后来所说：“显然，（摩根）看到了新的曙光。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216683) 目前尚不清楚施瓦布是受卡内基指使，还是打算先拉拢皮尔庞特，再把方案交给卡内基。无论如何，三周内，摩根、培根、盖茨和施瓦布在摩根“黑色藏书室”里通宵达旦，共同拟定了一份方案。拟议的托拉斯将控制超过一半的钢铁业务。除卡内基钢铁和摩根的联邦钢铁外，它还将囊括美国马口铁公司、美国钢箍公司、美国钢板公司、美国桥梁公司、美国钢铁与线材公司、国家钢管公司、国家钢铁公司、谢尔比钢管公司以及苏必利尔湖联合矿业公司。

In forging U.S. Steel, Pierpont had to deal with two industrialists who represented very different aspects of American business—Andrew Carnegie and John D. Rockefeller. Both were hard-bitten individualists, scornful of bankers, who preferred to finance their operations from retained earnings. Rockefeller entered the deal through his ownership of ore mines and shipping companies on Lake Superior. Pierpont considered both men too crude for his stuffily refined tastes; they saw him as pompous and overbearing. The prudish Carnegie also disapproved of Pierpont’s adulterous escapades. “Carnegie frowned on anything savoring of the flesh and the devil,” Schwab said.[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216855)
在锻造美国钢铁公司的过程中，皮尔庞特不得不与两位代表着美国商业截然不同面貌的实业家打交道——安德鲁·卡内基和约翰·D·洛克菲勒。两人都是顽固的个人主义者，对银行家嗤之以鼻，更倾向于用留存收益来资助自己的运营。洛克菲勒凭借其对苏必利尔湖铁矿和航运公司的所有权参与了这笔交易。皮尔庞特认为这两人过于粗俗，不合他那种拘谨高雅的品味；而他们则视他为自命不凡、盛气凌人。保守的卡内基还看不惯皮尔庞特的风流韵事。“卡内基对任何带有肉欲和魔鬼气息的东西都皱眉头，”施瓦布说。 [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2216855)

After the meeting in the “black library,” Schwab sounded out Carnegie on his willingness to sell his steel company to the trust. After a game of golf at Saint Andrews Golf Club in Westchester, Carnegie ruminated, then penciled his selling price, \$480 million, on a scrap of paper. He wanted payment in bonds, not watered stock. When Schwab delivered the slip of paper to Morgan, the banker stared at it and said promptly, “I accept this price.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217010) In the hurly-burly, Pierpont didn’t formalize the deal with a signature and weeks later had to send a lawyer uptown with a contract. Despite his veneration of Junius Morgan, Carnegie enjoyed petty jousting with Pierpont. When Pierpont invited him to 23 Wall Street, Carnegie insisted that Morgan come to his own Fifty-first Street office instead. After a cool fifteen-minute chat, Morgan said in parting, “Mr. Carnegie, I want to congratulate you on being the richest man in the world.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217145)
在“黑图书馆”会谈后，施瓦布试探了卡内基是否愿意将他的钢铁公司出售给托拉斯。两人在西切斯特的圣安德鲁斯高尔夫俱乐部打了一场球后，卡内基沉思片刻，在一张纸条上用铅笔写下了他的报价——4.8 亿美元。他要求以债券支付，不要掺水股。当施瓦布把纸条交给摩根时，这位银行家凝视着纸条，立刻说道：“我接受这个价格。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217010) 在一片忙乱中，皮尔庞特并没有用签字来正式敲定这笔交易，几周后不得不派一名律师带着合同前往上城区。尽管卡内基对朱尼厄斯·摩根心怀敬意，但他却喜欢与皮尔庞特斗斗嘴。当皮尔庞特邀请他去华尔街 23 号时，卡内基坚持要摩根到自己位于第五十一街的办公室来。在冷淡地聊了十五分钟后，摩根临别时说：“卡内基先生，我要祝贺你成为世界上最富有的人。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217145)

Thin-skinned and vindictive, Carnegie gloated over the deal: “Pierpont feels that he can do anything because he has always got the best of the Jews in Wall Street. . . . It takes a Yankee to beat a Jew, and it takes a Scot to beat a Yankee.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217312) Carnegie celebrated too quickly. He later admitted to Morgan that he had sold out too cheap, by \$100 million. Not about to spare the industrialist’s feelings, Morgan replied, “Very likely, Andrew.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217480)
脸皮薄且记仇的卡内基为这笔交易沾沾自喜：“皮尔庞特觉得他无所不能，因为他在华尔街总是能胜过那些犹太人……要一个美国佬才能打败犹太人，而要一个苏格兰人才能打败美国佬。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217312) 卡内基得意得太早了。他后来向摩根承认自己卖得太便宜，少赚了一亿美元。摩根毫不顾忌这位实业家的感受，回道：“很有可能，安德鲁。” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217480)

In trying to coax recalcitrant companies into the steel trust, Pierpont showed his ringmaster’s flair for cracking the whip. He was irate with those who tried to extract undue advantage. During negotiations at 23 Wall, one major holdout was Bet-a-Million Gates and his American Steel and Wire. To break a deadlock, Pierpont materialized like the wrath of God and thumped a desk. “Gentlemen, I am going to leave this building in 10 minutes. If by that time you have not accepted our offer, the matter will be closed. We will build our own wire plant.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217637) His bluff called, Gates capitulated and sold out. Pierpont then went home, boyishly elated.
在试图说服那些不配合的公司加入钢铁托拉斯时，皮尔庞特展现了他挥舞鞭子的马戏团主风范。他对那些试图谋取不当利益的人怒不可遏。在华尔街 23 号的谈判中，一个主要的顽固分子是“赌一百万”盖茨和他的美国钢铁与线材公司。为了打破僵局，皮尔庞特如同天罚降临般出现，猛拍桌子。“先生们，我将在 10 分钟内离开这栋楼。如果到那时你们还不接受我们的报价，这件事就到此为止。我们将自己建线材厂。” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217637) 他的虚张声势被揭穿，盖茨投降了，卖掉了公司。随后皮尔庞特回到家，像孩子般兴高采烈。

The House of Morgan generally didn’t sponsor new companies and abhorred stock speculation. Junius Morgan had long ago advised his son, “I would recommend your forming a resolution never to buy any stock on speculation.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217800) So Pierpont’s promotion of U.S. Steel in early 1901 lent “old money” cachet to the rage for trusts. The year 1901 was not unlike 1929 or 1987: the stock market was on everybody’s lips. Daily share volume tripled. Wall Street seers babbled of a new age, and news-papers recounted tales of hotel waiters, business clerks, doormen, and dressmakers who made fortunes on Wall Street.[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217954)
摩根家族通常不屑于扶持新公司，对股票投机更是深恶痛绝。老朱尼厄斯·摩根早年就告诫过儿子：“我建议你立下规矩，永远不要投机买股票。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217800) 因此，当 1901 年初皮尔庞特力推美国钢铁公司上市时，这股托拉斯热潮便因此蒙上了一层“老钱”的光环。1901 年与 1929 年或 1987 年何其相似：股市成了人人挂在嘴边的话题。每日成交量翻了三倍。华尔街的先知们喋喋不休地谈论着一个新时代，报纸上则充斥着酒店侍者、公司职员、门卫和裁缝在华尔街发家致富的故事。 [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2217954)

U.S. Steel stoked the bonfire of speculation. At a time when million-dollar issues were considered large, the new corporation was capitalized at a whopping \$1.4 billion (\$23 billion in 1989 dollars)—the first billion-dollar corporation in history. At the time, all U.S. manufacturing combined had only \$9 billion in capitalization. To manage the flood of bonds and stock that financed the deal, Pierpont mustered a monster syndicate of three hundred underwriters. He appointed ace stock manipulator James R. Keene—a sharp-faced man with a pointed beard, known as the Silver Fox of Wall Street—to make a market in the shares. By simultaneously buying and selling shares, Keene created steadily rising prices and the illusion of tremendous volume. Despite predictions that so much stock would saturate the market, the issue’s success confirmed the boast of Morgan partner George W. Perkins that a Morgan issue “from the desert of Sahara” would find buyers.[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218110) For its services, the syndicate took in \$57.5 million in stock (nearly \$1 billion in 1989 dollars). The U.S. Steel promotion made explicit the marriage of finance and industry that marked the Baronial Age; when four Morgan partners joined the new trust’s board, the marriage was consummated.
美国钢铁公司点燃了投机之火。在当时，百万美元的融资规模已属罕见，而这家新公司的资本竟高达惊人的 14 亿美元（按 1989 年币值计算约合 230 亿美元）——成为历史上首家资本突破十亿美元的企业。而当时全美制造业的资本总和也不过 90 亿美元。为了处理这笔交易所需的巨额债券和股票，皮尔庞特组建了一个由 300 家承销商组成的庞大银团。他任命了股票操纵高手詹姆斯·R·基恩——这位尖脸蓄着山羊胡、人称"华尔街银狐"的人物——来为这些股票做市。基恩通过同时买卖股票的手法，营造出股价稳步攀升和交易量巨大的假象。尽管此前有人预测如此巨量的股票会令市场饱和，但此次发行的成功印证了摩根合伙人乔治·W·珀金斯那句豪言：摩根承销的股票"就算来自撒哈拉沙漠"，也能找到买家。 [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218110) 作为服务费，该财团收取了 5750 万美元的股票（按 1989 年美元价值计算接近 10 亿美元）。美国钢铁公司的推广明确表明了金融与工业的结合，这标志着巨头时代的特征；当四位摩根合伙人加入新信托的董事会时，这种结合便完成了。

For many observers, the sheer size of U.S. Steel seemed sinister and unnatural. Even the *Wall Street Journal* admitted to “uneasiness over the magnitude of the affair.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218275) Among others, Yale president Arthur Hadley, a noted economist, saw a new need for federal control of large corporations. Ray Stannard Baker, later Woodrow Wilson’s biographer, pointed out that the new corporation would have revenues and expenses exceeding the budgets of all but a few world governments.[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218428) Yet Wall Street was heedless of the critics and celebrated with a record volume of trading. In January 1901, the Big Board traded a record two million shares in one day; after the launching of U.S. Steel that spring, volume reached three million shares. Wall Street was so awash in shares that the Stock Exchange declared a special holiday just to catch up on paperwork.
在许多观察者看来，美国钢铁公司庞大的规模令人感到不安且不自然。就连《华尔街日报》也承认“对其规模的担忧”。 [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218275) 此外，耶鲁大学校长、著名经济学家阿瑟·哈德利认为，联邦政府有必要对大型企业实施新的监管。后来成为伍德罗·威尔逊传记作家的雷·斯坦纳德·贝克指出，这家新公司的收入和支出超过了除少数几个国家政府之外的所有政府预算。 [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218428) 然而，华尔街对批评之声充耳不闻，并以创纪录的交易量庆祝此事。1901 年 1 月，纽约证交所创下单日交易 200 万股的纪录；当年春天美国钢铁公司成立后，交易量达到了 300 万股。华尔街股票泛滥，以至于证券交易所专门放了一天假来处理积压的文书工作。

An unending controversy would surround U.S. Steel: was it Pier-pont’s greatest deal, as he believed, or a giant scam? The share flotation made multi-millionaires of dozens of steelmen, and the spectacle of so much sudden wealth appalled the public. In 1905, Charles Schwab, U.S. Steel’s first president, built a seventy-five-room mansion on Manhattan’s Riverside Drive, complete with a pipe organ, art gallery, bowling alley, private chapel, and sixty-foot swimming pool. Gaudy mansions went up all over Pittsburgh with the new steel money, symbolizing a new class of *nouveaux riches* industrialists.
关于美国钢铁公司的争议从未停歇：这究竟是皮尔庞特眼中最伟大的一笔交易，还是一场惊天骗局？股票上市让数十位钢铁大亨一夜暴富，如此巨量财富的突然涌现令公众瞠目结舌。1905 年，美国钢铁公司首任总裁查尔斯·施瓦布在曼哈顿河滨大道建起一栋 75 个房间的豪宅，内设管风琴、艺术画廊、保龄球馆、私人教堂及 60 英尺长的游泳池。随着钢铁新贵们财富的膨胀，匹兹堡各地纷纷矗立起浮夸的宅邸，象征着新兴工业暴发户阶层的崛起。

Later the U.S. Bureau of Corporations, a federal agency set up by Teddy Roosevelt, would value U.S. Steel at only half its \$1.4-billion selling price, suggesting that investors had purchased an enormous bag of hope, at least half of it hot air. From Vanderbilt, Morgan had learned the trick of basing value not on current assets but on projected earnings. U.S. Steel’s subsequent history provided evidence for both detractors and admirers. From an opening price of 38, its stock zoomed to 55, only to skid to less than 9 during the “rich man’s panic” of 1903. By January 1904, U.S. Steel couldn’t even cover its dividends. Yet it is fair to say that in time the enterprise expanded to the contours of Morgan’s vision, becoming America’s foremost steel company. It amply rewarded its investors—at least, the patient ones.
后来，由西奥多·罗斯福总统创立的联邦机构——美国企业管理局，对 U.S. Steel 的估值仅为它 14 亿美元售价的一半，这意味着投资者买下了一大袋希望，其中至少一半是空头支票。摩根从范德比尔特那里学到了一招：价值不基于现有资产，而基于预期收益。U.S. Steel 后来的历史，既为批评者提供了口实，也为崇拜者提供了佐证。从 38 美元的开盘价一路飙升至 55 美元，随后在 1903 年的“富人恐慌”中暴跌至不到 9 美元。到 1904 年 1 月，U.S. Steel 甚至无力支付股息。然而，平心而论，这家企业最终扩张到了摩根愿景中的规模，成为美国首屈一指的钢铁公司，并为它的投资者——至少是那些耐心的投资者，带来了丰厚的回报。

BEHIND the growing pomp of Pierpont Morgan lay an ever-present vulnerability. If tragedy, as Aristotle said, has the power to arouse fear and pity, then Pierpont wore a tragic mask. In 1903, Pierpont sat for two minutes as Edward Steichen snapped the famous photograph of him: from deep shadow and gripping the blade-like chair, Pierpont stares out, a tense crease between his brows, his collar stiff, his eyes pitiless points of lights, the gaze legendary in its terror. Steichen tried to make him turn, but Pierpont, self-conscious about his nose, stared straight ahead. The photographer snapped him bristling with anger. Pierpont hated the photo and tore up the first prints. Yet there was sadness as well as fire in the eyes—volcanic energy and despair. The photograph captured the man whole. When Pierpont later relented and offered to pay a stratospheric \$5,000 for the photo, the wounded Steichen took two years to deliver copies.
皮尔庞特·摩根日益显赫的排场背后，隐藏着一份挥之不去的不安。如果说悲剧——如亚里士多德所言——能唤起恐惧与怜悯，那么皮尔庞特戴着的，便是一副悲剧的面具。1903 年，爱德华·施泰肯为他拍下那张著名的照片，仅两分钟的静坐：皮尔庞特从深邃的阴影中斜靠在那把刀刃般锋利的椅子上，眉头紧锁，衣领僵挺，双目如两点无情的寒光，那眼神以骇人著称。施泰肯试图让他转头，但皮尔庞特因对自己的鼻子在意，始终直视前方。摄影师在他怒意勃发时按下了快门。皮尔庞特讨厌这张照片，当场撕毁了初版样片。然而，那双眼眸里不仅有怒火，还有悲戚——火山般的能量与绝望交织。这张照片捕捉到了他的全部。后来皮尔庞特松了口，愿意出五千美元的天价买下照片，而受伤的施泰肯则拖了两年才交付样片。

The blazing eyes were linked to the grotesque nose. As the years went by, the acne rosacea made Pierpont’s nose monstrous in size and hideous in shape. The nose was invariably touched up in official photographs, perhaps adding to the shock of those who saw him in person. Of his initial encounter with Wall Street’s Cyrano, art dealer Joseph Duveen wrote, “No nose in caricature ever assumed such gigantic proportions or presented such appalling excrescences. If I did not gasp, I might have changed color. Morgan noticed this, and his small, piercing eyes transfixed me with a malicious stare.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218600) Many anecdotes link Morgan’s nose with his short temper—an old story of the vanity of the mighty. He would furiously avenge taunts, and one writer said he never recovered from the phrase “a ruby-visaged magnate.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218776) When Bet-a-Million Gates dubbed him Livernose, the jest proved costly: Pierpont blackballed Gates from the Union League and New York Yacht clubs. About his nose, Pierpont could be more sensitive than he was about his trusts. After the newspapers of clubmate Joseph Pulitzer attacked his business dealings, Pierpont complained to the newspaperman not about the allegations, but about the prominence of his nose in the papers’ cartoons, which he thought very unfair.
那双炯炯有神的眼睛与那只奇形怪状的鼻子相得益彰。随着岁月流逝，酒糟鼻让皮尔庞特的鼻子变得尺寸骇人、形状可怖。官方照片中，这只鼻子总是被修饰过，或许正因如此，见过他本人才会愈发震惊。艺术品经销商约瑟夫·杜维恩在首次见到这位“华尔街的西拉诺”时写道：“漫画里从未有过如此巨大的鼻子，或是这般骇人的赘疣。就算我没有倒抽一口凉气，脸色也一定变了。摩根注意到了，他那双锐利的小眼睛恶狠狠地盯着我。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218600) 许多轶事都将摩根的鼻子与他暴躁的脾气联系起来——这是关于权贵虚荣心的老故事了。他对任何嘲弄都会愤怒报复，一位作家曾说他永远无法摆脱“红脸大亨”这个绰号带来的阴影。 [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218776) 当“百万赌徒”盖茨戏称他为“Livernose”时，这个玩笑代价高昂：皮尔庞特在联合会所和纽约游艇俱乐部中投票反对盖茨加入。 关于他的鼻子，皮尔庞特比对待自己的信托基金还要敏感。同为俱乐部成员的约瑟夫·普利策旗下的报纸攻击他的商业交易时，皮尔庞特向那位报人抱怨的不是那些指控，而是报纸漫画中对他鼻子的突出描绘——他认为这极不公平。

Everybody came to terms with the nose differently. Lady Victoria Sackville-West, probably Pierpont’s last mistress, recorded in her diary in 1912, “I have never met anyone so attractive. One forgets his nose entirely after a few minutes.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218947) Perhaps intimates did, but not rival businessmen. And children found it scarily hypnotic. When a later partner, Dwight Morrow, brought Pierpont to his home, his wife Betty—having warned the children not to mention the nose—asked the tycoon, “Do you like nose in your tea, Mr. Morgan?”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219127)
每个人面对这只鼻子的方式各不相同。维多利亚·萨克维尔-韦斯特夫人——大概是皮尔庞特的最后一位情妇——在 1912 年的日记中写道：“我从未遇见过如此有魅力的人。几分钟后，你就会完全忘记他的鼻子。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2218947) 或许密友能做到，但商界对手不会。而孩子们则觉得它有种令人发怵的催眠感。后来，合伙人德怀特·莫罗带皮尔庞特回家时，他的妻子贝蒂——事先警告过孩子们不许提鼻子——问这位大亨：“摩根先生，您的茶里要加鼻子吗？” [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219127)

Pierpont tried everything to cure it, including an electrical remedy recommended by England’s Queen Alexandra. But it persisted, like nature’s revenge, reminding him of his humanity. In philosophic moments, he converted it into a mark of pride. When the Russian minister of finance, Count Witte, suggested surgery, he replied, “Everybody knows my nose. It would be impossible for me to appear on the streets of New York without it.”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219287) Still more grandly, he said his nose “was part of the American business structure.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219458)
皮尔庞特试遍了各种方法，甚至包括英格兰亚历山德拉王后推荐的电疗。但这症状始终顽固不化，像大自然的报复，时刻提醒他终是凡人。在沉思的时刻，他反倒将其转化为骄傲的象征。当俄国财政大臣维特伯爵建议他做手术时，他答道：“人人都认得我的鼻子。没了它，我根本没法在纽约街头露面。” [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219287) 更夸张的是，他还说自己的鼻子“是美国商业结构的一部分。” [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219458)

It was probably the nose that made Pierpont eager to hire handsome young men, and he often sent pedigreed collie puppies as a sign of impending partnerships. Over time, the early reputation of Morgan partners as harried technicians caught in the grinding machinery of railroad reorganizations gave way to another equally pronounced tradition: the Morgan partner as elegant fashion plate, suave member of the Social Register catering to rich clients. “A homely man had no chance of being selected a Morgan partner,” wrote an early Pierpont biographer. The same could be said, with a few exceptions, of the bank under his son, Jack.[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219614)
很可能是因为那副鼻子，皮尔庞特总是急于雇佣英俊的年轻人，他经常送出血统纯正的柯基幼崽，作为即将建立合伙关系的信号。随着时间的推移，摩根合伙人早期那种在铁路重组机器的碾压下疲于奔命的技术员形象，逐渐让位于另一个同样显著的传统：摩根合伙人成为优雅的时尚标杆，是《社交名人录》中迎合富裕客户的温文尔雅成员。“相貌平平的人绝无可能被选为摩根合伙人，”皮尔庞特的一位早期传记作者这样写道。在他儿子杰克执掌下的银行，除了少数例外，情况亦是如此。

The prototype was Robert Bacon, taken on as partner in 1894 after J. Hood Wright died suddenly. As soon as Bacon was hired, his former boss, Major Henry Lee Higginson, warned him, “Don’t overwork like Coster just because you can and like to do it. He is wonderful—and unwise—to do so.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219748) Trim and athletic with a strong, wide face and debonair mustache, Bacon was called the Greek God on Wall Street. As a Harvard undergraduate (and classmate of Teddy Roosevelt’s), he boxed, ran the hundred-yard dash, captained the football team, was president of the Glee Club, and was number seven on the university crew team and Model Man of his class; his presence at the Corner of Broad and Wall inaugurated a new image for the Morgan partners. With Bacon in mind, a novelist wrote, “When the angels of God took unto themselves wives among the daughters of men, the result was the Morgan partners.”[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219905) Pierpont doted on Bacon and wanted him constantly by his side. It was said Morgan had “fallen in love” with Bacon and “rejoiced in his presence.”[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220081)
原型是罗伯特·培根，他在 1894 年 J·霍德·怀特突然去世后成为合伙人。培根刚一入职，他的前上司亨利·李·希金森少校就告诫他：“别因为自己有能力、也喜欢工作，就像科斯特那样过度操劳。他那样做确实了不起——但也并不明智。” [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219748) 培根身材匀称、体格强健，脸庞宽阔而轮廓分明，胡须潇洒不羁，被称为华尔街上的希腊男神。在哈佛读本科时（与西奥多·罗斯福同级），他练拳击、跑百米、担任橄榄球队队长、主持合唱团、是大学赛艇队的七号桨手，还被评为班级模范男生；他出现在百老汇与华尔街的转角，为摩根合伙人开创了全新形象。一位小说家以培根为原型写道：“当上帝的天使与人间女子结合时，生下的就是摩根合伙人。” [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2219905) 皮尔庞特对培根宠爱有加，总想让他时刻陪伴左右。 据说摩根“爱上了”培根，并“对他的存在感到欣喜”。 [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220081)

Bacon’s elevation in the bank signaled a problem with the Morgan empire: Bacon, a charming lightweight, reflected Pierpont’s fear of hiring commanding figures. That Bacon was second in command spoke poorly of his boss’s managerial judgment. Art critic Roger Fry saw Morgan as a vain, insecure despot who “likes to be in a position of being surrounded by people he has in his power to make and unmake.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220238) The most talented early partners—the apostles of Pierpontifex Maximus, or Jupiter’s Ganymedes, as they were called—might have been legal and financial wizards, but they were not leaders. Since they were few in number—New York had six partners in the 1890s; the Philadelphia office, four—they had to pull enormous weight.
培根在银行的晋升暴露了摩根帝国的一个问题：培根虽是迷人的小角色，却反映出皮尔庞特对聘用强势人物心存忌惮。培根位居二把手，反倒说明他老板的管理眼光不怎么样。艺术评论家罗杰·弗莱认为摩根是个虚荣、缺乏安全感的独裁者，“喜欢被自己可以随意提拔或推翻的人包围”。 [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220238) 早期最出色的合伙人——他们被称为“至高无上的皮尔庞特”的门徒，或朱庇特的伽倪墨得斯——或许都是法律和金融奇才，却并非领导者。由于人数稀少——纽约在 1890 年代有六位合伙人，费城办公室只有四位——他们必须承担极其沉重的职责。

The danger of Pierpont’s despotism was glaringly exposed during the so-called Northern Pacific corner of 1901, perhaps the most controversial takeover fight in American history. After U.S. Steel was successfully launched, Pierpont had sailed to France, where he entertained a dark French countess on the Riviera, leaving the firm in Bacon’s hands. Since Coster’s death the year before, Bacon knew he was in over his head and reeled under the responsibility. “My life is simply engrossed in this maelstrom,” he told his wife.[53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220394) He was soon blindsided by the most powerful Wall Street combination outside that of the Morgan firms—an amalgam of Edward H. Harriman, William Rockefeller, the National City Bank, and Kuhn, Loeb. It was a ganging up of Pierpont’s most determined enemies.
1901 年所谓的“北太平洋铁路收购案”中，皮尔庞特的专制危险暴露无遗，这或许是美国历史上最具争议的收购战。在成功创建美国钢铁公司后，皮尔庞特乘船前往法国，在里维埃拉招待了一位神秘的法国的伯爵夫人，将公司交由培根打理。自前年科斯特去世后，培根深知自己力不从心，被责任压得几乎晕头转向。“我的生活完全被这场漩涡吞噬了，”他在给妻子的信中写道。 [53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220394) 他很快就被摩根财团之外最强有力的华尔街联盟——由爱德华·H·哈里曼、威廉·洛克菲勒、国民城市银行以及库恩-洛布公司组成的联合体——打了个措手不及。这是皮尔庞特最顽固的对手们的一次联手。

A battle had been brewing since 1895, when Pierpont decided not to reorganize the bankrupt Union Pacific, which he scoffed at as “two streaks of iron rust across the plains.”[54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220551) His willingness to write off America’s southwestern states provided an opening for outsiders. Edward Harriman took up the Union Pacific and merged it with the Southern Pacific. He and his bankers, the Jewish house of Kuhn, Loeb, dominated the southwestern roads as invincibly as Morgan did those of the East and the Northwest. The Northern Pacific corner was the thunderous, head-on crash of the railroad systems under the personal dominion of Harriman and Morgan.
一场酝酿已久的战役自 1895 年便已悄然展开，彼时皮尔庞特决定不重组濒临破产的联合太平洋铁路公司，对其嗤之以鼻，称之为“两条横贯平原的铁锈带”。他甘愿放弃美国西南部各州的做法，为外来者打开了可乘之机。爱德华·哈里曼接手联合太平洋，并将其与南太平洋铁路合并。他与他的银行家——犹太家族库恩·勒布公司——如同摩根本人主宰东部和西北部铁路那般，牢牢掌控了西南部的铁路网络。而北太平洋铁路的角力，则是哈里曼与摩根各自麾下的铁路系统迎头相撞、天雷地火般的正面交锋。

Harriman was a very different type from Pierpont. He was short and bandy-legged, had shifty eyes, and wore wire-rimmed spectacles, an unkempt mustache, and a peevish expression. Like many on Wall Street, he was the son of a poor clergyman and an unabashed social climber. A crack shot, he had a taste for blood sport and played tough on the stock exchange as well. Where Pierpont preferred back-room deals sealed with a handshake, Harriman was a market operator—more a raider than a deal maker. Where Pierpont usually served as proxy for bondholders, Harriman preferred to buy common stock and exert direct control. Finally, where Morgan was the establishment figure, Harriman was an embittered outsider who showed the damage that could be done by a bright man barred from Pierpont’s club. If bankers proved they could dominate companies through voting trusts and other devices, Harriman showed that the stock raider could dominate both the bankers and their companies.
哈里曼与皮尔庞特截然不同。他身材矮小，双腿弯曲，眼神游移不定，戴着金属丝边框眼镜，胡须蓬乱，一脸不耐烦的神情。和华尔街许多人一样，他是穷牧师之子，也是毫不掩饰的攀附权贵者。他枪法精准，嗜好血腥运动，在股票交易所也作风强硬。如果说皮尔庞特偏爱幕后交易、握手成交，那么哈里曼则是市场操盘手——更像是掠夺者而非交易撮合者。如果说皮尔庞特通常代表债券持有人行事，哈里曼则更倾向于购买普通股并施加直接控制。最后，如果说摩根是体制内的大佬，哈里曼则是心怀怨恨的局外人，他展示了被拒于皮尔庞特俱乐部之外的聪明人所能造成的破坏。如果说银行家们通过表决信托和其他手段证明了他们能掌控公司，那么哈里曼则表明，股市掠夺者既能掌控银行家，也能掌控他们的公司。

Harriman’s banker was the German-born Jacob Schiff, the unbending, white-bearded patriarch of Kuhn, Loeb who was second only to Pierpont as a financial railroad overlord. Schiff was such a grandee that one private Pullman car was seldom enough for him when he traveled.[55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220727) He was stiff and formal and as haughty as Pierpont Morgan himself.
哈里曼的银行家是德裔的雅各布·希夫，这位库恩-勒布公司里固执、白须飘飘的掌门人，在铁路金融大亨中仅次于皮尔庞特。希夫地位显赫得令人咋舌，出行时一节私人普尔曼车厢往往还不够他摆排场。他为人刻板拘礼，趾高气扬的程度丝毫不输皮尔庞特·摩根本人。

Like the London merchant bankers, the early Jewish bankers on Wall Street had started out as dry-goods merchants: the Lehmans began as Alabama cotton brokers; Goldman, as the owner of a Pennsylvania clothing store, Kuhn and Loeb, as Cincinnati clothiers; and Lazard, in a New Orleans dry-goods business. These firms were dynastic, with only blood or marriage securing partnerships. They worked in the interstices left by the big Christian houses and dealt more directly in markets than the Morgans did. Markets were considered coarse by fancy gentile bankers. So Goldman, Sachs specialized in commercial paper, Lehman in commodity trading. Around 1900, they began underwriting shares for companies that were spurned by the gentile firms as too lowly—retail stores and textile manufacturers, for instance. Among them was Sears, Roebuck, introduced by Goldman, Sachs and Lehman Brothers in 1906. Of such relatively small issues, the gentile firms would sniff, “Let the Jews have that one”—snobbery for which they paid dearly in the twentieth century.[56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220900)
与伦敦的商人银行家类似，早期华尔街的犹太银行家也是从纺织品商人起家的：雷曼兄弟最初在阿拉巴马做棉花经纪；高盛老板曾经营宾夕法尼亚的一家服装店；库恩洛布公司发迹于辛辛那提的布商生意；拉扎德则在新奥尔良从事纺织品贸易。这些家族式企业靠血缘或姻亲维系合伙关系。他们在基督教大银行的夹缝中求生存，相比摩根家族更直接地涉足市场交易。当时上流社会的新教徒银行家视市场为粗鄙之地，因此高盛专攻商业票据，雷曼专注大宗商品交易。1900 年前后，他们开始为那些被新教徒公司瞧不上、觉得太低贱的企业承销股票——比如零售商店和纺织厂。1906 年，高盛与雷曼兄弟联手将西尔斯罗巴克公司推向了资本市场。 对于这类相对次要的问题，非犹太人的公司总会嗤之以鼻：“让犹太佬捡这个便宜吧”——这种傲慢的势利眼让它们后来在 20 世纪付出了惨痛代价。 [56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2220900)

Schiff didn’t want to settle for the scraps left to the Jews. Alone among the Jewish bankers he had the gumption to play the grand game and contest Morgan in government issues and railroad financing. He fun-neled German and French money into American shares no less expertly than Pierpont did with British money. Much of Kuhn, Loeb’s exceptional power derived from the fact that it voted stock shares in American railroads as proxy for legions of German investors.
希夫不甘心只捡犹太人剩下的残羹冷炙。在犹太银行家中，他是唯一有胆量玩这场大游戏、在政府债券和铁路融资上与摩根一较高下的人。他将德国和法国的资金引入美国股票，其手法和皮尔庞特运用英国资金一样娴熟。库恩-洛布公司之所以拥有如此强大的实力，很大程度上源于它作为大批德国投资者的代理人，在美国铁路公司中行使股票投票权。

Morgan referred to Schiff dismissively as “that foreigner.”[57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221077) Schiff, in turn, professed to admire Morgan, but his compliments sometimes had a slightly hollow, envious ring. After Pierpont’s heroic role in the 1907 panic, Schiff said, “Probably no one could have got the banks to act together . . . as he did, in his autocratic way.”[58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221243)
摩根轻蔑地称希夫为“那个外国人”。 [57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221077) 而希夫则自称敬佩摩根，但他的恭维有时却略带空洞和嫉妒的意味。在皮尔庞特于 1907 年恐慌中扮演英勇角色后，希夫说：“恐怕没人能像他那样，用他那独裁的方式，让银行们团结一致……” [58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221243)

Political, ethnic, and religious differences among bankers permeated Wall Street in the early 1900s. The Yankee-Jewish banking split was the most important fault line in American high finance. And since the two groups would come to dominate American investment banking, their feuds form a recurring theme in the Morgan banking saga. Pierpont’s anti-Semitism was well known. Said an early biographer: “He had a deep-seated anti-Semitic prejudice and on more than one occasion needlessly antagonized great Jewish banking firms.”[59](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221404) His dislike of Jews may have been sharpened by dealings with the Rothschilds. The Jewish tycoon Joseph Seligman noted Pierpont’s “freeze-and-thaw attitude” toward him, which he attributed to his discomfort with Jews.[60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221573) During thaws, the two men collaborated on issues, and when Seligman was barred from a fashionable Saratoga hotel, the Morgan bank signed an advertisement protesting the exclusion. In addition, Kuhn, Loeb, in particular, managed many syndicates with the Morgans. The strain of anti-Semitism running through the Morgan story is fascinating precisely because it had to be so carefully suppressed.
20 世纪初，华尔街银行家之间的政治、民族和宗教分歧渗透其中。扬基与犹太银行家的分裂是美国高端金融业中最重要的一条断层线。由于这两个群体后来主导了美国投资银行业，他们的恩怨也成为了摩根银行传奇中反复出现的主题。皮尔庞特的反犹主义众所周知。一位早期传记作者曾言：“他怀有根深蒂固的反犹偏见，并且不止一次不必要地激怒了大犹太银行机构。” [59](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221404) 他或许因为与罗斯柴尔德家族的交往而加深了对犹太人的厌恶。犹太巨富约瑟夫·塞利格曼注意到皮尔庞特对他“忽冷忽热的态度”，并将其归因于皮尔庞特对犹太人的不适感。 [60](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221573) 在关系缓和时，两人会合作处理事务；当塞利格曼被禁止入住萨拉托加的一家高级酒店时，摩根银行也签署了一份广告抗议这一排斥行为。 此外，库恩-洛布公司尤其与摩根家族共同管理了许多银团。而贯穿摩根家族故事的那股反犹暗流之所以耐人寻味，恰恰就在于它必须被小心翼翼地压制。

The group making common cause with Harriman and Schiff against Morgan in 1901 was the Rockefellers. In 1881, John D. Rockefeller had financed the Standard Oil trust from its huge cash reserves, staying free of Wall Street. As the 1880s progressed, Standard Oil was generating so much cash that the Rockefellers looked about for a financial repository. They chose the National City Bank—the forerunner of today’s Citibank—and pumped in so much money that by 1893 it ranked as New York’s largest bank. It was a significant development: at a time when bankers tightened their grip on industry, here was an industrial empire fastening its grip on banking. National City became known as the oil bank, much as J. P. Morgan and Company would be called the steel bank. National City Bank’s president, James Stillman, with his coldly alert and penetrating eyes, would oppose Pierpont in the Northern Pacific battle but become a close ally later on. Two of Stillman’s daughters married two of William Rockefeller’s sons, sealing the union of the Rockefellers with the National City Bank.
1901 年，与哈里曼和希夫联手对抗摩根的集团，正是洛克菲勒家族。1881 年，约翰·D·洛克菲勒用标准石油信托巨额的现金储备为其提供资金，始终游离在华尔街之外。随着 19 世纪 80 年代的发展，标准石油产生的现金多到洛克菲勒家族不得不寻找一个金融金库。他们选择了国民城市银行——如今花旗银行的前身——并注入大量资金，到 1893 年它已成为纽约最大的银行。这是一个重大转折：当银行家们加紧控制工业时，一个工业帝国反而牢牢抓住了银行。国民城市银行后来被称为“石油银行”，正如后来的 J.P.摩根公司被称为“钢铁银行”。国民城市银行的总裁詹姆斯·斯蒂尔曼，目光冷峻而犀利，曾在北太平洋铁路争夺战中与皮尔庞特为敌，但后来却成为其亲密盟友。 斯蒂尔曼的两个女儿嫁给了威廉·洛克菲勒的两个儿子，巩固了洛克菲勒家族与国民城市银行的联合。

The Northern Pacific quarrel began when northwestern railroad magnate James J. Hill decided to buy a midwestern road called the Chicago, Burlington, and Quincy. Hill was a garrulous man with a bushy, white untamed beard, shoulder-length hair, and a troll’s face. With Morgan’s help, he had consolidated the Great Northern and Northern Pacific into a railroad system that dominated transport in the northwestern United States. The purchase of the CB&Q, Harriman feared, would provide Hill with an entree into Chicago and a possible connection for a transatlantic line; it might even link up with Morgan’s New York Central.
北方太平洋铁路公司的争端始于西北铁路大亨詹姆斯·J·希尔决定收购一家名为芝加哥、伯灵顿和昆西的中西部铁路公司。希尔是个话匣子，留着浓密杂乱的白胡子，头发披肩，一张像地精似的脸庞。在摩根的帮助下，他已将大北方铁路和北方太平洋铁路整合成一个主导美国西北部运输的铁路系统。哈里曼担心，收购 CB&Q 将为希尔打开进入芝加哥的大门，并可能连接一条跨大西洋线路，甚至可能与摩根的纽约中央铁路接轨。

Schiff and Harriman pleaded with Hill and Morgan for a stake in the road but were rebuffed. Harriman said implacably, “Very well, it is a hostile act and you must take the consequences.”[61](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221747) In a manner that anticipated mergers of the 1980s, Schiff and Harriman decided to swallow the railroad that had swallowed the CB&Q—the Northern Pacific. The Northern Pacific ran west from Wisconsin through North Dakota and Montana, terminating in Seattle, Washington. Schiff, torn between dreams of glory and dread of Morgan, passed a sleepless night before acceding to Harriman’s plan. It was an extraordinary act *lèse-majesté*, because the House of Morgan had a substantial stake in the Northern Pacific and wouldn’t tolerate such an attack.
希夫和哈里曼恳求希尔和摩根让出一部分铁路股份，但遭到拒绝。哈里曼毫不留情地说："很好，这是敌对行为，你们必须承担后果。" [61](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221747) 以一种预示了 20 世纪 80 年代并购浪潮的方式，希夫和哈里曼决定吞并那条已经吞并了 CB&Q 的铁路——北太平洋铁路。北太平洋铁路从威斯康星州向西延伸，穿过北达科他州和蒙大拿州，终点是华盛顿州的西雅图。希夫在荣耀梦想与对摩根的恐惧之间挣扎，辗转难眠一整夜，最终同意了哈里曼的计划。这是一次极其僭越的举动，因为摩根财团在北太平洋铁路持有大量股份，绝不会容忍这样的攻击。

The raiders went into the market secretly, buying up \$78 million in Northern Pacific shares—at the time, the largest such market operation in history. As share prices rose in April 1901, Pierpont credited it to the bullish tone of stocks set by the launching of U.S. Steel. Schiff cunningly circulated rumors that the rise reflected Northern Pacific’s enhanced value after the CB&Q purchase. When a block of shares came into Robert Bacon’s hands, he gladly sold. Even the railroad’s board sold. It was a masterly con job by Harriman’s forces, camouflaged by the ebullient financial markets that followed McKinley’s reelection. The newspapers noted that many young men-about-town with newfound stock market fortunes were now calling themselves financiers. At the same time, many investors, apprehensive about the giddy market activity, predicted a general panic.
这批投机者秘密潜入市场，买进了价值 7800 万美元的北太平洋铁路公司股票——这在当时是史上规模最大的市场操作。1901 年 4 月股价上涨时，皮尔庞特将其归因于美国钢铁公司上市所带动的牛市氛围。希夫狡猾地散布谣言，声称股价上涨反映的是北太平洋铁路公司收购 CB&Q 后价值提升。当一批股票落到罗伯特·培根手中时，他欣然抛售。就连该铁路公司的董事会也抛售了股票。这是哈里曼阵营精心策划的一场骗局，巧妙地掩盖在麦金利连任后金融市场的狂热浪潮之中。报纸注意到，许多在股市中暴富的城中阔少开始自封为金融家。与此同时，许多投资者对令人目眩的市场活动感到忧虑，预言将出现全面恐慌。

Then, in May, Northern Pacific stock shot up so fast it seemed to levitate. Hill, who had been beguiled by Bacon’s beauty, was troubled by bad dreams. Asleep in his private railroad car in Seattle, he was visited by “a dark-complected angel” who warned of trouble in New York. Hill raced clear across America to Wall Street. On Saturday, May 4, he alerted Bacon to what he saw as a catastrophe in the making. They cabled Pierpont, now in Aix-les-Bains, and awaited instructions.
到了五月，北太平洋铁路公司的股票一路飙升，简直像要飞起来似的。希尔早就被培根的美貌迷得神魂颠倒，眼下更是噩梦缠身。他在西雅图的私人列车里酣睡时，一个"肤色黝黑的天使"闯进梦中，警告他纽约那边要出乱子。希尔当即横跨美国，直奔华尔街。5 月 4 日星期六那天，他提醒培根，一场灾难正在酝酿。两人给正在艾克斯莱班的皮尔庞特发了电报，等候指示。

At this point, the Harriman-Schiff forces were 40,000 shares short of majority control of the Northern Pacific. That Saturday morning, Harriman ordered Kuhn, Loeb to buy the needed stock, but Jacob Schiff was attending services at Temple Emanu-El, and the order never got executed. The lapse was fateful, for the next day Pierpont told Bacon to purchase 150,000 shares at any price. That Monday morning, Morgan brokers fanned out across the Exchange floor, and insane trading in Northern Pacific ensued.
彼时，哈里曼和希夫一方距离掌控北太平洋铁路公司多数股权还差四万股。那个星期六上午，哈里曼下令让库恩-勒布公司买进所需股票，但雅各布·希夫正在以马内利会堂参加礼拜，命令始终未能执行。这一耽搁，后果致命——第二天，皮尔庞特就指示培根不惜一切代价买下十五万股。周一早上，摩根公司的经纪人纷纷涌向交易所大厅，北太平洋铁路的股票随即陷入疯狂交易。

The jumps in the stock were staggering. On Tuesday, May 7, the stock closed at over 143—a gain of 70 points in three days. The next day, it shot up to 200. This was a corner, a bloody trap for speculators. Speculators kept “shorting” the stock—that is, selling borrowed shares in the belief that the bubble would pop and enable them to buy back the shares at a cheaper price. Instead, the Northern Pacific geyser kept rising, forcing them to liquidate shares of other companies to pay for their borrowed Northern Pacific shares. Hence, the problem was generalized to the entire stock market.
这只股票的涨幅令人瞠目结舌。5 月 7 日周二收盘价已突破 143 点——短短三天内飙升了 70 点。次日更是一路猛涨至 200 点。这是一场逼空大战，是投机者血淋淋的陷阱。投机客们不断“做空”这只股票——即借入股票卖出，妄图泡沫破裂后低价买回获利。然而北太平洋铁路公司的股价却如喷泉般持续暴涨，逼得他们不得不出清其他公司股票来偿还借入的北太平洋股票。于是这场危机迅速蔓延至整个股市。

By Wednesday, almost every stock on the Exchange was crashing, with money sucked from the rest of the list to feed the spectacularly surging Northern Pacific. Then came Thursday, May 9, and the biggest market crash in a century. Northern Pacific zoomed up as much as 200 or 300 points per trade, finally hitting 1,000. Then it dropped 400 points on a single trade. The Exchange was a scene of wild pandemonium as speculators found it impossible to locate certificates to cover short sales. The *New York Times* reported: “Brokers acted like insane men. . . . Big men lightly threw little men aside, and the little men, fairly crying with indignation, jumped anew into the fray, using hands and arms, elbows, feet—anything to gain their point. . . . To the spectators in the distant gallery of the Produce Exchange it was something incomprehensible, almost demonic—this struggle, this Babel of voices, these wild-eyed excited brokers, selling and buying, buying and selling.”[62](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2221904)
到了周三，交易所里几乎所有股票都在崩跌，资金从其他股票中抽离，涌向涨势惊人的北太平洋铁路股。接着是 5 月 9 日周四，一个世纪以来最大的股市崩盘来临。北太平洋铁路股每笔交易狂飙 200 到 300 点，最终冲上 1000 点大关。然后单笔交易又暴跌 400 点。交易所内一片疯狂混乱，投机者发现根本找不到股票凭证来平仓空头头寸。《纽约时报》报道："经纪人们像疯了一样……大块头随手把小个子推到一边，而小个子们带着满腔怒火，哭喊着重新投入混战，手、胳膊、肘、脚——无所不用其极……在农产品交易所远处看台上的观众看来，这场争斗、这片嘈杂声浪、这群眼冒金光的疯狂经纪人——买进卖出，卖出买进——简直难以理解，近乎魔幻。"

When brokers appeared with Northern Pacific certificates, they were clawed at by men who feared they would be ruined without them. One broker hired a train from Albany just to deliver one certificate of five hundred shares. Amid this free-for-all, Pierpont Morgan regained control of the Northern Pacific, but at the price of a full-blown panic. It was the madly destructive act of an egotist bent on winning at any cost. The carnage ended when a new Morgan partner, George Perkins, acting with Schiff and Harriman, announced that short sellers would be allowed to buy up shares at only \$ 150 a share. Had the action not been taken, more than half the brokerage houses on Wall Street might have gone belly-up. It had been a pageant of extreme cupidity, one that sparked public apprehension about the omnipotent new financial magnates. The *New York Herald* banner headline of May 9, 1901, summed up the popular view: “GIANTS OF WALL STREET, IN FIERCE BATTLE FOR MASTERY, PRECIPITATE CRASH THAT BRINGS RUIN TO HORDE OF PYGMIES.”[63](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222051)
当持北方太平洋铁路公司股票凭证的经纪人出现时，人们像饿狼般扑上去争抢，生怕错过就会倾家荡产。有经纪人甚至从奥尔巴尼专门包下一列火车，只为运送一张五百股的凭证。在这场混战中，皮尔庞特·摩根重新夺回了北方太平洋铁路公司的控制权，但代价是引发了一场全面恐慌。这是一个不惜一切代价求胜的自大狂所做出的疯狂破坏之举。直到摩根的新合伙人乔治·珀金斯与希夫、哈里曼联手宣布，允许卖空者以每股仅 150 美元的价格购买股票后，这场惨剧才告终结。若未采取这一措施，华尔街半数以上的经纪行可能已经破产。这场极尽贪婪的闹剧，引发了公众对权势滔天的新金融巨头的普遍忧虑。 1901 年 5 月 9 日，《纽约先驱报》的头条标题道出了普遍看法：“华尔街巨鳄，为争霸业鏖战正酣，骤然崩盘，令无数小虾米倾家荡产。”” [63](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222051)

The devil-angel nature of Pierpont Morgan was such that he alone started and stopped panics. He often appeared to be two different people of identical appearance but contrasting personalities. Comically, at the panic’s height, a *New York Times* reporter found a forlorn investor named Jefferson M. Levy at the Waldorf-Astoria; Levy sighed, “If Mr. Morgan had been here this never would have happened.”[64](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222198)
皮尔庞特·摩根兼具魔鬼与天使的双重特质，凭一己之力便能引发和终结恐慌。他常常看起来像是两个长相相同但性格迥异的人。滑稽的是，在恐慌最严重的时候，一名《纽约时报》记者在华尔道夫-阿斯托里亚酒店发现了一位名叫杰斐逊·M·莱维的绝望投资者；莱维叹息道：“要是摩根先生当时在场，这一切就永远不会发生。” [64](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222198)

Pierpont brooked no criticism of his role in the Northern Pacific. Appearing at the Morgan, Harjes offices in Paris, he said with baronial bluntness, “I owe the public nothing.”[65](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222345) The closest he ever came to an explanation was a reiteration of the Gentleman Banker’s Code: “I feel bound in honor when I reorganize a property and am morally responsible for its management to protect it, and I generally do protect it.”[66](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222506) Yet his power on Wall Street was now such that like a female elephant charging to protect her young, he couldn’t help but crush innocent bystanders. He was too large for the flimsy regulatory structures that encased him; he had outgrown his age. Coming after the U.S. Steel promotion, the Northern Pacific corner reinforced the view that the public was being held hostage by the stock manipulations of a few Wall Street moguls.
皮尔庞特绝不容忍任何人在北太平洋铁路问题上对他指手画脚。在巴黎摩根-哈杰斯公司办公室露面时，他用一种贵族式的直率说道：“我不欠公众任何东西。” [65](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222345) 他离解释最接近的一次，是重申了“绅士银行家准则”：“我在重组一家企业时，深感荣誉所系，并在道义上对其管理负有保护责任——我通常也确实做到了保护。” [66](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222506) 然而，当时他在华尔街的权力之大，如同护犊的母象冲锋陷阵，难免会伤及无辜的旁观者。他过于庞大，以致于那些围绕他的脆弱监管框架形同虚设；他已经超越了那个时代。在美国钢铁公司成立之后，北太平洋铁路的囤积行为进一步强化了公众的看法：大众正被少数华尔街大亨的股票操纵所挟持。

For the most part, President McKinley was deaf to such outrage. Then, on September 6, 1901, he was shot by an anarchist named Leon Czolgosz as he stood in the Temple of Music at the Pan-American Exposition in Buffalo. We have graphic descriptions of Pierpont’s reaction to the news. He was about to leave 23 Wall Street for the evening and already had on his silk hat when a *New York Times* reporter rushed in with the report. “What?” said Pierpont, seizing the man’s arm. He stared into his eyes, overcome with amazement. Then he slumped into a desk chair, awaiting the confirmation that soon came by telephone. “This is sad, sad, very sad news,” he told the *Times* reporter.[67](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222683) Other accounts describe him as red-faced and almost reeling with shock.
大多数情况下，麦金莱总统对这些暴行置若罔闻。然而，1901 年 9 月 6 日，他在布法罗泛美博览会的音乐殿堂里，遭到一名叫利昂·乔尔戈斯的无政府主义者枪击。我们对皮尔庞特听闻此消息时的反应有生动的描述。他正要离开华尔街 23 号回家，已经戴上了丝绸礼帽，这时一名《纽约时报》记者冲进来报告了消息。“什么？”皮尔庞特说着，一把抓住那人的胳膊。他凝视着对方的眼睛，惊愕不已。接着，他一屁股瘫坐到办公椅上，等待随后电话传来的确认消息。“这是悲哀、悲哀、非常悲哀的消息，”他对《纽约时报》记者说。 [67](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222683) 另一些描述称他当时面红耳赤，震惊得几乎站不稳。

McKinley’s assassination would be a turning point in Pierpont Morgan’s life, for it installed in the presidency forty-two-year-old Theodore Roosevelt, a man whose view of big business was far more ambivalent than his predecessor’s. Jack Morgan was mildly hopeful about the new president, although TR’s noisy chatter had grated on him after the March inauguration. “What I fear is that he may perhaps talk too much which would be very undesirable,” he said.[68](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222864) In fact, the presidency of Teddy Roosevelt would mark the start of periodic warfare between the White House and the House of Morgan, warfare that would rage through three straight presidencies—those of Roosevelt, Taft, and Wilson.
麦金利遇刺成了皮尔庞特·摩根人生的转折点——这一枪把四十二岁的西奥多·罗斯福送进了白宫，而这位新任总统对大企业的态度远比前任复杂得多。杰克·摩根对这位新总统还抱着一丝微弱的期待，尽管罗斯福在三月就职后那聒噪的谈吐让他颇为不耐。“我只怕他话说得太多，那可就太不妙了。”他如此说道。 [68](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2222864) 事实上，西奥多·罗斯福的总统任期，标志着白宫与摩根家族之间周期性角力的开端——这场较量将席卷连续三任总统：罗斯福、塔夫脱，直至威尔逊。

Two months after McKinley’s assassination, the feuding parties of the Northern Pacific corner made their peace. They set up a holding company, the Northern Securities Co., which merged the Northern Pacific, Great Northern, and CB&Q lines. Both Hill and Harriman were given seats on the board. If this brought peace between the two most important groups on Wall Street, it also heightened public alarm that a railroad monopoly had taken hold west of the Mississippi. “And it will be much easier for them to obtain the second half than it was the first,” said one newspaper editor, foreseeing a subsequent eastern rail monopoly. “One railroad after another will slide gently into their grasp until any passenger anywhere who objects to traveling on their lines can take a trolley car or walk.”[69](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223064) The dreams of the architects of Northern Securities went beyond the most vivid Populist fear. After tying up transcontinental railroads, they planned to link them with steamship lines to Asia—a vision that later would culminate in Edward Harriman’s plans for an around-the-world transportation network. Pierpont, meanwhile, meditated on a rail-ship monopoly of the North Atlantic, extending his domain beyond the borders of the United States. Wall Street increasingly gazed abroad.
麦金利遇刺两个月后，北太平洋铁路争夺战的各方言归于好。他们成立了一家控股公司——北方证券公司，将北太平洋、大北方和 CB&Q 铁路线合并。希尔和哈里曼均获得董事会席位。此举虽让华尔街两大集团握手言和，却也加剧了公众对密西西比河以西铁路垄断的担忧。“他们拿下另一半会比拿下前半部分容易得多，”一位报纸编辑预见随后会形成东部铁路垄断，“一条又一条铁路将悄然落入他们手中，直到任何地方的乘客若不想坐他们的线路，就只能乘电车或步行。” [69](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2223064) 北方证券公司架构师们的野心，早已超越了最生动的民粹主义恐惧。 在贯通了横跨大陆的铁路之后，他们又计划用蒸汽轮船航线将这些铁路与亚洲连接起来——这一愿景后来在爱德华·哈里曼的环球运输网络蓝图中达到了顶峰。与此同时，皮尔庞特正谋划着北大西洋的铁路航运垄断，将自己的版图延伸至美国国境之外。华尔街的目光日益投向海外。

Besides bankrupting thousands of investors, the Northern Pacific corner claimed a last casualty—Morgan partner Robert Bacon. Although he remained at 23 Wall for another year and a half, his nerves were shot by the strain. On doctor’s orders, he rode to hounds for two years—a very Morgan form of therapy.
