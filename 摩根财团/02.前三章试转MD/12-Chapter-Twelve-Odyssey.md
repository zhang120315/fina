# Chapter Twelve: Odyssey

NOTHING better symbolized the House of Morgan’s postwar supremacy or its fusion with American policy in the Diplomatic Age than its new prominence in the Far East. At first, the bank had entered Asia at the government’s behest, reluctantly joining the China consortium. Then William Jennings Bryan had condemned such foreign “meddling,” and the group was disbanded. But the world war, by strengthening America and weakening Europe in the Pacific, tempted Secretary of State Robert Lansing with new regions. In 1919, rebuffed by his own Treasury Department, he resurrected the China group of private bankers instead. Jack Morgan remarked, “But Mr. Lansing, Mr. Bryan asked us to desist.” Lansing, shamefaced, conceded the striking policy reversal.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276733)
摩根银行在战后至高无上的地位，以及它在“外交时代”与美国政策的融合，最显著的体现莫过于它在远东地区的新兴影响力。起初，这家银行是应政府之命才进入亚洲的，并不情愿地加入了中国银团。后来，威廉·詹宁斯·布赖恩谴责了这种外国“干涉”，该集团随即解散。然而，世界大战在太平洋地区壮大了美国、削弱了欧洲，这让国务卿罗伯特·兰辛看到了新的机遇。1919 年，在遭到财政部拒绝后，他转而恢复了由私人银行家组成的中国银团。杰克·摩根对此评论道：“可是兰辛先生，当初是布赖恩先生让我们停手的啊。”兰辛羞愧地承认了这种政策的惊人逆转。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276733)

For this second China consortium, Tom Lamont played the exasperating chairman’s role that previously had fallen to Harry Davison. In December 1919, Lamont visited the White House for his marching orders and found his idol, Woodrow Wilson, confined to a wheelchair. In a moving farewell, the president was rolled into the sunshine of a wide bay window. Calm and pensive, even joking about his disability, he hoped Lamont could reconcile differences between the two rival governments struggling to control China. Ever since the 1911 revolution, power had been divided between an official government in Peking and a nationalist one in Canton, with warlords ruling over Manchuria. From a banker’s standpoint, this divided China was no less risky than the Manchu dynasty, for there still existed no ultimate guarantor of debt, no government bedrock on which to base loans.
对于这第二个中国财团，汤姆·拉蒙特扮演了此前由哈里·戴维森担任的令人恼火的董事长角色。1919 年 12 月，拉蒙特前往白宫接受指示，却发现他的偶像伍德罗·威尔逊已困于轮椅。在一次感人的告别中，总统被推到宽敞凸窗的阳光里。他平静而沉思，甚至拿自己的残疾打趣，希望拉蒙特能调和两个争夺中国控制权的敌对政府之间的分歧。自 1911 年革命以来，权力一直分裂为北京的官方政府与广州的国民政府，而军阀则统治着满洲。从银行家的角度来看，这个分裂的中国并不比满清王朝风险更小，因为仍然没有最终的债务担保人，没有可以放贷的政府基石。

In 1920, Lamont went on a mission to the Far East to see whether the time was ripe for Chinese loans. Coolly watchful, he moved through a China convulsed by strikes and student riots prompted by aggressive Japanese moves in Manchuria. The students were outraged by the Treaty of Versailles, which seemed to ratify Japan’s seizure of German possessions in China during the war. Lamont was caught up in the Sino-Japanese rivalry. With diplomatic tact and evenhandedness, he included a side trip to Tokyo on his itinerary. During this 1920 trip, Lamont moved about with royal pomp and a touch of splendor, borrowing a leaf from Pierpont’s book. Each morning in Peking, he received local merchants, who brought to his hotel camel caravans laden with costly wares—furs, rugs, silk, jade, and porcelain.
1920 年，拉蒙特肩负使命前往远东，考察向中国提供贷款的时机是否成熟。他冷静观察，穿梭于一个因日本在满洲的侵略行径而陷入罢工与学潮动荡的中国。学生们对《凡尔赛条约》愤慨不已，该条约似乎认可了日本在战时夺取德国在华权益的行径。拉蒙特深陷中日对峙的漩涡，他以老练的外交手腕和公允态度，将东京之行也纳入了行程。此次 1920 年的远东之旅，拉蒙特模仿皮尔庞特的做派，出行时极尽排场与奢华。每天早晨在北京下榻的酒店，他都会接见当地商人，他们牵着满载贵重货物——皮草、地毯、丝绸、玉器和瓷器——的骆驼商队前来拜访。

Lamont was pursued by more than just vendors. The Japanese set spies on his trail—such shameless eavesdroppers that they booked rooms on both sides of his hotel room. The insouciant Lamont kept a single item at his side—the code book for deciphering Morgan cables. His secretary lacked such sang froid. Lamont later wrote, “Although I thought it unnecessary precaution, my secretary always took \[the code book\] to bed with him and insisted upon sleeping with a loaded revolver under his pillow.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276942) Afterward, reading a cable on a train, Lamont found a Japanese spy craning to steal a peek over his shoulder. He put the man out of his misery by offhandedly handing him the message.
拉蒙特身后跟着的可不只是商家。日本人派了间谍盯梢——这帮不知廉耻的偷听者，竟把他酒店房间两边的客房都订了下来。而漫不经心的拉蒙特身边只放着一件东西——解读摩根电报的密码本。他的秘书可没这份沉着。拉蒙特后来写道：“虽然我觉得没必要防备到那个地步，但我的秘书总把\[密码本\]带上床，还坚持要在枕头底下放一把上了膛的左轮手枪。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2276942) 后来有一次在火车上看电报，拉蒙特发现一个日本间谍正伸长脖子偷瞄他的肩膀。他索性随手把电报递了过去，算是解了那人的馋。

News reports of Lamont’s visit stirred nationalist fears that foreign bankers would try to impose a new financial protectorate over China. His arrival was protested by student demonstrations, which he thought were instigated by the Japanese. He liked to tell of how he had pacified a mob of students in Shanghai. If perhaps slightly embellished, the story testifies to Lamont’s belief in reason and refinement as all-purpose weapons:
拉蒙特访华的消息见诸报端，立即激起了民族主义者的恐慌，他们担心外国银行家会试图对中国建立新的金融保护。他的到来引发了学生示威抗议，而他认为这是日本人在背后煽动。他喜欢讲述自己如何平息上海一群骚乱学生的故事。虽然可能略有夸张，但这个故事印证了拉蒙特的信念：理性与修养是万能武器。

> One mid-afternoon in Shanghai I was told that a group of a couple of hundred of the Chinese student body were waiting in front of my hotel in order to show their disapproval of the Consortium by stoning me. I sent out word suggesting that the leaders come in for a cup of tea, and talk it all over. A dozen of them turned up at first in rather an ugly mood. But the tea was soothing and as soon as I was able to explain the facts about the Consortium, that it was designed to free China from the worst of her financial difficulties and help put some of her state enterprises on their feet, they readily understood and agreed to cooperate.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277110)
> 一个上海的中午，有人告诉我，有几百名中国学生聚集在我住的酒店门口，准备用石头砸我，以表达他们对银行团（Consortium）的不满。我传话出去，邀请他们的领头人进来喝杯茶，把事情好好谈谈。一开始来了十几个人，情绪相当激动。但茶让人平静下来，当我向他们解释银行团的本意是帮助中国摆脱最严重的财政困难，并助力一些国有企业走上正轨时，他们很快就理解了，并表示愿意合作。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277110)

Did Lamont believe tea-time chatter had changed the students’ minds? Probably not. Yet the story suggests his constant advantage in confrontations. He always sounded so friendly and reasonable that he disarmed his most vocal critics. Nobody could bait him, shake his poise, or make him surrender that casual but impenetrable self-control.
拉蒙特真的相信下午茶时的闲聊就能改变学生们的想法吗？大概不。但这个故事表明，他在对峙中总是占尽优势。他说话总是那么友善理性，让最激烈的批评者都无从反驳。没人能激怒他，动摇他的镇定，或迫使他放弃那种随意却无懈可击的自制力。

Lamont never warmed to the Chinese and often spoke of them disparagingly. Contrary to his usual reticence in such matters, he retailed stories of Chinese corruption and intolerance. In Shanghai, he wanted to see Dr. Sun Yat-sen, head of the nationalist government in southern China. Because the Chinese leader feared a terrorist attack if he went to Lamont’s hotel, Lamont visited him, under a heavy police escort. He saw nothing venerable about Dr. Sun, who had once attended school in Hawaii and had once been an omnivorous reader at the British Museum Library. Repeating Wilson’s question of whether peace could be achieved between the two Chinas, Lamont was shocked by the reply. “Peace between the South and North?” Sun echoed. “Why, yes. Just you give me \$25 million, Mr. Lamont, and I’ll equip a couple of army corps. Then we’ll have peace in short order.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277275) Lamont was equally disenchanted by his contacts with the Peking government. Over tea, President Hsu suggested that if a government loan fell through, he might be in the market for a \$5-million loan for himself.
拉蒙特始终对中国人抱有好感，常出言不逊。一反平日对这些事的缄默，他四处散布关于中国腐败与狭隘的传闻。在上海，他想会见南方国民政府领袖孙中山博士。由于这位中国领导人担心前往拉蒙特下榻的酒店会遭刺杀，拉蒙特便在严密警力护送下登门拜访。他对这位曾在夏威夷求学、曾在大英博物馆图书馆博览群书的孙中山并无敬意。当拉蒙特复述威尔逊关于南北中国能否实现和平的疑问时，对方的回答令他震惊。“南北和平？”孙中山应道，“哦，当然能。拉蒙特先生，只要你给我两千五百万美元，我就能装备几个军团。届时和平指日可待。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277275) 拉蒙特与北京政府的接触同样令他大失所望。 喝茶时，徐主席暗示，如果政府贷款谈不成，他可能会为自己寻求一笔 500 万美元的贷款。

Reporting back to the American group, Lamont recommended that no Chinese loans be made until north and south were unified, with a parliament that could take responsibility for a loan. It was the same problem faced by the first bankers’ consortium—an unstable polity. China never met the group’s conditions. By 1922, Lamont was asking Secretary of State Hughes whether the China consortium should be disbanded. An ersatz diplomat, Lamont wished to persevere less for reasons of profit than for reasons of state. But the question was moot: the China group had been stillborn. This didn’t upset the House of Morgan, for Japan would be its most profitable customer in the Far East and China only an annoying factor in that relationship. Before long, Morgan involvement in Japan would be so deep as to deprive Tom Lamont of any incentive to renew his Chinese initiatives.
回复美国总部时，拉蒙特建议在中国南北统一、建立能承担借款责任的议会之前，不应向中国放贷。这仍是首个银行团遭遇的老问题——政局不稳。中国始终未能满足该财团的条件。到 1922 年，拉蒙特向国务卿休斯询问是否应解散中国银行团。这位形似外交家的银行家，之所以希望坚持，更多是出于国家利益而非利润考量。但这个问题已无意义：中国银行团从一开始便胎死腹中。摩根财团对此并不在意，毕竟日本才是其在远东最赚钱的客户，中国不过是这段关系中的麻烦因素。不久之后，摩根在日本深度介入，使得汤姆·拉蒙特再也无意重启在华计划。

IN contrast with his tumultuous journey through China, the Japanese leg of his 1920 journey was far more congenial, the start of a lasting friendship. Japan was already known as the England of Asia—the highest possible recommendation for a Morgan partner—and with Japan and America ascendant in the Pacific, the time had come for closer financial relations. Like the United States, Japan had prospered during the war by selling ships and supplies to the Allies. Its gold reserves had grown a hundredfold—a war chest likely to impress any banker. And where the United States was already Japan’s best customer, Japan was now the fourth best market for U.S. exports.
与他在中国的动荡旅程相比，1920 年日本之行则要惬意得多，也是他与日本持久友谊的开端。日本当时已有“亚洲的英格兰”之称——这对于摩根公司的合伙人而言无疑是最高赞誉——随着日美两国在太平洋地区日益崛起，双方加强金融关系的时机已然成熟。与美国一样，日本在战争期间通过向协约国出售船只和物资而获利颇丰。其黄金储备增长了百倍之多——这笔战争财富足以令任何银行家瞩目。而美国既是日本最大的客户，日本也已成为美国出口的第四大市场。

The political context, too, was auspicious: the Japan that Lamont encountered contained liberal elements eager to cultivate Western bankers and open the country to new influences. For the moment, enlightened aristocrats held the upper hand over militarists, and the cultural mood favored tolerance, openness, even a touch of bohemianism. The Japanese economy was dominated by *zaibatsu*—combination trading houses and industrial conglomerates formed around core banks—and they were fast expanding overseas. So as Britain weakened its long-standing alliance with Japan, Washington moved to fill the vacuum.
当时的政治氛围也十分有利：拉蒙特所见的日本带有自由派色彩，他们渴望结交西方银行家，愿意让国家接受新思潮的影响。彼时，开明的贵族阶层压制住了军国主义者，文化风气倡导宽容、开放，甚至带有一丝波西米亚式的自由气息。日本的经济由财阀主导——这些围绕核心银行形成的贸易与工业联合体——并且正在迅速向海外扩张。因此，当英国逐渐削弱与日本长期结盟的关系时，华盛顿便顺势填补了这一真空。

Tom Lamont and his wife, Florence, were greeted by Japan’s elite: the merchant emperors of the houses of Mitsui and Mitsubishi. Cultured and patrician, these families possessed a natural appeal for someone as ceremonious and attentive to style as Lamont. A friend later remarked that Tom “simply outsmiled the Japanese.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277443) Eager to meet Wall Street’s newest ambassador, the Japanese business leaders entertained him as a visiting monarch. He marveled at how they produced No dancers at a moment’s notice or “bevies of graceful, dancing geisha girls.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277608) Florence was taken on a private tour of the twenty-five-acre central Tokyo estate of Baron and Baroness Iwasaki, a maze of lakes, gardens, and secluded courtyards. The Iwasakis were probably Japan’s richest family and the founders of the Mitsubishi conglomerate, which owned Japan’s largest steamship line.
汤姆·拉蒙特和妻子弗洛伦斯受到了日本精英阶层的隆重欢迎：三井和三菱的商界巨头们亲自接见。这些家族教养深厚、气质高贵，对于拉蒙特这样注重礼仪和风度的人来说，自然极具吸引力。后来有朋友评论道，汤姆“光是笑容就让日本人甘拜下风。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277443) 急于结识这位华尔街新使者的日本商界领袖们，将他奉若来访的君主般盛情款待。让他惊叹的是，这些人竟能随时召来能剧舞者，或是“一群优雅动人的艺妓翩翩起舞。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277608) 弗洛伦斯则受邀参观了岩崎男爵夫妇位于东京市中心、占地二十五英亩的私人宅邸，那是一座由湖泊、花园和隐秘庭院交织而成的迷宫。岩崎家族可能是日本最富有的家族，也是三菱财团的创始人，而三菱旗下拥有日本最大的轮船公司。

The power of the House of Morgan in the 1920s owed much to its intimacy with the world’s major central bankers, its ability to provide private channels of communication among them. Lamont conferred about the China consortium with Junnosuke Inouye, governor of the Bank of Japan, a gravely erect man with round, black-rimmed glasses and a solemn mien. The towering figure of his generation in Japanese finance, Inouye had served as president of the Yokohama Specie Bank, whose Wall Street office was fiscal agent for the Japanese government. He would twice be governor of the Bank of Japan and three times minister of finance. Like Ben Strong in America, Montagu Norman in England, and, later, Hjalmar Schact in Germany, Inouye made his nation’s cental bank a strong, independent voice in the country’s affairs. Like so many Lamont meetings, this one was providential. For Wall Streeters eager to believe that justice and decency would prevail over militarism in Japan, Inouye was heaven-sent. He was an apostle of sound currencies and balanced budgets and remained a steadfast, courageous opponent of the militarists.
1920 年代摩根家族的权势很大程度上源于其与全球主要央行行长的密切关系，以及其为他们之间提供私人沟通渠道的能力。拉蒙特曾就中国银团问题与日本银行总裁井上准之助进行磋商——这位戴着圆框黑边眼镜、神情庄严、身形挺拔的男士，是日本金融界同代人中的翘楚。他曾担任横滨正金银行总裁（该行华尔街分行是日本政府的财务代理），两次出任日本银行总裁，三次担任大藏大臣。如同美国的本·斯特朗、英国的蒙塔古·诺曼以及后来的德国的亚尔马·沙赫特，井上使日本央行在国家事务中成为强有力且独立的声音。像拉蒙特的诸多会晤一样，这次谈话也堪称天赐良机。对于渴望相信正义与体面终将战胜日本军国主义的华尔街人士而言，井上简直是天降福音。 他是稳健货币与平衡预算的坚定倡导者，并始终勇敢地站在军国主义者的对立面，毫不退缩。

Lamont established another fateful friendship, with the head of the Mitsui conglomerate, Baron Takuma Dan, a slight, fragile man, with a gentle manner and distinguished gray hair and mustache. His nickname was the Morgan of Japan. Fluent in English, with a mining degree from MIT, he was no less international and cosmopolitan than Tom Lamont. As managing director of the Mitsui conglomerate and chairman of the Mitsui bank, he controlled an empire that extended into every branch of the Japanese economy. It controlled a third of Japanese overseas trade—25 percent of the silk trade, 40 percent of coal exports—and managed a shipping fleet the size of the French merchant marine.
拉蒙特与三井财阀的掌门人——团琢磨男爵结下了一段命运攸关的友谊。团琢磨身材瘦削，看似孱弱，却举止温文尔雅，一头灰白华发与胡须尽显不凡。他被誉为“日本的摩根”。他英语流利，拥有麻省理工学院的采矿学位，其国际化视野与博闻广识丝毫不逊于汤姆·拉蒙特。作为三井财阀的常务董事兼三井银行董事长，他掌控着一个渗透日本经济各个领域的商业帝国。这个帝国控制着日本三分之一的海外贸易——占丝绸贸易的 25%、煤炭出口的 40%——并拥有一支规模相当于法国商船队的庞大航运舰队。

The Mitsui group made the Morgans seem like yesterday’s upstarts. For nine consecutive generations, its bank had faced the sacred mountain, Fujisan. The House of Mitsui had become financial agents of the shogunate in the seventeenth century and bankers to the imperial house by 1867. It provided a convenient overseas network for the Japanese government, having more agencies abroad than the government had embassies. At the Mitsui compound in central Tokyo—a fortresslike structure with a huge gate and stone wall bristling with bamboo spikes—Baron Dan entertained Lamont with the same magnificence he later displayed to the Prince of Wales. He showed his guest Gobelin tapestries in his grand salon. Then they strolled by lotus ponds and under pine trees festooned with thousands of paper lanterns. The following year, to promote closer American ties, Dan led a Japanese delegation to Wall Street and dined with the Lamonts at their East Seventieth Street townhouse.
三井集团让摩根家族看起来像是昨日的暴发户。其银行连续九代面向圣山富士。十七世纪时，三井家族成为幕府的金融代理人，到 1867 年又成为皇室银行。它为日本政府提供了一个便捷的海外网络，其在国外的代理机构数量甚至超过了日本政府的大使馆。在东京市中心的三井大院——一座堡垒般的建筑，有着巨大的大门和布满竹刺的石墙——团琢磨男爵以后来招待威尔士亲王时的同样奢华款待了拉蒙特。他在豪华沙龙里向客人展示了戈贝兰挂毯。随后，他们漫步于莲花池畔，在挂满数千只纸灯笼的松树下徜徉。次年，为促进美日关系，团琢磨率领日本代表团前往华尔街，并在东第七十街的联排别墅中与拉蒙特一家共进晚餐。

Lamont’s success on his 1920 trip bore fruit with amazing speed. On September 1, 1923, an earthquake erupted in the Tokyo-Yokohama vicinity. It was a hot, windy day, and fires fanned over both cities, causing unspeakable damage. It was the century’s worst earthquake, and over a hundred thousand people died. More than half of Tokyo and Yokohama was reduced to ashes. The property damage alone wiped out 2 percent of Japan’s wealth.
Lamont 1920 年之行取得的成功，以惊人的速度结出了硕果。1923 年 9 月 1 日，东京与横滨地区突发大地震。那是一个炎热多风的日子，大火在两座城市蔓延，造成了难以形容的破坏。这是本世纪最严重的地震，超过十万人丧生。东京和横滨半数以上的区域化为灰烬。仅财产损失一项，就抹去了日本 2% 的财富。

When the news was learned at 23 Wall, Morgan publicity chief Martin Egan paid a condolence call at the Wall Street office of the Yokohama Specie Bank. Dwight Morrow became chairman of the Red Cross Japan Fund, and the Corner was converted into the New York headquarters for relief work. Rumors circulated that Japan planned to float its first bond issue in America since the Russo-Japanese War. Lamont wrote to Inouye, who was now the finance minister, and advised against such an issue. Lamont realized that candor more than greed would pay off in this situation. In his cable he said, “People who are contributing millions of dollars out of pocket for suffering and disaster are a little chary at the same moment of buying bonds for the people whom they are trying to assist.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2277776)
消息传到华尔街 23 号时，摩根公司的公关主管马丁·伊根特地前往横滨正金银行的华尔街办公室吊唁。德怀特· Morrow 出任了日本红十字基金会主席，华尔街角就此成了赈灾行动的纽约大本营。坊间传言日本打算自日俄战争以来首次在美国发行国债。拉蒙特致信时任大藏大臣的井上，建议他打消这个念头。拉蒙特心里清楚，此刻坦诚比贪婪更能奏效。他在电报中写道："那些自掏腰包为灾难灾民捐出数百万美元的人，恐怕不会愿意同时购买他们想要援助之人的债券。"

By late 1923, the Japanese, with their exceptional resilience, had restored Tokyo’s electric lights, gas service, and water supply. The Tokyo Stock Exchange was back in service in under three months. The mass destruction had one beneficial side effect: it forced Japan to scrap many old factories and modernize its industrial plants. By declaring a bank holiday that saved many financial institutions, Inouye attained heroic stature in Japan. And when the House of Mitsui rebuilt its bank, the building’s white marble facade was designed by Trowbridge and Livingston, the architects of 23 Wall Street. Some saw the House of Mitsui paying tacit homage to the House of Morgan and honoring their new ties.
到 1923 年底，日本人凭借非凡的韧性，恢复了东京的电灯、燃气和供水系统。东京证券交易所在不到三个月内重新开业。大规模破坏带来一个意外的好处：它迫使日本淘汰了许多老旧工厂，推动了工业设备的现代化。通过宣布银行假期挽救了许多金融机构，井上准之助在日本获得了英雄般的地位。而当三井财阀重建其银行大楼时，其白色大理石外墙由设计华尔街 23 号的 Trowbridge and Livingston 事务所操刀。有人将此视为三井财阀向摩根财阀默默致敬，并彰显双方新建立的纽带。

Once the calamitous mood disappeared, Lamont set about to win the Japanese government as an exclusive Morgan client. The Japanese had found Pierpont rather rough and abrasive—he had offended them by demanding collateral on loans—and preferred doing business with Jacob Schiff of Kuhn, Loeb. For aid provided during the Russo-Japanese War, Schiff was decorated by the mikado with the Order of the Sacred Treasure. On Wall Street in the 1920s, it was a delicate affair to steal away valued business while adhering to the Gentleman Banker’s Code. So with guile and subtlety, Lamont had to coach Inouye’s emissary, Tat-sumi, on Wall Street etiquette. Slyly, he put words in his mouth, tutoring him in the preferred style for an amicable breach. Afterward, Lamont explained how he primed his target:
一旦那股灾祸般的情绪消散，拉蒙特便开始着手将日本政府发展为摩根的专属客户。日本人觉得皮尔庞特相当粗鲁傲慢——他要求贷款抵押的做法冒犯了他们——因此更倾向于与库恩-洛布公司的雅各布·希夫打交道。日俄战争期间，希夫提供援助，天皇授予他瑞宝勋章。在 20 世纪 20 年代的华尔街，要想在遵守《银行家绅士守则》的同时挖走别人的宝贵生意，可是一件微妙的事。于是，拉蒙特凭借机巧与心计，不得不向井上的特使辰巳灌输华尔街的礼节。他狡猾地借辰巳之口，教他如何以一种体面的方式友好分手。事后，拉蒙特解释了自己如何铺垫目标：

> However as to the handling of any loan we have told Tatsumi frankly that it appeared to us there were only 2 courses for him to adopt. First to go to Kuhn, Loeb & Co. and state to them that because of the relations existing during the loan operation of the Russian War 20 years ago they desire them now to undertake the projected operation; or second as a complete alternative to go to them and say that because of the national crisis confronting their country; because of the grave necessity they felt themselves under for securing co-operation throughout the entire American investment public; because too of the importance for careful co-operation between the New York and London markets they had determined to invite us to make the lead in the projected operation and expected their friends Kuhn, Loeb & Co. to tell them this course was the wise one.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278005)
> 然而，在如何处理任何贷款事宜上，我们已坦率地告知辰巳，我们认为他只有两条路可走。其一，去找库恩-洛布公司，直言二十年前日俄战争贷款操作期间建立的关系，使他们如今希望这家公司来接手当前的计划；其二，作为完全替代的方案，去找他们并说明：鉴于国家面临的危机，鉴于他们深感亟需争取整个美国投资公众的合作，也鉴于纽约和伦敦市场之间审慎合作的重要性，他们已经决定邀请我们来主导这项计划，并期待他们的朋友库恩-洛布公司能告诉他们，这条道路是明智之举。 [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278005)

Kuhn, Loeb was now too small to handle the projected \$150-million earthquake loan, which would be the largest long-term foreign loan ever placed in the American market. The firm was also still suffering from the damage done to its standing on account of its supposed German sympathies in the war. When the issue appeared in February 1924, J. P. Morgan and Company brought in its old allies, National City and First National, as syndicate managers. To soothe ruffled feelings, they included Kuhn, Loeb. Whatever the private gloating at Morgans, the firm outwardly respected propriety. There was a twin loan of £25 million in London, and Barings, Schroders, and Rothschilds now had to include Morgan Grenfell in their Japanese financing.
库恩·洛布公司如今规模过小，无法处理预计 1.5 亿美元的地震贷款——这将是美国市场有史以来最大的一笔长期外国贷款。该公司也仍因战时被指同情德国而声誉受损。当这笔债券于 1924 年 2 月发行时，J.P.摩根公司拉来了老盟友国民城市银行和第一国民银行作为承销团牵头方。为安抚受挫情绪，它们将库恩·洛布纳入了承销团。尽管摩根内部可能暗自得意，但表面仍恪守礼节。伦敦方面则有一笔 2500 万英镑的双胞胎贷款，巴林银行、施罗德银行和罗斯柴尔德银行如今不得不将摩根建富纳入其日本融资业务中。

The American loan had a concealed agenda. On two occasions, Lamont had talked with Secretary of State Hughes, who said he would be gratified “to have the Japanese people have clear evidence of the friendly feeling on the part of the two great English speaking nations toward Japan and the Japanese.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278243) Once again, Wall Street financing was the visible face of a shift in government policy.
这笔美国贷款隐藏着一个不可告人的目的。拉蒙特曾两次与国务卿休斯交谈，休斯表示，如果“让日本人民清楚地看到两个英语大国对日本及日本人民的友好情谊”，他将感到欣慰。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278243) 华尔街的融资再次成为政府政策转向的表象。

One problem inherent in employing bankers as *de facto* ambassadors was that they might transfer their allegiance to foreign powers. After all, private bankers were schooled in a tradition of absolute loyalty to their clients. A Tom Lamont would feel no less responsible to Japanese bondholders than a Pierpont Morgan would to railroad bondholders. So the House of Morgan believed it had a stake in Japanese success and prosperity and felt obliged to perform political favors for its important new client. Even as the Morgan bank sponsored the big earthquake loan, partners were embroiled in a political controversy on behalf of the Japanese. They protested the Japanese Exclusion Act, which was designed to check Japanese immigration and had a racist tinge. And they complained to the White House about American fleet maneuvers around Hawaii, which were troubling the Japanese. Tokyo and 23 Wall now played a game of mutual adulation. By 1927, the emperor of Japan would invest Jack Morgan with the Order of the Sacred Treasure and Lamont with the Order of the Rising Sun; in 1931, Russell Leffingwell would receive the Second Class Order of the Sacred Treasure. These were rare honors for American bankers.
雇佣银行家作为事实上的大使存在一个固有弊端：他们可能会将忠诚转移到外国势力身上。毕竟，私人银行家接受的训练是绝对忠于客户。汤姆·拉蒙特对日本债券持有人的责任感，不会比皮尔庞特·摩根对铁路债券持有人少半分。因此，摩根财团认为自己在日本的成功与繁荣中有利害关系，并有义务为其重要的新客户提供政治上的协助。就在摩根银行赞助大地震贷款的同时，其合伙人还代表日本卷入了政治争议。他们抗议带有种族主义色彩的《排日法案》（该法案旨在限制日本移民），并向白宫抱怨美国在夏威夷附近令日本不安的舰队演习。东京与华尔街 23 号就此上演了一出相互吹捧的戏码。 到了 1927 年，日本天皇授予杰克·摩根瑞宝勋章，授予拉蒙特旭日勋章；1931 年，拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔又获得二等瑞宝勋章。这些荣誉对于美国银行家而言实属罕见。

The tendency to switch loyalties to foreign clients and acquire a strong interest in their survival would have profound consequences for the House of Morgan. For by the mid-1920s, Lamont had recruited three new clients—Japan, Germany, and Italy—whose course would sharply clash with America’s. It was strictly by chance that the bank became involved with three future enemies. But over time, these business conquests would create an extraordinary situation in which the true-blue banker of the Allies ended up in the precarious position of banker to the future Axis powers.
这种对国外客户转变忠诚、对其存亡异常关切的倾向，对摩根家族产生了深远的影响。因为到了 20 年代中期，拉蒙特招揽了三位新客户——日本、德国和意大利——而它们的走向将与美国的利益尖锐冲突。摩根银行与这三个未来的敌国打交道，纯属偶然。但随着时间的推移，这些商业征服却造成了一种非同寻常的局面：这位同盟国的忠实银行家，最终竟陷于为未来轴心国服务的危险境地。

IN the new vogue for foreign securities, the major area of attention was Latin America. Bond peddlers from Wall Street banks badgered small investors into buying bonds issued in places they could scarcely pronounce. Few knew the checkered history of Latin American lending or that as early as 1825 nearly every borrower in Latin America had defaulted on interest payments. In the nineteenth century, South America was already known for wild borrowing sprees, followed by waves of default. Now too many bankers again chased too few good deals, and credit standards eroded accordingly. In describing the 1920s, Otto Kahn later said, “A dozen American bankers sat in a half a dozen South and Central American States . . . one outbidding the other foolishly, recklessly, to the detriment of the public.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278478) The default of Latin debt in the 1930s would profoundly shake America’s faith in Wall Street.
在新一轮海外证券投资热潮中，拉丁美洲成为最受瞩目的焦点。华尔街银行那些兜售债券的掮客们，一个劲儿地游说散户投资者购买他们连名字都念不利索的国家发行的债券。鲜有人知晓拉丁美洲借贷史那充满波折的一面，更不知道早在 1825 年，拉丁美洲几乎每个借款人都曾出现过利息违约。19 世纪的南美洲，早已因疯狂借贷后接连违约而闻名。如今，太多的银行家又在一窝蜂地追逐寥寥无几的好项目，信贷标准也随之放宽。奥托·卡恩后来在描述 20 世纪 20 年代时曾说：“十几个美国银行家挤在南美和中美的六七个国家里……一个比一个愚蠢、轻率地抬价竞标，最终损害的却是公众利益。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278478) 20 世纪 30 年代拉丁美洲债务的违约，将深刻动摇美国人民对华尔街的信任。

Latin American loans had always been risky because of the region’s dependence on fluctuating commodity prices. A dip in copper prices instantly hurt Chile, while lower tin prices could cripple Bolivia. When the price of sugar collapsed in 1920-21, the Cuban economy plunged with it. Faced with many business failures, National City Bank—which held 90 percent of Cuba’s deposits and served as the country’s national bank—foreclosed on properties and ended up owning a fifth of the island’s sugar mills. The Guaranty Trust was also heavily invested in Cuban sugar and had to be rescued by a Morgan-led group of bankers in May 1921. William C. Potter, a manager of the Guggenheims’ smelting trust, was brought in as a caretaker executive and loan liquidator for the bank; George Whitney and other Morgan partners went on its board. Devastated by the experience, Guaranty Trust would deteriorate into such a sleepy, stodgy, risk-fearing institution that by 1959 it would be ripe for merger with the far smaller J. P. Morgan and Company.
拉丁美洲的贷款向来风险很高，因为该地区经济高度依赖波动剧烈的大宗商品价格。铜价一跌，智利经济立刻受创；锡价走低，玻利维亚就可能陷入瘫痪。1920 至 1921 年间糖价暴跌，古巴经济也随之崩盘。面对大量企业破产，持有古巴 90%存款、相当于该国央行的 National City Bank 不得不没收抵押资产，最终拥有了岛上五分之一的糖厂。Guaranty Trust 也在古巴糖业中投入巨资，被迫在 1921 年 5 月接受由摩根牵头的一个银行家集团救助。古根海姆冶炼信托的经理 William C. Potter 被请来担任该行的临时高管兼贷款清算人，而 George Whitney 等摩根合伙人则进入了该行董事会。 那次经历让 Guaranty Trust 元气大伤，沦落为一家死气沉沉、因循守旧、畏惧风险的机构，到 1959 年时，它已准备好与规模小得多的 J. P. Morgan 公司合并。

As the prestige bank of Wall Street, the House of Morgan didn’t need to coerce Main Street investors into buying Latin American bonds. It preferred European industrial states, Commonwealth countries (Canada and Australia), and developed states on the periphery (Japan and South Africa) although it had long shared business in Argentina with Barings. This was the privilege of success: the bank could choose the soundest foreign borrowers, lending its imprimatur only to those countries that probably didn’t need it. The only poor country the bank dealt with was Mexico, which had gone from the model Latin American debtor in Pierpont’s day to the *bête noire* of global bankers. During the prolonged turmoil of the Mexican Revolution, it had repudiated over \$500 million in government and railway debt, an unusual loss of principal on Morgan-sponsored foreign bonds. Adding to the bank’s indignation was the fact that the defaulted debt included Pierpont’s sacred loan of 1899—the first foreign issue ever floated in London by an American banking house.
作为华尔街的尊贵银行，摩根财团无需迫使普通投资者购买拉美债券。它更青睐欧洲工业国、英联邦国家（如加拿大和澳大利亚）以及处于发展边缘的发达国家（如日本和南非），尽管它长期与巴林银行共享阿根廷业务。这是成功的特权：银行可以选择最稳健的外国借款人，只将其信誉背书给予那些或许根本不需要它的国家。该银行唯一打交道的贫穷国家是墨西哥，这个国家从皮尔庞特时代的模范拉美债务人变成了全球银行家的眼中钉。在墨西哥革命旷日持久的动荡中，该国拒付了超过 5 亿美元的政府和铁路债务，这对摩根担保的外国债券而言，是一次罕见的本金损失。 使银行更加愤慨的是，这笔违约债务中包含了皮尔庞特 1899 年的那笔神圣贷款——这是第一家美国银行机构在伦敦发行的外国债券。

Before examining the Mexican debt morass, it is important to note some differences between Latin American debt then and now. During the interwar years, the debt was package’d as bonds and sold to small investors; in our own day, the debt would take the form of bank credits, meaning that the public is not directly at risk. During the 1920s, banks negotiated with Latin American debtors, not on their own account, but as “moral trustees” for small bondholders. Such was the nature of Morgan involvement in Mexico, with Tom Lamont serving as chairman of the International Committee of Bankers on Mexico—the splendidly initialed ICBM. Formed in 1918 with the approval of the State Department and the British Foreign Office, the ICBM negotiated for two hundred thousand small bondholders. In the nineteenth century, Mexican debt talks had been handled by Barings. But citing the Monroe Doctrine, the State Department demanded that the United States have the controlling hand on the committee. With over \$1 billion invested in Mexico, the United States behaved like a jealous landlord. Mexico was a resource-rich country that always held out a seductive promise of prosperity, which it never quite fulfilled. And it had a weak political system, always making debt repayment problematic.
在探讨墨西哥债务泥潭之前，有必要先指出拉丁美洲债务问题在当年与当今的几个不同之处。两次世界大战期间，债务被打包成债券出售给散户投资者；而如今，债务则以银行信贷的形式存在，这意味着公众并不直接承担风险。20 世纪 20 年代，银行与拉丁美洲债务人谈判时，并非为自己谋利，而是作为散户债券持有人的"道德受托人"。摩根大通在墨西哥的介入便是如此，汤姆·拉蒙特担任了墨西哥国际银行家委员会（ICBM，这个缩写颇为响亮）的主席。该委员会于 1918 年经美国国务院和英国外交部批准成立，代表了二十万名散户债券持有人的利益进行谈判。19 世纪时，墨西哥的债务谈判由巴林银行负责。但美国国务院援引门罗主义，要求美国必须在该委员会中掌握主导权。 美国在墨西哥投资超过十亿美元，却表现得像个善妒的房东。墨西哥资源丰富，总描绘出诱人的繁荣前景，却从未真正实现。而其脆弱的政治体制，也使得债务偿还问题始终棘手。

Lamont spent so much time wrestling with Mexican debt that a slightly paternal tone crept into his comments, as if Mexico were the backward child of the Morgan brood. Writing a birthday greeting to his son, Corliss, in 1923, he grew mawkish: “Much of my life for 2 years past has been devoted to help poor Mexico to her feet. . . . The accomplishment of that task is one of my daily prayers.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278655) Lamont claimed that Mexico was the first thing to occupy him each morning, and he often talked about the widows and orphans who had waited years without receiving interest on their bonds. The Mexican debt crisis demanded saintly patience and something of a romantic’s penchant for lost causes. Lamont was ideally suited for the task.
拉蒙特在墨西哥债务问题上耗费了大量时间，以至于他的评论中不自觉地带上了一丝家长式口吻，仿佛墨西哥是摩根家族中那个落后的孩子。1923 年，他在写给儿子科利斯的生日贺词中变得多愁善感：“过去两年里，我生命的大部分时间都用来帮助可怜的墨西哥站起来了……完成这项任务是我每日的祈祷之一。”拉蒙特声称，墨西哥是他每天早晨首先要操心的事，他还经常提到那些等待多年却仍收不到债券利息的寡妇和孤儿。墨西哥债务危机需要圣人般的耐心，以及对失败事业某种浪漫主义的偏好。而拉蒙特正是处理这一任务的理想人选。

In working with Japan, Lamont had some room in which to maneuver. This wasn’t the case with Mexico, where he had close State Department supervision. When it came to Third World countries, Washington more openly exploited American financial power. Secretary of State Hughes opposed diplomatic recognition of Mexico, which had continually threatened powerful U.S. interests there. In 1917, under leftist president Venustiano Carranza, Mexico had enacted a radical constitution, which asserted Mexican ownership of subsoil minerals—a measure denounced as nationalization by American oilmen, who wanted to send in gunboats to repeal it. After Pancho Villa’s troops looted his huge cattle ranch in Mexico, William Randolph Hearst began to editorialize in favor of a Mexican invasion in 1916. Hughes was also bothered by Mexico’s default on foreign debt and its confiscation of American-owned lands. Until these demands were met, Hughes demanded a credit quarantine around Mexico. The House of Morgan was his main instrument for enforcing it.
与日本合作时，Lamont 尚有些回旋余地，但在墨西哥则不然，他受到国务院的严密监督。面对第三世界国家时，华盛顿更公开地利用美国的金融力量。国务卿 Hughes 反对外交承认墨西哥，因为该国不断威胁到美国在那里的巨大利益。1917 年，在左翼总统 Venustiano Carranza 执政下，墨西哥颁布了一部激进宪法，宣称地下矿产归国家所有——美国石油商谴责此举为国有化，甚至想派遣炮舰来废除它。在 Pancho Villa 的部队洗劫了 William Randolph Hearst 位于墨西哥的巨型牧场后，Hearst 从 1916 年起便开始在社论中鼓吹入侵墨西哥。Hughes 还对墨西哥拖欠外债及没收美国人拥有的土地耿耿于怀。在这些要求得到满足之前，Hughes 坚持对墨西哥实施信贷封锁。 摩根家族是他执行这一政策的主要工具。

Like the China consortium meetings, the ICBM meetings were held at 23 Wall Street. It was the same act of ventriloquism: the State Department talked, and Tom Lamont moved his lips. The Mexicans preferred this charade, for it enabled them to bargain with Washington while being spared the public stigma of negotiating with *a gringo* government. Private banks such as Morgans were perfect channels for frank exchanges between Washington and foreign governments.
与银行团会议一样，国际银行家委员会会议也在华尔街 23 号举行。这依然是同样的腹语术：国务院在说话，而汤姆·拉蒙特则在动嘴唇。墨西哥人更喜欢这场装模作样的把戏，因为这让他们既能与华盛顿讨价还价，又不必公开背上跟美国佬政府谈判的骂名。像摩根这样的私人银行，正是华盛顿与外国政府之间坦诚交流的完美渠道。

But while Lamont was rapturously fascinated by Japan, he knew virtually nothing of Mexico, which was thought too wild for tourism. Hence, Lamont acted as proxy for two hundred thousand bondholders whom he never saw, negotiating with a country he never visited. He became a familiar figure in the Mexican press, the personification of American finance. Interviewing him in 1921, a Mexico City correspondent wrote: “He is not the man behind the throne, he is the man on the throne. He is the most clever, the most listened to, the most powerful of the partners of Morgan.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278812)
然而，尽管拉蒙特对日本如痴如醉，他对墨西哥却几乎一无所知——当时人们认为那片土地过于蛮荒，不适合旅游。于是，拉蒙特充当了二十万从未谋面的债券持有人的代理人，与一个他从未踏足的国家进行谈判。他成了墨西哥媒体眼中的熟面孔，美国金融的化身。1921 年，一位墨西哥城记者在采访他时写道：“他不是幕后之人，他就是台上之人。他是摩根合伙人中最聪明、最受重视、也最有权力的一位。”

In 1920, after counterrevolutionaries murdered Carranza, General Álvaro Obregón rose to power. To win recognition from Washington, he embarked on a conciliatory strategy, courting American businessmen, hiring a Washington lobbyist, and distributing favorable literature in the United States. In 1921, when William Randolph Hearst went to tour his vast Mexican properties—which his father had gotten cheaply from the former dictator, Porflrio Díaz—he found Obregón a pleasant surprise. Afterward, he said his properties had been “in continual trouble and turmoil during the several preceding administrations, but have been in complete peace and security during the administration of President Obregón.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278971)
1920 年，反革命分子暗杀卡兰萨后，阿尔瓦罗·奥夫雷贡将军上台执政。为了赢得华盛顿的承认，他采取和解策略，讨好美国商人，聘请华盛顿说客，并在美国散发有利的宣传材料。1921 年，威廉·伦道夫·赫斯特前往巡视其父亲从前独裁者波菲里奥·迪亚斯手中低价购得的墨西哥大片地产时，惊喜地发现奥夫雷贡政府治理有方。他随后表示，自己的地产“在之前几届政府时期一直麻烦不断、动荡不安，但在奥夫雷贡总统执政期间却完全和平稳定”。 [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2278971)

Eager to please American bankers and reestablish Mexican credit, Obregón plied Lamont with invitations to visit Mexico. But Secretary of State Hughes wanted a treaty of friendship and commerce from Obregón and insisted that Lamont stall to increase the pressure. When the bank received alarming reports of rebel troop movements against the president, Lamont told Hughes that if he went to Mexico, it might bolster Obregón’s standing. Hughes relented. In October 1921, Lamont boarded the bank’s private railroad car, Peacock Point, and headed south.
为了讨好美国银行家并重建墨西哥的信用，奥夫雷贡多次邀请拉蒙特访问墨西哥。但美国国务卿休斯希望与奥夫雷贡签订友好通商条约，坚持让拉蒙特拖延行程以施加更大压力。当银行收到关于叛军向总统进攻的惊人报告时，拉蒙特告诉休斯，如果他前往墨西哥，或许能提升奥夫雷贡的地位。休斯最终松口。1921 年 10 月，拉蒙特登上银行专列"孔雀角"，一路向南驶去。

Obregón, a chick-pea farmer from Sonora, was a crafty politician who knew how to temper reforms with authoritarian toughness. To gain peasant support, he would praise revolutionary ideals while scaling back Carranza’s reforms. Lamont found the one-armed general a charming host, friendly, expansive, and not without humor. With Prohibition in the United States, Obregón greeted Lamont and gave a brisk summons for some liquor. “At last, Mr. Lamont, you see you are in a free country,” he said. One detail of the visit riveted Lamont’s attention. Obregón had placed his desk in the middle of a hardwood floor, so he would be able to hear the squeak of an assassin’s footsteps.
奥夫雷贡，这位来自索诺拉的鹰嘴豆农人，是个精明狡黠的政治家，深知如何以威权铁腕调和改革。为了争取农民支持，他会一边高喊革命理想，一边悄然缩减卡兰萨的改革措施。拉蒙特发现这位独臂将军魅力十足，待客热情、豪爽健谈，还不乏幽默感。当时美国正值禁酒令时期，奥夫雷贡迎上拉蒙特便利落地吩咐上酒。“拉蒙特先生，您终于看到，您正身处一个自由的国度，”他说道。此行有一细节令拉蒙特印象深刻：奥夫雷贡把办公桌摆在了硬木地板正中央——这样刺客走近时，地板的吱嘎声便会暴露行踪。

During his talks with the Mexican president, Lamont confronted the dilemma that accompanies every global debt crisis: the victim threatens to default unless he receives more money. What leverage do bankers ultimately have over a defaulting country if not the prospect of new loans? As Lamont later reported to Secretary of State Hughes, Obregón “could not see the advantage of the government’s attempting to live up to its obligations, even in greatly diminished measure, unless, at the same time, it were assured fresh loans upon a large scale.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279266) Lamont was saved from this course by a structural obstacle: the debt was in the form of bonded debt, and capital markets wouldn’t swallow more Mexican bonds; so the lending had built-in limits. Lamont told Obregón that no new loans would be granted until the old ones were at least partly honored. The Mexican replied that their debt should be proportionate to their ability to pay—an argument that will sound drearily familiar to bankers of a later day—and wanted a 50-percent reduction in principal.
在与墨西哥总统会谈时，拉蒙特面临全球债务危机中常见的两难困境：债务人威胁称，除非获得更多资金，否则就会违约。对银行家而言，除了提供新贷款的希望，他们还能用什么筹码来制约一个违约国家呢？正如拉蒙特后来向国务卿休斯报告的那样，奥夫雷贡“看不到政府努力履行义务的好处，哪怕只是大幅削减后的偿还额度，除非同时能确保获得大规模的新贷款。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279266) 一个结构性障碍让拉蒙特免于走上这条路：该债务以债券形式存在，而资本市场已无法消化更多墨西哥债券，因此贷款本身就存在内在限制。拉蒙特告诉奥夫雷贡，除非旧债至少部分清偿，否则不会批准任何新贷款。墨西哥人回应称，他们的债务应与偿还能力相匹配——这一论调在日后的银行家听来会显得无比耳熟——并要求将本金削减 50%。

Lamont began to sense that Obregón had a secret agenda. By holding back customs revenues pledged to the defaulted bonds, Mexico drove down the bonds’ market price. This was convenient, since the government could then use those revenues to buy back depreciated bonds in the marketplace. Lamont thought this a betrayal of bondholders’ trust. At this point, he still insisted that the bonds be redeemed at par. He tried to scare Mexico with arguments that default would make it a pariah in the international marketplace, that it would be unable to secure future loans.
拉蒙特开始察觉到奥布雷贡暗藏心机。墨西哥扣押了本应用于偿付违约债券的海关收入，以此压低债券的市场价格。这可谓一举两得，因为政府随后便能动用这些收入，在市场上回购贬值的债券。拉蒙特认为这是对债券持有人信任的背叛。此时，他仍坚持债券应按面值赎回。他试图用违约将使墨西哥沦为国际市场的弃儿、未来将无法获得贷款等说辞来吓唬对方。

When Lamont left Mexico two days ahead of schedule, he had armed guards posted on the rear platform of his train. It turned out he narrowly escaped harm: when he reached San Antonio, Texas, he learned that his originally scheduled train was attacked by bandits, who planned to kidnap him and demand a half million gold pesos in ransom.[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279459) Back at 23 Wall Street, Lamont received a wire from Jack Morgan expressing disgust with Mexico. Jack thought it a point of family honor to make sure Mexico repaid his father’s 1899 loan: “I did not think any Government of modern times would so frankly proclaim its complete dishonesty or its abandonment of all decent finance or morals. Hope you did not have too trying a time, and congratulate you in getting out before they stole your pocketbook or watch.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279625) Again Jack personalized bank policy abroad, while Lamont assumed a diplomat’s disinterested professionalism and was thus better attuned to the Diplomatic Age.
拉蒙特提前两天离开墨西哥时，他在火车尾部的平台上安排了武装警卫。结果证明他侥幸逃过一劫：到达得克萨斯州圣安东尼奥后，他得知原定乘坐的那趟火车遭遇了匪徒袭击，对方计划绑架他并索要五十万金比索的赎金。 [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279459) 回到华尔街 23 号，拉蒙特收到了杰克·摩根的电报，字里行间满是对墨西哥的厌恶。杰克认为，确保墨西哥偿还父亲 1899 年的贷款是家族荣誉所在：“我没想到任何现代政府会如此公然宣称自己彻底失信，或抛弃一切体面的财政与道德。希望你没有经历太艰难的时刻，恭喜你在他们偷走你的钱包或手表之前脱身。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279625) 杰克再次将银行海外政策个人化，而拉蒙特则秉持外交官般超脱的专业精神，因而更能与外交时代同频共振。

There is a tendency to portray Wall Street bankers of the period as reactionary ogres. In Latin America, they certainly had a bias toward strong, authoritarian regimes. But the weakness wasn’t for totalitarian or *laissez-faire* regimes so much as for stability, whatever its form. Bankers probably had a higher ethical standard than industrialists of the period, as became evident from the contrasting attitudes taken by the House of Morgan and the oil companies in dealing with Mexico.
人们往往将那个时期的华尔街银行家描绘成反动的恶棍。在拉丁美洲，他们确实倾向于支持强势的威权政权。然而，他们并非偏爱极权或自由放任体制，而是更看重稳定——无论这种稳定以何种形式呈现。与当时的实业家相比，银行家可能秉持着更高的道德标准，这一点在摩根家族与石油公司处理墨西哥事务时截然不同的态度中得到了充分体现。

Throughout the twenties, American oilmen tried to persuade bankers to protest the hated 1917 Mexican constitution. They also bristled at higher Mexican export taxes and a government requirement that they obtain concessions on land they thought they owned. Both J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Grenfell had performed underwriting for Standard Oil of New Jersey, and Lamont was badgered by Standard, the Texas Company, and Sinclair Oil to join their campaign against Mexico. By 1921, Mexico was already the world’s biggest oil exporter and a high-priority area for American oilmen.
整个 20 年代，美国石油商一直试图说服银行家们抗议他们深恶痛绝的 1917 年墨西哥宪法。墨西哥提高出口税以及政府要求他们对自己原本视为已有的土地重新申请开采权，也令他们大为光火。J.P.摩根公司和摩根建富银行都曾为新泽西标准石油公司承销过债券，而拉蒙特也一直受到标准石油、德克萨斯公司和辛克莱石油的纠缠，要求他加入他们针对墨西哥的施压行动。到 1921 年，墨西哥已成为世界最大的石油出口国，也是美国石油商眼中的重中之重。

Lamont didn’t want to jeopardize his debt negotiations by entering the murky, often violent strife between the oilmen and Mexico. He performed some perfunctory lobbying for them but generally kept his distance. The oilmen weren’t squeamish about their tactics and didn’t hesitate to trample governments that defied them. After Lamont returned from Mexico in 1921, Walter Teagle, head of Standard Oil, passed along a memo to him from an unnamed Mexican. In a covering note, Teagle said blithely that it “might be of interest to you in a general way.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279817)
拉蒙特不想卷入石油大亨与墨西哥之间那潭浑水——一场常常充满暴力的纷争——以免危及他的债务谈判。他为这些人进行了一些敷衍了事的游说，但总体上保持距离。这些石油大亨对手段毫不讲究，毫不犹豫地践踏任何胆敢违抗他们的政府。1921 年拉蒙特从墨西哥返回后，标准石油公司总裁沃尔特·蒂格尔转交给他一份来自某位未具名墨西哥人的备忘录。在附函中，蒂格尔漫不经心地说，这份东西“或许能让你在泛泛层面上产生点兴趣。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279817)

Preserved in Lamont’s files, the memo is shocking, nothing less than a blueprint for bribing the entire Mexican government. It starts out with a nasty portrayal of the Mexican national character: “The Mexican, and particularly the traditional professional politician of Mexico, after four hundred years of training, is actuated by two dominant motives; one, the fear of force—physical force; the other is the incentive of personal gain. . . . An appeal to patriotism or to idealism is not understood.”
拉蒙特保留的文件中，这份备忘录令人震惊，简直就是一份贿赂整个墨西哥政府的蓝图。备忘录开头对墨西哥的民族性格进行了恶毒的描述：“墨西哥人，尤其是墨西哥那些传统的职业政客，经过四百年的训练，其行为受两种主要动机驱动：一是对武力——身体力量的恐惧；二是对个人利益的追求……爱国或理想主义的呼吁根本无法被理解。”

The nameless author goes on to say that the use of force would be too costly, leaving only pecuniary gain as a motivating force in Mexico. He contended that Obregón was an unwilling captive to party radicals and could not satisfy the needs of his greedily ambitious lieutenants. How to free him from their influence? “This force can only be removed from and returned to the President himself by putting him in such a financial position as to give him dominance. Money will change his cabinet, make over his congress, give him domination over his governors and allow him to abrogate or modify present unsatisfactory laws.”
这位佚名作者进一步指出，在墨西哥，动用武力代价过高，因此金钱利益便成了唯一的驱动力。他认为，奥夫雷贡是党内激进分子不情愿的俘虏，无法满足其副手们贪婪而膨胀的野心。如何让他摆脱这些人的影响？“唯有让总统掌握财政上的主导权，才能将这股力量从他身边剥离并重新归还给他本人。金钱能让他改组内阁、重组国会、压制州长，并有权废除或修改现行不完善的法律。”

To provide Obregón with the necessary funds-—and this is where the House of Morgan came in—the writer of the memo suggested setting up a Mexican bank that would masquerade as a bank for agricultural development, but would exist to place money at Obregón’s personal disposal. The writer concluded that the money, liberally distributed, would achieve miraculous results: “The undesirable elements in his cabinet would be given a certain sum of money and sent to desirable foreign posts. The obstructive radical elements in his congress could be removed. It would soon be seen that the radical *diputado* would become a staid conservative the moment he came into possession of property. . . . Such a bank might well dominate the financial and economic life of Mexico and the American directors of such an institution might well keep in close touch with Washington.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2279993)
为了让奥布雷贡获得所需的资金——这正是摩根大通发挥作用的地方——备忘录的撰写者建议成立一家墨西哥银行，伪装成农业开发银行，但实际上是为奥布雷贡的个人开支提供资金。撰写者总结道，只要资金慷慨散出，就能取得奇迹般的效果：“他内阁中的不良分子可以用一笔钱打发到令人满意的外国职位上。国会中那些碍事的激进分子可以清除掉。很快就能看到，激进派议员一旦拥有财产，就会变成稳健的保守派……这样的银行完全可以主导墨西哥的金融和经济生活，而该机构的美国董事们则可以与华盛顿保持密切联系。”

Lamont’s file shows no reply or follow-up. Perhaps he responded orally. In all likelihood, he was shocked. He may have regarded silence as the most eloquent expression of scorn, or at least the best way to avoid antagonizing an important client. Lamont was no choirboy in politics, but the House of Morgan shied away from blatant skulduggery. The bank had a strict policy against paying so-called fees or commissions and usually reacted to such requests with frigid New England reserve. The Standard Oil memo provides a benchmark for judging the Morgan bank against the dismal standard of American business conduct in Latin America during the 1920s.
拉蒙特的文件显示没有回复或后续跟进。也许他口头回应了。十有八九，他是被震惊到了。他可能认为沉默是最有力的蔑视表达，或者至少是避免得罪重要客户的最佳方式。拉蒙特在政治上并非什么善男信女，但摩根家族对明目张胆的阴谋诡计避之不及。该银行严格禁止支付所谓的费用或佣金，通常以新英格兰式的冷淡克制来回应此类请求。这份标准石油备忘录为我们提供了一个基准，用以评判摩根银行在 20 世纪 20 年代拉丁美洲美国商业行为这一可悲标准下的表现。

The ensuing Mexican debt talks of the early 1920s can be quickly summarized. There were a few fleeting triumphs, always followed by fresh defaults and despair. Lamont’s ingenuity could never win more than a short reprieve. In 1922, he negotiated an agreement with the Mexican finance minister, de la Huerta, that won Obregón the U.S. recognition he had craved. The agreement called for steep concessions by Lamont, including lower interest payments spread over forty-five years. The deal was suspended by early 1924. Among other factors, Mexico was suffering from declining oil production as oil companies vengefully switched to the politically more pliant Venezuela. Another debt agreement was reached in 1925—the initial payment this time was down to a paltry \$10.7 million—but it, too, was soon dead. The bankers who had once boldly insisted on full payment had to settle for ever-smaller fractions of the original loans. When final disposition was made of this Mexican debt later in the decade, Lamont would find himself negotiating not with the Mexicans, but with an unexpectedly resourceful adversary: his own former partner, Dwight W. Morrow, recently appointed ambassador to Mexico.
20 世纪 20 年代初的墨西哥债务谈判可以简要概括如下。期间有过几次短暂的成功，但总是紧接着新的违约和绝望。拉蒙特的才智从未能赢得超过短暂的喘息。1922 年，他与墨西哥财政部长德·拉·韦尔塔达成协议，使奥夫雷贡获得了渴望已久的美国承认。该协议要求拉蒙特做出大幅让步，包括将较低的利息支付分摊至四十五年。但到 1924 年初，该协议就被搁置了。其中一个原因是，由于石油公司报复性地转向政治更顺从的委内瑞拉，墨西哥的石油产量持续下降。1925 年又达成了一项债务协议——这一次的初始付款金额仅为可怜的一千零七十万美元——但同样很快便告吹。曾经强硬坚持全额偿还的银行家们，最终不得不接受原始贷款越来越小比例的清偿。 当这个十年后期最终处理这笔墨西哥债务时，Lamont 发现自己不是在和墨西哥人谈判，而是面对一个意想不到的足智多谋的对手：他以前的老合伙人，Dwight W. Morrow，最近被任命为驻墨西哥大使。

THE Republican evasion of world responsibility presented new opportunities for the House of Morgan. Glorifying entrepreneurs and scorning politicians, the Harding, Coolidge, and Hoover administrations drafted financiers to represent them at economic conferences. This move reflected a 1920s cult in which businessmen were revered as far-sighted problem solvers who could succeed where politicians failed. The new mood suited Morgan partners Tom Lamont, Dwight Morrow, and Russell Leffingwell, who fancied themselves financial diplomats and sometimes joked about their technical ineptitude in more prosaic forms of banking. During the 1920s, Morgan partners spent enormous time at overseas conferences, serving as a legitimate cover for Republican administrations more global-minded than they cared to admit; thus the bank profited from the isolationism it deplored. This was the same use of private proxies that Washington had employed since the days of the first China consortium.
共和党逃避世界责任的行为，为摩根家族打开了新的机遇之门。哈丁、柯立芝和胡佛政府推崇企业家、轻视政客，转而派遣金融家代表他们出席经济会议。这一举措反映了 20 世纪 20 年代的某种狂热——商人被奉为远见卓识的问题解决者，能在政客失败的领域取得成功。这种新风正好契合摩根合伙人汤姆·拉蒙特、德怀特·莫罗和拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔的胃口，他们自诩为金融外交家，有时还会自嘲自己连那些最普通的银行业务都搞不定。整个 20 年代，摩根合伙人花大量时间参加海外会议，为那些其实比他们愿意承认的更具全球视野的共和党政府提供合法掩护；就这样，银行一边谴责孤立主义，一边却从中获利。这正是自第一个中国财团时代以来，华盛顿就一直在使用的私人代理策略。

If private bankers enjoyed new stature, they shared it with central bankers, who assumed new power and autonomy. Beneath the euphoria, the Jazz Age was a despairing time. The populace grew disenchanted with politicians who had led them into war and then squabbled over reparations and postwar security. A clique of western central bankers hoped to transcend this political opportunism and forge a banking elite dedicated to sound economic principles. They espoused free trade and an unrestricted flow of capital, balanced budgets and strong currencies. They saw it as their function to maintain financial standards and prod politicians into painful, necessary reforms.
若私人银行家获得了新的地位，那么央行行长们同样分享了这份荣耀，他们掌握了新的权力与自主权。在表面的欢愉之下，爵士时代实则是一个令人绝望的时期。民众对那些曾将他们卷入战争、随后又在赔款与战后安全问题上争吵不休的政客们日益失望。一批西方央行行长希望超越这种政治投机主义，打造一个专注于稳健经济原则的银行精英群体。他们倡导自由贸易、资本自由流动、预算平衡以及强势货币。他们视维护金融标准、推动政客们进行痛苦但必要的改革为己任。

The American representative of this trend was Benjamin Strong of the New York Federal Reserve Bank. When the Harding and Coolidge administrations disclaimed leadership in postwar European reconstruction, the role devolved upon Strong, who was the Fed’s contact with the central banks of Europe. Strong was solidly in the Morgan mold—a descendant of seventeenth-century Puritans, counting theologians and bank presidents among his ancestors, and the son of a New York Central superintendent. Like his Morgan friends, Strong matched conservative domestic views with a cosmopolitan receptivity to European thought—so much so that Hoover later chided him as a “mental annex of Europe.” Hobbled by a regulation that he couldn’t lend directly to foreign governments, Strong needed a private bank as his funding vehicle. He turned to the House of Morgan, which benefited incalculably from his patronage. In fact, the Morgan-Strong friendship would mock any notion of the new Federal Reserve System as a curb on private banking power. In the 1920s, real power in the system resided at the New York Fed’s new Florentine palazzo on Liberty Street.
这一趋势的美国代表是纽约联邦储备银行的本杰明·斯特朗。当哈丁政府和柯立芝政府拒绝对战后欧洲重建承担领导责任时，这一角色便落到了斯特朗身上，他是美联储与欧洲各国央行沟通的桥梁。斯特朗完全属于摩根式的人物——他是 17 世纪清教徒的后裔，祖先中既有神学家也有银行总裁，父亲则是纽约中央铁路公司的总监。与他的摩根朋友们一样，斯特朗在国内秉持保守观点，同时却以世界主义的姿态接纳欧洲思想——以至于后来胡佛批评他是“欧洲的附属大脑”。受制于不得直接向外国政府提供贷款的规定，斯特朗需要一家私人银行作为融资渠道。他选择了摩根家族，而摩根家族也因他的支持获益无穷。 事实上，摩根与斯特朗的友谊本身就嘲弄了那种认为新成立的美联储体系能约束私人银行权力的观念。20 世纪 20 年代，美联储体系中真正的权力掌握在纽约联储那座位于自由街、新落成的佛罗伦萨风格宫殿里。

Strong was capable of great warmth and sudden anger. Unlike the smooth Morgan partners, he was a moody and troubled man. He was divorced by his second wife and in 1916 contracted tuberculosis, which would keep him from the bank for several months each year. Perhaps in reaction to his personal disappointments, he became passionately devoted to the Fed. He tried to endow it with the Bank of England’s austere, unassailable dignity. A giant presence in American finance, Strong tutored the still green Federal Reserve governors in the art of central banking.
斯特朗既能展现极度热情，也容易突然暴怒。与圆滑的摩根合伙人不同，他是个喜怒无常、内心备受困扰的人。第二任妻子与他离了婚，1916 年他又染上肺结核，此后每年都要因此休养数月。或许是对个人失意的反应，他将全部热情倾注于美联储，试图赋予它如同英格兰银行那般庄重肃穆、无可辩驳的尊严。作为美国金融界的巨擘，斯特朗亲自教导那些尚显青涩的美联储理事们中央银行业务的艺术。

Ben Strong participated in postwar European reconstruction and currency stabilization with his British counterpart, Montagu Norman, governor of the Bank of England after 1920. In Monty, he found a dear friend and alter ego. The divorced Strong and the bachelor Norman plunged into a relationship of such secret intimacy and convoluted intrigue as to arouse fears in both their governments. Taking long vacations together in Bar Harbor, Maine, and southern France, they fortified each other’s distrust of politicians. They shared faith in the gold standard and hoped to create autonomous central banks that could conduct global monetary policy free of political tampering. To their two-man cabal, Strong brought the unmatched financial power of Wall Street, while Norman lent British knowledge and professionalism ripened over many generations. The postwar pound was simply too weak for Norman to conduct unilateral financial diplomacy. After the Treasury embargoed foreign loans to shore up the pound, diverting foreign borrowers to New York, Norman desperately needed a Wall Street link to offset the City’s weakness. He found it in Ben Strong and the House of Morgan.
本·斯特朗与他的英国同行、1920 年后担任英格兰银行行长的蒙塔古·诺曼共同参与了战后欧洲重建与货币稳定工作。在蒙蒂身上，他找到了一位挚友和另一个自我。离异的斯特朗与未婚的诺曼之间发展出一种隐秘亲密、盘根错节的关系，甚至引起了两国政府的担忧。他们一起在缅因州巴尔港和法国南部长时间度假，互相坚定了对政客的不信任。两人都笃信金本位，希望建立能独立实施全球货币政策、不受政治干预的自主中央银行。在这个二人小团体中，斯特朗带来了华尔街无可匹敌的金融实力，而诺曼则贡献了英国历经数代积累的知识与专业素养。战后的英镑过于疲弱，诺曼根本无法单独开展金融外交。 在财政部为支撑英镑而禁止向外国贷款，将外国借款人引向纽约之后，诺曼急需一个华尔街的联系来弥补伦敦金融城的疲软。他在 Ben Strong 和 House of Morgan 中找到了它。

For twenty-four years, Monty Norman reigned mysteriously in his mahogany office at the Bank of England. He had been perfectly bred for the job. One of his grandfathers was a long-time bank director, and the other was a governor of the bank. He himself came to the bank via the Anglo-American merchant bank of Brown Shipley and Company (Brown Brothers in New York). Many labels have been applied to Norman—madman, genius, hypochondriac, megalomaniac, conspirator, eccentric, visionary—all of which were true. One banker said he resembled “a painting by Van Dyck—tall, pointed goatee, great hat, like a courtier of the Stuarts.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280216) He had a wizard’s face—sharp and chiseled, with pointed nose and beard. Despite—or perhaps to counter—rumors of Sephardic Jewish blood, he was viciously anti-Semitic. As he moved about in funereal black beneath a wide-brimmed hat, he retained a touch of Oriental splendor in the emerald that adorned his tie. Sensitive and high-strung, he often suffered breakdowns or lumbago attacks during currency crises. A suppressed hysteric, he would erupt in tantrums that terrified bank employees and made his rule absolute. His thin smile rarely opened into laughter, as if that might shatter his mystique. A proud *prima donna*, he would say he felt “faint” for “want of food” if he didn’t eat every two hours.
在英格兰银行那间桃花心木办公室里，蒙塔古·诺曼神秘地统治了二十四年。他天生就是为这份工作而生的。他的两位祖父中，一位长期担任银行董事，另一位曾是银行行长。他本人则是通过英美商人银行布朗·希普利公司（纽约的布朗兄弟公司）进入这家银行的。诺曼身上贴着许多标签——疯子、天才、疑病症患者、自大狂、阴谋家、怪人、幻想家——所有这些标签都名副其实。一位银行家形容他“就像范戴克画中的人物——身材高挑，山羊胡尖尖的，大帽子，活像斯图亚特王朝的朝臣。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280216) 他长着一张巫师般的脸——棱角分明，鼻子和胡须都是尖尖的。尽管外界流传着他有塞法迪犹太血统的传闻（或许正是为了对抗这种传闻），他本人却极其反犹。当他戴着宽檐帽、身着葬礼般的黑衣走动时，领带上点缀的祖母绿宝石仍为他保留着一丝东方的华贵气息。 敏感而神经质，他在货币危机期间经常崩溃或腰痛发作。他是个压抑的歇斯底里患者，动不动就大发雷霆，吓得银行员工魂飞魄散，也让他彻底大权独揽。他很少开怀大笑，只有淡淡的微笑，仿佛一笑就会破解他的神秘感。他像个骄傲的戏剧女王，如果每隔两小时不吃东西，就会说自己因“缺少食物”而“头晕”。

One of Norman’s biographers describes him as giving “the appearance . . . of being engaged in a perpetual conspiracy.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280380) This conformed to his sense of central banking, which he approached as a priestly mystery, a rite best conducted in deep shadows. “The Bank of England is my sole mistress,” he said; “I think only of her, and I’ve dedicated my life to her.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280543) For Norman, the central banker was answerable only to higher principles, not to any elected representatives. When challenged, he often cited a favorite Arab proverb: “The dogs may bark, but the caravan moves on.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280703) He received visitors alone, as if his office were a confessional, and he was privy to the inner thoughts of powerful men. Years later, Franklin Roosevelt unnerved him and stripped him of his wizard’s magic by insisting that others be present at their White House meeting. It was Norman who incarnated Washington fears that British financiers were a sophisticated and treacherous lot who gulled innocent Americans.
诺曼的一位传记作者形容他“总给人一种……好像随时在密谋些什么的感觉。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280380) 这与他对中央银行的认知如出一辙——他将其视作一种神秘的神职仪式，只应在暗处进行。“英格兰银行是我唯一的情人，”他说，“我心中只有她，并愿为她奉献一生。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280543) 在诺曼看来，央行行长只对更高准则负责，而非任何民选代表。每当受到质疑，他总爱引用一句阿拉伯谚语：“任凭群犬狂吠，商队依然前行。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280703) 他独自接见来访者，仿佛他的办公室就是告解室，而他则能洞悉权贵们的内心。多年后，富兰克林·罗斯福坚持要求在白宫会面时有其他人在场，这一举动不仅令他不安，更彻底戳破了他的魔法师神话。正是诺曼，活生生印证了华盛顿方面的忧虑——英国金融家们是一群老谋深算、诡计多端的家伙，专门哄骗天真的美国人。

Monty Norman was a natural denizen of the secretive Morgan world. Among old friends, he counted his former classmate Teddy Grenfell and Vivian Hugh Smith of Morgan Grenfell. Brooding and melancholy, he liked Grenfell’s prankish wit, while he was dedicated to Smith for having helped him to overcome doubts about becoming a director of the Bank of England in 1907. Bucking him up, Smith had written, “Of course you will accept and, when you are on the Court, remember that you are as good as they are.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280859) As a solitary bachelor, Norman created a mysterious circle of married female confidantes, including Smith’s wife, Lady Sibyl, the beautiful society suffragette. A follower of theosophy and faith healing, she appealed to Norman’s kooky side. “Through her influence,” says a Norman biographer, “he widened his interest in the esoteric and the occult; for Sybil was emphatic about the crucial importance of religion to a spirit as easily bruised as his.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280993) Lady Sybil would disappear for long, platonic weekends with Monty, who became a godfather to the Smith children. Thus, by happenstance, Morgan Grenfell was extremely close to the most influential central banker of the interwar years.
蒙蒂·诺曼天生就是那个隐秘的摩根世界里的常客。在老朋友中，他视昔日的同学泰迪·格伦费尔和摩根建富的维维安·休·史密斯为知己。他性格沉郁、多愁善感，喜欢格伦费尔那狡黠的幽默，而对史密斯则心怀感激，因为他在 1907 年帮助诺曼克服了成为英格兰银行董事时的犹豫不决。史密斯曾写信给他打气：“当然要接受，等你进了董事会，记住你跟他们一样优秀。”作为一个独身的单身汉，诺曼构建了一个神秘的已婚女性知己圈，其中包括史密斯的妻子——西比尔夫人，这位美貌的上流社会女权运动者。她信奉神智学和信仰疗法，这吸引了诺曼身上古怪的一面。“受她的影响，”一位诺曼传记作者说，“他对神秘学和超自然现象的兴趣更广了；因为西比尔坚信，宗教对他这样一颗脆弱的心灵至关重要。”” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2280993) 西比尔夫人会和蒙蒂一起度过漫长的柏拉图式周末，蒙蒂后来成了史密斯家孩子们的教父。就这样，机缘巧合之下，摩根·格伦费尔与两次世界大战之间最具影响力的中央银行家关系极为密切。

The House of Morgan formed an indispensable part of Norman’s strategy for reordering European economies. America had the means to accomplish the task but was still ambivalent about exercising power in Europe. Even among Morgan partners, there was a reservoir of doubt. Russell Leffingwell, a former Treasury official who became a Morgan partner in 1923, told Basil Blackett of the British Treasury, “We feel that we got you out of a pretty pickle \[during the war\] but we rather think that it is time you were looking out for yourselves. We have never had any taste for world finance and our brief experience has not developed it. We like you and want to see you prosperous and happy and peaceful but we don’t like the game you play nor the way you play it and don’t want to be forced to sit in it.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281152) Norman loved the game. Imperial to the core, he wanted to preserve London as a financial center and the bank as arbiter of the world monetary system. Aided by the House of Morgan, he would manage to exercise a power in the 1920s that far outstripped the meager capital at his disposal.
摩根财团是诺曼重组欧洲经济战略中不可或缺的一环。美国虽有实力完成这项任务，但在欧洲行使权力方面仍显犹豫。就连摩根内部合伙人也心存疑虑。曾在财政部任职、于 1923 年成为摩根合伙人的拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔对英国财政部的巴兹尔·布莱克特坦言：“我们觉得（战争期间）已经帮你们摆脱了困境，但也认为你们该自己照顾自己了。我们从来对世界金融提不起兴趣，短暂的经验也没能培养出这种兴趣。我们喜欢你们，希望看到你们繁荣、幸福与和平，但我们不喜欢你们玩的把戏，也不喜欢你们玩的方式，更不想被迫卷入其中。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281152) 诺曼却乐在其中。他骨子里是个帝国主义者，希望维持伦敦作为金融中心的地位，并让英格兰银行成为世界货币体系的仲裁者。 在摩根财团的协助下，在 20 世纪 20 年代，他成功施展出的影响力，远超过其手中那点微薄资本所能企及的范围。

Norman thought in large, geopolitical terms. He saw the rehabilitation of central Europe as a precondition for restoring prosperity and political order and exempted reconstruction loans from the foreign loan embargo. Through his leadership, Morgans first became involved in Austria. In late 1921, the British sounded out Jack about an Austrian loan, saying its government would furnish Gobelin tapestries as collateral. The next year, Austria’s finance minister, Dr. Kienbock, pleaded with Dean Jay of Morgan, Harjes in Paris (now in plush headquarters on the place Vendôme) for a loan. Kienbock cited famine, misery, and a worthless Austrian schilling. He again asked for a loan backed by tapestries and other *objets d’art*.[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281359) At first, the House of Morgan frowned on this unorthodox request, afraid it would create a “pawn-broking impression”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281510)—even beggars had to come suitably attired to Morgans. Lamont—now known as the Morgan empire’s secretary of state—wondered whether another bank should undertake the loan. He feared that as former banker to the Allies and fiscal agent for England and France, J. P. Morgan and Company was a poor choice and might even engender hostility in Austria.
诺曼思考的是宏观的地缘政治格局。在他看来，复兴中欧是恢复繁荣与政治秩序的前提，因此他豁免了重建贷款，使其不受外国贷款禁令的约束。在他的引领下，摩根财团首次涉足奥地利事务。1921 年底，英国方面向杰克试探奥地利贷款事宜，表示奥地利政府愿以戈布兰挂毯作为抵押品。次年，奥地利财政部长基恩博克博士向巴黎摩根-哈耶斯公司（彼时已迁入旺多姆广场的豪华总部）的迪安·杰伊恳求贷款。基恩博克提及饥荒、苦难以及形同废纸的奥地利先令，再次请求以挂毯及其他艺术品作为担保。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281359) 起初，摩根财团对这种非常规要求颇不以为然，担心会留下“典当行般的印象” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281510) ——毕竟，即便是乞讨者，来摩根也得衣着得体。 拉蒙特——如今被称为摩根帝国的国务卿——在考虑是否应由另一家银行来承担这笔贷款。他担心，作为协约国曾经的银行家以及英法两国的财政代理人，J. P.Morgan and Company 是一个糟糕的选择，甚至可能在奥地利招致敌意。

The Austrian loan was worked out under the auspices of the League of Nations, which aided Monty Norman’s grand design of reconstruction. Impressively wrapped, it was payable in gold coin and backed by Austria’s customs and tobacco monopoly. It was issued simultaneously in several capitals. The \$25-million New York portion was co-managed by J. P. Morgan and Company and Kuhn, Loeb. In retrospect, the League of Nations cachet perhaps gave a specious air of security to a risky venture.
奥地利贷款是在国际联盟的主持下敲定的，国际联盟为蒙特·诺曼的重建大计提供了助力。这笔贷款包装精美，以金币偿付，并以奥地利的关税和烟草专卖权作为担保。它在多个首都同时发行。其中在纽约发行的 2500 万美元部分由 J.P.摩根公司与库恩-洛布公司联合管理。回过头来看，国际联盟的招牌或许给这场冒险之举蒙上了一层虚假的安全感。

Austria led to Germany. By early 1922, Germany was already pleading for relief from onerous reparations payments. The British sympathized, but France kept up its grudge, citing extensive war damage on its soil. (The extraordinary Anne Morgan was gathering hundreds of American women to rebuild French villages and raise money for schools, hospitals, and libraries. As a fund-raiser, her American Friends for Devastated France co-sponsored the July 1921 championship fight between Jack Dempsey and Georges Carpentier.) In the most potent form of default imaginable, the Germans expanded their money supply, ran large budget deficits, and depreciated the mark. This had the fatal side effect of unleashing hyperinflation. The Allies felt betrayed as German monetary policy undermined reparations payments. In January 1923, French and Belgian troops occupied the Ruhr. Irate troops ripped German bank notes from the hands of manufacturers and seized the customs machinery.
奥地利拖累了德国。截至 1922 年初，德国已不堪重负，请求减免沉重的战争赔款。英国对此表示同情，但法国因本土遭受严重战争破坏，依然心怀怨恨。（非凡的安妮·摩根本人正召集数百名美国女性重建法国村庄，并为学校、医院和图书馆筹集资金。作为募捐活动的一部分，她创办的“美国援助法国之友”组织还联合赞助了 1921 年 7 月杰克·登普西与乔治·卡彭铁尔之间的冠军拳击赛。）德国人采取了最具破坏性的违约手段——他们扩大货币供应量，大幅增加预算赤字，并让马克贬值。这带来了致命的副作用：恶性通货膨胀一发不可收拾。德国的货币政策破坏了赔款支付，让协约国感到被背叛。1923 年 1 月，法比联军占领鲁尔区。愤怒的士兵们从制造商手中夺走德国纸币，并没收了海关设施。

Monty Norman warned Ben Strong that occupied German soil was the “black spot” of the world and could ignite another war. Germany remained England’s main trading partner, and Norman saw its revival as the crux of his master scheme for European prosperity. He was also personally attached to Germany, where he had studied music. Washington similarly placed a high priority on German revival. America had ended the war with vastly expanded factory capacity and needed export markets to absorb its surplus. American corporations were also eager to acquire advanced German technology.
蒙蒂·诺曼曾警告本·斯特朗，被占领的德国土地是世界的“黑点”，可能再次引发战争。德国仍是英国的主要贸易伙伴，诺曼认为德国的复兴是他推动欧洲繁荣大计的关键。他个人也对德国怀有感情，曾在那里学习音乐。华盛顿同样高度重视德国的复兴。战争结束时，美国工厂产能大幅扩张，需要出口市场来消化过剩产品。美国企业也急于获取德国的先进技术。

The result was a massive Anglo-American commitment to keeping Germany afloat, with the House of Morgan assigned a central role. As Lamont later wrote, “The British and ourselves regarded Germany as the economic hub of the European universe. We feared that unless Germany were rebuilt and prospered all the surrounding countries of the Continent would likewise languish.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281697) Bankers of an earlier generation would probably never have fretted in this way about the fate of the Western world or thought in such explicitly political terms.
最终结果是美英两国达成了支持德国生存的大规模承诺，摩根财团在其中扮演了核心角色。正如拉蒙特后来写道："英国和我们自己都把德国视为欧洲经济世界的中心枢纽。我们担心，如果德国无法重建并繁荣起来，欧洲大陆所有周边国家也将同样萎靡不振。" [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281697) 早一代的银行家们大概永远不会以这种方式为西方世界的命运忧心忡忡，或者以如此直白的政治思维来思考问题。

The new demands of the Diplomatic Age were graphically seen in Jack Morgan’s *volte-face* on Germany. In 1922, Secretary of State Hughes and Commerce Secretary Herbert Hoover asked Jack to participate—allegedly as a “private citizen”—in a global bankers’ committee in Paris, which was considering an international loan to Germany. Jack, implacable toward Germany, had sworn after the war that the United States should never trade with that country. He and Blumenthal were then winding up their spying forays against German-Jewish bankers on Wall Street. So Jack’s acceptance of Hughes’s suggestion must have been disorienting, especially given the outpouring of publicity he received in Paris. The *New York Herald* reported, “Paris dispatches tell us that J.P. Morgan’s presence at the international bankers’ conference is attracting more attention than has been devoted to any American since President Wilson arrived in the French capital for the Versailles conference. . . . He is a symbol of the tremendous American power that may be used for the rehabilitation of Europe.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281890) Jack handled himself ably and raised valid reservations about a German loan but was obliged to suppress his more extreme private opinions of Germany.
外交时代的新需求在杰克·摩根对德国态度的彻底转变中得到了生动体现。1922 年，国务卿休斯和商务部长赫伯特·胡佛邀请杰克——据称是以“私人公民”身份——参加巴黎的一个全球银行家委员会，该委员会当时正在考虑向德国提供一笔国际贷款。曾对德国毫不妥协的杰克，战后曾发誓美国绝不应再与这个国家做生意。当时，他正与布卢门撒尔一起结束对华尔街德裔犹太银行家的间谍活动。因此，杰克接受休斯的建议一定令人摸不着头脑，尤其是他在巴黎获得的铺天盖地的关注。《纽约先驱报》报道称：“巴黎消息告诉我们，J.P.摩根出席国际银行家会议所吸引的关注，比自威尔逊总统抵达法国首都参加凡尔赛会议以来任何美国人都要多……” “他是美国强大力量的象征，这股力量可以用于欧洲的复兴。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2281890) 杰克处理得当，对德国贷款提出了合理的保留意见，但不得不压抑自己私下对德国更为极端的看法。

From now on, Jack would be the sober financial statesman in public, the confirmed foe of Germany in private. After the Ruhr occupation, he fired off a letter to Hughes condemning it. In eloquent terms, he told Clarence Barron that the Allies shouldn’t strip Germany of hope by confiscating all its earnings through reparations. Yet his *personal* correspondence reveals the old demonology of the Hun. To Grenfell, he wrote, “I must say that it begins to look to me as though France is really talking to Germany in the only language that the Germans understand.” Of Germany’s state of mind, he added, “it calls for the whip and not for conversations.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282038)
从此以后，杰克在公开场合是冷静的金融政治家，私下里则是坚定的反德分子。鲁尔区被占领后，他立即致信休斯予以谴责。他用雄辩的言辞告诉克拉伦斯·巴伦，协约国不应通过赔款没收德国的全部收益，从而剥夺其希望。然而，他的私人信件却暴露了那种老一套的“匈奴恶魔论”。他写信给格伦费尔说：“我必须说，在我看来，法国似乎真的在用德国人唯一能懂的语言与德国对话。”至于德国的心态，他补充道：“它需要的是鞭子，而不是对话。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282038)

Meanwhile, German inflation worsened. The government was printing so much money that newspaper presses were commandeered. Thirty paper mills worked around the clock to satisfy the need for bank notes. Prices soared so fast that wives would meet their husbands at factory gates, collect their wages, and then rush off to shop before the next round of price increases. In January 1922, about two hundred marks equaled one dollar. By November 1923, it took over four *billion* marks to buy a dollar. A stamp on a letter to America cost a billion marks. At the end, in a final absurdity, prices doubled hourly.
与此同时，德国的通货膨胀进一步恶化。政府大量印钞，甚至征用了印刷报纸的机器。三十家造纸厂昼夜不停地运转，以满足纸币的需求。物价飞涨，以至于妻子们会在工厂门口等候丈夫，拿到工资后立刻冲去购物，赶在下一轮涨价之前。1922 年 1 月，大约 200 马克兑换 1 美元。到了 1923 年 11 月，买 1 美元需要超过 40 亿马克。寄往美国的一封信上的邮票就要花费 10 亿马克。最后，荒诞至极，物价每小时翻一番。

To restore Germany, a new conference was summoned in early 1924. Again the House of Morgan represented the Coolidge administration, which kept up a bogus air of indifference. In fact, Charles Evans Hughes was very disturbed by reports of starving children and mounting extremism in Germany. As the “private” American representatives to the conference, Hughes chose two people close to J. P. Morgan and Company—Owen Young, chairman of General Electric, and General Charles Gates Dawes, the lone Chicago banker to join the Anglo-French loan of 1915. The German problem was so fraught with risk that upon departing for Europe, Dawes joked, “Oh, well, somebody has to take the garbage or the garlands.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282240) The fiction was maintained that these businessmen were just plain private citizens.
为重建德国，1924 年初召开了一次新的会议。摩根财团再次代表柯立芝政府出席，而美国政府则摆出一副假惺惺的漠不关心姿态。事实上，查尔斯·埃文斯·休斯对德国儿童饥饿蔓延、极端主义抬头的报告深感不安。作为出席此次会议的“民间”美方代表，休斯挑选了两位与 J.P.摩根公司关系密切的人物——通用电气董事长欧文·杨，以及 1915 年唯一参与英法贷款的芝加哥银行家查尔斯·盖茨·道斯将军。德国问题充满了风险，以致道斯在启程前往欧洲时打趣道：“唉，总得有人去收拾烂摊子，或者接受赞誉吧。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282240) 这种虚构依然维持着：这些商人不过是普通的普通公民。

This conference generated the Dawes Plan to settle Germany’s problems. It was full of financial ingenuity and political hazard. It scaled back reparations and tied them to Germany’s capacity to pay. It also stipulated that the Allies would select an agent general to preside over Germany’s economy and reparations transfers. This effectively placed Germany in international receivership. (And many reparation payments were funneled through the Morgan bank.) Germany was mortgaged to the Allies, with its railways and central bank subjected to foreign control, a situation that would provide a propaganda bonanza for the Nazis.
这次会议制定了解决德国问题的道威斯计划，其中充满了金融上的巧思和政治上的风险。计划减少了赔款数额，并将其与德国的支付能力挂钩。同时还规定，由协约国选出一位总代理，负责掌管德国经济及赔款移交事宜。这实际上使德国陷入了国际接管状态。（而且许多赔款款项都是通过摩根银行输送的。）德国被抵押给了协约国，其铁路和中央银行均受外国控制——这种局面为纳粹党提供了绝佳的宣传素材。

Aside from a stipulation ensuring that it would get back the Ruhr, what reconciled Germany to the Dawes Plan was the prospect of a giant loan floated in New York and Europe. Reparations would largely be paid with borrowed money. With Germany now a financial outcast, bankers everywhere were dubious about the loan’s chances. Montagu Norman mused, “It can only be accomplished, if at all, through the Bank of England and in New York through J.P.M. &. Co.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282404) Once again, the State Department was a guiding, if unseen, presence. Hughes told the House of Morgan that it would be a “disaster” and “most unfortunate” if the Dawes Plan miscarried for lack of American participation. Such official wishes were never lightly ignored.
除了确保能收回鲁尔区这一条款外，让德国接受道威斯计划的，是在纽约和欧洲发行一笔巨额贷款的前景。赔款将主要用借来的钱支付。此时德国已是金融弃儿，各地银行家对贷款前景都持怀疑态度。蒙塔古·诺曼沉思道：“此事若想办成，唯有通过英格兰银行，在纽约则需借助 J.P.摩根公司。”国务院再次扮演了幕后推手的角色。休斯告诉摩根家族，如果道威斯计划因美国不参与而失败，那将是“一场灾难”和“极为不幸”。官方的这种意愿从来不会被轻易忽视。

To help along the prospective German loan, Monty Norman arranged a mid-1924 meeting at the Bank of England between Jack Morgan, Tom Lamont, and the new Reichsbank president, Dr. Hjalmar Horace Greeley Schacht (whose father had once worked in a New York brewery and been an admirer of publisher Horace Greeley). To stop the ruinous inflation, Dr. Schacht abolished the old mark and issued a new renten-mark, making him an instant hero in the banking world and winning him the Reichsbank post. On New Year’s Eve of 1924, he arrived in London for Bank of England talks. As he disembarked from the train at Liverpool Street Station, he later recalled, “I was not a little surprised to see . . . a tall man with a pointed grayish beard and shrewd, discerning eyes, who introduced himself as Montagu Norman, governor of the Bank of England.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282565) This began another of Norman’s close, mysterious friendships.
为了促成这笔可能的德国贷款，蒙蒂·诺曼安排杰克·摩根、汤姆·拉蒙特与新任德国国家银行行长希尔马·霍勒斯·格里利·沙赫特博士（他父亲曾在纽约一家啤酒厂工作，是出版商霍勒斯·格里利的崇拜者）于 1924 年年中在英格兰银行会面。沙赫特博士为遏制恶性通货膨胀，废除了旧马克，发行了新地租马克，这使他迅速成为银行界的英雄，并赢得了德国国家银行行长的职位。1924 年除夕，他抵达伦敦参加英格兰银行的会谈。他后来回忆道，当他在利物浦街火车站下车时，“我颇感惊讶地看到……一位高个子、留着尖尖的灰白胡子、目光精明敏锐的人，他自我介绍说是英格兰银行行长蒙塔古·诺曼。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282565) 这开启了诺曼另一段亲密而神秘的交情。

In our narrative, we shall see Schacht playing many different roles-—evil genius of Nazi finance, daring plotter against Hitler, boisterously self-righteous defendant at Nuremberg—but we first encounter him at a moment of glory. Under Schacht, the Reichsbank was freed from government control, extending Norman’s dream of banker autonomy in Europe. A brilliant, narcissistic windbag prone to extravagant metaphors and bombast, Schacht assured Morgan and Lamont that the Dawes loan would be repaid. He obsequiously remarked that the American offering “would fail completely if it lacked the prestige and moral endorsement of the Morgan bank.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282742) For J. P. Morgan and Company, it was critical that the loan take priority over other claims on Germany. The bank had no outstanding German loans and was only being drawn in under political pressure by Britain and France—a fact that would be loudly repeated when the loan defaulted in the 1930s. Then, in a very different political environment, Lamont would bitterly remind Schacht of his unctuous pledges.
在我们的叙述中，将会看到沙赫特扮演着多重角色——纳粹金融界的邪恶天才、大胆的密谋推翻希特勒者、纽伦堡法庭上自以为是、喋喋不休的被告——但我们初次与他相遇时，正是他风光无限的时刻。在沙赫特的领导下，德意志帝国银行摆脱了政府控制，将诺曼关于欧洲银行家自治的梦想进一步发扬光大。这位才华横溢、自恋成性、爱用夸张比喻和豪言壮语的吹嘘者，向摩根和拉蒙特保证道威斯贷款定会如期偿还。他阿谀奉承地表示，美国这笔债券“若没有摩根银行的声望和道德背书，必将彻底失败”。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282742) 对 J.P.摩根公司而言，至关重要的是这笔贷款必须优先于德国其他债务。该银行此前并未涉足德国贷款业务，这次完全是迫于英法两国的政治压力才被拉下水——这一事实在 1930 年代贷款违约时，将被反复高声提起。 随后，在截然不同的政治环境下，拉蒙特意有所指地提醒沙赫特，别忘了他当年那些虚情假意的承诺。

To give the loan international seasoning, half the issue appeared in New York and the other half in London and other European capitals. The \$110-million New York portion was enthusiastically received and oversubscribed. By seeming to settle the German question, the loan lifted a weight from financial markets. It electrified Wall Street and spurred foreign lending to Latin America and elsewhere. For Weimar Germany, it was a turning point. It became the decade’s largest sovereign borrower. American capital and companies poured in: Ford, General Motors, E. I. Du Pont, General Electric, Standard Oil of New Jersey, and Dow Chemical. Unemployment plunged and Germany’s economic slide was reversed into a five-year upturn. This revival would provide Adolf Hitler with a splendid industrial machine and the money to finance massive rearmament. In the meantime, the world was trapped in a circular charade in which American money paid to Germany was handed over as reparations payments to the Allies, who sent it back to the United States as war debt.
为了使这笔贷款具有国际色彩，一半债券在纽约发行，另一半则在伦敦及其他欧洲主要城市发售。纽约发行的 1.1 亿美元债券受到热烈追捧，认购远超预期。这笔贷款看似解决了德国问题，从而为金融市场卸下了一块重担。它令华尔街为之一振，并刺激了对拉丁美洲及其他地区的海外放贷。对魏玛德国而言，这是一个转折点——它由此成为该十年间最大的主权借款国。美国资本与企业蜂拥而至：福特、通用汽车、杜邦、通用电气、新泽西标准石油、陶氏化学。失业率急剧下降，德国经济的下滑势头得以逆转，开启了长达五年的上升期。这场复兴为阿道夫·希特勒提供了强大工业机器和资金，用以支撑大规模重整军备。 与此同时，世界陷入了一个循环的闹剧：美国支付给德国的钱，被德国转手作为赔款交给协约国，而协约国又将其作为战争债务送回美国。

Most remarkable in Morgan archives is the partners’ skepticism of the Dawes Plan. Russell Leffingwell, now the resident economist, saw the scheme as riddled with dangerous contradictions. Why would investors have faith in a politically neutered Germany? And why did the Allies wish to resurrect their former foe? Prescient, he feared a political backlash, a day of reckoning: “My political doubt about Germany is how long her people will consent to be sweated for the benefit of her former enemies.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282932) Montagu Norman and Philip Snowden, chancellor of the Exchequer, also feared Germany had submitted under duress and would later resent its position.
摩根档案中最引人注目的是合伙人们对道威斯计划（Dawes Plan）的怀疑。现任常驻经济学家拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔（Russell Leffingwell）认为该方案充斥着危险的矛盾。投资者凭什么相信一个政治上被阉割的德国？协约国又为何希望复活昔日的敌人？他颇有先见之明地担忧政治反弹和清算之日的到来：“我对德国的政治疑虑在于：其人民还能忍受多久被榨干以造福昔日敌人的日子？” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2282932) 蒙塔古·诺曼（Montagu Norman）和财政大臣菲利普·斯诺登（Philip Snowden）也担心德国是在胁迫下屈服的，日后必会对其处境心生怨恨。

In August 1923, President Warren G. Harding had died of an embolism. His successor, Calvin Coolidge, wasn’t any more enlightened about the problem of world debt. He was adamant that the Allies should pay their war debts—“They hired the money, didn’t they?” he asked—and kept up the fiction that those debts had nothing to do with reparations.[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283144) But so long as the United States demanded war-debt payments, the Allies couldn’t be flexible on German reparations.
1923 年 8 月，沃伦·G·哈定总统因栓塞去世。他的继任者卡尔文·柯立芝对世界债务问题并未表现出更开明的态度。他坚称协约国必须偿还战争债务——“他们借了钱，难道不是吗？”他问道——并维持着这些债务与赔款毫无关系的虚构说法。 [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283144) 但只要美国要求偿还战争债务，协约国就无法在德国赔款问题上灵活处理。

A final aspect of the reparations problem was Morgan involvement in the contest for Germany’s new economic czar, the agent general. Amid much hoopla, the press labeled the job the world’s most important, since the occupant would supervise the German economy. He would have to extract the last penny from Germany while staving off renewed inflation. Hoping the United States would exert a moderating influence, Germany wanted an American for the post. On Wall Street, a powerful consensus formed behind Morgan partner Dwight Morrow.
赔款问题的最后一个方面是摩根财团参与争夺德国经济新总管——代理总长一职。在一片喧嚣中，媒体称这个职位是全世界最重要的，因为任职者将监督德国经济。他必须在德国榨取出最后一分钱，同时防止通货膨胀再次抬头。德国希望美国能发挥调节作用，因此想要一个美国人担任此职。在华尔街，各方形成了强有力的共识，支持摩根合伙人德怀特·莫罗。

An old friend of President Coolidge, Morrow had already been widely touted for numerous government posts. Short, bespectacled, and bookish, he was Morgan’s philosopher-king, a man marked for an elusive greatness. Now his moment arrived. He had formidable supporters—Jack Morgan, Charles Dawes, and Owen Young in the private sector; Hughes and Hoover in the cabinet. After a long White House meeting in July 1924, he seemed like a shoo-in. Among other things, the White House thought Morrow’s appointment would guarantee the success of the Dawes loan.
柯立芝总统的老朋友莫罗，此前早已被广泛看好出任多个政府要职。他身材矮小，戴着一副眼镜，满身书卷气，是摩根的哲人王，一个注定成就非凡却难以捉摸的人物。如今，他的时刻来临了。他有着强大的支持者——私营部门的杰克·摩根、查尔斯·道斯和欧文·杨；内阁中的休斯和胡佛。1924 年 7 月，在一次白宫长谈之后，他看起来已是十拿九稳。此外，白宫认为，莫罗的任命将确保道斯贷款的成功。

The next evening, however, at another White House meeting, the U.S. ambassador to Germany, Alanson Houghton, argued against Morrow’s appointment. He said the choice of a Morgan partner would be incendiary in German politics, even fatal to the Dawes Plan. The long, heated meeting ran till midnight. It was difficult for Coolidge to reverse the appointment of a close friend, but the Morrow nomination was nonetheless spiked. As Dawes afterward explained, “Houghton, with great earnestness, pointed out that the appointment of a member of the firm of Morgan &. Co. would probably enable the Nationalists in Germany to defeat the Republican Government there by raising the demagogic cry that it was a scheme of the international bankers to crush the life out of Germany instead of helping her. He gave this as the private opinion of the German Government itself.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283332)
次日晚上，在白宫的另一场会议上，美国驻德国大使阿兰森·霍顿对莫罗的任命提出了反对意见。他指出，选择一位摩根（Morgan）合伙人将在德国政坛引发轩然大波，甚至可能葬送道威斯计划。这场激烈而漫长的会议一直持续到午夜。虽然柯立芝很难驳回一位密友的任命，但莫罗的提名最终还是被否决了。正如道威斯后来解释的那样：“霍顿极其恳切地指出，任命摩根公司的成员，可能会让德国民族主义者利用煽动性的口号——声称这是国际银行家压榨德国而非帮助德国的阴谋——来推翻共和政府。他声称这是德国政府本身的私下看法。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283332)

Other analysts saw less strategic cunning than cowardice behind Coolidge’s desertion of his old friend. Because of its wartime role, the House of Morgan was still anathema in German-American communities of the Midwest. Coolidge’s aides apparently warned him to avoid any Morrow link. This episode shows that the Morgan bank carried serious political liabilities even in a decade dominated by conservative Republican administrations.
其他分析人士认为，柯立芝抛弃老朋友背后更多是出于懦弱，而非策略上的精明。由于在战时扮演的角色，摩根银行在中西部的德裔美国人社区仍是众矢之的。柯立芝的助手显然警告过他，要避免与莫罗有任何关联。这件事表明，即使在保守派共和党政府主导的这十年里，摩根银行也背负着沉重的政治负担。

Bitterly disappointed, Lamont and Norman demanded a Morrow clone. The dark horse who emerged victorious was a future Morgan partner, thirty-two-year-old S. Parker Gilbert. Tall and boyish, dubbed the Thinking Machine, he was a protege of Russell Leffingwell, whom he had replaced as assistant secretary of the Treasury in 1920, becoming the *wunderkind* of the department. At twenty-eight, he was elevated to under secretary, and in the absence of Treasury Secretary Andrew Mellon, he ran the department—the youngest person ever to do so. Paul Warburg described him as a “practical young man with the eyes of a dreamer and the sensitive mouth of a scholar.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283467) The Germans would see him far less poetically. Heinrich Kohler, the finance minister, described him thus: “Reserved and taciturn, the tall lanky man with the impenetrable features appeared considerably older than he really was and . . . made an eerie impression.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283657)
拉蒙特和诺曼大失所望，要求再找一个莫罗那样的人。最终胜出的黑马是一位未来的摩根合伙人，三十二岁的 S.帕克·吉尔伯特。他身材高挑，一脸孩子气，被称为“思考机器”，是拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔的门生。1920 年，他取代莱芬韦尔成为助理财政部长，一跃成为该部门的奇才。二十八岁时，他升任副部长，在财政部长安德鲁·梅隆缺席期间，他负责管理整个部门——是有史以来最年轻的一位。保罗·沃伯格形容他是“一个务实的年轻人，有着梦想家的眼睛和学者般敏感的嘴唇”。 [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283467) 德国人眼中的他则远没有那么诗意。财政部长海因里希·科勒这样描述他：“这个沉默寡言、身材瘦长、表情深不可测的男人，看起来比他实际年龄老得多……给人一种诡异的印象。” [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283657)

During his five years in Berlin, Gilbert oversaw the transfer of \$2 billion in German reparations. As Germany’s economic czar, he was burned in effigy at mock coronations and vilified as a new kaiser. He apparently never learned to speak German and worked compulsively, never attending cultural events or entering into German society. Despite his youth, he was a stern taskmaster, constantly accusing the Germans of fiscal extravagance. He thought they could pay reparations by following sound fiscal policy. Another finance minister, Paul Moldenhauer, noted, “He spoke with a mixture of awkwardness and arrogance, mumbling the words so that one could hardly understand his English.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283841) But Gilbert’s reports on Germany’s financial conditions would be models of lucidity and precision, winning him a tremendous reputation in Anglo-American financial circles; he would be a figure of worldwide influence in the twenties.
在柏林任职的五年间，吉尔伯特经手了二十亿美元的德国赔款转移事宜。作为德国的经济沙皇，他曾在模拟加冕礼上被焚烧模拟像示众，还被骂作新皇帝。他显然从未学过德语，只埋头工作，既不参与文化活动，也不融入德国社会。尽管年纪轻轻，他却是个严厉的工头，动不动就指责德国人财政挥霍无度。他坚信只要奉行稳健的财政政策，德国就能赔得起钱。另一位财政部长保罗·莫尔登豪尔这样评价道："他说话时既别扭又傲慢，含含糊糊地嘟囔，让人几乎听不清他说的英语。" [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283841) 但吉尔伯特关于德国财政状况的报告却堪称清晰精准的典范，为他赢得了英美金融界的极高声望；在二十年代，他成了全球范围内举足轻重的人物。

Dwight Morrow didn’t long regret his loss and felt that he had been spared a burden. He was soon writing to Hughes and confessing to doubts about the Dawes Plan. Even as the world celebrated the great triumph, there was an undercurrent of deep unease at the House of Morgan. Morrow declared: “It is the foreign control to which Germany is to be subjected that has made us somewhat fearful about the permanent success of the Dawes Plan. . . . It is almost inevitable that this loan will be unpopular in Germany after a few years. The people of Germany, in our opinion, are almost certain, after sufficient time has elapsed, to think not of the release of the Ruhr but of the extent to which what was once a first-class Power has been subjected to foreign control. ”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283967) The fear was prophetic, for it became a cardinal tenet of Nazi propaganda that Germany had been stampeded into the Dawes plan by international bankers. And the House of Morgan would reap the fruits of these mistaken policies of the twenties.
德怀特·莫罗很快便不再为他的失利而懊悔，反而觉得卸下了一副重担。他随即写信给休斯，坦言对道威斯计划心存疑虑。正当全世界为这一伟大胜利欢呼时，摩根财团内部却暗流涌动，深感不安。莫罗宣称：“正是德国将要遭受的外国控制，让我们对道威斯计划的长期成功感到担忧……几乎可以肯定，几年后这笔贷款将在德国不得人心。在我们看来，德国人民在足够长的时间过去后，几乎必然不会想到鲁尔区的解放，而是会想到一个曾经的一流强国在多大程度上陷入了外国控制。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2283967) 这一担忧颇具先见之明，因为后来纳粹宣传的核心教条之一，就是德国被国际银行家胁迫接受了道威斯计划。 而摩根家族将自食 20 年代这些错误政策带来的苦果。
