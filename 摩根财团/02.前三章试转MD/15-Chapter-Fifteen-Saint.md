# Chapter Fifteen: Saint

DWIGHT Whitney Morrow vied with Tom Lamont for honors as the chief Morgan statesman and theoretician. His twenties celebrity owed much to his friendship with the president. When Coolidge took office, reporters flocked to Morrow for comments and speculated on which high post he would occupy. The two had been close friends in Amherst’s class of 1895, boarding together for a year. They both remembered the day when they sat on a hilltop and fantasized about their future. According to legend, in their senior class everybody voted Morrow the Most Likely to Succeed—except Morrow, who voted for Coolidge. “Coolidge in college was a quiet, unassuming man,” Morrow later said, and he was one of the few to penetrate the president’s reserve.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296836)
德怀特·惠特尼·莫罗与汤姆·拉蒙特争相成为摩根家族的首席政治家与理论家。他二十多岁时就已声名鹊起，很大程度上归功于他与总统的友谊。柯立芝上任时，记者们蜂拥而至，向莫罗请教看法，并猜测他将出任哪个高位。两人是阿默斯特学院 1895 届的挚友，曾同住一年。他们都记得坐在山顶上畅想未来的那一天。据传，在毕业班里，所有人都投票选莫罗为“最可能成功的人”——唯独莫罗本人投了柯立芝。“柯立芝在大学时是个安静、谦逊的人，”莫罗后来回忆道，而他是少数能穿透总统沉默外壳的人之一。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2296836)

As a student of ancient civilizations, Morrow wanted to clothe the mundane, often sordid world of the twenties in some larger classical dimension. Spearheading the Amherst College Coolidge-for-President Committee in 1920, Morrow viewed his old friend in grandiose terms: “Coolidge is a very unusual man and a strange combination of a transcendental philosopher and a practical politician.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297042) With similar hyperbole, Morrow confided to Lamont, “I think it is a miracle that a man of Coolidge’s type has been produced for this emergency.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297254) Coolidge talked of Morrow no less reverently. Morrow had been a brilliant student, Coolidge said, but without the usual bookworm qualities. “While he was . . . friendly and sympathetic, he was always dignified. . . . He had no element of selfishness. He never strove to excel anyone or defeat anyone.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297415)
作为一名古代文明学者，莫罗希望为二十年代这个平凡甚至常显污浊的世界，披上一层宏大的古典外衣。1920 年，他牵头成立阿默斯特学院库利奇竞选总统委员会，并用夸张的言辞评价这位老友：“库利奇是个非同寻常的人，是超验主义哲学家与务实政治家的奇异结合。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297042) 同样以夸张的口吻，莫罗向拉蒙特吐露：“我认为在这个危急关头能出现库利奇这样的人，简直是个奇迹。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297254) 而库利奇谈起莫罗时也同样满怀敬意。库利奇说，莫罗曾是位才华横溢的学生，却丝毫没有书呆子的习气。“他待人友善且富有同情心，但始终不失庄重……他毫无自私之心，从不试图超越他人或击败他人。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297415)

One suspects that Coolidge cleverly presented Morrow with the scholarly image the latter wanted to see. During the 1920 presidential race, Morrow sent four volumes by the Yale economist William Graham Sumner to Coolidge, who replied, implausibly, from the campaign trail that he had almost finished all four! “I regard his arguments on the whole as sound,” Coolidge said, but added, “I do not think that human existence is quite so much on the basis of dollars and cents as he puts it.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297567) Coolidge, as they say, had Morrow’s number. While Morrow addressed him as “Dear Calvin,” Coolidge usually responded with “Mr. Morrow,” as if he were writing not to an old school pal but to an elderly sage.
有人怀疑，柯立芝巧妙地呈现了莫罗想看到的那种学者形象。在 1920 年总统竞选期间，莫罗送给柯立芝四卷耶鲁经济学家威廉·格雷厄姆·萨姆纳的著作，而柯立芝竟在竞选途中回复说，自己快把这四卷书全读完了！"我认为他的论点总体上是站得住脚的，"柯立芝说，但又补充道，"不过我不觉得人类生活真像他说的那样完全建立在金钱之上。" [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297567) 就像人们常说的，柯立芝早就看透了莫罗。莫罗在信中称呼他"亲爱的卡尔文"，而柯立芝通常回以"莫罗先生"，仿佛他写信的对象不是昔日同窗，而是一位德高望重的长者。

Along with Lamont and Russell Leffingwell, Morrow gave the House of Morgan its patina of culture, its reputation as a home to bankers who wrote essays, gave speeches, joined foreign policy councils, and served on foundation boards. He belonged to a 1920s cult that believed in the wisdom of businessmen as managers of America’s political affairs. Witty, bristling with ideas, the pint-sized Morrow had a professorial air. With penetrating blue eyes and a faraway look, he wore pince-nez and baggy trousers and never fit into the foppish Morgan world. The bank used to post a page at the men’s room door just to remind him to pull up his suspenders when he left. Attending the wedding of Harry Davison’s daughter, he smelled so badly of mothballs that the other partners made him don a fur coat to mask the odor. His sartorial deficiencies, it seems, symbolized a deeper unease in the posh Morgan world of tall, rich, self-confident men.
与拉蒙特和拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔一道，莫罗为摩根家族镀上了一层文化的光环，使它成为银行家们撰写文章、发表演讲、加入外交政策委员会、担任基金会董事的知名所在。他属于 1920 年代的一个群体，坚信商人在管理美国政治事务方面的智慧。这位身材矮小的莫罗机智风趣，满脑子点子，带着一副教授般的气质。他目光锐利，眼神深邃，戴着夹鼻眼镜，穿着宽松的裤子，在浮华的摩根世界里格格不入。银行总得在洗手间门口安排个人，提醒他出来时把背带拉好。参加哈里·戴维森女儿的婚礼时，他身上樟脑丸味重得让其他合伙人不得不让他套上毛皮大衣来掩盖这股味道。他那糟糕的穿着品味，似乎象征着在这个由高大、富有、自信的男人组成的奢华摩根世界里的某种深层不安。

Like many bright, obsessive people, Morrow was notoriously absent-minded. He once spent a dinner at the Lamonts gesturing with a partially eaten olive until Metcalf, the Lamont butler, offered a plate for the well-chewed pit. Everybody at J. P. Morgan told the story about Morrow’s riding the train. When the conductor asked for his ticket, Dwight couldn’t find it and with his hands restlessly searched every pocket. The ticket, it seems, was clenched between his teeth. “I bet you thought I didn’t know it was there,” Morrow said to the conductor. “Actually, I was just chewing off the date.” Once, while taking a bath, he called out to his valet for soap that lathered better; the problem turned out to be not the soap but that he was still wearing his pajamas.
像许多聪明又执着的人一样，莫罗的心不在焉是出了名的。有一次在拉蒙特家吃饭，他手里捏着一颗吃了一半的橄榄不停比划，直到管家梅特卡夫递上一个盘子来接他嚼了很久的橄榄核。摩根集团的每个人都在讲莫罗坐火车的故事。列车员查票时，德怀特翻遍每个口袋都找不到车票——原来票就咬在他自己嘴里。“你肯定以为我不知道票在那儿吧？”莫罗对列车员说，“其实，我只不过是在嚼掉日期。”还有一次洗澡时，他朝男仆喊话要更起泡的肥皂；结果问题不是肥皂，而是他还穿着睡衣。

Like Lamont, Morrow craved something finer than mere banking. He professed ignorance of the business’s technical side and styled himself “a lawyer in a banking firm.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297774) A banker of the Diplomatic Age, he was as much at home in Washington as on Wall Street. He clung to his intellectual ambitions, reading Bryce and Thucydides and writing essays, studded with recondite references, in favor of the League of Nations. He plied Coolidge with books such as Hallam’s *Constitutional* *History of England.* What makes Morrow’s story unique in Morgan annals is that he never entirely renounced his youthful goals or political ambitions and saw his partnership as a springboard.
像 Lamont 一样，Morrow 渴望比单纯的银行业务更美好的事物。他自称对银行业的技术层面一无所知，并自诩为“一家银行公司里的律师”。 [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297774) 作为外交时代的银行家，他在华盛顿就像在华尔街一样如鱼得水。他坚守着自己的学术抱负，阅读 Bryce 和 Thucydides 的著作，撰写文章，文中充满晦涩的引用，以支持国际联盟。他向 Coolidge 推荐诸如 Hallam’s Constitutional History of England 这样的书籍。Morrow 的故事在摩根家族史上独一无二之处在于，他从未完全放弃自己年轻时的目标或政治野心，而将自己的合伙关系视为一块跳板。

There is pathos in Dwight Morrow’s journey from a poor Pittsburgh home to the summit of world finance, and the story of his boyhood makes for uncomfortable reading. His father was a high-school principal and supported the family with difficulty. Pale and sickly, Dwight inherited both his father’s reverence for education and his fear of poverty. After he graduated from high school, at fourteen, he worked as an errand boy for four years until he was old enough to go to college. He attended Amherst with the aid of a student loan and wore hand-me-down shirts given him by Jacob Schiff’s son Mortimer. To make ends meet, he tutored other students. He practiced such austerity that he shared a pipe with a roommate to save money. After graduating from Columbia Law School, he landed a job at the Wall Street firm of Reed, Simpson, Thacher, and Barnum, which specialized in utility law. Within seven years he was a partner in the firm, now called Simpson, Thacher, and Bartlett. Living in Englewood, New Jersey, he locked umbrellas with Harry Davison in the rain one day and befriended Tom Lamont along the way. The two Morgan partners recruited him in 1914.
德怀特·莫罗从匹兹堡贫寒之家一路走到世界金融之巅，这段历程透着几分悲怆。他少年时代的往事读来让人心绪难平。父亲是一所中学的校长，勉强维持全家生计。德怀特从小面色苍白、体弱多病，既继承了父亲对教育的崇敬，也遗传了他对贫穷的恐惧。十四岁高中毕业后，他当了四年杂役，才长到可以上大学的年纪。他靠着学生贷款进入阿默斯特学院，穿着雅各布·希夫的儿子莫蒂默送给他的旧衬衫。为了维持生计，他还给其他学生做家教。他过得极为节俭，甚至和室友共用一只烟斗来省钱。从哥伦比亚法学院毕业后，他在华尔街的里德、辛普森、撒切尔与巴纳姆律师事务所找到一份工作，这家事务所专攻公用事业法律。短短七年之内，他就成为了这家后来更名为辛普森、撒切尔与巴特利特律师事务所的合伙人。 他住在新泽西州恩格尔伍德，某日雨中与哈里·戴维森共伞同行，并因此结识了汤姆·拉蒙特。1914 年，这两位摩根合伙人将他招入麾下。

To become a Morgan partner was then a national event, and Morrow’s mother was mobbed by well-wishers in the Pittsburgh streets. But after his first day, Dwight admitted to his wife, Betty, that he felt “pretty lonely and blue all day.” To a friend he confessed that he felt “like a cat in a strange garret.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297935) This was partly beginner’s jitters, but the uneasiness never entirely deserted him.
当时，成为摩根合伙人堪称全国瞩目的大事，莫罗的母亲在匹兹堡街头被祝福者团团围住。但第一天工作结束后，德怀特向妻子贝蒂坦言，自己“整天都感到格外孤单和沮丧。”他对一位朋友袒露心声，觉得自己“像一只误闯陌生阁楼的猫。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2297935) 这固然有初来乍到的紧张，但那份不安从未真正消散。

Morrow performed spectacularly at 23 Wall and mastered every subject through sheer diligence. He mutualized the Equitable Life Assurance Society and oversaw Morgan lending to Cuba. He also masterminded Kennecott Copper, a public company formed around the Morgan-Guggenheim syndicate in Alaska and other properties. Daniel Guggenheim was awed by Morrow’s retentive brain and said that “six months after Morrow had started upon his investigation, he knew more about copper than I or any of my six brothers.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298069) In his absentminded way, however, Dwight neglected one detail of the Kennecott operation: “You have forgotten to provide for our commission,” Davison gently chided him.[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298210)
莫罗在华尔街 23 号表现惊艳，凭借超乎常人的勤奋精通了每一个科目。他成功将公平人寿保险互助化，并监督了摩根对古巴的贷款业务。他还策划了肯尼科特铜业公司——这家上市公司围绕摩根-古根海姆在阿拉斯加及其他地区的资产组建而成。丹尼尔·古根海姆对莫罗过目不忘的头脑深感敬佩，坦言：“莫罗着手研究六个月后，对铜的了解就超过了我和我六个兄弟中的任何一个。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298069) 然而，德怀特心不在焉的毛病却让他忽略了肯尼科特业务的一个细节：“你忘了给我们留佣金，”戴维森温和地揶揄道。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298210)

Morrow was always torn between idealism and materialism. One life couldn’t encompass his dreams, and he was tormented and made extremely nervous by the choices he faced. He and his wife traveled in monied circles and were frequent guests of Pierre du Pont at Longwood in Pennsylvania, with its fountains, conservatories, and ten-thousand-pipe organ. Yet they felt misplaced in this rich world. While still at Simpson, Thacher, Dwight would have pangs of Puritan guilt and say, “This, Betsey, is not the life for you or me.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298344) Together they daydreamed of how they would save up \$100,000, and Dwight would then teach history and Betty write poetry—Betty Morrow was a Smith graduate and a poet whose work appeared in *Harper’s* and *Scribner’s Magazine.* They could never acknowledge their overpowering ambition.
莫罗始终在理想主义与物质主义之间挣扎。一世的时光无法承载他的梦想，而他被面前的选择折磨得极度焦虑。他与妻子出入上流社会，时常作为皮埃尔·杜邦（Pierre du Pont）的座上宾，造访宾夕法尼亚的长木花园（Longwood），那里有喷泉、温室，还有一架万管风琴。然而，他们在这个富贵圈中却感到格格不入。还在 Simpson, Thacher, Dwight 律所工作时，德怀特偶尔会泛起清教徒式的负罪感，说道：“贝琪，这种生活不适合你，也不适合我。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298344) 他们一起白日做梦，想着如何攒下十万美元，然后德怀特去教历史，贝琪去写诗——贝琪·莫罗是史密斯学院的毕业生，也是一位诗人，作品曾发表在《哈珀斯》（Harper’s）与《斯克里布纳杂志》（Scribner's Magazine）上。他们始终无法承认自己那难以抑制的野心。

Morrow’s conflicts gnawed at him even in his sleep. One night, he bolted up in terror from a nightmare. “I dreamt, Betsey, that we had become rich,” he explained. “But *enormously* rich.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298479) Offered a Morgan partnership, he went through “weeks of acute spiritual crisis,” according to his biographer, Sir Harold Nicolson. While pondering his choice in Bermuda, Morrow saw a malicious cartoon that showed a vulturelike Jack Morgan feasting upon the innards of New Haven shareholders. This libel persuaded him to take the Morgan job, he said, and the bank’s defense of its New Haven financing was his first assignment. Morrow used lofty rhetoric to justify actions prompted by lesser motives. After accepting the Morgan partnership, he told an old Amherst professor that service—not the prospect of a \$ 1-million-a-year income—drew him to the House of Morgan.
即使在睡梦中，莫罗内心的冲突也如影随形。一天夜里，他从噩梦中惊醒，恐惧地坐起身来。“贝琪，我梦见我们发了大财，”他解释道，“而且是富可敌国的那种。”当摩根向他抛出合伙人的橄榄枝时，据传记作家哈罗德·尼科尔森爵士记载，他经历了“数周剧烈的精神危机”。在百慕大权衡抉择之际，莫罗看到了一幅充满恶意的漫画——画中的杰克·摩根如秃鹫般啃噬着纽黑文铁路公司股东的内脏。他说正是这幅诽谤之作促使他接受了摩根的职位，而捍卫银行对纽黑文公司的融资方案成了他的首项任务。莫罗用冠冕堂皇的辞藻为那些源于卑微动机的行为辩护。接受摩根合伙人职位后，他告诉阿默斯特学院一位老教授：是服务精神——而非年薪百万美元的前景——把他吸引到了摩根财团。

Morrow always flirted with the idea of retreating to the cloistered university world. He spent so much time on Amherst matters that Jack Morgan reportedly said one day, “Dwight, if you will get off that Amherst Board of Trustees I’ll make a present of a hundred thousand dollars.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298614) In 1921, his bluff was called: he was approached with a tentative offer to become president of Yale. He turned it down, saying he wasn’t a Yale alumnus and lacked special training. The excuse was flimsy, and for months afterward Dwight was depressed. Amherst and the University of Chicago also pursued him in vain.
莫罗一直对隐居大学校园生活有所向往。他在阿默斯特事务上投入了大量时间，以至于据说杰克·摩根有一天对他说：“德怀特，如果你能退出阿默斯特董事会，我就捐赠十万美元。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298614) 1921 年，他的虚张声势被揭穿：有人向他试探性提出担任耶鲁大学校长的职位。他拒绝了，理由是他不是耶鲁校友，也缺乏专业训练。这个借口很牵强，之后几个月德怀特都郁郁寡欢。阿默斯特和芝加哥大学也试图邀请他，但都未能如愿。

Morrow’s real passion was politics. He balked at the Morgan partnership lest it prove a political handicap—an accurate fear, as it turned out. The British press baron Lord Beaverbrook once told him that if he were an Englishman, he would already be a cabinet minister. The remark haunted and distressed him.[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298767) At first, Calvin Coolidge’s election seemed a godsend, and Morrow was touted for Treasury secretary and other positions that never materialized. “My mother was upset and felt rather bitterly about it,” said his daughter. “She felt that my father didn’t ask for things.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298929) Morrow used to tell his children to obey “Rule 6—Don’t take yourself too seriously!”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299095) Yet the Morrows took every setback in earnest.
莫罗真正的热情在于政治。他对摩根合伙人的职位犹豫不决，担心这会成为他政治生涯的绊脚石——事实证明，这种担忧不无道理。英国报业大亨比弗布鲁克勋爵曾告诉他，如果他是个英国人，早该当上内阁大臣了。这番话让他耿耿于怀，倍感苦恼。 [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298767) 起初，卡尔文·柯立芝当选总统似乎天赐良机，莫罗曾被提名为财政部长及其他职位，但这些机会最终都化为泡影。“我母亲非常难过，对此颇有怨言，”他的女儿回忆道，“她觉得父亲从不主动争取。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2298929) 莫罗常教导孩子们要遵守“第六条规则——别太把自己当回事！” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299095) 然而，莫罗夫妇对每一次挫折都格外较真。

One suspects that Coolidge kept a self-protective distance from Morrow. “Ever since Mr. Coolidge became President Mr. Morrow has, of course, been a frequent guest at the White House,” wrote Ivy Lee, who was a publicity consultant for the Morgan bank as well as for the Rockefellers. “It is an open secret that the President has consulted with him on numerous occasions.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299274) Harold Nicolson, by contrast, claimed that Coolidge telephoned Morrow only once between 1923 and 1929. Morrow’s files suggest that the truth lay in between, with Nicolson’s comment nearer the mark. Coolidge had wanted to make Morrow, not Parker Gilbert, agent general for Germany and only capitulated after warnings from the U.S. ambassador to Germany. Clearly, some Coolidge advisers feared the stigma of associating with a Morgan partner.
有人认为柯立芝与莫罗保持着一种自我保护的距离。摩根银行及洛克菲勒家族的公关顾问艾维·李写道：“自柯立芝先生成为总统以来，莫罗先生自然是白宫的常客。总统曾多次与他商讨国事，这已是公开的秘密。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299274) 相比之下，哈罗德·尼科尔森则声称，在 1923 年至 1929 年间，柯立芝仅给莫罗打过一次电话。莫罗的档案显示，真相介于两者之间，而尼科尔森的说法更接近事实。柯立芝本想让莫罗而非帕克·吉尔伯特担任德国代理总长，直到美国驻德大使发出警告后才作罢。显然，柯立芝的一些顾问担心与摩根合伙人交往会招来非议。

Perhaps to soften up public opinion for an appointment, Coolidge assigned Morrow in 1925 to chair a board studying the application of airplanes to national defense. Coolidge first mentioned it in a letter to Morrow a few days after his 1925 inauguration, but Morrow learned about it officially from the Sunday papers that September. The Morrow board drew up plans for army and navy use of airplanes. In 1925, Daniel and Harry Guggenheim—old friends of Dwight’s from his Kennecott Copper days—set up a special \$3-million fund to advance aviation. Through Morrow, they got Coolidge to accept the money on behalf of the government to speed up airplane development.
也许是出于为某项任命铺平舆论道路的考虑，柯立芝在 1925 年指派莫罗主持一个委员会，研究飞机在国防领域的应用。柯立芝在 1925 年就职几天后，在给莫罗的信中首次提及此事，但莫罗正式得知这一消息，却是通过那年九月的周日版报纸。莫罗领导的委员会为陆军和海军制定了飞机使用方案。1925 年，德怀特在肯尼科特铜业时期的老友丹尼尔·古根海姆和哈里·古根海姆设立了一笔 300 万美元的专项基金，用于推动航空业发展。通过莫罗的牵线，他们说服柯立芝代表政府接受了这笔资金，以加速飞机研发进程。

Through his stint on the Aviation Board, Dwight Morrow became friends with the young Charles Lindbergh. In fact, Morrow’s files show that the Morgan partners ended up paying for Lindbergh’s historic flight to Paris aboard *The Spirit of St. Louis.* Under the original scheme, Lindbergh had planned to compete for the \$25,000 Orteig Prize, set up to reward the first nonstop flight between New York and Paris. The trip was thus supposed to be self-financing. Lindbergh contributed \$2,000, and a number of other Saint Louis sponsors added \$1,000 or \$500 apiece. Altogether, they raised \$8,500 in subscriptions against a loan of \$15,000 from a Saint Louis bank. Then, in his rush to be first across the Atlantic, Lindbergh decided that he couldn’t afford certain delays demanded by the Orteig Prize and so forfeited his chance. In June 1927, one St. Louis sponsor, broker Harry F. Knight, told Morrow that the historic flight had actually cost between \$16,000 and \$17,000. The Morgan partners pitched in \$10,500, not only repaying the bank loan, but permitting Lindbergh to retrieve his own \$2,000 investment.
通过在航空委员会的任职，德怀特·莫罗与年轻的查尔斯·林德伯格成了朋友。事实上，莫罗的档案显示，摩根合伙人最终为林德伯格驾驶“圣路易斯精神号”飞往巴黎的历史性航程支付了费用。按照最初的计划，林德伯格本打算争夺 2.5 万美元的奥泰格奖——该奖项旨在奖励首次从纽约直飞巴黎的飞行员。因此，这次飞行本应自行筹资。林德伯格自己出资 2000 美元，其他几位圣路易斯的赞助人各出 1000 或 500 美元。他们总共通过认购筹集了 8500 美元，并向圣路易斯一家银行贷款 1.5 万美元。然而，为了抢在别人之前横跨大西洋，林德伯格认为他无法承担奥泰格奖所要求的某些时间延误，因此放弃了获奖机会。1927 年 6 月，一位来自圣路易斯的赞助人、经纪人哈利·F·奈特告诉莫罗，这次历史性飞行的实际成本在 1.6 万至 1.7 万美元之间。 摩根合伙人们凑了 10,500 美元，不仅还清了银行贷款，还让林德伯格拿回了自己投资的 2,000 美元。

When Lindbergh made a triumphant trip to Washington, Coolidge invited him to stay as his guest at the temporary White House on Du Pont Circle. The president saw that Lindbergh’s celebrity would make him a major force in the budding airline industry and so he invited the Morrows as guests as well. Morrow and Lindbergh liked each other immediately. As a trustee of the Daniel Guggenheim Foundation for the Promotion of Aeronautics, Morrow steered Lindbergh to Harry Guggenheim, who sponsored Lindbergh’s three-month tour in *The Spirit of St. Louis.* And Morrow became Lindbergh’s personal financial adviser.
当林德伯格凯旋华盛顿，柯立芝邀请他住在杜邦环岛上的临时白宫。总统意识到，林德伯格的声名将让他成为新兴航空业的一股重要力量，于是也邀请了莫罗一家同住。莫罗和林德伯格一见如故。作为丹尼尔·古根海姆航空促进基金会的受托人，莫罗将林德伯格引荐给哈利·古根海姆，后者赞助了林德伯格驾驶“圣路易斯精神号”进行为期三个月的巡游。而莫罗本人，则成了林德伯格的个人财务顾问。

While the Morrows were staying at the temporary White House, Coolidge sounded out Dwight about becoming ambassador to Mexico. Dwight had been restless at 23 Wall and apparently told Coolidge of his willingness to leave. The offer of the ambassadorship was formalized a month later. This position wasn’t just a bone tossed belatedly to an old friend, but an extremely sensitive appointment. As Coolidge later said, “It would be difficult to imagine a harder assignment. But Mr. Morrow never had any taste for sham battle.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299426)
当莫罗一家暂居临时白宫期间，柯立芝试探性地向德怀特透露了让他出任驻墨西哥大使的意向。此时的德怀特在华尔街 23 号早已按捺不住，显然向柯立芝表达了离职的意愿。一个月后，大使一职的任命正式提上日程。这绝非是给老友迟来的慰藉，而是一项极其敏感的外交任命。正如柯立芝后来所言："这恐怕是最棘手的差事了。不过莫罗先生向来不屑于打马虎眼。" [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299426)

American Catholics and oilmen were agitating to break diplomatic relations with Mexico, and some called for a military invasion. Secretary of State Kellogg had already condemned the regime of President Plutarco Elias Calles as a “Bolshevik threat.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299579) In American eyes, Mexico had committed multiple sins. It had nationalized church property and closed Catholic schools, defaulted on foreign debt, insisted that oil companies trade in property titles for government concessions, and confiscated American-owned land without compensation. Newspapers were calling Mexico America’s foremost foreign-policy problem.
美国天主教徒和石油商人们正激烈鼓动与墨西哥断绝外交关系，甚至有人叫嚣武装入侵。国务卿凯洛格早已将总统普鲁塔科·埃利亚斯·卡列斯的政权斥为“布尔什维克威胁”。在美国人眼中，墨西哥犯下了多重罪过：将教会财产收归国有、关闭天主教学校、拖欠外债、强制石油公司以产权换取政府特许经营权、并无偿没收美国人的土地。各大报纸纷纷将墨西哥称为美国外交政策的首要难题。

The Morrow appointment was an inspired choice. Coolidge was under pressure to do something dramatic, and he did it. Walter Lippmann hailed it as the “most extraordinary appointment made in recent years” and helped to shepherd it through the Senate Foreign Relations Committee.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299729) As a specialist in Latin American loans and an opponent of dollar diplomacy, Morrow had moderated Wall Street’s often truculent attitude toward Latin American debtors. When Cuba threatened to default on foreign bonds during the 1921 sugar debacle that nearly collapsed Guaranty Trust, Morrow was credited with keeping out the marines. “Is there any one who thinks that if a man owes him money and cannot pay it, there is profit in going out and killing him?” he wrote.[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299915) Morrow favored diplomacy over armed intervention, an enlightened attitude for the time.
任命莫罗可谓是神来之笔。柯立芝面临采取重大举措的压力，而他确实做到了。沃尔特·李普曼称赞这是“近年来最非凡的任命”，并协助推动参议院外交关系委员会通过了这项任命。 [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299729) 作为拉丁美洲贷款专家和美元外交的反对者，莫罗缓和了华尔街对拉美债务人一贯的强硬态度。1921 年糖业危机几乎导致 Guaranty Trust 倒闭时，古巴面临外币债券违约风险，据称正是莫罗阻止了海军陆战队的介入。“难道有人会认为，如果别人欠自己钱却无力偿还，杀了对方能有什么好处吗？”他写道。 [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2299915) 莫罗主张外交而非武力干预，这在当时是颇为开明的态度。

Betty Morrow expressed both elation and bitterness at the appointment. The Morrows had just decided to build a new home in Englewood, and she didn’t want their lives disrupted. *She* didn’t see Coolidge as a transcendental philosopher: “The blow has fallen! President Coolidge wrote to Dwight today asking him to be Ambassador to Mexico and Dwight is going to do it. It is a hard job, and not much honor, and it comes late. . . . Coolidge won’t run again, but Dwight goes and does a hard job for him when there is no chance of reward. How characteristic![21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300063) Betty sarcastically told friends that Coolidge was like a father who had given away valuable gifts and thrown Dwight a little tin whistle at the end.
贝蒂·莫罗对这一任命既感到欣喜又充满苦涩。莫罗一家刚刚决定在恩格尔伍德建一所新房子，她不想让他们的生活被打乱。她不认为柯立芝是一位超凡的哲学家：“打击终于降临了！柯立芝总统今天写信给德怀特，请他出任驻墨西哥大使，而德怀特答应了。这是一份苦差事，谈不上什么荣誉，而且来得太晚了……柯立芝不会再竞选了，但德怀特却在他毫无回报机会的时候去为他干这份苦活。多么典型啊！ [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300063) ”贝蒂讽刺地对朋友们说，柯立芝就像一个父亲，送出了贵重的礼物，最后却只扔给德怀特一个小锡哨。

Morrow was sufficiently pessimistic about Mexico to say privately that the best he could do was get Mexico off the front page. Lamont advised him against taking the post, saying the turmoil of a pending presidential campaign made it an inauspicious time for action. Friends concurred and were aghast that Dwight would surrender a Morgan partnership for such a risky position. Even Lindbergh was dubious: “From what little I have seen at our border stops, I am afraid that the position will be a difficult one.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300225)
莫罗对墨西哥的前景十分悲观，私下里坦言自己最多只能做到让墨西哥不再出现在报纸头条。拉蒙特劝他不要接受这个职位，认为即将到来的总统大选乱局让眼下并非采取行动的良机。朋友们也都赞同，并对德怀特放弃摩根合伙人的身份，去接手这样一个充满风险的职位感到震惊。就连林德伯格也表示怀疑：“根据我在边境关卡所见所闻，恐怕这个职位会相当棘手。”

The Mexicans were also wary of Morrow, believing he would function as a collection agent for the New York banks. They chanted, “First Morrow, then the marines.” The fear was unjustified. The Lamont-led bankers’ committee for Mexico was less interested in military action than in peaceful negotiations to get Mexico to resume debt repayment. They wanted stability, not further turmoil, in Mexico. In the end, it would be the Mexicans who would be pleasantly surprised by Dwight Morrow and the House of Morgan that would feel embittered and betrayed.
墨西哥人也对莫罗心存戒备，认为他会充当纽约银行的收债代理人。他们高呼：“莫罗先来，海军陆战队随后。”这种担忧实属多余。由拉蒙特领导的银行家委员会在墨西哥问题上，更倾向于通过和平谈判促使墨西哥恢复债务偿还，而非诉诸军事行动。他们希望墨西哥保持稳定，而非陷入进一步动荡。最终，德怀特·莫罗给墨西哥人带来的竟是意外之喜，而摩根财团却感到心寒与背叛。

AS ambassador to Mexico, Dwight Morrow patented a new style for a *gringo* emissary in Latin America—warm and voluble, treating Mexicans as peers, not wayward children. Soon after his arrival, he told the local U.S. Chamber of Commerce that it should respect Mexican sovereignty. (With slight embarrassment, he had to write the White House and ask for a photograph of Coolidge to hang above his desk—another telltale sign of the distance between the two.) Morrow developed a close rapport with President Calles and would casually drop in on him, like an old friend. They would breakfast at Calles’s ranch or tour Mexican dams and irrigation works together. Morrow’s friendly, trusting manner contrasted with that of his predecessor, James R. Sheffield, who had treated nonwhites patronizingly, taken a pro-invasion stand toward Mexico, and studiously served the interests of American oil companies.
作为驻墨西哥大使，德怀特·莫罗开创了美国在拉丁美洲使节的全新风范——热情健谈，将墨西哥人视为平等的伙伴，而非任性的孩子。到任不久，他便对当地的美国商会表示，应尊重墨西哥的主权。（他略带尴尬地致信白宫，请求提供一张柯立芝的照片挂在办公桌上方——这同样是两人关系疏远的明证。）莫罗与卡列斯总统建立了密切的私交，常以老友身份随意造访。两人或是在卡列斯的庄园共进早餐，或是一同考察墨西哥的水坝与灌溉工程。这种友善信任的作风，与前任大使詹姆斯·R·谢菲尔德形成了鲜明对比：后者对非白人高高在上，对墨西哥持干预立场，且不遗余力地为美国石油公司谋利。

Morrow not only respected Mexican culture but liked the easygoing informality of the people. He and Betty spent weekends at Casa Mafiaña, a villa in the semitropical town of Cuernavaca. It overlooked two volcanoes and overflowed with Mexican pottery and Indian handicrafts. Morrow commissioned Diego Rivera, the left-wing Mexican muralist, to paint frescoes at the Cortes Palace, including one that depicted the revolutionist Zapata. To improve U.S.-Mexican relations, he even brought down Will Rogers to tour with him and Calles. While Rogers was there, Morrow threw a banquet complete with Mexican songs and dance. At one point, the ebullient Morrow said to Rogers with a smile, “Imagine going to war with a people like this!”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300433)
莫罗不仅尊重墨西哥文化，还喜欢当地人那种随和不拘小节的气质。他和贝蒂常去库埃纳瓦卡——一个亚热带小镇的“玛尼亚纳别墅”度周末。别墅能俯瞰两座火山，里面摆满了墨西哥陶器和印第安手工艺品。莫罗委托左翼壁画家迭戈·里维拉在科尔特斯宫绘制壁画，其中一幅描绘了革命家萨帕塔。为改善美墨关系，他甚至邀请威尔·罗杰斯与自己及卡列斯一同巡游。罗杰斯在墨西哥期间，莫罗举办了一场盛宴，席间有墨西哥歌舞助兴。兴高采烈的莫罗笑着对罗杰斯说："想想看，要和这样的人民开战？"

Sometimes Morrow seemed more popular among Mexicans than in the American colony. The public debate about Mexico in the United States became more inflammatory in late 1927. William Randolph Hearst nursed a grudge against President Calles after the latter appropriated sections of his gigantic Babicora Ranch. That November, the Hearst newspapers ran sensational articles purportedly showing Mexican plots against the United States. Some observers thought Hearst was not only expressing pique against Calles but was deliberately fomenting trouble for Dwight Morrow; the isolationist Hearst had always disliked the Anglophile House of Morgan. On December 9, 1927, the twenty-six Hearst papers published documents that supposedly outlined a Mexican plot to bribe four U.S. senators with over \$1 million. These documents were later exposed as forgeries, but in the meantime they damaged relations with Mexico.
有时候，莫罗在墨西哥人中的受欢迎程度甚至超过在美国侨民中。1927 年底，美国国内关于墨西哥的公开辩论愈发激烈。威廉·伦道夫·赫斯特因卡列斯总统征用其庞大的巴比科拉牧场部分土地而怀恨在心。那年 11 月，赫斯特旗下的报纸刊登了耸人听闻的文章，声称揭露了墨西哥针对美国的阴谋。一些观察人士认为，赫斯特不仅是在表达对卡列斯的不满，更是蓄意给德怀特·莫罗制造麻烦——这位孤立主义者赫斯特向来反感亲英的摩根财团。1927 年 12 月 9 日，赫斯特旗下 26 家报纸发布了据称揭露墨西哥用超过 100 万美元贿赂四名美国参议员的文件。这些文件后来被证实是伪造的，但当时已对美墨关系造成了损害。

BEFORE leaving for Mexico, Morrow had invited Charles Lindbergh to his East Sixty-sixth Street apartment. Acting on a suggestion from Walter Lippmann, Morrow proposed that the young aviator pilot *The Spirit of St. Louis* to Mexico as a goodwill gesture. Lindbergh liked the idea. He had flown to Paris on a spring day, and before donating his plane to a museum, he wanted to prove the practicality of night and winter flights. To strengthen the political message, Lindbergh suggested a flight linking Washington and Mexico City.
在前往墨西哥之前，莫罗曾邀请查尔斯·林德伯格到他位于东六十六街的公寓做客。采纳了沃尔特·李普曼的建议后，莫罗提议这位年轻飞行员驾驶“圣路易斯精神号”飞往墨西哥，作为一项友好姿态。林德伯格对这个想法很感兴趣。他曾在某个春日飞抵巴黎，而在将飞机捐赠给博物馆之前，他想证明夜间和冬季飞行的可行性。为了强化政治信号，林德伯格建议进行一次连接华盛顿与墨西哥城的飞行。

So on December 14, 1927, with rifle, machete, and tropical medicine on board, Lindbergh took off through stormy night skies. It was a few days after the Hearst “expose” and a perilous moment in U.S.-Mexican relations. When the sun rose the next morning, Lindbergh sailed through a cloudless Mexican morning but couldn’t figure out where he was. He dipped low enough to read the names of a hotel and train stations and briefly thought all Mexican towns were named Caballeros, because he kept seeing that sign at the stations. Then he spotted a sign for Toluca, a town about fifty miles from Mexico City.
于是在 1927 年 12 月 14 日，林德伯格携带着步枪、砍刀和热带药品，在暴风雨的夜空下起飞了。那时正值赫斯特“爆料”事件后几天，美墨关系处于危险时刻。次日清晨，当太阳升起时，林德伯格在墨西哥晴朗的晨空中飞行，却无法确定自己的位置。他低空飞行到能看清一家酒店和火车站的名字，一度以为墨西哥所有小镇都叫“卡巴列罗斯”，因为他在各个车站都看到这个标识。后来他发现了托卢卡的指示牌——那是一个距墨西哥城约五十英里的小镇。

Sharing picnic sandwiches and lemonade, Morrow and President Calles awaited Lindbergh in sweltering heat at Valbuena Airport, where a special grandstand was set up for dignitaries. Morrow nervously paced up and down. When Lindbergh landed—six hours late—a crowd of Mexicans estimated at 150,000 rushed exuberantly across the field. As Lindbergh accompanied Morrow and Calles to their car, they were thronged by shouting, delirious spectators. They rode to the embassy in triumph, horns blaring, horses rearing, and crowds posted “on trees, on telegraph poles, tops of cars, roofs, even the towers of the Cathedral,” as Betty Morrow recalled. “Flowers and confetti were flung every moment.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300609)
莫罗和卡列斯总统在酷热的瓦尔布埃纳机场分享着野餐三明治和柠檬水，等待着林德伯格的到来——那里专门为要员们搭建了一座观礼台。莫罗紧张地来回踱步。当林德伯格迟了六个小时降落时，估计有十五万墨西哥民众欢呼着涌向机场。林德伯格陪同莫罗和卡列斯走向座车时，他们被狂叫着的、神志恍惚的围观者团团围住。一行人凯旋般驶向大使馆，喇叭声震天，马匹受惊直立，人群“攀上树木、电线杆、车顶、屋顶，甚至大教堂的塔楼，”贝蒂·莫罗回忆道，“鲜花和彩纸一刻不停地抛撒着。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300609)

Lindbergh spent Christmas at the embassy with the Morrows and took Calles on his first plane ride. He also took notice of Dwight’s daughter, Anne, on vacation from her senior year at Smith College. She was a shy, pretty poetess, as slight of build as Charles was rangy and with Betty’s heavy eyebrows. Lindbergh liked the fact that when he first sat next to her, she didn’t ask any questions. Theirs was the strong bond of two shy people who had found each other.
林德伯格与莫罗一家在使馆共度圣诞，还带卡莱斯体验了他人生中第一次飞行。他也留意到了德怀特的女儿安妮，正逢史密斯学院大四假期回家。她是个羞怯而清秀的女诗人，身形纤细，与林德伯格的高瘦恰成对比，眉宇间颇有贝蒂那种浓重的英气。林德伯格欣赏的是，初次坐在她身旁时，她一句追问也没有。两个同样羞怯的灵魂找到彼此，由此结下深厚纽带。

Morrow hadn’t especially liked the young men his daughters dated—Anne and Elisabeth having gone out with Corliss Lamont, among others. He approved of Charles Lindbergh as a “nice clean boy” who didn’t drink, smoke, or see girls.[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300771) But when Anne announced that she and Charles wanted to get married, Morrow seemed flabbergasted. “He’s going to marry Anne? What do we know about this young man?” he asked.[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300946) He insisted they be engaged first and get to know each other better. Despite his flustered reaction, Morrow was very fond of Charles and would beam with gee-whiz delight when he narrated his aviation adventures.
莫罗对自己女儿们交往的年轻小伙并不特别中意——安妮和伊丽莎白曾与科利斯·拉蒙特等人约会过。他对查尔斯·林德伯格倒是颇为认可，觉得他是个"干干净净的好男孩"，不抽烟、不喝酒、也不拈花惹草。 [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300771) 但当安妮宣布她和查尔斯想结婚时，莫罗似乎惊呆了。"他要娶安妮？我们对这小伙子了解多少？"他问道。 [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2300946) 他坚持要两人先订婚，多了解彼此。尽管反应慌乱，莫罗其实非常喜欢查尔斯，听他讲述飞行冒险故事时，总会露出惊叹又欣喜的笑容。

On May 27, 1929, Anne and Charles were married at the Morrows’ new Georgian mansion in Englewood, called Next Day Hill. It was an event of such fascination worldwide that the Morrows had to fool the press and bill it as a prewedding party. Even the guests were told only to drop by for lunch and a bridge game. Then Anne suddenly appeared in a white chiffon wedding gown, and a brisk ceremony took place. Only after Anne and Charles had changed clothes and escaped through the back door did Dwight and Betty broadcast the news to reporters. The young couple briefly stayed at the Leffingwell house in Oyster Bay during their secretive honeymoon, and the servants were threatened with dismissal if they mentioned the couple’s presence to tradespeople in town.
1929 年 5 月 27 日，安妮和查尔斯在莫罗家族位于恩格尔伍德的新乔治亚式豪宅"次日山"举行了婚礼。这场婚礼举世瞩目，莫罗夫妇不得不瞒过媒体，对外宣称只是一场婚礼前的派对。就连宾客也只被告知前来共进午餐、玩场桥牌。谁料安妮突然身披白色雪纺婚纱现身，一场简洁的婚礼旋即举行。直到安妮和查尔斯换好衣服从后门溜走后，德怀特和贝蒂才向记者公布了消息。这对新婚夫妇在神秘的新婚蜜月期间，短暂住在牡蛎湾的莱芬韦尔宅邸。仆人们被警告：若向镇上商贩透露二人的行踪，就会被立即解雇。

It was a strong, intense match but one riddled with contradictions. Anne was the daughter of a former Morgan partner and had imbibed her father’s idealism and internationalism. Charles’s father, who had died in 1924, was the populist Minnesota congressman who had instigated the Pujo hearings, fulminated against the Money Trust and the Morgan cabal with the Federal Reserve Bank, and castigated the bankers who dragged America into the war. The congressman’s son inherited his father’s suspicion of eastern bankers and would never entirely slough it off. In the late 1930s, his isolationism would place him at odds with the House of Morgan and create a painful dilemma for Anne. But in the late 1920s, he socialized with the Morrows and Guggenheims and thrilled the Davisons by taking their beach-party guests at Peacock Point for seaplane rides.
这是一场激烈而紧张的博弈，却充满了矛盾。安妮是前摩根合伙人的女儿，继承了父亲的理想主义和国际主义情怀。查尔斯的父亲于 1924 年去世，他是明尼苏达州一位民粹主义众议员，曾发起普乔听证会，猛烈抨击金钱信托和摩根财团与美联储的勾结，并痛斥那些将美国拖入战争的银行家。这位众议员的儿子继承了父亲对东部银行家的猜疑，而且永远无法完全摆脱这种怀疑。20 世纪 30 年代末，他的孤立主义立场使他与摩根家族产生分歧，给安妮带来了痛苦的困境。但在 20 世纪 20 年代末，他与莫罗家族和古根海姆家族交往甚密，还驾驶水上飞机载着戴维森家族在孔雀角海滩派对上的一众宾客兜风，令他们兴奋不已。

THOSE who saw Ambassador Morrow as a proxy for the House of Morgan in Mexico were in for a rude shock. The ambassador already had a separate political agenda, Morrow having confided to Walter Lippmann that he coveted a seat in the U.S. Senate. Hence, he needed to distance himself from the bank. During the 1928 presidential campaign, he was already being toasted as a potential senatorial candidate at Republican dinners. It was now in Morrow’s political interest to function as a sensitive, fair-minded arbiter in settling Mexican disputes.
那些把莫罗大使视为摩根大通在墨西哥代理人的人，很快便大失所望。这位大使早已另有一套政治算盘——他曾向沃尔特·李普曼吐露，自己觊觎美国参议院的一个席位。正因如此，他必须与银行保持距离。1928 年总统竞选期间，共和党晚宴上已有人举杯祝酒，将他视为潜在的参议员候选人。如今，在解决墨西哥争端时扮演一个敏锐、公正的仲裁者角色，更符合莫罗的政治利益。

Morrow had quick success with the long-running oil controversy. He developed an ingenious scheme of “perpetual concessions” for American oil companies. It gave them new concessions on pre-1917 wells, while Mexico saved face and retained theoretical ownership. This rational statesmanship delighted Walter Lippmann, who told Morrow afterward, “There is a disposition in some quarters to ascribe it to some kind of private magic which you have at your disposal.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301112) For Lippmann, Morrow qualified as the most talented public man of his generation, far beyond the common run of politicians.
莫罗在旷日持久的石油争端中迅速取得了成功。他为美国石油公司设计了一个巧妙的“永久特许权”方案。该方案让这些公司获得了 1917 年之前油井的新特许权，同时墨西哥保住了面子，并保留了理论上的所有权。这种理性的治国之道令沃尔特·李普曼大为赞赏，事后他对莫罗说：“某些方面倾向于将其归功于你掌握的一种私人魔法。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301112) 在李普曼看来，莫罗堪称他那一代人中最杰出的公职人员，远超一般政客之流。

Another major dispute involved the Catholic church. Calles had tried to nationalize church lands, and the violent Cristeros movement had arisen in protest. A state of war existed in parts of Mexico, with thousands of men marching under the church’s banner. Morrow smuggled Walter Lippmann into the country on a secret diplomatic mission. They negotiated a compromise under which Calles agreed not to interfere with the church while Mexican priests agreed to call off their protest strike. Morrow and Lippmann sold the deal to the Vatican, and the settlement reopened the churches. One morning in Cuernavaca, Betty and Dwight were awakened by the ringing of church bells. “Betty, I have opened the churches,” Dwight said, laughing. “Now perhaps you will wish me to close them again.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301279)
另一大争议涉及天主教会。卡列斯曾试图将教会土地国有化，由此引发了激烈的基督派武装运动。墨西哥部分地区陷入战争状态，成千上万的人高举教会旗帜游行。莫罗秘密将沃尔特·李普曼带入墨西哥，执行一项秘密外交任务。他们协商达成妥协：卡列斯同意不干涉教会事务，而墨西哥神父则同意取消抗议罢工。莫罗和李普曼将协议兜售给梵蒂冈，和解协议得以重新开放教堂。一天早晨，在库埃纳瓦卡，贝蒂和德怀特被教堂的钟声吵醒。“贝蒂，我打开了教堂的门，”德怀特笑着说，“现在你可能又希望我把它们关上了。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301279)

The most frustrating area for Morrow was, ironically, the foreign debt. By 1928, Mexico had been in default for fourteen years, and its budgetary situation worsened with the lower oil revenues. In a desperate mood, bankers didn’t see how Mexico could satisfy all its creditors. The country owed money to the foreign bondholders represented by Lamont as well as to western U.S. railroads and domestic lenders. Lamont thought the two hundred thousand bondholders he represented should have first claim. He argued that they had patiently waited many years for payment. Morrow, in contrast, favored a comprehensive settlement for all creditors, on the model of a bankruptcy settlement. He feared that if Mexico struck a series of separate deals, it would promise more money than it could deliver. To Lamont, the notion of one big settlement was an impractical dream that would only penalize his bondholders. And it would be so cumbersome that nobody would ever get paid.
最具讽刺意味的是，让莫罗最头疼的领域竟是外债问题。到 1928 年，墨西哥已连续十四年出现债务违约，而石油收入锐减更让财政状况雪上加霜。在焦头烂额之际，银行家们根本看不到墨西哥能同时偿还所有债权人的希望。这个国家既欠拉蒙特所代表的海外债券持有人的钱，也欠美国西部铁路公司和国内贷款机构的债。拉蒙特认为，他代表的二十万债券持有人应当享有优先受偿权，理由是这些债主已耐心等待还款多年。而莫罗却主张效仿破产清偿模式，为所有债权人制定一揽子解决方案。他担心若墨西哥与各方分别达成协议，最终承诺的还款金额会远超实际偿付能力。在拉蒙特看来，这种整体清偿方案简直是不切实际的幻想，只会损害他身后债券持有人的利益——方案本身过于臃肿繁琐，到头来谁都拿不到钱。

A heated feud arose between Morrow and Lamont. Although he would never admit it, Lamont had secret reservations about Morrow. He would later eulogize him as “sparkling, brilliant, whimsical, lovable,” yet he thought Morrow had an unearned reputation for sainthood. There was perhaps envy here, a feeling that Morrow threatened his own image as the leading liberal banker. Posing as Morrow’s friend, Lamont gave Harold Nicolson a 125-page critique of a draft of Nicolson’s biography of Morrow and reproached him for idealizing his subject. Morrow and Lamont were perhaps too much alike to be completely fooled by one another. Each was more worldly and ambitious than he cared to admit.
莫罗与拉蒙特之间爆发了一场激烈的争执。尽管拉蒙特从不承认，但他对莫罗暗藏保留意见。后来他虽赞誉莫罗“才华横溢、妙趣横生、令人喜爱”，却认为莫罗的圣人美名名不副实。这其中或许掺杂着嫉妒——他感到莫罗威胁了自己作为自由派银行家领军人物的形象。拉蒙特佯装莫罗的朋友，却将一份长达 125 页的批评稿交给哈罗德·尼科尔森，针对尼科尔森所著莫罗传记的初稿大加挞伐，指责他将传主过度理想化。莫罗与拉蒙特或许过于相似，以至于彼此都无法完全蒙蔽对方。两人都比自己愿意承认的更加世故与野心勃勃。

It’s hard to know whether Lamont found Morrow’s position on Mexican debt a political ploy to separate himself from 23 Wall or a quixotic plan that only an absentminded professor could espouse. In any event, by 1929 Lamont decided to break with the Morrow-inspired State Department plan for a comprehensive debt settlement. He circulated bitter memos at 23 Wall Street, sarcastically referring to Morrow as the ambassador. The ICBM, he warned, “will be by no means content to stand by idly for a year while the ambassador is perfecting his Government claims.” A few days later, Lamont informed his partners that he planned to cut a separate bargain with Mexico “despite Ambassador’s attitude.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301416) George Rublee, the legal adviser to the U.S. embassy in Mexico and a close friend of Morrow’s, later said, “Mr. Lamont would rather take his chances and get what he could ahead of somebody else than to cooperate in a general settlement.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301656)
拉蒙特很难判断，莫罗在墨西哥债务问题上的立场究竟是一种政治策略——好让自己与华尔街 23 号划清界限，还是一个只有心不在焉的教授才会支持的堂吉诃德式计划。无论如何，到 1929 年，拉蒙特决定放弃莫罗主导的国务院全面债务解决方案。他在华尔街 23 号散发充满怨气的备忘录，讽刺地称莫罗为“大使”。他警告说，国际债权银行团“绝不可能在大使完善其政府债权要求的一年里，无所作为地袖手旁观”。几天后，拉蒙特通知他的合伙人，他打算“不顾大使的态度”，与墨西哥单独达成协议。 [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301416) 美国驻墨西哥大使馆法律顾问、莫罗的密友乔治·鲁布利后来表示：“拉蒙特先生宁可自己冒险，抢先捞到好处，也不愿配合达成一揽子解决方案。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301656)

For all his charm, Lamont could play rough when crossed. He tried to figure out an elegant way to get rid of Morrow while appearing to help him. In November 1929, he had Martin Egan hand a letter to President Hoover that recommended Morrow as secretary of war. Lamont stressed that Morrow knew nothing of the request—implying that Hoover should keep the suggestion confidential. He doubted his stratagem would work, however, owing to Hoover’s insecurity in the face of Morrow’s massive intellect. “Dwight was so brilliant that he would talk around \[the president\] in circles,” Lamont said.[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301793) Hoover had already rebuffed an earlier request from Calvin Coolidge to appoint Morrow as his secretary of state. Hoover didn’t rise to Lamont’s bait. Close to Morrow—they spoke several times weekly—the president wasn’t eager to advance a potential political competitor.
尽管拉蒙特魅力十足，但若有人与他作对，他也能毫不手软。他试图想出一个既能除掉莫罗，又看似在帮他的体面办法。1929 年 11 月，他让马丁·伊根交给胡佛总统一封信，推荐莫罗担任战争部长。拉蒙特强调莫罗对此一无所知——暗示胡佛应对此建议保密。然而，他怀疑自己的计谋能否奏效，因为胡佛面对莫罗的才智时会感到不安。“德怀特太聪明了，他能把（总统）绕得团团转，”拉蒙特说。 [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301793) 胡佛此前已经拒绝了卡尔文·柯立芝要求任命莫罗为国务卿的请求。胡佛没有上拉蒙特的当。总统与莫罗关系密切——他们每周会通好几次电话——并不急于提拔一个潜在的政治对手。

That month, two events made Lamont’s exertions unnecessary. On November 12, Hoover appointed Morrow to represent the United States at the upcoming Naval Conference in London. Later in the month, New Jersey governor Larson asked Morrow if he would fill the brief time remaining in the unexpired term of Senator Walter E. Edge, who had just been appointed ambassador to France. A deal was struck whereby David Baird would occupy the Senate seat with the understanding that he would step aside if Morrow wanted to campaign for the Republican nomination in the spring. This provided Morrow with further incentives to oppose Lamont on the debt issue and eliminate his former Morgan partnership as a potential campaign issue.
那个月，两件事让拉蒙特的努力显得多余。11 月 12 日，胡佛任命莫罗代表美国出席即将在伦敦举行的海军会议。同月下旬，新泽西州州长拉尔森询问莫罗是否愿意填补参议员沃尔特·E·埃奇剩余的不完整任期——后者刚被任命为驻法大使。双方达成协议：戴维·贝尔德暂任参议员席位，但若莫罗有意在春季角逐共和党提名，贝尔德须让位。这进一步促使莫罗在债务问题上反对拉蒙特，并消除其昔日摩根合伙人身份可能成为竞选议题的隐患。

In December, the simmering political dispute between Morrow and Lamont boiled over. By this point, Morrow, the liberal do-gooder, had moved into the position of self-appointed overlord of Mexico’s finances. The man who had held back the marines now minutely reviewed Mexico’s budget. When Lamont’s assistant, Vernon Munroe, met with Morrow, he was shocked by the extent to which the ambassador wanted to dictate Mexican financial policy. According to Munroe, Morrow wanted to cut the Mexican budget by “eliminating the courthouse entirely, cutting 2.5 million off the education appropriation, one million pesos off the public health, 2.5 million pesos off statistics and some 4 million pesos off communications.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301978) Under the guise of helping his Mexican brothers, Dwight seemed to succumb to delusions of grandeur.
12 月，莫罗与拉蒙之间暗流涌动的政治纷争终于爆发。此时，这位自由派理想主义者莫罗，已俨然以墨西哥财政最高掌权者自居。这位曾阻止海军陆战队介入的人，如今开始逐项审查墨西哥预算。当拉蒙的助手弗农·门罗与莫罗会面时，大使对墨西哥财政政策的干预程度令他震惊。据门罗回忆，莫罗想通过"完全撤销法院系统，削减教育拨款 250 万、公共卫生 100 万比索、统计部门 250 万比索、通讯部门约 400 万比索"来压缩墨西哥预算。 [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2301978) 德怀特打着援助墨西哥兄弟的旗号，似乎陷入了妄自尊大的迷梦。

During the May-June campaign for the Republican Senate nomination in New Jersey, Morrow was still serving as Mexican ambassador, and as such he followed the debt situation. Then a campaign blunder drastically reduced his influence in that post. While he was at the London Naval Conference, his military attache in Mexico, Colonel Alexander J. MacNab, made a speech in which he extravagantly praised Morrow’s role in Mexican reform. He actually made Lamont’s point—that Morrow was intruding more in Mexican domestic affairs than any Wall Street banker. “There is no department of government in Mexico which he has not advised and directed,” MacNab said of Morrow. “He took the Secretary of Finance under his wing and taught him finance.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302155) The Mexican press treated the speech as a scandal. It made Mexican officials look as if they were the ambassador’s puppets, and Morrow never again had the same influence in Mexico. Nevertheless, he won the Republican nomination.
在五六月份共和党新泽西州参议员提名竞选期间，莫罗仍担任墨西哥大使，因此对债务问题始终心系牵挂。随后一场竞选失策让他这个职位的威信大打折扣。当他在伦敦海军会议期间，其驻墨西哥武官亚历山大·J·麦克纳布上校发表演说，对莫罗在墨西哥改革中的作用极尽溢美之词。这番话恰恰印证了拉蒙特的观点——莫罗干预墨西哥内政的程度已远超任何华尔街银行家。"墨西哥政府没有哪个部门他没给出过建议和指引，"麦克纳布谈及莫罗时说道，"他将财政部长纳入麾下，亲自教授他财政之道。" [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302155) 墨西哥媒体将此言论视为丑闻大肆报道，让墨西哥官员看起来像大使的提线木偶。此后莫罗在墨西哥的影响力再难望其项背。尽管如此，他仍赢得了共和党提名。

During the summer of 1930, Morrow kept flying down to Mexico to advise on the debt. The Morrow-Lamont dispute resulted in some blistering exchanges. Morrow kept urging Lamont to lecture the finance minister about the growing Mexican budget; Lamont did this, then regretted it. In a July 24 letter, his pent-up contempt for Morrow surfaced: “I have a feeling that you are a bit disgusted with our mental processes up here and are genuinely upset that we are unable to adopt in toto your point of view.” He referred to a talk with the finance minister: “He retorted by telling me politely that it was really none of my business. . . . Now, my dear Dwight, you may have some means of compelling the Finance Minister to give you precise information as to his budget plans for several years ahead, but I must confess that in any such effort I, myself, am powerless.” In the end, Lamont bluntly warned Morrow to stay away from his debt settlement with Mexico: “I hope you can see your way clear to letting the matter rest where it is rather than feeling called upon to defeat this plan.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302317) In a cool reply, Morrow repeated that Mexico was bankrupt and should treat creditors equally. He warned Lamont that if he persisted in his course, he would ultimately have to deal with the State Department.[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302505)
1930 年夏天，莫罗频繁飞往墨西哥，就债务问题提供建议。莫罗与拉蒙特的争执引发了数次激烈的交锋。莫罗一再敦促拉蒙特去教训墨西哥财政部长，指责其不断膨胀的预算；拉蒙特照做了，随后又后悔不已。在 7 月 24 日的一封信中，他对莫罗积压已久的轻蔑情绪终于爆发：“我感觉你对我们这儿的思维方式有点看不顺眼，而且真心为我们无法全盘接受你的观点而恼火。”他提及与财政部长的谈话：“他礼貌地回敬我说，这其实根本与我无关……现在，我亲爱的德怀特，你或许有办法迫使财政部长向你透露未来几年的详细预算计划，但我必须承认，在这方面我本人无能为力。”最终，拉蒙特直截了当地警告莫罗不要插手他与墨西哥的债务协议：“我希望你能看清形势，让这件事就此作罢，而不是觉得非得阻挠这个计划不可。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302317) 莫罗冷冷地回复，再次强调墨西哥已经破产，应当平等对待所有债权人。他警告拉蒙特，如果一意孤行，最终将不得不面对国务院。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302505)

The day after Lamont wrote Morrow—without awaiting a reply—he signed a separate accord at 23 Wall with a representative of Mexico’s Chamber of Commerce. It nearly halved Mexico’s debt, reducing it from \$508 million to \$267 million, in one stroke. True to his threats, Morrow advised Mexico to delay ratification, but his influence with President Pascual Ortiz Rubio, Calles’s successor, was much diminished. As it turned out, the feud between the two Morgan men was for naught. Mexico kept postponing the date of debt repayment, and the whole farce would collapse by 1932. The outcome would have been laughable had it not consumed so much of Lamont’s life and impoverished small Mexican bondholders. By 1941, Mexican debt had shrunk to \$49.6 million, or a tenth of the original amount.
拉蒙特给莫罗写信的第二天——没等对方回信——他就在华尔街 23 号与墨西哥商会代表单独签署了一项协议。这笔交易几乎将墨西哥的债务减半，从 5.08 亿美元一举降至 2.67 亿美元。莫罗说到做到，建议墨西哥推迟批准该协议，但他对卡列斯的继任者帕斯夸尔·奥尔蒂斯·鲁维奥总统的影响力已大不如前。结果，这两位摩根门徒之间的恩怨完全白费了。墨西哥一再拖延还款日期，这场闹剧到 1932 年便彻底崩盘。要不是这场风波消耗了拉蒙特太多精力，且害得墨西哥小债券持有者倾家荡产，这结局简直可笑至极。到 1941 年，墨西哥债务已缩水至 4960 万美元，仅剩原金额的十分之一。

Although Morrow’s break with the House of Morgan was now complete, the association haunted him in the Senate race that fall. As one New Jersey paper described his opponent’s strategy: “The Ambassador was to be pictured to New Jersey voters as the tool and puppet of Big Business interests, and his candidacy as a Wall Street conspiracy to capture the Presidency via the U.S. Senate route.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302699)
尽管莫罗与摩根家族的决裂已彻底完成，但这段关联仍在当年秋天的参议员竞选中如影随形地困扰着他。正如新泽西一家报纸所描述的对手策略：“这位大使将被描绘成新泽西选民眼中大企业利益的工具和傀儡，而他的候选资格则是华尔街通过参议院途径夺取总统宝座的阴谋。”

Morrow was exhausted and dispirited. He suffered from insomnia and headaches and ran a lackluster campaign. Nicolson suggests he had a serious drinking problem. Coincidentally, Prohibition became a central topic in the campaign. Not ducking the issue, Morrow became the first federal official to favor outright repeal of the Eighteenth Amendment.
莫罗筋疲力尽，心灰意冷。他饱受失眠和头痛的折磨，竞选活动也毫无起色。尼科尔森指出，他还有严重的酗酒问题。巧合的是，禁酒令成了竞选中的核心议题。莫罗没有回避，而是成为首位公开支持彻底废除第十八修正案的联邦官员。

Again, he seemed driven by his ambition, and the Senate campaign produced more anxiety. Betty recorded in her diary that “Dwight is so tired; so discouraged; so *wild* that he has been trapped into this Senatorial campaign. He is exhausted, does not want it, would be glad to lose.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302850) Fate, devising new ways to punish him, produced a landslide victory in November.
他似乎再次被野心驱使，而参议院竞选带来了更多的焦虑。贝蒂在日记中写道：“德怀特太累了；太沮丧了；他对自己陷入这场参议院竞选感到无比愤怒。他筋疲力尽，根本不想要这个，甚至宁愿输掉。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2302850) 命运却用新的方式惩罚他，在十一月让他赢得了压倒性的胜利。

As senator, Morrow seemed worn from the immense burdens he had carried over the years. He immediately disappointed liberal admirers. Despite the Depression, he voted against food relief, the soldiers’ bonus bill, and tougher utility regulation. This prompted one journalist to declare that in three months he had wiped away the liberal reputation of a lifetime.[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303012) Such remarks stung Morrow. He approached problems in his thorough, dogged way but got mired in their complexity. He passed sleepless nights reading tomes on unemployment, and Betty warned him that he was getting too little sleep. “That’s nonsense,” he replied. “Most people have exaggerated ideas about sleep. If I can get two solid hours I’m all right.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303147) At the family Fourth of July celebration in 1931, Morrow stared sadly at the lawn of his Englewood home and said to his son-in-law, “Charles, never let yourself worry. It is bad for the mind.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303297)
担任参议员期间，莫罗似乎已被多年来肩负的重担压得疲惫不堪。他很快就让自由派崇拜者们大失所望。尽管大萧条肆虐，他仍投票反对粮食救济、军人奖金法案和更严格的公用事业监管。这一举动促使一位记者宣称，他在三个月内毁掉了一生积攒的自由派声誉。 [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303012) 这番言论刺痛了莫罗。他以其一贯的彻底和倔强去处理问题，却深陷复杂泥潭。他彻夜不眠地研读关于失业问题的巨著，贝蒂警告他睡眠太少。“胡说八道，”他答道，“大多数人对睡眠的认知都有夸张。只要我能踏实睡上两小时，就完全没问题。” [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303147) 在 1931 年家族独立日庆祝活动上，莫罗悲伤地望着英格尔伍德家中的草坪，对女婿说：“查尔斯，永远别让自己焦虑。这对精神有害。” [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303297)

In September, Morrow had a minor stroke while he and Betty lunched with newspaper publisher Roy Howard on a yacht in Maine. Yet he couldn’t stop his compulsive activity or moderate his exhausting pace. On October 2, 1931, after spending a sleepless night on a train from Washington to New York, he told a passenger, “I kept waking up thinking what a hell of a mess the world is in.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303432) That day he attended a political reception at his Englewood home. He shook hands with four thousand people; his right hand became blistered, and he had to use his left. Three days later, then in his late fifties, Dwight Morrow died in his sleep of a cerebral hemorrhage. The man once unsettled by his dream of great wealth left a million dollars in charitable bequests alone.
九月，莫罗与贝蒂在缅因州的一艘游艇上与报业大亨罗伊·霍华德共进午餐时，突发轻微中风。但他仍无法停止强迫性的活动，也无法放慢那令人筋疲力尽的节奏。1931 年 10 月 2 日，在从华盛顿到纽约的火车上度过了一个不眠之夜后，他对一位乘客说：“我一次次醒来，想着这世界真是一团糟。” [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303432) 那天，他在恩格尔伍德的家中参加了一场政治招待会，与四千人握手，右手起了水泡，不得不改用左手。三天后，年近六旬的德怀特·莫罗在睡梦中因脑溢血去世。那个曾因一夜暴富的梦想而焦虑不安的人，仅慈善遗赠就留下了一百万美元。

And Harold Nicolson left an appropriately ambiguous epitaph: “There was about him a touch of madness or epilepsy, or something unhuman and abnormal. . . . He had the mind of a super-criminal and the character of a saint. There is no doubt at all that he was a very great man.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303594) Yet Nicolson tempered this judgment with a far less generous one: “Morrow was a shrewd and selfish little arriviste who drank himself to death.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303761)
哈罗德·尼科尔森留下了一段模棱两可的墓志铭：“他身上带着一丝疯狂或癫痫的气息，或者某种非人且异乎寻常的东西……他有着超级罪犯的头脑和圣人的品格。毫无疑问，他是个非常伟大的人。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303594) 然而，尼科尔森又用一句远没那么宽容的评价缓和了这一判断：“莫罗是个精明又自私的小野心家，最后把自己喝死了。” [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2303761)

In one respect, fate proved merciful to Dwight Morrow. Five months after Morrow died, his grandson, Charles Lindbergh, Jr., was kidnapped from his family’s home near Hopewell, New Jersey. The House of Morgan tried to help solve the famous case. Jack Morgan sounded out an underworld contact, and the bank fielded tips from several sources, including a palmist. It also bundled and numbered the ransom money that Lindbergh’s associate, Dr. John F. Condon, passed across a dark cemetery wall to the kidnapper. When the baby’s body was discovered in a wood two months later, Anne and Charles moved to Next Day Hill, the Morrow house in Englewood. Haunted by the press and bad memories, they left the United States for England in 1935. There they lived at Long Barn, a thatched Kentish house owned by Harold Nicolson, Anne’s father’s biographer.
从某种程度上说，命运对德怀特·莫罗还算仁慈。他去世五个月后，其外孙小查尔斯·林德伯格在新泽西州霍普韦尔附近的家中遭人绑架。摩根家族试图协助侦破这起轰动一时的案件。杰克·摩根试探性地联系了地下世界的线人，银行也收到了多方线索，甚至包括一位手相师的建议。他们还把林德伯格的合伙人约翰·F·康登医生在昏暗墓地围墙边交给绑匪的赎金，逐一编号并捆扎好。两个月后，孩子的尸体在树林中被发现，安妮和查尔斯搬到了恩格尔伍德的莫罗宅邸"明日之丘"。由于不堪媒体骚扰和记忆折磨，他们于 1935 年离开美国前往英国，住在长谷仓——一座由安之父的传记作者哈罗德·尼科尔森拥有的肯特郡茅草屋。

The Lindbergh kidnapping also spread fear through the House of Morgan. Afterward, an army of 250 bodyguards protected the families of Morgan partners, and many of their grandchildren would remember growing up surrounded by opulence and armed guards.
林德伯格绑架案也让摩根家族的成员们惶恐不安。此后，250 名保镖日夜守护着摩根合伙人及其家人的安全。许多孙辈回忆自己的成长时光，总绕不开奢华的生活和荷枪实弹的警卫。
