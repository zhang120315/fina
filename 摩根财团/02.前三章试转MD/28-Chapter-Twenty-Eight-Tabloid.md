# Chapter Twenty-Eight: Tabloid

ALTHOUGH Glass-Steagall had supposedly banished banks from the securities markets, the House of Morgan and other Wall Street banking firms exerted a major impact on the stock market through their trust departments. Despite their unequal size, J. P. Morgan and Company and Guaranty Trust brought \$3 billion apiece in trust assets to their merger, forming America’s biggest trust operation. Morgan’s money was mostly in pension funds and Guaranty’s in personal trusts. The merged bank also provided “corporate trust” services that formed the infrastructure for much stock trading on Wall Street. As transfer agent for nearly six hundred companies, it received over twenty-five thousand certificates daily and sometimes handled a quarter of all shares traded on the New York and American stock exchanges. It mailed out twelve million dividend checks a year and maintained updated shareholder lists for many companies.
尽管《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》名义上已将银行逐出证券市场，但摩根银行与华尔街其他银行机构仍通过其信托部门对股市施加着巨大影响。尽管规模不对等，J.P.摩根公司和担保信托公司在合并时各自携约 30 亿美元的信托资产，组成了全美最大的信托业务体。摩根的资金主要集中于养老基金，而担保信托则侧重于个人信托。合并后的银行还提供"公司信托"服务，为华尔街大量的股票交易构建了基础设施。作为近 600 家公司的过户代理人，该行每天接收超过 2.5 万份凭证，有时处理的股票交易量占纽约证券交易所和美国证券交易所全部成交量的四分之一。它每年寄出 1200 万张股息支票，并为众多企业维护着最新股东名册。

The old House of Morgan had commanded a sizable portfolio for its own account, but it was a less-than-scientific operation. Allied with the Guggenheims and the Oppenheimers, it gambled 30 percent of its entire portfolio on copper stocks in the early 1930s. Before 1940, the bank offered investment advice informally to wealthy individuals and old families. Partners managed money for their pet institutions, such as Dwight Morrow for Amherst College, Tom Lamont for Phillips Exeter Academy, and Russell Leffngwell for the Carnegie Corporation. The House of Morgan was always a magnet for ecclesiastical institutions, which shared its discretion and somber dignity. In 1917, Jack Morgan contributed start-up money for the Episcopal church’s pension fund, and the bank always handled part of that money. Under Henry Alexander, the Presbyterians also switched their money to the Morgan Trust Department.
老摩根财团曾为自己的账户管理着相当规模的投资组合，但这并非严谨科学的运作。20 世纪 30 年代初，它与古根海姆家族和奥本海默家族联手，将整个投资组合的 30%押注在铜业股票上。1940 年之前，该银行只是非正式地向富有的个人和世家提供投资建议。合伙人则为他们青睐的机构管理资金，比如德怀特·莫罗为阿默斯特学院，汤姆·拉蒙特为菲利普斯埃克塞特学院，拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔为卡内基基金会。摩根财团始终吸引着教会机构，二者同样行事谨慎、气质庄重。1917 年，杰克·摩根为圣公会养老基金提供了启动资金，该行也始终管理着其中部分资金。在亨利·亚历山大掌舵期间，长老会也将其资金转交给了摩根信托部。

Eligible for trust work after the 1940 incorporation, the bank converted Jack Morgan’s mirrored, marble-walled barber shop into a waiting room for patrons. The first objective was to snare estates of the rich and recently dead. This required patience, waiting until a sufficient number of clients had died. After a dinner spent wearily sifting through names of potential chieftains, George Whitney turned to a young associate, Longstreet Hinton, and said apologetically, “Street, I guess you’re it.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406543) It was then considered quite daring and unorthodox for a nonlawyer to run a trust department, which was always entangled in legal estate questions.
在 1940 年合并后，该银行有资格开展信托业务，于是将杰克·摩根的镜子与大理石墙壁装饰的理发店改造成了客户的等候室。首要目标是争取富人和新近去世者的遗产，这需要耐心等待足够多的客户离世。某次晚宴后，乔治·惠特尼疲惫地筛选着潜在负责人的名字，最后转向年轻同事朗斯特里特·欣顿，略带歉意地说道：“斯特里特，看来只能是你了。”彼时，让非律师出身的人掌管信托部门被认为是相当大胆且不合传统的做法，因为该部门总是纠缠于法律遗产问题中。

Street Hinton was from Vicksburg, Mississippi, and reminded everybody of a Southern cavalry general. He was tall and spare, ramrod straight, somewhat curmudgeonly, with a long face and prominent ears. His father had ended up as minister of Saint John’s of Lattingtown in Locust Valley, the “millionaire’s church” so dear to Jack Morgan. Hin-ton’s formative experience was in settling Jack Morgan’s estate. He drew upon the art expertise of Belle da Costa Greene. “She told me that Fifty-seventh Street was the crookedest place in the world and not to trust anyone,” recalled Hinton of his first foray into the world of art dealers.[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406741) A tough customer, he quickly took control after the merger, telling the people who had run the Guaranty Trust Department: “What makes you think you know how to run a trust department?” He reminded them that they had lost their largest account, Ford Motor, to J. P. Morgan. Hinton ran the combined show.[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406902)
斯特里特·欣顿来自密西西比州的维克斯堡，让人不由自主地想起南方的骑兵将领。他身材高瘦，腰板笔直如杆，带着几分乖戾，长脸，招风耳。他父亲最终成了蝗虫谷拉廷敦圣约翰教堂的牧师——那座杰克·摩根珍视的“百万富翁教堂”。欣顿职业生涯中最重要的经历，是处理杰克·摩根的遗产。他借重了贝尔·达科斯塔·格林的艺术鉴赏力。“她告诉我，五十七街是世界上最弯弯绕绕的地方，谁都别信，”欣顿回忆起自己初次踏入艺术经销商世界时的情景。 [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406741) 他是个难缠的硬茬子，合并后迅速掌控局面，对曾经管理担保信托部的人说：“凭什么觉得你们懂怎么运营信托部门？”他提醒他们，正是他们把最大的客户——福特汽车——丢给了 J.P.摩根。欣顿成了合并后业务的掌舵人。 [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406902)

Trust departments had been regarded as loss leaders. Hinton thought they should make money. Most trust managers were sober men with iron-gray hair who put money into government bonds and they weren’t notable for their imagination. When the Morgan Trust Department made its first common-stock purchase, in 1949, it was thought so audacious that Hinton had to telephone Russell Leffing-well, on vacation in Lake George, New York, to clear the purchase. After 1950, changes in tax laws and collective bargaining prompted an explosion in pension funds, and much of this money gravitated to commercial banks. After General Motors designated Morgans as one of its pension-fund managers and allowed investment of up to 50 percent in stocks, the business boomed. “What made us was the General Motors fund,” said Hinton. “When we led the parade there, then everybody else wanted us.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407060)
过去，信托部门一直被视为赔钱货。但辛顿认为它们应该能够盈利。大多数信托经理都是头发灰白、性格沉稳的人，他们把钱投进政府债券，并不以想象力著称。1949 年，摩根信托部门首次购入普通股时，此举被认为过于大胆，以致辛顿不得不打电话给正在纽约乔治湖度假的拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔，请求批准这笔交易。1950 年后，税法和集体谈判的改变引发了养老基金的激增，其中大量资金流向了商业银行。当通用汽车指定摩根作为其养老基金管理人，并允许将高达 50%的资金投资于股票后，这项业务迅速繁荣起来。“成就我们的是通用汽车的基金，”辛顿说道，“当我们引领了那里的潮流，其他所有人也都找上了我们。”

In the early 1960s, the Morgan Trust Department operated from a wood-paneled room with antique furniture, facing the New York Stock Exchange. Forty impeccably dressed managers in dark suits and black shoes sat in leather armchairs before glossy rolltop desks. Invoking Pierpont Morgan’s philosophy, Hinton initiated the gospel of buy and hold. When a corporate director asked for a policy statement, he replied, “It’s easy. We don’t have one. We never sell stocks.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407221)
20 世纪 60 年代初，摩根信托部坐落在一间装饰着木墙板和古董家具的房间里，正对着纽约证券交易所。四十位衣着考究的经理人，身着深色西装、脚蹬黑皮鞋，坐在皮质的扶手椅上，面前是光洁的卷盖式办公桌。辛顿援引皮尔庞特·摩根的理念，倡导起了买入并持有的信条。当一位公司董事向他询问政策声明时，他答道：“很简单，我们没有什么政策。我们从不卖出股票。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407221)

Hinton had more flexibility than he conceded and was a cagey manager. At meetings with portfolio managers, he would call on Peter Ver-milye, the resident bull, if he wished to buy stocks; if he wished to sell, he favored Homer Cochran, a durable bear. During the Kennedy bull market, the Morgan Trust Department became a trendy place. Young Ivy League portfolio managers loaded up with the glamorous growth stocks of the day—the so-called nifty fifty that so mesmerized money managers. Hinton’s young highfliers bought Schlumberger before people could pronounce the name and were early buyers of Xerox, which multiplied a hundredfold. Symbolic of the new style was Carl Hathaway, who drove a fire-engine-red sports car and issued solemn dicta, such as “Never invest in companies run by fat men.” “Lazy people bore me,” he said. “The most successful of my friends remind me of a 727 at takeoff: full throttle and straight up.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407346)
欣顿比他表面上看起来要灵活得多，而且是个精明的管理者。在与投资组合经理开会时，如果他想买入股票，就会请驻场多头彼得·沃米利发言；如果他想卖出，则倾向于让长期空头霍默·科克伦表态。在肯尼迪牛市期间，摩根信托部门成了热门之地。年轻的常春藤盟校投资组合经理们大量买进当时耀眼的成长股——即后来让资金管理者们痴迷的所谓“漂亮 50”。欣顿麾下那些意气风发的年轻操盘手，在人们还念不对名字时就买入了斯伦贝谢，并且早早入手了施乐股票，后者后来翻了上百倍。卡尔·哈撒韦代表了这种新风格，他开着一辆消防车红色的跑车，并发表一些庄严的论断，比如“永远不要投资由胖子经营的公司”。“懒人让我烦，”他说，“我那些最成功的朋友让我想起起飞时的波音 727：全油门，直冲上天。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407346)

By the mid-1960s, Morgan was managing an incomparable \$15 billion in assets, yet trouble lurked ahead. Control of such gigantic sums invited new public scrutiny, especially from Representative Wright Pat-man, the bullnecked populist chairman of the House Banking Committee. Like Louis Brandeis, Patman saw bankers’ possible abuse of “other people’s money” and feared that banks would use trust assets to exercise influence over business. In 1966, he issued a report that accused big commercial banks of dangerously “snowballing economic power” over large chunks of U.S. industry.[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407517)
到了 20 世纪 60 年代中期，摩根管理的资产已高达惊人的 150 亿美元，然而危机正悄然逼近。掌控如此庞大的资金规模，自然招致了更多公众审视的目光，尤其是来自众议院银行委员会那位强硬的民粹主义主席赖特·帕特曼（Wright Patman）。和路易斯·布兰代斯（Louis Brandeis）一样，帕特曼也看到了银行家们可能滥用“他人钱财”的风险，担心银行会利用信托资产对工商企业施加影响。1966 年，他发布了一份报告，痛斥大型商业银行对大量美国工业领域“集中经济权力”的行为已危险到“雪崩”的地步。 [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407517)

The Patman report disclosed how banks had hijacked the new pools of investment capital. Of more than \$1 trillion in assets held by U.S. institutional investors, 60 percent resided in commercial banks’ trust departments. Because of the Wall Street merger wave, most of these assets were now concentrated in the hands of Morgans and four other banks. There were eye-popping figures on Morgan holdings. It held gargantuan stakes in two old Guggenheim standbys: 17.5 percent of Kennecott Copper and 15.5 percent of American Smelting and Refining,-and it could have bossed around the entire airline industry, with a 7.4-percent stake in Trans World Airlines, 7.5 percent in American Airlines, and 8.2 percent in United. The danger was more latent than actual, for the conservative Morgan bank usually sided with management in disputes and didn’t try to substitute its own judgment. But since a 5-percent holding could now control most companies in an age of dispersed share ownership, the numbers were disturbing.
帕特曼报告揭露了银行是如何劫持新的投资资本池的。在美国机构投资者持有的超过 1 万亿美元资产中，60%存放在商业银行的信托部门。由于华尔街的并购浪潮，这些资产中的大部分如今都集中在摩根银行及其他四家银行手中。摩根银行的持股数据令人瞠目结舌——它持有两家古根海姆老牌重仓股的巨额股份：肯尼科特铜业 17.5%，美国冶炼与精炼公司 15.5%；同时它还握有环球航空公司 7.4%、美国航空公司 7.5%以及联合航空公司 8.2%的股份，足以左右整个航空业。这种威胁更多是潜在的而非实际存在的，因为保守的摩根银行在争议中通常站在管理层一边，并不会试图用自己的判断取而代之。 然而，在一个股权分散的时代，持有 5%的股份就足以控制大多数公司，这些数字令人不安。

There were also new fears about insider trading of a sort that took the banks by surprise. In the twenties, fortunes had been made through whispered tips and sly winks. The public tolerated this because only a tiny percentage of them owned stock. As personal investing grew in the 1950s and early 1960s, the public didn’t wish to take part in a rigged game. It took time for the banks to perceive the danger, or at least the new public apprehension. In the 1960s, trust departments weren’t yet segregated from other departments. At Morgans, senior bank officers—many of them directors of five or ten companies—sat on the Trust Committee, which also had as members outside directors of the bank, including, at various times, people such as Alfred P. Sloan of General Motors and Henry Wingate of International Nickel. These men were expected to bring their specialized knowledge to bear in picking stocks. At that point, banks didn’t worry that they might misuse confidential information from the lending side in making investment decisions; there weren’t yet legal barriers, or “Chinese walls,” between the commercial and the trust departments.
此外，一种令银行措手不及的内幕交易也引发了新的担忧。在 20 年代，人们通过耳语般的内幕消息和狡黠的眼色就能赚得盆满钵满。公众之所以容忍这种现象，是因为当时只有极少数人持有股票。到了 20 世纪 50 年代和 60 年代初，随着个人投资的兴起，公众不愿再参与这场被操纵的游戏。银行花了很长时间才意识到其中的危险，或者说，至少是公众萌生的新忧虑。在 60 年代，信托部门尚未与其他部门分离。在摩根银行，资深银行高管——其中不少人同时担任五到十家公司的董事——都位列信托委员会成员，委员会还包括银行的外部董事，不同时期有通用汽车的阿尔弗雷德·P·斯隆、国际镍业的亨利·温盖特等人。这些人的职责就是运用自己的专业知识来挑选股票。 在当时，银行并不担心自己会滥用从放贷业务中获取的机密信息来指导投资决策；商业部门与信托部门之间尚未设立法律屏障，也就是所谓的“Chinese walls”。

It was amid new concerns that the SEC in April 1965 charged thirteen people associated with Texas Gulf Sulphur of profiting from inside information about an Ontario ore bonanza. One illustrious name leapt from the headlines—Tommy S. Lamont, son of the famous partner and only recently retired as Morgan Guaranty’s vice-chairman. Lamont was accused of relaying information to Longstreet Hinton, who had bought Texas Gulf for Morgan trust accounts. It was a shocking charge, for since the 1930s the bank had been obsessed with its integrity and was undoubtedly one of the world’s most reputable banks. Street Hinton worshiped George Whitney and had watched his anguish over his brother’s scandal. Tommy Lamont had gone through the Pecora hearings, in which he was charged with making “wash sales” to his wife—an experience he didn’t care to repeat—and he had cherished a reputation as a highly principled banker.
就在新的担忧中，1965 年 4 月，美国证券交易委员会（SEC）对与得克萨斯湾硫磺公司（Texas Gulf Sulphur）相关的 13 人提起诉讼，指控他们利用安大略省一处矿藏的内幕消息牟利。头条新闻中跃出一个显赫的名字——汤米·S·拉蒙特（Tommy S. Lamont），他是那位著名合伙人的儿子，不久前刚从摩根保证信托（Morgan Guaranty）副主席的职位上退休。拉蒙特被指控将信息透露给朗斯特里特·辛顿（Longstreet Hinton），后者曾为摩根信托账户买入得克萨斯湾的股票。这一指控令人震惊，因为自 20 世纪 30 年代以来，这家银行一直极度重视自身声誉，无疑是当时全球信誉最好的银行之一。辛顿崇拜乔治·惠特尼（George Whitney），亲眼目睹过他因兄弟丑闻而备受煎熬的痛苦。汤米·拉蒙特曾经历过佩科拉听证会（Pecora hearings），当时他被指控向妻子进行“洗售”——这种经历他可不想再来一次——而他一直珍视自己作为一位原则性极强的银行家的名声。

Thomas Stillwell Lamont resembled his father. Perhaps the face was rounder, the neck a shade fuller, but he had the same strong voice that emerged surprisingly from a slight build. Like many at Morgans, he patterned himself after a sainted father, adopting his interests. He became president of the Phillips Exeter board and a member of both the Harvard Corporation and the Council on Foreign Relations. Adopting his father’s literary bent, he mailed birthday verses and sentimental greetings to friends and proudly chronicled the Lamont family history. Yet despite certain outward similarities, Tommy Lamont was more proper, more remote, and certainly more private than his famously gregarious father.
托马斯·斯蒂尔韦尔·拉蒙特酷似其父。或许脸庞更圆润些，脖颈也略显丰腴，但他同样以瘦削的身躯迸发出令人惊讶的洪亮嗓音。就像摩根银行的许多人一样，他效仿被奉为圣贤的父亲，沿袭了他的志趣。他出任菲利普斯埃克塞特学院董事会主席，同时身兼哈佛大学校务委员会和外交关系委员会成员。承袭父亲的文学雅好，他会向友人寄赠生日诗篇与温情贺词，并自豪地编纂拉蒙特家族史。然而尽管外表有诸多相似，与他那位以热情著称的父亲相比，汤米·拉蒙特却更为拘谨疏离，也无疑更注重隐私。

Tommy Lamont had been the House of Morgan’s ambassador to the mining world, joining the Texas Gulf board in 1927. He had helped install Claude Stephens as company president and rated him so highly that he recommended the stock to Street Hinton for the Trust Department in the early 1960s. When the company cut its dividend, an already skeptical Hinton soured on the stock, and there matters stood for a while.
汤米·拉蒙特是摩根财团派驻矿业界的代表，1927 年加入得克萨斯海湾公司董事会。他一手扶持克劳德·斯蒂芬斯坐上公司总裁之位，对其赏识有加，以至于在 20 世纪 60 年代初向街头欣顿的信托部门推荐了这支股票。当公司削减股息时，本就心存疑虑的欣顿对这支股票彻底失去了兴趣，此事便暂时搁置了下来。

In November 1963, Texas Gulf drilled a secret hole at Timmins, Ontario, that flabbergasted its chief mining engineer: it was richer than anything he had seen, richer than anything ever reported in the technical literature. This mother lode of copper, zinc, silver, and lead was later valued at up to \$2 billion, it was rich enough to supply 10 percent of Canada’s need for copper, 25 percent of its zinc. It was a vein so fabled that the ore sat right on the surface and could be “scooped up like gobs of caviar,” as one miner said.[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407664) As Texas Gulf ran tests that winter, its stock doubled, based on rumors of feverish miners mortgaging their houses to buy more shares in the company. Texas Gulf withheld an official announcement of the strike, fearing it would drive up the cost of surrounding property.
1963 年 11 月，Texas Gulf 在安大略省 Timmins 钻了一口秘密探井，结果让首席采矿工程师目瞪口呆：这矿脉之富，他前所未见，连技术文献里都从未有过记载。这条蕴藏铜、锌、银、铅的母脉后来估值高达 20 亿美元，足以满足加拿大 10%的铜需求、25%的锌需求。矿脉富得不可思议，矿石就裸露在地表，用一位矿工的话说，“像挖鱼子酱一样一铲一铲就能铲起来”。 [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407664) 那年冬天，随着 Texas Gulf 进行测试，流言四起——疯狂的矿工们抵押房子也要加仓——公司股价直接翻倍。Texas Gulf 迟迟没有官方发布发现消息，担心会推高周边地块的价格。

How to hush up reports of El Dorado? On April 10, 1964, Claude Stephens telephoned Tommy Lamont and asked whether he had heard the rumor. Lamont said he had. “Is there any truth in it?” Lamont inquired. “We don’t know,” Stephens replied. “We need a little time to evaluate our program in this particular area.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407815) Lamont advised him to delay any statement, suggesting that he wait for the April 23 annual meeting in New York to make a formal press announcement.
如何平息关于黄金城的传闻？1964 年 4 月 10 日，克劳德·斯蒂芬斯致电汤米·拉蒙特，询问他是否听到了那个谣言。拉蒙特回答称已有所耳闻。“其中有多少可信度？”拉蒙特问道。“我们也不太确定，”斯蒂芬斯答道，“我们需要一些时间来评估在该特定领域的计划。” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407815) 拉蒙特建议他暂缓任何声明，提议等到 4 月 23 日在纽约举行的年度会议上再正式向媒体公布。

The next morning, the *New York Herald Tribune* reported the biggest strike since Yukon days, a legendary bed of copper six hundred feet thick. On April 12, prodded by the SEC, Texas Gulf issued a statement of such cool understatement that the government later condemned it as false and misleading. Even though it knew it had at least ten million tons of copper and zinc, the company blandly described Timmins as a “prospect” that required more drilling for “proper evaluation.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407995) Texas Gulf scheduled a board meeting and press conference at the Pan Am Building in New York for April 16. Street Hinton had taken notice of the wildly gyrating stock and asked Lamont to report to him after the meeting.
次日清晨，《纽约先驱论坛报》报道称，这是自育空地区淘金热以来规模最大的罢工，传说中的铜矿脉厚度达六百英尺。4 月 12 日，在美国证券交易委员会（SEC）的催促下，Texas Gulf 发布了一份极其轻描淡写的声明，以至于后来被政府指责为虚假和误导性信息。尽管该公司明知自己至少拥有一千万吨铜锌矿藏，却仍平淡地将 Timmins 描述为一个"有待探勘"的矿区，需要更多钻探来"进行准确评估"。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2407995) Texas Gulf 计划于 4 月 16 日在纽约泛美大厦召开董事会和新闻发布会。Street Hinton 注意到了这只剧烈波动的股票，要求 Lamont 在会后向他汇报情况。

April 16, 1964 would be a day nightmarishly imprinted on the memories of both Hinton and Lamont. The Texas Gulf meeting began at nine sharp, with a full complement of fifteen directors. At about ten o’clock, twenty reporters were herded in for a press conference. It was “a New York corporate version of the old days when the old prospector rushed into the saloon to announce he had struck it rich,” said Jerry E. Bishop, who was there for the *Wall Street Journal.*[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2408130) The company wanted to keep all reporters in the room until the press conference was over. But once Claude Stephens finished his announcement, Norma Walter, covering her first spot news story for Merrill Lynch’s monthly magazine, managed to slip out a side door. She got the news on the firm’s in-house network at 10:29 A.M. A reporter for a Canadian wire service got out another door. Meanwhile, the other reporters stayed put, forced to watch color slides of the ore deposit. At the first moment of freedom, they dashed to the telephones to report the sensational news. Jerry Bishop’s dispatch appeared on the Dow Jones “broad tape” of market news at 10:55 A.M.

After the meeting, Lamont mingled with reporters, viewing core samples and color slides of Timmins. About 10:40 A.M., he phoned Hinton from the Texas Gulf offices. “Take a look at the ticker,” Lamont said. “There is an interesting announcement coming over about Texas Gulf.” “Is it good?” Hinton asked. “Yes, it’s good,” Lamont said.[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2408300) Later Hinton would testify to a curious sense of having seen the news flash over the broad tape. In fact, he failed to verify that it had appeared. Hinton then was treasurer of the Nassau Hospital Association and personally ran its portfolio. He at once telephoned the trading desk and bought three thousand Texas Gulf shares for the hospital. Then he advised Peter Vermilye to add the stock to a Morgan Guaranty profit-sharing plan and a mixed fund that invested for two hundred pension plans; Vermilye bought one thousand and six thousand shares respectively. All the while, the story hadn’t shown up on the Dow Jones broad tape, even though it had been flashed to 159 Merrill Lynch branches.

Unaware of having committed any crime, Lamont returned to his office at 15 Broad Street and at 12:33 P.M. bought three thousand Texas Gulf shares for himself and his family. Now the news had been on the Dow Jones wire for over an hour and a half. The stock market had reacted deliriously. Driven by a 7-point rise in Texas Gulf stock, by day’s end the New York Stock Exchange smashed all previous volume records. Far from feeling guilty, Hinton was disappointed the next day to learn how modestly Vermilye had bought. Only twelve days later did Lamont learn of the Trust Department purchases triggered by his call. He hadn’t advised Hinton to buy. He later claimed he was discharging a duty and not phoning in a hot tip.
拉蒙特并未意识到自己已犯下罪行，他回到布罗德街 15 号的办公室，在下午 12 点 33 分为自己和家人买入了 3000 股得克萨斯海湾公司的股票。此时，消息在道琼斯电讯上已经播报了一个半小时，股市反应近乎疯狂。受得克萨斯海湾股价上涨 7 点的推动，纽约证券交易所当日收盘时打破了所有历史成交量纪录。希顿非但没有感到内疚，反而在第二天得知弗米利只买了那么点股票时感到失望。直到十二天后，拉蒙特才得知自己的电话引发了信托部门的买入操作。他并没有建议希顿买入，后来他声称自己只是在履行职责，而不是在电话里透露内幕消息。

A year later, Texas Gulf directors were planning a reunion to savor their Timmins triumph. Though still a Morgan director, Tommy Lamont had now taken his mandatory retirement at age sixty-five. On the eve of the Texas Gulf meeting, a reporter reached him at home to say that the SEC had just charged him with “tipping off other bank officials” about the Timmins strike. Lamont was stunned. “I did not tip off other bank officials,” he shot back.[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2408517) The publicity value of his golden name was such that it dominated a front-page headline in the *Times* the next morning. He was tossed in with Texas Gulf executives and geologists who had blatantly traded on the inside information—a bit of editorializing that deeply embittered him. The *Times* sent a reporter to eighty-three-year-old fudge Ferdinand Pecora, now an elderly New York Supreme Court justice. Leaning back in his large red-leather chair on East Seventieth Street, Pecora marveled at the “extraordinary coincidence” that Thomas W. Lamont’s son was involved in the insider trading scandal: “It’s history repeating itself. It’s symptomatic of the temptations of Wall Street.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2408667)
一年后，Texas Gulf 的董事们计划重聚一堂，回味他们在 Timmins 的胜利。尽管仍是 Morgan 的董事，Tommy Lamont 此时已按规定在六十五岁退休。Texas Gulf 会议前夕，一名记者在他家中联系到他，称 SEC 刚刚指控他向其他银行高管透露了 Timmins 矿藏的“内幕消息”。Lamont 惊呆了。“我没有向其他银行高管透露内幕消息，”他反驳道。他名声的新闻价值使其成为第二天《纽约时报》头版头条的焦点。他被与那些公然利用内幕信息进行交易的 Texas Gulf 高管和地质学家混为一谈——这种编辑方式令他深感愤慨。《纽约时报》派记者采访了八十三岁的前法官 Ferdinand Pecora，他现在是纽约最高法院的资深大法官。在东七十街他那张宽大的红色皮椅里向后一靠，Pecora 对 Thomas W. Lamont 被卷入一场他已确信含有大量内幕交易的丑闻感到“异乎寻常的巧合”。 拉蒙特的儿子卷入了内幕交易丑闻：“历史在重演。这正是华尔街诱惑的典型症状。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2408667)

Although not directly charged, Hinton was crushed, thunderstruck. Inside the bank, he had a reputation for sometimes ferocious independence and for telling the rich and powerful where to go. Some people charged by the SEC were clearly guilty of insider trading. One geophysicist had bought shares the day before the news conference; another company official, the previous night. Ordinarily, insider prohibitions disappeared once news was publicly disclosed. Now the SEC promulgated a new standard, arguing that news had to be released *and* digested by the public before insiders could trade—a hazy definition that would prohibit buying for minutes or days afterwards. At first, the SEC identified 10:55 as the moment the legal embargo ended, when the Timmins news moved on the Dow Jones tape. A year later, it arbitrarily stretched the period to encompass Tommy Lamont’s purchase at the office at 12:33 P.M.—an outrageously long time after the news conference was disbanded. As Hinton said hotly, “If the SEC intends to make a new rule on that point, well and good . . . but it is not fair to write a rule retroactively.”[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409297)
有些被美国证券交易委员会（SEC）指控的人明显犯了内幕交易罪。一位地球物理学家在新闻发布会前一天买了股票；另一家公司高管则在发布会前一晚买入。通常情况下，一旦消息公开披露，内幕交易的禁令就自动解除。然而，SEC 这次颁布了一条新标准，声称内幕消息必须经过公开披露并被公众消化后，内部人士才能进行交易——这种模糊的定义可能导致在消息公布后的几分钟甚至几天内都不能买入。起初，SEC 把法律解禁时间定为 10:55，也就是蒂明斯公司的消息出现在道琼斯行情带上的那一刻。但一年后，它又随意延长了这个期限，把汤米·拉蒙特在中午 12:33 于办公室进行的购买也囊括在内——这比新闻发布会结束的时间晚了离谱的一大截。正如欣顿气愤地说：“如果 SEC 想在这方面制定新规，那没问题……但事后追溯性地定规矩，这可不公平。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409297)

Lamont’s defense team dwelled upon a supposed twenty-minute delay before Jerry Bishop’s report ran on the Dow Jones broad tape. Lamont was alleged to be the victim of a technical glitch. But Bishop and his editors didn’t think there was any delay at all. A year or two after the trial, Bishop figured out why there appeared to be a delay. Lamont’s lawyers had assumed that Norma Walter had filed her Merrill Lynch report after the news conference; in fact, she had filed her story twenty minutes before the other reporters. Whether or not Bishop is correct doesn’t affect the guilt or innocence of Tommy Lamont, who instructed Hinton to watch the tape. But if he is correct, Lamont must have gone to the phone almost immediately.
拉蒙特的辩护团队抓住了一个所谓的延迟：杰里·毕肖普的报道登上道琼斯新闻宽幅带之前有 20 分钟的延迟。拉蒙特被指是技术故障的受害者。但毕肖普和他的编辑们认为根本不存在任何延迟。审判后的一两年里，毕肖普终于弄明白了为什么会出现所谓的延迟。拉蒙特的律师原本假设诺玛·沃尔特是在新闻发布会后才提交了美林的报告；事实上，她比其他记者提前了 20 分钟提交了报道。毕肖普的判断是否正确，并不影响汤米·拉蒙特的有罪或无罪——他指示辛顿去盯着行情带。但如果毕肖普是对的，那么拉蒙特几乎一定是立刻就去打了电话。

In December 1966, District Judge Dudley J. Bonsai exonerated eleven of the thirteen defendants, including Lamont. He said the facts were public information once relayed to reporters on August 16, 1964, and that Lamont and Hinton had acted entirely properly. While the SEC appealed, Lamont’s health took a turn for the worse. He had a heart condition and suffered from fibrillations exacerbated by tension and depression. In April 1967, he checked into Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center; he never regained consciousness after undergoing open-heart surgery. As soon as the SEC heard the news, they called S. Hazard Gillespie, Lamont’s lawyer at Davis, Polk, and said they were dropping their appeal. Perhaps in atonement, the *Times* ran a fulsome, three-column obituary of Lamont that was disproportionate in length to his historical importance. It was a recurrence of an old Morgan condition—public exposure and political harassment leading to death.
1966 年 12 月，地区法官达德利·J·邦赛（Dudley J. Bonsai）宣告包括拉蒙特在内的十三名被告中的十一人无罪。他表示，自 1964 年 8 月 16 日向记者披露以来，这些事实已属公开信息，拉蒙特与辛顿的行为完全正当。然而，就在 SEC 提起上诉期间，拉蒙特的健康状况急转直下。他本就患有心脏问题，加之紧张与抑郁加剧了纤维性颤动的症状。1967 年 4 月，他入住哥伦比亚长老会医疗中心，在接受心脏直视手术后便再未苏醒。SEC 一听到消息，立刻联系了拉蒙特在戴维斯·波尔克律师事务所的律师 S·哈泽德·吉莱斯皮（S. Hazard Gillespie），表示将撤销上诉。或许是为了赎罪，《纽约时报》为拉蒙特刊登了一篇长达三栏、辞藻浮夸的讣告，篇幅与其历史重要性极不相称。这不过是摩根家族旧日厄运的重演——公开曝光与政治迫害终致死亡。

However misguided in its pursuit of Lamont, the SEC in the Texas Gulf case drew attention to the growing dangers of financial conglomeration in the Casino Age. As both commercial and investment banks developed huge, diversified operations, it would be increasingly difficult for them to segregate diverse and often legally incompatible operations. A few years later, the Morgan bank was accused of selling stock in the failing Penn Central based on information passed to the Trust Department by a lending officer—charges the bank always denied and that were never conclusively settled. Its Trust Department was finally moved to Fifty-seventh Street so that it would be physically separate from the rest of the bank. Years later, the problem posed by conglomeration would reappear for Morgans as it entered merger work, and it would generally shadow the burgeoning Wall Street banks and brokerage houses throughout the postwar years.
然而，美国证券交易委员会在德克萨斯湾公司一案中对拉蒙特的追查或许有失偏颇，但它确实引发了人们对“赌场时代”金融集团化日益加剧的风险的关注。随着商业银行和投资银行纷纷发展庞大而多元化的业务，它们越来越难以将各种往往在法律上互不相容的业务分隔开来。几年后，摩根银行被指控利用贷款官员传递给信托部的内幕信息，出售濒临破产的宾州中央铁路公司的股票——银行始终否认这些指控，且最终也未有定论。其信托部最终迁至五十七街，以便在物理上与银行其他部门分离。多年后，随着摩根银行涉足并购业务，集团化带来的问题再次浮现，并在整个战后时期一直笼罩着迅速崛起的华尔街银行和券商。

IN London, meanwhile, the City had sloughed off its sleepy atmosphere. By the mid-1960s, there were now two Cities. One was a clubby, bowler-hatted core that dealt in sterling and was protected from foreigners by the Bank of England. Here whispered references to Grandma meant the governor of the Bank of England. This world required attendance at good schools and the right contacts for success and was ruled by patrician families.
与此同时，伦敦金融城也摆脱了往日的沉寂。到了 20 世纪 60 年代中期，这里已经分化成两个截然不同的世界。一个是充满俱乐部氛围、戴着圆顶礼帽的精英圈子，专营英镑业务，并受到英格兰银行的庇护，免受外国势力侵扰。在这里，人们低声提及“奶奶”时，指的就是英格兰银行行长。要想在这个世界里取得成功，必须就读名校、拥有良好的人脉关系，而统治这个世界的则是贵族世家。

The second City was a rich colony of foreigners trading in the new Euromarkets, and it would eventually surpass the domestic City in size. As if deploying an army for battle, Morgan Guaranty sent its crack young executives to London. So many Americans flocked to the City that the British press muttered about “Yank banks” and dubbed Moorgate the Avenue of the Americas. Starting with a Bankers Trust issue for Austria in 1967, these foreign bankers put together large syndicated loans, for many countries, paving the way for massive Latin American lending in the 1970s. In this more egalitarian City, capital, not contacts, determined success.
第二个金融城是一个富饶的外侨聚集地，他们在新兴的欧洲市场进行交易，并最终在规模上超越了本土金融城。摩根保证信托如同调兵遣将一般，派遣其精锐的年轻高管进驻伦敦。蜂拥而至的美国人如此之多，以至于英国媒体私下抱怨“美国佬银行”，并将摩尔盖特街戏称为“美洲大道”。以 1967 年银行家信托为奥地利发行的债券为起点，这些外国银行家为众多国家组建了大规模银团贷款，为 20 世纪 70 年代对拉美的巨额放贷铺平了道路。在这个更为平等的金融城里，决定成功的关键是资本，而非人脉。

With the Guaranty merger, Morgans inherited a full-fledged London branch on Lombard Street, a short walk from Morgan Grenfell. This sharpened an old dilemma: were Morgan Grenfell and Morgan Guaranty partners or competitors? Protestations of fraternal warmth were often clouded by mutual suspicion. Morgan Guaranty’s London staff thought Morgan Grenfell “didn’t help them any more than any other merchant bank and, in fact, were inclined to be a little more suspicious of them,” declared Rod Lindsay, later a Morgan Guaranty president.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409447) When Lew Preston managed the London branch in the late 1960s, he found Morgan Grenfell opportunistic, quick to share bad deals but tending to hoard the good business. “Lew perceived the one-way-street nature of things,” remarked an associate. “The benefits were flowing in one direction.” Preston found it hard to convince his troops that Morgan Grenfell wasn’t a competitor.
随着与担保公司的合并，摩根继承了一家位于朗伯街、功能完备的伦敦分行，距离摩根建富仅几步之遥。这加剧了一个由来已久的困境：摩根建富与摩根担保究竟是合作伙伴还是竞争对手？兄弟情谊的表白常常被相互猜疑所掩盖。后来成为摩根担保总裁的罗德·林赛指出，摩根担保的伦敦员工认为摩根建富“并不比其他商人银行给予他们更多帮助，实际上反而更倾向于对他们心存疑虑。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409447) 20 世纪 60 年代末，卢·普雷斯顿管理伦敦分行时，他发现摩根建富颇为投机，分享坏交易时很积极，却倾向于独揽好业务。一位同事评论道：“卢看穿了这种单向街的本质——好处只流向一个方向。”普雷斯顿很难让他的团队相信摩根建富不是竞争对手。

There was cultural friction, too. Junior Morgan Guaranty people were excluded from the partners’ room at 23 Great Winchester Street when seniors went inside. Especially infuriating was Morgan Grenfell’s condescending treatment of a young British foreign-exchange trader, Dennis Weatherstone, son of a London transport worker. In 1946, Weather-stone, at the age of sixteen, had started as a bookkeeper at Guaranty’s London branch while attending night school. With his quick mind for figures, he excelled in the lightning-fast trading that was now transforming banking. In the mid-1960s, he was spotted by Lew Preston, who noticed that people kept stopping by his desk to ask questions; Preston promoted him to deputy general manager in London. The short, wiry Weatherstone was a local hero for working-class recruits to the City. Some of Morgan Grenfell’s aristocrats found no romance in this proletarian success story and snubbed Weatherstone, who later ended up president of the House of Morgan: he would decide that his career opportunities in New York were wider than those in the class-bound City.
文化摩擦同样存在。在 23 大温彻斯特街，摩根保证的低级职员被禁止进入合伙人会议室，只有资深员工才能入内。尤其令人愤慨的是，摩根建富对一位年轻的英国外汇交易员丹尼斯·韦瑟斯通颐指气使的态度——这位伦敦运输工人的儿子，1946 年 16 岁时便开始在保证银行伦敦分行担任簿记员，同时就读夜校。凭借对数字的敏锐头脑，他在快节奏交易领域脱颖而出，而这一领域正彻底改变银行业格局。20 世纪 60 年代中期，卢·普雷斯顿注意到总有人驻足他的办公桌请教问题，便提拔他为伦敦副总经理。身材矮小精干的韦瑟斯通，在投身金融城的工薪阶层新秀眼中，堪称本土英雄。 摩根建富的一些贵族成员对这段平民逆势翻身的故事毫无兴趣，甚至冷落了韦瑟斯通——他后来成了摩根银行的掌门人。他当时就认定，在纽约的发展空间远比等级森严的伦敦金融城要广阔得多。

Morgan Grenfell was an inbred, marginally profitable firm that was being strangled by its own pomposity, as the acrimonious aluminium war had shown. Its stagnation spawned gloomy humor. One City journalist, Christopher Fildes, recalled his editor keeping a headline in standing type: “FIRST WIN FOR MORGAN GRENFELL”; the editor joked he might need it some day.[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409609) Morgan Grenfell directors stuck to their old, informal style of doing business. They wouldn’t advertise, seldom held formal meetings, and made decisions informally in their partners’ room.
摩根建富是一家内部盘根错节、利润微薄的公司，正如那场火药味十足的铝业大战所揭示的那样，它正被自身的傲慢习气所窒息。这种停滞催生了黑色幽默。一位叫克里斯托弗·菲尔兹的金融记者回忆说，他的编辑一直留着一副排好的标题：“摩根建富首胜”；这位编辑打趣道，说不定哪天能用上。 [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409609) 摩根建富的董事们固守着陈旧的、不拘形式的行事风格。他们不打广告，很少召开正式会议，所有决策都在合伙人房间里非正式地敲定。

Entering the 1960s, the firm was owned by a tight-knit cluster of families—Grenfells, Smiths, Harcourts, and Cattos—and by Morgan Guaranty, which held a one-third share. To create incentives for its young executives, the bank issued new shares, which would gradually dilute the power of the old families. Morgan Grenfell also created new “assistant directors”—a seemingly petty organizational detail that for the first time allowed commoners to ascend into the formerly closed caste of directors: the senior partner, Viscount Harcourt, wanted to end the rigor mortis. In 1967, right before the second Lord Bicester—the jolly Rufie—died in a road accident, Harcourt recruited the virile Sir John Stevens, executive director of the Bank of England, to open up overseas outposts.
进入 20 世纪 60 年代，这家公司由几个紧密抱团的家族——Grenfells、Smiths、Harcourts 和 Cattos——以及持有三分之一股份的 Morgan Guaranty 共同掌控。为了激励年轻高管，银行发行了新股，这逐步稀释了旧家族的话语权。Morgan Grenfell 还增设了“助理董事”——看似不起眼的组织调整，却首次让平民得以跻身此前封闭的董事阶层：资深合伙人 Viscount Harcourt 决心打破僵化体制。1967 年，就在第二任 Bicester 勋爵——乐天派的 Rufie——死于车祸前夕，Harcourt 招募了精力充沛的英国银行执行董事 Sir John Stevens，负责开拓海外分支。

Among its young professionals, Morgan Grenfell’s stodgy reputation bred an exaggerated thirst for freedom. In 1967, Stephen Catto, the former partner’s son, invited film producer Dimitri de Grunwald for lunch at 23 Great Winchester. De Grunwald had a brainstorm: if distributors could finance film production through a global consortium, they could shatter America’s monopoly in filmmaking; he denied that only Americans could make westerns. Eager to show it could move with the times, Morgan Grenfell decided to back the venture.
在年轻的专业人士眼中，摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）保守刻板的名声反而催生了他们对自由的极度渴望。1967 年，前合伙人之子斯蒂芬·卡托（Stephen Catto）邀请电影制片人迪米特里·德·格伦瓦尔德（Dimitri de Grunwald）到大温彻斯特街 23 号共进午餐。格伦瓦尔德灵机一动：如果发行商能通过全球财团为电影制作融资，就能打破美国对电影制作的垄断；他否认只有美国人才能拍西部片。急于证明自己能与时俱进，摩根建富决定支持这一冒险计划。

To prove his point, de Grunwald signed up Sean Connery and Brigitte Bardot for *Shalako*, a film about an aristocratic safari of Europeans deep in Apache territory; it was an instant success. The idea of a staid old merchant bank financing Brigitte Bardot was symptomatic of Morgan Grenfell’s inner ferment, its itch for experimentation. Elated, de Grunwald talked of his dreams for developing film talent. “He believes the secret of success is not to play safe,” noted the London *Times.* These famous last words set him up for the catastrophic *Murphy’s War* (Murphy’s Law might have been more apt), on which Morgan Grenfell took such a bath that the Bank of England intervened; Grandma placed a man at 23 Great Winchester to straighten the mess out. “At least it kept us out of the shipping and property disasters of the early 1970s,” said Lord Catto philosophically.[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409776)
为了证明他的观点，德格伦瓦尔德签下了肖恩·康纳利和碧姬·芭铎出演《Shalako》，一部讲述欧洲贵族深入阿帕奇领地狩猎的影片，结果大获成功。一家古板的老牌商业银行竟然出资捧碧姬·芭铎，这恰恰反映了摩根格兰费尔银行内部的暗流涌动——他们对尝试新事物跃跃欲试。德格伦瓦尔德兴高采烈地谈起他培养电影人才的梦想。《伦敦泰晤士报》评论道：“他相信成功的秘诀不是求稳。”这句经典名言为后来的灾难《墨菲的战争》（或许叫墨菲定律更贴切）埋下了伏笔，摩根格兰费尔因此亏得血本无归，连英国央行都出手干预；"老祖母"在温彻斯特大街 23 号安插了一个人来收拾烂摊子。卡托爵士哲学地总结道：“至少这让我们躲过了 70 年代初航运和房地产的灾难。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409776)

But the firm was shedding its past caution too quickly. The man who accelerated the process was Lord William Harcourt, a great-grandson of Junius Morgan’s. With trim mustache and narrow face, round spectacles, and a bow tie, Bill Harcourt was a funny, snobbish man who wouldn’t deign to shake hands with a junior member of the firm. Behind the imperious air, he hid a wicked wit, an acute sense of the ridiculous. He had served as British economics minister in Washington and U.K. executive director at the IMF-World Bank and was known as Morgan Grenfell’s political fixer.
但这家公司摆脱过去谨慎态度的速度太快了。加速这一进程的是威廉·哈考特勋爵，他是朱尼厄斯·摩根的曾孙。比尔·哈考特留着修剪整齐的小胡子，窄脸圆镜，系着领结——一个风趣又势利的人，不屑与公司里的初级成员握手。在傲慢姿态之下，他隐藏着尖刻的幽默和对荒诞事物的敏锐感知。他曾担任英国驻华盛顿经济大臣、英国在国际货币基金组织和世界银行的执行董事，被誉为摩根建富的政治协调人。

The son of a colonial secretary and grandson of a chancellor of the Exchequer, Harcourt graduated from Eton and Oxford and married the only daughter of Baron Ebury. Harcourt and his wife lived in splendor at Stanton Harcourt, a family estate with several acres of gardens behind high walls. A visitor, Danny Davison of Morgan Guaranty, recalls gaping at its magnificence. “Gee, this is a magnificent place you have here,” he boyishly told Harcourt. “When did you get it?” “Ten-eighty,” was Harcourt’s crisp retort.
身为殖民地官员的儿子和财政大臣的外孙，哈考特毕业于伊顿公学和牛津大学，并迎娶了埃伯里男爵的独生女。哈考特夫妇住在斯坦顿哈考特庄园，极尽奢华——家族庄园内，高墙环绕着数英亩的花园。一位访客，摩根担保公司的丹尼·戴维森，回忆起当时被其壮丽景象惊得目瞪口呆的情景。“哇，你这地方可真气派啊，”他孩子气地对哈考特说，“你什么时候得到的？”“1080 年。”哈考特干脆利落地回了一句。

The contradictory Harcourt piloted the firm into the treacherous waters of controversial takeovers. They were common in the City by the mid-1960s, driven by a new management creed that only multinational corporations could survive in the new age. By 1968, seventy of Britain’s one hundred largest companies had been involved in takeovers in just two years. Where Morgan Grenfell had indignantly protested Siegmund Warburg’s tactics in the aluminium war, it was now determined that the firm would surpass him in verve and even ruthlessness.
矛盾重重的哈考特公司引导其企业步入了争议性并购的险境。到 20 世纪 60 年代中期，这类收购在伦敦金融城已屡见不鲜，其背后是一种新兴的管理信条——只有跨国公司才能在新纪元中生存。截至 1968 年，英国最大的 100 家公司中，有 70 家在短短两年内卷入了并购浪潮。摩根建富曾对西格蒙德·沃伯格在铝业大战中的策略愤然抗议，如今却决心要在魄力上乃至冷酷程度上超越他。

The takeover that revealed this shift was the battle for Gallaher, the manufacturer of Benson and Hedges cigarettes. In May of 1968, Morgan Grenfell and Cazenove, the blue-blooded stock broker, helped Imperial Tobacco sell off a 36.5-percent stake in Gallaher; Imperial had to divest it on antitrust grounds. The underwriting was a fiasco that left the underwriters with a third of the slumping shares and made Gallaher feel vulnerable to unwanted suitors. Indeed, by June, Philip Morris, backed by that Morgan Grenfell nemesis, Warburgs, began to close in on its prey.
揭示这一转变的收购案是对 Gallaher 的争夺——这家公司正是 Benson and Hedges 香烟的制造商。1968 年 5 月，声望显赫的股票经纪商 Morgan Grenfell 与 Cazenove 协助 Imperial Tobacco 出售其持有的 Gallaher 36.5%的股份；Imperial Tobacco 因反垄断原因必须剥离这部分股权。此次承销惨遭失败，承销商手中积压了三分之一不断下跌的股票，也让 Gallaher 自觉易受不速之客的觊觎。果然，到了 6 月，在 Morgan Grenfell 的死对头 Warburgs 的支持下，Philip Morris 开始向猎物步步逼近。

Following these events in New York, Barney Walker of American Tobacco told Bill Sword and Jack Evans of Morgan Stanley that he wanted to expand his Gallaher stake and rescue the company from Philip Morris. Sword called Ken Barrington of Morgan Grenfell, who had just returned to his flat after a summer lawn party with the queen at Buckingham Palace. Barrington and his colleague George Law promptly flew to New York. In American Tobacco’s boardroom, Barney Walker—a red-faced Irishman with no college degree—gave Sword and Barrington their marching orders. “Look, I wanna win,” he said. “I want an absolute guarantee that we will win. What will it take?” “I suppose it depends on the size of your purse,” said Barrington. “What’d he say?” grumbled Walker. “It depends how much money we’re prepared to pay,” an aide translated. Walker barked that money was no object. At this pivotal moment, it was industry, not the bankers, who demanded a new red-blooded, cutthroat style of business. Both Morgan Grenfell and Morgan Stanley would later plead, with some justice, that they were provoked into aggressive takeovers by their clients and that their metamorphosis into merger artists didn’t occur in a financial vacuum. In the 1960s, the bankers were still more the instruments than the engines of takeovers.
在纽约的这些事件之后，美国烟草公司的巴尼·沃克对摩根士丹利的比尔·斯沃德和杰克·埃文斯表示，他想增持加拉赫公司的股份，并把它从菲利普·莫里斯手中拯救出来。斯沃德给摩根建富银行的肯·巴林顿打了电话，后者刚在白金汉宫参加完女王举办的夏季草坪派对回到公寓。巴林顿和同事乔治·劳立刻飞往纽约。在美国烟草公司的董事会会议室里，巴尼·沃克——一位没上过大学、面色红润的爱尔兰人——给斯沃德和巴林顿下了命令。“听着，我要赢，”他说，“我要百分之百的保证，我们一定能赢。需要什么条件？”“那得看你钱包有多鼓了，”巴林顿说。“他说啥？”沃克咕哝道。“这取决于我们准备花多少钱，”一名助手翻译道。沃克大声说，钱不是问题。在这个关键时刻，是产业界——而非银行家——要求以一种充满血性、不择手段的新风格来经营生意。 后来，摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）和摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）都会理直气壮地辩称，它们是被客户逼着去搞激进收购的，它们蜕变为兼并艺术大师也并非发生在金融真空里。20 世纪 60 年代，银行家们在收购中更多是工具，而非引擎。

The Morgan Stanley-Morgan Grenfell team flew off to the City to mount the most vigorous operation in London Stock Exchange history, fortified by a \$150-million credit led by Morgan Guaranty. Stymied by LBJ’s capital controls, American Tobacco could afford only a partial bid, and this enforced economy led to the controversy. All over the City, insurance companies, pension funds, and other underwriters sat with unwanted, depressed Gallaher shares, gnashing their teeth at Morgan Grenfell. At a Sunday night meeting, the takeover team plotted its strategy with Sir Antony Hornby, senior partner of Cazenove and Company, who was assigned to handle the Stock Exchange operation. They decided to go into the Exchange the next morning and buy the shares from the May underwriters. This controversial tactic—which would redeem Morgan Grenfell in the eyes of the underwriters—also guaranteed that the takeover would enrich a handful of institutions while small shareholders wouldn’t learn about it until afterward.
摩根士丹利-摩根建富团队飞赴伦敦金融城，在摩根担保牵头提供的 1.5 亿美元信贷支持下，展开了伦敦证券交易所历史上最激进的操盘。受约翰逊总统资本管制掣肘，美国烟草公司只能提出部分收购要约，这种迫于无奈的节俭引发了争议。整个金融城里，保险公司、养老基金及其他承销商们手持着无人问津、跌跌不休的 Gallaher 股票，对摩根建富咬牙切齿。在周日夜间会议上，收购团队与负责操盘证交所业务的 Cazenove 公司高级合伙人安东尼·霍恩比爵士密谋策略。他们决定翌日清晨便杀入交易所，从五月承销商手中抢购股票。 这种引发争议的策略——在承销商眼中能够挽尊摩根建富——同时也确保了此次收购会让少数机构赚得盆满钵满，而小股东直到事后才得知内情。

Lately there had been many questions raised about the City. As takeovers grew bloody and unscrupulous, the Labour government threatened to impose strict regulations. To avert that, the Bank of England had created a committee, chaired by Morgan Grenfell’s Ken Barrington, to strengthen the Takeover Code. To enforce it, Sir Leslie O’Brien, the Bank of England governor, had set up a Takeover Panel with offices in the bank itself. Lacking statutory powers, the panel was criticized as unhealthily close to the people it oversaw. Yet despite Barrington’s role in devising the new code, Morgan Grenfell posed the first and stiffest challenge. Article 7 of the Takeover Code said no shareholder in a target company should receive an offer “more favorable than a general offer to be made thereafter to the other shareholders.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409942) This principle was challenged by the American Tobacco takeover, which was a so-called “street sweep”—a huge share purchase without a tender offer.
近来，有关伦敦金融城的质疑声不断。随着收购行为愈发血腥且无所顾忌，工党政府威胁要实施严格监管。为避免这种情况，英格兰银行成立了一个由摩根建富的肯·巴林顿主持的委员会，旨在强化《收购守则》。为确保守则的执行，英格兰银行行长莱斯利·奥布莱恩爵士设立了收购委员会，其办公室就设在银行内部。由于缺乏法定权力，该委员会被批评与监管对象关系过近，有失健康。然而，尽管巴林顿参与了新守则的制定，摩根建富却率先发起了最严峻的挑战。《收购守则》第 7 条规定，目标公司的任何股东都不应获得"比随后向其他股东发出的一般要约更优惠的报价。" [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2409942) 这一原则受到了美国烟草公司收购案的挑战，该收购属于所谓的"扫街"式收购——即未通过要约收购直接大规模购入股份。

On Monday morning, Hornby began whirlwind buying such as London had never seen. He went to the May underwriters and paid 35 shillings for Gallaher shares that had slumped to 18 shillings; he was instructed to purchase about five million shares that day. Instead, he was engulfed by a tidal wave of twelve million shares by morning’s end. Most small shareholders didn’t hear about the sudden windfall until lunchtime, by which point it was too late. They could get 35 shillings for only *half* of their holdings and were outraged by the apparent collusion between big institutions and merchant banks.
周一早上，霍恩比开始了伦敦前所未见的疯狂扫货。他来到梅氏承保商那里，以 35 先令的价格买进已跌至 18 先令的 Gallaher 股票——当天他被指示收购约 500 万股。然而，到上午收盘时，他却淹没在 1200 万股的洪流中。大多数小股东直到午餐时间才听说这场突如其来的暴富机会，但为时已晚。他们只能以 35 先令卖出手中一半的持股，并对大型机构与商业银行之间明显的勾结行为感到愤慨。

Morgan Grenfell had always cooperated with the Bank of England instinctively and sided with the financial authorities. Now it was acting more like a cheeky, defiant outsider, bent on stirring things up and testing the rules of the new Takeover Panel. Suddenly it resembled the assertive, iconoclastic Warburgs that had so offended its sense of propriety a decade before. In an amazing transformation, it had turned into its own worst enemy.
摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）向来本能地与英格兰银行保持合作，站在金融当局一边。如今，它却更像一个厚颜无耻、桀骜不驯的局外人，一心要搅动风云，挑战新成立的收购委员会（Takeover Panel）的规则。突然之间，它变得像十年前那个令其优越感备受冒犯的、激进叛逆的沃伯格（Warburgs）。一场惊人的转变，让它成了自己最大的敌人。

The Takeover Panel censured both Morgan Grenfell and Cazenoves. To the astonishment of the press, Harcourt and Hornby, those City demigods, didn’t beat their breast in atonement. Within an hour of the panel’s ruling, they simply rejected it out of hand! Here were Sir Antony Hornby, on the board of the Savoy Hotel and Claridge’s, and Lord William Harcourt, with his huge estate, thumbing their noses at authority and rebuffing a panel clothed with the prestige of the Bank of England. Hornby sounded like a ruffian: “There’s a certain cut and thrust in the market that is the essence of City dealing. If you’re going to wait for the amateurs then business will stop.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410112)
收购与兼并委员会对摩根格伦费尔银行和卡泽诺夫证券公司进行了公开谴责。令媒体震惊的是，那些金融界的泰斗——哈考特和霍恩比——并没有表现出悔过自新的姿态。委员会裁决后不到一小时，他们就当场予以否决！萨沃伊酒店和克拉里奇酒店的董事会成员安东尼·霍恩比爵士，以及拥有庞大产业的威廉·哈考特勋爵，竟然公然蔑视权威，对这家享有英格兰银行威望的委员会嗤之以鼻。霍恩比的话语听起来像个粗鲁的莽夫："市场上本就充满尔虞我诈，这才是金融交易的灵魂。要是总等着那些业余玩家，生意就没法做了。"

Bill Harcourt’s hauteur was memorable. He came up with a classic retort that expressed both his mischievous humor and magnificent contempt: “Can’t a man go in on Monday morning and buy a few shares?” He finished by telling a startled news conference that he would entertain no questions. “I am totally confident that the purchases are in fully conformity with the City code.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410257) Bill Sword later claimed that the American Tobacco team had secured approval of the Takeover Panel for its action but that Harcourt courageously withheld this information, lest the panel’s authority be weakened. But his initial manner seemed far more defiant than respectful toward the panel and was so reported in the press.
比尔·哈考特那令人难忘的傲慢姿态。他抛出一句经典反驳，既透着顽皮的幽默，也饱含极度的轻蔑：“难道一个男人不能在周一早上走进办公室，顺手买上几股股票吗？”最后，他在一场气氛紧张的新发布会上宣布，不会回答任何问题。“我完全相信，这些收购行为完全符合《城市准则》。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410257) 比尔·斯沃特后来声称，美国烟草团队已获得收购委员会的批准，但哈考特勇敢地隐瞒了这一信息，以免削弱委员会的权威。然而，他最初的态度似乎远非尊重，而是更为挑衅，媒体也据此进行了报道。

The public was in an uproar. Not only did the press back the panel, but the increasingly powerful institutional investors displayed almost unanimous support for censure. Three-quarters even favored further action. These institutions were the new countervailing powers; merchant banks no longer held all the cards. As would happen on Wall Street, the providers of capital—the mutual funds, pension funds, insurance companies and so on—were growing powerful at the expense of capital-short merchant banks in London and investment banks in New York.
公众一片哗然。不仅媒体力挺专家组，那些势力日益雄厚的机构投资者也几乎一边倒地支持谴责决议，甚至有四分之三的人主张采取进一步行动。这些机构构成了新兴的制衡力量，商业银行不再手握所有筹码。正如华尔街将要上演的剧情一样，资本提供方——共同基金、养老基金、保险公司等——正迅速崛起，而伦敦资本短缺的商业银行和纽约的投资银行则日渐式微。

In a carefully staged reconciliation, Lord Harcourt, a humble, hair-shirted suppliant, told the panel, “I should like to assure the committee that it was never my firm’s intention to show any lack of respect for the authority of the panel.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410404) The Takeover Panel allowed that perhaps he and Hornsby had misunderstood the rules. Although Morgan Grenfell emerged unscathed and banked a gigantic \$1-million fee, the damage to its reputation was severe. As London’s *Sunday Telegraph* said, “The days when Morgans spoke only to Cazenoves and Cazenoves spoke only to God were clearly at an end.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410554)
在一场精心策划的和解中，哈考特勋爵（Lord Harcourt）以谦卑懊悔的姿态恳求道：“我想向委员会保证，我们公司从未有意藐视专家组的权威。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410404) 收购委员会也表示，或许他和霍恩斯比（Hornsby）确实误解了规则。尽管摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）最终全身而退，并从中赚取了高达 100 万美元的佣金，但其声誉已然严重受损。正如伦敦《星期日电讯报》所言：“摩根只与卡泽诺夫（Cazenoves）交谈，卡泽诺夫只与上帝交谈的日子，显然已经结束了。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410554)

For Morgan Grenfell, the American Tobacco takeover showed that merchant banks with modest capital and large client lists could make a fortune in the new takeover game—precisely what Siegmund Warburg had seen. Here they could capitalize on old-school ties while both lending and securities work became commodity businesses, dominated by the biggest and strongest. At first, the old financial aristocracy was squeamish about performing hostile takeovers, giving Warburg his head start. But now, only ten years later, the old guard had already lost its compunctions, proving that it could behave with a ferocity ordinarily not associated with the country-house set.
对摩根建富而言，美国烟草收购案表明，那些资本规模不大但客户名单庞大的商业银行，也能在新的收购游戏中大赚一笔——这正是西格蒙德·沃伯格所预见到的。在这里，他们可以借助旧式关系大展身手，而借贷和证券业务则沦为巨头垄断的大宗商品生意。起初，老派的金融贵族们对执行敌意收购颇为顾忌，这给了沃伯格先发制人的机会。然而仅仅十年后，这些守旧派便早已抛却了道德顾虑，展现出与通常印象中乡绅阶层毫不相称的凶悍作风。

SEVERAL months after the American Tobacco takeover of Gallaher, Morgan Grenfell entered a publishing battle that confirmed its new zest for controversy. In this case, it assisted Rupert Murdoch in his purchase of the *News of the World*, a London tabloid. The paper was a trashy mix of sports, pinups, Tory editorials, and royal gossip. Its major coup was the 1964 purchase of Christine Keeler’s memoirs recounting her dalliance with Secretary of State for War John Profumo and a Russian military attache. The paper sold six million copies every Sunday, topping all English-language newspapers. Half the British adult population loyally enjoyed its salacious pages.
美国烟草公司收购加莱赫数月后，摩根建富投身一场出版业大战，这次战役印证了该行对争议话题的新热衷。在这场战役中，它为鲁珀特·默多克收购伦敦小报《世界新闻报》提供了助力。这份报纸内容低俗，混杂着体育、性感女郎、保守党社论和皇室八卦。其最大手笔是 1964 年购得克里斯汀·基勒的回忆录版权，书中详述了她与陆军大臣约翰·普罗富莫及一名俄罗斯武官的艳情。该报纸每逢周日销量达 600 万份，位居所有英语报纸之首。英国半数成年人热衷于品读其中淫秽内容。

Breaking into British newspapers was then exceedingly difficult: Fleet Street was the preserve of family fiefdoms, and major papers seldom came up for sale. “They were almost looked down upon as toy things by the proprietors,” said Lord Stephen Catto, who was to advise Murdoch.[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410702) Since the nineteenth century, the *News of the World* had been controlled by the Carr family, with Sir William Carr alone holding a 30-percent share. He was oblivious to the paper’s declining performance, said one observer, “because he was invariably drunk by half-past ten every morning, a habit which had earned him the popular alias ’Pissing Billy.’ ”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410868)
当时打入英国报界极其困难：舰队街曾是家族领地的专属领域，主流报纸极少易手。“报业主们几乎把报纸视为玩物，”后来成为默多克顾问的斯蒂芬·卡托勋爵回忆道。 [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410702) 自 19 世纪以来，《世界新闻报》一直由卡尔家族掌控，仅威廉·卡尔爵士一人就持有 30%的股份。据观察者称，他对报纸日渐衰落的表现浑然不觉，“因为他每天上午十点半前必定酩酊大醉，这一嗜好为他赢得了‘撒尿比利’的绰号。” [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2410868)

When Murdoch, Australia’s third-largest publisher, began scouting British newspapers in 1967, the objective was less to buy at rock-bottom prices than to crash the Fleet Street gates. He had already befriended Lord Catto, who was married to an Australian and, as a director of the Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation, would stop by to see Murdoch on his Asian tours. Easygoing and convivial, Stephen Catto was less starchy and rigidly upright than his father, the former Bank of England governor. But his breezy manner and quick smile hid a shrewd detachment. Educated at Eton and Cambridge, Catto had trained at Morgan Stanley and J. P. Morgan and was good with “colonials.”
当澳大利亚第三大出版商默多克在 1967 年开始物色英国报纸时，他的目标并非低价抄底，而是打入舰队街的大门。那时他已与卡托勋爵交好——这位勋爵娶了一位澳大利亚妻子，同时身兼汇丰银行董事，每次亚洲出差都会顺道拜访默多克。斯蒂芬·卡托为人随和风趣，不像他那位前英格兰银行行长的父亲那样古板拘谨。但他那洒脱的举止和爽朗的笑容背后，却藏着精明的疏离感。这位伊顿公学与剑桥大学出身的人物，曾在摩根士丹利和 J.P.摩根历练，对付起"殖民地人"来很有一套。

Catto was emblematic of his era, just as his father had symbolized the City’s solemn prewar rectitude. Catto *fils* didn’t shrink from publicity and liked the fact that Murdoch was on call, day or night, in pursuit of a hot deal. In 1967, Murdoch visited Catto, saying he wanted to expand beyond Australia. “Much to my surprise, he sat down and said, ’I want to buy the *Daily Mirror,’ “* recalled Catto, who patiently explained that Murdoch would have to pry it loose from the formidable International Publishing Corp. “Then let’s start buying IPC,” said Murdoch.[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411028) Catto liked Murdoch’s confident, forthright style and had a premonition of bigger things ahead.
卡托是他那个时代的标志性人物，正如他父亲象征着战前城市的庄重正直。小卡托不回避公众关注，并且喜欢默多克为了追逐热门交易而昼夜待命的事实。1967 年，默多克拜访卡托，表示他想扩展到澳大利亚以外。“让我大为惊讶的是，他坐下来就说，‘我要买下《每日镜报》，’”卡托回忆道，他耐心解释说默多克得从强大的国际出版公司手中夺过来。“那我们就开始买 IPC 吧，”默多克说。卡托欣赏默多克自信直率的风格，并预感更大的事情即将到来。

Accumulating a small stake in London’s *Daily Mirror*, Catto and Murdoch spotted a more promising opportunity when Derek Johnson, a cousin of Sir William Carr, decided to sell his 26-percent stake in *News of the World.* Residing in France and Switzerland, Johnson had variously been a pilot, a steeplechase rider, and an Oxford spectroscopy professor. He wanted to spare his sixth wife onerous inheritance taxes by selling off the stake. Yet he had enough reservations about the Carr family that he didn’t automatically sell them the shares.
当威廉·卡尔爵士的表亲德里克·约翰逊决定出售其持有的《世界新闻报》26%股份时，已在伦敦《每日镜报》积累少量股份的卡托和默多克看到了更有利可图的机会。长居法国和瑞士的约翰逊曾担任飞行员、越野骑手和牛津大学光谱学教授。他希望通过出售这部分股份，为第六任妻子免除繁重的遗产税。然而他对卡尔家族心存顾虑，并未直接将股份售予他们。

Carr knew that by controlling the shares, he would possess a solid majority holding in the tabloid and so offered 28 shillings per share for the stake. This was a foolishly stingy offer that fell a shilling short of the stock’s current price on the London Stock Exchange. Not bothering to reply, Johnson’s London banker, Jacob Rothschild, peddled the block at 37 shillings a share to Robert Maxwell, the publisher of Pergamon Press, the largest scientific and technical publisher in the country. Labeling the move “cheeky,” Carr had *his* banker, Hambros, begin buying shares in *News of the World.*
卡尔明白，只要控制了股份，他就能在这家小报中占据绝对多数，于是出价每股 28 先令收购该股权。这一报价吝啬得可笑，比伦敦证券交易所该股当前市价还低 1 先令。约翰逊的伦敦银行家雅各布·罗斯柴尔德懒得回复，转而以每股 37 先令的价格将这批股票卖给了珀加蒙出版社的掌门人罗伯特·麦克斯韦——这家出版社是英国最大的科技出版机构。卡尔将此举斥为"厚颜无耻"，随即命其银行汉布罗斯开始收购《世界新闻报》的股份。

Maxwell wasn’t yet the world-devouring mogul of the *Daily Mirror.* Like Murdoch, he saw the Carr tabloid, for all its peephole proclivities, as his entree into the upper echelon of publishing. Born into a Czecho-slovakian peasant family as Jan Ludwig Hoch, Maxwell emigrated to Britain in 1940, changed his name, served in the British army, and took over Pergamon Press after the war. Massive, brawny, and smart, he had a prickly style that scared the daylights out of respectable folk. He was a self-made man who had been elected to Parliament as an avowed socialist. Recalling the episode, Catto stressed Maxwell’s somewhat murky business reputation at the time: “Pergamon Press had the hard sell with their encyclopedias. They were virtually forcing them on poor people. There were also doubts about the way he managed his financing. He mixed his own private company with publicly quoted companies in a way that made one uneasy.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411194) Nevertheless, Maxwell made a tender offer of over 37 shillings per share for *News of the World*, making Carr’s bid look cheap and unsporting in comparison.
麦克斯韦当时还不是那个吞并《每日镜报》的传媒大亨。和默多克一样，他看中了卡尔家族那份热衷窥探隐私的小报，将其视为跻身出版业上层的敲门砖。麦克斯韦出生于捷克斯洛伐克的一个农民家庭，原名扬·路德维希·霍赫，1940 年移民英国，改了名字，在英国陆军服役，战后接管了培格曼出版社。他身材魁梧、精力充沛、头脑聪明，行事风格咄咄逼人，把那些体面人士吓得够呛。他是个白手起家的人，当选议员时公开宣称自己是社会主义者。回忆这段往事时，卡托强调了麦克斯韦当时颇为可疑的商业声誉：“培格曼出版社推销百科全书的方式就是硬塞，几乎是在强迫穷人购买。他的融资方式也让人起疑。他把自己的私人公司与上市公司搅在一起，这种做法令人不安。”” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411194) 然而，麦克斯韦尔对《News of the World》提出了每股超过 37 先令的收购要约，这样一来，卡尔的出价看上去既寒酸又不地道。

To the Carrs, Maxwell was a foreigner unfit to run their Tory paper. This set them up for the blandishments of Rupert Murdoch. One morning in the fall of 1968, Catto’s wife heard on the news that the *News of the World* stake was up for sale. “Why don’t you get your friend Murdoch to buy it?” she asked Stephen. He soon cabled Murdoch that he had spoken with Sir William Carr’s bankers, Hambros, which had expressed interest in his support against Maxwell. Murdoch needed no coaxing.
在卡尔家族看来，麦克斯韦尔是个外人，根本不配经营他们旗下的保守党报纸。这一点，让他们更容易被鲁珀特·默多克的甜言蜜语打动。1968 年秋天的一个早晨，卡托的妻子在新闻里听到《News of the World》的股份正在出售。“你为什么不让你朋友默多克把它买下来？”她问斯蒂芬。斯蒂芬很快给默多克发了电报，说自己已经和威廉·卡尔爵士的银行 Hambros 谈过，对方对他的支持表示兴趣，愿意联手对抗麦克斯韦尔。默多克根本不需要人劝。

On Saturday, October 19, Catto summoned Murdoch to come to London at once, tracking him down at a sporting event in Melbourne. Murdoch jumped on a plane to Sydney, where his wife, Anna, handed him a suitcase and his passport. Then he jumped on a Lufthansa plane for Frankfurt, where he switched to a flight for London. Landing at Heathrow’s Terminal 2, he evaded reporters thronging Terminal 3. London was by now rife with rumors about Murdoch’s arrival, and the press hunted for him relentlessly. Murdoch thought his room at the Savoy might be bugged. So Catto put him up at his country place, where he paced up and down, jotting notes on backs of envelopes. The looming battle gave Morgan Grenfell another chance to shuck its musty image. As the London *Observer* said, “Morgan Grenfell, having been for long regarded as a grouse-moor bank, with a record of unsuccessful defensive battles, is nowadays determined to show it can be as aggressive as the rest of ’em.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411320)
10 月 19 日星期六，卡托火速召唤默多克即刻来伦敦，当时正在墨尔本参加体育赛事的默多克被找到后，立即飞往悉尼。妻子安娜在那里递给他一个行李箱和护照。接着他转乘汉莎航空航班飞往法兰克福，再换机前往伦敦。在希思罗机场 2 号航站楼降落后，他巧妙避开了聚集在 3 号航站楼的大批记者。此时的伦敦早已流言四起，媒体对他穷追不舍。默多克怀疑自己在萨伏伊酒店的房间可能被监听，于是卡托安排他住进了自己的乡间别墅。他在那里踱步沉思，在信封背面匆匆记下要点。这场一触即发的较量，给了摩根·格伦费尔又一次摆脱保守形象的机会。正如《伦敦观察家报》所言：“长期以来被视为‘猎场银行’、防守战屡战屡败的摩根·格伦费尔，如今决心向世人证明，它也能像其他银行一样充满攻击性。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411320)

Despite the strident denunciations of Maxwell by the Carr forces, Murdoch resembled his competitor in many ways. Both were loners who hated committees and enjoyed a scrapping good fight. Even their politics weren’t so dissimilar. As an Oxford student, Murdoch had flirted with political radicalism, and his supposed anti-British sympathies were cited as reasons to oppose his ownership of the *News of the World.* Like Siegmund Warburg, Murdoch thought the British upper class weak and effete, and this emboldened him in his maneuvering. Murdoch’s output was already mixed. Though he published the staid *Australian*, he also put out a racy weekly called *Truth.* Nonetheless, Sir William Carr would embrace Murdoch as an unblemished white knight.
尽管卡尔阵营对麦克斯韦尔进行了激烈的谴责，但默多克与他的对手在许多方面颇为相似。两人都是独来独往的人，厌恶委员会，热衷于激烈的争斗。甚至连他们的政治立场也相差无几。默多克在牛津求学时曾对政治激进主义产生过兴趣，而人们认为他同情反英势力，这曾成为反对他收购《世界新闻报》的理由。与西格蒙德·沃伯格一样，默多克认为英国上层阶级软弱无力，这使他在周旋中更加大胆。默多克手下的出版物已经鱼龙混杂。他虽然出版了保守的《澳大利亚人报》，但也推出了一本名为《真相》的煽情周刊。尽管如此，威廉·卡尔爵士仍将默多克视为完美无瑕的白衣骑士。

During their country-house weekend, Catto outlined to Murdoch a three-pronged strategy that called for securing Carr’s support, beating Maxwell, then taking full control of *News of the World.* (Events would unfold in that precise order.) Carr wanted to use Murdoch to destroy Maxwell but without ceding full power to him. As Murdoch later said, “I was expected not as a white knight, but as a Sancho Panza to Carr’s Don Quixote.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411469) Catto laid out a plan to turn the tables on Carr. After buying a small stake in the *News of the World*, they would take advantage of Carr’s vulnerability to gain control of the tabloid. Catto’s guile was a revelation to Murdoch, who had equated the City with gentility, said one biographer; “yet here was Lord Catto, a principal in one of the City’s most celebrated banks, proposing a strategy that bordered on the Machiavellian in its slyness—perhaps even of the deceptive and fraudulent, as Sir William Carr, its victim, would later claim.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411631)
在他们的乡间别墅周末，卡托向默多克概述了一项三步走的战略：先争取卡尔的支持，再击败麦克斯韦尔，最终全面控制《世界新闻报》。（事件正是按这一顺序展开的。）卡尔想借助默多克摧毁麦克斯韦尔，但又不愿将全部权力交给他。正如默多克后来所说：“他们期待的我不是白衣骑士，而是卡尔这位堂吉诃德身边的桑丘·潘沙。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411469) 卡托制定了一个扭转局面的计划。在购入《世界新闻报》少量股份后，他们会利用卡尔的脆弱之处，最终控制这家小报。一位传记作者称，卡托的狡诈让默多克大开眼界——后者此前一直将伦敦金融城与绅士风度划等号；“然而这位卡托勋爵，作为金融城最著名银行之一的要员，竟提出了一项近乎马基雅维利式的狡猾策略——甚至可能带有欺骗和欺诈性质，正如受害者威廉·卡尔爵士后来所声称的那样。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411631)

Coached by Catto, Murdoch then had breakfast with Carr at his Cliveden Place residence on Tuesday morning, October 22. Murdoch brashly said that he would buy a majority stake in *News of the World* but that he wanted Carr to step aside as top executive. When Carr balked, Murdoch got up to leave. “I’m here to help you if you want it,” said Murdoch. “But I don’t like to waste time on dither.” “Sit down, Mr. Murdoch,” Carr replied.[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411765) In a complicated deal, they agreed that Murdoch would buy more *News of the World* shares and secure their combined majority. In exchange, Murdoch would get a 40-percent stake in the paper through newly issued shares. They would jointly manage the paper, but Carr would remain as chairman. Murdoch chafed at these terms, but Catto assured him it was the “foot in the door” he needed.[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411891)
在 Catto 的指导下，默多克于 10 月 22 日星期二上午在 Carr 位于 Cliveden Place 的住所与他共进早餐。默多克直言不讳地表示，他打算收购《News of the World》的多数股权，但希望 Carr 退居二线，不再担任最高管理者。Carr 犹豫不决时，默多克起身准备离开。“如果你需要帮助，我愿意伸出援手，”默多克说道，“但我不喜欢把时间浪费在犹豫不决上。”“请坐，默多克先生，”Carr 回答道。 [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411765) 在一项复杂的交易中，双方同意默多克将购买更多《News of the World》的股份，从而确保他们的联合持股占多数。作为交换，默多克将通过新发行的股票获得该报 40%的股份。他们将共同管理这份报纸，但 Carr 将继续担任董事长。默多克对这些条件感到不满，但 Catto 向他保证，这正是他需要的“入门之阶”。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2411891)

The first phase of the tabloid battle resembled a straight bidding war. Maxwell put together a 30-percent stake by buying the original Derek Johnson block plus additional purchases. The Murdoch forces adopted more controversial tactics. Carr’s banker, Hambros, bought *News of the World* shares in apparent violation of the Takeover Code, which forbade companies to buy their own shares. And through a Morgan Gren fell account, Catto bought a 3.5-percent stake in the paper that would be set aside for Murdoch.
小报之战的第一阶段类似于一场直接的竞价战。麦克斯韦通过收购德里克·约翰逊原本的股份加上额外购买，拼凑出了 30%的股份。默多克一方则采取了更具争议的手段。卡尔的银行 Hambros 购买了《世界新闻报》的股份，这明显违反了禁止公司购买自身股份的《收购守则》。此外，卡托通过摩根建富的一个账户购入了该报 3.5%的股份，这部分股份将留给默多克。

In the American Tobacco-Gallaher affair, Lord Harcourt had arrogantly dismissed the press at his peril. Now, in announcing his pact with Carr, Murdoch hired a publicist. Catto found this departure exciting—while his father would doubtless have found it abhorrent and beneath a banker’s dignity. At a press conference on Wednesday, October 23, Murdoch, age thirty-seven, made his debut on the British stage. He was tagged the “quiet Australian” by the London press, which knew little about him. At first relaxed and grinning, he tried to answer questions frankly, but he was stunned by a barrage of hostile questions accusing him of violating the Takeover Code. Catto sat quietly by his side, a contemplative finger at his lips.
在美国烟草-加莱赫事件中，哈考特勋爵曾傲慢地无视媒体，最终自食其果。如今，默多克在宣布与卡尔达成协议时，聘请了一位公关顾问。卡托觉得这种改变令人兴奋——而他的父亲若在世，想必会对此深恶痛绝，认为有失银行家的体面。10 月 23 日周三的新闻发布会上，37 岁的默多克首次在英国舞台上亮相。伦敦媒体对他知之甚少，给他贴上了“安静澳大利亚人”的标签。起初他神态放松、笑容满面，试图坦诚回答问题，却被一连串指责他违反《收购守则》的敌意质问所震惊。卡托安静地坐在他身旁，手指若有所思地抵着嘴唇。

Robert Maxwell protested to the Takeover Panel what he saw as a side deal between management and Murdoch not in the best interests of shareholders. He also claimed that the Carrs were violating the code by buying their own shares through a surrogate, Hambros. Maxwell had boosted his bid to a hefty 50 shillings per share, but was thwarted by the pact negotiated at breakfast at Cliveden Place. The panel found enough merit in the charges and countercharges to suspend trading in *News of the World* stock for two months. At the time of the standstill, neither side had a 51-percent stake. The panel turned the battle into a proxy fight to be decided at a general shareholders’ meeting on January 2, 1969. Catto rallied Murdoch’s spirits, saying the decision enhanced their chances for victory. Shortly before the meeting, the panel said neither side could cast votes obtained before Maxwell’s first tender offer.
罗伯特·麦克斯韦向收购与兼并小组提出抗议，他认为管理层与默多克之间达成了私下交易，这并不符合股东的最佳利益。他还声称，卡尔家族通过代理行汉布罗斯购买自家股票，违反了相关准则。麦克斯韦将收购报价大幅抬高至每股 50 先令的惊人价位，但最终被克里夫登宫早餐谈判达成的协议所挫败。收购与兼并小组认为双方的指控与反指控均有理有据，因此决定暂停《世界新闻报》股票交易两个月。在停牌期间，任何一方都未持有 51%的股份。该小组将这场较量转变为代理权之争，交由 1969 年 1 月 2 日的股东大会裁决。卡托为默多克打气，称这一决定反而增加了他们获胜的几率。就在股东大会前夕，收购与兼并小组宣布，双方均不得行使在麦克斯韦首次要约收购前获得的投票权。

Sir Leslie O’Brien, the Bank of England’s governor, fretted that the angry contest would wreck the code. Voluntary self-regulation seemed a feeble way to restrain the mercenary tendencies of the Casino Age. At the Lord Mayor’s banquet on November 11, Prime Minister Harold Wilson had reiterated his dislike of the new marauding style in the City, exhorting merchant bankers to police their own behavior. Once again, Morgan Grenfell, long part of the City establishment, openly sparred with City authorities.
英格兰银行行长莱斯利·奥布莱恩爵士忧心忡忡，担心这场激烈的争斗会破坏行业准则。在赌场时代，指望自愿自律来约束唯利是图的倾向，似乎力不从心。11 月 11 日的伦敦市长宴会上，首相哈罗德·威尔逊再次表达了对金融城新式掠夺作风的不满，敦促商业银行家们自行规范行为。而作为金融城老牌机构之一的摩根建富，又一次公开与金融城监管当局叫板。

When the Carr-Murdoch deal was voted on at the Extraordinary General Meeting held on January 2, the atmosphere was ugly and xenophobic. The Great Queen Street hall was packed with ringers. Murdoch later admitted that some pro-Carr shareholders who couldn’t attend had temporarily signed over their shares to *News of the World* staffers. When Sir William Carr marched in like a benevolent patharch, he was lustily cheered. Dressed in a flashy blue suit, Maxwell was hooted and booed by a chorus shouting “Shame!” “Withdraw!” and “Go home!”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412025)
在 1 月 2 日召开的特别股东大会上，卡尔-默多克交易进行表决时，现场气氛充满敌意和排外情绪。大皇后街大厅里挤满了临时凑数的股东。默多克后来承认，一些无法到场的亲卡尔股东曾临时将股份转让给《世界新闻报》的员工。当威廉·卡尔爵士像一位仁慈的族长般阔步走进来时，现场爆发出热烈的欢呼。而身穿闪亮蓝色西装的麦克斯韦尔，则在齐声高喊“可耻！”“退出！”和“滚回去！”的嘘声和倒彩声中狼狈不堪。 [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412025)

Although Maxwell’s standing offer of 50 shillings was the financially superior one, the discussion pivoted on his fitness to run the paper. While Murdoch pretended that he would retain Carr as chairman, Maxwell candidly said he would replace him, telling the paper’s publisher, “Every time I have a haircut at the Savoy late in the afternoon, around 4 P.M., I find you and your *News of the World* cronies still drinking Martinis and I don’t think that is suitable training for any chairman of mine.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412208) Maxwell’s combative style didn’t work nearly as well as Murdoch’s crafty, self-effacing manner. In the final vote, the Carr-Murdoch group got 4.5 million shares, and Maxwell, 3.2 million. Murdoch celebrated with a party at his Embankment flat that night. For Morgan Grenfell, it started a long relationship with the world’s most powerful publisher. As an influential board member of Murdoch’s News International board, Catto would negotiate his future U.K. newspaper purchases, including that of the London *Times.* Yet the relationship between Murdoch and Morgan Grenfell would have a curious ambivalence, for the banking side of the firm wouldn’t lend to Murdoch, believing his operations too dangerously leveraged.
尽管马克斯韦尔公开出价 50 先令在财务上更为优厚，但讨论的焦点却转向了他是否适合经营这份报纸。默多克假装会保留卡尔的主席职位，而马克斯韦尔则直言不讳地表示要替换他，他对报社交付人说：“每次我在萨沃伊酒店下午四点多理发时，总看到你和你的《世界新闻报》那帮老友还在喝马提尼，我认为这对我未来的任何一位主席来说都不是合适的训练。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2412208) 马克斯韦尔咄咄逼人的风格远不如默多克那狡猾而谦逊的做派奏效。最终投票中，卡尔-默多克集团获得了 450 万股，马克斯韦尔获得 320 万股。当晚，默多克在泰晤士河堤的公寓里举办派对庆祝。对摩根建富而言，这开启了与这位全球最强大出版商的长期合作。作为默多克新闻国际董事会颇具影响力的成员，卡托日后还为其在英国未来的收购交易进行了谈判。 报业收购案中，也包括了《伦敦时报》。然而默多克与摩根·格伦费尔之间的关系却暗藏玄机——这家银行的信贷部门拒绝向默多克放贷，认为他的运营杠杆过高，风险太大。

By mid-1969, Sir William Carr saw that with Murdoch he had admitted a Trojan horse. The Australian continued to buy shares after the meeting, so he would safely control more than 50 percent of the paper. He fired Carr’s jingoistic editor, Stafford Somerfield. Then he demoted Carr to president and took the chairmanship himself. Murdoch was launched in Britain. That December, he bought the London *Sun*, which would prove his real profit maker. Loading it with pinups, he soon doubled its circulation, to two million, and established it as Britain’s biggest daily paper.
到了 1969 年中期，威廉·卡尔爵士终于意识到，默多克这匹“特洛伊木马”已被他亲手迎进了门。会议结束后，这位澳大利亚人继续买进股票，稳稳掌控了该报逾 50%的股份。他先是炒掉了卡尔手下那位狂热的民族主义主编斯塔福德·萨默菲尔德，接着把卡尔降职为名誉主席，自己坐上了董事长之位。默多克就此在英国站稳脚跟。同年 12 月，他买下了《伦敦太阳报》，这家报纸后来成了他真正的摇钱树。他让报纸充斥着性感女郎照片，发行量很快翻了一番，达到 200 万份，一跃成为英国发行量最大的日报。

The American Tobacco-Gallaher affair and the Murdoch-Maxwell brawl prompted reform of the Takeover Panel, which got a full-time chairman in Lord Hartley William Shawcross, a Morgan Guaranty adviser and a director of Morgan et Compagnie International. The code was revised to forbid partial bids of the American Tobacco variety, and new sanctions were introduced. In a single tumultuous year, Morgan Grenfell’s character had changed almost beyond recognition. Mergers were suddenly pitching in a third of the firm’s profits. It was operating publicly and flouting authority in a way that would have been inconceivable a decade earlier. Though the firm still issued securities and managed money, it increasingly would take its tone from the piratical world of mergers. This change would also affect the firm’s sociology; now a premium would be placed on intellect and experience, Morgan Grenfell would attract a new breed of talented, well-trained lawyers and accountants who could master the intricacies of the complex deals. The new City would be more ruthless but also more democratic, and it would look much more like the Warburgs of the 1950s than the Morgan Grenfell of the 1950s.
美国烟草-加拉赫事件以及默多克与麦克斯韦的激烈争斗，促使收购委员会进行改革，由摩根担保公司顾问兼摩根国际公司董事哈特利·威廉·肖克罗斯勋爵出任全职主席。规则被修订，禁止了美国烟草式的部分收购，并引入了新的制裁措施。在短短一年动荡中，摩根建富的特性几乎变得面目全非。并购业务突然贡献了公司三分之一的利润。它公开运作、蔑视权威，这在十年前简直不可想象。尽管该公司仍在发行证券和管理资金，但其行事风格日益受到并购领域那海盗般作风的影响。 这一变化也将影响公司的社会生态；如今，智慧和经验将更受重视，摩根建富会吸引一批才华横溢、训练有素的律师和会计师，他们能驾驭复杂交易的错综细节。新的伦敦金融城将更冷酷无情，但也更民主，而且它会更像 20 世纪 50 年代的华宝银行，而非 20 世纪 50 年代的摩根建富。
