# Chapter Thirteen: Jazz

BY 1924, the House of Morgan was so influential in American politics that conspiracy buffs couldn’t tell which presidential candidate was more beholden to the bank. As far as the partners’ support of a candidate was concerned, most backed Calvin Coolidge—out of ideological comfort and respect for his friendship with Dwight Morrow. Coolidge’s running mate was Charles Dawes, who had profited from the sudden renown of his plan for Germany’s reparations payments. Others might dismiss Coolidge as dour and complacent, but Jack Morgan perceived in him an extraordinary blend of deep thinker and moralist: “In a somewhat long life, I have never seen any President who gives me just the feeling of confidence in the Country and its institutions, and the working out of its problems, that Mr. Coolidge does.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284235) He disagreed that Coolidge was a tool of business—sure proof to some that he was. Between the White House and the House of Morgan there existed clear amity, moving *The New Republic’s* TRB to say, “I would rather not have these Morgans boys quite so much at home around the White House.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284433)
到 1924 年，摩根家族在美国政治中的影响力已如此之大，以至于阴谋论者都分不清哪位总统候选人与这家银行关系更紧密。至于合伙人们支持哪位候选人，多数人选择了卡尔文·柯立芝——这既出于意识形态上的认同，也出于对他与德怀特·莫罗友谊的尊重。柯立芝的竞选搭档是查尔斯·道斯，此人因针对德国赔款问题的计划突然声名鹊起，并从中获利。其他人或许认为柯立芝沉闷又自满，但杰克·摩根却在他身上看到了一种深邃思想家与道德家的非凡结合：“在我漫长的人生中，从未见过哪位总统能像柯立芝先生那样，让我对国家、制度及其解决问题的能力充满信心。” [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284235) 他不同意柯立芝是商业工具的论调——这在某些人看来，恰恰证明了他就是。 白宫与摩根家族之间存在着显而易见的亲密关系，这促使《新共和》杂志的 TRB 评论道：“我真不愿看到摩根家这些少爷们在白宫如此随意自在。”” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284433)

The bank’s peerless renown in the Roaring Twenties was such that the Democratic candidate was the chief Morgan lawyer, John W. Davis. Davis was a backgammon and cribbage partner of Jack Morgan’s—they played for a nickel a game—and they both belonged to the Zodiac Club, a secret society in which each member took a different astrological sign. A former solicitor general and ambassador to the Court of Saint James’s, Davis was recruited by Allan Wardwell in 1920 to join the law firm that would become Davis, Polk, and Wardwell. They were counsel to both J. P. Morgan and Company and Guaranty Trust. Taking charge of Davis’s life in his vigorous style, Harry Davison had whisked him through a weekend of golf at the Piping Rock Country Club on Long Island and convinced him to join Wardwell’s firm. In his familiar role as Henry Higgins, Davison even influenced Davis’s choice of a home: “We must find the right place in our own island neighborhood.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284610) Davis bought a Locust Valley estate right near Jack’s and Harry’s. He had exactly the right credentials for a Morgan man: debonair and dignified, he favored a larger U.S. role in Europe, supported the League of Nations, and opposed the welfare state and a progressive income tax. He was also a devout Anglophile and one of the duke of Windsor’s lawyers. Another friend, King George V, termed him “the most perfect gentleman” he had ever known.[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284779)
这家银行在咆哮的二十年代声望无与伦比，以至于当时的民主党总统候选人就是摩根的首席律师约翰·W·戴维斯。戴维斯和杰克·摩根是玩双陆棋和克里比奇牌的搭档——他们每局赌五分钱——两人还同属黄道十二宫俱乐部，这是一个秘密社团，每位成员都对应不同的星座。戴维斯曾任副检察长和驻圣詹姆斯宫大使，1920 年被艾伦·沃德韦尔招募，加入了后来更名为 Davis, Polk, and Wardwell 的律师事务所。他们同时为 J. P. Morgan and Company 和 Guaranty Trust 提供法律顾问。哈里·戴维森以他雷厉风行的作风接管了戴维斯的生活，带他在长岛 Piping Rock Country Club 度过了一个打高尔夫球的周末，成功说服他加入沃德韦尔的律所。戴维森就像亨利·希金斯那样，甚至左右了戴维斯选择住宅：“我们得在咱们岛上这片儿给你找个合适的地方。”” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284610) Davis 在 Locust Valley 买了一座庄园，紧邻 Jack 和 Harry 的住处。他具备成为摩根一员的绝佳资历：风度翩翩、威严庄重，主张美国在欧洲扮演更大角色，支持国际联盟，反对福利国家和累进所得税。他还是个不折不扣的亲英派，并担任温莎公爵的律师之一。另一位朋友英王乔治五世称他为“所知最完美的绅士”。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284779)

At first, Davis was deterred from seeking the Democratic nomination because of the Morgan handicap. Then he published a cogent letter stating that a lawyer could have rich clients *and* maintain the public trust. His cause was taken up by Walter Lippmann of the *New York World*, who praised his talent and integrity. Davis’s Democratic opponent, William McAdoo, drew support from the South and West, always aflame with anti-Morgan sentiment. At the June 1924 national convention, William Jennings Bryan, mustering the strength for one last vendetta against the bank, said, “This convention must not nominate a Wall Street man. Mr. Davis is the lawyer of J.P. Morgan.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284924) In fact, the convention was so bitterly and hopelessly divided that Davis received the nomination after a record 103 ballots—by which point the prize was worthless. The Republicans retained power.
起初，摩根集团的不利影响阻碍了戴维斯争取民主党提名。随后，他发表了一封言辞有力的公开信，指出律师可以既服务富裕客户又不失公众信任。《纽约世界报》的沃尔特·李普曼为他发声，盛赞其才华与正直。戴维斯的民主党对手威廉·麦卡杜获得了南方和西部的支持，这些地区始终弥漫着反摩根情绪。1924 年 6 月的全国代表大会上，威廉·詹宁斯·布莱恩鼓起最后力量对银行进行报复，他说："这次大会绝不能提名一个华尔街的人。戴维斯先生是 J.P.摩根的法律顾问。" [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2284924) 实际上，大会分歧严重且毫无希望，戴维斯在创纪录的 103 轮投票后才获得提名——而此时这一奖项已毫无价值。共和党保住了权力。

One industrialist called Coolidge’s 1924 victory a cocktail for financial markets, and the decade now began to bubble and fizz. It was the Gatsby era on Wall Street, with money-making newly glorified. Young Ivy Leaguers turned away from the social protest of the late teens and flocked to Wall Street. In Pierpont’s day, the Street had been raw and brawling, no place for the squeamish. Now it became smart and tony, and “many of the old-line brokerage firms were staffed by the sons of the rich—to give them something to do during the mornings.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285093) Stockbrokers fancied themselves squires, bred polo ponies, and hunted foxes. Charles E. Mitchell, chairman of National City Bank, traveled about in a special railroad car, complete with kitchen and chef, making business tours as if he were a whistle-stopping president. Corporate directors went to board meetings by private railroad car, the status symbol of the day.
一位实业家将柯立芝 1924 年的胜选称为金融市场的一剂鸡尾酒，此后十年便开始沸腾冒泡。华尔街进入了盖茨比时代，赚钱被赋予了新的荣光。常春藤联盟的年轻人们不再像十年前那样投身社会抗议，而是蜂拥至华尔街。在皮尔庞特的时代，华尔街还是粗野喧嚣之地，胆小者无法立足。如今它变得时髦而高雅，“许多老牌经纪公司里全是富家子弟——好让他们早上有事可做。”股票经纪人自诩为乡绅，培育马球小马，猎狐为乐。国民城市银行董事长查尔斯·E·米切尔乘坐配有厨房和主厨的专用火车车厢巡回商务，仿佛一位沿途拉票的总统。公司董事们也乘私人火车车厢参加董事会——这是当时地位的象征。

For the House of Morgan, it was a time of unmatched supremacy. The firm attained a pinnacle of success no other American bank would ever match. It stood at the gateway to American capital markets just as the whole world clamored to gain entry. To those who penetrated its tall glass doors, it offered a world of fireplaces and leather armchairs, as sedate and intimate as a British gentleman’s club. All the secretaries were male, although their assistants might be female. As one reporter said, “Entering there was like stepping into a page of Dickens.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285252) The partners’ rolltop desks were apt symbols for the bank. They were made of mahogany or walnut, honeycombed with compartments and closed by sliding down the tambour top; they expressed the private, discreet Morgan style. The employees were as seduced by this atmosphere as the clients. As publicity man Martin Egan once said, “If the firm ever fired me I was lost, having been spoiled for service with any other outfit in the world.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285402)
对摩根银行而言，那是一段无可匹敌的鼎盛时期。这家公司登上了美国其他银行永远无法企及的成功巅峰。当全世界都渴望叩开美国资本市场的大门时，它恰恰就站在那扇入口处。那些穿过高大玻璃门走进来的人，会发现一个壁炉与皮扶手椅的世界——沉稳而私密，宛如英国绅士俱乐部。所有秘书都是男性，不过他们的助理可能是女性。正如一位记者所言："踏进那里，就像走进了狄更斯笔下的篇章。" [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285252) 合伙人们使用的翻盖卷帘办公桌正是这家银行的绝佳象征。这些桌子用红木或胡桃木制成，布满蜂窝般的小隔间，滑动卷帘桌面即可关闭，彰显出摩根银行私密而谨慎的风格。员工们和客户一样沉醉于这种氛围。正如公关人员马丁·伊根曾说："如果公司开除我，我就彻底完了——因为被这里惯坏后，再也没法去世界上其他任何地方工作了。" [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285402)

The vast majority of people walking by 23 Wall couldn’t bank there. As a wholesale bank, J. P. Morgan and Company would take deposits only from important clients—large corporations, other banks, foreign governments. Like other private New York banks, it rejected deposits from the general public and accepted money only from wealthy people with proper introductions. It paid no interest on deposits of less than \$7,500 and held no deposit of less than \$1,000.
行走在华尔街 23 号门前的绝大多数人，都无法在那里开户存款。作为一家批发银行，J. P. Morgan and Company 只接受重要客户的存款——大型企业、其他银行以及外国政府。与纽约其他私立银行一样，它拒绝接受普通公众的存款，只有经过适当介绍的富人才有资格存钱。该行对低于 7500 美元的存款不支付利息，且不接受低于 1000 美元的存款。

The bank’s power was more than monetary. No other firm had its political links or spoke with its authority. At a time when the Anglo-American axis reigned supreme, it was embedded in the power structure of both Washington and Whitehall. Reporters tried to isolate its essence. “It is not a large bank, as Wall Street banks go,” said the *New York Times. “A* dozen other institutions have much larger resources. . . . What really counts is not so much its money as its reputation and brains. . . . It is not a mere bank; it is an institution.”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285594) Trust, goodwill, integrity—these were the strengths always cited by business clients. This was only part of the story, but it mattered a great deal that the bank always paid its bills promptly, honored its commitments, and stood by its clients during emergencies.
这家银行的实力远不止于金钱。没有哪家公司拥有它那样的政治关系网，或说话有它那样的分量。在英美轴心主导世界的年代，它深深嵌入华盛顿和白厅的权力结构之中。记者们试图捕捉它的本质。《纽约时报》写道："按照华尔街银行的标准，它不算大。其他十几家机构的资源比它雄厚得多……真正重要的不是它的钱，而是它的声誉和智慧……它不只是一家银行，它是一个机构。"信任、商誉、诚信——这些始终是商业客户们津津乐道的优势。这只是故事的一部分，但至关重要的是，这家银行始终按时付款、信守承诺，并在客户遭遇危机时坚定地站在他们身边。

As in Pierpont’s day, the bank seemed remarkably small beside the scope of its work. The Morgan houses preferred smallness, which ensured intimate contact among the partners. Harry Davison used to say \$400 million was all they could handle without diluting Morgan style. By the end of the 1920s, there would be fourteen partners at 23 Wall, eight at Drexel in Philadelphia, and seven each at the Morgan houses in London and Paris. At these firms, the partners all sat in one big room in the venerable City tradition. Each offered a different secret for the firm’s success. George Whitney saw conservative financial management as the critical factor: partners never fooled themselves about the quality of their loans and stayed 80 percent liquid at all times. Lamont had a flywheel theory—the bank thrived because it was cautious in boom times and aggressive in bad times. Jack would later state memorably that the bank did “first-class business in a first-class way.”
如同皮尔庞特时代一样，这家银行与其业务规模相比，显得格外小巧。摩根旗下的各家机构偏爱这种精干，因为它能确保合伙人之间的紧密联系。哈里·戴维森曾说过，四亿美元是他们能驾驭的极限，再大就会稀释掉摩根风格。到 20 年代末，华尔街 23 号共有 14 位合伙人，费城德雷克塞尔有 8 位，伦敦和巴黎的摩根机构各有 7 位。在这些公司里，合伙人们都坐在同一个大房间里，沿袭着伦敦金融城的古老传统。每位合伙人都对公司的成功有着各自不同的秘诀。乔治·惠特尼认为审慎的财务管理是关键因素：合伙人从不自欺欺人地评估贷款质量，并始终保持 80%的流动性。拉蒙特则抱持一种“飞轮理论”——银行之所以繁荣，是因为它在繁荣时期保持谨慎，在萧条时期敢于进取。杰克后来留下一句名言：这家银行以“一流的方式，做一流的生意”。

Wall Street legend accurately claimed that Morgan partners made \$1 million a year; with Jack Morgan and Tom Lamont, this figure rose to as much as \$5 million by decade’s end. A Morgan partnership was the plum of American banking. Many firms chose partners who brought in new capital or new clients, but J. P. Morgan stuck to Pierpont’s meritocratic approach; any white, Christian male might qualify. Many partners had family ties, and new Morgans, Lamonts, and Davisons would join the bank in the 1920s; 23 Wall never had rules against nepotism. But the outstanding partners, those who created the Morgan mystique—Harry Davison, Tom Lamont, Dwight Morrow, and Russell Leffing-well—were chosen strictly on their merits. As much as any other factor, the caliber of the men recruited would explain the extraordinary staying power of the House of Morgan.
华尔街传奇人物曾准确指出，摩根合伙人年薪可达百万美元；而到了十年末，杰克·摩根和汤姆·拉蒙特的薪酬更是飙升至 500 万美元。摩根合伙人职位堪称美国银行业的顶尖殊荣。许多公司选择合伙人时会考虑其带来新资本或新客户的能力，但 J. P. 摩根始终坚持皮尔庞特任人唯贤的原则——任何白人基督教男性都有资格入选。尽管不少合伙人与家族有渊源，20 世纪 20 年代摩根、拉蒙特、戴维森家族的下一代相继加入银行，华尔街 23 号从未明文禁止裙带关系。但真正塑造摩根传奇的杰出合伙人——哈里·戴维森、汤姆·拉蒙特、德怀特·莫罗、拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔——无一不是凭借真才实学入选。正是这些顶尖人才的素质，在很大程度上解释了摩根财团历经风雨却屹立不倒的非凡韧性。

The activities of these Jazz Age celebrities were avidly followed in the press. Those partners involved in international finance and diplomacy traveled constantly and spent several months abroad each year. When transatlantic liners left New York, reporters would scan the passenger lists for Morgan partners, hoping to land a shipboard interview. Partners were so prominent that B. C. Forbes even reviewed their golf games, which he found disappointing, as if some chance for perfection had been missed.
这些爵士时代名人的一举一动都备受媒体关注。那些投身国际金融与外交事务的合伙人常年奔波，每年要在海外度过数月。每当跨洋邮轮驶离纽约，记者们便会仔细扫描乘客名单搜寻摩根合伙人的身影，以期在船上争取到专访机会。这些合伙人声名显赫，以至于 B.C.福布斯甚至会点评他们的高尔夫球技——这位财经评论家总对他们的表现感到失望，仿佛错失了某种臻于完美的可能。

From the marble Morgan halls emerged \$6 billion in securities under-writings between 1919 and 1933—far more than from any other bank. A third were railroad bonds, another third foreign bonds, and the last third corporate bonds. Like the growing government accounts, the domestic roster was matchless—U.S. Steel, General Electric, General Motors, Du Pont, AT&T, IT&T, Montgomery Ward, Kennecott Copper, American Can, Con Edison, and the New York Central. By managing securities issues for these companies and assigning syndicate places to other banks, the House of Morgan defined the pyramid of Wall Street power. It also performed humdrum services—foreign exchange, banker’s acceptances, and commercial credits—that were the bread-and-butter of merchant banking. Not every partner enjoyed the fantasy life of a Tom Lamont or a Dwight Morrow.
从 1919 年到 1933 年间，摩根银行那大理石砌成的大厅里，累计承销了 60 亿美元的证券——远超其他任何一家银行。其中三分之一是铁路债券，三分之一是外国债券，剩余三分之一为企业债券。与不断增长的政府账户一样，其国内客户名单更是无可匹敌——美国钢铁、通用电气、通用汽车、杜邦、AT&T、IT&T、蒙哥马利·沃德、肯尼科特铜业、美国罐头、联合爱迪生，以及纽约中央铁路。通过为这些公司管理证券发行，并将银团席位分配给其他银行，摩根家族定义了华尔街的权力金字塔。与此同时，它们也提供外汇、银行承兑汇票和商业信贷等平凡而实在的服务——这些正是商人银行赖以生存的根基。不过，并非所有合伙人都能像汤姆·拉蒙特或德怀特·莫罗那样，过上令人艳羡的梦幻生活。

The Gentleman Banker’s Code was alive and well on Wall Street in the 1920s. The House of Morgan didn’t advertise or post a nameplate. It didn’t chase customers or open branches; clients still paid Morgan partners the ancient tribute of traveling to see them. Competition was elegant and masked behind elaborate courtesies. Clients were mortgaged to one bank and needed permission to switch to another. As Otto Kahn explained, “Kuhn, Loeb & Co. and firms of similar standing would not even touch any new business, on any terms, if the corporation concerned was already a regular client of another banker, and had not definitely broken off relations.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285759) From the outside, it resembled polite collusion; underneath, it could be vicious. Far from objecting to exclusive relations, businessmen boasted about their bankers and considered a Morgan account a hallmark of success.
在 20 世纪 20 年代的华尔街，"绅士银行家准则"依然大行其道。摩根银行从不打广告，也不挂招牌；不主动招揽客户，更不设立分行——客户们反倒要按古老的传统，亲自登门拜访摩根合伙人。竞争优雅而体面，被层层繁复的礼数所遮掩。客户与银行之间形同抵押关系，若要转投别家，必须获得原银行的许可。正如 Otto Kahn 所言："库恩-勒布公司（Kuhn, Loeb & Co.）以及同等地位的机构，哪怕对方开出任何条件，也绝不会沾染那些已是其他银行固定客户、且尚未正式断绝往来的企业。" [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285759) 从表面看，这像是彬彬有礼的共谋；暗地里，却可能残酷无情。商人们非但不排斥这种排他性关系，反而以拥有自家银行为荣，把能成为摩根银行的客户视作成功的标志。

Morgan partners still sat on boards of favored companies but were more selective than in the days when Charles Coster kept abreast of fifty-nine companies. Partners didn’t descend lightly from Olympus. Martin Egan remarked that “there is a constant plea to get Morgan partners on all manner of committees and into all sorts of organizations. The process is diffusive and cheapening.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285927) Although the Morgan bank took stakes in its companies, partners agreed in the 1920s not to get involved in outside enterprises. Gradually, if imperceptibly, the banker was becoming less a corporate partner and more a professional, a disinterested intermediary. This was the transition favored by Louis Brandeis, and it would be markedly speeded up by New Deal reformers. In Pier-pont’s day, weak companies needed to lean on strong bankers. But by the 1920s, a Standard Oil of New Jersey or a U.S. Steel had a stability comparable to that of the House of Morgan itself.
摩根合伙人仍然在那些受青睐的公司董事会中占有一席之地，但与查尔斯·科斯特同时兼任五十九家公司董事的时代相比，他们如今更加挑剔。合伙人不会轻易从奥林匹斯山降临。马丁·伊根评论道：“不断有人恳请摩根合伙人加入各类委员会和各种组织。这个过程既分散精力又自降身价。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2285927) 尽管摩根银行在其所投资的公司中持有股份，但合伙人们在 20 世纪 20 年代达成共识，不再涉足外部企业。不知不觉间，银行家逐渐从企业合伙人转变为专业人士，成为超脱利益的中介。这正是路易斯·布兰代斯所青睐的转变，而新政改革者将显著加速这一进程。在皮尔庞特的时代，弱小的公司需要依赖强大的银行家。但到了 20 世纪 20 年代，新泽西标准石油或美国钢铁公司这样的企业，其稳定性已可与摩根财团本身相媲美。

Who were the other Morgan partners? They fit a rough profile—white, male, Republican, Episcopalian, and Anglophile, with an Ivy League education and eastern seaboard antecedents. Harvard was the alma mater of Jack Morgan and his sons and was clearly the preferred school. The bank was perhaps most selective about religion—race wasn’t even an issue then, so remote were blacks from the world of banking. Jews were definitely forbidden but had opportunities elsewhere on Wall Street. Private Jewish banks continued to win business, such as retail underwriting, thought vulgar by the blue-blooded Yankee banks. Lehman Brothers had both R. H. Macy and Gimbel Brothers among its clients. Some Jewish bankers lived in an opulent style surpassed only by the Morgans. Otto Kahn of Kuhn, Loeb built a Norman castle on Long Island’s North Shore that had 170 rooms, 11 reflecting pools, a zoo complete with lions, an 18-hole golf course with a resident pro, a Georgian dining room that seated 200, and a staff of 125 servants. It would later be the set for *Citizen Kane.* But until after the Second World War, no Jew would penetrate the House of Morgan.
摩根的其他合伙人是什么样的人呢？他们大致有着相似的背景——白人、男性、共和党人、圣公会教徒、亲英派，接受过常春藤盟校教育，祖上来自东海岸。哈佛大学是杰克·摩根和他儿子们的母校，显然也是首选学府。这家银行最挑剔的大概是宗教信仰——当时种族问题甚至不在考虑范围之内，黑人离银行业的世界实在太遥远了。犹太人则被明确排斥，但他们在华尔街其他地方还有机会。犹太私人银行继续赢得业务，比如零售承销，那些蓝血扬基银行觉得这种业务很粗俗。雷曼兄弟的客户中就包括 R.H. Macy 和 Gimbel Brothers。一些犹太银行家生活极为奢华，其气派程度只有摩根家族能超越。 库恩-洛布公司的奥托·卡恩，在长岛北岸建造了一座诺曼式城堡。城堡内有 170 个房间、11 座镜面池塘、一个带狮子的动物园、一座配有驻场职业球手的 18 洞高尔夫球场、一间可容纳 200 人的乔治亚风格餐厅，以及 125 名仆人。后来，这座城堡成了电影《公民凯恩》的取景地。 但直到第二次世界大战后，才有人能打入摩根银行。

On the Wall Street of the 1920s, Catholics were borderline cases and often found it harder than Jews to enter high finance. Snubbed by Protestants, they turned to stock market speculation by default, and Jazz Age plungers were disproportionately Irish. Armed with ticker tape and telephones at the Waldorf-Astoria Hotel, Joe Kennedy made a fortune in stock pools but still found Morgan acceptance elusive. One day, he decided to break the ice with the bank. He marched into 23 Wall and asked for Jack Morgan. He was curtly told Mr. Morgan was too busy to see him. Before the Morgan gates, he bore the double stigma of being a Catholic and a stock market operator.
在 20 世纪 20 年代的华尔街，天主教徒处于边缘地位，进入高端金融领域往往比犹太人还要困难。由于受到新教徒的冷落，他们默认转向了股市投机，而爵士时代的投机者中，爱尔兰裔的比例异常高。乔·肯尼迪在华尔道夫-阿斯托里亚酒店，凭借股票行情机和电话，通过联合坐庄大发其财，但依然难以获得摩根银行的接纳。有一天，他决定主动打破僵局，走进华尔街 23 号，要求见杰克·摩根。对方简短地告诉他摩根先生太忙，没空见他。在摩根银行的大门前，他背负着双重污名：既是天主教徒，又是股市操盘手。

The best-known Morgan Catholic was certainly Edward Stettinius, yet even he switched to his wife’s more acceptable Episcopalianism and became a vestryman of Saint James Episcopal Church. Settling his spiritual accounts in 1921, Stettinius reverted to Catholicism and wrote Saint James a guilt-ridden letter of resignation: “I have come to feel strongly that a Vestryman should not only be a regular attendant at your services, but also a communicant and an ardent and consistent supporter of the Episcopal faith. Unfortunately, however, I find myself steadily drawing away from, rather than toward, the Episcopal Church.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286131)
最著名的摩根天主教徒无疑是爱德华·斯特蒂纽斯，但就连他也转而改信了他妻子更为人接受的圣公会，并成为圣詹姆斯圣公会的教区委员。1921 年，斯特蒂纽斯了结了自己的精神账目，重新皈依天主教，并给圣詹姆斯教堂写了一封充满负罪感的辞职信：“我强烈地感到，一个教区委员不仅应该定期参加你们的礼拜，还应该是一名领圣餐者，以及圣公信仰的热忱而坚定的支持者。然而不幸的是，我发现自己在稳步地远离，而非靠近圣公会。”

A compulsive record keeper, Stettinius has left us a detailed inventory of a partner’s life in the twenties. He entertained magnificently at his Park Avenue mansion. For the debutante ball of their daughter Betty, the Stettiniuses invited three hundred guests, including dancers and musicians from Tokyo’s Royal Theater. (Betty later married Juan Trippe, founder of Pan American Airways.) In the cellar of Stettinius’s mansion was enough liquor to float a battleship: 336 bottles of gin, 196 of sauterne, 79 of sherry, 60 of champagne, 114 of vermouth, 40 of Haig and Haig Scotch, 88 of claret, 32 of port, 53 of amontillado, 26 of Park and Tilford Topaz—over a thousand bottles of fine liquor.[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286293) From a Broad Street tobacconist, he would order six thousand Havana cigars at a time and then draw down his “balance.” With six cars and several houses to maintain, it cost Stettinius \$250,000 a year just to cover basic living expenses—perhaps the reason why he approached his work with such famous thoroughness.
斯特蒂纽斯是个有强迫症般的记录狂人，他给我们留下了一份详尽的清单，记录了 20 年代一位合伙人的生活点滴。他在公园大道的豪宅里大宴宾客。为了女儿贝蒂的社交舞会，斯特蒂纽斯夫妇邀请了三百位宾客，其中包括来自东京皇家剧院的舞者和乐手。（贝蒂后来嫁给了泛美航空创始人胡安·特里普。）斯特蒂纽斯豪宅的酒窖里，藏酒多得足以让一艘战列舰浮起来：336 瓶金酒、196 瓶苏玳白葡萄酒、79 瓶雪利酒、60 瓶香槟、114 瓶味美思、40 瓶海格威士忌、88 瓶红葡萄酒、32 瓶波特酒、53 瓶阿蒙蒂亚雪利酒、26 瓶帕克和蒂尔福德黄玉酒——总共超过一千瓶佳酿。 [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286293) 他还会从布罗德街的一家烟草商那里一次订购六千支哈瓦那雪茄，然后从“账上”慢慢扣除。"要维持六辆车和几处房产，斯泰蒂纽斯每年仅基本生活开销就高达 25 万美元——这也许正是他做事以极其细致周全著称的原因。

In 1922, he bought a thirty-four-acre estate overlooking Long Island Sound and was among those partners who lived close to Jack’s Glen Cove mansion, like medieval vassals biding near their lord. A meticulous man who left nothing to chance, Stettinius decided to create a Morgan cemetery at Locust Valley. The local church, Saint John’s of Lattingtown, provided spiritual solace to many tycoons and was called the millionaire’s church. On Sunday mornings, the collection plate was passed around by Jack Morgan himself—surely a heavenly treat. Jack was so fond of the church that he redecorated its interior in carved oak brought over from a small Scottish church. Stettinius’s plan was to buy up property beside the church burial ground on which to create a cemetery.
1922 年，他购置了一处俯瞰长岛海湾的 34 英亩庄园，成为那些住在杰克·格伦科夫豪宅附近的合伙人之一，如同中世纪封臣簇拥在领主身边。斯泰蒂纽斯是个事无巨细、不留任何变数的人，他决定在洛克斯谷地建造一座摩根家族墓地。当地教堂——拉丁镇的圣约翰教堂——为众多大亨提供精神慰藉，被称为"百万富翁教堂"。每逢周日早晨，杰克·摩根本人会亲自传递募捐盘——这无疑是天赐的荣幸。杰克对这座教堂喜爱有加，甚至从一座苏格兰小教堂运来雕花橡木重新装饰了教堂内部。斯泰蒂纽斯的计划是买下教堂墓地旁的土地，用来建造一座墓园。"

The one obstacle was a New York State law forbidding cemetery expansion. So in April 1923, Stettinius lobbied state lawmakers for special legislation. Then he plotted a takeover of the Locust Valley Cemetery. On June 1, 1925, the cemetery’s annual board meeting was packed with Morgan luminaries, including Jack’s son-in-law Paul G. Pennoyer, Harry Davison’s son, Trubee, and Stettinius. It was perhaps the greatest show of financial strength in cemetery history. After winning control, they hired architects and landscape gardeners to spruce up the shrubbery and install fancy wrought-iron gates. What resulted was a double cemetery: “The older, open section of small plots continued very much as it had been, while the new sections of woods and spacious glades became a Valhalla for Morgan partners and their friends.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286428) Having made these arrangements, Stettinius took a well-earned rest in plot number 7. Many blamed his wartime labors for his death. In any case, he perpetuated the Morgan tradition, started by Charles Coster, of heroic exertion and premature death.
一个障碍是纽约州法律禁止墓地扩建。于是 1923 年 4 月，斯特蒂纽斯游说州议员通过特别立法。随后他策划收购了洛斯特谷公墓。1925 年 6 月 1 日，墓地年度董事会上挤满了摩根家族的名流，包括杰克的姻亲保罗·G·佩诺耶、哈里·戴维森的儿子特鲁比，以及斯特蒂纽斯。这或许是墓地史上最具金融实力的亮相。夺取控制权后，他们聘请建筑师和园林设计师美化灌木丛，安装精致的锻铁大门。最终形成了一座双拼墓地：“老旧的开放式小墓区基本维持原样，而新辟的林地开阔地带则成为摩根合伙人及其友人的瓦尔哈拉。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286428) 安排妥当后，斯特蒂纽斯在 7 号墓穴安息，可谓当之无愧。许多人将他的去世归咎于战时劳顿。 无论如何，他延续了由 Charles Coster 开创的 Morgan 传统——英勇奋斗与英年早逝。

The partner who most typified the Jazz Age was that small miracle of sophistication, Tom Lamont. Socialite and sportsman, he loved to camp in the High Sierras or fish for Canadian salmon. To the bank’s image he added an urbane 1920s gloss, a shade literary. If a Morgan partner suggested a stylish, well-rounded Anglophile, a Republican who could travel in Democratic circles, a liberal internationalist orthodox in domestic affairs, then Lamont was surely his prototype. Yet this figure who symbolized the 1920s on Wall Street harbored a secret ambivalence toward it. “That decade, with its exotic exuberance of prosperity and its speculative excesses in almost every phase of economic life . . . was for America a decadent one,” he later wrote.[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286563)
最能代表爵士时代的合伙人，是那位集精致与聪慧于一身的小奇迹——汤姆·拉蒙特。身为社交名流与运动健将，他钟情于在高山间露营，或垂钓加拿大鲑鱼。他为银行形象增添了一层都市化的 1920 年代光泽，略带几分文学气息。如果说一位摩根合伙人象征着时髦、全面、亲英的共和党人，一个能在民主党圈中游走的共和党人，一位在国内事务上正统的自由国际主义者，那么拉蒙特无疑是这种原型的化身。然而，这位华尔街 1920 年代的标志性人物，内心却对那个时代怀有隐秘的矛盾。“那十年，以其非凡的繁荣活力与几乎在经济生活各个层面上的投机过度……对美国而言，是一个颓废的时代，”他后来写道。

Lamont became the richest Morgan partner, his wealth matched by a progression of stately homes. First he and his wife, Florence, lived in Englewood, New Jersey—so thickly populated with Morgan partners it was called the bank’s branch office. They joined the Dwight Morrows in the local Shakespeare club, where they all took parts and read plays aloud. From 1915 to 1921, the Lamonts rented Franklin Roosevelt’s house on East Sixty-fifth Street while its owner served as assistant secretary of the navy. Then, in 1921, Lamont bought the townhouse at 107 East Seventieth Street that would become a stopping place for visiting politicians, writers, and socialites, it featured everything from a British butler to a solarium. The Lamonts were terribly ambitious, determined to know everybody of importance in New York and to snare every celebrity who passed through town. To a remarkable extent, they succeeded.
拉蒙特成了最富有的摩根合伙人，他的财富伴随着一座又一座豪宅的购置而不断增长。起初，他和妻子弗洛伦斯住在新泽西州的恩格尔伍德——那里摩根合伙人扎堆，简直被戏称为银行的“分行”。他们加入了德怀特·莫罗夫妇所在的当地莎士比亚俱乐部，一起分配角色、大声朗读剧本。1915 年至 1921 年间，拉蒙特一家租下了富兰克林·罗斯福在东六十五街的宅邸，而房主当时正担任海军助理部长。1921 年，拉蒙特买下了东七十街 107 号的联排别墅，这里后来成了来访政客、作家和社交名流的驻足之地，从英式管家到日光浴室，一应俱全。拉蒙特夫妇野心勃勃，决心结识纽约所有重要人物，并拢络每一位途经此地的名人。在相当程度上，他们做到了。

For recreation, they bought Sky Farm, an island retreat off the Maine coast with a panoramic view of Penobscot Bay. In 1928, they purchased Torrey Cliff, a hundred-acre estate on the Palisades that formerly was owned by a well-known botanist, John Torrey, and later would be donated to Columbia University for its geological observatory. The property encompassed cliffs and woodlands, brooks, flowers, and spectacular vistas of the Hudson River. Finally, Lamont and John Davis regularly stayed at Yeamans Hall in South Carolina, a millionaires’ development with over a thousand acres of golf courses, forest paths, and giant moss-covered oaks.
为了消遣，他们买下了缅因州海岸外的小岛度假地“空中农场”，可一览佩诺布斯科特湾的全景。1928 年，他们又购得了帕利塞兹地区占地百英亩的“托里悬崖”，这里曾是著名植物学家约翰·托里的产业，后来被捐赠给哥伦比亚大学用于地质观测站。这片庄园拥有悬崖、林地、溪流、花卉，以及哈德逊河的壮丽景色。最后，拉蒙特和约翰·戴维斯还定期前往南卡罗来纳州的“耶曼斯庄园”——这是一处为百万富翁打造的度假区，拥有超过千英亩的高尔夫球场、林间小径和长满苔藓的参天橡树。

Florence Lamont was a short, bright, pleasant-looking woman with a thoughtful stare. She took herself seriously, both as a hostess determined to snare celebrities and as an intellectual. A graduate of Smith with a master’s degree in philosophy from Columbia, she supported numerous causes, including birth control and women’s trade unions. Earnest, a trifle tedious, always craving intellectual companionship and stimulating company, she sometimes had a bluestocking intensity. Anne Morrow Lindbergh, after hearing Florence make grandiloquent speeches about pacifism at a social gathering, noted in her diary: “Mrs. Lamont distrusts all informal, feminine discussion, thinking it gossip. Is it because she can’t do it very well?”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286739)
弗洛伦斯·拉蒙特是个身材矮小、神采奕奕、面相讨喜的女人，目光中总带着若有所思的神情。她对自己颇为认真——既是下定决心要网罗名流的女主人，也是一位知识分子。作为史密斯学院的毕业生，又在哥伦比亚大学取得哲学硕士学位，她支持众多事业，包括节育和妇女工会。她热忱恳切，略有些絮叨，总是渴望结交知性伙伴和有趣的同伴，有时会透出一种女学究式的执着。安妮·莫罗·林德伯格在一次社交聚会上听到弗洛伦斯高谈阔论和平主义后，在日记中写道："拉蒙特夫人不信任所有非正式的、女性化的讨论，认为那都是闲言碎语。是不是因为她自己并不擅长这些呢？"

High-spirited and sociable, the Lamonts were on everybody’s party list in the Jazz Age, and they knew dozens of celebrities. When their son Corliss went to Oxford in 1924, he lived with the Julian Huxleys, lunched with Lord and Lady Astor at Cliveden, and took a weekend with H. G. Wells—such was the range of his parents’ friends. The Lamonts’ East Seventieth Street townhouse echoed with laughter and ideas, as their children Corliss, Tommy (later a Morgan partner), and Margaret turned it into an exuberant debating society. Again, Anne Morrow Lindbergh has left a vivid impression of this animated household: “To the Lamonts’ for dinner. . . . We had decided before we got there that we would *not* argue. Instead we let the Lamonts argue among themselves. Tommy and Corliss, Mrs. Lamont and Mr. Lamont, Margaret and Tommy, Margaret and Mrs. Lamont, back and forth across the table. Tommy, loudly and platformly; Corliss, lightly, nervously, and humorously. Margaret, of course, dead serious. Mr. Lamont suavely and Mrs. Lamont petulantly. We all sat back quite happily . . . and listened. It was great fun.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286910)
拉蒙特一家热情洋溢、善于交际，在爵士时代，他们是所有派对的座上宾，认识几十位名人。1924 年，他们的儿子科利斯去牛津读书时，与朱利安·赫胥黎一家同住，在克利夫登与阿斯特勋爵和夫人共进午餐，还和 H.G.威尔斯一起度周末——他父母的朋友圈子就是如此之广。拉蒙特家位于东七十街的联排别墅里充满了欢声笑语和思想碰撞，他们的孩子科利斯、汤米（后来成为摩根合伙人）和玛格丽特把这里变成了一个热闹的辩论俱乐部。安妮·莫罗·林德伯格再次对这家充满活力的人家留下了生动的印象：“去拉蒙特家吃晚饭……我们去之前就决定不参与争论。结果我们任由拉蒙特一家人自己争论。汤米和科利斯、拉蒙特太太和拉蒙特先生、玛格丽特和汤米、玛格丽特和拉蒙特太太，在餐桌上来回交锋。汤米声音洪亮、像在演讲；科利斯轻快、紧张又幽默。玛格丽特当然是一本正经。拉蒙特先生……” 拉蒙先生温文尔雅，拉蒙太太却任性胡闹。我们都乐滋滋地坐了回去……洗耳恭听。真是有趣极了。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2286910)

If Florence sometimes took herself too seriously, Tom, with his genial energy, roused her from it. He could never have enough friends, enough dinners, or enough lively chatter. He had a marvelous sense of humor, which surprised many people who imagined that Morgan bankers must be dour and self-important. He once said of an enemy that if he had ordered a trainload of sons of bitches and received only that man, he would consider the order amply filled. Once, Tom Lamont and Betty Morrow wanted the two couples to throw a party together—they were the good dancers—but Florence and Dwight balked. So guests received the following invitation:

JACK Morgan moved through the twenties like a monarch. One journalist described him embarking from his limousine at 23 Wall: “I saw two other men inconspicuously draw themselves up in an attitude of attention, like soldiers in mufti, acting on their instincts or through force of habit. . . . The great doors with their huge panes of spotless glass and their polished brass swung open and shut.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287211) He enjoyed his spot at the top of the Morgan empire. About to present Pope Pius XI with restored Coptic texts in 1922, he made this observation: “My special job is the most interesting I know of anywhere. More fun than being King, Pope, or Prime Minister anywhere—for no one can turn me out of it and I don’t have to make any compromises with principles.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287355)
JACK Morgan 在二十年代里像君王一样周旋。一位记者曾描述他从华尔街 23 号走下车来的场景：“我看到另外两人不引人注目地挺直身子，摆出注意的姿态，像是穿着便装的士兵，出于本能或习惯使然……那扇装有巨大无瑕玻璃和抛光黄铜的大门悄然敞开又合上。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287211) 他享受着自己坐拥摩根帝国顶端的地位。当 1922 年即将向教皇庇护十一世呈交修复的科普特文本时，他这样感慨道：“我的特殊工作是我所知世上最有趣的事。比当国王、教皇或首相都要有趣——因为没人能把我赶下台，我也不必在原则上做任何妥协。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287355)

Jack lived regally at his 250-acre island estate, Matinicock Point, off the North Shore. Visitors passed through enormous wrought-iron gates and down an endless drive shaded by linden trees; in season bloomed several thousand tulips and daffodils under the direction of Jessie Morgan. The estate required several dozen full-time gardeners. There were also cows, horses, greenhouses, boxwood and rose gardens, cottages for the staff, and a dock down at the Sound.
杰克在他那座位于长岛北岸、占地 250 英亩的马提尼科克角庄园里，过着如王侯般的生活。访客们穿过巨大的铸铁大门，沿着一条被椴树荫蔽的无尽车道前行；到了花季，在杰西·摩根的打理下，数千株郁金香和水仙花竞相绽放。这座庄园需要几十名全职园丁来维护。此外，还有牛群、马匹、温室、黄杨木与玫瑰花园、员工小屋，以及长岛海峡边的码头。

Amid open lawns and high trees, the red-brick mansion was grander than any of Pierpont’s residences. It was designed by Grant La Farge with an imposing columned entrance. Inside, three famous ladies—Rubens’s Anne of Austria, Gainsborough’s Lady Gideon, and Sir Thomas Lawrence’s countess of Derby—stared down from the walls of a house specially fireproofed for them. The majestic stairway of the forty-rive-room house was lined with beautiful floral arrangements.
在开阔的草坪和高大的树木之间，红砖豪宅比皮尔庞特的任何一处住所都要气派。它由格兰特·拉法奇设计，入口处立着气势恢宏的廊柱。屋内，三位名媛——鲁本斯笔下的奥地利安妮、庚斯博罗笔下的吉迪恩夫人、以及托马斯·劳伦斯爵士笔下的德比伯爵夫人——从特制防火墙上方的画框中俯视着一切。这座拥有四十五个房间的豪宅里，气派的楼梯两侧摆满了美丽的花卉装饰。

Jack liked quiet, domestic pleasures. Delicate and sedentary, a born dabbler, he enjoyed detective novels and crossword puzzles. His literary hero was Rudyard Kipling. He disapproved of contact sports, and when his two sons, Junius and Harry, went to Groton, he protested the introduction of football, calling it immoral, dangerous, and brutal.[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287519) He loved taking drives in his chauffeured cars and had four of them—two Rolls-Royces, a Lincoln, and a Buick roadster.
杰克喜欢安静的家庭生活。他性格细腻、喜静不喜动，天生是个什么都爱沾一点的人，喜欢读侦探小说和填字游戏。他心目中的文学偶像就是拉迪亚德·吉卜林。他不赞成对抗性运动，当两个儿子朱尼厄斯和哈里去格罗顿上学时，他还反对学校引进橄榄球，称这项运动不道德、危险且野蛮。他喜欢坐司机开的车出去兜风，家里有四辆车——两辆劳斯莱斯、一辆林肯和一辆别克敞篷跑车。

He was fanatical about his privacy and hostile to the press. The Morgans always kept their daughters and granddaughters off the society pages. Like his father, Jack often threatened intrusive photographers and would screen his face with his Panama hat as he left church on Sunday mornings. Because he commuted to Wall Street by water, photographers awaited him on boats in the Sound. To foil them, he constructed a flowery archway that curved down to the dock and obscured his movements. The only problem was the gangplank. For the final seconds it took to board the boat, his butler, Henry Physick, would remove his coat and hold it up to shield his master from the press. Sailing home from work, Jack would take afternoon tea on board.
他对隐私极度狂热，对媒体充满敌意。摩根家族向来不让女儿和孙女们出现在社交版面上。杰克和他父亲一样，常常恐吓那些爱偷拍的摄影师，周日早上离开教堂时，他会用巴拿马草帽遮住脸。因为他乘船去华尔街上班，摄影师们就守在长岛湾的船上等他。为了避开他们，他修建了一座花拱门，一直延伸到码头，遮挡住他的行动。唯一的问题是跳板。在登船的最后几秒，他的管家亨利·菲西克会脱下外套，举起来为主人遮挡媒体的镜头。下班乘船回家时，杰克会在船上享用下午茶。

Only once did Jack submit willingly to press photographs. One day, as a motorboat was taking him out to the *Corsair* the photographers were in their usual hot pursuit, when Jack’s Panama hat blew into the Sound. A photographer fished it from the water and gave it to Jack’s boatswain, saying, “Your boss hasn’t treated me with much courtesy, but I am glad to do him a favor.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287653) Like Pierpont, Jack was sentimental and could be disarmed by a gallant gesture. When he heard the story, he ordered the photographer up on deck and posed for twenty minutes of pictures.
杰克唯一一次心甘情愿让摄影师拍照。那天，他乘摩托艇前往"海盗号"，摄影师们照例紧追不舍，结果杰克的巴拿马草帽被风吹进了海湾。一名摄影师从水里捞起帽子，交给杰克的水手长，说道："你们老板对我可不算客气，不过能帮到他我很乐意。" 跟皮尔庞特一样，杰克是个重情义的人，一个得体的举动就能化解他的戒心。听说了这件事后，他让那名摄影师上到甲板，配合拍了二十分钟的照片。

Both Jack and Jessie loved England, which they visited each spring and summer. When the London *Times* tagged him an English squire, Jack was thrilled.[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287805) He had a London townhouse at 12 Grosvenor Square and bequeathed Princes Gate, the old family townhouse, to the U.S. government as a residence for the American ambassador. Just as George Peabody had hosted annual Fourth of July dinners, Jack hoped future ambassadors would “live like gentlemen and have their Fourth of July receptions in adequate surroundings.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287955) Later on, Princes Gate would be the wartime residence of Ambassador Joe Kennedy, who finally slipped into the House of Morgan through a back door. It was the house opposite Hyde Park fondly remembered by the Kennedy sons.
杰克和杰茜深爱英格兰，每年春夏都会到访。当《伦敦时报》称他为英国乡绅时，杰克欣喜若狂。 [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287805) 他在伦敦格罗夫纳广场 12 号拥有一处联排别墅，并将家族老宅王子门赠予美国政府，作为美国大使官邸。正如乔治·皮博迪曾主持年度独立日宴会，杰克希望未来大使们能“像绅士般生活，在体面的环境中举办独立日招待会”。 [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2287955) 后来，王子门成为乔·肯尼迪大使的战时居所——这位大使最终从后门悄悄进入了摩根家族。那座面朝海德公园的房子，是肯尼迪兄弟心中珍藏的温暖回忆。

Jack’s major British residence was Wall Hall, his three-hundred-acre estate north of London with artificial lakes and gardens. He ruled the village like a whimsical Prospero. He didn’t simply live in the village; he owned it. As *Fortune* magazine explained, “At Wall Hall he is a Tory squire with the whole of Aldenham Village as his property except the ancient church, and with all the villagers in his employ, each supplied with a rent-free house and registered milk and free medical treatment and an old-age pension and membership in the Aldenham Parish Social Club.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288154)
杰克在英国的主要住所是沃尔庄园，这座占地三百英亩的府邸位于伦敦北部，拥有人工湖和私家园林。他像个随心所欲的普洛斯彼罗般统治着这座村庄——他并非单纯在此居住，而是整个村庄的主人。正如《财富》杂志所述："在沃尔庄园，他是位保守党乡绅，除了古老教堂外整个奥尔德纳姆村都是他的产业，所有村民都是他的雇工，每人享有免租住宅、登记配给牛奶、免费医疗、养老金以及奥尔德纳姆教区社交俱乐部的会员资格。" [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288154)

A paternalistic landlord, he fretted about his villagers. So that they wouldn’t loaf, he provided them with cricket grounds, tennis courts, and bowling greens. He was afraid that the village tavern, the Chequers, might be bought by a brewer. So he dispatched Teddy Grenfell on a secret mission to buy it at any price. This high-level corporate raid occasioned some drollery between them. “It is a new kind of business for me altogether,” Jack said, “as I have owned many kinds of property, but never a public house before, and I am quite excited at the investment.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288300) Rather old-maidish, Jack considered taking away the tavern’s liquor license. But the problems that accompanied Prohibition in America persuaded him to retain the pub license. He even added a hall for movies and dancing, saying, “It’s really going to make a difference to a good number of honest, hard working and powerfully dull men and women.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288499)
作为一位家长式的地主，他总为村民操心。为了防止他们游手好闲，他建了板球场、网球场和草地滚球场。他担心村里的酒吧“棋盘酒吧”会被酿酒商收购，于是派泰迪·格伦菲尔去执行秘密任务，不惜代价买下它。这场高端的企业收购让他们之间开了些玩笑。杰克说：“这对我是个全新的买卖——我拥有过各种产业，可还从没当过酒吧老板，这笔投资真让我兴奋。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288300) 有点老古板的杰克曾考虑吊销酒吧的售酒执照，但美国禁酒令带来的麻烦让他打消了念头。他甚至加建了一个能放电影和跳舞的大厅，说道：“这真的能让那些老实巴交、勤恳工作却无聊透顶的男男女女们生活有点不一样。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288499)

To an extraordinary degree, he was a creature of custom and comfort. So that he could drive without removing his plug hat, he had an English firm design a car with a special high top. He corresponded with a haberdasher about socks that didn’t slide smoothly enough over his heel. Perhaps fearing his own emotions—or else in homage to his Yankee-trading ancestors—he always wanted things around him to proceed tidily. He was obsessed with punctuality. At Wall Hall, he had so many clocks that someone came in weekly just to rewind them. For gifts, he often gave his partners rare gold watches.
他简直是个习惯成瘾、贪图安逸的人。为了开车时不用摘下礼帽，他特意让英国公司设计了一款高顶轿车。他曾就袜子后跟滑动不够顺畅的问题，与一位服饰商互通书信。或许是怕自己情绪失控，又或是向祖上那些精明的扬基商人致敬，他总希望周围的一切都井然有序。他痴迷于守时。在沃尔庄园，他拥有无数座钟，每周都得有人专程来上弦。至于送礼，他常常赠予合作伙伴稀有的金表。

The year had its unchanging rhythms for Jack. The high spot was August 12—the Glorious Twelfth that launched the Scottish grouse-hunting season. “Nearly everybody I know has started for shooting in Scotland,” he once wrote a partner in early August.[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288663) Who else in America could make such a statement? After 1913, Eric Hambro and Jack jointly owned Gannochy Lodge near Edzell, in Scotland. They and their eminent guests bagged up to ten thousand birds a year, and each hunter was attended by a butler. In honor of Jack, the Scottish retainers even put together a “Morgan tartan.”
一年中，杰克的生活有着不变的节奏。高潮总是 8 月 12 日——那个开启苏格兰松鸡狩猎季的“光荣第十二日”。“我认识的人几乎都动身去苏格兰打猎了，”他在八月初曾这样写给一位合伙人。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288663) 放眼全美，还有谁能说出这样的话？1913 年后，埃里克·汉布罗与杰克共同拥有了苏格兰埃泽尔附近的 Gannochy Lodge。他们和那些显赫的宾客每年猎获多达一万只鸟，每位猎人还有一名贴身男仆侍候。为了向杰克致敬，苏格兰的仆从们甚至拼凑出了一款“Morgan tartan”。

For his Manhattan residence, Jack retained the townhouse at Madison Avenue and Thirty-seventh Street that his father had bought for him. Somber and brooding outside, it was light and airy within. It had white marble fireplaces, French revival furniture, and crystal chandeliers. When staying there, Jack would visit the library next door each day. He added four thousand books and manuscripts to his father’s nineteen thousand and continued his raid on illuminated books and British literature. A tip from Tory leader Stanley Baldwin brought him the manuscript of Sir James Barrie’s *Shall We Join the Ladies?* “I hate manuscripts leaving the country,” Baldwin confessed to Jack. “But if they have to go, I would far rather they found a home with you than anybody else!”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288812)
杰克在曼哈顿的居所，保留了父亲为他购置的麦迪逊大道与三十七街交汇处的那栋联排别墅。外表沉郁凝重，内里却通透轻盈。屋内陈设着白色大理石壁炉、法式复古家具和水晶吊灯。每回在此小住，杰克都会去隔壁的图书馆。他在父亲一万九千册藏书的基础上又增添了四千册书籍与手稿，继续搜罗彩饰手抄本与英国文学作品。保守党领袖斯坦利·鲍德温出于信任，将詹姆斯·巴里爵士《邀女士共舞否？》的手稿赠予了他。“我实在不愿让这些手稿流落海外，”鲍德温向杰克坦言，“可如果非走不可，我宁愿它们在你这里安家，而不是落入他人之手！” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288812)

After his father’s death, Jack hadn’t been able to pursue collecting and was preoccupied with settling the estate. Now, in another parallel to Pierpont’s life, Jack widened his collecting as he moved into his midfifties. Once again, Belle Greene went on buying trips to Europe, and Jack regarded her with affection and slightly fearful awe. When four rare manuscripts owned by the earl of Leicester came on the market, Greene was afraid to commit so much money on her own. She asked Jack to negotiate. He went to Europe and after a sleepless night bought them for an estimated \$500,000. Jack told the seller: “My librarian told me she wouldn’t dare spend so much of my money. But just the same, I wouldn’t dare face her if I went home without the manuscripts.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2288965)
父亲去世后，杰克一直未能继续收藏，而是忙于处理遗产。如今，就像皮尔庞特的人生轨迹一样，步入五十五岁后，杰克的收藏范围也随之扩大。贝尔·格林再次前往欧洲采购，杰克对她既充满喜爱，又略带敬畏。当莱斯特伯爵的四部珍贵手稿出现在市场上时，格林不敢擅自动用这么大笔资金。她请杰克去交涉。杰克前往欧洲，经过一夜未眠的考虑后，以约 50 万美元的价格买下了它们。杰克对卖家说：“我的图书管理员告诉我，她不敢花我这么多钱。但同样，如果我空手回去，我也不敢面对她。”

Jack hadn’t yet erected the sort of monument to Pierpont that Pierpont had to Junius. In 1924, he incorporated the Pierpont Morgan Library in his father’s memory, with Belle Greene as the first director; he provided a \$1.5-million endowment. Perhaps recalling the brouhaha over the art collection’s breakup, he summoned reporters for a wistful interview. Seated in the West Room, where Pierpont had worked and Junius stared down from above the mantel, Jack said, “This is the room where my father literally lived. I think it is probably the most peaceful room in New York. You never hear a thing here except occasionally a bad automobile horn.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289091)
杰克尚未为皮尔庞特立起像皮尔庞特为朱尼厄斯所立的那样的纪念碑。1924 年，他为纪念父亲创办了皮尔庞特·摩根图书馆，并任命贝尔·格林为首任馆长；他提供了 150 万美元的捐赠基金。或许是想起了当年艺术品收藏拆分时引发的轩然大波，他召集记者进行了一次怅然的采访。坐在西厅——皮尔庞特曾在此工作，壁炉上方朱尼厄斯的画像俯视着一切——杰克说道：“这间屋子是我父亲真正生活过的地方。我认为它很可能是纽约最宁静的房间。除了偶尔有恼人的汽车喇叭声，这里什么也听不到。”

As he took reporters around the library, he snatched up interesting items and talked about them. Taking up a Dickens manuscript, he said, “Scrooge and all the rest of them are there. Isn’t that nice?” In his remarks may be heard the plaintive note of a man seeking public love—love that he felt he deserved but was always denied. At the end of his tour, he asked, “Now what do you think of it? Have I done a good thing in making this gift?”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289255)
他带着记者们参观图书馆时，会随手抓起有趣的物件兴致勃勃地介绍。拿起一本狄更斯的手稿，他说：“看，斯克鲁奇和其他角色都在这里。多棒啊！”从他的话里，可以听出一种渴望公众之爱的哀怨——他觉得自己理应得到这份爱，却始终求而不得。参观结束时，他问道：“现在你觉得怎么样？我做出这个馈赠，做得好吗？”

In 1928, Pierpont’s brownstone mansion next to the library was torn down and replaced by an annex, designed by Benjamin Wistar Morris, to provide exhibition facilities and more space for scholars. (One now enters the library through the annex.) Under the imperious sway of Belle Greene, the library remained a jewel-box institution with a tiny staff. Belle could be curt with dilettantes, voyeurs, and just plain fools, but her stature in the museum world was mythical on account of her devotion to genuine scholars.
1928 年，皮尔庞特位于图书馆旁的那栋褐砂石豪宅被拆除，取而代之的是一座由本杰明·威斯塔·莫里斯设计的配楼，用于提供展览设施和为学者们创造更多空间。（如今人们正是通过这座配楼进入图书馆。）在贝尔·格林威严的掌控下，图书馆始终像一座珍宝盒，员工寥寥无几。贝尔对浅薄之徒、偷窥者和纯粹的傻瓜可能态度生硬，但她在博物馆界的地位堪称传奇，这都源于她对真正学者的无私奉献。

While Jack was paying tribute to his father, he tried to groom his two sons to take over the bank. His eldest, Junius Spencer, Jr., graduated from Harvard in 1914. He served as a junior officer on a destroyer off the English coast in the war, an experience that left him with shattered nerves. Tall and extraordinarily handsome, with the face of a sensitive actor, he became a Morgan partner in 1919. Warmly approachable and possessed of a dry sense of humor, Junius was probably the nicest Morgan—but the most dismal businessman. Jack tried to fool himself that Junius was cut out for banking and sent him to Morgan Grenfell for a two-year apprenticeship in 1922. Jack wrote to Grenfell, “I cannot tell you how glad I am to have him go over and learn London methods, and the London outlook of business, under your careful eye.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289390) Junius went through the motions of being a banker and served as a director of U.S. Steel and General Motors. But his real dream was to be a marine architect. His would be a sad, wasted business life, showing the limited options open to sons in a banking dynasty. Like his father and grandfather and brother, Junius became a commodore of the New York Yacht Club—the only certifiable Morgan activity that really fit him.
就在杰克向父亲致敬的同时，他也着力培养两个儿子接掌银行。长子朱尼厄斯·斯宾塞二世于 1914 年从哈佛毕业。战争期间，他在英国海岸附近的一艘驱逐舰上担任低级军官，这段经历让他神经饱受摧残。他身材修长、英俊非凡，长着一张敏感演员般的面孔，1919 年成为摩根合伙人。朱尼厄斯为人热情随和，带有冷幽默，可能是摩根家族中最讨人喜欢的一位——却是最糟糕的生意人。杰克自欺欺人地认为朱尼厄斯天生适合银行业，1922 年送他去 Morgan Grenfell 学徒两年。杰克写信给格伦费尔：“他在你悉心指导下学习伦敦的方法和商业视野，我别提多高兴了。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289390) 朱尼厄斯表面做着银行家的差事，担任美国钢铁和通用汽车董事。但他真正的梦想是成为造船工程师。 他的一生将是可悲而虚度的商业生涯，暴露出银行王朝子弟在职业选择上的狭窄空间。 如他的父亲、祖父和兄长一样，朱尼厄斯也成了纽约游艇俱乐部的准将——这是唯一真正适合他的、有据可查的摩根式活动。

Jack’s younger son, Harry Sturgis, looked more promising as a businessman. Born in London in 1900, he was short and stocky, with a more aggressive and temperamental air than Junius. If Junius looked friendly and rather languid, Harry was all thrusting energy, his chin assertive, his lips tightly compressed, his gaze fiery. In 1923, one week after graduating from Harvard, he married Catherine Francis Adams, daughter of Charles Francis Adams, secretary of the navy under Herbert Hoover. That year, Harry started as a \$15-a-week Morgan messenger and ran securities around Wall Street. As a Christmas present in December 1928, Harry received his \$1-million-a-year Morgan partnership.
杰克的幼子哈里·斯特吉斯看起来更有商人的潜质。他 1900 年生于伦敦，身材矮小结实，比朱尼厄斯更具攻击性和暴躁脾气。如果说朱尼厄斯看上去友善而慵懒，哈里则浑身是冲劲——下巴咄咄逼人，嘴唇紧抿，目光如炬。1923 年，从哈佛毕业仅一周后，他便迎娶了赫伯特·胡佛任内海军部长查尔斯·弗朗西斯·亚当斯的千金凯瑟琳·弗朗西斯·亚当斯。同年，哈里以周薪 15 美元的摩根信使身份入职，在华尔街附近穿梭递送证券。1928 年 12 月，一份圣诞厚礼降临：他正式成为摩根合伙人，年收入达百万美元。

During this period, Jack suffered two emotional blows from which he never recovered. In 1924, he lost his mother, who was still living in the original Madison Avenue mansion; it was torn down only after her death. She had survived into her eighties, by then a stone-deaf old woman. Jack, as a boy, had gravitated toward his mother’s warmth and gentleness. His own close marriage to Jessie probably recapitulated the earlier relationship.
这段时间，杰克接连遭受了两次情感重创，从此再也没能真正走出来。1924 年，他失去了母亲——她一直住在麦迪逊大道上的那栋老宅里，直到她去世后房子才被拆掉。母亲活到了八十多岁，那时已经全聋了。小时候，杰克总是被母亲的温柔与和善所吸引。他自己与杰西的亲密婚姻，或许正是对早年那段关系的重演。

Like Fanny, Jessie Morgan became somewhat weak and sickly, but there were major differences. Jessie was a natural executive and extremely efficient in running four giant households, supervising butlers, footmen, and housekeepers. She had a shy, matronly look that hid steely discipline. Feminine on the outside, she was tough at the core—as when she lashed out at the would-be assassin in 1915. Neither she nor Jack believed that women should be emancipated, and she tended her gardens, collected lace, and gathered drawings of flowers. She had no interest in politics or lunching with other women. Beneath the frills, she was determined and even slightly fearsome.
和范妮一样，杰茜·摩根也变得有些体弱多病，但两者有很大不同。杰茜天生是个管理者，治理四座大宅、调度管家男仆和女管家都极为高效。她外表腼腆、端庄贤淑，却暗藏着铁腕般的纪律。外表柔美，内心刚硬——正如 1915 年她痛斥那名刺客时那样。她和杰克都不认为女性应当解放，她打理花园、收藏蕾丝、收集花卉画稿。她对政治毫无兴趣，也不爱和别的女人共进午餐。在那层温柔装饰之下，她意志坚定，甚至有些令人敬畏。

Jack Morgan’s happiness revolved around her and his children. In April 1925, he told Grenfell, “The only excitement in the family—and it is real excitement—has been the arrival in this woeful world of Frances’ twins, running the number of my grandchildren up from nine to eleven in twenty minutes. This satisfies even my most ambitious aspirations.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289591) What didn’t jibe with this picture of family harmony was that Jack and Jessie were so engrossed with one another that their children felt excluded. Jessie served Jack, adored him, and advised him in every aspect of his life. She was the invisible safety net that stopped him from falling, and he relied on her judgment implicitly.
杰克·摩根的生活幸福是围绕着她和孩子们的。1925 年 4 月，他告诉格伦费尔：“家里唯一的兴奋点——而且是真正的兴奋——是弗朗西斯的双胞胎降临到这个悲惨的世界，二十分钟内我的孙子孙女从九个变成了十一个。这甚至满足了我最雄心勃勃的愿望。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289591) 与这幅家庭和谐画面格格不入的是，杰克和杰西太过专注于彼此，以至于他们的孩子感到被冷落。杰西服务于杰克，崇拜他，并在生活的方方面面为他出谋划策。她是隐形的安全网，阻止他坠落，而他完全信赖她的判断。

Then, in the summer of 1925, Jessie contracted a sleeping sickness—an inflammation of the brain—then prevalent in the United States. It was thought to derive from the influenza pandemic of 1917-18. Jessie fell into a coma and had to be fed through a tube. Antibiotics weren’t yet available, and eminent physicians could only counsel patience. They told Jack the disease was running its course and that Jessie would eventually wake up. A trusting person, Jack waited and prayed. Afraid of submitting to melancholy, he put on a brave front and reported to 23 Wall every day, taking comfort from Jessie’s smallest stirrings and from the fact that, as she slept, she was well nourished and gaining weight. He wrote: “Jessie is getting on well, the doctors assure me, and they all assure me that the recovery, though very slow, will ultimately be complete. Though she is still wholly unconscious, there are little almost imperceptible signs that she will eventually emerge from that state.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289794) And: “Of course no one can tell how long she must sleep, but while she sleeps she is not conscious of any pain or discomfort, and the cure is proceeding all the time.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289997)
接着，在 1925 年夏天，杰西感染了当时美国流行的昏睡症——一种脑部炎症。人们认为这是 1917-1918 年流感大流行的后遗症。杰西陷入昏迷，只能靠鼻饲管维持营养。那时还没有抗生素，名医们也只能劝家人耐心等待。他们告诉杰克，疾病自有其病程，杰西最终会醒来。天性信任他人的杰克，一边祈祷一边等待。他害怕陷入悲伤，于是强打精神，每天照常去华尔街 23 号上班。从杰西最细微的反应中，从她沉睡时营养良好、体重增加的事实中，他找到了慰藉。他写道：“杰西恢复得不错，医生们向我保证，他们都保证虽然康复很慢，但最终会完全痊愈。尽管她仍然完全不省人事，但有些几乎难以察觉的迹象表明，她终究会从这种状态中醒来。”” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289794) 接着说：“当然，没人能说得清她究竟要睡多久，但她在沉睡时感受不到任何痛苦或不适，而治疗一直在持续进行。”” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2289997)

How to reconcile this tender Jack Morgan with the stern anti-Semite of earlier pages? He was a true Morgan. His humanity was deep but narrow, his world divided between those who counted and those who did not. With his family, he was capable of total love. By mid-summer, Jessie had slowly improved, and Jack was buoyed by reports that her condition was better than at any time since the onset of the illness. Doctors assured Jack he could go to work without fear. On August 14, 1925, he went to the office only to receive a late-morning call to return home at once. By the time he arrived, Jessie was dead. Her heart had stopped from what doctors thought was an embolism. They were stunned by the sudden reversal.
该如何将这温柔似水的杰克·摩根，与先前那个铁石心肠的反犹主义者联系起来？他骨子里就是个地地道道的摩根。他的悲悯之情深厚却狭隘，在他的世界里，人分三六九等，有的值得他倾注心力，有的则不值一提。面对家人，他能付出毫无保留的爱。到了仲夏，杰茜的情况慢慢好转，有消息称她的身体状况比发病以来任何时候都要好，这让杰克备受鼓舞。医生也向他保证，可以安心去忙工作，不必担忧。1925 年 8 月 14 日，他照常去了办公室，不料临近中午时接到电话，要他立刻回家。等他赶到家时，杰茜已撒手人寰。医生推测，她的心脏是因栓塞而骤停。面对这突如其来的变故，所有人都震惊不已。

Still recovering from his mother’s death, Jack was distraught, inconsolable. He mourned deeply and reverently, much as Pierpont had mourned the sainted Mimi. In a moving outburst of grief and affection, he told Lamont, “Well, I have all these years to look back upon, everything to remember and nothing to forget.”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290172) In a letter to a partner, Lamont described Jack during Jessie’s illness: “\[He\] had felt perfectly confident the last few weeks that his wife was going to come out all right. He was determined that she should. He thought of nothing else day and night. He was looking forward to the time when she would emerge from her sleep. He was most anxious to be with her at that time, thinking that his presence might be an aid to her in regaining her normality . . . he had been wonderful, courageous, and perfect throughout it all.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290357)
杰克还未从母亲去世的打击中恢复过来，他悲痛欲绝，无法安慰。他哀悼得深沉而虔诚，就像皮尔庞特哀悼圣洁的米米那样。在一阵令人动容的悲恸与爱意中，他对拉蒙特说：“唉，我有这么多年可以回望，一切皆可铭记，没有什么是需要遗忘的。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290172) 在给一位合伙人的信中，拉蒙特描述了杰西生病期间杰克的状态：“过去几周，他完全相信妻子会好起来。他坚信她能挺过去。他日夜所思，别无他念。他盼望着她从沉睡中醒来的那一刻。他极其渴望在那时陪伴在她身边，认为自己的出现能帮助她恢复正常……在整个过程中，他表现得无比出色、勇敢而完美。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290357)

In her will, Jessie left the bulk of her property to her two sons and two daughters. She included an oddly touching tribute to her husband—one can almost see the ironic twinkle in her eye as she wrote, “I feel sure that if, through any unforeseen circumstances, my dear husband should ever be in need, my children will share with him the property derived from me.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290527)
在她的遗嘱中，杰茜将大部分财产留给了两个儿子和两个女儿。她附上了一份对丈夫的古怪而感人的致意——几乎能想象她写下这段话时眼中闪烁的讽刺光芒：“我确信，倘若因任何不可预见的情况，我亲爱的丈夫有朝一日陷入困境，我的孩子们将与他分享从我这里获得的财产。”

After his wife’s death, Jack became more withdrawn. At Matinicock Point, he left her bedroom as she had left it and tended her tulips and English rose garden. (Becoming a dedicated gardener, he entered dahlias in a Nassau County show and won the J. P. Morgan Prize!) Well drilled by Jessie, the servants ran the estates without her. Although now alone, Jack closed no houses and sold no boats or cars. In some ways, he refused to acknowledge the change in his life. Many of his friends testified to the eerie sense that Jessie still was present—not a superstitious feeling so much as one of Jack refusing to let her rituals die. In 1927, Jack bought up waterfront property in Glen Cove to dedicate a \$3-million Morgan Memorial Park to his wife. From its quaint clubhouse with its fluted eaves, Jack embarked on his Mediterranean cruises each year.
妻子去世后，杰克变得更加孤僻。在马蒂尼科克角，他保留着妻子的卧室原样，细心照料她留下的郁金香和英式玫瑰园。（他成了一名专注的园艺爱好者，还参加了拿骚县的大丽花展，捧回了 J. P. 摩根奖！）仆人们受过杰茜的严格调教，即使没有她，庄园照样运转得井井有条。尽管如今形单影只，杰克没有关闭任何一处宅邸，也没有变卖船只或汽车。某种程度上，他拒绝承认生活已然改变。许多朋友都有一种诡异的感觉：杰茜仿佛从未离开——倒不是出于迷信，更像是杰克固执地延续着她的生活方式。1927 年，杰克买下格伦科夫沿岸的地产，斥资 300 万美元建造摩根纪念公园，献给亡妻。每年，他都会从那座有着波纹状屋檐的别致俱乐部出发，开启地中海巡游之旅。

Solitary, clad in tweeds, and smoking his meerschaum pipe, Jack wandered about his formal gardens, a melancholy widower. His partners noticed his loneliness. Easily wounded, he had had a tendency toward melodrama and self-pity, both of which now became pronounced. Writing of his fourteen grandchildren in 1928, he told a friend, “It makes a great difference to me in my life, which is necessarily very lonely.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290679) Sometimes he asked his chauffeur of twenty-five years, Charles Robertson, to drive him down to the Morgan Memorial Park. He would sit beside the chauffeur and stare silently at the water. For all his money, he now thought himself the loneliest of men.
杰克身穿粗花呢，抽着海泡石烟斗，独自在他那规整的花园里徘徊，成了一位郁郁寡欢的鳏夫。他的合伙人们察觉到了他的孤寂。他本就心思敏感，容易受伤，如今更是变得爱演苦情戏、自怨自艾。1928 年他在信中对朋友提及自己的十四个孙辈时说：“这对我的人生来说意义重大，毕竟我的生活实在太过孤单。” [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2290679) 有时，他会让跟随自己二十五年的司机查尔斯·罗伯逊开车送他去摩根纪念公园。他会坐在司机身旁，默默凝视着水面。尽管坐拥万贯家财，他却觉得自己是天底下最孤独的人。
