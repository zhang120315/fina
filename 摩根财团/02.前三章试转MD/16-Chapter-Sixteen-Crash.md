# Chapter Sixteen: Crash

WE picture the bull market of the twenties as spanning the entire decade, when in fact it was compressed into the second half. It was largely a Wall Street phenomenon, not matched by other stock markets around the world. Germany’s market had peaked in 1927, Britain’s in 1928, and France’s in early 1929. Why the enormous burst of Wall Street optimism? It was partly a reaction to the unsettled postwar years, with their inflation and labor strife, their bitter Red-baiting and anarchist turmoil. Financial history teaches us that the desire for oblivion is the necessary precondition for mayhem.
我们通常认为 20 年代的牛市贯穿了整个十年，但实际上它只集中在下半段。这很大程度上是华尔街独有的现象，全球其他股票市场并未同步。德国股市在 1927 年见顶，英国在 1928 年，而法国则在 1929 年初达到顶峰。为何华尔街会爆发出如此巨大的乐观情绪？部分原因是对战后动荡年代的反拨——那时的通货膨胀、劳工冲突、恶毒的反共浪潮和无政府主义骚乱。金融史告诉我们，渴望遗忘是混乱爆发的必要前提。

The euphoria was also generated by a liquidity boom of historic proportions. Cash was everywhere. In 1920, Ben Strong sharply raised interest rates to cool off an inflationary commodity boom. This created not only a recession but disinflationary conditions that lasted for several years. Money fled hard assets. As commodity bubbles burst—ranging from Texas oil to Florida land—the money poured into financial markets. Stocks and bonds floated up on a tremendous wave.
这股狂热也源于一场历史性的流动性繁荣。现金无处不在。1920 年，本·斯特朗大幅加息以给通胀性商品热潮降温，这不仅引发了经济衰退，还造成了持续数年的通缩环境。资金纷纷逃离硬资产。随着从得克萨斯石油到佛罗里达土地等商品泡沫的破裂，资金涌入金融市场。股票和债券在巨大的浪潮中水涨船高。

With Europe devastated by war, America’s economy outpaced competitors and created a large trade surplus. The economic boom was lopsided. Commentators spoke of “sick sectors” in farming, oil, and textiles. With half of America still living in rural areas, the Wall Street rally seemed unreal and irrelevant to farmers. Nor did all banks prosper. Weakened by farming and oil loans, small-town banks failed at the rate of two a day, a fact not noticed in urban areas, where finance and real estate thrived together. For instance, in late 1928, John J. Raskob, Democratic national chairman, started plans to build the Empire State Building as a monument to “the American way of life that allowed a poor boy to make his fortune on Wall Street.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304029)
欧洲因战争而满目疮痍，美国经济却一路领先竞争对手，积累了巨额贸易顺差。然而这场经济繁荣并不均衡。评论家们纷纷提及农业、石油和纺织业的"病态部门"。当时美国仍有半数人口生活在农村地区，华尔街的牛市对他们而言既虚幻又毫无关联。并非所有银行都蒸蒸日上。受农业和石油贷款拖累，小镇银行以每天两家的速度倒闭——这一现象在金融与房地产共舞的大都市无人察觉。例如 1928 年末，民主党全国委员会主席约翰·J·拉斯科布开始筹划建造帝国大厦，将其作为"让穷小子能在华尔街白手起家的美国生活方式"的纪念碑。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304029)

Raskob and other prophets of the age espoused an ideology of endless prosperity and talked of a new economic era. Their shibboleths were readily believed by the large number of young, inexperienced people recruited by Wall Street. As the *Wall Street Journal* said after October 1929’s Black Thursday, “There are people trading in Wall Street and many over the country who have never seen a real bear market.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304175) If many on Wall Street were determined to forget past panics, most were too young to have ever known about them.
Raskob 和那个时代的其他预言家鼓吹无限繁荣的理念，大谈一个全新的经济时代。他们的口号被大量由华尔街招募的年轻、缺乏经验的人轻易相信。正如《华尔街日报》在 1929 年 10 月“黑色星期四”之后所言：“在华尔街交易的人以及全国各地的许多人，从未见过真正的熊市。” [2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304175) 如果说华尔街上的许多人决心忘记过去的恐慌，那么大多数人都太年轻，根本不知道它们的存在。

For many pundits, the sheer abundance of cash precluded any crash. A big worry of the late 1920s was that America might run short of stocks. The day before the 1929 crash, the *Wall Street Journal* reported, “There is a vast amount of money awaiting investment. Thousands of traders and investors have been waiting for an opportunity to buy stocks on just such a break as has occurred over the last several weeks.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304339) The excess cash was viewed as a sign of wealth, not as an omen of dwindling opportunities for productive investment.
对许多专家而言，现金的极度充裕阻止了任何崩盘。20 世纪 20 年代末的一大担忧是美国可能会面临股票短缺。1929 年崩盘前一天，《华尔街日报》报道称：“有巨额资金等待投资。成千上万的交易员和投资者一直在等待像过去几周出现的那种回调机会来买入股票。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304339) 这些过剩的现金被视为财富的标志，而非生产性投资机会减少的预兆。

Riding this cash boom, the American financial services industry grew explosively. Before the war, there were 250 securities dealers; by 1929, an astounding 6,500. A critical shift in the popular attitude toward stocks occurred. Bonds had always dwarfed stocks in importance on the New York Stock Exchange. Before the war, banks and insurance companies might trade stocks, but not small investors. We recall Pierpont Morgan’s steady disdain for stocks. When asked why the market went down, he would say dismissively, “Stocks will fluctuate,” or “There were more sellers than buyers,” as if the subject weren’t worthy of analysis.[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304473)
乘着这股现金热潮，美国金融服务业迎来了爆炸式增长。战前证券交易商仅有 250 家，到 1929 年竟飙升至 6500 家。公众对股票的态度发生了重大转变。在纽约证券交易所，债券的重要性一直远超股票。战前，银行和保险公司可能会交易股票，但散户投资者不会参与。我们还记得皮尔庞特·摩根对股票一贯的轻蔑态度。当被问及市场为何下跌时，他会不屑地说“股票总有波动”，或“卖家比买家多”，仿佛这个话题根本不值得分析。 [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304473)

In the 1920s, small investors leapt giddily into the stock market in large numbers. They frequently bought on a 10-percent margin, putting only \$1,000 down to buy \$10,000 worth of stock. Of a total American population of 120 million, only 1.5 to 3 million played the stock market, but their slick, easy winnings captured the national spotlight. The 1929 market disaster would be heavily concentrated among the 600,000 margin accounts.
20 世纪 20 年代，大量散户投资者蜂拥涌入股市，狂热不已。他们通常只付 10%的保证金，花 1000 美元就能买进 10000 美元的股票。当时美国总人口 1.2 亿，参与炒股者仅 150 万至 300 万人，但这些人轻松获利的风头却抢尽了全国目光。1929 年的市场灾难将高度集中在 60 万个保证金账户上。

With active securities markets, it was cheaper for big corporations to raise money by issuing securities than it was for them to raise money by paying for short-term bank credit. Many companies also financed expansion from retained earnings, continuing to wean themselves away from the banker dominance of the Baronial Age. In fact, some businesses had so much surplus cash that they engaged in stock speculation and margin lending—much as Japanese companies in the 1980s would use spare cash for *zai-tech* investment—so that the Federal Reserve’s pressure on banks to stop margin lending was offset by unregulated industrial lenders.
在证券市场活跃的情况下，大型企业通过发行证券融资的成本远低于支付短期银行信贷的利息。许多公司还利用留存收益为扩张提供资金，逐步摆脱了贵族时代银行家主导的局面。事实上，一些企业拥有如此充裕的剩余资金，以至于它们投身股票投机和保证金贷款——正如 20 世纪 80 年代的日本企业将闲置资金用于财技投资一样——以至于美联储对银行停止保证金贷款的压力，被不受监管的工业贷款机构所抵消。

In this pre-Glass-Steagall era, corporate preference for securities issues posed no threat to Wall Street. The big New York banks profited through their new securities affiliates, which could also bypass limits on interstate banking. Guaranty Trust opened offices in Saint Louis, Chicago, Philadelphia, Boston, and even Montreal. The securities unit of the Chase National Bank not only operated coast-to-coast but set up offices in Paris and Rome. The world of integrated global markets was thus already foreshadowed by the 1929 crash. In 1927, Guaranty Trust invented American depositary receipts (ADRs), enabling Americans to buy overseas stocks without currency problems. This would be an extremely lucrative business for J. P. Morgan and Company when it later took over Guaranty Trust.
在这个《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》尚未出台的时代，企业对证券发行的偏好对华尔街构不成威胁。纽约的大银行们通过新成立的证券附属机构大赚其钱，这些机构还能绕过跨州银行业务的限制。担保信托公司在圣路易斯、芝加哥、费城、波士顿甚至蒙特利尔都开设了办事处。大通国民银行的证券部门不仅在全美各地运营，还在巴黎和罗马设立了分支机构。1929 年的股灾已经预示着全球一体化市场的到来。1927 年，担保信托公司发明了美国存托凭证（ADRs），让美国人无需受货币问题困扰就能购买海外股票。当摩根大通公司后来接管担保信托公司时，这项业务成了极其赚钱的生意。

There were now two securities worlds on Wall Street. One was retail-oriented and pedestrian and was typified by the National City Company, the affiliate of the bank. National City chairman Charles Mitchell lent a carnival tone to securities marketing. He would organize contests and pep talks for his nearly two thousand brokers, spurring them on to higher sales. Bankers took on the image of garrulous hucksters. Among these men, there was a fad for foreign bonds, especially from Latin America, with small investors assured of their safety. The pitfalls were not exposed until later on, when it became known that Wall Street banks had taken their bad Latin American debt and packaged it in bonds that were sold through their securities affiliates. This would be a major motivating factor behind the Glass-Steagall Act’s separation of the banking and securities businesses.
如今华尔街存在着两个证券世界。一个是面向散户、庸常普通的，以银行下属机构国民城市公司为代表。国民城市公司董事长查尔斯·米切尔为证券营销注入了嘉年华般的喧闹氛围。他会为近两千名经纪人组织竞赛和动员演讲，激励他们创造更高业绩。银行家们俨然成了喋喋不休的推销员。这些人中流行起投资外国债券的风潮，尤其是拉丁美洲债券，小额投资者被保证其安全性。直到后来真相大白，人们才意识到华尔街银行将自己手中的不良拉丁美洲债务打包成债券，通过旗下证券分支机构销售。这正是推动《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》将银行业与证券业分离的重要动因。

By the 1929 crash, large deposit banks had vanquished many old partnerships in the securities business and originated a startling 45 percent of all new issues. The National City Bank Company sponsored more securities than J. P. Morgan and Kuhn, Loeb combined. Nevertheless, elite Wall Street survived, typified by the august House of Morgan. The bulk of prime securities business remained with the prestigious, old-line, wholesale houses. J. P. Morgan had no distribution network but originated issues distributed by as many as twelve hundred retail houses; still distant from markets, the bank allocated shares to “selling groups.” It co-managed issues with its Money Trust allies—National City, First National, and Guaranty Trust—while Morgan Grenfell worked with the Houses of Baring, Rothschild, Hambro, and Lazard.
到 1929 年股市崩盘时，大型存款银行已击败了许多证券业的旧式合伙公司，并主导了高达 45%的新发行证券。国民城市银行公司发行的证券比 J.P.摩根、库恩-洛布两家公司加起来还多。尽管如此，以威严的摩根银行为代表的华尔街精英阶层依然屹立不倒。优质证券业务大部分仍掌握在历史悠久、信誉卓著的批发机构手中。J.P.摩根虽然没有自己的分销网络，但其发行的证券由多达 1200 家零售机构分销；这家银行仍与市场保持距离，将股票分配给"销售集团"。它与"货币信托"盟友——国民城市银行、第一国民银行和担保信托公司——共同管理发行，而摩根建富则与巴林、罗斯柴尔德、汉布罗和拉扎德等银行家族合作。

As in the days of the Pujo hearings, big bond issues were still conducted according to fixed rituals. AT&T provides an excellent example. At the Morgan Library in 1920, Jack Morgan, Harry Davison, and Robert Winsor of Kidder, Peabody worked out a secret deal to divide American Telephone and Telegraph issues. They kept identical participations throughout the decade: Kidder, Peabody, 30 percent; J. P. Morgan, 20 percent; First National Bank, 10 percent; National City Bank, 10 percent; and so on. The Gentleman Banker’s Code prohibited the raiding of customers. It was thought not only bad form, but dangerous. J. P. Morgan and Kuhn, Loeb feared that if they competed for each other’s clients, they would destroy each other in bloody, internecine battles.
如同普约听证会时期一样，巨额债券的发行依然遵循着固定的仪式。美国电话电报公司（AT&T）便是一个绝佳的例子。1920 年，在摩根图书馆，杰克·摩根、哈里·戴维森与基德尔-皮博迪公司的罗伯特·温瑟秘密达成协议，瓜分了美国电话电报公司的债券发行份额。整个二十年代，他们始终保持着相同的份额比例：基德尔-皮博迪占 30%，J.P.摩根占 20%，第一国民银行占 10%，国民城市银行占 10%，以此类推。绅士银行家准则严禁抢夺客户。这被认为不仅有失体面，更暗藏风险。J.P.摩根与库恩-勒布公司都担心，若彼此争夺客户，必将陷入一场两败俱伤的血腥内斗。

On its marble pedestal, the wholesale House of Morgan didn’t need to twist the arms of small investors. As *The New Yorker* said in 1929, “It is doubtful whether any private banker ever enjoyed the individual prestige of Morgan senior, but the firm now is vastly more powerful than it was in his day.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304637) By decade’s end, it would have less to repent than many other banks. Some of this was the result of tradition, as the bank profited from its Victorian disdain for the stock market—as Pier-pont once told Bernard Baruch, “I never gamble.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304788) Jack Morgan had held a Stock Exchange seat since 1894 but never conducted a transaction. Only once, during a Liberty Bond rally, did he appear on the Exchange floor. He kept the seat only to reduce commissions from the thirty-odd brokers the bank used. In addition, common stock issues accounted for a mere 3 percent of Morgan-sponsored securities. Since the chief damage of the 1920s would be done by stock manipulation, the House of Morgan was spared involvement in some of the worst excesses.
在大理石基座上，以批发业务为主的摩根银行根本不需要强迫小投资者。正如《纽约客》在 1929 年所言：“很难说还有哪位私人银行家能拥有老摩根那样的个人声望，但如今这家公司的权势已远胜他的时代。” [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304637) 到十年末，它需要忏悔的事情比许多其他银行都要少。部分原因是传统使然——这家银行从维多利亚时代对股市的鄙夷中获利颇丰，正如皮尔庞特曾对伯纳德·巴鲁克说的那样：“我从不赌博。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304788) 杰克·摩根自 1894 年起就持有证券交易所席位，却从未进行过一笔交易。仅在一次自由债券上涨期间，他曾出现在交易所大厅里。他保留这个席位只是为了降低该银行所用三十多个经纪人的佣金。此外，普通股发行仅占摩根承销证券的 3%。 由于 20 世纪 20 年代的主要破坏来自股票操纵，摩根财团得以幸免于某些最恶劣的过度行为。

J. P. Morgan and Company engaged almost solely in a wholesale bond and banking business. With glaring exceptions, it refused to water down standards. It recommended conservative investments, such as railroad bonds, but shied away from the tipster’s art of plugging stocks. On the notable occasion when this Morgan policy was violated, the bank was deeply embarrassed. In July 1926, Morgan partner Thomas Cochran, setting sail for Europe, entertained a reporter aboard the liner *Olympic.* (In the Roaring Twenties, even luxury liners had brokerage offices.) When Cochran was at sea, the Dow Jones ticker quoted him as saying that General Motors would eventually sell at 100 points higher than its current price. Aware of the Morgan-Du Pont interest in the company, traders drove up GM stock by 25 points in two days. Aghast at this proof of its power, the bank made sure such incidents didn’t recur.
J. P. Morgan and Company 几乎只做批发债券和银行业务。除了少数令人瞩目的例外，它拒绝降低标准。它推荐保守型投资，比如铁路债券，但对荐股专家鼓吹股票的那一套敬而远之。曾有一次，摩根打破了这一政策，结果让自己陷入尴尬境地。1926 年 7 月，摩根合伙人 Thomas Cochran 启程前往欧洲，在奥林匹克号邮轮上招待了一位记者（在咆哮的二十年代，就连豪华邮轮上也设有券商营业部）。Cochran 在海上时，道琼斯行情收录机引述他的话说，通用汽车最终将比当前价格高出 100 点。交易员们意识到摩根与杜邦在该公司拥有利益，于是在两天内将通用汽车股价推高了 25 点。银行对自己竟有如此影响力感到震惊，此后确保这类事件不再发生。

Although the Morgan bank, as an institution, was distant from the stock market, its partners weren’t averse to speculation. They were in an excellent position to take advantage of insider trading, which was a common vice of the 1920s, and not illegal. Not only did Jazz Age Wall Street echo with rumors, but being in a position to plant false reports was considered a mark of financial maturity. Lax Stock Exchange rules and meager corporate reports made inside information more valuable, and investors milked their Wall Street friends for news. Inside tips didn’t guarantee success—many investors perished on Black Thursday clutching them—yet they were profitable enough to be considered a major perk of Wall Street employment.
尽管摩根银行作为一个机构与股市保持距离，但其合伙人并不排斥投机活动。他们处于利用内幕交易的绝佳位置——这在二十世纪二十年代是普遍存在的陋习，且并不违法。爵士时代的华尔街不仅充斥着各种传言，甚至能够散布虚假消息都被视为金融成熟的标志。证券交易所松散的监管规则和公司财报的匮乏，使得内幕信息价值连城，投资者们纷纷向华尔街的朋友打探消息。内幕消息并不能保证成功——许多投资者在黑色星期四那天紧握这些消息却血本无归——但它们带来的收益足以被视为华尔街从业者的一大福利。

In the 1930s, Morgan partners joined those favoring an end to insider trading. Lamont would say flatly, “This is a simple and unanswerable proposition in business ethics.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304956) Some had had the courage to take this stand earlier. Judge Elbert Gary, chairman of U.S. Steel, used to hold board meetings each day after the stock market closed; following these meetings, he would brief the press, denying directors an exclusive opportunity to benefit from his news. By and large, however, Morgan partners, like others on Wall Street, did benefit from insider trading, not so much on pending deals as on routine corporate information.
20 世纪 30 年代，摩根合伙人转而支持禁止内幕交易。拉蒙特直截了当地表示：“这是商业伦理中一个简单且无可辩驳的命题。” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2304956) 有些人更早就有勇气表明这一立场。美国钢铁公司董事长埃尔伯特·加里法官过去常常在股市收盘后召开董事会会议；会议结束后，他会向媒体通报情况，从而杜绝董事们利用他的消息牟取独家利益。不过总体而言，摩根合伙人与华尔街上的其他人一样，确实从内幕交易中获益，但与其说是在待处理的交易上，不如说是在日常的公司信息方面。

Edward Stettinius was a loquacious director of both General Motors and General Electric. In 1922, Harry Davison asked whether he should buy GM preferred stock for his wife. Stettinius replied: “I hesitate . . . about buying any common stock until after the statement has been published showing the results of last year’s operations. This statement, which is now in the course of preparation, will probably show a total debit against Surplus Account of about \$58 million as indicated in the memo attached hereto. I think it possible that this statement may have a depressing effect on the stock and would not favor purchases of the stock until after the statement shall have been published.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305137) Stettinius handled GM purchases for both Jack Morgan’s and Tom Lamont’s personal accounts.[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305326) We might call this the shooting-fish-in-a-barrel school of finance.
爱德华·斯特蒂纽斯是一位健谈的人，曾担任通用汽车和通用电气的董事。1922 年，哈里·戴维森问他是否该为妻子买入通用汽车的优先股。斯特蒂纽斯回答：“我有些犹豫……在年报公布之前，不建议买入任何普通股。这份年报目前正在编制中，很可能显示盈余账户总借方约 5800 万美元，如所附备忘录所示。我认为这份报告可能会对股价产生负面影响，因此建议在年报公布后再考虑购买。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305137) 斯特蒂纽斯还曾为杰克·摩根和汤姆·拉蒙特的个人账户处理通用汽车的股票交易。 [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305326) 我们可以称此为“瓮中捉鳖式”的金融操作手法。

In passing tips, Stettinius displayed a vague sense of the impropriety of doing so. In 1923, Morgan partner Herman Harjes in Paris asked about buying General Electric shares. Note how Stettinius hesitates before spilling the news:
在随口透露消息时，斯泰蒂纽斯隐约感觉到这样做有些不妥。1923 年，摩根合伙人赫尔曼·哈耶斯在巴黎询问是否该买入通用电气的股票。注意斯泰蒂纽斯在透露消息前的犹豫：

> I would much prefer to buy than to sell the stock of the General Electric Co. at present prices, say 196. I do not feel that I can properly tell you what information has come to me as a member of the Executive Committee and Board of Directors of the Co., but I do think that I can say to you (to be treated confidentially) that it is my guess that some action will be taken within the next 6 months to still further enhance the value of the company’s shares. I shall be surprised if the shares do not sell on a basis of 225 or 230 per share, within the next six or nine months. The Co. is doing a wonderful business—and this again in confidence—profits for 11 months of 1923 show an increase of 50\$\$\$ over the profits for the corresponding period of 1922.[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305517)
> 以当前价格（比如 196 美元）来说，我更倾向于买入而非卖出通用电气公司的股票。我觉得自己不方便直接告诉你，作为公司执行委员会和董事会成员，我得到了哪些内部信息，但我确实认为可以（请保密）对你说一句：我猜测未来六个月内公司会采取某些行动，进一步推高股票价值。如果未来六到九个月内股价达不到每股 225 或 230 美元，我会很惊讶。公司业绩极佳——同样请保密——1923 年前 11 个月的利润比 1922 年同期增长了 50%。 [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305517)

If Stettinius displayed scruples, it was less about revealing corporate information than in having it leak out to those beyond the inner circle of J. P. Morgan partners.
如果斯泰蒂纽斯有所顾忌，与其说是担心泄露公司信息，不如说是怕这些信息传到 J. P. Morgan 合伙人核心圈以外的人耳中。

The House of Morgan was involved in another period phenomenon that belied its well-advertised repugnance toward common stock. Between 1927 and 1931, the bank participated in more than fifty stock pools, which weren’t outlawed until the New Deal. They were regarded as racy and glamorous, attracting cocktail party sophisticates, and their progress was reported in the press. These syndicates blatantly manipulated stock prices. Some would hire publicity agents or even bribe reporters to “talk up” a stock. Pools made Joe Kennedy’s reputation after he was enlisted in 1924 to defend John D. Hertz’s Yellow Cab Company against a bear raid; afterward, Hertz suspected Kennedy of carrying out such a raid against the stock himself. By October 1929, over one hundred stocks were being openly rigged by market operators. So while Morgan partners claimed they preferred sound long-term investments, they were far from immune to the speculative atmosphere.
摩根财团还卷入了一个时代现象，这与它公开宣扬的对普通股票的厌恶背道而驰。1927 年至 1931 年间，该银行参与了 50 多个股票操纵集团，直到新政时期这些行为才被宣布为非法。这些集团被视为刺激而迷人的操作，吸引了鸡尾酒会上的老手，媒体也会报道它们的进展。这些财团公然操纵股价。有些甚至会雇佣公关人员甚至贿赂记者来“吹捧”某只股票。这类操纵集团为乔·肯尼迪赢得了声誉——1924 年他被请来保卫约翰·D·赫兹的黄色出租车公司，抵御空头袭击；此后，赫兹怀疑肯尼迪本人曾对该股票进行过类似袭击。到 1929 年 10 月，已有超过 100 只股票被市场操纵者公开操纵。因此，尽管摩根合伙人声称他们更偏好稳健的长期投资，但他们远未免受投机氛围的影响。

The 1920s were also a time of manic deal making. As Otto Kahn recalled, there was “a perfect mania of everybody trying to buy everybody else’s property. . . . New organizations sprang up. Money was so easy to get. The public was so eager to buy equities and pieces of paper, that money was . . . pressed upon domestic corporations as upon foreign governments.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305708) Although J. P. Morgan had no formal merger department, it informally spun many webs. It specialized in deals of strategic import, requiring sensitive contacts abroad or covert government support. Many of its deals were directed against British interests; first and foremost, 23 Wall operated as an arm of Washington.
20 世纪 20 年代也是并购交易疯狂的年代。正如奥托·卡恩所回忆的那样，"每个人都疯狂地想买下别人的财产……新组织如雨后春笋般涌现。钱太好赚了。公众如此热衷于购买股票和纸质凭证，以至于资金……被源源不断地塞给国内企业，就像塞给外国政府一样。" [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305708) 尽管 J. P. 摩根没有正式的并购部门，却暗中编织了无数关系网。它专注于具有战略意义的交易，需要海外敏感人脉或政府暗中支持。许多交易都针对英国利益；最重要的是，华尔街 23 号实际上是华盛顿的一只手臂。

Consider telecommunications. After the war, the United States feared the British military monopoly of undersea cable communications, which had yielded invaluable wartime intelligence. The U.S. Navy favored the use of a new private corporation, supported by Washington, to battle Britain in the emerging field of radio technology. Privately, President Wilson notified General Electric that he wanted to counter Britain’s cable monopoly with an American radio monopoly. Morgan money helped GE to buy out British interests in American Marconi, which became the core of Radio Corporation of America. Washington stayed on RCA’s board as a nonvoting observer.
以电信业为例。战后，美国担心英国垄断海底电缆通信——英国曾借此获取了极具价值的战时情报。美国海军倾向于扶持一家由华盛顿支持的新兴私营企业，在无线电技术这一新兴领域与英国抗衡。私下里，威尔逊总统通知通用电气（General Electric），他希望用美国的无线电垄断来对抗英国的电缆垄断。摩根财团的资金帮助通用电气收购了英国在美国马可尼公司（American Marconi）的股份，这成为美国无线电公司（Radio Corporation of America）的核心。华盛顿方面则以无投票权的观察员身份留在 RCA 董事会中。

During the 1920s, the House of Morgan also helped Sosthenes Behn to launch his worldwide empire of International Telephone and Telegraph. Again the bank’s role wasn’t raiding but arbitrating. A historic truce was hatched at 23 Wall, whereby AT&T ceded overseas markets to Behn, who promised, in turn, that ITT wouldn’t build telephone plants in the United States. The deal, amazingly, lasted sixty years. This cartel arrangement showed that the Morgan bank still preferred collusion among big industrialists to the competitive economy *laissez-faire* ideologues.
20 世纪 20 年代，摩根财团助力索斯特内斯·贝恩缔造了他的国际电话电报全球帝国。这一次，银行扮演的依然不是掠夺者，而是仲裁者的角色。在华尔街 23 号，一项历史性的停战协定悄然诞生——美国电话电报公司（AT&T）将海外市场拱手让给贝恩，而后者则承诺国际电话电报公司（ITT）不在美国本土兴建电话设备厂。令人惊叹的是，这项协议竟维持了整整六十年。这一卡特尔式的安排昭示着：摩根银行依然更青睐大实业家之间的联手合作，而非自由放任主义者所鼓吹的竞争经济。

With his taste for political intrigue, Behn and the House of Morgan were a natural match. Through Morgan partner Herman Harjes in Paris, Behn took over the Spanish telephone system, which became the crown jewel in his international empire. In the mid-1920s, J. P. Morgan and Company helped Behn to take over telephone systems in Brazil, Argentina, Chile, and Uruguay, ousting the British from their former preeminence. The bank championed Behn’s cause in numberless ways. When it learned during Austrian loan negotiations that the government planned to buy telephone equipment from Siemens, it mentioned that ITT was eager to tender bids. Lamont sometimes functioned as Behn’s secret plenipotentiary. In 1930, he had an audience with Mussolini solely to advance Behn’s desire to build a factory in Italy. Deal making in this era was always a discreet, behind-the-scenes operation and had none of the flamboyance associated with the stock market raiders of a later day.
贝恩对政治权谋的热衷，与摩根家族简直是天作之合。通过摩根在巴黎的合伙人赫尔曼·哈耶斯，贝恩拿下了西班牙电话系统，这成了他国际帝国中最璀璨的明珠。20 世纪 20 年代中期，J·P·摩根公司帮助贝恩收购了巴西、阿根廷、智利和乌拉圭的电话系统，将英国人从其昔日的霸主地位上赶了下来。这家银行在无数场合为贝恩撑腰。在奥地利贷款谈判期间，得知政府计划从西门子购买电话设备时，摩根便暗示 ITT 急切希望投标。拉蒙特有时充当贝恩的秘密全权代表。1930 年，他专门为促成贝恩在意大利建厂的愿望，与墨索里尼进行了一次会面。那个时代的交易总是谨慎隐秘的幕后操作，丝毫不像后来股市掠夺者那般张扬。

IN early 1929, a sure sign of impending disaster occurred when the House of Morgan cast aside its traditional aversion and joined the flurry of stock promotion. Wall Street was being swept by new forms of leveraging. Borrowing a British concept, many brokerage houses, including Goldman, Sachs, introduced leveraged mutual funds, called “investment trusts.” A second favorite device was the holding company. Holding companies would take over many small operating companies and use their dividends to pay off their own bondholders, who had financed the takeovers in the first place. This permitted an infinite chain of acquisitions.
1929 年初，一个不祥的预兆出现了：摩根财团一改往日对股票投机的谨慎态度，也加入了这场狂热的促销浪潮。华尔街正被各种新型杠杆手段席卷。许多经纪公司，包括高盛在内，借鉴英国的做法，推出了被称为"投资信托"的杠杆式共同基金。另一个备受青睐的工具是控股公司。控股公司会收购众多小型运营公司，并用这些公司支付的股息来偿还自己债券持有人的债务——而这些债券持有人原本就是为收购提供资金的人。如此一来，便可以形成无穷无尽的收购链条。

Adopting the vogue for utility holding companies, the House of Morgan in 1929 sponsored the United Corporation, which took over Mohawk-Hudson, the Public Service Corporation of New Jersey, Columbia Gas and Electric, and other companies that controlled more than a third of the electric power production in twelve eastern states. It was a throwback to the days when Pierpont promoted trusts, kept a large block of stock for himself, and appointed the directors. The United Corporation’s books were kept at 23 Wall, and its board was filled with Morgan friends and partners. The bank also sponsored Standard Brands, an amalgam of food-product companies that included Fleischmann, Royal Baking Powder, Chase and Sanborn, and E. W. Gillette.
顺应公用事业控股公司的潮流，摩根家族于 1929 年发起成立了联合公司，接管了莫霍克-哈德逊、新泽西公共服务公司、哥伦比亚天然气与电力公司，以及其他控制着东部十二州超过三分之一电力产量的企业。这仿佛回到了皮尔庞特推动托拉斯、为自己保留大量股份并亲自任命董事的时代。联合公司的账簿存放在华尔街 23 号，董事会里全是摩根的朋友和合伙人。该银行还牵头组建了标准品牌公司，这是一家由食品企业合并而成的集团，包括弗莱施曼、皇家发酵粉、蔡斯与桑伯恩，以及 E.W.吉列公司。

The master boondoggle of 1929 was the Morgan-sponsored Alleghany Corporation, a holding company for the railroad and real estate empire of Cleveland’s Van Sweringen brothers. Oris P. and Mantis J. Van Sweringen were a queer, taciturn pair who lacked much formal education. Short, dumpy, and round-faced, they seemed as inseparable as Siamese twins. Living at Daisy Hill, their seven-hundred-acre Swiss chalet farm outside Cleveland, the bachelor brothers ate together, shared a bedroom, seldom socialized, avoided alcohol and tobacco, and dispensed with chauffeurs, valets, and other trappings of wealth. On the eve of the crash, they were worth over \$100 million.
1929 年那场惊天骗局的始作俑者，便是摩根家族一手扶持的 Alleghany Corporation——这家控股公司掌控着克利夫兰范斯韦林根兄弟的铁路与地产帝国。奥里斯·P.和曼蒂斯·J.范斯韦林根这对古怪寡言的兄弟，并未受过多少正规教育。他们身材矮胖、圆脸盘，形影不离宛若连体双胞胎。兄弟二人住在克利夫兰郊外占地七百英亩的瑞士风格农庄黛西山丘，终身未婚，同食共寝，鲜少社交，烟酒不沾，连司机、贴身男仆这类富家排场也一概免除。大萧条前夕，他们的身家已逾一亿美元。

Beginning with their development of suburban Shaker Heights, the brothers had learned the art of using other people’s money. They got into railroads when they built a line from downtown Cleveland to the development. They whirled into the Morgan orbit in 1916 when the Justice Department pressured the New York Central into divesting the “Nickel Plate” railroad, which ran into Cleveland; the Van Sweringens arose as the friendly party who would take the road off New York Central’s hands for a mere \$500,000 cash. Alfred Smith, president of the New York Central, took the boys into 23 Wall, threw his arms around them, and told Lamont, “I have had many experiences with these two boys. They are very capable. . . . I want you to cooperate with them in any way you legitimately can.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305868) Lamont complied.
从他们开发夏克高地郊区开始，范斯韦林根兄弟就学会了借鸡生蛋的艺术。当他们在克利夫兰市中心和那片开发区之间修建一条铁路线时，便踏入了铁路行业。1916 年，司法部迫使纽约中央铁路公司剥离其通往克利夫兰的“镍盘铁路”，他们趁机闯入了摩根的势力圈；范斯韦林根兄弟作为友好方出现，仅以 50 万美元现金就接过了这条铁路。纽约中央铁路公司的总裁阿尔弗雷德·史密斯带着这两兄弟走进华尔街 23 号，热情地拥抱他们，然后对拉蒙特说：“我和这两个小伙子打过很多交道，他们非常能干……我希望你在合法范围内尽一切可能与他们合作。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2305868) 拉蒙特照做了。

The House of Morgan and Guaranty Trust orchestrated financing for the brothers’ railroad and real estate takeovers. As masters of leverage, the Van Sweringens used each new purchase as collateral for the next. Their holding companies took control of other holding companies in an endless hall of mirrors, all supported by little cash but powerful Morgan connections. By 1929, the Van Sweringen railroads ruled America’s fifth largest railroad system from atop a forty-story Cleveland tower and controlled trackage equal in length to all of Britain’s railroads.
摩根财团与担保信托公司联手，为范斯韦林根兄弟的铁路和地产收购大业筹措资金。作为杠杆操作的行家，这对兄弟每完成一笔收购，便将其抵押作为下一笔交易的筹码。他们的控股公司层层嵌套，如同无穷尽的镜中幻象，在几乎没有自有资金支撑的情况下，全凭摩根人脉的强力背书。到 1929 年，范斯韦林根的铁路帝国从克利夫兰一座四十层高塔上发号施令，掌控着全美第五大铁路系统，运营的轨道长度足以匹敌整个英国的铁路网。

Issued by J. P. Morgan and Company in January 1929, the stock of the Alleghany Corporation was meant to be a summary achievement for the brothers—the super holding company atop their pyramid of debt. In the words of the *New York Times*, it was “the holding-company device pushed to its uttermost limits.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306089) The association with the Van Sweringen brothers showed how the recklessness of the 1920s had at last infected the citadel of respectability itself—the Morgan bank. Even the mystical Morgan name couldn’t hold together a pyramid built of nothing but faith. It would be four years before the public finally knew the questionable circumstances under which the Alleghany shares had been floated. In early 1929, however, the issue looked like the best buy in town.
由 J. P. 摩根公司于 1929 年 1 月发行的 Alleghany Corporation 股票，本应是兄弟俩的集大成之作——那座债务金字塔顶端的超级控股公司。用《纽约时报》的话来说，这是“控股公司机制被推向了极致”。与范斯韦林根兄弟的关联表明，1920 年代的鲁莽最终侵蚀了尊严的堡垒——摩根银行。即便是神秘的摩根之名，也无法维系一座仅凭信仰搭建的金字塔。公众在四年后才终于得知 Alleghany 股票发行时的可疑情况。然而在 1929 年初，这支股票看起来像是城里最划算的买卖。

FOR much of 1929, Jack Morgan and Tom Lamont were distracted from the coming storm by the thorny problem of Germany’s reparations. They had continually warned against excess German borrowing only to find themselves subsequently making the loans. In this twilight of the businessman-diplomat, an admiring America still looked to Morgan bankers for guidance. GE chairman Owen Young and Jack Morgan were chosen as American delegates to a Paris conference that was supposed to devise an ultimate solution to the reparations issue; Tom Lamont and Boston lawyer Thomas W. Perkins were alternates. The group was technically unofficial although again in close touch with Washington. In February, they set sail on the *Aquitania.* Upon landing at Cherbourg, they were greeted by French officials, who quickly transferred them to a private railroad car for the trip to Paris.
1929 年的大部分时间里，杰克·摩根和汤姆·拉蒙特都被德国赔款这一棘手问题所困扰，无暇顾及即将到来的风暴。他们曾反复告诫德国不要过度借贷，到头来却发现自己在放贷。在这个商人外交家时代的暮色中，崇拜美国的民众依然仰赖摩根银行家们的指引。通用电气董事长欧文·扬和杰克·摩根被选为美国代表，参加旨在最终解决赔款问题的巴黎会议；汤姆·拉蒙特和波士顿律师托马斯·W·珀金斯则是候补代表。尽管这个代表团名义上非官方，但依然与华盛顿保持密切联络。二月，他们乘阿基塔尼亚号起航。在瑟堡登陆时，法国官员迎接了他们，并迅速将他们转送至私人列车前往巴黎。

Chaired by Owen Young, the conference took place at the plush new Hotel George V. Once again the sticking point was Germany’s capacity to make reparations. As usual, the French, represented by Emile Moreau of the Banque de France, were obstinate in their opposition to lower reparations. And the U.S. refused to lower war debts. Believing reparations financially insupportable, the Reichsbank president, Dr. Schacht, disrupted the conference several times, flying into a rage and storming from the room. One of the British delegates, Lord Revelstoke, noted that with “his hatchet, Teuton face and burly neck and badly fitting collar . . . he reminds me of a sealion at the Zoo.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306251)
会议由 Owen Young 主持，在豪华的新 Hotel George V 举行。再次，争议焦点在于德国的赔款能力。一如往常，由 Banque de France 的 Emile Moreau 代表的法国人顽固地反对降低赔款。而美国拒绝减免战争债务。Reichsbank 行长 Dr. Schacht 认为赔款在财政上无法支撑，多次扰乱会议，勃然大怒并愤然离席。英国代表之一 Lord Revelstoke 评论道，他“带着他那条顿人的斧头脸、粗壮的脖子和不合适的领子……让我想起动物园里的海狮。” [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306251)

At this conference, Jack Morgan had difficulty hiding his intense dislike of the Germans. Dr. Schacht made the mistake of buttonholing him about the House of Morgan’s financing of Germany’s railways. Jack was scornful. “From what I see of the Germans they are 2nd-rate people,” he cabled New York, “and I would rather have their business done for them by somebody else.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306416) He grumbled about how the conference was ruining his plans for a *Corsair* cruise on the Mediterranean, not to mention those for shooting in Scotland; Dr. Schacht noted Morgan was the first to slip away. This was a rare occasion when Jack let his feelings surface in public, and Lord Revelstoke compared him to “a wild bison in a shop that sells Dresden china.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306622)
在这次会议上，杰克·摩根几乎毫不掩饰他对德国人强烈的厌恶。沙赫特博士犯了个错，硬要跟他聊摩根财团资助德国铁路的事。杰克一脸鄙夷。“就我对德国人的观察来看，他们不过是二流货色，”他发电报给纽约说，“我宁愿让其他人来替他们打理生意。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306416) 他抱怨这次会议打乱了他乘坐“海盗号”游艇巡游地中海、以及去苏格兰打猎的计划；沙赫特博士注意到摩根是最早脚底抹油溜走的人。这种公开流露情绪的场合实属罕见，雷弗尔斯托克勋爵将他比作“闯进德累斯顿瓷器店里的野牛”。 [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306622)

In Paris, Dr. Schacht hoped to win substantial decreases in reparations and was irate at French intransigence. He shocked the Allies, in turn, by proposing that Germany get back the Polish corridor and take overseas colonies in exchange for the high cost of reparations. To help break a diplomatic logjam, Owen Young responded to a suggestion by his young assistant, David Sarnoff, shortly to be president of RCA, that he attempt informal negotiations with Schacht. Lamont told Sarnoff, “Good luck. If anyone can do this job, you can.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306797) On May 1, the Russian-Jewish immigrant and the German Hjalmar Schacht had their first dinner at Schacht’s room at the Hôtel Royal Monceau. There was instant rapport. Schacht had once studied Hebrew, a language Sarnoff learned while studying for the rabbinate, and they ended up talking about everything from German opera to the Old Testament. They also discussed reparations, and the first dinner turned into a marathon, eighteen-hour negotiating session. Sarnoff later took credit for selling a “safeguard clause” to Schacht that related reparations to German economic performance. This idea reconciled Schacht, however briefly, to the plan.[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306956)
在巴黎，沙赫特博士希望大幅削减赔款，并对法方的强硬态度感到愤怒。他反过来让协约国震惊，提出让德国收回波兰走廊并获取海外殖民地，以换取高额赔款。为打破外交僵局，欧文·扬采纳了其年轻助手大卫·萨尔诺夫（即将担任 RCA 总裁）的建议，尝试与沙赫特进行非正式谈判。拉蒙特对萨尔诺夫说：“祝你好运。如果谁能做成这事，那一定是你。” [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306797) 5 月 1 日，这位俄裔犹太移民与德国人亚尔马·沙赫特在巴黎皇家梦索酒店的沙赫特房间共进首次晚餐。两人一见如故。沙赫特曾学习过希伯来语——这正是萨尔诺夫为成为拉比而苦学过的语言——他们从德国歌剧聊到《旧约》，无所不谈。两人还讨论了赔款问题，这顿晚餐最终演变成一场长达十八小时的马拉松式谈判。 萨诺夫后来将向沙赫特兜售一项“保障条款”归功于自己，该条款将赔款与德国经济表现挂钩。这一想法暂时让沙赫特接受了该计划。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2306956)

Jack was so delighted by Sarnoff’s initiative that he brought him a big bunch of ripe French strawberries. He also told Sarnoff, “David, if you actually bring back a signed agreement, you can have anything you ask for that is within my gift.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307168) After another lengthy bargaining session in late May, Sarnoff brought an agreement back to the Ritz Hotel. Jack, amazed, tipped his black homburg to Sarnoff. “I doff my hat to you,” he said with a bow. “And I propose to stick to my promise. Ask for anything you want, and it will be yours.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307327) Sarnoff asked for a meerschaum pipe of the sort Jack smoked. It was made by an elderly London pipemaker, who first made Pierpont’s. Jack chartered a plane so someone could fly to London and fetch the pipe for Sarnoff.
杰克对萨诺夫的主动行动欣喜万分，送了他一大捧成熟的法国草莓。他还对萨诺夫说：“大卫，如果你真能带回一份签好的协议，但凡我能力范围内能给的，你随便要什么都行。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307168) 经过五月底又一场漫长的谈判，萨诺夫带着一份协议回到了丽兹酒店。杰克惊叹不已，摘下他的黑色汉堡帽向萨诺夫致意。“我向你脱帽致敬，”他鞠了一躬说道，“我承诺兑现诺言。你随便提要求，我一定满足。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307327) 萨诺夫要了一个杰克抽的那种海泡石烟斗。那烟斗出自伦敦一位老匠人之手，对方最早是为皮尔庞特制作的。杰克特意包了一架飞机，让人飞到伦敦去把烟斗取回来给萨诺夫。

The Young Plan reduced the schedule of reparations payments from that designated by the earlier Dawes Plan, stretching them over a fiftynine-year period. It also attempted to depoliticize German debt by converting it into tradable bonds. Instead of paying the Allies directly, Germany would pay bondholders through a new Bank for International Settlements. This would free Germany from political interference and lift the yoke of the hated agent general’s office. The boyish Parker Gilbert left Berlin to become a J. P. Morgan partner, a move that didn’t surprise the Germans. When Gilbert warned the Germans against seeking any foreign loan beyond the Young loan, Karl von Schubert of the German Foreign Office spied an ulterior motive. J. P. Morgan was about to float a big loan for France, and a German loan “would be seen \[by Gilbert\] as a disagreeable competitor for the project of the House of Morgan, to which he is known to be close,” said von Schubert.[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307472)
杨格计划将此前道威斯计划规定的赔款支付日程延长至五十九年，同时试图通过将德国债务转换为可交易债券来去政治化。德国无需直接向协约国支付赔款，而是通过新成立的国际清算银行向债券持有人付款。这样一来，德国便能摆脱政治干预，卸下那令人憎恶的督察长办公室的枷锁。稚气未脱的帕克·吉尔伯特离开柏林，成为 J. P. 摩根的一名合伙人，这一举动并未让德国人感到意外。当吉尔伯特告诫德国不要寻求除杨格贷款以外的任何外国贷款时，德国外交部官员卡尔·冯·舒伯特察觉到了别有用心的动机。J. P. 摩根正准备为法国发放一笔巨额贷款，而德国的贷款“在吉尔伯特看来，会成为摩根家族计划的一个令人生厌的竞争对手——众所周知，他与摩根家族关系密切，”冯·舒伯特说道。 [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307472)

Set up in a hotel off the main square of Basel, Switzerland, the new Bank for International Settlements fulfilled Montagu Norman’s dream of a place where central bankers could forge international monetary policy without political interference. Norman lovingly called it a confessional. A provincial U.S. Congress didn’t like the word *international* and refused to let the Federal Reserve join, although several private American banks bought shares in it. The BIS would outlast the Young Plan and develop into a central bank for central bankers, just as Monty Norman had envisioned.
在瑞士巴塞尔主广场附近的一家酒店里，新成立的国际清算银行实现了蒙塔古·诺曼的梦想——让央行行长们能在不受政治干预的情况下制定国际货币政策。诺曼亲切地称其为“告解室”。当时美国国会中一些保守派议员不喜欢“国际”这个词，拒绝让美联储加入，尽管几家美国私人银行购买了其股份。国际清算银行的存续时间超越了杨格计划，最终如蒙蒂·诺曼所设想的那样，发展成了央行行长们的中央银行。

In June 1929, the German debt settlement was announced. Newspapers showed Dr. Schacht leaning across Owen Young to shake hands with Emile Moreau of France’s central bank. No sooner were the documents signed than a window curtain caught fire and burst into flames—a lurid omen suggesting the Young Plan’s fate in Germany, where it would prove no more popular than the Dawes plan. Dr. Schacht signed the document with strongly felt ambivalence, insisting that the German Cabinet take responsibility. Before long, he would denounce it and become a Nazi favorite. The \$100-million portion of the Young loan, sponsored by the House of Morgan in June 1930, would be its second and final effort for Germany. Unlike the robust reception given to the Dawes loan, the Young loan aroused scant enthusiasm. Nevertheless, in 1929 the Paris conference gave a sense of closure to the era’s most intractable problem and helped spur the final upward rise of the stock market in New York City. Owen Young was even mentioned as a possible presidential candidate.
1929 年 6 月，德国债务结算方案公之于众。报纸上刊登的照片显示，沙赫特博士斜身越过欧文·扬，与法国央行的埃米尔·莫罗握手。文件刚刚签署完毕，窗帘便突然起火燃烧——这一不祥之兆预示着杨格计划在德国的命运，它最终将和道威斯计划一样不得人心。沙赫特博士在签署文件时内心充满矛盾，坚持要求德国内阁承担责任。不久之后，他便公开谴责这一计划，并成为纳粹的宠儿。由摩根财团在 1930 年 6 月承销的杨格贷款中 1 亿美元的部分，是其对德国的第二次也是最后一次援助。与道威斯贷款受到的热烈追捧不同，杨格贷款几乎无人问津。然而，1929 年的巴黎会议让那个时代最棘手的问题有了一个收尾，并助推了纽约股市的最后一轮暴涨。 欧文·杨甚至被提名为可能的总统候选人。

WHILE Jack stayed behind for the grouse season, Lamont returned to New York. As a rule, Morgan partners didn’t belong to the select group of financiers who could later point out their apocalyptic warnings about the stock market. (Joe Kennedy later said he sold stocks after hearing his bootblack touting them.) Lamont was an exponent of the new economic era and thought only a business downturn could derail stocks. George Whitney thought the Federal Reserve Board a “set of damn fools” for tightening credit in 1929. The only Morgan seer was Russell Leffingwell, the former assistant Treasury secretary who had come to the Corner in 1923 from the law firm of Cravath, Henderson, Leffingwell, and de Gersdorff. An entertaining and courtly man, Leffingwell had a long pointed nose and a shock of premature white hair that gave him an air of wisdom. He was a liberal and sometime Democrat. In his combative, curmudgeonly intellectual style, he pilloried ideologues of both the left and the right. A perpetual worrier, he was withering in his view of the optimistic Andrew Mellon: “Meanwhile, the greatest Secretary of the Treasury since Alexander Hamilton grows richer on paper and thinks that all is for the best possible in the best of possible worlds.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307657)
杰克留在苏格兰打松鸡，拉蒙特则返回纽约。通常来说，摩根银行的合伙人并不属于那些日后能吹嘘自己曾对股市发出灾难警告的精英金融家之列（乔·肯尼迪后来声称自己是在听到擦鞋童都在推荐股票后卖出的）。拉蒙特是新经济时代的代言人，他认为只有商业衰退才能让股市下跌。乔治·惠特尼则称美联储在 1929 年收紧信贷的做法是“一群蠢货”。摩根家族中唯一的预言家是拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔——这位前助理财政部长于 1923 年从克拉瓦斯、亨德森、莱芬韦尔与德格斯多夫律师事务所转投华尔街。他风趣儒雅，长着尖长的鼻子，早生华发，一副睿智模样。作为自由派与无党派民主党人，他以好斗执拗的知识分子风格，对左右两派的空想家都大加挞伐。 他天生就是个忧心忡忡的人，看到乐观的安德鲁·梅隆日渐消沉，便不无讽刺地说：“与此同时，这位自亚历山大·汉密尔顿以来最伟大的财政部长在纸面上越来越富有，还自以为眼前的世界已是尽善尽美。”” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307657)

Leffingwell subscribed to the cheap-money theory of the crash; that is, he blamed excessively low interest rates for the speculation in stock. In 1927, Monty Norman had visited New York and asked Ben Strong for lower interest rates to take pressure off the pound. Strong obliged by lowering his discount rate. Leffingwell believed this had triggered the stock market boom. In early March 1929, when Leffingwell heard reports that Monty was getting “panicky” about the frothy conditions on Wall Street, he impatiently told Lamont, “Monty and Ben sowed the wind. I expect we shall all have to reap the whirlwind. . . . I think we are going to have a world credit crisis.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307853) Later he held the two directly responsible for the Depression. It may be recalled that Jack Morgan and others at 23 Wall had favored England’s return to gold in 1925, but only with the proviso that Monty raise rates, not that Ben lower them.
莱芬韦尔信奉廉价货币理论来解释股市崩盘；也就是说，他认为过低的利率是导致股票投机的原因。1927 年，蒙蒂·诺曼曾到访纽约，请求本·斯特朗降低利率以减轻英镑压力。斯特朗照做了，下调了贴现率。莱芬韦尔认为这触发了股市繁荣。1929 年 3 月初，当莱芬韦尔听说蒙蒂对华尔街的泡沫行情感到"恐慌"时，他不耐烦地告诉拉蒙特："蒙蒂和本播下的是风，我想我们都得收获风暴……我认为我们将面临一场世界性信贷危机。" [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307853) 后来，他直接指责这两人应对大萧条负责。或许还记得，杰克·摩根和华尔街 23 号的其他人在 1925 年曾支持英国恢复金本位制，但前提是蒙蒂提高利率，而非本降低利率。

Benjamin Strong didn’t live to see the crash. He went through a hellish series of illnesses—tuberculosis, influenza, pneumonia, and shingles—and was shot full of morphine when he died, at age fifty-five, in October 1928; Montagu Norman, disconsolate, mourned Strong’s death for years. During the spring and summer of 1929, Strong’s hand-picked successor, George Harrison, pleaded with the Federal Reserve Board in Washington to increase interest rates. Instead, the board vetoed rate hikes in New York. Russell Leffingwell saw a Greek tragedy unfolding. He feared that Harrison had inherited the antagonism left by Strong and that “the immense resistance offered by the Washington Board may be partly the result of ten years of bottled up bitterness against poor Ben’s domination.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307979) At the worst possible moment, the system was undercut by bureaucratic feuding. When the discount rate was belatedly raised in August 1929 from 5 to 6 percent, it was too late to cool off the boom.
本杰明·斯特朗没能活着看到那场崩盘。他经历了一连串地狱般的疾病——肺结核、流感、肺炎和带状疱疹——1928 年 10 月去世时，才五十五岁，体内被注射了大量吗啡；蒙塔古·诺曼悲痛不已，为斯特朗的离世哀悼了多年。1929 年春夏之际，斯特朗亲手选定的继任者乔治·哈里森恳求华盛顿的联邦储备委员会提高利率，结果委员会却否决了纽约的加息动议。拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔仿佛看到一场希腊悲剧正在上演。他担心哈里森继承了斯特朗留下的对立情绪，“华盛顿委员会施加的巨大阻力，可能部分源于十年来面对可怜的本（斯特朗）的强势而积压的怨气。” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2307979) 在最糟糕的时刻，整个体系被官僚内斗所削弱。当贴现率终于在 1929 年 8 月从 5%姗姗来迟地调升至 6%时，已为时过晚，无法给过热的市场降温了。

On September 5, 1929, the tragedy of that Black Thursday was foreshadowed when an obscure economist named Babson repeated a warning he had been making for years: “Sooner or later a crash is coming, and it may be terrific.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308158) In ordinary times, the remark would have been ignored. Instead, circulated on news wires, it briefly cracked the stock market. Professor Irving Fisher of Yale, high priest of academic hope, rallied the faithful: “Stock prices have reached what looks like a permanently high plateau.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308309) But the American economy had peaked in August and was falling even as Fisher spoke.
1929 年 9 月 5 日，那个黑色星期四的悲剧已现端倪。一位名不见经传的经济学家巴布森再次重复他多年来一直发出的警告："崩盘迟早会来，而且可能极其惨烈。" [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308158) 若在平时，这番言论无人理会。但这一次，消息通过新闻电讯传播，竟令股市短暂受挫。耶鲁大学的欧文·费雪教授——学术乐观派的大祭司——鼓舞着信徒们："股价已进入一个看似永久的高位平台。" [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308309) 然而，美国经济在八月份已见顶，就在费雪发言之时，经济正在下滑。

By mid-October, the stock market gyrations so worried Hoover that he sent an emissary, Harry Robinson, to consult Lamont, his chief adviser on Wall Street. Hoover, the first president with a telephone on his desk, frequently rang Lamont before breakfast. Despite Hoover’s closeness to the House of Morgan, many partners secretly ridiculed him as cold, pompous, and pigheaded. Parker Gilbert once called him “Secretary of Commerce and Under-Secretary of all the other departments.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308469) During the 1928 campaign, the Democrats had released a memo written by Leffingwell in his Treasury days that said, “Hoover knows nothing about finance, nothing about exchange and nothing about economics.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308643) Hoover was petulant because the bank hadn’t done more to aid his reelection. Before the primary, he sent a threatening note to Lamont, accusing him of working for Charles Dawes.
到 10 月中旬，股市的剧烈波动让胡佛忧心忡忡，他派特使哈里·罗宾逊去征求华尔街首席顾问拉蒙特的意见。作为首位在办公桌上装有电话的总统，胡佛经常在早餐前就给拉蒙特打电话。尽管胡佛与摩根财团关系密切，但许多合伙人私下却嘲笑他冷漠、自大又固执。帕克·吉尔伯特曾称他是"商务部长兼其他所有部门的副部长"。1928 年竞选期间，民主党公布了一份莱芬韦尔在财政部任职时撰写的备忘录，其中写道："胡佛对金融一窍不通，对外汇一无所知，对经济学更是不甚了了。"胡佛之所以愠怒，是因为银行没有为他的连任提供更多帮助。预选前，他给拉蒙特送去一封带有威胁意味的便条，指责他为查尔斯·道斯效力。

To his credit, however, the president wasn’t heedless of the Wall Street peril. In early 1928, while commerce secretary, he was flabbergasted by Coolidge’s cavalier lack of concern about the stock market. And in March 1929, as president, he summoned to the White House Richard Whitney, vice-president of the New York Stock Exchange and brother of Morgan partner George Whitney. Hoover wanted the Exchange to curb speculation—a plea that was ignored. Hoover also blamed the Fed for low interest rates and providing banks with ample reserves, which were then used to finance buying on margin.
不过，值得肯定的是，总统并非对华尔街的危机视而不见。早在 1928 年初，时任商务部长的他就对 Coolidge 在股市问题上那副漫不经心的态度感到震惊。到了 1929 年 3 月，就任总统后，他将纽约证券交易所副总裁、Morgan 合伙人 George Whitney 之兄 Richard Whitney 召入白宫。Hoover 希望交易所遏制投机——这一恳求却被无视了。Hoover 还指责 Fed 维持低利率，并为银行提供充足的储备金，而这些资金随后被用于融资买入（buying on margin）。

Now Hoover’s messenger, Harry Robinson, wished to know the answers to two questions: Were the increasing number of stock mergers grounds for concern? And should the federal government take action to stop speculation on Wall Street? Five days before Black Thursday, Lamont wrote Hoover a memo in which he whitewashed the practices of an era. He blandly waved away Hoover’s well-founded worries: “First we must remember that there is a great deal of exaggeration in current gossip about speculation. . . .” He paid tribute to the self-correcting forces of the marketplace. Citing industries that had lagged in the rally—automobiles, lumber, oil, paper, sugar, and cement—he declared that the market hadn’t overheated. With a nod to United Corporation and Alleghany, he praised the new holding companies that now dominated railroads and public utilities. His rousing peroration set all fears to rest: “Since the war the country has embarked on a remarkable period of healthy prosperity. . . . The future appears brilliant.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308793) The only fault he found was with the Federal Reserve Board in Washington—for blocking higher interest rates at the regional reserve banks.
此时，胡佛的信使哈里·罗宾逊正急切想要得到两个问题的答案：不断增加的股票并购案是否值得担忧？联邦政府是否应该采取行动，遏制华尔街的投机行为？在黑色星期四来临的五天前，拉蒙特给胡佛递交了一份备忘录，为那个时代的操作方式涂脂抹粉。他轻描淡写地驳斥了胡佛确有根据的担忧：“首先，我们必须记住，当前关于投机的流言蜚语中，有太多夸大其词……”他盛赞市场的自我纠偏能力。他列举了在上涨行情中掉队的行业——汽车、木材、石油、造纸、制糖和水泥——宣称市场并未过热。他顺带提及联合公司和阿勒格尼公司，对如今主宰铁路和公用事业的新兴控股公司大加赞赏。他那振聋发聩的结语让所有疑虑烟消云散：“自战后以来，国家已步入一段非凡的健康繁荣时期……” 未来看似一片光明。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308793) 他唯一的不满就是华盛顿的 Federal Reserve Board——因为它阻止了地区储备银行提高利率。

Martin Egan brought the memo to the White House. The president was so eager to hear Lamont’s report that he held up a parade for ten or fifteen minutes in order to talk to Egan, who found him generally confident about his presidency, if edgy about Wall Street. The satisfaction didn’t last long. On October 22, the president sent a frantic messenger to Lamont expressing concern about the “speculative situation which seemed to him to be running very wild,” as Lamont relayed the message to Jack.[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308981) Hoover was correct—if a little late. The next day, panic selling hit selected blue chips, with Westinghouse dropping 35 points and General Electric 20. The balloon was about to burst.
马丁·伊根把备忘录带到了白宫。总统迫不及待想听拉蒙特的报告，甚至为了跟伊根谈话，把阅兵队伍都耽搁了十到十五分钟。伊根发现总统对自己的执政总体充满自信，只是对华尔街有些焦躁。然而这份满足感没能持续多久。10 月 22 日，总统派了个急使匆匆找到拉蒙特，表达了对“在他看来已经失控的投机局面”的担忧——这是拉蒙特转述给杰克的原话。 [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2308981) 胡佛的判断没错——只是稍微晚了一步。第二天，恐慌性抛售席卷了部分蓝筹股，Westinghouse 暴跌 35 点，General Electric 下跌 20 点。泡沫即将破裂。

The following morning, Winston Churchill stood in the visitors’ gallery of the New York Stock Exchange. Two weeks earlier, he had lunched with the Morgan partners who had helped him to restore England to the gold standard in 1925. Now he looked down on a scene that many would circuitously trace to that 1925 decision, with its need for lower U.S. interest rates. Within the first two hours of trading, almost \$10 billion was lost on paper. The drops posted were so sharp and the resulting shrieks so fearful that the gallery was closed by late morning.
第二天上午，温斯顿·丘吉尔站在纽约证券交易所的参观席上。两周前，他刚刚与摩根公司的合伙人共进午餐——正是这些人曾在 1925 年帮助英国恢复金本位制。如今他俯瞰着交易大厅，许多人日后会曲折地认为，眼前这一幕与 1925 年那项决策（及其带来的美国降息需求）脱不了干系。开盘仅两小时，账面蒸发近 100 亿美元。暴跌之剧烈、随之而来的惊恐尖叫如此骇人，以至于参观席在午前便被迫关闭。

As in 1907, desperate men stood on the steps of Federal Hall, hands in their pockets, their hats pulled low, staring grimly ahead. Their shocked silence is almost palpable in the photographs taken that day. They stood six deep outside the Stock Exchange. Having bought on margin, many investors were ruined outright. Newspapers noted a strange noise filtering through the canyons of the Street—a roar, a hum, a murmur. It was the cumulative sound of thousands of stunned people giving vent to their feelings. Violence was in the air. When a workman appeared atop a building, the crowd assumed he wanted to leap and was impatient with his hesitation. A dozen suicides were reported, some poetically apt. “Two men jumped hand-in-hand from a high window in the Ritz,” Galbraith noted. “They had a joint account.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309175) Only the Stock Exchange pages, who lacked investments, savored the ruin of their bosses.
如同 1907 年那样，绝望的人们站在联邦大厅的台阶上，双手插在口袋里，帽檐压得很低，目光阴沉地凝视前方。那天拍摄的照片中，他们震惊的沉默几乎触手可及。证券交易所外，人群层层叠叠地站了六排。由于保证金交易，许多投资者瞬间倾家荡产。各家报纸注意到，华尔街的峡谷间回荡着一种奇异的噪音——咆哮、嗡鸣、低语。那是成千上万惊愕的人们宣泄情绪汇聚而成的声响。空气中弥漫着暴力的气息。当一个工人出现在楼顶时，围观的人群以为他要跳楼，甚至对他的犹豫显得不耐烦。报道称有十几人自杀，有些死法还颇具诗意。加尔布雷思记录道：“两个男人手拉手从丽思酒店的高窗跳下。他们持有联合账户。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309175) 唯有那些没有投资、手头拮据的股票交易员，暗自品味着老板们破产的滋味。

Around noon, the master bankers of Wall Street marched briskly up the steps of 23 Wall. These were the men whose exploits had thrilled America—Charles Mitchell of National City, Albert Wiggin of Chase, Seward Prosser of Bankers Trust, William Potter of Guaranty Trust. They represented \$6 billion in assets, perhaps the world’s greatest pool of wealth. For the last time, they enjoyed the heroic stature that the Jazz Age had conferred upon them. To Street veterans, it came as no surprise that the meeting was chaired by fifty-eight-year-old Tom Lamont. The Morgan role in rescues was now automatic. Whatever its flaws, it was the banker’s bank, the arbiter of disputes, the statesmanlike firm that offered confidentiality no other house could match. In the words of B. C. Forbes, “Thomas W. Lamont, foremost Morgan partner, stepped into a role on Thursday which the original J. P. Morgan used to take in past panics.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309336)
中午时分，华尔街的银行大亨们快步登上 23 号华尔街的台阶。这些人是曾让全美为之振奋的金融巨子——国民城市银行的查尔斯·米切尔、大通银行的阿尔伯特·威金、银行家信托公司的苏厄德·普罗瑟、担保信托公司的威廉·波特。他们掌控着 60 亿美元的资产，这或许是当时全世界最庞大的财富池。这是他们最后一次享受爵士时代赋予的英雄光环。对于华尔街老手而言，此次会议由 58 岁的汤姆·拉蒙特主持并不意外。摩根在危机时刻的救市角色已成惯例。无论存在何种缺陷，它始终是银行的银行、纠纷的仲裁者，以及提供无与伦比保密性的老牌商行。正如 B.C.福布斯所言："周四，摩根首席合伙人托马斯·W·拉蒙特接过了昔日 J.P.摩根在历次恐慌中扮演的角色。" [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309336)

Even in a crisis, Lamont was debonair, his sangfroid now legendary. He was Wall Street’s mystery man, and Black Thursday would be his celebrated moment. He was a strange candidate for the part. In his youth, he had lost a year’s salary by selling short and thereafter forswore stock speculation. He was also the banker who had advised a skittish Hoover to take a posture of benign indifference toward Wall Street.
即使在危机中，Lamont 依然风度翩翩，他的沉着冷静如今已成传奇。他是华尔街的神秘人物，而黑色星期四将成为他的高光时刻。他扮演这个角色着实有些出人意料。年轻时，他曾因做空亏掉了一整年的薪水，此后便发誓不再进行股票投机。此外，正是这位银行家建议心神不宁的 Hoover 对华尔街采取一种善意漠视的姿态。

The bankers’ rescue on Black Thursday proved longer on symbolism than on substance. The men knew they couldn’t prop up a collapsing stock market, so they tried to introduce liquidity and engineer an orderly decline. There had been terrifying moments that morning when no buyers appeared. So they pledged \$240 million to buy up assorted stocks and stabilize the market. (The Guggenheims joined this pool.) It was just a finger in the dike, but it was the best they could manage.
银行家们在黑色星期四的救援行动，象征意义大于实质。他们深知无法支撑一个正在崩溃的股市，于是试图注入流动性，引导市场有序下跌。那天早上曾出现过没有买家的可怕时刻。因此他们承诺投入 2.4 亿美元来购买各类股票以稳定市场。（Guggenheim 家族也加入了这一资金池。）这不过是杯水车薪，但已是他们所能做到的最好地步。

Because the president of the Stock Exchange was in Honolulu, the acting president, Richard Whitney, was agent for the bankers’ rescue. He seemed an ideal choice because his brother was a Morgan partner and his own firm was a Morgan bond broker. Richard Whitney was also a great pretender, and his cool demeanor masked the fact that he and his wife, an heiress, were absorbing stock losses that would amount to \$2 million.[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309462) So when he took his jaunty stroll across the Exchange floor at 1:30 P.M., it was an extraordinary performance. He went to the trading booth for U.S. Steel and bid 205 for twenty thousand shares—topping the previous bid by several points. As news of his purchase spread, the market seemed to steady for a time.
因为证券交易所主席正在火奴鲁鲁，代理主席理查德·惠特尼便成了银行家救市行动的代理人。他似乎是理想人选——他哥哥是摩根合伙人，他自己的公司也是摩根债券经纪商。理查德·惠特尼还极其会装腔作势，他冷静的外表掩盖了一个事实：他和身为女继承人的妻子正承受着高达 200 万美元的股票亏损。 [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309462) 因此，当他在下午 1 点 30 分趾高气扬地踱步穿过交易所大厅时，那简直是一场精彩的表演。他走到美国钢铁公司的交易台前，以 205 美元的价格竞购两万股——比之前的出价高出几个点。随着他买入的消息传开，市场似乎暂时稳定了下来。

The choice of U.S. Steel, a Morgan ward, was no accident: as gentleman bankers, Lamont and the others felt they should support companies they had sponsored. It later turned out Whitney had bought only two hundred shares of Steel and pulled his order when others jumped in. At another fifteen or twenty trading posts, he repeated his bidding, placing almost \$20 million in orders. By day’s end, only half the bankers’ money was actually spent. Yet their lingering magic was such that the market briefly rallied that afternoon. It was the last conjuring trick of the 1920s.
选择美国钢铁公司——这家摩根旗下企业——并非偶然：作为绅士银行家，拉蒙特等人认为应当扶持他们曾资助过的公司。事后发现惠特尼最初只买了两百股钢铁股，当其他人蜂拥跟进时便撤回了订单。在另外十五到二十个交易点，他重复着竞价操作，累计下了近两千万美元的订单。到收盘时，银行家们实际只花掉了一半资金。然而他们残存的魔力依然强大，当天下午市场一度短暂回升。这是二十年代最后一次障眼法。

At the end of the trading day, the bankers regrouped for a second meeting and designated Lamont their spokesman. He was mobbed by newsmen shouting questions. Dangling his pince-nez, he came up with the most memorable understatement in American financial history: “There has been a little distress selling on the Stock Exchange.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309623) Although often mocked, the line was actually a reply to a sarcastic reporter who asked whether Lamont had noticed the selling on the Exchange that day. Lamont blandly blamed the decline on a “technical condition” and spoke of “air pockets” in the market. In a phrase of incomparable ambiguity, he said the market was “susceptible to betterment.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309786) These hand-holding sessions with reporters continued for weeks and made Lamont a celebrity. He leapt to the cover of *Time* magazine.
交易日结束时，银行家们再次聚首开会，并指定拉蒙特 (Lamont) 为他们的发言人。他被蜂拥而至的记者团团围住，大家七嘴八舌地抛出问题。他捏着夹鼻眼镜，说出了美国金融史上最令人难忘的一句轻描淡写：“证券交易所出现了一些轻微的恐慌性抛售。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309623) 尽管这句话常被嘲讽，但实际上是针对一位记者尖刻提问的回答——那人问拉蒙特是否注意到了当天交易所的抛售情况。拉蒙特不动声色地将下跌归咎于“技术性因素”，并提到了市场中的“空头陷阱”。他用一句无比模棱两可的话说道，市场“有望好转”。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309786) 这种与记者们“手把手”沟通的套路持续了数周，让拉蒙特成了名人。他一跃登上了《时代》周刊 (Time) 的封面。

Almost at once, Wall Street began to issue bravely hopeful statements. The silver-lining specialists appeared in force. That night, the retail brokers met at the brokerage house of Hornblower and Weeks and pronounced the market “technically in better condition than it has been in months.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309938) The headline in the *Wall Street Journal* the next morning featured not the crash but the rescue: “BANKERS HALT STOCK DEBACLE: 2-HOUR SELLING DELUGE STOPPED AFTER CONFERENCE AT MORGAN’S OFFICE: \$1,000,000,000 FOR SUPPORT.’[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310099) The bankers asked Hoover to plug stocks as a cheap buy. Instead, he disgorged his well-known platitude: “The fundamental business of the country, that is production and distribution of commodities, is on a sound and prosperous basis.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310251) The market staggered through Friday and Saturday morning trading without a fresh crisis.
华尔街几乎立刻开始发布充满勇气的乐观声明。那些擅长寻找一线希望的人纷纷现身。当晚，零售经纪商在 Hornblower and Weeks 的经纪公司开会，宣称市场“从技术层面看，状况比过去几个月都要好。” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2309938) 第二天早晨，《华尔街日报》的头条不是关于崩盘，而是关于救援：“银行家遏制股市灾难：在摩根办公室会议后两小时抛售狂潮停止：10 亿美元支持。” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310099) 银行家们请求胡佛出面鼓吹股票是廉价买入的机会。然而，他却抛出了那句老生常谈：“国家的基本产业，即商品的生产和销售，处于稳健繁荣的基础之上。” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310251) 市场在周五和周六早盘的交易中踉跄前行，没有出现新的危机。

The 1929 crash unfolded in two stages, with a weekend in between. On Sunday, the mood was grim as tourist buses made ghoulish swings through Wall Street to see where the crash had occurred. Those who pondered Hoover’s statement over the weekend apparently rushed to sell on Monday. American Telephone and Telegraph dropped 34 points, and General Electric 47 points. Both the market and public faith in bankers were collapsing.
1929 年的崩盘分两个阶段展开，中间隔了一个周末。周日，气氛阴郁，旅游巴士阴森地穿行华尔街，去看看崩盘发生的地方。那些在周末仔细琢磨胡佛声明的人，显然在周一就匆忙抛售。American Telephone and Telegraph 下跌 34 点，General Electric 下跌 47 点。市场和公众对银行家的信心都在崩溃。

On Tragic Tuesday, October 29, investors looked back on Black Thursday as a halcyon time. On this worst day in market history, the ticker lagged two and a half hours behind. More than sixteen million shares changed hands—a record that would stand for forty years. By day’s end, the two-day damage had dragged share prices down by nearly 25 percent. Buying didn’t dry up this time: it simply disappeared. At the rally’s peak, White Sewing Machine sold for 48, then slumped to 11 on Monday. On Tuesday, when no buyers surfaced, an alert messenger bought shares at \$1 apiece. When commuters entered Grand Central Terminal that evening, newsboys hawking papers shouted, “Read ’em and weep!”
在十月二十九日这个悲惨的星期二，投资者们回望过去，竟觉得黑色星期四已是风平浪静之时。在这个市场历史上最黑暗的一天，行情记录仪滞后了两个半小时。超过一千六百万股易手——这一纪录保持了四十年。到当天收盘时，两日的暴跌已将股价拖低了近 25%。买家这次不是枯竭，而是彻底消失了。在大涨顶峰时，White Sewing Machine 股价曾卖到 48 美元，周一却暴跌至 11 美元。周二，当无人问津时，一个机灵的送信人用每股 1 美元的价格买进了一些股票。当晚，通勤者走进中央车站时，卖报的报童们喊道：“看看吧，哭吧！”

Unlike Black Thursday, Tragic Tuesday exposed the bankers’ frailty. They were little men standing before a tidal wave. The *New York Times* wrote, “Banking support, which would have been impressive and successful under ordinary circumstances, was swept violently aside, as block after block of stock, tremendous in proportions, deluged the market.”[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310412) Where rumors on Black Thursday were hopeful—men winked and talked of the “organized support” taking control—Tragic Tuesday was marked by reports of bankers dumping stocks to save themselves.
与"黑色星期四"不同，"悲惨星期二"暴露了银行家的脆弱无力。他们不过是一群站在海啸面前的小人物。《纽约时报》写道："在正常情况下本应令人印象深刻且成功的银行护盘行动，被市场无情冲垮，一卖单一波接一波地、以大得惊人的规模淹没了市场。" [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310412) 黑色星期四时还流传着充满希望的传言——人们挤眉弄眼，议论着"有组织的救市"即将掌控局面——而"悲惨星期二"的标志性新闻，则是银行家们为了自保而大肆抛售股票。

Lamont now faced a more hostile group of reporters. He had to deny reports that his group was sabotaging the market for profit. “The group has continued and will continue in a cooperative way to support the market and has not been a seller of stocks.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310564) With his usual cunning phraseology, he said the situation “retained hopeful features.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310690) In a vain effort to bolster confidence, U.S. Steel and American Can declared extra dividends.
拉蒙特此刻面对的是一群更富敌意的记者。他不得不否认自己的集团正在通过做空市场牟利的报道。"该集团过去、现在和将来都会以合作的方式继续支持市场，并未抛售过股票。" [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310564) 他使用了一贯狡猾的措辞，称当前形势"仍保留着希望的特征。" [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310690) 为了提振信心，美国钢铁和美国罐头公司宣布增发特别股息，但这不过是徒劳之举。

As if expressing a new bunker mentality, the Stock Exchange governors met on Tragic Tuesday in a basement room under the Exchange floor. When Lamont and George Whitney tried to slip in unnoticed, they were briefly detained by guards. The main topic was whether to shut the market. Richard Whitney thought a closing would unsettle the public and create a black market on the curb, as happened at the outbreak of World War I. He also feared it would create chaos among banks with heavy call loans to brokers. At various points in 1929, Morgans had \$100 million outstanding in such loans, with stocks pledged as collateral. How would Wall Street banks and brokers function with stock collateral frozen?
仿佛流露出一种新的碉堡心态，证券交易所的董事们在“悲惨星期二”这天，于交易大厅地下的一间密室里召开会议。拉蒙特和乔治·惠特尼本想悄悄溜进去，却被警卫拦下盘问了一番。会议的主要议题是是否要关闭市场。理查德·惠特尼认为，关门歇业会引发公众恐慌，并催生场外黑市，就像第一次世界大战爆发时那样。他还担心，这会给那些向经纪商大举发放通知贷款的银行带来混乱。1929 年期间，摩根公司此类贷款的未偿金额一度高达 1 亿美元，并以股票作为质押担保。一旦股票质押品被冻结，华尔街的银行和经纪商该如何运作？

As in 1987, the group decided to shorten Exchange hours instead. An excuse was at hand: overworked clerks were haggard from lack of sleep; shorter hours would let them catch up on paperwork. Instead of ringing at ten o’clock, the opening gong would sound at noon on Thursday, and the Exchange would be shut Friday and Saturday. Richard Whitney left a graphic impression of the basement meeting: “The office they met in was never designed for large meetings of this sort, with the result that most of the governors were compelled to stand or to sit on tables. As the meeting progressed, panic was raging overhead on the floor. . . . The feelings of those present were revealed by their habit of continually lighting cigarettes, taking a puff or two, putting them out and lighting new ones—a practice which soon made the narrow room blue with smoke.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2310851)
与 1987 年类似，交易所决定缩短营业时间。理由现成：过度劳累的职员因缺觉而疲惫不堪；缩短时间能让他们赶上文书工作。开市钟声不再于十点敲响，改为周四中午鸣锣，周五和周六休市。理查德·惠特尼对地下室会议留下了生动印象：“他们开会的那个房间本来就不是为这种大型会议设计的，结果大多数理事不得不站着或坐在桌子上。随着会议进行，恐慌在楼上的交易大厅蔓延……在场者的情绪从他们不停点烟、抽一两口就掐灭、再点新烟的习惯中显露无遗——这种做法很快让狭小的房间烟雾缭绕。”

In the weeks after Tragic Tuesday, rumor mills produced tales of clandestine lunchtime meetings in the Stock Exchange basement. One version had Lamont and Richard Whitney spying on traders through a periscope. Whitney continued to walk about with a rakish air, exuding confidence, although he later spoke of the “war atmosphere” of those days. Before emerging into public, he would exhort his associates, “Now get your smiles on boys!”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311002) As it happens, the real remedial action that was taken came not from the old Wall Street club but by a force new to financial panics—the Federal Reserve.
惨淡星期二过后的几周里，流言蜚语传遍了华尔街——据说有人在证交所地下室偷偷摸摸地召开午餐密会。其中一个版本是，Lamont 和 Richard Whitney 居然用潜望镜窥视交易员的一举一动。Whitney 依旧一副吊儿郎当的潇洒模样，信心满满地四处走动——尽管他后来提起那段日子时，形容那是一种"战争氛围"。每次公开露面之前，他都会给身边人打气："伙计们，把笑容给我挂上！" [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311002) 然而，真正力挽狂澜的救市举措，并非来自华尔街那帮老牌俱乐部，而是由一个在金融恐慌中崭露头角的新力量——美联储。

In late October, Jack, back from Europe, chaired a meeting at the Morgan Library with George Harrison, Ben Strong’s successor at the New York Fed. Son of an army officer, a graduate of Yale and Harvard Law School, Harrison was a handsome, pipe-smoking man who limped as a result of a boyhood accident. An activist in the Strong mold, Harrison lowered interest rates and pumped in billions of dollars in credit to buoy banks with heavy loans to brokers. “The Stock Exchange should stay open at all costs,” Harrison announced. “Gentlemen, I am ready to provide all the reserve funds that may be needed.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311163) Rebuked by Fed governor Roy A. Young in Washington, Harrison courageously replied that the world was “on fire” and that his actions were “done and can’t be undone.” He bought up to \$100 million in government bonds per day and made sure Wall Street banks had adequate reserves with which to deal with the emergency. In scale and sophistication, his postcrash actions made Pierpont’s in 1907 look antediluvian in comparison, for he could expand credit as needed. Harrison confirmed the principle of government responsibility in financial panics.
十月下旬，刚从欧洲回来的杰克在摩根图书馆主持了一场会议，与会者是乔治·哈里森——本·斯特朗在纽约联储的继任者。哈里森是一名军官的儿子，毕业于耶鲁大学和哈佛法学院。他相貌英俊，爱抽烟斗，因童年一次意外而跛足。作为斯特朗式的积极行动派，哈里森降低利率，注入数十亿美元信贷，以支撑那些向经纪人提供大量贷款的银行。“证券交易所必须不惜一切代价保持营业，”哈里森宣布，“先生们，我已准备好提供所有可能需要的储备资金。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311163) 在华盛顿遭到美联储理事罗伊·A·扬的斥责时，哈里森勇敢地回应称世界已“燃起大火”，他的行动“已经做了，无法挽回”。他每天买入高达 1 亿美元的政府债券，并确保华尔街银行拥有充足的储备来应对危机。 就其规模和复杂程度而言，他崩盘后的应对措施，让 1907 年皮尔庞特（Pierpont）的行动相形见绌，仿佛史前时代的产物——因为他能够根据需要扩张信贷。哈里森（Harrison）由此确立了政府在金融恐慌中的责任原则。

The days after the crash were a great time for pep talks and false bravado. The redoubtable Irving Fisher found it consoling that weak investors were shaken from the market and that stocks now rested in stronger hands. He described the postcrash market as a bargain counter for shrewd investors.[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311298) From the fastness of his Pocantico Hills estate, John D. Rockefeller issued an oracular statement: “Believing that fundamental conditions of the country are sound . . . my son and I have for some days been purchasing sound common stocks.”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311451) Rockefeller’s words were relayed to Eddie Cantor, then starring in *Whoopee* on Broadway and a victim of the collapse of the Goldman Sachs Trading Corporation. Cantor replied, “Sure, who else had any money left?”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311609)
崩盘后的日子里，充满了鼓舞士气的讲话和虚张声势的豪言。令人敬畏的欧文·费雪（Irving Fisher）觉得，弱小的投资者被甩出市场，股票因而落入更强大的手中，这让人颇感欣慰。他将崩盘后的市场形容为精明投资者的打折柜台。 [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311298) 在波坎蒂科山庄园（Pocantico Hills）的隐居之所，约翰·D·洛克菲勒（John D. Rockefeller）发布了一句神谕般的声明：“我相信国家的基本面是健康的……我和我儿子连日来一直在买入稳健的普通股。” [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311451) 洛克菲勒的话被转达给了艾迪·坎特（Eddie Cantor）——他当时正在百老汇主演《欢呼》（Whoopee），同时也是高盛交易公司（Goldman Sachs Trading Corporation）倒闭的受害者。坎特回答说：“当然啦，可除了他谁还有钱剩下呢？” [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311609)

Eddie Cantor later filed a \$100-million suit against Goldman, Sachs. This was probably less damaging to the firm’s future than his new vaudeville routine. In it, a stooge walked out on stage violently wringing a lemon. “Who are you?” Cantor asked. “I’m the margin clerk for Goldman, Sachs,” the stooge replied. So many suits were filed against Goldman, Sachs that during listless Depression days brokers with a taste for black humor would call up the firm and ask for the Litigation Department. From now on, even humor would puncture Wall Street’s pretensions. The age had come to an abrupt, calamitous end. The crash was a blow to Wall Street’s pride and its profits. As Bernard Baruch later said, “The stereotype of bankers as conservative, careful, prudent individuals was shattered in 1929.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311769)
埃迪·坎托后来起诉高盛集团，索赔一亿美元。但相比他在杂耍舞台上的新段子，这起诉讼对公司未来的打击恐怕还稍逊一筹。段子里，一个跟班走上台，恶狠狠地拧着一颗柠檬。“你是谁？”坎托问。“我是高盛集团的保证金清算员，”跟班答道。起诉高盛的案子多到在大萧条时期，那些偏好黑色幽默的经纪人会给这家公司打电话，径直要求转接诉讼部。从那时起，连笑话都开始戳穿华尔街的虚张声势。一个时代戛然而止，以灾难收场。这场崩盘击碎了华尔街的骄傲，也重创了它的利润。正如伯纳德·巴鲁克后来所言：“银行家保守、谨慎、稳妥的刻板印象，在 1929 年彻底崩塌了。” [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_048.html_filepos2311769)
