# Chapter Twenty-Six: Mavericks

IF the Wall Street of the 1950s was a closed, privileged club, the trend-setting firm and social arbiter was Morgan Stanley. It was a remarkably small place, with fewer than twenty partners, a hundred staffers, and a paltry \$3 million in capital. Nonetheless, it was the paragon of investment banking and exerted enormous influence. Its one office at 2 Wall Street, with green carpets and white walls, overlooked Trinity Church. In an elevated area called the platform—analogous to the partners’ room at Morgan Grenfell—stood a double row of mahogany rolltop desks, exact replicas of those at 23 Wall. Like a twin separated at birth, it showed common ancestry with J. P. Morgan and Company down the block.
如果说 20 世纪 50 年代的华尔街是一个封闭、特权俱乐部，那么引领潮流、制定社交规则的公司非摩根士丹利莫属。它规模极小，合伙人不到二十人，员工仅百余名，资本金区区三百万美元。尽管如此，它却是投资银行业界的典范，影响力巨大。其位于华尔街 2 号的唯一办公室，铺着绿色地毯，白色墙壁，俯瞰着三一教堂。在一个被称为“平台”的高台上——类似于摩根格伦费尔的合伙人房间——摆放着两排红木卷盖式办公桌，与华尔街 23 号的办公桌如出一辙。就像一对出生即分离的双胞胎，它显示出与街那头 J.P.摩根公司的共同血统。

Morgan Stanley boasted a matchless list of Fortune 500 clients and had tight handcuffs on many old House of Morgan stalwarts, including General Motors, U.S. Steel, Du Pont, General Electric, and Standard Oil of New Jersey. In the late 1940s, it added Mobil, Shell Oil, Standard Oil of Indiana, Bendix, H. J. Heinz, and numerous others. It represented six of the seven-sister oil companies and pumped out more bonds than any other firm. As confidants of the mighty, Morgan Stanley partners dealt mostly with chief executives and were privy to their secret long-range plans. They monopolized the stock and bond issues of client companies. Nobody tried to steal Morgan Stanley clients, which was considered bad form and fruitless to boot.
摩根士丹利曾拥有一份无与伦比的《财富》500 强客户名单，并与众多摩根财团的长期支柱企业紧密捆绑，包括通用汽车、美国钢铁、杜邦、通用电气和新泽西标准石油。20 世纪 40 年代末，它还新增了美孚、壳牌石油、印第安纳标准石油、本迪克斯、亨氏等众多公司。它代表七姊妹石油公司中的六家，发行的债券数量超过任何其他公司。作为权贵的密友，摩根士丹利的合伙人主要与首席执行官打交道，并了解他们秘密的长期计划。他们垄断了客户公司的股票和债券发行。没人敢试图挖走摩根士丹利的客户，这被认为是不合规矩且徒劳无功的。

A palpable warmth still existed among the Morgan firms, many senior people having worked together at J. P. Morgan and Company or Guaranty Trust in the 1920s and 1930s. They might be divided by the Glass-Steagall wall, but they sent a thick trail of vines snaking over the top. J. P. Morgan and Morgan Stanley encouraged their employees to fraternize and referred business to each other. Each year, they hosted an honorary dinner, assigning ten promising young people in each firm to attend; like doting parents, they pushed the children together. Morgan Stanley shared a cafeteria with J. P. Morgan and Company at 120 Wall Street. Morgan Stanley partners had personal accounts at 23 Wall and were among the few mortals to possess J. P. Morgan home mortgages.
摩根系公司之间依然存在着一种可感知的温情，许多资深员工早在 1920 至 1930 年代就曾在 J. P.摩根公司或担保信托公司共事过。尽管格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案筑起的高墙将他们分隔，但墙头攀爬的藤蔓却依然茂密相连。J. P.摩根与摩根士丹利鼓励员工相互联谊，甚至互相推荐业务。每年，两家公司都会联合举办荣誉晚宴，各自指派十名有前途的年轻人参加——就像溺爱子女的父母，刻意撮合孩子们亲近。摩根士丹利与 J. P.摩根公司共享华尔街 120 号的员工餐厅，而摩根士丹利的合伙人们不仅在华尔街 23 号拥有私人账户，更是少数持有 J. P.摩根住房按揭贷款的凡人。

Wherever possible, the two Morgan firms cooperated on business. J. P. Morgan managed Morgan Stanley’s pension fund and profit-sharing plan, while Morgan Stanley sponsored J. P. Morgan securities issues. If Morgan Stanley floated a bond, J.P. Morgan paid out the dividends. They were wedded by a special bookkeeping arrangement that dated from the Depression, when Morgan Stanley feared cyclical fluctuations in securities work and wanted to keep overhead low. Morgan Stanley had no clerical or back-office staff, and the “closing” on bond issues—the physical exchange of checks and securities—still took place at 23 Wall. At this point, however, the fraternal Morgan relationship was highly unequal. Morgan Stanley was now the uncontested leader in investment banking, while J. P. Morgan was a shabby genteel aristocrat in commercial banking, backed by a great deal of tradition, but without comparable contemporary power. As partners in their firm, Morgan Stanley people made far more money than their 23 Wall counterparts. During these community-of-interest days, Morgan Grenfell people also apprenticed at both J. P. Morgan and Morgan Stanley. Despite Glass-Steagall, it was still a happy Morgan family.
两家摩根公司在业务上尽可能合作。J.P. 摩根管理着摩根士丹利的养老基金和利润分享计划，而摩根士丹利则承销 J.P. 摩根的证券发行。如果摩根士丹利发行债券，J.P. 摩根便负责支付股息。它们通过一项源自大萧条时期的特殊记账安排紧密相连——当时摩根士丹利担心证券业务的周期性波动，希望保持较低的运营成本。摩根士丹利没有文员或后台办公室，债券发行的“交割”——即支票和证券的实际交换——仍然在华尔街 23 号进行。然而，此时这对兄弟摩根公司的关系已高度不平等。摩根士丹利已是投资银行业无可争议的领导者，而 J.P. 摩根则不过是商业银行中一位体面却寒酸的贵族，虽有深厚传统支撑，却已没有与之匹敌的当代实力。 作为公司的合伙人，摩根士丹利的员工赚的钱远超过华尔街 23 号的同行们。在那个利益共同体的时代，摩根建富的员工也分别在 J. P. 摩根和摩根士丹利进行学徒训练。尽管有《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的约束，摩根家族依然其乐融融。

Far more than J. P. Morgan and Company, Morgan Stanley shrank from political involvement and never displayed an equivalent sense of either public service or *noblesse oblige.* Harold Stanley was all business, and Harry Morgan shared his father’s distaste for politicians. As mostly an issuer of blue-chip bonds, Morgan Stanley seldom dealt with the SEC and had little need to lobby Washington on industry issues. At one point in the 1950s, Eugene Rotberg (later World Bank treasurer) and Fred Moss of the SEC visited Morgan Stanley to study “hot issues”—new stock offerings that soared and gyrated wildly after issue. The SEC visit was unprecedented, even an occasion for mirth at 2 Wall Street. The two SEC men were greeted by a man in livery—a red jacket with white bands across the chest—who escorted them to the platform. At a desk in the middle stood Perry Hall, the funny, fiery managing partner, who introduced himself by saying “My name is Perry Hall—partner, Morgan Stanley, Princeton.” Fred Moss retorted, “My name is Fred Moss—SEC, Brooklyn College. Before my name was Moss it was Moscowitz. And before that it was Morgan, but I changed it in 1933.”[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388598) Following an old House of Morgan custom, Morgan Stanley didn’t sell, trade, or distribute securities but allocated them to other firms, its partners worked far from the vulgar din of the Stock Exchange and wouldn’t stoop to sponsor new companies. It turned out that Morgan Stanley partners didn’t know what “hot issues” were.
远不止 J. P. Morgan and Company，Morgan Stanley 对政治避之不及，从未展现出相应的公共服务精神或贵族责任感。Harold Stanley 一心扑在生意上，而 Harry Morgan 则继承了他父亲对政客的厌恶。作为主要发行蓝筹债券的公司，Morgan Stanley 很少与 SEC 打交道，也几乎不需要就行业问题游说华盛顿。20 世纪 50 年代某次，Eugene Rotberg（后来的世界银行财务主管）和 SEC 的 Fred Moss 到访 Morgan Stanley 研究"热门股"——那些发行后价格暴涨暴跌的新股。SEC 的来访史无前例，甚至在华尔街 2 号成了一件趣事。身着制服——红色外套胸前饰有白色条纹——的侍者迎接了这两位 SEC 人员，并将他们引至平台。站在中间办公桌前的是风趣而火爆的管理合伙人 Perry Hall，他自我介绍道："我是 Perry Hall——Morgan Stanley 合伙人，普林斯顿毕业。"弗莱德·莫斯反驳道：“我叫弗莱德·莫斯——证券交易委员会，布鲁克林学院出身。在姓莫斯之前，我姓莫斯科维茨。再往前还姓过摩根，不过 1933 年我改掉了。”遵循老摩根家族的传统，摩根士丹利不销售、交易或分销证券，而是将承销的证券分配给其他公司，其合伙人远离证券交易所的喧嚣嘈杂，不屑于为新公司做保荐。结果，摩根士丹利的合伙人压根不知道什么叫“热门新股”。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388598)

In the Truman years, there was still a lingering New Deal suspicion of Wall Street which culminated in one last cannonade against the Morgan interests. In October 1947, the Justice Department filed suit against seventeen investment banks and their trade group, the Investment Bankers Association, charging them with conspiracy to monopolize underwriting in violation of antitrust laws. The suit, *U.S. v. Henry S. Morgan et ah*, designated Morgan Stanley the leader of the plot and Harold Stanley its devious mastermind. Now in his sixties, the very proper Stanley—nobody’s idea of a conspirator—gruffly dismissed the case as “utter nonsense.” He thought the instigator of the suit was Cleveland financier Cyrus Eaton, the head of Otis and Company, who had tried to make a financial comeback after the collapse of his investment trust in the 1929 crash. In a thinly veiled reference to Eaton, Stanley said that “someone, for whatever reasons, has misled the Department of Justice.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388756)
在杜鲁门执政时期，新政时代对华尔街的怀疑余波未散，最终以对摩根财团的一记重拳收场。1947 年 10 月，司法部对十七家投资银行及其行业组织“投资银行家协会”提起诉讼，指控他们合谋垄断承销业务，违反反垄断法。这起名为“美国政府诉亨利·S·摩根等人”的案件，将摩根士丹利定为阴谋的核心，而哈罗德·斯坦利则被指为幕后黑手。时年六十多岁的斯坦利——一个极其正派、绝无阴谋家气质的人——对此案嗤之以鼻，称其“纯属胡扯”。他认为这场诉讼的始作俑者是克利夫兰金融家、奥蒂斯公司掌门人赛勒斯·伊顿。伊顿在 1929 年大崩溃中投资信托破产后，一直试图东山再起。斯坦利含沙射影地提到伊顿时说：“某些人，不知出于何种原因，误导了司法部。”

Dubbed the Club of Seventeen, these swank gangsters handled 70 percent of Wall Street underwritings. The rich procession of suspects included Kuhn, Loeb; Goldman, Sachs; Lehman Brothers; First Boston; Smith, Barney; Kidder Peabody; Dillon, Read; and Drexel and Company. Such firms as Lazard Frères, Merrill Lynch, and Salomon Brothers (which sympathized with the government’s case), weren’t yet influential enough to be suspected of gross criminality. Some, however, were secretly heartsick at being excluded from this band of class martyrs. As defense lawyer Arthur Dean of Sullivan and Cromwell said of those snubbed by the government, “It made them feel like second-class citizens.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388924)
被称为“十七人帮”的这群阔绰黑帮，掌控了华尔街 70%的承销业务。涉案豪杰名单之长，包括 Kuhn, Loeb；Goldman, Sachs；Lehman Brothers；First Boston；Smith, Barney；Kidder Peabody；Dillon, Read；以及 Drexel and Company。而 Lazard Frères、Merrill Lynch 和 Salomon Brothers（这些公司对政府立场表示同情）当时尚不够分量，未被怀疑犯下重大罪行。不过，其中有些公司却因被排除在这群“阶级烈士”之外而暗自神伤。正如 Sullivan and Cromwell 的辩护律师 Arthur Dean 谈及那些被政府冷落的公司时所说：“这让它们感觉自己像是二等公民。” [3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2388924)

The suit extended charges raised in the late 1930s by the Temporary National Economic Committee. The chief instigators were vocal Morgan critics who had then advocated competitive bidding for railroads and public utility issues—Cyrus Eaton; maverick railroad man Robert Young, chairman of the Alleghany and C&O railroads, who had ambushed Harold Stanley by demanding competitive bids at a C&O board meeting in 1938; and Harold Stuart of Halsey, Stuart and Company, formerly banker to utility mogul Samuel Insull. Although larger than some Club of Seventeen firms, Halsey, Stuart and Eaton’s Otis and Company were excluded from the suit, confirming suspicions that those firms had provoked it. Toward the end of World War II, Stuart and Eaton held dozens of briefings with the Justice Department. Their efforts got a fillip when Truman became president: Truman, a disciple of Brandeis, favored compulsory bidding for securities to drive a wedge between companies and their customary bankers.
该诉讼扩展了 20 世纪 30 年代末临时国家经济委员会提出的指控。主要煽动者是几位对摩根高声批评的人士，他们当时主张对铁路和公用事业债券采取竞标方式——其中包括赛勒斯·伊顿；特立独行的铁路大亨罗伯特·杨，他是阿勒格尼和切萨皮克与俄亥俄铁路公司的董事长，曾在 1938 年 C&O 董事会上突然要求竞标，打了哈罗德·斯坦利一个措手不及；还有哈罗德·斯图尔特，来自 Halsey, Stuart and Company，曾是公用事业大亨塞缪尔·英萨尔的银行家。尽管规模比“十七人俱乐部”的一些公司更大，但 Halsey, Stuart 和伊顿的 Otis and Company 却被排除在诉讼之外，这证实了外界对其挑唆诉讼的怀疑。在二战接近尾声时，斯图尔特和伊顿与司法部进行了数十次简报会。他们的努力在杜鲁门总统上任后得到了推动：杜鲁门是布兰代斯的追随者，支持强制竞标证券，以此打破公司与传统银行之间的关系。

When the Justice Department first lodged its suit, in 1947, some pundits saw an attempt by Truman to resurrect FDR’s crusade against the “money changers.” If so, Truman quickly lost interest, for there was no longer a public clamor to slay the bankers, who now looked more like dwarfs in giants’ robes. The suit came at a time of meager earnings, and the Money Trust had never looked less forbidding. The New Deal had chased the real financial giants—the old House of Morgan, National City, and Chase—out of the securities business. Ten members of the Club of Seventeen couldn’t even muster \$5 million in capital. If one added up the combined capital of Morgan Stanley and the next seven investment banks, together they were only a third the size of the Chase and National City securities affiliates of 1929. Investment banks were populated by genteel, graying men in their fifties and sixties; younger men still shied away from a stodgy Wall Street that had never fully recuperated from the damage of 1929.
1947 年司法部首次提起诉讼时，一些权威人士认为这是杜鲁门试图重拾罗斯福对"金钱兑换商"的讨伐。若果真如此，杜鲁门很快就失去了兴趣，因为公众已不再高呼要打倒银行家——此刻他们更像是穿着巨人袍的侏儒。这场诉讼正值银行业收益微薄之际，金钱信托从未显得如此温和。新政已将这些真正的金融巨头——老摩根家族、国民城市银行、大通银行——逐出了证券业务领域。十七人俱乐部的十名成员甚至凑不出 500 万美元资本。若将摩根士丹利与其余七家顶级投资银行的资本总和相加，也仅为 1929 年大通银行和国民城市银行证券附属公司总规模的三分之一。 投资银行里多是温文尔雅、头发花白的五六十岁男性；年轻人们依然对沉闷的华尔街敬而远之，毕竟华尔街从未真正从 1929 年的重创中恢复过来。

The case was assigned to Judge Harold Medina, who would monopolize it like a stand-up comic working a nightclub audience. With clipped mustache and glasses, bow tie and furrowed brow, the cigar-smoking Medina sat through the interminable trial like a frazzled Groucho Marx, murdering the self-confidence of the prosecution. Appointed to the bench by Truman in 1947, Medina had sacrificed a lucrative law practice. He specialized in real “stinkers,” as he called them—long, tough, complex cases. After presiding over a stormy trial of eleven Communist party officials charged with conspiracy to overthrow the government, he won the nickname of the Patient Judge. But his patience flagged during the juryless trial against the Club of Seventeen. As the case dragged on for more than six years, producing a thirty-two-thousand-page transcript, Medina turned it into a comic purgatory, from which he delivered occasional howls of pain.
这个案子被分配给了哈罗德·梅迪纳法官，他像一位掌控夜店观众的单口喜剧演员一样，牢牢把控着审判过程。修剪整齐的胡须、眼镜、蝶形领结、紧锁的眉头，这位抽着雪茄的梅迪纳法官，像个疲惫不堪的格劳乔·马克斯，坐在漫长的庭审中，消磨着检方的自信。1947 年被杜鲁门任命为法官前，梅迪纳放弃了一份收入丰厚的律师执业。他专攻那些他称之为“烫手山芋”——冗长、棘手、复杂的案件。在主持了一场对十一名被控密谋颠覆政府的共产党官员的激烈审判后，他赢得了“耐心法官”的绰号。但在对“十七人俱乐部”的无陪审团审判中，他的耐心渐渐流失。随着案件拖延超过六年，产生了三万两千页的庭审记录，梅迪纳将其变成了一座喜剧炼狱，偶尔从中发出痛苦的嚎叫。

The trial itself began on November 28, 1950. The government’s case was fine sociology but inept prosecution. It mistook a club for a conspiracy and a highly ritualized form of competition for oligopoly. Prosecutors got the externals of investment banking right, showing a white-glove world governed by gentleman’s agreements, back scratching, and tacit understandings—the Gentleman Banker’s Code. These practices were unquestionably clubby and unfair and worked to exclude outsiders. They just weren’t illegal.
1950 年 11 月 28 日，审判正式开始。政府提出的案情在社会学层面颇为精彩，但在起诉环节却显得笨拙不堪。它把一家俱乐部误当成阴谋集团，把一种高度仪式化的竞争形式错判为寡头垄断。检察官们倒是看对了投资银行业的外部表象，呈现出一个由绅士协议、互相吹捧和心照不宣的默契所主宰的“白手套”世界——也就是所谓的“绅士银行家守则”（Gentleman Banker’s Code）。这些做法无疑是圈子式的、不公平的，而且确实将局外人排斥在外。只不过，它们并不违法。

The case hinged on something called the triple concept. This said that blue-chip companies possessed “traditional bankers,” who retained exclusive rights to manage their issues. When these bankers formed syndicates to float a company’s securities, the rules of the game required that they assign the same “historical position” to participating firms—that is, the same allotment as on previous issues. Finally, by the rule of “reciprocity,” investment banks would swap places in each other’s syndicates. The triple concept captured the collusive form but missed the cutthroat spirit of Wall Street. The rules didn’t civilize the sharks but kept them from devouring each other in vicious feeding frenzies. Any firm would happily steal away another’s client—*if they could*—but most of the territory was pretty well carved up. Even Morgan Stanley never chased department-store business, which was locked up by Jewish houses.
此案的核心在于所谓的“三重概念”。该概念指出，蓝筹股公司拥有专属的“传统银行家”，他们独家享有管理这些公司证券发行的权利。当这些银行家组建承销团来推销公司证券时，游戏规则要求他们赋予参与承销的同行相同的“历史头寸”——即与上次发行相同的配售份额。最后，根据“互惠原则”，投资银行会在彼此的承销团中互换位置。三重概念捕捉了华尔街的合谋形态，却忽略了其残酷竞争的本质。这些规则并未让鲨鱼变得文明，只是防止它们在凶残的抢食中互相吞噬。任何公司都乐意挖走别人的客户——只要他们能做到——但大部分领地早已被瓜分殆尽。就连摩根士丹利也从未涉足被犹太家族企业牢牢掌控的百货商店业务。

At first, the government traced the conspiracy to Morgan’s \$500-million Anglo-French loan of 1915. While this added a little wartime drama, it also introduced a problem: how did the conspiracy survive Glass-Steagall and the breakup of so many banks? To solve this, the government devised the notion of “successor” firms—that is, J. P. Morgan had metamorphosed into Morgan Stanley, Guaranty Trust into Smith, Barney, and so on. Although Harold Stanley dismissed this as “farfetched” and “silly,” it had a rough plausibility. Old-timers still called First Boston “First of Boston,” which was an echo of its derivation from the First National Bank of Boston. To keep the trial’s length manageable, Medina cut off the successor issue. So the government revised the conspiracy to date from Jack Morgan’s 1933 statement before Ferdinand Pecora. Why Jack would have broadcast the new conspiracy to a nationwide audience before a hostile investigating committee wasn’t clear.
起初，政府将这场阴谋追溯到 1915 年摩根公司价值 5 亿美元的英法贷款。虽然这为战时增添了些许戏剧性，却也引出个问题：历经《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》和众多银行分拆后，这场阴谋何以能存续？为解此谜，政府搬出了“继承者”企业的概念——也就是说，J.P.摩根蜕变为摩根士丹利，担保信托演变成史密斯·巴尼，诸如此类。尽管哈罗德·斯坦利斥之为“牵强附会”和“愚蠢至极”，但这套说辞大致还算说得通。老辈人仍把第一波士顿称作“波士顿第一”，这恰恰是它脱胎于波士顿第一国民银行的印记。为控制庭审时长，梅迪纳法官叫停了继承者问题的纠缠。于是政府便将阴谋的起始点改为杰克·摩根 1933 年在费迪南德·佩科拉面前的证词。至于杰克为何要在充满敌意的调查委员会面前，向全国听众广播这场新阴谋，就不得而知了。

Swamped with thousands of documents, Medina ordered an intricate, custom-made cabinet to manage the flow of paper. To learn more about underwriting, he followed a syndicate put together for a Con Edison issue at Halsey, Stuart’s Wall Street office. Yet the trial nearly brought him to a nervous collapse, a strain relieved only by doomsday humor. Bemoaning the suit’s slowness, he said, “I guess I was never supposed to have been a judge.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389110) At one point, he counted six children born to lawyers during the trial. When a government attorney suggested a recess, his face brightened. “It’s wonderful to see that little glimpse of paradise,” he said.[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389258) Coming back from one summer recess, he said bluntly that he “hated to get back to the trial.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389402) At another point, the tension became so great that he leaned across the bench and whispered to the opposing lawyers, “How about a ball game?”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389545) They recessed to attend a Dodgers-Giants baseball game. When it came to gallows humor, Medina vied with Morgan Stanley’s lawyer, Ralph M. Carson of Davis, Polk, who described the proceedings as an “endless sandy waste” and “a Sahara of words.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389684)
面对堆积如山的文件，麦迪纳定制了一个精巧的专用柜子来管理纸张流转。为了深入了解承销业务，他跟随黑尔西·斯图尔特华尔街办公室为联合爱迪生公司债券组建的承销团学习。然而，这场审判几乎让他精神崩溃，唯有靠末日幽默来缓解压力。他抱怨案件进展缓慢，说：“看来我本来就不该当法官。” [4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389110) 有一次，他数了数，审判期间共有六名律师的孩子出生。当政府律师提议休庭时，他脸上露出了光彩。“看到那一丝天堂的曙光，真是太棒了，”他说。 [5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389258) 从暑假休庭回来时，他直言不讳地表示“讨厌回到审判中”。 [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389402) 还有一次，紧张气氛达到顶点，他俯身越过法官席，对双方律师低语：“去看场球赛怎么样？” [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389545) 于是他们休庭，去看了场道奇队对巨人队的棒球赛。 说到黑色幽默，麦迪纳不甘示弱，与摩根士丹利的律师拉尔夫·M·卡森（来自 Davis Polk 律师事务所）一较高下，后者将这场诉讼形容为“一片无边无际的荒芜沙地”和“语言的撒哈拉沙漠”。” [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389684)

As a legal duel, the trial was highly uneven—three or four government prosecutors lined up against thirty-five of the highest-priced attorneys in New York. The courtroom crackled with sophisticated repartee. Terrified of losing, Morgan Stanley thought the suit was too important to be left only to lawyers. Young associates dredged up soot-blackened syndicate records from 23 Wall’s basement, and Perry Hall proofread the trial transcript daily. The partners only reluctantly opened their files to competitors and spent a lot of time studying other firms’ documents. As letters and memos were made public, clients were also examining them in what turned into a great game of rampant voyeurism. Some at Morgan Stanley thought that Con Edison was never again as close to the firm after certain documents were made public.
作为一场法律对决，这场审判极不公平——三、四名政府检察官对阵纽约最昂贵的三十五名律师。法庭上充斥着精妙的唇枪舌剑。因害怕败诉，摩根士丹利认为这桩官司太过重要，不能仅交由律师处理。年轻的助理们从华尔街 23 号的地下室翻出被煤烟熏黑的辛迪加记录，佩里·霍尔每天核对庭审笔录。合伙人极不情愿地向竞争对手开放文件，并花大量时间研究其他律所的材料。随着信件和备忘录被公之于众，客户们也参与其中，将其演变成一场肆无忌惮的窥探游戏。某些文件公开后，摩根士丹利内部有人认为，联合爱迪生公司与该行的关系再也回不到从前了。

As managing partner until 1951, Harold Stanley was most directly involved. Unlike the feisty, red-blooded Perry Hall, Stanley was austere and remote and, to young associates, seemed older than God. He was so aloof from everyday affairs that at one syndicate meeting at 2 Wall, he was asked for his name by a young Morgan clerk. When he said, “Harold Stanley,” the young man replied, “And the name of your firm?”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389826) He was prepped for the trial by two young assistants, Alexander Tomlinson and Sheppard Poor. One day, Poor was waiting for a cab when Stanley appeared on the same corner, and the assistant graciously yielded to the older man. As Poor held the door open for him, Stanley said, “Thank you, Tomlinson.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389992) Clerks were indistinguishable. But Stanley’s depositions proved a major factor in the trial.
作为管理合伙人直至 1951 年，哈罗德·斯坦利是直接参与其中的人物。与急性子、血气方刚的佩里·霍尔不同，斯坦利为人严肃、疏离，在年轻同事眼中，他简直比上帝还老。他对日常事务如此漠不关心，以至于有一次在华尔街 2 号的一场银团会议上，一位年轻的摩根职员竟然问他的名字。当他说“哈罗德·斯坦利”时，那年轻人又追问：“您公司的名字呢？” [9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389826) 他由两位年轻助手——亚历山大·汤姆林森和谢泼德·普尔——协助准备庭审。一天，普尔正在等出租车，斯坦利也出现在同一街角，这位助手礼貌地把车让给了年长者。普尔为他打开车门时，斯坦利说：“谢谢你，汤姆林森。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2389992) 职员们对他来说都差不多。不过，斯坦利的证词在庭审中起了关键作用。

At first, Medina was impressed by the plethora of government documents. Yet in scanning charts of the Club of Seventeen’s performance, he noticed that while Morgan Stanley always stood at or near the top, telltale shifts occurred down below. First Boston zoomed up from number-ten underwriter during World War II to second place behind Morgan Stanley by the time of the trial. If the defendants were united by a deep, dark pact, why these striking shifts? Medina was also struck by the fact that no Morgan Stanley letter or memo even vaguely referred to the conspiracy. What sort of conspiracy lasted for decades but left no fingerprints? Without a documented agreement, Medina refused to apply the antitrust provisions of the Sherman Act.
起初，大量政府文件给梅迪纳留下了深刻印象。然而，在查看"十七人俱乐部"的业绩图表时，他注意到摩根士丹利始终位居榜首或接近榜首，但排名靠后的位置却出现了明显变化。第一波士顿从二战时期的第十位承销商，到案件审理时已跃升至仅次于摩根士丹利的第二名。如果这些被告真是被一个深藏不露的黑暗联盟所维系，这些显著变动又该作何解释？梅迪纳还注意到一个事实：摩根士丹利的任何信件或备忘录中，甚至没有隐约提及这种共谋。什么样的共谋能持续数十年却不留痕迹？由于缺乏书面协议，梅迪纳拒绝适用《谢尔曼法》的反垄断条款。

By the time Medina published his landmark 212-page opinion in February 1954, he believed he was chasing a phantom conspiracy constructed from flimsy circumstantial evidence. Where the government saw collusion, Medina saw “a constantly changing panorama of competition among the seventeen defendant firms.”[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390118) He noted that when companies switched bankers, the winning firm gladly accepted the new client—a violation according to the rules of a conspiracy. Firms didn’t hustle Morgan Stanley’s august clients, he said, because “there was no point in running around, wasting one’s time, in a patently futile attempt to get business, where a competitor was on good terms with an issuer and doing a good job.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390287)
到 1954 年 2 月，当梅迪纳发表他那份具有里程碑意义的 212 页意见书时，他相信自己正在追踪一个由薄弱间接证据拼凑而成的虚幻阴谋。在政府眼中是勾结，而梅迪纳看到的却是“十七家被告公司之间一幅不断变化的竞争图景。” [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390118) 他指出，当企业更换银行时，获胜的银行会欣然接受新客户——这根据阴谋的规则反而构成了违规。他说，这些公司并非不愿争取摩根士丹利的尊贵客户，而是因为“明知竞争对手与发债方关系良好且表现出色，还四处奔波、浪费时间去做徒劳无功的生意尝试，毫无意义。” [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390287)

Medina’s opinion was a paean to Morgan Stanley and probably the best advertising the firm ever got. He was amused by its policy of appearing alone atop syndicate mastheads or not at all, which reminded him of Hollywood starlets fussing over their marquee billing. He was enormously impressed with Harold Stanley. He praised Stanley’s “absolute integrity” and said Morgan Stanley’s entire history would have been different without him. Then he added, “The fact that Stanley denied the existence of any such conspiracy as charged . . . is one of the significant facts of the case.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390451) This was a very peculiar statement: Medina was saying that a defendant’s mere assertion of his own innocence was somehow proof of that innocence.
梅迪纳的评论是对摩根士丹利的赞歌，或许也是该公司有史以来最好的广告。他对摩根士丹利坚持要么独自出现在银团报头顶端、要么根本不出现的规定感到好笑，这让他想起好莱坞小明星们为自己在海报上的署名顺序而斤斤计较的样子。他对哈罗德·斯坦利印象极深，称赞其"绝对正直"，并说如果没有他，摩根士丹利的整个历史都会改写。接着他又补充道："斯坦利否认存在任何所指控的阴谋……这一事实本身就是本案的重要事实之一。" [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390451) 这番话非常奇特：梅迪纳的意思是，被告仅仅声明自己无罪，就莫名成了无罪的证据。

The Medina trial would soon seem an almost nostalgic glance at a rapidly fading Wall Street. “Banker domination” wouldn’t be the problem of the Casino Age, and even dedicated trustbusters at the Justice Department thought the suit about fifteen years too late. The cozy banker-company ties would finally end, not through judicial ruling or executive fiat but by structural changes in the marketplace. Over the next generation, the entire system that the Justice Department exposed would be rudely torn apart, and the firm most directly threatened would be the one with the most loyal clients to lose—Morgan Stanley.
梅迪纳案的审判很快就成为对迅速衰落的华尔街的一种近乎怀旧的回望。“银行家主导”不再是赌场时代的问题，就连司法部里最坚定的反垄断斗士也认为这起诉讼大约晚了十五年。银行与企业之间那种亲密的纽带最终并非通过司法裁决或行政命令被终结，而是被市场结构的变化所摧毁。在接下来的二三十年里，司法部所揭露的整个体系将被粗暴地撕碎，而最直接受到威胁的公司，正是那个拥有最忠实客户、损失也最大的一家——摩根士丹利。

IN the last stages of a trial conducted by depositions, Judge Medina yearned to question a live witness—someone he could “look in the eye,” as he said eagerly. The government obliged with Robert Young, chairman of the Chesapeake and Ohio Railroad and certifiably America’s most rabid Morgan-hater. He was the man who smarted after being rebuked by Tom Lamont for his testimony at the Wheeler railroad hearings in the late 1930s. Touted by the press as the Justice Department’s “anti-Morgan machine gun,” he so ardently supported the suit that Davis, Polk’s Ralph Carson suggested it be renamed *Young v. Morgan.*[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390586) Sounding his favorite theme of Morgan and Kuhn, Loeb domination of the railroads, Young fired broadsides from the witness stand until Medina glared at him. “This is a courtroom and there will be no appealing here to the public over the head of the judge,” Medina snapped.[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390736) He criticized Young’s “hell raising propensities” and mocked the notion that any banker could control Robert Young.[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390881) When Young stepped down from the stand, he extended his hand to Medina, who just gave it a withering look.
在依据证词录进行的庭审最后阶段，梅迪纳法官渴望亲自询问一位活生生的证人——一个他能“直视其眼睛”的人，他急切地这样说道。政府方面满足了他的要求，提供了切萨皮克与俄亥俄铁路公司董事长罗伯特·杨——这位确凿无疑的全美头号摩根仇恨者。此人因在 1930 年代末惠勒铁路听证会上作证时遭汤姆·拉蒙特斥责而一直耿耿于怀。媒体称他为司法部的“反摩根机关枪”，他对这场诉讼的支持如此狂热，以至于戴维斯·波克律师事务所的拉尔夫·卡森建议将此案更名为“杨诉摩根案”。在证席上，杨大谈摩根与库恩·洛布公司控制铁路这一他最热衷的话题，连珠炮般猛攻不休，直到梅迪纳法官怒视着他。“这是法庭，不允许越级向公众呼吁而藐视法官，”梅迪纳厉声说道。 [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390736) 他批评杨的“惹是生非的本性”，并嘲讽了任何银行家都能控制罗伯特·杨的说法。 [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2390881) 当杨从证人席上走下来时，他向梅迪纳伸出了手，而梅迪纳只是轻蔑地瞥了一眼。

A dapper, pint-sized Texan, Young could seem boyish, with his bulbous nose, pink cheeks, and dimples. Then his face would tighten, his blue eyes would blaze, and he would stare with icy fury. His lifelong Morgan obsession bespoke secret envy. He told Medina that as a young man, he felt “in banking all roads led to Rome and to me the Corner was Rome.”[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391027) He rose through the Morgan universe, first as a worker at a Du Pont plant during World War I, then as a General Motors assistant treasurer in the 1920s. Before the 1929 crash, he advised Pierre du Pont to switch from stocks to bonds and won a following as an investment adviser among rich executives. And in 1937, Young and his sidekick, Allen P. Kirby, had bought control of the bankrupt Alleghany empire, still heavily indebted to J. P. Morgan and Guaranty Trust. The House of Morgan always suspected that he espoused competitive bidding to camouflage the fact that, by controlling six railroads, he was a monopolist himself.
一位衣着考究、个子矮小的得克萨斯人，杨看上去有些孩子气，长着圆鼻头、粉红脸颊和酒窝。但转眼间，他的脸就会绷紧，蓝眼睛闪闪发光，冷冰冰地怒视着。他一生对摩根的痴迷，暴露了内心的隐秘嫉妒。他告诉梅迪纳，年轻时他觉得“在银行业，条条大路通罗马，而对我来说，街角就是罗马。”他在摩根的体系中步步高升，先是在一战期间进杜邦工厂当工人，20 世纪 20 年代又成了通用汽车的助理财务主管。1929 年股市崩盘前，他建议皮埃尔·杜邦从股票转向债券，因此在高管中赢得了投资顾问的追随者。1937 年，杨和他的副手艾伦·P·柯比买下了破产的阿勒格尼帝国的控股权，而这家公司仍欠着 J.P.摩根和担保信托银行巨额债务。摩根家族一直怀疑，他鼓吹竞标制，不过是为了掩盖自己控制六条铁路、本身也是个垄断者的事实。

Robert Young was a prototypical man of the new age, a publicity monger adept at courting public opinion. In the early 1950s, he seemed to grin from every magazine cover, deriding the sleeping cars he called rolling tenements and blaming “Wall Street banker control” for decaying railroads. In one celebrated ad, he showed a happy hog riding in style in a cross-country cattle car, the caption read, “A HOG CAN CROSS THE U.S. WITHOUT CHANGING TRAINS—BUT YOU CANT.’[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391179) He even had a magazine moniker dreamed up by his publicists—the Daring Young Man of Wall Street. This exponent of people’s capitalism lived like a mogul, buying a forty-room Tudor mansion in Newport from a Drexel family member. He had a cream-colored Spanish villa in Palm Beach and a sumptuous apartment at Manhattan’s Waldorf Towers.
罗伯特·杨是新时代的典型人物，一个善于操控舆论的炒作高手。20 世纪 50 年代初，他的笑脸几乎霸占了每家杂志封面，他嘲笑卧铺列车是“移动的贫民窟”，将铁路衰败归咎于“华尔街银行家的控制”。在一则著名广告中，他展示了一头肥猪在横跨全国的牲口车厢里惬意旅行，配文写道：“猪能不用换车横跨美国——但你不能。” [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391179) 他的公关团队甚至为他量身打造了一个杂志绰号——“华尔街的狂飙青年”。这位人民资本主义的代言人，生活却堪比大亨：从德雷克塞尔家族手中买下纽波特一栋四十个房间的都铎式庄园，在棕榈滩拥有一座奶油色的西班牙别墅，还在曼哈顿的华道夫塔楼置办了一套奢华公寓。

For a man of such ambition, the giant C&.0—a dusty, coal-carrying railroad—lacked suitable cachet. Instead, he craved the glamorous New York Central, America’s second-biggest railroad, which ran sleek passenger trains, such as the Twentieth Century Limited from Chicago. For a century, it was known as the Vanderbilt road or the Morgan road. It still boasted two authentic Vanderbilts on its board, plus George Whitney and five other Wall Street bankers. For a Texas insurgent like Young, the New York Central epitomized the eastern financial establishment. It was the final inner sanctum that he longed to enter. By 1947, Young, with four hundred thousand shares of the railroad, was its largest stockholder. But feeling threatened, the board refused to grant him more than two seats, and even these were then withheld by the Interstate Commerce Commission on antitrust grounds.
对于像杨这样雄心勃勃的人来说，那条尘土飞扬、专运煤炭的 C&0 铁路实在配不上他的身份。他真正渴望的是美国第二大铁路——光彩夺目的纽约中央铁路，这条铁路运营着诸如"二十世纪快线"等豪华客运列车，从芝加哥出发。一个世纪以来，它被称为范德比尔特铁路或摩根铁路。其董事会中至今仍有两名范德比尔特家族成员，外加乔治·惠特尼和其他五位华尔街银行家。对于像杨这样的德克萨斯反叛者来说，纽约中央铁路就是东部金融权势集团的象征。这是他渴望进入的最后圣殿。到 1947 年，杨持有该铁路公司四十万股，成为其最大股东。但董事会感到威胁，拒绝给他超过两个董事席位，而这两个席位随后也被州际商业委员会以反垄断为由否决了。

By late 1953, Young and his troops amassed one million shares of New York Central stock, or nearly 20 percent of the total. Ordinarily this would translate into control, but the railroad wouldn’t submit gracefully to its fate. In February 1954, its blue-ribbon board met at the University Club and adamantly refused to put Young on the board or make him chairman, as he demanded. It was a pompous, hidebound reaction of people who were clinging to outdated prerogatives. Perhaps to forestall charges of Vanderbilt-Morgan control, one Vanderbilt and George Whitney skipped the critical meeting. A humiliated, vengeful Young launched a proxy battle that would turn into the decade’s most bellicose corporate skirmish, prefiguring takeover wars of a generation later. To avoid antitrust problems, he resigned from the C&O board and sold its New York Central stake to a friend, Cleveland financier Cyrus Eaton. Now he could storm the Central.
到 1953 年底，杨和他的团队已积攒了 100 万股纽约中央铁路（New York Central）的股票，约占总股本的 20%。通常情况下，这足以掌握控制权，但这家铁路公司并不打算乖乖认命。1954 年 2 月，其精英董事会成员在大学俱乐部（University Club）开会，坚决拒绝将杨纳入董事会，也拒绝按他的要求任命他为董事长。这是那些固守过时特权者傲慢、僵化的反应。或许是为了避免被人指责为范德比尔特-摩根（Vanderbilt-Morgan）控制，一位范德比尔特家族成员和乔治·惠特尼（George Whitney）缺席了那次关键会议。受辱而复仇心切的杨发起了一场代理权争夺战，这场战役成为该十年中最具火药味的企业硝烟，预示了二三十年后的收购大战。为避免反垄断问题，他辞去了 C&O 董事会职务，并将其持有的纽约中央铁路股票卖给了一位朋友——克利夫兰金融家赛勒斯·伊顿（Cyrus Eaton）。如今，他终于可以大举进攻中央铁路了。

Though Young mouthed old Money Trust cliches, the financial landscape had changed markedly. Family ownership was a disappearing force in the American economy. Where William Vanderbilt had once inherited 87 percent of the New York Central from Commodore Vanderbilt and hired Pierpont Morgan to disperse the shares, his descendant, Harold Vanderbilt, now owned less than 1 percent of outstanding shares. The “banker-dominated” board held less than 2 percent of all shares. After Glass-Steagall, Morgans, Chase, National City, and the rest couldn’t hold large equity stakes in companies, further eroding their influence. So the glue that compressed companies, banks, and rich families into a coherent financial class was coming unstuck. Meanwhile, New York Central stock was dispersed among forty thousand small shareholders, whom Young called Aunt Janes and assiduously courted. However much he railed against the “interests,” Young knew that financial power was becoming more pluralistic in the new age. The real threat to the House of Morgan would come, not from Washington, but from new financial powers beyond the control of the old eastern elite. The short Texas raider was a portent of later raiders and mavericks, many from the old populist strongholds of the South and West, who would take pleasure in taunting the Wall Street establishment.
扬格虽然嘴上挂着老派"货币托拉斯"的陈词滥调，但金融格局早已今非昔比。家族所有制在美国经济中正日渐式微。当年威廉·范德比尔特从"准将"范德比尔特手中继承了纽约中央铁路 87%的股份，又请来皮尔庞特·摩根帮忙分散股权；而如今他的后代哈罗德·范德比尔特，持有的流通股还不到 1%。那个"银行家主导"的董事会，持股比例也不到 2%。《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》之后，摩根、大通、国民城市银行等机构不能再持有企业的大额股权，影响力进一步被削弱。曾经把公司、银行和豪门家族紧紧黏合成一个金融阶层的纽带，正在瓦解。与此同时，纽约中央铁路的股票分散在四万名小股东手里——扬格管这些人都叫"简阿姨"，还殷勤地拉拢他们。 无论他如何痛斥那些“利益集团”，杨都知道金融力量在新的时代正变得更加多元化。对 House of Morgan 的真正威胁并非来自华盛顿，而是来自那些旧东部精英无法控制的新兴金融势力。那位矮小的德克萨斯突袭者预示着后来那些突袭者和特立独行者——其中许多来自南方和西部那些旧民粹主义根据地——他们以嘲弄华尔街建制派为乐。

A proxy fight, an attempt to elect a dissident slate of directors, was the favorite takeover device of the 1950s. It stacked the cards in favor of management, which could usually marshal more resources and out-gun the opposition. As a rich outsider, however, Young waged a campaign in the style of a national election, producing a flurry of press releases, newspaper ads, and even direct-mail pleas. The new age would witness many such loud, brassy, vituperative campaigns. Pierpont Morgan and Tom Lamont had waged their corporate struggles behind closed doors, dealing with like-minded bankers. In the New York Central battle, Young forced the sedate clubmen of Wall Street to fight in the open—where they felt naked and profoundly uncomfortable. Both sides spent over \$ 1 million and grew so paranoid that they swept their respective headquarters in search of hidden microphones.
代理权争夺战，即试图选举一批异议董事的做法，是 20 世纪 50 年代最受欢迎的收购手段。这种策略对管理层有利，因为他们通常能调动更多资源，压制反对派。然而，作为一位富有的局外人，杨以全国大选的方式发动了一场运动，密集发布新闻稿、刊登报纸广告，甚至进行直邮恳求。新时代将见证许多这样喧嚣、刺耳、充满攻击性的运动。皮尔庞特·摩根和汤姆·拉蒙特曾在幕后进行企业斗争，与志同道合的银行家打交道。而在纽约中央铁路的争夺中，杨迫使华尔街那些沉稳的俱乐部成员公开对战——这让他们感到暴露无遗，极度不适。双方各自花费了超过 100 万美元，且变得疑心重重，以至于彻底搜查各自总部，寻找隐藏的麦克风。

Robert Young did everything that gentlemen bankers thought undignified. He appeared on *Meet the Press* and promised to triple the railroad’s profits, evoking a vision of high-speed, futuristic train service. He hired a small army of three hundred vacuum-cleaner salesmen to telephone shareholders and even sued directors on the New York Central board, including George Whitney. Despite his own tremendous wealth and railroad empire, he managed to portray himself as a doughty little David combating the Goliath of the New York Central’s board.
罗伯特·杨做了所有正派银行家认为有失身份的事情。他现身《媒体见面会》，承诺将铁路利润翻三倍，描绘出一幅高速、未来化的铁路服务愿景。他雇佣了一支由三百名吸尘器推销员组成的小型队伍，致电股东，甚至起诉纽约中央铁路公司董事会的成员，包括乔治·惠特尼。尽管他坐拥巨额财富和铁路帝国，却成功将自己塑造成一位英勇的小大卫，对抗纽约中央铁路董事会这个巨人歌利亚。

Although it seemed tangential, Young spent much of the campaign assailing the House of Morgan. He urged companies to renounce their exclusive relations with Morgan Stanley and solicit competitive bids from other bankers. He blurred the identities of J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Stanley and lumped them together as the “Morgan crowd.” “He assumed that fighting one meant fighting both, plus Guaranty Trust and other banks,” said Clifford H. Ramsdell, then an Allegh-any vice-president.[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391325) Young revived ancient myths that a single Morgan director on a board could bully the rest, claiming the “real issue” was whether the railroad would “continue to submit to a Morgan non-ownership board with countless conflicting interests.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391504) The Brandeisian rhetoric is less notable than its application by a millionaire corporate raider in the middle of a takeover battle. The New Deal had only wanted to curb Morgan power; Robert Young wished to appropriate it.
虽然看似旁敲侧击，但杨在竞选过程中花费了大量精力攻击摩根财团。他敦促各公司放弃与摩根士丹利的独家关系，并向其他银行公开招标。他将 J.P.摩根公司、摩根士丹利混为一谈，统称为“摩根帮”。“他以为攻击其中一家就意味着同时与两家为敌，连带担保信托银行和其他金融机构，”当时担任阿勒格尼公司副总裁的克利福德·H·拉姆斯德尔回忆道。 [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391325) 杨还重提旧事，声称只要摩根的人在董事会占有一席，就能胁迫其他董事，并宣称“真正的问题”在于铁路公司是否“继续屈从于一个利益冲突无数的摩根非控股董事会”。 [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391504) 这番布兰代斯式的论调之所以引人注目，并非因为其言辞本身，而是因为一个身家百万的企业掠夺者在收购战正酣时对其加以运用。新政只是想要遏制摩根的权力；而罗伯特·杨却想将其据为己有。

There was an element of bear baiting in Young’s attacks on the Morgan interests. He must have known these proper gentlemen wouldn’t emerge from their clubs, roll up their sleeves, and resort to fisticuffs, they had no tactical repertoire for street fights, which they considered ill-bred and highly offensive. Morgan Stanley lacked any publicity apparatus and so found itself contending in a strange, alien world. “Young was beneath our respect,” said Perry Hall. “Why get into a public fight with a person like that?”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391657) In an unprecedented move, Morgan Stanley ran a large advertisement attacking Young and denouncing government-stipulated competitive bidding. However strong this seemed to Morgan Stanley partners, it was tame stuff compared with Young’s merciless guerrilla warfare.
杨对摩根集团的攻击带有几分“斗熊”的意味。他一定清楚，这些体面的绅士绝不会走出俱乐部、挽起袖子用拳头解决问题——他们根本没有街头斗殴的那套战术，在他们看来那既失体统又令人不齿。摩根士丹利没有公关机制，因此发现自己陷入了一个陌生而格格不入的战场。“杨根本不值得我们去尊重，”佩里·霍尔说道，“何必跟这种人公开对抗？” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391657) 史无前例地，摩根士丹利刊登了一则大幅广告，抨击杨并谴责政府规定的竞标制度。这则在摩根士丹利合伙人看来颇为强硬的做法，与杨无情而如游击战般的攻势相比，不过是小巫见大巫。

J. P. Morgan and Company was no less baffled in countering Young. Like Saint Sebastian, it stood still taking the arrows. The bank dispatched an emissary to Allen Kirby and asked whether Young could please stop making such nasty statements in public. “Publicity is the only effective weapon we have and we are going to use it,” Kirby replied.[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391815) In April 1954, the Morgan bank published an open letter from president Henry Clay Alexander denying Morgan control of the New York Central. Alexander noted that the bank couldn’t own stock and competed with several other banks on the railroad’s board. “You are wrong and I have a deep suspicion you know it,” Alexander lectured Young. “You think, no doubt, it is good propaganda in seeking stockholders’ votes . . . we welcome the opportunity to demonstrate once again that the theory of Morgan banker domination is a fantasy and a myth.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391976) He called Young a Little Caesar setting up straw men. This was the closest 23 Wall ever came to invective.
摩根大通公司在对抗杨时同样束手无策。它就像圣塞巴斯蒂安一样，站着不动，任凭箭矢袭来。银行派了一名特使去见艾伦·柯比，问他能否请杨停止在公开场合发表如此尖刻的言论。“公开宣传是我们唯一有效的武器，我们会好好利用它，”柯比回答道。 [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391815) 1954 年 4 月，摩根银行发表了董事长亨利·克莱·亚历山大的一封公开信，否认摩根控制了纽约中央铁路公司。亚历山大指出，银行不能持有股票，并且与铁路董事会的其他几家银行存在竞争。“你错了，而且我深表怀疑你是明知故犯，”亚历山大训诫杨说。“你无疑认为，这是在争取股东投票时很好的宣传手段……我们欢迎有机会再次证明，摩根银行家主导铁路的理论纯属幻想和神话。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2391976) 他称杨是个树立稻草人的小恺撒。这是华尔街 23 号有史以来最接近谩骂的言辞。

Two months later, Young startled Wall Street when he won the proxy battle by more than a million votes. The speculators backed Young, as did the big retail houses, such as Merrill Lynch and Bache, whose margin accounts went for Young. Lifting his hands like a boxing champ—he didn’t mind rubbing it in—Young strode into the New York Central headquarters at 230 Park Avenue and sat down beneath a portrait of Commodore Vanderbilt. When the board met in June, no Morgan banker or Vanderbilt sat on it for the first time since the nineteenth century. The Visigoths had sacked the Holy City. Young’s board included Lila Acheson Wallace of the *Reader’s Digest* and Indianapolis publisher Eugene C. Pulliam—businesspeople from beyond the Wall Street pale. Ever since the 1930s, economists had commented on the separation of management from ownership in the modern corporation. Now a corporate raider had acted on that momentous shift.
两个月后，杨以超出百万票的优势赢得代理权争夺战，震惊了整个华尔街。投机商们支持杨，美林、巴奇这样的大型零售经纪商也站在他这边，它们的保证金账户都投给了杨。他像拳击冠军那样举起双手——丝毫不介意炫耀这番胜利——大步走进位于公园大道 230 号的纽约中央铁路总部，在范德比尔特准将的肖像下坐定。六月董事会召开时，自十九世纪以来，摩根银行的银行家或范德比尔特家族成员首次没有出现在董事会中。西哥特人攻陷了圣城。杨的董事会成员包括《读者文摘》的莉拉·艾奇逊·华莱士和印第安纳波利斯出版商尤金·C·普利亚姆——这些都是华尔街圈外的商界人士。自 20 世纪 30 年代以来，经济学家们一直在谈论现代公司中所有权与管理权的分离。如今，一位企业掠夺者正是利用了这一重大转变。

On Wall Street, crestfallen financiers wondered why the Morgan houses hadn’t mounted a more spirited defense or formed an informal syndicate to keep the road in friendly hands. *Fortune* asked, almost plaintively, “Why did Morgan not use its prestige?”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392126) The answer was partly that the Morgan houses were still smarting from the New Deal controversies. As president Henry Alexander said, “We don’t try to run other people’s business and there have been so many charges in the past that we want to avoid the appearance of doing so.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392272) While Young played on old associations, J. P. Morgan power stood at its modern nadir. Young’s success had proved, paradoxically, that bankers didn’t control the railroads. The lack of a more tenacious banker defense also reflected the decaying fortunes of the roads. Morgan Stanley hadn’t handled a major public offering for the New York Central since 1936. There just wasn’t that much business at stake.
在华尔街，那些垂头丧气的金融家们百思不得其解：为何摩根家族没有发起更坚决的防御，或私下组建一个银团，让这条铁路继续掌握在友好人士手中？《财富》杂志几乎带着哀怨问道：“摩根为何没有动用它的威望？” [24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392126) 答案的一部分在于，摩根家族对“新政”时期的争议仍心有余悸。正如董事长亨利·亚历山大所言：“我们不去插手别人的生意，而且过去有太多指责，我们不想给人留下这种印象。” [25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392272) 就在杨氏利用旧日交情周旋之际，摩根财团的影响力却跌至现代史上的最低点。讽刺的是，杨氏的成功恰恰证明了银行家并未真正掌控铁路。银行家们缺乏更顽强的防守，也反映出这些铁路的日渐衰败。自 1936 年以来，摩根士丹利就再未为纽约中央铁路公司承销过大宗公开发行。毕竟，涉及的利益并没有那么大。

The Morgan houses had one last sour laugh on Robert Young. Like many hostile raiders, he was ignorant of the true state of his target. And the New York Central was bankrupt. All those slick passenger trains that had dazzled Young were losing money, and freight traffic was being siphoned off by trucks and planes. Young appointed Alfred E. Perlman as the railroad’s president, the first Jew to hold that position. When they first examined the Central’s books, Young said, “Al, aren’t you afraid?” Perlman replied, “No, but we’d better get to work.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392398)
摩根家族对罗伯特·扬发出了最后一声酸溜溜的嘲笑。和许多敌意收购者一样，他对自己目标的真实状况一无所知——纽约中央铁路早已资不抵债。那些曾让扬目眩神迷的豪华客运列车其实都在亏钱，而货运业务又被卡车和飞机不断蚕食。扬任命阿尔弗雷德·E·珀尔曼为铁路公司总裁，这是犹太人首次担任此职。当他们第一次翻看中央铁路的账簿时，扬问：“阿尔，你不害怕吗？”珀尔曼答道：“不怕，不过我们得赶紧干活了。”

During the 1957 recession, the New York Central, battered by heavy losses, opened merger talks with its historic rival, the Pennsylvania Railroad. In January 1958, the Central skipped its dividend payment, which plunged Young into a terrible state of depression. For a long time, he had struggled with deep psychological problems, veering between brisk optimism and deep melancholia. A close friend, Edward Stettinius, Jr., son of the late Morgan partner, had once found him sitting alone in his Newport library, staring absently into space while a gun lay on his desk. Perhaps after so much brave talk, his failure with the New York Central was too shameful for him to face. On January 25, 1958, he went into the billiard room of his Palm Beach mansion, the Towers, picked up a shotgun, and shot himself to death.
在 1957 年的经济衰退中，纽约中央铁路公司因遭受重创而与其历史宿敌宾夕法尼亚铁路公司开启了合并谈判。1958 年 1 月，中央铁路公司暂停了股息支付，这使杨陷入了极度抑郁的状态。长期以来，他一直与严重的心理问题作斗争，情绪在乐观开朗与深沉忧郁之间剧烈摇摆。他的密友、已故摩根合伙人之子小爱德华·斯特蒂纽斯曾发现他独自坐在纽波特的书房里，目光呆滞地望向空中，桌上放着一把枪。或许在那么多的豪言壮语之后，他在纽约中央铁路项目上的失败令他羞耻得无法面对。1958 年 1 月 25 日，他走进自己棕榈滩豪宅“塔楼”的台球室，拿起一把猎枪，饮弹自尽。

THE chummy world of Wall Street bankers and corporate executives that so enraged Robert Young reached its peak in the 1950s and began to slip. In this high noon of industrial power, before the European economies rebounded or the Pacific rim threatened, the United States dominated automobiles, steel, oil, aluminum, and other heavy industries. As investment banker to big smokestack firms, Morgan Stanley was in an enviable position. Like a caretaker of a cache of crown jewels, it didn’t need to scout out new wealth. The sole objective was to stand guard over the franchise—the superb client list inherited from the old House of Morgan. As the firm’s William Black later said, “All you needed to do in the 1950s was to execute superbly on client business.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392560)
让罗伯特·杨如此愤怒的华尔街银行家与企业高管们那种称兄道弟的亲密圈子，在 20 世纪 50 年代达到鼎盛后便开始走下坡路。在工业力量的鼎盛时期——欧洲经济尚未复苏，太平洋沿岸地区也尚未构成威胁——美国主导着汽车、钢铁、石油、铝材及其他重工业。作为大型烟囱企业的投资银行，摩根士丹利处于令人艳羡的位置。它就像王室珠宝的保管人，无需四处寻觅新财富。唯一的目标就是守护好这份家业——从老摩根家族继承来的那份顶级客户名单。正如公司后来的威廉·布莱克所言："在 50 年代，你只需要出色地完成客户的业务就行。"

In pleasing clients, a smooth golf swing or a convivial party style was the standard weapon in the investment banker’s arsenal. By modern standards, it was a very sociable, leisurely world, with two-hour lunches at the Bond Club still in fashion. The master at entertaining clients was Perry Hall, the managing partner from 1951 to 1961. Where Harold Stanley was gray and austere, Hall was pleasantly brash and garrulous, blessed with a salesman’s patter. Freckled and chunky, he had a broad face and penetrating eyes. He terrified subordinates, charmed women, and lorded it over corporate chieftains. He could sell refrigerators to Eskimos. Like Andre Meyer of Lazard Frères or Sid Weinberg of Goldman, Sachs, he was on a first-name basis with every American CEO. “He would shout at presidents and thump the table and tell them what he thought,” said one person who observed him in those years. “His relationship with all those tycoons was unique.”
在取悦客户方面，流畅的高尔夫挥杆或风趣的派对风格曾是投资银行家武器库中的标准利器。以现代标准来看，那是一个社交氛围浓厚、悠闲自在的世界，在债券俱乐部享用两小时午餐依旧时髦。1951 年至 1961 年间担任管理合伙人的佩里·霍尔，堪称款待客户的高手。哈罗德·斯坦利沉闷刻板，而霍尔却爽朗健谈，天生一副推销员的伶牙俐齿。他脸上长着雀斑，身材敦实，宽脸膛配上一双锐利的眼睛。他对下属令人生畏，对女性魅力十足，在公司高管面前颐指气使。他简直能把冰箱卖给爱斯基摩人。就像拉扎德公司的安德烈·迈耶或高盛公司的席德·温伯格一样，他与每一位美国 CEO 都直呼其名。“他会对着总裁们大吼大叫，拍着桌子说出自己的看法，”一位当年目睹此景的人说道，“他与那些大亨的关系独一无二。”

Hall had emerged from an F. Scott Fitzgerald world where Princeton eating clubs and Yale secret societies were the passports to Wall Street success. A 1917 Princeton graduate, he had sat next to Fitzgerald in many classes, due to the alphabetical proximity of their names. (Hall was unimpressed by Fitzgerald’s prose and insisted that several forgotten classmates were superior stylists.) For Hall, Ivy League sports provided his all-purpose pantheon of heroes. Whatever his partner’s business accomplishments, for example, Harold Stanley remained for him captain of the Yale baseball and hockey teams. Hall hired his own successor, Bob Baldwin, two weeks after watching him play baseball for Princeton. A varsity letter was perhaps the most eloquent letter of introduction at Morgan Stanley.
霍尔来自一个 F. Scott Fitzgerald 笔下的世界，在那里，普林斯顿的饮食俱乐部和耶鲁的秘密社团是通往华尔街成功的通行证。作为 1917 年普林斯顿的毕业生，由于姓氏首字母相近，他在许多课程中都坐在 Fitzgerald 旁边。（霍尔对 Fitzgerald 的文笔不以为然，坚称几位已被遗忘的同学在文采上更胜一筹。）对霍尔而言，常春藤联盟的体育项目是他心中英雄的万神殿。例如，无论他的合伙人取得了怎样的商业成就，Harold Stanley 在他心中始终是耶鲁棒球队和冰球队的队长。霍尔在观看了 Bob Baldwin 为普林斯顿打棒球比赛的两周后，就选他作为自己的继任者。在 Morgan Stanley，一封校队推荐信或许就是最有说服力的介绍信。

As the last managing partner from the old House of Morgan, Hall never modified his conviction that FDR was the “worst enemy the U.S. ever had.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392705) Hall had worked at the Guaranty Company, survived the 1920 bomb blast, and become a bond manager at J. P. Morgan and Company in 1925. After the 1929 crash, Jack Morgan separately summoned Hall and Charles Dickey. He asked Dickey to become a J. P. Morgan partner and Hall to become a Drexel partner in Philadelphia. Jack, it seems, had bungled his instructions to offer both young men Drexel partnerships. The error deeply wounded Hall and led to a Morgan Stanley tradition of having two people present at important announcements. In 1935, Hall moved over to the new Morgan Stanley, which he liked to regard as his personal creation. He was boastful, but invested his vanity with considerable charm. “We were the *crème de la cième,”* Hall remarked. “Everybody was jealous of us.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392863)
作为老摩根家族的最后一位管理合伙人，霍尔始终坚信 FDR 是“美国有史以来最糟糕的敌人”。 [28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392705) 霍尔曾在 Guaranty Company 工作，在 1920 年的炸弹爆炸中幸存，并于 1925 年成为 J. P. Morgan and Company 的债券经理。1929 年股市崩盘后，杰克·摩根分别召见了霍尔和查尔斯·迪基。他要求迪基成为 J. P. Morgan 的合伙人，而霍尔成为费城 Drexel 的合伙人。杰克似乎弄错了他的指示，原本是要给这两位年轻人提供 Drexel 的合伙人职位。这个错误深深伤害了霍尔，并导致摩根士丹利形成了一项传统：在重要宣布时必须有两人在场。1935 年，霍尔转投新成立的摩根士丹利，他喜欢将其视为自己的个人创举。他虽爱自夸，但虚荣心却带有相当的魅力。“我们是精英中的精英，”霍尔说道，“所有人都嫉妒我们。” [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392863)

Hall was perfectly suited to the relationship banking of the 1950s. He would entertain clients while shooting wild turkeys in South Carolina or fishing near his house in Woods Hole, Massachusetts. (At age seventy-three, he was still powerful enough to harpoon a 552-pound sword-fish.) An amateur golf and tennis champ, he attracted corporate executives who wanted to test their game. Hall would perform elite missions for clients and behaved like a member of their family. When a General Motors chairman was upset by his daughter’s plans to marry a Pakistani, Uncle Perry went to reason with the young woman. He asked whether her children would get into the right schools, have the right friends, and so on. She was persuaded. Such services made General Motors an untouchable Morgan Stanley client in the 1950s.
霍尔完美契合了 20 世纪 50 年代的关系银行业务模式。他会在南卡罗来纳州猎火鸡时招待客户，或是在马萨诸塞州伍兹霍尔自家附近钓鱼时款待他们。（73 岁时，他仍有力气用鱼叉捕获一条 552 磅的剑鱼。）作为业余高尔夫和网球冠军，他吸引了许多想切磋球技的企业高管。霍尔常为客户执行精英级别的任务，行为举止宛如他们的家人。当通用汽车董事长因女儿计划嫁给一位巴基斯坦人而烦恼时，佩里叔叔便去劝说那位年轻女子。他询问她的孩子能否进入理想的学校、是否会有合适的社交圈等等。最终她被说服了。这些服务使得通用汽车在 20 世纪 50 年代成为摩根士丹利不可动摇的客户。

Hall admired Tom Lamont and his prankish spirit. Once, at an all-night party on Gramercy Park, Hall got separated from his wife. Wandering about, he came upon Marlene Dietrich and Salvador Dali in a doorway. Hall told the actress that he had idolized her ever since *The Blue Angel.* His wife, Alice, then appeared, and Hall pretended not to know her. “Hey, blondie,” he called to her. “Wanna try this bed?” Alice sat down, pretended to test the bed. “This feels pretty good,” she replied. Later Dietrich cornered Hall. “Did you know that woman?” “I never saw her before in my life,” Hall replied. “You’re the freshest man I ever met,” said Dietrich and stormed off. Hall treasured this anecdote more than he did the biggest General Motors or U.S. Steel underwriting.
霍尔非常欣赏汤姆·拉蒙特的恶作剧精神。一次在格拉梅西公园的通宵派对上，霍尔和妻子走散了。闲逛时，他在一个门口撞见了玛琳·黛德丽和萨尔瓦多·达利。霍尔对这位女演员说，自《蓝天使》之后他就一直崇拜她。这时他的妻子爱丽丝出现了，霍尔假装不认识她。“嘿，金发妞，”他冲她喊道，“想试试这张床吗？”爱丽丝坐下来，假装试了试床。“感觉挺不错的，”她答道。后来黛德丽把霍尔堵在角落。“你认识那个女人吗？”“我这辈子从没见过她，”霍尔回答。“你是我见过最不要脸的男人，”黛德丽说完愤然离去。霍尔珍藏这段轶事的程度，远超过他经手的任何一桩通用汽车或美国钢铁公司的巨额承销交易。

Morgan Stanley people were extremely bright—like the old House of Morgan, the firm rewarded intelligence—but investment banking didn’t require an enormous amount of financial ingenuity. Inflation was low, currencies were stable, and the securities business was relatively straightforward—if you had the right clients. Underwriting spreads were fat in industrial issues. Right off the bat, Hall told his young recruits from Princeton and the other Ivy League schools to coddle their clients and study their needs. “I’m interested in the man who can bring the business in,” he said. “Leave the rest to the business school students. Once you do the deal, put on your hat and go home.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392989) With securities issues pretty standardized, companies had little incentive to shop around among investment banks; the astrophysicists had not yet arrived on Wall Street. That Morgan Stanley offered an extra, indefinable mystique was all the inducement most clients needed to remain loyal.
摩根士丹利的人个个聪明绝顶——就像当年的摩根财团一样，公司论功行赏，看重才智——但投行其实并不需要多么高深的金融造诣。当时通胀低、货币稳，证券业务相对简单——只要你有对口的客户。工业股的承销差价肥得很。一上来，霍尔就叮嘱他从普林斯顿和其他常春藤招来的年轻新人：要哄好客户，摸透他们的需求。“我欣赏能拉来生意的人，”他说，“剩下的交给商学院那帮人就行。做完一单，戴上帽子回家吧。” [30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2392989) 由于证券发行基本标准化，企业没什么动力去货比三家找投行；那些天体物理学家还没涌进华尔街。摩根士丹利自带一种说不清道不明的神秘光环，这足以让大多数客户心甘情愿地死心塌地。

After the New Deal, it was vital to prepare a good securities prospectus and comply with the new legislation. Investment bankers had to exercise “due diligence” and testify to the accuracy of offering documents. Wall Street feared the legal liability that came with the securities laws. Here the firm’s secret weapon was the profane, irreverent Allen Northey Jones, who had headed the J. P. Morgan bond department. A Trinity College alumnus, Jones enjoyed tweaking his partners and would grouse within earshot of Hall about “those goddamn stupid Princeton bastards.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393149) Son of an impoverished Episcopal minister, bald, and moon-faced with bulging eyes, Jones would chug around the office in red suspenders, smoking a pipe.
新政之后，准备一份高质量的证券招股说明书并遵守新法规至关重要。投资银行家必须履行"尽职调查"义务，并证明确凿发行文件的准确性。华尔街对证券法带来的法律责任忧心忡忡。此时，公司秘密武器是粗俗不羁的艾伦·诺西·琼斯——他曾执掌摩根大通债券部门。这位三一学院的校友以戏弄合伙人为乐，常在霍尔能听到的范围内抱怨"那些该死的普林斯顿蠢货"。 [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393149) 作为一位贫困圣公会牧师之子，琼斯秃顶圆脸、双目凸出，总戴着红色背带在办公室来回踱步，嘴里叼着烟斗。

He enjoyed shocking people. Once, when the partners were interviewing a new recruit, he bellowed at the nervous young man, “Are you spoiled?” When the interviewee said he had been lucky in life and was indeed spoiled, Jones jumped up, barked “Hire him and send him to me,” and walked out.[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393307) In new recruits, he inculcated a meticulous attention to detail. He would dump a thick prospectus in a rookie’s lap and tell him there was a single error and the young man had until the next morning to find it. He wanted Morgan Stanley to produce the best prospectuses, and corporations counted on the firm to shield them from legal problems. This led to such manic perfectionism at Morgan Stanley that Harvard MBAs proofread every SEC submission. If the old Wall Street was a restricted club, it had the luxury of exercising extreme care in the kind of business it did.
他喜欢吓唬人。有一次，合伙人们正在面试一名新员工时，他对着那个紧张的年轻人吼道：“你被惯坏了吗？”当面试者表示自己运气不错、确实被惯坏了，琼斯立刻跳起来，吼道：“录用他，让他到我这儿来”，然后径直走了出去。 [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393307) 对于新员工，他灌输的是对细节一丝不苟的态度。他会把一本厚厚的招股说明书扔到新人腿上，告诉他里面只有一个错误，必须在第二天早上之前找出来。他希望摩根士丹利能做出最好的招股说明书，而企业则指望这家公司帮他们规避法律问题。这导致摩根士丹利陷入了一种近乎偏执的完美主义，连哈佛 MBA 都得逐字校对每次提交给 SEC 的文件。如果说旧华尔街是个门槛森严的俱乐部，那它的优势就在于可以在业务上极尽审慎。

Northey Jones trained a generation of Morgan Stanley partners. If a trainee wished to learn railroad finance, Jones would sit with him late at night, poring over maps of a railroad’s tracks and unlocking the company’s secret business strategies. His dedication was total, almost monastic. A perennial bachelor, he glanced at his watch one Saturday and sprang to his feet. “I’ve got an appointment in half an hour,” he said. The appointment was for his wedding. Jones did as much as anyone to ensure Morgan Stanley’s reputation for excellence.
Northey Jones 一手培养了一代摩根士丹利合伙人。如果学员想学习铁路金融，Jones 会陪他熬夜到很晚，摊开铁路轨道地图，解读公司隐秘的商业策略。他全身心投入，几乎像苦行僧一般。这位终身未婚的单身汉，某个周六看了一眼手表突然起身："半小时后我有约。"这个约会正是他的婚礼。为确保摩根士丹利的卓越声誉，Jones 的贡献无人能及。

The influence of Harry S. Morgan, Jack’s younger son, was more elusive. He probably stayed at Morgan Stanley out of a sense of family duty and actually spent more time yachting than issuing securities. He worked beneath a framed certificate of U.S. Steel shares from Pierpont’s 1901 issue. Harry had a classic Morgan resume—commodore of the New York Yacht Club, trustee of the Metropolitan Museum of Art, General Electric director, Harvard overseer. His North Shore estate at Eaton’s Neck, near Huntington, included a manor house, cottages for butler, chauffeur, and gardener, a swimming pool, and an eight-car garage. Sometimes crusty, he was also kindly and gentlemanly and fairly popular at the firm—with reservations.
Jack 的小儿子 Harry S. Morgan 的影响力更为隐秘。他留在摩根士丹利或许出于家族责任感，实则花在游艇上的时间远多于证券发行。他在皮尔庞特 1901 年发行的美国钢铁公司股票凭证镶框挂件下办公。Harry 拥有典型的摩根家族履历——纽约游艇俱乐部会长、大都会艺术博物馆理事、通用电气董事、哈佛大学监事。他位于亨廷顿附近伊顿颈的北岸庄园，拥有主宅、管家、司机和园丁的独立小屋、一座泳池和八车位车库。他时而暴躁，却也温文有礼，在公司颇受欢迎——不过大家对他仍有所保留。

Like his father, Harry was wary of the public and obsessively private. In the 1960s, Princeton made a pitch to house the Morgan papers and sent several distinguished scholars to lobby him over lunch. When they had finished their presentation, Harry startled them by saying, “I’m sorry to tell you gentlemen, there *are* no Morgan papers.” Faces dropped. Arthur Link, a noted Woodrow Wilson scholar, stammered, “But there *must* be Morgan papers.” Harry said his father had warned him to scatter or destroy any papers, lest the government get hold of them and again harass the Morgan family as Pujo and Pecora had. In fact, there were papers, a rich collection, which Harry eventually left with the Pierpont Morgan Library.
和他父亲一样，哈利对公众充满戒心，极度注重隐私。20 世纪 60 年代，普林斯顿大学曾想争取摩根家族的文件收藏，并派了几位知名学者在午餐会上游说他。等他们做完陈述，哈利一句话把众人惊得目瞪口呆：“很遗憾告诉各位先生，摩根家族根本没有文件。”学者们顿时垂头丧气。著名的伍德罗·威尔逊研究专家阿瑟·林克结结巴巴地说：“但摩根家族肯定有文件啊。”哈利解释，父亲曾叮嘱他要么销毁文件，要么分散藏匿，免得政府像当年普约委员会和佩科拉委员会那样，又拿这些文件来找摩根家的麻烦。事实上，文件是有的，而且收藏颇丰，最终哈利将它们留给了皮尔庞特·摩根图书馆。

Perry Hall was somewhat disdainful of Harry Morgan, whom he felt occasionally got in the way. “The other partners were jealous and annoyed at the continued presence of an older man who contributed nothing in their view but yet held the reins in his hand,” said someone close to the firm. “This got more and more true as time went on.” In 1956, there was a bruising fight over whether Harry’s son Charles F. Morgan would be made a partner. Harry had retained legal title to the Morgan name, which he threatened to withdraw unless his son were brought into the firm. To other partners, it seemed that Charlie was a pleasant fellow with little interest in, or special aptitude for, banking. After some testy exchanges, Harry traded his rights to the Morgan name for Charlie’s partnership.
佩里·霍尔对哈里·摩根颇有些不屑，认为他有时碍手碍脚。“其他合伙人对这位年长者的长期存在既嫉妒又恼怒，在他们看来，此人毫无贡献却大权在握，”一位与公司关系密切的人士说道。“随着时间的推移，这种情况越来越明显。”1956 年，围绕是否接纳哈里的儿子查尔斯·F·摩根为合伙人，爆发了一场激烈的争斗。哈里一直保有“摩根”名称的合法使用权，他威胁说，除非儿子能加入公司，否则就撤走这个名号。在其他合伙人看来，查理是个讨人喜欢的家伙，但对银行业务既没兴趣，也缺乏特殊天赋。经过几次尖锐的交锋后，哈里用他对“摩根”名称的权利换来了查理的合伙人资格。

Charlie Morgan would be the only partner in Wall Street history to serve primarily as office manager—he often sat behind heaps of construction blueprints. Years later, when a new partner arrived for his first day, he was told that the man down on his knees fixing his doorknob with a screwdriver was his new partner Charlie Morgan. “If ever two people, father and son, were miscast in life, it was Harry and Charlie Morgan,” sighed an ex-partner. When Morgan Stanley moved uptown to the Exxon Building, Charlie supervised the installation of the new telephone system.
在华尔街历史上，查理·摩根大概是唯一一位主要职责是担任办公室主管的合伙人——他常常坐在一堆建筑蓝图后面。多年后，一位新合伙人来上班的第一天，就被告知那个正跪在地上用螺丝刀修门把手的男人，就是他的新合伙人查理·摩根。“如果说有哪对父子被生活彻底错置了，那就是哈里·摩根和查理·摩根，”一位前合伙人感叹道。当摩根士丹利迁往上城区的埃克森大厦时，查理亲自监督了新电话系统的安装。

After the Charlie Morgan feud, so much residual anger remained that when Harry’s younger son, John, was proposed as a partner, an antinepotism rule was invoked. (The rule was passed after one partner, the son-in-law of a prominent partner, proved to be an alcoholic.) Morgan Stanley now rebelled against the Morgans. Thus John Adams Morgan, who even bore his great-grandfather’s bulbous nose, was blackballed. “Harry Morgan was told, ’You’ve got Charlie, that’s enough,” said an ex-partner. The irony was that John A. Morgan proved the son most interested in finance and later headed the corporate finance departments at both Dominick and Dominick and Smith, Barney.
在查理·摩根的争执之后，积攒了太多残余的怨气，以至于当哈里的小儿子约翰被提名为合伙人时，竟然搬出了一条反裙带关系的规则。（这条规则是在一位重要合伙人的女婿被证实是酒鬼之后通过的。）摩根士丹利现在公然反抗摩根家族。于是，就连继承了曾祖父那蒜头鼻的约翰·亚当斯·摩根也被拒之门外。“哈里·摩根被告知：‘你已经有查理了，够了。’”一位前合伙人说道。讽刺的是，约翰·A·摩根后来被证明是对金融最感兴趣的儿子，并先后在多明尼克与多明尼克以及史密斯·巴尼两家公司担任企业融资部门主管。

In his time, Harry Morgan tried to set the tone and uphold standards at Morgan Stanley. Following family tradition, he gave everyone in the firm a bonus on his twenty-fifth anniversary there, in 1960. “Harry stood for the gentlemanly, principled way of doing business that we felt in those days Morgan Stanley and J. P. Morgan epitomized,” said a former Morgan Stanley partner, Sheppard Poor. At the annual partners’ dinner at the Union Club, he would say, “Gentlemen, the hardest ship to sail is a partnership.”[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393433) In an often greedy business, he presented himself as “brakeman on the Morgan Stanley express train.” Harry prevented the place from degenerating into a haven of the Social Register and perpetuated the Morgan tradition of taking smart, ambitious people from modest backgrounds and turning them into aristocrats. He would say, “We recruit and hire in accord with Morgan tradition—which is to hire people who are brighter than the partners.” Each year, he visited the Harvard Business School and spoke with finance professors about their most promising pupils; he would often conduct initial job interviews himself. Because Harry Morgan also lent money to young people to become partners, he had more than his nominal \$2 million in the firm, giving him veto power.
在哈里·摩根的时代，他努力为摩根士丹利定下基调并坚守标准。1960 年，他遵循家族传统，在公司任职 25 周年之际给每位员工发了奖金。前摩根士丹利合伙人谢泼德·普尔回忆道：“哈里代表了我们那个年代认为摩根士丹利和 J. P. 摩根所体现的绅士、有原则的经商之道。”在联合俱乐部的年度合伙人晚宴上，他常说：“先生们，最难驾驭的船就是合伙制。” [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393433) 在这个充满贪婪的行业中，他把自己定位为“摩根士丹利快车上的刹车手”。哈里阻止了公司沦为《社会名流录》的避风港，并延续了摩根的传统——招募出身平凡但聪明有抱负的人，将他们培养成贵族。他曾说：“我们招募和聘用遵循摩根传统——即聘用比合伙人更聪明的人。”每年，他都会访问哈佛商学院，与金融学教授们聊起他们最有潜力的学生；他本人也常常亲自进行初轮面试。由于哈里·摩根还会借钱给年轻人，让他们成为合伙人，因此他在公司中的实际出资超过了名义上的 200 万美元，从而拥有了否决权。

Though the world’s prestige investment bank, Morgan Stanley seldom appeared in the press. It didn’t promote itself and conscientiously avoided publicity. “It was like a doctor not advertising,” said Perry Hall. To advertise would be “kind of cheap.”[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393591) Investment bankers subordinated themselves to clients and tried to keep their profiles low. There was a huge internal row about whether to put the partners’ pictures into a promotional booklet—an agony resolved in the affirmative after GM chairman Fred Donner said they were all so ugly they would probably scare clients off anyhow. This aversion to publicity was related to the restrained style of competition: if you couldn’t raid other firms’ clients, why bother to advertise? Morgan Stanley’s goal was to freeze the status quo.
虽然摩根士丹利是全球顶尖的投资银行，但它很少出现在媒体报道中。它不自我宣传，刻意避开公众视线。“这就像医生不打广告一样，”佩里·霍尔说道。做广告会显得“有点掉价”。 [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393591) 投行家们始终将客户置于首位，尽量保持低调。曾有一次，内部为是否要把合伙人的照片放进宣传手册而爆发激烈争论——最终在通用汽车董事长弗雷德·唐纳说他们长得都太丑、恐怕会把客户吓跑后，这场纠结才以肯定的方式收场。这种对宣传的抵触，与其克制的竞争风格一脉相承：既然不能挖其他公司的客户，那又何必费心打广告？摩根士丹利的目标就是维持现状。

Morgan Stanley did have one form of advertising, however—the tombstone ads listing the members of underwriting syndicates. All Morgan-sponsored issues were printed in Ronaldson Slope typeface. Sometimes, when traveling, Morgan people stuffed Ronaldson Slope type into their pockets, in case local printers lacked the numerical fractions. Prospectuses were always done in royal-blue type. The great Morgan Stanley hobbyhorse was that its name stand alone atop tomb stone ads and that the firm single-handedly manage issues. This enabled it to price issues and allocate shares among participating firms; it also didn’t have to split lucrative management fees with a co-manager. On the rare occasions when Morgan Stanley deigned to join somebody else’s syndicate, it asked that its name be omitted. By managing the huge industrial syndicates, Morgan Stanley shaped the Wall Street pyramid and decreed the relative standing of firms. This produced a self-assurance that partners would describe as pride but competitors would see as arrogance.
不过，摩根士丹利确实有一种广告形式——即列出承销团成员的墓碑广告。所有由摩根士丹利主导的发行项目，均采用罗纳德森斜体字体印刷。有时出差时，摩根的人会往口袋里塞满这种字体的铅字，以防当地印刷厂缺少分数符号。招股说明书则永远使用皇家蓝字体印刷。摩根士丹利最引以为傲的执念，就是它的名字必须独占墓碑广告的顶端，并且由它独家管理发行项目。这样一来，它就能自主定价并在参与公司之间分配股份，同时也不用与联席管理人瓜分丰厚的管理费。在极少数情况下，摩根士丹利屈尊加入其他公司的承销团时，它也会要求把自己的名字隐去。通过主导那些大型工业承销团，摩根士丹利塑造了华尔街的层级结构，并决定了各家公司的相对地位。 这催生了一种自信——合作伙伴称之为骄傲，竞争对手却视作傲慢。

As the Justice Department noted in the Medina suit, syndicate rankings seldom changed for a particular company. If Morgan Stanley expelled a firm from a syndicate, the firm might not regain admittance for a long time. Risks were widely distributed in the 1950s, so firms didn’t need much capital. On big industrial issues, Morgan Stanley might enlist three hundred underwriters and eight hundred dealers, endowing itself with godlike powers. The firm had virtually nothing to do with selling securities and was strictly a wholesale outfit. It had a clerk on hand to sell unsold syndicate shares around the Street, usually at a loss. This was as close as it ventured into the world of trading.
正如司法部在梅迪纳案中所指出的，某家公司的辛迪加排名很少发生变化。如果摩根士丹利将一家公司逐出辛迪加，这家公司可能很长一段时间都无法重新加入。20 世纪 50 年代风险被广泛分散，因此公司并不需要太多资本。在大型工业股发行中，摩根士丹利可能会召集三百家承销商和八百家经销商，赋予自己近乎神祇般的力量。这家公司几乎不参与证券销售，严格来说只是个批发机构。它派出一名职员负责在华尔街兜售未售出的辛迪加股份，通常以亏本收场——这便是它最接近涉足交易世界的一次尝试。

Nobody could afford to alienate Morgan Stanley, which presided over most of the decade’s record issues, such as the General Motors \$300-million debt issue of 1953 and its \$328-million stock issue of 1957, the \$231-million IBM stock offering of 1957, and the \$300-million U.S. Steel debt issue of 1958. These securities didn’t finance speculation or line the pockets of a self-serving management. They went for new V-8 auto engines or a steel plant on the Delaware River or IBM’s expansion into the computer business. At this point, investment banking still functioned according to a textbook model in which capital was tapped for investment, not financial manipulation. Investment bankers were still intermediaries between providers and users of capital, and they considered it unprofessional to function as the “principal” in a transaction. The age of financial engineering hadn’t yet dawned.
没人敢得罪摩根士丹利，这家投行包揽了那个年代多数创纪录的债券发行，比如 1953 年通用汽车 3 亿美元债券、1957 年其 3.28 亿美元股票发行、1957 年 IBM 2.31 亿美元股票发行，以及 1958 年美国钢铁公司 3 亿美元债券。这些证券既不是用来炒作，也不是给自私自利的管理层塞腰包，而是用于开发新的 V-8 汽车发动机、建设特拉华河畔的钢厂，或者支持 IBM 进军计算机业务。彼时，投资银行依旧遵循教科书模式运转——融资是为了实业投资，而非金融操纵。投资银行家仍是资本供需双方的中介，他们视在交易中充当"自营方"为不专业之举。金融工程的时代尚未到来。

Morgan Stanley’s monopoly of so much of America’s industry made the firm far less adventurous than J. P. Morgan and Company in exploring foreign markets. In the early postwar years, its few foreign financings had a distinctly Anglo-Saxon or European bias. It sponsored large issues for Australia and Canada, smaller ones for France and Italy. During the 1950s, Morgan Stanley made only one exception to its sole-manager policy, and that was for the World Bank, where it co-managed issues with First Boston. The names of the two firms alternated in the top-left corner of the prospectuses. Through the World Bank, Morgan Stanley partners believed that they made their contribution to European reconstruction and the Atlantic alliance.
摩根士丹利对美国众多行业的垄断，使其在开拓海外市场方面远不如 J.P.摩根公司那样大胆冒险。战后初期，它为数不多的海外融资项目明显带有盎格鲁-撒克逊或欧洲倾向。摩根士丹利为澳大利亚和加拿大承销了大量债券，为法国和意大利则发行了规模较小的债券。20 世纪 50 年代，摩根士丹利对其独家承销政策仅破例一次，那就是为世界银行发行债券——当时它与第一波士顿共同管理发行。两家公司的名字在招股说明书左上角交替出现。摩根士丹利的合伙人认为，通过世界银行，他们为欧洲重建和跨大西洋联盟做出了贡献。

In the early days, the World Bank was a highly conservative institution. The International Monetary Fund, however—and contrary to its later image—was then feared as a hotbed of left-wing activism. Russell Leffngwell derided it as a “dream child” that would prop up overvalued currencies, and the American Bankers Association lobbied vigorously against its creation. But the World Bank seemed a pillar of sound finance and was congenial to Morgan Stanley. Because the bank depended on U.S. capital markets for money, early World Bank presidents were chosen from Wall Street. In 1949, Eugene Black, formerly a senior vice-president at Chase, replaced John J. McCloy as president. After a brief experiment with competitive bidding, Black (whose son Bill was later a Morgan Stanley executive) chose Morgan Stanley and First Boston as a permanent team to market the Bank’s triple-A-rated issues in 1952. Black later explained his choice: “Morgan Stanley has a close connection with Morgan Grenfell in London, and with the old firm of Morgan in Paris. They had a very fine reputation in Europe.”[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393749)
早期，世界银行是一家高度保守的机构。而国际货币基金组织——与其后来的形象相反——当时则被视为左翼激进主义的温床，令人畏惧。拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔将其讥讽为一个支撑高估货币的“梦幻产物”，美国银行家协会也极力游说反对其成立。但世界银行看上去是稳健金融的支柱，与摩根士丹利颇为投契。由于该行依赖美国资本市场获取资金，早期的世界银行行长均来自华尔街。1949 年，曾担任大通银行高级副总裁的尤金·布莱克接替约翰·J·麦克洛伊出任行长。在短暂尝试竞争性招标后，布莱克（其子比尔后来成为摩根士丹利高管）于 1952 年选定摩根士丹利和第一波士顿作为固定团队，负责承销世界银行 AAA 评级的债券。 布莱克后来解释了他的选择：“摩根士丹利与伦敦的摩根建富以及巴黎的老摩根公司关系密切，在欧洲享有极佳声誉。”” [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393749)

In selling the World Bank to investors, Morgan Stanley and First Boston faced a formidable job. Its very name—the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development—was a mouthful. There were fears—noted earlier—that it might repeat the foreign lending disasters of the 1920s. To promote the bank, Morgan Stanley and First Boston organized huge syndicates of up to 175 underwriters, put on road shows, published booklets, and even seconded people for brief stints at the bank. Morgan Stanley got a critical guarantee that World Bank bonds were backed by America’s capital contribution and were therefore as good as obligations of the U.S. Treasury itself. Morgan Stanley partners always took immense pride in the World Bank account, which marked the summit of the firm’s success: they were banker to the world’s bank, a big enough honor to satisfy even the most swollen Morgan ego.
在向投资者推销世界银行时，摩根士丹利和第一波士顿面临艰巨挑战。光是其全称——国际复兴开发银行——就够拗口的。如前所述，人们担心它可能重蹈 20 世纪 20 年代对外贷款失败的覆辙。为了推广该银行，摩根士丹利和第一波士顿组织了多达 175 家承销商的庞大银团，举办路演，印发宣传册，甚至派人到银行短期任职。摩根士丹利获得一项关键担保：世界银行债券由美国出资支持，因此与美国国债无异。摩根士丹利的合伙人始终以世界银行账户为傲，这标志着公司成功的巅峰——他们成了世界银行的银行家，这份殊荣足以满足摩根士丹利最膨胀的自尊心。

IN the 1950s, the City of London hadn’t yet awakened from its Depression slumber. It was stuffy, inbred, and unimaginative, feeding off past glory. England had lost a quarter of its national wealth in defeating Germany and couldn’t function as a world banker. It had lost Italy to the Marshall Plan and China and Eastern Europe to the Communists. Its old foreign clients were fair game to be picked off by Wall Street firms: in 1946, Dudley Schoales of Morgan Stanley snared the first postwar loan to Australia—already a J. P. Morgan client in the 1920s—and the firm sponsored Qantas Airlines two years later.
20 世纪 50 年代，伦敦金融城尚未从大萧条的沉睡中苏醒。这里沉闷、封闭、缺乏想象力，靠着昔日的荣光过活。英国在击败德国时损失了四分之一的国家财富，已无力担当世界银行的角色。马歇尔计划让意大利倒向美国，而中国和东欧则落入共产主义阵营。它昔日的外国客户成了华尔街公司争相抢夺的肥肉：1946 年，摩根士丹利的 Dudley Schoales 争取到了战后首笔对澳贷款——而澳大利亚在 20 世纪 20 年代就已曾是 J. P. Morgan 的客户——两年后，该公司又赞助了澳洲航空。

The City was hobbled by exchange controls and a weak pound. Under the postwar Anglo-American Loan Agreement, the United States lent Britain \$3.75 billion to cover its payments deficit. In exchange, Britain was supposed to make sterling convertible to other currencies by July 15, 1947. The attempt failed abysmally as investors rushed to dump pounds for dollars. Speaking at the Lord Mayor’s Dinner in October 1947, Lord Catto, governor of the Bank of England, ruefully reviewed this blow to British pride: “Confidence was returning; sterling balances were being more and more freely held in London as in the days before the war. . . . At any rate, we were obliged to try.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393895) The sterling market was largely shut to foreigners until Margaret Thatcher dismantled exchange controls in 1979. In its century-long contest with the City, Wall Street had won hands down.
伦敦金融城因外汇管制和英镑疲软而步履蹒跚。根据战后的《英美贷款协定》，美国向英国提供了 37.5 亿美元贷款，以弥补其支付赤字。作为交换，英国须在 1947 年 7 月 15 日前使英镑可自由兑换成其他货币。但这一尝试以惨败告终，投资者纷纷抛售英镑换取美元。1947 年 10 月，英格兰银行行长卡托勋爵在伦敦市长晚宴上痛心地回顾了这次对英国自尊的打击：“信心正在恢复；英镑余额正像战前一样越来越自由地存放在伦敦……无论如何，我们不得不尝试。” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2393895) 英镑市场基本上对外国人关闭，直到 1979 年玛格丽特·撒切尔废除外汇管制。在与伦敦金融城长达一个世纪的竞争中，华尔街轻松获胜。

Like most places of obsolete splendor, the City was full of charming eccentricities. At one merchant bank, incoming mail was laid on a table each morning so partners could scan each other’s correspondence. At N. M. Rothschild’s townhouse, partners shook little bells marked “butler” when they sought refreshment. At the manorial Hambros, senior people were called Mr. Olaf or Mr. Charles. Self-respecting merchant bankers still wore bowler hats and carried furled umbrellas; their reading glasses were always crescent-shaped. Junior men wore stiff collars and were considered dangerously uppity if they let them soften. In this conformist world, when a Lloyds Bank chairman appeared in black suede shoes, people buzzed for days about the frightful lapse in taste.
与大多数日渐褪色的辉煌之地一样，伦敦金融城处处透着迷人的古怪。在一家商业银行，每天早晨邮件会摊在桌上，合伙人可以互相翻阅彼此的往来信函。在 N. M. 罗斯柴尔德家族的宅邸里，合伙人们想要茶点时会摇动标有“管家”字样的小铃铛。在具有庄园气派的汉布罗斯公司，资深员工被称作奥拉夫先生或查尔斯先生。自尊自重的商业银行家依然戴着圆顶硬礼帽，手持收拢的雨伞；他们的老花镜总是半月形。初级职员穿着硬挺的衣领，若让领子变软，就会被视为犯了大忌、过于冒进。在这个循规蹈矩的世界里，当劳埃德银行董事长穿着黑色绒面革皮鞋出现时，人们会一连好几天议论这骇人听闻的品味失当。

With slightly over one hundred employees, Morgan Grenfell emerged from the war in relatively strong shape. In U.S. banking terminology, it was a cross between a commercial and an investment bank, underwriting bond issues but also managing pension funds and making loans. Like Morgan Stanley, it seemed to have a monopoly on major industrial accounts. In 1945, it sponsored the first postwar share issue and floated debt for virtually every British electric company, including Associated Electrical Industries and British General Electric. It also handled denationalization of steel companies—the legacy of Teddy Grenfell’s work with Monty Norman to rationalize the industry in the 1930s—and participated in World Bank issues. But the firm was softened by prewar success. The partners (technically directors) had a lazy, custodial attitude toward accounts and wouldn’t dig up new business or stir from their chairs. When they disappeared to Boodle’s or Brooks’s for lunch, they might return—or they might call it a day. Rod Lindsay, a later Morgan Guaranty president who apprenticed at Morgan Grenfell, recalled the somnolent mood: “By Thursday afternoon at four, one of the senior partners would come across to the juniors and say, ’Why are we all still here? It’s almost the weekend.’ ”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394078)
摩根·格林菲尔（Morgan Grenfell）凭借区区一百多名员工，在战后仍保持了相对强劲的态势。按美国银行业的说法，它介于商业银行和投资银行之间，既承销债券发行，又管理养老基金和发放贷款。和摩根士丹利一样，它似乎垄断了主要工业客户。1945 年，它承销了战后第一只股票发行，并为几乎所有的英国电力公司（包括联合电气工业公司和英国通用电气公司）发行债券。它还参与了钢铁公司的非国有化进程——这是泰迪·格林菲尔（Teddy Grenfell）上世纪 30 年代与蒙蒂·诺曼（Monty Norman）合作整顿该行业留下的遗产——并参与了世界银行的债券发行。然而，战前的成功让这家公司变得懈怠。合伙人（严格来说是董事）对客户抱着一种懒散、守成的态度，不愿开拓新业务，也懒得动一动。 当他们消失去 Boodle's 或 Brooks's 吃午餐时，他们可能会回来——也可能就此收工。曾在 Morgan Grenfell 学徒、后来成为 Morgan Guaranty 总裁的 Rod Lindsay 回忆起那种慵懒的氛围：“到了周四下午四点，一位资深合伙人会走过来对年轻员工说，‘我们还待在这儿干什么？周末都快到了。’”’ ” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394078)

J. P. Morgan and Company still held a passive, one-third share in Morgan Grenfell. It was the only foreign bank with a sizable stake in a merchant bank on the elite Accepting Houses Committee. Lacking a London office, J. P. Morgan and Company used the firm as its U.K. branch equivalent, and the two houses traded apprentices and clients. When Esso mapped out big postwar expansion plans for refineries in Western Europe, 23 Wall Street steered the company to Morgan Grenfell. Ditto for Procter and Gamble, Monsanto, Inco, Alcan, and General Foods. After stepping down as Bank of England governor in 1949, Tom Catto took a desk back at Morgan Grenfell (though he didn’t resume his partnership) and extended the special access of both J. P. Morgan and Morgan Grenfell to the Bank of England.
J.P.摩根公司在摩根建富仍然持有三分之一的被动股份。它是唯一一家在精英承兑行委员会旗下的商业银行中拥有可观股份的外国银行。由于在伦敦没有办事处，J.P.摩根公司便将摩根建富作为其在英国的分支机构使用，两家机构之间互换学徒和客户。当埃索公司为西欧的炼油厂制定大规模战后扩张计划时，华尔街 23 号将这家公司引荐给了摩根建富。同样的情况也发生在宝洁、孟山都、英可、阿尔肯和通用食品身上。1949 年，汤姆·卡托卸任英格兰银行行长后，又回到了摩根建富的办公桌前（尽管他未恢复合伙人身份），并延续了 J.P.摩根和摩根建富与英格兰银行之间的特殊通道。

Morgan Grenfell was so heavy with peers that it was derided as the House of Lords (in sometimes sniggering tones) by its J. P. Morgan counterparts. In a caste system common in the City, partners were drawn largely from family members, with only Sir George Erskine, a brilliant, driving Scots banker, rising from the managerial ranks to become a partner. (By no coincidence, he was the best banker.) The aging Lord Bicester—Vivian Hugh Smith—reigned, somewhat terrifyingly, as senior partner, and his authority was unquestioned until his death, in 1956. He treated other partners like errand boys as they rushed in and out to get his approval. Everybody called him the Old Man. He was a sphinx who kept his own counsel and never tipped his hand. During eighteen years in the House of Lords, he never delivered a speech. Once, on a deadlocked charity board, he was asked whether he favored a proposed measure. “No,” he said, then added, “Or have I said too much?”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394240) To be interviewed for a job by Bicester was to endure an array of skeptical snorts, grunts, and harrumphs.
摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）的合伙人中贵族扎堆，甚至被摩根大通（J. P. Morgan）那边的人（偶尔带着窃笑）嘲讽为"上议院"。在伦敦金融城常见的等级制度中，合伙人大多来自家族内部，只有乔治·厄斯金爵士（Sir George Erskine）——一位才华横溢、干劲十足的苏格兰银行家——从管理层一路晋升成为合伙人。（毫不意外，他是最优秀的银行家。）年迈的比斯特勋爵（Lord Bicester）——即维维安·休·史密斯（Vivian Hugh Smith）——以资深合伙人身份掌舵，有些令人畏惧，他的权威直到 1956 年去世都无人质疑。他把其他合伙人当跑腿小伙计使唤，任凭他们进进出出只为争得他的首肯。人人都叫他"老家伙"。他像个人面狮身的斯芬克斯，凡事自有主张，从不露半点口风。在上议院任职十八年，他从未发表过一次演讲。有一次，在一个陷入僵局的慈善董事会上，有人问他是否支持某项提案。"不，"他说，接着又补了一句，"还是说我话太多了？"” [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394240) 被比斯特面试，就意味着得忍受一连串充满怀疑的哼声、咕哝和咳嗽。

Even when he was in his late seventies, Vivian Smith wouldn’t pass the reins to his son, Rufus, who had patrolled on the roof of 23 Great Winchester Street during the wartime buzz-bomb raids. Rufie was relegated to a sad Prince of Wales role. A portly man with a jolly well-fed look, round-faced and mustachioed, he acted the grandee: he was the sort of large, stately man who would rap on doors with the knob of his cane. He loved steeplechase horses and hunting and tossed off whiskey by the tumblerful. Like his father, he had connections everywhere. He served as a director of Shell, Vickers, and AEI and also sat on the Court of the Bank of England. His wife, Lady Helen, was a daughter of the earl of Rosebery.
即便到了七十多岁，维维安·史密斯依然不肯把权杖交给儿子鲁弗斯——二战期间，鲁弗斯曾在温彻斯特大街 23 号屋顶上巡逻，躲避飞弹袭击。鲁菲只能扮演一个可怜的“威尔士亲王”角色。他身材魁梧，面色红润，圆脸蓄须，一副养尊处优的富态相，行事派头十足：是那种会用手杖头敲门的高大威严人物。他热爱越野赛马和狩猎，喝威士忌都是一杯接一杯地干。和父亲一样，他人脉遍布四方，担任过壳牌、维克斯和 AEI 的董事，还曾是英格兰银行董事会的成员。他的妻子海伦夫人是罗斯伯里伯爵的女儿。

Rufie was cowed by the thunderous presence of the Old Man and patiently suffered a marathon apprenticeship lasting well into late middle age. In the late 1940s, Sir Edward Peacock, the senior partner of Barings, told Russell Leffngwell how the Old Man was pleased that Rufie had taken the lead in a Shell financing and proven himself as a good, sound fellow.[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394401) Yet Rufie had already been through two world wars! In 1949, Lord Bicester relented and let his son take part in a major steel business. “Oh well, the boy’s got to learn sometime,” he sighed.[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394621) The boy was then fifty-one and had been a partner for almost twenty years.
鲁菲在老爷子的雷厉风行面前畏首畏尾，硬是熬过了马拉松式的学徒生涯，直到中年后期才得以出头。20 世纪 40 年代末，巴林银行的高级合伙人爱德华·皮科克爵士告诉拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔，老爷子很高兴鲁菲在壳牌融资项目中挑了大梁，证明自己是个靠谱的好伙计。 [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394401) 可鲁菲那时都已经经历过两次世界大战了！1949 年，比斯特勋爵终于松了口，让儿子参与一桩大型钢铁生意。"唉，这小子总得学点东西，"他叹道。 [40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394621) 而这位"小子"当时已经五十一岁，当合伙人也快二十年了。

In the City of the 1950s, with most business revolving around relationships, Morgan Grenfell was hard to match. It was the City’s major portfolio manager for the Vatican, thanks partly to the flamboyant, multilingual Francis Rodd (the second Baron Rennell), son of a former ambassador to Italy. A portly, snuff-taking man who blew his nose into a big red handkerchief, Rodd was a protege of Monty Norman’s and a former British manager of the Bank for International Settlements in Basel. As a close friend of T. E. Lawrence (Lawrence of Arabia), he was once asked by Monty Norman to recruit Lawrence as secretary of the Bank of England. (Lawrence declined.) Rodd himself was spirited away to Morgan Grenfell by his father-in-law, Vivian Smith, in 1933.
在 20 世纪 50 年代的伦敦金融城，大多数业务都依赖于人际关系，而摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）几乎无人能及。它是梵蒂冈在金融城的主要投资组合管理方，这在一定程度上要归功于那位张扬且通晓多种语言的弗朗西斯·罗德斯（第二代伦内尔男爵），他是一位前驻意大利大使的儿子。罗德斯身材肥胖，爱吸鼻烟，擤鼻涕时总用一块红色大手帕。他是蒙蒂·诺曼的门生，曾担任巴塞尔国际清算银行的英方经理。作为 T.E.劳伦斯（阿拉伯的劳伦斯）的密友，蒙蒂·诺曼曾请他招募劳伦斯担任英格兰银行秘书，但劳伦斯拒绝了。1933 年，罗德斯本人被岳父维维安·史密斯引荐至摩根建富。

Assigned to Harold Macmillan’s wartime staff in 1943, Rodd was made chief civilian aide to Sir Harold Alexander, who administered occupied territory in Italy. Left-wing commentators criticized the choice, noting that Morgan loans had propped up Italian fascism and warning that Rodd might help to give former fascist finance officials a voice in postwar Italy. Nevertheless, Rodd acted ably to alleviate hunger and sickness in liberated Naples. Macmillan thought Rodd a *prima donna* and an intriguer but also praised him as “quick, intelligent and persistent.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394777) So long as Rodd was around, the Vatican business stayed in Morgan Grenfell’s hands.
1943 年，罗德被调至哈罗德·麦克米伦的战时参谋部，担任哈罗德·亚历山大爵士的首席文职助理，负责管理意大利占领区事务。左翼评论家对此任命提出批评，指出摩根贷款曾助长意大利法西斯势力，并警告罗德可能会让前法西斯金融官员在战后意大利获得话语权。尽管如此，罗德在解放那不勒斯的过程中，在缓解饥荒和疾病方面表现出色。麦克米伦虽认为罗德是个爱出风头、精于权谋的人，但也称赞他“反应快、有头脑且持之以恒”。只要罗德在，梵蒂冈的业务就始终掌握在摩根建富手中。

The chief partner for portfolio management was Wilfred William Hill Hill-Wood, who provided Morgan Grenfell with entree to Buckingham Palace. A shrewd, entertaining fellow and a brilliant cricketer, Hill-Wood had served as intermediary between Morgan Grenfell and 23 Wall. Like Jack Morgan, he was a close friend of George VI. “Uncle Willy became friends with George VI at Trinity College, Cambridge, and the king asked him to look after some of his personal finances,” said his nephew Sir David Basil Hill-Wood.[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394938) Hill-Wood reported regularly to the king on his finances, keeping details of the account to himself. His friendship with George VI guaranteed that when Elizabeth became queen in the early 1950s, Morgan Grenfell would manage a significant portion of her wealth as well. The queen was amused by Willy and apparently on easy terms with him. When she knighted him at Buckingham Palace, she took the sword from behind the curtain, tapped him, and then whispered slyly, “You can get up now, Willie.”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395108)
投资组合管理的主要合伙人是威尔弗雷德·威廉·希尔·希尔-伍德，他凭借人脉为摩根建富打开了通往白金汉宫的大门。此人精明风趣，还是个出色的板球手，曾充当摩根建富与华尔街 23 号之间的中间人。与杰克·摩根一样，他也是乔治六世的密友。他的侄子大卫·巴兹尔·希尔-伍德爵士回忆道：“威尔叔叔在剑桥大学三一学院与乔治六世结为挚友，国王委托他打理部分个人财务。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2394938) 希尔-伍德定期向国王汇报财务情况，账户细节则始终由他自己掌控。正是他与乔治六世的这层交情，使得 20 世纪 50 年代初伊丽莎白女王登基后，摩根建富得以继续管理其大部分财富。女王对威尔颇感亲近，两人相处自然融洽。 当她于白金汉宫为他授爵时，她从幕布后取出宝剑，轻点他的肩膀，随后俏皮地低语道：“现在可以起来了，威利。” [43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395108)

Rich in memorabilia, Morgan Grenfell’s atmosphere in the 1950s was antiquated. Partners sipped sherry by coal fires while young clerks on tall stools copied accounts into large bound books. These victims of the “fagging system” didn’t emerge into adulthood until about age forty, by which point many were thought brain dead. Sexual segregation at Morgan Grenfell was strict. To mask their sexuality “tea ladies” were required to wear linen dusters around the office and leave their jobs when they married. Nomenclature was highly revealing: the firm called itself a countinghouse and directors were partners; it was listed under “merchants” in the London telephone directory.
摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）办公室里摆满了各类纪念品，但其 1950 年代的氛围却显得古旧过时。合伙人们围坐在煤火旁啜饮雪利酒，年轻的职员们则高踞在高脚凳上，将账目誊抄进厚重的装订簿中。这些“公学仆役制”下的受害者要等到大约四十岁才算真正成年，而那时多数人已被认为思维僵化。摩根建富内部严守性别隔离，为了淡化女性气质，“茶点女士”被要求穿着亚麻罩衫在办公室内走动，并且一旦结婚就得离职。公司的命名也极具暗示意味：它自称“账房”，董事被称为“合伙人”；在伦敦电话簿中，它被归在“商人”条目之下。

The thunderclap that roused the City from this profound torpor was Siegmund Warburg’s first hostile raid in the famous aluminium war of 1958-59. To understand the furor, it is necessary to note the City’s cultural homogeneity. It was a hermetic world of men who had passed through Eton and Oxford, Cambridge, or the Guards and met at Lord’s or Wimbledon on weekends. Shot through with class barriers, the City made upward mobility all but impossible for foreigners. From an eminent Hamburg banking family, Siegmund Warburg had fled Hitler in the 1930s and started a merchant bank in 1946. As a Sephardic Jew with a German name and a German accent, bored by shooting and yachting, he seemed to grate on City bankers. One merchant banker admitted, “Siegmund’s Jewishness was a problem. He was a little *too* Jewish, as they say in the City.”
将这座城市从深沉倦怠中惊醒的惊雷，是西格蒙德·沃伯格在 1958-59 年著名的铝业战争中发起的首次恶意收购。要理解这场轩然大波，必须注意到伦敦金融城文化上的同质性。那是一个封闭的世界，成员们皆出身伊顿、牛津、剑桥或近卫军团，周末在罗德板球场或温布尔登碰面。金融城里等级森严，外国人几乎不可能实现向上流动。出身汉堡显赫银行世家的西格蒙德·沃伯格在 1930 年代为躲避希特勒流亡英国，并于 1946 年创办了一家商业银行。作为操着德国口音、顶着德国姓氏的塞法迪犹太人，他对狩猎和游艇毫无兴趣，这让金融城的银行家们感到格格不入。一位商业银行家坦言：“西格蒙德的犹太身份是个问题。按金融城里的说法，他有点太犹太了。”

Warburg was an unlikely revolutionary who followed all the old merchant-banking folkways. He posted no nameplate, opened no branch offices, and valued personal contacts. But he was always an activist, an innovator, and he would quote Dwight Morrow, whom he had met as a young man in the 1920s. “The world is divided into people who do things and people who get the credit. Try if you can to belong to the first class, there is far less competition.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395234) In his Belgravia apartment were books in six languages, and he said he would rather hire someone steeped in George Eliot than someone steeped in banking. His use of handwriting analysis for recruiting employees added to his eccentric image.
沃伯格是一位看似不可能的革新者，却恪守着旧式商人银行的一切传统。他不挂招牌，不开分行，极为重视私人关系。但他始终是实干家与创新者，年轻时在 20 世纪 20 年代结识德怀特·莫罗，常引用其言："世上分为做事之人与争功之人。设法成为前者吧，竞争要小得多。" [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395234) 他在贝尔格莱维亚区的公寓里摆放着六种语言的书籍，他说宁愿雇佣熟读乔治·艾略特的人，也不选精通银行业务的人。他用笔迹分析来招聘员工的做法，更添其古怪形象。

While Morgan Grenfell floated through long, pleasant lunches, Warburg ran a firm disciplined in Prussian punctuality. Some Warburg people sat through two lunches—one at 12:30, one at 1:30—to maximize the business they conducted. Young recruits arrived early, stayed late, and worked weekends, while young Morgan Grenfell men were out shooting blizzards of birds from the sky. Warburgs, significantly, was the first firm to scrap the bowler-hat-and-umbrella costume in favor of modern dress.
摩根建富在悠长惬意的午餐时光中悠然度日，而华宝则恪守普鲁士式的守时纪律管理公司。华宝的一些员工会参加两场午餐——一场 12:30，一场 1:30——以最大化开展的商务活动。年轻的新人早到晚走，周末加班，而摩根建富的年轻员工则在外面漫天射击飞鸟。值得注意的是，华宝是第一家抛弃圆顶礼帽和雨伞的传统装扮、改穿现代服装的公司。

With a cool outsider’s perspicacity, Siegmund Warburg saw that the City disliked unpleasantness and would tolerate mediocrity just to avoid a row. This wasn’t surprising, with so many family-run banks and such extensive intermarriage in the City. Warburg also saw that merchant banks no longer had the capital to finance industry or government on a large scale. In the advisory area, by contrast, small capital was no handicap. “In the sense that bankers provide money for industry, they’re becoming less important,” he said; “but in the sense of being consultants—what I call ’financial engineers’—they’re becoming much more important.”[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395379) This was the critical insight of the Casino Age, the idea that would push merchant bankers from the staid world of securities issues into the piratical world of takeovers. The merchant bankers would no longer hand out free merger advice to preserve underwriting relationships. Before Siegmund Warburg was through, the “stuffy” City would be rife with marauders.
西格蒙德·沃伯格以局外人冷峻的洞察力看到，伦敦金融城厌恶不愉快之事，为了避开争吵，宁愿容忍平庸。这并不奇怪，金融城里有那么多家族式银行，而且联姻关系盘根错节。沃伯格还看到，商人银行已不再拥有为工业或政府提供大规模融资所需的资本。相比之下，在咨询领域，小资本并不构成障碍。他说："从银行家为工业提供资金的角度来看，他们正变得越来越不重要；但从顾问——我称之为'金融工程师'——的角度来看，他们正变得重要得多。" [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395379) 这正是赌场时代的关键洞见，这个理念将推动商人银行家从证券发行的安稳世界，进入收购兼并的海盗世界。商人银行家将不再为了维护承销关系而免费提供并购建议。 在西格蒙德·沃伯格大功告成之前，这座“沉闷”的金融城将满是掠夺者。

In 1958, Warburg mounted the first major hostile takeover in postwar Britain. Takeovers had existed there for decades—they had formed Imperial Chemical Industries, Unilever, Shell, and the big deposit banks. As early as 1925, Morgan Grenfell had negotiated General Motors’ investment in Vauxhall Motors. But these were genteel affairs, consummated over sherry. By mid-1958, Warburg had persuaded Reynolds Metal of Virginia to launch a hostile bid for British Aluminium. To give the move a British veneer, Reynolds allied itself with Tube Investments, a Midlands engineering group. Moving stealthily, Warburgs had bought more than 10 percent of British Aluminium by October 1958. Siegmund Warburg would shift the City battleground from contacts to capital and introduce an unsettling new form of democracy.
1958 年，沃伯格在英国发动了战后首场重大恶意收购。数十年来，英国并购交易早已有之——帝国化学工业、联合利华、壳牌以及大型储蓄银行皆由此诞生。早在 1925 年，摩根建富便促成了通用汽车对沃克斯豪尔汽车的投资。但这些交易均属彬彬有礼的社交场合，一杯雪利酒下肚即可敲定。到 1958 年中，沃伯格说服弗吉尼亚州的雷诺兹金属公司对英国铝业发起恶意收购。为赋予此举英国色彩，雷诺兹与米德兰兹工程集团管材投资公司结盟。沃伯格家族暗中行动，至 1958 年 10 月已购入英国铝业逾 10%股份。齐格蒙德·沃伯格将伦敦金融城的战场从人脉关系转向资本力量，引入了一种令人不安的新型民主。

When British Aluminium learned of Warburg’s scheme, management summoned Olaf Hambro and Lord Kindersley of Lazard Brothers. (Lazards was close to Morgan Grenfell in the 1950s, with the two firms even sharing a box at Covent Garden.) In comparison with the upstart raiders, British Aluminium had a true-blue patriotic image. The managing director, Geoffrey Cunliffe, was a son of the World War I Bank of England governor. Its chairman was the bemedaled Lord Portal of Hun-gerford, a wartime hero as chief of air staff and a president of the Marylebone Cricket Club. Although the firm was already negotiating a partnership deal with the American colossus Alcoa, the Hambro-Lazard defense rested on a bogus threat to national sovereignty. “One day, a party consisting of Olaf Hambro and other senior figures paid a state visit to the partners’ room at Morgan Grenfell,” recalled Tim Collins, a later Morgan Grenfell chairman. “They said, ’This is a patriotic duty and the City is going to collapse otherwise.’ The Morgan Grenfell partners joined in without a fight.”[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395555)
当英国铝业公司得知沃伯格（Warburg）的计划后，管理层紧急召见了奥拉夫·汉布罗（Olaf Hambro）和拉扎德兄弟公司（Lazard Brothers）的金德斯利勋爵（Lord Kindersley）。（上世纪 50 年代，拉扎德与摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）关系密切，两家公司甚至在科文特花园（Covent Garden）共用一个包厢。）与这些暴发户式的突袭者相比，英国铝业公司拥有根正苗红的爱国形象。总经理杰弗里·坎利夫（Geoffrey Cunliffe）是一战时期英格兰银行行长的儿子；董事长则是战功赫赫的亨格福德·波特尔勋爵（Lord Portal of Hungerford）——二战时期的空军参谋长、战时英雄，同时也是玛丽勒本板球俱乐部（Marylebone Cricket Club）主席。尽管公司当时已在与美国巨头美铝公司（Alcoa）谈判合作协议，但汉布罗-拉扎德防御策略的核心，竟是捏造出一个有关国家主权的虚假威胁。"有一天，由奥拉夫·汉布罗和其他几位高层组成的代表团，对摩根建富的合伙人办公室进行了国事访问般的造访，"后来担任摩根建富董事长的蒂姆·柯林斯（Tim Collins）回忆道。 “他们说，‘这是爱国义务，否则这座城市就要崩溃了。’摩根建富的合伙人毫不犹豫地加入了进来。” [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395555)

In November, Sir Ivan Stedeford, the self-made chairman of Tube Investments, presented a proposal to Lord Portal by which Tube and Reynolds would buy a majority stake in British Aluminium for a generous 78 shillings per share. Lord Portal curtly refused, made veiled references to talks in progress, and brazenly withheld Stedeford’s plans from shareholders. Later he issued the following mystifying statement: “Those familiar with negotiations between great companies will realize that such a course would have been impracticable.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395711) Although its defense was predicated on scare talk about a Yankee invasion—Tube was dismissed as Reynolds’s “window dressing”—British Aluminium continued talks with its “white knight,” Alcoa. Within a week, it negotiated a deal that allowed Alcoa to buy one-third of the company at a miserly 60 shillings a share. The institutional investors—the new powers of the age—were inflamed by this wanton disregard for shareholders. And they became a key constituency in Warburg’s camp.
11 月，白手起家的管材投资公司董事长伊凡·斯特德福德爵士向波特尔勋爵提出一项提议：由管材与雷诺兹公司以每股 78 先令的优厚价格收购英国铝业的多数股权。波特尔勋爵断然拒绝，含糊其辞地暗示已在进行的谈判，并厚颜无耻地向股东隐瞒了斯特德福德的计划。随后，他发表了如下令人费解的声明："熟悉大企业间谈判的人都会明白，这样的做法根本不切实际。" [47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395711) 尽管英国铝业的防御策略建立在"美国佬入侵"的危言耸听之上——管材公司被贬低为雷诺兹的"遮羞布"——但它仍继续与"白衣骑士"美国铝业谈判。一周之内，它便敲定了一项协议，允许美国铝业以每股仅 60 先令的低价收购公司三分之一的股份。机构投资者——这个时代的新兴力量——对这种肆意漠视股东利益的行为深感愤怒。 他们成为了沃伯格阵营中的关键力量。

In the popular mind, it was still axiomatic that nobody could prevail against the unified power of the merchant banks. Schroders and Helbert Wagg sided with Warburgs. Otherwise, the City closed ranks behind British Aluminium in a seemingly invincible phalanx, including Hambros, Lazards, Morgan Grenfell, Flemings, Samuel Montagu, and Brown Shipley. From an *aide-mémoire* prepared by Hambros and Lazards, it’s clear that Warburg’s ungentlemanly method upset the group far more than the noisily trumpeted demerits of the Reynolds-Tube proposal. This internal document conceded the offer’s soundness, only inveighing against its irresponsible manner. It was clear that Warburg himself, not some alleged American invasion, was the real issue. The City establishment thought he had failed to play by accepted rules. Members of the establishment either had to join forces to defeat him, or he would wreck British industry.[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395877)
在公众心目中，仍然有个不言自明的道理：没有人能战胜商人银行联合的力量。Schroders 和 Helbert Wagg 站在了 Warburg 一边。除此之外，伦敦金融城团结在 British Aluminium 身后，组成了一个看似坚不可摧的方阵，其中包括 Hambros、Lazards、Morgan Grenfell、Flemings、Samuel Montagu 和 Brown Shipley。从 Hambros 和 Lazards 准备的一份备忘录来看，很明显，Warburg 那种不绅士的做法远比 Reynolds-Tube 提案被大肆宣扬的缺点更让这个团体恼火。这份内部文件承认了该报价的合理性，只是谴责其不负责任的方式。很明显，真正的症结在于 Warburg 本人，而非所谓的美国入侵。金融城的建制派认为他没有遵守公认的规则。建制派成员要么联合起来击败他，要么他就会摧毁英国工业。 [48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2395877)

The next day, these City men, who ordinarily negotiated unseen in their clubs, published the first defensive advertisement that had ever been used in a hostile takeover. The game was no longer being played in their preferred cloakroom style. In late December 1958, fourteen City institutions created a war chest of £7 million, with Morgan Grenfell chipping in £500,000. Where Lord Portal had been prepared to sell his company for 60 shillings a share, the City consortium now made a partial bid for British Aluminium at 82 shillings a share. This not only topped the Tube-Reynolds offer by 4 shillings but indirectly exposed the cheapness of the earlier deal.
次日，这些平日里惯于在俱乐部里暗中斡旋的伦敦金融城人士，发布了敌意收购史上第一则防御性广告。这场博弈不再以他们偏好的私密方式进行。1958 年 12 月下旬，十四家金融机构筹集了 700 万英镑的战争基金，其中摩根建富出资 50 万英镑。此前波特尔勋爵本打算以每股 60 先令出售公司，而金融城财团如今则以每股 82 先令对英国铝业发起部分收购——这不仅比管材-雷诺兹的出价高出 4 先令，也间接暴露了先前交易的低价。

Awed by this strength, the London *Times* referred to “an array of City institutions on a scale never before seen in a take-over battle.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396067) The *Daily Express* likewise trembled before the heroic show of firepower: “Lined up on the City side supporting British Aluminium are such famed financiers as Lords Bicester, Harcourt, Rennell, Astor, Glenconner, Kindersley, Cowdray, Poole, and Brand. . . . But as history has seen in the past when the big battalions of the City unite, they can almost be sure of victory.”[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396217) One paper toted up twenty-seven titles on the British Aluminium-Alcoa side, including a marquess, sixteen lords, ten knights, and—as if tossed in for good measure—the queen’s uncle.
《伦敦时报》为其声势所慑，称其为"收购战中前所未见的一众金融机构阵容"。《每日快报》同样在这英雄般的火力展示前战栗："在金融城一方支持英国铝业的，有比斯特勋爵、哈考特勋爵、雷内尔勋爵、阿斯特勋爵、格伦康纳勋爵、金德斯利勋爵、考德雷勋爵、普尔勋爵和布兰德勋爵等著名金融家……但正如历史所见，当金融城的雄师劲旅联合起来时，他们几乎总能稳操胜券。" [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396217) 有家报纸数了数英国铝业-美铝一方的头衔，竟多达二十七个，包括一位侯爵、十六位勋爵、十位爵士，以及——仿佛为了凑数——女王的叔父。

By New Year’s Eve, the British Aluminium side had two million shares and felt confident of victory. Lord Cobbold, governor of the Bank of England, and D. Heathcoat Amory, chancellor of the Exchequer, asked Warburg to desist, noting that Prime Minister Harold Macmillan concurred. But Warburg had coldly analyzed the situation and later said, “It was not a deed of genius at all; I had just mobilized big amounts of money for the cash purchases of my clients.”[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396369) Defying government pressure, Warburgs lifted its bid to 85 shillings a share and began huge share sweeps on the Stock Exchange, sometimes buying hundreds of thousands of shares per day. By January 9, 1959, Tube-Reynolds obtained over 50 percent of British Aluminium and declared victory.
到新年前夜，英国铝业公司一方已持有两百万股，对胜利胸有成竹。英格兰银行行长科博德勋爵与财政大臣 D.希思科特·阿莫里要求沃伯格收手，并告知首相哈罗德·麦克米伦也持相同立场。但沃伯格冷静分析了局势，事后坦言：“这根本谈不上神来之笔；我只是调动了大笔资金为客户进行现金收购。” [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396369) 沃伯格顶住政府压力，将出价提高到每股 85 先令，并在证券交易所发起大规模扫货，有时单日购入数十万股。至 1959 年 1 月 9 日，Tube-Reynolds 已拿下英国铝业公司超过 50%的股份，宣告胜利。

The City was stunned. It was an apocalyptic moment. At first, the merchant bankers refused to alter their style or acknowledge that things had changed. Lord Kindersley of Lazard said flatly, “I will not talk to that fellow” and would cross the street to avoid Warburg. The dazed elite couldn’t comprehend why the press and investors had lionized the outcast Warburg. Like Robert Young in his battle for the New York Central, Warburg realized the need to court public opinion as share ownership became dispersed. Henceforth, the City would shift from its opaque, secretive style to greater visibility. As one banker commented prophetically, “No company \[head\] whose shares are publicly quoted could sleep well from now on, because he must always wake up in the middle of the night and wonder who will make a raid on the company.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396514)
整个金融城都为之震惊，仿佛末日降临。起初，那些商业银行家们拒不改变作风，也不承认形势已然不同。Lazard 集团的肯德斯利勋爵直截了当地说：“我不会跟那家伙说话。”甚至宁愿绕道过街，也要避开沃伯格。茫然失措的精英们无法理解，为何媒体和投资者会对那个被排斥的沃伯格如此追捧。正如罗伯特·杨争夺纽约中央铁路控制权时那样，沃伯格意识到，随着股权日益分散，必须争取公众舆论的支持。从此以后，金融城将从其晦暗、隐秘的风格转向更加透明的姿态。正如一位银行家预言的那样：“从今往后，任何股票公开上市的公司老总都别想睡个安稳觉了，因为他总会在半夜惊醒，担心有人会对他的公司发起突袭。”

After a period of estrangement, Olaf Hambro went around to see Siegmund Warburg. Embracing him, Hambro cried out, “Siegmund, haven’t we been awful fools?”[53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396640) The bitterness persisted much longer at Morgan Grenfell, which had thought Warburg’s behavior monstrous and unforgivable. After all, if capital and cunning counted for more than contacts, what would happen to Morgan Grenfell? For an astonishing fifteen years, the firm refused to deal with Warburgs, even as the latter became London’s most innovative firm in the Euromarkets. Warburg made peace overtures and even asked Morgan Grenfell to share in a deal for Associated Electrical Industries. Morgan Grenfell refused and, far from appreciating the gesture, haughtily said it wanted to do the deal alone.
在闹了一阵别扭之后，奥尔 af·汉布罗主动去见了西格蒙德·沃伯格，一见面就拥抱住他，大声说道：“西格蒙德，咱俩是不是都蠢到家了？” [53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396640) 但摩根建富的怨恨却持续得更久，他们一直认为沃伯格的行径穷凶极恶、不可饶恕。毕竟，如果资本和谋略比人脉更重要，那摩根建富还怎么混？令人难以置信的是，在长达十五年的时间里，摩根建富拒绝与沃伯格家族有任何业务往来，即使后者已经成为伦敦欧洲市场上最具创新力的公司。沃伯格主动示好，甚至邀请摩根建富一起参与英国联合电气工业公司的一笔交易。但摩根建富不仅断然拒绝，还傲慢地表示他们要独自完成这笔交易，丝毫不领情。

It’s tempting to say Morgan Grenfell’s fate was decided by the aluminium war. For beneath the indignation flowed new subterranean currents. A group of Young Turks, notably Stephen Catto (son of Tom) and Tim Collins, son-in-law of Rufus Smith, felt the firm was stuck in suicidal snobbery. In many ways, they wanted to ape Warburg, not condemn him. “The aluminium war showed that Morgan Grenfell wasn’t aggressive enough,” said Stephen Catto. “It came as quite a shock here. We were outmaneuvered and demoralized. It was almost the first time and it had a marked effect.”[54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2396800)
人们很容易认为，摩根·格伦费尔的命运是由铝业战争决定的。因为在表面的愤慨之下，暗流早已涌动。一群年轻改革派——尤其是汤姆之子斯蒂芬·卡托和鲁弗斯·史密斯的女婿蒂姆·柯林斯——认为公司陷入了致命的势利心态中。在很多方面，他们想要效仿沃伯格，而非谴责他。斯蒂芬·卡托说：“铝业战争表明摩根·格伦费尔还不够激进。这在这里引起了相当大的震动。我们被智取，士气低落。这几乎是头一遭，而且影响深远。”

Within a decade, Morgan Grenfell would not only undertake but specialize in flamboyant takeovers and flaunt its transformation. It would learn to beat Warburg at his game and come to symbolize the new, aggressive way of doing business. Like Morgan Stanley in New York, Morgan Grenfell would show in bold relief the death of the sleepy old world of high finance and the dangerous birth of the new. As the firms that had profited most from old-fashioned relationship banking, the Morgan houses had the most to lose and would react to the threat in an unaccustomed, bare-knuckle style.
不出十年，摩根建富不仅会涉足张扬的并购业务，更会将其打造成自己的专长，并引以为傲地展示自身的蜕变。它将学会在沃伯格擅长的领域击败它，并最终成为那种咄咄逼人的新型商业模式的象征。如同纽约的摩根士丹利，摩根建富将以鲜明的方式宣告那个昏昏欲睡的高端金融旧世界的终结，以及充满危险的新世界的诞生。作为从传统关系型银行业务中获利最丰厚的机构，摩根家族的两家银行面临最大的损失风险，而它们将以一种前所未有的、毫不留情的凌厉姿态来应对这一威胁。
