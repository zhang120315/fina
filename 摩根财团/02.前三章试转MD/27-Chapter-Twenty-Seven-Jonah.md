# Chapter Twenty-Seven: Jonah

IN the late 1950s, it seemed the parade had passed J. P. Morgan and Company by and that the name would take on a venerable but slightly antiquated ring, as Rothschild and Baring had. It seemed to be a banking dynasty in terminal decline. While Morgan bankers stuck to their wholesale formula, the competition took banking to the masses. Such large commercial rivals as National City and Chase were raking in consumer deposits, invading shopping centers, and appealing to the new suburban middle class of the Eisenhower era. Bankers Trust, which had insisted on a \$5,000 minimum account, dropped the rule and went retail, too.
在 20 世纪 50 年代末，J·P·摩根公司似乎已被时代潮流甩在身后，这个名字可能会像罗斯柴尔德和巴林一样，带上一种尊贵却略显过时的气息。这家银行王朝看起来正走向末路。当摩根银行的 bankers 坚守批发业务模式时，竞争对手们已经将银行业务推向了大众。国民城市银行和大通银行等大型商业银行大举吸收个人存款，渗透进购物中心，吸引艾森豪威尔时期新兴的郊区中产阶级。而曾经坚持最低 5000 美元开户金额的银行家信托公司，也废除了这一规定，同样转向了零售业务。

Henry Clay Alexander, who succeeded George Whitney as chairman in 1955, saved Morgans from genteel oblivion. Despite a shared sense of the essence of banking, the two men were very different. Whitney was the East Coast patrician, while Alexander “was graced with an easy Southern affability, relaxed in conversation, intense and enthusiastic at business—Hollywood handsome with an unruly forelock,” recalled Jim Brugger, then the bank’s publicist.[1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397077) Both Whitney and Alexander were so handsome that when they appeared in public, women chased them down the block.
亨利·克莱·亚历山大（Henry Clay Alexander）于 1955 年接替乔治·惠特尼（George Whitney）出任董事长，将摩根银行从被优雅遗忘的边缘拯救了回来。尽管两人对银行业本质有着共同的理解，但他们截然不同。惠特尼是东海岸的贵族，而亚历山大则“拥有南方人那种随和的亲切感，谈吐轻松，在生意场上热情专注——好莱坞式的英俊，额前总有一绺不羁的头发，”当时银行的公关吉姆·布鲁格（Jim Brugger）回忆道。 [1](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397077) 惠特尼和亚历山大都非常英俊，以至于当他们出现在公共场合时，女人们会沿街追逐他们。

Henry Alexander was probably Wall Street’s most popular banker in the fifties. He appeared on the cover of *Time*, and his winning personality took some starch from the Morgan image. As a young Davis, Polk lawyer, he had been assigned to lack Morgan during the Nye “merchants-of-death” hearings. “I like that young man,” Jack had said. Those five words secured Alexander’s fortune. On Christmas Eve 1938, Jack invited him to become the first new partner since the Pecora hearings. “Think about it,” Jack said. “We will have a talk a month hence.”[2](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397251)Alexander agonized over whether to be a Morgan or a Davis, Polk partner. “You have been dealt two straight flushes,” a law partner said, “and you’ve got to pick between them.”[3](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397396) He chose Morgans and performed legal work for the bank’s incorporation. He was a protege of Lamont, who thought him precociously wise, and of Whitney, who said, “Henry’s so remarkably able.”[4](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397521)
在 50 年代，亨利·亚历山大很可能是华尔街最炙手可热的银行家。他登上了《时代》周刊的封面，他那迷人的个性为摩根的形象平添了几分亲和力。当年，作为 Davis Polk 律师事务所的一名年轻律师，他在奈伊“死亡商人”听证会期间被指派去协助摩根。“我喜欢那个年轻人，”杰克说。这五个字成就了亚历山大的命运。1938 年圣诞夜，杰克邀请他成为佩科拉听证会以来的第一位新合伙人。“考虑一下，”杰克说，“一个月后我们再谈。”亚历山大痛苦地纠结于成为摩根还是 Davis Polk 的合伙人。“你手里攥着两副同花顺，”一位律所合伙人说，“你必须从中选一个。”他最终选择了摩根，并为该银行的合并提供法律服务。他是拉蒙特的门生，拉蒙特认为他少年老成；也是惠特尼的门生，惠特尼评价说：“亨利的能力非常出众。”

Like Lamont, Alexander was a self-invented figure whose elegance appeared hereditary. Tall and slim with wavy hair and a weak chin, his dapper look was sometimes accentuated by a pocket handkerchief and homburg. Yet he was from Murfreesboro, Tennessee, the son of a grain-and-feed merchant. He attended public high school, Vanderbilt University, and Yale Law; he first learned law hanging about a sleepy southern courthouse. He had a politician’s versatility. Once on a visit to Tennessee, he chatted with a mule farmer who said afterward, “He is the nicest mule trader I ever met.”[5](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397653)
像拉蒙特一样，亚历山大也是个自我塑造的人物，他的优雅仿佛是骨子里带来的。他身材高挑，波浪卷发，下巴略显单薄，口袋里塞方巾、头戴霍姆堡毡帽时更显潇洒。然而他来自田纳西州的默弗里斯伯勒，是个粮饲商人的儿子。他读过公立高中、范德比尔特大学和耶鲁法学院；最初学法律是在南方一个慵懒的法院旁听。他有着政客般的多面性。一次回田纳西州时，他和一个骡农聊天，那人后来评价道：“他是我见过最会做骡子买卖的人。”

Alexander projected contradictory images. He was a Jacksonian Democrat by birthright, he said, yet a registered Republican. He favored sound, orthodox financial policy—as well as tax cuts to spur growth. As a Methodist with an Episcopalian wife (who was a former Powers model), he would say, “I’m a Methodist in town and an Episcopalian in the country.”[6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397803) This kept everyone thoroughly confused about his identity. Tutored in secrecy, Alexander wouldn’t name clients and once told a reporter, with excruciating circumlocution, that the number of Morgan clients was “more than half-way up to 10,000.”[7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397928)
亚历山大展现出的形象自相矛盾。他自称天生是杰克逊民主党人，却注册为共和党人；他主张稳健正统的财政政策，又支持减税以刺激增长。作为卫理公会信徒，他的妻子曾是 Powers 模特，信奉圣公会——他常打趣道：“我在城里是卫理公会派，到了乡下就成了圣公会派。” [6](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397803) 这番说辞让众人对他的身份更加摸不着头脑。他深谙保密之道，从不透露客户姓名，有次对记者绕了半天弯子，才憋出一句摩根客户数量“超过五千，正向一万迈进”。 [7](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2397928)

Alexander relaxed the bank’s pontifical image. He sailed a ten-foot dinghy, drove a Chevrolet station wagon, and bought suits off the rack. As American business power shifted toward the South and the West, the home base of many oil companies and defense contractors, it helped to have a chairman with a southern accent who could drum up business in Texas, California, and other places so long *terra incognita* for the bank. Alexander played the smart hick superbly. His occasional corn-pone patter—his sly, down-home aw-shucks manner—belied real sophistication. “When and if you decide you would like to borrow a little money,” he would tell corporate executives, “I hope you will not forget your country cousin at 23 Wall Street.”[8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398079) It was a shrewd way to disguise the fact that the bank badly needed new business.
亚历山大彻底重塑了这家银行曾经高高在上的形象。他亲自驾驶十英尺长的小艇，开雪佛兰旅行车，连西装都买现成的。随着美国商业重心向南方和西部——那些石油巨头和军工承包商云集之地——转移，一位带着南方口音、能在德克萨斯、加利福尼亚以及银行此前从未涉足之地拉拢生意的董事长，无疑成了巨大优势。亚历山大把"精明乡下人"的角色演绎得淋漓尽致。他偶尔冒出的乡村俚语，那种狡黠又朴拙的"哎呀，不值一提"做派，恰恰掩盖了真正的老谋深算。他会对公司高管们说："要是您哪天想借点小钱，千万别忘了您在华尔街 23 号的那位乡下亲戚。" [8](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398079) 这招着实高明，巧妙掩饰了银行急需新业务的事实。

During Eisenhower’s second term, the Morgan bank had excellent access to the White House. In early March 1956, Ike had wrestled with the decision of whether to keep Richard Nixon as his vice-president. A flurry of rumors reported that he would dump Nixon, who prepared to announce his retirement. Eisenhower made it the subject of a “stag dinner” and invited George Whitney to attend. Whitney recommended that Ike choose the older and more experienced Christian Herter as his running mate. Nixon, he said in a subsequent letter, could be better groomed as a future Republican leader in a high-appointed post—a tactful way of shoving him aside. In a reply marked “personal and confidential,” the president agreed, but added resignedly, “The attitude \[among politicians\] seems to be ’do the thing that seems most popular at this moment.’ ”[9](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398224)
在艾森豪威尔的第二个任期内，摩根银行与白宫关系密切。1956 年 3 月初，艾克曾就是否让理查德·尼克松继续担任副总统一事反复权衡。当时流言四起，称他将抛弃尼克松，而尼克松也准备宣布退出政坛。艾森豪威尔将此事作为一场“男士晚宴”的议题，并邀请了乔治·惠特尼出席。惠特尼建议艾克选择更年长、经验更丰富的克里斯蒂安·赫脱作为竞选搭档。他在随后的一封信中表示，尼克松更适合在高级任命职位上被培养为未来的共和党领袖——这是一种巧妙的边缘化方式。总统在标注为“个人机密”的回信中表示同意，但无奈地补充道：“（政客们）的态度似乎是‘此刻什么最受欢迎就做什么’。”

Henry Alexander was so popular at the White House that the press dubbed him “Ike’s banker.” Although Alexander was the most domestically oriented chairman in Morgan history—he came in after the foreign loans of the twenties and never lived abroad—he fully internalized the Morgan identification with Britain. This was patent during the Suez affair. On July 26, 1956, Egypt’s prime minister Gamal Abdel Nasser nationalized the Suez Canal. The next day, the British prime minister, Sir Anthony Eden, informed Eisenhower that Britain was drawing up military contingency plans to reclaim the canal. By early November, Britain, France, and Israel invaded Egypt, to the great dismay of Eisenhower and his secretary of state, John Foster Dulles.
亨利·亚历山大在白宫如此受欢迎，以至于媒体戏称他为“艾克的银行家”。尽管亚历山大是摩根历史上最专注于国内事务的董事长——他在 20 年代的外债业务之后上任，且从未在海外居住过——但他完全内化了摩根对英国的认同。这一点在苏伊士运河事件中表现得淋漓尽致。1956 年 7 月 26 日，埃及总理加麦尔·阿卜杜勒·纳赛尔将苏伊士运河收归国有。次日，英国首相安东尼·艾登爵士告知艾森豪威尔，英国正在制定夺回运河的军事应急计划。到 11 月初，英国、法国和以色列入侵埃及，这让艾森豪威尔及其国务卿约翰·福斯特·杜勒斯大为震惊。

The Suez affair produced a deep rift in the Atlantic alliance—always painful for the House of Morgan—and the bank tried to win back U.S. support for Britain. Speaking at the Executive’s Club of Chicago on December 7, Henry Alexander, in a rare bit of verbal pyrotechnics, conjured up a Nasser who “stirs the Arab world and breathes fire and damnation.” He argued that the Soviet Union planned to join with Nasser in strangling NATO through their joint control of Middle East oil. Alexander proposed an American doctrine for the Middle East like that which the United States had extended to protect Greece, Turkey, and Formosa. In his peroration, he urged that the United States get back on “speaking terms” with Great Britain and France. He said, “We must save our alliances. They are mainstays of our defense, the floodgates holding back the Communist tide.”[10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398421)
苏伊士事件在大西洋联盟中造成了深刻的裂痕——这对摩根家族（House of Morgan）来说始终是痛苦的——该银行试图挽回美国对英国的支持。12 月 7 日，亨利·亚历山大在芝加哥行政俱乐部（Executive’s Club of Chicago）发表演讲时，罕见地放了一通“言语烟火”，虚构了一个“搅动阿拉伯世界、口喷烈焰与诅咒”的纳赛尔（Nasser）。他声称，苏联计划与纳赛尔联手，通过共同控制中东石油来扼杀北约（NATO）。亚历山大提议，美国应像保护希腊、土耳其和福尔摩沙（Formosa）那样，为中东制定一个主义。在演讲结尾，他敦促美国与英法恢复“对话关系”。他说：“我们必须拯救我们的联盟。它们是我们防御的支柱，是阻挡共产主义潮水的闸门。” [10](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398421)

George Whitney, meanwhile, had always refrained from exploiting his friendship with Eisenhower; this modesty had enhanced his credibility. But on December 26, 1956, in an unusual step, he sent Ike a grave letter bluntly advocating a tougher approach toward Nasser:
与此同时，乔治·惠特尼一直避免利用他与艾森豪威尔的交情；这种谦逊反而提升了他的可信度。但在 1956 年 12 月 26 日，他破例给艾克写了一封措辞严峻的信，直截了当地主张对纳赛尔采取更强硬的手段：

> At some point somebody has got to tell \[Nasser\] where he gets off in no uncertain terms, taking the calculated risk of what this may blow up. Probably you have already done so; if not, I am afraid you might. Every day that goes by without some forward motion carries with it more serious risks. It is not only the financial plight of Western Europe, it is the injury to the prestige of the Western powers that to me is the most unfortunate repercussion. I am ready to assume the United States’ position has been improved with a good many people in Asia and Africa, but I am afraid that this may have been attained at an unprecedented cost to the Western world.[11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398634)
> 总得有人明确告诉\[纳赛尔\]他算老几，并承担这可能会引爆局势的风险。或许你已经这么做过了；若还没有，我担心你终究得这么做。每拖延一天，没有采取任何行动，都会带来更严重的风险。这不仅关乎西欧的财政困境，更关乎西方大国的威望受损——在我看来，这才是最不幸的后果。我承认美国在亚非许多民众心中的地位有所提升，但我担心这种提升是以西方世界前所未有的代价换来的。 [11](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398634)

Eisenhower showed the letter to Dulles, who knew Whitney well. The secretary of state reminded Eisenhower that the Morgan bank was the fiscal agent for the British government and submitted that Whitney’s sources were “somewhat biased.”[12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398837) Ike sidestepped Whitney’s letter. By the time he replied, he reported that he had just heard of Anthony Eden’s resignation as a result of England and France’s lack of success in the Suez affair. Then he abruptly turned to personal pleasantries.
艾森豪威尔将那封信交给了与惠特尼相熟的杜勒斯。这位国务卿提醒艾森豪威尔，摩根银行是英国政府的财务代理人，并指出惠特尼的消息来源“有些偏颇”。 [12](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2398837) 艾克对惠特尼的信函避而不谈。待他回信时，先是提及刚听闻安东尼·艾登因英法在苏伊士事件中受挫而辞职，随后便话锋一转，聊起了私人闲话。

Unlike the situation of the 1920s, the Morgan influence at the White House was vastly disproportionate to the bank’s slender resources. During the fifties, the bank seemed to shrink, if only because its rivals grew so rapidly. It had to cobble together syndicates to serve large clients, such as France. Nevertheless, Alexander stayed aloof from branch banking and the spree of banking mergers. The old Wall Street vanished as musty, dignified old banks were snapped up by hungry retail giants. The First National Bank of New York—the bank of Pierpont’s pal George F. Baker—was illustrative of the situation. Refusing to hustle for business and demanding client introductions, it was dying with dignity, like a fussy old dowager, and was acquired by National City. Spurned by Morgans, Chase took over the Bank of the Manhattan Company; Chemical acquired New York Trust; and Manufacturers Trust later merged with Hanover Bank. Over a third of New York’s banks vanished. They had to merge if they were to grow to a size commensurate with their multinational clients.
与 1920 年代的情况不同，摩根在白宫的影响力与其微薄的资源极不相称。五十年代期间，这家银行似乎在萎缩——哪怕只是因为其竞争对手增长过快。它不得不拼凑银团来服务大客户，比如法国。尽管如此，亚历山大依然对分行银行业和银行并购热潮敬而远之。那些陈旧、高贵的银行被饥渴的零售巨头吞并，古老的华尔街就此消失。纽约第一国民银行——皮尔庞特的老友乔治·F·贝克旗下的银行——就是这种情况的缩影。它不屑于主动招揽业务，要求客户须经介绍才能进门，像一位挑剔的老贵妇般体面地走向衰亡，最终被国民城市银行收购。被摩根冷落的化学银行收购了曼哈顿公司银行；化学银行又吞并了纽约信托；而制造商信托后来与汉诺威银行合并。纽约超过三分之一的银行就此消失。 如果他们想要发展到与跨国客户相称的规模，就必须合并。

It was a brand-new age of banking, one with a less austere image. The stereotypical banker had been a grumpy Scrooge who closely scrutinized loan applications and was congenitally biased toward rejecting them. That befit a historic situation of scarce capital rationed by bankers. But the situation was reversed in the Casino Age, which was characterized by new financial intermediaries and superabundant capital. The banker now evolved into an amiable salesman who belonged to the Rotary Club, played golf, and smiled in television ads. Where banks once resembled forbidding fortresses or courthouses flanked by Corinthian columns, they switched now to inviting exteriors. In 1954, Manufacturers Trust opened a Fifth Avenue branch that wooed pedestrians. Its thirty-ton safe sat behind the bank’s plate-glass window so that strollers could peer through its open door. Inside the new banks, marble corridors and tellers’ cages gave way to soothing pastel shades, open counters, and soft furniture. Chase launched its advertising campaign with the slogan “You Have a Friend at Chase Manhattan.” For elitist Morgan bankers, this was too much. “You can’t provide custom tailoring to a mass market,” sniffed Henry Alexander.
这是一个银行业的新时代，形象不再那么刻板严肃。传统银行家的形象就像个暴躁的吝啬鬼，仔仔细细审视每一份贷款申请，天生就倾向于拒绝。这符合历史上资本稀缺、由银行家配给的局面。但在“赌场时代”，情况发生了逆转——新兴金融中介涌现，资本变得极度充裕。银行家摇身一变，成了亲切和善的推销员，加入扶轮社、打高尔夫、在电视广告里微笑。过去银行常像由科林斯柱廊环绕、令人望而生畏的堡垒或法院大楼，如今则换上吸引人的外观。1954 年，Manufacturers Trust 在第五大道开设分行，吸引路人驻足。它那三十吨重的保险柜就摆在银行的落地玻璃窗后面，行人透过敞开的门就能看到。在新式银行内部，大理石走廊和出纳员隔间让位于柔和的色调、开放式柜台和舒适的家具。 大通银行推出了以“你在大通曼哈顿有位朋友”为口号的广告宣传活动。对于高傲的摩根银行家们来说，这简直太过分了。“你不可能为大众市场提供量身定制的服务，”亨利·亚历山大嗤之以鼻。

Showing their new subordination to corporate clients, many Wall Street banks moved their headquarters to midtown. Now past were the days when supercilious bankers expected company chairmen to troop to them. Between 1950 and 1965, hardly any new construction occurred on Wall Street. Chase, a large downtown landlord, feared property values might fall. To protect the bank’s interests and restore faith in Wall Street, John J. McCloy and David Rockefeller worked out a deal with real estate mogul William Zeckendorf to create Chase Manhattan Plaza, one block from Wall Street.
为了向企业客户示好，许多华尔街银行将总部迁至中城区。过去那些目中无人的银行家们指望公司董事长们亲自登门拜访的日子一去不复返了。1950 年至 1965 年间，华尔街几乎没有任何新建工程。作为下城区的最大地主之一，大通银行担心房产价值会下跌。为了保护银行利益并重振华尔街信心，约翰·J·麦克洛伊和大卫·洛克菲勒与房地产大亨威廉·泽肯多夫达成协议，在距离华尔街一个街区的地方兴建了大通曼哈顿广场。

As part of this package, Chase had to find a buyer for its thirty-eight-story tower at 15 Broad Street. The natural buyer was the adjoining House of Morgan. When Zeckendorf broached the subject with Alexander in 1954, they had a highly revealing conversation:
作为这一揽子交易的一部分，大通银行必须为其位于布罗德街 15 号的三十八层大厦寻找买家。最自然的买家自然是隔壁的摩根家族。1954 年，当泽肯多夫向亚历山大提起这件事时，两人之间进行了一次极富深意的对话：

> “We’re not real-estate people,” Alexander said. “We already have this beautiful little corner here. We play a special role in finance; we are not big, but we are powerful and influential, we have relationships. Furthermore, we don’t want to be big and don’t need the space.”
> “我们不是搞房地产的，”亚历山大说，“我们已经有了这个漂亮的小角落。我们在金融领域扮演着独特的角色；我们规模不大，但我们有实力、有影响力，有人脉。而且，我们不想变得庞大，也不需要更多空间。”

> “Henry,” said Zeckendorf, “you’re going to get married.”
> “亨利，”泽肯多夫说，“你们要‘联姻’了。”

> “What?”

> “Someday you are going to merge with another bank, a big one. When you do, this property will be in the nature of a dowry coming with a bride; you will be able to make a better deal with your partner.”
> “总有一天，你们会和另一家银行合并——一家大银行。到那时，这处房产就像新娘带来的嫁妆；你能借此与你的伙伴谈成更有利的条件。”

> “Morgan will never merge.”
> “摩根永远不会合并。”

> “Well, that’s just my prediction.”[13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399017)
> “嗯，这只是我的预测罢了。” [13](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399017)

Zeckendorf would later remind Alexander of this talk.
后来，泽肯多夫还跟亚历山大提起过这段对话。

Bankers who had survived the Depression shied away from property speculation, and Alexander bargained fiercely for 15 Broad. He got it for \$21.25 million with a 3’/2-percent mortgage—terms so unfavorable for Chase that it later bought out the mortgage. Fifteen Broad was then joined internally to 23 Wall, which became the larger building’s triumphal entryway. The flamboyant Zeckendorf used the deal to conquer Morgan’s aversion to real estate lending and ended up getting loans from the bank. He later told of how a journalist he met while flying back to New York from a trip had coaxed him into stopping *en route* to attend a wedding at a nudist camp. By the time he arrived at a 23 Wall meeting, press photos had appeared of him with the wedding revelers. He thought this publicity might end his relationship with the decorous Morgans. Instead, every high officer, including Henry Alexander and George Whitney, turned out to hear the juicy details.
熬过大萧条的银行家们对房地产投机避之不及，亚历山大在 15 Broad 的收购谈判中寸步不让。最终他以 2125 万美元成交，附带 3.5%利率的按揭贷款——条件对 Chase 极为不利，银行后来不得不买断这笔贷款。15 Broad 随后与 23 Wall 内部打通，成为这栋主楼恢弘的入户门厅。浮夸的泽肯多夫借此交易瓦解了摩根家族对房地产贷款的抵触情绪，最终成功从该银行获得贷款。他后来讲述，某次出差飞回纽约途中偶遇一位记者，对方哄他在途中停留参加天体营婚礼。等他赶到 23 Wall 开会时，他与婚礼狂欢者的合影已经见诸报端。他本以为这桩八卦会断送他与恪守礼节的摩根家族的关系。谁知连亨利·亚历山大和乔治·惠特尼在内的高管悉数到场，争相打听香艳细节。

Many Morgan people opposed a merger because they liked working in a small, paternalistic bank with terrific perks; they thought a merger would cheapen the genuine article. There was a deeper dilemma: if the bank merged with a bigger bank to increase capital—the only sensible reason for doing so—it would become the junior partner, and J. P. Morgan and Company would effectively cease to exist. Finally, though, a decision would have to be reached. But even in late 1958, Alexander was still bluffing about the bank’s self-sufficiency: “Some mergers are a good thing. But while I wouldn’t say it can’t happen here, we have no desire to merge. We’ve been doing very well, thank you, sticking to our last.”[14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399188) He told people, “We don’t have the urge to merge.”
许多摩根员工反对合并，因为他们喜欢在一家规模不大、充满家长式作风、福利优厚的小银行工作；他们认为合并会降低这份"真材实料"的价值。更深层的困境在于：如果银行为了增加资本而与更大的银行合并——这是唯一合理的理由——它将成为次要合伙人，J. P. Morgan and Company 实际上将不复存在。不过，最终还是要做出决定。但即使在 1958 年底，亚历山大依然虚张声势地宣称银行能够自给自足："有些合并是好事。但我不会说这里不可能发生，我们并没有合并的意愿。我们一直做得很好，谢谢，我们坚持本业。" [14](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399188) 他告诉人们："我们没有合并的冲动。"

Henry Alexander solved the problem with brilliance and extraordinary luck. Around the corner at 140 Broadway stood the fat, sleepy, dowdy Guaranty Trust. Long on capital and short on talent, it was the mirror image of Morgans. Its huge lending limit was larger than that of all the Chicago Loop banks combined. A former Money Truster, it had been a Morgan ward after its disastrous sugar lending in the early 1920s. After merging in 1929 with the National Bank of Commerce—once known as Pierpont Morgan’s bank—it became New York’s second largest bank. In the 1930s, George Whitney chaired its trust committee and Tom Lamont its executive committee. It was a blue-chip bank with almost all of America’s top-one-hundred companies as customers. “We used to think of Morgans as a nice small bank,” remarked Guido Ver-beck, then a Guaranty officer. “Because of their lending limits, when they participated in large loans, they could only take a small share and they were very worried about it.”[15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399340)
亨利·亚历山大凭借其非凡的才智和罕见的运气，成功解决了这个难题。就在百老汇 140 号拐角处，肥硕、慵懒、邋遢的 Guaranty Trust 银行静立在那里。它资本雄厚但人才匮乏，与摩根银行形成了鲜明对比。其巨大的贷款额度甚至超过了芝加哥卢普区所有银行的总和。这家银行曾是货币信托公司，在上世纪 20 年代初的灾难性食糖贷款之后，它成了摩根银行的附属机构。1929 年与曾被称为皮尔庞特·摩根银行的国家商业银行合并后，它成为了纽约第二大银行。20 世纪 30 年代，乔治·惠特尼担任其信托委员会主席，汤姆·拉蒙特担任执行委员会主席。这是一家蓝筹银行，客户几乎囊括了美国所有前一百强企业。“我们过去认为摩根是一家小而美的银行，”时任 Guaranty 银行官员的 Guido Ver-beck 评价道，“由于贷款额度的限制，当它们参与大额贷款时，只能分得一小部分，它们对此非常担忧。” [15](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399340)

Guaranty’s chairman was J. Luther Cleveland. An old-school banker, he had rimless spectacles, neatly brushed hair, and a somber mien. Curt and humorless, he tried to run the whole bank, and his autocratic style prompted an exodus of talented people. He was the imperious Mr. Cleveland to subordinates, and grown men quailed in his presence. His own son would pop up like a jack-in-the-box when he entered the room. Cleveland would let visitors wait in his outer office, then grill them when they came inside. Despite shareholder discontent and sluggish business, he snorted at the idea of branch offices and small checking accounts.
担保公司的主席是 J. Luther Cleveland。他是个老派银行家，戴着无边眼镜，头发梳得一丝不苟，神情阴郁。他说话生硬，毫无幽默感，试图一手掌控整个银行，这种专横作风导致大批人才流失。下属对他敬畏有加，称他为威严的克利夫兰先生，连成年男子在他面前都噤若寒蝉。他儿子一见他进屋，就像弹簧玩偶般猛地弹起来。克利夫兰总让访客在外间办公室候着，等人进来后再严加盘问。尽管股东不满、业务低迷，他对开设分行和小额支票账户的想法嗤之以鼻。

J. Luther Cleveland was an expert practitioner of relationship banking. He sat in a gloomy office, a dark, sleep-inducing room, attending to a single document on his desk. “It was a list of ten names,” recalled A. Bruce Brackenridge, then with Guaranty and later a group executive at Morgan Guaranty. “These were ten very important clients to the bank. He made sure that he called them periodically to let them know he was interested in their business.”[16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399498) A former Oklahoma oil banker, Cleveland had a powerful array of oil clients, including Cities Service and Aramco, the four-member consortium (today’s Exxon, Mobil, Texaco, and Chevron) with exclusive rights to pump Saudi Arabian oil on very sweet terms. To stay on good terms with his board, he played poker with the directors. One oil director even packed a rare \$10-thousand bill in his wallet, always ready for a quick game. The whole operation, ex-employees allege, was riddled with cronyism. “The only loan I ever saw Cleveland approve was a stock option loan to a crony of his,” said a Guaranty banker. “It was later criticized by bank examiners.” Adding to Guaranty’s troubles was a paralyzing conservatism left over from the sugar debacle. “It was more important not to lose money than to make money,” remarked Frank Rosenbach, then a Guaranty credit analyst.[17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399664)
J. Luther Cleveland 是一位关系银行业务的资深高手。他的办公室幽暗压抑，让人昏昏欲睡，他坐在里面，只专注于桌上的一份文件。“那是一张列着十个名字的清单，”A. Bruce Brackenridge 回忆道，他当时在 Guaranty 任职，后来成为 Morgan Guaranty 的高管。“这十个人对银行而言至关重要。他会定期给他们打电话，让他们知道他对他们的业务很上心。” [16](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399498) Cleveland 曾是俄克拉荷马州的石油银行家，手下有一批重量级石油客户，包括 Cities Service 和 Aramco——这个由四家公司组成的财团（即如今的埃克森、美孚、德士古和雪佛龙），以极其优厚的条件独享沙特阿拉伯石油的开采权。为了与董事会保持良好关系，他时常陪董事们打扑克。有位石油董事甚至随身携带一张价值一万美元的稀有钞票，随时准备来一场速战速决的牌局。前雇员们声称，整个运作体系充斥着任人唯亲的裙带关系。 "我唯一见过克利夫兰批准的贷款，是给他的一个亲信提供的股票期权贷款，"一位 Guaranty 银行家说道。"后来这笔贷款还遭到了银行审查员的批评。"令 Guaranty 雪上加霜的是，从糖业危机中延续下来的那种墨守成规的保守作风。"在当时，不赔钱比赚钱更重要，"时任 Guaranty 信贷分析师的 Frank Rosenbach 评论道。 [17](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399664)

Eventually Cleveland’s monstrous ego precipitated a board rebellion. When a director asked who could replace him, Cleveland thundered, “Nobody!” So the board opened merger talks with Henry Alexander in order to dump Cleveland. The last straw came when Ford Motor, dismayed by Guaranty’s handling of its pension fund, switched the fund to Morgans. The board told Cleveland he couldn’t be doing a very good job if he couldn’t keep his largest account. At first, Guaranty’s board came to 23 Wall with a proposal for a new bank called Guaranty Morgan—an insufferable thought to Alexander. A year later, in December 1958, with mounting frustration over Cleveland, the board swallowed hard and consented to Morgan Guaranty. When the autocratic Cleveland assembled his vice-presidents to break the news, it was the only meeting of the bank’s officers anyone could remember having ever taken place.
最终，克利夫兰那膨胀到极点的自我引发了董事会的反叛。当一位董事问谁能接替他时，克利夫兰咆哮道：“没人！”于是，董事会为了甩掉克利夫兰，开启了与亨利·亚历山大的合并谈判。压垮骆驼的最后一根稻草是，福特汽车对 Guaranty 银行管理其养老基金的表现大为不满，转而将基金交给了摩根银行。董事会告诉克利夫兰，如果他连最大的客户都留不住，那他的工作肯定干得不怎么样。起初，Guaranty 的董事会带着成立“Guaranty 摩根”这家新银行的提案来到华尔街 23 号——这对亚历山大来说简直无法忍受。一年后的 1958 年 12 月，随着对克利夫兰的不满愈演愈烈，董事会咬牙同意合并为摩根 Guaranty。当专横的克利夫兰召集副总裁们宣布这个消息时，那是人们记忆中该银行唯一一次举行全体高管会议。

In taking over a bank four times the size of J. P. Morgan and Company, the press likened Morgans to Jonah swallowing the whale. Alexander had engineered the dream deal. Guaranty was strong in railroads and public utilities. While J. P. Morgan was the lead bank for U.S. Steel, Guaranty had Bethlehem Steel. While Morgan had Kennecott Copper, Guaranty had Anaconda. While Morgan was peerless in the northeastern United States and Western Europe, Guaranty was well-connected in the South, the oil patch, the Middle East, and Eastern Europe. It had historic branches in London, Paris, and Brussels, having been the U.S. Treasury’s agent in Europe during World War I. Guaranty had provided financing for Thomas Watson’s IBM in the 1920s, and several of its executives had grown rich investing in the company. It held more American Express deposits than any other bank. And it claimed the account of Huntington Hartford and the A&P. What a prize\!
在接管一家规模相当于摩根大通四倍的银行时，媒体将摩根比作约拿吞鲸。亚历山大促成了这桩梦幻般的交易。担保信托在铁路和公用事业领域根基深厚。摩根是美国钢铁的首席银行，而担保信托则握有伯利恒钢铁；摩根拥有肯尼科特铜业，担保信托则掌握阿纳康达；摩根在美国东北部和西欧无可匹敌，担保信托则在南方、石油产区、中东和东欧人脉广泛。它在伦敦、巴黎和布鲁塞尔设有历史悠久的支行，一战期间曾是美国财政部在欧洲的代理行。20 世纪 20 年代，担保信托为托马斯·沃森的 IBM 提供了融资，其多名高管因投资该公司而致富。它持有的美国运通存款超过任何其他银行，还拥有亨廷顿·哈特福德及 A&P 的账户。这是何等珍贵的战利品！

On Wall Street, people said that Guaranty had really merged with Henry Alexander. When Bill Zeckendorf came to congratulate him, Alexander said, “You know, I’ve often thought of that conversation we had and how right you were.” “I wasn’t right, Henry,” Zeckendorf replied; “I was wrong.” “How so?” asked Alexander. “You’re not the bride,” Zeckendorf answered.[18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399824)
在华尔街，人们都说担保信托公司其实是和亨利·亚历山大合并了。比尔·泽肯多夫前来祝贺时，亚历山大说：“你知道吗，我时常想起我们那次谈话，你说得太对了。”“我没错，亨利，”泽肯多夫答道，“是我错了。”“这话怎么说？”亚历山大问。“因为你不是新娘子。”泽肯多夫回答。 [18](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399824)

Alexander chaired the merged bank while Luther Cleveland played almost no part, retiring after a year. Tommy S. Lamont and Henry P. Davison, Jr., became vice-chairmen, with Dale Sharp as president—the sole Guaranty person to retain a top post. While 23 Wall and 15 Broad were refurbished for the merged bank, Alexander and the others temporarily moved into Guaranty’s offices at 140 Broadway. Far from feeling defeated or humiliated, the Guaranty troops in the trenches felt liberated by the advancing Morgan army. The one grievous error Alexander made was not notifying Morgan Grenfell of the merger until an hour before it was publicly announced. It was a terrible blow to the London bank, especially since Guaranty had a large, competitive London office.
亚历山大主持了合并后的银行，而卢瑟·克利夫兰几乎未参与其中，一年后便退休。汤米·S·拉蒙特和小亨利·P·戴维森担任副董事长，戴尔·夏普出任总裁——他是唯一保留高级职位的信孚银行人士。当华尔街 23 号和百老汇 15 号办公楼为合并后的银行翻新时，亚历山大及其他高层暂迁至信孚银行位于百老汇 140 号的办公室。信孚银行的一线员工非但没有感到挫败或屈辱，反而因摩根大军的进驻而如释重负。亚历山大犯下的严重错误是，直到合并消息公开前一小时才通知摩根格伦费尔。这对伦敦分行造成了沉重打击，尤其因为信孚银行在伦敦拥有一家规模庞大且极具竞争力的办事处。

After the merger was consummated on April 24, 1959, Alexander summoned the combined staff and indoctrinated its members with Morgan groupthink: “I want all of you to know—as the relatively fewer Morgan people here know—that an important element of your career path will be how well you train the people underneath you to replace you.”[19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399995) This close-knit corporate culture, which stressed the group over the individual, would distinguish Morgan Guaranty from other Wall Street banks, which functioned as collections of contending egos.
1959 年 4 月 24 日合并完成后，亚历山大召集全体职员，向他们灌输摩根的团队思维：“我希望各位明白——正如相对少数的摩根老员工所知——你们职业发展的重要因素之一，就是如何培养下属接替自己的位置。” [19](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2399995) 这种强调集体高于个人的紧密企业文化，使摩根担保公司与其他华尔街银行截然不同——后者不过是各路自负者的角斗场。

Even with swollen ranks, Alexander kept up the traditional meetings with department heads. Although Morgans had been stingy with titles, Alexander liberally handed out promotions in order to smooth relations with Guaranty officers. In merging the two banks, petty problems of style proved most intractable. There was prolonged squabbling about a typographical style for the stationery. Since both banks used mono-grammed silver in their dining room, weighty talks occurred over silverware and matchbook covers.
即使队伍不断壮大，亚历山大仍坚持与各部门负责人定期会面。虽然摩根斯在头衔上颇为吝啬，但亚历山大为了缓和与担保公司高管的关系，大方地给予了晋升。在合并两家银行的过程中，最为棘手的竟是风格上的琐碎问题。双方就信纸的字体样式争执不休。由于两家银行都在餐厅使用刻有各自字母的银器，关于银餐具和火柴盒封面的谈判也变得异常沉重。

In April 1960, Junius S. Morgan celebrated the merger with a luncheon for eight hundred people at his North Shore mansion, catered by Louis Sherry’s. Jack’s elder son was even less suited for banking than his brother, Harry, and had remained in the business out of family loyalty. The colossal Morgan energies had petered out in this pleasant but somewhat ineffectual generation. Junius, a commodore of the New York Yacht Club, had yearned to be a marine architect, and his home was full of model ships in glass. Generous, charming, but lacking ambition, he’d become another Morgan male lashed to the wheel of the family dynasty. Though he put on pinstripes and fedora each morning, he never quite looked the part. “Junius was the nicest man you’ve ever known,” a colleague remembered. “But he should have been in the Navy. He didn’t know anything about banking and it was pitiful to watch him.”
1960 年 4 月，朱尼厄斯·S·摩根在他位于北岸的豪宅里举办了一场午宴，宴请八百人，宴席由 Louis Sherry's 餐厅承办。杰克的长子比他弟弟哈里更不适合做银行，完全是出于家族忠诚才一直留在这个行业。摩根家族那股巨大的能量，到了这一代，在这群性情温和却有些无所作为的后辈身上，已然消退殆尽。朱尼厄斯是纽约游艇俱乐部的准将，他梦想成为一名船舶设计师，家中摆满了玻璃罩中的船模。他慷慨大方、魅力十足，但缺乏野心，最终成了又一个被家族王朝的船舵牢牢捆住的摩根男性。尽管每天早晨他照样穿上细条纹西装、戴上软呢帽，可怎么看都不像那个角色。“朱尼厄斯是你见过的最和善的人，”一位同事回忆道，“但他本该去海军的。他对银行业一窍不通，看着他都让人觉得可怜。”

That luncheon would be Junius’s farewell to the bank. Tall, and handsome in an old patched jacket, he greeted his guests in the doorway of his forty-room stone mansion, Salutation, a place of faded elegance and English furnishings. Seven massive glazed Ming pottery figures stood in the main hall’s niches. Shaking hands, Junius stood by his wife, Louise, whose cardigan had a hole in it. Described by some family members as artistic and eccentric—by others as pushy and spoiled—Louise yearned to “touch up” John Singer Sargent’s portrait of Jessie Morgan. She bred golden Labradors, and dozens of them ran about the tents and tables, the twenty acres of gardens, the tennis courts, and the swimming pool. Six months later, at age sixty-eight, Junius died from a sudden attack of ulcers while on a hunting trip in Ontario.
那顿午宴是朱尼厄斯告别银行的最后场合。他身形高大，穿着缀补丁的旧夹克，站在四十个房间的石砌宅邸“问候庄”门廊前迎客——这座宅邸陈设英式家具，透着褪色的优雅。大厅壁龛里陈列着七尊厚重的明代彩釉陶俑。朱尼厄斯与妻子露易丝并肩而立，握手寒暄，露易丝的羊毛衫上还破了个洞。在亲友口中，露易丝被描述为“艺术而古怪”，也有人说她“跋扈又娇纵”。她总想给约翰·辛格·萨金特为杰西·摩根绘制的肖像画“润色”。她饲养的金毛猎犬足有数十只，在帐篷餐桌间、二十英亩花园里、网球场和泳池边四处奔跑。六个月后，六十八岁的朱尼厄斯在安大略省狩猎时突发溃疡，猝然离世。

By merging with Guaranty, the House of Morgan regained its status as the world’s largest wholesale bank. Suddenly flush, with over \$4 billion in deposits, it now stood fourth in size behind First National City, Chase Manhattan, and Bank of America. But this didn’t tell the whole story of its corporate strength. It had an unmatched number of corporate accounts, ten thousand including ninety-seven of the hundred biggest U.S. companies. By the mid-1960s, the newly merged bank would do more corporate lending yearly than the next five competitors combined.
通过与 Guaranty 的合并，摩根财团重新夺回了全球最大批发银行的地位。其存款额突然激增至超过 40 亿美元，规模跃居第四，仅次于第一国民城市银行、大通曼哈顿银行和美国银行。但这并未完全展现其企业实力——它拥有无与伦比的企业客户数量，多达一万家，其中囊括了美国百强企业中的 97 家。到 20 世纪 60 年代中期，这家新合并的银行每年的企业贷款额将超过排名其后五家竞争对手的总和。

The new bank produced fears of a sort missing since the New Deal. But they were expressed by other banks, not by Washington. Twenty years before, a Morgan-Guaranty merger would have raised impassioned shouts of protest in the populist heartland. Now there were only mild peeps, notably from Texas Congressman Wright Patman, who wanted to stop the merger on antitrust grounds. Approving it, New York State banking authorities noted certain altered facts of the Casino Age: corporations could now bypass banks and turn to life-insurance companies for capital, raise money through bond issues, or finance expansion from retained earnings. As banks lost their special position as providers of capital, the old fears of excessive bank power disappeared as a major issue in American politics.
这家新银行引发的恐慌程度，自新政时代以来前所未见。但表达这些担忧的不是华盛顿，而是其他银行。二十年前，摩根与担保信托的合并会在民粹主义腹地激起愤怒的抗议声浪，如今却只有几声微弱的反对——主要是来自得克萨斯州众议员赖特·帕特曼，他试图以反垄断为由阻止合并。纽约州银行监管机构批准合并时，指出赌场时代的一些现实已经改变：企业如今可以绕过银行，向人寿保险公司寻求资本、通过发行债券筹资，或利用留存收益来支撑扩张。随着银行失去了作为资本提供者的特殊地位，昔日对银行权力过大的恐惧已不再是美国政治中的重大议题。

At first, the Kennedy years looked auspicious for Morgans. Although his father had been snubbed by Jack Morgan and the financial establishment, President John F. Kennedy wanted to court Wall Street to counteract his slim victory over Nixon. “He was also financially conservative,” remarked C. Douglas Dillon. “A lot of people didn’t realize that. I think it was the influence of his father.”[20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400157) He turned to Robert Lovett, then of Brown Brothers Harriman, for advice on cabinet selections. Lovett suggested John J. McCloy, Douglas Dillon, or Henry Alexander for Treasury secretary. Apparently Alexander had the appointment sewn up but then made a strategic blunder. After Kennedy spent an hour with him during the campaign, Alexander declared his support for Nixon. “I don’t think there is any question that the head of the Morgan bank . . . would have received the job,” said Robert Kennedy of Alexander’s faux pas. “Jack felt that this was a personal insult.”[21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400319) Dillon won the job. Alexander probably wouldn’t have fit into the Kennedy cabinet anyhow. Even as cabinet selections were being considered, he was telling bankers, apropos of Nixon’s defeat, “Let’s not, as businessmen, wall ourselves off or sulk in our tents.”[22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400507)
起初，肯尼迪时代对摩根家族来说似乎颇为有利。尽管老摩根曾冷落过老约瑟夫·肯尼迪，但约翰·F·肯尼迪总统为了弥补以微弱优势击败尼克松的局面，仍希望拉拢华尔街。C·道格拉斯·狄龙评论道：“他在财政上也持保守态度。很多人没意识到这点，我认为这受了他父亲的影响。” [20](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400157) 他转而向布朗兄弟哈里曼公司的罗伯特·洛维特咨询内阁人选。洛维特推荐了约翰·J·麦克洛伊、道格拉斯·狄龙或亨利·亚历山大担任财政部长。亚历山大本已稳拿这个职位，却犯了个战略错误——肯尼迪竞选期间与他交谈一小时后，亚历山大竟公开支持尼克松。罗伯特·肯尼迪谈到亚历山大的失误时说：“毫无疑问，摩根银行的掌门人本应得到这个职位……杰克觉得这是对他个人的侮辱。” [21](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400319) 最终狄龙赢得了这个职位。 亚历山大很可能并不适合肯尼迪的内阁。就在内阁人选还在斟酌之际，他却在谈及尼克松败选时对银行家们说：“我们作为商人，别把自己封闭起来，也别躲在小帐篷里生闷气。” [22](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400507)

Alexander was drawn into one historic episode in the Kennedy White House, however—JFK’s confrontation with U.S. Steel chairman Roger M. Blough over a steel-price increase in 1962. The administration had applied pressure to the steelworkers’ union to accede to a moderate wage settlement in exchange for price restraint by management. So Kennedy felt double-crossed when Blough came to him on April 10, and informed him of a 3.5-percent price increase. This was the betrayal that prompted Kennedy’s famous outburst: “My father always told me that all businessmen were sons of bitches, but I never believed it until now.”[23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400660)
不过，亚历山大还是卷入了肯尼迪白宫的一段历史插曲——1962 年，JFK 就钢铁涨价一事与美国钢铁公司董事长 Roger M. Blough 正面交锋。当时政府向钢铁工人工会施压，要求他们接受温和的薪资协议，以此换取资方在价格上的克制。因此，当 Blough 在 4 月 10 日来找肯尼迪，告知他钢铁价格将上涨 3.5%时，肯尼迪感觉自己被出卖了。正是这种背叛引出了肯尼迪那句著名的怒吼：“我父亲一直告诉我，所有商人都是狗娘养的，但直到现在我才相信。” [23](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400660)

While Kennedy started a campaign against the price increase and resorted to harsh invective against businessmen, the administration cast about for more discreet ways to influence U.S. Steel. Henry Alexander was on the company’s board, and John M. Meyer, Jr., of Morgans was on its executive committee. Robert V. Roosa, under secretary of the Treasury and former Brown Brothers Harriman partner, telephoned Alexander and asked him to appeal to Blough. The House of Morgan no longer had the mythical power to rescind a U.S. Steel increase, but Alexander might have gotten Blough to soften his anti-administration rhetoric at a news conference during the standoff. After Blough finally responded to Kennedy’s pressure and rolled back the increase on April 16, Alexander accompanied Blough on a series of meetings to repair relations with the White House.
肯尼迪发起了一场反对涨价的运动，并对商界人士进行了严厉抨击，而政府则在寻找更巧妙的方式来影响美国钢铁公司。亨利·亚历山大是该公司的董事会成员，摩根公司的小约翰·M·迈耶则在其执行委员会任职。财政部副部长、前布朗兄弟哈里曼合伙人罗伯特·V·鲁萨打电话给亚历山大，请他向布劳施加影响。摩根财团早已没有那种神话般的力量来撤销美国钢铁公司的涨价决定，但亚历山大或许能让布劳在对峙期间的一次新闻发布会上缓和其反政府言论。当布劳最终迫于肯尼迪的压力，于 4 月 16 日取消涨价后，亚历山大陪同布劳参加了一系列会议，以修复与白宫的关系。

Still, the Kennedy years provided a politically friendly environment for bankers, who were no longer the bogeymen, as they had been in the 1930s. The Morgan bank even got giddy and overreached itself. In 1961, finally catching deposit fever, Alexander decided to drop Morgan’s ancient aversion to retail business. By affiliating with six large upstate banks, he hoped to create America’s biggest bank, a holding company monstrosity called Morgan New York State. “The basic idea was that the bank would have a Cadillac division and a Chevrolet division,” explained Bruce Nichols, a partner with Davis, Polk, and Wardwell. The stately Morgans would suddenly have 144 offices in places like Oneida and Binghamton. It turned out there was some vestigial fear of bankers among the populace, and Morgans had awakened it. James J. Saxon, JFK’s comptroller of the currency, torpedoed the move on antitrust grounds. Some believed the bank had bungled things by proposing an overly grandiose plan. Afterward, Alexander sighed to colleagues, “Well, we’ll just have to stick to wholesale banking.” Later the bank would feel that Saxon had saved it from a ghastly mistake.
尽管如此，肯尼迪执政时期为银行家们营造了一个政治友好的环境，他们不再像上世纪 30 年代那样被视为令人畏惧的角色。摩根银行甚至得意忘形，变得过于冒进。1961 年，亚历山大终于染上“存款热”，决定摒弃摩根长期以来对零售业务的厌恶。通过与纽约州北部的六家大型银行结盟，他试图打造美国最大的银行——一个被称为“摩根纽约州”的怪物式控股公司。“基本构想是，这家银行既要有凯迪拉克部门，也要有雪佛兰部门，”戴维斯-波克-沃德威尔律师事务所的合伙人布鲁斯·尼科尔斯解释道。原本庄重的摩根银行突然要在奥奈达和宾厄姆顿这样的地方开设 144 家分行。结果证明，民众心中对银行家仍存有某种残存的恐惧，而摩根的举动恰恰唤醒了它。肯尼迪任命的货币监理官詹姆斯·J·萨克森以反垄断为由否决了这一计划。有人认为，摩根银行失败的原因在于提出了一个过于宏大的方案。 随后，亚历山大向同事们叹了口气，“唉，看来我们只能坚守批发银行业务了。”后来银行觉得，是萨克森帮他们避免了一个可怕的错误。

When the Morgan Guaranty entourage swept back into the renovated Corner, the building’s interior mirrored a new era of banking. Everything was open: the glass-and-marble enclosures had been torn down. The signature rolltops, with their secret cubbyholes, were traded for flat, leather-topped mahogany desks. An enormous Louis XV chandelier, of a sort found in old German and Austrian palaces, now shed a rich glow over the room; the old mosaic panels were covered with apple-green fabric. The grandeur remained, but the old mystery had vanished. The most important change was that this banking floor—once the entire bank—was now just a gorgeous anteroom for the 15 Broad Street skyscraper, although top officials kept their offices on the second floor of 23 Wall. As if showing off its disregard for mundane concerns of cost, the bank rejected proposals to expand its short landmark building. Standing in the perpetual shade of skyscrapers. 23 Wall probably remained the least cost-effective use of real estate in the world.
当摩根保证银行的随行人员重返翻修一新的街角大楼时，内部装潢已映射出银行的新时代。一切变得通透：玻璃与大理石隔间被悉数拆除；标志性的卷盖式办公桌——连同那些隐秘的抽屉格子——换成了平顶、皮革覆面的红木桌。一盏曾属德国与奥地利古老宫殿的巨型路易十五水晶吊灯，如今为整个房间投下温润的光芒；旧时的马赛克镶板被覆上苹果绿织物。气派依旧，但往昔的神秘已荡然无存。最重大的变化在于，这间交易大厅——昔日便是整家银行——如今仅仅成为百老汇街 15 号摩天大楼的华美前厅，尽管高层官员仍将办公室设在华尔街 23 号二楼。仿佛炫耀其对世俗成本的漠视，银行拒绝了扩建这座短粗地标建筑的提议。这座建筑永远矗立于摩天大楼的阴影之下。 23 号华尔街大概是全世界性价比最低的房地产项目了。

SHORTLY after the merger, American banking began to wriggle free from its regulatory confines. Under Eisenhower, bankers had dreamed of deposits: in pursuit of billions in beautiful deposits, Henry Alexander had wooed Guaranty. But as interest rates floated up to a heady 4.5 percent by the late 1950s, corporate treasurers were loath to leave behind interest-free deposits (“compensating balances”) in exchange for loans. In what some bankers thought heresy, Morgans helped clients move their deposits into higher-yielding money market instruments. As George Whitney told critics, “My customers are not stupid.”[24](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400818)
合并后不久，美国银行业开始从监管束缚中挣脱出来。艾森豪威尔时代，银行家们对存款梦寐以求：为了追逐数十亿计的美妙存款，亨利·亚历山大曾向担保信托银行大献殷勤。但随着利率在 1950 年代末一路飙升至令人咋舌的 4.5%，企业财务主管们不愿再为了贷款而放弃无息存款（即“补偿性余额”）。摩根士丹利帮助客户将存款转入收益更高的货币市场工具，这在一些银行家看来简直是离经叛道。正如乔治·惠特尼对批评者所言：“我的客户不傻。”

The prospect was for a progressive erosion of the free balances. For the House of Morgan, without a cushion of consumer deposits, the specter of losing corporate deposits was especially ominous. Some inside the bank saw the dismal future of wholesale banking with remarkable clarity. Thomas S. Gates, Jr., who would succeed Alexander as chairman, used to say to him kiddingly, “You know, this isn’t a very good business to be in.”[25](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2400971)
前景是自由余额将逐步流失。对于摩根银行而言，由于缺乏消费者存款作为缓冲，公司存款流失的威胁格外令人不安。行内一些人清醒地预见到了批发银行业务的暗淡未来。后来接替亚历山大担任董事长的小托马斯·S·盖茨曾调侃地对他说：“你知道，这可不是什么好生意。”

Emancipation was at hand. In 1961, George Moore and Walter Wriston of First National City figured out how to circumvent the regulatory cap on interest rates. By law, banks couldn’t pay interest on deposits held under thirty days. But by selling “negotiable certificates of deposits” that matured in more than thirty days, banks *could* pay interest. These CDs could also be traded (hence, the “negotiable” in their name). Their use sparked a revolution in the way commercial banks operated, freeing them from reliance on deposits. Bankers no longer had to wait for deposits and were liberated from both companies and consumers. Now they could roam the world and raise money by selling CDs in overseas wholesale markets. The new system was known as managed liabilities. (In banking parlance, loans are assets and deposits, liabilities.) So relationship banking was crumbling on two sides—that of the restless corporate treasurers, who demanded yields from their deposits, and that of the freewheeling bankers, who could dispense with deposits and turn to money markets.
解放的时刻到了。1961 年，花旗银行的乔治·摩尔和沃尔特·瑞斯顿找到了规避利率监管上限的方法。根据法律，银行不能对 30 天以内的存款支付利息。但通过出售期限超过 30 天的"可转让定期存单"，银行就可以支付利息了。这些存单还可以交易（因此得名"可转让"）。这一创新彻底改变了商业银行的运营方式，让银行摆脱了对存款的依赖。银行家们不再需要等待存款，也不再受制于企业和消费者。他们可以满世界跑，通过在国外批发市场出售存单来筹集资金。这种新体系被称为"主动负债"。（在银行业术语中，贷款是资产，存款是负债。）因此，关系型银行业务正从两端瓦解——一端是躁动不安的企业财务主管，他们要求存款带来收益；另一端是无拘无束的银行家，他们可以抛开存款，转而投向货币市场。

The Morgan innovator was the tall, florid Ralph Leach. A University of Chicago graduate and a disciple of Milton Friedman, he started out as a Federal Reserve Board staffer and tennis partner of Fed chairman William McChesney Martin: the two would dash from morning meetings of the Federal Open Market Committee to grab the Fed’s court by noon. When Leach left for Guaranty Trust in the early 1950s, Martin, who’d been the first salaried president of the New York Stock Exchange, told him, “Don’t forget, Ralph, your associates in the next year or two will be people we could have put in jail fifteen or twenty years ago.”[26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401129) As Morgan Guaranty’s treasurer, Leach still advised the Fed and coached its board of governors and staff on money market operations. In the new era, Morgans’ intimacy with the Fed would come not through lending, as in the Twenties, but through its Treasury operation. It would act as the Fed’s eyes and ears in the marketplace and sometimes receive central-bank intelligence in return. It would now have better connections at the Washington Fed than it did during the New Deal. In the 1950s, Morgans had hired Arthur Burns as a consulting economist, and he would follow Martin at the Fed.
摩根银行的创新者是高大红润的拉尔夫·利奇。他是芝加哥大学毕业生、米尔顿·弗里德曼的信徒，最初在美联储委员会任职，并与美联储主席威廉·麦卡斯基·马丁是网球搭档——两人常从联邦公开市场委员会的晨会中抽身，赶在中午前抢占美联储的网球场。当利奇在 20 世纪 50 年代初离开去往担保信托银行时，曾担任纽约证券交易所首位带薪主席的马丁对他说：“别忘了，拉尔夫，未来一两年内你的同事，可能正是我们十五二十年前就能送进监狱的那帮人。” [26](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401129) 作为摩根担保信托的财务主管，利奇仍为美联储提供建议，并指导其理事会成员及员工进行货币市场操作。在这个新时代，摩根与美联储的亲密关系不再像 20 年代那样通过贷款维系，而是借助其国债业务。 这家银行将成为美联储在市场上的耳目，有时也能从央行那里获取内部情报。如今，它与华盛顿美联储的关系将比新政时期更为紧密。20 世纪 50 年代，摩根银行曾聘请阿瑟·伯恩斯担任咨询经济学家，后来伯恩斯接替马丁出任美联储主席。

At Guaranty Trust, Leach had peppered Cleveland with memos showing how the bank could manage its capital more aggressively. The patronizing Cleveland would reply, “Young man, go upstairs and run the portfolio and we’ll run the bank.”[27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401288) After the merger, Leach got to pursue his experiments and pioneered in the Federal funds market. Fed funds were reserves that commercial banks deposited with the Fed. Some banks would temporarily have “surplus” Fed funds—that is, reserves beyond their legal requirements. Morgans began to take the temporary, unused reserves from small interior banks and either use them or lend them to other banks on an overnight basis. The size of these short-term loans rose spectacularly, to \$1 billion or \$2 billion a day. Some banks believed the new market shouldn’t be used for trading profits. Leach, however, a born trader, viewed the Fed funds market as a source of profit.
在 Guaranty Trust 公司，Leach 不断向 Cleveland 提交备忘录，阐述银行如何更积极地管理资本。自视甚高的 Cleveland 总是回复：“年轻人，上楼去管理投资组合吧，银行由我们来经营。” [27](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401288) 合并后，Leach 得以开展自己的实验，并率先涉足联邦基金市场。联邦基金是商业银行存放在美联储的储备金。有些银行会暂时拥有“多余”的联邦基金，即超出法定要求的储备金。Morgans 开始从内陆小银行吸收这些暂时闲置的储备金，要么自己使用，要么隔夜拆借给其他银行。这些短期贷款的规模急剧膨胀，达到每天 10 亿至 20 亿美元。一些银行认为这个新市场不应用于赚取交易利润。然而，天生交易员的 Leach 却将联邦基金市场视为利润来源。

For commercial bankers, the world of negotiable CDs and Fed funds signified a dramatic change. As banking switched from a deposit to a money-purchase business, the center of gravity shifted from the banking floor to the trading room. The business acquired a new speculative cast as banks built up huge, diversified investment portfolios. Banking became not only riskier but more impersonal. The old-fashioned banker lunched with corporate treasurers to make sure they kept deposits at the bank. But traders were a lean, hyperthyroid breed who spent days on the telephone, riveted to the changing prices; they didn’t need to be particularly polite or cultured. The leisurely pace of deposit banking was replaced by the traders’ snap judgments.
对商业银行家而言，可转让存单与联邦基金的世界意味着一场剧变。当银行业从吸存放贷转向资金买卖，业务重心也从银行大厅转移到了交易室。随着银行构建起庞大而多元化的投资组合，这项生意染上了新的投机色彩。银行业不仅风险更高，也变得更加疏离人性。传统银行家会与企业财务主管共进午餐，确保他们把钱存在自家银行；而交易员则是一群精瘦、亢奋的生物，终日泡在电话线上，紧盯着跳动的价格——他们无需格外礼貌或优雅。存款银行那种慢悠悠的节奏，已被交易员们雷厉风行的决断所取代。

The Fed saw perils in this volatile new form of banking. Would savings and speculation become jumbled, as they had in the 1920s? Hadn’t Glass-Steagall shielded banks from such fast-moving markets? Morgans handled its trading operation with great panache, and its trading desk would be a postwar strength. But how would the new system work in clumsier hands? Would it turn into a dangerous instrument? “The Fed would say to us, ’It’s all right for Morgan to do it, but what if Bank of America or City did it?” recalled Leach. “Their feeling, in many cases, was, ’It’s good for you guys, but bad for the country.’ When they asked how other banks would fare, I would duck it by saying that I wasn’t arrogant enough to answer.”[28](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401414)
美联储认为这种波动性大的新型银行业务存在风险。储蓄与投机是否会像 20 世纪 20 年代那样混为一谈？格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案难道不是让银行免受此类快速变化的市场冲击吗？摩根银行以极其高超的手法运营其交易业务，其交易部门将成为战后的优势所在。但若这套新系统落入能力欠佳的机构手中，结果又会如何？它是否会变成一件危险的武器？“美联储会对我们说，‘摩根这么做没问题，但如果美国银行或花旗也这么做呢？’”利奇回忆道。“在很多情况下，他们的感觉是，‘这对你们来说是好事，但对国家来说是坏事。’当被问及其他银行会如何应对时，我会回避这个问题，说我没那么狂妄，不敢妄下结论。”

Gradually the House of Morgan drifted back into capital and money markets. Banned from corporate securities by Glass-Steagall, it became the most active dealer in Treasury and municipal securities in the 1960s. Unlike the straitlaced bankers of old, Leach would place large bets on the direction of interest rates. Now a commonplace banking practice, this was a frighteningly novel departure for conservative souls at 23 Wall. In 1960, Leach saw an excellent chance to speculate on one-year Treasury notes being auctioned by the Fed. When he calmly proposed a huge bet to the Morgan board, Henry P. Davison, the vice-chairman, asked, “Ralph, what kind of numbers are we talking about?” Leach said airily, “Oh, \$800 million to \$1 billion.” Swallowing hard, Davison replied, “This is going to take us time to digest, Ralph. That was the size of our entire bank a year ago.”[29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401540)
摩根银行逐渐重返资本与货币市场。受 Glass-Steagall 法案限制无法涉足企业证券，它便在 20 世纪 60 年代成为国库券和市政债券最活跃的交易商。与往日循规蹈矩的银行家不同，利奇敢于重金押注利率走势——如今这已是银行业家常便饭，但在华尔街 23 号的保守派看来，当时这简直是骇人听闻的离经叛道。1960 年，利奇瞅准美联储拍卖一年期国库券的绝佳投机机会，气定神闲地向摩根董事会提出豪赌方案。副董事长亨利·P·戴维森问道："拉尔夫，你说的金额大概是多少？"利奇轻描淡写地回答："哦，8 亿到 10 亿美元吧。"戴维森艰难地咽了口唾沫："拉尔夫，这数字够我们消化一阵子了。要知道一年前我们整个银行的规模也就这么大。" [29](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401540)

This new banking would wake up the drowsy Wall Street of the 1950s. Soon the tenth floor of Morgan’s building at 15 Broad Street had scores of frenetic young traders taking positions in T-bills, negotiable CDs, foreign exchange, and Fed funds. Before long, Leach oversaw \$ 1 billion of market transactions daily. In 1966, *Fortune* claimed that Leach “very likely handles more money in the course of a year than any other man in private industry.”[30](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401666)
这种新型银行业务将唤醒 1950 年代昏昏欲睡的华尔街。很快，在布罗德街 15 号摩根大厦的十楼，大批疯狂的年轻交易员开始操作 T-bills、negotiable CDs、外汇和 Fed funds。没过多久，Leach 每天监督的市场交易额就达到了 10 亿美元。1966 年，Fortune 声称 Leach"一年中经手的资金很可能比私人企业中任何其他人都都多"。

At one point, Leach became too assertive, and the government stepped in. In August 1962, the Treasury auctioned \$1.3 billion in bills maturing in three months. Leach placed a shockingly large bid—\$650 million, then the largest bid ever submitted for T-bills. Wall Street saw an attempt to corner the market. Although Leach blandly denied any sinister intent, Treasury Secretary C. Douglas Dillon promulgated a new policy, courtesy of the Morgan bank. Henceforth, no single bidder would be awarded more than a quarter of the bills offered at any weekly auction. The Morgan allotment was halved to \$325 million.
一度，利奇变得过于强势，政府不得不介入。1962 年 8 月，财政部拍卖了 13 亿美元的三个月期国库券。利奇出价惊人——6.5 亿美元，这是当时国库券拍卖史上最大的单笔出价。华尔街认为这是在试图垄断市场。尽管利奇温和地否认有任何不良意图，但财政部长 C.道格拉斯·狄龙还是在摩根银行的授意下颁布了一项新政策。此后，任何单一竞标者在每周拍卖中获得的国库券份额不得超过总发行量的四分之一。摩根银行获得的配额因此减半，降至 3.25 亿美元。

It would take the general public many years to catch on to these changes. The rise of bought money, negotiable CDs, and daring trading would have an enduring effect on banking. Bankers formerly had been preoccupied with the “asset” side of the business—that is, making loans. Now the liability side—the money on which loans were based—took on equal importance. Profits could be expanded in two ways—by securing higher interest rates on loans or by buying money more cheaply in the marketplace. In this new environment, that bastion of conservatism, the House of Morgan, elevated the trader to unaccustomed eminence.
普通大众需要很多年才能跟上这些变化。购买资金、可转让存单以及大胆交易方式的兴起，对银行业产生了深远的影响。过去，银行家们主要关注业务中的“资产”端，即发放贷款。而现在，“负债”端——即贷款所基于的资金——变得同样重要。利润可以通过两种方式扩大：要么在贷款上获得更高的利率，要么在市场上以更低的价格购买资金。在这种新环境下，那个保守主义的堡垒——摩根银行，让交易员获得了前所未有的显赫地位。

Unfortunately for the banks, this new world of wholesale money markets also worked to the advantage of their corporate customers. Just as the Morgan bank could sell its CDs around the world, so a General Motors or a U.S. Steel could circumvent the bank and sell promissory notes called commercial paper at interest, rates lower than those they would pay for a bank loan. In the wholesale corporate world in which Morgans operated, the banker was shedding his unique place as an intermediary between the providers and the users of capital. In the Casino Age, large corporations would increasingly serve as their own bankers, creating a crisis in the wholesale lending business, which had seemed so safe to the J. P. Morgan partners back in 1935.
不幸的是，对银行来说，这个批发性货币市场的新世界也给了他们的企业客户可乘之机。就像摩根银行能在全球销售它的 CDs 一样，通用汽车或美国钢铁公司也能绕过银行，出售一种称为商业本票的期票，其利率比银行贷款还低。在摩根所处的批发企业世界里，银行正逐渐丧失其作为资本供需双方中介人的独特地位。进入赌场时代后，大公司越来越倾向于自己当自己的银行，这给批发性贷款业务带来了危机——而 1935 年那会儿，J. P. Morgan 的合伙人们还以为这项业务稳如泰山呢。

THE rise of the Euromarkets accelerated the banking revolution of the early 1960s. With scarcely a whisper of public protest, these unregulated overseas markets subverted the spirit of Glass-Steagall. In the 1950s, so long as America was rich and other countries poor, bright young Morgan bankers avoided international banking. Henry Alexander’s career was emblematic: he lacked the ties to foreign ministers that were symbolic of the careers of Tom Lamont and Russell Leffingwell. Yet he foresaw foreign trade and investment as the next phase of American economic life. American companies were expanding overseas at a rapid clip. Soon after the Morgan-Guaranty merger, Alexander and Walter Page went abroad to set up Morgan offices in Frankfurt, Rome, and Tokyo, resurrecting the old international network. Morgans used the 1919 Edge Act, which allowed American banks to take equity stakes in foreign banks if a country didn’t allow U.S. bank branches. By 1962, the House of Morgan had interests in eleven financial houses from Australia to Peru to Morocco. Once again, in the Casino Age, American banks were trailing after their multinational customers, not leading them.
欧洲市场的崛起加速了 20 世纪 60 年代初的银行业革命。几乎未引起任何公众抗议，这些不受监管的海外市场就颠覆了《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的精神。20 世纪 50 年代，只要美国富裕而其他国家贫穷，摩根银行的年轻精英们便对国际银行业务避而远之。亨利·亚历山大的职业生涯颇具代表性：他缺乏与外国部长的交情——这种交情曾是汤姆·拉蒙特和拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔职业生涯的象征。然而，他预见到对外贸易和投资将成为美国经济生活的下一阶段。美国公司正以迅猛势头向海外扩张。摩根与担保信托合并后不久，亚历山大和沃尔特·佩奇便远赴海外，在法兰克福、罗马和东京设立了摩根办事处，重建了昔日全球网络。摩根利用了 1919 年的《埃奇法案》，该法案允许美国银行在外国禁止设立分行时，持有该国银行的股权。 到 1962 年，摩根财团的业务已遍及从澳大利亚到秘鲁再到摩洛哥的十一家金融机构。 在"赌场时代"里，美国银行又一次跟在跨国客户身后亦步亦趋，而非引领它们前行。

To round out the foreign side, Henry Alexander recruited Thomas Sovereign Gates, Jr., Eisenhower’s last defense secretary. They had complementary contacts: Alexander knew the corporate heads and central bankers, Gates the prime ministers and foreign secretaries. It was also hoped Gates would use his administrative talents to organize the larger, more bureaucratic bank produced by the merger.
为了完善海外布局，亨利·亚历山大招募了小托马斯·索弗林·盖茨——艾森豪威尔的最后一任国防部长。二人的关系网相得益彰：亚历山大熟稔企业高管与央行行长，盖茨则与各国总理及外长私交甚笃。人们还期待盖茨能运用其行政才能，将合并后规模更大、官僚气息更浓的银行整顿得井井有条。

Gates seemed a rare lateral entrant into the Morgan hierarchy but really had true-blue Morgan roots. His father was a Drexel and Company partner and president of the University of Pennsylvania. As a Drexel bond salesman in the 1930s, Tom, Jr., had apprenticed at J. P. Morgan and Company. Drawn to intrigue, he served with Naval Air Intelligence in World War II. Starting his Washington career in 1953, he served as under secretary and secretary of the navy and finally succeeded Neil McElroy as defense secretary.
盖茨看似是摩根体系中少有的空降兵，但实际上有着纯正的摩根血统。他的父亲曾是 Drexel and Company 的合伙人，还担任过宾夕法尼亚大学校长。小汤姆在 20 世纪 30 年代作为 Drexel 的债券销售员，在 J. P. Morgan and Company 当过学徒。他天生喜欢神秘刺激，二战期间曾在海军航空情报部门服役。1953 年开启华盛顿生涯后，他先后担任海军副部长和部长，最终接替 Neil McElroy 出任国防部长。

Rich and affable, a cowboy in well-tailored suits, Gates gave off an easy air of authority, an engaging conviviality. A macho hero to subordinates, he loved wine, women, and warplanes. “Gates liked living and liquor better than anybody I knew,” recalled an admiring associate. At the Pentagon, he was a blunt, no-nonsense manager. After receiving a bulky study arguing for the retention of a troublesome traffic light that caused congestion near a Virginia navy arsenal, Gates scrawled across the top, “Turn off the damn light.”[31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401814) He took flak as navy secretary by closing useless bases. When he closed one in Texas before consulting Lyndon B. Johnson, the future president never forgave him and later harassed him with an FBI investigation.
富有而和蔼，穿着剪裁得体的西装，像个牛仔，盖茨散发出一种从容的权威感和迷人的亲切。在下属眼中他是硬汉英雄，他喜爱美酒、女人和战机。“我认识的人里，没人比盖茨更懂得享受生活和美酒，”一位钦佩他的同事回忆道。在五角大楼，他是一位直言不讳、务实高效的经理。在收到一份厚厚的报告，主张保留弗吉尼亚州海军兵工厂附近一个造成拥堵的麻烦交通灯后，盖茨在报告顶部潦草地写道：“把那该死的灯关掉。” [31](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401814) 他担任海军部长期间因关闭无用基地而受到抨击。在未事先咨询林登·B·约翰逊的情况下关闭了德州的一个基地，这位未来的总统始终没有原谅他，后来还通过联邦调查局的调查来骚扰他。

As defense secretary, Gates loved covert activity. Through the National Security Council, he contributed to a four-point plan to topple Fidel Castro, an early blueprint for the Bay of Pigs disaster. He revered Secretary of State John Foster Dulles, a frequent dinner guest at the Gates household. Gates was closely involved with the U-2 spy plane and authorized its final flight, even though Ike told the CIA to stop such activity. “It was just an unbelievable thing, that U-2,” he said nostalgically while Morgan chairman. “I often dream about the U-2.”[32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401967) When the plane was shot down, just before Ike’s summit in Paris with Nikita Khrushchev, Gates advised the president to take responsibility. He also added to the controversy by putting U.S. forces on alert during the tense summit. “The timing of the exercise was just a shade worse than sending off the U-2 on its perilous mission two weeks before the Summit,” noted Walter Lippmann.[33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402121)
作为国防部长，盖茨热衷于秘密行动。他通过国家安全委员会参与了一项旨在推翻菲德尔·卡斯特罗的四点计划，这后来成为猪湾事件的最初蓝图。他敬重国务卿约翰·福斯特·杜勒斯，后者是盖茨家的常客。盖茨深度参与了 U-2 侦察机项目，并批准了它的最后一次飞行，尽管艾克曾命令中央情报局停止此类行动。“那架 U-2 真是不可思议，”他在担任摩根董事长时怀念地说，“我时常梦见 U-2。” [32](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2401967) 当这架飞机在艾克与赫鲁晓夫举行巴黎峰会前夕被击落时，盖茨建议总统承担责任。他还在紧张的峰会期间下令美军进入戒备状态，进一步加剧了争议。“这次演习的时机之糟糕，比起在峰会前两周派 U-2 执行危险任务，简直有过之而无不及。”沃尔特·李普曼评论道。 [33](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402121)

The day before his inauguration, John Kennedy was briefed by Gates, who painted an alarming picture of the imminent fall of Laos to the Communists and advocated limited American military involvement. He said it would take a couple of weeks to get American troops into Laos. An early plan had Gates being reappointed as defense secretary, with Bobby Kennedy his under secretary, and a year later Bobby would succeed him. This scheme ran into trouble when JFK’s advisers pointed out an embarrassing discrepancy between Kennedy’s campaign rhetoric about a U.S. Soviet “missile gap” and a Gates reappointment. When Robert S. McNamara, president of Ford Motor, got the job instead, Henry Ford II proposed a “swap”—Gates as president of Ford and McNamara as defense secretary. Gates was also asked to head General Electric. Nonetheless, he chose Morgans. “He said he was always a banker and didn’t want to learn how to make toasters,” said his son-in-law Joe Ponce.[34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402278)
就职前一天，约翰·肯尼迪听取了盖茨的汇报，盖茨描绘了一幅老挝即将落入共产党之手的令人担忧的图景，并主张美国进行有限的军事干预。他说美军进入老挝需要几周时间。早前曾有一项计划，让盖茨留任国防部长，博比·肯尼迪担任副部长，一年后博比接替他。但当肯尼迪的顾问指出，肯尼迪竞选时关于美苏“导弹差距”的言论与盖茨留任之间存在令人尴尬的矛盾时，这一方案便遇到了麻烦。福特汽车公司总裁罗伯特·S·麦克纳马拉最终获得了这个职位，亨利·福特二世便提议“交换”——盖茨担任福特总裁，麦克纳马拉任国防部长。盖茨也曾受邀执掌通用电气。但他还是选择了摩根。他的女婿乔·庞塞说：“他说自己一直是个银行家，不想学怎么做烤面包机。” [34](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402278)

Gates brought an easygoing style to the bank. One subordinate remembered a meeting between Gates and Jimmy Ling, head of Ling-Temco-Vought, the acquisitive aerospace and electronics conglomerate. Gates was bubbling over in his enthusiasm for a favorite warplane, while Ling kept asking whether Morgan would finance his acquisition of Wilson Sporting Goods. “No problem, Jimmy,” Gates said, then returned to his beloved warplane. When Gates at last dispatched a subordinate to Stuart Cragin, head of the Credit Policy Committee, the latter flatly turned down Ling’s request and overrode the casual Gates. Morgans thus became the first Wall Street bank to stop Ling’s acquisitions binge.
盖茨给银行带来了一种随和的风格。一位下属回忆起盖茨与林氏-特姆科-沃特公司（Ling-Temco-Vought）负责人吉米·林（Jimmy Ling）的一次会面，这家公司是一家热衷于收购的航空航天与电子集团。盖茨对自己喜爱的一款战机热情洋溢，而林则反复询问摩根银行是否愿意为他收购威尔逊体育用品公司（Wilson Sporting Goods）提供融资。“没问题，吉米，”盖茨说完，又回头聊起他心爱的战机。当盖茨最终派一名下属去询问信贷政策委员会（Credit Policy Committee）负责人斯图尔特·克拉金（Stuart Cragin）时，后者断然拒绝了林的请求，推翻了漫不经心的盖茨。摩根银行因此成为华尔街第一家阻止林氏收购狂潮的银行。

Gates never fully recovered from Potomac fever. He was a good friend not only of Eisenhower, who volunteered to back him for a Senate seat, but of two later Republican presidents, as well, Richard Nixon and Gerald Ford. (His subordinates speculated as to whether the second phone on Gates’s desk was a hot line to the White House.) His connections extended everywhere. He belonged to an exclusive group formed by Stephen Bechtel, Sr., of the secretive San Francisco-based construction firm and an active Morgan director after 1954. At the Carlyle Hotel, Bechtel regularly convened a study group that included Pan Am founder Juan Trippe, Texaco chairman Augustus Long, General Lucius Clay, and Gates. These brandy-and-cigar discussions might feature Bechtel on Saudi Arabia, Long on oil-price trends, and Gates on NATO and the Russian threat.[35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402438) Gates would exploit his numerous contacts to spread Morgan influence around the globe.
盖茨从未完全从"波托马克河热"中恢复过来。他不仅与艾森豪威尔（后者曾主动表示支持他竞选参议员）交情深厚，还与后来的两位共和党总统——理查德·尼克松和杰拉尔德·福特——保持着密切的友谊。（他的下属曾揣测，盖茨桌上的第二部电话是否就是白宫的热线。）他的人脉遍布四方。他是老斯蒂芬·贝希特尔创建的精英团体的成员——这家总部位于旧金山的建筑公司行事隐秘，而贝希特尔在 1954 年后成为摩根银行的活跃董事。在卡莱尔酒店，贝希特尔定期召集一个研究小组，成员包括泛美航空创始人胡安·特里普、德士古董事长奥古斯都·朗、卢修斯·克莱将军以及盖茨。在这些白兰地与雪茄相伴的讨论中，贝希特尔会谈论沙特阿拉伯，朗会分析油价趋势，而盖茨则分享对北约和俄罗斯威胁的看法。 [35](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402438) 盖茨利用其广泛的人脉，将摩根的势力辐射至全球各地。

WHEN Kennedy first took office, nobody could have foreseen the international thrust of banking in the 1960s. What was apparent was that the president had to staunch a massive outflow of American capital. In early 1962, Eisenhower convened a meeting of his old cabinet and Republican leaders. Tom Gates was impressed by a talk by Arthur Burns, who warned that a continuing drain of U.S. dollars and gold abroad would so badly damage the country’s balance of payments that JFK would have to resort to extreme measures. Burns “believes the only thing left will be some direct controls,” Gates warned Alexander. “The Administration does not wish such controls, but is drifting into a situation where they will probably be the only answer.”[36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402610) The House of Morgan braced for a new era in which American multinationals would get their financing abroad. As Alexander said, “As business goes, so goes banking.”
肯尼迪刚上任时，谁也没能预见 20 世纪 60 年代银行业会如此迅猛地向国际扩张。但有一点显而易见：总统必须遏制美国资本的大规模外流。1962 年初，艾森豪威尔召集了原内阁成员和共和党领袖开会。汤姆·盖茨对阿瑟·伯恩斯的发言印象深刻——伯恩斯警告称，美元和黄金持续流向海外将严重破坏美国的国际收支平衡，导致肯尼迪不得不采取极端手段。伯恩斯“认为唯一剩下的办法就是实施某些直接管控”，盖茨向亚历山大预警道，“政府并不希望这样做，但正逐渐滑入一种局面，使这些管控很可能成为唯一的出路。” [36](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402610) 摩根家族准备迎接一个新时代：美国跨国公司将从海外获取融资。正如亚历山大所言：“商业怎么走，银行业就怎么跟。”

In late 1962, Alexander, presiding over a turbulent meeting, asked a question that had not been heard for thirty years: should the House of Morgan return to underwriting, this time in Paris? In a decision that produced mild wonder at 23 Wall—wonder that the bankers kept to themselves—the Fed had passed a tentative ruling that Glass-Steagall would pose no obstacle outside the United States. But would it withstand a legal challenge? People were wary. “There was reluctance on the part of the other senior people to do something that could be seen as skating close to the edge of legality,” recalled Evan Galbraith, then with the bank and later ambassador to France. “But Henry was quite visionary about it.” Alexander went around the room listening to antagonistic opinions. At last, overriding objections, he said, ” ’Well, I think this will be what you call a business decision.’ ”[37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402793) The plan called for a new Parisian underwriting subsidiary, Morgan et Compagnie, Societe Anonyme, with Morgan Grenfell and Mees and Hope of Holland as passive, minority shareholders. (The name Morgan et Compagnie had been dormant since the Guaranty merger.) Content with its American business, Morgan Stanley spurned this first invitation to enter Europe.
1962 年末，亚历山大主持一场气氛紧张的会议，提出了一个三十年来未曾有人问过的问题：摩根财团是否应该重返承销业务，这次地点设在巴黎？纽约联邦储备银行做出了一项初步裁决，认为《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》在美国境外不会构成障碍——这一决定在华尔街 23 号引起了轻微的惊讶，而这种惊讶被银行家们默契地埋在心里。但这一裁决能否经得起法律挑战？人们心存疑虑。“其他资深合伙人不太愿意去做那些可能被视为游走在法律边缘的事情，”当时在银行任职、后来出任驻法国大使的埃文·加尔布雷思回忆道，“但亨利对此颇有远见。”亚历山大在房间里转了一圈，倾听反对意见。最后，他力排众议，说道：“嗯，我认为这将是你们所说的商业决策。”’ ” [37](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402793) 计划要求成立一家新的巴黎承销子公司——Morgan et Compagnie, Societe Anonyme，由 Morgan Grenfell 与荷兰的 Mees and Hope 作为被动的小股东。（自担保合并以来，Morgan et Compagnie 这个名字一直处于休眠状态。）摩根士丹利对其美国业务心满意足，因而拒绝了这首次进入欧洲的邀请。

On July 18, 1963, Kennedy proposed an Interest Equalization Tax to throttle the dollar outflow. By penalizing the sale of some foreign securities to American investors, it provided incentives for banks to flock abroad. Hearing the news, Alexander divined a watershed. Assembling Morgan officers that afternoon, he made a quick, prescient judgment: “This is a day that you will all remember forever. It will change the face of American banking and force all the business off to London. It will take years to get rid of this legislation.”[38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402952) Two years later, Lyndon B. Johnson imposed voluntary restraints on lending to foreign borrowers and personally stressed their importance in White House meetings with Gates.[39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403118) Suddenly overseas banking became the preferred career path for the ambitious.
1963 年 7 月 18 日，肯尼迪提出征收利息平衡税以遏制美元外流。通过向美国投资者出售某些外国证券征收惩罚性税费，此举刺激银行纷纷涌向海外。听闻消息，亚历山大嗅到转折将至。当天下午召集摩根高管，他迅速做出极具先见之明的判断："这一天你们都将永远铭记。它将彻底改变美国银行业的面貌，把业务全部逼到伦敦。要花上好多年才能废除这项法案。" [38](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2402952) 两年后，林登·B·约翰逊对向外国借款人的贷款实施自愿限制，并在白宫与盖茨的会面中亲自强调其重要性。 [39](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403118) 突然间，海外银行业成为野心家们的首选职业道路。

Luckily, dollars abounded outside the United States—in part from the payments deficit itself—forming a pool of stateless money. The first Eurodollars had come into being after World War II, when the Soviet Union, wary of reprisals by American authorities, deposited its dollars at the Banque Commerciale pour l’Europe du Nord in Paris and in London’s Moscow Narodny Bank. In time, *Euro* came to signify any currency held outside its country of origin. In other words, Eurodollars are dollars held outside the United States, Euroyen are yen held outside Japan, and so on. By the mid-1980s, this free-floating unregulated market—a free marketeer’s pipe dream—would swell to \$2.5 trillion in deposits.
幸运的是，美元在美国境外大量存在——部分原因正是支付赤字本身——形成了一个无国籍的资金池。第一批欧洲美元诞生于二战后，当时苏联担心遭到美国当局报复，将其美元存款存入巴黎的北欧商业银行和伦敦的莫斯科国民银行。后来，“欧洲”一词逐渐用来指代任何在发行国境外持有的货币。换句话说，欧洲美元就是在美国境外持有的美元，欧洲日元就是在日本境外持有的日元，以此类推。到 20 世纪 80 年代中期，这个自由浮动、不受监管的市场——自由市场主义者的幻梦——存款规模将膨胀至 2.5 万亿美元。

A wholesale world catering to big business, governments, and institutions, the Euromarkets were immediately congenial to the House of Morgan. Here banks didn’t pay deposit-insurance premiums on dollar deposits or set aside mandatory reserves against deposits; they could lend dollars as freely as they pleased. Conditioned by New Deal legislation, American bankers were initially edgy about this freedom but soon adapted. Along with the new trend of buying money instead of gathering deposits, the creation of the Euromarkets lifted restrictions on growth. If the Fed tightened credit in the United States, banks could sell large CDs in London and use Eurodollars to finance their domestic lending.
一个面向大企业、政府和机构的批发市场——欧洲市场（Euromarkets）与摩根家族（House of Morgan）一拍即合。在这里，银行无需为美元存款缴纳存款保险费，也无需留存法定准备金；它们可以随心所欲地发放美元贷款。受新政（New Deal）立法影响，美国银行家起初对这种自由感到不安，但很快便适应了。伴随着购买资金而非吸收存款的新趋势，欧洲市场的诞生打破了增长的限制。如果美联储（Fed）在美国收紧信贷，银行可以在伦敦出售大额存单（CDs），并借助欧洲美元（Eurodollars）来支撑其国内放贷业务。

The New York banks doggedly fought to retain these privileges. At one point early in the Johnson administration, Washington tried to stop American banks from keeping Eurodollar accounts in branches abroad. An assistant Treasury secretary named Paul Volcker invited Morgan’s head of international banking, Walter Page, and others to Washington for comment. The bankers delivered a stern warning. “We said it was the end of the American banking system,” recalled Page. “You will throw us out of Europe and Singapore and Japan. And, my God, Paul that evening rewrote the whole basis with me. He got it all done before you could say Jack Robinson.”[40](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403291) The regulation was dropped. In Volcker, Morgans had its paladin for the next twenty-five years.
纽约的各大银行顽强地捍卫着这些特权。约翰逊政府执政初期，华盛顿曾试图阻止美国银行在海外分行持有欧洲美元账户。时任助理财政部长保罗·沃尔克（Paul Volcker）邀请摩根银行国际业务主管沃尔特·佩奇（Walter Page）等人前往华盛顿征求意见。银行家们发出了严正警告。“我们当时说，这将终结美国银行体系，”佩奇回忆道，“你们会让我们被欧洲、新加坡和日本扫地出门。天哪，保罗当晚就和我一起重新起草了整个方案。他三下五除二就搞定了。”这项规定最终被废止。在接下来的二十五年里，沃尔克成了摩根银行的守护者。

While Morgan Grenfell dozed, that perennial London iconoclast, Siegmund Warburg, sponsored the first Eurobond issue for the Italian *Autostrade* in 1963. Morgan’s new Paris subsidiary was an early star in this market. Since the Guaranty merger provided duplicate Paris offices and an embarrassing surplus of mansions, Morgan kept its place Vendome branch, while the new Morgan et Compagnie, S.A., moved into the chandeliered Guaranty branch at 4 place de la Concorde near Maxim’s. Once called the Hotel de Coislin, the building was a national monument. In it, Benjamin Franklin signed the treaty with France recognizing U.S. independence, and Chateaubriand wrote his romances. During World War II, it was occupied by the Gestapo. From its glittering interior, the House of Morgan would launch its assault on global securities markets.
当摩根·格伦费尔还在昏昏欲睡时，伦敦那位一贯离经叛道的西格蒙德·沃伯格，于 1963 年为意大利 Autostrade 高速公路公司发行了首支欧洲债券。摩根新成立的巴黎子公司在这一市场上崭露头角。由于与担保信托银行合并后，巴黎办事处重复设置，且豪宅多得令人尴尬，摩根保留了其在旺多姆广场的分行，而新成立的摩根股份有限公司则迁至协和广场 4 号——紧邻马克西姆餐厅——那间灯火璀璨的担保信托银行旧址。这座建筑原为科瓦兰公馆，是国家级古迹。本杰明·富兰克林曾在此签署承认美国独立的法美条约，夏多布里昂在此撰写其浪漫小说。二战期间，这里被盖世太保占据。从这金碧辉煌的内廷，摩根家族发起了对全球证券市场的攻势。

Besides opening up the Paris operation, the new Euromarkets provided a chance for the Morgan banks to expand their relationship with the Vatican. During the 1950s, almost all Vatican funds in New York were managed by the J. P. Morgan Trust Department, just as almost all Vatican funds in London were under Morgan Grenfell’s supervision. In the late 1950s, after the retirement of Bernardino Nogara—the mysterious, powerful creator of the Special Administration of the Holy See—the House of Morgan lost its foremost papal ally. To fortify the relationship, Morgan Guaranty, Morgan Grenfell, and Morgan Stanley joined with the Vatican in 1963 to form a Rome investment bank called Euramerica. The Vatican was financially rich and innovative in the 1960s—it controlled Immobiliare Roma, which built the Watergate Hotel in Washington—and Euramerica was supposed to be the first American-style investment bank in Italy.
除了开设巴黎分行，新兴的 Euromarkets 还为摩根银行提供了扩大与梵蒂冈关系的机会。20 世纪 50 年代，梵蒂冈在纽约的几乎所有资金都由 J. P. 摩根信托部门管理，正如其在伦敦的资金几乎全部由 Morgan Grenfell 监管。50 年代末，罗马教廷特别行政区的神秘而强权的创建者 Bernardino Nogara 退休后，摩根家族失去了最重要的教廷盟友。为巩固关系，Morgan Guaranty、Morgan Grenfell 和 Morgan Stanley 于 1963 年与梵蒂冈联手，在罗马成立了一家名为 Euramerica 的投资银行。60 年代的梵蒂冈财力雄厚且富有创新精神——它控股的 Immobiliare Roma 正是华盛顿水门酒店的承建方——而 Euramerica 则被寄望成为意大利首家美式投资银行。

The new operation was managed by Dr. Nicola Caiola, whose father headed a prewar Vatican trade department and who himself grew up in Vatican City. After working as a junior stock analyst under Nogara in the late 1940s, he got a Banca d’Italia fellowship and apprenticed at J. P. Morgan and Company and Morgan Stanley in the early 1950s. While Caiola was visiting Rome in the early 1960s, the Vatican expressed interest in sharing an investment bank with the Morgans; Caiola returned to the United States to prepare a blueprint. Morgan Guaranty and Morgan Grenfell leapt at the chance, but Morgan Stanley, which then had a curiously provincial and complacent attitude toward the outer world, joined in only reluctantly. On the eve of Caiola’s departure for Rome, Harry Morgan summoned him and said, “Remember, it took us a long time to establish our name. Our name is now in your hands.”[41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403450)
这次新行动由尼古拉·凯奥拉博士主持，他的父亲曾主管战前梵蒂冈的一个贸易部门，而他本人则在梵蒂冈城长大。1940 年代末，他在诺加拉手下担任初级股票分析师，之后获得意大利银行的奖学金，并于 1950 年代初在 J.P.摩根公司和摩根士丹利实习。1960 年代初，凯奥拉访问罗马时，梵蒂冈表示有意与摩根家族共同成立一家投资银行；凯奥拉随即返回美国着手制定蓝图。摩根保证信托和摩根建富对此欣然接受，但当时的摩根士丹利对外部世界抱有莫名狭隘且自满的态度，只是勉强参与。就在凯奥拉动身前往罗马的前夜，哈里·摩根召见了他，说道：“记住，我们花了很长时间才建立起自己的名声。现在，这个名字就交到你手里了。” [41](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403450)

The Vatican took a one-third interest, and the Morgan houses another third, while the remainder was divided among Italian companies. Despite its weighty Vatican patronage, Euramerica was a swinging operation, a Euromarket pioneer. Although based in Rome, it did dollar financing and challenged the investment banking monopoly of Italy’s omnipotent Mediobanca. It was profitable every year until 1971, when the Morgans bowed out because they faced a conflict of interest with their thriving Paris operation.
梵蒂冈持有三分之一股份，摩根家族也占三分之一，其余部分由意大利公司瓜分。尽管有梵蒂冈的强力背书，欧美公司仍是个灵活运作的机构，堪称欧洲货币市场的先驱。虽然总部设在罗马，它却从事美元融资业务，并挑战了意大利无所不能的 Mediobanca 在投资银行领域的垄断地位。从成立到 1971 年间，它每年都保持盈利——直到摩根家族因与自己在巴黎蓬勃发展的业务存在利益冲突而选择退出。

Meanwhile, in Paris, Morgan et Compagnie, S.A., made what looked like an extremely auspicious debut. In February 1963, it launched a Euroequity (stock) issue for Germany’s largest mail-order house, Neckermann, which owned twenty-three department stores. In taking his company public, founder Josef Neckermann planned to retain a majority interest. Friedrich Flick, possibly Germany’s richest man, the scion of a steel family and a convicted war criminal, wished to sell his Neckermann stake. Neckermann feared that these shares would fall into the hands of German banks, which are permitted to hold industrial stakes. He was particularly eager to bypass Deutsche Bank, Germany’s largest, which controlled a veritable industrial empire. Neckermann favored global syndication, with only a small portion of the shares allotted to Germany.
与此同时，在巴黎，Morgan et Compagnie, S.A. 迎来了一个看似极为顺遂的亮相。1963 年 2 月，它为德国最大的邮购公司 Neckermann 发行了一笔欧洲股权（股票），这家公司旗下拥有 23 家百货商场。创始人 Josef Neckermann 计划在公司上市后保留多数股权。而 Friedrich Flick——或许是德国最富有的人、钢铁家族后代、且曾是定罪战犯——则希望出售自己所持的 Neckermann 股份。Neckermann 担心这些股票会落入德国银行之手——德国银行被允许持有工业股权。他尤其急于绕过德意志银行（Deutsche Bank），这家德国最大的银行掌控着一个名副其实的工业帝国。Neckermann 倾向于全球联合发行，只将少量股票分配给德国。

For the new Paris operation, the Neckermann issue seemed a smashing success. Morgans bought the \$30-million issue, then turned around and resold it to Belgian, Swiss, and Dutch banks. In London, where Morgan Grenfell led a large purchasing group, the issue shot up to a premium. Said Evan Galbraith, then a leading Morgan man in Paris: “It was the first internationally marketed issue. People saw you could distribute something on an international basis.”[42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403612) There was one telltale hint of trouble, however. When Morgans sent out the offering by telex, German banks didn’t answer. When Deutsche Bank then complained about the issue’s being sold outside Germany, Galbraith said Morgans was simply heeding the client’s wishes. He didn’t quite fathom the depth of anger he had aroused or how deeply he had offended tradition. Deutsche Bank would bide its time and get even in extremely dramatic fashion.
对于新的巴黎业务，Neckermann 的发行似乎取得了巨大成功。摩根买下了这笔 3000 万美元的发行，然后转手卖给了比利时、瑞士和荷兰的银行。在伦敦，摩根建富领导了一个大型购买集团，该发行迅速升至溢价。当时摩根在巴黎的主要人物埃文·加尔布雷思说：“这是第一个国际发行的项目。人们看到了你可以把东西分销到国际范围。” [42](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403612) 然而，有一个不祥的预兆。当摩根通过电传发送发行文件时，德国银行没有回应。当德意志银行随后抱怨该发行在德国以外销售时，加尔布雷思说摩根只是顺应客户意愿。他并未完全理解自己激起的愤怒有多深，以及他对传统的冒犯有多严重。德意志银行将耐心等待时机，并以极其戏剧化的方式报复。

Though extraterritorial in nature, the early Euromarkets were riled by fierce nationalistic clashes. Except in the Eurodollar market, banks expected to lead issues denominated in their own currencies. (The U.S. Treasury even briefly insisted that American firms lead Eurodollar issues.) The now-swaggering Morgans came up against this parochialism when it tried to invade the most sacred banking monopoly of all—that of Switzerland. Crédit Suisse, Swiss Bank Corporation, and Union Bank of Switzerland formed a cartel that dominated Swiss franc issues, and outside banks defied them at their own peril. The Morgan Paris operation did just that in September 1963, when the city of Copenhagen wished to raise money and its treasurer consulted friends at Morgan Grenfell. As Tim Collins of Morgan Grenfell, recalled, “Somebody had the bright idea that since interest rates were low in Switzerland, why not denominate the issue in Swiss francs?”[43](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403771)
尽管早期欧洲市场具有离岸性质，激烈的民族主义冲突却使其动荡不安。除欧洲美元市场外，银行们希望由自己所在国家的金融机构主导以本国货币计价的债券发行。（美国财政部甚至一度坚持要求由美国公司主导欧洲美元债券发行。）如今志得意满的摩根银行在试图侵入银行业最神圣的垄断领域——瑞士时，就遭遇了这种狭隘观念。瑞士信贷、瑞士银行公司和瑞士联合银行组成卡特尔，垄断了瑞士法郎债券发行，外部银行若胆敢挑战将自食其果。1963 年 9 月，摩根巴黎分行恰恰这样做了——当时哥本哈根市希望筹集资金，其财务主管向摩根建富的朋友征求意见。据摩根建富的蒂姆·柯林斯回忆：“有人灵机一动，既然瑞士利率低廉，为何不以瑞士法郎计价发行呢？”

This time, Galbraith warned 23 Wall Street to expect an angry response, though nobody quite expected the furor that erupted. “The Swiss banks went bananas,” said Galbraith. “They called up Henry Alexander and said, ’You can’t do this. Swiss francs are *not* an international currency. They should be controlled by the Swiss. . . . Henry was swamped with phone calls, threatening all sorts of things.”[44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403927) The Swiss government told Washington that if further flotations occurred, they would convert dollars into gold and sink the dollar. They refused to let their money function as an international currency. They also pressured the Bank of England. “There was a *froideur* between the Bank of England and the Swiss central bank for some time,” recalled Collins.[45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404139) The ill-fated Copenhagen issue was both the first and the last Swiss-franc Euroissue for a generation.
这一次，加尔布雷思警告华尔街 23 号要准备好迎接愤怒的回应，但谁也没想到会引发如此轩然大波。“瑞士银行简直疯了，”加尔布雷思说，“他们打电话给亨利·亚历山大，质问道：‘你不能这么干。瑞士法郎不是国际货币，它应该由瑞士管控……’亨利的电话快被打爆了，威胁层出不穷。” [44](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2403927) 瑞士政府向华盛顿表态：如果再发生此类发行事件，他们将把美元兑换成黄金，从而拖垮美元。他们拒绝让自己的货币充当国际货币，同时还向英格兰银行施压。“有很长一段时间，英格兰银行与瑞士央行之间关系冷淡，”柯林斯回忆道。 [45](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404139) 那次命运多舛的哥本哈根发行，既是一代人以来首笔瑞士法郎欧洲债券，也是最后一笔。

Meanwhile, the Germans still smarted from the Neckermann issue and awaited a chance to retaliate. When Morgan et Compagnie, S.A., announced an issue for another German mail-order house, Friedrich Schwab and Company, Deutsche Bank saw its golden opportunity for revenge. Instead of obtaining written contracts from underwriters, Morgans proceeded with far more tenuous “indications of interest.” This would prove a fatal mistake. The firm also enlisted a small German trade-union bank that would prove too weak to stop the coming onslaught. Once the issue was announced, Deutsche Bank sprang its power play, putting intense pressure on banks around the world not to participate. It was a full-scale disaster for Morgans, which had to swallow \$9 million worth of the \$13-million stock issue, a huge amount for those days. The New York office was stunned.
与此同时，德国人仍因内克曼事件而耿耿于怀，伺机报复。当摩根公司宣布为另一家德国邮购公司弗里德里希·施瓦布公司发行股票时，德意志银行看到了复仇的黄金机会。摩根公司没有与承销商签订书面合同，而是仅凭更为脆弱的“认购意向”推进此事。事实证明，这将成为致命的错误。该公司还拉拢了一家实力薄弱的小型德国工会银行，而这家银行根本无法抵挡即将到来的攻势。股票一经发行，德意志银行便施展其权力手腕，向全球各大银行施加巨大压力，迫使其不参与认购。这对摩根公司来说是一场彻头彻尾的灾难，在 1300 万美元的股票发行中，他们不得不自己吃下 900 万美元，这在当时是一笔巨额资金。纽约办事处震惊不已。

The passive partner, Morgan Grenfell, was very upset by the brash “American” way in which the Morgan Guaranty people had conducted themselves. Under U.S. law, 23 Wall couldn’t pump in more capital, and Morgan Grenfell had to organize a temporary rescue among London’s merchant banks—an act for which it felt itself insufficiently appreciated by its American cousins. The Paris operation was later bailed out when Singer Company chairman Donald Kircher, who sat on Morgan Guaranty’s board, bought Schwab for \$16 million.
作为被动方的摩根建富（Morgan Grenfell）对于摩根保证信托（Morgan Guaranty）那伙人张扬的“美国式”作风深感不满。根据美国法律，华尔街 23 号（23 Wall）无法注入更多资金，摩根建富不得不在伦敦各大商业银行之间组织一场临时救援——它觉得美国表亲对此并未给予足够感激。后来，辛格公司（Singer Company）董事长唐纳德·基彻（Donald Kircher）——他同时也是摩根保证信托董事会成员——以 1600 万美元买下施瓦布（Schwab），这才解救了巴黎的业务。

In the meantime, another complication arose, deepening the sense of disaster in Paris. The SEC ruled that Morgans couldn’t both act as a trustee for companies in New York and underwrite for them in Paris. This was the last straw: Morgan Guaranty withdrew from running the Paris operation. “John Meyer, head of international operations, was very downhearted as a result of the Schwab deal,” recalled Galbraith.[46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404295) Shattered by the Schwab affair, Morgan Guaranty wouldn’t return to Euromarket issues for more than a decade. For a bank so surefooted in foreign markets in the 1920s, it was a crushing defeat and left a legacy of self-doubt in securities work.
与此同时，另一个棘手的问题接踵而至，让巴黎的危机感愈发浓重。美国证券交易委员会裁定，摩根公司不能既在纽约担任公司受托人，又在巴黎为其承销债券。这成了压垮骆驼的最后一根稻草：摩根担保公司退出了巴黎业务的运营。加尔布雷思回忆道：“国际业务主管约翰·迈耶，因施瓦布交易一事，心情格外低落。” [46](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404295) 受施瓦布事件重创后，摩根担保公司超过十年未再涉足欧洲美元市场。对于这家在 1920 年代海外市场游刃有余的银行而言，这无疑是一次惨败，并在其证券业务中留下了挥之不去的自我怀疑阴影。

Enter Morgan Stanley, then a bit belatedly discovering the Euromarkets. While Henry Alexander had busily set up operations around the world, Morgan Stanley still lacked a single European office. It began to shed its insularity in 1966, when Bill Sword and Frank A. Petito made a secret trip to Rome and met with Guido Carli, head of the Banca d’Italia. Petito, born in Trenton, New Jersey, was the first Italian-American partner at Morgan Stanley and had always been a potential secret weapon in Italy. But he didn’t speak Italian, and the aging, aristocratic Giovanni Fummi, who still advised the Morgan houses in the 1950s, scoffed at him as a peasant.
摩根士丹利登场了，不过它发现欧洲美元市场时已有些姗姗来迟。当亨利·亚历山大在全球各地忙着布局时，摩根士丹利在欧洲连一个办事处都没有。1966 年，它开始摆脱这种孤立主义，比尔·斯沃德和弗兰克·A·佩蒂托秘密出访罗马，会见了意大利央行行长圭多·卡利。佩蒂托出生于新泽西州特伦顿，是摩根士丹利首位意大利裔合伙人，在意大利一直是个潜在的秘密武器。但他不会说意大利语，而那位上了年纪、贵族气十足的乔瓦尼·富米——他在 20 世纪 50 年代仍为摩根家族提供咨询——嘲笑他是个乡巴佬。

The imaginative Petito had an inspiration. From Italian exports and overseas remittances, Carli had stored up \$4 billion in excess dollars. Petito suggested that Morgan Stanley’s big clients in Italy—Exxon, General Motors, and Du Pont—could borrow in liras and convert them into dollars the same day, relieving Carli of his excess. Carli was delighted and swore Morgan Stanley to silence about this exclusive deal. In two whirlwind months, Morgan Stanley did \$600 million worth of the secret lira loans, whetting the firm’s appetite for European work and building its reputation for finding pockets of money buried around the world.
富有想象力的 Petito 灵机一动。Carli 从意大利的出口贸易和海外汇款中攒下了 40 亿美元的过剩美元。Petito 提议，摩根士丹利在意大利的大客户——埃克森（Exxon）、通用汽车（General Motors）和杜邦（Du Pont）——可以借入里拉，并于当日兑换成美元，这样就能帮 Carli 消化掉过剩的美元。Carli 大喜过望，并要求摩根士丹利对这笔独家交易守口如瓶。在短短两个月的旋风式操作中，摩根士丹利完成了价值 6 亿美元的隐秘里拉贷款，这既点燃了公司对欧洲业务的热情，也为其在全球寻找隐藏资金的口袋赢得了声誉。

In January 1967, Morgan Guaranty brought in Morgan Stanley to run the ailing Paris operation, selling it two-thirds of the business; it retained a one-third share with Morgan Grenfell, the Dutch firm of Mees and Hope, and the Wallenberg family’s Enskilda Bank of Stockholm. The one-third stake was patterned after Morgan Guaranty’s one-third stake in Morgan Grenfell. Petito was willing to give Morgan Guaranty half the Paris operation, but the bank’s confidence was shattered after the Schwab debacle, and it preferred a minority share.
1967 年 1 月，摩根保证信托引入摩根士丹利来管理其亏损的巴黎业务，并将三分之二的股权出售给它；摩根保证信托自身保留了三分之一股份，与摩根建富、荷兰的米斯霍普公司以及瓦伦堡家族的斯德哥尔摩恩斯基尔达银行共同持有。这三分之一的持股模式仿照了摩根保证信托在摩根建富持有的三分之一股份。佩蒂托原本愿意将巴黎业务的一半分给摩根保证信托，但在施瓦布事件惨败后，该银行的信心受挫，更倾向于持有少数股权。

For this new Morgan et Compagnie International, the old crew was pushed out and a more seasoned Morgan Stanley group under Sheppard Poor came in to run it. Their advent coincided with a Eurobond boom. Once Morgan Stanley shook off its insularity and discovered the outside world, it made a spectacular success in Paris, financing Standard Oil of New Jersey, U.S. Steel, Eastman Kodak, Texaco, American Tobacco, Procter & Gamble, Amoco, and so on. As the lugubrious atmosphere waned, the Paris venture surpassed all rivals. By 1975, it would issue \$5 billion in yearly offerings.
对于这家新兴的 Morgan et Compagnie International，原班人马被挤出，由 Sheppard Poor 领导的一支经验更丰富的摩根士丹利团队接手管理。他们上任之际，正赶上欧洲债券市场的繁荣期。一旦摩根士丹利摆脱了固步自封的姿态，开始探索外部世界，便在巴黎大获成功，为新泽西标准石油、美国钢铁、伊士曼柯达、德士古、美国烟草、宝洁、阿莫科等公司提供融资。随着沉闷气氛的消退，这家巴黎合资企业超越了所有竞争对手。到 1975 年，其年度发行额将达到 50 亿美元。

With Morgan et Compagnie International, the Morgan community of interest evolved into a more direct fusion of overseas securities work. Without fanfare, the House of Morgan was being welded back together. It was a loose partnership. Morgan Guaranty’s involvement in Paris was passive, one of many minority stakes it held, and John Meyer, Jr., saw it mostly as a way to avoid having to refer clients to Chase or First National City for Eurobusiness. Yet whatever its limitations, Morgan et Compagnie International represented partial repeal of Glass-Steagall.
随着摩根国际公司（Morgan et Compagnie International）的成立，摩根的利益共同体在海外证券业务上走向了更为直接的融合。在不声不响中，摩根财团正在重新凝聚。这是一种松散的合伙关系。摩根担保（Morgan Guaranty）在巴黎的参与是被动的，只是其持有的众多少数股权之一，而小约翰·迈耶（John Meyer, Jr.）主要将其视为一种途径，以避免在欧元业务上不得不将客户推荐给大通银行（Chase）或第一国民城市银行（First National City）。然而，无论存在何种局限，摩根国际公司都标志着《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》（Glass-Steagall）的部分废除。

At 23 Wall, it hurt Morgan Guaranty to hand over the Paris reins to Morgan Stanley. The Morgan Stanley people felt Morgan Guaranty never delivered the promised clients, while Morgan Guaranty always sensed inadequate gratitude for having launched Morgan Stanley abroad. (The Morgan houses were always amazingly thin-skinned with each other.) It was a turning point for Morgan Stanley, which gained a critical foothold in Europe. It sent “revolvers” to Paris, who got international seasoning. Morgan Stanley proudly applied its new Morgan et Compagnie label to all overseas issues except those of Australia. In those days, it never dawned on Morgan Guaranty that it was breeding a competitor or that it would emerge as a rival investment bank of Morgan Stanley in the 1980s.
在华尔街 23 号，摩根担保公司将巴黎业务交给摩根士丹利时，内心颇感不甘。摩根士丹利觉得摩根担保从未兑现承诺过的客户资源，而摩根担保则始终认为，自己当初把摩根士丹利推向海外市场，对方却从未心怀感激（这两家摩根机构彼此之间总是出奇地玻璃心）。这对摩根士丹利而言是个转折点——它借此在欧洲站稳了脚跟。公司派"轮岗员工"赴巴黎历练国际业务，除了澳大利亚的海外项目，所有海外发行的债券都骄傲地打上了"摩根公司"的新标签。在那个年代，摩根担保根本没想到自己正在培养一个竞争对手，更没料到这家公司会在 20 世纪 80 年代成为与摩根士丹利分庭抗礼的投资银行。

Morgan Guaranty kept one piece of European business all to itself. In 1968, it started Euro-clear in Brussels, the largest clearing system for Eurosecurities and the first to automate the market. Initially it aroused powerful, paranoid resistance from European banks, which thought their inner secrets would be divulged to the House of Morgan. The genius of Euro-clear, in fact, lay elsewhere. It became enormously lucrative because traders left in the system money that could be lent to other participants, who used their Eurobonds as collateral. Morgan Stanley was never invited to share in the Brussels operation. The community of interest among the Morgan banks was always a community of convenience. Whenever one bank dug up buried treasure, it hoarded it and tried to keep it from its Morgan brethren. Hence, this era of collaboration among the Morgan banks, far from bringing them closer together, would eventually drive them apart, breeding mutual suspicions and accusations of double-dealing. Their relations would end up having the special rancor of a family feud.
摩根担保公司独揽了一项欧洲业务。1968 年，它在布鲁塞尔创立了 Euro-clear，这是当时最大的欧洲证券清算系统，也是首个实现市场自动化的系统。起初，这一举动引发了欧洲银行强烈的戒备与抵触，它们担心自己的内部机密会泄露给摩根家族。实际上，Euro-clear 的精妙之处在于别处。它变得极为赚钱，因为交易商留在系统中的资金可以贷给其他参与者，后者则用手中的欧洲债券作为抵押。摩根士丹利从未被邀请参与布鲁塞尔的这项业务。摩根系银行之间的利益共同体始终是一种便利性的联盟。每当某家银行挖到宝藏，它就会私藏起来，试图不让摩根系的其他兄弟染指。 因此，摩根各家银行之间的这一合作时代非但没有让它们走得更近，反而最终将彼此推得更远，滋生出相互猜疑和指责对方背信弃义的暗流。它们的关系最终染上了家族世仇特有的那种怨恨。

JAPAN was the country that produced the most persistent friction between Morgan Guaranty and Morgan Stanley. Outside Europe and North America, finance ministers frequently assumed Morgan houses were closely affiliated and constituted a *de facto* House of Morgan. This confusion was most pronounced in Japan, which had its own conglomerates, or *zaibatsu*, organized around core banks. “Every time they wrote about us in the Japanese newspapers,” recalled Jack Loughran of Morgan Guaranty, “they would refer to the Morgan *zaibatsu* that controls General Motors and U.S. Steel.”[47](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404461)
日本是让摩根保证信托与摩根士丹利之间摩擦最为持续不断的国家。在欧洲和北美之外，财政部长们常常认为摩根各家银行关系密切，构成一个事实上的摩根财团。这种混淆在日本最为明显——日本本身也拥有围绕核心银行组建的企业集团，即“财阀”。摩根保证信托的杰克·洛兰回忆道：“每次日本报纸写到我们，都会提到‘控制着通用汽车和美国钢铁的摩根财阀’。”

For a long time after the war, the problem seemed academic as Japan emerged slowly from its defeat. When Tokyo’s stock exchange reopened in 1949, it was a small, provincial affair. During the occupation, General Douglas MacArthur reformed Japanese finance along American lines and even authorized a Glass-Steagall equivalent, Article 65, separating banking and securities work. MacArthur wanted to splinter and neutralize the *zaibatsu* that had dominated interwar Japan and cooperated with the military in their East Asian conquests. Briefly, Japanese banks took on neutral occupation names. When the Americans left, Mitsubishi, Sumitomo, and the others reverted to their traditional names. During the occupation, four American banks—National City, Bank of America, Chase, and Manufacturers—set up branches to serve military personnel. After admitting American Express for traveler’s checks, the Ministry of Finance halted further foreign penetration, and the “bamboo curtain” descended.
战后很长一段时间里，随着日本缓慢走出战败阴影，这个问题似乎只是纸上谈兵。1949 年东京证券交易所重新开市时，规模极小，活脱脱一个地方性市场。占领期间，道格拉斯·麦克阿瑟将军按美国模式重塑日本金融体系，甚至批准了相当于《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》的第六十五条，将银行与证券业务分离开来。麦克阿瑟想拆分并削弱那些曾在两次世界大战期间主宰日本、并勾结军方进行东亚侵略的财阀。一时间，日本银行纷纷改用中立的占领期名称。等美国人一走，三菱、住友等银行又恢复了原名。占领期间，四家美国银行——国民城市银行、美国银行、大通银行和制造商银行——设立了分支机构，为军人提供服务。在允许美国运通开展旅行支票业务后，大藏省便叫停了进一步的外资渗透，“竹幕”就此落下。

As their economy rebounded in the early 1950s, the Japanese wanted to reinstate their spotless credit reputation and make good on old Morgan-sponsored debt—the 1923 earthquake loan and the 1930 gold-standard loan—on which they had stopped payment after Pearl Harbor. Boasting that they hadn’t defaulted in two thousand years, they made a great ceremony of resuming payment and refurbishing their Morgan ties. After Japan signed the peace treaty with the United States in 1951, a Ministry of Finance official came to 23 Wall saying, “I have come to honor my signature.”[48](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404619) With help from Smith, Barney and Guaranty Trust, Japan serviced its bonds in full, and two Smith, Barney officials were decorated by the emperor.
20 世纪 50 年代初，随着经济复苏，日本政府迫切希望恢复其无懈可击的信誉，并偿还当年由摩根承销的两笔旧债——1923 年的地震贷款和 1930 年的金本位贷款——这些债务在珍珠港事件后便停止偿还。日本政府以"两千年未有过违约记录"为傲，隆重举行了恢复还债的仪式，并借此重续与摩根的关系。1951 年日美签订和平条约后，一位日本财务省官员来到华尔街 23 号，郑重表示："我来履行我的签字承诺了。"在史密斯·巴尼公司与信孚银行的协助下，日本全额偿还了债券本息，两位史密斯·巴尼的高管因此获得了天皇授勋。

J. P. Morgan and Company had always been proud of its preeminence in Japan. The bank would cite decorations bestowed by Emperor Hirohito upon Jack Morgan, Tom Lamont, and Russell Leffingwell. But in the 1950s, its scant resources were exhausted by England and France, and it couldn’t re-create its special relationship with Japan. This began to change after the merger with Guaranty, which had been a major trustee for Japanese government and electric-utility bonds. It was also a Wall Street training ground for many Japanese bankers, who went home and copied its forms for their own banks.
J. P. 摩根公司向来以在日本举足轻重的地位而自豪。公司曾多次提及裕仁天皇授予杰克·摩根、汤姆·拉蒙特和拉塞尔·莱芬韦尔勋章的往事。然而到了 20 世纪 50 年代，其有限的资源已被英法两国消耗殆尽，无力再续与日本的那段特殊关系。直到与担保信托银行合并后，局面才开始改观——担保信托银行曾是日本政府及电力债券的主要受托机构，也是众多日本银行家接受华尔街训练的地方，他们回国后纷纷效仿其模式来打造自己的银行。

The two banks had another advantage in pursuing Japanese business—a virtual monopoly in American depositary receipts or ADRs, which were invented by Guaranty Trust back in 1927. ADRs permitted American investors to buy foreign stocks in the United States with a minimum of difficulty. They would actually buy receipts against shares held in a foreign bank vault. The coopera-ting American bank would convert dividends into dollars and spare the investor foreign-exchange problems. In the spring of 1960, Regis Moxley of Morgan Guaranty, an evangelist for ADRs, visited Japan to preach their virtues. Afraid ADRs would breach the nation’s capital controls, the Ministry of Finance warily consented to an ADR for Sony, the first ever for a Japanese stock. Setsuya Tabuchi, chairman of Nomura Securities, later said, “If there was a single milestone in the internationalization of the Japanese financial market, it came in 1961 when Sony issued American depositary receipts in the U.S.”[49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404745)
这两家银行在开拓日本业务时还有另一大优势——美国存托凭证（ADRs）市场近乎被它们垄断，而 ADRs 正是由 Guaranty Trust 早在 1927 年发明的。ADRs 让美国投资者能够便捷地在美国购买外国股票，实际上他们买的是存放在外国银行金库中的股票凭证。合作的美国银行会将股息兑换成美元，为投资者省去外汇兑换的麻烦。1960 年春，摩根担保银行（Morgan Guaranty）的 ADRs 狂热推广者 Regis Moxley 前往日本宣扬其好处。日本大藏省担心 ADRs 会突破国家资本管制，勉强批准了索尼公司首次发行 ADRs，这也是日本股票首次发行 ADRs。野村证券（Nomura Securities）会长 Setsuya Tabuchi 后来表示：“如果说日本金融市场国际化进程中有一个里程碑，那就是 1961 年索尼在美国发行了美国存托凭证。” [49](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404745)

As with Schwab, Morgan Guaranty, long absent from foreign markets, inadvertently stirred up local ire. With ADRs, Morgans had to assign a foreign bank to hold the actual shares while it issued tradable receipts in New York. Naively hoping to spread business democratically among Japanese banks, Moxley tapped the Bank of Tokyo as custodian for Sony’s ADR. He didn’t realize that as Sony’s principal banker, Mitsui would resent encroachment on its territory. An incensed Mitsui delegation appeared at Morgan’s doorstep to protest this serious violation of protocol. “They almost chopped my head off,” declared Bob Wynn of Morgan Guaranty. When the bank issued ADRs for Toshiba, Hitachi, and Fuji Iron and Steel, it didn’t repeat the error.
与 Schwab 相似，长期缺席海外市场的摩根担保银行（Morgan Guaranty）也无意中激起了当地的不满。通过发行 ADR，摩根必须指定一家外国银行来持有实际股票，同时在纽约发行可交易凭证。天真的莫克斯利（Moxley）以为能民主地将业务分散给各家日本银行，便选择了东京银行作为索尼 ADR 的托管方。他未料到，三井银行作为索尼的主办银行，会对自家地盘被侵吞感到不满。愤怒的三井代表团直接找上门来，抗议这一严重的越界行为。“他们差点砍了我的头，”摩根担保银行的鲍勃·温（Bob Wynn）心有余悸地说道。此后，当该行为东芝、日立和富士制铁发行 ADR 时，便再未重蹈覆辙。

In the 1960s, Morgan Guaranty decided to pierce the bamboo curtain and upgrade its representative office into a Japanese branch—extremely difficult at the time. It faced a terrible handicap because of Morgan Stanley’s attitude toward the country. Morgan Stanley had mostly confined its foreign dealings to tried-and-true Western clients—Canada, Australia, France, and Italy. Spoiled by its rich American clientele, it was more ambivalent about foreign markets than was Morgan Guaranty. The problem was compounded by the fact that several partners were war veterans and openly antagonistic toward Japan. This attitude didn’t matter in the 1950s, when Japan was still poor and borrowed heavily from the World Bank. Later in the decade, however, World Bank president Eugene Black told two Ministry of Finance representatives that a rejuvenated Japan had outgrown the World Bank and should tap Wall Street on its own. When they asked Black whom they should see, he handed them—just by coincidence—a World Bank prospectus with First Boston and Morgan Stanley on the top.
20 世纪 60 年代，摩根担保银行决心穿透竹幕，将其代表处升级为日本分行——这在当时极其困难。由于摩根士丹利对日本的态度，它面临着严重的劣势。摩根士丹利的海外业务大多局限于久经考验的西方客户——加拿大、澳大利亚、法国和意大利。被其庞大的美国客户群宠坏了的摩根士丹利，对海外市场的态度比摩根担保银行更加矛盾。问题更为复杂的是，几位合伙人是二战老兵，对日本公开表现出敌意。在 20 世纪 50 年代，这种态度无关紧要，因为当时日本还很贫穷，严重依赖世界银行的贷款。然而，到了 50 年代末，世界银行行长尤金·布莱克告诉两位大藏省代表，重振旗鼓的日本已经超越世界银行的扶持阶段，应该自行涉足华尔街了。 当他们问布莱克该见谁时，他顺手递给他们一份世界银行的募股说明书——纯属巧合——封面赫然印着第一波士顿和摩根士丹利的名字。

Preparing for a large metropolis of Tokyo issue, the Japanese went first to First Boston and were so impressed that they accepted them as co-managers. Expecting equally considerate treatment at Morgan Stanley—wasn’t the House of Morgan Japan’s honored friend?—they were coldly and uncivilly rebuffed. “The old-timers in the Ministry of Finance were really horrified,” said Morgan Guaranty’s Loughran, who had to deal with the unpleasant consequences for 23 Wall.[50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404918)
为了筹备东京这座大都市的一笔巨额债券发行，日本人首先拜访了第一波士顿，对方给他们留下了极佳的印象，于是便接受了第一波士顿作为联合管理人。他们本以为在摩根士丹利也会受到同样周到的礼遇——毕竟，摩根家族不是日本的老朋友吗？——结果却遭到了冷漠且无礼的拒绝。“当时日本财务省的老臣们真是吓坏了，”摩根担保银行的劳伦说道，他后来不得不为华尔街 23 号处理这些棘手的后果。 [50](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2404918)

Why did Morgan Stanley spurn Japan? The decision contained elements of both business calculation and xenophobia. Morgan Stanley still held to an unswerving policy of managing securities issues alone or not at all, a lucrative form of snobbery that allowed the firm to pocket all management fees. Barging in blindly, the Japanese didn’t realize that their casual decision to accept First Boston first made it impossible for Morgan Stanley to participate without violating its cardinal rule. The sole exception it had made was for the World Bank itself, which doubtless misled the Japanese.
为何摩根士丹利对日本敬而远之？这一决定既基于商业盘算，也夹杂着排外情绪。摩根士丹利始终坚守一条不可动摇的原则：要么独立承销证券发行，要么干脆不做——这种高姿态实则利润丰厚，能让公司独吞全部管理费。日本人贸然闯入市场，并未意识到自己随意选择第一波士顿银行的决定，已使摩根士丹利无法在不违背核心原则的前提下参与其中。而摩根士丹利此前唯一的例外给了世界银行，这无疑误导了日本人。

Why didn’t it make another exception? “The prestige of being banker to the World Bank was regarded as greater than being banker to a defeated country,” explained former Morgan Stanley partner Alexander Tomlinson. “The Japanese didn’t realize what a sensitive subject it was for us. The partners involved in the war weren’t enthusiastic about doing business in Japan anyway. And the older partners had deeply resented the attack on Pearl Harbor. They had a personal relationship with Japan that they felt had been offended.”[51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405076) Also, in Western eyes, Japan seemed less of a full-fledged industrial power than a superior version of a developing country. In the early 1960s, it was still the second most heavily indebted country in the world, after India.
为什么不能再破例一次？“能成为世界银行的‘银行’，可比当战败国的‘银行’光彩多了，”前摩根士丹利合伙人亚历山大·汤姆林森解释道。“日本人根本没意识到，这对我们来说是多么敏感的话题。那些参加过战争的合伙人对在日本做生意本来就不热心。老一辈的合伙人对珍珠港偷袭耿耿于怀，他们觉得与日本的私人交情被玷污了。” [51](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405076) 此外，在西方人眼中，日本与其说是个羽翼丰满的工业强国，不如说是个升级版的发展中国家。60 年代初，它仍是仅次于印度的全球第二大债务国。

Whatever the business rationale, the Morgan Stanley decision was laced with a subtle racism, for similar objections never stopped the firm from doing business with Italy or Germany. “The Germans somehow converted themselves into nice guys,” an ex-partner said cynically. “All the Nazis seemed to have been purged.” At the time, a single Morgan Stanley partner could blackball a major decision. One partner who was a former fighter pilot made rabble-rousing patriotic speeches invoking Hirohito, Pearl Harbor, the sale of war bonds, and so on. To this day, Perry Hall is unrepentant about the decision: “I wouldn’t do business with the Japanese even now.”[52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405243) Although younger partners thought the older ones a bunch of stubborn fools, the latter wouldn’t budge.
无论出于何种商业考虑，摩根士丹利的决定都暗藏着微妙的种族歧视，因为类似的反对意见从未阻止过该公司与意大利或德国做生意。"不知怎么的，德国人把自己变成了好人，"一位前合伙人讽刺地说，"所有纳粹似乎都被清除了。"当时，摩根士丹利的一位合伙人就能一票否决重大决策。一位曾当过战斗机飞行员的合伙人，发表了煽动性的爱国演讲，搬出裕仁天皇、珍珠港事件、战争债券销售等等。直到今天，佩里·霍尔仍然对这个决定毫无悔意："即便是现在，我也不跟日本人做生意。" [52](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405243) 尽管年轻合伙人认为老一辈是一群顽固的傻瓜，但他们却不肯让步。

This intransigence was a big problem for J. P. Morgan and Company, which was then trying to wrest big balances from Japan’s Ministry of Finance. Fearing fallout from Morgan Stanley’s insult, international chief John Meyer had long, angry talks with his close friend John Young, who was Morgan Stanley’s senior partner for foreign business. The problem acquired new urgency for Morgan Guaranty after a September 1964 meeting in Tokyo. Morgan Guaranty director, Steve Bechtel, Sr., and his friend General Lucius Clay, former military governor of Germany, prevailed on Meyer to try to open a Japanese branch. Bechtel said Tokyo was becoming a world information capital. More to the point, his own firm planned to open an office there—always strong inducement for Morgans to follow. A decision was made to open a Japanese branch as part of Morgan’s strategic blueprint to establish branches in major world markets and end its Eurocentric bias.
这种固执态度对 J. P. Morgan and Company 来说可是个大麻烦，当时他们正想从日本财政部那里拿下大笔存款。摩根大通国际业务主管 John Meyer 担心摩根士丹利的冒犯行为会引发连锁反应，于是和自己的好友、摩根士丹利海外业务高级合伙人 John Young 进行了长时间且激烈的交涉。1964 年 9 月在东京开完会后，这个问题对 Morgan Guaranty 来说就更加紧迫了。Morgan Guaranty 的董事 Steve Bechtel, Sr.和他的朋友、前德国军事总督 Lucius Clay 将军，说服了 Meyer 去尝试开设日本分行。Bechtel 说东京正在成为世界信息中心，更关键的是，他自己的公司也计划在那里设点——这对摩根来说向来是个强烈信号。于是他们决定在日本设立分行，作为摩根全球战略布局的一部分，目的是在世界主要市场都建立据点，摆脱过去以欧洲为中心的老路子。

Japan was then far more closed than it is today, and no bureaucrat cared to accept the political stigma of admitting Morgans. The government felt there were enough foreign banks; approving more was extremely sensitive business. In 1965, Tom Gates, who had fought at Iwo Jima and Okinawa, made an initial presentation for a branch license to Michio Mizuta, Japan’s foreign minister. Even with the Japanese, Gates had a straightforward style; skipping ceremony, he bluntly asked for a branch. Far from settling anything, this meeting was the opening round of a long, dreary battle. For all the bowing and deference to the Morgan name, the Japanese made the bank grovel for twenty-nine months. The Finance Ministry laid down two rules: Morgans couldn’t discuss the negotiations with the U.S. embassy (honored) or with a lawyer (flouted). The talks sometimes seemed to be endurance contests, conducted by the Japanese in an elaborate language of shrugs, sighs, and veiled allusions to nameless difficulties.
当时的日本比现在封闭得多，没有任何官僚愿意背负批准摩根银行入驻的政治污名。政府认为外资银行已经足够，再批准新银行是极其敏感的事情。1965 年，曾在硫磺岛和冲绳战场作战的汤姆·盖茨，首次向日本外相水田三喜男提交了设立分行执照的申请。即使面对日本人，盖茨依然直来直去，省去寒暄，开门见山地要求开设分行。这次会面非但没有解决任何问题，反而拉开了一场漫长而令人沮丧的拉锯战。尽管日本人对摩根的招牌毕恭毕敬，但银行方面却不得不低声下气地熬过了 29 个月。大藏省立下两条规矩：摩根不得与美国大使馆讨论谈判事宜（这条他们遵守了），也不得咨询律师（这条他们违反了）。 谈判有时似乎成了耐力较量，日本人以一种复杂的语言进行——耸肩、叹气，以及对种种无名困难的隐晦暗示。

The bank deployed many emissaries and sent international head John Meyer, Jr., for early talks. Meyer, who would follow Gates as Morgan Guaranty chairman in 1969, was the most austere and humorless of the postwar bank chairmen. Tall and granite-hard, he had a bald domed head and huge bushy eyebrows, which the Japanese interpreted as the sign of a great samurai spirit. He seldom smiled as he puffed watchfully, enigmatically, on his pipe. With his elephantine memory and vast experience, he always seemed several steps ahead of everybody else, and his thoroughness at the House of Morgan was legendary. Having started at the Guaranty Company in 1927, he could remember obscure details of railroad bonds from forty years before.
该银行派出了多名特使，并派遣国际业务主管小约翰·迈耶（John Meyer, Jr.）参与早期谈判。迈耶于 1969 年接替盖茨成为摩根保证银行董事长，是战后各银行董事长中最为刻板严肃、毫无幽默感的一位。他身材高大，如花岗岩般坚硬，光秃的圆顶脑袋上长着浓密粗大的眉毛，日本人视之为伟大武士精神的象征。他很少微笑，总是警惕而神秘地抽着烟斗。凭借大象般的记忆力和丰富的经验，他似乎总是比别人领先几步，他在摩根银行的严谨细致堪称传奇。他于 1927 年加入担保公司，能记得四十年前铁路债券的琐碎细节。

Unlike the charming Henry Alexander and Tom Gates, Meyer made subordinates feel uneasy. Borrowing an old trick from FDR, he would assign more than one person to the same task, although he often knew the answer in advance. He would pretend to defer to the judgment of some young banker on a giant loan, then watch the young person squirm. He had an incomparable mastery of detail that some colleagues found counterproductive. “He would read every last word of a credit report on a tiny \$9-million loan to Ireland,” said a former colleague.
与魅力十足的亨利·亚历山大和汤姆·盖茨不同，迈耶让下属感到不安。他效仿富兰克林·罗斯福的旧招，总是将同一任务分派给多个人，尽管他往往早已知道答案。他会假装在巨额贷款上听从某位年轻银行家的判断，然后看着对方局促不安。他对细节的掌握无与伦比，但一些同事认为这反而适得其反。“他会逐字逐句地读完一笔 900 万美元的小额爱尔兰贷款信用报告，”一位前同事说道。

Meyer carried Morgan secrecy and discretion to new lengths. Despite a vast awareness of political developments, he had the lowest public profile of any Morgan chairman. Constantly absorbing financial intelligence from around the world, he was thick as thieves with Arthur Burns, Fed chairman after William McChesney Martin, and they had long phone talks each Sunday. “Meyer should have been in the CIA,” remarked an admiring former associate; “he was a real inside man, with a style of quiet influence.” With Meyer, the Morgan bank would no longer exercise the visible Wall Street leadership that had come so naturally to his predecessors.
迈耶将摩根银行的保密和谨慎推向了新的高度。尽管对政治动态了如指掌，他却是在所有摩根董事长中公众曝光度最低的一位。他不断吸纳全球金融情报，与威廉·麦克切斯尼·马丁之后的美联储主席亚瑟·伯恩斯关系铁得像亲兄弟，两人每周日都要通上很久的电话。“迈耶真该去中央情报局工作，”一位钦佩他的前同事评价道，“他是真正的内部人，风格是不动声色地施加影响。”在迈耶的掌舵下，摩根银行不再像他的前任们那样自然而然地展现出华尔街的显赫领导地位。

A man of legendary strength, Meyer’s idea of a happy weekend in Tokyo was climbing Fujisan. He was capable of outlasting even the Japanese. Each time that Meyer in New York would ask Loughran in Tokyo what he needed, Loughran would cable back, “Patience, patience, patience.”[53](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405401) The patience would be rewarded: Morgan Guaranty became the first American bank since 1952 to win branch approval and penetrate the bamboo curtain.
迈耶拥有传奇般的体力，他在东京度过愉快周末的方式就是攀登富士山，甚至比日本人还能坚持。每当纽约的迈耶问东京的洛夫伦需要什么时，洛夫伦总会回电说：“耐心，耐心，再耐心。”这份耐心最终得到了回报：摩根担保银行成为自 1952 年以来第一家获批设立分行、穿透竹幕的美国银行。

In breaking through Japan’s *cordon sanitate*, Morgan negotiators profited unexpectedly from history. Some Finance Ministry old-timers remembered Tom Lamont. Of even greater help was the memory of a beautiful, fated *geisha.* In 1904, Pierpont Morgan’s nephew George Morgan, living in Yokohama and collecting Japanese art, married Yuki Kato. Although George’s friends told reporters, “I understand that the young lady he has married comes of an excellent family,” George had actually bought out the contract of a young *geisha.*[54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405559) During their honeymoon in Newport and on Long Island, Yuki Morgan was ostracized by George’s family, and the couple ended up living in Paris. When George died, in Spain in 1915, his wife inherited his wealth.
在突破日本的卫生隔离防线时，摩根集团的谈判代表意外地从历史中获益。一些财务省的老人还记得汤姆·拉蒙特。而一段关于一位美丽艺伎的宿命回忆，更是帮了大忙。1904 年，皮尔庞特·摩根的侄子乔治·摩根住在横滨，收藏日本艺术品，并迎娶了加藤由纪。尽管乔治的朋友们对记者说：“我听说他娶的这位小姐出身名门”，但实际上乔治是花钱赎出了一位年轻艺伎的契约。 [54](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405559) 在新港和长岛度蜜月期间，由纪·摩根遭到乔治家族的排斥，夫妇俩最终定居巴黎。1915 年乔治在西班牙去世后，他的妻子继承了他的财富。

Yuki’s trust fund was administered by J. P. Morgan and Company, which was unable to send her payments during World War II. Afterward, Henry Alexander went to Cologne as vice chairman of the U.S. Strategic Bombing Survey Committee. He tracked her down and gave her not only the accumulated interest but interest on the interest as well. When Yuki later moved back to Kyoto, she told her neighbor, “You always must trust the Morgans.”[55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405711)
Yuki 的信托基金由 J. P. Morgan and Company 管理，二战期间这家公司无法向她汇款。战后，亨利·亚历山大（Henry Alexander）以美国战略轰炸调查委员会副主席的身份前往科隆。他找到了 Yuki，不仅给了她累积的利息，还给了她利息的利息。后来 Yuki 搬回京都时，她对邻居说：“你一定要永远相信摩根（Morgans）。” [55](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405711)

On March 24, 1969, the day the Morgan branch finally opened in Tokyo, one of Yuki’s Kyoto neighbors traveled in to deposit her life savings of Y 8 million (by this point, Yuki was dead); the neighbor was gently told that Morgans wasn’t that kind of bank. Adding to Yuki’s fame was a musical based on her life, which depicted her pining for a young student as she was signed over to George Morgan. When Morgan negotiators made the rounds in Tokyo, staid bureaucrats would stop to ask about Yuki Morgan. “The Japanese are very sentimental,” explained Morgan’s Loughran, “and everybody over forty knows the story.”[56](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405869)
1969 年 3 月 24 日，摩根银行东京分行终于开业那天，Yuki 在京都的一位邻居专程赶来，存入她毕生的 800 万日元积蓄（彼时 Yuki 已离世）；邻居却被告知，摩根并非那种储蓄银行。而一部根据 Yuki 生平改编的音乐剧更让她声名大噪，剧中描绘了她被转让给 George Morgan 时，仍对一名年轻学生魂牵梦萦。当摩根的谈判代表在东京四处奔走时，那些古板的官员也会驻足打听 Yuki Morgan 的故事。“日本人很念旧情，”摩根的 Loughran 解释道，“四十岁以上的人都知道这段往事。”

Another arrow in the Morgan quiver was Aisuke Kabayama. As the prewar Count Kabayama, he had squired Tom and Florence Lamont around Tokyo and in the 1930s had helped Lamont set up an information bureau. After the occupation, he had to renounce his title; now he would be an elite Morgan adviser. Employed by the new Morgan branch, he could perform no prosaic mortal labors but only advise. Even without his title, his noble identity was well-known, and he could get an appointment with anybody from Emperor Hirohito on down.
摩根箭袋中的又一支箭，便是樺山愛輔。战前，他还是樺山伯爵时，曾陪同汤姆·拉蒙特和弗洛伦斯·拉蒙特夫妇游览东京，并在 1930 年代协助拉蒙特设立情报局。占领结束后，他被迫放弃了爵位；如今，他成为摩根的一位精英顾问。受雇于摩根新设的分支机构，他无需从事任何凡俗的日常工作，只需提供咨询意见。即便没有了头衔，他高贵的身份依然广为人知，从昭和天皇往下，任何人他都能约见。

In its quest for a branch, the House of Morgan had had one last weapon, a man of indeterminate nationality known as both Satoshi Sugiyama and David Phillips. In the 1950s, John Phillips, an American professor working with the U.S. Air Force in Japan, befriended a Mr. Sugiyama of the Asahi newspapers, who wanted an American education for his son Satoshi. (An American education then inspired veneration in Japan.) Phillips adopted the boy; rechristened David Phillips, he lived for thirteen years with the Phillips family in Long Beach, California. He studied Japanese daily. After graduating from Berkeley, he worked in New York for Morgan Guaranty’s stock-transfer unit. Then the Immigration and Naturalization Service challenged his adoption and threatened to deport him; Davis, Polk lawyers contested in vain. So in 1964, David Phillips, *né* Sugiyama, was posted to Morgan’s representative office in Tokyo.
摩根家族在寻求设立分支时，最后的手段是一位国籍不明的人士——他既叫杉山聪，又叫大卫·菲利普斯。20 世纪 50 年代，一位受雇于美国空军、在日工作的美国教授约翰·菲利普斯，结识了《朝日新闻》的杉山先生，后者希望儿子杉山聪能接受美国教育。（当时美国教育在日本备受推崇。）菲利普斯收养了这个男孩，改名为大卫·菲利普斯，此后他在加州长滩的菲利普斯家生活了十三年，每日研习日语。从伯克利毕业后，他在纽约为摩根担保公司的股票过户部门工作。随后，移民归化局质疑收养的合法性，并威胁将其驱逐出境；戴维斯·波克律师事务所的律师虽据理力争，却无济于事。于是，1964 年，原名杉山聪的大卫·菲利普斯被派往摩根驻东京代表处任职。

The deportation had unexpected consequences within the Morgan empire. While Phillips started as office manager, he was soon caught up in the secret lobbying for the Morgan branch. As he said, “Because of my Japanese face, the Japanese press would never question why I was going into the Ministry of Finance all the time.”[57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405995) With no real bank training, he was good at scouting out new business and didn’t mind going out nightly on the Ginza, Tokyo’s main commercial district. He was a perfect Morgan hybrid—a fully bilingual, bicultural man who wore expensively tailored suits and cuff links and smoked Dunhills.
遣返事件在摩根帝国中引发了意想不到的连锁反应。菲利普斯最初只是办公室经理，但很快就被卷入摩根分行的秘密游说活动。正如他所说：“就因为我长着一张日本脸，日本媒体从不质疑我为什么总往财政部跑。” [57](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2405995) 虽然没受过正经银行培训，但他很擅长挖掘新业务，也不介意每晚在东京银座——这座城市最繁华的商业区——出没应酬。他是个完美的摩根复合体：精通双语、深谙两国文化，身穿量身定制的昂贵西装、佩戴袖扣，抽着 Dunhill 香烟。

The advent of Phillips helped Morgan Guaranty solve its Morgan Stanley problem. To overcome any lingering Japanese doubts about the Morgan banks, John Meyer kept urging John Young of Morgan Stanley to open a Tokyo office. With the imposition of U.S. capital controls, Morgan Stanley needed to find money around the world for its clients, and Japan was becoming too big to ignore. So in 1970, Morgan Stanley agreed to set up a Tokyo representative office on two conditions—that it get space adjoining a new World Bank liaison office in Tokyo and that David Phillips head the office. Morgan Guaranty obliged on both counts.
菲利普斯的到来帮助摩根担保信托解决了它的摩根士丹利问题。为了消除日本方面对摩根银行仍存的疑虑，约翰·迈耶不断敦促摩根士丹利的约翰·杨在东京开设办事处。随着美国资本管制的实施，摩根士丹利需要为全球客户寻找资金，而日本市场已庞大到不容忽视。于是，1970 年，摩根士丹利同意在东京设立代表处，但有两个条件——一是办公室要毗邻世界银行在东京新设的联络处，二是由戴维·菲利普斯负责该办事处。摩根担保信托对这两个条件都予以满足。

So remarkable was Phillips’s work for Morgan Stanley that in 1977 he became its first nonwhite managing director. He always surprised new clients. “I’ve been with David a few times when we are meeting clients,” said Morgan Stanley’s Bob Greenhill, “and you can just see their jaws drop.”[58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406145) Phillips signed up Hitachi, Mitsubishi, Industrial Bank of Japan, and Nippon Steel. He won a large private placement from Sony despite competition so feverish that Goldman, Sachs reportedly asked Henry Kissinger to talk with Sony chairman Akio Morita. Yet as much as Morgan Guaranty people admired Phillips, they watched with dismay as he exploited the old confusion about the Morgan *zaibatsu.* Morgan Guaranty, for instance, had floated a convertible Eurobond issue for Takeda, a Japanese pharmaceutical company. Yet after the senior Takeda died, his son took a bond issue to Morgan Stanley, thinking he was rewarding an old friend. David Phillips, thought the Morgan Guaranty people, didn’t always clear up such misconceptions.
菲利普斯在摩根士丹利的工作表现极为出色，1977 年成为公司首位非白人董事总经理。他总能令新客户大跌眼镜。“我和大卫一起见过几次客户，”摩根士丹利的鲍勃·格林希尔说，“你能看到他们惊得下巴都要掉了。” [58](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406145) 菲利普斯签下了日立、三菱、日本兴业银行和新日铁。尽管索尼的竞争异常激烈，高盛甚至据说请动了亨利·基辛格去游说索尼董事长盛田昭夫，他还是为索尼赢得了一笔大规模的私募配售。然而，摩根保证信托的人虽然钦佩菲利普斯，却眼睁睁地看着他利用人们对摩根财团由来已久的混淆来谋利。例如，摩根保证信托为日本制药公司武田发行了一笔可转换欧洲债券。但武田老董事长去世后，他的儿子以为是在回报老朋友，竟把一笔债券发行业务交给了摩根士丹利。 摩根保证信托公司的人认为，大卫·菲利普斯有时并不会去澄清这类误解。

Business for the new Morgan Guaranty branch was extremely profitable, with huge profit margins on loans. The bank made yen loans to American multinationals in Japan and dollar loans to Japanese companies, including Hitachi, Toshiba, Nippon Steel, Honda, and Nippon Tel and Tel. Both Morgan houses targeted the Mitsubishi group, which stressed shipping and heavy industry and meshed with Morgan’s smokestack orientation. As a purveyor of clothing and other army provisions, the Morgan bank’s prewar favorite *zaibatsu*, Mitsui, hadn’t fared as well after the armistice.
新设立的摩根保证信托分行业务利润极其丰厚，贷款利率差巨大。该行向在日本的美国跨国公司提供日元贷款，并向日立、东芝、新日铁、本田以及日本电报电话公司等日本企业提供美元贷款。两家摩根机构都将目标锁定在三菱集团上——该集团专注于航运和重工业，与摩根偏重传统工业的取向不谋而合。作为服装及其他军需品的供应商，摩根银行战前最青睐的三井财阀，在停战后却没那么风光了。

What made Japanese business irresistible was that dollar loans to companies were guaranteed by their Japanese bankers. In 1976, Ataka and Company, Japan’s third largest trading house, failed after taking losses on a Newfoundland refinery; Morgans had a loan outstanding to Ataka. The morning he received the news, Bob Wynn called Lew Preston, head of international banking, and said, “Lew, it looks like you’ve got your first loss in Japan.” Before the day was over, however, the Bank of Japan stepped in and ordered Ataka’s principal banker, Sumitomo Bank, to bail the company out. That afternoon, an astonished Wynn called Preston back to say their unsecured loan would be honored after all. “Why the hell are they doing that?” asked a bewildered Preston. “They’ve got their marching orders from the government,” Wynn replied.[59](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406271) In this banker’s paradise, Morgan Guaranty would steadily expand its country lending limit. Nobody ever regretted the marathon ordeal of acquiring the Tokyo branch.
让日本企业变得不可抗拒的原因是，向这些公司提供的美元贷款由其日本银行提供担保。1976 年，日本第三大商社安宅产业因在纽芬兰一家炼油厂亏损而倒闭；摩根大通当时还有一笔贷款未收回。鲍勃·韦恩接到消息的当天早上，打电话给国际银行业务主管卢·普雷斯顿说：“卢，看来你在日本出现了第一笔坏账。”然而，当天结束前，日本央行介入，命令安宅产业的主要银行住友银行出手救助。当天下午，震惊的韦恩再次致电普雷斯顿，告知他们的无担保贷款最终将得到偿付。“他们到底为什么要这么做？”困惑的普雷斯顿问道。“他们接到了政府的命令，”韦恩回答。 [59](#The_House_of_Morgan_split_049.html_filepos2406271) 在这个银行家的天堂里，摩根保证信托公司稳步提高了对国家贷款限额。没有人后悔为获取东京分行牌照而经历的漫长磨难。
