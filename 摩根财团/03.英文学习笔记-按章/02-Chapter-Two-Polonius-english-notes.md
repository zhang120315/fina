# Chapter Two Polonius 英文学习笔记

## 本章词汇与短语

### 1. culpability

- **原文语境**：The extent of Pierpont’s culpability ... has been endlessly debated.
- **核心意思**：责任；罪责；应受指责的程度。
- **用法拆解**：比 `responsibility` 更偏“过错责任 / 道德或法律上的可责性”。常见搭配：`the extent/degree of culpability`, `criminal/moral culpability`, `deny/admit culpability`。
- **举一反三**：`blameworthiness`, `liability`, `responsibility`；反义可用 `innocence`。
- **顺手辨析**：`culpability` 有 blame 的味道；`responsibility` 更宽，可中性表示职责、责任范围。
- **例句**：The report focused less on technical errors than on the executives’ moral culpability.

### 2. alternative role model

- **原文语境**：he had an alternative role model in his grandfather...
- **核心意思**：另一个可供效仿的榜样。
- **用法拆解**：`alternative` 表示“可替代的、另一种路径的”；`role model` 是榜样。整句意思是：他本可以效仿祖父那种服务国家的路线。
- **举一反三**：`an alternative path`, `a different example to follow`, `a competing model of conduct`。
- **例句**：For young lawyers, public-interest attorneys can serve as an alternative role model to corporate partners.

### 3. a stand-in $300

- **原文语境**：Pierpont paid a stand-in $300 to take his place...
- **核心意思**：花 300 美元雇替身。
- **用法拆解**：`stand-in` 是“替身、临时代替者”；`pay someone + money + to do sth`。这里是内战征兵制度下的“替人服兵役”。
- **举一反三**：`substitute`, `proxy`, `replacement`；`stand in for sb` = 代替某人。
- **顺手辨析**：`stand-in` 更口语/具体，指临时代替某人的人；`substitute` 更通用，可指人或物。
- **例句**：He hired a stand-in to attend the ceremony, but the decision later damaged his reputation.

### 4. was drafted after Gettysburg

- **原文语境**：when he was drafted after Gettysburg
- **核心意思**：在葛底斯堡之后被征召入伍。
- **用法拆解**：`draft` 作动词是“征兵、征召”；被动式 `be drafted`。Gettysburg 指美国内战关键战役，也可作为时间节点。
- **举一反三**：`be conscripted`, `be called up`, `be enlisted`。注意 `draft` 也可指“草拟文件”。
- **顺手辨析**：`draft` 多指被征召；`enlist` 多指参军，可自愿也可被动语境。
- **例句**：Many young men were drafted after the battle, whether or not they supported the war.

### 5. draft riots

- **原文语境**：a practice that contributed to draft riots in July 1863
- **核心意思**：征兵暴动。
- **用法拆解**：`draft` = 征兵，`riot` = 暴动；复数指一系列骚乱。这里说明“富人可花钱免役”的不公平激化社会冲突。
- **举一反三**：`bread riots`, `tax riots`, `urban unrest`, `public disorder`。
- **例句**：The exemption policy helped fuel draft riots among working-class communities.

### 6. leapt into wild speculation

- **原文语境**：he also leapt into wild speculation in the infamous “gold room”
- **核心意思**：一头扎进疯狂投机。
- **用法拆解**：`leap into` 有“突然投入、贸然进入”的动作感；`wild speculation` 指高风险、非理性的投机活动。
- **举一反三**：`plunge into speculation`, `jump into a bubble`, `engage in reckless trading`。
- **顺手辨析**：`speculation` 偏短期、高风险、赌价格；`investment` 更中性，强调投入与长期回报。
- **例句**：After hearing rumors of a takeover, investors leapt into wild speculation.

### 7. rig the market

- **原文语境**：tried to rig the market by shipping out a large amount of gold...
- **核心意思**：操纵市场。
- **用法拆解**：`rig` = 暗中操纵、做手脚；常见于 `rig an election`, `rig prices`, `rig the market`。
- **举一反三**：`manipulate the market`, `corner the market`, `distort prices`。
- **例句**：The traders were accused of trying to rig the market before the announcement.

### 8. by shipping out a large amount of gold on a steamer

- **原文语境**：by shipping out a large amount of gold on a steamer
- **核心意思**：通过用轮船运出大量黄金。
- **用法拆解**：`by + doing` 表示手段；`ship out` = 运出、发货；`steamer` = 蒸汽船。这里是操纵黄金供给以影响价格。
- **举一反三**：`export gold`, `drain supply`, `move bullion overseas`。
- **例句**：They tried to tighten local supply by shipping out a large amount of grain before prices were set.
